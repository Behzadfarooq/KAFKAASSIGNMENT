[edit] Thanks!

Thanks for your constructive criticism on the Typography article. I'm acting on every suggestion. As a token of gratitude I give you this sacred cat of Halicarnassus, bringer of good wiki vibrations.

Arbo 15:12, 3 September 2006 (UTC) @

"My apologies for just writing up comments and not contributing. My intent on specialist articles such as this is to talk first, and edit later. It gives an opportunity to touch base with the principal authors (hello) of the article before messing with their workflow."
That's no problem at all. I appreciate your cautious approach and welcome critical views and suggestions. If my talk page responses seem knee-jerk at first don't take it too seriously because I usually refactor my responses soon afterwards by reducing copious detail to the essentials (writing is re-writing). At the same time I don't mind contributors diving right in and adding new content to an article, as this is often quicker and more direct than posting on the talk page—as long as you're prepared for bold editing of your contributions. So feel free to add content first and talk later. Often talk is unneccessary as two editors can "talk", so to speak, inside the article by the collaborative editing process and detailed edit summaries.
"Also, I've become a little busy lately with moving city and finding a new job, so I'll probably sit down and have a decent blat at it in about a month or so. I've been keeping a close watch on the page, and I'm impressed with the progress. Keep up the good work!"
Cool, take as much time as you need, and thanks for the encouragement. The so-called Sacred Cat of Halicarnassus is an example of rewarding good behaviour. Good Wikipedians encourage each other.
