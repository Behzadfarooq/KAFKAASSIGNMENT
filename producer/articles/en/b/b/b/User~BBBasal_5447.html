Basil Saour is often referred to by fans and critics alike as "Toronto's finest." Since his birth his parents knew that he was destined for greatness and enrolled him in an Al-Quida training camp at the age of four. After graduating from the academy at the staunch age of 12, he moved to Toronto where he quickly rose the ranks at U of T. He often competed with students from Harvard and left them eating his dust. While at U of T, Basil worked with the worlds best immunologist, geneticist, biochemist, histologist and physiologist. He then decided to pursue a carrier in medicine. After scoring a 40 on his MCAT, he decided that US and Canadian Schools were beneath someone of his far superior intellect and so he set sail for the lovely island of St. Martin to attend AUC.

While at AUC Basil quickly made friends with everyone. He was known as a real go-getter and had a remarkable ability to relate with people. People just loved being around him. In an effort to devote as much time as possible to his studies, Basil decided to forgo some of the minor daily tasks most people consider a necessity. Basil felt that there was not enough time in the day to shower, wash his clothes, brush his teeth, cut his hair or even wear socks. If anything interfered with his studies, he wanted nothing to do with it.

Basil is known around campus as having a profound insight and many of his words of wisdom echo in the halls of AUC. Things like:

- I wish I had the motivation to get cardio because I could so easily get it, my Dad knows every important cardiologist in the world - That test was soooooo easy if you studied for it, how could you not get a 100% - You have to be a dumb ass to fail - U of T is the #1 school in the world, it is far superior to US schools (including Harvard) - I worked with the #1 immunologist in the world

Currently Basil is working on his MD at AUC and will bless the US with his presence in 2008.
