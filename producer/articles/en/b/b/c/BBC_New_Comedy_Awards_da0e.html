The BBC New Comedy Awards first appeared in 1995 and were considered to be one of the top UK comedy newcomer awards until they were axed in 2006. It was announced in the summer of 2006 that they were being replaced by a nationwide talent hunt that places its emphasis on sketch writing and filmed performance. During their decade long life span the finals of this prestigious event have boasted many well known names that have continued to work in comedy to great acclaim - amongst them are Peter Kay, Julian Barrett & Nina Conti.
