BBC Newsline

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

The BBC Newsline ident
BBC Newsline Studio Shot

BBC Newsline is the BBC's regional television news service for Northern Ireland. The programme is broadcast on BBC One from BBC Northern Ireland's headquarters in Broadcasting House, Bedford Street, Belfast. It produces a series of short bulletins and a 30 minute evening news bulletin.

As well as being available via all multi-channel outlets in Northern Ireland (including via Sky Digital channel 101), the programme is available in Great Britain on Sky channel 973 and in the Republic of Ireland on channel 143. As the BBC UK regional TV on satellite service is broadcast unencrypted, it is also possible to receive the programme anywhere in Europe using an appropriate receiver.

BBC Newsline is unique amongst the BBC's regional news programmes in its use of an on-screen DOG throughout its programmes.

[edit] On air

BBC Newsline airs seven days a week:

Weekdays

  • 06.27-06.30 - during BBC Breakfast
  • 06.57-07.00 - during BBC Breakfast
  • 07.27-07.30 - during BBC Breakfast
  • 07.57-08.00 - during BBC Breakfast
  • 08.27-08.30 - during BBC Breakfast
  • 08.57-09.00 - during BBC Breakfast
  • 13.30-13.40 - following the BBC One O'Clock News
  • 15.20-15.25 - before CBBC. This bulletin is signed on-screen; a signer appears beside the presenter in the studio
  • 18.30-19.00 - the main bulletin of the day, following the BBC Six O'Clock News
  • 22.25-22.30 - during the BBC Ten O'Clock News

BBC Newsline also broadcasts regular 30 second news updates at various points during the evening schedule on BBC ONE and BBC TWO Northern Ireland.

Weekends

BBC Newsline broadcasts one bulletin on Saturday and one bulletin on Sunday, the broadcast time of these bulletins varies but they are usually transmitted in the early evening period of the schedule.

[edit] Programme history

The heritage of BBC Newsline dates back to 1957 from which time various incarnations have included Ulster Mirror, Scene Around Six (1969-1984), Inside Ulster (1984-1996) and, since February 1996, Newsline. The main programme was originally titled Newsline 6.30 though the name was shortened with the rebrand two years later.

[edit] Weather presenters

  • Cecilia Daly (Relief weather presenter, 1996 to date)
  • Angie Phillips (Main weather presenter, 1996 to date)
  • Declan Teague (Relief weather presenter, 1996-2002)
  • John Wylie (Relief weather presenter, 1996-2002)

All the above presenters were also staff of the Met Office's Northern Ireland office based at RAF Aldergrove.

When the local presenters are unavailable to present the weather, staff at the BBC Weather Centre in London fill in. So far, Sean Batty and Daniel Corbett have been seen presenting weather bulletins on BBC Newsline. The London staff also present weather bulletins as part of BBC Newsline bulletins during BBC Breakfast.

Short weather bulletins appear at various times during the evening schedule on BBC TWO Northern Ireland. These are presented by the on-duty weather presenter.
