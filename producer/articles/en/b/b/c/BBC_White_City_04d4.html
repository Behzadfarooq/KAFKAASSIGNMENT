BBC White City refers both to a collection of BBC buildings at Wood Lane, White City in West London, and an office building within that collection of buildings.

The buildings are on the site of the Franco-British exhibition of 1908 and also the 1908 Summer Olympics.

The White City building houses most of the BBC's current affairs and factual and learning programmes, such as Panorama, Top Gear, Watchdog and many others.

A second phase of buildings was opened in 2004, known as the BBC Media Village. This new phase consists of the Broadcast Centre, the Media Centre, and two other office buildings: the North and South Perimeter buildings.
