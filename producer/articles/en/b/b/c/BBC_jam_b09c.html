BBC jam

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

BBC Jam logo

BBC jam (formerly known as BBC Digital Curriculum) is an online educational service launched by the BBC in January 2006. Putting the learner at the heart of the content, the service was available for free across the United Kingdom. Offering multi-media rich educational resources, its content was available to all, whether pupils, parents or teachers.

Most content was developed to be highly accessible, offering aids for hearing, sight & mobility difficulties. Numerous subjects were also translated into Welsh, Scottish Gaelic and Irish Gaelic.

One major focus of BBC jam is pupils with SEN. This group of audience is especially ignored by publishers who had little or no interest in developing contents in this area. Many people who are involved in the special needs area are particularly hoping the BBC jam will help encourage more contents to be developed and published.

[edit] English

Based in a back garden, together with pond, shed, and even a beehive, you are introduced to the colourful world of it's 6 characters, Padma, Opera, Earwax, Teardrop, Rollabout & Yikes. Here the user can choose to play with songs, poetry or stories, each of which offers them numerous pieces which they can watch, change, or use as the basis to create their own. Localised in English & Welsh.

[edit] History

BBC jam was commissioned in 2003 by the Secretary of State for Culture, Media and Sport, and designed:-

  • to stimulate, support and reflect the diversity of the UK
  • to innovate continually and to promote technological and pedagogical experimentation
  • to be distinctive from, and complementary to, services provided by the commercial sector

In consultation with BECTA, the Governments educational technology department, the service will cover no more than 50% of the learning outcomes that are amendable to ICT.

The service was suspended on 20 March 2007 at the request of the BBC Trust. The Trust has requested that the BBC management prepares a fresh proposal. This will cover how the BBC should deliver its Charter obligation - promoting formal education and learning, whilst meeting the online needs of school age children. A full Public Value Test will be conducted by the Trust once the proposal is completed, this will include a market impact assessment by Ofcom, the United Kingdom's telecoms regulator.
