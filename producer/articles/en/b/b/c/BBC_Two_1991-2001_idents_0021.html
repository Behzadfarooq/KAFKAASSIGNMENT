This article is about the set of idents used on BBC Two between 1991 and 2001. For more of the channel's identity before and after this timescale, please see the channel's section within the BBC television idents article.

The BBC Two 1991-2001 idents were a set of television idents, broadcast from 16 February 1991 until 19 November 2001 on BBC Two. The idents went on to become one of the most popular series of idents in the world, to the point where they received fan mail.[1] Several of the idents sported a consistent viridian hue, used in various fashions.

[edit] Ident conception

The idents were designed by branding expert Martin Lambie-Nairn, and first aired on the same day as the BBC1 virtual globe ident, also designed by Lambie-Nairn. They were introduced following a survey of viewers by the BBC, which revealed the previous ident for the channel - a computer-generated image of the letters 'T' 'W' 'O' receding into a white background - was considered 'dull' and 'worthy' by viewers.[1][2] The new idents featured a Gill Sans figure '2' in a variety of different forms and environments, a format which is still followed today. All of the original eleven idents, and most of those that followed, were shot as live action using physical models.

Name Image Description Air dates
Paint "Paint" featured a silver '2' on a black & silver background, splashed sideways by a blob of viridian paint. This illusion was created by moving the 2 and the camera onto its side, to make it appear as though the paint was falling sideways. It was both the first and last in this series of idents to be broadcast. 1991-2001
Water This ident featured an aqua-coloured '2' on a background of the same colour, half submerged in a pool of water at a 45 degree angle. One of the four original idents to be shown from start to finish in its run. 1991-2001
Blade One of the four idents to last the entire run, "Blade" featured a sharp, metal 2 falling into a viridian-coloured block and impaling it on its edge, standing and wobbling. 1991-2001
Neon Also known as "Sign", it had a '2' on a dark, black background with seven green neon lights attached to it, flashing in random sequences. It was one of the four original idents to be shown throughout the entire run. 1991-2001
Copper Cut-Out As the title suggests, it featured a copper cut-out of a '2', with sparks flying through the gap. It was withdrawn before the new millennium to make way for a set of new idents. 1991-1999
Glass Featured a glass-cut '2', which reflected light in various directions. 1991-1993
Silk The ident included a swathe of viridian silk literally drifting over the top of a '2' shape, as if they were ocean waves. This ident was also dropped from the line-up on New Year's Eve, 1999. 1991-1999
Paper Cut-Out "Paper Cut-Out" featured a completely grey, paper like foreground, with a '2'-shaped cut-out in the middle. The background behind it featured a blue sky, with white clouds. 1991-1993
Shadow A simple ident, showing a silhouette of a spinning '2' on a green wall. Withdrawn from the line-up in 1997, when the BBC logo was revamped. 1991-1997
Copper Cut-Out B/W Essentially the same as "Copper Cut-Out", but it was recoloured black & white with green sparks. It was withdrawn from the roster in 1997. 1991-1997
Water Reflection A subtle variation on "Water", the light was altered in such a way that it cast a reflection of the water line onto the top half of the '2'. This was also withdrawn before the 1997 revamp. 1991-1997
Garden "Garden" depicted a circular patch of grass in the middle, which sprouted several white flowers that formed the shape of a '2'. Usually appeared before gardening programmes. 1991-1997, 2001
Powder This featured a '2' falling from the top into a layer of white powder, with a layer of viridian paint hidden beneath it. Various coloured lights were cast onto the '2' as it fell. 1992-2001
Optics "Optics" included many optic fibres swirling up and down, slowly and eventually settling into the shape of the '2' logo. Along with powder, this was one of the first new idents to be added to the roster, allegedly replacing "Glass" and "Paper Cut-Out". 1992-2001
Car One of a new series of idents introduced in 1993. It featured a viridian '2' with an aerial moving about in a yellow studio, in the same manner as a remote-controlled car. It's possible that this inspired the 2001-2007 series of idents. 1993-2001
Dog This ident included a fluffy green '2' whirring, yapping and doing backflips in the manner of a toy mechanical dog. 1993-2001
Balloon Another of the more simple idents, "Balloon" featured a silver, helium-filled balloon in the shape of a '2' bobbing around gently in a fixed spot. This was withdrawn in 1997 to avoid a clash with the BBC One balloon. 1993-1997
Crystal Ball A rarely seen ident that was introduced as one of the 1993 idents, "Crystal Ball" was inexplicably dropped after just a few weeks. It featured a crystal ball rolling over a blue and green background, revealing a distorted image of a '2' in the middle. One possible explanation for its removal so quickly after launch was the similarity between it and the BBC1 globe idents. 1993
Firecracker This featured a usual viridian '2', on a desk with live firecrackers attached to it. The firecrackers go off, launching the '2' towards the camera, before it is pulled back into place by an attached cable, which can be clearly seen after the final explosion. The final firecracker actually fails to go off. 1993-1999
Diary This ident featured a white '2' on a black background, with paper layers peeling off and drifting away. This was originally meant to have several layers quickly peel off, but instead looped the same four layers in different directions due to tangling wirework. 1995-1999
Steam Featured a silver '2' with water droplets falling onto and around it. The drops that landed on the '2' boiled away instantly. Withdrawn at the end of 1999. 1995-1999
Aerial "Aerial" featured a '2' with aerials attached to it in a red studio, appearing in random places between flashes of static, before finally appearing in the centre of the screen. 1997-2001
Duck This ident depicted a soapy setting, from which a yellow '2' resembling a rubber duck emerged. This was one of the few occasions the logo was changed to black in order to be visible on the light background. 1997-2001
Paint Pot In a parody of the original "Paint" ident, a silver paint pot is bombarded by lots of little viridian '2's, once again falling sideways. 1997-2001
Zapper This featured a fly zapper in the shape of a '2' upon a wall. A fly is attracted to it, but the zapper breaks on contact and the fly buzzes away, leaving a smoke trail behind it. 1997-2001
Gorilla There were two variations on this ident. A gorilla stands up a yellow '2' in one ident and knocks it over in the other. Originally a special ident for a wildlife programme, it became a regular ident. 1997-2001
Swan Introduced at the start of 1998, it featured three white '2's swimming past a clearly bemused swan, which shakes its head shortly after. 1998-2001
Dalek Originally a special ident for Doctor Who Night, its popularity meant it went on to join the roster of regular idents. It featured Daleks in the shape of '2's moving between a grey corridor. 1999-2001
Excalibur One of six new idents to be introduced at the start of 2000, "Excalibur" featured the ever-familiar viridian '2' rising from a pool of water sideways, á la "Paint". 2000-2001
Catalyst In this ident, droplets hit the '2' from the top right, causing flames to flare up around it. The flames die down after about 20-25 seconds, revealing the 2 to be completely undamaged, before the cycle starts again. 2000-2001
Wave Day Originally called "Wave", This featured a '2' within a sheet of blue liquid that splashes around it throughout. 2000-2001
Predator/Venus Fly Trap "Predator" begins centered on a '2'-shaped butterfly sitting within a jungle environment. The camera then zooms out, and the '2' is gobbled up by a larger '2' in the form of a venus flytrap. This ident was later revived in mid-2006 (renamed "Venus Fly Trap"), and remained in occasional use until the entire set was revamped in 2007. 2000-2001
Kebab Featured a '2'-shaped kebab being grilled. Featured noticeably less than other idents. 2000-2001
Woodpecker This ident depicted five metal poles, of varying distance, against an orange background. '2's edge their way down the poles in the same manner as a mechanical woodpecker. 2000-2001
Wave Night Introduced in the last half of the year, it was a retake on the original "Wave (Day)" ident. It now featured a darker background and liquid pool, with a glowing '2'. This was the final ident to be added to the regular roster. 2000-2001

[edit] Short animations

An example of a trailer animation.

During the run of the idents, there were also quick, usually humourous short animations shown before the trailers. Such examples included a 2-shaped pancake landing in a frying pan, a 2 performing stunts on a bicycle, or a set of spinning plates in the form of the 2. These were sometimes themed to suit a particular event, such as sport or Christmas. Such examples include a set of snooker balls racked in the 2 shape. Also, a set of short animations featuring Wallace & Gromit were used during one Christmas run along with a similarly themed ident.

Today, these animations are not remembered as prominently as most of the idents. They were usually only a few seconds long and, particularly from 1997, the 2 would become a character rather than a symbol, an idea that was carried on for the BBC Two Yellow idents from 2001.

[edit] Christmas Variants

During this period, BBC Two continued with their tradition of creating a brand new ident especially for broadcast in the Christmas season. Ten were made in total during its run.

Year Image Description
1991 This ident featured a star in a wintery sky, throwing snow. It expoded to form a 2 covered in gift wrap.
1992 This ident had a Christmas tree, saturated with lights, blinking a 2 shape in the centre from red lights.
1993 This one featured a group of small creatures hoisting up a 2 on top of a tree. However, the 2 catches fire from the surrounding candles.
1994 A simple ident, with the 2 encased in a wired dome structure amongst a snowy setting.
1995 Created by Aardman Animations, this ident featured popular characters Wallace & Gromit sat around a dinner table, with a 2 covered in blinking lights in the style of Neon. The full version of this is included on the DVD release of the cartoon series.
1996 This featured a 2 made out of sparkling stars, bringing a "magic" feel to that year's Christmas.
1997 Featured the 2 representing a snowman inside a snowglobe. There was a variant of this containing various celebrity faces.
1998 A simple ident was produced this year, with a brightly lit 2 sitting atop a Christmas tree.
1999 This ident saw a 2 with angel wings flying over to a Christmas tree, knocking the fairy off the top and taking its place.
2000 The final Christmas ident of this series saw several 2s ski jumping off a giant slope. The camera then panned back to reveal it was actually a giant 2 itself. This ident was brought back for the 2006 Winter Olympics (with the addition of the purple box BBC Two logo from the then current set).
