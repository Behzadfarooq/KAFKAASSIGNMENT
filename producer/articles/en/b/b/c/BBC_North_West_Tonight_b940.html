BBC North West Tonight is the news programme for the BBC North West English region region. Produced by BBC, it broadcasts at 6.30pm every weeknight. Like all BBC's regional news programmes, it uses the corporate BBC font and idents.

[edit] BBC North West region

The North West region comprises Greater Manchester, Merseyside, Lancashire, Cheshire, South Cumbria and North West Derbyshire. The programme also serves the Isle of Man. Transmitters in the extremes of West Yorkshire, covering towns like Todmorden broadcast this regional version but broadcast the YTV news show, Calendar. The programme can be watched in any part of the UK (and Europe) on digital satellite channel 978 on the BBC UK regional TV on satellite service.

For a few years in the late 1980s North West Tonight covered the whole of Cumbria but after campaignimg by viewers the area was returned to a reformed BBC North East and Cumbria Region.

BBC North West Tonight broadcasts on BBC One from its studio at the New Broadcasting House, Oxford Road, Manchester. The equivalent daytime news bulletins are transmitted as North West Today. The programme began as Look North, but was changed to Look North West in 1980, then News Northwest in 1981, then from September 3 1984, North West Tonight began. The new corporate BBC ident started on September 16, 2002. The main signal for the programme comes from the Winter Hill transmitting station near Horwich.

[edit] Presenters

Current presenters include Gordon Burns. Burns was previously best known as presenter of ITV game show The Krypton Factor, although he did start as a local evening presenter for Ulster Television, then Granada News in the 1970s. When he joined the programme in 1997, it apparently experienced a large increase in ratings becoming more popular than ITV's rival Granada Tonight (now Granada Reports), and has held onto its lead ever since.

[edit] Former presenters

Former presenters include Stuart Hall, Martin Henfield, David Davies and Philip Hayton. Stuart Hall left in 1990, though can be heard often on BBC Radio Manchester waxing lyrical about Manchester City and he appears on Greater Manchester's local television station, Channel M, presenting the segment Hall's Heroes on Channel M News.

[edit] BBC North West Today weather game

Every weekday, the presenter of the lunchtime BBC North West Today (usually Gordon Burns) plays a word game with the weather presenter standing next to his desk. The weather presenter, usually Dianne Oxberry, presents her report and includes in it a secret word. The secret word is an anagram of the initial letters of placenames shown on the map. After the report is finished, the newsreader has to guess what the word is. The weather game was said to increase interest in watching the local weather.
