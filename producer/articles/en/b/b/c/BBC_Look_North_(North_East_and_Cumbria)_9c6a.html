BBC Look North (North East and Cumbria) is the BBC's regional news programme for the BBC North East and Cumbria region. Presented in the evenings by Carol Malia and on most mornings by Colin Briggs (though on some occasions, Gerry Jackson) from BBC Broadcasting Centre on Barrack Road in Newcastle upon Tyne the programme features reports from Northumberland, Tyne and Wear, Teesside, County Durham, North Yorkshire and Cumbria. Daily sports bulletins are presented by Jeff Brown and former olympic triple-jumper Jonathan Edwards.

The program can be watched in any part of the UK (and Europe) on digital satellite channel 975 on the BBC UK regional TV on satellite service.

[edit] History

First broadcast in 1964 from 54 New Bridge St, Look North was presented for much of existence by the legendary North East anchorman, Mike Neville MBE, noted for his booming gravelly voice, distinctive glasses and longevity (thirty two years at the BBC). Its original title was Home At Six[1], hosted by Frank Bough (who moved to BBC Sport in 1964), which began in 1963. The TV region started in 1962. In 1986, it moved to the studios in Fenham, with Radio Newcastle.

For a short period in the late 1980s Cumbria was switched to the BBC North West Region and began receiving North West Tonight instead of Look North. The North East and Cumbria region was reformed after campaginging by viewers in Cumbria that they were being overlooked in favour of news from the more populous areas of the north west such as Greater Manchester. In 1996, Neville was poached by Tyne Tees Television to present their re-launched news coverage. Look North is now anchored by Carol Malia.

Look North was the second English region to switch to the BBC generic look on 18th October 1999. It followed a second generic look across regional news on the 16th September 2002. The current look for the programme was introduced on 16th May 2005.
