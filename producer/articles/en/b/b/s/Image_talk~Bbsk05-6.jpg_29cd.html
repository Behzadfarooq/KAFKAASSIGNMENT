[edit] fair use disputed

A company putting an image on their web site is not the same as the image being released as a promotional picture. Nor is the image necessary for the article, nor does it contribute anything of great value. Several other images uploaded by this user are also being disputed as fair use, and many of them are considered simply an attempt to shock. See Talk:Hentai, Wikipedia:Images and media for deletion/2006 March 30 (Image:AlGoreAndGlowingOrb.JPG) (was used for vandalism), and Special:Contributions/SlashDot -- Ned Scott 01:24, 4 April 2006 (UTC)

There is nothing "shocking" about this image. It is an accurate depiction of bukkake. Whether it violates a copyright is another matter. —The preceding unsigned comment was added by 207.200.116.68 (talk • contribs).

Shocking isn't a measure of how extreme something is, but more of an issue of contrast. If someone clicks on a link because they don't even know what bukkake is, and then see this, that is shocking. Maybe not a bad thing, but I think it's pretty clear that it was the intent of the uploader to shock people (wether or not he was successful is up to interpretation). In other words, I am not calling this image shocking, but saying I suspect the uploader uploaded it with the intent to shock. I agree it does accurately depict bukkake, but with that logic it's also ok to supply an image for child rape. Just because the image is about the subject at hand does not mean it is good for the article. Like the example I stated, most people who will read this article will likely be people who don't know what the term means. If Wikipedia was filled with images like this then more people would be reluctant to even use Wikipedia. They wouldn't know what image would greet them around the corner. Even if the images are OK for the reader, they might still not want to access such "surprise" articles, as many times they are in a public place when accessing Wikipedia (such as a school library, a coffee shop, from work, etc). -- Ned Scott 07:20, 8 April 2006 (UTC)
Shocking content or not, Wikipedia is not censored. Many images (example) hosted here are shocking and potentially offensive, but they have demonstrative value that is simply incommunicable with words. Child rape is a poor counterexample, as images or portrayals of such are illegal in the state of Florida, where Wikipedia hosts its servers. Adding this particular image to Bukkake does not qualify as fair use, however, as it is a commercial product whose value is compromised by unauthorized dissemination.
