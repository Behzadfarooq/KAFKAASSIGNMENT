[edit] How to get an article kept

There are various things you can do to get an article kept. You'll have to wait until the article is unprotected. This may mean requesting unprotect later. The vandals may get fed up and go away, but even if they don't your edits will be in the memory and can be put back easily.

1. The first thing is to make sure the spelling is correct. There are several spelling mistakes in the article.

2. Also the article should be wikified and cleaned up

3. This is an encyclopaedia. You therefore have to prove in the article that the subject has had a wide effect, deserving to be in an international encyclopaedia (albeit an enormous one). If you can do this successfully and I see something truly new I would consider changing my vote to keep.

While you are waiting for the article to be unprotected, you can make new edits on the talk page.

By the way, I went through a similar baptism of fire in my first few days at Wikipedia. See Wikipedia:Votes for deletion/Lordship Lane, an article which I started and was nearly deleted. Even if this article is deleted you can start many more. Make sure they adhere to Wikipedia:Importance. A small word of caution. Not all the votes in the voting are always counted equally. The administrator who counts the votes must weigh up all the different votes, look at the article and come to a decision. --File Éireann 16:41, 10 December 2005 (UTC)
