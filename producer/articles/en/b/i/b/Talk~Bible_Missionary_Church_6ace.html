This was added to the article by someone else, I'm moving it here because it doesn't belong in the article:

"I was a student at that school. It was the worst 4 yrs of my life. From it I developed insecurities, depression, and confusion about how life in the real world works. There is so much more to life than just learning how to pray, and read your Bible. I came out lacking social skills, believing that contact between men and women was sin unless you were married and it was ordered by God, and condoned by a religious leader. It screws you up, basically, and when you do get thrown into the real world, you make decisions and do things without having the common social knowledge of the consequences. You end up feeling more lost than before, and that your whole life is a complete mess. And you don't know who is more to blame, you-or them. And most of all you feel there is no way to fix it or go back; so you build on top of the rubble that lies beneath, and learn in your 20's what you should've learned in your teens."

Hanako 00:33, 28 October 2006 (UTC)
