The Bible Missionary Church is a Christian denomination that is part of the Conservative Holiness Movement. Its unofficial beginning was a 5 week tent meeting held on September 8, 1955. The body included roughly 150 individuals and was led by Rev. Glenn Griffith. The denomination itself was founded shortly after with a total of 126 members.

The church finds its roots in Methodism with more recent ties to the Church of the Nazarene. The group broke away from its mother church as a result of perceived liberal trends.

The denomination has experienced international growth throughout its roughly 50 years of existence and presently has between four and five hundred members.


[edit] The Bible Missionary Church Today

The heartland of the Bible Missionary Church is located on the Illinois-Iowa border, signified primarily by Bible Missionary Institute of Rock Island, IL, the denomination's private religious institution. The Rock Island area is also home to one of the nation's largest concentrations of BMC members and Milan's First Bible Missionary Church.

The BMC as a denomination is headed by two General Moderators. Other Administrative controls of the BMC include a Church manual which dictates dress codes and other standards as well as general theology and other member requirements. The Bible Missionary Church forbids the private use of internet, though members are permitted to exercise its use outside the confines of home for business purposes. This rule has resulted in a dearth of online information regarding the denomination.

The Bible Missionary Church also sponsors the Beulah Mountain Christian Academy, a privately operated boarding school in Kentucky for at-risk children, which has been investigated for child abuse in the past. These false charges have been adjudicated in the state courts of Kentucky no evidence of abuse has ever been substantiated with all persons involved found innocent of all charges.

[edit] Bible Missionary Institute

Bible Missionary Institute is a 4 year college that offers associate and bachelors degrees. The curriculum is geared toward theology, missions and religious education.

BMI has trained hundreds of preachers, missionaries, teachers, and laymen of the Bible Missionary Church and other conservative churches.

BMI is not an accredited college, therefore the credits earned there are not recognized by most universities. However, there are those that have used it for a sound foundation to go to the higher degrees such as Masters or Ph D. of which those schools have accepted the credits from BMI.

BMI has no professors or instructors with Ph D's, many consider it just an advanced Sunday school, or a place for the young women of the church to meet future husbands. It has long been said that the main degree offered at BMI is the Mrs. degree.
