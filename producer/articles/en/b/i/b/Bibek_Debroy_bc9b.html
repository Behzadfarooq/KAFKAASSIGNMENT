Bibek Debroy was the Secretary General of the PHD Chamber of Commerce and Industry in Northern India and a columnist with the Indian Express, the Business Standard and the Financial Express. He is now a Contributing Editor with the Indian Express and a Professor with the International Management Institute in Delhi.

He was Director at the Rajiv Gandhi Institute for Contemporary Studies, Rajiv Gandhi Foundation, in New Delhi, between 1997 and 2005. He resigned from the foundation over controversy arising out of one of his studies which concluded that Gujarat was the most economically free state. Later on he clarified that, the ranking was done on aggregate basis with at least 3 main variables and was subject to the way aggregation was done. Reportedly, it created differences with the Sonia Gandhi, Trustee of the foundation and wife of Late Rajiv Gandhi.

Debroy is a well respected and clear-headed Indian economist. He was educated in Presidency College, Kolkata, Delhi School of Economics and Trinity College, Cambridge). He has worked at Presidency College (Kolkata), Gokhale Institute of Politics and Economics (Pune), Indian Institute of Foreign Trade (Delhi), National Council of Applied Economic Research (Delhi) and as Consultant, Department of Economic Affairs, Ministry of Finance, Government of India. He was also the Director of a project known as LARGE (Legal Adjustments and Reforms for Globalizing the Economy), set up by the Ministry of Finance and UNDP to examine legal reforms.
