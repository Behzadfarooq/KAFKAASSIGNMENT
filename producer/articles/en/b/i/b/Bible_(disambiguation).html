The word Bible can mean:

  • The Bible, a holy book in Judaism and Christianity (see also Tanakh, Hebrew Bible)
  • "Bible" may be used as a generic term to describe a book or text that any devotee to an idea should read, or a book that lays out a way to do things in a particularly well-accepted way. Outside the main use, this is the second most common usage. Use of the word in a figurative sense, as in "any authoritative book." Example: the Gardening Bible.
  • The term Bible may also be used as a term to compare another faith's religious scriptures to the way the Bible is for Christians, such as the Tao te Ching being the Taoist "Bible." This term usually only applies to the scripture viewed as central to the faith, so in the above example, the I Ching would not be called a "Bible.":
    • Tao te Ching (Taoism)
    • The Quran (Islam)
    • Analects (Confucianism)
    • Upanishads (Hinduism)
    • Tripitaka (Buddhism)
    • Satanic Bible (Satanism)
    • Dianetics (Scientology)
  • We usually think of it as one book, but it is really means a collection of many different books, the Kolbrin could be called a "Bible." The word comes from the Greek ta biblia ("ta biblia"), which is a plural word meaning "books."
