The Biblical Research Institute (BRI) is a service department of the Seventh-day Adventist Church with the three stated functions of research, apologetics (defense of the church's beliefs), and service to the church.[1] It serves as a theological consultant to the General Conference. It consists of four Adventist theologians working at the church headquarters in Silver Spring, Maryland, and currently directed by Ángel Manuel Rodríguez. The institute also directs the Biblical Research Institute Committee (BRICOM) which consists of about forty members worldwide, and BRISCO, which investigates the relationship between science and religion.

The institute is an official service department of the General Conference of Seventh-day Adventists.

It is representative of the conservative end of mainstream Adventist scholarship.[2]

[edit] History

The Biblical Research Institute was established as a service department of the church on September 25, 1975 by the General Conference. It has historical roots in a number of other organizations.

Previously it had been known as the Biblical Research Committee. This committee did not have authority to represent the church on theological matters. It was formed in 1969 from a merger between the Defense Literature Committee and the Biblical Study Research Committee, which had previously worked independently.

The Defense Committee was established in 1943 with an essentially apologetic purpose.

The Biblical Study and Research Committee was created on September 24, 1952. To a certain extent it took the place of the Biblical Research Fellowship, which was being dissolved at the time. The fellowship was an unofficial society of Adventist Bible teachers, organized in 1940.[3]

[edit] Biblical Research Institute Committee

The Biblical Research Institute Committee examines theological issues that the Seventh-day Adventist Church faces. Its members are elected at General Conference Sessions.
