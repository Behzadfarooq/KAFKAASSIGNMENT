[edit] Article figures contested

The claim that BBN reaches 200 million people does not reflect the truth. About 90% of all stations owned and operated by BBN are low powered and have a very limited range. This is specially true of radio stations overseas, but also a reality in the United States (even in Charlotte, where BBN is headquartered, its presence is negligible). In Latin America there is not a single station that ranks at the top of its market share . Furthermore, many of the outlets are found in unregulated countries where a myriad of stations abound and where finding a BBN station is almost an impossible task. BBN also sabotages its impact by purchasing AM stations, a medium that is unanimously seen by experts as decadent. These undeniable facts translate into very few listeners and invalidate any claim that millions are reached in the Americas. BBN also claims that by streaming into several languages the exposure of its message is amplified. The only three countries where online streaming could be of any impact would be Japan, USA and Germany, where broadband is easily accessible. All the remaining countries where BBN streaming ministry is present, the standard venue is still dial up, which makes it prohibitive for most people to listen to the ministry on a regular basis. The concept of streaming a radio programing is not one that promises success. During the daytime, most businesses and companies frown upon their employees using their broadband to listen to radio stations, be they secular or evangelical. Nightime is usually reserved for family and other endeavors. The percentage of people dropping everything off to sit around the computer to listen to BBN at night(like our grandparents used to do in order to hear president Truman on the radio) is statistically null. The streaming concept is a very shrewd marketing tool but a poor and unwise use of donated funds. BBN depends on the generosity of its listeners. This is a theme focused ad naseum during Shareatons and spots encouraging people to contribute. This concept, however, is obliterated once you consider how the ministry deals with its employees. This is specially true when employees decide to leave the ministry or are laid off. Severance pay is never considered and ex employees are forced to come to the realization that most secular companies will treat you in a more Christlike manner than the Bible Broadcasting Network.
