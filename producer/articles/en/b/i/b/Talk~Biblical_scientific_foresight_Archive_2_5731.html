Why the article is POV

I wanted the article to be de-protected, so I agreeded to let a paragraph back in the article. However, I still feel that the overall tone of the article is overly slanted towards promoting BSF, rather than providing a balanced look at the subject.

Specifically, many "facts" are noted without enough context to be meaningful, and without the context that really explains how they fit into the whole argument.

Here's one sentence:

For example, it has been found that Pakastani caregivers who use topical antibiotics for their babies circumcision wounds instead of using dung, ghee, urine etc, prevent that babies from getting neo natal tetanus.

Interesting stuff. Types of dung, ghee (clarified butter) and urine have been used for thousands of years (since pre-egyptian times) for their anti-biotic and other medicinal purposes. Yet what does this have to do with BSF?

Examine the chain of logic implied by the presence of that sentence in this article:

  • Since: Use of antibiotics to treat circumcisions leads to less neo-natal tetanus than the use of dung.
  • Therefore: Dung might be a contributing factor to neo-natal tetanus when applied to open wounds (the study's conclusion).
  • Therefore: The use of dung as a medicine is harmful
  • Since: The Egyptians used dung in some remedies
  • Therefore: Egyptian medicine contains harmful remedies
  • Since: The Mosaic code contains no harmful remedies
  • Therefore: The Mosaic code is more advanced medicinally than Egyptian medicine
  • Therefore: The Mosaic code must have been the word of God.

The problems here are obvious: this argument is basically an appeal to emotion - "Egytian medicine was based on excrement, Mosaic medicine was based on cleanliness". This ignores that fact that there are thousands of detailed and effective remedies and medical procedures in the Egyptian papyri, and almost none in the Mosaic code. The ommision of context makes the inclusion POV.

The facts of the matter are:

  • The levels of hygiene suggested by a strict following of the Mosaic rituals of cleanliness are relativly modern.
  • A small minority of fundamentalist Christians regard this as evidence of knowledge not possible at that time, and hence of the inerrancy and divine inspiration of the Bible.

And that's it for that topic. Very little other than is actually adding to the article qua Wikipedia article. The dung argument is POV, and if mention of it is to be made in the article, it needs to be in proper context.
