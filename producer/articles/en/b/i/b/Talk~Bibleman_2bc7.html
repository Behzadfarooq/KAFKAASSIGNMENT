[edit] My experience with Bibleman (I am not a Christian parent and not interested in anything other than making fun of this show)

I cannot explain how unintentionally hilarious this show is. For those not aware, Bibleman is the ultra-low-budget super-hero series written, produced, directed by and starring the one and only Willie Aames. That’s right, folks: Buddy Lembeck is a super-hero. Bibleman is an earnest, if utterly pathetic, attempt to win kids over to Christianity by trying to underbid the Power Rangers for the contract to their souls. In the episode I watched, Bibleman battles the forces of the neither terribly evil nor terribly competent “El Furioso”, who uses something akin to pixie dust to incite rage and fury in those (in this case, a preteen named “Jordan”) unwittingly exposed to his gold craft sparkles. It was at this point that I began to wonder why El Furioso—who looks like something the Borg rejected—couldn’t just expose people to a series of Jack Chick tracts, or perhaps to his grating sidekick “Ludicrous”, who makes Jeff Spicoli look bottomlessly reserved and endlessly dignified. Just hearing Ludicrous call his employer “Your Dudeness” made me ache; what sort of actor’s hell do you have to be in to play the idiot sidekick to the idiot bad-guy who fights Willie Aames?

Bibleman does intense research back at his lab—which is decorated with items from Spencer’s Gifts, so you know it’s a real lab—to assertain the source of young Jordan’s rage. He peppers his pseudo-research with one-liners from Scripture and follows up his near-discovery of facts with his “transformation” sequence, in which he steps into a closet without a door and has a machine zap his armor onto his body. It must be noted that his costume includes things like the “Shoes of Peace” and a helmet with a cleft chin built into the strap. Bibleman tracks down El Furioso, who appears to be doing his evil research while working the night shift at an industrial refinery, and the two do battle in what could only be described as the height of tepidity. Of course, Bibleman vanquishes the sinister El Furioso—which surely leaves the rest of his bowling team in trouble of having to find someone to replace him—and evil is vanquished, at least until someone in town steps on a crack, which has been known to break their mother’s back.

If there is a God (and Bibleman assures me there is) then this show will be riffed upon by a reunited cast of Mystery Science Theater 3000.--robnospamdumas+wikipedia@gmail.nospam.com 23:31, 1 May 2005 (UTC)
