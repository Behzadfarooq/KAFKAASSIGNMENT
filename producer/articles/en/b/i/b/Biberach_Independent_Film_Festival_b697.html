The Biberach Independent Film Festival (B.I.F.F.) is a film festival that premiered in November 2004 in the southwestern German town of Biberach an der Riß, which is situated just south of Ulm. The festival is for a German audience and presents recent German and international shorts and documentaries. B.I.F.F. runs parallel to a local festival of German movies - the "Filmfest Biberach" - which focuses mainly on feature films. B.I.F.F. was temporarily discontinued in 2006. Negotiations for a revival are under way.

[edit] Categories

In 2005 the B.I.F.F. competition accepted entries in the following categories:

I: Transformat – This category is about changes, whether from 16mm to DVD, male to female, swords to plowshares, feature to short, etc.

II: Setbacks/Backlash The times they are a-changing – for the worse. Broken dreams and social decline. And striking back. Against globalisation etc.

III: Beyond Germany Exciting things are happening in the field of documentaries and experimental shorts outside of Germany. In Asia, Africa, etc. And German film makers see the world.

IV: Blood relations Violence within and outside the family. Forced or desired. Relations between victims. Bullies, terrorists, violent offenders and their victims

V: Camp! High camp and trashy entertainment. How bad can it get? And what turns trash into cult movies?
