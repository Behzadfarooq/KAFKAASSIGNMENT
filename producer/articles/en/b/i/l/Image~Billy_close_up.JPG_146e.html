[edit] Summary

×Billy Yeager is the nephew of the legendary pin-up photographer Bunny Yeager. Billy plays every single musical instrument and has written and recorded over 700 songs. He has 22 C.D.'s that vary in styles from pop, surf jazz, gypsy Mexican, Spanish flamenco, bluegrass trance,and folk rock. Billy has toured and played with Wild Cherry, Inner Circle, Ira Sullivan, Jaco Pastorius and has recorded with Rita and Pearl Marley. He has won several songwriting contests- in 1983 all 5 songs submitted into The Miller High Lifes Rock to Riches Talent Search made the finals, he was the only one chosen from 600 people for Star Search audition in 1985, he has been a featured performer for The Nationally syndicated Song Writers in The Round series. In 1993 he was discovered by Grammy Award Winner Bruce Hornsby and was managed by Gerald Georgettis who worked with Perry Farrell and Jane's Addiction and organized the Lolapoolooza tour. Billy received a development deal with Capitol Records and many musicians such as Gerry Marrotta wanted to record on the album. The deal went sour and Billy had 15 years of struggling in the industry. At the time Billy had been filming his life turning his story into a documentary. He was discovered by Rod Stewart, Doc McGhee and many others but never had the chance to receive a record deal. Billy decided to pull off an elaborate hoax in 1997 and film the escapade. He dyed his skin black and forged many crucial pieces of evidence and "supposed lost music" and called himself 'Jimmy Story" the long lost son of Jimi Hendrix. The press fell for the story and Billy soon ended up in News Papers, Magazines, breaking cover story's on the Television News, and even in International music magazines. After a year of this many were sure Billy had went crazy and thought he was talking out his anger and frustration on the industry. Billy had based his idea from watching the movie "Tootsie" with Dustin Hoffman and he knew if he could pull this off, his documentary film would have just the catchy hook his story needed. He was right. Independent filmmaker Kevin Smith / '"Clerks" "Dogma" "Chasing Amy" saw Billy's footage and recommended that John Pierson see it. John desribed in the Independent film world as the most powerful man in Indy films did a story about 'The making " of "Jimmy's Story" (now the name of Billy's film) and featured it on Bravo and The Independent Film Channel. He was then introduced to Dan Myrick the creators and producers of The Blair Witch Project who added their creative editing to Billy's film in 1998. Billy kept filming his life, his relationship, his dying father, and turned the musical journey into a more personal heart felt story. Jimmy's Story won awards at The Palm Beach Film Festival and won 4 awards at The Dahlonega International Film Festival in 2003. In 2004 Billy wrote, produced, directed his first feature film called "A Perfect Song". He also scored all the music tracks and played every instrument. The story of a man "Lloyd" who is obsessed about composing the worlds perfect song. The film will be premiered at The Delray Beach Film Festival. Billy continues to write and record music from home the same way he always has on his old 4- track recorder. He produces films and shorts, teaches acting and writes music for other known artist.
