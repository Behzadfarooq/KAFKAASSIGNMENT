Bill DeBlasio

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Bill de Blasio represents District 39 in the New York City Council, which comprises the Brooklyn neighborhoods of Borough Park, Carroll Gardens, Cobble Hill, Gowanus, Kensington, Park Slope, and Windsor Terrace.

Elected in 2001 and re-elected in 2003 and 2005, he had previously represented some of the same areas as a member of Community School Board 15. Councilman de Blasio chairs the Committee on General Welfare, and is a member of the Finance, Civil Rights, Environmental, and Civil Rights Committees.

Prior to his election, de Blasio had served as the campaign manager for Hillary Rodham Clinton's successful candidacy for a seat in the United States Senate in the year 2000. He had also served in the Clinton administration in the Department of Housing and Urban Development, as well as in the David N. Dinkins administration, and as a staffer for the New York City Council.

In 2005 de Blasio made a bid to succeed Gifford Miller as Speaker of the City Council, but eventually conceded to Christine Quinn from Hell's Kitchen, the current Speaker, after realizing that he did not have sufficient backing.

Councilman de Blasio holds a B.A. in Urban Studies from New York University, and an M.P.A. from Columbia University's School of International and Public Affairs.
