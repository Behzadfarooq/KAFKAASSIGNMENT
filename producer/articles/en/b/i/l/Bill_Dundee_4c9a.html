William Crookshanks (born October 24, 1943)[citation needed] is a professional wrestler better known as "Superstar" Bill Dundee.

[edit] Career

William Crookshanks grew up in the circus as a trapeze artist. He was born in Scotland and move to Australia at the age of 16. He started wrestling in Australia in 1967 and finally arrived in the United States as "Superstar" Bill Dundee in 1974.

Dundee made a name for himself in the Memphis, Tennessee area where he regularly teamed with Jerry Lawler and Jimmy Valiant for years. Dundee & Lawler ventured to the American Wrestling Association together in 1987 and captured the AWA World Tag Team Titles twice.

As a singles wrestler, he held the Southern Heavyweight Championship belt several times from roughly 1975-1985.

He also had a successful team with "Nature Boy" Buddy Landel that wreaked havoc in Tennessee.

Dundee had a brief run in the NWA's Jim Crockett Promotions, Central States Wrestling and Championship Wrestling from Florida in 1986 where he teamed with Jimmy Garvin and feuded with Sam Houston for the NWA Central States Heavyweight Championship. He also briefly managed The Barbarian and The MOD Squad while in those territories.

He also had a run in World Championship Wrestling in the early 1990s as Sir William, the manager for Lord Steven Regal.

Dundee is still active as of 2005 in Lawler's Memphis Wrestling where he has been a heel and a baby-face. He is the proprietor of "Bill Dundee's Club" a topless entertainment club located at Exit 126 on Interstate 40, near the Tennessee River in West Tennessee. He frequently appears on a talk show on the community-access cable channel in McKenzie, Tennessee.

Dundee has a son who wrestles as "JC Ice", Jamie Crookshanks.
