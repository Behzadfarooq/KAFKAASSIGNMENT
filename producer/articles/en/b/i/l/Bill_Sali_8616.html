William T. "Bill" Sali (born February 17, 1954) is the United States Representative from Idaho's 1st congressional district. The district includes the western third of the state, as well as most of Boise, the state capital. Sali previously served in the Idaho Legislature as a member of the Idaho House of Representatives.

[edit] Personal life

Sali was born in Portsmouth, Ohio in 1954, and moved to Idaho with his family in 1962. He graduated from Boise State University in 1981 and the University of Idaho law school in 1984.

Sali and his wife, Terry, reside in Kuna, Idaho. They have been married since 1976 and have six adult children, who were home-schooled. Prior to his career in politics, Sali was a professional musician, a farmer, and a Caterpillar machinery salesman. [1]

He was first elected to the Idaho House of Representatives in 1990.

[edit] Career in the State Legislature

During his tenure in the Idaho House of Representatives, Sali gained a reputation as a staunch conservative on social and economic issues, frequently drawing the ire of the Republican house leadership [2][3].

Sali has served as vice-chairman of the state House Health and Welfare Committee and as Chairman of the Special Committee on Health Care. He also sat on the Commerce, Industry and Tourism Committee, the Human Resources Committees, and the Judiciary, Rules, and Administration Committee.[4]

[edit] 2006 Congressional race

Main article: Idaho 1st congressional district election, 2006

On May 23, 2006, Sali won the Republican nomination in Idaho's 1st congressional district with 26 percent of the vote in a six-way race. [5] Several of his primary competitors did not endorse him in the general election [6][7], which was characterized by animosity towards Sali from these and other prominent state Republicans. Sali's general election opponent was Democrat Larry Grant. Speaker of the House Dennis Hastert, RNC Chair Ken Mehlman, and Vice President Dick Cheney made appearances on behalf of Sali in what became a surprisingly competitive race. Both Sali's primary campaign and general election campaign received substantial financial backing by the Club for Growth. The Republican National Committee and National Right to Life PAC also contributed heavily to his general election campaign.

Sali was elected to the United States Congress on November 7, 2006, defeating Grant 50% to 45%.

[edit] In The House of Representatives

Sali was elected president of the 2007-8 Republican freshman class. [8]

He opposed legislation to raise the minimum wage to $7.25 per hour, as well as a Republican move to recommit. In protest of the bill's passage he facetiously proposed the Obesity Reduction and Health Promotion Act, a law to reduce gravity by 10%. According to Sali, both moves would "change the rules that would otherwise affect the affairs of mankind." [9]
