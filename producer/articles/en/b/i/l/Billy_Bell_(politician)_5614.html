Billy Bell OBE JP (born 09 October 1935) is an Unionist politician from Northern Ireland.

He served as a Councillor on Belfast City Council from 1976 to 1985 and was Lord Mayor of Belfast from 1979 to 1980. He now serves on Lisburn Borough Council (1989-2007) and is a former Mayor of the borough of Lisburn, the only person to be mayor of two council areas in Northern Ireland.

He was Personal Assistant to Rt Hon Sir James Molyneaux M.P. (Lord Molyneaux of Killead) from 1976 to 1997.

He was elected to the Northern Ireland Constitutional Convention (1975-1976), and to the Northern Ireland Assembly for the Lagan Valley constituency in 1998 and again in 2003. Bell stood in the 2007 election after re-selection by his party, however he lost his seat to his fellow running mate, Basil McCrea.

He was appointed a Justice of the Peace in 1985, and is a former member of the Northern Ireland Housing Council

[edit] Trivia

He is a cousin of the physicist John Stewart Bell
