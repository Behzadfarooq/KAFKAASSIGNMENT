Billy Boyd (born 28 August 1969 in Glasgow) is a Scottish actor and musician most widely known for playing Peregrin Took (Pippin), in the film adaptations of The Lord of the Rings (2001-2003) and Barrett Bonden in Peter Weir's film Master and Commander: The Far Side of the World (2003).

[edit] Biography

He and his sister Margaret were raised by their grandmother after their parents died when he was 13. In 1984, aged 16, he appeared in local musical company Theatre Guild, Glasgow's production of "Hans Anderson".

Boyd at Ring*Con 2005 (link) in Fulda, Germany.

He worked as a book-binder for 7 years before pursuing an acting career. One of the books he bound was The Lord of the Rings.

He is a graduate of the Royal Scottish Academy of Music and Drama, where he earned a diploma in Dramatic Arts. He is a stage actor as well as a screen actor. He has been in several plays; recent ones include "San Diego" and "The Ballad Of Crazy Paola."

As well as being an actor, he can sing, and play the guitar, bass, and drums. He wrote and sang a song in Peter Jackson's The Lord of the Rings: The Return of the King called Edge of Night. He now is a member of the band Beecake.

He currently owns a house in Glasgow with his partner, Alison McKinnon. On 26 April 2006, he and McKinnon welcomed their first child, son Jack William Boyd.
