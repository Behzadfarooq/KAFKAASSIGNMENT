Bill Burns (artist)

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

For other people with the same name see Bill Burns (disambiguation).

Bill Burns (birthday unknown) is a Canadian artist.

Burns was born in Regina, Saskatchewan, and received his Bachelor of Fine Arts in 1980 from the University of Victoria in Victoria, British Columbia. He also attended Goldsmiths' College at University of London, England, and obtained a Masters of Fine Arts in 1987

[edit] Career

Burns' work is comprised mainly of interactive materials, and display or reimagining of everyday or mundane materials.

He is best known for his "Safety Gear for Small Animals" (SGSA) series, and the accompanying museum, where gear for animals and guidelines on how to assist them are displayed. [1] Using the conventions of both traditional museum display and print-media marketing, Burns exhibition spotlights miniscule safety vests, work gloves, bullet-proof vests, U.V. goggles and respirators, and acts as a commentary about human stewardship of the environment. Publications by SGSA include titles like "How to Help Animals Escape from Natural History" and "How to Help Animals Escape from Degraded Habitats".

Burns has also created other works with similar themes; one recent kit displayed at an exhibition was a collection of the items that a prisoner at Guantanamo Bay receives upon arrival. [2]

[edit] Awards and recognition

Burns is the recipient of awards from the Canada Council for the Arts, Ontario Arts Council, Toronto Arts Council and the Pollock-Krasner Foundation.
