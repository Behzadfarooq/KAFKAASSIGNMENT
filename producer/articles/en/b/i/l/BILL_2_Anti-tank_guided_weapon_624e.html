The BILL 2 Anti-Tank Guided Weapon is a guided anti-tank missile. It currently is in service with the Swedish Army

[edit] About the BILL 2

The BILL 2 was designed upon the original BILL that has been in the service of the Swedish Army since 1988. The BILL 2 is currently produced by Saab Bofors Dynamics, who are located in Karlskoga, Sweden. The BILL 2 comes with one 10.5kg missile, a launching tube, tripod with x7 magnification day sight, and one thermal imaging sight.

The BILL 2 uses OTA or Overfly Top Attack to attack its target. What this mean is that the missile flies on a standard horizontal trajectory, with the difference being that instead of directly hitting the target head on, it overflies it, detonating its warheads on top of an armored vehicle, where the armour is usually lighter. It also utilizes new guidance accuracy via the installed rate gyro, which monitors the tracking movement of the launcher. It is designed primarily to attack armored or unarmored vehicles, but can be used to attack helicopters or soft ground targets, such as light buildings.

[edit] The BILL 2 Missile

The BILL 2 Missile uses a SACLOS or semi-automatic command to line-of-sight guidance system, and is controlled via wire. The BILL 2 warhead has an optical and magnetic sensor. The optical sensor serves to find the targetâ€™s range, while the magnetic sensor detects metallic targets to determine the best point for the missile to detonate. The BILL 2 contains both a proximity fuse and an inertial impact fuse. The BILL 2 is armed with a double vertically striking shaped charge, which is a HE or high-explosive warhead. The front warhead destroys the reactive Armour and leaves the rear warhead to penetrate the Armour. The charges are shaped to direct all of the hot fragments to one point on the vehicle.

[edit] Firing Modes

The BILL 2 has four firing modes that the launcher's operator can select, before launching the missile.

[edit] Basic

In this mode the missile will use both of its sensors to hit the armored vehicle from the top.

[edit] Non-Armored

In this mode the missile uses no sensors and flies on a LOS or line-of-sight, while using the proximity fuse.

[edit] Soft Target

In this mode the optical sensor is used, but the magnetic sensor is not. The missile uses OTA or Overfly Top Attack to attack its target.

The BILL 2 can be mounted onto a variety of vehicles and then fired remotely from inside the vehicle.
