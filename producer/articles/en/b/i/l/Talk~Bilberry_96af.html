[edit] Where to buy bilberry?+

I am interested in growing bilberry, and was wondering where can I get the plants? Do they need some kind of special fertilizer? Lime?

It’s a good question – “Why doesn’t it work for others if it did for them?” First, I would give possibility that these stories may be true, because why would people lie about this? Then I would think that there could be complications as: amount, diet combination with the other food, degree of the eye problem, & maybe reactions of genes... etc. I believe it because my cousin vision improved of bilberry. We spent week in the forest eating bilberries. She told everyone that her vision improved, but real proof came when doctor prescribed her much lover glasses; change recorded on the paper by doc. Eating bilberries was the difference, but we ate a lot of it all day, and that is all that we ate.

Health effects are nice, but more than anything I would eat it because of the taste. I think it tastes better than any berry. Blueberry tastes like a sheet in comparison to bilberry. Bilberry is like biological candy.

You're not alone, but as far as I can tell, no-one has so far succeeded in cultivating proper bilberry (though not for lack of trying). And as you say, the cultivated species are pretty disappointing substitutes. So back into the woods it is, I guess. --Rallette 08:08, 9 May 2006 (UTC)
