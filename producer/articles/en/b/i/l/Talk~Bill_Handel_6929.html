[edit] Nobel Peace Prize nomination

  • On December 1, 2005, Handel announced that he was nominated for the Nobel Peace Prize by an unnamed member of the United States House of Representatives. Reasons provided for his nomination include, among other things: the promotion of racial harmony through his radio program. Handel's nomination is believed to be in direct response to the fact that Stanley Tookie Williams, one of the founders of the Los Angeles criminal gang "The Crips," is a multi Nobel Prize nominee and he sat on California's Death Row for multiple murders until his execution. Handel has stated he received the nomination in under 24 hours of asking for any political representative to nominate him. <ref Stewart, Jill. "The value of a Nobel nomination" San Francisco Chronicle, December 23, 2005

Being nominated for the Nobel Peace Prize is an honor, but it is not official or even prestigious. Any national legislator or about a third of the university professors in the world can make a nomination, and there have been as many as 140 some years. Nominators are requested to keep their nominations secret, so it's only those wishing publicity who make announcements, and more often it is impossible to verify. I see no reason to keep it. No offense to the subject, this is a general Nobel Peace Prize "nominees" issue. -Will Beback · † · 08:39, 21 December 2006 (UTC)

This is precisely the satirical point Handel was making, and therefore this point should be retained in his biog. Tookie Williams' supporters were touting his nomination as a sign of his redemption; Handel his own nomination to demonstrate that being nominated is easy and of no particular merit. User talk:Nikitin 27 Dec 2006
Wikipedia doesn't exist to help radio personalities make satirical points. When he actually wins the prize we'll be happy to include the reference. -Will Beback · † · 03:52, 12 January 2007 (UTC)

"We" beg to differ. The Nobel nomination is and example of the Handel schtick that has led to his popularity and commercial success. Handel doesn't need Wikipedia to make satirical points; but this example helps Wikipedia understand Handel. User talk:Nikitin 12 Jan 2007
