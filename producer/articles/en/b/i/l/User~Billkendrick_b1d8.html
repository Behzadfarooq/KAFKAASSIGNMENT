Bill Kendrick, born May 4th, 1975 in Novato, California. Visit Bill Kendrick's home page.

In case Wikipedians decide I'm article-worthy, you might still be able to find the Wikipedia article about me. I certainly do poke my nose in enough different communities (mostly Linux-, open source-, video game- and Atari 8-bit computer-related) that I'm a kind of minor celebrity in those communities.

My claims to fame include:

  • Bill's Games, a collection of web-based games that remains immensely popular with WebTV users, due to it's simple design.
  • New Breed Software, the monicker I use for open source video games for Linux, Windows, Mac OS X, and a plethora of other operating systems. Most notable to date is Tux Paint, a popular edutainment computer drawing program.
  • Involvement in many, many open source communities
    • GIMP graphics tool
    • Simple DirectMedia Layer multimedia library
    • Sharp Zaurus and Agenda VR3 Linux-based PDAs
    • Indrema vaporware video game console
    • The K Desktop Environment
    • A dozen or so Linux User Groups, including co-founding LUGOD in Davis, California
    • Regular contributions to DesktopLinux.com and LinuxDevices.com news websites
