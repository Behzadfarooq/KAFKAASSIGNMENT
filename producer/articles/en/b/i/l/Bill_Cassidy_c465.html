William Morgan "Bill" Cassidy, M.D. (born September 28, 1957) is a Louisiana State Senator, physician, teacher, community leader, and Republican Party activist. Cassidy specializes in the treatment of diseases of the liver, both in private practice and at the Earl K. Long state hospital. He is married to Laura Cassidy, herself a physician, and they have three children: Will, Meg, and Kate. They reside in Baton Rouge, Louisiana.

On December 9, 2006, Cassidy was elected State Senator for District 16 of the Louisiana Senate. In his first bid for public office, Cassidy defeated long time state representative William Daniel and Libertarian candidate S.B. Zaitoon to win the election. The special election was held to replace Jay Dardenne, who vacated the seat he had held since 1992 upon his election as Secretary of State. Cassidy was officially sworn in on December 20, 2006.
