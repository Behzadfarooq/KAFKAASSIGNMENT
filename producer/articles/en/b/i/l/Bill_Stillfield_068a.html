Colonel William "Bill" Stillfield was a character on the television situation comedy, Designing Women played by Douglas Barr.

Bill was a wealthy Air Force colonel who met the Sugarbaker Design Firm when he was looking to have them design a wing of the Veterans Hospital in the name of his late father. He was previously married to a woman named Nancy, who had died of cancer. A young widower, he had met and fell in love with Charlene Frazier. At first, they felt uncomfortable, but after a time, they settled into a wonderful relationship.

Charlene had met his widowed mother, and her sister, Phoebe. At first, Charlene felt totally out of place, because his family was wealthy and came from old Virginia money. However, Bill's mother grew to love Charlene, and even stood up to her snobby sister about her.

The wedding was beautiful and befitted a new military couple. On the honeymoon, they conceived and nine months later, gave birth to their only child, daughter, Olivia Frazier Stillfield, nicknamed Livvie.

Because of Bill's assignments, taking him all over the world, and also because of the secrecy of the missions, made Charlene very insecure. When Bill was called into active duty for Operation Desert Storm, she was nearly frazzled. However, they persevered and despite his absences, it did make the heart grow fonder.

When Bill was assigned to England, Charlene and Olivia accompanied him, leaving Atlanta behind.
