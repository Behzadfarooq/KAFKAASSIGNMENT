Bill, the links are still inappropriate, regardless of the fact that a single additional editor concurred and regardless of the fact that you consider them to be non-commercial. The reason is that you are adding links to promote your site. Doing so dilutes the value of Wikipedia. If your links are truly of value, other editors researching the topics of related articles on Wikipedia will themselves find your articles and add links to them. The fact that you are doing so yourself casts doubt on their value. I never add links to my websites to Wikipedia, because as the owner I have a vested interest in promoting them. If any of my sites are ever useful enough to belong in Wikipedia, they will already have attracted enough audience on their own to be well-known by other, uninvolved editors of any related Wikipedia articles.

If you would like to seek the input of the wider Wikipedia community on this issue, please ask on the Village Pump to see if the general community believes that adding your links would be appropriate. Jdavidb (talk • contribs) 15:03, 6 March 2006 (UTC)
