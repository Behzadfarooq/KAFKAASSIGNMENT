[edit] Attribution / Original Research

The lists of students do not appear to be included on the school's official website or in any other reliable source, so they must be removed per Wikipedia's attribution policy. For the students and others not accustomed to Wikipedia's policies, Wikipedia is strictly a secondary source, and original research is not permitted here. "Original research" includes anything that you know from personal involvement with a subject or from a published source that is not recognized as reliable. It's not prohibited because it's not true, or because it's not important, just because it hasn't been taken from a reliable published source. (Students, you might want to talk to your school administration about posting these lists on the school website.) --Butseriouslyfolks 05:25, 23 March 2007 (UTC)

[edit] Lists

Please don't make lists of everything. First of all, lists of student officers are not acceptable. Wikipedia is not a directory and does not include lists of officeholders or the like. There are several reasons not to and I direct you to this page to get a better idea of what Wikipedia includes. Please also consider this policy, or at least the thought behind it. People have a right to privacy that many here feel Wikipedia should be careful not to infringe needlessly. Second, lists of school subjects are just boring. Really, they are. The point of an article is to tell us how this school differs from others. Only those facts that express that should be included. "You can learn maths there" is not a salient fact in this context. If the principal had outlawed maths, well... Grace Note 07:28, 25 March 2007 (UTC)
