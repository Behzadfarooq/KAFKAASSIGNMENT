Bill Vicenzino

Assoc. Professor, Dr. Bill Vicenzino is a physiotherapy researcher and teacher at the University of Queensland's School of Health and Rehabilitation Sciences. He is a certified member of the Australian Physiotherapy Association.

As a researcher Vicenzino's passion is within the fields of pronation of the foot and aassociated problems arising with the condition, and Lateral Epicondyalgia, known more commonly as Tennis Elbow. Vicenzino has written and published many papers currently circulating the physiotherapy field. His findings and publishings have also helped professions outside that of physiotherapy, making him well regarded among other physical health professions such as Podiatry.

As a lecturer, Vicenzino specialises in teaching, and is the course co-ordinator at the University of Queensland in the Musculoskeletal Lower Limb subject. In Australian Physiotherapy courses Vicenzino's methods and treatments are taught as standard procedure. He introduced to the course teaching of the Vincenzino Manipulations, a series of physiotherapy manipulative techniques and was involved in the creation of the Souvlis model of Pain in neuromusular physiotherapy.

[edit] Specialites

  • Ankle and foot disorders (and associated lower limb disorders), and their relation to ankle pronation
  • Lateral Epicondylalgia
  • Vicenzino Manipulations
