Bill Joyce (born April 8, 1877 in Prestonpans) was a Scottish footballer who played as a forward.

Joyce started his career at Greenock Morton before moving to England and Bolton Wanderers in 1884, where he suffered a broken leg in 1896.

Joyce played for Tottenham, scoring 26 goals in 38 games, before joining Thames Ironworks for the 1999-1900 season (the club's last season before becoming West Ham United). He made 28 Southern League appearances for the club, scoring 11 goals, including three goals in a 5-1 away test match against Fulham on 30 April 1900. He also averaged a goal a game in seven FA Cup appearances that season.

Joyce went on to join Portsmouth as a replacement for Sandy Brown. A year later, he moved to Burton United and made 29 appearances over two seasons.
