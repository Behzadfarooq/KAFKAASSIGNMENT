Bill Blass

Bill Blass (1922–2002) was an American fashion designer, born in Fort Wayne, Indiana. He began his New York fashion career in 1946, joining Maurice Rentner Ltd. in 1959. He gained recognition, produced his own label, and in 1970 bought Rentner Ltd., renaming it Bill Blass Ltd. He is known for his highly priced, beautifully cut and tailored women's wear, notable for its inventive combinations of patterns and textures. Among his many honors were seven Coty Awards and the Fashion Institute of Technology's Lifetime Achievement Award (1999).

Bill Blass, born William Ralph Blass in 1922, grew up in Fort Wayne, Indiana. He was the son of a dressmaker and a traveling hardware salesman. His father committed suicide at the age of five, after Bill found refuge in the arts.

In his autobiography Blass wrote that most of the margins in his school books were not filled with notes, but with sketches of Hollywood-inspired fashions. At fifteen he began sewing and selling evening gowns for $25 each to a New York manufacturer. At 17 he had finally saved up enough money to move to Manhattan and study fashion. He excelled in his fashion studies immediately and at the young age of 18, was the first male to win Mademoiselle’s Design for Living award. He spent his salary of $30 a week on clothing, shoes, and elegant meals.

In 1942 Blass enlisted in the Army. He was assigned to the 603rd Camouflage Battalion, with a group of writers, artists, sound engineers, theatre technicians, and other creative professionals. Their mission was to fool the German Army into believing the Allies were positioned where they really weren’t. They did this by using recordings, dummy tanks, and other false materials. The US Camouflage Battalion proved to be more successful than the European Camouflage Battalion.

Blass’ designs are best known for being wearable. In a time when other designers were designing clothes which were known more for being a work of art, Blass was designing clothing which women could adorn themselves with day and night. He designed garments that could be worn by every woman, movie stars, first ladies, and the every day housewife. “He took American sportswear to its highest level…giving it a clean, modern, impeccable style… He, probably more than any designer knew his customer and understood her”. (Ellin Saltzman, in a NY Times review.)

After over two decades of success, Blass established Bill Blass Limited in 1970. At this point he was extremely successful in both male and female fashions. Over the next 30 years he expanded his line to include everything from swimwear to furs, luggage, perfume, and chocolate. The line was hugely successful. His fame and income grew over the years and by 1998 his company had grown to a $700-million-a-year business. In 1999 Blass sold Bill Blass Limited for $50 million and retired to his home in New Preston, Connecticut. In 2002 Blass passed away due to oral cancer.

Bill Blass Limited’s head designer is Michael Vollbracht, who began working with Blass in 1999. Prior to taking over as head designer, Vollbracht worked with famous designers such as Geoffrey Beene and Donald Brooks. He also worked with Henri Bandel at the New York Times as their in-house illustrator. He was the illustrator for Bloomingdales, where his ads were featured in Vogue and The New York Times. His signature illustration for a Bloomingdales Bag caused a sensation and his modernistic works became known worldwide.

He is a true renaissance man, as a designer, painter, author, and illustrator. His designs have been popular with the likes of Angelina Jolie, Oprah, and various First Ladies. His artwork is also featured in many private collections such as those of Former President Ford, Diane von Furstenburg, and Elizabeth Taylor.

The Blass 2007 fall collection showcased in New York Fashion week in February 2007 was a big hit. With famous models such as Jessica Stam donning his latest fall 2007 collection his newest designs will continue to be worn by the celebrities and the women of the world.

In February of 2007 Bill Blass Limited was acquired by NexCen Brands. Nexcen Brands announced on February 15, 2007 that they had completed a $54.6 million cash and stock acquisition of the company.
