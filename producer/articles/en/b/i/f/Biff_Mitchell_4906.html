Biff Mitchell (b. 1947 in Toronto, Ontario) is a novelist, satirist, and humorist living in Atlantic Canada.

Mitchell graduated from the University of New Brunswick with an honors degree in English Literature in 1974. After college, he returned to his hometown and worked at the University of Toronto bookstores on the Erindale Campus.

He returned to New Brunswick in 1975 and worked for the New Brunswick Department of Finance for five years where he wrote employee manuals, annual reports and computer documentation. After quitting his job, he traveled to Vancouver in 1981 and returned to New Brunswick at the end of the year.

For the next six years, he worked as a bartender/bouncer at a nightclub in Oromocto, New Brunswick. The character studies he made during these years formed most of the characters in his first novel, Heavy Load (a laundromance).

In 1987, he formed his own company, T.G. Mitchell Communications (mostly writing public relations material and computer documentation), and closed it two years later to work as a copywriter for Radio One (CIHI and CKHJ) in Fredericton.

In 1993, after winning a first place Atlantic Canada Radio Award (ACRA), he left Radio One to work for a computer-based training company, FirstClass Systems, which later became LearnStream. His experience with these two companies inspired his second novel, Team Player.

He left LearnStream in 2001 to work for another eLearning company, Engage Interactive. While he was at Engage, he finished his third novel, The War Bug.

Since 2005, Mitchell has worked at Virtual Expert Clinics as a public relations and marketing manager.

Other:

In 2005, Mitchell served as International Spokesperson for Read an eBook Week, during which time, he worked with John Hart (founder of Project Gutenberg) on a Brief History of Project Gutenberg, which can be downloaded free of charge at http://www.biffmitchell.com/eBook_Week/Gutenberg.pdf.

In 2006, Mitchell taught the first genre writing workshop at the Maritime Writers’ Workshop and Literary Festival (of which Mitchell is member of the board) at the University of New Brunswick. The workshop was on science fiction and cyberpunk. Mitchell was invited to teach a workshop in mystery writing the following year.

Mitchell maintains an active blog at http://biffmitchelldotblog.blogspot.com
