[edit] More Information Needed (History and Folklore)

I was thinking about things to add to a potential "history and folklore" section of this page. One of them I thought of was the story of a plane crash on the O'Connell football field, maybe about 20 years or so ago. I was told tha some kind of small aircraft like a cessna crashed into the football field behind O'Connell. If anyone can find any sources that corroborate this story or have anything to add, including other such stories, please let me know. --johnkellystyle 07:18, 29 September 2006 (UTC)

The health teachers are still talking about that one. Maybe also add in something about the school starting out divided by gender originally and how you weren't allowed to cross this one white line painted on campus. Not really appropriate for that section though. Outside Center 03:04, 25 October 2006 (UTC)
The gender division might be appropriate for the history part of that section, actually, so not totally off base. I never heard about the white line, but I can confirm, based on testimonials from family members who went to O'Connell in the 70s, that the genders were divided by building. As they explained, areas in the front (roughly from the cafeteria forward) were for one gender and from the cafeteria back was for another. I'm not sure which was which. Feel free to correct me on this one or clarify. --johnkellystyle 03:24, 26 October 2006 (UTC)

[edit] Controversy

It doesn't really seem as though that belongs here, as it deals with what two students did and how the school reacted to it in a normal way. There is no "controversy" that surrounds the school because of it.

Well, the issue was relevant school and the school's public image. The focal point of the controversy, and I think, what ultimately resulted in a fine was that Elliot essentially slandered the school. Whether or not you think the school administration's role was relevant, it was the most significant media exposure the school has had in a long time. So, given that fact, it deserves to be recognized.
If you think the heading "controversy" isn't appropriate, then that can become a point of discussion. In any case, there's no excuse to keep deleting the text. Nobody here is trying create a negative image of the school, just to state the facts and let history be written. If you object to that, then you'd best take your argument elsewhere. --johnkellystyle 06:24, 29 December 2006 (UTC)
That should work. It was the controversy part that made it seem like it didn't belong.
