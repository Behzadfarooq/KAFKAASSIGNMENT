Biscúter Pegasin
Manufacturer Autonacional
Production 1957–1960
Class Roadster, sports car
Body style FF cabriolet

Biscúter Pegasin (or 200-F) was a sports car from Autonacional. It was revealed in 1957 in an attempt to attract the wealthier buyers. It was more upscale then the very basic Biscúter and it had two-colour plastic body, hard- and soft-top. The styling was similar to the Pegaso Z-102. It was powered by a Hispano-Villiers, 197 cc two stroke, single cylinder 9 hp engine giving a top speed of 75 km/h. By the early 1960s, Biscúter sales and production stopped, after a total production run of about 12,000. It is thought that almost all of the cars were eventually scrapped.
