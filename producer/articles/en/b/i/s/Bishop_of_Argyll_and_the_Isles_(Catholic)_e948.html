Bishop of Argyll and the Isles (Catholic)

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

This article is about the Roman Catholic office. For the Episcopal office, see Bishop of Argyll and the Isles (Episcopalian)

The Bishop of Argyll and the Isles heads the Catholic Diocese of Argyll and the Isles in the Province of Saint Andrews and Edinburgh, in Scotland.

The diocese covers an area of 31,080 kmÂ² The see is in the town of Oban where the seat is located at the Cathedral Church of Saint Columba.

The diocese was erected on 5 March 1878. The current bishop is the Right Reverend Ian Murray, the 9th Bishop of Argyll and the Isles.
