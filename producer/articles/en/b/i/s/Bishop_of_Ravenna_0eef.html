This page is a list of Roman Catholic bishops and archbishops of Ravenna, and (from 1985[1]) of the Archdiocese of Ravenna-Cervia. The earlier ones were frequently tied to the Exarchate of Ravenna. (The city also became the centre of the Orthodox Church in Italy in 1995.)

[edit] Unknown period

  1. Ursus who, c.381, built the original basilica to the Resurrection of Our Lord (called Anastasis in the Byzantine period)
  2. Neon, Archbishop, 449-552
  3. Severus, 12th bishop
  4. Fusca and Maura
  5. Vitalis

[edit] Diocese of Ravenna (1st c - 5th c)

  1. Apollinaris, legendarily to 79, historically in the era of Septimius Severus
  2. ?
  3. ?
  4. Marcian (Italian - San Mariano) - died c 127 - feast day May 22
  5. Mauro, bishop, 642-61
  6. Reparatus (671-77), archbishop, granted privileges by Tiberius
  7. George of Ravenna, died 846

[edit] Archdiocese of Ravenna (5th century - 1947)

[edit] 5th century

  1. Joannes Angeloptes (430-33)
  2. St Peter Chrysologus (433-449)

[edit] 6th century

  1. Ecclesius, Archbishop, 526-34
  2. Ursicinus (535-39, who ordered the Basilica of Sant'Apollinare in Classe to be built).
  3. Maximianus (546 - Feb 21 566), after whom the Throne of Maximianus is named, 27th bishop
  4. Marianus, bishop (546-556)

[edit] 11th century

  • Wibert, fl. 1080
  • Anselm of Havelberg 1155-8
