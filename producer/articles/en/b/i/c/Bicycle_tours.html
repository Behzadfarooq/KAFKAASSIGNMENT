Bicycle tours

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

You have new messages (last change).

Bicycle tours are a type of group athletic activity involving vacations (holidays) on bicycles. Private companies located primarily in the United States and Europe organizes these type tours. Though there is a strong history of bicycle tours throughout Europe, many sources attribute the rise of bicycling in the United States to Lance Armstrong and his seven Tour de France wins.[1] Normally, on most private tours the participants ride their bicycles throughout the day and then stay at a bed-and-breakfast, inn, hotel or campground each night. The touring company provides lodging, most meals, directions and luggage transportation.

Prices start at $125 (US) per person per night and go up to $300 per person based on a survey of Internet websites in January 2007.[2] Though the quality of the tours seem to be consistent throughout all touring companies the smaller companies with less overhead seem to have a price advantage over the large competitors due to lack of overhead. Also, those companies with headquarters based in the Southeastern United States seem to have a substantial price advantage over those based in the Northeastern or Pacific Coast of the United States or in Europe based on Internet surveys in January 2007.[2]

[edit] Sources for tours
