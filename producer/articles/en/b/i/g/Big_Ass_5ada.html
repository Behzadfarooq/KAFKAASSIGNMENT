Big Ass is currently one of the most popular bands in Thailand. It was originally a pop rock band but the music moved toward hardcore punk in some of the band's recent works. Big Ass latest release is Begins, their fifth album.

[edit] History

Big Ass attracted a rather limited group of audience during their first three albums. It left the old music label, Music Bugs, to join Genie Records label of GMM Grammy. Their fourth album Seven (signifying seven years in the music scene) is very successful in terms of awards and popularity. The album was recognized by both major music awards in Thailand: Season Awards, and Fat Awards [1] (See Awards section below). Popularity-wise, they were also voted the most popular artist by fellow artists in Fat Awards #3 and nominated for the most popular group in 4th Channel [V] Thailand Music Video Awards.

[edit] In the news

"Dax" Ekarat Wongcharat, the leading vocal of Big Ass, made the headline when a 17 years old men's magazine model nicknamed "Fai" appeared with her baby "Justin" and claimed that Dax is the baby's father on February 2006. Allegedly they had sex when Fai was 16 years old, and this meant that Dax would be a child sex offender if the accusation was true. Dax was subsequently arrested and released on bail on 27 February. While Dax gave his blood sample for DNA testing in March, Fai and Justin did not give theirs until early May. On 10 May 2006 police announced that the result was negative. A retest performed by Khunying Pornthip Rojanasunand drew the same conclusion. Even through he is not the child's father, the trial for underage sex offense remained. The trial begun on 13 February 2007 and the court decision was announced on 21 March 2007. He received two years of suspended sentence and must produced two anti-drug songs as pledged by the singer in exchange for a light sentence.[5][6]
