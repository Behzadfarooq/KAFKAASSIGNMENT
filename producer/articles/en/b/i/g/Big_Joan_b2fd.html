Big Joan

Big Joan are a four piece rock band based in England. They formed in Southampton in 1999 and moved to Bristol later that year. Their distinctive, bass-heavy, percussive style of music is influenced by a wide range of musical genres from alternative rock to jungle, breakcore and industrial. Because of this they are as likely to be seen playing at free parties or rave and breakcore-orientated club nights as they are at venues on the indie circuit.

Big Joan are strong believers in the DIY Ethic. Though they have released material on other independent labels, the main outlet for their music is their own Blood Red Sounds imprint which has also released music by bands such as Mooz, Geisha and Hunting Lodge. They are also self-managed and self-promoted.

Big Joan are not prolific. In seven years they have only released one album and two EPs. Their most recent release, The Flood EP, is a collaboration between the band and some of their friends and heroes; namely Parasite, My Ambulance Is On Fire, Shitmat, Terry Edwards,Twocsinak and Bronnt Industrial Kapital.

Big Joan's German singer has been known as Annette Berlin since she was mistakenly credited under that name on a record by the group Madnomad. She was also a member of Male, a female rock duo who split up in late 2006. The other three quarters of Big Joan are English. Adam (guitar) and Simon (bass) were founding members of Bristol's CHOKE collective; Adam as the editor of its magazine and Simon as the main organiser of its monthly live music club.

Simon has also played with Steveless and Shooting At Unarmed Men while Adam is an occasional member of The Satanic Funeral Aftershow Party Band and Keith (drums) has played with outfits as diverse as Hookers For Jesus and The Fabrics.

Big Joan are currently arranging songs for a new album which they plan to start recording in June 2007.
