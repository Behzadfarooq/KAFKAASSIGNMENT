Big Shiny Tunes is a series of rock albums featuring Various Artists compiled and released by the Canadian television station MuchMusic, beginning in 1996. It has been cited as the best-selling album series in Canadian history,[1][2] with 5 million albums sold in ten years.[3]

[edit] Characteristics

The Big Shiny Tunes albums are each thought to be comprised of a combination of rock songs that are major hits by well-known artists, less successful songs by well-known artists and songs by more obscure artists, released in the same year as the albums' publication.[4] There are a combination of Canadian, British and American performers. One common feature among the selected artists is that they are attached to Universal Music, Warner Bros., EMI Music Canada, and/or MuchMusic, who come together to produce Big Shiny Tunes albums.[1]

Part of the appeal of the series is that those who do not usually buy albums will find Big Shiny Tunes provides several well-known songs, as opposed to a CD by a single artist whose work is mostly unheard and may not be as likable.[1] The music is aimed at teenagers and young adults.[5]

In addition to the ordinary albums in the series, MuchMusic also released albums such as Big Shiny 80's (2005) that included classics like Billy Idol's "White Wedding." There have also been two volumes of Big Shiny 90's released.

[edit] History

The album Big Shiny Tunes was released in 1996. A new edition has since been released every year, with Big Shiny Tunes 2 being particularly successful (the second and third albums alone account for 2,000,000 of the CDs and tapes sold under the brand)[6]. Big Shiny Tunes 2 had been accompanied by heavy advertising.[7] The next year, MuchMusic assembled Big Shiny Tunes 3 by having its audience vote on which songs they would like to have included, a novel process.[8] The success of the series continued as Big Shiny Tunes 6 sold 68,500 copies in its first week in 2001, more than any other album in Canada that week.[9]

However, the series would soon lose its predominance with the growth in popularity of rap music, as the next year Big Shiny Tunes 7 was outsold in its first week in Canada by Eminem's soundtrack to the film 8 Mile. Still, Big Shiny Tunes 7 sold 25,100 copies that week.[9] Big Shiny Tunes 8, released in 2003, would also make the best-seller list in Canada.[10] The latest album in the series, Big Shiny Tunes 11, was released on November 7, 2006. It made a bestseller list, but it was noted in the press that it was not as popular as past albums.[11]
