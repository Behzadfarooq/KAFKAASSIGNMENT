Space Pirates Discussion ==

There are three rules for articles on Wikipedia:

  • WP:V (Verifiability) - The information should be published by reliable sources so that if another editor has a question they can go and confirm it. This is the basis for the notability guidelines.
  • WP:NOR (No original research) - My interpretation of this overlaps a lot with verifiability. This also is the basis for WP:NFT (Wikipedia is not for things made up in school one day).
  • WP:NPOV (Neutral point of view) - We need to present both sides of an argument. This is the basis for our guideline on autobiographical material (don't start/contribute to articles about yourself or your creations).

There is a lot of overlap between these. They are also for material in the main space (i.e. Space Pirates) not the User space, where the information now appears. My advice if you want to try to keep this material is to move it to the Space Pirates page, and include links to third party coverage (websites, media, etc). Try to make it sound 'encyclopedic' - like a newspaper article about why Space Pirates is different and remove first person pronouns and requests for support.

I haven't gone out and looked at your myspace page, but it seems like the content that you've posted here would make a lot more sense somewhere like myspace or geocities, and you could include a link to that page from the article here.

The underlying problem is that Wikipedia is not a medium for advertising new things, it is a place for information about reasonably well-known things. When Space Pirates there will be fans coming out from all over to create an article for it here and there will be more leniency about keeping it, but until it is well known, the community is leery of it.

There are bands who started this week. The first thing they did was to create a myspace page and a Wikipedia article. If they break up next week, myspace doesn't care. Their myspace page will still be there 5 or 10 years from now. Wikipedia would rather not have their article today because we're confident that when they go on their first national tour in 5 years, they'll be notable enough to meet the notability guidelines for a Wikipedia page, and any verifiable information from this weekend will still be available then.

I hope that this helps you understand. If you want to keep this as a User page you can participate in the discussion, just go to Wikipedia:Miscellany for deletion/User:Space Pirates click the edit button at the top, add a bullet (*) to the last empty line at the bottom and present your case. ~ BigrTex 06:11, 13 January 2007 (UTC)
