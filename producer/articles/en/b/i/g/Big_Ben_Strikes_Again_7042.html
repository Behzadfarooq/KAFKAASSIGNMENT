"Big Ben Strikes Again" is the third episode of the Supermarionation television series Captain Scarlet and the Mysterons. Its original UK air date was 13 October 1967 on ATV Midlands. It was written by Tony Barwick and directed by Brian Burgess.

[edit] Synopsis

Spoiler warning: Plot and/or ending details follow.

The Mysterons threaten to destroy London and a transporter carrying an atomic device goes missing in the city. Facing a race against time to locate the bomb, Spectrum must rely on the eyewitness account of the truck driver, left unconscious in a side street. To their amazement, he swears that he heard Big Ben strike thirteen…

[edit] Plot

At night, a transporter is carrying an atomic device through the deserted streets of London. As the driver, Macey, makes a routine call to the police escort, Captain Black watches in the shadows as the convoy passes a street corner. Suddenly, the transporter goes out of control, forcing the police car in front off the road. Macey is powerless to act while an unseen force operates the controls, and the police car behind is pushed into a wall. Following a reckless drive through the London backstreets, the transporter surges down a ramp into the underground Park-Vu car park. As the vehicle brakes sharply and Macey is knocked unconscious, sliding doors slam shut, while police in the street outside report that the transporter has vanished.

In a transmission to Earth, the Mysterons warn that they will destroy London. Two hours after the disappearance, Colonel White briefs his personnel in the Cloudbase control room. The transporter’s lead lining makes it impossible to locate it by searching for radiation, and the trigger mechanism is timed for twelve hours. White instigates a Red Alert and the Angel craft are launched.

In the underground car park, Macey regains consciousness shortly before midnight. With the radio link dead, he turns on a music programme and ventures outside to inspect his surroundings, but his banging on the car park door is futile. A radio announcer ends the programme and Big Ben sounds midnight, but Macey is astonished to hear thirteen chimes. Suddenly, the hold of the transporter opens, and the trigger dials on the atomic device turn themselves. Macey tries to stop the arming, but is struck by the invisible force and knocked out. With the procedure complete, the bomb’s twelve-hour countdown commences.

Some time later, Captain Scarlet stumbles across the injured Macey while searching a side street in a Spectrum Saloon. Brought to Cloudbase, Macey describes his experience to help Spectrum locate the transporter. With three hours to go before the bomb’s detonation, Scarlet and Captain Blue comb the audio recording for leads, but there are over 2,000 London car parks matching Macey’s description and a test on his clothing has not yielded clues. However, Blue brings attention to the driver’s claim that Big Ben struck thirteen and deduces that the mystery car park must be within 1,500 yards of the tower. Programming a circle, Lieutenant Green reports that the Jupiter Way and Park-Vu car parks match the description and are inside that radius. Scarlet is puzzled, but with less than an hour remaining, he and Blue immediately fly to London.

While driving to Park-Vu in an SPV, Scarlet and Blue receive a call from Captain Ochre, who reports that the Jupiter Way car park is clear. Scarlet destroys the Park-Vu door with the SPV’s cannon and they soon find the transporter, but the bomb will take too long to defuse and must be taken to an excavation at a construction site to be safely allowed to detonate.

While Blue leads in the SPV, Scarlet drives the transporter and they arrive at the construction site after small delays. With time running out, Blue lowers Scarlet two miles to the excavation in a huge lift. Scarlet drives the transporter out and quickly returns to the shaft, but the lift is still caught in the atomic explosion on its ascent and the captain is fatally injured.

That night, Blue, Destiny, Melody and the recovered Scarlet dine at a restaurant overlooking the River Thames. Scarlet is still confused as to how Blue located the car park. Blue announces that he has been waiting for midnight and turns on their table’s radio, instructing Scarlet to count Big Ben’s tolls while the actual clock rings a mile away from them. Scarlet reaches twelve, but a thirteenth is then heard. Blue explains that the distance between them and Big Ben meant that the sound waves live from the tower were reaching them one chime after those heard on the radio, and that the thirteenth came directly from Big Ben. Enlightened, Scarlet declares that he will make thirteen his lucky number.
