A Big Mac is a hamburger from the fast food chain, McDonald's.

Big Mac may also mean:

  • Big Mac, a character from the children's series TUGS
  • The title of a third season episode of M*A*S*H, in this case, "Big Mac" refers to General Douglas MacArthur
  • A nickname for Mark McGwire, a former Major League Baseball player for the Oakland Athletics and St. Louis Cardinals
  • Big Mac (supercomputer), a supercomputer created in 2003 by Virginia Tech
  • Macintosh 512K, an early Apple Macintosh computer with 512K of RAM, as opposed to the original model's 128K. ("Fat Mac" was more common.)
  • Big Mac (Musician)
  • Big MAC, the term given to an ethernet adapter used in Sun Microsystems machines. The interface name is shown as be when one views the interfaces via the ifconfig command
  • Big Mac (computer game), a computer game series on the Commodore 64 platform
  • Big Mac, a character from the fictional McDonaldland world, created as part of the McDonald's advertising campaign
  • A nickname for a man who fought Kimbo Slice
  • A nickname for Michigan's Mackinac Bridge
  • A nickname for the Municipal Assistance Corporation, created by the State of New York in 1975 to deal with New York City's fiscal crisis; for example, in the phrase "Big Mac bonds" [1]

Big Mac may also refer to:

  • Big Mac index, an economics term
