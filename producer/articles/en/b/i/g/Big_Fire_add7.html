Big Fire is the name of an illuminati style organization in the series Giant Robo, which has appeared in Manga, Anime, and Tokusatsu series, even appearing in the Super Robot Wars Video Game. Pretty much in all appearances Big Fire is a secret criminal orginization with dreams of world domination.

In the original manga and tokusatsu series Big Fire is run by Emperor Guillotine an alien with designs on conquering the earth. His human henchmen make up the bulk of Big Fires forces. In the English version Johnny Sokko and his Flying Robot they are called "The Gargoyle Gang", however you can spot their insignia badges that have the letters BF over a flame.

In the 90's OVA series by Yasuhiro Imagawa they are a shadowy group trying to control the world, often referred to as a terrorist or criminal organization. Big Fire is controlled by the Magnificent Ten, a group of 10 leaders: all powerful warriors with special powers. The actual leader is Big Fire, a young male with almost godlike powers. He is never shown to speak in the Anime, but his mere presence seems to strike fear in the hearts of members of the Magnificent Ten. His plans are revealed through Komei, a master strategist. There seems to be some tension between the Magnificent Ten members and Komei, as they wonder if he is truly acting under the will of Big Fire or advancing his own agenda.

The characters that make up Big Fire are drawn together from many of Mitsuteru Yokoyama's works. Big Fire himself seems to be Babel II, Komei is linked to characters from Romance of the Three Kingdoms which Yokoyama did a manga version of.

Big Fire has also been used by a group of Anime fans throwing themed parties at Anime cons across the United States. This group has thrown a series of parties under different names including; Big Fire Bar B Q, World Domination Social Engagement, Ultralounge, Speakeasy, Big Fire Poker, and many others. They have been linked to other shady groups including Punk Rock Anime, Drink Club, Anime54, and the 3headed Monster. The parties are attended by many of the guests, and industry people who regularly attend anime cons.

The stories of these parties oftentimes grow out of control and separating the truth from fiction is not always an easy task. The fact that most people's memories of the parties are fuzzy at best never helps. All though everything you hear may not be all true, often there is some truth to the stories told. The two shadowy figures that are seen running these events go by the names of Big Dave and Ultra Bob, but who they really are remains a mystery.
