Big Brother Australia in 2005

Big Brother Australia 2005 was the fifth series of Big Brother Australia, and was aired on Network Ten in Australia, and TV-2 in New Zealand with a four-week delay. The series started on May 8, 2005, with housemates going into the House the day before, and finished on August 15, 2005, lasting 101 days. The theme of the series was "single, sexy, and competitive".

Auditions for housemates were held in March 2005. In a departure from usual procedure, candidates were not required to send in videos of themselves as had been the case for prior auditions. Instead, the producers toured major Australian mainland cities and conducted interviews. They searched for sexy singles that were willing to have sexual relations on camera. Promos for the show suggested that Big Brother would be different this year, and phrases such as "Assume Nothing, Expect Anything", "Let's Play", and "Think Again" were heavily used throughout the series, especially during Opening Night.

[edit] Changes and additions

A BB05 Promo

[edit] Fines

Main article: Big Brother Australia#Fines

On Opening Night, it was revealed that $5,000 Australian dollars would be subtracted from the $1,000,000 prize money every time the housemates broke a house rule. Such house rules included discussing nominations, which must remain secret; not giving adequate reasons for nominating; and forgetting to wear microphones at all times. Michelle was known for being the housemate to get the most such fines: $65,000.

[edit] The Logans

It was also revealed that twins would be entering the house, regularly changing places without being informed of what the other twin had done while in the house. Greg and David entered the house as a single person named Logan (their shared middle name). Their task was to remain undetected for two weeks while swapping places at Big Brother's command.

The secret of the twins was discovered one week into the series by housemate Glenn, and this was revealed during Lies Exposed. As a result, both Logans were able to continue living in the house, but would be treated as a single person in matters such as evictions and nominations.

Late in the series a special eviction was held where one twin was evicted based on the votes of the other housemates, and the remaining twin allowed to continue in the house as a normal housemate. During this eviction process David was evicted. However, because Greg was the last housemate to be evicted, both David and Greg shared the prize equally.

[edit] Disqualification

Almost immediately upon entering the house, two housemates, Constance and Nelson openly admitted they were in relationships. A third housemate, Dean, had been confronted by Big Brother about the same matter just hours before entering the House, and he admitted that he was in a relationship. For this series, only single housemates had been sought by the show's producers, and these contestants had lied during the audition process in order to still be eligible for the show; going as far as taking their partners into lockdown with them, telling producers that they were friends. These housemates reasoned that once the show began airing there was no need to continue the pretense as there was nothing Big Brother could do about it now that they were already in the house (and the TV show was underway).

One week into the series the situation was addressed in the show. The producers announced that the threesome had broken the terms of their contract. They were sent to the stripped down Rewards Room before Big Brother allowed them back into the house. Unknown to the housemates, the three were nominated for eviction and the Australian public selected two housemates to be evicted. Constance and Nelson were evicted during Viewers Verdict, while Dean remained in the house and refuted Big Brother's assessment of his wrongdoing. The following week Dean was again nominated for eviction for this infraction, but again survived the public vote and someone else was evicted.

Big Brother decided that neither Constance nor Nelson should have prizes, which was disappointing for Nelson in particular because he said he only wanted to be in Big Brother for the prizes; especially the car - a prize that had been typically awarded to evictees of the previous year.

Constance broke up with her boyfriend a week after her disqualification.

When Dean was ultimately evicted many weeks later (after being nominated for eviction during the usual nomination process), the problem was again addressed, and after the prizes he was eligible for were presented there was some question in the Live Eviction show as to whether he would receive them. Gretel Killeen first asked the crowd for their opinion on whether Dean should receive the prizes. After a mixed reaction, Gretel received instructions through her earpiece and then announced that Big Brother had decided that as he had survived in the house until after the Intruders had arrived, he had earned the position of housemate, so would receive the prizes.

[edit] Housemates

[edit] Angela

Angela Aiken was evicted on Day 15.

[edit] Christie

Christie Mills currently resides on the Gold Coast, Queensland. She used to live in Sydney. In 2006, she appeared in Zoo magazine with fellow housemate, Michelle.

[edit] Constance

Constance Hall, from Melbourne, Victoria, was disqualified on Day 10. She broke up with her boyfriend just days later.

[edit] Dean

Dean Glucina, originally from Canberra, now lives in Melbourne. While in the House he was nicknamed "Tina".

[edit] Geneva

Geneva Loader lives on the Northern Beaches of Sydney, and once attended Collaroy Plateau Primary School. She revealed that she was bisexual whilst in the house.

[edit] Gianna

Jane Pattison, known as Gianna in the House, from South Australia. Appeared in Ralph magazine following her eviction. In late 2005, her Adelaide home suffered flooding and she was interviewed by Ten News.

[edit] Glenn

Glenn Dallinger is a sheep shearer from Stawell, Victoria. Know affectionately inside the house as "Shearer".

[edit] Heath

Heath Tournier was an electrician from Grovedale, Victoria. Heath entered the House on Day 53 as an Intruder.

[edit] Hotdogs

Simon Deering, self-nicknamed Hotdogs, was a 27 year old real estate agent from Western Australia at the time of entering the House. He was evicted on Day 64. The day after Big Brother 2005 finished, Hotdogs was host of new Network Ten show The Up Late Game Show from 2005 - 2006. In July 2005, he was arrested in Cairns for committing an indecent act. He currently lives with former housemate Vesna.

[edit] Kate

Richelle Benson, better known in the House as Kate (born May 1983), is an administration worker from Perth, Western Australia.

[edit] Logan David & Logan Greg

'Twins David Mathew' and Greg Mathew, known as Logan David and Logan Greg respectively while in the house, were treated as a single entity in Nominations and Evictions, Friday Night Live, and potential winner of Big Brother 2005. Logan was their shared middle name. He was also joint AU$836,000 first prize winner with Greg . They trekked to the North Pole soon after their stay in the Big Brother house.

[edit] Melanie

Melanie Smerdon entered the House as an Intruder on Day 53.

[edit] Michael

Michael Farnsworth was a 27 year old demolition man from New South Wales when he entered the house. He was probably most notable for his nose ring.

[edit] Michelle

Michelle Carew-Gibson was born May 11, 1980 and was a 26 year old topless barmaid from Western Australia. She lives in Queensland. Michelle completed her high school studies at Belridge Senior High School.

Michelle's officially disclosed occupation to producers prior to appearing on the show was that of a promotional model for Queensland based firm Pin-Up Promotions under the stage name of Harley.

Michelle had appeared nude at least once in an edition of Australian-based softcore men's magazine Platinum Girls.[1]. She was evicted on Day 36. After eviction, she went on to perform photo shoots for a number of men's magazines including a full shoot for Platinum Girls.

[edit] Nelson

Nelson Russell was a 23 year old assistant manager at the time of entering the House. During his short stay, Nelson received a strike for calling Big Brother a Jew. Nelson was disqualified on Day 10 for lying about his relationship status.

[edit] Rachael Burns

Rachael Burns entered the House on Day 25 during Housemates: Incoming! as replacement housemate.

Rachael has recently completed a 1yr part time acting course, at the Actors College of Theatre and Television receiving a Certificate III in Theatre Performances and Practices. Rachael has also completed a presenting and producing course with Sydney Academy of Film and Television, Also producing and presenting a segment for EWTV which was aired on channel 183 Foxtel in 2006. Rachael also played a small role in "SHAPE" a horror film which will be released in Australia in 2007.

[edit] Rita

Rita Lazzarotto was a 29 year old nurse when she entered on Day 53 as an Intruder.

After the removal of two housemates from the Big Brother Australia 2006 House due to alleged sexual assault, Lazzarotto reported that she had been subjected to a similar incident during her time in the Big Brother house.[2]

[edit] Tim

Tim Brunero (born 1976) is a journalist from Sydney, New South Wales. He entered the house on Day 0 and ended up as a runner-up. Now writing a book on his Big Brother experience and publishing magazine and newspaper articles. Brunero also writes for the ABC's show The Chaser.

[edit] Vesna

Vesna Tofevski (born January 1977) is a hairdresser from Melbourne, Victoria of Macedonian descent. She entered the house on Day 25 with Rachel Burns as a replacement housemate. She started a relationship with fellow housemate Hotdogs; and in June 2006, he announced on Big Brother UpLate that they had sinced moved in together in a multi-million dollar mansion in Brisbane.

[edit] Weekly summary and highlights

[edit] Week 1

  • On Day 1, housemates had to, one by one, go to the Diary Room and nominate which housemate wouldn't receive their suitcase for the duration of their stay in the house. Constance received the most nominations, so had to take her suitcase to the Diary Room, never seeing it again for the duration of her stay; while at the same time providing the rest of the housemates with the keys to their suitcases.
  • On Day 2, the housemates were given a quiz.
  • Also on Day 2, the Logans swapped places for the first time, during a special show named Live Surprise.
  • On Day 5, housemates were given their first task: to see how well they knew each other. They wager 50% of their budget, and passed the task.
  • Housemates found out that they have passed their task, and receive ice-cream.
  • On Day 6, Glenn won the first ever Friday Night Live, and invited Logan into the Rewards Room.
  • On Day 8, Big Brother told the housemates that they were constantly told during the pre-production process, that should their relationship status change during the auditions process, that they should tell Big Brother immediately. Because Constance, Dean, and Nelson didn't do this, and lied to get into the house, they were all "evicted" to the Rewards Room. There, Big Brother told them what they have done wrong: lying about being single to get into the house.

[edit] Week 2

  • Nominations took place on Day 9. The housemates in the eviction line-up are Geneva, Michelle, and Tim. After Glenn's Three Point Twist, the eviction line-up changes to Geneva, Tim, and Angela.
  • The three housemates previously sent to the Punishment Room were allowed back into the House.
  • Constance and Nelson were disqualified by public vote on Day 10 in a full-scale eviction. They were each told during their eviction interview that they wouldn't receive any prizes.
  • Kate's birthday was on Day 10.
  • On Day 11, the housemates were told of their new task: to build the perfect housemate. They each had to have a part of their body moulded, they were then all put together to make a person. The housemates bet 100% of their budget on their task.
  • Big Brother announced that the housemates passed the task on Day 12.
  • Day 12 was Friday Night Live. Gianna won Friday Night Live, and took Christie to the Rewards Room.
  • Angela was evicted on Day 14.

[edit] Week 3

  • Geneva left a shopping bag in the supermarket on Day 15
  • A new task was announced on Day 17. A packing machine was set up in the garden, with conveyor belts. Housemates had to be on guard 24/7 because goods would be passed through the machine at any time. It was the housemates' job to pack all items into crates. On a majority vote, housemates wagered 75% of their budget.
  • On Day 18, housemates showed their disgust of Dean's reluctance to clean the house; he was appointed janitor by Gianna.
  • Big Brother announced on Day 19 that the housemates had failed their task, leaving them with only $48 to spend.
  • Big Brother banned all housemates from using the spa until Dean cleaned the house.
  • Christie got very drunk on Day 20, and was locked in the Airlock by Big Brother.
  • Gianna was evicted on Day 22.

[edit] Week 4

  • On Day 23, the housemates were given a Tango task. They all had to learn how to tango, and could use a dance mat provided by Big Brother. They also had to learn using dummies. They wagered their full budget on the task.
  • Housemates up for eviction were Dean, Michael, Christie, and Glenn. Michelle used her Three Point Twist on Glenn, but the line-up didn't change.
  • Big Brother informed the housemates that because of the fines they had incurred during the previous week, their $48 shopping budget had been reduced by $50, $5 for each fine incurred. Because of this, the housemates owed Big Brother $2, and could only take staples from the shop. Dean was able to retrieve the bag left there the week before.
  • Kate was given an Insider mission on Day 24. She was told that she had to sabotage the Tango task by removing at least three heads from three dummies, and hide them without being found out. She was given five days to complete her mission, and was told that she might be tailed. Tim was later given the task of discovering that Kate was trying to sabotage the task.
  • On Day 25, Kate managed to remove and hide some heads. The housemates suspected Tim of doing it.
  • Intruders Rachael and Vesna entered the house on Day 25 during Housemates: Incoming!.
  • Rachael won Day 27's Friday Night Live, and took Tim to the Rewards Room.
  • On Day 28, it was announced that the housemates had failed their Tango task, but because Kate accomplished hers, and Tim failed his, the group passed the task.
  • Michael was evicted on Day 29.

[edit] Week 5

  • On Day 31, the housemates were given a Housekeeping task. Housemates had to pair up as a chef and a waiter. The housemates wagered their full shopping budget again.
  • Because housemates didn't always wash their hands during the task, and one housemate picked their nose while making spaghetti, Big Brother announced on Day 32 that the housemates failed their task.
  • Hotdogs received a video message.
  • Glenn won Day 34's Friday Night Live, and took Michelle into the Rewards Room.
  • Michelle was evicted on Day 36.

[edit] Week 6

  • The housemates were given a Calendar Task on Day 37. When the housemates heard a camera shutter, they had to strike a pose and hold it, as though for a calendar. They wagered their full shopping budget.
  • Day 38's dinner was confiscated after Dean broke the rules.
  • The housemates found out that they passed their task on Day 40. Big Brother threw a party in celebration of their passing the task, that went into Day 41. There was a lot of alcohol, and Hotdogs got considerably drunk. He refused to clean up, and upset Geneva, Vesna, and Glenn. On Day 42, he apologised for his behaviour. He received a strike from Big Brother on Day 43.
  • Rachael was evicted on Day 43.

[edit] Week 7

  • On Day 44, Big Brother introduced the Masters and Slaves task, where half of the housemates are allocated slaves to one other housemate each. The masters were not allowed to say please, thank you or sorry to any of the slaves. They had hot water available to them whenever they wanted, but slaves had to shower outside and do laundry.
  • Housemates up for eviction were Geneva, Hotdogs, and Vesna. Dean used his Three Point Twist on Geneva, and added himself to the line-up.
  • The Masters were given new slaves on Day 46. Dave became Greg's slave.
  • On Day 47, the Masters became slaves, and the new Masters got to choose whoever they wanted as their slave.
  • Geneva was evicted on Day 50. The girls expressed disappointment in the fact that Dean didn't get evicted.

[edit] Week 8

  • On Day 51, Big Brother set a Schoolyard Task, where housemates had to act as though they were back at school and form a band. They weren't allowed to swear from breakfast time up to the end of school. Big Brother provided the housemates with school uniforms. The housemates wagered their whole budget on the task.
  • New Intruders Heath, Melanie, and Rita, entered the House on Day 53.
  • Glenn was evicted on Day 57.

[edit] Week 9

  • Heath was given a strike on Day 57, for his abusive behaviour on Day 56.
  • Heath was evicted on Day 59 in Intruder Eviction.
  • The Logans won Friday Night Live on Day 62, and they took Dean to the Rewards Room.
  • Hotdogs was evicted on Day 64.

[edit] Week 10

  • On Day 65, it was announced that Kate, Logan, and Rita were up for eviction. The Logans used their Three Point Twist on themselves, changing the final line-up to Dean, Kate, Rita, and Tim.
  • The housemates were given a tapdancing task on Day 66. They had to learn how to tapdance.
  • Christie won Day 69's Friday Night Live, and took the Logans to the Rewards Room.
  • On Day 70, the housemates had to give a final performance, that decided whether or not they passed their test. Big Brother later announced that he was impressed by the performance, therefore the housemates have passed their task.
  • Dean was evicted on Day 71.

[edit] Week 11

  • Big Brother told the housemates on Day 72 that they had to form the "Big Brother Brigade" as part of their Survival task. The housemates decided to wager 100% of their budget.
  • All housemates were up for eviction this week, but Big Brother gave the Logans the opportunity to save themselves, as the winners of Friday Night Live. They were given two boxes, and were told to pick one. They picked the box that means they are nominated, but they wouldn't find this out until Sunday.
  • David was evicted in a Surprise Twin Eviction on Day 74.
  • Big Brother told the housemates on Day 75 that they would have passed their task if they didn't swear so much, and if they didn't shout instructions to the other housemates when they were to do something without help.
  • Tim won Day 76's Friday Night Live, and he invited Vesna to the Rewards Room.
  • As part of his prize, Tim won a family message. He gave it to Kate.
  • Christie and Rita were evicted in a double eviction on Day 78.

[edit] Week 12

  • As part of the housemates' new task, announced to them on Day 79, they had to make a movie. They wagered their whole budget on it.
  • The housemates up for eviction are Greg, Melanie, and Vesna. After Tim uses his Three Point Twist on Greg, housemates nominated for eviction are Kate, Melanie, and Vesna.
  • On Day 83, the housemates were invited to watch their movie. When the movie had finished, they were told they had passed their task.
  • Vesna was told on Day 84 that she had to host a dinner, but keep her mission a secret. If she succeeded, Big Brother would provide her with a sweets pack.
  • Kate was evicted on Day 85.

[edit] Week 13

  • Housemates nominated for the last time on Day 86. Logan Greg, Vesna, and Tim were up for eviction. Tim used his Three Point Twist on himself, and changed the eviction line-up to Logan Greg, Vesna, and Melanie.
  • Big Brother set a task on Day 87. Housemates had to be chained together at the wrist.
  • Melanie and Tim's chain broke on Day 88, and Tim wanted to find out if they had failed the task because of it. Big Brother said he would tell the housemates later.
  • Big Brother announced a bowling tournament, as the housemates were bowling at the time. Tim and Melanie won, and their prize was chocolate. They were not allowed to share it with any other housemates.
  • On Day 89, Vesna deliberately broke her chain, and tried to convince the other housemates to give up on the task. Big Brother later switched the chained pairs and punished them for being disobedient.
  • Day 90's Friday Night Live was an All-Stars game, and Bree, Ryan (Fitzy), Paul, and Wesley, all from Big Brother 2004; Joanne (Jo) from Big Brother 2003; and Jessica (Jess) from Big Brother 2002, all competed in the games. In this game, the housemates were given the opportunity to win back some of the prize money they lost in fines. At the end of the games, they received a $20,000 cheque.
  • Big Brother announced to the housemates on Day 90 that they had failed their chain task.
  • The housemates were given a task to build a tower, and the team with the highest would win a takeaway meal. The housemates had to work with whoever they were chained to, as teams.
  • Melanie was evicted on Day 92.

[edit] Week 14

  • Nadia Almada, winner of Big Brother 5 UK, entered the House on Day 93 as a houseguest. She was served dinner by the housemates, but they were soon discussing the fact that Nadia used to be a man.
  • Nadia left the House on Day 95, after she was given framed photos of her time in the house. She was given the opportunity to record a goodbye message, similar to what housemates up for eviction do every week. She started the message by saying she hated all the housemates and that they were the worst people she had ever met, then left the screen. After a short while, she returned on screen and said that was a practical joke, and that she loved everybody really.
  • The housemates finally received their video messages from their families and friends on Day 96.
  • Vesna was evicted on Day 99.
  • Tim and Greg both left the house on Day 100.
  • Greg Mathew was announced winner of Big Brother 2005, and $836,000 for himself and his twin brother David.

[edit] Special shows

[edit] Live Surprise

Live Surprise was a live show broadcast on Day 2. During this show, the Logan twins swapped places with each other for the first time. They were given two minutes in the Diary Room to exchange as much information as possible, and Greg then had to go to the Isolation Room while David went into the Big Brother House. The Daily Show was broadcast as a part of Live Surprise.

[edit] Lies Exposed

Lies Exposed was a show broadcasted on Day 8. After Glenn's discovery that Logan was in fact two twins, this was revealed to all of the housemates. Before this, Nelson, Constance, and Dean, were all evicted to the Punishment Room; and were told their fate by Big Brother. Hosted by Gretel Killeen.

[edit] Viewers Verdict

Viewers Verdict was a live show broadcast on Day 10. This special was aired because Dean, Constance, and Nelson, had all lied about their relationship status. A public vote was held, that was started on Day 9 and was closed during the show, minutes before two of these housemates who had broken the rules were evicted. Constance and Nelson were evicted in this show, and were reunited with their partners. They both had separate eviction interviews with Gretel, and were both told that because they had deceived Big Brother, they would receive no prizes.

[edit] Housemates: Incoming!

Housemates: Incoming! was a live show broadcast during Day 25, where replacement housemates Vesna and Rachael were introduced to the public and put in the House.

[edit] Intruders Arrive

Intruders Arrive was a live show broadcast on Day 53, where Intruders Melanie, Rita, and Heath entered the Big Brother House. Hosted by Gretel Killeen.

[edit] Intruder Eviction

Intruder Eviction was a live show broadcast on Day 59. The housemates were given 1 minute 30 seconds to discuss amongst themselves which of the new Intruders, Rita, Heath, and Melanie, they wanted to keep. They decided to keep Rita, so Heath and Melanie were put up for eviction. As the public SMS vote started and finished during the show, lasting only twenty minutes, viewers in Western Australia, South Australia, and the Northern Territory were not able to vote as television shows in Australia are broadcast with a delay depending on the state to compensate for differences in Australian time zones. Heath got the most votes and was evicted.

[edit] Surprise Twin Eviction

Surprise Twin Eviction was a live Eviction aired on Day 74. With David and Greg both living in the House as a single housemate, the twins were put up for eviction as two separate people. David was evicted.

[edit] Friday Night Live

A special All Stars edition of Friday Night Live took place on Day 90. Greg, Melanie, Tim, and Vesna, all competed against a team of ex-housemates to try and win a $20,000 AUD addition to the prize fund. The team they were competing against was Paul and Wesley from Big Brother 2004, Joanne from Big Brother 2003, and Jess from Big Brother 2002; and if they won Friday Night Live, they would win the $20,000 in the form of $5,000 each. Bree Amer and Ryan Fitzgerald, Friday Night Live co-presenters and former Big Brother 2004 housemates, participated in one of the games, each as an additional member of one of the teams. The All Stars team won the Friday Night Live.

[edit] The Final Countdown

The Final Countdown was aired on Day 93. In this show, Nadia Almada, winner of Big Brother 5 UK, entered the Big Brother Australia House as a houseguest. Hosted by Gretel Killeen

[edit] The Prize Fight

The Prize Fight was a special Friday Night Live that aired on Day 97. The remaining housemates were given the opportunity to win back some of the prize money they had lost in fines.

[edit] The Final Sunday Eviction

The Final Sunday Eviction was the last Eviction of Big Brother 2005, and was aired on Day 99, the day before The Finale. Vesna was evicted in The Final Sunday Eviction.
