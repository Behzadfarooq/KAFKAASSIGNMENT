Big Green Lake is a lake in Green Lake County, Wisconsin.

The lake has a maximum depth of 237 feet, making it the deepest natural inland lake in Wisconsin.[1] The lake covers 7,346 acres, has 29.3 miles of shoreline and has an average depth of 100 feet. [2]

Big Green Lake is known for its fishing. Fish found in Big Green Lake include northern pike, largemouth bass, muskellunge, crappie, walleye, smallmouth bass, channel catfish, white bass, perch, rock bass and cisco and lake trout.
