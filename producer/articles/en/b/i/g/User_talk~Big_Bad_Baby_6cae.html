[edit] linking individual years

Please don't wiki-link individual years in articles - e.g., don't turn 1967 into 1967, or 1930s into 1930s . Those links don't add any information, and just create more blue words to distract readers. - DavidWBrooks 11:23, 23 March 2006 (UTC)

[edit] Duplicate links

Hi,

I reverted your addition of links to Cereal Partners Worldwide; we normally only link the first occurrence of a word.

There may be exceptions to this; personally, I feel that in a long article where someone shouldn't have been assumed to have read every preceding section, it's quite reasonable to re-link the first occurrence in a distinct section.

However, in short articles, or within sections (as in this case), we only need one link. Thanks!
