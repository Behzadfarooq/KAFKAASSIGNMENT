Don't smear my talk page with cyrilicBigz 00:11, 31 October 2006 (UTC)

I had no idea that you could not read Cyrillic. There is no indication of this. I may be excused for thinking that you could since you belong to a project based on a country to which Cyrillic is used co-officially. Either way, it is not important now. If you do not like the content, I take no offence in you removing it permanently from your talk page. I do however take offence when people remove my contributions as you did on Haris Silajdzic. I have restored it, but in a way that I have not interfered with your edits. You only have the right to remove passages if they are forms of vandalism; I do not vandalise nor disrupt talk pages with nonsense. If the details are erroneous, you may edit them replacing incorrect details with correct details by proving sources. If the details are correct but do not belong on that page, you may "move" the passage onto the relevant page. As for "dumb observation", just because you feel this information is dumb, it does not automaticly mean that all Wikipedians do. If the content is that bad, I am sure others will remove it, but given that nobody has touched the Nebojsa Radmanovic page which has the same passage in reflection, I may assume that it is not considered irrelevant. Many articles have "Trivia" sections. These may be kept at the bottom of the page after all serious writing is out of the way, these may include such observations. I mean who actually cares about Milo Djukanovic being over 1,90cm tall? He is only a country leader now, not a basketball player any more. The Silajdzic page is biographical, and as information about his life emerges, it may be placed on the article. The Trivia, along with other trivial features can go on the end. If you have a problem with this, good luck in deleting ALL Trivia sections on all articles, you will however lose that battle. If you remove my contributions again then I will report your actions as vandalism. Евлекис October 31, 2006
