Currently the owner and CEO of Scrilla Shop Records, a leading independent urban music label based out of Compton, California, Timothy Hawkins, aka Big Money, has perservered through a long, hard journey to realize his dreams. Orginally from Quebec, him and his brother Rex Hawkins(aka Chump Change) were forced to fend for themselves. Their mother had a heroin addiction that forced them to live in undesirable neighborhoods and find their own food. The elder Timothy became involved with drug trafficking and it wasn't long before Rex followed suit. The street life led them to a love for urban music, as they felt it was an artistic expression of the life they were handed. While in Canada, they began posting songs on acidplanet.com and soon after, released their first hit, "If We Eva Make It". This song was a heartfelt tribute to the hard life and obstacles they were confronted while trying to do something positive with their lives.

At the age of 19, Big Money realized his hip hop dreams wouldn't rise above the poverty stricken streets of Quebec if he didn't make a move of his own. After networking with his business affiliates in New York, he found his way to Compton, California, where he wasted no time in establishing himself in the local urban music scene. Soon after, his little brother Chump Change came to live with him to help promote the Scrilla Shop label. It didn't take long for the Scrilla Shop label to gain credibility with a nonstop barrage of releases. Unfortunately, the street life began to take a toll on Big Money and Chump Change's relationship, as Chump Change felt that his brother wasn't being true to his roots, and began insulting Big Money in public internet forums. After Scrilla Shop's studio was robbed two days before Chump Change bought a new Cadillac DTS, Big Money released the song, "Chump Change Best Recognize". Chump Change heeded the warning and vowed his loyalty to his brother, but the damage was done. Not long after he joined the Army to get away from the streets that were causing him to alienate those who got him where he was. He signed the title of the DTS over to Big Money as an apology and as a thank you for the opportunities he was given.

Currently, Big Money is working on a concept album to be titled "Nu Vishinz" but is holding off completion until Chump Change is released from active duty.

His reason: "I came up with my little brother, and I almost fell with my little brother. How could I drop the hottest record in a decade without the man who was riding shotgun the whole time? Shoot, the way I see it, his being in the Army gives me more time to work on the arrangements and musicality of this release. It's called Nu Vishinz 'cause that's what it is. It's the songs I started hearing in my head when the momentum of what we were doing fell off and I started seeing the ugly side of the industry. Nu Vishinz is like a reset button. Forget all the hard times and the blood and gunshots that made you and focus on developing yourself into the best person you can be, know what I mean? But you gotta be hard, no doubt. Feel Me?"

With a mentality like that, nothing but success is in store for Big Money and whoever he deems worthy of riding the Scrilla Shop train.
