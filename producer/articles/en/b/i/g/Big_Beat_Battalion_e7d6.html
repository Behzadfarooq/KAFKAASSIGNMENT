Big Beat Battalion is an American two-man hip hop group centralized in Las Vegas, Nevada. Famous for its unique rapping style and clean messages, Big Beat Battalion is different from all other hip hop groups in existence. The Battalion (as it is frequently called by fans) has performed at numerous events around the Las Vegas area and are recognized widely with high school students.

[edit] History

Smooth J and Grammar G first began their lyrical crusade in the late 1980’s. Smooth, originally from Minnesota, began his hip-hop career in junior high school. He entertained at school dances by mixing the hottest rap tracks and showing off the latest break dancing moves with Lee’s on his legs and Adidas on his feet. Meanwhile, Grammar G was honing his incomparable beat box and practicing his signature metaphorical style. Soon the two would cross paths and begin their hip-hop conquest.

At first, Smooth and Grammar used the instrumental b-sides of cassettes to flow on, but later began creating their own tailor made beats. At present, the Big Beat Battalion have mastered their own instruments to give the duo a unique sound: old school rap meets alternative rock with sample driven block rocking beats. The Big Beat Battalion has performed with Elektra recording artists Better Than Ezra and was recently featured at length in several local newspapers.

[edit] Music Influences

According to Smooth J and Grammar G, the biggest influences on their music have been Run DMC, Public Enemy, the Beastie Boys, Beck, the Beatles, Weezer, the Smashing Pumpkins, Oasis, and U2. Although they are a hip-hop group, the Battalion tends to employ rock emphasis on most of their tracks. This, in turn, produces a sound very similar to the works of the Beastie Boys and Beck.

[edit] Discography

The Big Beat Battalion currently has two studio albums, two underground albums, and a third studio album in the works. "Geek Mythology" and "Changing the World One Song at a Time" were produced before the BBB gained their fame. "Big Beat Battalion" (self-titled) and "Laptop Hip Hop" are the two mainstream albums the BBB has produced. Both studio albums are hailed by Las Vegas journalists as "the big bar of Dial in rap's potty mouth".
