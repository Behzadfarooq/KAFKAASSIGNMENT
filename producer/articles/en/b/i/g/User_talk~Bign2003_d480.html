[edit] Signing your comments on talk pages

Hello. In case you didn't know, when you add content to talk pages and Wikipedia pages that have open discussion, you should sign your posts by typing four tildes ( ~~~~ ) at the end of your comment. You may also click on the signature button located above the edit window. This will automatically insert a signature with your name and the time you posted the comment. This information is useful because other editors will be able to tell who said what, and when. Thank you!--Crossmr 20:42, 16 July 2006 (UTC)

[edit] Hi and welcome

I wanted to tell you in as friendly a manner possible that it seems to a lot of people that you created 2 additional accounts, JustinChimento and JeffDeHart, to help you in the discussion about Gangsters in Love. These sort of accounts are referred to as "sockpuppets" and they are not permitted per policy. It is also possible that these are friends you recruited to particpate in the debate; in that case, they are still covered under the sockpuppet policy because they do not represent the views of the "wikipedia community" (i.e. regular editors), Your actions were fairly obvious because this happens all the time. But since it is a generally mild infraction, if you want to stick around and build some credibility, this will easily blow over (as long as it doesn't go farther).

Regarding your question about some articles you want to work on, articles that have been deleted once are subject to speedy deletion if recreated in substantially the same way. However, you can work on the articles in your user space, that is, subpages of your user page. Just write a link to a new page, like this [[User:Bign2003/Gore]], which will look like this when you're done User:Bign2003/Gore. Click on the link and start editing, and you will have a user page! There is a lot more (but not unlimited) flexibility about what you can put in your user space, but you should be able to create your articles there. (the user space policy is WP:USER)

To move the articles back into main space, you will have to address whatever the reasons were for deletion (not notable, not verifiable, or whatever). If you feel you have addressed any concerns and the article now meets all the applicable policies, you can use the move tab to move it to a proper article title, or you can cut and paste into a new window. (*there are a lot of policies but the most important are everything must be verifiable, you must use reliable sources, there should be no original research, and the subject should be notable enough to deserve an encyclopedia article.

If you have other questions about editing you can put {{Helpme}} on this page and someone will stop by to see what you need.Good luck. Thatcher131 05:42, 26 July 2006 (UTC)
