Yeah, I know...

I did take your comment at Wikipedia talk:Protecting children's privacy lightly. I just feel very strongly that this proposed policy is flawed, possibly unsalvageably so. I've yet to see a good reason why we need this; we're not in the business of protecting kids, what actions we could be taking to help do not need policy to enforce or can be enforced under other policies, and if these were necessary measures then it wouldn't be us volunteer editors forming the policy. With that point of view in mind, seeing a suggestion to prevent me, an adult in mine and Wikipedia's jurisdiction, from disclosing my age feels like oppression for oppression's sake.
