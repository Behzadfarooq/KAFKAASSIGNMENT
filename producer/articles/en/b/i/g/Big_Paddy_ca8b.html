Big Paddy is a popular Colorado-based American band. They currently play throughout Colorado, with regular shows at some of their favorite pubs. With Sean Gaughan singing while playing a djembe hand-drum, and Jon Sherman playing various instruments including mandolin and guitar, they created an energetic and atypical sound -- mixing traditional Irish folk songs with contemporary rock and world elements, they maintained a strong Irish flavor throughout.

[edit] Band history

Sean Gaughan (vocals, djembe) and Jon Sherman (mandolin, tenor banjo, guitar, bodhran) were the original members of what would become Big Paddy, in the days when they gigged at the Pearl St. Pub in Boulder, Colorado. The partnership originally formed in 1997 when Sean Gaughan and Jon Sherman met and started experimenting with traditional Irish folk songs such as “Mug Of Brown Ale” and “New-Rigged Ship”. After working together for a little while they called themselves "Pickin' and Stickin'" and began seeking shows. There was briefly another member of the band, only there from 1999 to 2000, but soon it was back to the original pair.

In 2001 when Sean began focusing on djembe-work and singing, with Jon carrying the rest of the tune on whatever instrument was needed, they decided to take up a new name, and both agreed on “Big Paddy” as an indication of both the Irish music they loved and the expansive energy they sought after. At the Pearl St. Pub in Boulder they found what they have called “the smallest setting in Boulder” ready to welcome them. They played mostly Irish folk music, but also enjoyed covering artists from their eclectic collections, particularly Irish and folk artists. They began to build their repertoire, peppering their stock of traditional Irish songs with the occasional cover of such diverse artists as Johnny Cash, The Pogues, Richard Thompson, Shane MacGowan, and The Humblebums.

Big Paddy is especially notable based on their success of introducing a new generation to the struggles and trails of Ireland through traditional songs that beautifully portray the character and pride of Ireland in an unexpected and brilliantly crafted rock format that grabs attention, and teaches at the same time. They tell age-old musical stories at weddings, private parties, Irish pubs, beer festivals, and the like performing in venues in and around Boulder, Denver and Denver suburbs, and Colorado Springs. They soon took up regular weekly and monthly shows, some of which they have maintained for years -- particularly the Saturday night show at an Irish pub in Colorado Springs. To draw a crowd EVERY Saturday night for years running, is truly remarkable and a tribute to this band's musicianship and story-telling talent.

Big Paddy recorded their first album, "Jigs, Reels, And Songs About Whiskey" in the winter of 2003, at Red Door Studio in El Dorado Canyon, Colorado. Due to lack of funds at the time, this album was recorded in one-take sessions. Their second album, "A Terrible Thirst", was recorded in early 2005 at Flabby Road Studios in Boulder, Colorado.

[edit] New Album?

Big Paddy has finally (as of November 2006) begun production on a live album that has been in the works for a little while, to be recorded at shows in Colorado Springs and possibly other locations.
