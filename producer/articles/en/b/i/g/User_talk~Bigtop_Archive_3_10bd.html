Vandalism is a serious word

I came to your page because I spotted a "final warning" from you on the talk page of a guy who seems to be a fairly serious editor. I was surprised to see him accused of vandalism, and following the link to the page I saw that it was nothing of the kind--it was original research, maybe, but certainly not vandalism. Going to your talk page I see you've sparked the ire more than once of editors whom you've accused of vandalism in situations that seem to be similar. It's worth bearing in mind the words of WP:VANDAL:

Any good-faith effort to improve the encyclopedia, even if misguided or ill-considered, is not vandalism. Apparent bad-faith edits that do not make their bad-faith nature inarguably explicit are not considered vandalism at Wikipedia. For example, adding a personal opinion once is not vandalism — it's just not helpful, and should be removed or restated.

And as another piece of Wikiwisdom has it: Assume good faith.

I know you're doing the lord's work--but don't assume every problem is a nail just because you're fond of hammering. Nareek 15:58, 7 August 2006 (UTC)

Program Feature: To-Do List
The Esperanza To-Do List is a place where you may list any request, big or small, for assistance. If you need help with archiving your usertalk, for example, all you need to do is list it here and somebody will help you out. Likewise, if you need help with some area of editing on Wikipedia, list it here! Again, any matter, trivial or not, can be placed on this page. However, all matters listed on this page must not be of an argumentative nature. You do not need to be a member of Esperanza (or this program) to place or fulfill requests on this page.
