Bight (geography)

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

For other meanings of bight, see Bight.

In geography, bight has two meanings.

A bight can be simply a bend or curve in any geographical featureâ€”usually a bend or curve in the line between land and water.

Alternatively, the term can refer to a large (and often only slightly receding) bay. It is distinguished from a sound by being shallower.
