Big Brother Australia 2002 was the second season of the Australian reality television series Big Brother Australia. The season started on April 8, 2002 and ended on July 1, 2002, lasting 85 days.

Big Brother 2002 was very similar to Big Brother 2001, the first series; and was the only one to use the same house from a previous series, albeit with some small superficial modifications. Simultaneous with the main house was a house of six potential Intruders. A number of television specials allowed viewers to get to know the intruders and viewers were able to vote which three actually went into the house.

A close relationship formed between Nathan Martin and Jessica Hardy (Marty and Jess) who later married. Their wedding plans and the ceremony were showcased in television special Marty & Jess: An Outback Wedding. The couple separated in late 2005.

The Australian rock band Killing Heidi entered the house for a special surprise birthday party for Jess. During an Eviction, a 25 year old male ran naked onto the stage and was subsequently fined $100 in court.

The reserved Peter Corbett won the $250,000 first prize, with Marty coming second.

[edit] Housemates

[edit] Turkan

Turkan Aksoy was a social worker from New South Wales.

[edit] Aaron

Aaron Benton was a former member of the Royal Australian Navy. Was the third evictee, leaving on Day 28.

[edit] Alex

Alex Christie, from Melbourne

[edit] Shannon

Shannon Cleary was a personal trainer from Sydney and the fifth to be evicted from the house. Admitted she had once suffered from anorexia nervosa.

[edit] Peter

The winner of Big Brother 2002, Peter Corbett, lost both his parents at a young age. He is currently studying Medical Science at the University of Wollongong. He has also been an active charity supporter, assisting Banardos Australia and becoming an ambassador for Daffodil Day.

[edit] Nicole

A video producer, Nicole Dickmann was one of the three intruders voted into house by viewers. She was evicted several days later.

[edit] Jessica

Jessica Hardy became well known during her time in the house for her relationship with Marty, a relationship which led to their marriage outside of the house after the season ended. They are now divorced. Her brother was killed in the 2002 Bali bombing.

[edit] Damian

Damian Hoo, a teaching student at the time of entering the House and currently a professional rugby player. His father, Chan, was briefly involved in the show and gave his eviction predictions until Damiam left the House in the season's first Eviction on Day 14. Currently plays rugby for a team in QLD

[edit] Sahra

Sahra Kearney AKA "Sarah Kearney" was evictee # 12. Worked as an actress on Australian Television programs "Home & Away" and "Heartbreak High". Sahra also appeared on several commercials and continues to work as an Actress between Australia and the UK.

[edit] Marty

The runner-up of Big Brother 2002, Nathan Martin. Marty's aunt, Sciona Browne, was the runner-up contestant on Australian Survivor, which co-incidentally was aired at the same time as Big Brother 2002 but on rival Nine Network.

[edit] Katrina

Katrina Miani is a legal secretary from Melbourne.

[edit] Nathan

Nathan Morris grew up in Kalgoorlie. He moved to Perth permanently after his stint on Big Brother to become a presenter on Perth radio station Nova 93.7, a role he still holds as of 2006.

[edit] Mirabai

Mirabai Peart was a musician.

[edit] Kieran

Kieran Tanner, from New South Wales, entered the House as an Intruder after being voted into the house by viewers, and retained after a housemate vote on which of the Intruders to keep.

[edit] Brodie

Brodie Young entered the House as an Intruder from Melbourne voted into the house by viewers and retained after a housemate vote on which intruders to keep. Currently the host of the Quizmania late night game show on the Nine Network (as a rival to Hotdogs', from Big Brother 2005, The Up-Late Game Show).

[edit] Intruders

The six potential Intruders of Big Brother 2002 entered the Intruder House (hidden away in the Gold Coast Hinterland) at the same time the original housemates entered the Big Brother compound on Day 0. Handycam footage of the Intruder House aired during the Intruders special on Day 28, revealing that the group of six (Hayley, Nicki Cahill, Nicole, Will, Brodie and Keiran) had not been allowed any access to the outside world and for the past 28 days, their experiences mirrored the housemates' with a diary room available to them at all times, the six having to share a bathroom, the participation in mini-tasks set by Big Brother, and wild themed parties thrown for them. The edited down footage of the past month showed that the Intruders were feeling the effects of being in extended isolation, with many of them becoming paranoid and speculating that they were actually being tricked and they really were never going to get the chance to enter the main Big Brother house. The overnight voting after the six Intruders made their plea to the public to vote them into the main house, revealed that Nicki, Hayley and Will were going home and that Keiran would enter first, followed by Nicole and then Brodie on Day 29.
