Big Dummy

The Big Dummy is a long-wheelbase bicycle frame manufactured by Surly to be compatible with all of Xtracycle's racking products.

Building a bicycle using a Big Dummy frame effectively achieves the same end result as attaching an Xtracycle Free Radical onto a normal bicycle frame. The major advantages over the Free Radical are increased frame rigidity, reduced overall weight, and long-wheelbase-specific geometry for improved handling. The major disadvantage is cost, since the Big Dummy frameset is expected to retail for about $820 for the frame and fork. The Free Radical, on the other hand, retails for around $400 and includes everything necessary to convert a complete bicycle.

The Big Dummy is historically significant to bicycle culture because it is the first mass-produced Xtracycle-compatible single-piece frame. This is one of the first major milestones in Xtracycle's push to make their racking system less of a niche market by creating more options and variety for the end user.

The Big Dummy is compatible with the Stokemonkey human-electric hybrid power assist system manufactured by Portland Oregon-based CleverChimp.

The name "Big Dummy" is apparently a reference to the 1970s television series Sanford and Son where Red Foxx often uses the exclamation "You big dummy!" in reference to Lamont, his son. This connection is not known to have been officially affirmed by Surly at this point, but it is supported by photographs of Red Foxx around the display of the Big Dummy prototype at the 2006 Interbike trade show, where the frameset was first unveiled to the public.
