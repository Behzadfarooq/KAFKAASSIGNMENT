The Big Bend of Florida, U.S.A., is an informal region of the state around Apalachee Bay, an arm of the Gulf of Mexico. It runs from Wakulla County on the west end through Jefferson, Taylor and Dixie counties to Levy County on the southeast end.[1] Common usage may also include Franklin, Gadsden, Leon, Liberty and Madison counties in the region.[2][3] The principal city in the region is Tallahassee.
