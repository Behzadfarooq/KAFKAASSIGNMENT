Big International Non-Government Organisation

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Big International Non-Government Organisation (BINGO) is an acronym applied to large international non-governmental organizations. These are typically organizations with enough resources that they can be expected to act quickly in a global emergency.

Some quick examples of BINGOs are:

  • AIESEC
  • Amnesty International
  • CARE
  • Greenpeace
  • Médecins Sans Frontières (Doctors Without Borders)
  • Oxfam
  • Red Cross
  • The Nature Conservancy
  • SOS Children's Villages
  • World Vision
