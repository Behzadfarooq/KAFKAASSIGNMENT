This is a list of Big Four lists related to music:

[edit] Artists

  • The Big Four bands of the British Invasion: The Beatles, The Rolling Stones, The Kinks, and The Who
  • The Big Four bands out of San Francisco, California, U.S.A. in the mid-late 1960s: The Grateful Dead, Big Brother and the Holding Company, Jefferson Airplane, and Quicksilver Messenger Service
  • The Big Four punk rock bands of 1977: Ramones, Sex Pistols, The Clash, and The Jam
  • The Big Four punk rock bands to emerge from the California punk scene during the mid-1990s: Green Day, NOFX, The Offspring and Rancid
  • The Big Four thrash metal bands of the 1980s: Metallica, Megadeth, Slayer, and Anthrax
  • The Big Four grunge music bands that emerged from Seattle, Washington, U.S.A in the early 1990s: Alice in Chains, Nirvana, Pearl Jam, and Soundgarden
  • The Big Four hip hop artists and groups to emerge from the New York hip hop scene during the mid-1990s: The Wu-Tang Clan, Nas, The Notorious B.I.G., and Boot Camp Clik
  • The Big Four early doom metal bands that were influenced by the band Black Sabbath as well: Saint Vitus, The Obsessed, Trouble, and Candlemass
  • The Big Four male singers in Hong Kong in the 1980s and 1990s: Jacky Cheung, Aaron Kwok, Leon Lai, and Andy Lau

[edit] Albums

  • The Big Four Aerosmith albums: Toys in the Attic, Rocks, Permanent Vacation, and Pump.
  • The Big Four Bad Religion albums: Suffer, No Control, Against the Grain and Stranger Than Fiction.
  • The Big Four Black Sabbath albums: Black Sabbath, Paranoid, Master of Reality and Sabotage.
  • The Big Four Death, and also the first, albums: Scream Bloody Gore, Leprosy, Spiritual Healing and Human.
  • The Big Four Def Leppard, and also the first, albums: On Through the Night, High 'n' Dry, Pyromania, and Hysteria.
  • The Big Four Judas Priest albums: British Steel, Screaming for Vengeance, Defenders of the Faith, and Painkiller.
  • The Big Four Megadeth albums: Peace Sells... But Who's Buying?, Rust in Peace, Countdown to Extinction and The System Has Failed.
  • The Big Four Metallica, and also the first, albums: Kill 'Em All, Ride the Lightning, Master of Puppets, and ...And Justice for All.
  • The Big Four Obituary, and also the first, albums: Slowly We Rot, Cause of Death, The End Complete and World Demise.
  • The Big Four Offspring albums: Smash, Ixnay on the Hombre, Americana and Conspiracy of One.
  • The Big Four Pink Floyd albums: Dark Side of The Moon, The Wall, Wish You Were Here, and The Piper at the Gates of Dawn.
  • The Big Four Radiohead albums: Pablo Honey, The Bends, OK Computer and Kid A.
  • The Big Four Scorpions albums: Virgin Killer, Taken by Force, Blackout and Love at First Sting.
  • The Big Four Slayer albums: Hell Awaits, Reign in Blood, South of Heaven, and Seasons in the Abyss.
  • The Big Four Social Distortion albums: Social Distortion, Somewhere Between Heaven and Hell, White Light, White Heat, White Trash and Sex, Love and Rock 'n' Roll.
  • The Big Four Testament albums: The Legacy, The New Order, Practice What You Preach and The Ritual.
  • The Big Four Van Halen albums: Van Halen I, Van Halen II, Fair Warning and 1984.
  • The Big Four Voivod albums: War & Pain, Rrröööaaarrr, Killing Technology and Nothingface.
  • The Big Four - biggest-selling - albums released on Epitaph Records (Bad Religion's Suffer, NOFX's Punk in Drublic, The Offspring's Smash, and Rancid's ...And Out Come the Wolves)
  • The Big Four hardcore punk albums of the early 80s (The Adolescents' self-titled debut album, Black Flag's Damaged, Circle Jerks' Group Sex and Dead Kennedys' Fresh Fruit for Rotting Vegetables)
