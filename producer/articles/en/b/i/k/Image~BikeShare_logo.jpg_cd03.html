[edit] Summary

BikeShare, founded in 2004, is an on-campus program that allows students at the University of Toronto at Mississauga to sign out bicycles free of charge for a time span of up to 24 hours. Its goal is to promote a healthy and active lifestyle amongst students and to provide an alternate form of transportation that is environmentally friendly.


[edit] History

The history behind the BikeShare program still remains quite new as it had just recently been established in 2004.This program was launched by UTM’s environmental affairs officer, Aubrey Iwaniw, and Jason Nicols, the Student Center events coordinator. These student leaders have set out to embrace community development through promoting alternative forms of transportation for students at UTM, but also emphasize the importance of environmental issues such as the local air quality and the ever-increasing demand for (and lack of) parking spaces.


BikeShare’s motto is “Think green.”


[edit] Organizational Structure

BikeShare’s organizational structure currently operates as an independent program solely at the University of Toronto at Mississauga campus. The program was established by the UTM Green Team and is facilitated by Vlado Mazic, an executive member on the Action Sports Coalition representing the Mountain Bikers of Mississauga. The BikeShare Office is located at the Student Center of the UTM campus.

Other affiliates include BikeChain, which is the same program that is run at the University of Toronto, St. George.


[edit] Funding

The BikeShare program is currently funded by the UTM Green Team, which is a student run committee that is associated with the Geography and Environmental Sciences Departments at UTM. The Green Team themselves are sponsored by UTM but they have also raised money through several fund-raising activities. In addition, all the bikes which are owned by BikeShare have been donated by members of the community. See next section for activities.


[edit] Activities

The main function of the BikeShare program is to provide both UTM students and faculty with the availability of bikes which can be rented out free of charge for a period of up to 24 hours. BikeShare also provides free repair service for broken or malfunctioning bikes. The BikeShare Program is just one of the many programs run by the Green Team that promotes students and members of the community to be more environmentally friendly. The majority of Green Team’s activities include public awareness campaigns and workshops such as Waste Reduction Days on campus and “Make Your Own Beauty Products at Home”. All activities run by the Green Team are often run with the assistance of other various student organizations in addition to UTM, which include UTMSU (formerly known as ECSU), SAC, and the EEA.


[edit] Awards

The BikeShare program and the Green Team have received numerous awards and recognition. Here are some main highlights: Phil Green Recognition Awards: Aubrey Iwaniw (Environmental Affairs Officer) and Jason Nicols (Student Centre Events Coordinator) were granted this award by Her Worship Mayor Hazel McCallion and Mr. Phil Green in recognition of their work on the BikeShare program at UTM. UTM wins Healthy U of T Award 2004: The UTM/Evergreen Naturalization Steering Committee has won this year's Healthy U of T Award (group category). Sponsored by the university's Centre for Health Promotion, this award recognizes an individual, department or organization within the University of Toronto that has made a healthy difference through a substantial and lasting contribution to the health and well-being of campus life. The 2003 Credit Valley Stewardship Award of Distinction, awarded by the Credit Valley Conservation Board of Directors. The UTM Evergreen partnership was recognized for enhancing the look and feel of the campus and contributing to the health of the Credit River Valley. These efforts included planting trees, shrubs and flowers. UTM was awarded the Environmental Excellence Award, which is presented annually by the Mississauga Board of Trade’s Environment Committee. The award is based on the contributions made by organizations and/or individuals in the City of Mississauga that foster environmental excellence and the environmental wellbeing of the City of Mississauga or the environment as a whole.

Quoting Iwaniw, “"We're proud of our green campus. BikeShare is one way to help protect our unique campus environment. The program nurtures a bike culture on campus, which, in turn, promotes environmental awareness among our students. The program encourages students to enjoy the natural landscape around them while doing their part to protect the environment. I'm thrilled the City has recognized our efforts and the program in this way."
