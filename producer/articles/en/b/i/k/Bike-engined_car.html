Bike-engined car

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

You have new messages (last change).

A bike engined car is a small or light weight car using an engine from one of the larger capacity motorcycles. The advantage for the car builders in doing this is that small car engines in high states of tune are relatively rare whereas large motorcycle engines are frequently designed for high specific outputs.

Also, most of these cars use the sequential gearbox from the motorcycle, allowing for faster shifting than a traditional automotive gearbox.

Bike engined cars are often based around the Lotus 7 type kit cars.

The 750 Motor Club in the UK runs a national race series for cars powered by road going bike engines (RGB series).

[edit] Common bike engines used in cars

  • Honda Fireblade
  • Honda CBR1100XX
  • Kawasaki ZX-12R
  • Suzuki GSX-R1000
  • Suzuki GSX1300R Hayabusa
  • Yamaha YZF-R1
