Bieldside

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Bieldside is a suburb to the west of Aberdeen, Scotland.

Together with the neighbouring village of Cults, Bieldside is known as a wealthy area. Among its residents are golfer Paul Lawrie and Stewart Milne. It has also been recognised as the second wealthiest area in Scotland[citation needed].

There are two Camphill Communities at Bieldside, Newton Dee Village and a set of Camphill schools known as Murtle Estate for 3 to 19 year olds, providing Curative Education for those with learning disabilities, including deprivation and autism. Another Camphill school (Camphill Estate) is in nearby Miltimber.
