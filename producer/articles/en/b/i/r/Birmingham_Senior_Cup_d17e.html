The Birmingham Senior Cup is a football (soccer) competition for Birmingham County FA club teams, organized by the Birmingham County Football Association. It began in 1875 with Tipton taking the first honours in the final in 1876 and is one of the oldest county football competitions still active.

[edit] History

The Birmingham Senior Cup is a local cup contest, which has featured all of the West Midlands' big clubs, Birmingham City, West Bromwich Albion, Coventry City, Walsall Wolverhampton Wanderers and Aston Villa. However, in recent years most of the bigger clubs have tended to treat the cup as a "reserve" competition, giving the non-league sides a greater chance of success.
