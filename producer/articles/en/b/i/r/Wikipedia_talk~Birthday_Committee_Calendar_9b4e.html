[edit] A 2007 Calendar?

Are we going to have a 2007 calendar? Just wondering, because if not, it would be very hard to wish people happy birthdays, first edit days etc. Perhaps for the 2007 calendar, we can just use the 2006 calendar but remove all the first edit days and admin anniversaries. Then people can add their new admin anniversaries, first edit days etc. Thanks, Jam01 09:10, 21 December 2006 (UTC)

The calendar is not a 2006 calendar. It will automatically repeat. As birthdays, adminship anniversaries, and first edit days will not change from year to year, it makes sense to have the calendar repeat. The calendar was created to not matter what year it was. Otherwise each year there would have to be 356 pages created. The calendar will repeat forever. (If that still does not make sense, I can try to explain better.) – Heaven's Wrath   Talk  00:40, 22 December 2006 (UTC)
That makes sense. However, 1st edit days would kind of become 2nd edit days. Well, I guess it's an excuse to spread cheer and happiness throughout the community, so its a good thing! Thanks for the explaination, Jam01 01:51, 28 December 2006 (UTC) - Happy New Year!
I think you are misinterpreting the meaning of a "First Edit Day." It means "the day when you made your first edit." It has nothing to do with how many years you have participated on Wikipedia. – Heaven's Wrath   Talk  16:22, 28 December 2006 (UTC)
