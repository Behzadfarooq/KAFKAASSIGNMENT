The Birmingham Metro Baseball League, BMBL, is Birmingham, Alabama's local amateur baseball league for adults 18 years and older. As of 2006, the league has 8 teams. The league is an 18+ league requiring players to turn 18 years old prior to the start of the season (May 13th in 2006). The season begins with a pre-season tournament which was sponsored in 2006 by Jim 'N' Nicks Barbecue. The season's 15 games are featured at area high schools (Spain Park High School and Hoover High School) and area public fields (Spain Park Athletic Complex and Shades Mountain Community Park). Additionally each team plays one game in America's oldest ballpark - Rickwood Field.

The BMBL regular season was broken up halfway through the season by an all-star game at Samford University. A double elimination playoff tournament caps the season.

[edit] History

The BMBL was founded in 2006 by two local Birmingham amateur baseball players. The two individuals discovered the need in Metropolitan Birmingham for a well-run amateur baseball league and were surprised that this did not exist in a city with over a million people. Immediate the league was a success with 8 teams and was featured in an article in the fall of 2006 in Hardball Magazine.
