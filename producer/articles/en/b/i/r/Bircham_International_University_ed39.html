Bircham International University

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Bircham International University is an international distance education university. According to IAU Universities List of accredited schools, Bircham is not accredited in Spain.[1][2]

According to John Bear's guide, Bircham was founded in 1992 in the United Kingdom as Oxford International University, but changed names in 2000 and has addresses in the Bahamas, Spain, England, Florida, New Zealand, and Taiwan.[3] Bircham International University is not accredited by any accreditation body recognized by its country. As such, its degrees and credits might not be acceptable to employers or other institutions, and use of degree titles may be restricted or illegal in some jurisdictions. [1]

Bircham International University was referred to by a former DipScam federal agent as "pure hokum".[4] It is also listed as a questionable non-accredited school by Quackwatch.[5] The Maine Department of Education says that Oxford International University is a "Degree mill. [with] No connection to legitimate Oxford University in Great Britain."[6] According to the Texas Higher Education Coordinating Board Bircham has "No degree-granting authority from Spain (AACRAO evaluation)".[7]
