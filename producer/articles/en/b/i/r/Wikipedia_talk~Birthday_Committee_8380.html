[edit] A Change of Strategy?

As many of you may have realised, the Birthday Committee is expanding rapidly, with new members, new templates and more activity. In fact, we're growing so quickly that at this rate we'll add 10kb to people's talk pages with greetings, wishes and the rest, with 20 odd members wishing each person. So to counter this effect, I propose that we convert all our templates to dynamic (I can help with this) so that instead of subst'ing templates, we are merely using this sort of code:

{{Happy Birthday 9|~~~~|Have a good day!}}

Where we specify the template, pass our signature to it dynamically (is this possible? i assume so..) and if we so desire add our own note to the template, so we end up having something like this on a person's talk page:

{{Happy Birthday 4|~~~~|Have a good day!}}
{{Happy Birthday 6|~~~~|Best wishes!}}
{{Happy Birthday 9|~~~~|Have a happy birthday!}}

Instead of (potentially) a hundred lines of code. I also propose that, not only do we wish members, we focus on encouraging Wikipedians to add their birthdays and other events to the Esparanza Calendar so that we do not have 50 members wishing 2 people every day. This could be done through a number of ways:

  • Templates for members to add to their user page
  • Notices in places like Esperanza, Village pump etc.
  • Small notices in our signatures (e.g. Draicone (talk)BDC) (Maybe not the best idea due to the masses of links to this page)
  • Other public places on Wikipedia

We should also aim to have our older members, who are more familiar with the process and the inner workings of the committee (e.g. the first ten or so members, or up to a few before me). In addition, we should allocate some people to work on things like the project page (i.e. layout, up to date information, new members?), the talk page (i.e. archiving old discussions, pointing out the not so obvious) but not limit these tasks to those allocated to them so as to ensure that these pages are of the highest quality. Once we have a lot of data, it may be best to put things like the calendar on sub pages of WP:BDC.

Please comment here, and rememeber that this is not official policy, merely a proposal of mine. (As is now fairly clear from my header at the top of this discussion page) --Draicone (talk) 09:01, 3 August 2006 (UTC)

[edit] Calendar and status

Two things, firstly - currently, days with holidays have to be manually marked on the month pages, which is inefficient and there are be heaps of editors who won't bother with it. I say eliminate this bold-ness in month pages (or automate it). Second of all, why is the status column in the membership bar being cluttered with all these user descriptions? They're charming, but if they were required there'd be a column for them, which there isn't. The status column should be restricted to noting different rights, for users like bureaucrats, stewards or admins; noting special user positions in relation to the project (eg. leader); or noting current statuses like wikibreak and such, as was the original intention. Statuses like "Very happy to be here, molto felice di essere qui!! ^_^ ," (sic) are simply turning an important user reference into an MSN buddy-list. +Hexagon1 (t) 05:51, 29 January 2007 (UTC)
