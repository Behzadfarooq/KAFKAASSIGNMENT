Birka Line is an Ålandian shipping company that operates a cruise ship on the Baltic Sea with Stockholm as the starting point under the name Birka Cruises, as well as cargo-carrying ships under the brand Birka Cargo. During the 1970s the company was often marketed as Ålandslinjen.

[edit] History

Birka Line was founded in 1971, they bought M/S Prinsessen from DFDS and renamed her M/S Prinsessan. On November 21, she made her first voyage between Stockholm and Mariehamn. In 1972, Prinsessan's sister ship M/S Olav was bought and renamed M/S Baronessan. In 1973 Birka started traffic on the routes Stockholm — Helsinki with M/S Freeport and Stockholm — Helsinki — Leningrad with M/S Drottningen. However, due to raising fuel costs and the high fuel consumption of the Freeport, both lines were terminated in late 1973. After this the Freeport was sold to Rederi AB Svea and the Drottningen returned to her owners SJ. The company continued operating with the Prinsessan and Baronessan until 1978. The first Prinsessan was renamed Prinsen to free the name for a newer ship, M/S Finnhansa that was purchased from Finnlines in January 1978 and renamed M/S Prinsessan. In 1980 the Baronessan was sold to Panamian interests, and from there on Birka has usually operated with only one passenger-carrying ship at the time.

On April 22, 1986, Birka's first newbuilding M/S Birka Princess was delivered from the Valmet shipyard in Helsinki. As an attempt to show their focus on cruises they started to market themselves as Birka Cruises. The Birka Princess was to be the companys flag ship for the following eighteen years. The following year the Prinsessan was sold to Louis Cruise Lines, becoming their M/S Princesa Marissa. In 1990 Birka Line purchased the majority of United Shipping. In the late 80's the company placed an order for a second cruise ship to operate alongside the Birka Princess from Wärtsilä Turku shipyard that would have been named M/S Birka Queen. However, the shipyard went bankrupt while constructing the vessel which raised the cost of the ship. Birka did not accept delivery of the ship with such a high price, and it was completed instead for Majesty Cruise Line as M/S Royal Majesty. In place of the Birka Queen Birka Line purchased M/S Sunward from Norwegian Cruise Line in May 1992, and renamed her M/S Birka Queen for cruising from Stockholm to Riga and St. Petersburg. In October of the same year the ship was chartered back to NCL. Although she remained under Birka Line ownership until 1997, the second Birka Queen never sailed for Birka Line again.

In 1999 the Birka Princess was rebuilt with additional cabins and more streamlined superstructure. In January 2002 Birka Line purchased the entirety of United Shipping which was renamed Birka Cargo. In November of the same year the company placed an order for a new cruise ship with Aker Finnyards, Rauma. The ship was completed as M/S Birka Paradise in 2004. The ship was delivered in a time when there was a very strong competition for cruise and ferry passengers on the northern Baltic Sea. Due to lack of passengers and because of financial problems, the Birka Princess was sold to Louis Cruise Lines in early 2006. On 8 March 2007 it was reported that the fellow Ålandian shipping company Eckerö Line are looking to purchase Birka Line in it's entirety (Eckerö already owns 42% of Birka Line).[1] On 21 March the offer was turned down as being too low.
