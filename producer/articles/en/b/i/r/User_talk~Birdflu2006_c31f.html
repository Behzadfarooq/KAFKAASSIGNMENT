[edit] Reply to your question

Birdflu2006, hi. I deleted the article according to the process spelled out at Wikipedia:Proposed deletion. The article was tagged for deletion on April 9, and on April 14 I deleted it, because nobody had removed the tag or said anything about why it shouldn't be deleted.

As is also explained at Wikipedia:Proposed deletion, all it takes is somebody saying they think the article should exist, and the deletion may be reversed (or the article recreated from scratch) without prejudice. The reason given by the nominator was "Vanity", which is a reference to the Wikipedia:Vanity guidelines, which you might want to read, and decide whether you think they apply in this case. If you want, I'm happy to undelete the article, but that's no guarantee that it won't be taken to Wikipedia:Articles for deletion if it fails to meet our inclusion criteria. Wikipedia:Notability is a good page to read, in this regard, as is Wikipedia:Verifiability, which is an official policy page, and carries more weight than the others.

I hope I've answered your question fully. Please let me know if I can help with anything else. -GTBacchus(talk) 03:39, 21 April 2006 (UTC)
