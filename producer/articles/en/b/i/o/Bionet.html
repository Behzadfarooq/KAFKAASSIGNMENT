Bionet refers to a network of personal user "bionodes" (distinct user websites and related informational databases) linked together through biological relatedness. The bionet concept combines a permanent relation (as opposed to friend or acquaintance based) between user bionodes, thus limiting one individual to one bionode. By associating people with their respective bionodes, a bionet user has their own reserved space in the bionet. The concept of permanence of space within the internet addresses one of the limitations of the internet, which otherwise is an anonymous environment that is well adapted for searching and information retrieval, but has limitations in adapting to the concept of an interactive internet. The term 'bionet' was coined first in this sense by Genebase in 2005 as part of its Bionet Builder web portal.

[edit] Bionet vs. Internet

The Bionet format is an evolution of the prototypical social networking website/database (such as Friendster or Orkut) due to its unique property of linking user bionodes, both living and deceased into a '3-dimensional' network spanning back into time such that a deceased or "ancestral" user continuing to be linked to a present day user such that no information is lost (with a single blog site or personal website being '1-dimensional', and 'social networking' websites such as Friendster being '2-dimensional'). Due to its ability to include deceased users, the Bionet furthermore permits a reinterpretation of the conventional family tree used in genealogy by allowing information from ancestors of living users to be databanked.
