Bindi Kullar (born January 11, 1976 in Richmond, British Columbia) is a Canadian field hockey player, who played his first international senior tournament for the Men's National Team in 1996, at the World Cup Preliminary in Sardinia. The defender followed his father, who also played field hockey for Canada and got him started.

[edit] International Senior Competitions

  • 1996 – World Cup Preliminary, Sardinia (2nd)
  • 1997 – World Cup Qualifier, Kuala Lumpur (5th)
  • 1998 – World Cup, Utrecht (8th)
  • 1998 – Commonwealth Games, Kuala Lumpur (not ranked)
  • 1999 – Pan American Games, Winnipeg (1st)
  • 2000 – Americas Cup, Cuba (2nd)
  • 2000 – Olympic Games, Sydney (10th)
  • 2001 – World Cup Qualifier, Edinburgh (8th)
  • 2002 – Commonwealth Games, Manchester (6th)
  • 2004 – Olympic Qualifying Tournament, Madrid (11th)
