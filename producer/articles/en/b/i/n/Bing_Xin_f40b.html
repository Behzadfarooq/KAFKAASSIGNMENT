Bing Xin (Chinese: 冰心; pinyin: Bīng Xīn) (October 5, 1900 - February 28, 1999) was one of the most prolific and esteemed Chinese writers of the 20th Century. Many of her poems were written for young readers. She was the chairperson of the China Federation of Literary and Art Circles.

[edit] Works

  • Ji xiao duzhe (To Young Readers) (1926)
  • Chunshui (Spring Water) (1923)
  • Fanxing (A Myriad Stars) (1923)

[edit] Works available in English

  • The Photograph. Beijing: Chinese Literature Press (1992)
  • Spring Waters. Peking, (1929)
