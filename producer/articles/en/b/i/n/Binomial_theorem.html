This formula and the triangular arrangement of the binomial coefficients are often attributed to Blaise Pascal, who described them in the 17th century. However, it was known to many mathematicians who preceded him. 13th century Chinese mathematician Yang Hui, 11th century Persian mathematician Omar Khayyám, and 3rd century BC Indian mathematician Pingala all derived similar results.[citation needed]

[edit] Binomial number

A binomial number is a number in the form of . These binomial numbers can be factored algebraically.

Examples:

x2 − y2 = (x − y)(x + y)
x3 − y3 = (x − y)(x2 + xy + y2)
x3 + y3 = (x + y)(x2 − xy + y2)
x8 − y8 = (x − y)(x + y)(x2 + y2)(x4 + y4)

[edit] A quick way to expand binomials

To quickly expand binomials of the form

(x + y)n

The first term is

xn

(this follows directly from the generalized binomial theorem) and the coefficient of each subsequent term is the current coefficient multiplied by the current exponent of x, divided by the current term number. Exponents of x decrease each term, while exponents of y increase each term (from 0 in the first term) until the exponent of x is 0.
