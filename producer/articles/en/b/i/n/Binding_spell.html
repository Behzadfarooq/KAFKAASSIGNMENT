Binding spells are a type of magic used in antiquity by both men and women to bind victims. These victims include political opponents, business competitors, prospective lovers and future relationships for males and females. Binding Spells are a larger category of Greco- Roman magic that includes curse tablets and voodoo dolls and are commonly known as love magic or erotic magic. Love magic is commonly associated with underclass men and women whom do not have enough social status to obtain the desired lover. Binding spells are also associated with women lacking an appropriate dowry who are in need of social status. A Greco-Roman woman could bind a lover into a relationship while a Greco-Roman man binds potential lovers. No Greek or Roman Male citizen would need binding spells to get a wife or a long standing lover but only to get the unattainable woman, through erotic magic. The women on the other hand would use binding spells for social position and advancement through marriage, or through love magic. Although neither of these characteristics of males using erotic magic and females using love magic are set in stone; they are usually used this way but with exceptions. A man would try to get an unattainable woman, maybe even a married woman or try to bind political or business opponents from further competition. A person living in Antiquity might also use binding spells in healing practices. The curse tablets and voodoo dolls that are what we have left of the bindind spells were often made by professional artisans and writers for the participant in Greco-Roman magic.
