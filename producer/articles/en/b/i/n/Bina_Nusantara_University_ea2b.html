Bina Nusantara University (Binus) is a university in Jakarta, Indonesia. The university's primary field of study is in information technology.

[edit] History

Bina Nusantara University was originally founded as a short-term computer training institute, Modern Computer Course, October 21, 1974.

Rapid growth and people's interest led to the school's induction. Modern Computer Course had developed into the Computer Systems Academy (ATK) on July 1, 1981. It offered courses in Information Management and Information Technology. Three years after, on July 13, 1984 ATK acquired the "Registered" status and changed its name to AMIK Jakarta. On July 1, 1985, AMIK opened a course in Accounting Computerization. AMIK started using the name Bina Nusantara on September 21, 1985.

Ministry of Education and Culture awarded AMIK The Best Computer Academy through Higher Education Board District III on March 17, 1986[citation needed]. It established STMIK (Institute of Management and Computer Science) Bina Nusantara on July 1, 1986. This Institute offered Information Management and Computer Science Study Program (S-1) while Computer Systems Study Program (S-1) was still in process.

On November 9, 1987 AMIK Bina Nusantara was merged with STMIK Bina Nusantara. It created a single institute offering Diploma (D-3) and Strata 1 (S-1) programs. STMIK Bina Nusantara achieved "Accredited" status for all its programs and levels on March 18, 1992. Having already carved a good reputation, STMIK Bina Nusantara inaugurated its Master Program in Information System Management, the first of its kind in Indonesia on May 10, 1993.

Bina Nusantara University (UBiNus) was established on August 8, 1996 and then legalized by the Government. STMIK Bina Nusantara merged into Bina Nusantara University on December 20, 1998. Bina Nusantara University has 5 faculties: Computer Sciences, Economics, Engineering, Letters, and Mathematics & Statistics, and Masters Program.
