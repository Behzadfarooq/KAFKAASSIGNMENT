Bit cell

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

You have new messages (last change).

Bit cell is the length of tape, the area of disc surface, or the part of integrated circuit in which a single bit is recorded. The smaller the bit cells are, the better the storage density of the medium is.

In magnetic storage, the magnetic flux or magnetization doesn't necessarily change at the boundaries of bit cells to indicate bit states. For example, the precence of magnetic transition within a bit cell might record state 1, and the lack of such transition might record state 0. Other encodings are also possible.
