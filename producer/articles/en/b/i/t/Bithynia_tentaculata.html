Bithynia tentaculata, mud bithynia is species of freshwater gastropod from family Bithyniidae.

Forms:

  • Bithynia tentaculata f. codia
  • Bithynia tentaculata f. excavata
  • Bithynia tentaculata f. gigas
  • Bithynia tentaculata f. producta Menke, 1828

[edit] Description

foot of Bithynia tentaculata

Height of shell: 8-11 (15) mm. Width of shell: 5-7 mm.

[edit] Distribution and biotope

Distribuiton: palearctic.

  • Not listed in IUCN red list - not evaluated (NE) [1]
  • Canada - nonindigenous
  • The Czech Republic - least concern (LC) [2]
  • Germany - common species in the whole Germany, but it is in listed as endangered (gefährdet) in Sachsen and in Thüringen [3]
  • Greece
  • Netherlands [4]
  • Scandinavia
  • Slovakia
  • Ukraine
  • United states - nonindigenous [5]
  • ...

Biotope: freshwater slowly runing waters and standing water bodies.

[edit] Ecology

Parasites:

  • as first intermediate host for Prosthogonimus ovatus [6]
  • as first intermediate hosts and as second intermediate host for Cyanthocotyle bushiensis. [7]
  • as second intermediate host for Echinostoma revolutum [8]
  • intermediate host for Syngamus trachea [9]
  • Capillariidae, probably Capillaria obsignata [10]
  • Bithynia tenataculata is suspected intermediate host for Leyogonimus polyoon [11]
