Bitsie Tulloch

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Bitsie Tulloch is an American actress who is primarily known for her work on television shows Cold Case, The West Wing, and her role as R2D2's 'girlfriend' in R2-D2: Beneath the Dome, a mockumentary produced by George Lucas telling the life story of the fictional robot R2-D2 from the Star Wars film series. [1] She starred as 'Sally' in the world premiere of Sam Forman's new play, "Quarterlife", which opened at the Pico Playhouse in March 2006. The L.A. Times wrote, "Superbly performed...the scene at the end of the play, between Sally (Bitsie Tulloch) and Jack (Clark Freeman), was heartbreaking and beautiful...The lead actress, Bitsie Tulloch, [was] absolutely brilliant." [2] Nominated for a 28th Annual LA Weekly Theater Award for her performance (March 2007).


[edit] Early Life

Her first name is Elizabeth, Bitsie is a nickname. Bitsie grew up overseas in Spain, Uruguay, and Argentina. She went to high school in New York, and then went to Harvard University. Bitsie graduated Magna Cum Laude from Harvard with a double-major in English & American Literature, and Visual & Environmental Studies. Her senior year photography thesis entitled, "Turning Twenty," opened at the Carpenter Center Gallery in Boston to rave reviews. Bobby Iafolla of the Boston Weekly dig wrote, "Bitsie Tulloch's "Turning Twenty" is powerful, thought-provoking and unsettling in its portrayal of examination and self-examination." In the summer of 2002, she won the prestigious Emmy's Internship through the Academy of Television Arts & Sciences (ATAS).[3]

[edit] Where She Is Now

She recently finished filming the lead role in Ed Zwick and Marshall Herskovitz's pilot, "Quarterlife" (coincidentally the same name as the play she did in 2006, but they're unrelated).

On March 11, 2007, Bitsie premiered in the recurring role of "Alex" in the LonelyGirl15 series of online videos.
