I actually specifically avoided those icons. I may be misinterpreting the point of this template, but my understanding is that it is used for new users who have, for any of a variety of reasons, unwittingly chosen an improper username. If the block appeared immediately, then I'd agree with you absolutely - an x or other technical error-type icon would be appropriate. But if the block is delayed, the x and the tone come across as rude - given the amount of time that has passed, a human being must have made the decision that there was something wrong with the username. But perhaps no icon is needed at all.
The other aspect of the original template I tried to address was the assumption that a new user would know how to email an administrator or even how to use a talk page - the present template highlights the inexperience of the new user by implying that they should know how to do something that they probably have no idea of how to do. I may be overly sensitive (that wouldn't surprize me), but I worry about biting the newcomers.
Question about templates - I guess that the intention behind the template is to be able to just type {template XYZ} on the userpage and the template writes out everything else? --Badger151 02:25, 28 October 2006 (UTC)
