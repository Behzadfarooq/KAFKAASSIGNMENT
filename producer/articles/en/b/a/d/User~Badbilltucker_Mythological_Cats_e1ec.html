[edit] Alphabetical listing

[edit] A

  • Abasambo (also called Ikimizi, Bung Bung, Bakanga and Ntarargo) - from Ethiopian folklore. [1]
  • Artemis - turned herself into a cat & hid in the moon so Typhon wouldn't find her. [2]

[edit] B

  • Bakanga - myth of the Central African Republic. [3]
  • Bast - cat-goddess of women, children and the sun, from Egyptian mythology, also called Bastet, Ubasti, and Pasht. [4]
  • Butter cat - also known as Para and Smieragatto, benevolent cat from Scandinavian folklore. [5]

[edit] C

  • Cactus cat - cat with spines from stories of American frontiersmen. [6]
  • Cat Sidhe - fairy black cat with white locket believed to be a transformed witch, from Scottish folklore. [7]
  • Ccoa - demonic cat believed by the Quich√© Maya of Peru to bring hail and lightning to destroy crops. [8]

[edit] E

  • El Broosha - cat Lilith transformed into after leaving the Garden of Eden. [9]

[edit] M

  • Matagot - also called Chat D'Argent and Coste, a legendary French cat saig to bring gold to a home if it is is well fed. [10]
