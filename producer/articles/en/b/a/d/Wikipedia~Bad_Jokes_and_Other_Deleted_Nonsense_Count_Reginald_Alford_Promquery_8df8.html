Count Reginald Alford Promquery (April 20, 1353 – December 24, 1416) was born to Sir Hamilton Ian Truebred and Princess Isabell Snodgrass Soothspoon. The Count was born in a small town of Eastwood in Nottinghamshire, England. The Count of course, created his outlandish name and history to try to achieve certain social goals. But it is Geoffrey Chaucer's The Canterbury Tales, that Count Reginald Alford Promquery is best known for. It is in Chaucer's Tale of Sir Topas, that it is clear that Sir Topas is based on this seemingly random person.

[edit] Early Life

Since most of the life, even in an autobiographical since is fabricated, very little is known about the Count. Romantically, according to his own personal accounts, was quite cavalier. He claims to have defrocked "twenty young girls" in one letter, then claiming to have defrocked an "entire village" in another. However seemingly promiscuous his claims might be, there is no outside sources that will verify his outlandish comments. It is documented in numerous sources that he did, at least, claim to have slept with all three Popes (see Western Schism) and "did not like a single one". It was this outlandish remark that caused him to be forever banished from the Catholic Church. When asked about the Count, Pope Urban VI famously said "I hope that heretic burns in Hell".

[edit] Chaucer Encounter

It was, in fact, Chaucer who befriended Promquery at a chance meeting in a tavern close to Glastonbury. Chaucer found the Count to be outrageously provocative and vile, but despite all negative connotations, more unique than anything. The Count, allegedly, asked Chaucer to join him in his chamber upstairs. Chaucer, outraged, began to start a very violent brawl. It was during this fight that Count Reginald famously lost his left earlobe. After the two were separated it was evident that the had each found each other’s bane of existence.

[edit] Love Life

Promquery was a modern day playboy that was born in the fourteenth century. He showed no bias or restraint when it came to who or what he desired. It was very wrong then, and still is today, to have sex with the many things that he did. His various convulsions were condemned by virtually everyone he met. He notoriously proclaimed: “I have, by all accounts, penetrated every single living thing that was asking for it”. It was said that his semen was “the most potent of all” due to the amount of women he impregnated.

[edit] Death

It was his untimely death that actually inspired Chaucer to base a character after this very unique person. The Count had been suffering from many undiagnosed illnesses. (It is now believed that during his last months that Promquery was showing signs of Gonorrhea, Syphilis, Herpes, and Hepatitis). It is believed by many that he knew of his dwindling health, and that is what prompted such outlandish behavior. It was his death on Christmas Eve, that immortalized him in the annuls of history. In his last act of his life, Promquery got incredibly drunk and tried to climb the walls of the Tower of London to steal the Crown Jewels of the United Kingdom. He actually made it to the very top of the wall before he was shot an incredible 53 times by the guards on duty. After being ripped to shreds by the numerous musket balls, Count Reginald Alford Promquery fell to his death. (It is widely believed that the Count was the inspiration for the Nursery Rhyme 'Humpty Dumpty'.
