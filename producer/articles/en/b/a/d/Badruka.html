[edit] PREFACE

In year 1950, a proposal for establishing a commerce college in Hindi medium was mooted by a few philanthropists including Raja Bankatlal Badruka and Sri Pannalal Pittie. Nawab Ali Yawar Jung Bahadurji, the then Vice Chancellor of Osmania University appreciated the initiative and suggested that a college be started in English or Urdu medium with an endowment fund of Rs. 2.5 lakhs. Raja Bankatlalji, true to his character, spontaneously donated the amount and obtained the approval to start a college with Intermediate Section in English medium. The first exclusive institution for students of commerce came into existence on 2nd August 1950 and was inaugurated by the renowned celebrity Sri.M.K.Vellodi, ICS, the then Vice-Chancellor of Osmania University.

Initially, the trustees of Seth Gopikishan Educational and Charitable Trust were managing the activities of the College. In 1966, Seth Ghasiram Gopikishan Badruka Educational Society was established and was registered under Public Society Registration Act. Over the years, the Society's educational activities expanded. Today, there are 9 institutions of higher learning, managed by the Society covering such diverse areas as Information Technology, Economics, Commerce, Management, Charted Accountancy, Vocational courses, Music & Dance etc.

[edit] MISSION

To foster an intellectual and cultural environment in which the spirit and skill of the creative individual will thrive, research flourish, and the artistic, educational and business needs of the national and international community be served.


[edit] GOLDEN -JUBILEE YEAR


The year 2000-2001 was the Golden Jubilee year for the Badruka Educational Society. Over the years, more than 50,000 students have passed through the portals of Badruka Society and some of the Alumni are occupying high positions in India and abroad.

Thus, during the fifty years of fruitful existence, the Badruka Educational Society has seen a phenomenal growth of its Institutions.

1. Badruka College of Commerce (BCC) was established in the year 1950. The aim is to impart instruction of excellence in commerce education.

2. Badruka College Post Graduate Centre (BCPGC) was established in 1984. This College offers PG courses in Business Management, Commerce, Management Information Systems etc. The aim is to promote instruction of excellence in these courses.

3. Bankatlal Badruka College for Information Technology (BBCIT) was established in 1990. The main aim is to impart instruction of excellence in the area of IT.

4. Badruka Institute of Foreign Trade(BIFT) was established in 2001 with the purpose of imparting instruction of excellence in foreign trade and has a tie up with the Indian Institute of Foreign Trade(IIFT), New Delhi.

5. Badruka Institute of Professional Studies (BIPS) was established in 1990 and accredited to the Institute of Chartered Accountants of India(ICAI). The objective is to impart instruction of excellence in Chartered Accountancy,CS and ICWAI.

6. Badruka Junior College for Girls (BJCG) was established in 1995. Its aim is to serve the purpose of women education at plus 2 stage.


7. Bansilal Badruka School of Music and Dance(BBSMD) was established in 2001. This school imparts high professional skills in music & dance.

8. Badruka Institute of Foreign Education(BIFE) was established in 2001 to offer educational programmes from recited Institution-Universities across the globe

9. Shri Laxminivas Badruka Vidyarthi Gruha(SLBVG) was established in July 1996 The aim of starting this institution was to provide boarding and lodging facilities to outstation students during the academic year.


[edit] Bankatlal Badruka College for Information Technology (BBCIT)

Bankatlal Badruka College for Information Technology (BBCIT), named after the founder, was established in 1990 to impart instruction of excellence in the area of IT. The college has been able to carve niche for itself in the academic world in a short period of time due to hard work, dedication and scholarship of staff.
