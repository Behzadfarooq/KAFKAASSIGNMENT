Baradise is the name of the backyard owned by Kevin Baranek. The nickname was coined by Kevin "Milky" Staats after a particularly exhilarating game of wiffle ball, the sport Baradise is famous for. Baradise also features a swimming pool, lawn chairs, and many shrubs and trees.

While some consider Baradise the high point when it comes to Hunterdon County backyards, others point out its problems with bugs and losing wiffleballs.

[edit] Controversy

Baradise has recently come into the spotlight due to a disagreement over just what constitutes Baradise. The "Backyard Brave Bears", led by Baradise creater Kevin Staats and all-around good guy Marcus "Po" Krzastek contend that Baradise is made up of Kevin Baranek's backyard, and only the backyard. They argue that Baranek's house, while amazing in its own right, is not outstanding enough to warrant inclusion in the nickname. The "I Can't Believe I Want The Whole Thing...ers" led by Kevin Baranek himself as well as A.J. "The (Bela)Russian Rocket" Vaynerchuk and Tom "Bojangles" Borghard believe that Baradise is Kevin Baranek's entire property.

[edit] Wiffle Ball Field

  • Left Field: 63 feet
  • Dead Center: 55 feet
  • Right Field: There is no right field fence. Instead anything hit over the fence that lines the right of the field is foul.

[edit] Wiffle Ball Field Rules and Regulations

  1. Any ball hit that does not reach the mulched raised area on the fly and is recovered by a fielder before rolling stops is considered an out.
  2. Any ball hit that does not reach the mulched raised area on the fly but is not recovered by a fielder before movement stops is a single.
  3. Any ball that hits the face of the rock wall is a single.
  4. Any ball landing on the top of the rock wall or anywhere in the mulched area is a double.
  5. Any ball that hits the outfield fence on the fly is a triple.
  6. Any ball hit over the fence is a homerun.
  7. Batters are given three strikes. There are no called strikes, only swinging strikes. Balls are for communists.
  8. Teams are given two outs per inning.
  9. A ball hit on the ground that does not pass the light fixture is considered foul.
  10. A ball that is mishandled or dropped by a fielder is a hit. If a ball is dropped on the fly and it lands on the mulch, it is a double.
