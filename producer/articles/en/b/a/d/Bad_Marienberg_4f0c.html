Bad Marienberg is a town in the Westerwaldkreis area of the state of Rhineland-Palatinate, Germany.

[edit] Geography

The town is located in the Westerwald mountains between Limburg an der Lahn and Siegen. Past it, from east to west, flows the river Nister. Three villages belongs to the district of Bad Marienberg, named Eichenstruth, Langenbach und Zinhain.

Bad Marienberg is also the seat of the Verbandsgemeinde ("collective municipality") Bad Marienberg, which consists of the following Ortsgemeinden ("local municipalities"):

[edit] History

Bad Marienberg was the capital of the Oberwesterwaldkreis from 1866 to 1939.

[edit] Politics

[edit] Stadtrat(town council)

Bad Marienberg's town council has 23 members, and elects its mayor from among their number.

[edit] Mayor

As of 2004 Ernst-Dankwart Neufurth (SPD) was the mayor of Bad Marienberg.

[edit] City partnerships

Bad Marienberg has city partnerships with Marienberg in the Ore Mountains, and with Pagny-sur-Moselle in France.
