Bad Camberg

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Bad Camberg is a town in the Limburg-Weilburg district, in Hesse, Germany. It is situated 18 km southeast of Limburg an der Lahn, and 44 km northwest of Frankfurt. Bad Camberg is the second largest city in the district Limburg because of its castle; as well as the southernmost city in the Limburg-Weilgurg governmental district. It lies in the western Hintertaunus. The recognized Kneippkurort is the oldest in Hessen and the third-oldest in Germany. The landmark of the city is the cross chapel. Bad Camberg lies at approx. 200 m altitude, on the north of the main comb of the Taunus, between Wiesbaden and Limburg. The highest collection is with 526 meters height the "cow bed".

Closer larger cities are Frankfurt (44 km southeast, 60 km over motorway), Wiesbaden (30 km south) and Limburg (18 km northwest).

On 6 February 1000 the emperors Otto III gave the monastery of Burtscheid the possession Cagenberg. From the name Cagenberg developed Cainburg, Camburg, changing to mountain (berg) and Camberg. In the year 1281 Rudolf lent I of municipal rights, which were confirmed 1300, 1336 and 1365.

Bad Camberg borders in the north on the municipality Selters, in the east on the municipality Weilrod, in the southeast on the municipality Waldems, in the south on the city Idstein, in the southwest on the municipality Huenstetten (all three in the Rhine gau rope US circle), as well as in the west on the municipality Huenfelden (district Limburg because castle).

Except the municipalities belonging to the circle Limburg because castle all Bad Camberger neighbour municipalities is not part of the governmental district, but belongs to south Hessen.
