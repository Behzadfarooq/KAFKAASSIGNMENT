Bad Medicine is a 1985 comedy film starring Steve Guttenberg, Alan Arkin, and Julie Hagerty. The film was written and directed by Harvey Miller, and was based on the novel Calling Dr. Horowitz, by Steven Horowitz and Neil Offen.

The film was criticized for its negative ethnic stereotypes. It was released by 20th Century Fox, and was one of three films that Hagerty starred in 1985 (Lost in America and Goodbye, New York were the other two).

[edit] Plot

Bad Medicine concerns a below-average medical student named Jeffrey Marx (Guttenberg) who, after being rejected by prestigious medical schools, is sent by his father (Bill Macy) to a seemingly sub-standard medical school in Central America. Jeffery eventually discovers the plight of local villagers in need of medical assistance and, with the help of fellow students (including romantic interest Hagerty), illegally provides medical services to the villagers. Eventually, the school's authoritarian dean (Arkin) discovers the students' activities, and decides to prosecute. However, the dean dismisses the charges at the urging of the villagers.
