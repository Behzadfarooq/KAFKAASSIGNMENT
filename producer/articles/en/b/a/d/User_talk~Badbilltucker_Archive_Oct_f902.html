[edit] Work via WikiProjects

Hi Bad Bill, I just read your latest comments at Wikipedia_talk:WikiProject_Council#Public_Relations. A lot of what you are proposing is already being done, albeit slowly, at Work via WikiProjects. You can see a typical posting and response at the Anglicanism project, and the resultant worklist here. Likewise the Cathlicism is generating their worklist via the bot. All of this information is then collected here and summarized here (this last one needs an update). Our main goal is to get lists of the best and most important articles from all WikiProjects, then try to put those all together for a CD, DVD or paper version of Wikipedia. The secondary goal is essentially the same as the new WP Council, to help projects (and notice boards) talk to each other and share ideas - and this has indeed happened. We have already contacted most of the projects that were listed on the main list about a year ago, this posting is in fact our "second round".

I was wondering if you might be interested in helping out with this project. We have only had a handful of people, typically only 2 or 3 active people at any time, and only one (me!) active right now. At the same time, this small project helped to promote the idea of article assessments and key articles, and was instrumental in arranging for Mathbot to work for the WikiProjects. So I think the time invested in this project has been extremely fruitful so far - and, quite frankly, exciting at times. We have also already laid the foundations for the inter-project discussions you were proposing. Would you be interested in working on this a bit? I'd really appreciate some help! Thanks, Walkerma 23:13, 27 August 2006 (UTC)

The other main person likely to work on WVWP is Tito (User:Titoxd) and he is on the council too, so it won't upset anyone.
