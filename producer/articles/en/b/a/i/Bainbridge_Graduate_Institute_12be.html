The Bainbridge Graduate Institute on Bainbridge Island, Washington, USA, was founded in 2002. It offers a two or three-year executive MBA program in Sustainable Business. Its purpose is to transform business toward sustainability and social justice through business education.

Elizabeth and Gifford Pinchot III and Dr. Sherman Severin founded BGI to prepare diverse leaders to build enterprises that are economically successful, socially responsible and environmentally sustainable. Combining their collective expertise in business innovation and business education, they designed BGI to offer world-class sustainable innovation and management training.

BGI was recognized as the #1 MBA program in NetImpact's (www.netimpact.org) report: "Business as UNusual: The 2006 Net Impact Student Guide to Graduate Business Programs". BGI was rated # 1 in 12 of the 20 factors surveyed. In 2007, BGI has 144 students and has graduated its third class.

BGI's student body has a average age of 37, and a range from 22 to 66. The two year MBA program includes 9 intensives per year from Thursday at 1:00pm to Sunday noon with an active online learing community between intensives. BGI's high-involvement learning community is the aspect of BGI its students most often mention when describing what is different about BGI.

BGI's adult learning philosophy includes liberal use of action learning projects in which students apply what they learn to real world challenges. This includes solving operational problems, measuring sustainability practices in real organizations and creating business plans for sustainable enterprises that students plan to launch.

The majority of BGI students hold full time jobs. They come from all parts of the US, with about three quarters coming from within 500 miles of Seattle.

In addition to its MBA program, BGI also offers two one-year Certificate programs, one in Sustainable Business and the other in Sustainable Entrepreneurship and Intrapreneurship. BGI is in process of seeking accreditation.
