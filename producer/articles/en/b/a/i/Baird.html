Baird

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

The name 'Baird' is a surname of primarily Scottish origins although some historians claim the Bairds originally came to Scotland from France around the time of William the Conqueror. An old legend says that the family obtained their lands in Scotland when one Baird rescued King William the Lion while he was being attacked by a wild boar. While the validity of that legend is unknown the Baird family did obtain lands in Aberdeenshire.
