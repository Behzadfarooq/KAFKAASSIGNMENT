Baile (Spanish play)

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

You have new messages (last change).

A baile (which means 'dance' in Spanish) is a short theatrical piece in the Spanish Golden Age (Siglo de Oro) tradition. It consists of an elaborate production number with singing and dancing, and is used between the acts of a comedia in place of the usual entremés.
