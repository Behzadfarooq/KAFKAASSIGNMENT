Basement waterproofing is needed in basements where outside water (such as: rain, melting ice, flooding) gets in through high water table, footing, cracked walls, plumbing leaks, etc. There are several ways to waterproof basements. Some common methods are excavating and using exterior drain tiles, interior water drainage system, spray painting basement walls, etc.

Spray painting basement walls is the least effective and least expensive solution.

An interior basement waterproofing system works by draining water from around the perimeter of the basement to a sump pump. This lowers the hydorstatic pressure and keeps the floor dry. It is the easiest system to service if there are any problems, and the least likely to clog. Wall leaks can be addressed with various membranes, some for energy efficiency and some for visual appeal, that drain down into the interior drain system. The costs of an interior system are moderate. This could be very ugly if done wrong, however it could work wonders and look great if professionals work on the basement and do it the right way.

An alternative method, and typically the most expensive solution, is to install an exterior foundation drain around the perimeter of the house that drains to a sump pump located inside the basement (on rare occasions where the yard around the house is sloped and there is room to trench out a drain across the yard, a sump pump is not necessary). This is done by excavating to the base of the foundation (unfortunately you must remove the driveway, landscaping, deck, porch, and anything else that sits within 8-feet from the foundation perimeter), applying a waterproof and vapor proof membrane to the exterior walls, and installing protective filter fabric, perforated PVC pipe, and plenty of crushed stone.

A homeowner should understand that this is the same waterproofing method that originally failed and why they now have a wet basement. The PVC pipe and filter sitting in the mud clog over time (even within the first year). The biggest issue with any filter is that it needs to be changed once it gets dirty, but this type of system would require excavation to get at the filter. Homeowners should also be aware that servicing any warranty associated with an exterior installation requires the same excavating process of digging down to the base of the foundation again.

Interior waterproofing, especially a system like WaterGuard that is highly clog resistent, is the best and most reliable method of waterproofing a basement.
