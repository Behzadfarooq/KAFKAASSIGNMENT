Bashkaleh Resistance
Date May, 1889
Location Van Province
Result Political conflict and armed conflicts over the martyrdom.
Combatants
Ottoman Empire Comrades of Armenakan party

The Bashkaleh Resistance (Turkish: Başkale çatışması) was the bloody encounter between the Armenakans and the Ottoman Empire on May 1889. Even though it is named as Bashkaleh Resistance, the event happaned in the Van Province. The event was important as it was reflected on main Armenian newspapers as the recovered documents on the Armenakans showed an extensive plot for an uprising. The event was first of the conflicts (Ottomans name it as terrorism).[1]

[edit] The event

The comrades Karapet Koulaksizian, Hovhannes Agripasian, and Vardan Goloshian, left the village of Haftvan (Salmast district of Persia), for Van on May 16, 1889. They were stopped near Van by Ottoman police. The police demanded that they disarm to protect the accompanying caravan. In the conflict Goloshian and Agripasian died and Koulaksizian escaped. The police recovered two letters (accompanying documents) addressed to Koulaksizian, one from Avetis Patiguian of London and the other from Mekertitch Portugalian in Marseille.

[edit] Reflections

Ottoman Empire believed that the men were members of a large revolutionary apparatus and the discussion was reflected on newspapers, (Eastern Express, Oriental Advertiser, Saadet, and Tarik) and the responses were on the Armenian papers. In some Armenian circles, this event was considered as a martyrdom and brought other armed conflicts.[2]
