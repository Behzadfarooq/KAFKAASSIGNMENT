[edit] 1960

[edit] Events

  • 25 March – Representing Luxembourg, Camillo Felgen finishes thirteen (and last) in the Eurovision Song Contest 1960 with the song So Laang We's Du Do Bast.
  • 4 July – The completed grounds of the Luxembourg American Cemetery and Memorial are dedicated.

[edit] Births

  • 30 January – Claude Wiseler
  • 10 May – Sophie, Princess of Hohenberg
  • 3 July – Carlo Weis
  • 29 November – Claude Turmes
  • 4 December – Eugène Berger

[edit] 1961

[edit] Events

  • 13 March – Representing Luxembourg, Jean-Claude Pascal wins the Eurovision Song Contest 1961 with the song Nous les Amoureux.
  • 28 April – Grand Duchess Charlotte declares her eldest son, Hereditary Grand Duke Jean, to be her Lieutenant Representative.

[edit] Births

  • 17 August – Lydia Mutsch

[edit] 1962

[edit] Events

  • 18 March – Luxembourg City hosts the Eurovision Song Contest 1962 after Jean-Claude Pascal's victory the previous year. Representing Luxembourg, Camillo Felgen finished third with the song Petit Bonhomme.

[edit] Births

  • 13 September – Michel Wolter

[edit] 1963

[edit] Events

  • 23 March – Representing Luxembourg, Nana Mouskouri finishes eighth in the Eurovision Song Contest 1963 with the song A Force De Prier.
  • 9 October – Jeunesse Esch beat FC Haka 4-0 to become the only Luxembourgian side to progress to the last sixteen of the European Cup.
  • 30 October – The Luxembourg national football team knocks the Netherlands out of the 1964 European Nations Cup, winning 2-1 in Rotterdam having drawn 1-1 in Amsterdam on 11 September.
  • 4 November – President Lyndon B. Johnson visits Luxembourg.

[edit] Births

  • 1 May – Prince Guillaume of Luxembourg
  • 18 July – Marc Girardelli

[edit] 1964

[edit] Events

  • 21 March – Representing Luxembourg, Hugues Aufray finishes fourth in the Eurovision Song Contest 1964 with the song Des Que Le Printemps Revient.
  • 12 November – Grand Duchess Charlotte abdicates. Her eldest son, Hereditary Grand Duke Jean succeeds her.

[edit] Births

  • 16 June – Yolande, Princess of Ligne
  • 14 August – Jean-Louis Schiltz

[edit] 1965

[edit] Events

  • 20 March – Representing Luxembourg, France Gall wins the Eurovision Song Contest 1965 with the song Poupée de cire, poupée de son.

[edit] Births

  • Unknown – Georges Lentz

[edit] Deaths

  • 10 October – Claus Cito

[edit] 1965

[edit] Events

  • 5 March – Luxembourg City hosts the Eurovision Song Contest 1966 after France Gall's victory the previous year. Representing Luxembourg, Michele Torr finishes tenth with the song Ce soir je t'attendais.

[edit] Births

  • 15 November – Octavie Modert

[edit] 1967

[edit] Events

  • 4 April – Representing Luxembourg, Vicky Leandros finishes fourth in the Eurovision Song Contest 1967 with the song L'amour est bleu.

[edit] Births

  • Unknown – Michel Majerus

[edit] Deaths

  • 19 August – Hugo Gernsback

[edit] 1968

[edit] Events

  • 6 April – Representing Luxembourg, Romuald finishes eleventh in the Eurovision Song Contest 1968 with the song Catherine.

[edit] Births

  • 12 June – Princess Sibilla of Luxembourg

[edit] 1969

[edit] Events

  • 29 March – Representing Luxembourg, Chris Baldo and Sophie Garel finish eleventh in the Eurovision Song Contest 1969 with the song Nous Vivrons D'amour.
  • 22 November – A Luxair flight from Frankfurt hits a snowback upon touchdown at Luxembourg-Findel. No-one is killed, but the plane is written off.

[edit] Births

  • 22 May – Nancy Kemp-Arendt
