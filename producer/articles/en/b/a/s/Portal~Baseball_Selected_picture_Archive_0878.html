[edit] February 21 - March 4, 2007

Ebbets Field was a Major League Baseball park located in the Flatbush section of Brooklyn, New York. It was the home of the Brooklyn Dodgers of the National League. Two different incarnations of a Brooklyn Dodgers football team also played at the stadium. The park opened on April 9, 1913, and was demolished in 1960, three years after the team relocated to Los Angeles.

[edit] March 5 - March 11, 2007

Mordecai "Three Finger" Brown was a pitcher, primarily for the Chicago Cubs, in the early part of the 20th century. Brown lost parts of two fingers in a childhood accident with a farm machine. His unique grip resulted in an usual amount of spin on his pitches. The extra topspin made it difficult for batters to connect solidly with the ball. His has the sixth-lowest career earned run average in Major League history, and was elected to the Hall of Fame in 1949.
