Basarabi (IPA: [ba.sa.'rabʲ], Murfatlar until 1924 and between 1965–1980) is a town in Constanţa County, Romania. It is a port on the Danube-Black Sea Canal and has a population of 11,070. A complex of caves was found carved in the hills nearby, see the Basarabi Cave Complex.

Basarabi is also the birth place of the Romanian president, Traian Băsescu.

The village of Siminoc (historical name: Turc-Murfat) is administered by the town of Basarabi. The name of the village comes from the flower Helichrysum arenarium (siminoc in Romanian), which can be found abundantly in the area.[1]

The Murfatlar Vineyard is located nearby.
