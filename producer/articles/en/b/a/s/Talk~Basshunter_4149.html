A Danish remix has been made by the Danish Internet radio ''MMradio.org''. The remix is a parody, based on the fact that a lot of people think the song is about a boat, instead of a bot. Hence, the parody is about a boat, which further heightens the misunderstanding. The producers who made it never told what they would like to call it and thus received a name identifying Anna as a boat by a listener upon its first airing. [edit] True or not?

Vi sitter i Ventrilo och spelar DotA

This song is about BassHunter and his friends, using the voice communication application Ventrilo while they are playing DotA, a custom map for Warcraft 3. It's a eurodance song, very similar to Daddy DJ's song Daddy DJ or Scatman.

This was added at the bottom of the article.. Considering there is really no mention to DotA in the song, does this have any basis in fact? If somone else agrees, please delete it. —The preceding unsigned comment was added by 216.196.176.249 (talk • contribs).

Ehh, the song has DotA in the name. Doesn't really get more obvious than that. Also, the extended version has sounds from the game. —The preceding unsigned comment was added by 81.231.243.116 (talk • contribs).

Yeah its true, watch the video clip. The lyric is 'Vi sitter har i venten och spelar lite dota' which translated means something like 'we play dota and talk/sit on vent'. The videoclip features a few pieces of warcraft iii gameplay which appears to be dota. - 134.7.77.147 08:20, 7 September 2006 (UTC)

According to this subtitled video < http://basshunter.m0o.eu/dota/index_en.php > "Vi sitter har i venten och spelar lite dota" translates to "We're sitting here in Ventrilo playing some DotA." Xchbla423 17:37, 12 September 2006 (UTC)

[edit] IKEA

The IKEA website features a bot called Anna. Is this a coincidence? ABoerma 03:09, 3 September 2006 (UTC)

I doubt it. Anna is an extremely common girl's name in Sweden, good for about 8% of the Swedish women. Han-Kwang 22:02, 3 September 2006 (UTC)
Anna is the second most common first name among women in Sweden. But I must say it's a funny coincidence! Does other countries IKEA pages also have the Anna bot? abelson 11:54, 4 September 2006 (UTC)
Cool, they do! Check out the American IKEA bot Anna abelson 11:57, 4 September 2006 (UTC)
Yes, it's a coincidence. As Abelson already mentioned, it's a very common Swedish name and it makes sense naming a bot Anna if you're after the image of being a Swedish company like IKEA is. The bot itself is also not famous by any means, as far as I can tell. -Obli (Talk)? 13:37, 7 September 2006 (UTC)
