Basingstoke railway station is a railway station in the town of Basingstoke in the county of Hampshire in England. The station is the terminus of the Basingstoke Branch from Reading, served by both local services operated by First Great Western.The station is also on the South Western Main Line from London Waterloo, which is served by both local and fast services operated by South West Trains. Long distance cross country services operated by Virgin Trains from Birmingham - and points north - to Bournemouth switch from the branch to the main line here.

[edit] History

see Basingstoke’s railway history in maps

In 2001, a suitcase was left outside the station containing the mutilated body of a man in his twenties. He was stabbed to death.

[edit] Engineering Works

During May and November 2006 the station was closed at weekends to allow for junction renewal and signalling works, similar to the works which took place near Woking railway station during December 2005. Further closures for the benefit of engineering works are planned for Spring 2007. The works include moving the signal box, and junction renewal works to the West of England Main Line.

[edit] Interesting facts

Basingstoke station was formerly the terminus of the Basingstoke and Alton Light Railway and a short length of track is preserved on the Viables Roundabout in Basingstoke, which is near the former Cliddesden station.

[edit] Platforms

The Station has 5 platforms in use. Typical platforms uses for the majority of the day are listed below.

  • Platform 1 - Terminating slow services to/from London Waterloo, Local Southampton and South Coast stopping services and Virgin Trains services to Bournemouth.
  • Platform 2 - Mainline services to Salisbury and West Country, Winchester, Southampton and South Coast bound trains all from London Waterloo. Freight Trains from the Midlands pass through this platform on the way to Southampton and Bournemouth docks.
  • Platform 3 - Semi-fast and fast services to London Waterloo from the south coast and west country.
  • Platform 4 - Stopping London Waterloo bound trains, South West Trains services from Brighton to Reading and Virgin Trains services from Bournemouth to the Midlands and beond. Freight trains from Southampton and Bournmouth docks going to the Midlands, go through this platform.
  • Platform 5 - Local services To/From Reading, First Great Western
