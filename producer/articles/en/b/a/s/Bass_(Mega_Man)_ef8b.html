Bass, known as Forte in Japan, is a video game character, designed to be a rival to the character Mega Man. Bass is a fiercely independent and rebellious robot, who is obsessed with the thought of defeating Mega Man in battle so that he can prove that he is - as he claims to be - the strongest robot on Earth. The only friend he seems to have, is his loyal purple wolf robot, Treble (known as "Gospel" in Japan). Treble is the dark equivalent of Rush, and is able to 'merge' with his master, allowing Bass to take flight and shoot more powerful projectiles from his buster (arm cannon) for a limited amount of time.

Bass' name originates from the musical term, bass (most of the main robots in the series are named after musical terms), as well as Treble's name that also originated from the musical term, treble. His Japanese name, Forte, also has musical origins, which in notation indicates a high volume (suitable for Bass), as does his wolfbot's Japanese name, Gospel. He wears black armor, which has gold accents and markings, along with a large, rounded-triangular blue "gem" on the chest part. Under the armor, Bass wears a jumpsuit which is grey in color. He also wears a distinctive helmet (which, as is to be expected, matches his armour in general color-scheme and design), which has two large "fins" on it, and a star-shaped blue "jewel" on the part which covers his forehead. This shape seems to resemble a cobra's head. Last of all, he has a pair of purple marks on his face - one stripe on each cheek. His pupils are a distinctive red color, although they appeared black in his debut in Mega Man 7 due to the color limitation on the sprites. It is indicated in the Continue screen of Mega Man and Bass that he can remove his helmet, although he himself has not appeared in a game without it as of yet.

[edit] History

[edit] Classic series

Bass was first introduced in Mega Man 7 (SNES, 1995) as a "potential" ally (he attempted, and somewhat succeeded in tricking Mega Man into believing that he was on his side). Later in the same game, he pretends to be injured, and fools Mega Man into sending him to Dr. Light's lab for repairs. When Mega Man returns to the lab, and finds it in ruins, Dr. Light explains that Bass went berserk, destroyed the lab, and stole the plans for the Rush adapter. He is revealed to be a creation of Doctor Wily, and is later fought twice in the final stages, with Bass using his new "Treble Adaptor" in the second battle. He is also playable in a hidden Street Fighter-esque mini-game in Mega Man 7.

Bass then appeared in Mega Man 8 (Sony PlayStation/Sega Saturn 1997), and was a playable character in Mega Man & Bass (Nintendo Game Boy Advance 2003; originally released as Rockman & Forte for the Super Famicom in 1998). He has also appeared in multiple side-games, such as Mega Man Battle & Chase (Sony PlayStation, 1998 - Europe and Japan, and included in Mega Man X Collection), and a follow up to this game, Rockman GP, which was released exclusively for Japanese cellphones in 2004.

In Mega Man 2: The Power Fighters, Dr. Wily reveals that Bass' power comes from an energy source dubbed "Bassnium" (known as Fortenium in Japan) - that he discovered by accident when trying to create a robot to rival Mega Man. He also reveals that he feels that creating Bass was a mistake, due to Bass' belief that only he can destroy Mega Man, and utter disregard for his creator.

Bass' fate, like many characters in the original series, is unknown in the Mega Man X series.

[edit] Abilities

Bass is notably based upon Mega Man. He possesses a similar weapons plan that allows him to absorb the powers of his fallen foes, just like Mega Man. He can also fuse with his dog Treble in a form called the Treble Boost, a form which includes an angelic wing-jetpack and crest-like helmet, after stealing the technology built to let Mega Man merge with Rush. Oddly enough, after Mega Man 7, the so-called "Rush Adaptor" is no longer seen, although Bass still merges with Treble on occasion. Combined with Treble, Bass is capable of flight, and in Mega Man 7, he can fire a homing missile in addition to his normal attacks. In Mega Man and Bass, he fires a triple shot that is twice as strong as his normal buster.

[edit] Trivia

  • In Mega Man 2: The Power Fighters, Bass sees Wily working on his newest robot, an early build of Zero. When Wily tells him that this new robot, though he won't be completed for a long time, will be much stronger than him, Bass scoffs and calls it a "girly-looking, long-haired robot".
  • Bass's opinion of humans seems to be one of limited respect, as he tells King, in Mega Man and Bass, that he shouldn't try to wipe out humans because humans created robots.
  • Bass's main objective is actually becoming the strongest, not defeating Mega Man. Since he was defeated by Mega Man he has simply switched his desire to defeating Mega Man for the time being.
  • Mega Man constantly attempts to reason with and befriend Bass, believing Bass to be a good robot; however, Bass always rejects these actions of kindness, although he has shown some honorable qualities.
  • Some fans[citation needed] view Bass as an anti-hero instead of a real villain, partly because Mega Man wants Bass to become one of the good guys, and partly because Bass has often fought on the side of the good guys against Dr. Wily.
  • Bass uses a capsule of Evil Energy to transform with Treble in Mega Man 8. It is unknown what became of the Evil Energy he used; some theories say Duo purged him of it, while others say he still has it.
  • It is possible that Bass's different form when merged with Treble seen in Mega Man 8 and Mega Man and Bass is caused by the evil energy, the form he takes in the eighth game when he merges with Treble is very different then the form he takes when he merges with Treble in the seventh game, however this could also be because of artistic differences in graphic styles just like Mega Man and Bass do between Mega Man 7 and Mega Man 8 / Mega Man & Bass.
  • Although Bass was designed using Mega Man's design in order to create a rival robot, he somewhat looks more like X (from Mega Man X) with Sigma's purple eye-scars.
  • The only reason Bass entered the race in the game Mega Man Battle and Chase was for the chance of beating Mega Man, in his ending however Bass realizes that a race proves who's faster, not who's stronger, tossing the prize money over his shoulder Bass simply walks off in anger.
