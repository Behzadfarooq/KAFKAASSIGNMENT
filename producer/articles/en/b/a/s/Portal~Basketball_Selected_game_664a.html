In the Games of the XXth Olympiad, held in 1972 in Munich, West Germany, the Soviet Union and United States dominated round-robin pool play; the Soviet Union won all seven of its Group B games, outscoring opponents by 160 points, while the United States won all seven of its Group A games, outscoring opponents by 230 points. The Americans, coached by Henry Iba and starring Tommy Burleson, Doug Collins, Dwight Jones, and Mike Bantom, and the Soviets, trained by Aleksandr Gomelsky and led by Sergei Belov, easily reached the finals of the basketball tournament and played a hotly-contested game.

The United States trailed, 49-48, with fewer than 10 seconds to play, but Thomas McMillen blocked a Belov shot and Collins stole the ball. Collins attempted a layup and was fouled; he successfully converted the two ensuing free throws to give the United States a one-point lead. The United States appeared to win when the Soviets, inbounding the ball with fewer than three seconds in the game, failed to score before the horn sounded, but one official announced that he had blown his whistle to stop play with one second remaining in view of a disturbance on the sideline. The Soviet Union claimed also to have requested a timeout, and the officials determined that three seconds ought to be put on the clock. While the time was being added, the ball was inbounded, but the Soviet pass was errant and the horn once more blew.

Renato William Jones, the secretary general of the International Basketball Federation, who was seated in the arena, determined that the clock had not properly been reset and thus ordered three seconds placed on the clock; this time, a pass from Ivan Edeshko found Belov, who laid the ball into the goal as time expired to give the Soviets a 51-50 victory.

After an American protest was rejected, the members of the American team, believing the Jones intervention to have been improper, resolved not to accept their silver medals; the International Olympic Committee sought in 2004 to award the medals, but the surviving players once more declined.
