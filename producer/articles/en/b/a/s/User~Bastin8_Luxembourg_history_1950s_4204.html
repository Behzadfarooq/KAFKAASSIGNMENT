[edit] 1950

[edit] Events

  • 9 May – Foreign Minister Robert Schuman proposes the European Coal and Steel Community.
  • 25 June – The Korean War begins. Luxembourg pledges its support to the United Nations.
  • 17 August – Princess Alix marries Antoine Maria Joachim Lamoral, 14th Prince of Ligne.
  • 2 October – The first Luxembourgian soldiers depart for Korea.
  • Unknown – Goodyear tyre factory is opened at Colmar-Berg.

[edit] Births

  • 19 May – Anne Brasseur
  • 16 July – Henri Grethen
  • Unknown – Mady Delvaux-Stehres

[edit] Deaths

  • 1 February – Pierre Prüm

[edit] 1951

[edit] Events

  • 20 March – The United States is given right to use the land of the Luxembourg American Cemetery and Memorial at Hamm in perpetuity.
  • 18 April – Luxembourg and five other countries sign the Treaty of Paris, establishing the European Coal and Steel Community.
  • 10 August – The first meeting of the High Authority of the ECSC is held, in Luxembourg City.
  • 17 August – Prince Sigvard, Duke of Uppland, Carl Johan Bernadotte, and Prince Lennart, Duke of Småland are created Counts Bernadotte af Wisborg.

[edit] Births

  • 27 April – Viviane Reding
  • 26 May – Michel, 14th Prince of Ligne

[edit] 1952

[edit] Events

  • Unknown – Josy Barthel wins a gold medal in the Men's 1,500 m at 1952 Summer Olympics.

[edit] Births

  • 6 February – Charles Goerens
  • 27 June – Mars Di Bartolomeo
  • 10 July – Wauthier, Prince of Ligne
  • 22 November – Lydie Polfer

[edit] Deaths

  • 25 March – Léon Kauffmann

[edit] 1953

[edit] Events

  • 9 April – Hereditary Grand Duke Jean marries Joséphine-Charlotte of Belgium.
  • 24 June – A law is passed to build a hydroelectric dam on the Sauer, creating the Upper Sauer Lake.

[edit] Births

  • 3 July – Carlo Wagner

[edit] Deaths

  • 4 October – Prince Oscar, Duke of Gotland
  • 23 December – Pierre Dupong

[edit] 1954

[edit] Births

  • 17 February – Archduchess Marie Astrid of Austria
  • 3 April – Anne-Marie, Princess of Ligne
  • 9 December – Jean-Claude Juncker

[edit] Deaths

  • 31 July – Antoinette, Crown Princess of Bavaria

[edit] 1955

[edit] Births

  • 16 April – Henri, Grand Duke of Luxembourg
  • 11 August – Christine, Princess de Ligne

[edit] 1956

[edit] Events

  • 9 May – Princess Elisabeth marries Franz, Duke of Hohenberg.
  • 24 May – Representing Luxembourg, Michèle Arnaud competes in the inaugural Eurovision Song Contest with the song Les amants de minuit. Her finishing position is unknown.
  • 29 September – The new electrified railways from Luxembourg City to Arlon (Belgium) and Thionville (France) are opened.
  • Unknown – Charly Gaul wins the 1956 Giro d'Italia.

[edit] Births

  • 22 March – Maria Teresa, Grand Duchess of Luxembourg
  • 13 September – Lucien Lux

[edit] 1957

[edit] Events

  • 3 March – Representing Luxembourg, Danièle Dupré finishes joint-fourth in the Eurovision Song Contest 1957 with the song Amours Mortes.
  • 25 March – The Treaty of Rome, establishing the European Economic Community and Euratom, is signed.
  • July – RTL Télé Lëtzebuerg is launched.

[edit] Births

  • 1 February – Jean-Marie Halsdorf
  • 23 April – Sophie, Princess of Ligne
  • 15 May – Prince Jean of Luxembourg
  • 15 May – Princess Margaretha of Liechtenstein

[edit] 1958

[edit] Events

  • 12 March – Representing Luxembourg, Solange Berry finishes joint-ninth in the Eurovision Song Contest 1958 with the song Un Grand Amour.
  • 19 July– Charly Gaul wins the 1958 Tour de France.

[edit] Births

  • 18 August – Anita, Princess of Hohenberg
  • 28 September – François Biltgen
  • 3 October – Alex Bodry

[edit] Deaths

  • 27 April – Michel Rasquin
