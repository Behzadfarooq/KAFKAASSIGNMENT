[edit] Comments for users concerned about "spam"

As you can see from the discussion above, the external links that I have added to many plant and ecoregion pages may have the appearance of "spam". Before any hasty attempts to remove those links, please examine them and you will find that they are NOT spam, but rather useful references to image content that is reliable, non-commercial, often not at all represented in Wikipedia, and in some cases unique on the Internet. Because of licensing incompatabilities between Wikipedia and the Bioimages website (http://bioimages.vanderbilt.edu), I can't upload the images to the Wikipedia commons. However, all of the images are freely available for personal and education use.

[edit] Euonymus & ext links

Hi Baskaufs - sorry not to have got back to you before on your points raised, I only just saw them now (I don't look at the spindle page very often). On the links, it looks like Mwanner has already mentioned the general wiki policy that adding links without content is generally not liked. Personally, I think your links are good additions to individual species articles of American native species; on genus pages, I, like Mwanner, would rather encourage you to start at least stub pages for the species you add links for, rather than just adding the links at the genus page. On links to your site for species not native to North America, I feel these are a little less appropriate as they may confuse readers as to the origin and status of the species, and risk giving an inappropriate American point of view to a European or Asian, etc., topic.

On the names I removed, I did this because there is really only room in the list for a single common name for each species; adding two or more messes up the formatting (except for people with very wide computer screens!). Again, the individual species page is the place to list additional common names; if the species page is not there, why not start it? It is as simple as clicking on the red link, and start typing - one tip for an easy way to do it is copy the text from a related species which already has a page (e.g. European spindle), paste it in, and then change the relevant details (names, distribution, leaf dimensions, etc); anything that's not relevant on the new page just delete.

BTW, the spindle (shrub) page is now moved to the scientific name, to avoid the different name uses between UK/US (I thought 'spindle' was used on both sides of the Atlantic). Hope this helps, and again, sorry for not contacting you earlier - MPF 18:34, 3 June 2006 (UTC)
