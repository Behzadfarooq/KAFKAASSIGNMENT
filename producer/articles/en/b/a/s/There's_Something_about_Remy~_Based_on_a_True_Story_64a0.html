There's Something About Remy: Based on a True Story is Remy Ma's debut album which was released on February 7, 2006 (it was released on the 6th anniversary of Big Pun's death).

It features R&B Singer Keyshia Cole, new artist Ne-Yo, late MC Big Pun, Fat Joe (with whom she is in Terror Squad with), and Ivy Queen. The album is mainly rap and hip-hop mixed with a little bit of modern R&B. Growing up in the rough surroundings of the Bronx inspired many of her songs, which deal with everything from a hit & run accident to an early pregnancy to objectifying women to a friendship between a Puerto Rican girl and an African-American girl.

The album debuted at No.33 on the Billboard 200 albums chart selling 37,000 copies in its opening week. The next week it followed-up with 6,000 copies. It soon fell out of the top 200 albums chart in just three weeks. Since July 14, 2006 her album has sold over 125,000 units in the U.S. since its release on Soundscan.

The album has been removed from some stores because of its disappointing sales. In May they decided to release one or two more singles from the album. They also want Remy to immediately start working on a new album with hardcore, East Coast hip hop.

The new singles will be Feels So Good and the airplay/promotional single Thug Love which both were released June 30, 2006. Neither one of them are a success and are not improving the album's sales.
