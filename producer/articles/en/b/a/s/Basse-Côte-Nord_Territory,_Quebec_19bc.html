Basse-Côte-Nord Territory, Quebec

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Basse-Côte-Nord Territory (fr. Territoire de la Basse-Côte-Nord) is a census division in eastern Quebec, Canada on the Gulf of Saint Lawrence. It includes all the communities along the Gulf of Saint Lawrence between the Natashquan River and the Newfoundland and Labrador border. It separated from Minganie Regional County Municipality in 2002, and consists of the following municipalities:

  • Blanc-Sablon
  • Bonne-Espérance
  • Côte-Nord-du-Golfe-du-Saint-Laurent
  • Gros-Mécatina (Baie-des-Moutons/La Tabatière)
  • Saint-Augustin
  • Kégashka
  • La Romaine
  • Chevery
  • Harrington Harbour
  • Aylmer Sound
  • Tête-à-la-Baleine
  • Pakuashipi
  • Saint Augustine River
  • Vieux-Fort
  • Rivière-Saint-Paul
  • Middle Bay
  • Brador
  • Lourdes-de-Blanc-Sablon
