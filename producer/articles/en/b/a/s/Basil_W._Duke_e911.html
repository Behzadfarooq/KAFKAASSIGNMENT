General Basil Duke

Basil Wilson Duke (May 28, 1838 or 1837 â€“ September 16, 1916) was a Confederate general during the American Civil War, perhaps most noted for his service as a brigade commander in the 1863 Morgan's Raid (Duke later wrote a popular account of the raid). In postbellum Louisville, he served as chief counsel for the Louisville & Nashville Railroad. Duke also fought at the Battle of Shiloh. He was the brother-in-law of John Hunt Morgan, and took over Morgan's command after Morgan was shot by Union soldiers in 1864.
