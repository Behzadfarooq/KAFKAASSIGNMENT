[edit] Overveiw

The Entente: Battlefields World War I is a real-time strategy pc game published in 2004 by 5yka Burka Entertainment. It simulates the main battles and campaigns of World War I from the perspective of the five main combatants involved in the war-Russia, France, Germany, Britain and the Austrian-Hungarian Empire. It is also the first game to be based on the events of World War I.

The Big Bytes version of the game.

[edit] Single Player

The single player battles in The Entente are the standard battles of the game and are also the most commonly used element of the game.

The first option for single player games is the missions. They are battles of the Great War that don’t really fit any of the campaigns, particularly on the Eastern Front (World War I).

The second option is the combatants’ own campaigns. These represent the main battles that the countries in the campaigns fought that were linked in some way.

The final option is the normal games that are played by the players-random maps. Similar to the Age of Empires random and death matches, these games start with the player having a city centre and nine workers to start a town. The victory condition for these games is always the same-wipe the enemy out completely.

[edit] Multiplayer

Games can also be played over the Internet using LAN with up to eight players.

[edit] Units

The Entente: Battlefields World War I sports an array of different units from all aspects of the First World War and can be broken up into several different types-infantry, artillery, vehicles (including tanks and transport vehicles), ships, and planes.

Infantry are normally just ranks of foot soldiers supported by a couple of officers (or workers), although specialist infantry such as machine gunners, flame throwers, elite foot soldiers, mortar men, and mounted infantry can be used in a players forces. Also, if an officer is in a group of soldiers (made by the player during a game) he can enhance the abilities of the troops, even though officers aren't a very effective unit as they are only armed with pistols.

Artillery is a must-have in The Entente because it allows the player to relatively easily wipe out key enemy positions like buildings and large blocks of units. The basic artillery units in the game are howitzers and guns such as the 105mm Schneider and the 80mm Gun. A more powerful piece of artillery, the mortar, is also useable. A disadvantage of artillery in the game is that it is venerable if it is attacked itself.

Vehicles are quite versatile in The Entente: Battlefields World War I because they can be used in a variety of ways on the virtual battlefields of the game. Light and heavy tanks such as the Whippet and the A7V can be used to support infantry and cavalry during an offensive and trucks can quickly bring troops to the front line or retreat with them. Anti-aircraft guns are far cheaper that planes and when used in large numbers are a menace to enemy aircraft crews.

The most destructive units in the game are ships. These include destroyers such as the Russian Narvik, cruisers, battleships and merchant ships like the Pasadena. All of the ships in the game apart from the merchant ships can produce considerable amounts of firepower.

In the First World War planes were a very new development. The Entente includes several of the most famous aircraft of the era like the SPAD and Focker Dr.1,[recognisance planes such as the Etrich Tube and light and heavy bombers like the Farmen and IIya Muromec.

One other weapon that can be used in the game is another of the inventions of the First World War-poison gas. The player has to first research it and then the player can buy it. For it to be used, the player has to allocate any of his or her artillery-related units (apart from A-A Guns) to fire the gas shell in a certain area. The gas cloud will slowly kill any human enemy units in the gas cloud for a few minutes until it disappears.

[edit] Resources

The great armies of the First World War wouldn’t have been as grand as they were without the resources that each country involved possessed during the conflict.

The six resources in The Entente are food, wood, iron, gold, oil and electricity. The resources in the list have to be gathered by workers working in the barns/farms, sawmills, iron mines, gold mines, oil rigs, and electric power stations.

Resources are required in the game to build [armies and towns. A good combatant in The Entente will have access to a large number of resources.

[edit] Buildings

To complete the overall feel of 1914 -1918 The Entente has buildings in the game that accurately represent each country’s style of architecture that it had during the war.

Buildings in the game are always built and mostly run by workers. Structures in the game that can be built are as follows-city centres, academy of sciences’, hospitals, military academies, mints, hangers, markets, gold mines, barns/farms, stables, oil rigs, electric power stations, building academies, tank works, recruiting centres, sawmills, ports, watch towers, artillery deports, medical tents, iron mines, houses, and two different types of pillboxes.

Some units and all upgrades can be made at certain structures; such as planes can only be built if the player has made a hanger.
