Battle Clash (an 8 megabit video game, known as Space Bazooka in Japan) is an anime-influenced game for the Super NES which makes use of the bazooka-shaped Super NES Super Scope light gun. The title was released in 1992 by Nintendo and developed by Intelligent Systems.

[edit] Story

In a distant future, Earth lives in chaos and the only order that rules resides in the Battle Game, where the winner takes control over the world. All battles are fought with mechs called Standing Tanks (ST). A merciless fighter called Anubis eventually becomes the champion of the Battle Game.

Mike Anderson, whose father was one of the many victims of Anubis, refuses to succumb, and trains himself in everything ST-related, in order to stop the current era of chaos and get revenge for his father's death. But in order to accomplish this, he must first defeat the Battle Chiefs (Anubis' subordinates) if he wants to face his nemesis.

[edit] Gameplay

In the game, the player is the gunner of the ST Falcon and also Mike's partner, who fights against a group of other STs in one-on-one battles. In order to attack, the player must shoot using the Super Scope. It is possible to shoot rapid shots by holding down the fire button, as well as energy bolts (charged shots). Energy bolts can be shot when an energy bar fills up (this happens when the player doesn't fire). When the energy bar has filled up, the energy bolt can be fired, though it will consume the energy bar. It is also important to defend against enemy attacks by shooting their fire. Some attacks can only be deflected by firing energy bolts.

The objective of the game is to defeat the rival ST in order to clear the stage. The damage inflicted on the enemy depends on the spot where the ST has been shot (all STs have some kind of weak point) and how powerful is the shot. The player wins if the rival ST's energy is depleted first. If the enemy depletes the player's energy or if the stage is played for ten minutes, then the game is over, but it is possible to continue the game indefinitely.

[edit] List of Battle Chiefs

  • Garam

Description: a green gorilla-like ST. It is the first ST. It has regular speed and its attacks involves firing low and medium powered shots at a slow pace. His most powerful attack is the rocket punch, which can deplete more than the half of the player's energy. Pilot: Guido, a mountain man from the Rocky Mountains. Chief of North America. Stage: New York

  • Scarab

Description: a brown pudgy humanoid ST. It is seemingly very powerful and invincible, but after discovering its weak point, it becomes an almost helpless target. Its attacks are on par with Garam's, if not weaker. Pilot: Ikhnaton, an oil magnate and monopolist. Chief of Africa. Stage: Egypt

  • Lorca

Description: A purple humanoid ST. It is very fast and can change directions with relative easiness. His limbs are not heavily fixated to the body, which means that they will be blown away if they receive an impact, but after a time they will be back in their place if not properly destroyed. Its most powerful attack is the "Battle Hymn", a somersault in which it attacks with a volley of shots. Pilot: Lorca, an effeminate battler nicknamed "The Bard of Battle". Chief of Europe. Stage: London

  • Artemis

Description: A humanoid pink ST with rocket shoulders, which enables it to fly and move in all directions, since the fight will take place in the air. It is also a fast ST, though not as fast as Lorca's. Its special attack is a volley of missiles. Pilot: Tasha, the only female of the Battle Chiefs and protector and Chief of South America. Despite being a Chief she dislikes Anubis and waits for someone able to defeat him. Stage: Andes

  • Schneider

Desciption: A anti-gravity based blue ST with flying orbs that enables Schneider to amplify his energy attacks. Its armor is more resistant than other STs. Because it uses anti-gravity, destroying its legs doesn't incapacitate it but can still damage it. It also shoots spiral-moving shots. Pilot: Alfred, a Japanese-German egotistical scientist and Chief of Asia. Stage: Kyoto

  • Ivan

Description: An extremely huge spider-like ST, what lacks in agility or movement, it excels with its attack and defenses. Its armor is resistant to the player's attacks unless it reveals his weak points. It is also heavily armed in almost every part of this body. Pilot: Antonov, a construction worker and Chief of Oceania. Like Tasha, he also longs for Anubis' defeat. Stage: Oceania

  • Valius

Description: A mantis-patterned ST, it is the fastest ST in the game, with unpredictable movements. It is not rare for it to escape from the player sight and wait him with an attack, which includes from bolts to volleys of missiles. Pilot: Edward (Eddie in the game's script), a fair general brainwashed by Anubis because of his combat skills. Stage: Tower of Babel

  • Baron

Description: An ST similar to the Falcon, it uses a medium sized gun and a shield. Most of his best attcks involve his gun, while covering with the shield. If the gun is destroyed, Baron throws his shield to the player in order to inflict serious damage. Pilot: Carlos, Anubis' top subordinate. He is a truly vicious and emotionless fighter. Stage: Moonbase Luna

  • Thanatos

Description: The final ST in the game. In its first form, it launched energy bolts from either his arms or feet (he kicks his bolts). After beating Thanatos, only the head and torso remains, and it calls other mouth-shaped limbs. In this second one, the limbs attack with powerful energy bolts, whereas the main body still attacks the player. Pilot: Anubis, the ruthless champion of the Battle Game. Stage: The Moon

[edit] Sequel

In 1993 a sequel was released by Nintendo called Metal Combat: Falcon's Revenge (16 megabits) which offered similar game play. However, by the time it was released, the Super Scope's popularity in North America was waning, and as a result, the sequel was not very popular.
