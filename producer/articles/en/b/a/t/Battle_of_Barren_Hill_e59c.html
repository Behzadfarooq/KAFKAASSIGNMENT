The Battle of Barren Hill was a skirmish of the American Revolutionary War fought in what is now known as Lafayette Hill, Pennsylvania on May 20, 1778.[2]

On May 18, 1778, General George Washington sent 2,200 men and five guns under the Marquis de Lafayette from Valley Forge to the area north of the Schuylkill River to scout the British forces there and harry their foraging parties.[3]

British and Hessian forces from Philadelphia, Pennsylvania were sent to trap the Americans. Lafayette avoided the entrapment and returned to Valley Forge.[4]
