Bristoe Campaign
Auburn I – Auburn II – Bristoe Station – Buckland Mills – Rappahannock Station II

The Battle of Bristoe Station was fought on October 14, 1863, between Union and Confederate forces in the American Civil War. The Union army was led by Maj. Gen. George G. Meade, the Confederates by General Robert E. Lee. Maj. Gen. Gouverneur K. Warren, commanding II Corps in Maj. Gen. Winfield S. Hancock's absence, hid his forces behind a railroad embankment near Bristoe Station. Warren sprung a powerful ambush as the corps of A.P. Hill moved to attack the Federal rear guard. Heth's division suffered serious losses, but Warren eventually had to withdraw. Lee is supposed to have cut off Hill's excuses by saying, "Bury these poor men, general, and we'll say no more about it." The Union forces won the battle, but the Confederates destroyed a railroad during their retreat.
