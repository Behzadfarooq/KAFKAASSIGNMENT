The Battle of Europe or Battle for European Fortress refer as all air offensive and defensive operations over European skies during World War Two.

It included all aerial operations amongst strategical and tactical bombing offensives realized by French Armee de L'Air (previously to defeat in 1940) American USAAF, British RAF, and later Russian V-VS over European soil from west, east and Mediterranean areas.

If related also minor bombing offensive and defensive air operations realized for German Luftwaffe, Italian Regia Aeronautica joining other European Axis Powers supporters during that times.

In such actions not included any operations in North Africa, Atlantic and Arctic Oceans amongst Middle East areas or European Neutral countries also.

These operations was analyzed in next stages:

[edit] First Enemy Aerial Incursions Period (1939-43)

  • British raid over Sylt island in German Coast, in reprisal over German attack over british Orkney islands (1939)
  • British next raid against German naval units (September 4, 1939)
  • Russian Air Force raids against Finnish soil (1939-40)
  • French Air Force raid against Berlin (June 7-8, 1940), the first Allied strike against German Capital.
  • French Air Force aerial strikes against B.M.W. factories in Munich and launching of propaganda on German territory (1939-40)
  • French night raids against German soil (May-June 1940)
  • French air attacks against north and south Italy (1940)
  • French Air Force raids over German lines between Rhin and Belgium (1940)
  • French air strikes against German advanced forces from Belgium (May 12, 1940)and German bridgeheads in Sedan (May 14, 1940)
  • French Air Force air attack against German forces in Arras (May 20, 1940)
  • First British strikes against Northern Italy (June 1940)
  • British raids over Berlin, the cominzed of British Bombing offensive against Germany (1941).
  • British aerial strike against Athens airport (May 17, 1941)
  • Soviet raid over Berlin (August 8-9, 1941), the cominzed of Russian limited bombing offensive.
  • Russian daylight-night light and heavy raids against Axis Powers land forces in Eastern Front
  • British attack against German Battleships Scharnhorst and Gneisenau in Brest (April 1941)
  • British strike against German cruiser Koeingsberg in Norway coasts (April 1940-41)
  • British Dayligth operations (Raid of Augsburg, Destroy of Mohne and Eder Powerplants amongst others actions) over German territory, with scarcies results
  • First American raid against Rome, Italy (1942)
  • First British great scale operation, "One thousand Bombers" raid against Cologne, Germany (May 30, 1942)
  • British air attack against Phillips Factories in Eindhoven, Nederlands ("Oyster Operation"), 1942
  • British offensive against Lubeck (February 1942)
  • British raids over Milan, Italy (October 24, 1942) and night attack over same plane (Febrary 14, 1943)
  • First American daylight raid over European soil, against Petrol pits, in Ploesti Romania amongst objectives in Bulgaria (June 11-12, 1942), the first stages of American Bombing offensive
  • American first raid in western area (Rouen, France), (August 1942) and first American attacks against ball-bearing factory in Schweinfurt and Messerschmitt factories in Regensburg, Germany (january 1943)
  • British raid over Turin and Genoa (1943) amongst next strike over Milan (august 1943)

[edit] Last European Axis Powers Limited Bombing Offensives (1942-45)

  • German minor incursions over British territory (1942)
  • German "Baedeker Strikes" against Bath, York, Exeter and Canterbury, England (spring 1942) and navigation strikes in Biscay Gulf (1943)
  • Italian defensive operations in Pantelleria, Sicilia, Naples and Southern Italy (1943)
  • Luftwaffe "Steinbock Operation" against London (January 1944)
  • German strikes against Allieds Operations in Anzio and Salerno (1943) and Normandy (1944)
  • Luftwaffe night raid against Poltava (21-22 June, 1944) were destroyed 60 USAAF Bombers and figthers in Russia.
  • German Air Force air attacks against England with Bombers carrying V-1 (1944-45)
  • Last Luftwaffe attempt to contained Western Allied advances with air strikes over Rhin Bridges (included Remagen also) (March 1945)
  • Last Luftwaffe attempt to detain Russian Advances with strikes against Bridges of Oder and Neisse rivers (1945)

[edit] Second Enemy Aerial Incursions Period(1943-45)

  • Bombing dayligth offensive leaved by American USAAF against occupied territories and German Soil (1943-44)
  • Nexts American raids against Oil pits in Ploesti, Romania amongst objectives in Bulgarian soil
  • British night RAF bombing offensive against Germany and European occupied held lands.
  • British aerial strikes against Ruhr, Hamburg amongst raid over Berlin (November 22, 1943)
  • American air strikes against Brux chemical factories, Germany
  • Next British raid over Berlin (March 24-25, 1943)
  • Russian next phase of air strikes against Axis Powers land forces in Eastern Front (1943-44)
  • American raids over Focke-Wulf factories in Hamburg, Bremen and Marienburg
  • American Strike against Messerschmitt factories in Augsburg
  • British Raid over Peenemunde V-1/V-2 Research and Factory center (August 17, 1943)
  • Raid over Italian aircraft factory CANT in Trieste (1944)
  • American attack against Dornier aircraft factories
  • Air attack against Italian aircraft factory Macchi (April 1944)
  • American raid over petrol refineries in Pardubice, occupied Bohemia
  • Aerial Attack against German vessel Tirpitz, in Altafjord (1944)
  • British strike against aircraft factory in Tolon, France (April 5, 1944) and raid over Munich.
  • Some British daylight minor raids against Amiens (February 15, 1944), La Hage (April 11, 1944) and Copenhagen (March 21, 1945), between other similar actions
  • British aerial attack against Nuremberg (March 30-31, 1944)
  • American raid over Drohobycz petrol refinery
  • Russian Aerial offensives over Baltic areas, East Prussia, Poland and Balkans (1944)
  • American and British air operations in support to Allied Advances over Rhine (1945)
  • British raid against Dresden (February-March 1945)
  • British strike against Kiel (May 2, 1945)
  • American daylight heavy raid over Berlin (March 18, 1945)
  • British night heavy raid over Berlin (1945)
  • Daylight and night Soviet Bombing offensive from Stalingrad in Russia to Germany (Battle of Berlin) (1944-45)
