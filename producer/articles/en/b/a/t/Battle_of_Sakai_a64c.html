Ouchi Yoshihiro, the powerful shugo of Suo and Nagato provinces, had been an important Ashikaga adherent in the war with the 'Southern Court' and his greatest contribution came in 1392, when he managed to convince the emperor of the Southern Court to surrender, thus bringing the Nambuchuko Period effectively to a close. Yet Yoshihiro was to rise in revolt against the Bakufu as a result of what he considered unfair demands on his resources (the shogun requested that he build him a villa at Kitayama, for instance). To this end, he had carefully made arrangements for support among various other shugo, and then withdrew his forces from Kyoto to the city of Sakai. Yoshimitsu initially attempted to solve the crisis peacefully, but finding Yoshihiro determined to fight, elected to make the first move. He gathered an army composed of the Hatakeyama, Hosokawa, and Shiba and made a general advance on Sakai by land while at the same negotiating with the Inland Sea pirates (whose support Yoshihiro had been counting on) to affect a naval blockade.

[edit] The battle

Yoshihiro moved his troops from Kyoto to Sakai, where he waited for Yoshimitsu's army. Yoshimitsu, already known about the pirates, dispatched a naval force and quickly subdued them. Meanwhile, the reinforcements from Iwami and Izumi troops Yoshihiro received was completely unreliable. Yoshimitsu's men broke through the Yoshihiro defenses and set fire to te city. Yoshihiro committed suicide.
