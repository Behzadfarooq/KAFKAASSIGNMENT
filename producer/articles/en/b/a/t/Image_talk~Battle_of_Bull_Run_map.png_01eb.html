The attack on Fort Sumter by the Southern rebels was the first was the first shots fired in the American Civil War; however, the actual first battle took place at Bull Run Creek and Manassas Junction, Louisiana. The first question, why two names for the same battle? The First Battle of Bull Run and the Battle of Manassas was the same battle. The soldiers from the North called it the Battle of Bull Run because it was fought at Bull Run Creek. The soldiers from the South called it the Battle of Manassas because it was fought at Manassas Junction. Therefore, both names are correct. Today it is more commonly referred to as the First Battle of Bull Run, but if you go to the South they still refer to it as the First Battle of Manassas. Now the second question, were the people fighting the battle and their commanders ready for battle? The answer to this question would be no. The North was forced in to battle before the troops were adequately trained. On the way to the battle the troops lost food, ammunition, and men. This caused it to be near impossible to win the battle. Question number three, who were the commanders of the forces? Pierre Gustave Torrent Beauregard and Irvin McDowell took those places. “Beauregard was a Frenchman who had extensively studied Napoleon. He graduated second from his West Point class in 1853. Beauregard was commissioned as an engineer, distinguished himself in the War with Mexico, and served as the Super attendant of West Point, but resigned in 1861 to serve the Confederate Army. He saw action at Shiloh and Charleston, South Carolina. He ended the war as commander of the Department of North Carolina and southeastern Virginia” (General Bee Bernard 2). “Irvin McDowell was educated in France and graduated twenty-third from his West Point class in 1838. He carried the rank of major and was serving under General Winfield Scott in Washington D.C., when the Civil War began. He was in close contact with Lincoln and Government Officials and impressed them so that they prompted him to Brigadier general in May, 1861. McDowell was also involved at Second Bull Run. Afterwards he was removed from field command and never returned to command troops in combat” (General Bee Bernard 2). The fourth question now, who fought in the battle? The two sides that fought the battle were the Northern Union Troops and the Southern Confederate Troops. McDowell was commander of the Northern Union Troops and Beauregard was commander of the Southern Confederate Troops. The fifth question, what was the Number of injured people? The Southern Confederate Troops lost 387 people due to death, 1,582 people wounded and 13 people captured/missing. But The Northern Union Troops had 460 people that died, 1,124 people wounded, and 1,312 people captured/missing. The sixth and final question, who won the battle?
