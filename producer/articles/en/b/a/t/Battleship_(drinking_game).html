Battleship is a slight variation of the drinking game Beer pong (also called Beirut, Lop pong and Scud). Instead of shooting for a triangle of cups, players place cups in the shape of the 5 ships from the board game Battleship. Players must throw a ping pong ball into the cups, and once all cups are hit from a ship, the ship is sunk. Played with teams of 2-3 players.

Another variation is Greenwich Battleship. This variation is designed to make it seem more like the actual game of battleship. The whole table is covered with cups filled with beer, 10x10 on each side, for a total of 200 cups. Players mark certain cups in rows or columns in order to signify which cups are the "ships". Players shoot at the cups and when they hit a cup with a ball in it they are notified. If they hit a cup with a ball in it, the other team must drink. However, if they hit a cup without a ball in it, they must drink that cup. When they hit all cups with balls in them, the ship is sunk. The winning team is the one that sinks all of the other team's ships. The losers must drink the remaining cups on the table.

Yet another version of Battleship includes one large pitcher full of alcohol with a glass of the same alcohol floating within it. The glass floating inside should float with the edges of the glass slightly sticking up over the rest of the alcohol within the pitcher so as to make a sort of glass boat full of water almost ready sink. A group of players stand around the pitcher with the floating glass inside it with a bottle of the same beer in hand. Their aim is to take turns in a circle and drop by drop fill the floating glass until it sinks into the pitcher thus filling up all the way. The player who adds the one drop too many into the floating glass causing it to sink must fish it out, chug its contents, fill it up from another pitcher and replace it floating in the pitcher. Naturally, the game continues until either two men are left fighting it out for champion by remaining standing or the party runs out of beer.
