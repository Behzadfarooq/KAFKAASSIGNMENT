[edit] Korolev

Article needs updating to reflect the true cause of the ships loss. While it did appear in the season finale that it was destroyed by weapons fire the opening episode of the next series showed the ship was actually destroyed by a nuke they were planning to beam to an Ori ship.—The preceding unsigned comment was added by 83.104.138.141 (talk • contribs).

No it didn't, the nuke had nothing to do with the ships destruction. JBK405 03:44, 11 March 2007 (UTC)

Watch the episode! they are in the engineering bay prepping a nuke for beaming to the ori ship, The fuse is activated (length couple of seconds) then ships hit and it rolls off the pad, seing this daniel beams himself there instead as he knows its about to explode, seconds later the ship does explode FROM THE REAR without any direct fire.

Sorry but perhaps you should watch the episode prior to telling people your theories, the Korolev is destroyed by two instantaneous hits from an Ori cannon. Matthew 13:04, 12 March 2007 (UTC)

I agree the nuke may have played a part but the later video shows the Ori firing upon and destroying the ship. The nuke would likely have destroyed it but the later episode proves that while Asgard shield technology slows the effects of the Ori weapons it is only a matter of time before the Ori cannon destroys the shield and the ship. 70.226.133.209 08:07, 16 March 2007 (UTC)

This is one of the craziest things I've ever heard here! Plus, a nuke if destroyed without detonating will not detonate so it had nothing to do with it. Remember in "No Man's Land", when the Daedalus fired it's nukes at the hive, most of them were shot down before they hit and they didn't make a big explosion, just the one that actually hit.

Vala M 12:21, 16 March 2007 (UTC)

A site note as well, had the Korolev been destroyed by a nuke we'd of seen a larger explosion as there is atmosphere within the Korolev. Matthew 12:24, 16 March 2007 (UTC)
