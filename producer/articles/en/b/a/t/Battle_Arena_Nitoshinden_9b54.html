NiToshinden (full name Battle Arena NiToshinden) is a spin off game from the Battle Arena Toshinden series of games and was only released in Japan. It was going to be released in the U.S under the name of Toshinden Kids. However said release was canceled.

NiToshinden (believed to translate to “Little Toshinden”) is a light hearted take on main series of Toshinden games in which all the characters have been shrunk and their personalities altered to be more child like.

[edit] Gameplay

The gameplay of NiToshinden is notably different from the main series. Each characters move list has been reduced to 6 attacks (including normal attacks): 4 standard attacks, a special attack and a desperation attack.

The normal attacks roughly corespond to standard Toshinden attacks. Theres a hard attack, light attack, low attack and either a medium or 360 degree attack (depending on the character). There is no real combo ability but several attacks can be chained together if the you can fool the opponent into defending incorrectly.

The game features a different defense system to standard Toshinden games. The side step moves still exist (they are the only way to avoid a low attack or projectile), however blocking of other attacks is now done with a parry button that you press to repel an opponents attack and stun them allowing you to follow up with an attack of your own). There is also a dive manovere that allows you to dive under projectiles and through the opponents legs.

Areanas in NiToshinden are all school based locations (to keep in line with changed style of the characters) and walled in (though not always visually) as arenas would be in Toshinden 3. However unlike Toshinden 3, being hit into a wall throws your character back towards the centre of the arena.
