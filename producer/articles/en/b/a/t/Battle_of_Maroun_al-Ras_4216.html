Battle of Maroun al-Ras

Maroun al-Ras is a small town in southern Lebanon on the border with Israel.

The village, which was a Hezbollah stronghold, was the site of combat during the 2006 Israel-Lebanon conflict.

On July 22, 2006 2,000 Israeli troops entered southern Lebanon. The battle for Maroun al-Ras began on July 25, and lasted for 4 days, ending with Israeli control of the village[1]

To IDF reported 7 sldiers killed in the battle, and estimated Hezbollah losses at 12-30 fighters. Hezbollah reported 8 of its fighters were killed. [2]
