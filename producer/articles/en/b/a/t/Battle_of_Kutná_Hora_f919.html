An early campaign in the Hussite Wars, the Battle of Kutná Hora was fought on December 21, 1421 between German and Hungarian troops of the Holy Roman Empire and the Hussites, a group of early Protestants founded in what is now the Czech Republic. Following a siege by royalist troops, the remaining Hussites plowed through enemy lines and went on to win many battles against the Roman Catholic forces.[1]
