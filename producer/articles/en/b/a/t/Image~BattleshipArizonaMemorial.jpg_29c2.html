Description

Battleship USS Arizona Memorial. Taken from a ConSubPac (Commander, Submarines, Pacific) web site* no longer available, this image not found on the current ConSubPac site.

In the background is Ford Island. The bollards to which battleships were tied, forming Battleship Row, are still maintained and marked with the numbers and names of the ships here on that day.

The memorial is accessed by boats from the visitor's center across the harbor (to the left in the image) where visitors view a short movie that sets the historical context for this site.

* http://subase.hnl.mrms.navy.mil/users/index.html

Uploaded by en:User:Leonard G.

[edit] Additional notes

  • The memorial does not touch any part of the ship
  • Some of the usable gun mounts, each with three fourteen inch rifles, were removed and employed locally as coastal defence weapons.
  • The base of one of the tubular spars of the gun director tripod mount carries the flagpole.
  • The ship is no longer commissioned
  • Fuel oil still bubbles slowly from the hulk
  • The hull is gradually disintegrating.
  • The foundations of the memorial have been modified to arrest its descent into the mud, a problem unforseen at the time of its construction.
