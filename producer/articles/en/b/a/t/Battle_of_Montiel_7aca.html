The Battle of Montiel was fought in 1369 between Franco-Castilian forces, and an alliance of pro-English forces led by the Portuguese. Enrique II of Castile led the Franco-Castilian force, while Pedro of Castile led the Portuguese force. The Franco-Castilians were victorious.
