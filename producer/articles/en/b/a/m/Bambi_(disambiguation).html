The name Bambi is used in multiple contexts:

  • Bambi, A Life in the Woods is a book written by Felix Salten, published in 1923.
  • Bambi is a 1942 animated feature produced by Walt Disney, based upon Salten's book.
  • Bambi is slang for deer, usually pejorative.
  • Bambi is the stage name of French transsexual entertainer Marie-Pier Ysser
  • Bambis are also semi-naked women hunted with paintball guns in the video series Hunting For Bambi.
  • The Bambi Molesters are a surf rock band from Sisak, Croatia.
  • "Bambi" is a famous episode of the BBC 1980s sitcom, The Young Ones.
  • Lawrencia "Laurie" Bembenek, also known as "Bambi" Bembenek, (born 1958) is a woman convicted of murdering her husband's ex-wife.
  • Bambi Meets Godzilla is a 1969 animated short film, spoofing the Disney film.
  • The Bambi Effect is the emotional response of revulsion to attractive animals being harmed.
  • Bambi and Her Pink Gun, manga series by Atsushi Kaneko.
  • "Bambi eyes" is a term that describes an innocent look that people can make with their eyes to generate sympathy (see also: crocodile tears).
  • "Bambi" is the nickname of Lance Alworth, a former American football wide receiver and member of the Pro Football Hall of Fame.
  • "Bambi" is the name of a character in the James Bond film Diamonds Are Forever.
  • "Bambi" is a name of a publication by Hachette
  • Bambi is the name of a biscuit manufacturer in Serbia.
  • Bambi is a media and television prize awarded by German media company Hubert Burda Media.
  • "Bambi" is the title of a song in Prince's 1979 self-titled album.
  • Bambi is Carla's nickname for J.D. in the American sitcom Scrubs
  • Bambi is the name of female wrestler Selina Majors
  • Bambi is the name of one of Paris Hilton's chihuahuas.
  • Bambi is the nickname of the supporters of a Brazilian football team, São Paulo FC.
