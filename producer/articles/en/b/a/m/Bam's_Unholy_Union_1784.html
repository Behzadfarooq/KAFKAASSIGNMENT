Bam's Unholy Union is a TV series airing on MTV and MTV Canada. It stars Bam Margera and his fiancee Melissa "Missy" Rothstein and follows Bam and Missy as they plan their wedding (which occurred on February 3, 2007[1]). The show stars a limited number of the CKY Crew. Some of the CKY Crew members that have appeared are Brandon DiCamillo, Brandon Novak, Rake Yohn, Ryan "Shitgoose" Gee and Bam's brother Jess. Ryan Dunn said he'll appear in two or three episodes of the show. Most of the members of the band CKY stated that they will make a few appearances on the show.[2] The show is filmed at various locations in and around West Chester, Pennsylvania, including Delaware. Bam's parents, April and Phil Margera, also appear in the show. Bam's uncle Don Vito does not make regular appearances due to MTV's concerns about his legal problems, stemming from charges in Colorado in August 2006.[3]

According to recent interviews based on the show, Unholy Union will run 9 episodes,[3] Bam has mentioned that MTV has approached him about a second season.[4]

The DVD for the show will come out June 12, 2007.[5]

Episodes

Spoiler warning: Plot and/or ending details follow.

1.1: Welcome to Hell

Bam and Missy find a venue for the engagement party, but have trouble finding a place to hold the wedding due to such short notice. (aired on 1/30/2007)

1.2: Open Season on Brides

Bam and Missy meet their wedding planner to help them relieve the stress of planning it themselves. Also, Bam and the crew make a mess out of Missy's wedding dress using paintball guns and Rake Yohn as the shooting dummy, only to find out that it's not Missy's real dress. (aired on 2/6/2007)

1.3: Detroit Rock City

Bam and Missy travel to Detroit to buy their rings (which Bam said cost $50,000.[4]) Also, Bam and Missy find a venue for their wedding in Philadelphia, and Bam gives Missy his purple Lambo as an early wedding present under the circumstance that Missy does one skate trick. (aired on 2/13/2007)

1.4: Off With Her Shirt

Bam and Missy continue planning their wedding, Bam hires his uncles to build steps for his hot tub, and the couple gets an offer from Playboy Magazine for Missy to pose nude and Bam to be a guest photographer.[6] (aired on 2/20/2007)

1.5: Flipping the Christmas Bird

Bam and his friends decorate their house for Christmas, Bam cuts down a 50 foot tree from his parents backyard to set up in his house and tows it home with his Hummer, April teaches Missy to cook the Christmas turkey/meal, Bam sets up and participates in the West Chester Christmas parade.[7](aired on 2/27/2007)

1.6: The Unicorn Whisperer

Brandon Novak wants to become best man, but Bam gives Novak five challenges to become one. Meanwhile, Missy tries to get the invitations ready and has her bridal shower. (aired on 3/06/2007)

1.7: Bam's Gone Wild

Bam and crew go to Las Vegas for his bachelor party while Missy has her bachelorette party in Palm Springs. While in Vegas, Bam is reunited with the Jackass Crew and has Vains of Jenna perform. The bachelor party continues in his suite...while he's passed out. (aired on 3/13/2007)[8]

1.8: I'll Never Be Single Again

With only a week until their wedding, Bam and Missy apply for their marriage license and hold their rehearsal dinner. Novak binges on free wine in a winery while Bam removes all the toilets from Phil's house. Also, Bam gets a surprise call to find out that Iggy Pop and the Stooges will be playing at the wedding, the guys and Missy go bowling and Bam dyes his hair purple. (aired on 3/27/2007)

Spoilers end here.

Cast

Picture Cast member Information
Bam Margera Bam Margera (born Brandon Cole Margera on September 28, 1979) is a professional skateboarder and television personality. He released a series of videos under the CKY banner and came to prominence after being drafted into MTV's Jackass crew. He has since appeared in Viva La Bam, both Jackass films and Haggard: The Movie, which he co-wrote and directed.
Missy Margera Missy Margera (born Melissa Ann Rothstein on June 3, 1980) is the new bride of Bam Margera. The two wed on February 3, 2007.[1] Bam proposed to Missy at the King of Prussia Mall.[9] Missy currently works in a signshop.[10]
Brandon Novak Brandon Novak (born December 10, 1978) is a skateboarder and best friend of Bam Margera from Baltimore, Maryland. Novak wants to be best man for the wedding, but Bam's brother, Jess Margera, is already fulfilling that role. Bam tells Novak that in order to become best man he must complete 5 tasks. Some of the tasks include shaving and bleaching his hair, getting punched in both eyes (must get a black eye), and getting into a raccoon outfit to become the first human raccoon.[11][12] Novak broke both his feet the day before the wedding, skating on a new ramp in Bam's backyard with Tony Hawk.
Brandon DiCamillo Brandon Ralph DiCamillo (born November 15, 1976 in West Chester, Pennsylvania), is a longtime friend of Bam Margera. DiCamillo can be seen sniffing onions to make himself cry in the first episode.
April Margera April Margera (born March 28, 1956 in Glen Mills, Pennsylvania, United States) is Bam's mother. She is not happy Novak was in the wedding party due to his checkered past with drugs and crime.
Phil Margera Philip Ron Margera (born July 13, 1957 in Concordville, Pennsylvania, United States) is Bam's father. He is laid back and is used to Bam and the crew's way of doing things. For example, during interviews for the show's website, April asks, "What's wrong with our son?" and Phil replies, "There's something wrong with our son?"

Trivia

This article contains a trivia section.
Content in this section should be integrated into other appropriate areas of the article or removed, and the trivia section removed.
  • Wedding designer Mark Ingram appears in an episode. Ingram is most notably known for his bridal shop in New York City.
  • Kooma and the Birds & Bees have also appeared on Viva La Bam. DiCamillo once had a job at Kooma, handing out flyers in a fish suit and carrying a rickshaw (Season 4, Episode 3). Don Vito has also been at the Birds & Bees for the Scavenger Hunt (Season 1, Episode 8) and in the CKY Gets Jobs episode (Season 4, Episode 3). Other places that have been seen in Viva La Bam are April and Phil's house and the Tree-Top Casino.
  • A few references allude to Viva La Bam. One reference included Bam saying "Why should the township be pissed off when I make kids happy everyday". This was a reference to the township kicking Bam (along with the CKY crew, plus April and Phil) out of the township (Season 1, Episode 8), because of all the violations he broke and his behavior.
  • The show's main title theme is a snippet of "Don't Give a Damn," by Vains of Jenna, a band currently signed to Margera's Filthy Note Records.
  • In Episode Seven, Bam and Dico took Jess's Tetris machine. Jess said that it was actually Dico's. He said the dust was from the basement because he's finishing it up.[13]
