Bamidele Ojo

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Bamidele A. Ojo is a professor of political science and teaches African politics, human rights and globalization at Fairleigh Dickinson University in Teaneck, New Jersey.

He holds a PhD. His book on Human Rights and the New World Order is used by the United Nations University International Human Right Course. He has published extensively in his areas of expertise and his most recent publication is on Africa's Triple dilemma: The challenges of state building, democratization and globalization.

He is the publisher of the leading Internet resource portal, AfricaPoliticsOnline.com.

He was a Fulbright senior scholar to the University of Lagos, Nigeria in 2001-2002. He was also a governorship candidate under the Nigerian ruling People's Democratic Party in Ekiti State Nigeria in 2003. He is also expected to be appointed a Chairman of a Federal parastatal by the President of Nigeria in December 2004.
