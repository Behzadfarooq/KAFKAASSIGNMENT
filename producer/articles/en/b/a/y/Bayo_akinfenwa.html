[edit] Adebayo Akinfenwa

Known as "Bayo" to Swansea City Fans, the lovable striker of Nigerian origin has endeered himself to Swansea City fans.

After years of obscurity playing bit-part roles for teams such as Doncaster and Barry he began to settle at Torquay in the 2004/2005 season. Torquay were fighting relegation and his skill and power helped net a few goals, making an appearance on the poppular Sky Sports morning show, Soccer am!

Yet Torquay were relegated from League 1 on the last day of the season, and against fans' wills the big gangster was sold.

He ended up at Swansea City.

He began the 2005/2006 season netting a few goals for the Swans. Yet his fitness was poor, having been labelled the Heaviest Player in European Football at 17.5 stone! With the acquisitions of Leon Knight and Rory Fallon, Akinfenwa slipped out of favour, having received criticism for his lack of effort. Yet many were angry that while Fallon couldn't score goals Bayo was left on the bench. His season came to a devastating end in the play-off final at the Millenium Stadium in Cardiff. With the game at 2-2 against Barnsley, Akinfenwa was brought on alongside fans' favourite Lee Trundle. He came close with a 25-yard strike that driffted past the post. Yet it was when the game went to a penalty shoot-out that it all turned bad!

Akinfenwa stepped up to take the third penalty with the scores at 2-2. He stepped up, stopped and nervously blasted the ball over thecrossbar into the Swansea fans. After Alan Tate missed his penalty they were out, with many calling for his head.

A religious man, he confessed that miss made him question for the first time his belief in God (He'd wanted to be a Pastor before football became an option).

Yet he worked hard and lost a lot of weight, becoming more agile and more powerful.

On the opening day of the 2006/2007 season he came on as a substitute for the lackluster Rory Fallon! Though he didn't score, he'd frightened the Cheltenham defenders and convinced Swansea fans to love him!

Following that came a season of struggle for Bayo, who despite sparkling shows of dribbling and effort couldn't get a place in the team, thanks to the bizare mentality of manager Kenny Jacket. He scored his first goal of the season against Darlington in the FA Cup.

In Kenny Jacket's last game in charge against Oldham Akinfenwa broke his leg in an aerial challenge. This spelled the end of his season, and Swansea's, despite the entrance of wonderful Catalan manager Roberto Martinez.

Akinfenwa can be described as a man who tries his hardest, has a good touch, and the strength and presence of a bull. Though not a very good finisher, one of the nicest people in lower-league football!
