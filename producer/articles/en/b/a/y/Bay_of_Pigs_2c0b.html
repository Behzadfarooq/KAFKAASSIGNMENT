Map showing the location of the Bay of Pigs.

The Bay of Pigs (Spanish: Bahía de Puercos or Cochinos) is an inlet on the southern coast of Cuba. It is located in the province of Matanzas and is west of the city of Cienfuegos.

It is the site of the failed Bay of Pigs Invasion, a 1961 US-supported invasion by Cuban exiles during John F. Kennedy's presidency. The intention of the invasion was to overthrow the government of Fidel Castro. The invasion took place at a beach called Playa Girón. The CIA did much surveillance of the Bay of Pigs, sometimes mistaking coral reefs for seaweed.[citation needed]
