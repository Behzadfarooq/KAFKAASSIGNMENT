[edit] Eo nomine

Having nominated your article for speedy deletion, I thought I should take a moment to explain the reasoning and how you can improve it - I realise it's a bit disconcerting to simply be told that the article you created isn't good enough, without an explanation as to the reasoning.

The {{Template:db-nocontext}} template is used on articles that are very short, and so provide no meaningful information to newcomers to the article's subject. Articles should always provide some context, explaining clearly to the reader (among other things):

  • Why their subject is important;
  • It's history;
  • It's usage (in the case of eo nomine);
  • Any controversy it has been a part of.

Those are the things you should try to include in any new article. This article may be deleted anyway, even if such information is provided, since it seems to be an article regarding a legal term that can only be defined - as in, there might not be that much else worth writing about it. Of course, I'm just a seventeen year old school student: please be bold and include this information in that article, to prevent its unnecessary deletion.

I hope this is vaguely helpful in one way or another. If you have any questions at all, please don't hesitate to leave a message at my talk page, or simply write "{{Template:helpme}}" (without the quotes) anywhere on this page. Happy editing in the future, and I hope to see you around. RandyWang (chat me up/fix me up) 10:41, 10 August 2006 (UTC)
