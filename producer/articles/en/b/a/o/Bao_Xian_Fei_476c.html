Bao Xian Fei (October 4, 1983 in Nanjing/People's Republic of China) is a Wushu-expert, who lives and teaches in the Netherlands today.

[edit] Biography

Bao Xian started in the age of 5 to train with his father, Grandmaster Yuliang Fei, different Wushu-Styls. This naturally includes also forms with weapons.

His first appearance in the movies was 2003 as security guard in the american TV-production "Second Nature", Alec Baldwin taking the leading role. When there was the first Martial-Arts Production in the Netherlands, he became a more important role as "Wong" in "Fighting Fish", 2004.

[edit] Sport Results

His successes include:

Wushu World Championship 1999 - HongKong

  • Silvermedal Gun Shu (Stick)
  • Bronzemedal Dao Shu (Broadsword)

Wushu European Championship 2000 - Rotterdam

  • Goldmedal Gun Shu (Stick)
  • Goldmedal Dao Shu (Broadsword)
  • Goldmedal Chan Quan (Long Fist)

Wushu World Championship 2001 - Yerevan

  • Goldmedal Dao Shu (Broadsword)
  • Silvermedal Gun Shu (Stick)
  • Silvermedal Chan Quan (Long Fist)

Wushu European Championship 2002 - Porto

  • Goldmedal Dao Shu (Broadsword)
  • Goldmedal Gun Shu (Stick)
  • Silvermedal Chan Quan (Long Fist)

Wushu World Championship 2003 - Macau

  • Goldmedal Gun Shu (Stick)
