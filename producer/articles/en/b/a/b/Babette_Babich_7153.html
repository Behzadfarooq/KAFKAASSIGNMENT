Babette E. Babich (born 1956) is an academic philosopher and historian, known for her studies of Nietzsche.

Babich is currently Professor of Philosophy at Fordham University in New York City, and has taught as Visiting Professor at the University of Tuebingen and SUNY Stony Brook. She is also Adjunct Research Professor at Georgetown University. She received her Ph.D from Boston College in 1987, after studying with Hans-Georg Gadamer, Jacques Taminiaux and William Richardson, SJ. She has held three Fulbright Fellowships to Germany (Tuebingen, Berlin, Weimar). She is also Executive Secretary (Director) of the Nietzsche-Society in the United States and Founding Editor of the journal New Nietzsche Studies.

Babich has written extensively on aesthetics, philosophy of science and techonology (Feyerabend, Fleck, Kuhn, Mach, Bachelard, Duhem, Heidegger, Nietzsche, Heelan, Badiou) as well as on architecture, sculpture, and postmodern music. She has also written political analyses of the analytic-continental divide in philosophy and the Sokal hoax.
