Babybel

The Babybel, or Mini Babybel is a snack cheese. It was created in 1952 as a more portable update of the smash hit Bonbel cheese. It is a variety of Edam, encased in a distinctive red wax. The cheese is marketed as an all natural, convenient snack. It originates from France, and was originally manufactured by Fromageries Bel, though the brand has been franchised - notably to the Laughing Cow company. Other varieties of Babybel include Cheddar which is encased in a purple wax, Goat's cheese in a green wax, Gouda, and a 'light' variety. It is a common misconception that the common red wax, paraffin, used in the sealing of the cheese creates the distinct 'Babybel' taste, however, the unique taste that can be noted in Babybel is the adepsine oil which is secreted from the wax. Adepsine oil is often found in aged cheese (such as Babybel), so thus the taste of the Babybel is not attributed from the adepsine oil, but only highlighted by it.

A famous advertising jingle played on the lyrics of the Beach Boys song 'Barbara Ann'.

[edit] Ingredients

Babybel lists only four ingredients in the standard version, which are: cows milk, salt(1.8%), milk ferments, and vegetarian rennet. It also contains calcium (680mg per 100g), phosphorus, and vitamins A & B12.
