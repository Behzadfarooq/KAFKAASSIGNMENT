Babati is a small city (actually called Babati Towne) located in Tanzania, 172km south of Arusha. It is the capital of Manyara region. Babati is also a district and is bordered to the North by the Arusha Region, to the Southeast by the Simanjiro District, to the South by the Dodoma Region, to the Southwest by the Hanang District, and to the Northwest by the Mbulu District.

According to the 2002 Tanzania National Census, the population of the Babati District was 303,013.[1]

The District Commissioner of the Babati District is Hadija R.R. Nyembo.[2]

In 2003, King Edward VI Five Ways School in Birmingham, UK set up the Babati Link Group to promote educational links with Babati. The link group has worked closely with Babati Day Secondary School in the city of Babati.

[edit] Cultural Tourism

Babati is also home to Kahembe's Trekking and Cultural Safari's. Cultural tourism enables those interested to visit the rural areas, interact with the local people in their villages and homes, eating and drinking the various foods and drinks, joining local ceremonies/dances/rituals, so as to learn something about the country and thus enjoy the full experience of visiting and learning about Tanzania.
