Babo Violent 2 is a freeware multiplayer game for Windows. It is a 3d Top-down shooter similar to Crimsonland, combined with elements from Counter-Strike and Quake3.

[edit] Gameplay

In Babo Violent 2, the player controls a heavily armed sphere with customizable skin color and texture, known as a Babo. The game is played over the Internet or a LAN. There are a variety of game modes to choose from, ranging from the popular free-for-all (Deathmatch) to the team-based (Capture the Flag, TeamDeathmatch, Counter Baborist). In some modes holding or defending a flag is the primary goal, where in others it is simply to obtain the most frags.

For all game modes, the basic objective is to kill enemy players. Before spawning, the player is presented with a large arsenal of weapons to choose from. A few grenades and a molotov cocktail are also supplied by default and more grenades can be picked up from the ground. Some servers enable secondary functions like knifes, shields or nuclear kamikaze attacks. Upon death players leave back a health pack for healing injury, the weapon and any equipped grenades for others to pick up. After waiting for a server-specified amount of time (usually less than five seconds), players respawn and may choose another weaponâ€”this cycle repeats for the duration of the map. After a certain allotted time (usually 30 minutes), the map changes and scores are reset as players continue in another round. Players can chat with each other throughout matches (and to their own teams, for cooperative team-based game modes).

Most games are fast-paced and chaotic. Babos explode in a spectacle of blood.

[edit] History

The game was originally developed as a test for a new network library. Released only to get some feedback on the stability, the simple yet addictive gameplay attracted a growing fan base. Soon a community of hundreds of regulars and thousands of casual players formed, organized clans (called squads) and developed unofficial mods and additional content packs.

Surprised by the unexpected success the developers agreed to continue developing the game. However, its origin as a simple test caused some problems and a major rework was required. A lot of bugs and security problems need to be fixed and many elementary features are still unimplemented. Feeling a bit overburdened by this RndLabs announced to open source the game.[2]

[edit] Weapons

There are currently eight primary weapons officially available:

  • Sub Machine Gun
  • Shotgun
  • Sniper Rifle
  • Dual Machine Gun
  • Chain Gun
  • Bazooka
  • Photon Gun
  • Flamethrower

Additionally, shields, popup knifes, kamikaze nukes, grenades and molotov cocktails are available as secondary and tertiary weapons.

[edit] Tactics

Although the tactics commonly used in similar games translate well to Babo Violent 2, there are particular aspects that let players improve considerably.

  • In close combat, circlestrafing, or even any movement at all while at the same time shooting at enemies, is essential.
  • Newbies often resort to spamming while camping in a corner. Grenades can often be an effective countermeasure.
  • A slight lull in combat can be a good moment to reload a gun. For lack of a reload button this can be accomplished by picking up a gun from the ground.
  • The tangential attachment of guns allows taking cover behind corners while shooting.
  • Lacking the possibility of height advantage, sniping is most effective in large open maps.
