Babacar

Babacar was a short-lived British rock band formed by Caroline Crawley, former lead singer of Shelleyan Orphan, and her then-boyfriend Boris Williams, former drummer of The Cure. Porl Thompson, another former member of The Cure, also made some contributions to Babacar's self-titled album, and Jemaur Tayle, the other half of Shelleyan Orpan later joined them as well. Babacar only released two albums in their brief existence, Babacar and Bartolo. The band dissolved after Shelleyan Orphan reunited briefly in 2000.

[edit] Members

  • Caroline Crawley - vocals
  • Bruno Ellingham - violin
  • Tristan Powell - e-bow
  • Roberto Soave - bass guitar
  • Rob Steen - guitar
  • Jemaur Tayle - guitar
  • Boris Williams - drums

[edit] Special guests

  • Porl Thompson - guitar, banjo

[edit] Discography

  • Babacar (1998)
  • Bartolo (2000)

[edit] Trivia

  • Roberto Soave and Rob Steen have also previously worked with Lol Tolhurst, another former member of The Cure, in a band called Presence, which also involved Gary Biddles, a former Cure roadie. Biddles has also worked with Simon Gallup, another key member of The Cure, in a band called Fools Dance.
  • Roberto Soave has also played a brief stint with The Cure. When Simon Gallup fell ill during the European leg of the Wish tour, Soave filled in for him on bass. [1]
