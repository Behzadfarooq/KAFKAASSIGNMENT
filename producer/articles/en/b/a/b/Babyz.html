Babyz is a computer game in which one can play with and take care of a group of cute, mischievous babyz who live in a virtual house on the computer. The game was released in 1999 by The Learning Company, developed by members of PF Magic working there at the time.

Babyz were among the most advanced, directly interactive virtual characters, with rich personality, emotion, and artificial intelligence. They were among the first 3D characters rendered in a non-photorealistic cartoony style.

Babyz was built on top of the Petz 3 game engine, adding additional AI and voice recognition. For example, players can teach their Babyz baby talk, how to play with objects, and how to walk.

Babyz reused some of the Petz toyz and had a similar home setting for its playscenez. The rooms included:

  1. Nursery
  2. Changing Room (with Clothez Closet)
  3. Play Room (with Toy Shelf)
  4. Family Room
  5. Kitchen
  6. Back Yard
  7. Attic

[edit] Development

As with Petz, each of the initial 15 babyz were drawn from balls joined by linez or polygons made from four balls, with paintballs making up the hair. New babyz are made by users who have reverse-engineered the system, using a hex-editing tool they created.

Many of the file editing discoveries were made by Carolyn Horn and software has since been developed by Sherlock Software to edit files from all Ubisoft games.

Even today, years after the game's release, the Babyz community still lives through many forums and websites.

Babyz is now copyright Ubisoft Entertainment since they indirectly purchased the Entertainment Division of The Learning Company in 2002.
