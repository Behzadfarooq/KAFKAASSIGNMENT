Baby Sinclair, occasionally referred to as Junior, is the youngest child of Earl and Fran Sinclair on the television sitcom Dinosaurs. He has an elder brother, Robbie, and an elder sister, Charlene. He is perhaps one of the most popular and most marketed characters on the show.

Baby is associated with many of the show's catch phrases. One of the most frequent of these is the exclamation, "Not the mama!" to Earl, whom he sees with contempt. Often, with this, Baby will repeatedly hit his father in the head with a hard object, most frequently a frying pan. When Baby is hurt, he will throw his arms up enthusiastically and yell, "Again!" A music video was produced for a song based on another of Baby's catchphrases, "I'm the baby, gotta love me!"

Spoiler warning: Plot and/or ending details follow.

In the second-season episode "Switched at Birth", controversy arises when it is realized that Baby's egg may have been switched with the egg of Aubrey Molehill, who looks exactly like Baby, except he is green. At first, it appears that the babies have indeed been switched: Aubrey is green like the Sinclairs, and Baby is red-tinted like the Molehills. Also, Gus Molehill, the father, is wild and rambunctious like Baby and shares all of Baby's catchphrases. A series of tests suggests that Baby and Aubrey had been switched, but it is revealed at the end of the episode that the scientist had mixed up the test results.

Indeed, Baby is the character's legal name since the second-season episode "And The Winner Is..." At the beginning of the episode, it is revealed that Earl has only just realized that they were calling him Baby all the time. After taken to the Chief Elder to receive a name, Baby is temporarily named "Aaah Aagh I'm Dying You Idiot" until the new Chief Elder rightfully names him "Baby".

Spoilers end here.

Baby was voiced and puppeteered by Kevin Clash, best known as the voice of Elmo on Sesame Street. The laughs are nearly identical, and Baby actually sounds like a gleefully destructive Elmo.
