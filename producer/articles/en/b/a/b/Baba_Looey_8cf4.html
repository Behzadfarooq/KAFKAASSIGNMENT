Baba Looey

Baba Looey is a fictional character of the animated cartoon series Quick Draw McGraw. Baba Looey is a Mexican burro who is the faithful sidekick to Quick Draw, a horse caricature, whom he calls "Queekstraw".

The relationship between Baba Looey and Quick Draw was very similar to that of another pair from the Hanna-Barbera stable, Yogi Bear and Boo-Boo Bear: the somewhat dim-witted, idealistic and adventurous leader, and the more intelligent, practical and cautious follower; a timeless literary device, as with Don Quixote and Sancho Panza, and Jeeves and Wooster, for example.

The name "Baba Looey" is almost certainly taken from the song "Babalu", which was a major hit for Desi Arnaz in the 1940s, and which re-appeared from time to time in the 1950s hit TV series I Love Lucy.

[edit] Trivia

  • Gary Dell'Abate, producer of The Howard Stern Show, derives his nickname Baba Booey from his botched pronunciation of Baba Looey's name.
  • In Spanish-speaking Latin America, he is known as Pepe Trueno (Joe Thunder) or Pepe Luis (a colloquial version of the name "José Luis").
  • In Brazil he is known as Babalú.
