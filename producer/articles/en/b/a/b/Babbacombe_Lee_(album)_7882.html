Babbacombe Lee (album)

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

"Babbacombe Lee" is Fairport Convention's only concept album. It is constructed around the life story of John "Babbacombe" Lee, a Victorian-era condemned murderer.

[edit] Album Concept

The album is a concept album that follows John "Babbacombe" Lee's life story. The events of his life are described in song, from his boyhood through his conviction for murder, sentence of death, and the failure to carry out the sentence. The songs describe his boyhood poverty, his time in the Royal Navy, and his being invalided out. The album then describes how Lee went to work in the service of certain Miss Keyes. While Lee was in her service, she was murdered, and he was accused, tried and convicted of the crime, for which Lee was sentenced to death. However, when authorities attempted to hang him, the gallows failed three times, resulting in his release. These events are all told in song, and all but one of those songs are originals. The original album did not have titles, however, for the individual songs, instead stringing them together in longer sections. Later releases of the album on compact disk have provided separate track listings for each song or fragment.

Fairport fiddle-player, Dave Swarbrick, has explained that he came upon the idea for the album after discovering a file of old newspaper clippings in a junk shop. The file contained John Lee's own copies of the newspaper articles and was bound by him, signed and dated January 30, 1908.

Because of its relatively complete narrative structure, Babbacombe Lee has been called a rock opera. Because of the band's musical style, the album has been called the first folk rock opera.

[edit] Album History and Personnel

Released in November of 1971, on Island Records, the album was generally acclaimed by critics. It received some radio airplay in the United States. However, it sold very poorly, being the least successful album commercially since the band's debut.

The band members playing on the album are:

Simon Nicol - guitar, dulcimer; Dave Mattacks - drums, electric piano; Dave Swarbrick - fiddle, mandolin; Dave Pegg - bass, mandolin.

The album was recorded between August and September 1971 at Sound Techniques London. It was issued on Island Records ILPS 9175 (LP) and first issues on CD as Island Records IMCD 153 (CD).

[edit] Album Tracks

The tracks from the original album appeared as follows:

  1. John's Reflections On His Boyhood...
  2. This Was The Happiest Period In His Life...
  3. Tragedy Now Strikes hard...
  4. John Was Hardly More Than A Bewildered Observer...
  5. When It Comes He Cannot Sleep...

Each of these five sections is composed of a number of songs and fragments of songs that were not originally listed on the album.

The 2004 [Compact Disk] release of the album abandoned the original five part division of the album and lists the songs as separate tracks complete with songwriting credit. The list of tracks is as follows:

  1. The Verdict (Read by Philip Sterling-Wall) 0'28"
  2. Little Did I Think (Dave Swarbrick) 2'19"
  3. I Was Sixteen [Part 1] (Simon Nicol / Dave Pegg) 1'29"
  4. John My Son (Simon Nicol / Dave Pegg) 0'44"
  5. I Was Sixteen [Part 2] (Simon Nicol / Dave Pegg) 1'17"
  6. St Ninian's Isle (Ronald Cooper) / Trumpet Hornpipe (Trad. Arr. Dave Swarbrick) 1'14"
  7. Sailor's Alphabet (Trad. Arr. A.L. Lloyd) 5'50"
  8. John Lee (Dave Swarbrick) 3'04"
  9. Newspaper Reading (Read by A. L. Lloyd) 0'46"
  10. Breakfast In Mayfair (Simon Nicol) 3'09"
  11. Trial Song (Dave Swarbrick, Dave Swarbrick / Dave Pegg) 3'55"
  12. Cell Song (Dave Swarbrick) 3'35"
  13. The Time Is Near (Dave Pegg) 2'31"
  14. Dream Song (Dave Swarbrick, Dave Swarbrick / Dave Pegg) 5'24"
  15. Wake Up John [Hanging Song] (Dave Swarbrick / Dave Pegg) 5'25"

Two additional bonus tracks appear on some releases of the album on CD after 2004:

  1. Farewell To A Poor Man's Son (Dave Swarbrick) 4'55"
  2. Breakfast In Mayfair (Simon Nicol) 3'59"

These tracks were recorded in late 1974 for the BBC 2 Documentary about John Lee narrated by Melvyn Bragg. The programme was broadcast in the BBC 2 " 2nd House" Series as " The Man They Couldn't Hang - John Lee" on February 1, 1975. The are performed by a different lineup of the band than performed on the original album, one formed after the departure of [Simon Nicol] and the return of lead singer [Sandy Denny]

Personnel on these tracks are Dave Swarbrick, Dave Pegg, Dave Mattacks, Trevor Lucas, Jerry Donahue ave Swarbrick, Dave Pegg, Dave Mattacks, Trevor Lucas, Jerry Donahue, Sandy Denny. Denny sings only on "Breakfast In Mayfair"
