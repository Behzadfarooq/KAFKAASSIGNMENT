Babia Góra National Park

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Babia Góra National Park
IUCN Category II (National Park)
Location: Lesser Poland Voivodeship, Poland
Area: 33,92 km²
Established: 1954
Governing body: Ministry of the Environment

Babia Góra National Park (Polish: Babiogórski Park Narodowy) is one of the 23 national parks in Poland, located in southern part of the country, in Lesser Poland Voivodeship, along the border with Slovakia. The Park headquarters is located in the village of Zawoja (address of the headquarters - 34-223 Zawoja 1403, Poland).

The area of the park is 33.92 square kilometres, of which forests take up 31.98 square kilometres. The park covers northern and southern sides of the Babia Gora massif together with the highest peak of Beskid Wysoki - Diablak (1725 meters). The area of the park was for the first time legally protected in 1933, when the Babia Gora Preserve was created. On October 30, 1954 it was transformed into the National Park and since 1977 it is considered by UNESCO as one of world's biosphere reserves and as such is part of MaB (UNESCO'S Man and the Biosphere Programme).

[edit] Fauna

  • 105 species of birds (including woodpeckers and eagle owls).
  • Animals such as deer, lynx, wolves and bears
  • Insects, especially beetles ,including some which are unique for the area.
