Babahoyo founded the 30 of Septiembre of 1948(Legislative decree)is the capital of Los Ríos Province, Ecuador. It is surrounded by two rivers, the San Pablo and the Caracol, which joined form the Babahoyo river, which flows into the Guayas river, eventually ending in the sea. One of the most interesting excursions that the area offers is sailing down the Babahoyo, taking in the countryside and the traditional lifestyle of the area's campesinos who live off of the land, sowing crops and raising cattle. One feature of this journey that is sure to catch your attention will be the floating houses unique to the area.

A processing and trade centre for the surrounding agricultural region, the city handles rice, sugarcane, fruits, balsa wood, and tagua nuts (vegetable ivory). Rice and sugar are milled.

[edit] Attractions

Noteworthy attractions of the capital itself include the verdant gardens of Parque 24 de Mayo and the city's main cathedral, which is decorated with an enormous mosaic mural depicting the Virgin Mary.

[edit] Gastronomy

In Babahoyo the main plate is the "ceviche" of shrimp, fish and shell. Also another plate that is famous in Babahoyo is the "Caldo de manguera(sausage soup)."

[edit] Transportation

Most bus companies running between Quito and Guayaquil stop or passes through Babahoyo. Quevedo connects with Guayaquil by two highways, one through Balzar and Daule (not very popular); and the other one that passes through Babahoyo.
