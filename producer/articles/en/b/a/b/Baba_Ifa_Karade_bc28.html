Baba Ifa Karade also known as Baba Oloye Ifa Karade has been involved in the Ifá/Yoruba religion for over two decades. As the chief priest and founder of his temple, Ile Tawo Lona (The Temple of the Mystic Path) located in East Orange, New Jersey. He was initiated by Yoruba elders, into the priestly orders of Obatala and Ifá while in Ejibo, Nigeria in 1991. In 1992 Baba returned to Nigeria to complete his rites and make the pilgrimage to the Holy city of Ile-Ife. Baba Oloye Ifa Karade graduated from Rutgers University with a focus on Education and Africana/ Eastern Studies. He received a Teachers Certificate for the state of New Jersey. He served in the US Military 82nd Airborne and was honorable discharge. Baba Oloye is also an African Musicologist and formed his own band called Fa Fusion, which specializes in African drum rhythms with a touch of Jazz.

Baba is also the author of four texts on the subject of Ifa/Yoruba traditional religion: The symbolic reasoning for creating four texts is to mark his journey in the tradition and to honor each direction of the world. As it is customary to start from the east when honoring the day; the book of the east is: The Handbook of Yoruba Religious Concepts, (1994); This book ranks as a best seller in the African Religious category.[citation needed] It has been translated into German, Spanish and Dutch. The Handbook of Yoruba Concepts is an introductary volume on the Yoruba/Ifa tradition. It is a reference book for the initiated and a guidebook for the uninitiated. This book explains the origins of the religion. It also goes into detail about how to practice the tradition with prayers and songs included. Ojise- Messenger of the Yoruba Tradition (1996); is the book of the west. This book is about Baba Oloye's journey in the tradition. This book has been translated in to German and Dutch. It is a personal journey in the transformation of his life to an Ifa priest. He takes us step by step on his path in the tradition and his travels to Africa.

The reader gets to walk the journey of an Orisha and Ifa priest with Baba in this book. The book of the North is Imoye- A Definition of the Yoruba Tradition (1999). This book has made groundbreaking success in the West Indies as well as the United States. Imoye is a study guide for those who are interested in the tradition and who wish to be initiated by Baba Oloye Ifa Karade.

The book of the south is the unpublished manuscript called Ka Maa w’Orisha which was created in (2002). This book is focus on the Ifa Corpus: the sacred text of Odu. It is the basic guide on how to live Ifa as a lifestyle.
