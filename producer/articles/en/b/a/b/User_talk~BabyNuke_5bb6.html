[edit] Quick reply to yours on zoophilia

Yes, a template makes sense. At present though, 1/2 of the relevant material or articles aren't yet written, or are in draft from only. Once various editors get those in order, then a template would be an obvious navigational aid. Much of my current work on the article agrees with your suggestion -- I'm trying to farm off the major areas to their own articles, or as you say "larger sections be given their own smaller articles" (ie, Summary style).

I've set the titling issue to one side for now, though. Rather than discuss it now, may I make a suggestion? Let's get the asa article done, and a couple of others being drafted, and then look at them overall. My concern here is that the field itself has confusing namings, and in trying to copy those we're inheriting their contradictions. For example, zoophilia to many people means sex with animals, its widest use seems to be affinity/attraction/sexuality, and psychologically its a paraphilia. Culturally zoosexuality is identical to zoophilia but in sexology its utterly a different concept. technically much of "zoophilia" actually relates to zoosexuality, only its more commonly known as zoophilia. The present titles try to respect the major uses of the main terms - and you're right, it's probably worth discussing (in the sense its logical but doesn't match people's expectations).

What I'd do to solve this, if you're agreeable, is tackle it the other way round: move what we need to into separate focussed articles, summary style (makes it simpler), then look at what's left, consider what articles (covering what) should be useful or what confusions exist, and then discuss between editors what titles each should be given individually to the necessary articles (based upon their content and focus).

FT2 (Talk | email) 22:27, 5 July 2006 (UTC)
