Baby William (b. 2001) is a fictional character on the popular science-fiction television show The X-Files. The youngest re-occurring character, William appears at the end of Season 8 and during many episodes of Season 9.

Spoiler warning: Plot and/or ending details follow.

William is the son of Special Agent Dana Scully. It is never explained how William is conceived, as Scully was left barren as a result of her abduction. Near the end of the series, Scully makes the decision to have a child on her own through in-vitro fertilization, and asks her FBI partner, Fox Mulder, to be the donor, who agrees in episode "Per Manum". It is unclear if she was successful in her efforts. Shortly afterward, she and Mulder began a relationship and when William is born, Mulder alludes to a 'truth [they] both know'. By the show's last episode, both agents refer to William as "their son." In addition, when faced with the accusation that she and Mulder "were lovers and she got pregnant and had his love child," she does not deny it. Thus, although it is never explicitly proven and left to speculation, William was most likely fathered by Mulder. William was born in the episode "Existence," out of Democrat Hot Springs, in Northern Georgia.

William is evidently of some importance to the New Syndicate, who frequently had him and Scully under surveillance. During Season 9, William turns out to have some paranormal abilities, including telekinesis. After a visit from a disguised Jeffrey Spender, William is injected with a magnetite substance, and, according to Spender, seemingly cured of his paranormal abilities. After being kidnapped by a UFO cult, but safely rescued, and because of growing danger to William from conspirators, Scully decides to give him up for adoption in "William." William appears to be around 10-12 months of age at this time.

He was named after Scully and Mulder's fathers, both of whom are named William.
