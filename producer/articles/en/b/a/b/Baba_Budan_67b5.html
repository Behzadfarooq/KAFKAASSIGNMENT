Baba Budan

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Baba Budan (Hindi: बाबा बुडन, Urdu: بابا بُڈن) was a 17th century Sufi, revered by both Muslims and Hindus, whose shrine is at Baba Budangiri, India. According to legend, he introduced coffee to India by bringing beans from the port of Mocha, Yemen.
