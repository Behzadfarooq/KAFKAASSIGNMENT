Baby AT is a motherboard standard equivalent to the AT format.

It was created by IBM in 1985. It was the motherboard format used in the computers from the 286 processors to the first Pentiums.

The size (220x330 mm) and the flexibility of this kind of motherboard were the key of success of this format, but when the chips began to generate heat, Baby AT was consigned to oblivion because the proximity of the components produced the increase of the CPU temperature.
