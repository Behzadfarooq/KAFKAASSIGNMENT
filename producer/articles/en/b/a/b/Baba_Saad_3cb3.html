Baba Saad (November 26, 1985 in Beirut, Lebanon), born Saad Al-Haddad (Arabic: سعد الحداد ), is a German rapper of Lebanese heritage.

[edit] Early Life

Baba Saad was born in Lebanon, but came to Germany in 1997 because of the Lebanese Civil War. He first lived with his family in Syke, but later moved to Bremen. After his graduation from school,the German rapper Bushido, took him, also known as Saad, under his wing.

[edit] Career

When Bushido heard Saad rap for the first time, he immediately asked him to participate in the making of "Electro Ghetto." In early 2005, Saad is heard alongside Bushido in the joint album "Carlo, Cokxxx, Nutten II." The best-known single off of "CCN 2" was "Nie ein Rapper," which immediately climbed the German singles chart. Since the summer of 2005, he has been under contract with Bushido's music label, ersguterjunge. At the end of 2005, Saad was featured on Bushido's album "Staatsfeind Nr. 1," and continued playing on the tour that followed.

In the spring of 2006, Saad released his own album, called "Das Leben ist Saad." He has also appeared in compilations for his label, in which he has both appeared solo and with other members of the label. Saad also has recently been featured on the track "Auge des Sturms" with Azad.
