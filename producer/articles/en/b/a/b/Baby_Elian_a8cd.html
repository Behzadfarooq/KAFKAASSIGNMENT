"Baby Elian" is a song by Welsh rock group Manic Street Preachers, from the album Know Your Enemy. It refers to the Elián González affair of early and mid-2000. The song is critical of the USA, with lines like "Blockades won't win you more votes" (referring to the Cuban Adjustment Act), and "Across the unfair divide/Where black will never meet white" citing the perceived racism in the USA, compared to Cuba. The song has references to other American-Cuban issues, such as the Bay of Pigs Invasion, and Operation Peter Pan.

The band often has Cuban and Cuban-American references in their songs, such as in "Let Robeson Sing" ("Went to Cuba to meet Castro/Never got past sleepy Moscow") and "The Convalescent" ("Alberto Juantorena unique in his field/These are the things that, that make you feel").
