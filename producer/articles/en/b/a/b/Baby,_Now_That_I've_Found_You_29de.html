"Baby Now That I've Found You" is the name of a song written by Tony Macauley and John MacLeod. In 1967, The Foundations released it as their debut single. It met with great success, becoming a top twenty hit on the Billboard Hot 100 and topping the UK Singles Chart for two weeks in November 1967. The lyrics are a plea that an unnamed subject not break up with the singer.

The song was later a 1995 country hit for Alison Krauss from the album Now That I've Found You: A Collection.

In Philippines, it was revived by MYMP on the album MYMP Live at the Music Museum.
