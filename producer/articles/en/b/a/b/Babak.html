Babak (Persian: بابک ) is a common Persian male name. It is the modern form of its Pahlavi root, Papak (Persian: پاپک ), meaning "Young father". An alternate spelling is Bobak. Two famous historical figures named Babak are:

  • Babak Khorramdin (eighth century), the leader of Khurramites movement who fought against the Abbasid Caliphate.
  • Ardashir I of Persia (Ardashir Papakan (Babakan) (third Century), the founder of the Sassanid Empire.
  • Babak (given name)
  • Babak Castle
  • Shahr-e-Babak in Kerman Province
