Babel-17 Language is an artificial language first put forth in Samuel R. Delany's 1966 science-fiction novel Babel-17.

One of its interesting linguistic features is the absence of a pronoun or any other construction for "I".

The novel takes an implicitly Whorfian view of language, as the heroine finds her perceptions (and even her physical abilities) altered once she has learned Babel-17. In this it resembles a number of other science fiction novels which deal with language, such as Native Tongue by Suzette Haden Elgin and The Languages of Pao by Jack Vance.
