Baby, The Stars Shine Bright

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Baby, The Stars Shine Bright (Shortened to Baby or BTSSB) is the name of a Japanese clothing boutique chain created in 1988 by Akinori Isobe and his wife, Fumiyo. The original fashion house is located in Shibuya, Tokyo, Japan. Baby specializes in Lolita fashion, and is mainly associated with the "sweet Lolita" subcategory of Lolita.

The CEO and creator, Akinori Isobe, states in an interview for the Lolita-rich Shimotsuma Monogatari (aka Kamikaze Girls) book, film, and comic, that he has plans to make an official English-language version of the Baby website and formally enter the American and European markets [1]. He has not done this yet, but there is talk of a Baby the Stars Shine Bright opening in San Francisco, although there still are English-language instructions on how to order from the all-Japanese website. [2] Currently there is a Baby the Stars Shine Bright in Paris, France. The New York City branch of Kinokuniya has also been selling clothing from the label, including dresses, headdresses, shoes and socks.

As mentioned in Shimotsuma Monogatari, the label's name was taken from the title of an album by English pop music group Everything But the Girl.

[edit] SubBrands

[edit] Hello Kitty's and Charmmy Kitty

Baby, The Stars Shine Bright also sells other brand names such as a variant on the Hello Kitty character: Hello Kitty's pet cat, Charmmy Kitty. The Charmmy Kitty dolls they sell relate with the store's own gothic lolita and sweet lolita fashion style. Hello Kitty has played a role in the sweet lolita fashion as an on-side accessory. As it will not be easy to find a Lolita carrying a Hello Kitty or Charmmy Kitty doll on the streets, the character instead might reside in the girl's bedroom, cellphone, accessories, and so on for decoration.

In relation to Charmmy Kitty, Japanese author Novala Takemoto's Pour Lolita version of Hello Kitty has become a prevalent icon among wearers. The character is often linked to Baby, the Stars Shine Bright, appearing wearing a pink bonnet and carrying a lacey handbag strongly reminiscent of the fashions offered by this brand.

[edit] Pour Lolita

Pour Lolita is Novala Takemoto's line of clothing as sponsored by Baby, the Stars Shine Bright. "God Save The Lolita" is the catch phrase of the Pour Lolita line.

[edit] Alice and the Pirates

"Alice and the Pirates" is a line of Baby, the Stars Shine Bright entirely pirate themed. A store devoted to this line opened in Harajuku on the 25th of August 2006.

[edit] Baby and Kamikaze Girls

Baby, The Stars Shine Bright was featured in the movie, book, and manga of Shimotsuma Monogatari (English title "Kamikaze Girls"). One of the lead characters, Momoko, is obsessed with Baby clothing, and wears Lolita fashion throughout the story.
