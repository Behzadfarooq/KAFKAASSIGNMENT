Mazaar-e-Fakhri, Taherabad (Galiako)

The Dawoodi Bohra community acknowledges Babjee Moula Syedi Fakhruddin Ash-Shaheed to be one of their most noble and faithful leaders in service of Dawat, one who was brave enough to give his life for the love of his religion. Moulaya Yaqoob was the representative of Dawat in India at that time, or the Vaali of Hind. He sent Syedi Fakhruddin Ash-Shaheed to that part of Rajasthan which is called Waghar. There, he established Dawat and undertook the task of leading the community of Dawoodi Bohras living there. In Galiakot, he delivered "waaz" in the beginning of Muharam-ul-Haraam, remembered the sad incident of Karbala, made the mumineen weep on Imam Hussain (AS) and do maatam, an expression of grief. There, he took the monetary collections, or waajebaat, of the mumineen.

On 27th of Muharram-ul-Haraam, when he was leaving Galiakot and was still near the town, some dacoits attacked on him near a pool. He fought with them courageously and his companions were killed. When the sun set, he performed the compulsory prayers (namaaz). At that time the dacoits attacked from behind and slaughtered him there. When the mumineen of Galiakot heard about this incident, they came quickly to that place, but found that their had Maula breathed His last breath.

According to Shariat, mumineen buried him in that place. Afterwards, that place became a centre of ziyarat of thousands of people - Dawoodi Bohras and Non-Bohras. He was called "Babjee Maula".

Syedi Fakhruddin Shaheed (AQ) Steel melts at the threshold of his house. Like the shaan of Dawood Nabi (AS), this is one of the most renowned miracles associated with Syedi Fakhruddin Shaheed (AQ) who rests in Galiakot, which is commonly referred to as Taherabad by the Dawoodi Bohras. Upon his grave a mausoleum (qubba mubaraka) has been built. It is of elaborately carved and pristinely kept white marble. Anyone who has visited Galiakot has felt the tranquility that surrounds the qubba, and been moved by the conviction that wishes will be granted here. Syedi Fakhruddin (AQ) is known as Babjisaab throughout Waghar. Mumineen from far and wide flock here for ziyarat: some even come on foot from miles away from places like Sagwara and Waswara. Even the local tribals (Bheels and others) pay their tribute at his threshold. Syedi Fakhruddin Shaheed (AQ) was sent by the Wali of Hind to Waghar for dawat. At the beginning of the year in Moharram, he narrated the shahadat of Imam Husain (AS) moving one and all to tears and maatam. He collected the wajebaat of mumineen. On the 27th of Moharram he bid them farewell and started out on his journey. On the outskirts of the village, near a lake, he was attacked by bandits. Rajput that he was, he fought them valiantly until sunset, when he took a break to pray maghrib namaaz. The bandits seized the opportunity and killed him. Syedi Fakhruddin (AQ) is the son of Maulaya Tarmal. As reward for the selfless khidmet of his father and himself, Duat Mutlaqeen (RA) and Hudud Fodala followed in his progeny. Aqa Maula (TUS) Syedna Mohammed Burhanuddin (TUS) is a descendant of Syedi Fakhruddin Shaheed (RA). Syedi Abdeali Imaduddin (RA) says of him: If he so directed the moon, it would fall at his feet. If he so directed a corpse, it would come alive.
