"Baby We've Got a Date" is a 1973 song by Bob Marley & The Wailers released on the Catch a Fire album. An alternate title of the song is "Rock It Baby".

The song is composed by Marley who takes the lead vocals. The backing vocals are taken by Peter Tosh and Bunny Wailer along with Marcia Griffiths and Rita Marley of the I-Threes. The musical key is a major; the song has a length of about four minutes.

"Baby We've Got a Date" is about a girl and love (also sexual) affairs. The girl has got a date for the evening and is pleased to have some faith and not to be late. The couple are walking in the pale moonlight and are happy about the love they share. The repeating chorus passages "rock it baby!" and "we got to get together" refer to the couple's happiness and the physical love, although less clearly than in Marley's "Stir It Up".

There's an alternate version of "Baby We've Got a Date" which was originally only released in Jamaica, but has been included on the deluxe edition of the Catch a Fire album.
