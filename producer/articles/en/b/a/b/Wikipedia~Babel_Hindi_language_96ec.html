The Hindi language, one of the world's major languages, has not yet had its User-language-level-tags and its matching Language-level-category-headers formatted in Wiki (Aug-2005). This is quite amazing, considering that India happens to be the world's greatest source of software programmers! Here is what the User-language-level-tags look like now:

Wikipedia:Babel
hi-1 यह सदस्य हिन्दी भाषा का प्रारंभिक ज्ञान रखते हैं।
hi-2 यह सदस्य हिन्दी भाषा का मध्यम स्तर का ज्ञान रखते हैं।
hi-3 यह सदस्य हिन्दी भाषा में प्रवीण है।
hi-4 यह सदस्य हिन्दी का मातृभाषा जैसा ज्ञान रखते है।
hi इस सदस्य की मातृभाषा हिन्दी है।
Search user languages

They are supposed to have roughly the same phrasing in each of them, but alas, they still need much work. It appears that User level 1 is the nearest to being complete. I inserted my poor hindi into it to try to get it started (Scott P. 13:21:12, 2005-08-18 (UTC)). These User-language-level-tags can be found at:

  1. Template:User_hi-1
  2. Template:User_hi-2
  3. Template:User_hi-3
  4. Template:User_hi-4
  5. Template:User_hi

The matching Language-level-category-headers also need to be formatted to match, and still need 'bahut cam'. They can also be found at:

  1. Hindi level 1 category
  2. Hindi level 2 category
  3. Hindi level 3 category
  4. Hindi level 4 category
  5. Hindi level 'native' category

Again, these headers should properly all match, and I have attempted to do Category 1, to which the others should probably be matched.
