Babbu Mann is a well-established Indian Punjabi pop singer, actor, lyricist, composer and playback singer. Mann is an alumnus of Punjab University, Chandigarh. His first album, Tu meri Miss India (1999) was a moderate hit. His second album Saun Di Jhadi (T-Series, India) was more successful.

His next album Pyaas (T-Series, India) released in May 2005 was a huge success. This album earned him a lot of fame. The album was his best-selling album in India, selling more than 10 million copies worldwide. He is the only Punjabi singer to release four albums consecutavely that sold over 5 million copies. He also produced and worked as a singer and music director for a bollywood movie called Hawayein. Later Pyaas Babbu Mann came out with an album called Rabb Ne Baneyian Jodeyian which is a movie track (2006).

His most recent album is called Mera Gham (2007). Babbu mann is one of the highest paid musicians in punjab for live performances. He has been known to drink before going on stage. He writes his own music and produces his own albums. Many of his fans have begun to call him the modern-day Chamkila because of his song Punjab in Rabb Ne Banaiyaan Joidean, where he talks about strips clubs, marajuana, and taking a virgin girl to a sugarcane farm. In his recent album he has a song where he says "Can I have a one night stand with you?"


[edit] Drugs

There have been rumors that Babbu Mann is on drugs, because of this his father in India has removed Babbu Mann from his family. In 2005 Mann released his album Pyass. On the photo of Babbu Mann he had longer, oilier hair, along with a short weedy beard, and his skin seemed unnoticabally darker than before.
