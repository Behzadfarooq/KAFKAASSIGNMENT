For fun, I want to look up all these bits from Kevin Kelly's Listof "Recursive devices." I'm not sure why they're called "recursive." But the basic idea is that these are key devices and practices in the history of hte development of the Scientific method.

version 1 version 2

Sorted together:

  1. 200 (280?) BC — Cataloged Library of Alexandria: First text indexes
  2. 1000 — Collaborative encyclopedia
  3. 1410 — Cross-referenced Encyclopedia
  4. 1550 — Invention of the Fact
  5. 1590 — Controlled experiment (Roger Bacon)
  6. 1600 — Laboratory
  7. 1609 — Telescopes (Galileo (for astronomy, anyway)) and microscopes (Galileo!)
  8. 1650 — Society of experts
  9. 1665 — Repeatability (Robert Boyle)
  10. 1665 — Scholarly journals
  11. 1675 — Peer review
  12. 1687 — Hypothesis/prediction (Isaac Newton)
  13. 1752 — Peer Review Referee
  14. 1780 — Journal Network
  15. 1920 — Falsifiability (Karl Popper)
  16. 1926 — Randomized design (Ronald Fisher)
  17. 1937 — Controlled Placebo
  18. 1946 — Computer simulation
  19. 1950 — Double blind experiment
  20. 1962 — Study of scientific method (Thomas Kuhn)
  21. 1974 — Meta-analysis
