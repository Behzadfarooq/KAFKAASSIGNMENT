A surname

  • Corneliu Baba (1906 - 1997), a Romanian painter.
  • Meher Baba (1894 - 1969), a religious author.
  • Jaroslav Baba, a Czech high jumper.
  • Baba (馬場) is a Japanese name.

In folklore

  • Baba Yaga, a witch in Slavic mythology and Hungarian folklore.
  • Bau (goddess), a goddess in Sumerian and Akkadian mythology.

A title

  • Derived from its original meaning of "father", the name has become a term of endearment in India, especially for religious figures or sadhus, e.g.
    • The Standing Baba, Hindu holy men who practice self-castigation by never sitting or lying down.
    • Shrii Shrii Anandamurti, who was known to his followers as Baba, leader of the Indian socio-spiritual movement Ananda Marga.
    • Sai Baba of Shirdi (1838 - 1918)
    • Sathya Sai Baba (born circa 1926-1929), a famous Indian guru in Puttaparthi.
  • Certain Indian feudal lords (some styled Maharajas, but not ruling princes), notably in Rajasthan.
  • In Turkish, also derived from father, it is used for saints, e.g.
    • Gül Baba
    • Gani Baba
    • Somuncu Baba

In cuisine

  • Baba warszawska or babka, a Polish cuisine yeast cake.
  • Rum baba or baba au rhum is a small yeast cake saturated in liquor.
  • Baba ghanoush is a Middle Eastern eggplant mezze.

In geography

  • Baba Mountain in Manasdır .
  • Baba Dağ in Bosnia
  • Babadağ, a city in Denizli. It means baba mountain
  • Sahi Baba and Laghar Baba, cities in Afghanistan.

An ethnonym

  • One of the Bamileke ethnic groups of Cameroon.
  • Baba-Nyonya are Peranakan people in the Malaysian Straits

Literature:

  • Ali Baba of Ali Baba and the Forty Thieves.

A Coptic month

  • Baba is another name for Paopi, the second month of the Coptic calendar

Popular culture:

  • Baba Booey, a comedic alias used by Gary Dell'Abate, producer of the Howard Stern Show.
  • Uranai Baba, a character in the manga series Dragon Ball.
  • Baba Orninion is a pilot in the F-Zero series.
  • Baba (2002 film), a Kollywood film by Suresh Krishna.
  • Baba O'Riley, first track from the album "Who's Next" by British rock group The Who.
