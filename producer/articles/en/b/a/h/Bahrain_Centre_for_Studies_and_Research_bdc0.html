The Bahrain Centre for Studies and Research (BCSR) was established with the mission to serve the Bahraini community by conducting applied research, particularly of a contractual type, and to offer consultancies to leaders and decision makers in both the public and the private sectors.

Since the inauguration of the Bahrain Centre for Studies and Research (BCSR), the Centre has been determined to disseminate information on the critical issues and problems which affect the growth, prosperity and development of the Kingdom of Bahrain, using professional and scientific research methodologies.

The BSCR has never neglected its role as a consultant and advisor. Nor has it forsaken the need, to retreat to fundamentals in some cases in order to devise efficient solutions to overcome particular barriers that stand in the way of achieving sustainable development in the Kingdom of Bahrain.

According to the goals of the Bahrain Centre for Studies and Research, the Centre undertakes strategic and scientific research, including surveys, observational studies and experiments for local or external organizations, which are funded either by the Centre or from outside organizations on a contractual basis. The request for contractual research reflects the expertise, high reputation and the degree of confidence that the BCSR has attained.

The BCSR has never stinted from the support and encouragement of Bahraini researchers. It has continuously sought to encourage, direct and support the efforts of researchers in the scientific and technical fields. This has been achieved through several programmes, the most notable being the Crown Prince's Award for Scientific Research, which supports young researchers and offers both academic and financial recognition for work in the fields of the natural sciences, engineering, health sciences and the social sciences.

The Centre also seeks to fully meet its responsibilities with regard to scientific research in the Kingdom of Bahrain and endeavours to seize all opportunities to promote scientific research and study in support of the efforts of the wise leadership of the Kingdom and for the benefit and interests of the Bahraini people and society. The government of Bahrain has also fully supported and taken a keen interest in the work of the Centre. Their continued support has ensured that the reputation and status of Bahrain is, and will remain, on a par with other developed countries in this new age of development.

The current Crown Prince of Bahrain, Shaikh Salman was appointed Chairman of the Board of Trustees of the Bahrain Centre for Studies and Research (BCSR) in 1995.
