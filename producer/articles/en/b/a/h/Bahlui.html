Bahlui

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Iaşi County

Bahlui is the largest river of Iaşi County, in eastern Romania. Its name is derived from Cuman and it means "muddy river". The Bahlui river has a length of 104 km and an average volume flowing of about 2.8 m³/s. The surface of the basin has about 1915 km².

Its spring is located at an altitude of 500 metres in the Tudora Comune, Botoşani County, in the eastern part of Suceava Plateau. It flows through the Jijia Plain, from north-west toward south-east and through the cities of Hârlău (formerly named after the river: Târgul Bahluiului) and Iaşi.

Its most important affluents are Gurguiata, Duruşca, Hoiseni, Valea Lupului, Ciric, Bahlueţ, Nicolina, Voineşti and Valea Locii. The quality of its waters is rather low, due to spills of industrial plants, especially in Iaşi.
