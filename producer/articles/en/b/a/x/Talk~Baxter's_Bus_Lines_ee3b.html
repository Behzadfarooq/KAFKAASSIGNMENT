[edit] Regarding deletion proposal

Oppose. This article deals with a bus company that previously operated bus routes and now operates as a charter company. It contributes to the history of public transport in Sydney. I see no reason why it is not notable and it should not be deleted Quaidy 10:17, 13 December 2006 (UTC)

Support. That it once existed as a public transport operator can be noted at Buses in Sydney and 800 series bus routes, Sydney. The remaining company does not, so far as I can see, satisfy generally-accepted criteria listed at Wikipedia:Notability (companies and corporations). Joestella 10:37, 13 December 2006 (UTC)

Wikipedia deals with the past as well as the present. The fact that something no longer exists or is no longer notable in its current form does not affect whether it gets an article. We have many articles about companies that simply no longer exist or are now entirely dormant, existing only because no one bothered to disincorporate them. --NE2 16:41, 13 December 2006 (UTC)
I think the company's claim to notability was always tenuous. And now that it's no longer part of a notable public transport network, what little there is to be said of it can be said on Buses in Sydney and 800 series bus routes, Sydney. Joestella 17:13, 13 December 2006 (UTC)
I disagree; I think any company that operated bus routes (assuming the buses were branded by them, and it wasn't simply a contract for the city or other government) is notable. Your comments are not a reason to delete in any case; please use templates like template:mergeto. --NE2 18:23, 13 December 2006 (UTC)
Almost all the existing info is on other pages, though. Joestella 19:42, 13 December 2006 (UTC)
Did you not read what I said? That is a reason to merge, not delete. --NE2 22:53, 13 December 2006 (UTC)

Folks, don't forget to get your voices heard at the AfD... B.Wind 22:01, 14 December 2006 (UTC)
