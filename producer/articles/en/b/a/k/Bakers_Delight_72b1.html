Bakers Delight

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

The original Bakers Delight logo.

Bakers Delight is a chain of bakeries operating throughout Australia and New Zealand. They are also affiliated with COBS Bread in Canada.

Bakers Delight operates on a franchising business model, although there are some Company Owned Bakeries.

Bakers Delight, the world's largest bakery franchise, was established in 1980 as a single bakery on Glenferrie Road in the Melbourne suburb of Hawthorn. Bakers Delight celebrated its 25th birthday in 2005 and boasts 700 bakeries employing more than 15,000 people, serving 2.5 million regular customers per week throughout Australia, New Zealand, and Canada.

Bakers Delight has been a major corporate partner of the Breast Cancer Network Australia since 2000, raising more than $1.4 million over the past seven years. Bakers Delight also donates over $53 million in bread to charities each year. In 2004 an independent corporate citizenship study named Bakers Delight, the leading corporate citizen among a large number of reputable organisations.

[edit] Recent events

Bakers Delight, Australia's most successful retail bakery chain continues to experience significant growth and will take its first slice of the American market in March 2007, with the opening of a Seattle store.

The announcement of its American expansion coincides with the opening of Bakers Delight's 700th store, in Vancouver, Canada, trading under the COBS Bread brand name and its first Bakers Delight stores in the central business districts of Melbourne and Adelaide.
