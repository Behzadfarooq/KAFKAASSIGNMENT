Bandwings, or band-winged grasshoppers (subfamily Oedipodinae) is a group of insects classified under the family Acrididae.

[edit] Description

These species are colorful, usually with hindwings that are yellow or red and edged with black. Others have black hindwings with pale edges, and a few species (including the most economically important ones) have clear hindwings.

The arolium is extremely small or absent.

[edit] Habitats

They inhabit primarily xeric weedy fields.
