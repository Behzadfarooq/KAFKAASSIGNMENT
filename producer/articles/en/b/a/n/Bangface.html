Bang Face is a monthly event held at the Electrowerkz in Angel, London. Run by Saint Acid, it has been running since October 2003.

These events have grown in popularity during its three year life and has necessitated a constant program of expansion, with ever larger venues being needed to accommodate the steadily increasing number of ravers who wish to attend. Currently, the event occupies two rooms of the Electrowerkz nightclub, Angel, London.

[edit] Music policy

Bang Face does not profess to have a music policy as such, citing only Neo-Rave Euphoria as its aim and asking prospective DJs who wish to play at the event to remember that Bang Face is a Neo-Rave explosion of Acid, Jungle, Old Skool Rave, Drum & Bass, Breakcore, Techno, and Electronica when composing their Demo CDs.

However, during the actual events, the DJs are given a free reign to play whatever they want, with everything from Happy Hardcore to Gabba, as well as Speedcore, Terrorcore, Freeform and pretty much every varient of Acid available, including Acid house and Acid Techno. What is actually played on a set to set basis is mostly dependendent on the DJ and what he is best known for playing when booked, although sometimes, a few DJs have been known to play sets which are completely different to what they are known for.

[edit] Events

To date, there have been over 41 Bang Face parties, the majority of which have been held at the Electrowerkz, but other venues include Public Life, Medicine and Trafik. As well as that Bang Face also hosted the Overkill tent at the 2006 Glade Festival on the 15/07/2006 with its "League of Spiritual Discovery" event. This event was the largest ever Bang Face with approximately 2000 ravers filling the circus Big top to capacity, despite the evening finishing at 2am, when its regular events finish at 6am.

Each event is themed to a particular occurrence or notable date in history and given its own subtitle to reflect this. Quite often these milestones are quite obscure, a good example being Bang Face's forty first party, subtitled "Bang Gang"

A cross-dressing Neo-Rave marking the World's first legal sex change operation in 1952...

However, some events are themed around milestones in the actual event, such as the "We are three" party which celebrated the parties' third birthday or the afforementioned "League Of Spiritual Discovery" event, which took place during the Glade Festival.

[edit] Ravers

The Ravers are an integral part of the Bang Face parties. The Bang Face Hard Crew are listed on each flyer as one of the attractions of the night and are encouraged by the organisers to

Come masked up and Bring the toys!

in their promotional emails and to do so in the manner consistent with each themed event.

At the actual parties, the atmosphere generated is formidable to say the least. Mosh pits (which have been renamed by the organisers as "Mash Pits") are a frequent occurrence during the more popular sets which wouldn't normally warrant them if they were at other events.

To Increase the atmosphere, bags of toys are distributed at regular intervals throughout the night. Often these include large cardboard signs with humorous slogans written on them, Inflatable animals, whistles, masks and ravehorns. The cardboard signs are referred to as "Rave Banners" and the slogans are submitted each month via email by the Bang Face Hard Crew.

The walls of the venue are also adorned with hundreds of computer printouts that tend to reflect the theme of each event. Some areas of the venue are also consistently sub-themed to humorously reflect the type of activity that might take place there. The areas include "Chin Strokers Corner", "Casualties Corner" also know as "Mullered Corner" and "Voyeurs Corner" which is mistranslated into French as "Corner de Voyeur".

As well as that, the Bang Face website also contains links to masks which are available for download as well as instructions for construction to enable the ravers to bring them to the events themselves.
