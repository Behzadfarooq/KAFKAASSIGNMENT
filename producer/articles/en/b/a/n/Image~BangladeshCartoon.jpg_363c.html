[edit] Summary

India's leading cartoonist, R. K. Laxman's cartoon published in The Times of India, 1971 shows Nixon and Kissinger in a crashed car after ignoring signs of Bangladesh seccession and supporting Pakistan.

[edit] Rationale for fair use

The cartoon is non repeatable and comes from an eminent Indian cartoonist in India's leading english daily. The cartoon follows WP:FAIR use policy "to illustrate either the publication of the article or issue in question". The cartoon illustrates the issue in question, i.e. Bangladesh Liberation War and the foreign policies of Nixon aided by Kissinger which resulted in a disaster for both USA and their ally, Pakistan. The tone is political and is not used merely to show Nixon and/or Kissinger in a cartoon format. Idleguy 10:06, 15 November 2006 (UTC)
