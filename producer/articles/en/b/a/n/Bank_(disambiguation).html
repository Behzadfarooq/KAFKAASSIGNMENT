Bank (disambiguation)

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Look up bank in Wiktionary, the free dictionary.

Bank may refer to:

  • Bank, a financial institution
  • a shallow area in a body of water, see, for instance, Saya de Malha Bank, Great Chagos Bank
  • For a stream bed, river, or canal, a bank is the area of land closest to water found in a channel - see also shore for a similar concept on non-channel waterways
  • a shot in cue sports, see Glossary_of_cue_sports_terms#Bank_shot
  • Banking education, a model of knowledge transmission first identified by Paulo Freire
  • Georges Bank or Grand Banks, elevations, or rising ground, under the sea
  • Embankment
  • Phone bank, a collection of items
  • The area of London close to the Bank of England, and to Bank and Monument stations
  • Bank engine (or Helper), on the Railways, a locomotive attached to the rear of a train, usually to add haulage power over a short distance.
  • Cylinder bank, a single row of cylinders in an internal combustion engine.
  • A "bank" of computer memory, as in bank switching.
  • Bank or roll, an aerobatic maneuver, also see flight dynamics.
  • A type of shot used in Basketball.
  • Bank Street, Ottawa's main street
  • Bank Station, a bus stop in Ottawa's downtown transit corridor
  • Bench Long seat
