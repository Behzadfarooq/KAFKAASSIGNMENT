[edit] Regarding Wikipedia:Articles for deletion/Disconnection in Scientology

For future reference, since you are the only author of this page, and as you said it duplicates the existing disconnection article, you can simply place the {{db-author}} tag on the page to nominate it for speedy deletion. BryanG 18:38, 21 April 2006 (UTC)

Thanks for the information. Does it make sense to change it now, or should I let the process run its course for this one? Or can I change it at all now?--Bantab 18:58, 21 April 2006 (UTC)
Well, technically after I wrote the above comment I decided that it could potentially be a valid search term and boldly redirected it to Disconnection, so there's probably no point in doing anything now. But normally, it's my understanding that you could add the tag to the article even after the AfD nom, since you were the one who started the AfD nom in the first place. It would probably be a good idea to mention you did that on the nom too, just in case. Having said that, my guess is sooner or later an administrator would have seen the AfD nom and speedy deleted it as you suggested, so there wouldn't be any harm in letting it run its course either. It's just faster and more efficient to use speedy deletion in these cases. BryanG 19:12, 21 April 2006 (UTC)
