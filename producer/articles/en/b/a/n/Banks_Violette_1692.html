Banks Violette

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Bank Violette's church, 2005 (courtesy the Frank Cohen Collection, Manchester

Banks Violette (born 1973, Ithaca, New York) is an artist based in New York.

Violette studied at the School of the Visual Arts [1] in New York earning at BFA in 1998, and graduated with an MFA from Columbia University [2] in 2000.

Violette’s work has shown internationally at galleries and museums including the Whitney Museum of American Art [3] in New York, the Yerba Buena Center for the Arts in San Francisco, Schirn Kunsthalle Frankfurt [4], Museum Boijmans van Beuningeng [5] in Rotterdam, the Migros Museum [6] in Zurich. He participated in the “Greater New York” exhibition at PS1 Center for Contemporary Art [7] and MOMA [8] in New York and “USA Today” [9] at The Royal Academy [10] in London. His work is featured in several prominent collections including the Musee d’Arte Moderne et Contemporain Geneva [11], Museum of Contemporary Art in Los Angeles [12], the Saatchi Gallery [13] in London, and the Guggenheim Museum [14] in New York. He is represented by Team Gallery [15] in New York and Maureen Paley Gallery [16] in London.

Death metal, ritual murder and teenage suicide are starting points for Banks Violette. His work is notable for combining references to excess from youth culture with minimalist form, often using glossy black and ghostly white materials. Citing examples where musical lyrics become instigating factors to real-life violence, Violette refers to an over-identification with fiction, where fantasy and reality are blurred. For example, for his first solo exhibition at the Whitney Museum of American Art in New York, May 27-Oct. 2, 2005, Violette erected a life-sized recreation of a burned-out church on a black stage, inspired by an image from the cover of a black metal record and surrounded by a 5.1 surround score composed by Thorns Ltd consisting of a varied backdrop of ambiences ( www.thornsltd.no ). According to Violette, the inspiration of the piece was a series to instances of arson committed by rival metal enthusiasts in Norway, which culminated in a knife murder of one a member of one band by a rival.[17]
