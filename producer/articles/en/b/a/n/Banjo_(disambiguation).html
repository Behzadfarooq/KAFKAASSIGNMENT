Banjo may refer to:

  • Banjo is a stringed instrument common in folk and popular music.
  • Banjo is the title of a novel by Harlem Renaissance novelist, essayist and poet Claude McKay.
  • Banjo a videogame character.
  • Banjo is a drywall tool used to seal the joints of freshly hung drywall by applying both tape and cement in one smooth action.
  • Banjo Bowl is a football game played between the Saskatchewan Roughriders and Winnipeg Blue Bombers.
  • "Banjo" was the nickname of Australian poet A.B. Paterson.
  • In baseball, a "banjo hitter" is a hitter who lacks power.
  • Banjo clock is a style of American wall clock.
  • Banjo fitting is a perforated and hollow bolt used to connect hoses to devices in such a way that fluid transported in the hose is led into the device to which it is attached. The system is widely used in the transportation industry in fuel, brake and oil systems.
  • Banjo is a slang term for the Australian $10 note, owing to the fact that one side has a picture of the poet, Banjo Patterson on it.
  • Banjo was a popular British Chocolate bar during the 1970's.
