[edit] About Banana Pie


"Y'all Know Wuddahm Sayin'..." Album Cover

Banana Pie is a comedy music group hailing from the region of South Wales, UK. Based on the internet, with their main page based on the growing internet phenomenon, Myspace. Their movies and Music have generated much attention, and as Nick Patterson, PR Manager for Banana Pie said in early 2007 in a press conference in Pontyclun: "With internet plays reaching over 10,000 in just under a year, we are proudly waving the flag for Welsh teenage comedy. Banana Pie continue to work hard, planning to make our motto of "Sacrifice your mam to the Gods of Comedy!" known far and wide."

[edit] History


Banana Pie was founded on October 23, 2005 in Pontyclun, South Wales, by the founding members: Nick Patterson, Jack Hannigan Popp, Josh Thomas and Tomos Wigley-Jones. Production of the first video commenced that day, "Can I Have Some Banana Pie?" which spread the name throughout the local music and social scene. Soon joined by Gareth Rees, Ceirion Worgan, Iolo James and Dafydd Lewis, the group proceeded to move onto music as well as movies. With such tracks as "I Love Gays", "Ten Minutes of Pain" and "Jesus Christ", their first Album, Y'all know what I'm saying B*tch was released in late-April 2006, but suffered poor sales. This was not helped by the embezzlement by Dafydd Lewis, now former member of the group, which cost the group almost 70% of album sale income. Their next movie project, entitled "Power Wars" was a success in terms of ratings, and the Banana Pie name was also reaching the shores of the US and further afield.

With the success of the "Power Wars" series, as well as the release of "Gay! With Nick Patterson", the group were at a highpoint, and this has been dubbed by many as their golden era, during the summer of 2006, with Myspace plays averaging a large amount for a group of this size. Complications arose in late 2006, as internal affairs with the group saw Dafydd Lewis part ways with Banana Pie, and the group took a small break. With the first anniversary of Banana Pie growing nearer, the group decided to launch new material to mark this occasion. The release of new songs such as "Babies as Pets" and "Cantre'r Gwaelod", as well the movies "Gowen Owen", "Guns of Mecca" and "The Recording Session", this marked a revival of Banana Pie, and the group continues to produce material to this day with releases averaging at least once or twice a month.
