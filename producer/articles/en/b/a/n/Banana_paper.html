Banana paper

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

You have new messages (last change).

Banana paper is used in two different senses: to refer to a paper made from the bark of the banana tree, or paper made from banana fibre. This paper can be either hand-made or made by machine. The volume of raw resources for making banana paper around the world on plantations is vast and largely unutilised. The market for banana paper is seen as a growth industry. Certain kinds of banana paper do not easily tear or burn.

Banana bark paper retains the texture and patterning of the original bark and is primarily used for decorative purposes like gift wrapping and lamp shades.

Paper made from banana fibre are called Banana Ply Paper or BPP in short. This new technology produces a fibre mat without the need to pulp the banana plant fibre, hence greatly reduces the energy required and eliminates the need for external water and chemicals.
