[edit] Usefulness

Banned by whom? This category needs subcategorization if it is to be of any use. Books on the Index librorum prohibitorum, books banned in Iran, Nazi Germany, the People's Republic of China,...a good portion of books noteworthy enough for encyclopedia articles will have been banned somewhere at some time, especially nonfiction works more than say 100 years old. On the other hand, too many subcategories and some books will end up in 20 subcategories at once. Hmmm. I reckon List of banned books would be sufficient if it were better organised. Joestynes 08:58, 16 Jun 2005 (UTC)

I agree that merely being in a category is not enough. I am going to cfd this cat. What is need is at least a suggestion (if not clear documentation) of WHY it has the trait. The "category" scheme does not work well for this kind of property. We should just maintain a list with a one-liner explaination for each entry. -- Fplay 21:55, 24 December 2005 (UTC)
I agree with Joestynes. There have been so many bannings of books, sometimes only taking place at one or two schools or libraries, that books that are only slightly controversial are often banned somewhere. Because of this, the banning is often an insignificant event in the book's history. Also, it would be impractical to include places outside the United States because of the large number of countries, and the frequency that some of them ban books. I think that some countries ban them categorically, rather than specifically. For example, any book that criticizes their religion or a figure in their religion would be banned. If other countries are not included, the category should be renamed Banned books in the United States, or something similar. -- Kjkolb 21:33, 17 February 2006 (UTC)

[edit] Banned category

It is undiluted idiocy that this category should have been deleted. Any vagueness inherent in the phrase "banned books" is quite obviously outweighed by its usefulness, and now because the category has been deleted Wikipedia has a big gaping hole in it. As long as I've been participating in Wikipedia, I've wished that people would find constructive rather than destructive ways to participate in the project, but the fact is that adding actual, informative, encyclopedic content is hard work, and voting to delete stuff isn't. There will always be more people tearing down than building up, and it's a shame. MrBook 17:09, 2 October 2006 (UTC)
