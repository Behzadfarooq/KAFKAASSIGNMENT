More of a serving suggestion than a recipe, bangers and mash is a British colloquial name for sausages (bangers) served with mashed potato. The sausages may be one of a variety of flavoured sausages; such as pork, pork and apple, tomato, beef, Lincolnshire, or Cumberland. The full meal will usually include a vegetable (e.g. baked beans) and is usually served with a rich onion gravy. The term has its origins in World War II when sausages were often of such low quality they partially exploded when cooking.

It is a very popular winter dish, and can range in quality from the very cheapest sausages and instant mashed potatoes accompanied with an instant gravy mix such as Bisto, to high-end gourmet sausages and homemade mashed potatoes and gravy.

This dish may, even when cooked at home, be thought of as an example of pub grub - relatively quick and easy to make in large quantities as well as being tasty and satisfying. More up-market varieties, with exotic sausages, are sold in gastropubs, as well as less exotic alternatives being available in regular pubs.

One variant is the "Banger Clock", for which twelve cooked sausages are placed on a mound of mashed potato in a pattern resembling the hours of a clockface; eating an entire banger clock is considered a feat of prowess, similar to drinking a yard of ale.


[edit] References in popular culture

Bangers and Mash was a children's television series in 1991 on the BBC.

In the American television series Arrested Development, Tobias FÃ¼nke, while dressed as a British housekeeper (a la the film Mrs. Doubtfire) named Mrs. Featherbottom, offers to put a "banger in the mouth" of his brother-in-law Michael when he serves breakfast to the family. Tobias quickly corrects himself by mentioning that in the states it's called "a sausage in the mouth." Michael further clarifies by telling him that "we just call it a sausage." This joke was featured in the season 2 episode entitled "Meet the Veals" (a pun on Meet the Beatles).[1]

Bangers 'n' Mash is the title of a song by British alternative band Radiohead.

"Bangers and Mush" is the name of a worm in the "Breakfasts" Team in the computer game Worms World Party.

"Bangerz and Mash" are two characters in the Playstation 2 video game Dragon Quest VIII.

Bangers and mash figured into a running gag in the TV series All Creatures Great and Small. With the housekeeper away on vacation, Tristan took over as cook, and his first breakfast of bangers and mash was well-received by Siegfried and James. After two weeks of the same breakfast fare, though, Tristan's fellow veterinarians were on the verge of rebellion.
