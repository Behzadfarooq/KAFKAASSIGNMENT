Bansal

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Emperor Agrasen and Queen Madhavi ruled Agroha around 3200 B.C. Agrasen established 18 Gotras (or clans) for each of his 18 sons based on the names of their Guru and divided his empire among them. Collectively these 18 Gotras are called Agarwal- one of the most important trading communities in India.

Bansal (or Vansal) is one of the original 18 Gotras established by Agrasen. The name itself appears to be related to Sanskrit vamša (lineage).

Gotra: Bansal/Vansal

Original Gotra: Vatsya

Lord: Virbhan

Saint(Guru): Vishist/Vatsa

Veda: Samaveda

Branch: Kouthmi/Kauttham

Sutra: Gobhil

Those with this last name are primarily Hindus but in the modern times this last name has also been adopted by Sikh, Jain and Marwari communities.

Interestingly, Ancestry and Geneaology experts also trace a branch of Bansal clan to early times Champagne, France having “Blue shield with three gold oak leaves” as its coat of arms. Correlation and/or connections (if any) between French Bansals and Indian Bansals is unknown. This French branch of the family has many spelling variations including: Ban, Bans, Band, Bant, Bands, Bants, Bande, Bandes, Bante, Bantes, Baun, Bauns, Baund, Baunds, Baunt, Baunts, du Ban etc.
