Bancroft Prize

The Bancroft Prize was established in 1948 with a bequest from Frederic Bancroft and is awarded by the trustees of Columbia University for books about diplomacy or about the history of the Americas that were first published during the previous year. The prize is generally considered to be the most prestigious American award in the field of history writing, and comes with a $10,000 award (up from $4,000 previous to 2004).
