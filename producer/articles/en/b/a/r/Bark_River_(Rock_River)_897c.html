The Bark River is a tributary of the Rock River, about 55 mi (90 km) long, in southeastern Wisconsin in the United States. Via the Rock River, it is part of the watershed of the Mississippi River. According to the Geographic Names Information System, it has also been known historically as the "Peelbark River."

The Bark River rises in southern Washington County and flows generally southwestwardly through Waukesha and Jefferson Counties, past the communities of Hartland, Delafield and Rome and through several small lakes. It joins the Rock River in Jefferson County just east of Fort Atkinson.

In Jefferson County the Bark River collects the Scuppernong River.
