Barrington is a village and parish, situated ten miles south east of Taunton and ten miles west of Yeovil in the South Somerset district of Somerset, England. The village has a population of 427[1].

The village is located on the southern edge of the Somerset Levels and many of the houses are built from cob and thatched with local reeds. There is also a large council estate in the village.

The 13th-century St Mary's Church has an unusual octagonal tower. Barrington Court, the local manor house, is a fine Tudor building which anticipates some features of the later Elizabethan style. It is owned by the National Trust.
