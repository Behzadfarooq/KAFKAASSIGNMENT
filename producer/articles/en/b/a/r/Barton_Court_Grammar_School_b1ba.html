Barton Court Grammar School is a co-ed selective school in Canterbury.

It currently holds a Foreign Language College status, and the school is transferring to the International Baccalaureate award scheme for those pupils in Years 12 and 13, which will be fully introduced in Autumn 2007.

The school is situated on a World Heritage Site, making for an interesting and dynamic learning environment for the students[citation needed].

There are two magazine-style publications circulated within the school, one of these being Courtside (formerly The Little Barton) run by Mr Hayes of the English Department, and the other known as The Old Bartonian which presents a lively ironic take on the school and students. Both have approximately the same number of readers.

The first and original magazine-style publication was "BCGS Times." An ironic and satirical look at life, love and school. It perished under strict conditions imposed by an official magazine to be produced professionally by the school. Along with the magazine there was the renowned song "BCGS Times." A satirical plug for the magazine and a defining moment for the publication. Often the words: "BCGS Times, Barton Court Grammar School Times," could be heard drifting through the corridors and rooms of the old house and new block. It was not a quick-money-making plan.

The school is widely regarded as a pioneer for innovative teaching methods, including the three period day and extensive use of ICT within lessons[citation needed].
