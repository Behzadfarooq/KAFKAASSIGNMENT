Barbara Osborn Kreamer

Barbara Osborn Kreamer (b. December 8, 1948) is an American politician from Aberdeen, Maryland and a former Democratic member of the Maryland House of Delegates.

Kreamer served one term on the County Council of Harford County, Maryland from 1978 to 1982. Kreamer then represented District 34 from 1983 until 1990. She served as the President of the Maryland Association of Elected Women in 1985. She was a Delegate to the Democratic National Convention in 1988 that nominated Michael Dukakis, who lost to Republican George H. W. Bush.

In 1990, Kreamer gave up her seat to run for Congress in the 1st Congressional District. She lost in the Democratic Primary to Roy Dyson, who then lost to first time candidate Republican Wayne T. Gilchrest in the General election.

In 1994, Kreamer ran unsuccessfully for lieutenant governor on a Democratic primary slate headed by then-state Sen. Mary A. Boergers, D-Montgomery, in what was billed as “Maryland’s first all-woman ticket.” She received only 9% of the vote, coming in fourth place in the Democratic Primary to the winning ticket of Parris Glendening and Kathleen Kennedy Townsend.

Originally an English and Creative Writing teacher at Bel Air High School from 1971 to 1976, Kreamer became a lawyer after graduating from law school and passing the Maryland bar, specializing in family law.

On February 2, 1999, Kreamer was suspended from the practice of law by the Maryland Court of Appeals for professional malpractice. She received another reprimand by the Maryland Court of Appeals on November 19, 2002.

In the 2002 elections, Kreamer attempted to regain a spot on the Harford County Council in District E, representing Hickory, Forest Green, Aberdeen and Churchville. The Baltimore Sun endorsed her in the General election [1]. The Sun’s endorsement, however, seemed to not help, as she received only 36% of the vote to first time candidate Republican Richard Slutzky, a teacher at Aberdeen High.

In the 2002 General election, she was re-elected to the Democratic Central Committee for Harford County.

On June 21, 2005, Kreamer was disciplined a third time by the Maryland Court of Appeals.

She received a B.A. from Washington College in 1970, an M.L.A. from The Johns Hopkins University in 1975, and a J.D. from University of Maryland School of Law.
