[edit] Why this page exists

While I do not know if this page has existed before, I do know that it definitely has a place on Wikipedia. First of all, it is popularly known by most of the people who play world of warcraft, who number over 8 million [1]. Furthermore, the T-shirts mentioned in the article have become quite popular amongst players of WoW, and people in the community who have seen them may think that Wikipedia would be a good place to look for a reference. The popularity of the Barrens Chat has made it a meme similar to Leeroy Jenkins. It undoubtedly merits an article. Hojimachong 20:59, 27 January 2007 (UTC)

I'm going to propose a merge into Internet Phenomenon? I really do feel that the subject deserves some reference on Wikipedia, as it is quite well-known amongst WoW and non-WoW players (who may have seen the T-shirts). Hojimachongtalkcon 03:55, 7 February 2007 (UTC)
While I agree that the page has some value, there is a rather big chance of this actually being original research. While the phenomenon is easily observable, has it been described by an outside source that can be quoted? As much as I enjoy reading trivia about a game (not that I mean it is trivial, like Leeroy Jenkins it is a big part of the WoW culture), I have to wonder the actual research done on this topic. While I understand the merge with Internet Phenomenon merge, I think this would benefit, like many other elements that may have been reported in articles concerning WoW, to be made into another article, something like "World of Warcraft Phenomena" which could cover a broader range of elements found in WoW without being mixed with other phenomenon from the internet. This could include the Melt Faces meme, the "Hunter Gear" claims, as well as many other aspects are present in WoW as the Barrens Chat, which are as likely of being referenced in outside sources.Youkai no unmei 20:46, 13 February 2007 (UTC)
