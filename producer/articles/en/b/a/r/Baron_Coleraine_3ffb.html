Baron Coleraine

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Baron Coleraine is a title that has been created three times, twice in the Peerage of Ireland and once in the Peerage of the United Kingdom. The first creation came in the Peerage of Ireland in 1625 for Hugh Hare. This creation became extinct on the death of the third Baron in 1749. The second creation came in the Peerage of Ireland in 1762 in favour of Gabriel Hanger. This creation became extinct on the death of the fourth Baron in 1824.

The third creation came in the Peerage of the United Kingdom in 1954 when the Conservative politician Richard Law was made Baron Coleraine, of Haltemprice in the East Riding of the County of York. He was the youngest son of the former Prime Minister Andrew Bonar Law. As of 2007 the title is held by the first Baron's son, the second Baron, who succeeded in 1980.
