Baron Cawley

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Baron Cawley, of Prestwich in the County Palatine of Lancaster, is a title in the Peerage of the United Kingdom. It was created in 1918 for the Liberal politician Frederick Cawley. He had previously represented Prestwich in the House of Commons and served as Chancellor of the Duchy of Lancaster from 1916 to 1918. Before his elevation to the peerage, Cawley had been created a Baronet, of Prestwich in the County Palatine of Lancaster, in the Baronetage of the United Kingdom in 1906. His grandson, the third Baron, notably served as Deputy Chairman of Committees in the House of Lords from 1958 to 1967. As of 2007 the titles are held by the latter's eldest son, the fourth Baron, who succeeded in 2001.

The former seat of the Cawley family was Berrington Hall near Leominster in Herefordshire.

[edit] Barons Cawley (1918)

  • Frederick Cawley, 1st Baron Cawley (1850-1937)
  • Robert Hugh Cawley, 2nd Baron Cawley (1877-1954)
  • Frederick Lee Cawley, 3rd Baron Cawley (1913-2001)
  • John Francis Cawley, 4th Baron Cawley (b. 1946)
