Baraka School

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

The Baraka School was a small education program that took at-risk 12-year-old boys from the Baltimore public school system to the Kenyan outback for two years to live and study. The school was located in Laikipia and the program began in 1996 with funding from the Abell Foundation, a local Baltimore philanthropy.

The program was shutdown in 2003 because of security threats. After the 2002 hotel bombing in Mombassa and the 2003 invasion of Iraq, the school administrators decided not to re-open the school.

The school program was featured in the 2005 documentary The Boys of Baraka.
