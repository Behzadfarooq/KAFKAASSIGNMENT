Barnegat High School is a comprehensive community public high school that will ultimately serve students in ninth through twelfth grades from Barnegat Township, in Ocean County, New Jersey, United States, as aprt of the Barnegat Township School District.

Barnegat High School is now open for Grades 9 through 11, with Grade 11 having been added starting in September 2006. The last group of students from Barnegat are completing twelfth grade at Southern Regional High School in Stafford Township through a soon-to-be-ended sending/receiving relationship with the Southern Regional School District.

[edit] Facility

The school's building was completed under the supervision of the New Jersey Schools Construction Corporation. The 156,000 square-foot, two-story building is located on a 66-acre campus, and includes 29 classrooms, six science labs, two technology labs and art and music rooms, along with a cafeteria, media center, gymnasium and a 600-seat auditorium. The campus includes six athletic fields and a track. The new building opened to students in September 2004, with ribbon cutting ceremonies held on November 13, 2004. A contract for a second phase of work was awarded on October 16, 2006, and will include 50,000 square feet of new space, with 28 additional classrooms and an alternate gymnasium.[1]

[edit] Athletics

Barnegat High School South competes in the Shore Conference is an athletic conference comprised of private and public high schools in Monmouth County and Ocean County. The league operates under the jurisdiction of the New Jersey State Interscholastic Athletic Association (NJSIAA).
