Skeptic? (2001 - Present)

Well, it had to happen sooner or later, so why not now. Skeptic? (the question mark IS a part of the name) was and still is (at least as of this writing) an Old School Punk band from Birmingham, Alabama (sometimes known as "The Tragic City"). The original lineup, consisting of Barron (ex-Thik Chicken) Greg (ex-Thik Chicken, Caustic Outlook), Jon (ex-Caustic Outlook) and Billy first started practicing in the back of Birmingham's notorious Boiler Room in the fall of 2001, but didn't actually play out until the summer of 2002. After three shows, Greg moved on, with Jason replacing him for two years and some change, before Atlanta-transplant Emanual took over, giving Skeptic? the dirty, gritty hardcore sound which the others had always sought. But just as things seemed on even ground, Billy, who really did have family obligations, was also replaced, first by Fred, who did a six-month stint, then Richie, who continues to play to this day. There has always been talk of adding a second guitarist, but whether or not it will happen remains to be seen, as Birmingham, a small town by these kind of standards, is not known for an overabundance of Old School punk musicians, especially as old and crusty as these guys, but you never can tell...

Current Skeptic? discography:

2003 - Skeptic? demo CD (self-produced)

2004 - 5 songs on the "Southern Hardcore: The Way It Is" split w/ Bloody Sods and Random Conflict (D-Fens Records)

2005 - "Against The Wall" CD (self-produced)

2006 - "Nobody Cared" 7" EP (self-produced)

Known compilation appearances include:

"Working" appears on "Boxcars On 1st Volume 1" and "We Did It Our Own Way", both Birmingham compilations.

"Working", "Against The Wall" and "Dead Or Alive" (a GG Allin cover) all appear on "Liquid Adrenaline", an extreme snowboarding video produced by DB Foto in Utah.

Over the years, Skeptic? has been very fortunate in meeting and playing with some amazing bands and people, including The Bloody Sods, Jackpot Justice, APA, The (English) Subhumans, Fear, Murphy's Law, The Queers, The Vibrators, Loose Skrews, The Ouchies, Slag, Steel City Crime, The Hell, Lockbody, Cretin Grims, Random Conflict and COS, just to name a few. It was always about the music, and not so much the money, and bands like these are what make it successful. After all, what price do you put on following your dreams?
