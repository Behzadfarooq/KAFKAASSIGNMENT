Baranof River

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

The source of Baranof River.

Baranof River is a glacially-fed river on the eastern side of Baranof Island, in the Alexander Archipelago, in the Alaska Panhandle. The source of the river is from the unnamed icefields surrounding Peak 5390. Baranof River feeds into Baranof Lake, and the outlet of Baranof Lake that feeds into Warm Springs Bay is also commonly referred to as a Baranof River as well.

Baranof River is one of the larger rivers in terms of volume on Baranof Island.

Baranof River (and everything else named "Baranof") was named for Alexandr Baranov, the first governor of the Russian-American Company.
