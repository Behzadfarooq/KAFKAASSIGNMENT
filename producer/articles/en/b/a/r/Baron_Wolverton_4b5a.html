Baron Wolverton

Baron Wolverton, of Wolverton in the County of Buckingham, is a title in the Peerage of the United Kingdom. It was created in 1869 for the banker George Carr Glyn. He was the fourth son of Sir Richard Carr Glyn, 1st Baronet, of Gaunt's House, Lord Mayor of London in 1798, himself the fourth son of Sir Richard Glyn, 1st Baronet, of Ewell, Lord Mayor of London in 1758. Lord Wolverton was succeeded by the eldest of his nine sons, the second Baron. He was a Liberal politician and served under William Gladstone as Paymaster-General and as Postmaster General. His nephew, the fourth Baron, served as Vice-Chamberlain of the Household from 1902 to 1905 in the Conservative governments of Lord Salisbury and Arthur Balfour. On the death in 1988 of his son, the fifth Baron, this line of the family failed. The title was inherited by the late Baron's second cousin, the sixth Baron. He was the grandson of the Hon. Pascoe Charles Glyn, younger son of the first Baron. As of 2007 the title is held by his son, the seventh Baron. As a descendant of both the first Glyn Baronet of Gaunt's House and of the first Glyn Baronet of Ewell, he is also in remainder to these titles.

[edit] Barons Wolverton (1869)

  • George Carr Glyn, 1st Baron Wolverton (1797–1873)
  • George Grenfell Glyn, 2nd Baron Wolverton (1824–1887)
  • Henry Richard Glyn, 3rd Baron Wolverton (1861–1888)
  • Frederick Glyn, 4th Baron Wolverton (1864–1932)
  • Nigel Reginald Victor Glyn, 5th Baron Wolverton (1904–1986)
  • John Patrick Riversdale Glyn, 6th Baron Wolverton (1913–1988)
  • Christopher Richard Glyn, 7th Baron Wolverton (b. 1938)
