Barleeia, or Barleysnail, is a genus of very small snails (micro shells: usually a couple of millimeters in length) from the family Barleeidae.

[edit] Species with common names

  • Abalone barleysnail, Barleeia haliotiphila (P. P. Carpenter, 1864) This intertidal snail is one of the most common Gastropods to be found in the biotic community at moderate tidal levels. Its diet consists of algae, such as Rhodoglossum affine, Gigartina papillata, Gigartina leptorhynchos. Range : East Pacific, North America.
  • Acute barleysnail, Barleeia acuta (P. P. Carpenter, 1864) - America
  • Atlantic mud piddock, Barleeia truncata (Say, 1822)
  • California barleysnail, Barleeia californica (Bartsch, 1920) - America
  • Caribbean barleysnail, Barleeia tincta (R. J. L. Guppy, 1895) - eastern Florida, Trinidad
  • Fragile barleysnail, Barleeia subtenuis (P. P. Carpenter, 1864) – America
