The Barstable School, also known as The Federation of Chalvedon and Barstable School is a mixed intake secondary school in Basildon, Essex. The school is for students aged 11-16 (school years 7-11). The school is currently run by headmaster Alan Roach, who incedentially, is also head of Chalvedon School. Barstable recently received a vastly improved Ofsted inspection, bringing the school up to standard requirements. The school has received the Sportsmark designation and is part of Creative Partnerships.

[edit] History

The Barstable School first opened in the early 1960s as the Timberlog Grammar and Secondary School. In 1968, the Grammar and Secondary School merged with the Timberlog Secondary Modern School. When the two schools merged into one school, it took the name of Barstable School.

[edit] Blocks

Currently the Barstable School has five blocks - A Block, B Block, L Block, S Block and T Block.

A Block

This block houses the Art department, Performing Arts, Citizenship and Religious Studies, the Science department, History and Geography. This building contains three floors, and also contains an elevator to ensure ease of access.

B Block

This block houses Food Technology, the Maths department, the English department and Modern Foreign Languages. It is very similar in structure to "A Block", in that it has three floors, and also contains an elevator.

L Block

This block is used for any lessons that need to be vacated somewhere else thean the other blocks.

S Block

This block houses the Gym, Sports Hall and a Swimming Pool. It also houses "Jake's Gym". "Jake's Gym" is a gym that has been created in partnership with Pulse. The gym is named after a student who sadly passed away whilst attending the school. It contains an aerobics centre as well as a fully-functioning gym.

T Block

This block houses the ICT department and the Technology department.
