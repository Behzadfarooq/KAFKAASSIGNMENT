Barton Street (Hamilton, Ontario)

Barton Street, is the longest street (21.0 km.) in Hamilton, Ontario, Canada. It starts off at the Western end of town at Locke Street North and is a two-way street most of the way that stretches eastward through a number of different and varied communities in the city and ends in Winona at 50 Road just West of the Hamilton/Niagara Falls regional border.

[edit] History

In the early days Hamilton was known as Barton township, named after a township in Lincoln County, England. Barton Street is all that remains of the township. [1] In 1816, Barton township Population was 668. [2]
