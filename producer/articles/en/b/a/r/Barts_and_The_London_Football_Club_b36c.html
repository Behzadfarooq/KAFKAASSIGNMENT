Barts and The London Football Club is a team that represents Barts and The London School of Medicine and Dentistry. The club has three teams and competes in the University of London (ULU) league and cups, the British University Sports Association (BUSA) league and the United Hospitals' Challenge Cup (UH cup).

[edit] 2005-6 Season

This has been the most successful season in the club's history. The first team won the UH Cup and look safe in the ULU Premier One League. The second team have gone from fighting relegation relegation to pushing for promotion in ULU division 2 The club also won The Association Cup at this year's Association dinner for being the most successful team within Barts and The London Students' Association. The Third team won the ULU vase, but were controversially relegated from ULU division 3 despite being one game away from promotion. This was due to an unfortunate ruling by the ULU disciplinary committee who decided that a disagreement between the Barts captain and the Goldsmiths captain about appropriate conduct on the pitch, could only be resolved by relegating both teams. King's, who broke several ULU regulations by refusing to field a team against Barts in the last match of the season were granted promotion however. A disappointing end to a spectacular season for the club. At the 2006 Annual General Meeting the elections for next years captains was made. Adrian Conner (Cottee) taking 1st team captain. Raphael Teotino (Speroni) the 2nd team captain and Joseph Weston-Price (Stamp) as the new 3rd team captain.


[edit] History

The club was formed in 1995 when St. Bartholomews's Hospital Medical College and The Royal London Hospital Medical College merged. Both clubs have a rich history and have won the oldest cup competition in the world, the United Hospitals' Challenge Cup many times.

[edit] Honours

The club won its first trophy on Saturday, 11th of March 2006 when the third team won the ULU Vase 6-3 after extra time. This was quickly followed by the United Hospitals Challenge Cup the day after which was won 2-0 against RUMS by the first team.

[edit] Events

As well as meeting every Wednesday to watch football in the union there are many annual events. The biggest of these is the AGM which is traditionally held on the day before the FA Cup Final at a football stadium in London. There is also an annual tour to a "mystery" destination and another tour to compete in NAMS.
