Arman Barkhudaryan was born in Yerevan, Armenia on June 3, 1975. He currently lives in Thessaloniki, northern Greece.

Background He has graduated from Yerevan State University (www.ysu.am) with a Masters Degree in Economics (Operations Research and Econometrics), 1991-1996 and a Masters Degree in Mathematics, 1992-1998, Faculty of Applied Mathematics. In 1999 he received his PhD in Economics from Institute of Economics, National Academy of Sciences of Armenia for research in enterprise support policies in transitional economies (cesww.fas.harvard.edu/diss/CESWdiss_Economics.html).


Arman Barkhudaryan started his professional carreer in 1994 with a European Union funded TACIS project for SME support (Armenian Business Support Center - ABSC), where he worked on privatization and post-privatization projects, prepared strategic development programs and business plans for Armenian companies, presented export and investment opportunities of local enterprises to foreign counterparts, consulted local entrepreneurs on cost accounting, management, budgeting and finance.


In 1998, Arman Barkhudaryan and his business partner established AMERIA Investment Consulting Company, associate of Troika Dialog (www.troika.ru) in Armenia (one of the largest investment banks in Russia). Within 1 year the company built up the initial client base locally and abroad; rendered investment consulting services, preparation of investment projects, business plans, feasibility studies and loan appraisal reports for newly privatized industrial enterprises and development of greenfield investment opportunities; structured project finance and trade finance transactions; prepared syndicated loan facilities, bridge financing, trade financing and buy-back agreements.


In August 1999 Arman Barkhudaryan was invited to join the International Finance Corporation's (www.ifc.org)(World Bank) newly established office in Yerevan, Armenia as an investment analyst. His duties included identification of viable investment opportunities in the private sector; assessment and analysis of financial statements and business plans of prospective clients; project investment, appraisal and due diligence work in accordance with the IFC project cycle and procedures; analysis of the economic, legal and investment environment in the country; assistance in organization of a major IFC/USTDA financed Armenia Investor Conference in New York.

He played a key role in initiation, due diligence and closing of a project establishing the first leasing company in Armenia set up by IFC, Credit Agricole (www.creditagricole.com) and a local bank; worked with IFC consultants and the Armenian Government (www.gov.am) on creation of legislation and enabling environment for leasing in Armenia, worked on development, structuring and feasibility study of the first Venture Capital Fund for Armenia supported by the IFC, assisted in finalizing due diligence and negotiation of the IFC’s first equity investment in Armenia, coordinated preparation of a key investment conference in New York to showcase investment opportunities in Armenia, co-funded by IFC and USTDA and attended by more than 400 participants


After the successfull completion of IFC's technical assistance project, Arman Barkhudaryan joined the European Bank for Reconstruction and Development (www.ebrd.com)(EBRD), headquartered in London, UK as an Associate Banker (www.dignityandpeace.netfirms.com). Duties included identification, full project appraisal, due diligence and negotiation of EBRD operations in Armenia, work with the project teams on all operations involving Armenia, monitoring of active portfolio of projects (total amount over Eur. 90m), liaison with local Government and donor community, assistance in preparation of country strategies and budgets, assistance in coordination of EBRD technical assistance projects in Armenia, representation of the office in the absence of a permanent head of office.

He contributed to appraisal, due diligence, structuring and negotiation of the first EBRD equity investment in an Armenian commercial bank; initiated, developed and negotiated trade finance and SME finance facilities leading to signing of 6 new operations with Armenian banks; identified and conducted full project appraisal, due diligence and negotiation of eleven projects in manufacturing, agriculture and energy sectors leading to signing of 3 new operations and development of advanced project pipeline, coordinated EBRD technical cooperation programs with Armenian banks for development of trade finance products, assumed responsibility for portfolio monitoring of the bank’s 12 active projects in the country.


Since September 2004, Arman Barkhudaryan has been invited to join the Black Sea Trade and Development Bank (www.bstdb.org)(BSTDB - headquarters in Thessaloniki, Greece) as a Senior Banker at Trade Finance and Financial Institutions Department. His duties include initiation, appraisal, negotiation, approval and structuring of trade and SME finance operations with financial institutions in the 11 member countries of the Bank; development of new products for micro/SME finance and leasing sectors; supervision and monitoring of current portfolio (about Eur. 120m); project preparation in cooperation with key co-financiers and financial institutions active in the region. Completed initiation, approval and signing of 15 new operations with commercial banks and leasing companies in member countries in 2 years.
