Baradine County, New South Wales is one of the 141 Cadastral divisions of New South Wales. It is bounded in the north by the Namoi River, and includes the land south to Coonabarabran, which is on its southern edge. This includes land on both sides of the Baradine Creek and the town of Baradine

Baradine is believed to be derived from a local Aboriginal word.[1]
