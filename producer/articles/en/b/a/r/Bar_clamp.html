Bar clamp

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

It has been suggested that this article or section be merged with F-clamp. (Discuss)

A Bar clamp is a clamp, typically used for woodworking, that consists of a bar or rod with two jaws. Typical construction has one jaw fixed, the other moveable with a screw type clamping mechanism. Bar clamps in the industry terminology have the jaws mounted on a flat bar, while a pipe clamp, which has the same construction, is mounted on a pipe, normally of 1/2" or 3/4" diameter.
