Barkly West, Northern Cape

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Barkly West is a town situated in the Northern Cape Province of South Africa. It was established as Klip Drift in 1869 but after the discovery of diamonds it was absorbed into the Colony of Griqualand West and changed its name to Barkly West. Like Barkly East, the town is named after Sir Henry Barkly, governor of Cape Colony, 1870-1877.

Renowned writer Sarah Gertrude Millin grew up in Barkly West and her father opened the first shop in town.
