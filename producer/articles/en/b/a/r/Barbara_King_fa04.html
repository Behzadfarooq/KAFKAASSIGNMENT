Barbara King

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Barbara King is an American actress. She was born on June 31, 1937 in Oswego, New York.

Barbara's passion for acting began as a child, constantly putting on plays for friends and family. Recognizing her talent, Barbara's parents enrolled her in the Center for the Creative Arts dancing school, where she studied ballet and modern dance. As she grew older she participated in local community theatre and school productions. Barbara's first professional acting was in voice-over advertisements for local businesses. As a freshman in high school, she was cast in the lead of a short film titled "The Pumpkin Patch", which appeared on PBS. With the film experience added to her credits, it only confirmed her desire to pursue acting as a career. Barbara moved to New York City to attend the prestigious acting program at NYU's Tisch School of the Arts. She plunged into the "college experience," studying internationally, taking a wide variety of courses and was cast in several productions including Portia in the Merchant of Venice. She graduated with a BFA in drama and as a Founder's Day Scholar and the President of the Tisch Undergraduate Student Council.

Theatrically, Barbara has created several off-off-broadway productions, including the "bitingly funny" [New York Sun] Maiden America and most recently, Rapt Inside, proving herself as "a name to be looked for in downtown theatre" [nytheatre.com].

Recently, Barbara is focusing more of her energy towards work in film, TV and commercials. Barbara can be seen in several independent films screened in festivals throughout the country, as well as on the Independent Film Channel.

In her spare time Barbara practices yoga, enjoys writing, and is an avid reader. She also volunteers weekly at All Souls Soup Kitchen and as a Leader of an East Harlem Girl Scouts Troupe.

In the 2006 Season Premier of NBC's Law & Order Barbara King plays the Guest Lead role of "Sky Sweet", a superstar celebrity caught in the scandal of her career.

Barbara plays "Angela Who Knits" in the 2007 indy film Urchin.
