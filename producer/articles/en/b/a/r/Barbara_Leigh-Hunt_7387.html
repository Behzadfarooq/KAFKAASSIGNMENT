Barbara Leigh-Hunt

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Barbara Leigh-Hunt,(b. 1935), Bath, England, is a British actress who has appeared on stage, film, television & radio. Among many roles, She appeared in one of Alfred Hitchcock's later films Frenzy (1972), as a victim raped and murdered in arguably the most graphic sequence Hitchcock ever filmed and as Lady Catherine de Bourgh in the BBC adaptation of Pride and Prejudice. She has also appeared in such films as Paper Mask (1990), Billy Elliot (2000) and much work on TV, mainly in guest character parts.
