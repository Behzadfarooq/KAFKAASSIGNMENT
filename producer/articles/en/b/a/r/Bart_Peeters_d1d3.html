Bart Peeters

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Bart Peeters (Mortsel, 30 November 1959) is a Flemish singer, drummer, television presenter and actor. Peeters studied Germanic philology and drama in Antwerp. There, he met Jan Leyers and Hugo Matthysen, with whom he worked later. Bart Peeters is married and has two daughters and a son.

[edit] Musician

In 1978, Bart Peeters, Hugo Matthysen, Jan Leyers and Marc Kruithof played in a coverband (Beri Beri), but as a musician, Bart Peeters gained his fame with The Radios. This band (with Ronny Mosuse, Robert Mosuse and Dany Lademacher had a world hit with She goes nana. Nowadays, Bart Peeters has a solo career. In 2004 he made his first solo album: Het plaatje van Bart Peeters (The Record of Bart Peeters). In 2006 he made Slimmer dan de zanger (Cleverer than the singer).
