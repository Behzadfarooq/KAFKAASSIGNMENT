Barbara Knox

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia


Barbara Knox (born Barbara Brothwood, September 30, 1933 in Oldham, Lancashire, sometimes credited by her first married name, Barbara Mullaney) is an English actress mainly known for playing 'Rita Sullivan' in the television soap opera Coronation Street.

She married Denis Mullaney in 1954, with whom she had one child. Upon their divorce in 1977, she married John Knox. That marriage also ended in divorce in 1994, but she chose to keep the name "Knox" professionally.

Barbara Mullaney 'On The Street Where I Live'

Barbara first played the role of Rita in November 1964, when she appeared for only one episode. She returned in 1972 as Rita Bates, the common-law wife of Harry Bates, and in the decades since then, the character has been married twice, to Len Fairclough and Ted Sullivan. She is currently known as Rita Sullivan.

In 1973, Barbara cut an LP for Philips Records.

In 1989 she won the TV Times award for best actress following her involvement in the dramatic Alan Bradley storyline.

She won the 'Best On-Screen Partnership' award (jointly with Malcolm Hebden) at the British Soap Awards in May 2006 for her role as Rita Sullivan.
