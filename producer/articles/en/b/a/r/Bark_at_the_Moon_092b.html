Bark at the Moon is a heavy metal album by Ozzy Osbourne, released in December 10, 1983. This album features former Ratt and Rough Cutt guitarist Jake E. Lee. This was one of the first metal albums to incorporate synthesisers extensively[citation needed].

Of all his solo albums only here is writing credited to Ozzy alone, although he states the title track was co-written with guitarist Jake E. Lee in the liner notes to The Ozzman Cometh. Lyrics were actually by Bob Daisley, although this time it had been agreed that he would not be credited for his writing. (On The Ultimate Sin he was erroneously uncredited initially, although this was rectified with later pressings.)

[edit] Remastering

Bark at the Moon was remastered in 1995 and again in 2002, 19 years after the release of the album. Many fans were heavily displeased with the result of the 2002 remaster, due to the heavy changes in the actual music[citation needed]. However, some (mostly younger) fans embraced the remaster as a more modern and slick version of the album. Nonetheless, there is little debate to the fact that the remaster was more of a remix than a remaster.

Bonus Track (2000 Remaster)

  1. "Spiders in the Night" (also on 1995 remaster)
  2. "One Up the "B" Side"

On the original UK release (vinyl and cassette) "Spiders" was in place of "Slow Down", the latter being released as a B-side.

The "Bark At The Moon" single gave Ozzy his first UK hit single and the band appeared on Top Of The Pops (miming 'live' in the studio). "So Tired" was also a successful single in the UK.

The video "Bark At The Moon" features drummer Carmine Appice,although drums were played by Tommy Aldrige on the recordings.
