Baron Forres

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Baron Forres, of Glenogil in the County of Forfar, is a title in the Peerage of the United Kingdom. It was created in 1922 for Archibald Williamson, a Scottish businessman and Liberal politician. He had already been created a Baronet in 1909. Williamson was the son of Stephen Williamson who represented St Andrews and Kilmarnock in the House of Commons. As of 2007 the titles are held by the first Baron's great-grandson, the fourth Baron, who succeeded his father in 1978.

[edit] Barons Forres (1922)

  • Archibald Williamson, 1st Baron Forres (1860-1931)
  • Stephen Kenneth Guthrie Williamson, 2nd Baron Forres (1888-1954)
  • John Archibald Harford Williamson, 3rd Baron Forres (1922-1978)
  • Alastair Stephen Grant Williamson, 4th Baron Forres (b. 1946)

The Heir Apparent is the present holder's son Hon. George Archibald Williamson (b. 1972)
