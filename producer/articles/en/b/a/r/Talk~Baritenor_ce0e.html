A baritenor is a fairly derogatory classification for a singer who has not developed his art to the point where his natural unstrained vocal production has become apparent. It is for this reason that most reasonable teachers do not rush to decide where the voice may lie in the general range of natural harmonic distribution. It also creates a problem between the early student and a rational teacher.

Most new students have a pre-conception of where their voice should go, based often on mistaken ideas of their successes in the past. It often destroys the relationship between student and teacher when the teacher gently suggests that the quailty or timbre the student has become accustomed to using, is physically contrived and needs to be altered.

Provided both can come to an agreement to develop the natural voice, in time, the classification will become apparent but it is generally agreed that it is not a legitimate voice that changes timbre at the upper range from baritone to a nasal tenor.

In relation to the possible earlier experience of a baritenor; it is conceivable that an untrained voice may have had considerable success while using a contrived technique. This "owning' of a particular song or part is derived from the unique distortion the singer may apply and the audience find acceptable. The fact that no-one else can produce the unique distortion a certain baritenor has done, is hardly surprising when considering how differently all humans are built. However, this does not over-ride the fact that there are more similarities than differences in voice characteristics. For this reason a "baritenor" is a transitionary classification.
