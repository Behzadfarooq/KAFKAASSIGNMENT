[edit] Minimum requirements

Bartenders have to have their grade 12 education to work in a bar. —The preceding unsigned comment was added by 199.216.215.4 (talk • contribs) December 7, 2005 (UTC)

[edit] Terminology

As I understand it, in the UK 'barkeep' is short for 'barkeeper' which specifically means someone who *owns* a bar, not someone who just serves drinks in one Justinep 21:59, 2 April 2006 (UTC)

Mixologist should certainly not be merged into bartender since it indicates not only stature (a mixologist isn't a type of bartender), but rather an education/understanding of the art/craft of creating/mixing drinks. Suggesting that a Mixologist is a sort of Bartender is akin to suggesting that an architect is a sort of construction worker. —The preceding unsigned comment was added by 64.131.246.82 (talk • contribs) August 6, 2006 (UTC)

Bar a place where where we drink, tender which means kindness, so bartender means a place where we drink which has kindness. —The preceding unsigned comment was added by 142.232.8.8 (talk • contribs) October 20, 2006 (UTC)
