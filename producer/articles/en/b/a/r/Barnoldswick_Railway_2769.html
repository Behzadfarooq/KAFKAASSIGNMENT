The Barnoldswick Railway Company was a railway company in the United Kingdom, which existed from 1862 to 1899 when it became part of the Midland Railway. It was formed to build a branchline from the Midland Railways line from Skipton to Colne. It obtained it's Act of Parliament on 12th August 1865. Construction started 2 years later.

[edit] Opening

The 1mile 1342 yards long single track line was opened on 8th February 1871. It left the Midland line at Barnoldswick Junction, near Kelbrook, and was worked by the Midland Railway from the start.

[edit] Absorbtion

In March 1898 the Barnoldswick Railway approached the Midland Railway with a view to selling. As the branch had always paid a regular and reasonable dividend the Midland obtained powers to purchase the branch in 1899.
