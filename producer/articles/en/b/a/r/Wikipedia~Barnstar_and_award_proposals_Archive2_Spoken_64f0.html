Barnstar wearing a microphone/headphone combo

User:Luigi30's soundstar

I think people who greatly contribute to WP:SPOKEN should get a barnstar like the one above. The criteria should be something like you have to do one of the following:

  • Create ten good Spoken Wikipedia articles (only three people currently qualify).
  • Create several long, high quality Spoken Wikipedia articles (a few might qualify).
  • Do an incredibly good job making a Spoken Wikipedia article of one very long and very tricky featured article, possibly by adding external audio (nobody yet, I think).

This recognition of non-text contributions is in the same spirit as the Photographer's barnstar.

— Chameleon 13:54, 29 May 2005 (UTC)

Should we just accept it then? I think it works just fine. Bratschetalk 5 pillars 13:57, Jun 21, 2005 (UTC)
Sure, why not? Is a vote necessary in this case, or do we jump right to the final proposal (with the first image)? Sango123 15:38, Jun 21, 2005 (UTC)

Well, I don't know about the vote. Here's the proposal:

[edit] The Spoken Barnstar

Category: Spoken articles

The Spoken Barnstar may be awarded to an editor who creates three or more high-quality spoken articles for Wikipedia.

This award was introduced on May 29, 2005 and designed by Chameleon.

Here's my proposal: — Chameleon 20:53, 22 Jun 2005 (UTC)

[edit] The Spoken Barnstar

Category: Spoken articles. See WP:SPOKEN

The Spoken Barnstar may be awarded to an editor who makes outstanding contributions in the form of spoken articles for Wikipedia. Outstanding contributions may include: recording ten good articles, recording three featured articles, or doing an incredibly good job of recording one long, complex, excellent, featured article despite all odds.

This award was introduced on May 29, 2005 and designed by Chameleon.
