Bareilly pronunciation (help·info) (Hindi: बरेली, Urdu: باریلی) is a city in the northern Indian state of Uttar Pradesh. Standing on the Ramganga river, it is the capital of Rohilkhand Division and is a center for the manufacture of furniture and for trade in cotton, grain, and sugar. The city's population in 2001 was 617,350. Geographically it forms the outer gateway to enter Uttarakhand state of India. Bareilly is the fiftieth largest metropolitan city of India.

This fast-growing city is also known as Bans-Bareilly, due to its big bamboo market. Bareilly is also known as the counter-magnet city because it is equidistant from New Delhi and Lucknow and has a lot of potential for setting up industries to attract people to settle. Historically it was the part of the ancient kingdom of Panchal. In the Medieval period it was under the Rohillas. The modern city's foundation was laid by Mukrand Rai in 1657.

[edit] Famous Personalities & Items

  • Bareilly is famous for being the home-town of former Miss World Priyanka Chopra.
  • Another notable personality of Bareilly is Prof. Waseem Barelvi, a famous urdu poet[citation needed].
  • Ahmed Raza Khan, founder of the Barelwi Islamic movement, which gets its name from the city.
  • A famous Ayurveda practitioner of the region was the Late Vaidya Narrottam Tripathi.
  • The place is also related to the late Kalpana Chawla, an astronaut at NASA[citation needed].
  • Late Pandit Radhey Shyam katha vachak was an important personality born in Bareilly. He contributed a lot to Hindi literature and drama[citation needed]. The book Radheshyam Ramayan is his seminal work. Even today most of the Ramlila performers use the quotations from his book.
  • Bareilly was the location of the famous Verma Seed Company founded in early 1900s[citation needed]. It exported seeds world over. Govind Verma, one of the descendents of the owner of the company, Amba Prasad Verma, was a famous lawyer of the city, whose legal briefs are included in citations of the Supreme Court of India and Allahabad High Court. Another descendent, Narayan Verma, is a famous Neurologist in the United States and the President of AIIMSONIANS of America[citation needed].
  • Bareilly is famous for its surma (EyeLiner) & furniture made from bamboo sticks.

[edit] Defense Installations

Bareilly has a sizable settlement of the Indian Army's regimental headquarter of Jat Regimet, as well as an air-base (known as Trishul) of the Indian Air Force. The Trishul air-base had foxbats aircraft which were capable of flying up to 1,00,000 feet[citation needed]. Bareilly has regional Headquarters of ITBP and BSF.

[edit] Temples in Bareilly

Bareilly is home to a number of famous hindu temples. It has four Nath (Shiva) temples at its four corners, Alakhnath, Tiberinath, Madhinath, and Dhopeshwarnath. One of the well known temples is the Shri Laxmi Narayan Mandir (located in Katra Manrya street near Bara Bazar). This temple was built by the efforts of a Muslim named Chuuna Miya(Fazal-Ur-Rehman). There is also another reputed temple of lord DoopeshwarNath which is assumed to be the place where Draupadi and Drishtadyumn was born by a grace of lord shiva in Mahabharata era. Other famous temples are

  • Tiberi Nath Temple
  • Hari Mandir
  • Bankey Bihari Mandir
