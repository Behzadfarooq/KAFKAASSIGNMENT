Barré Lyndon

Barré Lyndon (pseudonym of Alfred Edgar) (August 12, 1896 – October 23, 1972) was a British playwright and screenwriter.

Born in London, he may be best remembered for three screenplays from the 1940s: The Lodger (1944) and Hangover Square (1945) and The Man in Half Moon Street (1945). The latter was remade by Hammer Film Productions in 1959 as The Man Who Could Cheat Death. Lyndon began his writing career as a journalist and short-story writer before becoming a playwright. His first play, The Amazing Dr. Clitterhouse, was made into an Edward G. Robinson film. After that success, Lyndon moved to Los Angeles, California, in 1941 to concentrate on writing for films full time.

[edit] Partial Filmology

  • The House on 92nd Street (1945)
  • Night Has a Thousand Eyes (1948)
  • The Greatest Show on Earth (1952)
  • Man in the Attic (1953) (based on the novel The Lodger)
  • The War of the Worlds (1953) (screenplay)
  • Dark Intruder (1965)
