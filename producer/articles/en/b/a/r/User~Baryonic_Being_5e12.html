Hello. My name is Justin Morgan and I am a horse. There is no use my denying it. The ancestor of the horse in its evolution was Eohippus, and the name for a fear of horses is hippophobia. Although I am neither an Eohippus nor a hippophobic, I am a hippopotomonstrosesquipedaliamaniac (that's a person obsessed with long words). The common linguistic phonemes are striking, but if that weren't evidence enough, I was also born in the Chinese year of the horse, and when I disagree with something I invariably say 'neigh'. See Morgan horse.

QED.

In general I am an inclusionist Wikipedian, but I'm thinking of deleting this sentence in order to maintain neutrality.

Due to a shift in philosophical perspective, I have named my 'likes' section, 'Is positively reinforced by'. This is a behaviorist (or behaviourist) term, and basically it means that by being reinforced positively by something, I am more likely to want to experience it again.

Consider this: if you suppose that we (you the reader and I) were born at the same time and were swapped at birth, then aside for our genetic differences, would you be leading my life and I, yours? Spooky, isn't it?

[edit] Is positively reinforced by

  • Quantum mechanics - It may be wacky but it's the foundation of the universe. Quite sobering to think that the universe is founded on wackiness.
  • Mathematics - People often say that maths has nothing to do with the real world. To that I say, "you're right! It doesn't." That's what makes it so useful.
  • Grammar - If you can't take the trouble to present your speech and writing in good form, then how can your ideas be taken seriously?
  • Linux - Because penguins are great.
  • Apple Macintosh - If you look at a computer all day, like me, you want a nice one to look at.
  • Video editing - With Final Cut Studio.
  • Myst - Revere the Maker!

For books, I am greatly positively reinforced by the works of Douglas Adams, Patricia Highsmith and Lemony Snicket. On videography, see ZAZ. On music, Johann Pachelbel's Canon in D, as well as many of Mozart's works, are harmoniously received by my auditory faculties, though many independent artists and game soundtracks have caught my ear too. Mussorgsky's Great Gate at Kiev is particularly fine, too.

  • Sport. I refuse even to link to its article. *Spits*. I am especially negatively reinforced by ball games of the podiatric variety.
  • Enormous trucks travelling with alarming velocity in my direction.
