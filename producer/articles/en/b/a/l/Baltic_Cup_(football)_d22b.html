Baltic Cup (football)

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

You have new messages (last change).

Baltic Cup is an annual football competition contested between national teams of Baltic states: Estonia, Latvia and Lithuania. During Soviet occupation Belarus also participated in some of the competitions.

[edit] List of winners

  • 1928: Latvia
  • 1929: Estonia
  • 1930: Lithuania
  • 1931: Estonia
  • 1932: Latvia
  • 1933: Latvia
  • 1934: not organised due to disagreements over the 1933 competition
  • 1935: Lithuania
  • 1936: Latvia
  • 1937: Latvia
  • 1938: Estonia
  • 1939: not organised due to strained sports-relations between Latvia and Lithuania after a controversial basketball game
  • 1940: Latvia
  • 1941-1947: not organised
  • 1948: Lithuania
  • 1949: Lithuania
  • 1950: Lithuania
  • 1951: not organised
  • 1952: Estonia
  • 1953-1956: not organised
  • 1957: Latvia
  • 1958: Latvia
  • 1959: Latvia
  • 1960: Estonia / Latvia / Lithuania (first place shared between all three teams)
  • 1961: Latvia
  • 1962: Latvia
  • 1963-1968: not organised
  • 1969: Estonia
  • 1970: Lithuania
  • 1971: Lithuania
  • 1972: Estonia
  • 1973: Estonia
  • 1974: Latvia
  • 1975: Lithuania
  • 1976: Latvia
  • 1977-1990: not organised
  • 1991: Lithuania
  • 1992: Lithuania
  • 1993: Latvia
  • 1994: Lithuania
  • 1995: Latvia
  • 1996: Lithuania
  • 1997: Lithuania
  • 1998: Lithuania
  • 1999-2000: not organised
  • 2001: Latvia
  • 2002: not organised
  • 2003: Latvia
  • 2004: not organised
  • 2005: Lithuania (Estonia did not participate due to scheduling conflicts)

[edit] Total wins

  • 19 Latvia (including 1 shared)
  • 16 Lithuania (including 1 shared)
  • 8 Estonia (including 1 shared)
