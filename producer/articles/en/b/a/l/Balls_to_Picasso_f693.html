Balls to Picasso is a heavy metal album released in 1994 and is the second solo effort in Bruce Dickinson's career (see 1994 in music).

This marked the beginning of Bruce's collaborations with Roy Z, who would work on many of Bruce's later albums including The Chemical Wedding, Accident of Birth and Tyranny of Souls. Style-wise it is substantially different from Tattooed Millionaire but is still more traditional than the follow up album Skunkworks released in 1996.

The metal ballad Tears of the Dragon stayed for many months on the top of charts in countries like Brazil where Dickinson has a large fan base. Balls To Picasso was a Rock album rather than Dickinson's other solo works and albums with Iron Maiden, which were Heavy Metal and Hard Rock albums.
