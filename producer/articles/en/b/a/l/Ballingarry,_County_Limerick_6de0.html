Ballingarry (Irish: Baile an Gharra√≠ or "Town of the Gardens") is a village in County Limerick in the province of Munster, Republic of Ireland. It lies between Rathkeale and Kilmallock on the R518 road.The pictureesque town holds much in the way of touristic wonderment, the highlight of which is the award winning Mustard Seed restaurant situated at Echo Lodge. The town has a long and proud sporting tradition with Hurling and in more recent years Soccer coming to the fore. The local Junior Soccer team won the Desmond League Premier Division in 2003/04 and the year later the Granagh Ballingarry GAA club won the county Intermediate Hurling Championship to achieve Senior status for the first time since 1910.
