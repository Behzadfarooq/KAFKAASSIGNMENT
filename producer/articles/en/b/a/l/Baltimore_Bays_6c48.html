[edit] Baltimore Bays (Soccer Club)

This Baltimore area soccer club is the product of a 2002 merger between the Soccer Club of Baltimore (S.C.B.) and the Baltimore Football Club (B.F.C.). It is consistently ranked in the top 10 programs in the United States. They field both Boys and Girls teams from ages 7 - 21.

[edit] NASL

The first Baltimore Bays were a soccer team based out of Baltimore, Maryland that played in the North American Soccer League. They played from 1967 to 1968 at the Memorial Stadium. The 1969 season was played at Kirk Field, Baltimore's high school football stadium.

Following the 1968 NASL season, the league was in trouble with ten franchises having folded. The 1969 season was split into two halves. The first half was called the International Cup, a double round robin tournament in which the remaining NASL clubs were represented by teams imported from the United Kingdom. The Bays were represented by West Ham United F.C.. They were runners-up for the Cup with a 5-2-1 record. For the second half of the 1969 season, the teams returned to their normal rosters and played a 16 game schedule with no playoffs.

[edit] ASL

The second Baltimore Bays were a soccer team based out of Baltimore, Maryland that played in the American Soccer League. They were called the Baltimore Stars in 1972.

[edit] USISL

The third Baltimore Bays were a soccer team based out of Baltimore, Maryland that played in the USISL. They became the Eastern Shore Sharks in 1998 when they moved to Salisbury, Maryland.
