Baldehri

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Baldehri is one of the 57 Union Councils that make up the Abbottabad District of Pakistan's North West Frontier Province[1], the union council of Baldehri is named after one of the main village in the area, (similar to the way that the English county of Yorkshire is named after York city.)

[edit] Location

The village of Baldehri itself is situated about 11 km east from Abbottabad city centre on the route towards Mansehra on the main Mansehra Road. Geographically Mansehra lies directly to the north, but the hilly terrain results in these winding roads, hence Baldehri is onroute to Mansehra from Abbottabad city.

The area covered by Baldehri union council, is between Sian Da Katta village to Tanan Village and Mangal to Halmaira.

[edit] Geography

Like much of the Abbottbad area, the terrain and layout of land make UC Baldehri a very beautiful place, with fresh water streams running down from the hills and mountains. The main steam is called Mangal, and its water is very important for the cultivation of the area. There are primary schools almost in every village. Bach Christian Hospital is also located here in Baldehri UC, this hospital was very important during the 2005 earthquake that struck the area, due to its location on the Karakoram highway.
