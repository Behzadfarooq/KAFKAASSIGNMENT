[edit] Balanced arm lamp types

There are different methods to balance the lamp cap of a pivoting arm lamp.


[edit] 2 parallelogram & 2 Extension spring sets

  • A Luxo L-1 type of lamp with 2 parallelogram & 2 Extension spring sets

One strong spring set lift a parallelogram that's lifts a lamp cap

A more stronger spring set lift a second parallelogram that lifts the first parallelogram, spring set, and lamp cap.



[edit] 1 parallelogram & 2 Extension spring sets

An lamp type like the anglepoise lamp has 1 parallelogram & 2 Extension spring sets

One set of springs controls the shortest arm of a parallelogram. parallel on this shortest arm there is a "lamp arm" to keep these arm parallel there is a are two other parallel arms that are controls by a second set of extension springs.

[edit] 1 Parallelogram + 1 Counterweight

This lamp has a heavy standard , with hinged on a parallelogram construction formed by bars

The two parallel long bars and two parallel short bars

Which are all hinged on each other

One long bar extends past the parallelogram construction and haves a counter weight

The short bar also extends past the parallelogram construction and haves a lamp cap

[edit] 2 balancing arm sets & 2 Counter weight


Image:Lamp 2 counter weight outer position.PNG

The Tizio lamp and the WILFRID BUQUET lamp has two Weights

A balancing arm haves on one side of a axis a lamp cap and on the other side a counterweight. These parts are lifted by a second balancing arm that has on the other side of a second axis a more heavy conterweight. The two weights has a height from the floor position, A heavy foot keep the lamp stable.


Lamp cap x Arm = Arm x Small weight

(lamp cap + Small weight) x arm = Arm x Big weight.
