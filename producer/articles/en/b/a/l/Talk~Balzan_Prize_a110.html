Well, I'd say that using the logo in the article after asking and being denied would be rude (even if perhaps not strictly illegal under terms of fair use). Public, private, or otherwise, no organisation can control whether or not Wikipedia has an article about them. Provided we don't use any of their copyrighted materials (including the logo), the article's okay to stay. Sjorford 15:23, 7 Apr 2004 (UTC)

We should respect their refusal for us to use their logo, but we should also explain to them:

  4. Unless they have a copyright problem with our text, it's our sake to keep an article on them. They should be happy to have one.

The Wikiverse version is the same as Wikipedia's except it includes advertising under related links and does not completely fulfill the GFDL conditions. Here is the Stiftung's email, partly anonymised to avoid spam.
