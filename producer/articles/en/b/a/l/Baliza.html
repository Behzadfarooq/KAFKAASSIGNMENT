Baliza

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Baliza is a small town and municipality in eastern Goiás state, Brazil. The population was 1,106 (2005 estimate) in a total area of 1,783.00 km².

  • Elevation: 342 meters
  • Became a municipality: 1942
  • Postal code: 76250-000

Baliza is located in the Aragarças Microregion and is on the Araguaia River, 54 kilometers southwest of Aragarças. There is a paved road with connections to Piranhas, 95 km. to the east.

The distance to the state capital, Goiânia, is 415 km. Highway connections are made by GO-060 / Trindade / Turvânia / Israelândia / Iporá / Piranhas / BR-158 / Bom Jardim de Goiás / GO-421 / GO-194. For the complete list see Seplan

Municipal boundaries are with:

  • north and west: Mato Grosso and Aragarças
  • south: Doverlândia
  • east: Bom Jardim de Goiás

Economy

The main economic activities are cattle raising, agriculture, tourism, commerce (12 units), wood extraction and processing, and small transformtion industries. Public administration is one of the largest employers. There is also a substantial informal economy.

There were 66,000 head of cattle in 2003. The main agricultural products were bananas, charcoal, hard woods (120 cubic meters), rice (500 hectares), manioc, corn (160 hectares), and soybeans (3,000 hectares).

There were no financial institutions registered in 2003.
