Ballyhale Shamrocks GAA is a Gaelic Athletic Association club located in the parish of Ballyhale in County Kilkenny. The club is almost exclusively concerned with hurling.

[edit] Hurling

[edit] History

The Ballyhale Shamrocks GAA club was formed in 1972 when the two existing GAA clubs in the parish of Ballyhale agreed to amalgamate. They were Ballyhale and Knocktopher. A third club in the Parish, Knockmoylan, had ceased to exist at some time in 1959. The Club adopted as its logo a three-leaf shamrock with the letters K, B and K inserted on a leaf each. The club colours are green and white.

Since its foundation the Ballyhale Shamrocks club has stood out as one of County Kilkenny's most outstanding club at senior level. Since entering the senior grade in 1975 the club has won a remarkable ten Kilkenny Senior Hurling Championship titles. These county wins were subsequently converted into five Leinster Club Hurling titles. On St. Patrick's Day, 2007 Ballyhale Shamrocks won its fourth All-Ireland Senior Club Hurling Championship title. This means that Ballyhale Shamrocks are, along with Birr GAA club, the most successful club in the history of the All-Ireland club series.

[edit] Honours

  • All-Ireland Senior Club Hurling Championships: 4
    • 1981, 1984, 1990, 2007
  • Leinster Senior Club Hurling Championships: 5
    • 1978, 1980, 1983, 1989, 2006
  • Kilkenny Senior Hurling Championships: 10
    • 1978, 1979, 1980, 1982, 1983, 1985, 1988, 1989, 1991, 2006
