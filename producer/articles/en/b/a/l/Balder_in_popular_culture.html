Balder in popular culture

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

The Norse god Balder has inspired many works of art and poetry.

[edit] Depictions in modern popular culture

[edit] Comics

  • As an Asgardian, Balder the Brave is among the Norse gods featured in various stories among the Marvel range.
  • In Alan Moore's Top 10, the police are summoned to investiage the crime scene after Balder's death.
  • The manwha series Ragnarok, which is based largely on Norse mythology, features a reincarnated Baldur as the protagonist.

[edit] Games

  • Balder is starring as the main character in the upcoming videogame "Too Human", developed by Silicon Knights and published by Microsoft. Too Human is the first video game in a planned trilogy for Microsoft’s videogame entertainment system Xbox 360. "Too Human" uses characters and allegories from ancient Norse myths in a science fiction setting.
  • The Ogre Battle series has a number of weapons described as being 'made from Balder metal'. Aside from having the same name, there is nothing specifically to link them to the myth.
  • Baldur's Gate is a PC game named after, and largely taking place in, a city from the Forgotten Realms campaign setting for Dungeons & Dragons. The resemblance between Balder and Balduran, the founder and namesake of Baldur's Gate, may be coincidental.
  • The world of Ragnarok Online 2: The Gate of the World was saved by St. Lif, the son of Baldur, from a cataclysm prior to the time the game it set in.
  • In the 2001 PC game Max Payne, the story of which borrows many names and concepts from Norse mythology, Alex Balder is a DEA agent who has maintained contact with the game's protagonist, who at the start of the game had been working undercover for years.

[edit] Literature

  • In the novel, American Gods, by Neil Gaiman, Loki says that he will sharpen a stick of mistletoe and ram it into the eye of Shadow (the novel's protagonist), a reference to what he did to Balder. Also notable is the fact that Shadow is Odin's son. Furthermore it is revealed in the sequel novella, The Monarch of the Glen (anthologized in Fragile Things), that Shadow's real name is, in fact, Balder.

[edit] Music

  • In 1997, Burzum released the dark ambient album Dauði Baldrs [Balder's Death]: focused exclusively on the legend of Balder.
  • In 2006, Amon Amarth released the With Oden on our side album; the song "Hermods ride to Hel" portrays the journey of the god Hermod to Hell, to rescue his brother Balder.

[edit] Objects

  • Balder is a wooden roller coaster in the Swedish theme park of Liseberg.

[edit] Poetry

  • Henry Wadsworth Longfellow has written a poem about Balder.
  • Robert Duncan also referred to Christ as Balder in "The Adoration of the Virgin," a poem in his Medieval Scenes published in 1950.

[edit] Pet name

  • Balder has become a very popular pet name in Scandinavia, especially for cats, but also for dogs.
