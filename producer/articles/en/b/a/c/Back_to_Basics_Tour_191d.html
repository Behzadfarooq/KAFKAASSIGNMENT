Back to Basics Tour is a concert tour to support Christina Aguilera's third album Back to Basics. The show will currently feature 64 shows worldwide.

On the second leg of the tour, The Pussycat Dolls will be the main opening act. Danity Kane will also open on the US dates. In the third leg of the tour.

The third leg in Latin America has created confusion among fans, but only the Mexican Dates had been confirmed. The Mexican tickets will be released April 10, 2007 via Ticketmaster. [1]

[edit] The Show

Back To Basics Tour is a concert tour to support Christina Aguilera's Back To Basics album. There are 64 shows worldwide so far. Opening acts include Danity Kane and The Pussycat Dolls for her North American Leg. Danity Kane, however, will not be performing for the Canadian leg. The show has four segments : Jazz (1930's) , Reggae/Urban , Circus , and Encore with 6 costume changes. Most songs from the setlist are mostly from her latest album Back To Basics but also includes other songs from her previous albums. From her Stripped album : Dirrty , Beautiful , Fighter and Can't Hold Us Down. From her self-tilted album Christina Aguilera : What A Girl Wants and Come On Over Baby (All I Want Is You) but are turned into different versions instead.

Christina Aguilera performed in Las Vegas, Navada at Mandalay Bay Events Center on March 3, 2007.

[edit] Recordings

The concert will be recorded during the 1st Mariner Arena show in Baltimore, Maryland on April 28, 2007.

The full concert will be released in a DVD+CD package in July of 2007.

[edit] Controversy

On March 23, 2007 it was reported that Aguilera was caught lip-syncing her show at Madison Square Garden in New York City. According to her manager Irving Azoff they were experiencing technical problems with the sound on the night, however he neither confirmed or denied the lip-syncing allegations. Fans were supposedly infuriated at the possibility of Aguilera miming to pre-recorded tracks. Despite this bad press, the tour has so far received rave reviews.[2]
