Backus Mill Heritage and Conservation Centre

The Backus Heritage Conservation Area is a place where children and adults can re-enact life in Norfolk County, Ontario, Canada, in the mid 19th century. Education programs include a War of 1812 day where students are able to experience the life of a soldier. Highlights include caring for their own wooden musket, creating camp, and cooking their lunches over an open fire. The Heritage Village also has an extensive program on pioneer life.

BHCA also has conservation education programs that include pond studies, habitat walks, learning about diversity of life, amongst many entertaining and educational activities. The Education Centre has a Waterfowl Gallery where one can learn about the shores of Port Rowan and Long Point, as well as a display regarding 'species not at risk' in this area.

The eastern part of the conservation area has a seasonal swimming pool, as well as camping facilities for seasonal campers and those just visiting. The Backus Woods are an excellent source of the few remaining areas of Carolinian Canada where many species of plants and animals live. Tulip and sassafras trees are notable.

This conservation area is full of local history found in the Backhouse Homestead as well as the Backhouse Mill. This is a national historic site, as it remains from the War of 1812. Built in 1798, the mill is still in operation today.

It has been said to be haunted; although no one knows the identities of the spirits. The Mill and the Backhouse homestead are areas of the hauntings.
