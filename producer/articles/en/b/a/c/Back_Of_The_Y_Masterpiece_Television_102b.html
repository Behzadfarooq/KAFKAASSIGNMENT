Back Of The Y Masterpiece Television was a cult TV series produced in New Zealand, created by Chris Stapp and Matt Heath. Featuring such memorable characters as Randy Campbell, Danny Parker, Dick Johansonson, Cindy Cockburn and Spanners Watson, the show glorified stupid stunts and the consumption of copious amounts of alcohol. The Hollywood special episode featured Randy Campbell jumping off the back of the Y in the famous Hollywood sign.

Back of the Y is centered around a mock live TV show, featuring segments such as Randy Campbells extreme stunts and "Cunstables". The show is low budget and apparently filmed with amature equipment and edited on home computers. The show description at the start of the first episode references one of the cast members and sets the agenda for the rest of the show "Phil Bruff had a dream. To make a New Zealand TV show that wasn't complete shit. He failed... Miserably!!!"

The show consisted of 7 episodes of Back Of The Y Masterpiece Television and The Back Of The Y Goes To Hollywood Special.

In addition to being screened upon New Zealand television, the show has been given airtime on MTV2 in the UK and on Channel V in Australia. They are also responsible for That C*nt Rabbit (an animated TV show for MTV UK), Heck (animated show also for MTV UK) and Balls Of Steel (for Channel 4 UK).

Back Of The Y featured the New Zealand band "Deja Voodoo", then a fictional house band. After Back Of The Y Stapp and Heath developed Deja Voodoo into a proper band.

Back Of The Y then released a TV special, this time titled "Bogan's Heroes" which was a prison comedy.

They are currently working on a film called The Devil Dared Me To (The Randy Campbell Story). The film is backed by Headstrong Productions, in association with the New Zealand Film Commission. This film tells the story of Randy Campbell, Back of the Y's Stuntman character, and how he became "the worlds greatest stuntman". Due to be released in 2007.

[edit] Short Films

Vaseline Warriors: Set in a post apocalyptic future where all women have been destroyed. The men fight over ripped up porno mags and vaseline. All is settled following the tragic explosion in the lubridome.

Bottlestore Galactica: Battlestar Galactica parody. Space pilots Steineken and Heinlager get drunk and fly around the galaxy. Most episodes centered around copious amounts of alcohol.
