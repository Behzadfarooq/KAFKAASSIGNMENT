Bachelor Father was a British sitcom starring Ian Carmichael that aired for two series from 1970 to 1971. It was written by Richard Waring. It is not currently available on DVD.

[edit] Background

Bachelor Father was losely based on the life of Peter Lloyd Jeffock. Jeffock was a bachelor who had fostered twelve children. He later wrote an autobiography called Only Uncle. Richard Waring, who wrote Bachelor Father, based some of the plots on incidents told in Only Uncle. Waring said that he would have kept more to Only Uncle, but many of the true stories were so far-fetched, he thought the public wouldn't believe them [1].

[edit] Plot

Peter Lamb is a rich man who has always wanted a family, but failed to substain any relationships. In the first episode, he decides to foster children. He then fosters a variety of diverse children
