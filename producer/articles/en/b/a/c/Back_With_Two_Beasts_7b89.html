Back With Two Beasts is an album by Australian rock band The Church, released in 2005. It is supposedly another in their series of "jam" albums promised to fans via their official website yet there are songs here that are completed and left many fans scratching their heads as to why this is not considered an album proper by Kilbey and the rest of the band.

Starting with the ethereal "Snowfaller" the album signifies that the listener is not in for another jam album ala 1998's bonus Hologram of Baal or 2004's Jammed. "Pantechnicon" uses a vocal distorter to alter Kilbey's voice to chilling effect and is a standout track.

"Pearls" is a tune that would fit alongside their best singles and many question whether or not these gems will see the light of day worldwide considering that like the b-sides compilation Beside Yourself, this album is limited to mostly Church Merch as well as their live appearance where rare and hard to find albums are available for sale at the venues.

The "jam" aspect of the album can be found in the epic length "Night Sequence" which is representative of the bands sonic experimentations.
