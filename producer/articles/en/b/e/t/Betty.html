[edit] Terms

  • Slang for a cigarette, some alcohol, or some illegal drugs.
  • Slang for a pretty woman. e.g., "Check out that Betty."
  • Japanese Mitsubishi G4M aircraft (Allied reporting name)
  • Slang for being deceived or deceiving someone (e.g., "He stole my cheese, betty" or "that woman bettied me over 5 bucks")

[edit] Actresses

  • Betty Grable - American actress, singer, dancer, and pin-up girl whose sensational bathing-suit photo became the number one pinup of the World War II era.
  • Betty Driver - British singer and actress.
  • Betty White - American television actress best known for her roles in the sitcoms The Mary Tyler Moore Show and The Golden Girls.
  • Betty Buckley - American theater, film, and television actress.
  • Betty Ann Reyher - known for her Crazy Betty Ann videos
  • Betty (porn star) - German movie actress dealing mostly with bukkake, urolagnia and urophagia ("golden showers").

[edit] Fictional characters

  • Betty La Fea or Ugly Betty, a fictional character created in a Colombian Soap opera with tremendous international success and which was the origin for the US version Ugly Betty
  • Betty Boop, a Paramount Pictures cartoon character.
  • Betty Rubble, Flintstones character
  • Betty Cooper, a character of Archie Comics.
  • Betty Crocker, a brand name.
  • Betty Kane, now known as Bette Kane.
  • Betty Spaghetty
  • Atomic Betty, Cartoon Network-Teletoon's first child superhero.
  • Betty, formerly known as Master Pain, the main villain from Kung Pow
  • Betty Suarez, the title character and heroine of ABC's dramedy series Ugly Betty
  • Betty is the name of a fictional Cairn Terrier, formerly owned by Pauline Fowler in the British soap Eastenders.

[edit] In Music

  • Betty, used in the name of at least four albums.
  • "Black Betty" was the name of a song redone several times.
  • Betty, a pop rock alternative band from New York City fronted by Elizabeth Ziff, Alyson Palmer, and Amy Ziff.
  • Pray for the Soul of Betty, the name of a hard rock band from New York City.
  • Betty is a long-running British musical from 1915.
