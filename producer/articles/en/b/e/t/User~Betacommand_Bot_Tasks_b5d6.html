[edit] Adding template to the talkpage of a list of chemicals

I started adding/changing templates on the talkpages of a list of chemicals ( here), but a) found out that AWB cannot do it in one go (the prepend action is performed after the regex-actions), b) got tired of it, and c) it seems to be a typical bottask. I've done a fair bit of them, but could a bot please finish the remainder for me (some 1700–1800 pages, I guess)?

The tasks:

  • From the list:
    • Change an existing {{chemistry}} into a {{chemicals}}, taking the parameters 'class' and 'importance' from the {{ chemistry}}-template, where available, or adding them as empty parameters when not available, or
    • Add {{chemicals | class= | importance=}} to the pages when there is no {{ chemistry}} on the page, except when the term '(data page)' is in the title of the page, in which case it should get {{chemicals | class=NA}}.
  • When the template has an empty 'class' parameter at this point, steal that information from another template when that is available, importance should not be copied in this case,
  • Warn me somewhere when there are different classes used in existing templates (log these pages to a page somewhere under my userspace)

The {{ chemicals}} can carry a third parameter 'core', but that one should be ignored, that one is for a specific subgroup, which will be handled by hand.

[edit] Recategorise political parties to child categories

Can someone write a bot to assign many political parties to some narrower categories? We've been doing things manually up to now, but it's getting too much - there seems to be hundreds more to do, maybe over a thousand. Additionally, some categories need creating along the lines of Category:Political parties established in 1932.

Essentially, what's needed is to recategorise articles for political parties that currently have category "YYYY establishments" to category "Political parties established in YYYY". ("YYYY" here is a calendar year.)

For example, Liberal Party (Puerto Rico) is currently categorised as Category:1932 establishments, but it should be in subcategory Category:Political parties established in 1932.

Here's my "pseudocode" for one way it could be done:

FOR EACH article in a subcategory of "Political parties"
   IF article is in category "YYYY establishments"
   THEN
      IF category "Political parties established in YYYY" does not exist
      THEN
         create category "Political parties established in YYYY"
      END IF
      reassign article from category "YYYY establishments" to category "Political parties established in YYYY"
   END IF
NEXT article
