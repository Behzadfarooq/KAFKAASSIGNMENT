Beth Leavel (born November 1, 1955 in Raleigh, North Carolina) is an American musical theatre actress.

Leavel attended Meredith College in her undergraduate years and completed a musical theatre degree at the University of North Carolina at Greensboro. She acted during college, appearing in productions such as Cabaret and Hello, Dolly!.[1] Her Broadway debut was in the 1980 production of 42nd Street as a replacement for Annie. Twelve years later, she was seen in Crazy For You as Tess and an understudy for Polly Baker. In 1999, she played Mabel and Mrs. Bixby in The Civil War. Leavel returned to the revival of 42nd Street in 2001 as a standby for Maggie Jones and Dorothy Brock, eventually playing the latter.

In 2006, Leavel earned the starring role of Beatrice Stockwell in The Drowsy Chaperone. She helped to create the character's backstory, which she referred to as "a compilation of a lot of different women in theatre".[2] The role netted her two major awards: a Tony Award and a Drama Desk Award, both for Best Featured Actress in a Musical.
