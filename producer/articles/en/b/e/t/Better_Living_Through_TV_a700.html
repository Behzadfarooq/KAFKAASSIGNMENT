Better Living Through TV is the 7th episode of the TV series The Honeymooners. Ralph and Norton try their luck pitching an all-in-one kitchen utensil on late-night TV.

[edit] Plot

Spoiler warning: Plot and/or ending details follow.

Ralph believes he finally has found his perfect get-rich-quick scheme. He bought a product from someone at the Bus Depot, which is an all-in-one kitchen utensil: it slices, it dices, it opens cans. Actually, he didn't buy just one - he bought the whole box the man was selling, a 1,000 of them. His plan is to go on Late Night TV, place an ad for the utensils, and get rich from the profits. Alice, as usual, is very skeptical and refuses to have any part of this whatsoever, but Ralph is determined to prove her wrong.

Teaming up with Norton, Ralph and Norton go over the commercial they're going to make themselves. Norton would greet everyone "out there in TV land", and complain about needing so much time to do simple tasks. Ralph would then come in from a side door, announcing himself as "The chef of the future." Ralph would show how the same tasks Norton does can be done so much quicker with their utensil.

Everything goes fine, until they're informed that they'll be going on the air in a couple of minutes. Suddenly, Ralph gets panic striken at the thought of going on live to millions of people across America. Norton, on the other hand, remains calm, even saying, "Testing, 1, 2, 3, testing; how now brown cow?" Once the commercial starts, Ralph nervously walks out to the kitchen - then runs back in to the side door, before being pushed back out again. Norton nails all his lines perfectly, but Ralph stammers through his "Ch..ch..chef of the future!" Then when it comes time to show how quick the utensil is compared to other tools, they start by coring an apple - except that Norton is able to core an apple in a couple of seconds, while Ralph requires a lot of time (and needing to practically cut the apple open) to do the same thing. Ralph then nervously tries to open a can with the utensil, but winds up hitting his thumb instead and running around the kitchen stage, screaming. Norton quickly gives the number where they can call for the utensil, before yelling over Ralph's screams, "We now return you to Charlie Chan!"
