Betts Stradivarius

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

The Betts Stradivari is a violin made in 1704 by the Cremonese luthier Antonio Stradivari. It has been owned by the French violin maker J.B. Vuillaume, the English instrument dealers Hills & Sons, and is currently in the collection of the Library of Congress in Washington, D.C., having been donated in 1935.
