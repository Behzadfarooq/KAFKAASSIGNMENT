Betrayal in Antara is a Windows 3.1 computer role-playing game published by Sierra On-Line in 1997. It was a sequel to Betrayal at Krondor.

Betrayal in Antara was poorly received by fans of Krondor for a variety of reasons. First, Sierra had lost the rights to produce games based on Raymond Feist's Krondor world, and therefore had to generate the world of Ramar. The game follows the adventures of Aren Cordelaine, a fledgling mage, Willem Escobar, a young aristocrat, Kaelyn Usher, a female ranger, and Raal, a werewolf-like creature of the Grrrlf race.

The game consists of wandering through the countryside of Ramar, going from town to town on different quests. While your party is not limited from travelling far away from the desired endpoint, the game does work in a fairly linear fashion such that if you wind up in the wrong place, you will have to do extensive backtracking in order to be able to do anything, including get money to buy more food.

For some reason, the game was not written for Windows 95 but for Windows 3.1, an operating system with little support for games. As a result, it suffered from both substandard graphics for its time and poor performance. The 3-D world had very few polygons: "forests" in the game were not recognizable as such, as they consisted of flat walls with a forest-camouflage texture. A lot of the more interesting scenes (and all of the dialogue) consist of static comic-book-like drawings of the characters. The game did not run properly under 16-bit color or higher resolutions, and crashed regularly.
