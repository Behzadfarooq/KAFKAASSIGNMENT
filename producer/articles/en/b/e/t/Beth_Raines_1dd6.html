Elizabeth "Beth" Raines is a fictional character on the daytime soap opera Guiding Light. She is currently by played by Beth Chamberlin since October 2nd, 1989 to February 18th, 1991 & November 1st, 1997 to October 17th, 2003 on contract and October 20th, 2003 as a recurring character. She was previously played by Judi Evans from May 1983 to July 1986.

[edit] Character History

[edit] Beth's arrival, rape

Beth Raines arrived in Springfield in 1983, a victim of an abusive stepfather, Bradley. Beth used art (painting & drawing) as a means of self-expression. When Bradley caught her drawing a portrait of her natural father, he became so enraged that he threw her down a flight of stairs. During her stay in the hospital, she roomed with Mindy Lewis and drew quite a bit of attention from orderlies, Rick Bauer and Phillip Spaulding. Though she initially chose Rick, she and Phillip found themselves drawn to one another. Though both tried to deny their feelings out of respect for Rick, fate kept throwing them together--such as when they were named King of Queen at the Senior Prom. Finally the night of Mindy's eighteenth birthday party the two shared their feelings for one another. During their confession, they were interrupted by Bradley, who had shocking news for Phillip (that he wasn't a Spaulding). Afterwards he became even more abusive towards Beth finally raping her one night after Beth came home after a date with Phillip. Ashamed of what happened Beth told no one and rejected Phillip. Afraid, she then moved into a boarding house after a talk with her teacher, Annabelle Reardon, but was ordered by the court to return home.

[edit] Runaways

But when Beth returned home Phillip was waiting for her, and they jumped out of a window and ran away to New York. After they had been running for a while they found themselves near Central Park and nearly out of any money, Beth was working on some artistic sketches when both of them came upon a sidewalk Santa Claus (who preferred to be called Nick) who asked them if they needed help. At first Phillip warned Beth to not to trust Nick or anyone else who was a stranger in New York City and perhaps at any other time that might have been the correct advice. But then Nick pressed further whether they needed help, the lessons Phillip learned from his adoptive father, Alan Spaulding surfaced and he actually yelled at the man. Beth started hitting Phillip and then yelling at him that it was very rude of him to yell at Nick and Phillip backed down, knowing how much Beth might have appreciated having someone like Nick being so attentive to wanting to help them than hurt Beth as her stepfather had. After Phillip apologized to Nick, Beth told the man that they were nearly broke and had no where to stay and perhaps could not afford a hotel. Nick being the Good Samaritan he was and is, took pity on the young teenage couple and urged them to follow him through Central Park. Phillip at first was hestitant, but eventually the love of his life (at that point), Beth persuaded him to follow Nick and that's what they did. Nick safely led them out of Central Park to the Delacorte Theatre in Manhattan. When they got safely to the Theatre, Phillip and Beth found that Nick had led them to a place where he had led many people who were lost and homeless or poor or down on their luck. Apparently, Nick had turned, one of the wings of, the Delacorte Theatre into a half-way house. Nick turned them over to the others that were staying there and asked them to watch over Beth and Phillip. But, instead Beth and Phillip helped many of them and they helped Beth and Phillip in a mutually beneficial manner. Eventually with Nick (Santa’s) helped Beth tell Phillip about the rape. Phillip promised to protect her and they pledged to never let anything come in between them again. A couple of days after New Year's Day, Phillip and Beth ran into Nick again and thanked him profusely for helping them, but all Nick had to say was, "Well that is my job." He then smiled at Phillip and Beth and after they turned around, he was gone (and in his stead were their friends from Springfield, Mindy and Rick.) Mindy and Rick had followed them to New York and they bonded as they finally found Phillip and Beth. The four friends took odd jobs in order to survive and they enjoyed the sights and sounds of New York.

[edit] Alan's influence

In January 1984, Bradley tracked them down and, in order to evade him, Phillip and Beth dressed up as clowns and engaged in a mime act in the park. But he eventually saw through their disguise and got in a violent struggle with Phillip in Central Park that ended when Bradley almost fell off of a cliff. Beth and Phillip saved him though and warned him to back off. Not long after, Phillip's father, Alan, hired Bradley at Spaulding in the hopes of transferring the lower class Raines out of town and getting Beth away from his son. However, Alan's sister, Alex was a great supporter of the couple and when she learned what Bradley had done, she helped Beth charge him with rape. Bradley maintained his innocence, but at the trial he finally confessed saying that he had to mark her so no one else would want her, because no one else could love her like he did. Bradley was sent to a psychiatric hospital and Lillian divorced him. Beth then became engaged to marry Phillip. At the same time, Mindy was pregnant and told everyone that Rick ws the father. Then the day of Beth's wedding, Mindy's father, Billy, went to the church where Beth and Phillip were about to be married and confronted Mindy. He wanted to know if it was true that the father of her baby was really Phillip! Unfortunately, Beth overheard everything. The wedding started and Beth met Phillip at the altar. There, she slapped Phillip and called off the wedding. Beth left Phillip at the altar but she still loved him and wanted to marry him, while Billy thought Mindy ruined her whole life. Beth then even considered adopting Mindy's baby. Phillip wanted to be part of the baby anyway but Mindy only wanted to allow this if he would marry her. And since even Billy approved of a marriage, Phillip finally reluctantly agreed despite his love for Beth. So Phillip married Mindy and they spent their honeymoon at Cross Creek.

[edit] LuJack

About this time, Beth was hired to tutor Lujack, a member of the gang, the Galahads, who trying to turn his life around. (Beth was teaching him how to read, in exchange for him teaching her how to drive) Soon she fell in love with him and eventually convinced him to accept Alex as his mother and to leave the Galahads, to the dismay of his ex-girlfriend Darcy who then lead the Gang with her friends Martin "I.Q." Wilson and Gina Daniels. When Darcy terrorized Springfield's citizens, Lujack went undercover to spy on her while he let Beth believe that he returned to the gang. Finally, Darcy discovered Lujack's motives and in a rage, she entered Cedars on the opening day of the new Spaulding wing, tied up Katie Parker and stole her uniform, shot Lujack, kidnapped Alex and Beth, pushed a burning crash cart into Cedars Head Admin Warren Andrews Warren and turned Cedars into a general war zone. Luckily, Lujack was able to convince her to give up the fight and Darcy was arrested while I.Q. and Gina were proud of Lujack and decided to give up their criminal lives when Lujack gave them jobs at Spaulding. Meanwhile, Mindy had since miscarried and Phillip was free to return to Beth. Unfortunately Beth was happy with Lujack and refused to go back to him. Feeling very rejected and angry, Phillip discovered that Lujack and Floyd Parker were turning the Galahads' old garage into a Night Club and hired a crooked tradesman named Andy Ferris to sabotage the construction of the club. But Andy went too far, and when the building blew up Beth was injured and blinded. Phillip felt very guilty but he could not bring himself to admit his wrongdoing so he enrolled Beth in a school for the blind where she befriended Andy's blind sister, Nancy Ferris and rejected Lujack. Suspicious of Andy, Lujack went undercover as a blind man named Lenny to find out the truth and to be near Beth. Beth eventually regained her sight and was thrilled to discover her friend Lenny was actually Lujack. When Andy saw Lujack with Nancy, he tried to shoot him but was arrested. However, Alex got him released since she wanted him to keep quiet about the club plot. After India, now Phillip's wife, noticed that Phillip and Beth were renewing their friendship, she told Beth how Phillip had caused her blindness. Beth angrily rejected Phillip, but she did not tell him that she knew the truth because she did not want to cause any more bad blood between him and Lujack. Lujack was suspicious though and, in February 1985, got Andy to attend Alex's Valentines Day party with the intent on exposing Phillip. But once Andy got to the party with a copy of the tape he began to have second thoughts. Suddenly the lights went out and a shot was heard. When the lights returned Lujack was standing there over the dead body of Andy with a gun in his hand. Lujack stood trial and was found guilty of murder. In prison he found an unlikely ally in Bradley, who befriended Lujack at Lillian's request. When Lujack escaped prison during a benefit party, Beth helped him but both were stopped by Bradley who begged for forgiveness for what he had done to Beth. Beth forgave him and hid with Lujack on Kyle Samson's yacht. She eventually convinced Phillip to come clean about his deal with Andy and they became friends once again. Ironically the police did not believe Phillip's story but by now plenty of people shared the suspicion the India had murdered Andy. But the real culprit was holding India in a cabin where he forced her to sign a false confession. It was Floyd Parker, who had killed Andy out of his secret love for Beth. Lujack and Beth eventually tracked them down and convinced Floyd to give himself up.

Now cleared of all charges with Floyd in jail, Lujack decided to become a rock star with the help of promoter Suzette Saxon. But Suzette wanted Beth out of Lujack's career and so she brought the rock promoter Jackson Freemont to town for help, and Jackson immediately fell in love with Beth. Although Beth only had eyes for Lujack, they often argued over Suzette and Jackson. Unfortunately Lujack's music career also unknowingly brought Beth and Lujack into the schemes of the nefarious Largo Organization, which was trying to take over Sampson Industries. Their logo was ironically the same as the logo Beth and Jackson had just created for Lujack’s music video, a double ring that merge into one another. Then Beth accidentally identified Bea Reardon’s Company guest, Darryl, as Largo and was kidnapped by the organization onto their tanker. Lujack made plans to rescue her and Jackson wanted to help him but then he was also kidnapped. He and Beth wanted to escape from the tanker but Largo stopped them and shot Jackson who fell over the tanker leaving Beth to believe he was dead. Finally the tanker exploded after Kyle, Kurt and Lujack rescued Beth. Unfortunately the explosion not only killed Largo, but also mortally injured Lujack, who died in Beth and Alex's arms in Cedars. After Lujack’s death, Jackson turned up alive and comforted Beth and they kept Billy Lewis from going to jail for murder by testifying to Largo's plot. Brokenhearted, Beth sought comfort with Phillip in 1986 and the pair became engaged again. Tragically their happiness wouldn't last long when Beth began to work at a museum for Professor Blackburn. Blackburn had gotten his hands on the cornerstone of the von Halkein estate in Andorra. India knew that it contained gold and she tried to steal it from him. But the Professor was desperate to hold on to the cornerstone. He knocked India unconscious, shot Phillip, and held Beth hostage on a boat in Stony Lake. Sometime later Blackburn’s dead body turned up, Beth's shoe and some fabric from her dress was found but she was nowhere to be seen. Phillip was devastated and vowed to find her, but Alan, who had always looked down on Beth, fabricated some evidence to make it look like Beth was really dead.

Trying to piece together her memories, Beth went (without Neil) to the Springfield Country Club stables, where she encountered just released from prison, Bradley! overcome with emotion upon seeing his former stepdaughter alive, Bradley apologized for the horrible things he'd done to her Phillip. Afterwards, Neil decided to tell Beth about her past and accompanied her to Lillian's house. (it can be assumed he learned about this from Gary Swanson who learned from Blake) But Beth seeing her childhood home and remembering Bradley refused to go in and then ran away when she saw Bradley, yet again. Later a bus operator told Rusty that Beth had been on his bus to Clayton! When Blake heard this from Rusty she went into panic and very well she should have. Because as Rusty was telling Blake this news from the bus operator, Beth was being taken to the infirmary at Willow Hills and when Phillip heard the intercom that Beth was going to the infirmary he rushed there to see for himself that Beth was indeed alive! Later Blake and Phillip's aunt, Alexandra Spaulding visited him at Willow Hills and were astonished to find that Phillip wanted to voluntarily stay in Willow Hills, not realizing that Phillip knew about Beth and Blake's deception. Beth attempted to tell Phillip about what happened to her back in 1986 that made her disappear, but couldn't and then she "escaped" Willow Hills; which made Blake upset when Phillip got violent to her when she came for a "visit." Blake tried to keep Phillip in Willow Hills, but Rick and Lillian both having seen Beth alive, told the court that Phillip was as sane as they were and he was released. Phillip asked Beth to marry him anyway. Not wanting to rush into anything, Beth said no, but their feelings ran deep and on Valentine's Day 1990 they made love. Beth kept working on Blake to let Phillip go. One day in March, when Blake and Gary were rummaging through Neil's trailer to find away to force Beth to stay with Neil, Beth caught them and after having a major verbal fight with Blake over Blake destroying some of her art sketches, Beth collapsed. When Gary and Blake took Beth to Cedars, they were all shocked to learn from Rick that Beth was pregnant! In April 1990, Beth was ecstatic when Dr. Sedgwick showed her a sonogram revealing her child had been conceived in February 1990, and not in December 1989, which meant the child was Phillip's! However, Neil's jealousy caused him to be dangerous when he cooperated with Gary and planned to kill Phillip with a bomb at the opening of the Waterview Towers. When she had plans to follow Phillip to Mexico, Beth got pains and when she was rushed to Cedars by Mallet, she gave birth to a daughter, Lizzie. Beth kept in touch with Phillip by phone and although she asked him to return very soon, Phillip refused and became obsessed with his search. Beth visited Alan and stated that although she was glad he wasn't dead, she intended for their marriage to go back to what it was – before any feelings developed. Afterwards, an upset Lizzie called Beth and asked her to meet her at Cedars. When Beth got there, she saw Lizzie talking to Rick and abruptly dragged Lizzie away. Lizzie tried to tell Beth how sorry she felt for Rick and asked why Beth couldn't have helped Rick avoid suspension. Although Beth tried to maintain there was nothing she could have done, she finally lied that she didn't help because she didn't want to. An unforgiving Beth felt that Rick got what he deserved for the lie. Meanwhile, Lizzie started ranting about Jonathan Randall trying to ruin her life and blurted out that he wasn’t the father of her baby—Jonathan was! Although Beth tried to convince Lizzie to tell the truth (since it would come up sooner or later), she agreed to keep the secret for Lizzie’s sake. Unfortunately, Reva overheard their conversation and wasted no time telling Jonathan the truth. Beth tried to remedy the situation by bribing Jonathan to leave town but Josh Lewis ended up tearing up the check and warned Beth to stay away from Jonathan. Meanwhile, Lizzie and Coop made plans to marry ASAP. Although Beth was happy for her daughter, Lillian wasn't and made her opinion known. Lillian tried to get through to Beth by comparing this situation with the time Phillip married Mindy--in both cases the groom did not love the bride. The stroll down memory lane only got Beth upset and she was adamant that Lizzie would get what she wanted--Coop. On the day of the wedding, Beth wanted to make sure that everything went without a hitch and arranged for Ava to be locked in a meat locker during the ceremony. Unfortunately things unraveled when Jonathan, himself, crashed the wedding by declaring that he was the baby's father. During the fracas, Beth accidentally let it slip that she knew about this all along, leading Alan to warn her never to keep secrets from him again. Despite Beth's efforts, Coop walked out of the ceremony and out of Lizzie's life. Beth became increasingly concerned when Lizzie's preoccupation with winning Coop back outweighed her concern for her baby. Later, Lizzie concocted a dangerous scheme to win back Coop--stage a suicide attempt! Though Lizzie almost lost her life in the attempt, she was rescued by Gus and came away with a greater appreciation for the child she was carrying. The next day, Lizzie and Beth concocted a plan to lure Coop back into her life by Beth “fretfully” telling Coop about Lizzie’s suicide attempt. Unfortunately, Alan ruined it by alerting Coop that the attempt was a ruse. A few days later, Alan upset Lizzie again when Jonathan savagely tore up the contract he’d signed reneging on his parental rights (after Alan told Reva she wouldn’t have anything to do with the baby) Later, Lizzie became convinced that Alan had an obsessive concern for her baby, however Beth disagreed. Later, Tammy visited Beth and appealed to her to stop the war between Jonathan and Alan since it would only end up hurting the baby. Unfortunately, Beth sided with Alan—she felt Jonathan was a cancer that had to be dispelled from her daughter’s life. In the next few weeks, things became more and more tense between Lizzie and Alan, especially when Alan hired a psychologist and kept Lizzie under lock and key. Finally, Lizzie had enough and announced she was moving in with Jonathan and Tammy! Though Alan was livid, Beth tried to calm him by stating that Lizzie was asserting her independence. When Alan happily gave Lizzie the keys to her penthouse so she and Tammy and Jonathan could move in, Beth was sure Alan had an ulterior motive. She was right--Alan had a bug on the keychain. A few weeks later, Beth was surprised when Lizzie agreed to go to Italy with her and Alan. Her surprise turned to shock when Alan announced that Lizzie's baby would have the best parents--them. Though Beth was appalled at the thought of taking away Lizzie's own child, Alan won her over by telling her that the baby was a chance at redemption; a chance for him to correct the mistakes he'd made with his other children and a gift to Beth as well. Beth kept quiet to Lizzie as the trio prepared to go to Italy. Unfortunately, Lizzie got the upper hand and set up Alan to be detained at the airport. (by placing a meat cleaver in his brief case) Beth then approached Alan-Michael about helping his father. Although Alan-Michael laughed at first, he relented and agreed to place some calls to get Alan released. After he was released, Alan cancelled Lizzie's credit cards. When Lizzie demanded her money back, Alan refused, and implied that she was unfit to raise her baby. Lizzie became hysterical right in front of Beth, Jonathan and Tammy. When he and Beth returned home, Alan pointed out to a worried Beth how Lizzie was just like Phillip at the end. Later, Alan called a family meeting and informed Beth, Alex, Alan-Michael and Lillian that Lizzie had lost touch with reality. Beth then went to Jonathan and Tammy and asked for help in protecting Lizzie from Alan. When Beth was gone, Lizzie showed up at the mansion and hysterically claimed that Alan wanted to steal her baby and threatened to kill Alan if he tried to take her baby away. That same evening, Alan supposedly went to see Lizzie at her place at the Beacon and she shot him! Though the gun she used was (unbeknownst to her) full of blanks, Alan apparently suffered a heart attack. When Beth went to see Alan, she was astonished to learn that he faked the entire thing--including the heart attack. Alan admitted to that he arranged for Lizzie to be given the gun filled with blanks and set it up so she would shoot him. Alan then, with twisted logic, informed Beth that Lizzie was not fit to be a mother, and he wanted Beth and himself to raise the baby as their own. Again Beth was horrified and tried to convince Alan that he could not replace the child they lost but he refused to listen. He was determined to have what he wanted-- Beth, Lizzie and the baby. Disturbed by what she heard, Beth returned to the bed of the man she'd been having an affair with for weeks--Rick! Apparently, after the Bauer Barbecue the two former friends ended up hooking up and commiserated over their bad marriages. After talking and drinking for hours, the two fell in to bed and began seeking each other out for comfort. Eventually, Beth had Rick's suspension overturned. Though at this point, Rick wanted to end the affair and work on his marriage. Later, when Rick announced that Mel finally felt the same way, Beth asked where she stood. Rick stated that they had to get together and talk. When Beth went to kiss Rick goodbye, he turned his cheek to her. However, when Rick found himself again on the outs with Mel, he sought comfort from Beth. Unfortunately for Beth, their kiss was caught by Jonathan who took pictures. As for Alan, he dropped charges of attempted murder against Lizzie only to take her to court for being an unfit parent. The day of the trial, Jonathan showed Beth the photos he’d taken of her and Rick and blackmailed her with them. Although she refused to testify against Alan, but she did give Jonathan proof of how Alan's been setting Lizzie up. When Alan confronted her about why she was speaking with Jonathan, Beth lied that she was just bribing him. However, Alan did buy it and locked Beth in a bathroom so she couldn't hurt his case. Later, Beth learned from Rick that Jonathan offered to marry Lizzie to ensure she kept the baby. When Beth finally spoke to Jonathan, she threatened to have the marriage annulled but Jonathan stated that wouldn’t be possible, implying that he and Lizzie had already consummated the marriage. He then threatened to expose Beth and Rick’s affair if she made trouble for him and Lizzie. In the meantime, someone did threaten to squeal about Rick and Beth’s affair—Rick. Beth accidentally let it slip that Jonathan was blackmailing her and had pictures. Tired of lying to his wife, and afraid that she would find out the truth from someone else, Rick told Beth he intended to tell her everything. Realizing that once Mel knew, Alan would find out, Beth begged him to keep their secret but Rick was adamant. Racing after Rick, Beth followed him to Main Street where he was ready to meet Mel. Luckily for Beth, she spotted Leah and brought her over to Rick and Mel before he had a chance to say anything. Not wanting to make the confession in front of his daughter, Rick kept mum. Several weeks later, things got tense during a family dinner with the Billy and Reva and finally erupted in a shouting match. It all became too much for Tammy and she ran out. Fueled by Tammy’s words, Jonathan decided to show everyone how “respectable” people behaved and produced a photo of Beth making love to Rick! The dinner at Towers erupted into chaos and Alan left with Beth quickly running after him. At home, a cold Alan reminded her that their relationship started for the sake of business, but he believed that it had grown into a real marriage over time. Though Beth tried to explain herself, saying that she was angry at him because he hadn’t told her that Philip was alive, he wasn’t interested and gave her a choice. She could either leave, taking nothing but the clothes on her back or she could stay and consider her marriage vows, especially “death to us part”. Days later, after a confrontation with Mel, Beth got drunk and then was approached by Alan. Alan announced to Beth that he was having her followed from now on and after intimidating her, enlisted her in getting rid of Ava for Olivia. Beth did what she was told and approached Ava. Beth was basically blunt and honest with her, but Ava refused to leave Olivia alone – especially when Alan-Michael arrived to diffuse the situation. Afterwards, Beth confessed to Alan-Michael that she loved being Mrs. Alan Spaulding and claimed that was enough, until a better option came along. In the meantime, Beth tried to lure Rick back but he insisted that he wanted to work on his marriage and offended Beth by stating that what they had was not "real". Believing that Rick didn't belong with Mel anyway, Beth pulled some strings and arranged for Rick to be transferred to the clinic in Clayton--with her as his boss. Meanwhile, weeks later, Lizzie finally had her baby--a girl. The day of the baby's birth, Alan served Jonathan with a legal document asking for custody again. Jonathan--fed up with fighting with Alan proposed, for the sake of Lizzie and the baby, to move his new family into the Spaulding Mansion. Unfortunately for Alan, Jonathan's offer was an empty one. While Alan's guard was down, Jonathan secretly whisked Lizzie and the baby (who was suffering from a heart murmur) out of town. With the pair out of town, Alan went to court to get custody of the baby. In court, Beth awkwardly admitted that Jonathan fled town because Alan threatened to sue for custody. (which she really didn't know) Unfortunately for Alan, the case was dismissed. Not long after, Lizzie and Jonathan returned with the baby, whom they named Sarah. Alone with Beth, Lizzie bemoaned the fact that Jonathan didn't love her but confessed that Sarah was the best trick she had to woo Jonathan away from Tammy. Weeks later, after Christmas, Beth found Jonathan's wedding wing at the Jessup farm and realized that he'd had a tryst with Tammy. Beth confronted Jonathan but decided to keep quiet for Lizzie's sake.
