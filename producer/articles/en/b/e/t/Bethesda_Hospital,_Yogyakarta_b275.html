Bethesda Hospital is the oldest hospital in Yogyakarta, Indonesia. It has been familiar with Yogyakarta people since it was founded in 1899. It is one of the biggest private hospitals in the city. It also has ISO 9002 qualification[citation needed].

[edit] Name

The hospital has changed its name many times.

  • Petronella Hospital (RS Tulung) (1899-1942)
  • Yogyakarta Chūō Byoin (ジョクジャカルタ中央病院) (1942-1945)
  • Rumah Sakit Umum Pusat (1945-1950)
  • Rumah Sakit Bethesda (1950-present)

The names proved that Bethesda Hospital had a significant rule in the history as a health service modernization pioneer.

[edit] History

[edit] Dr. J.G. Scheurer Era(1899-1906)

The hospital was founded by Dr. J.G. Scheurer, a Dutch, as Petronella hospital. He was the first person to found a hospital in Yogyakarta with the support from Sultan Hemengkubuwono VII. It was famous as RS Tulung, because its services were all free of charge.

[edit] Dr. H.S. Pruys Era(1906-1918)

Dr. Pruys was the first person to introduce referral system by building the hospital branches in the villages with Bethesda as the main hospital. The branches were also supported by Sultan Hamengkubuwono VII. The branches were located in Wates, Medari, Randugunting, Patalan, and Wonosari.

[edit] Dr. J. Offringa Era(1918-1930)

During Dr. Offringa leadership, Petronella had a method reformation, especially admission office.

The building of the hospital branches was continued with the support from Yogyakarta Sultanate government, companies, factories, and banks.

Auto policlinic was established during this time.

[edit] Dr. H.P. Groot Era(1930-1942)

During Dr. Groot leadership, the hospital had many problems because of the World Depression and World War II. Auto policlinics were stopped because they were too expensive.

[edit] Dr. L.G.J. Samallo Era(1942-1949)

Dr. Samallo was the first native hospital president. During his time the hospital was taken over by the Japanese. During this time the hospital name was changed into Yogyakarta Chūō Byoin.

During 1946 the hospital was functioned as the office of The Health Ministry of Indonesia with Dr. Leimena as the health minister.

Finally, the hospital name was changed into Bethesda Hospital.

[edit] Dr. Kasmolo Paulus Era(1950-1958)

During this time, the hospital faced hard time because of the situation of politics.

[edit] Dr. R.D. Reksodiwiryo Era(1958-1964)

During this time the hospital was reformed.

[edit] Dr. Wardoyo Era(1964-1972)

During Dr. Wardoyo's leadership, Indonesia faced economic resession.

[edit] Dr. Guno Samekto Era(1973-1988)

Dr. Guno introduced Without Wall Concept and Holistic Concept. During his leadership Bethesda Community DEvelopment and UPKM were founded.

[edit] Dr. R. Nugroho Hadi Purwowidagdo Era(1989-2000)

During Dr. Nugroho's leadership the modern management principal and Costumer Oriented Principal were introduced.

[edit] Dr. Sugianto Era (2000-)

During Dr. Sugianto's leadership the hospital system was computerized and was awarded by The President of Indonesia as the hospital with zero working accident.

[edit] Bethesda Community Development

CD Bethesda is a non-profit organization focused on providing health solutions and services for marginalized communities, especially those in rural areas. Structurally, CD Bethesda is the outreach service unit of Bethesda Hospital which is governed by YAKKUM (Yayasan Kristen untuk Kesehatan Umum - Christian Foundation for Public Health). YAKKUM is one of Indonesia's largest health service foundations.

CD Bethesda was founded in 1974 as a result of a YAKKUM planning meeting that mandated each of its hospital units to extend their comprehensive services through community development efforts in order to serve societies that are unable to access public health services. The original mission was to assist poor patients whose problems could not be solved by the hospital alone. However, illnesses in communities are often caused by poverty, ignorance and injustice. In order to help the communities solve these problems, CD Bethesda evolved into a primary and holistic health service institution focused on building healthy, prosperous and self-reliant communities. By providing solutions, knowledge and resources, CD Bethesda empowers the people to help themselves. This refinement in CD Bethesda's mission came as the result of a statement by Unicef that said the service efforts of CD Bethesda should be executed with a holistic approach and be integrated with their hospital services. CD Bethesda's first director, Dr. Paulus H. Santosa, took it a step further when said "a hospital is a service not a building, therefore it has no walls", hence creating CD Bethesda's slogan of "A Hospital without Walls".
