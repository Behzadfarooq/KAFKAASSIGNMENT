Berserkers or figures influenced by the concept or mythos of the Berserker sometimes appear in modern popular culture, although they are rarely based off of historical depictions.

[edit] Depictions in modern popular culture

[edit] Literature

  • In Brian Jacques' Redwall series, Badgers are known to fall under a battle frenzy called the "Bloodwrath" which is similar to berserker rage.
  • Fred Saberhagen wrote a number of stories about robotic self-replicating spaceships which seek to destroy all organic life, which he called Berserkers, in reference to their violence and singleness of purpose.
  • Beorn in J. R. R. Tolkien's The Hobbit seems to be modelled on legends of berserkers; in the Battle of Five Armies, he goes into a berserker rage, and can literally turn into a bear. His name (which means "bear" in Old Saxon) also hint at this.
  • Some of Robert E. Howard's characters, such as Conan the Barbarian, King Kull, and Turlogh O'Brien are known to be prone to berserk rages.
  • Daniel Hecht writes in his 1998 Skull Session novel about berserkers being hyperkinetic/hyperdynamic individuals who can control their transformation into hyperventilating killing machines via a range of stimuli.
  • In N. M. Browne's novel Warriors of Alavna, the character Dan becomes a berserker, or 'Bear Sark' as it is referred to by the other characters.
  • King Touchstone in the Garth Nix Sabriel trilogy.
  • In Tim Lebbon's Berserk, the main character Tom, in search of his son, promises to restore a little girl to fellow berserkers.
  • In the Discworld novels by Terry Pratchett, the character Samuel Vimes sometimes allows 'the beast,' a personification of the fury in his soul, to become unleashed, most notably in The Fifth Elephant and Night Watch. In Thud!, this is represented by an outside, demonic presence of hatred known as the Summoning Darkness. Yet in that same novel, there is the introduction of another spirit in Sam's soul that restrains the Summoning Darkness - and likely 'the beast' in turn - and this is called the Guarding Darkness, i.e. 'Who watches the watchmen?'
  • In Lloyd Alexander's novel The Kestrel, the hero Theo experiences war and psychological damage that turns him into a berserker known as Colonel Kestrel.
  • A seafaring gang of Norse Berserkers figures prominently Nancy Farmer's 2004 young adult novel The Sea of Trolls. Known as the Queen's Berserkers, the group, led by Olaf One Brow, travels by sea to raid and loot other lands. The Berserkers drink a potion of bog myrtle to bring on their rages, during which they pant and howl like wolves(though it is mentioned that some can do it naturally). A teenage female, Thorgil, known as a shieldmaiden, travels with the group. Her ambition is to become a full fledged Berserker like her father before her.
  • In David Gemmell's Drenai series, Druss the Legend himself was a beserker and was called the Deathbringer by the Nadir people.

[edit] Comics, manga, and anime

  • There is a manga series (and subsequently anime) entitled Berserk. The main character, Guts, was sometimes shown to exhibit Berserker rages in fighting, but after acquiring the "Berserker's Armor" he becomes completely savage in his fighting, unable to tell friend from foe.
  • The Marvel Comics character Wolverine is known to go into "berserker rages". In combination with his adamantium claws and his healing factor, he is known to be an unstoppable, invincible, killing machine. Marvel also has a character known as Berzerker, who has a quick temper and possesses powers over electricity. The Hulk is another well-known Marvel character who fights in a constant state of rage, and whose shape shifting is similar to some of the mythological berserkers.
  • Gundam Seed's Kira Yamato is called a once called a berserker in the series. In the series, berserkers were described as peaceful and good-natured until they get into battle, become war-like, vicious, and lose sight of everything outside of the battle.
  • A robot known as the "Berserker" was excavated by British army scientists in the manga Spriggan.
  • In the manga/anime Record of Lodoss War, Orson, a main character assumes a beast-like state (he is in fact possessed by the spirit of rage) and is referred to as a "Berserker". Instead of the now traditional reckless fighting associated with berserkers, Orson will attack both friend and foe until he is either subdued or killed.
  • In the manga/anime Weiß Kreuz, Jei Farfarello, member of the antagonist assassin team Schwarz, is code-named "Berserker".
  • In the manga/anime Neon Genesis Evangelion, the term Berserker is used when one of the mechs goes out of control.
  • In the manga/anime/game Fate/Stay Night Berserker is one of the servant classes which are summoned by the wizards.

[edit] Film and television

  • A tribe of Berserkers appear in the movie The 13th Warrior, based on the book Eaters of the Dead by Michael Crichton. (In the book and film, the Berserkers are hinted to be a remnant population of Neanderthals.)
  • A couple of Berserkers are featured in the Norse mythology parody film, Erik The Viking.
  • In Kevin Smith's first film, Clerks., Olaf, the cousin of Silent Bob (Kevin Smith) makes numerous comical references to Berserkers. Comical for the word's juxtaposition to seemingly unrelated things, he is quoting lyrics from the song "Berserker" by Love Among Freaks, which is also featured on the movie's soundtrack. Also in Clerks, one of the last lines refers to Randall, the main character's counterpart, as a berserker. Dante, the protagonist, sings, "Here comes Randall / He's a berserker..." as Randall makes his final exit out the Quick Stop's front door.
  • A Thundercats episode featured laser toting pirates called "Berzerkers Berzerkers Berzerkers".
  • Many professional wrestlers such as Chris Benoit, Steve Austin and Dave Batista sometimes 'snap' into an intense, angry, berserker state midway through a match, no-selling moves by their opponent and cleaning house.
  • A pro-wrestler named Berzerker wrestled in the WWE in the early-90s.
  • In the Japanese Super Sentai series Mahou Sentai Magiranger, there is a race of monsters known as the Hades Beastmen Berserkers led by King Glúm do Bridon. They are a race of demons in Viking-like armor.

[edit] Music

  • "Berserker" was the title of an album by Gary Numan in 1984. The title track was also a Top 30 UK hit single.
  • The Berzerker is the name of an Australian death metal band.
  • Zakk Wylde's Black Label Society wear patches on the front of their leather vests that say "BERSERKER" which is an indication that they are either a member of the band, a member of the road crew or one of Zakk's inner circle. Black Label Society released a song by the name of "Berserkers" on both the album 1919 Eternal, and on the Ozzfest CD from the 2002 tour.
  • "Berserker" is the name of a song by Love Among Freaks, featured in Kevin Smith's Clerks.
  • "Return of the Berserker" is the name of a song by The Futureheads off of their album News and Tributes.

[edit] Games

  • The original Advanced Dungeons and Dragons Monster Manual gave statistics to battle Berserkers in the game, and later manuals (beginning with Unearthed Arcana) added the Barbarian as a character class with a berserk rage ability. The Dungeons & Dragons 3rd Edition supplement Deities and Demigods presented a new Berserker prestige class that was meant to be used in pseudo-Norse settings.
  • In many console role-playing games, there is a Berserker character class. These include several Final Fantasy games where the Berserk status ailment causes a character to do greater damage but cease to follow commands, leaving no ability to heal. The Fire Emblem games and Untold Legends: Brotherhood of the Blade, as well as the MMORPGs Dark Age of Camelot and "Everquest 2". World of Warcraft has a Warrior ability called "Berserker Stance". Several Dungeons & Dragons computer role-playing games feature adaptations of the barbarian character class.
  • In Kingdom Hearts II, the Organization XIII member Saïx fights as a berserker, focused completely on offense with absolutely no worries about keeping himself alive beyond endurance. He also controls primitive Nobodies called "Berserkers" who use a similar battle strategy.
  • There's a "Berserkr" enemy in Capcom's RPG Breath of Fire III. The Berserkr enemy is a black robot who starts the battle confused, and when is about to die cast the spell "Berserk" on himself, gaining strength at the cost of death 3 turns later.
  • Several strategy games include berserker units, including Heroes of Might and Magic IV. In Myth: The Fallen Lords and Myth II: Soulblighter, Berserks are a fictional hybrid of Scottish and Norse warrior traditions. In Age of Empires II: Age of Kings and Civilization III Play the World, Berserkers are unique units of the Scandinavian civilization (Vikings). In the Total War series, Viking berserkers are introduced in Viking Invasion, units from several Germanic tribes in Rome: Total War, and Celtic berserker-like units in Barbarian Invasion.
  • In the game Warhammer 40,000, some of the Chaos Space Marines dedicated to the fictional god of war and destruction, Khorne, are known as Berzerkers due to their focus on close combat and the shedding of blood, even their own. There are several other units in Games Workshop games which appear inspired by the berserker concept, notably the Death Company of the Blood Angels Space Marines and the Night Goblin Fanatics of Warhammer Fantasy.
  • Berzerker is a powerful alien entity in the computer game Warhead.
  • Berserker is the third trained unit in the Wolf Clan in Battle Realms. It can transform into a werewolf by Lycanthropy.
  • Berserker is the name of an unlockable character in the medieval fighting game Soul Calibur II.
  • In Lost Kingdoms (Rune in Japan) for Nintendo Gamecube, the Berserker is one of the usable and enemy monsters. It indiscriminately attacks any other creature. In the sequel, Lost Kingdoms II, the berserker could be upgraded to a Berserk Master which made every nearby creature attack the creature closest to it.
  • In the space combat game Space Wolves, Berzerkers are a race of combat machines turned against their human creators.
  • In Quake II and Quake 4, Beserkers are powerful Strogg enemies. Their arms have been replaced with mechanical counterparts with two distinguishing features: the left arm ends in a large mace (club) where the hand should be, and the right forearm has been replaced with a massive, sharp spike.
  • In Diablo 2, the class Barbarian has a skill named Berserk which negates all armor while doing extreme damage.
  • In the Doom, series, there is an item called "berserk" that looks like a black medkit. This increases the player's health to full capacity and permanently increases the effectiveness of the fist attacks until the end of the level.
  • In Lineage 2, the class Prophet has a positive (but also negative) buff called Berserk which increases physical/magical damage, attack speed, magical cast speed and run speed while decreasing magical and physical defence.
  • A type of Uruk-hai is known as a berserker in most of the Lord of the Rings movies and games
  • In Star Wars Galactic Battlegrounds the Wookiees have Beserkers as their special unit.
  • In the Fallout series, one can acquire the reputation of "Berserker" by often resorting to violence.
  • In Gears of War, there is a blind overmuscled enemy called the Berserker.
  • In Fire Emblem, Berserker is an unit class that resembles Warriors (another unit class) and in addition can destroy houses.
  • As a mercenary hired at the Mercenary Post in Stronghold 2, the Berserker enters into battle wielding a labrys with a potent charge ability.
  • The Orc race (called Orsimer) in The Elder Scrolls series has the "Berserk" ability, which causes them to inflict a large amount of damage for a short period of time.
  • In Halo 2, when all but one of a brute platoon is killed, the last remaining brute will berserk, throwing down his weapon and flinging himself at enemies with increased strength.
  • In RuneScape there is a helmet worn by the Fremenniks called a Beserker Helm.
  • In Pirates, Vikings, and Knights, which is a mod for Half-Life, there is a class on the Viking team called Berserker. Their special ability is a berserking rage, where they gain a bonus to their health, attack speed, and damage.
  • In Age of empires 2 berserker is the viking unique unit, infantry that regains healt by itself.
