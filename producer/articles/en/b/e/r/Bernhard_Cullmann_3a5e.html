Bernhard Cullmann

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Bernhard Cullmann, (born November 1, 1949) nicknamed "Bernd", is a former German footballer.

He began his footballing career in 1969 with SpVgg Porz, until he was signed by 1. FC Köln in 1970. He played 341 matches in the Bundesliga for them before his retirement on health grounds in 1983. He played 40 matches for the German team between 1973 and 1980, scoring six goals. He participated at the 1974 FIFA World Cup, the 1978 FIFA World Cup, and the 1980 UEFA European Football Championship. Between 1991 and 1996 he was on the board of 1. FC Köln. Since 1999 his son Carsten Cullmann has also played for 1. FC Köln.
