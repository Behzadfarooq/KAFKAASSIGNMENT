[edit] Notability in Question

I would suggest thay the authors of this page review the guideline for notability and the expectation of some supporting evidence of this man's notability other than the webpages of his organization. To me there is intangible evidence that he is notable, but this article is not likely to withstand a review of editors if it was tagged for deletion for lack of notability (see WP:N).

--Kevin Murray 21:06, 24 December 2006 (UTC)

A quick Google search indicates that the most notable fame that this person has outside his own web sites, is having been mentioned several times in testimony to the Gomery Commission (Commission of Inquiry into the Sponsorship Program and Advertising Activities). News reports and testimony indicated that he was accused of being involved in a secret fundraising operation for the Federal Liberal Party.

The Institute for Public Affairs of Montreal web site claims that it is "Canada's largest public interest advocacy alliance with over 2000 national political, labor, corporate, and communal leaders as members or associates." However another quick Google search finds that most references to this organization originate from its IAPM web site or letters to various newspaper editors and politicians.

Two points if I may. Firstly,a "quick Google" search will show some 1,000 entries that include our policy papers, articles, social action projects, and yes some 20% are references to the Gomery Inquiry. I had attcked the Inquiry in my press columns as an abuse of due process for some six months before I even appeared. I was not named for any blame nor accused of any "involvement". I was asked to clarify what went on at a lunch I attended with another witness some two months after I had severed all ties to the Liberal Party in 2001. My entire appearance lasted less than two hours - when most lasted much longer - in a year-long inquiry with over one hundred witnesses including two Prime Ministers and some half-dozen cabinet ministers.

Secondly, as I mentioned in my response to Judith, if you would like a list of organizations that reference the Institute I would be glad to help you with a list of documentation much of which appears on the site itself. Cheers, Beryl Wajsman info@iapm.ca
