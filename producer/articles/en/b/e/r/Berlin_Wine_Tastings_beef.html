The Berlin Wine Tastings have highlighted the quality of Chilean wines. The four tastings to date, using the same set of wines, have produced varying results.

The tasters evaluated two vintages each of eight top wines from France, Italy and Chile. In each tasting, wine lovers blind tasted two vintages each of Château Latour, Château Lafite, Château Margaux, Don Maximiano Founder´s Reserve, Sassicaia, Seña, Tignanello, and Viñedo Chadwick.

[edit] Berlin 2004

In the Berlin Wine Tasting of 2004, a professional blind tasting was held with 36 experts drawn from European wine journalists and wine buyers.

These were top 10 results of the tasting:[1]

Rank Country Wine
1  Chile Vinedo Chadwick 2000
2  Chile Seña 2001
3  France Château Lafite 2000
4  France Château Margaux 2001
4  Chile Seña 2000
6  France Château Margaux 2000
6  France Château Latour 2000
6  Chile Viñedo Chadwick 2001
9  Chile Don Maximiano 2001
10  France Château Latour 2001

[edit] Tokyo 2006

The Tokyo Wine Tasting of 2006 was organized and moderated by Fumiko Arisaka, founder of Vinotheque magazine and by Steven Spurrier. The competition was held at the Grand Hyatt Hotel on June 14, 2006.

These are the top 10 results of the tasting:[3]

Rank Country Wine
1  France Château Latour 2000
2  Chile Seña 2000
3  Chile Viñedo Chadwick 2000
4  Chile Seña 2001
5  Chile Don Maximiano Founder’s Reserve 2003
6  France Château Margaux 2000
7  France Château Lafite 2000
8  Italy Tignanello 2000
9  Italy Sassicaia 2000
10  Chile Viñedo Chadwick 2001
