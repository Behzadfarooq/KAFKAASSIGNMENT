Bernardo Castello or Castelli (1557 - 1629) was a portrait and historical painter.

Castello was born in Albaro, now a quarter of Genoa.

He worked and apprenticed under Alessandro Semini and Luca Cambiaso; though he needs to be distinguised from Giovanni Battista Castello, Il bergamesco, who was an elder friend of and collaborator with Cambiasi. He was a friend of Torquato Tasso, and took upon himself the task of designing the figures of the Gerusalemme Liberata. Some of these subjects were engraved by Agostino Caracci. Besides painting a number of works in Genoa, mostly in a rapid and superficial style, Castello was employed in Rome and in the court of the duke of Savoy.

He was the father of Valerio Castello.
