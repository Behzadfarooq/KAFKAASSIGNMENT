Bert Karlsson

Bert Willis Karlsson (born June 21, 1945 in Skara, Västra Götaland County) is a Swedish record company manager, entrepreneur, former politician and front figure of the New Democracy (Ny Demokrati) party.

As the owner and manager of the record company Mariann Grammofon, Bert Karlsson became a familiar face to the Swedish public as the man behind Carola Häggkvist. In 1984 he started Skara Sommarland, a theme park.

In 1991, Bert Karlsson and count Ian Wachtmeister started the populist political party New Democracy. Attracting major media attention, and with a patriotic platform (some would say racist), the party received 6.7 percent of the vote in the parliamentary election in September. With no previous political experience, Bert Karlsson suddenly found himself elected to the Riksdag as a Member of Parliament.

When New Democracy imploded due to internal struggles and failed in the 1994 parliamentary election Karlsson went back to being a full-time businessman. He has started the popular Swedish reality television show Fame Factory, (a programme somewhat similar to the British show Pop Idol), in which he plays an important role. Many Mariann artists have entered Melodifestivalen, the Swedish qualifications for the Eurovision Song Contest. From 1978 to 2005 approximately 120 songs from Karlsson's record company have been competing, and 14 have gone on to represent Sweden in the European Song contest. Mariann artists include Herreys and Charlotte Perrelli, who went on to win the European competition.

In March 2005, Bert Karlsson filled out an application for membership in the Liberal People's Party during a television interview. When the party immediately responded with criticism of him, calling the move a media stunt, he did not go through with his application.

[edit] Trivia

  • In the browser-based online music roleplaying game, Popomundo, it is possible to see a message saying: "You spot Bert Karlsson in the audience", whilst viewing a band's concert. An evident homage to one of the most respected men in the Swedish music industry from the Swedish developers of the game.
