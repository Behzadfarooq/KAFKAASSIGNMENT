Berlin Radio Symphony Orchestra (East Berlin)

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

This article is about the orchestra founded in 1923 and situated in East Berlin during the Cold War. For the orchestra founded in 1946 and situated in West Berlin during the Cold War, see Deutsches Symphonie-Orchester Berlin.

The Berlin Radio Symphony Orchestra (Rundfunk-Sinfonieorchester Berlin) is an orchestra in Berlin, Germany. It was founded in 1923. The orchestra has a reputation for twentieth-century music.

[edit] Principal conductors

  • Marek Janowski (2002–)
  • Rafael Frühbeck de Burgos (1994–2000)
  • Heinz Rögner (1973–1993)
  • Rolf Kleinert (1959–1973)
  • Hermann Abendroth (1953–1956)
  • Sergiu Celibidache (1945–1946)
  • Eugen Jochum (1932–1934)
  • Bruno Seidler-Winkler (1926–1932)
