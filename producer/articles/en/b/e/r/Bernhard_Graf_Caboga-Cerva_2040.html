Bernhard Graf Caboga-Cerva

Coat-of-arms of the House of Caboga

Bernhard Caboga (February 6, 1785 - November 19, 1855) was a count and general from the Austrian Empire later dualist Austria-Hungary Army. He was born in Dubrovnik in 1785. He was from old Ragusan noble family of the House of Caboga.

He graduated from an engineering academy. In 1803 he was lieutenant in the cartographic service, fastened 1806 Triest, defended 1809 the passport lies and was assigned 1813 to the army of the Swedish Crown Prince. After diplomatic missions to the Joni islands, Albania and Montenegro working at Okkupation Neapels 1821 1827-19 with the prince of Hessen Homburg in Russian headquarters, 1831/32 in the staff of the marshals Dibitsch and Paskevic, then on the attachments Dalmatians and Bohmens and commander of the Mineur corps. 1836-38 at the “modenensichen Hofe”, with the sons of the duke of Modena, then their Colonel master. 1838 into the general genius management appoint and major general. 1846 field marshal lieutenant, 1849 Gerneraldirektor and 1854 Feldzeugmeister.

He married in 1833 with the widow Princess Julianne Wanda of Potocki, 1788, 18 September 1876 in Lemberg, not have any children.

He died in Vienna in 1855.
