Bert's Big Adventure

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Bert's Big Adventure is a charity created by Bert Weiss of radio station WWWQ-FM (Q100) in Atlanta, United States.

The mission of the charity is to take chronically ill and physically challenged children and their immediate families on a VIP weekend trip to Walt Disney World in Orlando, Florida, giving them a chance to be together as a family without being reminded of the challenges they face in their day-to-day lives.

The inaugural trip in 2003 featured 5 families who stayed at the Dolphin hotel near Epcot. Air travel was provided by the Atlanta Hawks and Atlanta Thrashers.

The 2004 trip featured 7 families, who stayed at the Animal Kingdom Lodge near Animal Kingdom. Air travel was provided by Delta Air Lines.
