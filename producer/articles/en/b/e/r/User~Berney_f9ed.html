I am Berney. I obtained a user name on December 14, 2005, but have been editing (minor edits) since November of that year. My main methodology of editing for Wikipedia is simply clicking on Random Article, then reading the article and fixing any errors I see. I rarely actually write an article, and when I do, it's usually a stub. (Sorry!)

To see a list of my contributions, click anywhere on this sentence.

Please feel free to fix any typographical or spelling errors you may find on this page. If you feel that a sentence (or paragraph!) needs rewriting, however, don't rewrite it. Instead, please tell me about it on my talk page.

I am a member of the Association of Wikipedians Who Dislike Making Broad Judgements About the Worthiness of a General Category of Article, and Who Are In Favor of the Deletion of Some Particularly Bad Articles, but That Doesn't Mean They are Deletionist.

The motto of the AWWDMBJAWGCAWAIFDSPBATDMTD is Est omnino difficile judicare inclusionis meritum cuiusdam rei in encyclopaedia cum ratio sciendi quid populi referat incerta sit, sed nihilominus aliquid encyclopaediam dedecet, which translates to, "it is generally difficult to judge the worthiness of a particular topic for inclusion in an encyclopedia considering that there is no certain way to know what interests people, but some topics nevertheless are not fit for an encyclopedia." This motto reflects the desire of these Wikipedians to be reluctant, but not entirely unwilling, to remove articles from Wikipedia.
