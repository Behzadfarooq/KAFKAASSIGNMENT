Berean Christian High School is a private, Christian coeducational high school in Walnut Creek, California, serving grades nine through twelve. Berean Christian's name comes from the Bereans in Acts 17:11 who "received the word with great eagerness, examining the Scriptures daily to see whether these things were so.[1]" Berean Christian has served the East Bay since 1969, having moved to its current Walnut Creek campus in 1985. Berean Christian's goals are two-fold: to prepare the student to enter the college of his choice and to integrate fully a Christ-centered education through the Word of God for its young people.

[edit] Enrollment Criteria

In January, an optional student preview day is offered in which prospective students are treated to a chapel service and a skit from the advanced drama class, toured around the school, and treated to lunch, usually pizza. Furthermore, parents have an optional informational night in which they may meet the administration and teachers and receive an informational packet. Following parents' night, applications to the school and application fees may be sent for the next school year. After an application has been sent, each family is contacted in order to schedule an interview. Each child is made to fill out a informational form and to sign that he is a Christian. The child is interviewed by either the principal or one of the two deans. A decision committee reviews each application file; letters are then mailed to parents regarding their child's application. Upon this, the family is then responsible for notifying the school of their intent and if intending to apply, to file all necessary transcripts.

[edit] Philosophy

[edit] Educational

Berean Christian's educational philosophy holds that all reality, and thus all academic matters, should be based and interpreted by God's standards, as found in His divine revelation, the Bible. Since God is held to have created the universe, all has the purpose of glorifying Him. Thus, education is a training, through the Holy Spirit, to bring the Christian into a closer fellowship with God the Father by the strengthening of his mind for godly living. Every aspect of man, the spiritual, mental, social, and physical must be educated in the light of God; namely, that all truth is the truth of God and that all reality must be interpreted in the light of His Word.
