[edit] Spivak and Horshkov

Hi Berkut, there is a common misconception following from the dual citizenship issue. Ukraine, or any country for that matter, cannot effectively "not allow" dual citizenship because no country can prohibit other countries from granting citizenship and, at the same time, the conventions severely restrict cases when the country can involuntarily revoke its citizenship from the person. That person's acceptance of the foreign citizenship is not among the allowed reasons to withdraw the citizenship, be it for Ukraine or the US. What the country can do, and not RECOGNIZING the dual citizenship, like in case of Ukraine means just that, is that the country can refuse to treat its dual citizens as foreign nationals despite their having a foreign citizenship as the second one. That is it does not have to notify the foreign consulate in the case of arrest, it can refuse to recognize the foreign conscription as the valid reason of no-show in the national conscription, etc. The country can also refuse to grant its citizenship to the foreign nationals until their renounce their foreign citizenship. However, this does not make a dual citizenship an impossibility. The Ukrainian citizen accepting the foreign citizenship attains dual citizenship de-facto irrespective of the Ukrainian position of the issue. BTW, USA has a similar stance to Ukraine. Canada's and Israel's position on the dual citizenship is different as they extend the recognition of that to their nationals. This has little to do with "allowing" or not "allowing" and under no circumstances makes the D.C. impossible. --Irpen 08:23, 4 July 2006 (UTC)

I found that I was only partially correct (see http://www.opm.gov/extra/investigate/IS-01.pdf). A country may withdraw its citizenship upon voluntarily acquisition of another one. However, this needs to be done (the action is not automatic) and it is rarely done. The basic idea that dual citizenship is possible even with countries that do not recognize it stands. More in the document I linked and a lot more on the web. --Irpen 09:35, 4 July 2006 (UTC)

[edit] Thailand_coa.png

Although you were not the original uploader of this file, please be aware that the file name is inaccurate and is probably in violation of licensing terms. Thailand_coa.png is not Thailand's coat of arms. The real Tra Khrut should have sharp wing tips - the wingtips of Thailand_coa.png‎ are blunt. The real Khrut should have 7 external wing-tips per side - there are 9 wingtips in Thailand_coa.png‎. The real Khrut should be wearing wearing what looks like a high belt which connects to its necklace - the one in Thailand_coa.png‎ is only wearing a necklace. The fingertips of the real Khrut should be pointed directly at the apex of its Monthien (hat) at 45 and 225 degree angles - the one in Thailand_coa.png‎ has fingers that are horizontal. The tail design is distinctly different. The source attributed to Thailand_coa.png is a pay site (vector-images.com), and it seems that the bitmap demo image was copied from the site without requesting or receiving a license. This image should be immediately pulled from Wikipedia. Patiwat 18:27, 5 August 2006 (UTC)
