Bernhard Philberth (born 1927 in Munich, Germany) is an independent physicist, engineer, philosopher and theologian.

Philberth is a member of the Academy of Science of Chieti/Italy, the Academy of Sciences of Besan√ßon/France in which he was the first German to be accepted in 100 years, the Physical Society of Japan in Tokyo and the International Glaciological Society in London.

He is the originator of the project to dispose of radioactive waste deep inside inland icesheets [1], [2] (presented by the French High Commissioner for Nuclear Energy), [3], [4].

In relativity physics Philberth discovered the "Zeitgradient" (FR: "gradient de temps"; EN: "time gradient") [5] (presented by Louis de Broglie), [6] and the relationship between the electromagnetic fundamental values [7].

Together with his brother, Prof. Karl Philberth, he has been granted in excess of one hundred patents for inventions in various areas of physics (inter alia "Philberth-Probe", "Philberth-Transformer") and is the author of numerous technical publications on nuclear energy, energy management and patent law.

Philberth is a promoter of environmental protection and was the initiator of the tenet to protect the environment by taxation laws [8] (submission to the Deutsche Bundestag resulting in change of petrol tax laws) and the originator of taxation of lead in petrol [9] (law passed by the Deutsche Bundestag in 1984).

As firm believers in philanthropy, the Philberth brothers are founders of the "PH-Foundation" in the Philippines, sponsoring the education of youths from disadvantaged families (with over 2,000 graduates to date).
