Berkley (grid reference _region:GB_scale:25000 ST810493 ) is a village and civil parish in the Mendip district of Somerset, England. According to the 2001 census it had a population of 361. The village is north-east of Frome.

[edit] Church

The church, which was erected in 1751, is dedicated to St. Mary, and includes a recently restored organ.[1] It is a grade II* listed building.[2]

Several toombs in the graveyard are listed buildings in their own right;

  • Joseph Singer Chest Tomb, in churchyard 22 metres West of porch. Images of England. Retrieved on 2006-06-28.
  • 267057 Unknown Chest tomb, in churchyard 2 metres North West of porch. Images of England. Retrieved on 2006-06-28.
  • Unknown Chest tomb, in churchyard 21 metres West of porch. Images of England. Retrieved on 2006-06-28.
  • Unknown Chest tomb, in churchyard 27 metres West of porch. Images of England. Retrieved on 2006-06-28.
  • William Hall chest tomb, in churchyard 3 metres North of nave. Images of England. Retrieved on 2006-06-28.

[edit] School

Berkley First School is a small village school catering for 4 to 9 year olds.

[edit] Famous residents

Alexander Barclay, author of "The Ship of Fools," was a native of this village. He died in 1552.
