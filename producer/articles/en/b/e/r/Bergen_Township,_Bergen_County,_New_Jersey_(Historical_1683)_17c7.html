Bergen Township was a Township that existed in the U.S. state of New Jersey, from 1661 to 1862, first as part of Bergen County, and later as part of Hudson County.

[edit] The Lenape and The Netherlands

The name Bergen was originally given to the peninsula between the Hudson River and Hackensack River by the Dutch, possibly from the word for "mountains", which is likely the way the hill at the southern end of the New Jersey Palisades appeared the to the people from the lowlands, or Netherlands, from whence they originated. Alternatively, it is said to have been named for the Norewgian city of Bergen.[1], which in that language also means mountains. It is also the Dutch word for "recover" or "hold", which is what they were trying to do with the establishment of a village at what is now Jersey City's Bergen Square.[citation needed] Previous settlements at Pavonia and Communipaw had been abandoned after a series of raids and reprisals by them and the Lenape, later known as Delaware Indian, who were the first known inhabitants. Bergen was founded in 1660 by settlers who wished to return to the west bank of the Hudson. Its semi-independent government was formed on September 5, 1661, by Peter Stuyvesant, Director-General of New Netherland, as part of his efforts re-gain a foothold on the North River's western shore and expand beyond New Amsterdam on the southern tip of Manhattan, under the condition that a garrison be built. It is the first continuously-occupied permanent European settlement in what would become the state of New Jersey.[2] It became and remained the seat of government for the province until 1709, when the British moved it to Hackensack, which was seen as more centrally located.[3] Several contemporary places still bear the name: Old Bergen Road, Bergen Avenue, Bergenline Avenue, Bergen Point, Bergen Junction, Bergen Hill and Bergen Arches.

In 1664, a negotiated surrender gave control of New Netherlands to the English on and September 22, 1668, the original town charter was confirmed by Philip Carteret, the first English provincial Governor of New Jersey.[4] The Treaty of Westminster formalized the British/Dutch deal in 1674 and the area officially became part of the proprietary colony of East Jersey. In 1675, it was divided in four administrative districts, or counties: Essex, Middlesex, and Monmouth, and Bergen. On March 7, 1683, the garrison at Bergen and surrounding areas at became a town within Bergen County.

[edit] Colonialism

In 1710, when Bergen County, by royal decree of Queen Anne of Great Britain was enlarged to include what had been part Essex County, the village of Hackensack (in the newly formed Township of New Barbadoes) was designated as being more centrally located and more easily reached by the majority of the Bergen Countyâ€™s inhabitants, and hence was chosen as the county seat (as it remains today). Bergen was re-established by Royal Charter on January 4, 1714.[4]

Bergen Township was created by an Act of the New Jersey Legislature on February 21, 1798, as one of the first group of 104 townships formed in New Jersey.[4] Bergen County was thus split into two parts: Bergen Township to the south, and Hackensack Township to the north. As originally constituted, Bergen Township included the area between the Hudson River on the east, the Hackensack River to the west, south to Constable Hook and north to the present-day southern border of Bergen County.

[edit] Subdivision

On January 28, 1820, Jersey City was formed within Bergen Township. In 1838, Jersey City was reincorporated as a city, becoming independent of Bergen Township.[4]

On February 22, 1840, the New Jersey Legislature created Hudson County from southern portions of Bergen County.[4] The new county included the entirety of the original Bergen Township (including Jersey City) and the portions of Lodi Township south of today's Paterson Plank Road.

While part of Hudson County, portions of township were taken to form Van Vorst Township (April 12, 1841, annexed by Jersey City on March 18, 1851), North Bergen Township (April 10, 1843), Bergen town (March 24, 1855, ultimately annexed by Jersey City on May 2, 1870) and Bayonne Township (April 1, 1861).[4]

The remaining portions of the township were absorbed by Bergen town, in a sort of reverse takeover, and the township was dissolved on March 11, 1862.[4]
