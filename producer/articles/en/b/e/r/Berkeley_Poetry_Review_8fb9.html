The Berkeley Poetry Review (BPR) is UC Berkeleyâ€™s longest-running undergraduate poetry journal. The Review has been in publication since 1974 with the mission of promoting the serious consideration of creative writing in the Berkeley community. During the spring semester of each academic year, the staff works to produce a volume of poetry with submissions from students, faculty, staff, and alumni from all departments.

[edit] Poets Featured in the Journal

  • Robert Hass, a Californian poet well-known for the West Coast subject and attitude of his poems.
  • Lyn Hejinian, a poet, essayist, translator, and current poetics professor at the University of California, Berkeley
  • Vladimir Nabokov, the Russian-American poet and novelist, author of the infamous novel Lolita (1955).
  • Pablo Neruda, the Chilean writer and poet, considered one of the greatest and most influential poets of the twentieth century.
  • Lawrence Ferlinghetti, poet and founder of the San Francisco bookstore City Lights, immortalized by its publication of the literary works of beat poets such as Jack Kerouac and Allen Ginsberg.
  • Robert Pinsky, poet and former Poet Laureate of the United States (1997-2000).
  • C.K. Williams, poet and winner of the Pulitzer Prize for Poetry in 2000 for Repair (1999).
  • Billy Collins, poet and former Poet Laureate of the United States (2001-2003).
  • Galway Kinnell, considered one of the most influential poets of the second half of the twentieth century.
  • Thom Gunn, a British poet.
  • John Ashbery, an influential and controversial American poet, winner of the 1975 Pulitzer Prize for Three Poems (1973).
  • Gary Soto, a Californian poet acclaimed for his pieces on the Mexican American experience.
  • Ishmael Reed, a poet and novelist, considered one of the best-known and controversial African-American poets of the twentieth century.
  • Frederico Garcia Lorca, a Spanish poet and dramatist.
  • Josephine Miles, a poet and the first woman to be tenured in the English Department at the University of California, Berkeley.
  • Geoffrey G. O'Brien
  • Mark Levine
  • Brenda Hillman
  • Victor Hernandez Cruz
  • Leonard Nathan
