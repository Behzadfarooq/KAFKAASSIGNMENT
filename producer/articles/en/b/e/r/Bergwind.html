Bergwind

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

You have new messages (last change).

Bergwind (i.e. mountain wind) is the South African name for a Föhn wind, a hot dry katabatic wind blowing from the mountainous interior to the coast. It varies from mild (about 10km/h) to gusts up to 100km/h that cause structural damage.
