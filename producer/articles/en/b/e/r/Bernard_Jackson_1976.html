Bernard Jackson is a former law professor, and since 1997 has been Alliance Professor of Modern Jewish Studies at the University of Manchester, where he is Co-Director of the Centre for Jewish Studies (http://www.mucjs.org). His major academic interests are legal theory, semiotics, and Jewish law. As the university states in its website: Major Publications include: Theft in Early Jewish Law (1972); Essays in Jewish and Comparative Legal History (1975); Semiotics and Legal Theory (1985); Making Sense in Law (1996)....

Jackson was the founding editor of The Jewish Law Annual, 1978-97; and (with others) has published of An Introduction to the History and Sources of Jewish Law (Oxford: Clarendon Press, 1996).

Jackson works with colleagues in the Mishpat Ivri movement yet he criticizes the dominant approach based on legal positivism and has been an active participant in The Jewish Law Association (http://www.mucjs.org/JLAS).

Jackson is a highly prolific author.
