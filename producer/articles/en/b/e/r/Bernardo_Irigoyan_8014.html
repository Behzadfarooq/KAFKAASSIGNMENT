Bernardo Irigoyan

Bernardo Irigoyan (born September 3, 1969) is an Argentine cricketer. He is a right-handed batsman and a right-arm medium-pace bowler who has played for Argentina since 1990. He was born in Buenos Aires.

Irigoyan made his debut in the first match of the 1990 ICC Trophy against Papua New Guinea. Irigoyan scored two ducks from three matches in the competition, though he returned to play in 1994. This time around, Irigoyan played much more impressively, and during this competition, he made the highest batting score of his ICC Trophy career of 43 runs.

His good form was to be repeated in the competition in 1997, as he made the second highest Argentine average of the competition, bettered only by team-mate Matias Paterlini, the two shining lights in the Argentine attack in an otherwise unfulfilling ICC Trophy run. Irigoyan went nine years without representing his country, before being recalled in the Argentine squad for two matches during their winless ICC World Cricket League stint in 2006. Irigoyan has played as a lower-middle order batsman throughout over sixteen years representing his country.
