"Be Prepared" is a song from the 1994 Disney film and 1997 Broadway musical The Lion King. The song was composed by Tim Rice and Elton John and originally performed by Jeremy Irons, Cheech Marin, Jim Cummings, and Whoopi Goldberg in the film and by John Vickery, Kevin Cahoon, Stanley Wayne Mathis and Tracy Nicole in the original Broadway cast for the musical.

The song continued what had become a Disney tradition in their newer movies at that time, where the villain had his or her own song in the movie. This included Professor Ratigan's "The World's Greatest Criminal Mind" in The Great Mouse Detective, Kaa's "Trust in Me" in The Jungle Book, Ursula's "Poor Unfortunate Souls" in The Little Mermaid, Gaston's "The Mob Song" and "Gaston" in Beauty and the Beast, and Jafar's "Prince Ali (Reprise)" in Aladdin.

[edit] The Lion King

Spoiler warning: Plot and/or ending details follow.

Before the song begins, Scar is talking to the hyenas about their failed attempt to kill Simba and Nala. Banzai says "What were we supposed to do, kill Mufasa?" and Scar, with a wicked grin, replies "Precisely!" Scar sings about the lack of intelligence displayed by the hyenas and how, if they assist him in his plan of killing Mufasa and Simba, he can ensure that they are never hungry again. At the start of the song, the tempo is low and gradually increases and intensifies at certain points throughout the song. The song is set is a geothermically unstable area, with earthquakes and geysers slowly intensifying throughout the song.

In the beginning of the second verse, an army of hyenas is shown goose-stepping in front of Scar, who is perched on an overlooking cliff. This is modeled after footage from the Nazi propaganda/documentary Triumph of the Will.

In the song a xylophone is played to a hyena playing the bones of an elephant in the background.

Jeremy Irons, the voice of Scar, sings the bulk of the song, but from the line "You won't get a sniff without me" and onwards the song is sung by Jim Cummings, who voices Ed the hyena. This was due to Irons's vocal chords getting weak.

[edit] Deleted "Be Prepared" Scene

In the early version of Lion King, the Disney animators made a Cut Scene of Be Prepared. This is what the cut scene says before it shows in the Lion King laser disk:

In the final movie, Scar introduces the hyenas immediately following Mufasa's death. In the cut version, he waits until a time he is challenged by the lionesses.

In this scene, the hyenas chase away a certain lioness, who probably said something nasty about Scar. Some people think the lioness is Sarafina, for she isn't shown in the movie after that.

[edit] The Lion King musical

Like the original soundtrack recording, Scar begins the song with a brief soliloquy. The song is similar to its film counterpart in most respects, but there is a dance number performed by the hyena ensemble halfway through. Scar later sings a brief reprise while declaring himself King at Mufasa's funeral.

In part of the song, an actor dressed in a tribal hyena costume performs a dance with a flaming torch.
