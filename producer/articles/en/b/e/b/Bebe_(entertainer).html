Nieves Rebolledo Vila more popularly known as Bebe (May 9, 1978 in Valencia, Spain) is a Spanish singer and actress. She is noted for her fierce personality, feminist lyrics and androgynous looks.

[edit] Biography

She was born in May 9, 1978 as Nieves Rebolledo Vila in Valencia, Spain. Her parents were members of the Extremaduran folk group Surberina. Her breakthrough album in Spain was entitled Pafuera Telarañas though she gained international recoginition after winning the Best New Artist award at the 2005 Latin Grammy Awards. She was nominated for five awards total. The first single from Pafuera Telarañas, "Malo" charted worldwide.

[edit] Break

On 25 June 2006, Bebe announced that for now her album Pafuera Telerañas would be her debut and her farewell because she was retiring from the stage for a while. Bebe said that even if there is pressure to build up a discography, "I am not thinking of making another CD for quite some time. For a little more than two years I've promoted the same CD and I'm still not done." Nonetheless, she planned to continue touring the US and Mexico. "One needs to be reassigned in order to not get suffocated and give themselves time in order to not lose their mind," she stated .[1]

[edit] Acting Career

She has performed in several Spanish films, most recently "Al Sur de Granada".
