[edit] Reverting

Hey man. I noticed you do a good job at reverting valdalism, esp on pages such as Scott Mills. I too try to revert when I can but Ive never found an easy way of giving a detaield revert such as Last version, date, author..etc. I notice some say 'Using popups' or some other meathod. Any advice? Cheers, -Coolmark18 22:35, 7 March 2007 (UTC)

Hey, thanks :) I use Navigation popups, which pop up a preview of the article when you hover over a wikilink, or the changes made to the article when you hover over a diff link. You can then revert to the previous version by using the Actions menu within the popup. After I've reverted I usually check the page's history to make sure there weren't multiple vandal edits before I reverted (otherwise I'll just have reverted to another vandalised version). All you need to do to use popups is add {{subst:navpop}} to the bottom of your monobook.js
Other editors use VandalProof or similar programs, but I've never tried those. — Matt Eason (Talk • Contribs) 10:24, 8 March 2007 (UTC)
