Becky Blasband (born July 8, 1967, in New Hope, Pennsylvania) is a singer and songwriter who gained notoriety as one of the original cast members of television reality show The Real World. She was a housemate during the show's first season, which was set in New York City, and debuted in 1992.

Her father is a psychiatrist and her mother, a German immigrant, ran an antique store in Philadelphia. The family moved to Philadelphia when Blasband was 13. She later enrolled in New York University's film school and was an actress with David Mamet's theater company. She began writing music during her spare time, and after her discovey of Bob Dylan's influenced her own music style. It was at this time when she was discovered by the producers of The Real World.

Blasband went on to the show to help promote her music, which she showcased during that season. She later regretted the performances of hers that were spotlighted on the show, as she thought, "I wasn't very good."

Blasband was involved in two incidents on the show. The first was her romance with the show's director, Bill "Corky" Richmond. During the show, the three female cast members were sent to Jamaica's Hedonism II resort. Blasband and Richmond became close while in Jamaica. This enraged the show's producers; Richmond was subsequently reassigned, and a rule was established that off-camera personnel could not get involved with on-camera members of The Real Worlds production.

The second incident involved a heated argument she had with her African-American roommate, Kevin Powell, about the quality of life in The United States, and racism. Both called each other a racist and the argument was heightened by the national attention of the Rodney King police beating. Blasband said that the intense argument match was completely blown out of proportion and edited to portray her and Powell as deliberately obtuse and ignorant.

After her season ended, she was noticed by Warner Chappell and was given a recording deal. Soon afterward, she released a CD under her full name of Rebecca Blasband entitled Rapt. Blasband has had mixed success as a singer since her Real World stint, having acted on Off-Broadway productions, and, in 2001, having released a hip hop CD titled Million Dollar Movie, which sold modestly.

In a 2001 interview with People magazine, she said that "music is my husband, acting is my mistress", and she described herself as a naughty girl with a heart of gold.

Becky Blasband was signed by Warner Chappell Music Publishing soon after The Real World. She attended Miles Copeland's troubadours songwriting retreat at his castle Marrouatte in the south of France with artists and writers such as Patty Smyth, Cher, Chris Difford, Alannah Myles. After this Blasband was invited to open for the Squeeze and Aimee Mann acoustic tour in the U.S. Blasband was signed to Mercury Records and recorded "Rapt" to high critical reviews from Billboard, radio and press. Becky Blasband opened for such artists as Chris Whitley, Fountains of Wayne, Matchbox Twenty, Jon Bon Jovi. "Rapt" was her only record released to the public so far.

Becky attended New York University Tisch School of the Arts and participated in David Mamet's Practical Aesthetics Workshop at Lincoln Center New York and Vermont, studying with William H. Macy, Steven Shacter and The Atlantic Theater Company.
