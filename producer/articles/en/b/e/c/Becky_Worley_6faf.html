Becky Worley

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Becky Worley was TechLive's Computer Security Analyst. She reported, anchored the news, and hosted a how-to show for TechTV. Worley wrote TechTV's Security Alert: Stories of Real People Protecting Themselves from Identity Theft, Viruses, and Scams (TechTV, November, 2003) (ISBN 0-7357-1352-9). She received a Master of Arts in Education from the Learning, Design, and Technology program at the Stanford University School of Education in June 2005.

In February 2005, Becky Worley helped as host for a new series on the National Geographic Channel called Mother of All, a show in which fine arts and industrial arts are blended together using whatever material is available to make the "Mother of All" things.

Currently, Becky is the tech contributor for Good Morning America on ABC, host and blogger for a web show on Yahoo! Tech, and host of a show for the Travel Channel called The Best Places to Find Cash and Treasures that debuted in December 2006.

She is also a huge Rugby fan, and looks fantastic in a bikini or wetsuit.
