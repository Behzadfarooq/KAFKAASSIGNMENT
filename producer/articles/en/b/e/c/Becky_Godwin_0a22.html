Becky Godwin

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Rebecca 'Becky' Godwin (born 1954 Richmond, Virginia, USA; died 1968 Virginia Beach, Virginia) was the adopted daughter and only child of the then Governor of Virginia Mills E. Godwin, Jr. (1914-1999). In August 1968, Becky Godwin, aged fourteen, was spending a day with her mother at Virginia Beach while her father attended the 1968 Democratic Convention, when she was struck by lightning and killed. She had just stepped out of the surf onto the sand when it happened. Several people were in the vicinity either on the beach or in the surf, but Godwin was the only one affected. It was a clear sunny day with no storms in the area.

[edit] Memorials

There are two scholarships set up in Becky Godwin's name. There is the Becky Godwin Memorial college scholarship fund for the young people of Chuckatuck, Virginia and the Becky Godwin Fund at Virginia Commonwealth University School of Nursing.
