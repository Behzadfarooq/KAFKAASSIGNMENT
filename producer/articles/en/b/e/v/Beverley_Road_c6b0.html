Beverley Road is one of several major roads that run out of the city of Hull in the East Riding of Yorkshire, England. It runs north from Hull city centre and carries the designation of A1079. Upon leaving the city boundaries, Beverley Road continues north towards the town of Beverley, through the villages of Dunswell and Woodmansey. The A1079 follows the route of the Beverley Bypass and leads to the city of York.

Beverley Road is home to a significant proportion of Hull's student community, given its proximity to the University of Hull, and the now-relocated Humberside campus of the University of Lincoln. Its shopping and nightlife is comparable to throughfares in neighbouring cities such as Sheffield's Ecclesall Road and Leeds' Headingley. It has a high number of pubs and other businesses catering for both the student and local communities.

In parts, it is lined with large pre-war housing, but extensive bombing during the Second World War destroyed many such buildings. Hull City Council's new flagship comprehensive school, Endeavour is based on Beverley Road, on the site of Kingston General Hospital.

Many major national and international chains have businesses along the length of Beverley Road including pub chains such as Wetherspoons, Hogshead, and Scream. High street chains, like Tesco, Jackson's at Sainsburys, Subway, and Cooplands.

It is officially known as Beverley High Road, north of Cottingham Road. Whilst within the Hull boundaries, Beverley Road runs through the parliamentary constituencies of Hull East and Hull North.
