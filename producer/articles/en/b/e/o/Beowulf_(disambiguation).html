Beowulf (disambiguation)

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Beowulf is the hero of an Old English epic poem. The name Beowulf may refer to:

  • Beowulf (hero), the main character of the epic poem
  • Beowulf, the name given to the epic poem
  • the Nowell Codex, the manuscript volume containing the epic poem

The name may also refer to two different metal bands from the Los Angeles area:

  • Beowülf (Venice band), a 1980s crossover thrash band based in Venice, California.
  • Beowulf (Los Angeles band), a 1980s heavy metal band based in Los Angeles, California.

The name may also refer to:

  • Beowulf (computing), a high-performance design for clustering computer systems for parallel processing
  • Beowulf (1999 film), a 1999 motion picture starring Christoper Lambert, directed by Graham Baker
  • Beowulf (Dune), a character in the Dune cycle
  • Beowulf & Grendel, a 2006 motion picture starring Gerard Butler, directed by Sturla Gunnarsson
  • Beowulf (2007 film), a 2007 motion picture starring Ray Winstone and Angelina Jolie, directed by Robert Zemeckis
  • Beowulf - The Legend, a 2005 board game designed by Reiner Knizia
  • Beowulf (comics), a 1970s comic book published by DC Comics.
  • Beowulf Shaeffer, a fictional character created by Larry Niven in 1966 and featured in several short stories in his Known Space universe
  • Beowulf, a fictional character from the novel Blart.
  • .50 Beowulf, a high-power round used mainly in modified AR-15 rifles.
  • Beowulf (Honorverse), a fictional planet in the works of David Weber.

There are references to Beowulf in:

  • Devil May Cry 3: Dante's Awakening, in which a beast called Beowulf is a boss character
  • Fire Emblem: Seisen no Keifu, a video game which has a character by that name
  • Final Fantasy Tactics, a game which contains a character named Beowolf
  • Buck Rogers XXVC, a TSR game setting in which characters in an organization may use that nickname
  • Beowulfmon, the Fusion form of Koji Minamoto from Digimon Frontier, is based off of Beowulf
  • Super Robot Taisen Original Generation 2, where Kyosuke Nambu's Shadow Mirror counterpart is known as Beowulf.

It is sometimes misspelled Beowolf.
