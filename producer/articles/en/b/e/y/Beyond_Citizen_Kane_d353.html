Beyond Citizen Kane is an English language documentary made by Simon Hartog in 1993. The documentary details the dominance of the TV Globo media group in Brazilian society, discussing the group's influence, power, and political connections. Globo's President and founder Roberto Marinho came in for particular criticism, being compared with fictional newspaper tycoon Charles Foster Kane in Citizen Kane. Globo, the documentary said, engaged in the same wholesale manipulation of news to influence public opinion as did Kane.

The documentary tracks Globo's involvement with and support of the military dictatorship, its partnership with the Time Warner (at the time Time-Life) group, Marinho's political maneuverings (which included airing a non-live, edited version of a 1989 presidential debate favoring Fernando Collor de Mello) and a controversial deal involving shares of NEC Corporation and government contracts.

The documentary has interviews with noted Brazilians, such as Chico Buarque, Leonel Brizola and Luis Inácio Lula da Silva.

Beyond Citizen Kane was first shown in September 1993, on the United Kingdom's Channel 4. It was subsequently repeated, most recently the day after Marinho's death in 2003. The Marinho family bought the national rights to the documentary, and, by refusing to license it to other broadcasters or release it on video, curtailed its distribution in Brazil. Copies of the documentary are available on the Internet, via peer-to-peer networks, and have been informally distributed by members of Brazilian political parties and between university students. The movie is available in Brazil, though mostly in libraries and private collections.
