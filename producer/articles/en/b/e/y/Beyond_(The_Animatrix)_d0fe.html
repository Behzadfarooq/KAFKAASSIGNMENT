Beyond is a short film that is one of nine Animatrix films. Beyond is directed by Koji Morimoto (of Mind Game and Akira fame).

[edit] Plot synopsis

Beyond deals with a group of kids who stumble across an amalgamation of spatial anomalies within a dilapidated and abandoned building, indicatively somewhere in Japan. The kids learn to exploit the major glitch in the Matrix for their own enjoyment, through several areas which seem to defy real world physics. Glass bottles reassemble after being shattered, broken lightbulbs that flicker briefly (during which they seem perfectly intact), a door which opens to nothingness, and a feather that moves peculiarly through the air. There is a large open space in the middle where they take turns jumping off a high point, freefalling towards the ground yet somehow stopping just inches before impact; this proves amusing and they seem unbothered by the inherent strangeness. The activity seems to be painless, although one boy starts to nosebleed even though he didn't actually hit anything. Soon enough, the agents come to the abandoned building, finding the kids and the main character (older, around 17) inside.

The agents come with a team of people in masks and rubber suits hoping to clean up this "glitch". The story ends when the girl and the kids are outside of the previously haunted house that is now abandoned - they take a look inside and the house is no longer there, converted into a parking lot. The girl spots the floating can, from one of the previous glitches, which is no longer floating. She picks it up and cuts her finger on the opened lid of the can, blood dripping.
