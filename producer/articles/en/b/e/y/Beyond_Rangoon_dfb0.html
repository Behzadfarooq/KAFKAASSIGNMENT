Beyond Rangoon is a 1995 film about Laura Bowman (played by Patricia Arquette), an American tourist who vacations in Burma (Myanmar) in 1988, the year in which the 8888 Uprising takes place. She joins, albeit initially unintentionally, political rallies with university students protesting for democracy, and travels with the student leader U Aung Ko throughout Burma (now known as Myanmar). There, they see the brutality of the military dictators State Law and Order Restoration Council (SLORC), and attempt to escape to Thailand. The film's tagline is "Truth has a witness". The film was mostly filmed in Malaysia, and is based on a true story.

Beyond Rangoon is also an original soundtrack music album that features in the film of Beyond Rangoon. Amost all of the soundtracks were composed by the German composer Hans Zimmer and the album was released in 1999.

[edit] Plot

Laura Bowman (Patricia Arquette)'s son and husband were brutally murdered by a break-in. She is convinced by her sister to travel to Burma to get away. There she discovers the brutal suppression of democracy under General Ne Win, and gets her first glimpse of Aung San Suu Kyi, the embodyment of hope for a peaceful future in Burma. When she loses her passport, she gets detained in Burma. While doing last minute sightseeing she becomes involved with a former professor and various pro-democracy students, monks, and others. Much of the film is involves evading soldiers while attempting to make their way out of the country.
