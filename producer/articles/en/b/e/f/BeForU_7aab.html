BeForU is an all female J-Pop group who performs music primarily for the Bemani series of rhythm games. BeForU debuted in 2000 with their popular song "DIVE, which was notable as being the first J-POP song in the Dance Dance Revolution series. BeForU is produced by Naoki Maeda, one of the lead Konami musical producers. At the present time, they were primarily producing songs in the whole BEMANI series.

"BeForU" is a play on words. For fans of Bemani, a popular song from Dance Dance Revolution 4th Mix was the song "B4U." Both are plays on the words "before you." In another play on words, when BeForU originally formed, they had four members. In 2004, Maehara left the group. BeForU has since added three new members bringing the total to six.

Their first album: "BeForUファーストアルバム" (BeForU First Album) was released on November 28, 2003.
Their first single with Konami: KI・SE・KI was released on September 1, 2004.
Their second album: BeForU II was released on February 14, 2006. Their first single with avex: "Red Rocket Rising" was released on November 1. 2006.

In addition to the songs the group BeForU has done, the members of BeForU have been successful on solo, performing additional popular Bemani songs.

BeForU performed live for the first time in Zepp Tokyo on February 19, 2006. Their performances were recorded and released on DVD later that year, BeForU FIRST LIVE at ZeppTokyo 2006.

At a secret live event on August 6, 2006, BeForU announced that they had been signed to Avex Records. Both Riyu Kosaka and Noria Shiraishi released solo singles on October 11, 大和撫子魂 (Yamato Nadeshiko Damashi), LOVE ②くらっち (LOVE②Clutch), respectively, in addition to Red Rocket Rising.

On December 22, 2006, BeForU released Get set GO!! 〜BeForU Astronauts Set〜, which contained the single Get set GO!!, and Strike Party!!!, was released on January 17, 2007. Strike Party!!! is featured during the ending credits of the 3rd season of the anime "MAJOR".

BeForU released an EP titled "6NOTES" on March 3 that was under their Be+Wings Records label. Each of the 6 songs is a solo by each member.

BeForU's third album, "BeForU III: Breaking Into the Probability Changes" was released on March 14, 2007 BeForU is also expected to return to Zepp Tokyo for BeForU LIVE 2007. BeForU NEXT is to perform their first new song, 'Infinity', at BeForU LIVE 2007. Recording of this song on any CD or game has not yet been announced. In addition to BeForU III, Riyu has announced a solo project called Guilty Sky for the anime CLAYMORE.

[edit] The Original BeForU

Website BeForU Official Site, BeForU Contest Winners

BeForU began as a competition for a new J-Pop group singing for Konami and their video games, namely Dance Dance Revolution. The four winners were chosen for their talent, ablility and emphasis. In the end four girls were chosen. They were Riyu Kosaka at age 15, Noria Shirashi at age 19, Yoma Komatsu at age 26, and Shiyuna Maehara at 21. Their first single DIVE was promoted widely throughout the J-Pop community. DIVE made its first appearance on DDR Extra Stage, and its official debut on DDR 5th Mix.

By DDR -Max- 6th Mix, BeForU had released another hit: Firefly. Riyu also performed two solos, of which were placed in full form in her single true...

By DDR -Max 2- 7th Mix, Shiyuna was the only member of the group that had not received a solo in any BEMANI game. As a group, they released yet another hit called BRE∀K DOWN!, that was later re-arranged for Guitar Freaks and DrumMania. Yoma debuted as a soloist with her song "ever snow", her only solo to be featured in any game to date. Due to their success as an artist, they were able to create an album entitled BeForU First Album-BeForU. Later on, Riyu released her solo album, entitled begin..., which featured covers of many BEMANI songs. In early 2004, Shiyuna left BeForU group to pursue other venues. She was the only BeForU member without any solos.

[edit] The New Beginning - BeForU NEXT

After Shiyuna's depature in 2004, BeForU had another contest for two new members. Somewhere along the line, their was a tie for either first or second place, so the grand total six instead of five. The additions were Miharu Arisawa, Sayaka Minami, and Lisa Sotohana. These girls would form the second generation of BeForU known as BeForU NEXT. (Note that BeForU NEXT refers exclusively to Arisawa, Minami and Sotohana) The new BeForU debuted with their single mini-album entitled KI・SE・KI. The album/dvd included a message from Naoki Maeda, their founder producer and who is also the sound producer of Dance Dance Revolution, and each of the girls.

By 2006, BeForU had created a hit single and album. They also remade most of their previous songs. Their first album was BeForU Second Album-BeForU II. This featured a remix to DIVE entitled DIVE 2006. The went on tour songs from the original BeForU and from BeForU. Later that year, BeForU released and conert DVD entitled BeForU FIRST LIVE at ZeppTokyo 2006. At a secret event in August, they announced they had been signed to Avex Trax. Since then, every BeForU release has been on the avex trax label.
