Beskid (car)

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Beskid 106

Beskid was a series of economy hatchback car prototypes in Poland designed by BOSMAL Automotive Research and Development Center during 1982-1987, but never went into mass production.

The body style was patented, but BOSMAL had no money for the extension of the patent, and it was re-used by French Renault Twingo, released more than 10 years later, when the Polish patent expired.

The name comes from the Beskids mountains.
