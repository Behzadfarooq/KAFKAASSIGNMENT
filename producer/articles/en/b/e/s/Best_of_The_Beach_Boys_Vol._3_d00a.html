Best of The Beach Boys Vol. 3 is the third in a series of hits compilations of music by The Beach Boys released by Capitol Records. It would prove to be the last official "best of" package by the band on their first record label.

Released in August 1968, Best of The Beach Boys Vol. 3 was primarily put together to compensate for the disastrous U.S. sales of The Beach Boys' Friends album. Containing another helping of hits, the album had its good points and bad ones.

This album was the LP debut of the pre-Pet Sounds single "The Little Girl I Once Knew", and contained "She Knows Me Too Well" from The Beach Boys Today!, as well as the passage of time that enabled the inclusions of "Good Vibrations", "Heroes And Villains" and "Darlin' ". However, the appearance of "Frosty The Snowman" from The Beach Boys' Christmas Album is indeed puzzling and out of place. Long out of print, Best of The Beach Boys Vol. 3 limped to an embarrassing #153 in the U.S. before expiring. Once again, the UK fans were more charitable, sending it to #9 there.
