[edit] About Bede RFC

Bede RFC are the men's rugby team for the Durham University college of Hild-Bede (Hild RFC are the women's team). Infamous for becoming banned (all members past, present and future) from Hatfield bar indefinitely for urinating into the tumble dryers of the college. Currently barred from touring to Dubai and Barbados due to outstanding financial debts. Bede RFC participate in the College League, the College Floodlit Cup & the North-East Rugby League. The season runs (during the x and x term) from October to March. Bede RFC are immensely proud to have bucked the public school trend of the teams membership and have welcomed a grammar school member into the team for the last two years. Bede RFC have thrown down the gauntlet to Durham University 3rd XV to play them in both 2006 and 2007, but there challenge has not been taken up.

Team mascot for 2005/06: 'The Real' Saxon Ridley. Team mascot for 2006/07: Doble.

[edit] Bede RFC and the Hill versus Bailey

The Hill versus Bailey rugby match in an annual fixture dating back to 2005/06. It exploits the geographical collegiate divide of those college on the Hill (outside the city) and those on the Bailey (inside the city). There has always been fierce competition and rivalry between the two, fueled by the apparent large public school make up and large state school of the Bailey and Hill college respectively. The match exists to raise money for Steve Cox, a student from St. Chads College who broke his neck in a floodlit Rugby match in 2005.

The all-star teams are made up form selection of players chosen from the respective colleges.
