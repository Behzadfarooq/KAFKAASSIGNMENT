[edit] History

Beacon is one of 57 small city school districts in New York State. Located 65 miles north of NYC along the easterly banks of the Hudson River, the Beacon City School District covers the City of Beacon and portions of Fishkill and Wappingers. The district is composed of four elementary schools, a middle school, a high school, administration building as well as a transportation facility. It has a student enrollment of 3,700 students and a budget of 48.2 million dollars.

[edit] Elementary Schools

Forrestal Elementary School is located at 125 Liberty Street, Beacon, New York 12508. The school has students ranging from Pre-Kindergarten to the 5th Grade. [1]

South Avenue Elementary School is located at 60 South Avenue, Beacon, New York 12508. As with Forrestal Elementary School, it's students range from Pre-Kindergarten to 5th Grade. The school is the largest of all the Elementary Schools in Beacon.

Glenham Elementary School is located at 20 Chase Drive, Glenham, New York 12524. As with all other Elementary Schools in the Beacon City School District, ranges from Pre-Kindergarten to 5th Grade. Beacon City Schools

[edit] Beacon High School

The old Beacon High School was shut down in order to make a proper transaction from the old high school to the New Beacon High School. The school was then turned into an alternative high school for several years. It was school to many children throughout the Dutchess County.

[edit] New Beacon High School

The new Beacon High School was finished in 2000 and was opened in 2001. The school currently hosts 1,050 students. The school has several wings and is made up of mostly democratic students. Only about 5%-10% of the people currently within the school are Republican/Conservative. Circa 2006.
