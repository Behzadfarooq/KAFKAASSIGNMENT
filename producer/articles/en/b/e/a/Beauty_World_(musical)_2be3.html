Beauty World (musical)

Beauty World is a Singapore musical written by Michael Chiang and composed by Dick Lee in 1988. Drawing on the tradition of 1950s black-and-white Cantonese movies, it tells the story of a young Malaysian girl who comes from Batu Pahat to 1960s Singapore in search of her father and winds up in the eponymous sleazy night club.

Written in English with some Singlish elements and Singaporean slang, it is one of the best known musicals of Singapore. With Makan Place, which was also written in 1988, it is one of the first two Singaporean musical.

The cast of the first production included Jacintha Abishegam, Margaret Chan, Ivan Heng, Tan Kheng Hua, and Claire Wong.

Beauty World toured Japan in 1994. An amateur production took place on April 15th 2006 at the University of Chicago. Dubbed Return to Beauty World it was directed by Andy Tan a fourth year economics major in the college.
