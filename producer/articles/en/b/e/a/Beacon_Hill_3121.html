Beacon Hill

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Beacon Hill is a name shared by many hills, suburbs, villages and other places around the world. Many are so called because they were historically the site of a warning beacon. Others are named after other places of the same name.

[edit] In the United Kingdom

The United Kingdom has many Beacon Hills. Most of them take their name from their use as sites for warning beacons as part of Britain's early warning system in Elizabethan times, when they were lit to warn of the coming of the Spanish Armada.

Here are a few of them:

  • Beacon Hill, Buckinghamshire is a village in Buckinghamshire, England.
  • Beacon Hill, Burghclere, Hampshire is one of the best known hill forts in the United Kingdom.
  • Beacon Hill, Warnford, Hampshire is another hill in Hampshire.
  • Beacon Hill, Kent is a village in Kent, England.
  • Beacon Hill, Leicestershire is a popular country park in Leicestershire, England.
  • Beacon Hill, Norfolk is the highest point in the county of Norfolk.
    • Beacon Hill, Colkirk, Norfolk is a high point in Mid Norfolk.
  • Beacon Hill, Powys is a hill near Knighton near the Welsh-English border.
  • Beacon Hill, Torquay is the highest point in the unitary authority of Torbay.
  • Beacon Hill School (England) is one of three schools in the UK with the name Beacon Hill.
  • Barr Beacon ("Barr" means "hill")
  • Beacon Fell or Penrith Beacon is a hill that overlooks the town of Penrith, Cumbria
  • Beacon Hill, Surrey
  • Beacon Hill, Wollaston, Northamptonshire
  • Beacon Hill, Morpeth, Northumberland
  • Beacon Hill, Dorset

See also other places named "Beacon" in the UK. These include Dunkery Beacon, Inkpen Beacon, Ivinghoe Beacon and Worcestershire Beacon.
