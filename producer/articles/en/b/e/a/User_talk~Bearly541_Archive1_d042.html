Admin Coaching needs coaches!!! If you are an administrator, or even a generally experienced user, do consider signing up to be a coach.

Admin Coaching, now being coordinated by HighwayCello, is a program for people who want help learning some of the more subtle aspects of Wikipedia policy and culture. People are matched with experienced users who are willing to offer coaching. The program is designed for people who have figured out the basics of editing articles; they're not newcomers any more, but they might want some help in learning new roles. In this way, Esperanza would help keep hope alive for Wikipedia because we would always be grooming the next generation of admins.
What's New?
The Tutorial Drive is a new Esperanza program! In an effort to make complicated processes on Wikipedia easier for everyone, Esperanza working to create and compile a list of tutorials about processes here on Wikipedia. Consider writing one!
A discussion on how Esperanza relates to the encyclopedia has been started; please add your thoughts.
Many thanks to MiszaBot, courtesy of Misza13, for delivering the newsletter.

Re: Two questions

One: No, unfortunately I've not made it to Egypt yet. I'm hoping to go when I finish school. I'm actually more interested in the lesser-known tombs of the Valley of the Kings, and places such as Amarna and Abydos than the pyramids: amazing, yes, but too hyped up and tourist-ized for my liking.

Two: Userboxes don't need to be formally approved, but now have to be made in userspace (any made outside of userspace are now deleted -- trust me, I know. Six of mine were deleted without warning!). There's no reason why it shouldn't be approved as long as you follow the guidelines: no political anything, nothing that attacks anyone or any race/society, etc...

Some basic rules:

  1. No politics
  3. No attacks
  4. No addition of useless categories
  5. Follow naming rules
    1. All boxes must start with "user ... "
    2. Lowercase is suggested
  6. Make it readable (black background and brown text don't show up well)

My suggested steps:

  1. do extensive searches to make sure it isn't already created
    1. look through the Userbox Category pages
    3. Search on Wikipedia
    4. And Google
  2. title the page something like User:Bearly541/user example
  3. copy the construction code you wish to use and paste it in your page
  4. edit colours, text, etc. to your liking
    1. ALWAYS PREVIEW, never assume it's right. There's nothing more annoying than 100 edits that change one little apostrophe and one number...
  5. save
  6. ask someone for their opinion on whether or not it should be added to the main lists of userboxes
  7. Decide where the most logical and useful categories for it to be located are

If you'd like I'll look at them for you, once you've finished, and let you know if they should be advertised on the main Userboxes pages. Good luck! — Editor at Large(speak) 11:33, 7 November 2006 (UTC)

about Pikachu

Hi there, unfortunately my knowledge of the Japanese language is very limited, and I read it better than I speak it. According to the article about Pikachu, the name is made up of two examples of onomatopeia or sound effects. One of them, "pika", is apparently how people in Japan describe the sound of a spark, much as we might say Bzz or Sizzle. The other part, "chu", is how the Japanese describe the squeak of a mouse. I hope this helps. --Kyoko 20:55, 15 November 2006 (UTC)
