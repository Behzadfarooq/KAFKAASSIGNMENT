Beaver Dam High School is a secondary school serving grades nine through twelve. It is part of the Littlefield Unified School District.

Beaver Dam High School maintains a population of nearly 130.

[edit] History

Virtually a landmark in LUSD, Beaver Dam High School was first brought to the table at a Governing Board Meeting in 2001.

The school first opened it's doors for the 2004-2005 school year. The Governing Board members at the time were Tammy Giebink, Cheryl Graham and Shannon Hartley. The Superintendent was Dr. Jose L. Trujillo.

The first year it housed only grades nine through eleven. The seniors attended high school in neighboring Mesquite, Nevada, ten miles southwest to Virgin Valley High School. The following school year, 2005-2006, the school was housing all four grades, 9-12.
