Beau Jo's is the name of a Pizza Restaurant founded in Idaho Springs, Colorado in 1973[1] Despite only having six locations, it is well known[citation needed] throughout Colorado for its large pizza pies (called Mountain Pies) measured by the pound instead of by the inch. Beau Jo's is somewhat of an institution in Colorado, especially among those with an outdoor lifestyle and is often an obligatory stop after skiing, mountain climbing, mountain biking and white water rafting.

[edit] History

According to the Beau Jo's website, the "legend" begins with one Pete ZaPigh, a French trapper who carved his secret Pizza recipe on the shell of a box turtle. Unfortunately, he was eventually stepped on by a woolly mammoth and killed. This turtle (named Beau Jo) was later found by Chip Bair who started the first Beau Jo's Pizza in Idaho Springs.

[edit] Menu

The menu consists of two kinds of pizzas - Mountain Pies and Prairie Pies. The Prairie Pies are your standard thin crust pizza with normal amounts of ingredients. The Mountain Pie is Beau Jo's claim to fame, with copious amounts of ingredients and a thick crust "rolled over" to keep all the ingredients from falling off. There is a bottle of honey on each table so that after the pizza is eaten, the consumer can consume the remaining crust as a dessert. Beau Jo's also serves pasta, calzones, and sandwiches, although it is the "high altitude" pizza which most people come to Beau Jo's for.

[edit] Locations

In addition to the flagship location in Idaho Springs, there are 5 more locations scattered throughout the Front Range, mostly in the Denver area, but also one in Fort Collins. The Front Range locations have been less successful than the original location in Idaho Springs.

There is also a Beau Jo's Mountain Bistro with locations in Steamboat Springs and Durango which has a somewhat similar pizza, but with a more extensive menu.
