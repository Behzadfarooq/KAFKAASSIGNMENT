Edward Michael Bear Grylls (born 1974), son of conservative politician Michael Grylls is a best-selling author, mountaineer, TV presenter, adventurer, and international motivational speaker. Grylls made his name when, at the age of 23, he became the youngest Briton to climb Mount Everest and return alive[citation needed]. He holds the honorary rank of Lieutenant Commander in the British Royal Naval Reserve.[1][2]

He is currently the host of the television program Man Vs Wild a Discovery Channel survival show, re-titled Born Survivor: Bear Grylls for Channel Four in the United Kingdom. He was formerly the host of the television show, Escape to the Legion which aired on the military channel.

[edit] Timeline

  • 1974 - Born the son of Sally & Michael Grylls and younger brother of Lara Grylls.
  • 1995 - Served for three years with 21st Special Air Service Regiment, a Territorial Army special forces unit of the British Army. During this time he broke his back in three places, while parachuting over southern Africa.
  • 1997 - Became the youngest British person to summit Ama Dablam in the Himalayas, a peak once described by Sir Edmund Hillary as 'unclimbable'.
  • 1998 - On May 26, became the youngest person ever to summit Mount Everest and return alive. His book on this expedition, titled Facing Up, reached number nine in the best seller list.
  • 2000 - He married Shara in January and later that year led the first team to circumnavigate the UK on personal watercraft, in aid of the Royal National Lifeboat Institution (RNLI) Lifeboats.
  • 2003 - Led team across North Atlantic in a rigid inflatable boat (RIB). The book on this world record is titled: Facing the Frozen Ocean. It was shortlisted as 'Sports Book of the Year.' Along with Dame Ellen MacArthur, he was awarded an honorary commission in the Royal Navy in recognition of this achievement. Their first son Jesse was born in June.
  • 2005 - Led the first team ever to attempt to paramotor over the remote jungle plateaux of the Angel Falls, Venezuela, in aid of the charity "Hope & Homes for Children"[1]. The team was attempting to reach the highest, most remote high tepuis, made famous by Conan Doyle's Lost World.
  • 2005 - Grylls broke the world record for the highest ever open air dinner party [2], slung under a hot air balloon at 25,000 ft. Details of this adventure, in celebration of 50 years of The Duke of Edinburgh charity Award scheme.
  • 2005 - During July, Channel 4 aired Escape to the Legion, a four-part documentary series following Grylls & 11 volunteers over the course of a month in North Africa as they endured a course based on the basic training of the French Foreign Legion to find out "what kind of men [they] really [were]." The show was co-produced and presented by Grylls.
  • 2006 - The show Man vs. Wild premiered in November, hosted by Grylls, which features him demonstrating survival techniques in hostile environments. Bear & Shara's second son, Marmaduke was born in April.
  • 2007: Channel Four in the UK air their 8-part TV series titled: 'Born Survivor: Bear Grylls'. A book by the same title was published in the Spring to accompany the series, featuring survival skills learned from some of the world's most hostile places.

[edit] Current life

Grylls demonstrates escaping from quicksand.

Grylls owns an island on the Welsh coast, which includes a nature reserve, and lives on a converted barge on the River Thames with his wife Shara and their sons Jesse and Marmaduke. He works as a motivational speaker, and is currently filming a TV series known in the US on Discovery Channel called Man vs. Wild, and re-titled Born Survivor: Bear Grylls for Channel Four in the United Kingdom. The series features Bear being dropped into some of the most inhospitable places on earth and showing viewers how to survive. His current book, "Man Vs Wild: Survival Techniques from the most Hostile Places on Earth", will be available early 2007.

In addition to Man vs. Wild, Grylls filmed a four-part documentary in 2005 called Escape to the Legion which followed Grylls and 11 other British recruits in the French Foreign Legion as they endured the month-long basic desert training in Morocco. The show was broadcast in the UK on Channel 4.[3] and in the US on the Military Channel.[4]
