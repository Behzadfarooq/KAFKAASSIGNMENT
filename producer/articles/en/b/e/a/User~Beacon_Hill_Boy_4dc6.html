[edit] My Biography

Hi, my name is Beacon Hill Boy. I'm 19 years old and I like to play a lot of sports like basketball, baseball, and football. I graduated from West Seattle High School and I'm attending University of Washington (Go Huskies!). My favorite Husky player is Adrian Oliver. I also like to do some stage work at local theatres doing sound effects and other stuff. I first started doing stage work in 1999 at Franklin High School when I got to be in play with Alvin "AJ" Snow (the best basketball player at Franklin High School) and it was funny because he wasn't in the acting class, they had to recuit him to be in the play (and yes, they gave him a big part in the play). AJ went on to play for Eastern Washington Eagles and led them to their first NCAA Divison I Basketball Tournament in school history. In my free time I watch movies (Donny Darko is the best movie I have seen so far), play video games (best video game so far hands down is The Elder Scrolls: Oblivion with The Elder Scrolls: Bloodmoon coming in with a close second), play ping pong, and play fooseball. I created pages for Rob Brown (loved him in Coach Carter), Lee Thompson Young (loved him in Friday Night Lights), Sam Jones III (loved him in Glory Road and is the son of basketball great Sam Jones), and TJ Cumming (son of one of my favorite basketball players Terry Cummings). My favorite teams are the Seattle Supersonics, Seattle Seahawks, Miami Heat, Seattle Storm, and Los Angeles Avengers. My favorite players are Ray Allen, Rashard Lewis, Dwyane Wade, Shaquille O'Neal, and "The Underrated Talent" Dontell Jefferson (not many people has seen him play but if you do, he'll blow you off your feet). My favorite reality show is Big Brother (season 3 was the best). My favorite rappers are Chingy and Jibbs and my favorite songs are Holidae Inn, I Don't Like the Look of It by Da BackWudz, and Walk It Out by Unk. Check out the movie I'm working on Pinhead vs. Chucky and feel free to edit the page. I would like to give a special shout out to my friends at maxpreps.com for making such a great website.
