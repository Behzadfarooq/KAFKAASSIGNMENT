Beall High School

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Beall High School (pronounced like the word "bell") is a public high school located in Frostburg, Maryland. It has a population of over 600 students from the cities of Frostburg, Mt. Savage, Eckhart, and Finzel.

The school's mascot is the Mountaineer and the school colors are blue and gold.

[edit] History

The school was was established in the 1930s and since then has consolidated with Mount Savage High. In late 2007, it will be torn down and will consolidate with Westmar High School to form Mountain Ridge High School which is currently under construction on the former Beall High Stadium.

[edit] Newspaper

The Chime is Beall's newspaper. The staff are the members of Mrs. Shaw's Journalism class. Mrs. Francis Ann Shaw is the advisor of the newspaper staff.

[edit] Yearbook

Beall's yearbook is the Mountaineer which is created and organized by the staff which are the members of Mrs. Marchbank's Journalism class. Mrs. Stephanie Marchbank is the advisor of the yearbook staff.
