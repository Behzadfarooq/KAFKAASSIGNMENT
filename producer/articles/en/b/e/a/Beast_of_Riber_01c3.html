The Beast of Riber is a wild animal that may live in the Derbyshire Dales around the hamlet of Riber near Matlock.

Some believe it to be a phantom wild cat, like the Beast of Bodmin, whilst other contest that it is simply a fox or other similar creature. Some suggest that the animal may have escaped from the Wildlife Park which used to exist at Riber Castle. It could also be one of several non-native big cats which are thought to be living wild in the United Kingdom, having being released after the introduction of the Dangerous Wild Animals Act in 1976, which forced owners of exotic pets to license premises where such animals are kept.
