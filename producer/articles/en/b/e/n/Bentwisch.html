Bentwisch

Bentwisch is village in Germany to the east of Rostock. The village has 2.455 inhabitants and covers 14.74 square kilometres. Due to its proximity to Rostock, the B 105, and the A 19, Bentwisch became a place of industry after German reunification.

Bentwisch has a substation for 220kV/110kV and the static inverter plant of the HVDC Kontek. Both facilities are a few kilometres away from each other. In 2002 the static inverter plant Bentwisch was enlarged by a substion for 380kV/110kV and between the static invertor plant and the 220kV/110kV-substation, which was built in difference to the static invertor plant at times, East Germany existed, a 110kV powerline for 3 phase AC current was built.
