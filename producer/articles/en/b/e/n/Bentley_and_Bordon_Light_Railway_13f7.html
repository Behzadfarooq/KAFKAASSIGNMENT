Bentley and Bordon Light Railway

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

The Bentley and Bordon Light Railway was constructed in 1905 under an 1896 Light Railways Act.

It ran from the London and South Western Railway station at Bentley, Hampshire to the village of Bordon to serve the Bordon and Longmoor Military Camp. The line was built with the help of the War Department to ease troop movements and bring stores to Bordon Camp.

The route of the line was:

  • Bentley - junction with the LSWR mainline
  • Kingsley - built three months after the line opened, the station and goods yard was to serve the expected residential development in the village
  • Oakhanger Halt - built as the main station for Bordon Garrison
  • Bordon - the southern terminus, and inter change junction with the Longmoor Military Railway

In 1905, the same year the Bordon branch opened the War Department decided to build its own standard gauge line from Longmoor to Borden where there was to be an exchange siding with the LSWR. The Woolmer Instructional Railway (later renamed The Longmoor Military Railway in 1935) was extended south to Liss in 1933. Although built as a passenger line the majority of traffic on the Bordon branch was always military and it was heavily used in both world wars.

After World War II passenger traffic dropped off quickly and the line was closed to passengers in 1957, remaining open to serve military purposes in times of emergency. The line closed to goods traffic in 1966, with the track lifted later the same year. None of the stations now survive except Bentley, while the Bordon station is now buried under an industrial trading estate.

A bus service now provides a link from Bentley to Bordon, and onwards to Liss - effectively replicating the line and the LMR. It has been mooted that as this bus service is now so well utilised, that it should be replaced by a railway link.
