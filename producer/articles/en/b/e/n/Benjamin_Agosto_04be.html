Benjamin Agosto (born January 15, 1982 in Chicago, Illinois) is an American ice dancer. With ice dancing partner Tanith Belbin, Agosto is the 2006 Olympic silver medalist, three time Four Continents Champion (2004-2006), and four time US National Champion (2004-2007).

[edit] Biography

Ben Agosto was born to a Puerto Rican father and Jewish mother and raised in Northbrook, Illinois (a suburb of Chicago) and started skating at age six. In 1999, he was partnered with Tanith Belbin and the team immediately saw results on the junior level. They won the bronze medal at the World Junior Figure Skating Championships their first year together. Belbin and Agosto qualified for the 2002 Winter Olympics with a second place finish at the United States Figure Skating Championships, but were ineligible to compete there because Belbin was still a Canadian citizen. Instead, Belbin and Agosto went to the 2002 World Junior Figure Skating Championships and won. In 2004, Belbin and Agosto won U.S. Championship and have repeated twice. At Nationals in 2005, the last year of the 6.0 system, Belbin & Agosto won straight sixes for presentation in their free dance. Of the 30 6.0s given out in ice dance at US Nationals, Belbin & Agosto have 14 of them. Their 6.0 count is second only to Michelle Kwan's career 38 at nationals. Though they earned silver medals for the United States at the 2005 World Figure Skating Championships, Belbin and Agosto were originally ineligible to compete at the 2006 Winter Olympics as Belbin was not yet an American citizen.

However, by a special act of Congress passed on December 28, 2005, which President Bush signed on New Year's Eve, 2005, Belbin became a naturalized citizen of the USA, making her able to compete for the United States at the 2006 Winter Olympics.

Belbin and Agosto went on to win the Olympic silver medal on ice dance on February 20, 2006.

Agosto attended Glenbrook North High School for two years before moving to Michigan where he graduated with honors from Groves High School in June 2000. Agosto played in a high school jazz band and hopes to become a blues player someday. He is also an ice skating coach.

Benjamin Agosto trains in Canton, Michigan. He previously competed with Katie Hill at the novice and junior levels.
