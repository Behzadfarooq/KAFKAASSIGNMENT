Benjamin Mark Sternick

Spc. Benjamin Sternick, 3rd BCT [Center]. Source: U.S. Army Photo by Spc. Ryan Stroud, 3rd Brigade Combat Team, 1st Cavalry Division Public Affairs
Born: October 8, 1983
Greenville, South Carolina
Occupation: author, soldier
Influences: J.R.R. Tolkien, Robert Frost

Benjamin Mark Sternick (1983-present) is an author and poet known for his work in both the fiction and non-fiction genres. He is most reknowned for his poem, Journey's End and an award-winning essay entitled They Served with Pride: A Brief History of African-American Servicemen.
