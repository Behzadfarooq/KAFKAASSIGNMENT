Ben Campopiano was born on October 6, 1979 in Walnut Creek, California. He grew up in Walnut Creek and attended Walnut Acres Elementary School, Foothill Middle School, and Northgate High School. At Northgate High, Campopiano lettered in soccer, football, and baseball, playing two years at the varsity level in each sport. He also was involved in the student government and conflict managment programs. After graduating high school in 1998, Campopiano attended California Polytechnic University, San Luis Obispo, where he earned Dean’s List honors. He then transferred to Diablo Valley College in Pleasant Hill, California before enrolling at Saint Mary's College of California in 2001. Campopiano earned a Bachelors Degree in Communications from Saint Mary's in 2003 and was a Dean’s List member during his senior year. While attending college, Campopiano was a high school football, baseball, and soccer coach at his alma mater, Northgate High School.

After college, Campopiano moved to Washington, DC and interned on Capital Hill at the Democratic Policy Committee. He worked closely with the policy advisors handling foreign affairs, environmental issues, and budgetary concerns. During the 2004 presidential election campaign, Campopiano served as a Field Manager for the Democratic National Committee in Washington, DC. His duties with the DNC included fundraising, marketing, and registering new voters.

In January of 2005 he moved to Boston, Massachusetts and worked in the public relations and marketing industry. As the Media Relations Director for The Ictus Initiative, Campopiano promoted clients and products to media outlets, organizations, and businesses around the country. He also worked closely with clients to improve their their marketability and further their popularity.

After spending more than a year in the marketing and PR world, Campopiano decided to return to the political field, accepting a job as a Campaign Director for Grassroots Campaigns, Inc. Campopiano directed the San Francisco office that ran a canvass operation for the Democratic National Committee. Directing a team of several dozen canvassers and campaign directors, Campopiano helped raise tens thousands of dollars from small donors and sign up thousands of new members for the party.

After leading the San Francisco office for three months, Campopiano switched projects for the final two months of the 2006 midterm campaign and began working with MoveOn.Org and their Call For Change Campaign. Call For Change, a project of MoveOn's Operation Democracy, is anationwide effort to get voters to the polls in the 50 most competitive Congressional races across the country. Campopiano helped recruit, train, and organize hundreds of MoveOn members during his time on this project.
