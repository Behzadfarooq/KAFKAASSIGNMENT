The Benedicta Arts Center (BAC) is a performing arts center located on the campus of the College of Saint Benedict. Built in 1964 by the nuns of the Order of Saint Benedict in the city of St. Joseph, MN, the Benedicta Arts Center has remained a powerhouse for the arts in central minnesota.

The BAC presents three annual performances by the Minnesota Orchestra and has recently commissioned works by Diavolo and Merce Cunningham Dance Company

The BAC houses the Theater Department, and half of the music and art departments for the The College of St. Benedict/St. Johns University. The building also houses 2 art galleries, a music library and 6 class rooms.


[edit] Petters Audiotorium

Petters Auditorium functions as both a concert hall as well as a 1000 seat theater. The back wall of the theater can be raised to reveal a traditional theatrical stage house which is used for dance companies and concerts. The auditorium has 2 balcony levels above the main floor that seat 997 audience members. The main stage is eqiped with a pair of hydrolic lifts that can also be lowerd to house level, allowing space for either an orchestra or additional audience memebers.


[edit] Gorecki Family Theater

The Gorecki Theater functions mainly as the space for the CSB/SJU theater department. It seats 400 patrons and, thanks to a hydrolic elevator and seating system, is capable of featuring both Proscenium and Thrust performances.
