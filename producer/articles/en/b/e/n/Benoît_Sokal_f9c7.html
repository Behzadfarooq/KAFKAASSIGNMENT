Benoît Sokal (born June 28, 1954) is a Belgian comic artist and video game developer, best known for his comic series Canardo.

[edit] Biography

Benoît Sokal was born in Brussels in 1954.[1] He studied at the École Supérieure des Arts Saint-Luc in Brussels, together with many contemporary Belgian comic artists like François Schuiten. He began drawing for À Suivre in 1978. He created Inspector Canardo, a depressed anthropomorphic duck detective with a penchant for cigarettes, alcohol and femmes fatales, before working on other titles.

Later he joined the software developer Microids and designed the adventure games Amerzone, Syberia and Syberia 2. He then founded his own game company called White Birds Productions, where he created the adventure game Paradise and published it at Ubisoft.
