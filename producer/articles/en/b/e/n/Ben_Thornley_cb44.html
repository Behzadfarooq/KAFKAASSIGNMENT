Benjamin Thornley (born April 21, 1975) in Bury, England, is a footballer who currently plays for Salford City in the North West Counties Football League Division One.

He is without doubt most widely known for his career as a youth and reserve team player at Manchester United, where he played the position right-wing from 1992 to 1998, but he has also served in the position of forward, and with several other teams. At one point in time, he seriously injured his knee.

Thornley then went on to play 127 games for Huddersfield Town, and scored eight goals. Then Stockport County and Aberdeen.

Thornley then joined the non-league scene with Bacup Borough where he was joined by former Manchester United F.C. team-mate David May. Ben spent a season with Bacup Borough before joining Salford City F.C. at the beginning of 2006.

Thornley has a younger brother, named Rod, who currently plays for Altrincham in the Nationwide Conference.
