Benito is a village in the Canadian province of Manitoba located in the Rural Municipality of Swan River. The village is situated in the Swan River Valley, 37 kilometres southwest of Swan River, 475 kilometres northwest of Winnipeg 2kilometres east of the Saskatchewan border. To the south is Duck Mountain Provincial Park, to the north is Thunder Hill and futhur north of Swan River is Porcupine Mountain Provincial Park. The village is served by highway 83.

People from Eastern Canada, England, and Europe first settled Benito around the turn of the century. In 1905, the Canadian National Railway went through and the hamlet of Benito slowly grew and was incorporated as a village in 1941.

The economic base of the region is agriculture and logging.

[edit] Famous Benito Residents

  • Ed Werenich, World Champion Curler, known as "The Wrench"
