The Bennett S. LeBow College of Business, also called LeBow College of Business, is one of the primary colleges of Drexel University. The College is primarily housed in the Matheson Building as well as holds classes in the Pearlstein Business Learning Center. The College was formerly called the College of Business and Administration until 1999 when the program was named after alumni Bennett S. LeBow after a contribution of $10 Million dollars to Drexel in "the largest by an individual donor in the University`s 108-year history." [1]

The College offers degree programs for undergraduates, graduate, postgraduate, and executives. According to Business Week the LeBow College of Business is among the top 60 in the nation, and top 30 among private institutions in the nation.[2]
