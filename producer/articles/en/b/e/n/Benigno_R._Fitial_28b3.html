Benigno Repeki Fitial (born November 27, 1945) is the Governor of Northern Mariana Islands, elected on November 6, 2005. He was previously the Speaker of the House of Representatives of the 24th Territorial Legislature. He is a member of the Covenant Party. He is a resident and former representative of district 3, which includes parts of Saipan and the northern islands.

Fitial is the sixth elected Governor of the United States Commonwealth of the Northern Mariana Islands having been sworn in during 2006. He defeated incumbent Republican Governor Juan N. Babauta during the 2005 gubarnatorial election with 28.1% of the total vote. The 2005 gubernatorial election was the closest in the history of the CNMI. It’s Now Governor Fitial

Fitial is the first elected Northern Mariana governor of Carolinian decent. [1] The Carolinians are a minority group whose ancestors immigrated to the CNMI in the late 1800s and early 1900s, from the islands of Yap and Chuuk in what is now the Federated States of Micronesia. The majority indigenous group on the CNMI, as well as neighboring Guam, are the Chamorros. Chamorro candidates have usually won the governor elections in the past.

Fitial faces many challenges during his term as governor including budget shortfalls, a weak economy and declining numbers of tourists from Japan. He implemented a dress code with business attire for all of his Cabinet members shortly after his inauguration. [2]

Many observers and prominent publications (such as the Marianas Variety) have accused Fitial of concentrating power in his office and making decisions autocratically [3]. This includes a decision made to abolish the autonomy of at least two government agencies, with their functions being transferred to the executive [4]. The governor and his supporters have asserted that drastic measures need to be taken to cut excessive government spending during the tenure of the previous governor, Juan Babauta. However, according to tradition, the governor and lieutenant governor were issued new Nissan Pathfinder SEs worth $30,000 each [5].

Fitial describes himself as a "good friend" of convicted US lobbyist Jack Abramoff [6].

Fitial graduated with honors from Mt. Carmel High School in 1964. He also holds a Bachelor of Business Administration with an emphasis on business management from the University of Guam. According to his official website he is recognized as an University of Guam "Distinguished Alumni" [7].

He is married to his wife, Josie Fitial. The couple have six children and four grandchildren.
