Operating Theory: Wikipedia maintains a bias against renewable energy, in favor of nuclear energy.

[edit] Bias Categories:

[edit] Submarine attacks

In places, articles go out of their way to denigrate renewable energy - often going into more detail about the faults of renewable energy than into the advertised subject matter.

  • Nuclear energy speculates on the cost of wind power but fails to disclose the cost of nuclear accidents like Chernobyl.
  1. ... Wind power was calculated to be more than twice as expensive as nuclear power. [1]


[edit] Double standards for sources

I have found examples in which government studies of comparable merit have been treated differently when their differences are largely limited to whether or not they support the bias.

  • Two speculative reports - one published - one censored.
  1. Nuclear Power ...For example, studies in Britain have shown that increasing wind power production contribution to 20% of all energy production, without costly pumped hydro or electrolysis/fuel cell storage, would only reduce coal or nuclear power plant capacity by 6.7% [2].


(from an Arbitration on this subject) Comments by Arbitrators:

  1. You are not wrong regarding wind power, but there is not basis for a finding that Wikipedia is biased against it. It is almost impossible to include information about an event which has not occurred and is even relatively unlikely (a major nuclear accident in the West) in an article. We are a compilation of known information. Fred Bauder 13:48, 9 January 2006 (UTC)


[edit] Presumption in favor of status quo

When Government reports support the bias, they have been published as fact - and where government studies call into question the safety of nuclear, they have been censored.
