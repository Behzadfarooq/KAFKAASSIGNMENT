The Bennett family is a middle class family living in the fictional town of Harmony on NBC's daytime drama Passions. The Bennetts have been on the ruthless Alistair Crane's bad side for years, but they earned Alistair's full wrath in the summer of 2005 when Alistair's granddaughter, Fancy, complained to her grandfather about Sam's son, Noah, infuriating the Crane patriarch, Sam was fired from his position as chief of police.

[edit] Family Members

[edit] First Generation

  • Sam Bennett is the Chief of Police in Harmony, and one of Alistair Crane's biggest enemies. Unaware that his marriage to Grace is not invalid, after all, he was engaged to first love Ivy Winthrop but after discovering her role in the destruction of his marriage to Grace, Sam has called off the engagement. Sam is the father of four children: Ethan, Noah, Kay, and Jessica.
  • Hank Bennett is the much younger brother of Sam. Hank was wanted by the FBI under the alias of Jack Gallagher for unknown reasons. His ties to the French drug cartel eventually led him to nearly kill his then-girlfriend, heiress Sheridan Crane. This at first led to a deep rift between Sam and Hank, but the two brothers eventually patched things up. Hank disappeared from the canvass in 2004, and has not been seen or mentioned since.
  • Grace Standish Bennett was the wife of Sam Bennett. She believed that her marriage to Sam is invalid after discovering that she was married to David Hastings before developing amnesia; she is unaware that David was hired by Ivy to break up her marriage. Grace was furious to learn, in 2006, that David and Ivy had deceived her. Grace has three children: Noah, Kay and Jessica.
  • Ivy Winthrop was the fianc√©e of Sam Bennett. The two were once lovers until Sam went away to work on the fishing boats and Ivy chose to marry new lover Julian Crane, not realising that her father had conspired with Alistair Crane to force Julian to court her. Ivy, desperate to get Sam back, blackmailed photographer David Hastings to pose as Grace's husband in order to break the two up, and her plan succeeded. Ivy was married from Julian Crane (1974 to 2001; divorced), a marriage that ended once Julian discovered that their son, Ethan, was really the result of Ivy's affair with Sam on their wedding night. Ivy has four children: Ethan Winthrop (with Sam), Fancy Crane, Pretty Crane and her youngest child, Nicholas Foxworth Crane (all three with Julian).

[edit] Second Generation

  • Ethan Winthrop is the son of Sam Bennett and Ivy Winthrop, who was conceived before Ivy married Julian Crane. Ethan grew up believing himself to be the eldest son of Julian Crane, and, therefore, heir apparent to the Crane Empire. He was devastated to learn that he was really a Bennett, but soon grew to care deeply for his newfound family. An attorney, Ethan often represents most of Harmony's residents when they come across legal troubles. Ethan was the high school sweetheart of Gwen Hotchkiss, and, after graduating from law school, he proposed to her. The two were about to be married when Ethan realized he had fallen in love with Theresa Lopez-Fitzgerald,leading Ethan to break off their engagement and propose to Theresa. After being lied to repeatedly, however, Ethan broke off that engagement as well. He and Gwen grew closer once more, but he was still in love with Theresa. He was about to choose Theresa once and for all when Gwen discovered that she was pregnant. Ethan relised that he had to do the right thing, and decided to marry the mother of his child instead of the woman he loved, Theresa. Ethan and Gwen were married on December 19, 2002, and are now separated since Jan 2007 due to Ethan's inability to get Theresa out of his heart. Together, the couple had a daughter Sarah (October 8, 2003; stillborn). Ethan also has a daughter, Jane (born December 2, 2004, as a result of being date-raped by Theresa (Ethan is unaware that Theresa had given him a dangerous date-rape drug that night).
  • Noah Bennett is the eldest child and only son of Sam and Grace Bennett. When the show began in 1999, Noah was a sophomore in college, which he attended off-screen. In 2003, Noah dropped out of graduate school, and forged letters from the Dean and grade transcripts in order to make his father believe that he was still in school. In reality, he was squatting in hotels and gambling in Las Vegas, where he met Fancy Crane. After returning to Harmony and facing his father's wrath, Noah eventually came to love Fancy, though plots by Alistair have separated the couple once more.
  • Kathleen Elizabeth Bennett is the eldest daughter of Sam and Grace Bennett. Kay had had a crush on best friend Miguel Lopez-Fitzgerald ever since she was a little girl. Kay's hopes were dashed, however, when her cousin, Charity Standish, came to town, and Miguel fell for her instead. Kay tried everything to separate the two, resorting to dark magic, but nothing worked, and, after Miguel left town in 2004, became engaged to Fox Crane. Kay is the mother of Maria Lopez-Fitzgerald (July 21, 2003) as a result of her mystical rape of Miguel.
  • Jessica Bennett is the youngest daughter (and child) of Sam and Grace Bennett. Jessica was always the good child, trying to deter Kay from her evil schemes. After her mother left with David Hastings, however, Jessica lashed out, dressing trashily and hanging out at seedy clubs. At one such club, she was raped, and began cutting herself to cope with her emotional pain. She eventually began dating Spike, the owner of one of the clubs that she frequented, unaware that Spike was just toying with her. Her father and brothers both attempted to put Spike out of business by trashing and burning down his clubs, and, in retaliation, Spike convinced Jessica to leave home and live with him, as well as becoming a prostitute. While high on drugs and prostituting herself, however, Jessica is believed to have lashed out violently at her johns, as four of her customers have wound up dead after sleeping with her (though one of them was actually killed by Spike). Jessica has since left prostitution, though she recently married Spike in June of 2006.


  • Gwen Hotchkiss Winthrop is the only child of Jonathan and Rebecca Hotchkiss. She met Ethan Winthrop (then Crane) when she was twelve, and the two quickly became inseparable. She was elated when Ethan finally proposed to her in 1999, and was devastated when he fell in love with Theresa Lopez-Fitzgerald, and she was forced to break off their engagement. After Ethan and Theresa broke up, however, Ethan and Gwen slowly found their way back to each other, and, after discovering that Gwen was pregnant, they were married on December 19, 2002. Their daughter, Sarah, was stillborn on October 8, 2003, leaving Gwen barren. Ethan and Gwen now have custody of Jane, Ethan's daughter with Theresa, after Theresa reneged on Ethan's deal between the two women (Theresa wouldn't charge Gwen with attempted murder if Ethan changed his mind about filing for custody of Jane), and Gwen loves and cares for Jane as if she were her own daughter. Gwen left Ethan in January 2007 because she couldn't deal with Ethan's love for Theresa anymore.
  • Spike Lester is the former pimp and husband of Jessica Bennett. One of Alistair Crane's henchmen, Spike was ordered to prostitute Jessica to hurt her father, Sam. Jessica left him in the spring of 2006, but, after pretending to be unable to kill her, the two were married in Rome, Italy in June, 2006.
  • Nicholas Foxworth Crane is the husband of Kay Bennett. Fox recently tricked Kay into marrying him by convincing her that he was dying of a rare illness.

[edit] Third Generation

  • Ethan Lopez-Fitzgerald Crane (Born 2002) is the biological son of Ethan Winthrop and Theresa Lopez-Fitzgerald, long believed to be Julian Crane's son. He was born on 21 June 2002, and adopted in 2004 by Ethan and Gwen Winthrop after Theresa's parental rights were rescinded then later given to Theresa in 2005 and then adopted once more by his grandfather, Alistair, upon Alistair's marriage to Theresa. Little Ethan is currently the heir to the Crane empire. Alistair planned on molding Little Ethan to follow in his footsteps. Theresa recently discovered that her eldest child was fathered by Ethan and not Julian but is keeping quiet because, according to Alistair's will, she and Little Ethan will lose the Crane fortune if he isn't of Crane blood.
  • Sarah Winthrop (Born 2003, Died 2003) was the daughter of Ethan and Gwen Winthrop. When her mother attacked Theresa Lopez-Fitzgerald, Gwen fell, and as a result gave birth to a stillborn Sarah on October 8, 2003. Sarah's death was a tragic event that Ethan and Gwen still mourn to this day.
  • Jane Winthrop (Born 2004) was conceived when Theresa slipped Ethan a date-rape drug, dressed up as his wife, Gwen, and seduced him in order to trade Jane for Little Ethan, who had been adopted by the Winthrops. Ethan and Gwen were unaware of Theresa's deception, however, believing Jane to be the child that Theresa was to be the surrogate for. It was months before Ethan recalled the rape, but Theresa lied and claimed she had not given him anything. When Jane was born on December 2, 2004, and revealed to be Theresa's daughter with Ethan, Gwen, who was barren, was devastated, and went temporarily insane due to an interaction of medications that she had no control over, kidnapping the baby, whom she and Ethan had planned to name Ashley. Ethan, Theresa, and Fox eventually found Gwen and Jane and returned them home. Ethan and Gwen currently have temporary custody of Jane.
  • Maria Ivy Lopez-Fitzgerald is the product of Kay's mystical rape of Miguel Lopez-Fitzgerald. Born on July 28, 2003, Maria was premature, and came close to dying several times. Once she made it past the first six months, however, Maria was fine. Maria currently lives with her mother at Tabitha Lenox's house.

[edit] Hidden Passions

In the book Hidden Passions, Sam and Hank's parents were Benjamin Bennett and Margaret Joyce Bennett. They had at least four sons, including Sam and Hank (at least two sons were between Sam and Hank). Information in the book has not been confirmed on-screen and is not considered to be official since the information seen on the show contradicts much of what was said in the book. The show has never confirmed the names of Sam and Hank's parents, or the existence of the other brothers.
