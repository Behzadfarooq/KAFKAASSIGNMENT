Ben Keith

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Ben Keith is an American pedal steel guitarist. He worked as a session musician in Nashville in the late 1950s and 1960s. He is most famous for his work with Neil Young, both in stage shows and on albums such as Harvest, Comes A Time, Harvest Moon, and Prairie Wind. He has also worked with Todd Rundgren, Lonnie Mack, The Band, David Crosby, Graham Nash, Paul Butterfield, J.J. Cale, Linda Ronstadt, Warren Zevon, Ian and Sylvia, Hungry Chuck, Emmylou Harris, Willie Nelson, Waylon Jennings, Ann Murray and Ringo Starr. He is also known as the producer of Jewel's debut album Pieces of You and as a solo recording artist.
