Bentinck Stakes

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

The Bentinck Stakes is a Group 3 flat horse race in the United Kingdom for three-year-old and above thoroughbreds run over a distance of 6 furlongs (1,207 metres) at Newmarket's Rowley Mile course in October.

It is named in honour of Lord George Bentinck, a successful racehorse owner in the 19th century.

Prior to 1993 the race distance was 5 furlongs (1,006 metres). It was promoted to Group 3 status in 2003, having previously been a Listed race.
