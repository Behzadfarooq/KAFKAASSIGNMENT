Benalla is a railway station on the Albury railway line in Victoria, Australia. It is 195km from Southern Cross station. As well as having a Broad Gauge platform, there is also a Standard Gauge platform, used for Countrylink services between Sydney and Melbourne. The station also has a goods yard and once contained a tower, although it has now been demolished. It is an important juncture in northeastern Victoria with surrounding towns using the station for travel to the state capital Melbourne. It is the second last stop on the Sydney to Melbourne train service and with a travel time to Melbourne of 2hrs is the longest distance between stops on that service. Recently invovled with a train derailment, the station has a rich history involving both the famous Kelly Gang and Royal Tours, as well as hosting the Flying Scotsman during its visit in the early 90's. It is one of the oldest country stations in the state once boasting a bar and restuarant for rail travelers. The old time dance hall, located next to the station and run by ex railway workers was a mecca for oldies wanting to dance like it was 1947, unfortunately rising insurance costs ended its nearly 50 year run and more recently part of the building was knocked down by the recent train derailment. The railyard has a workable turntable but due to falling rail deliveries and multi directional engines, its use is restricted to exhibition only.
