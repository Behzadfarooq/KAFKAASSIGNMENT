Benmore Peak is the highest point of the Benmore Range, which is located in the southern half of the Mackenzie Basin, South Island, New Zealand.

The 30 km (18½ mile) by 17 km (10½ mile) Benmore Range is aligned approximately north–south and situated between the town of Twizel at the northern end, and the village of Omarama at the south-western end. The Benmore Range is bordered by Lake Benmore to the south, and the Haldon Arm of Lake Benmore to the east.

There are more than 30 surveyed peaks above 1,000 m (3,280 ft), with at least 12 of these above 1,500 m (4,920 ft), although only five have official names.

Benmore Peak (1,932 m / 6,338 ft) is near the centre of the range, with Sutherlands Peak (1,846 m / 6,054 ft) and The Cairn (1,464 m / 4,801 ft) to the north, and Totara Peak (1,822 m / 5,977 ft) to the south. The Buscot (1,245 m / 4,084 ft) is an attached subpeak on the western side of the main range.

The range is comprised mostly of greywacke, and some schist. Flora includes slim snow tussock (Chionochloa macra) and narrow-leaved snow tussock (Chionochloa rigida) as well as an array of alpine herbs, while bird and animal species include the New Zealand falcon/kārearea, sky lark, chukar, California quail and the New Zealand pipit/pihoihoi, as well as the common skink and common gecko.

Although it is almost entirely New Zealand Crown land, and a protected Outstanding Landscape Area, several large farm stations have legally run stock on the Benmore Range for more than a century, including Ben Omar, Benmore, Buscot, Glenbrook, Glencairn, Peak Valley and Totara Peak.

The Benmore Peak astronomical observatory is sited on privately-owned land, at the summit plateau. The facility has various structures on-site, from the peak down to the 1,830 m (6,002 ft) level. It is the highest altitude observatory in Oceania, and tenth highest in the Southern Hemisphere. (It is the highest altitude astronomical observatory in the Southern Hemisphere outside of the Andes of South America, where all nine higher elevation facilities are sited.)

The NZ Department of Conservation acquired 1,200 ha (2,965 acres) of land between The Cairn and Sutherlands Peak from Glenbrook Station, and it has been officially designated a public Conservation Area. It incorporates an area of wetlands as well as much drier elevated areas.
