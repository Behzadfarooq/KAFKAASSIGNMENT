[edit] History

The libretto was written by Léon de Wailly and Auguste Barbier. It was first performed in Paris at the Paris Opera on September 10, 1838. At its premiere, the audience, disturbed by the radical new opera, rioted.

Benvenuto Cellini is rarely performed and is not part of the standard operatic repertoire. The United States premiere took place in New York's Philharmonic Hall on March 22, 1965. The first performance of the work at the Metropolitan Opera on December 4, 2003, with James Levine conducting.

[edit] Roles

"Teresa" (Soprano) - beautiful daughter of Balducci, in love with Cellini, but promised to Fieramosca

"Ascanio" (Mezzo-soprano, a breeches role) - Cellini’s trusted apprentice

"Benvenuto Cellini" (Tenor) - brilliant artist/goldsmith and a real Renaissance man. Benvenuto loves adventure, fighting and wine. He has the metal to take a stand, even with the Pope.

"Francesco" (Tenor) - artisan

"Fieramosca" (Baritone) - Pope’s sculptor, foolhardy and fear-filled

"Pompeo" (Baritone) - friend of Fieramosca amd skilled with the sword

"Pope Clement VII" (Bass) - ruler of Rome whose obsession with art makes him favor Cellini and other artists

"Balducci" (Bass) - Pope's treasurer and Teresa’s stingy father

"Bernardino" (Bass) - artisan
