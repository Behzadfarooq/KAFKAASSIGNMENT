Benenden is a village and civil parish in the Tunbridge Wells District of Kent, England. The parish is located on the Weald six miles (10km) to the west of Tenterden.

The parish church is dedicated to St George, and is a 19th century building on the site of a medieval building destroyed in a fire. Benenden School is located to the north of the village.

[edit] Origin of Name

The place name of Benenden (pronounced Ben-in-dn) derives from Old English meaning Bynna's wooded pasture. Bynning denn became Benindene (1086) Binnigdaenne, Bennedene (c1100) Bynindenne (1253) then the current spelling from 1610.[1][2]
