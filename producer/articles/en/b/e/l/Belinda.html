The name Belinda is a common female first name, often interpreted as "beautiful serpent," though its meaning and origins are still contended. It can refer to:

Astronomy:

  • Belinda is a moon of the planet Uranus.

Politics:

  • Belinda Stronach is a Liberal Member of Parliament in the Canadian House of Commons.
  • Justice Belinda Ang is a judge of the Supreme Court of Singapore.

Literature:

  • Belinda is a novel by Anne Rice written under the pseudonym Anne Rampling.
  • Belinda is the name of a fictional character in Alexander Pope's The Rape of the Lock.
  • Belinda is the title of a 1918 play by A. A. Milne and of three novels. See Belinda (novel).
  • Belinda is an Italian comic book character created by Guido Crepax.

Entertainment:

  • Belinda Carlisle is the lead vocalist for the rock & roll band The Go-Go's and also a solo artist.
  • Belinda Peregrin is a Mexican singer.

Sports:

  • Belinda Cordwell is a former tennis player from New Zealand.
