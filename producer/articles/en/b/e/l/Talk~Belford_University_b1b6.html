According to degreeadvice.com, Speedy Degrees (speedydegrees.com)is Affiliate of Ashwood University, Belford University, Affordable Degrees (affordabledegrees.com), and Rochville University are run by the same people. [1]

I've moved the above text from the article; in addition to the text itself being extremely unclear, Degreeadvice's information on Speedydegrees does not appear to be current; it no longer appears to be possible to link Belford to the other entities based on this source. Robertissimo 14:11, 14 August 2006 (UTC)

[edit] Link to official site

An editor has questioned on my Talk page the neutrality of including a link to this entity's own site. While Wikipedia is not a repository of links, neither is it a soapbox, and the presence of a link so closely related to the subject should not be construed as an endorsement. Links to debatable, even detestable sites abound, especially in regard to controversial matters. Westboro Baptist Church contains links to its own site, as does Amway. Including the site as a reference only provides context for the article, not least because the organization apparently does most of its business online. Robertissimo 09:50, 19 August 2006 (UTC)


This is obviously a flawed policy. Wikipedia is helping fraudulent sites by hyperlinking to them. Editors should have the option to not include a hyperlink if the source is verifiably fraudulent and/or criminal in nature. SarahTeach 19:55, 29 August 2006 (UTC)

[edit] Ownership/Connections

All of the above shows a series of connections among a range of online universities and essay-writing sites. However, it also seems to stray into original research, and certainly would if one went further and in the article claimed outright shared ownership. As it stands, the article has a "Connections" section, drawing on an appropriate source. If a further reliable source can be found to illustrate additional connections, it should certainly be added.

A note to those editing this page: other editors' Talk-page contributions should very, very rarely be edited. Wikipedia is not censored. Robertissimo 03:22, 4 September 2006 (UTC)

[edit] Link to official site

I do not think an edit war is the solution to whether or not the organization's official site should be included among the article's sources and references. As has been stated, Wikipedia is not censored. Editors' own feelings regarding any individual article subject's merits or lack thereof should not trump that fundamental, whether one is discussing religion, politics, or even the shadiest corner of academics. The article is quite forthright in discussing what Belford is and is not, and has drawn on good sources to do so. Robertissimo 02:23, 6 September 2006 (UTC)

With all due respect, that has nothing to do with hyperlinking. A plain text URL is perfectly acceptable, but does not help the criminals increase their Google/Yahoo page rank. SarahTeach 02:43, 6 September 2006 (UTC)

I understand that you feel strongly about issues surrounding education and fraud; I share many of your concerns. However, I disagree that we should dilute the usefulness of Wikipedia as a source for information solely out of concern for something like search-engine ranking. The solution you are proposing regarding Belford University's own site (to write out the URL, but not hyperlink it) is not one I have seen anywhere else in Wikipedia, whether in articles on religion, sexuality, science, or any other controversy-engendering field that comes to mind. In all areas we can find articles that include related links that many would find potentially objectionable or legally dubious. The bottom line is that we seek to provide neutral, verifiable information about each article's subject. Finally, I hope you will assume good faith and not think that I am in way an apologist for any organization seeking to defraud consumers. I would also ask that you not simply revert out the official site, but, if you continue to have concerns in this regard, post a request for comment. Robertissimo 15:52, 6 September 2006 (UTC)
