Belhaven College is a Christian liberal arts college[1],located in Jackson, Mississippi. Belhaven offers five bachelor degrees in twenty majors, associate degrees, master degrees, and various pre professional programs in a wide range of fields including ministry, dentistry, medicine, law and nursing. Belhaven College is accredited by the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools. The college has a covenant relationship with the Synod of Living Waters of the Presbyterian Church (USA). The college is also a member of the American Council of Education, the Association of Presbyterian Colleges, and the Council for Christian Colleges and Universities.

[edit] History

Belhaven College was chartered in 1894 by Dr. Louis T. Fitzhugh as a private all women's college. The college was named "Belhaven" after the Scottish ancestral home of Col. Jones Hamilton. Seven months later, the college burned in a tragic fire in 1895 but reoppened in 1986. In 1910 the college again caught fire resulting in a total loss. The college moved to Peachtree Street location after the fire and opened under the name of Belhaven Collegiate and Industrial Institution and the leadership of the Prebyterian church.

In 1972, the Presbyterian Synod of Mississippi turned over ownership of the college to the board of trustees. A covenant relationship with the Presbyterian church was initiated which provided spiritual and financial assistance to the college but not ownership. In 1986, the art department was accredited by the national Association of Schools of Art and Design. In 1986, Ballet Magnificat, a ballet company began on Belhaven College. Under the leadership of Dr. Roger Parrott, who became president in 1996, the college continues to expand, modernize and continue its tradition of excellents in the liberal art program. Today, the college offers a wide range of associate, bachelor, and master degrees. [2]

[edit] Degree Programs

Undergraduate Majors: Art (Visual), Accounting, Biblical Studies and Ministries, Biology, Business Administration, Chemistry, Church Music, Communications, Computer Sciences, Computer Information Systems, Creative Writing, Dance, Elementary Education, English, History, Humanities, International Studies, Mathematics, Music, Philosophy, Political Science, Psychology, Social Services, Sports Administration, Sports Medicine and Exercise Science, Sports Ministry, and Theatre. [3]

[edit] Adult and Graduate Programs

Belhaven College offers Graduate programs and Adult Education programs in the following fields, Associate of Arts, Bachelor of Arts in Social Service, Bachelor of Arts in Business Administration, Bachelor of Science Management, Bachelor Health Administration, Master of Business Administration, and Master of Science in Management, Master of Education, Master of Arts in Teaching.
