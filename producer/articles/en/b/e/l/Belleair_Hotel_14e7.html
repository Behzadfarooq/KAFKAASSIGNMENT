The Belleair, Florida Biltmore Hotel opened in 1897 as the Belleair Hotel, and was acquired by the Biltmore Hotel chain of John McEntee Bowman in 1920.

During World War II the hotel became an Army Air Corps training center.

Orinignally built in 1897 by Henry B. Plant,the railroad and hotel tycoon,as the Hotel Belleview.There are two additions the first in 1910 and the second and the last in 1924. The Belleview is considered the oldest and largest all wooden building still in occupation in the world. The Belleview is affectionally known as "The White Queen of The Gulf". This beautitiful Victorian hotel features many unique and beautiful architectural features such as shady Victorian verandas, a 13,000 square ft. Tiffany stained glass ceiling in the Tiffany Ballroom,charming hand carved woodwork in the lobby and through out the hotel,unique slopping roof with green shingles (the shingles were originally red but changed to green at a later date.)and much more.The Belleview is built out of virgin Florida pine wich is termit proof, fire proof,and now very rare. The Belleview is a rare survior of the Victorian wooden resort hotel such as the Hotel Del Coronado in Coronado,California.But the Belleview is now in danger. In 2004 the De Barto brothers filed a permit to demolish the hotel and it's grounds including the Palm Cottage and the historic golf course desined in 1925 by famous architect Donald Ross to put up hundreds of condos and a new hotel. The Belleview was added to "Americas 11 Most Endangered Places" in 2004. There are many preservationists fighting to save this historic hotel. The Belleview is rumored to be haunted by a beautiful young woman who took recidence at the hotel at the turn of the century. Hopefully this historic hotel will be preserved for future generations to enjoy.
