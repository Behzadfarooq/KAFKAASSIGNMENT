Bellingham, Tasmania

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Bellingham is a tiny coastal hamlet in Northern Tasmania, situated on the mouth of the Pipers River directly opposite the town of Weymouth. About 60 km from Launceston, 20 km from Bridport and 30 km from George Town. The township is mostly made up of several shacks used during the summer time. The permanent residence is about 25. In recent years the George Town Council has upgraded many of the roads allowing better access. There is a camping ground, tennis court and a few beaches.
