December 1

  • 659 or 660: death of Saint Eligius, bishop of Noyon-Tournai and chief counsellor of King Dagobert I
  • 1530: death of Margarete of Austria, regent of the Netherlands, at Mechelen
  • 1633: death of Infanta Isabella Clara Eugenia of Spain, joint sovereign of the Seventeen Provinces, at Brussels
  • 1874: death of André Henri Constant van Hasselt, poet
  • 1922: birth of Ernest Sterckx, cyclist, winner of the Tour of Belgium, three times winner of the Omloop Het Volk
  • 1923: birth of Morris, comics artist, creator of Lucky Luke
  • 1923: death of Virginie Loveling, writer
  • 1954: birth of François Van Der Elst, soccer player, active with R.S.C. Anderlecht, New York Cosmos and West Ham United F.C., played 44 times for the Belgium national football team

December 4

  • 771: death of Carloman, King of Austrasia

  • 1918: first edition of De Standaard
  • 1934: death of Adrien de Gerlache (pictured), leader of the Belgian Antarctic Expedition of 1897-1899, the first to spend the winter in Antarctica
  • 1953: birth of Jean-Marie Pfaff, soccer player, 64 times goal keeper of the Belgium national football team, goalkeeper of Bayern Munich, one of only three Belgians listed on the FIFA 100 list by Pelé, now star of the reality soap De Pfaffs on VTM
  • 1976: birth of Mbo Mpenza, soccer player with 53 appearances for the Belgium national football team

December 6

Saint Nicholas Day (Dutch: Sinterklaas; French: Saint Nicolas) , a day when children receive gifts (mostly toys)

  • 1556: death of Pieter Coucke van Aelst (pictured), painter, teacher and father-in-law of Pieter Brueghel the Elder
  • 1874: death of Egide Charles Gustave Wappers, painter
  • 1919: birth of Paul de Man, literary critic, professor at Yale University
  • 1922: birth of Guy Thys; soccer player and coach, caoch of the Belgium national football team for 101 matches between 1976 and 1989 and again between 1900 and 1991, reaching the semi-finals of the 1986 World Cup in Mexico
  • 1938: birth of Patrick Bauchau, actor in e.g. 24, The Pretender, and Panic Room
  • 1985: last attack by the Communist Combatant Cells or CCC, a terrorist organization, notable for mainly targeting property and trying to avoid humans
  • 2004: death of Raymond Goethals, football coach, winner of the Champions League with Olympique de Marseille
