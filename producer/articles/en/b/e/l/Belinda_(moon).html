Belinda (bə-lin'-də, IPA: [/bəˈlɪndə/]) is an inner satellite of Uranus. It is named after the heroine of Alexander Pope's The Rape of the Lock. It is also designated Uranus XIV.[2]

Belinda was discovered from the images taken by Voyager 2 on 1986-01-13, and was given the temporary designation S/1986 U 5.[3]

Based on light curves and direct imaging, Belinda is apparently a very elongated object with its lower bound of prolateness being 0.4.[1] Other than its size and orbit, virtually nothing is known about it.
