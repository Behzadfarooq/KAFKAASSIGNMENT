Belvidere Mall

Belvidere Mall is a small shopping mall located in Waukegan, IL, and was one of the first shopping malls in Chicagoland. It's main anchor is a Home Depot (formerly a Montgomery Ward store.)


[edit] History

Belvidere opened in 1965 and was Lake County's first enclosed shopping mall. The mall has been renovated twice, in 1985 and again in 1998. The main anchor is a Home Depot, which was originally a Montgomery Ward and a Builder's Square. The mall is currently owned by Imperial Realty. As part of the 1998 renovation, the mall was renamed Belvidere Discount Mall. Today it is partially an indoor mall and a retail strip center.


[edit] Competition with Lakehurst Mall

When Belvedere opened, it was one of the biggest things to hit the Waukegan area, but in 1971, a much larger mall opened minutes away from Belvedere, Lakehurst Mall, a mall more than twice the size of Belvedere with three anchor stores. When Lakehurst opened, many predicted that Belvedere would close down. When Montgomery Ward moved out of Belvedere to Lakehurst, things did not look good for the declining mall. Eventually, the Wards store was expanded and became a Home Depot, Lakehurst however, was declining, and eventually was shuttered, a surprise to the Waukegan area. The mall couldn't compete with Hawthorn Center and Gurnee Mills.
