Bella Vista High School, a member of the San Juan Unified School District, is a public high school in Fair Oaks, California. In 2003, the California Department of Education's School Recognition Program honored Bella Vista High School as a "California Distinguished School." [2] It was also the only school in Sacramento County to receive this honor in 2003. [3]

[edit] Academic Curriculum

Bella Vista offers various degrees of high school courses to its students. Aside from the fundamental courses taken by most of the student body, qualified students are eligible to take Honors-level courses in English, French, Chemistry, Physics, and World History. The Advanced Placement (AP) program is available to qualified students as well; however, the prerequisities to take one of the AP courses are more rigorous than others because these courses grant university credits once the students pass the AP examinations. AP courses are offered in English, Spanish, French, Calculus A/B, Calculus B/C, U.S. History, and Psychology. A special business course called Advanced Computer Applications is available to qualified students, and once completed, will be eligible for 3 units of Sacramento State University credit through the Accelerated College Entrance off-campus program.

Art

Band (Marching Band and Jazz Band) Known throughout Northern California for their commitment, dedication, and perfection, the Bella Vista Bronco Band is a model for success. Their outstanding field shows stand out from other marching bands in the region. The band's performances include professionally choreographed dance routines, well-trained vocals, and colorful props. It was undefeated in Northern California for 7 years.

Under the direction of Edward Moore, UOP alumni, the band has accumulated hundreds of trophies over the years, competing out of state and even in Europe in 2006.

[edit] Extracurricular and Academic Activities

Bella Vista High School provides students diverse choices to join to various extracurricular activities. Every September, the high school student government sponsors "Club Day," where students can join to different clubs such as the National Honors Society, California Scholarship Federation, Medical Workers of Tomorrow, Rock Climbing Club, Los Aliados, French Honors Society, and many others.

The school newspaper is known as La Bandera, while the school yearbook is called La Remuda.

Aside from extracurricular activities, Bella Vista's renowned marching band has been consistently recognized by Sacramento Magazine as one of the best marching bands in the Sacramento area in 2004 [4] and 2005. [5] The academic decathlon team has represented Sacramento County in the California Academic Decathlon numerous times. Also, the moot court team earned the first-place team award at the National High School Moot Court Competition in Washington, D.C in 2003, 2005 and 2006. [6]
