February 1

  • 1248: death of Henry II, Duke of Brabant
  • 1801: birth of Jean Théodore Lacordaire, entomologist
  • 1864: the Belgian Red Cross is founded
  • 1947: birth of Gaston Rahier, motocross racer, three-time world champion and two-time winner of the Paris Dakar Rally
  • 1972: birth of Johan Walem, former football midfielder

February 2

  • 1973: death of Hendrik Elias, politician
  • 1983: Godfried Danneels, Archbishop of Mechelen-Brussels, receives the title of Cardinal of the Holy Roman Church

Edit February 2 anniversaries • February 2 anniversaries on English Wikipedia

February 3

  • 1399: death of John of Gaunt, 1st Duke of Lancaster, born in Ghent, ancestor of the English kings of the house of Lancaster
  • 1796: birth of Jean Baptiste Madou, painter and lithographer
  • 1920: birth of Stan Ockers, cyclist, runner-up in the Tour de France in 1950 and 1952
  • 1941: birth of Antoine Duquesne, politician, former chairman of the MR and minister in the Belgian federal government
  • 1958: The Treaty establishing the Benelux Economic Union is signed at The Hague
  • 1975: death of Ernest Sterckx, cyclist, winner of the Tour of Belgium, three times winner of the Omloop Het Volk
  • 1993: death of Karel Goeyvaerts, composer

February 4

  • 1168: death of Thierry, Count of Flanders (also known as Diederik Van den Elzas)
  • 1894: death of Adolphe Sax, inventor, creator of the saxophone
  • 1921: death of Xavier Mellery, Symbolist painter
  • 1926: birth of Albert Frère, allegedly the richest man in Belgium
  • 1957: birth of Walter Van Beirendonck, fashion designer
  • 1981: birth of Johan Van Summeren, cyclist, winner of the Omloop Het Volk and Luik-Bastenaken-Luik
  • 1994: death of Fred De Bruyne, cyclist and sports commentator, winner of Paris-Roubaix

Edit February 4 anniversaries • February 4 anniversaries on English Wikipedia

February 5

  • 1602: birth of Franciscus van den Enden, teacher of Baruch de Spinoza and an early promotor of democracy
  • 1885: King Léopold II of Belgium establishes the Congo as a personal possession.
  • 1937: birth of Gaston Roelants, winner of the gold medal at the 1964 Summer Olympics in Tokyo on the 3,000 m steeplechase in a new world record
  • 1940: birth of Panamarenko, artist
  • 1985: birth of Tatiana Silva, Miss Belgium 2005
  • 1995: the draft is abolished in Belgium
  • 2000: death of George Koltanowski, holder of the world record for most simultaneous blindfolded chess games

Edit February 5 anniversaries • February 5 anniversaries on English Wikipedia

February 6

  • 1497: death of Johannes Ockeghem, leading composer of the Franco-Flemish School
  • 1862: death of Louis-Joseph Seutin, physician, the first to use chloroform for anesthesia
  • 1961: birth of Florence Aubenas, journalist with French newspaper Libération, taken hostage in Iraq in 2005
  • 1984: birth of Julie Taton, Miss Belgium 2003
  • 2004: birth of Princess Louise of Belgium, the first child of Prince Laurent of Belgium and Princess Claire

February 7

  • 1844: birth of Jan De Vos, politician and Mayor of Antwerp from 1909 to 1921
  • 1978: birth of Daniel Van Buyten, soccer player with Bayern Munich, played 33 times for the Belgium national football team as of October 2006

February 10

  • 1477: signing of the "Great Privilege" between Mary of Burgundy (pictured) and the cities of Flanders
