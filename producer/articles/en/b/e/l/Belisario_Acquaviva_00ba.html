Belisario Acquaviva, Duca di Nardò (ca. 1464—24 July 1528[1]) was a Neapolitan nobleman and writer. The younger brother of the literary figure Andrea Matteo Acquaviva, he took the opposite side of politics to his brother, and was a steadfast adherent of the Spaniards; hence he was enbabled, not only to preserve his own patrimony, but to procure the restitution of his brother's. Like that brother, he applied himself to letters, and left several dissertations, collected since into one volume.[2]

Of this illustrious name were several other members attached to literature. Two dukes of Atri were poets.[2]
