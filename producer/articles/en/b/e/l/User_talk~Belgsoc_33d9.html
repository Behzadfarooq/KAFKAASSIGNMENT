[edit] About TTF-Bucksfan

Dear Belgsoc, I'm flattered that you decided to do one against User:Gene Poole, and insert an entry about the "Kingdom of Bucksfan," but you have incurred several mishaps in doing so.
For starters, the nation's full name is TTF-Bucksfan, or The Tallini Family-Kingdom of Bucksfan, so you actually didn't mention the entire national entity, and you also did not mention the oldest entity, the one with the most real world connections.
Let me also add that if you believe that TTF-Bucksfan is a "crankish vanity project with limited serious appeal," then why do you include it with the nations under the "Vehicles for the promotion of an agenda?" TTF-Bucksfan has among its citizens both a former Italian Minister of Parliament, and an Internet scientist, one of the founders of the Internet; it owns a 256-node IPv4 network, which is equivalent to the network of the Falkland Islands; it is known to several US VIPs and politicians, to many political VIPs in Italy, and is also known personally by the House of Savoy; it runs and owns 8 alternative top-level domains (TLDs); it runs a university doing quite a bit of original research; it runs a professional news agency; and finally, it is also the founder of the Fifth World Council (5WC), an IGO-NGO with 5/6 nation members, and one member happens to be a Dutch-Australian multinational. If TTF-Bucksfan is a "crank," then what do you call the rest of them, including real cranks like Atlantium, listed in the Crank.net Micronations Page as the crankiest nation (yet you made no mention of that in Atlantium's entry). Please be fair, and if you don't call Atlantium a crank, don't use such words to describe an entity with real world bank credit with the same term.
In the past I have placed Nova Roma under a separate micronational category (Historical Simulations), and I left the quote by Nova Roma itself, because I felt it was significant. User:Gene Poole however eliminated both the category, which was entirely appropriate in the case of Nova Roma, and he also eliminated the significant quote. I did nothing to show Nova Roma in a crankish light, and in fact the Italian article on micronations does reflect that basic decency. This is what the Nova Roma entry says in Italian: "Nova Roma (micronation), not to be confused with the historical Nova Roma, is without doubt one of the most populated micronations with over one thousand enthusiasts. Nova Roma has issued coins, and uses Latin as an official language." Did I say anything negative here? I don't believe I did, and everything stated is factual, NPOV, and Italian readers love that article that I authored.
Please edit that entry for TTF-Bucksfan, and eliminate the negative bias as appropriate. I cannot do that without appearing to embellish my own nation's entry.
