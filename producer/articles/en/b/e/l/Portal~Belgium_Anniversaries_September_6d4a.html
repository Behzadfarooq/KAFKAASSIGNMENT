September 4

Image:Simenon liège.jpg
  • 1925: birth of Leo Apostel, philosopher
  • 1944 - World War II: the British 11th Armoured Division liberates the Belgian city of Antwerp
  • 1965: birth of Marc Degryse, soccer player with 63 appearances with the Belgium national football team, scoring 23 goals
  • 1976: birth of Bjorn Haneveer, snooker player, European Champion in 2000
  • 1989: death of Georges Simenon (pictured), prolific writer, creator of Maigret, winner of the Mystery Writers of America Grand Master Award
  • 2006: death of Rémy Belvaux, film director, also producer and actor in Man Bites Dog

September 5

  • 1235: death of Henry I, Duke of Brabant, father-in-law of Otto IV, Holy Roman Emperor
  • 1659: death of Pieter de Carpentier (pictured), born in Antwerp, Governor-General of the Dutch East Indies between 1623 and 1627
  • 1771: birth of Archduke Charles, Duke of Teschen, last Governor of the Habsburg Netherlands
  • 1940: death of Charles de Broqueville, Prime Minister of Belgium during World War I
  • 1977: death of Marcel Thiry, poet

September 25
