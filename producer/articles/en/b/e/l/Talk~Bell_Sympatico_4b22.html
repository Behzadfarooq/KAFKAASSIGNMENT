"These modems are backwards compatible with ADSL, and as such Sympatico caps them at 3 or 4 Mbit/s like any other modem." This sentence is a bit inaccurate since the modems aren't capped at all, only the profile set at the other end on the DSLAM card determines which speeds the modem will try to synchronize at. I did not dare modify the entry myself though.

[edit] Terrible ISP

69.158.4.62's edits are not acceptable for an encylopedia, although I concur with the sentiment. Customer service is awful, with the added insult of a sales pitch thrown in at the end.

Recently I phoned to cancel my BS service because I'd found a low cost alternative. Incredibly they offered to see if they could match or beat the new price, which was half of what I was paying. In effect, their customer rep was admitting they have a policy of gouging their customers for as long as possible. If you are a BS dial-up subscriber as I was, I would advise checking on a regular basis to see if alternatives arrive in your area. Don't give this terrible ISP more money than you have to. Pendragon39 19:08, 15 October 2006 (UTC)
