Belle Reve is a fictional prison and sanitorium in the DC Universe. Many supervillains were imprisoned there, thus allowing the Suicide Squad to operate from the prison. It is located near the Gulf Of Mexico. It has been the site of numerous supervillain attacks and outright riots. Darkseid's forces have infiltrated it, the U.S. military has been manipulated into attacking it, and long-time Green Lantern villain Major Force has caused much structural damage.

Multiple well known villains have been imprisoned there, including Deadshot, the now deceased Captain Boomerang and Hector Hammond.

The staff of the prison have been featured heavily in the Suicide Squad stories, often fighting side by side with the heros or forced to fight with the villains.

More recently, Belle Reve was the site of a Mageddon-caused riot in Justice League #34, in which many guards and other prison staff members were killed, including the warden. It is not known if it is the same warden from the Suicide Squad.

On the television series Smallville, Belle Reve Sanitarium is an insane asylum. The metahuman villains Clark fights are almost always held here for their insantiy. In a recent episode, "Freak", Chloe Sullivan laments that all meteor rock-infected Smallville citizens end up either dead or in Belle Reeve, indicating that insanity is a likely side effect of kryptonite exposure in humans.

In the DC Comics Elseworlds limited series and graphic novel Kingdom Come, Belle Reve had actually been destroyed by Bane and Two-Face. It provides the inspiration for a massive containment facility called The Gulag and used as a concentration camp for all the metahuman beings who won't submit to the rules Superman and his superteam of heroes impose on Earth's metahuman community.

Belle Reve is likely named for the DuBois household that is lost by Blanche DuBois in Tennessee Williams's play, A Streetcar Named Desire, and/or Christopher Reeve.

Belle Reve translates from French as "Beautiful Dream".
