Belarus competed at the 2004 Summer Olympics in Athens, Greece.

Before gaining independence many Belarusian athletes competed for the USSR team: Vitaly Scherbo is a 6-times Olympic champion, Nellie Kim is five times Olympic champion, Olga Korbut, Olga Belova, Viktar Sidziak are 4-times Olympic champions, Uladzimir Parfianovich, Svetlana Boguinskaya are 3-times Olympic champions.

[edit] Medals

The Belarus national team won 15 medals at the 2004 Summer Olympics in Athens: 2 gold, 6 silver, and 7 bronze. By comparison, in the 2000 Summer Olympics in Sydney Belarus won 17 medals (3 gold, 3 silver, 11 bronze), and in the 1996 Summer Olympics in Atlanta, won 15 medals (1 gold, 6 silver, and 8 bronze).

[edit] Boxing

Belarus entered six boxers in 2004. They won a pair of silver medals, with a combined record of 9-6 in competition. Belarus ranked 6th in the medals chart for boxing.

Flyweight (51kg)

  • Bato Vankeev
    1. Round of 32 - Lost to Juan Carlos Payano of Dominican Republic, 26-18

Bantamweight (54kg)

  • Khavazhi Khatsigov
    1. Round of 32 - Defeated Juan Lopez of Puerto Rico, 27-19
    2. Round of 16 - Lost to Worapoj Petchkoom of Thailand, 33-18

Featherweight (57kg)

  • Mikhail Biarnadski
    1. Round of 32 - Defeated Likar Ramos Concha of Colombia, 32-18
    2. Round of 16 - Lost to Viorel Simion of Romania, 38-13

Light Heavyweight (81kg)

  • Magomed Aripgadjiev - silver medal
    1. Round of 32 - Defeated Ramiro Goben Reducindo Radilla of Mexico, 29-10
    2. Round of 16 - Defeated Edgar Ramon Munoz Mata of Venezuela, 18-10
    3. Quarterfinal - Defeated Lei Yuping of China, 27-18
    4. Semifinal - Defeated Ahmed Ismail of Egypt, 23-20
    5. Final - Lost to Andre Ward of United States, 20-13

Heavyweight (91kg)

  • Viktar Zuyev - silver medal
    1. Round of 16 - Defeated Daniel Betti of Italy, injury
    2. Quarterfinal - Defeated Devin Vargas of United States, 36-27
    3. Semifinal - Defeated Mohamed Elsayed, Egypt, walkover
    4. Final - Lost to Odlanier Solis Fonte of Cuba, 22-13

Super Heavyweight (over 91kg)

  • Aliaksandr Apanasionak
    1. Round of 16 - Lost to Oleksii Mazikin of Ukraine, 23-5

[edit] Canoeing
