Belén Gopegui

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Belén Ruiz de Gopegui (Madrid, 1963) is a Spanish writer.

She studied laws in the Universidad Autónoma de Madrid, and she started working in several newspapers like El sol.

Her first novel La escala de los mapas was awarded with the prices : Premio Tigre Juan and Premio Iberoamericano Santiago del Nuevo Extremo. Gerardo Herrero based his film Las razones de mis amigos (2000) in her third novel La conquista del aire (1998).

Francisco Umbral considers her the best writer of her generation. She is a communist, and she has written several articles about Cuba.
