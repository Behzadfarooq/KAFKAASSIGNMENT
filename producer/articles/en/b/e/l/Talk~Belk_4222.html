[edit] Accurate characterization of Belk's "marketing?”

In the first paragraph of this article, there is a sentence that attempts to define Belk’s marketing position that places it at the same level as Dillard’s and Macy’s but below the level of Parisian, Bloomingdale's, Nordstrom, Neiman Marcus, and Saks Fifth Avenue. While I agree that the first two chains are peers and the latter four retailers could subjectively be viewed as more sophisticated than Belk, I do not agree that Parisian occupies a higher market position.

My research and shopping experiences at Belk and Parisian suggest that they are more alike than different, and have been for some time. Most conventional measures of the overused term “upscale retailer” are evident at both chains, and they occupy many of the same kinds of locations; a case in point being the ongoing, largely seamless, conversion of Parisian locations to Belk stores.

That store conversion has been a point of contentious argument on a message board I participate on. Alabamians in particular vilified Belk for purchasing the venerable retailer, and the arguments got so vicious the thread discussing it had to be closed. Few of the opinions expressed were objective and obscured the fact that in an age where more department stores close than open, these stores were staying open and largely intact.

By most measures, Belk is a respectable retailer and one of the few success stories in an industry riddled with failure and mismanagement. Converting Parisian stores to Belk is not a step down, as that sentence suggests but, rather, a continuation of what was under a different name.

I have tried to change the sentence in question in the article, only to have it changed back numerous times, probably by someone who intends to diminish Belk’s status because they purchased Parisian and plan to discontinue the nameplate. I have no plans to change the sentence anymore: “pissing matches” over sour grapes have no place in Wikipedia. But I do question the neutrality of the statement in regards to Parisian, and rather than anonymously changing it, I’m going to state that I don’t agree with the characterization.

Stevenswain 01:46, 25 February 2007 (UTC)
