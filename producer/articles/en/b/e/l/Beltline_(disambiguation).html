Beltline (or Belt Line) refers to the following:

In transportation:

  • A general term for a beltway.
  • Beltline, Calgary, a greater downtown neighbourhood of Calgary, Alberta.
  • The Belt Line (Atlanta) (may also be written as "Beltline") is a proposed rapid transit line for central Atlanta, Georgia.
  • The Belt Line Road is a freeway (originally proposed as a beltway) in the Eugene, Oregon area.
  • The Belt Line Road is a loop in the Dallas, Texas area.
  • The Beltline Highway is a major east-west freeway running along the south side of Madison, Wisconsin.
  • The Raleigh Beltline (Interstate 440), a loop road around Raleigh, North Carolina.
  • The Belt Line Railway was a commuter railroad built in Toronto, Ontario in the 1890s.
  • Beltline (automotive) refers to the topmost horizontal line on an automobile before reaching the vehicles greenhouse area.
  • Beltline Road ("The Beltline") is one of the main commercial thoroughfares of Decatur, Alabama
  • Belt Line (Williamsburg Bridge), a trolley line connecting Manhattan and Brooklyn, New York City, United States

Other:

  • A belting (beating) can also be a (usually double) line of people using belts on someone in a running the gauntlet-type set up, as in hazing
  • Beltline (band), an indie rock act from Portland, Oregon
