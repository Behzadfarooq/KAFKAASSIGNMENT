Begur, India

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Begur is a village in the Indian state of Karnataka. It is in the state capital Bangalore. It is off Bangalore-Hosur highway. It is said to have been a prominent place during the rule of the Chola Kingdom. A historical tank and a beautiful old temple(Naganatheshwara temple) dating back almost 1200 years can be seen there along with a broken old fortress which is the home of another temple. The temples are between 1 and 2 km apart. The main idol is that of lord Shiva. On the eve of Shivaratri devotees perform pujas and stay awake the whole night. In the month of April there is a car festival visited by many people. This place is yet to get a mark as a tourist destination due to improper transport facilities and roads. Transport is mainly by BMTC buses and autos.

This village can boast of a handful of well developed residential layouts.Vishwapriya Nagar,Classic paradise,Duo Heights layout etc are situated near this village.The area is quiet for residential needs.But the area lacks good roads. Transportation facilities to this village is not a problem as Bengalooru's BMTC buses arrives and departures every 5 min to all parts of the city.

Its strategically located equidistant from Electronic City & M G Road.This area is expected to develop drastically once BMIC road is open for all
