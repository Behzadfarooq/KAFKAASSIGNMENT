Beginnings is a book that features three fantasy/fiction stories with different subjects and themes:

  • The Tale of Princess Keiko, a story set in medieval Japan, featuring Princess Keiko, the Princess of Kyoto Castle.
  • The Tale of Prince Roy, a story set in the fictional South Kingdom, featuring Prince Roy, the younger Prince of South Castle.
  • The Tale of Will Kaine, a story set in the futuristic world of the Andromeda Galaxy, featuring Will Kaine, a space pirate who stole a Space Navy flagship and renamed it the Tech-Eagle.

[edit] The Tale of Princess Keiko Details

  • Protagonists
    • Princess Keiko: the Princess of Kyoto Castle
    • Akiyama Shingen: the Samurai Deputy of Edo
    • Zakoji Kenshin: the Samurai Sheriff of Edo
    • Misao Kenshin: Zakoji's sister, a treasure hunter
    • Taro (fighter), Semi (fighter), and Kingyo: the Animal Fighters
  • Antagonists
    • Neko the Cat: the Half-Cat-Demon Sorceress
    • Bloodred Samurai: a ronin who kidnapped Keiko
    • Ninja: a ninja who tried to assassinate Keiko
    • Daisuke (Beginnings): a giant cobra
  • Supporting Characters
    • Empress Chinatsu: Keiko's mother, who died after Keiko was born
    • Emperor Murakami (Beginnings): Keiko's father, Emperor of Kyoto Castle
    • Goku Mitsurugi: Zakoji's old sensei
  • Places
    • Kyoto Castle: fictional capitol of Japan
    • Kyoto: a prosperous city in Japan; capitol before Tokyo
    • Edo: a prosperous city in Japan(Tokyo in modern times)
    • Osaka: a proserous city in Japan; only mentioned
  • Items of Note
    • Katana: the famous samurai sword
    • Saber: another sword used by samurai
    • Reverse-Blade Sword: a dull-edged blade not meant for killing

[edit] The Tale of Prince Roy Details

  • Protagonists
    • Prince Roy: the younger Prince of South Castle
    • Elizabeth (Beginnings): a young traveler, a friend of Roy, Alexander's sister
    • Alexander (Beginnings): a young traveler, a friend of Roy, Elizabeth's brother
  • Antagonists
    • Fighters of Coliseum Isle: the Disciples of Anubis, Landon of London, and The Maebashi Murderer
    • Gizzard Dragon: a fearsome dragon that few have killed. It has two mouths, bat-like wings, claws, a tail bristling with spikes, and breathes fire from its secondary mouth.
    • Slavers: a large band of foreign slavers
    • The Necromancer: an evil sorcerer that is never seen
    • Alastor the Wizard: a sinister sorcerer who tries to take over South Kingdom
    • Rob the Rebellious: Roy's brother, who started a rebellion after Roy became King
  • Supporting Charaters
    • Mayor: Roy and his companions meet with the mayor of Deepwood, who askes them to find and defeat the slavers
  • Places
    • South Kingdom: the southernmost of four kingdoms, covered by a giant plain.
    • Seaside: South Kingdom's most prosperous city, a port-town with a giant spire in the center.
    • Coliseum Isle: a small island where an illegal coliseum was built.
    • Southern Woods: the woods in the southern region of South Kingdom.
    • Chasm and Kassim: the chasm is a huge gorge where gizzard dragons live; Kassim is a mining town near there, touching both the chasm and the plain.
    • Deepwood: a logging town in the Southern Woods.
    • The Valley of the Necromancer: once known as The Valley of the Holy Ones, the Necromancer took over decades ago when he died. Anyone who passes through his domain joins an undead army.
    • South Plain: a massive plain that covers most of South Kingdom.
    • South Castle: South Kingdom's capitol. The King before Roy was his father, King Ron.
  • Items of Note
    • Shrink Mushroom: a fictional fungus that, when eaten, makes the consumer shrink. Roy ate one, not knowing its properties, and shrank to the size of a mouse.
    • Growth Mushroom: a fictional fungus that, when eaten, makes the consumer grow. It is commonly used to counter the effects of a shrink mushroom. It is unknown how big someone will grow when eating just one or two, because Roy ate three before growth took place, making him eighty feet tall.
    • Morph Staff: a magical weapon that can transform into any weapon that the wielder knows of, such as a bow, sword, hammer, or shield. This is Elizabeth's weapon.
    • Blaze Blade: a powerful blade of unknown make. It's guard is shaped like flames, the guard and hilt are fiery red, a sapphire is embedded in the pommel, and it can manipulate fire. This is Rob's weapon.
    • Storm Sword: a powerful blade of unknown make. It's guard is shaped like a lightning bolt, the guard and hilt are yellow, a ruby is embedded in the pommel, and it manipulates lightning and electricity. This is Roy's weapon.

[edit] Status

Currently, Beginnings (book) remains unpublished, so not that many details will be given out just yet.
