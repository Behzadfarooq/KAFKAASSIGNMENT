Beg for Mercy is the 2003 debut album from G-Unit. After the success of Get Rich or Die Tryin', 50 Cent was granted his own label, G-Unit Records, on which he signed Lloyd Banks, Young Buck and Tony Yayo. As the album was recorded, however, Yayo was sentenced to jail on charges of gun possession, and so makes only two appearances on the entire record. Beg for Mercy sold 2.3 million in the U.S. and 4 million worldwide; the album has only one featured guest, singer, Joe.

[edit] Samples Used

  • "G-Unit" contains elements from "Million Dollars" by Triumvirat.
  • "Poppin' Them Thangs" contains uncredited samples from "Christmas Camel" by Procol Harum.
  • "My Buddy" contains elements from "Agony Or Ecstacy" by Ennio Morricone & audio clips from the motion picture Scarface.
  • "Stunt 101" contains uncredited samples from "Tobacco Road" by Tommy Youngblood.
  • "Wanna Get To Know You" contains elements from "Come Live With Me Angel" by Marvin Gaye.
  • "Groupie Love" contains elements from "Simply Beautiful" by Al Green.
  • "Betta Ask Somebody" contains replayed elements from "Blue Leopard".
  • "Footprints" contains elements from "Walk With Me" by Martha Bass.
  • "Eye For Eye" contains replayed elements from "Hello Love".
  • "Smile" contains elements from "I Too Am Waiting" by Syreeta.
  • "Salute U" contains elements from "Brandenburg Concerto #1 in F Major (Allego Moderato)" performed by Bach.
  • "Beg For Mercy" contains elements from "Back Down" by 50 Cent from Get Rich or Die Tryin'.
  • "Lay You Down" contains elements from "Doctor Marvello" by Klaatu.
  • "I Smell Pussy" contains elements from "The Greatest Sex" by R. Kelly.
