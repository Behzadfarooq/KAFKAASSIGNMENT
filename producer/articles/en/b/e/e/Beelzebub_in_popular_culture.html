Beelzebub in popular culture

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Beelzebub may mean:

  • Beelzebub, a Judeo-Christian demon

[edit] In Literature

  • Lord of the Flies, a novel by William Golding
  • A character in Steven Brust's To Reign in Hell
  • A character in Eoin Colfer's The Wish List
  • The main character in the first series of G. I. Gurdjieff's All and Everything
  • A character in the manga Sand Land
  • Mentioned in Shakespeare's Macbeth

[edit] In Games

  • Baalzebul (Dungeons & Dragons)
  • In Diablo 2:Lord of Destruction
  • In Castlevania: Symphony of the Night, Beelzebub appears as a giant corpse covered by flies and maggots. In the Enemy List at the Library Shop, he is described as "Lord of Flies".
  • In Dragon Quest VIII: Journey of the Cursed King, a fly-like enemy called Beelzebuzz can be encountered.
  • In Bomberman 64: The Second Attack, an Astral Knight named Bulzeeb has his name derived from Beelzebub.
  • In Ragnarok online, as a new MVP monster, and also as a powerfull card.
  • In Final Fantasy II, there is an optional boss enemy in Castle Pandamonium named Beezlebub, looking like a large insect.

[edit] In Music

  • In the song "Bohemian Rhapsody" by Queen there is a line that says "Beelzebub has a devil put aside for me" during the opera section.
  • ex-King Crimson drummer Bill Bruford 's first solo album's first track is entitled Beelzebub.
  • The disk to Therion's album Theli has Collin de Plancy's Beelzebub depiction from the Dictionnaire Infernal on it.
  • "Bus to Beelzebub", song on the Soul Coughing album Ruby Vroom
  • "Beelzeboss", a song from the soundtrack of the movie "Tenacious D in The Pick of Destiny".
  • "Beelzebubba" album by The Dead Milkmen.
  • "Songs from Beelzebub", an album from the Japanese band Fake?.
  • The cuban-born artist Voltaire mentions Beelzebub in his song "When You're Evil"
  • In the song, "The Pit", moe. mentions Beelzebub.
