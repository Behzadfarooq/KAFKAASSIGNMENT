Beef Wellington is a Canadian professional wrestler known for his appearances in Combat Zone Wrestling as one half of the tag team Team Masturbation with Excalibur. He has also wrestled for UWA Hardcore, Jersey All Pro Wrestling, and International Wrestling Syndicate.

[edit] Career

Beef Wellington made his wrestling debut in 2001. He received a lot of exposure in 2004 at CZW's Cage of Death VI, losing a match to Kid Kamikaze. He spent 2005 in CZW competing in a variety of matches. He entered the Tournament of Death, but was eliminated in the first round by Brain Damage. At Down with the Sickness, he wrestled Excalibur. The two then began teaming together under the name Team Masturbation, and would make masturbation references for comedy in their matches. At the next show, they wrestled together for the first time in CZW and won against DJ Hyde and Jon Dahmer. Team Masturbation gained popularity among CZW fans, leading Beef and Excalibur to refer to them as "little masturbators". At Point of No Return, Beef and Excalibur got a shot at the CZW World Tag Team Championship against the Kings of Pro-Wrestling. Team Masturbation came close to winning, but ultimately were defeated. Beef and Excalibur teamed together for the last time in CZW at Cage of Death 7. There, they lost an interpromotional match to Larry Sweeney and Franky The Mobster which saw each team give lengthy promos before the wrestling took place. After this, Excalibur had some backstage issues with CZW and was released. However, in September 2006, it was announced by CZW that Excalibur would return to CZW to reunite with Beef Wellington as Team Masturbation to face the Kings of Pro-Wrestling in the first round of the Last Team Standing Tournament to crown new tag champions. They were eliminated in short fashion.

Beef also wrestles for many other promotions. He competed in CHIKARA's Tag World Grand Prix 2005 and wrestles for International Wrestling Syndicate where he holds victories over Chris Hero and Super Dragon.

At the moment, Team Masturbation is broken up. But the future looks bright for Wellington as a singles wrestler, with numerous northeast independent promotions coveting his services, including Jersey All-Pro Wrestling.
