"Beethoven (I Love to Listen to)" is a song recorded by British pop music duo Eurythmics. It was written by group members Annie Lennox and David A. Stewart and produced by Stewart. The song was released as the first single in the UK from the duo's Savage album. Although not released as a single in the United States, the track appeared as a double A-side of the 12 inch single for "I Need a Man".

Lennox as the housewife
Lennox as the vixen

The Savage album returned Eurythmics to electronic experimentation and the song itself is mostly spoken-word from Lennox, the most noticeable exception being the repeated phrase "I love to" throughout the track. The lyrics portray a battle between voices in the head of a housewife suffering from mental disorders.

Music videos were produced for all twelve tracks on the Savage album, with "Beethoven", "I Need a Man", and "You Have Placed a Chill in My Heart" connected as a video trilogy following the housewife, who displays characteristics of dissociative identity disorder or split personalities (which is often confused with schizophrenia) as she transforms into a disco vixen and then into a third androgynous figure.

As the first part of the trilogy, the "Beethoven" video begins with Lennox, as the repressed housewife, knitting in her apartment. She exhibits characteristics of obsessive-compulsive disorder through her habitual cleaning and chopping of vegetables. She then suffers a mental breakdown and trashes the apartment. As one of her dormant split personalities surface, makes herself up as the sexually extroverted diva, and walks out onto the street laughing.

Not released as a single in the U.S., "Beethoven" still received heavy play on MTV.
