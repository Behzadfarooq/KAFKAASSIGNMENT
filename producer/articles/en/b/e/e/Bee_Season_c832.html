Bee Season is a 2000 novel (ISBN 0-385-49879-9) by Myla Goldberg. A feature film based on the book, directed by Scott McGehee and David Siegel and written by Naomi Foner, was released in 2005.

[edit] Plot introduction

This novel charts the story of a girl with an extraordinary spelling gift who comes from a Jewish family with its roots in Kabbalism.

[edit] Plot summary

Spoiler warning: Plot and/or ending details follow.

While the literal plot simply follows a girl from a somewhat dysfunctional family moving through the world of competitive spelling, the actual plot of Bee Season is a much more complex one on personal religious views. The father can be seen a Kabbalistic figure, desperately trying to become closer to God, though instead of using knowledge (as one does in Kabbalah), Saul uses each of his family members to deepen his own religious sense. Meanwhile, his wife Miriam is caught stealing and is sent to a mental institution. The relationships between all members of the family have gone from relatively close and warm to very strained. A recurring theme in the book is Tikkun olam.

[edit] Characters in "Bee Season"

  • Saul – the father and cantor
  • Miriam – mother in the family
  • Eliza – the daughter who is good at spelling
  • Aaron – the son

[edit] Main themes

It explores the ideas of God-knowledge (shefa) described by the Kabbalist, Abraham Abulafia.

[edit] Film, TV or theatrical adaptations

Spoiler warning: Plot and/or ending details follow.

The movie generally follows the plot of Goldberg's novel, but with some notable changes. In the novel, Eliza did not get second place in the national spelling bee, though she did well. It was not until months later, when it came time at her school spelling bee to make her second attempt at the National Spelling Bee, that Eliza decided to get a word wrong on purpose. Also Chali, the woman who introduces Aaron to the Hare Krishna religion in the movie, is actually a man in the book. Instead of being a college professor, Saul is a cantor in the novel. The setting is moved from Pennsylvania to California for the movie, and is modernized from the 1970s.

Spoilers end here.
