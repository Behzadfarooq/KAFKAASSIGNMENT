Beer snake

Beer Snake is a term used to describe the stacking of numerous plastic beer cups to form a "snake." Beer Snakes are most commonly found at sporting events that are played out over many hours, such as Cricket. Some snakes have been reported in the media as being up to 23 m long. They are typically formed during breaks in play, for example when the 4th test of the Pakistani cricket team in England in 2006 tour at The Oval was halted after ball tampering allegations, a large beer snake was constructed in the OCS stand.

[edit] Materials

A beer snake is made with a large number of empty plastic beer cups, usually those issued by the bars on site at the stadium. Glass is not used for safety reasons, but also as plastic glasses make the snake flexible.

[edit] Procedure

As such a large number of cups are needed, gathering normally occurs with a large group of people. The cups are simply stacked within each other.

When the snake has reached the desired length the snake is picked up. Snake length is often restricted by the width of the bay of seats as anything longer will protrude into the aisle. One solution to this is to team up with other groups and link your snakes together. One of the biggest challenges is trying to keep it in one piece.
