Beeswing

Beeswing (1833-1854) by Dr. Syntax out of Ardrossan Mare (by Ardrossan), was a 19th century champion racehorse from the north of England. She raced at many venues between 1835 and 1842 and was a real crowd favourite. Beeswing was hailed as the greatest Mare in Britain.

Her sire was described by the noted racing writer "The Druid" as "...scarcely fifteen hands, very broad at the base of the nose, with open nostrils, an eye full and bright as a hawk's, a high, drooping rump, and on the side view rather short quartered. He was quite a mouse in his colour." But he took the Preston Gold Cup seven times, the Richmond Gold Cup five times, and the The Lancaster Gold Cup five times as well. How many races he started in is unknown. On both her dam and her sire's side, Beeswing went back to the immortal Eclipse.

Entering 64 events, she won an incredible 51 times. Of the 57 races she finished, she was placed lower than 2nd on only one occasion.

Her most noteble victory was the 1842 Ascot Gold Cup. Her other notable wins were:

  • The Newcastle Cup, 6 times
  • The Doncaster Cup, 4 times
  • The Craven Stakes, 3 times
  • The Fitzwilliam Stakes, 3 times
  • The Ashton Stakes
  • The Champagne Stakes
  • The Cleveland Stakes
  • The Gold Cup
  • The Gold Shield
  • The Hornsby Castle Stakes
  • The Lancaster Cup
  • The Northallerton Gold Cup (twice)
  • The Queen's Plate (twice)
  • The Queen's Purse
  • The Newcastle St. Leger
  • The Stockton Cup

After 1842, four of her foals went on to become top class runners, two of which were Classic winners. Many of today's top racehorses can trace their genealogy back to Beeswing.
