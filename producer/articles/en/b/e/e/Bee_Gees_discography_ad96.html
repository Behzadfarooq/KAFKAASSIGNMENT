  • The first LP is titled "Inception," whereas the second is titled "Nostalgia."

[edit] Robin's Reign [Robin]

Polydor, 1970

Side 1

  • August October
  • Gone Gone Gone

  • Give Me A Smile
  • Down Came The Sun
  • Mother And Jack

Side 2

  • Saved By The Bell
  • Weekend
  • Farmer Ferdinand Hudson
  • Lord Bless All
  • Most Of My Life
  • One Million Years

Album Notes

  • "One Million Years" was not originally part of the album, but was added to the German LP and as well as the CD release.
  • The released version of "Farmer Ferdinand Hudson" is a mere fragment of a much longer orchestral piece entitled "Hudson's Fallen Wind." Fortunately, a recording of the full-length piece managed to escape into the collector's circuit, unfortunately marred by several instances of a skipping needle.
  • Much to Barry's dismay, Maurice contributed bass and (allegedly) piano to this album.

[edit] The Kid's No Good [Barry]

Polydor, 1970 (unreleased)

Side 1

  • Mando Bay
  • One Bad Thing
  • The Day Your Eyes Meet Mine
  • Happiness
  • Peace In My Mind
  • Clyde O'Reilly

Side 2

  • I Just Wanna Take Care Of You
  • I'll Kiss Your Memory
  • The Victim
  • This Time
  • What's It All About
  • Born

Album Notes

  • No definite title is known, but this is the title constantly given to Barry's 1970 album.
  • This is most likely the intended lineup (though some sources swap "Mando Bay" and "Born"); however, bootleg versions add a number of extraneous bonus tracks. It seems obvious that "Born" was intended to be the final track, as it ends with a long, anthemic ending that would've made an appropriate finale to the LP.
  • Despite a bootleg claiming to be on the Polydor label, no official CD release has appeared as of yet.
  • "I'll Kiss Your Memory" and "This Time" were released on a single, which failed to chart.

[edit] 2 Years On

Polydor, 1970

Side 1

  • 2 Years On
  • Portrait Of Louise
  • Man For All Seasons
  • Sincere Relation
  • Back Home
  • The First Mistake I Made

Side 2

  • Lonely Days
  • Alone Again
  • Tell Me Why
  • Lay It On Me
  • Every Second, Every Minute
  • I'm Weeping

Album Notes

  • With this album, Robin returned, and remained a member until the group's final split in 2001.
  • Barry's participation in this album was somewhat limited, as he was still planning to release "The Kid's No Good." Robin was also continuing work on his solo album during the time that this album was being recorded.
  • Publicity material featured an alternate running order: (Side A) 2 Years On / Lonely Days / Lay It On Me / Portrait Of Louise / Man For All Seasons / The First Mistake I Made; (Side B) I'm Weeping / Every Second, Every Minute / Sincere Relation / Back Home / Alone Again / Tell Me Why.

[edit] Trafalgar

Polydor, 1971

Side 1

  • How Can You Mend A Broken Heart
  • Israel
  • The Greatest Man In The World
  • It's Just The Way
  • Remembering
  • Somebody Stop The Music

Side 2

  • Trafalgar
  • Don't Wanna Live Inside Myself
  • When Do I
  • Dearest
  • Lion In Winter
  • Walking Back To Waterloo

Album Notes

  • This LP marked the debut of a new member, Geoff Bridgeford.
  • Maurice is the only brother to appear on the title track.
  • At one point, this album was going to be a double LP set, featuring 20 songs. However, the contents of this unreleased version have never been revealed.
  • A rough mix sent to Atlantic featured the following track list: (Side A) How Can You Mend A Broken Heart / Israel / The Greatest Man In The World / It's Just The Way / Don't Wanna Live Inside Myself / Country Woman / Somebody Stop The Music; (Side B) Trafalgar / We Lost The Road / Dearest / When Do I / Lion In Winter / Remembering / Walking Back To Waterloo. Note the inclusion of 'Country Woman' and 'We Lost The Road', neither of which appeared on the final album.
  • The cover art features another alternate lineup: (Side A) How Can You Mend A Broken Heart / Trafalgar / The Greatest Man In The World / Remembering / When Do I / Dearest; (Side B) It's Just The Way / Walking Back To Waterloo / Somebody Stop The Music / Israel / Lion In Winter / Don't Wanna Live Inside Myself.

Album Notes

[edit] The Supernaturals (sndtrk) [Maurice]

unknown label, 1985 (unreleased)

  • Score 01
  • Score 02
  • Score 03
  • Score 04
  • Score 05
  • Score 06
  • Score 07
  • Score 08
  • Score 09
  • Score 10
  • Score 11
  • Score 12
  • Score 13

Album Notes

  • Possibly the most mysterious album in the Bee Gees' catalog. It is unknown if Maurice's score for the obscure thriller "The Supernaturals" (in which he has a cameo) was actually meant for release, and certainly, the lack of known song titles seems to disprove this. However, the soundtrack eventually appeared on CD in the United States, with a cover note describing it as a 'fan club release', though which fan club exactly is not known. The tracks on the CD (titled exactly as seen above) are obviously taken directly from the film.
  • Incidentally, Maurice's score was not even used in the film, and was replaced with a critically-acclaimed score by Robert O. Ragland. However, rare VHS copies do exist with Maurice's original music.

[edit] My Favorite Christmas Carols [Robin]

Edel, 2006

  • Mother Of Love
  • In The Bleak Midwinter
  • O Come All Ye Faithful
  • Silent Night
  • God Rest Ye Merry Gentlemen
  • Good King Wenceslas
  • Away In A Manger
  • Once In Royal David's City
  • I Saw Three Ships
  • Hark! The Herald Angels Sing
  • The First Noel
  • Come Some Christmas Eve Or Halloween
  • Ellan Vannin
  • Christmas Medley

Album Notes

  • Only the original German release had 14 tracks; later releases are mysteriously missing "Mother Of Love," "Come Some Christmas Eve Or Halloween," "Ellan Vannin," and the closing medley.
  • "The First Noel" has been partially rewritten as an anti-war protest ballad.
  • "Come Some Christmas Eve Or Halloween" is actually the original Bee Gees recording from 1968, which also appeared in the "Studio Albums 1967-68" boxed set.
  • The title of the US release was shortened to MY FAVORITE CAROLS, and credited to 'Robin Gibb of the Bee Gees.'
  • All editions feature a bonus video, which includes an interview with Robin and footage from the album's recording sessions.

[edit] Scrapped Ideas

[edit] Modulating Maurice [Maurice and Ringo Starr]

unknown label, 1969

Included

  • Modulating Maurice

Album Notes

  • In early 1969, Maurice contributed spoken-word 'vocals' (in fact, just random phrases) to a bizarre synthesizer album that was being recorded by his neighbor, Ringo Starr. Nothing ever became of it, and it is rumored that the recordings no longer exist.
  • Despite popular belief, the track "Modulating Maurice" has not been bootlegged. (Bootleggers attempted to be clever by giving this title to an unreleased Bee Gees song from 1970, actually entitled "To Dance Again.")


[edit] untitled Bee Gees album (1977)

RSO, 1977

Included

  • If I Can't Have You
  • Night Fever
  • Warm Ride
  • More Than A Woman
  • (Our Love) Don't Throw It All Away
  • How Deep Is Your Love
  • Stayin' Alive

Album Notes

  • The Bee Gees were at work on this album when Robert Stigwood approached them about contributing songs for "Saturday Night Fever." Thus, four of these recordings were used on the soundtrack of that film, and "If I Can't Have You" was re-recorded for the film by Yvonne Elliman. Andy would record "Don't Throw It All Away" for his SHADOW DANCING album, and "Warm Ride" for AFTER DARK.
