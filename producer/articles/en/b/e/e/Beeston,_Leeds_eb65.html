Beeston, Leeds

Beeston is an area of inner-city south Leeds, West Yorkshire, England. It is a mixed but largely working-class area, with - unusually for south Leeds - a sizable south Asian population. Most of the housing is of nineteenth-century stock, varying from small back-to-back terraces to larger terraces and semi-detached houses.

Crime figures do not bear Beeston's reputation of being a notorious crime hot-spot as it is no worse or better than the majority of Leeds districts. The areas reputation was not helped when it emerged that three of the July 7 London bombers came from the area and the media painted the area as black as possible. There was little reporting of the remembrance and peace marches that the community organised.

Beeston itself has two distinct areas - Beeston Hill which is home to the majority Asian and ethnic population and which has been a run down area but is getting some much needed investment in the early part of the 21st century - and Beeston Village/Beeston Park which, whilst still working class and mostly terrace housing, is largely a white and more afluent area. There does however seem to be an emergence of young professionals to the area.

Despite its reputation, Beeston probably has the best facilities of any area in Leeds. It has two large health centres, the White Rose shopping centre on its doorstep, Elland Road stadium, South Leeds Stadium which includes the indoor bowling, indoor Tennis Centre, athletics stadium, rugby pitches and five a side pitches and a full size swimming pool (due late 2006). The city centre of Leeds is only a short bus ride away and the M1 and M62 motorways are easily accessed.
