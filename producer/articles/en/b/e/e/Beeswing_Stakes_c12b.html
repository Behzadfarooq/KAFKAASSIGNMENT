The Beeswing Stakes is a flat horse race in the United Kingdom for three-year-old and above thoroughbreds run over a distance of 7 furlongs (1,408 metres) at Newcastle Racecourse in late July.

It is currently run as a handicap (the Beeswing Handicap) and is a relatively unremarkable race, but prior to 2000 it was a conditions race which held Group 3 status. The race takes its name from Beeswing, a successful 19th century racehorse bred in Northumberland.
