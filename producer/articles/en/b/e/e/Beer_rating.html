Rating beer is an activity that combines the enjoyment of beer drinking with the hobby of collecting. People drink beer and then record their scores and comments on various internet websites. This is a worldwide activity and people in the USA will swap bottles of beer with people living in far-flung locales such as New Zealand and Russia. A beer rater's scores are aggregated to create lists of the best or most popular beers in each country as well as the most highly rated beers in the world. There are several of these beer rating sites—the most notable being RateBeer.com and BeerAdvocate.com.

[edit] RateBeer

RateBeer was founded in May 2000 by Bill Buchanan as a forum for beer lovers to share their opinions of beer, brewers and places to buy and enjoy beer. In addition, users can write opinions of cider, mead and sake.

In June 2000, the Canadian beer writer Josh Oakes joined RateBeer and soon became editor-in-chief. Finally, in June 2001, the web-site consultant Joe Tucker joined, eventually assuming full ownership of RateBeer.

As of March, 2007, RateBeer has 1,112,000 ratings, making RateBeer the most complete and up-to-date source for online beer information on the Web.

RateBeer has become a useful database of consumer-driven beer ratings with features on beer culture and industry events, weekly beer-related editorials, and an internationally recognized, semi-annual RateBeer Best competition. RateBeer now has about 50,000 members, from more than 60 countries, who have rated tens of thousands of beers from around the world.

RateBeer lists nearly 60,000 beers from 7,270 breweries and has grown to become the largest beer aficionado web site in the world in addition to serving as a forum for discussions about the craft brewing industry.

BeerAdvocate was founded in 2000 by Todd and Jason Alström. There are over 425,000 beer reviews in BeerAdvocate's database contributed by registered members in the past six-plus years.

Recent controversy erupted when it was discovered by several Beer Advocate members that their private "beermails" (an internal email system) to each other were in fact being read by the Alströms. Several members were deleted and several quit on their own accord.

As of March 2007, BeerAdvocate claims 100,000 members active in the past year, nearly 30,000 of whom have used the site in the past three months, though recent coding changes prevent independent verification. BeerAdvocate.com carries no advertisements. Users are required to complete a free registration and email verification process before being able to access basic functions like searching for reviews of beers or bars.

The Alström brothers organize three Beer festivals a year in Boston. These events are attended by brewers and the public, and new and limited-release beers are often introduced. They contribute a beer column in Boston's The Weekly Dig, as well as their new BeerAdvocate magazine. An Alström-owned brewpub, Purgatory Ale House, has been in the works for several years but as yet no space, capital, or beer has been allocated. Several test batches of beer were brewed 2 years ago and are evidently still lagering, awaiting the opening of the brewpub.

[edit] The Oxford Bottled Beer Database

The Oxford Bottled Beer Database was founded in 1992 by Sparks Computer Solutions Ltd. and is probably the oldest of the Internet beer rating sites. As of February, 2007, Oxford lists approximately 2,700 beers.
