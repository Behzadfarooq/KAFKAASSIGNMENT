Beer is the fifth episode of the BBC sitcom Blackadder II, the second series of Blackadder, which was set in Elizabethan England from 1558 to 1603.

[edit] Summary

An embarrassing incident with a turnip, an ostrich feather and a fanatically puritan aunt leads to a right royal to-do in the Blackadder household.

[edit] Plot

Spoiler warning: Plot and/or ending details follow.

Lord Blackadder's Puritan relatives, Lord and Lady Whiteadder, announce their plans to visit him to discuss his inheritance over dinner. Meanwhile, Blackadder mocks Lord Melchett who is suffering an agonizing hangover; in response Lord Melchett reminds Blackadder that "one drop of the ale and you fall flat on your face and start singing that song about the goblin." Blackadder denies this and challenges Lord Melchett to a drinking bout with ten thousand florins at stake.

When Blackadder returns home and tells Percy to start planning the guest list, Percy points out that they will have to include Lord and Lady Whiteadder since "they'll already be here." A panicked Blackadder attempts to hold both events the same evening but in different rooms (the drinking party, complete with "wall-to-wall vomiting," is of course to be held in Baldrick's bedroom). Despite his best efforts things go awry when Queenie ends up in the closet, Baldrick passes water, Lady Whiteadder has an embarrassing encounter with a thingy-shaped turnip, and Blackadder ends up with an ostrich feather down his pants singing "that song about the goblin." Fortunately, all concerned get drunk and soon forget their differences.

[edit] Trivia

  • This episode marks Hugh Laurie's first Blackadder appearance, and Miriam Margolyes' second.
  • Queenie's statement, "I may have the body of a weak and feeble woman, but I have the heart and stomach of a concrete elephant!" is an adaptation of Queen Elizabeth I's famous statement, "I may have the body of a weak and feeble woman, but I have the heart and stomach of a king, and of a king of England too."
  • The unique locations of this episode were a chamber in the castle where the hungover Melchett rested, and Baldrick's bedroom.
