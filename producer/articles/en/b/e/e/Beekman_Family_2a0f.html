Wilhelmus Beekman was the founder of the American branch of the family. He arrived in American in 1647 with Peiter Stuyvesant. He was a director in Dutch East India Company. His sons and daughter went on to wed some of the most prominent individuals involved in the colonisation of the New York / New Jersey, namely members of the Stuyvesant and Livingston family. Wilhelmus Beekman's son, Hendrick, founded the Beekman Patent via Royal Grant. Beekman family members can still be found in Dutchess County, NY, and Somerset County, NJ, and many street and places in New York proper hold the name, Beekman.
