"Beercan" is a single by Beck, taken from his first major record label release, Mellow Gold. It was produced by Karl Stephenson and featured his characteristic layers of sound and bizarre samples: this song in particular prominently features samples from a Care Bears album. Lyrically, Beercan talks about going to a party and being surrounded by 'freedom rock slimeballs talking in code and 'game show suckers.' The protagonist, who has just quit his job as a leafblower, is getting drunk to ditch himself and escape his worries.

Like most singles that Beck has released, Beercan continues to be played live often, usually as the last song before the encore, or one of the encores itself.
