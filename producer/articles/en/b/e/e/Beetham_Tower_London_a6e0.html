Beetham Tower London

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

This article or section contains information about expected future buildings or structures.
It is likely to contain information of a speculative nature, and the content may change as building construction begins and new information becomes available.


The Beetham Tower is a skyscraper being planned for the South Bank, London.

If built, it would be 180m high, with 51 storeys, making it one of the tallest buildings in the UK, and the tallest tower yet of property developer the Beetham Organisation. The height would make it taller than the famous Swiss Re office tower in the City of London. The architect is Ian Simpson, who has designed the 171 metre Beetham Tower in Manchester and the Beetham Tower in Birmingham.

It has undergone a redesign, as it was originally planned at 226m and 70 storeys. The design stays much the same, and the redesign was primarily to change the height.

There will be a viewing gallery at the top of the tower, which will have views right across London, higher than the London Eye. The Jumeriah hotel chain have already signed up as anchor tennants, wanting the building completed in 2011 in order to give them time to prepare for the coming London 2012 Olympics. This means the latest that construction could start is 2008. This tower is one of many schemes appearing across the UK by the Beetham Organisation.
