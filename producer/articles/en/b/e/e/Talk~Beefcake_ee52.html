I dispute that this term is used primarily by gay men. I've heard it often in various nongay pop culture outlets, for example South Park. I've not generally heard it in gay circles. Do you have a reference for this assertion? It _is_ used to suggest a man is attractive in a muscular way, similar to words like "hunk", but without any particular attachment to a demographic. --JoshuaRodman

I tend to agree. It might also be used by gay men, but in the UK I'm most familiar with the term being used in reference to individuals in male strip acts such as the Chippendales and similar acts inspired by The Full Monty. -- Solipsist 10:40, 9 Apr 2005 (UTC)
