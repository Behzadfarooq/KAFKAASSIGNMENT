Beedeville is a town in Jackson County, Arkansas, United States. The population was 105 at the 2000 census.

The current mayor of Beedeville is Wyant Beede. His ancestors founded Beedeville, which was originally named Beede. Once more people settled into the town, it became known as Beedeville. Mayor Beede had lived in Texas most of his life and moved to Beedeville about three years ago to be closer to his history. He and his wife Carolyn Beede run the Beedeville grocery store. The children of Beedeville attend Newport High School, which is a thirty minute bus ride. Due to the lack of population in Beedeville, there is no longer a school.

Mayor Wyant Beede received a grant from the State of Arkansas to build a playground for the children of Beedeville. The park was completed in the early months of 2006. The park's equipment includes a slide, swing set, jungle gym, pavillion, basketball goals, benches, sandbox, and a volleyball court. Since he has become mayor there is a park, a grocery store and a Cingular tower. Hershel Breckenridge said,"Mayor Beede has brought life back into this town, he has gotten the neighborhood to come together and start improving the town." Ruben Walton said,"Mayor Beede brought in a Cingular tower to bring in revenue for the town. I was excited knowing that I would finally be able to use my cell phone without having to stand in a certain pose." Wyant Beede and the residents of Beedeville came together last year to put together a town parade and carnival called Beedeville Days.

Mayor Wyant Beede threw a picnic in honor of a soldier who had returned from two oversea tours. That soldier was Sergeant Sam Walton, he had served in Egypt for eighteen months, and Iraq for eighteen months. Beedeville's mayor gave Sgt. Walton a plaque to thank him for his service to his country. Sgt. Walton was there with his family, his wife Gloria, his daughter Alicia, and his son Ruben. The event took place on November 6, 2004. Sgt. Sam Walton is also a volunteer fireman and has been for fourteen years.

The Beedeville Church of Christ often holds vacation bible school during the month of July. The church is almost always the destination of most devotionals. Residents of many other small towns in the area commute to the church for events.


[edit] Geography

Beedeville is located at 35°25′47″N, 91°6′31″W (35.429727, -91.108572)GR1. According to the United States Census Bureau, the town has a total area of 3.2 km² (1.2 mi²), all land.

[edit] Demographics

As of the censusGR2 of 2000, there were 105 people, 43 households, and 30 families residing in the town. The population density was 33.2/km² (86.2/mi²). There were 58 housing units at an average density of 18.4/km² (47.6/mi²). The racial makeup of the town was 95.24% White, 3.81% Black or African American and 0.95% Native American.

There were 43 households out of which 39.5% had children under the age of 18 living with them, 60.5% were married couples living together, 2.3% had a female householder with no husband present, and 30.2% were non-families. 23.3% of all households were made up of individuals and 9.3% had someone living alone who was 65 years of age or older. The average household size was 2.44 and the average family size was 2.83.

In the town the population was spread out with 29.5% under the age of 18, 7.6% from 18 to 24, 30.5% from 25 to 44, 19.0% from 45 to 64, and 13.3% who were 65 years of age or older. The median age was 34 years. For every 100 females there were 101.9 males. For every 100 females age 18 and over, there were 111.4 males.

The median income for a household in the town was $34,375, and the median income for a family was $37,500. Males had a median income of $24,500 versus $11,607 for females. The per capita income for the town was $24,995. There were 15.4% of families and 10.9% of the population living below the poverty line, including 14.3% of under eighteens and 4.8% of those over 64.
