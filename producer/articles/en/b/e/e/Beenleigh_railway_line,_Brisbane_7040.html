Beenleigh railway line, Brisbane

The Beenleigh railway line is a suburban railway line in Brisbane, Australia. It runs through Brisbane's southern suburbs and connects to Nerang and Robina on the Gold Coast via the Gold Coast line. The line is connected to the Ipswich Line via the Tennyson spur.

During peak hour times on weekdays, services run from the City to Corinda through Tennyson, branching at Yeerongpilly.

The Beenleigh line branches from the other southbound railway lines at Roma Street. It connects with the northern Ferny Grove line.

Currently, an extra track between Salisbury and Kuraby is currently being constructed, which will include the upgrading of stations for increased Beenleigh and Gold Coast services. It is expected to be completed by early 2008. [1]

[edit] Stations

During peak hour, some stations are skipped for faster travel times and to reduce overcrowding for peak-hr commuters working in Brisbane. In addition, some peak hour Gold Coast trains stop at Loganlea.

[edit] Stopping Patterns

  • all - all stations to Brisbane Central and v.v (with most services continuing to/from Ferny Grove)
  • A - (peak inbound only) - all stations to Coopers Plains, then express to Yeerongpilly, express to Park Road, then all stations to Brisbane Central (with some services continuing to Ferny Grove)
  • B - (peak inbound only) - all stations to Altandi, then express to Salisbury, then all stations to Brisbane Central (This service continues through to Nambour)
  • C - (peak outbound only) - all stations to Park Road, then express to Yeerongpilly, express to Coopers Plains, express to Sunnybank, then all stations to Beenleigh
  • D - (peak outbound only) - all stations to Park Road, then express to Yeerongpilly, express to Altandi, then all stations to Beenleigh.

Some peak services terminate at Bowen Hills.
