Beegden

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

The Church of Beegden

Beegden (51°11′N, 5°55′E) is a town in the Dutch province of Limburg. It is a part of the municipality of Maasgouw, and lies about 6 km west of Roermond. In 2001, Beegden had 1703 inhabitants. The built-up area of the town was 0.48 km², and contained 667 residences.[1]

Until 1991, Beegden was a separate municipality. During that year the town became part of the manicipalty of Heel en Panheel. In 2007 the manicipalty of Heel en Panheel, including Beegden, became part of the new manicipalty of Maasgouw. The town currently has about 1800 inhabitants. During the past few years toerism has developed in Beegden, the key attractions of the town are the church, the 17th centuary house Huis Nederhoven and the St. Lindert windmill. The last underwent a major restoration in 2000 and can be visited free of charge every Wednesday and Saturday.
