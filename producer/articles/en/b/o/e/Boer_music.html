Boer music

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Boer music is a type of South African instrumental folk music. Its original intent was to be an accompaniment to social dancing at parties and festivals. Boer music is originally European, but once it was brought to South Africa, it gradually changed and became its own style of Boer music. It is thought to have got to South Africa by British Empire military bands. There is no one way to describe the original and unique sounds of Boer music. Every artist or band comes up with their own recognizable sound and style. They come up with their own style by playing different types of concertinas and using different instruments in the band. A concertina is similar to an accordion and is the lead instrument in most Boer music bands. There are many different types of concertinas, which is how the Boer music has so many different unique sounds and styles, and the construction the concertina is what makes the different sounds in the Boer music band. Depending on where the slots and holes are put makes the difference on the sound that the concertina makes. Other instruments that might be in a Boer music band would be piano accordions, button harmonicas, accordions, pianos, harmoniums and the guitar, and sometimes, a cello or bass guitar may be seen, too.
