Bogdanov

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Bogdanov (Богданов), or Bogdanova (Богданова; feminine) is a common Russian last name and may refer to:

  • Alexander Bogdanov (1873-1928), Russian physician, philosopher, economist, writer, revolutionary, and author of Red Star
  • Alexander Bogdanov (1951-1984), a Soviet army officer and Hero of the Soviet Union
  • Alexei Bogdanov (1907-?), a Soviet geologist
  • Anatoly Bogdanov (1834-1896), a Russian anthropologist and zoologist
  • Anatoly Ivanovich Bogdanov (1932-?), a Soviet sportsman and rifle-shooter
  • Andrei Bogdanov (1692-1766), one of the first Russian bibliographers
  • The Bogdanov Affair, arising from the allegedly pseudoscientific physics papers of Igor and Grichka Bogdanov
  • Boris Osipovich Bogdanov (1884-1960), Russian Menshevik revolutionary and a leader of the February Revolution
  • Michael Bogdanov (born 1938), British stage director who specialized in interpretations of Shakespeare's plays
  • Mikhail Bogdanov (1914-?), a Soviet cinema art director
  • Modest Bogdanov (1841-1888), a Russian ornitologist and zoogeographer
  • Nadezhda Bogdanova (1836-1897), a Russian ballet dancer
  • Nikolai Bogdanov-Belsky (1868-1945), a Russian painter
  • Pavel Bogdanov (1871-1919), a Russian bibliographer
  • Pyotr Bogdanov (1882-1939], a Soviet statesman
  • Pyotr Bogdanov (1920-1945), a Soviet army officer and Hero of the Soviet Union
  • Semyon Bogdanov (1894-1960), a Soviet marshal of armored units and double Hero of the Soviet Union
  • Vasili Bogdanov (1837-1886), a Russian poet
  • Vladimir Bogdanov, President of Surgutneftegaz, Russia's second largest oil company
  • Yelliy Bogdanov (1872-1931), one of the founders of the Soviet zootechnics
