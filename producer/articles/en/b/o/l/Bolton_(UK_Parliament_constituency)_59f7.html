Bolton (UK Parliament constituency)

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Bolton
Borough constituency
Created: 1832
Abolished: 1950
Type: House of Commons

Bolton was a Borough constituency in the county of Lancashire of the House of Commons for the Parliament of the United Kingdom. Created by the Reform Act of 1832, it was represented by two Members of Parliament. The constituency was abolished in 1950, being split into east and west divisions.
