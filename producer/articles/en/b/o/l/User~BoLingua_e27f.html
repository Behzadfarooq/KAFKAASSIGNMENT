Hello, Tjena, Hola, Guten tag, Bonjour!

I'm a student of Linguistics at Brigham Young University. I'm currently leaning toward Germanic languages, and am studying German and Swedish. I'm looking into Anglo-Saxon, mostly so I can participate in the Englisc Wikipedia.

I'm married, and my wife, baby and I want to go live overseas, either in Europe or East Asia. First preference for my wife is France. My second preference is anywhere but Utah. I was born in Idaho, moved around the western United States most of my life, and spent two years in France. Bo is a nickname from my father and Lingua, of course is from my propensity to languages and linguistics.

I'm looking to finish a degree in Linguistics, and to teaching French, German, Spanish and Creative Writing on the High School level. That is, if I can ever finish a bachelor's degree.

I currently participate heavily in two sister wiki projects, the Alternate History Wiki and the Ill Bethisad Wiki.

I don't seem to have any particular pattern as to the particular pages that I choose to edit, however I do keep track of those I do edit. I guess I could say that I peruse the wiki, and when I find information that grabs my interest, I keep an eye on it to see how each article progresses.

I like this quote:

"There can never be a conflict between true science and true religion, because they both describe reality."
