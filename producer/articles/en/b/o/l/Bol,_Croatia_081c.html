Bol
Bol (Croatia)

Bol is a small town on the south of the island of Brač in the Split-Dalmatia county of Croatia, population 1,661 (2001).

Bol is renowned for its beach the Zlatni rat ("Golden cape"). It is a promontory composed mostly of pebble rock that shifts with the tidal movement. The water at Zlatni rat is quite crystalline. The rocks beneath the boat that are 30 feet down they look only an arm's length away, and there are spectacular pine trees that grow down the middle of Zlatni rat (there is a beach on either side of the horn). Bol itself is a very popular tourist destination and has a number of harbourside bars and restaurants.
