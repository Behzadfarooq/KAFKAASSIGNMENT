Boldklubben Frem (also known as Frem, BK Frem or BK Frem Copenhagen) are a Danish sports club based in the district of Sydhavnen, in Copenhagen. It is best known for its semi-professional football team. Since its foundation in 1886 Frem has won the Danish Championships six times and the Danish Cup twice. Until the disastrous bankruptcy in 1993 Frem had played in the top division in all but six seasons.

The club also has a youth and amateur amateur football branch, as well as a cricket team. It is also involved in the running of the KIES sports boarding school.

[edit] History

See also: Boldklubben Frem seasons

BK Frem was founded July 17, 1886 under the name of Fremskridtsklubbens Cricketklub by a group of young men from the party of Venstre. In 1887 football was introduced and the name was changed to Boldklubben Frem, stiftet af Fremskridtsklubben (lit. Danish:The Ball Club Frem, founded by the Progress Club).

In the early years Frem were located on Østerbro in Copenhagen, playing its matches at Blegdamsfælleden, alongside its main rivals in the early years; AB and KB. In 1905 Frem moved to a its own field at Enghavevej, Vesterbro, obtaining its current working class profile. To this day, both the Social Democrats and the Union of Metalworkers holds Frem sponsorships.

In 1942 Frem moved to its current facilities at Valby Idrætspark.

Players from Frem made up most of the Copenhagen XI for the Inter-Cities Fairs Cup played between 1958 and 1960, and the representative team even played in BK Frem's strip.

[edit] Professional football and fiscal problems (1978-present)

Since 1983 Frem has been battling economically, peaking in 1993 when they saw themselves demoted to the Danmarksserien following a bankruptcy, which was due to a debt of dkk 8.5 mill. and allegations of fraud.[2]

Following a short but expensive spell in the Superliga in 2003-2004 Frem were once again on the verge of bankruptcy with a debt of dkk 10 mill.[3]
