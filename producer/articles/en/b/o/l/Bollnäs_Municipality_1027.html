Bollnäs Municipality is a Municipality in Gävleborg County, in east central Sweden. Its seat is located in Bollnäs (pop. 13,000).

[edit] Geography

The town is located alongside river Ljusnan, which also follows the path of the northern main line railway (Norra Stambanan). Some kilometer south, Ljusnan is also intersected by river Voxnan.

Around the town are several water areas with fish such as trout, brass and pikes. The streams Ljusnan and Voxnan also offer fishing.

There is also a possibility for fishing in the town Bollnäs, in the tributary lake Vågen.

Natures reserves: Grossjöberget, Galvån, Kölberget, Hästhagsberget, Djupsjön-Römmaberget.

[edit] History

Bollnäs church was built in the 14th century, but the tower is from 1460.

In written sources Bollnäs is traced from 1312, by a vicar named Ingemund who referred to it as Baldenaes which means "the large isthmus" and referring to the isthmus into a nearby lake.

The city of Bollnäs received its privileges in 1942 when the towns of Bollnäs and Björkhamre merged. Bollnäs is a station along the Northern Railline (Norra Stambanan) but has never served any higher purposes more than as a centre of trade for the surrounding lands.

The current size of municipality was created in 1974 with the reform then, when the Swedish city Bollnäs was merged with three smaller municipalities, and the "city" per se was abolished into the municipality.


[edit] Famous figures

  • Finnish writer Timo K. Mukka (1944 - 1973) was born in Bollnäs, to where his family had been evacuated from the Lapland region during the Lapland War.
