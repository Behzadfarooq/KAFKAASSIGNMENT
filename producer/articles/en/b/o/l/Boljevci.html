Boljevci

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Boljevci (Serbian Cyrillic: Бољевци) is a suburban settlement of Belgrade, the capital of Serbia. It is located in Belgrade's municipality of Surčin.

[edit] Location

Boljevci is located on the left bank of the Sava river, in the south-central part of the municipality, in the Syrmia region (the sub-region of Podlužje, east of the Bojčinska woods). It is 12 kilometers south of its municipal seat, Surčin, and some 30 kilometers of downtown Belgrade. It is located on the road which connects Surčin to Progar (through Jakovo) and coninues further into the province of Vojvodina (the village of Kupinovo).

[edit] Population

Boljevci is statistically classified as a rural settlement (village). It had a steady population growth until 1960s when the population began to fluctuate. Population of Boljevci according to the official population censuses:

  • 1921 - 2,179
  • 1931 - 2,802
  • 1953 - 3,641
  • 1971 - 4,008
  • 1981 - 3,974
  • 1991 - 4,284
  • 2002 - 4,056

Ethnic structure (2002): Serbs 2,653 (65,41%), Slovaks 1,132 (27,91%), Yugoslavs 40 (0,98%).

[edit] Characteristics

Boljevci is located in the marshy floodplain of the Sava. A monastery Fenek is located to the north-east so as the pond named after the monastery ("Fenečka bara"). On the opposite side, west of Boljevci, is a larger pond of Živača, used for the "Boljevci" fish pond.

Until early 1960s Boljevci had its own municipality which was then annexed to the municipality of Surčin.
