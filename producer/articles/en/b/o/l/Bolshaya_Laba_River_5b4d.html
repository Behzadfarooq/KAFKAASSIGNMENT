Bolshaya Laba River

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Bolshaya Laba River (Russian: Больша́я Ла́ба), or Big Laba River, is a 214 km long river in North Caucasus, Russia. It is a left tributary of the Kuban River, which it meets in Ust-Labinsk. It is used for irrigation and timber floating. It is also suitable for rafting. It flows through Krasnodar Krai and the Republic of Adygea.

Malaya Laba River (Ма́лая Ла́ба) is a river in North Caucasus, a tributary of the Bolshaya Laba. It is also used for rafting.
