Bonnie Brown (born Bonnie Jean Brown on July 31, 1937 in Sparkman, Arkansas) is an American country music singer.

From a musical family, in 1955 Bonnie Brown joined her older sister Maxine Brown, and brother Jim Ed Brown to form the musical group, The Browns. Signed by RCA Records in 1956, the highly successful trio were members of the Grand Ole Opry in Nashville, Tennessee until disbanding in 1967.
