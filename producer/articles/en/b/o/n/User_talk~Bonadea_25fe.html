[edit] Non-Biased Articles

Hello!

Quote: “NPOV requires views to be represented without bias. All editors and all sources have biases. A bias is a prejudice in a general or specific sense, usually in the sense of having a predilection for one particular point of view or ideology. One is said to be biased if one is influenced by one's biases. A bias could, for example, lead one to accept or not accept the truth of a claim, not because of the strength of the claim itself, but because it does or does not correspond to one's own preconceived ideas.”

I would like to bring to your attention the bias in the Final Cut Pro article.

Quote from article: “Final Cut Pro is a professional non-linear editing system created by Apple Computer that allows users to edit video.”

Correction – Final Cut Pro is a consumer to prosumer product.

Quote from article: “From the early 2000s, Final Cut has developed a large user base and is the first low cost mass market product to seriously challenge the monopoly of Avid editing products - the most well-established non linear editing system.”

Corrections: Yes, Final Cut Pro is developing a larger user group, but not to the point it is ‘seriously’ challenging the monopoly of Avid. Using this logic, it’s safe to say Windows Movie maker is seriously challenging the monopoly of Avid. Avid have over 2800 feature film editing seats. Final Cut have under 100. Yup, that’s challenging Avid’s Monopoly.

Quote from article: “As such, it has driven down the cost of professional video editing and has created a new class of product in professional applications – the desktop editor.”

Corrections: I’m sorry, but if you expect this to be true, I’m a monkey’s uncle. Real professional editing systems, like that of Avid Symphony, cost in and around $2,300,000. Prices of video, and all other technology are going down not because of Apple, but because this technology is getting cheaper.

If you want to be neutral, you would have no problem leaving in the negative aspects of Final Cut Pro (which out rule the positives). We are trying to educate, not land a job with Apple as a sales manager.

Clearly you are trying to land a job as an Avid sales manager. It's galling that you accuse more balanced users of your own agenda specific behavior. --FBFP 09:18, 31 December 2006
