Bonniconlon, County Mayo

Bonniconlon (or Bunnyconnellan) is a village in County Mayo, in the Republic of Ireland. The village is on the R294 to the east of Ballina, County Mayo, and close to the Ox Mountains.

Bonniconlon (Mhuine Conlon) was located in Sligo when the Sligo border went as far as one shore of the moy in Ballina. It is located in Mayo since the border was changed back to the Ox Mountains Windy Gap. The Howley family originated in Bonniconlon and were Catholic land agents, famous for building Beleek Castle in Ballina. Though the local population is relatively small, it is a vibrant community which each year organizes the the largest agricultural show west of the Shannon. The Bonniconlon Agricultural Show attracts large numbers of visitors to the area. A recent development of 48 houses and one-off housing between the village and Ballina has boosted population numbers in the village.

The village has an old church, a GAA hall and playing pitch, a town hall, three pubs, three shops and a graveyard. Judges Bar & Shop in Bonniconlon is a location in the small film Black Velvet. The old school house used to be used for local meetings and events was located between the newer school and the church. The roof still had bullet marks left by the black and tans during their local rampages. The rosery priest, Father Peyton (1909 - 1992) was an Altar boy in the local church. The Fr. Peyton Centre was built in his honour and is located in Attymass near Bonniconlon.

The local church is quite old and is older than the cathederal in Ballina. Bonniconlon (or Kilgarvan) parish is in the Achonry Diocese which crosses into Sligo. The Local Bofield Ceili Band sometimes plays at the local church. There is a monument to the O'Dowd family in the village for their work during the famine. It is located close to the church and was paid for by the local people. Famine walks are also available from Bonniconlon.
