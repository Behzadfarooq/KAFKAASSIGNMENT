[edit] Edit summaries

Hi! I see that you've been actively patrolling recent changes, as well as making a lot of good article contributions. It's always good to have another infantryman in the trenches.

It would be a great help to other contributors if you could try to make use of the edit summary box when you make edits, however. For other editors who have articles on their watchlists and for those of us who are watching for vandalism life is a lot easier if you mention–very briefly–what you've done.

For an edit like this one where you reverted the edits made by a page blanking vandal, a short summary along the lines of rvv (revert vandalism) is sufficient. (Otherwise people see that 37 kB has been added to the article, and wonder if it's spam or other vandalism.) Many thanks for your future edit summaries, and keep up the good work.

Cheers, TenOfAllTrades(talk) 03:42, 8 November 2005 (UTC)

[edit] Criminology

Welcome to Wiki! Good to have someone else interested in more theoretical issues. I am passing through criminology by accident (it was not really one of my stronger areas of expertise). I noticed what I considered a grave situation on victimless crime but was thrown out by some Libertarians. This inspired me to update and upgrade the whole criminology coverage (and, in the process, go round the obstruction by renaming the page victimless crime (political philosophy): you might be amused by the debate I provoked on the Talk page to test out how rational they were). Feel free to contribute where you have the expertise. I am working my way through the Schools and Theories. I will be doing Classical, Positivst and Left Realism next, and infilling more on Social Control/Bonding, etc. Why not cast a young and up-to-date eye over my creaking efforts and improve them at will. Or do something to deviant behavior which is woefully underdeveloped. In other words, any and every contribution you can make is welcome. David91 12:38, 3 February 2006 (UTC)

Most recently, I have been working on Right Realism so you must tell me whether I have managed to maintain an NPOV stance. I will be separating out Social Bonding, Social Learning Theory, Situation Theory, and Rational Choice to their own pages. Similarly, I will take Strain Theory out of anomie where it currently resides. It is quite good fun to think about all this again. Glad to have someone else to share the load.
