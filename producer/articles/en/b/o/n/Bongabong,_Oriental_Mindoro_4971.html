Bongabong, Oriental Mindoro

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Bongabong is a 2nd class municipality in the province of Oriental Mindoro, Philippines. According to the 2000 census, it has a population of 59,477 people in 12,020 households.

[edit] Famous people and families of Bongabong

The Umali clan is one of the most well-known families in Bongabong. The Umali clan includes Mariz Umali, a newscaster for GMA Network, Mercedes Umali Reyes-Liboon, an actress during her time, and many other Umali who are business executives or succesful and rich people abroad. Umali is into political dynasty. They've been into in since time immemorial and since God knows when.

Liwanags are known for business and politics. They own "The Country Bank" which is considered as the best bank in Oriental Mindoro. In politics, Ernesto Liwanag is the lone member of Liwanag clan. He is on his second term as a member of Sangguniang Panlalawigan and formerly the Municipal Administrator of the province. Hopefully, Owen will be the additional if he win in the May 2007 elections as a councilor.

Atty. Nestor M. Mantaring, Director of the National Bureau of Investigation is also from Bongabong. His mother, Asuncion M. Mantaring is a former councilor of the town. His relatives in Bongabong are known educators, being the stockholders of Eastern Mindoro College.

Lito Camo, the composer of hit novelty songs is also from the town of Bongabong
