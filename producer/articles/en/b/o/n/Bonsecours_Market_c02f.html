Bonsecours Market (French: Marché Bonsecours (m)), at 350 rue Saint-Paul, Montreal, is a two-storey domed public market. Named for the adjacent Notre-Dame-de-Bon-Secours Chapel and designed by architects William Footner and George Browne, it opened in 1847.

Other than a market, it served as public market, concert hall, and Montréal's city hall from 1852 to 1878. Today, the market houses cafés-terrasses, restaurants and boutiques.
