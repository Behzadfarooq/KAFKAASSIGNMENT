Bonnie Large (born September 9, 1952 in Glendale, California) was Playboy magazine's Playmate of the Month for March 1973. Her centerfold was photographed by William Figge and Mel Figge.

At the time of her pictorial, Bonnie worked for a marketing firm. Beforehand, in 1969, she was a finalist for the Rose Queen prior to that year's Rose Bowl, but was disqualified at the last minute for being too young.

Bonnie went on to practice several alternative therapies, including neurotherapy, biofeedback therapy and hypnotherapy. While performing Neurotherapy, she was able to help a number of young children with emotional, behavioral and academic difficulty, as well as children with attention deficit disorder (ADD/ADHD). In addition to providing neurotherapy, behavioral modification, and neurometric assessments, she was skilled in electroencephalography (EEG) and Quantative Electroencephalography (qEEG). Bonnie was highly respected by her clients, their parents, and her professional peers. She is a graduate of the Los Angeles School of Biofeedback, and completed an internship at the Neurophysiological Institute. She also wrote a book of erotic poetry and was the manager of the Playboy Playmate Alumni Association. She lives in Los Angeles.
