Bonanza (Drinking Game)

Bonanza is a drinking game played primarily by students at the University of Florida. While relatively new, its simple set of rules combined with a fast-paced, high-volume style of play ensure it a place in the pantheon of drinking games for years to come.

[edit] Preparation

In order to play Bonanza, between 2-10 people must be seated in a circle around a table or some other flat surface. Each must have a full Beer or other alcoholic drink. The group needs to have playing cards. While a full deck is preferable, the rules only require there be enough cards for each player to receive one.


[edit] Rules

The play of Bonanza is dictated by three simple rules. Players are each dealt one card by a randomly chosen dealer. At this point, no one may look at his or her card. Once each player has been dealt into the game, all players begin counting in unison to three in French--"Un, Deux, Trois." When trois is reached, each player flips over his or her card, revealing it to all players. The player with the highest card of the group wins the game.

[edit] The Winner's Prize

The consequence, or prize, for winning Bonanza is substantial. The winner must chug his or her entire beer while the remaining players sing the theme song from the television show Bonanza. If the winner finishes the beer before the song is done, the song is completed and the winner is greeted by gunshots from the hands of the other players. If the singers finish the song before the winner is done drinking, the last note of the song is held out 3-5 seconds. If the beer is not finished in this allotted time, the singers have the right to begin singing the phrase "Ultra-gay" on the same pitch as the last note in the song.

[edit] Repeating the Game

Once the game is completed, a new round can be started by filling up the winner's beer,shuffling the cards, and beginning again.
