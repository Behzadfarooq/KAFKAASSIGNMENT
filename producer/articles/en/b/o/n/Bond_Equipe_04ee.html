Bond Equipe

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

A British 2+2 sports car, manufactured by Bond Cars Ltd, this was their first 4 wheeled car. Production started in 1963.

The original Equipe was based on the Triumph Herald chassis with a fastback fibreglass body and utilised some Triumph parts including the windscreen/scuttle assembly and doors. 1964's GT4S model saw revisions to the body with twin headlights and a opening rear boot. It was powered a Herald based 1147 cc engine in Spitfire tune (63/67 BHP). This was upgraded to the 75BHP 1296 cc Spitfire engine in 1967 (GT4S 1300).

The GT4S was joined by the 2-litre GT with a very stylish smoother body in 1968. This was based on the similar Triumph Vitesse chassis and 2000 cc 95BHP six cylinder engine. The 2-litre GT was available as a closed coupe or convertible. The was capable of 100 mph with respectable acceleration. Horsepower and suspension improvements were done in line with Triumph's upgrades. Production ended in 1971 when Triumph stopped production of the chassis.
