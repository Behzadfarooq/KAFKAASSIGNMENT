Bodo Illgner (born April 7, 1967 in Koblenz) is a former German football goalkeeper, who backstopped West Germany to the 1990 FIFA World Cup title. He became the first goalkeeper to keep a clean sheet in a World Cup final.

His penalty save in the 1990 World Cup Italia against England in the semi-finals brought Germany into the final and proceed to win the nation's 3rd World Cup.

Illgner was an exceptional shot-stopper who had a superb command of the backline then consisting of the experienced Klaus Augenthaler, Andreas Brehme, Thomas Berthold, Guido Buchwald and Jürgen Kohler. Illgner was almost unbeatable in a 1-on-1 situation and was superb when handling crosses and shots.

Overall, Illgner appeared 54 times for his country, and also played at Euro 92 and the 1994 FIFA World Cup. On the club level, he played at 1.FC Köln (1985-96) and Real Madrid (1996-2001), helping the latter to a Champions League title in 1998.
