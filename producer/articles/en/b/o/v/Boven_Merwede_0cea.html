Boven Merwede

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

The Boven Merwede is a stretch of river in the Netherlands. The Afgedamde Maas river joins the Waal at Woudrichem to form the Boven Merwede, which at Hardinxveld-Giessendam splits into the Beneden Merwede river on the right and the Nieuwe Merwede river on the left. Its length is 8.8 km. The Merwede is part of the main shipping route between Rotterdam and Germany.

There is a road bridge, from the west side of Gorinchem on the north to the west side of Sleeuwijk on the south.

There are three passenger ferries.
