"Boston Tea Party" is an episode of The Suite Life of Zack & Cody.

[edit] Plot

Spoiler warning: Plot and/or ending details follow.

Zack and Cody find out the local park is going to be torn down and made into a parking lot. Cody decides to write a 18-page letter to the mayor which doesn't make a difference. While Cody is trying to read it to him, Zack falls asleep and dreams that it is 1775 and the British are in control of the United States and are raising prices on everything. Everyone at the Tipton gets together for the Boston Tea Party. After the revolution Bob walks in wearing a dress and asks Zack to dream that he is a viking. Zack then wakes up and discovers the park is an historical landmark because The Boston Tea Party was planned there. While they are protesting Moseby goes to the mayor and gets an injunction to stop the bulldozing from taking place. Meanwhile, Maddie helps Esteban study for his American citizenship test which he passes.

Spoilers end here.

[edit] Trivia

  • At the end of the episode when Mr. Moseby gives the injunction to the construction worker, he says to the worker "Read it and weep!" Possible reference toward the Disney Channel Original Movie "Read It and Weep."
  • In this episode the park is across the street from the Tipton. But in other episodes they say the Saint Mark Hotel is across the street. Perhaps the hotel is next to the park.
  • In this episode Carey's voice sounds different than in other episodes. It may have been possible she had laryngitis, or a cold.
  • In Zack's dream sequence, London's hairdo is more appropriate for the courts of France and Italy than for Colonial America. Not even aristocratic American women wore such a hairstyle.
  • After Zack hears that hot chocolate prices have increased he says "King George has gone too far this time" a possible reference to what Padmé Amidala says in Star Wars about the trade federation.
  • When Arwin gets the things from his basement he says "Hey guys look at all this cool stuff I found in the basement!" then Maddie says "Arwin your right!" but the closed caption 1 says "Herman your right!"
  • When Esteban is making crafts, his hair looks like the printer in Johnny Tremain.
