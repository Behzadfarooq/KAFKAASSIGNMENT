[edit] Copyright violations

Hi! I noticed the images you've added to the Sarajevo article say they're Creative Commons-licensed on their image pages, but in fact they're copyrighted according to Flickr, Scryscraper City and the other websites you've mentioned as a source. Please refrain from uploading images when you're not sure about the copyright status, this is harmful to Wikipedia. Todor→Bozhinov 19:59, 25 June 2006 (UTC)

Well, unfortunately, they have to be deleted. They're great images, there's no doubt, but Wikipedia's aim is to be a free encyclopedia, and these photos are not free content. They can't be fair use, because they can be easily reproduced, and I know the Sarajevo article would look a bit humble without them, but you can always find free equivalents — try these 125, for example, all free Creative Commons licensed. Generally, when you search Flickr for images to use in Wikipedia, mark all three CC fields on the Advanced Search page, so all you'd get is suitable to use here.
Don't be cast down, I know how it is with that war and everything, and I understand your ambition to show the world just how beautiful your city is after all, but that's not the way, I think. I'm sure there are enough free image resources for Bosnia and Herzegovina, I've found some for Bulgaria that are quite sufficient, for example. Todor→Bozhinov 20:10, 25 June 2006 (UTC)
On a related note, I've started removing the copyrighted images from the article and am listing them at Wikipedia:Copyright problems. I understand your desire to improve the article, and I'm glad that you are willing to do so, but Wikipedia just can't accept such copyrighted images. Hopefully we'll be able to replace these images with free alternatives. Pagrashtak 01:13, 26 June 2006 (UTC)
