Bosche

Bosche as he appears in Breath of Fire: Dragon Quarter.

Bosche is a fictional character in the Breath of Fire series, specifically the Breath of Fire: Dragon Quarter installment.

[edit] History

Spoiler warning: Plot and/or ending details follow.

Bosche is the son of the regent Vexacion. He is an arrogant boy who believes that his skills with his rapier alone are enough to overcome any obstacle or opponent. Bosche and Ryu start off as friendly rivals. Bosche proves to be the more skilled of the two as the fight their way to the train station. As they ride the train, Bosche asks Ryu if he could assist him in acquiring a higher rank, and in return Bosche would do the same for him. However, before Ryu can respond, Lin, riding on a Cyclopse genic, attacks, derailing the train and throwing it into the abyss below. Bosche is left hanging on for his life to a twisted piece of rail. Bosche, in shock and nearly delirious, returns to the station only to be sent back with the irate order to destroy the train's cargo, which, coincidentally, turns out to be Nina. Bosche and two grunts arrive at a train station the same time Ryu, Lin and Nina do. While the guards arrest Lin, Bosche coldly remarks to Ryu that their mission is to "get rid of the cargo". Ryu, having no idea what Bosche is talking about, steps between him and Nina and demands that Bosche elaborate. Bosche responds by stabbing Ryu in the knee, sadisticly jerking the blade around while taunting him. When Nina rushes over to help him, Bosche prepares to kill her. Ryu, however, grabs the rapier and stops Bosche's death blow. In a fit of rage, Bosche impales Ryu through his throat. Just before he dies,Ryu performs a D-Dive for the first time, fully healing himself. Bosche retaliates with one of his attacks, but when Dragon-Ryu is undamaged by the attack, he realizes that his skills can be bested, even by someone of a lower D-Ratio. Bosche and his two lackies are easily beaten, and Bosche's ego is majorly deflated. After Ryu defeats Captain Zeno in a battle that Bosche runs away from, he is no longer confident that he can defeat Ryu by himself and decides to risk sanity in order to merge with an artificial D-construct. By doing so, he gains greatly improved speed and strength at the cost of his sanity and appearance. Half of his body now resembles a greatly emaciated Ryu mid D-Dive. In his new form, Bosch engages Ryu at the Top Sector of Lifeline, where Ryu, along with Lin and Nina, defeats him. He tumbles over the edge of the platform into the darkness below.

As Bosche, utterly broken and defeated, was lying on the stones below and wondering if Ryu is even capable of hate, his own hatred and pain activated the D-construct and enabled the resurrection of the dragon Chertyre.

Much later in the game at Geofront, Bosch (now a full dragon like Ryu) finishes off Elyon and proceeds to the surface gate with the two goals of killing Ryu and reclaiming the sky. At the surface gate, Ryu and Bosch do battle three times.

Initially, Bosche eschews his D-Dive form and simply attacks Ryu and his friends with his human form. Ryu easily vanquishes him, prompting Bosche to D-Dive for the second battle. During this battle, Nina and Lin's attacks prove useless unless Bosche is first weakened by Ryu's own D-Dive attacks.

The finale comes as Bosche succumbs to the power of Chertyre, releasing the Dragon's full form into the world. Once again, Ryu D-Dives, and he is forced to utilize his D-Breath to the limit and beyond, finally killing Chertyre and himself in the process.

Spoilers end here.

[edit] Trivia

  • Bosche is a character that is playable at the start of the game, where players can see the awesome power of his "Beast Blade" skills.
  • It's also noted that Bow from Breath of Fire 2 carries the name of "Bosch" in the Japanese version, although there's really no connection between the two characters.
  • Rather than being an incarnation with the same dragon powers as Ryu, akin to that of Breath of Fire III's Teepo or Breath of Fire IV's Fou-Lu, Bosche's character more closely resembles that of BoF IV's Rosso, who is also filled with almost as much vanity and narcissism, while also being born from an aristocratic family and is driven to exel in the ranks as per starting off as a grunt.
