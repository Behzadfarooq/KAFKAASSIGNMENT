The Bosnian-Herzegovinian Patriotic Party-Sefer Halilović (Bosanskohercegovačka Patriotska Stranka-Sefer Halilović) is a Bosniak political party in Bosnia and Herzegovina. It was founded by the former commander of the Army of the Republic of Bosnia and Herzegovina, Sefer Halilović.

The party did not participate in the 1996 elections, but did obtain parliamentary status in the 1998 elections. In the 2002 elections the party won no seats in the House of Representatives of Bosnia and Herzegovina, but 1 out of 140 seats in the House of Representatives of the Federation of Bosnia and Herzegovina. At the most recent elections, 1 october 2006, the party won;

  • 1 out of 42 seats in the House of Representatives of Bosnia and Herzegovina.
  • 4 out of 98 seats in the House of Representatives of the Federation of Bosnia and Herzegovina
  • 3 out of 35 seats in the assembly of the Zenica-Doboj Canton
  • 2 out of the 35 seats in the assembly of Sarajevo Canton
  • 1 out of 35 seats in the assembly of the Tuzla Canton
  • 1 out of 25 seats in the assembly of the Bosnian Podrinje Canton
