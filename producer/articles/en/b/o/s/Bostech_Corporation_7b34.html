Bostech Corporation, which was founded in 1998, is a company that provides SOA and application integration software.

Originally an IT consulting business, the company began marketing ChainBuilder, its first product, in 2001. ChainBuilder, a technology that gets incompatible software packages to exchange information, helped the company transition to global enterprise integration software. Bostech's ChainBuilder became a prominent cross-industry integration tool used by organizations ranging from e-commerce retailers like Amazon.com to energy producers like Aventine Renewable Energy. Bostech helps these businesses seamlessly share, exchange, and transact critical information with their customers, suppliers, partners, and employees.

In 2006, Bostech made a technology advancement in the Service Oriented Architecture space completely re-coding a new open source Java version of ChainBuilder into ChainBuilder ESB,a JBI-complaint Enterprise Service Bus. Based on Bostech's years of experience in the integration space, ChainBuilder ESB uncommonly includes many components that bring disparate non-XML applications into the new SOA fold. All versions of ChainBuilder include development through graphical interfaces.

With company operations in Columbus, Ohio and a development team in Beijing, China, Bostech Corporation was founded by Brad Bostic in Indianapolis, Indiana, and is privately owned.
