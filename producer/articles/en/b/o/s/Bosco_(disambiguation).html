Bosco may refer to any of the following people:

  • Saint John Bosco (Don Bosco), Roman Catholic priest and founder of the Salesian Society
    • Bosconia a town in northern Colombia named after Saint John Bosco.
  • Henri Bosco, a French writer
  • Bosco Hogan, an Irish actor
  • Hieronymus Bosch, a Flemish painter known in Spanish as El Bosco.
  • Philip Bosco, an American actor
  • João Bosco, a Brazilian singer
  • Jean Bosco Mwenda, a Congolese guitarist
  • Bosco Wong, a Hongkong actor
  • Johannes de Sacro Bosco, an English scholar
  • Juan Bosco Maino Canales, a Chilean political activist
  • John Bosco Manat Chuabsamai, a Catholic bishop in Thailand

Bosco means "forest" in Italian. It is part of Italian placenames like:

  • Bosco Gurin, a German-speaking village in Switzerland.
  • The Sacro Bosco garden in Bomarzo.

Bosco may refer to the following fictional characters:

  • Officer Maurice 'Bosco' Boscorelli on the TV series Third Watch played by Jason Wiles
  • Bosco, the owner of Bosco's Inconvenience store in the Sam and Max series of computer games

Commercial usages of the term include:

  • Bosco, an Irish children's television program
  • Bosco Chocolate Syrup, a drink mix and dessert topping referenced famously in Seinfeld
  • Bosko, an early Warner Brothers cartoon
  • The name Bosco is also used by a Princeton, New Jersey based hip-hop DJ
  • Bosco di Ciliegi, a chain of fashion shops, sponsor of Russian Olympic Team.
  • Bosco Sticks, a brand of cheese-filled breadsticks

Bosco, or Bosco Tech may also refer to an institution named after the saint, including:

  • St. John Bosco High School, Bellflower, California
  • Don Bosco Technical Institute, Rosemead, California
  • Don Bosco Preparatory High School, Ramsey, New Jersey
  • Don Bosco, Buenos Aires
  • Don Bosco Institute, Matunga, Bombay
  • Don Bosco Institute, Borivali, Bombay
  • Don Bosco Institute of Technology, Banglore
  • Don Bosco Matriculation School, Chennai
  • Don Bosco Institute of Technology, Mumbai
