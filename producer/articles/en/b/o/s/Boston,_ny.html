[edit] The Iroquois

Iroquois under various names lived here until the Senecas became predominant. This all ended when the Sulivan Expedition during the period of the Revolution reduced them.

[edit] First Known Church

Free Will Baptist Church (circa 1811).

[edit] Cemetaries

Boston, NY has many historical cemetaries that provide links to the ethnological composition of early settlers.

[edit] Epidemic

In 1843 there was an epidemic. It is believed to have been typhoid fever, likely from a local well.

[edit] Glaciers

Geologically speaking, Boston, NY is one of the more interesting places in the Greater Niagara region, for many glacial activities, over very many long years, caused sufficient pressure indentation to alter dramatically the landscape and even the water table.

[edit] Local stories

  • Wet fire. A distillery across the street from St. Paul's took fire and a bucket brigade of the faithful fought valiantly to put it out. They failed. The distillery was razed. But the liquor survived. They used the buckets to haul the stuff to St. Paul's where the persuaded the faithful to imbibe.
  • Love murder. A poor family borrwed money from a boarder, John Love. He was a peddler by trade and sailor by pleasure. John Love disappeared in 1824, after having stayed with the host family, the Thayers. The Thayer boys soon showed money to burn. They fund Love in a shallow graved gulley.

[edit] Post Office

A post office was opened in 1832, in North Boston.
