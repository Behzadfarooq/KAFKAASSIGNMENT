Bostock Stanley are a Five-a-side football team based in Bolton, in the north of England.

They were founded in 1999 by Matthew Atkinson, Mark Kershaw, David Bossom and Mark Whalley. The team was entered in The Premier Fives league U-16 1st Division soon after the formation, however a series of heavy defeats were encountered during the first few games. Inspirational manager Andrew Lace was recruited to provide some stability and his first actions were to add Mark Jones and Wesley Duraman to the squad. Relegation was prevented during the first two season due to a techincality, however they soon found themselves at the wrong end of the table again and were consiquentley relegated to the 2nd division. Duraman soon left for Egerton Eagles, a move many believe was motivated by the money provided by the blues, leaving a bitter taste amongst supporters.

Relegation proved to be a blessing in disguise for the Stanley as confidence grew within the team and new players were recruited. Nick Pearson provided some exciting moments and new hope after his arrival. Bostock now had a stable line up and were pushing for promotion and eventually regained their status in division 1 a few seasons later. This enabled the club to compete in it's first local derby against Egerton Eagles, unfortunatley The Eagles came out on top and were promoted to the Premiership the same season.

The following season allowed Bostock to gain back some local pride, with games against Red Diamonds and Barcelona B's, with Bostock coming out on top both times against B B's. The team sustained their division 1 status for a number of seasons and gained a reputation as one of the more entertaining teams in the league with some superb attacking football and a never say die attitude, typified by captain Mark Kershaw, who's reputation was constantly growing. After over 2 years at the club Matthew Atkinson announced his shock departure, by announcing he would be joining Egerton Eagles. This move forced manager Andrew Lace to resign from his position as manager as the pressure became too much for him. The fairwell present from the pair was a place in the coverted premiership for the first time. A replacement was quickly found for Atkinson as Andy Cartridge joined from rivals Red Diamonds, who themselves had fallen on hard times and consiquently dropped out of the league. Bostock maintained their top flight status and continued to build a strong squad by recruiting from other teams. The club continued for just a couple more seasons before withdrawing from the league due to age restrictions and disputes with the league commitee over falling standards. A year break followed for Bostock, before they returned in a different league, starting off in the Tuesday 4th division. Bostock found it hard to adjust at first to the more physical league, however after recruiting some experienced players they soon found their feet. The team played for a total of 4 seasons in the league, with many of the seasons ending in mid-table mediocrity. As players began to leave due to other commitments, Bostock withdrew from the league and ended a 4 year association with premier fives.

In March 2007 the team was re-formed under the guidance of previous founders David Bossom and Mark Whalley. Cartridge was recruited back to the team and there was a suprise return for Atkinson.Liam, Robbo,Jazmik and Barlow were also added to the team as they entered a cup competition at the JJB Soccerdome in Blackburn. Stanley finished 4th in a group of 6, with 1 win, 1 draw and 3 losses. However they bounced back the next week with a 4-2 win in a qualifying game which gained them 1st division status in the newley formed sunday evening league. They started the campaign with a 14-3 victory to send them top of the table.

[edit] Trivia

Bostock Stanley is also the name of a fictional football team in the UK telivision program Bostock's Cup (1974). Many believe this was the inspiration behind the teams name
