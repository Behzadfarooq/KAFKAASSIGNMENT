[edit] Television

From 1999 to 2006 (1998-2005 seasons), all games of the BCS were televised by ABC Sports (now ESPN on ABC). Generally, coverage consisted of two games on New Year's Day, one on January 2, and one on either January 3 or 4.

Starting with the 2006 season, coverage will be split between ABC and FOX. ABC will continue to broadcast the Rose Bowl, while the remaining games will be on FOX: the Orange Bowl, Sugar Bowl, Fiesta Bowl, and a new fifth game, the BCS National Championship Game. In 2007, ABC and FOX will show one game each on January 1, FOX will then show one game each on January 2 and 3 and come back with the championship game on January 8.

FOX will show all BCS championship games over the length of the contract, which is four seasons, even if the Rose Bowl hosts the title game.

Chris Rose and Jimmy Johnson will be two of the commentators during pregame, postgame, and halftime segments; Fox is seeking current or former coaches to join them. John Saunders, Craig James, and Doug Flutie will have similar duties at the Rose Bowl.

[edit] Radio

ESPN Radio provides coverage of all five games. Usually, the announcers called games on television throughout the regular season. For example, Ron Franklin and Davie called the 2006 Rose Bowl for the network, and Holly Rowe was the sideline reporter.

Musburger, Davie, Blackledge and Salters will call the 2007 BCS National Championship Game for ESPN Radio.
