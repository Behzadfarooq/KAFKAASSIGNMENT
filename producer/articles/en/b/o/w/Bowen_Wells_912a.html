Bowen Wells

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Petrie Bowen Wells, known as Bowen Wells; (born August 4, 1935) is a Conservative Party politician in the United Kingdom.

Wells was educated at St Paul's, the University of Exeter, and Regent Street Polytechnic.

He served as Member of Parliament for Hertford and Stevenage from 1979 (defeating Shirley Williams) to 1983, and after boundary changes, for Hertford and Stortford from 1983 until 1997.

Notable positions held during office include Chief Whip of the Conservative Party, and Chairman of the International Development Select Committee.

Wells is married with two sons, Adam and Simon Bowen, and resides in the South of England.

Wells famously lent his name to the Bowen Wells Appreciation Crew of Keble College, Oxford.
