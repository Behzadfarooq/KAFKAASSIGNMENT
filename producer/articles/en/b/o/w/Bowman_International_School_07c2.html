Bowman International School is a K-8, Montessori school located in Palo Alto, California. It emphasizes cultural awareness, with a multi-disciplinary curriculum focused on a different area of the world each year, along with courses in Japanese, Spanish, and Latin. All though K-8 the school is best for younger children as it provides a great educational environment for younger students.

It was founded in 1995 as the “Children’s International School” by a group of parents and educators. In 2003, CIS changed its name to Bowman International School, after Margret Bowman Ricks, the founding director. Enrollment is 185 students.

Rather than offering the traditional United States school schedule with summer vacation, it offers a year-round, full day program, operating on a 12-month calendar with approximately 10 to 15 fixed vacation days per year. In addition, students do not have regularly assigned homework.

The Bowman Website (bottom of page) states some incorrect facts (mostly on the curriculum highlights page) just make sure to ask them about this when vistiting as it could have changed.

[edit] Grades

The school does not run the same as a traditional school as it doesn't seperate classes by grade instead by level. Some of the levels overlap by their equivilent grade because some students may be willing to move up sooner than others so the system is to help accommodate.

  • JUNIORS: K-1
  • LOWER ELEMENTARY: 1-3
  • UPPER ELEMENTARY: 4-6
  • MIDDLE SCHOOL: 6-8
