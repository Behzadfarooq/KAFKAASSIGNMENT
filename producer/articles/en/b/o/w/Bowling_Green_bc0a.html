Bowling Green

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

For the lawn used for playing the game of Bowls, see Bowling green.

Bowling Green is the name of some places in the United States of America:

  • Bowling Green, Florida, named after the town in Kentucky.
  • Bowling Green, Kentucky, name of unknown origin, and the home of Western Kentucky University
  • Bowling Green, Missouri
  • Bowling Green, Ohio, named after the town in Kentucky and the home of Bowling Green State University
  • Bowling Green, South Carolina, abandoned
  • Bowling Green, Virginia, named after the bowling green on the Hoomes family estate, and the seat of Caroline County
  • Bowling Green, a historic triangle of Colonial green, oldest public park in New York City.
    • Bowling Green is also the name of a major subway station located nearby.
  • Bowling Green State University, a university in Bowling Green, Ohio
