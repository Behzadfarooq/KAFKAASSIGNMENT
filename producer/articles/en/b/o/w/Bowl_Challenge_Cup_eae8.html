Bowl Challenge Cup

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

The Bowl Challenge Cup was created by ESPN in 2001 as a competition among the conferences in the NCAA's Division I Bowl Subdivision (formerly known as Division I-A) to see which one was the best in the college football bowl games that take place. The eleven conferences are the Atlantic Coast Conference (ACC), Big East, Big Ten, Big 12, Conference USA (C-USA), Mid-American Conference (MAC), Mountain West Conference (MWC), Pacific 10 Conference (Pac-10), Southeastern Conference (SEC), Sun Belt Conference and the Western Athletic Conference (WAC). ESPN no longer sponsors the Bowl Challenge Cup and does not publish active standings on its web site. However, the network has continued to post the standings during college bowl games on ESPN.

In order to qualify to win the trophy, a conference has to have the highest winning percentage with a minimum of three teams from that conference. As the only bowl tie-in for the Sun Belt champions is the New Orleans Bowl, they have not had enough teams to be participate and are unlikely to do so in recent years. The MAC has participated on occasion, when major conferences failed to have enough eligible teams to fulfill their required commitments and, as of 2006, the MAC now has the minimum three automatic invitations to regularly particitate in the Bowl Challenge Cup.


Bowl Challenge Cup Champions

2004- Pacific 10 Conference (Pac-10) & Conference USA (C-USA) (3-2) .600

2005- Big 12 & Pacific 10 Conference (Pac-10) (5-3) .625

2006- Big East (5-0) 1.000
