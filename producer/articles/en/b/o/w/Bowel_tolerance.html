Bowel tolerance

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

The "bowel tolerance threshold", according to Robert Cathcart, is the minimum dose of vitamin C that brings about diarrhea, an effect of vitamin C megadosage.

When taken in huge doses, vitamin C will always causes diarrhoea (see hypervitaminosis). This exact amount needed for this varies with the individual and Cathcart claims the onset of diarrhoea to be an indication of where the bodyâ€™s true vitamin C requirement lies.[1] Typical quantities range from 5 to 25 grams per day in healthy individuals, but both Cathcart and Ewan Cameron have demonstrated that very sick patients with cancer or influenza do not display any evidence of diarrhoea at all until ascorbate intake reaches levels as high as 200 grams (half a pound).

Gradually increasing the dosage in order to find where the tolerance lies for an individuals is often referred to as "titrating to bowel tolerance".
