Bombo

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Bombo (Spanish for "drum") can refer to several things:

In geography:

  • Bombo, New South Wales, a suburb of the Municipality of Kiama
  • Bombo, Uganda, a part of Uganda where Nubi language is spoken

In music:

  • Bombo (musical instrument), a bass drum popular in Spanish/Portuguese-language folk music
  • Bombo legüero, an Argentine form of this drum

In computer and video games:

  • Bombo (video game), a 1986 British game on the Commodore 64

In sport:

  • Manolo el del bombo (a.k.a. Manuel Cáceres Artesero), the Spanish national football team's most famous supporter

In film:

  • Bombo Sports & Entertainment, a U.S. film and television production company
  • Ecce Bombo, a 1978 Italian film [1]
  • Bombos, a 2004 Argentine film [2]

In theatre:

  • Bombo (musical), a 1920s production starring Al Jolson
