Bournemouth Gasworks Athletic F.C.

Bournemouth Gasworks Athletic were a highly successful amateur side in both county and national football during their existence .

[edit] History

Founded in 1899 as a works side the club joined the Hampshire League West Division in 1904. In 1921 Bournemouth Gasworks were placed in the County Division (later to become Division 1) and this marked the start of a very successful period for 'the Gas Men' as they were crowned champions in 1922-23.

The late Twenties saw the side record some fine runs in the FA Amateur Cup, most notably in 1929-30 when they reached the final, which was played at West Ham United's Upton Park stadium where a crowd of 21,800 saw them hold Ilford 1-1 at the break before they eventually lost by a 1-5 scoreline.

The Gasworks remained a strong force in county football and were Hampshire League Division 1 champions a further four times during the Thirties with the Reserve side also winning the Division 2 title.

[edit] Post War

The post war era saw Bournemouth Gasworks remain a prominent force in the Hampshire League and the cups. During this time they were regular entrants in the FA Cup qualifying rounds and they won the Hampshire Senior Cup in 1952-53 and 1953-54. In 1956-57 the Gasworks were relegated after finishing bottom and after a mid-table finish in Division 2 the club left the Hamsphire League to play in the Dorset League, which they would proceed to win nine times.

[edit] Demise

Following the loss of their magnificent Alder Road home ground in 1972 Bournemouth Gasworks Athletic decided to call it a day and folded.

During their history the Gasworks won many honours (also including the Dorset Senior Cup ten times as well as the Dorset Amateur Cup nine times!) and succeeded in obtaining national aclaim not just for themselves but the competitions they played in.
