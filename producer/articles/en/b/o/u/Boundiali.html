Boundiali

Boundiali is one of the fifty-eight departments of Côte d'Ivoire, located in the northern region of the country, and also the name of the town at the center of that department. The economy of the town of Boundiali is largely based on the cotton industry. It is a centre for Senoufo people and is known for crafts. There are also Malinkés coming from Mali, Guinea and Burkina Faso and some Fulas, totalling around 50,000 people in the town.

The writer Ahmadou Kourouma was born in Boundiali.
