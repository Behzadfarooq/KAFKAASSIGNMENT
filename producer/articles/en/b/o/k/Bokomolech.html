Bokomolech is a band from Athens, Greece.

The members met sometime in 1991 in the National Technical University of Athens. They started making an impression on Indie audiences in 1993, after having played at the Indie Free Festival.

Their name comes from a Ukrainian physician, Alexander Alexandrovic Bogomoletz, who came up with a drug of the same name sometime in the 1950s, supposedly postponing the aging of cells in the human body.

Their debut LP "Xero" (1995 - Lazy Dog Records, which an important indie label from Salonica) as the best Greek Rock Record (with English lyrics) ever.

They later signed with Hitch-Hyke and released the E.P. "Insect(songs)". Steve Albini was impressed by their music and invited them to Chicago to work on a record together at his own well-known studios "Electrical Audio". The product of this collaboration was a double L.P., "Jet Lag". Steve and Shellac fitted an extra day in their European tour that autumn, so that they could play in Athens with Bokomolech.

Their next release was another E.P., entitled "Slowburner", which was produced by one of the best musicians/producers in Greece, Coti.K.

"Exit: Trance" is their last L.P. to date and was recorded in the summer of 2003, once again with Steve Albini, but this time in Iain Burgess' Black Box Studio in rural France. The album was very well received. It was their first L.P. that got properly distributed abroad. Exit: Trance is out in Portugal and Germany.

  • Lila Katerinaki - bass / lila@bokomolech.gr

  • Kostas Ragoussis - guitar / kostas@bokomolech.gr

Past members:

  • Evi Fraghedaki - guitars (on all L.P.'s except "Exit: Trance")
