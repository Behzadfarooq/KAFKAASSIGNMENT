The Bohemian Society of Sciences, the first official scientific organization within Bohemia, follows a history of the sciences in Czechoslovakia. Before the creation of the Bohemian Society of Sciences, one can find evidence of scientific research and development through the establishment of Charles University in the 14th century, as well as in the works of Astronomers such as Johannes Kepler and Tycho Brahe under the reign of Rudolph II in addition to various other forms of scientific research.[1]

The Bohemian Society of Sciences was created from the Private Society for Mathematics, Patriotic History and Natural History, the first scientific society within Czechoslovakia. This was founded in 1772 and published six volumes before becoming the Bohemian Society of Sciences, and then later becoming the Royal Bohemian Scientific Society in 1784.[2] Its members included Masons and Illuminatis, and the Royal Bohemian Scientific Society it later established some ties with the Private Scientific and Patriotic Society of Moravia.[3]

In the early 18th century, the institution began to become, partially due to its usage of both Czech and German languages, which caused it to lose some of the more radical Czech scientists while the creation of the Vienna Academy caused the loss of some of the German speaking scientists.[4] By 1847, members of the Royal Bohemian Society of Sciences moved to the Vienna Academy, however, some of the members moved to other academies. Members moving to academies other than the Vienna Academy included: Palacká»³, Safaric, Zippe, Pressl and Prkyn.[1]

After 1847 the sciences have continued to play a role in the Czech state, continuing through the creation of the Czech Academy of Sciences and Arts, which was created in 1890 through a decree issued by Emperor Franz Joseph , which existed among many other institutions[1]. During the Second World War, most scientific research was halted due to the Nazi occupation, but was restarted in 1952 with the creation of the Czechoslovak Academy of Sciences, which continues to operate today.[5]
