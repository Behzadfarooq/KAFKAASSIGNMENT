[edit] Cherokee language tags

I'm not sure what you were doing at Template:User chr and Template:User chr-1 when you removed the following text:

[[Category:User chr|{{PAGENAME}}]][[Category:User chr-1|{{PAGENAME}}]]

Admittedly, it looks a little dense and much like nonsense to many new editors. However, it exists to automatically add users to a category for Cherokee speakers. For example, when you place {{User chr}} or {{User chr-1}} on your user page, that that extra code will automatically add you to the corresponding category or categories. You'll subsequently see, at the bottom of your user page, something that looks like "User chr-1" or "User chr;" you'll now be listed in that category. The alternative would be having to manually add the category, which is both a pain and is unstandardized with other language userboxes as well. For future reference, it's best not to erase Wiki tags just because they don't do anything to alter something's immediate physical appearance. Let me know if you need any further help or explanation by leaving me a message on my talk page; remember — we're just here to help you. — Rebelguys2 talk 19:42, 7 February 2006 (UTC)

[edit] Ethnic Background Categories

You're also having some issues implementing your ethnic background categories. Categories in Wikipedia are automatic; that is, if you insert "[[Category:Celtic Wikipedians]]" into your user page, you will automatically be placed onto that page. There's no need to manually place your name there. The category list will not be editable, because there's no need. When you hit the edit box at the top of the Category pages, anything you enter should just be a small header or introduction. If you'd like User:Waya sahoni to be in the category, ask him to place the necessary tag onto his user page. This automation is what makes categories easy and, therefore, is why it's so useful! I've gone ahead and blanked those pages, as it's already, automatically, there.

In addition, remember that categories are hierarchical. As a result, the other reason to edit category pages is to place them under another, more general category. I've gone ahead and placed Category:Celtic Wikipedians and Category:American Indian Wikipedians under Category:Wikipedians by ethnicity.

The ethnicity categories don't seem very clean and clear-cut to me, but that's because it doesn't really matter. We're here to write an encyclopedia, after all; not our own biographies.

One last note; I saw that you updated the code for your userboxes. However, it's much easier to just place the template onto your page automatically. Say you wanted the template at Template:User chr-1 (Cherokee level 1) on your page. Just place {{User chr-1}} on your page to have it automatically appear, with the category and everything. This syntax works for any template. That way, if the template is updated, your page will automatically update as well. Either way, it's must cleaner looking when you're editing your page. Take a look at the bottom of my user page for an example. — Rebelguys2 talk 22:48, 7 February 2006 (UTC)
