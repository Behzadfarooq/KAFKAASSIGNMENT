Vince DeMentri is a news anchor currently at WCAU-TV (NBC 10) in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania, the 4th largest television market, DMA, in the United States. DeMentri is a co-anchor along with Tracy Davidson for the weekday 5pm newscast on NBC 10.

DeMentri, a Philadelphia native and graduate of Temple University, started his broadcasting career in his as an associate sports producer at rival Philadelphia station WPVI-TV. From WPVI, DeMentri moved to Beckley, West Virginia, where he was a weekend anchor/reporter at WOAY-TV. Starting in 1989, DeMentri was employed at WOI-TV in Des Moines, Iowa where he was assigned to duties as a weekend anchor, as well as a general assignment and environmental reporter. DeMentri has also served as an special investigative reporter at both WDIV-TV in Detroit, Michigan, and WPRI-TV in Providence, Rhode Island.

The majority DeMentri's broadcasting ccarrier has been spent in the New York City market. From 1994 to 2002 DeMentri worked at CBS flagship affiliate WCBS-TV, where he served as both a general assignment reporter and a weekend anchor. It was while at WCBS that DeMentri earned a national award from the Radio Television News Directors Association for his coverage of the September 11, 2001 attacks. Though during his coverage of September 11th, DeMentri was arrested or impersonating a federal agent [1].

Prior to his arrival at NBC 10, his official start date was August 11, 2003, and his departure from WCBS, DeMentri spent several months at rival New York station WPIX-TV as a reporter and substitute anchor.

Besides his award for coverage of September 11th, DeMentri has won several peer and industry awards mainly pertaining to his investigative reporting.

DeMentri has also coverd the Impeachment of Bill Clinton, while at WCBS, was walk on guest severla times on the Late Show with David Letterman, and had a bit part as a reporter in the film U.S. Marshals

DeMentri is married, his wife Pat James-Dementri is a host on QVC, a cable/satellite shopping network, and has a daughter.
