[Jiu] Tang Shu, "[Old] Book of Tang" (also, [Chiu] T'ang shu), is the first classic work about the Tang Dynasty. Kao-Tse (or, "Chudi") of the Later Jin Dynasty ordered its commencement in 941 CE. Liu Xu (887-946 CE, also "Liu Hs√º"), chief minister and director of the National History redacted it during the last years of his life. Liu Xu presented it to the emperor Shao-ti (or, "Shi Chonggui") in 945 CE.

It is a compilation of earlier annals, now lost; it further incorporates other monographs and biographies, using as sources (for instance) the T'ung tien of Tu Yu. (c.f. Chronicles of the Chinese Dynasties; Twitchett, Official History under the T'ang, 191-236)

After this was revised during the Song dynasty into the Xin Tang shu ("New Book of Tang"), Liu Xu's work continued to be preserved as the "Old Book of Tang". It has been canonised in the Twenty-Four Histories.
