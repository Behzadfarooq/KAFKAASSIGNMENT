The Boonanza is a promotion run by Victoria Bitter (VB) during the Australian summer cricket season. The title of the promotion is a play on the title of the television series Bonanza.

During the 2005/2006 summer cricket season, the purchaser of two slabs (24 cans or stubbies) of beer would receive 1 "Boony Doll", a free talking doll of David "Boonie" Boon, who once drank 52 cans of beer on a flight from London to Sydney in 1989. The doll talks when it is placed near the screen during the Nine Network's cricket telecasts during the season, although it has been reported to talk at other times when cricket is not being telecast [1]. There is also a button that can be pressed which can make it talk. In addition to this there is also other associated merchandise available.

The promotion was originally launched for the 2005/06 Australian test cricket season & was such a success that for the 2006/07 season this promotion had returned now called Boonanza II which now included former England cricket captain Ian Botham and a talking figurine was created for him alongside to interact with the David Boon figurine. Also a feature built into the new series of figurines is they could interact with each other regardless if the Nine Network cricket coverage was broadcast or not.
