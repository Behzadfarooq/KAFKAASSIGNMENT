Boodle, or boodler, was a barroom or street term for money or booty applied by the yellow press (in 1884-1886) to members of the New York Board of Aldermen who were charged with accepting bribes in connection with the granting of a franchise for a street railroad on Broadway. Thereafter, the term came into common use to signify bribery in general and particularly in municipal governments.

Of the Boodler clan of Nothern Scotland, also associated with random slang ie boodle to the boodley. Tweed is a gimp

Source: Dictionary of American History by James Truslow Adams, New York: Charles Scribner's Sons, 1940
