Boom Town was a 1940 adventure drama Hollywood film starring Clark Gable, Spencer Tracy, Claudette Colbert, Hedy Lamarr, and Frank Morgan. A story written by James Edward Grant in Cosmopolitan magazine titled "A Lady Comes to Burkburnett" provided the inspiration for the film.[1]

[edit] Synopsis

Spoiler warning: Plot and/or ending details follow.

"Big John" McMasters (Gable) and "Square John" Sand (Tracy) are two down-on-their-luck oil wildcatters who team up. They fast talk a sceptical Luther Aldrich (Morgan) into providing drilling equipment and strike it rich.

When Elizabeth Bartlett (Colbert) shows up, McMasters sweeps her off her feet (without knowing that she is Sand's girl) and marries her. Later, he builds a company that's big enough to have to worry about interference from the federal government, so he hires ambitious political advisor Karen Vanmeer (Lamarr). When he gets a bit too close to her, Sand tries to take him down, to bring him back to his wife and child.

Sand loses his costly battle with McMasters and goes broke. Later, McMasters is prosecuted by the government and loses his business. In the end, poor, but happier, Sand and McMasters reconcile, with the blissful Elizabeth looking on. Aldrich supplies them with equipment and the whole cycle begins again.

[edit] Trivia

  • Gable's father was an oil-well driller, so Gable was familiar with the subject of this film.
