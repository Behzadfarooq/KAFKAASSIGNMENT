Booker Arts and Science Magnet Elementary School

Booker Arts and Science Magnet Elementary School is an elementary school in Little Rock, Arkansas, and is a part of the Little Rock School District.[1]

[edit] History

Opened in 1963, the school was named for Joseph Robert Booker (1893-1960), a prominent African-American attorney who became president of the National Bar Association. Built on the same property as the old school (named simply "Bush Elementary"), Booker began as a junior high school. A library was added to the school in 1973. In 1982, it made the conversion into an elementary school, and in 1983 it became an "arts magnet school". Emphasis in the school's curriculum is on the visual arts, music, drama, and the creative movement, as well as striving to master traditional academic curriculum.[2]
