Boom Chicago is an improvisational comedy theater, founded in Amsterdam in 1993 by a group of American tourists, who named it after their hometown. Although many visitors believe the theater to be European, it is purely American based; in fact, for a long time the city of Amsterdam refused to include it into the official list of Dutch theaters, because Boom Chicago was considered "foreign".

Boom Chicago's comedy show has recently became a centre of the city's cultural life, creating long-running stage performances, numerous performances in Edinburgh International Festival, and tours around the continent. Boom Chicago does not hesitate to address the most important issues including dangers of emerging extreme right, xenophobia in the post Pim Fortuyn Dutch society, conspiracy theory, and satire on 9/11 attacks or government bureaucracy. European media[attribution needed] considers Boom Chicago to be one of the most serious and influential comedy shows on the European continent.[citation needed] Mixing political and social issues with traditional cabaret elements makes their performances influential enough to affect even the political climate.[citation needed] Some of Boom Chicago's shows have become part of urban folklore because of the conceptual and politically oriented character of their performances.

[edit] History

In 1997, they moved into the 300 seat Leidseplein Theater, the theater where Dutch legends Wim Kan, Wim Sonneveld, Toon Hermans began in the 1930s. Boom Chicago renovated the theater, added a kitchen and installed tables and chairs. With the added capacity, their productions became among the biggest in Amsterdam in terms of ticket sales. Co-founder and Ken Shaefle designed the technically capable spaces for improvisation in terms of lighting and sound, setting the stage for further innovation at the Leidseplein Theater.

Three years later they hired their first video director, Jamie Wright, to help introduce multimedia into their shows incorporating live and recorded video and fitted the theater (and the Leidseplein outside) with cameras. Soon they added a green screen studio which makes Boom Chicago a technically advanced comedy show.

Boom Chicago Video Productions was launched in 2004 after the world-wide success of the Florida Voting Machine Video, where an American citizen tries to vote for Kerry, but the rigged machine only allows him to vote for George Bush. Today comedy videos are a major component of their comedic output, and they create topical videos for TV, internet, mobile phones and business campaigns. In 2006, Boom Chicago mounted a major initiative around the World Cup. A live show, Kick This: A World Cup Comedy With Balls, was complimented by a daily internet and mobile video series The Unlikely Fan which was seen by almost a million people on various networks including MSN, ZoomIn/FTV and KPN.

In September, 2006 BCVP took another step forward under director and editor Matt Chapman. Boom Chicago built an additional video studio, hired Becky Nelson as an additional video director, revamped their website and doubled their video output. Two weekly series, Full Frontal News and Slacker Fantasy Football, premiered on Sprint Mobile in the U.S.

Also in September, their new live show about media and technology Me, MySpace and iPod opened to their best reviews to date. Merijn Henfling wrote in Het Parool the show was "Genius... We are once again grateful that [Boom Chicago] decided to make Amsterdam their home." Peter de Bie and Francisco van Jole raved on TROS radio that "people are saying this is the best Boom Chicago show ever, and we would agree." NRC Handelsblad's Henk van Gelder called the show, "Sharp and interactive. They have created an entirely new form of theater; call it digital comedy." Leading tech bloggers Dutch Cowboys said Me, MySpace and iPod was "a surprising show you have to see."


Seth Meyers was a member of Boom Chicago in the late 90s. Other famous alumni include Josh Meyers (That 70s Show}, Ike Barinholtz, Jordan Peele, Colton Dunn (MAD TV), Jason Sudeikis, Liz Cackowski (Saturday Night Live), Allison Silverman (Colbert Report), Kay Cannon (30 Rock). Nicole Parker recently starred with Martin Short in Fame Becomes Me on Broadway.
