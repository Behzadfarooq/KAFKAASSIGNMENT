Boogie Shoes was a widely popular song released in 1975 on the self titled album KC and the Sunshine Band by the disco group KC and the Sunshine Band. The song became a hit when it appeared on the Saturday Night Fever Soundtrack in 1977. The song is still heard on radio stations today as a disco era staple.

The song is played at New York Mets games whenever Paul Lo Duca goes up at bat.

The song has been sampled by The Bloodhound Gang in the song "One Way" and more recently by Trick Daddy in his song "Take It To Da House."

The song was featured in the Sports Night episode, "Dear Lousie."

The song is featured in the soundtrack for the 1997 film Boogie Nights.

The song is part of the Saturday Night Fever musical, representing the 'ritual' men did before they go to the discotheque.
