The Bookhouse Boys are a secret society on Twin Peaks. They derive their name from the "bookhouse" in which they meet, and, as the name suggests, they are all male.

Spoiler warning: Plot and/or ending details follow.

They are formed partly to circumvent a kind of darkness surrounding the town, and many within the police department are members. They also play a kind of vigilante role against drug dealers etc. The series says that it was formed about twenty years previous to the events, so probably some time in the 1960s.

Like the Freemasons, they have their own secret gestures.

They are not afraid to take the law into their own hands, e.g. when Bernard Renault is interrogated in the Bookhouse.

When Sheriff Truman loses Josie Packard, he goes on a drunken "bender" and trashes the place.

[edit] Members

The club appears to be well attended, but the best known members are -

  • Big Ed Hurley
  • Sheriff Truman
  • Deputy Hawk
  • James Hurley
  • Joey Paulson, biker friend of James Hurley
  • Agent Dale Cooper, made an honorary member later on, one of the few non-residents to be so.

It is told by Sheriff Harry S. Truman that Hank Jennings was also once one of the Bookhouse Boys, and in fact, "one of the best". However, Hank's later behaviour, which resulted in him getting sent to prison, and has involved drug dealing and assassination attempts, suggests that he was almost certainly expelled.
