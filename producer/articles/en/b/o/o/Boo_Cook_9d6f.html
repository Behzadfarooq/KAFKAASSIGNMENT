Boo Cook is a British comic artist, whose work mainly features in the comic 2000 AD.

His chunky, detailed style, with debts to both Richard Corben and graffiti art, has graced such staples as the ABC Warriors and Judge Dredd, as well as newer series such as Asylum (written by Rob Williams) and Dead Men Walking (written by David Bishop).

In 2005, Cook graduated to regular work on 2000AD's lead strip, Judge Dredd, mostly to scripts by Gordon Rennie and Simon Spurrier. With Spurrier he developed new comedy character Harry Kipling.

Recently Boo Cook has contributed and started the 2000AD Online Charity Auction which you'll find on e-bay. Many famous artists of the comic have contributed as have some writers such as John Wagner.

Although much about the artist still remains a mystery, rumour has it that he spent many years living above a sex shop having visions mostly concerned with blue women in Cambridge whilst studying for his second degree. During this time (the early ninties) he hung about with a number of folk musicians and got involved with the local music scene, he could often be seen on stage at "the boat race" or busking in Lions yard banging a big drum. If one looks in the bar at the Ruskin University his art work can still be seen on a few walls. He is allergic to nuts and he also has an extra finger on his right hand.

    • "Skin Game" (with Steve Moore, in 2000 AD #1268, 2001)

  • ABC Warriors: "The Zero Option" (with Pat Mills, in 2000 AD #1243-45, 2001)

    • "Asylum 2" (in 2000 AD #1406-1414, 2004)

  • Harry Kipling (with Simon Spurrier):
