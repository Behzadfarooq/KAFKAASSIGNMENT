[edit] Boomtown Institute

[edit] Mentoring America's Hometowns

Due to the phenomenal success of Jack Schultz’s book, Boomtown USA; The7 ½ Keys to Big Success in Small Towns, there is BIG news from Boomtown! Jack and Agracel, Inc., Schultz’s industrial development firm which he founded in 1986, have established a new company, Boomtown Institute. The Institute’s mission is "To provoke innovative thinking, encourage leadership and foster investment in communities across the U.S.A." There are many initiatives planned to achieve this mission. As well as Jack’s national speaking tour that has taken him to 42 states and more than 220 towns, Boomtown Boot Camp, an intensive, two-day, strategic planning session for communities is now being offered. Fasten your seatbelts for this fast paced, action packed summit that’s worth every dollar. Extensive research on matters that affect America’s hometowns is a constant mainstay for the new entity. Future initiatives include additional publications and books, mentoring services to communities as well as media projects that highlight the advantages of living, working and building a business in rural America.

Institute team members include Megan Beeler as Community Project Coordinator, Trisha Mason as Marketing/Public Relations Coordinator, and Michael Rauch as Administrative/Creative Specialist. Coleen Phillips has been appointed President of the Institute. Jack Schultz is CEO.

Jack Schultz’s work as the CEO of Agracel, Inc., a firm that specializes in industrial development in small towns, led him to ponder the roots of rural prosperity. The result of more than three years of research was his popular book, Boomtown USA: The 7 ½ Keys to Big Success in Small Towns. In it, Schultz attempts to answer a perennial question: What separates the thriving towns from the struggling ones? Boomtown clearly has struck a nerve. The book is in its fourth printing, and Schultz has traveled hundreds of thousands of miles since it published. With great enthusiasm, Schultz and team begin this new venture aimed at mentoring America’s hometowns to realize their greatest potential.

[edit] The Agurban® E-zine

The Agurban® E-zine is a free weekly electronic newsletter distributed by Boomtown Institute. Each week, The Agurban focuses on economic development best practices that Jack has seen during his Boomtown USA speaking tour. We share insights on revitalization, regionalism, developing entrepreneurs and many other economic development issues through examples of communities and organizations that are getting it right. We highlight the 7 ½ Keys to Big Success in Small Towns and also share Boomtown Institute’s continuing research on America’s hometowns. Our subscription list has grown to nearly 4,000 since November 2004.
