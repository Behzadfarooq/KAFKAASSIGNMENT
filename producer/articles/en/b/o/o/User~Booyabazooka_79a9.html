User profile: Booyabazooka...

I try to help Wikipedia as much as I can; if you have any suggestions for projects to work on, I'd love to hear them.

I've been around since 2002, although judging by the detailed edit counters, it looks like my addiction really started in February 2006. I don't have any specific area of concentration on Wikipedia; I'm mostly just hopping around from task to task, trying to absorb the full community. There is a lot to learn, as Wikipedia is more extensive than meets the eye. I've tried to summarize my area of focus before, but haven't come up with anything satisfactory.

My idle time I spend doing miscellaneous cleanup... deadends, vandal patrol, infoboxes. Also, every time I encounter a raster image, I feel compelled to convert it to SVG format using Inkscape.

My area of interest for article improvements is computer science, because that's what I know and love. A lot of the CS articles need improved consistency and illustrations.

Among the social aspects of Wikipedia, I most enjoy promoting civility and encouraging the newcomers. A recurring problem I've noticed is people who join, post something, have it deleted, leave in a huff, and tell their friends how terrible Wikipedia is. I believe that friendly reasoning is capable of curtailing this to some degree.
