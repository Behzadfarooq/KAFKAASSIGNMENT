[edit] Tests

[edit] Expected result "-1-"

-{{boolor|1=x|2=y}}- gives -1-

-{{boolor|1=x}}- gives -1-

-{{boolor|2=y}}- gives -1-

-{{boolor|1=|2=y}}- gives -1-

-{{boolor|2=y}}- gives -1-

-{{boolor|1=x|2=}}- gives -1-

[edit] Expected result "--"

-{{boolor|1=|2=}}- gives --

-{{boolor}}- gives --

[edit] Something strange

Something strange happened to this template with the simplification. It stopped recognising the existence of its second argument. I noticed this as I was editing Ladin. {{Language}}, which is used in that article, uses a {{boolor}} to determine whether to include the {{language/official}} sub-template. This sub-template displays the countries in which the language is official ({{{nation}}}) and the regulating authority for the language ({{{agency}}}). The {{boolor}} was set up in {{language}} to call the sub-template if either of these were defined. In most cases, it is more likely that a language is official in a country before it gets a regulting authority. However, Ladin has regulation, but no official status: I was suprised that defining {{{agency}}} without {{{nation}}} did not call the sub-template. Reverting to the original design of {{boolor}} produced the required result. I had checked and double checked my syntax, but that was not the problem. There must be something odd about the way {{if}} handles the output from this template. --Gareth Hughes 10:55, 24 November 2005 (UTC)
