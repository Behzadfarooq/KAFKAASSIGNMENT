No, you misunderstand my point. It's not that "Nats" was the most obvious abbreviation of "Nationals," or that the nickname didn't first derive from the original name of the team. It was simply convenient that "Senators" could yield the same nickname as "Nationals." So the momentum of traditional undeniably did play a significant role, but it's unlikely that the name would have continued to be used had the team changed its official name to something which didn't have the letters NAT somewhere in succession. – Seancdaug 05:41, Apr 9, 2005 (UTC)
