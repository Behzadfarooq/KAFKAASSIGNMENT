Boogiepop and Others (ブギーポップは笑わない, Bugiipoppu wa Warawanai?) is a film directed by Ryu Kaneda, based on the novel Boogiepop and Others from the Boogiepop series, by Kouhei Kadono. The story is presented in vignettes, each major section told from the perspective of a different character. Each character tells the whole story, as they saw it, but the full picture does not become clear until the end. Two Interludes fill in the gaps in the narrative.

[edit] Plot

Spoiler warning: Plot and/or ending details follow.

While in the city, Keiji Takeda sees a ragged-looking man stumbling down the stairs. A strangely-dressed stranger appears and speaks to the distressed man, but when he reveals his face, Takeda recognised the face of Toka Miyashita. Chasing who he thought to be Miyashita, the man tells Takeda that he is Boogiepop. Takeda later asks Miyashita about Boogiepop, but she had never heard the name. Other girls at school, however, tell stories about a whistling shinigami named Boogiepop. Spotting Boogiepop on the school roof, Takeda confronts him.

As the days passed, Takeda and Boogiepop speak with each other from the roof of the school, and become close friends. Boogiepop explains that he was seeking Manticore, and had in the past defeated a serial-killer. One evening, following a strange electrical disturbance, a pillar of light pierces the sky. Boogiepop later explains that his mission is over, and he will disappear. To the end, Tanaka is convinced that Boogiepop was Miyashita's way to fight her inner-demons.

At University, Akio Kimura receivs a letter telling him that Naoko Kamikishiro, his girlfriend back in High School, was dead. He returns to his hometown, and runs into Toka Miyashita. As they speak about relationships, he reminisces about Kamikishiro, and how the two of them, and a third boy named Shiro Tanaka, dated. Kamikishiro had told the two of them that she had found an injured alien, and was looking after him at school. His name was Echoes. Kamikishiro told Kimura that Echoes had been captured and cloned by some organisation, but the clone was imperfect, and turned out to be a monster. Echoes only had a short time left in this world before he would turn into light and return to space. One day, Kimura found Kamikishiro looking for Echoes, who had disappeared without warning: that was the last time he saw Kamikishiro.

Masami Saotome finds Manticore after she had killed a student; elsewhere, Naoko Kamikishiro meets Echoes, collapsed in an alley. Whilst Saotome and Manticore slowly take control of the students using a drug created from Manticore's tears, Kamikishiro nurses Echoes back to health. Meanwhile, Nagi Kirima investigates the drug, believing it to be related to the one Echoes sought. One day, Echoes disappears without a trace.

Five years ago, Kazuko Suema was an intended victim of a serial killer, but they were mysteriously stopped before she could be attacked. Hearing stories of Nagi Kirima attacking people, and possibly killing some, Suema finds Kirima fighting a group of students, only to be disappointed that they were 'ordinary humans'. Unable to let things pass her by again, Suema asks Kirima about what was happening, and learns about Kirima's messiah complex. At night, Sumea watches a pillar of light pierce the sky; the next day, Kirima is back at school as normal. Kirima informs Suema that it was Boogiepop who saved them all five years ago, and saved them all the previous night.

Masami Saotome uses Kei Niitoki to summon Nagi Kirima over the school PA system. Shiro Tanaka arrives, having heard them say the name "Naoko Kamishishiro". Kirima arrives soon after, but bursts in suddenly and knocks them all out with a stun-gun. Ever since he had met Manticore, Saotome Masami has been helping her make slaves of the other students, using her bodily fluids as a drug. They found themselves to be deeply in love, but one day Naoko Kamikishiro walked in on them, looking for Echoes; Manticore killed her immediately. Saotome thought to use this as an opportunity to trap Kirima.

Kirima presents the three of them to Echoes, but he indicated that none of them was Manticore. When Manticore suddenly appears, Echoes and Kirima overpower her, but Saotome stabs Echoes with a pen filled with Manticore's tears. As Echoes falls, Saotome slashes Kirima's throat with a box-cutter, killing her. Tanaka flees immediately, and when Saotome indicates that Manticore should take someone else's face, Niitoki also ran away. Chased by Saotome and Manticore, Niitoki flees towards the sound of whistling. Saotome tackles her, but Boogiepop captures Manticore with his wire. Tanaka fires an arrow, with the poison-filled pen attached, at Manticore, killing it. Echoes returns, and transforms into light. Saotome jumps into Echoes' path, but both he and Manticore are destroyed as a result. Echoes returns to space as a pillar of light, and brings Kirima back to life.

[edit] Background/Production

Director, Kaneda, said he did not wish to simply depict modern children as they were. "Here, the characters that appear on screen embrace all of their loneliness, and that's something that doesn't change in any era." He continues, "I thought perhaps I should turn it into the message that, no matter what, we must express our 'sense of isolation from deep inside that won't let us smile.'"[1] He encouraged the actors to adapt their characters, and allowed ad-libbing[2]

Asumi Miwa's role as Naoko Kamikishiro was considered the most demanding of all. Her scenes were shot in quick succession, so she finished before the rest of the cast.[3] Miwa considered the scene where she meets Echoes for the first time to be the most demanding of all.[4] Sayaka Yoshino gave up her summer break to appear in the film, and had to perform in Boogiepop's "sauna suit" in days reaching 35°C (95°F). With the actors performing entire days in full sun, cool packs had to be brought in to keep them going.[3] Maya Kurosu spend two months in training for her role as Kirima Nagi, so that she could perform in the action scenes.[3]

The climax, filmed from September 15, 1999 (the 25th day of filming), was the greatest challenge. 50 shots had to be taken in 2 days, all at night. A Typhoon reached the Kantō Metropolitan area on that day, but against the forecasts it cleared up before they were due to begin filming. At 6pm, they were ready to begin.[3]

[edit] Reception

The English release of Boogiepop and Others has met mixed reception. Whilst the characters and plot were well received, the special effects and costumes have been described as "campy", but "par for the course of something of this level and budget".[5] It has been primarially recommended to fans of Boogiepop Phantom, so as to gain "a complete understanding of the Boogiepop events".[6]
