Booches

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Booches is a restaurant and pool hall at 110 S. 9th Street in downtown Columbia, Missouri, USA. Their cheeseburgers, which are served on wax paper, were named one of the top ten burgers in America by USA Today in 2000. In 2005, Jerry Shriver of USA Today included Booche's hamburger on the list of top 25 dishes from his "Down-home Dining" project. [1]

Many people rave over the burgers, but neglect to mention some of the finest antique (and playable) pool tables in Missouri. You'll find three full-sized pool tables, two snooker tables, and one billiard table (no pockets) for three cushion billiards play. Booche's hosts yearly invitation-only Snooker and 3-cushion tournaments.

Booches is known for the saying "Closed on Sunday, see you in church," among other witticisms posted on their walls, including "Unattended children will be sold as slaves" and "No sniveling." Also adorning the walls are original comic strips incorporating Booche's regulars, team photos of the Mizzou football teams in the early 1900's, and other old-time sports memorabilia.

Booches received its name when its owner was nicknamed "Booch" by writer Eugene Field. The restaurant has gone through many owners in its time and was male-only until the 1970s. Today, it is a haven for University of Missouri and St. Louis Cardinals sports fans, and has a considerably more family-friendly atmosphere.

You can also purchase handwritten gift certificates at this restaurant.
