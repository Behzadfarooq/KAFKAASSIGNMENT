Booker's

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Booker’s bourbon is one of the small batch bourbons produced by the Jim Beam distillery. It was personally selected from barrels by the late distiller emeritus Booker Noe. Noe originally bottled this straight-from-the-barrel bourbon as gifts to close friends and family, but eventually made it available to the general public in 1988. It is un-cut and un-filtered, just as it was at its inception, and consequently its 126 proof is made to be "cut" with water and/or ice before it is drunk. Booker's bourbon is aged between six and eight years.

Booker’s has won numerous awards, and is considered to be a true connoisseur’s sipping bourbon.
