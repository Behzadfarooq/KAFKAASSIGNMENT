Boorama or Borama is a city in the Awdal region of Somaliland, near the border with Ethiopia. It is located at around 9°55′59″N, 43°10′54″E.

a small section of the city

The population of Borama in 2001 was approximately 200,000 residents. Borama is the commercial center of an arid region in which livestock herding is mixed with subsistence farming.

Borama is an important education center and is the home of the historic Amoud University. The first postwar institution of higher learning throughout Somaliland.

The city also is home to two newly built schools: the Al-Aqsa and Ubaya-Ibnuka'ab Secondary Schools. There are also other primary and preschools in the city. Also, Borama is the home of the first Deaf school in Somaliland. Borama Deaf School trains and provides the deaf children with education that extends up to high school. Since the school is the first and the only one of its type, it has attracted a great number of deaf pupils from across Somaliland and even beyond.

Waa geel dhergay oo iska taagan (beautiful camels)

Borama is a mountainous and hilly city and has one of the wonderful and beautiful landscapes in the country. It has green meadows and fields and a key wildlife area in the country. The fertility and greenery of Borama has attracted many animals into the region: gazelles, birds, camels and other animals as well.

Borama has two major hotels: Hotel Rays and Hotel Nasaaye both in the West side of the city.

Annalena Tonelli, winner of the 2003 Nansen Refugee Award from the UNHCR, she was murder at the Tuberclerosis hospital she founded in Borama in October, 2003.
