[edit] Transportation

Baltimore has served as a major transportation hub for the Mid-Atlantic region of the United States since it's inception as a city. The city is served by several major interstste highways, a major airport, ocean going port facalities, and service along the northeast rail coriordor.

[edit] Road

For the most part the road system in Baltimore is a grid patter, as the city expanded it borders it encompassed new communities that had established road paterns whcih were not always grid patter in type. This grid patter and the circular develpoment around Baltimore led to a number of radial routes leading out of the city, many with the names of thier destnation among them. Some of theose radial routes would include:

  • Eastern Avenue MD 150
  • Philadelphia Road/Pulaski Highway MD 7/US 40
  • Belair Road US 1
  • Harford Road MD 147
  • York Road MD 45
  • Chalres Street MD 139
  • Falls Road MD 25
  • Park Heights Avenue MD 129
  • Resiterstown Raod MD 140
  • Liberty Heights Avenue MD 26

  • Frederick Road MD 144

Baltimore served by several major Interstate highways with I-95 (by way of the Fort McHenery Tunnell), I-83 and I-70 (its eastern terminus is just beyond the city limits) serving at the major interstates for Baltimore. I-97, with an end just south of baltimore serves as a connector along the Baltimore-Annapolic corrirod.

Along with the major interstates, Baltimore is served by a Beltway, I-695 (the Baltimore Beltway), linking the communites that surround the city. The Harbor Tunnel Threwway, I-895, serves as a by-pass of the downton area that I-95 serves.

Just out side of the city I-795, the Northwest Expressway, links the Beltway to the communites in northwest Baltimore County and beyond, though orgional plans had it entering the city. To the south, and also outside of the city is I-195 (Airport Connector) which is an expressway connection from I-95 and MD 295 to BWI Aiport.

[edit] Public Transportion

Public transit in Baltimore City is provided by the Maryland Transit Administration. Baltimore City has many bus routes, and a light rail and a subway system. Additionally, MARC commuter rail connects Washington, DC's Union Station with the city's two rail stations, Camden Station and Penn Station.
