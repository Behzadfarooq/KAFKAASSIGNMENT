Book swapping

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Book swapping or book exchange is the practice of a swap of books between one person and another. Practiced among book groups, friends and colleagues at work, it provides an inexpensive way for people to exchange books, find out about new books and obtain a new book to read without having to pay.

[edit] College book exchange programs

Many colleges and universities provide a book exchange program to help students save money. Free sites such as The Book eXchange Network also help students exchange books across multiple campuses. Other sites such as KYBookExchange.com help students exchange books at individual campuses.

[edit] Timeline of significant developments

With the growth of the Internet, book swapping Internet sites are becoming more common.

  • The first such site was SF-Books.com, which introduced the 'credit' system to book swapping. This allowed the swapper to choose a book from the aggregate collection of all members, not just the individual he or she sent the book to. SF-Books.com specialises in science fiction and fantasy books, whereas other book-swap sites are more generalised.
  • In March of 2001, BookCrossing is launched.
  • In August of 2003, The Book Exchange Network is launched to help students buy and sell books to each other.
  • In February 2004, the first book swap site in the UK.
  • In September of 2004, PaperBackSwap is launched.
  • In September of 2004, TitleTrader launches.
  • In December of 2004, FrugalReader launches.
  • Bookins officially launched in August 2005.
  • Novel Action launched as the first book exchange with centralized inventory in July, 2006.
  • WhatsOnMyBookshelf launched on July 21, 2006.
  • Bookmooch became publicly available on August 6, 2006.
  • In December 2006, Readersunited.com was launched.

[edit] Book swapping sites by geography

[edit] Germany

  • Bookswapper - Non profit Bookswap in Germany for English books only

[edit] Global

  • Bookcrossing.com - Book tracking site
  • BookMooch - Worldwide site
  • Readersunited.com - A bookswapping site aimed at English speakers around the world.
  • SF-Books.com - Specialising in science fiction, fantasy & horror books
  • Titletrader.com
  • WhatsOnMyBookshelf - Global site

[edit] Israel

  • AngloBooks - Free swapping of English books in Israel

[edit] Philippines

  • Bookswappers326 - Book swapping community in the Philippines

[edit] United Kingdom

  • BookHopper - Free Uk book pool where you can choose any book you like without having to arrange a swap
  • Nothingbutbooks - A free UK Book Swapping Service
  • Readitswapit.co.uk - UK site
  • RevolvingBooks.co.uk - UK book exchange service - book for credit/credit for book system

[edit] United States

  • AmericasBookShelf.com - The internets only Netflixian style book club,any book any time
  • The Book eXchange Network
  • Bookins.com - Free to send books, earn points, postage printed from the web, flat rate shipping, US only
  • Frugalreader.com - US site
  • KYBookExchange.com - A Book Exchange service for several schools in KY - connects local students
  • Novel Action - Centralized book exchange store; Flate rate shipping; US only
  • Paperbackswap.com - US site
  • PhpBookExchange - GPL book exchange software designed for local communities (such as colleges)
