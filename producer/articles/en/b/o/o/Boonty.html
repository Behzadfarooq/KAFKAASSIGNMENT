Boonty is a leading [1] global supplier of digital distribution solutions for online and PC Gaming. [2]

The company’s white label casual game platform is utilized by over 100 partners worldwide, including Internet portals, ISPs, and mobile operators.

Boonty has also launched its own branded destination sites. Boonty.com is available in localized versions for the United States, France, UK, Belgium , Italy, Sweden, Norway, Denmark, Japan, Korea, Mexico, Austria, Australia, Taiwan, Germany, Portugal, Finland, Singapore, The Netherlands, and Spain. [3]

In September 2006, Boonty launched Clickgames.com, [4] a destination for 100% free, first-run casual games. The available portfolio features popular games including Zuma, Luxor, Diner Dash, Poker Superstars Invitational Tournament, and Virtual Villagers. Free play is supported by advertising, ensuring there is no spyware or adware. [5]

In October 2006, Boonty announced the acquisition of Beijing based casual game developer Gamehub. Via this acquisition, Boonty is strengthening the Company's focus on providing high-quality, advertising supported social casual games featuring multi-player and community features, game item purchasing, and personalization functionality.[6]

[edit] Corporate History

Boonty was launched in Paris, France in 2001 by brothers Mathieu and Romain Nouzareth. Prior to establishing Boonty, the brothers founded WebConcept, one of the first e-business consulting companies in France, which they sold to Sweden’s IconMedialab (Euronext: icon) in 1999. [7]

Boonty Inc., based in New York, was founded in July 2004. Boonty Asia, based in Singapore, followed in September that same year. Boonty Tokyo was founded in early 2006. Local offices have also been opened in Seoul and Shanghai. [8]

[edit] Boonty Services

Boonty provides a turn-key solution for digital game content and management. The platform’s functionalities include:

  • Game Catalogue Management
  • Editorial Management
  • Download Management
  • Digital Rights Management
  • Payment Management
  • Revenue Sharing Management (Clearing)
  • Marketing Analysis
    • Data mining
    • Sales Analysis
    • Customer Relationship Management

[edit] Portal & ISP Clients

Boonty provides a turn-key technological platform for game distribution to a network of over 100 partner portals, ISPs, and mobile phone operators in Asia, Europe, and North America. [9]

[edit] Game catalogue

Boonty’s catalogue comprises more than 1,500 PC games and 2,000 mobile games from over 100 of the world’s leading game developers including Atari, CodeMasters, Disney, EA, Eidos, Hachette Multimedia, MC2, Popcap, Sega, and UbiSoft. [10]

Games are available as try-before-you-buy and as browser games. [11]

Business models include: [12]

  • Subscription
  • Advertising-supported
  • Microtransactions (item purchasing, avatars)
