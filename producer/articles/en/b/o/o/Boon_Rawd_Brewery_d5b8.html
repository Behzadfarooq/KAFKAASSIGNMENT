Boon Rawd Brewery (Thai: บริษัท บุญรอดบริวเวอรี่ จำกัด) is an Asian brewery founded in 1933 by Boonrawd Srethabutra in Thailand. Their best known product is the pale lager Singha.

[edit] Brief details

Boon Rawd Brewery was the first of its kind in Thailand, and produces beer under the Singha brand name. The brewery was founded by Boonrawd Srethabutra, on whom HM King Prajadhipok Rama VII bestowed the aristocratic title of Phraya Bhirom Bhakdi. The brewery is still under the management of the Bhirom Bhakdi family (3rd and 4th generations).

Boon Rawd bought two German breweries in Hartmannsdorf and Mittweida in Saxony in 1994, which until 2001 produced Singha beer and Singha Gold for the Europe market, along with their traditional brands. Today, all of the Singha brands are produced only in Thailand, to preserve the original taste for the export market.

[edit] Brands

Boon Rawd produce Singha Beer, Singha Light Beer, Singha Soda Water, Singha Water, Leo Beer, Thai Beer, and Moshi Green Tea.

[edit] Singha

Singha beer

Singha, (Thai: สิงห์, pronounced sing or singh) , is a 6% abv pale lager. It is also available in 5% abv draught version as Singha Draft, and the new 3.5% abv Singha Light introduced in 2006.

Beer production capacity is 1 billion liters per year. Until recently Singha was the best selling beer in Thailand, but is now challenged by the cheaper and more potent Chang, brewed by ThaiBev (formerly the Beer Thai Company).
