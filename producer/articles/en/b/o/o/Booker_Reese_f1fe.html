Booker Reese

Booker Reese (born September 20, 1959 in Jacksonville, Florida) is a former American Football defensive end who played four seasons for the Tampa Bay Buccaneers and the Los Angeles Rams from 1982 to 1985 in the National Football League. Reese is considered as the biggest bust in Buccanners history. [1]

Reese played college football in Bethune-Cookman College and was drafted in the second round of the 1982 NFL Draft by the Buccaneers after trading a first round pick in the 1983 NFL Draft to get him. Reese best season in the NFL came in 1983 in which he played all 16 games that season, making one sack and two interceptions. Resse only lasted two seasons with the Bucs before getting traded to the Rams for a 12th round draft pick in 1984 and played 11 more games with the Rams before retiring because of drug problems.

Reese was convicted for cocaine possession in 1999 and is currently behind bars at Okaloosa Correctional Institution in Florida.
