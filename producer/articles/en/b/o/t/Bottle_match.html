Bottle match

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

The Bottle Match is a varsity match played between the Camborne School of Mines (CSM) and the Royal School of Mines (RSM), Imperial College London. The first recorded match between the two sides took place on 16 December 1902, making it the second oldest varsity match in the world.

The Bottle Match started as a rugby match but as the schools sporting interests diversified other sports began to be played and from 2006 is now made up of seven games played between the institutions: men's and women's rugby, football, men's and womenâ€™s hockey, squash and golf.

The Bottle is traditionally held during the month of February normally on the last weekend of the month. Ancient rivalry between the RSM and CSM ensures that the competition gets fiercer by the year.

[edit] Trophies

The winner of the Bottle trophy itself hinges entirely on the outcome of the rugby match. The winning team take home the 'Bottle', a 3-ft tall, replica beer bottle, with the two institutions crests engraved onto it.

The winners of the men's hockey take the Sharpley Cup, a silver sup presented by J.E. Sharpley RSMA in the 1950s.

The winners of the ladies' hockey take the Golders Cup, a small silver cup presented By Golder Associates the 1990s.
