[edit] Song information

The song was released as Michael Gray's second single in mid-2006 (see 2006 in music). While "Borderline" failed to match the success of its predecessor "The Weekend" it entered the top twenty on various charts including - the Official UK. Singles Chart and Dance Chart, UK. Upfront Club and Australian ARIA Club charts. It often fell off then re-entered the Australian ARIA Dance chart, for example it charted at #20 on 12/02/2007, months after its initial release.

Produced by Michael Gray, the song contains a sample from Washington's "Ready For Your Love". It was released as Gray's second solo single in July 2006 in the UK. It debuted on the UK singles chart at #58 and then rose to its peak at #12. It features guest vocalist Shelly Poole (formerly of Alisha's Attic) on vocals, she also co-wrote the track. It got to the B-list on Radio 1 in late July. It has been included on many compilation albums.

[edit] Music video

"Borderline" video

The plotless music video does not include Michael or Shelly. It features dancer Keeley Malone lying on a sun chair touching another dancer and lipsynching parts of the song - this scene switches back and forth through others which are of female dancers (including Shelina Gallacher among others) on rooftops of buildings in London (St. Paul's Cathedral can be seen at times in the background). The outfits in the video consist of what looks like; a white schoolgirl top and black tie, a black coat, a black bikini bottom, black stockings and black heels (as seen in the video screenshot). It has been banned on 3 UK TV channels. [1] It was directed by Phil Griffin.
