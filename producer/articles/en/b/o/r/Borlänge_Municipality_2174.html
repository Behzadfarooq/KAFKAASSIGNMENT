Borlänge Municipality is a Swedish municipality in Dalarna County, in central Sweden, with an area of 586.4 km². The municipality has a population of 47,206; of these, around 40,000 live in the town Borlänge, which is the seat of the municipality.

[edit] History

Modern Borlänge emerged in the former municipality of Stora Tuna during the last few decades of the 19th century when the railway and the industries reached the district. Originally Borlänge had been the name of a tiny village and the first noted information regarding Borlänge is from 1390. The village were insignificant up until 1870. In 1875 the railway between Falun and Ludvika, via Borlänge were inaugurated and at the same time the construction of Domnarfvets Jernverk, the ironworks of neighbouring village Domnarvet, had started. Thanks to the railway station the village of Borlänge became of high service importance to the ironworks.

In 1891 Borlänge parted from the Stora Tuna municipality and in 1898 Borlänge became a town in its own right, "köping", with 1 300 inhabitants. But, up until this day Borlänge still belong to the parish of Stora Tuna. In circa 1900 Stora Kopparbergs Bergslag, the owners of the ironworks in Domnarvet, built a papermill in the contiguous village of Kvarnsveden. Many of the residents of Borlänge emigrated to the Untied States in the late 19th and early 20th centuries.

In 1944 the City of Borlänge of Borlänge received its Royal charter after the market town of Borlänge emerged with the industrial towns of Domnarvet and Kvarnsveden. In 1971 the municipality of Borlänge were establish when the municipality of Stora Tuna emerged with the City of Borlänge.

During all of the 20th century Borlänge has been a typical industrial city with a relatively good economic growth, but today the service industry is spreading.

[edit] Demographics

With its 40,880 inhabitants, Borlänge is the largest city in Dalecarlia (Dalarna), surpassing its northern neighbour Falun, 20 kilometres away. Together the two cities form a region with close to 100,000 inhabitants. First or second generation immigrants make up about 12,7% ot the population, with Finland, Turkey and Somalia being the most common countries of origin.

[edit] Politics

Chairman of the Municipal Executive Committee is Nils Persson, from the Swedish Social Democratic Party. Opposition leader is Ulla Olsson, from the Moderate Party. As in most of Dalarna, the Social Democrats hold a strong position. As a result of the Swedish municipal elections, 2006 the social Democrats are holding 28 of 61 mandates and thereby the largest party in Borlänge. The Moderate Party, which are the second largest party, hold 11 mandates.

[edit] Industry

Borlänge has always been an industrial town surrounding the iron mill of Domnarvet (SSAB) and the paper mill of Kvarnsveden (Stora Enso). The city has two state authorities: the department of rails (Banverket) and the department of roads (Vägverket). Suitable for a city which structure is severely divided by rails and roads, with a modern city centre.

[edit] Shopping

Borlänge promotes itself as The Number One Shopping City in Dalarna. This is possible due to the Kupolen area, situated about 1 kilometer west of the city centre. The Kupolen area includes Kupolen ('The Dome') Shopping Centre (Kupolen Köpcentrum in Swedish) and the surrounding region. Though the district was first built to catch the interest from bypassers it has withdrawn the focus from the nearby downtown area and has overtaken the role as the pulsating heart of Borlänge.

[edit] Sport

The city has a previously successful football team, although currently in a free fall through the series. IK Brage, named after the Norse god of poetry, has a history of 18 seasons in the Swedish Premier Division (Allsvenskan). Brage play in green and white and host their home games at Domnarvsvallen Stadium in Borlänge with a seating capacity of 6,500.

[edit] Notability

  • Borlänge was notable for having a relatively high crime rate during the 1990's.
  • Borlänge is probably the municipality with the highest roundabout density in Sweden.
  • Borlänge has among the best tap water in Sweden.
