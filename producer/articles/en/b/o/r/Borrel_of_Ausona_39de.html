Borrel of Ausona

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

The territories of Borrel.

Borrel or Borrell was the first count of Cerdanya, Urgel, and Ausona from between 797 and 799 to his death in 820. He was a Visigoth nobleman, probably from Cerretana (Cerdanya, that is).

In the final years of the eighth century, the Franks under Charlemagne and his son Louis the Pious, king of Aquitaine, were subduing the Marca Hispanica and expanding southwards into Moorish territory. When Urgel and Cerdanya were subdued around 798, Borrel was appointed count. He took a very active part in the subsequent conquest of Ausona in 799 and the successful siege of Barcelona in 801. He may have been named count in Ausona as a reward for his services.

In 804 and 805, he participated in the expeditions to Tortosa, but no in the subsequent campaigns of 807, 808, and 809. He died in 820 and Ausona was given to the new count of Barcelona, Rampo. Urgel and Cerdanya were granted to Aznar Galíndez.
