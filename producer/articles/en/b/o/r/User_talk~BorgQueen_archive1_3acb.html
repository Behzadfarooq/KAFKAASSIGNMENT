[edit] Moreton bay bug

The taxobox looks good (except for one small mistake in the authority, which I corrected).

You have included more ranks in the taxobox than usual. In general, you're encouraged to include only the main ranks in the taxobox (kingdom, phylum, class, order, family, genus, species), plus only those minor ranks that are important for the rank you are describing. So the article Scyllaridae (a family) shows the minor ranks between order and family, but not the ones above "order"; an article on a species like Moreton bay bug should probably not include the infraorder and suborder. I'm not a biologist, so I do not know the importance of those ranks; if you had a special reason to include them, that's fine. For example, the subphylum Crustacea is commonly known, and the inclusion of that rank does convey useful information to the reader, so it should stay.

Only thing missing now is a picture; I've looked for it on commons, and on images.google.com, but could not find one that we can use. Pity. Eugene van der Pijll 11:46, 10 December 2005 (UTC)

List of zoologists by author abbreviation may be useful. Also, you may just add (taxonomy) to the name: [[Lund (taxonomy)|]]. That redirect works correctly for most abbreviations. Eugene van der Pijll 12:25, 10 December 2005 (UTC)
Oh. It links to Peter Wilhelm Lund (1801-1880), who described the species in 1793. There might be a problem here. Perhaps this is the point at which we ask the real experts to join in, at Wikipedia talk:WikiProject Tree of Life.
