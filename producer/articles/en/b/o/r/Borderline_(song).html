"Borderline" is the fourth U.S. single from American dance-pop singer Madonna released from her 1983 debut album, Madonna, re-issued in Europe (1985) as The First Album.

[edit] Song information

"Borderline" was both composed and produced by Reggie Lucas. The 7" Version, 12" Version & Dub Version were remixed by John "Jellybean" Benitez, Madonna's then boyfriend. In 2006 Grammy Award-winning singer Jody Watley covered the song for her album The Makeover. Rob Thomas has also been known to cover this song live.

"Borderline" became a modest Adult Contemporary hit and another top five hit on the Hot Dance Music/Club Play chart. The single's sales were also strong, and eventually led to a gold certification.

"Borderline" became the first of a 17-hit string of U.S. Billboard Hot 100 Top 10 singles (ending in 1989 with "Oh Father" stalling at #20). Counting Top 20 singles, "Borderline" was Madonna's second single of a 26-single-string of top 20 singles between 1984 and 1992 ("Deeper and Deeper" was Madonna's last consecutive U.S. single to crack the Top 20). "Borderline" slinked its way up the Billboard Hot 100 for a steady 13 weeks, finally peaking at a lofty #10 the week of June 16, 1984.

In Australia, "Borderline" became Madonna's third consecutive Top 20 single, reaching #12.

When first released in the UK, it had a very weak showing of #56 as the third single from the parent "Madonna" album, following "Lucky Star". However, upon re-entry in January 1986, after a phenomenally successful U.K. chart year in 1985 which saw Madonna accrue 8 top ten hits, the track eventually peaked at #2. The 1984 release was packed in the UK with a 7" single of Holiday, and shrinkwrapped with a cover sticker. This is extremely rare, and sells for up to £350.

Lines from "Borderline" are used on Madonna's song "Push" from her 2005 album Confessions on a Dance Floor.

[edit] Music video

Madonna in the "Borderline" music video, directed by Mary Lambert.

This was the first video that Madonna made with Mary Lambert, who would later also direct the videos "Like a Virgin", "Material Girl", "La Isla Bonita" and "Like a Prayer". In this video Madonna is a colorful street girl, toying with one of the boys. A photographer then gets interested in her and she lets him take pictures while she sips champagne. However, Madonna quickly gets bored and tries to win back the heart of her boyfriend. Lambert creates a contrast with color scenes in the street (with the boyfriend) and black and white scenes during the photoshoots. It was the first Madonna video that received sustained play on MTV.

Then unknown Actor John Leguizamo is in the video as one of Madonna's boyfriend's friends.
