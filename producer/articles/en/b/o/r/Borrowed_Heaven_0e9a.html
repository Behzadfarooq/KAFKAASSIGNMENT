Borrowed Heaven is an album by The Corrs and it was produced by Olle Romo. The album was released 31 May 2004 (see 2004 in music).

For this album Sharon Corr managed to write and play and make a significant contribution with songs such as 'Hideaway', 'Long Night', and 'Goodbye'.

The Corrs created a folkier, edgier, and more guitar oriented album than previous albums, notably their year 2000 album In Blue. This is because Jim Corr plays most of the guitars and keyboards.

Reviews for this album varied greatly. Fans of the band generally agree the album represents a return of sorts to the group's Celtic folk pop origins after the much 'poppier' (see pop music) In Blue released in 2000 (see 2000 in music).

The band released three singles from this album: 'Summer Sunshine', 'Angel', and 'Long Night'.

[edit] Success

The album was a great success in their homeland Ireland where it peaked at number 1 with all three singles entering the Irish music charts. The album was greeted with success in western Europe, charting in nearly every country. Success in eastern Europe was varied from number 1 chart positions to not charting at all. The album also entered the top ten albums in the United States with the single Summer Sunshine entering the top 20 singles. The album also charted successfully in Australia and Japan.

With album sales totalling just over 14 million, it was the highest selling album of The Corrs' career (aside from the Best of The Corrs compilation album.) It also showed that the The Corrs were able to continually outsell their previous albums, as their album sales continue to rise.
