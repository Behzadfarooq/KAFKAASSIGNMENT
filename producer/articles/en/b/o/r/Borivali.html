Borivali

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Borivali (Marathi बोरीवली) is the name of a railway station on the Mumbai suburban railway on the Western Railway (India) railway line. This suburb lying on the north-west corner of Mumbai is also spelled as Borivli or Borivali. The estimated population of Borivali in 1991 was over 6 lakhs (609617). The Borivali railway station is a terminus for most of the slow and fast trains on the Mumbai Suburban Railway system. It also serves as the final city-limit stop for all mail / express trains on WR (now including the August Kranti Rajdhani Express to New Delhi) before leaving Mumbai. As of 2006, there are plans to extend the Harbour Line to Borivali, and expansion plans are in full steam at the railway station.

Accessibility

Borivali is approx. 18 km from Mumbai Airport and approx. 32 km from Churchgate Railway Station.

[edit] Landmarks

Mumbai is one of the few metropolitan cities in the world which boasts of a natural animal and plant wildlife reserve within the city limits. The park is called Sanjay Gandhi National Park, and it lies east section of Borivali near the Western Express Highway (NH-8). It has a few lions and panthers and also includes a 4th century B.C. archaeological site called Kanheri Caves and Mandpeshwar Caves. The 2005 census of Leopards was 68.

Borivali has good educational instititions and schools like the Saint Francis D'Assisi High School, Swami Vivekanand International School [S.V.I.S.], Ajmera Global School (formerly known as S.S. Ajmera High School), Our Lady Of Remedy High School, Gopalji Hemraj High School (GH), Gokhale Education Society, Don Bosco High School,Himalaya High School near poisar, and Jr. College, Mary Immaculate, St. Anne's, St. Xaviers, Suvidya Group of schools & colleges, J B Khot High School, Sardar Vallabhabhi Patel Vidyalaya and Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel Polytechnic by the manan patel education trust.

There is also a very famous Ganapati Temple called Shree Swayambhu Ganesh Devsthan popularly known as "Vajira Ganpati" [1] located in Borivali. Devotees flock to the temple every Tuesday, causing traffic problems.

Club Aquaria is a Five Star Club nested in the green suburbs, just a few minutes from Shanti Ashram and Borivali(W) Station. The Club consisting of over 5 acres of land has unique facilities like a bowling Alley (fitted by Brunswick Corp. USA), a water park consisting of a wave pool, a lazy river, water slides ( modelled on the lines of Sun City and installed by the White Water Co. UK), glass back squash courts , badminton and tennis courts , Cards & billiards rooms and a health club with gymnasium and sauna / steam chamber.

Borivali also has an ancient Church, Our Lady of Immaculate Conception Church, Mt. Poinsur, popularly known as IC church, located close to Shanti Ashram. The Church has more than a lakh families of Catholics, most of them original settlers from Mangalore, Kerala or Goa. I.C. Church is surrounded by many colonies such as I.C Colony, LIC Colony, Madonna Colony, Marian Colony and Kanderpada.

The name is derived from the Church of the Immaculate Conception, or I.C Church.

[edit] The Church

The original church was built in the year 1547 by the Franciscans. It had a monastery on top of the church. The New church was built by 5 patrons from Bandra in the early 1900s.

The parish is one of the largest in Mumbai. The feast of the church is held on The second Saturday/Sunday of December in the time of Advent.



Extravaganza, Essel World & the largest water park in Asia, Water Kingdom are located at Gorai Creek, just west of Borivali.

Apart from all these, Borivali also provides good markets for shopping. Major Shopping areas are Borivali Station(W) where complexes such as Indraprastha, Thakkar Mall, Goyal Arcade provide varied choices to shop.

On July 26, 2005, the torrential downpour that flooded most of Mumbai and surrounding regions severely affected areas along the Dahisar river. The river flows from the Sanjay Gandhi National Park, and has its origins at the Kanheri caves. There are a couple of small dams on the river that were allegedly opened on the fateful day. One can reach the Gorai beach by taking a ferry from the Gorai creek, which is in Borivali.

On 11 July 2006 the area was affected by the Mumbai train bombings. One bomb exploded at this location in a train during peak office hours.
