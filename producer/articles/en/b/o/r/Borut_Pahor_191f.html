Borut Pahor (born on 2 November 1963 in Postojna) is a Slovenian politician and Member of the European Parliament. He is the president of the Social Democrats, which are part of the Party of European Socialists, and sits on the European Parliament's Committee on Budgetary Control and its Committee on Constitutional Affairs.

He is also a substitute for the Committee on Foreign Affairs and vice-chair of the delegation to the EU–Croatia Joint Parliamentary Committee. He is one of the most popular Slovenian politicians and is considering running for the office of President of Slovenia in the 2007 presidential elections.

[edit] Career

  • 1987: Political science graduate, majoring in international politics
  • 1990-1992: Delegate of the Assembly of the Republic of Slovenia
  • 1992-2000: Member of the National Assembly of Slovenia
  • 2000-2004: President of the National Assembly
  • Chairman of the Committee on Youth Issues
  • 1990-1992: Vice-Chairman of the Foreign Relations Committee
  • 1992-1996: Member of the European Affairs Committee, the Committee for Supervision of the Work of the Security and Intelligence Services, and the Defence Committee
  • 1995: Chairman of the Foreign Policy Committee
  • 1996-1997: Vice-President of the National Assembly
  • 1996-2000: Member of the Foreign Relations Committee, the Constitutional Commission and the Defence Committee
  • 2000-2004: President of the National Assembly and Chairman of the Constitutional Commission
  • 1993-1997: Vice-President of the Združena lista socialnih demokratov (ZLSD - United List of Social Democrats)
  • since 1997: President of the SD (former ZLSD)
  • 1996: Head of the Slovenian delegation to the Parliamentary Assembly of the Council of Europe (PACE) (1993-2000) and one of its Vice-Presidents
  • 2001: honorary member of PACE
  • 1993-1997: Member of the Assembly of Western European Union
  • 1995-1996: Member of the Executive Committee of the Interparliamentary Union
