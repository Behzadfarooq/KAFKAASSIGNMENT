[edit] A story I think you'll like

Your Jimmy Wales quote reminded of a 30 year old memory. The year was 1975. I was still in college studying digital electrical engineering. Gene Roddenberry spoke on campus, and I attended. He screened the pilot of the original Star Trek series, and he told some anectodes about production and getting the series past the suits at the network. But then he diverged into what technology would be giving us in the future. That night he predicted that the day would come within 20 years that digital technology would connect everybody in the world. And he specifically spoke of a time when "the entire body of human knowledge" would be available electronically at every desktop. I went home that night and related what I'd heard tomy roommate. He said something like, "You mean that in 20 years everybody will have their own computer? That's B.S." We made a $100 bet that night whether that much would come true. I think I won that bet, but these many years on, I have lost track of him, so I can't hit him up for a concession (and less importantly the $100). Although not everything Roddenberry predicted that night happened by 1995, I certainly had my own computer and internet access by then, and there was already widespread access to some of knowledge held at universities. And today, well it's hard to see how anything will stop Roddenberry's prediction about the entire body of human knowledge from coming true, and soon. So I thought that somebody who calls herself BorgQueen would find this recollection interesting. Karlhahn 19:36, 26 December 2006 (UTC)

Thanks for the recollection. Yes, Gene Roddenberry was a visionary and made a remarkable contribution to the humankind. --BorgQueen 20:12, 26 December 2006 (UTC)
