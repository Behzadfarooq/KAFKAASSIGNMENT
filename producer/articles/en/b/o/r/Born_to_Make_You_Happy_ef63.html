"Born to Make You Happy" is the fourth European single from American pop singer Britney Spears' debut album, ...Baby One More Time. In Canada and Europe, among other regions, it was released during the fourth quarter of 1999.

The song was written by Andreas Carlsson and Kristian Lundin, and was also produced by the latter. In this mid-tempo ballad, Spears recalls a relationship she desires to correct, not quite understanding what went wrong as she comes to the realisation that she was "born to make you happy."

The single is widely regarded as Spears' greatest to date with critic Tim Finney saying "it couldn’t be more at odds with the prevailing nature of disposable pop today."

[edit] Music video

Spears in the futuristic room.

Directed by Billie Woodruff in Los Angeles, the music video for Born to Make You Happy begins with a shot of Spears sleeping. In her dream she is sitting in a futuristic room with several different levels. Following this part of the video, Spears appears atop the apartment building where this room is found, performing a dance segment in a red top and black skirt. The last part of the dream has her love interest come to her room to see her. The video concludes with the pair having a pillow fight.

[edit] Chart success

The single was originally given a UK-only release, where it was commercially successful, debuting at number one and becoming the 32nd best seller of 2000, selling a total of 305,000 copies. It was later released in the rest of Europe where it made the top ten across the board. Born To Make You Happy was also released in Canada where it failed to break into the top twenty. Although the single wasn't officially released in Mexico, radio stations began playing it and it became massively popular.
