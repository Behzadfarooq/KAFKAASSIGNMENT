Capcom released the original Rockman game, first in the Rockman series, in 1987, and since then the series has released over 100 games on a multitude of platforms. The straightforward action series has since expanded into the darker Rockman X series (and its sequel, Rockman Zero) and the 3D RPG Rockman Dash series, and was completely reimagined from the ground up in the Rockman EXE series.

Each incarnation - save the last - shares a single timeline with the others, and resolving what-happens-when is a rather difficult and involved task. Because of the uncertainty of the dates involved - most games only specify a century, and some are even less specific - the years given here should be taken with a grain of salt, not as the canon word of Capcom. Nonetheless, what follows, cribbed from multiple sources, is a rough timeline of the events surrounding the Rockman series.

[edit] 20th Century

1959-ish: Future robot designers Thomas Right and Albert W. Wily are born. (Wily is 57 by R&F).

(late) 1980s: Dr. Light and Dr. Wily receive degrees in Electrical Engineering from Robert University.

(late) 1990s: Dr. Light and Dr. Wily found Light Labs, an independent robotics research group. Early creations in artificial intelligence, materials science, and robotics itself revolutionize the industry.

[edit] 21st Century

2000s: Computing and robotics technology, especially in the field of artificial intelligence, has vastly improved. Dr. Right and Dr. Wily work together to create many different robots that are beneficial to soceity, including industrial and military robots as well as ones for domestic use. They become recognised as the world's foremost leaders in the field of robotics. In their AI experiments, the two work together to develop the Robot Master, a humanoid robot which can operate independently of human supervision. Their first successful attempt at the project, a military robot codenamed Blues; the robot, due to its programmed sense of independence, rejects the scientists' authority and escapes. Intending to salvage the useful work, Dr. Right has the idea to build a paired unit, so that each RM will have a peer. Rock, a tool-using lab assistant, and Roll, a housekeeper, are created as "twins", and the results are amazing. With the success of these two robots, the two scientists work together to create a series of industrial robots: Cutman, Elecman, Bombman, Fireman, Iceman, and Gutsman. These robots were designed to perform dangerous tasks in construction, land reclamation, and public works.

2007: The Nobel Prize is awarded for the creation of the Robot Master to Dr. Right. The Nobel committee does not, however, recognize the contributions of Dr. Wily. This trend is mirrored in other fields, with Dr. Right claiming for the 4th year running the top prize in the LIT Manual Design contest; Dr. Wily receives top nominations, but his work only nets him runner-up status.

2008: (Events of Rockman - officially, December of 200X). Dr. Wily grows jealous of Dr. Light taking the spotlight and feels that he should get fair credit for his accomplishments. In order to extract an apology, he reprograms the eight remaining Robot Masters, but fails to compensate for Rock and Roll's unique architecture. With the six industrial Robot Masters serving as commanders for his new army, he sets off to take over the world, starting with Right Labs' home city. Dr. Right, realizing that the armies and police would be powerless against this new robot force, struggles to find a new way to stop the madman. Rock volunteers to be converted into a fighting robot, and the super fighting robot Rockman is born.

2009: (Events of Rockman 2, Rockman World). A few months later, Wily, having escaped justice, modifies the designs for the existing Robot Masters, creating eight more. He again seizes control of the city, but this time he is prepared for the meddling of Mega Man and has tailored his robot army to destroy the boy robot. Rockman prevails, and Wily repents, once more becoming a lead researcher at Right Labs (now renamed Right and Wily Industries in his honor).

2010: (Events of Rockman 3 - the first game to give a year of 20XX). Right and Wily Industries has made further advancements in space and mining technology, providing the technology and robot workers to oversee mining in the asteroid belt. The reformed Dr. Wily proposes a peacekeeper robot, "Gamma", to replace Rockman as the protector of the public. For this purpose, they need energy crystals found on eight of the mining facilities - but the robots in the asteroid belt have gone out of control. Rockman, paired with the newly-built mobility and transportation support mecha "Rush", is sent to defeat the rebelling Robot Masters and retrieve the crystals. Yet, when he returns with them, Wily runs off with Gamma and attempts to use it for world domination. Due to the actions of Rockman and a mysterious red robot - later revealed to be the enigmatic Blues, the first Robot Master - Gamma is destroyed, but in the confusion, Dr. Wily has gone missing.

2011: (Events of Rockman World 2, Rockman Megaworld, Rockman 4)

2012: (Events of Rockman World 3, Rockman's Soccer, Wily and Right's RockBoard, Rockman 5)

2013: (Events of Rockman World 4, Superadventure Rockman, Rockman World 5)

2014: (Events of Rockman 6, Rockman 7, Rockman Kiki Ippatsu, Rockman: The Power Battle - the latter three take place within a span of 2 weeks)

2015: (Events of Rockman 8, Rockman & Forte)

2016: (Events of Rockman 2: The Power Fighers, Rockman Battle & Chase, Rockman & Forte: Challenger From the Future)

18 September 20XX: X is sealed. (This happens near the end of Dr. Right's life, so it is not likely for quite a while.)
