Robert James Hamilton "Bob" Lambert (born 18 July 1874 in Dublin; died 24 March 1956 in Dublin) was an Irish cricketer. He was a right-handed batsman and a right-arm off-break bowler.

He made his debut for Ireland against I Zingari in August 1893, and went on to play for Ireland more than fifty times in an almost 40 year career, playing his last game against the MCC in August 1930 at the age of 56. 23 of his matches for Ireland had first-class status.

He played two other first-class matches, one for London County against Lancashire in 1903 and one for Woodbrook Club and Ground against South Africa in 1912.

He was also president of the Irish Cricket Union twice, and was an Irish selector for many years. Outside of cricket, he also represented Ireland at badminton. On his death in 1956, the Wisden Cricketers' Almanack called him the best all-rounder produced by Ireland. His brother and two sons also played cricket for Ireland.
