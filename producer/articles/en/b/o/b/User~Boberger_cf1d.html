Bengt Oberger, living in the suburb of Mälarhöjden in Stockholm, Sweden.

Below is a map of Stockholm from 1888, when the first houses were erected between the estates of Pettersberg and Hägersten just south of lake Mälaren (near the south western corner of the map).

One of my interests is visual arts, and you will find below a gallery of photos I´ve shot of (mostly Swedish) sculptures, many of the also captured in wikipedia articles (mostly in Swedish, sometimes articles in English, German or French].


See also User_Boberger on Wikipedia in Swedish boberger


[edit] Mälarhöjden, on old maps of Stockholm


Stockholm map around 1914

When World War I started, Mälarhöjden (at that time called Fridhem) was fairly well established, as you can see from this map from the 1910s, if you look upon it in high resolution. You will also find that a suburban tram line (No 13) has been built to its Fridhem terminal. To get there today, you still take Line 13, but this is now an underground line, and Malarhöjden is a station halfway to the Norsborg where the line ends.
