Bob Fothergill

Robert Roy Fothergill (August 16, 1897 – March 20, 1938), nicknamed "Fats," was an outfielder in Major League Baseball who played twelve seasons with the Detroit Tigers (1922–1930), Chicago White Sox (1930–1932), and Boston Red Sox (1933).

Born in Massillon, Ohio, Fothergill weighed 230 lb. at 5 ft. 11 in. and threw right-handed. Under Tigers manager Ty Cobb, he was often used as a pinch hitter. During a game against the St. Louis Browns on May 5, 1922, Browns pitcher Bill Bayne had his no-hitter broken up after Cobb uses five consecutive pinch-hitters, including Fothergill. [1] He is the only batter to ever pinch-hit for Cobb.

His best overall season was 1927, in which he batted .359, hit nine homers, drove in 114 RBI, and scored 93 runs. In 1,106 career games, Fothergill had a .325 batting average with 36 homeruns and 582 RBI, he also had 1,064 hits. He once hit for a natural cycle.

According to Baseball's Greatest Managers (1961), When Leo Durocher, then an infielder with the Yankees, saw Fothergill at bat for the first time, he called time and protested to the plate umpite that Fothergill was "illegal!" Everybody stopped, baffled at Durocher's words. He continued, "Both those men can't bat at once!" The umpires ordered Leo to return to his position and stop delaying the game. But Fothergill was so angry he struck out.
