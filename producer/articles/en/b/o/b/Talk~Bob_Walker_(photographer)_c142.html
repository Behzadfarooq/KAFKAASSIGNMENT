I was pointed over this way after visiting WP:3O, and the first thing that hit me was "Why would someone from Dubuque be interested in this guy? What was his primary claim to fame (this was never mentioned in the first section)? What sets this photographer/activist from all the others who are currently attending college?" Clearly his death by AIDS was not his claim to fame as American people were dying of it by the hundreds by the early '90s. I was also struck by the lack of Wikification within the article, most notably the first half.
This needs to be less of a biography/tribute page and more of an encyclopedia article. It should be summarized in the first section (I think I found a dit of the "notoriety" - finally - in the fourth section, which would make most readers get impatient as they strive to reach the point of the article). There is much too much detail in the article: it is written more in the style of a magazine article, but some things in there obscure, rather than enhance the message - the detail about his dog, the long quotations, the last three paragraphs of the "Photography" section (which, along with the "Travels" section, truly do more harm than good to an encyclopedia article as adding to the "overload cloud"), the overly dense and long "Environmental Activist" section (which is better written for a PBS documentary than here), and an unnecessary "Walker's Refuge" section that can disappear without anybody noticing (OBTW, an encyclopedic article is never written in the second person!).
My apology if this seems overly long, but I hope the people on the two sides take it for what it is supposed to be - a way to resolve a dispute, and the best way to do it is to ask yourselves those questions I posed in my first paragraph above and go from there. Best wishes to both participants. 147.70.242.39 00:38, 27 April 2006 (UTC)
