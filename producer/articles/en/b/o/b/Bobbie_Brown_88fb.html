Bobbie Jean Brown sometimes credited as Bobbie Brown-Lane (born on October 7, 1969 in Baton Rouge, Louisiana) is a former beauty queen who has competed in the Miss Teen USA pageant and an American actress and model. She is best known for her appearance in Warrant's hair metal anthem, "Cherry Pie".

[edit] Pageants

Brown won the Miss Louisiana Teen USA 1987 title and represented Louisiana in the Miss Teen USA 1987 pageant broadcast live from El Paso, Texas in July 1987. She made the top ten in ninth place with an average score of 8.350 after the preliminary competition, placed sixth in interview (8.712), fourth in swimsuit (8.908) and sixth in swimsuit (8.754). Her average combined score of 8.791 for the final competitions was just enough to put her in the top five in fifth place, where she excelled in the final question and eventually placed second runner-up.

[edit] Notable other activities

Brown won the spokesperson competition on Star Search a record thirteen times and has also starred in TV shows such as Married... with Children (she appeared in 3 episodes) and fantasy films ranging from Flash Gordon (1980) to The Last Action Hero (1993). In 1988 she sued Penthouse for featuring a model also named Bobbie Brown. She has also worked as a model for Budweiser and other companies. Brown has also appeared in music videos, including Great White's "Once Bitten Twice Shy", "House of Broken Love" and Hurricane's "I'm On To You". She is remembered mostly as the girl in Warrant's "Cherry Pie" video which came out in late 1990. Although tame by todays standards the music video was considered sexually outrageous at the time leading to a lot of media attention and criticism from some feminists who called it sexist. Bobbie Brown has since defended the "Cherry Pie" video saying that sex sells, there wasn't anything obscene depicted in it and she was just being herself. During the shooting of the video she met the lead singer of the band, Jani Lane and they fell in love. She left her boyfriend Matthew Nelson for him and they wed a couple of months later, in July 1991. They were married for three years and had a daughter Taylor Lane, before divorcing in 1993. She has also dated Mark McGrath the lead singer of Sugar Ray, Stevie Rachelle from the group Tuff, Jay Gordon from Orgy, Motley Crue drummer Tommy Lee - until he left her and married Pamela Anderson - and Leonardo DiCaprio.

Bobbie Brown was 31 when she appeared on the Blind Date TV show saying that she is a very bold and extroverted person. She also says in that TV show that she actually is opposite from what one would expect by her exterior appearance. Bobby revealed on Blind Date that she no longer has an interest in dating rockers or musicians in general, and that her interest has shifted to younger men. At age 31 she dated a 19 year old man. The reason she gives for this is the respect she gets from younger men and how attentive to her needs they are compared to musicians. A source informed the show that the real reason for this is that Bobby Brown resents the fact that she is not as famous as her former lovers. The date did not go very well. The man featuring as her date on the show eventually described Bobby as was a washed-up, has-been Hollywood bitch and that Bobbie Brown had "more mileage than his Harley Davidson bike" and he sarcastically wished her luck on her next Hollywood video and in return Bobbie Brown called her date an idiot and said that she could have been a real bitch on the date but she restrained herself.

[edit] VH-1 interview

In 2006, during an interview on VH-1, Bobbie Brown said "I used to date rock stars. I don't anymore. You think you can change them and you can't."
