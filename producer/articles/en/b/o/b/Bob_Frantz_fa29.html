Bob Frantz is an American radio talk show host. He can currently be heard on WTAM in Cleveland.

Frantz's show runs on WTAM from 9:00 a.m. to 12:00 p.m. weekdays. He was named to host the show in the spring of 2006, ending the station's run of nine years worth of syndicated programming in that slot.

Frantz grew up in Ohio and attended Heidelberg College, and is a former junior high school and high school English teacher. He went into radio in 1996 as a high school sportscaster and sports talk show host at WOBL 1320-AM, a small station in Oberlin, Ohio. He left WOBL to join WTAM as a sports talk host and reporter from July 1998 to March 2000. He left WTAM when he was offered the afternoon drive position at "The Ticket 1050" KTCT-AM in San Francisco in March of 2000, where he also served as sideline reporter for the Oakland Raiders radio network.

Frantz then moved to Orlando, where he was an afternoon-drive sports talk host and program director at ESPN Radio AM 1080. Before his current tenure at WTAM, he was the morning drive host at WTAM's news-talk sister in Toledo, WSPD 1370-AM. In just over two years, the WSPD Morning News jumped from #8 to #2 in the morning-drive ratings. In the fall of 2005, a change in management resulted in Frantz's reassignment to hosting an hour-long hard-news evening program at WSPD before rejoining WTAM in his current slot.
