Bobby Skinstad

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Robert Brian "Bobby" Skinstad (born 3 July 1976 in Bulawayo, Zimbabwe) is a South African rugby union player who has represented the national team, the Springboks, scoring 50 points (all tries). He plays in the positions of flanker and Rugby union positions#8. Number eight. He has signed to represent the Natal Sharks in domestic and Super 14 competitions for 2007 having previously represented the Western Province (Stormers in the Super 12). He stands 1.93 meters tall and weighs 104 kilograms.

He lived in Kloof, KwaZulu-Natal while he attended Highbury Preparatory School, before boarding at Hilton College.

[edit] Career

[edit] Stellenbosch University

His university studies took him to Stellenbosch University where he played provincial rugby for Western Province, and Super 12 for the Stormers.

[edit] Springbok

Having represented South Africa at all levels, including captaining the under 21 Springbok side in 1996 and 1997, and playing for the SA sevens in 1997, Skinstad made his début for the Springboks on 29 November 1997 against England. In all he played 34 games for the Springboks, his career ending on 28 June 2003 as a reserve against Argentina.

[edit] Injuries

Skinstad's career was greatly hampered by injuries. He damaged a knee in an auto accident in April 1999. He came back to play in the 1999 World Cup, but injured the same knee at the start of 2000 and did not play for the rest of that year. He missed most of the 2002 Super 12 season due to shoulder surgery, and missed the 2003 World Cup after breaking an arm.

[edit] Springbok captain

From 30 June 2001 until 15 June 2002 Skinstad captained the Springboks in 10 tests though he was not particularly successful as captain, leading his men to victory in only 5 of the 10 tests.

[edit] Richmond, England

Skinstad is currently plying his trade for Richmond in England's London South-East Division 1. He also rounds up the Super 14, Currie Cup and NPC Tournaments on the Sky Sports programme, The Rugby Club. He is also an analyst and occasional presenter for Live rugby union matches on Sky Sports.

He is also a regular player for the Barbarians and has captained them on several occasions. One of his most recent appearances for the Barbarians was against Scotland where he came off the bench in the second half. Skinstad, shortly after, returned to South Africa to play in South African legend Corné Krige's testimonial match against a World XV.

Bob now head up his own sports management company called Esportif which is a joint venture with Saatchi & Saatchi and is based in their London headquarters.

[edit] Dubai 7's 2006

In December 2006 Bob played alongside friend, ex Scottish international Kenny Logan in the Dubai 7's[1] for 'Stefan BHF' to raise awareness for the [['British Heart Foundation'[2]]] in memory of the late Stefan Czerpak [3], ex England colts Newbury RFC and Richmond RFC rugby coach who died in 1998 from a heart attack.

[edit] Natal

After months of speculation about a return to professional rugby, it has been confirmed that Bob Skinstad will play for the Natal Sharks in the 2007 Super 14. Questions have been raised as to whether he will be the force he was and if he will be able to go from playing social rugby occasionally to full time rugby.

On Thursday 13th December, Bobby Skinstad revealed to the South African public that he had decided to move back to South Africa whilst signing a contract with the Natal Skarks. It is widely believed he is hoping to make a mark in the Super 14 in the 2007 season, with the ultimate goal being to represent South Africa in the up and coming World Cup in Paris. There is no doubt significant opposition for him in the no.8 position with the likes of Danie Rossouw and Joe van Niekerk competing for that pivotal spot in the ‘loosies’ for the World Cup squad. Jake White had publicly announced Skinstad's ambitions to make the World Cup 22 during the Autumn Tests at the end of November, and said that if Skinstad is the best choice he will be in the team and if not he wont: Jake White told reporters how he is going to be doing no favours for the 'Golden Boy' of South African rugby. If Skinstad makes the World Cup Squad, he will be there on merit and merit alone.
