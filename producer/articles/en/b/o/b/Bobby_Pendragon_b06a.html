Bobby Pendragon is the main character from D.J. MacHale's Pendragon series.

Robert "Bobby" Pendragon was a fairly average fourteen year old boy and suburban basketball star living in the fictional suburb of Stony Brook, Connecticut. Except for the fact that he is a Traveler and must save Halla (every time, place and thing that ever has, or ever will, exist) from Saint Dane, an evil demon who wants nothing more than to plunge each territory into chaos.

One night, his Uncle Press Tilton takes him to an abandoned subway in New York, where Bobby and his Uncle are attacked by Second Earth’s quigs , which are guards of the flumes. Quigs change shape between territories. Press also engages in a gun battle with Saint Dane while Bobby tries to get to the flume. Saint Dane is the villain of the series. They escape by taking a “tunnel” to Denduron in the beginning of the first book, The Merchant of Death. The "tunnels" in the books are officially called flumes.

His adventures are chronicled in a collection of journals that he writes to his best friends and Acolytes on Second Earth, Mark Dimond and Courtney Chetwynde. As he follows Saint Dane from territory to territory, Bobby forms friendships with the Travelers of the other territories. He also grows wiser and becomes a better strategist every time he thwarts Saint Dane. Though, sadly, in The Reality Bug, book #4 of the series, Bobby loses his first territory, Veelox, to Saint Dane. In The Rivers of Zadaa, Bobby learns how to fight from Travelers Alder, Loor, and Loor’s adopted sister and Acolyte, Saangi. Bobby and the current generation of Travelers seem to have untapped powers, evident several times in the series, but most prominently in The Rivers of Zadaa, in which he resurrected Loor, the Traveler from Zadaa and recovered impossibly quickly from a near-death battle with Saint Dane.

Bobby ages throughout the series; he turns fifteen in The Never War, sixteen in Black Water and it is implied that he has become 17 by The Rivers of Zadaa and The Quillan Games.
