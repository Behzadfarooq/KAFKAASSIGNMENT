Bob Chandler is a retired American football Wide Receiver, who died of cancer in 1995.

[edit] Professional career

Chandler played in the National Football League between 1971 and 1982 for the Buffalo Bills (1971-1979) and the Oakland Raiders (1980-1982). He led the NFL in receptions from 1975-1977 with 176. He also caught 4 passes for 77 yards in the Raiders 27-10 Super Bowl XV victory.

Chandler finished his career with 370 receptions for 5,243 yards and 48 touchdowns, along with 11 carries for 18 yards.

Chandler hosted "2 On The Town" for CBS 1984-1987 and Amazing Games for ESPN in 1989, a global documentary series about the world's most exotic sports. He also served as a "Technical Advisor" for the humor book The Unofficial NFL Players Handbook.

[edit] College career

Chandler played college football at the University of Southern California. He was the MVP of the 1970 Rose Bowl.

[edit] Personal Life

Chandler was born in Long Beach, California and was raised in Whittier, California and graduated from Whittier High School in 1967. Chandler's father Gene Chandler was the mayor of Whittier in 1987-1988.
