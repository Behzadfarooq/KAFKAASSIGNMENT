Bobsleigh at the 2002 Winter Olympics

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

2002 Winter Olympic Games Bobsleigh

[edit] Four-Man

Medal Team Time
Gold Germany II (André Lange, Enrico Kühn, Kevin Kuske, Carsten Embach) (GER) 3:07.51
Silver USA I (Todd Hays, Randy Jones, Bill Schuffenhauer, Garrett Hines) (USA) 3:07.81
Bronze USA II (Brian Shimer, Mike Kohn, Doug Sharp, Dan Steele) (USA) 3:07.86

Germany continues its dominance of bobsleigh at the Olympics.

[edit] Two-Man

Medal Athletes Time
Gold Christoph Langen / Markus Zimmerman (GER) 3:10.11
Silver Christian Reich / Steve Anderhub (SUI) 3:10.20
Bronze Martin Annen / Beat Hefti (SUI) 3:10.62

Langen and Zimmerman both become four-time Olympic medalists.

[edit] Two-Woman

Bakken (left) and Flowers
Medal Athletes Time
Gold Jill Bakken / Vonetta Flowers (USA) 1:37.76
Silver Sandra Prokoff / Ulrike Holzner (GER) 1:38.06
Bronze Susi Erdmann / Nicole Herschmann (GER) 1:38.29

This is the inaugural women's bobsleigh event. Susi Erdmann previously medaled in luge, earning a silver at the 1992 Winter Olympics in Albertville. Vonetta Flowers was the first black person to win a gold medal at the Winter Olympics.
