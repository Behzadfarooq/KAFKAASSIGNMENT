Bob Cruz (Born 1953 â€“ Died 1995) was an American Disc Jockey who grew up in Levittown, New York, in the United States. In 1976 he was hired to replace Jay Reynolds at WABC (AM) radio.

Besides presenting the 'all night show' on WABC, Bob Cruz became the permanent replacement for Dan Ingram when the latter was not available, reportedly because his voice resembled that of Dan Ingram. Bob Cruz later took over afternoons in 1979 when Dan Ingram moved to morning drive. Bob Cruz left WABC in March of 1981, but remained in New York for a time doing voice-over work for ABCs 20/20.

Bob Cruz died at the age of 42.
