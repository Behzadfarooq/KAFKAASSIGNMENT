Bob Murray (hockey player)

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Robert Frederick Murray (born 26 November 1954 in Kingston, Ontario). A former NHL defenceman and executive, Murray played in the NHL 1975-88 and 1988-90 (all with the Chicago Blackhawks). He was playing for the Cornwall Royals of the QMJHL 1971-74 (where he won a Memorial Cup) when the Blackhawks drafted him 52nd overall in 1974.

[edit] NHL career

After playing with the Dallas Black Hawks of the Central Hockey League in 1974-75, he joined the NHL Hawks for the 1974-75. Developing a leadership role with the team, Murray served as captain, for two months of the 1985-86 season (Nov.'85 to Jan'86), while Captain Darryl Sutter was out of the lineup, with an injury. Starting the 1988-89 season briefly in the AHL, Murray completed his NHL career with Chicago in the summer of 1990.

[edit] Blackhawks executive

In 1991, the Blackhawks hired him as their Director of Player personnel, and from 1997 to November 1999 General-Manager. When Murray was drafted by the Blackhawks (in 1974), the WHA's Cincinnati Stingers drafted him 77th overall.
