Robert Martin Fink (born December 29, 1935) is a Canadian/American musicologist, writer and artist.

[edit] Biography

Born in New York City, Fink is a graduate of Wayne State University, at which he majored in musicology and minored in the sciences.

Fink has worked as an artist, a technical illustrator, a journalist, a political cartoonist, a machinery designer within the auto industry, and as a columnist for daily and weekly newspapers. His renderings of several Saskatoon heritage buildings hang in his city's city hall. Several of Fink's musicology papers have been published, reviewed or cited in academic journals, books and science media. He was invited, and served as a juror on ancient music for the science journal Nature.[citation needed]

He has participated in anti-war movements, and is an activist on issues of free speech, civil rights and liberties. Among a number of legal cases in which he has been involved is one Fink won dealing with free speech, referred to in a ruling by the Supreme Court of Canada for the right to put up posters on public poles.[1]

Fink currently lives in Canada.

[edit] Works

Most of Fink's books deal with music and its origins. Published as The Universality of Music (1970), a later 1981 edition was published in Canada under the new title: The Origin of Music. "The Neanderthal Flute" is an essay on what he describes as the "oldest musical instrument", found at the Divje Babe archaeological site. The essay was first published online, then later in print in 1997. Fink's most recent book, On the Origin of Music: Essays and Readings (2004), collects the main essays and archaeological material he has written since 1950.

His opera, Lysistrata and the War (1979), was composed as a protest to the Vietnam War. Based on the Aristophanes play, its scheduled 1968 debut at Wayne State University was cancelled when the tenor was drafted into the army four days before the performance. Subsequent conflicts with the opera director, prompted by concerns over the work's political nature, led Fink to withdraw the opera.[1].

Fink collected documents from that time and wrote a history of the anti-Vietnam war protest, which outlined the course of that movement. Fink was initial Chairman and an organizer of the takeover of Wayne State University by students after the murders at Kent State University.[citation needed]
