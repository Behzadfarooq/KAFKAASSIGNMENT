Staff Sergeant Robert "Bob" Brown (United States Army, callsign: Cool Breeze) is a fictional character on the CBS television series The Unit. He is portrayed by Scott Foley.

Spoiler warning: Plot and/or ending details follow.

Brown is a relatively new member of The Unit. His wife, Kim, and he have a daughter, Serena, and a son, Theodore. Brown has a Ranger tab, but at some point prior to his Army experience was in the Air Force. He is a licensed pilot in both fixed wing craft and helicopters and is one of the more skilled marksmen in The Unit, frequently serving as a sharpshooter.

During Season 1, upon Brown's first day after Training and Selection, terrorists hijack an airplane in Wyndham, Idaho. Brown was a part of the operation to reclaim the airplane, doing his part by eliminating terrorist spotters in the nearby woods. After the operation, when the FBI was looking to arrest Jonas Blane's team for conducting the operation prematurely, Brown was used as a decoy, as he was never on the captured aircraft.

Brown is responsible for saving the Secretary of State from assassination by radicals in Africa, as well as capturing an Air Force cryptologist who was selling secrets to the Chinese. He assisted in the capture of a Bosnian war criminal, which came back to haunt him in the Season 1 finale, as the criminal escaped UN custody and tracked the Unit to their headquarters at Fort Griffith, attacking them while they celebrated Colonel Tom Ryan's marriage.

During Season 2, Brown has become more confident in his skills as an operator, instructing trainees in sniping arts. He was responsible for helping to eliminate one of the world's foremost arms dealers.

Brown's membership in The Unit has at times strained his marriage, such as when he was late returning from a mission in Spain, conducted a mission under the guise of taking his wife on a vacation, and became hospitalized during a SERE exercise.
