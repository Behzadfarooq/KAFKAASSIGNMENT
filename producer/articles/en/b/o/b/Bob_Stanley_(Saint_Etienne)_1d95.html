Bob Stanley (Saint Etienne)

Bob Stanley (born Robert Andrew Shukman, 25 December 1964, in Horsham, Sussex) is a UK musician, film maker and journalist. He is best known as a member of the pop/dance group Saint Etienne for which he co-writes songs and produces. Live on stage, he normally plays keyboards and xylophone.

He has had a long parallel career as a music journalist writing for amongst others NME, Melody Maker, Mojo, The Guardian and The Times. In the late 1980s/early 1990s he briefly ran a record label and fanzine called Caff, which released early singles by the Manic Street Preachers and Pulp.

When Saint Etienne are between projects, Stanley DJs, playing generally 1960s pop music. He also promotes similar club nights in and around London.
