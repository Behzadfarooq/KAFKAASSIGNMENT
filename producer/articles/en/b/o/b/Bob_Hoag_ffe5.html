Bob Hoag is the pianist and keyboardist for American rock band The Ataris. He joined the band in 2005. His band, "The Go Reflex," includes Kevin Scanlon from the now defunct band, "Pollen" (Bob played the drums and wrote the songs). In "The Go Reflex," Bob once again writes the songs but also sings and plays piano as well as drums. Bob Hoag currently runs Flying Blanket Recording (www.flyingblanket.com) in Mesa, Arizona where he has produced and recorded musicians such as "Joel Plaskett," "The Format," "Adam Panic," "Dear and the Headlights," "Fine China," "Runaway Diamonds," "xrayok," "Recover," "Versus the Mirror," "Awake and Alert," "The Ruby Lee," "The Breakup Society," "Mr. Kline and the Wizards of Time," "The Bled," "Tickertape Parade," "Limbeck," "Scary Kids Scaring Kids," "Four Banger" and "Before Braille," as well as many others. Bob generally contributes musically to most of the records he produces as well and can be heard singing, playing piano/keyboards, and doing various percussion on most of those releases. "The Go Reflex" has not been very active since Kevin Scanlon moved to Los Angeles to pursue a career in photography (www.kevinscanlon.com) in 2004. There were two shows in late 2004, featuring a heavily expanded lineup but the group would not play again until the summer of 2006 (a benefit show), which was done with the band's original lineup. Hoag has cited his busy recording schedule as the main reason behind the group's recent inactivity and continuously claims that the band will one day become active again. Bob is married and has one child, a son.
