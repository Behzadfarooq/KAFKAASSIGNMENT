This is a list of shopping malls in Utah, accompanied by descriptions and (when I can come around to it), pictures.

  • Cache Valley Mall - Logan
  • Newgate Mall - Ogden
    • Opened in 1981, the Newgate Mall eventually ran the Ogden City Mall out of business.
  • Layton Hills Mall - Layton
  • Gateway District - Salt Lake City
    • First opened in 2001, the Gateway District is a large, mixed-used development project located on the western edge of downtown. The focal point is the two-level outdoor shopping center, but apartment complexes and office buildings are also part of the project. The shopping center includes a fountain open to the public, a Barnes & Noble, and a 12-screen Megaplex theater.
The entrance to the ZCMI Center Mall
  • Cottonwood Mall - Holladay
    • Opened sometime in the 1950s, this was the first enclosed shopping mall in Utah. Its current anchors are Macy's and J.C. Penney. The mall has been struggling, with more than half of the lots vacant.
  • Fashion Place Mall - Murray
  • Valley Fair Mall - West Valley City
  • University Mall - Orem
  • Red Cliffs Mall - Saint George

[edit] Dead malls

Main article: Dead mall
  • Ogden City Mall - Ogden
    • Opened in 1980, the Ogden City Mall was eventually run out of business by Newgate Mall and had become entirely vacant by 1999. The mall was bought by the City of Ogden and demolished in 2002.
  • Five Points Mall - Bountiful
    • One of the first enclosed shopping malls in the state, this mall was at least partially torn down and replaced with mixed-use development sometime around 2003 or 2004.
  • Crossroads Plaza - Salt Lake City, Utah
    • Crossroads Plaza was part of the urban reconstruction plan of the 1970s to serve as a complement to the ZCMI Center Mall. The mall cost $100 million to construct and covers 70% of the block. Construction of the mall began in 1978, and the mall officially opened on August 2, 1980. [1] The mall was bought in 2003, along with the ZCMI Center Mall across the street, by the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, who announced an ambitious redevelopment plan. The mall is now scheduled to be demolished in November 2006 as part of the $1 billion City Creek Center redevelopment plan announced on October 3, 2006 by the LDS Church.[2] All stores have left the mall and it is expected to be demolished by summer 2007.
  • ZCMI Center Mall - Salt Lake City
    • The ZCMI Center Mall, first proposed in 1965, finally began construction in the early 1970s and first opened its doors on July 17, 1975. The mall cost $50 million and has a total surface area of 775,000 square feet covering an 8-acre site. At the time of its opening, it was the largest downtown mall in the country. To make way for the mall, a parking structure and a 300-foot tall smokestack had to be demolished.[3] The mall was bought in 2003, along with Crossroads Plaza across the street, by the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, who announced an ambitious redevelopment plan. On October 3, 2006, the LDS Church announced its $1 billion City Creek Center redevelopment plan that called for demolition of the mall.[4] The last of the stores left the mall at the end of January 2007 in anticipation of demolition, which is expected to take place by summer 2007.
