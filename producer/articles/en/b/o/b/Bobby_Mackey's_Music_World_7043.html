Bobby Mackey's music world is a nightclub in Wilder, Kentucky. It is rumoured to be a gateway to hell, as there were reported hauntings there. It stands on the site of an old slaughterhouse, and was believed by the police to be the resting place of the decapitated head of Pearl Bryan, a woman who was murdered by two Satanists. The ghosts of the victim and the murderers are believed to haunt this nightclub. It is owned by Bobby Mackey and his wife Janet. The first employee of Mackey, Carl Lawson, was one of the first to witness the hauntings. "I’d double check at the end of the night and make sure that everything was turned off. Then I’d come back down hours later and the bar lights would be on. The front doors would be unlocked, when I knew that I’d locked them. The jukebox would be playing the ‘Anniversary Waltz’ even though I’d unplugged it and the power was turned off," he reported.

One night while cleaning in the tavern, he discovered a diary belonging to a person named 'Johanna', who supposedly haunted the house as well. The haunting seemed to be most serious in the basement, near a sealed-up well. This was later revealed to be the location of where the police suspected that Pearl Bryan's head was. Lawson decided to sprinkle some holy water into the well, but this only made the ghosts angrier. Mackey did not believe Lawson until his wife admitted herself that she had once been pushed down a flight of steps by what seemed to be a ghost when she was five months pregnant, the same as Johanna when she died. She reportedly looked up the stairs and heard a voice scream "Get out!".

Carl Lawson was possessed by a ghost after a psychic inspected the tavern. The ghosts that possessed him were assumed to be the two murderers. He was later exorcised by a local priest.

Author Doug Hensley wrote a book about the tavern, and it remains a nightclub and is still open for ghost spotters and enthusiasts.
