Bob Rogers (Disc Jockey)

Bob Rogers is an Australian disc jockey and radio broadcaster. He currently presents the morning show on Sydney radio station 2CH.

[edit] Early Career

Born to British parents[1], Rogers began his career as a disc jockey in Brisbane, Australia. He became successful and subsequently presented Australia's first Top 40 show on 2UE. He was Australia'a top radio DJ for the next 8 years.

[edit] Popular and Successful

In 1962 he joined 2SM which jumped to number 1. In 1964 Rogers was chosen to represent 2SM on Beatles' legendary tour through Europe, Asia and Australia. Consequently Rogers became known as "the fifth Beatle". He returned to 2UE and presented his morning radio show with a new format of provocative commentary, gossip and music. His programme became popular amongst interview-hungry artists.

[edit] Moving to Television

Due to his overwhelming popularity amongst the audience, Rogers ventured into television. The Bob Rogers Show, a late night TV variety Show, ran for five years on the Seven Network.

[edit] Return to Radio

In 1994 Rogers returned to 2UE to present a Sunday morning show.

[edit] Success at 2CH

In November 1997, Rogers accepted John Singleton's offer and joined Sydney radio station 2CH.

  • On 21 February 2007, according to the Nielsen radio ratings survey, Bob Rogers's Morning show became the fourth most heard radio programme in Sydney in that particular timeslot by beating John Laws of 2UE into fifth place. This is no mean feat as it is generally much more difficult for an easy listening station in Sydney to achieve a greater audience share than a talkback station. Overall, 2CH has overtaken 2UE to claim sixth place.[2][3][4]

[edit] Currently

Rogers currently presents the 2CH Morning show, Monday to Friday between 9am-12 noon and the Saturday Night Reminiscing program.

[edit] Trivia

  • In 1957, discovered the Slim Dusty song "Pub With No Beer" and was the first DJ in Australia to play the song and catapult it to number one on the charts.
  • In 1976 Rogers wrote "Rock And Roll Australia", a book about the birth of the Australian rock industry.
  • In 1982, he started a chain of Women's dress shops.
