Robert "Bob" Essensa (born January 14, 1965, in Toronto, Ontario, Canada) is a retired professional ice hockey goaltender. Essensa was selected in the fourth round of the 1983 NHL Entry Draft, 71st overall, by the Winnipeg Jets. Essensa went on to a 15-year professional career that saw him play for the Jets, Detroit Red Wings, Edmonton Oilers, Phoenix Coyotes, Vancouver Canucks and Buffalo Sabres. However, he never won the Stanley Cup.

Currently, he is the goaltending coach for the Boston Bruins.

[edit] College career

Following the 1983 Draft, Essensa played for Michigan State University and posted an overall record of 62-13-2 in his four years at Michigan State. He named the CCHA First Team All-Star his sophomore year and won the CCHA and NCAA Championships with Michigan State in his Junior year.

[edit] Professional career

Essensa made his NHL debut during the 1988-89 NHL Season, in which he posted a 6-8-3 record in 20 games. His workload would increase to 36 games during the 1989-90 NHL Season, and would serve the entire season as Winnipeg's starting goaltender from the 1990-91 NHL Season until the 1992-93 NHL Season, taking the team to the playoffs during his final two whole seasons with Winnipeg.

After playing as the starting netminder in Winnipeg, Essensa had a poor 1993-94 NHL Season, and was dealt to Detroit on 8 March 1994. Due to posting a 4-7-2 record in 13 games with the Red Wings, Essensa was assigned to the minor leagues while Detroit opted to go with veteran free agent Mike Vernon and goaltending prospect Chris Osgood as their goaltending tandem. Essensa continued to play in the IHL and AHL for the next two seasons due to his status as the fourth goaltender in Detroit's system, behind Vernon, Osgood, and Kevin Hodson. Although he had fetched a reasonable package of players when Winnipeg traded him, he was sent to Edmonton for future considerations on 14 June 1996.

In Edmonton, Essensa proved himself to be a legitimate NHL goaltender, taking the backup role to a young Curtis Joseph during the 1996-97 NHL Season and 1997-98 NHL Season. Essensa spent the 1998-99 NHL Season serving in a goaltending tandem with Mikhail Shtalenkov while sharing a limited amount of time with goaltending prospects Tommy Salo and Steve Passmore. Despite having played the most games during the regular season, Edmonton decided to go with Salo in the playoffs, where they lost in four straight games to the eventual Stanley Cup Champion Dallas Stars. Upon Essensa reaching Free Agency, the Oilers organization opted to go with Salo as their starting goaltender and reacquired Bill Ranford to serve as Salo's backup goaltender. Essensa eventually went back to the organization which drafted him, as the Phoenix Coyotes (formerly the Winnipeg Jets) signed him on 5 September 1999.

With the Coyotes, Essensa was once again working Shtalenkov (who was traded to Phoenix the season before), as well as starting goaltender Sean Burke and upstart Robert Esche. Despite posting a winning record and appearing in seven less games than Burke, the Coyotes opted to go with Burke and Esche for the following season. That said, Essensa signed as a free agent with the Vancouver Canucks on 26 July 2000.

In Vancouver, Essensa was initially given nicknames such as "Backup Bob" and "Sideshow Bob", indicating that he was expected to receive very limited playing time behind established star Felix Potvin. Soon, however, Potvin faltered and Essensa was resorted to as a stopgap measure. He responded phenomenally, posting an 18-12-3 record and leading the Canucks back into the playoffs, where they were ousted in the first round by the eventual champions, the Colorado Avalanche. In this year Bob made a face save, saving the puck with his metal part of the face mask, and warping it completely. Despite having become a fan favourite, then-Canucks GM Brian Burke did not re-sign Essensa, choosing to go with the younger Dan Cloutier and bringing in Martin Brochu to back him up. Once more, Essensa is on the move and was signed by the Buffalo Sabres on 4 August 2001.

In Buffalo, Essensa was the third goaltender in the system, along with Mika Noronen and Martin Biron, and was replacing goaltender Dominik Hasek, who ended up with the Red Wings prior to the start of the 2001-02 NHL Season. However, Essensa appeared in only 9 games and held a 0-5-0 record with an 85% save percentage.

Essensa announced his retirement on 10 September 2002.
