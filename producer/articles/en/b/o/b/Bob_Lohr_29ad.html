Robert H. "Bob" Lohr (born November 2, 1960) is an American professional golfer who has played on the PGA Tour and the Nationwide Tour.

Lohr was born in Cincinnati, Ohio. He attended Miami University in Oxford, Ohio and was a member of the golf team; he won All-American honors (honorable mention) in his senior year. He turned pro in 1983.

Lohr played on the PGA Tour from 1985â€“1996. He finished in a tie for first place at the end of regulation in 3 PGA Tour events. In the 1988 Walt Disney World/Oldsmobile Classic, he defeated Chip Beck on the fifth hole of a playoff. In 1993 at the H-E-B Texas Open, he lost in a playoff to Jay Haas. In 1995 at the Bell Canadian Open, Lohr lost in a playoff to Mark O'Meara. His best finish in a major was a T-33 in the 1990 U.S. Open.

During his late thirties and forties, Lohr played some on the Nationwide Tour. He was inducted into the Miami of Ohio Athletics Hall of Fame in 1993. He lives in the greater Orlando area.
