Bob Mayo

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Bob Mayo (1951 â€“ 2004) was a former session keyboardist and guitarist, most noted for his work with Peter Frampton. He was born in New York City and grew up in Westchester County.

Mayo began studying music at the age of five, focusing on classical piano. During the 60's, Mayo's interest in music grew due to the rock explosion. His first band was Ramble and the Descendants, where he played organ and vocals. Mayo played with several other popular local bands and had plans to attend Juilliard School in New York City. His career took a detour when he suffered injuries in a serious car accident at the age of seventeen, but Bob was determined and he was able to move on.

In 1971, Mayo formed Doc Holliday with Frank Carillo, Tom Arlotta, and Bob Liggio. Three years later, he teamed up Peter Frampton and joined his touring band. Because of this, he appeared on Frampton's album Frampton Comes Alive. It was on this recording that Frampton introduced Mayo with the words "Bob Mayo on the keyboards...Bob Mayo", which has since become something of a legend among Frampton fans. Mayo also appeared on the Frampton albums I'm in You and Where I Should Be.

In 1980, Mayo left Frampton's band to focus on recording. During this time, he recorded solo projects for Joe Walsh and Joe Vitale. Later he joined the touring band for Foreigner and played keyboards on "Waiting For A Girl Like You" and "Break It Up." He would spend the next two years touring with Foreigner, and also toured with Dan Fogelberg and Hall & Oates late in the decade. He continued to tour with H&O until 1998.

In 1992, Mayo returned to work with Frampton. The resulting tour turned into the recording of the album Frampton Comes Alive 2. He also appeared on the Live in Detroit CD & DVD as well as Frampton's 2003 recording Now.

Phish guitarist and singer Trey Anastasio referenced keyboardist Page McConnell as "Bob Mayo" during the December 18, 1999 concert at Hampton Coliseum during a cover of Do You Feel Like We Do.

On February 23, 2004, Mayo was touring with Frampton in Basel, Switzerland, when he had a heart attack and died promptly. Frampton said after his death ""Bob was like a brother to me. I have lost a close personal friend and a talented, professional and outstanding musician."
