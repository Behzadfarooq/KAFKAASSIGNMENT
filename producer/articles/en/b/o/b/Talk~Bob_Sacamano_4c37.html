I support Sacamano or Saccamano, not Sacomano, Sacomento, nor Sacamento. It appears to be a Spanish or Italian name; any Latin-American or Italian would hear it very natural. Besides, the character Cosmo Kramer is knowledgeable in a lot of different cultures that it would be strange that he couldn't pronounce correctly Sacamano's name. The spelling Sacamento suggests a different accent, which Kramer lacks.

Etymologically the first word saca- or sacca- may be related to take out (with the hand), or a bag or sack. And -mano literally means hand. 20:57, 20 December 2006 (UTC)
