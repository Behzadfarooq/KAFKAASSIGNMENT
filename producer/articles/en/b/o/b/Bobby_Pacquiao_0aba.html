Alberto Dapigran "Bobby" Pacquiao (born December 12, 1980 in General Santos City, Philippines) is a super featherweight/ lightweight boxer and the brother of the current World Boxing Council (WBC) International Super Featherweight Champion, Manny Pacquiao.

Pacquiao turned professional in 1997 and initially had very limited success while fighting in the Philippines. It was not until he fought and beat Carlos Navarro that his career picked up. On June 17, 2005, he defeated Navarro for the World Boxing Council Continental Americas super featherweight title. Late in 2005 he beat former champion Carlos Hernandez, and in 2006 he defeated former world Featherweight titlistKevin Kelley.

In a match held on November 16, 2006 to defend his WBC Continental Americas title against Hector Velazquez, Bobby Pacquiao lost his title on the scales for being three pounds over the allowable weight limit.

The fight was derided for Pacquiao's dirty fighting tactics. Referee Kenny Bayless warned Pacquiao four times for throwing too many low blows and disqualified him on the fifth.[1]
