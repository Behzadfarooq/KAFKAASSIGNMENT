Bob Geiger is an American political blogger who is the author of the blog, Bobgeiger.com. He comments about national American politics from a liberal point of view. His blog had its millionth visitor on February 5, 2007.[1]

[edit] Personal background

Geiger is a war veteran.[2]. He currently lives with his wife and son in New York.[3]

Geiger's political career began at San Francisco State University where he served as a representative on the student legislature. He received a degree in Economics and minored in Journalism. After he started blogging, he won the 2005 Weblog Awards prize for Best New Blog. He was a finalist in the Koufax Awards in 2005 for his column I Know This Little Boy in New Orleans.[4]

In addition to his own personal blog, he contributes to Alternet, Democrats.com, and Democratic Underground. The Huffington Post also reproduces his blog. He has made appearances on numerous radio programs to comment on Senate activity.[5]

[edit] Features of Bobgeiger.com

In addition to standard commentary on politics, bobgeiger.com has several recurring features:

  • Monday Check of the Osama Clock - Every Monday, Geiger posts an update on the "Where's Osama?" clock, which shows the number of days that Osama bin Laden has gone uncaptured since President George W. Bush promised to capture Osama bin Laden "dead or alive."[6]
  • The Saturday Cartoons - Every Saturday, Geiger posts political cartoons from a wide variety of publications.
  • The Friday Political Grab Bag - For a time, every Friday Geiger would post "The Friday Political Grab Bag," which would contain varying political anecdotes and internet memes. However, this feature has not occurred since November 26, 2006.[7].
  • 2006 Senate Elections Report - Prior to the 2006 United States Senate elections, Geiger would occasionally post his predictions about who would win each race. In his last such post before the election, he correctly predicted how each Senate race would turn out. [8]
