Bob Montgomery (February 10, 1919 - August 25, 1998) was an American boxer, fighting mainly in the 1940s.

[edit] Professional boxing career

Known as "Bobcat", Montgomery turned professional in 1938. He defeated Beau Jack for the lightweight world championship on May 21, 1943, and lost the title to Jack in a return match on November 19, 1943.

[edit] Promotional career

Montgomery promoted in Philadelphia at the Blue Horizon briefly in 1971, becoming the first black promoter ever in Philadelphia.

[edit] Additional accolades

  • Montgomery was inducted into the International Boxing Hall of Fame in 1995.
  • Montgomery was named on Ring Magazine's list of the 80 best fighters of the last 80 years in 2002.
