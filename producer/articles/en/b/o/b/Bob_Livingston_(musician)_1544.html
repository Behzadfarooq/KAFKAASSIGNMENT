Bob Livingston (born Robert Lynn Livingston November 26, 1948 in San Antonio, Texas) is a singer-songwriter currently living in Austin, Texas. Livingston attended Texas Tech University and was a member of the Sigma Alpha Epsilon fraternity. In the 1970s, Livingston was a member of the Lost Gonzo Band and played and recorded with some of Texas’ most colorful musicians including Jerry Jeff Walker, Michael Martin Murphey, Ray Wylie Hubbard and Steven Fromholtz. The Lost Gonzo Band made two records for MCA (The Lost Gonzo Band and Thrills) and one for Capitol (Signs of Life). In 1992, the Gonzos released their 20th year reunion CD called Rendezvous followed by Hands of Time in 1994. Both titles were released on Vireo Records. In 1997 a compilation CD of the two MCA titles was released on Demon Records.

Livingston has continued to play and record with many Texas musicians in including Terry Allen, Pat Green and Bobby Bridger. Since 1985, Livingston has toured several foreign countries for the U.S. State Department. He has traveled to India, Pakistan, Africa, the Middle East, Sri Lanka, Bangladesh and Nepal. In these concerts, he performs with local indigenous musicians mixing instrumentation and insights from both east and west. Inspired by these experiences, Livingston released a CD in 2004 on Vireo Records called Mahatma Gandhi & Sitting Bull. In 2006, Vireo released a compilation CD called Original Spirit.

Livingston is the leader of a multi-cultural group of musicians from Texas and India called Cowboys & Indians. Based in Austin, members of Cowboys & Indians include Oliver Rajamani, Richard Bowden, Tucker Livingston, Bradley Kopp, Paul Pearcy and Anu Naimpally.

Livingston is married (Iris) and has two sons, Tucker and Trevor. Tucker is also a musician and has accompanied his father on many of the foreign tours and Trevor is a computer programmer living in Austin.
