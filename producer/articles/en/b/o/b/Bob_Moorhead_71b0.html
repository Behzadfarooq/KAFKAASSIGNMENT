Bob Moorhead

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Charles Robert (Bob) Moorhead (January 23, 1938 - December 3, 1986) was a right-handed pitcher in Major League Baseball who played for the New York Mets in 1962 and 1965. He was born in Chambersburg, Pennsylvania.

In a two-season career, Moorhead posted a 0-3 record with 68 strikeouts and a 4.51 ERA in 119 2/3 innings.

Moorhead died in Lemoyne, Pennsylvania, at age 48.

[edit] Fact

  • Moorhead made his major league debut with the New York Mets in the franchise's original game played on April 11, 1962, at Sportsman's Park.
