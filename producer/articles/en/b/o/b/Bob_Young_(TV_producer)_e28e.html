Bob Young (TV producer)

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Bob Young

Bob Young, born Robert S. Young Jr. in Knoxville, Tennessee, attended college at the University of Pennsylvania and soon after his graduation began working in stand-up comedy in Philadelphia with fellow comedian Bob Myer in a duo called Myer and Young. Once Bob moved out to Los Angeles he began working in television. He has worked as both a writer and producer on eleven sitcoms. He currently lives in Los Angeles with his wife Joan and his three children Jesse, Zach, and Ethan.

[edit] Television Work

  • Family Affair (2002) Writer, Producer
  • Nikki (2000) Writer, Producer
  • Smart Guy (1997) Writer, Producer
  • Maybe This Time (1995) Writer, Producer
  • Boy Meets World (1993) Writer, Producer
  • Dinosaurs (1991) Writer, Producer
  • Singer & Sons (1990) Writer, Producer
  • My Two Dads (1987) Writer, Producer
  • 227 (1985) Writer, Producer
  • Who's the Boss? (1984) Consultant
  • The Facts of Life (1979) Writer, Producer
