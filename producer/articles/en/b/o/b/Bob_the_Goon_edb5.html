Bob the Goon

Bob the Goon was the Joker's henchman in the 1989 Batman film. He was originally a grunt in Carl Grissom's gang and was present at the Axis Chemicals heist where Jack Napier fell into a vat of chemicals and became the Joker. When the Joker murdered Grissom and took over the gang, he made Bob his "number one guy" in a twisted parody of Jack's own former relationship with Grissom.

Bob assisted the Joker in numerous capers, including defacing Gotham's art museum and taking a number of photos of various Gothamites. It was in one of these photos that the Joker first caught a glimpse of Vicki Vale. When Batman later stole the Joker's Smilex gas-spewing balloons, the Joker shot Bob out of frustration.

Bob the Goon has never appeared outside of the different mediums for the 1989 film, although he is notable because he had one of the few Toybiz action figures for the movie. Bob was portrayed in the film by Tracey Walter.

According to the clip-out biography card on the packaging of Bob's figure, his real name is "Robert Capistrano" while in the novelization he is called "Bob Hawkins". It is also strongly implied that he was the second mugger with a young Jack Napier when Napier killed Thomas and Martha Wayne, as his voice is heard in the scene shouting to Jack, although Bob being the second mugger is never explicitly stated.
