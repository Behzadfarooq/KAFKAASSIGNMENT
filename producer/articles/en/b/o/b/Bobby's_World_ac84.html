Bobby's World is a children's animated television series, which ran from 1990 to 1998, on FOX. It was about the daily life of Bobby Generic (pronounced /ˈdʒɛ.nɚˌɹɪk/, unlike the adjective that is pronounced /dʒəˈnɛ.ɹək/) and his very overactive imagination on how he sees the world. This show was created by Howie Mandel (now the host of NBC's Deal or No Deal), who was also the voice of four-year-old Bobby (as well as Bobby's father, who looks just like a cartoon version of Howie himself), and was produced by Film Roman for Fox Television Studios and Fox Kids Network.

[edit] History

The character of "Bobby" can likely be traced back to Mandel's days as a stand up comedian in Canada and later the United States. Mandel would commonly go into character with the same voice as Bobby, often conversing with an unseen "parent".[citation needed].

[edit] Show Format

Episodes often consist of a short live action segment either before or after the main story (and sometimes both before and after). The segment would include Mandel describing some aspect of the story and often relating it back to his personal childhood. Sometimes during these segments, the character of Bobby would appear in animated form and converse with Mandel. Other times, a live action child would appear and exchange words with Mandel. Endings of the show also featured Mandel breaking the proverbial "fourth wall" by talking to viewers about the preceding episode.

[edit] Characters

  • Bobby Generic, 4-year old
  • Howard Generic, Bobby's father (modeled upon and also voiced by Howie Mandel)
  • Martha Generic, Bobby's mother, who speaks with a heavy Minnesota accent and often punctuates her speech with odd phrases like "Fer cryin' in the mud", "gee golley" and "don't'cha know".
  • Kelly Generic, Bobby's 14-year-old sister
  • Derek Generic, Bobby's 10-year-old brother
  • The Generic Twins, Bobby's infant brothers
  • Aunt Ruth, Bobby's aunt and Martha's sister (voice of Edie McClurg)
  • Uncle Ted, Bobby's uncle and Martha's brother (voice of Tino Insana)
  • Roger, the Generic family dog
  • Jackie, Bobby's next door neighbor/classmate with the floor-length pigtails who always kisses him

[edit] Trivia

  • The theme song for Bobby's World was composed by John Tesh, along with Michael Hanna.
  • The voice of Martha Generic (as performed by Gail Matthius) was based on the voice of Roweena, a character that Matthius performed on Saturday Night Live.
  • In the US, during the show's final season on-air, the show was open-captioned for the hearing-impaired; this was done by using the show's closed-captions in a way that all viewers could see them. This is despite the fact that most network programming by this time were closed-captioned, and most televisions made after 1993 had closed-captioning capabilities.

[edit] The Return of Bobby's World?

Since early-2006, Howie Mandel had dropped several hints indicating a possible revival of Bobby's World:

  • In an April 2006 online interview, Mandel expressed his desire for a possible re-launch of the series.[1]
  • On September 28, 2006, Mandel mentioned on The Ellen DeGeneres Show that the series will be returning in 2007, in addition to doing his job hosting Deal or No Deal.
  • He further mentioned that the series was returning on The Late Late Show with Craig Ferguson.
  • While he did not mention it during his November 15, 2006 appearance on Late Night with Conan O'Brien he wore a shirt that read "Generic Rules", a possible reference to Bobby's last name.
  • Bobby made a cameo appearance on the February 12th, 2007 episode of the NBC game show hosted by Howie, Deal or No Deal. The premise here was that the contestant, Donato DeMarinis, wanted Howie to do his Bobby's voice, but Howie said that his throat wasn't up to the challenge. Later on, during an offer, Howie had Bobby come on stage (by magic of animation and post-production) to ask Donato the magic question -- "Deal or No Deal?" This was likely Bobby's first appearance on network television since 1998.[2]
  • While filling in for Regis Philbin on Live with Regis and Kelly on March 21, 2007, Howie said that Bobby's world will be returning.
  • On April 2, 2007, Howie told Ellen DeGeneres "Bobby's world is coming back. You can check it out on Kabillion.com." This information can be confirmed via his official site, where it says "Bobby's World is back!! Go to Kabillion.com"
