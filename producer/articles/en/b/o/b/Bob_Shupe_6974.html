Bob Shupe, known by his ringname The Pink Assassin, is an American professional wrestler and manager who has competed in New England and East Coast independent promotions during the late 1980s and 1990s, most notably as a mainstay of International World Class Championship Wrestling. He is regarded as one of the more flamboyant and controversal "heels" in the promotion for his colorful ring costumes and frequent use of spary cans as foreign objects. Although failing to win the ICW Junior Heavyweight Championship, he was involved in feuds with Robbie Ellis, "Jumping" Joe Savoldi and Ivan Putski for much of his career.

In 1993, Shupe was one of many wrestlers to leave the promotion in favor of IWCCW veteran Tony Rumble's Century Wrestling Alliance (later NWA Cold Front), winning the CWF Light Heavyweight Championship twice, before eventually becoming a prominant manager to many top independent wrestlers in the New England-area.
