Bob Morton

Bob Morton is an electrical engineer and Chairman/Executive Director of the Naturist Action Committee, an organization dedicated to defending the rights of naturists everywhere.

Bob is also an articulate and gifted writer who published one of the first club newsletters for the Hill Country Nudists that went beyond the fluff of everyday club events and tackled hard-core issues regarding the nudist movement. Bob also had a policy of printing everyone's full name in the newsletter. Back in those days nudist publication editors weren't allowed to use last names of club members as if there were something shameful in the practice of their chosen lifestyle.

The entire Morton family was plaintiffs in a NAC-supported lawsuit to return the rights of parents who wish to bring their children with them to Hippie Hollow, a clothing optional public park near Austin, Texas.

Bob has testified before various state legislative committees on behalf of naturists, and has been chairman of NAC since 1997. Before joining the board, he was a NAC Area Rep.

In addition to watching out for our interests in all 50 states of the U.S., he currently watches for anti-nudity legislation in all 254 counties in Texas since that state voted for home rule last year. This means any county in the state can enact its own anti-nudist ordinances and laws.
