Bob Clarke (historian)

Bob Clarke, born in Scarborough in 1964 is an English archaeologist and Historian.

Upon leaving school Clarke joined the Royal Air Force at the height of the Cold War in 1981. He served at RAF Leeming, Lyneham, Ascension Island and St Athan during a nine year term. After leaving the RAF he travelled around Europe with his wife, Sarah; this left him with an interest in the Cold War. This interest was demonstrated in 2005 when his first major book, Four Minute Warning was published by Tempus Publishing.Following on he has just completed his second book covering the Berlin Airlift.

Clarke is the senior tutor in archeology at the University of Bath in Swindon, he was educated in archeology under professors Mick Aston and Julian Richards, followed by a stint at Westminster Institute of Education, Oxford where he obtained his honours degree in post-compulsory education. His down to earth teaching style has earned him favour among his students, and his writing style has been described as "...very readable..." (purchasers review on Amazon.co.uk), a situation echoed by those who have worked along side him. Clarke holds the post of QinetiQ Archaeologist at Boscombe Down and sits on Boscombe Down's highly successful conservation group.

Clarke recently took up the position of honorary review editor for the Wiltshire Archaeological and Natural History Society, the Wiltshire County journal. His research areas cover a number of periods including Prehistoric and Roman Wiltshire and British Military Architecture of the 20th Century (airfields and nuclear), an area where his is considered an authority. He is credited with the discovery of Broad Town Man, a Saxon execution burial from North Wiltshire.
