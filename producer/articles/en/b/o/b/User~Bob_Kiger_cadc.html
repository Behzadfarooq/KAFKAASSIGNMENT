Graduate of Rochester Institute of Technology with BS, cum laude, in Professional Photography.

Author of the word videography, used as the descriptive title for a technical article in AMERICAN CINEMATOGRAPHER magazine in October, 1972, "Videography, What Does It All Mean?"

Directed and Produced for Videography Studios from 1972 until 1982.

Shooting directing and producing videography from 1982 to date, specializing in POV videography from bicycles, helmetcams and using varietal techniques to deliver high definition imagery to a waiting world.

Edited video since 1970 including a list of firsts in field including first non linear automatic assembly using CMX. First deployment of Quad Split editing on broadcast television (Barney Miller Show). First production Ultimatte studio in Hollywood.

Currently a beta tester for Adobe Systems and StreamStage. Soon to be moderator of generic "videography" forum on Creative Calf.
