Bob McDonald (CBC journalist)

Bob McDonald is a Canadian science journalist. He is the senior science correspondent for CBC Radio and CBC Television, and hosts a weekly radio show, Quirks and Quarks. He also hosted Wonderstruck, a science program for children, and the special The Greatest Canadian Invention.

On September 8, 2005, he premiered a new series on TVOntario called Heads Up!. Heads Up! was nominated for two Gemini awards (best children's TV program and best writing for a children's TV program) in August of 2006. Bob has written a number of books including Wonderstruck, Wonderstruck II and Measuring the Earth with a Stick: Science as I've Seen It.

Bob has been honoured for his contributions to the public awareness of science. In 2001, he received the Michael Smith Award for Science Promotion, from NSERC; and the 2002 Sandford Fleming Medal from The Royal Canadian Institute, and in 2005, the McNeil Medal for the Public Awareness of Science from the Royal Society of Canada. Bob has also been honoured for his work by the University of Guelph and Carleton University (Ottawa), which each granted him honorary doctorates.
