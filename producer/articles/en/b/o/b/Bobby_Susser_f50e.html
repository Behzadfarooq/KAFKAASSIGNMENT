[edit] BIOGRAPHY

Bobby Susser, born July 18,1942, in New York City, New York is an American young children's songwriter and record producer. He has an M.A in Communications Arts And Sciences And Early Childhood Education from Columbia University. After producing four single records from 1961 through 1962 with his childhood friend Paul Simon, for the group Tico And The Triumphs, which included his first billboard chart record "Motorcycle," he concentrated on writing and producing rhythm 'n blues songs.

From 1965 through 1971 he wrote and produced songs for Florence DeVore, Sharon Redd, Lily Fields, Mamie Lee, Robert John, and Trini Lopez. In 1972 he wrote and produced the controversial song, "Once You Understand," sung by his studio group, Think. After being banned from radio stations in several cities, the anti-drug novelty song sold 1.2 million records in the United States. It was also a top 5 hit in England and Germany.

Susser started receiving letters from churches, synagogues, and schools inviting him to play his song and talk with teenagers about its topic. This gave him a new direction and purpose that would shape his entire musical career and his future. He started to write and produce songs and activities for children ages 4 through 8. Titles in the 1970s included "Learning Basic Awareness Through Music," "Pre-Physical Education Through Music," "Pre-Square Dance," and "Let's Act As Consumers." He also created the series for young pre-readers, "Doctor Alphabet."

During the 1980s he visited schools with his songs and for a few years wrote and produced with and for the rock n' roll singer KZ Stella. But he left those projects to go back full time to his first love, young children's music. In 1992 he started his own record company, and wrote and produced songs for thematic albums that have become some of the most popular young children's songs and recordings used in schools, churches, synagogues, and homes. He created "The Bobby Susser Singers," and "The Bobby Susser Children's Chorus," for his series, "Bobby Susser Songs For Children," ages 2 through 8. Michael Green became his lead male vocalist and Deanna Jones his lead female vocalist for many of the early recordings within this series.

He also wrote, produced, and contributed the official theme song "Bikewell Bear And St. Jude," for St. Jude Children's Research Hospital. Billboard Magazine said that, "Bobby Susser is unusually prolific and consistently does excellent work."1 Manhattan School of Music has stated, "Bobby Susser has the rare ability to return to childhood at will."2 Early Childhood News says, "Bobby Susser is unlike other children's musical artists in that he writes for them, not for himself."3
