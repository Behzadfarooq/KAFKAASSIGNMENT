Boban Babunski

Boban Babunski (born May 5, 1968 in Skopje) is a Macedonian football coach and former player.

He was capped two times for Yugoslavia in 1991. Between 1993 and 2000 he played 23 matches and scored 1 goal for the Macedonian national team.

On club level Babunski played in Macedonia, Bulgaria, Spain, Japan, Greece and Germany.

Boban Babunski served as assistant coach for Macedonian national team manager Slobodan Santrač. When Santrač resigned on 23rd August 2005 Babunski was appointed caretaker manager. On 17 February 2006, Srecko Katanec took over the job on a permanent basis.

[edit] Clubs

  • 1986 – 1992 : FK Vardar,  Republic of Macedonia
  • 1992 – 1994 : CSKA Sofia,  Bulgaria
  • 1994 – 1996 : UE Lleida,  Spain (63 matches, 3 goals)
  • 1996 – 1998 : Gamba Osaka,  Japan
  • 1998 – 1999 : AEK Athens,  Greece
  • 1999 – 2000 : CD Logroñés,  Spain (34 matches, 0 goals)
  • 2000 – 2001 : Chemnitzer FC,  Germany
