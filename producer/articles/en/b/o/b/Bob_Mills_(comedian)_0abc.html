Bob Mills (comedian)

Bob Mills (born 1957) is a British comedian, notable for his appearances in cult TV series such as In Bed with MeDinner and The Show, the latter an attempt to do a non-fictional British version of The Larry Sanders Show.

In the 1990s, he also hosted the daytime quiz show Win, Lose Or Draw on ITV, and the computer games programme Games World on Sky One. When hosting Win, Lose Or Draw, he made every Wednesday 'Waistcoat Day', and had a mug full of fake paper money for the winner.

He appears periodically on BBC Five Live's Fighting Talk. He is also a well-known supporter of Leyton Orient Football Club, and writes a regular column in the Leyton Orient programme. He is often found on BBC London sitting in for Danny Baker.

He can also be found on shows such as Channel Four's 100 Greatest... as a talking head; most recently 100 Greatest Funny Moments, although oddly enough he did not feature on the list itself.

More recently, he has written and co-written scripts for television programmes including Shameless and Christmas Lights.
