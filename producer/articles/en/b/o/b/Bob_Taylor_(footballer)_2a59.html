Bob Taylor (born Feb 3, 1967 in Horden, County Durham) is an English footballer, currently without a club having most recently played for Kidderminster Harriers in the Conference National.

[edit] Early career

Brought up in the small mining community of Horden, Taylor enjoyed a successful schoolboy career, playing primarily as a defender, but later switching to striker, the position he would play for his entire professional career. At the age of 17 he joined his local non-league club, Horden Colliery Welfare. In 1986, after only a few months there, Taylor turned professional by signing for Leeds United, at that time managed by Billy Bremner.

He gradually worked his way into the Leeds first team, becoming a regular by the 1987-88 season, but with new Leeds manager Howard Wilkinson looking for instant success, the young centre forward found himself surplus to requirements. Thus in March 1989, Taylor moved to Bristol City (with Carl Shutt moving to Leeds as part of the deal) and became an instant success. In 1989-90 he scored 27 league goals (34 in total) as City achieved promotion to Division Two.

[edit] West Brom and Bolton

Although the following season proved to be less prolific for Taylor, he nevertheless attracted the attention of West Bromwich Albion. It was Albion manager Bobby Gould who brought him to The Hawthorns for a £300,000 fee, as a replacement for the departed Don Goodman. Taylor scored on his debut vs Brentford in a 2-0 Hawthorns win, and added another two on his away debut as Albion beat local rivals Birmingham City 3-0 at St. Andrews.

The 1992-93 season was a memorable one for Taylor. Revelling in the attacking football Albion played under manager Ossie Ardiles, he scored 37 goals (30 of those in the league), and the Baggies beat Port Vale 3-0 at Wembley to secure promotion to Division One.

When Andy Hunt arrived at Albion in 1993, he and Taylor forged a formidable partnership that would last several seasons. As the 1990s wore on, Albion managers came and went, but Taylor continued scoring goals. He enjoyed a great rapport with the fans and quickly became a club legend. Surprisingly he scored only one hat-trick for Albion, in a 4-4 draw against Watford on 12 March 1996.

Things changed however in 1998, when Denis Smith succeeded Ray Harford as manager. Recovering from an ankle injury and struggling with his fitness, Taylor was sent out on loan to Premiership club Bolton Wanderers. The spell was a successful one, with Taylor scoring against Manchester United at Old Trafford, and eventually led to a permanent move after Albion were only prepared to offer him a one-year contract.

He was the Wanderers top scorer in the 1998-99 season as they just missed out on a return to the Premiership by losing to Watford F.C in the divisional Play Off finals at Wembley Stadium.

When Sam Allardyce arrived as Bolton manager in 1999-2000, Taylor found his chances limited, but his career was about to take an unexpected twist. With West Bromwich Albion struggling near the bottom of the first division, manager Gary Megson swooped to make Taylor one of five deadline-day signings. Taylor's return to the Hawthorns paid off as he scored five goals in eight games, including one in a last day 2-0 victory over Charlton Athletic, to keep Albion in Division 1. The following season (2000-01) then saw Albion exceed all expectations, reaching the Division 1 playoffs, where they lost in the semi-final to Bolton.

It was however the (2001-02) season that would become the crowning glory of Taylor's Albion career. After scoring vital goals in the final few games of the season against Nottingham Forest, Coventry and Rotherham, he sealed Albion's promotion to the Premiership with the second goal in a final-day 2-0 win over Crystal Palace. Unfortunately Albion struggled in their first season in the Premiership, with Taylor starting only one game.

[edit] Later career

Bob Taylor's testimonial match was played at The Hawthorns on 13 May 2003, in front of over 16,000 fans. It was an emotional farewell, as it also marked the end of his second and final spell at West Brom. Taylor then spent a season at Cheltenham Town, before joining Tamworth in 2004. A highlight of his time at the latter club was a 10-minute 2nd-half hat-trick in a 3-2 win at Leigh RMI on 6 November 2004. Following his release from Tamworth in May 2006, Taylor linked up with Kidderminster Harriers for pre-season training and signed a non-contract deal with the club in September 2006. Taylor left Kidderminster in January 2007 having made 2 appearances as a substitute for the club.
