Hi Bob. Marking something up with {{copyvio|url=whatever}} or {{imagevio|url=whatever}} adds it automatically to Category:Possible copyright violations but not to Wikipedia:Copyright problems. As the latter allows for free wiki-style discussion and can be built up in chronological order, it serves as the main list of copyright problems. I occasionally pick out any pages from the category which (like the Maradona images you found) don't seem to be listed on Copyright problems, and add them there myself - hence, with tongue slightly in cheek, "lost+found". If you could add a listing on Copyright problems when you mark an article or image as a copyvio, it would make my (self-appointed) job a bit easier. Thanks! [edit] Magnificent!

Wow - what a wonderful set of photos of a bighorn. It must have been awe inspiring to be in the presence of such a magnificent example of such a rare animal. I'm truly jealous. As for writing about the flora and fauna of the park area - PLEASE DO! :) I've looked all over both on and offline for a good reference on the subject since that is the biggest current omission at Capitol Reef National Park. [edit] FAC briefing for you, re: Pink Floyd

"FAC" stands for Featured Article Candidacy, the rather grueling process which an editor goes through when nominating an article to be a featured article, one of the .01% of articles in the project which are considered outstanding. Not knowing quite what I'd got myself into, after retooling the Pink Floyd article, I entered it for FAC and spent 20-30 hours and re-wrote 70%+ of the article myself, along with other editors who pitched in and helped, getting it to featured status. One of the complaints that had to be addressed when it was run through was the use of plural tense referring to the band name, which a couple of editors complained about in "oppose" votes for the nomination. - dharmabum 10:39, 29 April 2006 (UTC)
