Boris "Bob" Said (May 5, 1932 in New York City - March 24, 2002 in Los Angeles) was a racing driver from the United States. He participated in one Formula One Grand Prix, on December 12, 1959. He spun off on the first lap and scored no World Championship points. He also made one NASCAR start, the 1959 Daytona 500 where he finished 50th after a transmission failure.

Said was also a bobsled racer, competing in the Olympics twice, 1968 in Grenoble and in 1972 at Sapporo, Japan. Later he was the Executive Producer of a documentary entitled Mystery of the Sphynx.

His son, Boris Said III, is a current NASCAR driver and road racing expert.
