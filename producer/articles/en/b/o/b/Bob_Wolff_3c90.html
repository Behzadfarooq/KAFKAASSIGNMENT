Bob Wolff

Bob Wolff is a former broadcaster who was the radio and TV voice of the Washington Senators from 1947 to 1960, continuing with the team when they relocated and became the Minnesota Twins in 1961. He was also nationally known for broadcasting the NBC Game of the Week during the 1950s and 1960s along with Joe Garagiola, and covered the World Series on the radio from the 1950s into the 1970s. He was on hand to call Don Larsen's perfect game in the 1956 Fall Classic. He later became well-known regionally, as the voice of the New York Knicks of the NBA (where he was teamed with Marv Albert) as well as the New York Rangers of the NHL (sharing duties with Sam Rosen).
