I believe that the image page already explains why this image is irreplaceable, which I quote again here:

This image depicts Bob Rae during his term as Premier of Ontario and leader of the New Democratic Party in the late 1980s and early 1990s. Current images cannot be used to accurately depict Rea during this era. Therefore, in articles about Rae as premier (as opposed to Rae as Liberal leadership candidate), this image is fair use as no alternative is available.

[edit] Arguments for deletion - speedy and otherwise

This image depicts a Canadian politician, Bob Rae, who was elected Premier of Ontario 17 years ago. Since that time he has changed political parties and after 11 years in political retirement was a serious candidate for the leadership of one of Canada's two major parties and is currently a candidate for the Canadian parliament.

When Bob Rae served as premier he wore, and was known for, his distinct glasses and also his boyish looks. Today he has white hair and does not wear glasses or even look much like his younger self.

It is important to be able to depict the "two Bob Raes" and there is no free image available to depict Rae of the early 1990s. - Jord 17:18, 23 March 2007 (UTC)
