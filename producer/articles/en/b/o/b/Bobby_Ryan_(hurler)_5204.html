Bobby Ryan (born 23 October 1961) is a former Irish sportsperson. He played senior hurling with Tipperary from 1981 until 1993.

[edit] Early life

Bobby Ryan was born in Borisoleigh, County Tipperary in 1961. He was born into a family that was steeped in hurling glory. His father, Tim Ryan, and his uncle, Ned Ryan, won All-Ireland medals with Tipperary in 1951, while his uncle, Pat Stakelum, captained Tipp to victory in 1949. Bobby Ryan was educated at Templemore CBS. It was here that he first tasted hurling success when he won a Harty Cup medal with the school in 1978.

[edit] Playing career

[edit] Club

Ryan played his club hurling with Borrisoleigh in Tipperary. He had much success with the club, beginning in 1981 when he won his first Tippereray SHC title. Ryan increased his medal tally to three when he captured further county titles in 1983 and 1986. This latter victory was subsequently converted into a Munster club title. On St. Patrick’s Day, 1987 Ryan completed his club medal collection when he won an All-Ireland club title following a victory over Wexford’s Rathnure club.

[edit] Inter-county

Ryan’s talent as a hurler was quickly spotted by the Tipperary inter-county selectors and he joined the minor panel in the late 1970s. In 1980 he first tasted major success when he won an All-Ireland medal with the under-21 hurling team. In 1981 he captured a second consecutive All-Ireland medal at under-21 level, before making his debut with the senior hurling team. Tipperary’s senior hurling fortunes were in the doldrums at this time, as arch-rivals Cork were the king-pins of Munster hurling. In 1984 Ryan was appointed captain of the senior hurling team. It was the centenary year of the Gaelic Athletic Association and every team was hopeful of success. Tipp reached their first Munster final since 1973, with the game against Cork turned out to be a thriller. Unfortunately, Ryan had to retire from the game early when he suffered a broken leg when Cork’s Tim Crowley fell on him. With four minutes to go in the game Tipp were leading by four points and looked set for victory, however, Seánie O'Leary and Tony O'Sullivan scored two quick goals to clinch victory for Cork. These goals ended Tipp’s involvement in the centenary championship.

In 1986 Ryan’s hurling skill was honoured when he collected his first All-Star award. His involvement in an All-Star trip to the United States in May 1987 cost Ryan his place on the Tipperary team, as new manager Babs Keating advised him not to go. Ryan quickly regained his place on the team and returned at full-forward. It proved to be a successful year for Tipp as the county won its first Munster title in sixteen years. 1988 began well for Ryan when he won a National Hurling League medal. He later captured his second Munster medal, however, Galway defeated his side in the All-Ireland final. In 1989 Ryan was apoointed captain of his county for a second time. Tipperary were still the best team in Munster and Ryan won his third provincial title. After three years of coming close Ryan subsequently won his first All-Ireland medal following a victory over surprise semi-final winners Antrim. It was the county’s first All-Ireland title since 1971. Ryan finished off the year by winning a second consecutive All-Star.

In 1990 Tipperary surrendered their Munster crown to Cork, however, the side returned in 1991 with Ryan collecting his fourth Munster medal. He later won his second All-Ireland medal following a 4-point victory over Kilkenny. In 1993 Ryan won his fifth Munster title, however, Tipperary were defeated by Galway in the All-Ireland semi-final. Ryan retired from inter-county hurling following this defeat.
