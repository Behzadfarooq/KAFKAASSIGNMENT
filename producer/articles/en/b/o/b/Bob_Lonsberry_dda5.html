Bob Lonsberry

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Image:Lonsberry.jpg
Lonsberry currently hosts a radio show at 1180 WHAM WHAM (AM).

Bob Lonsberry is a conservative radio talk show host and former newspaper reporter. He hosts shows on radio stations WHAM-AM (1180), in Rochester, New York (weekdays 11 a.m. to 2 p.m. ET), and KNRS (570) in Salt Lake City, Utah (weekdays 5 a.m. to 9 a.m. MT). His shows can be heard on several radio stations in New York and the intermountain west. Lonsberry has also guest hosted radio shows for Michael Reagan and Glenn Beck.

In 2003 Lonsberry was fired from WHAM-AM for an allegedly racist comment about Rochester's Democratic Mayor William A. Johnson, Jr. who at that time was running for Monroe County Executive. Following Lonsberry's dismissal, many WHAM-AM listeners, upset at his removal, boycotted the station in protest. After a year of declining ratings at WHAM-AM during his absence, he was rehired in 2004 and apologised for his earlier comments on the mayor.

At one time, Lonsberry's own promotion referred to him as "The most fired man in local media," since he previously held posts at the Rochester Democrat and Chronicle and several local TV stations before coming to radio. His show's theme song is "Won't Back Down" by Tom Petty. Apparently while at the Democrat and Chronicle, Lonsberry was pressured to reveal his sources regarding a controversial column. He refused to "back down" and was subsequently fired.[citation needed] In 1999, Lonsberry was an on-the-spot reporter for Channel 31 WUHF-TV, the local Fox affiliate. The relationship ended a year later.

Lonsberry also writes a weekday internet column posted on his website, http://www.lonsberry.com, at which he links to sites that may be of interest to his readers. Similar to a weblog, readers post comments on his column to which Lonsberry frequently responds. The website also includes a virtual writers colony called "Writers on the Loose".

Lonsberry served a mission for the The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints (the Mormon Church) to the Navajo and Hopi Indian reservations[1] in New Mexico and Arizona from 1978 to 1980. He has been married three times[2][3] and has five children. Lonsberry has reported on his radio show that he dropped out of college (Brigham Young University) after one year because he was not a good student.[4] Previous occupations include soldier in the U.S. Army.[5] and manager of a Kentucky Fried Chicken.[6] He grew up outside of rural Hornell, New York. Lonsberry's step dad worked for the Erie Lackawanna Railway. While in the Army, he received training as a journalist, and upon his return home, began his journalism career in Hornell. A proud member of the NRA, Lonsberry is an advocate for the rights of gun owners, yet stresses the great responsibility that comes with owning firearms.

Lonsberry annually hosts a Christmas-eve holiday broadcast with his family, and sometimes has hosted a New Year's Eve show as well.

[edit] Listeners on the Loose

One of the most distinct features of his show in Rochester is "Listeners on the Loose," where callers get to jump in and offer their last minute thoughts at the end of the show. Sometimes the comments are serious, others are hilarious, and some are just downright confused--all part of the charm. To the chagrin of many fans, this feature did not return with Lonsberry until 2007, but only as a podcast recorded off the air.

[edit] Recurring Callers

  • Fuita: A young to middle aged woman who has a crush on Lonsberry. Fuita speaks in a cutsey and shy tone (pronouncing 'r's as 'w's), and has openly wished that she was, "Mrs. Bob Lonsberry."
  • Sid Vicious: Calls nearly every episode of the podcast, speaking in a poorly imitated British accent, and who uses the phrases, "Bloody," and, "Mate," to excess. Tends to chastize Lonsberry about some point he made on the show. He has called back multiple times during one podcast after Bob cuts him off.
  • Rosie O'Donnell: An obviously male caller who speaks in a gruff, deep voice. Claims to be having an extra-marital affair with Bob on the weekends.
