Bob French

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Bob French is a drummer and a radio show host at WWOZ. He has lead The Tuxedo Jazz Band since 1977. (Other leaders of the band have been Oscar "Papa" Celestin (founder), Eddie Pierson and French's father Albert "Papa" French.)

As a child he took drumming lessons from Louis Barbarin. He organized a R&B band band in high school that included James Booker, Art Neville, Charles Neville, and Kidd Jordan, Alvin Batiste. He has played and recorded with Dave Bartholomew (he is a relative on his mother's side). He has also recorded with Fats Domino.[1]
