Bobaflex is an American alternative metal band from Point Pleasant, West Virginia.

Bobaflex is an odd beast, a bracing mix of heavy riffage, hip-hop-inspired beats and alternately growling and soaring vocals. There are four singers in the band, so the angle of attack constantly shifts, which is partly why the band's songs careen around your brain far longer than hard rock's usual offerings. Mostly, the songs on Apologize for nothing rock outrageously hard because Bobaflex is willing to try anything that sounds good, and couldn't care less how any of it might make them look. (Copied from their website)

[edit] History

The band's first album, Primitive Epic, is a great coming-out album with lyrics that seem to come right out of the writer's heart. Midnight Nation gets things moving with riffs and vocals that bring Queen to mind, but have moments of Metallica and Wu-Tang Clan. The album moves along nicely with songs that lean a little further toward standard metal and then come back to 70's feeling funk-rock with Tears Drip. The stuff gets heavy later with a song called Turn the Heat Up which is one of those songs which could really get a crowd jumping.

Their follow-up album, Apologize For Nothing, is on the label that launched Nine Inch Nails, TVT Records. The aggressive vocals, funky/heavy guitar and pounding drum beats continue. The style still flies from metal to funk to something else and back. But the sound seems more polished without losing its whetted edge.

According to Shaun McCoy the band is back in the studio in 2007, working on their third album.
