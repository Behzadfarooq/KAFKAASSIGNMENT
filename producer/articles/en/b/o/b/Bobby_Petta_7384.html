Bobby Petta (born 6 August 1974) in Rotterdam, The Netherlands) is a Dutch professional football (soccer) player currently playing as a midfielder for Adelaide United in the Australian A-League. His position is on the left wing.

Petta played in UEFA Cup and UEFA Champions League for his former club Celtic where he featured against Juventus (Italy) in the UEFA Champions League, 2001

Details of his senior career from June 1996 to September 2006: 224 appearances (18 goals)

Bobby Petta's early playing career was with Feyenoord, from the age of 16 to 21, but he never became a regular in the first team in that time, but had loan spells at Dordrecht'90 and RKC Waalwijk. His frustration led to a move to England in 1996 after he was spotted by Ipswich Town scouts. He signed for Ipswich, but for his first season found it difficult to adapt to the more physical British game, making only six appearances during his first full season. The Ipswich manager at the time, George Burley, was determined that his potential would not go to waste , and with hard work he was able to develop his game and physical resilience, with Bobby finally establishing himself in the first team, on the left side of midfield, making over 70 appearances and scoring nine goals.

However, eventually he appeared to become frustrated at Ipswich and his ambitions, and probably those of his agent, led him to allow his contract to expire. This resulted, in July 1999, to a free transfer to Celtic. Possibly the highlight of his career was a period of stardom in the 2001 season during the early managership of Martin O'Neill as Petta became a regular in the team that achieved the domestic treble in 2001 and played a starring role in the 6-2 victory for Celtic over Rangers in the first Old Firm game of the season - his rampant performance leading to the early substitution of Rangers right-back Fernando Ricksen. His form throughout that season also saw him called up to the Dutch national team but injury prevented him from playing for his country.

Following that his first team appearances became limited as a result of injury and the good form of other players, and he made only two starts during the 2002/03 season. Fulham then signed Petta on loan in January 2004 for the remainder of that season, making 13 appearances before returning to Scotland. However there was no way back into the Celtic first team and he left the club early in 2005.

He tried to revive his career in England, first with Darlington and then Bradford, but finally leaving the country in 2006 to join the Australian club, Adelaide United.

Petta was named in the 2006/07 A-League Dud XI on popular Australian football website, pintculture.com, a selection based on him not living up to his reputation.[1]
