Bob Culbertson is an influential Chapman Stick player. An early adaptor of the instrument, he is notable not only for his many recorded albums, but also for his extensive touring.

Bob Culbertson played with or opened perfomances by many popular musicians, including Robben Ford, Steve Morse, Alan Holdsworth, and members of Santana amongst others. He has taught hundreds of players and released a set of instructional Stick videos, Lessons on the Stick.

Culbertson is famous not only for his spectacular technics, which allows him to play three to four melodic, harmonic or rhythmic lines simultaneously (e.g. bass line, chords and melody in a time), but also due to well above average compositional talent. His official discography lists 7 albums, yet at least 2 others exist, which are either out of print or maybe just widelu-known bootlegs. Furthermore, a DVD of Bob Culbertson live (yet recorded in studio, in front of blue screen) is available to the public. According to info on his official page, Bob Culbertson has sold over 100,000 albums, even if they are hard to find in stores, and most of them are sold during live concerts, seminars or ordered from his web site.

Around 2002, Bob Culbertson together with Emmett Chapman and luthier Craig Anderson created the AcouStick, an acoustical Stick instrument, now featured on Culbertson's CD and DVD, and became the first men ever to play on it.

Most of Culbertson's albums are solo recordings without overdubbing or digital editing, except minor effect processing. Only Stick and New World Dance feature other musicians.
