Bob Dailey (born May 3, 1953 in Kingston, Ontario) was a professional ice hockey defenseman who combined both size (he was 6'5") and skill.

Dailey was selected ninth overall in the 1973 NHL Amateur Draft from the Toronto Marlboros, where he had won the Memorial Cup as a junior. Over the course of a 561-game NHL career he played for the Vancouver Canucks and Philadelphia Flyers. His career ended with a freak injury - he shattered his ankle after catching a rut on the ice during a game in 1982. He attempted a comeback with the Hershey Bears of the American Hockey League in 1985, but found he could not compete and retired for good after only five games.
