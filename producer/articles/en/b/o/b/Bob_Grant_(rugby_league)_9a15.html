Bob Grant (rugby league)

Bob Grant was an Australian rugby league player, a halfback of the late 1960s and 1970s for the South Sydney Rabbitohs.

[edit] Club Career

He featured in the talented South Sydney sides of the late 60s/early 70s. He played in the Premiership victories of 1968, 1970 and 1971. Grant starred with two tries in Souths' convincing 23-12 victory over Manly in the 1970 Grand Final.

In all he played 136 club games for South Sydney between 1966 and 1975.

Grant was voted Rugby League Week Player of the Year for his performances during the 1971 premiership season.

In 2004 he was named by Souths in their South Sydney Dream Team,[1], consisting of 17 players and a coach representing the club from 1908 through to 2004.

[edit] Representative Career

Grant made one Test appearances for the Australian national representative side and represented in two games for NSW.
