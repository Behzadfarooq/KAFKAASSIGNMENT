Bob Jones High School is a public high school in Madison, Alabama.

The school is a part of Madison City Schools. Despite its name, it is not affiliated with Bob Jones University of Greenville, South Carolina.

Bob Jones High School opened in 1974 at 1304 Hughes Road and moved to 650 Hughes Road in 1996. The school is named for former Alabama congressman Robert E. "Bob" Jones. The current principal is Robert Parker, and the school is in Region 8 of class 6A in the AHSAA. As of 2007, it is the largest high school in Alabama by enrollment.
