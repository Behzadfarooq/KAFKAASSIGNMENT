[edit] Bob Fitzpatrick

Bob Fitzpatrick is an entrepreneur and communications expert dedicated to defining systems that anticipate business and consumers needs in the digital age.


Biography

For the past fourteen years, Bob has worked as a media and communications consultant delivering business, creative, and technology solutions for Fortune 500 corporations and Sillicon Valley start-ups.


Bob is currently an independent media and communications consultant helping NY-based Ikana Media deliver media and communication strategies to enterprise clients. Ikana Media is a boutique consultancy founded by Sawyer Media Systems executives. Presently, Bob is acting Program Manager for a consumer-facing broadband web initiative funded by a major healthcare corporation. Further details are confidential at this time.


In 2000, leveraging his pre-IPO experience with Internet and media service firms, Bob was recruited to join Sawyer Media Systems (SMS) as Director of Consulting Services. SMS was founded by Mark Kvamme of Sequoia Capital and network TV journalist Forrest Sawyer to improve corporate communications across the enterprise.


Sawyer's products included MediaProducer™ and MediaAdministrator™. Services included multi-channel communications strategy, media production, software deployment, and system integration.


Initially, Bob was chartered with defining the company's market opportunity and related service offerings. Later, Bob was promoted to Managing Director, Delivery West and led engagements for Handspring, Cisco, Wells Fargo, Autodesk, and Hyperion.


Prior to Sawyer, Bob joined Viant, a pioneering e-services firm, to define and develop the creative service offering. During his tenure, Bob introduced a unified process for user interface and graphic design development (a synthesis visual design/UML best practices) and set the company standard for recruiting creative talent by recruiting the company’s first graphic designers as well as the first Western Regional Creative Officers.


Bob led creative delivery and development during the firm’s first “dot com” engagements for RadioShack, Kinkos, and the Schwab Institutional Managers site for Charles R. Schwab, Inc. Bob also helped develop a “click & mortar” web strategy and complete system re-architecture for Robert Half International.


As part of Viant's business incubator program, Bob worked with the founders of Ecast, Inc. to develop a private content distribution network. The magnet applications of the Ecast Network was the Siren system of Internet-enabled digital music jukeboxes and the Athena counter-top game unit. Bob also led UI design efforts for Replay TV and Covad Communications.


In 1996, Bob joined CKS Interactive as a Producer and helped develop major corporate web presences for General Motors, Transamerica Corporation, Tandem Computers, and Fujitsu Computer Products of America. Bob also led various web content development efforts for Levi Strauss & Co., Apple Computers, Ortho Biotech, Sears Craftsman, and Nike.


Bob began his consulting career as co-founded of the boutique media services firm Answers & Miracles. His company provided video, multimedia, and first generation web development services to on-line publishers, graphic design firms and communications agencies. A&M clients included White & Company, Arcada, Red Herring Magazine, New Media Magazine, HeyerTech, Inc., Media Synergy & Synergy Labs, Black Entertainment Television, IICS, SF MDG, and Foresight Communications.


Bob received his Bachelor’s of Science in Business Administration from Miami University, Oxford, Ohio in 1987.
