Bobby Goes Nuts is episode 105 in the Fox animated television series King of the Hill.

[edit] Plot

Connie invites Bobby over to liven up her slumber party that is getting boring. He shows up, and is hamming it up for the ladies, when Chane, Kahn's dream boyfriend for Connie, crashes the party, and is not happy to see Bobby. Chane successfully shames Bobby in front of Connie and the rest of the girls by making him eat dirt. Bobby, the pacifist, tries to talk himself out of the fight to no avail.

Hank is appalled to see Bobby in this condition, covered in dirt, and half beaten. Bobby tries to get Hank to go take care of it for him, but Hank tells Bobby that he is going to have to learn to defend himself. Hank sends Bobby to the YMCA to enroll in a boxing class.

Bobby is unable to get into the boxing course, but after he insists on being enrolled in something he is placed in a women's self-defense class. He quickly accepts this and gains confidence when he quickly masters the class technique of targeting the attacker below the belt, while yelling "That's my purse!" Armed with his secret weapon technique, Bobby makes certain that his next encounter with Chane, or Dooley, or any other bully has a different outcome.

One by one, Bobby takes down the bullies, gaining a reputation for applying vicious groin attacks to anyone who crosses him. Hank is thrilled, as he believes that Bobby is standing his ground and fighting hand to hand. The confrontations catch the attention of the principal, and Hank is more than happy to go deal with the situation. The situation takes an ugly turn when Hank is informed of how Bobby is winning these fights. Hank tells Bobby that there is no way he is to use that move and demands to know who taught him to fight like that. Bobby explains the YMCA situation. Hank decides that he will teach Bobby how to fight fairly.

During their lesson, Bobby becomes frustrated with Hank, and uses his kick. Hank is dropped to his knees, and ends up seriously hurt. Bobby is grounded. Later, after a talk with Kahn, Bobby breaks the "no video games" rule, and Hank orders him to put away a video game that he isn't allowed to play. Bobby, challenging his father, refuses. Hank again orders him to put down the video game, and again, Bobby refuses. Hank then attempts to get up, but is straining in pain from the groin kick. Bobby, knowing Hank is impaired, starts to walk away. Peggy, hearing the ruckus, goes outside and orders Bobby to comply with Hank's demands. Bobby tells his mother to go away, and that this is between him and his dad. Peggy then proceeds to physically take the video game from him. Neighbors gather, and everyone is watching Peggy and Bobby wrestle on the lawn. When they stand, Bobby uses his kick on Peggy, who stands strong, with a smirk. "I think you will find that I have no testicles". Kahn isn't convinced, shouting, "She bluffing, finish her!" Peggy then headlocks and noogies Bobby. The neighbors all watch as Bobby's mother punishes him.

Bobby is angry at Hank, saying that Hank didn't fight fairly. Hank, however, warns Bobby that he could "get her(Peggy) to do that any time (he) wanted". After sending Bobby inside to wash up, Hank then painfully hobbles in behind him.

[edit] Trivia

  • This was the first King of the Hill episode (apart from the title sequence) to use digital coloring. For the rest of season 6 and 7, the show went back to traditional ink-and-paint, finally going all-digital in season 8.
