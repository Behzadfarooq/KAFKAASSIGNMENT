Bobby Loves Mangos is a 1998 short film that touches on the consequences of time travel from the standpoint of a middle school principal who receives a mysterious video cassette in the mail.

[edit] Plot

Spoiler warning: Plot and/or ending details follow.

Troy Maine Middle School principal Dwight Stevenson receives a video cassette in the mail. Watching the cassette with his secretary, it is a simple recording of a man who claims to be one of Stevenson's students, Bobby Derek, years in the future. Derek says that he has been given the opportunity to test new technology that allowed him to send the tape into the past, and the reason for the tape is to warn Stevenson about an impending disaster.

A couple days after Stevenson receives the tape, Bobby's class is scheduled to take a trip. According to the man who identifies himself as Bobby on the tape, he was not able to go on the trip because the night before, his mother served him mangos, to which he had a serious allergic reaction. This proved fortuitous, as everyone who went on the trip was supposed to have died when the school bus swerved off a bridge. Derek pleads with Principal Stevenson to take an alternate route, and thereby prevent the accident that would haunt the town for years. He lastly advises not to contact authorities, as it would alter the timeline too much.

The evening after seeing the tape, Stevenson's secretary buys all the mangos in the grocery store so that Bobby won't have an allergic reaction and end up in the hospital; however, she fails to consider that the mangos were the only thing that kept Bobby off the bus and saved his life. Bobby does not eat mangos, and is well the day of the trip. Although he believes the timeline has already been irrevocably changed, Stevenson goes ahead with the plan and asks the bus driver to take an alternate route.

Stevenson cannot take the tension and waiting and leaves for lunch. Upon returning to the school, he finds the police are already there. He then learns from the detectives that the man on the videotape is not Bobby Derek, and is not from the future — but is his father who had recently lost custody. Bobby's father, Charles Derek, stopped the bus on its alternate route and kidnapped Bobby at gunpoint. While the police are still looking for Charles and Bobby, Bobby's mother says that Bobby isn't allergic to anything, and that he loves mangos.

Spoilers end here.

[edit] Reception

“ "It takes a certain unrelenting determination to pitch your project at Sundance," ”

—Roger Ebert

Despite not being a registered participant at the 1999 Sundance Film Festival, Director Stuart Acher attempted several times to have Bobby Loves Mangos played at the festival — without success. Resolute, Acher found the restaurant where Roger Ebert was dining one evening and convinced the management to play his video on their large-screen TV. After the impromptu screening of the film, Ebert both commended Acher's tenacity, and awarded Bobby Loves Mangos two thumbs up.
