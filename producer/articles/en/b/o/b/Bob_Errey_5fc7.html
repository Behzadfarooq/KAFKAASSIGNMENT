Robert "Bob" Errey (born September 21, 1964 in Montreal, Quebec) is a retired former professional ice hockey player who was drafted fifteenth overall in the 1983 NHL Entry Draft and played 895 NHL games over the course of his career. He played most notably for the Pittsburgh Penguins, but also played for the Buffalo Sabres, San Jose Sharks, Detroit Red Wings, Dallas Stars and New York Rangers. He won 2 Stanley Cups as a member of the Pittsburgh Penguins in 1991 and 1992 . With the Penguins he wore uniform number 12. He retired in 1999.

Bob is currently a TV color analyst with the Penguins. As a color analyst, Bob Errey is very excited about Penguins rookie Jordan Staal. He has been known to talk non-stop about Staal's reach and the excessive length of his stick. Bob has been known to talk about Jordan's stick even when the rookie is not playing.

Although Errey was born in Montreal, he grew up in Peterborough, Ontario and attended Crestwood Secondary School. He is also second cousin to hockey legend Ted Lindsay.
