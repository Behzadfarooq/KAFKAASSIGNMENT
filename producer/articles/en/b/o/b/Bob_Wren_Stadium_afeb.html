Bob Wren Stadium is the baseball field of the Ohio Bobcats. It has been home to Ohio Bobcats baseball since it was opened on April 18, 1998. On that day, Ohio defeated the Bowling Green Falcons by a score of 4-1 in front of a sell out crowd. The stadium is widely regarded as one of the best baseball facilities in the Mid-American Conference and the nation.

Bob Wren Stadium features all of the modern amenities expected of a modern college baseball facility and has a capacity of 4,000. The stadium has 100 chairback seats that are part of the VIP club, named the Baumholtz club in honor of legendary Ohio baseball player Frank Baumholtz. In addition, there are around 1,000 bleacher-back seats in the stadium and another 1,000 seats of regualr bleachers. Down both the third and first base lines there are two grassy knolls that are capable of seating up to another 1,000 fans. All seats were designed with the fan in mind and provide excellent sightlines of the entire field.

Bobcat fans taking in a game at Bob Wren Stadium.

The amenities do not stop with an innovative seating arrangement. There are two concession stands available for the average fan to enjoy traditional ballpark fare, and members of the Baumholtz Club have access to a private lounge that serves more upscale food items. The press box at Bob Wren Stadium is truly state-of-the art, providing multiple phones and computer lines for members of the media. There are two radio broadcast booths and one television broadcast booth available at Bob Wren Stadium.

To keep up with the ever changing landscape of college athletics, numerous renovations have already occurred at Bob Wren Stadium. In 2003, a large Daktronics digital scoreboard was added to the outfield of the stadium. That same year, heated batting cages were added to the northeast of the stadium, allowing Bobcat players to practice inside during cold weather. Most recently, lights were installed at Bob Wren Stadium in 2004. As a result of this new addition, 15 of Ohio's 28 home games in 2005 were played at night.

The stadium is named for former Bobcat head coach Bob Wren, who held the best all-time winning percentage of any Ohio baseball coach. In addition to being a great coach, the late Bob Wren was highly respected throughout the Athens, Ohio community.

In addition to serving as the home of Ohio Bobcats baseball, Bob Wren Stadium is home to the Southern Ohio Copperheads of the Great Lakes Summer Collegiate League and is the home of the state American Legion baseball tournament every year.
