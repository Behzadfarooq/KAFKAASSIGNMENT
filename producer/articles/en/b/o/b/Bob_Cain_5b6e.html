Bob Cain was born Robert Lyle Cain in Hartselle, Alabama.

Owner/operator of "The Canebreak" a night club located in Birmingham, Alabama from the mid-Sixties until the mid-Eighties. Bob was famous for his artistry with the trumpet. He composed numerous songs and recorded several 45s and LPs. His most notable hit was Butterbeans.

Bob retired from the night club business and, after a lengthy hiatus, began a successful career as a Gospel singer with the [Gaither Family][1].

Some of Bob's early recordings are:

  • What Changed Your Mind (45rpm)
  • Gonna Take a Little Time (45rpm)
  • Everything (45rpm)
  • Why When You Get What You Want You Don't Want It No More (45rpm)
  • Butterbeans (45rpm)
  • Lord, Don't Help That Bear! (45rpm)
  • Everything Is Beautiful (LP)
  • Bob Cain in the CANEBREAK (LP)

Bob recorded with: Kenny Wallis, backup vocal and bass, Dale Serrano, drums, Johnny Gore, guitar, and others

Bob died in August 2000.
