[edit] minor spelling change comment

Hi; no biggie, just comment on my double-l in the "crsytallizing" fix on Section 98; I don't even know if this is still in modern stylebooks, as I see all kinds of "odd" spellings nowadays which seem to be simplifications of what "people of my generation" were raised with (that's so odd to say...). Anyway, rule of thumb was that "short" vowels produced a double-consonant, "long" vowels the single; so "repealed" looks right, but "crystalizing", if I'd used that correction, really doesn't. But maybe it's more "modern", I don't know. Gotta go pick out my grey hairs now ;-).Skookum1 19:21, 26 January 2007 (UTC) PS another one that gets me is seeing "mould" where "mold" is meant; i.e. using the spelling for slime mould when you're talking about a cake mold.... ;=)Skookum1 19:21, 26 January 2007 (UTC)

[edit] Sam Sullivan

The use of 'His Worship' is not an address but rather a title that comes with the office. Notice that a lot of Canadian Politician's page are styled 'The Honourable' or 'The Right Honourable' as appropriate.

--Cahk 23:14, 31 January 2007 (UTC)

You've overlooked what it saids in the Style (manner of address) article (which, ironically, I created it from the first place) that 'Your Worship' is the oral form of addressing a mayor, but His Worship is a honorific prefix accorded to him by virtue of assuming the office of Mayor. Although I agree hardly anyone know this and use it, this does not mean the honorific prefix does not exist. [1] --Cahk 22:51, 2 February 2007 (UTC)
