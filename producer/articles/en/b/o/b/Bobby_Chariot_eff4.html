In the 1980s conventional comics, in their frilly shirts and bowties, were swept aside to make way for "alternative comedians" such as Ben Elton, Rik Mayall, Ade Edmondson, Jennifer Saunders and Dawn French and, of course, Alexei Sayle. As comedy moved into the "post-modern" era Alexei Sayle created an alter-ego called Bobby Chariot who appears in "The All New Alexei Sayle Show" as Alexei's warm up man and who's further adventures were chronicalled in "Alexei Sayle's Merry-Go-Round" who represents one such member of the "frilly shirt brigade". Like many other old mainstream comedians who were swept aside in this cultural shake-up he has fallen on hard times. He has a drink problem, an awful act, sleeps in his Jaguar and is in constant acrimony with his ex-wife. The scouse warm-up comic Bobby Chariot (Alexei's Alter-Ego) would appear on screen to fill in when The All New Alexei Sayle Show broke down for supposed technical reasons. When his catchphrase "How you Diddling? ...Bloody Sod Ya Then" he was a mix of the worst sides Les Dawson and Ken Dodd with a bit of Mr Sayle mixed in. Often he was pictured sitting in the audience rather than standing up infront of them: pouring his heart out to some poor uninterested, bored soul. Stephen Lewis (actor) played his even more depressing and hopeless gag writer. His agent/manager seen int Alexei Sayle's Merry-Go-Round was the repulsive "Edna" Denise Coffey.
