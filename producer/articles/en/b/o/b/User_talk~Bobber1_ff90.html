[edit] How do you know Fernando?

"How do you know that i'm Fernando Luis Ferrer Nieves?" Bobber1

The short answer is "Because I'm smart." You are clearly the same person formerly known as Bobber2 and any editor can figure this out quickly and easily. I'm not going to tell you any more than that. If you really want to know how I know, let me strongly suggest that you spend time reading the many pages that tell you how Wikipedia works. Like all the pages mentioned in my post above. By reading those pages you will hopefully gain a better understanding of how to edit here and become less of a "problem editor."

Let me also just warn you ahead of time that if you are thinking about trying to erase stuff in order to "hide your tracks". . . . DON'T. Seriously. The GFDL software license that allows us to operate as a free encyclopedia requires the edit history to be preserved. So the information will never be lost, just hidden, and still very easy to find. Any attempt to erase it will quickly be reverted. Furthermore, erasing edit histories is basically illegal, and is viewed as a very serious offense by the sysops. Messing about with that is a very good way to get yourself banned quickly.

Again, if you have any questions, please ask them on my talk page. I'll see them and be able to answer them much more quickly. Cheers. Soundguy99 17:36, 6 May 2005 (UTC)

[edit] House of Bloo's

Hmmmmm. . . . . . . I'm gonna assume that your edit summary ("Start and finish and added cleanup tag because the plot is not understandable") and addition of the cleanup tag is an attempt to improve your style. Which is great, and I appreciate the effort. However, writing a poor article and labeling it a poor article and asking other people to clean it up is still kind of impolite. If you can't write a clear plot summary. or if you didn't understand the plot, maybe you should just skip that part. There's still a lot of info that could be added to this article: When did the show air? What network was it on? Who starred in it? Is it a pilot for a new series? From the description it sounds like this was either an animated show or used puppets. You could mention that. Where is it set? In the U.S.? In England? In Australia? In a made-up fantasy world? Is it based on a book or a comic book? Who created it? Are they famous for creating other shows? How well did it do (what were its ratings)? You should start articles with facts like these, and then try to write about the plot. I'll be interested to see how this article develops. Soundguy99 17:38, 8 May 2005 (UTC)
