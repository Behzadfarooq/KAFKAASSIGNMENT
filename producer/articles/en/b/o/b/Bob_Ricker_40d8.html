Bob Ricker is Executive Director of the American Hunters and Shooters Association.

An experienced attorney and former top lobbyist for the firearm industry, Ricker spent over twenty years of his career representing gun makers and leading gun rights organizations before the United States Congress, state legislatures and state and federal courts.

[edit] Early career

An honors graduate of the George Mason University School of Law, Ricker’s career began in 1977 as a trial lawyer for a Northern Virginia law firm. In 1981, he took a paid position as an Assistant General Counsel for the National Rifle Association representing the NRA’s Institute for Legislative Action and NRA’s Political Victory Fund. In 1983, Ricker left Washington and moved to the west coast where he organized and developed the California Wildlife Federation’s legislative advocacy program. That same year, he led the legislative effort in California to pass the nations first firearm industry product liability immunity statute.

In 1985, Ricker was named Special Assistant to the Republican Leader of the California State Assembly. In 1991, he formed his own Sacramento based lobbying firm Ricker & Associates where he represented, among other clients, the nation’s leading firearm industry trade association the American Shooting Sports Counsel, Inc. (ASSC). In 1997, he returned to the nation’s capitol as the ASSC’s Director of Governmental Affairs and later as Executive Director.

[edit] Controversy

In 1997, Ricker angered his former employer, the National Rifle Association when he negotiated an agreement between the Clinton administration and major gun manufacturers to develop the nation’s first voluntary child safety lock program. Following the tragic shootings at Columbine High School in 1999, Mr. Ricker again angered the NRA when he led a delegation of industry executives to a White House conference on youth violence where gun manufacturers agreed to support key portions of President Clinton’s strategy involving guns.

In high profile litigation brought by several major U.S. cities, Ricker gained national prominence when he went on record against the very industry he used to represent. In an affidavit filed in California, and later in testimony before a federal court in Brooklyn, New York, Ricker, pointed to what he said were "irresponsible" and "negligent" behavior on the part of gun manufacturers, distributors and dealers.

[edit] Television appearances and publications

Ricker has been quoted in such national publications as The New York Times, The Wall Street Journal, The Washington Post, The Los Angeles Times, USA Today, Newsweek and Time. He has appeared on national news shows such as ABC’s Nightline, NBC’s Dateline, CNN’s Burden of Proof, The Newshour with Jim Lehrer and the Wall Street Journal Report. In 2003, he was profiled on the CBS news magazine 60 Minutes and on public television’s Now, with Bill Moyers. Ricker's political opinion articles have appeared in the San Francisco Chronicle, The Los Angeles Times, the Seattle Post Dispatch, and other newspapers.

[edit] Current work

Ricker manages his own Washington DC based legislative advocacy and policy consulting firm, Ricker & Associates.
