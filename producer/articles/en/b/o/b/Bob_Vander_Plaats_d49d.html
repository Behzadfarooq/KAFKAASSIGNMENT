Robert L. Vander Plaats (born April 12, 1963), an American politician. A former high school principal, human services organization director, and business consultant from Sioux City, Vander Plaats abandoned his own bid for the 2006 Iowa GOP gubernatorial nomination and agreed to become rival Congressman Jim Nussle's running mate in the November 2006 Iowa gubernatorial election. Calls by GOP party higher-ups for Vander Plaats to get out of the race are reportedly due to Vander Plaats raising only $459,000 compared to Nussle's $2,500,000.[1] Despite such calls, President Bush spoke at on April 11, 2006 at 5:05 p. m. at Hy- Vee Hall where, in his remarks, he referred to Robert Vander Plaats, candidate for Lieutenant Governor of Iowa and his wife, Darla.[2]

Vander Plaats ran earlier for the GOP gubernatorial nomination in 2002 and lost to Doug Gross.[3]
