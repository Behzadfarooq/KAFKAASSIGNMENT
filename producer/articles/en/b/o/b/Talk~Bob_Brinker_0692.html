Bob Brinker is the host of the ABC radio program Moneytalk, which has been on the air since 1986. He previously had a program on local radio in the New York area going back to 1982 and possibly earlier. He provides financial advice in a newsletter called Marketimer. There is a discussion forum to talk with other Moneytalk listeners here http://investment.suite101.com/discussion.cfm/7 and http://investment.suite101.com/discussion.cfm/8

Brinker is known for several major stock market timing recommendations. In January of 2000, near the all time high of the Dow Jones Industrial Average, Brinker suggested investors dramatically decrease their stock market exposure by going to 60% cash, and to 65% cash in August of that year. In the spring of 2003, Brinker recommended to his listeners and readers that it was time to get back into the stock market with the remainder of their money. This was within a few percent of the post-bubble lows for the overall market, although the NASDAQ 100 was up considerably. Unfortunately for Brinker's Marketimer subscribers he recommended investing 20-50% of the cash reserves into the NASDAQ 100 shares when they wee around $80 and then they fell to around $20. An aggressive investor could have put 1/3rd of the portfolio in the QQQQ if following the 50% advice.
