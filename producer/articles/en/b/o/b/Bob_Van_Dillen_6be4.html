Bob Van Dillen

Bob Van Dillen is a morning meteorologist for CNN Headline News. Based in CNN's world headquarters in Atlanta, Van Dillen joined the network in September 2002.

[edit] Life

Before joining CNN, Van Dillen was with the NBC affiliate WCNC in Charlotte, North Carolina, where he was a weekend meteorologist. Before this position, he was the morning meteorologist with CBS affiliate WTVH in Syracuse, New York, where he had also served as the weekend meteorologist. Previously, Van Dillen worked for ABC affiliate WUTR in Utica, New York, where he served as chief meteorologist. He began his career in Long Island, New York, as a forecaster for the Metro Weather Service. Van Dillen was awarded the American Meteorological Society (AMS) Seal of Approval in March 1997. He also is a full member of the AMS. Van Dillen earned a bachelor of arts degree in meteorology from Millersville University of Pennsylvania. You can see him every morning on Robin and Company every morning on CNN Headline News. He appears with anchor Robin Meade, sports anchor Will Selva, and entertainment anchor Adrianna Costa.
