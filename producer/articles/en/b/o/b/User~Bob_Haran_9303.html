BOB HARAN, Conservative Political Activist, volunteer Political Consultant.

Bio- Born in Long Branch, New Jersey, USA, on November 13, 1951. The eldest of three children. Bob Haran was raised in Brooklyn and Staten Island, New York. He was educated in New York City public schools and attended Erasmus Hall High School in Brooklyn. As a youth he was active in many organizations including; Boy Scouts, Flatbush Boys Club, Little League and ran the 880 yard run on his junior high track team. He was also a Cadet First Lieutenant with the New York Wing of the Civil Air Patrol, which is the official auxiliary of the United States Air Force. His first involvement in politics was passing out literature for John F. Kennedy during the 1960 presidential campaign at local subway exits in Brooklyn.

Twelve days after his seventeenth birthday, on November 25, 1968, during the height of the Vietnam War, Bob Haran enlisted in the United States Marine Corps. He completed boot camp at Parris Island, South Carolina and served in combat in Vietnam as an infantry squad leader with the First Marine Division. Mr. Haran was honorably discharged from the Marine Corps at Camp Pendelton, California, shortly after turning 20 years of age in 1971 and he is the holder of; The Navy and Marine Corps Combat Action Medal, National Defense Service Medal, Vietnam Service Medal with Two Bronze Stars, Vietnam Campaign Medal, and the Marine Corps Rifle Expert Badge.

After completing military service, Bob Haran attended Brooklyn College of the City University of New York as a pre-Law student with a major in Political Science. As a student he was a Legal Intern with the college Lay Advocate Center and Legislative Chairman with the New York State Concerned Veterans Association and was a member of Alpha Phi Omega. He also worked as a part time Assistant Pollster with Lou Harris and Associates, NYC, while a student at Brooklyn College.

Mr. Haran has worked as a Veterans Counselor at Staten Island Community College and as a licensed Real Estate and Insurance Agent. In 1977 he graduated from the New York City Police Academy as a Police Administrative Aid and worked for NYPD assisting crime victims. He was the Marketing Manager for Tridon Supply, which was a division of, The Ogden Allied Corporation. He has also worked as a paid political campaign consultant.

His political experience includes work as a volunteer for; Nixon for President, in 1972; Campaign Manager, Ken Grossberger for Congress, in New York in 1976; paid Campaign Consultant, Ed Towns for Congress, in New York in 1992; Full Time Campaign Volunteer, John Shadegg for Congress, in Arizona in 1994; Republican Legislative District Chairman in Arizona, 1994-99; Appointed by Glendale, Arizona, City Council as Housing Commissioner for City of Glendale, 1995-99; Full Time Volunteer, Bob Dole for President, 1996; Campaign Manager, Ray Barnes for Arizona House of Representatives in 2002; Legislative Assistant, Representative Ray Barnes, Arizona House of Representatives; Volunteer, Protect Arizona Now/Yes on Prop. 200 Citizen Initiative, 2003-4; Advisor, John Adkins for Phoenix City Council, 2003; Advisor, Ray Barnes for Arizona House, 2004; Volunteer, Arizona Family Project, 2004. Bob Haran is a 1994 graduate of, The Republican National Committee, Campaign School.

In 2000, Bob Haran was the Republican Nominee for the Arizona House of Representatives. He was endorsed by; Congressman John Shadegg, Congressman Bob Stump, County Attorney Rick Romley, Trent Franks, Sydney Hay, Arizona Right to Life, Arizona Consumers Council, The National Rifle Association with an "A" rating, Gun owners of America with a 100% rating, and The Fraternal Order of Police.

Bob Haran is currently a resident of Phoenix, Arizona and has one daughter, Ann Marie Haran, a Special Education Teacher in New York City.
