Bobbin Head is a locality in Sydney, in the state of New South Wales, Australia.

Bobbin Head is a scenic area that is a "near-urban" part of Ku-ring-gai Chase National Park. Bobbin Head is easily reached by taking Bobbin Head Road through North Turramurra, New South Wales or Kuringai Chase Road from near Hornsby, New South Wales.

As Bobbin Head is part of a national park, an entry fee is payable to the National Parks and Wildlife Service. Recently (Bobbin Head Road entrance, August 2006) the fee was $11 Australian per private car, and $4.40 Australian per passenger arriving by bus. The road down into the marina is also a popular route for cyclists, for whom entry is free.

Facilities at Bobbin Head include a marina, picnic areas with gas (coin operated) barbeques, a small store / takeaway food shop at the marina, and a lunch-time restaurant in what used to be the Bobbin Head Inn, which also contains a National Parks information center. The area also contains many fire trails and a mangrove boardwalk. Aboriginal engravings can be observed along some of the hiking trails.
