Bob Weltlich is a former American college basketball coach. Weltlich coached 22 seasons with a career record of 300-335 and three trips to the NCAA tournament. He was head coach at South Alabama, Florida International, Texas, and Mississippi[1] and one of only 19 coaches to lead three different teams to the NCAA Tournament.

[edit] Coaching career

[edit] Indiana

Weltlich got his degree in education from Ohio State in 1967 and was set to teach. But he met Army coach Bob Knight in Orrville, Ohio. Knight hired him as an assistant at Army, then took him to Indiana, where in 1976 he helped coach a 32-0 team to the NCAA title.[1]

[edit] Mississippi

He left Indiana to become the head coach at Mississippi.[1] Bob Weltlich manned the Rebel sidelines for six years (1977-1982) and directed Ole Miss to an SEC Tournament title and the programâ€™s postseason debut in 1981.[2]

[edit] Texas

Weltlich served as the Longhorns head coach from 1982 to 1988 with a 77-98 record. He was released after the 1988 season.[3]

[edit] Florida International

Weltlich served as coach of Florida International Golden Panthers from 1990 to 1995.[4]

Under Weltlich, the Panthers made their lone appearance to the NCAA Tournament in 1995, losing to Jim Harrick's UCLA Bruins 92-56 in the field of 64.[5]

[edit] South Alabama

The 17-year coaching veteran was named the interim coach at South Alabama on October 27, 1997 following Bill Musselman's sudden resignation on October 7, 1997.[6] Weltlich coached the Jags from 1997 to 2002 and compiled a record of 81-65 and three 20-win seasons.[2]

He resigned from South Alabama after the 2002 season, but he and his family remained in Fairhope, Alabama, where he is a middle-school teacher, trying to complete the 10 years of service he needs to qualify for retirement from the state of Alabama.[1]
