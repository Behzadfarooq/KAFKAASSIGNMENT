Boyd H. Anderson High School (located in Lauderdale Lakes, Florida) is a high school in Broward County, Florida. Boyd H. Anderson serves Lauderdale Lakes, and parts of Tamarac, Oakland Park, Fort Lauderdale and Lauderhill.

Boyd Anderson High School is the first public school in Broward County to contain an accreditted International Baccalaureate diploma program as a magnet program.

Their mascot is the cobra. They are known for their track and field program with state championships in Track and Field (1995,1997,2000), and three state championships in Cross Country (1992, 1996, 1998), and ranked #2 nationally in 1998 in Track and Field, and 18th in Cross Country, all while under former coach David Bowden.
