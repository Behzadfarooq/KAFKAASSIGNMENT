Boyd County, New South Wales is one of the 141 Cadastral divisions of New South Wales. It contains the locality of Coleambally. The Murrumbidgee River is the northern boundary.

Boyd County was named in honour of Benjamin Boyd Entrepreneur (1796-1851).[1]

The Carathool Bridge over the Murrumbidgee between Boyd and Sturt
