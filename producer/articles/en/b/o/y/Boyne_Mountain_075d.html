Boyne Mountain

Boyne Mountain is a year round resort in Northern Michigan (near Boyne City ) and was developed around the skiing industry of the region by Everett Kircher. Boyne Mountain now includes golf, tubing, cross country trails, Solace Spa, shopping, fine dining, and has an indoor waterpark, Avalanche Bay, and the recently completed Grand Mountain Lodge. Boyne Mountain is owned and operated by Boyne USA, a family owned company that operates many ski resorts, including Big Sky (Montana), and Crystal Mountain (Washington), as well as the closer Boyne Highlands, only a few miles north of Boyne Mountain. For live camera viewing of the mountain go to the Boyne Mountain website and see amaxing shots of the superpipe, views from the infamous Eagle's Nest restaurant at the mountain's summit, or inside of the waterpark.

[edit] Annual Mountain Events and Activities

  • Carnival Weekend, celebrated around St. Patrick's Day in honour of the coming Spring.
  • Crazy Days, in which people traditionally dressed in costumes and celebrate an all out drink-fest at the Halfway house on the side of Victor, one of the mountian's numerous ski trails.
  • Slush Cup, skiers attempt to cross a newly made pond on the side of the ski hill in honor of the warmer spring skiing weather.
  • Various Golf academies and tournaments.

[edit] Alpine Racing

  • Boyne Alpine Race Team
  • NASTAR and Boyne Fliers
  • Northern Michigan Ski Academy
  • Wednesday Night Race League
  • Boyne Mountian also hosts numerous middle school and high school races, USSA events, and other race events that circulate the region.
