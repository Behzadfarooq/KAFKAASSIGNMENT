Boy in the Box is the 14th episdoe of the American crime drama Cold Case. It originally aired as Episode 14 of Season 1 on February 15, 2004

[edit] Summary

Spoiler warning: Plot and/or ending details follow.

In April 1958 the body of a young boy is found wrapped inside a box.

In the present, Lt. Stillman shows detectives Rush and Valens a suitcase has several things related to the “Boy in the Box” murder that happened in 1958. Lilly notices a picture of a group of boys which has a message in the box. “His name was Arnold”.

Further details of the case are revealed. The boy was never identified and the cause of death is undetermined. The picture reveals another clue. A bus on the picture has “Fernwood Home for Orphans” written on it

Scotty and Lilly go visit the nuns who were responsible for Fernwood at the time, sisters Vivian and Grace. They talk to Sister Vivian and learn that Sister Grace died recently. Sister Vivian doesn't remember Arnold. The detectives get permission to look at the records from Fernwood

The detectives learn that there was a boy named Arnold Curville in Fernwood. Furthermore he had a sister and he was adopted two days before he was found in the box. Detectives Vera and Jeffries are assigned to find the couple while Lilly and Scotty visit Arnold's sister, Gretchen.

Gretchen looks at the picture of the boy in the box and says that can't be her brother, because Arnold had blonde curls; the boy in the box had his head shaved. She says that the picture of the group of boys is the “Science Club”. She also says Arnold can't be the murdered boy because he was adopted in 1958. She remembers that before being adopted, two other families had taken him and brought him back to Fernwood, because he acted up a lot. They ask her for a DNA sample to compare to the boy's, and she agrees.

Vera and Jeffries go visit Roger one of the boys from the photo.
