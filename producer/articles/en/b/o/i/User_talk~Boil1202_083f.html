[edit] Language

I know you're interested in raising your English to the next level. Here are a few pointers from your earlier messages on Plastictv's page.

  • But till now it haven't come true.

More natural would be: "So far it hasn't come true." Also you can't say "haven't" because it's a singular noun (it).

  • Where are you come from?

This is not accurate English. You must say, "Where do you come from?" OR "Where are you from?" Both are perfect. Try not to directly translate (e.g. 你是那裡來的--Where are you come from...we must say "Where do you come from" because "do" is a auxiliary verb of "come", using a different structure without "be/is" like in Chinese)

  • Where is your hometown?

Hometown is very overused by Chinese-native-speakers learning English. Try synonyms.

  • I will push my wife to write more about Gutian County.

"Push my wife", generally, would sound very aggressive here. "Encourage" might be good, among others.

  • I and my wife will treat you to a meal.

This is correct English, but not very colloquial. Instead of literal translation (請你吃飯), perhaps you can adopt a different expression altogether: "My wife and I will take you out to eat"...but I recognize one difficulty is that it's impossible in this circumstance to specify dinner, lunch, etc. Also, it's best to put "me/I" last: "My wife and I"

  • Could you help me to let the article more friendly?

讓 and 叫 are in certain contexts translated as "make": "make the article more friendly" (instead of "let", which is accurate in other contexts). 好複雜!

  • I have seen the newest article "PBB" in Wikipedia.

You may or may not have done this here, but be careful when using "have" for past tense...you should probably say "I saw" here. And don't let yourself say "I have been home" (I am home).
