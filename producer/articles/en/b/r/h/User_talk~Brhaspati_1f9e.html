[edit] Indian Railways

Hi - I think neutral zones and dead zones are the same thing. Official IR term is neutral zone, where the catenary wire is unelectrified. There are a few of those between Diva and Kalyan as well. Binand 15:59, 2 Apr 2005 (UTC)

Loco changes and Neutral Sections are unrelated (except in the old Mumbai when WCM locos used to operate; but these have been retired now). Loco changes happen at stations where a trip shed (different from a loco shed) exists. Igatpuri has one, Pune has another - hence CR's loco changes occur at these places. On WR, the next trip shed is at either Valsad or Vadodara (I don't remember which), hence loco change happens there. The WCAM/WCAG series locos do not drift away too far from Mumbai as though they can run elsewhere on AC regions, they are specifically needed due to their DC-ness in Mumbai region. On the other hand, I do think some trains go with WCAM locos upto Manmad (which also has a trip shed) and it would be interesting to see how they cover Igatpuri (like the trains from CST to Nanded or Nizamabad) - Binand 17:20, 2 Apr 2005 (UTC)
Oh ok, I get what you mean, and I think I was wrong. Check http://irfca.org/faq/faq-elec.html#dead which has more info on this. - Binand 18:20, 2 Apr 2005 (UTC)

The ticket image should probably be in the section on ticketing - Binand 23:18, 4 Apr 2005 (UTC)

[edit] India flag

I noticed the India flag you uploaded recently. I think its a little too yellow. It may be that my monitor is at fault. Could you verify the colours?  =Nichalp (talk • contribs)= 18:37, Apr 19, 2005 (UTC)

Here's the source. The "Colours of the flag" section mentions "255-204-0" for saffron (hex equivalent FFCC00) and "51-153-51" for green (hex 339933). The reasoning apparently was that the Indian flag saffron is supposed to be between the flag of Brunei and flag of Ireland in hue. I can't verify this myself, but FOTW in general is reputable. A zoomed-in version of the Ashoka chakra is provided at the bottom of the page. If you want the code I used to make this image, it's at User:Brhaspati/indflag. You're welcome to modify it. -- Brhaspati\talk/contribs 18:49, 2005 Apr 19 (UTC)
