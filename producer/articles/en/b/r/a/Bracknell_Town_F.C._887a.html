Bracknell Town Football Club are a football club based in Bracknell, Berkshire, England. The club was formed in 1896. The team are currently in the Southern League Division One South and West.

[edit] History

The club was formed in 1896 as Old Bracknell Wanderers and played local league football until 1959 when, to coincide with Bracknell becoming a New Town, the club joined the Great Western League. In 1963 they switched to the Surrey Senior League where they were champions in 1969-70, after which they changed leagues once again, this time to the Spartan League, which soon merged with the Metropolitan London League to become the London Spartan League.

In the early 1980s the club unsuccessfully applied to the Athenian League, but, a year after winning the Spartan League in 1982-83, they were able to step up to the Isthmian League. In 1985-86 the Robins were promoted to Division One, and nearly followed this with promotion to the Premier Division, but this success was not to last and the club went on to spend a number of years in the league's bottom division, Division Three. However, a successful 2001-02 saw them placed in Division One South when the league was reduced in size due to the formation of Conference South. In 2004 restructuring of the English football league system saw the Robins switched to the Southern League.

[edit] Club records

  • Best league performance: 3rd in Isthmian League Division One, 1986-87
  • Best FA Cup performance: 1st round proper, 2000-01
  • Best FA Trophy performance: 1st round proper, 2002-03, 2003-04, 2004-05
  • Best FA Vase performance: 5th round, 1975-76, 1982-83
