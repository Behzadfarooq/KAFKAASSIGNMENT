[edit] a heads-up on the List of Guantanamo Bay detainees

Greetings,

Since you voted to keep the article List of Guantanamo Bay detainees I thought I would give you a "heads-up". A copyright violation was filed against the article, on October 11th. It was filed by someone who had voted to delete the article on October 5th.

I believe that the copyright violation is entirely bogus. I believe it is bogus because, as explained in Feist Publications v. Rural Telephone Service, lists of facts, like lists of names, cannot be copyright. This Feist v. Rural case went all the way to the US Supreme Court, which made the possibly counter-intuitive ruling that the amount of effort someone put in to compiling a list plays no role in determining whether that list is eligible for copyright protection.

Even if alphabetic lists of names could be copyright, I believe the wikipedia list would not be violating copyright since the list was compiled from various sources.

Yes, I have considered that this user invoked a bogus copyright violation to achieve a result that failed in the {AfD}. Yes, I asked them to terminate the copyright violation process, in light of Feist v Rural. They declined. The backlog in the administrators dealing with copyright violations seems to be on the order of a month long.

Anyhow, I wanted the people who had shown interest in the article to not freak out, or feel betrayed, by seeing the copyright violation tag. -- Geo Swan 11:23, 19 October 2005 (UTC)

[edit] Jon Bithrey

Hi Brandon

I am not meaning to be difficult but I can't see how the article on Jon Bithrey should be deleted. This person is clearly notable enough to be on the site.

You do not live on the island of Jersey, a state governed under the British crown but not part of the UK or the EU. If you did you may well have heard about Jon Bithrey - he is a presenter who regularly appears on BBC Radio Jersey, the most listened to station on the island.

To give you an example - when members of the Royal Family including the Queen and the Duke of Edinburgh recently attended celebrations in commemoration of VE Day, Jon Bithrey was the presenter who presented the BBC Radio coverage of this visit.

You may not deem this important but can I make the point that there are many people who are profiled on this site that I too do not consider important or notable. I do not pointlessly go around deleting their profiles.

For example I bet there are some Iowa talk radio presenters who people might like to read about - but would not have been heard of by people who work on Jersey.

Thanks

B B.

FYI, if you will go back and look at the template I used, it was inappropriate for you to remove the speedy delete tag on an article that you yourself had created. Correct procedure would have been to note your objection on the article's talk page. The admin who reviewed the proposed delete would have taken your concerns into account.
