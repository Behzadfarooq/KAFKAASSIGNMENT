Brandeis

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Family name Brandeis is originated from Brandýs, Bohemia, or Brandys/Brandeis in Silesia or Brandis near Leipzig or Brandis near Berlin. Variations are Brandes, Brandys, Brandis, Brandiss, Barondes.

  • Louis Brandeis, Supreme Court Justice
  • Brandeis University
  • Louis D. Brandeis School of Law (University of Louisville).
  • the German name for the former city Brandýs nad Labem, now part of the Czech city Brandýs nad Labem-Stará Boleslav
  • Bezalel Brandeis of Prague
  • Bezalel Ben Mose Ha-Levi Brandeis (1727-1760), Bohemian rabbi and author
  • Irma Brandeis
  • Joseph Brandeis, Polish-born American college professor
  • Brandeis Award
  • Brandeis International Business School
  • Brandeis (Brokers) Ltd, a broker on the London Metal Exchange.
  • Friedl Dicker-Brandeis, Austrian-Jewish artist and Holocaust victim
  • Brandeis (department store), Omaha, Nebraska based department store chain, acquired by Younkers
