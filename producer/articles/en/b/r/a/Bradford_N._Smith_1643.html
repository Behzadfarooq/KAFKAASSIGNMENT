Bradford N. Smith (born May 12, 1991) is a character actor, and creator of his own good luck tokens.

[edit] Early life

Bradford was born in Santa Maria, California. At the age of 7, he was cast opposite Terry Bradshaw in a comercial for a Texas-based home insurance company ("The Associates"). Also at the age of 7, Bradford wrote and illustrated a children's book called "My Lucky Genes". After this brief acting career, he took a break from acting until a few years ago at the age of 14. At this same time, Bradford came up with good luck tokens which he not only spread throughout the country during some of his trips, but also gave to some of the most well known actors. It is these tokens that got him on The Today Show and The Tonight Show, among others.

[edit] Recent activity

Currently, Bradford acts from time to time, but is trying to stay more focused on his school work, and his goal to become a cartoonist.

[edit] Trivia

  • In his younger years, Bradford also had leading roles in his town's "Night Before Christmas" (which included the Thompson sisters Taylor and Tori) and "Cinderella" plays.
  • Some celebrities with Bradford's tokens include: Sherwood Schwartz, Adam West, Chevy Chase, Steve Martin, Sid Caesar, Russell Johnson, Carrot Top, Bob Newhart, Charlton Heston, and John Melendez.
  • He is a descendant of John Stafford Smith (1750-1836), the composer of "The Star-Spangled Banner".
  • Has dated Emily Osment and Lorraine Nicholson.
  • Good friends with Lil' JJ, Mitchel Musso, Danielle Riley Keough, Sherwood Schwartz, and Ray Harryhausen.
  • He's a huge fan of Get Smart, and hopes to get a part in the film currently in production.
  • In 2006, Bradford and his father were in the audience for Deal or No Deal, and could be seen cheering during various crowd shots.
  • Bradford also participated in the February 2nd, 2007 1 VS 100 kids episode, unfortunately he lost. He was #23.
  • A big fan of classic films, Bradford has become friends with such stars as Bruce Bennett, Phyllis Coates, and Stella Stevens.
