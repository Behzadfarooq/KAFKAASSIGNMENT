Brad St. Louis

Brad Allen St. Louis (born August 19, 1976) is a National Football League long snapper/tight end for the Cincinnati Bengals. He went to Southwest Missouri State University. He was drafted by the Bengals in the 7th round (210th overall) in the 2000 NFL Draft. St. Louis garnered some infamy on December 24th, 2006 when a poor snap of his caused a missed extra point within the last minute of the fourth quarter against the Denver Broncos, which cost the Bengals the game. This helped lead to their eventual failure to get into the 2006 playoffs. In the 2005 AFC wild card game against the Steelers he had a high snap on a field goal attempt. On the ensuing possesion Jerome Bettis ran for a touchdown to give the Steelers a 21-17 lead. The Steelers went on to win that game.

[edit] High School Years

Brad St.Louis attended Belton High School in Belton, Missouri. St.Louis lettered in football, wrestling, tennis, and track. In football, as a tight end, he caught 42 passes for 508 yards(12.10 yards per rec. avg.) over two years, and was named his team's Lineman of the Year as a junior. In wrestling, he was the Missouri State Champion as a senior. Brad St.Louis graduated from Belton High School in 1995.
