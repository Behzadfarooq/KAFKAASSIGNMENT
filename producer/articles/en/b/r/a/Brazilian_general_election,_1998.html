Brazilian general election, 1998

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

You have new messages (last change).

The Brazilian general elections of 1998 resulted in the re-election of Fernando Henrique Cardoso. The amendment that allowed a president to serve two consecutive terms had been passed by Congress the previous year. Cardoso won the election in the first round, after winning more than 50% of the votes.

That year elections for governors of the 26 states and the federal district were also held, as were races for senators and deputies.
