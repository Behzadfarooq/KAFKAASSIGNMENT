Yasmin, nicknamed Pretty Princess, is a character in the Bratz line of fashion dolls. As one of the first four characters in the Bratz lineup, she has appeared in many 9.5" editions, as well as in Bratz Babyz, Lil Bratz, and Bratz Kidz forms. She mostly has brown hair and brown eyes (except in Bratz TV Series and video games, where she appears with blue eyes), and she has a beauty spot under her left eye. She mostly appears in tan skin tone, but in Midnight Dance she appears in light skin tone. In Pretty n' Punk she appears in darker tan skin tone, and in Spring Break, she features in lighter tan skin tones.Might be of Mexican heritage.

Jade, nicknamed Kool Kat, is a character in the Bratz line of fashion dolls. As one of the first four characters in the Bratz lineup, she has appeared in many 9.5" editions, as well as in Bratz Babyz, Lil Bratz, and Bratz Kidz forms. She regularly features black hair and light skin, but her eyes have varied in color from line to line. In a few editions, Jade is given the pale skin tone, and in Sun Kissed Summer and Bratz Forever Diamondz, she appears with a tan. It is suggested this character may be intended to be of Asian heritage; her eye prints are often less rounded than most other characters'. The Style It collection's Jade caused some controversy; her alternate shirt features a Chinese take-out box with a Japanese flag on it and the words "Thank You" in a stroked font, suggesting cultural ignorance on the designer's part. Sasha, nicknamed Bunny Boo, is a character in the Bratz line of fashion dolls. As one of the first four characters in the Bratz lineup, she has appeared in many 9.5" editions, as well as in Bratz Babyz, Lil Bratz, Itsy Bitsy Bratz, and Bratz Kidz form. In most lines, her hair is deep brown, but she has also appeared with black, dark blonde, silver, and orange dyed-hair in others. She has the second-darkest Bratz complexion to date; however, Genie Magic Sasha is a shade lighter than other editions. Sasha is the older sister to one of the newest Bratz Babyz, Zama.
