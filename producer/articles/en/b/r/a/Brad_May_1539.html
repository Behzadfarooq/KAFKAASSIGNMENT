Brad May (Born: November 29, 1971 in Toronto, Ontario, Canada) is a hockey left winger currently playing for the NHL's Anaheim Ducks. Brad was drafted by the Buffalo Sabres in the first round, 14th overall in the 1990 NHL Entry Draft.

May signed with the Avalanche as an unrestricted free agent on August 20, 2005, for two years.

May is well known for scoring the series clinching goal in the 1993 Adams Division semifinals in Game 4 against Andy Moog and the Boston Bruins. Sabres broadcaster Rick Jeanneret in the moment spontaniously delivered the now famous May Day call repating "MAY DAY! MAY DAY! MAY DAY! MAY DAY! MAY DAY!"[citation needed]

He was traded on February 27, 2007 to the Anaheim Ducks by the Colorado Avalanche for goaltender Michael Wall.
