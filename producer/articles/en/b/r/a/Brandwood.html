Brandwood is a ward within the formal district of Selly Oak, Birmingham, England. It previously formed part of the Hall Green district and will continue to be part of the Hall Green constituency until the next general election, when it will move to Selly Oak.

The area is served by Druids Heath Library.

[edit] Population

The 2001 Population Census found that there were 24,530 people living in the ward with a population density of 4,167 people per km² compared with 3,649 people per km² for Birmingham. Brandwood is not an ethnically diverse community with ethnic minorities representing 12.6% (3,102) of the ward's population as opposed to 29.6% for Birmingham.

[edit] Politics

Brandwood is represented by three Conservative councillors at Birmingham City Council; Ken Hardeman, Mark Hill and Neville Summerfield.

The ward was represented by Labour for a long period in the early 1990s /early 2000s, with Steve McCabe (now the Member of Parliament for Hall Green Constituency), Roy Pinney, who served as Chair of and later Cabinet Member for Education, in addition to three female councillors, the last of these being Catherine Grundy, who lost her seat in 2004 and was subsequently elected to the Kingstanding ward in 2006.

Brandwood has adopted a Ward Support Officer with the current holder of the title being Wyn Jones.

[edit] Transport

The main road through the area is the A435 Alcester Road. The Stratford-upon-Avon Canal also passes through the area.
