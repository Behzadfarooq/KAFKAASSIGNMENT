[edit] Raj Sharda

Sharda is a 40-year old lawyer, who served both as a City Councillor in Oakville and Regional Councillor for the Region of Halton. He has practiced law in Brampton since 1997. He is currently a volunteer legal advisor to the Brampton Small Business Enterprise Centre and has previously served as President of the Halton Multicultural Council, as a member of the Allocations Cabinet for the Oakville United Way, and on the Board of Directors for the Indo-Canada Chamber of Commerce and the Advocacy Centre for the Elderly.

[edit] Verification of Sharda's councillorship

Kim Galione, the elections manager for the Town of Oakville has replied to me.

In response to your email Raj Sharda was appointed to Council in June 2000 to replace an incumbent that resigned. The position was for Ward 2 Town and Regional Council.
I have copied a section of the Minutes of Council appointing Rahneesh Sharda:
That Council appoint Rajneesh Sharda to complete the unexpired term of Councillor Kathy Graham ending November 30, 2000, and that the necessary by-law providing for this appointment as member of Council for Ward 2, and further appointing such person to sit as the successor member on the Council for the Regional Municipality of Halton, be approved.
CARRIED
Unanimously
(Councillor Liz Behrens declared a conflict of interest and did not vote.)

[edit] Candidate platforms and their issues of campaign

  • Susan Fennell - Fair and open City Government [1]
  • Sunny Ghandhi - Traffic gridlock [2]
  • Raj Sharda - Infrastructure shortfall and two-way communication with the Mayor’s Office [3]
  • Elaine Moore - To develop a strong two-way communication [4]
  • Stephanie Beaumier - Safe streets, traffic congestion, tax increases, poor air quality, seniors’ and youth programs, facilities and funding
  • Larry Lee - City growth vs infrastructure, neighbourhood play areas, noise pollution, quality of life and health [5]
  • Jeffrey Schrik - Being a community of communities and increasing government transparency [6]
  • Jagtar Shergill - Today's council have disappointed in too many areas. Changing demographics not appreciated by present council[7]
  • David Esho - Municipal officers to be elected for a maximum of three consecutive terms. Political process in Brampton to be made inclusive. [8]
  • Curtis Grant - Improving access to government, reducing traffic congestion and enhancing our infrastructure [9]
  • Chuck Jeffrey - New leadership and fair share of regional services [10]
  • Derrik Coke - Lack of leadership
  • Navdeep Gill - Property taxes, youth needs, access to council, reflect ethnic diversity, traffic, environment, quality of representation [11]
  • Mathew Njenga - The Environment and Health, Urban Sprawl, Crime, Business Development, Fair Representation[12]
