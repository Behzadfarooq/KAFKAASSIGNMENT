Branko Radivojevič (born November 24, 1980 in Piešťany, Czechoslovakia) is a Slovakian ice hockey forward of ethnic Serb origin.

[edit] Career

Drafted by the Colorado Avalanche in 1999, Radivojevic would sign with the Phoenix Coyotes on June 19, 2001 and play parts of the next three seasons there. Part of a mid-season trade in 2003-04, Radivojevic was sent to the Philadelphia Flyers along with Sean Burke and the rights to Ben Eager for Mike Comrie. After 2005-06, the Flyers declined to tender him a qualifying offer making him an unrestricted free agent. He signed with the Minnesota Wild as an unrestricted free agent on July 6, 2006.

[edit] Awards

  • 2000-01: First All-Star Team (OHL)
  • 2000-01: Jim Mahon Memorial Trophy Top Scoring Right Winger (OHL)
  • 2002-03: Played in the Young Stars Game (NHL)
