"Brass In Pocket (I'm Special)" was a popular single by The Pretenders, written by Chrissie Hynde and James Honeyman-Scott and produced by Chris Thomas.

The band's third single, proved to be their breakthrough hit, reaching #1 on the UK Singles Chart for two weeks in January 1980 and peaking at #14 on the Billboard Hot 100 pop singles chart in the United States. The song takes its title from an expression Hynde heard from a member of a Yorkshire-based support band who was looking for his money.

Hynde was initially reluctant to have the song released, in an interview with The Observer newspaper in 2004 she recalled, "When we recorded the song I wasn't very happy with it and told my producer that he could release it over my dead body, but they eventually persuaded me. So I remember feeling a bit sheepish when it went to number 1."

The song has been covered by Nazareth, Suede (for the NME's Ruby Trax charity compilation)[1], Ashlee Simpson (during concert performances), Kelis, and Ted Leo (during WFMU's 2007 fundraising drive), among others.

In the 2003 film Lost in Translation, Scarlett Johansson's character sings the song to Bill Murray's character in a karaoke bar.

The song has a distinctive "call and response" bridge where Chrissie Hynde sings "I'm special (so special) I gotta have some of your attention give it to me".
