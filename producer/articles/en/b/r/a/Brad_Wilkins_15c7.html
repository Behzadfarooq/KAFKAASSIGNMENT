Brad Wilkins (born 1976) is a American architect.

Brad Wilkins was born in Salt Lake City, Utah. He trained with Rocco Yim in Hong Kong and Skidmore, Owings and Merrill, and graduated in 2004 from the University of Pennsylvania's School of Design and in 2001 from the University of Utah's architecture program. He now works at Adrian Smith + Gordon Gill Architecture in Chicago.

While at Skidmore, Owings and Merrill in Chicago he worked on the Burj Dubai (soon to be world's tallest structure)[1] and the Chicago 2016 Olympic bid (he took part in the masterplanning of the venues and headed up the materplaning of the Olympic Stadium design). Also while at SOM, Brad was senior designer with Jeff Stafford on the Pearl River Tower under the design guidance of Gordon Gill[2].
