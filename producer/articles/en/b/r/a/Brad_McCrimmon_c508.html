Byron Brad McCrimmon (born 29 March 1959 in Dodsland, Saskatchewan) is a retired Canadian professional ice hockey defenseman from Plenty, Saskatchewan who played in the NHL for 18 years, between 1979 and 1997. He won the Stanley Cup in 1989 with the Calgary Flames. He and his wife Maureen, daughter Carlin and son Liam now live in Atlanta, Georgia. He is currently an assistant coach for the Atlanta Thrashers.

[edit] Playing career

McCrimmon was drafted 15th overall by the Boston Bruins in the 1979 NHL Entry Draft.
