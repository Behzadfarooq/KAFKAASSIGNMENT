Braylon Jamel Edwards (born February 21, 1983 in Detroit, Michigan) is a current American football wide receiver for the Cleveland Browns. His father, Stanley, played football at the University of Michigan (1977-81) and in the NFL with the Houston Oilers (1982-86) and Detroit Lions (1987).

[edit] College career

A graduate of Bishop Gallagher High School in Harper Woods, Michigan, Edwards followed his father's footsteps to Michigan, where he played from 2001-04. Edwards set Michigan season records in 2004 for receptions (97) and yards (1,330), and career records for receptions (252), yards (3,541), and touchdowns (39); the career touchdown mark is also a Big Ten Conference record. Edwards also set the UM record for the most games of 100 or more receiving yards with 17. That year Edwards won the Fred Biletnikoff Award, given to the nation's top wide receiver, and was named the Big Ten Conference MVP.

Edwards is the only wide receiver in Big Ten history (and third in NCAA Division I-A) to gain 1,000 or more receiving yards in three consecutive years. At Michigan, Edwards wore jersey number 80 during his freshman and sophomore years, but "earned" the prestigious number 1 jersey during his junior and senior years. Edwards concluded his college career by recording three touchdown catches in the 2005 Rose Bowl against Texas, tying a record for that game.

[edit] NFL career

Edwards was selected third overall in the 2005 NFL Draft by Cleveland. As a Brown, Edwards followed a current trend among NFL receivers by wearing a number in the teens, 17. Edwards began his rookie season as Cleveland's third wide receiver - he would have started higher, but a hold-out caused him to miss the beginning of training camp. Early in the season Edwards revealed that he had a staph infection, and missed a few weeks as a result of it. He moved into the starting lineup by midseason. He amassed 512 receiving yards and three touchdowns before suffering a season ending knee injury. Edwards had surgery in the offseason, and while rehabilitating bonded with Kellen Winslow, Jr.. Both were determined to make a full and speedy recovery.

Edwards, like Winslow, had a successful rehabilitation that enabled him to be ready to play in the team's opening game in 2006. [1] Edwards became the top receiver for the Browns after an injury to Joe Jurevicius. Edwards also established a $500,000 scholarship endowment fund for college players who earn the distinction of wearing the University of Michigan's No.1 football jersey.
