Brazabrantes is a small town and municipality in central Goiás state, Brazil.

  • Population (2005 estimate): 3,046
  • Total area: 124.0 km²
  • Elevation: 761 meters
  • Became a municipality: 1958
  • IBGE statistical microregion: Anápolis Microregion
  • Distance to the state capital: 37 km.
  • Distance to regional center (Anápolis): 59 km.
  • Highway connections: state highway GO-70 north to Goanira and then east to Brazabrantes.
  • Postal code: 75440-000

Neighboring municipalities: Nova Veneza, Inhumas, Damolândia, and Goiânia

Brazabrantes is in the Meia Ponte River valley.

Demographic and Political Data

  • Population density in 2003: 23.53 inhab/km²
  • Population growth rate 1991-2000: 1.93.%

  • Eligible voters in 2004: 2,545
  • City government in 2005: mayor (Joseile Farias de Mendonça), vice-mayor (Alaor Francisco de Oliveira), and 09 councilpersons

[edit] Economy

The economy is based on cattle raising (12,460 head in 2003), poultry raising and agriculture.

  • Main agricultural products: small production of bananas, citrus fruits, tomatoes, manioc, rice, corn, soybeans, and beans; large production of sugarcane--1,390 hectares producing 80,000 tons.
  • Commercial units: 40
  • Financial institutions: none reporting in 2004
