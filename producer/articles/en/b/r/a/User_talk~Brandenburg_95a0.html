[edit] Unspecified source for Image:180x150 2006-LovHam-Spurkeland.JPG

Thanks for uploading Image:180x150 2006-LovHam-Spurkeland.JPG. I notice the file's description page currently doesn't specify who created the content, so the copyright status is unclear. If you have not created this file yourself, then there needs to be a justification explaining why we have the right to use it on Wikipedia (see copyright tagging below). If you did not create the file yourself, then you need to specify where it was found, i.e., in most cases link to the website where it was taken from, and the terms of use for content from that page.

If the file also doesn't have a copyright tag, then one should be added. If you created/took the picture, audio, or video then the {{GFDL-self}} tag can be used to release it under the GFDL. If you believe the media meets the criteria at Wikipedia:Fair use, use a tag such as {{fairusein|article name}} or one of the other tags listed at Wikipedia:Image copyright tags#Fair_use. See Wikipedia:Image copyright tags for the full list of copyright tags that you can use.

If you have uploaded other files, consider checking that you have specified their source and tagged them, too. You can find a list of files you have uploaded by following this link. Unsourced and untagged images may be deleted one week after they have been uploaded, as described on criteria for speedy deletion. If you have any questions please ask them at the Media copyright questions page. Thank you. Nv8200p talk 16:43, 27 September 2006 (UTC)

Hey - just saw your work on 2006/07 Speed Skating World Cup. I've been working on 2007 Speed Skating World Cup; I don't care where the information is, really, but it seems a bit dumb to have 2 articles on the same thing - so would you mind if one was redirected to the other? Sam Vimes | Address me 15:08, 13 November 2006 (UTC)

Hi Brandenburg, in addition to what is said above I'd like to inform you about that your 2006/07 Ski jumping World Cup has a twin brother at 2007 Ski Jumping World Cup, created by me and helped with by Sam Vimes as well. The best thing would be merging them together, but as the lay-outs are pretty much different I'd also like your opinion on that before making any changes. Cheers, SportsAddicted | discuss 13:50, 28 November 2006 (UTC)
Yes I also think your name is better because the events take part in both years. The reason that I only named 2007 was because 2007 Alpine Skiing World Cup was already created inclusive previous years. I think we should rename all existing World Cups that cover 2006 and 2007. But before we do that we should merge these two articles. I don't mind much which article to keep as the information inside is pretty much the same, but in a different lay-out. The only reason why we might be using the article created by me is that other (winter) sports have the same lay-out, but I don't mind using your lay-out for ski jumping. I will drop a note to Sam Vimes so he can read this comment on your talk page and see what his opinion is. If you want to comment on this too, please comment it here, so we have it all together. Cheers, SportsAddicted | discuss 21:22, 28 November 2006 (UTC)
Hey, I got the notice from SA. :) I think it would be better if all the "winter sports" cups looked sort of similar, and had the same name. I'd also think that including both years is clever, but personally I'd write 2006-07 with a - instead of a /, because that's what football, cricket, American sports and a lot of other things on Wiki use, so it'd be easier for people to find.
I think your tables can contain more information, so even though the article can get very long to scroll through, I think a table like this for results
Rank Name Nationality 1st (m) 2nd (m) Points Overall WC points
1 Arttu Lappi FIN 141.0 - 154.8 100
2 Simon Ammann SUI 136.5 - 145.7 80
3 Anders Jacobsen NOR 137.5 - 145.5 60
4 Veli-Matti Lindström FIN 132.0 - 135.6 50
5 Michael Neumayer GER 130.5 - 129.9 45
is the best to use. (Perhaps without the pink background, though?) However, I kind of like my total season tables, as they show the overall scores and how the athlete performs over the season. Sam Vimes | Address me 21:52, 28 November 2006 (UTC)
Reply to User_talk:Sam_Vimes#2006.2F07_Ski_jumping_World_Cup.
What I was thinking was that someone (preferably me, I suppose) goes on to convert everything to the same format. 2006-2007 Cross Country Skiing World Cup, 2007 Speed Skating World Cup, 2006 Alpine Skiing World Cup, etc. I think there's an advantage to everything looking the same.
As for who should be included on the aggregate tables: top 15 is a reasonable cut-off IMO. Should include most athletes who were in the TV screen and in the media, without getting too big. Possibly also include everyone who has won a race during the season - so something like
(example from biathlon)
15. Ivan Tcherezov 405
23. Roman Dostal 281
(assuming Dostal won a race, which he didn't.) Sam Vimes | Address me 12:01, 29 November 2006 (UTC)
Sounds good, In my opinion, going down with the tables of each World Cup meeting by naming the top 5 takes too much space, while it can be easily reduced by naming them aside on one row for each event. SportsAddicted | discuss 20:57, 29 November 2006 (UTC)
I see your point and I do agree that it might be better to include more information in a sport like ski jumping than is possible to include in the one line reports like in some other sports. It's hard to decide which one to choose, but the most important in my opinion is that we're not overdoing things by having two separate articles like the current situation. SportsAddicted | discuss 21:20, 29 November 2006 (UTC)

[edit] Template:Ski jumper infobox

Hey. Thanks for creating this template. I made some changes, as the template transclusions used to create whitespaces, and the black text wasn't very readable on a #007FFF background. Thanks, Prolog 14:59, 3 December 2006 (UTC)

The reason why it should be a lighter, more neutral color is usability. It might look good to you, but for example, on my screen the text is barely readable.
