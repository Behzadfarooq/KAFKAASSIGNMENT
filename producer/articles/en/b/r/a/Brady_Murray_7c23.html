Brady Murray

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Brady Murray (born on August 17, 1984 in Brandon, Manitoba, but raised in Faribault, Minnesota) is an American ice hockey player.

He represented the U.S. at the 2004 World Juniors, where he helped the States win gold. He also played hockey at the University of North Dakota, and after 2 successful years there he signed with the Rapperswil-Jona Lakers of the Nationalliga A in Switzerland. He was born in Brandon, Manitoba but holds dual citizenship since his mother Ruth is American and his father Andy is Canadian.

He is the son of St. Louis Blues coach Andy Murray.

He was selected 152nd overall by the Los Angeles Kings in the 2003 NHL Entry Draft.
