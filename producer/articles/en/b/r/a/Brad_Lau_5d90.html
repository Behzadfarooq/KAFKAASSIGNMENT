Brad Lau (born on April 17, 1981 in Pocatello, Idaho) is an American football fullback who played collegiately for the Boise State University football team through the 2006 season. Lau graduated from Boise State University with a major in communications and is expected to be one of the top fullbacks picked in the 2007 NFL Draft.

[edit] High school career

Lau graduated from Capital High School, Boise, Idaho after lettering in football. A two-time All-Southern Idaho Conference first-team selection at running back. Rushed for 2,071 yards and scored 34 touchdowns at Capital High School. Caught 34 passes for 120 yards and was named first-team all-state following his junior season. High school coach was Steve Vogel, a former All-Big Sky Conference linebacker for Boise State in the early 1970s.

[edit] College career

2006: Started 11 of Boise State’s games at fullback. Finished with seven carries for 47 yards, as well as five catches for 36 yards and a touchdown. Had a season-high 24 yards on three catches against Fresno State. Caught one pass, a three-yard touchdown, against Utah as well as carrying the ball a season-high three times for nine yards. Had a season-best 19 yards rushing on two carries against Utah State. Selected to the 2007 East-West Shrine Game.

2005: Started all 13 games for Boise State at fullback. Had two carries for three yards and three catches for 29 yards. Scored a nine-yard touchdown on a reception at Louisiana Tech.

2004: Part of Boise State’s tailback by committee that accounted for 2,758 yards, an average of 229.8 per game (fifth-most in school history), and a school record 49 touchdowns. Started at fullback, where his blocking was instrumental in the Broncos potent rushing attack, and played some tailback in short yardage situations. Gained 53 yards on 21 carries with a touchdown against Idaho. Caught three passes for 43 yards and a touchdown against SMU. Recovered a fumble in the end zone for a touchdown against Louisville in the AutoZone Liberty Bowl. Holds running back position record in the weight room in the power clean (403 pounds set in spring 2005), the hang clean (452 pounds set in spring 2005) and the bench press (435 pounds set in spring 2005).

2003: Backup fullback and special teams player for the Broncos. Finished the regular season with 10 carries for 21 yards and two touchdowns. Scored touchdowns against Idaho State and Tulsa. Also had two tackles on special teams.

2002: Redshirted during the 2002 season, his first with the Bronco program after serving a two-year church mission. Named Scout Team Player of the Year.

[edit] Trivia

  • Lau is married to the former Brennan Erickson, an All-WAC Conference soccer player and starter for the Boise State University womens soccer team.
  • Lau served a two-year mission to Washington, D.C. (South) for The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints.
