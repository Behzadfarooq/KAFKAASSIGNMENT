The Bracewell-Smith Family are a family dynasty of hoteliers, who also have strong links to Arsenal Football Club. They are cousins of the Carr family. The dynasty was founded by Sir Bracewell Smith, 1st Baronet.

[edit] Sir George Bracewell Smith, 2nd Baronet

Sir George Bracewell Smith, 2nd Baronet MBE was born on 12 December 1912. He was educated at Wrekin College and Emmanuel College, Cambridge. He married Helene Marie Hydock from Philadelphia in 1951. They had two children, Guy Bracewell Smith and Charles Bracewell-Smith.

He was Chairman of Park Lane Hotel Ltd and Ritz Hotel Ltd before retiring in 1966.

[edit] Sir Guy Bracewell-Smith, 3rd Baronet

Sir Guy Bracewell-Smith, 3rd Baronet was born on 12 December 1952. He was educated at Harrow School. He died in 1983 and was succeeded by his younger brother Charles Bracewell-Smith.

[edit] Sir Charles Bracewell-Smith, 4th Baronet

Sir Charles Bracewell-Smith, 4th Baronet was born on 13 October 1955 and educated at Harrow School. He is the founder of the Homestead Charitable Trust and author of "The Song of the Saints". He is grandson of Bracewell Smith and son of George Bracewell Smith. He suceeded to the baronetcy after the death of his brother Guy Bracewell Smith in 1983 at the age of 30.[1] He married Carol Hough in 1977 who died in 1994. He subsequently re-married, in 1996, Nina Kakkar. Sir Charles is cousin of Richard Carr and Clive Carr, who with his wife are directors of Arsenal FC. He has no children.
