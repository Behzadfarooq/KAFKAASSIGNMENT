Brandywine High School is a public secondary school located in Wilmington, Delaware. Currently, it has slightly over 1200 students. More than 400 of these students are in 9th grade.

[edit] Academics

In the 2006 Newsweek rankings, Brandywine ranked #751 on the list of Best Public High Schools [1]. The ranking calculates an index - the number of AP exams taken in 2005 divided by the number of graduating members of the class of 2005. Another important number on the rankings is the percentage of students on subsidized lunches, which indicates socioeconomic diversity. Last year, 31.0% of the student body of Brandywine received subsidized lunches.[citation needed]

[edit] Activities

The BHS Drama department's 2005-2006 productions included the fall play Class Dismissed, the musical Footloose, and the annual student-directed One-Act festival. This year the BHS drama department put on a performance of Little Women, and in March the production of the musical Thoroughly Modern Millie will take place. The drama department is headed by Mrs. Kay Jolly, and the set building/crew for the productions are headed by her husband Mr. Roger Jolly.

[edit] Current News

In 2006-2007, principal Richard Gregg will be eliminating all traditional level classes, leaving only College Prep, Honors, and Advanced Placement level classes.[citation needed]


Starting in 2006-2007, freshmen will be segregated.[citation needed]


Another one of Mr. Gregg's changes in 2006 was to add cameras inside and outside of the school. This was to cut down on theft, smoking in school, and physical altercations. Students have felt that the addition of cameras have reduced their privacy. Since smoking in the school is a widespread problem, the cameras will be extremely helpful to administrators.[citation needed]

BHS shows strong DSTP results, partially due to a new motivational program that allows sophomore students with passing or better grades on the DSTP to be exempt from final exams.[citation needed]

In 2006-2007 BHS was placed under academic probation as DSTP grades were reduced. The school implemented several plans including an 'X' Period in an attempt to get higher scores on the DSTP.
