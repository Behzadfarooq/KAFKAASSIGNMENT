Brahmananda Swami Sivayogi (August 26, 1852 â€“ September 10, 1929) was an iconoclast sanyasi from Kerala, India who founded the Ananda Maha Sabha and Anandamatham (religion of bliss). An atheist, he denounced the idol worship, established religions and the existence of any god. He propounded the theory that ananda (happiness or bliss) should be the touchstone of any human activity. The movement he spearheaded played an important role in the renaissance of Kerala society.
