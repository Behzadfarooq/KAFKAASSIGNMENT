Brad Sykes is the writer of 20 direct-to-video (DTV) movies and the director of 19 at the beginning of 2007.

[edit] Other Movies Worked On

Other than his own movies Sykes has also worked on other films such as The Tomorrow Man (2001) (apprentice assistant editor) The Screaming (2000) (V) (as a telecine advisor) Murdercycle (1999) (set production assistant) Phantom Town (1999) (V) (assistant to director)The Killer Eye (1999) (production assistant) Spoiler (1998) (assistant to director) Iron Thunder (1998) (production assistant) and most famously Kiss the Girls (1997) (production assistant)

[edit] Producing

Sykes is now dipping his toes into other peoples pools by producing. He served as co-producer on Cold Ones (2006) which was written and directed by Garrett Clancy.

[edit] Fans

Brad Sykes is either loved or lothed as a director with most people leaning towards the latter. He does however have a cult fanbase to keep him able to sell his movies despite having any official site.

[edit] Pre-Film

Before the release of Camp Blood Sykes is said to have made eight features on video before attending Boston University's film program in 1993 and by the time he graduated in 1997, had already worked for both Paramount Pictures and Ridley and Tony Scott's Scott Free Productions.

[edit] Production Company

He runs Nightfall Picures with his wife Josephine Sykes in L.A.
