Bradlee-McIntyre House

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Bradlee-McIntyre House
(U.S. National Register of Historic Places)
Location: Longwood, Florida
Added to NRHP: March 28, 1991

The Bradlee-McIntyre House (1885) is probably the best example of Victorian Cottage Style architecture in Central Florida. It is now located in Longwood, Florida at 130 West Warren Avenue, after being moved there from Altamonte Springs, Florida. On March 28, 1991, the house was added to the U.S. National Register of Historic Places.

The Bradlee-McIntyre House was built circa 1885 for noted Boston architect Nathaniel Jeremiah Bradlee and family. It is the only surviving "cottage" in Orange and Seminole counties, a Queen Anne Style three-story, 13-room winter vacation house featuring an octagonal tower and "ginger-bread" trim typical of the flamboyant houses of the Victorian Period.

[edit] Other

The Bradlee-McIntyre House was used as a birthing center for a few years in Longwood.
