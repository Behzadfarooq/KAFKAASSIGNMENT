Brendan V. Sullivan, Jr., JD is a Washington, D.C. based criminal defense attorney and a senior partner of the law firm Williams & Connolly.

Sullivan is probably best-known for the role he served, in the late 1980s, as defense counsel for United States Marines Lieutenant-Colonel Oliver North in the wake of the Iran-Contra scandal. He also represented former Housing and Urban Development (HUD) Secretary Henry Cisneros, Walter A. Forbes, former chairman of Cendant Corporation, and a number of states against Microsoft.

His principal areas of practice involve complex commercial litigation, including securities, antitrust, banking, the Racketeer Influenced and Corrupt Organizations Act, and license disputes, defense of major law and accounting firms, product liability and mass tort, will contests, and high-profile criminal litigation.

[edit] Education and military career

Sullivan attended Central Catholic High School in Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania, and is a graduate of Georgetown University, where he earned his B.A. in 1964 and JD in 1967. He served as a Captain in the United States Army from 1968 to 1969.

[edit] Career

Sullivan received international media attention in his role as defense counsel to Iran-Contra scandal figure Oliver North. During the ensuing congressional hearings, chairman Daniel Inouye suggested that North should speak for himself, because he had wearied of Sullivan's constant objections to questions put to North. Sullivan responded, "What am I, a potted plant? I'm here as a lawyer. That's my job."

Among his more noteworthy cases was one against Microsoft, on behalf of nine state attorneys general who were unhappy with the federal government's decision to drop an antitrust case against the software behemoth. He also represented the four Federal Bureau of Investigation (FBI) agents involved in the controversial 1992 Ruby Ridge shootout. More recently, he defended former HUD Secretary Henry Cisneros against accusations of making false statements to the FBI during a background check. He also represents labor leaders and those accused of white collar crimes, including Cabinet secretaries and manufacturers.

Sullivan's principal partner in criminal cases is defense attorney Barry Simon. Sullivan is a Fellow of the American College of Trial Lawyers, and lectures at various law schools, continuing professional education programs, as well as various bar and judicial functions. He is also the senior member of Williams and Connolly’s Executive Committee.

[edit] Recognition

The Washington Post has described Sullivan as a “world class lawyer.” The National Law Journal regularly lists him as among “America’s One Hundred Most Influential Lawyers”. Legal Times listed him as the “Best Counsel Available” in Washington, D.C. and identified him as “the leader” of the group.

In 2006, Legal Times identified him as "The Leading Lawyer in White Collar Criminal Defense" as chosen by the D.C. legal community. The Washingtonian called him “D.C.’s Toughest Lawyer” and in a survey of 500 leading lawyers in Washington, Mr. Sullivan was ranked “Number One” and was regarded as “unmatched in preparation, quick-wittedness and aggressiveness” ([1]).
