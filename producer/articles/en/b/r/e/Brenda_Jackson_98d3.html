Brenda Jackson is an American novelist and writer, well-known as the award winning author of USA Today Best Selling African American Romance Writer.

[edit] Biography

Brenda Jackson was born and raised in Jacksonville, Florida. She was a graduate of William M. Raines High School and earned a Bachelor of Science degree in Business Administration from Jacksonville University. She works in management for a major insurance company.

Brenda Jackson is a member of the First Coast Chapter of Romance Writers of America. She began her writing career in the eighth grade at Northwestern Junior High School by hand-writing her stories for fellow classmates to read.

Her professional writing career began in 1994 when she signed on with Kensington Arabesque. Her first book, Tonight and Forever, became a success, introducing the Madaris Family. Since then she has introduced the Bennetts, the Westmorelands, the Savoys, and this year she's introducing the Steeles.

Jackson cites her husband as her greatest source of inspiration for her heroes. “I look at my husband as a very strong African-American male. He is supportive and centers his life around his family.” In fact, it is Jackson’s husband who is the main reason that she has chosen to write romance novels. “When I think of my life, I think of it as a romance.”

Brenda Jackson has received awards and made accomplishments by being the first African-American author to have a book published under the Harlequin/Silhouette Desire line of books and the first African-American romance author to make USA Today's Bestseller's List. [1]

Brenda has written for the following publishers: St.Martins Press, BET, Kensington, NAL, Harlequin/Silhouette and Harlequin Kimani Romance.

[edit] Personal life

Brenda Jackson got married to Gerald, her childhood sweetheart. About her marriage, Jackson very proudly said, “My husband and I have a very close relationship.”[2] They have two sons, Gerald Jr and Brandon.
