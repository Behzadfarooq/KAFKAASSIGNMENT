Brett Tolman

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Information may change rapidly as the event progresses.

Brett Tolman was appointed by U.S.A. President George W. Bush as the United States Attorney for the District of Utah. He was a former counsel in the Senate Judiciary Committee (when it was chaired by Arlen Specter, R-PA, when the Republican Party had a majority of the Senate during the 109th United States Congress). Tolman’s most noteworthy work in the Senate is his role in the passage of the Patriot Act reauthorization during 2005 and 2006. He was instrumental in the dismissal of U.S. attorneys controversy USA PATRIOT Act revisions changing the process for appointment of interim U.S. Attorneys.

During Senate hearings on February 6, 2007, Senator Specter claimed that Tolman, on behalf of the United States Department of Justice, added a clause to the bill allowing the President to bypass Senatorial approval process when interim U.S. Attorneys are appointed.[1] This clause is at the center of the Bush-Gonzales dismissal of U.S. Attorneys controversy.

[edit] Appointment as United States Attorney

On January 25, 2006, the then U.S. Attorney for the District of Utah, Paul Warner, announced he would step down to become a U.S. Magistrate. A struggle to succeed him in office ensued, the short list of candidates included Brett Tolman, then a member of the Department of Justice, and Kyle Sampson, Attorney General Alberto Gonzales’s chief of staff at the Department of Justice (who subsequently resigned from his position, during the Dismissal of United States attorneys controversy).[2][3] While Gonzales and the White House favored Sampson, Tolman received support from Utah Senator Orrin Hatch, Judiciary Committee chairman Arlen Specter, R-Pa., Senate Majority Leader Bill Frist, R-Tn., Senate Majority Whip Mitch McConnell, R-Ky., and and Judiciary Committee member Mike DeWine, R-Oh.[4] Senator Hatch made a personal appeal to Attorney General Gonzales to drop his nomination of Kyle Sampson.[5] On June 9, 2006, President Bush nominated Tolman to the Utah post, and on July 21, 2006 the senate confirmed Tolman by voice vote.[citation needed]
