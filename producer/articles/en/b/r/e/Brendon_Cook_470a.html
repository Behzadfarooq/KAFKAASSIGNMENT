Brendon Cook

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Brendon Cook (BJ), born 27 December 1970, best known as a race driver. Racing mainly in Australia, but also in Europe and the USA. Started racing at the age of 16 in Karts, winning club titles there and moved into Formula Ford in Britain. He raced 4 races in the British Junior series, which ran the same races as the outright series, but for different points. The highlight for him would have been out qualifying eventual Formula One driver David Coulthard. After an unsuccessful season of Formula 3 in Switzerland in 1990, he returned to Australia and competed in various club racing including Formula Ford, AUSCAR, HQ Holdens and Karts. He had a brief stoppage due to business reasons between 1997-2001. In 2002 he returned to run the Australian Formula 2 Championship where he finished 8th and took out the Rookie of the Year award. From there he moved to the Australian Mitsubishi Mirage series and competed in the Bathurst 2 hour race finishing 3rd. 2004-05 he raced a Citroen Xsara in the NSW Combined Touring Car Championship, winning the State Championship in his class. He raced a BMW in the 2006 Australian Production Car Championship. In the summer of 2006, he joined the ranks of Street Stockers, running at Parramatta City Speedway over the 2006-2007 season. Brendon has won 3 club, 2 State and one National Championship.

Business wise Brendon is better known for his work in the liquor trade, where he owned a premium wine store in Cammeray, NSW. He was awarded with a Top 10 Liquor store award twice, and nominated 4 times. Brendon has a Ba and MBa in Business. Eduacted at Barker College Hornsby and Sydney University.

Married to Kim Aspery in 2001, they had their first Son Jenson in October 2004. Living now in Lane Cove North. Brendon currently is the NSW Retail Manager ar Revoution Racegear.
