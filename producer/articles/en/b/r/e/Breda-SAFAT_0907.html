Breda-SAFAT was a series of machine guns used on Italian aircraft during World War 2. It was the result of a collaboration between Breda Meccanica Bresciana and Societ√† Anonima Fabbrica Armi Torino (SAFAT). The machine gun came in 7.7 mm (0.303 in) and 12.7 mm (0.50 in) variants. The 7.7 mm variant was similar to the M1919 Browning machine gun and could use some types of .303 British ammunition. The 12.7 mm version could fire a high-explosive/incendiary/tracing (HEIT) round with 0.8 grams of PETN.
