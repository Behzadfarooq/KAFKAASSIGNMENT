In mathematics Bregman divergence is similar to a metric, but does not satisfy the triangle inequality nor symmetry. Bregman divergence is named after L. M. Bregman, who introduced the concept in 1967. More recently researchers in geometric algorithms have shown that many important algorithms can be generalized from Euclidean metrics to distances defined by Bregman divergence (Banerjee et al. 2005; Nielsen and Nock 2006; Nielsen et al. 2007).
