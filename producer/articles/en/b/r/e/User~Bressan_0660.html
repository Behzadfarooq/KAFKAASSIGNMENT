Robert Bressan


[edit] Currently:

President, Rational Expectations

Rational Expectations provides NextGen electronic medical record consulting services to Medical practices.


[edit] Previously:

Project Coordinator of the UAW-Ford University at the University of Michigan's Institute of Labor and Industrial Relations and Office of Academic Ourtreach.

The UAW-Ford University (UFU) was the first Bachelors degree program offered by the University of Michigan over the Internet. It is a five million dollar project funded by Ford in cooperation with the United Auto Workers (UAW). It was nominated for the Computerworld Smithsonian Award in 1999.

Sr. Systems Analyst at the U of M's Survey Research Center Institute for Social Research

Pioneered the dissemination of social science data over the World Wide Web. I implemented the first NCSA webserver at ISR in 1992 and demonstrated it to the National Science Foundation in 1994. This system pioneered the ad hoc querying of survey research panel data.

Sr. Systems Analyst at the U of M's Institute of Labor and Industrial Relations

One of two senior developers of the Micro DBMS database project. Micro was the first relational database management system ever placed in a production environment
