Breeders' Cup Juvenile

The Breeders' Cup Juvenile is a Thoroughbred horse race for 2-year-old colts and geldings raced on dirt. It is held annually at a different racetrack in the United States or Canada as part of the Breeders' Cup.

Distance : 1-miles(1984-85,87); 1 1/16-miles(1986,88-2001,03-); 1 1/8-miles(2002).

In 2006, the National Thoroughbred Racing Association (NTRA) wrote in Part 2 of their special series titled Spiraling To The Breeders' Cup that "Arazi turned in what many still consider to be the single-most spectacular performance in Breeders' Cup history." [1]

Often discussed is the fact that no Breeders' Cup Juvenile winner has been able to win the Kentucky Derby in the following year.
