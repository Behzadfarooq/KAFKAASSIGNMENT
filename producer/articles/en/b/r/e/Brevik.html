Brevik

Brevik is a town in Norway with a population of 2,700. Located in the municipality of Porsgrunn in the county of Telemark, Norway, Brevik was its own municipality until 1964, when Brevik, together with Eidanger and Porsgrunn, became a new municipality named Porsgrunn. Brevik is regarded one of the best preserved towns from the sailing ship era. The town is located on the far end of Eidangerhalvøya, and was a former export center for ice and timber. The last shipment of wood to the United Kingdom was around 1960.

Brevik is Cort Adeler's birth town.

[edit] Important milestones in the development of Brevik

  • Railroad track in 1895, Brevikbanen.
  • Post office in 1689.
  • Pharmacy in 1846.
  • Town hall in 1761, built by Jørgen Christie.
