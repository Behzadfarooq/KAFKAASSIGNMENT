Breaking Atoms was the debut album of Main Source. It is widely thought of as a very influential album and is notable for the debut of hip hop superstar Nas. It is also highly regarded thanks to the virtuoso production of frontman Large Professor, whose heavy, and original, use of sampling influenced hip-hop producers for a good portion of the '90s.

It was re-released by Large Professor and Wild Pitch Records overseas in 1997 with eight bonus tracks.

[edit] Acclaim

Breaking Atoms is garnered as a classic album of hip hop's golden age by nearly all critics. It is mostly praised for its soulful production and charismatic rhyming. According to Dan Mennella of RapReviews, Breaking Atoms is on par with Nas's Illmatic and the first three albums by A Tribe Called Quest [1]. Dan Mennella later went on in the same review to say:

“ The cover art and title of "Breaking Atoms" are about as accurate as one can find. Crude high school chemistry teaches us that a tremendous amount of energy is required to split an atom, but once that is achieved, the subsequent release of energy is even greater. No one who has enjoyed or engaged in hip hop since the 1991 release of this album has done so exempt from the wide-ranging influence of this album—knowingly or otherwise. Easily on par with production-revolutionizing releases like Dr. Dre's "The Chronic" or Pete Rock & C.L. Smooth's "Mecca and The Soul Brother," "Breaking Atoms" is owed a serious debt of gratitude by any and all who have felt the power of the art form. ”

Steve Huey of All Music Guide also highly touts Breaking Atoms saying "Aficionados hype Breaking Atoms as one of the greatest hip-hop albums of all time, and at least musically speaking, they're not far off."

In 1998, the album was selected as one of The Source Magazine's 100 Best Rap Albums.

[edit] Miscellanea

  • Nas' famous debut appearance, "Live at the Barbeque", is played in the background of "Genesis", the intro to his critically acclaimed album, Illmatic.
  • Received a perfect rating of "5 Mics" in "The Source" magazine.
  • "Just A Friendly Game Of Baseball" has been recreated by The High & Mighty, Masta Ace and J-Live in their songs "Just A Friendly Game Of Football", "Unfriendly Game" and "Just A Friendly Game Of Basketball" respectively.
