Brentwood Town Centre (most often referred to as Brentwood Mall) is a shopping mall in Burnaby, British Columbia. It is located in North Burnaby, at the intersection of Willingdon Avenue and the Lougheed Highway, approximately 1.5 kilometres from Vancouver city limits. It is also very close to the British Columbia Institute of Technology (BCIT) campus. Its characteristic white domes and peaks were designed to imitate the snow-capped North Shore Mountains in the background.

By the number of stores, it is the second largest mall in North Burnaby (after Lougheed Town Centre) and third largest in the city (after Lougheed Town Centre and Metrotown), with 125 stores and services. Notable retailers include Sears, Zellers and London Drugs. During the Christmas season many Burnaby families bring their young children to the Mall to have their pictures taken with Santa Claus in his workshop.

Brentwood is rapidly growing with more highrises planned to spring up all around the mall and the station

Brentwood Town Centre is also the major transit hub for North Burnaby. Public transit, serviced by TransLink, have connections to the mall, with additional routes to Vancouver, Metrotown and the University of British Columbia. The mall is also serviced by Brentwood Town Centre Station of the SkyTrain's Millennium Line. The ongoing high-rise development boom is this part of Burnaby is linked to the strategically positioned Brentwood Mall and Brentwood Station.
