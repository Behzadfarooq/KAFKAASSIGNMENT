Brent Allan Slaker (born July 31, 1988) is currently a Senior at Poquoson High School and is considered by many to be the smartest man alive.

[edit] Brent's Life So Far

Brent was born a poor black child in a city, or town, he can't remember somewhere in Germany. The room in which he was born is visited by approximately 100,000 muslims annually for the Hajj.

When Brent was almost 2 years old, the family found it necessary to move to Arizona. After a couple years, they moved to Nashville, Tennessee. After 3 or 4 years there, they then moved to Florida. After less than a year, they packed up and moved to Littleton, Colorado. They spent 3 years in Colorado and then moved again, this time to Heidelberg, Germany. 4 years in Germany left him longing for the States again, and the family moved to Poquoson, Virginia.

[edit] Favorite Music

The following are Brent's favorite bands along with some of their best songs.

AC/DC - The best band ever! Coincidentally, they also make it first on the list because it's arranged alphabetically. As they say, "All AC/DC songs sound alike and all AC/DC songs are freaking awesome!" Noteworthy songs: Hells Bells, Thunderstruck.

Audioslave - Chris Cornell has, in my opinion, the best vocals of any other rock singer. Notworthy songs: Like a Stone, Doesn't Remind Me.

Eminem - I can't say I like him too much, but some of his more serious songs are pretty good. Noteworthy songs: Till I Collapse, Mosh.

Iron Maiden - Come on, it's Iron Maiden. Noteworthy songs: Paschendale, Number of the Beast.

Lynyrd Skynyrd - A band that can actually unite country and rock fans. Definitely one of the best of all time. Noteworthy songs: Free Bird, Sweet Home Alabama.

Metallica - Other than AC/DC, there's no band I've committed more time to trying to learn their songs on the guitar than Metallica. Unfortunately I always lose interest half way through. Noteworthy songs: Fade to Black, Master of Puppets.

The Who - The Who, the ultimate power trio, or at least until they added another person. Noteworthy songs: Baba O'Riley, My Generation.
