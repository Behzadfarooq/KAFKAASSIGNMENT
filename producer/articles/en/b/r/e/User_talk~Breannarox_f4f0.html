[edit] Wikipedia and "encyclopedic tone"

Hey there, I wanted to pass on some advice about editing Wikipedia articles. It's important that all the information added is verifiable. There are several ways to verify information, including books, magazine and newspaper articles, etc. Some of your recent additions to Laguna Beach: The Real Orange County are not verified at the moment, and might actually be really hard to find a source for. For instance: "It was fun to watch her sister and friends being filmed and then to watch it on TV. " This isn't very encyclopedic in tone ("it was fun") and it's not verified -- how do we know she thought it was fun? Has she been quoted in a reputable third party source saying so? If we can't source that sort of information, it really shouldn't be added. Also, sentences like "Not long ago, Breanna was just a normal girl living in a beautiful beach city. " are what's called "POV" -- they don't really comply with Wikipedia's policy on neutral point of view. Phrases like "just a normal girl" and "a beautiful beach city" are opinions, and they sound more like entertainment journalism than an encyclopedia article. You might want to read some other articles on similar topics to learn more about tone. Please feel free to ask me any questions you might have. Cheers. Dina 20:05, 2 December 2006 (UTC)
