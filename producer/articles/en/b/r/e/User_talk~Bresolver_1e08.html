[edit] How to create edit summaries etc

Hi Bresolver:

  1. Firstly, on all TALK pages, always use the four tildes ~~~~ after making comments even when answering or making comments on your own talk page so readers can know who is talking.
  2. Secondly, to create an "edit summary" is very easy: When you edit or are adding to an article and you click on "edit this page" then you will see at the very bottom of the page a long one-line blank box that has written in front of it "Edit summary" and you then decide in your mind the MAIN IDEA or REASON behind your edit and you type into that long one-line box a few words or a very short sentence explaining why you made your edits or why you wrote things the way that you did them. Your comments will then automatically become part of that article's "history" for anyone else to see, when they click on "history" at the top of any article's page. This also helps other editors check to see what was added if they are watching the article in their "watch list" (many people watch articles they have contributed to.)
  3. Thirdly, if you are making a very small or minor edit, then just check the "This is a minor edit" tiny box on the left-hand below the long one-line "Edit summary" box.
  4. Fourthly, if you don't want to watch an article, then always REMOVE the check sign from the little right-hand box at the bottom that says "Watch this page".

If you don't read English so well, then you should be careful about making any edits so that you do not then cause confusion. Feel free to ask. Best wishes, IZAK 04:32, 19 February 2006 (UTC)
