Bret Harrison (born April 6, 1982) is an American actor who played next-door neighbor Brad O'Keefe on Grounded for Life. Currently, he is now starring in his own show, The Loop. Bret has also had many guest appearances on That '70s Show and The O.C. as Danny, Seth's rival; he had a dramatic guest role on Law & Order: Special Victims Unit as Sam Cavanaugh, a young sexual abuse victim. He also starred in the first season of MTV's Undressed and the MTV film Everybody's Doing It. In film, he appeared alongside Jack Black and Colin Hanks in the film Orange County.

Harrison was born in Portland, Oregon. He started acting as a small child in his hometown. He was so inspired that he moved to Los Angeles in his senior year, where he took an online course to finish his high school requirements. Harrison came back home and graduated with his senior class. While he is not acting, he writes music and plays guitar in his band, Big Japan, with friend Adam Brody.
