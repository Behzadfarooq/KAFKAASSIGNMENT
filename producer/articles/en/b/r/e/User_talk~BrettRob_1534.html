[edit] Do you understand anything about the Betz Limit?

We are having a bit of a difference of opinion whether or not the Betz Limit limit is applicable to the Solar updraft tower or not.
The issue arose when I included a reference to the Betz Limit in an attempt to explain the low conversion efficiency of the Solar updraft tower. The paragraph I put in my Revision as of 23:11, 7 July 2006 was promptly taken out with the argument that the Betz Limit is not applicable, you may want to look at Talk:Solar updraft tower#Betz limit for the relevant discussion. I do not know enough about Wind turbines to argue the case, so I was hoping to get some input from someone who is more familiar with Wind turbines than I am.
The reason I landed here is that I just went through the history of the article on Wind turbines, and what I found is that the paragraph about the Betz Limit was put in by an anonymous editor 70.27.59.180, see Revision as of 05:31, 14 March 2006, but that you were the first the edit his addition. So I thought that perhaps you would know a little bit more about it. Thanks, JdH 14:39, 23 July 2006 (UTC)

Sorry, JdH. My edit was strictly grammatical, I should have marked it as minor. I can't help you. Maybe try the newsgroup sci.engr.mech? --BrettRob 23:44, 23 July 2006 (UTC)
