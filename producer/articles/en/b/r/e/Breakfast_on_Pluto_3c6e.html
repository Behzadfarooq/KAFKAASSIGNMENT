Breakfast on Pluto is a 1998 novel by Patrick McCabe. The book was shortlisted for the 1998 Booker Prize, and was adapted into a Golden Globe Award-nominated film by Neil Jordan in 2005.

[edit] Plot summary

Spoiler warning: Plot and/or ending details follow.

Set in 1960s to 1970s, the story tells Patrick "Pussy" Braden's escape from small-town Tyreelin and a drunk foster mother, to find himself as a transgender escort and cabaret singer in Piccadilly Circus, London. Bad luck surrounds him until he finds temporary contentment with a British politician who acts as a sugar daddy. The latter is killed by the IRA, leaving Braden alone once again. He hustles in Piccadilly Circus, and later is arrested on suspicion of a bombing, only to be released a few days later. He later embarks on a search to find his mother and is successful.

Spoilers end here.

[edit] Film adaptation

The 2005 film version of the story was directed by Neil Jordan. It stars Cillian Murphy as Patrick "Kitten" Braden and Liam Neeson in the role of the priest that Braden believes is his biological father. In the film this character is renamed Father Liam, although a number of reviews and press accounts erroneously have reverted to calling him Father Bernard, as in the book.

Cillian Murphy was nominated for Golden Globe (2006) as Best Actor in Musical or Comedy for his performance in the movie.

[edit] Trivia

The main character's nickname in the book is "Pussy", and "Kitten" in the film.
