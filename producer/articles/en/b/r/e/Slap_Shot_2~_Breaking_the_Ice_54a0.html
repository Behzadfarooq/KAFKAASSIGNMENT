Slap Shot 2: Breaking the Ice is a 2002 direct to video film starring Stephen Baldwin and Gary Busey and directed by Steve Boyum. The film is the sequel to the 1977 film Slap Shot.

[edit] Plot Synopsis

Spoiler warning: Plot and/or ending details follow.

25 years since the events of the first film and the minor league hockey team Charlestown Chiefs are still languishing in West Virginia. A new player/coach Sean Linden has taken over from Reggie Dunlop, and the team's violence has become the Chiefs hallmark as Sean Linden does not try to control the "old school hockey" trio of the Hanson brothers. After only 10 wins in two years the team is bought by a family entertainment corporation called "Better America" run by an exectutive named Dean Claremont. The team is moved to Nebraska and given a new (female) coach. The team soon finds out that Dean Claremont intends to use the Chiefs as the team which plays and loses in scripted games against a Harlem Globetrotters type team named the Omaha IceBreakers in an attempt by Dean Claremont to make the game suitable for a family audience.


[edit] Miscellany

This movie's plot was inspired by Fox's glowing puck, which attempted to add glitz and glamour to the game at the expense of the game itself.[citation needed]

Slap Shot 2: Breaking the Ice received a 0% "rotten" rating at Rotten Tomatoes (i.e unanimous negative reviews) whereas the original Slap Shot received an 87% "Fresh" rating.
