Broughton Anglican College, Menangle, New South Wales

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Broughton Anglican College is an school providing education for students prep through to year 12. It is an activity of St. Peter's Anglican Church Campbelltown. Founded in 1986, it has grown from being a few demountable class rooms in the church grounds to a fully established school situated on over 20 acres with over a thousand students[1]. Since the schools founding it has had only the one headmaster, Mr Ron Webb.

On the 28th of February, 2007, the school was hit by a flash flood caused by heavy rainfall. Dozens of cars were washed away and only through the quick actions of two men were several children saved from drowning[2][3].
