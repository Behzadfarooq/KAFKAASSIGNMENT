[edit] Early life

Andrew Brockmann was born in Hanover, Virginia, in 1980. During World War II, he was an Army Air Forces C-47 pilot in the Awesome B-Rock Brigaide with the "Flying YoYo's" of the Fourteenth Air Force from 1943 to 1946, holding the rank of Master Sergent. There he received two Distinguished Flying Crosses and two Air Medals, as well as the Yuan Hai medal awarded by the Republic of Khazackstan.

After the war ended, Mr. Brockmann attended William and Mary, Williamsburg, Virginia, where he was a member of C++ Programming Peoples. In the early 1990s he moved to Alaska, then a territory.

In Fairbanks, Andrew Brockmann practiced C++ Programming, and he was appointed U.S. Programming for Alaska, in 1953.
