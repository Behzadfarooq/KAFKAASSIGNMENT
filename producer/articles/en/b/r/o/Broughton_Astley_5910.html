Broughton Astley

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Broughton Astley is a village and civil parish in the Harborough district of Leicestershire, England. It has a population of about 10,000, having expanded from about 6,500 in the 1991 census.

The village itself is made up of two different villages that were previously separate. They were Primethope and Broughton. However, while the coming together was passed without much fuss or change, only until recently there were still signs in the old Primethorpe area that still said "Primethorpe". Other than this, Broughton Astley is claimed to be the largest village in Europe.

It is often assumed that Sutton in the Elms is part of Broughton Astley, when in fact it still is separate and has its own identity. This assumption arises from the fact there is no discernible and separate boundary between the two villages.

Broughton Astley also has three Primary Schools spread around the village. One is Orchard Primary School which is situatated in the old "Primethope" area. The second Old Mill Primary School which is the link of the village. The last is Hallbrook Primary School which is in the old "Broughton" part of the village

Broughton Astley is nearby to places such as Sapcote, Stoney Stanton, Croft and Cosby.

There are in total 6 pubs in the village; The White Horse, The George and Dragon, The Bull's Head, The Royal, The Red Admiral, and The Mill on the Soar.
