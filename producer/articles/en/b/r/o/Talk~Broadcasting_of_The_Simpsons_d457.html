So the one question I had about this topic isn't answered in the article: Do they keep the anti-French humor when the show is dubbed into French? Not that the show is dedicated to mocking France but they've certainly had a number of memorable gags over the years. And how do they handle scenes where the characters were originally speaking French, like the early episode when Bart goes to France as an exchange student? --Do Not Talk About Feitclub (contributions) 14:01, 12 February 2006 (UTC)

I actually saw that particular episode when I was in France. In the scene with the policeman there's no language barrier at all -- Bart simply tells the cop about how he's homesick and the cop says he can't do anything. At the moment where Bart (in the English version) has a revelation that he can speak French, Bart instead has a revelation that he can tell the cop about how the peasants mistreated him (and the wine too).
There are some other slight changes -- for example, in one episode where Lisa walks in on a French class, the class is learning Old French instead; the clumsy French waiter who claimed the mayor's nephew beat him up becomes a clumsy Italian waiter; and characters referring to "the English language" have their line changed to say "our language" or some other way of hiding this. A lot of the jabs at the French are preserved, though (including Groundskeeper Willie's "cheese-eating surrender monkeys," although they leave out the word "surrender," probably for reasons of synchronization rather than cultural sensitivity). if you can read French, http://www.simpsonspark.com/scripts/ is an interesting site because it has the full transcripts of a couple hundred Simpsons episodes in their European French dubbing, so you can see the differences. Andrew Levine 16:23, 15 March 2006 (UTC)

[edit] Super Bowl?

Is there Any truth to the 'current super bowl teams' item in the header? It seems as though it would be false. There is one episode where they are forced to dub in the names of the teams because the episode was aired on Superbowl Sunday. The show mocked this by having the characters raise mugs in front of their mouths while saying the team names (in an obviously unfitting tone of voice) while also joking that President (dubbed: Clinton) would be there with his wife (dubbed: Hillary). But has the show every been edited to change the teams involved in this or another episode (locally or in another country)? I know the original teams were the Atlanta Falcons and the Denver Broncos.

I wrote the lead, but I realise know that I might have been wrong. I can't remember which website I got the information from. Anyway, The Simpsons Archieve (8F12) doesn't mention the Super Bowl, but only a regular football match. --Maitch 10:11, 9 June 2006 (UTC)

[edit] Undubbed French Episodes

I think I know the reason why those three season six episodes weren't dubbed straight away - they were added later in the US version - the clip show was a filler, another episode was to advertise The Critic, which had switched networks, and the third was to kill off Bleeding Gums Murphy, as the actor was leaving the show. The rest of the season would've already been dubbed but the French producers probably got sent those three with season 7, and if The Critic wasn't shown in France, that episode serves no purpose. Digifiend 11:37, 14 June 2006 (UTC)
