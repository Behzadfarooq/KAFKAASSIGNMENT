Brooke Theiss

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Brooke Theiss (b. October 23, 1969 in Palos Verdes, California, USA) is an American actress who has starred in film and on television.

Brooke's first feature movie was in the 1988's Little Nikita, but later that year, Brooke's big role came in the hit horror movie A Nightmare on Elm Street 4: The Dream Master as Debbie Stevens, where the character was painfully transformed into a cockroach and crushed inside a roach motel. Her most recent movie is 2004's Catwoman.

Brooke's best known TV role is in the 1980s TV series Just the Ten of Us as Wendy Lubbock from 1988-1990. She also starred in the 1990s TV series Good & Evil, Home Free and in The Amanda Show from 1999-2000.

Brooke has made some guest appearances in some tv shows,some of those appearances range from Growing Pains where she played Wendy Lubbock, Parker Lewis Can't Lose, Beverly Hills, 90210, American Dreams, and Cold Squad.
