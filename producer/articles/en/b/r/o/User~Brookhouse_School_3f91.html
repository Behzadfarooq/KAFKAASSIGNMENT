Established in 1981 in a leafy suburb of Nairobi 10 minutes from the city centre, Brookhouse School offers co-educational international education for 4 to 19 year olds at primary, secondary and pre-university foundation levels. From Key Stage One through to A-levels, the school delivers an adapted form of the British National curriculum, catering mainly for the professional, business and diplomatic communities of the East African region. Brookhouse School balances traditional values and an innovative approach to curriculum.

The school features small classes and a wide range of subject choices. Most students go on to UK universities, with an increasing number choosing the USA, Canada, Australia and South Africa. With more than 30 nationalities represented, the school prides itself on fostering tolerance and understanding, promoting a diverse programme of extra-curricular activities to ensure the development of the whole child, including the President's Award Scheme, Model United Nations and a 'charity through action' community service programme.

Teachers are recruited from the UK and East Africa.

Situated on a thirteen acre campus adjacent to Nairobi National Park, the school is secured by an electric fence. On-site co-educational boarding accommodation caters for 200 students and a dozen staff, helping to create a strong sense of community.

Academic facilities include two computer laboratories, four science laboratories, two libraries, lecture theatre, Fine Art and Music studios, Careers and Sixth Form centres, and twenty general purpose classrooms. All classrooms are computer networked, and students have supervised e-mail and internet access.

The Arts receive major emphasis at Brookhouse: the Fine Art department mounts an annual public exhibition; there are regular drama productions; and the Music department includes a massed choir, orchestra, and several smaller instrumental groups. Individual instrumental lessons are also offered.

A Learning Support Unit caters for Special Educational Needs and for pupils who have English As An Additional Language backgrounds, as well as providing an Academic Extension Programme for highly able pupils.

Sporting facilities include a state-of-the-art gym and aerobics studio, swimming pool, tennis and basketball courts, and irrigated playing fields to ensure year round quality playing surfaces. A varied programme of team and individual sports is available.

There are three terms in the September - July academic year. Admission is by interview, entrance test and/or previous school reports. Entrance to A-level is determined by the level of success at IGCSE or equivalent.
