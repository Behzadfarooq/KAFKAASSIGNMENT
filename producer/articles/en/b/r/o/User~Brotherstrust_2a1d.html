The Brothers’ Trust is an artistic partnership founded on April 18th, 2004 by Brs. Shane Bengry and Seán Beahen, two Benedictine Canons of the Benedictine Community of St. Michael the Archangel based in Vancouver in the Dominion of Canada. The Brothers of the Brothers' Trust work within the LebensART theoretical framework first articulated when they were a part of an art group known as 'The New Congress'. Their work is interdisciplinary and includes printmaking, photography, painting, publishing etc. and can be found exhibited in Canada and on the internet.

Members: Br. Shane was formally trained in art & design at the Emily Carr Institute and was awarded the Medal of Her Majesty Queen Elizabeth II’s Golden Jubilee in 2002. Br. Shane was raised on the Canadian prairie and currently resides in Vancouver where he attends the Vancouver School of Theology. Br. Seán hails from the nation's capital, Ottawa; he attends the Emily Carr Institute in Vancouver and is actively involved in provincial politics, in the Anglican church and the United Empire Loyalist Association. Both Brothers are believed to be descendants of United Empire Loyalists. The Brothers associate themselves with other artists called 'Associate Brothers' most notably Gordon Hardy, an underground writer and conceptual artist.

Themes Addressed: The Brothers address a variety of artistic themes as they relate to the post-postmodern North American context that range from traditional Biblical subject matter, to abstract forms and work informed by popular culture. They are probably best known for their hand-printed postcards which they distribute select prints in Vancouver for no cost. Each postcard explores a unique theme and style. Their current work explores the interruption of secular space with contemporary Catholic iconography.

Former Affiliations: The Brothers were members of an art group called The New Congress (1995-2004) which was formed from a group called 'The United Congress' (c.1984-present) currently based in Calgary, Alberta. The disolution of The New Congress in 2004 gave rise to The Brothers' Trust as well as a short-lived art group called 'The General Assembly'.
