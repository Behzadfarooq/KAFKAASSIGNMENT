"The Brothers Line" (sometimes called "3 Brothers Line","Brothaz Line" Or "The Brothers with a Brotha") was an offensive-minded trio of forwards on the Vancouver Canucks of the National Hockey League which lasted throughout the 2005-06 season.

The line consisted of identical twins Henrik and Daniel Sedin, a centerman and left winger respectively, flanked by winger Anson Carter. The line was colloquially known as the 'Brothers Line' due to the presence of two biological brothers, as well as Carter, who is Caribbean-Canadian. (The term 'Brother' is often used by black North American men as a label for one another).

The Sedin twins have played together since their youth and have always had noticeable chemistry as a duo, but since arriving in Vancouver they had struggled at times to find a third forward partner who could take an active part in that chemistry. After limited success playing with Trent Klatt, Jason King, Trevor Linden, Todd Bertuzzi and others on the right wing since the twins joined the Canucks in 2000-01, Carter had by far the most success, and the line became Vancouver's most regular scoring threat in 2005-06. Both twins managed career highs in offensive figures, while Carter enjoyed his best season since 2001-02, when he played for the Edmonton Oilers. He racked up 33 goals and 22 assists, totalling in 55 points. (Coincidentally, the number "33" is Henrik Sedin's jersey number, while "22" is Daniel Sedin's.)

Carter's free-agent signing with the Columbus Blue Jackets in September 2006 ensured that the popular trio would not be reunited for 2006-07. Since then, the twins have had an equal success with former Buffalo Sabre Taylor Pyatt, who signed with the Canucks.
