Broadsword and the Beast is an album released by Jethro Tull on April 10, 1982 (see 1982 in music).

The 2005 reissue of the album is expanded to include eight bonus tracks recorded during the Broadsword sessions, but not included in the original 1982 album.

The runic symbols around the edge of the cover are from the Cirth rune system used by J.R.R. Tolkien in The Lord of the Rings. The words are, however, English: pretty much the opening lyrics to Broadsword:

I see a dark sail on the horizon, set under a black cloud was hiwies we Bring me my broadsword and clear understanding. Bring me my cross of gold as a talisman.
