The Broadcast Decency Enforcement Act of 2005 was a piece of legislation in the United States Senate (bill number S. 193) that increases penalties for broadcasters who violate decency standards.

The bill has been sponsored by Sen. Sam Brownback, Republican of Kansas.

On June 15, 2006, the bill became Public Law No: 109-235 and was signed by the president.
