Broadfield Stadium

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Broadfield Stadium is a multi-use stadium in Crawley, England. It is currently used mostly for football matches and is the home ground of Crawley Town F.C.. The stadium has a capacity of 4,996 people, and is owned by Crawley Borough Council.

[edit] Memorable moments

[edit] Southern League Cup Final 2003-4, 20 April 2004

Crawley Town 2-0 Moor Green

Danny Marney 53', MacDonald 88'

After winning the first leg 2-1 and winning the league against Welling,, Crawley Town were favourites to win the Southern League Cup for the second year running. The second leg of the 2004 Dr. Martens League Cup final saw Crawley Town 2-0 as they became the first team to win the Southern League and cup double since Wimbledon in 1976

Crawley Town team: Andy Little, Ben Judge, Sean Hankin, Kevin Hemsley, Karl Ready, Peter Fear (Paul Armstrong 58'), Robert Traynor, Robert Kember, Allan Tait (Joff Vansittart, 36), Danny Marney, Justin Gregory (Charlie MacDonald, 65') Subs not used: Ian Payne, William McFredrick

[edit] Southern League Premier Division Trophy Presentation 2003-4, 24 April 2004

Following a 3-0 defeat by Hinckley, Crawley Town were presented with the Southern League trophy.

Crawley Town 1-1 Hereford United

Charlie Mapes 86'

After the build-up to Crawley's first ever home game in a national division, Charlie Mapes got a draw for Crawley in a well-contested match.
