[edit] Broughton of Broughton (1661)

This baronetcy was created 10 March 1661 in the Baronetage of England for Sir Brian Broughton, 1st Baronet, Knight Bachelor of Broughton. High Sheriff 1660-61. Sir Brian Broughton, 3rd Baronet married Elizabeth, daughter of Sir Thomas Delves, 4th Baronet, and the family became Broughton-Delves. The Doddington estate came into the family from the Delves family. Sir Thomas Delves, 6th Baronet had a son Sir John Delves Broughton, 7th Baronet and the surname has subsequently been Broughton but the last forename Delves has frequently been used. On the death of Sir Evelyn Delves Broughton, 12th Baronet, the baronetcy passed to his cousin, David Delves Broughton who has not proved his succession.

Doddington Park, near Nantwich, Cheshire remains in the family of Sir Evelyn Delves Broughton, 12th Baronet.

  • Sir Brian Broughton, 1st Baronet (1618-1708)
  • Sir Thomas Broughton, 2nd Baronet (died 1710)
  • Sir Brian Broughton, 3rd Baronet (died 1724)
  • Sir Brian Broughton-Delves, 4th Baronet (1717-19-1744)
  • Sir Brian Broughton-Delves, 5th Baronet (1740-1766)
  • Sir Thomas Broughton, 6th Baronet (died 1813)
  • General Sir John Delves Broughton, 7th Baronet (1769-1847)
  • Sir Henry Delves Broughton, 8th Baronet (1777-1851) from whom is descended David Delves Broughton
  • Sir Henry Delves Broughton, 9th Baronet (1808-1899)
  • Sir Delves Louis Broughton, 10th Baronet (1857-1914)
  • Sir Henry John Delves Broughton, 11th Baronet (1883-1942)
  • Sir Evelyn Delves Broughton, 12th Baronet (1915-1993)

The Baronetcy then became dormant.

  • Potentially the heir apparent is David Delves Broughton (born 7 May 1942)
  • He has a son, Geoffrey Delves Broughton (born 1962).
