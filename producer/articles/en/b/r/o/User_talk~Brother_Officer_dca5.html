[edit] Regarding James Luther Slayden

For a first page mostly by yourself? Very nice! Unfortunately if the page is originally saved under an incorrect title spelling (the spelling at the top of the page), the only solution is to movepage (which someone did since you wrote the message to me). The subject's name should be bold faced, but not ALL CAPS, so you need to change that. Also, we don't list the subject's last name first on article space (sometimes on a list and always in a category, but don't let detail get to you yet.)

Notice that the same friendly user who moved the article to the correct spelling location also dropped in a wikify tag. This is becasue your new article has few wikilinks. These can be added over time.

You have two or three methods of proceeding here. You could continue to improve the article over time (most likely course), you could ask others to help (add biography task force tag, or some such), or you could do NOTHING, and watch what the wiki community does with it. It might be interesting to watch what others decide need done to the article, but I never had the patience with my first few, wanting them to be "finished" before I stopped. Remember, no article's ever finished around here. Good job! BusterD 16:47, 26 October 2006 (UTC)

Good job. I've removed the wikify tag; you've definitely passed that bar. Now perform a thorough "click through" of all your links to make sure they all work correctly. Click on San Antonio and Charlottesville. Notice that when you arrive at the page, there's tiny text underneath the title (click on the link to see what a redirect page looks like, then back on your browser to bring you to the page). This signifies what we call a "redirect" because the automatic change brings the reader to the correct page. This doesn't happen by accident; folks gradually put in pages that help users get to the right spot when folks accidentally type in misspelling and miscapitalized entries. We want to fix those. You fix them by using what we call a "stick". In edit mode, look at the link I put in for "Representative". Notice the link looks a bit more complicated than the regular wikilink. Inside of the double brackets, we have text before the | (the stick, a capitol "\", not an I or an L), and text after the |. The text after the stick is what the reader sees, the text before is the accurate article title. When I'm fixing such links, I often go to the desired page, and actually highlight and copy the title with my cursor and browser edit menu command, then click back to my edit and paste in the text before the stick, so that I can't accidentally messup misspelling, caps, etc that would force a redirect. (Using the stick, you can actually link white to black and war to peace, so be careful; such misleading links are considered vandalism).
Deal with all the redirects you see, and then we'll talk about fixing red links. You're learning quickly, and showing initiative (can't survive without it here). Every once in a while, go back to reading some of the basic "how to..." stuff, and some of it will make more sense each time.
Feel free to continue to ask me (and others you meet) questions. This all makes sense in a very short time. And keep your cool if you run into any wikibullies (they are fewer in the non-fiction clusters). Ask for help. Be clear in what you're tryinf to do, and most people will fall all over themselves with kindness. As someone recently reminded me, there's no body language or facial expression to guide us here. If you're meaning to be nice, be extra nice. If you're meaning to be regular, be nice. Be square and play nice (not too different from real life). BusterD 15:28, 27 October 2006 (UTC)
