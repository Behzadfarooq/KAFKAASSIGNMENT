Broken Top is an stratovolcano highly eroded by glaciation. It is located south of the Three Sisters group of volcanoes in the Cascade Range of central Oregon. Broken Top is about 20 miles (32 km) west of Bend, Oregon in Deschutes County.

Broken Top. USGS image.

[edit] Geology

Pleistocene eruptions originally created a broad shield of basaltic andesite. Intermittently, more silicic magma was erupted, ranging from andesite to dacite to rhyodacite. Intrusive dikes and pyroclastic flow deposits can also be found in the layers of Broken Top's flanks. Ice Age glaciers have carved into the volcano on three sides, exposing its internal structure.
