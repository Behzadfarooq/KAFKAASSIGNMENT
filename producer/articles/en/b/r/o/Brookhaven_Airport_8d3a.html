Brookhaven Airport (IATA: HWV, ICAO: KHWV), also known as Brookhaven Calabro Airport, is a public airport located one mile (2 km) north of Shirley in the Town of Brookhaven, Suffolk County, New York, USA.

Brookhaven Airport was constructed during World War II to provide logistical support for U.S. Army Air Corps operations. Title of the airport was transferred to New York State after the war. In 1961 the airport was acquired by the Town of Brookhaven. It was later renamed in honor of Dr. Frank Calabro, an important figure in the airport's development.

[edit] Facilities

Brookhaven Airport covers 795 acres and has two runways:

  • Runway 15/33: 4,224 x 150 ft. (1,287 x 46 m), Surface: Asphalt/Concrete
  • Runway 6/24: 4,200 x 100 ft. (1,280 x 30 m), Surface: Asphalt
