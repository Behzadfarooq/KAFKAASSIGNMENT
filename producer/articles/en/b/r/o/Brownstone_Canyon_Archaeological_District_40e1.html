Brownstone Canyon Archaeological District

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Brownstone Canyon Archaeological District comprises 2,920 acres[1] and is located in the La Madre Mountain Wilderness Area which 47,180 acres in southern Nevada.[2] The area is administered by the Humboldt-Toiyabe National Forest and the Bureau of Land Management and includes many petroglyphs.

[edit] History

The district was listed on the National Register of Historic Places on September 22, 1982 for its significance during the period of 1975-2000.[3]
