[edit] Re: Supreme Court question

Hi there. Thanks for the question regarding the citing of Small v. United States. Sorry it has taken me so long to reply, but I have been on leave from Wikipedia for some months now.

I'm sure the situation is settled by now, but the article is currently correctly placed in the category of 2005 law. It is common for a case to be argued in the Winter term and the opinion to be delivered in the Spring term. Whenever a case is cited, it is cited by the year in which the decision was recorded. This is when it actually becomes law. Until then, it is simply a pending case with no legal affect by means of stare decisis.

Thanks for your work on Supreme Court case articles and I hope you continue in the future.

Skyler1534 23:05, May 15, 2005 (UTC)

I've found that updating the page, and then adding a comment to admin noticeboard once in a while has really got things taken care of quickly.

I know many admins prefer to spend their time cleaning out VfDs and the like, but I believe speedy deletes are the ones that should be done first to ensure that at least anything that is on Wikipedia has some sort of value, even if it is later voted as deletable.

Anyway, that's my story. Thanks for taking notice of my work at SD. Harro5 02:27, May 22, 2005 (UTC)
