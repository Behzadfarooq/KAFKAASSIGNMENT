Brown & Bigelow

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Brown & Bigelow of St. Paul, Minn. is the art company that published Rolf Armstrong, Gil Elvgren, Vaughn Alden Bass, Mabel Rollins Harris, and Norman Rockwell. In the late 1940's, it was one of the biggest calendar printers in the world,[1] employing some of the United States' best pin-up artists and putting calendars into an estimated 50 million homes.[2]
