Brook Lopez is a 7'0" American basketball player who currently plays for Stanford in the Pacific-Ten Conference of the NCAA.

[edit] Career

Lopez's college career got off to a rocky start when he injured his back during summer workouts and had to have surgery in the fall of his freshman year. This caused him to miss the first five games of the 2006-07 season and spend the next few coming off the bench. As soon as he regained his fitness, however, Stanford coach Trent Johnson inserted Lopez into the starting lineup and it paid immediate dividends, as the Cardinal beat four ranked teams, including UCLA, in the first half of the Pac-10 schedule.

Over the course of the season, Lopez emerged as a more and more dependable offensive threat for Stanford. When their star guard Anthony Goods went down with an ankle sprain as the schedule was winding down, Lopez responded with four straight 20-point games. He also put in some stellar defensive performances, including a 12-block effort against USC. He recorded 18 points and 11 rebounds in that game for the first triple-double in school history. Also, his twin brother Robin Lopez plays for Stanford as well, pairing up with Brook to form considerable post power.

Despite speculation that he might bolt to the NBA after his freshman season--Lopez was ranked in the top 10 on ESPN's Chad Ford's draft board--Lopez decided to stay in school.

In his freshman year, Lopez averaged 12.6 points per game, 6 rebounds per game, and had a total of 45 blocked shots.
