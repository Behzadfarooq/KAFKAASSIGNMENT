Brooklyn Handicap

The Brooklyn Handicap is an annual American race for thoroughbred horses first held at Gravesend Race Track on Coney Island, New York in 1887. It is currently held at Belmont Park on Long Island. The race is the second part of what is sometimes referred to as the New York Handicap Triple series of races. It is preceded by the Metropolitan Handicap and followed by the Suburban Handicap.

Four horse have won the Handicap Triple:

  • Whisk Broom II (1913)
  • Tom Fool (1953)
  • Kelso (1961)
  • Fit to Fight (1984)

Currently a Grade II event, the Brooklyn Handicap is open to three-year-olds and up willing to race the one and one-eighth miles on the dirt. Offering a purse of $150,000, and usually a spring event, it will be raced in the fall due to the Grade I Woodward Stakes being moved to Saratoga Race Course. Because of its fall running, the Brooklyn will be raced as a Breeders' Cup race series event during the 2006 Belmont fall meet.
