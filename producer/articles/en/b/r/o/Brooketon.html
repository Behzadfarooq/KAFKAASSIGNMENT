Brooketon

Brooketon was a coal mine and settlement situated near the present-day port of Muara, Brunei and was named after the White Rajahs of Sarawak. The coal mine operated between 1888 and 1924, with unsuccessful Japanese attempts to reopen it during the Second World War. Brooketon was the location of the Australian landing which liberated Brunei from Japanese rule. All that remains of Brooketon today is an overgrown railway, locomotives, mine entrances and an abandoned Morris Minor. Brooketon was also where the first postage stamp in Brunei was used.
