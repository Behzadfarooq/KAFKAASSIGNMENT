[edit] Contentious paragraph

"Brooklyn Beckham is famous in the UK to a degree which is unprecented for a non-royal British child. His parents are the leading British celebrity couple of the 2000s and the media, especially the tabloids, has chosen to treat him as a celebrity in his own right in a way which has not occurred in the UK before. This can be seen as a significant development in British celebrity culture. Trivial aspects of Beckham's childhood, such as the cost of his birthday party, overheard comments he has made, and his father's difficulties helping him with his maths homework, have been given a degree of publicity which never occurred in earlier periods."

Above paragraph is original research unless citations are found. The first, third and final sentences are all highly disputable yet presented as fact. Steve block Talk 06:51, 30 June 2006 (UTC)
