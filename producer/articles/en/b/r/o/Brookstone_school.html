Brookstone School

[[1]]


School Type: Founded in 1951, Brookstone is an independent, coeducational college preparatory school for students in pre-kindergarten through grade 12. Accreditation and National Association for College Admissions Counseling, Southern Association for College Admission Counseling,

Memberships: The College Board, National Association of Independent Schools, Southern Association of Independent Schools, Georgia Independent School Association, Atlanta Area Association of Independent Schools, Council for Advancement and Support of Education, Georgia High School Association, National Association of Secondary School Principals, The Cum Laude Society, National Honor Society, Secondary School Admission Testing Board

Enrollment: 852 total; 280 students in the Upper School; 73 in the Class of 2006 Campus: The campus is in a college-like setting located on 100 acres in northwest Columbus. Over 170,000 square feet of technologically enhanced classroom space, two gyms, an auditorium, and 12 acres of athletic venues surround a central quadrangle. Currently under construction is a 40,000 square foot new Upper School, which is planned to open in the fall of 2006. State-of-the-art science and mathematics labs are two of the main features of the new building. The Upper School building will provide students and faculty with facilities that reflect and enhance the quality of our traditional liberal arts program.

Mission Statement: Based on Judeo-Christian values, Brookstone endeavors to develop in its students academic excellence, integrity, mutual respect, personal responsibility, love of learning, aesthetic appreciation, and athletic accomplishment. The balance of individual and communal commitment fostered at Brookstone is intended to enrich and guide a life of personal fulfillment as well as service to others. Although Brookstone is an independent school, we consider ourselves part of the larger Columbus community. Therefore, we instill and honor in our students a sense of community service and involvement that we believe will produce excellent leaders and citizens.

Faculty: The school has 70 teaching professional staff; more than 70% hold advanced degrees. The teaching faculty student-teacher ratio is 11:1. The average teaching experience of the faculty is 24 years.

Student Body: Brookstone is a learning community comprised of students and teachers from a variety of backgrounds that represent different ethnic groups and international heritages.
