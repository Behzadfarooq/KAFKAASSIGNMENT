Brookfield Central High School is a comprehensive public secondary school located in the city of Brookfield, Wisconsin. It is a sister-school to Brookfield East High School, also located in Brookfield. The High School is governed by the Elmbrook School District.

[edit] Academics

Brookfield Central offers courses in Art, Business Management and Technology, English, Foreign Language (French, German, Latin, and Spanish), Family and Consumer Education, Applied Technology and Engineering, Mathematics, Music, Physical Education, Science, and Social Studies. Honors, Advanced, and many Advanced Placement classes are available in most subjects.

[edit] Music

The band, chorus, and orchestra groups of BCHS are quite critically acclaimed. They have played at venues ranging from the DisneyWorld Magic Kingdom Parade, Toronto, and London. Combined, the programs have about 503 participating students. They are led by Band Directors Jason Gillete and Molly Walsh, Chorus Director Phil Olson, Orchestra Director Jane Anello. Mr, Marc Mueller also contributes and helps out when he can.

[edit] Athletics

Brookfield Central High School has a large number of competitive sports teams competeing in WIAA Division I and Division II. In 2006, the girls tennis team won the State Championship and the Varsity Football team made it to state and lost in OT to Franklin 29-36.
