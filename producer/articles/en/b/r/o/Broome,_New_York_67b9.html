Broome is a town in Schoharie County, New York, USA. The population was 947 at the 2000 census. The town is named after John Broome.

The Town of Broome is on the eastern border of the county.

[edit] History

The town was one of the six original towns when the county was formed in 1797. Originally, named the "Town of Bristol," the name was changed to "Broome" in 1811.

[edit] Past residents of note

  • Daniel Shays, leader of Shays' Rebellion.

[edit] Geography

According to the United States Census Bureau, the town has a total area of 124.5 km² (48.1 mi²). 123.7 km² (47.8 mi²) of it is land and 0.7 km² (0.3 mi²) of it (0.58%) is water.

The east town line is the border of Albany County, New York.

[edit] Demographics

As of the census2 of 2000, there were 947 people, 407 households, and 260 families residing in the town. The population density was 7.7/km² (19.8/mi²). There were 767 housing units at an average density of 6.2/km² (16.1/mi²). The racial makeup of the town was 96.41% White, 0.74% African American, 0.32% Native American, 0.11% Asian, 0.74% from other races, and 1.69% from two or more races. Hispanic or Latino of any race were 1.80% of the population.

There were 407 households out of which 23.8% had children under the age of 18 living with them, 53.6% were married couples living together, 5.9% had a female householder with no husband present, and 36.1% were non-families. 30.0% of all households were made up of individuals and 12.3% had someone living alone who was 65 years of age or older. The average household size was 2.33 and the average family size was 2.91.

In the town the population was spread out with 20.9% under the age of 18, 5.8% from 18 to 24, 25.4% from 25 to 44, 29.4% from 45 to 64, and 18.5% who were 65 years of age or older. The median age was 44 years. For every 100 females there were 99.8 males. For every 100 females age 18 and over, there were 103.0 males.

The median income for a household in the town was $32,368, and the median income for a family was $40,167. Males had a median income of $30,500 versus $23,750 for females. The per capita income for the town was $16,383. About 5.4% of families and 8.4% of the population were below the poverty line, including 2.5% of those under age 18 and 9.0% of those age 65 or over.
