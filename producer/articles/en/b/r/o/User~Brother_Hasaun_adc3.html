At the age of 23, Brother Hasaun has established himself as a young visionary whose goal of shining light upon the positive aspects that exist in Youth Culture (i.e. hip-hop culture), has helped to steer the course of youth today in a direction that best represents a promising future for generations to come. Working under the mentorship of powerful figures such as mogul Russell Simmons, Doug E. Fresh and long-time civil rights activist Dr. Benjamin Chavis, Hasaun upholds a thriving career in the field of community activism and entertainment media relations.

Hasaun always had a strong sincerity and passion toward the status and well-being of youth in the community and throughout the world. After experiencing a series of life defining events, at the age of 17, subsequent to the loss of his close comrade (former NY Youth Minister) his mission in life was set for fulfillment. His determination for positive change of the community's negative image that was being transmitted to the youth led him to the Hip-Hop Summit Action Network (HSAN) in June of 2001; becoming one of the establishing members of HSAN.

Over the years while at HSAN, he has developed various effective initiatives to fight the war of ignorance and poverty. Including campaigns such as a NY Educational Rally to restore funding in public schools that drew over 100,000 students to attendance (2002); coordinated development of Hip-Hop Team Vote (an initiative which lead over 4 million youth to the polls in 2004 election). He continues to make his mark in the communal realm by developing campaigns for youth empowerment featuring A-list celebrities; and acts as a voice for millions of young people as he currently serves in the capacity of the National Youth Director of the Millions More Movement; Youth Ambassador for CSIRI-ISP Permanent Observer Mission to the United Nations; and as an independent brand marketing specialist communicating trends between the corporate and consumer market.

ADDITIONAL ACCOMPLISHMENTS

Organized more than 40 successful Hip-Hop Summits in New York, Los Angeles, Detroit, Atlanta, Chicago, Houston, Miami, Washington, D.C., Kansas City, Philadelphia, Seattle, Birmingham and Dallas, providing a national template for engaging the Hip-Hop generation in community-building dialogues.

Joined with The Alliance for Quality Education, mobilizing 100,000 New York City public school students and top Hip-Hop recording artists to a protest rally at City Hall which resulted in Mayor Bloomberg restoring $300 million in proposed cuts to the New York City public school budget. The National Federation of Teachers also partnered in this rally and, in part through the advocacy of the Hip-Hop community, they were able to finally negotiate a fair compensation contract for New York City public school teachers.


Alicia Keys & Dr. Ben Chavis at education protest encouraging a crowd of 100,000 plus students at NY City Hall 2002

Organized a public awareness campaign on the unfairness of the Rockefeller Drug Laws in New York culminating in a public rally of over 60,000. Leading toward a bi-partisan legislative agreement which was reached by Senate Majority Leader Joseph Bruno and State Assembly Speaker Sheldon Silver to reform sentencing provisions of the Rockefeller Drug Laws (2002-2004).
