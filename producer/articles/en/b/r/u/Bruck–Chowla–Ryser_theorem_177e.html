Bruck–Chowla–Ryser theorem

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

You have new messages (last change).

The Bruck–Chowla–Ryser theorem is a result on the combinatorics of block designs. It states that if a (v; k; λ) design exists with λ(v-1)=k(k-1) (a symmetric design), then:

k − λ is a square,

when v is even; and the diophantine equation

x2 − (k − λ)y2 − (−1)(v−1)/2 λ z2 = 0

has a nontrivial solution, when v is odd.
