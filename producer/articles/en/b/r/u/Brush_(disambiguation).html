Brush (disambiguation)

The word brush may refer to:

  • A brush, a device with bristles, wire or other filament of any possible material used mainly for cleaning, grooming hair, painting, deburring and other kinds of surface finishing
  • A hairbrush
  • A toothbrush
  • A toilet brush
  • A Pastry brush or basting brush
  • Drum brushes are a special type of drum sticks used for smoother playing.
  • A verb, meaning the act of using a brush or hairbrush
  • Untamed grassy areas, as well as so-called "Transitional Zones"—areas between canopy forests and field type habitats
  • In golf, it is synonymous with the "rough"—the area outside the fairway
  • In 3D computer game development, brushes are the solid, in many game engines convex, polyhedrons that make up the level geometry
  • In electronics, a copper wire brush conducts current
  • A large, bushy tail of an animal (particularly a fox) is sometimes called a brush
  • Brush, Colorado is a town in the USA
  • Brush Turbogenerators is a part of FKI and a UK manufacturer of generators, for gas turbine and steam turbine drive.
  • Brush Electrical Machines is a UK manufacturer of generators, for gas turbine and steam turbine drive.
  • Brush HMA based in Holland is a manufacturer of generators, for gas turbine and steam turbine drive.
  • Brush SEM based in Czech Republic is a manufacturer of generators, for gas turbine and steam turbine drive.
  • Brush Traction is a UK manufacturer of railway locomotives
  • Brush was the name of a Great British royal Pembroke Welsh Corgi, belonging to Queen Elizabeth II
  • Brush Motor Car Company, an early 20th century United States automobile company
  • The paintbrush has appeared as a charge in heraldry
  • Paintbrush may also refer to the digital equivalent one would find in a bitmap graphics editor, i.e. a virtual brush that can modify a digital picture
  • Brush Creek
  • Brush Engineered Materials, a miner, refiner, and manufacturer of engineered metals, particularly beryllium and beryllium alloys
