Brunonian system of medicine

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

The Brunonian system of medicine is a theory of medicine which regards and treats diseases as caused by defective or excessive excitation. It was developed by John Brown and is outlined in his 1780 publication Elementa Medicinae. The theory was for a time popular.
