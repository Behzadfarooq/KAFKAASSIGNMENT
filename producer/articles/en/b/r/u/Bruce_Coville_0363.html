Bruce Coville (b.May 16, 1950) is an American author of children's stories. He was born in Syracuse, New York and lived around central New York except for one year he spent a Duke University. In September 1990, he moved to New York city and lived there for two years. After that he moved back to Syracuse. His books generally fall into the fantasy and science fiction genres, full of personable and humorous characters. His books stress values like saving the environment and fair treatment of others.

[edit] Early Career

Like everyone, he wasn't able to start selling his stories right away. He had other jobs along the way like a toymaker, gravedigger, cookware salesman, and assembly line worker. He eventually became an elementary teacher, and worked with second and fourth graders.

[edit] Career

His first published story was The Foolish Giant. He is most famous for his Space Brat, My Teacher is an Alien, Aliens Ate My Homework, and Magic Shop series. Other lesser-known series include: Camp Haunted Hills, I Was a Sixth Grade Alien, The Unicorn Chronicles, and The A.I. Gang.

The "Sixth Grade Alien" series was illustrated by Tony Sansevero, and was eventually the basis for a television series on YTV.

Mr. Coville has also written picture books, retellings of William Shakespeare, and three children's plays - The Dragonslayers, Out of the Blue, and It's Midnight, Do You Know Where Your Toys Are?, all musicals. He also co-wrote the young adult novel Armageddon Summer with Jane Yolen.

He is the founder of Full Cast Audio, an audio book company for children's and young adult books that uses full casts rather than solo readings.

[edit] Early Life

Bruce grew up around the corner from his grandparents' dairy farm, near the village of Phoenix, New York. He loved to read children's classics such as Mary Poppins and Doctor Dolittle, along with popular titles like Nancy Drew, The Hardy Boys, Tom Swift, and many comic books. He regrets that he sometimes watched TV when he could of been reading. "After all, mind is a terrible thing to waste!" he says. The first time he can remember thinking to become a writer was in sixth grade. His teacher, Mrs. Crandall, gave his class an extended period of time to write a long story. He loved doing it. He started to work seriously at becoming a writer at the age of seventeen. When he was 19 he married Katherine Dietz. She lived right around the corner from him. She was and still is a wonderful artist and they tried making books together. In 1997 they sold their first book together titled The Foolish Giant. They did two other books together—Sarah’s Unicorn and The Monster’s Ring.

[edit] Family

They have three children:a son born in 1970 (Orion), a daughter born in 1975 (Cara), and another son born in 1981 named Adam. Bruce and Kathy have lived apart for many years, but they are currently sharing a house again. They are working on new projects, such as the Moongobble and Me series. "I feel like a very lucky person. From the time I was young, I had a dream of becoming a writer. Now that dream has come true, and I am able to make my living doing something that I really love."
