Bruno Šundov (born February 10, 1980 in Split, Socialist Federal Republic of Yugoslavia (present-day Croatia)) is a Croatian professional basketball player most notable for his NBA career.

Šundov was born and grew up in Split, in the former Yugoslavia. He first distinguished himself in the 1998 International Junior Tournament won by the Croatian national junior team. He later won the silver medal at the 2001 World Championship for 22-and-under.

He was a second round draft pick of the Dallas Mavericks in the 1998 NBA Draft and has played for a number of NBA teams: the Mavericks, Indiana Pacers, Boston Celtics, New York Knicks, New Orleans Hornets and Cleveland Cavaliers. He has averaged 1.7 points and one rebound per game over his NBA career.

He signed with Maccabi Tel Aviv, Israel in February 2004, and then to Pepinster in Belgium in May 2004. He returned to the New York Knicks in September 2004 as a free agent.

In September 2005, Šundov signed with Etosa Alicante, located in Alicante, Spain.
