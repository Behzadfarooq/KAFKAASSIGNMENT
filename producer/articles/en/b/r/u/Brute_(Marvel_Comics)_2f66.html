The Brute (Henry "Hank" McCoy) is a comic book superhero created by Marvel Comics. He is an alternate reality version of the X-Men's Beast. In his reality he leaves the X-Men with fellow teammates to form their own team known as The Six.

[edit] Fictional character biography

In the main Marvel Universe the X-Man Havok attempted to stop his friend Greystone from using a faulty time machine to return to his native timeline. However, the device exploded, killing both men...or so it seemed. Havok found himself transported to an alternate reality where he was the leader of a hero group called The Six, where he met alternate versions of his friends and family. One of his teammates was a differently mutated Hank McCoy, who called himself the Brute in this reality.

As a young scientist, Hank McCoy went to work for the Brand Corporation and was able to isolate a chemical catalyst for mutation. He used this chemical to mutate his body, but it went farther than expected. Instead of having the furry simian-like form he was mutated into a green, amphibious form and, as a consequence, lost his intelligence. As well as having superb agility and strength, Hank had webbed hands and could breathe underwater.

He became further mutated during the Inferno crisis, when he made a deal with the demons S'ym and N'astirh. But instead of helping him, the demons turned his lower body into that of a goat.

Havok and his group had a disagreement with Magneto's leadership and split off into their own team, Hank choosing to stay with his friends, his lack of intelligence sometimes being a liability. For instance, the team once crashed in the Canadian wilderness and found themselves being hunted by members of The Pack (feral versions of Wolverine, Sabretooth, and Wild Child). The Brute had difficulty resisting his animal nature around them, even temporarily defecting to the Pack. However, in every instance his strength and fierce loyalty were an asset.

When the Goblyn Queen first ascended to power, she manipulated all the members of the Six (with the exception of Fallen who joined willingly) into serving her purpose. She used Brute's feral side against him, making him a near-mindless beast in her service. However, the team broke free of her influence and was able to defeat her, choosing to remain with Havok and reform the team with the new goal of saving mutants from the crazed Nick Fury and SHIELD.

Later, in Mutant X #23, Brute jumps in front of a psychic blast from an evil Professor X that was meant for Havok. This had the unexpected effect of restoring his intellect, and he remembered what had initially caused him to lose it. The Havok who was native to the reality had been having an illicit affair with Sue Storm of the Fantastic Four, cheating on his wife Madelyne Pryor. Maddie found solace in a meaningful friendship with Ice-Man, whom she couldn't be romantically involved with due to the nature of his powers. Havok ended his affair and became increasingly jealous of Ice-Man. Havok sabotaged the Brute's experiment which would have returned Ice-Man to his normal form. The experiment exploded in the Brute's face, giving him his childlike manner. With his intellect fully restored, the Brute tried to find a way to return Havok to his reality, to cure—as best he could—Bloodstorm and Gambit of their vampirism and to restore Ice-Man. Brute succeeded in all, but Havok opted not to return to the main Marvel Universe, as he had grown too close to his teammates. Sadly, the Brute's intelligence once more was lost as the after-effects of Xavier's blast wore off.

With the near destruction of the universe and the closing events of the Mutant X series, it is unknown what became of the Brute. He was attacked by that reality's Dracula, and was hanging on by a thread. He was still between life and death when Havok left that reality, and it is unknown if he lived or died.

[edit] Powers and abilities

The Brute is gifted with incredible agility, speed, dexterity, stamina, increased healing ability and strength—all of which can be augmented when he is in a "berserker rage". Physically he has webbing between his fingers, sharp claws and teeth, green fur and scales on parts of his chest and face, and his lower abdomen and legs have been transformed into that of a goat. He is also capable of breathing underwater.
