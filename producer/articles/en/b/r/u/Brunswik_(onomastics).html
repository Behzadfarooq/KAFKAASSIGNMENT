[edit] Etymology and Places

[edit] Braunschweig

  • the City (Großstadt) of Braunschweig ([bráUnšvajk]), or Brunswiek/Brunswīk (Low German, Low Saxon), Brunswick (Anglicised form from Middle Age), Lower Saxony (Niedersachsen)
Brūn-s-wīk meant originally "Master Bruno's wick/village, territory".

English Old word Wick (OE wīc ; LG wīk/wiek ; Latin vīc-us ; PIE *weik-) remains English place names like that Warwick, Berwick, Greenwich, Hampton Wick, etc.

Then, these word have not any relationship with German words like Schweigen, Schwegel, schwach, Švejk(Schweik).. etc.
