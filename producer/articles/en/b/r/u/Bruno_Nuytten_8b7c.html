Bruno Nuytten

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Bruno Nuytten (born August 28, 1945 in Melun, Seine-et-Marne, Île-de-France) is a French cinematographer-turned-director who directed Camille Claudel. Nuytten is a Flemish last name that can appear under several different forms.

His films as cinematographer include Les Valseuses, Barocco, The Bronte Sisters, Brubaker, Garde à vue, Possession, Fort Saganne, So Long, Stooge, Jean de Florette and Manon des Sources. He won the César Award for Best Cinematography in 1977 and 1984.
