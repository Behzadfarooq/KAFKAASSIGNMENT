The Book

Crack open a copy of Crooked Lake by Nelson Brunanski and you'll discover a romping account of life, love and murder on the Canadian Prairies. The drama unfolds in a small town in Saskatchewan where every nosy neighbor is an amateur sleuth and accusatory tongues wag freely down at coffee row.

When a man is murdered on the shores of Crooked Lake, easy-going Bart Bartowski sets aside work at his fly-in-fishing lodge to come to the aid of his childhood friend whose been accused of the crime. Tempted by a beautiful young cottager and beguiled by the murdered man’s wife, Bart is dogged by RCMP detectives who want him to stay out of what they consider an open and shut case. In his perilous pursuit of whodunit, Bart reveals the heart of a small town, taking a stab at its complacency and laying bare some of its juiciest moral dilemma.

The Author

Nelson Brunanski grew up in a town much like his fictional town, Crooked Lake. His prairie roots are much in evidence in his first work of fiction. As a young man, he headed for the coast, but his memories of small town life on the Prairies never left him. Brunanski received a B.A., B.Ed. and went on to a career teaching English. He won a mystery writers’ competition at the Festival of Words in Moose Jaw, SK (‘05). He spoke at the Shuswap Lake International Writers Festival in June. The response to Crooked Lake has been very positive both from readers and reviewers. Brunanski is currently at work on a sequel called Frost Bite. He lives with his wife Carole Clement at Inspiration Cove, Victoria, BC Canada.
