Bruno (died 26 May 1045) was prince-bishop of Würzburg from 1034 until his death. He was the son of Conrad I, Duke of Carinthia, and served as counselor to his relative, Conrad II, Holy Roman Emperor.

Bruno was not formally canonized by the Roman Catholic Church, but is revered as a saint. His feast day is May 27.
