Brunswick is a railway station in Melbourne, Victoria, Australia, located in the suburb of Brunswick, on the Upfield railway line. It is in Metcard Zone 1.

Brunswick station is unmanned.

[edit] Location

The station is located at the southern end of the Victoria Street level crossing and the northern end of the Albert Street level crossing. Tram route 19 is a short walk from the station, on Sydney Road.

[edit] Facilities

The station consists of two side platforms. Platform 1 contains a large red brick building while platform 2 contains a small brick building. A large Metcard machine is located at the entrance to platform 1, which is able to dispense most ticketing options available and also accept notes and coins. A small Metcard machine is located at the entrance to platform 2. A disused signal box is located at the DOWN end of the station. When the Host Station service was first announced, Brunswick was announced to be a host station in early media advertisements. However as of 2006 Brunswick has yet to become a host station.

Both platforms can be accessed via Victoria Street and Albert Street.

[edit] History

Brunswick station originally opened on Tuesday, 9th September 1884.
