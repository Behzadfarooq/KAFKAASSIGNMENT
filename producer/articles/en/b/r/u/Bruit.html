Bruit (pronounced (IPA) either /bru.i/ or /brut/) is the term for the unusual sound that blood makes when it rushes past an obstruction in an artery when the sound is observed with the bell portion of a stethoscope.

The location of the stethoscope when the sound is observed can affect the diagnosis.

[edit] Associated conditions

  • atherosclerosis
  • risk of stroke
  • renal artery stenosis
  • carotid artery disease
  • aortic aneurysm
  • Tinnitus - Objective Tinnitus may sometimes be due to this condition.
  • Polymyalgia rheumatica
  • Giant cell arteritis
