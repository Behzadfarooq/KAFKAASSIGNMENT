During the 2006-07 season, Bristol Rovers are playing in Coca-Cola League 2. There has been a subdued atmosphere among fans, caused by six previous seasons of poor performances and a bitter boardroom split, resulting in four directors leaving the club.[1][2]

[edit] Chronological list of events

  • 28 July: Played a pre-season friendly against Plymouth Argyle wearing pink shirts.[3]
  • 22 August: Knocked out of the Carling Cup at the first round by Luton Town on penalties, having drawn the match 1-1.[4]
  • 30 August: Junior Agogo leaves to join Nottingham Forest for an undisclosed fee.[5]
  • 31 August: Rickie Lambert is signed for a fee of £200,000 on transfer deadline day from Rochdale.[6]
  • 15 September: The club submit plans for a new 18,000-seat stadium to Bristol City Council.[7]
  • 26 September: Signed James Walker on loan from Charlton Athletic[8]
  • 27 September: Rovers record their first win in a home midweek evening game for almost two years. The 2-1 win over Hereford United was the first since beating Chester City 4-1 on 7 December 2004. This was also the first game this season where Rovers scored more than one goal in the match.[9]
  • 6 October: Chairman Geoff Dunford announces that he will step down from his position at the end of April 2007, but intends to remain as a director of the club.[10]
  • 7 October: Back to back home wins recorded for the first time since May 2005, thanks to a 1-0 win over Boston United following on from the victory over Hereford United the previous week.[9]
  • 9 October: Kevin Hodges is appointed as head of youth football. He also takes charge of the reserve team as part of his duties.[11]
  • 16 October: Rod King is appointed to the club's board of directors.[12]
  • 17 October: Former player Carl Saunders is appointed as the club's Community Liaison Officer.[13]
  • 26 October: Scott Shearer leaves to join Shrewsbury Town on loan for three months.[14] The loan is later extended until the end of the season.[15]
  • 27 October: Jamal Easter joins Rovers on a month's loan from Cardiff City.[16] The loan was later extended for a further month. [17]
  • 11 November: Sean Rigg suffers a broken jaw after being punched by James Cotterill during an FA Cup first round match at Barrow.[18]
  • 15 November: Stuart Nicholson joins Bristol Rovers on a one-month loan from West Bromwich Albion,[19] which was later extended to a three-month deal.[20]
  • 8 December: Ollie Barnes joins Gloucester City on a one month loan.[21]
  • 10 January: Rovers receive £50,000 as part of the deal taking Scott Sinclair to Chelsea[22] after he makes his debut for the London club.[23]
  • 15 January: James Hunt joins Grimsby Town on a month's loan,[24] later extended to three months.[25]
  • 17 January: Bristol City Council grant planning permission for a new 18,000 capacity all-seater stadium to be built on the site of the Memorial Stadium.[26]
  • 19 January: Stuart Nicholson is recalled from his loan four weeks early by West Brom.[27]
  • 27 January: Rovers knocked out of the FA Cup in the fourth round by Derby County.[28]
  • 31 January: Stuart Nicholson returns to Rovers for a second loan spell, this time until the end of the season.[29]
  • 1 February: Robbie Ryan's contract with the club was terminated by mutual consent.[30]
  • 15 February: Rovers sign Joe Jacobson on loan from Cardiff City until the end of the season.[31]
  • 16 February: Rovers make their second loan signing in as many days, signing Samuel Oji from Birmingham City on a one month contract.[32] The deal was later extended by a further month.[33]
  • 27 February: Rovers reach the final of the Johnstone's Paint Trophy by beating rivals Bristol City 1-0 on aggregate over the two legs of the southern area final.[34]
  • 29 March: Rovers announce that Ron Craig will succeed Geoff Dunford as chairman of the club from 2 April 2007.[35]
