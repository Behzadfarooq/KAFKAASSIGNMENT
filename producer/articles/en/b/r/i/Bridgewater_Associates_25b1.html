Bridgewater Associates is a global investment manager that manages approximately $163 billion in assets. Approximately $30 billion is invested in the firm's hedge fund product, Pure Alpha, making it one of the largest hedge funds in the world. Bridgewater began investment operations in 1975 and was located first in New York, New York, then in Wilton, Connecticut; it is now located in Westport, Connecticut.

Bridgewater manages portfolios for more than 180 clients from the United States and 19 other countries. These clients are mainly pension funds, endowments, foundations, foreign governments, and central banks. Bridgewater has approximately 390 employees. About a third of the employees work in research and trading.

[edit] Philosophy

Ray Dalio, the founder, President, and Chief Investment Officer of Bridgewater Associates, has established the company's philosophy as the uncompromising pursuit of excellence. Accordingly, the company is a meritocracy where the best ideas are acted upon, regardless of their source. Hierarchy is minimal, and it only exists to provide structure (rather than subordination).
