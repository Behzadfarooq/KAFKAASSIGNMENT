Britain's Next Top Model, Cycle 2 is a UK reality television show that is broadcast on LIVINGtv. It is based on the same concept as America's Next Top Model.

[edit] Episode summary

Week 1

The competition begins. The first photo shoot takes place in the Milton Keynes SnowZone with the girls forced to withstand temperatures as low as minus 5 degrees. Immediatley Lianna feels like an outsider, not getting along with other girls.

For this cycle, the host was changed to Lisa Snowdon due to Lisa Butcher's wooden performance in Cycle 1. Also Marie Helvin was replaced with Paula Hamilton. Jonathon Phang remains as a judge

  • Yvette was eliminated

Week 2

The girls get their makeovers and the inevitable tears and tantrums follow. The girls pair up for a girl on girl photo shoot.

  • Nina was eliminated

Week 3

The girls learn about cheese cake pin-up and dancing from Britain's own 'Queen of Burlesque' Immodesty Blaize. The models try to relax and have their own 'Oscar Night'. And two girls receive harsh criticism from casting director Beth Charkham when faced with the challenge of a commercial set in a swimming pool.

  • Asha was eliminated.

Week 4

The remaining models learn about ballroom dancing from top ballroom dancer Brendan Cole and Lianna struggles to get it right. Then they do a full dance with music. One girl gets upset when not chosen by the winner to join her for her reward.

  • Lucy was eliminated.

Week 5

The girls train with personal trainer Chris Mundle. Then straight after training, sweating and smelling, they head to meet top designers. They have a ‘Gothic Bride’ photo shoot, with most girls struggling to get a good shot.

  • Sophia was eliminated.

Week 6

The remaining models undergo a heavy metallic make-up photo shoot with John Swanell. Jasmia struggles with the shoot feeling self-conscious. Lisa arrives at the house and the girls have to critique each other, which causes even more fights. A practical joke gets taken the wrong way.

  • Samantha was eliminated.

Week 7

The girls shoot a thirty second jewellery promotion with top promo director Jason Clifford. Lucy, last years winner, visits the girls and gives them some tips. They have a fake press conference for a new lipgloss. They have a country life photo shoot and there is a shock at elimination.

  • Both Tamar and Georgina were eliminated

Week 8

With two people gone the house becomes even more divided. The girls meet Saffron Aldridge, top style expert, who goes through their wardrobes with no mercy. They have a one on one with Lisa and some take the opportunity to bring others down. They have a photo shoot covering each other with paint, which some see as revenge. And the girls are awe-struck when they meet a famous model.

  • Sarah was eliminated.

Week 9

The four remaining models head to Morocco and Lisa gives them the day off. They are given the challenge of finding an outfit for Lisa. They have their first Moroccan photo shoot and the final three are chosen.

  • Amber was eliminated.

Week 10

In the finale the three remaining models have their final photo shoot and the final two compete in a catwalk show and one girl is crowned 'Britain's Next Top Model'.

  • Lianna is 'Britain's Next Top Model'.
  • Jasmia and Abigail were eliminated. Jasmia came third and Abigail came second

[edit] And The Winner Is...

Final
The judges, along with Abigail and Lianna, watch clips of the catwalk show and both girls begin to cry, becoming very emotional over the thought of winning as their performances are assessed. The girls are then asked separately why they feel they should become Britain's Next Top Model and are asked to leave the room as the judges take one last look at the girls' best photos and ultimately decide who will be the winner. The girls return to the room and are told that the winner's final photo will appear on the screen in ten seconds... And Lianna is crowned Britain's Next Top Model.

Lianna has now won a modelling contract with Models1, one of Britain's leading modelling agencies. She has also got a campaign for the new Ford Fiesta as well as the car itself and she will also appear on the cover of the October edition of Company magazine in 2006, with an interview about life before and after winning the show.

In October 2006 Livingtv revealed a third series would be commissioned due to the success of the Second series and Application Forms were available from the livingtv website.
