Brian D. Ingoldsby (1939 â€“ 2005) was a forty-year recording industry verteran. Brian was with MCA Records in Universal City until 1974 when he was asked to serve as President of the American Broadcasting Company (ABC), Recording Studios Division until 1979. Prior to this, Brian designed and built his own studio in North Hollywood that was subsequently purchased by Warner Brothers.

Elton John, Mick Jagger, Cher, Olivia Newton-John, Neil Diamond, Eric Clapton, Neil Young, Al Kooper, Pink Frank Zappa, and Joe Cocker are just some of the artists Brian had gold and platinum records with. He also toured the nation and abroad with some of these artists, mixing their live concerts in stadiums, halls, colleges and night clubs.

Ingoldsby invented and patented many audio units for the recording industry. His Phasing and Doubling devices are famous and widely used. He also contributed to many industry journal articles and lectured at colleges across the nation.

Ingoldsby was an honorary member of the prestigious Audio Engineering Society. He also established the Sound Master Recording Engineering School in 1972 to train students in the science and art of audio engineering.

He died in January 2005 from complications following a liver transplant.
