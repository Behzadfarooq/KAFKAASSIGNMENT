Today the Endicott Estate is used by community organizations and rented out for private functions. The James Joyce Ramble, one of the top ten 10k road races in the nation, begins and ends at the Estate and the Dedham Junior Women's Club holds a weekly concert on the grounds there each summer for families. In back of the house, on Mount Vernon Street, the nine car garage has been turned into a branch of the Dedham Public Library.[1] The Estate listed on the National Register of Historic Places.[2] and has been featured in the films State and Main and The Perfect Storm in addition to appearing in the television series Spencer for Hire.

Nobles today is a private boarding school for students in grades seven through twelve sits on a and today it serves 525 boys and girls in grades 7-12, including almost 50 who board at the school during the week and go home for weekends.[3] Of the 80 faculty members, 30 live on campus and the school has an endowment of $72 million.[4]

Today Ursuline has nearly 400 girls in grades 7-12 is classes with an average class size of 20 and a student teacher ratio of 11:1. Ten varsity level sports are offered throughout the year and in keeping with the Ursuline's motto "Serviam: I will serve" all students must serve between 30 and 40 hours of community service a year. In addition, seniors leave school three weeks early so that they may volunteer full time.[5]

Dedham pottery is a cherished class of antiques, characterized by a distinctive crackle glaze, blue-and-white color scheme, and a frequent motif of rabbits and other animals.
