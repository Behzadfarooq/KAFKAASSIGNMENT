A tag has been placed on TalkImpact, requesting that it be speedily deleted from Wikipedia. In response to your question on how to save the page: First, you need to check out WP:WEB to see the criteria for keeping pages on web sites. If TalkImpact does not meet one of the three criteria on the page, there is nothing that you can do, and the page will be deleted. If it does meet one of the criteria, put a {{hangon}} tag on the TalkImpact page and describe how it meets one of the criteria on the talk page. Then, edit the TalkImpact page to show how it meets the criteria. Most importantly, you have to cite sources that demonstrat that TalkImpact meets one of the criteria. WP:CITE explains how to cite sources.--FreeKresge 05:28, 3 January 2007 (UTC)


A tag has been placed on TalkImpact, requesting that it be speedily deleted from Wikipedia. This has been done because the article seems to be about a person, group of people, band, club, company, or web content, but it does not indicate how or why the subject is notable, that is, why an article about that subject should be included in Wikipedia. Under the criteria for speedy deletion, articles that do not assert notability may be deleted at any time. Please see the guidelines for what is generally accepted as notable, and if you can indicate why the subject of this article is notable, you may contest the tagging. To do this, add {{hangon}} on the top of the page and leave a note on the article's talk page explaining your position. Please do not remove the speedy deletion tag yourself, but don't hesitate to add information to the article that would confirm its subject's notability under the guidelines.

For guidelines on specific types of articles, you may want to check out our criteria for biographies, for web sites, for bands, or for companies. Feel free to leave a note on my talk page if you have any questions about this. Kinu t/c 06:38, 3 January 2007 (UTC)
