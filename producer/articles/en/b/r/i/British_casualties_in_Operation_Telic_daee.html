British casualties in Operation Telic

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Since the start of operations in Iraq, 140 British service personnel have died (5 of them females)[1] and many more have been wounded by hostile action. Over 4,000 personnel have been evacuated for medical reasons since the start of operations and over 2,000 have received treatment for pychological problems. Of the 140 fatalities, 109 are classified as having been killed due to enemy action with the remaining 31 dying as a result of accidents, illness or suicide. The first casualties were sustained on 21 March 2003.
