British Atomic Scientists Association

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

The British Atomic Scientists Association (ASA or BASA), was founded by Joseph Rotblat in 1946. It was a politically neutral group, comprised of eminent physicists and other scientists and was concerned with matters of British public policy regarding applications and dangers of nuclear physics (including nuclear weapons and nuclear power).

In so doing it also sought to inform fellow scientists and the public of the essential facts, usually via published papers and other documents.

[edit] Members

The vice-president (VP) was the executive head while the president (P) was the honorary position.

  • Kathleen Lonsdale (VP, P 1967)
  • Harrie Massey
  • Nevill Mott
  • Joseph Rotblat (VP 1946)
  • Basil Schonland
