Bristol Mountain Snowsports Resort is a resort located in South Bristol, New York in the Finger Lakes region. It is approximately a 20 minute drive from the center of Canandaigua, the nearest city, to the resort. Currently, Bristol Mountain Resort features 33 slopes/trails ranging from easiest (green circle) to extremely difficult (double black diamonds). Two one-mile-long terrain parks are the largest in the region.

Bristol Mountain Resort also owns Roseland Waterpark, open during the summer season in Canandaigua. This resort is located on a private lake with wave pool, raft rides, body flumes, tube slides and much more.

[edit] Services

  • 33 trails/slopes ranging from easiest (35%) to extremely difficult (20%)
  • 2 lodges featuring multiple full food service amenities
  • Peak elevation at 2200 feet and base at 1000 feet yielding a ski/ride vertical of 1200 feet
  • 6 lifts (1 Garaventa-CTEC hi-speed detachable quad, 1 fixed quad, two triples, 1 CTEC double, 1 surface tow)
  • Snowmaking available on 97% of all skiable terrain
  • Trail lighting coverage of 96%
  • 450 foot long in-ground halfpipe
  • Longest run: 2 miles
  • 2 terrain parks (entry level and advance level)
  • Ski Magazine Summit Demo Center (over 300 skis & Burton boardsâ€”intermediate & above skill demoing)
  • Elan "Get Skiing" program for beginners and Elan rental program for all level skiers
  • Burton "Learn to Ride" program for women, children and freestyle along with general rental program
  • Children's Learning Center, Certified Child Care Center
  • Ski & Sport Retail Store, Tuning Center
  • Nationally (PSIA) certified Professional Ski & Snowboard School (up to 250 instructors/coaches)
  • Nationally (NSPS) certified Ski Patrol

[edit] More Mountain Facts

  • Averages 130" of snowfall per year
  • 24-40" average base
  • Highest vertical between the Adirondacks and the Rockies
  • Zogg Pipe Groomer
  • 5 Trac based Mountain Groomers
  • Fan Snowguns: 52 tower, 17 carriage fan guns
  • Air/Water Snowguns: 174 tower mounted, 36 sled mounted
  • 16 miles of pipe
  • 1 mile of hose
  • 396 Hydrant Stations
  • 4 state-of the-art computers which monitor/manage outdoor weather stations, air and water flows to the guns, temperatures of the incoming water, power meters, etc.
  • Two web cams (one at the base and one at the summit) visible on web site
  • Weatherbug Weather Station/Cam at the summit, visible on web site
  • Facilities for business conferences/meetings, weddings, picnics, group outings year round
  • Slope Side Lodging (Northstar Village)
  • Municipal water and sewage systems
