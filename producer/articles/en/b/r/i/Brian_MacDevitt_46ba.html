Brian MacDevitt

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Brian MacDevitt is a lighting designer for theatrical productions. He has worked extensively on Broadway and Off Broadway, as well as touring, Regional theatre, and Industrial productions.

A Long Island, New York, native, Brian graduated from SUNY Purchase with a degree in Lighting Design from the Department of Design/Technology of the Division of Theatre Arts & Film. After graduation Brian spent a decade honing his craft with Off Broadway and other productions, and also developed a reputation as a teacher of design. He began teaching at Purchase as a visiting professor in 1986, when his students included David Grill. He continued to balance his teaching career while breaking into Broadway in 1994 with "What's Wrong With This Picture". Brian started to achieve notice with theTerrence McNally play Love! Valour! Compassion!, a transfer from Manhattan Theatre Club in 1995. His success continued through the 1990's, and eventually culminated with a Tony Award in 2002 for the revival of Into the Woods. Brian has been nominated for a total of six Tony's winning again for Best Lighting Design for a Play in 2005 for The Pillowman.

Brian continues to teach lighting design at such schools as SUNY Purchase, New York University (NYU), and Yale University
