British air forces in the Falklands War

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

This is a list of the units, aircraft and casualties of the British air forces in the Falklands War. The numbers in bold are the number of aircraft used in the war, the numbers in brackets are the number of lost aircraft. For a list of air forces from Argentina, see Argentine air forces in the Falklands War.

[edit] Royal Air Force

All four engined heavies operated from Wideawake Airfield, Ascension Island, but flew in the war zone.

  • No. 1(F) Squadron - (Harrier GR.Mk.3) 10 (4) (HMS Hermes & Port San Carlos)
  • No. 18 Squadron - (Chinook HC.Mk.1) 4 (3) +1 at Wideawake
  • No. 29 Squadron - (Phantom FGR.Mk.2) 3; only at Wideawake

[edit] Armament

  • L44A1 GPMG (General Purpose Machine Gun)
  • 30 mm ADEN Cannon
  • * AIM-9D/G Sidewinder Air-to-Air Missile (AAM)]] F-4 at Wideawake
  • AIM-9L Sidewinder AAM
  • AGM-45 Shrike Air-to-Surface Anti-radiation missile (ARM)
  • BAe Sea Skua Air-to-Surface anti-ship missile
  • Aerospatiale AS.12 Air-to-Surface missile
  • Paveway Laser-guided bomb (LGB)
  • BL755 Cluster bomb
  • 1,000 lb General Purpose Bomb
  • CRV-7 2.75in Folding Fin Rockets
  • Mk. 46 Torpedo
  • Mk. 11 Depth charge

[edit] Casualties and aircraft losses

  • Human losses:
    • 2 Army Air Corps[1]
    • 4 3 Commando Brigade Air Squadron Royal Marines
    • 17 Fleet Air Arm
  • Passengers
    • 1 Royal Air Force [2]
    • 18 Special Air Service(Army) [2]
    • 3 Royal Corps of Signals(Army) [2][1]
  • Aircraft Lost in the Air:, no suffix: Fleet Air Arm
    • 3 Westland Gazelle AH.Mk.1 (Army) and Royal Marines [3]
    • 4 Harrier GR.Mk.3 (RAF)
    • 2 Sea Harrier FRS.Mk.1
    • 1 Scout AH.Mk.1 (Royal Marines)
  • Flying accidents in the war zone
    • 4 Sea Harrier FRS.Mk.1
    • 2 Westland Sea King HC.Mk.4
    • 2 Westland Sea King HAS.Mk.5
    • 2 Westland Wessex HU.Mk.5
  • Lost onboard a ship
    • 3 Chinook HC.Mk.1 RAF[4]
    • 3 Westland Lynx HAS.Mk.2
    • 1 Westland Wessex HAS.Mk.3
    • 6 Westland Wessex HU.Mk.5 [4]
  • Self-destruct in Chile
    • 1 Westland Sea King HC.Mk.4
