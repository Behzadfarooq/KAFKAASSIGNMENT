Bridgeland Community is a master-planned community under construction in unincorporated Harris County, Texas to the northwest of Houston between U.S. Highway 290 and Interstate 10. Bridgeland is being developed by General Growth Properties, whose previous work includes The Woodlands, Texas, Columbia, Maryland, and Summerlin, Nevada.

Bridgeland is planned to house approximately 65,000 people by completion.[1]

[edit] Education

The area of Bridgeland is served by the Cy-Fair, Katy, and Waller school districts.

[edit] Cypress-Fairbanks ISD

Based upon current (as of 2006) attendance zones, students in the CFISD portion will attend:

  • Robison Elementary School
  • Spillane Middle School
  • Cypress Woods High School

The school district purchased a 130 acre multi-school site directly across from the main entrance to the development on Fry Road. The future schools are

  • Warner Elementary School (planned for August 2007)
  • Cypress Ranch High School (planned for 2008)

[edit] Neighborhoods

  • The Shores: The Shores is located off of North Bridgeland Lake Parkway near the entrance to Bridgeland off of Fry Road. The Shores is located in the Lakeland Village of Bridgeland. The first residents of The Shores arrived in June 2006. At its completion it will have 571 homes. The neighborhood is located across the street from Oak Meadow Park and Cypress Creek Trail. House Hahl Trail also runs through the neighborhood.
  • First Bend: First Bend is located off of North Bridgeland Lake Parkway near the entrance to Bridgeland off of Fry Road. First Bend is located in the Parkland Village of Bridgeland. The construction of the 400 homes to be located in this neighborhood begins in 2007.
