Brian Holman

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Brian Holman (born on January 25, 1965, in Denver) is a retired Major League Baseball pitcher. He pitched for the Montreal Expos (1988-1989) and the Seattle Mariners from 1989 to 1991.

Holman was sent along with Randy Johnson and Gene Harris to the Mariners for Mark Langston and a player to be named later (Mike Campbell).

On April 20, 1990 against the Oakland Athletics, Holman retired the first 26 batters he faced before Ken Phelps hit a home run and turned his perfect game into a one-hitter. At the time, no Mariner had pitched a no-hitter.

His brother Brad Holman was also a MLB pitcher.
