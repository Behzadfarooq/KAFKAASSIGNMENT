Brian "Buck" Buchanan is an outfielder for the Fukuoka SoftBank Hawks of the Pacific League of Japanese professional baseball. He once played for the St. Paul Saints of the American Association of Independent Professional Baseball. He played in Major League Baseball from 2000 through 2004. He was drafted by the New York Yankees in the first round (24th overall) of the 1994 amateur draft. In February 1998, he was traded to the Minnesota Twins in the trade that sent Chuck Knoblauch to the Yankees. In July 2002, he was traded to the San Diego Padres for current Twins starting shortstop Jason Bartlett. In parts of five seasons in the majors, he hit .258 in 767 at bats, belting 32 home runs in the process. After 2006 season, he joined the Fukuoka SoftBank Hawks in Japan after 2 years out of Major League Baseball
