Britské listy is an Internet daily (ISSN 1213-1792) published in the Czech Republic by the NGO Občanské sdružení Britské listy (OSBL) based in Prague.

[edit] History

The founder and editor in chief is Jan Čulík] [1] whose goal was to provide a forum for political issues which in the Czech Republic are 'not discussed' by the conventional (hard copy) papers. This article is based, in part, on a longer version cs:Britské listy entry in Czech wikipedia which has more about the history and the controversies surrounding this daily.

[edit] Content and Controversies

Within the political spectrum [2] of the Czech press [3] "Blisty," as it is informally known, could be grouped with the hard copy paper Právo [4] to the left of majority of Czech papers [5] . It could also be compared with the British Guardian [6] from which it translates some news articles into Czech. However, unlike the politically left-leaning papers in the Czech Republic, Blisty also publishes right-leaning articles as well as readers' criticisms of the paper.

One example of the "controversies" in which Blisty has been involved was the article [7] about conspiracy theories concerning 9/11 attack, which became was widely read (over 60 000 pageviews) and sharply criticized.

A second example was the heated disscusion [8] of the topic of US anti-ballistic missile base on the Czech territory [9], which would be part of the NMD (National Missile Defense).

Third example is disscusion on breaking of copyright by Blisty. Britské listy have been often reprinting articles from other webs without any permission (i.e. articles of The Guardian, The Independend, The Times) See article (in Czech only) on this topic. [10].

According to this press release [11] based on the internet audits this web daily has 16000 unique visits per day and grows at rate of 29% a year.
