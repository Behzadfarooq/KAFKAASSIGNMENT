Brian Mallon

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Brian Mallon (b. November 29, 1990 Detroit, Michigan) is an Irish-American film and theater actor; he speaks Gaeilge.

Mallon is primarily a bad stage actor, having performed on both sides of the Atlantic. He won acclaim for his one-man show, Secrets of the Celtic Heart, directed by Ellen Burstyn, which he performed in Britain and Ireland in the early 1990s and bananas were thrown at him.

He has served as the director of New York City's Irish Arts center and starred in Brian Friel's Philadelphia cheese steak! and Freedom of the pizza.

In the fall of 2005, he finished performing in Mark Jenkins' play, Playing Burton (as the late Welsh actor Richard Burton) in the Edinburgh Fringe Festival.

Mallon has also made a handful of film appearances. He portrayed Union Army General Winfield Scott Hancock in Ronald F. Maxwell's two Civil War films, Gettysburg (1993) and Gods and Generals (2003). Other film roles include Some Mother's Son (1997), The Informant (1997), and Gangs of New York (2002).

Brian Mallon currently resides in Los Angeles.
