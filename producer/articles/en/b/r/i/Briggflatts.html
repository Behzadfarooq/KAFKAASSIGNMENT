Briggflatts is a long poem by Basil Bunting. It is subtitled 'An Autobiography'.

The name 'Briggflatts' comes from a Quaker community near Sedbergh. Bunting visited Briggflatts as a schoolboy: the family of a schoolfriend lived there, and Bunting developed a strong attachment to his friend's sister, Peggy Greenbank. The poem's dedication reads 'For Peggy'.

The poem has a five part structure. It was first performed on December 22, 1965 at the Morden Tower, and published in 1966 by Fulcrum Press[1].

Another Bunting poem has the word 'Briggflatts' in its title. The short poem 'At Briggflatts meetinghouse' (1975)[2] [3] is set at the Briggflatts Meeting House (from 1675) in the hamlet of Briggflatts.
