What is this??? When was Cuba ever a part of the British Empire? It is true that Havana was occupied, but the occupation of Havana has never been interpreted as having control over all of Cuba. Even more of St. Dominique had been under British control (during the Revolutionary Wars) when the British appointed a Governor of St. Dominique (though the area of control never extended further than the about half of the coastal area). As far as I know, no British/English governor of Cuba had ever been appointed. I also believe that during the same war in which Havana was captured, Manila also fell to the British (but this doesn't make the Philippines British). I have also never seen any reference to Rwanda and Burundi ever falling under British control even briefly, since the German East Africa didn't surrender until very late in the war, if not at the very end, so the British never occupied the area before 1918 and certainly never afterwards.

Also, who coloured in northern Iran? that area was never under British control in WWII, but under Soviet rule, hence it was not coloured pink.


Having done some checking again, I've seen some sites that refer to both Cuba and the Philippines as having been occupied by Britain, although most of the history books I have access to refer to only Havana and Manila. I suppose we can either represent this by colouring all of Cuba and the Philippines (which might be misleading) or only Havana and Manila. Can we have a show of opinion of these options?

With regards to Rwanda-Burundi they apparently went from German rule to Belgian occupation in 1916 and were never under British rule or occupation. The entire Cameroons was not under British rule, only the area shaded previously, the rest was under French rule.
