Brian Chapple

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Brian Chapple (born 1945, London) is a British composer. He studied at the Royal Academy of Music with Lennox Berkeley.

[edit] Compositions include

  • Green and Pleasant, winner of the BBC Monarchy 1000 prize in 1973.
  • Scherzos for four pianos
  • Choral symphony In Ecclesiis
  • Piano Concerto
  • Venus Fly Trap
  • Missa Brevis
  • Little Symphony
  • Lamentations of Jeremiah
  • Ave Verum Corpus
  • Cantica
  • Magnificat
  • Songs of Innocence
  • In Memoriam
  • Three Motets
  • Five Blake Songs
  • Five Shakespeare Songs
  • 3 volumes of piano pieces: In the Pink, Lazy Days, On the Cool Side
