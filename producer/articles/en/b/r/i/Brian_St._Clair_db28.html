Brian St. Clair

Brian St. Clair is one half of the rock duo Local H.

Brian was the 2nd drummer for Rights Of The Accused, one of Chicago's most famous underground punk rock bands who was fronted by Mike O'Connel. Herb Rosen, the owner of Chicagos famous The Liars Club was on bass guitar and featured guitarist Wes Kidd now of Silent Partner Management fame.

Kidd and St. Clair formed Triple Fast Action who released several singles on indie labels and a full CD on Capitol Records and one on Deep Elm records before Brian left the band to pursue other interests. In 1999 St. Clair joined Local H with Scott Lucas after drummer Joe Daniels left the band to pursue other interests.

Just prior to Local H he was the drum tech for Bun E. Carlos of Cheap Trick and has been the tour manager for Liz Phair, Triple Fast Action, & Local H. He has also been dubbed the "hardest working drummer" in rock history.

Brian St. Clair's hobbies include birdwatching and he was featured in a famous birdwatching book called "Red Tails In Love" by Marie Winn. In 1998 Brain St. Clair auditioned for the world famous "Blue Man Group" and was told to start taking acting classes so he could join in the future.
