Star Trek: Bridge Commander is a tactical simulation/flight sim computer game, published by Activision, based in the Star Trek universe. Generally, this game was well received by the reviewers of various gaming magazines, scoring higher than 80% in most cases.

The plot revolves around a newly promoted captain who is assigned to investigate an explosion of a star in the Maelstrom. Throughout the mystery, Captain Jean-Luc Picard and Commander Data occasionally assist in the investigation. It appears that a rogue Cardassian faction is responsible and is using a new weapon from an unknown race to threaten Alpha Quadrant races. The captain and his crew take command of the USS Sovereign to combat this new threat to the Federation.

The game allows two different flavours of gameplay: storyline mode and quick battle mode. Quick battle mode allows customizing missions for neverending playing -- from Starfleet defensive tactics, to piloting a shuttle craft in a cooperative assault, to a Cardassian outpost.

[edit] Storyline

Spoiler warning: Plot and/or ending details follow.

The game begins with a standard mission, the Galaxy-class starship USS Dauntless (NCC-71879) is in orbit of a planet near the Maelstrom system. Captain Wright is onboard a shuttle en route to an orbital research station when the Vesuvi star suddenly goes nova. The shockwave is devastating, destroying the planet's orbital stations and obliterating the Captain's shuttlecraft. The USS Dauntless exits the system suffering damage to her warp engines. The ship is able to safely return to a Federation drydock facility where it undergoes repair and refit. His Executive Officer (You, the player) is promoted to Captain. Soon the player is joined by Starfleet Captain Jean-Luc Picard. The ship departs the drydocks for Starbase 12, the operation command center for the Maelstrom region. As the game proceeds you and your crew are transferred to the USS Sovereign (NX-73811), the pathfinder of the Sovereign Class.

The vessel will eventually become a key factor in solving the mystery in the Maelstrom and reveal a Cardassian plan for an invasion of the Federation.

[edit] Gameplay and mechanics

Many game functions are carried out by selecting an individual bridge officer, and selecting from a set of commands or options in order to control that individual's set of responsibilities. In this way, the game emphasizes the command aspects of starship operation.

[edit] Modding

Over the years since its release, the game has been heavily expanded upon by the dedicated modders and gamers of Star Trek: Bridge Commander.

Modifications ("mods") have included:

  • Retextures, and in some cases brand new models to replace the existing bridges, to closer match them to the television / film versions.
  • Brand new versions of existing ships, and other canon starships not featured in the original Bridge Commander- such as the Enterprise-A, the Defiant, Excelsior, and so on.
  • New Bridges, including Prometheus, Excelsior, Defiant, Nebula, Intrepid, Enterprise A, TOS Constitution, NX Class, Generations Galaxy and many more.
  • Brand new original ships, designed by fans especially for the game. These designs come from the entire range of the Star Trek timeline, from Star Trek: Enterprise to the far future. Some of the more notable ships include LC's Galaxy Evo, Queball's/MayhemUK's USS Courageous, DJ Curtis' Supremus and Vivace Classes, and Raven Night's C9A Klingon Battleship.
  • Ships taken from other television shows, such as Battlestar Galactica, Star Wars, Stargate and others.
  • New menus and subsystems to make the control of the player's starship more complicated and true to the Star Trek universe; for example, a more detailed power management system, the ability to abandon ship, and to separate the Galaxy and Prometheus classes as seen on the show.
  • New and more elaborate graphical effects, from more detailed hull damage and blinking navigation lights to warp effects and large explosions. One very famous fx modification is the NanoFX mod which greatly enhances the graphical fx and even add's stuff like atmospheres, warp effects etc.

A Major mod that came out in late 2003 was the Unofficial bridge commander expansion entitled Enemies of life this mod was a major mod that also contained only Canon ships, new textures and many options from the mods that were out at that time. This Mod was the first major mod that allowed modded ships and options to be played in multiplayer. The Mod had a few problems during its time , its last release of a 1.4 patch came out at the beginning of 2005. The designer of the mod (Uss_Donoghue) decided to give up creating updates for the mod and left it unfinished and soon after this the creator vanished. For its time many people installed this mod and the multiplayer groups grew online. Rumour still has it that Uss_Donoghue might come back and create a brand new mod with the more up to date mods that are out.

Modding the game to personal taste renders it practically unplayable in multiplayer over the internet, as all gamers must have the exact same version of the game- ie, the same mods installed. To this end, the Kobayashi Maru mod, released in 2006, gathers together many of the separate mods that players made, into a cohesive downloadable patch. The mod includes many ships, canon or otherwise, texture effects, menu subsystems, and other options. By having each player only modding the game with the Kobayashi Maru patch the game is now playable for many people online. The 0.9.1 patch to Kobayashi Maru has also been released to fix certain bugs, and the team responsible for Kobayashi Maru was expecting version 1.0 to be released may 2007, but has since then been canceled according to the official project website, citing "lack of free time" being the reason they were unable to finish the project(this was later found to be an April Fools joke by the by Kobayashi Maru Team). This version would have promises to replace some ships with better versions, and alter the "hardpoints" (specifications) of some ships that are criticized for being too overpowered, for example the Romulan Shrike and Federation Prometheus.

Wars in Paradise is another mod currently in production for Bridge Commander that aims to fulfil a hole that has been gaping in its side, in the form of a new campaign. Apart from this it aims to bring together the latest mods in a workable and balanced state providing an enriched era based gameplay experience. Multiplayer is destined to have features such as capture the planet mode and the like and there are plans for a syndication package to be distributed along with the mod in order to keep the mod up to date with the latest ships and scripts etc, by doing this, the team hopes to ensure that the multiplayer experience will continue as all people will have the same version of the game. Other major modifications are on their way. In 2007, the New Frontier mod plans to let the player explore the universe in different time periods complete with missions, while Immersion Core v4++ will let the player walk around and interact with the vessel inside, similar to the Elite Force series, including weapons and tricorders. [edit] Availability

Star Trek: Bridge Commander is not available for purchase anymore from retailers, as the game's publisher, Activision, has discontinued it. This was mainly due to the lack of interest in the Star Trek franchise in the early part of the Millennium. It can, however, still be purchased on auction sites like eBay, along with other software that is no longer available.
