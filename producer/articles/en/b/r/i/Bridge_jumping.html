Bridge jumping

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Bridge Jumping is a popular activity among students at Cambridge University on the River Cam during the summer time. The object of the exercise is to climb (rather than literally jumping) over one of the bridges of the River Cam, both starting and finishing in a punt on the river. The aim is to climb onto a bridge as the punt on which you are riding goes underneath it, get up to the top, walk across, and climb back down again into the punt (it is theoretically possible to jump, but the chances of the punt staying afloat or the person directing the punt staying in it are less than good).

Along the river Cam, the most difficult bridge to jump is the Bridge of Sighs at St John's College since the bridge is enclosed by quite a high ceiling, and stands high out of the water. One of the easiest bridges is the Trinity Bridge, as it is quite low, has good handholds in the form of college crests standing out from the stonework, and provides places to stand at water-level. However, porters are often to be found nearby, so it pays to check that you won't be forced to stand at the bottom of the bridge asking people for a lift, having been sent back down from the parapet. The famous Mathematical Bridge at Queen's College is also fairly easy to jump.
