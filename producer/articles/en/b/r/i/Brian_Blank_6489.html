Brian Blank

Brian Blank is an associate professor of mathematics at Washington University of St. Louis. Recently, he co-authored a pair of calculus textbooks with his Washington University colleague, Steven Krantz. Titled Calculus: Single Variable and Calculus: Multivariable, the textbooks are currently used in calculus classes at Washington University. They are famed among students for their off-the-wall problems and references.

He is also a devotee of Lemony Snicket's A Series of Unfortunate Events and is reputed to enjoy woodworking.

Brian also works as a freelance correspondent for CNN Radio in New York City. He attended the famed University of Missouri-Columbia journalism school, working at NBC affiliate KOMU-TV and ABC radio affiliate KFRU-AM, before starting at CNN in 2004.

He's a big fan of long distance running and has run more marathons than he can remember. He even took second in a 50-mile race in Alabama.

He has won no major journalism awards, though has every intention to someday. He did write a fun article for CNN.com on ultramarathoning legend Dean Karnazes.
