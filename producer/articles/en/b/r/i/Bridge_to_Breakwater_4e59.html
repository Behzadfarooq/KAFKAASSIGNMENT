Bridge to Breakwater

The Bridge to Breakwater is an annual 12K/5K footrace held along the Los Angeles Harbor in San Pedro, California. The race, as reported by the Daily Breeze, debuted in 2006. The race is held during Labor Day Weekend on Saturday, giving racers a chance to still get away for the long weekend.

Runners start underneath the Vincent Thomas Bridge at the Cruise Ship Promenade, then venture around the Federal Breakwater at Cabrillo Beach, eventually ending back at the Cruise Ship Promenade.

Walkers typically trail behind the runners, consisting of competitive and recreational runners.

Inspired by the innovative Bay to Breakers race held annually in San Francisco, California, the Bridge to Breakwater is a celebration of running and life, encouraging participants to wear costumes and run together as centipedes.

The race was founded by San Pedro native Dave Behar to provide local runners a race to call their own and to give local and national charities and organizations, including the National Kidney Foundation, the American Parkinson Disease Association and Ronald McDonald House Charities, a fundraiser.
