It was released only in the UK and only 1,000 were pressed.

The single entitled "Tepid Peppermind Wonderland" was given out at screenings of the movie Dig! and with the movie as an extra disc- the back artwork is different on both.

[edit] Compilations

The BJM were featured on the following multiple-band compilations:

  • Pure Spun Sugar, Track #12 Good Morning Girl (CD, American Pop Project & Candy Floss, CF-017, AmPop 201CD, year unknown)
  • Delphonic Sounds Today!, Track #1 I Fought The Law (The Bobby Fuller Four) (CD, DEL-FI Records, DFCD 2114, 1999)
  • ORG15 Lollygagging ()

In 1993 Bomp! Records and Tangible Records coreleased a boxed-set of 6 singles, each by a different San Francisco "psych" band. The bands were apparently handpicked by Anton Newcombe himself, and it is rumored that he also produced the music. Besides the Brian Jonestown Massacre, the bands include Orange, Nebtwister, and Hollowbody. The other two singles (as credited to Acid and Reverb) are 4-track demos by Newcombe and Travis Threlkel (who designed the box-cover and record sleeves).
