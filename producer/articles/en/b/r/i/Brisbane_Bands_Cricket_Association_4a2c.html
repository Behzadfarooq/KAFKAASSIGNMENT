The Brisbane Bands Cricket Association is a group who compete in the Brisbane Bands Cricket Competition. This competition has been running since 1993 and is a social cricket competition played through the winter months in Brisbane, Australia. The teams that compete all represent and comprise various members of musical groups that perform in South East Queensland (and elsewhere when they get the chance).

The longest standing team in the competition is the Inner City Cricket Club, or ICCC Goats. This team have taken on the mantle of running the competition, although this mainly consists of creating the draw at the beginning of the season, collating the results after each match weekend and maintaining a table.

In the 2006 season there are 12 teams competing. The teams are - 4ZZZ Reds - representing the 4ZZZ community radio station; Fridge Magnet; Brewers; I.C.C.C.; NoMC.C.C.; J.C.B.; FunkFolk; Naked Apes; Zoo Bombers; Hackers; RACCA; Loofah.

The matches are held fortnightly from March through to September depending on the number of teams competing.

At times in its 14 year history the competition has included members of several bands that have gone on to varying degrees of national and international fame such as Powderfinger and Regurgitator.

The competition was featured on ABC Radio National's The Sport Factor in 2001.
