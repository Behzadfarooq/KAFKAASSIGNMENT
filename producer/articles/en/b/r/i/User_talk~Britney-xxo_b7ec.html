Firegirl65

The article Firegirl65 has been speedily deleted from Wikipedia. This was done because the article seemed to be about a person, group of people, band, club, company, or web content, but it did not indicate how or why the subject is notable, that is, why an article about that subject should be included in Wikipedia. Under the criteria for speedy deletion, articles that do not assert notability may be deleted at any time. If you can indicate why the subject is really notable, you are free to re-create the article, making sure to cite any verifiable sources.

Please see the guidelines for what is generally accepted as notable, and for specific types of articles, you may want to check out our criteria for biographies, for web sites, for bands, or for companies. Feel free to leave a note on my talk page if you have any questions about this. -- Merope 08:28, 25 March 2007 (UTC)

This page does not assert the notability of its subject. If you disagree with its deletion, you may request a deletion review. -- Merope 08:39, 25 March 2007 (UTC)

Why I deleted it

The article makes no assertion of the subject's notability--that is, why it should be included in an encyclopedia. Anyone can upload a video to YouTube. If you can provide reliable sources (that is, something other than YouTube or MySpace) that document this person's notability, then it might be worthy of inclusion. If not, then it's subject to deletion. -- Merope 08:57, 25 March 2007 (UTC)

Yeah, that's still not enough. We need something that actually addresses why she's notable.
