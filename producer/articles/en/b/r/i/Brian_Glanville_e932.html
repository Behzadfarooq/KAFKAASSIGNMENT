Brian Glanville

Brian Lester Glanville (born 24 September 1931) is a leading English football writer and novelist.

Glanville spent nearly thirty years as a football correspondent for the Sunday Times, and has also written for The People and currently World Soccer magazine. He spent much of his career based in Italy and has been seen as one of the leading authorities on Italian football as a result. Whilst based in both Florence and Rome he wrote regularly for the Italian daily Corriere Dello Sport, as well as occasional pieces for La Stampa and Corriere della Sera.

In his early days Glanville also worked as a writer for the satirical BBC TV programme That Was The Week That Was. As a novelist he has written mostly about football and life in Italy, with his 1956 novel Along the Arno particularly well-received by critics. He has also written The Story of the World Cup, a frequently updated history of the FIFA tournament.

Away from writing about football, Glanville is a life-long supporter of Arsenal F.C.
