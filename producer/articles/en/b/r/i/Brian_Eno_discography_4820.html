This page contains a comprehensive collection of information related to recordings by English electronic musician, music theorist and record producer Brian Eno.

[edit] Vocal/rock albums

  • 1973: Here Come the Warm Jets #151 (U.S.)
  • 1974: June 1, 1974 (live by Kevin Ayers, John Cale, Nico and Eno)
  • 1974: Taking Tiger Mountain (By Strategy)
  • 1975: Another Green World
  • 1977: Before and after Science #171 (U.S.)
  • 1977: 801 Live (Phil Manzanera with Eno, Lloyd Watson, Francis Monkman, Bill MacCormick, and Simon Phillips)
  • 1981: My Life in the Bush of Ghosts (with David Byrne) #44 (U.S.)
  • 1990: Wrong Way Up (with John Cale)
  • 1992: Nerve Net
  • 1994: Wah Wah (by James session with Eno)
  • 1995: Spinner (with Jah Wobble)
  • 1995: Original Soundtracks No. 1 (by Passengers and Eno)
  • 2005: Another Day on Earth

[edit] Instrumental/ambient albums

  • 1975: Discreet Music
  • 1978: Ambient #1 / Music for Airports
  • 1978: Music for Films
  • 1980: Ambient #2 / The Plateaux of Mirror (with Harold Budd)
  • 1980: Ambient #3 / Day of Radiance (by Laraaji, produced by Eno)
  • 1980: Fourth World, Vol. 1: Possible Musics (with Jon Hassell)
  • 1981: Fourth World, Vol. 2: Dream Theory in Malaya (by Jon Hassell, Eno played also)
  • 1982: Ambient #4 / On Land
  • 1983: Apollo: Atmospheres and Soundtracks
  • 1983: Music for Films, Volume 2 (with Daniel Lanois, reissued in 2005 as More Music from Films)
  • 1983: Textures (with Daniel Lanois and Roger Eno) discogs.com Standard Music Library
  • 1984: The Pearl (with Harold Budd)
  • 1985: Hybrid (with Daniel Lanois and Michael Brook)
  • 1985: Thursday Afternoon
  • 1988: Music for Films, Volume 3 (various artists)
  • 1992: The Shutov Assembly
  • 1993: Neroli
  • 1997: The Drop
  • 2000: Music for Onmyo-Ji (Japan only release with Peter Schwalm)
  • 2001: Drawn from Life (with Peter Schwalm)

[edit] Installation, compilations etc.

  • 1971: The Great Learning "Paragraphs 2 and 7" by Cornelius Cardew (with the Scratch Orchestra)
  • 1973: The Portsmouth Sinfonia Plays the Popular Classics - (With Portsmouth Sinfonia)
  • 1974: Hallelujah! The Portsmouth Sinfonia Live At The Royal Albert Hall (live with Portsmouth Sinfonia)
  • 1975: Peter and the Wolf (Various Artists) Eno performs the role of The Wolf in this progressive rock adaptation of the classic Prokofiev classical work
  • 1983: Working Backwards 1983-1973 (11 LP set of 9 previously released official albums with Music for Films II and Rarities)
  • 1986: More Blank Than Frank/Desert Island Selection - compilation
  • 1991: My Squelchy Life (Never released. Promo copies exist, and tracks appeared on later releases.)
  • 1993: Robert Sheckley's In a Land of Clear Colours (with Pete Sinfield)
  • 1993: Box I (3CD compilation of instrumental tracks)
  • 1993: Box II (3CD compilation of vocal tracks)
  • 1994: Headcandy (CD-ROM)
  • 1996: Generative Music 1 Generative Music album first published on floppy disk
  • 1997: Extracts from Music for White Cube
  • 1998: Lightness: Music for the Marble Palace
  • 1999: I Dormienti
  • 1999: Kite Stories
  • 2000: Music for Civic Recovery Centre
  • 2001: Compact Forest Proposal
  • 2003: January 07003-Bell Studies for The Clock of the Long Now
  • 2004: Curiosities Volume 1
  • 2004: Curiosities Volume 2
  • 2006: 77 Million Paintings

[edit] In Roxy Music

  • 1972: Roxy Music (by Roxy Music)
  • 1973: For Your Pleasure (by Roxy Music)

[edit] With Robert Fripp

  • 1973: Fripp & Eno (No Pussyfooting)
  • 1975: Evening Star
  • 1975: Air Structures (bootleg)
  • 1994: The Essential Fripp and Eno (Re-release of "No Pussyfooting" and two tracks from "Evening Star", plus four unreleaed tracks)
  • 2004: The Equatorial Stars
  • 2006: The Cotswold Gnomes - download only from DGMLive.com

[edit] With Cluster

  • 1977: Cluster & Eno (with Cluster)
  • 1978: After the Heat (with Roedelius and Moebius)
  • 1984: Begegnungen (compilation album with material by Eno, Roedelius and Moebius and Plank)
  • 1985: Begegnungen II (compilation album with material by Eno, Roedelius and Moebius and Plank)
  • 1997: Harmonia: '76: Tracks and Traces (with Roedelius)
