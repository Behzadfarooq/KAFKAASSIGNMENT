Lieutenant Brian Huff is an officer in the United States Coast Guard.

A native of Parkville, Maryland, Huff enlisted in the Coast Guard in October 1984 and underwent Basic Training in Cape May, New Jersey. Upon graduation, his first assignment was USCGC Munro, based at Honolulu, Hawaii. In 1986 he attended Boatswain’s Mate A-School at Yorktown, Virginia. Upon completion, he was transferred to Station Chincoteague Virginia, and was subsequently assigned to LEDET 5E, where he conducted Counter Narcotics Patrols on the Caribbean Sea and South Pacific Ocean, and two deployments in support of Operation Desert Storm.

He next served as First Lieutenant as a BM1 on USCGC Shearwater and USCGC Sitkinak, Key West, Florida. In 1995 he was assigned as the Executive Petty Officer of USCGC Point Jackson, Clearwater Beach, Florida. Upon advancement to Chief Boatswain Mate, he reported to Charleston, South Carolina, for assignment to USCGC Rambler as the Executive Petty Officer until her decommissioning. He then was assigned as the Officer in Charge, USCG Station Cortez Florida, until his appointment as Chief Warrant Officer in 1999, when he was re-assigned as the First Lieutenant of USCGC Firebush, Kodiak, Alaska.

In August 2001 he took command of USCGC Knight Island, St. Petersburg, Florida, and was part of the first deployment of Patrol Boats overseas since the Vietnam War. During this deployment of patrol boats to the Mediterranean Sea in support of Operation Enduring Freedom in the global War on Terrorism, he was promoted to Lieutenant and was part of the first trans-Atlantic crossing of Coast Guard Patrol Boats. Lieutenant Huff is currently reporting from USCGC OAK, Charleston, South Carolina, where he most recently served as the Executive Officer.

Lieutenant Huff has been awarded the Meritorious Service Medal, two Coast Guard Commendation Medals, five Coast Guard Achievement Medals, and the Navy Achievement Medal. Lieutenant Huff’s other awards include Officer in Charge Ashore and Command at Sea designations, Permanent Cutterman Pin, and numerous Unit, Campaign, and Operational decorations.
