Bristow Muldoon

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Bristow Muldoon (born 19 March 1964 in Glasgow) is a Scottish Labour politician.

He was elected to represent Livingston in the Scottish Parliament in 1999 and re-elected in 2003.

Prior to this, Muldoon worked as a manager at British Rail and then for GNER over a period of 13 years.

As a Member of the Scottish Parliament (MSP), he has been a convener of the Local Government and Transport Committee and is involved in a number of cross party groups, mainly in areas concerning transport and health.
