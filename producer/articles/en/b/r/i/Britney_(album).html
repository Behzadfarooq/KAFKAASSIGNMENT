Britney is the third studio album from American pop singer Britney Spears, released on November 6, 2001.

[edit] Album information

Britney debuted at number four in the United Kingdom and number one on both the Canadian and Billboard 200 album charts. In the U.S. Britney sold over 746,000 copies in its first week[1]; this number one debut on the Billboard made her the only female artist in SoundScan history to have her first three albums debut at number one. Although the album has sold 4.5 million copies in the U.S. according to Nielsen Soundscan[2], it was considerably less successful than Spears' first two albums, ...Baby One More Time and Oops!... I Did It Again. Britney is Spears' lowest selling studio album to date in the UK selling 462,000 copies, the album spent a total of 36 weeks on the chart. However, it is hailed by many critics as being her best album to date.

Britney is most notable for Spears' own increasingly prominent role in the songwriting as well as a greater focus on her own sexuality. The record boasts aid from producers such as The Neptunes, Rodney Jerkins, Max Martin and others. Singles off the album include "I'm a Slave 4 U", "I'm Not a Girl, Not Yet a Woman", "Overprotected", "Boys", "Anticipating" and a cover of The Arrows' "I Love Rock 'N Roll" (previously covered by Joan Jett in 1982).

Two of the tracks off the album, "I'm Not a Girl, Not Yet a Woman" and "I Love Rock 'N Roll", were featured in Spears' film Crossroads (2002).
