Brian Henry

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Brian Henry is a United States poet. He lives in Virginia.

[edit] Other Publications

Henry has published poetry in American Poetry Review, The Paris Review, Boston Review, The Canary, Conduit, Denver Quarterly, Grand Street, Jacket, New American Writing, TriQuarterly, Virginia Quarterly Review, Volt, and magazines in Australia, England, Wales, Ireland, Germany, Slovenia, Croatia, and the Czech Republic and has been translated into Russian, Croatian, and Slovenian.

[edit] Critical Writings

Henry's poetry criticism has appeared in the New York Times Book Review, the Times Literary Supplement, The Antioch Review, Jacket, The Kenyon Review, The Georgia Review, and The Yale Review, among other places. His essays have been reprinted in various scholarly books, including Imagining Australia (Harvard University Press).

[edit] Teaching

Henry has taught at Plymouth State College and the University of Georgia. He currently teaches at the University of Richmond.

[edit] Awards

  • Alice Fay di Castagnola Award from the Poetry Society of America for Quarantine, 2003
  • Carol Weinstein Poetry Prize, 2006
  • George Bogin Memorial Award from the Poetry Society of America, 2001
  • Finalist for the Forward Prize in England for Astronaut, 2000
  • Fulbright Scholarship to Australia, 1997
