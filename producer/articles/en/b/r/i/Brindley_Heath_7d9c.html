Brindley Heath is an area of heath land on Cannock Chase situated between Hednesford and Rugeley, Staffordshire.

[edit] History

A hand made sign stating that number 9 Brindley Village stood on this spot and was the home of the Anthony family [1]

There are remains of disused coal mines in a number of areas of Cannock Chase, incliding Brindley Heath.

The area has been used for many things including an RAF training camp which was set up in 1938 as home to the 6th Technical Training School [2][3]. In 1957 the camp was used to house around 900 Hungarian refugees. The camp was then demolished in 1960. The area was also used a tank training ground, leading to much soil disturbance.

There was a village on the heath between 1945 and 1951 when it was demolished. Details are very hard to come by about the village or its occupants.

There was also a POW camp at Flaxley Green near Rugeley where the foundations can still be seen to this day.

[edit] Flora

Brindley Heath is home to a high concentration of the relatively rare hybrid bilberry [4].
