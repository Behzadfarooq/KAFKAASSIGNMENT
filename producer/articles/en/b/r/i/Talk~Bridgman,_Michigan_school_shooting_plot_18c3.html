Lessee, grammar, punctuation, wikification, and the only the title lets the reader know what the point of the article is. It is more a convoluted survey of a rash of bomb threats made in the region than a focus on Madejczyk and his plot; and from what information the author did provide it is not even clear whether Madejczyk's threats centered around shooting or bombing his potential victims.

It could use some NPOV work--"reign of terror"? From bomb threats? The article doesn't even make clear whether he was actually homicidal or not, just that his threat was "more serious" than all the other prank ones. Hardly Robespierre. This sort of wording, as well as selective remarks made about the media and Madejczyk's punishment, gives the impression that the article was written more out of personal resentment towards how the situation was handled than an interest in recording the facts of the event.

The information seems relevant in that it occurred in the wake of Columbine and may be seen as copycat behavior, but it needs to be conveyed in a far more organized and professional fashion. - 69.140.62.82, 14 Jan 2005

  • Yah. I'm an inclusionist by nature, but here I would cut, cut, cut. Most of the other incidents deserve a single sentence or two, not whole paragraphs. So, just enough to get the flavor of the paranoia in town as one came after the other, then the investigation and prosecution, because it did involve the FBI and receive national media attention. [edit] No, It's not a Copyvio

and it's not a personal vindeta. It is my original work. Please give me a day or two to take your points into consideration and revise the article. I'm new to Wikipedia and I'd really like to contribute. Madejczyk is the focus of the article because all of the other "bomb threats" were silly pranks and his threat mimicked Columbine and Conyers and brought true terror to the entire region. he has also continued to make extortion threats now as an adult. This is why I felt the article needed to be out there as a reference point to what he's currently doing. The FBI, the U.S. Attorney General, State Police and Local Police all converged on the school the same day the threat was made. It is worthy of being in the encyclopedia. I feel that maybe using initials and reducing info on the other incidents will protect the more innocent pranksters and focus the article more. Please give me a few days to fix it. --Geordi Kajlor 18:07, Jan 18, 2005 (UTC)
