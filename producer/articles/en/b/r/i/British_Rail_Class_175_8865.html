British Rail Class 175

Class 175, no. 175 115 at Crewe on 1st November 2003, with a late-night service to Holyhead. This unit is painted in First North Western livery.

The Class 175 ‘Coradia’ is a model of diesel multiple unit passenger train used in the United Kingdom. They were built from 1999–2001 by Alstom at Washwood Heath in Birmingham. The fleet is currently based at Chester Depot.

They were originally built for First North Western, but since then franchise reorganisations have meant the fleet has been transferred to Arriva Trains Wales and TransPennine Express until they were replaced by class 185

[edit] Description

Eleven two-carriage and sixteen three-carriage units were built for First North Western to allow retirement of elderly Class 101 ‘heritage’ DMUs and as replacement of locomotive-hauled trains to Bangor and Holyhead.

The two-carriage units are numbered 175 001–011, with individual carriages numbered 50701–50711 and 79701–79711. The three-carriage units are numbered 175 101–116, with individual carriages numbered 50751–50766, 56751–56766 and 79751–79766.

They are part of the Coradia family along with the Class 180.

[edit] Current operations

[edit] Arriva Trains Wales

Arriva's new livery for the units.

The Class 175 fleet were transferred to Arriva Trains Wales when they took over responsibility of the North Wales Coast Line in 2004. While Arriva removed the First North Western logos, they did not otherwise change the colour scheme of the fleet for some time, but as of November 2005, they have started re-painting the fleet in their own livery. The services originally operated by these units were the Crewe and Manchester to Bangor, Llandudno, and Holyhead routes. However, Arriva Trains Wales, whilst still using the Class 175 on these routes, now also use them for their Cardiff to Holyhead service, Swansea to Manchester service and occasionally on the Gloucester to Maesteg via Cardiff service.
