Bryan Michael Stoller

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Bryan Michael Stoller (born in 1960 in Peterborough, Ontario, Canada) is an award winning film maker and made films such as Miss Cast Away, Undercover Angel and Lightyears Away which was completed in June 2006.

After moving to Ottawa, Ontario at age 6, Bryan began experimenting with movies and animation. He got his first job in the film and television industry when he was 11 years old as host of a TV show on CBC called "Film Fun" in which he showed kids how to make their own Super-8 movies. He later was able to transform that talent into preparing commercials for various Ottawa businesses that were used on air in the late 60's.

He is also the author of Film Making for Dummies and frequently lectures at various university film classes throughout California and Nevada.
