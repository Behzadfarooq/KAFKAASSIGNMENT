Bryant's triangle
Nélaton’s line and Bryant’s triangle.
Gray's subject #292 1345
Dorlands/Elsevier t_17/12820853

A surface marking of clinical importance is Bryant’s triangle (or iliofemoral triangle), which is mapped out thus:

  • the base of the triangle is a line from the anterior superior iliac spine to the top of the greater trochanter.
  • its sides are formed respectively by:
    • a horizontal line from the anterior superior iliac spine
    • a vertical line from the top of the greater trochanter.
