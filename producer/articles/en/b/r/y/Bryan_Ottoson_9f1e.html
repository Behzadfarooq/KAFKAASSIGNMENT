Guitarist Bryan Ottoson : 1978-2005

Bryan Ottoson (b. 18 March 1978 Minneapolis, Minnesota d. 19 April 2005 North Charleston, South Carolina) was a rock musician. He was the lead guitarist of American Head Charge. Prior to joining American Head Charge, he was first a member of the proto digital hardcore band A:POD with later AHC bandmate and close friend Aaron Zilch and British electro hooligan Luek Williamson (from 1997-98). Zilch introduced Ottoson to the AHC family in '99 and an immeadiate bond formed between Bryan and AHC bassist and co-founder Chad Hanks. He moved into the infamous "Pleasent House" and became Hanks' bass tech. Later he joined and became a key songwriter for Minneapolis-based industrial metal band Black Flood Diesel. When American Head Charge decided to part ways with guitarist Wayne Kyle in the spring '02 there was no question who would replace him. According to some sources the desire to have Ottoson in the band was a deciding factor in the lineup change. He was an immeadiate favorite of the fans due to his crazed energetic stage presence and off stage approachable, friendly demeanor. After touring for The War Of Art, the band went through a difficult period with personel change, label issues, drug madness and other behind the music worthy setbacks. Ottoson stepped up and helped push the band out of their slump, contributing much to the songwriting for The Feeding. He achived another dream ( to go along with partying with one of hie heros, Dimebag Darrel, and playing Castle Donnington) when he appeared with the band on MTVs Head Bangers Ball, introducing the video for a song he helped to write. He had one tattoo. "333" on the back of his neck representing the synchronicty of things happening in his life in threes and the clock reading that time at key moments. Since his passing many friends and fans have gotten their own "333" tattoos in many styles. Many have also had their own powerful syncronicities with that number. Jordis Unga drew the numbers on her hand when she delivered an incredibly moving rendition of David Bowie's "The Man Who Sold The World" on Rock Star: INXS.

He was found dead in his bunk on the American Head Charge tour bus on 19 April 2005( almost three years to the day after meeting the also tragically lost Dimebag). American Head Charge was on tour in support of The Feeding and were direct support to tour headliner Mudvayne. According to North Charleston police records, the cause of death was determined to be an accidental prescription-drug overdose. Bryan had been battling severe strep throat and had been prescribed penicillin and an un-named pain medication. American Head Charge paid tribute to Bryan by performing a full set in his memory at First Avenue in Minneapolis.
