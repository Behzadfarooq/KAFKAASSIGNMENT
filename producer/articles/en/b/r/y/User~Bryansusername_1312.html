Writing Assignment 2 - 9/14/06

Upon walking into the room, one’s senses force out a blink, a breath, and a mental pause of over stimulation. If barefooted, a cold hardwood floor’s touch slowly crawls into unprotected toes and settles there, while a sock would otherwise slide or a shoe’s grip prevent any notable sensation at all. This of course is assuming that the current portion of the rectangular floor is not hindered by other objects of various uses and purposes spotted across the polished wood. Dim and blurred reflections cast by the floor contrast greatly with the hard-edged and colorful books, clothing, and blankets casually strewn about the room. A dark wooden dresser about waist height stands to the immediate left and it’s surface is crowded with boxes of earrings, bottles of hair care products, combs, pictures, CD’s, and other random items. Straight ahead stands a chair with clothes piled in no orderly fashion, with the bright colors of a few shirts popping out against the overwhelming majority of darker colored shirts and pants within the pile. Next to this chair is a low table carrying an ancient television set, complete with knobs that are turned to find a predetermined channel number or to change the volume level. This television is connected in a mess of wires to a Playstation 2 lying on a nearby bookshelf. Directly in front of the television, in the far right corner of the room is the bed, a simple mattress showing out from under a disordered and worn out “Little Mermaid” bed spread. Above the bed is a wall hanging CD rack holding a dozen or so CDs, many of the rock, Pop Rock, and Metal genres. Under the bed are a few squeaky drawers holding more clothes. In the far left corner of the room is a desk and chair, the desk carrying candles of many colors and sizes.

From the candles comes an intoxicating smell of flowers and fruit, which spreads itself throughout the room. A fruited scent rises from the bed. An open window over the bed lets in green rays filtered through a great tree as well as the aroma of grass with a slight hint of pavement. The light from the windows cast squares of reflection on both a giant Invader Zim poster and another poster depicting the Incredible Hulk directly across from the window. Above the bed, hanging from the ceiling is a Lord of the Rings poster containing Aragorn staring down his vertically held sword at those who lie there. Legolas of the Lord of the Rings has an arrow knocked and aimed directly at the observer in another life size poster hanging on the wall next to the bed. Across the room in the direction of Legolas’ sight hang pictures of all the X-men cut out from a monthly calendar. The walls behind these posters are a dull white. Yet, on the space in between the many posters and on the bookshelves are hand painted designs and flourishes where the author made the space more friendly and forced the dull white’s feeling of apathy to diminish in the presence of bright red, purple, blue, and green swirls and designs.

A bass guitar leans on it’s amplifier in between the desk and dresser, its’ cable snaking around in a labyrinth of circles. Pictures of favorite comics, people, and places litter a bookshelf otherwise crowded with books right next to the bed. Cups still half full of water litter the room, with most an arm’s length from the bed. Through a vent in the floor, any activity in the basement T.V. room can be heard, usually involving a T.V. blaring with an occasional muffled laugh from a man.
