Bryn Smith

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Bryn Smith (born August 11, 1955 in Marietta, Georgia), is a former professional baseball player who pitched in the Major Leagues from 1981-1993.

Smith had a pair of 5-game winning streaks in 1985 and was picked Expo Player of the Month for July by Montreal baseball writers after 3-1 record and 1.74 ERA.

Was first winning pitcher in Colorado Rockies history, defeating Montreal, 11-4, on April 9, 1993.

[edit] Trivia

In a game played against the Philadelphia Phillies on June 12, 1987, Smith was the victim of an unusual "9-to-3" (right field-to-first base) putout, after hitting an apparent single into right field, and then being thrown out by the Phillies' right-fielder, Glenn Wilson, before he could safely reach first base.[1]
