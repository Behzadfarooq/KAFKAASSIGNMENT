Buck Danny is a Belgian/French comic strip created by Jean-Michel Charlier and Victor Hubinon, which chronicles the adventures of a trio of pilots in the United States Navy.

The first half dozen albums recounted Buck Danny's exploits in the Pacific war. He dueled over Midway, was parachuted into Burma, and fought the Japanese all over the Pacific ocean, while advancing in rank. Buck was later joined by two other main characters, Tumbler and Sonny Tuckson, with the latter becoming the comic relief figure of the trio.

The adventures of the three mainstays continue to this day, and although Colonel Danny and his comrades have never aged, they have advanced in history, and the series has always remained faithful to the most recent technology, such as having its heroes fly F-14 Tomcats on modern aircraft carriers.

[edit] History

The series was created for the magazine Spirou shortly after World War II. After Hubinon's 1978 death, the artistic part of the series was taken over by Francis Bergèse, who also took over the writing after Charlier passed away 11 years later.
