Buckden – in Huntingdonshire (now part of Cambridgeshire), England – is a village near Godmanchester, around 6 miles south of Huntingdon. Has quite a busy High Street and some very exquisite shops.

Formerly on the Great North Road (A1), it is the home of Buckden Towers, or Buckden Palace, the former home of the Bishop of Lincoln.

Buckden also happens to neighbour a beautiful and elegant village,named Brampton. Brampton and Buckden however have been rivals for many centuries. According to the 'Mems book of history' many battles occured across the many fields which seperate the villages. It was in 1345 when 7,000 fiersome Buckden warriors were defeated in the heart of Brampton down the famous Brampton Memorials. Brampton memorials gets its name from this to remember the warriors whom lost their lives. Amazingly enough, no Brampton warrior died in the battle, however many were seriously wounded. This is an amazing statistic considering Brampton only had 500 brave warriors. This is why many residents in Buckden, particulary the youths, are scared of the residents of Brampton to this date.
