The Buck Scholarship is a scholarship offered by the Frank H. and Eva B. Buck Foundation. The scholarship awards approximately twelve to fifteen students full-ride lifetime scholarships each year.

The mission of the Buck Foundation is to support education in all of its aspects. They achieve this through the Frank H. Buck Scholarship program which provides full scholarship support to students chosen from the six counties that were the congressional district served by Frank H. Buck in the 72nd through 76th United States Congresses (1932-42). The foundation also provide grant support to educational institutions, libraries and other nonprofit organizations in support of our educational goals.

Since its founding in 1990, the Buck Foundation has provided full scholarship support to more than 218 students from its geographic area. These students have attended colleges and universities throughout the United States, as well as international educational institutions. They have studied a broad range of fields — the arts, sciences, liberal arts, economics, government, law, medicine, management — and are now making a positive contribution to society and their communities. In addition to scholarships, the Foundation awards grants to support education in all of its aspects, seeking areas in which its resources can make a difference in providing quality educational experiences to a wide range of people.

The Buck Foundation, located in Vacaville, California, is housed in the Buck Mansion. The estate consists of a Victorian mansion built in 1891 on one acre of landscaped grounds including a Carriage House designed for use as a meeting space. The facilities are available on a limited basis for nonprofit meetings of 50 people or less.
