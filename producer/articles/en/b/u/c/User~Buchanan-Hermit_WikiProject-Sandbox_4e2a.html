This WikiProject is an attempt to organize articles about British Columbia into a coherent format. Some Wikipedians have formed a project to better organize information in articles related to British Columbia. This page and its subpages contain their suggestions; it is hoped that this project will help to focus the efforts of other Wikipedians. If you would like to help, please inquire on the talk page.

This WikiProject does not include the Greater Vancouver Regional District, which is managed by WikiProject Vancouver.

[edit] Title

WikiProject on British Columbia

[edit] Scope

This WikiProject aims primarily to establish a Community-driven Wikipedia standard British Columbia-related article. As members of the British Columbia community we understand how important British Columbia articles are to new users.

[edit] Goals

  1. Review and improve the quality of British Columbia-related articles.
  2. Increase the number of articles related to British Columbia in Wikipedia
  3. Organize articles and define categories relating to British Columbia and develop standard formats for same

[edit] Structure

[edit] Motivation

  1. British Columbia is the most populous province in Western Canada.
  2. British Columbia is Canada's only province with access to the Pacific Ocean.
  3. British Columbia has a diverse ecosystem, ranging from coastal areas to forests and deserts.
  4. British Columbia is home to Whistler-Blackcomb, often ranked the top ski resort in North America and co-host of the 2010 Winter Olympics.

[edit] Naming conventions

  • Places: as [[Barkerville]] with a redirect from [[Barkerville, British Columbia]], or a disambiguation page if there is more than one place or thing with this name.
  • Neighbourhoods: Urban neighbourhoods are not disambiguated (example: [[Chinatown]]) unless another place exists in the world with the same name (example: [[Chinatown, Victoria]]).
  • Schools: as [[Kelowna Senior Secondary School]], or a disambiguation page if there is more than one school with this name. This does not apply to Colleges or Universities.

  • {{BritishColumbia-geo-stub}} - Stub articles relating to British Columbia's geographical features.

  • {{BritishColumbia-sports-stub}} - Stub articles relating to British Columbia sports, teams and sports figures (other than broadcasters, who are journalists).
  • {{BritishColumbia-bio-stub}} - stub biographical articles not concerning politicians or sports figures.
  • {{BritishColumbia-hist-stub}} - stub historical articles on British Columbia or predecessor territories.
  • {{BritishColumbia-school-stub}} - Stub articles relating to British Columbia's schools and school districts. See Wikipedia:WikiProject Education in Canada for more information.
  • {{BritishColumbia-muni-stub}} - Stub articles relating to British Columbia's communities and municipalities.
