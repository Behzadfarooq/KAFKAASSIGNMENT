Surrounding Municipalities box for the GVRD

Hello, B-H. Hate to complain, since you do so much work around her, but I wanted to catch you before you go any farther on rejigging the "Surrounding Municipalities" boxes. I think there should be some sort of discussion as they're starting to look overcrowded and confusing. Personally, I don't think every space needs to be filled. It should only be for municipalities that are immediate neighbours, and we shouldn't put bodies of water. Vancouver's box is a prime example of clutter. There are so many things in there that it's become impossible to quickly interpret the data. (It even lists Hope!) The ones on the North Shore aren't perfect - they could use a restyle, and there are some layout issues - but it is much easier to get a feel for the layout of the communities. Using the TransLink station box as an analogy, you should be able to hop from one to another, each new box revealing the next municipality. Let's put our heads together and come up with something better. --Ckatz 08:39, 31 May 2006 (UTC)

Hey... just wondering if you'd had a chance to look at the latest revision in your sandbox. No rush... --Ckatz 03:48, 10 June 2006 (UTC)
Hello again... before we put this across the GVRD, we still need to revisit how exactly to fill it. I'm not convinced that every space needs to be filled - that just looks crowded. (Part of the reason for the new design code was to allow an easy way to NOT have to fill every space - the code handles it.) --Ckatz 19:02, 10 June 2006 (UTC)

Oh okay. I saw what happened [1]. I reverted your edit not because it's not accurate or anything like that. The reason for it was that it was written in caps and the sentence structure did not seem coherent. It also lacked sources and citations (which would violate WP:OR), and the use of the word "wrongly" violates WP:NPOV (as that word always has a POV slant to it). Also, the last part was added in the wrong place -- sections of an article should NEVER go after the categories. You may re-enter that information, but please make sure that a) your additions are properly cited and supported by other sources (to maintain verifiability) and b) they are written in a coherent manner that would make sense to outsiders as well (i.e. NO CAPS).
