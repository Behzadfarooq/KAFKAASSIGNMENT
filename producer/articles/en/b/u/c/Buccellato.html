Buccellato

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

You have new messages (last change).

Buccellato is a Sicilian circular cake given by godparents to the godchild and family on the christening day. The cake is supposed to be as large as possible to ensure good luck. Legend has it that the cake has even once reached the size of a cartwheel. Depending on the recipe used, it can include dried or candied fruit, etc.

A similar cake with the same name is the traditional cake of Lucca. It is donut-shaped and contains aniseed.
