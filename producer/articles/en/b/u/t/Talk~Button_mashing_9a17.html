[edit] Wear on Game Pad

Button mashing or button bashing does not wear down a gaming pad. To destroy a gaming pads buttons a great deal of force would have to be exerted, more than the quantity pressed when one is playing a stressful game.

That makes no sense. The weak link in a controller's button is the rubber "blister" that pushes the switch back up. I've had plenty of aftermarket GameCube controllers fail because the rubber gets cracked or loses elasticity. I have never seen a controller button become damaged from exerting too much force on it. You would need a lot of force, as you would have to crack the circuit board. I have a non-functional aftermarket GC controller that I could use to find out how plausible it is. I'll try using a thumb or finger, but I wouldn't be surprised if I needed to use a C-clamp to crack the circuit board. Joel Blanchette 20:28, 13 December 2006 (UTC)
