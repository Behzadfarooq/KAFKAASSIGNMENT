Butch Baird (born July 20, 1936) is an American professional golfer who played on the PGA Tour and the Senior PGA Tour (now known as the Champions Tour).

Baird was born in Chicago. He learned the game at the age of 14 from his father. He turned pro in 1959 after working in the Texas oil fields for a few years after high school.[1]

Baird won two official PGA Tour events during his career: the 1961 Waco Turner Open and the 1976 San Antonio Texas Open. The San Antonio victory came 15 years, 5 months and 10 days after the Waco win, and was the record for longest time span between victories in PGA history until eclipsed by Robert Gamez in 2005.[2][3]His career year came in 1976, when in addition to his San Antonio win, he finished 46th on the money list with $58,192. Baird now lives in Scottsdale, Arizona as do many other professional golfers. He has a golf-memorabilia marketing business, BBJM Golf Enterprises, with partner Jack Mishler. His son, Briny, is also a professional golfer.

[edit] PGA Tour wins

  • 1961 Waco Turner Open
  • 1976 San Antonio Texas Open

[edit] Other regualar career wins

  • 1965 PGA National Four-ball Championship (with Gay Brewer)
  • 1968 Texas PGA Championship
  • 1974 Florida PGA Championship
  • 1967-1969 six Caribbean Tour victories

[edit] Champions Tour wins

  • 1986 Cuyahoga Seniors International
  • 1989 Northville Long Island Classic
