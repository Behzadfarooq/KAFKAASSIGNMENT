User:Budwheizzah

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

In 1999, BudWheizzah revolutionized marijuana comedy internet radio and his station was one of the top choices of marijuana consumers. It all started in 1998 when he discovered the new software called SHOUTcast with which you could broadcast an internet radio show. He tested it on a laptop with a dialup connection for about one month and actually started getting some results right away!

Three months later, in Feburary of 1999, The BudWheizzah Weed Station was officially launched with a one-page website to go along with it. At this point an audience was already fully formed. BudWheizzah recorded over 6 months of material resulting in a near 30-hour playlist.

But at the end of the summer - the server's hard drive broke. 6 months of work were lost and it was impossible to retrieve anything. This is where BudWheizzah made the move to the name Bong.Dhs.Org: The WEED Station.

Once again in january 2001, the hard drive broke. However, this time, BudWheizzah managed to salvage most the shows.

BDO made its BIG return on March 10th year 2002. As BudWheizzah packed the schedule with live shows one after the other, he went on to going through some of the most intoxicated hosting experiences ever; He made a 8 hour shroom show, nights were he got drunk and sotned at the same time and would have to end the show, not because he was tired but because he knew he was about to pass out... or was not feeling very good ;)

As winter 2003 arrived, Bud relaxed on the live shows and concentrated on work - if he wasn't working he was out partying. Some shows were made during this time, but not enough to create a riot of fun at BDO.

In early 2004, BudWheizzah began adding what he calls "affiliate broadcasters" to his station and each gave them their own shows. Amongst these broadcasters are: Florida (Of_The_Matrix), Australia (Bevis and Heyallo), Arkansas (Georgiagrass), TheCreek (LiquidCreek) and Billingham (Jay_Man)

In late 2004, BudWheizzah arranged for the release of the first BDO label CD: Full Bowls and Other Things, Vol. 1. This volume was later followed by Vol. 2 in early 2006.

2005 marked a lot for BDO. Sales of Full Bowls and Other Things vol. 1 rushed over the expectations and winter audiences were insane.

Today, BDO Radio, BudWheizzah's long living Inetrnet radio station is still well and alive. In addition to the radio feed, BudWheizzah and his friends formed what they called "BDO Television": A series of footage from parties, magic mushroom trips and other intoxicated experiences. Some more organized videos were made, but most the videos in the BDO Television archive are direct reality.

BudWheizzah currently lives in Montreal, Canada where he works for a web development companies. To avoid avalanches of spam, an e-mail will not be provided on this page.
