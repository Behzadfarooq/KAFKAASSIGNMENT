Buddug Verona James was born in Cardigan, West Wales. She studied at the Guildhall, National Opera Studio and Rome.

Among her operatic roles Buddug has performed Gluck's Orfeo in America and Canada, Dardano in Handel’s Amadigi in New York and Europe, and Cherubino in Mozart's Marriage of Figaro in Tokyo and Toronto.

She has worked with Netherlands Opera, Cleveland Opera (USA), Glyndebourne, Almeida Opera, Opera Theatre Company, Opera Northern Ireland, Opera North, Opera Atelier, Opera Circus, English Pocket Opera, Opera 80, Cambridge Handel Opera Group, Operavox Cartoons, Siobhan Davies Dance Company, Music Theatre Wales and Mid Wales Opera.

She has premiered in operas by Gerald Barry, Jonathan Dove, Deirdre Gribbin, Wolfgang Rihm and John Woolrich.

She has filmed numerous straight acting roles for BBC, HTV and S4C and recently performed Lady Capulet in Romeo and Juliet for the National Theatre of Wales.

Buddug teaches voice at the Royal Welsh College of Music and Drama. She has directed Pirates of Penzance, Ruddigore, Acis and Galatea, Dido and Aeneas, Orpheus in the Underworld and Hercules for RWCMD.

[edit] Awards

• Welsh Singers Competition 1986

• In 2001 Buddug was honoured with the Druidic White Robe of Gorsedd y Beirdd at the National Eisteddfod.


[edit] Trivia

• Before pursuing her studies she worked in one of her father's butcher shops in Cardigan and won an award for her artistry in the Royal Welsh Show in 1978.

• In 2001 Buddug and her siblings performed in the Welsh Millennium Stadium as pre match entertainment before a Wales/Ireland match.
