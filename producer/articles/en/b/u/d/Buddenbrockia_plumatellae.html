Buddenbrokia plumatellae is a parasitic worm whose taxonomic placement long puzzled biologists. It is now classified as a Myxozoan of class Malacosporea on the basis of both genetic and ultrastructural studies. It was the first multicellular myxozoan identified and its vermiform shape gives strong support to the theory that that enigmatic phylum belongs among the Bilateria.
