Bud'da

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Bud'da
Background information
Birth name Stephen Anderson

Bud'da is a hip hop producer for artists such as Ice Cube, Dr. Dre and Xzibit.

[edit] Discography

[edit] Remixes

  • "Georgy Porgy (The Bud'da Sauce Remix)" on the single Georgy Porgy by Eric Benét. (1999)
  • "How Long" on the album Dawn by Dawn Robinson. (2002)
  • "Got To Give It Up (Remix)" on the album I Care 4 U by Aaliyah. (2003)

[edit] Production

  • "Land Of The Skanless" and "Supersperm" on the album South Central Los Skanless by Kausion. (1995)
  • "Bow Down", "Do You Like Criminals?", "The Gangsta, The Killa And The Dope Dealer", "Cross 'Em Out And Put A 'K" and "3 Time Felons" on the album Bow Down by Westside Connection. (1996)
  • "Been There Done That" (co-production with Dr. Dre), "Got Me Open", "Str-8 Gone", "Do 4 Love", "Sexy Dance" (co-production with Dr. Dre) and "Nationowl" on the compilation album Dr. Dre Presents…The Aftermath. (1996)
  • "3 Card Molly" and "Deeper" on the album 40 Dayz & 40 Nightz by Xzibit. (1998)
  • "React" and "Ghetto Starz" on the album Shut 'Em Down by Onyx.
  • "War & Peace" (co-production with Ice Cube), "Ghetto Vet", "Extradition" (co-producer with Ice Cube) on the album War & Peace Vol. 1 (The War Disc) by Ice Cube. (1998)
  • "Trust Me" on the album No Limit Top Dogg by Snoop Dogg. (1999)
  • "Record Company Pimpin'" on the album War & Peace Vol. 2 (The Peace Disc) by Ice Cube. (2000)
  • "Never No More", "Read Between The Lines" and "I Can Be" on the album Aaliyah by Aaliyah. (2001)
  • "Cash Sniffin' Noses" on the album L.A. Confidential Presents Knoc-Turn'al by Knoc-Turn'al. (2002)
  • "Stay Down", "Shake Da Spot" and "Nuthin Has Changed" on the album Thy Kingdom Come by King T. (2002)
  • "Smoke Some Weed" and "Chrome & Paint" on the album Laugh Now, Cry Later by Ice Cube. (2006)
