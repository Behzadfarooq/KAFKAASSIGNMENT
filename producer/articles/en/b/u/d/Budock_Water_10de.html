Budock or Budock Water (Cornish: Dowr Budhek) is a small village and civil parish in the Kerrier district of Cornwall, England, United Kingdom. It is on the fringe of the Falmouth urban area, about 2 miles west of the town centre. According to the 2001 census the parish had a population of 1,399.

Budock woods was a popular wooded area adjoining the village for weekend walks. One area of the woods was noted to have a great many bluebell flowers.
