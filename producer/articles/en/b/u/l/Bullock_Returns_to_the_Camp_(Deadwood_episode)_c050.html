"Bullock Returns to the Camp" is the 7th episode of the HBO original series Deadwood. It was the seventh episode of the first season. The episode was written by Jody Worth and was directed by Michael Engler. It originally aired on May 2, 2004.

[edit] Plot synopsis

Arriving at a stockade, Bullock and Charlie Utter locate the horse McCall rode out on and confront him inside the bunkhouse. After declaring themselves friends of Hickock and quickly emptying the bunk house, Bullock knocks out McCall and prepares to take him to Yankton. He tells Charlie that he’s willing to "ride ahead" and leave the two alone, but Charlie agrees to hand him over to the law in Yankton.

At the Gem, two teenagers – Flora and Miles Anderson – arrive in search of their missing father, armed only with a 12 year old photograph. Swearengen offers work for Flora, but she declines, but gives work to Miles as a sweeper. Flora heads to the Bella Union with the photograph, where she is also courted for employment by Tolliver; she agrees, and Joanie takes her under her wing.

As Alma prepares to attend the funeral of her murdered husband, Swearengen watches from the window. Feeling recovered from her withdrawal pains, Trixie warns her she must act high lest Swearengen be watching. Al sees through the ruse from his window – "That widow ain't high" and accuses Farnum of ruining his plan. Farnum stands up for himself, saying he’s tired of being a pawn and wants to know the real reason for Swearengen's interest in the claim beyond his pretext of thwarting Pinkerton involvement. Swearengen agrees to cut Farnum in on a percentage of the claim's wealth – just so long as he gets it bought.

At the funeral, presided by the Reverend Smith, Farnum sees Bullock returning and makes an inappropriate overture to Alma to buy the claim. She turns him away in disgust as Bullock arrives by her side. Later, she confides in him the details of Swearengen's scheming and her addiction. She offers to let Bullock go, but he promises to help her.

An outraged Swearengen abuses Trixie and confronts her with her betrayal, but she keeps calm and explains that opium wouldn't have made the Widow sell her claim – and that the orphan child needs someone to take care of her. Swearengen stays his hand, but warns her – "Don't kid yourself Trixie. Don’t get any mistaken ideas".

In the Bella Union, Flora reveals she isn't a virgin as Joanie instructs her on her new job. A revitalized Andy Cramed, having been nursed at the sick tent by Jane and the Doctor, arrives and confronts Tolliver. Tolliver tries to let bygones be bygones and offers to get something working, but Cramed is not willing to forget Cy's betrayal. "All I come back for, Cy, was my things, and you tossed them too."

Doc Cochran, Reverend Smith and Calamity Jane continue to tend to the sick at the plague tent - a matter complicated by Smith's seizures, which Doc fears may be attributed to a brain lesion, though the Reverend feels they may be divinely inspired.

An emotional Bullock tells Sol the story of the Sioux scout he fought, who was out to avenge his dead friend whose body had been desecrated. As he tells his story the former marshal realizes what he must do. Heading to the Gem, Bullock threatens Swearengen that he will hold him responsible for the outcome of Alma's claim; and expects him to recommend an assayer – lest he come for him. Swearengen is nonplussed and warns Bullock he should be more afraid of Swearengen coming for him.

Flora, having advanced to turning tricks at the Bella Union, returns to her prim self to collect her brother at work. Miles cheerfully greets her and recounts his day, before asking nonchalantly which of the two saloons would make a better score. Flora replies the Bella Union – but why not take them both. Both their talk and Swearengen's meeting with Bullock are disrupted by Dority, having grown fond of Flora and furious at a drunk staring at her, stabs him to death.

In Alma Garret's room, Trixie announces that she is headed back to the Gem – informing Alma that her boss saw through her subterfuge. Alma offers to finance a trip for Trixie to leave Deadwood with the orphan child and head to New York, an offer which makes Trixie angry at both her stubbornness and her ignorance at the difficulty of Trixie's situation. After shouting at Alma, she turns to storm out.

"She's about to say her name," she says to Alma, referring to the orphan. "Think of selling. If you took the money, you could hear her say it."

[edit] Notes
