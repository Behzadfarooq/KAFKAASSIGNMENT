In the fictional Star Wars galaxy, the Bulk Cruiser (officially known as the Neutron Star-class Cruiser) is a vessel designed and produced by Rendili StarDrive and has been in use since before the Clone Wars.

The bulk cruiser was designed and manufactured by Rendili StarDrive shortly after the conclusion of the Clone Wars, combining the attributes of a small warship and a cargo hauler. Although officially known as Neutron Star-class cruisers (as well as the slightly different Battle Horn-class developed shortly after), the ships were almost always referred to simply as "bulk cruisers". The ship quickly became one of the most common in the galaxy, as they could serve a variety of purposes, were relatively inexpensive to produce, and could be easily modified.

The stock bulk cruiser was slow, lightly armed, and notoriously prone to breaking down, so most owners tended to heavily modify their vessels. They saw little use in the Galactic Empire, which had Carrack-class light cruisers, Dreadnaught-class heavy cruisers, and Nebulon-B Frigates to fill any of the bulk cruiser's roles. Instead, the vessels quickly found their way in the hands of the Rebel Alliance, which used them in mass quantities early in the Galactic Civil War. Bulk cruisers also were used by pirate and smuggler groups, as well as private shipping companies and planetary defense forces, prior to, during, and after the Galactic Civil War.

Bulk cruisers could be easily modified for whatever mission they needed to carry out. Many in the Alliance were transformed into starfighter carriers, capable of carrying up to three squadrons. Their standard armament of thirty quad laser cannons was also often modified to include turbolaser batteries in order to make them better able to stand up against other capital ships. Internal modifications to create more room for cargo or passengers also occurred on some vessels.

Years after the Civil War, the ships continue to see service as pirate and smuggling vessels, private cargo haulers, or in planetary defense fleets, but are no longer a part of the New Republic navy.
