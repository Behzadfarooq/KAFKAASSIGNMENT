Bulat Aqchulaqov

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Bulat Aqchulaqov is the deputy Minister of Energy and Mineral Resources of Kazakhstan.[1]

During the 'Dialogue Organization on Asian Cooperation' seminar held on 6 October 2006, Aqchulaqov said Kazakhstan has 4.8 billion tons of oil, 3.4 trillion cubic meters of gas, and 8 billion tons of oil in the Caspian Sea. Aqchulaqov expressed his belief that by 2010 Kazakhstan's oil output to 84 million tons and its gas output to 50 billion cubic meters.[1]
