Bullwinkle's Family Restaurant was a "Chuck E. Cheese's" style family fun restaurant in Sunnyvale/Santa Clara, California throughout the 1990s, themed after its namesake, The Rocky and Bullwinkle Show.

[edit] Arcade

Bullwinkle's featured a token operated arcade section featuring popular video games such as Street Fighter II Turbo, Cruisin' World, The Simpsons, and also games which were played for tickets, such as Skeeball. The tickets could then be redeemed at a prize counter for various prizes, ranging from dracula teeth, toy soldiers or plastic spiders on the low end (5-40 tickets) to boomboxes and lava lamps on the high end (1000-2000 tickets).

[edit] Restaurant

The restaurant section of Bullwinkle's seemed to have a log cabin feel inspired by the Dudley Do-Right cartoon. The walls and support beams were made of logs and the floor had red carpeting, upon which sat many long wooden tables with wooden benches. A family would sit at one of these tables and order food from a small menu. The main fare usually included some sort of pizza, snowshoe fries and a pitcher or two of soda drink. The restaurant was often popular for the birthday parties of elementary school children, as they could play in the arcade until the food was ready.

[edit] Animatronic Show

The restaurant section of the restaurant also featured a main stage and two side stages. At regular intervals, the large red curtains on the stage would part, revealing animatronic renditions of The Rocky and Bullwinkle Show characters (though Rocky was auspiciously missing from the stage, instead existing as a voice originating presumably from somewhere out of view). Others included Dudley Do-Right and some other characters who were never characters on the show.

The characters would then put on a variety show with songs and comedy. The show included a water fountain performance, similar to that of the Bellagio in Las Vegas albeit on a smaller scale.

[edit] Fate of the Restaurant

Though the restaurant remained popular throughout the 90s among the local middle class families, it closed down unexpectedly near the end of the decade. Though not made public, the assumed cause was financial trouble. There was a void in the market for family fun restaurants after its closing, people going as far as San Jose for the restaurants Chuck E. Cheese's, The Jungle and Bamboola.

The space that the restaurant occupied was filled by a nightclub, The Backbeat, which had limited success before closing in the mid 2000s. The Backbeat was replaced by the Avalon Lounge, another nightclub, which is still operating.

[edit] Other Locations

While Bullwinkle's Restaurant is not well known in California outside of the Sunnyvale/Santa Clara community, there are other Bullwinkle's Restaurants in Wilsonville, Oregon; Cary, North Carolina; Richmond, Virginia; Miamisburg, Ohio; and Myrtle Beach, South Carolina. There is (or at least was) a Bullwinkle's in Michigan and one on New York's Long Island. These restaurants are all described as "Family Fun Center" in addition to "Bullwinkle's Restaurant".
