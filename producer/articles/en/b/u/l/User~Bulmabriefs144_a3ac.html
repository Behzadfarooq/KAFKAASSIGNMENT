I have a college degree in History with a minor in Religion. I originally planned to become a priest of the Christian or possibly Taoist order, but due to the hassle of applying to such a field in this day and age, I just basically gave up. Currently without friends my age, anything remotedly resembling a girlfriend, or any strong ambitions toward getting a job or career, I can best be defined as a Hikikomori living with his parents. In this way, my life can almost be compared with the anime show Welcome to the N.H.K. except I haven't gotten to the point where furniture talks to me.

I typically do agriculture or janitorial work when I am employed, and value working with my hands to any job that involves public image. This is as much to do with the fact that I abhor suit and tie work as it is that I like to do tasks myself rather than tell people "how can I help you?" This may contribute to my lifestyle of solitude, but on average I am much more likely to choose blue collar jobs than White collar ones.

In my free time, which is roughly 6 days a week in most cases, with an occasional job once or twice a week (though occasionally I get a part-time job). Aside from agricultural work, my second major skill is writing, and I have written everything from video games to scriptures.
