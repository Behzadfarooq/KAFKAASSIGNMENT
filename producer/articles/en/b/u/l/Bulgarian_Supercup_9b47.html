Bulgarian Supercup

The Bulgarian Supercup (Bulgarian: Суперкупа на България) is an annually held football match between the football club that has won the Bulgarian A Professional Football Group and the club that has won the Bulgarian Cup. It takes place before the new season begins.

The first Bulgarian Supercup match was held in 1989. An interruption of 14 years followed, until the competition was renewed for the 2003/2004 season.

In case the champion of Bulgaria has also won the Cup, then the Bulgarian Cup finalist and the champion compete in the match.

[edit] Winners

  • 1989 – CSKA Sofia (final match vs. Chernomorets Burgas 1-0)
  • 1990–2003 – not held
  • 2004 – Lokomotiv Plovdiv (final match vs. PFC Litex Lovech 1-0)
  • 2005 – Levski Sofia (final match vs. CSKA Sofia 1:1, 4:2 after penalties)
  • 2006 – CSKA Sofia (final match vs. Levski Sofia 0:0, 3:0 after penalties)
