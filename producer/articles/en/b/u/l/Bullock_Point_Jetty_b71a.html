Bullock Point Jetty is located on the Huon River, (the fourth largest in Tasmania at 170 kilometres long) in South-Eastern Tasmania, Australia. It lies 60 kilometres south of Hobart and 20 kilometres south of Huonville. The Jetty is located in the Municipality of the Huon Valley, the southmost council of Australia.

ACCESS - Bullock Point Jetty is located on the C639 (Cygnet Coast Road). From Huonville, take the Channel Highway (B64) 11 kilometres south to the C639 Cradoc Turnoff. Continue along the C639 for 10 kilometres until the sealed road turns into a gravel road. Bullock Point Road it the first road on the right after the start of the gravel road.

THE JETTY - The Jetty itself is an old structure and has suffered wear and tear over the years. Recently the jetty has been fixed up so there aren't so many planks of wood missing. It is stable and is around four metres from jetty to the bottom of the river.The Access to the main jetty is by a small metre wide by 10 metre long walkway. It does not have a handrail (it broke and fell into the water some years ago.)

THE FISHING - Bullock Point Jetty is one of the best fishing spots along the banks of the Huon River, and you are almost guaranteed a catch on a calm day. The Main Catches are Flathead (Dusky), Australian Salmon (Cockies) Couta and Catfish, with the occasional catch of an Antlantic Salmon Escapee from the Fish Farms downstream. After Heavy Rain, The water in the Huon gets muddy, and schools of Flathead are more common. The Huon is best fished in summer, the majority of the catch being reasonable sized flathead. At Bullock Point, the Huon River is well over 1 kilometre across.
