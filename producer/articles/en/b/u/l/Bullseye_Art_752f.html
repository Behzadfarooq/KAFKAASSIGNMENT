Bullseye Art

Bullseye Art was a graphic design and animation company that began in 1995. Founded by Josh Kimberg, Nick Cogan and Ryan Edwards, the company gained fame for its creation of unique and offbeat cartoons made using Macromedia Flash.

Bullseye Art became well known for its humour with such cartoons as Porkchops, which consisted of a donkey in surrealistic situations, and Miss Muffy and the Muff Mob, about a band of rapping muffin-headed girls. The small Asian dragon known as Hooptie-Goo was the generally used mascot for Bullseye Art, often appearing in the opening or closing credits fighting over the last slice of watermelon with a crow. The company was commissioned to do several high profile cartoon segments, the most notable of which was the opening theme to the Rosie O'Donnell show, for which they were nominated for two Emmy Awards. They also created the music videos First Tube and You enjoy myself for the rock band Phish.

Bullseye Art maintained a free website making money largely off the sales of material related to their cartoons or from other parties who would hire the company to create cartoons for them. The company changed their name to Raw Power near the end of 2003, after which the majority of the cartoons were unavailable.

In 2005 the founders of Bullseye Art relaunched their cartoons under the new moniker Magic Butter. While initially a pay site for a short while, the Magic Butter website now freely displays all of the old content along with new cartoons, T-shirts for sale, and free downloads such as MP3s, desktop wallpapers, and icons.
