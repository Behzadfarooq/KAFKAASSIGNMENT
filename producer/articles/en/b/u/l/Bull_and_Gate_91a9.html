Bull and Gate

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

The Bull and Gate is a public house in Kentish Town, London noted for its live music.

Parts of the building date back to the 17th Century although it did not open as a public house until the 1970's, with previous periods as a theatre and laundrette in the 20th century. The venue opened for live music in the mid 1980's and has hosted live music 7 days a week to this day.

The venue now enjoys a nationwide reputation as a place to see up and coming bands due to the fact that a very large proportion of now famous British artists played at the venue when they were unknown, especially among the Indie / Rock genre.

Of course most artists who play at the venue do not achieve any success, but many musicians and music fans still consider the venue to be an important part of musical history in London.

Some members of the music industry critisise the venue for it's small audiences, poor decoration and it's practice of asking performers to guarantee a minimum attendance through pre-show ticket sales. However the venue has not, as yet, operated a Pay to Play system as some other London venues will do.

Famous artists who played at the Bull and Gate on their way up include Blur, Carter USM, Suede, PJ Harvey, Ash, Kene, The Libertines, Muse, Manic Street Preachers, Coldplay and British Sea Power among many others.
