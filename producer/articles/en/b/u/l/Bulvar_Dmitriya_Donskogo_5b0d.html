 Bulvar Dmitriya Donskogo
edit

Bulvar Dmitriya Donskogo (Russian: Бульвар Дмитрия Донского) is a station on the Serpukhovsko-Timiryazevskaya Line of the Moscow Metro subway station. It is the first metro station in Moscow to be built outside of the MKAD radius. The station opened on 26 December 2002. It was built at the same time as Ulitsa Starokachalovskaya, as they are both in the same station complex. It is the only station in the metro network with direct access to the Butovskaya Light Metro Line. The light metro line opened in 2003. There are two vestibules for station access.

[edit] Station Design

The second floor of the station is flanked with black and white marble. It features thick black columns with light grey cieling tiles. A strip of green marble encases the outsde of the second-level balconies, as viewed from the lower level of the station. Black, grey, and red granite adorn the floors. There are stairs connecting the platform and the upper level of the station.
