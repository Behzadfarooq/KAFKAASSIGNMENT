Burning Brides

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Burning Brides is an American hard rock/heavy metal band. In the fall of 2002 they played selected dates as the opening band for ...And You Will Know Us by the Trail of Dead and Queens of the Stone Age. In 2004, they toured with A Perfect Circle. In 2006, they went on tour supporting Eagles of Death Metal and Peaches. They have also supported Zion, IL power duo Local H.

[edit] History

The Burning Brides formed in Philadelphia during the spring of 1999. Lead guitarist and singer Dimitri Coats and his longtime girlfriend/musical partner Melanie Campbell, the band's bassist, got the band rolling. The two lovebirds dropped out of Juilliard art school and spent two long years together traveling the country. They reportedly lived in San Francisco, Portland, Oregon, and Boston, before settling in the Philly area.

Coats and Campbell quickly teamed up with drummer Mike Ambs to form the band. Burning Brides played their first concert in May of 1999, opening up for Queens of the Stone Age. The Brides quickly realized that a lot of people were attending their local shows. Soon record companies were scouting them, attempting to sign them to a label. Eventually the signed with File 13, a local recording company. The band then kicked off a U.S. Tour with musicians/groups such as Zen Guerilla, J. Mascis, and Greenhornes. They were also building a steady fan base at their live shows.

Burning Brides first album Fall of the Plastic Empire was released on April 17, 2001. Fall of the Plastic Empire is considered the best by their fans, but didn't succeed well in the market. The album only sold just over 5,000 copies. File 13 dropped the band and another record company, V2, picked them up. The biggest hit off the album was "Arctic Snow", the band's first single. The single didn't chart however, so the band was still in little popularity. Fall of the Plastic Empire did mildly successful overall, grabbing the attention of some music magazines, writing that Burning Brides were somewhat a garage rock band on the verge. V2, the band's new label, re-released the album in 2002. The re-release of the album gained media attention, and thus gave the Brides some popularity. The NME loved it and rated the album a five out of five.

Mike Ambs left the band and so the Brides were stuck with no drummer. They replaced him with Pete Beeman. Beeman joined the band and was a perfect match for the new album the band was working on.

Burning Brides second album Leave No Ashes was released on June 29, 2004. Leave No Ashes wasn't as successful as Fall of the Plastic Empire but their fans still liked it. However, the most successful song of the album was the hit single "Heart Full of Black"" which got some radio airplay. "Heart Full of Black" was featured in two video games, Guitar Hero and Burnout 3.
