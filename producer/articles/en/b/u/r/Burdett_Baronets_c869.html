[edit] Burdett of Bramcote, Warwicks (25 February 1619)

Created in the Baronetage of England
  • Sir Thomas Burdett, 1st Baronet
  • Sir Francis Burdett, 2nd Baronet
  • Sir Robert Burdett, 3rd Baronet MP for Warwickshire 1679-1681 and Lichfield 1689-1698
  • Sir Robert Burdett, 4th Baronet MP for Tamworth 1748-1768
  • Sir Francis Burdett, 5th Baronet MP for Boroughbridge 1796-1802, Middlesex 1802-1804 and 1805-1806, Westminster 1807-1837 and Wiltshire North 1837-1844
  • Sir Robert Burdett, 6th Baronet
  • Sir Francis Burdett, 7th Baronet
  • Sir Francis Burdett, 8th Baronet (dormant on his death in 1951)
Robert Burdet had a grant of free warren in Seckington in 1327[1], and his son and heir Robert was born here in 1345[2]. The manor then followed the descent of Bramcote in Polesworth in this family[3], until 1919, when Sir Francis Burdett, 8th Baronet, sold the estate in lots. The manorial rights, attached to Seckington Hall Farm, were bought by Mr. Harry Arnold[4].
