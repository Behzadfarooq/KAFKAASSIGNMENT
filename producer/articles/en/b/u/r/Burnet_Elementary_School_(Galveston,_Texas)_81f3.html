Burnet Elementary School (Galveston, Texas)

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Burnet Elementary School is a Pre-K through 5th grade school. It is a part of Galveston Independent School District. The school was named after David G. Burnet.

The school's colors are blue and gold, and the mascot is a pirate.

[edit] Student body

During the 2004-2005 school year, Burnet enrolled 617 students [1]. 63% were Hispanic, 19% were White, 18% were African-American, and less than 1% were Asian. No Native American students were enrolled during that year. Additionally, 77% of the students qualified for free or reduced lunch.

[edit] School uniforms

Burnet requires school uniforms for its students [2].

Students may wear the following:

  • Royal blue or white polo shirts sans logos
  • Khaki, navy, or denim trousers and skirts
  • Brown or black shoes
  • Royal blue or white jackets and sweaters

[edit] Feeder patterns

The students go for middle school to either Central Middle School or Weis Middle School.

All GISD students attend Ball High School for high school.
