This event has grown rapidly and is very important to the Burning Man community. Although it only has 1500 attendees to BM's ~35000+, it's culture has a major influence on that of the larger event. Additionally, it has grown rapidly in less than a decade from a <50 person campout to its current size and next year will be moving to more land to encourage more growth.

[edit] Daily Texan article

In general, Wikipedia needs documented sources such as newspaper articles. However, I have to question whether we really want to source the Daily Texan article on this page.

To begin with, the article got many facts wrong. For example the author thought that "through appropriate holes in an ear and a vagina painted onto a large sign one could get condoms and Q-Tips, respectively" when clearly had she looked into the ear or read the sign she would have know that earplugs were being given, not Q-Tips.

Moreover, Burning Flipside is not a media event and the concept of documenting the event through media runs counter to the concept of Flipside as a participant-only event.

Please respond with your comments. I'd like to hear everyone's point of view and after 5 days either remove the article's link or leave it be. SteveHopson 15:53, 5 June 2006 (UTC)
