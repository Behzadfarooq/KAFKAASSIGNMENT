Burnside

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Burnside is the name a number of places around the world:

  • Burnside, Victoria, a suburb of Melbourne, Victoria, Australia
  • City of Burnside, a Local Government Area of Adelaide, South Australia, Australia
  • Burnside Avenue, A street/neighborhood in East Hartford, Connecticut
  • Burnside, South Australia, a suburb of the City of Burnside, South Australia, Australia
  • Burnside Business Park, in Dartmouth, Nova Scotia, Canada
  • Burnside, Canterbury, a suburb of Christchurch, New Zealand
  • Burnside, a suburb of Dunedin, New Zealand
  • Burnside, Glasgow, a suburb of Glasgow, Scotland
  • Burnside, Shetland, a settlement on the Shetland Islands
  • Burnside, Caithness, a district of Thurso, Caithness, in Highland Scotland
  • Burnside, Chicago, a community area of Chicago
  • Burnside, Kentucky, a city in Pulaski County, Kentucky, United States
  • Burnside Bridge, a draw bridge in Portland, Oregon which is the center of that city's numbering system
  • Burnside Hall, a building on the downtown campus of McGill University

Burnside may also be used to refer to the following people:

  • Ambrose Burnside, American general, for whom sideburns are reportedly named
  • William Burnside, mathematician
  • R. L. Burnside, blues musician
  • David Burnside, controversial Northern Ireland politician and public relations expert
  • John Burnside, inventor and lover of Harry Hay

Burnside is also a television series spin-off from The Bill

Burnside is a children, young people and family welfare service of UnitingCare in New South Wales, Australia.
