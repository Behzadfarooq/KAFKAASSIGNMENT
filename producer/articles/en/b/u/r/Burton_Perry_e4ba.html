Burton Perry

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

You have new messages (last change).

Burton Perry was an American who allegedly worked for the KGB San Francisco office during World War II. Perry's young brother also worked for Soviet intelligence. In 1944 Perry is alleged to have given the KGB technical particulars of a radar guided glide bomb which he had worked on.

Perry's code name in Soviet intelligence as deciphered in the Venona project is CEDAR.

[edit] Venona

Perry (Borton Perri) is referenced in the following Venona decryption:

  • 232 KGB San Francisco to Moscow, 1944 May 25.

  • John Earl Haynes and Harvey Klehr, Venona: Decoding Soviet Espionage in America, Yale University Press (1999), pgs. 292, 361, 460.
