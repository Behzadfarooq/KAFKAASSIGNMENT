Bushey Meads School (also commonly known as BMS by staff and students) is a secondary school in Bushey, Hertfordshire, UK. The current Head Teacher is Mr Keith Douglas (BA(hons) MA), who started as a new member of staff in the school in December 2005.

It is the one of the most popular schools in the area and is the most recommended place in Hertfordshire for Sixth form studies. It offers a wide range of subjects.

Various famous people have attended the school in the past. The two most notably are George Michael and Andrew Ridgeley who later formed the pop act Wham. During their time at the school, their school band was known as 'The Executive'.

[edit] History of the School

[edit] Founded

Founded in 1957, Bushey Meads reaches its 50th anniversary in 2007. It plans to celebrate this by having a major role in the annual Bushey Carnival this year, basing some events on the history and successes of the school.

[edit] The Name

The name Bushey Meads derives from the original surrounding area. The site where the school buildings now stand, was once fields surrounding the once small village of Bushey; which is where Meads (meaning fields) in the school name comes from.

[edit] Current Technology

In every Maths clasroom in the school, there is an Interactive Whiteboard, and a Computer in every room in the school.

[edit] The Future

[edit] Renovation

The new head of the school, Mr Keith Douglas, is currently pressing forward with a major renovation project at the school. This includes much needed refurbishment and redecoration of the older rooms, and repairing and painting the outside of the older blocks. As well as this, facilities are being improved, such as new equipment and books for certain departments which needed it the most.

[edit] Development

Most of the school buildings are 1970's, but there is a late 80's music block, a late 90's english block and a new science block completed in 2003. There are currently plans to make a new sixth form centre, which would improve the school's facilities even further. The school library is planned to be extended into the sixth form area, but this can only go ahead after the sixth form centre has been moved to its new location.
