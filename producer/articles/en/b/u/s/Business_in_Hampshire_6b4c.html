[edit] Law & regulations

Hampshire Trading Standards Service originated from the initial introduction of the Weights and Measures Act, and is still better known as the Inspectors of Weights and Measures. Trading Standards Service activities are still primarily enforcement of the Weights and Measures Act, but also has the following responsibilities, in whole or in part, as of 31st March 1988;

  • Agriculture Act 1970 (Part IV)
  • Agriculture Produce (Grading and Marking) Acts 1928 and 1931
  • Animal Health Act 1981
  • Business Names Act 1985
  • Celluloid and Cinematograph Act 1922
  • Consumer Credit Act 1974
  • Consumer Protection Act 1987
  • Control of Pollution Act 1974
  • Energy Conservation Act 1981
  • Estate Agents Act 1979
  • European Communities Act 1972
  • Explosives Acts 1875 and 1923
  • Fabrics (Misdescription) Act 1913
  • Fair Trading Act 1973
  • Fireworks Act 1951
  • Food Act 1984
  • Hallmarking Act 1973
  • Health and Safety at Work etc. Act 1974
  • Medicines Act 1968
  • Motor Cycle Noise Act 1987
  • Performing Animals (Regulation) Act 1925
  • Petroleum (Regulations) Act 1928 and 1936
  • Poisons Act 1972
  • Prices Acts 1974 and 1975
  • Road Traffic Acts 1972 and 1974
  • Road Traffic (Foreign Vehicles) Act 1972
  • Telecommunications Act 1984
  • Tourism (Sleeping Accommodation Price Display) Order 1977
  • Trade Descriptions Acts 1968 and 1972
  • Trading Representations (Disabled Persons) Act 1958
  • Trading Representations (Disabled Persons~ Amendment Act 1972
  • Trading Stamps Act 1964
  • Transport Act 1978
  • Unsolicited Goods and Services Acts 1971 and 1975
  • Weights and Measures Act 1985

Today the Hampshire County Council Regulatory Services Commission publishes advice on local business advice on its web site[1].

[edit] Business award schemes

Hampshire County Council and the Economic Development Office sponsor a number of local business awards.

The Portsmouth News Business Awards, sponsored by The News, Business Link Wessex and the EDO is an annual event with the categories Business of the Year, Small Business of the Year, Business Personality of the Year, Community Contribution of the Year, Exporter of the Year, Innovation of the Year, Team of the Year and Achiever of the Year. Entry is free by authorised entry form from The News business editor Jeremy Dunning on (023) 9262 2138.

Wessex Business Woman of the Year awards honour successful women in business and provide a role model to encourage other women to enter the business arena. Nominations for the award are invited from societies, businesses, the world of sport, entertainment, the arts and individuals. To get involved through sponsorship, nomination, or attending the event, contact Charlotte Davies on (01702) 559888.

Test Valley Business Awards are open to every business in Test Valley and every business can apply for an award. Categories include The EPS Networks Business in the Community Award, The Hampshire County Council Small Business of the Year Award, The Roke Manor Research Award for Technological Innovation, The Test Valley Borough Council New Business of the Year Award, The Town Centre Management Award for Retail Excellence and The Waitrose Achieving Business Excellence Through People Award. Applications for the event should be made through the web-based entry form [2].

The Hampshire Food & Drink Awards includes Best Farm Shop, Butcher/Meat Producer of the Year, Best Specialist Food Shop, Food Producer of the Year, Best Organic Trader, Drinks Producer of the Year, Newcomer of the Year, New Forest Local Food & Drink Award, Best Farmers' Market, Dining Pub of the Year, Country Pub of the Year, Chef of the Year, Restaurant of the Year and Most Distinctive Local Menu. Contact frances.stokes@hants.gov.uk for further information.
