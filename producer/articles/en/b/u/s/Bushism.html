Bushism

A Bushism is any of a number of peculiar words, phrases, pronunciations, semantic or linguistic errors that have occurred in the public speaking of United States Presidents George H. W. Bush[1][2] and, more recently, his son George W. Bush[3][4] and subsequently used in caricatures of them. The term (a neologism) has become part of popular folklore and is the basis of a number of websites and published books.

[edit] Characteristics

George W. Bush's uses of nonstandard grammatical constructions have some common characteristics:

  • Adding agentive endings to words in unusual ways, such as "suiciders".[5]
  • Constructing neologisms such as "tacular"[6][7][8] (a portmanteau of "tactical" and "nucular") and "misunderestimated" ("misunderstood" and "underestimated").[9]
  • Occasional use of spoonerisms such as "mexed missages" (mixed messages) and "terriers and bariffs" (barriers and tariffs).[10]
  • Use of words that sound similar to intended words but are wrong in the context (i.e., malapropism). "Nucular power pants" instead of "Nuclear power plants."[11]
  • Folksy pluralization of, or addition of articles to, familiar terms ("Internets",[12][13] "the Google"[14]).
  • Redundant or odd sentence construction, such as "We had a chance to visit with Teresa Nelson who's a parent, and a mom or a dad."[15]
  • Change of subject mid-sentence, such as, "I am here to make an announcement that this Thursday, ticket counters and airplanes will fly out of Ronald Reagan Airport."[16]

Some columnists, including the late Molly Ivins (the co-author of a book of Bushisms), have suggested that Bush may have difficulty speaking "Washington English", and that he may be trying to cover his accent by over-emphasizing words. Some have hypothesized that Bush is not familiar with some of the words that he feels he must use as a president.[17]

[edit] "Make the Pie Higher" poem

A poem composed entirely of Bushisms titled "Make the Pie Higher" has become popular on the Internet. Each line contains some sort of grammatical error, logical error, or unusual usage and is said to have been uttered by George W. Bush. Although its origin is uncertain, it has been attributed to Washington Post political cartoonist and satirist Richard Thompson. The poem has been criticized as apocryphal. However, the Urban Legends Reference Pages at Snopes.com have verified all but the line "I am a pitbull on the pantleg of opportunity" as having been spoken by Bush at one point or another during his presidency.[18] The site has also noted that the president made reference to "mential losses", and not "mental losses" as the poem claims. This phrase was possibly in reference to missile launches.

[edit] Other Bushisms

  • "Rarely is the question asked, is our children learning?"[19][20]
  • "I know the human being and fish can coexist peacefully."[19][20]
  • "I'm honored to shake the hand of a brave Iraqi citizen who had his hand cut off by Saddam Hussein."[19][21]
  • "Our enemies are innovative and resourceful, and so are we. They never stop thinking about new ways to harm our country and our people, and neither do we."[19][22]
  • "Make no mistake about it, I understand how tough it is, sir. I talk to families who die."[19][23]
  • "I think that the vice president is a person reflecting a half-glass-full mentality."[19][24]
  • Bush dares us to imagine ourselves as working mothers, "working hard to put food on your family."[18][25]
  • "... able to make a living and ... put more money on the table."[19][26]
  • "Too many good docs are getting out of the business. Too many OB/GYNs aren't able to practice their love with women all across the country."[19][27]
  • "Families is where our nation finds hope, where wings take dream."[19][28]
  • "This is still a dangerous world. It's a world of madmen and uncertainty and potential mential losses."[19][18]
  • "They misunderestimated ..."[19][29]
  • "There's an old saying in Tennessee—I know it's in Texas, probably in Tennessee—that says: fool me once, shame on ... (long pause) shame on you? (long pause) Fool me, you can't get fooled again."[30][31]
  • "One of the things I’ve used on the Google is to pull up maps."[32][33]
  • "You teach a child to read, and he or her will be able to pass a literacy test."[34][35]
