Bus station

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Bus station in Norwich, UK

A bus station or bus depot is a structure where city or intercity buses stop to pick up and drop off passengers. It is larger than a bus stop, which is usually simply a place on the sidewalk that buses can stop. It may be mainly intended as a terminal station for a number of routes into a downtown area, or as a transfer station where a number of routes continue.

[edit] Hong Kong

Hong Kong owns an extensive network of bus routes carrying millions of passengers in the city. Large bus termini are often built near housing estates, shopping malls, ferry piers, MTR and KCR stations. Most of them are in open areas, and others are beneath of high podia, so that double-deck buses can stop there, if lack of open areas nearby.
