BushBuck Charms, Viking Ships & Dodo Eggs

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

BushBuck Charms, Viking Ships, & Dodo Eggs was an educational computer game released in 1991. Designed by the Australian company Reckon, the game was published by PC Globe, a small US-based company that specialized in "edutainment" software in the late 1980s and early 90s. In the tradition of Brøderbund's Carmen Sandiego games, "Bushbuck Charms, Viking Ships, and Dodo Eggs" taught geography through a storyline that involved a global scavenger hunt for unusual items such as those in the title. The game ran on a DOS platform, and although out of print, is still downloadable.

[edit] Gameplay

The game is played by flying from city to city seeking treasures as laid out at the beginning of the round. The game has three possible modes, one player with no computer player (not available in advanced skill), one player with computer player, and two player.

The game starts in a randomly selected city which becomes the home base for that round's competition, to which the player must return to redeem treasures for points and additional tickets. If the player collects all available items, a one way flight back to the home base city is rewarded. Five items are made available at any one time with a total of fifteen items available for the entire round.

[edit] In-Game Menu

Eight different buttons are available on screen at all times, as well as the exit button and the indicator (thermometer in beginner and eye in intermediate and advanced).

City On/Off Toggle

Allows the player to choose whether or not to have the cities show on the map. Does not effect cities with clues.

? Toggle

When lit up, give player hints during play. If clicked, will pull up a red screen explaining the in-game menu options.

Pointer Mode Toggle

Gives the player control of a pointer which will, when placed over a city, indicates the city name.

Key Toggle

When clicked, brings up a legend to indicate what on the map is what.

Magnifying Glass Toggle'

Allows the player to choose between a close up view of the region he/she is currently in, or a full world map view. When lit up, indicates that zoomed in mode is in use, when dark, indicates world map mode is in use.

Music Note Toggle

Switches music on/off.

Airplane Toggle

Puts player in flight mode, allowing the player to choose which of the available cities he/she would like to fly to.

Item Toggle

Opens the item menu, allowing the player to see what items are onboard, what items are available, and allows the player to view any collected clues.

[edit] Weather

Different weather related obstacles are presented while heading from city to city. At times the player may have to choose to either fly through a blizzard, typhoon, hailstorm, rain storm, hurricane, or pick another city than the one intended. If the player chooses to go through the storm, they may make it, or they may be redirected back to the original city. If there are any items onboard when flying through inclement weather, they will be roughed up, and be worth less points when turned in.

[edit] Tickets

The player starts with a set number of tickets at the beginning of the round (60 in beginner, 50 in intermediate, 40 in advanced). One ticket is used per flight, and if a player chooses to fly through a storm and does not make it to the desired destination, between one and three tickets need to be cashed in to pay for damages to the plane. When the player returns items to the home base, 10 additional tickets are given per item returned.

[edit] Infoboxes

Each city has an "infobox" which pops up either when you fly into the city, or when you click on the city in the pointer mode. The infoboxes contain varied interesting information about the city, from famous people from there to who founded it, and have an underlined feature such as a monument or attraction present in that city.

[edit] Clues

Clues are available at certain cities as indicated by a little black box on the map. Five clues are available for each item, and the clues get progressively more helpful as the player finds them. City features, historical facts, and pop culture references, as well as infobox information is used.

[edit] Skill Levels

The game has three levels of skill: Beginner, Intermediate and Advanced.

As a "Beginner" you face a stereotyped French person called Pierre. On Beginner, there is a gauge on the left on the screen that shows how close you are to an item. So in theory, you could follow the gauge until you, by a process of elimination, stumble upon the item.

As an "Intermediate" you face a stereotyped Russian person called Natasha. On Intermediate, the gauge is removed and is replaced a button with an eye on it. When you are in the right city, the eye lights up, which when pressed gives the item over to you.

As an "Advanced" you face a German aristocrat called Otto. On Advanced, the eye remains, but the lighting up feature is removed so the city which the item is in must be pinpointed exactly to reveal the item in question. Also Otto occasionally flies long distance flights that the player cannot. He also occasionally sends the player to the wrong country, not the city the player clicked. If the player happens to be in the same city as Otto when an item is found, Otto will take it. Also if the player flies to a country Otto has visited a picture of Otto appears above the infobox with a speech bubble saying "Otto vaz ere!"

[edit] Trivia

  • As this was released in 1991, The Eastern Bloc consisting of East Germany, Poland etc were independent countries where as Russia was still called the USSR.
  • Czechoslovakia was still a country.
  • There are various MIDI snippets of classical music pieces when for example, an item is found.
  • The items rotate at random so occasionally an item is asked for that has been collected on a previous game, so it is possible to remember where the item is.
