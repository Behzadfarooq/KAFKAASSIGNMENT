[edit] 3RR

Please refrain from undoing other people's edits repeatedly. If you continue, you may be blocked from editing Wikipedia under the three-revert rule, which states that nobody may revert a single page more than three times in 24 hours. (Note: this also means editing the page to reinsert an old edit. If the effect of your actions is to revert back, it qualifies as a revert.) Thank you. Luna Santin 03:48, 5 October 2006 (UTC)

While I can sympathize with your wanting the article to reflect your particular point of view, it's often very difficult or impossible for anyone to maintain a truly neutral tone when writing about themselves, which is part of why Wikipedia has some autobiography guidelines.

I can't say for sure what the other editor's concerns are -- at this point, my belief is that his main concerns arise from our neutral point of view and verifiability policies, both of which will often serve to protect corporate articles from vandalism or opinion-pushing -- just as Coca-Cola wouldn't be allowed to turn their article into an advertising forum, another editor wouldn't be allowed to add "coke sucks," to the article; these policies protect all sides of an issue and help to maintain Wikipedia's credbility.
