Blythe Bridge Signal Box

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

It has been suggested that this article or section be merged into Blythe Bridge railway station. (Discuss)

Blythe Bridge Signal Box was opened by the North Staffordshire Railway (NSR) in 1884 on their Derby to Stoke line. The box was built to a standard McKenzie & Holland design and under the S.R.S designation system is referred to as a MKH Type1.

The Signal Box was equipped with a standard McKenzie & Holland lever frame and a Gate Wheel for operating the Level Crossing Gates which controlled traffic on the busy former A50, these gates were later replaced by a barrier crossing when traffic got too heavy for the Signalman. Blythe Bridge Signal Box finally closed in 1980 and the level crossing was converted to CCTV control with the barriers supervised by Caverswall.
