Blood, Guts, Bullets, and Octane is a 1998 independent action comedy film (with elements of dark humor) written and directed by Joe Carnahan. The film stars Joe Carnahan and Dan Leis as two salesman of a failing used car dealership who are paid $250,000 to allow a 1963 Pontiac LeMans convertible onto the dealership lot for two days.

The film has since been negotiated to be developed by Carnahan and producer Bob Levy as a prime time series on NBC [1].

[edit] Trivia

  • Much of the equipment used for the film came from the television station where Carnahan was employed.
  • Several of the crew also starred in the film including producers Ken Rudulph and Kevin Hale.
  • Mitchell Wayne Richter is played by Matt Carnahan, the younger brother of writer and director Joe Carnahan. Other relatives in the film include Leah Carnahan and Chuck Leis.
  • Mike Maas played both Victor Drub and the Dumpster Bum.
