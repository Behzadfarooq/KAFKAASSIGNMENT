Bloch Brothers Tobacco Company
(U.S. National Register of Historic Places)
Bloch Brothers Tobacco Company (1889)
Location: Wheeling, West Virginia  United States
Added to NRHP: 1992
Reference #: 92000881

The Block Brothers Tobacco Company (also known as Helme Tobacco Company) of Wheeling, West Virginia are best known for their Mail Pouch Tobacco. Mail Pouch was a popular chew advertised on thousands of barns, most of which were located in the rural Ohio Valley. Each barn had an end or side painted with the tobacco logo. The brothers began manufacturing stogies in 1879 as a side-line to Samuel Bloch's wholesale grocery business. It was discovered that the left-over stogie clippings could be flavored and packaged in a paper bag, and then sold
