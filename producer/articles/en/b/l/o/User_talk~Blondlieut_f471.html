  • Greetings Blondlieut! Frankly, I have no way of knowing how substantial the changes to an article are before I look at it. I just look through the entries in Recent Changes (there's a link on the navigation box). I click "hist" to view the history of an article, compare the last two editors, and if the last editor wasn't a vandal, I check the article for spelling. Ninty-nine percent of all my edits are minor, I just enjoy participating.

[edit] Re: Blocked.

Hm? Last time I blocked any AOL ranges was a full week ago, and then I only did it for fifteen minutes. I'm not trigger-happy with rangeblocks, I only turn them on in the event that vandalism is getting out of control, to the point that the RC patrollers can't keep up with it. Your suggestions are duly noted, but please understand that AOL doesn't work the way you think it does -- AOL users will often change IP addresses with each pageload, based on how their backbone is set up; in most situations, it's either block everybody, or let it all through. If I ever do block AOL, I always do so with a good reason, and more importantly, always with "block anon users only" checked. Luna Santin 03:11, 18 September 2006 (UTC)

What probably happened was that Wikipedia got hit by an AOL denial-of-service vandal. Because of the mechanism for enforcing blocks on Wikipedia, the underlying IP of a blocked account is automatically blocked if that account attempts to edit a page or perform similar actions. However, AOL users frequently change IP addresses, as Luna Santin just described. A blocked user who is operating from AOL can force an entire AOL range to be blocked from editing Wikipedia. The sysops of this project try to catch those blocks when they happen, but sometimes it takes some time before it is noticed. For more information, and some tips on how to avoid getting snagged by such vandals, you might try reading Wikipedia:Advice to AOL users. Hope that answers your query a bit better. --Grass Stains 06:51, 18 September 2006 (UTC)
