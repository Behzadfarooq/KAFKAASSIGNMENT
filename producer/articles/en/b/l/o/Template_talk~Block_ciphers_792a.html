[edit] Categorisation

This is a good idea; I really like this. I'm unsure about the categorisation, though. Current categories are:

  • Historical
  • AES finalists
  • Other
  • Broken
  • Misc

Is there a distinction between "Other" and "Misc"? Also, what qualifies as "Historical"? Also, "Broken" is a word with a few meanings: Three-way is broken "certificationally", i.e. a theoretical weakness not really much concern in practice (a related-key attack). By this measure, RC5 and DES are also "broken". Just criticisms at the moment, I haven't got a better categorisation myself... — Matt 10:26, 16 Apr 2004 (UTC)

Hi, I meant Misc to be ciphers which are not "regular" ones, like TEA and XTEA are special purpose, and the other two are hoaxes.
Ah, OK. (Though I would dispute that TEA and XTEA are especially special purpose...) — Matt 11:32, 16 Apr 2004 (UTC)
By broken I did mean theoretical weakness. So RC5 should be moved to broken. But I agree Broken is not a very useful categorization. Arvindn 11:13, 16 Apr 2004 (UTC)
You'd also need DES, TEA and Lucifer, which would mean they fall into a couple of categories; they don't form a partition; maybe it would be easier to have just an uncategorised list? — Matt 11:32, 16 Apr 2004 (UTC)
I'd say even a half-assed categorization is better than none at all, because such a large number of items without a visually helpful arrangement is hard on the reader. (Does it matter that its not a partition?) After all, the box is meant to be a navigational aid, not a formal classification system. Arvindn 11:48, 16 Apr 2004 (UTC)
Maybe a non-partition isn't a problem, but it would be best to list the cipher multiple times to avoid implying things by omission. My fear with categorisation is that there may not be a good one; e.g. if you have AES finalists, it might be fair to include NESSIE's selections, and CRYPTREC's recommendations, some of which overlap. Alternatively, classify by Feistel networks, vs SP structures? My rough stab at a nav box. is at User:Matt Crypto/MediaWiki:Block ciphers, but it doesn't include any categorisation of the ciphers. — Matt 12:12, 16 Apr 2004 (UTC)
Looks good. We'll go with yours. Arvindn 13:28, 16 Apr 2004 (UTC)

[edit] Bottom rather than right align?

A lot of the block cipher pages have (or will have) a long, thin diagram; currently, it's positioned aligned right on the pages that have them. This box sort-of collides with these diagrams at the moment, e.g. XTEA, especially at lower resolutions. I think this might be better floating at the bottom of the article, such as the topic box at Delta blues. — Matt 10:35, 16 Apr 2004 (UTC)

I tried to put it at the bottom right, but with the current shape I couldn't get it to fit under the diagram. Feel free to try out other shapes and placements. Arvindn 11:13, 16 Apr 2004 (UTC)
