Blood Sugar Sex Magik is the title track from the Red Hot Chili Peppers' 1991 album of the same name. It is the tenth track on the playlist and is one of the heaviest songs on the album with a very dark guitar riff, bass chords, and low vocals from Anthony Kiedis. The song portrays sex in many poetic forms and metaphors, with a very lively chorus. Thought it was never released as a single, it stills recieves occasional air time on radio stations and has been rarely played at live concerts.

[edit] Trivia

  • The verses of Blood Sugar Sex Magik are reminiscent of the song "Love Trilogy" off of their album The Uplift Mofo Party Plan.
  • According to Kiedis' auto-biography, several of the lyrics were written about him and his girlfriend of the time.
