[edit] Article name

The move of this from "blowfly" to "blow-fly" seems rather pointless, as both, plus "blow fly", are correct, and in common use. Also, according to Wikipedia:Naming conventions (common names), the most common name should be used, and according to the google hits for "Blow-fly" Calliphoridae (almost 1,000) versus "Blowfly" Calliphoridae (over 6,000), "blowfly", the original name of this article, is by far the most common, and "blow-fly" seems to be even less common than "blow fly". However, since the usage apparently varies partly by what country's English one learned (the OED was the only reference work I found using the hyphenated form [and even it had usage citations using the single-word form]--all others used either one word, or two separate words), I don't want to move it back and start a new 'whose English should we use' can of worms. Niteowlneils 03:44, 5 May 2005 (UTC)

[edit] Redirect

The subject "blow fly" (which really should not be hyphenated) should redirect to Calliphoridae. All blow flies belong to Calliphoridae, but not all Calliphoridae are commonly known as blow flies.
