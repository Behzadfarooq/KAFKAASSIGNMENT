Blood of the Vampire is a 1958 film which, despite the title, is not a vampire film.

[edit] Plot summary

Spoiler warning: Plot and/or ending details follow.

A young couple (Vincent Ball and Barbara Shelley) are terrorized by Dr. Callistratus (Donald Wolfit) who was executed but has returned to life with a heart transplant. Along with his crippled assistant Carl (Victor Maddern), the 'anemic' mad scientist, believed to be a vampire, conducts blood deficiency research on the inmates of a prison hospital for the criminally insane to sustain his return to life.

[edit] Trivia

  • Donald Wolfit is best remembered today as the inspiration for the film The Dresser (1983), in which Albert Finney plays a larger-than-life actor-manager modelled on Wolfit.
  • This was an early attempt to copy the Hammer Film Productions style. This style was copied more successfully a few years later by Amicus Productions and Tigon British Film Productions.
  • Future star of Scars of Dracula, Dennis Waterman appeared with Victor Maddern in the 1962 American sitcom Fair Exchange.
