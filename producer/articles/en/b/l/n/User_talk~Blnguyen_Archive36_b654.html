[edit] All-rounders

Personaly I don't see much difference between Warne, Gillespie, Kumble and Agarkar in batting ability. Gillespie has a marginally higher average than the others but that is solely down to a single knock accounting for nigh on 20 pct of his test runs. And that knock was of course not against the best attack in the world. Reiffel is slightly different - he is currently in the list of historic all-rounders so of course can't really be used as a bowler who can bat that misses the mark of all-rounder like the others. To my mind he is not a true all-rounder though, shouldn't be on the list and would be a good example of a near but not quite. Historically I think Wasim and Marshall are the two best examples - Wasim had the talent to be a top 6 batsman without doubt but perhaps not the application. On the other hand Marshall (like Warne perhaps) wasn't blessed with the innate ability as a batsman but worked hard at it and got better. For me Warne is the current best example of a near miss not because he is the nearest miss but simply because being the worlds leading wicket taker and with his off field antics he gets more news coverage than the others and therefore is likely to be more familiar to the non-cricket fan. --LiamE 20:31, 14 December 2006 (UTC)
