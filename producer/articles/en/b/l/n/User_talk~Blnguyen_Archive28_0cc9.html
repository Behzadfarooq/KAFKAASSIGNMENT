[edit] Protecting against edit wars

Full protection should be a temporary measure. If users are edit warring, they are obligated to stop and if they do not they can be blocked. Full-protect blocks everyone, when it is only a few aggressive offending parties causing the trouble. They, specifically, can be prevented, and semi-protect is sufficient to keep them out. They are of course free to behave. —Centrx→talk • 22:39, 6 October 2006 (UTC)

As far as the pages which I protect, mainly the Indian religious disputes, I strongly feel that the protection is the better option as it is always the same group who are always warring, and not thrid party collateral damage. As such it is better to lock the page as the users involved also have skills at article writing and it would be better to divert them to some more productive editing rather than lock them out completely. As for the Falun Gong stuff - those guys are incorrigible and do little else than fight, continuously and indulge in irrelevant flaiming on the talk pages. I am also astonished that you delete the {{deletedpage}} after two weeks and the pages are sometimes recreated. Blnguyen | BLabberiNg 02:55, 9 October 2006 (UTC)
Low time periods would not be a problem for that, and part of the effectiveness of a block is not that they are prevented from editing during that time period, but that at any other time they will also be discouraged from edit warring lest they be blocked. Regarding deleted pages, the vast majority of these are not re-created, and the reason I unblock some relatively recent ones that have only been re-created during a short period of time is because, working on the other end, there was a huge backlog that only grows and grows because no one cleans up the deletedpages, and they do prevent the creation of legitimate pages. —Centrx→talk • 03:03, 9 October 2006 (UTC)
