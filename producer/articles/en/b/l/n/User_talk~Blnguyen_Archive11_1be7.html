[edit] Chemical compounds are not "automatically notable"

There's even an official policy at Wikipedia:Notability (numbers)#Related issues. Chemical compounds are "not by definition encyclopedic, unless they have some sort of unusual chemical, economic or industrial significance". The particular compound antimony trichloride, however, is certainly notable, because it was known to the alchemists as "butter of antimony" and it is used as a reagent for detecting vitamin A and other carotenoids. If you had added a fact like that before removing the importance tag, I'd be much happier. In the future, please don't simply remove the importance tag without adding anything to show importance. —Keenan Pepper 04:55, 13 July 2006 (UTC)
