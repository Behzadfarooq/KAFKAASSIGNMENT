A note

It is too late for me to add this to my main page, as I'm banned, but I'll add this here. I have edited Wikipedia, on and off, for some time, without disruption, as an anonymous IP. I figured I didn't need to establish a reputation to make common-sense edits. When I ran into User:IronDuke on the Portland Seven page, everything changed, and I was suddenly called a "sock" because my IP address changes (inevitable side-effect of DHCP). He called me this enough that I have been banned, though for no reason I can figure out. Some look at my history and say "Your first edits were to WP:ANI!" or whatever, but in fact, the reason I got an account was to fight the accusations of being a puppet. Perhaps independent observers can see the circular logic here: 1) edit anonymously -- get called a puppet; 2) complain about this -- get told to get an account; 3) get an account -- get banned! I have recently complained on WP:ANI about this, but I will probably just go back to quiet anonymous editing until it happens again -- but this aspect of Wikipedia's "anyone can edit" policy is definitely not as advertised. -- User:BlindVenetian 21:19, 27 June 2006 (UTC)
