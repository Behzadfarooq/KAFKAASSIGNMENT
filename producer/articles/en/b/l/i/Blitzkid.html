[edit] History

Blitzkid began in the winter of 1997 in Bluefield, VA. with guitarist/vocalist/songwriter TB Monstrosity and bassist/vocalist/songwriter Argyle Goolsby. After the band recorded "Songs for The Aesthetically challenged" a third member was sort after.

In 1998, the trio began songwriting, rehearsing, and playing local gigs. In May of 1999, they recorded the 7-song REVISITED EP. Which showed a more "horror rock" approach that the band had already had. The band have also stated in an interview was that "We didn't set out to be a horror band it just kind of happened...we all gravitated towards that." [1]

By the winter of 1999, Blitzkid entered Lively Sound Studios in Lewisburg, WV to record the 13 song full length "Terrifying Tales" album. Soon after lots of touring which saw Blitzkid not onlyy just playing local shows but also shows further afield, the revamped creeprock trio began work at Real 2 Reel studios in Blacksburg, VA in the summer of 2001 for the ever popular among fans, 12 track, "Let Flowers Die" album.

"Let Flowers Die" was unleashed upon unsuspecting fiends December 29th, 2001, and in December of 2003, Fiend Force Records began working with the band; placing the track "Lupen Tooth" by them on their debut compilation album "This Is Horror Punk". This was then followed by the Euro release of their 2003 recording "Trace Of a Stranger".

Fiend Force Records officially released the Blitzkid/Mister Monster split EP "Exhuming Graves and Making Dates". After extensive touring to promote both releases (including the band's first successful stint in Europe with the Spook on the "Everyday is Halloween" tour in October 2004), Blitzkid regrouped with Fiend Force as their official label and continued to play nonstop shows.

In the time that followed, Blitzkid found themselves at SS Studios (Hamilton, NJ.) in May of 2005 tracking what would become their fifth full length and most sucessful album to date, "Five Cellars Below". By early 2006, Blitzkid had completed work on the new record and embarked on more live shows that led to them touring Germany once again (to promote the impending release of the new album) with Fiend Force's The Crimson Ghosts in June, and once they returned to their home country, the states, the band embarked on their biggest U.S. tour yet that saw them play 58 dates from June 29th to Sept. 12th 2006.

On August 25th of that year, "Five Cellars Below" was released worldwide via Fiend Force Records and in October, the band set out to tour Europe for a third time on the "Hell Nights Tour" with labelmates The Rezurex, the Other, and Bloodsucking Zombies from Outer Space. The tour saw them playing the Czech Republic, the Netherlands, Sweden, Finland, Denmark, Spain, Austria, Holland, Hungary, and finally Germany.

[edit] Future

At the present, Blitzkid plan on continuing to tour to promote "Five Cellars Bellow" album (as well as the newly re-issued first album "Terrifying Tales" with an accompanying live dvd) and early 2007 will see them make their first trip to the west coast as well as the southwest, and more dates in the southeast.
