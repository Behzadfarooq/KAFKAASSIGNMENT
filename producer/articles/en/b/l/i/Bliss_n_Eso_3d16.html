Bliss n Eso are an Australian hip hop crew (along with DJ Izm) based in Sydney.

The crew started after Bliss and Eso became friends in high school and found a common love for hip-hop music. Their first release was the EP "The Arrival". The crew then released a mixtape CD prior to signing to Obese Records, and releasing their full length Flowers in the Pavement LP. The album features the songs "Rubbed The Lamp The Wrong Way" and "This Is For You". The album features production by Suffa and Weapon X as well as Bliss himself.

The group's second official album, Day Of The Dog, was released in March 2006. It has achieved considerable commercial success with its singles "Up Jumped The Boogie" and "Party At My Place", easily surpassing that of Flowers In The Pavement. The album features production from Bliss, Weapon X, Suffa & MC Motley, among others. It features guest spots from British emcees MC Motley and Mystro, as well as fellow Sydney MC, Hyjak. The album was released on Illusive Sounds.

The group have released their third single "Then Till Now" and is receiving airplay on Rage and Video Hits.

Bliss n Eso embarked on their first national tour, entitled the "Day Of The Dog" tour, with support acts Phrase & DJ Flagrant. They will be embarking on their next tour, "Get Loose", in September 2006.[1]

The duo attended the first Australia & New Zealand Urban Music Awards this year and were interviewed on radio station 92.9 on their roles in the award show.
