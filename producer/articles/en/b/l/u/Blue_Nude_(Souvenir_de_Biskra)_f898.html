The blue nude Blue Nude (Souvenir de Biskra) is a 1907 oil painting by Henri Matisse. It is located at the Baltimore Museum of Art as part of the Cone Collection.

Matisse painted the nude when a sculpture he was working on shattered. He later finished the sculpture which is entitled Reclining Nude I (Aurore)

The Blue nude made out of paper cuttings is the 1952 version, and is not the one you are looking at now.
