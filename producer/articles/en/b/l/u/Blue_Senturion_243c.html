The Blue Senturion is a fictional character in the children's series Power Rangers: Turbo. He is based on the Signalman character from Gekisou Sentai Carranger.

[edit] Character history

He is a robotic law enforcer from the future, but came to the present to deliver the "Millennium Message" to the Rangers. Blue Senturion resembled an armored blue Power Ranger with a white helmet. He was captured by Divatox and reprogrammed to destroy the Power Rangers, but was able to restore his default programming. He summoned his own Zord, the Robo Racer (a white police cruiser), to destroy Divatox's monster.

He assisted both teams of Turbo Rangers, until he was turned evil with the aid of a magic wishing coin. Naturally, this was only temporary. Blue Senturion was highly disciplined and followed all laws and regulations on Earth, and when not fighting aliens, could be found performing police duties, such as giving parking tickets, and helping people cross the street. His Morpher/Weapon the Senturion Synergizer, could actually function as a traffic light, and control cars (this was very helpful when the Storm Blaster and Lightning Cruiser were "possessed" by pizzas on their tires). He left with Dimitria to help with a crisis on Zordon's home planet of Eltar. On the finale of Power Rangers in Space, the Countdown to Destruction, Blue Senturion could be seen fighting alongside the Phantom Ranger against the Machine Empire.

Despite the fact that Blue Senturion was not officially called a Ranger, some fans consider him to be an "Other Ranger", since he looked like a Ranger, had his own Zord, and even had a Morpher (the "Synergizer", which could change from Morpher Mode into a Sword Mode and a Gun Mode).

The Blue Senturion was voiced by David Walsh.

[edit] Trivia

  • The Blue Senturion was seen in an episode in S.P.D. Stakeout, in the video game being played by the Rangers.
  • David Walsh wasn't credited for doing the Blue Senturion's voice until the Power Rangers: Turbo episode, The Accident.
  • Despite his name, he stated in "Stitch Witchery" that his favorite color is yellow.
  • Despite being the most reliable form of robotic law enforcement in eight galaxies, Blue Senturion had an odd propensity for getting caught in Divatox's schemes and fighting against the Rangers--this happens in The Millennium Message, Stitch Witchery and the two-part story of Beware the Third Wish and The Gardener of Evil.
  • Blue Senturion also appeared to develop a friendly relationship with the Blue Ranger. Justin was noticeably the most welcoming of the rangers at the end of his debut episode, and, in the series finale, bequeathed to him the lucky coin used to wish him 'good again' in The Gardener of Evil. They hugged briefly and Blue Senturion said, "I will miss you, Blue Ranger."
  • His counterpart in CarRanger, SignalMan, had an ex-wife, Sigue, and a son, Sigtarou. It is unknown if the same goes for the Blue Senturion.
