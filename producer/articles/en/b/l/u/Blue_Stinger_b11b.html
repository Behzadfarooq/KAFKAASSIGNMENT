Blue Stinger is a game was notable for launching nine days before the Dreamcast needed to play it. It was localized in America by Activision, which took away the cinematic style cameras, and used a fixed, behind-the-player third-person view like Tomb Raider. This revamped camera style was met with both critical acclaim and criticism. Some argued that the change took away from the dramatic tension for which the Japanese version had become famous. However, others celebrated the new camera style as a feature that gave the game an edge over survival horror games such as Resident Evil, that utilized fixed angles.

[edit] Story

65 million years ago, a meteorite crashes into the Yucatán Peninsula, subsequently wiping out the dinosaurs and paving the way for humans. Now, in 2000, an island emerges where the meteorite is thought to have landed, and is granted the name "Dinosaur Island." A biotech corporation then takes up shop on the island. In 2018, Eliot Ballade, an elite member of the ESER forces, is vacationing near Dinosaur Island when something falls from the sky and leaves the island sealed under a mysterious dome of energy. When a mysterious, ethereal being called Nephilim shows up to chase Ballade, he takes it upon himself to solve the enigma of Dinosaur Island.[1]

[edit] Characters

  • Eliot G. Ballade: The game's protagonist, an ESER member on vacation.
  • Dogs Bower: Eliot's teammate, Captain of a HMS Sienna. Eliot finds Dogs on Dinosaur Island. Based on Resident Evil's Barry Burton (loosely).
  • Janine King: A member of KISS, Kimra's security; both a genius in computers and shooting. Some things remain a mystery about her, but strangely enough, she seems to know a lot about Dogs.
  • 'Nephilim': An alien creature that often observes Eliot and Dogs' progress. Although taking the shape of a fictional goddess from a fictional series, Nephilm has no distinct original shape.

[edit] Gameplay

Blue Stinger does offer some unique aspects. For one, the environments are entirely 3D, something that at the time was a rarity for games of its ilk. For another, with the US release, the camera was changed to something more analogus to Tomb Raider.[2]


Players healed themselves in real-time using food and drinks bought from vending machines, forcing players to heal only when it was safe. Combat comprised of purchasing weapons and ammunition in the same manner and the technology of each weapon was very high; players could afford lightsabers, rocket launchers, Napalm launchers and triple-barrelled shotguns. Players could access melee or fire arms in the player's inventory with the press of a trigger and each weapon was exclusive to the character the played as: Eliot started out with a pistol while Dogs started out with a crossbow.


Blue Stinger differs very much from the survival horror game genre despite popular belief. For example, there are no absolutely atmospherics in the game: the soundtrack is comprised of a triumphant and often overly-climactic action score and the enemies, despite being gruesome and partly amputatable, sprinkle the player with gold coins once killed.
