BlueStar

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

A DAF SB120/Wright Cadet in the "Bluestar" livery

BlueStar is a brand name used for premier Solent Blue Line services.

[edit] Fleet

The services are operated by the newest buses and also by refurbished older buses (typically Volvos), there are however a number of unrefurbished buses operating with BlueStar branding including a number of Volvo B10B transferred from the Oxford Bus Company in 2005. The newest buses are ten Mercedes-Benz Citaros.

On 13 October 2006, Solent Blue Line acquired 1 brand new Volvo B7TL/Enviro 400 and was given fleet number 758. This vehicle was originally intended for Wilts and Dorset; evidence can be seen with regard to the interior livery all done in the Wilts and Dorset branding (the seats and the colouring of the passenger safety bars). The vehicle is in BlueStar livery. An Optare Solo received BlueStar branding in February 2007 after spending some time in plain blue with no Solent Blue Line branding.


[edit] Routes

  • 1: Southampton - Chandler's Ford - Winchester
  • 2: Southampton - Eastleigh - Fair Oak
These were the first Bluestar services to be launched. Double decker buses are normally used on these services.
  • 3: Southampton - Hedge End - Botley
Bluestar 3 is the route most often served by the unrefurbished Volvo B10B. Originally only going as far as Hedge End the route was extended to Botley from 18 February 2007, however the service frequency was reduced from a bus every 20 minutes to every 30 minutes
  • 4: Southampton - North Baddesley - Romsey
Launched in early 2006. Replaced service 15.
  • 7: Southampton - Marchwood
Began on 18 February 2007 offering a fast hourly link between Southampton and Marchwood Monday to Saturday daytimes.
  • 8: Southampton - Totton - Marchwood - Applemore - Hythe
  • 9: Southampton - Applemore - Dibden Purlieu - Hythe - Blackfield/Fawley/Langley
These services were launched on 2 April 2006 and replaced Solent Blue Line services X8, X9 and 38. The services are usually operated by the Mercedes-Benz Citaros which carry branding specific to the routes. It is not unusual to see other Bluestar branded branded buses on this service despite it dedicated fleet.

Bluestar routes with a late night service are branded as 'Nightstar' with the route numbers having a 'N' prefix. Nightstar operates outbound from Southampton on Friday and Saturday nights for a flat fare of Â£3. There are no Nightstar services on routes 7 and 8.

[edit] Bluestar 10, 11 and 12?

The Southern Daily Echo[1] reported that more Solent Blue Line routes were to be branded as Bluestar services from 18 February 2007.

  • 10 and 11: Southampton - Totton - Cadnam
Remains as Solent Blue Line services 30 and 30A. It is possible that the reliveried Optare Solo was planned for use on these routes.
  • 12: Southampton - Totton - Calmore Estate
Remains as Solent Blue Line service 32.

The change did not take place. It is unclear if and when the routes will be renumbered.
