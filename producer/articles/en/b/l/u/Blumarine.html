Blumarine is a fashion company that was created in 1977 in the town of Carpi in Italy by Anna Molinari and her husband Gianpolo Tarabini. The late designer Franco Moschino also helped the company develop a "look" of edgy romanticism [1] This look has been continued by Molinari, who remains the company's chief designer.

The name was inspired by Molinari and Tarabini's favorite color and their love for the sea [2] The Blumarine fashion line debuted in 1980 at the Modit, a small fashion premiere in Milan. In 1981 the fashion line was featured in the first fashion show during fashion week. Both times, it was met with success. During those times, there were only a few items.

[edit] Timeline

  • In 1986, the first full collection designed by Molinari debuted at the show Milano Collezioni.
  • In 1987, the new collection Miss Blumarine for girls was created.
  • In 1988, Blumarine becomes Blufin, a joint stock company. Today, Gianpolo and his son Gianguido are the sole directors of the licensing department.
  • In 1990, the company's first boutique opened in Via della Spiga.
  • In 1995, a new line called Anna Molinari was added to Blumarine. This was actually designed by Molinari's daughter Rossella Tarabini. This line was more feminine, with small details and long flowing lines.
  • Also in 1995, Anna Molinari decided to add yet another line to Blumarine. Called Blugirl, it is a collection for girls and young women that ultimately debuted in 2002 at Milano Collezioni, where it enjoyed success.

[edit] Current activities

Blumarine is currently expanding it's fashion lines to Russia and other places, where it can be found in some of the most famous stores.
