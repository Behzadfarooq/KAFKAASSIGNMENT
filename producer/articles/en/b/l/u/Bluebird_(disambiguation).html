Bluebird may refer to:

[edit] Birds and plants

  • Bluebird, a medium-sized type of bird in the genus Siala of the thrush family Turdidae, found in the Americas.
  • Fairy-bluebird, specifically two species found in Asia, which are not related to the American bluebirds, belonging to the genus Irena.
  • 'Bluebird', a Hydrangea cultivar.

[edit] People

  • Blue Bird (person), youngest members of the Camp Fire Girls.
  • Blübird, a rock duo in Portland, Oregon

[edit] Business

  • Bluebird Compartment Car (New York City Subway car), a design of advanced rapid transit subway cars.
  • Blue Bird Corporation, a manufacturer of school and transit buses in the United States of America.
  • Blue Bird Group, a taxi service in Indonesia.
  • Bluebird Toys Plc, a British toy company.
  • Bluebird Records, a sub-label of RCA Victor designed to compete with ARC Records
  • Blue Bird Toffee, a British confectioner.
  • Bluebird Cargo, a cargo airline based at Keflavík airport in Iceland.
  • Bluebird Buses, previous name of Northern Scottish Omnibuses Ltd (a subsidiary of Scottish Transport Group) from 1992 until its absorption into Stagecoach East Scotland.
  • Bluebird Foods Ltd, a New Zealand foods manufacturer.
  • The Mad Bluebird, a bluebird photograph widely used on merchandise.
  • Nissan Bluebird, a car manufactured by the Nissan Motor Company.

[edit] Literature

  • The Blue Bird (fairy tale), also known as L'Oiseau Bleu, a fairy tale by Madame d'Aulnoy and collected by Andrew Lang in The Green Fairy Book.
  • L'Oiseau Bleu or The Blue Bird, a fairy play by Maurice Maeterlinck which popularised the image of the Bluebird of Happiness.
  • The Blue Bird, a poem by Mary Coleridge, set to music as a song by Sir Charles Villiers Stanford.
