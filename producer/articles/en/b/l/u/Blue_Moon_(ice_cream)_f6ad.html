Blue Moon (ice cream)

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Blue Moon ice cream is the name of a number of bright blue colored flavors of ice cream. All of the varieties of Blue Moon are available almost exclusively in the mid-west United States.

Blue Moon is found mainly in Michigan, Minnesota, and Wisconsin; found less frequently in Indiana, Iowa, Illinois and Ohio; and has reportedly been available (either in the past or currently) in Missouri and even western Pennsylvania. A possibly similar, possibly identical flavor has also been sold in Italy under the name "Puffo", which is Italian for "Smurf"; it was probably given this name because its blue color resembled the Smurfs.

The varieties of Blue Moon vary in both color and flavor. Many aficionados of each variety of Blue Moon claim that their variety is the "real one," the "original," etc. Some dairies that make Blue Moon keep their ingredients a secret, adding to the mystique.

Blue Moon ice cream is also known as a defence against "The Wigwam", a mythological place originating in central Wisconsin. It is said that Blue moon acts as though it were a shield against the "dark arts" of "The Wigwam". It is said that after eating Blue Moon ice cream your soul is encased in a holy aura that not even the darkest of crafts can break through.

  • Karin's Sweet Shoppe in Hudson, OH makes and delivers blue moon ice cream.
  • The curly Cone in South Bend Indiana makes a blue moon soft serve that taste kind of like fruity pebbles/licorice.
  • Sherman's Finest Ice Cream of South Haven, Michigan makes such a flavor and tasters have been known to believe that the flavor is reminiscent of anything from Pistachio to Licorice to Coconut to Almond. Sherman's was once available only in Western Michigan, but is now available through a large part of Michigan, some of Indiana, and the Chicago area. To add to the complexity of the story, Sherman's gets their flavoring and coloring from a company in New Jersey. It is a possible that many of the versions use this flavoring and coloring.
  • Cedar Crest Ice Cream makes a Blue Moon ice cream.
  • Hudsonville Ice Cream of Hudsonville, Mich., located on the west side of the state makes Blue Moon ice cream.
  • Plainwell Ice Cream Company of Plainwell, Mich., located in the southwestern part of the state, also makes Blue Moon ice cream.
  • Kopp's Frozen Custard made a Blue Moon frozen custard.
  • [http://www.handelsicecream.com/ Handel's Ice Cream makes Blue Moon seasonally/occasionally at various locations
  • Another version is described as a frozen version of the Blue Tail Fly Cocktail. The ingredients for the Blue Tail Fly are Blue Curacao, and White Creme De Cacao. The Blue Curacao gives the ice cream its distinct blue coloring and an orangy taste. The White Creme De Cacao adds a chocolate/vanilla flavor.
  • Bridgeview Vinyard & Winery makes a Blue Moon chardonnay.
  • Chocolate Shoppe Ice Cream of Madison, Wisconsin sells and distributes a version of Blue Moon ice cream that is light blue in color and tastes a lot like Fruit Loops.
  • Other versions of the Blue Moon flavor are claimed to have an Orange/Almond flavor.
  • Recipes can be found on the internet that include pineapple chunks. It is unclear if this is a different Blue Moon entirely or an older recipe. However, Willoughby Hills Sweets & Treats of Cleveland, Ohio, purportedly serves a Blue Moon flavored with pineapple and cantaloupe.
  • One ice cream stand (now defunct) in Plattsmouth, NE offered a Blue Moon ice cream with similar flavoring to the totally unrelated Blue Moon Beer. This flavor tasted like what you would expect a light blue marshmallow to taste like, minus the marshmallow taste.

The USPTO does not list any trademark for Blue Moon ice cream, which may partially account for the different versions.
