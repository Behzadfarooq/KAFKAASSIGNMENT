Blues and twos

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

A police car of the Devon and Cornwall Constabulary with the common blue light bar

Blues and twos is a colloquial term that refers to the Emergency vehicle equipment of blue flashing lights and two-tone sirens that the Emergency Services in the UK use when responding to an incident. Its use is restricted to:

  • Police
  • Fire
  • Ambulance
  • Mountain rescue - both military and civil
  • HM Coastguard
  • Mine Rescue
  • Human Tissue & Transplant
  • Forestry Commission Fire Service
  • Army Bomb Disposal
  • Naval Nuclear Monitoring
  • Blood Transfusions Services
  • RAF Armament Support
  • Royal National Lifeboat Institution
  • HM Revenue and Customs when investigating serious crimes

Regulations in the police force regarding driving under 'blues and twos' vary from force to force, but the generally accepted rule is that it is not permitted unless the driver is trained in response driving, although legally no qualification other than a driving license is required to drive 'blues and twos'. In only a few forces are the the use of lights alone to pull a vehicle over to the side of the road permitted by non-response trained drivers.

Blues and Twos was also the title of a fly-on-the-wall documentary series in the UK that followed one crew from a different emergency service in each episode.
