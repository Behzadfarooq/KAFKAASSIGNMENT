BlueMotion is a trade name (developed externally by Origin Brand Consultants) for fuel-efficient automobile models from Volkswagen. The company introduced the name in 2006 on the Polo BlueMotion, with BlueMotion versions of the Golf, Passat, and Touran planned. The name refers to VW's corporate colour, blue, and echoes DaimlerChrysler's BlueTec.

The BlueMotion Polo uses a special 1.4 L Diesel engine which achieves 3.9 L/100 km (72.4 imperial MPG, 60.3 US MPG) while producing just 102 grams of CO2 per kilometre. Both figures beat the hybrid Toyota Prius.

Volkswagen is using the introduction of what is now the most economical model in its car lineup to start the BlueMotion sustainability initiative. And this initiative not only applies to the car, or its fuel economy and emissions, rather it applies to the entire company.
