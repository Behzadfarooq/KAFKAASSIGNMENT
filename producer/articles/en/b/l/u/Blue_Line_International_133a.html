Blue Line International

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Blue Line ferry Ancona, Ancona Harbour, Italy

Blue Line International is an international maritime company, specialized in transport of passengers and vehicles between Croatia and Italy.

Blue Line International was established in 1993, with its first passenger ship Kraljica mira which sailed towards Italy transporting humanitarian aid and refugees. Kraljica mira was sailing at that time under the flag of SEM Maritime Company (SMC), and in 1997 the passenger ship Split 1700 joined the same company.

SMC existed on the Croatian - Italian market up to 2003, when the maritime company Blue Line International succeeded its maritime tradition.

The passenger ship Ancona, the largest ship in the Adriatic Sea was bought in 2003 and has been sailing together with the passenger ship Split 1700 for over several years on Blue Line's regular route Split-Ancona-Split, maintaining also the seasonal lines Ancona-Hvar-Ancona and Ancona-Vis-Ancona.
