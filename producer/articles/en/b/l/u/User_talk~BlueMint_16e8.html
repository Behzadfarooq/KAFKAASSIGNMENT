So if you could ban our IP address (71.216.49.150) from editing articles on Wikipedia, that would be great. If you need any sort of signature from the principal or the server administrator I can get that to you in a snap.
Much appreciated,
Lars Moede a.k.a. BlueMint
P.S.
If I should send this to someone else, such as a higher-level administrator, please tell me. Thanks.

Hi, your IP address was not banned but was merely temporarily blocked as a result of earlier vandalism. Vandalism blocks on IP addresses generally last only for a couple of days (this one expired after 31 hours). We do not generally "ban" IP addresses (block them permanently), especially since IP addresses are sometimes re-assigned to different users. See our blocking policy for details. Looking at the contributions for this IP address shows that most of the edits it has made are useful, and that blocks of contributions are usually a week or two apart, and that all the vandalism took place on one day (and was probably the work of one or two people). Fixing the odd bit of vandalism and issuing a temporary block is routine here, and we can simply repeat this if it happens again. We don't consider it worth discouraging useful edits by blocking indefinitely if the problem is not a serious one. Thanks – Gurch 21:15, 27 November 2006 (UTC)
