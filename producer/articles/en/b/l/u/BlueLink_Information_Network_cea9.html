BlueLink Information Network is a virtual network of Bulgarian non-governmental organizations (NGOs) based in Sofia, Bulgaria, which offers a variety of internet-based services for NGOs and others interested in issues related to the environment, natural resources and sustainable development.

[edit] History

BlueLink Information Network was founded in 1997 by social and environmental activists with the aim of improving the capabilities of the Bulgarian NGO sector through the use of Information and Communication Technologies. The BlueLink Pilot Project was started by ECO-CLUB 2000, a Bulgarian Environmental NGO, with the financial assistance of Milieukontakt Oost-Europa, a Dutch NGO which supports environmental sustainability in the countries of Europe and Asia. In April 1999, the Bulgarian NGO recognised BlueLink's as coordinator of the sector for electronic communications. This role was integrated into the “Final Declaration” of the National NGO Meeting.

[edit] Milestones

1998

  • The BlueLink Information Network was created from a joint initiative of eight NGOs from five major Bulgarian cities.
  • The BlueLink Pilot Project was launched by ECO-CLUB 2000 with the financial assistance of Milieukontakt Oost-Europa. A Steering Group was formed by the founding organisations and an Internet domain was registered
  • BlueLink launched it’s first mailing lists, ICT training workshops and regular website content (derived from ECO-CLUB 2000 newsletter)

1999

  • The role of BlueLink as the primary communication and information dissemination tool of Bulgarian NGOs was formalized at the “National Meeting” in Plovdiv. The meeting’s Final Declaration also recognized BlueLink as the Bulgarian NGO sectors electronic communication coordinator.
  • BlueLink began producing the “Day of the Earth” weekly television program
  • BlueLink created a network of over 40 environmental journalists to focus attention on issues in the Black Sea region

2000

  • The BlueLink team was nominated to participate in the concluding phase of the annual festival “Media and Sustainable Development” in Paris for its TV report “Kozloduv, - the Nuclear Dilemma”
  • BlueLink participated in an Environmental Journalism workshop organised in co-operation with the Bulgarian Ministry of Environment and Waters. BlueLink arranged for two international environmental journalists (BBC correspondent Alex Kirby and Columbia University professor Steve Ross) to speak at the workshop
  • BlueLink Information Network joins the Association for Progressive Communications
  • BlueLink organized a seminar on “Civil Practices of Access to Environmental Information” focusing on intersectional co-operation between NGOs, state institutions, business and media. Thirty representatives of environmental civil organizations, state institutions, business sector, and media took part in this event.

2001

  • Online, searchable NGO directory added to www.bluelink.net
  • BlueLink and Sofia University offer “BlueLink Internship for Information Technology Students” whereby technical students learn from and contribute to BlueLink Information Network as part of their degree program
  • Along with it’s municipal and NGO partners, BlueLink organizes six distinct ICT seminars and workshops for Bulgarian NGOs
  • BlueLink works with the Bulgarian Government to synchronize EU and Bulgarian environmental law

2002

  • Bluelink and Regional Environmental Center for Central and Eastern Europe (REC) organized an e-networking workshop in Priština, Kosovo (Serbia) and Pula, Croatia
  • BlueLink, along with Romania’s Strawberry Net and REC, organized the first meeting of the eight electronic networks in Europe’s southeastern Balkan region
  • BlueLink publishes the Manual for Access to Environmental Information
  • BlueLink launches a web portal for the protection of Pirin National Park(www.bluelink.net/pirin/)
  • The one year cooperative project “Regional Environmental Content Sharing in the SEE Region” launched by BlueLink with the financial support of REC
  • BlueLink and its partner EcoSouthWest hosted and presented a seminar series on the “Strategic Use of the Internet for NGOs”
  • In cooperation with the environmental organisation "EcoSouthWest" – Blagoevgrad, BlueLink built an online database on the river basins in Bulgaria (www.bluelink.net/water)
  • BlueLink launched of the NGO node (www.bluelink.net/chm) of the Bulgarian Clearing House Mechanism (CHM) for exchange of biodiversity information

2003

  • BlueLink presented several workshops on the Strategic Use of the Internet to NGO audiences
  • BlueLink launched the web portal www.see-environment.info – a website intended to facilitate cooperation between NGOs in the SEE Region and to encourage universal access to information
  • BlueLink presented the SEE Regional Environmental Content Sharing platform (www.see-environment.info) at the First meeting of the Aarhus Convention Task Force on Electronic Information Tools, in Sofia,
  • BlueLink printed the Manual on Electronic Networking and Internet Publishing for Environmental NGOs in SEE Region, intended to support environmental networks and NGOs in using the Regional Content Sharing Platform (www.see-environment.info)
  • BlueLink took part in the APC Council meeting in Cartagena, Colombia,
  • BlueLink organized an NGO meeting on climate change issues in partnership with DHV and the Energy Institute within the project for actualization of the Bulgarian National Action Plan on Climate Change.
  • The Environmental Content Sharing in SEE region (http://www.see-environment.info), a partnership project, initiated by BlueLink, became a finalist in the Stockholm Challenge Awards programme for pioneering IT projects worldwide. The Stockholm Challenge is a global awards programme for innovative NGO ICT projects.

2004

  • BlueLink was selected by the NGO community to administrate the election process by which NGO representatives are placed in different governmental bodies and working groups
  • BlueLink took part in the Second meeting of the Aarhus Convention Task Force on Electronic Information Tools in Geneva, Switzerland. At the convention, BlueLink presented the Regional Environmental Content Sharing in the SEE Region project
  • BlueLink launched the Bulgarian portal on Climate Change (www.bluelink.net/climate) developed as part of the Bulgarian National Action Plan on Climate Change.
  • BlueLink, in cooperation with Milieukontakt Oost-Europa and Journalists legal environmental center Erina, Macedonia organized the Aarhus and ICT Seminar in Struga, Macedonia, The subject of discussion was using ICT tools in the framework of the Aarhus convention and how civil society organisations can encourage implementation of legislation promoting access to the information on environmental matters.
  • BlueLink developed a website for the NGO campaign “NO to Belene Nuclear Power Plant” (www.bluelink.net/belene)
  • BlueLink participated in the Annual Networking Meeting of Milieukontakt Oost-Europa in Amsterdam, and presented the web portal “Environmental Content Sharing for South East Europe (www.see-environment.info).
  • BlueLink's project: Environmental Content Sharing for South East Europe (www.see-environment.info) was presented as an example of NGO collaboration, networking and exchange of information at The Free Media Forum in Prague. Czech Rebublic
  • BlueLink designed and implemented the Bulgarian ICT Policy Monitor (www.bluelink.net/wsis); a site built in support of the UN World Summit of Information Society program. BlueLink’s ICT Policy Monitor is part of a network of similar sites built by other APC members worldwide
  • BlueLink participated in the Third meeting of the Aarhus Convention Task Force on Electronic Information Tools in Geneva, Switzerland. The Recommendations on more effective use of electronic tools to provide public access to environmental information were finalized.
  • BlueLink conducted the first online election campaign placing NGO representatives into official municipal processes. The Ministry of Environment and Waters requested two representatives for the Bulgarian Government operating program “Environment”, and BlueLink used its NGO election platform to carryout the election process.
  • BlueLink co-facilitated the ICT Seminar ‘Electronic networking – GreenKit Shaping its Future”, organized by Milieukontakt Oost-Europa, in Khmelnitsky, Ukraine. The main goal of the seminar was to develop GreenKit's organization, management, strategy and structure.
  • BlueLink and it’s NGO coalition began monitoring the activities of the Sofia Municipal Council as part of an Open Society Institute supported initiative aiming to help guarantee public responsibility, the protection of the public interest and to encourage progress and development
  • In partnership with DHV and the Energy Institute, BlueLink presented the new National Action Plan on Climate Change to a NGO workshop participants.
  • BlueLink made a presentation at the “INTERAUDIO” training seminar organized by Radio Corax in Halle, Germany. BlueLink discussed electronic NGO networking and access to environmental information
  • BlueLink published the "Manual on the Strategic Use of Internet for NGOs" supported by C.S. Mott Foundation. The guide follows BlueLink’s CSO training curriculum, which is designed to demonstrate how to work effectively in an electronic network.. The manual is available for download at www.bluelink.net and www.bluelink.info.
  • BlueLink officially launched its Civil Society Information Portal: www.bluelink.info. The purpose of the new portal is to provide Civil Society Organizations (CSOs) with the service already available to environmental NGOs at www.bluelink.net. The new portal was intended to be a virtual community, allowing cooperation and information exchange between CSOs

[edit] Management and Organization

BlueLink Information Network is a joint project of several Bulgarian environmental NGOs named here as “Founders”. In its grassroots stage, the project was financially supported by the Dutch Foundation Milieukontakt Oost Europa.

BlueLink was registered as a foundation under Bulgaria’s Non-Profit Organizations Act in 1999. According to its charter, registered in the Sofia City Court, BlueLink is governed by an Executive Board appointed by the Council of the Founders – the supreme body of BlueLink. To fulfill its mission BlueLink receives grants from sponsor organizations in Bulgaria and abroad. BlueLink also receives donations from organizations, which make use of its services. BlueLink is operated by an Executive Board. The supreme body of BlueLink is the Council of the Founders made up of the eight NGOs and three individuals responsible for creating BlueLink.

The activities of BlueLink are managed by a team, headed by BlueLink's executive director. Other team members include a project manager, a content manager and a web designer. The team also includes part time and voluntary assistants.

The advisory body of BlueLink is called the “Steering Group”. It consists of one representative form each founding organization.

[edit] BlueLink’s Founders

Founders of this organisation were Centre for Environmental Information and Education, Sofia; Environmental Youth Club Terra, Blagoevgrad; Academica GeoEcoClub, Veliko Tarnovo; Balkani Wildlife Society, Sofia; environmental association Za Zemiata (For the Earth), Sofia; Bulgarian Biodiversity Preservation Society Semperviva, Pernik; Pavel Antonov, Vassil Beyazov and Willem Tiebbe Oostenbrink.

BlueLink’s Executive Board

It currently (2007) comprises Natalia Dimitrov (Chair) from the Environmental Club Terra - Blagoevgrad; Paval Antonov; Petko Kovachev, Centre for Environmental Information and Education; Petko Tsvetkov, Balkani Wildlife Society and Ilian Iliev, Public Centre for Sustainable Development.

It team comprises Milena Bokova as executive director; Milena Georgieva as project manager; Elina Racholova as content manager; Mikhail Bozgounov as web and network developer; and Michael Boudreaux as project and content coordinator, consultant and volunteer.
