The Blanchard Valley Regional Health Center, or BVHS, is a regional hospital serving a large northwestern Ohio area with campuses in Findlay and Bluffton, Ohio. The hospital has been headquartered in Findlay since 1895, while the Bluffton Community Hospital was founded in 1908 by Dr. J. J. Suter. The hospital later merged with Blanchard Valley in 1995.

[edit] History

The Blanchard Valley Regional Health Center, located in Findlay, Ohio, has served an eight-county area for more than 100 years. Blanchard Valley Regional Health Center is one of the largest employers in the area with 1,500+ associates and serves Allen, Hancock, Hardin, Henry, Putnam, Seneca, Wood and Wyandot counties in Ohio.

The hospital has gone through a series of official name changes. The hospital was considered Blanchard Valley Hospital since 1895, then its name changed in 1995 to Blanchard Valley Regional Health Center after the merger with the Bluffton Community Hospital.

In early 2007, the two hospitals plan to take on different identities. Blanchard Valley Regional Heath Center in Findlay will be reverted to Blanchard Valley Hospital as in 1995. The Bluffton campus will be changed to Bluffton Hospital. The hospital plans to obtain a new website and street address. Both hospitals will remain part of the Blanchard Valley Health Association.
