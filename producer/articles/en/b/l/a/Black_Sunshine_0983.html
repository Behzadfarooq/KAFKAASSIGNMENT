"Black Sunshine" is a song off the album La Sexorcisto: Devil Music, Vol. 1 by White Zombie which was used as a promo single in 1992 and 1993. The song can also be found on Rob Zombie's Past, Present & Future and the greatest hits album The Best of Rob Zombie. The song is about a racing car called "Black Sunshine". The audio sample "I work on this baby the same way, trying to get maximum performance." is taken from the 1965 movie Faster, Pussycat! Kill! Kill!. Also, the lyrics contain the words "to the devil a daughter comes", most likely a reference to the movie To the Devil a Daughter. The singer Iggy Pop provides the additional vocals for both the intro and outro of the song. He also plays the writer in the "Black Sunshine" music video.

In 2000, the song was covered by Lesser Known for the White Zombie tribute album Super-Charger Hell.

The music video of the song, which was filmed in black and white, features the band playing in the basement of an old dilapidated house. The animated characters Beavis and Butthead provided support for the band by "reviewing" the video during the episode "Home Improvement".
