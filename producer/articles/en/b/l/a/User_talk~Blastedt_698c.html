A better place to find help about signatures is Wikipedia talk:Signatures, so that's where I went to find more info on this issue. A search through the talk page archives yields several discussions about templates in signatures, and I think the best explanation is here. In short, using a template for a signature not only increases server load but is also too easy to vandalize, meaning that anyone could change every signature you've ever left anywhere on the wiki in the blink of an eye. I almost changed yours just a second ago to prove it, but my sanity quickly returned. :-)
Your code is fine – just put it into your user profile in your user preferences and check the 'raw signature' box. If you'd still like to pursue it, ask a question at Wikipedia talk:Signatures or at Wikipedia:Village pump (technical) for more expert advice. Happy editing - KrakatoaKatie 21:56, 5 April 2007 (UTC)
