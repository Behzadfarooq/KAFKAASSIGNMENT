[edit] The Axe, Revisited

Sadly, we've reached the stage of the game where we have to start following the rules if we want to keep going. You've probably read the lengthy citation list at Talk:FLCL, but I'll break it down for you anyway.

Most of the pictures you uploaded will simply have to go, and I task you with justifying the sustained existance of the ones you say we keep. Please don't take this the wrong way - I'm still grateful for your effort - but in your enthusiasm you forgot to add a mandatory justification of each image. If you name the ones you want to shaft, I'll file the wikiwork, but it's up to you to come up with justifications for the rest. It's a sad thing to say, but blind abandon is the wrong approach - we're not supposed to upload as many pictures as we can lay our hands on. I know. It saddens me as well.

Tell me when you've made your decisions; in the mean time, I've got more templates to screw around with. -Litefantastic 02:22, 16 August 2006 (UTC)

[edit] Fair use images

I removed your image on your user page because: "Fair use images may be used only in the article namespace. Used outside article space, they are not covered under the fair use doctrine. They should never be used on templates (including stub templates and navigation boxes) or on user pages. They should be linked, not inlined, from talk pages when they are the topic of discussion. This is because it is the policy of the Wikimedia Foundation to allow an unfree image only if no free alternative exists and only if it significantly improves the article it is included on. All other uses, even if legal under the fair use clauses of copyright law, should be avoided to keep the use of unfree images to a minimum. Exceptions can be made on a case-by-case basis if there is a broad consensus that doing so is necessary to the goal of creating a free encyclopedia (like the templates used as part of the Main Page)". MARromance
