Tennis: the Words





[edit] A

  • Ace- when you hit a really good serve and the opponent trips over himself, but doesn’t hit it
  • Advantage- when you are leading a deuce point. Oh Yeah!
  • Alley- what wimpy doubles players use to make the game easier
  • Anticipation- what good players say they are good at when trying to be modest
  • ATP- Guys chasing a yellow ball with a stick

[edit] B

  • Backhand- hitting the ball on the left side of your body. A basic stroke
  • Baseline- where players with bad reflexes play
  • Baseliner- a person with bad reflexes that hits the ball hard
  • Buggy Whip Shot- a forehand shot that no one will ever use
  • Bye- when you get a free pass to the second round. A.k.a. cheap

[edit] C

  • Call- what most umpires are bad at making
  • Cannonball- What Mitchell Ralph’s serves look like
  • Chip- A term that bad players use instead of “slice”
  • Chip and Charge- a strategy created in a mental institution. (Use only if retarded)
  • Court- Where I go when not at school

[edit] D

  • Deep-Where your practice pays off, and you drill a ball into backcourt
  • Deuce- A scoring term meaning 40-40, and also that you need to score two consecutive points to win the game
  • Dink- Chickening out and tapping the ball barely is a dink hit
  • Doubles- playing with two people on each side of the court. Something I would never play
  • Drop Shot- Dinking the ball on purpose, to drop the ball directly on the other side of the net

[edit] E

  • Eastern Backhand- a grip used mainly for backhand shots (duh!)
  • Eastern Forehand- a grip used mainly for forehand shots (duh!)
  • Extreme Backhand- a grip that only allows you to use one shot well. It’s so amazing
  • Exhibition Match- A match that you play just for the heck of it
  • Egg- another name for a tennis ball

[edit] F

  • Fault- When you accidentally get distracted and miss your first serve
  • Flat- A type of serve with no spin. Or it could be considered the only serve that I can hit
  • Follow Through- What coaches scream at you to do, even when you hit it in anyway
  • Forehand- THE main hit in tennis. What most players need to work on
  • Frame- The part of the racket that is made of graphite

[edit] G

  • Game- A scoring term meaning 1/7 of a set. The first to win 4 games, wins
  • Game Point- the last point in a game, where if you win you cheer and holler, but if you lose you cry and play the next point
  • Grip- “the most fundamental aspect of T…”How you hold your racket
  • Grand Slam- a huge tournament event that I can never get tickets to
  • Ground Stroke- Either a forehand or a backhand. A term you never use in real life

[edit] H

  • Hacker- a newbie usually missing Shots. Very easy to pick out
  • Hail Mary- a defensive lob used for extra time and positioning
  • Half Volley- Hitting a ball after a short bounce (not really a volley)
  • Head- The part of the racket that holds the strings.
  • Hold- To win the point while you are serving. When you are good, it is winning yours and theirs

[edit] I

  • In- what a ball is when it lands in between two lines
  • Inside-Out- A cross-court strategy
  • Inside-In- A down the the line strategy
  • Insurance Break- Kicking butt and winning two straight points on an opponents serve
  • ITF- "The lesser known" The unknown Tennis Foundation

[edit] J

  • Jack Knife- a tennis move that not even the pros use
  • Jamming- A term meaning that you hit the ball right at the opponents feet, or towards there body
  • Jimmy- To overcome impossible odds and win a point
  • Jump- if you need to read this definition to understand the word, you are dumb. If you are dumb, to jump is to push yourself into the air with your legs
  • Jump Smash- Smashing the ball while in the air

[edit] K

  • Kick- A shot with a lot of backspin and sidespin
  • Kick Serve- A plain serve, but with a kick
  • Kill- A shot that the opponent can’t return
  • Knockout- to beat someone in a knockout tournament
  • Knockout Competition- A competition where you are knocked out when you lose

[edit] L

  • Let- When you serve and miss, into the net. (Sucker)
  • Lob- To hit the ball really really really really high
  • Lob Volley- To hit the ball really really really really high on a volley
  • Love- A scoring term meaning 0
  • Lucky Loser- A person who lost, but didn’t get eliminated, because another player got disqualified

[edit] M

  • Match Point- A point that you win to win the match
  • Mis-Hit- when you don’t hit the sweet spot on your racket
  • Mixed Doubles- When a boy and girl play against another boy and girl in a doubles match
  • Moon Ball- a lob hit with topspin
  • Moon Volley a hard volley lob hit with topspin

[edit] N

  • Nap- what you do after you play a long match
  • N’ Error- An error in your play that causes you to miss your ball
  • Net- The thing in the middle of the court that looks like a stretched out net
  • New Balls- Replacing the old balls in a match to add more spin or bounce
  • No-mans land- the area in between the baack and middle lines

[edit] O

  • On- to be in your best form
  • Open Court- the area on the opponent’s court, where your opponent can’t reach
  • Out- when the ball bounces outside of the sidelines
  • Overhead- another term for a smash; a shot hit above your head
  • Overrule- when the judge opposes the call made by the court official

[edit] P

  • Passing Shot- Hitting the ball right by your opponent while he is running forward
  • Point- A scoring term meaning you beat your opponent once; the smallest unit of score
  • Post- the green things that hold the net up on the sides
  • Pressurization- what gives balls their bounce and lightness; the inside is pressurized air
  • Pusher- A player that is patient and tires his opponent out

[edit] Q

  • Quadrilateral- the shape of a tennis court
  • Quadriplegic- someone who can’t play tennis
  • Qualies- the qualifying round for a tournament
  • Qualifiable- someone able to participate in the qualies
  • Quitter- Wait? Who could quit playing tennis?

[edit] R

  • Racket- The “stick” that you hit the ball with
  • Rally- you and the opponent hitting the ball back and forth for a long time
  • Receiver- who the ball is being served to; i.e. returnee
  • Referee- The guy who sits in the tall chair and makes the overrules
  • Running- what you do everyday in practice

[edit] S

  • Serve- When you toss the ball into the air, and then hit it to the opposite side of the court
  • Set- a unit of score that is made up of 6 games; is 1/3 of a match
  • Spin- putting spin on the ball by “scraping” the ball with the racket
  • String- the part of the racket that touches the ball when you hit it
  • Stroke- hitting the ball (includes backswing, contact, and follow through)

[edit] T

  • Tank- To lose a game on purpose
  • Tap- To barely hit the ball (Wimp)
  • Tennis Ball- what people chase with a stick, to make a living
  • Toss- when you toss the ball into the air in preparation for a serve
  • Touch- when you run into the net, causing you to lose the point

[edit] U

  • Underspin- where the ball is spinning backwards, causing a low bounce
  • Umpire- the guy who makes calls on the court; a.k.a. court official
  • Underestimate- what you should never do to your opponent
  • Unforced Error- When you mess up, and it wasn’t the opponent’s fault
  • USTA- United States Tennis Association; runs all U.S. tennis programs

[edit] V

  • Variety- what you need to have on every shot
  • Vaunting- another word for Boasting (ill-advised)
  • Velocity- The only thing that the ball has a lot of
  • Vibrazorb- a piece of equipment put on your racket, to absorb the shock of impact when hitting the ball
  • Volley- hitting the ball before it bounces on your side of the court

[edit] W

  • Western Backhand- a grip used mainly for topspin backhand shots
  • Western Forehand- a grip used mainly for topspin forehand shots
  • Wild Card- A player who is invited to play in a tournament even though he didn’t register in time
  • Winner- A shot that the opponent can’t return, that wins you the game or set
  • WTA- girls in short skirts chasing a two inch yellow ball with sticks

[edit] X

  • X Chromosome- what everyone that plays Tennis has at least one of
  • X-Factor- something you can’t determine by looking at someone; i.e. Personality, Determination
  • X-Tra Soft Hands- What you need to hit good drop shots
  • X-Tra Work- What your coach fits in during practice
  • X-Tremity- what you hold your racket with; i.e. fingers

[edit] Y

  • Yacht- what most pro tennis players have
  • Yard Tennis- another name for tennis on grass
  • Yellow- the color of some tennis rackets
  • Yippee- what you try not to yell out loud after a match, for risk of being deemed a “spaz”
  • You- what you call your opponent when you forget their name

[edit] Z

  • Zero- what you want your opponents score to be at the end of a game
  • Zip- what you hope to remember to do before a match (double check, for everyone’s sake)
  • Zone- sections of the court; i.e. Backcourt, Deuce Court, and Ad Court
  • Zonked- a state in which you can’t do anything right; i.e. you’re zonked
  • Zoot Suit- What you NEVER wear to a match
