Is use of "black" to refer to anyone with black ancestors common anywhere other than the US? I (from the UK) was shocked when I first heard Americans saying "I knew her for years before I found out she was black" on discovering that someone had a black grandparent. I also find it strange when someone says "he's black you know" when someone clearly looks white!

[edit] Questions

I have only ever heard "black" used this way in America, and I think it is only used by White Americans. Is this so? Whould a Black American say "I knew her for years before I found out she was white" if someone had a white ancestor?
