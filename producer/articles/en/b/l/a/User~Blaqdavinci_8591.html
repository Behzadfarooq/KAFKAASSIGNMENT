Justin M. Whye, (born on December 28, 1980), better known as Blaqdavinci, is an eclectic artist(illustrator/musician/poet), of Lumbee/Cherokee/Black native descent. His classmates in middleschool, gave him the name Blaqdavinci (from the Renaissance artist: Leonardo Da Vinci) after witnessing his aptitude for multiple arts and sciences.[1] He is also a descendant of Irish/Carribean heritage.

TALENTS

In early childhood, Blaqdavinci took a keen interest in music, art and science. He was classically trained in voice, and participated in N.A.S.A.'s computer technology training camps. His most impressive work came through the area of fine-arts, where he blossomed in penciling and illustration techniques. In fact, his early childhood goal was to work as an animator for Walt Disney Studios. This dream would be marred by rumors and reports of racism within the company.

He also was asked to audition for the Mickey Mouse Club, the same year another Justin(Justin Timberlake), was recruited into the roster of mouse-katiers. It was decided that the move to filming cities would abruptly change his childhood. So, to counteract the loss of opportunity, his parents enrolled him in many theater arts and fine art groups. He even played a role in a 1988 Nickelodeon commercial. One acting guild he belonged to, "Arena Players", is the oldest all black theater group in Maryland.

Blaqdavinci has made in several on-air appearances. WBAL's "ON TIME", HBO's "THE WIRE", Nickelodeon Kids Network,Etc.

Blaqdavinci, illustrated 2 online comics, now on hiatus: "Go Wasabi" and "(N.NY)Native New Yorkers" - due to copyrights both comics and their timestamps have been removed from the net for their protection. Go Wasabi os bieng considered for national distribution through an, "as-yet-to-be-named" publisher.

During the early part of 2004, Blaqdavinci lent his vocals to the Skrap Metal(Ska/Rap) Band, Graffik Nature; where he took on the role of lead singer for three months. This was a temporary arrangement, as the original singer, and group creator, Robert Gerke, re-assumed his position in lead. Blaqdavinci still works with GN, as the resident artist and promotions specialist.

Prior to his time with GN, Blaqdavinci worked as a writer for several hip-hop artist in the tri-state area. He may have been influenced by his sister's friendship with "Tupac Shakur", the great fallen rap legend and activist. Blaqdavinci has said of Pac's influence, "I saw Pac as a real person, I was so young and here he was so smart, wild, and fun to be around!" "That's the Pac, I knew!" "When he left Baltimore, it was like he totally changed. The man I saw on television was not the same young man, I had grown up watching. But I understood, he went through a lot!"

Blaqdavinci's current musical venture, titled: Theory of Flight is described as a collection of work spanning the last 10 years. It has been both time consumming and painful to construct as it's creation has been plagued with loss and emotional struggle. During the summer of 2004, Blaqdavinci lost his producer and good friend Ronald "Ron Bocardi" Ramsey, to a heart-attack. Then again in late 2004 he lost producer and cousin "Cutt" of the producing duo "Moe & Cutt"; "Raised in Baltimore, I have lost many people throughout my life and sadly, grown accustomed to it. But these two men were different, they truly believed in my talent. I will always hold them as a beakon in my struggle to bring light to the world! RIP." - Blaqdavinci

Blaqdavinci, is the leader of his self-titled: Eklectik-Hip-Rock-Soul Movement!

Flying Machine Arts, the company started by, Blaqdavinci, is meant not only as a home for his creations, but also a vehicle for under-appreciated artist's work. He describes it as "An Umbrella of Rare, Artistic, Individuals!"

Blaqdavinci is related to NFL player, Copeland Bryan. They are first cousins.

Blaqdavinci is currently illustrating a children's book he also wrote. The story centers around a poor, Japanese fisher-boy, during the feudal era.
