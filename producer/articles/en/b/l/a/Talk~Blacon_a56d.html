[edit] Still POV

"Blacon people are commited to building a kind and caring community" is not NPOV and is not appropriate for a Wikipedia article. It is not enough for you (or even the entire population of Blacon) to think it true; it is still personal opinion. The obvious answer is to find a reliable source, say a local newspaper, in which a well-known personality has been quoted as saying this and use that instead. For the moment I've changed that part of the sentence to avoid stating an opinion as fact; please do not reinsert it in the original form. Sorry, but NPOV is a matter of non-negotiable Wikipedia policy, and it overrides your (or my) opinions, however strong they may be. Loganberry (Talk) 00:42, 1 December 2006 (UTC)

[edit] Cleanup tag

I added the cleanup tag to this article, because:

  • It's not all written from a neutral point of view. For example, Blacon people are commited to building a kind and caring community may or may not be true, but is certainly not neutral. Both are needed for Wikipedia articles; as Jimmy Wales has said, NPOV is non-negotiable and must always be conformed to regardless of individual editors' feelings or beliefs. I removed the intro sentence because it was redundant: all WP articles have to conform to NPOV, so there's no point in saying so explicitly in one article.
  • The tone is not always the formal one expected from an encyclopedia article. For example, if you take a 10 or 15 minute walk from any part of the estate, you are again in open countryside is in an inappropriate second-person voice, and It is a Village/Town... a Mini-Town...(?) is unclear; it should be described as whatever its official status is - if any reliable source has described it as a "Mini-Town" then that information can be included as long as it is verifiable.
  • The navigational templates at the bottom don't seem to fit here. For example, the "Historic counties of England" template is for articles about such, not for articles about places within them.
  • On a slightly different note, please don't anyone incorporate the comments above from the anon editor into the article. Personal opinions, positive or negative, are again a violation of the NPOV policy and as such would be unacceptable. It is not Wikipedia's job to change (or indeed confirm) opinions of a place; it is Wikipedia's job to report the facts.

Hopefully these comments will help editors who have more knowledge of Blacon than I do. Loganberry (Talk) 23:32, 29 November 2006 (UTC)
