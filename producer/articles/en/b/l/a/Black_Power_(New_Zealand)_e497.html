Black Power is a New Zealand gang that is part of the international United Black Power Movement. The gang was formed and organised in Whakatane in response to the rival Mongrel Mob gang. Black Power members wear a patch or leather jacket which bears the Black Power emblem — a clenched upright fist — and is recognised as a symbol of Power. Only during times of preparation to a Gang climatic event do members wear colours; the wearing of colours is mostly done by adolescent males, and the colour of which they wear is black and blue (all shades) bandanas.

Like other gangs in New Zealand, they are involved in a number of crimes, such as drug dealing.[1] While the gang has distanced itself from violent acts of some of its members in, for example, a child abuse case, police have in return accused the gang members of using violence as a 'learned behaviour from involvement in the gang'.[2]

[edit] History

Black Power rally

Black Power was formed in the late 1960s, becoming a prominent gang and a formidable force against the more numerous Mongrel Mob. They began due to ongoing violence within the Māori community, particularly those who were involved in random attacks by the Mongrel Mob, who had an All for Himself and Enemies are Outsiders mentality. The latter in particular means that all people who are Not Mongrel Mob are considered enemies without thought or reason. The retaliative offensive and defensive stance of the Black Power against their adversaries the Mongrel Mob has created a long lasting gang epidemic in Māori society.[citation needed]

[edit] Māori legends

There are legends as to why the Mob and the Blacks have meant to have been rivalling forces within the Māori community and different accounts that either justify or give reason to these proposed theories. The most popular account is that of a quarrel started between two Māori boys, which was witnessed by a kaumatua of a marae. The two young Māori boys who were fighting were quite popular amongst their friends. They had a simple disagreement that resulted in a violent conflict. The older of the two was well known for having a larger group of friends and for being fierce and ferocious, but was also known for his lack of self-control and dislike of discipline and work. The younger boy had a smaller group of friends but was self-controlled, disciplined and was humbly obedient, though he was slightly less stronger than the older boy. However, when the younger boy gained the upper hand in the fight the older boy resorted to using all manner of aggression and attacks to show that he was not the weaker one. But before he could do so the kaumatua swiftly put an end to the quarrel by splitting them up. With a patu clenched in his fist, the kaumatua proclaimed that one was the 'Toa' or 'warrior' and the other was to be cast out as 'kurikaka' or 'dog', representing Black Power and their adversaries, the Mongrel Mob, respectively.[citation needed]
