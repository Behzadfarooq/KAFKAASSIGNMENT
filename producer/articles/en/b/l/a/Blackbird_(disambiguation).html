Blackbird can refer to:

[edit] Birds

  • Blackbird (Turdus merula), a species of bird of the thrush family indigenous to Europe and Asia
  • New World blackbirds, about 20 species of icterid birds that resemble the Blackbird including:
    • Red-winged Blackbird (Agelaius phoeniceus)
    • Red-breasted Blackbird (Sturnella militaris)
    • Yellow-shouldered Blackbird (Agelaius xanthomus)

[edit] In the Arts

  • "Blackbird" (song), a song by the Beatles that includes the sounds of a Blackbird singing
  • Blackbird (online journal), an online journal of literature and the arts
  • Blackbird (play), a play by David Harrower, which played at the Edinburgh Festival in 2005 and London's West End in 2006
  • Blackbird, a play by Adam Rapp, which premiered in London in 2001
  • "If I Were A Blackbird", a song by Ronnie Ronalde that includes whistling of a blackbird's song
  • "Thirteen Ways of Looking at a Blackbird", a poem by Wallace Stevens, published in his Harmonium collection in 1923.

[edit] In fiction

  • Blackbird (comics), aircraft piloted by the X-Men, a fictional superhero team from comics, films and TV
  • Colonial Blackbird, a stealth fighter created by Chief Tyrol in the 2004 Battlestar Galactica science fiction series
  • Blackbird (Femizon), also known as Jackdaw, a Hulk villainess of Marvel Comics who could fly, a member of Superia's Femizons.

[edit] Other

  • SR-71 Blackbird, a supersonic reconnaissance aircraft built by Lockheed-Martin
  • A-12 Blackbird, the unofficial nickname for the forerunner to the SR-71 Blackbird, codenamed A-12 OXCART
  • Chief Blackbird (d.1800), chief of the Omaha
  • Super Blackbird, a nickname for the Honda CBR1100XX sport-touring motorcycle

[edit] See also

  • Musta Lindu, the second album by Värttinä re-released as Black Bird in 1998
  • Blackbirding, a recruitment practice utilizing trickery or kidnapping
