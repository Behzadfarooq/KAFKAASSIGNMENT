Initial D Special Stage is a racing video game based on Sega's Initial D Arcade Stage, which is based on the manga/anime Initial D. It combines elements from all three versions of the arcade game and includes a story mode that follows the story in the manga featuring scenes from the manga voiced by the original actors from the anime series.

[edit] Story

The game features races and story elements from First, Second, Third, and Fourth stages. The story is presented nicely here[[1]].

[edit] Gameplay

There are three modes of gameplay: Arcade, Story, and Time Attack.
Arcade mode is the equivalent of "Legend of the Streets" from Arcade Stage, where the player is a new racer who challenges other racers on their home courses. You choose a car and the kind of parts you want to gain. You attain new parts from collecting points from beating other racers. The goal is to defeat them all.
Story mode has three parts; Akina's AE86, Red Suns, and Project D. They are composed of stages that include scenes from the manga, followed by the race.
Time Attack allows you to go to any course from story or arcade mode to race for the fastest lap time, or to scout the course to help you in the game.

[edit] Physics

The physics in this game were designed to make drifting the main focus. The only controls given to you are: analog stick - turning, X - accelerate, Square - Brake, R1 - Shift up, L1 - Shift down, R2 - Change View (first/third person), L2 - Headlights on/off.
What sets this apart from other street racing games, is the absence of a handbrake.

[edit] Courses

Myogi - An easy circuit based loosely on a location from the story. It is found only in Arcade and Time Attack modes. Your opponents are Itsuki in his Levin, Takumi in the Levin, Shingo in his Civic, and Nakazoto in his GT-R.
Usui - A mildly twisty circuit based on a location from the story. It is found only in Arcade and Time Attack modes. Your opponents are Two Guys from Tokyo in their Silvia, seen in fourth stage,Akiyama, Mako and Sayuki in her Sil-Eighty, and Tsukamoto in his Cappuccino.
Akagi - This is where the circuits end and become more accurate to the story.
Akina - A more challenging coursed based on it's real life counterpart, Mount Haruna.
Happogahara - A course found in the Project D story.
Irohazaka - A twisty course full of hairpins that travels down the mountain.
Shomaru Pass - A course found in the Project D story.
Enna Pass - A course found in the Project D story.

[edit] Release Information

Image:InitialDSpecialStageTheBestGameCover.jpg
PS2 The Best version

ID:SS was released in Japan on June 26, 2003, alongside a special version that included a Logicool racing wheel designed for the game. Hori also released two special edition memory cards with Initial D designs on them.

The game was re-released on February 26, 2004 as a bargain edition.

There are no plans for a US release of this game.
