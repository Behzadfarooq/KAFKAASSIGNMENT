Blair Stewart-Wilson

Lieutenant-Colonel Sir Blair Stewart-Wilson, KCVO (born 17 July 1929) was Equerry to Her Majesty The Queen and Deputy Master of the Household in the Royal Household 1976-1994.

Stewart-Wilson was educated at Eton College. He joined the Scots Guards 14 July 1949 and retired 17 July 1984. In his later years he was a supernumerary list officer.

He served in the United Kingdom, the British Army of the Rhine (BAOR), and the Far East. From 1955 to 1957 he was adjutant of the 2nd Battalion of the Scots Guards, and Aide-de-Camp to the Governor-General of New Zealand 1957-1959, Viscount Cobham. In 1960-1962 he was Equerry to His Royal Highness the Duke of Edinburgh. He was regimental adjutant 1966 to 1968. He was staff qualified (sq) but did not attend the Staff College, Camberley.

Stewart-Wilson was promoted to Lieutenant-Colonel and was a General Staff Officer Grade 1 (GSO1) in the foreign liaison section (Army) 1970 to 1973, and Defence Military and Air Attache in Vienna 1975-1976. In 1976 he joined the Royal Household. From 1994 he was an Extra Equerry to Her Majesty The Queen.

Stewart-Wilson was an officer of the Atholl Highlanders from 1966, as a Captain.

He was made a LVO in 1983, a CVO in 1989 and a KCVO in 1994. He received the General Service Medal, Malaya bar, the Campaign Service Medal, Borneo and Malaya bars, and the Coronation Medal 1953.
