BLAG Linux and GNU is a Linux distribution made by the Brixton Linux Action Group.

BLAG is a single-CD distro with applications desktop users "expect" from a desktop including multimedia, graphics, desktop internet applications and more. BLAG also includes a collection of server packages. BLAG is based on Fedora Core plus updates, adds apps from Dag, Dries, Freshrpms, NewRPMS, and includes custom packages.

The first public release of BLAG was 22 October 2002. The latest stable release, BLAG60000, is based on Fedora Core 6, and was released 14 January 2007.

Richard Stallman mentioned the name of BLAG Linux and GNU and Ututo when asked which distro he recommends during his speech at Universiti Sains Malaysia, in 2005 [1].
