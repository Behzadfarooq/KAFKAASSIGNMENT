[edit] Wikipedia:Criteria for speedy deletion/Proposal/3

PBurka pointed out that an important omission from this proposal: a band could meet WP:MUSIC criterion #5 (sharing a member with a famous band) and still be speedily deletable by this criterion. I've added a sentence to the proposal to reflect this: it now reads An article about a musician or music group that does not assert having released at least one album, nor having had media coverage, nor having a member that is or was also part of a well-known music group. If the assertion is disputed or controversial, it should be taken to VFD instead. I believe you voted for different reasons, but you may want to vote on the alternative proposal as well. I'm sorry for the confusion, and let me assure you that I won't be asking for any other revotes on this (it's just ironic that you can discuss something for nearly two months, only to have two omissions pointed out within hours of asking for a vote...) Yours, Radiant_>|< July 5, 2005 09:59 (UTC)

[edit] Wiki for schools

I would also suggest that you and some of the others who vote to keep all schools should start a new wiki for schools and start transwikiing the school articles there so that you don't have to keep seeing people here who want to delete them. --Idont Havaname 04:04, 20 July 2005 (UTC)

  • Why would we start a separate wiki? Articles on schools are patently encyclopaedic and well suited for this project. Don't fix things if they aren't broke, eh! BLANKFAZE | (что??) 04:17, 20 July 2005 (UTC)
    • I was suggesting that because not everyone here believes that schools are inherently encyclopedic, and that if we did that, then nobody would need to waste time or energy in the debate over whether or not they were. Transwikiing has pretty much solved the problem for dicdefs and source material, for example, and I think that it would take quite a bit of stress off of VfD. It's obvious that a lot of the editors in those VfDs, inclusionists and otherwise, are getting sick of debating about it. I don't mind going to the newest school VfD and saying "Delete, it's just like any other school" or "Merge into the article about its city", but when we're getting silly votes like "Banana" and "Cow", then we're not getting intelligent discussion; we're getting people who, regardless of their stance, don't want to talk about it anymore. --Idont Havaname 04:27, 20 July 2005 (UTC)
      • Well if you guys would quit being silly, it would also fix things :-) ! BLANKFAZE | (что??) 04:30, 20 July 2005 (UTC)
        • Unfortunately, it's more complicated than that. Although previous to Schoolwatch getting started, schools were deleted almost without question, and now they're pretty much always kept, there are still deletionists among newbies and more experienced users. Schools are still a gray area around here, and WP:SCH is just a proposed policy, so the correct solution to the VfD controversies over them (which are becoming some of the worst problems in VfD, aside from general overload and... ha, repeated GNAA nominations!) is not to use one's preferred method of abuse upon the lister. I was hoping that the separate wiki suggestion would ease the tension there. Also, it would provide a better framework for filling in the pieces and would - I think - foster growth better by allowing the editors to concentrate on one topic without taking any grief over their work, if you want to create a more complete directory for schools (much like Wikitravel is in the process of doing with various destinations). Anyways, just a suggestion. --Idont Havaname 04:48, 20 July 2005 (UTC)
          • Having a separate wiki removes the advantages of having a wiki. The wiki's value increases with the number of links. Making interwiki links is much more trouble than internal ones. A less-notable school will probably not have many inbound links anyway, so only those interested specificially in that school will find it. — David Remahl 09:09, 20 July 2005 (UTC)
            • Well said. BLANKFAZE | (что??) 09:10, 20 July 2005 (UTC)
