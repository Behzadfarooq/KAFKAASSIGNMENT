Blaxland County, New South Wales is one of the 141 Cadastral divisions of New South Wales. It is bounded to the south by the Lachlan River and a small part of Waverley Creek. It includes Mount Hope and the Nombinnie Nature Reserve.

Blaxland County was named in honour of John Blaxland Landowner and Merchant between (1769-1845).[1]
