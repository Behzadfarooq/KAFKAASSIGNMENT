Please see Wikipedia's no personal attacks policy. Comment on content, not on the contributor; personal attacks damage the community and deter users. Note that continued personal attacks may lead to blocks for disruption. Please stay cool and keep this in mind while editing. Thank you. Gwernol 00:46, 19 October 2006 (UTC)

Let me quote what you actually wrote: "uninformed decision-making by GROSSLY incompetent people who lack professionalism". In which world is this not a personal attack? Certainly I consider it one, and I fail to see how you could possibly read Wikipedia's personal attack policy and honestly say this is not covered. This is deeply personal, since you are discussing the particular admins who deleted the article you are so anxious to retain. Simply avoiding naming them does not make the attack any less personal. You seem to be an intelligent guy, so I expect this point is not lost on you. Your claim that the Wikipedia guidelines were not followed in the deletion of Angry Nintendo Nerds is simply false. Have you actually read the WP:AFD discussion?
Your point about there being other bad articles is well made, but far from a good reason to allow the ANN article. Are you seriously arguing that we should allow all bad articles if there is even one? That's not going to improve the quality of Wikipedia at all. There are over a 1.5 million articles and around 1000 admins. It takes a while to get through all the crap. How about instead of complaining about the poor articles, you help out and propose some for deletion (as I just did for The Dildo Song).
Finally, just as a matter of practicality, do you sincerely believe that insulting, repeatedly, the only people who can get your article restored is the likely to be the most productive way to persuade them of the merits of your argument? Maybe you do, but I'm telling you, I'm now feeling in a particularly incompetent mood and cannot figure out how I might have helped you. Sorry us admins are all so dumb. Gwernol 01:18, 19 October 2006 (UTC)
The link to the AfD is right there on the Talk:Angry_Nintendo_Nerd page that you posted to. I know this because I posted it there. Take another look. The discussion is here. I'm amazed that you felt it appropriate to throw around accusations and personal attacks without researching the facts first. You seem to be under the impression that admins are somehow in a different position from all other editors. Fundamentally we're not. We aren't paid for our activities any more than you are (your expectations of "professionalism" are odd). Nor do we have a special mission. We just have a few extra tools. While its true that we are the only ones who can carry out the technical task of deleting an article, we rarely make the decision to do so: that's the community's privilege either via AfD discussions or by setting the policies we enforce. Which is exactly what happened here. Gwernol 02:10, 19 October 2006 (UTC)

And you misunderstand mine. Its not the responsibility of just the admins to identify articles for deletion; every editor on Wikipedia can and should help with this task. More importantly, the fact that a low quality article hasn't been deleted yet does not necessarily mean that it is endorsed by admins or anyone else. You say these articles are "are allowed to be posted and remain up" implying every article is manually inspected before being posted. This simply isn't true. When you see these articles, it usually just means no-one has noticed it yet. Again, there are literally thousands of new articles being created each day; some slip through the net. Instead of complaining about all these low quality articles out there, do something about it. I have, what have you done to help clean up Wikipedia? Gwernol 02:26, 19 October 2006 (UTC)

Regarding "professionalism": I am an editor, just the same as you. Being an admin indeed has an expectation of professionalism, but only in exactly the same way as it is expected of all editors.
