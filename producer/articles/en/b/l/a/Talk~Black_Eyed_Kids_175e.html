[edit] Category dispute

This article relates a paranormal phenomenon not an urban legend or legendary creature. This phenomenon is not an urban legend because the eye-witness accounts by other people are first-hand and not: "this happened to the cousin of my friend's girlfriend etc..." and are not based on hearsay or traditional folklore, nor are they legendary creatures because this is a relatively modern phenomenon which cannot be attributed to any older source as for instance the Mothman Prophecies or Jersey Devil can. Piecraft 16:52, 13 September 2006 (UTC)

Urban legend is essentially modern folklore. Simply calling the story folklore would be misleading in the same way that the category 'legendary creatures' might be, because, as you said, it implies that they've been around longer than they have.
The 'friend of a friend' type urban legends (dogs in the microwave, killer pop-rocks, etc) are very common and often the first thing one thinks of when the term is mentioned, but stories like the Roswell crash, the men in black, area 51 and most conspiracy theories are also prime examples of urban legend as modern folklore. It's not about the source (or lack thereof) of the story - there are plenty of people who claim to have met MIBs or to have been abducted by aliens. Those are still urban legends in part because of the way that people who did not experience them first hand continue to pass them around. This isn't about the reliability of those who've reported seeing them, it's not about passing a judgement on the story, it's not an attempt to make it seem less important or interesting, but the Black Eyed Kids are definitely a part of urban legend.
See the section urban legend versus urban myth - I think Jan Harold Brunvand, the folklorist who coined the term, would agree that it would be correct to apply it here. -- Vary | Talk 19:15, 13 September 2006 (UTC)
Look I'm not trying to cause any disruption, you do what you feel is correct, but as far as I'm concerned the definition of legend (although it adheres to the probability of an event or occurence having existed) it is still reliant on several factors - such as being a story that is pased on through society and is usually observd or recorded in larger numbers than is the case with the BEK. Anyway this is still clearly not folklore nor a modern legend. It is a phenomena which has as of yet minimal coverage. Piecraft 15:22, 27 September 2006 (UTC)
