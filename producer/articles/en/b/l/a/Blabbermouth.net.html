Blabbermouth.net

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

"Blabbermouth" redirects here. For other uses see: Blabbermouth (disambiguation)

Blabbermouth.net is a website dedicated to heavy metal news, hard rock news, and album reviews. Blabbermouth.net was founded and run by Borivoj Krgin. While the idea of Blabbermouth.net was conceived in early 2000, the website was actually launched in March 2001, and Roadrunner Records began hosting it in December 2001. Metal and rock fans can post comments on a selected news article or album review to contribute their thoughts and opinions, either in response to other user comments or in response to the article itself.

[edit] Criticism

[edit] Controversial news articles

Blabbermouth.net webmaster Borivoj Krgin posts news articles every day, some of which draws much attention and debate from Blabbermouth's readers. In a Metal Sludge interview, Borivoj Krgin admitted to knowingly posting stories that would (and often do) receive negative commentary from Blabbermouth users. Usually, these articles concern musicians and personas that are generally shunned by Blabbermouth.net's users and (to a lesser or greater degree) by the rock/metal community. These are a big draw for many of Blabbermouth's visitors, as many Blabbermouth users regard these articles and the comments that follow them as amusing. As of September 28, 2006, blabbermouth has established rules that will remove much of the comments that said posters have found to be amusing.

"To report any abusive, obscene, defamatory, racist, homophobic or threatening comments, or anything that may violate any applicable laws, please send an e-mail to bmouth@bellatlantic.net with pertinent details. Anyone posting such material will be immediately and permanently banned. IP addresses are recorded to aid us in enforcing these conditions."

"Bori" has also said he was going to clean out the archives of Blabbermouth. Lots of posts have been deleted. It should be stated that if you are going to read Blabbermouth comments that were posted before the rules were established, please know that there are a lot of posts missing and it isn't how it used to be.

The controversial articles include (but are not limited to) the following bands, musicians, and personas:

Axl Rose, Peter Dolving, Guns N' Roses, Richard Patrick, Lars Ulrich, Metallica, Evanescence, Sharon Osbourne, Ozzy Osbourne, Deicide, Glen Benton, Gene Simmons, KISS, Varg Vikernes, Tommy Lee, Jon Nödtveidt, HIM, Ville Valo, Disturbed, David Draiman, Korn, Brian "Head" Welch, James "Munky" Shaffer, Megadeth, Dave Mustaine, Sully Erna, DJ Starscream, Corey Taylor, M. Shawn Crahan (a.k.a. "Clown"), Slipknot, Marilyn Manson, Scott Stapp, Ted Nugent, Kid Rock etc.

Most recently was the tendency for Disturbed/David Draiman and KISS/Gene Simmons-related articles to be posted. These articles receive numerous scathing comments from Blabbermouth's users, who almost all have a strong dislike for Disturbed and KISS. This is very similar to the frequent articles on Fred Durst that would appear in 2003, which the users would often reply to in disgust.

News articles with musicians and personas giving their opinions on politics, current events, and Blabbermouth.net's users are frequently posted as well.

A minority of the news articles regarded as controversial are of serious or saddening nature, where less rant and debate occurs. An example would be the articles concerning Dimebag Darrell's murder.

[edit] Negative user commentary

Hundreds and even thousands of user comments are posted on the website within any given 24-hour time. Comments posted by users that are deemed negative, irrelevant, and/or immature by other Blabbermouth.net users are very prevalent, even to the point that these comments receive critical response from lurking, unregistered readers.

The popularity of Blabbermouth.net is thought to be sending a bad image about metal and rock fans, but it is a select online community, so others advise not to take it seriously. Some of Blabbermouth.net's users have taken it so far as to criticize specific Blabbermouth.net users en masse. An example of this behavior is a poll conducted at the BigNewsDay Blog in February 2006, where users could vote for the "most hated Blabbermouth.net user". T-boner.com, a frequent Blabbermouth.net poster, won the BigNewsDay Blog poll.

[edit] Roadrunner records bias

For the reason that Blabbermouth.net is hosted by Roadrunner Records, users often accuse the website of having favoritism or bias towards album reviews and news that involve bands currently on the Roadrunners Records roster.

[edit] Full-screen advertisements

In the first half of 2005, the website implemented more advertisements, many of which were full-screen and coded with Flash. These advertisements, especially the full-screen animations, are regarded as annoying by some Blabbermouth users. However, the website has greatly reduced these specific advertisements, probably in response to the user comments.

[edit] Artist response

Many musicians read Blabbermouth.net, and some have been known to post on the website occasionally. Some of them have also talked about the website and certain articles in interviews, or on their message boards. However, most of the time it is a negative reply to comments posted by the website's users. An example of this is an interview with Kevin Dubrow of the band Quiet Riot, who wondered why Blabbermouth users were leaving comments about the Swedish metal band Meshuggah, instead of acknowledging Quiet Riot. The alleged reason for Kevin DuBrow's comment was that molotov, a frequent Blabbermouth.net user, always includes the text "MESHUGGAH!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!" as a signature in his posts. This user most likely left a negative comment about Quiet Riot prior to the Kevin DuBrow interview, which would have likely sparked criticism from Kevin Dubrow.
