Blackhawk is a musical ensemble including Henry Paul (formerly of The Outlaws), singer/songwriter Dave Robbins, and Anthony Crawford (bandmember for both Neil Young and Steve Winwood). One of the more successful country music groups of the 1990s, their hits have included "Every Once in a While," "I'm Not Strong Enough to Say No," and "Goodbye Says It All."

The original lineup for the band throughout the 1990s was Paul, Robbins, and guitarist/vocalist Van Stephenson, who died of skin cancer in April, 2001. He was replaced by Randy Threet, the band's bass player, until 2003, when Anthony Crawford signed on. After losing a record deal with Arista Records (with whom they were signed from day one), the group switched to Rust Records.
