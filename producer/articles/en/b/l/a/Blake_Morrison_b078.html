Blake Morrison (born October 8, 1950) is a versatile British author who has published in a wide range of fiction and non-fiction genres. His greatest success came with the publication of his memoirs And When Did You Last See Your Father?. He has also written a study of the James Bulger murder, As If.

Morrison was born in Skipton, North Yorkshire. He studied at the University of Nottingham and UCL before working for the Times Literary Supplement. He is a Fellow of the Royal Society of Literature.

'And When Did You Last See Your Father' has recently been made into a film starring Colin Firth and Jim Broadbent. Filming took place in Cromford (Derbyshire) and the surrounding area.

He lives in Blackheath, London with his wife and three children.

[edit] Awards

  • 1980 Eric Gregory Award
  • 1985 Dylan Thomas Award
  • 1985 Somerset Maugham Award for Dark Glasses
  • 1988 E.M. Forster Award
  • 1993 Esquire/Volvo/Waterstone's Non-Fiction Book Award for And When Did You Last See Your Father?
  • 1994 J.R. Ackerley Prize for Autobiography for And When Did You Last See Your Father?
