Blackman High School

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Blackman High School is a high school located in Murfreesboro, Tennessee, United States. The school was founded in 2000.

Our Mission

It is the mission of Blackman High School to challenge, cultivate, and engage individual students in the learning process while addressing their diverse needs, thus preparing them for post-secondary opportunities.


Principal: Mrs. Gail Vick

Assistant Principals: Clark Harrell, Scott Lawless, and John Strickland

Grade Structure: 9-12

Enrollment (2005-06): 1,686

School hours: 8:30 A.M. to 3:30 P.M.

Mascot: Dante

Colors: Blue, Orange, and White

Technology All classes at Blackman High have a minimum of one computer for every six students. Computers are available in the media center, training lab, and technology lab. Each teacher at the school has a multimedia center that includes a TV, computer with TV out, computer network connection, and a printer.

Pupil-Teacher Ratios 30-1 (9-12) 23-1 (average for individual vocational classes)

Blaze Savings and Loan Student directed and run bank in conjunction with Rutherford Bank and Trust. The bank is part of the finance emphasis in the Business Academy. Students serve as members of the bank board, tellers, and loan officers. Students and staff at the school may open savings accounts at the bank and get lunch loans.
