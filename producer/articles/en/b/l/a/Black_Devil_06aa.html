Black Devil is an electronic duo credited to Joachim Sherylee and Junior Claristidge, pseudonyms of two french library writers Bernard Fevre and Jackie Giordano. Their album, "Disco Club" was extremely important to the rise of the acid house movement. The record was unearthed by Rephlex and many doubted its authenticity, due to its futuristic sound, despite it's 1978 release date.[1]

[edit] Disco Club

Originally released in 1978, Black Devil's "Disco Club" is an extremely rare disco masterpiece, an epic journey into the deepest electronic disco. The record is credited to Joachim Sherylee and Junior Claristidge. It was made manually in a recording studio in the suburbs of Paris using synths and occasional tape loops and a drummer. No midi or computers.

In 2004, Rephlex released a remix album with Luke Vibert taking on taking on the lead remix under his disco acid alias Kerrier District.
