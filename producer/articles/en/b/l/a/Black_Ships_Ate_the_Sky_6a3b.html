Black Ships Ate the Sky is a 2006 album by Current 93. The album features numerous guest vocalists, such as Antony Hegarty, Bonnie 'Prince' Billy, Marc Almond, and Shirley Collins. It features eight versions of the 1763 Methodist hymn "Idumæa", written by Charles Wesley, each featuring vocals by a different artist. The album was issued in digipak packaging, with a 56 page booklet containing liner notes, lyrics, photographs, and credits.

In order to assist with funding for the album, customers were able to pre-order a copy. These 'subscribers' received a mention in the liner notes, as well as a limited edition extra CD, entitled I Am Black Ship, consisting of alternate versions of the tracks from Black Ships Ate the Sky.

The track "Sunset (The Death of Thumbelina)" was featured on the National Public Radio program All Songs Considered on August 10, 2006.

[edit] Black Ships Eat the Sky

In December 2006, Current 93 released an "alternative version" of the album titled Black Ships Eat the Sky. This limited edition record features a different mix of the album, which Tibet described as "far more intimate and more emphatically acoustic than the original version." Black Ships Eat the Sky has the same tracklist as …Ate the Sky, with the exception that track 20 is titled "Why Cæsar is Burning Part I".
