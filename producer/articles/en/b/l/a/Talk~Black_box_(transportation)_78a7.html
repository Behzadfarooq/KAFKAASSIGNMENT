[edit] Why is this page necessary?

The above discussion notwithstanding, this page is largely pointless, and I feel it ought to be deleted. Here are my reasons:

  • There is already a 'black box' disambiguation page
  • All the devices mentioned in the opening sentence already have their own articles (aircraft flight data recorder, aircraft cockpit voice recorder (not mentioned, though it ought to be), train event recorder, automobile event data recorder)
  • Despite the variety of devices mentioned in the first sentence, the ENTIRE subsequent article discusses ONLY flight data recorders
  • Aside from the introductory sentence, all of the information presented here is already (or should already be) in the 'flight data recorder' article
  • Although working links to the car and train black boxes are given, the 'flight recorder' link just redirects back to this article instead of to the 'flight data recorder' article

If the article is going to be kept, which I strongly oppose, it should be changed into little more than a disambiguation page, as there is no point in duplicating information that can already be found in other articles. Surely there is not THAT much worthwhile information to be presented about 'transportation black boxes' as a group, and people who visit the page are likely to want only details about a particular type of recorder (probably auto or aircraft). Thoughts? -Grammaticus Repairo 21:00, 28 March 2007 (UTC)
