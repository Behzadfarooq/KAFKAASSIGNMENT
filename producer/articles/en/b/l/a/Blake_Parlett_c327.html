Blake Parlett (born May 13, 1989) is a defence hockey player.
His hometown is Bracebridge, Ontario.
He shoots right, and plays right defence.

He will be eligible for the 2007 NHL Entry Draft.

[edit] Playing Career

  • Played with the Huntsville-Muskoka Otters Jr. A team in the 2004 - 2005 season.[1]
  • First round draft pick (15th overall) selected by the Barrie Colts in the Ontario Hockey League draft on May 7, 2005.[2]
  • Played his first OHL game on September 29, 2005, Barrie Colts vs. Sudbury Wolves.
  • Recorded his first point in the OHL on September 29, 2005, in his first game, by scoring an assist.
  • Named to Team Ontario and participated in the World U-17 Hockey Challenge in Regina, Saskatchewan from December 26, 2005 to January 4, 2006.
  • Helped the Barrie Colts make it into the third round of the OHL Championship playoffs in 2006.
  • On January 3, 2007 Blake Parlett was traded to the Windsor Spitfires in a deal that sent the Spits team captain Mike Weber, also a defence player, to the Colts. Speaking of Parlett, Spitfires General Manager Warren Rychel said, "He will be a cornerstone for our defence for many years to come.
