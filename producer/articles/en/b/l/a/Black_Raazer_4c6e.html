Black Raazer

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Black Raazer is a fictional character in Marvel Comics. He first appeared in Alpha Flight # 31, written by Bill Mantlo.

He was a sorcerer whose spirit became trapped in the Black Blade of Bagdad centuries ago. The adventurer Eugene Judd was hired to steal the blade in the 1930s, and his actions freed the evil Raazer from his prison, but Judd used his knowledge of the occult to absorb the evil spirit into his own body, which reduced Judd to half his former height. Although Judd later managed to free himself from Raazer, the sorcerer was briefly contained again, only to be freed by Loki. He was seen as a member of Desert Sword during the Persian Gulf War.

[edit] Powers and Abilities

Since becoming a wraith, Raazer is generally intangible, he floats and exhibit ghost-like abilities. Raazer is also a sorcerer of limited ability. He has shown able to do some mystic scrying and levitation. Most importantly, Raazer can control the powerful Black Blade of Baghdad which he is capable of using without succumbing to its corrupting influence.

[edit] Weapons

The powerful Black Blade of Baghdad is an indestructable mystic sword that can cut through any substance or distrupt energy fields it comes into contact with. The blade drains the mystic energy from its vicinity and can also be used to drain lifeforce and, with a touch, it can also shrink the bodies of its victims in half.

[edit] Notes

The character's name has been spelled as Raazer, Raazar and Razaar. He has also been referred to as Arabic and Persian in the comics.
