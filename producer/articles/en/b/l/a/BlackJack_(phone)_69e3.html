The Samsung BlackJack (model Samsung SGH-i607) is a smartphone available through Cingular that runs Windows Mobile 5 for Smartphone.

[edit] Hardware Design

As listed on Cingular's Blackjack web page:

  • Weight: 3.5 ounces
  • Dimensions: 4.4 × 2.3 × .5 inches
  • Talk Time: Up to 5.5 hours
  • Standby Time: Up to 11 days
  • Dual-band domestic UMTS/HSDPA - 850/1900 MHz
  • Quad-band GSM/GPRS/EDGE - 850/900/1800/1900 MHz
  • 220 MHz processor
  • 128 MB ROM / 64 MB RAM
  • Large 2.2" 320×240 65k color screen with backlight
  • MicroSD(TM) expansion slot supports up to a 2 GB card
  • Bluetooth® v2.0 for up to six Bluetooth connections simultaneously
  • Warranty: 1 year on the phone and accessories
  • All talk, data and standby times depend on network configuration

[edit] Known Issues

A common complaint of Blackjack owners is the phone's relatively short battery life. To remedy this problem, Samsung now includes two batteries with the phone. When first released, the two batteries included were identical standard Samsung batteries, but when consumers and critics began to complain about the short battery life of the device, Samsung included one extended battery and one standard battery. This extended battery requires a different battery cover which is included with the phone, and makes the phone somewhat thicker, but increases battery life.
