[edit] Cascade Range ends at Fraser River

I know some American usages include the whole fam ding all the way to the Alsek, but that's not the Canadian usage; there is an archaic and quasi-legal usage in BC/Canada, such that on maps of the Lillooet Ranges you'll see "summit of the Cascades as defined for legal purposes" but that dates to colonial times when geographers hadn't gotten in the game or orotaxonomy (classification of mountains). Meager is in the Coast Mountains. NB "Coast Range" in Wiki redirects to Coast Mountains, "Pacific Coast Range" is for the Oregon coastal range, and what we call the Pacific Cordillera the Americans call the American Cordillera. Ontarians like to call it the Canadian Cordillera, apparently, and I've never been clear on whether the Pacific Cordillera is supposed to include the Selwnyns and the Brooks Range. Don't worry, it gets even more confusing....but Meager is in the Coast Mountains, not the Cascades (already took out the category; suggest we ask for a Category:Coast Mountains.Skookum1 18:35, 9 January 2007 (UTC)

Meager's not in the Garibaldi Ranges, either, only in the Pacific Ranges. There is no formal subgrouping of the Pacific Ranges it belongs to; Bivouac uses "Meager Group" and in older material I've seen the region around the Lillooet Icecap referred to as the Lillooet Crown, but for now that' uncitable. Try using Bivouac as an x-ref for what range something belongs in; I built their system, although there's necessarily a few invented name here and there - Meager Group, Railway Group, and more as you get up around Waddington/Queen Bess/Monarch and beyond.Skookum1 18:35, 9 January 2007 (UTC)

[edit] Bridge River Cones location/range

I just checked the lat-long you gave for the Bridge River Cones; it's in the Gunn Valley to the west of Lower Taseko Lake, near Yohetta Valley - north of the Tchaikazan. That location, if it's correct, is in the Chilcotin Ranges (which may or may not be a subset of the Pacific Ranges depending on who you're talking to). Can you located them more precisely; the boundary between the Pacific Ranges and Chilcotin Ranges isn't exact in Holland, and I'll have to refer to them somewhere; the crest of the Coast Mountains in that area is definitely Pacific Ranges; where the "fall-off" is I'll have to read Holland again to find out; if the Cones were in the upper Bridge River itself they might be considered Pacific Ranges; although the north bank of that river is part of the Chilcotin Ranges (because the Dickson Range is); I'd say the boundary - maybe - is the pass between the Lord and Bridge Rivers on the ENE side of the Lillooet Icecap - 50°56'11"-123°31'57" , although NW from there sort of NW across the Lord, Falls and Tchaikazan Rivers; Mount Monmouth is definitely in the Pacific Ranges, to the west of that line...; anything north of the Yohetta Valley, or the lower Tchaikazan, as your current Bridge River Cones location is, is in the Chilcotin Ranges for sure.Skookum1 14:49, 16 January 2007 (UTC)
