The name Blücher can refer to, depending on context:

  • Gebhard Leberecht von Blücher, a Napoleonic Era Prussian general, and the three German ships named after him:
    • Blücher, a corvette of the Imperial era
    • SMS Blücher, an armoured cruiser of World War I
    • Blücher, a heavy cruiser of World War II
  • Blücher Order, a GDR decoration
  • Heinrich Blücher, a German philosopher
  • Vasily Blyukher, Marshal of the Soviet Union (named after the Prussian general)
  • Blücher, an early railway locomotive built by George Stephenson
  • Blücher, a German wolf pack of World War II
  • Blücher or Blucher, a type of shoe whose laces tie over the tongue on two flaps, sometimes used in yachting
  • Frau Blücher, a character in the 1974 film Young Frankenstein
  • Blücher, a hand in the the British card game Napoleon or Nap
