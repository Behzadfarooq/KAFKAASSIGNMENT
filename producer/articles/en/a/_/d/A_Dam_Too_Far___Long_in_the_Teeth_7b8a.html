[edit] Plot for A Dam Too Far

Two monsterous rivers called The Big Happy, and Little Meanie are causing chaos, and the army has sent in beavers Norbert and Daggett to put them under control. Norb is assigned Big Happy, and Dag gets the Little Meanie.

  • Written By: Mitchell Schauer
  • Storyboard: Mitchell Schauer, Michael R. Gerard
  • Directed By: Robert Hughes


[edit] Plot for Long in the Teeth

Norb starts growing his teeth long despite Dag's concerned worries, as well as an instructional videotape claiming that if beavers don't chew, their teeth can grow so long and lock up their jaw, or go through their brain. Norb ignores this, and makes various attempts to get Norb's teeth back to normal length. Eventually Norb becomes partially convinced, until the forest animals, lead by Big Rabbit, compliment his look. A fad breaks out, and eventually Dag is swept in. The powers of nature, however, end up forcing in on Dag and Norb at the end.

  • Written By: Keith Kaczorek
  • Storyboard: Mitchell Schauer, Michael R. Gerard
  • Directed By: George Chialtas

[edit] Trivia

  • The voice of the General in "Dam Too Far" is Ed Winter, who will later become the voice for Scientist Number One.
  • "Dam Too Far is the first episode to feature the beavers, or some other character, eating cocktail weenies.
  • In Dam Too Far, it's revealed that the name of the pond, Dag and Norb live on is Golden Pond.
  • In "Long in the Teeth", a female beaver appears, whom resembles the later character Treeflower.
  • First appearance of Big Rabbit and Barry the Bear. Both characters are unnamed at this point.
  • After Big Rabbit comments on Norb's long teeth, Dag remarks, "Can you believe those guys? What a bunch of weirdos!" When Dag says this, his unibrow becomes two eyebrows for a second.
