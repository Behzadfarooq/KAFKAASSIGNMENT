Ali and Nino: A Love Story is an adventure romance published under the pseudonym Kurban Said, probably the pen name of Baku-born author Lev Nussimbaum.In Azerbaijan until recently the author of this novel was considered to be Yusif Vazir Chamanzaminli - prominent Azeri writer and public figure, but now it is supposed that the book was written by Azerbaijani writer Mahammad Assad bey, whose penname is tought to be Kurban Said[1]

The book was first published in German in Vienna in 1937, by the publisher E. P. Tal Verlag. An English translation was issued in 2000 by Anchor (ISBN 0-385-72040-8). It is considered by many to be one of the great romantic stories and has appeared in 27 languages[2]. The author was unknown for many years and it has been alleged that the Austrian Baroness Elfriede von Ehrenfels[3] wrote the book. Tom Reiss, in The Orientalist, has provided strong evidence that the actual author was Lev Nussimbaum, an Azerbaijani Jew[4][5] who had a supposedly romantic relationship with the baroness while living in Berlin.

[edit] Synopsis

The novel is set in Baku, Azerbaijan, in the years following the Russian revolution. It follows Ali Khan Shirvanshir, an Azeri Muslim boy in his last year of high school. He is in love with a Georgian Christian girl, Nino Kipiani.

Baku is located on the Caspian Sea, literally where East meets the West, and the boy's schoolteacher explicitly discusses the mission the children will have, to bring their country towards "progressive Europe" or "reactionary Asia."

Ali Khan Shirvanshir belongs to Asia. His love, however, is of the other world. In spite of the philosophical gulf and outside discouragement, the two marry. Ali faces a serious decision as the Russian Revolution forces the Azerbaijanis to choose sides, as the Red Army marches onto what would tragically become the original short-lived Azerbaijan Republic.

The novel is unique in its description of pre-Soviet Baku, complete with description of camels, eunuchs and poetry competitions.
