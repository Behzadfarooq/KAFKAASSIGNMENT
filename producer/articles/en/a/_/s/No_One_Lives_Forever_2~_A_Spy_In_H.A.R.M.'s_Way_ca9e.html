No One Lives Forever 2: A Spy In H.A.R.M.'s Way (abbreviated NOLF2) is a computer game developed by Monolith Productions, and published by Sierra Entertainment. It was released on September 30, 2002. It is the second game in No One Lives Forever (Series), an official sequel to the 2000 computer and video game No One Lives Forever. The story follows super-spy Cate Archer as she once again takes up arms against the criminal organization H.A.R.M. This second game features even more exotic locales, such as a Japanese ninja village, a secret submarine base, and a trailer park in Ohio during a tornado. A whole new range of outlandish gadgets and weapons are also introduced, including a blowtorch disguised as a can of hairspray and a robotic bomb disguised as an adorable kitten. An expansion pack to the game, entitled Contract J.A.C.K. was released in 2003.

[edit] Plot

A year after the first H.A.R.M. plot, tensions are rising between the US and USSR over the tiny but strategic isle of Khios. Cate infiltrates the Soviet Union only to discover another H.A.R.M. plot to use their new secret weapon.

[edit] Gameplay

As in the first game, missions can be completed by pure action, by using pure stealth, by using gadgets, or a combination of all methods.

[edit] Technical

No One Lives Forever 2: A Spy In H.A.R.M.'s Way ran on a then-new game engine, Lithtech version 2.0, "Jupiter." The new engine introduced a number of enhancements over NOLF's Lithtech 1.0 engine, including higher quality graphics, facial animation, pixel-shaded water, and a rudimentary implementation of ragdoll physics.

No One Lives Forever 2: A Spy In H.A.R.M.'s Way was also released on the Macintosh.

[edit] Reception

NOLF2 won several Game of the Year awards, for example from Gamespy [1]. It also won a prestigious Excellence in Writing Game Developers Choice Award [2].
