[edit] A more recent discussion

Hi, I've just found out that in February you added a template to Image:A_Hill.jpg saying that the photo is being "considered for deletion". Have you read the corresponding talk page? What more do you want / do we here at Wikipedia need for an image to stay?

Best wishes, <KF> 01:07, Jun 5, 2005 (UTC)

The talk page implies that the image (Image:A Hill.jpg) cannot be used for commercial use which is not allowed under GFDL. Someone has apparently changed the template (after I originally applied it to the image in question) to denote that non-commercial uses images are now being considered for deletion. So, you will either have to find an image that can be used for commercial purposes, get permission for commercial use or eventually the image will be deleted. RedWolf 02:59, Jun 7, 2005 (UTC)
As far as I'm concerned something is terribly wrong about the line of argumentation above. I asked for permission to include that photo in Wikipedia, I got it, and the photographer does not make any (explicit) statement on any other uses of that image. If I'm not completely mistaken, thousands of pictures would have to be deleted if the above interpretation were correct. <KF> 21:38, Jun 11, 2005 (UTC)
  • There are many types of permission for an image. The email at the top of the page implies (i.e. "not being used in a commercial venue") that the image cannot be used for commercial purposes which is in conflict with the GFDL and other like licenses required by Wikipedia. Ask the photographer to grant commercial use and/or ask if they wish to place the image under GFDL. Alternatively, you can make a fair use claim (simply stating "fair use" is not enough) but then the image can only be used in the USA. RedWolf July 2, 2005 01:09 (UTC)

[edit] Non commercial image.

Images that can only be used non-commercially unfortunately are not suitable for inclusion on Wikipedia. This image should therefore be replaced by a free version. I've orphaned the image accordingly. If it's really not possible to find a free version, then please provide a fair-use rationale for the image on it's description page, and ensure it's only used where that rationale applies. - Stephanie Daugherty (Triona) - Talk - Comment - 23:29, 10 January 2007 (UTC)
