A Hand of Bridge, a nine minute opera composed by Samuel Barber in 1959 with libretto by Gian-Carlo Menotti, is one of the shortest operas that is regularly performed. It consists of two couples playing a hand of bridge. During the opera each character has a short arietta in which he or she sings a monologue.

The soprano laments not loving her now dying mother while she was still well. The contralto recalls a hat she saw in a shop window earlier in the day. The tenor recalls an ex-lover and wonders where she is now. Finally, the baritone fantasizes about what he would do if he were as rich as his boss "Mr. Pritchett."

[edit] Characters

  • David, a florid businessman: baritone
  • Geraldine, his middle-aged wife: soprano
  • Bill, a lawyer: tenor
  • Sally, his wife: contralto
