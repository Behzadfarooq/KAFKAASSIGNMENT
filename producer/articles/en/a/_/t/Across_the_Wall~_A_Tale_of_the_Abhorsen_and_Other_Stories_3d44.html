Across the Wall: A Tale of the Abhorsen and Other Stories is a collection of short stories by Garth Nix, which return to the setting of his popular Old Kingdom series. A hardback edition was released in the UK on November 6 2006. There are two special editions of this book in the UK, one with "a unique cover" from WH Smith's, the other with a manuscript for a possible prologue and several other notes on the story of Lirael, available from Waterstone's.[1] All UK editions will include an FAQ section with the author, in which he provides "vital answers". All thirteen main stories had been published prior to the release of Across the Wall, usually in magazines or compilation books.

[edit] The stories

  1. Nicholas Sayre and the Creature in the Case — Originally published for World Book Day (as The Creature in the Case), this novella chronicles Nicholas Sayre's attempt to return to the Old Kingdom.
  2. Under the Lake — Based on the legend of Avalon. This story twists the myth of the Lady of the Lake, making her into a monster who gives Arthur Excalibur for motives that are not entirely pure.
  3. Charlie Rabbit — A war story about two brothers fleeing from their house during a raid.
  4. From the Lighthouse — A science fiction story about a developer who mistakenly believes he's purchased a tropical paradise.
  5. The Hill
  6. Lightning Bringer — A story about a man who strikes down his lovers with lightning.
  7. Down to the Scum Quarter — A parody of the Choose Your Own Adventure books.
  8. Heart's Desire — A discussion of why Merlin trained Nimue.
  9. Hansel's Eyes — A modern version of Hansel and Gretel.
  10. Hope Chest — A western short story with a fantastic twist.
  11. My Really Epic New Fantasy Series — A comedic work that parodies modern fantasy.
  12. Three Roses — A fable.
  13. Endings — An extremely short story about the meaning of sorrow and joy.
