A Tiger Walks is a 1964 family film, directed by Norman Tokar and produced by Disney. It is based on a novel of the same name by Ian Niall. It was Sabu's last film which released only a few weeks after his death.

[edit] Plot

Spoiler warning: Plot and/or ending details follow.

A mistreated Bengal Tiger escapes from a traveling circus, and hides in the woods surrounding the small town of Scotia. The new arrival starts a panic, and the townsfolk want the tiger killed with the exception of Julie Williams (played by Franklin), the sheriff's daughter.

Julie wishes to capture the tiger and put it in a zoo, and she fronts a nationwide attempt to raise enough money to purchase the tiger from the circus. But first, she, her father, and an Indian tiger trainer have to find the tiger before the National Guard, who are under orders to shoot it on sight.
