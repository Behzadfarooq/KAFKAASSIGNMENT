[edit] "Camisado (Relax, Relapse)"

I just checked the lyrics, and I don't see any references to Fight Club. This seems like just someone's interpetation. -- LGagnon 16:31, 28 June 2006 (UTC)

Cant take the kid from the fight, cant take the fight from the kid. Obviously references to fight club, but yes i see your point that its not a straight forward reference, and I also think that it seems as though the whole song is a reference, but yes i suppose it depends on how you interperate it. I would sign this, but i dont know how...

This song isn't about Fight Club (despite the 'take the kid from the fight' line). It's about Ryan's father's battle(s) with alcoholism. - FLM

Any references or links to articles to back this up? Has Ryan actually stated this at any point? It does seem to make some sense, but I would like to see some proof or evidence. - Sion Black

Yes, he has, in many an interview. You can look up any interview about his writing process and find it. He even commented that his father liked the album, but Ryan wasn't sure if he knew some songs were about him. Also, he's stated that he feels uncomfortable when some of the fans sing the lyrics without knowing what the song is about because it's very personal to him. So, in short, yes to your second question, but I don't have any actual links to put on here. - FLM

Camisado is by no means an "obvious" reference to Fight Club since it only mentions an ambigious fight and a hospital too specifically described to be paralled with the narrator's, aside from the truth of the personal content of the song. Palahniuk doesn't have quite enough books for every song by Panic! at the Disco to reference them. (Besides, if there was a song about Fight Club, it would definitely be There's A Good Reason These Tables Are Numbered Honey, You Just Haven't Thought Of It Yet...) Sybil Gray 00:29, 26 October 2006 (UTC)
