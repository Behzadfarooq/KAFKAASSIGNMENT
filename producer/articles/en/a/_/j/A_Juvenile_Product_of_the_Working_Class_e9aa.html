A Juvenile Product of the Working Class is the third full-length album by the Californian punk rock band $wingin' Utter$. It was released on September 1, 1996 as the band's first album on Fat Wreck Chords. It was recorded at H.O.S. in Redwood City, California.

[edit] Trivia

  • The album's name was taken from a line in Elton John's song "Saturday Night's Alright For Fighting".
  • The song "Fifteenth and T" appears on the compilation album Give 'Em the Boot (1997).
