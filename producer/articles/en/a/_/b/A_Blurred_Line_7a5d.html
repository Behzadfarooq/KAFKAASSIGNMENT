A Blurred Line is a RPG Maker 2000 game created by "Lysander86" and released in 2001.

[edit] Story

The game is set in the future, where a giant corporation known as 'the Agency' provides the lifeblood energy to the world through a solar semi-orbital station linked to the planet. This station is known as the Catch Colony.

The player starts in medias res, but the RPG swiftly flashes back to "One Year Ago". The protagonist of the game is named Talan, a low-level worker for Delcentric, a subsidiary of the mammoth Agency which exercises effective control of the world. (The world in question does not appear to be Earth.) Talan is essentially framed (seemingly by his supposed victim himself) for the apparent murder of the Director of the Agency, and finds himself on the run toward a society known as "Paradise". The main theme throughout the game is the blurred line between who is "good" and who is "evil." For example, at one point in the game, an Agency Captain is filled with the memories of an entire society that was destroyed by his own hands. However, once he recovers, the Captain returns to his "evil" goals. Talan's destruction of the Catch Colony at the beginning of the game is a direct contrast to how he acts during the remainder of the story.

[edit] Features

Despite being released in 2001, A Blurred Line featured some interesting innovations. Chief amongst these was the "magic" system. In most RPGs, the player acquires new spells as a matter of course, upon gaining a new level. This was not the case, at least, with Talan. Similar to Final Fantasy VIII, Talan acquires spells by first "identifying" them, then drawing them out of various enemies. Initially the spells would be lost after battle, but should Talan draw out the spells enough times in repeated battles, they permenantly become his. Moreover, Talan's android can equip weapons that determine both how much Skill Point (SP) it uses, but also the skills that it has at its disposal.

Other features include a built-in databse that can be accessed from the menu, alternative story paths that depend on which job Talan takes upon arrival at Paradise, and several side quests that enhance replay value.

[edit] Issues

A Blurred Line has received much praise from fans, and it is generally agreed that it is one of the best games of the RPG Maker community. Some fans also, however, have expressed frustration with Lysander for repeated delays in the release of the conclusion, Line's End. Lysander released an updated version in early 2003, named "A Blurred Line 2.1", which added hours of gameplay, as well as graphical fixes and other additions. Two years later, however, (as of November 2005), Line's End continues to remain unreleased. Nevertheless, a teaser trailer at the end of "A Blurred Line 2.1" shows that the game is very much in progress and continues right where "A Blurred Line" left off. It appears now that Lines End may not be finished for some time now, however, as Lysander86 himself has reportedly stated that he did not enjoy working on the game anymore and it may well be permenantly postponed.
