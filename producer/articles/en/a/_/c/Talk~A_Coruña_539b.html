Totally wrong. Note that "popularity" and "linked to" are different things, and even taken together they are a small part of the weighting process used by search engines. Putting a minus sign in front of a word is another part of it; in logical search engines, that means the results do not include any articles with that word, but obviously (to anybody familiar with set theory, at least) Google does things differently. [edit] La Coruña vs A Coruña

A typically stupid Spanish argument. The simple fact of the matter is that the name La Coruña is the Castillian Spanish name for the city. A Coruña is in the Galician language.

The sign with the city name on it as you enter says, La Coruña, although someone has spraypainted over the letter L. I know because I see it every day.

Certain members within the Galician National Party (BNG) insist that the name be A Coruña, as opposed to a large percentage of members within the Spanish Nationalist Party (PP) who insist that it is La Coruña. It is a politacal argument that has been going on for 25 years, when it is simply the name of a city in 2 different languages. Much the same as London vs Londres.

I really hope we are not going to start seeing petty Spanish political arguments thrashed out here.

All 3 names are valid as all have been used at variuos times in history. And obviously 2 are stll being squabbled over.
