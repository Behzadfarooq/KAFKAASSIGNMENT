A Good Man is the title of an essay written by Dr. Matt Egbe. It was originally published in the premier (February 2007) edition of The Journal of Alternative Scientific Research. It was intended as a homage to one of Dr. Egbe's co-workers, Sheridan "Buddy" Hensler, whose unique talents and many years of experience have proven invaluable to the company at which they both work.

Of greater importance is that it serves as a cautionary tale to those with unique abilities such as those attributed to the subject of the essay.

[edit] Personal background

Sheridan Montgomery Hensler was born in 1938 in Newtown, Pennsylvania. He entered the U.S. Navy after graduating from high school. During his 12-year naval career, he rose to the rank of Chief Petty Officer. His duties included working in the machine shop of several ships, including a deployment to Vietnam aboard the USS Oriskany. He was honorably discharged in 1969 and soon found civilian work as a mason outside of Philadelphia.

Sheridan met his wife, Shirley, at a biker bar in 1972. Shirley was a bartender and was being harassed by someone whom she would later describe as "a bad man". Her future husband (a regular at the bar) stepped in on her behalf and tossed the troublemaker out into the street. The bar's other patrons were thankful to be rid of the pest and started calling Sheridan by the nickname "Buddy". The name stuck and soon he and Shirley were dating. They eloped in Elkton, Maryland in 1973. They have two children and one grandchild and currently reside in Bally, Pennsylvania.

[edit] Buddy's unique talent

One day in the late 1970's, Buddy was working on a retaining wall at a remote rural worksite. A brown bear wandered into the worksite, presumably lured by the smell of the workers' lunches. Rather than flee to safety, Buddy took his sandwich (he says it was "a nice Italian hoagie... I'm still upset about losing it") and managed to lead the bear away from the worksite. After getting to a safe distance, Buddy threw the hoagie towards some nearby woods. Buddy says he still doesn't know why, but the bear went after the hoagie instead of the human and was not seen again. His co-workers said that it was one of the strangest things they had ever witnessed.

After getting home that night, Buddy told Shirley about the incident. Shirley, after giving her husband an appropriate tongue lashing for his foolish actions, pointed out that Buddy had always had a way with animals. He always liked animals, from the squirrels that he used to feed from his bedroom window as a child to the gecko that he kept as a pet on board the USS Oriskany. With the decline of construction during the Carter administration, it was decided that it was time for Buddy to do something different. He eventually enrolled at Bucks County Community College, majoring in biology.

After getting his associate's degree in 1981, Buddy re-entered the workforce. He quickly found a position at Ash Research, a small biotesting company located in Easton, Pennsylvania. His early assignments involved handling a wide variety of animals, including cicadas, stingrays, and horses. It quickly became apparent that Buddy had more than just a knack for working with animals. He seemed to have an almost-telepathic connection with all sorts of creatures. His mere presence was usually enough to calm even the most distressed test subject.

His talent is something akin to that of a horse whisperer although it is by no means limited to only one species.

[edit] Professional struggles and successes

Buddy's gift has proved to be a both a blessing and a curse. His superior work with animal test subjects has been a major factor in the development of improved appetite suppression and smoking cessation products.

While becoming an invaluable member of his department, his "star" status became a point of contention with some of his fellow scientists. On one notable occasion, former Ash Research employees Rick Hsu and John Fisher attempted to discredit Buddy's research findings by being whistleblowers, prompting an FDA investigation. A four-month search came up with no findings, thus clearing Buddy's name. A pair of lawsuits subsequently filed by Buddy and Ash Research were settled out-of-court for an undisclosed sum.

Despite these past incidents, Buddy has remained positive and upbeat. His unique gift remains of great use to the entire company, although he admits that retirement is not too far away. "Shirley has been on my case to clean the gutters. I don't know about you, but I'd rather get paid for doing that sort of thing."

[edit] Summary

"I have worked with a lot of people in my career. A few of them made me want to go on vacation and not come back to work. Most of them have been decent workers and upstanding individuals, but they don't come any better than Buddy Hensler. That is a good man... that is a fine man." - Dr. Matt Egbe
