A Girl Called Dusty is the debut album of singer Dusty Springfield, released on Philips records in 1964.

As a member of the girl group The Lana Sisters from 1958-1960, and the folk-pop trio The Springfields (with her brother Tom Springfield) from 1960-1963, Dusty Springfield was able to tour outside of London, and even Britain, with the Springfields. While in the US she was exposed to soul music which was to have a profound impact on Springfield's life and career. While the Springfields were moderately successful with songs such as Island Of Dreams, Bambino and Silver Threads And Golden Needles (a #16 US country hit), their style of music limited the wide range of material that Dusty Spingfield wanted to sing. So in 1963, Springfield decided to begin a career as a solo singer. Her first single I Only Want To Be With You, was actually recorded while still a member of the Springfields, and released a mere week after their final concert. The song was a success in both Britain and the US, and led to the recording of A Girl Called Dusty which was released in 1964. The album contained a mix of mostly straightforward pop songs, but also included a few Motown influenced numbers, such as When The Lovelight Starts Shining Through His Eyes, Will You Love Me Tomorrow and Mockingbird. It also marked Springfield's first collaborations with well known songwriters Burt Bacharach and Hal David, as well as Gerry Goffin and Carole King, whose songs Springfield recorded up to and on her final album A Very Fine Love in 1995. It also included the first single Springfield wrote herself, Once Upon A Time.

A US compilation of her singles and tracks recorded for her debut album, Stay Awhile/I Only Want To Be With You was released there and titled after her first two singles, both of which were Top 20 hits in the US. Later in 1964, the album Dusty was released, a version of her British debut album with a different tracklisting and different tracks, some of which had been included on Stay Awhile/I Only Want To Be With You.

In 1997, two years before Springfield's death, A Girl Called Dusty was released on CD for the first time, and included six bonus tracks not on the original LP release.
