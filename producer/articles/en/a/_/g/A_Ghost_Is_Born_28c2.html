A Ghost Is Born is an album by the band Wilco. It was released on June 22, 2004. A Ghost Is Born reached the top ten of the US album charts, the best performance by a Wilco album to date.

[edit] Making of the album

The album was co-produced by Wilco and Jim O'Rourke, who mixed Yankee Hotel Foxtrot, the band's previous album, and has also worked with Sonic Youth. The release of the album was delayed after lead singer Jeff Tweedy went into rehabilitation for addiction to migraine painkillers. The album was available for listening on the band's website months before its release.

[edit] Grammy

In 2005, A Ghost Is Born won two Grammy Awards for Best Alternative Album and Best Recording Package. It was Wilco's first ever Grammy win, although they had been nominated twice before for their work on Mermaid Avenue and Mermaid Avenue Vol. II. They were playing a show in Birmingham, Alabama, at the time.

[edit] Bonus EP

In 2005, Wilco made available a free, five-track bonus EP for listeners who purchased the A Ghost Is Born CD, and played it in their computer. And in a similar fashion to the release of Yankee Hotel Foxtrot, these tracks were also made available in a deluxe edition of the album, which contained them on a bonus cd.

  1. "Panthers" – 3:51
  2. "At Least That's What You Said" (Live) – 5:56
  3. "The Late Greats" (Live) – 2:41
  4. "Handshake Drugs" (Live) – 6:14
  5. "Kicking Television" – 2:51
