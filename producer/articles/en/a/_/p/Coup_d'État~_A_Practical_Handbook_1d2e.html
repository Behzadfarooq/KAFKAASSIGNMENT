Coup d'État: A Practical Handbook

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Coup d'État: A Practical Handbook is a politically neutral, 1968 book by Edward N. Luttwak, that examines the preconditions, strategy, planning, and execution of a coups d'état.

The book uses examples of real life attempted coup d'etats. Both failed and successful, to prove the books points which are covered through the five chapters. -

  • What is a coup d'etat?
Which explains the difference between revolution, civil war, and the coup d'etat itself.
  • When is a coup d'etat possible?,
This tells about what the preconditions must be for the coup to work. Namely that of a poor or developing country with a small and centralized voting government and/or few people with real power to change government and its policies.
  • The Strategy of a coup d'etat.
  • The Planning of a coup d'etat
  • The Execution of a coup d'etat.

Also, three appendices and numerous tables and charts are included along with a short section on dealing with the aftermath and making the people responsible for the coup legally in charge of the country involved.
