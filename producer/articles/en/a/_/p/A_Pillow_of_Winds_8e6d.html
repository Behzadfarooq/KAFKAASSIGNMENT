"A Pillow of Winds" is the second track from Pink Floyd's 1971 album Meddle. The song is a soft acoustic piece, and rather uncharacteristically for a Pink Floyd song, the lyrics deal with love. Guitarist David Gilmour composed the track, and Roger Waters wrote the lyrics.

According to Nick Mason, the song's title originates from a possible hand in the game of Mahjong, which the band had become enamoured with while touring.

[edit] Personnel

  • David Gilmour - Vocals, Acoustic guitar and Slide guitar
  • Richard Wright - Backing Vocals and Organ,
  • Nick Mason - Cymbals
  • Roger Waters- Bass
