Raggedy Ann and Andy: A Musical Adventure is a 1977 animated film from 20th Century Fox, directed by Richard Williams (also from Who Framed Roger Rabbit and The Thief and the Cobbler). It is the only film to feature the eponymous dolls created by Johnny Gruelle.

The film featured songs by Joe Raposo (who is best known for his work for Sesame Street). It was not a major commercial success, and as of October 2006 has yet to be released on DVD.

[edit] Plot Summary

Spoiler warning: Plot and/or ending details follow.

Raggedy Ann is the beloved doll of Marcella, along with her brother Raggedy Andy and a whole nursery full of colorful toys. Whenever Marcella leaves the room, the dolls spring to life. On Marcella's birthday, a new doll, Babette, arrives from Paris. Babette is a spoiled creature who is unaware she is a doll, but the friendly Raggedy Ann does everything she can to make Babette feel at home.

Soon, however, the pirate Captain Contagious, who lives in Marcella's snow globe, becomes enamored of Babette and kidnaps her. Raggedy Ann and Andy set off to try and rescue her before Marcella discovers Babette is gone. Out in the world outside the nursery, the two meet the Camel with the Wrinkled Knees, a blue toy camel who has been cast off by past owners and is now heartbroken and lonely. Raggedy Ann assures him a home with Marcella, and he joins their rescue party.

After Raggedy Ann and Andy hitch a ride on the Camel, he begins to follow his hallucinations and without looking, runs over the edge of a cliff, into a deep pit. In this pit they encounter the Greedy, who is a giant gluttonous blob of taffy who eats constantly and is never full. After singing about plight of never being fully, he reveals that the only things that could satisfy him is a candy heart; which is what Raggedy Ann has sewn up inside her toy body. The Raggedy's and the Camel narrowly escape being consumed by the Greedy and continue their journey to find Babette.

While travelling through the woods, they encounter Sir Leonard Looney, a purple knight with a penchant for humiliating gags. He tricks the trio and kidnaps them to Looney Land where all the practical gags are. They are introduced the court of King KooKoo, a near microscopic king who has a part of him expand every time he laughs at someone else. He finds that the curious little trio that has been brought to him does a good job of making him expand overall when they subjected to Sir Leonard Looney's gags.

Before he can find a better use for them, the Raggedys and the Camel escape out an exit leaving King KooKoo to shrink back down to his regular size. King KooKoo then decides that the Raggedys are perfect and will be just what he needs to accomplish his "Last Laugh." He calls up his cohort Gadzooks, a giant green sea creature with a massive array of arms, to tickle Raggedy Ann and Andy and their friends.

Meanwhile, the Raggedys and the Camel have found a get-a-way boat, and are making posthaste to get away from Looney Land. Using a telescope aboard the bizzarre little ship they are able to spot the Captain Contagious ship and are able to zoom in on board.

When Babette is finally found she is no longer in need of rescue. She has charmed the pirate crew, raised a mutiny against Captain Contagious, is now in command and is taking the ship back to Paris. King KooKoo, with assistance from Gadzooks, shortly arrive and begin to snatching passengers off of the boat, notably most of the male pirate crew, Raggedy Andy, the Camel and Captain Contagious. Gadzooks begins his "tickle torture" causing King KooKoo to expand completely, achieving his goal of being "the biggest king." Raggedy Ann and Babette are hiding underneath the canopy of one of escape boats, with the Queezy, trying to figure out what to do when they (with the help of Raggedy Andy) that King KooKoo should "blow - up!"

King KooKoo discovers Raggedy and Babette hiding has them subjected to the same tickle-torture, while Queezy unseen rockets towards the massive balloon that King KooKoo has become. Thinking he is victorious in his feat, King KooKoo celebrates his enormous size when Queezy flies into King KooKoo's massive body at a high speed, causing him to explode.

Raggedy Ann and Andy are seen falling down a giant tube, along with the Camel, before the screen goes dark, and they discovered, along with all of Marcella's other toys, in a small pond outside. Marcella brings her toys back in, missing the Camel in pile of leaves. Later that evening the Camel crawls up to the window where Raggedy Ann and her friends let him in, and he realizes he has finally found home.

[edit] Trivia

The plots of the Disney movies Toy Story and parts of Toy Story 2 are almost identical to plot points in Raggedy Ann & Andy, with only the characters and various events changed. It is unclear whether the influence is direct or inadvertent.

This film contains the longest tickle-torture scene ever animated.

[edit] Adult Themes

Some adult themes have been pointed out in this film, some noticeable of which are:

  • Raggedy Andy's statement that he is "No Girl's Toy", yet he is an utter wimp in any fight scene, if not effeminate in some instances.
  • The Greedy, while has been mentioned to resemble Marlon Brando, while changes from male to what would appear to be an overweight drag queen.
  • King KooKoo's need to be enormous would function as an allegory for male penis envy, from a Freudian standpoint.
  • On top of KooKoo's Freudian allegory, he specifically targets Raggedy Andy as making him "expand real well."
