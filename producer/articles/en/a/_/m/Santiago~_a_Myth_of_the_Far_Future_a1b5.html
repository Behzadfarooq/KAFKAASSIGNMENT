Santiago: A Myth of the Far Future is a novel by American science fiction author Mike Resnick. It was first published in 1986 and reprinted in 2004.

Spoiler warning: Plot and/or ending details follow.

The setting of the novel is the Inner Frontier, a region on the outskirts of the galaxy-wide Democracy which humans have formed. The title character, Santiago, is the most sought-after outlaw of the Inner Frontier. Each chapter in the book is headed by a four-line poem, purportedly written by the wandering ballad-writer Black Orpheus, the chronicler of the most interesting people along the Inner Frontier.

The story has several protagonists. The main one, a bounty hunter named Sebastian Nightingale Cain, is searching for Santiago in order to cash in the enormous reward on his head. The journalist Virtue Mackenzie wants an interview with Santiago to make her fortune, the crime lord Jolly Swagman wants some unique pieces of alien art which Santiago owns, and the preacher and gunman Father William claims not to be looking for Santiago at all - he is preaching Christianity and collecting money for churches, usually by brute force. Cain meets these people and a number of others, including other bounty hunters, assassins, cyborgs and bar maids, in his quest for Santiago.

The novel is divided into six parts, each named after one of the larger-than-life characters that populate the Inner Frontier. The story is basically a tall tale, reminiscent of stories about the Wild West, with lonely heroes, shoot-outs and faithless companions.
