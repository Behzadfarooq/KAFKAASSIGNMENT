[edit] Accents

"Oprah's minge has an Australian accent, while Gary, her asshole, has a Scottish accent." - I didn't realise it was meant to be an Australian accent until i read this... I'm Australian, and damn that was a pretty bad attempt if that's what it was meant to be. - steve dangerous

I'm Australian too, I recognised it pretty quickly. It actually sounds identical to the way they voiced Russell Crowe in an earlier episode. Pretty funny, although I don't get the reason behind the accents. Orichalcon 07:30, 20 April 2006 (UTC)

I'm also an Aussie and that is defenantly not an Australian accent, it sounds like a thick British accent to me, either that or its the worst attempt of an Australian accent i've ever heard.

Yeah, I was thinking it was brit/scot when I heard it. american cartoons seem pretty bad at aussie accents... reminds me of the simpsons' poor attempt. - steve dangerous

Mingy sounded more Ross Kemp cockney than anything else

Agreed. It's cockney "gangster" (think Lock, Stock and Two Smoking Barrels or Snatch). "Gary" is not Mancunian, more camp Scottish.

Gary's accent is definately not Scottish, sounds more Welsh to me (possibly lapsing into a Manchester Accent).

Well, they had me fooled. I thought it was the guys from Little Britain. Angr (talk • contribs) 12:33, 22 April 2006 (UTC)

Nononono. bloody hell. Minge alternates between cockney and Aussie and Arsehole is a pretty consistent camp scouser (person from Liverpool) sounded Welsh once or twice though, I'll give that.

Mingy was cockney and Gary was scouse, end of. 80.229.161.131 00:58, 8 May 2006 (UTC)

Agreed. As someone who is actually Scottish - Gary ain't. He is scouse.

Both accents were terrible. Minge is a bad attempt at a sort of cocney, while Gary is a bad a attempt at a Liverpudlian accent. I have to say that the British accents in South Park are pretty bad (Pip is an exception), but usually dont detract too much from the programme because they're not overused. Here though, the accents just got annoying. This said, I don't consider myself an authority on accents and could be wrong, but I'm Irish and have heard enough of both accents to be fairly sure.

Small point, but Welsh and Scottish are not 'British English' accents- they are British, but, obviously, are from Wales and Scotland respectively, which are definitely not part of England. This might seem like nitpicking to some, but many Welsh and Scots can get pretty offended if they are confused or lumped in with the English. 82.38.163.106 02:04, 4 January 2007 (UTC)
