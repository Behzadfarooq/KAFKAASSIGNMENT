The Lord of the Rings: A Reader's Companion (2005) is a nonfiction book written by scholars Wayne Hammond and Christina Scull. It is an annotated reference to J. R. R. Tolkien's The Lord of the Rings. The book includes some previously unpublished material by Tolkien, including part of a 1951 letter and extra notes on nomenclature used in The Lord of the Rings. It is available in both hardcover and paperback.

The Reader's Companion was designed to accompany the revised one-volume 50th anniversary edition of The Lord of the Rings and was issued with it as a slipcased set.

The Lord of the Rings: A Reader's Companion won the 2006 Mythopoeic Scholarship Award for Inklings Studies.
