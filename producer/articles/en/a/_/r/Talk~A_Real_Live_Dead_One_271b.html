It appears that there is a misprint in the A Real Live Dead One's song listing, that has been copied to it from A Real Dead One's... According to the booklets, songs The Trooper and Iron Maiden were recorded at Helsinki Ice Hall (In Finnish "Jäähalli", in Swedish "Ishallen") June 5th, 1992. However, the songs Bring Your Daughter....to The Slaughter, The Clairvoyant and Fear of The Dark (that are on A Real Live One and A Real Live Dead One) would have been recorded in the same place, but on August 27th, 1992. I checked the tour date listing on A Real Live One's booklet (which doesn't, btw, appear on A Real Dead One's booklet) and according to it, Iron Maiden's Fear of The Dark tour (during which all the songs for these live albums were recorded) began from Reykjavík, Iceland 5th of June, while the only Helsinki concert during the tour is on August 27th. By listening both Iron Maiden and the The Trooper from A Real Live Dead One, I am assured that they truly have been recorded in Helsinki ("Scream for me, Helsinki" -chants by the singer) but the date seems to be wrong. --KirvesAxe 14:01, 16 November 2006 (UTC)
