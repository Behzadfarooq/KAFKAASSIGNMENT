Normally, before making any kind of major change to an article, you should mention what you are thinking about on the talk page of the article. That will usually generate a discussion, and if the consensus agrees with you, then you can feel free to make the changes.
The above guideline applies only to major changes. If you notice an error, or if you want to make an isolated change to an article, then go right ahead and do that. A common phrase is to "be bold", however you should always be prepared for somebody to disagree with you and dispute your change. Usually, if you are willing to consider different perspectives, you will be able to get along just fine. ;^) wrp103 (Bill Pringle) 00:17, 10 January 2007 (UTC)
