"A Night in Sickbay" is the title of the 30th episode (production #205) of the television series Star Trek: Enterprise which originally aired on October 16, 2002 in the United States.

[edit] Plot summary

Spoiler warning: Plot and/or ending details follow.

A light-weight, dramedy episode, "A Night in Sickbay" sees Captain Jonathan Archer dealing with the Kreetassans â€” a race of grouchy, officious aliens whom Archer and his crew originally met in the episode "Vox Sola". This time, the Kreetassans are insulted and negotiations for a badly needed plasma injector break down when Archer's dog, Porthos, urinates on one of the aliens' sacred trees. A secondary subplot sees Archer and Dr. Phlox tending to Porthos after the dog contracts a potentially deadly virus on the planet, and Archer is so consumed with worry that he ends up spending the night in Sick Bay to look after his ailing pet. A tertiary subplot has Archer dealing with unbidden sexual dreams about his first officer, T'Pol. Through it all, Archer struggles to reach an emotional understanding with Phlox and T'Pol, as the two alien crew members have little grasp of just how important pets are to humans and Phlox keeps offering Archer unwanted advice about dealing with his new feelings for T'Pol. T'Pol also keeps urging Archer to apologize to the Kreetassans for Porthos' behavior, but Archer resists doing this because he blames them for Porthos life-threatening illness.

In the end, Porthos recovers from his illness. Archer puts aside his feelings for T'Pol, who is none the wiser that he had been so smitten with her. Swallowing his pride, the captain goes down to the Kreetassan capital city and delivers an intricate ritual apology which involves cutting off slices of a tree trunk with a chainsaw, arranging the pieces of wood in a complex pattern on the ground, and intoning ceremonial phrases in the Kreetassan language. Having mollified the high-strung aliens, the Enterprise crew finally manage to procure a plasma injector from them.

[edit] Criticism

This relatively low-key episode, intended to be a mix of comedy and drama that would showcase the normally stalwart Captain Archer's more vulnerable side as he frets over his ailing pet and suffers unwanted sexual fantasies about one of his crewmembers (T'Pol, his first officer), was panned by many fans and critics, and is often held to be the "jump the shark" moment for the series. Many held it up as a prime example of the problems they saw in the series which were not being fixed, including using sex for ratings instead of using a mature plot. [1]. It was nominated for a Hugo Award, received the highest number of viewers in season 2 (6.2 million according to TrekToday[2]) and was cited by cast members as one of their favorite episodes.
