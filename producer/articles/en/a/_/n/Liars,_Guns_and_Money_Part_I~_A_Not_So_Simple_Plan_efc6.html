"Liars, Guns and Money Part I: A Not So Simple Plan" is an episode from the second season of the American television series Farscape, written by Grant McAloon and directed by Andrew Prowse.

[edit] Synopsis

Stark appears to tell the Moyans that Jothee is a slave and is being sold in a lot of 10,000 slaves, which they can buy and therefore rescue him. But to do so, they must first rob a "shadow depository"--a bank for illegal money from criminal activities. Thus, they will only be stealing from criminals. Stark has the plans to the depository from the designer of the systems, who he helped over to the other side. John, Aeryn, and Chiana are hesitant and wants to know more, doubting this plan will work, and D'Argo furiously turns on John, feeling that he's being unsupportive. D'Argo goes ahead with the plan with Stark, while John and Aeryn decide to help him, though D'Argo now wants nothing to do with them. D'Argo tries to break into the depository, and is captured and tortured by Natira, the crab-like female who is in charge of the depository. But it's all part of the plan—because of the break-in, they are re-setting the secret codes, and Stark can steal the codes at that point. Meanwhile, Zhaan disguises herself as a female named Oralla, who wants to make a deposit. The plan involves Rygel being hidden in Zhaan's worthless deposit. Once in the vault, he will switch signals with another person's things, so when "Oralla" calls for her stuff back, they'll get the other person's things instead.

The plan starts to go awry when Scorpius shows up, which John sees on security monitors. It turns out that Scorpius and Natira are lovers. Natira owes Scorpius funds from his deposits, since, thinking him dead after the Gammak Base destruction, she disposed of his things. She agrees to give him three times the worth of his original deposit, in borinium ingots. To complicate matters, John starts hallucinating that he sees Scorpius in places where he doesn't. John sees a nurse change Scorpius's cooling rods, the first time John has seen that. He asks Zhaan to make up some kind of substance that will explode when hot. He and Aeryn then steal into the room where Scorpius' cooling rods are stored, and he attempts to coat the rods with the exploding substance. But he can't. John is going insane--he keeps hearing Scorpius' voice in his head, urging him to turn himself in to Scorpius, and preventing him from doing anything to harm Scorpius. Aeryn then coats the rods in John's place.

Meanwhile, the Moyans' plan continues--they get the ingots and Scorpius gets Oralla's worthless deposit. Scorpius realizes Crichton is there, and he and Natira try to capture him. The Moyans flee the base, but John is almost unable to function, Scorpius' control has gotten so powerful. The two have a confrontation, with John contorted by the Scorpius-chip, and Scorpius contorted by the exploding substance that John put on his cooling rods. Scorpius and the chip order John to change his rod, repeating "insert the rod". John tries to resist, and ultimately, it seems, doesn't change the rod. He crawls away, leaving Scorpius to die.

The Moyans return to the ship, rejoicing in their new wealth, plotting to buy Jothee's freedom. But D'Argo's resentmente of John is implacable--he feels he was betrayed, and won't forgive him. Meanwhile, John tells Aeryn about the Scorpychip, while, in the cargo hold, the ingots appear to come to life and turn into spider-like metal creatures.
