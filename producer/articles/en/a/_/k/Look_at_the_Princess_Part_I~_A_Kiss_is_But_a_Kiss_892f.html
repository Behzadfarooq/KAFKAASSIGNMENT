"Look at the Princess Part I: A Kiss is But a Kiss" is an episode from the second season of the American television series Farscape, written by David Kemper and directed by Andrew Prowse and Tony Tilse.

[edit] Synopsis

As the episode begins, Aeryn and John are in his module, and Aeryn is giving him flying tips. They banter back and forth and then start kissing, but Aeryn abruptly breaks the clinch and jumps out of the module--it is in the hangar bay--and yells at Crichton that she doesn't want to be subject to his hormones, and they fight. Crichton tries to make Aeryn jealous by going to talk to Chiana, but he walks in on her with D'Argo in flagrante delicto, to Crichton's surprise. Then Moya arrives at The Royal Planet, one of "The Breakaway Colonies" that left the Peacekeepers 2000 years earlier and now maintains strict neutrality between the PKs and the Scarrans. The Royal Planet is currently in a state of preparation for the wedding of the crown princess, Katralla, and an as yet unknown groom. Katralla's DNA was poisoned by her brother, Clavor, and if she cannot find a mate whose DNA is compatible with her own, Clavor will become heir to the throne instead. Unfortunately, Clavor is very stupid, and is friendly with the Scarrans, and is always seen with one, Cargn. But if the Scarrans gain influence in The Breakaway Colonies, the Peacekeepers will invade. If the Breakaway Colonies show preference to the PKs, the Scarrans will invade.

Compatibility can be determined via a kiss between couples after a special fluid has been applied to the tongues of each. Crichton unexpectedly finds he is genetically compatible with Katralla. Complicating matters, Scorpius shows up, ready to grab John. The Empress says she will turn John over to Scorpius if he doesn't agree to marry Katralla, so he agrees, angering Aeryn. Crichton apologizes, but explains that Scorpius has a powerful hold on him that he just can't shake and that terrifies him. Princess Katralla, while knowing the necessity of marrying Crichton, is actually in love with Tyno, a government official. And Crichton discovers to his dismay that after the wedding, he and Katralla will be turned into sentient statues, who will spend 80 years presiding over the senate and learning about the system of government, before being reanimated and ascending the throne. As the episode ends, a team of assassins breaks in to kill John to prevent the marriage.

Meanwhile, Moya feels called to a particular place, where she finds "The Builders"--the beings who built and gave life to the Leviathans. The builder Kahaynu tells Moya that she must die.
