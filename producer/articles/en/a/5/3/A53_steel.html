A53 steel

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

A53 is a carbon steel alloy, used for structural steel round tubing and pipes. The alloy specifications are set by ASTM International.

A53 comes in three subtypes, A53 Type A which is furnace butt welded (Grade A), A53 Type E which is electric resistance welded (Grades A and B), and A53 Type S, which is similarly welded but processed to avoid any seam on the inside or outside (Grades A and B). [1] [2]

[edit] Specifications

As with other steels, A53 has a specific gravity of approximately 7.85, and density of 0.285 pounds per cubic inch.

A53 tubing coumes in two grades, A and B. Grade A is not commonly used for structural applications.

Grade B has a minimum yield strength of 33,000 psi (33 ksi, where 1 ksi = 1,000 psi) (225 MPa), and minimum ultimate tensile strength of 45,000 psi (45 ksi) (310 MPa). [1]
