Blouza (Arabic: بلوزا‎, also spelt Blawza and Blaouza), is a Maronite Christian village in the Bsharri District of northern Lebanon. The population is approximately 3,000[1], and the village lies at an altitude of 1,320 metres above sea level[2].

Blouza lies in the Qadisha Valley, an area settled 1,000-1,300 years ago by Maronite refugees fleeing persecution in the remote mountains of Lebanon and Syria. In their isolation, the communities of the Qadisha Valley preserved their religion, customs and language until the modern era. Most villages in the region have Syriac Aramaic names, and the name Blouza comes from the Syriac Aramaic word " "[3].

In common with most of the Maronite villages in the region, Blouza natives speak Arabic with a distinct accent, as until the early 20th century, most villagers were native Aramaic-speakers. Unlike most Maronites, residents of Blouza (and the Qadisha Valley in general) claim to be of Aramean -- rather than Phoenecian -- descent.
