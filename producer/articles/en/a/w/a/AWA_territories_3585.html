AWA territories are small, independent professional wrestling promotions affiliated with AWA Superstars of Wrestling. AWA Superstars of Wrestling claims to be the successor to the American Wrestling Association (AWA), which folded in 1991. Unlike the original AWA, which was wholly owned and operated by Verne Gagne, the new AWA has adopted a territory system similar to the National Wrestling Alliance. However, unlike the NWA, most AWA territories keep their original names as well as their AWA territory name. These territories are neither as large nor as strong as the pre-1990 territories, which dominated pro wrestling under the NWA banner.

The top AWA Affiliate is Ernie Todd's CWF. Todd himself is thought of amonst the promotion's members as a modern day Verne Gagne. Todd is also a fine wrestler in his own right, beating the likes of Plumber Lou Dooley and Silverstreak 2000 both on the same night! Steve Corino claims to be Todd's #1 fan. He once claimed he'd go through a wall head first for Mr. Todd.

[edit] AWA Superstars of Wrestling territories

Full members are listed, with their "territory affiliates" listed below them. This list is current as of June, 2006:

  • AWA Minnesota, the original AWA Superstars promotion owned by Dale Gagner. Gagner uses the name Dale Gagne, presumably to draw a connection to the classic AWA.
    • AWA Great American Wrestling Federation, based in Panama City, Florida. GAWF was an affiliate of AWA Florida until that promotion broke with the AWA and sued Dale Gagner in February 2006.
  • AWA Japan (in Japanese), the Pro Wrestling ZERO1-MAX promotion in Japan. This is the successor to the original Zero-1 promotion, which was affiliated with the NWA. The promotion also maintains an English-language website.
    • AWA World-1, an affiliate of Pro Wrestling ZERO1-MAX. This is former AWA World Champion Steve Corino's home promotion, which maintains an associate membership through Zero-1.
  • AWA Mid-Atlantic, the Carolina Wrestling Federation, based in Burlington, North Carolina. Former AWA World Heavyweight Champion Ric Converse is based here. The CWF previously affiliated with Frontier Wrestling Alliance, a United Kingdom-based promotion that was trying to expand globally.
  • AWA Indiana, Indiana's Supreme Championship Wrestling.
  • AWA Kentucky, Bluegrass Championship Wrestling, based in Feds Creek, Kentucky.
  • AWA Hawaii, Hawai'i Championship Wrestling. This promotion uses former WWF star Don Muraco as its Commissioner. It held both NWA and AWA membership until it withdrew from the NWA in September 2006.
  • AWA Oregon, the West Coast Wrestling Connection, based in Salem, Oregon.
  • AWA Connecticut/Pennsylvania, the World Star Wrestling Federation. Based in Connecticut/Pennsylvania, they also run shows in Ohio and New York.
  • AWA Rocky Mountains, Rocky Mountain Championship Wrestling, based in Colorado.
  • AWA Canada, the Canadian Wrestling Federation. This promotion was an NWA member for years, with owner Ernie Todd rising to the NWA Presidency in October 2004, then resigning in the summer of 2005 over allegations of racism. Todd joined the AWA almost immediately after leaving the NWA.
    • AWA Ontario, the Northern Wrestling Xperience, a Canadian Wrestling Federation affiliate.
  • AWA California, Xtreme Zone Wrestling, based in San Bernardino, California.
  • AWA Chicago, SLAM Wrestling, based in Chicago, Illinois.
  • AWA United Kingdom, Chapman Promotions in Hertsfordshire, UK.
  • AWA West Virginia, APEX Wrestling, based in Oak Hill, West Virginia.
  • AWA Washington, Pinnacle Wrestling, based in Pacific, Washington.
  • AWA Wisconsin, Brew City Wrestling, based in Waukesha, Wisconsin.
  • AWA Texas, the Alliance of Lucha Libre of El Paso, promoting the Mexican lucha libre style of wrestling.
  • AWA Alabama, All Star Wrestling, based in Hanceville, Alabama.
  • AWA North Atlantic, New Wrestling Horizons, based in Bangor, Maine.
  • Wrestle Reunion, a company that promotes nostalgia weekends featuring past and current AWA wrestlers.
