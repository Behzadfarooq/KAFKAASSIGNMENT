AWA World Women's Championship

The AWA World Women's Championship was the female title in the American Wrestling Association from 1961 until 1990. The title was revived by AWA Superstars in 1996 when they awarded the title to Sherri Martel.

[edit] Title history

Silver-shaded lines in the history indicate periods of unknown lineage.

June Byers 1 1960 Recognized as the first AWA World Women's Champion after the AWA split from the National Wrestling Alliance. Byers defeated Mildred Burke for the NWA World Women's Championship on August 20, 1954 in Atlanta, Georgia.
Title vacated 1963 The AWA vacated the title. Byers continued to be recognized as NWA Champion until she retired in January 1964.
Kay Noble 1 April 13, 1963 St. Paul, Minnesota Defeated Kathy Starr to win the vacant title.
Vivian Vachon 1 November 4, 1971 Winnipeg, Manitoba, Canada Defeated Betty Nicoli to win the vacant title.

Candi Devine 5 December 6, 1989 Toronto, Ontario, Canada Defeated Judy Martin to win the vacant title.
