Awaiting Your Reply is the debut album by American Christian rock band Resurrection Band, released in 1978.

[edit] Recording history

A groundbreaking release by Christian music standards at the time, the album caused considerable controversy among Christian music critics, many of whom found fault with everything from its album cover art to its heavy rock sounds[citation needed], which are clearly influenced by Led Zeppelin. Maybe the critics didn't know what to make of it, but Awaiting Your Reply along with its follow-up, Rainbow's End, quickly solidified Resurrection Band's place in the upper echelon of Christian rock music due to the band's socially conscious Christian lyrics and solid musicianship. In 2001, the album was listed at #91 in the book, CCM Presents: The 100 Greatest Albums in Christian Music.

[edit] Miscellanea

  • According to Glenn Kaiser, the album was recorded for only $8000 US over a period of two weeks in marathon all-night sessions.
  • Every Christian record label in the United States and Great Britain turned down the album, until the independent Star Song finally signed Resurrection Band to a record deal. The deal put Star Song on the map, and the label soon became one of the largest independents in Contemporary Christian music.
  • Many Christian bookstores sold the album from behind the counter, as the cover art was considered too controversial to display openly.
  • Wendi Kaiser is pregnant with she and Glenn's first child in her photo on the rear cover.
