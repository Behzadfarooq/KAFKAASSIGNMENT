My interests lie in computer aided creation and my passion is the design and development of major applications in the areas of computer science that pose the most technical challenge.


Over the last 10 years the art and business of software has enthralled me. I often work on multiple brainchild projects at once. My publicly published applications include web servers. I had the top downloaded server on Download.com at one point. I've also had success with video capture media processing, and artificial intelligence software. I love to create applications for consumers, and for clients. This often leads me to create parts of larger enterprise software systems which recombine software components to create application workflows supporting the world's largest systems.


My approach to working with computers is to use an 'Evolvable' framework and methodology which I've been authoring over the last 14 years. The software development approach uses living concepts and embraces change.


The Framework places emphasis on the role of psychology, data analysis, genetic algorithms, networking, the mind, and usability on software creation. It attempts to dispel anything that overcomplicates the process of software creation.


I'm the author of the Evolvable Framework. The framework models and arranges software around the individual user, his communications and memory, and his interaction with external things, space, actions, time, goals and intent, and conflict.

I have been on the net since the release of winsock. When I was a consultant for the University of Minnesota, I tested the first version of NCSA Mosaic, and thought the web was very compelling and accessible alternative to previous forms linkable documents. I've had a T1 at my fingertips for a very long time and I live on the net. It pervades everything I do.
