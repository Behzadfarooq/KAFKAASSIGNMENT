Awa Dance Festival

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

The Dance of Fools (in Koenji, Tokyo)

Awa Dance Festival (阿波踊り, Awa Odori?) is held every year in the time of Bon Odori (Bon festival) in the area of Tokushima city (徳島市; -shi), the capital city of Tokushima Prefecture on Shikoku island of Japan. Koenji, an area of Tokyo, also has an Awa Dance Festival modeled on Tokushima's, which started in 1956.

The dancers dance and walk while they follow an accompaniment such as shamisen, drum, brass, and flute. Dancers wear regional costumes and often sing while dancing. Men and women follow separate dance steps and wear different costumes, although most professional Awa Odori troupes tightly choreograph the movements of their male and female members together.

The dance troupe sings "Ya-to-sa" as they dance through the city streets. The lyrics to the Awa Dance festival song include the phrase "the dancing fool and the watching fool are both fools, so let's dance!"

[edit] History

Awa Odori is popularly believed to have begun in 1587. Lord Hachisuka Iemasa, the daimyo of Awa Province hosted a drunken celebration of the opening of Tokushima Castle. The locals, having consumed a great amount of sake, began to drunkenly weave and stumble back and forth. Others picked up commonly available musical instruments and began to play a simple, rhythmic song, to which the revelers invented lyrics, alternately praising Lord Hachisuka and encouraging others to join the dance.

While Awa Odori occurs at the same time as the Bon Festival, most do not consider Awa Odori as a Bon Odori.
