[edit] Attachment Therapy

Thanks for your message. I would be interested in introducing some clarity into the position. I was only reading around attachment because a colleague is doing a PHD in adult attachment. As an outside reader I was struck by the defensive attitude and consequent lack of focus of the AT page. It makes the antennae tingle! AT is obviously a fringe thing, but still quite an important one I think given what these people are capable of doing. The Wikipedia article ought to give enough information so that people come away with a clear idea of exactly what it is and what it is not. At the moment, the main focus of the article seems to be to protect DDP. Surely unnecessary? Hughes makes it very clear that he has developed his therapy away from any previous notions of coercion. At the moment I am working my way through the list of therapies on the ACT page that they say are AT 'by another name'. Does this seem a sensible approach?Fainites 21:11, 13 March 2007 (UTC)
