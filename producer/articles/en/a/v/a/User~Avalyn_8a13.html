Namaste! I am, as the title of this page suggests, Avalyn. No more, no less. I prefer complete androgyny and null identity online; that way nobody knows you're a dog, or a fruitafossor, for that matter. I don't particularly care for gender-neutral pronouns, so feel free to use whichever one you prefer when addressing me, though what you choose may conflict with my gender identity, but don't worry, I'm used to it and it doesn't bother me (much).

I can also be found at Everything2, where I am an administrator[1], or, in E2-speak, a "god."

I live in New Orleans, and I was present during Hurricane Katrina, which I watched destroy the downtown area from the tenth-floor window of the Poydras Centre building, where my then-employer maintains a datacenter and colocation facility. I posted my account of the event at Everything2 because it has no place here, of course.

I've lived in Detroit, Michigan (1976-98), where I was born, Waltham, Massachusetts (for part of the summer of 1998), Brooklyn, New York (1998-99, I lived on a friend's floor, unable to find a job), New York, New York (1999, after being *ahem* asked to leave, a friend at NYU allowed me to crash at her place on Water Street in downtown NYC for a week), South River, New Jersey (1999-2000, I moved to South River after I found a job in Red Bank), Nashville, Tennessee (2003-04, for no particular reason other than it seemed like a good idea at the time), and New Orleans (2000-03, 2004-present, and my favourite city I've lived in thus far). If I'd paid attention in high school and what few community college classes I attended, I'd probably have a PharmD degree by now. (Which is odd because as a teen and in my twenties I had no idea what I wanted to be when I grew up; I became interested in pharmacology only after my various mental disorders manifested. So I suppose if I ever go back to school, it'll be to get a PharmD.) Oh well. I blame my mental disorders and my will to spend my teens and twenties having fun instead of remaining in school.

I keep three computers at home, for doing various things. They are, by name, purple (a desktop computer I built myself running Microsoft Windows XP Professional 64-bit Edition), orange (an HP ze2117 laptop running Microsoft Windows XP Professional SP2), and saffron, a Mac mini (a second generation mini running Mac OS X.4.8 (Tiger). I also built an AMD AthlonXP 2400-based box and donated it to Everything2, where it serves as a webserver.

Just in case you can't get enough of little old me, I maintain a LiveJournal, too. (Several, in fact, if you count the LJ communities I maintain, which, as of 1 May 2006, number six in all.)

I played World of Warcraft for about six months (November 2005 through early May 2006), but shortly after bringing my only character[2] up to level 60, I lost interest in the game altogether. In September of that same year, I started playing again. I'm a member[3] of the guild Twilight Euphoria on the Kirin Tor (US) server.

As of March 2007, I work at the Northrop Grumman campus south of New Orleans on a four-month contract to decommission old desktop computers and deploy new computers in their place.

If you feel an urgent need to contact me, leave me a message and I'll get back to you when it's convenient for me to do so.

I have a minor eccentricity in which, when editing, I become agitated whenever I see two spaces following a period. Like most other people here, I learned to type on a typewriter, but none of its now-obsolete legacies affect me in any way. Chances are, if I see two spaces while I'm copyediting here, I'll delete one of them.

Anyway, please remember, this is just a website, so have fun! Edit wars are teh suck.

[edit] Current drug roster

Thanks to the wonderful genes in my mother's side of the family (plus attention deficit disorder, carpal tunnel syndrome and seasonal arthritis from my father's family), I have a number of mental disorders, and thus I have to take a veritable feast of prescription medication every day. My current drug roster is a follows:

Adderall® 30mg/day (2005-present)

  • For treating attention deficit disorder without hyperactivity.

Ibuprofen 800mg/day as needed (2006-present)

  • For minor relief of major back pain.

Lexapro® 80mg/day (morning and mid-day) (2004-present)

  • For treating depression, dysthymia and agoraphobia related to an inhereted depressive gene and gender dysphoria. My twice-daily dose of 40mg (for a daily total of 80mg) is an unusually high dose; without it, however, I'm given to suicidal ideation.

Lunesta® 6mg/day (evening)

  • For treating chronic, lifelong insomnia.

Lyrica® 300mg/day (evening)

  • For treating panic disorder and insomnia.

Seroquel® 800mg/day (morning and evening)

  • For treating post-traumatic stress disorder related to Hurricane Katrina. I use it as a sedative, too, even though it's technically an antipsychotic. When combined with Lunesta, it's very effective at putting me to sleep and keeping me asleep for at least eight hours. Seroquel is tested on animals; I'm disgusted by this, but at the same time I feel I have no choice but to use this drug until a cruelty-free alternative becomes available.

Xanax® 3mg/day (morning, afternoon, evening, or as needed when panic symptoms manifest)

  • For treating panic disorder and agoraphobia; it lessens the likelyhood of having a panic attack, and if I feel panic symptoms, I can take one or two of these and calm down.

Supplements (non-prescription, all taken daily):

  • Natrol® 5-HTP 50mg
  • Nature Made® Zinc 60mg
  • Source Naturals® GABA Calm™ 50mg sublinguals
  • Spectrum® ground flax seeds with oatmeal daily or every day or other day.
  • Sundown® potassium gluconate
  • VegLife® Vegan Multiple One multivitamin
  • Vitamin Shoppe® GABA 750mg

[edit] Previous drugs

Most of my conditions were diagnosed in 2001 or later, and so I thought it'd be fun to keep track of what I've been prescribed since then. I've also listed a very small number of drugs prescribed to me prior to 2001, mostly because they're the only ones I can remember the names of and/or the impact they had on my pre-adult life.

[edit] Antibiotics

Minocycline 100mg (2006)

  • For treating an infection brought on by a spider bite. This was a two-week antibiotic course. Much to my chagrin, these were gelatin capsuls, which are abhorrent to me as a vegan, so I had to puncture them to get the mediciation out. This was a two-week course that I finished on April 28, 2006.

Zithromax® 250mg (2002)

  • For treating lymph nodes under my jawbone.

[edit] Anti-ADHD stimulants

Bupropion SR 150mg/day (2006)

  • Combined with Citalopram, it was supposed to help combat the effects of ADHD. It didn't. The only thing it did for me was give me night sweats that smelled like rancid Méxican food. OKTHXBI!

Citalopram 40mg/day (2006)

  • My new SSRI after my previous SSRI, Lexapro, plateaued. Combined with bupropion, it is said to help resolve concentration difficulties. It was a liar, however, and in October 2006, I requested my meds for this be changed back to Adderall and Lexapro. The request was granted.

Ritalin® 10mg (1986-1988)

  • For treating attention deficit disorder without hyperactivity. I was one of Ritalin's early adopters; a psychologist prescribed it to me in 1986, when I was ten years old. Contrary to its intended effects, it didn't help my concentration or school work, and as each dose wore off I would enter a period of deep depression, which lasted until my next dose. I was taken off Ritalin in 1988, at age 12, when it was decided by my parents that the depressive bouts were so severe as to be disabling.

Strattera® 40mg (2005)

  • A non-narcotic drug used to treat attention deficit disorder without hyperactivity. After about two months it stopped working.

[edit] Antidepressants

Depakote® 125mg (2002)

  • For treating bipolar disorder. I was misdiagnosed as bipolar and used Depakote for only two weeks.

Paxil® 40mg (2001-2002)

  • For treating dysthymia and depression. I stopped taking it after it plateaued on me. Coming off of it gave me vertigo for a month.

Remeron® 30mg (2003)

  • For treating dysthymia and depression. Stopped taking it after only two weeks because it made me extremely irritable. So far it's the only tricyclic antidepressant I've tried.

Zoloft® 50mg (2003-2004)

  • For treating dysthymia and depression. It also plateaued, but before then I considered it one of the gentler SSRIs.

[edit] Antihistamines

Allegra® 25mg (2001-2002)

  • For treating "seasonal" allergies, which for me last all year, every year.

Atarax® 40mg (2005)

  • For treating a really bad case of rhinitis. My pharmacist recommended this drug to me not only on the basis of its antihistamine properties, but also because he said it was a very strong sedative and, when he gave some to his father, his father slept for two days straight. The antihistamine effect is nice, but, like most sedatives, this one didn't have any effect on me other than causing strong to severe nightmares.

Meclizine 50mg (2002)

  • For treating the vertigo that came with SSRI discontinuation syndrome after I stopped using Paxil. It's basically a stronger version of Dramamine, a popular, over-the-counter anti-motion sickness drug. It helped calm the vertigo, but oddly enough, I've otherwise never suffered from motion sickness of any kind.

[edit] Anti-convulsants/Benzodiazepines

Ativan® 0.5mg (2003-2004)

  • For treating panic disorder.

Gabitril® 12mg (2004-2006)

  • I was prescribed Gabitril, a nonbenzodiazepine anti-convulsant drug, to treat panic disorder with seizures (panic attacks), but using it regularly made it very hard to think or speak, and I would get strange little shock feelings in my extremities when it took effect.

Halcion® 0.5mg (2004)

  • A dentist who was also a TMJ specialist prescribed this to me to help me sleep when my TMJ pain was at its worst.

Klonopin® 2mg (2001-2003)

  • For treating panic disorder.

Neurontin® 300mg/day (2006)

  • For treating panic disorder.

[edit] Beta-antagonists

Propranolol 25mg (2002)

  • My psychiatrist prescribed me propranolol as a sort of experiment, to see if reducing my blood pressure slightly would lessen the effects of the frequent panic attacks I was suffering at the time. I think I took it only for about one month, i.e., the length of a single prescription, at the conclusion of which I told the doctor that it wasn't helping. And that was that.

[edit] Hormones

Diethylstilbestrol (DES) 10mg (2001-2002)

  • A synthetic estrogen, I took this daily during my (in retrospect) ill-advised attempt at changing my gender. Despite it not being found in most clinical descriptions of this drug, it has some noticable anti-androgen properties.

Progesterone 100mg (2001-2002)

  • Progesterone is a female sex hormone that is in its highest abundance in the body during pregnancy; it's also the main ingredient in birth control pills. I used it to nurture my growing breasts. Argh. I could cry just thinking about those times, now.

[edit] Hypnotics (imidazopyridines/cyclopryrrolones/tetracyclics)

Ambien® 10mg (2002-2006)

  • An imidazopyridine used for treating insomnia. My insomnia is chronic and life-long. I became addicted to Ambien in early 2003, after approximately seven months of using it as intended, and would often consume a month's worth of it in three or four days, which is why I chose to stop taking it, after a long weekend I spent either asleep or in a drug-induced stupor, during which I did a number of extremely stupid, very dangerous things (driving, shopping, the Ambien equivalent of drunk dialing, etc). Strange how I spent three years addicted to a drug that was approved for only short-term treatment (i.e., 10-30 days) of insomnia. Coincidentally, an uncle of mine committed suicide in 2004 by intentionally overdosing on Ambien; he took thirty pills (30mg, the combined amount of one month's worth) at once, and the drug, in such a quantity, did indeed make him sleep. My family isn't counting on him ever waking up, though.

Ambien CR® 12.5mg/day (evening) (2006)

  • I tried this new formulation of Ambien to see whether or not I get addicted to it like I did with its predecessor (Ambien). On the plus side, it doesn't have any of the more notorious effects that regular Ambien has, but the problem is I had to take a double or triple dose to get to sleep. Compared to the LSD-like effects of its predecessor, Ambien CR is like a walk in the park.

Trazodone 25mg (2001)

  • A tetracyclic antidepressant that's sometimes used off-label, allegedly for treating insomnia. It's a terrible, loathesome drug. My mind entered an impenetrable fog after dosing, and the rest of the side-effects seemed equally disgusting to me. Whole-body dysphoria, slurred speech, priapism (ugh!), balance regulation difficulities (viz., "I've fallen, and I can't get up!"), and nightmares when sleep finally came.

[edit] Muscle relaxants

Flexeril® 5mg/as needed (2006)

  • For the treatment of lower back pain caused by a strained lumbar muscle.

Norflex® 100mg/as needed (2006)

  • A skeletal muscle relaxant, prescribed for treating a lumbar strain, suffered in June 2006.

Skelaxin® 800mg/as needed (2006)

  • For treating muscle pain in my neck, shoulders, arms and hands, possibly due to a combination of carpal tunnel syndrome and temporomandibular joint disorder.

[edit] Painkillers

Clinoral® 200mg (2006)

  • For treating a severe, extremely painful lumbar strain, suffered in June 2006. This drug is an NSAID.

Darvocet/APAP® 100/650mg (2006)

  • A weak narcotic opioid, prescribed for treating the bite of an unknown species of spider on my left hand, suffered in my sleep on April 16, 2006. I was wearing a splint glove because I have carpal tunnel syndrome, and the spider was most likely hiding inside the glove before I put it on. Disaster, of course, ensued. This prescription was a 3-day course that I finished on April 20, 2006.

Fioricet/APAP/Caffeine 40mg (2001)

  • For treating moderate-to-severe tension headaches.

Lodine® 300mg/day (as needed) (2006)

  • An NSAID for treating lower back pain.

Percocet®/APAP 5/325mg (2006)

  • An opioid painkiller prescribed for treating the aforementioned lumbar strain, suffered in June 2006.

Ultram® 50mg/day (2006-2007)

  • For treating pain caused by what now seems to be chronic lower back pain or fibromyalgia, the symptoms of which I'm also exhibiting.

Vicodin/APAP® 5/500mg (2001, 2006)

  • First prescribed after I had a tooth extracted from my mouth in 2001. In 2006, I had Vicodin (or maybe Vicoprofen; I can't recall which exactly) prescribed three separate times by two separate doctors. First, a neurologist prescribed it to me twice, about two months apart, for treating muscle pain in my neck, shoulders, arms and hands, caused by carpal tunnel syndrome. A second prescription was given to me by a dentist after I broke three teeth (tooth numbers one, two and three on the upper right side; two and three were broken down to about 30% of their previous length and the other tooth was split nearly in half, vertically) by walking into a doorframe while sleepwalking (which, to the best of my recollection, I'd never done before and have never done since) in early July 2006. Unfortunately, the total cost to get these teeth root canaled and crowned is approximately $4,500 USD, but my incompetent insurance provider[4] will cover no more than $1,000 USD per annum to pay for denistry, so I'm stuck with a broken smile indefinitely.

Vioxx® 25mg (2004)

  • Prescribed by the same dentist/TMJD specialist that prescribed me Halcion. I used the Vioxx during the day when it would've been impractical to take a strong hypnotic like Halcion. And no, I wasn't involved in the class action lawsuit against Merck, the drug's manufacturer, nor did I experience any of the side-effects for which Vioxx was withdrawn from the pharmaceutical market (viz., I didn't have a heart attack).

[edit] Trippin'

In my late teens (1994-96, ages 17 to 20), before spending most of my twenties on various prescription drugs, I frequently used LSD in blotter form; my best guess as to how many times is roughly 50. The last time I had it was in 1996, at age 20. Also, I am either allergic to marijuana, as I've had a number of strongly negative reactions to it, or my negative reactions were caused by some unknown agent, maybe meth or PCP, laced in pre-prepared packets (read: dimebags) I'd purchased. (See my talk page for more info.) Could go either way, really.

In my early-to-mid 20s, I used MDMA about half a dozen times (at times when I wasn't on any serotonin-effecting drugs like SSRIs), ketamine once, and I've experimented (unsuccessfully) with salvia divinorum on a number of occasions, although I never reached the "spirit journey" reported by some users.

Sometimes I pine for my drug-using days, but it's usually limited to simple nostalgia, since I had a few rather bad times then, so it's not all rose-coloured.

[edit] Public service announcement

I think that the song "American Pie" by Don McLean is the absolute worst song, out of all musical genres, that I've ever had the extreme displeasure of hearing. The lyrics are so awful and the hooks are so infectious, that once I hear even a tiny snippet, the whole song gets stuck in my head for days. Other than having a CD player installed in my car, this song is the greatest influence on my decision to never, ever listen to FM radio, if I can avoid it. Don't even get me started about "Weird Al" Yankovic's Star Wars-themed parody/homage to it; I consider it every bit as bad as the original.
