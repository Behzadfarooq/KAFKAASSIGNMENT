Avanti! is a 1972 comedy directed by Billy Wilder, starring Jack Lemmon, Juliet Mills, Clive Revill, Edward Andrews and Gianfranco Barra.

It was adapted by I.A.L. Diamond from a 1968 play by Samuel Taylor, and is probably one of the least famous movies of the tandem Wilder-Diamonds. It is a comedy about the behaviour of the Italians, the American executives and the average Englishwomen of the 1970s, full of surprises and subtle dialogs. As in One, Two, Three, the contrasts between people coming from different cultures are very intelligent, and everybody is laughed at.

[edit] Story

Wendell (Lemmon) goes to Italy to pick up his father's body after an automobile accident. He soon finds that his self righteous elderly father had been having an affair with Pamela's (Mills) mother for the past 10 years and were known as the model romantic couple at the hotel.

Although the story is supposed to take place in Ischia (a small island in the Gulf of Naples) the movie was mainly made in Sorrento, especially at the Grand Hotel Excelsior.

In the movie it is mentioned that Wendell is 42...he was actually 47 in real life. Pamela was 31 in real life in 1972. Juliet Mills is the older sister of Hayley. Pamela (Juliet) appears nude in the movie as does Wendell. At the end of the movie, look at the underside of the helicopter which is marked U.S. Navy and you will see an emblem which reads Italian Air Force.
