"Avalanche" is the 5th episode of the Supermarionation television series Captain Scarlet and the Mysterons. It was the 11th episode to be produced. Its original UK air date was October 27, 1967 on ATV Midlands. It was written by Shane Rimmer and directed by Brian Burgess.

[edit] Synopsis

Spoiler warning: Plot and/or ending details follow.

The Mysterons kill and reconstruct a maintenance man to cripple the Frost Line Outer Space Defence System. The first strike results in the deaths of all personnel at Red Deer Base and Captain Scarlet and Lieutenant Green are assigned to investigate. As a second outpost is knocked out, will the Spectrum officers uncover the method behind the attacks before General Ward starts an interplanetary war with Mars?

[edit] Plot

At the Command Centre of the Frost Line Outer Space Defence System, a 3,000-mile stretch of missile complexes, observation posts and feeder stations located in North America, officers discuss when a maintenance truck will arrive to service the air conditioning and humidity. Eddie, the truck driver, is making his way through the snow at that moment. However, he is unable to brake quickly enough when he spots a large branch obstructing the road and his vehicle crashes down the hillside. Up to his chest in the snow, Eddie is helpless as a flow of the material completely covers him. The Mysteron rings pan over the scene and the reconstructed truck appears with Eddie's duplicate at the wheel.

Transmitting to Earth, the Mysterons announce that they will destroy key links in the Frost Line. On Cloudbase, Colonel White is contacted by General Ward of the Command Centre, who arrogantly dismisses Spectrum's tactics for countering the Mysterons in favour of his own methods. White feels uneasy of the General's underestimation of the aliens.

The reconstructed Eddie arrives at Red Deer Base. He shoots Joe, the sentry, and crashes the barrier, speeding into the complex. An distress signal reaches the Command Centre and personnel find that they cannot get a radio response from Red Deer. It is assumed that the base's 250 staff have been killed and General Ward calls Cloudbase. White promises to investigate but Ward angrily demands military retaliation. The Spectrum Angels are launched and White assigns Captain Scarlet and Lieutenant Green to explore Red Deer and ascertain the cause of the deaths. As the officers fly out in a Spectrum Jet, Green is looking forward to finally seeing some action.

Destiny Angel overflies Red Deer and reports that there is no sign of activity. Scarlet and Green land and don respirator masks before entering the complex through an airlock. Inside, they discover the base's personnel dead with no signs of external injury. A strange mist is hovering above the ground but radiation and toxicity checks read negative.

Meanwhile, Eddie reaches the next base, Cariboo, and presents his pass to the sentry. He drops a capsule into the ventilation shaft inside the service room. Moments later, an operator at Cariboo informs the Command Centre that he cannot breathe before dying at his controls. A further 70 men have now been killed and General Ward places all bases on high alert. He radios Colonel White, threatening to launch missiles against Mars if another station is knocked out.

At Red Deer, Scarlet and Green are yet to find a cause for the deaths of the personnel. Green attempts to remove his respirator but finds that he cannot breathe. Scarlet enquires whether they have tested for oxygen and Green discovers that there is none present in the air. Scarlet informs White that the men have suffocated and the Colonel reports that Destiny Angel has sighted the wreck of a maintenance truck identical to the one that called at Red Deer and Cariboo. He orders the officers to stop the vehicle before it reaches Big Bear Base.

Scarlet and Green requisition an SPV from a nearby hunter and take a short cut to catch up with Eddie. Meanwhile, the Command Centre is locking out Cloudbase's calls and White realises that Ward has definitely decided to retaliate. Scarlet and Green arrive at Big Bear to find that the truck has already come and gone. Wary of Spectrum's presence, the oblivious sentry tells them that Eddie is heading for the Command Centre. Green knocks out the guard and searches the service room for devices responsible for the oxygen loss while Scarlet sets off in the SPV to stop the truck.

Eddie is only one mile from the Command Centre. Green radios Scarlet to report that he has removed the capsule from the shaft. The reaction was only just starting and the base personnel are safe. Eddie spots the SPV behind him and sprays slippery liquid oxygen onto the road, causing Scarlet to crash. The Captain is barely injured but realises that the truck is a time bomb.

As Eddie nears the Command Centre, Scarlet shoots at the snow beside the road and watches as the resulting avalanche completely blocks the truck's path. The vehicle is hit by a rockfall and rolls down the hillside, exploding at the base of the mountain.

On Cloudbase, White commends Scarlet and Green. A call comes through from the Command Centre and Scarlet assumes that it is General Ward, wanting to congratulate Spectrum. White replies that they are not in the organisation to be congratulated and opens the radio channel. Ward believes that the Mysterons were driven away by his ferocity and pompously bids the Colonel farewell. As White mutters that Ward is pig-headed and trigger-happy, Scarlet reminds him that they are not in Spectrum to be congratulated.
