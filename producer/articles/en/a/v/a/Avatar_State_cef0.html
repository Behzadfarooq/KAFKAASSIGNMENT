The Avatar State is a defense mechanism that only the Avatar of the American animated television series Avatar: The Last Airbender can utilize. In the Avatar State, all the knowledge and experience of the former Avatars are channeled into the current one. Even though the Avatar is near-invincible in this state, should he or she die in this state, the Avatar cycle will be broken and the Avatar will cease to exist.[1]

Aang mainly assumes this state, as the primary and current incarnation of the Avatar in the series. It was the Avatar State that kept him alive within an iceberg for a century.

[edit] Background

Initially, Aang (or presumably any Avatar) triggers the Avatar State when he is in potentially fatal physical danger or under extreme emotional stress, usually prompted by a major injury to his friends or other people for whom he cares deeply. Through this, the Avatar sends a kind a psychic distress signal to his spirit, to which all the past Avatars respond by augmenting his strength and power.

In the episode "The Guru," Aang traveled to the Eastern Air Temple to attempt to master the Avatar State under the guidance of Guru Pathik. He was to do this by opening the seven chakras, or pools of spiraling energy, within him. Once opened, not only would his judgment and disposition be clearer, but Aang would also gain complete control of the Avatar State, entering and exiting it at will and remaining conscious of his actions while in it. Guru Pathik warned, however, that to interrupt the process of purging his chakras before all seven have opened would mean that Aang would not be able to enter the Avatar State at all. Aang was also warned by Avatar Roku that, while he has untold power of all the previous Avatars in this state, if he is killed in this state, the Avatar cycle will be broken and the Avatar will cease to exist.

Aang attempting to master the Avatar state.

In "The Guru," Aang refuses to unlock his final chakra, the Thought Chakra, because it would require spiritually letting go of Katara as an earthly attachment in light of the grand scheme of the cosmos. During his metaphoric journey to reach mastery of the Avatar State, Aang has a spiritual vision of Katara in danger, which prompts him to return from his meditative state and go rescue her. In "The Crossroads of Destiny," Aang attempts once more to master the Avatar State by sorrowfully letting go of Katara. Aang didn't succeed in controlling it wholly, as Azula shot Aang down with a lightningbolt just after he entered the state.[2]
