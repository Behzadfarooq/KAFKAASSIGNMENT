Avalon Mall

The Avalon Mall is the largest shopping centre in St. John's, Newfoundland and Labrador, Canada. It offers over 140 stores and services, movie theatres, a children's play area, and the largest food court in Atlantic Canada. The mall opened in 1967 and is owned by Crombie REIT, a real estate investment trust controlled by the Sobey family. The mall had major extensions in 1977, 1987 and 1999.

The mall once contained a major local department store, Ayre and Sons, which declared bankruptcy in 1991; now occupied by The Gap, Scotiabank and a few tenants. Also, Woolco's sales slipped below its mall rent. Such low retail performance has concerned mall management and prompted managemant to embark on major renovation programs during the 1980s and 1990s. The mall also has a semi-dated/semi-renovated appearance. Despite these problems, the mall still generates sales above the national retail industry average.

Anchor tenants include Sears (which opened in fall 2005 in the location previously occupied by Wal-Mart and Woolco) and Empire Theatres. A combined Winners / HomeSense location opened in spring 2006, replacing Sobeys and a few other first-floor merchants. Other notable tenants include HMV, Gap, Eddie Bauer and Lawtons.

The Avalon Mall is considered "The Mall" in Newfoundland and Labrador and is a popular tourist destination, particularly among residents of the numerous small rural communities in eastern Newfoundland. While there are other malls in St. John's and elsewhere, most are much smaller. Moreover, consolidation and the rise of the power centre have meant that other St. John's malls have approached dead mall status. For instance, the Avalon Mall's largest rival, The Village Shopping Centre, has only recently filled space formerly occupied by another Wal-Mart location, while Sears may elect to close its older Village location once its lease there expires. Several malls, including The Village, have leased large chunks of space to call centres. Meanwhile, the Avalon Mall remains almost fully occupied by retail tenants.

The mall also contains underground and ground parkade accessed from O'Leary Avenue. There is a pedestrian overpass which leads to the mall from the ground parkade on O'Leary Avenue.

The Avalon Mall is located at 48 Kenmount Road, at the intersection with Prince Philip Drive, and is the focal point of a large strip of retail outlets, with a nearby power centre which now houses the above-noted Wal-Mart and Sobeys.

The Avalon Mall is currently undergoing some remodeling for a new modern look. One major change is the new Food Court and back entrance, as well as a new Metrobus stop location. The "Hub" area of the mall is being worked on at the present time.
