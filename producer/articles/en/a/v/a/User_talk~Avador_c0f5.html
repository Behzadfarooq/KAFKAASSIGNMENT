[edit] Re: Kingpriest of Istar copyright Vio

I do understand your concern. When I checked the article, I checked both the Fair use article and the fair use guidelines of Wikipedia. According to the guidelines:

Brief, attributed quotations of copyrighted text used to illustrate a point, establish context, or attribute a point of view or idea may be used under fair use. Text must be used verbatim: any alterations must be clearly marked as an elipsis ([...]) or insertion ([added text]) or change of emphasis (emphasis added). All copyrighted text must be attributed. In general, extensive quotation of copyrighted news materials (such as newspapers and wire services), movie scripts, or any other copyrighted text is not fair use and is prohibited by Wikipedia policy.

So, on one side brief, attributed quotations of copyrighted text to establish context is fair use. On the other side, extensive quotation of copyrighted text is prohibited. It is a matter of determining whether that quote was extensive or brief. Personally, I believe a paragraph in a 300 pages book isn't exactly extensive. Note that different articles quote text, in example, the full verse of the One Ring, the speech about mercy in the Fellowship of the Ring. It is up to interpretation, of course, and would be nice to hear some voices.

It seems you forgot to add the article into the list of articles with copyright problems. I have added it now.

Thanks for your input in this matter. -- ReyBrujo 02:11, 27 February 2006 (UTC)
