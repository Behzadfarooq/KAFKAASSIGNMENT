Avatar Press

Avatar Press is an independent US comic books publisher, founded in 1997 by William Christiansen, and based in Urbana, Illinois. Avatar mainly publishes mini-series. Their only ongoing book is the anthology book "Threshold" which comes out about six times in a year and features about 5 separate stories in it.

It is most notable for their publishing of "Bad Girl" comics such as Pandora, who is considered by many to be their flagship character. Other bad girl books include "Hellina", "Lookers" and "the Ravening". As part of an effort to expand beyond their reputation as a bad girl publisher, it offered a number of noted creators an opportunity to publish creator-owned books with no content restrictions whatsoever. Among the creators to take them up on their offer was Frank Miller, Warren Ellis, Alan Moore and Garth Ennis. Lately, the company has taken to licensed comics book adaptations of famous sci-fi and horror shows from movies and television, such as RoboCop, Stargate SG-1, or Friday the 13th.

Avatar Press is also known and criticized for their publishing of multiple variant covers for one issue. These variants include premium, wraparound, nude, leather, commemorative and foil covers. Most notable is Brian Pulido's Lady Death and Medieval Lady Death comics which receive about 15 variant covers/issue, although these are not released all at once. They continue to release more covers even after the issue has been published, labelling some as commemorative covers so as to raise the cover price. Many people have commented that if the publisher put less focus on covers they could publish more original works.

[edit] Overview

[edit] Bad Girl

Notable Bad Girl comics published at Avatar are:

  • Pandora (Avatar's flagship character)
  • Hellina
  • William Tucci's Shi
  • The Ravening
  • Lookers
  • Mike Deodato's Jade Warriors
  • Rob Liefeld's Avengelyne, Glory and The Coven
  • Marat Mychael's Demonslayer
  • Tim Vigil's Webwitch
  • Brian Pulido's Lady Death (of Chaos Comics fame), Belladonna, War Angel, Unholy and Gypsy
  • Everette Hartsoe's Razor

[edit] Adaptations

Notable adaptations performed by Avatar include:

Sci-fi series such as:

  • Frank Miller's RoboCop, based on his unused scripts for RoboCop 2 and RoboCop 3
  • Stargate, both Stargate SG-1 and Stargate Atlantis

Horror series such as:

  • Friday the 13th
  • A Nightmare on Elm Street
  • The Texas Chain Saw Massacre
