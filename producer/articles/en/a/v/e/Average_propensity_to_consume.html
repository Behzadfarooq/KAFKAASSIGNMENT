Average propensity to consume

Average propensity to consume (APC) is the percentage of income spent. To find the percentage of income spent, one needs to divide consumption by income, or . In an economy which each individual consumer saves lots of money, there is a tendency of people losing their jobs because demand for goods and services will be low. Thus saving as a leakage is an advantage from the savers' point of view, while for the economy as a whole it is a considerable disadvantage.

The inverse is the average propensity to save (APS).
