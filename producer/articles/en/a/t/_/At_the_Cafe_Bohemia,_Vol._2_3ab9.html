At the Cafe Bohemia, Vol. 2 is a 1955 live album release by jazz drummer Art Blakey. It was first released by Blue Note Records. This record featured the first incarnation of The Jazz Messengers, Blakey's most endearing band, and was the second of two volumes recorded at Cafe Bohemia, a famous night club in Greenwich Village in New York, New York on November 23, 1955.

[edit] Reception

This album, which sees the first version of The Jazz Messengers on record, was noted as not "match[ing] the intensity which the quintet secured at Birdland."[1] Tenor saxophonist Hank Mobley, in particular, is noted as "a somewhat unfocused stylist."[1] However, trumpeter Kenny Dorham is seen as an "elusive brillance [that] was seldom so extensively captured" and the playing in general "is just as absorbing" as the Birdland albums and has "influenced jazz up to present time."[1][2]
