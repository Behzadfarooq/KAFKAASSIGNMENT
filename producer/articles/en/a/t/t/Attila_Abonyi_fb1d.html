Attila Abonyi (born August 16, 1946[1]) is a former football player. Born in Hungary[2], he took up soccer after immigrating to Australia at age 10.[3] Attila played started playing professional club football for Melbourne Hungaria between 1962 and 1968, St. George-Budapest between 1969 and 1976 and Sydney Croatia between 1977 and 1979.

He is well known for being a member of the Australian 1974 World Cup squad in West Germany and also represented New South Wales and Victoria. He scored 36 international goals for Australia in 89 games between 1967 and 1977 making him the equal second highest goal scorer for Australia.

[edit] Coaching Record

  • 1978 - Sydney Croatia (NSW Div 1)
  • 1979 - Sydney Croatia (NSW Div 1)
  • 1980 - Melita Eagles (NSW Div 1)
  • 1981 - Riverwood (NSW Div 1)
  • 1982 - Riverwood (NSW Div 1)
  • 1983 - Canberra City NSL W7-D3-L8 (18)
  • 1984 - Sydney Croatia NSL W1-D3-L4 (8)
  • 1985 - Rockdale Ilinden (NSW Div 1)
  • 1986 - Rockdale Ilinden (NSW Div 1)
  • 1987 - St. George NSL Assistant Coach
  • 1988 - St. George NSL Assistant Coach
