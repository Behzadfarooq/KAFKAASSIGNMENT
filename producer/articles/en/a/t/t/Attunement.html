Attunement

Attunement is a process, similar to synchronization, wherein previously diffuse systems come into alignment, often spontaneously. It is therefore distinct from synchronized dancing, swimming, or other human aesthetic activities that are preplanned, practiced and then performed.

[edit] Attunement as Synchrony

Attunement can easily be seen as synonymous with the concept of synchronization but with the added stipulation that it be a natural tendency. Both involve at least two separate things moving, operating, working, etc., at the same rate and exactly together.

[edit] In Action

Technology and medicine depend on attunement. In the human heart, there are 10,000 pacemaker cells that trigger the rest of the heart to beat properly, and each those 10,000 cells could have its own rhythm, but they don't. They communicate by sending electrical currents back and forth to each other, but at an abstract level they're the same as many other phenomena that synchronize. Without proper attunement of our pacemaker cells, our cardiovascular system could not function. They are individual oscillators that need to have a periodic and synchronized repetition of their state. They do so in such a way that all come into sync. Of course, sometimes attunement is undesirable. If it occurs in the brain on a vast scale it results in epilepsy.

The laser, one of the most practical inventions of our time, depends on attuned light waves: atoms pulsing in unison, all emitting light of the same color and moving in phase, with all the troughs and crests of the light waves perfectly lined up. The light in a laser is no different from the light coming out of these bulbs overhead in that the atoms are not really that different; they're just attuned.[1]

[edit] Historical Overview

The understanding of the phenomena of attunement begins in 1665 with the Dutch scientist, Christiaan Huygens. While ill he noticed that the two pendulum clocks on his wall (which he had invented and perfected earlier in his life) always swung so that each pendulum was precisely opposite the one another; even if he intervened and disturbed the rhythm the pendulums always synced back up the same way. He described this phenomena as the "sympathy of two clocks."

In the 1700s Jean-Jacques Dortous de Mairan discovered the circadian cycling of plants on a 24-hour schedule that corresponded to days and nights even when no light was present.[2]

For more on the history of attunement/spontaneous synchronization see Bryan C. Daniels' research paper available here: http://physics.owu.edu/StudentResearch/2005/BryanDaniels/index.html

[edit] Synchronization in Nature

  • Circadian rhythms—Many living organisms synchronize to the day-night cycle.
  • Electrical generators—All of the generators producing power on a power grid must be synchronized to one another.
  • Josephson junction arrays—Josephson junctions are fabricated, microscopic devices that behave as compact electronic oscillators, and different geometric arrangements of Josephson junctions form systems of oscillators that have been found theoretically toexhibit synchronization.
  • Heart, intestinal muscles-The muscles in your heart, for example, must all be synchronized to create a coherent beat.
  • Menstrual cycles-It has been found that the menstrual cycles of women who live together often synchronize..
  • Fireflies—Certain species of fireflies have been found to synchronize, flashing at the same time.
  • Applause—In Eastern Europe, it is customary to clap in synchrony after a good performance.[3]

[edit] In Relation to Entropy

Attunement is a counterpoint to entropy.

The same laws that give rise to entropy and the tendency of systems to become more and more disordered are the same laws that will account for synchrony. It's just that we don't have a clear enough understanding of the thermodynamics of systems very far from equilibrium to see the connection—but we're getting there. We're learning a lot about spontaneously synchronizing systems, at least in physics. We know how a laser works, and there's nothing that violates entropy about that. For living examples, like heart cells, we have a rough idea about how electrical currents are passed back and forth. But synchrony also touches on some of the deepest mysteries of our time, like consciousness. There's some thought, at least according to some neuroscientists, that what distinguishes consciousness from other forms of brain activity is the synchronized firing of the cells involved at specific frequencies close to 40 cycles a second.[4]

[edit] Attunement in the Mind

Mental attunement occurs when people's conceptual understandings are in sync.

[edit] Theoretical Possibilities for Education

Education is an area where attunement on a mental level can be seen to take place. A teacher's job is to synchronize their student's understandings of the world with the epistemology of the society in which they live. This concept needs to be researched but the theoretical framework has been laid by James G. Greeno in his Journal for Research in Mathematics Education article entitled, "Number Sense as Situated Knowing in a Conceptual Domain." In the article Greeno draws a connection between conceptual domains regarding abstract notions of number ("number sense") that a math-teacher attempts to encourage in a child and conceptual domains regarding environment. Greeno indicates that in the same way two people trying to give street directions must be attuned to the same conceptual domain (directions for Buffalo, New York are worthless in Buffalo, Georgia), a child's abstract understanding of number needs to be attuned to the conceptual domain of the teacher. The learning process can therefore be seen as a process of attunement. The possibility of new attitudes and approaches in education theory/practice that incorporate a broader understanding of how children attune is an exciting frontier in the realm of education.[5]
