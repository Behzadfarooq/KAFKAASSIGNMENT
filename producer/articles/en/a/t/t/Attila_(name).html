Attila (name)

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

For other uses of "Attila", see Attila (disambiguation).

Attila is a popular name for boys in both Hungary and Turkey. Another version of Attila in Hungary is Etele,[1] the female equivalent of which is Etelka. Other versions of Attila used in Turkey are Atilla and Atila.

[edit] Etymology

The word possibly originates from Turkic Atyl/Atal (ancient name of Volga river) with adjective suffix -ly. (Compare also Turkic medieval notable title atalyk - "senior as father").[2][3][4] Alternative version claims possibility of origin from Gothic atte, literally "a father".
