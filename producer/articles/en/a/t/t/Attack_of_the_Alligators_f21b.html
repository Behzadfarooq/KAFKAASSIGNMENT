"Attack of the Alligators!" is the 23rd episode of the first series of the Supermarionation television series Thunderbirds. It was the 24th episode to be produced. Its original air date on ATV Midlands in the United Kingdom was 10 March 1966. It was written by Alan Pattillo and directed by David Lane.

[edit] Synopsis

Spoiler warning: Plot and/or ending details follow.

Dr. Orchard is a scientist who lives near the Everglades. The portion of the Everglades near his home contain several alligators. One day, a businessman named Blackmer comes to visit Dr. Orchard. However, during his stay, a thunderstorm arrives, causing him to stay overnight. Blackmer shows the doctor a new growth hormone called theromene. Theromene causes the subjected animal to grow ten times its original size. That night, when they go to sleep, Culp steals the keys from Dr. Orchard's housekeeper, Mrs. Files. Using the keys, he goes down to the doctor's laboratory and finds the theromene. Unfortunately, he accidentely knocks it over into a nearby sink. However, to everyone's dismay, the contents of the sink drain out into the Everglades. The next day, Blackmer leaves with Culp for the Junction, but on their way they are attacked by three alligators who, thanks to theromene, are ten times their original size. The alligators then proceed to Dr. Orchard's house, trapping the doctor and company inside. In an attempt to get at the people inside, the alligators begin to ram the door. McGill calls International Rescue, and the first part of the episode ends there.

The next part John receives the call and nobody can believe such things as giant alligators exist, but they still fly off to the far side of the Everglades. When Scott arrives he gets on his Hoverjet and scares the alligators away from the house. He meets them down in the laboratory but the alligators start thrashing, and caving it in, so they escape upstairs. Then Thunderbird 2 arrives with Virgil, Alan and Gordon, who fire Tranqulizer darts at two of the alligators who are then killed. Unluckily, the third has slipped back to Doctor Orchard's house, where it starts attacking it again. Alan decides to get on his Hoverjet and chase the allligator away from there - the plan works and it follows him. However, Alan isn't concentrating and hits a branch and falls off his bike: thankfully Gordon witnesses it and fires at the alligator, which saves Alan. The danger isn't over yet, though, because there is another tube of growth hormone remaining in the swamp, so Gordon launches Thunderbird 4. Then he stumbles across a fourth alligator and Virgil fires a missle at it - the alligator hits Culp's boat and Culp killed - poetically hoisted by his own petard, so to speak. Gordon finds the last of the hormone, and Doctor Orchard, Blackmer McGill and Mrs Files are found safe and well. The episode ends with a scene set later in that afternoon, where Jeff tells the boys that Blackmer and Orchard are putting it under International control. As he is talking, Tin Tin arrives home with a present for Alan ...

[edit] Trivia

  • Five baby crocodiles (each measuring approximately three or four feet long) were used in the episode. This was the first ever episode in any of Gerry Anderson's puppet series where live animals were used as part of an episode.
  • The camera and camera operator can be seen reflected in the glass during the shot of Mrs Files at the window in the opening sequence.

[edit] Bloopers

  • Although in the dialogue Jeff clearly tells Virgil to take Thunderbird 2 with Pod 6, when Thunderbird 4 is launched later in the episode, the number '4' can just be seen on the Pod hatch, albeit from a very narrow angle. On all other occasions, Thunderbird 4 launches from Pod 4.
