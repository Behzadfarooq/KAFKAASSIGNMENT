Attoor ('at-toor) is a term used in Perfumery and to designate places in India and elsewhere.

[edit] Perfumery

Attoor is an Arabic term used to designate essential oils which may be extracted from the petals of various types of flowers, including roses, jasmines, and gardenias. The process is also done with oud, patchouli, sandalwood and many other types of musks and ambers. These essenses are extracted through steam distillation, while flower and other absolutes are obtained through solvent extraction or supercritical carbon dioxide extraction, with the absolute being used more commonly in perfumery or Attoor. Even with their high prices and the advent of organic synthesis, Attoor oils are still the most widely used essential oils in perfumery.

[edit] Places

The word attoor is a combination of two words: aru and ur. Aru means river, while ur is a common suffix for thousands of place names in India and elsewhere. Thus, Attoor usually designates a village by a river. The term Ur in Sumerian culture and in Jerusalem has the same roots.

The largest place called Attoor is located in India. It is a cultural village in the Thrissur District of Kerala, where Sanskrit scholar Attoor Krishna Pisharoty and Malayalam poet Attoor Ravi Varma were born. The Kartyayani Bhagavati Temple in Attoor is one of the oldest temples in Kerala. Here, Karthika, the festival of lights, is celebrated every year in the presence of worshipers from all parts of Kerala.
