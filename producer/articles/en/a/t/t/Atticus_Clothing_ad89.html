Atticus Clothing

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

A black Atticus shirt

Atticus is a brand of clothing founded in 2001 by Dylan Anderson along with Mark Hoppus and Tom DeLonge (of the pop-punk band blink-182) and Jon Humphrey.[1]

The company's name is derived from the main character of the novel To Kill a Mockingbird by Harper Lee, Atticus Finch, as well as Herodes Atticus, a Greek rhetorician who supported music and the arts.[2] The brand's women's line is called Atticus Scout, after another character from the novel. Resting Bird which is owned by Hoppus and DeLonge is also based on this theme. The company's logo is a dead bird laying upside down. It is believed to be a pictorial representation of the novel's title.

The company is heavily influenced by music and have released three volumes of their Dragging The Lake compilation CDs, featuring songs from bands such as New Found Glory, Bane, Jimmy Eat World, Taking Back Sunday, Death Cab for Cutie, and Rise Against.

According to an August 2006 interview with b182.com, Mark Hoppus is in the process of selling his share of Atticus, as well as Macbeth and LoserKids.com, at least partially due to his falling out with Tom Delonge.

"I have not been creatively involved in any of the companies in nearly a year. That is why you don’t see any photos of me or any mention of my name on the websites, catalogs, etc. We started the companies as a fun way to be creative and work with our friends, and over the past year and a half, it has stopped being fun. Personally, I do not care for the direction the companies are going in, or how they are being run, so Tom and the other owner are going their way with the companies, and I am going mine. I wish all the employees there the best of luck in the future. It is just not for me anymore."[3]
