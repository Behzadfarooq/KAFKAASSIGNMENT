Attadale is a suburb of Perth, Western Australia, located within the City of Melville.

Attadale is believed to be named after a village in Scotland near the Scottish town of Applecross.

The suburb is located between the central business district and Fremantle. It borders the southern side of the Swan River, with views of the city centre. Its foreshore has many open spaces for residents to walk themselves and/or their dogs.

On Attadale's border is a popular recreational reserve at Point Walter which has a jetty, restaurant and children's play areas as well as facilities for boating, fishing and picnics.
