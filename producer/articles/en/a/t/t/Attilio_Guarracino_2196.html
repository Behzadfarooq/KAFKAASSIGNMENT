Attilio Guarracino

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Attilio Guarracino (1931, Ischia, Campania), is an Italo-Australian migrant who became famous in 1952 for his act of heroism which saved several people during a storm.

[edit] Biography

Guarracino's early life was one of extreme poverty and small time criminality on the island of Ischia. In 1948 he was befriended by the American poet Chester Kallman. He later was also become friend of the Australian artist Jeffrey Smart and, soon after, of Donald Friend (1950) becoming his model and lover. In 1952 he agreed to accompany Friend to Australia, who arranged for him to become an Australian citizen. He briefly lived with him at Hill End, New South Wales before moving to Melbourne. There Guarracino was employed variously by the PMG, the Howard Florey Institute, as a professional fisherman and on the land before working as Friend's agent in Australia between 1968 and 1976. Donald Friend left half his estate to Guarracino on his death in 1989.

In 1952 he saved several people from drowning on Port Phillip Bay during a wild winter storm. He made front page news for his act of bravery and was chosen by the Italian government as migrant of the year. This earned him a free return trip back to Italy and a meeting with the Pope.

His portrait may be found in the New South Wales Gallery of Art, South Australian Art Gallery and the National Gallery of Victoria, amongst others.

He is married to Ailsa, with two children, and lives in Melbourne.
