The Attack class patrol boats were small coastal defence vessels built for the Royal Australian Navy and operated between 1967 and 1985. Many were eventually donated to the defence forces of Pacific nations.

Twenty boats were ordered by the Department of Defence in 1965 at a cost of around $AUD 800,000 each from two Queensland shipyards, Evans Deakin in Brisbane and Walkers in Maryborough.

The Attack class was replaced in RAN service by the larger and more capable Fremantle class patrol boats.

[edit] In fiction

Two Attack class boats represented the fictional HMAS Ambush in the first series of the Australian Broadcasting Corporation TV series Patrol Boat.

[edit] Remaining examples

Advance is a (fully operational) museum ship at the Australian National Maritime Museum in Sydney. Aitape was sunk as a dive wreck off Port Moresby in 1995. Ardent is preserved as a memorial in Darwin. Bayonet was scuttled in Bass Strait in 1999 and has been successfully dived.
