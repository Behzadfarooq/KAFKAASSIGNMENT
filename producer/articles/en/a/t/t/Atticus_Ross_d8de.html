Atticus Ross English musician (born January 16, 1968).

[edit] Early Career

Atticus first came to notice in the mid '90s as a programmer for Tim Simenon's Bomb the Bass during the album periods "Unknown Territory" and "Clear." He worked on a number of production and remix projects with Simenon as well as forming a collaborative relationship with former Bad Seed Barry Adamson. He programmed "The Negro Inside Me" and "Oedipus Schmoedipus" and produced "As Above So Below" before forming his own band 12 Rounds with Claudia Sarne and Adam Holden. They released 2 albums, Jitterjuice (Polydor) and My Big Hero (Nothing Records). A third full-length album was produced by Trent Reznor for Nothing Records, but ultimately never released.

[edit] Works attributed to

  • One of the founders of the band Error
  • Programmer, writer for the band 12 Rounds
  • Member of the supergroup Tapeworm.
  • Additional programming and production on Nine Inch Nails' album With Teeth, and the upcoming Nine Inch Nails record, entitled Year Zero.
  • Worked on album projects with Bad Religion, Rancid, From First to Last, Zach de la Rocha
  • Worked on a number of projects with Trent Reznor 2001 to present
  • Co-wrote and Produced one-half of Korn's album See You on the Other Side, and will be collaborating with the band on their upcoming yet untitled release.
  • Programmed and played on Pink's album M!ssundaztood.

Remixed the Transplants, From First to Last, Dillinger Escape Plan, Neneh Cherry, Biggie Smalls, Korn

Atticus, along with the other two members of 12 Rounds - Claudia Sarne and his brother, Leopold Ross - composed the musical score for the USA Network television series Touching Evil.
