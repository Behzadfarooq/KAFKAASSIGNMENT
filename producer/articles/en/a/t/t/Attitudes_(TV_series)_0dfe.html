Attitudes was an American television talk show on Lifetime Television from 1985-1991. Hosted originally by Linda Dano and Nancy Glass, it was taped before a live studio audience at EUE Screen Gems Studios, 222 East 44th Street in New York City. Next, Glass was replaced by Dee Kelly and the show moved to Kaufman Astoria Studios in Queens. Dano's co-host later changed thrice more, with Jerry Penacoli, Dorothy Lucey and Rolonda Watts as her partners. Attitudes featured numerous celebrity guests from the worlds of entertainment, fashion, and politics. Although its main focus was women, the show developed quite a diverse following. There were often many hilarious comedic moments throughout the series. The show was nominated for Emmy and CableACE awards and frequently spoofed on Saturday Night Live.
