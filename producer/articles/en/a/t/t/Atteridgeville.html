Atteridgeville, part of the City of Tshwane Metropolitan Municipality, is a township located on the west of Pretoria, South Africa. It is bordered to the west by Saulsville, to the east by Proclamation Hill; to the south by Laudium and to the north by Lotus Gardens. It was established in 1939 for blacks by the apartheid government but development was frozen between 1968 and 1978 in accordance with the government's policy that housing provided for blacks be limited to the homelands. In 1984, Atteridgeville was granted municipal status.

[edit] Languages

Atteridgeville is a diverse township whose residents speak many languages. The most commonly spoken language is SeSotho, which is a more pure form of SeTswana or SePedi. A mixture of languages such as Afrikaans, SeSotho, English and iSiZulu are sometimes fused together to form what is now a unique language-style of the township with a slight inclination to a slang known as tsotsi taal.

[edit] Locations

Atteridgeville is commonly known as "Phelindaba" or "Pheli" which is a Zulu expression for "End of Story". Atteridgeville is so nick-named because of its proximity to the now defunct nuclear power sites of "Phelindaba" and "Valindaba" (Close the Story). Some areas of the township are divided in terms of ethnic groupings, a system of housing engineered and instituted by the apartheid gorvenment. Some of these areas were nicknamed by local residents.

Nicknames:

  • Tlhala mpya
- Newly built houses callously nicknamed "divorce the dog" in Sotho.
  • Ou Stad
- Sotho speaking residents are concentrated in this area.
  • Ten Morgan
- Same demographics as Ou Stad.
  • Black Rock
- A blurred concetration of Pedi and Tsonga speaking residents.
  • Matebeleng (derog.)
- Ndebele and Zulu speaking residents are based.
  • Extension 3,4,5,6
- Newly developed areas defying past tribal segregation.
  • Selbourne Side
- Tsonga and Venda Speaking people
  • Ghost Town
- Areas near the old graveyard of Atteridgeville

Atteridgeville has since evolved into an integrated multi-tribal society.

[edit] Serial Killers

Atteridgeville has the dubious distinction of spawning more than half a dozen serial killers.

[edit] Known serial killers from Atteridgeville

  • Atteridgeville Mutilator, killed boys during a five-month period in 1956, still unidentified.
  • Elias Xitavhudzi (Pangaman) killed and mutilated 16 women before being caught and executed in the 1960s. The fact that he was black and the women were white doubled the impact.
  • Ironman, killed and robbed victims with an iron bar during the 1970s, still unidentified.
  • Moses Sithole, killed 38 women in 1995. Serving 2,410 years.
  • Axeman
  • Beastman
  • Kgabi
  • Mukuku-Motho (The Chicken man) - Harassed kids
  • Rra Jasi (The Coat man) - Attacked residents in the streets at night. As a result, the streets were quiet and empty after sunset.
