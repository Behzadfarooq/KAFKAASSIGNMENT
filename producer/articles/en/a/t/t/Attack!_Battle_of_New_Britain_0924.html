Attack! Battle of New Britain

Attack! Battle of New Britain was a documentary/propaganda film produced by the US military in 1944. It detailed, as its name implies, the campaign to liberate the island of New Britain, in the Solomon Islands campaign.

The film follows a rather standerd format: it is a chronological narrative of the campaign from the arrival of the soldiers in New Guienia to their capture of most of the island. Opening with travelers book pictures of the area, reminding the audience what the adverage soldier image of what the South Seas would be like, and it then details the natural hazerds of fighting, or even being in the jungle, including the insects, diseases and heat. The marines and soldiers set up a little tent city, with the significant help of the local natives or "fussy wussies". After the base has been established and enough men are ready, General MacArthur arrives in person to disscus the upcoming campaign with the units CO.

Whith the aid of maps and non-combat footage, the audience learns about the plans for the attack, the geography of the island, and the variety of armoured vehicles that will be used in the upcoming battle, including the "allegator" and "buffalo" amphibious assault vehicles. D-day is on Christmas, and while the sailors and marines contemple in the rear, the airmen spend Christmas Eve strafing the island, to make way for their comrades assault. The campaign is then followed through chronologically, focusing, at the end, on the many wounded and killed who have sacrificed so much.
