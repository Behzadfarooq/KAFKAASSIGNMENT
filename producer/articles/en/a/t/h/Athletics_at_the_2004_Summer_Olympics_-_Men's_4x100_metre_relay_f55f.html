The 4 x 100 metres relay at the 2004 Summer Olympics as part of the athletics program were held at the Athens Olympic Stadium on August 27 and August 28. The United States were favourites going into the final but poor baton changes led to Great Britain's team narrowly defeating them in a photo-finish. The Great Britain final time of 38.07 seconds was actually slower than the 38.02 posted by the USA in their heat. The final runners, Mark Lewis-Francis and Maurice Greene, received their batons at roughly the same time but with Greene some 2 metres behind; in a spectacular race down the straight, Lewis-Francis held off his better-credentialled opponent to secure the win for his team.

[edit] Qualifying

From the initial two heats the first three teams in each plus two fastest losers progressed through to the final.
