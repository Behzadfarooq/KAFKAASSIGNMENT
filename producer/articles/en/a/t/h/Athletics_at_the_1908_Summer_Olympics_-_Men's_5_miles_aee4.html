Athletics at the 1908 Summer Olympics - Men's 5 miles

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

You have new messages (last change).

The men's 5 miles race was held at the 1908 Summer Olympics in London. It was discontinued after that in favor of the metric races of 5,000 and 10,000 metres.

There were six preliminary heats, with the top two runners in each advancing to the final.

[edit] Results

[edit] First round

[edit] First round, heat 1

Ragueneau retired in the first quarter-mile. Coales dropped out after about four miles. Hefferon lead for about half the race before Svanberg passed him.

1 J. F. Svanberg Sweden 25:46.2
2 C. Hefferon South Africa
3 G. B. Blake Australasia
DNF W. Coales Great Britain and Ireland
DNF A. G. Ragueneau France

Fitzgerald, Robertson, and Stevenson were close together for the first mile, then Robertson broke away. This left Fitzgerald and Stevenson to fight over second place.

[edit] First round, heat 6

Owen had the largest margin of victory in the preliminary heats, defeating Galbraith by a full lap of the track.

Rank Name Nation Time
1 E. Owen Great Britain and Ireland 26:12.0
2 W. Galbraith Canada
3 I. Nejedky Bohemia
DNF A. Lovas Hungary

[edit] Final

Fitzgerald, Murphy, Owen, Svanberg, Hefferon, and Voigt lead during the final race of the 5 miles. Owen crossed the one mile mark first, at 4:46.2. Hefferon was leading at the two and three mile marks, at times of 9:54.2 and 15:05.6. After four miles, Svanberg was in front, having finished 80% of the race in 20:19.2. In the last two laps, Voigt sprinted to the lead which he kept until the end of the contest.
