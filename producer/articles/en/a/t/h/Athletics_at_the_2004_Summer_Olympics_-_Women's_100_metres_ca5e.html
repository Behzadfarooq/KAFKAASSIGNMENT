The Women's 100 m at the 2004 Summer Olympics as part of the athletics program were held at the Athens Olympic Stadium on August 20 and August 21.

[edit] Records

Olympic Record 10.62 Florence Griffith Joyner USA Seoul, South Korea 24 September 1988
World Record 10.49 Florence Griffith Joyner USA Indianapolis, USA 16 July 1988

[edit] Medals

Gold: Silver: Bronze:
Yulia Nestsiarenka, Belarus Lauryn Williams, United States Veronica Campbell, Jamaica

[edit] Race Summary

[edit] Qualifying

Initially there were eight heats with the first three from each heat progressing along with the eight fastest losers. In the second round the first three from each of the four heats went through to the semi-finals along with the four fastest losers. The first four in each of those semi-finals then qualified for the final.

[edit] Round 1

[edit] Heat 1

  1. Aleen Bailey, Jamaica, 11.20 Q
  2. Veronique Mang, France, 11.24 Q
  3. Debbie Ferguson, Bahamas, 11.30 Q
  4. Mercy Nku, Nigeria, 11.37 q
  5. Liliana Allen, Mexico, 11.42 q
  6. Geraldine Pillay, South Africa, 11.44
  7. Tit Linda Sou, Cambodia, 13.47
  8. Katura Marae, Vanuatu, 13.49

Heat 1 saw Bailey take first in one of the slower heats ahead of Mang who ran a personal best and Ferguson. There was also a PB for Sou of Cambodia.

[edit] Heat 2

  1. Yulia Nestsiarenka, Belarus, 10.94 Q
  2. Merlene Ottey, Slovenia, 11.14 Q
  3. Larisa Kruglova, Russia, 11.23 Q
  4. Guzel Khubbieva, Uzbekistan, 11.31 q
  5. Rakia Al Gassra, Bahrain, 11.49
  6. Winneth Dube, Zimbabwe, 11.56
  7. Evangeleen Ikelap, Federated States of Micronesia, 13.50
  8. Danah Al Nasrallah, Kuwait, 13.92

Nestsiarenka, national record and fastest time of all heats, was never troubled, leading from the gun along with Khubbieva who faded towards the end and Kruglova, equalled personal best, who managed to stay on but could not hold off the 44 year old veteran Ottey who started slowly but ran down all but Nestsiarenka in the latter stages. Further down the pack were Al Gassra and Nasrallah who also ran national records and Ikelap equalling her PB.

[edit] Heat 3

  1. Lauryn Williams, United States, 11.16 Q
  2. Irina Khabarova, Russia, 11.32 Q
  3. Fana Ashby, Trinidad and Tobago, 11.43 Q
  4. Affoue Amandine Allou, Côte d'Ivoire, 11.46
  5. Melisa Murillo, Colombia, 11.67
  6. Elena Bobrovskaya, Kyrgyzstan, 11.76
  7. Ngerak Florencio, Palau, 12.76
  8. Aminata Kamissoko, Mauritania, 13.49

Williams held up the start for a minute or so as she asked a track side judge to remove some litter that appeared in her lane and this may have distracted her as she was slow away but ran down the fast away Khabarova in the last twenty metres. A hard running Ashby just held off Allou for the last automatic qualifying place. Florencio managed a PB in finishing sixth but even she was a different class to the Mauritanian who was clearly out of her depth.

[edit] Heat 4

  1. Yuliya Tabakova, Russia, 11.22 Q
  2. Sherone Simpson, Jamaica, 11.27 Q
  3. Chandra Sturrup, Bahamas, 11.37 Q
  4. Bettina Mueller, Austria, 11.39 q
  5. Johanna Manninen, Finland, 11.45
  6. Sina Schielke, Germany, 11.46
  7. Kaitinano Mwemweata, Kiribati, 13.07
  8. Philaylack Sackpraseuth, Laos, 13.42

Manninen held up her hand on the blocks after the starter had held them for an excessively long time. When they eventually got away Sturrup, returning from injury, burst out from the blocks but lack of fitness told as she fell apart at seventy metres and only just held off Mueller. Tabakova and Simpson both went past Sturrup with the Russian powering away to take first. There was a PB for Mwemeata in lane 4.

[edit] Heat 5

  1. LaTasha Colander, United States, 11.31 Q
  2. Endurance Ojokolo, Nigeria, 11.36 Q
  3. Natasha Mayers, Saint Vincent and the Grenadines, 11.45 Q
  4. Tetyana Tkalich, Ukraine, 11.58
  5. Basma Al Eshosh, Jordan, 12.09 NR
  6. Aleksandra Vojnevska, Former Yugoslav Republic of Macedonia, 12.15
  7. Xuemei Li, China, 12.21
  8. Jenny Keni, Solomon Islands, 12.76

Colander was impressive leading from the gun and easing up through the last five metres. Ojokolo took second away from Mayers who slowed considerably in towards the end and limped off the track clinging her right hamstring. Although not qualifying for the latter stages Al Eshosh ran a new Jordanian record.

[edit] Heat 6

  1. Veronica Campbell, Jamaica, 11.17 Q
  2. Zhanna Block, Ukraine, 11.25 Q
  3. Gail Devers, United States, 11.29 Q
  4. La Verne Jones, Virgin Islands, 11.38 q
  5. Agne Eggerth, Lithuania, 11.44
  6. Heather Samuel, Antigua, 12.05
  7. Robina Muqim Yaar, Afghanistan, 14.14 NR
  8. Fartun Abukar Omar, Somalia, 14.29 PB

Former world champion Block, and double Olympic champion, both looked past their best trailing in behin Campbell who was never behind. Yaar set an Afghani national record. The Somalian, Omar, who brought up the rear had some problems. After leaving the blocks she followed the lane markings that merge with the home straight and wandered into lane 6, fortunately Devers had left her behind so was not interfered with.

[edit] Heat 7

  1. Vida Anim, Ghana, 11.14 Q
  2. Christine Arron, France, 11.14 Q
  3. Kim Gevaert, Belgium, 11.18 Q
  4. Karin Mayr-Krifka, Austria, 11.40 q
  5. Rosemar Maria Coelho Neto, Brazil, 11.43 q
  6. Mae Koime, Papua New Guinea, 12.00
  7. Hawanatu Bangura, Sierra Leone, 12.11
  8. Alaa Jassim, Iraq, 12.70

A false start by the inexperienced Koime didn't put her off as ran a national record in coming sixth. Jaasim, Iraq's only female competitor at the games, was also not disgraced in coming last. These two were a fair distance behind the business end of the race where world indoor 60 m. champion Gevaert, who had burst out the blocks, was ran down by Anim (NR) and Arron who both recorded the same time.

[edit] Heat 8

  1. Ivet Lalova, Bulgaria, 11.16 Q
  2. Abiodun Oyepitan, Great Britain, 11.23 Q
  3. Lyubov Perepelova, Uzbekistan, 11.30 Q
  4. Delphine Bertille Atangana, Cameroon, 11.40 q
  5. Viktoriya Koviyreva, Kazakhstan, 11.62
  6. Marine Ghazaryan, Armenia, 12.29
  7. Carol Mokola, Zambia, 12.35

Atangana shot into an early lead from the start but was tiring by fifty metres from where Lalova took control of the race. Oyepitan had a strong steady race and just held off Perepelova who finished steadily in lane eight after a poor start.
