Athletics at the 1900 Summer Olympics - Men's 400 metres

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

The men's 400 metres was a track & field athletics event at the 1900 Summer Olympics in Paris. It was held on 14 July and 15 July 1900. 15 athletes from six nations competed.

[edit] Results

[edit] First round

In the first round, there were three heats. They were held on 14 July. The top two runners in each advanced to the final.

[edit] First round, heat 1

Place Athlete Nation Time
1 Maxey Long United States 50.4 seconds
2 Harry Lee United States Unknown
3 Harvey Lord United States Unknown
4 Georges Clément France Unknown
5 Walter Drumheller United States Unknown

This heat, featuring four American runners, resulted in an easy win for Long and the top three spots for the United States team.

[edit] First round, heat 2

Place Athlete Nation Time
1 William Moloney United States 51.0 seconds
2 Ernst Schulz Denmark Unknown
3 Charles-Robert Faidide France Unknown
4 Pál Koppán Hungary Unknown
5 Yngvar Bryn Norway Unknown

Again, an American won the heat easily. Schulz took second place to qualify for the final.

[edit] First round, heat 3

Place Athlete Nation Time
1 Dixon Boardman United States 51.2 seconds
2 William Holland United States Unknown
3 Henry Slack United States Unknown
4-5 Zoltán Spiedle Hungary Unknown
Umberto Colombo Italy Unknown

The United States runners again took all three of the top spots in this heat.

[edit] Final

Place Athlete Nation Time
1 Maxey Long United States 49.4 seconds
2 William Holland United States 49.6 seconds (est.)
3 Ernst Schulz Denmark Unknown
— Dixon Boardman United States Did not start
Harry Lee United States Did not start
William Moloney United States Did not start

Boardman, Lee, and Moloney did not start because the final was held on 15 July, a Sunday. Long and Holland did start, however, and took the top two spots easily. Long led the entire way.
