ABSURD!

Some people do believe in Thor and this doesn't prove that criticising Atheism is absurd. It proves that Atheism is pointless. I don't believe in Santa but I am not an 'Asantaist'. I don't believe in the Easter Bunny but I don't follow 'Abunnyism'! 86.131.245.248 11:22, 11 February 2007 (UTC)

 ???? Are you saying that you exercise faith when you say that you don't believe in Santa or the Easter Bunny? I don't know about you, but the usual answer will be "no", which shows that the argument in the article is not absurd (remember, it's only saying that a particular claim is fallacious, not that theism itself is). More importantly, the term and the argument in the article seem to be somewhat notable, so I don't see what your problem is. Saligron 00:36, 12 February 2007 (UTC)
That's exactly the point. Disbelief does not require proof. When a theist challenges an atheist, "You can't prove that God doesn't exist," the atheist can answer, "Nor can I prove that Thor doesn't exist, but I don't believe in him... and neither do you. You and I are athorists." --Rick MILLER 16:18, 28 February 2007 (UTC)

Richard Dawkins coined this term as a shortcut. He used to use a longer argument, "Do you believe in Thor or Zeus? Why not? Couldn't someone disbelieve in your god for the same reasons?" Then he switched to saying, "Everyone is an atheist in one way or another. You are an atheist, with respect to the gods Thor and Zeus for example." "Athorism" was the meat of a satirical essay which he wrote after the publication of "The God Delusion". --Rick MILLER 16:11, 28 February 2007 (UTC)
