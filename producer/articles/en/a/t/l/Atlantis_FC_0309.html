Atlantis FC is a Finnish football club, based in Helsinki. It currently plays in the Finnish First Division (Ykkönen). It was founded late 1995 when 1980 founded Johanneksen Dynamo and 1985 FC Norssi merged. The name "Atlantis FC was invented by Gösta Sundqvist, singer of Leevi and the Leavings.

Team started to play in second division (Kakkonen) 1996 and 1997 it was able to rise to Ykkönen. 1999 club reached qualification games for premium leaague (Veikkausliiga, betting league) but then was Vaasan Palloseura stronger. Next year it reached Veikkausliiga. 2001 Atlantis was seventh in league and won the cup of Finland.

The club had financial difficulties and went bankrupt 2002 and its' place in premium league was given to AC Allianssi. Reserve team of the club Atlantis Akatemia (Atlantis Academy) still continued in second division. 2003 it changed its' name to Atlantis FC and 2004 club raised to Ykkönen. 2006 it tries to reach premium league again.

The club's manager is Markku Ritala, coach is Pasi Pihamaa and it plays its home matches at Helsingin Pallokenttä.
