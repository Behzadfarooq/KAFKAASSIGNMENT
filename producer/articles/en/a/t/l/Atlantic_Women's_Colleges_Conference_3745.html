Atlantic Women's Colleges Conference

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

The Atlantic Women's Colleges Conference is an eight-member conference founded in 1995 and given official status in 1999. It competes in NCAA Division III and as its name implies, only offers championships in women's sports.

[edit] Members

  • Chatham College
  • Chestnut Hill College
  • Hood College Note: Hood College became co-educational in 2003.

Mary Baldwin College (set to join the USA South Athletic Conference in 2007)

  • College of Notre Dame of Maryland
  • Trinity University
  • Wells College
  • Wilson College (Pennsylvania)

[edit] Championships offered

  • Basketball
  • Field hockey
  • Lacrosse
  • Soccer
  • Softball
  • Swimming
  • Tennis
  • Volleyball
