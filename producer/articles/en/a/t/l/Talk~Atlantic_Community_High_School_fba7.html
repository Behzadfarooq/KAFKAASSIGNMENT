[edit] Band

How useful is the new "Band" section? Is the band an major part of the culture of the place? If this section is retained, it needs a lot of work to better describe its relevance, the bands history, and recent upheavals & achievements. Jayvdb 03:38, 18 April 2006 (UTC)

I agree with Jayvdb regarding the "Band" section. It is fairly incoherent with redundant content and very little depth, development, and insight. Also, as a former IB graduate (and band member) of ACHS, I am appalled at the quality of this wiki entry in general. As a response to Jayvdb, in my time of 1995-1999, the band was indeed a major part of this school's culture, with a quarter of the student body being member. ACHS attracted and retained a number of high-caliber musicians by virtue of its existing high-caliber band plus the IB magnet program. It appears that a lot has changed in the band program since then -- the band (marching/wind ensemble) has a LONG history of "straight superiors" which makes me wonder what happened to warrant this new boast of "superior" status. Please describe the downfall of this band, when, and how. I edited the Colleges section to remove misleading information. Most significantly, I changed "colleges which are known to accept many Atlantic students..." to "graduates have matriculated at many top-tier and state universities..." Notice the position of "many" in the two versions. 206.148.32.200 05:30, 8 June 2006 (UTC) by priestess[at gmail]

As a 2006 ACHS band graduate, I have to say that the band program has lost a great deal of its prestige from those years, and certainly is not well-known as a "straight superior" band. That said, I too am embarrassed by this article, and I'm going to try to clean it up as much as I can. Nevertheless, I think that "Band" is not significant enough â and certainly doesn't pass my "encyclopedic merit" standards â to be included here.
