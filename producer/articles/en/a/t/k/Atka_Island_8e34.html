Atka Island

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Atka Island is the largest island in the Andreanof Islands of the Aleutian Islands of Alaska, at 52°07′N, 174°30′W. The island is 80 km (50 miles) east of Adak Island. It is 105 km (65 miles) long and 3-32 km (2-20 miles) wide, and has a land area of 1,047.9 km² (404.6 sq mi). The northeast of Atka Island contains the Korovin volcano which reaches a peak of 1,533 meters.

The city of Atka, Alaska is on the east side of the island. A United States Air Force base, established during World War II is also on Atka Island. The 2000 census population of the island was 95 persons, almost all in the city of Atka.

[edit] Education

Atka is served by the Aleutian Region Schools.

The Yakov E. Netsvetov School serves grades K-12.
