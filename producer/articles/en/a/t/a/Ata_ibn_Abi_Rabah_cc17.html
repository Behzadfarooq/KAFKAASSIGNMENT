Ata ibn Abi Rabah (Arabic: عطى بن أبي رباح‎) was a prominent Tabi‘in Muslim transmiter of hadith, a student of Ibn Abbas, often quoted by his 8th century student, the Islamic scholar Ibn Jurayj [3]

[edit] Views

According to Muhammad ash-Shawkani, a 19th century Sunni Islamic scholar [4] and Ibn Hazm, a 11th century Sunni Islamic scholar[5], after the death of Muhammad, Ata verdicted that Nikah Mut'ah was legal.

[edit] Legacy

[edit] Hadith

He quoted hadith from:

  • Ibn Abbas [3]

His hadith were quoted by:

  • Ibn Manzar [6]
  • Ibn Jurayj [3]


traditions from him are recorded by Imams Bukhari, Muslim, al-Tirmidhi, al-Nasa'i, Abu Dawood, Ibn Maja, and Imam Ahmad bin Hanbal. 'Ataa narrates over 100 traditions in each of Sahih Bukhari and Sahih Muslim. [2].
