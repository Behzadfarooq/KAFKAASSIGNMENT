[edit] Andrew "Atama" Ambrose

Andrew A. Mudi was born on Tuesday, May 24, 1975 in the small village of Tambunan, Sabah. His father, Ambrose Mudi, was born in Tambunan in 1952 to Kuijau-Dusun father originated from Bingkor and Tuhawon mother who originated from Tambunan district. Ambrose Mudi was a musician, singer and composer, and also a teacher when he married Andrew's mother, Lily Wong, a 22 year old Sino-Dusun woman originated from Mottou village, Keningau. Ambrose provided financial support for his wife and childrens, Linda Ambrose born in 1973, Arnold Ambrose born in 1979, Lisa Sabrina Ambrose born in 1981 and AllenMax Ambrose born in 1986.

Both Ambrose & Lily served the government the teaching professions, where Ambrose pioneered the development of cultural music through Sompoton Singkokolu and was the first to pen tribal music into western scale. In 1978, Ambrose studied music in Kuala Lumpur and graduated as Master Of Music Education from Southern Illionois University, Carbondale, United States in 1985.

Upon returning to Sabah the following year, Ambrose organized Bintang Kaamatan [Now known as ‘Sungandoi’] an annual KadazanDusun singing contest for Kadazan Dusun Cultural Association, KDCA[1] during the Tadau Tagayo Do Kaamatan [Harvest Festival]. Many critics regard Ambrose not only as the founder of this singing contest and tribal music but also as the mentor of his eldest son.

[edit] Earlier Life

Andrew was exposed to stage performance at a tender age of 5 years old when the child performed on TV Musicals on Radio Televisyen Malaysia, RTM. He played the clarinet in Unity Point Elementary School band and later took up piano when they lived in Carbondale.


[edit] Rockin Nation

Andrew became friends with Jonathan Quan & Lester Maurice and formed Hip Hop group Rockin Nation in 1990 while still in his teens. Andrew left school at the age of 17 and started as apprentice DJ at a local pub, while still actively dancing & singing with Rockin Nation.
