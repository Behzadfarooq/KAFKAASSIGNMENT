Atarisoft

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Atarisoft was a brand name used by Atari in 1983 and 1984 to market video games they published for home systems made by their competitors. Each platform had a specific color attributed by Atarisoft for its game packages. For example, video games sold for the Commodore 64 came up in green packages, games for the Texas Instruments TI-99/4A in orange, and so on. Despite being in existence for less than two years, Atarisoft had a huge video game library with dozens of game versions being released for various home computers and consoles.

The Atarisoft label does not bear the popular logo (called "Fuji") that Atari has, and the letters from its name, although in capital like Atari, were significantly wider and different then Atari's (they were written in a font that is now popularly called Futuri Extra Bold [non-italic]). Atarisoft was a brand mainly used on game boxes and manuals because the name that appeared on the bottom of the title screen of these games was generally the name of Atari and not Atarisoft.

The label died in all countries except France when Warner Communications sold Atari to Jack Tramiel. It survived another year in France before being disbanded there as well in 1985.
