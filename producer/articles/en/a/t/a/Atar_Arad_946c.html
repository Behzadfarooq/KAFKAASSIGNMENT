Atar Arad is an Israeli violist and professor. Arad was a member of the Cleveland Quartet from 1980 to 1987 taking the seat of Martha Strongin Katz. He was later succeeded by James Dunham.

Arad currently teaches at Indiana University's Jacobs School of Music in Bloomington, and at the Steans Institute Ravinia Festival in Chicago as well as the Domaine Forget academy for the arts in Quebec. He has also performed at numerous festivals including the Sitka Summer Music Festival, and has recorded for the RCA, CBS, and Telarc labels.

Arad and his brother, architect Ron Arad, were born in Tel Aviv, Israel.
