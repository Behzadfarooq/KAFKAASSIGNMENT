ATA Holdings, Inc. is a private airline holding company, based in Indianapolis, Indiana. The company's primary asset is ATA Airlines, Inc. (ATA), which maintains a hub in Chicago, Illinois at Chicago Midway International Airport.

[edit] History

Originally founded as the successor to Amtran Holdings in 2002, ATA Holdings was as a publically traded company, ATA Holdings, Inc. traded on the NASDAQ as "ATAH" until the company was faced with bankruptcy problems in November, 2004. Today, ATA Holdings provides scheduled airline services and operates military/commercial air transport charter businesses throughout the United States.

An ATA Airlines Boeing 757-200 (N512AT) at Phoenix Sky Harbor International Airport.

[edit] Bankruptcy

On October 26, 2004, the Company and seven of its subsidiaries, ATA Airlines, Chicago Express Airlines, ATA Cargo, ATA Leisure Corp., ATA Training Corp., Amber Travel, and ExecuJet, filed voluntary petitions for relief under Chapter 11 of the United States Bankruptcy Code in the United States Bankruptcy Court for the Southern District of Indiana. The Company and its subsidiaries continued to operate their respective business as debtors-in-possession under the jurisdiction of the Bankruptcy Court, and in accordance with the applicable provisions of the Bankruptcy Code, the Federal Rules of Bankruptcy procedure and applicable court orders, and continued to do this for one and a half years. On February 28, 2006, ATA Holdings, Inc. emerged from Chapter 11 United States Bankruptcy Protection.

[edit] Restructuring

After the company's emergence into bankruptcy on October 26, 2004, drastic measures were taken to turn the company around and make it more profitable. In February, 2005, ATA named John G. Denison Chairman and CEO, and later that year, launched their first-ever codeshare agreement with Southwest Airlines, enabling ATA to connect their passengers to Southwest's markets and vice-versa. ATA also drastically cut down its scheduled operations, most significantly from its hub at Chicago Midway International Airport. When ATA Holdings exited bankruptcy on February 28, 2006, ATA was left with only five destinations at its Chicago hub.
