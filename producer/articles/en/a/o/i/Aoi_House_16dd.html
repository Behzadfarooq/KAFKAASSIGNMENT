Aoi House is a shōnen harem Original English-language manga series written by Adam Arnold, with art by Shiei, and published by Seven Seas Entertainment. The story follows the misadventures of two down-on-their-luck college guys named Alexis "Alex" Roberts and Sandy Grayson who are thrown out of their college dorm and join an anime club dominated by crazed yaoi fangirls.

It first appeared as a 36-episode webcomic written by Adam Arnold and illustrated by Jim Jimenez. It initially ran from January 24, 2005 through April 15, 2005. Aoi House quickly established itself as a "fandom harem comedy."

Proving popular enough, Aoi House was re-launched by Seven Seas Entertainment as part of its standard publishing line. For the re-launch, Shiei (artist of Amazing Agent Luna) took over as the illustrator leading to changes in the art style, as well as additional changes to characters and alteration an expansion of the story. The re-launch began serialization on the Seven Seas Entertainment website in late May, around which time (2005-05-27) a Aoi House theme song, performed by the J Brothers entitled "Itsumo Futaride", was also released.

In January 2006, Aoi House 4-panel color koma began appearing in Newtype USA.

Aoi House made its print debut on 2006-05-15 with the release of the first volume.

New updates of Aoi House appear every Monday, Wednesday, and Friday at Gomanga.com and are accompanied by blog-style entries called "Aoi Notes" by Adam Arnold.

On January 19, IGN.com announced their "Top 10 Manga of 2006," which picked Aoi House Vol. 1-2 as the #3 best manga of the year behind Death Note Vol. 7 (#2) and the Train Man (Densha Otoko) manga series (#1).[1]

[edit] Plot

Spoiler warning: Plot and/or ending details follow.

Alexis "Alex" Roberts and Sandy Grayson get removed from their dorm lodging, due to several infractions of the rules, including the showing of Hentai movies. Echiboo, Sandy's pet hamster, does not help matters when he scares the Dean and exposes the fact the Dean is wearing a toupee. Told they need to find new lodges by sundown they start to search campus for a place to live. In their search for lodging, as Sandy proves himself to be an otaku and Alex more focused on reality of their situation. Seeing a flyer for Aoi House which promises anime and manga viewing Sandy becomes very excited with the very idea of an anime club, however Alex is mostly happy to have found possible lodging. As they head over to housing, called Aoi House on the flyer, Maria is seen looking at the same flyer Sandy and Alex just left thoughtfully reading what is written.

Excited about finding the lodging they need before sundown, Sandy and Alex hurry over to "Aoi House". Upon arrival Sandy knocks a few times on the door but gets no answer so Alex takes the more direct approach and throws the door open. Once he has Alex and Sandy are startled to find a room full of beautiful girls watching anime. At this point Maria makes her own entrance not knowing anyone is in the doorway she bumps into Sandy who stumbles into the room eventually landing against Elle's lap, then with a hit from Elle falls into Jessica's chest. After Maria enters and mentions she desires to join Aoi House, Sandy and Alex express a similar desire to the shock of all the girls. The girls of "Aoi House" at first mistake Sandy and Alex as gay yaoi fans since they themselves are "yaoi crazed females".

Once the truth is learned that Sandy and Alex are not truly crazed gay yaoi fans but two straight males, the girls have some fun with Sandy and Alex in an attempt to turn them into yaoi lovers, or at least make them gay. It is also at this time that Sandy and Alex learn that Morgan, who made the flyer, left a few important details off the flyer. One is that Aoi House was once "Yaoi House" until the 'Y' fell off the sign some time ago and straight male roommates were not what the girls were looking for at least at first. On top of all of this Echiboo also almost gets them in deep trouble again by escaping from his cage to find and crawl amongst a pile of Elle's panties. Elle was already dead set against allowing two straight males to join "Aoi House". Fortunately (or maybe unfortunately) for Sandy and Alex though, as Nina allows them to stay, Jessica points out to an irate Elle, Aoi House is in as much need of members in order to keep their house as Sandy and Alex are in need of lodging.

Thus starts the tale of two straight males in a house of yaoi crazed females! [incomplete]

[edit] 4-Koma

Starting in the January 2006 issue of Newtype USA, Aoi House began appearing as a series of full-color, 4-panel gag strips. The 4-koma are drawn vertically and read right-to-left. The actual stories are all stand-alone and tend to be themed for the holiday's or events in a given month, such as Valentine's Day or college exams. Recent 4-koma from 2007 have involved spoofs of various anime series and video games.

[edit] Music Video

On December 4, 2006, Seven Seas' first-ever flash anime music video was released dedicated to [1]Aoi House. Aoi House: The Music Video was animated by Jonathan Talas and set to the Aoi House Theme Song - "Itsumo Futaride" by The J Brothers. Within its first week of release, the music video received over 150,000 views on Newgrounds.com, was awarded with Newground's Daily Second Place award for 12/05/06, and earned a spot on YouTube.com's "Top Favorites (This Week) in Arts & Animation" list.

[edit] Major characters

  • Alexis "Alex" Roberts
    • Morgan's "yaoi pet", and perhaps the more level-headed between himself and Sandy. He is Sandy's friend and roommate. Along with Sandy, he was kicked out of the dorm due to Sandy's viewing of Hentai anime, and the serving of Ramune which Dean Perkins mistook for an alcoholic beverage. It seems Sandy is in fact Alex's bestfriend as Alex continues to stand beside Sandy in spite of all the adventures he has faced as a result of just being near Sandy. It also seems he has almost the exclusive attention of Morgan McKnight. At a point in the story, the removal of his ponytail makes him look sufficiently like a bishonen to cast a 'fangirl' effect over the female cast. But as soon as a snippet of hair was cut the effect was broken.
  • Sandy Grayson
    • Sandy is the owner of Echiboo. Between Sandy and Alex, Sandy seems the larger otaku and much more immature. Sandy is also the one that Jessica seems the most interested in, as she calls him "tiger" and teasingly flirts with him. Sandy is also Alex's friend and roommate. Sandy appears to be a fan of almost all aspects of Japanese culture or at least the otaku culture as is seen by his drinking of Ramune while viewing anime, and likes to imagine different anime scenarios happening in real life. Has a strong dislike of corn beacause the Boku wa Sexual Harassment... famous corn scene...
  • Echiboo
    • Echiboo is Sandy's pet hamster. Morgan calls him a "panty hamster" because of the critter's obsession with panties. Echiboo seems to have taken a strong liking to Elle, or at least her panties. He is also one of the causes, by taking the Deans toupee, of Sandy and Alex needing to find new lodging and eventually ending up in Aoi House. Morgan likes to put a tiny camera on his head and let him run amuk, getting footage "of the ecchi kind."
Echiboo's name is a pun of Ebichu, the hamster from the Gainax anime series Orochuban Ebichu. His name also means "Perverted Fart" in Japanese.
  • Elle Mathers
    • The most mature of all the girls and very high maintenance. She comes from a wealthy family and continually acts upset about having Sandy and Alex as a part of Aoi House. But she does wear a very interesting costume shortly after their first arrival and seems to (barely) mellow toward them as they become more a part of daily Aoi House life, though she still holds an extremely bad attitude towards Alex. It's hinted that she is developing a crush on him, after stating to Oniisan how after the accidental kiss between the two, she "can't keep her mind off him". Thus, making her actions and attitude towards him a way of "defending" herself from the possible pain of heartbreak. Elle is on the tennis team.
  • Nina Parker
    • Nina is the most nonchalant member of Aoi House. She is also seemingly the least concerned about having two straight male housemates, as she at times continues to walk around without her shirt on regardless of the fact she now has two straight male housemates. She also makes a habit of smoking where she is not supposed to. She is also part of the a member of the swim team and practices synchronized swimming. Adam Arnold stated that Nina is the most "macho" of the Aoi House girls, and she also has the smallest bust. [2]
  • Morgan McKnight
    • The psuedo-lolicon of the group, Morgan is a very hyper girl who seems to be on a continual sugar high and loves candy. Morgan has a playful, childlike innocence about her and how she interacts with Sandy and Alex. In her first meeting with them she claims Alex as her "yaoi pet" and later uses something of Elle's to lead Sandy and Alex on a wild chase that ends in the girl's locker room. Her closest friend who is not an Aoi House member seems to be the mysterious "Oniisan" who she doesn't seem to hesitate in telling her secrets, almost as she would to a diary. Morgan also continues to demonstrate, both in her actions and what she tells her "Oniisan", a very strong attraction to Alex. She is much less energetic when talking to her confidant.
  • Jessica Kim
    • Jessica is a Korean-American medical student often found wearing her nurse outfit, the subject of Sandy's fetishes. She, along with Morgan, accepts Sandy and Alex joining Aoi House. Jessica is almost as playful as Morgan and joins in on one of hers schemes while helping to defend the ever hyper Morgan's desire for candy; both of these might be why she seems to be one of the few that can "control" Morgan. She acts as both motherly and teasingly towards Sandy and is the cause of many a nosebleed from him. She has three little brothers.
In the after notes of the manga it says she was originally suppose to be African American, but due to toning troubles was changed.
  • Maria Ortega
    • Along with Sandy and Alex, Maria is one of the newest members of Aoi House. Of Latino descent, she is a little self-conscious about her uncommonly large bust. She is happy to be in Aoi House, and is friendly toward Sandy and Alex, who once helped her shortly after they all joined. Her parents own a local Mexican Restaurant which is a place she uses along with the other members of Aoi House to celebrate the new members.

[edit] Minor characters

  • Oniisan
    • Somewhat of a mystery at this point in time. "Oniisan" seems to be the one that provides Morgan with an amazing amount of technological equipment including an "Ecchi-cam", a camera that can be strapped to Echiboo's head. "Oniisan" is also possibly Morgan's closest and only true confidant. At this time it is not certain who or what Morgan's Oniisan is, though, Oniisan has recently been revealed in "Diary Room Antics" to be human. The previous theory was that he was some sort of computer (an obelisk labeled "Sound Only", a reference to the anime Neon Genesis Evangelion) with various sound and visual links throughout the house. Oniisan means "big brother" in Japanese, and all the members of Aoi House (and former member Carlo) address him as "oniisan."
  • Carlo
    • Aoi House's (Yaoi House's) homosexual transvestite founder. When Sandy and Alex first meet him he is dressed as a woman complete with wig and skirt. In fact it is rather a nasty shock for them to learn, when the girls arrive and inform them of who Carlo is in fact, that the one they thought was a "she" is in fact a "he". It is interesting that though they have never met him, he clearly knows about them, indicating that the girls of Aoi House still keep close contact with their former founder. Carlo's face has stereotypical macho qualities. He also finds Alex very attractive and went on a date with him when Alex lost a bet.
  • Mr. Perkins
    • Dean of the University. Echiboo stole Mr. Perkins's toupee, causing Sandy and Alex to search for new housing, which eventually lead them to finding Aoi House. He continues to be somewhat of a problem for Sandy and Alex; he keeps a close on eye them because of the trouble they caused in the past. Mr. Perkins can occasionally be found watching the synchronized swim team practice and talking to the swim coach, Ms. Addison.
  • Ms. Addison
    • Coach of the women's synchronized swim team.
  • David, Daniel and Danny
    • The little triplet brothers of Jessica.

[edit] Books

Aoi House Vol. 1 collecting Episodes 1 through 7 was released on May 31, 2006. In the Aoi Notes on 04/21/06,[2] Adam Arnold said the book has been given "special edition" treatment that includes, "minor dialogue and art changes to fix continuity errors," is displayed with screentone whereas the web version presents only Shiei's black and white line art, and a new opening page for Episode 4 and an added scene, "with the guys singing Karaoke at the end of Episode 2." Other extras included are "Aoi Notes: Print Edition," "Student Records," "Fun Zone," and "Original Webmanga Designs."

Aoi House Vol. 2 collecting toned versions of Episodes 8 through 13, plus "Fruit Cake Fantasy" was released in December 2006. Extras include "Aoi Notes: Print Edition 2," an eight-page super deformed character gallery called "Chibified!," a "Fan Art" gallery, and a two-page story called "Aoi House: The Lost Scene."
