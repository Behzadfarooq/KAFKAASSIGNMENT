"Aozora no NAMIDA" is Hitomi Takahashi's 3rd single under the Sony Records (gr8! records) label. The single was released on November 30, 2005, in two different formats, CD Only and CD+DVD. This is the third retail single to be released for her first album "sympathy."

[edit] Overview

"Aozora no NAMIDA" was used as the first opening theme song for the anime "BLOOD+". The single was originally slated to be released on October 12, 2005, but was pushed back to a later date. This is Hitomi's first single to be in two formats, CD Only & CD+DVD. On its first day on the charts, "Aozora no NAMIDA" hit #5, and managed to get #8 for its first week on the charts. The music video for this single includes animation of "BLOOD+" made specifically for the video.

The title song is an upbeat song unlike her past single "evergreen." Like her other two singles, the song uses a guitar all through the song, but the guitar seems to be less apparent than in "Bokutachi no Yukue." A keyboard is used in parts of the song as well. The lyrics for the title song were once again penned by Hitomi herself.

Sample of the translated lyrics:

I wonder what kind of fate awaits me?
I don't want to regret being born
There is courage inside my sadness
I believe I can grasp that radiance
The tears of the blue sky are pouring down
One day they'll change into smiles

[edit] Music video

A screen capture of the music video.

"Aozora no NAMIDA's" PV was directed by AT, and like her previous two, was filmed at an unknown location. The PV starts off with the main character of BLOOD+, Saya, preparing to fight one of the Chiropterans. The scene suddenly changes to Hitomi waking up on a staircase, looking around, and then walking down the staircase. The scene changes again to Saya killing the Chiropteran, the scene also constantly switching from Hitomi to this scene as Hitomi continues to "search" around the place. As the PV progresses, Hitomi stubbles upon Saya fighting another Chiropteran, with Saya losing control of her sword. Also, other scenes of Hitomi can be seen; Hitomi sitting on a bunch of rocks with steam around her, and Hitomi singing in a subway-like setting (this is also where the fight against the second Chiropteran takes place). Hitomi grabs the sword and kills the Chiropteran causing Saya to disappear with a smile. The end of the PV features Hitomi walking out onto a cliff to view a city being ravaged by more Chiropteran, her eyes glowing red.
