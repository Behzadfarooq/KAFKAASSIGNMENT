Anugrah Narayan Sinha (Singh) (June 18, 1887 – 1957), known as Bihar Bibhuti, was the first Finance Minister of the Indian state of Bihar (1946 – 1957).[1]A.N. Sinha, affectionately called "Babu Saheb", was a very close associate of Mahatama Gandhi[2]during the freedom struggle movement and worked with Deshratna Dr. Rajendra Prasad to led the Gandhian movement in Bihar[3]

[edit] Political legacy

A. N. Sinha was known for his charismatic leadership and huge mass appeal and was a very prominent national leader[4]. Along with Bihar Kesari Sri Krishna Sinha, the first Chief Minister of Bihar, he is considered one of the makers of modern Bihar. He gave political patronage to the then young leaders like Loknayak Jayprakash Narayan, Jagjivan Ram, Bir chand Patel, K. B. Sahay and others. His younger son Satyendra Narayan Sinha (who was affectionately called "Chhote Saheb") also became a prominent freedom fighter leader and a distinguished politician and later became Chief Minister Of Bihar[5].His grandson [6], Nikhil Kumar (a former IPS), is currently a Member Of Parliament from the constituency Of Aurangabad. Dr. Sinha served Bihar continuously from 1946 till his death in 1957
