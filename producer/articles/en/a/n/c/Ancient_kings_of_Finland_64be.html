Ancient kings of Finland

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Ancient kings of Finland are totally unknown. Prehistoric Finland probably had chieftains called "kings" as the borrowed Finnish word (kuningas) has existed in the same meaning in the Finnish language since early Germanic times, but no record on them has survived. Possibility that a single sovereign had ruled all of Finland before the Swedish conquest, is very scarce.

Against a common belief, Norse saga sources do not mention any King of Finland. Ynglinga saga names a few important persons living in Finland, but does not attribute them with a title. Few other sagas briefly mention a couple of legendary kings that were said to have ruled also Finland, but without claiming them to be kings of Finland. None of these persons is said to be a Finn.
