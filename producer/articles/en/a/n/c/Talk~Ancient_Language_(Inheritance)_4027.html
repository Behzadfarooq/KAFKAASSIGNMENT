[edit] More words

On pg. 230 of Eldest, I noticed that Queen Islanzadi said the word Däthedr-vor, and then thank you right after that. Does that mean that Dathedr-vor means thank you in the Ancient Language? But the weird thing is that it doesn't have the word in the back. Should we add it to the article or not? Icelandic Hurricane #12 20:27, 5 April 2006 (UTC)


I believe that a common practice is to write the phrase in the foreign language and then its translation. Perhaps Paolini felt it was unnecessary to include the phrase in the back of the book since he provided a translation in the text itself. --Targeter 01:40, 31 May 2006 (UTC)

Actually, Dathedr-vor(sorry dont have the correct symbols) is a person. "Vor" is in the glossary as a male honorific for a close friend. Seeing as she was speaking to someone referred to as "a lithe elf lord" we can assume that she is referring to him and that dathedr is his name, also it is Arya speaking not Islanzadi.

[edit] Words Removed

That word list was inaccurate. Some of the words had been assigned their meanings through the writer's discretion; such as eddyr: there is no reason to believe that it means empty, and nowhere does it say that Eragon means "mighty". Aside from this, some of the words were also incorrectly spelled.
