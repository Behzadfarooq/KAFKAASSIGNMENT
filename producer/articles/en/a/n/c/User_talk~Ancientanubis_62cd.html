I fully agree with what 上村七美 has said above. Regardless of how you came across the images (screenshots, logos, etc.) in question, if their copyright holder hasn't released them under a free license, they can only be used on Wikipedia as fair-use images. While fair-use images are allowed for limited use in the encyclopedia (which is why Image:Gits-laughing-man.jpeg is available and used in the Laughing Man article), the use of fair-use images is strictly prohibited in user pages and userboxes. Item nine of our official policy on Fair use criteria is quite clear:

Fair use images should be used only in the article namespace. Used outside article space, they are often enough not covered under the fair use doctrine. They should never be used on templates (including stub templates and navigation boxes) or on user pages.

While I appreciate that you want to do your favorite anime justice by making the related userboxes look nice, you do need to steer clear of using and uploading fair-use images. To understand why, I think it's useful to keep in mind that our primary purpose here is the creation of an encyclopedia. Fair-use images are allowed for limited use in encyclopedia articles for those situations where free alternative images aren't available, as that furthers our goal of improving the encyclopedia. However, since we are "the free encyclopedia", it's important that we keep the use of non-free fair-use images to a minimum. Now, there is no similar need to use non-free images on user pages (i.e., outside the encyclopedia)—using such images to decorate user pages is unnecessary, and so we can better comply with our goal of using only free content by prohibiting their use in user pages and userboxes.

I hope this helps you understand the current situation with fair-use images and userboxes. I can try and answer any questions you might have. — Jeff | (talk) | 09:40, 20 June 2006 (UTC)

The only comment I'd make is that the situation with the FMA logo might be a bit dicey, even if it is an "original" work and not something from the web. The description of Image:Full metal crucified serpent.jpg says that it came from a manga scan. Now, if you modified that manga scan in order to create that image, then I believe your version represents a "derivative work". IANAL, but I think that you can't freely license something that's heavily derived from someone else's original, copyrighted work. If my thinking is correct, since the scans of the original manga could only be used as fair-use images, then derivative works from those scans might also be fair-use images. I'm not sufficiently versed on fair use law to speak definitively on this, but I'd probably advise erring on the side of caution. — Jeff | (talk) | 09:55, 20 June 2006 (UTC)
