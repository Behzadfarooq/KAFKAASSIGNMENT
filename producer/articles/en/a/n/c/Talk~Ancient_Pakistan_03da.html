[edit] Redirection:Why?

I have made this re-direction as many persons may be searching for information for Ancient Pakistan. As the modern state of Pakistan is hardly 6 decades old, and the territories now comprising Pakistan was part of Ancient India, this re-direction shall serve the purpose of knowledge seekers. --Bhadani 14:05, 12 June 2006 (UTC)

[edit] Please create a page

Please create a page about Ancient Pakistan. I have reverted the edit as the redirection to Pakistan did not give the reader any fresh input. Moreover, the editor concerned has perhaps the habit of vandalizing wikipedia. Re: User talk:82.225.228.35. I would request the editors really interested in developing this page to create at least a stub of 200 - 250 words about Ancient Pakistan. As the geographical area comprising Pakistan was part of Ancient India, so a redirection to that page is in order. We are building an encyclopedia, and not fighting games of patriotism. --Bhadani 16:51, 4 July 2006 (UTC)

[edit] Correction

Pakistan is not being used as a Political term, but a term to refer to the Pakistani people. Obviously "Indian" cannot be used, but thats not the only reason.
Pakistan was part of "India" for less than 100 years, and this was from 1850 - 1947. Prior to 1850, there was no such land called "India". So please get your facts right. Pakistani people have their own history, and referring to their history as "Indian" (all based on a 100 year old link) is misleading. In fact, Pakistani history should not even be included in the "Ancient India" article, since Indian refers to the people who call themselves Indian.
