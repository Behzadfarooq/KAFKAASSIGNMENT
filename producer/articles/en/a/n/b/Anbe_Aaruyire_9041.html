Anbe Aaruyire (2005) is a Tamil film directed by S.J. Suryah. The film stars himself, Nila, Urvasi, Santhana Bharathy and Santhanam. The film's score and soundtrack are composed by A. R. Rahman. The story tells the fantasy world of how memories reunite love.

[edit] Plot

Spoiler warning: Plot and/or ending details follow.

Siva (S.J. Suryah) is an investigative scribe who shares a live-in relationship with rich and bratty Madhu (Nila). They have hot-headed run-ins and even hotter patch-ups.

Things come to a head when Madhu starts a restaurant with a brother of her friend. Nosey and envious Siva can't take it, and this causes a split between the duo. Fun starts as the fantasy element appears in the form of their apparitional alter egos. Eventually they come together, but there is plenty of over-the-top entertainment aimed at a post-teen youth audience.

[edit] Miscellanea

  • Acknowledged as Nila's first film.
  • The visual graphics were created by Soundarya, daughter of Rajnikanth.
  • It was SJ Surya's fourth film after Vaali, Khushi and New.
  • For the first time in Indian cinema, visual graphics were created for the whole screen time of three songs.
  • The movie was a great hit for director SJ Surya after a hat-trick of successes. The film played for 100 days.
  • The songs of the film composed by A. R. Rahman were huge hits and the major reason for the film's success.
