Angel of Retribution is an album by Judas Priest, released in 2005. It marks the return of Rob Halford, after a 12 year hiatus. Impressively, the album debuted at #13 on the U.S. Billboard chart, making it the highest-ranking album the band has released to date (by comparison, British Steel, Screaming for Vengeance and Turbo had only achieved #17 after their respective debuts). It also marked the first time Judas Priest have been the number 1 artist on a national chart (in Greece).

[edit] Greek Chart

  • The album reached the 1st position of the Greek Charts in 2005. It is the first time ever that a "Priest" album reaches the 1st position worldwide.

[edit] Miscellanea

  • Some songs in this album reference other Judas Priest songs. For example, in the song "Demonizer", the lyrics read "Hellions the steed", a reference to the song, "The Hellion." In addition the lyrics on this song reads "The Painkiller rises again" which is a reference to the song "Painkiller". Also, in "Hellrider", the lyrics read "Time to ram it down, judgement of the Tyrant", references songs "Ram it Down" and "Tyrant". In "Eulogy", they reference Stained Class and The Sentinel.
  • "Deal with the Devil", tells the history of the band;

"Forged in the Black Country" - Black Country is a nickname of the industrial area of the Midlands/Birmingham, where the bandmates originate from. As a side note, the same reference is made in Ram It Down's "Monsters Of Rock". "Under blood red skies" - referencing to a song by that title on Ram It Down. The song is about a fight for life or death, a quite matching the situation in which the band was at its humble beginnings. "We all had a dream to realize" - their ambitions kept them detrmined to succeed despite hradships. "Driving in that Transit" - "Transit" is european slang for van, plain and simple, after the Ford Transit. For a while, while recording Rocka Rolla, the band literally lived in such a van. "Down To Holy Joe's" refers to the Church Of Holy Joseph in Walsall, which was used as a rehearsal studio for the band (as well as many other contemporary bands) The rest of the lyrics are quite self-explanatory, decribing their road to success, devotion to their music & fans & concerts. There are some other refernces to past songs such as "Took On All The World" /Take on the world.
