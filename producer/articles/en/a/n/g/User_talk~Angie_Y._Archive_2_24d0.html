[edit] Encyclopedic examples

Hi Angie: It seems to me that you continue to have difficulty identifying the boundary between the encyclopedic and the unencyclopedic. We really need to address this, because if you continue to add substantial amounts of unencyclopedic material that outweighs the good that you do with the encyclopedic material. I have just removed the example of male chauvinism that you added to Chauvinism. Gaston is a secondary character in a minor movie. He does not exemplify "male chauvinism" in the general consciousness. Most people would have to look him up to find out who he is. Because of this, he does not enlarge most reader's understanding of the nature of male chauvinism and, so, does not enhance that article. Please stop adding examples to articles. Until you have a clearer understanding of what makes a fact encyclopedic, please suggest your examples on talk pages. That way, we can discuss them first, and you can avoid becoming a problem editor. —Theo (Talk) 10:42, 10 August 2005 (UTC)

From her behavior and vocab. --User:Angie Y.

I can see that her behavior and vocabulary show that she is a spoiled brat. When you say "Veruca became the pop culture and stereotype image of the spoiled brat." do you mean that she conforms to an existing pop culture and stereotype image of the spoiled brat? Yes, she did, and other character with a bratty attitude such as hers soon followed. --User:Angie Y.

Veruca Salt follows in a tradition of such characters. She is not the first. Dahl would have read about Violet Elizabeth Bott in the Just William novels as a child, for example. Similar characters were commonplace in the instructional children's novels of the nineteenth century. If you look at the list in Spolied brat you will see that they do not all derive from Veruca Salt. Consequently, if we look again at the problematic sentence that started all this: They're also both British girls, creating the interpretation that the spoiled brat image came from London. I find that They're also both British girls is an objective fact. Creating the interpretation that the spoiled brat image came from London is not fact, however, because not everyone (or even most people) would deduce that spoiled brats were originally Londoners. Even if something seems obvious to you, the most important thing is how it appears to other people. Do you agree that the first part is a fact and the second part an opinion? —Theo (Talk) 18:42, 17 August 2005 (UTC)

Yes, I do. --User:Angie Y.

Encyclopedias do not include opinions. They only include facts. You can change an opinion into a fact by saying who holds the opinion. So "Scientology is a good thing" is an opinion while "Tom Cruise thinks that Scientology is a good thing" is a fact. One of the rules of Wikipedia is that it can contain no new ideas so we must not use anything that amounts to "I think that …". You should only include opinions that have been published in books, in magazines, or on web-sites that have something to lose if they are found to be wrong. Does this help you to understand why some of your additions get deleted as "speculation" or "opinion"? —Theo (Talk) 22:54, 17 August 2005 (UTC)

Yes, I do. --User:Angie Y.

[edit] Opinion

Today I removed "His blue suit colors and technojargon are very reminiscent of Billy Cranston, the Blue Ranger from Mighty Morphin' Power Rangers." from Super Robot Monkey Team Hyperforce Go! because I see it as opinion. I agree with your opinion that there are similarities, but encyclopedias are not about identifying such parallels unless someone else has identified the similarity in some checked source. The similarities are only important if they arise from influence and we can only record such influence if someone reputable (ideally the creator) identifies them for us. Remember that an encyclopedia is about gathering together stuff that is already known and agreed. I feel as though I spend more time scolding you than praising you and we may lose sight of the value of your contributions. I think that it is about time that I repeated that I spend so much time trying to help you to conform to policy because I am certain that your intentions are good and because I feel that, on balance, the encyclopedia is enhanced by your contributions. I am confident that you wil be an excellent contributor once you have a fuller understanding of policies. —Theo (Talk) 23:28, 18 August 2005 (UTC)

[edit] Extra info on warriors

Hi Theo: Can you add the following information in the warrior article?:

Warriors in some cultures, like the Powhatan Indians, are the chosen husbands of women, especially the daughters of emperors, kings, or chiefs.

Being a warrior, Kocoum would protect Pocahontas from any danger, as heard in the deleted scene "Dancing to the Wedding Drum", but Pocahontas doesn't want to marry him, in part because she is a free-spirited woman who loves adventure. --User:Angie Y.

According to The Real Pocahontas, marriages were often arranged by Powhatan chiefs; the princess could become a wife to any man that her father chose personally. Most men in the tribes were warriors, so my last sentence is correct. --User:Angie Y.A

If most of the men were warriors, it is not significant that Pocohontas' father chose a warrior for her. It would be significant if he chose a non-warrior, because that would be selecting from the minority. —Theo (Talk) 19:29, 25 August 2005 (UTC)

Yes, thaat's true. --User:Angie Y.

Thanks for uploading Image:CODE_LYOKO2_SCN-8.jpg. However, the image may soon be deleted unless we can determine the copyright holder and copyright status. The Wikimedia Foundation is very careful about the images included in Wikipedia because of copyright law (see Wikipedia's Copyright policy).

The copyright holder is usually the creator, the creator's employer, or the last person who was transferred ownership rights. Copyright information on images on Wikipedia is signified using copyright templates. The three basic license types on Wikipedia are open content, public domain, and fair use. Find the appropriate template in Wikipedia:Image copyright tags and place it on the image page like this: {{TemplateName}}.
