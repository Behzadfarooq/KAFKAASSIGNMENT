Angelo Poffo

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

John Angelo Poffo (born April 10, 1925) is a former professional wrestler and wrestling promoter. He ran International Championship Wrestling for a number of years, holding cards in Tennessee, Kentucky and Arkansas. He is most noted for being the father of "Macho Man" Randy Savage and "Leaping" Lanny Poffo/The Genius. He also held the world sit up record for several years, his total being 6033 in one sitting.

[edit] Career

Poffo started wrestling in 1948. He sometimes wrestled as The Masked Miser.

He formed a heel tag team with Chris Markoff, called "The Devil's Duo" and "The Graduates."

He made a few appearances in WCW in 1995 managing his son, Randy Savage, Arn Anderson, and Vader. He was once attacked by "The Nature Boy" Ric Flair, who put him in a figure 4 leg lock, .

In 1995, he was inducted into the WCW Hall of Fame as a favor to his son, Randy Savage.

[edit] Personal life

Poffo's parents were Italian Catholic immigrants.[1] He married Judy on June 5, 1949.
