Can you tell me on what basis you have that opinion about my "claims"? I do not think "criticizing" anything was a primary goal of the association. Certainly if the arbitration committee does something arbitrary it should be criticized and if a group of people can agree on the inappropriateness of their behavior that would help to improve that committee's future behavior (that is part of what advocacy is all about). However, I do not think that I ever said that that "Critiise the Arbitration committee" was why "we are here". Advocacy is, as you say, something that cannot be forced on people and it should not be organized in overly legalistic ways (though I do think that there have to be standards by which advocates operate). I feel when I say something people may be reading too much into it and are not talking to me about what I really mean because it appears to I have said. — Alex756 [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/User_talk:Alex756 talk] 15:58, 2 Mar 2004 (UTC)

Thanks for your comments. Being an advocate can mean to advocate for groups of people, not just individuals; in that way one can be advocating in a more public forum that might include mediation or arbitration. As far as becoming lobbyists I don't think that is really part of being an advocate's job here at Wikipedia; though what lobbyists do might be considered a kind of advocacy, it was not really a part of what I had in mind.
Regarding the idea of keeping Wikipedia's rules fair and responsive was a reference to Wikipedia's rules regarding the Arbitration and Mediation Committees (I thought this was obvious) because, as you say — and I agree with you completely — the main goal of the association was (and hopefully is) to help people who are faced with the rules of these committees and the dispute resolution process (i.e. you may be advocating for a particular person or you may also be advocating the way some rule make it hard for the person to be treated fairly in an effort to get the rule changed or modified for that person as well as others who will follow them in the future). Obviously (and once again maybe only to me), I don't think that the AMA should be dealing with questions of naming conventions or other content-based Wikipedia rules, but rules dealing with member interactions and disputes.
The primary goal of the AMA in my original conception of the "Office of Members' Advocates" was to be a resource center (hence the use of the word office in the name) that could help other Wikipedians faced with internecine disputes regarding volunteer behaviours. IT was not to become some kind of lobbying or politically motiviated organization with an agenda. Feel free to quote me on that. — Alex756 [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/User_talk:Alex756 talk]

I agree with Alex that the AMA should not promote lobbying or a particular agenda. The stuff on the AMA page about unilateralism and sysop tyranny was apparently added by User:The Cave Troll. I regret this problem, and would certainly welcome you back to the AMA if you wish to participate. The concept of the association is still relatively new, but its nature may make it a target for trolling behavior. The problem is that what you want is a browser glitch. I'm not sure what is the standrad in these kind of cases, but each browser handles them to its own. Let me see if I can explain it. =/

When you assign a width of 10px to the object/anchor, but the object is bigger than that size, the browser has to decide how does it render it:

  • hide all content that is outside the boundaries of the width (ie: only render what is seen in those 10px)
  • ignore the width attribute (what MSIE does, and what should be done)
  • render the object with the attributes given but show the content outside of its boundaries (what Mozilla does, and what it shouldn't do -- the glitch)
