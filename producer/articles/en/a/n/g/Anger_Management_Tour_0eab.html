Anger Management Tour

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

The Anger Management Tour is a concert tour that started in 2000 after the release of The Marshall Mathers LP. In 2005 the European part of the tour was cut short due to the exhaustion of tour founder Eminem and his addiction of sleeping pills. The tours over the years have included such artists as Eminem, D12, Xzibit, Papa Roach, Limp Bizkit (only on the inaugural Fall 2000 jaunt shortly before their fall-out with Eminem), 50 Cent, Killa-Kc, Young Buck, Mobb Deep, Tha Future, Royce Da 5'9", Murda Ma$e and more. Several dates, including an appearance at Slane Concert, were cancelled.

In 2005 a DVD of the 2002 performance in Detroit, Michigan was released and features behind-the-scenes footage as well as Eminem's performance in its entirety.
