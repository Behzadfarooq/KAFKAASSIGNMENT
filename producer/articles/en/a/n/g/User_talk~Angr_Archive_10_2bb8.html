[edit] Accent on Ìmola

You are mistaken when you say the "grave accent is never used" on Italian placenames. Indeed, in many Italian reference works (such as atlases and dictionaries), it is standard to show stress when it is not on the penult (or when the word ends in two vowels, e.g. -ia) using a grave or acute. In principle, the grave is for open vowels and the acute for closed, but this distinction is not followed systematically. Please contribute to the discussion at User talk:Macrakis/Italian accents. (I suppose there could be a better place for it...) --Macrakis 15:57, 5 February 2006 (UTC)
