Angewandte Chemie or Angewandte Chemie International Edition is a weekly peer-reviewed chemistry journal with broad scope. Its impact factor is 9.60 (in 2005), which is relatively high for a chemistry-specific journal.[1] It is the journal of the de:Gesellschaft Deutscher Chemiker (Society of German Chemists).

The "Angewandte", as it is informally called, publishes articles on all fields of chemistry. It is a weekly publication which publishes scientific reviews, highlights, communications, essays, and book reviews. Angewandte Chemie is German for applied chemistry, although this translation no longer really describes the scope of the journal. It is edited by Dr Peter GÃ¶litz, who is credited with elevating the impact and international scope of its authorship. Angewandte Chemie provides open access to author-written abstracts, but access to the graphical table of contents, with an image and editor's summary for each paper, requires a subscription.

It has been in publication since 1962, and the earlier volumes are indexed as Angewandte Chemie International Edition in English 1962 - 1997. It was originally, and is still, published as Angewandte Chemie, with articles in German and in English.
