Angelo Maffucci

Angelo Maffucci (October 27, 1847 - November 24, 1903) was an Italian pathologist born in the town of Calitri. In 1872 he received his doctorate at Naples, and later was a professor of pathology in Messina (1882), Catania (1883) and Pisa (1884). At the University of Pisa he became the school's first director of pathological anatomy, and he remained there until his death in 1903.

Maffucci is remembered for isolating the bacteria that causes avian tuberculosis. He also discovered that the avian type of tuberculosis had a different aetiology than human and bovine types. In 1881 he described a disorder characterized by enchondromatosis associated with multiple cavernous angiomas. This disorder was to become known as Maffucci's syndrome, which he documented in a article titled Di un caso encondroma ed angioma multiplo.
