Angelique Bates (Born December 1, 1979) is an American actress best known for the two seasons she served on the Nickelodeon sketch-comedy series All That.[1]

[edit] Early career

Angelique Bates got her start in a Borax commercial when she was a baby.[2] Then she landed an AT&T commercial and a co-starring role in the movie "Sweet Potato Ride". Many years later, she got the job on a new sketch comedy series.

[edit] All That

Main article: All That

In 1992, Angelique Bates got a call from her agent about the Nickelodeon show, All That. She auditioned by doing an impression of Urkel, which she would get to do various times on the show.[3]

After two seasons, she left the series when her contract ran out. [4] She did, however, return for the 100th episode, along with fellow All That alumni Katrina Johnson and Alisa Reyes.

[edit] Later career

After leaving All That, Angelique later booked a Co-Starring roles on "Strong Medicine" and "Boston Public". She also landed a few more commercials. Angelique is focusing on music. She goes by her nickname, "Alizay." Her MySpace page includes such songs as the "Numb / Encore"-sampling "Fly So High."

It is believed that she will co-star with Katrina Johnson and Alisa Reyes in the movie Sisters.

Angelique is also in the process of completing her debut album which is currently untitled. Angelique (Alizay) has teamed up with super producer Dre Ghost on this project as well. Her singles I Bet & Cali have already gained her a larger and more diverse fan base.

Although many remember her as the adorable girl from Nickelodeon (TV channel)'s All That, she's no amateur in the music business. Angelique has been seen live on stage performing at some large mainstream events with actor/recording artist H. Wood. Angelique has opened up for Big-Tyme artists such as Sugar Hill Gang, A Tribe Called Quest, Special Ed, Big Daddy Kane, Rampage (Flipmode Squad) and many more.
