[edit] Mary Poppins characters

Hi. I thought I'd explain my rationale for removing the links to the minor Mary Poppins characters. As mentioned in the above message re:Invader Zim, there is a guideline (I wouldn't call it a policy as done above) that minor characters be covered within a main article, rather than having individual articles written about them. Although a case can be made for an article on Mary Poppins, the character, by rights it would probably be seen as a duplicate of the main article. Bert could perhaps get an article -- if he was a major, ongoing character in the Poppins books, but I understand the movie character was an amalgam of several characters. The fact there are already articles on Mr. and Mrs. Banks is a bit of an anomaly, but they were major, ongoing characters in the book series so they might survive. An article on Ellen the maid or Mrs. Brill the cook would probably spark a votes for deletion process under the WP:FICT guidelines. If you still want to create articles of the minor characters, please by all means go ahead, but please make sure they have substantial content ... and perhaps be prepared to have to merge them into the main Mary Poppins article if enough people object. PS. Mary Poppins and Invader Zim aren't unique cases -- Star Wars and Star Trek characters are also undergoing similar scrutiny. Cheers! 23skidoo 20:12, 9 May 2005 (UTC)

[edit] Invader-Zim-related titles

Hi, Angie. I ran across the Category:Invader Zim characters in Fictional character#Lists of fictional characters in specific works or series, had the impression you intended the Cat to show up as a list under Simpsons, and fixed it (by adding a colon inside the brackets, as i also did just above; this creates a link ("lk") to that page, instead of putting the article into that category.) (Ignore the "summary" for that edit, which was the result of hitting Save by accident just after accidentally pasting in the wrong text.)
You may want to look at how it came out, and move it around to achieve what you actually had in mind.
I also noticed that the Cat's articles mostly have titles that end in "(Invader Zim character)"; there are two things worth saying about that:

  1. When parentheses are needed, what goes inside them only has to distinguish what kind of thing it is (in this case a character from "Invader Zim"), or what context it occurs in (in this case the "Invader Zim" stories). In this case, just specifying the context with, e.g., Tak (Invader Zim) would suffice, and the style of title that identifies what kind of thing the unparthenthesized portion is longer without any purpose. So Tak (Invader Zim) would be the better choice of title; the same shortening would apply to the other articles in the category.
  2. Parentheses are appropriate in a title (with exceptions so rare that it's hard to come up with a convincing example) only when they are needed. Dib needs them: Dib is a disambiguation page to route users who type "Dib", "dib", etc. in the box next to the "Go" button to either device-independent bitmap or Dib (Invader Zim), so Dib (Invader Zim) is the correct title. Tak (Invader Zim) is also correct, because Tak is presently about a town; Tak needs to either be renamed to Tak, Thailand with the name Tak becoming a disambiguation, or to have a "top-of-page disambiguation" added at the top of the article on the town. Zim, on the other hand, should become the title of the article presently named Zim (Invader Zim character), since there is presently no competition for either the Zim or Invader Zim title.

I have done a merge of Zim and Invader Zim articles' histories, in order to avoid the history of Zim being hidden under the redirect that was there. (A badly named article should be moved or merged, and merging and moves should not be done by cutting and pasting, or by writing a redirect over the existing article. Merging of histories and some moves require an admin (to delete some historyless redirects, and to temporarily delete an article in the process of merging histories). I would be glad, at least while you are learning the ropes, to do the appropriate deletions that you'll need, and in any case, you can request help at WP:AN or WP:RM. Ask me, if you'd like to understand what i did with the two pages i mentioned editing.
--Jerzy~t 06:29, 25 May 2005 (UTC)

[edit] Image copyrights

Hi, Angie,

It's very important that when you upload images, you provide source and copyright information, in addition to a description. Images which have no source may be deleted. If an image is copyrighted, the person who owns the copyright needs to license it under a free license (especially GFDL) for us to use it. We also might be able to use some items as Fair use. You can take a look at WP:ICT for some templates you can use to tag images. Please go back over your uploaded pictures and tag them with their source and copyright information. If you have any questions, I'll be happy to help. Thanks. kmccoy (talk) 7 July 2005 01:58 (UTC)

[edit] Dexter's Laboratory

Hi Angie: I deleted your essay on gender politics in Dexter's Laboratory because that sort of thing is not encyclopedic. I am going to analyse the section here (with my opinions/comments in <>) so that you may understand my reasoning:

There's quite a few instances of male chauvinism, sexism and male dominance on the series. <but no more than is normal for a US animated series about a nuclear family> For example, Dexter consistently refers to her as just "woman" instead of saying her name and considers her and her friends to be "bothersome" and "irritating".<a single statement in the section about Dexter to say that he is sexist would cover this> The structure of their family is also very <is it particularly extreme?> chauvinistic; the father claims superiority as head of the household, above his wife, who is depicted as the stereotypical 1950s female homemaker. To some fans of the series, Dee Dee is treated poorly by her brother as well; he considers her to be very inferior and stupid.<this is already covered in the section on Dexter> He often says "Silence!" at her <citing his precise phrasing is an undue level of detail> whenever she is speaking to him, and even created a potion to mute her forever, but instead gave her a "monster potion".

By creating a separate section about an aspect of the show that is inherent to the social structure of its subject, you unbalance the article. This is a series about the extraordinary son of an ordinary American nuclear family. To emphasize its sexism is to suggest that it is more sexist than equivalent shows. Dexter's competition with Dee Dee is one-sided sibling rivalry. The creator's awareness of gender differences and stereotyping is a source of the humor but to emphasize gender conflict rather than other stereotyping could mislead the reader. If I have not been clear in any of this, just ask any questions here. —Theo (Talk) 09:35, 30 July 2005 (UTC)

Hi. I am the New Era Outlaw, one of the people watching over the Kimi Finster entry in the Wikipedia. I thank you very much for your contribution in the page, however, I had to delete all of the changes because they were not correct pieces of information:

Also, your entries have caused several of the paragraphs in the page to be too close together, resulting in a couple of the pictures creeping over to the other section of the Wiki.

I apologize for the inconvenience. Rest assured that this was done to maintain the integrity of the Kimi Finster entry in the Wikipedia. As always, you are welcome to contribute, but ensure that your facts and entries are correct before entering them.
