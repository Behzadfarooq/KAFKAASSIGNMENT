Angelique Boyer

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Angélique Boyer (born July 4, 1988 as Angélique Monique Paulette Boyer Rousseau) is a Mexican actress, born in Saint Claude, France. In 1990, her family moved to Mexico and established their residence in the country. Monique Paulette became popularly known as Angelique, possibly because it was easier to say. At a very young age, and thanks in part to the preparation of Luis de Llano, she had the opportunity of being in the musical group Rabanitos Verdes (Little Green Radishes). At the end of the project she continued toward her dream of becoming an actress and looked for more chances to show off her talent and positive attitude; at the age of fourteen she entered the CEA, another project that helped her raise her popularity and establish her as an actress.

Two years after CEA, Angelique was cast into larger project. This time it was the Mexican television series Rebelde, which became largely popular throughout Latin America. In the program, in which she participated for three seasons, she played the role of Victoria (Vico) Paz, a young and beautiful girl with problems at home- a mother who dates younger men and tries to seduce her boyfriend, an older brother that goes out with her cute and chubby friend Celina and manipulates her, and a father who beats her regularly after he forces her to get money for his betting addictions. While on the telenovela, her character is parted of the popular girls, but is considered easy. She dated Miguel (Alfonso Herrera), during the first season. Her character goes on to date two others. First, she dated Giovanni (Christian Chávez), with whom she got stuck in a refrigerator overnight and had to cuddle with to avoid freezing, but separated from him when she thought he cheated on her. She then dated Rocco (Diego Gonzalez) and broke up with him. While filming Rebelde, She and two other costars- the characters of Celina and Jose Lujan- formed the band Citricus or C3Q'S, which only made one song- "No Me Importa" ("I don't care"); though it was only a single, the song went on to be played aired during a special concert show and was used as soundtrack during the rest of the show- when characters acted rebellious. The song became a particular favorite of many fans, but was shadowed by RBD's two albums. (RBD is made up of six other show characters and is considered the most successful and popular group of artists in Latin America.)

After the success of Rebelde, adolescent audiences have fallen for Angelique's fun, sensible, and down-to-Earth personality. Angelique applied for the show "Bailando por la Boda de mis Sueños" (Dancing for my Dream Wedding), a charity show in which less fortunate contestants are paired with celebrities in order to have their dream wedding paid for. Angelique was on the cover of Maxim in July. She is anticipated as part of Rebelde the cast for the "Rebelde: The Movie" and the upcoming television series.
