Angela Jones

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Angela Jones (born in Greensburg, Pennsylvania) is an American actress. She was born and raised in the Pittsburgh suburb of Greensburg, Pennsylvania, and she is a graduate of Point Park College in Pittsburgh.

Quentin Tarantino discovered her in the short movie Curdled (1991) directed by Reb Braddock. Tarantino was impressed with her performance and cast her in both Pulp Fiction and a 1996 remake of Curdled.

She appeared in Quentin Tarantino's popular film Pulp Fiction as Esmeralda Villalobos, the cab driver who picked up Butch (Bruce Willis) the prizefighter, after hearing that he had killed a man in a boxing match, and also hearing how he felt about it.
