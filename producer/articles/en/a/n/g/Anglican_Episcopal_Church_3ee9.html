Anglican Episcopal Church

The Anglican Episcopal Church is an Anglican church based in the western United States.

The AEC consists of three parishes in the American West, plus one each in Florida, and Alabama. It is usually considered part of the Continuing Anglican Movement. The Church was founded at St. George's Anglican Church in Ventura, California.[1]

It describes its faith as based on the 1928 Book of Common Prayer and the King James Version of the Bible. [2] The Thirty-nine Articles of Religion are accepted as authoritative.

The Church's first bishop was the Rt. Rev. Reg Hammond, now deceased. He was consecrated for the Anglican Episcopal Church by the Rt. Rev. Robert Godfrey, a former presiding bishop of the Anglican Orthodox Church. Following Bishop Hammond's death, the Church was reduced to the one parish in Ventura which then voted to ask for the episcopal oversight of two bishops of the Anglican Diocese of the Good Shepherd: the Rt. Rev. Melvin Pickering and the Rt. Rev.George Connor.

In 2006, St. George's withdrew from its association with the Diocese of the Good Shepherd along with several other parishes. Bishop Connor is now the AEC's Presiding Bishop, following an October, 2006 General Convention called to re-establish the Anglican Episcopal Church.
