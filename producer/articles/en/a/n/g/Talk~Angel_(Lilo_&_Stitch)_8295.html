[edit] Stitch's claws

Stitch's claws are not retractable. They're out all the time, like a Dog's claws. Cats claws are retractable because they only come out when the cat wants them to. Miriam The Bat 17:01, 23 December 2006 (UTC)

Please stop changing the page without explaining why. I have asked you several times to comment on this page and explain your actions. Miriam The Bat 22:02, 24 December 2006 (UTC)

Let me just make my points clear in case you decide to read the talk page.

  • Stitch's claws are not retractable because they are always visible. They are never seen going back into his paws. Stitch has paws like a dog, not a cat. Angel, if her claws are indeed retractable, would have claws like a cats.
  • Also, Angel is not a fictional Scott, since she's an alien experiment and is not from nor has ever been to Scotland. Her accent is irrelevant.

If you have any evidence to contradict my statements, then PLEASE post them so this can be resolved. This is ridiculous. Miriam The Bat 22:13, 24 December 2006 (UTC)

You mean Angel is more powerful than Stitch, cause retractable claws are useful, cause those sharped claws could heart 626. Actually, Angel's claws are only seen in the first part of her episode, when she trys to scare Lilo, but when she is fighting the Leroys, for example, the claws aren't there (and claws are more practical with fighting then with scaring), so it could have been a mistake of the episode.--Midasminus 19:02, 26 December 2006 (UTC)

Just because Angel has retractable claws doesn't make her more powerful than Stitch. As far as we know, Angel wasn't made to be a fighting machine like Stitch was. I'm not suggesting that she's more powerful at all, I'm just saying that Angel's claws might be retractable because they only appear when she's fighting. [edit] Fictional Scot? No.

I added the dispute template because the person who repetitively edits this back in to the article (the same one who thinks Stitch has retractable claws) refuses to speak to me on the talk page.

Angel is a genetic experiment who was created and born on another planet, and has very likely NEVER been to Scotland. She is NOT a fictional Scot and does not belong in the category.

Nor does she have a Scottish accent. Unless you can link me to an article or interview stating that Tara Strong did her voice acting (in a fictional language, she only speaks one single word of English) in a Scottish accent, it's wrong.

I can't believe someone could be so stubborn that they are edit warring me for this, but I guess some people get their kicks from stupid things like that. Miriam The Bat 00:51, 2 January 2007 (UTC)
