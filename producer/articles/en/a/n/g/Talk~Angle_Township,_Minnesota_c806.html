Angle Township is not simply an itty bitty town on the Northwest Angle, as you might be thinking, but rather a civil township which is the Northwest Angle. So, just considering each in a purely geographical sense, they are one in the same. The name Northwest Angle is used in Minnesota (and elsewhere I guess) to refer to this piece of land, and its claim to fame is...well ... you've read the page. [edit] No merge

I have three reasons for this.

  • They are two unique geographical names, despite the same place. Take Byzantium, Istanbul, and Constantinople for example.
  • The Northewest Angle article should refer to the information about the name, the history of the name, etc. Not about the actual place. A brief mention about the Angle should be included in the Township. IF it was to be merged it should of been done the other way.
