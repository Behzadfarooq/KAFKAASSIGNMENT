Angels with Dirty Faces is the title of the second album by UK girl group Sugababes released in August 2002. Four singles were released from it including "Freak like Me" and "Round Round", both of which debuted at number one on the UK Singles Chart.

[edit] Chart success

Angels with Dirty Faces did considerably better than its predecessor, One Touch with a highest position in the UK Album Charts of number two; it stayed in the charts for forty weeks. "Freak like Me", which sampled Gary Numan's "Are Friends Electric?", was the first released single and entered the charts at number one. "Round Round", produced by Xenomania was the second released single and also debuted in the UK Charts at number one. The third single released was a double A-side release of "Stronger" and "Angels with Dirty Faces", which reached number seven on the UK Singles Chart. The last single, "Shape", which samples Sting's single "Shape of My Heart", was the only one from the album not to achieve a position in the top ten of the UK Singles Chart; it entered at number eleven.

[edit] Uses in the media

  • "Virgin Sexy" was famously used in the Rimmel mascara commercials fronted by Kate Moss.
  • "Round Round" appeared in the soundtrack of The Guru starring Jimi Mistry and Heather Graham.
