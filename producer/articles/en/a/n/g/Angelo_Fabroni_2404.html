Angelo Fabroni (born 1732 in Tuscany; died 1803) was an Italian historian.

Angelo Fabroni was a clergyman. He was head of the University of Pisa beginning in 1769. He became an editor of Giornale de'letterati in 1771. In 1773 Fabroni published a collection of Galileo's telescopic discoveries of 1610, the anti-Copernican decree of 1616, and the activities of Prince Leopold de' Medici between 1639 and 1671. Fabroni published a second volume in 1775 of correspondence involving the trial of 1633 and Leopold's activities from the 1650s to the 1670s. Most of the published documents that he gathered were from Medici archives. This was meant to be a companion to a twenty volume biographical encyclopedia, Lives of Italians. He edited these between 1778 and 1803. Two more volumes appeared after Fabroni's death.
