Ang Mo Kio Secondary School National Cadet Corps (Air)

The Ang Mo Kio Secondary School National Cadet Corps (Air) has a long history. It started its humble beginning in 1996 and has more than a 100 members currently. NCC (Air) offers a wide variety of activities ranging from joyride rides in military aircraft such as C130 Hercules, Fokker 50, Piper Warrior and Chinook CH-47 to aviation courses like Silver Wing and Gold Wing where cadets learn more about planes. Cadets also get to go overseas to countries such as Canada, Hong Kong etc.

This Unit has won a totally of 3 Gold and 2 Silver Awards in the annual Best Unit Competition (BUC), conducted by NCC headquarters.

[edit] Recent Years

In 2005, 2 of the Cadets, MSG Tan Yi Jun and 2SG Tan Mei Yan both won the Overall Outstanding Cadet in the Specailist Course Phrase 1 and Phrase 2 respectively. MSG Tan Yi Jun was also awarded the Best Trainee in Flight 2 Air Company during the Senior Specialist Course in the same year. SSG Jocelyn Oh was awarded the Best in Footdrill Mutual Air Company in the same course.

2006 is another fulfilling year. MSG Benedict Lai was awarded the Air District Best Trainee and 1SG Koh Wei Jie was awarded the Alpha Platoon 1 Best Trainee in the prestigious 1st Advanced Drills Course (previously known as Supernumerary Course) held in 2006. 2 of the cadets, MSG Zheng Jingxian and 1SG Seitoh Baoyi were also sent overseas for overseas attachment to Pearce Airbase, Perth Western Australia, and 1 cadet SSG Koh Fei Yuan Louisa to Outward Bound School in Sabah for overseas training. Also, SSG Lee Kiang Hui Gordon managed to clinch the 5th postition in the annual Part C Shooting Competition.
