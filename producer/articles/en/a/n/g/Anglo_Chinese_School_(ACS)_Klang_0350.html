The first Anglo Chinese School (simply known as ACS) was built in Klang. It was built in the year 1893, which made it one of the oldest schools in Klang(and Selangor).

Formerly, the primary and secondary school was conjoined. As many students occupied this school later, they had to build separate blocks. Both the schools are now separated by a canteen.

[edit] History of ACS

In the year 1892, the "Pegawai Daerah Klang", Mr. Walter W Skeat and Tauke Loke Yew had taken a hard time to find help for money to build a school. They finally could rent a place at the Penghulu Mohid's house in Jalan Rembau. This school started with only 14 students and a teacher.

On March 10, 1893, this school was officially opened by Sir William Hood Treacher.

  • 1896 - This school became a British government school.
  • 1915 - This school was sent Episcopal Methodist Mission and it was named 'Anglo Chinese School'.
  • 1920 - The building that was made of wood got burnt. Nearly the whole school was destroyed.
  • 1921 - A new building was built in Jalan Raya Barat. The building was opened by 'The Honorable' Mr.Oliver Marks, resident of Selangor. The Sultan Alaeddin Sulaiman also was there on the opening of the school.
  • 1922 - The first batch took their Senior Campbridge Exam.
  • 1924 - The new school magazine, called The School Review was published.
  • 1925 - The First Klang Scouts Troop was made.
  • 1941 - Because many students came, they had to make a new building.
  • 1942-1945 (W.W.II) - The school became the head office of the Japanese army.
  • 1951 - A new Science lab was opened by DYMM Sultan Hisamuddin Alam Shah Alhaj.
  • 1957 - The new school building (1921) had been fully renovated.
  • 1957 - The first Klang Boys' Brigade was made by the help of Capten Peter Dawson and Reverend Ronald Buttler-White.
  • 1958 - The subjects Additional Mathematics and Pure Science (Physics, Chemistry, and Biology) were introduced to Form 4.
  • 1961 - The ACS Old Boys' association was registered officially, making Mr. Koh Liang Sih their president.
  • 1963 - The new 3-storey building was finished. It has 11 classrooms, one office, one teachers' room and one Geography room. It was opened by Capten Abdul Hamid, the minister of education at that time.
  • 1973 - The Parents and Teachers Association (PIBG) was created in the school hall. The first chairman was Mr. Adam bin Haji Yunus.
  • 1978 - The new 3-storey block was built with 11 classrooms and an additional block with 4 storeys which has the teachers' room, an office, and an air-conditioned library. Opened by Y.B. Dato' Musa Hitam, Minister of Education at that time.
  • 1979 - The form 6 classes were created.
  • 1980 - The Report Card day was introduced.
  • 1981 - The Mini Canteen day was held for the first time.
  • 1983 - The PIBG made a bicyle 'parking' place, tennis court, sepak takraw court, basketball court and a "serambi".
  • 2003 - Both ACS organise a grand celebration for their 110th anniversary.
