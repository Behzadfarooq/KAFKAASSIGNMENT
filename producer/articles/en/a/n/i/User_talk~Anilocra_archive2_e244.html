[edit] Destructive fishing techniques

Anilocra,

Thanks for dropping a note and offering to help! An offer I hope to make you regret... :) No, let me explain. Several months ago I revamped aquarium and took it to featured status; of course I hang around to monitor edits etc. Starting about a week ago, it started getting absolutely swamped with edits concerning the collection of wild animals for the aquarium trade. As you can see from the broad scope of the aquarium article, it can really only afford to touch briefly on the topic. I spent a few days wondering what to do about these additions, which seem well intentioned (and even well referenced!), but to my mind were out of place. So today I decided, as my edit summaries state, enough is enough! I removed almost all of the new info, chucked it into a new article I named destructive fishing techniques (perhaps not the best name in the world), linked to it from aquarium, cleaned up "my" article, and was ready to leave it at that. I'm still planning on trying to parse through the new text for any particularly nice tidbits I can work into aquarium, but as far as I'm concerned I was ready to leave destructive fishing techniques to its own devices.

Honestly, dealing with referenced text that appears that quickly, that massively, makes my head hurt! :) If you want to make that into a good article, there's plenty of material there (and I'm surmising from your note, elsewhere). I'd be happy to see it develop, and would be willing to help out, although I'm not too interested in taking the lead on that project. Also, I think there must be about a dozen newbies that seemed to sign up just to write about sustainable fishing techniques and seem well intentioned. (A curious occurrence -- my only explanation is that somebody somewhere posted to a newsgroup or message board and got a bunch of newbie participants to come in and make their point en masse.) Perhaps you could roll out the welcome wagon for them and invite them to work more on that article.

- Bryan is Bantman 23:20, May 26, 2005 (UTC)
