Anita Yuen Wing-Yi (Traditional Chinese: 袁詠儀; Simplified Chinese: 袁咏仪; pinyin: Yuán Yǒngyí; born September 4, 1971) is a Hong Kong film and television actress. She won the 1990 Miss Hong Kong Pageant.

[edit] Pageant career

At the age of 18, Anita entered the Miss Hong Kong 1990 pageant. She was a heavy favorite to win the crown from the semifinal to final. During the semifinals on August 5, 1990, she won the Miss Photogenic award. She had a semifinal score of 485, placing 3rd. The finals were held on August 21, 1990. She eventually beat out another big favorite, Helen Yung who was 1st runner up and the winner of 2 other awards. Anita later represented Hong Kong at the Miss Chinese International 1991 pageant, held on February 2, 1991. She took 1st runner up for Hong Kong. She also competed at the Miss Universe 1991 pageant in Las Vegas, Nevada. She failed to make the top 10. The pageant was won by Miss Mexico, Lupita Jones.

[edit] Movie career

Anita Yuen is best known for her role as the female lead in Derek Yee's 1993 tear-jerker C'est la vie, mon chéri (1993). She is also well remembered in her cross-gender comic role opposite Leslie Cheung in He's a Woman, She's a Man (1994). The two roles won for her the Hong Kong Academy Best Actress awards. She has also acted in a number of television serials, most notably in Taiwan. Her boyfriend of 14 years is Hong Kong television and film actor as well as singer Julian Cheung. From interviews and news reports, Yuen does not make it clear whether or not she and Cheung are married yet. In May 2006, Yuen announced that she was 3 months pregnant with Cheung's child. A son was born on November 12, 2006. His name is Morton. Just after their son's birth, Julian revealed that they were married a long time ago. They registered their union somewhere in this world but exchange their vows in a flying plane many feet up in the air.
