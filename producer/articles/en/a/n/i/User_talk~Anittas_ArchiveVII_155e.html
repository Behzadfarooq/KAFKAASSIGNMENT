Can someone, please create a new article on Sirac Dilber?

Sirac Dilber is a Turkish scientist and coordinator of the Center for Cell and Gene Therapy at the Swedish Karolinska Institute. Dilber discovered that cancer patients often lack enough NK cells. The cells are needed to fight against bacteria and cancerous tumours; but even when they are present, they cannot fight the disease effectively. Dilber and his team managed to increase the number of cells by 200, which showed in various experiments to be effective in cancer treatment. In an experiment on humans that lasted for six months, the tumour of one patient with liver cancer had completely dissapeared.

Small update for Battle of Vaslui

If someone could be bothered with this...copy the text by clicking edit, and then paste the relevant piece (the Background section) into the article.

The conflict between Stephen and Ottoman Sultan Mehmed II started when both laid claim to the historical region of Bessarabia. The region belonged at first to Wallachia, but would later succumb to Moldavian influence under Petru I of Moldavia and was possibly annexed to Moldavia in the late 14th century by Roman I of Moldavia.[1] Under Alexandru cel Bun, the region would become an integral part of Moldavia and was successfully defended in 1420 against the first Ottoman attempt to capture castle Chilia.[2] After the assasination of Bogdan II of Moldavia in 1451, Moldavia fell into civil war and the region was retaken by Wallachia. The civil war ended with the coronation of Stephen in 1457 and on June 22, 1462, Stephen made an attempt to retake the castle of Chilia from the shared Hungarian-Wallachian rulership (see the Night Attack). The siege failed and Stephen was wounded in his foot for life by artilery fire.[3] In November, the same year, Mehmed invaded Wallachia and tried to subdue it. Stephen's cousin, Vlad III Dracula, repelled the invasion, but after the Ottomans retreated, the boyars allied with Dracula's half-brother, Radu the Handsome, who in turn served the Sultan. Dracula fled to Transylvania where he was imprisoned by King Matthias Corvinus of Hungary and the boyars installed Radu as Prince of Wallachia. Three years later, between January 22 and January 26, Stephen successfully besieged Chilia. Corvinus reacted to Stephen's newfound ambitions by invading his country, but suffered a severe defeated at the Battle of Baia in 1467. Since Chilia was technically still counted as part of Wallachia, the Sultan, having subdued most of Wallachia and made it his vassal, made claims to the town and asked Stephen to handle it back to Wallachia.

References:

  • Spinei, Victor. Moldavia in the 11th-14 Centuries, Romania 1986.
  • Babinger, Franz. Mehmed the Conqueror and his time ISBN 0-691-01078-1
