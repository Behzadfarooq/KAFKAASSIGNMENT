"Animal I Have Become" is the first single from Three Days Grace's CD One-X. Unlike most singles, "Animal I Have Become" wasn't released in stores, and only had one track.

The video, directed by Dean Karr, features singer Adam Gontier as the main character. Gontier is seen looking into mirrors, and other reflective objects, seeing himself as a monster. Many believe that this video is a reflection of Gontier trying to figure out who he is. Gontier has said in an interview on the Three Days Grace website that, while addicted to alcohol, he had become abusive and angry, and had no idea who he was anymore. It is believed that he wrote "Animal I Have Become" based on these events. However, comic fans say that the lyrics of the song also have a similarity to that of the tormented Bruce Banner struggling to control the Incredible Hulk from within himself, and Science Fiction and more specifically Star Wars fans might relate it to the fall to the Dark Side of the Force that some Jedi experience in many of the movies and video games.

The song is featured on the video game WWE Smackdown vs Raw 2007.

Also, The Real World: Denver's Jenn appears in the video as Adam's "love interest".

"Animal I Have Become" has been considered as the most used entrance song in WWE SmackDown vs Raw 2007.
