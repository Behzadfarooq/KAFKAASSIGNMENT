Animation On Display is a multigenre convention in San Francisco, California, containing animation screenings, cosplay, panels, guests, vendors, fandom, J-Rock, and more in reference to East Asian culture.[citation needed]

Formerly Anime Overdose[1] and then AOD: The San Francisco Animation Convention,[2] Animation On Display is managed by the Federation for the Promotion of Animation, and is a non-profit organization. It is the goal of the FPA to increase awareness and understanding of the art of animation and its related interests in a fun and exciting family-oriented format, and to enable those interested in animation to meet and share their interests.[citation needed]

[edit] History

The Anime Overdose convention began in 2003 at Santa Clara University.[3] As the convention expanded, it was moved to the Nob Hill neighborhood of San Francisco.[1] Several smaller animation-related groups have come together to provide management for AOD.[citation needed]
