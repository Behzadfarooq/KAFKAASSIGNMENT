News archive

2006

January

  • January 13: VIZ Media announces that Simon and Schuster will handle its sales and distribution in the United States
  • January 27: TOKYOPOP announces layoffs of 10 personnel globally, including Steve Kleckner, VP of Sales; as well as a senior editor of their amerimanga titles. (source)

February

  • February 15: Anime News Network reports that the Canadian National Child Exploitation Coordination Centre has issued a fact sheet regarding anime, manga, anime games and other aspects of otaku culture that does not differentiate between ecchi/hentai and other genres within the two mediums. (source)
  • February 18: The Japan Times reports that a museum for the late mangaka Fujiko F. Fujio, creator of Doraemon, will be built in Kawasaki, Kanagawa. The museum will be built in the city's Tama district and is expected to be complete by 2010. (source)

March

  • March 1: Anime News Network reports that manga writer Mamoru Sasaki passed away on February 24 at the age of 69 due to pancreatic cancer. Sasaki's work included for TV series Ultraman, Jūdō Itchokusen and others. He also wrote the scripts for Otoko Do-Aho! Koshien (drawn by Shinji Mizushima) and Soldier Boy (drawn by Yumiko Kawahara). Sasaki also worked with Nagisa Oshima and wrote the scripts for some of his films.
  • March 4: The Daily Yomimuri reports that Japanese courts are using manga to notify the public of an upcoming change to the judicial system. Starting in 2009, Japanese citizens will sit as "lay judges" alongside professional judges during criminal cases. The Supreme Court of Japan has teamed with Kenshi Hirokane, creator of Jomu Shima Kosaku, to show two chapters of the manga in which an employee of the company depicted in the manga would be selected to sit as a lay judge. (source)
  • March 9: The Mainichi Daily News reports that Japan's politicians may be turning to manga for inspiration in their professions. The article mentions Foreign Minister Taro Aso, whose rumored reading of Golgo 13 have contributed to his "decidedly undiplomatic diplomatic skills — he has been widely criticized overseas, especially in a scathing New York Times editorial that admonished the way he handles ties with other Asian countries — have been dismissed as highly unlikely." The article continues into the history of how Diet politics and manga intertwine. (source)
  • March 10: MTV.com discusses the increasing trend of yaoi manga in the US and how it exists as a stable genre on the Japanese scene. (source)
  • March 16: In a press release, 4Kids Entertainment announced their fiscal 2005 year end results.[1] For the fiscal year ending December 31, 2005, 4Kids reported $5.1M in profit on $86.7M in sales, down from $12.7M in profit on $103.3M in sales the previous year. Most of the shortfall came from 4th quarter sales, where revenue dropped $7.2M to $26.9M, down from $4.5M to $34.1M.

    Most of the shortfall was attributed to decreased revenue from the Yu-Gi-Oh!, Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles, and Pokemon licenses. CEO Al Khan noted that the case of Yu-Gi-Oh!, the decrease in sales is largely due to a slow transition from Yu-Gi-Oh! to Yu-Gi-Oh! GX; while sales of the older products have been maturing, sales of newer items are doing briskly but are taking longer to get to market. Offsetting the losses were increased profits from The Winx Club, One Piece, and Cabbage Patch Kids.

    4Kids TV ratings rose 16%, but revenue dropped $0.6M to $24.1M, due to a shortfall in advertising revenue. Kahn stated that 4Kids expects broadcast profit to improve in 2006 and 2007 due to increased ad revenue because of the increased ratings. Additionally, 4Kids has renegotiated its deal with Fox for two more years at $20M a year, down from $25M for previous years.

    Kahn stressed that in the future, 4Kids wants to focus on properties it owns vice licenses, which in the latter case expire or have restrictions placed on them by licensors. Current properties that 4Kids is focusing on include Viva Pinata, a co-production with Microsoft; and Chaotic, a fully-owned property.

    4Kids also announced that it has sold all its shares in Pokémon USA to the remaining shareholders for $1M. 4Kids closed the year with $113.M in cash and investments and $30.5M in liabilities, but no bank or loan debt.

    When asked about the results of the year, Kahn said he was "[e]xtraordinarily disappointed & furious at the results of this year." (source)

April

  • April 3: The Mainichi Daily News reports that Italian authorities have raided three stores in Rome and Bologna in an investigation into piracy of anime DVDs, the first such undertaking in Europe. Approximately 400 titles were confiscated, with an estimated value of 900,000 EUR ($1.1M) (source)
  • April 12: The Mainichi Daily News reports that an otaku stayed in a manga cafe in Gifu after having lived in the cafe for over a month. Kiyoshi Ikeda, 37, was reported as having told police, "I ran out of money and I thought I'd stay there until I got kicked out." (source)
  • April 14: Industry website ICV2 reports that the San Bernadino County Board of Supervisors has ordered the county's libraries to remove the scholarly text Manga: Sixty Years of Japanese Comics from circulation. Board Chairman Bill Postmus announced the order, calling the book "obscene comics" and adding, "That book is absolutely inappropriate for a public library and as soon as I was made aware of it yesterday, I ordered it to be removed immediately." The move comes after a complaint at the county library in Victorville, California, where a 16-year-old checked the book out of the library's adult section. (source)

July

  • July 13: The Japanese magazine CoroCoro is reporting that a new Pokémon series, titled Pokémon Diamond & Pearl, will begin airing on TV Tokyo this fall. (source)

    Industry website ICv2 ranks Gen Fukunaga, President & CEO of FUNimation Entertainment, as the most powerful person in the US anime instustry. (source)

    Three former executives from FUNimation have announced the formation of Illumitoon Entertainment along with the acquisition of Beet the Vandel Buster. (source)
  • July 15: Adult Swim has announced at the annual Television Critics Association that Inuyasha the Movie: Swords of an Honorable Ruler will make its debut on August 12, 2006 at 11:00 p.m. (ET, PT) and that Bleach and Trinity Blood will begin airing on September 9, 2006 at 11:30 p.m (ET, PT) and 12:00 a.m. (ET, PT) respectively. InuYasha the Movie: Fire on the Mystic Island will also debut on December 23, 2006 at 11:00 p.m. (ET, PT). (source)

    Anime News Network reports that Toonami Jetstream is now online and is serving free full length episodes of Naruto, Hikaru no Go, MÄR and The Prince of Tennis.
  • July 18: AnimeNation reports that Digital Manga will hosts two Los Angeles area autographing sessions with yaoi manga artist Makoto Tateno on July 30, 2006 at Neotype Station from 1-3 PM, and at Epic Comics from 6-8 PM. Tateno will also appear as a Guest of Honor at Otakon 2006 during the first weekend in August. (source)
  • July 19: AnimeNation reports that plans for a second Black Lagoon anime television series has been confirmed. (source)
  • July 22: FUNimation announced at the San Diego Comic-Con that a redub Crayon Shin-chan will receive a test run on Adult Swim in August. (source)

August

  • August 17: Toonami Jetstream reports 9.5 million video streams during the first four weeks after its launch with 725,000 unique visitors per week. (source)
  • August 18: Anime News Network reports that Diamond Comic Distributors will no longer distribute FUNimation DVDs in Canada. (source)

December

  • December 1: ICv2 reports that the 2007 Sundance Film Festival will include a screening of the live-action feature, Bugmaster, based on the Mushishi manga series and directed by Katsuhiro Otomo. (source)
  • December 3: Anime News Network reports that Naoki Urasawa's 20th Century Boys will be made into a live-action movie for release in 2008. (source)
  • December 28: MSN names Samurai 7 the best new anime of 2006. (source)
