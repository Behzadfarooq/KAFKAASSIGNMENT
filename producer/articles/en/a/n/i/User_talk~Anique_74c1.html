Wikipedia is a free-content encyclopedia written in 250 languages by thousands of users like you and me from all walks of life. As a member of the Welcoming Committee, I would like to say thanks for being a part of this project.

Here are some links to help you, the new user, on your Wikipedia journey:

  • Introduction
  • Sandbox
  • Editing a page
  • Bootcamp
  • Manual of Style
  • Five pillars

Whether you're new to the project or have helped us for a while, remember to use edit summaries when you're done with a page. Summaries are important because, with those, other editors will understand what you have done with the content. REMEMBER: think twice before clicking "Save page" below the editing box, since whatever you write in the summary box will permamently go in the history of any page. (Spellchecking is important; you cannot change the summary data after you have finished editing.)

Remember, also, to sign your name with four tildes (~~~~) or by pressing this button () above the box, whenever leaving comments on talk pages.

If you want to know how you're doing on Wikipedia, feel free to check out your list of contributions.

If you don't know how to do something, type {{helpme}} on your talk page, and administrators will come to solve your problems.

After you've stayed here for a while, you might want to take an editor review, or, even better, take a chance at adminship.

Wherever the journey takes you, I wish you the best of luck and respect on Wikipedia. If you have any questions about my expertise or this site, you can e-mail me or talk to me, and I will see about the situation as best as possible.

In the meantime, happy editing!
