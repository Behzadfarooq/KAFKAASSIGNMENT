Anomalopus is a genus of smallish smooth-scaled burrowing skinks from Australia. These lizards all have a variation of the common name "worm skink" and are found in the eastern half of Australia.

The Five-clawed Worm-skink is a dark brown reptile growing to 10 cm, similar to Anomalopus leuckartii but with a distinctly didactyle (having two digits) hindlimb, a central dark spot within each individual scale and is yellow-green below with darker flecks (Cogger 2000).

[edit] Species information of genus Anomalopus

  • Anomalopus brevicollis (Greer and Cogger,1985), Short-necked Worm-skink, NE Australia.
  • Anomalopus gowi ( Greer and Cogger,1985), Speckled Worm Skink, NE Australia.
  • Anomalopus lentiginosus, Worm skink.
  • Anomalopus leuckartii (Weinland, 1862), Two-clawed Worm-skink, Eastern Australia.
  • Anomalopus mackayi ( Greer and Cogger,1985), Five-clawed worm skink, eastern Australia.
  • Anomalopus pluto (Ingram, 1977), Cape York Worm-skink, NE Australia.
  • Anomalopus swansoni ( Greer and Cogger,1985), Punctate Worm-skink, east coastal Australia.
  • Anomalopus verreauxi (Dumeril & Dumeril, 1851), Three-Clawed Worm skink.
