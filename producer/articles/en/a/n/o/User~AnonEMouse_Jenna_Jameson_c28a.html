[edit] Controversies

As the most prominent female porn star, Jameson often represents pornography and its controversies to the world.

An interview with Jenna Jameson contained in the 1999 Abercrombie & Fitch A&F Quarterly was one of the reasons prompting Michigan Attorney General Jennifer Granholm and Illinois Lieutenant Governor Corinne Wood to speak out against the hybrid magazine-catalog.[1] The campaign was joined by parents and Christian conservative groups, and got the Quarterly removed from shelves and eventually canceled in 2003.[2]

In November 2001, the venerable Oxford Union debating society invited Jameson to come to London to argue against the proposition "The House Believes that Porn is Harmful."[3] She has said "There are very few times when I get really, really nervous but this was certainly one of them."[4] and wrote in her diary at the time, "I feel like I am going to be out of my element, but, I could never pass this chance up... it's a once in a lifetime thing."[5] In the end, she won the debate 204 to 27.[6]

In February 2003, Pony International planned to feature her as one of several porn stars in advertisements for athletic shoes. This was attacked by Bill O'Reilly of Fox News in an editorial called "Using Quasi-Prostitutes to Sell Sneakers", calling porn stars inappropriate role models for teens.[7] In response, The Harvard Crimson proposed a boycott of O'Reilly and Fox News.[8] Jameson herself fought back with a sarcastic email to the show, writing:

“ I hope Bill understands the difference between a porn star and a hooker. I assume he has done some research on the subject because he requested some of my videos after we finished taping my appearance. I imagine he wanted them for professional reasons.[9] ”

In February 2003, when Pony controversially hired Jameson to promote athletic shoes, advertising expert Donny Deutsch commented on The O'Reilly Factor that "A Nike or Adidas, a much more established company, would never do it."[10] In April 2006, Jameson was the star of a Video Podcast ad for Adidas, advertising Adicolor shoes by playing a provocative game of Whack A Mole.[11][12]

The controversy around Jameson's profession has even affected those close to her. On November 12, 2006, her boyfriend Tito Ortiz was scheduled to be the guest of honor at the United States Marine Corps "birthday" ball at the Marine Corps Air Station Miramar in San Diego. When the Corps found he was planning to bring Jameson as his guest, they called her appearance inappropriate, and asked him to come without her. When he refused, they cancelled his appearance.[13][14]
