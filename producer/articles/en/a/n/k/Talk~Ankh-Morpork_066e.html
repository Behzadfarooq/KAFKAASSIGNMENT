Where does the figure of AM proper being one mile in diameter come from? That sounds awfully tiny for the Disc's largest city, and I distinctly recall a passage in Gaurds! Guards! in which Colon describes it as being eight miles wide... 20:55, 30 September 2005 (UTC)

In a thread on alt.fan.pratchett, Terry said, "The centre shown in the Mappe is about a mile across". It's the Disc's largest city because there's so much of it outside the city walls, which isn't "AM proper" in that sense. (It's kind of like the difference between London and the City of London.) --Daibhid C 16:47 5 Nov 2005 (UTC)

[edit] owl

is it merely coincidence that the morpork is a type of owl in new zealand, or does it have some particular significance? —The preceding unsigned comment was added by 202.147.43.82 (talk • contribs) .

I believe it is intentional. The small hooked cross being held by the owl (Morpork) is called an Ankh. Also, the description of the Coat of Arms says "...and crested with a morpork holding an ankh...". -=# Amos E Wolfe talk #=- 08:05, 8 August 2006 (UTC)
Is it possible that the owl was named after the city? And then Pratchett used it officially as part of the crest? After all, he does have an extinct species of turtle named after him. The Kinslayer 14:48, 22 September 2006 (UTC)
I'm pretty sure the owl was named long before the books, probably (as with most NZ birds) after the noise it makes. I also recall reading (possibly in the Annotated Pratchett File) that Pratchett came up with an interesting set of syllables then learnt there was an owl by that name and used it on the crest. Daibhid C 22:57, 10 October 2006 (UTC)

[edit] London Underground connection

As far as I can tell, the London Underground connection in the History section is pure speculation. Also, the mine sign in Thud! (eye with a tail) does not look like the London Underground symbol (circle with a horizontal line through it) so there goes that connection. I'm going remove that paragraph - feel free to revert if you can provide a decent source. YggdrasilsRoot 04:07, 12 January 2007 (UTC)

I think the conclusion is a fair one (theres a but coming though). Anyone who has followed Pratchetts work can see where he's going at the end of Thud, but until this crops up in a book it's just specualtion (however strong the likelihood). Incidently Yggdrsil, wrong mine sign. The one one that looks like the London Underground is the one mine sign fir 'mine': A circle with a horizontal line through the middle of it. Yiour thinking of the wrong sign. The Kinslayer 09:13, 12 January 2007 (UTC)
Yeah, I reread Thud! and found the passage the poster was talking about with the "Mine" sign, which is not the "Following Dark" sign. I think it's only mentioned the one time, while the eye-in-a-cirle-with-a-tail gets mentioned over and over again, so that's what stuck in my mind. I still think that while the whole Underground connection might be possible or even probable speculation, it's still speculation and doesn't belong in that section. cheers, YggdrasilsRoot 04:22, 19 January 2007 (UTC)
