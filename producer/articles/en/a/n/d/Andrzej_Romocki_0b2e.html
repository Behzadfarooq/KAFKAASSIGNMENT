Andrzej Romocki codename: Morro (April 16, 1923 in Dziektarzewo, Poland - September 15, 1944 in Warsaw, Poland) was a Polish Scoutmaster (harcmistrz) and captain of the AK-Szare Szeregi. Romocki died in the Warsaw Uprising in 1944. He was the commander of the Rudy Company and, after 31st August, the whole of the Zośka Battalion. He was probably killed by accident, by soldiers of People's Army of Poland.

[edit] Wola and Starówka

Zośka Battalion was fighting in the most difficult areas of Warsaw during the uprising. At the beginning, Batalion were in Wola, where the Germans first attacked the river, to prevent Polish insurgents from merging with the Red Army. Zośka liberated Jews from the Gęsiówka internment camp. After August 11th, when the Wola fell, Romocki and all the Radosław Regiment entered Starówka, where they fought valiantly for the next three weeks.

[edit] Merge

By the end of August 1944 the insurgents in Warsaw were in a very difficult position. They tried to defend Stare Miasto and to not allow the Germans to get to the river. Their major problem was that Starówka had been surrounded for three weeks, meaning a lack of food, ammmunition and weapons. The siege also meant that civilians had to live in very crowded conditions. In this situation, the Poles decided to attack the German position between Śródmieście and the Old Town.

The attack began on the night of August 30. However, the strike was poorly prepared and not well-coordinated. Only one Polish unit got to the Śródmieście. It was the Zośka battalion. But all companies lost most of their soldiers, so Romocki, despite being injured in the attack, became commander of the whole battalion.

[edit] Czerniaków

After Warsaw Old Town fell on September 2, all the best units of AK in Warsaw were transported to the Czerniaków. It was the last district in Warsaw near to the Wisła, which wasn't occupied by the Germans. After almost two weeks, the first units of LWP landed there, in spite of Stalin's ban. It is possible that they killed Romocki - he was disguised in a German uniform, like most of insurgents.
