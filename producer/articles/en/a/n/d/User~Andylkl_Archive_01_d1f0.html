[edit] re: striking out VfD votes

In a recent VfD discussion, you asked me not to remove the strike-out of a vote that you found to have been made in bad faith. I briefly replied in the discussion thread but also wanted to reply to you here.

According to WP:GVFD, you did the right thing by creating a sub-bullet to the comment showing your findings about the user and his/her attempt to spoof a user account. That sub-bullet was sufficient to make sure that the deciding admin recognizes that the vote was made in bad faith. Every deciding admin I know would see your comment and discount the anonymous vote.

Unfortunately, our experience on the VfD discussion pages has shown that the extra step of striking out the invalidated vote does more harm than good on average.

  • The troll who made the invalid vote responds with accusations of "censorship" and "disenfranchisement". Everyone tries to ignore the ranting but it wears on you after a while. (Oddly, the trolls don't seem to react nearly so badly to the sub-bullet even though it is even more damning to their claim. I've never figured out why.)
  • Meanwhile, users who are relatively new to the VfD process look at the page and see the stricken votes and don't understand the practice or the standard that led to the strike-out. They assume that VfD is an even more hostile and adversarial place than it already is. They react defensively and we spend time in damage control rather than in finding and evaluating facts.

I hope that explains why we said what we did in the Guide to Votes for Deletion. Take care. Rossami (talk) 21:42, 7 Apr 2005 (UTC)
