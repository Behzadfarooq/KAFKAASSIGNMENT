"And Then We Kiss" is a 2006 promo-single by American pop singer Britney Spears, from her 2005 remix album B in the Mix, The Remixes. It wasn't officially released, but received airplay in Asia. The song was originally recorded in 2003 for Spears' fourth studio album, "In The Zone", but did not make the final cut. So it was used two years later as a bonus track for her first remix album. The track was composed by Barry P., Spears, and Taylor Michael Jarvis. On the date of October 31st, 2005 the song leaked onto the internet. The original version of "And Then We Kiss" was never officially released, only the Junkie XL Remix was used as the track's main version.

[edit] Reviews

  • Rolling Stone commented that: "And Then We Kiss" brings a simpatico blend of symphonic strings and dance-rock guitars. [1]
  • CDUniverse called "And Then We Kiss" dreamy.
  • Blender magazine, in its latest issue, is quoted as saying Britney's And Then We Kiss, which was never officially released as a single, is "sexy" and an "instant classic". The track is also Blender's must download of the month.

Fans on Britney's fan sites, have praised the song, saying it is some of Britney's best work but others feel the sound is too over-produced. The track has also received positive reception from electronica music fans for its techno-dance sound[citation needed].

[edit] Chart performance

"And Then We Kiss" was never released anywhere, but surprisingly appeared on the Hot Dance Radio Airplay which is a great accomplishment. It also charted in Asia. The song has achieved something none of Spears' other singles has achieved; And Then We Kiss remained for 13 consecutive weeks at the #1 position in the Israeli 99ESC Top 20, a popular local radio show in Israel. Due the fact the song was #1 for three whole months, it was removed from the chart. The song also received some airplay in Mexico where it reached #74.

Although the track was never released as a single in Australia, it managed to get to #1 on most radio stations.
