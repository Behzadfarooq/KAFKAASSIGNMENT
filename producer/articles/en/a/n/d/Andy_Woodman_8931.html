Andy Woodman

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Andrew John Woodman (born August 11, 1971) is currently a youth team goalkeeping coach at West Ham United Football Club, where he joined in July 2006.

Andy Woodman has played for a number of clubs in his career. He started out at Crystal Palace in the 89/90 season and stayed with them for several years, until 93/94.He then moved to Exeter City F.C.. His spell at Exeter was cut short following two red cards in quick succession, one against Leyton Orient for violent conduct, and secodly against Colchester United in the FA Cup, for deliberate handball outside the area, when after making a total hash of a back pass he dicided to dive on the ball with both hands. Exeter fans never forgave him for this, and when he later played in goal for Northampton in a home game with Exeter he was forced to endure repeated chants of "We remember Colchester" from the away fans behind his goal.

Following his red cards he moved to Northampton Town F.C. which lasted until 98/99. Woodman is regarded as something of a cult figure at Sixfields after this time there, and has been granted a testimonial byt the club, to take place in summer 2007. He was also on loan to Brentford F.C. during this time, and moved there on a free during 98/99. While at Brentford he was loaned to several clubs; Peterborough United F.C. (99/00), Southend United F.C. (00/01) and Colchester United F.C (00/01). He joined Colchester on a free transfer in the 01/02 season, and was loaned to Oxford United F.C in that season. This was followed by a move to Stevenage Borough F.C in 04/05, as well as Redbridge F.C., Thurrock and a trial at Torquay United F.C.. He joined Rushden & Diamonds F.C. in the 05/06 season, but unfortunately injured his Achilles tendon early on in the season, which ruled him out for a number of months. In the spring of 2006 he was appointed Assistant Manager at Rushden and Diamonds.

He is a close friend of Gareth Southgate, with whom he has written a book about life in the Youth ranks of Crystal Palace, their first club. The book is called Woody & Nord: A Football Friendship, and describes an enduring friendship forged in the Palace youth team that has survived Southgate and Woodman's wildly differing fortunes in the professional game. The book won the Sporting Book of the Year Award for 2004 from the National Sporting Club.

His highest claim to fame was being goalkeeper for Northampton Town when they won the Division Three playoffs in 1997; a stark contrast to Gareth Southgate's achievements - regular England appearances plus League Cup winner's medals with Aston Villa F.C. and Middlesbrough F.C..
