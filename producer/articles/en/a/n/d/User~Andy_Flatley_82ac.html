Andrew Flatley was born on March 15th, 1993. Since then he has had an interesting life making friends with Neel Radhakrishnan in Year 1 ( English school years) of Ellison Boulters Primary School. He has gone on to become very clever and a great sportsman. He has shown these talents more then ever since he joined Queen Elizabeth's Grammar School, Horncastle where he now plays tennis for the school team. He is also a member of the National Academy for Gifted and Talented Youth.

[edit] Cricket

Andrew plays cricket for Scothern Cricket Club (his village club) in the U15â€™s squad. He is predominantly a medium pace swing bowler as well as an excellent batsman.

In July 2006 he made his debut against Sleaford Cricket Club; hitting 1 run off 1 ball as he came on to bat with 2 balls remaining; however, he didn't get to bowl as he is 1/2 a year younger than everyone else in his league. As the 11th man this was an impressive feat.

Since then he has played many more games for his club and for the Year 9 school team. The team played a tournament against two other local schools, one of which is their biggest rival; at first he wasn't selected for the team, but when a player was forced to drop out he was snapped up by the captain and opened the bowling in both matches. He had taken 5 wickets by the time the tournament had finished and he was then later selected for the mixed year 9 and 10 team.

[edit] Tennis

Andrew is an avid tennis player; with the majority of his life revolving around it. He has been playing since he was 4 (9 years) and has had a total of 10 coaches at 3 different clubs. Of all of these his favourite coach is Charlie Pick, because he was taught by him for 1 and a half years and because he was the first person to enter him in a tournament. Andrew's favourite club is his current one, Eastgate Tennis Club, Lincoln; closely followed by DLBW (also in Lincoln) as this is where his favourite courts are and where he has spent most of his life playing. Andrew is currently ranked 20th in Lincolnshire [1] and is aiming to be within the top 15 by the end of 2007.

Andrew plays tennis for the QEGS Tennis Team, and has played a total of 6 singles and 6 doubles matches for the team. The team plays against other local schools causing rivalries between the teams. Andrew has done his bit to help his school become one the favourite local teams by having a win/loss record of 10/2. This is an impressive achievement especially as he has never lost a school singles match.
