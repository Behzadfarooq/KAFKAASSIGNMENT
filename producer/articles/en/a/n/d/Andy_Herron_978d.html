Andy Herron (born Andy Francisco Herron Aguilar on March 2, 1978 in Limón, Costa Rica) is a Costa Rican football (soccer) player, currently playing for the Columbus Crew of Major League Soccer.

Before joining MLS and the Chicago Fire in late in 2004, Herron played two years with Costa Rican Club Sport Herediano, which he led to Costa Rica's 2004 Clausura, by leading the Costa Rican league with 12 goals and 13 assists. After two and a half seasons in Chicago, he was sent to Columbus for the second pick in the 2007 MLS SuperDraft, which turned out to be Bakary Soumare.

Herron has also played for the Costa Rica national team, registering 4 goals in 17 games with the team.
