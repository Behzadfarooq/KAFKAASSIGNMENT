Andrew Thomas (Andrew P. Thomas) (born 1966) is the Maricopa County, Arizona, County Attorney (Republican).

[edit] Current Position

Andrew Thomas has been the prosecuting attorney for Maricopa County, Arizona since 2005.

[edit] Early life, career, and family

Born in Long Beach, California in 1966, Thomas grew up mostly in the Ozarks of southern Missouri. He received his B.A. in Political Science from the University of Missouri in 1988 and his law degree from Harvard Law School in 1991.

After graduating from law school, Thomas moved to Arizona to work for a law firm in Phoenix, where he practiced civil litigation. In 1994, he left the private practice of law to serve as an Assistant Attorney General for Arizona. Next, Thomas served as Deputy Counsel and Criminal Justice Policy Advisor to the Governor of Arizona. After serving in the Governor's Office, Andrew became the Chief Attorney at the Arizona Department of Corrections.

Thomas is married to Ann Estrada Thomas, and has four children. [1]

[edit] Maricopa County Attorney

In 2004, Thomas ran successfully for Maricopa County Attorney. He took office in January, 2005. Prior to heading the office, Thomas was a Deputy County Attorney in 2003. As a Deputy prosecuted numerous cases both in the Pretrial and Juvenile Divisions.

Andrew Thomas has focused on the rights of victims, adopting tough policies related to violent crime, child exploitation, identity theft and repeat offenders. The successful prosecution of human smugglers and illegal immigrants who conspire to enter the United States has set a standard for local law enforcement in combating the epidemic of illegal immigration. He helped draft and led the fight for Proposition 100, which ended the right to bail for illegal immigrants accused of certain felonies, and Proposition 301, which toughened sanctions for abuse of methamphetamines. In 2006, the voters of Arizona approved both measures by a wide margin. [2]
