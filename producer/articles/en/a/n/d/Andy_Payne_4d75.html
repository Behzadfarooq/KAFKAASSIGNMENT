Andy Hartley Payne (born November 16, 1907, died December 1977) was the winner of the Transcontinental Footrace staged in 1928. He ran the 3,423.5 mile (5,509.6 km) route from Los Angeles to New York City, much of it along Route 66, in 573 hours, 4 minutes, 34 seconds, (84 days) averaging 6 miles per hour.
