Anders Frandsen

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Anders Frandsen is a Danish musician, singer, actor and TV presenter.

Anders Frandsen is not an educated actor, but he appeared in theatre performances during the 80'tys. He later became a TV presenter on Danish Kanal 2, alongside Camilla Miehe-Renard.

In 1991, he really broke through when he won Danish Melodi Grand Prix (The Danish national final for the Eurovision Song Contest with the song "Lige Der Hvor Hjertet Slår", composed by Michael Elo. The song didn't do well at the Eurovision Song Contest 1991, and ended 19th, with only 8 points. That didn't hurt his popularity however, and the following year at the Danish national final, he was the host.

Then he became a TV host on TV3 for the next many years. He appeared on lots of shows like "Knald eller Fald" (a dating program), "Stjerneskud" (a talent competition for look-a-likes), and he also presented the network's morning TV. In 1997, he slowly disappeared from tv again.

He later resurfaced at the Danish national final in 2001, presenting one of the songs, and he also guest appeared in an episode of Ørnen (a Danish TV series) in 2004. In 2005, he guest appeared in an episode of the Danish TV show Twist & Shout, on TV2 Charlie.
