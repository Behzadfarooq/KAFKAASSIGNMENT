[edit] Medzhybizh

The article informs, that the name was Polish. It was rather Ukrainian. Xx236 11:57, 6 July 2006 (UTC)

It sounds to me as a Polish word suited to Ukrainian phonetics. The original Polish version might be Międżybórz or something like it. The Polish ó usually corresponds to Ukrainian і (Like Lwów-Львів, Kraków-Краків, etc.), hence Меджибіж (Medzhybizh) in Ukrainian.--AndriyK 13:59, 6 July 2006 (UTC)

How can a Ukrainian village/town have a Polish name? Xx236 14:01, 6 July 2006 (UTC)

I do not know the history of this particular town, but in that part of Ukraine there are a lot of villages with ethnic Polish population. For instance there was a village Богудзєнька(Bohudzen'ka). (It was renamed by Soviets because it means "Gratitude to God" in Polish.) One should not be surprised with Polish names in this region.--AndriyK 14:18, 6 July 2006 (UTC)
There is/was indeed an essential Polish community in the town (see the photo of the Catholic church [34]). Still the name means likely not "between forests" (as it's stated in the article) but rather "between Buhs": the town is located between two rivers Southern Buh(Boh) and Bozhok(wich means "small Boh"). It used to have name Межибоже (Mezhybozhe), which sounds more Ukrainian than Medzhybizh.--AndriyK 14:41, 6 July 2006 (UTC)
