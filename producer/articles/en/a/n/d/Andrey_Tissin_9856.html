Andrey Tissin

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Andrey Tissin (Russian: Андрей Тиссин), born 5 July 1975, is a Russian flatwater canoe racer. As a member of Russia's K4 kayak crew he won the 500m world championship title in 2001.

Zarubin was three times a European champion, in 1999 (K4 200m and 500m) and 2001 (K4 500m). His most recent success came in the 2004 European Championships where he won the K4 500m bronze medal.

Tissin lives in Krasnodar. He is 1.86 m (6'1") tall and weighs 90 kg (198 lbs).

[edit] World Championship Medals

  • Gold K4 500m 2001 Poznań, Poland 1:21.772
  • Silver K4 500m 1999 Milan, Italy 1:22.454
  • Bronze K4 500m 1998 Szeged, Hungary 1:25.971
