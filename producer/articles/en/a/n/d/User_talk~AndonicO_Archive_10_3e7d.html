[edit] Assessing

Well...

  • Defining articles of the strategy genre should be top (strategy game, risk, starcraft).
  • Important games (spawned a series, changed the genre, added to gameplay) should be high.
  • Unless a sequel or game does something awesome (like Dune 2, which defined the genre and should be top or high) a sequel in a series or varation on game should be mid (like Chess variant).
  • Rules about games, expansion packs or obscure varations not worth mid should be low.

What do you think?--Clyde (talk) 18:07, 30 March 2007 (UTC)

I think a "game" in general could easily fit in any of the categories; it's more on a case by case basis. For example, I just rated School Tycoon as low, even though it is a game (and could be mid). I don't think we want every single game in a series as high, simply because all of those games aren't worth being in a paper encyclopedia. The first game should have a legacy section that says it had some sequels. However, not all of those sequels should have a complete article in a paper encyclopedia. In that sense, maybe the sequels should only be mid or low if they are not extremely important (in other words game by game basis).
As to the Total War series, I think it should be as you said. Be careful of how much you rate top and high though. According to the index of all the ratings, the ratios of top:high:mid:low is 6:15:47:68 or about 1:3:8:11. hmmm... I need to think about this, because we need to adjust our system a little. It's going to end up being 1:2:1:1 or something like that--Clyde (talk) 20:04, 30 March 2007 (UTC)
