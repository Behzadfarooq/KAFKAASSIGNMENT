Andre Faust

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Andre Faust (born October 7, 1969, Joliette, Quebec, Canada) was a Canadian professional ice hockey player. He was drafted by New Jersey Devils in the 1989 NHL Entry Draft in 9th round as the 173rd pick overall.

During his career Faust played in NCAA, NHL, AHL, DEL, Swedish Elitserien and Danish Superisligaen. In the NHL he played 47 games for the Philadelphia Flyers during the 1992/93 and 1993/94 season. After the 1995/96 season, in which he played for the AHL club Springfield Falcons, Faust moved to Germany and played for Augsburger Panther (1996-2000) and Kölner Haie (2000-02). In 2002 he signed with Swedish club Färjestads BK. After that season Faust moved a new club, Odense, and a new country,Denmark. Faust played their for one season and then he retired from ice hockey.
