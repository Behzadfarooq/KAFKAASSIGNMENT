Andrew Brel (born 28 September, 1960 in Johannesburg, South Africa) is a composer of New Age music having additonally enjoyed some success as a music producer and writer.

Andrew attended Bryanston Primary, Bryanston High and Damelin College before starting a medical degree at the University of Witwatersrand. Andrew ‘left’ Medical school to pursue a career in Music and found himself conscripted in the South African Army where he completed the mandatory 2 years (during 1979/80), touring war zones as a guitarist in the Entertainment Corps.

Released from the Army, Andrew pursued a career as a professional musician in Johannesburg’s live music circuit, often performing up to ten live appearances weekly, to daily audiences totaling thousands. Simultaneously Andrew completed a 3 year Computer Science Diploma. Immediately after graduating, at age 23 Andrew emigrated to England, driven in equal measure by despair at the iniquitous social order of South Africa and the quest for musical fulfillment.

Based in London, for the next ten years Andrew worked primarily as a musician by night and a studio producer by day, owning and running a number of Studios including Hampton Court Studios, Riverbank Studios and Black Barn Studios, and working with many gifted musicians.

In 1989 Andrew founded the 'Bridge Recordings' label with business partner Charlie Morgan, producing a series of high quality ‘Music by musicians’ recordings that continue to conjure international sales even though the business partnership ended when Charlie left Elton Johns band and moved to America. The web based ethically motivated business model for Bridge Recordings was ahead of time, and has been influential in the evolution of the independent label model, serving on a consultancy basis for a number of other successful small independents. Production credits include the SAS BAND (Spike Edneys All star band featuring artists such as Tony Hadley, Roger Taylor, Roy Wood and Chris Thompson. Leo Sayer. "Live in London.' And Hugh Burns 'Mindwash.'

Andrew has been at the forefront of using the computer as a tool for musical independence and Bridge Recordings was the first label to offer CD albums for sale on the internet in the UK. (1992.) Ronnie Johnson’s album ‘Give them enough rope.’

In 2000, Andrew's musical interest took a new direction when a chance meeting with popular English 'Angel' authoress Diana Cooper led to a commission to write a meditation Music album. In an unusual and remarkable seven day session, the album ‘Angel Inspiration’ was completed and soon became a best seller in the new age market.

Making albums based entirely on spontaneous improvisation arising from his own unique musical talent and compositional approach has proved to be rewarding for Andrew both personally and professionally. Following the successful reception of 'Angel Inspiration' a further two albums, MUSIC FOR KUMEKA and GOLDEN ATLANTIS followed, attracting positive sales reaction.

Angels and Unicorns - released in December 2006, is the fourth meditation music collection by Andrew Brel. This time featuring the remarkable guitar skills of Hugh Burns.

Hugh is well know for his featured contributions to many hit songs including ‘Careless Whisper’ ‘Jesus to a child’ and ‘Faith’ by George Michael and Gerry Rafferty’s ‘Baker Street.’ Hugh is also an accomplished classical musician, performing regularly with major orchestras around the world. The seven compositions are aimed at the higher consciousness, to provide an oasis of calm in which to nourish clear thought

Andrew continues to work from his studio on the Thames Riverbank, recording music and writing on various subjects from dog psychology, qualities of collectible guitars to Middle Eastern solutions.
