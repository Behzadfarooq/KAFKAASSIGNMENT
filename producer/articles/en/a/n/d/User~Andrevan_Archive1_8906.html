[edit] Per Ipsum Regent

Hello Andrevan

This is a response to your uncertainty about the relivance of the ariticle Robert Holborn, who was one of the first of 5 skilled shipwrights ever to be recorded in the history of English shipbuilding as employed directly by a monarch, on the kings ships.

As Shipbuilding has for some time been in decline, in the UK at least, some might say this information is of little use, but to those studing the history of the shipbuilding industry it must have some value. Maybe not at wikipedia, however, I cant see why?

I have no problems with the idea of putting the page up for deletion, as I doubt very much if it could be cleaned up given the lack of other information about this man. It is intersting to note that his family name reocours in the context of shipbuilding in almost every suceeding generation of his family right up until 1930, however, this information is not granted as public domain, at least not by me.

I see no bias or errors of a factal nature in the item, and so wonder, perhaps if you might enlighten me as to why you think it unsuitable for an encylopedia? - given the facts presented in my argument for placing it in the first place, in the context of the family of shipwrights named Pett, with who he was associate?

Please feel free to reply on my talk page, all the best Faedra 13:54, 12 Sep 2004 (UTC).

Perhaps the data could be merged with another page? - Like I say, I dont mind, the intention of my initial efforts are to inform, not to worry so much about the relivence to everyone, about everything I offer, given the universal nature of wikipedia.

Replied on your talk page. Andre 16:42, 12 Sep 2004 (UTC)
