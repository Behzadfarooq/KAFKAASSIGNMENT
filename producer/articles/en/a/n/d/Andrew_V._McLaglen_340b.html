Andrew V. McLaglen

Andrew V. McLaglen is an American film director. He is the son of actor Victor McLaglen.

He began his career as an assistant director and was the principal director on the first season of the television series Have Gun Will Travel. Since then he specialized in westerns and other adventure movies.

In the late 1970s and early 1980s he made two films for producer Euan Lloyd: The Wild Geese and The Sea Wolves. In 1976, he directed The Last Hard Men, starring Charlton Heston and James Coburn.
