And Now It's Come to This is a studio album released by Celtic Rock band Seven Nations in 2002. It is their only album comprised entirely of original material, and the first to feature Christian "Crisco" Miceli, the band's current drummer. Additionally, it is their only release on the Razor & Tie label.

The album is also notable for having a more mainstream sound than the band's other releases. Electric guitar features prominently, while the fiddle and especially the bagpipes are often buried in the mix. Additionally, Scott Long played electric bagpipes on some tracks, both on the album and on the supporting tour during songs from the album. This diminished the band's signature sound somewhat, and the followup, Thanks for Waiting, found them reverting to their "classic" sound.

The album contains the following tracks:

1. "You'd Be Mine"
2. "Wonderful"
3. "The Big Yellow Bus"
4. "Leave It"
5. "Waiting for Midnight"
6. "Very Nice"
7. "My Little Lady"
8. "Sweet Liberty"
9. "Up to Me"
10. "Asleep for Days"
11. "Jump Start"
12. "Last Call"

All tracks were written by Kirk McLeod, except "Jump Start" by Dan Stacey. All songs are new to this album, except "Up to Me" (known as "God" on previous releases), which appeared on Old Ground, Road Kill, The Pictou Sessions, and Live at the Palace Theatre.
