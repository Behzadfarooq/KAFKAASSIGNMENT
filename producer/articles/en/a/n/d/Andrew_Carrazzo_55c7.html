Andrew Carrazzo (born December 15, 1983) is an Australian rules footballer in the Australian Football League.

He made his debut for the Carlton Football Club in Round 21, 2004 against Melbourne.

Andrew made a stunning debut after coming off the rookie list towards the end of the 2004 season. He followed up on his good form in the 2005 season as he became an integral part of the Carlton attacking midfield, racking up 314 disposals in his first full year. He has also played defensive roles, including the nullification of dangerous Demon Aaron Davey and Docker Jeff Farmer.

Andrew continues to show good form into the 2006 season, playing 19 games with an average of 20 disposals and one triple-double. He was dropped to the Northern Bullants after quiet performances in rounds seven and eight, but amassed 51 disposals to ensure that he was speedily returned to the seniors (after a suspension received in the same game). He has played every game since return.
