Journal Community Publishing Group (JCPG) is a subsidiary of Journal Communications based in Waupaca, Wisconsin. It operates 44 small community newspapers in Connecticut, Florida, and Wisconsin; 37 shoppers in Louisiana, Massachusetts, Ohio, Vermont, and Wisconsin; and nine specialty niche publications in , Florida, Louisiana, New York, and Wisconsin. Additionally, it runs five printing facilities in Connecticut, Ohio, Vermont, and Wisconsin.[1] JCPG employs about 1,200 people.[2]

[edit] History

Formerly known as Add Inc., Journal Community Publishing Group was founded in 1972. In 1981, Journal Communications purchased a controlling interest in the small publishing company. Journal Communications finished purchasing the remainder of the company by 1986.[2]

Citing an advertising recession, the company ceased operations of its seven Appleton-area weekly newspapers on January 15, 2002. These closings eliminated 60 full- and part-time jobs. Included in the closings was The Times of Kaukauna, Wisconsin which had been published since 1880.[3]

The name of the company was changed in March 2004 to better describe the company. It was also believed to be more palatable to analysts for the newly publicly-traded Journal Communications.[4]

Due to damage from Hurricane Katrina, it was announced on September 30, 2005 that Dixie Web Printing, its publishing and printing facility in downtown New Orleans, would not be reopened. The workers at the printing facility--nearly 100--were laid off. While the publications printed at this facility continue to be produced, they are now outsourced to other printing facilities.[5]
