Andy Tod (born 4 November 1971 in Dunfermline) is a Scottish professional footballer currently playing for Scottish Premier League club Dunfermline Athletic.

Tod, a versatile player who can play in defence or attack, started his career with current club Dunfermline Athletic in 1992. He departed in 2001 to join Bradford City for £100,000 after an initial loan spell saw him score 4 goals in 12 league games, including a double against Wimbledon in October 2001[1].

He fell out of favour at Bradford after joining permanently, however, and returned to Scotland with loan spells at Hearts and Dundee United before returning permanently with former club Dunfermline Athletic in 2003[2]. During January 2006, the fans hero was told that he was no longer needed at East End Park by manager Stephen Kenny.
