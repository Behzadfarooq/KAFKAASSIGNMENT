André Pirro (February 12, 1869 - November 11, 1943) was a French musicologist and an organist.

Born in Saint-Dizier, Pirro learned to play the organ from his father. In Paris where he became and organist and a choirmaster for the Collège Stanislas de Paris. He studies with César Franck and teaches music history at the Schola Cantorum. Pirro publishes his academic thesis on the Aesthetics of Bach in 1907, followed by Descartes of the music'. His famous pupils include Vladimir Fédorov, Dragan Plamenac, Armand Machabey, Marc Pincherle and Jacques Chailley.
