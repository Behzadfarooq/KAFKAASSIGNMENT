[edit] Voting and accounts

I have a disagreement with the common practice of registered Wikipedian users in ignoring anonymous votes as a blanket decision. This is patently biased, and appears to violate the fairness rule that WikiPedia is supposed to use. That anon votes can be ignored is not at issue, that some admins always ignore them is an issue of fairness and openness. VfD is more prone to this sort of action than the other deletion pages. CfD for example, seems perfectly happy with anon votes. (Or atleast anons with significant edit histories). 132.205.15.43 20:58, 3 Apr 2005 (UTC)

It is not standard, it is merely default procedure. The regulations say that anon votes can be dismissed, but that the admin must be fair and impartial. Fairness and impartiality require that anon votes be considered on a case by case basis, otherwise, it will not be fair nor impartial. 132.205.15.43 01:29, 4 Apr 2005 (UTC)
Just curious, is your start date also the date you created your account? 132.205.15.43 01:29, 4 Apr 2005 (UTC)
Actually I meant, is the creation date listed for your page (presumably when you got your account, as most people create their page then), also the date which you started editting wikipedia. 3:132.205.15.43 4:132.205.15.43 02:22, 4 Apr 2005 (UTC) 5:02:22, 4 Apr 2005 (UTC)
