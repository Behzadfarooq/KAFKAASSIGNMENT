[edit] License tagging for Image:BookOfDaniel2006.jpg

Thanks for uploading Image:BookOfDaniel2006.jpg. Wikipedia gets thousands of images uploaded every day, and in order to verify that the images can be legally used on Wikipedia, the source and copyright status must be indicated. Images need to have an image tag applied to the image description page indicating the copyright status of the image. This uniform and easy-to-understand method of indicating the license status allows potential re-users of the images to know what they are allowed to do with the images.

For more information on using images, see the following pages:

  • Wikipedia:Image use policy
  • Wikipedia:Image copyright tags

If you believe this image is not replaceable, please:

  1. Go to the image description page and edit it to add {{Replaceable fair use disputed}}, without deleting the original Replaceable fair use template.
  2. On the image discussion page, write the reason why this image is not replaceable at all.

Alternatively, you can also choose to replace the fair use image by finding a freely licensed image of its subject, requesting that the copyright holder release this (or a similar) image under a free license, or by taking a picture of it yourself.

If you have uploaded other fair use media, consider checking that you have specified how these images fully satisfy our fair use criteria. You can find a list of 'image' pages you have edited by clicking on this link. Note that any fair use images which are replaceable by free-licensed alternatives will be deleted one week after they have been uploaded, as described on criteria for speedy deletion. If you have any questions please ask them at the Media copyright questions page. Thank you. Oden 19:58, 6 January 2007 (UTC)

[edit] Saint Acyndinus

I have added a "{{prod}}" template to the article Saint Acyndinus, suggesting that it be deleted according to the proposed deletion process. All contributions are appreciated, but I don't believe it satisfies Wikipedia's criteria for inclusion, and I've explained why in the deletion notice (see also "What Wikipedia is not" and Wikipedia's deletion policy). Please either work to improve the article if the topic is worthy of inclusion in Wikipedia, or, if you disagree with the notice, discuss the issues at its talk page. Removing the deletion notice will prevent deletion through the proposed deletion process, but the article may still be sent to Articles for Deletion, where it may be deleted if consensus to delete is reached, or if it matches any of the speedy deletion criteria. Pastordavid 18:04, 16 January 2007 (UTC)

Thank you very much for notifying this to me, and explaining your reasons. I understand, and I have tried to improve the article. I have moved the article to Saint Agathonicus and Companions, which is is where Acyndinus falls. I hope that this article now satisfies Wikipedia's criteria for inclusion. Again, thank you. Andrew 22:44, 16 January 2007 (UTC)
No problem. The merge definitely looks to be a move in the right direction - thanks. You may want to make a note of what calendar (e.g., Old Roman Calendar (pre-1959), current, Eastern Orthodox, etc) observe the feast day. You may also want to search the online catholic encyclopedia, I think I remember that there is an article about the author of the panengeric of Agathonicus (his name escapes me right now).
I am trying to clean up Wikipedia's treatment of saints some - expanding where there is notablility, remove where not, because I feel it is an important topic. FYI, I notice that you have created a number of articles on saints. The current naming conventions, WP:NCWC recommend not using "Saint" in the article title.
Thanks again for your quick attention to this. Pastordavid 23:05, 16 January 2007 (UTC)
Thanks! I'll be sure to change the titles of the articles, and I'm going to look for that calendar. I may not get around to changing article titles 'till tomorrow or the next day, but I will definitely get that very soon! Thanks! :-) Andrew 23:10, 16 January 2007 (UTC)
I have removed the 'Saint' from most of my articles about saints except for when an article already has an article to it's name (i.e. Aurelia/Saint Aurelia. I hope this is okay! Thanks. Andrew 21:23, 17 January 2007 (UTC)

That's great, thanks so much. FYI, when moving pages, click on "what links here" and make sure there are no "double re-directs." In essence, if you see a "redirect page" that links to another redirect page, send it to the actual article instead. Pastordavid 23:07, 18 January 2007 (UTC)
