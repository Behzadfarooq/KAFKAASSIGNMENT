Andrew Mawson, Baron Mawson

Andrew Mawson, Baron Mawson, OBE is a social entrepreneur. He co-founded the Community Action Network. He is renowned for his pioneering work at the Bromley by Bow Centre in East London, which became the UK's first Healthy Living Centre. Andrew received an OBE for his work there since 1984.

He is a founder board member of Poplar HARCA. In 2006 he launched the Water City initiative for East London with Richard Rogers.[1]

In February 2007, it was announced by the House of Lords Appointments Commission that he will be made a life peer and sits as a crossbencher.[2] The peerage was gazetted on 29 March 2007 as Baron Mawson, of Bromley-by-Bow in the London Borough of Tower Hamlets.
