Andrew Zornoza

Andrew Zornoza is a short-story writer and novelist born in Houston, Texas, on July 16, 1975, to a Spanish father and an American mother; he is the elder of two children.

Zornoza was raised in Houston for the first ten years of his life. The Zornoza family moved to Kansas when he was ten, stopping in Austin, Texas in between, before settling in Ridgefield, Connecticut at the age of 12. He attended Ridgefield High School before moving onto Princeton University where he majored in English.

He is the author of many short stories, including "Gas," "The Dishwashing Game" (which appeared in Porcupine Literary Review), "Soldiers Dandelions Accordions," "A View," "Restaurant Roundup," and "25th & Mission."

He is currently an associate editor at Pindeldyboz.

Zornoza's work combines elements of straight realism with those of pure fantasy. He often uses images and mixed media in his work, which is influenced by writers such as Paco Ignacio Taibo II, William Faulkner, Theodore Sturgeon, R.W. Apple, Jack London, Vladimir Nabokov, Flann O'Brien, and Yukio Mishima.
