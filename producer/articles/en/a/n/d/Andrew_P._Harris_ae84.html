[edit] Background

Andrew Harris was first elected to the Maryland Senate in 1999 for District 9 for Baltimore County.[1] His district was later redrawn to be District 9, representing Baltimore County and Harford County. Harris was the Minority Whip from 2003-06.

[edit] Education

Harris received his B.S. in biology from Johns Hopkins University in 1977. In 1980 he received his M.D. from Johns Hopkins from the School of Hygiene and Public Health. He later received his M.H.S. in 1995 in Health Policy & Management and also Health Finance & Management.

[edit] Career

Harris served in the Medical Corp and the U.S. Naval Reserve as a Lt. Commander and later as Commander.

He has worked as an Anesthesiologist and as an Associate Professor of Anesthesiology and Critical Care Medicine, and Chief of Obstetric Anesthesiology at the Johns Hopkins Hospital.

Harris has been an active member in the community as a member of the Knights of Columbus, an officer in the Thornleigh Neighborhood Improvement Association (vice-president, 1984-85; president, 1985-86), a member of the Board of Directors of the Sherwood Community Association, 1987-91, served as Vice President of St. Joseph's School Home-School Association from 1992-94. Also, he has been on the Board of Directors of the Maryland Leadership Council, 1995-98, a member of the North Central Republican Club (treasurer, 1997-98; vice-president, 1998),a nd finally as a Delegate to the Republican Party National Convention, 2004.

Harris has received the Laughlin Award for Distinguished Public Officer, Medical and Chirurgical Faculty of Marylandin 2001.

[edit] Current Events

In 2007, Harris submitted a bill to the Maryland State Senate to limit the scope of eminent domain. Currently, the law allows the government to seize private property and give it to private corporations for development, as per the Supreme Court ruling in the Kelo case in Connecticut.
