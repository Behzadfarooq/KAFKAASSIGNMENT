Andrey Korotkov (Андрéй Короткóв) (June 3, 1954, Sevastopol) worked as Deputy Communications and Informatisation Minister in Russia.

Currently - High-level advisor to the Chairman of Bank for foreign trade (Vneshtorgbank)

In 2003 he briefly attracted worldwide media attention, when he participated in an unusual attack on Russia's most prominent spammer, American Language Center (ALC).

Korotkov, like most Russian Internet users, has received spam from ALC many times. Several attempts to complain and "unsubscribe" were unsuccessful, but soon the spam messages started to address him by name.

During an interview to Rambler-Audio news project, Korotkov addressed the American Language Center:

I would like to warn you that if you do not stop your illegal activities, not only me, but the whole association recently formed to combat spam, would take adequate actions that would not only stop you from hindering normal use of e-mail, but would really make your life complicated. Once again I ask you to stop your illegal activities and think about legitimate ways to achieve your goals.

Golden Telecom, one of the largest Russian ISP, organised automatic dialing to ALC phone numbers. Every 10 seconds a phone call was made and the recorded message given above was played.

[edit] Biography in brief

Andrey Korotkov joined Russian Bank for foreign trade (Vneshtorgbank) in January of 2004 as Senior vice-president and CIO. In his role at the ICT Department of Vneshtorgbank, he, among other highly relevant for contemporary banking sphere IT innovations, initiated the development of the unified VTB group technological policy and started the infrastructural information systems and banking services and applications integration aimed at the final Service Oriented Architecture implementation.

Under his direction Vneshtorgbank has also realized a number of prominent ICT projects including the branch automated banking systems unification, corporate data warehouse construction ant others. Bank has also extended the roster of its international strategic IT partners after having signed contracts with Microsoft, IBM, Hewlett-Packard, Cisco Systems and others.

In his position of the High-level advisor to the Chairman of Vneshtorgbank, Mr.Korotkov has been recently steering through the analysis of mergers and acquisitions procedure legislation in the banking sphere both in Russia and in the EU in the framework of the extending of the financila group of Vneshtorgbank in Europe and is currently responsible for the IT-systems audit and monitoring within the network of subsidiary banks of the VTB group.

Andrey Korotkov holds a Ph.D from the Moscow State University in philology. The Ph.D thesis was dedicated to the problem of digital divide bridging as basis of the modern strategy of information society. As an expert on ICT and artificial languages and semiotics, Mr.Korotkov teaches in a number of Russian universities (ex.:Moscow State University, Moscow State Institute of International Relationship, Russian Academy of National Economy) with his educational courses on the information society and high technologies development. He has published numerous articles and books on subjects ranging from the information systems implementation in business and banking spheres, electronic financial system in the world economy and governmental policy for the information society development in Russia to artificial intelligence research. He has been currently working on his doctoral thesis dedicated to the informatization of the banking business in the framework of the knowledge based economy development.

The Government of the Russian Federation. Since 1996 to 2002 Mr.Korotkov held various positions in the Government of the Russian Federation and Administration of the President of Russia including journalistic and PR activities in the Television and Radio complex of Kremlin and the 5 cabinet office of the Prime Minister of Russia. In 2002 he was appointed the First Deputy Minister of the Ministry for Telecommunications and Informatization of the Russian Federation and in this position was responsible for a number of major federal information technology projects in Russia. Among them are the national critical infrastructure elements protection development, State automated system “Elections” elaboration, free software implementation in the governmental institutions promotion, etc.

He is famous as one of the most prominent ideologists of the information society development in Russia. As the First Deputy Minister of the Ministry for Telecommunications and Informatization of Russia, Mr.Korotkov was the head of the First Directorate of the Federal “E-Russia” Program and carried out tenders for the Federal projects realization in Moscow and regions of the Russian Federations. Mr.Korotkov has also regularly represented Russia in the UN information and communication activities, in particular was the head of the Russian delegation for the First phase of the World Summit on the information society held in December 2003 in Geneva. He is a member of the UN ICT Task Force and an adviser to the UN General-Secretary on the ICT issues. As a member of the UN ICT Task Force Bureau, he initiated the foundation of the UN ICT Task Force Bureau of the European and Central Asian regional network and at the moment s the head of that organization.

In the framework of his international activities Mr.Korotkov participated in a number of world economic and ICT oriented initiatives including negotiations with OECD in Honolulu and OPEC, and World Economic Forum.

Journalism. Mr.Korotkov has started his career in 1992 as a special correspondent of the TASS News Agency and traveled a lot around CIS countries making reports on political and economic issues. He resigned from the News Agency as the Deputy editor-in-chief of the audiovisual department.

Andrey Korotkov is married and has two sons. He is personally keen on photography and design.
