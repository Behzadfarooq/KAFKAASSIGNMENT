Andre Wadsworth (born October 19, 1974 in St. Croix, U.S. Virgin Islands) is a current American professional football player for the New York Jets. He began his college career as a 215-pound walk on at Florida State University and became a highly touted prospect at defensive end, drafted 3rd overall by the Arizona Cardinals in 1998. His career went on a long hiatus after 2002, when he was released by the Cardinals after a few injury-plagued seasons. Wadsworth is now back in professional football, signed by the Jets as a free agent on March 27, 2007[1].

[edit] Collegiate career

Recruited by only two small colleges (Liberty University and Bethune-Cookman College) coming out of Class A Florida Christian High School in Miami, where he saw most of his time at tight end, Wadsworth eventually earned a scholarship as a walk on at Florida State University. He earned 2nd team All-ACC honors his freshman, sophomore and junior seasons (1994-96) as a defensive tackle. Despite being a potential top-10 NFL Draft pick after his junior season, he stayed for his senior year, citing a pinched nerve in his neck that forced him out of four games in 1996 as a concern regarding his physical capabilities to perform for scouts at full capacity. He switched from defensive tackle to defensive end for his senior season, registering 16 quarterback sacks and 19 tackles for a loss, placing him second in both categories in the school's all-time records. One of four finalists for the Lombardi Award, he was named 1st team All-ACC and AP All-American.

[edit] Professional career

Generally regarded as the most sure thing in the draft by many media outlet's experts [1], he was selected 3rd overall in the 1998 NFL Draft by the Arizona Cardinals, making him the highest-selected player in school history. [2] A lengthy hold out ensued, centered almost directly on both his demand to be paid closer to the contracts signed by the two previous picks, Indianapolis Colts' Peyton Manning out of University of Tennessee at no. 1 and the San Diego Chargers' Ryan Leaf from Washington State University at no. 2 rather than one closer to University of Michigan's Charles Woodson selected by the Oakland Raiders at no. 4, and a provision allowing him to void the contract before expiration if he chose to do so. The hold out lasted until the night before the season opener (44 days) with a six-year, $42 million contract including a $10.49 million signing bonus (Manning received $11.6 million on a $48 million contract, Leaf got $11.2 million on a five-year contract totaling $31.2 million), though the player-triggered option out was never consummated. [3] He played in all 16 games his rookie season, finishing with 5.0 sacks and 57 tackles, including 42 solo. Bothered by a knee injury in 1999, he still managed to play in 11 games, tallying 2.0 sacks, 26 tackles, and his only career interception. Off-season knee surgery, the second of his career, held him out from most of the off-season activities, but still managed 13 tackles and 1 sack in 9 games. At the conclusion of the 2000 season, Wadsworth underwent microfracture surgery on his right knee, the third surgery on that knee in his career, and arthroscopic surgery on the left on January 3 with non-team physician Dr. Richard Steadman. Possibly due to concerns after teammate Eric Swann's semi-recovery from 9 knee surgeries, the Arizona Cardinals released Wadsworth before offering a one year, $512,000 tender that was eventually pulled off the table within days after it went unsigned.

On January 28, 2007 the St. Petersburg Times reported that the Tampa Bay Buccaneers were planning to give Wadsworth, now 32, a workout.[4] The New York Jets gave Wadsworth a workout later in the offseason. On March 26, 2007, the New York Jets signed Wadsworth to a minimum-based salary contract.[5]
