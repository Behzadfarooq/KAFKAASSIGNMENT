Andean House

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Andean House is a private international boarding school in Mendoza, Argentina. This notable institution is home to the Boys Choir of The Andes.

[edit] Background

Andean House is a preparatory school for boys from Junior First Form (Second Grade) through Upper Sixth Form (Twelfth Grade). Andean House was established to provide an exceptionally challenging education combining academic, choral and adventurous activities with an emphasis on languages and internationalism.

The school uses small group tutorials and peer tutoring in place of conventional classes. Year groups comprise around thirty boys. The Junior School in Andean House is contains the training choirs. The best singers join the performance choir.

Andean House has a unique approach to teaching languages. Linguistics is taught as a tool for learning languages. Boys learn six languages over their first six years at Andean House. The international mix of pupils is used to provide peer tutoring, which supplements language exchanges.


[edit] Foundation

The school was founded in 2007 by Nick Hockings, in order to apply the combination of teaching methods at the heart of the school. Hockings had previously been taught by Sir Toby Coghill, at Aberlour House, Gordonstoun, who had been Guardian at Gordonstoun under Kurt Hahn, and was subsequently Chairman of Aiglon.

Andean House is located in the Valle de Uco, in Mendoza_Province, Argentina, just eighty miles from Santiago. The school is at an elevation of 2,000 meters above sea level, or approx. 6,000 feet. The principle industry of the Valle de Uco is wine making, being home to many of Argentina's most important wine exporters.

[edit] Philosophy

Andean House subscribes to a "whole person" philosophy of mind, and body with an emphasis upon pastoral values and outdoor activity. The school expects pupils to aim high, and actively encourages pupils to lead expeditions and develop business projects.

While the basic fees are high, a large proportion of pupils benefit from academic or choral scholarships, and the fees include the cost of air travel to and from the school.
