Andhra Pradesh Express

The Andhra Pradesh Express is an express train run by Indian Railways. It runs from Hyderabad to New Delhi. The train takes 26 hours for the journey, passing through the states of Andhra Pradesh, Maharashtra, Madhya Pradesh, Uttar Pradesh, and Haryana.

As per the numbering of the Indian Railways, this train has been numbered 2723 for the Hyderabad - New Delhi run, and 2724 for the New Delhi - Hyderabad run. The train was first started by Madhu Dandavate in 1978. It was faster by 1 hour. It is an extremely fast train. Along with this, Tamil Nadu Express was started to ply between New Delhi and Chennai and it is slightly faster than this train.
