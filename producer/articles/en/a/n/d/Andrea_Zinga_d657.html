Andrea Zinga

Andrea Lane Zinga is a former television reporter, and was twice the Republican nominee for Congress in Illinois's 17th congressional district (map).

[edit] Personal history

Zinga was born and raised in Macomb, Illinois and is a graduate of Macomb High School. She has a Bachelors Degree in Education from Western Illinois University and a Masters Degree from University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign.

[edit] Career in television

Zinga has been a television reporter and anchorwoman for all three television stations in the Quad Cities-- WQAD-TV, KWQC-TV (while it was still WOC-TV) and WHBF-TV. She has also reported for CNN, CNN Headline News and The Airport Channel (a CNN network provided exclusively to airports), While at CNN, Zinga was the first anchor to report the breaking story of the Centennial Olympic Park bombing on July 27, 1996 [1].

[edit] 2004 Congressional race

Zinga ran for Congress in the 17th District in 2004 against 11-term incumbent Democrat Lane Evans. Zinga stirred controversy by using Evans' health as a campaign issue, noting he suffered from Parkinson's Disease. These statements did not sit well with the local media and many voters (including Republicans [2]) in the district, who felt this amounted to "kicking a man when he is down." She ended up only taking 39% of the vote (111,615 votes), much in part to the district's Democratic lean, Evans' strong name recognition and her largely underfunded candidacy.

[edit] 2006 Congressional race

Zinga won a highly contested primary on March 21, 2006 defeating Jim Mowen and Brian Gilliland. She garnered 42 percent of the vote, winning by just 325 votes. [3]. Zinga was going to be facing Evans again in 2006. However, Evans announced his retirement and withdrawal from the race after the March primary, because of the increasingly debilitating effects of Parkinson's Disease. After a vote by Democratic precinct committeemen from the 17th congressional district, Evans' district director, Phil Hare, was picked to be the Democratic candidate to face Zinga in November. Zinga was defeated by Hare.

Zinga opposes gun control and strongly supports the American effort in Iraq. She is pro-life but supports exceptions to protect the life of the mother and in the cases of rape and incest if reported within 72 hours [4]. Zinga believes the warrantless wiretapping dispute should be settled in the courts.

Zinga has expressed support for racial profiling and felt that all Middle Eastern men should get secondary security screenings as they board airplanes. Zinga said of terrorists, "They're like cockroaches. They're everywhere, and we can stamp and stamp in a million places and we're still going to be dealing with them." [5]

Zinga had been endorsed by the Peoria Journal Star for the 17th Congressional District. [6]
