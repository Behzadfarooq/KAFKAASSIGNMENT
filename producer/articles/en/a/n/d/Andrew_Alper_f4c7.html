Andrew Alper is the former President of the New York City Economic Development Corporation (NYCEDC).

[edit] President of the New York City Economic Development Corporation

Mr. Alper was appointed as President on January 15, 2002 by New York City Mayor Michael R. Bloomberg. He served in this position for four years before stepping down in January 2006. [1] In his time as President, Mr. Alper worked with the Mayor's Office to encourage industrial expansion, business development, and employment opportunities in New York City.[2] He was also actively involved in the planning for the redevelopment of the site of the former World Trade Center.

[edit] Goldman, Sachs & Company

Before joining the NYCEDC, Mr. Alper was employed by Goldman, Sachs & Company since 1981. Mr. Alper stared work there as a corporate finance generalist before becoming the youngest partner in firm history in 1990. He also served as the Vice President and then the Managing Director of Goldman, Sachs & Co.'s finance department.[3] In 1997 he became the Chief Operation Officer of the company's global investment banking business.

[edit] Involvement with the University of Chicago

Mr. Alper has also been actively involved with his alma mater: the University of Chicago. He received his A.B. from The College of the University of Chicago in 1980, concentrating in economics, and his M.B.A. from the University of Chicago Graduate School of Business (GSB) in 1981.[4]. As an undergraduate in the College, Mr. Alper resided in Pierce Hall and enjoyed working on a free weekly South Side newpaper he created: The Chicago Journal.[5] He also met his wife, Sharon Sadow, during Orienation Week. (With whom he has had two daughters, Karen and Jennifer Alper)

Since graduating, Mr. Alper has been an active alumnus, receiving a Young Alumni Service Citation in 1993. He was appointed to the Alumni Association Board of Governors in 1994 and served as the board's Vice President from 1996 to 1998. Since that time, he has served on the GSB Council and is currently the co-chair of the GSB's Capital Campaign.[6]

Currently, Mr. Alper is the Vice President of the University of Chicago Board of Trustees[7] and the Chairman of the Chicago Initiative[8], a University fundraising drive to raise $2 billion for Chicago's endowment.

Mr. Alper is also the namesake of Alper House in the University of Chicago House System. Alper House is located on the third and fourth floors of the Max Palevsky East Residential Commons.
