Andy Strachan

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Andy Strachan (born August 20, 1974) is the current drummer in the Australian punkabilly band, The Living End. He has been playing in the band since 2002, after previous drummer Trav Demsey left.

Andy drums on the One Said To The Other EP, Modern ARTillery and their newest album, State of Emergency.

[edit] Trivia

Andy won the 2006 Jack Award for the Australia's Best Drummer category.

Andy started playing drums at age 12, after an unsuccessful stint with the viola.

He has also played in bands called Social Insecurity, Alcotomic and the more famous Pollyanna.

He began at age 15 playing drums in an Adelaide band called The Runaways, playing covers of mostly '50's and 60's songs.

He broke a drumstick while rehearsing with The Living End for a performance on Rove Live.
