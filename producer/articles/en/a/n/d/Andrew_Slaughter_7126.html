Andrew Francis Slaughter (born 29 September 1960, London) is a United Kingdom politician. He is Member of Parliament for Ealing, Acton and Shepherd's Bush in London and was previously Leader of the London Borough of Hammersmith and Fulham Council.

Slaughter was educated at the University of Exeter, and is a barrister specialising in criminal and housing law, as well as a former Labour Party researcher for Michael Meacher. He is not married.

He became a Hammersmith and Fulham councillor for Gibbs Green ward in 1986, then from 2002 North End ward and council leader in 1996. When the government moved towards executive mayors, Hammersmith and Fulham was one of the first councils to style their leader 'mayor'. However, the measure initially failed to become law and Slaughter eventually reverted his title to 'leader'.

[edit] Uxbridge 1997 by-election

Slaughter stood as the Labour candidate in the Uxbridge parliamentary by-election on 31 July 1997, after the death of Conservative MP Michael Shersby. The by-election would take place less than three months after Labour's May 1997 general election landslide victory. The Labour Party put a great amount of effort into winning this Conservative marginal seat, and Tony Blair even visited the constituency, becoming the first Prime Minister in over thirty years to campaign in a by-election.

However Labour's campaign would be overshadowed by a row over the decision to select Slaughter instead of the party's previous general election candidate, David Williams. Many local party workers were angered that Williams was not even placed on the shortlist, and nearly a third of the members attending the selection meeting spoiled their voting papers in protest against his exclusion. In the end Labour won 39% of the vote, down sharply on Labour's performance in May 1997, and the Conservatives' local candidate John Randall was elected with over 50% of all votes cast.

[edit] 2005 election

Slaughter would eventually gain a seat in Westminster in 2005 as the elected MP for Ealing, Acton and Shepherd's Bush in London. In this capacity he replaced the retired Clive Soley.

Labour Party members of the the new Hammersmith Constituency Labour Party selected Slaughter as the Labour candidate for the Hammersmith seat on the 30th November 2006. Thus Slaugher will represent the Labour party in this new constituency at the next general election.
