Andreiana Kalisfena Anfiia Sveski

Czarina Andreiana Kalisfena Anfiia Sveski is the current czarina of the small, Russian principality of Paleostrovsk, which has retained its monarchial status in northeast Russia. The czarina was born on November 16, 1978 and inherited her position from her father upon his death on August 26, 2003 - when he suffered a fatal heart attack. The position of czar or czarina in the principality is almost entirely nominal, however - save for the tithe of taxes that is taken by the czarina.

Andreiana lived in St. Petersburg for the majority of her childhood, attending prestigious academies for her education, and opting to study abroad in Switzerland for her collegiate education at the University of Fribourg, with a major in theology. She returned to Paleostrovsk in 2002, at which point in time she began working as a volunteer at the local Russian Orthodox church, mostly as a religious education teacher. She was forced to give up the position upon taking her title, and fosters many programs dedicated to children's education. A local non-secular school bears her name and her financial support, the андреиана свески школа для религиозных и исполнительных видов искусства, or Andreiana Sveski School for Religious and Performing Arts.

The czarina is engaged to Ivan Ligir Landova, who she has dated for almost five years, however a wedding date has yet to be set. The couple officially resides in Paleostrovsk, however retains homes in Moscow, St. Petersburg, and various other European cities. Most of their time is spent in Geneva. The czarina is an avid fan of tennis, cross country skiing, and literature. She heads the Paleostrovsk Region Fund to Promote Literacy, among the numerous other causes she advocates.

Her name is derived from both her familial heritage and religious figures. Andreiana being her mother's name, Kalisfena and Anfiia being saints. Her mother died in 1986 in a car accident attributed to icy road conditions when Andreiana was seven.

The principality of Paleostrovsk is home to around 47,000 people. The largest city in the principality is Paleostrovsk, which has around 33,000 citizens.
