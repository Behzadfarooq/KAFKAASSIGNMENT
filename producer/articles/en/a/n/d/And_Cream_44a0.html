And Cream is a monthly Online Magazine which offers some amusing and some serious articles covering those topics that your average weekly/bi-weekly Magazine turn their noses up at. Don't expect to find anything about Jordan (Katie Price) discussing her latest single, nor will you find out which Z-List celebrities are sleeping with one another with the aim of producing Z-List babies.

Instead, editor Matt Waldram and his team of writers cover quite bizarre topics such as sailing down the River Meuse in a Bathtub, long-forgotten TV shows and the very finest in B-Movies. Writer, Andrew Barker regularly runs a spot called the DVD Graveyard which reviews Straight-to-DVD movies that have perhaps been overlooked and overshadowed by the Blockbuster releases.

There is also a section on the magazine called Cult Icons which includes interviews with people who the magazine deem 'worthy'. This worthiness is attributed to either being involved in something which has developed a huge cult following, or who are something of a cult phenomenon themselves. The interviews range from prose accounts of the conversation, Q+A sessions with the subject or Audio recordings of the conversation between the interviewer (usually Matt Waldram) and the subject, broadcast through the site.

The team invite their readers to send in any emails, even if it is to criticise them, and they seem to print up (in a section of the magazine called The Mailbag) most of the letters that they receive. They also claim that they are happy to read any reader-submitted articles, with the promise that they will publish the best things that they receive.

[edit] History

The magazine appears to have first surfaced on the internet around July 2006. In the short period of time that it has been around, it has already been re-designed twice. Messages on the website stating that it is still growing and changing indicate that it will probably receive more facelifts in the future.

Currently a mottled grey colour, with mostly dark images, the site could use a little more brightness and colour, though this in no way detracts from the articles which are, in the main, of a reasonably high standard, and mostly very amusing.

It is difficult to describe what the main remit of the magazine is, and what category of entertainment it would fall under. Though the theme of topics deemed to be "Cult" or "Bizarre" reappear throughout the pages. As a result of this, some sections/articles seem out of place. Though this can be forgiven as the magazine is still young.

[edit] The Writers

A brief biography of the team members can be found on the website in the About section. The team, currently, is made up of the following people.

  • Matt Waldram (writer/editor)
  • Andrew Barker (writer)
  • Stephen Watson (writer)
  • Joe Taylor (writer)
  • Ben Waldram (writer)
  • Debbie Norris (writer)

[edit] Interviews

The magazine seems keen to interview many cult heroes and make these interviews available through the site. The subjects interviewed to date are:

  • Steven Seagal
  • Larry Kenney (voice actor who provides the voice of Lion-O in cartoon show: Thundercats)
  • Tony Hart
  • Uri Geller
