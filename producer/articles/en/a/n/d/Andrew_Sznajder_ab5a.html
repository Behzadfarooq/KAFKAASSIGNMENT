Andrew Sznajder

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Andrew Sznajder (pronounced: shnigh-der) (born May 25, 1967 in Preston, Lancashire) is a former Canadian professional tour tennis player.

A native of Oakville, Ontario, Sznajder achieved a career-high singles ranking of World # 46 in September 1989.

[edit] Tennis career

Prior to his pro career, Sznajder played college tennis at Pepperdine University, where he was a two-time All-American selection (1987 and 1988). His .800 won-lost percentage there (40-10) is the 6th-best in the school's history.[1]

In 1988, he won the Intercollegiate Tennis Association indoor individual championship.

The summer of '89 was his best season as a pro - he won the Chicoutimi challenger event, reached the 3rd round at both the Stratton Mountain and Indianapolis Grand Prix events, the quarter-finals of the Canadian Open and Los Angeles Grand Prix tournament, and the 2nd round of the U.S. Open.

In April 1990, Sznajder was a finalist of the Rio De Janeiro Grand Prix event.

Sznajder was a record six-time winner of the Canadian Closed singles championship and three-time Tennis Canada singles player of the year.

Upon retiring from the tour, Sznajder founded his own tennis health and raquet club company, and co-directs Awakenings Tennis Academy.

He continued to play competitively, and captured the 2002 Ontario Indoor Championship.

He is now a top-ranking competitor on the ITF sanctioned Wilson/Mayfair Senior Circuit Over-35s.

[edit] Olympics

He competed in the 1992 Summer Olympics, reaching the 2nd round.

[edit] Davis Cup

As a Davis Cup competitor, he had a career win-lose record of 14-10, all in singles, including a win and a loss in a losing tie to Spain in the first round of the 1991 World Group, Canada's first appearance.[2]
