Andrew C. Brock

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Andrew C. Brock is a Republican member of the North Carolina General Assembly representing the stateâ€™s thirty-fourth Senate district, including constituents in Davie and Rowan counties. He is also the Republican deputy whip in the Senate. A political consultant from Mocksville, North Carolina, Senator Brock is currently (2006-2007) serving in his third term in the state Senate.

He is a member of the Finance Committee, the Judiciary I Committee, the Senate Rules Committee, Appropriations/Base Budget Committee, the Committee on Mental Health & Youth Services, the Information Technology Committee, and the Transportation Committee. He also serves on the Committee on Appropriations on General Government and Information Technology and the Agriculture Committee

Senator Brock is a member of Quail Unlimited, Ducks Unlimited and the National Wild Turkey Federation.[1]

Andrew Brock announced his candidacy for Chairman of the North Carolina Republican Party on November 16, 2006
