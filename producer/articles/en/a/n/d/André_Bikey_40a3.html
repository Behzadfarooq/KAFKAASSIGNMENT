André Stéphane Bikey Amugu (born 8 January 1985, Douala, Cameroon) is a professional footballer. His position is defender and he can play in the centre of defence or at right back.

[edit] Club Career

Bikey currently plays for Reading in the English Premier League, having signed for the club on a season-long loan from Lokomotiv Moscow on 26 August 2006[1]. Reading have an option to purchase the player at the end of the loan. Bikey has expressed a desire to obtain a permanent deal.[2]

Bikey was signed on the back of a successful trial period during Reading's pre-season trip to Sweden, where he impressed manager Steve Coppell. He was sent off in his final trial match for the club against Örgryte IS for head-butting an opponent, an incident that was thought to have ended Reading's interest in the player. Coppell however put the incident down to the player being "determined to impress" and after much negotiation with Lokomotiv, a deal was struck.

Bikey has recently been drafted into the Reading side as replacement for the injured Ibrahima Sonko.

[edit] International Career

Bikey also represents Cameroon at international level.
