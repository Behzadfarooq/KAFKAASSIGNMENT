And Then Nothing Turned Itself Inside Out is a 2000 (see 2000 in music) album by Yo La Tengo, an indie rock music group from Hoboken, New Jersey, U.S.

[edit] Miscellanea

  • The title "Let's Save Tony Orlando's House" comes from an episode of The Simpsons, "Marge on the Lam". It is the name of a telethon that actor Troy McClure previously hosted. During this period, many of their tracks were given temp-titles based on Troy McClure's filmography. [2]
  • The title "The Crying Of Lot G" is a reference to Thomas Pynchon's novel The Crying of Lot 49.
