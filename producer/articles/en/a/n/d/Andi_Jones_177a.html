Andrew (Andi) Jones (born on October 10, 1978 in Manchester, England) is an English athlete who specialises in road and mountain / fell running, competing for England & Great Britain. In 2003 he finished 4th in the WMRA World Mountain Trophy in Alaska. Closer to home he competes for Salford Harriers, where he is coached by Bob Merrell. Although one of the UK's leading distance runners Andi chooses to work full time alongside his running career and is employed as a Design and Technology teachers well as a form tutor at Saddleworth School, Oldham.

Personal Bests

  • 10Km 29:28 - Trafford (7/3/05)
  • 1/2 Marathon 64:38 - Lake Vyrnwy (2005)
  • Marathon 2h 17m 52s - Kosice, Slovakia (02/10/05)

Major Achievements

  • 2003 WMRA World Mountain Trophy, Alaska - 4th
  • 2006 North of England Cross Country Championships - 1st
  • 2006 Liverpool Half Marathon - 1st

January 27, 2007

Andi became the first athlete for 12 years to retain the senior men's title at the 2007 North of England cross country championships in Heaton Park, Manchester.

The last runner to win back to back crowns was Rossendale Harrier, Dave Lewis, in 1994 and 1995. For the third time in five years, Gareth Raven of Sale Harriers Manchester finished runner-up.
