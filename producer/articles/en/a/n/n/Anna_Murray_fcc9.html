Anna Murray

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Anna Murray (born July 25, 1966 in Southampton, New York) is an American entrepreneur and author.

She is president of the e*media division of tmg-e*media, a New York City-based technology consulting firm. The company was created through the 2005 purchase of e*media by Technology Management Group.

Murray is the great granddaughter of Thomas E. Murray, inventor, partner to Thomas Edison and one of the founders of Consolidated Edison. She graduated Summa Cum Laude from Yale College in 1988, and received her B.S. in Journalism from Columbia School of Journalism in 1991, where she won the James A. Wechsler scholarship for local reporting.

Murray’s career has spanned both media and education. She spent 5 years teaching English in both New York City and Michigan. In her early journalism career, she worked for local newspapers and at ABC News in New York. In 1994 she joined an educational software company, Lawrence Productions, in western Michigan, which first introduced her to multimedia. In 1996 she founded e*media, an early Internet company that built websites and interactive programs for national brands that included Keebler, Kellogg, Bayer and Del Monte.

Murray is also the author of a novel for young adults, Sarah’s Page (Sleeping Bear Press, 1998). In addition, she wrote the business book, The CPG eMarketing Guidebook (Marketing Sherpa, 2003), and has written numerous articles for business publications on emarketing.

Murray lives in New York City with her husband and business partner Christos Moschovitis.
