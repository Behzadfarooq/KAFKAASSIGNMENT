Ann Bradshaw (born c. 1880), along with Gemma Doyle, Felicity Worthington, and Pippa Cross, is one of the lead characters of Libba Bray's novels A Great and Terrible Beauty, Rebel Angels, and The Sweet Far Thing.

[edit] Name

Ann's name comes from Hebrew; it means "favored grace". [1] Her surname, Bradshaw, means "from the broad forest." [2]

In Rebel Angels, Gemma, Felicity, and Ann make anagrams of their names. Ann's name becomes Nan Washbrad; she is the only one of the girls whose anagram sounds like a "real name."

[edit] Education

Ann attends Spence Academy, where she has been for many years. She was sent to Spence, as a "scholarship pupil," but her nouveau riche aunt and uncle wish Ann to be well-learned; they can then employ her as an unpayed governess for their odious children.

Ann managed to stay fairly invisible while at school. The only subject that she stood out in was singing; Ann proved to have a beautiful voice. She lacked the money and beauty of the other girls, and so lacked friends. She was often bullied by the other girls, especially Felicity Worthington and Pippa Cross. In 1895, however, when Ann was fifteen, a new girl came to Spence. Gemma Doyle was Ann's roommate. The two became friends by virtue of their having no other friends. When Felicity came into Gemma's debt, Gemma became friend with Felicity and Pippa, under condition that they would befriend Ann, too. This relationship, first one of obligation, later became real. The girls bonded, and came to love each other. In December of that year, Felicity even took Ann home with her over Christmas break, as Ann would otherwise have had to stay at their school.

[edit] Family and Friends

  • Gemma Doyle, Ann's friend and roommate at Spence
  • Felicity Worthington, a friend of Ann's at Spence
  • Pippa Cross, another friend of Ann's at Spence
  • Thomas Henry "Tom" Doyle, Gemma's older brother, who works at Bethlem Royal Hospital. Ann suffers an unrequited love for him.
