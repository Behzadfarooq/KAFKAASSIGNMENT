Ann Nicole Nelson

Ann Nicole Nelson, (1971 - September 11, 2001), was a bond broker at Cantor Fitzgerald in the World Trade Center in New York City. She was killed during the September 11, 2001 terrorist attacks.[1]

Raised in Stanley, North Dakota, she was one of a handful of 9/11 victims with North Dakota ties. Her story gave North Dakotans a local connection to the attacks. An auditorium at Minot State University in Minot, North Dakota was renamed Ann Nicole Nelson Hall in her honor.

[edit] Life

Nelson, the daughter of Gary Nelson, a banker, and Jenette Nelson, a high school teacher, grew up in Stanley, North Dakota. She attended a boarding school in Wisconsin as a teenager, wanting a more rigorous education than she could obtain in her small town. She graduated from Carleton College in Minnesota with double majors in finance and political science. She enjoyed international travel.

She worked as a bond trader in Minneapolis and later in Chicago before moving to New York City a few months before the September 11 attacks. Just minutes before the attacks began, she called her boyfriend from her office on the 104th floor of Tower One to ask for information she had forgotten about a wine tasting class she was taking that evening.[2]

[edit] Memorial

An auditorium in Old Main at Minot State University in Minot, North Dakota was renamed Ann Nicole Nelson Hall in honor of Nelson two years after her death.[3]
