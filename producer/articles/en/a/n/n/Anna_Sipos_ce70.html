Anna Sipos

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Anna Sipos (born April 3, 1908 in Hungary; died in 1972) was a Hungarian table tennis player.

In all, Sipos won 21 medals in World Championship competitions.

[edit] Tennis career

Sipos won 11 gold medals in World Championship table tennis competition. She is ranked the 2nd best women’s player of her era.

Among Sipos’s many championships are the World Singles title in consecutive years — 1932 and 1933, the World Doubles championship in six consecutive years (with Mária Mednyánszky) — 1929-34, and the World Mixed Doubles championships with Istvan Kelen in 1929 and with Viktor Barna in 1932 and 1935.

Sipos was the first female player to use the penholder grip, but changed to the shakehand grip in 1932. After changing her grip, she was able to defeat her old nemesis and doubles partner, Mednyánszky.

[edit] Halls of Fame

Sipos was inducted into the International Table Tennis Foundation Hall of Fame in 1993.[1]

Sipos, who was Jewish, was inducted into the International Jewish Sports Hall of Fame in 1996.[2]
