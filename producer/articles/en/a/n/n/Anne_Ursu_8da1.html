Anne Ursu

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Anne Ursu is an American author based in Minneapolis, Minnesota, with (as of 2006) three published novels. She is also known as Batgirl, "the mistress of sass," and a popular unofficial blogger for the Minnesota Twins baseball team.

Batgirl's musings about her favorite team are found at www.bat-girl.com, which features her own take on the Twins' fortunes. Her friends and family also participate as guest bloggers, and are known collectively as "Team Batgirl". Batgirl may berate players when they play particularly poorly. During these "sucking times" players have "ass bats" and other colorfully named problems. When the Twins win, Batgirl names a "Boyfriend of the Day," usually bestowed upon the official boyfriend of a member of Team Batgirl. For example, her own official boyfriend is Joe Mauer.

Most players have nicknames that may only have relevance to regular readers, such as NBP — catcher Mike Redmond, who is said to have taken Naked Batting Practice to shake up his former team and get them out of a funk — or El Presidente — pitcher Johan Santana, the 2004 American League Cy Young award winner, who is recognized as the fearless leader of the Twins and the free world to all right-thinking peoples. Other names are more straightforward, such as Little Nicky Punto, Tiny Superhero (for Nick Punto's relatively small stature) and Sweetcheeks (for Torii Hunter's good looks and charming on- and off-field demeanor).

Batgirl is also fond of staging photographic re-enactments of great (or not-so-great) plays with Lego minifigures. Fearsome opponents will often be played by Lego Darth Vader.

[edit] Bibliography

  • Spilling Clarence, Hyperion, 2003—Winner of the 2003 New Voice (first-time author) award from the Minnesota Book Awards.
  • The Disapparation of James, Hyperion, 2004
  • The Shadow Thieves, Atheneum, 2006. (The first book in a trilogy, entitled The Cronus Chronicles).
