Annette M. Böckler

Annette Mirjam Böckler (born June 26, 1966) is currently a teaching assistant for Bible and Jewish Exegesis at the Hochschule fuer Juedische Studien (College for Jewish Studies) at the University of Heidelberg, and a writer and translator in the Jewish subject area. As of 2007 she will work as librarian at the Leo Baeck College London.

Böckler's scientific areas are Jewish Liturgy and the Bible. She originally studied Theology, Bible, and Ancient Near Eastern Studies in Tuebingen, Bern, Bonn and Cologne and concluded her studies in 1993 with the first ecclesiastical exam. After her practical training in adult education, paedagogy and homiletics she passed the second ecclesiastical exam in 1995 after which she worked as assistent teacher in Bible until autumn 1998. Then she gave up this career, because she was asked to translate the prayerbook for the rising liberal congregations in Germany. She translated the torah commentary by W.G. Plaut and other works for liberal Judaism in Germany. She helped to found a new Jewish publishing house with a focus on liberal Judaism in Germany. She became one of the first teachers of Abraham Geiger Kolleg.


Editorial and translation career: Annette M. Böckler translated and did the layout for a German Jewish prayer book Seder ha-Tefillot (Gütersloh Germany 1997). She also translated W. Gunther Plaut's Torah commentary into German and updated it as well as designed the layout of the book. The German edition appeared in five volumes between 1999 - 2004. For this project she needed to revise Moses Mendelssohn's torah translation. Other publications include an Introduction to the Synagogue Service from a liberal point of view (Einführung in den jüdischen Gottesdienst, Berlin 2001). She is the editor of the annual Jewish calendar Durch das Jüdische Jahr, published since 2001 by the Jüdische Verlagsanstalt Berlin.
