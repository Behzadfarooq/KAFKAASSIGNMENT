Anna of Austria (1573-1598)

Anna Queen of Poland and Sweden

Anna of Austria, or Anna of Habsburg (Polish:Anna Austriaczka,[1] Anna Habsburżanka, Anna Rakuszanka) (Graz August 16, 1573 - Warsaw February 2, 1598) was Queen of Poland and Sweden.

She was a daughter of Charles II of Austria and Maria Anna of Bavaria. Her paternal grandparents were Ferdinand I, Holy Roman Emperor and Anne of Bohemia (1503–1547), daughter of King Ladislaus II of Bohemia and Hungary and his wife Anne de Foix. Her maternal grandparents were Albert V, Duke of Bavaria and Anne Habsburg of Austria.

Anna was also a younger sister of Ferdinand II, Holy Roman Emperor, Margaret of Austria, Leopold V of Austria and an older sister of Constance of Austria.

Anna became the first wife of Sigismund of Poland and Sweden on May 31, 1592. This marriage was opposed by many nobles (szlachta) of the Polish-Lithuanian Commonwealth, who were opposed to the alliance with Habsburgs that Zygmunt pursued.

When Zygmunt sent a diplomatic mission, lead by cardinal Radziwill to Prague for his bride, Anna, the anti-Habsburg party with chancellor Jan Zamoyski guarded the borders to prevent archprincess from entering the country. Anna evaded the guards, arrived in Kraków and was coronated in May by Primas Karnkowski as the Queen of Poland, and two years later, in 1594, in Sweden, as the Queen of Sweden.

Anna of Austria coat of arms

Not very beautiful, she and Zygmunt fell in love. Anna gained the friendship and respect of many of her former enemies with her politeness and culture. It was during her lifetime that Zygmunt moved the capital of the Commonwealth from Kraków to Warsaw.

They had five children, but only Wladislaw lived to become an adult:

  1. Anna Maria (1593-1600)
  2. Katharina (1594-1594)
  3. Vladislaus (1595-1648), (reigned 1632-1648 as Władysław IV Waza of Poland)
  4. Katharina (1596-1597)
  5. Kristofer (1598-1598)

Anna died on February 2, 1598 and Sigismund III then married her sister Constance Renate Habsburg.
