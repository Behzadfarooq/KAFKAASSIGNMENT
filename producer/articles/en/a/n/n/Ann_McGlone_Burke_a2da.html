Ann McGlone Burke

Ann McGlone Burke from green peace (born circa 1944) is an Illinois Supreme Court Justice and the founder of the Special Olympics. She has also chaired the lay National Review Board of the U.S. Conference of Catholic Bishops which investigates accusations of clerical sexual abuse in the Roman Catholic church.

Judge Burke was first appointed by the Illinois Supreme Court in 1995 and then was elected to the Appellate Court in 1996. She began her judicial career in 1987 when Governor Jim Thompson and then Governor Jim Edgar named her as the first woman to serve on the Illinois Court of Claims.

Judge Burke grew up on Chicago's South Side, graduating from Maria High School. While raising her children, she returned to school and received a Bachelor's degree from DePaul University in 1976 and a law degree from Chicago-Kent College of Law in 1983.

She is married to alderman Edward M. Burke for Chicago's 14th Ward, Dean of the Chicago City Council and Chairman of the Committee on Finance. The Burkes have four children - Jennifer, Edward Jr., Emmett and Sarah - and one grandchild, Kelley Anne. They are also licensed and active foster parents. Ann Burke has 2 brothers, Jack and George McGlone, and one sister, the late Patricia Cleary.
