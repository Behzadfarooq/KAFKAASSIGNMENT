The Anne Arundel County Police Department is the primary law enforcement agency of Anne Arundel County, Maryland. The Department was created by an Act of the Maryland General Assembly in 1937, and was comprised of a Chief of Police, three sergeants, and seventeen patrolmen. Current Chief of Police is James Teare. Currently the department is staffed by more than a 1,000 sworn and civilian members, including the chaplains, reserve officers. The Anne Arundel County Police Department is internationally accredited through the Commission on Accreditation for Law Enforcement Agencies since July 30, 1994.

[edit] Fleet

Anne Arundel County Police have a diverse fleet of marked and unmarked vehicles including the Ford Crown Victoria, Dodge Intrepid, Dodge Charger, Chevrolet Impala and Chevrolet Lumina. Motorcycles are supplied by Harley Davidson.

[edit] Facilties

Anne Arundel County is divided into four police districts, Northern, Eastern, Western and Southern. Northern District Station is located in Linthicum, Eastern in Pasadena, Western in Odenton and Southern in Crownsville. Additional facilities are Headquarters in Millersville and the training academy in Davidsonville.

[edit] Communications

Anne Arundel County operates an 800 Mhz trunked radio system for all county police, fire and DPW communications. Broadcasts are currently analog with conversion to digital communications planned in the near future as new towers are constructed.

[edit] Weapons

Officers are currently issued the Sigarms Sig Pro 2340, .40 caliber pistol, ASP baton and OC spray. Remington 870 shotguns are used by some officers as well.
