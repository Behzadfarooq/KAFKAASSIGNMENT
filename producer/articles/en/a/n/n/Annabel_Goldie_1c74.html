Annabel Goldie (born 27 February 1950, Glasgow) is the leader of the Scottish Conservative and Unionist Party in the Scottish Parliament, and chairwoman of the party's Shadow Cabinet.

Goldie was educated at Kilmacolm Primary School and Greenock Academy, Inverclyde, and the University of Strathclyde, Glasgow, graduating with an LLB in 1971, and has been a solicitor with Glasgow law firm Donaldson, Alexander, Russell & Haddow since 1978.

Goldie was elected to the Scottish Parliament in the 1999 election as an additional member for the West of Scotland electoral region, and subsequently became deputy leader of the Scottish Conservatives. She retained this seat in the 2003 election. She also stands for the position of constituency MSP in West Renfrewshire but as of yet has lost out to the Labour incumbent.

Goldie became leader of the Scottish Conservatives in November 2005 following the resignation of David McLetchie, after details of McLetchie's expenses claims for taxi journeys were revealed. Goldie stood on a joint ticket with Murdo Fraser to take over her position as deputy. They were the only candidates nominated for the posts.
