[edit] Rock group

In 1998, the group was born on the cinders of the rock band Danger Sex, which had been led by Vice, the historical figure of Gorizia's rock, who unfortunately died prematurely.

Giulio Bonnes, the guitarist, and Ariberto Lacerenza, the bassist, started the musical adventure which led them to come across the (female) singer Anna Ballarin and the drummer Manuele Con. Together, they established the group Annanonvuole.

The group was born exclusively in order to produce its own music and their music was a pure rock (with lyrics in Italian).

One year later the bassist left the group because of disputes with the drummer. Ivo as a new bassist and Antonio Giaccone as the second guitar (these two coming mainly from pop background) joined the group, which turned towards a softer type of music. After a concert in Ronchi dei Legionari, the dispute about music erupted once again and the group dissolved. Giulio Bonnes, the guitarist, and Anna Ballarin, the singer, were the only members who stuck together. For a couple of months, while awaiting the arrival of bassist and guitarist, they continued by themselves to produce some new pieces.

In 2000 a new event took place, with Roman Šuligoj (bass and voice) and Jože Skok (drummer), both from the neighbouring Slovenia, arrived. The Annanonvuole were ready again to step on the scene. They represented the first mixed Italo - Slovene rock group (half of its members are Italian, and the other half are Slovene).

After a consistent number of concerts, mainly in Slovenia, the group (notwithstanding the lyrics in Italian) was able to arise a lot of interest and to date, the group has already been active for five years.

Meanwhile, their CD has been recorded by the Studio BOOM in Slovenia and the video has been prepared, too. One piece (Patetico il mondo) gained a notable success having been transmitted by the local radio station
(Radio Gorizia1). This success and the interest among the audience were so great that the piece remained on the programme for two months instead of the initially-planned two weeks.

Starting in 2006 singer Anna left the group, as for sake of her regular job couldn't join the others when the group moved to perform away. She was replaced by Monica. In July, Jože Skok, the drummer, left the group too, and was replaced by Miloš. The group has kept its own rock music unchanged and its concerts have been more and more attractive. The CD is in the making and should be released in 2007.
