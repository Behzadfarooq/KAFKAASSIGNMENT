Anna Wallace Suhr

Seoul City Sue is the nickname given by American GIs to the female announcer of a series of North Korean propaganda radio broadcasts during the Korean War. Her name is perhaps a play on the popular song "Sioux City Sue" of the 1940s.

She first went on the air in early August, 1950. Members of the US Army 588th Military Police Company first heard her about 10 August. She would read the names off of dog tags from killed American soldiers to a musical accompaniment.

Later that month, she was identified by the Methodist Missionary Organization as American-born Mrs. Anna Wallace Suhr, wife of a Korean newsman. The group said that Suhr, at the time in her mid-40s, was a former missionary schoolteacher in Korea, and had tutored American diplomats' children in Seoul. Suhr's defenders claimed the dull tone of her broadcasts as proof that she was being forced to make them.

[edit] References in Popular Culture

In the M*A*S*H episode "38 Across," a woman identifying herself as "Seoul City Sue" is heard on a radio broadcast over the camp's PA accusing Hawkeye Pierce of illegal experimentation on a North Korean soldier he had performed brain surgery on.
