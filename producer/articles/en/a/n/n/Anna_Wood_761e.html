Anna Wood

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Also: Anna Wood, Australian canoeist

Anna Wood was a schoolgirl from Sydney, Australia, who died at the age of 15 on October 24, 1995 at Royal North Shore Hospital, St. Leonards, N.S.W., after taking an ecstasy tablet at a dance party the previous night. The direct cause of death was a cerebral edema, caused by water intoxication and resultant hyponatremia. Her death, widely reported in the media, sparked off a moral panic regarding drugs and their perceived role in rave and dance party culture. The event had political implications on drug policy, particularly in her home state of New South Wales. Media coverage frequently played on her appearance as an average, middle-class, fresh-faced "good" girl to convey the message that similar tragedies could befall any youngster in Middle Australia. Her death is also notable in that it preceded by mere weeks that of the British teenager Leah Betts, the circumstances and resultant media coverage of which were strikingly similar.

Wood's death was the subject of Anna's Story, a drug-education book published in 1996.

More recently Australian NSW opposition leader, Peter Debnam, invoked the incident of Anna Woods in a March 2007 campaign event. Tony Wood, Anna Wood's father, was a signatory to a letter saying that they had been let down by the Labor government which was in power at the time of Anna's death.[1]
