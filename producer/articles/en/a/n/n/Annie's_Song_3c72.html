"Annie's Song" is a song recorded and written by singer/songwriter John Denver. It was his second number one song in the USA, occupying that spot for two weeks in July 1974. It also went to number one in the UK.

"Annie's Song" was written as an ode to Denver's then-wife, Annie Denver (Annie Martel) of Sleepy Eye, Minnesota. While hiking through a state park in nearby New Ulm, Minnesota, Denver started to marvel at the beauty around, and, thinking about his wife, penned the song in a few minutes. The song has since become a wedding standard and an expression of love for many people, due to its grand imagery and the fact it could apply to anyone (Annie is not mentioned by name in any part of the song).

[edit] Covers

  • In 1978, flautist James Galway took an instrumental version to number three in the UK. Its tune is also used in the UK by the "The Greasy Chip Butty Song."
  • Love Their Country, the latest album from Me First and the Gimme Gimmes, features a punk rock cover of Annie's Song. Some of the original lyrics were changed.
  • In 2004, Weezer front man Rivers Cuomo posted his cover of "Annie's Song" on his MySpace page.[1] The song was removed due to copyright issues though mp3s of the song are still floating around the web.
  • The song has been modified and used by fans of Sheffield United Football club as a chant on the terraces.
  • In 2006, Christian artist Travis Cottrell released a cover of "Annie's Song" on his album Found
