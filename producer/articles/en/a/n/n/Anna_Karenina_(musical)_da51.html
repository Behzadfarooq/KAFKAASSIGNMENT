Anna Karenina (musical)

Anna Karenina is a musical with a book and lyrics by Peter Kellogg and music by Daniel Levine.

Based on the classic Leo Tolstoy novel of the same name, it focuses on the tragic title character, a fashionable unhappily married woman, and her ill-fated liaison with Count Vronsky, which ultimately leads to her downfall.

After eighteen previews, the Broadway production, directed by Theodore Mann and choreographed by Patricia Birch, opened on August 26, 1992 at the Circle in the Square Theatre where, hampered by dismal reviews, it ran for only 46 performances. The cast included Ann Crumb, John Cunningham, Scott Wentworth, Gregg Edelman, Melissa Errico, and Jerry Lanning.
