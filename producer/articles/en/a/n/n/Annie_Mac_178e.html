Annie Mac

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Annie Mac DJing at Aston University

Annie MacManus (better known as Annie Mac) is a disc jockey who hosts an eponymous electronic dance music show on BBC Radio 1 in the United Kingdom which airs at 10pm (British time) on Friday evenings.

Born in Dublin, in the Republic of Ireland (18 July 1978[1]), Annie moved to Belfast at the age of seventeen to study at the Queen's University of Belfast. While at QUB she regularly frequented Shine, a club within the university's student union. This led to her working for the club, in promotion and marketing. After completing her degree and moving to London, she worked for a number of small radio stations, before moving to Radio 1. Before getting her own show she worked as a broadcast assistant and producer to Steve Lamacq, Colin Murray and Zane Lowe.

Annie hosts two shows on BBC Radio 1. Her main show, called Annie Mac's Mash Up airs 10:00pm-12:00pm on Friday nights. Her second show is on Sunday nights 9:00pm-10:00pm. A popular feature of Annie Mac's Mash Up is the Annie Mac Mini Mix, an opportunity to showcase various DJs ability to produce a five minute DJ mix. The Mini Mix was also a feature of her previous show on a thursday night.

As well as her own Friday and Sunday night slots Annie also covers for other Radio 1 shows. She is a regular stand in for Early Breakfast and Zane Lowe's show. Annie also stands in for Jo Whiley and Pete Tong's shows.

Annie makes regular appearances as a feature presenter on BBC2's 'The Culture Show', giving her opinions on the latest music offerings. She appeared on 'Never Mind the Buzzcocks' in January 2006 and she co-presented Top Of The Pops on June 18, 2006 with Reggie Yates and on July 11, 2006 with Rufus Hound in a T in the Park special. She regularly DJs at clubs such as Fabric and university venues such as Fitzwilliam College, Cambridge and Aston University (as shown by photo above).

Annie's brother Davey MacManus is in the British band The Crimea.

On 18th February 2007, BBC Radio 1 launched their first video podcast or vodcast. It featured Annie on a backstage tour around the Radio 1 buildings.
