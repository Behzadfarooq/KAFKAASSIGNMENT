Ann Plato

Ann Plato was a nineteenth century American Black educator and author. She was believed to be the second Black female to publish a book in America as well as the first Black woman to publish a book of essays.

Plato was born about 1820 in Hartford, Connecticut. Like many Blacks that lived in America during this time, there is very little information recorded about her. Most of the information that is known about Plato comes from the introduction to her book that was written by Reverend W.C. Pennington, pastor of the Colored Congregational Church of Hartford, who also called her "Platoess".

Plato was thought to have an Indian (Native American) father. She was employed as a school teacher at the Black Zion Methodist Church School of Hartford. She was also a member of the Talcott Street Congregational Church in Hartford.

In 1841, she published her only known book entitled, Essays: Including Biographies and Miscellanoeus Pieces in Prose and Poetry.

The essays reflected the New England Puritan values of her environment. Topics for the essays included "Benevolence," "Education," "Employment" and "Religion." The essays also stressed both the importance of education and of leading a pious, industrious life.

The book also contained some poetry and biographies of departed female friends and acquaintances.

Some critics from later generations found Plato's essays and poetry to be overly moralizing as well as routine and lacking in originality. Many of them also derided her for not mentioning the issue of slavery in America, as some of her near contemporaries like Frances Harper and Charlotte Forten Grimke did. Her one reference to slavery in her book concerns the abolition of slavery in the West Indies in 1838.

Nothing is known about Plato's life after her book was published in 1841. A year of death also cannot be located for her.

In 1988, Oxford University Press released The Schomburg Library of Nineteenth-Century Black Women Writers with Professor Henry Louis Gates as the series' general editor. Plato's book was reprinted as a part of this collection.

Trinity College established the Ann Plato Fellowship in her honor.

[edit] Quote

"A good education is that which prepares us for our future sphere of action and makes us contented with that situation in life in which God, in his infinite mercy, has seen fit to place us, to be perfectly resigned to our lot in life, whatever it may be."
