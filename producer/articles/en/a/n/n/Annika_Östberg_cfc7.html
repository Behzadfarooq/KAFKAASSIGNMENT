Annika Maria Östberg Deasy, born 1954 in Stockholm, Sweden is a Swedish citizen incarcerated in California, USA on a not determed period (25 years to life sentence) convicted for felony murder on a restaurant guest and a police officer in 1981.

[edit] Childhood and marriage

Annika Östberg grew up in Hässelby in Stockholm, but moved together with her mother to California in the 1960s. When she became older she escaped from home and run away to San Francisco, where she became a drug addict. She married Brian Deasy and left the drugs behind. But when the marriage failed she fell back to drugs.

[edit] Press and sentence's background

The Annika Östberg case has upset many Scandinavians since Mrs. Deasy was not armed and did not commit any murder herself. Although in her statements to the police she implied that she did the killings. If convicted in her home country she would get only 6-7 years imprisonment (murderers in Sweden usually don't have to serve more than 8-12 years in prison). But she was sentenced to 25 years to life for two murders, which she did not commit, but in the eyes of California law, she was just as culpable as the perpetrator because she was present. When her lawyers told her to fight for her life and her rights, she would not help them, and instead cooperated with the police.

[edit] Sentence

In 1983, Mrs. Deasy accepted her sentence of 25 years to life for the 1981 murders. At this time it was customary for well behaving prisoners to be free after serving approximately half of their sentence. It is obvious from documents from those years that Mrs. Deasy's lawyers believed she could be out after 12 1/2 years. However, time and politics changed throughout the years, and as the sentencing laws changed, so did the mindset of the Board of Prison Terms and the Governors office in Sacramento. Previously, life prisoners served their sentences according to the law and their behavior in prison, however, this was not the case after 2000. In 2005, the Board of Prison Terms, which determines whether parole should be granted, grants very few. The very few prisoners who receive a grant of parole, are then 'generally' denied by the Governor.

[edit] Governor Schwarzenegger's denial

California governor Arnold Schwarzenegger denied in an interview in August 2005 with the state Swedish television to let her be transferred to Sweden for serving the remaining period in her native country. Governor Schwarzenegger stated that Mrs. Deasy is "an evil murderer who has to serve her penalty".

[edit] Campaigns

Campaigns both in the United States and in Scandinavia urge for her sentence to be time determed and that she must be allowed to serve the remaining imprisonment in her home country.
