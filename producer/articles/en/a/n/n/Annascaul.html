Annascaul, or Anascaul, (Abhainn an Scáil or Abha na Scáil in Irish) is a village in County Kerry, Ireland. The name has different meanings, depending on which translation is used. It can mean Scáil's River (Scáil Ní Mhúirnáin is a character in a local legend), River of the Shadows or Ford of the Heroes.

It had a population of 202 according to the 2002 (CSO census).


[edit] Geography

Annascaul lies in the southern foothills of the Slieve Mish mountains. To the north of the village is Loch Scáil, which drains southwards into Dingle Bay via the river Scáil. Annascaul is located on the N86 road between Tralee and Dingle, on the Dingle Peninsula.

[edit] History

Annascaul was the birthplace of the Antarctic explorer Tom Crean, who was part of Robert Scott's ill-fated attempts to reach the South Pole
