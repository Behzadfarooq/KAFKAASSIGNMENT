Tomb Raider: Anniversary, previously known as Tomb Raider: 10th Anniversary Edition, is the next release of the Tomb Raider series. It is now confirmed by Eidos/SCi that this will be a remake of the original Tomb Raider game from 1996, using an improved version of the Tomb Raider: Legend game engine.[1] Eidos claims it will include all the original levels from Tomb Raider.[1]

The game is being developed by Crystal Dynamics for Sony's PlayStation Portable, PlayStation 2, and Microsoft Windows. The release date is set for May 2007,[2] which will actually mark the year of the 11th anniversary for the Tomb Raider series.

On October 27, 2006, Eidos Interactive revealed the first official Tomb Raider: Anniversary screenshot.[3] The level appears to be the Lost Valley.

Gamespot.com have since released a media overhaul of Tomb Raider Anniversary on December 21, 2006, with the very first trailer of the game (which ended dramatically with the arrival of the T-Rex) and screenshots (mostly from the "Lost Valley" level).[4] The official Tomb Raider website opened on the next day.[2]

[edit] Remake status

  • It is confirmed that Anniversary is an enhanced remake of the original 1996 platform best-seller, built using the Tomb Raider: Legend engine. It's not a 1:1 remake, but it follows the same basic plot and builds on it. It's supposed to feature the original game key moments and all the levels present on the original game rebuilt from scratch to match with the more dynamic new abilites of Lara.[5]
  • Tomb Raider: Legend composer Troels B. Folmann will be returning for Anniversary[6], and a rearranged version of the main theme of the original game was made available when the site first opened.[7]
  • Anniversary will feature the ring inventory from the first game.[8]

[edit] Core Design: Tomb Raider 10th Anniversary Edition for PSP

A video game trailer showing footage of a new Tomb Raider game was distributed on the Internet on June 8, 2006. The titles and logos of the trailer claimed that the title was Lara Croft Tomb Raider: Anniversary Edition, a PSP game by Core Design. The trailer featured Lara Croft in familiar yet remodeled environments from the original Tomb Raider, complete with new animations, and interactions which were much more elaborate than what was in the original release of the game. The footage from the trailer was running on the PSP hardware. Video game discussion forums had been speculating about the veracity of the trailer, because it featured a recognizable film score (Duel of the Fates from Star Wars Episode I: The Phantom Menace) that caused some viewers to question whether it was a real project. Core has since admitted the trailer was from a cancelled project.[9]The video is now difficult to find across the Internet, since it infriges copyright and was not supposed to be revealed to the public.

The next week, Eidos Interactive announced that it would be making Tomb Raider: 10th Anniversary Edition for PS2, PSP, and Windows. It would be designed by Crystal Dynamics, which replaced Core after the commercial failure of Tomb Raider: The Angel of Darkness and went on to create the seventh entry in the series, Tomb Raider: Legend.[10]

[edit] What will be in the game

[edit] Confirmed regular levels

  • Peru[1] - Lara is hired by Jacqueline Natla to find the Scion hidden in the Tomb of Qualopec, hidden in the Incan city of Vilcambamba. Peru has already been confirmed, with the Lost Valley appearing in the recent trailer. Confirmed enemies include: T-Rexes, Raptors, Bears, wolves, Bats and an appearance from Larson.
  • Greece[1] - After interrogating Larson, Lara finds herself in Greece, inside St. Francis Folly to find the second part of the Scion. Levels include: St. Francis Folly, Colosseum, Palace Midas, The Cistern, and the Tomb of Tihocan. Enemies from the original level included: gorillas, rats, lions, crocodiles, and many appearances of Pierre Du Pont.
A Black Panther attacking Lara in Egypt.
  • Egypt[1] - Lara tracks the last piece of the Scion to Egypt, hidden in the City of Khamoon. Levels include: City of Khamoon, Obelisk of Khamoon, and the Sanctuary of the Scion. Confirmed enemies include: panthers, mummy mutants (which bear the resemblance of tall biped mummified panthers and can now throw fireballs), crocodiles and the final appearance of Larson. Atlantean mutants, winged mutants, and centaur mutants have yet to be confirmed.
  • Atlantis[1] - Lara follows Natla to the Great Pyramid of Atlantis to retrieve, then destroy the Scion. Levels include: Natla's Mines, Atlantis, and the Great Pyramid. Enemies from the original level included: Natla's henchmen, Atlantean mutants, winged mutants, centaur mutants, giant mutant, and the final appearance of Natla.

[edit] Confirmed extra levels

  • Lara's Home[5] - PSW was the first source to confirm the appearance of Lara's Home in Tomb Raider: Anniversary. The magazine even states that it is "the biggest, most detailed mansion yet".

[edit] Confirmed gameplay elements

  • Zip and Alister will not return. There will not be a headset.[5]
  • Lara will no longer have a PDA, PLS or Binoculars, but the grapple has returned in the form of a hook grapple. It is not magnetic.[11][12]
  • Dual Uzis and the shotgun are confirmed to make a return. Recent official screenshots also show holstered black pistols believed to be the magnums, present in the original Tomb Raider.
  • Enemies include Bats, Wolves, Bears, Black Panthers, Crocodiles, Raptors, the T-Rex, and the mutants and monsters from the Atlantis levels.[1][11] They will be significantly more difficult to defeat as they will attack in groups.[11]
  • The only human enemies that have been currently confirmed are Larson Conway[13], Pierre DuPont[13] and Jacqueline Natla.[11]
  • The blackouts from Tomb Raider Legend (in which the screen faded to black almost immediately after Lara's death) will not return.
  • The Lara model has been upgraded with more polygons and has inherited a few new moves, a pole-hopping move where Lara can balance on narrow surfaces. Also from a recent trailer, it can be seen that Lara can run across walls while swinging from her hook grapple[5]
  • Most of Lara's "Legend" animations have been revamped to make everything more realistic
  • The levels are bigger and more detailed than they were in the original, players will be able to discover more than just one path for Lara to take.[5]
  • Players should expect a minimum of 15 hours of gameplay, significantly longer than Tomb Raider: Legend.[11]
  • Interactive cutscenes will make a return[14]
  • The auto-grab function from Tomb Raider Legend will be optional. [15]
  • A journal will be accessible in game for hints.

[edit] Confirmed extras

  • Finding secrets will play an important part in the game, like in the original Tomb Raider.[5]
  • Several costumes might be unlockable, like in Tomb Raider Legend. Journalists from the PSM3 magazine were able to see the swimsuit costume from Tomb Raider II during a press demonstration of Tomb Raider: Anniversary.[11]
  • An unlockable director's commentary with Toby Gard has been confirmed.[11]

[edit] Characters

  • Lara Croft: The heroine of the game, Lara Croft is a British archeologist working for hire to recover lost artifacts, whether from tombs, or the clutches of selfish collectors.
  • Winston Smith: Lara's butler. He has served the Croft family for generations. He became a live-in servant after his wife passed away.
  • Jacqueline Natla: The CEO of Natla Industries. She hires Lara Croft to search for three pieces of the artifact "The Scion". Natla, however, is not all she seems...
  • Larson Conway: One of the five mercenaries that Natla has hired for personal reasons. They are also 'experienced' Tomb Raiders.
  • Pierre DuPont: One of the five mercenaries that Natla has hired for personal reasons. They are also 'experienced' Tomb Raiders.
  • Qualopec: One of the ancient rulers of the lost continent of Atlantis, whose tomb is located in Peru.
  • Tihocan: Another ruler of Atlantis, Tihocan was buried in Greece.

[edit] ESRB Rating

The game has been rated T for Teen in the United States. This rating was given because of "Mild Suggestive Themes and Violence" in the game.

[edit] PEGI Rating

The game has been given the rating of 16+ in the UK, as confirmed by the packshot of the game that was revealed on 10th February, 2007. The rating is a step above from the 12+ of Legend and The Angel of Darkness.

[edit] Trailers

As of present, two trailers have been released. The first (December 21, 2006) revealed an FMV sequence, in which Lara slid into The Lost Valley, battled several raptors and suddenly turned at the sound of the T-rex approaching. What followed was then several seconds of gameplay footage from that famous level, including combat against bears, wolves and bats.

The second trailer (February 23, 2007), showed one of the opening cutscenes (Lara's guide being attacked by the wolves) and gameplay footage from Egypt. A new move, in which Lara ran across a wall whilst gripping onto the grapple, was shown during the video. It also showed some mummies attacking Lara. Some of the moves they did included a fireball throw, unlike their original counterparts, which didn't use fireballs (however, the unmummified versions did), and another part of the trailer showed the mummy standing up to look around, another move inherited from the original counterparts. The trailer revealed also that the game was planned for a May release.

[edit] Gameplay footage

On 22 March 2007, a four-minute Play TV video, revealing exclusive Egypt gameplay footage of Tomb Raider: Anniversary, was released. There was also a brief several seconds footage from the first level in Peru, in which Lara walks towards the entrance to the City of Vilcabamba. Later, there was a video of Lara fighting the T-Rex. Since then several clips from GameTrailers.com have been released, all showing gameplay footage of Peru.
