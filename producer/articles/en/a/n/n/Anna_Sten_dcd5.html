Anna Sten

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Anna Sten, born Anel Sudakevich, (3 December 1908-12 November 1993) was a Russian actress.

Sten was born in Kiev, Russia, (now the Ukraine) the daughter of an Ukrainian ballet master who died when she was 12 and a Swedish mother. She worked as a waitress when she was discovered by the influential stage director Konstantin Stanislavsky. He arranged for her to audition at the Moscow Film Academy. She acted in films and plays in Russia, then went to Germany where she made an impression in the film The Brothers Karamazov (1931)
