Ann Purzner is the mayor of Overland, Missouri who was elected by 5 votes in April 2006[1]. She has been the subject of some controversy.

[edit] Controversy

Immediately after taking office, Purzner attempted to replace the police chief and city attorney without the consent of the city council[2]. Purzner also ran into trouble over where to hold city council meetings. Purzner was fined $1 and the city was forced to file plaintiff's fees after a lawsuit arguing that Purzner's use of a small area for council meetings violated the state's open meeting law [3]. She misrepresented herself during her mayoral campaign by claiming to have been a registered nurse, even though the state Board of Nursing warned her in 2002 not practice or represent herself as a nurse [4] [5]. In May 2006, St. Louis County prosecutor Bob McCulloch labeled Purzner a "liar" with "zero credibility", and sued her for exceeding her power as mayor. A judge later ruled that against Purzner over the dismissal of the police chief and attorney and both have been reinstated.

A group of Overland residents have begun a recall campaign which is scheduled to be on the ballot in April, 2007[6]. The mayor stated that "I have always tried to do the best for the community...I am like an angel."[7]

Mayor Purzner attempted to block the Recall effort by filing suit against the election board. [8] The judge ruled against the Mayor, pointing out that a proper vote occurred on December 12th, 2006 to send the Recall to the election board. [9]
