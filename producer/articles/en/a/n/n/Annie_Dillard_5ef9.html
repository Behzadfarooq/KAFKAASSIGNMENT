Annie Dillard (born 30 April 1945 in Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania) is an American author.

Dillard was raised in Point Breeze. She attended The Ellis School, and began writing poetry while there. She earned her B.A. and M.A. at Hollins University in Roanoke, Virginia where she married her writing professor, R. H. W. Dillard. After nearly dying from pneumonia, Dillard began a diary, which formed the basis of Pilgrim at Tinker Creek.

She won the Pulitzer Prize in 1975 for Tinker Creek. She is also well known for her memoir about growing up in Pittsburgh, An American Childhood.

Dillard also wrote an essay called The Deer at Providencia and the book "Teaching a Stone to Talk".
