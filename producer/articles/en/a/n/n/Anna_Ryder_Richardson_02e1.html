Anna Ryder Richardson

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Anna Ryder Richardson (born 29 January 1964) is a British interior designer and television presenter.

She has appeared in several home-design shows; most notably "Changing Rooms" for the BBC. She currently lives in Glasgow, Scotland with her husband, Glasgow restaurateur Colin MacDougall and two children, Bibi Belle and Dixie Dot.

In summer 2006, she was a celebrity showjumper in the BBC's Sport Relief event Only Fools on Horses. She also appeared on the CBBC show Hider in The House. She has recently stated she wants to adopt a Chinese baby and that people should do this rather than get pets.

She is sometimes mistakenly linked with a lawsuit involving Arnold Schwarzenegger. It was actually a different presenter named Anna Richardson, presenter of Big Screen, a movie review programme.
