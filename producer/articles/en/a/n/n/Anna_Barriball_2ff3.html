Anna Barriball (born 1972, Plymouth, UK) is an artist based in Berlin.

Barriball received her BA from Winchester School of Art [1] in 1995 and her MA from Chelsea College of Art [2] in 2000.

She has shown work internationally in many exhibitions including “The British Art Show 6” at many venues throughout the UK, “Rewind” at Glassbox in Paris, “Superfluity” at Latvijas Makslas Akademija [3] in Riga and “Bonobos” at Niels Borch Jensen Galerie [4] in Berlin. She is represented by Frith Street Gallery [5] in London and Ingleby Gallery [6] in Edinburgh.

Anna Barriball’s revealing interventions into the lives of ordinary objects expose the limits of use, recognition and meaning. Taking found objects and making subtle adjustments, Barriball reanimates the forgotten and broken in order to highlight the everyday achievements of a plastic bag, an old wardrobe, a job lot of jumble sale photographs etc. By giving a new context and narrative to otherwise anonymous things, Barriball blurs the line between function and form, history and fiction, fact and fairytale. Making uncomplicated propositions with simple hands-on gestures, Barriball’s works are humble but forthright statements about the very nature of objects and their passage through the world.
