Annabeth Schott, played by Kristin Chenoweth, is a fictional character on the political drama The West Wing. She was first hired as media consultant/deputy press secretary during Season 6 (with Chenoweth in a recurring guest role) and later became a campaign staff member (when Chenoweth became part of the regular cast during Season 7).

Spoiler warning: Plot and/or ending details follow.

Schott was a media consultant before coming to work for the West Wing as a Deputy Press Secretary, a post she took after C.J. Cregg became White House Chief of Staff.

In the final episode of Season 6, Schott helped Leo McGarry coordinate the Democratic National Convention. She subsequently joined the staff of the presidential campaign of Matthew Santos. In the Santos campaign she has taken on the role of chief aide to McGarry, who is the Democratic nominee for Vice President. When Matthew Santos took office, Annabeth became the press secretary to the First Lady, Helen Santos.

[edit] Personal life

Little is known about Schott's private life:

  • She graduated with a degree in Art History.
  • She handles her fear of flying by routinely taking pills and drinking champagne before flying, steps designed to knock her unconscious for the duration of the flight.
  • She once remarked, "I always have a date."
  • In the seventh season episode "Running Mates," it is revealed that she has a cat. In this episode, someone emailed information to the press, bashing Leo McGarry's debating skills. In the end, we find out that Leo was the one doing it, to benefit himself. He then finishes by saying to Annabeth "Next time, don't use your cat's name for the password to your email account."
  • She was born in the South and watches NASCAR for the attractive drivers (who, according to Schott, are "a bunch of studs").

During Season 7 there seemed to be the suggestion of a romance between her and Vice-Presidential candidate Leo McGarry — this was hinted at when she turned down the chance to eat dinner with him, citing "tension" between the two of them, and when she was said to be singing about Leo in the final days of the campaign - however Leo remained oblivious to any developments.

Schott found McGarry unconscious in his hotel room at the end of election day. She had him rushed to the hospital, where he died.
