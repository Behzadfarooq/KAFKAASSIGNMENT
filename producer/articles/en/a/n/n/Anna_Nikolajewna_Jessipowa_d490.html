Anna Nikolajewna Jessipowa (russian Анна Николаевна Есипова, also Anna Essipova, Annette Essipoff, Annette Essipova, Annette von Essipow) (1851-1914) was a Russian pianist, born in St. Petersburg. She was one of Leschetitzky's most brilliant pupils. She made her début in St. Petersburg in 1874; then entered upon artistic travels which brought her in 1876 to the United States, where her playing was greatly admired. She heard the playing of Fanny Bloomfield and advised her to train under Leschetitzky, whom she married in 1880, but they were later divorced. From 1893 to 1908 she was professor of pianoforte at the St. Petersburg Conservatory. Among her students were Sergei Prokofiev and Thomas de Hartmann.
