The Annals of Human Genetics, formerly known as the Annals of Eugenics is a scientific journal concerning human genetics. The Annals of Eugenics was established in 1925 by Karl Pearson, the first Galton Professor, who earlier in 1901 had established Biometrika. The name was later changed to reflect changing views on the role of eugenics in society. It does not appear among the over 14,000 scientific journals listed by the Institute for Scientific Information indicating that it does not meet the standards of that organization for listing.[1]
