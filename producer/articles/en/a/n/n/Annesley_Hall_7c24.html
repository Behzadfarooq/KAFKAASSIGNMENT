Annesley Hall

Annesley Hall is the all-female residence at Victoria College, University of Toronto campus. The residence is a National Historic Site located across from the Royal Ontario Museum.

Built in 1903, it was home to the first female resident at the University, as well as the first female to graduate from a Canadian medical school.

Annesley Hall was featured as the setting in the 1974 horror movie Black Christmas.

The Original Annesley Hall is housed in Nottinghamshire,UK and dates back to 1188 when it was first built over the tears it has had many extensions and rebuilds until its previous occupiers left in 1977. The first owner was the Lord of Annesleye and it was occupied for 300 years by this line before it was sold to the Chaworths in 1501 who were a local wealthy family of military propriety until the 18th century when Marie-Anne Chaworth married John Musters another man of the military, after this point the family was known as the Chaworth-Musters. Most notably is the links with Newstead Abbey and the Poet Lord Byron who was in love with Marie-Anne Chaworth and often frequented the hall. The hall now lies in dereliction after a fire in 1997. It is also notable for its hauntings which many have seen through the years.
