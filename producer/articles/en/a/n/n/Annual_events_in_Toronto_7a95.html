Numerous festivals, shows and exhibitions are held annually in Toronto. They include:

[edit] January

  • Winterlicious.

[edit] February

  • Canadian International AutoShow - at the Metro Convention Centre.
  • Winter City.

[edit] March

  • Winter Salsa Carnival - an annual Salsa dancing festival starting in 2006.
  • St Patrick's Day Parade.
  • L'Oreal Fashion Week.

[edit] April

  • Easter Day Parade.
  • Markland Wood - Easter Egg Hunt - Held on Easter Monday for the community's children at Millwood Park.
  • Tartan Day T.O. - [1] - Held on Friday, April 13th 2007 at Nathan Phillips Square from Noon to 2:00 p.m.
  • Kirkin of the Tartan Fundraising Concert - [http://www.concertshowcaseinternational.com} - Saturday April 14th, 2007 at St. Andrew's Presbyterian Church featuring Caledon Scotland's Tenors and Canada's own Peter Ian McCutcheon.
  • Sporting Life 10K.
  • Markland Wood - Environmental Day Clean Up - Held in keeping with the City of Toronto's Environmental Day, Saturday.

[edit] May

  • Cabbagetown Forsythia Festival - Held the first Sunday in May.
  • Markland Wood - Community Garage Sale - Held on the last Saturday in May.
  • Doors Open Toronto.

[edit] June

  • Barbados on the Water Festival.
  • North by Northeast.
  • Pride Week is the world's second largest gay pride festival.
  • Toronto Downtown Jazz Festival.
  • Toronto International Dragon Boat Festival.
  • Template:Art of Jazz Celebration 2007.

[edit] July

  • Molson Grand Prix of Toronto.
  • Caribana is North America's largest street festival, showcasing Caribbean/West Indian culture (in some years early August).
  • Beaches International Jazz Festival.

[edit] August

  • Markland Wood - Family Fun Day.
  • The Canadian National Exhibition (CNE, or "the Ex").
  • The Taste of the Danforth festival showcases the mostly Greek culture of The Danforth and has expanded to include other cultures of the area.
  • The Mabuhay Philippines Toronto Summer Festival- the largest celebration of Philippine heritage and culture in Canada.
  • Festival of South Asia - A festival of the South Asian culture of Gerrard India Baazaar.

[edit] September

  • The Canadian International Air Show.
  • The Toronto International Film Festival is considered one of the big three global film events, with Cannes and Berlin, with more screens and more films than either.
  • The Beach Celtic Festival [2] on September 16th, 2007 from noon - 5:00 pm in Kew Gardens in the Beach to celebrate the Celtic-Canadian Heritage.
  • Markland Wood - Community Social - The Champagne/Marklandtini; held on the last Saturday of the month at the Markland Wood Country (Golf) Club.
  • Cabbagetown Fall Festival, - Held the second weekend in September.

[edit] October

  • The Toronto Marathon.

[edit] November

  • The Santa Claus Parade (Started in 1904 with just a single float, it now boasts over 24 floats, 24 bands, and 1700 participants. It is one of the biggest productions in North America, and broadcast to many countries around the world.)
  • Cavalcade of Lights.
  • The Royal Agricultural Winter Fair, an annual fall fair started in 1922.

[edit] December

  • Markland Wood - Christmas Caravan & Food Drive - Held on the last or second last Sunday in December.
  • First Night.
