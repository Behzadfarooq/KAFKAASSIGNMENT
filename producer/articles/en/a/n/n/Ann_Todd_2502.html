Ann Todd (January 24, 1909-May 6, 1993) was a popular English actress and producer.

She was born in Hartford, Cheshire and was educated at St Winifrid's School in Eastbourne. She was married three times. Her first husband Victor Malcolm was a grandson of Lillie Langtry. Her second (Nigel Tangye) and third husband (Sir David Lean) were first cousins.

She became a popular actress in such films as Perfect Strangers (1945) (as a nurse) and The Seventh Veil (1947) (as a troubled concert pianist), as well as several of Lean's films including Madeleine (1949) and The Sound Barrier (1952). She later produced a series of travel films. She died from a stroke aged 84.
