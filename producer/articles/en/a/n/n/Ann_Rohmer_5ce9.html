Ann Rohmer

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Ann Margaret Rohmer (born March 1958) is a television personality in Toronto, Canada.

[edit] Television career

In 1979, she hosted the syndicated programme Show Biz. Hired by Global TV, she was a host on the award-winning magazine show Thatâ€™s Life. Following that she was a features reporter on CTV's Canada AM.

In 1986, joined City TV as the CityPulse Weekend sports anchor. She was the first Canadian female sports reporter allowed to interview athletes in their changing rooms. Three years later, she began waking up at 5:00 a.m. to host BT-Breakfast Television - a routine she maintained for a 12-year run. Deciding it was time for a change, in October 2001, Rohmer became Anchor for CP24 and host of City Online, an interactive talk show that gives viewers the chance to comment on the news as it is covered. In February 2002, she also took on hosting duties for CP24's new real estate show, Hot Property. She also hosts Animal House Calls. Rohmer is also well-known for her support of the World Wildlife Fund and other animal-related charities.

Starting on September 11, 2006, she began co-anchoring with Mark Dailey on CityNews Tonight at 11. She follows Alex Pierson, who had been a co-anchor since 2002.

[edit] Personal life

Her parents are Richard Rohmer and Margo Rohmer and she has a younger sister, Catherine, who is a lawyer. Trained by top skating coach Sheldon Galbraith, Rohmer competed at the provincial level until she was involved in a car accident at the age of 14. Although this ended her skating career, it has not stopped her from playing tennis, being an aerobics instructor and avidly participating in both water and downhill skiing. She graduated from Northern Secondary School in 1976 and worked as a flight attendant for Air Canada.

Rohmer lives in the Queen Street West neighbourhood and is well-known for her feisty Jack Russell terrier, Lucy. She was previously married to Canadian skiier Steve Podborski.

[edit] Awards

Rohmer has been given the following awards:

  • 1999, voted by the Toronto Sun as one of the "10 Sexiest Women" in Toronto.
  • 1994 and 1999 voted by the Toronto Sun as one of Toronto's favourite television personalities.
  • 1998 Variety Children's Charity "Outstanding Community Volunteer.
  • Nominated for ACTRA's "Best Newcomer of the Year.
  • Gordon Sinclair Award for "Excellence in Broadcasting.
  • 2001 Escoffier Society Media Award.
