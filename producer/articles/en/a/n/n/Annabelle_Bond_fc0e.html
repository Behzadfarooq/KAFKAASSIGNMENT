Annabelle Bond

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Annabelle Bond (born 1969), is a British fundraiser and socialite.

She has held the title of second fastest woman to complete the 103 kilometre MacLehose Trail marathon in Hong Kong.

In 2005 she became the fastest woman and fourth fastest person ever to climb all Seven Summits, when she climbed them all in 360 days. She also reached the summit of Mount Everest in 2004, becoming the fourth British woman ever to successfully climb the mountain. Her televised climb of Everest portrayed her as the glamourous blonde who brought her lipstick to the summit of Everest [1].

She is a charity fundraiser and heads the Eve Appeal to raise money for Ovarian Cancer.

She is the daughter of British banker Sir John Bond, former Chairman of HSBC and current Chairman of Vodafone.
