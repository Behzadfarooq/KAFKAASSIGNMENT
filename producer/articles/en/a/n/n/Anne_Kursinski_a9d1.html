Anne Kindig Kursinski (born April 16, 1959) is an international rider from the United States, who competes in the sport of show jumping. She is one of the leading woman riders in the sport today. Kursinski currently resides at Market Street Farm, Frenchtown, New Jersey.

She is also the younger sister of Rob Kursinski, who teaches at the University of Arizona in Tucson.

[edit] Accomplishments

2002

  • 1st Garden State Grand Prix, Augusta, NJ (Eros)

2001

  • 3rd $100,000 Nevele Grand Prix, Ellenville, NY (Eros)

2000

  • 3rd $250,000 American Grand Prix Association Championship, Wellington, FL (Eros)

1999

  • 2nd $209,023 Grand Prix of Aachen, Aachen, Germany (Eros)
  • 1st $90,000 America Vs. Europe Bohemia Challenge, Monterrey, Mexico (Eros)
  • 1st $50,000 Cosequin Grand Prix, Ellenville, NY (Indeed)

1998

  • 1st $450,000 Pulsar Crown Grand Prix, Monterrey, Mexico—first American and first woman to win (Eros)
  • 1st Budweiser Upperville Jumper Classic, Upperville, VA (Indeed)

1997

  • Team 1st CSIO St. Gallen Nations’ Cup, St. Gallen, Switzerland (Eros)
  • Team 1st CSIO Rome Nations’ Cup, Rome, Italy (Eros)
  • 5th and highest-placed U.S. rider Volvo World Cup Final, Gothenburg, Sweden (Eros)
  • 4th Grand Prix of Rome, Rome, Italy (Eros)
  • USET Whitney Stone Cup award winner

1996

  • Atlanta Games (Silver medal) aboard Eros

1995

  • AHSA/Hertz Equestrian of the Year
  • 1st American Gold Cup, winner a record fourth time (Dynamite)

1994

  • 1st American Gold Cup, Devon, PA (Eros)

1993

  • 1st Cadillac American Gold Cup, Devon, PA (Top Seed)

1992

  • AHSA Horsewoman of the Year
  • Barcelona Games Team fourth place (Cannonball)

1991

  • USOC (US Olympic Committee) Female Equestrian Athlete of the Year
  • 1st CHIO Grand Prix of Aachen, Germany—second woman and third American to win (Starman)
  • 7th and leading female rider, Volvo World Cup Final, Gothenburg, Sweden (Starman)

1990

  • Team member, World Equestrian Games, Stockholm, Sweden Team 4th

1988

  • Seoul Games (Team Silver and fourth placed individual finish) aboard Starman

1991

  • Winner of three Nations Cup titles (Aachen, Germany; Hickstead, England; and Calgary, Canada) aboard Starman
  • AHSA Horsewoman of the Year

1984

  • Team Silver, 4th Individually, Alternate, U.S. Olympic Team

1983

  • Team/Individual Gold Medals, Pan American Games, Caracas, Venezuela (Livius)
  • 1st Grand Prix of Rome, Rome, Italy—first American to do so (Livius)
