Anne Shirley is a fictional character introduced in the novel Anne of Green Gables by Lucy Maud Montgomery. Montgomery wrote in her journal that the idea for Anne's story came from relatives who, planning to adopt an orphaned boy, received a girl instead.

Spoiler warning: Plot and/or ending details follow.

In the novel, Anne Shirley is an 11 year-old orphan who is sent to live with an shy, elderly man named Matthew Cuthbert and his sister Marilla at a house called Green Gables. Anne was orphaned as an infant when her parents, Walter and Bertha Shirley (née Willis), died of typhoid fever. Anne went to live with Mrs. Thomas, and later with the Hammond family. When Mr. Hammond died, Anne was sent to the orphanage at Hopetown, from where the Cuthberts adopt her. The setting is the fictional farming village of Avonlea, Prince Edward Island, in the 1870s. Anne, red-haired and freckled, has an overactive imagination and an impressive vocabulary as well as a temper, all of which influence her adventures. She does well in school, making a "bosom friend" in Diana Barry.

Matthew dies in Anne of Green Gables. In subsequent books in the series, Anne becomes a schoolteacher in Avonlea. She resumes her teaching career in Summerside, Prince Edward Island after graduating from Redmond College with a B.A. She works as a principal for three years while her fiance, Gilbert Blythe, finishes medical school. Rilla of Ingleside, which takes place against a backdrop of World War I, focuses on their youngest daughter.

Montgomery modeled the character's appearance after a photograph of actress Evelyn Nesbit she had cut out of a magazine.

The character features in the following books:

  • Anne of Green Gables (1908)
  • Anne of Avonlea (1909)
  • Anne of the Island (1915)
  • Anne's House of Dreams (1917)
  • Rainbow Valley (1919)
  • Rilla of Ingleside (1921)
  • Anne of Windy Poplars (1936; UK title: Anne of Windy Willows)
  • Anne of Ingleside (1939)
