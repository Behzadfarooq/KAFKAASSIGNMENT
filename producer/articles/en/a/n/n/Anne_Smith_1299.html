Anne Smith (born July 1, 1959 in Dallas, Texas, USA) is a former professional female tennis player from the United States.

[edit] Grand Slam Doubles

Event #
Women's Doubles 5
Mixed Doubles 5
Total 10
WTT Boston Lobsters coach Dr. Anne Smith with teammate Martina Navratilova

Australian Open

  • Doubles champion: 1981 (with Jordan)

French Open

  • Doubles champion: 1980 (with Jordan), 1982 (with Navratilova)
  • Doubles finalist: 1983 (with Jordan)
  • Mixed Doubles champion: 1980 (with Martin), 1984 (with Stockton)

Wimbledon French Open

  • Doubles champion: 1980 (with Jordan)
  • Mixed Doubles champion: 1982 (with Curren)

US Open French Open

  • Doubles champion: 1981 (with Jordan)
  • Mixed Doubles champion: 1981 (with Curren), 1982 (with Curren)

Highest World Rankings

  • Ranked #1 in Doubles in 1980 & 1981
  • Ranked #12 in Singles in 1982

Author

  • The MACH 4® Mental Training System: A Handbook for Athletes, Coaches and Parents
  • Grand Slam: Coach Your Mind to Win in Sports, Business & Life (foreward by Billie Jean King)

Education

  • Doctor of Philosophy: Educational Psychology, The University of Texas at Austin
  • Bachelor of Arts: Psychology, Trinity University, San Antonio, Texas

Professional Licenses

  • Licensed Psychologist, Texas, Massachusetts & Arizona
  • Licensed Specialist in School Psychology, Texas
  • Certified School Psychologist, Arizona

Awards & Achievements

  • Winner Mary Lowdon Award (Texas) -1974-1977
  • Winner Maureen Connolly Brinker Sportsmanship Award (Memphis, TN) -1976
  • Received “Most Promising Player award by “Seventeen Magazine” – 1976
  • Winner Orange Bowl Junior International Tournament – 1977
  • Winner Seventeen Magazine Tournament of Champions – 1977
  • Winner Maureen Connolly Brinker Outstanding Junior Girl Award (Philadelphia) -1977
  • WTA Doubles Team of the Year in 1980 (With Kathy Jordan)
  • Member of Wightman Cup and Federation Cup Teams
  • 35-and-Over United States Open Doubles Champion in 1997
  • 35-and-Over Wimbledon Doubles Champion in 1996 & 1997
  • First American to win the French Open Junior Singles Title
  • Enshrined into the Texas Sports Hall of Fame in 1993
  • Coach of the WTT Boston Lobsters – 2005, 2006, 2007
  • Mental Training Coach for Harvard University Women’s Tennis Team – 2005-2006
  • First American to win the French Open Junior Singles Title in 1977
  • Youngest athlete enshrined into the Texas Sports Hall of Fame in 1985
  • Inducted into the Trinity University Athletics Hall of Fame in 1999
