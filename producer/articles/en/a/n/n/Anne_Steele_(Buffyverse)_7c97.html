Anne Steele (born in 1981), formerly known as Joan, "Sister Sunshine", "Chantarelle" and "Lily", is a fictional character created by Joss Whedon for the cult television shows Buffy the Vampire Slayer and Angel. The character is portrayed by Julia Lee.

[edit] Biography

[edit] Character history

Anne (originally Joan) was born in 1983. Her birth name was revealed in the script of Buffy Season Two's "Lie To Me". She was only a minor character in both Buffy and Angel, but her character underwent substantial development over the course of the two series. Originally a homeless girl from an implied abusive household, Joan spent her adolescence joining whatever cult offered her a place and protection, changing her identity and persona as she moved through various groups. At one point, she followed a "loser preacher" and called herself "Sister Sunshine". She is first introduced in Buffy ("Lie To Me") as "Chantarelle", a member of the Sunset Club, a naïve cult who worships vampires ("The Lonely Ones"), considering them misunderstood creatures burdened with isolated immortality, higher beings who brought mortals into a more enlightened state of mind. She discovers the true nature of vampires when the club is raided by Spike's bloodthirsty gang; Buffy Summers saves her life.

Chantarelle subsequently moves to L.A. and falls in love with a homeless boy named Rickie T. Neither of them have money or employment, and they become homeless street kids. Rickie chooses "Lily", a name from a song, for her new identity. Lily encounters Buffy again in "Anne". Buffy is working as a waitress at a diner under her middle name, "Anne", after being forced to kill her boyfriend Angel. Buffy, wishing to remain anonymous and isolated, rebuffs Lily's attempts at friendship. Later, Lily, desperate to find her missing boyfriend, begs Buffy to help her with her search. Their search leads them into a hell dimension where they briefly become imprisoned as slaves in a demonic sweat shop. After they liberate the prisoners and kill the demons responsible, Buffy decides to return to Sunnydale. She leaves her job, her apartment, and her identity as "Anne" to Lily.

Over the next two and a half years, Lily, now "Anne Steele", settles down in Los Angeles and becomes an administrator at a shelter for homeless teenagers called The East Hills Teen Center. Here, she meets Charles Gunn and members of his vampire hunting gang, who are living in poor conditions at the time. She moves out of Buffy's rundown apartment and resides at 5632 Willoughby Avenue.

In Angel Season Two's "Blood Money", when the corrupt company Wolfram & Hart presents itself as a benefactor to the shelter, Angel convinces Anne to expose their plans to pocket a majority of the money raised on behalf of the shelter. Anne originally doesn't know what to do; though she knows Wolfram and Hart would steal most of the money, the idea of getting any for the shelter is tempting before she agrees to help Angel. However, Angel is merely trying to mess with W&H's Lindsey McDonald and Lilah Morgan, while an old honorable adversary stole the funds, which Angel offers to Anne. She accepts, despite the fact that the money is covered in Boone's blood, showing her dedication to the shelter no matter what the means.

She helps Gunn, Wesley and Cordelia find sanctuary in her shelter from undead police officers in Angel Season Two's "The Thin Dead Line", but is not seen again until the final episode of Angel; Season Five's "Not Fade Away". During what Gunn assumes will be his last day alive, he chooses to assist Anne at the shelter, which is apparently quite successful. Gunn asks Anne what she would do if she knew it was all pointless; she asserts that she would still do whatever needed to be done to help make the world a better place.

[edit] Powers & Abilities

Anne is a regular human being and has no supernatural powers.
