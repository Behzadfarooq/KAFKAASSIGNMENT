This article is about the news presenter. For the fictional character, see Anna Jones (Indiana Jones character).

Anna Jones is a news presenter for Sky News, who currently anchors Sky News Today, the channel's live news show from 9am - 12 midday alongside co-presenter Martin Stanford. Anna moved to Sky News in 2005 after 12 years at the BBC.

After four years as the editor of a business magazine in China, Anna joined the BBC in 1992. She spent three years as a senior producer in the BBC's business department, both producing and presenting the Business Breakfast programme from 6am - 7am. Anna then moved to BBC World as an anchor. In 1997 she joined BBC News 24 at launch as business presenter, before becoming a news presenter in 1999. Most recently she anchored BBC News 24's 9-1pm strand. She was on air as Allied forces began the bombing of Afghanistan, at the announcement of the death of the Queen Mother, and the death of Princess Margaret whilst also anchoring coverage of the aftermath of the 2004 Tsunami. Her last BBC broadcast was the announcement of London's victorious bid for the 2012 Olympics.
