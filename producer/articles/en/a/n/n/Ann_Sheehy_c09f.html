Ann Sheehy (born c. 1969) is an American researcher into immunology. She is credited with discovering the APOBEC3G protein used to fight HIV jointly with M. H. Malim.[1] [2]

She received the B.S. degree at Kalamazoo College in 1991, and a Ph.D. from the Johns Hopkins University School of Medicine in 1998. She was a Postdoctoral Fellow at the University of Pennsylvania and a Senior Research Associate, at Kings College, London. She is now Assistant Professor of Biology at College of the Holy Cross, Pennsylvania.
