Anne Randall (born Barbara Burrus 23 September 1944 in Alameda, California) is an American model of Irish, Yugoslavian and Cherokee ancestry. She was Playboy magazine's Playmate of the Month for its May 1967 issue. Her centerfold was photographed by Mario Casilli.

In 1959, Barbara became a regular on the KPIX Dance Party, an afternoon television show featuring teenagers dancing to popular music (http://www.kpixdanceparty.org), broadcast on KPIX-TV Channel 5 (CBS) in San Francisco. It was hosted by Dick Stewart and was a very popular show, airing from 1959 to 1963.

At the time of her photo session for Playboy, Barbara was studying drama at San Francisco City College; her ambition was to become an actress. Later in 1967, she married Dick Stewart.

It was at this point that Barbara Burrus took her agent's advice and changed her name to "Anne Randall". During the late 1960s and throughout the 1970s, Anne pursued a film and television career, appearing in such shows as Cannon, Barnaby Jones, and The Rockford Files. She also spent two years as as one of the beauties on Hee Haw. Sometimes she was credited as Anne Randall Stewart.

Anne now lives in Sun City, Arizona with her husband Richard, where she works as a community activist.
