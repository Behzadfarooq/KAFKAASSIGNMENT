Anni Blomqvist

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Anni Blomqvist (born Karlsson) (1909 - June 26, 1990), was an author from the Åland Islands, Finland, primarily famous for her Stormskärs-Maja and Anna Beata series of novels. In her books, she drew from her real-life experiences of the harsh and tedious life as a widow in the Åland archipelago.

[edit] Biography

Anni Blomqvist was born on the island Simskäla in Vårdö, Åland, as the first of ten children of fisherman Viktor Karlsson and his wife Anna. In a large family with few resources, Anni had to start helping to support the family at an early age. Also living with the family was Anni's maternal grandmother, who would serve as a role model for Anni and as basis for the protagonist in the later Anna Beata trilogy of books.

At the age of 26, Anni Blomqvist married seaman and long time friend Valter Blomqvist in 1936. Together they had four children, eldest son Tommy (born in 1939), twin daughters who died only hours after birth in 1940 and youngest son Bengt (born in 1945). The family would live on fishing, shipping and small scale farming.

Blomqvist was an active member of her community, frequently writing letters to the editor of the local newspaper Ålandstidningen. Also being the chairwoman of the local Martha organization, she arranged courses and organized vaccinations. She also worked tirelessly during the 1950s and 60s to get electricity to Simskäla. Her first short story was published in 1949, when she won first prize in a writing competition.

Tragedy struck in 1961, when Valter and Tommy disappeared during a fishing trip. Although their bodies were never recovered, their boat had presumably been overtaken by a raging storm and they had subsequently drowned. This event was one of the main catalysts for Anni's subsequent authorship. She began maintaining a diary of sorts, writing down her memories and feelings about her grief.

At the age of 57, Anni's debut novel, I stormens spår was published in 1966. The novel was well received and encouraged Blomqvist, whose health was in decline and rendering her unable to tend to her farm, to begin writing full time.

Two years later, the first novel in the Stormskärs-Maja series, Vägen till Stormskäret, was published. The series of five books, which is Anni Blomqvist's most famous works, depicts the life of Maja, the wife of a fisherman living in the fictional Stormskär, who's struggling with the hard life of a fisherman and being a woman in a patriarchical society. The stories are partially based on stories Anni had heard about her maternal grandmother's sister, Maria Mickelsdotter, who had died prior to Anni's birth. After the last novel in the series, Vägen från Stormskäret, was published in 1973, she began collaborating with professor Stig Jäätinen of Helsinki University on a study of the people and community on Simskäla island. This study was published as Simskäla in 1977.

Anna Beata, the first novel in the Anna Beata trilogy of books, was published in 1979. The stories were partially based on the life of her maternal grandmother.

Tragedy would strike again in 1987, when Anni's only remaining child Bengt drowned. She wrote about this tragedy in the autobiographical Havet finns inte mer, published in 1989. It would turn out to be her last book, as Blomqvist died in her home in Vårdö in 1990, at the age of 81.

Anni Blomqvist's home now serves as a museum, and a fund started in her name annually awards scholarships to authors and researchers.

[edit] Other information and trivia

  • Anni would live all her life on the island of Simskäla, and due to her poor health in later years she would rarely venture outside of Simskäla. Two Finnish presidents, Urho Kekkonen and Mauno Koivisto, have visited her in her home in Simskäla.
  • The Stormskärs-Maja series was turned into a popular television series in 1975
  • Another famous Åland writer, Sally Salminen, was also born in Vårdö
