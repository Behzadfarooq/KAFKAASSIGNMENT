Anna Manahan

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Anna Maria Manahan, married name Anna Maria O'Kelly (b. October 18, 1924, County Waterford, Irish Free State) is an Irish stage, film and television actress. She is considered an incomparable interpreter of the works of Sean O'Casey, John B Keane, J. M. Synge, Oscar Wilde, James Joyce, Martin McDonagh, Christy Brown, Brian Friel and other Irish writers.

Her career began when she was recruited as a young woman by the legendary irish theatre directors Micheal Mac Liammoir and Hilton Edwards. She later married their Stage Director Colm O'Kelly who tragically died not long afterwards of polio, which he contracted after swimming in the Nile, during on a theatre tour of Egypt. He was the love of her life. She never remarried.

In 1957, she played Serafina in the first irish production of Tennessee William's "The Rose Tattoo" and acheived unexpected notoriety when she and several other members of the cast were arrested for alleged possession of a condom on stage.

Anna Manahan played a minor role in the irish cult soap "The Riordan's" (1960s), and the greatly beloved lead role as Mrs. Kenefick in the TV comedy Me Mammy (1970's). She also played the lead starred in the irish comedy series "Leave it to Mrs O'Brien" (1980s) and Mrs. Cadogan "The Irish R.M." (1980s). In recent times she plays Ursula in the irish daily soap "Fair City".

She has an extensive theatre portfolio having played at theatres throughout Ireland, the U.K., continental Europe, the USA and Australia.

She won the Tony Award for Best Supporting Actress in a Drama for her role as the exasperating, doomed mother, "Mag", in Martin McDonagh's The Beauty Queen of Leenane. She previously received a Tony nomination in 1969, in the equivalent category of Best Supporting or Featured Actress (Dramatic) for Brian Friel's "Lovers."

The renowned irish Playwright John B. Keane wrote the play "Big Maggie" for her. In 2001 she starred in John B. Keane's The Matchmaker with veteran Irish actor Des Keogh. In 2005 she starred in Sisters, a new Irish play by Declan Hassett that was reportedly written especially for her. The production toured Ireland and was staged at the International Festival of World Theatre in Colorado. In 2006 the production played at 59e59 in New York City.

She has appeared in movies with Laurence Olivier, Peter Cushing, Kenneth More, Maggie Smith, Christopher Warren, Albert Finney, Ursula Andress, Brenda Fricker to name but a few.

She was granted the freedom of the city of Waterford in 2002 in recognition of her life's acheivement in the arts. She thus became the 28th Freeman of Waterford since Isaac Butt in 1877.

Anna Manahan is regarded as a living legend of irish theatre, but is equally admired for her selflessness and loyalty to friends, family and fans alike.
