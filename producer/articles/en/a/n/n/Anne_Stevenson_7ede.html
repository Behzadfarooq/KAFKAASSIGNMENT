Anne Stevenson (born 1933) is an American-British poet and writer.

Born of American parents in Cambridge, England, Stevenson was reared in the United States and was educated in Ann Arbor, University of Michigan, where her father, C.L. Stevenson, was professor of philosophy. After obtaining her bachelor's and master's degrees and graduating with honours, she returned to Great Britain where she has lived most of her life. She has been married four times. Stevenson is the inaugural winner of the Northern Rock Foundation Writer's Award in 2002. She is the author of over a dozen volumes of poetry, of some books of essays and literary criticism, of a controversial biography of the American poet Sylvia Plath Bitter Fame: A Life of Sylvia Plath (1989), and of two critical studies of Elizabeth Bishop.
