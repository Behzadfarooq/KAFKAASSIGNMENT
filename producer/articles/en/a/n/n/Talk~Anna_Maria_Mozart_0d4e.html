This article fails to explain why she is notable. --Joy [shallot] 12:02, 23 Jun 2005 (UTC)

Um ... did the article at that time not state whose mother she was?
Seriously, I realize that for most notable people, their parents are not automatically of interest, but Mozart's got to be an exception. After all, lots of minor royalty that virtually no one ever heard of outside their own family get accepted as sufficiently notable in Wikipedia simply because they are descendants of someone famous, often various generations removed. I think that's got to go double for someone who directly produced a figure that legendary; I don't see anyone questioning the inclusion of Elvis Presley's mama.
Moreover, one of the functions of Wikipedia — wherever you rank this function in importance — is to serve as a resource to students and similar types. Over time, this article can be developed with whatever additional information is out there about Anna Maria Mozart, and people who are studying up on Mozart in any depth are inevitably going to be interested in the supporting figures in his life.
