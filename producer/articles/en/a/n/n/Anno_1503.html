Anno 1503 (known in the Americas as 1503 AD) is a real-time strategy game developed by Germany-based Sunflowers Interactive Entertainment Software company. It revolves around building and maintaining a 16th century colony in the New World. It is the sequel to the similar game Anno 1602

[edit] Game

You start most games with a ship filled with men and material searching for an island to settle. When you find a promising island you claim it and start building your city. In the beginning your simple people will not require more than some food and cloth but as the game progresess your people grow more and more advanced and want more goods. As your people become Aristocrats you need to supply them with at least ten different goods and numerous services, such as access to a large church or a bathhouse. The game is constructed around one human player and three computers. Like its prequel Anno 1602, the AI computers are progressive, meaning that they will advance along with the human players. The computer players also have "different personalities", which can be customized before the beginning of a game. Each different player, human or computer, are represented by a color.

The game is amazingly detailed and realistic as to modeling real life. The complex processes needed to support a population requires intense development and planning skills; therefore the game is extremely popular with people fond of "micromanagement."

[edit] Economy

The economy in Anno 1503 is built on selling different goods to your colonists. In the beginning you can only sell cheap goods to them, but once they receive these they will progress to the next civilisation level where they will buy more expensive goods. Since the game is built on different level of civilizations (pioneer, settler, citizen, merchant, aristocrat), each level of civilization requires more and more goods and thus more and more balancing skills and finesse. The aristocrat level is actually not the best level. It is sort of a bonus that advanced players can upgrade to. Besides being able to build a custom designed palace when you are an aristocrat, having the entire population at merchant level is more strategically sound.

[edit] Technology

When you start the game your colonists are at the Pioneers level. Once you supply them with some basic goods like food, cloth and alcohol they will develop into Settlers, the second civilization level. They will allow you to build more advanced buildings but they also require more goods to stay alive. Some of the building do not have a specific function, although they are said to have some in the game. For example, the public courts are supposed to reduce crime rates, but they are in truth not very effective and sometimes there is no use for them at all. The only time that the effect is really felt of buildings like these are when your city gets to ridiculously big size, such as 50,000. Although it may not seem possible to get to such city sizes, audio was recorded that says things like "Well done, your city is now 50,000", it is speculated however that these were just put in 'just in case' but realistically it was considered impossible to reach that size. The game also says that ships can have the function of protecting other ships; thus technically, you could group ships together and they would protect each other and travel at the same speed. Unfortunately, this function was removed before the initial release of the game.

The military form of this game is also a lot more complex and thoughtful than that of Anno 1602. The game has more than seven different types of units while Anno 1602 only had four total. Thus, during battles, the game experience becomes more and more intense as deploying different units becomes key in strategy for winning a battle.

The buildings in this game are also a lot more extensive than those of its prequel. This is mainly due to the fact that the game play area and islands are many times bigger than that of 1602. Thus, it makes it possible to have many more plantations and diversity of farm houses. With the original houses and farms featured in the prequel, this game also has many more extensive farms for gems, silk, whale blubber, lamp oil, medicinal herbs, hops, wine, indigo, coal, hides, leather and salt. Thus the entire game play is made more challenging as high levels demand more fine goods. The buildings are also more detailed in graphics along with the houses. Overall, there is a tremendous improvement in the housing and framing details features in this game.
