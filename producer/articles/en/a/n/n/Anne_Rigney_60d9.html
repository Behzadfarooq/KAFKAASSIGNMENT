Anne Rigney

Anne Rigney (born 1957) is an Irish Artist and Sculptor. Her work is mainly abstract, using oils and mixed media.

She is a graduate of Athlone Institute of Technology, where she studied art and design, and is a co-founder of the professional art group Working Artists Roscommon (W.A.R.). She is also a member of The Association of Artists in Ireland.

Her work is influenced by her surroundings of the rural Irish midlands where she lives, and her life.

She has had many solo and group exhibitions, both in Ireland and internationally. Her exhibitions to date include The Kenny Gallery in Galway, The Dyehouse Gallery in Waterford, The Wexford Arts Centre, The Mullingar Arts Centre, and the American Embassy in Dublin. She has also exhibited in Scotland, Poland and Germany, and her many of her works are held in public collections, such as at Athlone Institute of Technology, Roscommon County Council, and the Office of Public Works, and Irish Distillers. Her exhibitions have been opened by Senator Mary O'Rourke, John Waters, and Kieran Meagher, head of Art & Design at Limerick Institute of Technology.

Anne Rigney has also been received many Arts Council awards, including a residency at The Tyrone Guthrie Centre in Annamakerrig, Artist in Schools, and Artist in the Community. She has also received the travel award for Cultural Events in Krakow.

Reviewer Ian Wieczorek wrote about her in The Irish Times, "in the abstract Anne Rigney produces striking and provoking works".

She works from her studio at her home in Knockcroghery, County Roscommon.
