Anna Pepple House

It had been founded by the legendary King who ruled Bonny from 1792 to 1830 and who established the pre-eminence of the city-state in the Eastern Delta. Named Hanno by European traders and corrupted by Bonny people to Anna or Annie (hence Opubo’s houses became known as Annie Pepple). Opubo was the third son of Perekule (also called Pelekule), the first King Pepple of Bonny. Opubo succeeded his deceased brother Fubara (2nd King Pepple) to become the 3rd King Pepple in 1792. The first King Pepple was the Wealthiest and most powerful man in Bonny and had set up his 3 sons with their own Canoe-houses as befitting Kings.:

1st son Ibulu (Best) Pepple Canoe-house, Passed on succession to the thrown. 2nd son Fubara (Manilla ) Pepple Canoe-house, succeeded Perekule as King of Bonny. 3rd son Opubo (Annie) Pepple Canoe-house, succeeded Fubara as King of Bonny.

Jack Jaja Annie Pepple became the head of the Royal house of Opubo (Annie) Pepple. After the bonny civil war in Bonny, Jaja immerged as the supreme leader and paramount chief of all the chiefs that left Bonny with him to find a new place to settle, this settlement was then named Opubo in memory of the Great Bonny ruler of modern times and founder of the Annie Pepple houses. The name of the settlement Opubo was later to be corrupted by Europeans to Opobo which became popular and is still used today. Jaja was crowned King Jaja (1st) of Opobo, thus creating the Jaja Dynasty of Opobo.

King Jaja (1st) too had 3 sons: 1st son Saturday Jaja, Passed on succession to the thrown. 2nd son Sunday Jaja, succeeded King Jaja (1st) as King Jaja (2nd) of Opobo. 3rd son Arthur MacPepple-Jaja, succeeded King Jaja (2nd) as King Jaja (3rd) of Opobo. Douglas, MacPepple-Jaja, succeeded King Jaja (3rd) as King Jaja (4th) of Opobo. Dandyson, Douglas, Jaja succeeded King Jaja (4rd) as King Jaja (5th) of Opobo. His Royal Majesty King Jaja (5th) is the reigning Monarch of the Opobo (Oil Rivers) Kingdom today.
