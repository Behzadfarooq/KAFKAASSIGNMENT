Anne McEwen (DBE)

Dame Anne Mills McEwen, née McLeod, DBE, best known as Annie McEwen, was the wife of former Australian Deputy Prime Minister, John McEwen.

She was active in the Country Party and devoted her life to working for the public good. She was appointed as a Dame Commander of the Order of the British Empire on 1 January 1966 for public services

Born in 1900 in Tongala, Victoria, Australia, she was educated at Girton Church of England Girls' Grammar School in Bendigo, Victoria. She married John McEwen on 21 September 1921 at Ballavoca, Tongala. They had no children.

She was an experienced farmer and with her husband, developed the soldier settler property. They sold it and bought others to eventually hold 3000 acres in the Stanhope, Victoria area.

A skilled organiser, she raised funds for local causes and was an active member of the Country Women’s Association in Victoria. She was appointed a Life Governor of Melbourne’s Prince Henry Hospital for her many years of voluntary work there. She was also an active figure in the Country Party and remained so in the period of McEwen’s expulsion from the party from 1938 to 1943. She spoke at women's meetings and was a key organiser in the Country Party during the early stages of her husband's political career.

She drove thousands of miles through Victoria to political meetings while her husband worked on his speeches in the back seat of his car. He was elected to the Federal Parliament in 1934 as member for Echuca. He later held the seats of Indi from 1937-1949 and Murray from 1949-1971, when he retired from politics. He became leader of the Country Party in 1958.

When John McEwen was Minister for Air, Anne McEwen arranged for the care of young Royal Australian Air Force recruits from other states who were training at the Flying School at Point Cook. She continued this work during the war and took over an old Toorak mansion as a recreation centre. Anne McEwen was also a founding member of the White Wings Auxiliary, a group formed to support the Women’s Auxiliary Australian Air Force (WAAAF), established in 1941 when her husband was Minister for Air.

[edit] DBE

On 1 January 1966 Anne McEwen was made a DBE in recognition of her long and active service in public life and for country people. An invalid for many years, she received the award from Governor-General Lord Casey at a private investiture at her home in Toorak.

[edit] Death

Anne McEwen died on 10 February 1967 at Toorak, Victoria, Australia. She did not live to see her husband assume the Prime Ministership of Australia for three (3) weeks after the disappearance of Harold Holt in December 1967.
