Ann Macbeth (1875â€“1948), born in Little Bolton, Salford, Lancashire, England, was a famous embroideress and author, a part of the Glasgow Movement, and an associate of Charles Rennie Mackintosh.

Macbeth studied at the Glasgow School of Art, and became head of its embroidery department. She published five books on embroidery, including Needleweaving, published in 1922.

From 1921 to 1948 she lived in Patterdale, Cumbria. St. Patrick's Church in Patterdale houses some of her embroideries.
