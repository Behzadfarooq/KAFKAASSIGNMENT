[edit] Biography

Anna Steiger is a British Opera Singer (born February 13, 1965) in Los Angeles, USA. She is the daughter of Claire Bloom, a British film and stage actress and Rod Steiger (April 14, 1925 – July 9, 2002) who was an American Academy Award-winning actor best known for his intense performances in such films as In the Heat of the Night, On the Waterfront and Doctor Zhivago.

[edit] Career

Anna Steiger performs regularly in opera, concert, and recital throughout Europe and the United States.

Since making her highly successful debut at Glyndebourne in the title role of Monteverdi’s "Incoronazione di Poppea" she has returned to sing in many productions there in roles such as Concepcion in "L’Heure Espagnole". She then began to sing at other Opera houses in the UK, at the Royal Opera House in Covent Garden, in "Parsifal", "Jenufa" and The Knot Garden, and at the English National Opera in Makropoulos Case.

Since then, she has sung roles as: Despina in Amsterdam, Stuttgart and Zurich, Zerlina in Seattle, Stuttgart and Nancy, Governess in "The Turn of the Screw" at La Fenice, Venice, Jenny in Mahagonny in Los Angeles, Composer in Ariadne auf Naxos in St Louis, Concepcion at the NY City Opera as well as in Geneva, Serpetta in La Finta Giardiniera in Frankfurt and Lausanne, Damigella in the Incoronazione di Poppea at the Salzburg Festival.

In 1994/1995 she decided to follow her natural vocal leaning towards the mezzo-soprano repertoire with some success, singing:

  • Maddalena in Rigoletto at the Opera Monte Carlo
  • Magdalene in Meistersingers in Stuttgart
  • Marcellina in Figaro at Monte Carlo, Amsterdam,and Teatro Real of Madrid
  • Giunone in Agrippina in Palermo
  • Tisbe in Cenerentola at Bastille and Geneva
  • Pierrot Lunaire for the Opera de Montpellier
  • Baronne in Chapeau de Paille by Nino Rota in Toulouse
  • Ragonde in Comte Ory in Toulouse
  • Auntie in Peter Grimes in Nancy
  • Dorothee in Massenet’s Cendrillon in Geneva
  • Marquise in Fille du Regiment in St Gallen
  • Kunstgewerblerin in Lulu at Bastille
  • Flora in Traviata in Amsterdam
  • Meg Page in Falstaff in Amsterdam
  • Melanto in Ritorno d’Ulisse at BAM in NY with Netherlands Opera
  • La Libellule, La Tasse Chinoise and La Mere in L’Enfant et les Sortileges at la Fenice, Venice.

Future plans include:

  • Baronne in Chapeau de Paille in Toulouse
  • Auntie in Peter Grimes in Toulouse
  • Tisbe at Bastille
  • Lulu at Bastille
  • Streetcar Named Desire in St Gallen
  • Faust in Avignon
  • Romeo and Juliette in Orange
  • Tisbe in Marseille

She has sung many times with Nicolaus Harnoncourt, and recorded with him the role of Despina for Teldec with the Concertgebouw Orchestra. She has also performed with important conductors such as Sir Neville Marriner, Simon Rattle, Riccardo Chailly, Bruno Campanella, Alberto Zedda, Bernard Haitink, Kent Nagano, Michael Tilson-Thomas.

She has made a videodisc of Varese’s Offrandes with Pierre Boulez and the IRCAM of Paris.
