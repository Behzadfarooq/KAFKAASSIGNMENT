Anna Coren (born. 9th April 1975)[citation needed] is an Australian journalist for the Seven Network and host of the East Coast edition of Today Tonight.

[edit] Career

She graduated from Charles Sturt University's Bathurst campus in 1996 with a Communications degree, and has spent time working for regional television networks Prime (12 months) and NBN (18 months). Anna was picked up by the Nine Network in 1999, and earned a spot as an on-camera reporter for National Nine News in early 2000. In 2002 she began presenting news updates and the Early Morning News.

Anna's career at Seven began in December 2003, presenting late news updates and hosting the summer edition of Today Tonight in the place of Naomi Robson. She has since hosted many one-off events such as Australia's Brainiest Kid, the 2005 Edinburgh Military Tattoo, Royal Wedding Night with Seven and Zero Hour - The Bali Bombings. Her role at Seven also involves filling in as newsreader with the morning news program Sunrise. Anna Coren is currently the host of the Seven Network's high rating new show True Stories.

In 2006, Anna was appointed US Correspondent for Seven News replacing Mike Amor who returned to Australia to read the 4.30 News. Anna also appears on the Global Notebook segment for Sunrise in addition to her continuing role as host of True Stories.

Anna Coren was reported on January 27, 2007 to be the new host of Today Tonight[1], replacing Naomi Robson who left the show on December 1, 2006. Nick Etchells is taking over her position as Seven's US Correspondent.

[edit] Controversies

There was a rumor in late 2006 and early 2007 that Anna was in a relationship with TV cameraman Ben Hanson. She has since said "I'm single. I'm happy for you to print that". Anna said the reports on the matter were "blatant misinformation", and she accused people at Channel 9 for starting the rumor.

On the 5 February 2007, News Limited newspapers published a story about Coren titled "Anna Coren 'does a Britney'". The article said Coren had "accidentally delivered a Britney Spears-style undies flash" at Rosehill Racecourse on the previous Saturday.[1]

Coren's complicated lovelife once again hit the headlines on 13 February 2007, with revelations she was in a relationship with three men at the same time.[2]

Coren's current boyfriend is Andrew Bourke, a Today Tonight producer, even though it has said in the news she has been linked to other men
