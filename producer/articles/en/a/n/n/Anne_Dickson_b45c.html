Anne Dickson

Anne Dickson, born Anne Letitia Dickson in London in 1928, is a former Northern Ireland Unionist politician.

Moving to Northern Ireland at an early age she was educated at Holywood and Richmond Lodge School, Belfast. After involvement as the Chair of the Northern Ireland Advisory Board of the Salvation Armys she become actively involved in Politics for the Ulster Unionist Party. Later elected as chair of Carrick division Unionist Association she was then elected as a member of Newtownabbey Urban District Council becoming Vice-Chair of the Council from 1967 to 1969.

She was then elected as an Ulster Unionist politician for the Carrick constituency in the Northern Ireland Parliament at Stormont as a supporter of the Prime Minister Terence O'Neill. After the dissolution of the Stormont Parliament, she was elected in the 1973 Assembly election for South Antrim as an Independent Unionist candidate having resigned from the UUP in 1972. After the Ulster Unionist party split in 1973/4 over the Sunningdale agreement she joined the newly formed Unionist Party of Northern Ireland (UPNI) along with other supporters of the former Northern Ireland prime minister Brian Faulkner. She retained her seat in South Antrim in the 1975 constitutional convention election. After the retirement of Brian Faulkner she became leader of the Unionist Party of Northern Ireland (UPNI) in 1976, becoming the first woman to lead a major political party in Northern Ireland. In 1979 she contested the Belfast North constituency in the Westminster election, polling 10% of the vote, the best performance by a UPNI candidate in Northern Ireland, however her intervention was sufficient to split the moderate Unionist vote resulting in the seat being gained by the DUP. The UPNI disbanded in 1981 after poor results in the local government elections that year and Dickson retired from active politics. Subsequently she was chair of the Northern Ireland Consumer Council from 1985 to 1990.
