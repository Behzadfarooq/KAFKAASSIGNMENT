Anna Flyover

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Coordinates: 13.05238° N 80.25104° E

Anna Flyover (also known as Gemini Flyover), lies in the heart of Chennai. Opened in 1973, it was the first flyover in India.[1][2] It's main purpose is to allow traffic movement on Mount Road — now renamed Anna Salai, near Nungambakkam High road — to continue unhindered by the cross traffic

It was built on the land originally housing The Gemini Studios (a contemporary of A.V.M) along with property from the Woodlands Drive-In Restaurant.{fact} On two sides of the circle roundabout below the center of the flyover are two identical statues of a man controlling a horse placed there to commemorate the banning of horse racing.

It is an extremely well-known landmark in Chennai as almost anyone in the city will know where it is.
