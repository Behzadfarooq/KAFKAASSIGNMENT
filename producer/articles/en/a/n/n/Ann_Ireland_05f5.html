Ann Ireland (born 1953) is a Canadian fiction author.

Born in Toronto, Ontario, she studied at the University of British Columbia, from which she earned a BFA in creative writing in 1976.

She is presently based in Toronto and has recently taught fiction courses for Ryerson University's continuing education programme.

Her 1985 novel A Certain Mr. Takahashi was the basis for the 1991 feature film The Pianist.

[edit] Awards and recognition

  • 1985: finalist, Books in Canada First Novel Award, A Certain Mr. Takahashi
  • 2002: fiction finalist, Governor General's Awards, Exile
