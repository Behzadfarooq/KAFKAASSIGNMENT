Annie Haslam (born in 1944, in Bolton, Lancashire, is an English progressive rock vocalist and songwriter. Originally a fashion student, she began studying under opera singer Sybil Knight in 1970 and developed her extraordinary five-octave vocal range. Next year in 1971, she became the lead singer of Renaissance after answering an ad in a magazine.

In 1977, she began her solo career with her album Annie in Wonderland, produced by Roy Wood who played most of the instruments, and who also duetted with her on one track. She has since released eight studio albums, three of which were released through her own record label, White Dove. She has also collaborated with Steve Howe on a number of projects.

Her latest release, Live Studio Concert, has also been released as her first solo DVD. Annie released an EP called Night and Day, her first solo recording for some years, with Welsh rock band Magenta in 2006. The song was specially written for her by the band.

Originally from Bolton, Greater Manchester, England, Annie currently resides in northern Bucks County, Pennsylvania.
