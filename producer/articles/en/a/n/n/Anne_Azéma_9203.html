Anne Azéma, French born soprano who has been an important or leading singer of early music since 1993. She has co-directed programs for the Boston Camerata and is also noted as a music scholar.[1] She is perhaps best known for performing music from the Middle Ages, lute songs from the Renaissance period, Baroque sacred music, and contemporary music theater. She is also an educator of music and a researcher. She has performed in Japan, Germany, the US, and elsewhere.[2]

Anne Azéma's special field is secular/vernacular song of France and Provence in the middle ages. Four solo recital albums, "The Unicorn", "The Game of Love", "Provence Mystique" and "Etoile du Nord" document her original work in this area.
