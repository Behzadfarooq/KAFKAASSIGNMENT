Anne Marie of Orléans

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Anne Marie of Orléans (Saint Cloud, August 27, 1669 - Turin, August 26, 1728), was a Queen of Savoy and Sardinia and maternal grandmother of Louis XV of France.

She was born in the Château de Saint-Cloud as daughter of Philippe I, Duke of Orléans and Princess Henrietta of England. Her grandfathers were Kings Louis XIII of France and Charles I of England.

On April 10, 1684 she married in Versailles with Víctor Amadeus II, Duke of Savoy and future king of Sicily (1713) and Sardinia (1720).

They had six children:

1. Marie Adelaide (b.1685-d.1712), married Louis, duc de Bourgogne and was the mother of Louis XV of France
2. Marie Anne (b.1687-d.1690)
3. Marie Louise (b.1688-d.1714), first wife of Philip V of Spain
4. Victor Amadeus (b.1699-d.1715), prince of Piedmont
5. Carlo Emanuele III (b.1701-d.1773), the next Duke of Savoy and King of Sardinia.
6. Emanuele Philibert (1705) Duke of Chablais.

Anne Marie died in Turin on August 26 1728. Her husband Víctor Amadeus II abdicated in favor of his son in 1730 and died two years later in Moncalieri.

[edit] Jacobite succession

Through Anne Marie derives the post-Stuart legitimist claim of the Jacobites to the English and Scottish thrones.

In 1807, almost 80 years after her death , Cardinal Henry Benedict Stuart died. He was the last of the descendants of her uncle King James II of England. The Jacobites viewed the legitimate succession to the English and Scottish thrones as devolving upon the senior living descendant of King Charles I. In 1807 this was Charles Emmanuel IV of Sardinia , the great-grandson of Anne Marie of Orléans and Victor Amadeus II of Sardinia.
