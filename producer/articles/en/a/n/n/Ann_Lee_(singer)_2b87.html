Ann Lee (singer)

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Ann Lee (born Annerley Gordon, 12 November 1972, in Sheffield, England) is an English pop singer, who rose to fame towards the latter part of the 1990s.

[edit] Career

Having moved to live in Italy in the late 1980s, she was talent scouted, and was often used as the lead singer on a series of Europop dance music singles, issued under various aliases. These included Lolita, Charme, Anne, Ally & Jo, Annalise, Ann Sinclair, DJ Space'C, A Kay BJ, Alex, Wienna and TH Express. Her first solo single, under her own name, more or less, was "2 Times". It was a Top 10 hit in Austria, France, Germany, Italy, Netherlands, and Norway, as well as in Australia and New Zealand. It also hit Number 2 in Belgium, the UK, and reached Number one in Denmark. In the United States, "2 Times" can also be heard in the 2001 feature film, Head Over Heels as the movie's theme song.

Her second single, "Voices", fared considerably less well, although it was a Top 10 hit in the Czech Republic, Denmark, and Spain. Since which despite releasing other material, she has seemingly disappeared from the popular end of the music making scene.
