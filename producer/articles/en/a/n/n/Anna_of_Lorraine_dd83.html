Anna of Lorraine

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Anna of Lorraine (July 25, 1522 – May 15, 1568, Diest) was the daughter of Antoine, Duke of Lorraine and Renée de Bourbon-Montpensier.

She married René of Châlon, Prince of Orange on 22 August 1540 at Bar-le-Duc. They had a single daughter, Maria (b. 1544), who only lived 3 weeks and was buried in the Grote Kerk at Breda.

After René's death in 1544, she married Philip II, Duke of Arschot on July 9, 1548. They had one son:

  • Charles Philippe de Croy (September 1, 1549, Brussels – November 25, 1613, Burgundy), Prince of Croy, married in 1580 Diane de Dammartin (1550 – aft. 1635), Countess of Fontenoy
