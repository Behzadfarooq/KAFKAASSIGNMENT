Delegate Anne B. Crockett-Stark (R-Wytheville) is a member of the Virginia House of Delegates representing the 6th District, currently serving her 1st term. Crockett-Stark was born in Wytheville, VA on December 12th, 1942.

[edit] Biography

Crockett-Stark is a devout Lutheran. She received her education from Radford University and Virginia Tech. Before election to the Virginia House of Delegates in 2005, Crockett-Stark served six years on the Wythe Board of Supervisors, including one as chairwoman. She also was the first woman to serve on the Wytheville Town Council.

[edit] 2006 Legislative Session

During her 1st year in the General Assembly, Anne B. Crockett-Stark proposed five bills targeted specifically to address the problems of local governments. She also sponsored five resolutions commending various groups and individuals. Perhaps more interesting though, were her votes on several of the more controversial measures that came up during the 2006 session.
