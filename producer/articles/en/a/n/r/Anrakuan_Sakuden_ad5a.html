Anrakuan Sakuden (安楽庵策伝, anrakuan sakuden?, 1554 – 1642) was a Japanese priest, tea master and writer in the Edo period. He is famous for Sobering Laughter (醒睡笑, seisuishō?, 1623), a humorous eight-part composition that had a strong influence on half book (半紙本, hanshibon?) and is sometimes said to be the earliest rakugo (落語, rakugo?) composition.
