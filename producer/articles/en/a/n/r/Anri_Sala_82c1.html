Anri Sala

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Anri Sala is a contemporary Albanian artist born in Tirana in 1974. He studied art at the Albanian Academy of Arts from 1992 - 1996. He has also studied video at the Ecole Nationale des Arts Decoratifs, Paris and film directory in Le Fresnoy-Studio National des Arts Contemporains, Tourcoing. He lives and works in Paris, France.

His video installation called "Dammi i colori" ("Give me the colors") is on display at Tate Modern in London. The installation reflects on Tirana's transformation in 2003 by means of colors. The installation includes a conversation with Tirana's mayor, Mr. Edi Rama, a personal friend of the artist's and the force behind this transformation.
