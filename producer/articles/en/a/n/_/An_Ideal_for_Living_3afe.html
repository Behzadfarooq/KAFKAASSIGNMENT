An Ideal For Living is an EP released by Joy Division in 1978, shortly after changing their name from "Warsaw".

All tracks were recorded at the Pennine Sound Studios, Oldham, on December 14, 1977. A 7" version was released in June, followed by a 12" version in September. Both releases were on the band's own label, Enigma Records (not to be confused with the American record label of the same name).

The EP consisted of:

  1. "Warsaw" (2:26)
  2. "No Love Lost" (3:42)
  3. "Leaders of Men" (2:34)
  4. "Failures" (3:44)

The cover has a black-and-white picture of a blonde Hitler Youth member beating a drum, which was drawn by guitarist Bernard Sumner, and the words "Joy Division!" printed in a Gothic font. The cover design fuelled the controversy over the band's supposed Nazi sympathies. The release reflects the bands' early punk tendencies, as opposed to the bleak post-punk the band would later develop.
