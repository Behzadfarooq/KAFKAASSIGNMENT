[edit] Merge proposal

Just added merge templates to An Slua Muiri and NSR. The latter is the more complete textually, but it seems more like an advert or press release. The former is more wikified and has the templates ready to go. Also NSR is a horrible article name. But the NSR article does seem more current. I am unaware which is more correct. Any suggestions? Frelke 19:14, 2 May 2006 (UTC)

Merge Agree with proposal. Content of NSR article should be merged to An Slua Muiri article, and NSR should be redirected to An Slua Muiri. Will likely do this myself in next few days if no objections, or unless someone gets to it before me. :) Guliolopez 10:31, 3 May 2006 (UTC)
The Irish Reserve Defence Forces were overhauled last year, and the An Slua Muirí name is entirely obsolete, since officially it no longer exists and the NSR is a successor organisation. The same is true of An Fórsa Cosanta Áitiúil, which is now the Army Reserve - see this comment in Dáil Éireann from Willie O'Dea, the current Minister for Defence. Move to Naval Service Reserve (currently a redirect to An Slua Muirí). --Kwekubo 22:17, 4 May 2006 (UTC)

[edit] New proposal

So I think the proposal now is to create a new article at Naval Service Reserve with most of the content from An Slua Muiri and NSR, but to include a History section mentioning the Slu. Redirect An Slua Muiri and NSR to the new article. Update the {{Irish Defence Forces}} template to take account of the move. Have I forgotten anything ? Oh, can someone do the same with the FCA ? Frelke 22:40, 4 May 2006 (UTC)

Agree with alternate proposal. Seems more appropriate. Per Kwekubo, the SM is now wholly replaced by the NSR (to the extent that the name in Irish is now officially changed to "Cúltaca Na Seirbhíse Cabhlaigh").
RE: Something similar for FCA. I understand that User:Stabilo boss was working on a similar restructure based on "FCA" move to "Army Reserve" (Move content to new page based on new name, and move references to "old" org to "history" section). He may have some thoughts/insights on doing the same for the FCA/AR.
