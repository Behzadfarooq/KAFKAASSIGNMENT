Anjali Dutt is a record producer/engineer from the United Kingdom[citation needed]. She has worked with many bands from Creation Records, including Oasis, Swervedriver, The Boo Radleys and My Bloody Valentine. She was of only a handful of female engineers working in the 1980s and 1990s. Starting at Matrix Studios, she worked for The Who at Ramport Studios, then Battery Studios in London and the mobile in Gaberone, Botswana before finally turning freelance.

Some of the more notable recordings she worked on are:

  • Oasis - Definitely Maybe (engineer)
  • My Bloody Valentine - Loveless (engineer)
  • Rollerskate Skinny - Horsedrawn Wishes (mixing)
  • Swervedriver - Raise (mixing)
  • The Jesus and Mary Chain - "Reverence" (single) (engineer)
  • Velo-Deluxe - Superelastic (producer, engineer, mixing)
  • Submarine (band) - Submarine
  • Spacemen 3 - Recurring (mixing)
  • The Boo Radleys - Giant Steps (mixing)
  • The Belltower - Popdropper (engineer)
  • Hugh Masekela - Technobush
  • Soon - Scintillate
  • Sleeper - Alice in Vain - (producer)
  • Edsel - Techniques of Speed Hypnosis - (producer)
