Anjou—Rivière-des-Prairies was a federal electoral district in Quebec, Canada, that was represented in the Canadian House of Commons from 1988 to 2004.

It was created in 1987 from parts of Gamelin, Montreal—Mercier and Saint-Léonard—Anjou ridings.

It consisted of:

  • the Town of Anjou;
  • the parts of the Town of Montréal bounded by:
  1. Sherbrooke Street East, Duquesne Street, Rosemont Boulevard and Lacordaire Boulevard; and
  2. Autoroute de la Rive Nord, Henri-Bourassa Boulevard East; the limits of the towns of Montréal-Est, Anjou and Montréal-Nord to the point of commencement.

It was abolished in 2003 when it was redistributed into Bourassa, Honoré-Mercier and La Pointe-de-l'Île ridings.

[edit] Members of Parliament

This riding elected the following Members of Parliament:

  1. Jean Corbeil, Progressive Conservative (1988-1993)
  2. Roger Pomerleau, Bloc Québécois (1993-1997)
  3. Yvon Charbonneau, Liberal (1997-2004)
