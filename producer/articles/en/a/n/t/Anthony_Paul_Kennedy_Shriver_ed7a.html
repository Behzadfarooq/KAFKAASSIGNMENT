Anthony Paul Kennedy Shriver

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Anthony Paul Kennedy Shriver (born July 20, 1965) is a United States activist for people with intellectual disabilities. In 1989, he founded Best Buddies International, an international organization that helps the intellectually disadvantaged to find employment and social opportunities.

He is part of the Kennedy family. His father is Sargent Shriver Jr. and his mother is Eunice Mary Kennedy who founded the Special Olympics movement in 1968. He graduated from the Georgetown Preparatory School and Georgetown University as an undergraduate, where in 1987, he had started a precursor Best Buddies volunteer program that paired university students wiht people with intellectual disabilities of similar age. He received a bachelor's degree with a double major in theology and history from Georgetown University in 1988. He is married and has four children.
