Further reading on Mediation is available at Wikipedia:Mediation. Please note that this is a personal guideline for my Mediation activites - i.e. cases where I act as a mediator, whether it be for the Mediation Cabal or the Mediation Committee; this is not an official policy, a guideline approved and/or generally accepted by the community or the Wikimedia Foundation. Please do not treat it as such, not cite it in disputes or discussions - it will just lead to embarrassment.

Mediation's purpose is defined as:

"...[Mediation] is meant to resolve disputes, and resolve issues of users who have repeatedly violated Wikipedia's policies, most specifically Wikipedia's policies of etiquette towards other users as stated at Wikipedia:Policies and guidelines and Wikipedia:Wikiquette..."

--Wikipedia:Mediation

I believe that this is not possible without impartial, neutral mediators to see over the process of calm, civil discussion between the disputing parties...a referee, if you will. I am of this belief for one simple reason: a mediator that was not impartial, neutral and indifferent right up until the end would be a hypocrite and therefore part of the dispute itself.

I also believe that civility is the key factor in mediation; a calm Wikipedia talk:Requests for Mediation/CASE NAME page is the most efficient area to foster a compromise, resolution or peaceful end to a dispute that the mediator has been charged with trying his or her utmost to resolve. Therefore, as a means to achieving the ends of a peaceful manner and refraining from hypocrisy, I follow the following guidelines and/or methods:

1 -- I will operate my personal neutrality policy (located here) right up until I post the compromise; when the time does to propose compromises and/or resolutions between the parties, it is impossible to remain impartial: the party that is in the right (or mostly in the right) is to receive a compromise that is most aggreable to them, and the party in the wrong is likely to receive a compromise that may not be aggreable to them. This is only fair - if you are right, you are right and Mediation does not seek to change that, only to identify it.

2 -- I will keep the talk page as tidy as possible; I will post clear-cut guidelines at the top of the talk page(s) that clearly state the Wikiquette and conduct that is to be followed, as well as the physical format that is to be followed. Examples are:

a) Replies are to be indented with the colon (:); therefore, the first post is taken to have zero colons and the first reply is to be prefixed with a single colon, the second reply to the post with two colons, and so on and so forth.
b) WP:CIVILITY is to be followed at all times; any posts that are contrary to this will be blanket removed or, in less serious cases, altered. Remember that altering may result in the unintentional altering of the tone or even meaning of your post - so why not just get it right first time?

3 -- From experience, the discussions leading up to the actual Request for Assistance/Mediation (for AMA/Mediation Cabal or Committee respectively) are normally very long and tend to be tangled, archived, deleted, undeleted, rearchived and generally muddled up. Therefore, I kick-start the mediation by asking all the parties to post a summary of both the dispute and what they want out of the mediation. The headers are especially important to conform to WP:NPOV, so I normally just make it a plain [[User:NAME|NAME]] after they have authored the statement.

These guidelines are built from my experience with the AMA, Mediation Cabal and, most recently, the Mediation Committee. Feel free to suggest possible extra points on my talk page.
