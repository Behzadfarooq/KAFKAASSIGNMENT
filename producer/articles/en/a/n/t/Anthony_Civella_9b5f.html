Anthony Civella

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Anthony Thomas "Tony Ripe" Civella (February 17, 1930-February 16, 2006) was a Kansas City mobster who headed syndicate operations following the conviction of his father Carl "Corky" Civella in 1984 for his role in skimming operation from Las Vegas casinos. Although having an extensive criminal record stretching back to 1952, Anthony Civella received five years for his own involvement. Reportedly controlling the organization through acting boss William "Willie the Rat" Cammasino, Civella continued its illegal gambling activities as well as maintaining its position in national gambling operations.

In 1991, less than three years following his early release, he became involved in reselling stolen prescription drugs for which he was convicted the following year. Released in 1997, he was listed in the "Black Book" by the Nevada Gaming Commission prohibiting his involvement in legalized gambling as well as similar exclusions in Missouri. Civella however retained his position in the Kansas City syndicate until his death on February 16, 2006.
