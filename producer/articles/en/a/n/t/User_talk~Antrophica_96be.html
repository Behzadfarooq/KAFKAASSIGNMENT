[edit] Apostrophes

With a singular subject, e.g. "Woody Allen", the possessive requires a "'s" after the "n".

Thus it would read, "Woody Allen's film"

With a singular subject which ENDS in an s, e.g. "Rhys-Meyers", the possessive simply needs the apostrophe.

Thus it would read, "Rhys-Meyers' performance".

SAME with a plural subject: "All of London's streets gold paving" looks absurd when written as "All of London's streets's gold paving".


Quote from Match Point: Match Point has snapped a cold streak for Allen's films at the box office. Having made its $15 million dollar budget nearly three times over with a worldwide gross of $42.9 million dollars as of February 2006.

So, "It", "It's" and "Its"

"It" is obvious.

"It's" = "IT IS", a corruption which drops the apostrophe.

The above sentence does not read: "Having made it is $15 million dollar budget"

"Its" = the possessive, belonging to. i.e. the $15 million belonging to the film....
