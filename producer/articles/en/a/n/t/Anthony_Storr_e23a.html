Anthony Storr

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Anthony Storr (18 May 1920â€“17 March 2001) was an English psychiatrist and author. Born in London, he was a child who was to endure the typical trauma of early 20th century boarding schools. He was educated at Winchester, Christ's College, the University of Cambridge and Westminster Hospital. He qualified as a doctor in 1944, and subsequently specialised in psychiatry.

Storr grew up to be kind and insightful, yet, as his obituary states, he was "no stranger to suffering" and was himself allegedly prone to the frequent bounts of depression his mother had.

Today, Anthony Storr is known for his psychoanalytical portraits of historical figures.
