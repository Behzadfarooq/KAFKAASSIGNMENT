[edit] AntiVandalBot mistakes / improvements needed

I find I'm really getting irritated by AntiVandalBot's disruption of a logical sequence of escalating warning messages on a user's talk page. It appears to post its standard boilerplate without looking at the comments and dates left by the previous substitution templates. In some cases I have taken to reverting the bot's edit and correcting it myself. I don't want to have to do this.

It doesn't seem hard to parse a page for the inline comments left by warning templates. For anonymous IP addresses, the bot could pay attention to warnings it finds within the past 24 hours and then post an escalated warning instead of the usual drivel it posts now.

Furthermore, the bot should stop inserting its own headings. It should use the -n templates such as {{subst:test2-n|PageName}} to insert the appropriate text with the name of the vandalized page built into it.

Just my $0.03 -Amatulic 22:52, 27 October 2006 (UTC)

It's something that's going to be added when we finally release version 2.0 or 3.0 depending on how you count of the bot... there really has been a war over the warnings the bot gives, people don't want an standard warning as it may confuse the rare new user who was acting IGF. As for the headers.... it's a major matter of discussion, personally I think it's easier (for me) at least to read... but we shall see - do you have a better warning template in mind? -- Tawker 23:17, 27 October 2006 (UTC)

You're right, the headers aren't a big deal, keep 'em.
The text is OK but I'd actually prefer the bot to use one of the standard warning templates and append its own text to it, something like this:
{{subst:test2-n|ArticleName}} ''This warning was added by [[AntilVandalBot]]. If this automated bot reverted a legitimate edit, please [[click here]] to bring it to the developers' attention so they can improve its behavior.''
That way the bot leaves behind the same standardized inline comments because it's using the standard templates. I use these comments to see what level warning has previously been added.
My major gripe is that the bot should be smart enough to know how to escalate the warnings based on what is already on the user's talk page. If that will be fixed in an upoming version, that's great. -Amatulic 23:28, 27 October 2006 (UTC)
Half the problem is the fact that the template is not stable... maybe we'll add an comment into the existing warnings which should help a bit -- Tawker 23:33, 27 October 2006 (UTC)
Ah, I see. Well, maybe a first step would be the capability for the bot to escalate its own warning level if the bot had previously placed one. Then at least you have control over the format and stability. And it would help if I could see the "level" of the bot's warning in a comment. -Amatulic 00:15, 28 October 2006 (UTC)
