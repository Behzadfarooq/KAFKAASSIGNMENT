[edit] Altermondialisation

I don't want an edit war, but I do not understand your objection to linking Anti-globalization to fr:Altermondialisation. Yes, I understand that one word is not neatly a translation of the other, but the articles seem to cover approximately the same territory. Many articles link to articles on closely related but not identical topics. Consider, for example European dragon linking to ca:Drac, which includes Asian dragons. Are you simply being more purist in this case, and if so why? Or is there something else going on here? -- Jmabel 04:33, 19 Apr 2004 (UTC)

Well, for what it is worth, few people in the movement in the English-speaking world like the term "anti-globalization", but it's rather what the press has stuck on us. Clearly, the notion of a global movement against globalization is oxymoronic. Clearly, the reality is a movement against corporate-driven globalization. But just like "anti-Semitism" means a dislike of Jews, not of all Semites, sometimes the effective meaning of a word does not correspond to its etymology. Unless I have the matter very confused (and please make your case if you think I do), what Anglophones generally call "Anti-Globalization" (or, outside the US "Anti-Globalisation") has more to do with Altermondialisation than with Antimondialisation. In any case, I presume someone could easily find there way between two French-language articles that ought, presumably, to link to one another. If you want to link to a different French language article, feel free, but having no link to any French article falsely implies that there is no closely related article in the French Wikipedia, ne c'est pas? -- Jmabel 04:55, 19 Apr 2004 (UTC)
True. Note that militants apparently convinced media to rather use alter than anti. I know not whether really your anti globalization is rather our alter or our anti. There is currently an edit war on the topic on alter on fr. When things are settled, I'll see. Meanwhile, anti should be linked, not alter.
