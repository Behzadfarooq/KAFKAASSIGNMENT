[edit] Puppets and pets

Would Anthropomorphobia be the appropriate way to describe someone who is afraid to or loath to use the toilet or shower while a household pet is present out of a sense of modesty? Mareino 17:27, 6 December 2005 (UTC)

No, I don't think that would be it, because you don't exactly fear instilling them with human values, but rather, you've instilled them with human values and are afraid to change in front of them. To be honest, I think it's more rooted in human methods of teaching privacy and total isolation and to be fanatically protective about nudity. That, and you are more vulnerable when naked so you may feel less dominant around an animal, who is always naked and thus not at a disadvantage. This may also be rooted in fear of being walked in on and thought of as a zoophile. Tyciol 02:36, 7 January 2007 (UTC)
Good question. I'm afraid of puppets and other things that are trying to appear human. Does this term apply to me? Clearly we need more information. Lotusduck 22:34, 31 May 2006 (UTC)
Yes, it would apply to you. Puppets don't 'try' to appear human, since they're not conscious, humans make them appear human. Tyciol 02:36, 7 January 2007 (UTC)
Yeah, that's an anthropomorphobic response. You're not gonna get a solid boundary for a phobia or an exclusive set of phobias: Phobias are more of a "method" of response to fear. basilwhite 18:48, 22 June 2006 (UTC)
Clearly, this is a real phobia, and it clearly needs more documentation. Unfortunately, I haven't been able to find much. Have they done any studies on this? 68.104.201.53 06:19, 18 August 2006 (UTC)
