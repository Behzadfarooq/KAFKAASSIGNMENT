Please be advised by a patient who has undergone both a patellar graft and a cadavar graft, that a cadavar graft is by far the best option. I was not informed before surgery that I had any options and the pain after the patellar graft was staggaring. Further, the rehabilitation of the injured knee was never sucessfull and eventually failed, requiring a second reconstructive surgery. To complicate matters, the graft resulted in degenerative arthritis and the delay in determining that the original graft had failed resulted in further injuries.

Practially, it took a month to move the affected leg of my on volition after the first surgery, two month's before any weight could be put on it, and a full six months before I could return to limited work.

With the cadaver graft, I could walk THE SAME DAY. The pain was exponentally less, and in retrospect, I believe that had this been the inital course of treatment, my further injuries and deteriorations would not have occured.

When encountering any such situation, PLEASE, check and see what options are available, as not all (or many) doctors will fully disclose your choices.
