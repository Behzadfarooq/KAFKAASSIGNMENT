[edit] Proposed compromise 2: User:Sbhushan

  1. change sentence The "Indigenous Aryans" position would be a necessary corollary of an Indian origin of the Indo-European languages.[1] to read Unavoidable corollary of the "Indigenous Aryans" position would be an Indian origin of the Indo-European languages.[2] to comply with WP:ATT to accuarately reflect referenced authors position. The changed sentence should also be moved to the first paragraph.Sbhushan 20:08, 8 March 2007 (UTC)
  1. ^ if proto-Indo-European was indigenous to India, some predecessor of any daughter langauge found outside India must have emigrated from there; Bryant 2001 page 6
  2. ^ It must be stated immediately that there is an unavoidable corollary of an Indigenist position. If the Indo-Aryan languages did not come from outside South Asia, this necessarily entails that India was the original homeland of all the other Indo-European languages. Bryant 2001 page 6
As background the full text from Bryant page 6 is It must be stated immediately that there is an unavoidable corollary of an Indigenist position. If the Indo-Aryan languages did not come from outside South Asia, this necessarily entails that India was the original homeland of all the other Indo-European languages. Indo-Aryan was preceded by Indo-Iranian, which was preceded, in turn, by Indo-European; so if Indo-Aryan was indignous to India, its predecessors must have been also. Hence, if proto-Indo-European was indigenous to India, all the other cognate languages must have emigrated from there.Sbhushan 20:10, 8 March 2007 (UTC)

Oppose - on basis that it is another paraphrase rather than providing the exact quote. I would support replacing the sentence that is there now with the direct quotation from Bryant which makes the same point, but which cannot be rejected as an interpretation of a reliable source, but merely a quotation from a reliable source. Buddhipriya 21:39, 8 March 2007 (UTC)

Agree - with Buddhipriya's suggestion to quote full text from Bryant provided above.Sbhushan 13:57, 9 March 2007 (UTC)
