António Pedro

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

António Pedro (1909-1966) was a Portuguese painter. He was one of the introducers of Surrealism in Portuguese painting, in the late 1930's. It's official start it's set to be the expostition he held, with António Dacosta, in Lisbon, in 1940.

His painting shows the influence of the great surrealist painters, that he admired, like Giorgio De Chirico, Max Ernst and Salvador Dalí. Her was a founding member of the Portuguese Surrealist Group, in 1947, but he left painting few before.

He dedicated himself to pottery and theater for the rest of his life.

He was also an active anti-fascist militant.
