The short version is that if you (1) go to the "History" tab at the top of a page, (2) view the version that you would like to revert to, (3) click the "Edit" tab at the top of the page, and (4) save the page, that will revert the page to the older version. [edit] Re-creation of deleted article

It appears to me that an article on Antoine Williams was deleted, and then you re-created it as "article in progress". This is not the proper procedure. Your alternatives:

  • If you think that the original deletion was improper, you can list the dispute at Wikipedia:Deletion review.
  • If you understand the original deletion but you think you can overcome the notability issue by adding additional information (with the sources cited), you may do so. The original deletion prohibits re-creation of the deleted content, but new content on the subject is permissible. To follow this course, however, please don't use Wikipedia as a scratch pad. Work on a new version on your hard drive. When you think it establishes notability, post it.

JamesMLane t c 10:40, 15 December 2006 (UTC)

I have a slightly different opinion. I think it is reasonable to use Wikipedia as a scratchpad, but certainly not the main article space.
