Antillocladius is a genus of flies belonging to the family Chironomidae, subfamily Orthocladiinae. The name Antillocladius means orthoclad (subfamily Orthocladiinae) that occurs in the Antilles, where it was primarily recorded.

The genus Antillocladius is distributed in the Nearctic, Neotropical, Palaearctic (East Asia) and Indomalayan ecozones. The genus was erected by Sæther (1981) based on A. antecalvus Sæther, 1981, from the British West Indies. Subsequently, Sæther (1982) described two new species from the southeastern United States and amended the genus. Wang and Sæther (1993) described two additional new species from Palaearctic and Oriental China. Andersen and Contreras-Ramos (1999) described the first species from Continental South America (Ecuador). Recently revised by Mendes, Andersen & Sæther (2004), with the addition of nine species, plus two Japanese species transferred to the genus (Yamamoto, 2004). The genus thus comprises:

  • Antillocladius antecalvus Sæther, 1981 - recorded from Saint Vincent, Brazil and Venezuela
  • Antillocladius arcuatus Sæther, 1982 - recorded from USA, Brazil, Mexico and Venezuela
  • Antillocladius biota Mendes, Andersen et Sæther, 2004 - recorded from Brazil
  • Antillocladius calakmulensis Mendes, Andersen et Sæther, 2004 - recorded from Mexico
  • Antillocladius folius Mendes, Andersen et Sæther, 2004 - recorded from Brazil
  • Antillocladius herradurus Mendes, Andersen et Sæther, 2004 - recorded from Mexico
  • Antillocladius musci Mendes, Andersen et Sæther, 2004 - recorded from Brazil
  • Antillocladius pluspilalus Sæther, 1982 - recorded from USA, Ecuador, Mexico and Nicaragua
  • Antillocladius scalpellatus Wang et Sæther, 1993 - recorded from China and Russia
  • Antillocladius skartveiti Andersen et Contreras-Ramos, 1999 - recorded from Ecuador
  • Antillocladius sooretama Mendes, Andersen et Sæther, 2004 - recorded from Brazil
  • Antillocladius tokarameneus (Sasa & Suzuki, 1995) - recorded from Japan
  • Antillocladius ubatuba Mendes, Andersen et Sæther, 2004 - recorded from Brazil
  • Antillocladius venequatoriensis Mendes, Andersen et Sæther, 2004 - recorded from Venezuela and Ecuador
  • Antillocladius yakyijeus (Sasa & Suzuki, 2000) - recorded from Japan
  • Antillocladius zempoalensis Mendes, Andersen et Sæther, 2004 - recorded from Mexico
  • Antillocladius zhengi Wang et Sæther, 1993 - recorded from China and Thailand

The species are based on male genitalia characters and only two females are described, A. musci and A. antecalvus. The pupae are known of only four species, A. antecalvus, A. folius, A. musci and A. pluspilalus. The larvae are known of only three species, A. folius, A. musci and A. pluspilalus.

The immatures of the genus are described from terrestrial and semi-terrestrial habitats, sharing the environment with many other genera in the subfamily Orthocladiinae.
