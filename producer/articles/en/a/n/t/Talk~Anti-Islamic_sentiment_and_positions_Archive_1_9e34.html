Why no article on the mass murder of Muslims?

There is an article on Persection of Christians, Anti-Semitism and on Homophobia, but what about the killing of Muslims? Worldwide, millions of Muslims have been murdered in the past few decades, by people such as Saddam Hussein, Ayatollah Khomeini of Iran, etc? RK 00:39, Oct 21, 2003 (UTC)

The National Islamic Front in Sudan has killed largely Christians and animists, but also hundreds of thousands of Muslims, especially among the Beja, Fur, Massaleit, Tama, and Nuba peoples. Its militias have also taken Muslim slaves.
The Armed Islamic Group in Algeria has murdered more than 100,000 Muslims in the last decade. In Chechnya, another 100,000 people, one-10th of the population, has been killed and almost half the population is displaced. In Afghanistan, the Taliban and their Al Qaeda allies killed thousands of Shia. In Mauritania, tens of thousand of Muslims are held as slaves. Tens of thousands more died in the Kashmir conflict and in the civil wars in Liberia, Ivory Coast, and Sierra Leone. Thousands more have died in Nigeria and Indonesia. The Burmese junta drove out more than a quarter million of its Rohingya Muslims in the early 1990s. In India last year some 2,000 Muslims were slaughtered in Gujarat, some disemboweled or burned alive while police stood by or joined in.
Yet these events are passed over in silence, even within much of the Muslim world. Meanwhile, the perpetrators of many of these atrocities sit in the UN condemning events in the West Bank.

Edit war

Can the people involved in the edit war explain their positions here on the talk page? I've put the page on Wikipedia:Requests for page protection

I've not done anything, but I'll start: there may be a useful distinction between "Anti-Islamism" and "Islamophobia" to be made (though I'm not so impressed by the suggestion); but in any case there is not enough substance here to support two articles: a single article explaining to two side-by-side is almost certainly better for readers. ---- Charles Stewart 20:10, 12 Nov 2004 (UTC)

For a start, I would demand empirical evidence that there is a difference in usage between the two terms before attempting to create one... - Mustafaa 22:47, 12 Nov 2004 (UTC)

My position, as mentioned during this "revert-war", is that the title of article "Islamophobia" itself is POV. IMO the title suggest that fear of Islam is irrational, which is not an opinion shared by everyone, especially not those on the side criticising Islam. (Ali Sina, Theo van Gogh, Daniel Pipes and others..) To redirect "Anti-Islamism" to the article "Islamophobia" would be to suggest that all anti-Islamism is about an irrational fear. That is as POV as it can get! The title "Anti-Islamism" on the other hand is quite neutral. Cphka 09:57, 13 Nov 2004 (UTC)

This is a serious matter, but it is in conflict with the policy to follow the most common name for something when naming pages. The article need not be POV if the problems with the title are clearly laid out, and it is not the purpose of Wikipedia to carry out language reform. The Islamophobia article documents usage of the term in the media: no documentation of alternate names have been proposed. I have a particular problem with the name "Anti-Islamism", in that Islamism is a very widely used term for a class of political positions, and so the name is very suggestive of a political reaction, and not of a religious or cultural reaction. If you want to pursue a different article name than Islamophobia, I siggest you either show this term is used as you say, or coin a better term (perhaps, egI Anti-Islamic positions). ---- Charles Stewart 20:47, 13 Nov 2004 (UTC)
The article "Anti-Islamism" is, I think we can all agree to that, about a HIGHLY controversial subject. IMHO, neutrality should in these cases be more important than the "rule of following the most common name". In this case it is because I cant see how critics of Islam, should be able to get their side of the discussion presented in a fair way, in an article which title suggest they are suffering from a mysterious mental illness called "Islamophobia". To explain somewhere in the article, that it is controversial to claim that critics of Islam are more or less suffering from insanity, is simply not enough. But again if it is the rule of Wikipedia, that using the most common media-word is more important than neutrality, then please follow that rule! I cant find a title that is more used in the media than "Islamophobia". Apart from that, I agree that "Anti-Islamism" is not the ideal title. "Islamism" should be avoided, for the reasons mentioned. Anti-Islamic positions I think is actually not a bad suggestion for a new title, or maybe it could be Anti-Islamic criticism? Cphka 22:53, 13 Nov 2004 (UTC)

User:OneGuy and User:Mustafaa's changes to the status of this article

I really dislike this article. It serves no useful purpose, housing almost no content not available elsewhere on wikipedia, but labelled in a misleading way that leads to ugly tension. I've had a reminder to sort out this article sitting at the top of my reminders list since the 12th November, and the only idea about it that has at all appealed to me is to put it up for VfD...

Feelings aside, the phrasing I put together for the NPOV sticker was a compromise position designed to end the edit war we had back then. Given this history, I think it is only responsible to tamper with the wording after explaining what the change is and why here on the talk page. Anything else risks conflagration. User:Mustafaa: please justify your change! I think I won't agree: the current wording of the lead paragraph indicates that the term is being used in this article to be about criticism about Muslims, and not about Islamism, contrary to the obvious meaning of the word, and without any authoratative precedent for this usage. We agree about the badness of the term, but I think moving the article, hopefully prior to a merge, is the right way to go about solving the problem; I don't see the sense in changing what is already a counterproductive article, especially to merge it with an article that doesn't already discuss positions against Islamism.

Oh, and User:OneGuy has a history of being a POV warrior, for those who have not seen his work elsewhere. ---- Charles Stewart 21:41, 9 Feb 2005 (UTC)

Ah - I see your point. I hadn't really thought about that, but certainly the current actual (as opposed to ideal) content of this article would call for a merger with Islamophobia. - Mustafaa 21:53, 9 Feb 2005 (UTC)
I should add that I've no personal bad experiences with User:OneGuy, and POV warrior was over the top, unjustified, and in violation of respect other editors rule. I regret what I said and apologise to User:OneGuy ---- Charles Stewart 09:01, 10 Feb 2005 (UTC)
