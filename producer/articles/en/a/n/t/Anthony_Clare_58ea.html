Dr Anthony Clare (b. 24 December 1942 in Dublin, Ireland) is well known in the British Isles for presenting programmes about psychiatry on BBC TV and Radio. Author of several popular books on psychiatry, he has held the positions of Professor of Clinical Psychiatry at Trinity College Dublin and Medical Director of St Patrick's Hospital, where he is now a Consultant General Adult Psychiatrist.

In the 1980s and 1990s he was the best known psychiatrist in Britain but since his return to Ireland his prominent position in the public mind has been replaced by Raj Persaud[1][2].
