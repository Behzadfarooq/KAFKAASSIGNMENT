Antoni Marian Łomnicki (b. January 17, 1881 in Lwów - July 4, 1941 in Lwów) was a Polish mathematician.

Antoni educated on the Lwów University and the University of Göttingen. In 1920 he became professor of the Lwów University of Technology. Stefan Banach was his assistant. Since 1938 he was member of the Warsaw Science Society (TWN).

He was murdered by the Germans during the Second World War on the Wzgórza Wuleckie in Lwów.
