Antonio Casas (11 November 1911 A Coruña, Galicia - 14 February 1982 Madrid, Spain) was a Spanish film actor who appeared in film between 1941 and his death in 1982.

Casas orignally began as a footballer but entered film in 1941 and made nearly 170 appearances in film and TV between then and 1982. He appeared in Sergio Leone's Spaghetti Western the The Good, the Bad and the Ugly in 1966, a film that has consistently been voted one of the greatest of all time.

In the early 1970s he worked in television but returned to film after 1975 until his death.
