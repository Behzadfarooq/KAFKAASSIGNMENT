[edit] Antivirus or Anti-virus?

ch-udi

Personally, I prefer antivirus—there isn't any reason to hyphenate off the two parts of the words because there isn't much room for the mispronunciation or ambiguity in the word. For example, one might say pre-emptive instead of preemptive to make it clear that pre-empt is two syllables. You might also use a hyphen in re-place, meaning to place something again, to distinguish it from replace, to exchange one thing for something else. I don't see any justification for "anti-virus". ETA: Theshibboleth 03:29, 26 April 2006 (UTC)

[edit] Requested move

Anti-virus software → Antivirus software – Antivirus yields more results on Google. Anti-virus is awkward and is less likely to be used by the "average user" than antivirus. Theshibboleth 03:34, 26 April 2006 (UTC)
