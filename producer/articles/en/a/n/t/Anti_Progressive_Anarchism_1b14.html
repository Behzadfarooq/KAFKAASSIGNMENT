Anti Progressive Anarchism is a variation on traditional anarchy and Anarcho-Primitivism. Little known outside of small anarchist undergrounds, this philisophical government system was created by Welsh born English professor J.T. Henderson. He wrote about it in his book, "Anarchy and Apocalypse: How Humanity Fucked Up." Written in 1953, it is the bible of Anti Progressive Anarchism. The book is very rare and out of print but a few copies will pop up on ebay every once in a while.

[edit] Concepts

The main principles of Anti Progressive Anarchism are laid out in the first few pages of Professor Henderson's book. He writes that he got the idea one day when he came home from work and noticed how unfulfilling his life really was and how the progression of humanity has stolen morality away from us. He speaks of the matter more further here:

Life has become less about living and more about how we can carry on humanity. Couples are having children not because of the joys of holding an infant in their arms or the euphoric feeling you get when you know that this child might save the world some day, but just so that their surname can live on another generation. We don't work so that we can help people, we work so that we can be superior. We're doing good things for all the wrong reasons.[1]

Henderson writes on that a revolution should occur in which everyone stops going to work and school and starts doing what makes themselves and others happy. In the epilogue of his book, he writes:

Humanity deserves to become extinct. I really do hope that God wants us to be rid of the universe. Earth should be once again given to the beasts and what ever made humans should occur again but let us hope that they do not follow the route in which we did.[2]

[edit] Anti Progressives on Creation

Anti Progressive Anarchist believe that we should all grow to accept the fact that we are here on this Earth and that it is pointless to question how, why and when we got here.

This same principle goes with all other things such as the big bang, evolution and reincarnation. To quote Anarchy and Apocalypse:

Christians preach their God, Buddists preach their reincarnation, scientists preach their various theories. Are they really any different? A bunch of hocus pocus that happened a long time ago that can't be proven or disproven. So what's the point of debating them?[3]
