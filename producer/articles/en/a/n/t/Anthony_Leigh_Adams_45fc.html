Anthony Leigh Adams is a writer, producer and director with credits in film, television and theatre. For television, he has written over 30 episodes for such diverse series as the Disney cartoon Duck Tales, Fraggle Rock and She Wolf of London[citation needed]. He also wrote and produced the ABC movie, Badge of Betrayal, starring Harry Hamlin and the NBC movie, Murder or Memory, starring Emmy-winner, Leigh Taylor Young[citation needed]. With his partner, Christina Adams, he’s developed a number of projects for Disney, Tri-Star, Universal, Hearst Entertainment, ABC and NBC, among others[citation needed]. He’s directed a number of award-winning videos and short films, including The Deflowering of Budd Stump, starring Christopher Atkins. Hiss feature film directing debut was Lost Lake, which he co-wrote and co-produced, was released on DVD (summer 2005) by Warner Brothers Home Video, after a limited theatrical release[citation needed].

In addition to his work in film and television Leigh-Adams is also a playwright and composer. He has directed several of his original plays and musicals in London and San Diego, including The Adventures of Olig and Obster, A Song For Gar, An Eye In Each Head, The Living Cell and The Great Relativity Bomb Plot[citation needed]. He also composed music for the national touring production of The Grapes of Wrath, starring Ed Harris and John Carradine and toured as a performer with the Harry Partch Ensemble[citation needed]. As a journalist, he’s written feature articles on the latest developments in the Independent Film world, including coverage of the Sundance Film Festival for Written By, the magazine of the Writers Guild of America and The Hollywood Reporter[citation needed].

In September 2006 Anthony co-wrote and directed the world premiere of Primal Twang: The Legacy of the Guitar, about the history of the guitar. Adams directed the filming of the performances for commercial release[citation needed].

After completing postproduction on the film version of Primal Twang, Leigh Adams will direct the feature film, Nuevo Sol, based on his original screenplay co-written with Christina Adams[citation needed]. He will also direct the theatrical concert, Summer of Love, a ‘60’s rock musical, co-written with Christina Adams, which will premiere in San Diego, summer 2007[citation needed].
