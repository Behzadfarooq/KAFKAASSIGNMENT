In summary, if things get heated, disengage. When it's back to room temperature, follow the steps. If you believe that you are in the right, consult DR for what to do next. I personally recommend the AMA if you feel there is an element of incivility towards you, or if you simply need help.

Lastly, don't hesitate to get help! Contact others via email, and ask for the opinion of others that may have been involved as third parties; conduct a survey (a straw poll). Just make sure you're civil!

Be neutral; be civil; have fun!

 Lesson the First: Disputes, and How to Handle Them;

Links on this page are rendered in orange; be sure to hover over important phrases - chances are, there will be a link.
Disputes are a part of Wikipedia that will never leave: at the end of the day, humans differ from one another and, at some point or another, will disagree. Wikipedians are humans, too, and as such sooner or later a dispute will be brought to your front door. That's when you need to tread carefully; before you do anything, take the time to get your facts in order: check out links, or manually fetch the pages if links aren't given in the post. Make sure you've got the full story, and that you didn't miss anything first time. Then, leave it for a bit; make sure you're perfectly calm and focused - don't post when you're angry!.

That's when the DR begins: the first step of Dispute Resolution (DR) is to discuss, so do that. When you reply, start of civilly; ask how their doing, then thank them for bringing the matter to their attention. Remain civil. Then, you can take one of these steps:

1. -- if you are in the wrong, apologise. "Please accept my humblest apologies" should do.
2. -- if they are in the wrong, then you need to carefully bring it to their attention: "I respectfully beg to differ: (why you beg to differ; include links, and link to policy: use the links at the left)"
3. -- if both of you are wrong, or if one is incorrect but the other has conducted themselves wrong, etc... if it's you that's wrong, follow step 1. If they are wrong, step 2. If you have conducted poorly, then apologise! For example, "I would like to apologise for (link to diff* of comment that you are apologising about); I acted with incivility, and I beg you to accept my humblest apologies. If they have conducted themselves poorly, bring it to their attention softly, with links and policy links.
