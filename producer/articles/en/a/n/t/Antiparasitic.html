Antiparasitic

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

You have new messages (last change).

Antiparasitics are a class of medications which are indicated for the treatment of infection by parasites such as nematodes, cestodes, trematodes, infectious protozoa, and amoebas.

[edit] Antinematodes

  • Mebendazole (for most nematode infections)
  • Pyrantel pamoate (for most nematode infections)
  • Thiabendazole (for roundworm infections)
  • Diethycarbazine (for treatment of Lymphatic filariasis)

[edit] Anticestodes

  • Niclosamide (for tapeworm infections)
  • Praziquantel (for tapeworm infections)

[edit] Antitrematodes

  • Praziquantel

[edit] Antiamoebics

  • Rifampin
  • Amphotericin B

[edit] Antiprotozoals

  • Melarsoprol (for treatment of sleeping sickness caused by Trypanosoma brucei)
