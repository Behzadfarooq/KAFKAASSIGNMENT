Antonio Raggi

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Antonio Raggi (also called also called Antonio Lombardo; 1624-1686) was an Italian sculptor of the Roman Baroque.

[edit] Biography

He was born in Vico Morcote near Como. His mentor in Rome for nearly three decades was Gianlorenzo Bernini. He was initially joined the studio of Alessandro Algardi, but none of his work there is independently recognized and by 1647, like Ercole Ferrata, Raggi was working for Bernini, for whom he was to become his closest and most prolific pupil[1].

He completed the stucco decoration of San Tomaso di Villanova in Castel Gandolfo (1660-1), the stucco decoration of Bernini's Sant'Andrea 1662-1665), the statues of Saint Bernardino and Pope Alexander VII Chigi for Duomo di Siena and the Virgin and child in Saint Joseph des Navona in Paris (1650-51). He made the Baptism of Christ for the altar of San Giovanni dei Fiorentini (c. 1665).

His marble masterpiece is the relief of the Death of Saint Cecilia[2], which is present the church of Sant'Agnese in Agone. Here it can be contrasted with the relief by the Algardi pupil, Ferrata, of the Stoning of Santa Emerenziana.

He was instrumental in completing illusionistic stucco decoration accompanying Giovanni Battista Gaulli's ceiling fresco as other stucco figures in il Gesù. He was helped by Leonardo Retti, Michele Maglia, and Paolo Naldini.

He completed one of the angels in the Bridge of Sant'Angelo, and the statue of the Danube in the Fountain of Four Rivers in Piazza Navona. He was elected to the Academy of Saint Luke on July 1, 1657. Raggi died in Rome.
