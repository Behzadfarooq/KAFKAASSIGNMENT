[edit] Myrmidonts

Just out of interest, where does the name Myrmidonts come from? Is that how the antlion guards are referred to in the code, or is it something from one of those dubious Prima guides? --Plumbago 08:47, 9 August 2005 (UTC)

The Vortigaunt that comes out after it's been defeated in Sandtraps and pulls out the bugbait glands calls it a Myrmidont. Of course, real antlions are very similar named, Myrmeleontidae, which makes me wonder if Myrmidont might be the species name as a whole and not just the Antlion Guard. Until more information is available, I'd keep it under the Guard section.
Also, the Combine call all antlions "Exogens". They use this quite frequently in the Nova Prospekt chapter. --YoungFreud 06:54, 12 August 2005 (UTC)

Ah-ha. Now that you say that I half-remember that vortigaunt saying something like this. Thanks.

As regards the antlions, it's not 100% clear (to me anyway!) in HL2 that they are the same species as the antlion guards. One would presume so, given that the guards contain bugbait, and the almost-ran "antlion king/queen" strongly suggests a social insect-like hive system. But it's also possible that, although much larger, the guards use the bugbait in their natural habitat (wherever that is) to stop the more numerous antlions from attacking them. Also, the guards don't strike me as a burrowing creature like the antlions. Anyway, my guess is that they will turn out to be the same species, and that Valve might resurrect the king/queen later (Aftermath perhaps?). Shame to waste any development hours they've spent on that.--Plumbago 08:05, 12 August 2005 (UTC)

Mytmi and dont apparently mean ant and tooth respectively. The origin of antlions has also been confirmed [1]
Myrmidont is a reference to the Myrmidons, the people created from ants infesting an oak tree (their king's city had been decimated by a plague, and he wanted subjects). They followed Achilles to the Trojan War and were known for their willingness to carry out orders, no matter how brutal. It fits because the Antlions react in exactly the same way to the Antlion Guard, carrying out its orders no matter what they are or the risk to their own safety. This also applies to Gordon after he gets the pheropods. AK-17 13:45, 12 May 2006 (UTC)

[edit] There are no thumpers in Xen

I removed a bit of far-reaching and false speculation stating that there are devices resembling thumpers on Xen. This would imply that these devices could be thumpers. However, this is incorrect. These devices are definitely the mining equipment as they appear to be. The official Half-Life strategy guide refers to the lower underground areas seen in the Xen chapter as "The Mines". Obviously, mining equipment would go with mines. Although Antlions may come from Xen, this equipment has absolutely no relation to them. MarphyBlack 02:09, 4 September 2006 (UTC)
