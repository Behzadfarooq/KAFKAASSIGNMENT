[edit] This article is getting out of contol

Ok everyone, filling an encyclopedia article with un-sited, OR children's songs, simply does not work. This entire article is filled un-sourced POV claims, a perfect example would be the Anti-Christ section (and very poorly written at that). Yes, it is well known that Barney has attracted a significant amount hatred, and is frequently mocked and made fun of across the media. That does not mean however, that an article written about the topic should be poor and biased like the jokes themselves are. In other words, this article needs a serious complete re-write, and one that will cover the topic honestly, and not fill 3 pages full of riducules children's jokes. Avador 05:08, 9 April 2006 (UTC)

Actually, the Barney = Satan thing has been around for a LONG time, almost since the show itself came out. I don't see how you do a "serious" re-write about anti-Barney humor and not include at least one example of what you are talking about. In the case of Barney = Satan, it's certainly not as outlandish as some of the stuff I have seen or heard. I do agree however that the topic can be whittled down somewhat to become more "disciplined"...this is an encyclopedia, not The Laugh Hour.
  • Indeed. I think the jokes should be reduced to a mention; writing out the jokes in their entirety is not necessary (they can be externally linked for those who are interested).--Aleron235 01:45, 19 April 2006 (UTC)
I'm going to go ahead and delete the 10 or so versions of the Barney song.--Aleron235 01:46, 19 April 2006 (UTC)

[edit] Barkley vs. Barney

I don't know how an article like this cannot contain a mention of Barkley vs. Barney, a Saturday Night Live sketch where Charles Barkley plays one-on-one basketball with Barney. Barkley plays the game in his usual style complete with elbows, hard fouls, pushing and shoving, and power slam dunks right in Barney's face. This to me is the most memorable "Anti-Barney humor" moment on television. The fact that the article neglects to mention this tells me that it is seriously lacking as an article, in my opinion.
