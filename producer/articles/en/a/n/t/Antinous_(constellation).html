Antinous (constellation)

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

You have new messages (last change).

Antinous was a former constellation south of Aquila. According to a tale, an oracle told emperor Hadrian that only death of his most beloved person would save him from a great danger. It is said that Antinous, a beautiful teenage boy loved by Hadrian, saved the man by drowning in the Nile. For his memory Hadrian created the constellation in 132. In modern times, Antinous was variously considered an asterism within Aquila, or a separate constellation, until IAU formalized the constellations in 1930, when Antinous was discarded.
