Antioch on the Maeander

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Antiochia on the Maeander also Antioch on the Maeander (Greek: Αντιόχεια του Μαίανδρου; Latin: Antiochia ad Mæandrum), earlier Pythopolis, was a city of ancient Caria, in Anatolia. The city was situated between the Maeander and Orsinus rivers near their confluence and, though it was the site of a bridge over the Maeander had "little or no individual history" (ref. princeton) The scanty ruins are located on a hill (named, in Turkish, Jeni-Sher or Yenişer) a few km southeast of Kuyucak, Aydin Province, Turkey. The city already existed when Antiochus I enlarged and renamed it. It was home to the sophist Diotrephis.[1] It has not been excavated.
