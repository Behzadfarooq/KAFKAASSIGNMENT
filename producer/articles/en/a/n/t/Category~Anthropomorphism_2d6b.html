Category:Anthropomorphism

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Anthropomorphism, also referred to as personification or prosopopeia, is the attribution of human characteristics to animals, plants, objects, natural forces, symbols or abstract ideas. "Anthropomorphism" comes from two Greek words, ανθρωπος, anthrōpos, meaning human, and μορφη, morphē, meaning shape or form.
