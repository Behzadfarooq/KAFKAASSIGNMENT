Antonio Molino Rojo (born c.1925) was a Spanish film actor who appeared primarily in Spaghetti westerns in the 1960s and 1970s.

He made nearly 90 appearances in film between 1955 and 1988 but is probably most recognizable in western cinema for his roles in the Sergio Leone trilogy of Spaghetti westerns a A Fistful of Dollars (1964), For a Few Dollars More, (1965) and the The Good, the Bad and the Ugly in 1966. He also appeared in the Sergio Leone picture C'era una volta il West in 1968.

Rojo did not always play gang members in the westerns, in the The Good, the Bad and the Ugly, Rojo portrayed the good captain at the Union concentration camp whose leg was being eroded by gangrene. In the film he warned the character of Angel Eye (Lee Van Cleef not to be dishonest.
