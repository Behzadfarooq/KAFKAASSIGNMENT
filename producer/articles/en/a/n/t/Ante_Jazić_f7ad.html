Ante Jazić (born in February 26, 1976 in Bedford, Nova Scotia, Canada) is a professional soccer player. A Canadian national team member, Jazić currently plays for the Los Angeles Galaxy of Major League Soccer. He played for a number of European clubs during the late 1990s and early 2000s and is the highest level soccer player to come out of Nova Scotia.

Playing for one the nation's strongest amateur teams Halifax King of Donair and a year with Dalhousie University in which he was named All-Canadian CIAU, Jazic began his professional career by joining NK Hrvatski Dragovoljac in 1997 after a successful try-out arranged by an uncle in Croatia. After two years with Dragovoljac, the midfielder / defender joined Croatian First League club Hajduk Split for the 1999-00 and 2000-01 seasons.

Jazić played four seasons with Rapid Vienna. He most recently played for FC Kuban Krasnodar in the Russian First Division. On June 27, 2006, he signed a contract with the LA Galaxy of MLS, which will reunite him with former Canadian National team coach Frank Yallop.
