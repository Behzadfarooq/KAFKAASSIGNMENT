Anton Hecht

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Anton Hecht (b. November 14, 1971) is an English artist born in Durham.

His work varies in form as much as it does in subject matter. Critically acclaimed work for the theatre includes Mr Benn (a piece which ran backwards), Jean-Marie, The War-Dregs (an anti-liberal protest), Wrestling Bernard Shaw, Ladies To Be Loved, Growl and Whoops! Mr Turner.

For the visual arts, he submitted Orchestrations, which took a whizzing steadicam look from one end of the region to the other played alongside Beethoven's Fifth Symphony, to the 2001 Middlesbrough Arts Festival, and Less Of That, an experiment in minimalist filmmaking that resulted in little more than blank film, was showcased in Newcastle's prestigious Tyneside Cineplaza. He has also constructed representations of box girder bridges for The Baltic Art Gallery and provided both written pieces and vocal talent for BBC Cumbria's New Drama Talent competition.

Steve Kenney, Michael Sibley and Sean Carruthers have all aided him, often alongside with choreographer Kelly Turnbull and Paul Aziz (Art Director/Designer) who have helped Anton on his latest project "Falling Down", a video installation work for Whitley Bay Council which centres on The Minors' Strike but also questions the integrity of modern speech, as well as one of his most successful - 1997's "Blinking Ballet" (for which the Cambridge Arts Soc. awarded him £23,000).

To date, he has won four nationally-recognised awards. He lives with wife Jill Gasgoine and daughter Marie in Heaton, and often contributes to the regional Northern Fringe evenings. Anton Hecht can currently be seen as Timbo Bigwig in the Glasgow Customs House production of 'Ou Est Ma Democracy?'
