Antonio Pappano

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Antonio Pappano (born 30 December 1959 in Epping near London, England) is a British conductor whose family has its origins near Naples, Italy. When he was 13, his family moved to Connecticut, USA. After musical training in piano, composition, and conducting, he became a rehearsal accompanist at the New York City Opera[1] by the age of 21. Antonio Pappano is the youngest conductor to lead London's prestigious opera (and ballet) orchestra and chorus.

As a pianist as well as a conductor, he attracted the attention of fellow pianist and conductor Daniel Barenboim, whose assistant he became at the Bayreuth Festival[2]. He worked in Barcelona and Frankfurt, and served as an assistant to Michael Gielen.[3] In 1990 he was made Musical Director of the Den Norske Opera (Norwegian National Opera) after his conducting debut there in 1987. His role as Sjefdirigent has been taken by Olaf Henzold.

In 1992, at the age of 32, Pappano became music director of La Monnaie, the Belgian Royal Opera House, a post he held until 2002, but meanwhile in 1999, he was named Music Director of the Royal Opera House, Covent Garden effective as of September 2002.

He has also been principal guest conductor of the Israel Philharmonic Orchestra. He has also conducted at the English National Opera (ENO), the Metropolitain Opera (1997) the San Francisco Opera, the Lyric Opera of Chicago and the Berlin State Opera (Berlin Staatsoper).

Recently he has been working his way through the operas which are part of Der Ring des Nibelungen by Richard Wagner, with stars that have included Plácido Domingo, but, with the Covent Garden orchestra and chorus, he has also conducted a new David McVicar staging of Mozart's Le Nozze di Figaro from the harpsichord to widespread acclaim. His reputation as accessible yet charismatic and as someone who can work with even the most prima of donnas is helping him to build a formidable reputation in London and the opera world. His recordings include the following operas:

  • Philippe Boesmans: Wintermärchen
  • Jules Massenet: Manon, Werther
  • Giacomo Puccini: Il Trittico, La Bohème, La Rondine, Tosca
  • Giuseppe Verdi: Don Carlo, Il Trovatore
  • Richard Wagner: Tristan und Isolde

Since 2005, Antonio Pappano became Music Director of the Orchestra dell'Accademia Nazionale di Santa Cecilia in Rome and conducts regularly with the London Symphony Orchestra, the Cleveland Orchestra, the Chicago Symphony Orchestra, the Berlin Philharmonic Orchestra and the Royal Concertgebouw Orchestra.

In December 2006, The Independent reported that Pappano had renewed his contract with the Royal Opera House, Covent Garden[4].
