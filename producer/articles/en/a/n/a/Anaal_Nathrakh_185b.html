Anaal Nathrakh are a British metal band, formed in 1999 which fuses industrial and grindcore elements onto an underlying black metal style. They are signed to Season of Mist.

The name comes from Merlin's Charm of Making in John Boorman's Excalibur (1981). Given Michael Everson's transcription, Anaal Nathrakh means "serpent's breath".

[edit] Description

Anaal Nathrakh are typically described as black metal; however their aesthetics differ: dressing in a casual manner, as opposed to the theatrical corpsepaint and spiked leather clothing traditionally favoured by the black metal subculture.

The band originally started as a raw black metal band, but with the release of Domine Non Es Dignus, they shifted towards a blackened death metal fusion sound, except with a stronger grindcore influence than death metal influence. Through the use of distorted samples, sound effects and programmed drums, much of their work also falls into the industrial metal category, though they use no keyboards in their work. Some Anaal Nathrakh fans, and at times, Anaal Nathrakh, refer to the group's music as "necro metal." On their MySpace page, they describe themselves as black metal/grindcore, and many consider their music to be best described as a fusion of these two.

They do not publish their lyrics, but from the titles and information they have given in interviews, their work would seem to revolve around apocalyptic themes, the nature of mortality, and misanthropy.

The act, as many black metal bands, draws much inspiration from the writings of Friedrich Nietzsche. For example, the song titles "Human, All Too Fucking Human" and "Revaluation of All Values". However, based on what little can be gleaned from interviews and titles, they do seem to differ from most other bands in the style of their interpretations.

[edit] Playing live

Anaal Nathrakh have until recently been a purely studio-based project, the band never looking to take Anaal Nathrakh to the stage. However, following a successful BBC Radio 1 live session with John Peel (as heard on the 2006 re-release of The Codex Necro), they sought to play live again, although only for special one-offs. They have stated they are not to become an ordinary touring band.

Anaal Nathrakh made their live debut at London's Underworld on December 15, 2005, as headliners for Terrorizer Magazine's 'A Cold Night In Hell' Christmas show. This was followed by another at Birmingham Edwards No. 8 on December 16, supported by Narcosis and Ramesses. Supporting them at the London show were The Axis of Perdition, Ted Maul, and Leech Woman.

Anaal Nathrakh were given great reviews for their live performances.

On December 25, 2006, it was announced that Anaal Nathrakh will be performing at the Inferno Festival April 5-7, 2007.

Anaal Nathrakh will be performing on the No Mercy Festival 2007, touring through Europe.
