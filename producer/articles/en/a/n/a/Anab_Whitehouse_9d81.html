Dr. Bill (Anab) Whitehouse was born in Denver, Colorado in 1944. He attended schools in Rumford, Maine and Mattawamkeag, Maine. He received an Honors degree in Social Relations from Harvard University in 1968 and a doctorate in Educational Theory from the University of Toronto.

Anab became a Muslim via the Sufi Path while living in Toronto and was chairperson of the Sufi Study Circle at the University of Toronto for 17 years before returning to the US.

He was a guest speaker at the World Parliament of Religions in Chicago, Illinois in 1990. He has given lectures on the Sufi Path and Islam at numerous universities, and as well has been asked to speak on Islam and the Sufi Path on CBC radio and BBC radio. Anab played an active role on a committee that developed a prime-time program on Islam for the Canadian Multi-Faith Network.

Dr. Whitehouse taught a variety of courses at colleges in Canada and the United States, ranging from Criminology, Philosophy of Law, and introductory courses on Philosophy, Psychology, and Diversity Counseling.

He founded the Interrogative Imperative Institute in 1997, the Spiritual Health Learning Center in 2002, Bilquees Press in 2005, Sufi Amanesis Blogin 2005, and began the Sufi Reflections Podcast in 2006.

Dr. Whitehouse began a counseling practice with a focus on spiritual abuse in 2002 and began the Sufi Spiritual Abuse website in 2002.

Whitehouse's books include Streams to the Ocean, The Chaco Canyon Tapes, The Sufi Lighthouse: Illuminating Spiritual Abuse, Evolution on Trial, Reality without a Name, and Spiritual Stories for the Heart.
