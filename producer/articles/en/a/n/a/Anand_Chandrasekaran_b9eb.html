Anand Chandrasekaran

Anand Chandrasekaran (born 1978 in Bombay, India) co-founded Aeroprise Inc. in 2001. Aeroprise pioneered end user personalization driven mobile computing and is a leading Mobile Service Management software company with customers like Safeway, Harvard University, U.S. Air Force and IBM and partners like BMC Software, Sprint Nextel and BlackBerry.

In 2003, Anand helped launch Aeroprise products at the exclusive DEMOmobile conference where Chris Shipley called it "the missing piece that will drive CIOs' adoption of enterprise mobility strategies." Aeroprise is the only company to be voted "Product of the Year" by customers of BMC Software three years in a row (2004-2006) and has won several other awards. In 2004, Anand was named to the MIT Global Technovators list of innovators of South Asian origin (others on this list include Dr. Vanu Bose of Vanu Inc., Dr. Sandeep Pandey of ASHA and Dr. Kumar Sivarajan of Tejas Networks).

Anand is a member of the advisory board of the Mobile Enterprise Alliance (MEA), an industry-body with over 100 member organizations. In 2001, his team won first place at PlanEDU, a national business plan competition. This led to the founding of Aeroprise (formerly called Kwaish). He graduated from Stanford University with a Masters in Electrical Engineering, and received an undergraduate degree from PSG College of Technology, India. He conducted research during that time at Indian Institute of Science and Raman Research Institute.

He has been featured in CNBC News and in BusinessWeek, Business 2.0, InfoWorld, Economic Times and other leading publications.

Anand's father runs a software company in India, and both his mother and sister are classical dance teachers. Like M. Night Shyamalan and Jay Chandrasekhar, Chandrasekaran is of South-Indian origin. He branched out in 2006 to executive produce CARMA, a film that was recognized for its several pioneering efforts at the cusp of technology and media. His second project was invited to the 2006 Sundance Producers' Conference.
