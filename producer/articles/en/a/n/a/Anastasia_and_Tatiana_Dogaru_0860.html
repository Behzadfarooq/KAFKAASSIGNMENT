Anastasia and Tatiana Dogaru

Anastasia and Tatiana Dogaru (born 2003) are three-year-old craniopagus conjoined twins. They are scheduled to begin the first of several surgeries to separate them at Rainbow Babies and Children's Medical Center in Cleveland, Ohio. The first surgery will likely take place in May 2007.

The twins were born in Rome, Italy to Romanian parents, Alin Dogaru, a Byzantine Catholic priest, and Claudia Dogaru, a nurse. Their mother heard about the successful separation of Egyptian-born twins who were also joined at the head and hoped her children could also be successfully separated. The Dogaru family, who also have a six-year-old daughter Maria, were brought to north Texas by the World Craniofacial Foundation to have Anastasia and Tatiana evaluated for possible separation.

The girls are currently developing normally for their age and speak both Romanian and English. They get around with Anastasia leading the way and Tatiana following. The top of Tatiana's head is attached to the back of Anastasia's. Anastasia, whose kidneys don't function, relies on her sister's weakening kidneys. Anastasia will need a kidney transplant following the separation. Doctors want Tatiana's circulatory system to function separately from Anastasia's before the separation can be completed. The girls also share blood flow to the back of the brain and some brain matter. Doctors estimated the twins have only a 50 percent chance of surviving the surgery. There are also risks of complications, such as brain damage, but the girls also risk eventual death if they remain conjoined. Their parents believe separation will give them their best chance at living a normal life.[1] [2] [3]
