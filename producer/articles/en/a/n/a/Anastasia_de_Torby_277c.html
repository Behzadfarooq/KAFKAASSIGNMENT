Anastasia de Torby

Countess Anastasia de Torby CBE (September 9, 1892 - December 7, 1977) was the elder daughter of Grand Duke Michael Mikhailovich Romanov and his morganatic wife Countess Sophia von Merenberg, later created Countess de Torby in Luxembourg, herself related to the Luxembourg grand ducal house and descended from the Russian poet Alexander Pushkin, as well as his ancestor Abram Petrovich Gannibal, Peter the Great's African protégé. Anastasia de Torby was an older sister of Nadezhda de Torby, wife of the 2nd Marquess of Milford Haven, a descendant of Queen Victoria and maternal uncle to the Duke of Edinburgh.

Her paternal grandparents were Grand Duke Michael Nicolaevich of Russia and his wife Princess Cecily of Baden. Michael was the seventh and last child and the fourth and youngest son of Nicholas I of Russia and his wife Alexandra Fyodorovna, sister of the first German emperor Wilhelm I. Anastasia's mother, the Countess de Torby, was a granddaughter of Alexander Pushkin.

On July 20, 1917 she married British Major-General Sir Harold Augustus Wernher (1893-1973), son of wealthy financier, Sir Julius Wernher, 1st Bt, who had made his fortune in South African diamonds. Known in British social circles as Lady Zia, they had issue, including an elder daughter, Georgina Wernher born in 1919. Georgina's elder daughter Alexandra married James Hamilton, 5th Duke of Abercorn, whilst her youngest Daughter, Natalia, married Gerald Grosvenor, 6th Duke of Westminster.

On the death of her husband's father Sir Julius Wernher, 1st Bt, they inherited Luton Hoo, a magnificent mansion in Bedfordshire. Anastasia Wernher died in London in 1977.
