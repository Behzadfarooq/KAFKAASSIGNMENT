Anatoly Pavlovich Bondarchuck (Russian: Анатолий Павлович Бондарчук) (born May 31, 1940 in Starokonstantinov) is a retired Soviet athlete, who is considered one of the best coaches in the hammer throw. An Olympian in his own right, he won the 1972 Summer Olympics, setting the Olympic Record at 75.50 meters[1] and placed third at the 1976 Summer Olympics. He also set two world records in the hammer throw in 1969: at 74.68 meters and 75.48 meters [2] Being an athlete, Bondarchuk trained at VSS Kolos in Kiev [3]

He has coached a very impresive 23 athletes to the Olympics, including the hammer world record holder Yuri Sedykh. Bondarchuck is currently living and coaching in Kamloops, BC, Canada. [4]He coaches at the local track and field club as a retirement activity.[5] One of his best qualities is that he is very patient with all his athletes.[citation needed] Most people would not think that the former head of the Soviet Union Throws Program would coach children, but he always has a big smile on his face when they come to practice.[citation needed]

Bondarchuk was awarded the Order of the Red Banner of Labour (1976) and the Order of the Badge of Honor (1972) [3]
