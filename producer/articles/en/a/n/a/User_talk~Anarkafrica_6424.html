If you want to talk about the difficulties surrounding being a homosexual in Ghana, the article that probably belongs in is homophobia, I would think, or, if there is (a lot) more content about the issue, maaaybe homosexuality in Ghana or, perhaps more broadly, homosexuality in Africa. The QPOC article should not be used as an umbrella to discuss all issues related to non-whites in the LGBT community. Instead, IMO, the article should focus very specifically on movements for and explicitly within that group. I think OUTFEST and ALLGO are both good in this regard, because they specifically focus on this group of people, and even more, use the terminology in the article heading. Both of those organizations make it appear that there is a growing movement for queer people of color in the United States if not elsewhere. I would suggest that the article focus on that movement, and not lose its way by becoming a sort of index of non-whites who are also gay.
The article should, without becoming a source of advocacy (which may be difficult given your POV), discuss the issues that arise in the "double minority" status this community faces. I know that there have been books published on the subject: that's probably a good place to start. Rather than the article saying "these are people who are both ethnic and sexual minorities", it would probably be more valuable to say "On the difficulties of being both black and gay, Langston Hughes said, 'I am twice separated, by one part of me I cannot hide and another I cannot show.' " (Except with something he, you know, actually said.) Instead of saying "society does not accept queer people of color", write "several books have been published on the status of non-white sexual minorities. One such book, by noted author (whoever), focuses specifically on the difficulties of being Korean and gay, at once a member of a minority that prides itself on inclusivity within the community, but also is generally strongly opposed to homosexuality: 'Growing up I lived a sort of double life, as part of a community that almost seemed like it didn't know what to do with me.' " Obviously I'm just making these quotations up as I go along, but I hope you see my (rambling) point: the best way to write this article is probably going to be to use quotations from verifiable, notable sources. Instead of making direct commentary about being a queer person of color, lay out the facts about movements related to it, books about the subject, etc. Quotations are going to be pretty key here. I hope this made some sense -- please let me know your thoughts on all that. Truly, JDoorjam Talk 19:10, 27 April 2006 (UTC)
