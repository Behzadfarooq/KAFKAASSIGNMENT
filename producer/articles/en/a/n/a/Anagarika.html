Anagarika

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

In Buddhist context, an anagarika is a white-robed student in some modern forms of the Theravada tradition who, for a few months, awaits being considered for Samanera's ordination. The student's time is generally spent helping others with tasks, while adapting to a new life. An anagarika can choose to leave the monastery at any time, without shame.

Etymology:

  • Pāli: anagārika, meaning homeless; used originally for any member of the sangha; in modern times the self-adopted title of Anagarika Dharmapala of Ceylon; later adapted to its current meaning above
