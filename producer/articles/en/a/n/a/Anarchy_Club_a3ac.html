Anarchy Club is a heavy rock / electronica duo from Boston, Massachusetts. Singer/guitarist Keith Smith is a former member of the band C60. Adam Buhler, who plays guitar, bass, and other instruments, is a former member of the band Splashdown.

In the winter of 2004, long time friends Adam Buhler and Keith Smith decided to start a musical project that would eventually become Anarchy Club. Fusing their love for Asian cinema, video games, muscle cars, anime, and heavy rock, the two set to writing songs.

Their debut album, The Way and Its Power mixes everything from death metal riffs and hip hop grooves to Chinese classical and waltz beats, all with a decidedly heavy rock flavor throughout.

Anarchy Club has received a boost in popularity by having their song "Behind the Mask" appear in the recent PlayStation 2 game Guitar Hero as a bonus track. Smith is an employee of Harmonix, the company that developed Guitar Hero and counts Buhler's former bandmate, Kasson Crooker, as its Audio Director.

"Collide" (from their forthcoming release, "A Single Drop of Red") is a bonus track for Guitar Hero II. Of the 24 bonus tracks in the game, GameSpy named "Collide" as one of the five best. [1]
