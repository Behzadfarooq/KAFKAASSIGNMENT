Anam is an album released in 1990 (US: 1992) by the Irish music group Clannad. "Harry's Game" is likely the most well-known song on the album for its multiple uses on television and in movies. "In A Lifetime" is a duet between Bono and Máire Brennan. This album continued a departure from the more traditional sound of their earlier albums. Also it was one of the first to gain international attention and current copies state how "Harry's Game" was in the film Patriot Games. "Úirchill An Chreagáin"is of some interest for being an arrangement of an eighteenth century song by poet Art MacCúmhaigh and it is still been sung in Armagh. It was re-issued in 2005 with the Lazyboy mix bonus track of "Rí Na Cruinne".
