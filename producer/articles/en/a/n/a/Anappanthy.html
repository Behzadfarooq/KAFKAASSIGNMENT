Anappanthy (Anappanthi) is a small town on the banks of Kundoor puzha in the Ayyankunnu Village, Kannur, Kerala. In the early days of settlement by farmers from the central Kerala this small town had a major role in the economy and education of the entire village. Today's Anappanthy Service Cooperative Bank was the first financial institution for the whole village. The bank was started in 1940s as a society with a few members. Today the society had grown in to a full-fledged bank with many branches in the area. In the olden days, the main traffic of bullock carts and a few motorized vehicles used to pass through the town's unpaved road to Vallithode and beyond. Until early 1980s there were no bridges for the many small river or any paved roads in the village. During the monsoon season the village used to be disconnected from the outside world for many days due to the flooded rivers. In those days one could only walk kilometers and catch a ferry to cross the flooded Valapattanam River to contact the outside wold. Mathai Pambackal, Cheriyan Kanjamala, Valiyathottiyil brothers, Kochumala, Kuruvila Asan Pattamana and Kalayathinamkuzhy family,Kulathinkal Kunhutti (to name a few) were the early farming settlers of this small town.

In the year 1956 the first first elementary school was started here with a single teacher and approximately 30 students. Today the school is known as Government L P School. The proposed Hill highway project will pass through this town from Kasaragod to Trivandrum, when it is completed.

Today the village is well connected with paved roads to the main highway. Good schools and colleges are there in the Ayyankunnu Village. Broad band Internet connectivity and Mobile telephone is available in the village. The main economy is still depended on the village's agriculture and no industry has reached the village yet. The major agricultural products are Rubber, Pepper and coconut.
