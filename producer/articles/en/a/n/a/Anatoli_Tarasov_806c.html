Anatoli Tarasov

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Anatoli Vladimirovitch Tarasov (December 10, 1918 - June 23, 1995, both in Moscow) was an ice hockey coach, styled by Encyclopædia Britannica "the father of Russian hockey", who established the Soviet Union as "the dominant force in international competition". [1] He was one of the first Russians to be inducted into the Ice Hockey Hall of Fame.

As the ice hockey coach of the Soviet Union he led the team to 9 straight world championships and 3 Olympic gold medals. He was fired when the team won the silver medal only. Tarasov was known for his ruthless training methods, tough discipline among his players and innovative, instinctive decisions. Many of his methods are continued by his daughter Tatiana Tarasova.
