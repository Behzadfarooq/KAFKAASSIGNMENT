Anatoly Shalyto

Anatoly Abramovich Shalyto (Russian: Анато́лий Абра́мович Шалы́то, May 28 1948, Leningrad, Soviet Union) — professor, doctor of science, head of the Programming Technologies Department in St.-Petersburg State University of Information Technologies, Mechanics and Optics, Russia, the author of Automata-Based Programming paradigm, for supporting which Switch-technology was developed, the initiator of Foundation for Open Project Documentation.

[edit] Biography

In 1965–1971 Anatoly Shalyto was studying at Leningrad Electrotechnical Institute at the Automation and Telemechanics Department.

Since 1971 he is working at scientific production association “Aurora” (Saint Petersburg, Russia), specializing in logical control systems design and programming technologies.

In 1985 he got an academic status of senior researcher on the subject of Elements and Devices of Computer Engineering and Control Systems.

Since 1999 he is a science secretary at “Aurora”.

In 1977 he completed the postgraduate course and presented a Ph.D. thesis on Elements and Devices of Computer Engineering and Control Systems. Candidate of Technical Science (since 1977).

In 1999 presented a doctoral thesis on the same topic. Doctor of Technical Science (since 2000).

In 1974–1991 he worked as a lecturer in the Institute of Postgraduate Education for Specialists in Shipbuilding Industry (Saint Petersburg).

In 1991 he got an academic status of associate professor at Control Computer Systems Department.

Since 1998 he works as a lecturer at the Computer Technologies Department in SPbSU ITMO.

Since 2000 he is a professor at this Department.

In 2001 he got an academic status of professor at this Department.

In 2001 he became the head of Information Technologies Department of SPbSU ITMO.

On 25 of May 2004 he was elected the head of Programming Technologies Department of SPbSU ITMO.

[edit] Main Achievements

In 1991 Anatoly Shalyto introduced Automata-Based Programming — a programming paradigm, which is based on finite automata — and also a technology for automata-based programming called “SWITCH-Technology”. He is also a coauthor of a UniMod tool that supports automata-based programming.

In 2002 he started a new initiative in programming — Foundation for Open Project Documentation.

Anatoly Shalyto took part in many all-Russian and international conferences where he made reports on the problems of hardware and software implementation of logical control algorithms and programming technologies (e.g., Linux Summit (Finland, 2004), Software Engineering Conference in Russia (Moscow, 2005), 4-th International Conference in Central Europe on .NET technologies (Czech Republic, 2006)).

He is the author of plenty of scientific works that were published in Soviet and Russian magazines, such as “Automatics and Telemechanics”, “Automatics and Computer Engineering”, “Proceedings of the Academy of Sciences. Technical Cybernetics”, “Proceedings of the Academy of Sciences. Theory and systems of automatic control”, “Problems of Information Transfer”, “Autometry”, “Programming”.

In 1981 he (in co-authorship) published a book called “Customizable modules for logical control devices”.

In 1996 he became a winner of publishing projects contest, which was held by Russian Fundamental Research Fund, and published a book called “SWITCH-technology. Algorithmization and programming for logical control”. In this book he introduced the concepts of Automata-Based Programming, program observability and state coding.

In 2000 he published a book called “Logical Control. Approaches to hardware and software implementation of algorithms”, in which he summarized all his previous scientific results.

In 2000–2005 he headed a research on the subject of “Technology for automata-based logical control software development”. The research was accomplished in the context of fundamental research program by Education Department of Russian Federation.

In 2002–2003 he headed the work on grant of Russian Fundamental Research Fund for research on the subject of “Automata-based programming technology development”.

In 2003 Anatoly Shalyto became the winner of the contest of research projects on the subject of integral scheme design automation, that was organized by Intel Corporation in Moscow Physicotechnical Institute.

In 2003 and 2005 co-chair seccion "Teoretical Aspects of Multi-Agent Systems" on International Conference on "Integration of Knowledge Intensive Multi-Agent Systems" KIMAS’03. KIMAS’05. USA. IEEE. DARPA.

In 2005 and 2006 member of program committee "IEEE International Conference on Computational Intelligence for Measurement Systems and Applications". CIMSA 2005, CIMSA 2006. Italy. Spain.

In 2005 he became the winner of the contest of projects called “Research and Development in Priority Directions of Science and Technology” for 2002—2006, that was organized by Federal Department of Science and Innovation. The project “Automata-based programming: application and tools” was considered one of the 15 most innovation-challenging and socially important.

Anatoly Shalyto is a member of editorial boards of magazines “Management-information systems” and “Marine radio electronics”. He is also a member of advisory, scientific and technical committees.

In 2003–2005 he published a series of articles devoted to the problems of computer science and education.

Russian Federation President grant for outstanding achievements in technical industry, 2006-2008.
