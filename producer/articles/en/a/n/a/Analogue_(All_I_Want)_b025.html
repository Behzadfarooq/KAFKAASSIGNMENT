The single "Analogue (All I Want)" was the first single released in the United Kingdom by a-ha on 23 January 2006, on 30 December 2005 in Germany and 30 January 2006 in Norway.

[edit] Tracklisting

UK CD1:

  1. "Analogue (All I Want)" (Album Version)
  2. "Case Closed on Silver Shore"

UK CD2:

  1. "Analogue (All I Want)" (Album Version)
  2. "Minor Key Sonata (Analogue)"
  3. "Keeper of the Flame" (Live at Frognerparken)
  4. "Analogue (All I Want)" (Video)

A very limited edition 7" picture disc is available in the UK and features the same two tracks as the 2-track CD single.

"Analogue (All I Want)" was re-recorded and re-written from the first version produced for the album. "Minor Key Sonata" was the original version, and (like the rest of the album) was produced by Martin Terefe and mixed by Flood. Max Martin was brought in to turn "Minor Key Sonata (Analogue)" into a more commercially-friendly product - with a less surreal lyric, and catchier chorus. "Analogue (All I Want)" became a hit in the UK, and is the only track on the album not produced by Terefe.

[edit] Music video

This is the second video made from the album "Analogue" and both videos are recorded in black and white. It was directed by Howard Greenhalgh, who has done over 100 music videos.
