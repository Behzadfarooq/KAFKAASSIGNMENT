Anatoly Kuznetsov

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Anatoly Vasilievich Kuznetsov (18.08.1929, Kyivâ€“13.06.1979, London) was a Ukrainian language Soviet writer who described his experiences in German-occupied Kiev during WWII in his internationally acclaimed novel Babi Yar. The book was originally published in a censored form in 1966 in Ukrainian.

The book was controversial after its publication. It opened up materials which had previously been unknown about the execution of over 100 000 inhabitants of Kiev, a third of whom were Jewish, in a ravine in Western Kiev known as Babi Yar. The original book was thought to have been anti-Nazi Soviet propoganda. Subsequent editions of the work included materials which were detrimental to the Soviet Union.

The English translation of his book included material removed by censors from the original Ukrainian publication. This material was printed in a different typeface. Material which Kuznetsov later added was printed in a third typeface.

This final edition appeared in London after Kuznetsov's defection to the West in 1969 under the nom-de-plume A. Anatoli. In order to smuggle the book out of the Soviet Union, Kuznetsov took pictures of every page of Babi Yar to get it past the Soviet customs in Moscow.

Kuznetzov was murdered in London in 1979 by a shot to the neck with a poisoned ice pellet. It is thought that the KGB were involved in the slaying.

Anatoly Kuznetsov is also the name of an actor, who was the star of White Sun of the Desert, often considered the greatest Soviet film.
