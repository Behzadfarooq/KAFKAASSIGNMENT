Anadyr (Russian: Ана́дырь) is a town in and the administrative centre of Chukotka Autonomous Okrug, the extreme north-eastern region of Russia. It is located at the mouth of the Anadyr River. Situated at 177°30′E, Anadyr is the easternmost town in Russia. It was founded on August 3, 1889 as Novo-Mariinsk, and was renamed to its present name on August 5, 1923. The town status was granted on January 12, 1965.

Population: 11,038 (2002 Census).

[edit] Transportation

The town is an important sea port on the Gulf of Anadyr of the Bering Sea. There is an airport, which serves several major and minor cities in the Russian Far East, has regular connection to Moscow, as well as charter flights to Nome, Alaska on Bering Air airline.

[edit] Famous people

  • Valery Ivanovich Tokarev, a cosmonaut who was raised in Anadyr
  • Roman Abramovich, an oil oligarch and the governor of Chukotka who invested much of his wealth in the town's infrastructure
