[edit] Welcome!

Welcome!

Hi, and welcome to the Biography WikiProject! We're a group of editors working to improve Wikipedia's coverage of biographies.

A few features that you might find helpful:

  • The project has a monthly newsletter; it will normally be delivered as a link, but several other formats are available.

There are a variety of interesting things to do within the project; you're free to participate however much—or little—you like:

  • Starting some new articles? Our article structure tips outlines some things to include.
  • Want to know how good our articles are? The assessment department is working on rating the quality of every biography article in Wikipedia.

If you have any questions, please don't hesitate to ask another fellow member, and we'll be happy to help you. Again, welcome! We look forward to seeing you around! plange 19:15, 2 October 2006 (UTC)

[edit] Welcome to Novels WikiProject

Hi, and welcome to the Novels WikiProject! As you may have guessed, we're a group of editors working to improve Wikipedia's coverage of topics related to fiction books often referred to as "Novels".

A few features that you might find helpful:

  • Our navigation box points to most of the useful pages within the project.
  • The announcement and open task box is updated quite regularly. You can watch it if you're interested; or, you can add it directly to your user page by including {{WikiProject_Novels_announcements}} there.
    While you are updating your userpage, don't forget our userbox {{User WikiProject Novels}}.
  • The project has a monthly newsletter; it will normally be delivered as a link, but other methods are available.

There are a variety of interesting things to do within the project; you're free to participate however much—or little—you like:

  • Starting some new articles? Our article structure guidelines / template outlines some things to include.

If you have any questions, please don't hesitate to ask one of the members, and we'll be happy to help you. Again, welcome! We look forward to seeing you around! :: Kevinalewis : (Talk Page)/(Desk) 08:41, 9 October 2006 (UTC)

[edit] WikiProject Philosophy Query

What is it you are suggesting doing? There have been a few attempts to use the Wiki for undergrad assignments and so on, something that it seems to me it would be quite useful to do. I think that the Wiki offers great potential as a teaching tool, enabling writers to expose their interpretation to criticism in fairly safe conditions. Tell me more. Banno 22:12, 12 October 2006 (UTC)

  • I am co-lecturer for a new compulsory course for Honours' and Masters' students, that may also be attended by some thesis students. We want the course to be both practically and theoretically useful, and to be fun. The students will have to complete 4 pieces of assessment throughout the year, 2 minor and 2 major. My thought was that students could either take on a Wikipedia article as individuals or work as a team to improve one or more entries. Because Wikipedia allows you to keep track of the history of an article, collaboration would not be a barrier to grading individuals' contributions. It would also be great if it leads the students into conversations on and off line about the topic (we would also have a chat room outside of the wikipedia site), and any alterations to the articles from others would only be a help. I know wikipedia disapproves of original research, and that could be in tension with the desire to teach students to think critically. However, the wiki part of the course would be the minor assessment tool, and decent research skills, and exposition of positions and critiques is a vital prerequisite to carrying out your own research. In other words, I really think it could work!

Anarchia 02:05, 13 October 2006 (UTC)
