Anarchy or Anarchists may refer to:

  • Anarchy, a stateless society.
  • Anarchism, the belief that all forms of rulership are undesirable and should be abolished
    • Anarchy (book), L'anarchia, Errico Malatesta's 1891 pamphlet explaining his version of anarchism
    • The Anarchy, the period of civil war and unsettled government in England during the reign (1135–1154) of King Stephen
    • Anarchy in international relations, posits that there is no universal sovereign or worldwide government
    • Anarchy: A Journal of Desire Armed, a North American anarchist magazine
    • Anarchy Magazine, a London anarchist magazine from the 1960s
  • Anomie, a social condition characterized by an absence or diminution of standards or values. See also chaos, nihilism.

Anarchy may also refer to:

  • Anarchy (album), by Busta Rhymes.
  • Anarchy (song), by KMFDM.
  • Anarchy(wrestler) professional wrestler.
  • Anarchy in the U.K., a song by The Sex Pistols.
  • Anarchy Comics, a series of underground comics published between 1978 and 1987.
  • Anarchy Films (adult DVD production co)
  • Anarchy Online, a science fiction MMORPG game.
  • Annarchy, a supporting character in the Penny Arcade webcomic.
  • Captain Anarchy, a song by Anti-Flag.
  • Anarky, a fictional comic book character from DC Comics, created by Alan Grant and Norm Breyfogle.
  • Chaos (Kaos).

Anarchists may also refer to:

  • Anarchists (film), a South Korea action movie, made in 2000.
  • The Anarchists, by the historian James Joll, a relatively brief history of the anarchist movement.
  • "The Anarchists (song)", "Les Anarchistes", a famous song from Léo Ferré.
  • The Anarchist, a fictional character from Marvel Comics, and created by Peter Milligan and Mike Allred.
  • Jewish Anarchists, anarchism in Jewish history and culture.
  • The Anarchist Cookbook
