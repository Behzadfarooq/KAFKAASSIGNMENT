Anakapalle is a town in the Visakhapatnam district of Andhra Pradesh, a state in India.

[edit] History

The town was originally under the rule of the Kalinga Empire, and later came under the rule of the Gajapati, Kakatiya, and Qutb Shahi empires. Around 1450, Appalaraju began to rule the region under the Nawab of Arcot, with Anakapalle as his fortified headquarters. When the British took control of the area in 1803, local leaders were poisoned or hung.

[edit] Temples

  • Appalaraju had a temple built to Kakatambika, a family goddess; later rules would rename the goddess Nookambika, or Nookalamma. The temple exists to this day, now controlled by the Endowments Department, a controversial government bureau that manages many Hindu temples. On “Kottha Amavasya,” the Telugan New Year’s Eve, thousands of people from Andhra Pradesh come to the temple to worship. [1]
  • Anakapalle is also known for the nearby ruins of a Buddhist temple known as Bojjannakonda, or Sankaram. Thousands of old Buddhist monuments can be seen. It is nice place to visit for day.[2]
  • Sathyanarayana Swami temple is situated on the top of a small hill at Kasimkota about 2kM from Anakapalli. It is a pleasant place to visit.
  • Devipuram has a famous temple of Sri Rajarajeshwari Temple is 12 km from this town. This temple has the specialty of being world's largest and only temple in the structure of Sri Chakra.[3]

Most of the population of Anakapalle is Hindu, and thus it has quite a number of Hindu temples. The popular temples are

  • Gowri Parameswara temple
  • Peda Ramaswami temple
  • China Ramaswami temple
  • Venkateswara temple
  • Santoshimatha temple

The major community celebrations are around religious ceremonies. The month of January is particulary festive season for the local population with a sambaram (a community festival) almost one every day for the first 20 days of the month.

[edit] Industries

The chief crop cultivated in the region is sugarcane, and Anakapalle is well known for its jaggery industry. It is the largest source of jaggery for southern India, and the second largest for all of India. Velagapudi Steels also owns a steel mill near Anakapalle. [edit] Geography

Anakapalle is located at 17.68° N 83.02° E[5]. It has an average elevation of 26 metres (85 feet).

[edit] Demographics

As of the 2001 Indian censusGRIndia, Anakapalle had a population of 84,523. Males constitute 50% of the population and females 50%. Anakapalle has an average literacy rate of 67%, higher than the national average of 59.5%, with 54% of the males and 46% of females literate. 10% of the population is under 6 years of age.

[edit] Parliamentary and Assembly Constituency

Anakapalle is a parliamentary and assembly constituency in Andhra Pradesh.

List of Members of Legislative Assembly:

  • 1951 - Koduganti Govinda Rao
  • 1955 - Beesetti Appa Rao
