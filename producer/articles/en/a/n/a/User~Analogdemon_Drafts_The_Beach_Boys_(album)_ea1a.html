The Beach Boys is an album by The Beach Boys, released in 1985. An attempt to modernize the band's sound and bring them into 1980s digital technology, the release produced a top 30 hit in "Getcha Back", and sold better than their last several albums.

In 1983, former leader Brian Wilson had been forcibly returned to the care of Dr. Eugene Landy, who had had success in rehabilitating him in 1976. After detoxing and learning to deal with his mental illness, Wilson was well enough to appear on stage with The Beach Boys that summer. But middle brother Dennis deteriorated further, and drowned that December at the age of thirty-nine. The band allowed themselves a mourning period before instigating work on their next album, the group's first in five years.

Wanting to sound current in a new era of music, they hired Culture Club producer Steve Levine, who took them into the world of drum machines, synthesizers, sampling, and hi-tech recording technology. All the members – Brian Wilson, Carl Wilson, Mike Love, Bruce Johnston, and Al Jardine – took an active role in the project, writing several new songs for it, with Stevie Wonder and Culture Club each donating a song.

After several months of intermittent activity, The Beach Boys was complete and was released in June 1985. Although "Getcha Back" (composed by Love and his new musical partner, former Byrds producer Terry Melcher), was a hit, the album only reached #52 in the U.S. The Beach Boys was technically their biggest album since their 1976 U.S. top-ten 15 Big Ones, but that wasn't enough for CBS Records (now Sony Music). The Beach Boys' contract expired, and for the first time in years, although their live shows were still popular, they were without a record deal.
