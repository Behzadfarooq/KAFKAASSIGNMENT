Anagrams of Desire is an academic text book about Angela Carter's dramatic writings. Written by Charlotte Crofts and published by Manchester University Press in 2003, the full title is Anagrams of Desire: Angela Carter's Writing for Radio, Film and Television.

The book is divided into three sections, one on Radio (examining Carter's five radio plays), one on Film (exploring her two film adaptations, The Company of Wolves (1984) and The Magic Toyshop (1987)) and one on Television (discussing the critically neglected television documentary The Holy Family Album (1991) and the BBC2 Omnibus documentary about Carter Angela Carter's Curious Room (1992).

Each section is contextualised with an introductory chapter exploring Carter's engagement with each specific medium and outlining the theoretical underpinning of the research. The chapter on radio examines Carter's enjoyment of writing for the "invisible" medium of radio, which offered her a kind of "three-dimensional storytelling". The chapter on film draws on Carter's extensive film journalism, and includes an in-depth analysis of an early Carter poem, 'The Unicorn' (1966) in which she explores themes of gender and the gaze which to become central to her later work. The chapter on television suggests that Carter decried the medium of British Television Drama as "that dreadful spectacle of painted loons", yet got into trouble over her own irreverent television documentary The Holy Family Album.

The book concludes with a brief look at Carter's unrealised dramatic writings, a libretto of Virginia Woolf's Orlando, a stage adaptation of Frank Wedekind's Lulu and an unproduced screenplay on the Parker-Hulme New Zealand murders which influenced Peter Jackson's film Heavenly Creatures, entitled The Christchurch Murders.

The book is based on correspondence and interviews with producers and directors of Carter's work in media, including Glynn Dearman (director of her radio plays), David Wheatley (director of The Magic Toyshop), John Ellis and JoAnn Kaplan (producer and director respectively of The Holy Family Album) and Kim Evans (director of Angela Carter's Curious Room).

According to the publisher, Manchester University Press:

"Angela Carter is best known for her novels, short fiction and journalism, but she also produced a substantial body of writing for media other than the printed page. Despite increasing academic interest in Carter's work, these dramatic writings have largely been ignored. In this book, Charlotte Crofts redresses this lack of critical attention by examining Carter's dramatic writings together for the first time (including two unpublished works), giving them a more central position in the Carter canon".
