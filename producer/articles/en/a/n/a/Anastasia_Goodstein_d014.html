Anastasia Goodstein

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Anastasia Goodstein is the publisher of Ypulse, a Forbes magazine Best of the Web blog. Ypulse was launched in May 2004 to provide daily news and commentary about Generation Y for media and marketing professionals. Ypulse is also intended as a resource for people such as counselors and teachers who are interested in gaining insight into youth culture. Ypulse features teen news, commentary, marketing buzz, features about youth media, and more. Ms. Goodstein is also the author of Totally Wired: What Teens and Tweens Are Really Doing Online (St.Martin’s Press), which will be available March 20, 2007.

Anastasia graduated from Antioch College, with a B.A. in Journalism/Women's Studies in 1995. After graduating, she began her youth-media career at Teen Voices magazine, which was written by and for teen girls. Her interest in working with teens spurred her to do three years of extensive fundraising for Women Express, the organization that publishes Teen Voices. She even raised money to pay her own salary. Some of her fundraising projects included creating an annual event called Rhythm & Voices that combined readings from up-and-coming literary stars with live jazz.

After leaving the magazine she continued her education at the Medill School of Journalism at Northwestern University where she got an MSJ in 1999. Interestingly, she was one of the first three students to concentrate in New Media at Medill. After graduating from Medill, she moved to New York City to work at About.com and for Oxygen Media as an editorial Web producer. She left New York for Silicon Valley almost one year later to take a position as a managing editor at Kibu, which was a website for teen girls. After Kibu became a casualty of the dot.com bubble burst, she went to work for AOL Web Properties as a senior editor, and then helped launch KeepMedia, a premium content service, that delivered current and archived articles from 150+ popular magazines and newspapers on one Website. Anastasia’s last position before deciding to work on Ypulse full-time was at Current TV, a television network started in part by Al Gore, as a director in the Online Studio group.

She has been working on Ypulse full-time since September of 2006, and has been quoted by numerous websites, magazines, and newspapers on teen related matters. She has also written a number of articles about Generation Y for such publications as The Huffington Post, and BusinessWeek. Anastasia’s Ypulse blog chronicles the areas where marketing and popular teen culture overlap.

As is evident by examination of the history of this Wikipedia entry, Ms. Goodstein herself has made a number of edits to this entry over the past several months. This is generally discouraged by the Wikipedia rules and casts a shadow of doubt over the veracity of the entire entry, particularly those parts of the entry that trumpet her accomplishments without any reference to unbiased external sources.
