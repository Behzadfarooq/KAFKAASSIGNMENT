Anaphalis javanica or Javanese Edelweiss is a flowering plant species endemic to Indonesia. Found mostly in mountainious regions of the Greater Sunda Islands, a mature plant can reach eight metres in height. A bird species, the Sunda Whistling Thrush (Myophonus glaucinus), nests in the plants branches.

[edit] Threat

Known as bunga abadi in Indonesian, literally means eternal flower, this plant is popular among tourists. Dried flowers are often sold as souvenirs. This could lead to the destruction of the wild grown species. In the Bromo-Tengger region in East Java this plant is considered extinct. [1] This species is constantly decreasing in number and is currently protected in Gunung Gede Pangrango National Park.
