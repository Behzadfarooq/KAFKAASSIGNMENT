Anatoliy Izrailevich Shapiro (Russian: Анатолий Израилевич Шапиро) is a Russian-American artist.

He established the Shapiro's Art Studio in 1972 after graduating from the Leningrad (now St. Petersburg) Institute of Art where he had earned his Master's Degree in Art. In 1976 he accepted the position of Diorama and Mural designer at the Decorative and Fine Art Trust in Leningrad. In 1985 joined the St. Petersburg Artists' Union. After emigrating to the United States in 1992, he produced dioramas and murals for the "Fantasy of Flight" attraction in Polk City, Florida; the W. Chrysler Museum in Detroit, Michigan; Children's Science Museum in Phoenix, Arizona, American Wilderness Experience in Ontario, CA; National Civil War Museum of Medicine, Frederick, Maryland; and many others. Now Shapiro's Art Studio works in areas such as mural and diorama, portraiture, scenery painting, and set design.
