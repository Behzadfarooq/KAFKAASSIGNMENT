[edit] Health risks

From a health standpoint, anal-oral contact may carry serious risks because of the presence of bacteria, viruses or parasites on or in the anus or rectum. Applying the mouth to the genitals immediately after applying it to the anus can inadvertently introduce the bacterium Escherichia coli ("E. coli") into the urethra, causing a urinary tract infection. Unprotected anal-oral contact also involves the risk of contracting sexually transmitted diseases, such as genital herpes or intestinal parasites.
