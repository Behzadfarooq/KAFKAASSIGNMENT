An article with 2 or more possible names?!

I wrote an article on the village of Zweitina. The name of the village however, can be written in other forms. It can be written as 'Zuweitina' or 'Zouweitina' and there are a few other possibilites too. The article was named Zweitina. Now, how do I make it possible for searchs on the other possibilites redirect the user to, in this case, 'Zweitina', the hypothetically correct page? Thanks in advance. Anas Salloum 01:05, 15 October 2006 (UTC)

Hi and welcome to Wikipedia. I created the two redirections you mentionned (check the links I added on your text). Usually, you create an other article with the alternative spelling and you just click the #R icon on the toolbar and put the name of the main article. Here for example, I wrote #REDIRECT [[Zweitina]] as the article text. I hope this was useful, don't hesitate to put the helpme tag or ask me if you have any other question. Happy editing! -- lucasbfr talk 00:48, 15 October 2006 (UTC)

Arabnet, Arabcircle, Stars.net, Dreamnet

Hi Anas, Thanks for your note. As I said in the edit summary, these articles did not meet Wikipedia's "assertion of notability" requirement. Under Wp:csd#Articles, the article must at least assert the companies are somehow notable, because this is an encyclopedia. Do they serve 4 million customers a day? Was one of these companies the first ISP in Saudi Arabia? Are they known for any controversies? Have they received any awards in the industry? Are they owned by someone famous? Do they have any claim to fame? If so, say so in the article, including a reference. Without some bare assertion of notability, there is no point in keeping such articles, as Wikipedia is not a directory of ISPs. I do not object to recreation of the articles, as long as their notability is included in the article. The articles as they were written included no claim to notability. Best wishes, Firsfron of Ronchester 16:32, 26 October 2006 (UTC)

Good luck and happy editing, Anas. :) Firsfron of Ronchester 16:46, 26 October 2006 (UTC)
