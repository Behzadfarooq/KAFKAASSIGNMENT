[edit] Cleanup

I've started the process of cleanup by:

  • formatting most dates to Wiki style, and linking them
  • fixing some paragraph spacing and quote formatting
  • and - probably most controversially - removing a lot of quotations, as well as a couple of other trivia sections ("Poem by his son", "Favorties" [sic]). This sort of trivia might belong on Wikiquote, but it bulks up the Wikipedia article unnecessarily.

The article still needs a lot of work; in particular, the biography reads more like a list of anecdotes. I know very little about Bakshi, though, so I can't easily determine what is significant and what isn't (although I can probably make reasonable guesses, in most cases). More importantly, though, this article needs sources! Right now, there are zero sources cited in the entire article. This is unacceptable; Wikipedia requires that the information presented be verifiable. Again, as I know little about Bakshi, I can't easily find sources for the statements made here. Someone with more knowledge than I is going to have to find some. Zetawoof(ζ) 07:46, 4 July 2006 (UTC)

07/08/06: Added an "Anecdotes" section and moved a lot of anecdotal stuff (unsourced, to boot) to that section. Removed some personal (unsubstantiated) stuff that really belongs to a full-fledged biography in book form. Added a "Quotes" section to group together all the (unsourced) quotes.

There isn't a consensus on his year of birth. While I haven't come across 1920 as a candidate, opinion seems to be divided between 1928 and 1930.

  • 'Screen' obituary citing 1930
  • 'The Pioneer' cites 1928
  • 'The Indian Express' chooses the "1920's"
  • 'The Music Magazine' goes with 1928
  • Pakistan's 'Dawn' chooses 1930

--Navneeth 03:15, 9 July 2006 (UTC)
