Anhangüera, Goiás

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

This article is about the town Anhanguera. For the pterosaur genus, see Anhanguera (pterosaur).

Anhanguera is a small town and municipality in south Goiás state, Brazil. In 2005 the population was 911 and the total area of the municipality was 55,0 km², making it the smallest municipality in the state of Goiás both in population and area.

  • Elevation: 561 meters
  • Became a city: 1953
  • Postal code: 75770-000

Anhanguera is part of the Catalão Microregion and is located close to the Paranaíba River, which forms the boundary between the states of Goiás and Minas Gerais. It is between two large reservoirs--Lago Itumbiara and Lago Emborcação. Poor highway connections are made with the important cities of Araguari, in the south, and Catalão in a northeast direction. The municipality is completely surrounded by Cumari in the north.

The distance to the state capital, Goiânia, is 316 km. Highway connections are made by BR-153 / Bela Vista de Goiás / Piracanjuba / GO-217 / GO-139 / Caldas Novas / Corumbaíba / GO-210 / Goiandira / GO-305 / Cumari. [1]

The town is located near the Embarcação de Furnas reservoir. The waters are ideal for tourists who seek rest and recreation. On long holidays there are aquatic sports like sailing and jet-ski.

Economy

The economy is based on cattle raising, agriculture, services, and public service. There were 4,300 head of cattle in 2003. The main agricultural products were rice (8 hectares planted area), sugarcane (10 hectares), manioc (10 hectares), and corn (10 hectares).

  • Gross Domestic Product: 3.2 million Reais in 2002, which was 0.01% of the state GDP of 31.298 billion Reais. Activities employing the population in 2003 were commerce with 12 units and public administration employing 157 persons.
  • Gross Domestic Product per capita: 3,578 Reais in 2002, which was well below the state average of 5,291 Reais. [2]
