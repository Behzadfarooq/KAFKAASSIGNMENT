[edit] Neve Campbell

I am new to Wikipedia and I seem to have a problem. You may recall discussing this article earlier today.

I understand that you are an administrator at Wikipedia, so perhaps you can help. Please refer to the discussion page for all of the details; including my deleted comments therein that I am unable to restore.

Thanks, --Lance 08:11, 7 October 2006 (UTC)

I read your message to me at "my talk," and I'm not sure where to reply. So I came back here to reply.

I was astonished to read your suggestion that I should "apologize" to the user that had clearly made several hyperbolic statements about Judaism, which evidently appear to be acceptable to you. As a Jew, I consider that this user's comments were both hostile and antisemitic.

The whole tenor and tone of this user's comments were hostile and confrontational. So it is reasonable to ask:

Were this user's hostile and hyperbolic comments on the "discussion" page, that at best can be described as insensitive, acceptable to you? And if so, why?

And, by the way, I am a Canadian Jew, and it just struck me as suspicious that Neve Campbell, who receives a lot of press coverage in Canada, has never claimed Jewish ancestry in Canada (where such an identity doesn't confer any "advantage" as it does in America). But, in Long Island, N.Y., perhaps the most Jewish place in the world outside of Israel, Neve Campbell claims Jewish descent. This explained my interest in the article.

The user in question was just ready to by-pass this whole issue as not controversial, when it is indeed controversial. Since when does a single self-serving source represent an acceptable reference for an encyclopedia? --Lance 21:23, 7 October 2006 (UTC)

I don't know about anyone else, but I would personally view Lance's comment "has never claimed Jewish ancestry in Canada (where such an identity doesn't confer any "advantage" as it does in America" as a slightly offensive comment, as I would "Catholic churches in particular, were viewed as creepy places". What it comes down to is that we go by what sources say; any of our own thoughts or opinions on the subject matter violate WP:NOR and WP:V unless they have also been expressed by reputable sources specifically on the subject Mad Jack 22:05, 7 October 2006 (UTC)
