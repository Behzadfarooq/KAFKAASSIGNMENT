AEC Regent II

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

You have new messages (last change).

The AEC Regent II despite officially being a new type was very similar to the 1929 Regent. The Regent IIs were all documented as being new with the A173 (also known as the 7.7-litre) engine and a four speed sliding mesh gearbox. The only vehicles that were not standard was the 100 purchased by B.M.M.O. (Birmingham & Midland Motor Omnibus Company), they were classified as O661/20 as the front had to be redesigned so they could carry similar bonnets and radiator grills as B.M.M.O. had designed for their own double deckers.

[edit] Operators

The only other Regent IIs to differ from standard were Dundee Corporation's fleet of Ex-London Transport STLs all of which carried Weymann bodies. Dundee changed the sliding mesh gearbox for the pre-selective version - a move which may have been expected from a concern like London Transport (who favoured pre-select vehicles) but not of a corporation buying vehicles second hand. Other operators who bought Regent IIs new included Lowestoft Corporation, Mansfield and District, Oxford City Transport, Ebor Bus Co of Mansfield. The twenty from London purchased by Norths, a dealer and were sold to a variety of operators such as Grimsby-Cleethorpes Transport and Dundee Corporation.

[edit] Survivors

Out of almost 700 produced between 1945 and 1947, only nine survive. Two of those are derelict in America and one in England has been converted into a towing vehicle. Now the only place you can have a ride on a Regent II is at the East Anglia Transport Museum near Lowestoft.

[edit] Replacement

AEC Regent II was superseded by the next generation of Regent double-decker, AEC Regent III and AEC Regent III RT.
