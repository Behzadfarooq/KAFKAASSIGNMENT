Ringette is a non-contact team sport played on ice. Originally designed to be a unique winter team sport for girls and an alternative to hockey, ringette has evolved into a fast-paced, exciting sport that combines the speed of hockey with the strategy of basketball. It is played on a rink, each team having five players plus a goaltender actively playing at the same time. The first game, played in Espanola, Ontario, Canada, was nothing like the sport of today.

[edit] History

Ringette was created in 1963 by the late Sam Jacks. As the Parks and Recreation Director for the city of North Bay, Ontario, Canada, he saw the need for a team ice sport for girls and women in the area. Working on this premise and utilizing existing recreational facilities, Sam presented the idea of ringette to the Northern Ontario Directors of Municipal Recreation Association in 1963. Red McCarthy, at the behest of the Association, was the first to take the rules from the paper to the ice. The first game was played by a high school women's hockey team in Espanola, Ontario, Canada. The original rules were experimented with in several northern Ontario communities, and can be found at [Ringette Canada].

The 1970s saw much greater attention to opportunities to develop competitive skills. The sport spread throughout Canada and was beginning to be played in the United States and in Finland.

The 1980s saw ringette being introduced in a number of countries, including Russia, Sweden, Australia and New Zealand. In 1986, the International Ringette Federation was formed, with the goal of introducing both recreational and competitive ringette in new countries and in promoting a World Ringette Championship Tournament.

The first championship tournament was held in Canada in 1990. The championship tournament is held every two years, with the site rotated amongst member nations. So far, there have been 6 World Ringette Championships in 1990, 1992, 1994, 1996, 2000, 2002 and an International Summit Series in 1998. Since its creation, ringette has grown internationally and is being played in Canada, Finland, Estonia, Sweden, France, and the United States. The sport has also been introduced to Australia, Japan, Czechoslovakia and Switzerland.

[edit] Rules of the Game

[edit] Overview of the Rules

Although often compared with ice hockey, there are few similarities. Ringette is played on the same ice surface as hockey, much of the same equipment is worn as hockey, and the players also move by skating. However, the nature of the game resembles football (soccer) or basketball much more than it does ice hockey. Two teams of six players (five skaters plus one goaltender) compete to put a rubber ring into the opposing team's goal, thereby scoring a goal. The team that scores the most goals by the end of the game is the winner. Players control the ring with a long stick which, unlike the stick used in hockey, is completely straight, not having a blade on the end. The goal is protected by the goaltender, who has special equipment and privlieges to that end. Play is started with a free pass or a goalkeeper ring.
