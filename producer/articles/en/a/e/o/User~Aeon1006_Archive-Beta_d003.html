Aeon1006, As you may have discovered while researching this matter, the crux of the issue is whether or not amateur championships are considered "majors." As he has sourced, in the era of Bobby Jones, the four majors were the U.S. Open, the Amateur U.S. Open, The British Open, and the Amatuer British Open. The Contemporary majors are the U.S. Open, The British Open, The Masters, and the PGA Championship. This is not disputed by either of us. However when attempting to compare the two, it is standard practice to either count all amateur championships, or none. It is not standard practice to count championships for some people, but not for others. You cannot do that because the matter of when the amateur championships ceased to be majors is a matter of opinion. Therefore there are two standard ways to list "most majors won." Either including the amateur championships, or not. For example, see: http://golf.about.com/cs/historyofgolf/a/menmajorwins.htm

As you can see, on neither of these lists is Bobby Jones listed with 13 wins, while Tiger Woods is listed with 11 wins. It's important to note that the article in question here is a statistical chart, similar to the ones linked here. The first chart is the one most commonly referenced, as it is more applicable to todays standards, however both are correct. Neither are what is currently displayed.

Another chart depicting the same information (however it is outdated) http://www.pgatour.com/story/7871150

Articles which reference the first chart.

   Washington Post: http://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-dyn/content/article/2006/07/23/AR2006072300284.html?nav=emailpage

   CNN: http://sportsillustrated.cnn.com/2006/golf/specials/british_open/2006/07/23/sunday.ap/index.html

   ESPN: http://sports.espn.go.com/golf/britishopen06/news/story?id=2527586

   Sportsline: http://sportsline.com/golf/story/9568097 Aktornado 20:57, 8 August 2006 (UTC)
