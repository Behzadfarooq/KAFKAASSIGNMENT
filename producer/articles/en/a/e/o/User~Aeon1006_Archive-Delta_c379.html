[edit] Message received

Hello, I received your message about your advocate status for User:Iantresman. --ScienceApologist 01:34, 28 August 2006 (UTC)

Ian is a good contributor who has a slight problem in that he views Wikipedia as a forum that can aid in the promulgation and visibility of ideas opposed to the scientific mainstream (see What Wikipedia is not). While Wikipedia certainly should have articles that describe ideas which are outside of the scientific mainstream as per Wikipedia's Neutral Point-of-View policy, Ian takes this as an opportunity to insert fringe perspectives as fact into Wikipedia. Specifically, Ian advocates for compromising the factual accuracy and editorial integrity of articles towards a skewed perspective where non-standard cosmologies and catastrophism idealizations are described as notable controversies in fields where they generally ignored or dismissed outright. Ian has a tendency to hide behind a few policies (including verifiability) claiming that since the "standard is verifiability and not truth" that articles should conform to his perspective rather than one that doesn't compromise the accuracy and integrity of the subject material at hand. This has resulted in the past with protracted circular discussions that have left some contributors, including myself, disgusted with the utter tendentious nature of the arguments. --ScienceApologist 11:02, 28 August 2006 (UTC)
