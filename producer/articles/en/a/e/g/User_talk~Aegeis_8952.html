[edit] homeopathy

Hi and welcome to wikipedia and homeopathy. It seems like you have been editing a lot of material in the homeopathy page. Please note that the page is tagged with the Neutral Point of View tag. This means that you should consult people in the discussion page before you go making major edits. The page edits you have made are have strong point of view (POV) and so we have reverted your edits. Please join the discussion before making anther major edit, as you do seem to know plenty on homeopathy and can contribute a lot. PhatRita 13:25, 7 August 2005 (UTC)

Aegis - I agree with your POV about homeopathy, but feel that your contributions, while factual (from our shared POV) are not well written or persausive. Please try to contribute to the article usefully, rather than persisting with aggressively pushing a POV that will be reverted, no matter what its merit may be -82.33.53.68 23:22, 19 September 2005 (UTC)

[edit] Please sign talk page comments

Just a note -- it's conventional here to sign comments on talk pages. It makes it easier for people to follow discussions. On Talk:Homeopathy and elsewhere, you can sign your old comments by marking them at the end with --~~~, and you can sign new comments with --~~~~. The difference between the two is that the former doesn't add today's date. Thanks! --FOo 06:28, 13 September 2005 (UTC)

[edit] Homeopathy - please make edits separately

Yo. I've put in more than my two cents on Talk:Homeopathy -- I think you clearly have some useful information to contribute. However, the way you have been contributing it makes it difficult for others to discuss and integrate with your changes.

Please consider making piecemeal changes to specific sections of the article, and including meaningful edit summaries -- rather than simply replacing broad swaths of the existing text. Thank you! --FOo 22:15, 25 September 2005 (UTC)

Aegeis, we seem to be engaging in a revert war. Once again, I ask you to make your edits one paragraph or sentence at a time. Every day people make additions and corrections to the article. When you revert the whole thing back to your old version from a month or two ago, all these contributions are lost. I'm asking you to engage in a dialog about the article content. If we can't settle this in a collegial manner, the issue will have to escalate to Wikipedia:Requests for comment and Wikipedia:Requests for mediation. --Lee Hunter 20:54, 26 September 2005 (UTC)
