Aes grave (heavy bronze) is a numismatical term indicating bronze cast coins used in Central Italy during IV and III century BC from Italic populations.

The value was indicated by signs: I for the as, S for semis and pellets for unciae.

Standard weights for the as were 272, 327 or 341 gramms, depending from issuing auctority.

Roman main cast coins had this marks and images:

Image value mark
Ianus As I
Saturn Semis S
Minerva Triens four pellets
Hercules Quadrans three pellets
Mercury Sextans two pellets
Bellona Uncia one pellet

[edit] Issuing cities

Main series were from Rome, Ariminum (Rimini), Iguvium (Gubbio), Tuder (Todi), Ausculum (Ascoli Piceno), Firmum (Fermo), Hatria - Hadria (Atri), Luceria (Lucera), and Latins. Other series have unknown provenience.
