[edit] Rewrite due to spoiler issues?

The problem: Aelita's parentage is a major spoiler, on a par with Luke's in Star Wars. At the very least it seems a bad idea to have "Aelita Hopper" as the article title, at least until the third season is well underway. I propose the following:

  • Rename the article back to Aelita (Code Lyoko)
  • Isolate all reference to Aelita's origins to one section of the article, protected with a spoiler warning

Any thoughts? Haikupoet 02:35, 4 January 2006 (UTC)

  • Done and done, though the History section still has references to it since it's pertinant information in that case. Someguy0830 02:56, 4 January 2006 (UTC)
  • Now to fix all the adjusted Wikilinks. Now that's done, too. Someguy0830 07:29, 4 January 2006 (UTC)
  • On a side note I prefer Aelita (Code Lyoko) considering the characters history. Aelita had no last name for most of season 1, Was named Lyoko by Yumi when talking to her parents, Adopted the name Stones upon enroling in school in season 2 and was revealed to have been named Hopper all along. Calling her Aelita and forgoing the last name seems simpleist to me, and is less likly to confuse those unfamiliar with the show.
  • Already taken care of. Only this article mentions her real last name. The others go by her first alone. - Someguy0830 04:31, 12 January 2006 (UTC)
  • I'm sure this probably doesn't matter much, but all three names (Aelita Lyoko, Aelita Hopper, and Aelita Stones) redirect to this page. Dunno how much this does or doesn't affect the spoiler things, because all three are accepted names for her that have been used in the show, and not in the first five episodes.

[edit] Mentions to classic rock

This show has more mentions to classic rock than any other French show I've seen, especially Aelita.

One, William Dunbar is either named after the house of Dunbar or the Journey drummer Aynsley Dunbar. Two, Aelita has a fake last name of Stones, "like the rock group". She may be talking about the Rolling Stones or Stone Temple Pilots, an alt-rock band. Three, in the pilot Garage Kids, Lyoko's named Xanadu, a word that means "a world without danger", but is also a Rush song written by Geddy Lee.

The Xanadu reference may gotten the writers of the show insert some injokes about rock groups, especially Aelita's name.
