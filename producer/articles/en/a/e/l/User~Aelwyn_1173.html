Italian, male, born on 1 March. Now student of what used to be Natural Sciences at the University of Padua (in the Italian tradition Natural Sciences is a synonym of Natural History). Due to my studies and my personal interest I have some knowledge about Zoology and Botany, which I actually prefer.

[edit] Computer

Interested in Free software and Open Source, I use SuSE Linux since three years. Not having studied computer science at school at any level and not spending most of my life in front of a screen, I am the living evidence that Linux is now easy enough for everyone and not only for geeks.

[edit] Languages and politics

I'm also interested in languages, although unfortunately I know few and I'm basically unable to write or speak proper English (someone said: [my English is] understandable, although some awkward word choices). A conlanger since the age of 12, I've been working for 6 years on Nawrean, a personal language. Active in trying to preserve from extinction the venetian language, one of my mother tongues (with Italian), but not sharing the political views of most people who defend their traditions here in northern Italy. In fact I feel Italian and I'm strongly europeanist. This is why I keep trying to write and speak British English and to cultivate a British pronunciation (this one is so difficult having to watch American films to hear some of this nowadays-so-popular language).

[edit] Wikipedia

/Sandbox

I am currently more or less active in en.wikipedia, it.wikipedia, vec.wikipedia and commons.

At present I'm editing some plant family articles.

  • Cannabaceae, Geraniaceae, Ranunculaceae = Almost done
  • Urticaceae = Work in progress
  • Polygonaceae = Next victim
  • Cyperaceae, Violaceae, Salicaceae = Will do these
  • Oxalidaceae = Possibly
  • Fabaceae, Malvaceae = Would like to do it, but it's a difficult topic, I need help and encouragement

This is the general format I use:

  • General part
  • Introduction, most famous plants, etymology
  • Number of genera and species (according to the Royal Botanical Gardens of Kew)
  • Geographical distribution
  • Taxonomy part
  • Position of the family according to APG and older systems
  • Possible subdivisions of the family (APG/non-APG)
  • Description
  • Plant habit
  • Stem, leaves, hairs
  • Flowers (K, C, A, G, Inflorescence)
  • Pollination
  • Fruit and seed
  • Seed dispersal
  • Other
  • Cultivation and uses (
  • Image gallery (priority to useful pictures)
  • Reference

As a hobby I collect plants to put in my herbarium. I plan to upload some pictures of my specimena for species where nothing better than a dried plant photo is available. Also interested in cooking, gardening, playing cards. I really love cards! I also have a small mineral collection.
