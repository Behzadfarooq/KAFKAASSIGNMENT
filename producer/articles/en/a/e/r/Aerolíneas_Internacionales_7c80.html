Aerolíneas Internacionales was born on July, 1993 by Ing. Luis Rodriguez Dorantes and his brother Ing. Alfredo Rodriguez Dorantes, making the airline the most successful with flights to all the major cities in Mexico. They offered the best rates for national flights, making air travel accessible to all citizens, while maintaining high expectations in the area of customer service.

According to the Dirección General de Aeronáutica Civil DGAC, Aerolineas Internacionales is an airline that ceased operations on June 13, 2003, to upgrade the aircraft and restructure the airline. Before restructuring, the airline operated flights from Cuernavaca to Acapulco, Tijuana, Aguascalientes, Hermosillo ,Cancún , Reynosa , Ciudad Juárez ,Veracruz , Monterrey , Culiacan , Morelia , Guadalajara and Mexico City. The airline still had slots at Mexico City and Cuernavaca's airports.

After extensive negotiations with Raul Julia-Levy and his family, Ing. Luis Rodriguez Dorantes sold the airline to the Levy family on December 5, 2006. Ing. Luis Rodriguez Dorantes has maintained is position of the president of the airline. Ing. Alfredo Rodriguez Dorantes holds the position of Chief Enterprise Officer and Hector Rodriguez Dorantes holds the position of the chief financial officer. Hollywood actor and producer Raul Julia-Levy has been appointed by his family as the vice president of the airline.

The new fleet consists of 16 Boeing 737-400 for national and U.S. flights and seven 747-400 for the European and Asian flights.[citation needed]

The airline plans to resume operations in February, 2007.
