Aero Vodochody (commonly referred to as Aero, (Vodochody is location) is a Czech (and Czechoslovak) aircraft company notable for producing the L-29 Delfin, L-39 Albatros and the L-159 Alca.

After the fall of socialism in Czechoslovakia (1989) and in the rest of Eastern Europe, the company lost a major portion of its main market in jet trainers. The sales of military aircraft declined in the early 1990s in Eastern Europe as well as in the NATO countries where the entry of a new producer was obviously unwanted. Aero was controlled for several years by Boeing but this did not help it to compete on international market at all, as Boeing has been, through the acquired McDonnell Douglas Co., involved in the manufacture of the competing BAe Hawk (the T-45) for the US Navy. Also the past company management concluded a very strange contract with the United Technologies/Sikorsky for assembly/manufacture of the S-76 Spirit helicopter. According to the data published in the Czech daily press, the Aero was losing 21 million Czech crowns on each manufactured helicopter, until the contract was modified after Boeing was bought out for one crown by the Czech government.

At the end of October 2006 Aero Vodochody was privatized once again. A Czech-Slovak investment group Penta bought it for roughly 3 billion CZK. The new owner vows to bring Aero back among the world's leading aircraft manufacturers.

Aero is also likely to upgrade its runway in Vodochody near Prague to a new international airport that would serve mainly the low-cost air-carriers and charter flights heading to Prague.
