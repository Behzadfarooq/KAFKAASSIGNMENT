Aerotaxi

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Aerotaxi is a charter airline based at Cayo Largo and Varadero, state-owned Aerotaxi operates basic services within Cuba and some Caribbean charters.

[edit] Fleet

Aerotaxi's fleets consists of

    • Douglas DC-3
    • LET L-410
    • Antonov AN-2
    • Cessna 337

[edit] Services

  • Aerotaxi operate flights to touristy places like Cayo Largo, Cayo Coco, Varadero, Trinidad, Cienfuegos, Nueva Gerona and as well to Santiago de Cuba. This is one of the very few airlines around the world that still flies the venerable Douglas DC-3.

[edit] Accidents and Incidents

  • On 14 March 2002 an Aerotaxi Antonov AN-2 with 2 crew and 14 passengers crashed with all on board killed. The aircraft was on its way from Cienfuegos Jaime González Airport (CFG) to [[Cayo Coco Airport (CCC). While flying at an altitude of 3000 feet the top left wing snapped off. The aircraft entered a spin and crashed.
  • On 6 December 2002 an Aerotaxi Embraer EMB 110 Bandeirante on a flight from Holguín - Frank Pais Airport (HOG) to Havana - José Martí International Airport (HAV) crash-landed short of the runway while approaching in heavy rain. All 10 occupants on board survived.
  • On 19 March 2003 an Aerotaxi Douglas DC-3 enroute from Nueva Gerona - Rafael Cabrera Airport (GER) to Havana - José Martí International Airport (HAV) was highjacked by six men using kitchen knives, tape and the airplane's own emergency hatchet. They demanded to be flown north to Miami. Florida Air National Guard F-15 Eagle fighter jets from Homestead Air Reserve Base and a UH-60 Black Hawk helicopter from the United States Customs Service escorted the Douglas DC-3 to Key West, Florida where it landed at 20:06. There were 3 crew members and 26 passengers on board, no one was injured.
