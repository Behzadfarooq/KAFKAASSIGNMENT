Aeroflot-Nord (Russian: Аэрофлот-Норд) (IATA: 5N, ICAO: AUL, and Callsign: Dvina) is an airline based in Arkhangelsk, Russia. It operates domestic and regional services and is a subsidiary of Aeroflot. Its main base is Talagi Airport (ARH), Arkhangelsk.

[edit] History

The airline was established and started operations in 2004. It was formed following acquisition of the assets of Arkhangelsk Airlines. It is owned by Aeroflot (51%) and Aviainvest (49%).

[edit] Fleet

The Aeroflot-Nord fleet consists of the following aircraft (at March 2007):

  • 5 Antonov An-24RV
  • 4 Antonov An-26B
  • 5 Boeing 737-500
  • 13 Tupolev Tu-134A
  • 5 Tupolev Tu-154B-2[1]
