Appalachian Ohio is defined by the State of Ohio to consist of the 29 south-easternmost counties. It is administered by the Governor's Office of Appalachia as part of the Appalachian Regional Commission, with three regional offices covering East Central Ohio, Southeast Ohio and Southern Ohio.

As defined by the state government, the three regions include the following counties:

East Central Ohio

  • Belmont
  • Carroll
  • Columbiana
  • Coshocton
  • Guernsey
  • Harrison
  • Holmes
  • Jefferson
  • Muskingum
  • Tuscarawas

Southeast Ohio

  • Athens
  • Hocking
  • Meigs
  • Monroe
  • Morgan
  • Noble
  • Perry
  • Washington

Southern Ohio

  • Adams
  • Brown
  • Clermont
  • Gallia
  • Highland
  • Jackson
  • Lawrence
  • Pike
  • Ross
  • Scioto
  • Vinton
