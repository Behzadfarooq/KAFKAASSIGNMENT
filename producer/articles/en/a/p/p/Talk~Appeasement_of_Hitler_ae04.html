[edit] Major improvements needed

This article requires citations and the attention of an expert. Many of the claims are deeply tendentious and simplistic. For instance, the claim that Hitler was seen as less of a threat than Soviet Russia because the Nazi holocaust had not yet taken place is a matter of interpretation, and it ought at least to be sourced if it isn't going to be balanced by other interpretations. The claim that the 'Allied' powers had come to regard Versailles as unfair may be accurate, but if it isn't going to be elaborated on properly it could at least be sourced.

This definitely needs to be reconsidered: They feared that as Britain and France were busy fighting Germany in the West, the Soviets would invade Poland and then eastern Germany. After the "War of German Suppression", Germans and Allies alike would be at the mercy of the Soviet Union, essentially "1945 without the United States or the atomic bomb". This is either speculation or there is some documentary or secondary evidence that can be sourced for it.

And this too is speculative: Even if a 1938 war against Germany was won, the most likely regime to replace the Nazis would be a military dictatorship. Alternatively, a post-Nazi Germany could have swung leftward, forcing the Western democracies to fight a German-Soviet alliance. Upon what basis this is offered as a matter of fact rather than interpretation and downright speculation is unclear. I would suggest that this article be substantially pared down if these claims cannot be supported. They are highly tendentious, if not outright apologetic.

Mistaken presumption that Hitler was a rational actor. Like many other dictators, diplomats of the time operated under the assumption that the Nazi Party, and Hitler, were rational actors who would stop when their defined goals were achieved. The failure of this assumption led to containment policies by anti-Communist nations during the Cold War, and such assumptions have remained in question against other dictatorships and allegedly expansionist totalitarian states including (but not limited to) China, North Korea, Iran, Iraq, and Libya.

This is almost a complete failure - aside from there being no source, it fails to specify in what sense Hitler wasn't rational in pursuing his defined goals (those defined privately). It isn't at all clear that Hitler's methods were incommensurate with his ends. —The preceding unsigned comment was added by 86.138.6.90 (talk • contribs).

I would have to agree with you on this. The article reads as though it was written by an A-level pupil who had just learned about it. The matter of appeasement in a great deal more complex than a simple Hitler vs. Chamberlain view. An expert needs a take on this, including perhaps the economic circumstances of the 1930s, and the military situation (and the lack of agreement in the British government with this).
