"Apple Hill" is an area in the Sierra Mountain foothills in El Dorado County, California, that is a widely visited place for its rural ambiance, apples products including pies and cider. It is old fashioned and if you wish to take a day in the past, visit Apple Hill. The beautiful countryside and yummy cider and delicious apples make it irresistible. What was an attempt to make a wish come true became a legendary American dream.

[edit] Background

In 1951, a man named Floyd Bolster had retired to a little community called Camino, located in El Dorado County, California. When he moved there, he had purchased a ranch that had 10 acres of blooming apple trees and hoped that he could work this land and have a great life of a farmer. Six years later, Floyd Bolster died leaving everything to his son, Gene. Gene left his job in southern California to come and work his fathers land in hopes to accomplish his father's dream.

In 1964, Gene Bolster, who was the local grower; Dick Bethell, the county's pomology specialist and farm advisor; Ed Delfino, the county's agricultural commissioner; and Bob Tuck, a retired army officer, all came together to form what is known today as the [Apple Hill Growers Association]. Apple Hill might have been known today as "Pear Hill" or something to do with pears. There was an abundance of pears 36 years ago, so the farmers went to learn how to make the soil fertile for apples and due to a stroke of luck, they switched to apples and became Apple Hill.


[edit] Starting a Mission

The team started in mid-June 1964. They had finished preparing everything in August for the first press picnic. The picnic consisted of each Apple Hill family hosting a meal for some of the press members. Many of these press members became close friends. All the growers also produced 50,000 little litter bags that they had passed out at the state fair. They offered 2 pounds of free apples to anybody who brought the bags with them.

Apple Hill consisted of 16 original ranches, eventually growing to 55 ranches including Christmas tree growers, and wineries, vineyards, a microbrewery, and a spa. Apple Hill was the first ranch marketing endeavor in northern California. Its success has inspired other ranch marketing groups to do the same, and now there are lots all over the United States.


[edit] Landmarks

Apple Hill's [Larsen Ranch] houses what is believed to be the oldest living apple tree in El Dorado County, its name is the Rhode Island Greening.

[edit] Antiques

Apple Hill not only sells apples, but also sells ways to use them in their very own cookbook. The original cookbook was first published in 1967 and it is now in its 20th edition. The farmers from Apple Hill all decided on the favorite recipes and put them together in Apple Hills very own cookbook. One of Apple Hill's residents thought that Apple Hill should have its own sampling room to show the guests what they could do with the apples. So they opened the first little shop that they modeled how to make special desserts and ciders with them, then letting their guests sample the product.


[edit] Harvest Season

You cannot find every fruit to be grown at the same time. Peaches are grown from late July to early September, Nectarines the same start, but end in August, Plums from mid June to early July, Pears are from September to October, Berries from mid June to late September, Cherries from Mid June to late June, and finally the Apples, from late august to early September. Apple Hill grows seven main types of apples; some individual ranches have other kinds. In addition, the fruit grows at all different times, so one would have to check several places to determine if they have the right apples and if they are ripe. The main seven are Gala, McIntosh, Jonathans, Golden Delicious, Red Delicious, Red Rome, and Granny Smith.

[edit] Attractions

There are several attractions for each ranch. There can be a bake shop, cider mill, wine tasting, crafts, picnic area, lunch, u-pick, nature walks. At the bakeshops, you can purchase bakes goods, food, cider, and little souvenirs. The Cider Mill shows the cider being made and the process the apples go through to becoming cider. At the end, they often have some to sample and some to purchase. Wine tasting is for those over the age of twenty-one and who wish to sample the different types of wines and beers. They have apple cider wine, apricot beer, etc. For the children, some ranches will have a crafts table where they can carve a pumpkin, draw, or help make a tasty treat. Every countryside business should have room for picnics. Apple Hill has picnic benches everywhere for people to sit and enjoy their food in the middle of the scenic countryside. Some places serve lunch items such as corndogs, tri tip barbeque, and sandwiches. You can also pick berries and other fruits at some of the ranches. Nature walks are held on some ranches. If you ask where you can go on a walk, often they will point you in the right direction of a walk that you will never forget. Bus parking is available for buses with tour groups.

[edit] Lodging and Dining

There are only a handful of lodging opportunities. There is St. Pauli Inn with German and American dining. Best Western Placerville Inn and Best Western Stagecoach Inn, and the Motherlode Motel. For lunch, you can visit High Hill Ranch, Boa Vista Orchards, Larsen Apple Barn, Rainbow Orchards, Grandpa's cellar, etc. For Dinner at a Restaurant in Apple Hill, The Forester Pub & Grill, Golden Poppy Cafe, Durango's Mexican Restaurant. For Wine Tasting, Jack Russell Ranch is a reputable place. They have beer and wine, crafts for sale such as necklaces, bracelets, and rings. They also have accessories for your dogs. Rainbow Orchards is a great place for families. They sell apples, pies, cobblers, apples cider doughnuts, corn dogs, cider, etc. They have a Tri Tip Barbeque that also serves burgers, fries, chicken, and ribs. Its kid friendly, with small tables for children to sit at, and hay bails to take pictures on, and is pet friendly.

If you do not have a car to take around Apple Hill, then never fear, the shuttle is here! Apple Hill has its very own shuttle now thanks to a grant from El Dorado County's Air Resources Board and a grant from the Apple Hill Growers association. This shuttle picks up its potential passengers in the Schnell School parking lot for a free tour of Apple Hill, and it visits almost all of the ranches. You get an all day pass to visit anywhere you like in Apple Hill, and a shuttle will come by every 15-30 minutes. The shuttle runs only during October and operates from 10 a.m. to 5 p.m. every weekend.

[edit] Events

December 17th is the end of the season sale on everything. Every year in February, Molino de Viento wine releases a new wine. In March of every year, people travel from far away to see the 1000 daffodils at Argyres Orchard. Every year around St. Patrick's Day, they throw a bash to celebrate at Jack Russell Farm Brewery. April 7th is the mug club auction.

[edit] Not only an Apple Farm

You can get Christmas trees here too. You can pick and cut down the tree that you want. The Christmas tree farm has hundreds of trees to choose from and there is always a good tree out there. There is a Fudge Shoppe located near the Christmas tree farms. They sell all kinds of fudge and candy.
