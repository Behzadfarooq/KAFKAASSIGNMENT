An approved school is a term used chiefly in the United Kingdom for an educational instutution catered for young offenders. In the United States of America, the term reform school is used.

Offenders sent to approved schools, as well as receiving academic tutition, are assigned to work groups for such activities as building and bricklaying, metalwork, carpentry and gardening.[1] Approved schools are known for strict and sometimes overly harsh discipline,[2] as illustrated by 'The Court Lees Approved School Affair' in 1967 (see this report).
