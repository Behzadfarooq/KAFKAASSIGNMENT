Appleton School for the Destitute, 56 Oxford Road Dewsbury WF13 4LL

is a English Public School run and founded by Sisters Katharine and Ruth Appleton, nuns of who cared about the poor. (from the Society of St Paul religious order]]

[edit] foundation

Founded on November 12th 1989, the School takes on those deemed intelligent by SAT or NFER scores and offers a private education funded by local benificiaries and the trust established in the will of the Appleton's stepfather, L Patrick Roman.

[edit] controversy

As the the school enters pupils for exams as private candidates, the Kirklees LEA has threatened to bring the school into line, as it presently evades the rigours of establishments that are officially registered, such as OFSTED inspections and league table positions.

[edit] other schools

As there are two other schools on the same road Westborough High School and St John Fisher Catholic High School (Dewsbury), some students attending the school are often intimidated by pupils and teachers from other schools. To this extent, the Headmistress, Sr K Appleton, is "seriously considering either moving the school, or introducing a residential element"
