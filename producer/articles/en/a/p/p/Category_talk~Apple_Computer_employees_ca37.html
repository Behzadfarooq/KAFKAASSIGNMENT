I'll be working on this in the next few weeks as it is a very sparse and uneven selection. It doesn't list more than a half dozen of the people responsible for major newsworthy events in the history of the company, or those who once worked at Apple but moved on to start up other companies or organizations. It does, on the other hand, list a few individual engineers who have worked on mid-level projects at Apple and not done much else.

Before I go on a categorization spree, though, I want to check with more experienced Wikipedians as to whether I've got the wrong idea.

  • Is a category supposed to be a useful (if not definitive) list, or is it just a grab-bag of things with a common trait?
  • Should I make stubs for notable Apple employees who don't already have entires, in order to make the category more useful?
  • Should I remove minor employees from the list? Or (heaven forbid) challenge their entries as autobiographical?

I appreciate feedback.

Chris Espinosa, Apple employee 1977-present

[edit] Who's a minor employee?

I wouldn't advise taking on the task of deciding who does or doesn't rate a mention in the list of Apple employees. For my part, I'd love to leave a certain bunch of poseurs and marketing dinks from the mid-80's to the mid-90's out of the story of Apple altogether, but Wikipedia has a lot more stuff in it than Britannica, because it can.

I'm in favor of stubbing an article for anyone who springs to mind as important to you, and let other people edit the list (and the articles they point to) as they see fit. —The preceding unsigned comment was added by 67.161.42.199 (talk • contribs) 09:47, 29 March 2005 (UTC).
