[edit] Merge with Mac zealot

Mac zealot refers to the same people as mac evangelists, only in a pejorative way. These two terms are very much dependent on each other and need to be compared to gain a proper context. The best choice would be to merge zealot into this one, since zealot is the less neutral term of the two. Arru 16:14, 22 February 2006 (UTC)

[edit] Original research / Notability

Isn't this bordering on original research? Sure, the terms Apple evangelist and Mac zealot are used by some, but that doesn't mean they're notable enough for an article. People use terms for just about other computer platform (Windows zealot, Linux zealot), but we don't have articles for those.

Furthermore, where a derogatory term is considered notable, Wikipedia must reflect it's usage as a derogatory term, and not be used to further push the insulting stereotype! (E.g., see articles like Chav, Mall goth.)

Unless anyone objects, I suggest the easiest solution is to redirect to Computer zealotry (as is done for Linux zealot). Alternatively, if notability for "Apple evangelist" can be established (and I don't mean some AOL page - does Kawasaki self-identify as an Apple evangalist, for example?) - then I would still suggest deleting the "Zealotry" section. Computer zealotry is sufficient for this, and there's no need for a POV section with insults like "Zealous Mac advocates have been known to verbally abuse computer users who do not embrace the Macintosh platform". Mdwh 19:50, 8 July 2006 (UTC)

  • I'm inclined to agree on the merge. Note though, that mac evangelism/zealotry has been the most exposed and discussed form of this during the last 10 years - if you attempt a source search for this you are more likely to find citings of "mac zealots" than on computer zealots in general or any other flavor. So, yes, it's notable.
The Operating system advocacy article seems to be a better merge target since it contains (shorter) counterparts to the description of the Mac advocate. This is part of a bigger need for merges where we have Technical evangelist - Computer zealotry - Operating system advocacy that seem to be repetitions of the same theme for most parts. As you mention on perpetuating stereotypes, we should really keep the positive (advocate/evangelist) and negative (zealot) together att all times.
Guy Kawasaki is cited as coining the term "evangelist" in relation to technology. So he deserves mention in a general article on computer evangelism/zealotry. --Arru 14:40, 9 July 2006 (UTC)
I agree, Operating system advocacy would be a better choice. Though Mac zealot, which currently redirects here, would perhaps then be more appropriately redirected to Computer zealotry? And I agree it's notable then, but we should be careful to stick with notable sources rather than making possibly-insulting/pov comments about users (as I say, I guess it's only the Zealotry section that's a problem). Mdwh 15:17, 9 July 2006 (UTC)

Nobody's objected, so I'm deleting the Zealotry section - this is just POV and stereotyping; instead we should be focusing on facts and reliable sources.

As a result of that, I'll also redirect Mac zealot to Computer zealotry, since that seems to be the article with more relevant information on "zealots". Mdwh 22:41, 21 July 2006 (UTC)
