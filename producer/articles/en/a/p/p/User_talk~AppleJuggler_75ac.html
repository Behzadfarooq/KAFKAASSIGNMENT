[edit] Image:Dewan negara.jpg

Hi, under American copyright law, no permission is required to use an image under the terms of fair use. American law imposes strict requirements for a use to qualify as fair use, however, and Wikipedia policy imposes some further restrictions that you should be aware of. Thanks, and HTH. :) Johnleemk | Talk 12:23, 6 February 2007 (UTC)

Of course the Malaysian authorities could question the usage of the image, but as Wikipedia's servers are located in the United States, American laws apply. As a result, the Malaysian authorities would not be able to compel Wikipedia to take down the image. Fair use as a legal doctrine runs foul of copyright law in many jurisdictions, and Malaysia is probably no exception, but since it is a de facto right in most countries to make use of material in the way we have done with fair use, there probably shouldn't be a problem. Just FYI, IANAL, so if you're interested in any further clarification, it might be wise to seek the advice of the community at WP:VP or the mailing list. Johnleemk | Talk 13:37, 6 February 2007 (UTC)

[edit] Image:Ilaiyaraaja.jpg

Hi,

This image of Ilaiyaraaja uploaded by you carries a license that says it is Wikipedia-only permission. Such licenses are not allowed on Wikipedia, and the image being non-free may be deleted. Since the image fails the fair-use criteria since a free alternative can be created, it can't be used as a fair-use image. If you created it yourself and want to release it to Public Domain, use {{PD-self}} as image license. Contact me if you need any help. — Ambuj Saxena (☎) 13:51, 14 February 2007 (UTC)

In that case, I would suggest using {{PD-author}}, with a quote from the author on the page stating verbatim that he is releasing the image in the public domain. Alternately (actually preferably), you can ask him to mail you the photograph with the permission, and then you should forward the mail to permissions (at) wikimedia (dot) org. This would be archived as a proof of permission. Copyright laws are very convoluted, but for this case, I think simplification can be done. If you are sure that the concert was open to photography, you can go ahead with the tagging. — Ambuj Saxena (☎) 14:59, 14 February 2007 (UTC)
