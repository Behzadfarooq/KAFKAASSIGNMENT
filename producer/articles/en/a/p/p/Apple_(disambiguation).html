The apple is a tree and its pomaceous fruit.

Apple or apples may also refer to:

People named Apple:

  • Apple Martin, the daughter of Gwyneth Paltrow and Chris Martin
  • Charlie Apple, a veteran R&B and oldies radio disc jockey from Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania
  • Fiona Apple, a Grammy award winning American singer-songwriter
  • R. W. Apple, Jr., an associate editor at The New York Times

In business:

  • Apple Bank, an American bank in the New York City area
  • Apple Inc. (formerly Apple Computer, Inc.), an American computer technology corporation with worldwide annual sales in its fiscal year 2006 of US$19.3 billion
  • Apple Corps, a multi-armed multimedia corporation
  • Apple Corps v. Apple Computer, Inc., a lawsuit between the two aforementioned companies

In music:

  • Apple (album), an album by Mother Love Bone
  • Apple (band), a British psychedelic rock band
  • Apple Records, record label founded by The Beatles

In technology:

  • any of the computers made by Apple Inc. since 1976, notably the Apple Macintosh
  • APPLE, an experimental communication satellite with a C-Band transponder
  • Apple (automobile), a short-lived American automobile manufactured by Apple Automobile Company in Dayton, Ohio from 1917 to 1918

In other fields:

  • Apple Cup, the annual college football game between cross-state rivals the University of Washington and Washington State University
  • Apple Daily, a Hong-Kong-based newspaper that was founded by Jimmy Lai in 1995 and is published by his company, Next Media
  • Apple Pugetsound Program Library Exchange, a computer users group
  • Apples, Vaud, Switzerland
