[edit] Your RfA

I don't have very high standards for adminship, but I think that at a bare mininum, a user should have been on the project for 4 months and have had 1000-1500 edits. The purpose of this is twofold- 1) to ensure that the candidate has sufficent experience with wikipedia and the community and conventions within it. 2) to make sure that the user is a responsible editor who doesn't break the rules. I see no evidence that you have broken the rules (vandalism, 3 revert, bad behavior), but the short time you have been on the project just isn't enought to see what kind of an editor you are. Many have higher standards than mine, for instance I have recently been rejected for adminship despite 6 months time and over 3,000 edits (Rogerd's RFA), even though I have made many good edits and have never broken the rules.

Why don't you just participate, and learn about wikipedia for a few more months. Edit content, and contribute in areas you are interested and have knowledge. Also, you could participate in WP:AfD, WP:RfA, WP:RA and other areas of the project. Good luck --Rogerd 04:35, 17 October 2005 (UTC)

[edit] Speedy deletes

Hi Appleboy! I notice you're using {{d}} when marking things for Speedy Delete. This tag is depreciated. Would you consider helping the Admins who will eventually delete the page by using one of the descriptive tags instead? The main ones are:

  • {{nonsense}} for patent nonsense
  • {{db-empty}} for articles with little or no content or no context
  • {{nn-bio}} for articles about real people where no notability is asserted
  • {{db-attack}} for articles that exist purely to attack the subject of the article
  • {{deleteagain}} for articles previously deleted and recreated
  • {{deletebecause|reason}} where you fill in your own reason, provided it meets one of the Speedy Delete criteria.

Also, remember to warn the user who created the article on his/her talk page - this gives newbies the chance to put things right if the edit wasn't meant as vandalism.

Thanks very much for your time! If you've got any questions or need any advice, drop me a line on my talk page and I'll be happy to help. ➨ ❝REDVERS❞ 16:51, 25 November 2005 (UTC)

[edit] benvenuto Tisi (Il Garofalo)

I am new to this Wikipeding, but I have decided out of my own interests to use it as a way of keeping track of reviews that I make of Italian Art, focusing mostly on second tier artists.

I recently redid the article on Il Garofalo, basing my findings on both internet sources and SJ Freedberg's monograph on Italian Art, since like many such articles from either the 1911 encyclopedia or the Catholic encyclopedia, it contains much vacuous and often gossip-full nonsense.

I was just curious as to why you and others keep reverting it back, is this a reflexive response? You can respond in the talk section for this article.
