Approved Driving Instructor

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Approved Driving Instructor (or ADI) is a UK term for a trainer of car driving who has been tested and licensed by the Driving Standards Agency. It is a legal requirement that instructors of car driving in the UK must be thus qualified in order to charge for their services.

Free tuition or supervision may however be given by any individual over the age of 21 who holds a full license in the same class of vehicle as that being used for at least 3 years. The UK has no law requiring the compulsory use of an ADI but research shows that over 95% of prospective drivers use an ADI for at least some of their training.

[edit] Register of Approved Driving Instructors

To be entered on the Register of Approved Driving Instructors one must:

  1. Be over 21.
  2. Have held a full UK license for at least 3 years.
  3. Be "of good character". Unspent criminal convictions will be considered, and exceeding 5 penalty points on the driving license will preclude entry.
  4. Pass 3 examinations. 'Part 1' is a multiple choice test of law and relevant knowledge, also a Hazard Perception Test is now included, which may be likened to a poor video game entailing 'spotting' developing hazards on video clips taken from a moving car. This Part is similar to the Theory and Hazard perception Tests taken by Learners, but longer and scored to a higher standard. 'Part 2' is a test of practical driving, and 'Part 3' is a test of Instructional Ability, where the examiner drives the car and expects to be 'Instructed' suitably.
  5. Agree to 'Check tests' conducted every 2 to 5 years (depending on 'Grade' achieved.)
  6. Display a green badge in the window of the training vehicle showing their photo, name and confirming their place on the Register.[1]

Instructors are rated according to this Check Test as Grade 1 to Grade 6; where 6 is best, 4 is average, and 1, 2 and 3 are unacceptable.
