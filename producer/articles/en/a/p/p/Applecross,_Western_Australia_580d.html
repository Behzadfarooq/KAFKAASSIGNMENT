Applecross is a suburb of Perth, Western Australia, located within the City of Melville.

Like the name Perth, Applecross's name is borrowed from a region in Scotland. Many of the suburb's streets have Scottish names.

[edit] Jacarandas

A distinguishing feature of the suburb are the jacaranda trees which line its streets and which flower in the late spring. In keeping with this theme, Applecross is home to an annual Jacaranda festival.

Perth is a booming city in property prices with Applecross going up in price to become an 'elite' suburb. Property prices have risen from $300 000 to well over $1 000 000.
