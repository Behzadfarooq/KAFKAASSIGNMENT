Appleton West High School

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Appleton West High School is a high school located at 610 North Badger Avenue in Appleton, Wisconsin. It is a member of the Appleton Area School District.

Built in 1938, the building housing Appleton West High School was originally home to Appleton Senior High School. In fact, "Appleton Senior High School" is still etched into the front of the building. After Appleton East High School was built, the school was renamed to Appleton West.

Appleton West has an enrollment of approximately 1,600 students.

The mascot of Appleton West is the Terror, a cross between a wolf and a fox, as the school is situated in between the Wolf River and the Fox River. The athletic department has a strong history of success. In 1993 the varsity football team won the Division I State Championship. The team also reached the championship game in 1992 and 1994. Following the creation of the archrival school, Appleton North, in the mid 1990s, West athletics experienced a period of decline as several coaches and players decided to leave their school and head across town to North. Today West's athletics are successful once again, thanks partially due to a brand new, health club quality fitness center which was built in 2002. Highlights of Terror athletics over the past years have been a baseball state championship in 2004, two consecutive trips to the basketball state tournament in 2002 and 2003, and a football playoff run in 2004. Recently, the football team finished its 2006 season with a record of only two wins, including their major upset victory over the #2 team in conference (Fond du Lac) on homecoming. The wrestling team did exceptionally well with a 23-8 dual meet record (as of February 2007). Varsity wrestlers include Adam Jones (103), Shane McQuade (112), Zack Schemmal (119), Anthony Little (125), Josh Dubman (130), Eric Moorehead (130, 135, 140, 145), Randy Christensen (135), Matt Prentice (140)Clete Ketter (145), Luke Justman (152), Dan Kaiser (160), Quinn Levandoski (171), Dan Sikowski (189), Matt Roberts (215), and Ryan Oslund (HWT).

Appleton West also plays as host to another High School. Renaissance School for the Arts is housed inside of West. This afternoon school allows aspiring artists in Acting, Dance, Visual Arts, Music, and Writing to craft and perfect their arts through a series of courses offered by permanent and temporary staff.

As of September 2006, plans for an expansion to the high school have yet to be approved. The proposed plan calls for the addition of a sports complex to the northeast side of the school. This complex would include 8 new tennis courts, a new football/soccer field, a new track, a 3,000 seat grandstand with press box, bathrooms, concessions, and storage, and long jump/ triple jump pits. The demolition of several homes bordering the school to the north east is required for the addition of these features. Although some residents have agreed to sell their homes, others have yet to agree.

The boys tennis team will begin its season with high expectations this year. Coach Endter stated "there is no team in the conference we can't beat besides Neenah". The coaches expect the team to finish second in the conference
