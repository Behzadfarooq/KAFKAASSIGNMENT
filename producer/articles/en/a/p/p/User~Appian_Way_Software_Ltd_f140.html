User:Appian Way Software Ltd

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Appian Way is a British educational software publisher founded in 1988 by Peter and Anne Britton, with the aim of becoming the leading supplier of electronic history resources. By the early 1990s popular titles such as the Data-100 series and ‘Picture It!’, meant the company had succeeded in becoming the leading producer of history software in the UK.

In 1997 Appian Way merged with another, larger, educational company, Actis Ltd., and Peter became Actis’ MD as well. Actis flourished under Peter’s leadership, but, as the company was a general educational publisher, covering all areas of the curriculum, this move took him well away from his roots. So, a couple of years after Actis was taken over by a financial institution in 2000, Peter left to re-start Appian Way as a specialist history provider again.

Appian Way was able to publish its first new title, The Rise and Fall of Nazi Germany, in September, 2004 as part of a new range of software programs called TimeMaps. This has been followed by an initial launch in September 2005 of the TimeMaps Interactive Map of World History. Having undergone a major upgrade, this is now available in two versions for primary schools and lower secondary students, and a full version of this programme will be launched in September 2006 for older students and adults.

For more information visit www.timemaps.com
