The apportionment of seats in the European Parliament is not strictly in accordance with size of population. Rather, European Parliament seats are allocated by degressive proportionality, so that, while the size of the population of each country is taken into account, smaller states elect more MEPs than would be strictly justified by their populations alone.

The number of MEPs apportioned to each country is determined by treaty (see also EU treaties). No change in the number of MEPs granted to each member state can occur without the unanimous consent of all governments. The distribution of seats shown in the tables at right and below has been in place since June 2004. The last change in the apportionment of seats occurred in January 2007, after Romania and Bulgaria joined.
