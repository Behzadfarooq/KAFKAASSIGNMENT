Ape Escape 2 (Saru! Get You! 2 in Japan), is a platform game for the PlayStation 2 and is the second in the Ape Escape series.

In the game, a boy named Hikaru (Jimmy in the United States version) along with a baby monkey named Pipotchi and a teenage braniac named Natsumi (Natalie) have to assist the professor in catching monkeys that are wreaking havoc. Spike, the hero of the first game and Jimmy's cousin makes a cameo appearance, but once the player defeats Specter in the final battle, a secret is revealed in which if you hold L1 and Start when starting a new game, the player will play as Spike.

The character names are the same as the original Japanese names in the United Kingdom version of the game, but are different in the United States version of the game. Ubisoft and Namco tributed this game.

[edit] European Issues

In the Game released in Europe, the characters all had the Japanese names. This turned out to be much annoyance to the European fans, whom were used to the character names and voices from the last game.


[edit] Gadgets

Gadgets are Jimmy's main forms of offense and defense.

  • Stun Club- This is Jimmy's basic weapon. It can defeat most enemies in a few hits and can break things and answer help telephones.
  • Monkey Net- This is Jimmy's most important tool. It catches monkeys.
  • Monkey Radar- This device helps Jimmy locate monkeys and find out their statistics.
  • Super Hoop- A hula hoop that allows for Jimmy to travel very fast.
  • Slingback Shooter- A slingshot that fires normal, explosive, and guided pellets.
  • RC Car- This car helps Jimmy attack enemies, collect items, and reach switches from afar. There are 5 different chassis.
  • Sky Flyer- A helicopter-like device that lets you glide for a short while.
  • Bananarang- This boomerang emits a banana-like scent that attracts monkeys.
  • Water Cannon- A water gun that can put out flames and distract monkeys.
  • Electro Magnet- A magnet that can move or attract metal items and remove enemy armor.
  • Magic Punch- This is the ultimate weapon that defeats all enemies in one hit, except bosses.
  • Secret gadget-On Jimmy, you have a powerful punching glove. When you play Spike, you'll get a new Monkey Net. To get this, go to the gadget selection screen, select the monkey net and press R1. And your net is a blue coloured Water Net. The net can be used in land or water. To switch back to the normal net, press R1 agin in the gadget selection screen with the Monkey Net selected.You can do this only while playing as Spike/Kakeru.

Jimmy has to face the common enemies using his various gadgets as well. Most of the enemies are pig-like, but there are different ones as well.

[edit] Minigames

Like in the previous game, Ape Escape 2 features minigames, this time to get them you pay for them via a vending machine in the hub when you've got up to a certain stage in the game. The minigames are:

  • Dance Monkey Dance: A dancing minigame similar to the Dance Dance Revolution Series where the player competes against other monkeys in a dancing contest. Alternatively, the player can dance alone and is reviewed on his or her dancing by judges.
  • Monkey Soccer: A minigame similar to the FIFA Series where the player plays against other teams in a soccer game.
  • Monkey Climber: In this minigame the player has to make the monkey climb up the vines by repeatedly tapping the analog stick buttons.

[edit] Trivia

Like from the first game,Ape Escape 2 is packed with popular references(e.g. Bruce Monkee is a pun off of Bruce Lee,Apewood is off of Clint Eastwood,Cruiser is a pun off of Tom Cruise)but there are also more references,too,like gameplay.

  • Jimmy bears a strong resemblance to Sora from Kingdom Hearts,not just by the hair,but by clothing and personality.
  • Red Monkey's attacks resemble Hercules's attacks in Kingdom Hearts.
  • When you summon Mushu in Kingdom Hearts,Mushu sits on Sora's head,like Pipotchi does to Jimmy.
  • The Freaky Monkey Five resembles characters,too.Blue Monkey resembles Sonic the Hedgehog ,while Yellow Monkey resembles the Fat Bastard,& Pink Monkey's dark form resembles Super Sonic by the hair.
  • This is the first game to feature a different protagonist other than Spike.The second game to do so was Ape Escape 3.
  • Interestingly,there are few differences between the English and the Japanese versions other than names.For example,monkeys' quotes.Johhny's,in the Enter the Monkey stage,quote is,in the English version,is,"Smells something funny."However,in the JPN version,it is,"Caught a fart in the urm."The same difference is with Alex,who is,"Just went to the restroom,"in the US version.In JPN,it's,"Took a wee in the bin."Monkey's name are also changed due to pop culture in the US and the JPN.In the Port Calm stage,the monkey drinking coffee

at the table is Monkichino in the US,a reference to Cappuchino.However, in the JPN,it's Dannyboy,for cappuchino and coffee are only popular in the US.

  • In the American version,Jimmy is voiced by Veronica Taylor ,who also voiced Ash from the Pokemon anime.


[edit] Content

While Ape Escape 2 is rated E,it has some quite questionable content.For example,Yellow Monkey is quite the controversy.He may be a homosexual if a male,if a woman,she is quite a pornographic symbol.Like its predecessor,it contains a "Hot Spring",showing monkeys in nudity,as well as the monkeys scrubbing the towels between their crotch.Finally,the game contains language, such as damn,sexy,and ohmigod.While Ohmigod was said once by White Monkey,damn was said twice by Spike in "Nightmare Scenario,"which is perplexing,because Spike is a quite innocent kid,and sexy was used thrice,two for descriptions of the monkeys,and another in a Monkey Fable.The content label on the back didn't warn about the crude humor and mild language,but instead cartoon violence and comic mischeif.This mistake was,however,corrected when Ape Escape 3 and Ape Escape Academy was released,and rated it E10+ for its Crude Humor.
