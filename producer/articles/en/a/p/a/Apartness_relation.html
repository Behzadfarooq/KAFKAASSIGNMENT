Apartness relation

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

In constructive mathematics, an apartness relation is a symmetric irreflexive binary relation with the additional condition that if two elements are apart, then any other element is apart from one of them. That is, a binary relation R is an apartness relation if it satifies:

The negation of an apartness relation is an equivalence relation, as the above three conditions become reflexivity, symmetry, and transitivity. In classical mathematics, therefore, it also follows that the negation of an equivalence relation is an apartness relation, and so the concept is not useful in that domain. In constructive mathematics, however, this is not the case. Often, in constructive topology especially, the apartness relation on a set is taken as primitive, and the equivalence relation derived from it.
