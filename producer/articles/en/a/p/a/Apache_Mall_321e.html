Apache Mall

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

This article is about a shopping center in Rochester, Minnesota. For the dead mall in St. Anthony Village, Minnesota, see Apache Plaza.

Apache Mall is the largest enclosed shopping space in Rochester, Minnesota. It was built in 1972 at the intersection of U.S. Route 52 and U.S. Route 14. The Mall's food court has had free wi-fi access provided by the city's only cable television company, Charter Communications since January 2007.

[edit] Anchors & Majors

  • Barnes & Noble (25,134 sq. ft.)
  • Herberger's (91,400 sq. ft.)
  • JCPenney (128,196 sq. ft.)
  • Macy's (162,790 sq. ft.)
  • Sears (113,876 sq. ft.)

[edit] Former anchors

  • Montgomery Ward (now Herberger's)
