Apache wickiup


American Memory from the Library of Congress


Apache Indian wick-i-up, Arizona / N. H. Rose.

Rose, Noah H.

CREATED/PUBLISHED 1880.

SUMMARY View of two Native American Apache women outside their cloth covered wickiups in a camp in Arizona. They wear print skirts, blouses and shawls and hold baskets. A basket, pitch covered basket jar, metal coffee pot, cup, can, and a horse show in the camp.

NOTES Title typed on label on original; stamp on back of print: "This is a print from the famous Rose Collection of old time photographs N. H. Rose photographer P.O. Box 463 - San Antonio, Texas."

Photoprint shows creasing, worn edges and red ink staining.

SUBJECTS Indians of North America--Arizona--1880-1890. Apache Indians--Arizona--1880-1890. Wickiups--Arizona--1880-1890.

MEDIUM 1 photoprint ; 13 x 21 cm. (5 x 8 in.) 1 copy photonegative ; 13 x 18 cm. (5 x 7 in.)
