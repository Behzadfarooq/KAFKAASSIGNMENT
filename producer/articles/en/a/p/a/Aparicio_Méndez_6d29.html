Aparicio Méndez

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Aparicio Méndez (1904-1988), was a Uruguayan political figure. He was President of Uruguay from 1976-1981.

Born in the northern city of Rivera, Méndez was a member of the National Party, traditionally strong in the interior of the country from whence he originated. He built up a reputation as an expert in administrative law.

He was Health Minister from 1963-1965.

Méndez was one of various civilian political figures who participated in the civilian-military administration which took office following President Juan Maria Bordaberry's coup in 1973 at a time of great social tension. It was as one who had built a reputation for reliability with its military participants that he subsequently served as President for five years.

Some would argue that, in agreeing to serve as President with military support, Aparicio Méndez was effectively repudiating the principles of the National Party with which he had been associated. Others would point out that Méndez was far from alone among the various civilian party political figures who participated in the civilian-military administration of 1973-1985.

In addition to his political life, Méndez was a close personal friend of the great Spanish classical guitarist Andrés Segovia. Segovia lived in Montevideo during the 1940s, and came into contact with Méndez during this time. Segovia composed two original pieces for Méndez , the Anecdote #4 (published in Guitar Review Magazine in 1947), and the Preludio #8 (subtitled "on a theme by Aparicio Méndez") which was published by Edizioni Musicali Bèrben in 1998.
