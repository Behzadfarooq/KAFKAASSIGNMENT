Aparecida do Rio Doce is a small town and municipality in southwest Goiás state, Brazil. The population was 2727 inhabitants (2005 estimate) in a total area of 290 km².

  • Elevation: not available
  • Became a city: 1993
  • Postal code: 75827-000

The municipality of Aparecida do Rio Doce belongs to the Southwest Goiás Microregion and is 301 km. from the state capital, Goiânia. Connections are made by BR-060 / Guapó / Indiara / Rio Verde / GO-174/ GO-422 / BR-364. [1]

Belonging to the Paraná River basin, the river system is varied, with the most important rivers being the Rio Claro and its tributary, the Rio Doce. Along these rivers there are several small waterfalls, the most important being Salto do Rio Claro. The two rivers are used for recreational fishing and swimming by locals and tourists.

Municipal boundaries are with:

  • North and West: Jataí
  • East: Rio Verde
  • South: Caçu

Economy

The main economic activity is cattle raising for meat (69,697 head in 2003). Farming is secondary and is mainly subsistence, with farmers growing rice (15 hectares planted), corn (30 hectares planted), and soybeans (100 hectares planted).

Another economic activity is production of poultry (537,000 in 2002) and pigs (52,204 in 2002), due to the proximity of the important Perdigão factory in Rio Verde. One farm, Fazenda Santa Thereza, produces over 500 thousand chickens and 25,000 pigs a year. It also has almost 1000 head of confined cattle. [2]

  • Gross Domestic Product: 28.8 million Reais in 2002, which was 0.08% of the state GDP of 31.298 billions in the same year.
  • Gross Domestic Product per capita: 9368 Reais in 2002, which was higher than the state average of 5928 Reais.

There were 178 automobiles, 42 trucks, 14 pickups, and 64 motorcycles in the municipality in 2004.
