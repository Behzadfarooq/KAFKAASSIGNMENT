Apopka High School is a high school located in Apopka in southwest Orange County, Florida, United States.

The school serves grades 9 through 12. The campus is divided into a 9th grade center and a Main Campus

[edit] Sports

Apopka has about 26 total sports teams. The football team won the first State Championship in 2001. This marks the first such title for an Orange County Public School. The team also won the District Title in 2000. In 2006 the team won the district title, and the metro conference title.

  • The school's home football field is Roger Williams Field.

[edit] Notable alumni

  • Warren Sapp, who played for Apopka in his High School years and now is currently playing with the Oakland Raiders
  • Brandon Meriweather, who played and graduated from Apopka High School. Went to the University of Miami, while there he was named a Pre-season All-American for the 2006 season.
  • Zack Greinke, Major League Baseball player for the Kansas City Royals


In 2007 a new school located at 7315 North Hiawassee Road will open [1]. The school, temporarily named Apopka Relief, will take some students away from Apopka High School. Relief school named Wekiva High School. (http://www.apopkahighrelief.ocps.net/index.htm)

Students from mostly Lockhart Middle and Piedmont Lakes Middle will go to this school.

[edit] Trivia

In spring 1932, students were asked to choose a mascot for the athletic teams. Dr. Carey C. Womble proposed the blue darter, which won. Before that the school mascot was a 'Blue Devil'. [2].
