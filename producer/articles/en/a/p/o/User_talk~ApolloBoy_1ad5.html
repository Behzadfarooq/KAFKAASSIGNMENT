[edit] Concordia Newsletter

NEWSLETTER

Concordia is currently trying to relaunch. I, and all the members of the ex-council, wish to welcome new members to the group. We are a group who aim to promote remaining civil, in an environment where messages can easily be interpretated wrongly.

[edit] Help out now!

  • Try and help people remain civil! Talk to them, and help them in any way possible. Do not be afraid to use the talk page.
  • Give people the Civility Barnstar.
  • Make and spread some Wikitokens so people know there are people to help if they want assistance.
  • Add banners or logos to your userpage to show your support.
  • Suggest some ideas! Add 'em to the talk page.

We are a community, so can only work though community contributions and support. It's the helping that counts.

[edit] New Romantic

Hi, I'm not doubting you; you seem to know more than I do about the "New Romantic" movement, but I want to learn more. I added some bands to the "List of New Romantic Bands" and you deleted them saying they're not really New Romantic.

The bands I added were mostly European, from the right era, were techno-pop and mostly, had "that sound" the one that's similar to the other bands that are acknowledged to be New Romantic.

So I wonder can you define "New Romantic" or give the criteria that you use to say what is and what is not such a band.

Specifically, why aren't these bands New Romantic? Electronic, Erasure, George Michael and Wham, New Order, Pet Shop Boys and Roxy Music? Depeche Mode is and they aren't? How so? Thanks very much. 68.214.15.98 00:36, 4 February 2007 (UTC)

Well, New Romanticism had begun around 1979 and fell out of fashion by around 1983. Bands like New Order and Roxy Music, while they existed during that time, didn't exactly have the same sound or fashion sense (i.e. frilly shirts, heavy makeup) that true New Romantic bands like Duran Duran and Spandau Ballet had; Pet Shop Boys, Erasure, and Electronic arrived too late (the former two got their start in the mid-1980s, and Electronic appeared in 1989) and never had the New Romantic sound or image. George Michael and Wham! simply did regular pop music, not New Romantic or even general New Wave.
Anyway, that should give you a good idea of what bands are truly New Romantic and which ones aren't. --ApolloBoy 00:47, 4 February 2007 (UTC)
That helps, thanks! 68.215.207.186 23:59, 5 February 2007 (UTC)
