Apollon Limassol (Greek: Απόλλων Λεμεσού) is a Cypriot sports club, based in Limassol. It has football, basketball and volleyball teams.

[edit] History

Apollon F.C. was founded in Limassol, Cyprus on the 13th of February 1954. The Club applied for a membership to the Cyprus F.A. and was accepted as a second division club on October 16th, 1955.The emblem ofthe newly formed club was that of the ancient Greek god of sun "Apollonas" (Apollo) and ever since is the biggest team in town with the most fanatic followers in Limassol.

[edit] Football

At its first year in official football, Apollon suffered 8 defeats in 8 matches in a regional group of the second division. Before the beginning of the next season (1956-57) a crisis erupted in AEL, a first division team based in Limassol and most of the players left the club and joined Apollon. It was not a surprise that the team won the regional group in second division and then the play-offs to win promotion to the first division. That was in 1957 and ever since Apollon is playing in the first division.

Things were not very easy for the newly promoted club. Apollon was struggling in the middle of the table in the league for many years. But in the mid-60's things went better for the Club. In 1965 Apollon reached the Cup Final but it was on the losing side. A year later, in 1966, Apollon was again in the Final but this time it finished on the winning side.

Apollon managed to maintain its Cup title in 1967, but that was it. The team had to wait for 15 years to start making front page stories. It hit the headlines in 1982 when it reached the Cup Final but failed to win it. That marked the beginning of a new era. In the last 20 years Apollon won the league title three times (1991, 1994, 2006); was four times runner-up (1984, 1989, 1993, 1997); won the Cup five times (1966, 1967, 1986, 1992, 2001); and Cup finalist six times (1965, 1982, 1987, 1994, 1995, 1998).

In 2006 Apollon won the Cyprus First Division championship title after 12 years, while being the only undefeated team in Europe. Apollon hadn't had a defeat for 36 league games (since 12 March 2005 - September 2006).

[edit] Basketball - Pizza Express Apollon

The basketball team was established in 1966 but until 1999/2000 had no success except twice that achieved to play in European competitions, two years in a row, in 1989 and in 1990. For three. In 1999/2000 the team qualified in the Championship final for first time but they were beaten by Keravnos 3-1 in victories. The team subsequently reached another Championship final in the next season, was beaten again by Keravnos. What the team didn't achieve in those two championship finals, they achieved the next year in 2002, where the team won the Cup.

In 2003, Apollon participate in Southern European Regional Challenge Cup, and reached the final four after eliminating Hapoel Galil-Elyon beating them 67-65 at home and a draw 86-86. As a Final Four team, the club achieved to host the Final Four to its own stadium, Apollo Stadium. Opponent of the team in semi finals was their arch-rivals, the Cypriot and Limassol team, AEL Limassol. The winner would have been the first Cypriot sports team to reach a European Final. Unfortunate for the team, they lost 52-73 but they won the third position after they beat 79-65 the Romanian team, Arad Basket.

In 2004, Apollon lost in the Cup final but the next year, the team reached the final four again, after 2003, in the FIBA Southern EuroCup Challenge after eliminating Dinamo Bucuresti, 80-73 and 63-57. In the Final Four they became fourth, after they were beaten in the semi finals by the Romanian West Petrom 65-71 and in the Third Position Final by the other Cypriot team, Keravnos Nicosia 89-95.

The team reached the for first time in the semi finals of a Paneuropean cup (previous time was for southern region), the EuroCup Challenge after eliminating BU Poli Mobitelco Cluj-Napoca 82-71 at home and 86-75 away. In the semi finals the 77-75 was not enough to eliminate CSK-VVS Samara and they were eliminated in second leg after the defeat 76-50 in Russia.

[edit] Volleyball team

Founded member of Cyprus Volleyball Federation, the women's team participated in three Championship finals (1999, 2001, 2003) and in five Cup finals (1992, 1997, 1998, 2000, 2003) but lost all of them by AEL Limassol. In 2003, the team lost both Championship and Cup finals by AEL again, however since AEL won the double, they participated in the Super Cup and on September 23, 2003 they won their first trophy, the Super Cup in their own Stadium by beating AEL Limassol 3-0.

[edit] Stadium

Main article: Tsirion Stadium

The team's current stadium is the 14,400 seater, Tsirion Stadium, also known as the Olympia Stadium (G.S.O). Tsirion Stadium is a multi-use stadium in Limassol, Cyprus. It is currently used mostly for football matches and is the home ground of Apollon Limassol, AEL Limassol and Aris Limassol. The stadium was built in 1975. Apollon Limassol currently uses the West Stand of the stadium but most of the times is overcrowded therefore also use the East Stand of the stadium.

Apollon has its own Indoor Hall, the Apollon Stadium, with capacity of 2,600 and as it was said it hosted the Southern European Regional Challenge Cup Final Four. In the Cup Final, AEL Limassol beat KK Igokea 82-92 and became the first Cypriot team to win a European Trophy. It is home of Apollon's basketball and volleyball team.
