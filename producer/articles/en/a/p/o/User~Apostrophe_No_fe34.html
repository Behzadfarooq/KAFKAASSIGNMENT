[edit] Radiant Garden timeline

  • Xehanort found by Ansem the Wise.
  • Ansem Report 1 written by Ansem the Wise.
  • Secret Ansem Report 1 written by Ansem the Wise.
  • Psychological experiments on Xehanort's heart to probe the depths of the heart and to find his lost memories.
  • Ienzo suggests a castle basement laboratory, which is granted.
  • Xehanort, Bleig, Dilan, Even, and Ienzo collect subjects to use in their experiments.
  • Three experiments are used: extracting darkness from hearts, cultivating darkness in a pure heart, and suppressing and amplifying the darkness within.
  • The experiments cause all hearts to collapse.
  • The subjects are confined beneath the castle.
  • Xehanort, going below, discovers "shadows".
  • Xehanort writes Ansem Report 2.
  • "Shadows" multiply.
  • Xehanort writes Ansem Report 3, naming the "shadows" Heartless.
  • Heartless are provided living and nonliving samples; Heartless react to living, and the samples disappear, with the Heartless multiplying afterwards.
  • Xehanort writes Ansem Report 4.
  • Xehanort takes one Heartless aside for observation. The Heartless leads Xehanort to the Radiant Garden's Keyhole. Xehanort is able to open this and see the heart of the world.
  • The walls between worlds break down, showing itself as a meteor shower made of gummi blocks.
  • Xehanort writes Ansem Report 5.
  • Xehanort writes Ansem Report 6.
  • Xehanort finds gummi blocks.
  • Xehanort writes Ansem Report 7.
  • The Heartless Manufactory is built by Xehanort and the apprentices to artificially create Heartless by "recreating the conditions that spawn the Heartless naturally".
  • The natural and artificial Heartless behave the same, but for the purposes of experiment, the artificial Heartless are marked with an emblem. Those Heartless are referred to as Emblems, while the others as Purebloods.
  • Xehanort writes Ansem Report 8.
  • Ansem the wise discovers the Heartless experiments and demands that his apprentices cease research and destroy all results.
  • Mickey arrives at the Radiant Garden in his Gummi Ship.
  • Mickey talks with Xehanort about many things, including gummi blocks and the Keyblade.
  • Xehanort writes Ansem Report 9.
  • Mickey speaks with Ansem the Wise about many things, as well.
  • Xehanort asks for permission to proceed with an experiment, to which Ansem the Wise refuses.
  • On Mickey's advice, Ansem studies the data, finding the reports Xehanort wrote under his name.
  • Ansem the Wise writes Secret Ansem Report 2.
  • Ansem the Wise is banished to the realm of nothingness.
  • Xehanort writes Ansem Report 10.
  • Xehanort gives his heart to the darkness, forming his sapient Heartless and Xemnas.
  • The apprentices follow suit, forming normal Heartless and the humanoid Nobodies Xigbar, Xaldin, Vexen, Lexaeus, and Zexion.
  • Xehanort's Heartless writes Ansem Report 11.
  • To find the Keyblade, Xehanort's Heartless sends off Kairi, a Princess of Heart, thus linked to the Keyblade, into the "ocean of worlds".
  • Xehanort's Heartless writes Ansem Report 12.
  • Xehanort's Heartless writes Ansem Report 13.

[edit] "Artificial Kingdom Hearts"

[edit] Arguments for and rebuttals

  • Organization XIII claims ownership.
    • Claiming ownership indicates nothing about creation. I own my dog, but I certainly didn't create it.
    • Xemnas has made many overexaggerated claims, such as being "eternal" and ignoring any positive emotion. He is not a completely reliable source of indication.
  • Nomura's line.
    • See below.

[edit] Quotes against

  • Naminé: Bad or good, I don't know. They're a group of incomplete people who wish to be whole. To that end, they're desperately searching for something.
  • Roxas: What?
  • Naminé: Kingdom Hearts.
    • Statement makes no sense if Organization XIII was just concerned about collecting hearts for some "artificial" Kingdom Hearts.
  • Saïx: They gather in darkness, masterless and free...until they weave together to make Kingdom Hearts. And when that time comes, we can truly, finally exist.
    • Statement indicates that hearts form Kingdom Hearts of their own volition.
  • Maleficent: Kingdom Hearts belongs to me! The heart of all kingdoms, the heart of all that lives. A dominion fit to be called Kingdom Hearts must be MY dominion!
    • Statement is nonsensical if it's just an "artificial" Kingdom Hearts.
  • Sora: What do you guys really want?
  • Xaldin: ...Kingdom Hearts. When Kingdom Hearts is ours, we can exist fully and completely. So you see, Beast---that's why we need your Heartless AND your Nobody!
    • Again, statement makes no sense if Organization XIII was just concerned about collecting hearts for some "artificial" Kingdom Hearts.
  • Ansem the Wise: It's a device to reclaim Kingdom Hearts and encode it as data.
    • "Reclaim" indicates that Kingdom Hearts was claimed by Organization XIII previously, and Ansem seeks to get it "back".
  • Mickey: Kingdom Hearts...
    • Mickey wouldn't be showing concern if it was just "artificial".
  • Secret Ansem Report 13: Xehanort, who took everything away from me. Though as a Heartless he is no more, as the leader of Organization XIII his ambition once again is to capture Kingdom Hearts, the most colossal heart of all. ... His Nobody, however, is now almost finished gathering human hearts to be assimilated into Kingdom Hearts as well.
    • "Once again" indicates the same goal as Xehanort's Heartless: getting Kingdom Hearts. Not creating an "artificial" one. Both sentences would be logical place to put any mention of it being "artificial", but that is not the case. It is treated as if it were real.
  • Jiminy's Journal's story sections contain absolutely no mention of Kingdom Hearts being "artificial". For such a important plot point, there isn't any real mention of it.
  • For those who wish to convince themselves that they're somehow clever for "deciphering" the truth of Kingdom Hearts from absolutely nothing: this is the game that drops the main plot in three chunks of tell-all dialogue: at the beginning (Yen Sid), middle (Mickey), and end (Ansem). It's not going to suddenly become subtle in one particular aspect.

[edit] Nomura's line

The line is: [1]

ゼムナスが作つた "キングダムハーツ"

Zemanasu ka saku tsu ta "Kingdamu Hātsu"

The relevant part is saku, which, according to [2], may mean "make", "produce", "prepare", or "build". Or even "harvest". Clearly, the translator has to pick which word they believe is better. This line, especially not translated, isn't definitive proof of anything. Even more so when this doesn't state whether it's real.

It's also not a good idea to get definitive answers from some interview in Japanese; it's not actual story material, and both parties are unlikely to apply exact wording in causal conversation.

[edit] Larxene's "kunai"

Larxene's weapons are not kunai. To begin with, kunai are gardening tools; the persistent belief that they're inherent weapons arises from cruddy anime line Naruto. They also lack sharp edges, making them unsuitable to slash people, as Larxene is oft to do (and looking at them would confirm the sharp edge).

'Course, if this isn't goo enough, Ultimania contains the word "ナイフ" (naifu), instead of just using a more native word. Square Enix's judgement should be preferable than fan opinion tainted by crappy anime.
