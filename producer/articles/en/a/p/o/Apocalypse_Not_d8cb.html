[edit] Synopsis

Spoiler warning: Plot and/or ending details follow.

Prue, Piper and Phoebe try to relax and enjoy singer Paula Cole's performance at P3, but they soon find themselves back in action against the legendary Four Horsemen of the Apocalypse who are causing violent fights and road rage incidents all over San Francisco. When Prue and one of the Horsemen are sucked into a vortex between good and evil, Phoebe and Piper are forced to choose between saving their sister and saving the world. Meanwhile, Leo infiltrates the war room of the Horsemen, where he encounters organized evil.

[edit] Book of Shadows

[edit] Enemies

  • Four Horsemen of the Apocalypse: See Four Horsemen of the Apocalypse
  • The Demon of Cruelty: The work of this demon is particularly insidious. This demon hardens the heart and corrodes the soul, leaving the soul vulnerable to all manner of evil. The Demon of Cruelty is a vicious opponent and she must be approached with great caution. She can be disarmed only by one pure of heart. That one must place a garland containing three pearls around her neck, then invoke hope, joy and love to use the pearls to cleanse the demon of her cruelty.

[edit] Spells

  • To Summon the Demon of Anarchy: Sower of discord, your health we implore. I summon thee now with these words of truce.
  • To Vanquish the Demon of Anarchy: Sower of discord, your works must now cease. I vanquish thee now with these words of peace.

[edit] Trivia

  • The episode's title is a reference to the movie "Apocalypse Now".
  • Though the episode didn't center on the Demon of Cruelty, her background and means of vanquishing are shown.
  • Actor Brian Thompson, who plays War, later appears as the Titan Cronus in the two part season five finale Oh My Goddess.
  • This is Dorian Gregory's 25th episode on the show.
  • The Horsemen's symbol is Omega.
  • The Source is referred to as the Horsemen's boss and it is said The Source and the Elders communicate with one another.
  • In this episode, the Source actually saves the sisters by vanquishing the Horsemen and opening a vortex which allows Prue to return to the living.
  • In episodes before Shannen directs a "Charmed episode," Prue's roles are always diminished. For example, in Bride and Gloom, Prue has fewer lines than usual, and in Look Who's Barking, Prue is turned into a dog.
  • 65 individual crew members, not including production companies, were credited for this episode.
  • If you look closely at the pentagram when the camera switches back and forth between Leo, Piper, and Phoebe and the three remaining horsemen, the candle and statue keep changing places on the table.
