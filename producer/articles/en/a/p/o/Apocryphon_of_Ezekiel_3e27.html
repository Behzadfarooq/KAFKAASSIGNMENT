Apocryphon of Ezekiel

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Apocryphon of Ezekiel is an apocryphal book, written in the style of the Old Testament, as relevations of Ezekiel. It survives only in fragments including quotations in Epiphanus, Clement of Rome andClement of Alexandria, and the Chester Beatty Papyrus 185. It is likely to have been composed circa 50 BCE - 50 CE.

4QPseudo-Ezekiel, one of the Dead Sea Scrolls, is possibly further fragments of this text, or it may be a different work concerning Ezekiel, but it is difficult to tell.
