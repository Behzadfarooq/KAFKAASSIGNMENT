[edit] Original Apocrypha Discordia

I don't want anybody to misunderstand my edits and think I don't respect DrJon because I do! We owe him a lot for finding, compiling and releasing some great Discordian material. Hail DrJon!

But he didn't begin Apocrypha Discordia. It was mentioned as a concept in Steve Jackson Games edition of Principia Discordia published in 1994 (this is currently in this article, but I'm putting it here too because these articles change so fast). Reverend Loveshade submitted some writings, but the book was never published. So BloodStar posted stuff by Reverend Loveshade and others including Rev. Ivan Stang of the Church of the SubGenius as the non-existent Apocrypha Discordia in 1995. This was copied onto several Discordian sites.

When Rev. Loveshade learned of DrJon's book, which was produced years later, he voluntarily changed the name of his book to Ek-sen-trik-kuh Discordia, which is still evolving.

So the concept was first mentioned by somebody online in the 1990s, picked up and later dropped by Steve Jackson Games beginning in 1994, then created online by Reverend Loveshade in 1995, then compiled as a book finished in 2001 or 2002 (I've seen conflicting dates--does anyone, including DrJon, know the actual year?) A little more. The Apocrypha Discordia was created independently to what's now known as the Ek-sen-trik-kuh Discordia. The title was influenced by the mention by Steve Jackson (who himself got it off Russel Dalenberg), not by the previous name of the (subsequent) Ek-sen-trik-kuh Discordia, and the research for the work itself stretched back to the 1970s. Although one of the extant three short pieces from Rev.Loveshade's work was included, that piece shares space in the book with over a hundred other works - the primary ones being "apocryphal" but genuine contemperary passages omitted from the Principia Discordia (for which the Apocrypha was named) -- including a piece by Robert Anton Wilson which is also an adaption of the X blind men and an elephant story, and which dates from the 60s. But for whimsy, Rev. Loveshade's piece would not have been included at all. Please keep in mind also that three short pieces do not a book make (no offense - the defining feature of his "non-existent Apocrypha Discordia" was its non-existence: if it had existed, I would have chosen a different name, as indeed Rev. Loveshade eventually did). So yes, in fact, this Apocrypha Discordia was both begun and finished by me. The Ek-sen-trik-kuh Discordia (although no doubt a worthy work) is entirely incidental and wholly separate to this Apocrypha Discordia, which is the only Apocrypha Discordia to have ever actually been published. Which (since you asked) happened in 2001. Cheers, Drjon 14:37, 17 May 2006 (UTC)
