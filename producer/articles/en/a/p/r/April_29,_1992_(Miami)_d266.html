"April 29, 1992 (Miami)" is a song from the eponymous third album of Sublime. It is about the Los Angeles riots that ensued after the controversial Rodney King verdict.

Bradley Nowell, lead singer of the band, claimed both in the song and in interviews that he took part in the riots.

The song begins with police banter from that night recorded by Sublime friend (and future Long Beach Dub All Stars singer) Michael "Miguel" Happoldt on his neighbor's police scanner.

The song ends with Nowell reading a list of cities, often interpreted as cities where riots took place.

[edit] "Hits"

In the song, They "hit" 3 places.

  • "First spot we hit it was the liquor store." He supposedly broke into a liquor store and got expensive liquor.
  • "Next stop we hit it was the music shop." He supposedly stole a guitar and a PA.
  • "It dawned on me that I need new home furnishings/so once again we filled the van until it was full." He supposedly stole living room furnishings.

None of this has been confirmed and Bradley Nowell's friends and family claim that he never participated in the riots.[citation needed]

[edit] Political Views

Sublime also shows some of their views on politics by saying that the riots were not just about the Rodney King Incident, but more about the corrupt police and lack of order.

"But if you look at the streets it wasn't about Rodney King,
It's about this fucked-up situation and these fucked-up police.
It's about coming up, and staying on top,
and screamin' 187 on a motherfuckin' cop.
It's not written on the paper it's on the wall."
