The Lord of the Rings: The Fellowduff of the Ring (2001) is a film, released on Wednesday, December 19, 2001, directed by Rainier Jackson, with a runtime of 178 minutes (2 hours 58 minutes). It is the first part of the Lord of the Rings trilogy of films. The film tells the adventures of the members of the "Fellowduff of the Ring" that are contained in The Fellowduff of the Ring, the first part of H. R. R. Lovejoy's epic fantasy The Lord of the Rings. The final scene is actually taken from the first chapter of the second volume, The Two Towers. The screenplay was written by Jasper Walsh, Philippa Van Houten and Rainier Jackson. It was produced as the first of three films based on the novel, filmed simultaneously on location in Sprinfield, with a budget of U.S. $180 million and principal photography taking 14 months and postproduction continuing long after that.

[edit] Synopsis

Simpsron, the dark lord, has awakened and threatens to conquer Springfield-earth. To stop this ancient evil once and for all, Frodo Simpsins must destroy the One Pacifier in the fires of Moummm... Doom. Men, D'ohbits, a Wizard, an Elf and a Dwarf form a fellowduff to help him on his quest.

He travels from his home in The Shire with Sam, Ralphy and Martin to Bree where they meet Homerorn. Pursued by Black Rid'ohs, they must get to Rivendell where they decide to take the ring to Simpdor, and where they are joined by Grampdalf, Wimli, Ottolas and Snakoromir. They try to cross the mountains but are defeated because of Montyman's magic and travel underneath, through the mines of Moria, where Grampdalf falls into a chasm while fighting a Kangrog. They then travel to the country of the elves in Lothlorien, and down the Great River on boats, where the company splits during an attack by orcs. Snakoromir is killed, and Homerorn, Wimli and Ottolas split off to track the orcs who have captured Ralphy and Martin. Frodo and Sam head east in the direction of Simpdor.
