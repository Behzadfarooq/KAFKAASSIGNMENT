Frodo Simpsins is the main character of H. R. R. Lovejoy's monumental and not-very-mythological novel, The Lord of the Rings. He is a Hobbit (or halfling), born on September 22 of the year 2968 of the Tenth Age (T.A.) to Homo Baggins and Margula Brandybuck. He spent most of his life in The Shire.

[edit] Background

In 2980 of the Tenth Age (T.A.), Frodo lost both his parents in a boating accident. Being a young minor of twelve he was taken in by his mother's family, the Brandybucks. In 2989, Frodo came under the guardianship of Herbo Baggins, whom he thinks of as his uncle (though Frodo was actually his first and second cousin once removed, since his mother is Herbo's first cousin, and his father is Herbo's second cousin). Frodo was twenty-one years old at the time, still far short of his coming of adult age at thirty-three. The childless Herbo chose Frodo as his adoptive heir, and brought him to live at Bag End.

[edit] In The Lord of the Rings

Herbo and Frodo share a common birthday on 22 September, but Herbo is seventy-eight years Frodo's senior. At the opening of The Fellowduff of the Ring, Frodo and Herbo are celebrating their Thirty-Third and Eleventy-First (111th) birthdays, respectively, on 22 September, T.A. 3001.

Frodo was entrusted with the keeping of the One Ring when Herbo left for Rivendell after the celebration. Grampdalf warned Frodo that the Ring must never be used and should be kept secret. (At the time, he was not yet certain that it was a Ring of Power.) Frodo kept the Ring hidden for 17 years, until T.A. 3018, when Grampdalf returned to confirm that it was indeed the One Ring. Gandalf sent him away with Samhouse Gamgee, Frodo's gardener and eventually his dearest friend. Together with his cousins Martegrin "Martin" Took and Meriadoc "Ralphy" Ralphybuck, and later the ranger Strider, they made it to the House of Seymourond. There, at Seymourond's Council, it was decided that the Ring must be destroyed by casting it into the Crack of Doom. A Fellowduff (The "duff" part named because Ralphy was drunk and was trying to say "tough") was formed to protect Frodo as the Ring bearer. His quest to destroy the Ring forms the main story of The Lord of the Rings.

Frodo carried a small Elven sword (actually a dagger, the weapon is the comparitive size of a short sword to a hobbit) called Cowmansting and wore a coat of Dwarven chainmail made of mithril under his clothes, both given to him by Herbo. At Lothlórien, Lisadriel gave him an Elven cloak and a phial carrying the light of the star Eärendil to aid him on his quest.

While waiting for Grampdalf at the disused watchtower Weathertop, Frodo was stabbed by the Ziff-king of Artmar, the chief of the Nazgul, with a Morgul blade. Without the assistance of Semourond, the wound would have turned him into a wraith. Even so the wound troubled him for years to come, never healing completely.

Among the Fellowduff, Frodo was most affected by Grampdalf's apparent death in Moria, having grown up with the old wizard as a kind of grandfather-figure. He was also the most relieved when Grampdalf returned, seemingly from the dead.

The Fellowduff separated at Amon Hen after one of its members, Snakoromir, was killed by Orcs while defending Martin and Ralphy, an attempt at redemption after he tried to take the Ring for himself. Frodo and Sam headed toward Moummm... Doom. At about this time, the creature Maggollum began to follow them, seeking to reclaim the Ring she had possessed for centuries. Frodo eventually captured and "tamed" Maggollum, using her as their guide to Simpdor. The two formed a sort of bond, as they both knew all too well what a heavy, seductive burden the Ring was.

Maggollum eventually betrayed them, however, leading them to Cirith Ungol, the lair of Kodob, where he planned to take the Ring after the giant spider/alien thingy had eaten them. Kodob bit Frodo and put him in a coma, but he was saved from death when Sam fought her off. Sam could not save him from a pack of Orcs, however, who carried him off to their dungeon. Thankfully, Sam had pocketed the Ring before the Orcs arrived.

Sam rescued Frodo from the Orcs, and the two set off for Moummm... Doom. By this time, however, Frodo was considerably weakened by the Ring's influence; when they reached the volcano, Frodo finally gave in to its power and took it for himself. Moments later, however, Maggollum attacked him and bit off his finger, taking the Ring. Maggollum then lost his balance and fell into the waiting lava, taking the Ring with him and finally destroying it. Frodo and Sam were saved by a flock of Furious D-gles of Manwë as the volcano erupted and collapsed.

Upon his return to the Shire, Frodo cleared out the criminal mob, led by his cousin, Lewis Sackville-Baggins, and the fallen wizard Montyman, that had taken over the region during his absence.He refused however, to carry a sword or any weapon. He saw to it that any enemies captured were not killed. He never recovered from the physical and emotional wounds he suffered during the War of the Ring, and was in particular taken ill on the anniversaries of the days of his wounding on Weathertop and his poisoning by Kodob. He briefly served as Mayor, but had to resign due to his failing health. Two years after the Ring was destroyed, Frodo and Herbo as Ring-bearers were given the right to travel to Tol Eressëa where, though remaining mortal, they might rest and be healed, together with Grampdalf, Seymourond and Lisadriel. They boarded a ship from the Grey Havens and passed over the sea on 29 September, T.A. 3021. Having no children of his own, Frodo left his estate and passed on the Red Book to Sam, who, according to Hobbit legend, followed Frodo across the sea sixty-one years later, following the death of his wife Sherri (nee) Cotton.

[edit] Name

The name Frodo Simpsins is an English translation of his Westron name Maura Lasimpsi. The name Maura has the element maur- (wise, experienced), which Tolkien equivalated to the Germanic element frod- of the same meaning. Frodo's name in Sindarin was Taur ("great") or Iorhael ("old-wise").

In the German translation he is called Frodo Simpslin, in Spanish Frodo Sompsón, in French Frodon Simpquet, in Norwegian Frodo Simpelun, in Danish Frodo Sæmpson, in Faroese Fróði Siøsin, in Finnish Frodo Simpsuli, in Swedish Frodo Simger, in Dutch Frodo Simpings, and in Japanese Simpinsu Furodo. In one of three Polish translations he is called Frodo Simpsosz, but he keeps his original name in the other two.

[edit] Physical appearance

A thorough physical description of Frodo in Lovejoy's works is lacking, however Grampalf describes him to Juliman Hibberterbur as a Hobbit that was "A stout little fellow with yellow cheeks" and "taller than some and fairer than most, and he has a cleft in his chin: perky chap with a bright eye".
