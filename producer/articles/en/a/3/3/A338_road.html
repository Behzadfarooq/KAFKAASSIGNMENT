The A338 is a road in southern England, that runs from Bournemouth in Dorset to Bessels Leigh in Oxfordshire.

[edit] Route of Road

The road begins in central Bournemouth, before heading northward as a grade separated dual carriageway, one of the main roads in the South East Dorset conurbation. It joins the route of the A31 road heading north-east for 1Â½ miles, before turning left at Ringwood in Hampshire and again heading northward. It passes the town of Fordingbridge, before crossing the border into Wiltshire. It meets the A354 road at a roundabout on the southern outskirts of Salisbury, before joining the route of the A36 road as it bypasses the city centre to dual carriageway standard. It meets the A30 road at a roundabout, before continuing in a north-easterly direction. It has a junction with the main A303 road, which it crosses, and then travels through the town of Tidworth. It turns right at a roundabout with the A346 road at Burbage, and heads east then north-east crossing the border into Berkshire. It passes through the town of Hungerford, where it meets the A4 road (to Marlborough and Newbury). It meets the M4 motorway at junction 14, and heads roughly northward into Oxfordshire. It goes through Wantage where it meets the A417 road, and past the villages of Grove, East Hanney and Frilford Heath. It terminates at its junction with the A420 road near Bessels Leigh, six miles south-west of Oxford.

The part of the A338 south of the A31 consists mostly of relatively newly built road. Originally, the A338 went from Ringwood to Christchurch along what is now the B3347.
