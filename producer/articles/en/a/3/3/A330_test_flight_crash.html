F-WWKH was the registration of a Airbus A330-300 which crashed while doing a test flight.

[edit] Background To Crash

The aircraft was being tested on its performance in critical take-off conditions. The test that the aircraft was doing a simulated engine failure after take off. This meant shutting down one of the Pratt & Whitney PW4000 engines and closing down a hydraulic circuit. Also part of test was to use the autopilot to climb to an altitude of 2000 feet. The aircraft was being taken off and landing at Toulouse airport

[edit] The Crew

In total there were 7 passengers and crew, 3 were crew and 4 were passengers there were 2 Airbus employees and 2 Alitalia employees, they were selected by Alitalia to observe the A330 which they were considering buying.

Crew

  • Nicholas Warner, chief test pilot and captain. Born in Colchester England on June 7, 1943, he had 7713 flying hours
  • Michel Cais, co-pilot. Born in Paris France on November 4, 1940, he had 9558 flying hours
  • Jean Pierre Petit, engineer. Born in Boulogne-sur-Mer France on August 23, 1943, he had 6225 flying hours

Passengers

  • Alberto Nassetti, Alitalia pilot
  • Pier Paolo Racchetti, Alitalia pilot
  • Philippe Tournoux, Airbus employee
  • Keith Hulse, Airbus employee

[edit] The Crash

The aircraft had just successfully completed a landing in extreme weather conditions and was in the process of completing a take off in the same conditions. The aircraft was flown by the co-pilot while the captain shut off the engine, hydraulic circuit and engaged the autopilot. The aircraft took off successfully and the captain shut off the engine and hydraulic circuit. However there was a problem with the autopilot as it took several attempts to engage it. When the autopilot finally engaged the aircraft started to ascend to 2000 ft. However the aircraft rose too sharply and began losing speed, the speed decreased to 100 knots while the minimum speed for controlling the aircraft is 118 knots. The aircraft started to roll and bank so the crew reduced power to the second engine to try and compensate however this made the problem worse and the aircraft pitched down by 15 degrees and crashed into the ground.

[edit] Reasons for crash

There were many reasons for the crash one is pilot error mainly the captain having too many things to do in such a short space of time and pilots setting the altitude tow low for such a dangerous test. Also another reason is the autopilot not working correctly mainly because the crew did not know how to use it properly but the autopilot was using experimental software, the software was being tested in Category III this means testing the autopilot on take off and landing in many different situations. The autopilot was also be in control of speed and angle of attack however this vital part was malfunctioning.

[edit] The future of F-WWKH

After the completion of its tests the aircraft was going to be sold on to Thai airways.
