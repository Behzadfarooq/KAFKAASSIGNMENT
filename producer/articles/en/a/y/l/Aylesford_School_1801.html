Aylesford School and Language College is a secondary school in Warwick, England, located on Shelley Avenue.

Aylesford is a mixed-sex school comprised of years 7 to 11, with an added Sixth Form making up years 12 and 13. The school has several awards, such as its status as a Language College, resulting in a focus on languages as a subject within the school, including lesser-taught languages such as Japanese and Punjabi. Italian is also taught after school.

Roughly 1,000 pupils attend the school, with around 80-100 staff providing teaching skills, learning support, administration, and general maintenance work.

Its current Headteacher is Mr Steven Hall who took over from Ms Susan Dudley in 2005, who left to pursue charity work. So far the changeover has been smooth, and Mr Hall recently celebrated GCSE results equivalent to the best ever achieved by the school.

Upcoming events in Aylesford School include the annual 6th form charity week, which raises money for several charities and is highly successful. In addition, the school has recently added the title of Music College to its already sizeable list of awards and credentials. Work is due to begin in the future on a new road and entranceway to the school in order to better suit its growing student body.
