Aylmer "Alymer" "A.J.E" John Eggert Liesemer (born: November 27, 1905 Didsbury, Alberta was a politician and teacher and is currently retired from Alberta, Canada.

[edit] Early life

Aylmer was born in Didsbury, Alberta in 1905. He recived all of his early public gradeschool in Didsbury with the exception of 1 year in Calgary.

In 1921 and 1922, Mr. Liesemer attended Normal School in Calgary at what became the Administration Building for the Southern Alberta Institute of Technology. He was one of the first class of graduates from that school.

Aylmer became a teacher and taught in Didsbury and Calgary. Aylmer started teaching High School students in 1930 through to his retirement in 1970, he was an instructor in history, geography and social studies at Crescent Heights High School in Calgary. He kept teaching while still performing his duties in the public office's he held.

[edit] Muncipal politics

Aylmer served as an Alderman for the city of Calgary from 1934 to 1937. In 1937 he challenged Incumbent Mayor Andrew Davison but failed in his bid to defeat the mayor.

[edit] Provincial career

Aylmer Liesemer first ran as a Labor politician in the 1935 Alberta general election and was badly defeated finishing 15th out of 20 candidates.

Aylmer would run again in the 1944 Alberta general election this time as a candidate for the Cooperative Commonwealth he would succeed winning the 5th spot in the Single Transferable Vote. Aylmer would be relection to a second term in the 1948 Alberta general election and ultimatly defeated after serving two terms in the 1952 Alberta general election.

Aylmer ran in Calgary North in the 1959 Alberta general election but was badly defeated by incumbent Rose Wilkinson from Social Credit.

Aylmer is currently retired and lives in Calgary.
