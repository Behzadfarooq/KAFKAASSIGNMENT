Ayu Tsukimiya (月宮 あゆ, Tsukimiya Ayu?) is a fictional character from the Japanese visual novel Kanon, voiced by Yui Horie. She is the main female character in the series' marketing, followed closely by Nayuki Minase. Ayu is a short, strange, and mysterious girl that literally bumped into Yuichi early in the story. She is immediately recognizable by her winged backpack, red headband, and tendency to refer to herself with the masculine pronoun boku (僕, boku?), meaning "I". Ayu is notorious for her catch phrase, "Ugū!" (うぐぅ, "Ugū!"?), which she mutters as an expression of various negative emotions such as frustration, anger, and fear.

Yuichi and Ayu become fast friends after she meets him several more times in the shopping district, though the truth is that they first met and became friends during Yuichi's last stay in the city seven years previous. She comes off as somewhat strange, such as initially tackling Yuichi and yelling "Yuichi-kun~!" whenever she spots him. These tackling fits eventually diminish in severity to simple taps on the back as time goes on or even simply calling out his name when appearing seemingly out of nowhere behind him.

Yuichi quickly discovers how terrified Ayu will become at even the mere mention of ghosts or demons. When put into an uncomfortable situation, such as having to walk alone at night, she is noticeably jumpy and is prone to screaming. A main point in her storyline is that Ayu is constantly searching for something she has lost, and even she does not know what it is. Yuichi plays an active role by helping her try to find whatever it is, though is initially unsuccessful.

[edit] Story

[edit] Seven years ago

Ayu Tsukimiya was a young girl that Yuichi Aizawa knew seven years prior to the beginning of the story. He met her crying in the shopping district, where she introduced herself as "Ayu"; when asked for her surname, she also said "Ayu". To get her to calm down, he bought her some taiyaki, her favorite food. Before parting, the friendless "Ayu Ayu" asked Yuichi to promise to meet her again, so they could eat taiyaki together. Yuichi came back every day after that and they eventually became very close friends. One day, Ayu came across a claw vending machine, and a small angel doll caught her eye. Yuichi eventually won it for her, and when he gave it to her he declared that the doll would grant three wishes for her. However, it had to be a wish that he could grant.

Ayu in the tree she loved to climb.

Ayu's first wish was that they could go to school together. Although Yuichi could not fully grant that wish, as he lived far away, he told her that the forest could be their school. The meeting place would be a large tree they liked to play around, and climb on. They could come whenever they wanted, there would be no homework, they could eat whatever they wanted for lunch (Ayu always ate taiyaki), and there would be no teachers to boss them around. Ayu's second wish was a much more simple one, as she asked simply that Yuichi would never forget her. Ayu then wanted to save her third wish for the future, so she and Yuichi buried the doll in a glass jar, much like a time capsule. She wanted somebody who "wanted the doll with all their heart" to find it, and get the final wish.

One day, while Yuichi and Ayu were attending their "school", Ayu was sitting up in the tree when a gust of wind came and knocked her out of it. She ended up falling on her head, which caused her to go into a seven-year-long coma. However, from the revelation of the accident to the ending of the series, the viewer is led to believe that she is dead. This accident is what caused the young Yuichi to suppress his memories of his childhood.

[edit] Present

The Ayu that appears in the story is really a manifestation of her dream, and her wish to stay with Yuichi. Throughout the series, there are a lot of hints that she is stuck in the past, such as not knowing what a mobile phone was and believing that businesses are still as they were in the past. Akiko Minase, Yuichi's aunt, is taken aback by the appearance of Ayu, takes note of everything that Yuichi remembers, and muses that Ayu must have been waiting for him.

Yuichi then begins to wonder about this mysterious Ayu. He only barely recalls her being his friend from the past, and forgot about the accident all those years ago. One day, Yuichi and Ayu go out to the movies where they watch a horror movie. Ayu is terrified during the entire showing, most of which she spent under her coat or with her eyes closed.

Eventually, it is decided that Ayu should start living in the Minase house as one of the family, and when everyone agrees, Ayu happily joins her new family. During this time, Yuichi believes that she goes to a different school which she said was in the forest. This is in fact the "school" that they had promised each other existed seven years previous; at this point, Ayu truly believed that school existed. When Akiko falls ill, Ayu starts to panic despite it not being that serious. This goes back to Ayu's own mother dying from an unnamed illness, and Ayu was afraid the same thing would happen to Akiko.

One day, he asks her where she goes to school, and Ayu happily takes him to the tree. Once she realises that there is no school there, reality begins to hit her and she realises that she is not really there either. This implies that Ayu can only continue living in her dream, as long as she believes in it. During this period of realization, Ayu frantically tries to search for the lost doll, even going as far as to use her hands to dig in the snow to unearth it. After not finding the doll, her fake reality crashes and she disappears.

Ayu about to make her third wish on the doll.

After this, Yuichi finally remembers what happened all those years ago. He remembers that Ayu 'died' and he goes into depression. During this time, Yuichi remembers the doll that Ayu buried, and gathers his friends to go find it. Once he gets it, and his friends leave, he stays at the forest until Ayu appears again. They have a tearful reunion, where Yuichi reminds her of the angel doll, and that she has one more wish. Yuichi asks her to use her final wish to come back and go to a real school with Yuichi. Ayu brings up another option; she might wish that Yuichi not remember her at all, and move on. After this, Ayu does not say which wish she chooses, and simply vanishes again.

(In the anime adaptations, Nayuki Minase's story is coming to a close and Akiko has just been hit by a car, so Ayu says she wants to use her last wish to make a miracle, and to save Akiko. Yuichi then brings up the option that she save herself, and the story continues as normal.)

At this point, the story cuts off in the game if the player, as Yuichi, has not chosen all the correct answers for Ayu's storyline. This is the bad ending, and the player is left to believe that Ayu is either dead or forever comatose.

Yuichi is then ready to leave this town, and is just about on the train to go back home, when Nayuki runs up to him and tells him that somebody has been waiting for him for a long time in the hospital. He races over there to find Ayu in a coma. When he holds her hand, Ayu finally wakes up after all those years. Following this, Ayu recovers to the point where she is released from the hospital.

[edit] Cultural impact

Ayu, Nayuki and Makoto from the Air anime.

Ayu has appeared in many works outside of Kanon, has been stated as the one dating sim character with the most appearances outside her own game, and has come to represent Kanon itself. Below are some examples of parody, homage and inspiration drawn from Ayu.

  • Ayu has appeared in countless dōjin games such as Glove on Fight and Eternal Fighter Zero.
  • Megatokyo, a webcomic that takes much of its inspiration from Kanon, references Ayu. First, in strip #67 "Saving Points",[1] Ayu is shown eating taiyaki. This is, in fact, a reference to a Kanon dōjinshi called Quarter Iceshop, as stated by Megatokyo's artist, Fred Gallagher. This was stated in his commentary during the publication of volume one of his webcomic, as well as the "rants" accompanying the strip itself. However, the lack of information presented by the book (while the rant links to the comic and mentions Kanon, the book does no such thing) has led many to believe that Quarter Iceshop has nothing to do with Kanon and, in turn, that the girl is an original character and not Ayu at all. Also, in strip #224 "packing away",[2] Gallagher draws himself wearing Ayu's backpack and mentioning her catchphrase "Ugū!", as well as stating that the backpack was the "official winged backpack from Kanon".
  • Magical Shopping Arcade Abenobashi has one episode that parodies Kanon and other eroge. In it, Yui Horie, Ayu's voice actor, plays a girl that is a clear take-off on Ayu.
  • Air, the anime adaptation of another of Key's video games, inserts Ayu into the story. Here, along with Nayuki Minase and Makoto Sawatari, Ayu is a regular girl and Kano Kirishima's friend from school.
  • Ayu has been referred to as "the original sad girl in snow", and many scenes featuring such girls borrow from Ayu's story and those of the other Kanon girls.
