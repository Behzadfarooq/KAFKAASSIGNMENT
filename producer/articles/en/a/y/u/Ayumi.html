Ayumi

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Ayumi is a female Japanese given name. The kanji of Ayumi (歩- Ayu.mu) comes from the Chinese word (步- Ayu.mu); they're similar but not the same. Ayumi is most commonly written as:

  • あゆみ — in hiragana, the most common way.
  • あゆ美 — mixture of hiragana and kanji; this is rarely used, but nowadays you can see this form increasing.
  • 歩 — literally means step or walk, but for names is understood as walker. Most parents that give their child this name wants a child that is independent, that can walk on her own.
  • 歩美 — "歩" means walker and "美": beauty.
  • 歩実 - "歩" means walker and "実": reality.
  • 歩弓 - "歩" means walker and "弓": archery.
  • 安祐美 - "安" means relax, "祐" means help and "美": beauty.
  • 亜弓 - "亜" means Asia and "弓": archery.
  • 亜由美 - This name does not have a meaning. It is just phonetic.
  • 亜裕美 - "亜" means Asia, "裕" means bountiful, rich and "美": beauty.
  • 鮎美 - "鮎" means sweet trout and "美": beauty.
  • 優美 - "優" means gentle and "美": beauty.
  • 彩友美 - "彩" mean colorful, "友" means friend and "美": beauty.

Ayumi literally means step or walk, but for names is understood as walker.

It does also exist as a surname (漢人, 阿弓)
