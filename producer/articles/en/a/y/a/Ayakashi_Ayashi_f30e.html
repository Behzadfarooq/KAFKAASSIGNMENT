Tenpō Ibun Ayakashi Ayashi (天保異聞 妖奇士, Tenpō Ibun Ayakashi Ayashi?) is a Japanese anime series created by Bones and Mainichi Broadcasting System, Inc. (MBS). Written by Sho Aikawa, directed by Hiroshi Nishikiori and with character designs by Toshihiro Kawamoto, the series premiered across Japan on 6:00 PM, October 7, 2006 on TBS, in the same time slot previously occupied by anime series such as Blood+, Fullmetal Alchemist and both series of the Gundam Seed franchise. It received its satellite television premiere across Japan on Animax on November 14, 2006.

[edit] Plot

Spoiler warning: Plot and/or ending details follow.

In 1843, the fourteenth year of the Tenpō Era, ten years before the arrival of Commodore Matthew Perry and the Black Ships, Edo is under attack by beasts from the underworld, known as Yōi (妖夷, Yōi?). Members of the Bansha Aratamesho, called the Ayashi (奇士, Ayashi?), are assembled to fight against the beasts.

[edit] Characters

[edit] Bansha Aratamesho Ayashi

Ryūdō Yukiatsu (竜導往壓, Ryūdō Yukiatsu?)
Voiced by: Keiji Fujiwara
The protagonist, a 39-year-old drifter with the power of Ayagami. He goes by the name of "Yuki". Originally a son of a Hatamoto Samurai, he left home 25 years ago. He is the only person known to have visited the "Other World" and returned.
Ogasawara Hōzaburō (小笠原放三郎, Ogasawara Hōzaburō?)
Voiced by: Tokuyoshi Kawashima
The 20-year-old leader of the Bansha Aratamesho. He is also a scholar in Rangaku (蘭学, Rangaku? lit. Western studies).
Edo Genbatsu (江戸元閥, Edo Genbatsu?)
Voiced by: Shin'ichirō Miki
A cross-dressing Shinto priest. He fights using several different firearms.
Saizō (宰蔵, Saizō?)
Voiced by: Michi Nino
A young girl who dresses as a boy because of her background in the theatre, where women are not allowed to perform. She fights using a paper fan which unfolds to become a long rope of paper. She was also born with the power to perform a mysterious dance which attracts Yōi.
Abi (アビ, Abi?)
Voiced by: Rikiya Koyama
A man from the mountains, fights using a spear which can split mid-throw into four lesser, seemingly magical spears. He seems to have a close relationship with Edo Genbatsu.

[edit] South Edo Magistrate

Torii Yōzō (鳥居耀蔵, Torii Yōzō?)
Voiced by: Norio Wakamoto
The South Edo Magistrate, Lord of "Kai". A strong supporter of the Chief Elder Mizuno Tadakuni Tenpo Reforms.
Honjō Tatsusuke (本庄辰輔, Honjō Tatsusuke?)
Voiced by: Seiji Sasaki
A retainer of Torii Yōzō. He works under him as spy.
Hanai Toraichi (花井虎一, Hanai Toraichi?)
Voiced by: Tomohiro Nishimura
A Rangaku scholar.
Matsue Sote (松江ソテ, Matsue Sote?)
Voiced by: Shoko Tsuda
A relative of Honjō Tatsusuke.

[edit] Shogunate government

Atobe Yoshisuke (跡部良弼, Atobe Yoshisuke?)
Voiced by: Takaya Hashi
Biological brother of Mizuno Tadakuni, the financial magistrate and political opponent of Torii Yōzō. Ogasawara's superior.
Abe Masahiro (阿部正弘, Abe Masahiro?)
Voiced by: Kenji Hamada
Appointed as Elder at age 25. He negotiated on behalf of the Shogun government with Matthew Perry.
Toyama Kagemoto (遠山景元, Toyama Kagemoto?)
Recently been dismissed from the post of North Edo Magistrate and has been transferred to the post of Chief Censor. Political opponent of Torii Yōzō.

[edit] Other characters

Atoru (アトル, Atoru?)
Voiced by: Fumiko Orikasa
An Aztec girl who works in a circus alongside her massive horse, Yukiwa (雪輪, Yukiwa?).
Kumoshichi (雲七, Kumoshichi?)
Voiced by: Yuji Ueda
Yuki's friend. No one except Yuki can see him. He is actually a Yōi created by Yuki. After fusing with another Yōi, Quetzalcoatl, he takes the shape of a talking horse.
Ōta (央太, Ōta?)
Voiced by: Chinami Nishimura
A youngster offered in sacrifice to a Yōi, but escaped with her mother.
Tae (たえ, Tae?)
Voiced by: Aya Hisakawa
Ōta's mother.
Tamahei (玉兵, Tamahei?)
Voiced by: Wizumi
A bumbling auxiliary policeman (Okappiki) obssessed with catching Yuki.

[edit] Cancellation

Tenpō Ibun Ayakashi Ayashi, being in the 6pm Saturday primetime slot on MBS and TBS in Japan, was originally slated to be 52 episodes. However, due to bad ratings during its broadcast run, the series' length was cut in half (into 25 episodes). A new anime series titled Toward the Terra... will take over the timeslot, starting this April.

[edit] Media

Tenpō Ibun Ayakashi Ayashi was first created as an original anime series which was later adapted into a manga.

[edit] Anime

The anime series of Tenpō Ibun Ayakashi Ayashi first aired in Japan on October 7, 2006.
