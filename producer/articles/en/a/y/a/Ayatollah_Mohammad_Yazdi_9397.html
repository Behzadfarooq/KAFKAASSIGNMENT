Ayatollah Mohammad Yazdi

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Not to be confused with Mohammad Taqi Mesbah Yazdi or Mirza Mohammad Farrokhi Yazdi.

Ayatollah Mohammad Yazdi (In Persian: محمد یزدی) was head of Judiciary System of Iran between 1989 and 1999, following Ayatollah Abdolkarim Mousavi Ardebili and succeeded by Ayatollah Mahmoud Hashemi Shahroudi.

Yazdi is now a member of Guardian Council. He has served as the interim friday prayer leader of Tehran. During Iranian elections December 15, 2006 Ayatollah Mohammad Yazdi was a candidate from Jame Modarresin and Jame Rohaniyat (the conservative list, rival to Ayatollah Mohammad Taqi Mesbah-Yazdi) [1]
