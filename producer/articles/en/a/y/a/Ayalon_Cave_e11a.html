Ayalon Cave

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

The Ayalon Cave is a large underground limestone cave located near Ramla, Israel. The cave, 300 feet deep, with its branch extends almost 2.5 kilometres making it the second largest limestone cave in Israel. Researchers announced that they have so far discovered eight new species, all without eyes, including four crustacean species and four terrestrial species, previously unknown to science. They added that the crustaceans included two saltwater species and two freshwater species, indicating that this diversity might help better understand the water history of the region.

The cave was first discovered when a small opening was discerned in a quarry near Ramla. According to Professor Amos Frumkin of the Hebrew University, the cave is unique in that a thick layer of chalk left it impermeable to water.

The cave will remained closed to the public to allow scientific investigation to continue undisturbed.
