Aydon Castle is a fortified manor house rather than a castle near to the town of Corbridge, Northumberland (grid reference NZ001663).

It was built by Robert de Reymes, a wealthy Suffolk merchant, starting in 1295, adjacent to the steep valley of the Cor Burn. It was captured by the Scots in 1315 and again in 1346. In the middle of the 16th century it was renovated and in the middle of 17th century it was converted into a farm. The building remained in use as a farm until 1966, but has since been restored to its medieval appearance.

It remained in private ownership until 1975 and is now owned by English Heritage.
