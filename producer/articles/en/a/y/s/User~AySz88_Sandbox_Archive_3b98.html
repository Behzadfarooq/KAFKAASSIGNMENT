[edit] New hurricane color palatte

Talk at: Talk:2005 Atlantic hurricane season statistics#New colors

  • Weaker = cooler colors
  • Stronger = warmer colors
  • <= Cat1: low saturation
  • >= Cat3: high saturation
  • No white, no green
  • No bright colors at lower categories

[edit] More examples

At [1] are all the storm images with the new colors.

I'm not entirely happy with the new colors, but I can't say exactly way. Maybe I'm just not used to them. Maybe they're too "pastel"...but this may be needed since they're used as backgrounds for text also. It's also a little hard to distinguish TD/TS/Cat1 I think. Jdorje 00:44, 10 December 2005 (UTC)

It seems to work great for strong storms (Rita) and well-behaved (intensity-wise) moderate storms (Jeanne and Irene). But also seems to have trouble with storms that bounce between 1 and 2 (Marilyn 1995) or stay low but has dots far apart (Alpha). It dosen't work that well for weak storms (Lee), though that might be expected, since it doesn't matter much there. Not sure what to do with the 1 and 2; maybe I'll have a go at it tomorrow (too sleepy now to do anything reliable). --AySz88^-^ 04:24, 10 December 2005 (UTC)
