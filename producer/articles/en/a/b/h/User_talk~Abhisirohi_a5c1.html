Abhi Sirohi March 2nd S.S P.1 Westward Expansion

Through the 1800’s till the 1900’s a lot of very important events occurred in the United States history. The Westward Expansion was one of the most important of these events. The Westward Expansion brought America land. The travels of Lewis and Clasrk brought about extensive changes in the United States. The louisiana purchase opened up the West for America. The Louisiana Purchase was gave America 529,911,681 acres of land from France in 1803. The cost of this was 3 cents per acre, 15 million dollars. per acre. The lands purchased contained Arkansas, Missouri, Iowa, Minnesota west of the Mississippi River, North Dakota, South Dakota, Nebraska, New Mexico, northern Texas, Oklahoma, Kansas, the portions of Montana, Wyoming, and Colorado east of the Rocky Mountains, the portions of southern Manitoba, southern Saskatchewan and southern Alberta that drain into the Missouri River, and Louisiana on both sides of the Mississippi River including the city of New Orleans. All this land together creates about 22.3 percent of present day United States. The Oregon Trail was an overland migration route which was over 2,000 miles west through Missouri, Kansas, Nebraska, Wyoming, Idaho and Oregon. The Oregon Trail was finally completed in 1841 and used by long distance travelers until 1869, when the Transcontinental railway had been completed. The mexican cession gave America land after the war. The United States still payed 20 million dollars for the land. In conclusion the westward expansion gave america land and if it had not happened we would not have as much land as we do now.
