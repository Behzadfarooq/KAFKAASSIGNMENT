Syed Abid Ali Abid, M.A., L.L.B. , Urdu and Persian critic, poet, former principal Dyal Singh College, Lahore, Pakistan was born on 17 September 1906 at Dera Ismail Khan, Pakistan and died in Lahore, Pakistan on 20 January 1971.

[edit] Life

He was very famous for his literary criticism and had hundreds of students, many of them now themselves poets and writers. He wrote lot of books on literary criticism in Urdu and Persian. He also started and was editor of some literary journals like Sahifa-Lahore. He also was one of the initial drama writers and feature writers at the newly established Radio Pakistan Lahore in the late 1940s and 1950s. He also was the story and dialogue writers of the first sound film (talking film) of the Punjab Heer Ranjha (1931). He survived 3 heart attacks but succumbed on the 4th.
