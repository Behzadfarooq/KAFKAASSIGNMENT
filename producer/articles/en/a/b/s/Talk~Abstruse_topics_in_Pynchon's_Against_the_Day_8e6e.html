[edit] Initial discussion

To be honest, the "abstruse subjects", especially the ones about history, didn't strike me as "abstruse". For example, the articles under the "Mexican Revolution" sub-section are subjects that are bound to be mentioned when the writer talks about the Mexican Revolution - and I don't find them more "abstruse" than the "Mexican Revolution" described in some authentic history research books. I would suggest giving excerpts and writing some prose regarding every one of the abstruse subjects listed out here, and showing why they are abstruse and how they are mentioned in the book. Otherwise there's no way of defining, for example, how one topic is more abstruse than the other. Another thing: Constantinople, I think, is called "Istanbul" in modern times. Moreover it's not an European geographical location - it's both European and Asian. Mexico is in North America by some definitions, but more precisely it's in Central America. I have made changes regarding these to the article. Another question: What "Macedonia" is the list referring to? The current one lists to a disambiguation page, which gives three definitions of Macedonia - the historical region, the region in Greece and the modern country. I have also removed "Landmines" from the list of Abstruse subjects, which is too general (like, "pencils" and "rulers") unless you can give an example of how the book specifically talked about landmines.User:Aranherunar|heru|nar 07:56, 16 December 2006 (UTC)

I'm changing back some of these edits, keeping some others:
  1. Mexico is in North America by every definition. "Central America" is sometimes used, but that introduces confusion becuse the nations from Guatamala to Costa Rica are usually considered the bounds of Central America. Also, there is no need to say "Americas". If we have information that South America is a location, we can change it to Americas if that is found to be convenient.
  2. As a matter of convenience, we can put things like "Mexican Revolution" and "Marco Polo" in lists with abstruse topics. The "topic" of "Mexican Revolution" may not be abstruse at all, but information to be found in those articles may be.
  3. "Constantinople" was the name used in the book, apparently. The standard for this is what would be easy for the reader. I have no objection to your putting in "Istanbul" in parentheses, but if Pynchon says "Constantinople" then we help the reader by saying "Constantinople." Moreover, it's in Europe — consult any geography book.
  4. I have no idea which "Macedonia" is referred to. Leave it in and let the reader figure it out, or wait for a reader who knows to make a change. Until then, the "Macedonia" link is of some use.
  5. Landmines — like "Macedonia" it's referenced in the book apparently, and keep it in the topics as an aid to the reader until some reader who knows that a more specific topic or no reference at all is needed.

In general, these topics have been put in because some book review has said they were mentioned in the book. If you have any information showing that the topic is irrelevant, then we can talk. Until then, each topic stays.Noroton 22:57, 17 December 2006 (UTC)
