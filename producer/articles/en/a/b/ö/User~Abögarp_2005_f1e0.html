[edit] Encinas, mayors and delegados

Hi Abögarp, regarding your question about mayors and delegados:

  1. The Mexican Federal District is, legally speaking, a city (Mexico City, see Art. 44 of the Mexican Constitution and the Art. 2 of Mexico City's statute). Therefore, the jefe de gobierno del distrito federal = mayor, and as such is named by most English-speaking media (check out The Economist, the BBC, The L.A. Times, The Washington Post or The New York Times ). Clearly, a delegado can't be called a mayor.
  2. Chief administrator is a convention used here in Wikipedia when dealing with the delegados. I am not 100% happy with it but until we find something better, I think we should keep using it for the sake of coherence.

Cheers, Ruiz 23:59, August 8, 2005 (UTC)

[edit] Cats

Hi Abögarp, I just read your comments. Obviously, I don't mind adding cats to those articles, but the way they were organized before was a bit confusing:

a) The cat Mexican state governors was under Mex. Exec. Branch and Mexican governors are not members of the Mexican Exec. branch (they don't take orders from the president), they are members of their own state exec. branch, which is independent. In addition, all Mexican governors are state governors so I decided to abrv. the cat name.

b) Your comments on George W. Bush, etc. are correct, but please notice that in Category:State governors of the U.S. (which is the equiv. of what we have) only the "Governors of XYZ" subcats are listed, not the "Governor of XYZ" articles. It makes sense because otherwise we would have 31 subcats and 31 articles about the same topic in the same cat.

c)Articles such as Governor of Texas are classified in cats such as Category:Governors of Texas, just like we have them now.

Cheers, Ruiz 07:59, 22 September 2005 (UTC)
