The Soviet Socialist Republic of Abkhazia (abbreviated Abkhazian SSR) (Russian: Советская Социалистическая Республика Абхазия) was a shortlived (1921-1931) Soviet Republic. It was established on March 31st of 1921, and its independence was recognised by the Georgian SSR on May 21st.[2] In February 1922 a union treaty was signed between the Abkhazian and the Georgian SSR, forging confederal ties, and it was along with the Georgian SSR that Abkhazia joined the Transcaucasian SFSR on the 12th of March. Finally, as part of the Transcaucasian SFSR, Abkhazia joined the Soviet Union when it formed on December 30th of 1922.

[edit] Background

In 1916 the Abkhaz delegation arrived in Tbilisi to develop the cultural and economic projects while addressing their opposition to the separating church in Abkhazia from the Eparchy of the Georgian church. The concerns were addressed by the leader of Abkhaz delegation, Prince Shervashidze.[3]In 1917, the same members of Abkhaz delegation established the Abkhaz People’s Council (ACP) which was dominated by ethnic Abkhaz. The Assembly adopted the constitution of APC and the Declaration. On February 9, 1918, before adoption of the Declaration of the Independence of Georgia, the APC delegation arrived in Tbilisi to finilize the agreement between APC and the Georgian Government. The APC and the Menshevik Government of Noe Ramishvili agreed to grant Abkhazia the autonomous status within Georgia.[4] The documents and the new constitution was ratified in June of 1918, signed by the Menshevik government of Georgia and representatives of ACP. The constitution gave the authority to the Abkhaz Counsil to govern the domestic affairs. The central government in Tbilisi established the Ministries of Abkhazia, appointing Prince of Abkhazia, Revaz Emkhvari as the Minister of Abkhaz Affairs.[5] The ACP and Abkhaz Ministry drafted the new constitution of Abkhazia in 1920, which emphasized the status of Abkhazia as an autonomous republic within Georgia.[6] The new drafted constitution included the following: “common affairs for Georgia and Abkhazia are the followings: a) Foreign Affairs… b) Currency system… c) Customs…d) Post and telegraph offices… e) Army; but Abkhaz soldiers are conscripted by the territorial principals and serve only on the territory of Abkhazia except wartime. During the peacetime, allocation of any other forces is a subject of special permission of APC.”[7]

Abkhaz delegation (later called: Abkhaz Peoples Council) in Tbilisi, 1918

In 1921, the President of Georgia Noe Zhordania approved the new constitution of DRG, reaffirming Abkhazia as an autonomous republic. In March of 1921, the Bolsheviks annexed the DRG and the Soviet Socialist Republic of Abkhazia was proclaimed, however politically and economically Abkhaz SSR was fully dependent on the Georgian Socialist Republic and Transcaucasian Federal Republic[8] The Soviet Central Committee of RKP did not consider Abkhazia an independent entity and made a following statement: " On November 1, 1921 the Presidium of the Caucasus Bureau had the special discussion on Abkhazia; the Special Commission had been instructed to elaborate a draft treaty between the Abkhaz and Georgian Republics.[9]

The Abkhazian SSR adopted its constitution on the 1st of April 1925.[10] Throughout its existence, the republic was led by Nestor Lakoba. On February 19th of 1931, Abkhazia's status was demoted to that of Autonomous Republic.[11]

The status of Abkhazia during the period of 1921 - 1931 has not always been clear as it was sometimes erronously referred to as Autonomous Republic.[citation needed] The existence itself of the Abkhazian SSR is questioned by some Georgian sources.
