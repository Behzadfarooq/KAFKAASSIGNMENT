Preparation:
The  reverse DNS entry for an IP is found by reversing the IP, adding it to "in-addr.arpa", and looking up the PTR record.
So, the reverse DNS entry for 209.122.160.124 is found by looking up the PTR record for
 124.160.122.209.in-addr.arpa.
All DNS requests start by asking the root servers, and they let us know what to do next.
See How Reverse DNS Lookups Work for more information.

How I am searching:
Asking a.root-servers.net for 124.160.122.209.in-addr.arpa PTR record:  
       a.root-servers.net says to go to henna.arin.net. (zone: 209.in-addr.arpa.)
Asking henna.arin.net. for 124.160.122.209.in-addr.arpa PTR record:  
       henna.arin.net [192.26.92.32] says to go to auth2.dns.rcn.net. (zone: 122.209.in-addr.arpa.)
Asking auth2.dns.rcn.net. for 124.160.122.209.in-addr.arpa PTR record:  Reports lexingtont160.l.subnet.rcn.net. [from 207.172.11.14]

Answer:
