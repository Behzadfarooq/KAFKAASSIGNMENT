Abraham Malpan was one of the main reformers of the Malankara Church.

[edit] Early Life

He was born in 1796 to the Palakunnathu family. When Malpan was a child he lost his parents due to illness and was raised by his uncle Thomas Malpan.

[edit] Religion

Later he went to a seminary, learned Syriac and was ordained as a priest by Mar Thoma 8th in Malayalam era 900. During the 1790s CMS missionaries came to Kerala and started their work in Kottayam. CMS missionaries appointed Malpan to work as a Syriac professor. As a result the CMS missionaries got to him and he knew that there must be change in the Malankara Church. He along with 10 other priests submitted a letter to the British about all of the malfunctions of the church. Chepat Mar Dionysus the metropolitian stopped programs with the CMS and eventually priests with Malpan stopped supporting him. But his close associate Kaithayil Geevarghese Malpan and he stood firm.

Later Malpan sent his nephew Deacon Mathew to the Patriarch in Syria to be ordained episcopa. Eventually he was ordained as Mathews Mar Antanasius. On his way back to Kerala he recivied a royal proclaimation which was against Malpan's wishes. The proclamation meant that Mathews Mar Anthanasius was Malankara Metropolitian. Malpan was later excommunicated and retreated to Maramon where the first revised Holy Qurbana took place. He served the church until his death in 1021 and is known today as the Eastern Wickliffe.
