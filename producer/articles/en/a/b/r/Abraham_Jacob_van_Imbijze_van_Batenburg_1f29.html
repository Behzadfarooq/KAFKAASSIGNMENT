Abraham Jacob van Imbyze van Batenburg (Breda, The Netherlands 1753 - Barbados, 9 October 1806) was a Dutch governor of Berbice and Essequibo (Guyana) during the period 1789-1806.

[edit] His life

Abraham Jacob was baptized on 17 June 1753 in the Walloon Church ("Waals Gereformeerd") in Breda, The Netherlands, as the son of Johan Bernhard Imbyze van Batenburg (bef. 1723-1768) and Susanna de Wit (1723-). He died on October 9, 1806 in Barbados, at the age of 53.

His father Johan Bernhard Imbyze van Batenburg was a draughtsman of maps, military-engineer (1743) and captain (1760). His grandfather was probably Paschasius Diederick van Batenburgh.

He married in Tilburg, The Netherlands, on 20 May 1782 with Wilhelmina Suzanna Zurmeged√©. She was born on 21 April 1759 in Veere, The Netherlands, possibly as daughter of Hendrik Cornelis Zurmegede, free citizen of Batavia, Dutch East Indies, and Susanna Lints.

In Berbice (Guyana) they had nine children: Suzanna Maria (1784-), Hendrik Christiaan (1786-), Adriana Baldwina (1788-), Jan Jacob Hendrik (1788-), Lambert Abraham (1791- Java (Dutch East-Indies, 1829), Catharina Cordelia (1793-), Elizabeth (1796-1864), Henriette Maria (1797-) and Hendrika Maria (1801-).

Alternative names are: van Imbijze van Batenburg and Imbyse van Batenburg,

[edit] Jobs

Initially for the Dutch East India Company (until 1791), later for the English government (1796) and the Batavian Republic (1795-1806) Abraham Jacob van Imbyze van Batenburg was governor of:

  • Berbice (Guyana) (between 1789 and 27 march 1802)
  • Essequibo (Guyana) (between 22 april 1796 en 27 march 1802)
  • Berbice (between june 1804 and 1806).
