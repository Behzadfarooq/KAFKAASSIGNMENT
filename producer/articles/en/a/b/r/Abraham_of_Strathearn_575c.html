Abraham of Strathearn

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Abraham was an early 13th century Scottish cleric who held the position of Bishop of Dunblane. He was a chaplain to the Mormaer of Strathearn, Gille Brígte.[1] There are no exact accounts of his origin, but he was almost certainly a native Scot from Strathearn. There is certainly no evidence to the contrary. Neville says that his "Hebrew name conceals an English provenance",[2] but there is no evidence of this, and in fact the Hebrew is more consistent with established Gaelic-naming patterns.[3] He was a son of a priest, and had at least one son of his own, Arthur by name.[4] Evidence from the charters of Inchaffray Abbey shows that he was bishop-elect by some date between the years 1210 and 1214.[5] A charter from Arbroath Abbey shows he had been consecrated by December 4 1214.[6] The date of his death is not known, but Radulf was bishop-elect in an Arbroath document datable between 1223 and 1225.[7]
