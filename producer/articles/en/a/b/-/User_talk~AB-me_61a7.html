Hi AB-me, and welcome to Wikipedia! Thanks for your contributions to the coolest online encyclopedia I know of =). I sure hope you stick around; we're always in need of more people to create new articles and improve the ones we already have. You'll probably find it easiest to start with a tutorial of how the wikipedia works, and you can test stuff for yourself in the sandbox. When you're contributing, you'll probably find the manual of style to be helpful, and you'll also want to remember a couple important guidelines. First, write from a neutral point of view, second, be bold in editing pages, and third, use wikiquette. Those are probably the most important ones, and you can take a look at some others at the policies and guidelines page. You might also be interested in how to write a great article and possibly adding some images to your articles.

Be sure to get involved in the community – you can contact me at my talk page if you have any questions, and you can check out the village pump, where lots of wikipedians hang out and discuss things. If you're looking for something to do, check out the community portal. And whenever you ask a question or post something on a talk page, be sure to sign your name by typing ~~~~.

Again, welcome! It's great to have you. Happy editing! --Spangineer (háblame) 15:17, Jun 13, 2005 (UTC)

[edit] Crossing a flag

I would like to ask you not to cross an official flag on your user page. Imagine someone did that to the Danish flag. This is even considered a crime in many countries. I just regard that as bad manners. I am sure you are able to express your reasonable opinion in a respectful way. ROGNNTUDJUU! 20:43, 4 March 2006 (UTC)

Geez - I have to say I don't understand your opinion. I merely see that (former) template as a humorous way indicating your political standpoint (see the expression "about as far as he can throw it"?). I also believe there is difference in crossing a national flag and any other flag (such as the European Union's), although I don't get offended if I were to see Dannebrog crossed (I don't even care about people who are angry about the Muhammad drawings burning it off - at all) unless it is in the sense of saying: "No Swedes (for example) here". Anyway, I don't see how crossing a flag is disrespectful. But then again, I can only speak for myself, and you can only speak for yourself. /AB-me (chit-chat) 21:21, 4 March 2006 (UTC)
Thank you for answering. I do not speak just for myself, as I already pointed out, it is even considered a crime in many countries to vilify official symbols. Some countries had to work real hard to make it to the EU. Why don't you just write a short line about your objections against it? That would be respectful. ROGNNTUDJUU! 21:27, 4 March 2006 (UTC)
(Note: I looked up "vilify" (which, in turn linked to defamatory) in Wiktionary before writing this.) I don't think crossing a flag is vilifying. (In that case, I feel sorry for the people of Scotland, for insulting the color blue (joking).) I'm not minorizing certain countries' effort to get into the EU, all I'm saying is that I think that EU generally is bad. And I have chosen to express it conveniently with a userbox. That's all. If you can find a image that represent the same opinion in a less vilifying way, please do insert it. Maybe "EU" would be less vilifying? /AB-me (chit-chat) 21:43, 4 March 2006 (UTC)
Crossing official symbols is considered vilifying. Crossing letters is not. I would find it more appropriate if you expressed what you do not like about the EU - there are many good reasons. If you just have a crossed flag people may think you disrespect Europeans. ROGNNTUDJUU! 21:47, 4 March 2006 (UTC)
Okay, two things: 1) I am a European. A lot of Europeans dislike the EU. A lot of people like the EU. The people who like the EU respect the people who dislike the EU's opinion about the EU, regardless of how they express it. 2) Crossing a flag isn't vilifying, in my opinion. Putting a cross on something merely says "I disapprove this". I would say that, in any case, crossing a flag is using Freedom of Speech, which the EU endorses. Thus, the EU endorses the right to strike a cross on the EU-flag. My conclusion: I don't think something is vilifying unless the nation/union/company thinks so. And in this case, it doesn't. /AB-me (chit-chat) 22:09, 4 March 2006 (UTC)
I am very sorry that you do not see that many people are upset if an official symbol of something that represents them is crossed. ROGNNTUDJUU! 22:21, 4 March 2006 (UTC)
Don't be. I ain't. Besides, the European Flag represents the Union itself (the political cooperation) rather that all of the people living within the borders of it. /AB-me (chit-chat) 22:29, 4 March 2006 (UTC)
The EU flag represents a political entity just as national flags do. People who identify with it might be offended if you cross it. Especially when there are so many respectful ways to express your fully legitimate opinion. I guess we have to agree to differ. ROGNNTUDJUU! 22:31, 4 March 2006 (UTC)
I agree. Let's get on with writing this encyclopedia! /AB-me (chit-chat) 22:35, 4 March 2006 (UTC)
