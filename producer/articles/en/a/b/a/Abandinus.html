Abandinus

Abandinus is an obscure Celtic deity, possibly a river-god. He is currently known only from a single inscription from Godmanchester in Cambridgeshire, England: a bronze votive feather is dedicated to him with the fragmentary text "to the god Abandinus, Vatiaucus gave this from his own resources" inscribed on a plaque.

Some believe that Abandinus was associated with the river Ouse, which is near the Godmanchester temple site and was formerly known as Aban.[citation needed] Additionally, Abona or Afon are Celtic river names, and a water tank and a well in one of the three temples on the site suggests the worship of a water deity. He may also have been associated with a local spring. Others have suggested that Abandinus is related to Maponos, a more popular Celtic deity.[citation needed] Though this seems unlikely, Abandinus and Maponos have linguistic characteristics that make this theory possible: an alternate reading of the god's name is Maband.

Etymologically, this theonym appears to be derived from Proto-Celtic *Ad-bandi-n-os.[citation needed] The name literally means 'to melody', which may have been a byword for the notion of "tunefulness" (cf. [1] [2] [3]).
