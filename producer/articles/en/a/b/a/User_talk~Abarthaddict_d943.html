[edit] Fiat Marea

Hello again! Talk about timing ;) First and foremost, thanks for correcting my rudimentary English grammar and terrible spelling mistakes. I was actually halfway through editing the article, but had to do a short break, so I didn't manage to give it the final look I intended to, and an appropriate message to you. I apologize for the fact that it made it look quite impudent.

As concerns production sites, I have added the [citation needed] template because I have never heard of that and it seems unlikely. I of course believe you know it from a certain source, and I wanted to draw your attention to that by adding this template, which I hoped you would substitute with a link or reference to the said source. I would be most obliged if you would. In general, this article needs good references - what I used as a reference in this last edit was the Marea page @ CarsFromItaly.com, accessed by the Wayback Machine, as this wonderful site is unfortunately down now. Thank God there's Internet Archive!

Other than that, I also think the engine section can be remodelled in a more clean and informative form. I personally like an arrangement like in the Daihatsu Applause article. I am going to get back to the article sometime later today - it would be great if you could add more of the information you have in the meantime! The Fiat Bravo/Brava and Fiat Stilo articles are a total mess too... Bravada, talk -

As concerns Perućac, I take it you found it interesting because you have stationed (is this the correct verb in English?) in Bosnia. I am not sure whether I understand everything there, but isn't the power station there a pumped-storage one?
As concerns Renault Modus - there are heaps of orphaned car articles like that and that's great you took care of this one! First of all, I recommend you to join the Wikipedia:WikiProject Automobiles. What we are striving towards now as concerns articles on individual models is replacing old-school tables with the appropriate Infobox, cleanup, getting rid of POV undertones and in general structuring the articles better and expanding them. Also, adding references is what many Wikipedians forget about, though it is crucial - otherwise, how do we know what is actually true here?- Bravada, talk - 19:05, 5 June 2006 (UTC)
Forgot about one thing - what plagues automotive articles are unreferenced rumors, gossip and speculation (referenced would become information per se), like the Grand Modus thing. They do not belong in an encyclopedia at all, even if they are interesting and relevant. That is what forums and car mags are for. So, if you come accross anything like that, could you please ask the author to provide an appropriate reference (which would be manufacturer's press release or equivalent, no forum postings or media gossip), tag it with [citation needed], or if the author fails to do so or it seems generally unlikely, just delete it. Thanks! Bravada, talk - 19:24, 5 June 2006 (UTC)
Thanks for all the great stuff you're doing here! Bravada, talk - 20:55, 5 June 2006 (UTC)
PS. Procuring a photo from Brazil seems a great idea to me!
