Abandonia Reloaded, commonly referred to as AR, is a site dedicated to the development and distribution of freeware games. It was created on 2005-05-07 by Kosta Krauth, Monica Schoenthaler, Maikel Kersbergen & Tom Henrik Aaberg. Currently (September 2006), the site offers reviews, interviews, screenshots and links to downloads for over 200 freeware games, as well as extras like walkthroughs written exclusively for AR. The site features a progressive system of game evaluation, in which the quality of the game is rated not only by the reviewer but also by the votes of regular visitors. The site is maintained by a large number of staff, most of whom have previously been involved with Abandonia Reloaded's sister-site Abandonia.

[edit] Community

As a non-profit site, AR is a community-driven project. With the exception of the featured games themselves, all content available on the site, including reviews, interviews, and graphics (such as featured banners, screenshots, etc.) are created by the community as a volunteer effort.

The AR community mostly revolves around its forum, which is where members can read contribution guidelines and coordinate submissions, as well as discuss issues that are not related to the functions of the site.

Since AR relies heavily on the contributions of its visitors, content is usually varied in amount and frequency. In order to motivate community members into submitting contributions, and being as helpful and well-behaved as possible, constant support of the site eventually allows members access into the VIP section of the site. Including the ability to submit content and access different features that usually aren't available to the bulk of the community, VIP status is both a ticket to new content, and a badge of exceptional service to the Reloaded system.

[edit] History

The concept for AR was conceived by Kosta Krauth (the owner of Abandonia) in 2004, due to a desire to separate independently produced freeware from the abandonware already featured on Abandonia. He was joined by Maikel Kersbergen and Tom Henrik, both already admins for Abandonia. Maikel Kersbergen developed the layout for the site, while Tom Henrik assisted with developing the concept of the site, and preparing content. Soon after, Monica Schoenthaler was invited to join the team to assist with technical brainstorming, content preparation, and development. These four are listed as the original "Founders" of Abandonia Reloaded.

During its development, the concept of AR shifted from the desire to simply showcase freeware games into the hope of bringing the mostly non-mainstream independent game development scene to a wider audience of game enthusiasts. To achieve this goal, in addition to the reviews and downloads commonly associated with game download sites, the founders of the site planned to work closely with independent game producers. This included allowing producers to create accounts on the site which they could use to promote their productions through various means (such as mailing lists, RSS feeds), as well as conducting interviews with producers.

Previously, finding a freeware game could be difficult as they are spread out all over the internet, and are distributed independently and with little fanfare by their creators through internet download directory sites such as Download.com and personal sites, or via insular communities focused on game creation of particular genres such as the Adventure Game Studio community and the Game Maker community, or via small, independent project sites such as with the Noctis project.

In May 2005, AR was opened as a "sister-site" of Abandonia. Many of the first members of AR were originally members of Abandonia, but the site also attracted freeware enthusiasts and game developers not associated with the original Abandonia.

Nearly a year after its unveiling, AR was nominated for "Best New Site" by DaFastLane, a gaming news site.

[edit] Content on Abandonia Reloaded

The games showcased on AR are both old commercial games released as freeware (like The Elder Scrolls: Arena, The Black Cauldron and Beneath a Steel Sky), in addition to newer independent freeware (like Ark 22, Trilby's Notes and Enclosure).

AR has also been successful with interviewing different game makers, to get more info from behind the scenes of many of the games hosted on the site.

Currently, AR has a much larger amount of Adventure games in its library than other genres, but efforts are underway to expand the selection.
