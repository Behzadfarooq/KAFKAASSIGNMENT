Abadín is a municipality in the province of Lugo, Galicia, Spain. It covers 196,1 km² and has a population of 3,250 (2004) for a population density of 16.57/km².

[edit] Geography

Abadín is situated at the north-east of the country of Terra Chá. The municipality is crossed by the Serra do Xistral, the Cordal de Neda, and the rivers Labrador and Abadín. The medium elevation is of 500 m., and the highest point is Lombo Pequeno with 1.015 m.

The countryside of Abadín its determined by a fertile valley crossed by the rivers Ouro, Labrador, Abadín, Pontiga, Santandrea, Pedrido, Anllo, Arnela, and Fraga Vella; and surrounded by mounts. They are important the sierras of Cordal de Neda, Toxoso, Tremoal, Coto da Cal, and A Fraga de Vigas, nearby the mountains of Costa do Sol and O Picouto.

Climate: The medium annual temperature is soft, about 10°C. The rainfalls are high, 1799 mm./year. The winters are cold and rainy, and the summers are dry.

Historically has been linked to the Ancient Mondonedo Province (Ancient Galicia Kingdom) and the monastic sites of Meira or Lourenza Abbey. This religious and political centres have been related to the arrival in this Galician land of hordes of sailing Briton inmigrants in the Dark Ages period (C.V and VI) from the British Isles (see Bishop Maeloc and Britonia)
