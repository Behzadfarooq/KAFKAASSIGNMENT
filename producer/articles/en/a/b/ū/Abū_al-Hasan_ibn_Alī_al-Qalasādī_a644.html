Abū al-Hasan ibn Alī al-Qalasādī

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Abū al-Hasan ibn ʿAlī al-Qalaṣādī (1412 Baza, Spain – 1486 Béja, Tunisia) was an Arab mathematician known for being one of the most influential voice in algebraic notation since antiquity. He wrotes numerous books on arithmetic and algebra, including al-Tabsira fi'lm al-hisab (Arabic: التبصير في علم الحساب‎ "Clarification of the science of arithmetic".)
