This article is about the Australian ABC news service. For information about the news organization which broadcasts under the same name in the United States, see ABC News.

ABC News is produced by ABC News and Current Affairs. Its flagship bulletin is broadcast at 7:00pm every night, with a separate bulletin for each state and territory. This is in contrast to the metropolitan commercial networks whose news services focus heavily on the metropolitan cities.

[edit] Bulletins

[edit] The Midday Report

  • Anchor: Ros Childs
  • Finance: Whitney Fitzsimons

The Midday Report is broadcast each weekday at 12pm. It goes out live from the ABC's Sydney Headquarters in Ultimo. It is broadcast at 12pm across the eastern and central states and territories, despite often different timezones.

In Western Australia, a separate edition is produced, as Western Australia, at 2 hours behind Sydney, is deemed too far behind for news items presented in the Sydney bulletin to be still 'up to date'. The programme was launched in Feb 2005, replacing the less successful 'Midday News and Business' which in turn replaced the long running 'World at Noon'
