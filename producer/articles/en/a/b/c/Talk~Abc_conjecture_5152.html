I've read that Fermat's Last Theorem is an easy consequence of this conjecture. If I get the chance I'll see if I can find out why and add it, unless someone else wants to do so in the meantime. --ScottAlanHill 16:54, 9 March 2006 (UTC)

Since the conjecture says nothing on the constant Cε, I don't see how it should rule out the existence of finitely many solutions for FLT. However, the abc-conjecture does easily imply that for n>3 there are at most finitely many solutions: If X,Y,Z are positive integers without common divisor with XN + YN = ZN , then rad(XNYNZN) = rad(XYZ) and and therefore the abc-conjecture (if true) yields

which implies an upper bound for Z.
