[edit] ABC2 Infobox

"Cyberjunkie" has reverted the ABC2 infobox back to a channel's infobox. I do not understand this, as ABC TV has a network infobox, yet ABC TV is not a network, it is merely a channel. The Australian Broadcasting Corporation is the network. And ABC TV and ABC2 are just channels. Can we have one uniform infobox for both ABC TV and ABC2? - Stickeylabel 07:16, 22 October 2006 (UTC)

If your definition of a network is a company that broadcasts TV, then yes, the ABC is a network. However, I think a more common usage is for a nationally distributed FTA channel, especially one with regional variation. Thus ABC TV is a network, comprised of ABC TV Victoria, ABC TV NSW, etc. The Nine Network includes Nine Sydney, Nine Melbourne, etc. When Viacom owned both CBS and UPN, no-body was calling Viacom the "network" - CBS and UPN both had that title. matturn 08:58, 1 November 2006 (UTC)

[edit] Programming pre-2007

What exactly did ABC2 show prior to the lifting of the genre restrictions? I'm curious myself, but this info should also be on the page. Nova Prime 04:24, 12 March 2007 (UTC)

Using [1] you can see ABC2's scehdule throughout its entire life. Stickeylabel 05:15, 12 March 2007 (UTC)
Mainly a lot of repeats - there were genre restrictions on the channel so children's programming, repeats of news and current affairs shows, archived sport, and plenty of Stateline - not much has changed as of yet, although there are quite a few more imported shows and the amount of non-repeated content is slowly increasing. timgraham 06:05, 12 March 2007 (UTC)
