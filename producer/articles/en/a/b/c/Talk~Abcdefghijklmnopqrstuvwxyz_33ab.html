[edit] Pronounciation

(ab-cud-ef-gï)(jek'l-m'nâp-kwûr)(stööv-wik-ziz

What's the pronunciation scheme used here? --Brion 08:04 29 Jun 2003 (UTC)
  • I couldn't find any wikipedia-user-guide-standards for pronounciation guidelines, so I used my Merriam-Webster Dictionary as a reference. Kingturtle 09:26 29 Jun 2003 (UTC)
See Wikipedia talk:Manual of Style (pronunciation) for some old discussion... SAMPA seems to be the de-facto standard so far, though it leaves something to be desired. Personally I find the (various) dictionary systems incomprehensible. :( At least we've got some charts & tables online for SAMPA which can be linked to (eg SAMPA/English)... (IPA is trickier to do online. Best way, of course, is to record the sound and upload it.) --Brion 09:31 29 Jun 2003 (UTC)
SAMPA is used with the understanding that when it one day becomes easier to use IPA we can do some sort of automatic conversion because the symbols are in 1-to-1 correspondence. -- Tarquin 09:37 29 Jun 2003 (UTC)

Uh, this particular sort of pronunciation guide is not guiding me.... It is like staring at Cascugian pictograms. --Menchi 22:58, 11 Jun 2004 (UTC)

[edit] Should this page be? If so, what?

My main opinion is that this should be merged with Latin alphabet; however, I've given a nod to separateness by at least cross-referencing that article to here in the See Also section. Assuming that this page should continue to exist, should it be a straight disambiguation page, or a signpost article (per Category:Signpost articles)? My gut says "signpost", but I know there are strongly opposed factions that dispute the appropriateness of that class of pages altogether. I don't want to re-nominate on VfD, but I do want to reopen the debate on whether this is what the page should be and where it should be. Regards, Courtland 15:15, 29 October 2005 (UTC)
