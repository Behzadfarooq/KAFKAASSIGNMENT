[edit] A note about self promotion and excessive linking

Greetings. I realize you may not be aware of a notable Wikipedia policy, so I would like to present it to you, on the assumption that you may not have seen it yet.

What Wikipedia is not: Wikipedia is not a soapbox: Wikipedia is not a soapbox or a vehicle for propaganda and advertising. and Self-promotion. You are free to write about yourself or projects you have a strong personal involvement in. However, do remember that the standards for encyclopedic articles apply to such pages just like any other, including the requirement to maintain a neutral point of view, which is difficult when writing about yourself. Creating overly abundant links and references to autobiographical articles is unacceptable.

Please also see: Wikipedia:Notability, which will come into play as to why I am removing 'abcoffice.com' from a 'list of notable dot-coms'.

I will be removing mention of this company from 'dot-com bubble', and I will be removing mentions of ABCOffice.com from pages where I do not feel it is appropriate.

Please read these policies, and do not re-add links back to this page where they are not notable, or constitute advertising or self promotion. They will likely be reverted.

Thanks, Skybunny 18:06, 28 June 2006 (UTC)


[edit] Fair use rationale for Image:Abcoffice.jpg

This file may be deleted.

Thanks for uploading Image:Abcoffice.jpg. I notice the 'image' page specifies that the image is being used under fair use but there is no explanation or rationale as to why its use in Wikipedia articles constitutes fair use.

Please go to the image description page and edit it to include a fair use rationale.

If you have uploaded other fair use media, consider checking that you have specified the fair use rationale on those pages too. You can find a list of 'image' pages you have edited by clicking on the "my contributions" link (it is located at the very top of any Wikipedia page when you are logged in), and then selecting "Image" from the dropdown box. Note that any fair use images lacking such an explanation will be deleted one week after they have been uploaded, as described on criteria for speedy deletion. If you have any questions please ask them at the Media copyright questions page. Thank you. --Kevin_b_er 07:35, 5 August 2006 (UTC)
