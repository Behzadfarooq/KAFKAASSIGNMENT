"ABC-DEF-GHI" is a song sung by Big Bird of Sesame Street. (In recent years, it has also been sung by Elmo.) It is also erroneously known as "Abcdefghijklmnopqrstuvwxyz."

After stumbling upon the Latin alphabet, which was written in chalk on the sidewalk, he mistakes it for a very long word ("abcdefghijklmnopqrstuvwxyz"). With marching band-like accompaniment, he breaks into song, pronouncing the "word", as "Abb-kuh-deff-g(as in garment)ee-jeckel-muh-nopp-kwer-stoov-wix-iz" (IPA: [æbkəˈdɛfgɪʤəklmn̩ˈɔpkwərstuvwɪksɪz]). His brilliance as a bird, he thinks, will become known to all the world if he can ascertain the word's meaning.

A verse lists Big Bird's assumptions of what the word might mean:

It might be a kind of an elephant
Or a funny kind of kazoo
Or strange, exotic turtle:Or maybe a kind of a doggie
Or particular shade of blue ABCDEFG..HIJK...LMNOP..QRS..TUV..W.x.Y and ZZZZZZZZ
Or maybe a pretty flower
Naah, not with a name like that, uh-uh!


The bridge illustrates the daunting task of trying to spell, let alone pronounce the word:

It starts out like an A word, as anyone can see
But somewhere in the middle, it gets awfully QR (pronounced "kwer") to me.

The humor comes from the irony that while Big Bird can read the word, he does not seem to know what an alphabet is, as well as the fact that he is saying the whole alphabet as one word. This kind of humor is generally not intended to be noticed by children but instead is meant for adults so they can enjoy the show as well, and are more inclined to watch it with their children. This is a typical tactic of Sesame Street.[citation needed]

"ABC-DEF-GHI" is one of many similar songs about the Latin alphabet, as well as songs that set the alphabet to music and use the same title or sometimes simply shortened to ABC's. Their main purpose is generally didactic.
