I reside in Arcata, California and attend Humboldt State University. My major is Religious Studies and minors are Multicultural Queer Studies (i.e. post-colonial queer theory) and History.

I have this crazy idea that we should title articles with the name of the subject, not what name is convenient. Such as using United States of America, not United States, because that's the country's name (and you say, "but so many things link to 'United States,' well, that's what redirects are for) and saying Ireland instead of Republic of Ireland, because that's the country's name. Ireland's official name is Ireland, it's constitution establishes it as Ireland. Beyond country names, another issue is romanization on articles such as Tao Te Ching (should be Dao De Jing using pinyin) and Laozi (which is correct, for now, but there is an urge to change it back to Lao Tzu). "But those are the more common uses in English," you might say, but this is an encyclopedia, and that is what redirects are for.

I prefer my name be spelled with a lowercase A: abexy.
