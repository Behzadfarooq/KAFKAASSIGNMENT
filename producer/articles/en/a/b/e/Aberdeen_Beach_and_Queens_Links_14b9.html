Aberdeen Beach and Queens Links is located in Aberdeen, Scotland. The beach itself is famous for its golden sand and its long curved length between the harbour and the River Don's mouth.

The beach suffers from significant erosion of the sand so there are distinctive groyne or walls, to help keep the sand in place. The beach is popular with walkers, surfers and windsurfers.

Recently in an expensive million pound operation, sand was taken by ship from further down the coast to the south of Aberdeen and sprayed on the beach to replace some of the lost sand. Rocks were then placed in a v-shape formation to try and prevent erosion, much like the groyne.

The beach has a popular amusement area along the famous Beach Esplanade where there are restaurants, a cinema and the city's amusement park Codonas.

The actual park area is called Queens links and takes the form of a large grassy area parallel to the beach. It is popular with many people and often kite-boarders can be seen taking advantage of the strong see winds. There is a public golf course, the Queens Lynx.

There is a city council recreation site, the Beach Leisure Centre with a swimming pool, ice rink and indoor sports hall.
