Abercorn, Quebec

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Abercorn(2006 population 366) is a village, ranked 3,426 in terms of population, in south-central Quebec, Canada on the United States border in the Regional County Municipality of Brome-Missisquoi, led by Bloc Quebecois deputy Christian Ouellet. It is near the larger town of Sutton, Quebec, Bromont, Quebec, and Jay Peak, Vermont, all three of which are renowned ski stations. Abercorn is located in the Eastern Townships, a popular daytrip or vacation spot for people living in Montreal. The town is located about 105 km from Montreal, via Autoroute 10.
