Sir Abraham "Abe" Bailey, KB (b. 6 November 1864, Cradock, Eastern Cape, South Africa - d. 10 August 1940, Cape Town, South Africa).

He was a South African diamond tycoon and cricketer who played three first-class matches for Transvaal. He was later knighted for his contributions.

Bailey was a friend of Winston Churchill's and Churchill's eldest daughter, Diana, was married to Abe Bailey's son. However, the marriage ended in divorce a few years later.

His wife, Mary Bailey, was the greatest British aviatrix of her time, who "personally guided a plane from England to the nether tip of South Africa and back" (Time, 28 January 1930). In January 1930, aged 40, she became Dame Mary Bailey, a Dame Commander of the Order of the British Empire.
