[edit] Subjective vs. Objective Wisdom

"I am That That I am" may be understood as the essence of wisdom, and it is this simplest principle that we want to justify in the name of Martinism here now. The cohesive principles of locomotivation will be a better topic for the sanctification process, and I can continue that on my own website as I explore Sufism there. Here I simply see fit to give an explanation according to the temporal, dimensional and circumstantial details of the interests our Martinist community has in this Wikipedia project.

Some of you have gathered your ideas into articles throughout this site, and you check back from time to time to see who might have contributed to their development. Now I want to gradually interest you in the perspective I'm developing, but I have to justify this presentation both in terms that are appropriately maintainable within Wikipedia and in terms that are fair to the regulative principles that maintain the integrity of Martinist fraternity and sorority. Where "I" am the subject (just as any of "you" can recognize yourselves as "I"s in turn) addressing Martinism, "that" is the object, which in this case is the Martinist or very specifically the applicant on the path towards Martinism.

This very article and the words which compose it are in some ways both "I" myself and "that" Martinism toward which my path has pursuit. These words serve as a sort of logistical bridge and are both subjective and objective. I speak, or more precisely, am enscribing or putting into digital print, that which I am calling Martinism. Can I justify these statements to the satisfaction of you the audience? I certainly hope so, for thereby might I be allowed to keep this article here as an invitation to the other studends of Martinism here on Earth and especially in connection with the English Language.
