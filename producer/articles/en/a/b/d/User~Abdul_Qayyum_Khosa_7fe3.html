Background of Mr. Abdul Qayyum Khosa is varied. He started his career as an Administrative Officer for the Pakistan Times and Daily Imroze at Multan, the regional city of southern Punjab. In 1988, he resigned to join the Mazari Group of Companies as Group General Manager. The Mazari Group, incorporated in Karachi, consisted of diversified businesses in Agro forms, insurance companies, textile mills, consultancy and trading. As the Group General Manager, he was responsible for the adoption of new and modern practices in operational management, industrial relations and marketing management. The development and application of new management and marketing theories have always been of practical and academic interest to him. So in 1994, he enrolled in Keele University, Staffordshire, England, to indoctrinate himself with new developments in the management sector. During his studies in England, he also assisted the management of, the WestCroft Nursing Home, an 85-bed elderly care residential home in United Kingdom. In 1996, upon his return from England to Pakistan, he purchased the shares in a family concern, the Al-Khaliq Patients Care, a 150-bed hospital, and managed the business as Chief Executive. In 1999 after the first medical institutions, the Allama Iqbal Medical College and the 1,100-bed Jinnah Hospital in Lahore, were declared autonomous bodies in Pakistan, he had applied for the post of pioneer Director, Health Care Marketing, in the public sector for South Asia. He was selected and had accepted the challenge. Unfortunately no rules of hospital autonomy made him disappointed and he applied in Pakistan Railway the second largest organization in the country. He was appointed Director of Public Relations. During this time, his application was immigration to Canada was approved. He was given a deadline of April 20th, 2001, to land in Canada and resigned and went to Canada. In Toronto, He volunteered for two positions. He was working as E-Health Strategist at the Baycrest Centre for Geriatric Care and the Aassisting Communication Specialist Toronto Western Hospital (University Health Network). He wrote the e-health strategy for Baycrest and a biweekly newsletter for “Western Wire” on current initiatives and changes underway at the Toronto Western Hospital. Canada was a new country for him with many demands but he accepted the challenges. He ran for election as Co-chair of the Management Council of Sir John A. MacDonald Collegiate Institute in Toronto and won the seat. He was also elected Vice-Chair of the Volunteers Advisory Committee for the Toronto Western Hospital. In April 2002, to update his knowledge and heighten his skills in keeping up with global and recent developments in business administration, he started a two-year unique Masters Degree in Business Administration in Executive Management offered by Royal Roads University and it was successfully completed in 2004. His organisational consulting project which is mandatory for MBA program was challenging and he effectively satisfied all stakeholders. The project was, “Review of PharmaCare program BC” and Assistant Deputy Mister Dr. Stephen Brown was his supervisor. Currently based at Mississauga, Canada, he is working as self employed management consultant.
