Abdank - is a Polish Coat of Arms. It was used by several szlachta families in the times of the Kingdom of Poland and the Polish-Lithuanian Commonwealth.

[edit] History

Some people say the beginning of this shield dates from Grach or Krakus' time, a Polish monarch who gave the name and foundation to the city of Kraków. On Wawel Mount, where Kraków's castle stood, from the Wisła (Vistula) river side, a dragon showed up; some called him Holophag, others Boa. He was very venomous: not only did he poison the air with his breath, but he also went out of his lair to the nearby streets and suburbs and ate people and cattle and it was never enough for him. One day a man called Skuba, a young shoemaker, touched deeply by his neighbors' losses, came up with a solution. He took the skin of a flayed calf, put tar and sulphur and fire-brand into it and threw it into the dragon's lair. The dragon didn't recognize the deception. In the morning he went out of his lair and ate what he assumed to be a sacrifice from the people. Then he was very thirsty because of the fire that was brewing in his intestines, so he drank and drank the water from the Wisła river until he finally exploded and died. Then Krakus granted Skuba the letter "W" to his shield (The "W" either stands for snake in Polish (wąż), or "Wawel" Mount).

You can still see the dragon's lair in Kraków today, a reminder of those times.
