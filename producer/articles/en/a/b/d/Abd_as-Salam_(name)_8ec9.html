Abd as-Salam is the transliteration of a Theophoric Arabic given name.

[edit] Etymology

The name is built on the Arabic words Abd, al- and Salam. The name means "servant of the peaceful", As-Salam being one of the 99 names of God.

Other tranliterations inlude Abdul Salam, Abdoul Salam, Abdus Salam, Abd-ul-Salam, Abd al Salam.

[edit] Usage

People named such include:

  • Abdul Salaam (Afghan witness, March 2003), witnessed the killing of two Red Cross workers in March 2003.
  • Abdul Salaam (football player) of the New York Jets American Football team
  • Abdul Salaam (Guantanamo detainee 826), a citizen of Afghanistan, a Guantanamo detainee ID # 826
  • Abdul Salam (Taliban Chief Justice), Chief Justice of the Taliban's Supreme Court
  • Abdul Salam (Taliban leader, Kabul front, 2001), American intelligence analysts assert he was one of the commanders of the Taliban's Kabul Front during its 2001 collapse.
  • Abdul Salam (Taliban leader, Karabak front, 2001), American intelligence analysts assert commandef the Taliban's Karabak Front.
  • Abd al-Salam Ali al-Hila, a Yemeni citizen, a Guantanamo detaiee ID # 1463
  • Abdul Salam Arif, president of Iraq
  • Abdul Salam Gaithan Mureef Al Shehry, a citizen of Saudi Arabia, a Guantanamo detainee ID # 132
  • Abdul-Salam Ojeili, a Syrian novelist and politician
  • Abdul Salam Sabrah, an acting Prime Minister of the Yemen Arab Republic
  • Abdul Salam Rocketti, a Taliban commander famous for shooting down a Soviet helicopter with a rocket propelled grenade, who ran for office in 2005.
  • Abdoul Salam Sow, a Guinean football player
  • Abdul Salam Zaeef, a Taliban ambassador to Pakistan
  • Ashraf Salim Abd Al Salam Sultan, a Guantanamo detaiee ID # 263
  • Mehdat Abdul Salam Shabana, a member of the board of directors of Konsojaya Trading Company, a terrorist front organization based in Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia.
  • Abdesslam Yassine, leader of Moroccan Islamist organisation Al Adl Wa Al Ihssane
