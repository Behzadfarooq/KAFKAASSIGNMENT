Abdul Raheeda Hodge (born September 9, 1982 in Saint Thomas, U.S. Virgin Islands) is a current American football linebacker for the Green Bay Packers. He was drafted in the third round, 67th overall, of the 2006 NFL Draft. Hodge played college football at the University of Iowa. He scored his first NFL touchdown on a fumble recovery during his first start against the Seattle Seahawks on Monday, November 27, 2006.

[edit] High School Years

Hodge attended Boyd Anderson High School (Lauderdale Lakes, Florida) and won four varsity letters in football, two in track, and one in basketball. In football, he was a three-year starter, a three-time team captain, and as a junior, he was a first team All-County honoree and a third team All-State honoree. He graduated from Boyd Anderson High School in 2001. He has a daughter by the name of Amari Hodge. Abdul has a younger brother who plays middle linebacker for the university of Wisconsin
