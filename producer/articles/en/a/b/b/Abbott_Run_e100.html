Abbott Run

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Abbott Run is a de facto river in the U.S. states of Massachusetts and Rhode Island. It flows approximately 16 km (10 mi).

[edit] Course

The river rises from an unnamed pond in Wrentham, Massachusetts, then flows south to Cumberland, Rhode Island where it provides water to numerous ponds, including Diamond Hill Reservoir, Arnold Mills Reservoir and Rawson Pond. It then flows back into Massachusetts and flows south through North Attleborough, past the village of Adamsdale. From there, the river flows back into Rhode Island and continues through Cumberland, providing water to Robin Hollow and Happy Hollow Ponds before flowing into the Blackstone River at the village of Valley Falls.

[edit] Tributaries

Burnt Swamp Brook and the Millers River are the only two named tributaries of Abbott Run, though it has many unnamed streams that also feed it.
