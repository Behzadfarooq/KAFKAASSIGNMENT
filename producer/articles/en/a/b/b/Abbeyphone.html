[edit] Abbeyphone

Abbeyphone is a fully customizable hosted VoIP enabling platform developed by Abbeynet

[edit] History

It was born as a pre-paid, fully web-based VoIP service in April, 2003, where only outbound calls were allowed by means of an ActiveX component. After a short experimental period, new web-based, nomadic services like SMS, fax, e-mail reading, incoming call management, have been added thoroughly.

Abbeyphone has relied on Abbeynet's proprietary platform named EVT (Enterprise Video Telephony) for three years, during which Abbeynet has forged a team of professionals with strong knowledge on VoIP protocols and architectures, proficient on both network and system related issues on scalability, accessibility, usability. At the end of 2005, a major switch has occurred: the platform started to become SIP-compliant and, in late April 2006 the full SIP web-based version has been released.

[edit] Technical information

Abbeyphone is an hosted VoIP platform that is based on SIP for audio/video communication, and on a web interface to provide various communication tools. Its peculiarity resides in the "any-network" capability, achieved by means of a proprietary tunneling strategy over the well known SIP protocol. Abbeynet developed its own SIP stack, based on a full "home brew" toolkit, which was also published as MammoothLibrary on Sourceforge (http://sourceforge.net/projects/mammoothlibrary). The resulting SIP client component has been successfully deployed as a web activex (for Microsoft Internet Explorer) and a plugin (for Firefox or Mozilla Suite), as a Windows standalone application client that resides in the windows taskbar (http://www.abbeyphone.com/products/deskbar/eng/download_page.html ) and a beta extension for Firefox (http://labs.abbeyphone.com)

In addition to that, abbeyphone features a Session Border Control component that overcomes most of the nat-traversal problems by setting up and keeping alive a channel for inbound messaging. Abbeyphone billing system is fully developed inside Abbeynet, it is based on Radius protocol and features a real-time partial credit reservation mechanism and a flexible pricing profiling with 9 parameters to set up the Cost over Time curve.
