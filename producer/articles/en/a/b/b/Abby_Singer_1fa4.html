Abby Singer

Abner E. "Abby" Singer worked as a production manager and assistant director in film between the 1950s and the 1980s. He's famous for misleading his crew into thinking that the second-to-last shot of the day was actually the last shot. For this reason, the second-to-last shot of the day on a film set is now called an "Abby Singer."

Abner E. Singer is currently a faculty member and mentor at the AFI Conservatory.

Abby Singer is also the name of a 2006 film revolving around a casting director experiencing an emotional crisis from the loss of true love. The film showcases that life is all about the second-to-last shot; A second chance for happiness.
