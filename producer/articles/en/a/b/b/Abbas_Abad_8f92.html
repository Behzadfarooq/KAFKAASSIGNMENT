Abbas Abad

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Abbas Abad is a large north-central neighborhood of Tehran, Iran.

It is particularly noteworthy for its commercial corporate business district. Many domestic and foreign firms are headquartered there.

Iran's largest ceremonial mosque (still under construction) is located there. (Mosallā-e Tehran).

A lesser known but interesting fact is that the world renowned American architect Louis Kahn designed the Abbas Abad Development Plan in mid seventies for the Shah, aimed to become ( shahestan pahlavi), a Tehran version of manhattan or La Défense,to house embassies , ministeries and high rises. The plan, which was commissioned at the peak of the shah's euphoria of oil boom (which quardled the country's income), never materialized due to mismanagement, budget problems, and most important of all, Islamic revoloution, which put an end to many of the Pahlavi regime's high flying, and perhaps over extravagant, perojects.the sole remainant of that project today is Jahan koodak (now Haghani) highway, which was designed to function as the connecting axis of the new town, but in practice is now Tehran's most impractical and uncrowded highway.

Architect Farshid Moussavi also designed a modern theater in 1997 (called The "Azadi Cineplex") for Abbas Abad, which was not received by The Islamic Republic authorities of The Ministry of Culture.
