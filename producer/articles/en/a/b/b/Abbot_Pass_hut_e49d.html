Abbot Pass hut

The Abbot Pass hut is an alpine hut located on the continental divide at Abbot Pass, nestled between Mount Victoria and Mount Lefroy. It is directly on the border of Banff National Park, Alberta and Yoho National Park, British Columbia. The hut is maintained by the Alpine Club of Canada. Coordinates: 51°21′54″N, 116°17′12″W NAD83 11U 549660 5690657

Abbot Pass (and the hut) may be approached from either the Lake O'Hara area on the British Columbia side (hiking past Lake Oesa, or the more technically demanding route from Lake Louise on the Alberta side. The hut is rarely used in winter due to avalanche hazard.

The hut sleeps 24, and has both a wood stove for heading and drying, and a propane system for cooking and lighting.

It is notable that until the Neil Colgan hut was built above Moraine Lake, the Abbot Pass hut was the highest permanent habitable structure in Canada.
