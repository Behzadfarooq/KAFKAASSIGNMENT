Abbesses is a station of the Paris Métro.

Abbesses is one of the few stations in Paris metro which have been built at a deep level, 30 meters under earth. Indeed, the station is located on the butte (hill) of Montmartre, precisely on its western side. The access to the platforms is mainly made through elevators, however there are also decorated stairs which can be also used for this purpose.

The station's entrance, designed by Hector Guimard (1867-1942), is one of just two original ones left in Paris. (The second is located at Porte Dauphine.) The entrance was originally used for the Hôtel de Ville metro station, but transferred to its current location in 1970.

Abbesses station is currently in a very bad condition, but renovation is underway.

[edit] Abbesses in popular culture

  • Abbesses is Amélie's metro stop.
  • Louis Vuitton has a messenger style bag in the Monogram Canvas line named after the station.
