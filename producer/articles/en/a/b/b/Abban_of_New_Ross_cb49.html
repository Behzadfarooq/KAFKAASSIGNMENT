Saint Abban of New Ross (also known as Ewin, Abhan, or Evin; also Nevin, Neville, Stephan) (fl. 550â€”650)[2] was a contemporary and namesake of Saint Abban of Magheranoidhe.[3]

[edit] Life

Abban was involved in the foundation of the abbey of Ros-mic-treoin (modern New Ross) and evangelized in the area of County Wexford, Ireland.[1]

[edit] Conflation

Some writers have conflated him with Saint Evin of Rosglas, County Kildare. Even Colgan (followed by Dr. Lanigan) fell into the error of identifying "Rosglas" (Monasterevan) with "Ros-mic-treoin" (New Ross). The confusion here arose because Evin of Rosglas died on 22 December at Monaster Evin in County Kildare. However, Abban, that is, Evin of Ros-mic-treoin, died at Ross, County Wexford.
