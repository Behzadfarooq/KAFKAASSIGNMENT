[edit] Björn & Benny, Agnetha & Frida era

  • "En hälsning till våra parkarrangörer" (1972) is a recording promoting Björn & Benny, Agnetha & Frida's Summer 1973 Folkpark tour. Includes spoken words and excerpts from People Need Love, Man vill ju leva lite dessemellan (solo work of Frida) and Så glad som dina ögon (solo work of Agnetha).

[edit] ABBA era

[edit] 1974

  • "Rikky Rock 'N' Roller" is a song recorded in 1974 but it was dropped. The Swedish rock star Jerry Williams recorded and released it as a single after ABBA had decided their version was unsuitable for release. An excerpt of this song was released in the boxset Thank You For The Music.
  • Here Comes Rubie Jamie is a song recorded in 1974 with the lead vocals by Frida and Benny. An excerpt of this song was released in the boxset Thank You For The Music This song was also known as Terra Del Fuego, and the excerpt on the box set was particularly edited to delete this chorus, as Benny violently dislikes it.
  • Baby is the early version of "Rock Me" recorded in 1974 with the lead vocals by Agnetha. An excerpt of this song was released in the boxset Thank You For The Music.

[edit] 1975

  • To Live With You is an attempt to record the song "Lycka" (previously released by Björn and Benny in their first album, also by Frida on her first album) in English. The song recording commenced in 1975. Later released on the remastered CD of Björn & Benny's Lycka album in May 2006.
  • Dancing Queen (Early Version) is a version with an extra verse. The extra verse can be heard on the documentary "The Winner Takes It All". The extra verse appears on the Spanish version of Dancing Queen.
  • I Do, I Do, I Do, I Do, I Do (early version) is a version with an extra verse.

[edit] 1976

  • When I Kissed The Teacher (Early mix) is the video version of the song. Later remixed for the album release.
  • Funky Feet is a song dropped due the similarity of Dancing Queen. Later the song was recorded by Svenne & Lotta and by Alcazar.
  • National Song is a short track recorded for the Australian TV Commercial of the National appliances. It's a re-recording of "Fernando" with new lyrics.
  • Monsieur, Monsieur is the early version of "My Love, My Life" with lead vocals by Agnetha.

[edit] 1977

  • I Am An A is a song never recorded in the studio, only performed on the Europe and Australia tours in 1977. The chorus was later reused in "Free As A Bumblebee".
  • Get On The Carousel is a song never recorded in the studio, performed only on the Europe and Australia tours in 1977 as part of the mini-musical "The Girl With The Golden Hair". Later the chorus was used in the track "Hole In Your Soul". A performance of this song can be seen and heard on the 2005 DVD release "ABBA - The Movie".
  • Scaramouche is a recording from 1977. An excerpt of this song was released in the boxset Thank You For The Music
  • Billy Boy is the early version of Take a Chance On Me. An excerpt of this track was released in the boxset Thank You For The Music.
  • Take A Chance On Me (New Mix) is a remix made in December 1977.

[edit] 1978

  • Free As A Bumble Bee is a demo recorded in 1978 with lead vocals by Björn and Benny, released in the boxset Thank You For The Music. Part of the chorus was later reused in "I Know Him So Well" from the musical Chess.
  • Mountain Top/Dr.Claus Von Hamlet nos. 1, 2 and 3 are several demos recorded in 1978.The composition underwent several changes and [fun] demo titles during the sessions. At one point the demo featured lyrics and vocals by Björn titled "Mountain Top", and another instrumental attempt ("Part I") and later reworking "Part 2" (with beautiful lead vocals by Agnetha & Frida) was released in the boxset Thank You For The Music. Parts of the composition would re-surface in the June 1980 demo "Burning My Bridges", but eventually the song was defined as a 'schottis' and put aside until Benny made his first instrumental folk album "Klinga Mina Klockor" in 1987, and there named "Lottis Schottis".
  • Summer Night City (Early mixes) according to Michael B. Tretow there are over 30 different mixes of this song preserved on old tapes, some which are very different to that which was later released as a single. One version was released on the 1994 box set Thank You For The Music, with an instrumental string intro. This version, in an improved version, was released on The Complete Studio Recordings.
  • Just A Notion is a demo recorded in 1978. A snippet of this track was released in the boxset Thank You For The Music. The complete version was recorded by ABBA cover band "Arrival".
  • Crying Over You is a demo recorded in 1978 with lead vocal by Björn. An excerpt of this track was released in the boxset Thank You For The Music.
  • If It Wasn’t For The Nights (Early Mix) was performed on the TV special ABBA In Japan. Later remixed for album release.
  • In The Arms Of Rosalita is an early demo version of Chiquitita. An excerpt can be heard in the TV documentary "The Winner Takes It All". It featured lead vocals by Frida. A later version Chiquitita Angelina was sung by both girls in unison.
  • Does Your Mother Know (Early Mix) is a complete version with a different intro that can be heard on the TV special "ABBA in Switzerland". Part of this version comes from the unrecorded song "I Want You" (1975) and was included in the Does Your Mother Know scene in Mamma Mia! musical. Oddly enough, this part of the recording also can be heard in the intro to "You Owe Me One" from one of ABBA's last recording sessions in 1982.

[edit] 1979

  • Sång till Görel is a tribute song for Görel Johnsen's 30th birthday recorded in 1979. It was never commercially released; however, a very limited number of records were pressed and given to people who attended the party.
  • Lady Bird is a song with no vocals recorded, later used in "Someone Else's Story" from the musical Chess.
  • I'm Still Alive (with music by Agnetha and lyrics by Björn) was performed live on the North America and Europe tours in 1979. Later recorded in Swedish by Kicki Moberg.
  • Rubber Ball Man is a song recorded in 1979 with shared lead vocal by Agnetha and Frida. An early version known as "Under My Sun" was recorded during rehearsals for the 1979 Tour. An excerpt of this track was released in the boxset Thank You For The Music.

[edit] 1980

  • Burning My Bridges is a song recorded in 1980 with lead vocal by Björn. An excerpt of this track was released in the boxset Thank You For The Music.

[edit] 1981

  • Hovas vittne is a song sung in Swedish, recorded as a tribute to Stig Anderson's 50th birthday in 1981.
  • Tivedshambo is an instrumental song recorded in 1981. It is the B-side of "Hovas vittne".
  • When All Is Said And Done (Demo Version and various mixes) is an instrumental track recorded in 1981 with a different bridge. A version with vocals featured a more emotional repetition of the first verse after the third. When the video was released in 1981 it featured another alternate version with a different ending. On the current DVDs it can clearly be seen that they overdubbed the album-version, the footage with Frida right at the end is slowed down to match the words.
  • Nationalsång is an instrumental track that later was used in "Anthem" from the musical Chess. The song is well-known as "Opus 10" among ABBA fans due a misunderstanding made by a journalist.
  • I Am The Seeker is an instrumental track recorded in 1981. Later the song was included in the musical ABBAcadabra and recorded by B.A. Robertson.
  • Fanfare For Icehockey World Championships '81 is a short instrumental track first heard on the television special Dick Cavett Meets ABBA in 1981.
  • Two For The Price Of One (Early Version) has different lyrics.
  • Givin’ A Little Bit More is a song recorded in 1981 with led vocal by Björn. An excerpt of this track was released in the boxset Thank You For The Music.
  • An Angel's Passing Through My Room is an early version of "Like An Angel Passing Through My Room" with both Frida and Agnetha on lead vocals. The track was originally written in the style of "Lay All Your Love On Me" and later heavily reworked.

[edit] 1982

  • Just Like That
  • Cassandra (Cold Ending Version) is an edited version of the original song.
  • Under Attack (Cold Ending Version) is an edited version of the original song.
  • The Day Before You Came (Cold Ending Version) is an edited version of the original song.
