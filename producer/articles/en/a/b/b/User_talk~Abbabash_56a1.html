[edit] Italian grammar

Hi. Thanks for your contribution to the Italian grammar article. I understand your effort to use a lively and amusing style, but perhaps you could limit the use of colloquial idioms (for instance, those about the "mess" of irregular Italian plurals) or nice but unwarranted hyperboles (for instance, the fact that "about 90% of intellectual jobs in Italian end like this", that is, in -logo): this way of exposing may be useful and interesting when lecturing or writing something more personal, but Wikipedia aims at a neutral point of view and tone. Thanks, Goochelaar 16:53, 18 March 2007 (UTC)

P.S. Actually, they are way less than 90%: think matematico, fisico, chimico, filosofo, insegnante, economista, giornalista, avvocato... :-) --Goochelaar 16:53, 18 March 2007 (UTC)
