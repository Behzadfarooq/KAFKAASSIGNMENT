Ab Tumhare Hawale Watan Saathiyo is a Bollywood film released on 24 December 2004. Amitabh Bachchan, Akshay Kumar, Bobby Deol (in a dual role), Divya Khosla, Sandali Sinha and Nagma. Anil Sharma is the director of the film. He earlier directed the blockbuster Gadar: Ek Prem Katha (2001). Despite a huge star cast the film was a box office failure [1]. The film released to average reviews. Anu Malik is the music director of the film.

[edit] Synopsis

Spoiler warning: Plot and/or ending details follow.

Major General Amarjeet Singh (Amitabh Bachchan) is a dedicated officer for the Indian army. His son Lieutenant Commander Vikramjeet Singh (Bobby Deol) follows in his footsteps and joins the army. Amarjeet is extremely proud of Vikramjeet but tragedy strikes when Vikramjeet is killed in action. Many years pass, Vikramjeet's son Captain Kunal Singh (Bobby Deol) joins the army but has no intentions of remaining there. He instead plans to leave, settle abroad and earn lots of money. Amarjeet is disappointed with Kunal as he hopes his grandson would do something to honour his deceased father.

Kunal comes across a desert party and sees Shweta Bhansali (Divya Khosla). It is love at first sight for him but she totally ignores him and does everything to avoid him. However she is doing this for a reason- she was once married.

Shweta fell in love and married Major Rajeev Singh (Akshay Kumar), unfortunately he was deployed for action on his wedding day and he had to leave his newly wed bride. That was the last time she saw him. Rajeev has in fact been captured by Pakistani officers, when he refuses to give info on a military operation he is tortured. Eventually Rajeev and other POW's manage to escape.

Shweta slowly begins to accept Kunal who is determined to marry her but when Rajeev shows up she finds herself at crossroads...

Spoilers end here.

[edit] Trivia

  • Akshay Kumar was going to drop out of the film because of the many anti-Pakistani lines but when these were changed he stayed with the film.
  • Divya Khosla makes her debut in the film as does Anil Sharma's younger brother Kapil Sharma
  • The love triangle is partly inspired by the film Pearl Harbor (2001) [2]
  • This was Amitabh Bachchan and Akshay Kumar's fourth film together. They previously starred in Ek Rishtaa: The Bond of Love (2001), Aankhen (2002) and Khakee (2004). They later starred in Waqt: The Race Against Time (2005) and Family - Ties of Blood (2006).
