AboutUs.org is an open Internet domain directory. It is similar to DMOZ and Alexa in that it lists websites along with information about their contents. One difference is that it is a wiki, with open editing, allowing Internet users to add entries or modify information. Ray King created AboutUs with a staff of five people.

[edit] Website contents

There were more than three million entries on AboutUs.org as of August, 2006. Most entries were created by a web robot. In many cases the content is simply a republication of the contents of the "About us", "About me", or similar page on the website. Such pages typically describe the entity that owns the site, and may include self-promotional information, which AboutUs.org does not restrict. In many other cases the content of an entry consists of the whois data for the website.

[edit] Data use

Some websites that analyze domains and traffic link to AboutUs.org as a point of additional reference. Notably, the "Whois" site DomainTools now references the Aboutus.org listing along with standard server information, domain ownership and history, and additional data.

The site is also referenced from whois data comment from the registrars Network Solutions and Register.com [1].

[edit] Criticisms

A simple search engine inquiry will return many articles and weblogs stating criticisms or mixed opinions of the aboutus.org site.

Privacy

Some website owners dislike the publication of the contact information for the website owner or domain name registrant. Although this information is publicly available from other sources, AboutUs makes it available in a searchable format, making it more easily accessible to the public. AboutUs has responded to this criticism by discontinuing its former practice of rendering phone numbers in text format, instead rendering the phone number of the registrant in an image format.

Republishing

Some site owners complain about AboutUs' practice of republishing copyrighted contents from 'about us' pages. AboutUs.org takes an image and sampling of random text and republishes it. AboutUs.org defends this by saying Google and Alexa do the same thing, by extrapolating a part of a website for publishing in a search.

It is possible to for a visitor to change the context of the about us information, such as to edit the contributor's name, or to paste in statements not made by the author.

One concern is that AboutUs derives revenue from advertising placed on pages that contain republished contents from other websites.

[edit] Funding

The developers of Aboutus.org have reportedly received more than one million dollars in financing. They have a goal and beginning funding for listing every website for inclusion. (See "Startup hits the money with biz wiki", Portland Business Journal, November 10, 2006.)
