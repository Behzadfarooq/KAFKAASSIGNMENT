[edit] Summary

'Takeo Tomanitsu's example of using custom flash animation with his own photography to convey his character rather than using words.

This image and file is created by Craig Burrows and featured online on his myspace as part of his photography showcase. The image is deemed too small to be of commercial use therefore I declare this file public domain.

Takeo Tomanitsu is the alter-ego creation of Craig Burrows.

Takeo1775 is the username associated with teenager Craig J. Burrows. The first half of the username refers to the character 'Takeo' who is a hybrid creation of a personality blend between the main character in the novel series Tales of the Otori and Craig Burrows's adaption of that character in his own style. The latter numbers in the username refer to the Edo Period in Feudal Japan where Japan's infamous samurai were still active.

[edit] Craig Burrows

Craig Burrows also known as 'Takeo Tomanitsu'

Many concider Craig Burrows to be a minor East Asian celebrity under the name 'Takeo Tomanitsu' through aid of 'Myspace' and the popularity of his Photography.

Craig Burrows was born in 1989 on the 26th of May in the Glasgow Royal Infirmary. He grew up in Mount Vernon, Glasgow where he attended Primary school and currently attends a Secondary School in Uddingston.

Many of his school-time hobbies include:

  • Karate
  • Ninjutsu
  • Photography
  • Snowboarding
  • Rock climbing

[edit] Takeo Tomanitsu

Craig Burrows's alter-ego 'Takeo Tomanitsu' is arguably the more accurate portrayal of Craig's true personality.

The origin of Takeo Tomanitsu dates back to Craig's first reading of the Tales of the Otori book 'Across the Nightingale Floor'. The first book in the series introduces the quiet character 'Tomasu' who after a series of events propel the characer's development into 'Lord Takeo' the adopted son of a Samurai Lord.

Upon completion of reading this novel Craig Burrows Takes the name 'Takeo' as his new alias in PC Gaming. He chose this name because of several personality similarities between the character in the book and his own character in real life.

For example;

  • Takeo in the book is a quiet boy who loves the country, especially mountainous terrain.
    • In real life Craig Burrows also seeks similar retreats on any given opportunity.
  • The character Takeo is taken to join 'The Tribe' which is a network of ninja spies.
    • For a year Craig Burrows also undergone ninja training.
  • The Character Takeo portrays morals of honor and justice associated with Japanese Samurai
    • Craig decorates his room with the Kanji 'Bushido' 'The way of the warrior'.

[edit] Myspace culture

Sometime in early 2006 Craig created a myspace account which through into 2007 gained popularity amongst communities in the east Asia. This is likely due to Craig's adaption of the alter-ego 'Takeo Tomanitsu' into his own personality creating the online entity 'Takeo' (an adapted Takeo, not the character in the novel but Craig's hybrid creation)

Using his photography skills, Craig set out to inspire and capture the imagination of his now global audience on myspace and other networks. Craig uses 'Flash' technology to present his photography to online audiences on myspace through custom written flash presentations designed to heighten the impact of the photography he as taken on the layout of his profile to make his profile unique to other users. His photography is now being further developed and taken to higher levels. This includes the introduction of a Photobook and an online gallery where prints can be purchased. However, morally Craig refuses to do professional photography. His views on photography being taken professionally is that it is forced and pushed whereas the photos he takes currently are natural and not forced or pushed in any way.


Custom Flash animation of photography used to replace words

Craig set out to gradually learn Japanese with aid from friends in Japan and a teacher at his School who used to be a translator in Japan. As a result of his language skills growing in Japanese and his knowledge in east Asia culture expanding, popularity kept growing.
