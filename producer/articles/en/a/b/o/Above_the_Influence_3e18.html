Above the Influence is an advertising campaign in the United States by the National Youth Anti-Drug Media Campaign that proselytizes against recreational drug use, focusing explicitly on cannabis use by American teenagers. The program's primary medium is television advertisements, but it also makes use of others such as online web banners.

These advertisements usually discuss the consequences of drug use, through metaphor of the effects drug use has on the individual, contrary to the analogous Truth campaign against tobacco. Unlike the Truth campaign, Above the Influence relies heavily on a fictional story to relay its message to its audience, as opposed to performing attention grabbing stunts to bring attention to disconcerting facts, as Truth does.

An example of metaphor is a recently released television commercial where students in an archetypal American high school are seen placing leeches on their legs and in their mouths in a fictional process called "slomming," which is an acronym for Sticking Leeches On Myself. The commercial ends with a recording of a teenager saying "What could you be convinced to do?" dubbed over a stop animation clip of cannabis being rolled into a joint, implying that recreational use of drugs based on peer pressure is equivalent, in terms of sensibility, to attaching leeches to oneself based on peer pressure.
