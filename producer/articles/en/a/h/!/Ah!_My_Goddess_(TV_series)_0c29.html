In 2005, a new television anime series of Ah! My Goddess! (Japanese: ああっ女神さまっ, - Aa! Megami-sama!) began airing in Japan, again produced by AIC and distributed in the US by Media Blasters[1]. This version has returned to the manga roots of the series, retelling events much closer to their original form. Though there are minor differences, typically based on what is considered unacceptable for television, this version follows the canon of the manga quite closely. Although only 24 episodes and one recap were aired, the Japanese DVD release had two more bonus episodes that haven't and will not air on TV.

A second season titled Ah! My Goddess: Everyone Has Wings (Japanese: ああっ女神さまっ それぞれの翼, - Aa! Megami-sama: Sorezore no Tsubasa) had resumed the story on 6 April 2006 from where the series left off in season one.

Director Hiroaki Gohda has already stated that he'd like to be able to cover all of the manga in the TV series, making future seasons beyond the 2nd a likely possibility[citation needed]

[edit] Story

The plot and story of the TV series sticks as close as possible to the manga, however some changes have been made. The order of occurrence of some events has been changed, while other events have been replaced with similar events that borrow concepts from the manga but place them in the context of a new story.

[edit] 1st Season

The 1st season of the Ah! My Goddess TV series follows the manga from Keiichi's first meeting with Belldandy up to the "Lord of Terror" ("Terrible Master Urd" in the Dark Horse version) story arc. In general, the 1st season covers the same territory as the manga up to that arc with some minor omissions. There are also some additions, mostly from later chapters, notably the events of chapter 50 (meet me by the seashore) now occur before the Lord of Terror arc and not after, also a new story involving a women's kart race is inserted that borrows elements from chapter 121 of the manga. Also, events concerning the angels of the two elder goddesses (Belldandy and Urd) are inserted (since the angels themselves make an earlier appearance in the anime).

After the original 24 episode run, 2 bonus episodes based on the "Trials of Morisato" story arc were added to the DVD release

[edit] 2nd Season: Sorezore no Tsubasa (Everyone Has Wings)

Logo from season 2

The 2nd season, while still following the manga, takes a bit more liberty with the events. After a few one-shot episodes, this new season jumps straight into the "Fourth Goddess" story arc, seemingly rearranging the order of the story arcs (this arc came after the "Ninja Master" and "the Devil in Miss Urd" arcs in the manga, but these arcs come after it in the anime). The series also skips the "Welsper" arcs and rewrites some of the later events that feature Welsper.

Two OVA episodes were released with the final DVD on 23 February 2007.

[edit] 3rd Season and Beyond

TBS has announced that they are waiting until sales figures on the final DVD, Volume 8 of the Season 2 DVD's, before deciding whether to go forward or not. While sales of their DVD's have been moderate, and the upcoming 20th Anniversary celebration that Kodansha has planned, it's rumored that there will be a third season. News will be forthcoming at the end of February 2007 on the decision by TBS whether to continue the series.

[edit] Season One

  • In the episode 11 of the first season, Keiichi is turned to a Honda Cub by Marller, but in the original manga he is turned as a salamander.

[edit] Season Two

  • In episode 7 of the second TV season, Keiichi mentioned about wishing to Peorth for making Valentino Rossi to be the champion for 2006 MotoGP season. In the original manga, Keiichi mentioned for Kenny Roberts instead.
  • In episode 17 of season two TV series, Urd was the one used as part of a ritual and not Peorth from the manga version. Welsper was also not in this episode.
  • In episode 18 of season two TV series, Peorth and Welsper was not featured as one of the 7 who was trapped by the cursed lamp. Additional characters were Toraichi Tamiya, Otaki Aoyama, and Sentaro Kawanishi.

[edit] Trivia

This article contains a trivia section.
Content in this section should be integrated into other appropriate areas of the article or removed, and the trivia section removed.
  • Notably, Kikuko Inoue still does Belldandy's voice work, along with Masami Kikuchi, Yumi Toma, and Aya Hisakawa as Keiichi, Urd, and Skuld, despite how long it is since the original show was made.
  • The first episodes of both seasons begin with a short narration by one of the goddesses; in the first season, Urd does the introductory narration, in season 2 it's Peorth.
  • Numerous well known cars and motorcycles make cameo appearances, including the exceedingly rare Enzo Ferrari and SLR McLaren (used by Toshiyuki Aoshima and Sayoko Mishima respectively, these replace the 288 GTO and a AMG SL that they respectively drove in the original manga), reflecting series creator Kosuke Fujishima's love for cars and motorcycles.
  • In episode 18 of the 1st TV series (based on volume 8, chapter 50 of the manga), Skuld, in response to Keiichi being unable to swim gives him "Marine configuration Banpei", dubbing it "Blue 6". This name is likely a reference to the OVA series, Blue Submarine No. 6.
  • In episode 20 of the second TV season, Skuld mentions that in order to reset the Space Doubler, Keiichi has to put the Konami Code (↑ ↑ ↓ ↓ ← → ← → B A), except instead of pressing B A, he has to press "Set" twice.
