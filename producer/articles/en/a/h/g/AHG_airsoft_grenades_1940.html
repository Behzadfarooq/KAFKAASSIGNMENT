[edit] AHG airsoft grenades

AHG stands for "Airsoft Hand Grenade". AHG grenades have two designs, the AG-01 (Airsoft Grenade mach 1) and AG-02 (Airsoft Grenade mach 2).


[edit] Composition and Mechanics

Each grenade is composed of a disposable foam shell and a "primer" which sets the mechanics of the grenade into motion. The grenade utilizes the effects of a chemical reaction using a raising agent (soda bicarbonate [household baking soda]) and an acid (citric acid as found in lemons]). The effect is similar to that of a "baking soda and vinegar" reaction: when the acid is released into the base via the "primer", the reaction builds gas. When enough gas builds, the foam shell ruptures and the BB's encased inside and pieces of foam shrapnel erupt in all directions. The rupture also gives off a short audible report, making the detonation known around the playing field. Disposable replacement shells can be applied to the reusable "primer" after detonation.

[edit] Operation

For operation, pull the pin lock out of its hole under the detonator button. Then, push the button and throw it a minimal safe distance.

[edit] AG-02

The difference between the mach 1 and mach 2 models is that the mach 2 model has an 8-second delay. It also seems to have a harder foam shell than that of its predecessor.
