Hi Ahostett.

Welcome to Wikipedia. Just a quick note about the Havelock (which, as a native Grimbarian, is always how I've spelt the word, although I bow to your knowledge there). I freely acknowledge that the other page about Havelok was much more complete and already existed when I created the 'Lay' page. Indeed I have just completed merge the two articles! However, could I please point out a couple of points of etiquette. Firstly, never just delete a page and leave it blank. If you think another page does a better job, then please use a redirect link to point the user in the correct direction. Secondly, rather than just deleting the page outright, it is better to post a tag proposing a merge with the other page. And finally, whatever you do, it is always polite to try and complete the edit summary box and write in the discussion page about what your doing.

Again, I'm really not trying to raise any hackles! I'm just trying to help you avoid raising other people's in future. Cheers! --Byron Farrow 04:00, 9 July 2006 (UTC)

[edit] Thank you, sir

Yes, I am a bit fuzzy on the netiquette in these parts -- thank you for the tips and comments. And thank you for the kind instruction.

The name hardly has a standard spelling at the time. "Havelock" would be the way we would spell in modern English. "Havelok" is how it is spelt in the manuscript (though even there its spelled numerous different ways).

I have a question for you, sir. Have you seen the Grim stone? I have just seen some photos, but will try to make a pass through Grimsby on my next research trip.
