[edit] Move from Ahn Eak-tae to Ahn Ikte

This was all totally accidental.

I didn't know that Ahn Eak-tae article existed, so I began this article. But I didn't want to destroy my work & it was already much better than the other article & had tons of citations. So, I hope everybody understands.

(Wikimachine 05:44, 1 July 2006 (UTC))

The move was done improperly: 1) moved without discussion to a romanization that the composer himself never used, against our normal policy; 2) page "history" was not also carried over. Let's have more "discussion" first in the future when trying to do this sort of thing. Badagnani 12:49, 1 July 2006 (UTC)
I'm fine with this as long as my work isn't destroyed. Thanks a lot. I don't mean to begin any disputes. (Wikimachine 14:34, 1 July 2006 (UTC))

[edit] Move proposal

According to Google, the preferred romanization is Ahn Eak-tai. I hereby propose moving to Ahn Eak-tai. We should probably use the romanization Ahn preferred (if this is indeed the one), as is the case for other Korean names (such as those of Korean presidents). Badagnani 14:46, 1 July 2006 (UTC)

Support. But it will take a lot of work to relocate all the redirects. (Wikimachine 05:51, 2 July 2006 (UTC))
