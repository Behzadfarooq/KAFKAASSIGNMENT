"Ah, the Nostalgic Gloominess of Youth" is the third episode of the anime Yamato Nadeshiko Schichi Henge. This episode was released on October 17th, 2006 from Nippon Animation. Yamato Nadeshiko Schichi Henge is based on the manga titled The Wallflower by Japanese author Tomoko Hayakawa.


[edit] Plot summary

Spoiler warning: Plot and/or ending details follow.

The episode begins with Sunako and the boys heading to school amidst a throng of female admirers. Unable to cope with Kyohei's good looks, Sunako further fantasies about turning him into a beautiful corpse. Unfortunately she is unable to imagine Kyohei without him becoming nude, and collapses in a nosebleed. Kyohei is then approached by a group of black-suited men who try to recruit Kyohei for a host club. He turns them down, despite being offered a salary of 1, 000, 00 yen.

Later that night as Sunako prepares dinner, the Landlady arrives unannounced. She states that she couldn't wait to see her Sunako once she heard that her niece is already becoming a beautiful lady. The boys begin to make excuses as to why the Landlady can't see her, saying that she is cooking. The Landlady then says that she is preparing a coming out party for Sunako, complete with rich and powerful people. She then departs in her helicopter.

At dinner none of the boys (except Kyohei) are able to eat. As she watches Kyohei eat, Sunako debates on which method she will use to murder Kyohei. Sunako is brought out of her imaginings by Takanaga, who tells her that she must become a lady by Sunday. Sunako initially refuses, but is bribed into the training by the promise of ultra violent movies. She is placed through intense training, only to meet with comic results.

Despite her earlier promise to cooperate, Sunako tries to get out of her training. This sparks an argument between Kyohei and Sunako, with Sunako claiming that Kyohei could not understand what she is going through. Angry, Kyohei responds that everyone has worked hard for her sake, and that she is truly an ugly girl. The two stalk off in opposite directions, despite the remaining boys attempts to placate Sunako. As Sunako leaves Yuki discovers a notepad containing Sunako’s assassination ideas.

Several days progress without Sunako emerging from her room. As the boys go over the plans for the party they realize that no one has been taking her food. They break into her room, finding her half starved and delirious. After force-feeding her onigiri, Kyohei still refuses to apologize. However he does tell her that she shouldn’t shut herself off because of one guy; that there are other men out there for her. A brief “moment” passes between them, only to be ruined by Sunako’s nosebleed.

Later that night Sunako attempts to end Kyohei’s life, only to be met by a seductive set up by Kyohei. As he expected, Sunako is overwhelmed by Kyohei’s appearance and becomes petrified. While Kyohei congratulates himself on his success the host club recruiters break into his room and kidnap him.

After Sunako regains consciousness everyone realizes that Kyohei has been abducted. They discover the club’s address from a card dropped on the floor. Later, after an arduous battle due to forcing Sunako to dress up, they arrive at the club. Noi, having called during the makeup application, tags along. Noi is puzzled, yet thrilled, at the sight of the boys in drag. They state that the cross-dressing was necessary for anonymity. Much to everyone’s surprise Sunako adapts easily to social scene, saving them from being immediately discovered. The room then grows silent as the club’s owner announces an auction with Kyohei as the prize!

A furious Sunako rescues Kyohei, but not without demolishing the club in the process. As the club owner tries to escape the Landlady (whom he had been dating) appears to administer her own sense of justice as well. Outside the club Kyohei whispers thank you into Sunako’s ear, only to have her pass out again.

Later the next day the Landlady cancels the party due to the night’s antics. She also cancels the free rent, making the boys pay half. The Landlady then departs in an overly long limousine with her new “darling”. As she departs Sunako joins them on the front steps holding a rose, only to have it wilt and die at her touch.

[edit] Trivia

  • The Landlady's "darling" is actually Nabeshin, a director of the series. He has had cameo appearances in other works of his, most notably in Excel Saga.
