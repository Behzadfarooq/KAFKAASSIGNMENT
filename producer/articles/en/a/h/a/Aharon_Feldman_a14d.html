Aharon Feldman

Rabbi Aharon Feldman is the Rosh Yeshsiva (Dean) of the Ner Israel Rabbinical College (also known as NIRC) an Orthodox yeshiva in Baltimore, Maryland and one of the premier yeshivas in the world. He has held this position since 2000.

His is part of the Feldman rabbinic family, his brother being Emanuel Feldman the current editor of Tradition Magazine.

In 1994 he spoke publicly against the actions of Baruch Goldstein saying that there could be "no justification," and describing the actions as "way beyond the pale."[1]

He was interviewed in the 2001 documentary film Trembling before God about young orthodox Jewish gay men.[2]

In 2005 he was one of 15 Jewish educators invited to an informal discussion on Jewish education in the White House's Roosevelt Room.[3]
