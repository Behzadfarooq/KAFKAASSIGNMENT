[edit] Background

Lee Quinn is a high school teacher at Needham B. Broughton High School in Raleigh, NC.

He teaches IB History of the Americas at Broughton, a well known class among many generations of IB students. Quinn has become a trademark at Broughton High School, known for his interesting lectures and odd stories, as well as excellent teaching.

[edit] Richard H. Jenrette Teaching Excellence Award

Lee Quinn won this award after being selected by a review commitee led by The Wake Education Parntership. After intensive review of many candidates, Mr. Quinn was selected for the award for his excellence in teaching. The award includes a $15,000 education grant.

[edit] NEH Seminar

Quinn participated in the 2004 NEH Summer Seminar, where he researched Joseph Quinn in Ireland, and his reasons for immigrating to the United States.

The seminar first went to Ireland for two months, where they studied primary documents (ex: letters) at many small villages. The seminar then returned to Washington, D.C. to meet and present the findings.

The seminar was led by notable historian, Dr. Meagher

[edit] Quinn Trademarks

This section attempts to summarize the numerous Quinn "trademarks" famous among his students, past and present.

[edit] Fresh Max

Quinn's best friend, a mysterious man who goes by the name of "Fresh Max", is a central figure in the Quinn "culture". Fresh Max has been on all of the Quinn road trips, and is a central figure in many of Mr. Quinn's stories.

[edit] Road Trips

A trademark of Quinn, his infamous travel journals and stories are a source of endless amusement for his IB students. His lectures occasionally bring up random events from his road trips, which he then shares with the class (excitedly). These have included: Reworking the nations currency and camping in North Dakota.

Quinn has also been on several research trips to Europe, notably Ireland where he studied the Irish Potato Famine.

[edit] Currency Redesign

He developed his currency plan in a closing Seattle, Washington coffee shop. With Freshmax's assistance, all American currency was redone, complete with sketches and summaries of each coin/bill. The sketches have been shared with many of his IB classes, and he has some interesting ideas.

[edit] IB World Programme

Lee Quinn is a strong proponent of the IB program, which has tried to set an international standard of learning. He teaches IB History of Americas, the core [IB] history class, and has also written the curriculum for a world religions class, which he is trying to get approved by [IB] for inclusion in the official [IB] curriculum.

[edit] IB History of the Americas

Quinn's most popular class, all IB Senior years students must take IB History of the Americas as a higher level (HL) course. You must take three (3) high level (HL) courses if you are in the IB senior years program, in order to get an IB diploma. IB HoA is taken a students Junior high school year, and followed with IB Twentieth Century the students Senior year.

Students in this class learn about American history in it's entirety, from around 1650 to present day. Quinn presents this information usually in lecture form. Lee Quinn is actually interested in what he teaches, making him a very good and effective teacher.

This class is often seen as "hard" by underclassman and actually deters many students from choosing the IB program over an alternative such as AP. While the class is challenging at times, you learn:

  • A large amount of history, how it really happened, because you examine primary documents, not what the history books say.
  • Your writing skills GREATLY improve. Quinn is known to be a stickler for this, but he helps you alot.
  • How to research for papers, find primary documents, cite sources, format formal research papers.
  • Helps alot in preparing for college. Many of Quinn's former students have come back to tell underclassman how much this class helped them thier freshman year in college.
