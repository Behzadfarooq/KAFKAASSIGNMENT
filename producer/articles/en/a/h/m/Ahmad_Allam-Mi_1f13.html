Ahmad Allam-Mi

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Ahmad Allam-Mi has been Minister of Foreign Affairs & African Integration of Chad[1] since 7 August 2005 when Prime Minister Pascal Yoadimnadji reshuffled the government.[2] He had previously been in the 1980s[3] and in the 1990s Chad's Ambassador to France.[4]

Allam-Mi told the General Assembly on 22 September, 2006, "the continuing armed conflict and humanitarian crisis engulfing Sudan's Darfur region threaten the security of the entire region." Allam-Mi said Chad has had to house over 300,000 refugees since the Darfur crisis began and at least 50,000 Chadians have been displaced and hundreds have died. Allam-Mi called on the United Nations to take over peacekeeping operations as approved in United Nations Security Council Resolution 1706, and that the deployment of peacekeepers would not violate Sudan's sovereignty.[5]

On August 6, 2006, Allam-Mi attended the meeting in Beijing, People's Republic of China, where Chad and China formally re-established diplomatic ties. The two countries had initiated relations in 1972, but the PRC broke them off in 1997 when Chad established ties with the Republic of China. [6]
