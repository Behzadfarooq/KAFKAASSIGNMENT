Ahmet Dağtekin

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Ahmet Dağtekin was the head of the pro-Kurdish Democratic People's Party in Turkey.[1] In July, along with Handan Çağlayan, he was convicted in a Halfeti court for using the Kurdish language during a 2004 campaign event.[2] Dağtekin was sentenced to a 6-month prision term by the court, in addition to a fine of $326 (440 lira).[2] The rulings were under appeal at year's end.[2]
