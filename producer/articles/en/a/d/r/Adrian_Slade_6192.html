Adrian Slade, born 1939, is a British Liberal Democrat politician.

He was educated at Trinity College, Cambridge, where he became Presidents of the Footlights, and famously recruited Peter Cook to the society.

He was a Liberal parliamentary candidate in the 1970s. In 1981 he scored an upset win in the GLC elections, winning the Richmond seat from the Conservatives by just 115 votes [1], making him the first and only Liberal to win a seat on the GLC. He became Leader of the SDP-Liberal Alliance group on the GLC, and remained so until the GLC's dissolution in 1986.

He served as the last ever President of the Liberal Party, from 1987 to 1988, conducting its merger negotiations with the SDP.
