Adrián Campos Suñer (born in Alcira, Valencia, June 17, 1960) was a Formula One driver from Spain. He participated in 21 grands prix, debuting on April 12, 1987. He scored no championship points.

He only completed two of the 21 races he entered, although more of these were due to mechanical failures than driving errors. However, these retirements caused him to lose his motivation in 1987, and he was replaced by Pierluigi Martini after failing to qualify three times in five races. After F1 he has been successfully involved in team management, helping the careers of Fernando Alonso and Marc Gené among others. He also runs the Campos Racing team in the GP2 Series.
