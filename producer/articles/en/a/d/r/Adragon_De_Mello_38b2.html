Adragon De Mello

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Adragon De Mello (born 1977) graduated from the University of California, Santa Cruz with a degree in computational mathematics in 1988, at age 11, becoming the youngest college graduate in U.S. history.[1]

It was widely publicized that his father, Agustin Eastwood De Mello (born 1930), had set the goal that his son would become a Nobel Prize laureate by age 16.[1] The elder De Mello was a karate master and former weightlifting champion, and he vigorously mentored Adragon from an early age.

By 1989, his parents had separated and, after a brief period in foster care, he returned to the custody of his mother, Cathy Gunn. Although a college graduate, he opted to enroll in Sunnyvale Junior High School (now Sunnyvale Middle School) under the assumed name of James Gunn. He found it "nice because no one knew who [he] was" and was "upset" when local papers identified him after his graduation.[2] In 1994, he graduated from Homestead High School.

In 2000, he was training to be an estimator for a commercial painting company.[2]

On March 15, 2001, the elder De Mello, who had allegedly called 9-1-1 claiming to be suicidal, was involved in armed standoff with Santa Cruz police. He was subsequently charged with assault with a deadly weapon. De Mello, suffering from bladder cancer, was released to the custody of his son.[1] Agustin died on May 30, 2003.[3]

As of 2003, De Mello was working for The Home Depot.[3]
