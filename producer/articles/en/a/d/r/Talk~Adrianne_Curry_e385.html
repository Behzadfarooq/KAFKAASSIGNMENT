[edit] fair use and the Playboy Cover

This quote from the WP:FU guidelines was used to justify replacing (for the second time) the playboy cover image in this article:

"However, if that magazine issue itself is notable enough to be a topic within the article, then fair use may apply."

The issue is not a topic within the article; it's simply mentioned in one sentence and not discussed at all. A topic would involve the article something notable at the shooting, the public reception of the picture, and so on. The article only devotes a sentence to the issue, and that doesn't discuss the magazine itself or its notability. This article doesn't discuss the Playboy issue other than to mention Adrianne's presence in the issue, and doesn't provide critical commentary of the issue or the photograph.

I have removed the cover image again, as I don't believe it falls short of establishing fair use for its inclusion in this article. If anyone disagrees, I'd welcome a third opinon review. -- Mikeblas 01:53, 23 July 2006 (UTC)

I believe the image was used as a visual for Adrianne Curry. Practically every biographical article here includes a photo of the person the article is about. Jtrost (T | C | #) 02:06, 23 July 2006 (UTC)
Of course. Problem is, magazine covers are protected copyright. Using the image to discuss the magazine is fair use; using the magazine image to discuss the person on the cover is not. -- Mikeblas 02:13, 23 July 2006 (UTC)
I have removed the image again. -- Mikeblas 04:33, 10 November 2006 (UTC)
