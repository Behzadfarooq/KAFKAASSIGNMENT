The Help Section

Could someone please tell me where to download skins for Wikipedia and how to install them?

Can you clarify what you mean by skins? Skins are available in user preferences but are not downloadable and installable other than by the wikimedia server admins. --pgk 18:56, 9 September 2006 (UTC)
The reason for my question is that if you look around wikipedia some peoples user space looks different to others (e.g. User:Essjay), some people refer to those as skins, though they are create through normal wiki markup on the pages. If you are interested in general look of all wikipedia pages you can override .css elements via your own User:Adriaan90/monobook.css, though I'm not that familiar with it nor do I know of any guides. --pgk 19:10, 9 September 2006 (UTC)

SouthAfrican geo stub template

Hi Adriaan

I don't know which unwritten convention you are referring to, but the stub templates are maintained by WP:WSS which has not approved anything of the sort. We format our templates according to a standard look specified by WP:STUB and we try very hard to preserve a consistent look for the templates. You can see the standard code on WP:STUB. I have sorted countless stubs and cleaned up the code of several hundreds of similar stub templates, and the South African one is the one sticking out. This becomes more obvious when articles use more than one stub template, which is often the case. If you feel that our guideline's standard code should be revised, feel free to propose it at WP:WSS, but for the moment I am reverting your edit to our standard layout. I see no reason why the South African template should use a different format than - effectively - all other stub templates. Cheers. Valentinian (talk) / (contribs) 18:42, 6 October 2006 (UTC)

No harm done :) You are right that a few templates used a differing format for some unknown reason, but WP:WSS is trying to straighten things out. Stub templates aren't pretty but they look a bit better with a more consistent format. Happy editing. Valentinian (talk) / (contribs) 19:45, 6 October 2006 (UTC)
