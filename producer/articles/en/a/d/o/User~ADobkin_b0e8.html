I am a system and network administrator. I operate a computer and network consulting/support company in Metro Atlanta, Georgia called Maestro IT Services, LLC. We support the use of many commercial and open source software applications and utilities for our clients, including ClamAV, Cygwin, Horde, MailScanner, OpenOffice.org, Nmap, RealVNC, Webmin/Usermin, and of course, the Linux operating system (mostly the Red Hat-based distributions, CentOS and Fedora Core).

Formerly, I worked for Nortel Networks, which acquired the smaller company Alteon WebSystems, and the even smaller startup company Pharsalia Technologies. Prior to that, I worked for Emory University in the IT Division, which I started while I was a college student there.
