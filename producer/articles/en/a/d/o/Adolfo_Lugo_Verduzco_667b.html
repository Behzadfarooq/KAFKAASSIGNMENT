Adolfo Lugo Verduzco

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Adolfo Lugo Verduzco (b. March 24, 1933 in Huichapan, Hidalgo) is a Mexican politician affiliated to the Institutional Revolutionary Party (PRI). He is a former senator, chairman of the PRI and governor of Hidalgo (1987 – 1993).

Lugo Verduzco is the son of Adolfo Lugo Guerrero and Magdalena Verduzco Andrade. He received a bachelor's degree in law from the National Autonomous University of Mexico, a master's degree in public admnistration from the Institute of Social Studies at The Hague (Netherlands) and took a specialization in public administration at the École nationale d'administration (France).

He joined the Institutional Revolutionary Party in 1952 and chaired it from 1982 until 1986. He also served in the Mexican Senate from 1982 until 1988.

Source: Diccionario biográfico del gobierno mexicano, Ed. Fondo de Cultura Económica, Mexico, 1992.
