Adonis Hilario

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Adonis Hilario Vieria is a former Brazilian soccer striker who played most of his career with Deportivo Saprissa of Costa Rica, during the 1980s and 1990s.

With Saprissa, he won three national championships, as well as one CONCACAF Champions Cups. He also played with C.S. Herediano and A.D. Guanacaste, as well as Club Deportivo Aguila of El Salvador, among other teams in his native Brazil.

Hilario was an excellent goal scorer, very quick and gifted, with great skills for shooting, and scoring headers. He is always remembered by Saprissa's fans, because of his unique talents.

He was the best goal scorer in the 1991 Costa Rica's first division tournament, in which he managed to score 26 goals. After retiring, he began coaching in Saprissa's minor league system, where he still works.
