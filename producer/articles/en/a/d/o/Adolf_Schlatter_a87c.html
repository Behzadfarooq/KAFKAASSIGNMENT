Adolf Schlatter (* 16 August 1852 in pc. Gallen; † 19. May 1938 in Tübingen) was an Evangelist theologian and professor for new will and systematics in grab forest, Berlin and Tübingen.

Schlatter was son of a pietistischen Predigers, in the years 1871-1875 studied it philosophy and theology in Basel and Tübingen. In the year 1880 it habilitierte. In the year 1888 he became lecturer at the University of Berne. Professuren in grab forest and Berlin, where it should form a Gegenpol to Adolf of Harnack, followed (since 1893) and Tübingen (from 1898 to 1930).

It was since the year 1897 beside Hermann Cremer co-editor of the magazine of “contributions for the promotion of Christian theology”.

Schlatter became in particular by its comments the new will admits, which were addressed to a broad public also. It was convinced of revealing God in nature and in Jesus Christ. This conviction brought it to a criticism of the religion-philosophical ideas of the German idealism. The realistic Bible interpretation brought Schlatter also in conflict with contemporary currents in the Evangelist church. Schlatter strove besides for the development of a theory of knowledge, which was to be brought with its religious convictions in agreement.

In Stuttgart Adolf Schlatter archives and a Adolf Schlatter donation exist.

In Tübingen the “Adolf Schlatter house” is designated in the Österbergstrasse after it and into Recke the “Adolf Schlatter home”.
