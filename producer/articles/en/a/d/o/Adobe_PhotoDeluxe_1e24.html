Adobe PhotoDeluxe

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

PhotoDeluxe was a consumer oriented image editing software line published by Adobe System until July 8, 2002. At that time it was replaced by Adobe's newly launched consumer oriented image editing software, Photoshop Elements. Technical support is no longer provided by Adobe for the PhotoDeluxe software line.

PhotoDeluxe had a limited range of image processing capabilities such as removing red-eye, cropping, and adjusting brightness, contrast, and sharpness. It also included software to extract pictures from an image scanner.
