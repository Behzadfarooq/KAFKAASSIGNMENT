Adolf Meyer (architect)

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

For other persons with the same name, see Adolf Meyer

Adolf Meyer (1881 – 1929) was a German architect. A student and employee of Peter Behrens, Meyer became the office boss of the firm of Walter Gropius around 1915 and a full partner afterwards. In 1919 Gropius appointed Meyer as a master at the Bauhaus, where he taught work drawing and construction technique. Meyer is also credited as co-designer of the Gropius entry for the 1922 Chicago Tribune Tower competition.

From 1925 he practiced as an independent architect in Frankfurt am Main.
