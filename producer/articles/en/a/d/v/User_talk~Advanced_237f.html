Hello, Advanced, and welcome to Wikipedia! Thanks for your contributions; I hope you like it here and decide to stay. We're glad to have you in our community! Here are a few good links for newcomers:

  • If you haven't already, drop by the new user log and tell others a bit about yourself.
  • Always sign your posts on talk pages ~~~~ so others will know who left which comments.

  • Our policies, guidelines, and simplified ruleset

Happy editing!

[edit] Re: RI

To quote from WikiProject Schools, "the key to writing a good school article is to explain why the school is unique". I'm not a deletionist by any means, but the RI article is filled with information that either a) could apply to 90% of the other secondary schools in Singapore (such as the two-sentence-long section on OBS) or b) is non-notable to anyone other than Raffles Archive (such as the very short-lived existence of SIGs, or (for God's sake) the sort of ties that the male teachers receive from the school).

It wouldn't even be so bad if the information was

  • well-substantiated — ("The performing arts groups have also done consistently well in the Singapore Youth Festival Central Judging, held once every two years, while the clubs and socities have won many awards too.")
  • written in a neutral point of view — ("The gryphon is a stately creature, majestic and strong, symbolizing stability and success for the school.") and/or
  • referenced — ("It is said that it is because the nowadays students spend too much time on the computer, and since the mission of the programme was to encourage students to use computers, its purpose had already been achieved"; "The inclusion of Prometheus is for his flame-bringing characteristic, signifying entrepreneurship while 'God' in this context is used ambiguously and as a reference to a higher being.").

The article is simply riddled with problems that no one wants to solve. Every day, RI boys ignore the cleanup tags on the article and continue to add more useless, unverifiable original research to the article. Verifiability is the key word here - any information that cannot be verified or sourced is most likely too non-notable to be included in the article. If you want the information I removed to remain in the article, then take up the challenge and fix it. Wikipedia has three fundamental policies governing article content: WP:V, WP:NOR, and WP:NPOV. As it stands, the article ignores the first two policies completely and rarely conforms to the third. If you can rewrite the article to follow all three policies, then I will stop deleting information I consider to be detrimental to the quality of the article. -ryand 15:38, 12 February 2007 (UTC)

Thanks for your reply. All the best then - it's a lot of work to do. Drop me a message if you need any help and I'll try my best to. :) -ryand 16:22, 12 February 2007 (UTC)

[edit] C151 photography

The photos for the refurbished C151 were great, please keep up the good job!

In the meantime, if you have time to spare I think it's time we started taking photos of empty unrefurbished C151's interior (public accessible areas) inside out while they are still in the majority. Ideally we should have a extensive, free collection (perhaps a commons page of its own!) before the last C151 rolls into the depot for refurbishment.

Some things you may want to consider :

  • All three compartments D-M-M.
  • Corridor, door and safety features.
  • For driving motor, the front with the builder plate and the door in full view. If you have a lot of time, collect all four plates!
  • Experimental units w/ the dynamic in-train system

If you have some in your hard drive, do upload them! Happy Chinese New Year and train spotting! :) - Cheers, Mailer Diablo 08:28, 18 February 2007 (UTC)
