[edit] History

Adventure Games is the largest gaming store[citation needed] in NEPA, since it was opened in November of 2001. It is located in Dickson City, PA, which is near Scranton, PA. It started out as a computer gaming, Collectible Card Games, Role-Playing Game and retail store. But, since being open for 5 years[citation needed], they have expanded to have 2 store fronts. They now have a Dance Dance Revolution machine with a spacious lounge, a Console Theater, which is much like a home theater system in a large room, a private gaming room (for Dungeons and Dragons, etc...}, a karaoke room and scheduled events. The last Saturday of every month, they have an event called an All-Nighter. Every other week, they have a group of miniature battle gamers come in, called The NEPA Table-Top Warlords. They play varied miniature games and the store is full of people for these Saturday events. They recently won 2 Best of NEPA awards. One was for Best Place To Game, the other was Best Local Website.[citation needed] See the article here.

[edit] About Adventure Games

[edit] Retail Store

Adventure Games sells equipment for role-playing games: books, dice, miniatures and paint. They sell Magic: The Gathering cards and have thousands of cards in stock[citation needed]. They also sell the rare Star Wars RPG miniatures from the original West End Games, along with Reaper Miniatures and other types as well. They have everything you need for collectible card games as well.

Candle light Magic: The Gathering

They also have snacks and beverages for sale in the store.


[edit] The LAN

Image from the LAN
Image from the LAN

Adventure Games also has a 10 computer LAN, with many games on it[citation needed]. This is in a comfortable environment[citation needed] in the main store front. They will be getting a new LAN in the later spring/early summer of 2007.[citation needed]

[edit] Rules

Rules of LAN use are as follows:

  • No Internet Browsing
    • Does not include game related (FAQs, etc) webpages
  • No food at the LAN
  • No Pornography
  • You must pay before you use the LAN

[edit] Console Theater

The Console Theater is two 85 sq.ft. gaming screens, and in the room itself, it has a Playstation 3, Wii Playstation 2, Xbox, Gamecube, and Super Nintendo. Customers may bring their computers, games and consoles to the room. They have a selection of games for each system as well. It is able to be used as a movie theater as well. It has fiberoptic cabling, a Bose Corporation sound system and many places to sit down and play or watch. This is in the second store front, right near the Dance Dance Revolution lounge.
