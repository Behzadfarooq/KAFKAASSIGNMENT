[edit] Re: Big Four

I got Christ Illusion by Slayer to GA, and with the effort of others am going to get it to FA. In response to your proposal, I'm just keen to know a few things;

1. Your honest opinions on the average efforts of the Metal Wikiproject in improving Metal articles.

2. How you want these articles improved - are you of the mind of the Metal Wikiproject, or not? I personally am not for several reasons. LuciferMorgan 01:20, 25 February 2007 (UTC)

In response to your message, I'm afraid I won't be joining though thanks for the offer.
I find the Metal Wikiproject to be a disappointment frankly, and a shame considering what it could be (as opposed to what it is). When I attempted to keep Iron Maiden as an FA, I found their members largely disruptive, and very unhelpful to my cause. Additionally, when Skeletor worked his backside off to get Megadeth to FA hardly anyone helped, and the same for Metal Head when he worked on Slayer. As mostly a lone force in the Slayer Wikiproject, he's got down to some serious business and notched up an additional 3 GAs largely of his own accord. I got Christ Illusion to GA but that's it, and I rewrote Eyes of the Insane to B status.
If the Metal Wikiproject considers itself serious, then it's having a laugh. Me and Metal Head are more serious than all them put together, and the Slayer Wikiproject runs rings around the Metal Wikiproject to be frank.
It makes no valid effort to improvise articles, no nothing. It spends more time debating which genres a band belongs in than actually doing work, and when it does work all it does is add crufty original research in an article - typical words they use are "legendary", "classic" etc. in articles, and that's exactly what they shouldn't do. How about inline citations, WP:GA? or WP:FA?? Scan most of the Metal articles and they don't strive towards such goals. The Slayer article should be what other metal articles strive for, but they don't. I can't see Metal Head folding Slayer into the Big Four for the above reasons to be honest, as when the Metal Project members arrive with their crufty edits it means me and him would have to mop up behind them. I'm sorry if my tone seems derogatory, but it's aimed at the Metal Wikiproject generally as opposed to yourself - I don't know you as a Wikipedian, so cannot comment on your efforts. If I thought the Big Four could aspire to what the Slayer Project is but on a larger scope, I would've joined - but unless Wikipedia has more editor s like Metal Head, I don't think it could aspire to such levels. Good luck though - it's a shame I can't join really, as I love the Big Four. LuciferMorgan 07:24, 25 February 2007 (UTC)

[edit] Wikipedia:WikiProject Big Four of Thrash Metal

Please be advised that this project you created is being discussed in accord with wikipedia policy. Please feel free to go to Wikipedia:Miscellany for deletion/Wikipedia:WikiProject Big Four of Thrash Metal to take part in the discussion. Thank you. John Carter 19:32, 17 March 2007 (UTC)

As I stated at Wikipedia:Miscellany for deletion/Wikipedia:WikiProject Big Four of Thrash Metal, perhaps renaming & re-focusing the WikiProject to Wikipedia:WikiProject Thrash Metal or Wikipedia:WikiProject American Thrash Metal would avoid deletion and encourage greater participation.
