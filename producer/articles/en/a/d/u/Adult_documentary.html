An adult documentary is a website about issues related to human sexuality. Although often graphic, these sites are not in the same category as pornography. Common goals among adult documentaries can be to help, educate, inform, encourage awareness, and/or to collect data for research on a particular sexual issue.

The sites are generally not suitable for young children, but most sites contain information that can be applicable to teenage individuals as well as adults. Some sites offer separate sections for younger teens as an adjunct to their main site. [1]

Adult documentaries often encourage participation from the public, especially while the site is in its beginning stages of set up. This has lead to some controversy since such a wide rage of sexual activities and practices are often documented. Activities that are in conflict with certain moral or religious beliefs usually spur debate.

[edit] Overview

An adult documentary is a category of web sites that is difficult to classify in most mainstream web directories and search engines. Due to the nature of their sexual content, they are often mistaken as entertainment sites. However, over two thirds of the readers polled on a popular adult documentary said their intentions for visiting the site was something other than entertainment.[2] To understand the importance an adult documentary, the reader is required to see past certain stereotypes and expectations when it comes to web sites about sex.

Although the sites can be labeled "adult" due to the their topics, most adult documentaries do not require the reader to be of a certain age. However, it is generally understood that the content of the sites is intended for people who have passed through puberty. Some sites are set up for, or have special pages, for teen readers. [1]

[edit] Presentation Format

The following is a list of the most common attributes among adult documentaries. [3],.[4],.[5], [6]

  • The major part of the theme for the site is to help people, educate, and/or generate understanding.
  • Free for the reader. No paid memberships are required to view any content on the domain.
  • The site clearly addresses one or more aspects of human sexuality as its primary purpose, by way of unique content.
  • The site is not simply an informational site with common knowledge or research. The majority of the content is unique to the site.
  • Advertising is secondary in importance, if at all, and the content of the site is always what the author wants people to see most. The site does not exist simply to promote or redirect readers to another site or affiliate.
  • The purpose of the site is stated either on the index page or on a page easily accessible from the index page.
  • The information that is collected and reported to the public is often submitted or suggested by readers of the site.

[edit] Why People Visit Adult Documentaries

An ongoing poll that questions the readers why they visited is generally reflective of most adult documentaries on the Internet. The following lists the results of the poll. [2]:

  • (A) To find out what other people are doing/saying about sexuality - 45.8%
  • (B) Entertainment 30.0%
  • (C) Research - 9.0%
  • (D) Personal Health Concern - 5.6%
  • (E) To write a story or message for the world to read - 1.1%
  • (F) Other reason - 8.6%

Number of persons participating in this survey - 13,243

[edit] Topics

In one word, it could be said that the topic is sex. However, most documentaries go beyond the basic ideas about sex and explore more complex issues that relate to things like: masturbation, sexual orientation, specific techniques or methods, the politics involved in sexuality, philosophy, personal sexual history (biographies), and theory.

[edit] Examples

Examples of adult documentaries include web sites such as: Clitical.com, JackinWorld.com, NVSH.nl, PeterFiles.com, SoloTouch.com, SexEditorials.com, and many others.

[edit] Concerns and Criticism

Most specific and personal concerns can be resolved if the individual takes the time to contact the site about a specific issue (e.g., having a particularly offensive article removed, having an individual's full name removed, or deleting an email address). [7]

The most common overall blanket criticism cites morality as the issue of the complaint. "A significant number of people believe that freely documenting sexual ideas and behaviors leads individuals to such problems as: moral decay, sex addiction, infidelity, increased homosexuality, premarital sex, and various other moral issues." [8]

The moral concerns are usually surrounded by the fact that the documentary may not be professionally monitored, and participation on some sites allows instant and unreviewed publishing on the pages. Most webmasters claim it is an expression of free speech. However, this notion is actually in conflict with the reality that they usually enforce restrictions on their contributors regarding what they write.[9]

Another concern is the outbound links on the site (sponsors). Often the sites are sponsored by people or industries that sell sexual products or services because they are typically the only companies interested in pledging money towards the cost of maintaining this type of documentary site. Although most adult documentaries try to make it clear that outbound links are not part of the original site, people that are inexperienced with this type of format can become confused. Some popular sites have discontinued working with financial sponsors, but now they ask for monetary contributions from readers and participants. Sites with massive amounts of traffic are usually the only ones successful at operating purely on donations. [10]

Concerns about pre-pubescent children participating in the site is somewhat of a concern to the public, and it is usually a major concern for the editors or webmasters of the sites. But there are no effective ways to check the age of participants on free sites with anonymous contributors. However, some documentaries have now taken the approach of linking or redirecting young and sensitive viewers to more age-appropriate sexuality related pages either on their own domain or on other websites. [11]
