    [edit] Another outsider's view

    Greetings, I'm Quadell. I've been away from Wikipedia for a while, but I wanted to come in and give my opinion on this latest mess.

    1. Linuxbeak made a mistake in good faith. He didn't violate any specific policy, but he showed poor judgment. I've shown poor judgment in the past too. He also apologized for unblocking without discussing it here first, and he has said he won't do it again. Dumping on Linuxbeak is rude and inappropriate. Every time you act incivilly, the Wikipedia community loses a little respect for you.
    2. It's horrible that SlimVirgin and others have (temporarily?) left the project over this. I understand why they did, and I sympathize, and I hope this can all be resolved peaceably. But Linuxbeak should not have to apologize for the fact that these admins have left -- they chose to leave. I'm in a similar situation: I left because of the actions of a long-time admin. And I think that admin should apologize for her actions -- but she should not have to apologize for the fact that I left.
    3. I think attempting to unblock someone indefinitely banned should be done no sooner than 3 months after his or her ban, and 6 months is better. Additionally, if that user has recently caused other problems (using sockpuppets to evade the ban, for instance, or posting inflamatory and stalking comments on WR, or permitting the blackmailing of a fine admin) then one should wait 3-6 months before even thinking of unblocking that user.
    4. People who post images and addresses of Wikipedians in order to intimidate or blackmail them are scum. I don't think anyone who does this, or admins on a board which does this, should be tolerated here unless they seek to make amends to the people they have harmed. But that's just my opinion.

    Anyway, I hope this blows over soon. I miss you all. – Quadell (talk) (bounties) 16:50, 1 June 2006 (UTC)
