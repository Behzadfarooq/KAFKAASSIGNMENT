[edit] Another Iraq move problem

The article title "2003 Invasion of Iraq" was criticized by some, and a poll was begun on the talk page on possible alternatives. A day or two ago, while the discussion continued, there was a unilateral move of the article, which prompted someone who objected to the move to do a cut-and-paste move to a title he preferred (see discussion above). Violet/Riga patiently unscrambled the mess, and added an admonition on the talk page: "The current article name (2003 Invasion of Iraq) should not be changed without a lot of discussion...." This sound advice was ignored. Now we have yet another unilateral move, from a user who (it appears to me) hasn't even participated in the ongoing discussion and vote.

As I write this, there are eight votes for "2003 Invasion of Iraq" as the title (some noting that they'd prefer a lower-case "invasion"). There are four votes for "Iraq War". Meanwhile, a user who didn't comment or vote moved the article to "Iraq War of 2003", an alternative no one has even proposed on the talk page.

So, with regret, I must trouble the admins again. Although I also prefer the lower-case "invasion", I think the sensible thing to do is to return this article to the title it had when the vote began, 2003 Invasion of Iraq, and await the outcome of the ongoing process. JamesMLane 03:50, 2 May 2005 (UTC)

Addendum: The user who did the unilateral move tried to make amends by restoring the prior situation. The "Move this page" function didn't work, because of the history, so the user further bollixed things by doing a cut-and-paste move. Now the article text is at 2003 invasion of Iraq but the history is at Iraq War of 2003. The ideal would be to restore everything to 2003 Invasion of Iraq and prevent further page moves until the voting is concluded, but I'm told that can't be done without full-scale protection. JamesMLane 08:01, 2 May 2005 (UTC)

Done. violet/riga (t) 08:11, 2 May 2005 (UTC)
