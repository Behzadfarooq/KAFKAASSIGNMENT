[edit] Protecting the page

I see no reason why this whole page has to be protected. Are there any objections to splitting off the list of admins so that can be protected without affecting the rest of the page? Angela. 19:20, Jan 22, 2004 (UTC)

Then you'd have two vandal targets instead of one. ;) - Hephaestos 19:23, 22 Jan 2004 (UTC)
No, still only one as the list would be protected. I suppose if no one else minds the whole page being protected, then it might as well be left as one page. Angela. 22:10, Jan 25, 2004 (UTC)
I don't see why any regular user would want to edit it in the first place. Ilyanep 23:07, 25 Jan 2004 (UTC)

I mind it being protected. See talk page history (moved to Wikipedia talk:Administrators/protection of this page for your convenience). Regular users have made productive edits to this page in the past, and I hope they will continue to do so. We have over 200,000 "vandal targets", and like wikipedia:this page is protected, a few lightning rods do no harm. Martin 18:03, 7 Feb 2004 (UTC)

Why not create a MediaWiki page with the list on it and protect that while leaving this page unprotected? --mav 10:55, 9 Feb 2004 (UTC)

I think the better question is "why?". It's completely not the end of the world if someone mis-edits the list: it's not like they get adminship by saying they are, and adminship is meant to be a technical thing, not a power thing. Reverted. Martin 23:34, 9 Feb 2004 (UTC)
I agree there seems to be no reason to protect this unnecessarily. Angela. 23:46, Feb 9, 2004 (UTC)
