Adelândia' is a small town and municipality in eastern Goiás state, Brazil.

Important Facts

  • Population (2005 estimate): 2,536
  • Total area: 115.7 km²
  • Elevation: 649 meters
  • Became a municipality: 1989
  • IBGE statistical microregion: Anicuns Microregion
  • Postal code: 76155-000

Location and Communications

  • Distance to the state capital--Goiânia: 129 km.
  • Distance to regional center (São Luís de Montes Belos): 6 km.
  • Highway connections: state highway BR-069 west from Goiânia, through Trindade, São Luís de Montes Belos, and then 6 kilometers to Adelândia

Neighboring municipalities: Mossâmedes, Anicuns, Turvânia, São Luís de Montes Belos, and Sanclerlândia.

  • Population density: 21.65 inhab/km² (2003)

  • Rural population: 2,120 (2003)
  • Urban population: 377 (2003)
  • Total number of households: 737
  • Registered voters: 1971 (09/06/2004)
  • City government: mayor (Mauricio Martins de Freitas), vice-mayor (Adma Duarte Gomes e Silva), and 09 councilmembers

Economy

The economy is based on cattle raising and growing of corn, bananas, and beans. There is also some wood extraction and transformation. The size of the cattle herd was 12,331 head.

Gross Domestic Product in 2002

  • $R 7.6 million Reais, which was 0.02% of the state GDP of $R 31.29 billion.[1]

Gross Domestic Product per capita in 2002

  • $R 3,045, which was much lower than the state GDP per capita of $R 5,291 in 2002. [2]
