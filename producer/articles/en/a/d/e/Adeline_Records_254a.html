Adeline Records is a record label which was formed in Oakland, California in late 1997. It is co-owned by Billie Joe Armstrong, lead singer of Green Day, his wife Adrienne Armstrong, and Jim Thiebaud, professional skateboarder. Armstrong's close friend and bandmate, Jason White currently oversees the day-to-day operation of the label, and Green Day's manager, Pat Magnarella, has recently agreed to deal with Adeline's record label. Adeline Records was named after a street (Adeline Street) that runs through Berkeley, California.

[edit] Merchandise

Adeline Records now sells punk clothing for men [1] and women [2] as well as other clothing, providing advertising for the label.

The range is called "Adeline Street" and is gradually expanding, including clothes (skirts, tops, underwear, dresses, hats, hoodies, jackets), accessories and jewellery for men and women. The Adeline street logo is a skull and crossbones with a heart in the centre of the crossed bones in pink for women and khaki for men. The entire range of Adeline Street items can be browsed on their website: http://adelinestreet.com
