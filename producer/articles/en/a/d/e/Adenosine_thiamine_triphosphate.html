Adenosine thiamine triphosphate (AThTP), or thiaminylated adenosine triphosphate (ATP), was discovered in Escherichia coli where it may account for up to 15 - 20 % of total thiamine under carbon starvation. AThTP exists also, though at much lower levels, in eukaryotic organisms such as yeast, roots of higher plants and animal tissues.
