The Association démocratique des Français de l'étranger - Français du Monde (Association of French people overseas - French people of the World), also known as ADFE-FdM, ADFE and Français du Monde, is a French political and cultural group with a world-wide presence. It was established following the extension of the right to vote to French citizens resident abroad in 1982.

The Conseil Supérieur des Français de l'Etranger (CSFE), established by law in 1982 to represent French citizens overseas, was replaced by the Assemblée des Français de l'étranger (AFE) in 2004. The AFE elects twelve senators to the French Senate. Proposals to allow the election of members of the National Assembly of France have not been adopted as of 2006.

The ADFE is one of the three groupings in the AFE with 53 of the 155 elected members, and has chosen three of the twelve senators representing French people abroad. While the groups in the AFE are not affiliated directly with French political parties, the ADFE is a left-wing grouping. Its senators sit with the Citizen and Republican Movement (one) and the Socialist Party (two).

The ADFE also encourages and assists voter registration for presidential elections and referenda. In addition to its political role, the ADFE extends assistance to its members overseas in various fields, especially those relation to dealings with the civil service, such as changes of status, issue of documents, pensions, and similar matters.
