Addictionary is a free, online dictionary whose definitions are written and submitted by users. Most words and phrases featured on Addictionary are a play on words, particularly ambiguous "non-words" that may or may not already exist in popular English slang.

The site encourages users to participate in word-play by offering features like a “Wordformer” game based on the Washington Post Mensa Invitational Word Challenge and a "Contest Corner" where users can establish their own word-creation competitions in their work place, clubs, families, etc…

Once a non-word has been submitted, users can rate the non-word on a scale of one to five, suggest a synonym or even submit an alternate definition. The top 10 words of the day are posted on the Addictionary homepage.

At the 2007 Sundance Film Festival in Park City, UT, Addictionary.org was featured on NPR’s live onsite broadcast, as part of the Says You! program hosted by Richard Sher. In March 2007, Addictionary.org became a regular fixture on the nationally syndicated Says You! program.

Launched in 2006, Addictionary is owned and operated by SpectrumDNA, Inc., of Park City, Utah.
