Addleshaw Booth & Co

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Addleshaw Booth & Co. was an English law firm who merged with Theodore Goddard in May 2003. Itself the product of a merger between Booth & Co. and Addleshaw Latham & sons, the firm was a 'heavyweight' in the North of England legal sector, with offices in Leeds and Manchester. Its local standing, amongst other things, led to the firm's high-profile appointment as the official lawyers to the 2002 Commonwealth Games.

However, the firm's small office in London, and its failure to garner City-based clients, led to the tie-up with Theodore Goddard in the spring of 2003 to become Addleshaw Goddard. Seen by many commentators in the legal sector as a takeover, the majority of the management board of the new firm were made up of Addleshaw Booth partners.
