Addington Workshops

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

The Addington Railway Workshops were a major railway facility first established on the Addington site in Christchurch in May 1880 by New Zealand Railways Department. Previously, workshops had been established in Carlyle Street. Steam locomotives, passenger carriages and goods wagons were produced there.

As part of the rationalisation of the New Zealand Railways Corporation following deregulation, the workshops closed in 1990. At its peak hundreds were employed there and aside from the core business of building and repairing locomotives, carriages and wagons, at times the shops also manufactured gold dredges and aeroplane components.

Addington specialised in later years in carriage construction and overhaul as well as wagon construction.

[edit] Locomotive building

Addington Workshops built NZR's first locomotive, W 192. Locomotive building ceased in the 1960s with the DSC class (although four DSJ locomotives were assembled from imported kitsets in the early 1980s)
