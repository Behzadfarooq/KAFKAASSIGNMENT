"Addicted" is a Pop song from singer Enrique Iglesias. It is the first single from his album 7(Seven). Iglesias claims he wrote the song while on tour in East Germany on a dismal day when he was inspired by the Gothic Architecture. It was a modest hit in both the U.S. and the UK, charting in the Top twenty in both countries. The Spanish version Adicto made the top ten on Billboards Hot Latin Tracks and the song was also played extensively in India becoming number one on radio.

[edit] Video Infomation

The video is directed by Peter Berg, and features actress Mischa Barton as Iglesias's love interest. In a radio intervew with KIIS-FM Iglesias claimed that the idea for the video was inspired by the movie Midnight Express.

In the video, Iglesias is depicted as a jail prisoner in his cell but is led into room for visitation from his girlfriend played by Barton with the two communicating through a glass barrier making them unable to touch. When the visitaion is over Iglesias is reluctant to leave and has to be pulled away by Prison guards. When Iglesias stops resisting they let him go only to have Iglesias attack the guards and try to escape. The guards manage to subdue Iglesias and knock him to the floor and drag him back to his cell. The video is intercut with scenes of Iglesias and Barton in a hotel room having fun by taking photos of each other, drinking champagne, kissing and both of them taking off their tops (though Barton was in reality taped) this section is supposed to imply that Iglesias's character is addicted to her. The video ends with Iglesias being snapped out of a dream by Barton before they leave the hotel leaving it ambiguous whether the prison scenes were a dream by Iglesias or a premonition of the future.

A version of the video aired on VH1 in the states and MTV Hits in the UK uses some different takes of the prison scenes and the contrast of the video is visibly less bright.
