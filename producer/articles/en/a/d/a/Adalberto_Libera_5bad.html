Adalberto Libera (July 16, 1903 - 1963) is one of the main representative architects of the Italian Rationalist movement.

[edit] Biography

Adalberto Libera was born in Villa Lagarina, near to Trento in northern Italy. He graduated from Parma's Institute of Art in 1925 and then in 1928 from Rome's Scuola Superiore di Architettura and became acquainted with the modernist movement through the likes of his fellow Trentino Fortunato Depero. Even before graduating he was member of Gruppo 7 (Movimento Italiano di Architettura Razionale or “Italian Movement for Rational Architecture”) and later became its secretary. He was invited by Ludwig Mies van der Rohe to the 1927 Stuttgart Exhibition (Werkbund). He organized in 1928 and 1931 the Exhibitions "Architettura Razionale" in Rome.

He designed several rationalist buildings during the 1930s, the most important being the Palace of congresses at the EUR in Rome. The building shows on the facade the architectonic details typical of the "simplified neoclassicism" (see Marcello Piacentini). However the use of the sail vaults create an innovative architectural space and make this building the most successful rationalist building he designed.

He also designed the Villa Malaparte in Capri (1938), a parallelepipedon with a scaled roof terrace.

Libera managed to avoid being involved with the Fascist regime and the war, unlike other rationalist architects of his time (e.g. Giuseppe Pagano and Giuseppe Terragni). However, the fall of the fascist regime and the loss of the war caused a psychological set back for Libera, and he returned to his home town for several years. Starting in the late 1940s, he worked again on numerous projects, including public dwelling units (1954) and office buildings (1959).

In 1954–1962 he designed and built the seat of the Regional Council of the Trentino Region in Trento.
