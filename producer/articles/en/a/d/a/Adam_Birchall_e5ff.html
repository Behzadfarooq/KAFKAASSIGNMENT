Adam Birchall (born 2 December 1984 in Maidstone) is a footballer who currently plays for Barnet. Despite being born in England, he has represented Wales at international level. He hasn't played for Wales at senior level, but has several caps for the Welsh under-21 team.

Birchall began his career at Arsenal, but never made it to the first-team. He can play up front or on either wing. Opinions are divided about his best position. In 2004-05, Birchall had a successful loan spell at Wycombe Wanderers, scoring four goals in 11 league games. He was released by Arsenal at the end of that season, and joined Mansfield in June 2005 on a two-year contract.

The move to Mansfield has been something of a disappointment for Birchall. Big things were expected from the youngster, but despite occasional flashes of brilliance, he has failed to live up to expectations, scoring only three goals, and has spent most of the season on the bench. He did, however, score in Mansfield's nationally televised FA Cup victory against Grays Athletic on his 21st birthday.

On November 23, 2006, Birchall was loaned to division rivals Barnet, a move which was made permanent on January 4, 2007.
