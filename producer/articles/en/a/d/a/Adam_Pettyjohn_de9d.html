Adam Christopher Pettyjohn is a former Major League pitcher. He was born on Saturday, June 11, 1977 in Phoenix, AZ. He batted right handed but threw left handed. Pettyjohn attended Exeter High School (former third baseman Brad Mills also attended Exeter) in California and then Fresno State University where he was a teammate of pitcher Jeff Weaver.

In 1998, Pettyjohn was drafted by the Detroit Tigers in the 2nd round (73rd overall). Unlike many other players, he signed the first time he was drafted. He signed for a $392,500 bonus. Pettyjohn spent less than three years in the minor leagues, posting an ERA as low as 1.97. On July 16, 2001, the 6'3" 190 pound pitcher made his ML debut at the age of 24. He wore the number 57. The 1-6 record he posted while in the Major Leagues would to many be considered subpar. His final major league game was on October 4, 2001.

In 2002, Pettyjohn didn't play any professional baseball because he had to undergo a colectomy due to ulcerative colitis. In 2003, he played in the Tigers organization. He was released by the Tigers on March 28, 2003. He spent in 2004 he played in the San Francisco Giants and Oakland Athletics organizations. He spend the 2005 season and the start of the 2006 seaon with the Long Beach Armada of the Golden Baseball League before spending time again with the Athletics as well as the Seattle Mariners organizations. In late 2006 he signed a minor league contract with the Milwaukee Brewers. He is expected to start the 2007 season with the Brewers Triple A team the Nashville Sounds.

[edit] Other Facts

  • Pettyjohn pitched for team USA in 1997.
  • The colectomy procedures Pettyjohn underwent were all performed by Dr. John Eggenberger.
  • Pettyjohn's first strikeout was Ken Griffey Jr.
  • Pettyjohn currently resides in Exeter, California.
  • Pettyjohn was a teammate of Mike Rivera for five years-longer than any other teammate.
