Adam Zwar was born in Cairns, Australia and is an actor, voice artist and writer.

[edit] Career

In 2006, as well as starring in the first and second series of Network Ten's sketch comedy, The Wedge, he teamed-up with Oscar winner Geoffrey Rush to write the 2006 AFI Awards on Channel Nine.[1] He also stars-in and has co-written the darkly comic eight-part TV series, Wilfred, which premieres on SBS on March 19. This year. Adam will appear in the cinematic release of the feature film Rats and Cats. Co-written with Jason Gann, Rats and Cats, is a new Australian comedy that premiered to sell-out audiences and critical acclaim at the 2006 Melbourne International Film Festival.[2] Adamâ€™s other screen-acting credits include Blue Heelers, Stingers, Crash/Burn, Sea Change, Back-Berner and MDA. His theatre credits include Kissing for Australia, for which he received a Green Room Award nomination, and Cyrano De Bergerac for the Melbourne Theatre Company.[3] He has also written the plays The Inner Sanctum, Kissing for Australia and The Fall and Fall of Jeremy Hawthorn.

He is married to Amanda Brotchie, the AFI award winning filmmaker. He is the son of author Desmond Zwar, who wrote the best selling book "The Loneliest Man in the World" about Nazi prisoner, Rudolf Hess. And his mother, Delphine, was a long time writer for House and Garden Magazine.[citation needed]

Adam has appeared in such commercials as HBA's Free for Free campaign, VB and Radio Rentals. Currently, he is the voice of Australia Post.
