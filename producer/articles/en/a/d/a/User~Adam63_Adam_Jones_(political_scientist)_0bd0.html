Adam Jones is a Canadian political scientist whose writing focuses on genocide, human rights, gender, and mass media.

[edit] Research and activism

Jones co-founded Gendercide Watch, a Web-based educational initiative, with Carla Bergman in 2000. The project drew on the work of feminist scholar Mary Anne Warren, who coined the term "gendercide" in her 1985 book Gendercide: The Implications of Sex Selection. The site compiles case studies of gendercide against men and women, including "gendercidal institutions" such as female infanticide and corvée labor.

[edit] Biography

Jones was born in Singapore in 1963, and grew up in England before his family emigrated to Canada in 1970. In 1979, he returned to Singapore on a two-year scholarship to the United World College of South East Asia, followed by a year (1983-84) studying Mandarin language at East China Normal University in Shanghai, China. He obtained a B.A. in History/International Relations from the University of British Columbia in 1989, a Master's in Political Science at McGill University in 1992, and a Ph.D. in Political Science at the University of British Columbia in July 1999.

From 2000 to 2004, Jones taught in the International Studies division of the Center for Research and Teaching in Economics (CIDE) in Mexico City, and also part-time at the Colegio de San Luis (COLSAN) in San Luis Potosí. In 2005, he took up a two-year position as Associate Research Fellow in the Genocide Studies Program at Yale University. He is scheduled to join the Department of Political Science at the University of British Columbia Okanagan as Associate Professor in July 2007.
