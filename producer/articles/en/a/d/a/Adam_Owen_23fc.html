Adam Owen

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Adam Owen is a Welsh football coach specialising in fitness and conditioning of players. He is currently the Fitness and Rehabilitation Coach at Scottish Premier League club Rangers.

As a player Owen started as a trainee at Wrexham where he also started coaching in their youth programme. After obtaining a Sports Science degree and a UEFA 'A' license coaching badge Adam continued his playing career in the League of Wales.

Owen's big break came when he moved to Celtic in 2003 as a coach for the under-17 and under-19 teams. After a year at Parkhead he progressed to a reserve team coach before moving on to the first team.

During his short career so far he has worked with John Hartson, Alan Thompson, Chris Sutton, Martin O'Neill, Tommy Burns, Denis Smith and Paul Sturrock.

He later had a spell with Sheffied Wednesday before moving to Rangers in March 2007.
