Adam Keefe Horovitz, also known as King Ad-Rock or Adrock, (born October 31, 1966, in South Orange, New Jersey) is a rapper, musician, producer, and actor. He is best known as a member of the pioneering rap group, the Beastie Boys.

Horovitz began his musical career with a stint in the punk rock band, The Young and the Useless, who would often perform with the Beastie Boys. When Beastie guitarist John Berry quit, Horovitz was an obvious choice to replace him.[citation needed] Adrock, with Mike D and MCA, would endure as the classic Beastie Boys line-up.

He has been linked romantically to various entertainers including Molly Ringwald (1987-1988), Ione Skye (1988-1999; married 1992-1999), and musician Kathleen Hanna (1997-present; married 2006-present).

In addition to his work with the Beastie Boys, Horovitz has released music with a side-project called BS 2000 and has done production for the Japanese group Teriyaki Boyz. For a time, he also pursued an acting career, with appearances in films such as Lost Angels, Roadside Prophets, and A Kiss Before Dying.

[edit] Trivia

  • Adam is the son of the playwright and screenwriter Israel Horovitz. The film Author! Author! is based on the experiences of the Horovitz family.
  • Horovitz and the Beastie Boys can be seen in the video games NBA Street V3 and NBA Jam Tournament Edition.
  • Has a tattoo of The Silver Surfer on his right shoulder.
  • The character Adrock Thurston from Eureka 7 was named for him.
  • He starred, alongside the rest of the Beastie Boys, in the 1985 film Krush Groove, performing a rendition of "She's On It".
