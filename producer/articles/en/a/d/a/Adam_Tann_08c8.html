Adam Tann (born May 12, 1982) is an English footballer. He was born in Fakenham , Norfolk, England. He is a defender.

Tann started his career with Cambridge United, where he had played since being offered a contract at the beginning of the 1999 season. He made his debut in the Football League Trophy defeat to Barnet, and remained in the second team until the first-half of the 2001-02 season, when he appeared for the Cambridge side which finished bottom of the second division, and winless at home.

Tann was to move to the Lilywhites of Cambridge City on loan, but otherwise stayed with United until 2005. He then signed for Gravesend, making one emergency appearance before moving to Notts County. Since January 2006, he has played for Leyton Orient.

Tann was diagnosed with testicular cancer in the autumn of 2006, but following a successful operation is currently resting from the game.
