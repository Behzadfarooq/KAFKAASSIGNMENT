[edit] contents of article

I think the POV concerns are real. Most WP articles about scholars emphasize a listing of the academic positions, the published works, and the awards, with a brief description of what the guy is working on and what the main themes are. There are not devoted to a detailed presentation of the person's theories or abstracts of his specialized articles.

In fact, including such material is commonly a sign of either POV pushing or self-advertising. Not always--sometimes it is an indication of unsophistication, or lack of knowledge of WP standards.
The amount here is excessive. sometimes the subjects of articles -- or their close friends -- are not capable of effectively epitomizing their interests, and in that case it is the job of the other WP editors to do it for them.
A further possible indication of COI problems is the qualification: if the individuals notability is his academic work, then it should be Adam Jones (political scientist) not (Canadian).
I invite the author/subject to make these changes on his own. But I came here in response to the listing on the COI noticeboard, and I am perfectly prepared to make the necessary changes myself. DGG 08:01, 7 March 2007 (UTC)
Go for it. Wikipedia is a collaborative project, after all, and look out for the many repeat/duplicate/near-duplicate external links while you're at it. — Athænara ✉ 08:49, 7 March 2007 (UTC)

[edit] Tagging

It is disappointing to see this article repeatedly defaced by multiple tags. Attempts have been made to establish notability through external links, but this results in protests about the amount of links! I proceeded to remove most of the external links, but the use of the "revert" feature restores them, which would seem counterproductive.

The following sources are intended to establish notability -- arguably, the final source alone (an in-depth treatment of the author's gender theories in one of the world's leading International Relations journals) does so. If this is disputed, it should be by an argument more coherent than simply slapping on a tag.

  • "Capsule College Lecture Looks at Genocide", by Cara Brady, The Morning Star, 15 March 2006
  • "Hass und Gewalt können auch Demokratien erfassen", by Lutz Mäurer, Das Parlament, 20 February 2006 (review of German edition of Genocide, War Crimes & the West)
  • Gendercide and Genocide, Review by J. Steven Svoboda, MENSIGHT Magazine, 2006
  • Gendercide and Genocide, Review by Maureen S. Hiebert, H-Net, 2005
  • "Vesten har andel i folkedrab", by Danny Rødgaard, AMNESTY-bladet, No. 3, 2005 (in Danish)
  • "Beyond the Barricades: Nicaragua and the Struggle for the Sandinista Press, 1979-1998", Review by Dennis L. Gilbert, Hispanic American Historical Review, 84: 2 (May 2004), pp. 375-377
  • "Adam Jones: Beyond the Barricades", Review by John J. Dwyer, H-Diplo, February 2003
  • "Genocide Scholar 'Silenced' on Academic List For Comments About Bombing of Afghanistan", CounterPunch, 26 October 2001
  • "Some Grim Conclusions about Gender and Mass Killing", by Christina Spencer, The Ottawa Citizen, 6 March 2000
  • "Gendering Jones: Feminisms, IR, Masculinities", by Terrell Carver, Molly Cochran, and Judith Squires, Review of International Studies, 24: 2 (1996), pp. 405-29
