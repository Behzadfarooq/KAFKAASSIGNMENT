Adam Mogg

Adam Mogg (born July 31, 1977 in Toowoomba, Queensland) is a rugby league player for Les Catalans in the European Super League competition. He started his professional career for the Redcliffe Dolphins before he was scouted in 2002 by the Parramatta Eels. He is a maneuverable player who primarily plays in the centres, but can play wing or even lock if necessary. In 2004 the Canberra Raiders picked him up and was a regular member of the squad.

He was selected as a winger for Queensland in time for game two of 2006 in the State of Origin when Greg Inglis was dropped due to injury. Mogg scored two tries on his surprise Origin debut. He is also one of the oldest players to make an Origin debut.

Mogg is commonly known as "Egg Mogg" to diehard Raiders fans.

  • Position - Centre
  • Previous Clubs - Eels 2002-2004, Raiders 2004-2006
  • NRL Debut - Eels v Melbourne Storm, Olympic Park, Round 10 2002
  • Career Games - 78
  • Career Tries - 33
  • Career Goals - 0
  • Total Career Points - 132
  • Representative Honours - Two games for Queensland Maroons 2006 (3 tries)
