Saint Adalgott is the name of an eleventh-century Roman Catholic saint.

Adalgott was a bishop and a comforter of the poor. He was a monk in the Benedictine monastery in Clairvaux. He soon was given the position of abbot of the Benedictines in Disentis. While here, he became quite well known for being a caregiver of the sick and poor. He then was appointed bishop of Chur, and he conducted an apostolate for the poor and suffering the region. In the year 1150, Adalgott founded a hospital.

His feast day is celebrated October 3.
