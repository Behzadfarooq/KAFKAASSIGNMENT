Adams Township, Champaign County, Ohio

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Municipalities and townships of Champaign County.

Adams Township is one of twelve civil townships in Champaign County, Ohio, United States. It is located in the northwestern corner of the county.

[edit] Geography

It borders or touches the following other townships and municipalities:

  • Johnson Township, on the south;
  • Green Township, Shelby County, on the west;
  • Perry Township, Shelby County, on the northwest;
  • Miami Township, Logan County, on the north;
  • Harrison Township, on the east;
  • Concord Township, on the southeast

[edit] Name and history

It is one of ten Adams Townships in Ohio.

[edit] Government

The township is governed by a three-member board of trustees, who are elected in November of odd-numbered years to a four-year term beginning on the following January 1. Two are elected in the year after the presidential election and one is elected in the year before it. There is also an elected township clerk, who serves a four-year term beginning on April 1 of the year after the election, which is held in November of the year before the presidential election. Vacancies in the clerkship or on the board of trustees are filled by the remaining trustees.
