Adam Hildreth

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Mr Adam John Hildreth
Born: March 25, 1985 (age 22)
Leeds, West Yorkshire, England
Occupation: Entrepreneur
Net worth: £5 million (US$10 million)

Adam Hildreth(born 25th of March 1985, Leeds, West Yorkshire, England) was one of the successful dot com millionaires after setting up a company called Dubit Limited when he was 14. [1] Dubit Limited went onto become one of the biggest teenage websites in the UK and now markets itself as a 'Youth Marketing Agency', advising major brands on how to market their products to young people. He was reportedly worth £2m in the 2004 UK top 20 richest teens list. He was predicted to be worth £40m by the year 2020 in a study on British millionaires of the future. In 2006 he won the Young Entrepreneur of the Year which was awarded to him by Duncan Bannatyne and Peter Jones from Dragons Den.

[edit] Life and career

In 1999 he founded [2] Dubit with 7 friends, making the guiness book of records for being the youngest group of directors in the UK.

In 2003 he won an Achievement in Information Technology at the Yorkshire Young Achievers Awards

In 2005 he started [3] Crisp Thinking, which is a company aimed at developing child protection technology for Internet Service Providers.

In 2006 won the CBI’s Young Entrepreneur of the Year accolade at the 2006 Growing Business Awards for his development of Crisp Thinking and its products. He was presented the award by Dragons’ Den judges Duncan Bannatyne and Peter Jones at an awards ceremony compared by Rt Hon. William Hague MP.
