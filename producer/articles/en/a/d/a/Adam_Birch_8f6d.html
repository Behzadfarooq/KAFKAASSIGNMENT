Adam Birch (born July 18, 1979), is an American professional wrestler. He is best known for his appearances with Extreme Championship Wrestling under the ring name Joey Matthews and with World Wrestling Entertainment under the ring name Joey Mercury.

[edit] Career

Birch began his career, under the name Joey Matthews, in Maryland Championship Wrestling, where he held a variety of titles. He was also a member of the North Carolina based Organization of Modern Extreme Grappling Arts (OMEGA) run by Matt and Jeff Hardy.

His first national exposure came after he left OMEGA and formed a tag team named "the Badstreet Boys", with fellow OMEGA alum Christian York. Together the Badstreet Boys wrestled in multiple smaller promotions, and had nine independent tag team championship reigns. In late 2000 they joined Extreme Championship Wrestling (ECW), where they stayed until it's closure in April of 2001, and were on the companies final pay-per-view Guilty as Charged.

At the tail end of their ECW careers they changed their names to simply York and Matthews, and floated around the independent circuit after ECW folded, most notably competing in Ultimate Pro Wrestling and Phoenix Championship Wrestling. York's 2004 retirement from the wrestling business dissolved the team and left Matthews to wrestle in singles matches in promotions like Ring of Honor and Total Nonstop Action Wrestling.

[edit] World Wrestling Entertainment

In early 2004, Matthews was signed to a developmental deal by World Wrestling Entertainment and assigned to the Ohio Valley Wrestling (OVW) "farm territory". There he formed a tag team with Johnny Nitro, which along with manager Melina Perez, was called MNM.

MNM wrestled in OVW for around a year, winning the OVW Southern Tag Team Championship once, before being called up to the main SmackDown! roster, where Matthews was renamed Mercury. While on SmackDown! they feuded with teams of Rey Mysterio and Eddie Guerrero,Hardcore Holly and Charlie Haas, LOD, Mysterio and Batista, & Paul London and Brian Kendrick. Together they captured the WWE Tag Team Championship on three occasions, all in their rookie year on the brand.

At the May 2006 Judgment Day pay per view, where MNM lost the tag team title to London and Kendrick, Nitro and Melina suddenly turned on Mercury, breaking up the group. Later that night they were (kayfabe) fired from the SmackDown! brand and sent to RAW. The legit reason for the teams sudden split was later revealed to be Mercury's failing a WWE Wellness Policy drug test and being forced to serve a 30 day suspension.

Matthews actually stayed off of WWE television for six months. In that time he went through a drug rehabilitation, and afterwards was sent back to OVW. He made a surprise return on the November 27, 2006 RAW, reforming MNM with Nitro and Melina to take up the also recently reunited Hardys (Matt and Jeff) "open challenge" for ECW December to Dismember.

MNM feuded with the Hardys across brands, and in a fatal-four way ladder match at December's Armageddon -- also involving London and Kendrick & Dave Taylor and William Regal -- Mercury suffered a (legit) medical injury when he was struck in the face with a ladder, breaking his nose. He immediately left the match and was rushed to an emergency room where he received 5 stitches to the inside of his nose and 15 to the outside. [1]

After missing a few weeks, Mercury returned wearing a protective face mask and his injury was worked into the angle, with both he and Nitro attempting to injure the Hardys in various ways for revenge. After returning later in the month Mercury continued to wrestle, both as a singles competitor on SmackDown! and with Nitro on RAW until he was released on March 26.[2]
