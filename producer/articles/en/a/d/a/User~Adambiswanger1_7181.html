[edit] About me

728672597
Welcome to my userpage. My name is Adam, and I am a freshman at Syracuse University. My interests include philosophy, art, poetry, laughing, and history, blah blah blah. I also find sneezing and yawning quite enjoyable.

I really believe in Wikipedia, and I do whatever I can to improve its comprehensiveness, usefulness, and validity. I try to get involved in as many areas as I can here: I float around, reorganizing articles, putting in my input at AfD and RfA, reverting some vandalism, getting involved in discussions, and then occasionally undertaking something more ambitious.

Hopefully I'll be spending a semester abroad

In terms of Wiki-politics, I consider my self a moderate: I view every decision separately and always reasoning the problem out. Usually this does not provide for any erratic or contradictory decision patterns because, well, the rationality I administer to the problem is all the same. And let's talk big picture. To me, an ideal Wikipedia is one that provides a firm foundation of a topic and provides references (REAL BOOKS!!!) for further reading.

When it comes to disputes on articles, I like to challenge assumptions and question knee-jerk decisions. One thing I can't stand is an uninformed yet strong opinion.

You can learn more about me from my userboxes.

Feel free to leave me a message on my Talk page.

[edit] What I think

  • AfD is for deleting wacko theories, things made up in school one day, and non-notable companies. This is an encyclopedia. I support fully any article of an academic nature, no matter how obscure (barring absurdity).
  • When people ask me about the unreliability of Wikipedia, and how they can never trust such information, I tell them to "stay on the beaten path", meaning popular articles that have had plenty of editors pass over them, such as Philosophy or Nelson Mandela. But eventually, the beaten path will become wider and wider and the presence of strong, accurate articles will be much better.
  • Well-written fancruft for a fairly popular subject is acceptable.
  • Articles should be deleted based on their premise, not their content.
  • The opening sentence is the most important part of the article.
  • Secondary schools are inherently notable--and why fight the battle when it will never be won?
  • What I think about images
  • If an RfA is like 0/7/0, and the user clearly is going to lose, for god's sake don't pile on!
  • Articles must be properly organized to best facilitate learning and easy access of information.
  • Articles should contain plenty of redirects to aid in bad searches. To err is human.
  • Helpful information should not always be transwikied--for example, the text of a short poem or a universally recognized and standard alcoholic drink recipe should be contained right within the article.
  • WP:IAR is a sacred text that should be used only in desperation.
