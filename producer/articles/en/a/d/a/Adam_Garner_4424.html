Adam Garner (born December 7, 1984) is a semi-professional footballer. He currently plays for Bedford Town in the Nationwide Southern Conference League after leaving previous teams Chippenham Town, Nationwide Conference side Forest Green Rovers, and Oxford United. He plays in a defensive position at either left back, right back or centre back.

He was a trainee and professional with Oxford United and has had experience with Conference club Gravesend & Northfleet. He joined Chippenham Town in July 2006 and is English.

In February 2007 Adam Garner became the first British football player to be owned by a commercial brand. The non-league player joined the elevated company of Argentine internationals such as Carlos Tevez and Javier Mascherano in a growing list of players who are not directly owned by the club they play for.

The deal came about after Bedford Town manager Nick Platnauer let slip that he had been using the PC footy game to help him scout for players that might help the Eagles avoid relegation from the Conference South.
