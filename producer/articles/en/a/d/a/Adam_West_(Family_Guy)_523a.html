The fictional version of Adam West on the animated television series Family Guy is a caricature of actor Adam West, who also supplies his voice.

“ The character we've created is kind of this alternate-universe Adam West where he's mayor of this town, and we deliberately have not made any references to Batman, because we like keeping that separate. It's the obvious place to go. We tried it, we thought it would be funny to do something different with the mayor of this town. People like Clint Eastwood and Martin Sheen who have taken whacks at this sort of thing — there's a precedent for it, actors getting into politics. He's the mayor, but he's this guy who clearly does not have it all together. ”

—Seth MacFarlane, A.V. Club interview[1]

[edit] Biography

Adam West is the mayor of Quahog — as with most Family Guy cameos, the reason he is in this position is unexplained.

Mayor West is an intense, soft-spoken eccentric whose delusional acts include using public money to fund an investigation into where water goes when added to flowers or poured in a sink and dispatching the entire Quahog police force to Colombia to assist in the rescue of the fictional protagonists of Romancing the Stone.

"Perhaps it is the Noid who should have avoided me."

Sometimes, however, his delusions appear to be real even in the presence of other characters. He once sent Alex Trebek back to the fifth dimension by making him say his name backwards. He also warned Meg about The Noid who later appears and tries to ruin his pizza, but West catches and brutally kills him.

West does not appear to have family (apart from having legally married his right hand). His romantic entanglements include once having slept with Luke Perry (because he thought it would make Perry stop stealing his water), and an intimate but chaste liason with then-intern Meg Griffin.

[edit] Role as Mayor

As Mayor of Quahog, Adam West is the city’s most powerful civil servant. However, he generates a massive amount of controversy with abstract plans and restrictive legislation- such as banning gay marriage and spending the entire city budget on building a gold statue of the Dig 'Em Frog in dedication to Iraqi War heroes.

West is careless with public resources, as stated, and he is in fear of the District Attorney-indicating that he takes part in corrupt activities.

Yet West has never been removed from office and does occasionally display strong qualities for a good mayor, such as forcing Peter Griffin’s “four-bedroom republic” to rejoin the United States without sacrifice and protecting citizens in need.
