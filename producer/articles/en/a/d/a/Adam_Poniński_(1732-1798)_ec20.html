Adam Poniński (1732 or 1733 - 23 July 1798 was a Polish nobleman. Prince, one of the leaders of the Radom Confederation of 1867, Grand Treasurer of the Crown (from 1775), he is remembered as the infamous Marshal of the Sejm of the Partition Sejm (1773-1775). Considered by many contemporaries and historians a traitor, serving Russian ambassadors, he was stripped of all titles and exiled by the decree of the Great Sejm in 1790 but restored soon afterwards by the Confederation of Targowica.

His son, Adam Poniński, born in 1758, became a military general.
