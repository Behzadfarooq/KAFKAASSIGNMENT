Adam Beattie

Adam Beattie was the Captain of the New York 26th Independent Battery, Light Artillery Barnesâ€™ Rifle Battery during the American Civil War. He was born in or near Seneca, New York, to John and Nancy Beattie, around 1833.

On November 28, 1862 he was mustered into the New York 26th Independent Battery (Light Artillery), at the age of 29 as a 2nd Lieutenant. On March 29, 1863 he was promoted to Vice First Lieutenant, then Captain of the Battery, by George W. Fox. On September 12, 1865 he was honorably discharged and mustered out at New Orleans. He returned to his family in Seneca/Portage, New York and in 1865 a son, Willard Grant Beattie, was born to Beattie and his first wife, Mary E. Johnson.

Along with many residents of Seneca Falls County, New York, Beattie made his way to Ovid, Michigan where he was a grocer, ran for school board, and became Ovid's postmaster. His Civil War friend George Fox followed him to Ovid, where on February 13th, 1871 Fox was the master carpenter on a 22-man team building Ovid's First Congregational Church. The church was moved by oxen in 1899 and turned to face Main Street where it stands today as a residence.

Adam Beattie was married three times, first to Mary E. Johnson, then on November 1, 1869 to Kate A. Hann (July 1843 - 10 September 1879), and finally to Mary E. Hagin (1840 - April 1918), on April 3, 1883. Adam Beattie died at age 59 on June 26, 1893. Adam Beattie, George Fox, Mary E. Hagin, and Kate A. Hann are all buried in Ovid, Michigan's Maple Grove Cemetery; as some of the first settlers to the area to die, their tombstones are near the front entrance. Adam shares a large granite monument with his second wife Kate. His third wife Mary E. Hagin is buried nearby in an unmarked grave.
