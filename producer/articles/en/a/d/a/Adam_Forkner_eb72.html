Adam Forkner (b. 1976) is a psychedelic, new-age, ambient and experimental musician and artist based in Portland, Oregon who goes by the moniker White Rainbow He was a founding member of several bands including Yume Bitsu, World, Surface of Eceon and VVRSSNN as well as many other short term collaborations.

[edit] White Rainbow

White Rainbow is the solo project of psychedelic/experimental musician and multi-media artist Adam Forkner. Forkner's past projects include Yume Bitsu, Surface of Eceyon, World and VVRSSNN. He's also collaborated with a huge array of notable artists over the last decade as a guitarist, bassist, drummer, trumpeter, engineer, producer or re-mixer, including: Jackie-O Motherfucker, Devendra Banhart, The Microphones, The Blow, YACHT, Dirty Projectors, Landing, D Yellow Swans, The Watery Graves, Thanksgiving, Tussle, Greg Weeks, Kinski, Wolf Colonel/Jason Anderson, Calvin Johnson/Dub Narcotic, and others.

The first White Rainbow record "ZOME" was was recorded in 2003 with members of Landing and released on States Rights Records in late 2005 . The record features a more full band effort, with guitars, keyboards, drums, electronics and some vocals creating a kraut-rock inspired psychedelic drone feel.

The second White Rainbow release "BOX" was a 5 CD + 1 DVD boxed-set released by Marriage Records in early 2006. It featured a wide variety of music (over 4 hours) recorded by Forkner from 2003 to 2005. The DVD included collaborations with renowned video artists E*Rock and Alex Hubbard as well as live footage of both traditional audio performances as well as White Rainbow's trademark site-specific audio/video performance/installations.

Forkner has focused on the construction of these "Full Spectrum Vibrational Healing Centers" since 2004. The first major actualization of this was as part of the Portland Institute for Contemporary Art's Time Based Arts Festival. This performance/installation was reprised at the Arthur Ball, a music and film festival in Los Angeles, California in February of 2006. Other performance/installations occurred at the 2006 PDX Film Festival and the 2006 Portland Biennial Art Exhibit at the Portland Art Museum. These performance/installation/light sculptures focus on the interplay between the geodesic/utopian modernism of visionaries like Buckminster Fuller, West-Coast New Age "Guru Culture", and the all night tape-loop performances of minimalist innovators like Terry Riley.

A new album entitled "prism of eternal now" is scheduled for release on the highly esteemed experimental label Kranky in fall of 2007.

[edit] Collaborations

Forkner has collaborated both live and on record with many artists. Most recently he was featured on Devendra Banhart's Cripple Crow album as well as Jackie-O Motherfucker's Flags of the Sacred Harp. Other artists that Forkner has played with, recorded music with or produced recordings by include: Dirty Projectors, Little Wings (aka Kyle Field), Yellow Swans Greg Weeks (of Espers) Thanksgiving (aka Adrian Orange) The Microphones Wolf Colonel (aka Jason Anderson) YACHT (aka Jona Bechtolt) Bobby Birdman The Watery Graves
