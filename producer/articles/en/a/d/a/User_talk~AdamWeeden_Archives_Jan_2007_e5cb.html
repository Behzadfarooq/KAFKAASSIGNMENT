[edit] Your move of "Weeden Island Culture" to "Weedon Island Culture"

I really wish you had proposed this move on the talk page first. I think it was mistake, and I intend to move it back unless I can be convinced that archaeologists really use "Weedon Island Culture" instead of "Weeden Island Culture". -- Donald Albury 13:26, 16 December 2006 (UTC)

I just realized that you also changed "Weeden" to "Weedon" in the titles of references. That was not a "spelling correction". -- Donald Albury 13:38, 16 December 2006 (UTC)
I apologize if I was in error. I live near the actual namesake Weedon Island and it is indeed named that way. I was under the impression that Weed(e/o)n Island Culture would be named in the same way. Looking back, it was rather hasty of me to have moved it without discussion first, and for that I definitely apologize. How do you think we should go about resolving this? Adam Weeden 14:49, 16 December 2006 (UTC)
No need to apologize. I understand what prompted you to do it. I wasn't aware of the name discrepancy until this came up. If you agree, I will move the article back, and add the information that the name of the island was mis-spelled at the time of the first report in 1923. 'Weedon Island culture' will then be left as a redirect, as there is some use of that name. -- Donald Albury 13:07, 17 December 2006 (UTC)
