13 January 2007

Uncategorized?

Stubs are categories; you shouldn't be adding an uncategorized tag to articles that are marked as stubs. Only articles without category tags and without stub tags should be marked as uncategorized. Kafziel Talk 15:53, 13 January 2007 (UTC)

I don't think I would agree with this. Whilst declaring an article as a stub, adds it to the Category:Stubs, I don't think this can be seen as removing the reasoning behind tagging an article as uncategorized. I think this view is supported by the existence of a Category:Uncategorized stubs. Of course, if you could point out any specific guidelines regarding this, I would appreciate your help. Adambro 16:12, 13 January 2007 (UTC)
Uncategorized stubs are for stubs that are not sorted further into categories. This is not the case with the articles you've been tagging. For instance, {{kerala-geo-stub}} puts an article into the category of Category:Kerala geography stubs, as specific a category as any other. Same goes for {{architecture-stub}} or {{corporation-stub}}. Those are categories. Even a simple {{stub}} tag is a category. Although it should be categorized further if possible, placing a tag on it is not necessary. Kafziel Talk 16:31, 13 January 2007 (UTC)
I'm still not entirely sure about this. I guess the real question is, does a stub category count as a 'real' category. The text on the template, Uncategorizedstub would suggest it doesn't as it indicates that the article in question should be improved by "adding it to one or more categories, in addition to a stub category". Also, may I suggest that you should have discussed this with me before reverting my edits especially as some of them were more than just adding an uncategorized tag. Adambro 16:51, 13 January 2007 (UTC)
The answer is yes, a stub category does qualify as a "real" category. That's not really up for debate. A category is a category. In theory they're not meant to be permanent, but they are categories and tagging them just increases the backlog of supposedly uncategorized articles while doing nothing to actually improve the situation. I'm not sure what evidence you're looking for. Perhaps Wikipedia:Stub sorting will help? There are also two comments on the subject at Template talk:Uncategorized#Don't put this tag on sorted stubs!. I guess confusion is rare enough that nobody has bothered to annotate the actual template, but not so rare as to be unheard-of. It's really not that a big of a deal, as long as you understand now.
Sorry about the reverts; you were using AWB and therefore moving very quickly. I had to edit fast to keep up while I waited for a reply (I left the message on your talk page right after my first revert). I will replace the other fixes I may have removed. Kafziel Talk 17:08, 13 January 2007 (UTC)
Ok, thanks for clearing that up. I'll refrain from adding uncategorized to stubs in future. Understand regarding AWB, I guess maybe I should look into how I can stop it suggesting adding uncatergorized to stubs. Thanks again. Adambro 17:52, 13 January 2007 (UTC)
Glad I could help! Kafziel Talk 18:22, 13 January 2007 (UTC)

I'm nota Adambro and he will answer better then me, but I have to mention WP:COI... by Snowolf (talk) on 17:12, 21 January 2007 (UTC)
Snowolf makes a good point. If you are going to create an article about a project you are involved in then you would be seen to have a conflict of interest. This is important because Wikipedia strives to maintain a neutral point of view. I feel that copying info straight from your website, which one would assume is aimed at promoting your project, leaves doubts as to whether the article complies with the neutral point of view guidelines. I would suggest you try to rewrite the article in accordance with the Manual of Style and from a neutral point of view. If you intend to do this, you should place {{hangon}} at the top of the page and explain your intention on the talk page. This will prevent the article from being deleted. Thanks.
