Adamsville (Atlanta)

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Adamsville is a predominantly African-American neighborhood on the west side of Atlanta, Georgia. Adamsville is held as one of Atlanta's toughest neighborhoods. It's located just to the west of the West End neighborhood near where I-20 and I-285 meet.

Entertainers Killer Mike, Bone Crusher, Ludacris and Stevie-the-Steve-Man Corby are from Adamsville. Adamsville is on the western outscapes of Atlanta, Georgia. There are several gangs or cliques that call Adamsville home such as the ADV Clique, 10, Y.M.A., Bloods,Crips and Down By Law.

The skating rink Cascades for the movie ATL is located in Adamsville. Right around the corner from the new projects Allen Temple, Flatland, Hightower Ridge, and Fairburn Central.

[edit] History

Founded as a farming villiage, it was possibly named for early resident G.W. Adams who farmed there in 1882 when it was called Lick Skillet. The name was changed to Adamsville in 1906.[1]
