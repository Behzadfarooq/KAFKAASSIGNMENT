[edit] Adam Johnston

Adam Johnston was Born on June 30, 1980 in Winnipeg Manitoba Canada. Adam is a graduate of the Computer Accounting Technican Program from Red River College and is working on his Business and Administrative Studies Certificate from Red River College:Continuing Education. He also graduated Miles Macdonnell Collegiate in 1998. He also works at H&R Block as a tax associate, as well as TAB Services as a bookkeeper

Adam is a member of the Mantioba New Democratic Party. He was chair of the Manitoba NDP Environmental Commitee from March 2004 untill February of 2006.

In February of 2006 at the Manitoba New Democratic Party, he stepped down as chair of the Environmental committee at the 2006 , because he said "someone else deserves to lead".

Another reason is he wants to devote more time for more personal endevours, including more community work. Lastly and perhaps most importantly is he wants to develop policy on building a more market based socialist/populist, pro small business platform, an "Opportunity Society" that former Democratic Party vice presidental cadidate John Edwards has promoted vigourisly with the One America Committee.

In February 2006 at the Manitoba NDP Convention, Adam wrote a resolution that passed, in creating a new small business/co-operative committee, with the goal of disscussing issues on promoting small business, co-operatives and credit unions.

Although a member of the NDP, Adam has frequently gone against the grain against his own party on, namely a more conservative stance on crime & punishment (tougher punishment), marijuana (wanting it to stay illegal), as well as a more fair and equitable taxation policy for small based businesses.

Adam views are more of a populist, anti big business/big government viewpoint. He believes that more co-operatives, could provide some services just as well as government crown corporations, as well as small business being the great leveller in lifting people out of poverty and cleaning our environment.

Adam has made it clear in numerous speeches that the Manitoba NDP needs to create new taxation polices to promote worker owned businesses, worker owned co-ops, consumer owned co-ops, credit unions, small businesses and environmental entrepreneurship, in helping to create a more modern, decentralized version of 21st centrury democratic socialism.
