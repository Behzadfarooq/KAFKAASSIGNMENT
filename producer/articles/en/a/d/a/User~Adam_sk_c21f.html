Hi all. I'm Adam from Saskatchewan (hence the catchy Adam_sk name).

[edit] Brief bio

- born in 1980
- grew up in Oxbow, Saskatchewan
- since the Scouts maintain a List of notable Scouts on Wikipedia, I note that I was a member of Scouts Canada, though I must admit that I was a Beaver and a Cub, but never went on to Scout level
- in high school, I was really involved in Debate and represented Saskatchewan at Junior Nationals in Halifax in 1995 and at Senior Nationals in 1997 - I also debated on the CUSID circuit as an undergraduate, but didn't have much success there
- other activities I was involved with during my elementary and high school years: baseball, band, volleyball, drama, the Jr. Students' Representative Council, and our school's environmental club
- I did my undergrad degree (B.A., 2002) in History at the University of Saskatchewan
- during undergrad, I debated, was a member of Circle K, spent some time in Campus Crusade for Christ before growing disillusioned, and spent a year as chair of the University Students' Council (the governing body of the University of Saskatchewan Students' Union)
- I spent a period thinking I wanted to be a History Professor, and therefore spent three years in the Ph.D. program in History at Brown University, getting my master's degree in 2003
- in 2004, I passed my preliminary exams and became ABD - my dissertation (had I completed it) would have focused on the emergence of a tradition of religious toleration in later Stuart England - I got to work with a lot of awesome profs during my time there, one of whom has a Wikipedia entry about him, Gordon S. Wood
- I've since decided that I want to be a lawyer and, as such, in Fall 2005, I moved on to Harvard Law School
- spending next summer (2007) at Jenner & Block in Chicago
- raised in the United Church of Canada, but, having discovered that at heart I'm a theological conservative, and having moved to the United States, currently worship in the Presbyterian Church in America

[edit] Contributions

At this point, the biggest article I've written (and one which I created) is on the Fundamentalist-Modernist Controversy, which neatly tied together my personal and former professional interests. I also created an article a notable conservative leader during that controversy, Clarence Macartney.

I wrote an article on prominent Providence, Rhode Island philanthropist Alan S. Feinstein (whose face is everywhere in Providence) after being particularly impressed by him during a trip to the Feinstein Imax Theater in the Providence Place Mall.

At one point I looked up John W. Snow's biography, thought it was woefully inadequate, and thus spent a fair amount of time revising it.

Finding a non-functioning link to the Canadian Club of Toronto in some Wikipedia article, and having long been aware of the Canadian Club as a rival to the Empire Club of Canada, whose addresses I make a point to read every year when they are published, I thought I should add a brief article, containing what I know about the Canadian Club.

Other articles I made somewhat substantial contributions to:

- Politics Drawn from the Very Words of Holy Scripture by Bossuet
- Geerhardus Vos
- Farley Mowat
- Heinrich Bullinger

- Theology (particularly adding to the section "Divisions of Theology", which I thought was not systematic enough prior to my edit)
