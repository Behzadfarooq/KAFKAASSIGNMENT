Adamm currently works as a senior executive in the public health sector in Melbourne Australia.

He holds undergraduate qualifications in both nursing and prosthetics & orthotics, and postgraduate diplomas in operating room nursing and health services management. He completed a Masters of Health Administration, the thesis paper investigating the issues associated with the capture and management of prosthetic data in operating rooms. He is a Member of the Royal College of Nursing Australia, and an Associate of the Australian Health Services Financial Management Association. He has worked in a number of hospitals and health services across Melbourne, and has completed consultancies in both the private health insurance industry and in overseas hospitals. His particular professional interest is the economic consequences arising from the management of data relating to surgical prostheses.

Adamm may be emailed; although diatribes from religious fundamentalists are unwelcome as he prefers to respect a person's individual religious beliefs without having the odious task of having to acknowledge them.

His Wikipedia interests include Melbourne railways, local history, music, the Anglican Church in Australia and (more recently) professional issues such as nursing, especially the transfer of nursing education from hospitals to the tertiary sector.
