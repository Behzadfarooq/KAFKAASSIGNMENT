Adagio
Studio album by Jade Villalon
Released 2004

Adagio is the third album by singer Jade Villalon of Sweetbox. It was released in 2004. There are two different versions of the album. One was released in Japan and the other was released in Europe, Korea and Taiwan. Adagio debuted at #3 in Japan.

Adagio sees new sounds of music that were left out of Sweetbox when Tina left, like R&B, Rap, and Hip-Hop, although it keeps the pop and dance sound which Sweetbox are famous for. Lyrically, this albulm is Jade's deepest yet, taking on themes of depression and suicide, which is something Sweetbox is new to. There are also a handful of more bright, 'Life Is Cool' songs in the album to remind everyone that you should still keep trying and enjoy life.

[edit] Trivia

  • "1000 Words" and "real Emotion" were recorded for the console game Final Fantasy X-2
  • Lacrimosa, meaning 'tearful' in Italian, is musically based on Mozart's 'Lacrimosa' from his unfinished Requiem in D Minor, which he died while writing.
  • The song "Chyna Girl" refers to the porcelain china, where-as saying the girl in the song is delicate, much like porcelain china, not the country of China.
  • According to Jade, the reason why she was influnenced to write mainly about depression and suicide on this album is because of a girl she knew named Adriana who committed suicide.
  • The chorus in the demo version of "Liberty", featured in the Raw Treasures album, has Adriana's name in it, asking why she gave up her life. This chorus's lyrics were cut from the final version of "Liberty"
