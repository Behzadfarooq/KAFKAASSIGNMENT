Adam at 6 A.M. is a 1970 movie directed by Robert Scheerer. The film did not receive much attention when it was released, but was one of the first movies Michael Douglas first starred. The movie was filmed almost entirely on location in the small midwest town of Excelsior Springs, Missouri,[1] as well as Cameron, Missouri.[2]

[edit] Movie plot

Spoiler warning: Plot and/or ending details follow.

The film revolves around Adam Gaines (Douglas), a Semenatics professor for a California college. He becomes complacent in his life and hears about the death of a relative in Missouri. He drives cross country to attend the funeral and pay his respects. He decides to spend the summer there working as a laborer. He meets Jerri Jo Hopper (Purcell), and falls in love, along the way developing new friendships with the town locals. He then must decide what direction he wants his life to go, whether to return to Calfornia or stay in Missouri.
