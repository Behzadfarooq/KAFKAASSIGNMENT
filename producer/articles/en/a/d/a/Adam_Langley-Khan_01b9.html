Adam Khan (born 24th May, 1985), is a British racing driver of from Bridlington, Yorkshire, who represented Pakistan in the A1 Grand Prix series.

Eddie Jordan endorsed Adam Khan's career when Khan was 17 years of age. Khan competed in various races in the British F3 championship whilst undertaking studies at school. Khan won in the scholarship class in his second ever F3 race at Rockingham motor speedway and has finished on the podium in the majority of F3 races in which he contested, including one off races in the German F3 championship and the British F3 championship at Monza in 2005.

In 2005 Adam Khan was selected as the driver for the Pakistani A1 Team for the A1 Grand Prix. Team Pakistan's 05/06 A1 season started off with an immediate impact with Khan getting up to 3rd position at the season opener at Brands Hatch against vastly more experienced drivers, though a stuck wheel nut during the pitstop put an end to a chance of a podium result. Further costly team errors occurred during races where Khan was in strong positions, however Team Pakistan managed to finish the season on a high note with a 5th place at Shanghai. For 2006 Khan will be taking a break from racing, instead concentrating on his business studies course at King's College London.

Khan has pledged to wear the Pakistan flag whenever he races in the future, as part of an initiative to integrate Pakistan into the exciting world of professional motor sports, and has been officially appointed as the Ambassador for motor sports in Pakistan by the government. Khan was recently awarded a young achievers award by the President of Pakistan in London on September 29, 2006.

For 2007, Khan will return to racing in the World Series by Renault, driving for the Italian RC Motorsport team [1].
