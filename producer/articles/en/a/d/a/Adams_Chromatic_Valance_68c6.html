Adams Chromatic Valance color spaces are a class of color spaces suggested by E Q Adams in an article in 1942. In it, he also suggested Adams Chromatic Value color spaces. Some important color spaces are Adams Chromatic Valance spaces, including CIELUV and Hunter Lab.

[edit] Elements of a Chromatic Valance Space

Chromatic Valance Spaces incorporate two elements: a (relatively) perceptually uniform chromaticity scale, and a (relatively) perceptually uniform lightness scale.

The Lightness scale forms one axis of the color space. The remaining two axes are formed by multiplying the two uniform chromaticity coordinates by the lightness.

[edit] Uniform Chromaticity Scales

In chromaticity scales, luminance is factored out, leaving two dimensions. Two lights with the same spectral power distribution, but different luminances, will have identical chromaticity coordinates. The familiar CIE (x, y) chromaticity diagram is very perceptually non-uniform; small perceptual changes in chromaticity in greens, for example, translate into large distances, while larger perceptual differences in chromaticity in other colors are usually much smaller.
