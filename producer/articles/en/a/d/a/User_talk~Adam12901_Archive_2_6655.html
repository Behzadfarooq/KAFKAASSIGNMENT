[edit] Canoeing and Camping on the Lower Wisconsin River

Hi,

I am the author for that article. I was under the impression that I could copy something directly from a website if permission was granted. If I am wrong please let me know. I have created and written the website that it came from myself. They are my own words, just also on my website.

please let me know what to do, I would like to keep the article on.

Thanks,

Erik —The preceding unsigned comment was added by Ecras (talk • contribs) 04:20, 8 February 2007 (UTC).

Even if there is permission (whether expressed or in the public domain), you cannot copy word for word. The same rules apply here as do when writing a university research paper. Copying word for word is considered plagarism. If you wish to recreate this article, please make sure that it is not word for word, or anything that could be considered plagarism. Wikipedia is not in the business of having information that is exactly the same as other websites. If people want that information, they should just go to those websites. Also, if you are going to recreate this article, please keep in mind that #2 of WP:NOT#IINFO states that Wikipedia is not a travel guide. If you wanted to write about the flora/fauna, historical geography, or tourism industry of the lower Wisconsin River, then that would be fine. If you feel that you want to write a travel guide, please use Wikitravel, where an article like the ones you previously posted would be better suited. If you have any more questions, please feel free to leave another comment on my talk page. --Адам12901 Talk 04:33, 8 February 2007 (UTC)
