The CC-by-sa license is a true free documentation license that is similar to Wikipedia's license, the GFDL, but it allows other projects, such as WikiTravel, to use our articles. Since you are among the top 2000 Wikipedians by edits, I was wondering if you would be willing to multi-license all of your contributions or at minimum those on the geographic articles. Over 90% of people asked have agreed. For More Information:

  • Multi-Licensing FAQ - Lots of questions answered
  • Multi-Licensing Guide
  • Free the Rambot Articles Project

To allow us to track those users who muli-license their contributions, many users copy and paste the "{{DualLicenseWithCC-BySA-Dual}}" template into their user page, but there are other options at Template messages/User namespace. The following examples could also copied and pasted into your user page:

Option 1
I agree to [[Wikipedia:Multi-licensing|multi-license]] all my contributions, with the exception of my user pages, as described below:
{{DualLicenseWithCC-BySA-Dual}}

OR

Option 2
I agree to [[Wikipedia:Multi-licensing|multi-license]] all my contributions to any [[U.S. state]], county, or city article as described below:
{{DualLicenseWithCC-BySA-Dual}}

Or if you wanted to place your work into the public domain, you could replace "{{DualLicenseWithCC-BySA-Dual}}" with "{{MultiLicensePD}}". If you only prefer using the GFDL, I would like to know that too. Please let me know what you think at my talk page. It's important to know either way so no one keeps asking. -- Ram-Man (comment| talk)

[edit] Attempts to avoid taxation in the UK

I'm responding to your question about Taxation in the UK and whether there is mass avoidance or attempts to move out of the country. For tax purposes, there is little disadvantage of being a UK resident, as our tax burden is overall quite low (both by European standards, but also compared to the US by comparison to Government services provided). While UK residents are taxed on worldwide income, there is also the issue that it's rather hard for HM Customs & Revenue to find foreign assets, so wealthier British citizens, should they wish to hide assets, can do so rather easily. Similarly, because Income Tax is applied differently to rental income in the UK, as well as Inheritance Tax for leaseholds/freeholds, a wealthy person can easily maintain a lower rate of tax liability, and do, without having to move assets abroad.

While in the 1970s the UK had an extremely high tax burden (as did the US, where both countries had upper marginal rates in excess of 70 per cent) those rates have come down dramatically in the past twenty-five years. The top rate of tax, at 40p, is comparable to the US without much trouble (as the US levies Federal, State, and sometimes City/County income taxes), and the services provided for remaining in the UK (healthcare, educational, etc.) are quite good, considering the tax burden.

It's also prudent to understand that the predominent number of Britons with money are from the middle classes, and thus benefit both from the existing system, but also that they benefit from living in the UK, as they continue to work for livings, rather than residing on large family estates. So the ability to migrate abroad for tax purposes is limited, as they would simply migrate to somewhere with a similar or worse tax regime. Hope this explains a bit and do feel free to ask if you have any specific questions. Nick Kerr 13:42, 14 December 2005 (UTC)
