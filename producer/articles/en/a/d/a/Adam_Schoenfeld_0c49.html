Adam Schoenfeld is an American professional poker player and former columnist for Card Player magazine. Schoenfeld decided to quit his job as the vice president of an Internet analysis firm in order to play poker full-time.[1] He is considered one of the best players to never have cashed in the World Series of Poker. In the 2004 WSOP, Schoenfeld played in ten events but did not make any money.[2] That same year, he made the top 15 in World Poker Tour events three times, making one final table. In 2005, he was the analyst for the Boston vs. New York Poker Challenge and appeared in the "Poker for Dummies" DVD along with Chris Moneymaker.[3]

After his poor 2004 performance, Schoenfeld did not play in the 2005 World Series of Poker, but returned for the 2006 WSOP, in which he again failed to finish in the money in any event. As of 2006, his total live tournament winnings exceed $350,000.[4]

Schoenfeld is the ex-boyfriend of Evelyn Ng. She mentions him as one of the poker players she respects most.[5]
