[edit] The Victors

I left a response on the article's talk page. Can you address it as soon as possible? Thanks. PentawingTalk 05:38, 11 June 2006 (UTC)

With your suggesting to place the parody under "folklore," maybe it will work. However, the wording has to reflect that (in order to prevent the idea that the parody, if added, is vandalism). Also, it would probably be a good idea to explain why such lyrics were sung, when someone outside UM believes that most, if not all, people from UM are rabid partisans (personally, I never heard the parody lyrics on campus). So far, this is the wording that I am coming up with (it is also the article's talk page):
A parody of the lyrics exist since the 1950s. It had been sung in UM residence halls, such as East Quad and West Quad, from 1979 to 1984.
Hail! to the victims valiant
Hail! to the conquered heroes
Hail! Hail! to Michigan
the doormats of the west
To clarify why I might seem overprotective of the "alma mata," I should note that such vandalism has occurred with every school article that I have looked at (not to mention the huge amount of vandalism received when the UM article was on the front page). At first, the vandalism was obvious (e.g. a picture of a pig for Michigan Stadium and the words "Michigan suck!"), though eventually vandals became so discreet over the vandalism that it is difficult to detect if not for article difference comparisons. Hope this clarifies my actions to you. Also, feel free to leave a message on my talk page. PentawingTalk 04:17, 12 June 2006 (UTC)
