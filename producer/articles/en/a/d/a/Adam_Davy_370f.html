Adam Davy (born 30.08.1977) is an actor and an accomplished multi-instrumentalist.

[edit] Biography

Adam Luke Davy grew up in London, England. At the age of 8 he moved with his family to Oxford, where he was educated at 'The Cherwell School', then later at 'Henley College'.


[edit] Career

At the age of 8 Adam joined Humphrey Carpenters, 'Mushey Pea Theatre Company', in which, at the age of 15, Adam played the title role in 'MR Majeika' and Charlie Chaplin in 'Babes' at the Shaw Theatre, London (1995).

In 1997 Adam Davy won a place at the world famous Bristol Old Vic Theatre School, where he was part of the last group to receive tuition from guru acting teacher, Rudi Shelley. Shortly before graduating in 2000, Adam Davy, was asked to take part in, 'The story of Christmas', he was awarded Â£1000, to contribute to course funding, by the patron of the Charitable oragnisation: Dame Judi Dench. In 2003 he got the role as Joseph in the stage play 'We Happy Few', (which transferred from, 'Malvern theatre', to the, 'Gielgud', in the west end), it was written by Imogen Stubbs and Directed by Trevor Nunn. He then went on to play the young C S Lewis in, 'C S lewis: Beyond Narnia', directed by Bafta award winning director Norman Stone for BBC television and Hallmark channel and DVD.

On the music front: Adam Davy has toured with many bands and worked with many musicians including, Andy Leek, of the 'Dexys Midnight Runners' and legendary poet/musician Pete Brown. Tour work has taken Adam to the Pink Elephant Jazz, the Cork Jazz festival, to name a couple. Currentlty Adam is touring with, 'Us Not Them', a top Pink Floyd tribute act. Also he headlines in the opening and closing band at Ain't Nothing But' Londons' finest Blues venue.

Annually Adam's has a residency at the ,'Kay Club',Ireland, home of the world famous, Ryders cup tournament. He has also played at the Royal polo event: Cartier.

[edit] Private Life

Adam Davy currently lives in the Angel, London.
