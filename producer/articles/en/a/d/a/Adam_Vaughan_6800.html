Adam Vaughan

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Adam Vaughan (born ca. 1961 in Toronto, Ontario) is the city councillor-elect for Ward 20 Trinity—Spadina in Toronto, Canada. For the last 25 years, Vaughan has worked as a political journalist, most recently he was the Political Specialist at Citytv/CP24. Before that for ten years, he worked at the Canadian Broadcasting Corporation as a municipal reporter and producer. Vaughan has covered Toronto City Hall, Queen's Park and Parliament Hill in his career.

[edit] Private life

He is married to Lina Cino with whom he has a son (Salvador). He also has a daughter (Mimi) from a previous marriage to Suhana Meharchand. [1]

His father, Colin Vaughan, was also a noted television journalist and former city councillor, who preceded Adam as Citytv's political reporter until his death in 2000.

[edit] Politics

Vaughan ran as an independent in Trinity-Spadina - Ward 20 to fill the vacant seat left open by Olivia Chow's departure after her win in the 2006 federal election. He won the seat, beating Helen Kennedy, Chow's executive assistant. He currently serves as Vice Chair of the Toronto and East York Community Council.

[edit] Career in journalism

Adam Vaughan worked at Ryerson University radio station CKLN from 1982 to 1987, and was manager of the station from 1985 to 1987. He joined Citytv in 1987 to produce CityWide. He left in 1989 to join the board of the World Association of Community Radio Broadcasters.

In 1990, he joined CBC Radio's CBLA Metro Morning as a segment producer. He subsequently joined CBC Television's CBLT in 1994, covering City Hall. He returned to the Citytv team in 2000.

As a reporter, Vaughan became noted as a critic of Mel Lastman, especially with regard to shoplifting charges against Lastman's wife. The colourful Lastman threatened to kill him as a result. On Vaughan's entry into public life, Lastman said: "Hey let me tell ya, this is a new low for politics. For him to win it means politics is going to the dogs. They're a bunch of duds, and this guy is going to be the king of them if he gets elected."[1]

Vaughan has also written for Toronto Life magazine and the Toronto Star. Before becoming a journalist Adam Vaughan was a cartoonist for Books in Canada, Quill and Quire, Canadian Forum and several other publications.
