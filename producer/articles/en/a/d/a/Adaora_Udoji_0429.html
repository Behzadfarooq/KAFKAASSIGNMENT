Adaora Elizabeth Udoji (born 1967) is a host on Court TV. She is a former correspondent for CNN and is based in New York City. Her father, Godfrey Udoji, is the chief engineer for the City of Dearborn, Michigan. Her mother, Mary, is the director of the Washtenaw County Library in Ann Arbor, Michigan.

Udoji holds a B.A. from the University of Michigan and a J.D. from UCLA.

She joined CNN in 2003 from ABC News, where she served as an international correspondent based in London. Starting in 1995, she reported and produced international stories for ABC News, covering Africa, the Middle East and Europe. She also contributed to Good Morning America, World News Weekend and ABC Radio.

International assignments that she has covered include the war in Iraq (from Qatar and Iraq); the war in Afghanistan (from Pakistan); the Israeli-Palestinian conflict (from Israel and Jordan); Pope John Paul II from Vatican City; and several sporting events including Wimbledon, the British Open and the Tour de France. Stateside, Udoji covered the 1996 presidential campaign, the crash of TWA Flight 800 over New York, and the O.J. Simpson criminal and civil trials. She was part of the team that won a Cine Eagle Award for an ABC primetime documentary about death row in 1997.

In February 2005, she was named as a Woodrow Wilson Visiting Fellow from the Ohio Dominican University. She was a member of the CNN news team covering the aftermath of Hurricane Katrina in and around New Orleans, Louisiana.

She married fellow TV journalist, Ron Allen of NBC News, in 2002.
