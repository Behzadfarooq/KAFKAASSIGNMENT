Adambakkam is a suburb of Chennai, a city situated on the east coast of the southern India. It is a thickly populated suburb flanking the railway line, close to the St. Thomas Mount Railway Station, Chennai. Adambakkam is surrounded by suburbs such as Alandur, Madipakkam, Nanganallur and Velachery. The residents mostly belong to the middle and upper middle classes. Unlike many other suburbs in Chennai, this place has more individual houses rather than apartments and so it makes an ideal residential place. In the past few years there had been acute water problems which was historically very rare to this locality. Mainly because this area had been a lake area and so it was never short of ground water and yearly rainfall was catering to the water needs to the residents. This is one of the localities in Chennai which has come up without much planning contribution from Municipality for a long time. Lately this place has been blessed with underground drainage, relayed roads and surprisingly broad band connection as well.

Adambakkam has many sub-localities, such as AGS Colony, Bhagyammal Nagar, Brindavan Nagar, EB Colony, New Colony, NGO Colony, Ramakrishnapuram, and others. Ramakrishnapuram connects Brindavan Nagar and Kakkan Nagar via Kakkan Bridge. Adambakkam post office is located in Ramakrishnapuram locality.

[edit] Geography

Brindavan Nagar Main Road in Adambakkam

Adambakkam is located at 12.99° N 80.20° E[1]. It has an average elevation of 7 metres (26 feet). Adambakkam is a low-lying area and light rains lead to water-logging. The underground drainage and storm water canals help very rarely when there is a downpour during monsoon season.

[edit] Culture

Lord Subramanya temple in Brindavan Nagar, Adambakkam

Adambakkam has a population with diverse cultures where Hindus, Christians and Muslims co-exist. Prominent Hindu temples are the Devi Nagamuthu Mariamman temple in EB Colony, Lord Subramanya temple in Brindavan Nagar, Bhuvaneshwari Amman temple in Andal Nagar and Nandeeswarar temple near St. Thomas Mount railway station. Besides, Chennai's only Viswaroopa Sarvamangala Shaneeswara Bhagawan sannidhi is located inside Devi Nagamuthu Mariamma temple.

Other temples:

  • Pazhandi Amman Koil (Near St.Thomas Railway Station)
  • The Sundara Vinayagar temple in N.G.O. colony.
  • Sri Varasidhi Vinayagar Koil (opposite to Adambakkam Police Station).
  • Sri Karpaga Vinayagar Temple and Sri Rukmani Pandruranga Temple in Shanthi Nagar.

[edit] Transport

Karuneegar Street, adjacent to the St. Thomas Mount Railway Station, is a business center of Adambakkam, and connects Adambakkam to Alandur

There is very high two-wheeler traffic in Adambakkam. The residents are served by the bus numbers 9M,51P,51R,51N,M14 of the Metropolitan Transport Corporation. Bus routes connecting Madipakkam, Medavakkam areas to the City which ply through this area also caters to commuting. The St. Thomas Railway Station, part of the Chennai suburban railway serves the city's railway commuters.

[edit] Education

Adambakkam has both public (government run) and private schools teaching students from kindergarten to high school. Notable schools are,

  • Mount Christian Matriculation Higher Secondary School (MCMHSS)
  • G.K.Shetty Hindu Vidyalaya Matriculation Higher Secondary School [1] located in Brindavan Nagar.
  • Vyasa Vidyalaya Matriculation Higher Secondary School
  • DAV School located in AGS colony.
  • Indra Gandhi Matriculation School
  • Little Flower School
