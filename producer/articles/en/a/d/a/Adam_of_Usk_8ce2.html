Adam of Usk

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Adam of Usk (Welsh: Adda Brynbuga) (c.1352 - 1430 ) was an Welsh priest, canonist, and chronicler.

Born at Usk in South Wales, he studied at Oxford, where he obtained his doctorate and became extraordinarius in Canon law. He practised in the archiepiscopal court of Canterbury, 1390-1397, and in 1399 accompanied the Archbishop and Bolingbroke's army on the march to Chester. After Richard II's surrender Adam was rewarded with the living of Kemsing and Seal, and later with a prebend in the church of Bangor. However, he forfeited the King's favour by the boldness of his criticisms, and was banished to Rome in 1402, where in 1404 and later he was successively nominated to the sees of Hereford and St. David's, but was unable to obtain possession of either.
