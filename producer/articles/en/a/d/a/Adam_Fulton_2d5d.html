Adam Fulton

Adam Christopher Fulton (1982-2004) (aka Livecorpse) was a Flash animator of Newgrounds.com. From September 6th of 2003 to his death in June of 2004, Fulton submitted 100 flash animations to Newgrounds.com; a feat that very few of the website's users have achieved in a timeframe of several years. He gained a very large following due to his dark sense of humor and -- ironically -- lighthearted approach to death and suicide.

[edit] Incident

In June of 2004, Adam Fulton committed suicide after a series of events leading to a three day search for his arrest. As described in Adam Fulton's suicide letters sent via e-mail, Fulton claims to have unintentionally stabbed his persistent neighbor who refused to leave his house while the two were intoxicated and under the influence of marijuana. Shortly after, as Fulton attempted to leave his apartment complex, Tucson police confronted Fulton whom, as claimed in the letters, he fired upon in a blind stupor from the drugs and alcohol, wounding one officer in the leg. Three days later, Fulton returned to his apartment, where the events had taken place, and was soon surrounded by local police. During this time, Fulton wrote suicide letters and e-mailed them to his closest friends and family members, before shooting himself in the head.

[edit] Effect on Newgrounds community

In the months following Adam Fulton's death, the Newgrounds community mourned the loss. Many animations and songs were made in memory of the popular animator, and many of Newgrounds.com's forum users posted goodbye's and R.I.P.'s in their signatures. Occasionally still in the Newgrounds community, authors will pay tribute or dedicate their films to Adam Fulton and his legacy.

One user named Piconjo [1], a name that parodies the mascot of Newgrounds, "Pico", created a dark tribute to the animator which offended many. Unfortunately, Piconjo has somewhat of a following on the internet and the parodies continued. See Livecorpse Tribute [2]. However, unknown to the people who were offended by the Piconjo flash, Fulton was one of the four people who actually created Piconjo. He submitted a large portion of his flashes co-authoring Piconjo, and ironically the satire of his suicide would most likely be the way he would have most liked to be remembered by.
