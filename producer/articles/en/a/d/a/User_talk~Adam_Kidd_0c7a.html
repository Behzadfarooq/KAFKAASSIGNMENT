Adam Kidd is the lead singer and guitarist in a UK band, based in Brighton, UK named Kiyomori. He also plays solo and has recorded a solo album titled '...from the bedroom'. A collection of songs recorded in a very lo-fi fashion. Adam played most parts himself, although there are occasional appearances from Brighton musicians Adam has associated with. (Such as Matthew Twaites, from Restlesslist; Steve Hoile from the Mojo Fins and Hanna Tuuliki.)

Adam was born in Hull in 1981 and moved to Brighton aged 5. His father Jeremy was the singer in cult 80's band the Red Guitars, once darlings of the NME. Red Guitars 1984 album 'Slow To Fade' hit the top of the indie charts, and has recently been licesnced for re-release by Cherry Red records.

Adam has been seen playing music in Brighton's many music venues for the last 10 years, in numerous local bands, with verying degrees of longevity and success. His band rift flirted with potential success on a number of occasions, but never quite had what it takes. He is also known for playing in Sunburst, Pornography, Marcel Marcel Marcel and The Promiscuous Autocracy, but it is his latest group, Kiyomori, who have gained the highest profile.

One recent review, in Brighton magazine 'the Source', describes the band thus, "If Muse came from more metal territory they would sound like Kiyomori, who are less operatic and theatrical, yet just as nimble., with an ear for unexpected twists in the details.".

Examples of Adams music can be found on his myspace page, or the Kiyomori myspace page. Kiyomori are named after a samurai warlord from feudal Japan. The name is also said to lead to good fortune in the music and the arts, according to the kabalarian philosophy of names.
