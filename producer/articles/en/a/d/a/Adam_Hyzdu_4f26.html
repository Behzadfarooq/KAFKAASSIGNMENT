Adam Hyzdu

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Adam David Hyzdu [highs-du] (born December 6, 1971 in San Jose, California) is an outfielder for Fukuoka SoftBank Hawks in the Pacific League (Japan).

In Major League Baseball, he had played for the Pittsburgh Pirates, (2000-2003), Boston Red Sox (2004 and (2005), San Diego Padres (2005) and the Texas Rangers (2006).

[edit] History

Hydzu was originally drafted by the San Francisco Giants in the first round of the 1990 amateur draft, but never reached the Major Leagues as a Giant. He bats and throws right-handed. He was signed by the Texas Rangers to a minor league contract on November 3, 2005.

Hyzdu is very well known for his strides in the minor leagues. During his two seasons with the Altoona Curve, he wrote the record books of the young franchise, and many of those records still stand today. He was an Eastern League all-star both seasons and even had his number 16 retired by the club. He is still regarded as a legend in the town of Altoona.

In a six-season major league career, Hyzdu is a .229 hitter with 19 home runs and 61 RBI in 219 games.

With the Red Sox in 2004, Hyzdu batted .300 (3 for 10) with a home run, two RBI, and 2 doubles in 17 games. In 129 games with Pawtucket he batted .301 with 29 home runs, 79 RBI, and led the team in walks with 84.

Hyzdu attended Moeller High School in Cincinnati, where he broke Ken Griffey Jr.'s home run record (22) and led his team to win the state championship in 1989.

After 2006 season, he joined the Fukuoka SoftBank Hawks of Japanese Pacific League with one year contract.
