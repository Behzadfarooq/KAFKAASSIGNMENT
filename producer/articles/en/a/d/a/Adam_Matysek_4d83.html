Adam Matysek

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Adam Matysek (born July 19, 1968 in Piekary Śląskie) is a retired Polish football goalkeeper.

He played for clubs such as Śląsk Wrocław, Fortuna Köln (Germany), FC Gütersloh (Germany), Bayer Leverkusen (Germany), Zagłębie Lubin or RKS Radomsko.

He also played for the Polish national team and was a participant at the 2002 FIFA World Cup.

After his playing career, Matysek worked for the Polish Football Association as their U21 goalkeeper coach. He was then recruited by Martin Bader, the Director of Football at 1.F.C. Nürnberg, as the new goalkeeper coach for the ‘Club’. He has since helped the club turn their goalkeeper Raphael Schäfer into contention for the German National team.

Matysek is married and has three children.
