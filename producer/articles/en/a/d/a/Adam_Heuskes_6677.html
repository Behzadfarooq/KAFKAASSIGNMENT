Career Highlights

Adam Heuskes (born March 20, 1976) is a former Australian rules footballer in the Australian Football League most remembered for his flamboyance.

Recruited from SANFL club Norwood, Heuskes made his AFL debut in 1994 with the Sydney Swans and immediately attracted attention in the media and the football world for his outrageous hairstylings.

He was traded to the Port Adelaide Power in 1997, a decision which the Swans later regretted. His attacking flair as a defender set up numerous goals.

For the 1999 season, Heuskes moved up to play with the Brisbane Lions, where he was a solid contributor during the Lions' 1999 finals campaign. At the end of 2000 his career was over after playing 14 games during the season.

He later played 31 games for SANFL club North Adelaide before succumbing to an ankle injury in 2005 which forced his retirement from all forms of the game.

Adam Heuskes has a daughter, Tayla, born in 1992, and is married to Nikki Kosmider.

[edit] Off-field behaviour

Two notable haircuts included the image of the Sydney Opera House shaved into the back of his head, as well as his guernsey number (39). He was also, very famously, a cross-dresser who appeared on The Footy Show and Live And Kicking regularly during 1998 and 1999. He once dressed up as Madonna and performed the song Holiday.

Aside from his antics in performance, Adam Heuskes has gained notoriety for some infamous incidents involving alcohol, and an alleged inability to control his primal urges[1].

[edit] Retirement

Heuskes' career finished at the end of 2000.
