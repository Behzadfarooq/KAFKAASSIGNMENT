Adam Burbank Lazzara (born September 22, 1981 in Sheffield, Alabama), is the lead singer of the American rock band, Taking Back Sunday. Along with singing lead vocals, Adam Lazzara occasionally plays guitar and harmonica as well.

[edit] Biography

Adam Lazzara grew up in High Point, North Carolina, attending Southwest Guilford High School, before moving to Long Island. He has a younger brother, Nathan Lazzara, who is also a musician. In 2001 Adam joined Taking Back Sunday, replacing Antonio Longo who sang briefly on their first EP. Later that year the band released their debut album Tell All Your Friends. On the album he sung co-lead vocals with guitarist John Nolan. It gained popularity by the single Cute Without The 'E' (Cut From The Team), which had a Fight Club themed video. After a fairly bitter fight between the two of them (Adam had previously had romantic relations with Nolan's sister, Michelle Nolan, and rumors suggest that this may have been the real cause), John and bassist Shaun Cooper left to form Straylight Run. They were replaced by singer\guitarist Fred Mascherino and bassist Matt Rubano, a former child friend of Mark O'Connell. Adam is engaged to Eisley guitarist Chauntelle DuPree. The proposal took place on February 14, 2007, at a Starbucks in Tyler, Texas.[1]

[edit] Stage Antics

He has performed singing as well as "hardcore" screaming on his studio albums, and his live shows are known to be energetic. He tries to interact with the fans as much as he can during concerts. In 2006 his stage antics were noticeably less energetic. The reason to this is unknown, but it has been reported that the band's label has warned them of dangerous stunts resulting in injuries that might cause of death.[citation needed]

On February 17th 2007 the band issued a statement on their Myspace saying this was not the case: "Anyone who has been to recent shows knows that Adam is as active as ever. Anyone remember when he knocked Matt out by swinging the mic in the UK in April? Anyone see the last show on the summer tour where he climbed about 30 feet in the air to hang upside down to finish the last song? Our new label has been nothing but supportive with everything we have done. It's an incredible partnership. They would never tell us to change anything. Please challenge yourselves to think about things you hear." (myspace.com/takingbacksunday)

[edit] Give it A Name Accident

Recently, in front of roughly 12,000 people, at Earls Court during the Give It A Name Festival of 2006 he accidentally hit bassist Matt Rubano in the head while swinging his microphone, which resulted in six stitches. This incident re-popularized a t-shirt design: a bloody microphone wrapped around the neck. This tendency was once mocked by fellow Long Island band Brand New, who released T-shirts reading "Mics are for Singing, Not for Swinging". The shirts was designed during the feuding between bands in the early 2000s. In response, Taking Back Sunday released a shirt that read: "Proudly Swinging Since 1999".

[edit] Trivia

  • He originally joined Taking Back Sunday to play bass.[1]
  • Adam enjoys regularly altering his appearance (in particular his hair). He once went completely bald (Long before Louder Now) and has also surprised fans by dying it blonde, but then (as you see in the making of "Liar (It Takes One to Know One)" video) dyed it back to brown at the request of the director who said that the contrast between his hair and face wouldn't work for the black and white video. "You don't like blondes, man. Whatever," Adam says as we see the director talk to him while his hair is being dyed. [2]
  • Recently made his acting debut (alongside his band) on the Degrassi episode, "What's It Feel Like To Be A Ghost, Pt. 2".
  • Adam's vocals appear on the Cyndi Lauper song "Money Changes Everything." He also appeared in the music video.
  • During Taking Back Sunday's 2006 Taste of Chaos tour of the UK, Adam Lazzara had to step in to cover for absent guitarist Fred Mascherino after he had returned home early due to family problems. On every date of the 2006 tour TBS shared the stage with members of the other bands on the tour to temporarily replace Mascherino. He also performed solo acoustic guitar and harmonica performances of 'Divine Intervention' from their Louder Now album and a cover of The Cure's classic, 'Just Like Heaven.' At the London date of the tour, as well as March 13th in Irvine, CA, he performed a cover of 'When You Were Young' by The Killers, as well as "My Love" by Justin Timberlake at another UK date and at the Dublin performance[3].
  • Adam and Gerard Way have been known as close friends. And both have appeared on the other band's stage. Adam has sung I'm Not Okay (I Promise) with My Chemical Romance, replacing Gerard when he was unable to perform.
  • Adam used to skateboard, as seen in the Louder Now Partone: DVD.
