Adal Ramones

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Liza Echeverría and Adal Ramones hosting Bailando por un sueño.

Adal Ramones (born Adalberto Javier Ramones Martínez , December 3, 1961 in Monterrey, Nuevo León) is a Mexican television show host and comedian who is known for his comments on Mexican and international social life. Ramones is the host of a popular Mexican television show, Otro Rollo, which is produced by Televisa and televised in 53 other countries, including the United States, where the show is transmitted by Univision.

[edit] Biography

Ramones suffered heart disease for a long period of his life. After several operations, he recuperated. In 2000, Ramones also suffered a kidnapping. Since 1998, Ramones is romantically involved with Gaby Valencia and in 2001, their first child (a daughter) was born. She carries the name Paola Ramones.

In February of 2003, Ramones suffered burns to several parts of his body as he was taping his show, requiring hospitalization. He had to wear glasses and a baseball hat for a period of time after this.

On July 30 of that year, American pop star Britney Spears sang at his show, and on September of that year, he led the La pesera del amor[1] contest, where a man chose among a number of women to pick a future wife. Ramones promised the man and the contest winner that the show would pay for their wedding and he would broadcast it live. La pesera del amor was later kept as a regular feature and more contestants participate for the chance to meet someone.

In November of 2003, Sylvester Stallone became the second American superstar to participate in the show that year.

Other international stars in the show along the years are: Will Smith (twice), Arnold Schwarzenegger, Kevin James, Diego Maradona, Christina Aguilera, David Copperfield, Drew Barrymore, Cameron Díaz, Lucy Liu, Chris Rock, John Leguizamo, The Backstreet Boys and The Rock. Worthy of note, although Paris Hilton was scheduled to appear on the show, she did not show up, leaving him to wait live in front of millions of viewers.

Ramones announced in 2004 plans to move to Miami, Florida. In March of that year he suffered an accident, breaking an ankle and requiring crutches.

In 2005, he was also nominated for a Mexican MTV Movie Award, being Favorite Actor (for role in Puños rosas). He is also a host of the TV reality show Cantando por un sueño.

In December 2006 he will be participating in the Mexican production of the Mel Brooks musical The Producers in the role of Leo Bloom
