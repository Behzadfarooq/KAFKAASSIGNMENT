The adapids, members of the extinct family Adapidae, are prosimian primates that lived during the Eocene epoch. Adapids are believed to have been generally lemur-like, and are considered by many to be the probable ancestors of modern lemurs and lorises.

They lived during the same time period as Omomyid, another early primate precursor. They were likely a diurnal species who subsisted on a diet on fruits and leaves and maybe insects. Their snouts were elongated with almost parallel rows of teeth. They had large canines that showed marked sexual dimorphism. The tibia and fibula were unfused which could mean they were not a strong tree climbing species.[1]
