Adam Hepburn, 2nd Earl of Bothwell

Adam Hepburn, 2nd Earl of Bothwell (d. September 9, 1513) was a Scottish nobleman, who succeeded his father Patrick Hepburn, 1st Earl of Bothwell in 1508. Prior to that he was known by one of his territorial designations, Adam Hepburn of Crags, under which he drew up his Testament.

He married Agnes (d. February 1557), the illegitimate daughter of James Stewart, 1st Earl of Buchan by Margaret Murray. (Agnes Stewart subsequently had Letters of Legitimacy confirmed by Queen Mary of Guise in 1552). Adam Hepburn and Agnes had one child, Patrick.

The Earl of Bothwell was killed at the Battle of Flodden, where he commanded the Scottish reserve.
