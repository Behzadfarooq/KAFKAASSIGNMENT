Adam Goucher

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Image:Adam Goucher.jpg
Adam Goucher winning the 1999 Cross Country National Championships 4 Kilometer race

Adam Goucher (born February 18, 1975) is an American cross-country and track and field athlete. He primarily competes in middle distance events and is featured in Running With The Buffaloes, a book revolving around the 1998 season of the University of Colorado cross country team.

Adam attended Doherty High School in Colorado Springs, Colorado and graduated in 1994. He won the Footlocker Championship in 1993, as well as having personal bests of 1:53 at 800 m, 4:18 at 1600 m, and 9:35 at 3200 m.

While attending the University of Colorado, Adam won many NCAA indoor and outdoor titles as well as winning the NCAA Division I cross country title in 1998. It is during this season in which Running With The Buffaloes is written. He also competed in the United States Olympic Trials and placed 14th in the 3000 m during 1996.

After graduating, Adam continued to compete with a contract from Nike. During 2000, he made his first Olympic team, placing first in the Olympic Trials in the 5000 m. He was 13th in the 5000 m at the Sydney Olympic Games, despite serious battles with back troubles.

Adam's career has been plagued with injury. While a formidable runner, and having continually been ranked as one of the foremost American competitors at distances between 1500 m and 5000 m, he was prevented from performing optimally at the 2004 Olympic Trials by an Achilles heel problem and thus did not qualify for the Olympic team. The Olympic Trials were a culmination of a very rough period for Adam, and soon thereafter he left Mark Wetmore, his longtime coach and friend, to train with Alberto Salazar (who was famous as a runner and is now gaining a reputation as coach of junior star Galen Rupp) in Oregon.

Whether it was a change of scenery, or just a new approach to training, the move was an excellent one for Goucher. Adam's 2005 season has seen him improve on his 5000 m time to 13:10.19 and take 2nd place at the USA Cross Country 4 km Championships.

In 2006, Adam ran a 10:50 at the USA Cross Country Championships to win first place in the short course (4 km). He went on to finish 6th in the IAAF World Cross Country Championships in Fukuoka, Japan, the highest finish by an American in quite a few years. He finished only 8 seconds behind the winner, world-renowned Ethiopian champion Kenenisa Bekele, the 5k and 10k world record holder. At the 2006 Prefontaine Classic, Adam finished third in the 2 mile with a time of 8:12.7; the third fastest 2 mile ever run by an American.

Goucher's performance at the World Cross Country meet and his 2 mile at the Prefontaine Classic opened many distance running gurus eyes across America, and at the moment he is one of the country's foremost hopes for a high finish at the upcoming world championships and 2008 Olympic Games. He currently resides in Portland, Oregon, with his wife, Kara Grgas-Wheeler, an outstanding runner herself.

Goucher is currently preparing for the U. S. cross country championships in Boulder which take place in February.
