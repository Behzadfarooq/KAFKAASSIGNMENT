Ad Trucks, Why Ban Them; an example from Montreal


Out of all causes, why fight ad trucks? There are many other issues which deserve public attention and political action. Ad trucks cause atmospheric pollution in a time where global warming is a concern amongst a great deal of environmental scientists. If ad truck use increases the problem environmental problems will only get worse. Ad trucks also increase traffic during times when most people are out. Ad trucks companies would like to reach the biggest amount of people with their mobile ads, therefore they drive in heavily populated areas contributing to more traffic. These areas are in Ville Marie (aka. downtown) and the Plateau. They add to the already numerous amount of vehicles leading to faster deterioration of Montreal’s streets. They also add to the clutter of billboard advertisements saturated in our visual environment. Furthermore, they are in violation of municipal by-laws, cultural protection and infringe on the environmental promises outlined in the Charter of Rights and Responsibilities and Kyoto protocol. At the last Plateau and Ville Marie council meetings Uberculture Collective teamed up with the Optative Theatrical Laboratories bring public attention to the problems caused by ad trucks and request that borough councilors stop their circulation through proper regulation. The Optatif Theatrical Laboratories members represent Pub Partout a fictitious advertising company who want to brand everything. They were handing out flyers to everyone entering council about their company. They chanted various slogans like “ad trucks, ad trucks there our right. We want more and we will fight.” They also warned about Erik Chevrier who presented reports to both Ville Marie and Plateau council meetings about why the city should stop ad trucks. These reports demonstrate that ad trucks are problematic for the city, citizens and visitors of Montreal especially in downtown boroughs like the Plateau and Ville Marie. Through this document we provided possible suggestions of how to prevent ad trucks from occupying our streets. By submitting this document, we requested to participate with the Montreal administration in a joint effort to ensure that the environmental and sustainable development rights and responsibilities are met. This right is granted to citizens through the same Charter of Rights and Responsibilities as mentioned above. This request was submitted to council at their meetings on March 5th and 6th, 2007. Uberculture and the Optative Theatrical Laboratories will continue to attend every council meeting until our councilors take this issue seriously. Come participate, see theatrical performances or get involved. If you want ad trucks off our streets and help protect our air get involved with us. If you are from Montreal, please sign our petition. To find out more information about ad trucks, continue to (b)ad trucks.
