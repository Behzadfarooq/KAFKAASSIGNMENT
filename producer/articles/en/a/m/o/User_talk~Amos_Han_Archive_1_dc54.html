[edit] 1991 and newbie biting

Hi, with regards to the 1991 article and User:Jcsoccer91's edits, can I remind you of the importance of not biting newcomers to Wikipedia. We were all newbies once, and had to learn how things are done on Wikipedia; how a new user is treated can make the difference in weather or not they become a constructive contributor or not. Nothing scares off a potentially valuable contributor faster than hostility from established editors. Thanks, AJR | Talk 23:48, 10 January 2007 (UTC)

I see exactly the same thing on the date articles that I have watchlisted (Jcsoccer91's comments above could well be seen as "biting back" - while that doesn't excuse him/her from such behaviour, my personal attitude is to ignore it unless it becomes a serious issue. But that's just me, I don't ask others to do so.) When I encounter such behaviour from newbies, I tend to use one of the welcome messages along with the appropriate warning, to give more pointers as to what to avoid doing and to present a friendlier face. I'm aware that you're a fairly new user yourself and possibly still finding your feet on Wikipedia, so just consider this a friendly word of advice. And since no-one's said it yet, welcome to Wikipedia and thanks for all your contributions! :-) -- AJR | Talk 00:51, 11 January 2007 (UTC)
Of course we want to stop people from vandalising, but we also want to encourage them to contribute constructively; being friendly and assuming good faith (even if it is clearly misguided good faith) help with this. I knew that you are quite new because your contributions history (which is kept for every user) only goes back about a week. -- AJR | Talk 01:23, 11 January 2007 (UTC)
