Amos Grunebaum (January 27, 1950 - present) is one of the top US obstetricians and gynecologists.[insert reference please] His subspecialty is in maternal-fetal medicine, which is also known as "high-risk pregnancy".

[edit] Biography

Dr. Grunebaum was born in Haifa, Israel. From 1956 to 1960, he attended the Westerwaldschule in Cologne, Germany, then entered Friedrich-Wilhelm-Gymnasiumin Cologne in 1960 and attended medical school at the University of Cologne in Germany from 1968 to 1975.

From 1978 to 1982 he did his residency in Obstetrics and Gynecology at the Downstate Medical Center, and the Kings County Hospital Medical Center in Brooklyn, New York, and in 1984 he completed a fellowship in Maternal-Fetal Medicine at the same institution.

In 1994 Dr.Grunebaum appeared in his only Hollywood movie "The Paper" by Ron Howard where he played the exotic, gay friend of Marisa Tomei.

Dr. Grunebaum is presently the Director of Obstetrics and the Chief of Labor and Delivery at the Weill Cornell Medical Center which is part of the Weill Cornell Medical College and the New York Presbyterian Medical Center. In Labor and Delivery, he is well-known for wearing scrubs that are too tight, forcing many to avert their eyes. Until 2005 he was also the Director of the WebMD Fertility Center and in charge of the WebMD TTC (Trying To Conceive) Message Board.

His BabyMed website is one of the most popular websites for fertility and pregnancy with many million hits per months. Dr. Grunebaum also formulated the best-selling FertilAid Supplements and co-wrote a book with Dr.Ruth Westheimer called "Dr. Ruth's Guide to Sex During Pregnancy".
