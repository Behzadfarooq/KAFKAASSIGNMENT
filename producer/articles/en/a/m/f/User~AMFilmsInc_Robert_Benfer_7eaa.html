Robert "Knox" Leon Benfer (Born December 18, 1987) is an independent movie maker who created the extremely popular Klayworld internet series of short clay animation films.

[edit] Early Life

Robert Leon Benfer Jr. was born on December 18, 1987 in Hinesville, Georgia, USA in the Fort Stewart army base when his dad was serving the military. His Mother (Janeese Schoelzel) and his dad (Robert Benfer) then, had to move to Germany where his sister Nikki Benfer (b. March 2nd, 1988) was born. When Robert was two, his parents got divorced, so Robert, his sister, and his mother moved to Kerrville, Texas, where she met Robert's future stepfather, Curtis Schoelzel. In 1994, Janeese and Curtis got married, and all moved to Schertz, Texas, where Robert has been living ever since. Robert also has two step sisters, Jakki Schoelzel (b. November 1st, 1995) and Jill Schoelzel (b. September 15th, 2000). In 1995, he attended Schertz Elementary School where he met Sammie Penrod, his best friend to this day, in the first grade. At age six, Robert started getting really interested in film making. From age seven to twelve he used his parents' camcorder to make short videos with his sister, Nikki. At age 15, Robert finally began to post short 45-second clay animation videos on a very popular website called Newgrounds, such as Yellow Seed, Bar Fight, Hulky, Joy Ride, Dr. Bob, Tree Hand Thing and many other short movies.

Though the whole "blue blob with arms and legs" look was only for practice, after a while it became Robert's signature style before he could try better looking characters and was the birth of the popular "Klayworld| Klay World Series", a series based on the blue clay people.

These small animations were the first of many Robert Benfer Clay animations. At this time, there were very few clay animations on Newgrounds.com, as Newgrounds is mostly for flash animations, not live-action or clay animations. Because of this, Robert was able to open a whole new door for future stop motion creators all over the web, such as Dan MacKenzie (Mack Productions) and Tyler Vandenberg (Freemont).

He posted 45 short films on the Internet in 2003, and won 35 awards for them on Newgrounds.

In 2004, Benfer created some of his most well known Internet clay animation films such as Glass of water, Sniper Time!, and Help, Im Stuck. His first step at turning his film making into a career was releasing a DVD in titled "Knox in a box", which contained all of his short clay animations. At the end of 2004, after the success of his first DVD, Robert decided to take the next step by beginning production of his first ever full feature clay animation film, "Klay World: off the table", a movie based on his Klay World series.

Robert has earned over $125,000 from the movie, which has marked the beginning of his film career.

Now Robert, at age 19 is working on much bigger projects, as he steers away from the Internet film world, and into the world of feature film.[1]

[edit] KnoxKast Radio

Knox Kast Radio is a podcast hosted by Jason Steele, a film maker from Florida, and Robert. The show, which started in summer of 2006, has been a Podcast hit due to its "combined sense of humor" and has appeared as a "Featured Podcast" on the iTMS Comedy Podcast Directory. This podcast is surprisingly successful with about 10,000 downloads on iTunes per episode[2], and the show is entering its ninth month of "syndication".
