[edit] Stubs

For a generic stub about computer and video games, use {{cvg-stub}}. This will put the article into the Computer and video game stubs category.

For a more specific stub by genre, use:

  • {{action-cvg-stub}} (action or exercise computer/video game)
  • {{adventure-cvg-stub}} (adventure or roleplaying computer/video game)
  • {{anime-cvg-stub}}} (games based on anime and/or manga properties)
  • {{shooter-cvg-stub}} (2D or 3D shooter computer/video game)
  • {{simulation-cvg-stub}} (simulation computer/video game)
  • {{sports-cvg-stub}} (sports or racing computer/video game)
  • {{strategy-cvg-stub}} (strategy or puzzle computer/video game)
  • {{educational-cvg-stub}} (educational game)
  • {{fighting-cvg-stub}} (fighting game)

Or for stubs by company, use:

  • {{Capcom-stub}}
  • {{Konami-stub}}
  • {{Namco-stub}}
  • {{Nintendo-stub}}
  • {{Sega-stub}}

Other stub types include:

  • {{cvg-company-stub}} (computer/video game corporation or company)
  • {{cvg-culture-stub}} (computer/video game culture)
  • {{cvg-bio-stub}} (computer/video game designer or specialist)
  • {{cvg-char-stub}} (computer/video game character)
  • {{cvg-fict-stub}} (computer/video game fictional element)
  • {{cvg-web-stub}} (computer/video game website)
  • {{cvg-hardware-stub}} (computer/video game hardware element, such as an arcade board or a gamepad)
  • {{cvg-software-stub}} (computer/video game software element, such as a 3d engine or an gameplay editor)
