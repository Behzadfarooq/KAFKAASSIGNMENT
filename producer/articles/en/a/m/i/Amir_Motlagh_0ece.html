Amir Motlagh is an American independent filmmaker and actor whose titles have garnered many awards in the film festival circuit. Titles include Still Lover, Dino Adino, the mini feature documentary Pumkin Little, and Love @ 11:47. He also worked with the daughter of the late Filipino fine artist Santiago Bose, Lilledeshan Bose on the film, my break ups into a million pieces in which he directed. He is set to release a new narrative feature film titled whale in late 2006 and is keeping a blog on its development. He also has a musical side project called Shanks and the Dreamers and did guest guitar work on Unfamiliar Occurrence's album Anesthesia and has been featured in several art shows with his ongoing art series, Mothers, Lovers and F****s. Other projects include 2005's six-part video art series titled Lessons in Self-Destruction, and the most recent short film tracing the edges.
