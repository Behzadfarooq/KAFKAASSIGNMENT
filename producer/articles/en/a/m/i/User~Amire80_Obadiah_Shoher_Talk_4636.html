[edit] Crap

Third Aliyah is 1919-1923. USSR was founded in 1922. That is a bit on the edge.

Anyway, if he came to Israel in the 3rd Aliyah, that would make him over 80 years old.

I couldn't find any party in the official list of registered parties in Israel which is lead by an 80 years old born in USSR or any related territories.

Avigdor Lieberman holds views which are a bit similar to Shoher's, but he was born in 1958.

Avraham Shmulevich is another extreme right "politician" who was born in USSR, but his "party" Bead Artseinu is not in the list of registered parties. On the picture in his blog he doesn't look like he's 80 years old.

Natan Sharansky is not such an extremist and he was born in 1948.

In fact, the Israeli politician who comes anywhere near the description "born in the USSR, emigrated to Israel during the Third Aliyah and leads a political party" is ... Shimon Peres!

So the data in the article is obviously wrong.

Plus, this Shoher guy is a bit of a spammer. He sends requests to download and review his book to any Israeli he can find on Amazon and MySpace, and probably more sites.

The only reason i don't yet propose it for deletion is that this character may be somewhat curious as a case study in Amazon.com's censorship. --Amir E. Aharoni 06:54, 14 November 2006 (UTC)
