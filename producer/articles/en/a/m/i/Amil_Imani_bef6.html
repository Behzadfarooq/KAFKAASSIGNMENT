Amil Imani (born in Iran) is a Radical American commentator who advocates the overthrow of the Islamic Republic of Iran. He has published in the Baltimore Chronicle and elsewhere. He is known to be an extreme opponent of Islam and Islamism.

[edit] Views

Imani has asserted that the Islamic clergy of Iran are not Iranian[1] and are barbaric. He is a critic of Islamic democracy and deems it un-workable. He has also lashed out against Shirin Ebadi[2] by stating,

“ [Shirin] Ebadi, speaking in Paris, denied that Islam needs reformation or modernization. Instead, she said, the West needs to understand Islam. We quickly inform Ms. Ebadi that not only does the West understand Islam, but so do the Iranian people who are being held hostage in their own country. We reject Islam as a form of government![2] ”

His method is questionable, he is described by some as a demagogue, due to his subjective and ill-supported arguments.
