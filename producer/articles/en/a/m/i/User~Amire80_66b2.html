[edit] Sources and references

After over a year in Wikipedia i grew up and understood how important sources are. So, when i've got time and means, i try to add some myself. Mostly i tag dubious statements with {{fact}}, which you may know as [citation needed]. In many cases responsible editors responded to my requests and added good sources. In some other cases it didn't happen, but at least readers will know not to trust every edit. It's one of the most important things to do for Wikipedia's integrity.

[edit] Spelling

I love languages, dictionaries and encyclopediae of all kinds since i was about five years old. I know English grammar pretty well, but in my personal writing (not in the articles) i spell the word "i" intentionally wrong, because i believe in equality and i don't think that i deserve a capital letter any more than you do.

[edit] Belarus

  1. I am not Belarusian. I was born in Moscow and i live in Israel. My mother tongue is Russian.
  2. My preferred spelling for the name of the country that lies between Lithuania, Russia, Ukraine and Poland is Belarus. Not Belorussia, not Byelorussia, and not White Russia. My preference for the spelling of the name of its national language is Belarusian.
  3. The spelling Belarus and Belarusian was promoted by all Belarusian governments since 1991.
  4. For rationale see Pravapis and Etymology of Rus and derivatives.
  5. When i see one of the spellings that i consider wrong, i usually change them, unless i have a good reason not to, for example if it's a direct quote.
