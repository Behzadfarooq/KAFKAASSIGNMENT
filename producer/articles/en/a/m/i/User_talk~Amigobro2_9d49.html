[edit] References

(reply to: message)

No problem, I'm still getting the hang of it too. Seems there's a few ways to do it, such as the <ref> tag. Basically, you can write something like

I like tacos <ref>Tacos are a food</ref> and then make sure the article has a <references /> tag somewhere on the article, if it's not already there. Where ever <references /> is, that's where all the notes show up. For example:

I like tacos. <ref>Tacos are a food</ref>
==References===
<references />

Would look like:


I like tacos. [1]

[edit] References

  1. ^ Tacos are a food

Another way, when the reference is just a web site, is you can just make a non-titled link that shows up as a [1] kind of link. Like:

I like tacos. [http://www.tacos.com]

Would look like:

I like tacos [1]

Meta:Cite/Cite.php and Wikipedia:Citing sources/example style go into even more details. Hope that helps! -- Ned Scott 06:17, 22 June 2006 (UTC)

For formatting of references, you can always have a look at thetemplates in category:Citation templates. Circeus 13:12, 22 June 2006 (UTC)
