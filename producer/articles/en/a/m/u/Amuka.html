Amuka is the stage name used by singer/songwriter Sheila Brody. Originally from Detroit, she was discovered by funk musician George Clinton. Although she has performed as a rock and funk singer, Brody has earned the most success as a dance music vocalist. In 1999 (as Blackwood) she released a best-selling album (Friday Night) and single ("Peace") in Italy before returning to the United States. Teaming with producer/remixer Peter Rauhofer she has released several singles that have charted well on the U.S. Hot Dance Music/Club Play chart. Brody's 2003 song "Appreciate Me" appeared on the television soundtrack Queer as Folk: Club Babylon. In 2006 Brody earned her first number-one U.S. dance hit with the song "I Want More (Cling on to Me)".

[edit] Chart singles

  • 2003 "Appreciate Me" (as "Amuka featuring Sheila Brody") #7 U.S. dance
  • 2005 "U Ain't That Good" (as Sheila Brody) #3 U.S. dance
  • 2005 "Craving" (as Amuka) #2 U.S. dance
  • 2006 "I Want More (Cling on to Me)" (as Amuka) #1 U.S. dance
