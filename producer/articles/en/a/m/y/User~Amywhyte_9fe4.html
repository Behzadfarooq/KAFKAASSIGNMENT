[edit] Carole Nash Insurance

Carole Nash Insurance is an insurance intermediary based in Altrincham, England. They have been trading since 1985 and were founded by Carole Nash. They provide a range of insurance products in the UK and Ireland including motorcycle insurance, car insurance and household insurance. Carole Nash Insurance is owned by Groupama UK, the French insurance underwriter.


History
Carole Nash Insurance was founded in 1985 after Carole Nash [1] was made redundant from her job at an insurance company. She founded the company using her redundancy cheque and set up an office in her garage in Timperley, Manchester. Carole Nash insurance now insures around 1 in 4 bikes in the UK.

In 1998, Carole Nash launched Classic and Private Car Insurance. This was followed in 1999 by the opening of the Dublin office by Ronan Keating.

Alarmed at rising theft rates and the knock-on effects on bikers’ premiums CN Secure is launched fitting security products to customers’ bikes to reduce the theft risk.

In 2002 the Service and Repair centre was opened close to the companies Altrincham HQ. This was a move designed to reduce customers’ premiums by reducing repair costs. 2002 saw the acquisition of the AA’s UK motorcycle business. This increased the number of policies to beyond 240,000.

Carole Nash launched a combined insurance policy in November 2004 at the NEC Bike Show. This allowed customers to insure their bikes, house and cars on one policy. In December 2006 the company was acquired by Groupama UK with Carole Nash staying on in an ambassadorial role.


Other
Guide to bike insurance terms:

Third Party Only (TPO): This is the minimum level of motorcycle insurance cover required by law if you want to ride your bike on the road so is usually the cheapest cover. It provides third party liability protection for injury or damage you may cause to others.

Third Party Fire and Theft (TPFT): As TPO but includes fire and theft cover for your bike.

Comprehensive (COMP): As TPFT but includes accidental damage to your motorbike.

Accidental Damage, Fire and Theft (ADFT): This level of bike insurance cover is available for classic motorcycles and off road vehicles. It covers you if the motorcycle is damaged accidentally, by fire, or if the bike is stolen. This cover excludes road use and is not available in Ireland.
