Sayed Ammar Nakshawani is a leading Muslim lecturer, born in 1981.

[edit] Biography

Sayed Ammar Nakshawani is regarded by many as being amongst the top Muslim lecturers in the world. The grandson of the late Sayed Mortadha Nakshawani, representative and secretary of Sayed al-Khoei in Najaf, Ammar was born in 1981 and migrated to England in 1987. Sayed Ammar’s lineage goes back to the Prophet of Islam, Mohammad through his grandson Imam Musa al-Kadhim (peace be upon them).He was awarded with a degree in psychology and law from University College London. Having completed a one year course in `Political Change in Modern Britain’ at LSE, Sayed Ammar completed his MA in 2005. Sayed Ammar has given lectures throughout the world and began lecturing at the age of 19. As well as having lectured around the United Kingdom, including London, Glasgow, Manchester and Birmingham to name but a few, he is renowned for his lectures worldwide. Within the past 3 years, he has spoken in Australia, Canada, the USA, throughout Europe, Dubai and across Africa in the holy months of Muharram and Ramadhan.

Sayed Ammar has studied under some of the most renowned scholars and academics, including Sayed Fadhil Milani, Sheikh Mohamad Saeed Bahmanpour , Prof Ezzati as well as Dr.Jasim Hussain.

In his MA, Sayed Ammar undertook the study of subjects such as Islamic Law, Quranic Studies, Islamic Philosophy, Principles of Islamic law as well as legal systems in Muslim Countries. The Sayed’s thesis was an analysis of Bewley's book on Muawiya. He was awarded with an MA from the Islamic College for Advanced studies, London.

He is currently studying and teaching for his Phd at Exeter University in Islamic studies.
