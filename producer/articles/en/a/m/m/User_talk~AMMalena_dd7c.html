Given that you claim to be the founder of Orbiter Online, it is probably conflict of interest and may not be neutral. In addition, "advertising" does not mean for-profit, in this context it means any sort of promotion. It may be "accurate", but we don't know because there are no third-party reliable sources to corroborate any of this information. [edit] Response

I'd surely like to understand what site Alexa has to do with this. I'm quite certain there are large quantities of entries in Wikipedia that do not have anything even POSTED to Alexa, much less that Alexa is any kind of driving force behind Wikipedia. As the entry I have posted states, Orbiter's [web traffic] is very minor at this point (several hundred hits per month) since the online service (BBS and web-based games) are all but non-existant. The sites it HOSTS are quite alive (malena.net, creeksideband.org, lakesidejazz.org and many more). And only two of the sites it hosts are PAY sites (where money is exchanged). The rest are community, school and non-profit sites.

I'd just like some guidance on what will make the article acceptable, that's all. I find it bizarre that such a fuss is happening over a system that has survived over 12 years and hosted so many events, online marriage, multiplayer competitions well before the "internet" anywhere near as popular as it is now... Orbiter is a landmark in the Central Florida area, so I take this as being MY FAILURE to create an adequate article, so I'm "begging" for more guidance. Thanks.
