Ammonihah is a city mentioned in the Book of Mormon. According to the book, the city was founded by an otherwise unknown man named Ammonihah (Alma 8:7).

After Alma the Younger had visited several cities, setting the church in order and preaching, he went to Ammonihah to do the same. He was rejected by the people, probably because the religion of Nehor had become very popular. On a trip away from the city, he saw an angel that instructed him to return to the city and preach that the city would be destroyed. He met Amulek on the way back, where he stayed for some time. Amulek joined him in his efforts to preach.

Engaging in a verbal confrontation with Zeezrom, Amulek was able to read his thoughts and confound him. In that state, Alma stepped forward and began preaching against Nehor's teachings. He was able to convince a large number of people in the city and they joined with Alma. Displeased, the leaders, lawyers, and judges brought Alma and Amulek before the Chief Judge, accusing them of denouncing their laws and claiming that there was only one God and the God would send his Son but his Son would not redeem everyone. Although Zeezrom, a highly reputed lawyer, stood to defend Alma and Amulek, the chief judge ordered that the believers be burned with their books. Forced to watch, Alma and Amulek were threatened with a similar fate. Imprisoned, persecuted, mocked, beaten, and starved, the lawyers, teachers, and judges challenged Alma from within the prison to show the power of God. After Alma called on God, they were freed from their bondage and the prison tumbled down on the teachers, lawyers, and judges, killing them, leaving Alma and Amulek unharmed. When the city people rushed to the prison to see what happened, and saw Alma and Amulek, they ran away in fear. After Alma and Amulek left the city, it was destroyed by an invasion of the Lamanites.
