[edit] PROPOSED ENTRY FOR ORBITER ONLINE

[edit] History

Orbiter Online Internet and Entertainment System, located in Daytona Beach (later Port Orange), Florida) was conceived and created by Anthony Malena (originally having been called Starship Sirius) as a multiline bulletin board system (BBS), initially serving the Volusia County area. It opened in October 1994 in Anthony's apartment with seven dial up lines and a 28.8k internet connection allowing users to access TELNET, RLOGIN and FTP internet servers, and a few months later, WWW access. Users were also given free webspace to host their own internet "presence" (later managed via the BBS using WebBlaster Pro).

The system originally operated under MajorBBS v6.25 and Galacticomm. It later expanded and used Vircom's TCP/IP stack and DMA server for greater capacity and functionality.

Financial backing for Starship Sirius' startup came solely from Anthony Malena (aka "Starfleet") and Joan Gonsalves (silent partner, aka "Coyote Woman"), while ongoing operations and maintenance were financed by user donations in exchange for varying access privileges.

In 1995, the number of dialup lines was expanded to 12 thanks to quick growth and local appreciation for the unique interface and wide variety of multiplayer games and worldwide linked-chat options. In 1996, the dialup internet connection was replaced with dedicated 128k ISDN connectivity and the number of dialup lines expanded to 20.

Starship Sirius began it's life with a "Star Trek" motif, where functions within the system were labeled with Trek-like titles. For example, internet connectivity (such as TELNET and FTP) was referred to as Transporters, games were referred to as Holodecks, and Teleconference was called Ten Forward. The system even had an assigned ship's registry number of NX-40911 (experimental Stealth class). At that time, the system operated under the subdomain sirius.america.com. Starship's original provider (PSS Internet) eventually went out of business (due reportedly to non-payment of T1 lines to BellSouth at the time) forcing a change in providers. The change ended up helping Starship (by then renamed to Orbiter and using the domain orbiter.com) by cutting internet costs in half.

The change in name was a decision to make Orbiter a more "professional" looking and sounding system to help attract more users. Almost all references to Trek-motif were removed from the system, and the change prefaced even more growth. Multiple servers (separate game servers primarily) were added to help fuel continued growth.

At the end of 2000, the Malena family moved to Port Orange and due to poor planning combined with family problems at the time, the system was reduced to two dialup lines and 56k internet connectivity for several months. Seven months later, they moved to their permanent residence (still in Port Orange), but Orbiter never fully returned to the level of service it had offered. For a brief time, incoming TELNET connections were allowed so users could access the system, but there was little demand since internet connectivity was already getting very cheap from regular providers around the local community.

Orbiter experienced a diverse history of family-based entertainment, including the first online marriage ceremony in Central Florida (possibly Florida, not verified yet) of users "Big Dog" and "Darlasha" with friends and family from around the United States. User gatherings happened throughout it's history, both official and unofficial (held at public parks, as well as local nighttime restaurants such as Denny's). "Virtual LAN" parties were also held via the game networking available on Orbiter, allowing players to enjoy games with each other from their own homes such as Doom, Duke Nukem, Warcraft and many more.

[edit] Access

Orbiter Online offered various access levels for those who donated money to help maintain the system. Access levels gave users access to games, internet and more.

Before the change in name, Starship Sirius used access levels named after Star Trek designations, separated by basic access types, such as Enlisted (Private, etc) for game-only access, Officers (Ensign, etc) for internet access, and Admiral and Borg for Lifetime access.


[edit] Advertising

Orbiter worked out different advertising deals with local businesses over the years. At one point, AMC Theater (located in Daytona Beach) listed their weekly movie listings on Orbiter's logoff page. Orbiter also offered the main menu pages of various games and other system modules for low fees, as well as lifetime ads for the "cost of the module", though this only met with very minor success.


[edit] Rules and Regulations

Orbiter is a privately-owned, publicly-accessible service, advertised to users that they were literally "visiting the living room" of the owner/operator, and therefore were expected to have the same respect for each other that would be assumed visiting someone's home. Since the majority of users were high school and and below, this became a big attraction to many users who knew that juvenille behavior would not be tolerated.


[edit] Future

As of 2007, Orbiter Online still operated as a web hosting service, providing a home for many web domains, and giving free hosting and webmaster services to local school and non-profit groups including the Lakeside Jazz Festival, Spruce Creek High School Band, Creekside Middle School Band and others.
