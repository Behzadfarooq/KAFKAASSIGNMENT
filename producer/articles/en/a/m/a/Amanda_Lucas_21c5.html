Amanda Lucas (born 1981) is the older daughter of acclaimed movie director and producer George Lucas.

[edit] Biography

Amanda was born while George Lucas was married to Marcia Lucas. They were divorced in 1983. Then he adopted his first daughter as a single parent.

Amanda and her younger sister Katie (born in 1988) both appeared in Star Wars Episode I: The Phantom Menace (1999) in credited roles. Amanda was the voice of Tey How, the alien controller in the droid control ship. She was credited as Tyger. She can also be seen in Jabba the Hutt's box during the Podrace scene as Diva Funquita.

Amanda would appear in the other two prequels, as did her sister Katie and her younger brother Jett. None of them was, however, credited for Star Wars Episode II: Attack of the Clones (2002). In this movie, Amanda makes her cameo as Adnama, one of the patrons in the Outlander Club, in the scene where Anakin Skywalker chases Zam Wessell.

Amanda was credited again, this time as Amanda Lucas, for Star Wars Episode III: Revenge of the Sith (2005). Her role was given the name of Terr Taneel, although she does not have any lines. She is one of the Human senators next to Bail Organa and Mon Mothma.
