Amazonomachy

An Amazonomachy (Greek - Amazon battle; plural, Amazonomachies or Amazonomachoi) was a portrayal of legendary battle between Greeks and Amazons. These foreign creatures succumbed in myth to the likes of Herakles and Theseus, and symbolised the triumph of Greek or Macedonian civilization over the barbarian, but they were objects of sympathy and fascination.

[edit] Examples

  • Pheidias represented an Amazonomachy on the shield of the Athena Parthenos, the monumental cult statue of the Parthenon.
  • Another representation was painted by Micon, on the Stoa Poikile at the Ancient Agora of Athens.
