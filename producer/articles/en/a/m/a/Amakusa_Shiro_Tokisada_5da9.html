Amakusa Shiro Tokisada (天草 四郎 時貞, Amakusa Shiro Tokisada?) is a video game character from SNK Playmore's Samurai Shodown series of fighting games. His westernized name is Shiro Tokisada Amakusa.

[edit] History

According to his official biography, Amakusa was born in Nagasaki between 1621 and 1623, and died February 28, 1638. He stands 1.81 metres; his weight and blood type are unknown. His weapon is a crystal sphere named "Gadamar". His distinguishing features are his wild red hair and huge multicoloured robe.

Amakusa led a Christian rebellion in the Shimabara region. The Bakufu army crushed his revolt and executed him. He made a deal with the demon Ambrosia, and returned to the mortal world 150 years later, possessing the body of Shinzo Hattori, the son of Hanzo Hattori. In his attempt to bring Ambrosia to the mortal world with the power of the Palenke Stone, Haohmaru and Nakoruru killed him.

This time, Amakusa was split into two forms: good and evil. The evil, blue-skinned Amakusa (better known as Aku Amakusa) resurrected Zankuro Minazuki, but could not control him, so he sealed him in stone. He then kidnapped Hazuki Kazama and used her to maintain his demon castle in Shimabara. The good Amakusa planned to stop his evil counterpart, but Kazuki Kazama killed the evil incarnation, and Mizuki Rashojin sealed him.

When Amakusa freed himself, he fought Mizuki, and saved the souls of Haohmaru, Jubei Yagyu, Kyoshiro Senryo, and Neinhalt Sieger before Mizuki destroyed him.

[edit] Trivia

Aku Amakusa
  • Amakusa is a recurring boss of the Samurai Shodown series, appearing in almost all of the games in the series, and has been playable since the third game.
  • Amakusa is based on the historical figure Amakusa Shiro. Also, he is based on a character from Makai Tensho, who was in turn based on the historical figure of the same name.
  • Because of his androgynous appearance, Amakusa is often mistaken for a woman (to the point that the Samurai Shodown TV anime film actually depicts him as a woman), although these assumptions were stronger by the time of the first game.
