The Amarillo Civic Center is a multi-purpose convention center in Amarillo, Texas. Built in 1964, it consists of multiple facilities including:

  • A 2,848-seat auditorium with 2,324 permanent seats and used for concerts, Broadway shows and other events.
  • The Cal Farley Coliseum, a 4,987-seat multi-purpose arena serving as home to the Amarillo Gorillas Ice hockey team of the Central Hockey League, and the Amarillo Dusters of the af2 (an affiliate league of the Arena Football League). The arena, which has 4,879 permanent seats, is also used for concerts, banquets, conventions, ice shows, wrestling and trade shows (the arena features 17,100 square feet of floor space). The arena measures 38' 10 from floor to rafters, 50' 10" from floor to ceiling.
  • A 16,000-square-foot Grand Plaza, designed as a tribute to Texas and seating up to 1,100 for smaller concerts, banquets, and other special events. The Grand Plaza contains a Texas-accented floor, skylight ceiling, and fountains and planters.
  • Two exhibit halls, the North which has 24,565 square feet of edxhibit space and used for trade shows, conventions, meetings and banquets (capacity is up to 2,200) and with a 25-foot ceiling height; and a 26,000 square-foot, 14-foot South Exhibit Hall, also used for similar events.
  • Three meeting rooms--the Heritage, Hospitality and Regency rooms.

There is a memorial statue of the late space shuttle commander Rick Husband, one of the city's most famous sons, in front of the building.
