"Amapola" is a popular song, written by Joseph LaCalle with English language lyrics by Albert Gamse.

Though the song was published in 1924, the most popular recorded version was made nearly two decades later by the Jimmy Dorsey Orchestra with vocalists Helen O'Connell and Bob Eberly. The recording was released by Decca Records as catalog number 3629. The record first reached the Billboard magazine charts on March 14 of 1941 and lasted 14 weeks on the chart, peaking at #1.
