Amay Khurasiya pronunciation (helpÂ·info) (born May 18, 1972 in Madhya Pradesh) is an Indian cricketer. He is a left-handed batsman and a slow left-arm bowler.

His career began in a promising fashion, having scored a half century against Sri Lanka in the Pepsi Cup in 1999. He was included in the Indian World Cup squad in the same year, but did not play a single match.

His first class career stretches back to 1989/90, and includes three consecutive seasons (1990-91 to 1993-94) where he scored 500 runs in a season. Though his career chances were boosted in 2001 by an injury to Sachin Tendulkar, he did not perform quite as well, as his style of play means that on several occasions, he failed to guard his offstump.
