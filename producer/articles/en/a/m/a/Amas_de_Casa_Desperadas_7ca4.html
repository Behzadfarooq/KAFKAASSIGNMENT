Amas de Casa Desesperadas is the name of the Argentinean and Colombian-Ecuadorian adaptation of the American television series Desperate Housewives, created by Marc Cherry.

Set in the fictitious of Manzanares, the series follows the life of four housewives and their domestic problems and many daily mysteries that surround their husbands, friends and neighbours, which don’t appear to be all that bad. The theme of the series combines elements of drama, comedy, mystery, farce, soap opera and satire.

Buena Vista International, a subsidiary of the Walt Disney Company, which is also the owner of ABC (the channel on which Desperate Housewives is aired in the United States), searched throughout Brazil, Ecuador, Argentina and Colombia in order to find a producer who’d be able to adapt the series for the Spanish speaking market. In Argentina, it found Adrian Suar and Fernando Blanco’s Pol-Ka Producciones.

The first Latin American version of the Desperate Housewives phenomenon was the Argentinean version, whose transmission commenced in August 2006. It has been confirmed that the next series to be filmed will be the Colombian version, followed by the Brazilian version, and lastly an Ecuadorian version. It is rumoured too that there are negotiations currently in place to create adaptations for Chile, Mexico and Venezuela.
