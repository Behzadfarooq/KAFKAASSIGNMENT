Amal Saad-Ghorayeb

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Amal Saad-Ghorayeb is a Lebanese writer and a visiting scholar at the Carnegie Endowment Middle East Center known for her writings on the Israeli-Lebanese conflict and Hezbollah. Previously, she worked as an assistant professor of political science at the Lebanese American University. She received her Ph.D. from the University of Birmingham, England.[1] Her father, Abdo Saad, is a prominent Shiite pollster; her mother is Christian.
