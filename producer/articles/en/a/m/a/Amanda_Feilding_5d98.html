Amanda Feilding

Amanda Feilding, Lady Neidpath (born 30 January 1943) is a British art dealer and painter. She gained notoriety in the early 1970's when she performed a trepanation on herself, while her partner Joey Mellen filmed the operation.

Fielding is the youngest child of Basil Fielding (himself a great-grandson of the 7th Earl of Denbigh) and his wife, Margaret. In 1968, she married Joseph Mellen and they had two sons, Rock Basil Hugo Fielding-Mellen (b. 1979) and Cosmo Birdie Fielding-Mellen (b. 1985). She and Mellen divorced in 1993 and on 29 January 1995, she married James Charteris, Lord Neidpath, heir to the earldom of Wemyss.

Feilding and other advocates believe that trepanation allows greater blood flow to the brain by relieving cranial pressure, thus returning the brain to its original state of oxygenation at birth, prior to fusion of the cranial bones.

Feilding ran for British Parliament twice, on the platform that trepanation is beneficial, should be made available for all British Citizens, and should be covered under the National Health Plan.

Feilding is the founder of the Trepanation Trust and the The Foundation to Further Consciousness.
