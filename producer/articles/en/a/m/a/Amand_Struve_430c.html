Amand Struve (Ukrainian: Аманд Єгорович Струве, Russian: Аманд Егорович Струве) (30 May 1835 - 12 September 1898) was an engineer who worked in Kiev, Russian Empire (now Ukraine). He is not known much outside of Kiev, and mostly to specialists.

He is now famed for constructing the: Struve Railroad Bridge, central sewarage system, horse-pulled railway in Kiev, the Kiev tram, the first electric tramway in the Russian Empire, and a gas-powered street lighting system.

He was born Amand Struve on the 30th of May, 1835 into a Baltic German family. His relatives, which consisted of diplomats, engineeres, government officials, military personnel, and others, moved into Russia in the XVII Century. When he finished the main artillery school, he entered the army service. But the army career didn't satisfy him and he went off to study at the Nikolayivsky Engineer Academy.
