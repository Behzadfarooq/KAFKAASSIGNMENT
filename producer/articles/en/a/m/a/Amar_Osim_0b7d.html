Amar Osim (born in Sarajevo, SFR Yugoslavia) is a former Bosnian football player and now the manager of Japanese club JEF United Ichihara Chiba. He took over in July 2006 from his father, Ivica Osim.

[edit] Playing career

Born and raised in Sarajevo, Amar Osim started playing football in FK Željezničar. After playing for youth team, he have entered the senior squad in 1986. In 1991, he left to France where he played for some low division clubs. In 1996, he have returned to his home country. He played one more season for his favourite FK Željezničar before he retired from professional playing career.

He have never managed to reach the playing glory of his father. He was a talented player, with good technical skills, but the pressure of his father's name was maybe too much to deal with.

[edit] Managing career

And although he have never managed to reach the level of play his father was showing, he is definitely the manager of the same qualities. His coaching career started in FK Željezničar youth squad which he guided to success in domestic competitions. After Hajrudin Đurbuzović was sacked in spring of 2001, club officials promoted him to a place of the first team manager. He immediately led the club to two championship titles in 2001, in 2002, and two national cup titles in 2001, and 2003. He was sacked at the beginning of 2003/2004 season, allegedly because of the poor results.

His father invited him to Japan to be his assistant at JEF United. After Ivica Osim was named the head coach of the Japanese national team, Amar have been promoted.
