Qazi Abdul Jaleel (Urdu: قاضی عبد الجلیل) (born 1936 in Rohri), also known as Amar Jaleel (Urdu: امر جلیل) is a Pakistani Sindhi language writer and a columnist of the Dawn.

[edit] Early Life

He started writing stories when he was 10 years old. He played for his NJV School and also featured briefly in first class cricket as wicketkeeper-batsman. He is still big fan of cricket and football (soccer) and according to him his day starts by reading sports section of newspapers.

[edit] Education

[edit] Professional Life

He started his career from Radio Pakistan, Karachi and then he was transferred to Islamabad where he served at different positions in Radio and Educational institutions and finally retired as its Vice Chancellor. Nowadays he is living a retired life in Karachi, Sindh. He spends his leisure time in writing articles for various leading newspapers in Pakistan and is known as a popular columnist for Daily Dawn and The Nation.

[edit] Thinking

As a political analyst he has harped on one theme: why partition was wrong. Unsourced and usually without any primary source backing, Amar Jaleel is the foremost polemicist against the All India Muslim League and its efforts to make Pakistan. While grudgingly admitting the paradox that Jinnah, who Jaleel praises as a leader of great calibre, presented, Amar Jaleel nonetheless has continued his invective against the creation of Pakistan and its antecedents. Most of his articles however do not stand the test of facts and sources, as most declassified documents pooh pooh his vision of history thoroughly, especially those works like H M Seervai's "Partition of India: Legend and Reality" which have questioned the Indian Nationalist mythology to which Amar Jaleel subscribes.

Nonetheless, his articles find publication in Pakistan's leading newspapers especially those like the Dawn, which has historical foundations in the Pakistan Movement itself.
