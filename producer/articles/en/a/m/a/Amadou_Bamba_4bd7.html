Ahmadou Bamba (1850-1927) (Aamadu Bàmba Mbàkke in Wolof, Shaykh Aḥmad ibn Muḥammad ibn Ḥabīb Allāh in Arabic, also known as Khadīmu 'l-Rasūl or "The Servant of the Prophet" in Arabic, and as Sëriñ Tuubaa or "Holy Man of Tuubaa" in Wolof), muslim Sufi religious leader in Senegal, founder of the large Mouride Brotherhood (the Muridiyya). See Muslim brotherhoods of Senegal. He was born in the village of Mbacké (Mbàkke Bawol in Wolof) in the Kingdom of Baol, the son of a marabout from the Xaadir (Qadriyya) brotherhood (the oldest in Senegal).Cheikh Ahmadou Bamba was a true believer who produced a prodigious quantity of poems and tracts on meditation, rituals, work, and Qur'anic study, and taught his disciple to worship the lord. Although he did not support the French conquest, he did not wage outright war on them as several prominent Tijaan marabouts had done.

[edit] The mission of rehabilitation of Islam

Cheikh Ahmadou Bamba is considered one of the greatest spiritual leaders in Senegal. He founded Mouridism. Mouridism advocates an aspiration to Allah the Almighty in accordance with the Muhammad's message. In a struggle against the colonizer he did use peaceful way to restore the value of an Islam no longer practiced in a good way due to the oppressor influence. In other words, in a period where resistance was made by weapons, Cheikh Ahmadou Bamba revolutionized the struggle by using his spiritual strength against the oppressor. The Koran and the Hadith were his weapons. His mission reveals two aspects: the aspiration to the rank of Servant privilege of the Prophet but also the rehabilitation of Islam.

[edit] Historical context of his mission

Cheikh Ahmadou Bamba appeared in the second half of the 19th Century. This period was characterized by the end of the Slave trade and the beginning of Colonization. Indeed, due to the fact that France needed natural resources as well as human resources respectively for their industries and plantations, the colonizers started a territorial conquest in Senegal. Consequently, Senegal military dominated had to submit. Furthermore willing to assimilate the colony to the western cultural values, the colonial administration attempted to establish their religion: Christianity. At this point started a process of evangelization. In other words, they (colonial administration) tried to convert the whole population to Christianity. Thus in this process they created many schools and churches. [1]

[edit] The colonization and the destruction of Islam

Besides their attempt to convert the whole colony, Colonial administration knew their success will go through the capitulation of religious guides and local authorities. Thus they were offering awards for those who cooperated and a threat was made to reticent people.[1]

[edit] Weakness of the colony

In the colonial period, most of the local authorities were said to be interested by Honor they received from the Colonial Administration. Facing the threat of the colonizer and willing to have opportunities and recognition from the administration, they did send their family to colonial school, getting ride of the Koranic education.[1]

[edit] Mission based on Non-violence

Although some of the dignitaries did submit to the colonizer, the attempt of destroying Islam has faced a strong resistance from religious guides such as Cheikh Ahmadou Bamba. However, the Holy Man will have understood well before all his fellow-citizens the screen of the plot against Islam and, across, the culture of his people. However, He knew the safety of his people resided in a non-violent fight to awake the counscious in lethargy and to bring them back on the path of God.[1]

[edit] Real foundation of the mission

In a period where Islam was considered to lose his upholder and was not practiced in a good way due to colonial influence, Cheikh Ahmadou Bamba assignated himself a mission of defender.[1] As a result he created Mouridism: a doctrine that advocates an aspiration to Allah in accordance with the prophet's message.

[edit] Meeting with the Islamic Prophet

Cheikh Abdoul Ahad Mbacke, the third Caliph and son of Cheikh Ahmadou Bamba explained a meeting of Cheikh Ahmadou Bamba with the prophet. In Spiritual retirement during the month of Ramadan in the year 1301 H. (1895) in his (Cheikh Ahmadou Bamba) mosque at Darou Khoudouss in the holy city of Touba, Muhammed and his companions appeared to him (Cheikh Ahmadou Bamba) to give him the rank of highest spiritual holy Man of that Period. In his spiritual ardour, Cheikh Ahmadou Bamba desired to be part of the Prophet Muhammad companions. In other words, he did seek for the statue of "Servant Privilege of the Prophet".[2]

[edit] Pact with the Islamic Prophet

The Prophet meant to Him the form and the methods of the price of the transaction: The Prophet warned him of this: "what will make you count among them[companions of the Prophet] is a sum of too heavy tests; because on several occasions a person arrived at the stage where you are currently and with whom I appeared exactly as I do it with you and who had of another ambition only to form of it part but test which is the pledge of its admission once put at its load, he finishes by being helped, if not he would have fallen into disgrace; but only one subject put under the weight of test having carried it until being promoted with their row did not exist yet... thus if that were of my liking, you would not engage there, because you have an attachment towards Me which is not a disguised affection besides, whereas I could not come you to assistance in the test, because by supporting you of the kind, one will make me the reproach and I would not accept the blame of my attitude towards somebody. "Cheikh Ahmadou Bamba (opposing the objection to Him):" as for me, I have a total ignorance of the nature of the test which you will put in my load, not having caused my heart also, I cannot know what it is with same or not to support; but I can certify by oath that weight of some suffering which I would receive, if my heart resists it, in all the cases my moral Strength will handle it."[3]

[edit] Means and strategy of the colonial administration

From the beginning of the 19th century, the policy imperialist of France ended with the defeat of all the resistances armed to Senegal and the installation with a policy of christianization and assimilation of new colony to the cultural values of the metropolis. This will lead to a policy of distance or systematic elimination of the Moslem spiritual guides who dared to mark their distrust. Thus, Cheikh Ahmadou Bamba, whose only wrong was to have dared to persist openly in the sermon for his religion (Islam) was, 32 years during the object of all kinds of deprivations and tests. In exile during 7 years in Gabon, 5 years in Mauritania, placed during 15 years under house arrest with Diourbel in Senegal, he did not cease, until his recall to ALLAH in 1927, to defend, by his teaching and his behavior, the message of Muhammed.[4]

[edit] Cheikh Ahmadou Bamba facing colonial administration

The phobia of the colonial administration at the place of any Islamic movement made the judgements given to the Private Council often constitute lawsuits of intention to the religious chiefs. Stopped to Diéwol, Cheikh Ahmadou Bamba is transferred to the office of the Governor of the colonial administration in Saint-Louis (Senegal). Thursday September 5, 1895,Cheikh Ahmadou Bamba is translated in front of the Private Council of Saint-Louis which must rule on his case. Cheikh Ahmadou Bamba made a prayer of two rakkas in the office of the Governor before addressing the word to the Council to mean his firm intention to be subjected only to God. By this symbolic pray and this bold standpoint in the sanctuary of the deniers of Islam, Cheik Ahmadou Bamba came to impel a new form of nonviolent resistance to the aimings evangelists of the colonizer.[1] As a result, the Private Council decided deportation of the Holy Man "in a place where its fanatic preachings would not have any effect".[1].They decided to exile the Holy Man in the equatorial forest of Gabon, where the he remained for seven years and nine months.

[edit] Servant privilege of the Prophet

The rank of Servant Privilegie of the Prophet is a statue of the Prophet Muhammad Companions. However this rank is special regarding the mission and its obligation. When Cheikh Ahmadou Bamba engaged in the service of Muhammed, the Statute of Servant Privilegie was one of his concern. This statute was the dedication of the quality of the rendered service.[5]

[edit] Recognition by the Muslim community

In all the stages which he crossed and everywhere where rebuilding of Islam led him, the work accomplished by Cheik Ahmadou Bamba took down positive appreciations unanimously. The universal dimension of this Servant of the Prophet, authenticates "Qutb"1 (pole of its time) was not long in appearing and one visited him from all the horizons, as it was the case of this Cherif of Médine, Mawlay Ahmad Tibri which made print specimens of "Masaalik-ul Jinaan" (a treaty of Sufism of the Sheik) or of this descendant of Abu Bakr Siddiq (khalif of Islam) which came from Mecca visit to the Holy Man.[4]
