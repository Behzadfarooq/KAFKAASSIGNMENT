[edit] Origin

the article says it is from a Latin word meaning lovable. other sites might even tell you that the name was first used for some 17th century play. What it won't say is the truth: it is a feminized form of the no-longer-popular name Amand. And the most famous Amand was St. Amand of Maastricht, a Catholic bishop. Because he was a famous saint in France, the name Amanda for girls historically starts in the late 7th/early 8th centuries, NOT the 17th century. 58.169.129.78 08:34, 2 February 2007 (UTC)
