Amarillo High School

Amarillo High School is a school located in the city of Amarillo, Texas, United States, in the Amarillo Independent School District.

[edit] History

[edit] Founding

Founded in 1889, Amarillo High School began in a converted courthouse which was outgrown and abandoned that same year. Moving to a larger building on Polk street served the schools needs until 1906 and yet another building was utilized until 1910.

Construction of a permanent home was completed in 1910, also on Polk street near downtown, and this facility served until a fire destroyed the entire school in 1970. Students of AHS were split off to the other AISD schools and facilities until construction of the current facility in southwest Amarillo was completed.

[edit] General Aspects of the School

[edit] Academics

Amarillo High School is reputed to have high academic standards, with scores on the state Texas Assessment of Knowledge and Skills test ranking higher than state and local averages. The school offers Advanced Placement classes as well as the full International Baccalaureate diploma.

[edit] Music

One of the musical groups of AHS during the 1950s was the Sandie Swingsters who played for pep rallies, dances and other special events at AHS

[edit] Clubs

Amarillo High School offers many diverse activities, in foreign languages, fine arts, community services, and athletics, including Spanish Club, Latin Club, German Club, French Club, band, orchestra, choir, Biology Club, Key Club, Ken Club, Junior Statesmen of America, National Honor Society, FCCLA, Math Club, football, baseball, swimming, golf, wrestling, and other curricular and extracurricular activities.

[edit] Rivalry

"Hell Week" happens each fall as the annual AHS vs. Tascosa High School football game approaches.

Pranks such as the toilet papering of homes and the "egging" of vehicles and mutual school property are common during "Hell Week."

[edit] Cell Phone Use

One of the main school policies on an unrecognized student trial is the cell phone policy. This policy states that a student may possess a cellular phone as long as it is turned off.

[edit] Athletic Focus

Though presenting numerous academic options for its students, Amarillo High School is often criticized for its focus on the athletic (primarily football) programs the school offers. Rather than concentrating on and funding more scholastic areas of the curriculum, Amarillo High School is said by some to have shifted its focus away from more scholarly applications of its facilities, but this is unverified.

[edit] Pep Rallies

Known by some for its exuberant and raucous pep rallies, Amarillo High School encourages all students to attend its weekly assemblies in order to raise school morale and excitement. However, attendance is not required. Students who do not wish to or cannot attend, for whatever reason, attend study hall in the school cafeteria. Not allowed to converse or move about (which is common and expected for a study hall), such students are also required to sign a sheet of attendance which is then kept by school officials. This is to assure that all students are in attendance at the school as required by state law. Students who attend the rallies are "head counted" via roll call by their classroom teacher at the beginning of every class, then ones who choose to attend study hall sign the attendence sheet there, to ensure arrival in the cafeteria. The teachers check roll once again when class resumes after the rally, and the hallways, activity center egress, and access to and from the campus proper is monitored during the event. Students are also monitored as they arrive and leave the activity center. This is also practiced at all AISD high schools, AHS is no exception.
