Amarildo Tavares da Silveira, also known as Amarildo (born in Campos, Rio de Janeiro, June 29, 1939) was a Brazilian football (soccer) player who played as a striker.

His career lasted from 1957 to 1974 and he played for Goytacaz, Flamengo, Botafogo and Vasco da Gama, and in Italy for Milan, Fiorentina and Roma. He won one "Coppa Italia" in 1967 with Milan and the Italian title ("Scudetto") in 1969 with Fiorentina. For Brazil he won the 1962 FIFA World Cup as a replacement for the injured Pelé.
