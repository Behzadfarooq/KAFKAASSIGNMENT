Amadocus

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Amadocus (in Greek Aμαδoκoς; lived 4th century BC) was a ruler in Thrace, who inherited in conjunction with Berisades and Cersobleptes the dominions of Cotys, on the death of the latter in 358 BC. Amadocus was probably a son of Cotys and a brother of the other two princes, though this is not stated by Demosthenes.1 Amadocus seems to have had a son of the same name.2
