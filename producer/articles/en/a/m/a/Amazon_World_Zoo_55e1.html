The Amazon World Zoo is a zoo on the Isle of Wight, UK is the largest exotic animal attraction on the island. Regular features include falconry shows and lessons in conservation.

It became famous after a penguin was stolen from the zoo leading to worldwide news coverage.

Toga was a Jackass penguin who was stolen from the zoo. Toga was the first South African Jackass Penguin bred at the zoo.

On December 19, 2005, burglars stole Toga. It is supposed that the thieves scaled a 6ft outer wall before climbing over an 8ft metal fence. Katherine Bright, manager at Amazon World, said: "Toga was last seen at 4.30pm on Saturday. 1

As the penguin refuses food from humans it is unlikely to survive more than a few days away from its parents. The zoo initially offered a reward of £1,000 2, which was swelled by additional offers of money from around the world (Including $600 from America) 3. Zoo staff speculated that the film March of the Penguins may have inspired the crime.

A young girl sent the zoo an e-mail offering to swap with the thief if Toga had been stolen as a Christmas present. "I'll give you my new Xbox 360 in exchange." By Thursday, hopes of finding Toga alive were fading. There was also a caller to the morning television programme GMTV claiming to have dumped the body of Toga in Portsmouth Harbour

Despite the unlikelyhood of success on the 27 December the zoo continued to search for the missing penguin. 4

By 17 January 2006, the zoo staff had given up all hope of ever finding the whereabouts of Toga.

Toga's parents Kyala and Oscar produced two more eggs early 2006 after recovering from the loss of their first born. On the 14th February 2006 one of the penguin keepers dashed to the office at Amazon World bearing the good news that one of the two eggs had hatched and bore a tiny new addition to the family. The other egg didn't hatch and was later found to be infertile.

The currently unnamed penguin will have its sex determined when it has become comfortable with its surroundings by way of DNA testing on two feather samples. Due to the size of the penguin the Zoo staff believe it to be Female. In the UK the newspaper group, Daily Mail are holding a competition to name the new addition to the penguin enclosure.
