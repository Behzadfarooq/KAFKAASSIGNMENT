Amazon Fishbowl

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Amazon Fishbowl is a talk show produced by and available exclusively on Amazon.com. It is hosted by Bill Maher and features a variety of guests promoting recently-released books, music, and movies. Amazon Fishbowl made its debut on June 1, 2006.

[edit] Format

Unlike Bill Maher's Real Time With Bill Maher which features panel discussions on mostly political topics, Amazon Fishbowl follows a fairly traditional talk show format: following a short comedy monologue, guests appear alone with Maher, in three-to-four minute segments promoting their latest project and often touching on their creative process. Towards the end of the show there is a musical performance, with the first three installments featuring Rob Thomas, Dixie Chicks, and Dashboard Confessional, respectively.

Below the video of the program itself are links to the product each guest is promoting, or similar works available on Amazon.com. In the case of a movie coming to (or still in) theatres, a link to its Internet Movie Database listing is used instead. Amazon.com has described this as eliminating the "separation between the artist discovery experience and the follow-up purchase opportunity". [1]

Additionally, there are brief commercial breaks for unrelated products and services between segments.

[edit] Technology

The show is viewed on the Amazon.com web site. It is streamed in the Adobe Flash format and offers the ability for the viewer to skip ahead or back to a specific point in the program (though each segment is contained in a separate Flash file that can be started from its beginning). There is a "Windows Media" link below the Macromedia Flash player, which streams the show using Windows Media Player on the user's computer, offering the ability to skip forwards and backwards in the show.
