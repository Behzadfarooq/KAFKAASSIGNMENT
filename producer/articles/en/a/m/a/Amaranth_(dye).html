Amaranth

Amaranth, FD&C Red No. 2, C.I. Food Red 9, Acid Red 27, Azorubin S, or C.I. 16185, is a dark red to purple azo dye once used as a food dye and to color cosmetics, but since 1976 it has been banned in the United States by FDA as it is a suspected carcinogen. [1] It usually comes as a trisodium salt. It has the appearance of reddish-brown, dark red to purple water-soluble powder that decomposes at 120 Â°C without melting. Its water solution has absorption maximum at about 520 nm. Amaranth was made from coal tar

Amaranth is an anionic dye. It can be applied to natural and synthetic fibers, leather, paper, and phenol-formaldehyde resins.

As a food additive it has E number E123.

Its CAS number is [915-67-3] and its SMILES structure is OC1=C(S(=O) ([O-])=O)C=C4C (C=CC(S(=O)([O-])=O) =C4)=C1N=NC2=C C=C(S(=O)([O-])=O) C3=C2C=CC=C3. Its risk and safety phrases are R36/37/38 S36/37/39.
