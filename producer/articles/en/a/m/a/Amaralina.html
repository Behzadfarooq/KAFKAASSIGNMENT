Amaralina is a small town and municipality in north Goiás state, Brazil.

  • Population in 2005: 3,115 (2005)
  • Total area: 1,418.1 km²
  • Elevation: 55 meters
  • Became a municipality: 1997
  • Microregion: Porangatu Microregion
  • Postal code: 76493-000
  • Distance from Goiânia: 373 km.
  • Highway access from Goiânia: GO-080 / Nerópolis / BR-153 / Jaraguá / GO-080 / Goianésia / Barro Alto / GO-342 / Uruaçu / Campinorte / GO-329. See Seplan

Amaralina is surrounded by the following municipalities:

  • north: Mutunópolis, Bonópolis and Porangatu
  • south and east: Mara Rosa
  • west: Mundo Novo

Economy

The economy is dependent on cattle raising, services, public service jobs, and agriculture. There were 70,000 head of cattle in 2003. The main agricultural products in 2003 were rice (500 hectares planted), corn (800 hectares), and bananas (45 hectares). [2]

  • The Gross Domestic Product was 15.1 million Reais in 2002, which was 0.05% of the state GDP of 31.298 billion Reais in the same year.
  • GDP per capita: 4,890 Reais in 2002, which was below the state average of 5,291 Reais of the same year. For information on all the municipalities of Goiás see Portal Sepin
