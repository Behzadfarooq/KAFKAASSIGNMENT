Amanda Somerville

Amanda Somerville is an American singer-songwriter and vocal coach better known for her collaborations with various european Metal bands. Since 1999 she has resided and worked mainly in Germany and the Netherlands.

Her voice range is alto and her vocal style goes from classical to soul music.

[edit] Solo Artist Discography

Amanda's solo career focuses on soft and elegant pop/rock with some hard rock, folk and soul music influences. Her solo discography includes a notable incursion on operatic Metal: Aina.

  • In the Beginning there was... (Debut Album, 2000)
  • Blue Nothing (EP, 2000)
  • Never Alone (EP, 2003)
  • Aina: Days of Rising Doom (2003) - Amanda wrote concept and all lyrics, co-wrote music, worked as vocalist and vocal coach.

She's played live as a solo artist with the following line-up made of german heavy metal related musicians:

  • Amanda Somerville - Main Vocals, Piano
  • Sascha Paeth - Guitar, Backing Vocals
  • Olaf Reitmeier - Bass, Backing Vocals
  • MIRO - Keyboards, Backing Vocals
  • Rober Hunecke-Rizzo - Drums, Backing Vocals

[edit] Collaborations

Mainly as singer, vocal coach, producer and choir conductor, Amanda has been involved in the following albums:

  • Epica - The Phantom Agony (2003), We Will Take You With Us (2004), Consign To Oblivion (2005), The Road to Paradiso (due in 2006); worked as vocal coach, vocalist, co-writer, producer
  • After Forever - Invisible Circles (2004), Remagine (2005); worked as vocal coach, vocalist, producer
  • Asrai - Touch in the Dark (2004): worked as lyrical editor
  • Edguy - Hellfire Club (2004), Rocket Ride (2006); worked as vocalist, lyrical editor
  • Kamelot - Black Halo (2005); worked as vocalist, lyrical editor
  • Shaman - Ritual (2002), Reason (2005); worked as vocalist, lyrical editor
  • Mob Rules - Hollowed Be Thy Name (2002); worked as vocalist
  • Luca Turilli & Rhapsody - Prophet of the Last Eclipse (2002); worked as vocalist
  • Virgo - Virgo (2001); worked as vocalist, lyrical editor
  • Various collaborations with other bands (Felony, Reverend Right Time and the First Cousins of Funk, Lunatica, Ebony Ark, etc.)

She also wrote music for:

  • Phaeno Science Center (2005); commissioned to write and perform song about and for the grand opening of science center.
  • Boehringer Ingelheim (2005); commissioned to write 2 jingles for new swine vaccine.
