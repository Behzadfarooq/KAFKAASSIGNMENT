The aMAZEine 7" was a split 7" that both Godspeed You! Black Emperor and Fly Pan Am participated on. It was released by aMAZEine! for free with their fourth issue. The first 550 copies were pressed on white vinyl and all others afterwards were pressed on regular black vinyl. aMAZEzine! is a Montreal-based independent music magazine published between 1995 and 1999 by Marie-Douce St-Jacques.

Each of the bands have one song taking up a complete side of the record. Godspeed You! Black Emperor contributed the song "Sunshine + Gasoline" on the a-side and Fly Pan Am finished off the record with the song "L'espace au sol est redessiné par d'immenses panneaux bleus" on the b-side, which would be used again on their eponymous debut album.
