Amadou

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

For other uses, see Amadou (disambiguation).
Fomes fomentarius

Amadou is a spongy, flammable substance prepared from bracket fungi. The species generally used is Fomes Fomentarius (formerly Ungulina fomentaria or Polyporus fomentarius) which in English is also called horse's hoof fungus or tinder fungus. It is used as tinder (especially after being pounded flat, and boiled or soaked in a solution of nitre) and in fly fishing, for drying out fly lures. It has great water-absorbing potential.

Amadou was a precious resource to ancient people, allowing them to start a fire by catching sparks from flint struck against iron pyrites.
