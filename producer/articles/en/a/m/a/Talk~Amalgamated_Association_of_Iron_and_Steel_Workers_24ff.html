As one of the most important labor unions in the pre-American Federation of Labor (AFL) and AFL periods, as well as a formative union in American history (e.g., the focus, along with autos and rubber, of much of the AFL's organizing efforts from 1910 to the mid-1950s), I'd argue the AA rises to Mid-Level importance.
