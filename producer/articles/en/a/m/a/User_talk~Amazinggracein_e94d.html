Amazing grace! It’s all about god’s grace over a deadly sinner. He loves sinners; he loves people who are dangerous to society. Above all he loves them who have none to love. A sinner after committing a sin is punishable according to law and the society will punish him for all the bad deeds done by a person if it is against the welfare of society. And therefore such a person, cannot be pardoned for he deserves punishment and not love. A person like that who has committed all sorts of sins and has none who will love him also has one person who loves and cares disregard of the degree of sin a person has done . So many have found this person to be very lovely, and are falling in love with this person. Even when the hands are dirty with sin, minds and face are filled with sinful desires and motives this person seems to be different he loves the sinner very much inspite of all bad stuff the sinner has. He takes care of the sinner as if he is the very one he has in this world. Whenever the sinner rejects him by displeasing him, he still sticks closer. He has nothing which will gratify him, but the sinner’s happiness. He is the one who has laid his life for the sinner. He is a father to the fatherless, a lover to one who has no one to love, and a comforter to one who is struggling with the troubles of life. Above all he gives peace. Perfect peace, like the one which a sparrow singing on the top of a tree has. All these he gives for an unqualified sinner, a person who deserves death for the sin he has done and not peace. That’s a kind of unconditional love of a person loving an unlovely, bad and outcaste. It is grace really amazing unexplainable but can be enjoyed only by letting him take care of our sins.

Amazing grace! How sweet the sound That saved a wretch like me! I once was lost, but now am found; Was blind, but now I see. ’Twas grace that taught my heart to fear, And grace my fears relieved; How precious did that grace appear The hour I first believed. Through many dangers, toils and snares, I have already come; ’Tis grace hath brought me safe thus far, And grace will lead me home. The Lord has promised good to me, His Word my hope secures; He will my Shield and Portion be, As long as life endures. Yea, when this flesh and heart shall fail, And mortal life shall cease, I shall possess, within the veil, A life of joy and peace. The earth shall soon dissolve like snow, The sun forbear to shine; But God, Who called me here below, Shall be forever mine. When we’ve been there ten thousand years, Bright shining as the sun, We’ve no less days to sing God’s praise Than when we’d first begun. (A song by John Newton)
