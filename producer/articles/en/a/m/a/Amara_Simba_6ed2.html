Amara Simba (born 23rd December 1961), Dakar, Senegal is a retired French football player. During his career he played for Paris SG, AS Cannes, AS Monaco, SM Caen and Lille OSC in France, Club Leon in Mexico and Leyton Orient, Kingstonian, Kettering Town, Barnet, St. Albans City and Billericay. He was also selected to play for the French national team on three occasions by then manager Michel Platini, but injury prevented his participation in Euro 92 and his international career came to an end.

[edit] Trivia

Simba's move to Leyton Orient came about as a result of training with the club in order to keep fit whilst on holiday in London.
