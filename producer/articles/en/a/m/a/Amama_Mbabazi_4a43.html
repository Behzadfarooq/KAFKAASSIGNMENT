Amama Mbabazi

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Amama Mbabazi and Paul Wolfowitz in November 2003

Amama Mbabazi (born January 16, 1949) is the Ugandan Minister of Security. He previously held the portfolios of Minister of State in the President's office, in-charge of Political Affairs, Minister State for Defence from 1986-1992, Minister of State for Foreign Affairs in charge of Regional Cooperation, Attorney General and Minister of Defence.

Amama Mbabazi is a member of President Museveni's Movement. Currently he is a 'mobiliser' for his party. He was elected to the Western Ugandan seat of Kinkizi West in 2001. This followed a violent campaign in which several people were killed. One of these was a man whose testicles were crushed with pliers. Such were the intimidatory tactics of the 'security and intelligence operatives' and Major Kakooza Mutale's Kalangala Action Plan, who were sent to support his campaign. His opponent, James Muzinguzi, was beaten at the polls but took his complaints of violence and ballot-rigging to the Ugandan High Court, where he won. The judgement was appealed and Muzinguzi won the appeal.

Following this, the court ordered a fresh by-election which took place in February 2003. At this time Amama Mbabazi was Uganda's Minister for Defence. He refused to step down, resign his Ministerial position or his Parliamentary seat.

Muzinguzi immediately complained that 7,000 UPDF soldiers had been sent to the Kinkizi West electorate to intimidate voters. After several violent incidents James Muzinguzi announced that he would withdraw from the election so that there would be no more killings. He was labeled a coward in the government newspaper, The New Vision.

The election was declared to have been won by default by Amama Mbabazi, who continued a Defence Minister till General Museveni appointed him to the position of Attorney General in 2004.
