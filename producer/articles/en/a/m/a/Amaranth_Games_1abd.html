The Aveyond series are mostly RPG games (except for Grimm’s Hatchery) from Amaranth Games. The first, Ahriman’s Prophecy, was introduced on March 7, 2004. It is considered by many as a "trial run"[1] to the hugely popular sequel, Aveyond (later rechristened Aveyond I: Rhen’s Quest, in anticipation of Aveyond II: Ean’s Quest[1]), released on January 18, 2006. Grimm’s Hatchery was recently released on February 19, 2007 as a casual in-between game set in the Aveyond universe while players of Aveyond await the much anticipated Aveyond II: Ean’s Quest, scheduled to be released in September 2007[1].

Ahriman's Prophecy is a free, light-hearted adventure game packed with more than 50 hours of engaging gameplay. Amaranth Productions created this game for individuals who enjoy long fantasy games with intricate plots, challenging puzzles, and enchanting atmospheres[2].

[edit] Plot

Ahriman's Prophecy, the first of the series, created a world where demons and fairies and many elements of fairy tales exist. The story starts as a young girl, Talia Maurva, is sent to be "named", a ritual set up by the people of her village to determine the profession of their adult life, although the seer the children were sent to wasn’t too keen about the job pressed upon her. Destiny changes for Talia, who, when looking into the seer’s pool, sees a dark ceremony by the younger prince of a nearby kingdom, Candar. The prince and his dark priest were attempting to resurrect the dead warlock emperor, Ahriman, whose history in battle and warfare was legendary. The seer, sensing that Talia was different, sends her and her escort to the mainland where a school of magic was situated in a city called Thais. Just after she completes her training, she is summoned in her dream by her headmaster and a high priestess to the dreamland. They want her to slip across the continent to warn another order of priestesses that Ahriman is being resurrected, and the prophecy that surrounded his resurrection must be stopped. Confused and disorientated, Talia nevertheless sets out on a journey that paves the way to a temporary peace...

[edit] Characters

Talia Maurva
Brought up by her grandmother in the village of Elden, Talia was sent by the village seer to the Collegium of War and Magick in Thais after the naming ceremony. Graduating as a gifted Magick Wielder, she is considered by her headmaster as the finest and most gifted student in the Collegium.

Devin Perry
Named "blacksmith" by the seer of Elden, his destiny nevertheless changes when he offered to escort Talia to the mainland. He is accepted and trained as a Knight at the Collegium, and is Talia's most loyal companion. His sister Lydia, however, does not share his friendship with Talia, but displays outright, if childish, hostility towards Talia.

Jack
A fourteen-year-old pickpocket who lives in Thais. He has the ability to open locked chests and sealed vats, and pilfer anything he can get his hands on.

Frederick Le Mew
A shapeshifter who resides in the animal sanctuary of the Ahriman's Prophecy world, Frederick's normal shape is a lizard-like creature. He has a brother who is frequently kidnapped by the travelling circus as their "talking toad" act.

Alicia Pendragon
Although she is the princess of Thais, she does not like the responsibilities of being a princess or the idea of marrying princes or noblemen, wishing instead to travel and fight. She is trained as a Knight at the Collegium, and resents being taken less seriously because she is a girl.

Haddan
He is a dwarf who lives deep within the dwarf mines located near Candar. He is a mercenary for hire, and will fight for the right price.

As a sequel to Ahriman's Prophecy, this game features a different map from its prequel except for two islands (see Backstory). Aveyond I: Rhen's Quest is played in battle mode attacks (a change from Ahriman’s Prophecy) which allow players to choose four of eight possible party members to fight. Each character has unique defense abilities, weapon skills, and personalities. The game is a blend of fighting and solving puzzles. In the game which has 60 quests[3], with some optional, but others essential in advancing the storyline[4], you must battle with different creatures (There are over 70 types of opponents, which includes monsters, rogues, mercenaries, demons etc.[4]) in order to gain experience and collect money to purchase items. Some decisions and quests are actually open-ended in this game, as are the endings, and, depending on your choices, will determine your options in the later part of the game, or in the case of the endings, the end of Aveyond I: Rhen’s Quest[4].

Aveyond I: Rhen's Quest costs about US$20 and is available for purchase on many game websites. There are also free one-hour trial versions available.

[edit] Backstory

Sixteen years prior to Aveyond I: Rhen’s Quest, the forces of the demon Ahriman destroyed and sunk most of the surrounding areas and isles around Thais (first seen in Ahriman’s Prophecy). This was because Alicia Pendragon, queen of Thais, was foretold to give birth to a child who would defeat a great demon and save Thais if she reached adulthood[4]. It was the foretelling of this birth that Ahriman wanted Thais destroyed. Tailor Darzon, a young but trusted general of Thais, offered to take the child to a safe place and raise her where the demon would not know to find her. As Thais and the queen fell, Tailor fled the kingdom with the child and escaped across the ocean to a faraway land[4]. He almost didn't make it, but Talia Maurva (the main character of Ahriman's Prophecy), now the Druid of Dreams, saved their lives. Tailor settled in the small mountain village of Clearwater, became a shoemaker, married the village seamstress, and raised the child as his own[4].

[edit] Plot

When the game starts, it is sixteen years later, and the girl, who was named Rhen by her adopted father, was as carefree as can be when a glowing butterfly teleported her into the dreamland. There she sees Talia, barely alive after the daeva Agas attacked her, quickly brings her back through the portal to Clearwater. Her thousand questions were only partially answered by Tailor, who didn’t know what to tell her after all this time. Unfortunately, before she knew more, a case of mistaken identity causes Rhen, instead of Talia, to be kidnapped by a slave trader and she was sold to a family[4] residing on the eastern island (Clearwater is in the Highlands of the western island).

This slave trader was, in fact, employed by Ahriman’s newest lieutenant, Dameon, who was also Talia’s son. A long and bitter family history prompts Dameon to forsake his duty as the Druid of Light to serve Ahriman, as his father, the previous Druid of Light, has. When they found out about the slave trader’s mistake, Ahriman had the Dark seer, Indra read Rhen’s apocalypse. He learns then, that this child will destroy him, but he couldn't kill her or he will also be destroyed. So, he sends his minions to find her and turn her to his side, as Indra proclaimed.

Meanwhile, Rhen was found to have a great aptitude for sword magic, and was released from slavery and sent to the eastern capital city to learn the art of sword singing[3][4] (similar to knights, only they were able to channel their magic into powerful spells through a Sword of Power). After she was raised to a novice, however, she reunites with Talia, who tells her that she must reunite all eight druids of the world so that an artefact of great importance could be released, and that it was her destiny to finally vanquish Ahriman once and for all. Along her journey, Rhen will discover secrets about her past and will have to make decisions that will determine the direction of her future as well as the fate of the world.

[edit] Characters

Rhen "Darzon" Pendragon
Peasant girl from a small mountain village. She is captured and sold as a slave in Ghalarah to Mistress Rona Tenobor, but is freed when it is discovered she can learn sword magic and become a sword singer.

Lars Tenobor
Mean-spirited noble boy who is from the eastern town Ghalarah. He is the son of Mistress Rona Tenobor, who owns Rhen. In the town, it is well known that Lars can learn magic and is going to become a sorcerer. When he learns that Rhen is going to attend his school and become a sword singer, he sets out to make her life miserable.

Dameon Maurva
Young, powerful sun priest who has turned away from the light. He joins Rhen and attempts to lure her into his evil master's grasp.

Elinidana'ter'Lithir
Exotic demon summoner who is from a city on the southern island called Veldt. She has three husbands, but has left her city to find a new husband to add to her collection.

Te'ijal Ravenfoot
Vampress who has the desire to leave her midnight village and see the surface. She has a mean bite and an unhealthy appetite for a pure-hearted paladin.

Galahad Teomes
Royal paladin of a western kingdom called Sedona who sees the world in black and white. He is convinced that magic does not exist and joins the party because he thinks Rhen must be protected from the forces of evil, which includes the vampress, Te'ijal.

Pirate John
Randy pirate who is good at getting in trouble. In his earlier days, he was a daring dragon rider for the empire of Veldt, but left in favor of piracy to escape a Veldt woman "before her other husbands drag me to the altar".

Mad Marge
Tough tavern owner with a short temper. She is as big as a horse and as mean as a bear with a sore tooth.

[edit] Grimm’s Hatchery

In the words of game developer Amanda Fitch[1],

I needed a break from RPGs, I wanted to work on a short 5-month project, and I wanted to draw more casual gamers into my world of Aveyond. The conversion rate for Aveyond is very high, and I think a lot of players haven't played this sort of game because it is completely new to them. I hope that Grimm’s Hatchery will help me convert more of these players. I want them to fall in love with the characters in Grimm's Hatchery (a traditional casual game), and feel brave enough to try out Aveyond II so that they can experience their favorite characters again.

Grimm's Hatchery costs about US$20 and is available for purchase on Amaranth's website. There is also a free one-hour trial version available.

[edit] Plot

You control the main, unnamed character, whose stepfather has died and left the Candar kingdom (previously appearing in Ahriman’s Prophecy), which belonged to your mother before she died, to your evil stepbrother, Lord Boras. However, if you earn 300,000 gold coins in 80 days (the target gold coins and days vary with the difficulty of the game), you can kick Lord Boras out and take the castle for yourself.[5]

You start off by taking over a run-down cottage called Grimm’s Farm, where you can raise, breed and sell new species of magical pets, while protecting your pets from the monsters invading the hatcheries[5]. As your herd of magical pets grows, you can complete the handful of side-quests available throughout the game[1] to be able to buy new, more luxurious hatcheries to raise your pets, as certain pets can only be raised in certain hatcheries, as well as unlock new areas of the kingdom to explore, unveiling different merchants and an egg-zapping pet lab.

[edit] Aveyond II: Ean’s Quest

[edit] Plot

Ean (male) and Iya (female) are two young elves who live in a far away place called the Vale. One day, Ean wakes up to find that Iya, his best friend, is mysteriously missing and that no one remembers who she is. A very strange thing has also occurred... snow has fallen in the Vale. Ean sets out on a quest to find his missing friend; a quest that will take him away from his beloved home to the mainland below. And on his quest, Ean will find that dear Iya has been swept away by the Snow Queen (who last appeared in Aveyond I: Rhen’s Quest), and that her heart is slowly turning to ice. Ean must save his friend and Iya must learn to control her wild powers that the Snow Queen desires for herself. The fate of Ean and Iya are the key to defeating the Snow Queen's terrible plot to cover the world in ice.[1]
