Amanda Muggleton is a British-born actor who emigrated to Australia in 1974.

Amanda Muggleton trained at London's Guildhall School of Music and Drama and the Royal Academy of Dance. After moving to Australia she had a bit-part in film Mad Max (1979).

Amanda Muggleton as Chrissie Latham in Prisoner.

In the 1980s she became a common face on Australian television. She is chiefly remembered for her performance as tarty Chrissie Latham in the long-running soap opera Prisoner, a role she played on a semi-regular basis from 1979 until 1983. She subsequently played the key role of Connie in soap opera Richmond Hill in 1988. She has since gone on to become one of Australia's most successful and highly sought after theatre actors.

She has appeared in several musical productions, including the Noel Coward show "Marvellous Party!", with Dennis Olsen, at the Cremorne Theatre at the Queensland Performing Arts Centre.

Muggleton is qualified as a speech and drama teacher. She is also a public speaker and can pilot single-engine planes.

In 2005, Muggleton received the Helpmann Award for Best Female Actor in a Supporting Role in a Musical for playing Mercedes Cortez in Eureka.
