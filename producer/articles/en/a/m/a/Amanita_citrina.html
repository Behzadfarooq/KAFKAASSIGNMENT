The False death cap (Amanita citrina), previously also known as Amanita mappa, is a basidiomycotic mushroom which grows in silicate soil in the summer and autumn months.

[edit] Description

This mushroom has a fleshy pale yellow, or sometimes white, cap from 4-10 cm (1.5-4 in) across, covered in irregular patches. The gills and flesh are white . There is a large volva at the base of the 6-8 cm (2.5-3 in) tall stem, which has a clear ring. This mushroom is not eaten, having a smell of rapeseed or potato. It is often confused with the related Death cap mushroom, hence the name.[1]

top view of an amanita citrina.

[edit] Distribution and habitat

The False death cap is found in deciduous and coniferous woodlands in Autumn in Europe.[1]


[edit] Toxicity

Scientific tests in the University of Cambridge has shown that this mushroom contains the Alpha-amanitin toxin. However, the amounts of this toxin were found to be very small and would not cause any adverse effects unless the mushroom was ingested in very large amounts.
