Amanda Marie Latona was born on March 24th, 1979 in Pittsburgh, PA. She is a singer and actress.

[edit] Innosense

Amanda's first claim to fame came as a member of the all-girl group Innosense . She left the band to go solo, and was replaced by Jenny Morris. Innosense released one single, titled Wherever You Are, before she left. They made a video for the song that was released in Germany in 1998.

[edit] Solo

Amanda Latona signed a solo contract with J Records and recorded an album. Can't Take It Back was released to radio in the US on July 22nd, 2002. Do You Still was released nearly a year later on May 12th, 2003. Neither single did well and she was later dropped from the label.

Latona was featured in an article in the August 4, 2002 New York Times Magazine. The article, entitled "Who's That Girl?," [2] chronicled J Records' grooming of Latona's career in an attempt to ensure her success.

[edit] Groove Kitty

She moved to Las Vegas, Nevada in the middle of 2006 and soon after secured a place in Groove Kitty. The group performs five days a week at the Mandalay.

[edit] Personal Life

At the end of 1998, Latona began dating AJ McLean, a member of the Backstreet Boys. She had previously been involved with Chris Kirkpatrick of *Nsync and Rich Cronin of LFO. They were together until the end of 2000.
