Amalia Mendoza (born Amalia Mendoza Garcia in 1923 in San Juan Huetamo, Michoacán – died June 11, 2001 in Mexico City, Mexico) was one of Mexico's most famous and passionate ranchera singers who had a career that lasted over 40 years. She was nicknamed “La Tariácuri” after her brothers' group the Trio Tariácuri.

[edit] Biography

After limited success as part of a duo with her sister Perla she struck out as a solo singer in 1954 after singing on a radio show. Throughout the majority of her career she was accompanied by El Mariachi Vargas de Tecalitlán. Throughout her long career she recorded numerous rancheras and boleros from noted composers such as José Alfredo Jiménez, Cuco Sánchez, José Ángel Espinosa “Ferrusquilla”, Gabriel Ruíz and Tomás Méndez. She died in 2001 of a paralyzing lung disease.
