Amrita Hunjan is a British Asian singer/actress and one third of the girl group Rouge. This group has been stylistically compared to Destiny's Child and Mis-teeq.

[edit] Pop Career

Amrita Hunjan is the oldest member of the London based Indian/Asian influenced pop group Rouge. Their songs include "Don't be shy" and "Chinese Whispers". The Rouge album was due to appear in summer 2006. Miss India 2004 runner up, Neha Raizada, was quoted after a singing performance from Amrita that "no one has ever seen an Indian girl sing like that before, she was awesome!" [1]

[edit] Academic Career

She is currently studying towards a degree in Dentistry at the University of Birmingham. She achieved all As in her GCSEs and A levels

[edit] Film Career

She is a previous Miss India UK 2004 Winner, and is due to feature in the British-made film Don't Stop Dreaming as Shalini, alongside Michelle Collins and Richard Blackwood.[2]


[edit] Rumors

In late 2006, several music forums and websites reported that Amrita was romantically involved with British-Asian producer and vocalist S-Endz, of the group Swami. This rumor was denied via S-Endz' official website.
