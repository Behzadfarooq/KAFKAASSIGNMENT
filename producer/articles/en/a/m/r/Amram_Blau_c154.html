Rabbi Amram Blau (1894–1974) was an Haredi Rabbi from the Hungarian community of Jerusalem. He was one of the founders of the fiercely Anti-Zionist Neturei Karta.

Amram Blau grew up in the Meah Shearim neighbourhood of Jerusalem a proponent of Torah observant Judasim. Like his brother Rabbi Moshe Blau he was active in the Agudat Israel during the British Mandate era.[1] But when the Agudat began to lean towards accommodating Zionism he broke away and founded Neturei Karta with the equally uncompromising Rabbi Aaron Katzenellenbogen in 1937.[2]

In the 40s he authored what became the anthem of Neturei Karta:

“ G-d is our King,
We are his servants

The holy Torah is our Law
We are loyal to it.
We do not recognize the Heretic Zionist Regime
Its laws do not apply to us
We walk in the ways of the Torah
In fire and water
We walk in the ways of the Torah
To Sanctify the Name of Heaven[3]

 ”

After the establishment of the State of Israel he continued his staunch opposition to any Jewish state aligning Neturei Karta with the Satmarer Rebbe Joel Teitelbaum, author of the anti-Zionist Vayoel Moshe. Prior to the Six-Day War he even went so far as to propose moving to Jordanian controlled East Jerusalem to avoid the secular temptations of modern Israel.[4]

After the 1961 death of his first wife, Hinda, he caused controversy by marrying the convert Ruth Ben-David. Born Madeleine Feraille, a Catholic, she had adopted Judaism and moved to Israel after saving Jews as a French Resistance fighter during World War II.[5] The couple were forced to move to Bnei Brak for a year until the wrath of the Haredi community had subsided. The controversy caused a permanent rift with the Edah HaChareidis and Rabbi Blau's two adult sons.[2]

Rabbi Blau died in 1974 never having reconciled himself with Zionism of the Israeli state.
