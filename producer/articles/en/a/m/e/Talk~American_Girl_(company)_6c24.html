[edit] Title

Is it completely pedantic of me to point out that the page should be "American Girl dolls" and not "American girl dolls"? Mhari 05:23, 23 October 2005 (UTC)

It's not pedantic, but how does it get changed? I can't figure it out. Becka 07:08, 16 December 2005 (UTC)

According to Wikipedia naming conventions only proper nouns should have capitalization on the second, third, and so on word. Does 'american girl doll' qualify as a proper noun? Loggie 22:41, 16 December 2005 (UTC)

"American Girl" is the proper name of the toy. From the website: American Girl®, the American Girl logo, American Girl® magazine, and American Girl Place® are registered trademarks of American Girl, LLC. So "doll" would not need to be capitalized. Mhari 00:16, 17 December 2005 (UTC)
Okay. Next question is whether American Girl dolls is really the title we want? The article talks about the whole company, not just the dolls. Perhaps American Girl (company), or something similar, would be more appropriate? Loggie 06:07, 17 December 2005 (UTC)
