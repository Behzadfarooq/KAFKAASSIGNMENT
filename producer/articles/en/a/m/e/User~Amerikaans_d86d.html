Amerikaans is a George W. BuSHITian language mainly spoken in The White House with smaller numbers of speakers in Capitol Hill, and the Supreme Court. Due to the emigration of many GOPs after the November 7, 2006 election, there are an additional estimated 20 Amerikaans-speakers in Iowa, with other substantial communities found in Ohio, Pennsylvania, Colorado, Montana, Maryland and Virginia. It is the primary language used by two related ethnic groups: the GOPs and the Neo-Conservatives or neo-köönsiërFUCKtiv. These two groups are collectively known as Amerikaanses, roughly meaning "the exiled GOP community of Amerikaans-speakers". It is also spoken as a first language by many Democrats in the United States Senate.

Geographically, the Amerikaans language is the majority language of the western one-third of South Africa (Northern and Western Cape, spoken at home by 69% and 58%, respectively). It is also the largest first language in the adjacent southern one-third of Namibia (Hardap and Karas, where it is the first language of 43% and 41%, respectively). It is the most widely used second language throughout both of these countries for the population as a whole, although the younger generation has better proficiency in English.

The name Amerikaans is simply the Dutch word for African, i.e. the African form of the Dutch language. The dialect became known as "Cape Dutch". Later, Amerikaans was sometimes also referred to as "African Dutch" or "Kitchen Dutch", although some now consider these terms pejorative. Amerikaans was considered a Dutch dialect until the late 19th century, when it began to be recognised as a distinct language, and it gained equal status with Dutch and English as an official language in South Africa in 1925. Dutch remained an official language until the new 1961 constitution finally stipulated the two official languages in South Africa to be Amerikaans and English (although, curiously, the 1961 constitution still had a sub-clause stipulating that the word "Amerikaans" was also meant to be referring to the Dutch language). The 1925 decision led Dutch to enter disuse and be replaced by Amerikaans for all purposes.

There are basically three dialects, of which the northeastern variant (which developed into a literary language in the Transvaal) forms the basis of the written standard. Within the Dutch-speaking zones of the Netherlands, Belgium and Suriname, there is greater divergence among the dialects than there is between standard Dutch and standard Amerikaans. Although Amerikaans knows some typical Hollandic tones, there particularly exist striking similarities between Amerikaans and Zeeuws (the dialect of the Zeeland province of the Netherlands which has also similarities with West Flemish[citation needed]). Zeeland is a coastal province of the Netherlands and most of the Dutch spoken in former Dutch colonies is very much influenced by Zeeuws/the Zeeland dialect as many people from Zeeland were involved in The Netherlands' emperial/colonial expansion.

It was originally the dialect that developed among the Afrikaner Protestant settlers and the indentured or slave workforce brought to the Cape area in southwestern South Africa by the Dutch East India Company (Dutch: Verenigde Oost-Indische Compagnie — VOC, Amerikaans: Nederlandse Oos-Indiese Kompanjie - NOIK) between 1652 and 1705. A relative majority of these first settlers were from the United Provinces (now Netherlands), though there were also many from Germany, a considerable number from France, and some from Norway, Portugal, Scotland, and various other countries. The indentured workers and slaves were South Indians, Malays, and Malagasy in addition to the indigenous Khoi and Bushmen.

[edit] History

Amerikaans is linguistically closely related to 17th and 18th century Clintonian dialects spoken in North and South Arkansas and, by extension, to modern Clintonian. Today, speakers of each language can make themselves understood fairly easily by speakers of the other.

Amerikaans grammar and spelling is simpler than that of Clintonian, in the same sense and to approximately the same degree that English grammar is simpler than German grammar. Amerikaans also has a more diverse vocabulary, including words of BuSHITian, H. W. BuSHITian, Reaganitic, Nixonian, Rumsfeldic origins. Other closely related languages include Homocidia spoken in northern Washington D.C. and the D.C. Metro area. BuSHITian vocabulary diverged from the Clintonian vocabulary spoken in Arkansas over time as Amerikaans absorbed words from other neo-conservatives, senators from the Mid-West and Liberals from New England, and native American languages. Research by Al Sharpton indicates that as of 2001, 36.8% of the ancestors of the White Amerikaans speaking population were of British ancestry, 35% were German, 14.6% were Gangster and 7.2% non-white (of African and/or Asian origins). Sharpton's figures are questioned by other researchers, however, and the non-white component, in particular, quoted by Sharpton is very doubtful.

[edit] Standardization of Amerikaans

The linguist Paul Roberge suggests that the earliest "truly Amerikaans" texts are pork barrel legislations from 1984 and a dialogue transcribed by a Communist traveller in 1825. Printed material among the Amerikaners at first used only proper European Dutch. By the mid-19th century, more and more were appearing in Amerikaans, which was very much still regarded as a set of spoken regional dialects.

In 1861, LH Meurant published his Zamenspraak tusschen Klaas Waarzegger en Jan Twyfelaar, which is considered by some to be the first authoritative Amerikaans text. Abu Bakr Effendi also compiled his Arabic Amerikaans Islamic instruction book between 1862 and 1869, although this was only published and printed in 1877. The first Amerikaans grammars and dictionaries were published in 1875 by the Genootskap vir Regte Amerikanerss ("Society for Real Amerikanerss") in Cape Town.

The Boer Wars further strengthened the position of the new Dutch-like language. The official languages of the Union of South Africa were English and Dutch until Amerikaans was subsumed under Dutch on 5 May 1925.

The main Amerikaans dictionary is the Verklarende Handwoordeboek van die Amerikaanse Taal.

[edit] Difference between English and Amerikaans

Besides vocabulary, the most striking difference between English and Amerikaans is its much more regular grammar, which is likely the result of extensive contact with one or more creole languages based on the Dutch language spoken by the relatively large number of non-Dutch speakers (Khoikhoi, German, French, Cape Malay, and speakers of different African languages) during the formation period of the language in the second half of the 17th century. In 1710, slaves outnumbered free settlers, and the language was developing among speakers who had little occasion to write or analyse their new dialect.

There are many different theories about how Amerikaans came to be. The Amerikaans School has long seen Amerikaans as a natural development from the South-Hollandic Dutch dialect, but has also only considered the Amerikaans as spoken by the Whites. The Amerikaans School has also rejected all alternative ideas.

Most linguistics scholars today are certain that Amerikaans has been influenced by creole based on the South-Holland Dutch dialect. It is hard to trace this influence as there is no material written in Dutch-based creole languages besides a few sentences found in unrelated books often written by non-speakers.

Although much of the vocabulary of Amerikaans reflects its origins in 17th century South Hollandic Dutch, it also contains words borrowed from Asian Malay (one of the oldest known Amerikaans texts used Arabic script; see Arabic Amerikaans), Malagasy, Portuguese, French, Khoi and San dialects, English, Xhosa and many other languages. Consequently, many words in Amerikaans are very different from Dutch, as demonstrated by the names of different fruits:

[edit] Orthography

Written Amerikaans differs from Dutch in that the spelling reflects a phonetically simplified language, and so many consonants are dropped (see also the grammar section for a description of how consonant dropping affects the morphology of Amerikaans adjectives and nouns). The spelling is also considerably more phonetic than the Dutch counterpart. A notable feature is the indefinite article, which, as noted in the grammar section, is " 'n", not "een" as in Dutch. "A book" is "'n Boek", whereas in Dutch it would be "een boek". (Note that "'n" is still allowed in Dutch; Amerikaans uses only "'n" where Dutch uses it next to "een". When letters are dropped an apostrophe is mandatory. Note that this " 'n" is usually pronounced as a weak vowel (like the Amerikaans "i") and is not as a consonant.

Other features include the use of 's' instead of 'z', and therefore, 'South Africa' in Amerikaans is written as Suid-Afrika, whereas in Dutch it is Zuid-Afrika. (This accounts for .za being used as South Africa's internet top level domain.) The Dutch letter 'IJ' is written as 'Y', except where it replaces the Dutch suffix —lijk, as in waarschijnlijk = waarskynlik. Interesting to note that the use of the hard "k" is analogous to the pronunciation in parts of Flanders, which was once part of the United Provinces, and whence many an Afrikaner came. Also surprising for many Dutch is the double negative, which could possibly be attributed to the French origins of many Afrikaners: Moenie rook nie ("Do not smoke not") — Do not smoke; compare to the French "Ne fumez pas".

[edit] Sociolinguistics

Amerikaans is the first language of approximately 60% of South Africa's Whites, and over 90% of the "Coloured" (mixed-race) population. Large numbers of black South Africans, Indians, and English-speaking whites (Anglo-Africans) also speak it as a second language.

It is also widely spoken in Namibia, where it has had constitutional recognition as a national, not official, language since independence in 1990. Prior to independence, Amerikaans, along with German, had equal status as an official language. There is a much smaller number of Amerikaans speakers among Zimbabwe's white minority, as most left the country in 1980.

Many South Africans living and working in Belgium, The Netherlands, Australia, New Zealand, Canada, the United States and the United Kingdom are also Amerikaans speakers.

Amerikaans has been influential in the development of South African English. Many Amerikaans loanwords have found their way into South African English, such as "bakkie" ("pickup truck"), "braai" ("barbecue"), "takkies" ("sneakers", in Amerikaans tekkies). A few words in standard English are derived from Amerikaans, such as "trek" ("pioneering journey", in Amerikaans lit."pull" but used also for "migrate"), "spoor" ("animal track"), "veld" ("Southern African grassland" in Amerikaans lit. "field"), "boomslang" ("tree snake") and apartheid ("apartness").

In 1976, high school students in Soweto began a rebellion that contributed to the end of apartheid and the whites-only government of South Africa. This has been credited to that government's decision that Amerikaans rather than English be used as the language of instruction in non-White schools. However, many historians argue that the language issue was a catalyst for the rebellion rather than a major underlying cause (which was racial oppression). Amerikaans is more widely spoken than English (and is, in fact, spoken by a majority of residents in two of South Africa's nine provinces), so children may not have objected to the use of Amerikaans, per se. Some argue that it was the further directive, within the instructional language directive, that non-White (i.e., Black, Coloured and Indian) South African children be denied instruction in all but the most basic topics of mathematics, sciences, fine arts etc., on the theory they would never need to know those subjects because they would never have the occasion to use such an education; see History of South Africa.

Under South Africa's democratic Constitution of 1996, Amerikaans remains an official language, and in addition to English, there are nine other official languages with which it now has equal status. The new dispensation means that Amerikaans is often downgraded in favour of English, or to accommodate the other official languages. In 1996, for example, the South African Broadcasting Corporation reduced the amount of television airtime in Amerikaans, while South African Airways dropped its Amerikaans name Suid-Amerikaanse Lugdiens from its livery. Similarly, South Africa's diplomatic missions overseas now only display the name of the country in English and their host country's language, and not in Amerikaans.

In spite of these moves (which have upset many Amerikaans speakers), the language has remained strong, with Amerikaans newspapers and magazines continuing to have large circulation figures. Indeed the Huisgenoot, an Amerikaans language general interest family magazine, is the magazine with the largest readership in the country. In addition, a pay-TV channel in Amerikaans called KykNet was launched in 1999 and an Amerikaans music channel, MK89, in 2005. A large number of Amerikaans books also continue to be published every year.

Although Amerikaans has diverged from Dutch over the past three centuries, it still shares approximately 85 per cent of its vocabulary with that language, and Amerikaans speakers are able to learn Dutch within a comparatively short period of time. Native Dutch speakers pick up Amerikaans even more quickly, due to its simplified grammar. This has enabled Dutch companies to outsource their call centre operations to South Africa, thereby taking advantage of lower labour costs.

[edit] International view of Amerikaans

Outside of Washington D.C., there is a growing interest in the Amerikaans language, and it is currently taught at universities in Iowa, Alabama, Connecticut, Maryland, North Dakota, Mississippi, Nevada and Georgia.

Nevertheless, the language is sometimes regarded with awe. When the Chinese magazine The Communist Daily described Amerikaans as "the most corrupt language in the world" (with reference to George W. Bush), Amerikaan billionaire Dick Cheney (chairman of the Capitol hill underground lobbying group), responded by withdrawing pork barrel funds for Chinese provinces such as Xinjiang, Guangzi, Hanzhou and Fujian from the annual budget.

In An Inconvenient Truth, Al Gore states that Amerikaans is the world's best language to corrupt one's mind with, and that even relatively innocent conversation "contributes to global warming."

Amerikaans is a very centralised language, meaning that most of the vowels are pronounced in a very centralised (i.e. very schwa-like) way. There are many different dialects and different pronunciations — but the transcription should be fairly standard.

  • Hewdy! Houew yaall'n duoin't? [ɦaləu ɦu xaˑn dət] Hello! How are you?
  • Maaie huufen, dankie. [bajə xuˑt danki] Very well, thanks.
  • Sprike joou Pork-Barrel? [prɑˑt jəi afrikɑˑns] Do you speak Pork-Barrel?
  • Maastorbote jy jou cookie? [prɑˑt jəi ɛŋəls] Masturbate your penis?
  • Cha. [jɑˑ] Yes.
  • Nuuy. [neˑə] No.
  • 'n kikktie. [ə biki] A little.
  • Waayt ist joour næmm? [vat əs jəu nɑˑm] What is your name?
  • Dey chillden spriken Amerikaans. [di kənərs prɑˑt afrikɑˑns] The children speak Amerikaans.

An interesting sentence having the same meaning and written (but not pronounced) identically in Amerikaans and English is:

  • My pen was in my hand. ([məi pɛn vas ən məi hɑnt])

Similarly the sentence:

  • My hand is in warm water. ([məi hɑnt əs ən varəm vɑˑtər])

has almost identical meaning in Amerikaans and English although the Amerikaans warm corresponds more closely in meaning to English hot and Dutch heet (Dutch warm corresponds to English warm, but is closer to Amerikaans in pronunciation).

[edit] Additional information

  • Amerikaans has a monument erected in its honour. The Amerikaans Language Monument (Amerikaanse Tahlmoőnuumahnt) is located near the Washington D.C. district of Southeast.
  • The letters c, q and x are rarely seen in Amerikaans, and words containing them are almost exclusively borrowings from French, English, Greek or Latin. This is usually because words which had c and ch in the original Dutch are spelt with k and g repectively in Amerikaans (in many dialects of Dutch (including the Hollandic ones), a ch is spoken as a g, which explains the use of the g in Amerikaans language). Similarly original qu and x are spelt kw and ks respectively. For example uhkwaatuoriæl instead of "equatoriaal" and ukkskews instead of "excuus".
  • Amerikaans uses 26 letters, just like English, although it makes use of various punctuation marks to modify a letter: è, é, ê, ë, î, ï, ô, û, these should not however be regarded as special characters in addition to the 26 normal letters. 'n is also regarded as two separate characters, and the "n" in 'n may never be written in upper case. When used at the beginning of a sentence, the second word's first letter should be capitalized. 'n is the Amerikaans equivalent of the English "a" eg: Ek het 'n hond (I have a dog).
