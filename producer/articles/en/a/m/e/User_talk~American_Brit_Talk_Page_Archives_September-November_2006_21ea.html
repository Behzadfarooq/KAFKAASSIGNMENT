[edit] U.S. Route 119

You are incorrect on your assertions. As evidenced through my research through WVDOH and through the King Coal Highway Authority, it is known that the highway will bypass Williamson on an alignment to the east. No other relevant WVDOH information backs your claims that the segment of Corridor G from US 52 to Williamson will be under the inclusion of the King Coal corridor. Seicer (talk) (contribs) 23:13, 19 November 2006 (UTC)

No I am not. It has been printed in newspapers and magazines. I am positive, if I wasnt I would not have added it to the article. H.J. Bellamy 23:22, 19 November 2006 (UTC)
You are incorrect and you have misread. Let's take it apart:
You wrote:
“ The entire section of Corridor G that is merged with US Route 52 will be ugraded to Interstate standards and become part of Interstate 73/74. This expected around 2011. ”
It is clearly cited under King Coal Highway that,
“ The Tolsia Highway is expected to meet the King Coal Highway at West Virginia Route 65 and Corridor G (US 119). [...] The King Coal Highway is defined to run from West Virginia Route 65 and Corridor G (US 119) near Belo to Interstate 77 at its US 52 interchange near Bluefield. ”
It is referenced with:
"King Coal Highway." West Virginia. Department of Transportation. Executive Summary. N.p.: West Virginia Department of Transportation, n.d.
Mitchem, Mike, and David B. Akers. King Coal Highway, I-73/74 Authority. Gilbert: West Virginia Department of Highways, n.d.
The map from the King Coal Highway also does not correspond to your assertion. Unreferenced sources and hearsay should not be included for that very reason. Seicer (talk) (contribs) 23:45, 19 November 2006 (UTC)
