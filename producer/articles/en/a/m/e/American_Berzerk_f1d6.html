American Berserk is a short (approximately 7 minutes long) solo piano composition by the American composer, John Coolidge Adams.

The first performance took place February 25, 2002, at Carnegie Hall, New York City, with Garrick Ohlssohn on the piano. It was commissioned by the Carnegie Hall Corporation and is dedicated to Ohlssohn. The composition is substantially influenced by jazz and bop piano playing.
