American Farmland Trust

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

The American Farmland Trust (AFT) is an organization founded to preserve farmland in the United States and to promote sustainable farming practices.

Farmers and ranchers founded the AFT in 1980, partly in response to the 1979 report of the National Agricultural Lands Study, titled Where Have the Farm Lands Gone? The organization has worked to pass state laws and local ordinances intended to enable the preservation of farmland.

The AFT is governed by a board of directors. Its staff has produced a number of reports on farmland issues, as well as a magazine for members.

As of 2006 the AFT includes:

  • a National Office in Washington, DC
  • nine state or regional offices
  • Farmland Information Center in Northampton, Massachusetts
  • Center for Agriculture in the Environment in Dekalb, Illinois
  • Agriculture Conservation Innovation Center in Columbus, Ohio
  • California Office
