American Foreign Policy Council (AFPC) has played an essential role in the U.S. foreign policy debate since 1982. AFPC is a non-profit organization dedicated to bringing information to those who make or influence the foreign policy of the United States and to assisting world leaders, particularly in the former USSR, with building democracies and market economies.

AFPC is widely recognized as a source of timely, insightful analysis on issues of foreign policy, and works closely with members of the US Congress, the Executive Branch and the policymaking community. It is staffed by noted specialists in foreign and defense policy, and serves as a valuable resource to officials in the highest levels of the U.S. government.

The International Relations Center's Right Web project's profile of the AFPC asserts that it "has consistently pressed for hawkish agendas in U.S. foreign and defense policies" and calls it "one of a number of hardline Washington-based think tanks—like the neoconservative American Enterprise Institute and the Center for Security Policy—that champion a broad war on terror and devote considerable time and resources to publicizing the 'growing threat from Iran.'"
