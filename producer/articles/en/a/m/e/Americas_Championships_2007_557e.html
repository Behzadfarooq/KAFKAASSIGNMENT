The Americas Championships 2007 will be held at Thomas & Mack Center in Las Vegas from August 22 to September 2. It will be the thirteenth staging of the FIBA Americas Championship (The Tournament of the Americas).

It was originally going to be hosted in Venezuela – however, they failed to pay a $1.5 million fee as of August 31, 2006. As such, their hosting privileges were taken away.

Several countries had shown interest in hosting the tournament, including: United States, Chile, Argentina and Puerto Rico. The United States has not hosted it since 1992, Chile has never hosted it, Argentina held it last in 2001 and Puerto Rico last hosted in 2003.

[edit] Competing Nations

The tournament draw took place Wednesday, March 21, in Las Vegas. The draw split the tournament into to two groups[1]:

Group A

Argentina
Mexico
Panama
Puerto Rico
Uruguay

Group B

Brazil
Canada
United States
US Virgin Islands
Venezuela

[edit] Qualification

The first two teams would advance to the 2008 Olympics in Beijing, China. Besides this the 3rd to 5th place finishers would go to participate in the 2008 Basketball Olympic Qualifier, where they will be joined by four European teams, two African teams, two Asian teams and one team from Oceania.
