Amelia Reynolds is a television journalist who works for the BBC's early evening regional programme Look East, famed for her unusual beauty.

Brought up in Fressingfield, Suffolk, Reynolds was educated locally in Suffolk at Stradbroke High School and Thomas Mills High School before attending Exeter University where she studied English and drama. After university she worked for an independent production company in Suffolk and travelled in Africa and Asia reporting for both BBC Radio 4 and the BBC World Service.

She joined Look East in 2000 as a researcher, before moving on to presenting; in 2006 she took over as the female anchor whilst Susie Fowler-Watt was on maternity leave. She has also worked on the "The Dating Channel" by Sky Digital.

Reynolds has been nominated for "The RTS Best Newcomer to Television Journalism - The Mike Read Award", supported by the ITC.
