The American Institute of Biological Sciences (AIBS) [1]is a nonprofit scientific association dedicated to advancing biological research and education. Founded in 1947 as a part of the National Academy of Sciences, AIBS became an independent, member-governed organization in the 1950s. Today, with offices in Washington, DC, and Reston, VA, and a staff of approximately 50, AIBS is sustained by a robust membership of 200 professional societies and scientific organizations representing more than 250,000 individuals.

AIBS advances its mission through:

  • Coalition activities in research, education [2], and public policy [3]
  • Publishing the peer-reviewed journal BioScience [4]
  • Providing scientific peer advisory and review services under contract (including GSA MOBIS) to government agencies and other clients through its SPARS division [5]
  • Convening scientific conferences
  • Managing scientific programs
  • Maintaining a job postings site [6], in collaboration with BioOne [7], with traffic in excess of more than 340,000 users per month

In addition, AIBS manages the project office for the National Ecological Observatory Network [8], assists the National Evolutionary Synthesis Center [9] with education activities, and houses the national office of the Coalition on the Public Understanding of Science [10].
