Can someone please offer suggestions as to how I can make this article neutral?

  • Well, you might have a problem as far as neutrality is concerned, since from your user name you appear to be affiliated with the company. But neutrality isn't the issue so much as notability--please see WP:CORP. It's not enough to announce that this business exists. You have to establish that it holds a unique, verfiably noteworthy place in business today. This is an encyclopedia, not a directory!
As it stands, it still reads far too much like advertising--like brochure copy, actually. Please see WP:SPAM for guidelines on that.
Good luck! In the meantime, I'm restoring the "reads like advertising" tag. --Pleather 22:33, 13 September 2006 (UTC)


I have a couple of general suggestions to improve the article:

- This article is about a specific division of an Oklahoma based insurance company. Perhaps instead consider writing a broader article about American Fidelity Assurance Company itself, and including a subsection about the ecommerce division within that main article. As an analogy, this article is like writing an article about the marketing division of Geico, but not writing an article about Geico itself.

- Assuming you follow my suggestion above, you then should establish some outside notability for American Fidelity Assurance Company. One way to do this is to include verifiable, independent media references about the company. WP:CORP has some guidelines along those lines, and you might want to look at other well-written insurance company articles to see how they go about it.

I think those two tasks will go a long way toward improving the article. Dugwiki 22:27, 20 September 2006 (UTC)
