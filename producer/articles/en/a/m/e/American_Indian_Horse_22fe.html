American Indian Horse

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

The American Indian Horse is essentially any horse of Spanish origin that has evolved to adapt to a particular environment within the Untied States (with the help of man in many cases.) Hence, the title American Indian Horse does not refer to one specific breed; rather, it applies to any breed that has proved itself capable of withstanding a distinct ecotone, whether it be the high plains of the Midwest or the low swamplands of the South.

[edit] History

The history of the American Indian Horse is varied according to which area of the U.S. from which you begin. Its earliest origin is from the Spanish horses which were brought over to the Americas by the conquistadoes. Nowadays, the American Indian Horse Registry, established in 1961, has created five catogaries in which to group the horse. Class A are those with unknown pedigrees, such as BLM horses. Class AA have at least a fifty percent traceable pedigree to distinct American Indian tribe horses. Class M horses have modern type breed blood, such as Quarter Horse and Appaloosa. Class O horses are those horses which follow a distinct bloodline that follows back to specific Indian tribes. Class P is reserved for ponies of Indian type.

[edit] Breed Characteristics

Due to the fact that the American Indian Horse Registry has not just one single breed or stock but rather a group that has developed over time in the Americas from Spanish stock, there is no singular characteristic of the horse. At the most general, the American Indian Horse stands between 13 and 15 hands high and comes in any colour.
