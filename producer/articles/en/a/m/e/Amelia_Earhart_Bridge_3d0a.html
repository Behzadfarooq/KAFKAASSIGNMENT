The Amelia Earhart Bridge is a truss bridge over the Missouri River on U.S. Route 59 between Atchison, Kansas and Buchanan County, Missouri.

It was built in 1937-1939 by the Works Progress Administration and is named for aviator Amelia Earhart, a native of Atchison. It was designed by Sverdrup & Parcel.

It is the topic of an intense preservation debate on whether to replace it with a new four-lane bridge or to keep it and build a second bridge. At night spotlights shine from the top of its two trusses.
