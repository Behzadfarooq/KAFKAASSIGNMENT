Yeah you are right it isn't biased, but I don't have a veryfying know source yet. Dustimagic 00:35, 8 December 2005 (UTC)

I am sympathetic to the assumption that the air marshal(s) acted appropriately in shooting an uncooperative, unstable person who had allegedly said he had a bomb (I'll suppose he referred to his backpack, as I've read in one source), as I've heard a number of administration spokespeople say, but could someone please comment on whether or not it was appropriate to blow up his suitcases as well? What I'm wondering is, if there is a possiblity of a dangerous device, who knows if it's not possibly some biologically active agent? If the packages were "sniffed" or inspected by some device, there might have been a positive result indicating, say, an explosive or biologically active agent, but a negative result doesn't mean that there's *not* something present. And if something was present but hidden from detection and released upon the explosion, it would be stupid (almost bipolar) to blow it up, at least to do so in the open air in an airport. That's just my opinion. Anyone read anything on this in an authoritative media source?

My(non-professional) opinion is that there is no advantage to putting anything biologically active on the plane. Explosives are easier to procure, there's no guarantee that whatever agent you have will even survive the explosion, and an explosion at 30,000 feet will do an awful job of spreading the bug.Attakmint 20:07, 8 December 2005 (UTC)

releasing a nasty virus at an international airport would spread it pretty well.

blowing up the suitcase is a good way to say "we really did think he had a bomb", even if they knew he should not have been shot.

What's the principle that says "never attribute to malice what can be attributed to ignorance"? Like pretty much everybody else on the planet, the Feds have policy and procedure, and part of that probably includes treating this as a bomb threat. I'm not willing to pass judgement on the shooting until the investigation is finished. Oh, and on the disposal of the bags - I know forum posts are hardly authoritative, but I've heard from a forum I frequent that the bags were opened with high pressure water, and that water was used to short out any possible electronics in bombs. So technically, the bags weren't blown up, they were blown open. It still strikes me as convoluted, unnecessary, and somewhat overdramatic to pack a biological agent in a luggage bomb though. Attakmint 11:12, 12 December 2005 (UTC)
