[edit] incorrect math

I've corrected the callout averages, but my edits were reverted. I think the results in the article are incorrect.

Melrose's callouts: 1, 12, 1, 6, 4, 2, 4, 2, 1, 2, and 1. These numbers total 36, for an average of 3.27.

CarrieDee's callouts; 11, 2, 6, 9, 1, 4, 2, 1, 5, 3, and 3. These numbers total 47, for an average of 4.27.

Eugena's callouts: 4, 10, 8, 10, 2, 6, 7, 5, 2, 1, and 2. These numbers total 57, for an average of 5.18.

Without comment, User:CrossingGuard reverted the article to give scores of 3.36, 3.36, and 5.09 (respectively). How can these scores possibly be accurate? -- Mikeblas 04:53, 20 December 2006 (UTC)

I believe callouts do not include the first "entry into the final" callout, because that's random. In addition, you didn't include the final callout (from when Eugena was eliminated). 2+6+9+1+4+2+1+5+3+3+1/11 = 3.36 = CariDee. 12+1+6+4+2+4+2+1+2+1+2/11 = 3.36 = Melrose. 10+8+10+2+6+7+5+2+1+2/10 = 5.3 = Eugene. HOWEVER (and this is a big however), we really don't have any proof that Tyra calls them out in order of performance, although getting called out first is the best, apparently. Thus...well, it's just statistics. They might even be baseless. SKS2K6 05:12, 20 December 2006 (UTC)
I didn't include those because the page says "(excluding semifinals and the last judging panel)". Did I skip the wrong set of numbers? -- Mikeblas 05:26, 20 December 2006 (UTC)
The "episode 1" callout, in which Melrose was first, was the last semifinal callout. Although it was the first hour of the two-hour premiere, it is called Episode 1 (because according to the CW, it counts as a two-episode premiere). SKS2K6 06:16, 20 December 2006 (UTC)
Oh, and I believe the last callout is when Melrose and Caridee were final two. SKS2K6 06:17, 20 December 2006 (UTC)
Thanks for the explanation! If these are random as you suggest, why are they included in the chart? Is this curious definition of "semifinals" widely understood? -- Mikeblas 12:44, 20 December 2006 (UTC)
The chart states: In episode 1, the pool of 21 girls was reduced to 13 who moved on to the main competition. However, this first call-out does not reflect their performance that first week. That's all I know. Like I said earlier, there's honestly no proof that Tyra calls them out from best to worst. She hinted that the first call out equals the best. And the last person is obviously the one kicked off. But in between...who knows? That's why I think many of the ANTM statistics on both wiki pages are pointless, or perhaps even wrong. SKS2K6 22:50, 20 December 2006 (UTC)
I thought I did the math right. I guess I got it wrong with Eugena, but I got it right with CariDee and Melrose. I added the "excluding semi-finals and final judging" thing to try and tell people that the first call-outs and the finals were not included. I guess that didn't really work. CrossingGuard 08:48, 23 December 2006 (UTC)
