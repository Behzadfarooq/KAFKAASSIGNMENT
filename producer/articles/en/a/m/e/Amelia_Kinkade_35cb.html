Amelia Kinkade is an actress, dancer, choreographer, and internationally renown "animal communicator." She is best remembered in her film career as "Angela," in the Night of the Demons film series. Other credits include appearances in Girls Just Want to Have Fun and Roadhouse. She also appeared on TV sitcoms such as The Golden Girls, and had a principle roll as 'Vivian' on the soap opera, The Young and the Restless.

In 2001 Kinkade's book, Straight From the Horse's Mouth - How to Talk to Animals and Get Answers was published by Crown Books. In this book she describes techniques which she states will teach readers to communicate or speak with their own pets. Her second book, The Language of Miracles - A Celebrated Psychic Teaches You to Talk to Animals (with forward by Dr. Bernie S. Siegel) was published by New World Library in 2006. This book discusses the scientific evidence such as quantum physics that help explain telepathic communication. Kinkade's assertion that this is a skill that can be learned is supported by the anecdotes from her students that are featured in the second book. Several of her students have gone on to become professional animal communicators themselves, including Heidi Wright in the USA, and Wynter Worsthorn in South Africa. Amelia Kinkade is an international speaker who lectures and teaches in 25-35 cities a year, in the US, Mexico, Canada, England, Scotland, Germany, Norway, Switzerland, Austria, and South Africa. Amelia was featured in the book, The 100 Top Psychics in America (Simon and Schuster).

Her claimed unique abilities have been the focus of countless magazines and newspaper articles world-wide including The New York Times, The Chicago Tribune, ABC News, The Boston North Shore Sunday, [[The London Sunday News of The World,]] Good Housekeeping, Cat Fancy, Dog Fancy, New Woman in England, Bilt de Frau in Germany and the cover of the Freitseit Kurier in Vienna. Miss Kinkade has appeared on television programs such as The View, The Other Half, The Ellen Degeneres Show, Extra, VH1, Jenny Jones, The BBC News, Carte Blanche in South Africa, and numerous news broadcasts in the US, Europe, and Australia.

Miss Kinkade states that her true passion is assisting animal rescue organizations in Africa who create safe havens for elephants, cheetah, and White Lions. In 2002, she was honored to accept invitations to Buckingham Palace to work with the household cavalry of Queen Elizabeth II and to “whisper” with the hunting horses of Prince Charles. Web site: www.AmeliaKinkade.com.

[edit] Personal life

Amelia Kinkade is the niece of Emmy Award-winning actress Rue McClanahan.

Kinkade is friends with movie star Amy Smart, and mentions Smart in her second book.
