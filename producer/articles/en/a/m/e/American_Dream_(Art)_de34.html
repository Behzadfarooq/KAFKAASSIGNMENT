American Dream Art uses images and symbols of American life and culture to surrealistic or horrific effect in order to critique the putative concept of the American Dream or to outline a deeper existentialist truth. Principal protagonists of the movement include the beat generation of Kerouac, Burroughs et al. filmmaker Stanley Kubrick and more recently, filmmaker David Lynch.

American Dream Art's roots lie in the French and German surrealism of the early twentieth century. American Dream Art is often associated with an aversion to consumerism, an underlying unease at the covert forces that control and maintain economic or racial inequalities between people and a tacit acceptance of the inherent evil in mankind. Classic motifs of American Dream Art include the sinister and symbolic cowboy in David Lynch's "Mulholland Drive" and Stanley Kubrick's use of the rainbow in a stylised New York City at Christmas in "Eyes Wide Shut".

American Dream Art has crossed the Atlantic to the UK, where it has found voice in many ethnic writers. The Magical realism Movement of Author Salman Rushdie and the Teen-Dream Realist Movement of Screenwriter Asheem Singh pay direct homage to American Dream Art.
