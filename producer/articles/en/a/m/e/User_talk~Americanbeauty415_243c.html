Census data indicate that the number of children in interracial families grew from less than one half million in 1970 to about two million in 1990. In 1990, for interracial families with one White partner, the other parent was Black for about 20 percent of all children, the other parent was Asian for 45 percent, and the other parent was American Indian and Alaska Native for about 34 percent.

[edit] Your recent comments and questions

Hello Americanbeauty. Recently I've been noticing a number of edits from you where you assert that User:Media anthro is posing as an editor. I feel that you are in rather significant confusion about our terminology. Everybody who edits Wikipedia, including you and Media anthro, are known as editors. Administrators (also called sysops) are a distinct group of editors who the Wikipedia community have assessed and approved to have access to extra tools such as block/unblock, delete/undelete and protect/unprotect. I feel that you may be confusing editors with administrators. Media Anthro never posed as an administrator; he is, in fact, an editor. When you have a question such as this, you don't need to ask it in multiple places. [1] [2] [3] [4] Asking it in one of those places would have been sufficient.

Additionally, I noticed here you accused User:SqueakBox of being Media athro's sockpuppet. This is a serious allegation and a violation of assume good faith and no personal attacks if you do not have substantial evidence to back it up. SqueakBox is a long-term editor with over 20,000 edits and it is inappropriate to make such allegations in such a casual manner without citing evidence. Please don't do that again.

In future, if you wish to know if an editor is an administrator, you may view the list of administrators or you may search the admin group on user log.

Finally, you chastised Media Anthro for replying to your message on his talk page rather than on yours. It is, in fact, completely acceptable for an editor to reply to messages in either location. Many editors, including many administrators, will reply to messages left on their talk page in the same place because they prefer to keep conversations in tact. For the same reason it was acceptable for Squeakbox to also comment on Media anthro's talk page. There is no hard rule for where to reply to messages. If you leave someone a message, it is best to watchlist their talk page for a little while so you can see if they reply there.

I hope this information may help to straighten out some of your confusion. If not, please feel free to let me know and I will try to help. Sarah Ewart 10:09, 18 December 2006 (UTC)
