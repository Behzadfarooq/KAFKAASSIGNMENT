This was the part that was not restored, since it was embeded in the {{db}} tag: This is a bogus, non-existent organization. It has no members, conducts no meetings, holds no tournaments or events. It's claim to have some connection with the 1857 organization named "American Chess Association" with Paul Morphy as its champion is pure fiction.

Basically, during the period 1995-1999 Stan Vaughan repeatedly used a trick of forming a corporation in Nevada with the same name as a well established and well known organization. He would then claim that he WAS that other organization. In this way, Vaughan formed the "World Chess Federation, Inc.", the "Nevada State Chess Association, Inc." and the "American Chess Association, Inc." He also formed the "Nevada Libertarian Party, Inc." Vaughan became president of all these corporations. He claimed the right to declare himself World Chess Champion, American Chess Champion and Nevada Chess Champion. He also threatened suit against the real World Chess Federation ("FIDE") when it scheduled a tournament for the real World Chess Championship in Las Vegas in 1999.

Stan Vaughan filed a lawsuit in Nevada Superior Court entitled Stan Vaughan vs. USCF. The result was that Stan Vaughan lost the cases and the court issued an injunction barring Stan Vaughan from claiming any relationship with the USCF.

The judge made it clear to Stan Vaughan that he would be spending time in jail if he violated this court order. As a result, Stan Vaughan dropped out and has not been seen in the world of chess again since 1999. 18:26, 29 August 2005 . . User:Sam Sloan

For example, has this organization ever been mentioned in any book, magazine or newspaper article? The answer is no.

Has this orgamization ever held a chess tournament or event of any kind whatever? The answe is no.

Does this organization have a physical premises or meeting place such as a club that meets weekly? The answer is no.

You can think of similar questions. The answer will always be no.

Although the organoization became legally defunct in 1998, Bill Brock has discovered that it was recently reinstated as a corporate entity in Nevada. (Thank you Bill Brock.) However, the only officers are the wife and the two minor children of Stan Vaughan, its "champion". As long as it has never held a chess event, it is not notable and should be speedily deleted.
