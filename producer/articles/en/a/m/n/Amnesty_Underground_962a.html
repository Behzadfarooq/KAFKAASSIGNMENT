Amnesty Underground[1] is a New England band which has been known for their heavy, groove oriented sound, their on-stage antics, and their presence within the New England Community. Originally a side project thought up by bassist Grimm, from the Mid-90's band The Heretics, Amnesty Underground has played hundreds of shows over the past nine years, and have released many CDs, including two full lengths (see Below), as well as several at home experiments. More notably, they made national headlines on January 20th, 2005, when they were booked to play an Anti-Bush show, along side Connecticut bands They Hate Us and Kimono Draggin' which was moved last minute due to audience reaction.[2] During the later part of 2006, the band was planning a large-scale tour in the North East, with a framework of shows revolving around New York City, Boston, and Providence. This "Death of the Underground Tour" will be in support of their new CD.

[edit] Shifting Members

As the Side project took center stage, and Grimm Grimmoire eventually left the Heretics. From there the Lineup has sifted through drummers and Guitarists, with the occasional Lead Singer (though Grimm does most of the singing when a lead is not in place), and has eventually settled on the line-up which it is today: Augie on Drums, Rob the Dentist on guitar, and Grimm on Bass. [3] With their upcoming tour in the works, Amnesty Underground has decided to stay with the three man front, and are not actively searching for a new man to front the band.
