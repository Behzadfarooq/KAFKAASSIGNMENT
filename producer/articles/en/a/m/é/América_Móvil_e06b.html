América Móvil (NYSE: AMX, BMV: AMX) is a publicly traded wireless communications company that provides services to over 108 million wireless subscribers in the Americas, but primarily in Latin America as of December 31st 2006. The company has been a venture of Carlos Slim Helú, the richest man in Mexico and third richest in the world according to Forbes.

World-wide headquarters are located in Mexico City, Mexico. It was ranked number 1 Information Technology company in 2005 by the Business Week magazine [1]. On February 25, 2007, it became the biggest corporation in Latin America by market capitalization, surpassing giants like Petrobras.[1]

Its Mexican subsidiary, Telcel is the largest mobile operator in Mexico, commanding a market share in excess of 80 per cent. In the United States, it operates under the trademark Tracfone, where they claim to be the leading national pre-paid wireless service.

It operates under the trademark Claro in Brazil, Chile, the Dominican Republic, El Salvador, Guatemala, Honduras, Nicaragua and Peru.

It operates under the trademark COMCEL in Colombia, and as CTI Movil in Argentina, Uruguay and Paraguay.

Last 15 November 2005, America Movil signed an international pact with Vodafone Group to jointly deliver various international services.
