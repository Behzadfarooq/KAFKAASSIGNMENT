First South Asian FM Radio Station in America Debuts in Dallas/Ft. Worth

Radio Salaam Namaste 104.9 offers wide range of programming for fast growing population segment


Dallas, Texas, February 5, 2007 – The “grand opening” of Radio Salaam Namaste 104.9 FM will be celebrated at a gathering of public officials, media, employees, advertisers and friends at the Hyatt Hotel in downtown Dallas on Saturday, February 10, 2007, according to Jaipal Reddy, president of Radio Salaam Namaste. This is a historic radio broadcasting event since Radio Salaam Namaste 104.9 is the first FM station in America to target the fast-growing South Asian community, which includes the countries of India, Pakistan, Sri Lanka, Nepal and Bangladesh.

Under the banner of Everest Heights, Radio Salaam Namaste (RSN) initially broadcasted on KZMP 1540 AM in 2006 exactly a year ago, as a result of a program brokerage agreement with licensee Liberman Broadcasting. However, the overwhelming response by listeners and advertisers in the South Asian community compelled Everest Heights to upgrade its signal quality and coverage by moving to the FM side of the dial on 104.9. Currently RSN airs programs in 8 South Asian languages on the new FM frequency. The extensive programming selection ranges from Songs by Request to News and informational shows like ‘Aapki Sehat’ (Health forum), giving the listeners a taste of everything they love.

“We aim to present not only ‘Desi’ ideas, concepts and ideals to our listeners through a variety of entertainment and informational programs, but are also always seeking to improve links with other communities within Dallas Metrolplex to reflect the cultural diversity of the area through FREE public service announcements”, said Mohammad Abbas, senior VP of 104.9 FM. “The name ‘Desi’, is derived from the word Des meaning a specific Space or Homeland, which for us is the entire Indian Sub-continent. Our object is to analyze our practices and heritage and so ensure a better understanding of the ‘desi’ culture and traditions with in the United States, explained Ambreen Hasnat, Executive VP of 104.9 FM.
