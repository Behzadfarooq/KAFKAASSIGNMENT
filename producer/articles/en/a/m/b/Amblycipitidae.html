Amblycipitidae is a family of catfishes, commonly known as torrent catfishes.

Members of this family feature a long cigar-shaped or loach-like body with four barbels. The lateral line is poorly developed or absent. The dorsal fin is covered by thick skin. An adipose fin is present, but in some species is confluent with the caudal fin.

These kinds of fishes can be found in swift freshwater streams in Southern and Eastern Asia, including Pakistan across northern India to Malaysia, China, Korea, and Japan.
