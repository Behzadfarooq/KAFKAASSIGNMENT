Amber Val Stachowski (born March 14, 1983 in Mission Viejo, California) is an American water polo player for the UCLA Bruins and the US National Team, who won the bronze medal at the 2004 Athens Olympics. Her position is center defender.

[edit] High school and college

At Capistrano Valley High School [1], Stachowski was a three-time First-Team All-American (1999-2001) and 2001 California Interscholastic Federation Player of the Year. In club play she was a member of the 2001 Golden West team that won the Winter Junior Nationals, and was named MVP of the tournament.

As a UCLA freshman in 2002, Stachowski scored a goal in her first collegiate game against UC Irvine, and recorded first collegiate career hat trick against Stanford. In her first year, she had 29 goals on 43 attempts.

[edit] International and Olympics

Olympic medal record
Women's water polo
Bronze 2004 Athens Team

At the fourth FINA Junior World Women's Water Polo Championships in 2001, UCLA freshman Amber Stachowski led the US to a gold medal. Stachowski led the US team with four goals in the 10-9 overtime victory over Australia. In 2003-2004, Amber Stachowski redshirted to train with the United States National Team and play in the 2004 Summer Olympics in Athens, Greece. After her Team USA earned first place in 2003 Pan American Games and 2003 FINA World Championships, Stachowski scored two goals in the Olympics in games against Russia and Australia, helping win the bronze medal for the USA.

[edit] Personal

  • In 2003, Stachowski sustained a concussion in a motor vehicle accident on a Los Angeles freeway, which kept her out of the water for two months. She recovered and qualified for the Olympic team 6 months later. [2]
  • The Stachowski family is a water polo dynasty. Amber's father, Mike, is a water polo coach at TRAP Water Polo Academy in Orange County, California. Her sister Aimee is an All-American water polo player at UC Santa Barbara, and competed with the 2005 US women's water polo team at the FINA World League Championship. Her brother Travis is a high school senior goalkeeper, attending Pepperdine University in Fall 2006, and has played for the men's Junior National Team since 2005.
