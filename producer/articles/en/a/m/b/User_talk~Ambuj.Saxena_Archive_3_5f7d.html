coin

Yeah, though it says that the script is Devnagari, the script on the coin has uncanny similarity with Bengali script. May be because it was from Sri lanka. There is some dispute, but often it is sain "Sinhala" came from "Vijaysingha", an Indian prince who arrived at the present Sri Lanka. I have read in "History of Bengali literature" (in our school texts) that this Vijaysingha was from Bengal-Orisa region. In fact, there is a famous line of a poem (I forgot the writer) Banglar chele bijaysingho helay Lanka korilo joy, meaning, Vijaysingha, son of Bengal, won Lanka easily.

This line is not used to portray the history though. It is rather used in an ironical sense. The usual sayings of the Bengalis - that we were famous in past, that we did so and so - this line is satirically used to refer to this attitude. However, even this idiom is gradually disappearing from usage.

Fair use

Fair use is a very complicated issue on WP. Most of it is caused my people who do not understand what is fair use. I have very tough time explaining users what it is and what is required. I myself took about half a year to realize some of the complexities of fair use. For a fact, I still don't know much about it. Also, fair use images are very convenient; the temptation to use them is extremely high. Most of the time no free alternative could be obtained. Also, WP does not have a short & sweet & easy to read tutorial/intoroduction about fair use and other copyright issues.

I remember reading somewhere a post that fair use should not have "download a high resolution version." And I agree. If I spot something like this, I would donwload it, use some picture program, scale it down and re-upload under the same name.

The magnitude is humongously giantnormous :) I spent a couple of days looking through some pics on WP... and I almost want to cry. Fair use logos are spammed everyhere, fair use screenshots are claimed public domain, magazine covers illustrate magazine models, etc etc etc. I haven't seen this week a fair use pic with a rationale. A gazillion of pictures don't have sources, descriptions, or have wrong copyright info. I would not be too surprised if after a very careful cleaning up, WP would be left with 15-20% of all the pics it has now.

What do I suggest? I was asking myself this question... The magnitude is simply... overwhelming. I think the biggest problem is education of users. They are very quick to shout "Copyright paranoia!" You could be spending years on removing fair use pics and they will still add them back (either because they don't believe WP should be so strict on fair use or because of simple ignorance). So I have a ton of half-baked ideas... For example, sweet & short tutorial/intorduction or exam/test/quiz.

But I don't think that is really what you are asking (I feel I need to vent a little, sorry :)). Renata 10:21, 19 May 2006 (UTC)

Thanks for the fixes. Some tips:
  • If creating any new text make sure that you select the text and click on Text>Unflow in the file menu. If the unflow is not done, the text won't be rendered by mediawiki.
  • Always use generic fonts such as Sans, Serif, Fantasy and Cursive. Sans is ideal for diagrams.
  • If tracing a document from a bitmap, ensure that the bitmap is stored on a new layer and locked. After completion, delete the layer which contains the bitmap.
  • Add metadata to the document in File>Document Preferences

Indian english

Indian English is modelled on British English. Just to brief you on what is considered correct for IE spellings:

  • -our vs -or (colour/color) : Use -our
  • -lled vs -led (labelled/labeled) : use -lled
  • (-ise vs -ize) (italicise/italicize) : both are acceptable. The -ise is commonly used in BE, --ize is used in academic publications, and by AmE almost exclusively. --Nichalp 08:38, 28 May 2006 (UTC)
