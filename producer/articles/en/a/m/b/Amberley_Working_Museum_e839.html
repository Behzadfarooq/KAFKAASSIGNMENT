Amberley Working Museum is a museum in Amberley, Arundel, West Sussex, England. It was founded in 1979 and is a 36 acre (146,000 mÂ²) open air museum, next to Amberley railway station dedicated to the industrial heritage of the south east. The museum is built on the site of a former chalk quarry where the chalk was converted into lime for use in mortar & cement.
