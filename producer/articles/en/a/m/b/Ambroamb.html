The Ambroamb (full name Ambroamb Amauro) is part of Western Latvian mythology, taking similar ideas from tales of The Holy Grail and other mythological items.

[edit] Origins

The Ambroamb is, in mythology, a chalice that posses the power to offer immortality, or eternal life. It is a little known name for a common idea of mythology. There are few tales remaning of the Latvian chalice, but what we do have tells us the cup was, in other words, the Holy Grail, but in this case the cup is black with waters like blood, or the blood of Jesus. As he was crucified, it is said that humble Mary Magdelene caught the blood in a black goblet. This was, over many years, taken slowly to the area we now know as Latvia, until knights from afar came to find it (possibly King Arthur and his Knights of the Round Table?) but, in the end, their quest failed and they journeyed home.


[edit] Ideas sorrounding the Ambroamb

Most modern historians agree that the Ambroamb is the Holy Grail and nothing more. However, more radical historians have said that it is more than a mere grail. Ideas suggested have included:

  • The bloodline of Jesus (see The Da Vinci Code)
  • A political allagory relating to the state of Latvia at the time the legend was written (date unknown)
  • A sacrifical table

On the other hand, many suggest the Ambroamb is not even real, but, as in many myths, pure fiction.

[edit] Discovery

The Ambroamb myths were dicovered by Professor John Freeman and his friend Professor Lewis Miles. Both professors were English and were on a dig in Western Latvia before discovering scrolls in tatters, revealing the tales.
