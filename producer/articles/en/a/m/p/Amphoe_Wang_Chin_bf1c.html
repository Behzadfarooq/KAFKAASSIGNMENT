Wang Chin (Thai: วังชิ้น) is a district (Amphoe) in the southern part of Phrae Province, northern Thailand.

[edit] History

Originally the area of the district was part of Mueang Lampang district. In 1930 it was reassigned to Long district, Phrae province. 1938 the minor district (King Amphoe) Wang Chin was established, which was upgraded to a full district in 1958.

[edit] Geography

Neighboring districts are (from the north clockwise) Long and Den Chai of Phrae Province, Si Satchanalai of Sukhothai Province, Thoen, Sop Prap and Mae Tha of Lampang Province.

The Wiang Kosai National Park is located in Wang Chin district.

[edit] Administration

The district is subdivided into 7 communes (tambon), which are further subdivided into 77 villages (muban). Wang Chin is a township (thesaban tambon) and covers parts of tambon Wang Chin. There are further 7 Tambon administrative organizations (TAO).
