Thai Charoen (Thai: ไทยเจริญ) is a district of Yasothon Province in northeastern Thailand.

[edit] History

Thai Charoen, formerly a commune (tambon) of Loeng Nok Tha, was established as a minor district (king amphoe) on April 1, 1992, comprising 5 communes formerly of Loeng Nok Tha.

On October 11, 1997, Thai Charoen was raised to district (amphoe) status, becoming Yasothon's 9th and Thailand's 784th district.

[edit] Geography

Neighboring districts are (from the south clockwise) Pa Tio, Kut Chum, Loeng Nok Tha, of Yasothon Province, Senangkhanikhom and Mueang Amnat Charoen of Amnat Charoen Province.

[edit] Primary occupations

[edit] Important products

  • Jasmine rice

[edit] Administration

The district is subdivided into 5 communes (tambon), which comprise 48 villages (muban).

  1. Thai Charoen (ไทยเจริญ)
  2. Nam Kham (น้ำคำ)
  3. Som Pho (ส้มผ่อ)
  4. Kham Toei (คำเตย)
  5. Kham Phai (คำไผ่)
