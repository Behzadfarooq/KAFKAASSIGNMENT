Mueang Trang (Thai: เมืองตรัง) is the capital district (Amphoe Mueang) of Trang Province, Thailand.

[edit] History

Mueang Trang became the capital district of Trang Province in 1915, when the capital was moved inland from Kantang, which was too prone to flooding.

[edit] Geography

Neighboring districts are (from the south clockwise) Na Yong, Yan Ta Khao, Kantang, Sikao, Wang Wiset, Huai Yot of Trang Province and Si Banphot of Phatthalung Province.

[edit] Administration

The district is subdivided into 15 communes (tambon), which are further subdivided into 118 villages (muban). The city (thesaban nakhon) Trang covers the whole tambon Thap Thiang. The township (thesaban tambon) Khlong Teng covers parts of tambon Na Tham Nuea. There are further 14 Tambon administrative organizations (TAO).
