Mueang Chachoengsao (Thai: เมืองฉะเชิงเทรา) is the capital district (Amphoe Mueang) of Chachoengsao Province, central Thailand.

[edit] History

Mueang Chachoengsao district was established in 1896. The present district office is located on the right bank of the Bang Pakong River in Tambon Na Mueang.

[edit] Geography

Neighboring districts are (from the north clockwise) Bang Nam Priao, Khlong Khuean, Bang Khla, Ban Pho of Chachoengsao Province, Bang Bo of Samut Prakan Province, Lat Krabang and Nong Chok of Bangkok.

The important water resource is the Bang Pakong River and Khlong Nakhon Nueang Khet.

[edit] Administration

The district is subdivided into 19 communes (tambon), which are further subdivided into 187 villages (muban). The town (thesaban mueang)) Chachoengsao covers the whole tambon Na Mueang and parts of Ban Mai, Bang Tinpet, Wang Takhian and So Thon. The township (thesaban tambon) Khlong Nakhon Nueang Khet covers parts of the same-named tambon. There are further 18 Tambon administrative organizations (TAO).
