Hua Taphan (Thai: หัวตะพาน) is a district (Amphoe) of Amnat Charoen Province, northeastern Thailand.

[edit] History

The minor district (King Amphoe) was established on March 15 1967, when it was split off from Amnat Charoen district. It was upgraded to a full district on November 17 1971. In 1993 it was one of the district which formed the new province Amnat Charoen.[1]

[edit] Geography

Neighboring districts are (from the north clockwise) Mueang Amnat Charoen and Lue Amnat of Amnat Charoen Province, Muang Sam Sip and Khueang Nai of Ubon Ratchathani Province, and Kham Khuean Kaeo and Pa Tio of Yasothon Province.

[edit] Administration

The district is subdivided into 8 communes (tambon), which are further subdivided into 85 villages (muban). Hua Taphan is a township (thesaban tambon) which covers parts of the tambon Hua Taphan. Nong Kaeo and Rattana Wari. There are further 7 Tambon administrative organizations (TAO).
