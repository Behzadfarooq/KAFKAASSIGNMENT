Ban Phaeng (Thai: บ้านแพง) is a district (Amphoe) in the province Nakhon Phanom in the northeast of Thailand.

[edit] Geography

Neighboring districts are (from the south clockwise) Tha Uthen, Si Songkhram and Na Thom of Nakhon Phanom Province, and Bueng Khong Long of Nong Khai Province. To the east across the Mekong river is the Laotian province Khammouan.

[edit] History

Originally the area belonged to the Mueang Chaiburi, now part of the Tha Uthen district.

The minor district (King amphoe) was established in 1948 by splitting it from Tha Uthen district. In 1952 it was elevated to full district status.

1956 the sanitary district (sukhaphiban) Ban Phaeng was established, which was upgraded to a township (thesaban tambon) in 1999.

[edit] Administrative divisions

The district is subdivided into 6 communes (tambon), which are further subdivided into 75 villages (muban). Ban Phaeng itself has township (thesaban tambon) status and covers part of the tambon Ban Phaeng. There are further 5 tambon Tambon administrative organization (TAO).
