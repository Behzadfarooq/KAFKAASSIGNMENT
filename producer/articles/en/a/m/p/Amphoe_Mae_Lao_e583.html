Mae Lao (Thai: แม่ลาว) is a district (Amphoe) in the central part of Chiang Rai Province, northern Thailand.

[edit] History

The area of Mae Lao district was separated from Mueang Chiang Rai district and established a minor district (King Amphoe) on May 31st, 1993. It was upgraded to a full district on December 5th, 1996.

[edit] Geography

Neighboring districts are (from the west clockwise) Mae Suai, Mueang Chiang Rai and Phan of Chiang Rai Province.

[edit] Administration

The district is subdivided into 5 communes (tambon), which are further subdivided into 63 villages (muban). There are two townships (thesaban tambon) - Mae Lao covers parts of tambon Dong Ma Da, and Pa Ko Dam parts of tambon Pa Ko Dam and Chom Mok Kaeo. There are further 5 Tambon administrative organizations (TAO).
