Phu Pha Man (Thai: ภูผาม่าน) is the northwesternmost district (Amphoe) of Khon Kaen Province, northeastern Thailand.

[edit] History

The district was established as a minor district (King Amphoe) on July 14 1981 with territory split off from Chum Phae district. The administration started operation on September 1 1981, and on October 1 1981 the district office building was opened. 1994 the minor district was upgraded to a full district.

[edit] Geography

Neighboring districts are (from the north clockwise) Phu Kradueng of Loei Province, Chum Phae of Khon Kaen Province, Khon San of Chaiyaphum Province and Nam Nao of Phetchabun Province.

[edit] Administration

The district is subdivided into 5 communes (tambon), which are further subdivided into 41 villages (muban). Phu Pha Man is a township (thesaban tambon) which covers parts of tambon Phu Pha Man and Non Dong. There are further 5 Tambon administrative organizations (TAO).
