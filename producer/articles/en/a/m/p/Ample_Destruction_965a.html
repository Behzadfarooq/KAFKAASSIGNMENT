Ample Destruction is the first studio album released by American power metal band Jag Panzer, released in 1984. It is a very influential album in American power metal, Jag Panzer being one of the first bands in America to alter a basic heavy metal sound to bring about the elements in power metal. Originally released on Azra Records, a small independent label, it received poor marketing from the label, and thus became only an underground hit amongst the metal community, and was bootlegged repeatedly. The band lost Conklin and Tafolla soon after the release to other projects, and the band went through several line-up changes until finally reuniting in a semi-stable form in 1997, with the release of The Fourth Judgement.

[edit] Re-issues

The album was re-issued officially twice by two different companies, though the tracklistings were the same, with three bonus tracks; "Black Sunday", "Eyes of the Night" and "Lying Deceiver".
