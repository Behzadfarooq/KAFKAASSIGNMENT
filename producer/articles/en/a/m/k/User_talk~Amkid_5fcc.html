[edit] Re:Admin

Yes, users can become administrators (see WP:RFA for the full details). However, most candidates are regular contributors (20+ edits per day) for at least 4 months (see the standards section of WP:RFA) and have an absolutely spotless record - no vandalism warnings, etcetera. Standards are set quite high these days!! I'd advise that you wait at least 3 months before nominating yourself and involve yourself in activities such as articles for deletion, miscellany for deletion, and reverting vandalism, if you're serious about it. At Wikipedia, adminship isn't an honor of any kind: it's just a bunch of extra chores (commonly referred to as "the mop and the flamethrower"). If you have any other questions, please feel free to contact me! Have a good day! :) Srose (talk) 02:13, 29 September 2006 (UTC)

[edit] Re:Talk page

Generally, users should not create extra talk pages unless they are archives or for particular, acceptable subjects. For example, I know of several established users who put all incivil remarks made toward them on a subpage (like User talk:Amrykid/incivil), or put all of their Signpost deliveries (see the one I get on my talk page) on a subpage, like User talk:Amrykid/Signpost. Creating a sub-talkpage to talk to your friends or something like that is not acceptable. It's also generally not a good idea to create a sub-talk page for vandalism warnings. Archives are also acceptable: see the Werdnabot header at the top of my talk page. Have a good day! :) Srose (talk) 15:16, 4 October 2006 (UTC)

<div style="position:absolute; z-index:100; right:10px; top:10px;" class="metadata" >I am currently: {{#switch:{{{1}}}
 | online=<span style="color: green;">online [[Image:ButtonGreen.png|40px]]</span>
 | busy=<span style="color: #FFAA00;">busy [[Image:ButtonYellow.png|40px]]</span>
 | away=<span style="color: red;">offline [[Image:ButtonRed.png|40px]]</span>
}}</div>

3) Then create User:Amrykid/Status2.

4) Add this html code:

{{User:Amrykid/StatusChange2|online}}

5) You are basically done now. Whenever you want to change your status (green, yellow, or red light), just go to the /Status2 page and then change the words "online" to "busy" to get a yellow traffic light, or "away" for a red traffic light.

[edit] Just a reminder: Wikipedia is an encyclopedia

Hi, Amrykid.

I've been watching your contributions for the past month or so. You seem to have grasped wikicode fairly well, judging by your userpage and subpages.

I would like to take this opportunity to remind you that Wikipedia is an encyclopedia first and foremost. If you would like to be an editor here, you should edit articles, like China, Charles Darwin, Fish, and Chocolate, just to give a few examples. You should not merely edit your user page and your friends' talk pages. I see that you and your friends are still using talk pages occasionally to communicate. While it's great that you've cut down on it, I would suggest that you use a website like MySpace.com or livejournal.com to keep up with each other. There are also instant messanger services like the chat feature on Gmail and AOL Instant Messanger.

You need to start contributing to our encyclopedia. Wikipedia is not a place to design a page about yourself (AKA your userpage) and to talk to your friends (AKA your talk pages). Our mission is to build an encyclopedia. Find a topic that interests you, type it in the search bar, and find an article on it. Improve that article: fix the grammar or the information. You can even revert vandalism (go to Recent changes and then Recent changes patrol).

Editors in good standing are beginning to become concerned that you and several of your friends are using server space without contributing to the encyclopedia. With your knowledge of Wikicode, I think you could make large and valuable contributions to the encyclopedic content we host. Srose (talk) 17:56, 7 October 2006 (UTC)
