[edit] Reference Desk

Please consider finding an area of Wikipedia in which to be constructive, instead of clogging up the Reference Desk with silly questions and responses to questions (I wouldn't call them answers). Wikipedia is not a forum or a messageboard. Wikipedia:Community Portal may give you some ideas on where you could best contribute. Regards, Proto t c 15:38, 10 January 2006 (UTC)

Regarding my question about sneezes, I was genuinely wondering if there was a real term. Then I thought that there might not be, so I invented one. Kid Apathy 15:39, 10 January 2006 (UTC)
As for 'jumping down your neck' - often, the Reference Desk is the first place people visit when they come to Wikipedia. It is important to keep things courteous, helpful and relevant, as the responses visitors recieve reflect upon Wikipedia as a whole. If someone has a problem with their PSP, describing them as 'stupid screwy breaking things' is neither helpful, nor relevant. Try to imagine what it would be like if you posted a proper question that you needed answering to a website, and that was the kind of response you got. Would you bother with that website again? Proto t c 15:58, 10 January 2006 (UTC)
I don't think I'll bother with this website again either. I found this response cruel and disheartening. Kid Apathy 16:02, 10 January 2006 (UTC)
That's a shame, because many of your contributions to Wikipedia have been excellent. I'm sure one little remonstration won't stop you from continuing to help improving Wikipedia. Proto t c 16:06, 10 January 2006 (UTC)
No they haven't. Kid Apathy 16:08, 10 January 2006 (UTC)
Yes they have. I looked through them. Proto t c 16:17, 10 January 2006 (UTC)

People can be mean here, and some lack a sense of humor, too. I prefer people who are nice and funny myself. Although I would stop at vandalizing pages, even if it is funny, because somebody has to take the time to fix it. That might seem funny if it happens once, but after a thousand times, it doesn't seem funny anymore. StuRat 11:58, 11 January 2006 (UTC)
