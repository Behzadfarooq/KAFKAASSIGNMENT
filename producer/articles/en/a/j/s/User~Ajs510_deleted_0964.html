The Full Contact Hockey League (FCHL) is an international fantasy sports organization composed of 12 independently owned and operated franchises.

The league was founded in September, 2005 and held its first season as a "keeper" league (meaning that players will carry over from season to season) in 2006-2007.

The FCHL is divided into 3 divisions of 4 teams each:

[edit] History

The FCHL was founded by a group of acquaintances from the Full Contact Poker Hockey Forum. After many months of discussing hockey together, and hearing about other keeper leagues such as the Alternative Hockey League (AHL), the decision was made to start a league and invite several handpicked forum members to join as General Managers.

The first FCHL season (2005-2006) was largely an exibition, with no players carrying over to the following season and a largely "all or nothing" mentality on the part of the General Managers. The initial fee for team ownership and participation was $20, and rather than having a cash prize it was decided that 1st place would be a custom-built poker table designed and handcrafted by Nate Gosse.

The team rosters were filled out in a somewhat controversial manner, by "bidding" on talent from NHL franchises in free-for-all fashion over a 12 hour period on Wednesday, January 5th 2006. Controversy arose when several managers (using a method that was well within the established rules) were able to "scoop" players at the last second before a league-imposed 12am deadline. This was done by waiting until approximately 15 seconds to midnight before entering a final, winning bid that would leave no time for rebuttal by another manager.

GM's were tasked with building the most competitive franchise possible within the constraints of the league-imposed Salary Cap, which is $60.

When the dust settled after the initial Free Agent draft, forward Ilya Kovalchuk of the Atlanta Thrashers had emerged as the FCHL's highest paid player, with a 2005-2006 salary of $12 (20% of the total salary cap).

Despite all efforts to round out each team with a full complement of 25 players, several teams were left with empty roster spots (led by the Hartford Whalers with an amazing 22 roster spaces left to fill). The decision was made to hold a supplemental draft later that week, and this took place on Sunday, January 8th, 2006.

Once all teams had filled their quota of 25 players, it was time for the season to begin. Unfortunately, the league controversy did not end with the climax of the draft. One of the league's GM's decided after having drafted his team that he was no longer interested in participating, and opted not to pay his entry fee, thus voiding his entry. This unfortunate turn of events led to the search for a replacement GM, and the FCHL was lucky enough to find "Mad" Mike Hoffman, an alumnus of Tulane University and a rabid hockey fan in his own right. Mike was willing to accept the fact that he was taking on a drowning franchise, and came on board as the 12th permanent GM and owner of the Tulane Flyers.

The exhibition season came to a close with The Injured Reserve defeating The asdfjkl; in the championship series.

In the postseason, two FCHL GM's were extended offers to join the rival Alternative Hockey League as expansion franchisees. Both offers were accepted, and the FCHL bid farewell to the Canadian Gothic and Team Goose franchises. The two open franchises were quickly purchased by Richmond Munro and Dan Goodman, two supporters of the FCHL who had made it very well known that they would be interested in joining the league, should a spot for them become available.

In September of 2006, the GM's gathered together in a private chat room for the first-ever FCHL Free Agent Auction Draft. The drafting process was noticeably different as NHL players were nominated one at a time, rather than en masse, to give each and every GM a fair chance at that player. At the end of the draft, superstar Sidney Crosby had emerged as the highest salaried player, with an FCHL record payday of $17.75 (roughly 29.5% of the $60 salary cap).

Following a hotly contested regular season, 6 teams emerged as the contenders for "The Jersey", the official trophy of the FCHL, awarded to the League Champion. The playoff teams were:

1. The Tulane Flyers (42-24-0 = 86 points) Tugnutt Division Champion

2. The Hartford Whalers (41-26-0 = 82 points) Gilmour Division Champion

3. The asdfjkl; (36-29-2 = 74 points) Division Supreme Champion

4. The Lost Cause (39-26-2 = 80 points)

5. 3rd Period Ninjas (38-28-1 = 77 points)

6. The Left Coast Lock (36-29-2 = 74 points)

The #1 (Tulane Flyers) and #2 (Hartford Whalers) seeds automatically advanced to the second round, therefore the first round playoff pairings were:

[edit] League Commissioners

The original commissioner of the FCHL was Nate Gosse, who additionally served as the GM of Team Goose. Following Nate's resignation from the league in favor of the rival AHL, Jason Vlaming (GM of the 3rd Period Ninjas) was selected as commissioner for the 2006-2007 season. Jason has since resigned his stewardship of the league, in favor of Darnelle, owner of the Altona Maroons.

[edit] Games

The FCHL holds game nights on any day during the NHL regular season featuring 7 or more NHL games (in rare instances, on nights where only 6 games are played).

An FCHL game is head-to-head between franchises, according to a schedule determined in the pre-season. Each General Manager submits his lineup for the evening, and accumulates points according to how his real-life players perform in the actual NHL games played that evening.

An FCHL lineup consists of 6 Forwards, 4 Defensemen, and 1 Goaltender (FCHL franchises own team goaltending, not individual goales, so a correctly submitted lineup will show Buffalo Sabres Goalies, rather than Ryan Miller as an individual).

Example:

F - Wayne Gretzky (OT)

F - Mario Lemieux

F - Brett Hull

F - Cam Neely

F - Pat LaFontaine

F - Mark Messier

D - Scott Stevens

D - Al MacInnis

D - Ray Bourque

D - Paul Coffey

G - Philadelphia Flyers Goalies

The method for accumulating points is simple. If a player (Forward or Defense) on an FCHL franchise's active roster for the evening scores a goal, that FCHL franchise receives 1 point. If a player contributes an assist, that FCHL franchise receives a half point. If the NHL franchise featured as the FCHL team's goaltender for the evening gives up a goal, one half point is subtracted from that team's score.

Goal +1

Assist +0.5

Goal Against -0.5

As shown above in the example lineup, FCHL teams will designate one of their skating players (generally a forward) as their "Overtime" or "OT" player. This designation is used in the event of a tie score. The OT player who has scored the most points for his franchise (in Overtime, goals and assists count equally) on that particular NHL game night will give his FCHL team the victory. If the score is still tied after overtime, the game ends in a tie.
