Ajahn Sobin S. Namto

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Achan Sobin S. Namto has taught Vipassana (Insight Meditation) and Abhidharma (Buddhist psychology) in South-East Asia and North America for over 50 years.

[edit] Biography

Born in Thailand in 1931, Sobin S. Namto became a novice monk in 1949 at Wat Maha Dhatu in Bangkok, under Abbot Somdej Phra Budthacharn, and began his study of Vipassana there. He was sent to Rangoon, Myanmar, for further study and then taught in Thailand.In 1960-62 he taught in Luang Prabang, Laos. Returning to Thailand, he resumed teaching and founded the Vidhayakorn Institute in SouthernThailand, He was one of the monks selected to establish the first Thai temple in Los Angeles, California,and was appointed its first Abbot. He was also the founder-Abbot of a Vipassana temple in Denver, Colorado. After many years of teaching in North America, he returned to teach at the wat in his home village Wangplado, in Borabu, Maha Sarakham province, North-EastThailand. Achan Sobin S. Namto is the author of two books in English on insight meditation.
