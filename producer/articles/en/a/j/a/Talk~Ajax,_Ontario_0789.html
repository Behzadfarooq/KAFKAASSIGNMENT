[edit] Governing body and MPPs in infobox

My understanding is that creating additional "virtual" rows in a table by embedding BRs in table entries creates accessibility issues (screen readers typically read tables cell by cell, left to right, HTML row by HTML row). The entries in the infobox for this article that list the governing body, MP, and MPPs use this technique (all being in the one HTML row created by the leader_title and leader_name parameters). Would anyone mind terribly if this information is simply deleted from the infobox and moved into the body of the article? -- Rick Block (talk) 20:15, 5 September 2006 (UTC)

I notice that you've discovered that multiple leader lines are supported by the infobox. Thank you for figuring this out - I certainly had no intentnion of creating accessibility issues where a better alternative existed. Given that it's possible to use multiple leader lines, I'd like to keep the information in the infobox for quick reference. Daveharr 22:58, 11 September 2006 (UTC)
The additional leader params are brand new. The accessibility issues are not well known (getting more well known at this point). Creating "virtual" rows has been a widely used technique, now eliminated from all references to Template:infobox city, Template:infobox country and Template:Infobox U.S. state. I'm trying to drum up support for eliminating use of this technique throughout Wikipedia (see Wikipedia talk:WikiProject Usability), which I think will be a VERY large effort. -- Rick Block (talk) 01:27, 12 September 2006 (UTC)
