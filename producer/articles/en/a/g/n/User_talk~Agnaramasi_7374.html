[edit] Removing Jewish references/influences on Judith Butler

I'm not sure what's happening with the edits that refer to Butler's Jewish influences, but let's be clear that (1) Butler is Jewish and has made clear in her recent writings and interviews that this is an important aspect of her current work and (2) certain thinkers that are Jewish have very much influenced her latest work and any up-to-date entry on Butler must include them. I would hate to think there's something subversively anti-Semitic about the edits on this page, but I am starting to wonder. Rebecca Kennison 07:22, 12 February 2007 (UTC)

In my latest edits (Jan 11, 2007) I did not remove anything Jewish-related from the entry. But to be clear, I am very used to reverting POV edits to articles that unduly emphasize a certain aspect of a persons's background... e.g. putting "lesbian" or "Jewish" in the opening sentence of the Butler article in an inappropriately politicized way. Once you cited your sources re: Butler's recent work on Jewish Philosophy I had no problems. I initially assumed that your claim was unfounded indentity-politicking... Also it is very wrong for you to threaten me with words like "anti-semitic", especially if you go back and see all the work I've done over the past year improving this article. I am very disappointed you would do that.--Agnaramasi 13:15, 12 February 2007 (UTC)
