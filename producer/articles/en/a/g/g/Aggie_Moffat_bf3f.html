Aggie Moffat

Agatha "Aggie" Moffat is a former tea lady for Scottish football club St. Johnstone who became famous for a verbal assault on former Scotland international and Rangers manager Graeme Souness.

In 1990, when Rangers visited McDiarmid Park to take on St. Johnstone, the Glasgow club left their dressing room in such a state that, upon delivering the tea, Moffat was moved to ask, "Would you leave your home like that?"[1] This led to Souness enquiring as to Moffat's ability to tidy up. A verbal ear-bashing from Moffat ensued. Souness left Scotland the following year and hasn't returned to manage there since.

Moffat began working for the Perth club in March 1981 and spent over fifteen years with them. She even appeared in the St. Johnstone team photo for the 1993/94 season.

[edit] Trivia

  • According to her husband, Bob, Moffat's proudest moment was when St. Johnstone gained promotion to the Premier Division after beating Ayr United 2-0 at Somerset Park in 1990. The players gathered in front of her and Bob at the end of the game and sang to them.[2]
