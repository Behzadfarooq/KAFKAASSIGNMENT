There really seems to be two major definitions of this sport and its culture. The definition held by outsiders who may have seen it for a second on the television 10 years ago, and the definition of those who actually do it. Because its fluctuating popularity most claims, descriptions, or definitions by its participants are discredited as attempts look cool basically. So then the sport's definition is left at the mercy of commercials and pop culture opinion which would rather deny the sports entire existence or poke fun at it with figure skating and/or homosexual references. Thus the creation of the derogatory title, "Fruitbooter."

It becomes increasingly difficult to define because there are so many different forms and uses of inline skates such as roller hockey, recreational skating, fitness skating, speed skating, ski cross training, and then this division that has loosely been referred to as "Aggressive Inline Skating." And because of its vague similarities to other sports such as skateboarding, roller skating, and ice skating people tend to want to mix all of that in as well. In the end the result is one big undefined lump of an activity/culture, when in reality it is far from that. It has been such a small, underground thing that no accurate documentation or publication even exists to back that statement, all I can offer is my word as an individual who has been doing this and following the sport for the past 15 years. The sport and its following does not lack definition in anyway, however, the publics understanding and knowledge of it lacks as much as something can possibly lack. And since there are as I said no accurate documentations on this specific type of skating it becomes basically impossible to create believable documentations on the subject because there is nothing to reference. The closest thing I can reference is a recent documentary titled, "Barely Dead," which for further information you can find at www.misledmedia.com

Skateboarding on the other hand has always been simple to describe because there is only one form or one use of the skateboard and that is to do stunts, tricks, dangerous maneuvers or what ever you want to call it. There is no need to invent a specialized name for it like, "Aggressive Skateboarding" to differentiate it from say fitness, or hockey skateboarding.

Many people try to lump skateboarding and aggressive inline skating into the same group, and though they are similar they are two entirely different sports. The same way there may be or have been individuals who preform tricks on roller skates, it is in no way the same thing as skateboarding or aggressive inline skating. I could go on and on here but I'm not sure if anybody will even read this, so I am just going to end it now.

- Alan Hughes, March 24th 2007.
