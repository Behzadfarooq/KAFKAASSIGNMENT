Aggron (ボスゴドラ, Bosugodora?, Bossgodora in original Japanese language versions) are one of the 493 fictional species of Pokémon creatures from the multi-billion-dollar[1] Pokémon media franchise—a collection of video games, anime, manga, books, trading cards, and other media created by Satoshi Tajiri. The purpose of Aggron in the games, anime, and manga, as with all other Pokémon, is to battle both wild Pokémon—untamed creatures encountered while the player passes through various environments—and tamed Pokémon owned by Pokémon trainers.[2]

"Aggron" is a conjunction of aggressive, because it has an aggressive nature, and iron, because its whole body is plated with iron. Its Japanese name contains ボス bosu, the transliteration of boss. ゴドラ Godora is a variation of Kodora, making it the "boss of Kodora" or the "Boss Kodora." It also resembles the name Gojira.

[edit] Biological characteristics

Aggron is a hefty, bipedal, reptilian-dinosaur like monster clad in rocky armor-plating and steel plating that covers its visible spine on its back and its head like a helmet. The horns growing out of its head, bearing somewhat of a resemblance to the horns of the Triceratops, gradually grow over time, so their length is therefore a good indication for Aggron’s age.

Aggron, a diurnal herbivore, bears tremendous power and is a fearsome sight in its mountain habitat. Its iron armor plating protects it from even the strongest physical attacks launched at it, its steel claws can slash straight through metal, and its hefty metallic tail can slam enemies away with bone-breaking force. This impressive natural armament is enough of a burden to make Aggron’s movement somewhat sluggish due to the physical weight, but Aggron does not mind; The gouges in its armor that it received in battle with wild Pokémon opponents are worn with pride as mementos from those battles.

Aggron’s power is compounded by its aggressive nature and territoriality; It easily claims an entire mountain as its domain, and mercilessly beats up anything that dares to violate it. It is very protective of its space, vigilantly patrolling it at all times. However, this does not mean that Aggron is unreasonable; It can be surprisingly gentle towards weaker Pokémon in the wild that don’t actually intrude its space, and is also surprisingly caring for its environment. If its mountain is damaged by a landslide, fire, or other natural disaster, Aggron will search for and haul topsoil to the area, replant trees, and do everything else necessary to restore its territory to a serene state. Also, in spite of its fearsome habits, it primarily feeds on vegetables rather than meat. So, it could be thought of as the protector of mountains.

[edit] In the video games

A Pokémon Emerald screenshot featuring an enemy Pupitar and Solrock fighting in a double battle against a player's Aggron and Smeargle.

Aggron evolves from Lairon at level 42. Some of its natural techniques are Iron Tail, Roar, and Take Down. It has a surprisingly wide range of different moves available through TMs (such as Thunder and Dragon Claw). Despite learning a relatively wide array of special attacks, it cannot use them effectively because of its low stats in that area. The most popular Aggron moveset for competitive battling includes Thunder Wave to offset its low speed and the effective combo of Substitute and Focus Punch. Rock Slide rounds out the set by taking advantage of its Rock type.

Its best feature by far is its defense, outdone only by Regirock, Deoxys in its defense form, Steelix, and Shuckle. Also, due to Aggron's Rock/Steel type, it is supremely resistant to most attacks. This type combination, however, gives it two double-weaknesses, Fighting and Ground. Aggron also has a weakness to Water type attacks, which is amplified by its low special defense. Aggron is notable for completely outclassing Golem in every stat but HP. Aggron is in many respects the steel equivalent to Tyranitar, with superb defense as its defining statistical trait. It also resembles Rhydon and, to a further extent, Dosidon.

[edit] In the anime

Aggron has appeared in the Pokémon intro that takes place before the feature presentations of Movies numbers 6 and 7. In it, Aggron is seen in a 2v2 battle against a Solrock and a Manectric with a Shiftry helping him.

This is also one of the Pokémon owned by Steven Stone, champion of the Elite Four in the Hoenn Region. In episode #298, "A Hole Lotta Trouble!", he used it to get rid of Team Rocket from the Granite Cave.

Also, Ash's Hoenn Rival Tyson once beat one in "Saved by the Beldum".

An Aggron is one of a Trainer's Pokémon seen at a Pokémon Contest.

In Episode 453 (Grating Spaces), Delibird gave Jessie and James a Charizard and Aggron, but Delibird took them back because the Charizard and Aggron were meant for Butch and Cassidy.

[edit] In the Pokémon trading card game

Aggron in the Pokémon Trading Card Game.

Aggron does for the Steel type what Ampharos did for the Electric type; it is the only Stage 2 Steel-type Pokémon as of now, both in the video games and in the card game. Aggron appears in the following sets:

  • EX Ruby and Sapphire
  • EX Sandstorm (as Aggron EX)
  • EX Team Magma vs. Team Aqua (as Team Magma's Aggron)
  • EX Legend Maker
  • EX Crystal Guardians (as Aggron EX)
  • EX Power Keepers
