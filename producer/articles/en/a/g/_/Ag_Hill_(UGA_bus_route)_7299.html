Ag Hill is a bus route of the University of Georgia Campus Transit system. The UGA Campus Transit system carries a greater volume of passengers than any other university transit system in the United States.

Ag Hill provides the fastest service between the residence and dining halls of West Campus and the more recently developed East Campus facilities such as the Performing and Visual Arts Complex and the Ramsey Center. The route shares its name with the hill on which Conner Hall is located, home of UGA's agricultural college.

The other Campus Transit routes are East Campus Express, East-West, Family Housing, Milledge Avenue, North-South, Orbit, Rivers Crossing, and Russell Hall.

[edit] Route

Full service is provided on days when classes are in session. On this route, the full service level is provided by two buses which run 13 minutes apart. Reduced service is provided during final exams and the summer terms. This route does not run during nighttime hours, intersession periods, or weekends. Some destinations on this route are served at those times by the East-West route, but most of this route, notably the Agriculture Hill itself, has no service at those times.
