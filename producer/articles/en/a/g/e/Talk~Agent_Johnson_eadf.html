This character shouldn't really have his own article. This page's content should be merged into either Agent (The Matrix) or List of programs and machines in the Matrix series.

The main reasons are:

  1. He only appears in one film in the series: personally, I think Cypher is the only Matrix character appearing in one film who should get a dedicated article; everyone else should be described in the "List" articles. This page breaks that consistency.
  2. This page is mostly an overly-detailed description of the events in The Matrix Reloaded that involve Johnson. Is all that really necessary?
  3. The number of images in the article appears, to me, to be too great compared to the size of the article - I don't think that this extensive use of images would fall under fair use.

--Nick RTalk 17:46, 20 April 2006 (UTC)

I shouldn't really use a horizontal rule there, but this is a fairly long resonse with a lot of formatting, and it should help keep this comment separate.
"Characters should get their own personal profile, no matter how least of importance the character is."
From Wikipedia:Deletion policy/Minor characters:
  1. Major characters (and places, concepts, etc) in a work of fiction should be covered within the article on that work of fiction. If the article on the work itself becomes long, then giving major characters an article of their own is good practice.
  2. Minor characters (and places, concepts, etc) in a work of fiction should be listed with short descriptions in a "List of minor characters." This list should reside in the article relating to the work itself, unless either becomes long, in which case a separate article for the list is good practice.
  3. Fictional characters which are cultural icons transcending their appearance in a particular work of fiction, or who cannot be neatly tied to a particular work of fiction or fictional universe deserve articles of their own, regardless of other circumstances.
I wouldn't class Agent Johnson as a major character (nowhere near as major as Neo, Smith, Morpheus or even Cypher), and he's definitely not a cultural icon. Therefore, he's a minor character, and should therefore be included only in the "List" article.
"In Dictionary of Matrix Agent Johnson has his own profile"
Dictionary of Matrix is not Wikipedia.
"I just want to expand and help the Matrix series, nothing else, is that too much to ask?"
Fair enough. But please bear in mind that:
  1. Adding content is not necessarily a good thing: as an example, it's often better to work a point into an article's prose rather than simply add an extra bullet point to a "trivia" section.
  2. All the Matrix-related articles need a lot of work, and it is often the case that the fewer articles there are, the easier it is to improve and maintain them, and the more consistent their style and quality.
  3. Wikipedia has a reputation as a place where fancruft runs rampant. IMO, articles on pop culture should be limited and properly organised in order to promote the idea that including pop-culture content does not inhibit Wikipedia's validity.
Why the obsession with this particular character, anyway? :) --Nick RTalk 13:32, 28 April 2006 (UTC)
