Agenda setting

Agenda setting is the process by which an individual defines the issues to be addressed in the agenda for a meeting, or by which a group of people define the issues to be addressed in a political process.

Agenda-setting theory is concerned with research on agenda setting, and is conducted in communication, sociology, political science, and organizational behavior.

This task usually falls to the chairperson of the gathering, giving the person which holds that office much power in terms of what gets discussed or what does not get discussed during the gathering.
