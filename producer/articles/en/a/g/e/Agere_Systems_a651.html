Agere Systems Inc. is an integrated circuit components company based in Allentown, Pennsylvania, in the Lehigh Valley region of Pennsylvania, in the United States.

Agere was incorporated on August 1, 2000 as a subsidiary of Lucent Technologies and then spun-off on June 1, 2002. The name Agere was that of a Texas-based electronics company that Lucent had acquired in 2000, although the pronunciations of the company names are different. The Texas company was pronounced with three syllables and a hard "g": A-gear-uh. The current company name is pronounced with two syllables and a hard "g": A-gear.

The company also maintains an India office in Whitefield, India, located in the city of Bangalore, which is involved in ASIC design and software development.

The company also maintains an Israel office located in Raanana. This office is based on Modem-Art a developer of advanced processor technology for 3G/UMTS mobile devices which Agere has acquired in 2005.

The China offices of Agere are located at Shanghai and Shenzhen.

[edit] Merger With LSI Logic

This article documents a current event.
Information may change rapidly as the event progresses.

On December 3, 2006, LSI announced that it would purchase Agere in a stock transaction valued at $4 billion[1].
