Agent 99

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Agent 99 of CONTROL

Agent 99 is a fictional television spy portrayed by Barbara Feldon on the 1960s television series Get Smart. She was the work partner, and later wife, of Secret Agent Maxwell Smart, Agent 86, portrayed by Don Adams. 99's true name was never revealed, not even when her mother appeared in four episodes, or after the character became a US Congresswoman in the FOX revival. In one episode, Smart called her "Ernestine". She seemed touched, replying "That's the first time you ever called me Ernestine... I wish it were my name!" She also used the name "Susan Hilton" in the well-remembered episode "99 Loses Control", but it was revealed by the end of the episode that it was only a cover name. Some trivia games incorrectly give "Susan Hilton" as the real name for 99. She is occasionaly addressed as Mrs. Smart after marrying Maxwell Smart

The character provided several functions in the series: being the standard "eye candy" of the presence of a beautiful woman; being a necessary "straight-man", setting up jokes for which Maxwell Smart or some other character would ultimately provide the punch line; acting as a "frustration symbol" for Smart's bumbling; acting as a sort of "mother figure" for the same; being a "side kick" figure even when not acting in the straight-man function; and being the constant and obvious love interest for Smart.

99 has fraternal twin children from her marriage to Maxwell Smart: Zachary (portrayed by Andy Dick), who eventually became a CONTROL agent in 1995, and his still-as-yet unnamed sister.

Agent 99 is being resurrected to the movie screen with the upcoming film adaptation (scheduled for 2008) of Get Smart, starring Anne Hathaway as 99. Steve Carell will play Maxwell Smart in the film.
