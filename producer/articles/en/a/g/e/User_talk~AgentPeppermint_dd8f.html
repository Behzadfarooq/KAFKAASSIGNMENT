[edit] Synesthesia

As to your desire to experience synesthesia, have you ever played the Playstation 2 game Rez? The game features a soundtrack that integrates with the enviroment and both evolve as you delve deeper into the game's levels (referred to as layers). Your avatar in the game evolves from a vaguely humanoid figure in a prone position with its limbs hanging limply below it through various forms, becoming a human in the lotus position and eventually evolving two levels further (I won't spoil those for you). Your avatar attacks by emitting beams of various shapes from various points on its "body" depending on which level it has currently evolved to, with the beams possibly representing the evolution of thought ( an example being the 4th form (if my memory serves me) shooting white beams which can only move in 90 degree angles towards the enemy). All of your actions in the game (shooting a projectile, destroying an enemy, moving to a new level, etc.) have sounds which blend into and become part of the song playing further increasing the ability of the player to experience synethesia. I would highly recommend purchasing or otherwise experiencing this game (preferably in a pitch black room with surround sound or headphones), and if you have any questions about it, obtaining it, etc. feel free to contact me on my user page.

I thought I'd add that I was of course referring to the voluntarily induced state of mind, not the involuntary mental disorder (which I'd actually never heard the word Synesthesia used for until reading that Wiki article). Just thought I'd clear that up, if you're referring to a desire to have the disorder I doubt I can help you.N9philim 01:45, 14 December 2006 (UTC)
