Agathe Martel is a Canadian Soprano.

Soprano Agathe Martel has attracted considerable critical and public acclaim for the beauty of her voice, for her remarkable artistry and her elegantly communicative stage presence. Opera News draws attention to her “lovely voice, rich in color, and capable of an attractive sensuality”, while Classics Today places her “in a long line of world-class contemporary Canadian singers”, and La Presse points out “a performance of the highest professional level” and that “the concert world has an urgent need of musicians so completely dedicated to their art”.

Agathe Martel has sung leading roles on operatic stages, including Gilda (Rigoletto) with l’Opéra de Québec , Opera Lyra Ottawa and Manitoba Opera , Violetta (La Traviata) and Lucia (Lucia di Lammermoor) with the Vermont Opera Festival, Marguerite (Faust), Mimí and Musetta (La Bohème) with l’Opéra de Dijon, Antonia (Les Contes d’Hoffmann) with Opera Lyra Ottawa, l’Opéra de Québec and l’Opéra de Montréal , and Adina (L’Elisir d’amore) with l’Opéra de Québec. Additionally, she has performed as a soloist with several Canadian symphony orchestras, including those of Montréal, Toronto, Edmonton, Québec City and Ottawa, singing under the baton of conductors such as Charles Dutoit, Simon Streatfeild, Yannick Nézet-Séguin and Jacques Lacombe, in works such as Haydn’s Creation, Mozart’s C minor Mass, Brahms’ German Requiem, Berlioz’s Nuits d’été, Strauss’ Four Last Songs, Mahler’s Fourth Symphony and Orff’s Carmina Burana.

Agathe Martel has given song recitals in Toronto, Zürich, Chicago, Victoria, Montréal, Lausanne, Washington DC, La Chaux-de-Fonds, Québec City and Ottawa, in repertoire covering three centuries and ten languages. Widely acclaimed by international critics, her discography includes Vai Azulão, a programme of South American songs released by Toronto-based Marquis Classics, and Mélodies françaises published by SNE. Winner of numerous awards, including the Prix d’Excellence by l’Opéra de Québec, the First Prize at the Montreal Symphony Orchestra Competition, the Adelina Patti Prize, the John Newmark Prize and the Raoul Jobin Prize, she studied in New York with Joyce McLean (Juilliard School) and in London with Vera Rozsa and Noelle Barker.
