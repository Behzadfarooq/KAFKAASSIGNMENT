"Against the Wind" is a song by Bob Seger & The Silver Bullet Band. The song helped propel the album Against the Wind to number one on the Billboard 200. Other hits that helped made the album top the charts included "Fire Lake" (# 6), "You'll Accomp'ny Me" (# 14), and "The Horizontal Bop" (# 42). "Against the Wind" was the highest ranking of the singles coming in at # 5.

According to Timothy White, a writer for Rolling Stone, "'Against the Wind' is about trying to move ahead, keeping your sanity and integrity at the same time." [1]

During an interview on 100 FM The Pike, Bob Seger said that "Against the Wind" came about from his days as a high-school cross country runner.

[edit] Cover versions

"Against the Wind" was covered by The Highwaymen on their first album, The Highwaymen.
