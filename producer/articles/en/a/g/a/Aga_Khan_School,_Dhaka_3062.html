The Aga Khan School, Dhaka, is an English Medium School, under the Aga Khan Development Network (AKDN). It is one of the first English Medium Schools in Bangladesh. The school has a great past both academically and in terms of sports. The School is rich in its history of 15 years in Dhaka. The Aga Khan School was found in 1988, in a small campus in Siddeswari, Dhaka. The school is currently situated in Uttara, Dhaka.

[edit] History

The Aga Khan School was found in 1988. The school first started with 14 students and 7 teaching staff. The AKDN funded the creation of the school. Since its start in 1988, the school has developed into one of the best academic schools in Bangladesh, if not South Asia. Later on, the school building was shifted to Uttara, with a larger premises.

[edit] Current Status

With 1,140 students, and 103 highly qualified teaching staff, The Aga Khan School, Dhaka is one of the leading English Medium schools in Bangladesh. The School starts from Playgroup to A level, following the British curriculumâ€™s requirements, leading to GCSE O and A level examinations. The school is in the process of moving to the International Baccalaureate (IB) Curriculum.

The current Principal of the school is Mr. George G. Kays. The current Chairman of the board is Mr. Azzani. The school organises annual events, functions and concerts. The most succesful portion of the School is its academic line. Every year, the O'Level and A'Level examinees of Aga Khan School, bring out the best results from all over Bangladesh, especially in subjects like Full Course of Pure Mathematics and Physics[citation needed]. Some of the most reputed teachers of the school are Mr. Faruq Ahmad Talukdar, Mr.Yameen Choudhury, Mr. Shayak Ahmed and Sunil Saha.

[edit] Trivia

  • The School holds elections every year to choose a leader among the student community who will be responsible for representating the School body.
  • A short abbreviation is used for The Aga Khan School, commonly the term "AKS" is used.
