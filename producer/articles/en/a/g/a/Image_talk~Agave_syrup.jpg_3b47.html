To respond to several of these points: First, note that the "promotional" tag specifically states that the image must not be replaceable. Wikipedia does not accept all promotional images, only those that meet our fair use criteria. Note, moreover, that those criteria are devoted not only to the goal of avoiding legal trouble, but also to that of maximizing the amount of free content we produce. While the image may not be right-this-moment replaceable, or replaceable by one particular editor, it is possible that someone could take and upload a free photo for this purpose.
To your accusation of a vendetta; I assure you that this is not the case. The fair use policy is being enforced more actively now than it has been in the past, and while many good-faith editors who had gotten the impression that certain types of images which were not permitted are permitted have been upset by this change, the policy is being enforced in good faith, and in pursuit of the goal of increasing the amount of free content we produce, one of the five pillars of Wikipedia. Please do not throw unfounded accusations at your fellow editors. --RobthTalk 22:41, 9 November 2006 (UTC)

Let's distinguish between photos of commercially distributed creative works and photos of ordinary commercial products. If I just take a picture of a copyrighted book cover, distributing that picture would infringe the cover artist's copyright. If I take a picture of a glass bottle of syrup, no copyright is being infringed. Thus, free images of commercial products that are not creative works can be created. --RobthTalk 06:40, 11 November 2006 (UTC)
