Agano class cruiser

The Agano class (阿賀野型) was a series of four light cruisers which served with the Imperial Japanese Navy during World War II.

Propulsion: 4 shaft; Geared turbines; 6 boilers; 100,000shp

Speed: 35kt

Range: 6,300nm at 18kt

Armour: belt 2.2(56mm); Deck 0.7(18mm); turrets 1in(25mm)

Armaments: 6x 6in(152mm)Guns, 4x 3in(76mm)Guns, 32x 25mm Anti-aircraft guns, 8x 24in(610mm)Torpedo

Aircraft: 2

Complement: 730


[edit] Description

The Agano class had a standard displacement of 6650 tons and carried a crew of 730. Primary armament was six 6.1" guns, along with eight 24" torpedo tubes and depth charges. The vessels could make 35 knots.

Initial design specifications were for a 5,000 ton vessel carrying six 6.1 inch guns and eight 3 inch dual purpose guns. Reduction of armament and armor, as well as an increase in displacement, was necessary to avoid overload. In the end armor was less than 10% of displacement and could only defeat 5 inch shells or smaller. An attempt was made to increase the main armament's utility against aircraft by giving the 6 inch guns an elevation of 55 degrees, but this was not enough to make them effective.

An improved Agano class was planned with as many as seven ships to be constructed. These would have a displacement of 8520 tons, an additional two 6 inch guns and a total of eight 3 inch dual-purpose guns while being capable of 37 knots.

The Agano was named after the city of Agano, in Niigata.

[edit] Ships in class

Four Agano class light cruisers were built in Japan, beginning in 1942, for service during the war. Three of the four were sunk.

[edit] Agano

Completed on 31 October 1942, Agano participated in the evacuation of Guadalcanal and reinforcement of Rabaul. Heavily damaged by an air strike on November 11, 1943 and submarine attack the next day, Agano sank on February 17, 1944 while attempting to return to Japan for repairs.

[edit] Noshiro

Completed on June 30, 1943, Noshiro participated in fleet operations in defense of Rabaul in November, 1943 and sustained damage from an air attack on January 1, 1944. Noshiro was heavily involved in operations around the Philippines in 1944 including the Battle of the Philippine Sea, the Battle of Leyte Gulf, and the Battle of Sibuyan Sea. After surviving the Battle off Samar, she was sunk the next day by air attack.

[edit] Yahagi

Completed on December 29, 1943, Yahagi served mainly in the Philippines, engaged in many battles along with the Noshiro. Yahagi was sunk on April 7, 1945 during Operation Ten-Go while covering for the Yamato.

[edit] Sakawa

Completed on November 30, 1944, Sakawa did not participate in any engagements by the end of the war. Initially demilitarized to operate as a transport, she was ultimately turned over to the United States and was utilized in the nuclear tests at Bikini Atoll in 1946.
