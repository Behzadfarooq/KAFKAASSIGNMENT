Agathe (atomic test)

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

You have new messages (last change).

Agathe (aka Agate) was the name of the first French nuclear underground test. It was an atomic bomb detonated in the Hoggar mount (near In Ekker) of the then French Sahara desert on November 21, 1961, during the Algerian War (1954-62)[1].

Agathe was the first test of the jewel designation series running from 1961 until 1966. Some incidents occured during these experimentations, the most famous being the Beryl incident on May 1st 1962 where the nine militarymen of the 621ème Groupe d'Armes Spéciales unit. They were heavily contaminated.
