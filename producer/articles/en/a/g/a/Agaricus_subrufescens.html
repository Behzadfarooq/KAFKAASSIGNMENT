Agaricus subrufescens (formerly known as; Agaricus blazei) is a species of mushroom, sometimes known as himematsutake and by a number of other names. This Agaricus is a choice edible, with a somewhat sweet taste and fragrance of almonds. The almond flavor is due to the presence of benzaldehyde, benzyl alcohol, benzonitrile, and methyl benzoate. [1] This mushroom is also well known for its purported medicinal properties.

[edit] History

Agaricus subrufescens was first described by the American mycologist William Alphonso Murrill in 1947. It was discovered again in Brazil during the 1970s, and thought to be a new species, Agaricus blazei. It was soon marketed for its purported medicinal properties under various names, including:

  • ABM
  • Cogumelo do Sol (mushroom of the sun)
  • Cogumelo de Deus (mushroom of god)
  • Cogumelo de Vida (mushroom of life)
  • Himematsutake (in the country of Japan)
  • Royal Sun Agaricus
  • Mandelpilz
  • Almond Mushroom

In 2002, Didukh Wasser (Intl J Med Mush 4:267-290) discovered the species was identical to Agaricus brasiliensis; this was confirmed in 2005, when Kerrigan (Mycologia, 97(I), 2005, pp.12-24) used DNA sequencing as proof. Because the latter is the oldest name, it is traditionally considered the scientifically, historically correct name.

Others propose Agaricus subrufescens is identical to Agaricus silvaticus, because they cannot easily be differentiated.[citation needed]

[edit] Commercial use

Because of its high beta glucan content – higher than both Reishi and Shiitake mushrooms – Agaricus subrufescens is used in oncological therapy, mainly in Japan and California. It has been commercially cultivated in Asia and South America since 1993. Because of this valuable polysaccharide, and lack of supply, Agaricus blazei used to be relatively expensive, until it was successfully artificiallly cultivated in mushroom farms. China(Maucua) and Brazil are major exporters.

Agaricus blazei mushroom specifically assists in the production of interferon and interleukin, which are potent in fighting off cancer cell metastasis, especially cancer of the uterus. It also reduces blood glucose, blood pressure, cholesterol levels and the effects of arteriosclerosis.

ABM is known to contain three different beta-glucans – beta-(1-3)-D-glucan, beta-(1-4)-a -D-glucan, and beta-(1-6)-D-glucan.
