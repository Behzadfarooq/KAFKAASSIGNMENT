"Again and Again" is the lead single taken from Jewel's sixth studio album, Goodbye Alice In Wonderland. Promotional singles of the song were sent out in late February 2006 (US), however the single didn't start to gain airplay till about mid April of 2006. Some fans and critics view "Again and Again" as a double A-side release to the song, "Goodbye Alice In Wonderland", which was the very first (unofficial) single lifted from the self-titled album.

[edit] Single information

"Again and Again" is a upbeat soft tempo ballad performed by the singer Jewel. This is Jewel's fifth single released to airways by the artists that did not receive any form of remixing.

The single eventually became the most added song to AC radio stations in the US. However, as the lead single from the album, it didn't do as well as her pervious lead singles ("Who Will Save Your Soul", Hands", "Standing Still" and "Intuition").

[edit] Music video

Jewel in the promotional video

The music video was officially released to television around March 2006. The video was directed by Matthew Rolston, and featured Jewel walking around a farm house and a plantation. While relatively plotless, the end of the video shows Jewel finding the man she sings about in the song.

[edit] Commercial release

"Again and Again" received no sort of commercial release within the US besides a promotional single (those are not available for purchase). A digital download single wasn't even released in the US for purchase, which even contributed to the lower chart position on the Billboard Hot 100 Singles Charts. However international singles were issued. For the international release of the CD Single, a different single cover was distributed.
