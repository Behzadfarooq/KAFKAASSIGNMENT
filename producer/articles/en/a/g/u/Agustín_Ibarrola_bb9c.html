Agustín Ibarrola

Agustín Ibarrola born in Basauri (Biscay), Spain in 1930 is a spanish painter.

In 1948 the Delegation of Biscay and the City council of Bilbao granted a scholarship to him to study in Madrid, where he lived until 1955.

He made diverse exhibitions in Spain and in 1955 he was transferred to Paris. In 1957 he was a member of the group Equipment 57 next to Duarte, Serrano and River basin, among others. This is the time of the constructivism.

Stopped by his communist militancy, he remained for several years in the prison of Towns. In 1963, an exhibition of his work in London was displayed, a critic compared him with the paper that Goya developed in his work Disasters of the War.

In the 1980's he initiated his escultórica activity and his singular vision of the pictorial work with the Forest of Oma, near Guernica, perhaps his best known work.

His work combines the Basque spirit with the social commitment with the humblest workers, people tend towards the expressionism.

One of his last and most spectacular works was the Buckets of the Memory, in the port of Llanes.

In the last years he has been tied to the founder of the platform Basta Already! a civic fight against the terrorism in the Basque Country. It is known that Ibarrola is persecuted by terrorist organization ETA.


He has been awarded the Medal of the Order of the Constitutional Merit.
