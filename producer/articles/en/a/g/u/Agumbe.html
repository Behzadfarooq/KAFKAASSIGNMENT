Agumbe is one of the most scenic places on the Western Ghats of the Karnataka. Added to the lush green view all around, the attractions for the visitors is the spectacular sunset and vast opportunities it offers to the trekkers.

[edit] Description

Malgudi Road in Agumbe

It is located about 90 km away from the District Head Quarters, Shimoga, and is accessible by road through dense forest coverage. The road through Agumbe connects Shimoga and Manipal, renowned for its hospital facilities. Agumbe is among the places that receive high rainfall and is called the Cherrapunji of South India. Agumbe also served the screening of most of the episodes of one of the most popular television serial Malgudi Days, directed by Shankar Nag based on R. K. Narayan's novel.

[edit] Attractions

There are two waterfalls near to Agumbe. First one is "Onake Abbi" and second one is "Barkana". In Kannada "Onake" stands for a pounding stick (made out of wood) which is used in villages. The words "Onake Abbi" means that a water fall which is as thick as "Onake", may be when viewed from a distance it looks like that.

House in which Malgudi Days was shot

Agumbe is famous for sunset. The sunset spot is directly raised from sea level to a height of 930 feet. Between sunset spot and sea (at a distance of 30 km),there are no obstacles and hence, one can have a very beautiful sunset from this spot. The best possible months for viewing sunset from Agume is December to mid of April. There afterwards, clouds will obstruct view. One can also visit Agumbe during peak rainy season i.e, mid of June to mid of August for enjoying rain.recently naxalites activites rising in the area because of karnataka government's anti-poor policy,

[edit] Transportation

Agumbe can be accesed by road from Udupi,Mangaluru,Thirthahalli and Shivamogga.There are private buses regularly plying from these towns.

The nearest airport is Mangalore International Airport.
