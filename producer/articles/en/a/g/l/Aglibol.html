Aglibol

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Palmyrene deities: from left to right: the lunar god Aglibôl, the supreme god Beelshamên, the sun god Malakbêl, 1st century CE, found near Bir Wereb, Wadi Miyah, Syria, Louvre Museum.

Aglibôl was a lunar deity in the ancient Syrian city of Palmyra. His name means "Calf of Bel" ("Calf of the Lord"). He is depicted with a Lunar disk decorating his head, and sometimes his shoulders, and one of his attributes is the sickle moon. He is linked with the sun god Yarhibol. His cult continued into Hellenic times and was later extended to Rome.
