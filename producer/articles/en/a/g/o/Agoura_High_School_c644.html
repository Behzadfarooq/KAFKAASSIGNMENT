Agoura High School is a four-year high school, freshman-senior, in Agoura Hills, California, USA. It is the largest high school in the Las Virgenes Unified School District, with an enrollment of approximately 2,400 students. In 2004, Agoura was ranked #1 in Newsweek's Poll of the Nation's 1000 best high schools.

Thousand Oaks Boulevard, a prominent backbone road that runs through several of the area's cities, terminates its western segment at Agoura High. A large hill behind (to the north of) the school supports a large stone "A." Agoura High also has rivals. Agoura's biggest rival in football is Westlake High. Agoura also has a rival in Cross Country and Track and Field, Royal High.
