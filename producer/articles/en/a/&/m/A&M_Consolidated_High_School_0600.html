A&M Consolidated High School, or Consol for short, is a four year public high school consisting of grades 9-12 located in College Station, Texas and is part of the College Station Independent School District.

[edit] History

In 1909, the State of Texas granted a charter for an independent school district to encompass the campus of Texas A&M University. By 1940, the school had grown to the point that it moved off the university campus, thus marking the birth of College Station ISD. Today, CSISD is one of three public school districts in Brazos County.

[edit] Newspaper

The school's newspaper is named The Roar. Since the first day of school (August 15, 2006) The Roar has distributed 4 issues.[2]. The Roar staff consists of about twenty members.

[edit] Feeder patterns

  • Rock Prairie Elementary

  • A&M Consolidated Middle School

[edit] Trivia

A&M Consolidated High School is listed in America's top 1,000 schools[4]

A&M Consolidated High School is accredited by the Texas Education Agency and the Southern Association of Secondary Schools and Colleges.[5]

[edit] Notable Alumni

  • Troy Walters is the wide receiver and punt returner for the Arizona Cardinals. After attending high school at A&M Consolidated, he played for Stanford University.
