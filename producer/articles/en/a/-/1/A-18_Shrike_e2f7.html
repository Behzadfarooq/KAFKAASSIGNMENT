Type Ground attack
Manufacturer Curtiss
Maiden flight July 1935
Status Service test
Primary user U.S. Army Air Corps
Number built 13
Developed from Curtiss YA-14

The Curtiss A-18 was a production test verion of the XA-14.

Thirteen aircraft, fitted with Wright R-1820 radial engines, were produced, and although successful further production was not ordered due to a lack of funds and better aircraft (A-20) under design.

After completion of testing the Y1A-18s were redesignated A-18 and assigned to operational units.

[edit] Specifications

Data from USAF Museum

General characteristics

  • Crew: two
  • Length: 41 ft 0 in (m)
  • Wingspan: 59 ft 6 in (m)
  • Height: 11 ft 6 in (m)
  • Max takeoff weight: 12,849 lb (kg)
  • Powerplant: 2× Wright R-1820 radials, 875 hp (kW) each

Performance

  • Maximum speed: 247 mph (km/h)
  • Range: 650 mi (km)
  • Service ceiling: 25,000 ft (m)

Armament

  • 4 x forwards-firing .30 calibre machine guns
  • 1 x aft-firing .30 calibre machine gun
  • 600 lb bombs in internal bay
