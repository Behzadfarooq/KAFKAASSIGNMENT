An aquarium is:

  • A vivarium, usually contained in a clear-sided container (typically constructed of glass or high-strength plastic) in which water-dwelling plants and animals (usually fish, and sometimes invertebrates, as well as amphibians, marine mammals, and reptiles) are kept in captivity, often for public display (see Public aquarium);
  • An establishment featuring such displays in which water-dwelling plants and animals (usually fish, and sometimes invertebrates, as well as amphibians, marine mammals, and reptiles) are kept in captivity, often for public display.

Aquarium, aquariums, aquaria may also refer to:

[edit] Bands

  • Aquarium, a Russian musical group
  • The Aquarium, an American indie rock band
  • Aquaria (band), a symphonic power metal band from Rio de Janeiro, Brazil

[edit] Music

  • Aquarium is the seventh movement of the The Carnival of the Animals by French composer Camille Saint-Saëns. Aquarium has been used in several movies and television shows.
  • Aquarium, an album by the Danish pop-dance group Aqua
  • Aquarium, an album by the German electronic music group Naomi

[edit] Other

  • Aquaria, a planet, one of the Twelve Colonies of Man, of Kobol featured in the Battlestar Galactica universe
  • Aquaria, a PC game under development by Bit Blot.
  • "Aquarium," a poetrybook by Heinz Polzer, better known as Drs. P.
  • Aquarium is also a desktop theme for Windows XP Media Center Edition, depicting several starfish on a sandy seafloor.
  • Aquarium, a book by Viktor Suvorov
  • Aquarium, a PC game from the nineties where the player would run an aquarium.
