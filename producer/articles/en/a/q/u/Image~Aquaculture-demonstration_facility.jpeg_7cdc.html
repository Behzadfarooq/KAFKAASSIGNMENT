Soil Survey of Calhoun County, Florida —The Mitchell Aquaculture Demonstration Facility in an area of Dothan sandy loam, 0 to 2 percent slopes.

The Mitchell Aquaculture Facility is operated and owned by the University of Florida (Gainesville) for research and outreach programs. Known species of interest ( as of 1999) for research are shortnose sturgeon, (Atlantic) Gulf of Mexico sturgeon, channel catfish, and hybrid striped bass. The demonstration facility is seated in Blountstown, Florida and is a mixed use facility incorporating intensive and extensive fish culture. The most relevant study (besides outreach to local and regional fish farmers) is the food to growth conversion study of Gulf of Mexico sturgeon (feasibility of raising this specie in aerated, earthen ponds vs. recirculating fiberglass tank systems). The PASS system (baffled pond-filtration) has also been studied by research staff. The demonstration farm is open to visitors of all walks of life and is open to employment and internships on a as-needed basis through the University of Florida's employment system.

From http://soils.usda.gov/survey/online_surveys/florida/calhoun/gallery/pages/figure-10.htm
