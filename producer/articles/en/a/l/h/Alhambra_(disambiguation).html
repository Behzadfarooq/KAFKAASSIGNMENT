Alhambra (disambiguation)

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Alhambra may refer to:

  • The Alhambra, a palace in Granada, Spain.
  • The Alhambra decree, a royal decree issued in 1492 ordering the expulsion, conversion, and/or death of all the Jews in Spain.
  • Alhambra (board game)
  • Alhambra, Alberta, in Canada
  • Alhambra, California, in the U.S.
  • Alhambra, Illinois, in the U.S.
  • Alhambra, Ciudad Real, in Spain
  • Alhambra, Frederiksberg, in Denmark
  • An urban village located in Phoenix, Arizona, in the U.S.
  • A Spanish brand of hand-made classical guitars
  • A Spanish brand of beer
  • A very rich sponge cake made with dark chocolate and hazelnuts
  • A track by the Italian guitarist Antonio Forcione, first released on the album Ghetto Paradise
  • A silverware pattern patented by Wm. Rogers on July 6, 1907. Produced until about the end of World War I. The pattern has a set of ornate scroll work said to be inspired by the moorish sculture at the Alhambra in Granada.
  • "The Alhambra", was a popular name for United Kingdom of Great Britain and Ireland and Commonwealth Music hall theatres, significant examples were situated in London, Bradford and Glasgow.
  • One of the theatres called "The Alhambra" in Evansville
  • One of the theatres called "The Alhambra" in San Francisco or Sacramento, California
  • A brand of water sold by Sparkletts in Northern California.
  • SEAT Alhambra, a Multi Purpose Vehicle made by the Volkswagen Group
  • A 1996 EP by the Canadian rock band The Tea Party
  • A saloon in the Western shooter video game GUN
  • A main character in the Ali Smith novel 'The Accidental' (2004)
