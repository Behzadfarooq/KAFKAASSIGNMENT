Alhambra (1996) is an EP by The Tea Party and was used as a bridge between The Edges of Twilight and Transmission. It feautures four intricately re-worked acoustic songs from The Edges of Twilight and two others; the first a song entitled "Time" with Roy Harper on vocals, the second a remix of Sister Awake by Rhys Fulber.

Alhambra is an Enhanced CD and feautures multimedia that the band used as a way to explain themselves, inviting fans to explore the details of exotic instruments, song meanings and video & audio clips.

[edit] Track listing

All songs written by The Tea Party (Martin/Burrows/Chatwood), except "Time" written by Jeff Martin and Roy Harper.

  1. "The Grand Bazaar" – 3:55
  2. "Inanna (acoustic version)" – 3:20
  3. "Silence (acoustic version)" – 3:57
  4. "Turn The Lamp Down (acoustic version)" – 4:58
  5. "Time" – 6:58
  6. "Sister Awake Remix" – 4:37

[edit] Additional musicians

  • Roy Harper, vocals on "Time"
  • Susann Richter, background vocals on "Sister Awake Remix"

[edit] Art Details

  • Design: Verve Graphic Design Consultants Inc. (Toronto)
  • Front & back cover image: Simone Orlando

[edit] Notes

  • Alhambra is the Moorish palace in Granada, Spain.
  • EMI Music Canada re-issued Alhambra on 5 March 2002 with the multimedia CD-ROM component updated to work correctly on the Windows XP Operating System. The EP was also long out of stock.
