Al Nabad

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Al Nabad Magazine, meaning "the Pulse", is a monthly Lebanese publication for Public Concern. The purposes of this magazine are to promote public concern in Lebanon and the Arab world in order to support the development of both of these entities; supply a new genre of magazine offering the readers the opportunity to publish their writings and thus express themselves to the public without any liaison; and provide a means of constant cooperation between the Full Members and the readers in support of the attainment of these goals.

[edit] Major Sections

Editorial: Article written by the Editor-in-Chief including a "No Comment" picture.

International Security: Raise the readers' awareness on a variety of challenges the international community faces.

Cultural Impulse: Enriching documentaries about diverse - local and international - topics

Public Surveillance: Articles that concern the public including economic and social articles, and investigative journalism

Public Awareness: Stresses on health issues and all kinds of awareness to the readers

Personal Maintenance: An "Express Yourself" section where staff writers and contributing writers express their opinions about day-to-day matters.

Entertainment: Music, Play and Movie Review; Sports, Jokes, Games, and Fortune Telling; in addition to monthly interviews.

بالعربي الفصيح: Arabic Section that completes the market niche of Al Nabad Magazine.

[edit] The Logo

The logo is an abstract format of the Arabic letter “نون” (for نبض), it is also a sign of victory. The Logo dates since November 2004 and was\is not affiliated to any organization.

[edit] The Layout

The Layout of the magazine is based on a revolutionary journalistic approach through the size (28 by 37 cm), the paper quality (90 g. Matte), the headers, the columns, and the style of pictures.
