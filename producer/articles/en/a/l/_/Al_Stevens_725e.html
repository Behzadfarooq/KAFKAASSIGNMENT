Al Stevens (born October 17, 1971) is an award-winning sportswriter and public relations practitioner.

[edit] Career

Stevens attended Arizona State University. He worked for the student newspaper, The State Press. While a student, he also worked as a freelance reporter for the East Valley Tribune and The Arizona Republic, interning at the Phoenix newspaper in the fall of 2001.

Upon graduation in 2002, Stevens began his journalism career at The Fountain Hills Times. Initially hired as an education reporter, he also covered sports and general assignment. Stevens was promoted to special publications manager in 2004. He also served as sports editor and web editor, helping to launch the newspaper's "4:30 News" in June 2004, the first such daily Internet news update by an Arizona weekly newspaper.

[edit] Awards

Stevens has received numerous awards for his reporting.

In April 2005, he was named the 2004 Arizona Community Journalist of the Year by the Arizona Press Club.[1]

He has won two first place Arizona Newspaper Association awards, receiving the award in 2002 for "Best Team, Sports or Sports Beat Coverage"[2] and again in 2005 for "Best Sports Story."[3]

In 2002, Stevens received a first place Society of Professional Journalists Mark of Excellence Award for sports writing.[4] He was also a recipient of a Gold Circle Award "Certificate of Merit" by the Columbia Scholastic Press Association.[5]

[edit] Public Relations

Stevens currently works for Mullen Public Relations in Phoenix, serving as an account executive for such clients as Desert Mountain, Superstition Mountain, Vistancia, Blackstone Country Club and The Lyle Anderson Company.
