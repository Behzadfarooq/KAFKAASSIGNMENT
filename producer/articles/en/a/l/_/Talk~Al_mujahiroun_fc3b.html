Al Mujahiroun is a banned Islamic organisation in both the UK and the United States which openly expressed support for the hijackers on September 11 and many other terrorist acts. They have openly expressed their goals to have an Islamic flag flying over 10 Downing Street in the UK

The military response to the attacks of September 11 has provided a fertile environment for the inflammatory rhetoric of Islamic pressure groups such as Al-Muhajiroun. The group, founded in Jeddah in 1983 by a Syrian cleric, Sheikh Omar Bakri Mohammed, is dedicated to the re-establishment of the khilafa or world Islamic state which it says was destroyed by imperialist Europe. Bakri Mohammed, who was expelled from Saudi Arabia and has lived in London since 1986, has thrived on the publicity generated by provocative statements calling for young Muslims to take up arms against the opponents of Islam in Chechnya, Kashmir and Israel.

He spoke in approval of the American embassy bombings in Africa and, in 1990, was interviewed by Special Branch after allegedly calling for the assassination of John Major. He denied this and was never charged.

Mainstream Muslim opinion in Britain has repeatedly dismissed the group as the "lunatic fringe". Dr Ghuyasuddin Siddiqui, leader of the Muslim Parliament, did so again in 2004, accusing Al-Muhajiroun's leaders of trying to make "political mileage" from the conflict in Afghanistan.

Al-Mujahiroun's British leader, Anjem Choudary, claimed yesterday that the organisation had a worldwide following, with 30 offices across Britain and others in Pakistan, Kuwait, France, South Africa, Lebanon, Bangladesh, Mauritius, Saudi Arabia, Syria and Algeria.
