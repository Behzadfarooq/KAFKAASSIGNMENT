Albert Harrison Clancy (born August 14, 1888 in Santa Fe, New Mexico - died October 17, 1951 in Las Cruces, New Mexico) is a former Major League Baseball player. Clancy, a third baseman, was a right-handed batter who threw with his right arm. He had an official listed weight of 175 pounds.

Clancy appeared in three games with the St. Louis Browns in 1911. He was hitless in five at bats, reaching base once on a hit by pitch. Today, he is best remembered as the first major league player from New Mexico; the second would not follow until Fred Haney made his debut in 1922.
