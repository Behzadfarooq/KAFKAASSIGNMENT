Albino Alligator is a 1996 film directed by Kevin Spacey. It stars Matt Dillon, Gary Sinise and William Fichtner as three small-time criminals who take hostages when they are cornered by the police.

[edit] Plot

Three criminals are on the tail end of a job gone wrong. With the police in pursuit and one of them badly wounded, the trio are cornered in a bar where they take the patrons and employees hostage. Through a series of misunderstandings, the police mistake the small-time thieves for major criminals, and the tense situation only escalates from there.

[edit] Reception

For the most part, Albino Alligator' received below average reviews. While the script was highly praised, the critics panned the cast as working below their potential.

The film's total US gross was less than $350,000, and spent only 4 weeks in US theaters.
