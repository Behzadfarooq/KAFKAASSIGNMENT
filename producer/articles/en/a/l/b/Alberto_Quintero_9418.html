Alberto Quintero

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Alberto Quintero (born in October 1970 in Palencia (Spain) is a Spanish composer, arranger, music producer, musical director and conductor.

[edit] Biography

Most of his career has developed in the record's world, although in the later years he has focused on theatrical's world where he has harvested several successes, between them two nominations to the Max Awards of the Scenic Arts (Years 2001 - 2002) as Best Musical Director, for his work in "My Fair Lady, the musical".

His first experiences in the world of music occurred in his home town, Palencia, and it is here that he had his first contact with the musicals through "Rock Quijote, the musical", where he was the arranger. Later, he moved to Madrid to improve his formation. He studied piano with Ricard Miralles, harmony and arrangement with Felix Santos, sing with Maria Luisa Castellanos and conducting with Miquel Ortega in Madrid and Antoni Ros-Marbà in Barcelona.
