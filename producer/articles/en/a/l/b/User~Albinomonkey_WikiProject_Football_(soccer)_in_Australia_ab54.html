This is a proposal to expand WikiProject A-League into WikiProject Football (soccer) in Australia. The new project would be dedicated to improving Wikipedia's coverage of all football (soccer) in Australia.

[edit] Goals

  • Keep all A-League clubs, seasons, player and other related articles up-to-date as the competition takes place.
  • Improve the Australian national football (soccer) team article, as well as creating and/or improving articles on Australia's other national teams.
  • Improve and expand articles relating to the history of football in Australia.
  • Improve and expand articles related to the National Soccer League, including clubs, notable players, seasons and managers.
  • Improve and expand coverage of Australia's state league competitions.

[edit] Scope

  • National teams
    • Socceroos
    • Matildas
    • Olyroos
    • Young Socceroos
    • Joeys
  • Domestic competitions
    • A-League, National Soccer League
    • Clubs, players, managers, etc
  • State competitions
    • Victorian Premier League, New South Wales Premier League, Brisbane Premier League, South Australian Super League, Western Australia State League
    • Clubs
  • Governing bodies
    • Football Federation Australia, Soccer Australia
    • Soccer NSW, FFV, Capital Football, Football Queensland, FFSA, Football West, FFT, FFNT
  • Australian players
  • History of football in Australia

[edit] Further Information

This project is proposed essentially to be an expansion of the current WikiProject A-League, and, if implemented, will supersede that project. All project pages from WP:A-L will be moved into the new project. If the projects were kept separate, the majority of members would be the same across both, and many discussion would be directly related to both projects – merging them ensures that everything is kept centrally. If members wished only to work on A-League or alternatively only on Australian football outside the A-League, this is still entirely possible with an expanded project, with the advantage that we would possibly be able to attract more members who are interested in Australian football in general.
