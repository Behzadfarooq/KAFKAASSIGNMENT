Albert Edmondson, 1st Baron Sandford

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Albert James Edmondson, 1st Baron Sandford (29 June 1887 – 16 May 1959) was a British Conservative Party politician.

At the 1922 general election, he was elected to the House of Commons as Member of Parliament for Banbury, and held the seat until he stepped down at the 1945 general election. He was then elevated to the peerage as Baron Sandford.

From 1939 to 1942, he served as a government whip, with the title of Vice-Chamberlain of the Household.
