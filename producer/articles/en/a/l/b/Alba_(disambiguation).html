[edit] Things

  • the female form alba of the Latin word albus 'white'; in Spanish, it also means sunrise.
    • as name of the white liturgical vestment Alb
    • in many botanical names (mostly grammatically female), for species etcetera that are lighter or color (not always white or even whitish) then related ones; e.g. Alba roses, a group of roses named after the botanical species Rosa alba
  • ALBA (synchrotron), a synchrotron radiation facility in Barcelona, Spain
  • Alba (rabbit), a genetically altered rabbit
  • Alba (poetry), a Provencal genre of poetry
  • Alba (1907 automobile), an Austrian vintage car
  • Alba (1913 automobile), a French vintage car

[edit] In Geography

In Britain: Alba is a common name for the country of Scotland in Scottish Gaelic and other Goidelic languages, derived from a Goidelic word which referred originally to the whole of Great Britain. In most other places Alba is part of an ancient Latin name containing - and/or a modern name deriving from the Latin female adjective meaning 'white'.

In Italy:

  • Alba (CN), a town in the Province of Cuneo, Piedmont
    • Republic of Alba (1796-1801)
    • Republic of Alba (1944)
  • Alba Docilia, modern Albissola
  • Alba Fucens near the lake Fucinus Lacus, in Samnium
  • Alba Longa, Latium

In Romania:

  • Alba Iulia, the capital city of the Alba County
  • Alba, a village in Hudeşti Commune, Botoşani County

In Spain:

  • Alba de Tormes, Castile-Leon (Origin of the Castilian noble House of Alba) and Alba de Yeltes
