Albertson v. Subversive Activities Control Board

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

You have new messages (last change).

In this case, the Supreme Court of the United States ruled on November 15, 1965 that persons believed to be members of the Communist Party of the United States of America could not be required to register as party members with the Subversive Activities Control Board because that would violate their self-incrimination rights under the Fifth Amendment to the United States Constitution.

The case can be found at 382 U.S. 70, 86 S.Ct. 194, 15 L.Ed.2d 165 (1965).
