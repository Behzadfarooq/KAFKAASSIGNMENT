Albuquerque Air Route Traffic Control Center (ZAB) is located at 8000 Louisiana Blvd, Albuquerque, NM. The Albuquerque ARTCC is one out of 22 Air Route Traffic Control Centers in the US.

Their primary resposibility is the separation of overflights, and the expedited sequencing of arrivals and departures along STARs (Standard Terminal Arrival Routes) and DPs (Departure Procedures) for Phoenix, Albuquerque, El Paso, Amarillo, Tuscon, and Roswell.

ZAB assumes control over one Class Bravo (Class B) airport.

  • Phoenix Sky Harbor International Airport (PHX) in Phoenix, AZ

Along with several Class Charlie (Class C) airports.

  • Albuquerque International Sunport Airport (ABQ) in Albuquerque, NM
  • El Paso International Airport (ELP) in El Paso, TX
  • Rick Husband Amarillo International Airport (AMA) in Amarillo, TX
  • Tucson International Airport (TUS) in Tucson, AZ
  • Roswell International Air Center Airport (ROW) in Roswell, NM

[edit] Center Breakdown

ZAB is comprised of 7 Ultra High Sectors, 16 High Altitude Sectors, 13 Low Altitude Sectors, and 3 Low-High Sectors. Ultra High Sectors run from FL370 (37,000 feet) and Above to FL600 (60,000 feet). High Altitude Sectors run FL290 (29,000 feet AGL) to FL360 (36,000 feet). Low Altitude Sectors are from Surface to FL280 (28,000 feet) and Low-High Sectors own Surface and Above.

[edit] Area Breakdown

Unlike most ARTCC's. ZAB along with a few other western Centers call their Area's - Specialties. In ZAB, there are 5 specialties all named after the region they cover within the airspace. Those five are Northwest, Southwest, North, East and Southeast specialties.
