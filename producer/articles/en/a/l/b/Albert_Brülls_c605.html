Albert Brülls

Albert Brülls (born March 26, 1937 in Anrath – died March 28, 2004 in Neuss) was a German footballer, who played 25 times for his national side, including matches in both the 1962 and 1966 Fifa World Cups.

Domestically he played for and was also captain of Borussia Mönchengladbach between 1955 and 1962. During this time he led the side to their first sporting achievement in 1960, when Borussia won the DFB Cup by defeating their archrivals FC Köln 3-1. He then went on to become one of the first Germans to play for a club side outside Germany, trasferring to the Italian team FC Modena for a fee of 100,000 marks.

Brülls died on 28 March, 2004, two days after his 67th Birthday.
