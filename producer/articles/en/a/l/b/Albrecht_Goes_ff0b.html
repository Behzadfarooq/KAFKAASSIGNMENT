Albrecht Goes (March 22, 1908–February 23, 2000) was a German writer. During the Second World War he served as a pastor with the German Army, and after the war wrote several novels which dealt with his experiences. One of these, Unruhige Nacht (1950), was translated into English as Arrow to the Heart and adapted for broadcast by BBC Television in the United Kingdom in 1952.
