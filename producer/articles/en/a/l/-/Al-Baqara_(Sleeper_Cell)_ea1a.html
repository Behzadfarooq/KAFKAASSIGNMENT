Al-Baqara is the first episode of the Second Chapter from the television miniseries Sleeper Cell: American Terror, which premiered on Showtime on December 10, 2006. The episode takes places several months after the FBI successfully foiled a terrorist attack at Dodger Stadium, which resulted in the capture of terrorist cell leader, Faris Al-Farik. The episode features FBI agent Darwyn Al-Sayeed trying to infiltrate another sleeper cell. While, Faris Al-Farik endures interrogation by the CIA.

Al-Baqara was directed by Clark Johnson, known for directing television shows such as The Shield and The Wire, and was written by Cyrus Voris and Ethan Reiff.

[edit] Synopsis

Spoiler warning: Plot and/or ending details follow.

Several months after successfully foiling a terrorist attack at Dodger Stadium, FBI agent Darwyn Al-Sayeed (Michael Ealy) plans to take a teaching job at the FBI Academy in Quantico, Virginia, when his FBI case agent, Patrice Serxner (Sonya Walger), asks him to participate in one last case. A Muslim ex-convict from Darwyn's tenure at Lompoc prison has recently come back on the grid and Serxner wants him followed. But the routine recon mission leads Darwyn down a dangerous road to a possible new Cell.

Meanwhile, terrorist leader Faris Al-Farik (Oded Fehr) endures CIA interrogation. Ilija (Henri Lubatti)–the Bosnian terrorist who escaped the authorities–has found refuge with a young woman who believes he is the victim of a government conspiracy and makes plans to escape the country.
