Alternative Ulster

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Alternative Ulster , or AUwas first used as the name of a fanzine published in Bangor in 1977. That publication has no relation to the [[Belfast-based)) music magazine, created in 2003.

The magazine takes its name from the Ulster punk band Stiff Little Fingers 1978 hit which was itself named for the aforementioned fanzine[1]. It is supported by The National Lottery through the Arts Council of Northern Ireland.
