Alterna Comics was founded by company president, Peter Simeti, in February 2006. They are a small press indie comic company that lets creators keep 100% ownership of their work. Creators are even allowed (if they choose to do so) to be published under the Alterna Comics name, while they pursue larger scale publishers.

[edit] History

Alterna Comics currently has 10 titles in their collection: Spectrum, The Chair, Morbid Myths, Birth, Diary of the Black Widow, Risers, Ruin, Novo, Mr. Puffinopolus, and an in-house anthology that will be published in the summer of 2007. Sunny Hills: Asylum for the Criminally Insane was one of their flagship titles but has since been discontinued by creator Travis Gore, citing personal creative differences with his own book. Only the first issue has seen production.

Alterna Comics publishes books with color covers and black and white interiors. Producing several hundred to a thousand copies of each book, depanding on demand. A print-on-demand production service is used to order the amount of copies needed.

Alterna Comics carries all kinds of books and is welcome to every genre, with the exception of superhero comics. Unless you feel your superhero comic puts a different twist on the genre, please do not submit your superhero works. While not to say that anyone at Alterna Comics does not love or like superhero comics, Marvel Comics, DC Comics and many others do those styles much better.

Since formation in early 2006, Alterna Comics has grown in popularity and recognition. Receiving positive reviews for their works, they are often touted as having better than average work, especially for a small press company. They have also received support in the indie comics community and intend on always bringing different, creative, and intelligent work to readers everywhere.

2007 holds many great things for the indie company as they begin a convention tour. Being based on Long Island, NY - Alterna Comics plans on attending many New York conventions as well as others throughout the country.

[edit] Comic Titles

Spectrum

  • A suit has been invented that will help the paralyzed walk - but the sponsor of the project has other plans in mind! "Spectrum" is the revenge story of a scientist that gets a new beginning when a tragic accident changes his life forever. Will scientist Pierre Glass get his revenge? Find out in this ongoing series!

The Chair

  • Richard Sullivan has been on death row for 10 years, for a crime he claims he never committed. To top it all off - the prison guards usually end up torturing inmates to death far before their execution dates! Find out who ends up in "The Chair" - a 6 issue mini-series brought to you by writer Peter Simeti and artist Kevin Christensen.

Morbid Myths

  • A series based on tales of horror and humor combined, "Morbid Myths" starts things off with a bang from the first issue!

Hard Way Studios teams up with Alterna Comics to release a true horror/comedy masterpiece in this bi-monthly series.

Morbid Myths received a write-up in Wizard: the comics magazine, issue #186. Here it is: 39. Morbid Myths - Librarians get medieval on demonic forces in this richly etched-out and stunningly well-inked ongoing series from Alterna Comics and Hardway Studios. Swinging a mighty sword of smartly paced short-form storytelling, writer Dale Mettam and penciler Dwayne Biddix prove that overdue library books can be more exciting than you think when librarian Abigail Cummerbund from the Miskatonic Municipal County Library stands up to demons on a daily basis. But that's the kind of thing that can happen when you have a copy of the Necronomicon in your building. She's the champion of information and organization every school and community should be luck enough to employ. - Wizard Staff

Diary of the Black Widow

  • Brought to you by writer/artist Bret M. Herholz, this title has inspired touches of Tim Burton and Edward Gorey. Based on a young woman that kills her mates and disguises their murders, this 3 issue small release will give you the chills!

Birth

  • A graphic novel prelude to an upcoming series, writer/artist Michael Bracco has created a world filled with passion, war, and revenge. Based on two alien worlds that depend upon one another for survival of their species, Birth tells the story of the conflict that becomes of both nations when joy and love turn into resentment and bloodshed.

Sunny Hills: Asylum for the Criminally Insane

  • Truly a head turning book brought to you by Travis Gore - "Sunny Hills" has a mix of humor/horror/and just about everything inbetween. Raven is a dangerously volatile young boy, but this doesn't mean he's not friendly! A highly anticipated series, "Sunny Hills" is GUARANTEED to please!
  • Alterna Comics announced the hiatus and perhaps permanent discontinution of "Sunny Hills" by creator Travis Gore on January 26th, 2007.

Mr. Puffinopolus

  • A war between Heaven and Hell is brewing and Satan has sent his right hand man, Vordrex, to corrupt mankind. Problem is that Vordrex has been transported to Earth in the form of a teddy bear named "Mr. Puffinopolus".

Risers

  • Writer Martin Fisher puts a new twist on an old genre with "Risers". Zombies aren't all they appear to be in this book about a normal girl that becomes one of the undead.

Ruin

  • Writer and creator Tom Pinchuk has created a world filled with destruction and despair. Luckily, a robot savior has arrived. Fighting through the warlords of a savage world, can he stop the destruction before it's too late?

Novo

  • Written by Michael Bracco and based on his graphic novel "Birth" - this series will be sure to impress when it hits the ground running in April 2007!

Alterna Tales Anthology

  • Strange tales are brought to you by various creators in this anthology of sci-fi, fantasy, dark humor, and horror.

4 stories (some are ongoing) are brought to you in this double sized book!

[edit] Distribution

Readers are able to purchase the comic books on their site at the "Alterna Store". Alterna Comics does most of their business through self-distribution and currently has comics distributed in comic shops around the United States as well as through indie distributor, DimeStore Productions. They also have PDF files of their comics that can be purchased on Drivethrucomics.com, a site that is similar in design to Itunes, only it is for comic books instead of mp3's.
