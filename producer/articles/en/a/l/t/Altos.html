Altos

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

This article is about the town. For the operating system/trademark (now owned by Acer Sertek) see Xenix (disambiguation).

Altos is a town in Paraguay, located in the Cordillera Department. The town contains a German clubhouse, a church, a cemetery, and a statue of José Félix Estigarribia, the President of Paraguay (August 15, 1939 - September 7, 1940) who died in a plane crash above the town.

It is most notable as the temporary hiding place of Joseph Mengele, the notorious Nazi scientist who experimented on concentration camp inmates. Mengele and his wife Martha fled to Altos in 1959, after the West German government discovered his address in Buenos Aires, Argentina and issued a warrant for his arrest. His wife failed to adjust to their new life and left him. In 1960, panicked at the increased interest in his whereabouts by Nazi hunters and the recent capture of Adolf Eichmann, Mengele left Altos for Hohenau in southern Paraguay.
