Cultura Londres is a Latin Hip Hop group based in London England mixing Spanish & English vocals combined with conscious lyrics and thickly sliced breakbeat heavy hip hop with a London edge. As well as the usual MC & DJ combination Cultura Londres also plays out as a live band with guitar/bass/synths/drums and percussion handled by Eric Bobo (Son of the late great Latin percussionist Willie Bobo) they always make efforts to include a strong visual element to their shows incorporating ideas such as live Capoiera & Flamenco artists into their performance.

Cultura Londres are a Latin-American hip-hop group from London, England. The group formed in 2004.

The group consists of Rich (Guitarist and producer), Tiago (Rapper), Eric Bobo (Eric Correa, Percussionist, August 27, 1968) (became a member in 2005). One aspect of their popularity is their blend of old school hip hop styles with strong song based melodies.

[edit] Name

The name Cultura Londres refers to the strong culture of a city shaping the sound of the bands music.

[edit] Early career

Initially a Producer and MC combination Cultura Londres started out in North London in early 2004, quickly making contacts with latino radio stations and rappers in the US they put together a 4 track demo which was printed up and distributed at the Latin Rap Conferance in San Diego which was purely to let the Latin rap commuinity of the west coast know of this new group coming out of London, England.

Through these demos the band was contacted by well-known Latin rapper Kemo The Blaxican (formerly of Delinquent Habits) and they soon arranged a colaboration on a track called Fiesta (which appears on the groups debut EP released in August 2006).

[edit] Continued career

Using 2004 & 2005 as building years Cultura Londres spent alot of time building industry contacts and recording radio specials for various Latino stations in the US.

During 2005 Cultura Londres moved a step further by starting to build the live band which would allow them to bring the energy of the record to the stage instead of the hip hop standard DJ and MC set up which they felt didnt really represent their sound or give the audience/fans a decent enough show.

Soon after the group where put in touch with Eric Bobo via DJ Muggs of Cypress Hill whom had been very supportive of the project, Bobo and Cultura recorded 2 songs and started on a third when Rich decided to offer Bobo an official place in the group as the percussionist/drummer. Now with a world class percussionist in the group they new that the live shows would now have something extra that would give the fans a real buzz from watching.

Next came an offer from a local director by the name of Arlen Figgis - the son of Director Mike Figgis - to shoot a music video. The video for "Good Times" was shot in under 3 hours and has been played out on MTV ESP around the globe and brought their music to a wider audience.

The groups headlining live debut came in October 2005 at Cargo which is a very exclusive and well regonized small venue in the heart of London. With practically no promotion the group managed to sell out the venue with over 400 people there for their debut show.

Next came a headline slot at the Academy in Islington (capacity 800) which the group brought Capoiera Artists on stage with them and brought the house down with over 600 people there just to see them!

During this time the group had songs played on various underground stations and gained a couple of places on various Latin hip hop compilation albums in the US all have which have been invaluable to building the project steadily on the underground scene providing them with a good foundation on which to break into a more main stream scene and bring Hip Hop en Espanol to more people.

2006 sees the group recording new material and rehearsing for their festival shows in 2007 with the full live band. Also they will be releasing their debut EP called Todo along with a new music video which will be availble worldwide through their record label Altered Beats

2007 will see their debut album released and their first official single and video will chart in April 2007.

[edit] Sound

Cultura Londres bring together a blend of various musica styles but rooted firmly in Hip Hop all presented live a more of a rock show. Main influences include, Pink Floyd Portishead Sepultura Company Flow Aesop Rock Madlib and Led Zepplin.

The sound for the debut EP Todo is a much more underground grimy sound, just to introduce the group. Expect the album to sound like a cross between Cypress Hill, Fun Loving Criminals & the Black Eyed Peas
