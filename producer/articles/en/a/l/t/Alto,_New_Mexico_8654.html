Alto is a small suberb of Ruidoso, New Mexico located in the Lincoln National Forest. The population was 1,213 at the 2000 census.

[edit] History

Alto was well-established as a settlement before 1900...farms and ranches speckled its canyons reinforcing the need for its very own post office. The original Alto post office was appropriately located in a small log cabin off of the larger of the pathways. And, of course, the post office centralized Alto's community providing a venue for what was happening with the locals and the outside world. The best I can establish from my current research is that the Alto post office was first established in 1901 and was first settled in 1882...

Alto is located only 5 miles northwest of Ruidoso...and has consistently grown in population over the years since the first log cabin post office was established. Lincoln County, where Alto resides, was established in 1869...

Alto received it's name from the spanish word "high" which describes the elevation it sits on.

[edit] Life in Alto

Alto is located at 7,300 feet, and is heavily wooded from the surrounding forest. The community mostly sits around the Alto Lakes Golf Course where most of the more upper class citizens of the Ruidoso Valley reside. Because of it's rural charm, Alto has been viewed as a more relaxing area within Ruidoso and as so, has dramatically seen more people and more housing developments go up in the past couple of years. Alto is home to many known New Mexico artists as well as the world famous Spencer Theatre for the Performing Arts.
