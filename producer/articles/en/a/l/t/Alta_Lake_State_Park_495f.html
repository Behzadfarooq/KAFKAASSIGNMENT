Alta Lake State Park

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Alta Lake

Alta Lake State Park is a 181 acre (732,000 mÂ²) camping park located in the mountainous northwest interior of Washington. The park and adjacent lake lie beneath towering stone cliffs formed by glaciation that rise 1000 feet above the valley floor. And carry on up to the top of Old Goat Moutain which sits 4200 ft above the park. The lake is about 2 miles long and .5 miles wide. The park has 2 mile long hiking trails. Popular activities at the park are sailboarding and golf at the nearby Alta Lake Golf Course. Although quite small, this park is a very popular summer camping location.

[edit] History

The lake was named "Alta" in 1900 by a miner from Wilbur, Washington after his daughter Alta Heinz. The area around Alta Lake was originally owned by the city of Pateros, Washington, and sold to the Washington State parks system in 1951.

[edit] Location

The park lies about 2 miles (3 km) southwest of Pateros, Washington and over 20 miles from Chelan, Washington.
