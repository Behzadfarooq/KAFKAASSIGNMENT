The Altamont School, located in Birmingham, Alabama is a college preparatory day school with coeducational enrollment of grades 5 through 12.

It was established in 1975 as a merger between the Brooke Hill School (a college preparatory school for girls, which was founded in 1940), and its counterpart, the Birmingham University School (founded in 1922). For most of that time, headmaster Martin Hames sculpted the school's educational profile. In recent years, under current headmaster Tom Wheelock, the school has adopted a multi-track curriculum on the semester system, one of the many changes that the school has experienced since Hames' death in 2002.[citation needed]

[edit] Notable alumni

  • Diane McWhorter, Pulitzer Prize-winning author
  • Daniel Wallace, author of Big Fish
  • Warren St. John, reporter for The New York Times
  • Walker Percy, National Book Award-winning author
  • William J. Cabaniss, ambassador to the Czech Republic
  • Margaret Tutwiler, former ambassador to Morocco
