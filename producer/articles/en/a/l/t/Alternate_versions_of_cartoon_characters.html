While most cartoons do not feature aging characters, there are some shows which show cartoon characters in a different form: they age, or they have a counterpart who is really similar to them, or they are found in an alternate universe. Some are found in cartoons, others are found in comics, video games, or, to a lesser extent, live action television series.

[edit] List of alternate versions of cartoon characters

  • Younger versions of cartoon characters, featuring established characters as babies, children and younger teenagers. Examples include Muppet Babies, A Pup Named Scooby Doo, Jungle Cubs, Little Archie, Smallville, etc.
  • Older versions of cartoon characters, featuring young characters as teenagers, pre-teenagers, or they increase their age by a year. Examples include The Pebbles and Bamm-Bamm Show, Quack Pack, Digimon Adventure 02.
  • Junior versions of cartoon characters, featuring the kids or sidekicks of established characters like James Bond Jr., Donkey Kong Jr.
  • Female versions of cartoon characters, featuring female counterparts like Supergirl, She-Ra: Princess of Power
  • Cartoon Characters that star with their junior version, more like a father and son team like Droopy and Dripple, Scooby Doo and Scrappy Doo, Popeye and Son
  • Next generation of cartoon characters: Characters with no relation to particular characters but are inspired like Tiny Toon Adventures, Generation X
  • Futuristic versions of cartoon characters: Characters seen in the future like Archie 3000, Sherlock Holmes in the 22nd Century, The Partridge Family 2200 A.D, Duck Dodgers
  • Prehistoric versions of cartoon characters: Shows depecting established characters in the Stone Age like Archie 1
  • Ancestors of cartoon characters: Shows depecting ancestors of established characters like The U.S of Archie
  • Descendants of cartoon characters: like Loonatics Unleashed
  • Foreign counterparts of cartoon characters: like Demashita! Powerpuff Girls Z
  • Pets of cartoon characters: like Krypto the Superdog
  • Characters that Age: like Daria
  • absurdities like Tale Spin
