Alps Electric Co., Ltd. (アルプス電気株式会社, Arupusu Denki Kabushiki-gaisha?) (TYO: 6770 ) produces electronic devices, including touchpads. Alps is a multinational corporation, headquartered in Tokyo, Japan. The nearest rail station to the headquarters is Yukigaya-otsuka station on the Tokyu Ikegami line. The company was established in 1948. As of 2004, the CEO is Masataka Kataoka. Alps is also well-known for the Alpine brand of car audio.

Alps licenses their touchpad hardware from Cirque Corporation, but write their own drivers. Their drivers are Windows certified. They all have simple features such as edge scrolling, but no programmability. They are mostly found in Sony notebooks. They also manufacture keyboards for Apple computers, including the original Macintosh and the first iMac.

They also created printers, but have since dropped support of them.
