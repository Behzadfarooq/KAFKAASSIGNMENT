Alphonse Beau de Rochas

Alphonse Beau de Rochas (1815-1893) was a French engineer who originated the principle of the four-stroke internal-combustion engine. His achievement lay partly in his emphasizing the previously unappreciated importance of compressing the fuelâ€“air mixture before ignition. He accomplished this around the same time as German engineer, Nicolaus Otto.
