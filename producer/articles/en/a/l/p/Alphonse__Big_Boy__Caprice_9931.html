Alphonse "Big Boy" Caprice was a fictional character in the comic strip, Dick Tracy, created by Chester Gould. He is an organized crime boss, based on Al Capone.

[edit] Origin

The Big Boy appeared in the first published story of the strip when his minions robbed and murdered Emil Trueheart right in front of his family and Tracy. In response, Tracy joined the police force and became the thorn in the gangster's side.

Big Boy was featured in several different continuties and Sunday stories (the Sunday strips and daily strips ran separately for the first few months) during the early years of the strip, and, even in those stories in which he wasn't the featured villain, was sort of a "gray eminence" behind the gangster faction Tracy was constantly fighting.

[edit] In later comics

The character eventually faded away until the 1970s, when Max Allan Collins revived him for one last story. By this time, Big Boy's power had faded considerably from an autocratic boss to merely one of a criminal ruling elite committee known as The Apparatus. Furthermore, his health declined seriously, to the point that he was terminally ill.

Determined to have his revenge on Tracy for ruining his power base, Big Boy created an open contract to kill him for one million dollars to anyone willing to attempt it. As a result, Tracy found himself repeatedly attacked by amateurs. As Tracy and his comrades took precautions and investigated, a professional criminal bomber, "Little" Lettel, made his own attempt with a car bomb. Junior's wife, Moon Maid, was killed by the bomb.

As evidence arose that the attempts on his life were mob related, Tracy publicly announced that he was taking command of the Organized Crime Unit to retaliate. This prompted the Apparatus board to confront Big Boy and tell him that they would not tolerate something so brazen with such serious consequences as attempting to murder police officers. To stop the contract, the Apparatus set up their own one million dollar contract on anyone who collects on Big Boy's.

With the contract neutralized, Big Boy resorted to an elite hitman, the Iceman, to murder Tracy. With the promise of a foreign home to escape the Apparatus' reprisal on top of the million, Iceman made his attempt and eventually failed. Fatally wounded, Iceman told Tracy about Big Boy's machinations.

Tracy came to Big Boy's home to arrest him for murder. Upon hearing of Tracy's arrival and intentions, Caprice had a fit of rage that was too much for his health and died.

[edit] Other media

In the 1990 film adaptation of the comic strip, Big Boy is played by Al Pacino in an Academy Award-nominated performance. It is in this film that Big Boy is first referred to as "Al 'Big Boy' Caprice." The movie character is physically very different from the comic strip character.

Spoiler warning: Plot and/or ending details follow.

In the film, Big Boy Caprice is the former protegé of crime lord Lips Manliss (Paul Sorvino), whom he eventually murders after rising to power. His criminal empire spreads through the city like cancer, prompting Tracy (Warren Beatty) to make the diminuitive gangster's destruction his own personal mission. Caprice's moll, Breathless Mahoney (Madonna), takes a sexual interest in Tracy; for his part, Tracy is tempted by the smoldering femme fatale, even though he is faithful to longtime girlfriend Tess Trueheart.

By the end of the film, Tracy has unraveled Caprice's hold on power, with help from a mysterious, faceless vigilante called "The Blank". Caprice kidnaps Tess, and holds her hostage in a tower containing the operating machinery of a drawbridge. Tracy corners him and is about to arrest him, when The Blank — whom Tracy has learned is none other than Breathless herself in disguise — emerges from nowhere and tries to kill Caprice, who shoots and mortally wounds her. The enraged Tracy lands a powerful haymaker punch to Caprice's jaw, sending him plummeting to his death in the drawbridge's gears. Tracy is able to rescue Tess, whom Big Boy has tied to one of the gears, just before she is crushed.
