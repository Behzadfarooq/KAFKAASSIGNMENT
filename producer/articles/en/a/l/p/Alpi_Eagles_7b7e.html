Alpi Eagles

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

AlpiEagles is an airline based in Italy. It is a privately owned regional airline operating scheduled passenger services linking 15 domestic destinations, as well as international services to Albania, Czech Republic, France, Russia and Spain. Its main base is Marco Polo International Airport), Venice[1].

  • IATA Code: E8
  • ICAO Code: ELG
  • Callsign: Alpi Eagles [2]

[edit] History

The company was established in 1979 as an aerobatic team and later diversified into the executive transport market. In May 1996, AlpiEagles started scheduled passenger services from Venice. It is owned by Interbanca, Ithifly-Veneto Svillippo and Titano-Airontours[1].

[edit] Services

AlpiEagles operates the following services (at October 2006):[citation needed]

  • Domestic scheduled destinations: Alghero, Bari, Bergamo, Bologna, Brindisi, Cagliari, Catania, Lamezia Terme, Milan, Naples, Olbia, Palermo, Reggio Calabria, Trieste, Verona and Venice.
  • International scheduled destinations: Athens, Barcelona, Bucharest, Chisinau, Cluj Napoca, Kiev, Moscow, Nice, Prague, Pristina, Timişoara, Tirana and Valencia.

[edit] Fleet

As of March 2007 the Alpi Eagles fleet includes [1] :

  • 10 Fokker 100
  • On order: 20 Embraer 175/Embraer 195[citation needed]
