[edit] Thailand, Laos and Cambodia

Thailand, Laos and Cambodia are incorrectly marked as "non alphabet": it should be "other alphabet". âˆ’Woodstone 22:10, 24 June 2006 (UTC)

there is a discussion on Talk:Thai alphabet that Thai is a Abugida not an alphabet --Astrokey44 01:03, 21 July 2006 (UTC)
If Thai, Lao, and Khmer are considered abugidas rather than alphabets, then the Devanagari script used in Hindi and Nepali should be treated the same way. It makes more sense to me to indicate the Brahmic family of scripts in the green color, which would include India, Nepal, Sri Lanka, Myanmar, Laos, Thailand, and Cambodia. Tom Radulovich 07:04, 24 July 2006 (UTC)
I agree. Bangladesh, Nepal, Sri Lanka, Myanmar, Laos, Thailand and Cambodia should all be included in the Brahmic family. That map definately needs to be updated. Jagged 20:45, 28 July 2006 (UTC)
alright, it has been updated --Astrokey44 00:35, 29 July 2006 (UTC)
Thanks, Astrokey44! In the interest of accuracy, however, the legend should probably be changed from "Brahmic alphabet" to "Brahmic alphabets", as the alphabets in the Brahmic family are rather distinct from one another. Also, Ethiopia uses the Ge'ez alphabet officially, which would put it in the "other alphabet" category rather than the "no alphabet" category. Tom Radulovich 05:15, 30 July 2006 (UTC)
