[edit] Copyright

Please stop adding "Copyright" statements to edit summaries on articles that you create. You are agreeing to license your contributions under the GFDL when you add content here, as you are clearly warned on every edit page., Thanks. Tonywalton  | Talk 14:55, 8 December 2006 (UTC)

Again, please stop adding "copyright" to your edit summaries, as you did when creating the article Armogeddon. By submitting contributions to Wikipedia you are agreeing to release that text under the GFDL - text marked clearly as copyrighted to some individual or organisation is liable to be deleted. Also, please take care to check your spelling - Wikipedia already has an article under the correct title, Armageddon, so I have changed the article to a redirect - it is Wikipedia convention to have only one article on a particular subject or aspect of a subject, rather than multiple articles which would be very hard to maintain, and to use redirects for synonyms, likely misspellings, etc. Thank you. ~Matticus TC 09:39, 12 December 2006 (UTC)
