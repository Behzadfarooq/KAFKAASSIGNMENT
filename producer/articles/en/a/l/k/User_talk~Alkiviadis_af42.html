[edit] Image copyright problem with Image:Daniel_Bouton.jpg

Thanks for uploading Image:Daniel_Bouton.jpg. However, the image may soon be deleted unless we can determine the copyright holder and copyright status. The Wikimedia Foundation is very careful about the images included in Wikipedia because of copyright law (see Wikipedia's Copyright policy).

The copyright holder is usually the creator, the creator's employer, or the last person who was transferred ownership rights. Copyright information on images on Wikipedia is signified using copyright templates. The three basic license types on Wikipedia are open content, public domain, and fair use. Find the appropriate template in Wikipedia:Image copyright tags and place it on the image page like this: {{TemplateName}}.

Please signify the copyright information on any other images you have uploaded or will upload. Remember that images without this important information can be deleted by an administrator. You can get help on image copyright tagging from Wikipedia talk:Image copyright tags. --OrphanBot 05:14, 22 January 2006 (UTC)

[edit] Société Générale

Hi Alkiviadis, trying to translate your excellent article about the french bank Société Générale into German, I read ocaisonally, that you speak German too. So may be, you can help me, because I think, you are extremly familiar with the banking language: How would you translate "mutual fund management business" into German? Although speaking English on an advanced level and using different dictonairies, I didn't find a translation, that really fits. Translating it literal, it would be "Gegenseitigkeitsmanagementgeschäft" or "Mehrzweckfondsmanagementgeschäft", but I do not know anything like this in banking business. Would you please describe a "mutual fund"? If you like to read my complete translation before publishing, please give me a note on my talk page. Best regards and thanks! --Rosa Lux 23:55, 28 February 2006 (UTC)

Hi Rosa Lux, sorry to be so late at my answer but I hadn't been checking wikipedia for a long time. So, I suppose that "mutual fund management business" would be best translated in German as "Investmentfondsmanagementgeschäft".

I hope I helped, (Alkiviadis 11:50, 29 March 2006 (UTC))
