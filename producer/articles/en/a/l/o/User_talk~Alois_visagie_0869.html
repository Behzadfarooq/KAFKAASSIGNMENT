[edit] ANTS

KEEP UP THE EXPANSION! GOOD WORK! James Janderson 12:49, 9 July 2006 (UTC)

I'm getting the basics down first, and then will progress to adding information on known ranges, habitats etc. Regarding wikipedia fair use of images, if I get permission from copyright holders to use their images of specific species, how do I go about showing this so the images arent pulled?

{helpme} I don't know the answer, but someone should be along shortly...--Kchase02 T 13:01, 9 July 2006 (UTC)
With fair use (Wikipedia:Fair use) you don't need permission from the photo owner. You are basically claiming that the image is so special (ie it's the only one in existence and no others will ever be made) that anyone can use it for critical commentary.
If you get permission from a copyright holder to use their image in Wikipedia, we can't accept it (unless we use fair use). Wikipedia images must be free, so we need the copyright holder to release it under a free licence (fair use is the exception to free images). Info: Wikipedia:Image use policy, Wikipedia:Image copyright tags.--Commander Keane 13:11, 9 July 2006 (UTC)
