Allamah

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

An Allamah (Persian: علامه), also spelled Allameh and Allama, is an honorary title carried by only the very highest scholars (marjas) of Islamic thought, jurisprudence, and philosophy.

While normally used as an honorific by Shi'ites, it is used in its linguistic meaning by some Sunnis. Sir Muhammad Iqbal, architect of Modern Pakistan is referred to, for example, as Allamah Iqbal. Some sunnis who have acheived the rank of a scholarship in several discipines are often referred to by the title. In Shia rank, those holding the title "allamah" surpass all other clerics such as Ayatollahs and Hojatoleslams. Currently, marjas are accorded the title Grand Ayatollah (Arabic/Persian: آية‌الله العظمی  Ayatollah al-Uzma).

In any given generation, there are only a few Allamas.

Some of the most famous Allamas are:

  • Allameh Tabatabaei
  • Allameh Helli
  • Allameh Majlesi
  • Allameh Mullasadra
  • Allama Mashraqi
  • Allama Hassan Turabi
  • Allama Muhammad Iqbal
  • Allama Rasheed Turabi
  • Allama Hamiduddin Farahi
