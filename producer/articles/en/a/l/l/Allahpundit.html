Allahpundit (also known as Allah) is a blogger best known as the author for defunct blog Allah Is in the House, a satirical political blog existing 2003-2005, and as an active contributor to Michelle Malkin's Hot Air.

Allahpundit originally wrote in the persona of "Allah," using similar language to that of terrorist organizations, but from the viewpoint of the God such organizations describe. (The author distinguished between the violent and hateful viewpoint being satirized and the viewpoint of mainstream Muslims.) The blog's unique approach and timely humor caused it to become greatly popular days after its inception, and its information and/or humor led it to be cited by most of the top conservative bloggers [1] [2] [3] [4] [5] [6] [7]and many blog-sympathetic writers [8] [9] of the time including Roger L. Simon, who wrote, "These days I learn more from Allahpundit, where I found this link, than I ever do from Meet the Press." [10]

Allah Is in the House began on August 27, 2003, at http://allahakbar.blogspot.com/, later moving to http://allahpundit.com/. Allah used a God-like persona and specialized in written and Photoshop-based political satire, until a hiatus in May 2004. When the blog reemerged, it preserved the satiristic nature while largely abandoning the Allah "voice." In September 2004, Allahpundit was a key player in the Killian documents controversy. Mary Mapes, producer of the 60 Minutes segment that triggered the Killian documents controversy, blamed Allahpundit, among others, for "working anonymously in what appeared to be huge numbers, in unison, to destroy the Bush-Guard story." (Truth and Duty, p. 201) However, the blog stopped being regularly updated after October 2004, the web address http://allahpundit.com/ being abandoned, then, after occasional entries at http://allahakbar.blogspot.com/, shutting down altogether. However, largely due to the Killian controversy, the website was among the Top Blogs Cited in Political Postings for the United States presidential election, 2004.

The author guest-blogged, under the name Allahpundit, for Michelle Malkin [11] [12] in March and April 2006, before becoming the most active contributor to Malkin's Hot Air website, which, like the blogger's old website, is referenced by bloggers and blog-sympathetic reporters [13]. Malkin has credited Allahpundit with "turn[ing] the site into a must-read" and thus causing the site to go "from nowhere to a top-30 site on Technorati's Top 100 list." [14] Allah Is in the House posts are archived at The Internet Archive, although the image files, which include the Photoshopped pictures for which the site was well known, are not.
