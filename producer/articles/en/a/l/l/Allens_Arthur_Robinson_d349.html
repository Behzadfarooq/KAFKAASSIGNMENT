Allens Arthur Robinson (AAR or "Allens") is an international law firm, with lawyers operating in:

Bangkok

Beijing

Brisbane

Hanoi

Ho Chi Minh City

Hong Kong

Jakarta

Melbourne

Perth

Phnom Penh

Port Moresby

Shanghai

Singapore

Sydney

In Australia, the firm is consistently ranked as one of the top three commercial law firms (the other two being Mallesons and Freehills). AAR is also ranked highly in its Asia-Pacific jurisdictions and is involved in most of the region's landmark corporate transactions.

AAR has a professional relationship with leading UK law firm: Slaughter and May, to work together in the Asia region (excluding Japan).

AAR was formed on July 1, 2001 by the merger of the Melbourne-headquartered Arthur Robinson & Hedderwicks and the Sydney-headquartered Allen Allen & Hemsley, which created one of the largest law firms in the Asia Pacific. It now has over 1500 staff.

The Sydney firm that became Allen Allen & Hemsley has been in existence since 1822, making it Australia's longest continuous legal partnership.

Arthur Robinson & Co. began trading in Melbourne on May 11, 1914, just prior to the outbreak of World War I. In 1984, Arthur Robinson & Co. merged with Hedderwick Fookes & Alston to form Arthur Robinson & Hedderwicks.

Because of its age and influential client base, AAR is often regarded as Australia's 'Establishment Firm.' Many AAR partners have left the firm to take up appointments on the Australian High Court and AAR partner James D. Wolfensohn served as President of the World Bank from 1995-2005.
