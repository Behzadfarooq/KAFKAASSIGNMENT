All That Jazz is a 1989 (see 1989 in music) studio album by the American jazz singer Ella Fitzgerald.

This album is notable as Fitzgerald's last studio recording.

All That Jazz eschewed a tendency by Fitzgerald in past decades to record popular and commercial songs of the day, and instead concentrated on jazz standards from the Swing era, a period which some fifty years earlier had seen her begin her legendary career in music.

Ella's performance on this album won her the 1991 Grammy Award for Best Jazz Vocal Performance, Female, her final Grammy award.
