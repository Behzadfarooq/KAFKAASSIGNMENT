Church on Blood in Honor of All Saints Resplendent in the Russian Land is a Russian Orthodox church in Yekaterinburg dedicated to Nicholas II of Russia and his family.

[edit] Romanov Execution

In 1916, Tsar Nicholas II and his family was taken captive and held as prisoners during the civil war that erupted in Russia between the Bolsheviks and those that preferred the Provisional Government of Tsarism.[1] The Tsar and his family were initially kept near St. Petersburg but as fears of a potential attempt of their liberation grew larger the decision to execute the royal family became a reality.[1] On July 17, 1918 while imprisoned in Yekaterinburg, a town in the Ural Mountains, the entire royal family, Tsar Nicholas II, Alexandra, Grand Duchesses Olga, Tatiana, Maria, Anastasia and Tsarevich Alexei Nikolaevich of Russia was taken to the cellar of the Ipatiev House and assassinated.[2]

[edit] The Temple

After the end of the Bolshevik rule in 1991 the plan to create a lasting memorial to the Romanov family had begun to progressively gain popularity.[4] A state commission was gathered and architechrual as well as economic funding plans were drawn. It was decided then to create a temple in the site of Ipatiev house that would include two churches, a patriarchal annex and a museum dedicated to the royal family.[4] The temple in finish covered a total of 29,700 sq. ft. and its architecture was created in Tsar Nicholas II preferred Russian/Byzantine style. 85 years after the execution of the Romanov family the temple was consecrated by hierarchs from all over Russia.[4]
