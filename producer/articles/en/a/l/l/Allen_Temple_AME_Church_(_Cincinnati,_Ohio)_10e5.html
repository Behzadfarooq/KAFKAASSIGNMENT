Allen Temple AME Church ( Cincinnati, Ohio)

The Allen Temple AME Church in Cincinnati, Ohio, is the mother church of the Ohio Chapter of the African Methodist Episcopal Church. Founded in 1824, it is the oldest AME church in Ohio[citation needed] and the oldest operating black church in Cincinnati.[1] It is named after Richard Allen, founder of the AME Church.[2]

The church was founded in 1823 as a congregation of the Methodist Episcopal Church because of the prejudicial treatment blacks received in the predominantly white churches.[2] The first pastor chosen by the black congregation was Rev. James King, at the time a slave living in Lexington, Kentucky whose owner allowed him to hire his time. In 1824, following the founding of the AME denomination, Rev. King and Rev. Philip Brodie led the congregation to join with the AME.[3] The congregation occupied at least four structures, each known by its own name, prior to 1870, the last of which was known as "Allen Chapel" and constructed around 1850.[2][4] During this period the church was a waystation on the underground railroad.[5] In 1862, the church helped launch the second AME church in Cincinnati, the Brown Chapel AME church.[6]

Because of growth and vandalism, in 1870 the congregation purchased the structure previously housing the Bene Israel Sephardic synagogue for $40,000, reflecting its position as one of the early black churches with a predominantly middle class congregation.[7][2] At this time the church took the name "Allen Temple". This structure is building #75001414 on the National Register of Historic Places.[8] Fiscal pressures from the mortgage and 1974 fire damage led to the formation of charity groups. After paying off the debts of the congregation, these groups turned to social and welfare work.

Issac Nelson Ross, later the 41st bishop of the African Methodist Episcopal, served as past of Allen Temple for 5 years between 1900 and 1907.[9]

The church remained at the former synagogue until 1979, when it moved to the former Roselawn Baptist Church. In 1996, The Reverend Donald H. Jordan, Sr. became their pastor and in 1998, they bought Swifton Commons Shopping Center, and began worshipping there in 2000. In 2002 they renamed it "Jordan's Crossing". In 2004, they built a 1,200 seat sanctuary and in 2005, Reverend Dr. Mark W. Thompson was appointed their pastor after Reverend Jordan retired after 28 years in ministry and nine and a half years at Allen Temple. Dr. Mark W. Thompson, who was appointed on November 13, 2005 and preached his first sermon at Allen Temple, "I'm Thankful For My Change" on November 20, 2005. Pastor Thompson came from Big Bethel AME Church in Atlanta, Georgia. Pastor Thompson has adopted the theme "Becoming Whole Through Christian Living".

Upward Bound Youth Ministries is 102 children strong and minister every 2nd and 5th Sunday. The youth minister is Cory W. Ferguson of Dayton, Ohio. UBYM has a Training Choir (ages 3-5), Children's Choir (ages 6-12), and Worship Chorale (ages 13 and up). They have anointed Bible Studies and rehearsals every Wednesday Night.
