Allegheny

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Allegheny (see also the variants Allegany and Alleghany) is the name of several places in the eastern United States.

The name derives from the Allegheny River of New York and Pennsylvania and originally comes from the Lenape (Delaware) Indian language. Its meaning is not definitively known, but has been translated as "fine river".

Geological features:

  • Allegheny Mountains, a mountain range & portion of the Appalachian Mountain Range
    • Mountains within the Allegheny Mountain Range:
      • Allegheny Mountain (West Virginia, Virginia)
      • Allegheny Mountain (Pennsylvania, Maryland)
      • Little Allegheny Mountain, Pennsylvania, Maryland
  • Allegheny Plateau, which terminates in the east at the Allegheny Mountains
  • Allegheny Front, the escarpment delineating the E edge of the Plateau & Mountains, separating them from the lower Ridge and Valley Appalachians (the Great Valley) to the E.

Geographic features:

  • Allegheny County, Pennsylvania
  • Allegheny, Pennsylvania, a city annexed by the city of Pittsburgh in 1911.
  • Allegheny Township, Pennsylvania
  • Allegheny National Forest, Pennsylvania
  • Allegheny National Recreation Area, Pennsylvania

Public works:

  • Allegheny Mountain Tunnel, a vehicular tunnel on the Pennsylvania Turnpike
  • Allegheny Trail, a hiking trail in West Virginia
  • Allegheny Reservoir on the Allegheny River

Organizations:

  • Allegheny College, in Meadville, Pennsylvania
  • Alleghany Corp.
  • Allegheny Airlines
  • Allegheny Foundation
  • Allegheny Technologies
