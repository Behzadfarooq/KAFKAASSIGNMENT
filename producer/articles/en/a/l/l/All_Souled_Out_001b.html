All Souled Out is an EP by Pete Rock & CL Smooth. It was released in the summer of 1991 to rave reviews and spawned a modest hit in "The Creator".

After producing and remixing songs by various hip hop and R&B artists in the late to early 90s, Pete and CL signed to Elektra Records and released All Souled Out as a warm up to their debut album, Mecca And The Soul Brother.

[edit] Samples Used

  • "Good Life" samples "Girl You Need A Change" by Eddie Kendricks, "Same Old Thing" by The Meters, "I Wanna Be Where You Are" by Levy O'Donel & "Long Red" by Mountain.
  • "Mecca & The Soul Brother" samples "UFO" by ESG, "Kool Is Back" by Funk Inc., "N.T." by Kool & The Gang, "People Make The World Go Round" by Freddie Hubbard & "We've Only Just Begun" by Levy O'Donel.
  • "Go With The Flow" samples "Funky Drummer" by James Brown & "Burning Spear" by S.O.U.L..
  • "The Creator" samples "From This Day On" by Eddie Bo, "Instant Groove" by King Curtis & the Kinpins, "Save Me" by Aretha Franklin & "Honey Pot" by The Mar-Keys.
  • "All Souled Out" samples "Shoot Your Shot" by James Brown & "Turtle Walk" by Lou Donaldson.
