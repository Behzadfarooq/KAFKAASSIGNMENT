Allan Crite

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Allan Rohan Crite (b. March 20, 1910, in North Plainfield, New Jersey) is a Boston-based African-American artist who studied at Boston's Museum of Fine Arts from 1929 to 1936. Crite's paintings fall into two categories: religious themes and general African-American experiences. His 1946 painting Madonna of the Subway blends the two genres, depicting a Black Holy Mother and baby Jesus riding Boston's Orange Line. Crite's works hang in such major American art galleries as the Smithsonian, the Museum of Fine Arts, and the Art Institute of Chicago.

In the 1940's, Crite created two books, including 1948's Three Spirituals from Earth to Heaven, in which he illustrated religious stories from such African-American spirituals as Swing Low Sweet Chariot and Nobody Knows the Trouble I See.
