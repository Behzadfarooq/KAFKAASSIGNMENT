"All I Wanna Do" is a pop–dance song written by Brian Higgins, S. McLennan, Tim Powell and Matt Gray for Dannii Minogue's third studio album Girl (1997). The song was produced by Higgins and Gray and received a positive reception from music critics. It was released as the first single in the third quarter of 1997 and reached the top five in the United Kingdom, selling 175,000 copies. In Australia the song missed the top ten, charting at number eleven. In the UK, "All I Wanna Do" was also released to success on the dance charts, where it reached the top position on the Upfront Club chart, becoming Minogue's first single to do so.

"All I Wanna Do" marked a significant change in Minogue's career and image, as did her album Girl from which it was taken. Her image had arguably, become highly sexualised, culminating in her nude calendar photo shoot and the suggestive videos and album artwork which accompanied her new releases.

This was also the first release on Minogue's new record deal with Warner Bros. Records. The multi-million dollar deal initially looked set to lauch her career worldwide, especially with the success of "All I Wanna Do". Unfortunately, the success of the first single would not be felt by the album Girl. Subsequent singles would not sell quite as well, though they were still hits.
