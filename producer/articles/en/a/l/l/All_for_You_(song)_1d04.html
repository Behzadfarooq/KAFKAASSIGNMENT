"All for You" is the first single from the album All for You by R&B singer Janet Jackson.

[edit] Song information and chart performance

The song is based upon a sample from Change's "The Glow of Love", which featured Luther Vandross. It made history by being the first single ever to be added to every major format of radio on the day of its release. The song became Jackson's tenth number-one on the Billboard Hot 100, staying at number one for seven weeks. It also reached number one on the Hot R&B/Hip-Hop Singles & Tracks and Hot Dance Music/Club Play singles charts. The song was also an international success, peaking in the top ten of almost all European countries, and reaching the top five in Australia and New Zealand. It hit number one in Japan, Canada, and other countries. The song won several music awards, including a Grammy Award for Best Dance Recording.

[edit] Music video

Janet smiling at cute guy on the subway.

The video, directed by Dave Meyers, is based upon a colorful two-dimensional world. Released in March 2001, it shows Jackson on the subway checking out a cute guy, actor Paul Kunstman, reading a book. When the train stops, Jackson gets off and her dancers show up, and they began high octane choreography in the subway station throughout the first verse and second chorus. Jackson and the dancers then go to a cafe and begin dancing. This also continues throughout the second verse and the third chorus.

Next, Jackson and the dancers move to the nightlife scene. Jackson and the dancers have a dance breakdown to the beat from Jackson's own 1986 hit "The Pleasure Principle". Her 1990 hit "Escapade" and her 1998 hit "Go Deep" is also sampled. After the breakdown, Jackson and the dancers continue with the choreography til the end of the song. This is when the dancers leave and Jackson spots the cute guy outside of a club. He smiles at her and she waves at him. Jackson turns around and leaves. The video fades out.
