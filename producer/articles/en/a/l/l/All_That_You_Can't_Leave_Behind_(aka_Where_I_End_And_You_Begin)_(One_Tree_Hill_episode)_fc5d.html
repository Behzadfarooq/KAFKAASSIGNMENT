"All That You Can't Leave Behind (aka Where I End And You Begin)" is episode 5 of season 1 of the television show One Tree Hill.

[edit] Plot

Spoiler warning: Plot and/or ending details follow.

It’s time for Tree Hill Ravens’ father and son charity basketball game and Dan looks definitely way too eager to come back on the court. Lucas asks Keith to play with him and he accepts, looking a bit surprised but honored at the same time. Anyway, when Keith finds out Lucas is thinking about changing his last name - thanks to Dan’s last uplifting speech, of course - Keith does invite him to think it over carefully. And so does Karen. “It’s just a name, Luke. What you do with it is up to you.” Nathan wants Peyton back but, at the moment, she looks kind of distracted: actually she is spending a lot of hours in her car, speeding through a red light. As she explains to Lucas, it’s all about her mother, who died in a car accident when Peyton was little. “My mom ran one red light. I run them all the time and nothing happens. It’s not fair”.

Hailey confesses to Lucas she decided to tutor his half-brother because this was the only way to convince Nathan to leave him alone. Lucas gets her point but, as soon as he sees Haley and Nathan hugging, he’s clearly bummed. Meanwhile, Peyton feels always worse but, surprisingly, coach Whitey tries to help her out. He knows what she’s going through, since he lost his wife. Later, Peyton and Lucas meet up and the kid lets her know that he wish he could change what happended to her mom. Even if Peyton seems - almost - touched by his words, she keeps on her creepy “car” experiments, endangering herself once again. “Father and Son” basketball game can get started and the fathers team doesn’t play that good. Dan cannot stand it and ends up pushing Nathan to the ground, humiliating him in front of the whole crowd. After that, Lucas can’t help but realize that he’s lucky to have Keith’s attachment: he decides not to give up the Scott name. The game is done and Peyton shows up. Nathan is happy to see her but she lets him know she didn’t come for him; in that moment, Lucas walks out the door. The three of them look at each other until Peyton walks away.

In the last scene Peyton sits in her car, waiting for the green light. Metaphorically, as soon as she starts driving, all the lights are finally green.

Spoilers end here.
