Allan Lamar Green (born September 20, 1979 in Tulsa, Oklahoma,USA) is a professional boxer of the super middleweight division. A notable prospect, Green has a current record of 23 wins with 16 knockouts, and one decision loss.

Green had a stellar amateur career despite not making the Olympic Games, winning the 2002 National Golden Gloves, at 178 lbs, and compiling a 55-6 record. During the 2002 Golden Gloves tournament Allan beat Mike Tyson's two decade old 8 second knockout record. He also was five time Oklahoma state champion, and four time regional champion, before coming pro at age 23.[1]

Green made his professional debut on November 9, 2002, with a one round TKO victory against Robert Dykes. Green's career was on the upside, and made his ShoBox (a notable prospect show) debut with a 7 round TKO victory against Rocky Smith. It was perhaps his second appearance in ShoBox, that gained notoriaty with his blistering eighteen second KO victory against Jaidon Codrington. Green stunned Codrington and pinned him to the ropes, then threw brutal combinations before finishing with a left hook to the chin and knocked Codrington unconscious and fell through the ropes. The one round victory earned Green the 2005 Ring Magazine knockout of the year.

Green's first challenge was against Donnie McCrary. after knocking down McCrary early in the 3rd round, Green got knocked down for the first time in his career, but rallied back won by a six round KO. Green's next fight was against Contender member Anthony Bonsante, where Green cruised and won by a sixth round knockout.

On October 14, 2006, Green brutally TKO'd former Olympian Jerson Ravelo on ESPN.

On March 3, 2007, Green moved down to 160 pounds to face middleweight contender Edison Miranda. Miranda would win by unanimous decision in San Juan, Puerto Rico. The bout was televised on HBO, and was noted for Green's surprisingly unspectacular performance.

Miranda dominated most of the fight and knocked Green down twice in the last round. Green mentioned afterwards that moving down to middleweight was a major factor in his lack of aggression. He did, however, manage to floor Miranda in the eighth round, but failed to capitalize on the opportunity. Green stated his intentions to remain at super middleweight after this fight.
