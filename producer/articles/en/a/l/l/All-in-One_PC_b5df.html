All-in-One PC

The Apple iMac, an All-in-One PC.

An All-in-One PC is a PC built into hardware which is usually a separate peripheral, such as a monitor or keyboard. It is sometimes abbreviated as "AIO". Apple computers are popular examples of this, with products such as the iMac, in which the computer is built behind the monitor. Older 8-bit computers, such as the Commodore 64 or Atari 800, also fit into this category.

These PCs include the usual items one might expect to find on any PC, including USB ports and a CD-ROM drive. A VGA port on a monitor-based all-in-one PC could also be feasible for dual screen viewing.

When the PC is part of a touchscreen monitor, the term panel PC or LCD PC might also be used. The Zero-Footprint-PC is an example of a PC-in-keyboard arrangement.

Gateway has released a PC-in-monitor arrangement and has announced a TV-sized all-in-one PC including Windows Media Center. NEC has announced a similar HDTV [1].

EUROCOM offers an entire line of all-in-one, Portable Eco-Friendly LCD PCs with optional touchscreen, privacy LCD surface or plexiglas protective surface.

Typically computers with this designation, even those marketed as "media center" devices, do not include audio amplification circuitry. An external device is still required if one wishes to use standard unpowered speakers to achieve louder, or higher quality audio reproduction than available with "computer speakers".
