Allegations of apartheid against Brazil have been made accusing the society or goverment of systematic racism. Growing inequities in the economic and social status of Afro-Brazilians in Brazil have been described as "social apartheid".[1] According to São Paulo Congressman Aloizio Mercadante, a leading member of Brazil's leftist Workers' Party (PT), "Just as South Africa had racial apartheid, Brazil has social apartheid."[2] The exclusion of youth (particularly street youth) from Brazilian society has also been described as "social Apartheid".[3] Carlos Verrisimo states these two inequities are often inter-related,[4] and Cristovam Buarque, Governor of the Federal District from 1995 to 98, Minister of Education from 2003 to 2004, and currently PT senator for the Federal District argues that "Brazil is a divided country, home to the greatest income concentration in the world and to a model of apartation, Brazilian social apartheid."[5] The Nation has described Brazilian president Lula as "fighting to bring the poor of Brazil out of economic apartheid".[6]
