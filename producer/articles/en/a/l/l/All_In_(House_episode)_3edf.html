All In is the seventeenth episode of the second season of House, which premiered on the FOX network on April 11, 2006.

[edit] Synopsis

Spoiler warning: Plot and/or ending details follow.

During a charity Casino Night, Cuddy gets a six-year-old patient whom she dismisses as being dehydrated. However, House realizes that the patient is exhibiting the same symptoms as an elderly woman named Ester Doyle who died under House's watch twelve years ago. House has only 12 hours to keep the new patient from dying in the same way. The disease the patient has was named in the episode as Erdheim-Chester disease.

[edit] Diagnosis

Erdheim-Chester disease

[edit] Trivia

  • The last name of Ester is Doyle. Arthur Conan Doyle was the creator of Sherlock Holmes, the famous detective which House is partially based on.
  • House writes "Esther" on his board though on the case folder it states her name is spelled "Ester".
  • The entire episode takes place at night.
  • House says his lucky number is 42.
  • The music playing in the first scene of the charity event is "'Deed I Do", performed by Diana Krall on the album Live in Paris.
