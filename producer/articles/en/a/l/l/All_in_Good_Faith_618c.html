All in Good Faith was a British sitcom starring Richard Briers that aired for three series from 1985 to 1988. It was written by John Kane, who also wrote Terry and June and Never the Twain. It was made for the ITV network by Thames Television. It is not currently available on DVD.

[edit] Plot

All in Good Faith was written especially for its lead star, The Good Life actor Richard Briers and it was his first ITV sitcom. He played The Rev. Philip Lambe, who in his middle age decides to move from his wealthy Oxfordshire parish to one in Edendale, a fictional urban town in the Midlands. He is determined to do things in his new parish, and is faced with new problems like homeless people. He is accompanied by his wife Emma, sixteen-year-old daughter Miranda and twelve-year-old son Peter.
