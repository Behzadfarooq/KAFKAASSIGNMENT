All Saints' Academy is a private Episcopal high school located between Winter Haven and Lakeland, Florida.

[edit] History

All Saints’ Academy was established in 1966[citation needed] at St. Paul’s Episcopal Church. Four-year-old pre-kindergarten (PreK-4) was added in 1967. Over the next twenty years, enrollment grew, and by 1989 the school had enrolled students through the seventh grade.

As the result of a donation by the Hampton sisters, the Hampton Campus was established in 1995 to accommodate the Middle and Upper Schools. In 1997, the first eight students graduated from All Saints’ Academy.

The Lower School and three-year-old pre-kindergarten (PreK-3) through fifth grade remained at St. Paul’s Church until 2001, when they moved to the Carl Floyd Road site, adjacent to the Hampton Campus. This campus was named the Bostick Campus in honor of its benefactors Guy and Jan Bostick. An Early Childhood Center was also added, accommodating PreK-3 through kindergarten students in the Hizer House, named in honor of its benefactors Cynthia and DeVon Hizer.

When attending All Saints' Academy, all students must sign and abide by the following Honor Code:[2]

  • I will act honorably at all times - never lying, cheating, stealing, or plagiarizing.
  • I will abide by the rules of the code of conduct.
  • I will show concern and respect for all members of the All Saints' Academy community and their property.
  • I will show concern for the school environment by helping to keep it clean and safe.
  • I will represent their school in an honorable and positive way at all times – on and off campus.
  • I will report violations of the Honor Code.
... All Saints’ Academy expects a commitment from both student and parent(s) in upholding the values set forth by the Honor Code.

[edit] Accreditation

All Saints’ Academy is accredited by the Florida Council of Independent Schools and the Florida Kindergarten Council. All Saints’ belongs to the Central Florida Episcopal Schools Association and as such is represented on the Board of the Florida Association of Academic Non-Public Schools. All Saints' Academy is also member of NAES, NASSP, and ASCD.

[edit] Fine arts

The arts form an important part of the curriculum at All Saints’ Academy. Originally a single Fine Arts Department, with Mrs. Pam Danis as department head, the department grew to the extent that, in 2003, it was split into two departments, Visual Arts under Mrs. Danis, and Performing Arts under Dr. Robert Phillips.

The Visual Arts Department offers courses in beginning through advanced 2- and 3-dimensional art. On average, anywhere from one to seven graduates of All Saints’ Academy claim Visual Arts as their major in freshman year of college. Colleges that these students have gone on to attend include Rhode Island School of Design, Georgia Tech Architectural School, Rollins College Art Department, Furman Art Department, Florida Southern College Art Department, Savannah College of Art and Design, Ringling School of Art, Boston University College of Fine Arts, the University of New Hampshire Art School, and the University of Central Florida Art Department. The faculty includes Mrs. Danis, an award winning artist, and Samuel Romero, who holds a masters degree from the School of the Art Institute of Chicago.

The Performing Arts Department offers courses in theatre, dance, classical guitar, orchestral string instruments, choir, keyboard, music appreciation, and AP Music Theory. One to three graduating students from ASA go on to major in music, and an equal number go on to major in dance. All Saints' theatre students have experience acting in regional theatre, and local and national television. The All Saints’ Academy guitar ensembles have taken a number of prizes in national level competitions, and the high school guitar quartet won first place in the 2006 University of Texas at Brownsville and Texas Southmost College national guitar ensemble competition. The performing arts faculty includes Dr. Phillips, a Japan Fulbright Memorial Fund scholar who enjoys an international performing, recording, and publishing career on classical guitar, Mark Thielen, a highly sought after clinician and the conductor and music director of the Imperial Symphony Orchestra, Bruce Oelker, a pianist and vocal coach who has toured with Broadway musical companies throughout the United States, Jeannine Wright, a former Disney dancer and teacher, and Paul Hughes, an equity actor and artistic director of the Lakeland Community Theatre as well as a recipient of the Japan Fulbright Memorial Fund scholarship, State of Florida Artist’s Fellowship, and the SURDNA Arts Fellowship.
