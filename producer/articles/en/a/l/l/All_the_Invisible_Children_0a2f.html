All the Invisible Children is an anthology film consisting of 7 short subjects with a total runtime of 116 minutes, averaging 16 minutes each. It premiered at the Venice Film Festival on September 1, 2005.

[edit] Segments

In order of appearance:

Spoiler warning: Plot and/or ending details follow.
  1. Tanza, directed by Mehdi Charef (set in Africa; the country is not specified, but it was filmed in Burkina Faso) - A child soldier is instructed to blow up a building. He enters the building to place a bomb. It turns out to be a school, and he decides not to do it.
  2. Blue Gypsy, directed by Emir Kusturica (set in Serbia & Montenegro) - A boy is released from a juvenile detention center. He wants to be a barber. However, his father forces him to steal again.
  3. Jesus Children of America, directed by Spike Lee (set in USA) - A couple is addicted to drugs and infected with HIV. They have a daughter who is also infected, and bullied for that at school. The film features among others Rosie Perez as Ruthie.
  4. Joao and Bilu, directed by Kátia Lund (set in Brazil) - A boy and a girl earn money collecting empty cans, cardboard, etc., for recycling.
  5. Jonathan, directed by Ridley Scott and Jordan Scott (set in UK) - War photographer Jonathan (David Thewlis) looks back at his youth.
  6. Ciro, directed by Stefano Veneruso (set in Naples, Italy) - Two boys rob a car driver from his watch, and sell it to a man working in an amusement park, for money and free rides.
  7. Song Song and Little Cat, directed by John Woo (set in China) - An old man is guardian of a young girl. When he dies she ends up in a group of children with an adult leader, and she has to sell flowers in the street. The leader gets angry when a child does not make money. The cheerfulness of the girl is contrasted with the unhappiness of a rich girl and her mother.

The soundtrack features a song by Tina Turner and Elisa Toffoli, "Teach Me Again".
