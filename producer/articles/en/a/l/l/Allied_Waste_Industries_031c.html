Allied Waste Industries NYSE: AW is a Fortune 1000 company headquartered in Phoenix, Arizona. Allied Waste Industries, Inc. is the second largest non-hazardous solid waste management company in the United States, with annual revenues of over $5 billion and assets of nearly $14 billion. Allied Waste serves approximately 10 million residential, commercial and industrial customers in 110 major markets within 37 states and Puerto Rico.

Allied Waste operates as a vertically integrated company through their network of over 700 collection companies, transfer stations, landfills and recycling facilities. Their structure allows for the most efficient operations in the industry, and has positioned Allied Waste as a true leader in the industry. They have the highest internalization rate in the industry and hold number one or number two positions in nearly every market they serve

Their nationwide team of 26,000 employees shares their commitment to exemplary customer service and efficient, innovative solutions that respect and safeguard the nation's public health and natural resources.

[edit] Operations

Allied Waste is an alliance of hundreds of companies that have been serving their communities with local pride and ingenuity for decades. Regardless of their size, they are and always will be a local business. More than half of their operating management team consists of former owners and second- and third-generation industry professionals. Their managers know the business, they know the communities, and they are proud of the service they provide. Allied Waste helps them succeed by providing company-wide resources, including technical support, best practices, direction in regulated areas and the benefits of economies of scale.

[edit] Collection Services

Allied Waste's network of 304 collection companies provides collection services to approximately 10 million customers nationwide. They serve residential customers under individual household subscriptions as well as municipal contracts. In addition, they serve industrial and commercial customers across the country, providing customized non-hazardous solid waste disposal services for local, regional and national accounts. Allied Waste is committed to providing excellent service to all their customers. They support this commitment by investing in equipment, technology and training programs that maximize their operational excellence and ensure the safety of their customers, employees and environment.

[edit] Transfer Services

Their 161 transfer stations are fundamental to their vertically integrated operations, as they improve their efficiency in collecting and transporting solid waste to recycling facilities and sanitary landfills.

[edit] Disposal Services

Allied Waste owns and operates 168 active landfills across the country, with an average remaining life of 40 years. Their professional landfill experts employ state-of-the art methods to maintain the landfills and protect the environment, while meeting or exceeding all federal, state and local regulations. They also provide customized consulting services for handling and disposing special non-hazardous wastes and keep their customers abreast of changing environmental and regulatory requirements.

[edit] Recycling Services

Their 57 recycling facilities offer waste handling and collection services and educational programs in certain markets. They recycle approximately 1.7 million tons of recyclable commodities a year, including paper, glass and plastics and reusable construction debris.
