[edit] 13th Congressional district

Prior to the 2001 reapportionment, Republican voters had the edge in 13th Congressional district registration. The changes eliminated some Democratic voting strength areas in Montgomery and added much of NE Philadelphia. Montgomery's central Congressional district henceforth only served 46% of its population. Although this decreased the Republican registration edge, Joe Hoeffel had never served any of the city before and found himself drawn into the same district as Congressman Robert Borski, who opted to retire. Republican nominee Melissa Brown campaigned agressively in the new city portion and came suprisingly close there, but lost to Hoeffel since he carried both county portions and maintained enough Montgomery Republican voter support. In 2004, Democratic voters claimed the registration edge in the 13th.
