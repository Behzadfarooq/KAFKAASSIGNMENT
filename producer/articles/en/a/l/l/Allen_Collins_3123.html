Allen Larkin Collins (July 19, 1952 – January 23, 1990) was one of the founding members and guitarists of Southern rock band Lynyrd Skynyrd. His unique guitar playing and songwriting skills were vital to the success of the band during his time with them. He was born in Jacksonville, Florida.

Collins and childhood friends Ronnie Van Zant and Gary Rossington formed the band that would later become Lynyrd Skynyrd in the summer of 1964. The band received national success beginning in 1973, and ending in 1977 with a tragic plane crash that killed three band members. Collins was seriously injured in the crash that killed Van Zant and guitarist Steve Gaines. His right arm was so severely damaged that doctors recommended amputation. Collins' father, however, stopped the amputation process and Collins would play the guitar again.

Noteworthy among the many songs Collins wrote are the rock opus "Free Bird", and "That Smell", both co-written with Van Zant. Collins provided the blistering guitar solo at the end of "Free Bird" that made the tune so memorable.

During the early 1980s, Collins continued to perform in The Rossington-Collins Band and the Allen Collins Band. The former band, which also included former Lynyrd Skynyrd members Billy Powell and Leon Wilkeson, enjoyed modest success, charting a few singles, notably "Don't Misunderstand Me," before tragedy would strike again. In 1980, Collins' wife Kathy suddenly died of a hemorrhage following a miscarriage. A 1986 drunk-driving accident killed Collins' girlfriend and left the guitarist paralyzed from the waist down, and with limited use of his arms and hands. He would never play guitar onstage again.

All remaining members of Lynyrd Skynyrd reunited to perform in 1987, but due to his injury Collins was only able to participate as musical director. Having pled no contest to a charge of vehicular manslaughter (resulting from the 1986 accident), Collins was asked by the presiding judge to address fans at every Lynyrd Skynyrd concert, explaining why he couldn't perform. Because of Collins' accident, Skynyrd donated a sizable amount of concert proceeds from the 1987-88 tour to the Miami Project, which is involved in treatment of paralysis.

Allen Collins died in 1990 from pneumonia as a result of his earlier accident. He is buried in Jacksonville, Florida.

For most of Skynyrd's tenure, Collins used a Gibson Firebird guitar. In 1976 he switched to a Natural finished Korina Gibson Explorer and would use that all the way through his tenure with the Allen Collins Band. Starting in late 1977, he would also use a Gibson Les Paul occasionally.
