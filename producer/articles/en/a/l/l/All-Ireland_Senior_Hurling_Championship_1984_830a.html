The All-Ireland Hurling Championship 1984 ran from May to September of that year. The All-Ireland Hurling Final, which was played in Semple Stadium in Thurles to mark the centenary of the Gaelic Athletic Association, was won by Cork who beat Offaly by 3-16 to 1-12.

[edit] Format

The All-Ireland Championship was run on a provincial basis as usual. The Munster Championship saw Cork emerge victorious and book a place in the All-Ireland semi-final, while Offaly captured the Leinster Championship to book their semi-final spot. Galway, who faced no competition in Connacht, played the All-Ireland "B" champions, Westmeath, in an All-Ireland "quarter-final" which would decide the third team to take part in the semi-final. Antrim, who faced no competition in Ulster, were nominated to play in the semi-final also.

[edit] All-Ireland Hurling Final

Since 1914, Croke Park in Dublin had been the home of the All-Ireland final every year. But 1984 was special as it was the centenary year of the Gaelic Athletic Association. To mark the occasion it was deemed fitting that the All-Ireland final be played in the "home of hurling" and the town where the association was established - Semple Stadium in Thurles.

3:00pm on Sunday, September 2 was the date of the Centenary All-Ireland Hurling Final between Cork and Offaly. In spite of the association celebrating it's 100th anniversary, this was the first evern championship meeting of Cork and Offaly. Cork had played in the previous two finals but had been defeated by Kilkenny on both occasions. A third consecutive defeat in a final had to be avoided at all costs. The game got off to a frantic pace, however, both sides remained evenly balanced for most of the first half. After 27 minutes Seánie O'Leary of Cork scored the goal that tipped the match in the Leesiders favour. At half-time Cork led by a single point, however, they were the much more dominant side.

Cork once again dominated the opening stages of the second half and led by 12 points after 17 minutes. Although Offaly battled hard two more goals from Cork's Seánie O'Leary and Kevin Hennessy sealed the game for the Leesiders. Cork won the day on as coreline of 3-16 to 1-12.
