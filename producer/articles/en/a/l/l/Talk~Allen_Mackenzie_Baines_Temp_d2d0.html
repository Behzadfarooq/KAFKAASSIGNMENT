Allen Mackenzie Baines

Dr. Allen Baines was an eminent Canadian physician and was the first Physician-in-Chief of the Toronto Hospital for Sick Children. He was born in Toronto on May 12th 1858, the son of Thomas Baines, Secretary of the Clergy Corporation and later Crown Land Agent for the Home District. Dr. Baines was educated at Upper Canada College and by F.W.Baron at Cobourg. He became interested in medicine and received medical degrees from Trinity College Toronto, the University of Toronto and also studied in London where he received his L.R.C.P. He soon returned to Toronto however and became deeply involved in the medical establishment of that city. In adition to his interest in medicine he was an exceptional sportsman, winning many a cricket match for his city and country against the United States. He was the toxicology and jurisprudence examiner for Trinity College while also being a physician at the home for incurables. In 1913 he was chosen to be the first Physician-in-Chief for the Hospital for Sick Children (Sick Kids Hospital Toronto) and continued in that possition until his death in 1919.
