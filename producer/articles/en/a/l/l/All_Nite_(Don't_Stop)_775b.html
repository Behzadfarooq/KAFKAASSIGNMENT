[edit] Song information

Released in 2004, the song contains a sample of Herbie Hancock's 1975 "Hang Up Your Hang Ups". It was also the lead double A-side with "I Want You", which appeared on some international releases of the single (for example, the United Kingdom) and contains the Damita Jo Japanese bonus track "Put Your Hands On". The song "R&B Junkie" can be found on the promo vinyl single of "All Nite (Don't Stop)".

[edit] Music video

Janet in the video for "All Nite (Don't Stop)".

The video was directed by Francis Lawrence (which also directed 2001 "Someone to Call My Lover"). It was filmed in an abandoned hotel in L.A. during a blackout. Dancers are seen lounging. One dancer finds a battery and hooks it up to large stereo system and the music starts. Jackson is laying on a table and says, "Attention, it's time to dance". Jackson and dancers do choreography until power is restored, lighting a sign saying "Damita Jo".

After the Super Bowl incident, MTV refused to air Jackson's new videos including "All Nite (Don't Stop)". The video could only be seen on MTV Hits and MTV Jams. It aired regularly on BET and VH1.

[edit] Charts

The song peaked at number one hundred and nineteen on the Billboard Hot 100 (number nineteen on the Bubbling Under Hot 100 Singles chart). Although not her first single to miss the Billboard Hot 100, this was a major disappointment. The song missed the top thirty in Japan and most European countries. It was a top thirty hit in Australia and a top twenty hit in the UK. Despite all this, "All Nite (Don't Stop)" managed to climb to the top of the Hot Dance Club Play chart, securing yet another number-one dance hit for Janet.
