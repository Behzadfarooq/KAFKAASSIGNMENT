Allpoetry.com is an online poetry website that was created in 1999 as a way for new and experienced poets alike to post their work online in a "community-like" environment. As of August 2006, it contains the works of almost 150,000 poets with over 1,700,000 poems, though many poets have multiple screen names.

[edit] Comment system

Users are able to leave comments and critiques on works written by other users, and by doing so, receive "site points" in return. These points allow users to:

  • promote their own works or that of someone else, such that they are visible from any webpage on the site;
  • hold contests for points and "site trophies";
  • attend classes that the site holds during certain times of the year; and
  • create and join specific social groups.

This point system has, however, been criticized by many users who post their works on the site. More specifically, they have criticized the "comment system" because the comments posted were not as constructive and as incisive as they would like. "Internet lingo," as it is commonly referred to, is rampant throughout the site, and many experienced users, whose poetry has developed significantly, often challenge newer poets due to the poor quality of their poetry. At times, the challenge arises because the newer poet cannot match the level of the experienced poet, whereas at other times, "internet lingo" and "StIcKy CaPs" are used throughout the poem.

The site itself has received criticism for its policies as well. Some users feel that the webmaster is too strict, while others feel that the policies are too lenient, depending on which policy is being challenged.

[edit] Groups

Regardless of these criticisms, however, the site has managed to become quite popular, with users exchanging comments virtually every second. A group system has been set up which allows a user to create an online group. Such groups comprise users who have either applied to participate or simply joined in without an application, depending on the policies of the group leader. Users that are not part of the group may not view the group's chat boards, or anything dealing within the group's boundaries.

[edit] Chatterbox

Another one of the features that Allpoetry provides is the existence of an integrated chat room, officially known as the "chatterbox", or "CB" to the users. This allows users to chat with each other while browsing through poems or other pages on the site. There are very few restrictions on the content of the discussion(s), but self-promotion is discouraged, and vulgar language is censored, ostensibly for the benefit of the many underage users. You can be banned from the chatterbox for inane reasons such as commenting on your bodyweight.

[edit] Sister sites

Allpoetry.com also has "sister" sites, consisting of:

  • Storywrite.com: Allows users to write and post stories and general prose of any length;
  • Oldpoetry.com: Houses many biographies, and the poetry of poets from all time periods, ranging from Geoffrey Chaucer to Sylvia Plath to Edgar Allan Poe.
  • Allphilosophy.com: Allows users to discuss views and ideas based on religion, love, ethics, morality, and society.
  • Sharepoetry.com: This was created as a place for more serious poets, ready to move to the next step. Among other things, it offers a chance to be constructively critiqued and "rated", based on various criteria such as rhythm, language, and form.
  • Findquotations.com: This site has over 20,000 quotes from over 5,000 authors divided up into 204 categories. It also has the feature to allow members to create their own lists of quotes and add their own quotes.
