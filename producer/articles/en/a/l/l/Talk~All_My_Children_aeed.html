[edit] Sorry....

Sorry about changing the All My Children page, but I think it’s an improvement. I’m a longtime fan of the show, I’ve read all the books, and have some inside sources about the program. I’m not writing anything with a bias. I thought the All My Children page needed the rewrite and revamping – not only to give some info about the writers and producers, but to add some interesting pictures as well. If you think the history section is too long, you could always move the opening sequence section or the actors/awards lists to another page. If you look at the OLTL page, the AMC history portion isn’t that long at all. If the readers just want storyline info read verbatim, then they could always go to ABC.com and read the yearly history there. I guess I wanted to make the AMC page at Wikipedia unique. Again, I’m not writing anything with a bias. From what I've seen in books, soap magazines, and all over the internet, is what I wrote about the "golden periods" and low periods. I didn't say these were for a fact, but were considered by a majority – through the soap press, through ratings, in books, and from what I've seen from a majority of fans. I don’t think that’s a “POV Mess”. I got the AMC cast picture from my ABC MediaNet account in January 2005. The other pictures I got from the AMC History site (with permission) and the Tad & Dixie screencap was made by me. Why was the article about Phil and Tara deleted? Just because their storyline was thirty years doesn't mean that people don't want to read about it!

[edit] Film vs. Tape

"As of July 31 2006, the show has a filmed (as opposed to taped) appearance." Right or wrong?

I've heard people ask me about the new appearance. So I say it's right. Mike H. I did "That's hot" first! 20:12, 4 September 2006 (UTC)


What's the technical difference between filmed and taped? I notice the appearance change, but can't account for it. 13:11 (EDT) September 26, 2006

Film is on photo matrieal used origonally (please ignore misspelling) for movies only. In 1947 they began using it to record Television broadcasts as well, known as Kinescope recording. Until the 1960's all TV prodoction was done on film. Then in the early 1960's some netwroks began using videotape. Videotape is a type of magnetic tape. It was popular in the 1970's for fived days a week programs, such as soap opera for its reusablilty. Until recently all soap opreras were recorded on videotape, but tape eventually fades unlike film. Due the fact old episodes of All My Childen now look muddy due to age deteration, the producers have switched to film for its lasting qualitly. Stralia 21:00, 12 January 2007 (UTC)
