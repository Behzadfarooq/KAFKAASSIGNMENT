All Mixed Up

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

All Mixed Up is a hit single released by the rapcore/punk rock/reggae band 311. It was on the album 311, also known as The Blue Album. This album was released in 1995 and later went triple platinum, selling over 3 million copies world-wide. The song was co-written by Douglas Vincent "SA" Martinez and Nick Hexum. The duo first started working on the song during the 311 tour supporting Grassroots. After the tour Martinez wrote the lyrics about feeling "confused and being unfaithful" and Hexum took the songs title from an early Elvis interview in which he claimed he was "all mixed up" over his new found fame.

All Mixed Up was also a song from The Cars's 1978 self-titled album. This song was later covered by the Red House Painters on their 1996 album, Songs for a Blue Guitar.

American Idol contestant Blake Lewis sang a cover of All Mixed Up on 6 March 2007, telling host Ryan Seacrest afterwards that 311 was his favorite band of all-time. Idol judges Randy Jackson and Paula Abdul were not familiar with the song. Hexum said his phone was "ringing off the hook" after Lewis' performance, and the performance received a great deal of positive attention on 311 message board. [1]
