All Parties Hurriyat Conference

The All Parties Hurriyat Conference (formed March 10, 1993) is a political front formed by the alliance of 26 political, social and religious organizations in Kashmir. It is a political front created to further the cause of Kashmiri separatism.

The Hurriyat succeeded another freedom-centric organisation started by the Kashmir Bar Association in 1989 when the current Kashmiri freedom struggle started. It includes parties like Yaseen Malik's Jammu and Kashmir Liberation Front (JKLF), Shabbir Shah's People's Democratic Front etc.

Hurriyat is a Urdu word from Turkish origin meaning "freedom". It is divided into two factions. One, led by Mirwaiz Omar Farooq, favours independence and the second faction, the Tehreek-i-Hurriyat, led by Syed Ali Shah Geelani favours the accession of Kashmir to Pakistan. Since 2004, the Omar Farooq faction has entered into a dialogue process with the Government of India, with three rounds of talks held between the two sides.

At the Round Table Conference at Srinagar in May 2006 where senior leaders of Jammu and Kashmir and the Prime Minister of India were present, the Hurriyat boycotted the event. This was a setback to the dialogue that the Hurriyat was having with New Delhi.

The All Parties Hurriyat Conference is not considered representative of the various minority groups within the Kashmir Valley (i.e. the Kashmiri Pandits).
