Alliant Energy Center

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

The arena during the 2004 WIAA Girl's State Basketball Tournament

Alliant Energy Center (Veterans Memorial Coliseum) is a 9,762-seat multi-purpose arena in Madison, Wisconsin. Also known as the Dane County Coliseum, it was home to the University of Wisconsin Badgers ice hockey team before the Kohl Center opened. The Coliseum was built in 1967.

The Alliant Energy Center is often a venue for big-name concert groups when they come to Madison.

The state high school hockey tournament takes place each year at the Alliant Energy Center. The skaters have a love-hate relationship with the facility and its ice. Many skaters complain about the ice surface and how it typically is cared for. Two other complaints include the boards of the rink as being too flexable, and not loud enought to hear pucks that are banked off them. However, the major gripe about how hot the building is on both the ice and in the locker-rooms. Yet for all the complaints, most realize the historical implications of playing in the building and how it is the symbol of the state high school hockey community's Championship game. State tournaments for hockey have been held in the Coliseum since 1970 (with only a handfull being played in Green Bay during the 1970's due to conflicts).

Depending on the scheduling availability of the much larger Kohl Center, the arena also has been the site of the state high school girl's basketball tournaments, and continues to host some Badger hockey games, as the Badger basketball teams have scheduling priority at the Kohl Center.

The Alliant Energy Center also has an exhibition hall that has a total of 255,000 square feet. It hosts many events and conventions ranging from small local events to the annual World Dairy Expo. More than 65,000 people visit Madison for the Dairy Expo on an annual basis.
