[edit] Description

Imperium Galactica II: Alliances is a PC game developed by Digital Reality and released in 2000. It is the sequel to Digital Reality's previous game, Imperium Galactica. Unlike in Imperium Galactica, Imperium Galactica II features complete control over your empire at the onset of the game and is therefore more similar to other empire-building 4X (eXpand, eXplore, eXploit, eXterminate) games such as Master of Orion.

Gameplay – There are eight unique races involved in this game which almost all can be traded with or spied upon, a great number of buildings, ships and tanks which all can be customized with their own weaponry. The gameplay involves managing your colonies, expending your empire, conquering enemy planets, researching, diplomacy with other races, spying, space battles, ground battles and more..

Multiplayer – The game supports multiplayer either by host or by IRC network. Every player can host their own game. The game uses port 5200 for incoming data and port 6667 for outgoing data (tcp protocol). Which means players need to enable port 5200 on their firewall to be able to host a game.

Skirmish mode – The game originally lacks a skirmish mode, but it was added with the 1.06 patch, and with the release of the 1.05 patch a mod was created to add this functionality.

[edit] Plot

Spoiler warning: Plot or ending details follow.

The story of Imperium Galactica II is not entirely linear. Instead, what is featured is several scenarios pitting one empire against another, and the main campaign, where the player can choose one of three factions: The Kra'hen Empire, The Shinari Republic, or The Solarian Federation. Each race has different objectives in their respective campaigns, and the player can only win the campaign by meeting these dynamic goals or conquering the galaxy by eliminating all other factions.

Kra'hen Campaign – As part of a brutal and bloodthirsty race, your race has a fetish for collecting the heads of your enemies (as soon will be revealed in the gameplay videos) and is entering the galaxy occupied by the other seven races in the game. As Imperator, your objective is to do whatever is necessary to appease your God-Emperor. His orders - to eliminate all other races from the galaxy!

Solarian Campaign – Legend tells of four "tears" (data crystals) strewn across the galaxy by your ancestors that have the power to make the human race invincible. Representing the humans of the far-future, your Solarian Empire must recover these "tears" through diplomacy, trade, and war. It is eventually discovered that many of the other races in the game such as the Iberon, Godan, and Toluen, were genetically-altered humans adapted to conform to specific climates.

Shinari Republic – As the weakest race militarily, the Shinari Republic resorts to espionage and trade to turn things to their advantage. Upon encountering the Kra'hen, you must aid the Solarians in uniting all the other races in the galaxy to face this common threat. But when the Kra'hen are defeated, you turn down the Solarian Federation's offer for a galaxy-wide alliance because "war is good for profit" - instead, you must somehow reanimate the mad Solarian Emperor Kaileron, whose clout and radical views would certainly plunge the galaxy back into conflict.

[edit] Colony management

Colonies are what keep your empire alive and must be managed if they are to prosper and grow. Colonies generate income in the form of tax and docking fees paid by for traders, but only if you build facilities for the traders. They also produce the ships and tanks necessary to sustain a war. If you don't manage colonies well enough, a colony will revolt and either join another empire or become independent instead. To make things easier, the computer can be told to "automanage" one aspect of the colony.

[edit] Citizens

Citizens need several buildings to keep them happy. You need to provide them with housing, food, law enforcement, medical care and recreation. If they have a shortage of either they will start to complain. Which at start will be noticed by a lower population increase and eventually will lead into a revolt.

Complaining – Citizens will complain for what they need. The population always complains about a colony hub and will start to complain about housing approximately after growing to 5.000, for food after reaching 6.000, for hospitals after reaching 7.500, for recreation after reaching 10.000 and for police after reaching 11.500 in size. Please note that these numbers are approximately and displayed here only to give an impression.

Recreation complaining – Citizens aren't easily satisfied when it comes to recreation. They both complain about recreation and about a certain level of recreation. With moderate taxes, when they reach 10.000 in size they will be satisfied with a park, 20.500 with a bar and when becoming larger they will want even more. Please note that these numbers are approximately and displayed here only to give an impression.

Taxes – Your citizens will always complain about taxes. Putting taxes down will make them more happy, as putting taxes up will give greater chances of starting a revolt. See #Income.

Citizen Growth – The more happy your citizens are the quicker your colony will grow. Since taxes are what they complain about most, asking no taxes at all is the most efficient way to rapidly increase the size of a colony.

[edit] Income

To be able to pay for your expenses, you will need some income from your colonies. Colonies generate income in the form of tax and docking fees paid by for traders.

[edit] Taxes

Increasing taxes is one of the ways to increase your income from colonies. It has a drawback however: citizens will complain more the higher you put your taxes, thus decreasing the rate at which your colony grows.

[edit] Auto building

To make it more easy to manage all your planets, the game also has autobuild features. Autobuilding will build one building at a time, waiting for it to complete, and then choose a new building to build. All autobuild settings build powerplants to make sure your buildings are powered. Setting the priority level changes how much money there will be used for autobuilding on the planet. The following autobuild features can be assigned to planets:

Civilian – Constructs mostly civilian buildings and makes sure the population is kept happy.

Production – Builds ship and tank factories.

Technological – Usually a ship research center will be build first, taking in consideration that the greatest treat to a colony usually comes from space. Then building and tank research centers will be build.

Ship Production – Only builds spaceship factories.

Tank Production – Only builds tank factories.

Military – Builds planet fortresses, planetary guns and shields.

Spy Centers – Builds spy centers.

Trade – Builds trade ports and trade centers.

[edit] Buildings

Every building in Imperium Galactica II: Alliances has a cost to build and needs energy and workers to run. If you have a shortage of either the computer will automatically choose building(s) to shut down. You also have the option to shut down buildings yourself, but doing so will also require you to pay attention to when you want to re-enable to building. Most of the buildings need to be #Researched before they can be build.

The game divides the buildings into four sections: civilian, industrial, military and commercial buildings.

Civilian buildings – Can be build to keep your population happy. See #Citizens for more details.

Industrial buildings – Research buildings, factories, and the spy centre.

Military buildings – Defence buildings against space and ground attacks

Commercial buildings – Trade buildings and powerplants.

[edit] Research

Everything in the game needs a certain amount of research centers before being available to research. Every research center that you have on top of the amount required will lower the time that is needed to complete the research. Every research costs money.

Research get divided in the following way:

Ship Research – Consists of spaceships, radars, weapons, shields & engines and bombs & space bases.

Tank Research – Consists of tanks and turrets

Building Research* – Building research consist of civilian, military, research (industrial) and powerplants & extra (commercial) buildings.

* – The research panel and the building panel have different names for the categories. The name for the building panel is added in parenthesis on the right.

[edit] Diplomacy

Diplomacy is crucial if an empire other than the Kra'hen is to maintain good relations with other factions. In the diplomacy window, a player may trade information and technologies, offer a gift, tell lies about another empire, negotiate treaties, or make a demand with another faction. The diplomacy window of Imperium Galactica II has been criticized by some game reviewers as a major weak point of an otherwise good game. Those detractors point out the player's capability to ally with two factions on opposing sides of a war (with neither faction pressuring the player to take their side) and the alleged predictability of the diplomatic AI.

[edit] Spying

An empire may sabotage or gather information on another empire (unless it is the Kra'hen Empire) through spying. Each empire may have up to twelve spies (one per 'Spy Center' that is built on colonies). These spies gain experience after the completion of each mission; consequently, a well-trained spy has a better chance to complete his mission and can perform a greater variety of missions. If a spy is captured by an opposing empire while on a mission, he or she may escape, be killed, defect, or become a double spy for that empire.

[edit] Levels and skill points

During missions spies get experience points, the more complex the mission, the more experience the spy will get. Every time a spy levels up he will gain 3 skill points that can be spend on the following area's:

Infiltrate – This is important when a mission's target is outside your empire. If a spy has a high offense skill, they will have a higher chance to return safely.

Counter intelligence – This skill is useful for spies that are sent on counter intelligence missions. The more spies a player has on counter intelligence missions, the less chance enemy spies have in succeeding their missions in the players empire.

Combat – This skill is used when two enemy spies meet, the spy with the best combat skill will win.

Loyalty – If enemy forces catch the spy, they may try to convert him. Spies with higher loyalty ratings are harder to convert.

[edit] Combat

[edit] Space Battles

Space battles take place between opposing fleets consisting of large capital ships and fighter craft. Both types of ships are armed with multi-purpose lasers, but capital ships are also equipped with torpedoes and heavy guns used only to destroy other capital ships. A space battle may also take place over a planet; before the player can invade an enemy planet, he or she must defeat its planetary defenses in the form of enemy starships, orbiting battle stations, planetary shields preventing the landing of ground forces, and planet-based guns with the capability of attacking ships in space. In either case, the space battle concludes when one side has retreated or has lost all of its ships and fighters. Retreating does have its drawbacks however; some ships may be destroyed by space debris or enemy fire.

[edit] Ground Battles

A ground battle occurs once a planet's anti-ship defenses have been destroyed and the planet's defender has tanks and/or defensive fortresses on the planet. These defending fortresses and tanks must be destroyed by the attacker's own tanks if the colony is to be taken over. Like in the space battle, an attacker in a ground battle may choose to retreat. Retreating however may cause some tanks to be demolished because of the hurry in which the spaceships remove the tanks from the planet.

[edit] Space Units

There are three different types of space-based military units in the game - fighters, capital ships, and immobile space bases.

[edit] Fighters

Space fighters are excellent at supporting ground troops and harassing capital ships, but have trouble destroying anything larger than a corvette. Fighters are also best able to dodge enemy laser fire but cannot attack planetary cannons, and many players have decided to eschew fighter production altogether due to their decreased effectiveness during the late game. At a shipyard, fighters are produced in groups of five and generally cost much less than capital ships.

[edit] Capital Ships

These ships range in size from relatively small destroyers to gargantuan battleships. Only capital ships possess the raw firepower needed to punch through a planet's defenses or the carrying capacity necessary for a contingent of tanks. Capital ships are also customizable and can be fitted with hyperdrives to enable them (and their accompanying fighters) to travel much faster than otherwise possible. These advantages are somewhat offset by a lack of maneuverability; it is extremely difficult for one of these ships to evade enemy fire so they must rely on shields and hull strength to withstand combat.

[edit] Space Bases

Space bases are immobile and defend the planet against any enemy space attacks. Once considered to be "useless" by many veteran players, the effectiveness of space bases has been upgraded significantly in version 1.06 of IG2. The most powerful space bases outgun even the largest battleship by a significant margin.
