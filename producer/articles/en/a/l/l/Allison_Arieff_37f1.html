Allison Arieff

Allison Arieff (born October 29, 1966) is designer in residence at IDEO and an author and editor of numerous books on art, architecture, and popular culture.

In 2000 Arieff helped found the architecture and design magazine Dwell, and in 2002, following the departure of founding editor Karrie Jacobs, she was promoted to editor-in-chief. During her tenure the magazine expanded its readership and received a number of awards, including the National Magazine Award for General Excellence in the 100,000-to-250,000 circulation category. In August 2006 Arieff abruptly resigned from Dwell, citing a "fundamental change in the magazine's mission and philosophy" as her reason for leaving.[1]

Before joining Dwell, she held editorial positions at Random House, Oxford University Press, and Chronicle Books. She now writes a design blog called "Living Design" for the OpEd section of The New York Times.
