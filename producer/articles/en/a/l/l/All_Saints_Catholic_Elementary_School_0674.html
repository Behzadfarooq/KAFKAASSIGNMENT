All Saints Catholic Elementary School opened in September 2000, upon the recent developement of the community Berczy Village. This Roman Catholic elementary school is operated by York Catholic District School Board.

[edit] School Environment

[edit] Main Building Description

The school is divided into four divisions, the kindergarten division (JK-SK), primary division (Grades 1-3), junior division (Grades 4-6), and intermediate division (Grades 7 and 8). There are over 32 classes, with an average of 4 classes per grade. The school is especially famous for its "double gym", which resembles a high school gymnasium. There are two stories in the school. The lower story consists of the kindergarten division, Grade 1 classes, a forum (for club meetings), a "double gym", school office, custodian's office, and a library resource center. The second story of the school consists of the Grades 2-8 classes (with the exception of grade 3s and grade 6s), a French department workroom, and ESL rooms. The school is also equipped with three different flights of stairs, and one set of elevators.

[edit] Remote Buildings Description

The school also has portable buildings for Grade 3s and 6s. There are 8 portables in total.

[edit] Field

The school's playground is divided into two portions, the asphalt portion, and the field portion. The asphalt is equipped with 3 basketball nets, and 100 meters running track. The field is equipped with soccer posts.

[edit] Facilities

[edit] ESL Department

All Saints Catholic Elementary School also provides an ESL, which stands for English as a Second Language. The ESL department is maintained by Ms. Minifie.

[edit] Core Resource/Special Needs

This department provides extra help for students who are in need of special needs and helps for their academic studies. The department is maintained by Ms. Langdon.

[edit] French Department

The French department composes of three teachers, each responsible for specific grades. The 3 teachers are: M. Montas, Mme. Gouin-Wolbert, Mme. Masserali.

[edit] Library Resource Center

The library is equipped with over 500 books and novels. There are 2 sets of encyclopedias, and over 200 media information. The library also has 8 computers, and over 10 laptops. In the Audio Visual room, musical instruments (such as orff) and other projectors (such as PowerPoint projectors) are stored. To the back of the library, the floor is paved with carpet designed for the primary grades. The library is maintained by a teacher-librarian, Mrs. Sharon Gusz.

[edit] Connection to Parish

The school's parish is St. Justin Martyrs church, located on Highway 7 and Warden Avenue. Each month, a priest from the church will visit the school for the school mass. By the end of the year, priests would visit the school for reconciliations. In addition, the whole school visits the church every May of each year. Grade 8s also participates in retreats every year at St.Justin Martyrs. On January 24, 2007, All Saints had chosen delegates to visit the Ark of the New Covenant.

[edit] Transitions to High School

All Saints Catholic Elementary School is a feeder school of two high schools in Markham, Ontario. Here is a list of the two high schools, and their boundaries.

  • St. Augustine Catholic High School - West of McCowan Road
  • Brother Andre Catholic High School - East of McCowan Road

Typically, every elementary school is a feeder school to only one high school. However, in this case, All Saints Catholic Elementary School has two. However, upon the change of school boundaries of the school year 2007-2008, All Saints will be a feeder school to only St. Augustine Catholic High School.

[edit] Over-enrollment

Due to the rapid developement of the the communities Berczy Village and Berczy Commons, th school quickly grew from less than 200 enrollment in 2000 to 753 in 2006. The school has one of the largest enrollment in all elementary schools of York Region.

[edit] Future of the School

The school is expected to shrink in size. A new school will be built in Wismer Commons to reduce crowdiness in the school. Students west of McCowan Road are to remain in the school, while others go to the other school.
