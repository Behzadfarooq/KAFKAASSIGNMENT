Allison Cameron, M.D. (b. (estimated) 1975) is a fictional character on the Fox medical drama House. She is played by Jennifer Morrison.

Spoiler warning: Plot and/or ending details follow.

Cameron is a member of Dr. Gregory House's team of handpicked specialists at Princeton-Plainsboro Teaching Hospital's Department of Diagnostic Medicine. She is an immunologist and was hired by House six months prior to the pilot episode (implied in the un-aired version of the pilot). Prior to working at PPTH, Cameron was a medical intern at the Mayo Clinic, and was one of the top students in her class in medical school. House explicitly told her that he chose her for her looks: he believes that a pretty girl who went into medicine shows greater dedication than an ordinary-looking student because a pretty girl could have used her looks to get an easier job.

Cameron is currently working for House under a medical fellowship, along with Drs. Robert Chase and Eric Foreman.

She is by far the most empathetic member of House's team and possesses the best bedside manner. She is also known for her honesty, sincerity, idealism and possesses a strong moral and ethical center. Cameron is hesitant and unwilling at times to do House's bidding in which she is required to lie or deceive their patients (cf. "Sleeping Dogs Lie", "Informed Consent)" and has gone head-to-head with her boss before on several occasions on the issue. She has also shown extreme reluctance to relate bad news to patients or their family (cf. "Maternity," "Acceptance").

In Season 3, Cameron has proved to be far more assertive in her beliefs and actions, and is more willing to employ House-like tactics (without any prompting) in order to get the job done. Despite this, her consistently good intentions have not changed and her loyalty to House has proved unquestionable as he was previously under investigation for alleged drug addiction and trafficking.

[edit] Biography

Despite her kind and earnest nature, Cameron has undergone extremely traumatic experiences in her life. According to a conversation between her and House in "Fidelity," she fell in love and married when she was 21. However, the marriage lasted only six months ending with her husband's death due to thyroid cancer that had metastasized to his brain. She also revealed to Dr. James Wilson that she had met her husband shortly after he was diagnosed and that if they hadn't married, he would have died alone (cf. "Acceptance"). In another surprising confession to Wilson, she told the latter that she fell in love with her late husband's best friend, Joe, but never acted on her feelings (cf. "Spin").

Cameron's marriage is again spoken of in "Finding Judas," as she was being questioned by Michael Tritter (who had earlier frozen her bank account in accordance with his investigation) about the reasoning behind her decision to protect House. Tritter spoke of an incident ten years ago involving a calculus exam in which she would have gotten an A had she not spoken up about a marking error, and attempted to use that story as an opening to talk about her late husband. She quickly cut in and warned Tritter not to go there. Tritter then observes that she used to be a person who did the right things and that House had changed her.

Her husband is again mentioned when Dr. Foreman was goaded into a conversation about it by Cameron in "Insensitive" after he expressed unspoken disbelief that she had the real thing as they were hitherto discussing their social lives. She had previously expressed a want to amend her lack of one when she has the time and Foreman scoffed at this remarking that if it's anything a "a good-looking woman can have", it's a social life. When she informs him that she's refusing to settle, her colleague's reaction lead her to mildly chastise him for judging the relationship she had with her late husband. This leads to an outburst from Foreman who claims that even though she married a dying man and that the sacrifices she made to be with him were huge, they were at the height of her love for him, declaring that "commitment is only commitment when it has no expiration date" and that she should only ask for sacrifices when she's been in a long-term relationship. His tirade ends, however, when he sees how much he has hurt Cameron assuring her that he wasn't criticizing by way of apology.

In another nod to her short-lived marriage, Cameron encountered a homeless man with inoperable lung cancer in "One Day, One Room" in the clinic and admits him. Her attempts to offer him palliative treatment are refused repeatedly as her patient believes he will bring some meaning to his life if he dies in pain, convinced it is his punishment for leading a wasted life. He requests she be by his side ("I need you to remember me. I need someone to remember me."), and she acquiesces to this, though she tells him she will not watch him suffer. After he passes away, she is seen cleaning him with a terry cloth, taking on the role usually relegated to nurses.

“ "When a good person dies, there should be an impact on the world. Somebody should notice. Somebody should be upset." ”
 
— (2.01: "Acceptance")

On a lighter note, Cameron was invited to move to Africa by Dr. Sebastian Charles, a world-renowned activist, a fellow immunologist, and patient, and then later, on a date, in "TB or Not TB." She confided in Dr. Chase, who expressed his doubt that she would go out with Dr. Charles despite their mutual attraction and instant connection, simply because he wasn't House. At the end of the episode, she declined both offers.

In "Hunting," a man infected with HIV coughed blood into Cameron's mouth and eyes, putting her potentially at risk for contracting the disease. In order to get her to take her final HIV test, House told her he loved her out of the blue; her mouth dropped open in shock and House quickly swabbed the inside of her cheek, and told her that she would get the results the day after. By the end of the episode, Cameron finds out that House had actually opened her test results before she had a chance to find out herself, justifying his actions by citing the letter's confidentiality and that he had simply "wanted to know." (cf. "Need to Know")

Cameron's friendship with Dr. Foreman became strained after she accused him of plagiarizing her article for a medical journal after his article was published in "Sleeping Dogs Lie." Her frustration and anger were further exacerbated by her peers' indifference (especially House's) to the entire affair. She eventually confronted Foreman and apologized for the angry way she had treated him, claiming she didn't want to damage their friendship. Foreman coldly replied by informing her that their relationship was purely professional, and they were by no means "friends." However, in a later storyline ("Euphoria," Parts 1 & 2), when Foreman was dying from an unknown disease, he apologized for his prior actions (including exposing Cameron to the disease on purpose which led her to go to the patient's house in an effort to find the source despite it being declared off-limits several times by House). Although she didn't accept his apology initially (fearing it was a knee-jerk reaction to his impending death rather than one made in sincerity), she recanted her position before he was put into a chemically-induced coma. Foreman also named her his medical proxy while he was incapacitated, giving her the right to allow a white-matter brain biopsy to be performed on him, despite House's vehement objections. Though Foreman thanked Cameron when he woke, she, House and Rodney Foreman (Eric's father) could only look on in horror at the end of the episode as his brain experienced a left-right reversal when House did a check-up on his motor skills.

Cameron and House clashed in "Forever" as Foreman returned to work two weeks later, unable to remember how to make coffee. Though there were no more reversals, Foreman proved to be a thorn in their boss's side by refusing to argue with him during diagnostic sessions and also with his new bright outlook on life. This drew ire from Cameron who believed House was pushing the neurologist too hard too fast, which, in turn, led the former to accuse her of feeling guilty for doing the biopsy. This accusation was repeatedly denied.

Drs. Lisa Cuddy and Wilson incurred Cameron's wrath in "Cane and Able" when Cameron found out about their deception of House involving a quadriplegic man Cuddy treated using his diagnosis. The latter had previously given up on the case (a rare event for House) and sent the patient home before Cuddy took a chance and successfully treated him prior to his departure (cf. "Meaning"). Cameron, incensed, over her and Wilson's adamant stance that he was going overboard with his diagnostic measures which led them to hold back the truth to teach House "some humility" pressed the issue and gave her superiors an ultimatum: tell House the truth or she will. Cuddy later had her hand forced into confessing when House gave up the Department's present case at the time too. It is currently unknown if House knows that Cameron knew the truth before he did.

In "Informed Consent," Cameron was torn regarding assisting Ezra Powell's (the episode's patient) wish to die for the duration of the episode, and this kept her from participating in much of the diagnostic procedures. House, in turn, subtly directed her to discover an article Ezra, a noted cancer researcher, wrote for a medical journal decades ago about his unethical use of babies as test cases. His assumption that this would lead her to change her mind infuriated Cameron who, despite her disgust, still believed Ezra had a right to live just like any other person. When all of their efforts to diagnose Ezra were laid waste, House finally lets loose at her and heatedly demanded that she do her job by getting him a skin sample. Cameron later achieves this by simply slicing off Ezra's skin sans anesthesia and with no prior warning after confronting him about the article in which the latter expressed no regret regarding his actions. This act only served to gain her Ezra's respect, who praised her for finally taking a stand. The ensuing biopsy only revealed what he had suspected (and believed all along): he was terminal; his illness untreatable. Later in the night, Ezra mysteriously dies only thirty minutes after being declared stable. Cuddy easily believes that House is behind it despite his denial the morning after but as she walks away, there is a look of recognition on House's face and Cameron is seen in the chapel with a tear-streaked face. House puts a comforting hand on her shoulder, and quietly tells her that he is proud of her. Thus, we are led to believe that Cameron assisted the patient's suicide.

Cameron fought for her patient's rights (a severely obese man) in "Que Será Será" with an intensity that not only surprised House (who goes on to discuss this with Wilson) but also Cuddy when she breaks a MRI machine after their comatose patient wakes up and thrashes around in panic whilst they were performing tests on him. Though Cuddy is quick to blame it on House, Cameron speaks up and admits that it was her doing, justifying her actions by pointing out that the hospital would have been sued if proper care had not been given since his condition is a legally-registered disability. After their patient is discharged and tries to leave the hospital premises, he falls through the lobby's glass window, taking Cameron down with him. Later, it transpires that she had drugged him before he left saying that she didn't think he was fit to leave the hospital. The patient, on the other hand, becomes deeply agitated when she suggests the problem might be diabetes and refuses to co-operate if any treatment suggested is related to his weight. Later, House realises that their patient's hands are clubbed and suggests x-raying them, which confirms his diagnosis of terminal lung cancer. Cameron breaks the news to him, and has an encounter with House at the end of the episode where he concedes defeat and finally questioned her about the importance of this patient. She wryly referenced his thoughts about her in "Love Hurts" ("I like damaged people, remember? Explains everything I do.") when House alludes that she cares more because she's seen someone else go through a similar experience and then later tells him, "Sorry to disappoint you, sometimes the answers just aren't that simple."

[edit] Relationships

[edit] Dr. Robert Chase

Chase has always been attracted to Cameron, and this was explored early on in the series when Foreman catches him looking at her longingly in the lab (cf. "Occam's Razor"). He immediately tells Chase that it is a "bad idea" when the latter rhetorically asks him if Cameron's weird. Foreman later informs her about the new development in their team dynamic claiming she now has "total control" over her relationship with him simply because she questioned if sex could kill in a diagnostic session. Cameron later took advantage of this by cornering him in the department's office and apologized for being glib when she was talking about sex before and immediately launched into a breathless tangent when a wide-eyed Chase tells her that they don't have to talk about it.

“ "Sex...could kill you. Do you know what the human body goes through when you have sex? Pupils dilate, arteries constrict, core temperature rises, heart races, blood pressure skyrockets, respiration becomes rapid and shallow, the brain fires bursts of electrical impulses from nowhere to nowhere and secretions spit out of every gland, and the muscles tense and spasm like you're lifting three times your body weight. It's violent. It's ugly. And it's messy. And if God hadn't made it unbelievably fun...the human race would have died out eons ago. [pause] Men are lucky they can only have one orgasm. You know that women can have an hour-long orgasm?" ”

Foreman walks in after this encounter, and Cameron, seeing him in a mirror, promptly acts as if nothing has happened, saying casually, "Hey Foreman, what's up?". Later, Chase attempts to ask her out but she cuts him off with a firm "no" before he can finish his question.

Cameron was exposed to HIV in season two after a patient coughed blood into her mouth and eyes (cf. "Hunting"). This led Chase to reach out to her by offering to take her out for a drink to relax as she was put under a course of antiretroviral drugs. The patient, on the other hand, was baffled as to why she was still so calm about her predicament after he apologized and later told her that contracting HIV might have been the best thing that happened to him since he now lives his life to the fullest. Later, Cameron called Chase to her apartment and he was taken aback when she suddenly kissed him. Prior to their one-night stand, he discovered that she had stolen some of their patient's meth from the lab and gotten high. Though he initially resisted her advances, Cameron told him "not to turn into a good guy on me now".

Their tryst was swiftly exposed by House the morning after during a diagnostic session after he observed an unravelled Cameron. Later, she and Chase met in the locker room and though they mutually agreed that the sex "didn't suck", he tells her that it shouldn't happen again.

In season three, Cameron proposes a casual relationship to Chase after he wishes her a Happy Valentine's Day (cf. "Insensitive"). This takes him by surprise and she justifies her request by making the argument that it would be "convenient" since they already work together and referenced their one-night stand and how they "didn't get weird about it". Chase sarcastically remarks upon this saying that she views him "like microwave pizza" and asks her what happens if he's offended by her judgment of his character.

Cameron: "Then, you're not the man I'm looking for."

The first allusion to their burgeoning relationship is made in "Half-Wit" when House observes them in the morning, takes note of their wet hair and asks them if they had showered together. Cameron and Chase immediately deny this which leads their boss to deduce that a "double negative" means yes. Later in the episode, they break into House's apartment to find evidence to confirm Cameron's suspicion that something is wrong and Chase suggests having sex in their boss's bed (to which she expresses incredulity) when she tells him the bedroom is down the hall. Chase questions her knowledge about House's home and she covers by responding, "Where else would the bedroom be?"

Their relationship intensifies in "Top Secret" as Chase and Cameron have clandestine sex all over the hospital whilst on the clock, surprisingly at Cameron's lead. This leads Foreman to be suspicious when he discovers them missing at the sleep laboratory where they were supposed to be monitoring a polysomnogram. He later questions the both of them and when he expresses his doubt that they were getting coffee, Cameron tells him outright about their midnight activities though Foreman doesn't believe her and finds the thought utterly hilarious. Chase, perturbed, later tells her that they're not doing it at work again expressing his belief that she wanted to get caught and particularly give House a reason to be jealous. Cameron vehemently denies this, claims that she is over House (repeatedly) and reminds him that all they're doing is "uncomplicated sex". His fear comes true by the end of the episode when House catches them making out and undressing, under the pretense that he's throwing out old files in the janitor's closet.

A photograph of Cameron and Chase taken by their patient, a celebrity photographer.

Confused by House's seemingly apathetic stance on their affair, Cameron discusses with Chase as to why he hasn't attempted to create trouble for them yet (cf. "Fetal Position"). Chase looks upon her concern with bitterness likening her curiosity about their boss's non-reaction to annoyance that House doesn't care that she's no longer available. Cameron's soon proved correct, however, when House blows their secret out of the water informing Cuddy (who attempts to dissuade Cameron from continuing the relationship, albeit hypocritically) and later, Foreman (who has a heart-to-heart with Chase). Emotions continued to run high throughout the episode when even Emma, their patient and a renowned photographer, tells Chase to keep a photograph of Cameron that she took when he expresses interest in it. Though he feigns indifference, she calls him out and tells him that she noticed the way he looks at her. She takes a photo of him while he is looking at it fondly and then quietly thanks her for the gift. Cameron later comes upon this picture as she's packing up Emma's equipment after her discharge and is awed by how he looks in it, saying, in an amazed voice, "She got you to--glow." Chase evades the compliment by quipping: "I always glow".

[edit] Dr. Gregory House

[edit] Season 1

In the pilot episode, Cameron asks House why he hired her, and he reveals that he did so because "you are extremely pretty." House says that beautiful women don't go to medical school unless they are as "damaged as they are beautiful," and surmises that Cameron is damaged.

Cameron's emerging feelings for her boss can be seen as early as in "Damned If You Do," where she showed extreme trust in House's leadership to the team and gave him a Christmas gift towards the end of the episode, despite his hostile reaction to her "just being nice", but later finds a gift for herself left sitting on her office chair. She was also one of the few who acknowledged his birthday (cf. "The Socratic Method). In "Sports Medicine," House invites Cameron to a monster truck rally.

Cameron: "Like a date?"
House: "Exactly. Except for the date part."

However, the episode where Cameron's interest becomes extremely clear is "Control," in which she asked him whether he liked her, and House replied, "No." However, House's behavior sometimes goes contrary to this claim.

In "Mob Rules," Cameron admitted to her feelings for House to Dr. Chase who comforted her by pointing out that their boss didn't like anyone and then went on to marvel that she, ironically, was the only person who did like him.

Things became increasingly tense in the Department in "Heavy," as House revealed that he was being made to fire one of them by the end of the week as office politics came into play, pitting the three colleagues against each other. There was a heated exchange between Cameron and House in the pathology laboratory as he implied that she had made a mistake administering the correct medicine to their patient and she accused him of stalling on having to make a decision. Cameron would later go on to confront him about his nonchalant behavior, in which she quietly threatened to leave her job to "make it easy on everyone." House called her out on her bluff and replied that, "Maybe she should." By the end of the episode, Cameron is seen writing up her CV in the lab.

Despite House's agreement to promote hospital board chairman Edward Vogler's new drug for his pharmaceutical company, in exchange for his Department to remain intact (cf. "Role Model"), things ended in disaster as he embarrassed his employer in a Vicodin-fuelled diatribe against the drug at a public presentation. Earlier in the episode, House had privately asked Cameron why she liked him. The conversation led nowhere as House bitterly accused her of psychoanalyzing him when she attempted to deflect the question. At the end of the episode, Cameron stopped by House's apartment to tender her resignation which he initially questioned as "another noble self-sacrificing gesture." She disagreed and revealed why she liked him: that he did things because it was right and not to help people as she had originally thought. House rejected Cameron's attempt to shake hands as a proper goodbye, prompting her to say that there were only two ways she could deal with their current situation and she was exercising the one option of which she was in control. House was visibly upset when she left his home.

With the departure of Vogler from PPTH, House attempted to bring Cameron back to work in "Kids." Cameron literally shut the door in his face the first time round as his replies as to why he wanted her back didn't impress her. By the end of the episode, House was back at her front door, asking her to come back to work despite her acceptance of a new job. He offered her more perks at work and was thrown off when Cameron replied that all she wanted was "dinner", clarifying that it couldn't just be "a meal between two colleagues." She wanted a real date.

Despite an uncharacteristic show of romance by House (he bought her a corsage), the date did not go well as Cameron rejected his attempt at small talk to instead try and gain an insight as to what her emotionally crippled boss feels about her (by (mistakenly) using Freud). House responded:

"You live under the delusion that you can fix everything that isn't perfect. That's why you married a man who was dying of cancer. You don't love; you need. And now that your husband is dead, you're looking for your new charity case. That's why you're going out with me. I'm twice your age, I'm not great looking, I'm not charming, I'm not even nice. What I am is what you need. I'm damaged." ("Love Hurts")

Cameron would later approach House in the later half of the first season finalé, "The Honeymoon," as he was observing his old flame, Stacy Warner, with her husband and acknowledged his rejection.

"I thought you were too screwed up to love anyone. I was wrong. You just couldn't love me. It's okay. [sic] I'm happy for you."

[edit] Season 2

The return of House's former girlfriend caused the relationship between House and Cameron to be placed in the back burner for the rest of the first season and half of the second season. While it was clear that Cameron still harbored some feelings for House, the future remained uncertain for the two characters despite the departure of Stacy from the show's canvas and a few "tease scenes" by the producers throughout the second season.

In the second season finalé, "No Reason," viewers gained an insight into House's mind when he hallucinates subsequent to being shot. During his hallucination, his subconscious has Cameron keep a vigil by his bedside for two days after his surgery and later, House uses a complex surgical machine to begin to undress her in the midst of treating a patient. When he awakens as he's being transported to the emergency room, House entrusts Cameron to inform Cuddy that he wants ketamine to relieve the pain in his leg and enable him to walk without a cane.

[edit] Season 3

In the third season opener, "Meaning", House asked Cameron out on a date in his first week back at work. Bewildered and flustered, she declined his invitation, citing a list of reasons. Cameron stopped with a sigh to point out that he was smirking at her as she was saying no. Bemusedly, House declared that it was because she was "full of crap" and accused her of only being interested in him when he was still a "sick puppy." Cameron, in response, smirked and replied that he was still not healthy, limp or no limp, and promptly walked away from him. House smiled and watched her go.

In "Finding Judas", Cameron denies that she's in love with House as she's being questioned by Detective Tritter. He makes the observation that although House is "unhinged", "unethical" and "does what he wants with no concern for others", she remains steadfastly loyal to him anyway.

Cameron visited House at his apartment in "Merry Little Christmas" to discuss their patient's condition and found her boss detoxing and cutting himself in order to release endorphins and lessen his leg pain. She tended to his wounds but still flatly refused any entreaties for Vicodin after he tried resisting giving her his medical opinion without it. Cameron also pleaded with House to stop his drug-seeking behaviour expressing confidence that he could go without it and pointing out that he'd stayed clean for months after being shot. The latter's only retort was that it was only because "it had something to do with the absence of pain".

Cameron gave House a hug in "Words and Deeds" after finding out that he had offered Wilson an apology. House seemed to tense, not knowing how to respond, and finally said, "Excuse me, I have to go back to jail."

Cameron kisses House to try to get a blood sample.

When House's team is led to believe that he has terminal brain cancer in the episode "Half-Wit", Cameron kisses House which initially startles him but he soon passionately reciprocates. The kiss ends, however, when House seizes her hand when she reaches for a syringe in her pocket, revealing the kiss to be a ploy to distract him long enough to obtain a blood sample. The scene mirrors a scene from season two (cf. "Need to Know") in which House tells Cameron that he loves her, prompting her to drop her mouth open in shock as House darts forward to swab the inside of her cheek for her final HIV test.

[edit] Trivia

  • Cameron once mentioned to House that she was not an only child (cf. "Paternity"). Chase confirms this in the third season, telling Foreman that Cameron "already has an older brother." (cf. "Fetal Position")
  • There have been theories that she might have lost a child once, as a result of her behavior in "Maternity." In fact, House took it upon himself to violate her privacy and pull her medical history in "Fidelity." Though there was no note that she had ever been pregnant (due to the lack of prescription to pre-natal vitamins), some belief to the contrary remains.
  • Cameron is an atheist (cf. "Damned If You Do," "Role Model"). Despite this, she has been seen seeking solace in the hospital chapel in "Informed Consent," though this could be attributed to the peaceful nature of the institution.
  • She is pro-life (cf. "Sports Medicine"). This was revealed during a conversation she had with House when she asked him if he would give up a baby's life to save a loved one. While House was of the opinion that it depended on the prognosis of the latter, Cameron replied that she couldn't do it. He, in turn, immediately assumed that she had "found religion" to which she asked him if one had to be religious in order to believe a fetus is a life.
  • Cameron is the only regular character that hasn't been in every episode of the show to date. She was missing primarily in "Babies & Bathwater," stemming from her resignation from the hospital.
  • She is the only member of House's department thus far to meet his parents, John and Blythe, when they stopped by to see him. They later invited her to come along for a drink with them but she declined. House would later thank Cameron for doing so, and then went on to compare her to his father whom he has issues with saying they both possessed an "insane moral compass" and that it was a "crappy quality for a dad." (cf. "Daddy's Boy"). Interestingly, Cameron is also the only member of House's department who has not had a parent meet House. Similar to this, as of Season Three to the present day, she is the only member of House's department to not have a parent appear on the show.
  • Cameron is from the Midwest as revealed by House. (Similarly, Jennifer Morrison is also a Chicago native.) (cf. "Que Será Será")
  • Cameron has to invoke a mnemonic to remember wrist bones: "Scared lovers try positions (that) they can't handle". (cf. "Needle in a Haystack")
  • She is seen in many episodes wearing glasses to read or examine an object close up, a trait not typically seen in television characters her age. In some episodes, she can be glimpsed reading without her glasses though this could be attributed to contact lenses.
  • The relationship between Cameron and Chase mirrors the real-life relationship between Jesse Spencer and Jennifer Morrison who became engaged in late 2006.
