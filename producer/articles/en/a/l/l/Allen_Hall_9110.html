Allen Hall

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

This article is about the University of Manchester Hall of Residence. For the Roman Catholic seminary, see Allen Hall (Chelsea).


Allen Hall is the smallest of the student halls of residence that constitute the University of Manchester's Fallowfield Campus, with just 120 students.

The hall features its own private bar which was recently refurbished to the tune of £15,000, as well as a common room with Sky TV.

Allen Hall is well known for its active Junior Common Room (JCR) committee who oversee the organisation of numerous social, sporting and cultural events within the hall.

On March 25th 2006 some 58 residents of the hall gathered to break the Guinness World Record for the 'Longest Line of Dancing Drag Queens.' The title had formerly been held by students of Nottingham University and stood at 49. The event raised approximately £600 for Christie's Hospital in Withington. St Anselms hall JCR are currently organising a challenge to this record to raise money for Manchester Childrens hospital in memory of a halls members child who died recently.

Furthermore, Allen Hall had the distinction of raising more money for charitable causes than any other hall of residence in Manchester last year. In 2005-6 some £4,200 was donated to causes as diverse as the Pakistan Earthquake Appeal, Christie Hospital, the Catholic Children's Rescue Society and the BBC's Children In Need.

The present Warden of Allen Hall is Norman Gillson, and Philip Wright-Christie is the current JCR President for 2006 - 2007. Steven Booth is Vice-President for this year also.

Allen Hall is comprised of three accommodation blocks: Newman, Teilhard, and More - centred around the main building where the office, common room, bar and laundrette are located. The hall is located approximately half a mile from the main Fallowfield campus.
