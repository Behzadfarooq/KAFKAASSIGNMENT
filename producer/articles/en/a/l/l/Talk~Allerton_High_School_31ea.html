[edit] cleanup

I nominated this article for deletion. I have since gone through the primary sources, removed the original research, and want to note that this article still fails to show any notability relying upon only a single government report which is carried out on all schools on a mandatory basis once every three to five years. This is simply a common school and I find it rather silly that of all of the people that voted to keep this article, none of them bothered to clean it up except to add the report from the inspector. To me, that demonstrates how much the article is worth to those that voted to keep it. The importance of this school is still apparently only notable to persons living in and around the local community, the employees of the school, and etc. I suggest that this article be renamed to "Allerton High School (Leeds)" and that those who voted to keep this article create a disambiguity page, and provide a similar article for the other High School in Iowa. To me, that would demonstrate sufficiency for the argument that all high schools should have articles. Thanks. Ste4k 16:49, 8 July 2006 (UTC)
