[edit] Song information

All or Nothing was used as the third international single release from American singer/actress Cher's thirty-first album Believe.

[edit] Music video

Cher in the music video for "All or Nothing".

The music video for All or Nothing was shot while Cher was on tour for her album, Believe. Although most of the video features random clips of some of Cher's Do You Believe? tour performances new footage was filmed in which Cher sang the song, wore a red wig and a silver outfit rather than the tour costume which Cher herself described as "Bozo the Clown-meets-Braveheart look."

In 1999, the video was released as a VHS and CD-R promos in North America, but later that year it was included in the Taiwanese release of the Believe album.

A remix video was made for the video and it's very similar to the original video. The main difference is that the remix video is in black and white colors. Although Dan Rux most famously known as Dan-O-Rama made all the remix videos for the Believe album's singles including a tour only video mix, it is unknown whether he created the All or Nothing remix video. The remix used in the remix video is the All or Nothing (Almighty Definitive Mix) which is actually the name of the video as well.
