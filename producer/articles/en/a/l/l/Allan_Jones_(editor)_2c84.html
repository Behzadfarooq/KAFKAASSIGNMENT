Allan Jones is a prominent British music journalist. Originally from Wales, he moved to London in the early seventies where he attended art school. Following his graduation he got a job working in the stock room of Hatchard's on Piccadilly. While there he applied for a job writing for the famous rock weekly Melody Maker by writing a letter to the then editor. He famously concluded the letter saying "Melody Maker needs a gun up its arse and I've got my finger on the trigger". He joined the staff in 1974, and became editor ten years later. He remained in this position until 1997, when he became editor of music and film monthly Uncut, a position he retains today.
