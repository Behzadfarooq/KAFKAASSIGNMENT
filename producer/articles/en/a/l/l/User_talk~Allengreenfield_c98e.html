The original article bearing my name "Allen Greenfield" was discussed for deletion, and - with due discussion, was voted to KEEP. Shortly thereafter, a personal antagonist with an obsessive axe to grind with me. He has "stalked" me on the internet for years, writing personally vindictive reader reviews on Amazon.com, private letters and posts, all of them self-represented as legitimate reviews or discussion, but based entirely on personal animosity. I contend that (A) this is not a legitimate reason for calling for the removal of any article from a legitimate encyclopedia, nor is any 'review' motivated by personal dislike of an author as an individual (unless perhaps labeled as such) in any sense legitimate. Amazon.com agreed with me on this, and removed his "reviews" of various of my books. I think Wikipedia needs to look into this more closely....the second vote seemed irregular in that (A) deletion was proposed by this individual "stalker" for personal reasons, (B) only five votes were recorded, 3 for removal (including said individual) and two for a keep, but I have been told by several other individuals that they voted "Keep" but said votes were not recorded. I would like someone with Administrator status to look into this matter, including a review of the two archived debates on whether to keep the article or not. It is easily verifiable false information that my books are published by Vanity publishing houses.
