The All Nations Cup is a football (soccer) tournament and cultural festival held annually in the region of Seattle, Washington, USA. Immigrant communities of the Seattle area, from countries all over the world, are represented both on and off the football pitch. The tournament and festival are organised by Seattle area nonprofit Sister Communities.

The inaugural event took place in 2003 over one weekend, and involved twelve teams. In 2006 the event will involve 37 different communities and will occur over the course of two weeks.

Structure The All Nations Cup is played by various players who represent a national team. Unlike the FIFA World Cup, the players are selected from smaller leagues with smaller participating teams. The first round is played by 32 different teams in a group stage format. The top 16 teams are then chosen to go on to the second round of the tournament. At this stage, it becomes a knockout phase. Teams that win in this round move on to the quarterfinals. This continues into the semi-finals. The two teams that lose in the semi-final round play against each other for a third place round. The third place round is a single game in which the winner is then titled as the "third place team". The two winners of the semi-final round will compete for the championship and the winner is awarded with the All Nations Cup Trophy. The most succesfull team in the tournament is Brazil who have already one two championships in the tournament's four hear history. Likewise, The Brazil National Soccer team has been the most succesful team to play in the FIFA World Cup and have won Five World Championships.

All Nations Cup 2006 will begin on July 14, 2006 and conclude on July 29.
