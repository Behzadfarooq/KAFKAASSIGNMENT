Allegiance were a heavy metal band from Perth, Western Australia.

After taking out the Western Australia state finals of a national band contest, Allegiance consolidated their line-up and set about recording their debut album D.e.s.t.i.t.u.t.i.o.n.

The album highlighted Allegiance's brash thrash style which incorporated strong commercial elements and the band toured nationally with Australian Hard Rock act The Poor, who were also at the time enjoying a high point. Coupled with this, in 1995 Allegiance became the first Australian heavy metal band to tour the country with the Big Day Out.

By now Allegiance was one of the most high profile metal bands in Australia and was soon found supporting every major overseas tour acts including Slayer and Machine Head.

Bassist Harrison was controversially sacked soon afterwards and the band fell apart in 1997.

However, Allegiance played a reunion show in late 2000 and late in 2002 it was reported that the band had reformed. They headlined a major Perth festival on New Year's Eve and were apparently in preparation for a new album, but since then nothing more has been heard from them.
