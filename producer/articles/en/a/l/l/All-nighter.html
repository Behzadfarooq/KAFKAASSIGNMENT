An all-nighter is a slang term for a night spent not sleeping. To "pull an all-nighter" means to stay up all night. The longest anyone has intentionally gone without sleep without using stimulants of any kind is 11 days; that record is held by Randy Gardner.

[edit] What constitutes an all-nighter

There are many opinions and debate as to what constitutes as an all-nighter. Below are different opinions being voiced around the internet.

[edit] Sleep after sunrise

When one (after a normal waking time and activity level) only allows themselves to fall asleep after the sun comes up, when night has passed.

[edit] 24 hours

When one goes a full 24 hours without sleeping, starting the all nighter at any point in time. By its nature, one needs to stay awake for an entire night and a day.

[edit] Sleep skip

When one wakes up at the usual time (e.g 7:00am), then skips that night's sleep, sleeping at the next normal bed time (e.g. 11:00pm) the day after the all-nighter began. This style usually results in a person being awake for around 40 hours.

[edit] In academic culture

All-nighters are common among university students, who stereotypically (in western cultures) put off studying until the night before an exam or deadline. Some universities and educators have expressed concern with this trend, and tried to find ways to decrease sleep loss among their students, either by reorganising work to enable better study habits, or simply exhorting students to improve their time management. However, increasing workloads and more after school activities also may contribute to "all nighters."

[edit] In popular culture

  • An all-nighter provided the storyline for an episode of The Facts of Life (TV series).

[edit] Risks

Sleep deprivation can cause many unwanted side effects, both short and long term. Regular instances of sleep deprivation may cause short-term memory loss.[citation needed]
