Brothers Allen Thompson (October 1, 1847 – February 27, 1906) and James G. Thompson (1849 – 1921) were Union Army soldiers during the American Civil War and recipients of the highest decoration of the United States military, the Medal of Honor. They are one of only a few pairs of brothers to have both received the medal.

Both brothers enlisted as Privates in the 4th New York Heavy Artillery, Allen joined from Port Jarvis, New York into Company I and James joined from Sandy Creek, New York into Company K. On April 1, 1865, the brothers were involved in an engagement at White Oak Road, Virginia. Thirty-one years later, on April 22, 1896, they were each issued the Medal of Honor for their actions during the battle.

The Thompson's official Medal of Honor citations are nearly identical; James' citation reads:

Made a hazardous reconnaissance through timber and slashings, preceding the Union line of battle, signaling the troops and leading them through the obstructions.

Allen Thompson died at age 58 and was buried in Lakeview Cemetery, Cheyenne, Wyoming. James Thompson died fifteen years later at age 71 or 72 and was buried in Mount Hope Cemetery, San Diego, California.
