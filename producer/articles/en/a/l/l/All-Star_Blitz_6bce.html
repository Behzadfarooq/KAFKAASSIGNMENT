All-Star Blitz

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

All Star Blitz was an American game show that aired on ABC from April 8, 1985 to December 20, 1985. The USA Network showed Blitz in reruns in 1986.

Peter Marshall hosted and John Harlan announced this Merrill Heatter production.

[edit] Object

Two contestants (one being the returning champion) attempted to solve phrase puzzles consisting of between two and six words with the help of four celebrity panelists. The board consisted of six video monitors arranged two high and three wide.

The location of the board (above the celebrities) led to the show's opening spiel, "All in the game that's just over their heads."

A contestant would pick one of the four celebrities and specify a row; e.g., "Abby Dalton at the top". Host Peter Marshall would then read a multiple choice question to the celebrity who would provide a response. Much as on Marshall's more famous The Hollywood Squares, the contestant would agree or disagree with the celebrity's response. A contestant retained control until he/she agreed with a wrong answer or disagreed with a correct answer.

When a group of four stars formed a box around a monitor, a correct agree/disagree was needed to reveal the letters or word therein. The contestant then had a chance to guess the puzzle or continue playing. An incorrect guess passed control to the opponent.

Correctly guessing the phrase won the game. A contestant had to win two games in order to win the championship.

[edit] Bonus Round

In the Blitz Bonanza round, the contestant spun a giant wheel four times to reveal as many of the six puzzle pieces as possible. (Landing on an already revealed piece was a "wasted spin".)

If there are fewer than four pieces of the puzzle revealed after the fourth spin, the contestant could earn a fifth spin by giving back the prize package won in the main game.

After all spins were taken, the contestant and celebrities were given ten seconds to come up with a solution. A correct guess by the contesant won at least $10,000 (the jackpot increased $5,000 for each non win to a maximum of $25,000). A correct guess by a celebrity won an additional $250.

Champions retired after winning five games.

[edit] Trivia

  • While All Star Blitz used the Hollywood Squares question-and-answer format, joke answers were not always used on All Star Blitz.
  • Contestants did not know how many words were in the puzzle, though in early weeks they did know for the bonus round.
  • The final puzzle used on the show was "All-Star Blitz".
  • Game show announcer Randy West was a champion on All Star Blitz.
  • All-Star Blitz is known for its bizarre vocal theme song, in which the title of the show is sung repeatedly amid random syllables ("hobba hum, hobba heeba humba").
