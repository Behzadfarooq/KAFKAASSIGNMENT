Allen Algernon Bathurst, Lord Apsley, DSO, MC, TD (3 August 1895–17 December 1942) was a British politician.

Lord Apsley was the eldest son of the 7th Earl Bathurst and was educated at Eton and Christ Church, Oxford. During World War I, he served overseas and was elected as Member of Parliament (MP) for Southampton in 1922 and for Bristol Central in 1931, seats he held until 1929 and 1942 respectively. During this time, he was Parliamentary Private Secretary to: the Under-Secretary of State for the Overseas Trade Department 1922-24, the Minister of Transport 1925-29 and the Minister for Co-ordination Defence 1936. He had also been President of the UK Pilot's Association in 1925 and sometime chairman of Western Airways, Western Air Transport Company and a director of Morning Post. During World War II, he served overseas again died on active service, serving under the Arab Legion in Malta, in 1942.

On 27 February 1924, Lord Apsley had married Violet Meeking (who succeeded him as MP for Bristol Central) and they had two sons: Henry Allen John (born 1927) and George Bertram (born 1929). As Lord Apsley predeceased his father, the latter's earldom later passed to Lord Apsley's eldest son, Henry.
