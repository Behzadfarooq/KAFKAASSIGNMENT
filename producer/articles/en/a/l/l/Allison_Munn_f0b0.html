Allison Munn (born October 7, 1974 in Columbia, South Carolina) is an actress who is best known for her role in The WB sitcom What I Like About You.

Munn's parents are Russell and Nadine Munn. Her mother is an elementary school teacher who encouraged her daughter in the arts by letting her take dance and singing lessons at an early age. Allison graduated from AC Flora High School and The College of Charleston. From there, she moved to New York, where she won a role in the Broadway epic, The Fantastiks. Further auditions led to small roles in Hollywood productions; most notably in White Oleander alongside Michelle Pfeiffer and in Elizabethtown, directed by Cameron Crowe. Her biggest break came in 2002 when she got a co-starring role, opposite Amanda Bynes and Jennie Garth, on the sitcom What I Like About You. She also has a level of notoriety for a recurring role as Caroline, Fez's borderline stalker girlfriend on That '70s Show. Allison is currently single and lives in Los Angeles with her pet terrier, Buster Keaton. She currently lends her name and support to the non-profit political organization, Rock the Vote, where she is actively involved in encouraging young people to register to vote.
