"All Happy Families..." is the 56th episode of the HBO original series, The Sopranos. It was the 4th episode for the show's fifth season. The episode was written by Toni Kalem and was directed by Rodrigo Garcia. It originally aired on March 28, 2004.

[edit] Episode recap

Anthony Jr. is having a difficult time in school and is being extremely resentful to his mother because of the separation. When Tony and Carmela go to a parent conference with A.J.'s guidance counselor, Mr. Wegler he advises him to improve his SAT scores and to do better with his classwork. A.J. is sent back to class when Wegler begins to talk to Tony & Carmela privately about his academic record. Carmela puts part of the blame on the separation, but says that A.J. should be devoting more time to academics. After they leave the office, the secretary informs Mr. Wegler that two girls were killed in a car accident coming to school that morning. A.J., still in the office talking to a friend, is shocked.

Tony then decides to cheer A.J. up by buying him a new Nissan Xterra as a gift. Carmela scoffs at this, believing A.J. does not deserve such a gift and it will hinder his studies further. A.J. snidely tells Carmela that it is not her business and he was not talking to her. Tony then tells A.J. that the car stays in the garage until his grades are up. The following day, A.J. asks his mother if he can attend a concert in New York City with a few friends. Carmela refuses, because after the concert they will all probably be very drunk and check into a hotel room. A.J. becomes very angered by this and decides not to talk to Carmela. Carmela gives in on the condition that after the concert he has to go to Meadow's apartment and be back at home by 10am the following morning. A.J. agrees and continues to look at his car manual.

On the night of the concert, A.J. calls Meadow to tell her that he will not be coming to her apartment afterward. Meadow reluctantly gives in, since she is in the middle of making out with Finn. A.J. and his friends then arrive at the hotel where they get high and drunk shortly after. The next morning, A.J. and his friend Matt are literally glued to the floor and A.J.'s eyebrows have been shaved off. They both wake up when A.J.'s cell phone begins ringing from a worried Carmela. A.J. and Matt manage to get themselves cleaned up and leave the room. Carmela becomes extremely worried and frantically calls Tony to go look for him. A.J. then returns home, where he runs upstairs and curses at his mother. Tony then arrives at the house where A.J. pretends to create a lie. Tony believes A.J.'s alibi which Carmela then suggests that A.J. go live with Tony at Livia's house. After receiving a phone call from Mr. Wegler, Carmela decides to go out to dinner to discuss her son. The discussion quickly turns to her social life and marriage.

Meanwhile in New York, Lorraine Calluzzo and her boyfriend Jason Evanina are murdered by Billy Leotardo and Joey Peeps after they refused to give their collections to Johnny Sack. Little Carmine becomes extremely upset by the news, and is advised by fellow captain Rusty Millio to take action. Together with Rusty and Angelo, Little Carmine tries to devise a plan to overtake Johnny and Phil Leotardo which is likely to result in complete turmoil.

Tony hears the news of Lorraine Calluzo's murder and decides to advise the captains and soldier not to get involved in the New York feud. Feech La Manna then recalls when Tony and Jackie Aprile decided to get ahead in the world by robbing his card game. Tony laughs at this, but then Feech asks if he can run the card game once again. Tony considers the offer since it is now run by Uncle Junior's crew. At the card game, Feech La Manna continues to recall events from the past which causes Tony to become extremely frustrated. Using Carmela's suggestion, Tony makes an obviously unfunny joke to the card players to see if they laugh. The only person not laughing is Feech, which means that the other associates are scared and have to laugh because of his title. When Feech carjacks a wedding from one of Tony's close doctor friends, Tony reluctantly decides that Feech must go since he is causing nothing but problems, and he is worried that Feech will try to usurp his authority a la Richie Aprile. Christopher and Benny Fazio visit Feech at his home and ask him where they can hide a set of flat screen televisions. Feech recommends that they place them in his garage, but charges them a TV for the service. The following day, Feech is ambushed when his parole officer arrives at his house and suspects the television of being stolen. The officer then asks Feech to show him his garage, and Feech has no choice but to comply. For violating his parole by receiving stolen mechandise, Feech is immediately returned to prison on a bus.

[edit] Deceased

  • Lorraine Calluzzo: shot by Billy Leotardo on orders by Johnny Sack
  • Jason Evanina: shot by Joe Peeps for being an associate with Lorraine

[edit] Title reference

  • The episode's title refers to a quote by Leo Tolstoy on the topic of family. It refers to the breakup of the Soprano household.

[edit] Trivia

  • The episode's script was written by Toni Kalem, who also plays Angie Bonpensiero on the series.
  • The character of Dr. Ira Fried was recast in this episode with actor John Pleshette. The role was previously played by Lewis J. Stadlen.
  • This is the first time we see Frankie Valli portraying Lupertazzi capo Rusty Milio. Valli was mentioned before in the season 4 episode "Christopher".
