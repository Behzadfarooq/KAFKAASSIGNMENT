St. Joseph's is one of the oldest schools in New South Wales, and in Australia. The Sisters of St. Joseph, founded by Fr Julian Tension Woods, established their first school at Lochinvar in 1883.

In 1992 the school amalgamated with two other Catholic secondary schools in the area, St. Peter's and St. Mary's in Maitland, under the name All Saints College. St. Joseph's became a coeducational secondary day school catering for years 7 to 10.

The first lay principle was Ms H Hemphill (1990-1994) follwed by Mr M Healy in 1995, who left the school at the end of 2004. Mr P.Hamill was the following principal until 2005. The current principal is Mr P.Greeves.

The Campus motto is "Vigor in Ardius Crescat" which in Latin for "May your strength increase in difficulties".
