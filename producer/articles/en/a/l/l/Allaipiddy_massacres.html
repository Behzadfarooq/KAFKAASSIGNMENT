Allaipiddy massacres is a series of massacres of thirteen minority Tamil civilians in a spate of incidents in three villages including Allaipiddy in Velanai Tivu or Kayts Island. Kayts is a small islet off the northwestern coast of the Jaffna Peninsula.

[edit] Controlling authority

The area where the massacres took place is strictly controlled by the Sri Lanka Navy, which has a major base there.

[edit] Casualties

On 13 May, 2006, at about 8.30 p.m., unidentified gunmen entered the home of Sellathurai Amalathas in Allaipiddy and opened fire. Eight people were killed on the spot, including a four-month-old baby and four-year-old boy, and one other person died later in hospital.

In another incident, at around 10:30 p.m. the same night, unidentified gunmen entered the home of 72-year-old Murugesu Shanmugalingam in Puliyankoodal village, also on Kayts Island, and shot him and two other members of his family dead. Ten shops in Puliyankoodal were burnt down.

In Vangalady another village on Kayts Island, gunmen entered the home of Ratnam Senthuran, a tea shop owner, and shot him dead. Other members of his family also were shot and injured, but managed to escape.

[edit] Amnesty International report

According Amnesty International[1] it has received credible reports that Sri Lanka Navy personnel and armed cadres affiliated with the Eelam People’s Democratic Party, a Tamil political party and a paramilitary organisation that is opposed to the rebel LTTE, were present at the scene of the killings.*[2]

[edit] Shelling of the local church

As part of the on going military operations, the Sri Lankan Army shelled the St. Philip Neri's Church church on August 13, 2006 in Allaipiddy killing 15 refugees on the spot. [3]No report of government investigations of this incident has been published.

[edit] Abduction of local priest

Fr. Thiruchelvam Nihal Jim Brown of the local church was abducted and presumed dead by the Sri Lankan military.
