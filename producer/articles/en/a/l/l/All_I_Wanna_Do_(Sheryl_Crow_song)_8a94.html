"All I Wanna Do" is a song performed by Sheryl Crow and written by David Baerwald, Bill Bottrell, Wyn Cooper, Sheryl Crow, and Kevin Gilbert. It was Crow's breakout hit from her 1993 debut album Tuesday Night Music Club. The song was the winner of the 1995 Grammy Record of the Year and Best Female Pop Vocal Performance.

The lyrics for the song are based on the poem "Fun" by Wyn Cooper[1] [2]. Crow's producer (Bottrell) discovered Cooper's poetry book "The Country of Here Below" in a Pasadena, California used bookstore. Crow adapted "Fun" into the lyrics for her song -- earning Cooper considerable royalties, and helping to push his book, originally published in a run of only 500 copies in 1987, into multiple reprints. The song dealt with sexual harassment in Santa Monica nightclubs.

[edit] Amy Studt version

English singer/songwriter Amy Studt released a cover version of the song as her fourth single. Released on January 12, 2004, the single reached a peak of #21 in the charts, Studt's lowest position to date. It is taken from the re-release of her debut album, False Smiles. Following the chart of "All I Wanna Do" Studt was dropped from her record label Polydor for poor sales [3].
