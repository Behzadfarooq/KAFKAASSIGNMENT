All the Way... A Decade of Song is Céline Dion's first English greatest hits album (7th English album and 26th in total if counting the French ones). It was released on November 13, 1999.

[edit] Album information

All the Way... a Decade of Song is a compilation of Dion's most successful hits coupled with seven new songs, including the leadoff single "That's the Way It Is," a cover of Roberta Flack's "The First Time Ever I Saw Your Face," and "All the Way," a duet with Frank Sinatra. One of the new songs, "Then You Look at Me" was used in the Bicentennial Man movie. Céline Dion also worked with Robert John "Mutt" Lange, best known for his multi-platinum recordings with Shania Twain, on "If Walls Could Talk."

A second consecutive one-hour Dion's CBS Special was presented on TV in late November 1999 to mark the release of her greatest hits album. It included guest appearances by Gloria Estefan and 'N Sync. On January 1, 2000 Dion started her two year break from showbizness.

An accompanying All the Way... A Decade of Song and Video DVD was also released in 2001, including all new songs videos. All the Way... A Decade of Song was re-released as a CD+DVD version on October 20, 2003.

Dion's duet with Sinatra "All the Way" was nominated for a Grammy Award for Best Pop Collaboration with Vocals. Céline Dion won a World Music Award for World's Best-Selling Female Pop Artist and an American Music Award for Favorite Artist in Adult Contemporary category.

According to Billboard, "That's the Way It Is" received the "BDS Certified Spin Award - 500,000 Spins" in August 2006. Since the song was released in November 1999, it has cycled 500,000 times on almost 1400 radio stations across Canada and the U.S.

[edit] Additional facts

  • "That's the Way It Is" was written and produced by a team who worked with Britney Spears and the Backstreet Boys at that time. Céline Dion performed it live with 'N Sync, during her CBS Special (1999). She also sang it at the end of her Let's Talk About Love Tour. The album version of this song was included on The Collector's Series Volume One compilation as well. Several club remixes were created by the Metro.
  • Shania Twain did the background vocals on "If Walls Could Talk." Although the video was made, the single release set for December 2000 (in Australia, Mexico, Europe and Japan) was cancelled.
  • "The First Time Ever I Saw Your Face" video was taken form the CBS Special (1999). Dion sang it earlier, during her Let's Talk About Love Tour and the CBS Special in 1998. It was also included on the 2004 Miracle album. Dion performed this song during her A New Day... show in Las Vegas.
  • "All the Way" is Céline Dion and René Angélil favourite song. It was co-produced by the latter. The video for this song was also taken form the CBS Special (1999). Dion performed this song during her A New Day... show in Las Vegas.
  • "Then You Look at Me" was written by the "My Heart Will Go On" team. It was a theme from the Bicentennial Man movie. The film, released in December 1999, became a flop. Dion made a video for the song, but the single wasn't released. However it was included as the B-side to the "Live (For the One I Love)," "The First Time Ever I Saw Your Face" and "I Want You to Need Me" singles.
  • "I Want You to Need Me" had two videos. First, directed by Paul Hunter was never released. Second, by Liz Friedlander came out in 2000. Club mixes of the song were made by the Thunderpuss.
  • "Live (For the One I Love)" was an English version of the song "Vivre" from the Notre Dame de Paris musical.
  • "Would I Know" (written by Diane Warren) was first supposed to be on the All the Way... A Decade of Song. But it remained unreleased until it leaked to the internet in May 2005. Charlotte Church recorded her version in 2003.

[edit] Chart success

All the Way... A Decade of Song was another big commercial success. It topped the charts in most countries and entered top ten elsewhere. The album sold 21 million copies worldwide including 8.5 million copies in U.S. and 5 million in Europe. It sold over 1 million copies in Japan, Germany and United Kingdom (969,034 as of 2000). All the Way... a Decade of Song was promoted by successful single "That's the Way It Is" which reached #1 for eight weeks on the United World Chart. Followed-up singles were: "Live (For the One I Love)" (#23 in Canada), "The First Time Ever I Saw Your Face" (#19 in the UK), and "I Want You to Need Me" (#1 in Canada).
