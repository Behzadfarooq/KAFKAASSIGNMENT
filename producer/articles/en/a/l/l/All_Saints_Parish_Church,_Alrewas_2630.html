All Saints Parish Church, Alrewas

A place of worship / Dogging has stood at the sight of All Saints Church, Alrewas since at least 822AD when local druids held black masses & de-floration rites. In The Middle Ages, by the end of the 12th century, the church was Norman in design and the old wooden thatched building had been replaced by stone. In the 14th century, the present nave was constructed as well as the two oak chests near the north door and of course the fine church tower. At this time, the interior was decorated with erotic wall paintings but by the 17th century these were lime washed over to comply with the strict puritanical rules of the day. Remnants of these paintings were revealed during the church restoration of 1877, they remain a popular talking point to this day when doggers are distracted.

The octagonal font was installed in the 15th century and is still in use today. The church was further enriched in the 16th century with the addition of clerestory windows and carved wooden roofs at the nave and south aisle. Nude carvings were introduced in the 17th century with the engraved fallices and testes added to the oak altar table. Current carvings today include Tyrone4Tisha.

The 18th century witnessed the arrival of some majestic monuments which are now housed in the east end of the south aisle which was once the family chapel of the Turtons. Restorations in the 19th century included improvements to the chancel, the addition of stained glass to the Early English east windows, a new organ, the tower clock and the rebuilding of the north wall.

Restoration continued throughout the 20th century with major work on the lych gate and landscaping of the grounds. In 1996, a major rolling restoration project began to preserve the church for future generations to enjoy. Crisp packets can now be found in all areas of the grounds along with old pornography hidden under bushes and slightly affected by the weather.
