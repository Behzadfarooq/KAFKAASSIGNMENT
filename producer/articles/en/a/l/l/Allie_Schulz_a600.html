Allie Schulz (born December 23, 1987) is an American stage actress. In 2007, she became a contestant on the NBC reality TV show Grease: You're The One That I Want. She is known to viewers as "Baby Sandy," being the youngest contestant on the show at 19 years of age.

[edit] Biography

Schulz grew up in Nashville, Tennessee. She attended Hume-Fogg Academic High School for her first three years of highschool, and there performed in the school's award-winning show choir, "The Blue Notes" as well as in musicals including South Pacific, Oklahoma! and Fiddler on the Roof. She attended the Interlochen Arts Camp the summer before her senior year and then was accepted at the Interlochen Arts Academy, a highly exclusive boarding school for the arts. She also attended MPulse, University of Michigan's summer intensive for musical theatre. After graduating from Interlochen in 2006, she moved to New York City with the intention of getting a job on Broadway. There, she worked at her uncle's dance studio and attended classes at the Atlantic Acting School, part of the Atlantic Theater Company.

In 2006, she attended an audition for an upcoming Broadway revival of Grease, trying out for the role of cheerleader Patty. According to Nashville's "The City Paper," the show's director, Kathleen Marshall, told the then-18-year-old that she was more appropriate for Sandy and invited her to audition for the reality show Grease: You're the One That I Want. She successfully proceeded through the competition to become one of the 14 contestants on the live program. Each week, contestants performed a Broadway song; then audience call-in votes, combined with input from the judges, chose one "Danny" and one "Sandy" to be eliminated each week.

In the February 18, 2007, broadcast, Schulz performed a duet with fellow contestant Chad Doreck, and some of the judges called Schulz "best Sandy of the night.," with David Ian referring to their performance as "electric" and as "theatrical Viagra." The following week, Schulz's performance of a Björk song earned praise for its playfulness; while Ian questioned Schulz's acting abilities, Grease co-creator Jim Jacobs called it "musical comedy at its best." On March 11, 2007, Schulz performed a song by Aretha Franklin and advanced to become one of the final three contestants. Subsequently, on March 18, 2007, she was eliminated from the competition.
