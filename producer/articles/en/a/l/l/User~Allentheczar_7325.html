Born Allen Louis Linton II, he is a rising star of the Morgan Park High School I.B program, an athelete of many sports, and soon to be writer of the book chronicling his senior with Jon Fair. Linton II was born in Chicago, IL and was raised by parents Allen and Dionne Linton. He currently stays with his mother and only brother Gregory Linton (10yrs old).


[edit] Growing Up

From the early age of seven, Allen has always been fond of education; he occupied his time by watching the Discovery Channel and developing an intrest in marine life. Going to to Edgar Allan Poe Classical School, Allen recieved high level education and began an uphill climb to the top of academic success. Although school was most important, Allen engages himself in baseball since the early age of six. Establishing himself in the best of both worlds, Allen developed a wide variety of cultural enhancement that gave him the ability to interact and function at all levels of society and with anyone he encountered. Also at the age of seven, Allen enjoyed the company of the forth member of the family Gregory.


[edit] Elementary School Troubles

Although success came to Allen, he struggled to get through Poe Classical due to a violent and wild temper. Grades four-five were particularly difficult and he required off site counsiling to help him control his anger and prevent it from getting in the way of his academic success. Happily, Allen did succeed and participated on trips that would turn out to be unforgettable. In fifth grade he went to New Orleans and in sixth grade visited the Bahamas (both choir trips). Allen graduated from Poe in 2001 and was accepted into Morgan Park's Junior High School program. Allen won all school science fairs held at his school (fourth - sixth grade), but it is noted that he never earned all A's while at Poe.

[edit] Morgan Park High School

Through the recent years, Allen attended Morgan Park High School's 7th/8th grade program. By tracking himself accordingly, Allen was able to take advantage of his interests to make his way into the International Baccalaureate programIB
