Allan Duncan Brown (born October 12, 1926 in Kennoway) is a Scottish former footballer and manager.

[edit] Playing career

Brown started his professional playing career at East Fife, joining them in 1944 from his local side Kennoway. He made his full Scotland debut whilst with East Fife in April 1950, a 3-1 win over Switzerland at Hampden Park. This was followed in the same season by two further games away to Portugal and France, as well as helping his club team win the League Cup.

He moved to Blackpool in December 1950 for a fee of Â£26,500 (then the largest fee received by a Scottish club),[1] joining compatriots Jackie Mudie, Ewan Fenton, Hugh Kelly and George Farm.

While at Blackpool, Brown won a further eleven international caps, scoring against Wales at both the start of the 1952-53 and 1953-54 seasons. His final two games coming in the 1954 World Cup finals against Austria and Uruguay. At Blackpool he enjoyed great success playing alongside Stanley Matthews. He missed the Seasiders' 1951 FA Cup Final after injuring his knee ten days earlier. He also missed the famous 1953 FA Cup Final after breaking his leg whilst scoring an 88th-minute winner in the quarter-final against Arsenal.

Brown moved to Luton Town in February 1957, for a fee of Â£8,000, scoring on his league debut at Leeds. He scored five goals in the Hatters' successful FA Cup run of 1959 and at last played in a Wembley final, which Luton lost 2-1 to Nottingham Forest (see 1959 FA Cup Final).

After 51 goals in 151 league appearances for Luton, Brown moved to Portsmouth in March 1961.

[edit] Managerial career

In August 1963, Brown joined then non-league Wigan Athletic as player-manager, replacing Johnny Ball. He remained at Wigan until July 1966. In November 1966, he returned to Luton, this time as manager of a side struggling in the Fourth Division. He soon changed the fortunes of the Hatters, leading them to the Fourth Division title in 1968. They started the next season promisingly (and were eventually to finish in third place), however, Brown was sacked in December 1968 after it was discovered he had applied for the vacant managerial post at Leicester City.

He wasn't unemployed for long, joining Torquay United as manager in January 1969. He kept Torquay comfortably in Division Three during his first two-and-a-half seasons in charge (finishes of 6th, 13th and 10th), but after a poor start to the 1971-72 season, he was sacked in October 1971. Torquay would eventually be relegated later that season.

On the June 20, 1972 he became manager of Bury, leading them to 12th place in Division Four at the end of his first season in charge. The following season, Bury started brightly, and would eventually be promoted in fourth place. However, Brown had left on November 19, 1973 to manage Nottingham Forest, and took them to 7th place by the end of the season. The following season, Forest started badly and Brown upset the fans by selling Duncan McKenzie to Brian Clough's Leeds United. He was sacked on January 3, 1975, shortly after a 2-0 home defeat to local rivals Notts County.

On May 5, 1976, Brown was appointed manager of another of his former playing clubs, Blackpool, who at the time were in the Second Division. The following season saw them battle for promotion, and eventually finish in 5th place. During the next season the club were again challenging for promotion, but after an argument with chairman Billy Cartmell, on February 6, 1978, Brown was sacked. After his departure, Blackpool won only one more game, dropped down the table, and were narrowly relegated at the end of the season.

Later that year he moved to Kuwait to manage Quadsia, but returned to manage Blackpool in March 1981 as a replacement for Alan Ball; however, he was unable to prevent their relegation to Division Four for the first time in their history. The following season saw a mid-table finish, and Brown left Blackpool in May 1982.
