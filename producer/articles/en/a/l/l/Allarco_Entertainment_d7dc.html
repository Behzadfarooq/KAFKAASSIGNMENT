Allarco Entertainment

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Allarco Entertainment Inc. was one of four applicants for an English language Canadian premium television service that was considered at a CRTC public hearing on October 24, 2005. [1] The Allarco application was approved on May 18, 2006, while the other three applicants - BOOMTV, The Canadian Film Channel and Spotlight Television - were rejected. [2]

Allarco is owned by the Allard family, who were also behind the service now known as Movie Central and now owned by Corus Entertainment. It is expected to compete directly with the existing English general-interest service, Movie Central and The Movie Network, with many of the same types of movies and first-run television series presently found on those services. However, unlike those services, they have pledged to reinvest 100 per cent of its profits in Canadian programming, in addition to 32 per cent of revenues annually, and will be spending $1 million a year on marketing these programs. They also plan to broadcast exclusively in high-definition television, and they will include a channel called HD Canada that will be dedicated to promoting and airing Canadian programs and films exclusively.
