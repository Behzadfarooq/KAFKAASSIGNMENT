All-interval tetrachord

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

An all-interval tetrachord is a tetrachord from which any interval class can be extracted by means of various inversions. There are only two possible all-interval tetrachords. In set theory notation, these are [0,1,4,6] and [0,1,3,7].

[edit] Use in modern music

The unique qualities of the all-interval tetrachord have made it very popular in 20th-century music. Composers such as Elliot Carter and George Perle used it extensively, as did Webern and Schoenberg. (Although the term all-interval tetrachord and indeed set theory itself was not in use during most of their life time.)
