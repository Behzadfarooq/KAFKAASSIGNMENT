"All I Really Want to Do" is a song written by Bob Dylan.

[edit] Song information

"All I Really Want to Do" was first released on Dylan's his 1964 album Another Side of Bob Dylan. The studio version was also included on Bob Dylan's Greatest Hits Vol. II in 1971, and live versions were released on Bob Dylan at Budokan in 1979 and on The Bootleg Series Vol. 6: Bob Dylan Live 1964, Concert at Philharmonic Hall in 2004.

Musically simple though playful, "All I Really Want to Do" is essentially a list of all the things Dylan doesn't want to do or be to the listener (perhaps a woman, but just as likely his audience as a whole). This song has also been interpreted as a parody of the way men were beginning to respond to the growing feminist movement, by assuming that platonic friendship is the only truly equal relationship.

[edit] History

One hit rendition of the song was by Cher. "All I Really Want to Do" was Cher's debut single, it went on to chart in several countries in 1965. She continued to perform the song in concerts, through her 2000s Living Proof: The Farewell Tour.

The version by The Byrds, also released in 1965, garnered popularity and became a hit as well. Their version set one of Dylan's verses to an original, minor-key bridge.
