Allen is a village in County Kildare in the Republic of Ireland located on regional road R415. The village, with a population in 2002 of XXX, is overlooked by The Hill of Allen, which in recent times has been scarred by quarrying. The hill is visible over much of Kildare and surrounding counties, and is the ancient seat of Fionn MacCool


[edit] History

In AD 722 The Battle of Allen took place between the Leinstermen, commanded by their King, Murchadh, son of Bran and the northern and southern Hy Neill, commanded by Ferghal, King of Erin, along with his son Aedh Allen, and Aedh Laighean, King of Hy Maine in Connaught.
