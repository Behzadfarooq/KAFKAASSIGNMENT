"All Hell's Breakin' Loose" is a song by the American hard rock band Kiss, that appeared on their 1983 album Lick It Up. It was the second and final single released from the album and did not chart in the U.S.[1][2]

Although the song is credited to all four members of the band, then consisting of Eric Carr, Paul Stanley, Gene Simmons, and Vinnie Vincent, Carr was the primary writer of the song, coming up with the music and arrangement.

Carr, who originally wanted the track to reflect his Led Zeppelin influence, was upset that Stanley used a talk-type for the verse.[3] Carr later stated that he felt that Stanley's contributions to the song helped it to be included on the album and in becoming a single.[4]

A video for the single was directed by Martin Kahan and produced by Lenney Grodin[5] that featured the band wondering around a burnt-out cityscape amongst thugs, bikers, scantily-clad women, circus performers, and other odd characters. The video received some air play on MTV, and was nominated for a MTV video music award in 1984.

The song also appears on The Box Set, released by Kiss 2001.
