Allen Kukovich, Pennsylvania politician and former state legislator.

[edit] Early life

Allen Kukovich was born in September, 1947 in Manor, Pennsylvania, a small town in Westmoreland County.

He attended Penn Township High School before heading to Kent State University in Ohio, where he graduated in 1969. In 1973, he received his J.D. from Duquesne University in Pittsburgh and worked for several years in private practice in Greensburg, Pennsylvania.

[edit] Political career

After the unexpected death of state Representative Jim Laudadio in 1977, Kukovich won a special election to complete the remainder of the deceased member's term of office. He would win elections to the state House from 1978 until his election to the state Senate in 1996.

While in the House, Kukovich served as Majority Policy Chairman and also chaired several other key committees.

A reformer known for his efforts to reduce the size of the Pennsylvania General Assembly and promote open and accountable government, he is credited with creating the Children's Health Insurance Program (CHIP) in Pennsylvania, which later served as a model for other state's health insurance programs, and which provides hundreds of thousands of children in Pennsylvania with free or low-cost health insurance each year.

In 1996, he decided to give up his House seat to take on incumbent state Senator Eugene Porterfield who represented the 39th Senatorial District. On April 23, 1996, Kukovich beat Porterfield by a 10% margin, clearing the way for an easy victory in November of that year. In 2000, Porterfield switched parties and attempted to unseat Kukovich as a Republican. Kukovich was re-elected with nearly 60% of the vote.

In the Senate, Kukovich served as Democratic Chair of the Aging and Youth Committee, where he played a pivitol role in the passing of the expanded PACE legislation.

In 2001, Kukovich announced his candidacy for the Democratic nomination for Lieutenant Governor. In a crowded field, Kukovich came in third place, but garnered much attention statewide.

Following the primary, Ed Rendell, the Democratic nominee for Governor, asked Kukovich to serve as Chairman of the Pennsylvania Democratic Party. Kukovich held that post through the successful gubernatorial campaign of Rendell/Knoll.

In November, 2004, Kukovich was defeated in his quest for a third term in the Senate.

Shortly after he left office, Kukovich was tapped to serve as Director of Governor Rendell's Southwestern Pennsylvania office, a post he currently holds.

[edit] Associations

Kukovich help found the Pennsylvania chapter of Common Cause in the early 1970s and is currently an Executive Board member of the national organization, Americans for Democratic Action (ADA).
