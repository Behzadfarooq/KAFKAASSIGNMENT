"All Alone" is the 61st episode of the HBO drama Six Feet Under. It is the 10th episode of Season 5 (the show's final season) and is important in helping bring the series to a close. It was written by Kate Robin and directed by Adam Davidson.

[edit] Episode Recap

Spoiler warning: Plot and/or ending details follow.

'All Alone' is the only Six Feet Under episode which does not open with a "death of the week" (the final episode begins with a birth). Nate's death at the end of the previous episode is sufficient, and the episode begins simply with the Fisher family's grief.

In the hours following Nate's death from a brain hemorrhage, David copes with his pain by planning his brother's funeral. He explains to Brenda and Ruth that Nate wanted a green funeral with no embalming or casket, just burial in a plot of land that's protected by a nature preserve. Ruth objects strongly but David bluntly disregards this, determined to stick to his brother's wishes.

With Ruth too bereft to take care of herself, Bettina comes to the house to help. When Ruth learns that there will be no preservation or viewing of Nate's body, she becomes even more distraught. She takes the last opportunity to see her son's body by helping David in the prep room as he washes the blood off Nate's scalp. She angers David with her persistent questioning over what must have gone wrong at the hospital, and the two end up fighting.

Brenda struggles with explaining to Maya that her father is dead. Margaret comes to help out, and it becomes clear that Brenda is not coping well but is putting on a strong face. Later, Maggie comes by Brenda's house with a quiche to apologise. Brenda finds this unbelievable and very insulting and she closes the door on her.

Keith prepares Anthony and Durrell for Nate's memorial service but is interrupted by a phone call from Roger. Roger demands that Keith take an angry young actor named Trevor to the airport. Worried that he is going to be late for the funeral, and then annoyed by Trevor, Keith eventually calls Roger and quits. He rushes back to the funeral home to be with David.

Desperate to escape her misery, Claire asks Ted to take her for a drive and they hit the road with no particular destination in mind. Although she's only known him a short time, Claire feels comfortable crying in front of Ted and wonders aloud why she's only able to recall painful memories of her brother. When she's ready, Ted takes her back home and she arrives just in time for Nate's memorial service to begin.

At the memorial service, David is haunted by images of Jake, the hitchhiker who attacked him a year ago. While eulogizing his brother, he suffers a panic attack and cannot continue. Federico speaks lovingly of his friend and business partner, and then George surprises everyone with his wisdom and comforting words. Afterwards, Ruth invites George to join the family at Nate's burial in a wooded nature preserve.

At the burial, David suffers another panic attack and seeks shelter in his car. Without his help, Federico, George, and Keith struggle to carry Nate's body out of the hearse. After Ruth helps David overcome his fears, he joins the family by Nate's open grave and Aunt Sarah reads aloud a poem from the Mystic Odes of Rumi. Claire remembers walking in on Nate, as a child, when he offered her cannabis. The memory makes Claire smile. All the members of the family then help in burying Nate, an act which is obviously very difficult for everyone.

After an emotional 'conversation' with Nate, Brenda succumbs to her grief and overwhelming anger with her dead husband. She drops Maya off at the funeral home, asking Ruth to look after her until she is ready. She returns home to find her brother, Billy, waiting for her. Brenda collapses into his arms and cries hysterically.

[edit] Death of the week

Nathaniel Samuel Fisher, Jr. (January 8, 1965 - May 21, 2005). Died of a brain hemorrhage. This is not included at the start, unusually, probably to make it more significant.

[edit] Title reference

The title links into the scene where Claire remembers Nate on the day Kurt Cobain died. Nate is listening to the song 'All Apologies' by Nirvana, although the line itself is actually: "All in all is all we are". The song is then used for the ending credits as well.

[edit] Chronology

The episode runs over the 22nd and 23rd of May, 2005 (Nate's obituary states that the memorial service is on May 23rd). The episode therefore continues from the day after the previous episode, where Nate dies.
