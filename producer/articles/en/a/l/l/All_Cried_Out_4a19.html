"All Cried out" is a pop song written by English singer-songwriter Alison Moyet, and producers Jolley & Swain for Moyet's debut studio album Alf (1984). In 2002, it was re-recorded in two versions by German pop band No Angels. While a re-worked pop version was included on the re-released edition of the group's second album Now ... Us! (2002), a re-arranged "big band version" was released on the band's first swing album When the Angels Swing (2002). Produced by Perky Park and Till Brönner respectively, both versions were released on one CD single on December 2, 2002 (see 2002 in music). However, "All Cried out" became the No Angels' least successful single with peak positions of number 18 in Germany, number 23 Austria, and number 59 in Switzerland.
