All Saints Episcopal School is an independent school in Fort Worth, Texas. The school is a member of the Southwest Preparatory Conference.

All Saints covers kindergarten through 12th grade and is a part of the Episcopal Diocese of Fort Worth.

[edit] The School's Mission

All Saints' Episcopal School of Fort Worth promotes academic excellence in a nurturing, Christian environment and upholds the Anglican tradition of worship.

[edit] Curriculum

"The All Saints’ curriculum serves the particular needs and developmental stages of each division but fits together as a comprehensive whole to prepare our students for college and ultimately life.

The Lower School curriculum is structured to build the foundation for a love of learning by focusing on the students and their diverse needs, motivating them to achieve excellence in all that they do. Beginning in fifth grade and continuing through the Middle School is an integrated concentration on the Western tradition, especially in history, literature and science. A central feature of this program, which connects the curricula of the Lower and Middle Schools, is the Biography Projects. It is designed to enhance the students’ study of history in grades five through eight.

Building on the strengths of the other two Divisions, the Upper School provides an integrated, college preparatory curriculum embracing the most noble aspirations of mind and spirit. It is a challenging program that includes a wide variety of honors and Advanced Placement courses. This is complemented by a strong and creative extracurricular program. Permeating the entire curriculum is a faithful commitment to the spiritual consciousness upon which the School was established."
