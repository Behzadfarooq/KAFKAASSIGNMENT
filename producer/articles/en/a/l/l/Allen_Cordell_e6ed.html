Allen Cordell

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Allen Cordell (born February 16, 1978 in Seoul, Korea) is a filmmaker and artist living in Manhattan. He attended high school in upstate New York and wanted to become a comic book illustrator before deciding to pursue film and fine art. Cordell attended Purchase College in Purchase, New York, to study cinema in the school's competitive filmmaking conservatory.

Developing a love for documentary, experimental and narrative cinema, Cordell's student films often blurred the distinctions between these genres and reflected his obsession with what he calls "the perpetual sense of mystery that permeates every experience".[citation needed]

He won an award for best documentary his sophomore year for his portrait of John D. Phillips, an outsider artist suffering from schizophrenia.

Cordell directed the music video for Dan Deacon's 2006 Big Big Big Big Big and is currently producing his first feature film, Some Kind of Awful, which he wrote and directed.
