All Hail the Power of Jesus' Name

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

All Hail the Power of Jesus' Name is a popular hymn sung by many Christian denominations.

The hymn is often called the "National Anthem of Christendom."[1]. The lyrics, written by Edward Perronet while he served as a missionary in India, first appeared in the November, 1779 issue of the Gospel Magazine, which was edited by the renowned author of Rock of Ages, Augustus Toplady. The text has been translated into almost every (if not every) language in which Christianity is known. There are two well known tunes that are most popular with the hymn: "Coronation" and "Miles Lane"; but there are also anumber of others including "Diadem", which is most often sung as a choir number.
