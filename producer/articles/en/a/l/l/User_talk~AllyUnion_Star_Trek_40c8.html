[edit] Canon vs. fanon section suggestion

  • The tug-of-war between canon and fanon has probably never been stronger than that witnessed with Star Trek fans. This section could borrow elements of the WikiProject page and explain what is canon and what isn't, and touch on how some aspects of fanon have become so entrenched within the fanbase that entire series have been rejected (namely Enterprise) on the basis that it did not confirm with fan-made expectations and assumptions. Have to be careful to maintain NPOV of course, but this is a rather unique aspect of Trek, I find. You don't see Star Wars fans campaigning (seriously at any rate) to decanonize Phantom Menace, even though it wasn't all that well liked.
  • Similarly, such a section might also touch on Star Trek as religion. There are fans who take some of the principles created by Roddenberry such as IDIC and treat it as personal philosophy but also, almost as a religion. DS9, ENT, and several of the movies have come under fire from factions of fans who claim the shows violate Roddenberry's word. The Kill Enterprise website went so far as to refer to ENT as "a blasphemy" which I personally found very offensive. 23skidoo 04:10, 27 May 2005 (UTC)
