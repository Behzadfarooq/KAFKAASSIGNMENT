The All Hallows' Middle School is a part of All Hallows' School. It is located at 547 Ann Street, Brisbane, Queensland, Australia and is run by the Sisters of Mercy.

[edit] Middle School History 1861 - 2006

All Hallows' School had a Middle School / Primary School from 1861 until 1979. In fact in 1861, Mother Vincent Whitty, the founder of All Hallows' School noted that the ages of students at All Hallows' ranged from "five years to late teens" (Mahoney, 1985, p5). Wartime concerns however led to the temporary closure of the Junior School in December 1944.

In a prophesy of more recent times, community pressure mounted to re-open what is now the AHS Middle School. In 1952 this community pressure "could no longer be disregarded" and the so-called "junior" school re-opened (Mahoney, 1985, p.248).

Initially this Junior School was located in the basement of Adderton building before moving to the more pleasant surrounds of Coolock - an area overlooking the Kangaroo Point Reach of the Brisbane River. By April 1952, the precursor of today's Middle School, had moved to Nazareth, a school house and grounds on the site of what is now the Loretto and Mercy Hall complex. In 1979, Nazareth became the All Hallows' art centre until it was removed to make way for the new Celestine Complex.

In 1979, despite community sadness, the Middle School section closed as All Hallows' began to refocus the school upon secondary education. After significant community pressure over numerous years and at the completion of an exhaustive process of consultation with other schools and educational authorities, the Middle School re-opened in 2006 with an intake of 150 year 5 and 6 students. These students were to find their home in a refurbished state-of-the art McAuley Hall.

McAuley Hall was initially built in 1957-8 as the McAuley Training College, a religious based teacher training insitute. In time, the McAuley College would expand and relocate to Enoggera as McAuley College. In the 1990s, it would move again to Banyo as the central component of Queensland's home of Catholic education in the tertiary sector, Australian Catholic University (McAuley Campus).

Current Middle School girls would be interested to know that the lawns on which they lunch were the original location of an aviary and guinea pig hutch which were much beloved by Junior School girls as McAuley Hall was built in the late 1950s.

In 2007, the All Hallows' Middle School further expanded as it reintroduced year 7 students. Middle School students, nick-named "Blue Sox", have been embraced by the All Hallows' School community and have added a greater vitality and excitment to education at the School.

[edit] The Middle School Since 2006

Despite opposition from some systemic schools within the Brisbane Catholic Education, the All Hallows' Middle School was re-established with the support of Archbishop John Bathersby in order to provide greater choice to Catholic families within Brisbane who wished for their daughters to be educated in an all girl environment. Such an opportunity for single sex schooling had been offered to male students within the Brisbane Archdiocese for many years. Catholic girls in Brisbane had not had such an opportunity however.

The re-establishment of the Middle School by All Hallows' in 2006 was, therefore, greeted with great enthusiasm by many members of the Brisbane Catholic community and enrolment at this premier educational institution for girls became highly sought after. All Hallows' School found that it rapidly had numbers of applicants for enrolment well beyond the number of places available.

Embracing current educational theory and pedagogy, students of the All Hallows' Middle School experience a wide variety of age appropriate, yet challenging, learning experiences within a high professional educational context. All students within the School are highly familiar with information technology and they make use of this technology in their studies each day.

Located within the inner City of Brisbane, All Hallows' Middle School emphasises the ongoing safety and security of its students.

Many students attending the AHS Middle School have older sisters within the Secondary section of All Hallows' School.

[edit] Curriculum

Middle School students at All Hallows' take part in a full and rounded curriculum including studies in:

  • Religious Education
  • English Language and Literacy
  • Mathematics
  • Science
  • Studies of Society and Environment
  • Italian
  • Connected Curriculum
  • Information Technology
  • Art
  • Music
  • Drama
  • Health and Physical Education

Great emphasis is placed upon developing in students skills in critical thinking, research and study which will serve them well in their secondary studies.

[edit] Co-Curricula Activities

Girls in the All Hallows' Middle School particpate in many co-curricula activities including:

  • Field Hockey
  • Football (soccer)
  • Futsal
  • Netball
  • Touch football
  • Waterpolo
  • Athletics
  • Cross Country Running
  • Swimming
  • Dancing (Ballet, Jazz, Irish)
  • Speech and Drama
  • Choirs and music ensembles.
  • Instrumental Programmes, Orchestras and Bands

Middle School students access a variety of local areas as part of their curriculum and often take part in excursions to Chinatown (Fortitude Valley), Queensland Parliament House, Brisbane City Hall, Taoist and Buddhist temples, and the Riverside Boardwalk.

As part of their studies, students in years 5, 6 and 7 take part in adventure camps and retreat days. In year 7, students may take part in a school based study trip to Canberra.

[edit] Facilities

Students of the Middle School at All Hallows' have access to a wide variey of state of the art learning facilities including:

  • McAuley Hall (a fully air-conditioned, custom built middle years of education learning facility which houses computer centred flexible learning areas, the Middle School age dedicated library, the McAuley Room thearette. All McAuley Hall classrooms feature interactive whiteboard technology)
  • St Gertrude's Courtyard (including long-jump, hurdling and sprint training facilities)
  • the Celestine Art Centre (incorporating custom built art facilities, photographic laboratories and computer work spaces)
  • the Consilo Aquatic Centre and Piazza (incorporating Olympic standard waterpolo pools and secure and safe on-campus multi-car pick up and drop off zone)
  • the Claver Theatre (a 300 seat wide screen theatre complex)
  • Mercy and Loretto Halls (wooden sprung floor indoor court spaces for netball, futsal, volleyball and other indoor activities)

All Middle School students make use of the Moodle Blue online learning environment and have complete access to the monitored and comprehensive school information technology network. Middle School students all carry individual electronic identification cards.

Middle School students make use of facilties across the entire All Hallows' School campus including the historical Adderton Convent and St Ann's Chapels.

The All Hallows' Middle School has numerous outdoor play spaces and daily access to full tuckshop facilities. The All Hallows' School tuckshop only stocks nutritional and healthy foods. Vending machines are available after hours for additional food and drink purchases.

The All Hallows' Middle School will see continued expansion and development under the All Hallows' School building programme.
