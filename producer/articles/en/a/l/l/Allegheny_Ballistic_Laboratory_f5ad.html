Allegany Ballistics Laboratory (ABL) located in Rocket Center, West Virginia, is a diverse state-of-the-art industrial complex employing some 1,000 people across 1,628 acres. About 80 military products are made here, including: 30mm shells for Apache helicopters, composite parts for F-22 jets, training grenades, fuze-proximity sensors, mortars and warheads, tank ammunition, and many other weapons components . ABL is the sole producer of more than a dozen critical weapons systems. The ABL facilities is a leading manufacturer of advanced composite structures and Operates 6 of only 11 known state-of-art advanced fiber placement machines in the world. Allegany Ballistics Laboratory (ABL) is part of the Federal Laboratory Consortium and is operated by ATK (Alliant Techsystems). Also on the site is the Robert C. Byrd Hilltop Office Complex and the Robert C. Byrd Institute for Advanced Flexible Manufacturing. At the Robert C. Byrd Complex on the hill, other companies have rented space to do secure research. Information Manufacturing Corporation, for example, is digitizing data on hurricane cleanup, avian flu, and weather records.

[edit] History

ABL was established in 1944 on the site of a former ammunitions plant on land owned by the Army. After world war II, the plant was transferred to the Office of Scientific Research and Development, where it helped build propulsion devices and engines for the solid-rocket industry. In the late 40's, ownership of ABL was transferred to the Navy and operation of its facilities was contracted out to ATK (Alliant Techsystems), a $3.4 billion corporation based in Edina, Minnesota that has operated the facility ever since.
