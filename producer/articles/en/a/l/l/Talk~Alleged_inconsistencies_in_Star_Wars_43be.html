[edit] Darth as a Title for Sith

In the original Star Wars, Obi-Wan refers to Vader several times in such a way that it sounds like "Darth" is merely his first name. The expanded universe doesn't give Sith the name "Darth" until after Episode I establishes that "Darth" is a Sith title, and that Sith typically (but not always, see Darth Maul) have a "Sith name." It might be useful to add something about this issue to the page. I don't think this is original research because I've seen fans discussion this on various forums and perhaps a website or two. It's more of an issue with the expanded universe and the prequels conflicting each other, but you could make a case for ANH as well ("You can't win Darth.") —The preceding unsigned comment was added by 68.53.95.31 (talk • contribs).

I don't see an issue here. There are plenty of times where people are addressed by their titles alone, both in real life and in fiction (real world: "Mr. President" for the US President; fiction: watch just about any episode of Star Trek and notice how many times people are addressed by just "captain" or "commander"). There are numerous times in Star Wars itself where people are addressed just by their title. "Darth" is no difference. EVula 18:15, 1 April 2006 (UTC)
  • Fine, but Obi-Wan is the only person to call Vader "Darth" as a proper name. Everybody else, even his enemies call him "Vader" "Lord Vader" Or "Darth Vader." Nobody calls Maul, Dooku or Sideous "Darth." That's why it stands out. When the original trilogy was created we had no reason to assume that "Darth" was anything but his name (even if by ROTJ it was his "new name" after he abandoned the name "Anakin Skywalker." This business about granting every Sith a new name with "Darth" as a title is something cooked up in the prequels. See "Lord Maul" and "Lord Tyrannus" and "Lord Sideous," etc. The expanded universe assumed that "Darth" was a title unique to Vader until the prequels came around (the TPM novelisation established "Darth Bane" as the first one to use the title 1,000 years prior to TPM and the one who started the "rule of two" something else the EU had never heard of before), hence you have "Sith" like Freedon Nad, Exar Kun, etc. Then all of a sudden you get supposedly ancient Sith like Darth Revan, Darth Malak, etc. The term "Sith" is never mentioned in the original trilogy (though the novelisations mention Vader as the Dark Lord of the Sith, as if he's the only one. The early scripts postulate a "Dark Knights of the Sith" order rival to the Jedi that are numerous (not just one or two guys). The Star Wars novel has no idea that Palpatine has force powers, that was something introduced in ESB. Anyway, sorry for the trivia, but I thought I'd throw that in there since it wouldn't belong in the article.

[edit] Answers

A lot of those can be fairly easily answered.

"- Obi-Wan serving Bail Organa in the Clone Wars (ANH)

Obi-Wan didn't have enough time to mention about any of his relations to people in the Clone Wars. All he said was "you must go with me to Alderann". If they had gotten there, it would be doubtful that Obi-wan wouldn't mention it upon meeting Bail.

- Clone WarS, plural (ANH)

Easily explained. People tend to mistakenly, or perhaps by slip of the tongue, mention large wars in plurals when there was only one. Like if there was a single, giant conflict between Sudan and another country, for instance, the scope of the conflict makes it seem as if there were a thousand wars going on at once (consider the fact that there were tons of almost mini-wars going about in all the campaigns of the galaxy during the clone war). Hence some people tend to refer to it as the clone warS.

- Age of the Republic - "for Over a Thousand Generations" (ANH) vs. "Which has stood for a thousand years" (AOTC)

The length and past of the Republic is cloaked in mystery. There isn't a lot of information about what it was like 10000 years ago, where the republic (approximately) was formed, so people tend to underestimate or mis-pinpoint the date of the formation of the Jedi and Republic.

- Obi-Wan Kenobi's master (ESB)

Yoda, and by extention the rest of the coucil members, all help in the training of initiates. The jedi go to them for advice, and while each Padawan is paired with a specific master, many Jedi consider the council and perhaps all the Jedi Masters in the Order their "masters" as well.

- Anakin wanting Luke to have his lightsaber when he was old enough but Owen wouldn't allow it (ANH)

Anakin could have told Padme if that anything happened to him, give his lightsaber to one of his children, and Padme told Obi-Wan before she died (off-camera). More likely, Obi-wan fabricated it on the spot to help shield Luke from the fact that Vader was his father.

- Design of the Tantive IV and Death Star (ANH)

The Geonosians designed the CONCEPT of the death star, and later the Empire picked it up and worked on it. The Correllian Corvette, of which class the Tantive IV belongs to, was around for a long time before ANH - the Tantive IV was later refitted to it's updated design in ANH.

- Leia remembers the mother who died thirty seconds after she was born - and Luke does not. (RotJ)

Answered in the article. Leia is force-sensitive, just like Luke, and she could have formed a mental bond with her mother in the womb. She could have tapped into her senses and memories and found out what her mother looked like. Luke may have simply not developed this connection with Padme

- Owen Lars doesn't recognise C-3P0 at all. (ANH)or doesn't remember.

C-3P0 is a standard protocol droid design. There are thousands of droids that look exactly identical to him. And it's not like he bothered to learn the droid's designation, anyway.

- Obi-Wan claims not to have heard his real name since before Luke was born. (ANH)

"Ben" could have been a fabrication of his, to aid his hiding. Or it might have been his nickname at childhood (perhaps a sibling kept calling him it) before he was taken away to join the Jedi Order.

- Anakin being 'a great pilot' when Obi-Wan first met him (and no, there is absolutely no way you're going to convince me that 'pod-racer' is the same thing as 'pilot'). (RotJ)

I know, I know what you're gonna say, but pod-racing has a ton in similarity with piloting. And while it's not the same thing, a lot of the controls, dynamics, and reflexes you need are quite the same. However, this isn't it - Obi-Wan is saying this out of fondness. He wasn't really aware of his amazing piloting skills when he "first met him" - he became aware just about a couple of weeks later, when Anakin flew his fighter at the Battle of Naboo.

- Obi-Wan's claim that "When I first knew him... I was amazed how strongly the Force was with him." (RotJ) Actually, he hadn't even met Anakin when he did the 'Midichlorian test'. (TPM)

Again, fondness and exaggeration.

- Vader tells Luke that "Obi-Wan once thought as you do" (i.e. that he could be redeemed), but there's absolutely no evidence of that in RotS. (RotJ)

Look at the landing pad scene on Mustafar. Anakin knew from one look at Padme and Obi-Wan, behind his rage at being "betrayed", that they were really trying to convert him back to the light path, and hoping in their own minds to do so. He could sense it.

- Obi-Wan's claim that Owen "didn't hold with your father's ideals. Thought he should have stayed here and not gotten involved". (ANH) But Owen barely knew Anakin and never said anything of the sort. (AotC)

Obi-Wan perhaps assumed this, or made it up - again, he was trying to present the Anakin Skywalker image to Luke, not the evil Darth Vader one, to shield him from the truth.

- The entire concept of Midichlorians, which was never mentioned - or even hinted at - in the entire OT. (Yes, introducing important plot elements only to completely forget about them later on DOES count as an inconsistency.)

Obi-Wan was barely with Luke before he was killed by Vader. He didn't have a chance to tell him about the complexities or cellular structure of the force. Yoda held a beleif that it didn't matter how many midi-chlorians someone had, that they could become just as strong as he was if they gave enough effort. Besides, telling him about it might have stunted his beleif in it and made his training slower. I mean, it kind of spoils the facination you have with it when you know exactly how it works, doesn't it?

- Ditto the prophecy of a Chosen One who'll bring Balance to the Force.

Again, not enough time to tell him about it, from neither Yoda (after Luke learned Vader was his father) or Obi-Wan (who was getting weaker and could return less and less as the series went on).

- Ditto the idea of the Force being 'out of balance' in the first place. In fact, Yoda states quite clearly in ESB that the dark side is not stronger.

Yoda is talking about the fixed, overall strength and potential of the light side in a single being compared to the dark side, not the total balance of light and dark power in the universe.

- If the Empire has camo-colored armor for their troopers (since the Republic becomes the Empire), why didn't they use them on Endor?

There's no point. Life-sign scanners make camo all but worthless, and there's no need to waste even a miniscule amount of money repainting armor to match the environment as technology can see right through it. The Rebels wear camo because they need every single advantage they can get, and often choose places where life-sign scanners are disrupted.

- If the Republic stormtroopers can "see through" dust clouds and shoot accurately, as they appear to demonstrate in AOTC, why do they lose this ability under the Empire? (see R2D2's "smoke screen" on Bespin in ESB)?

The stormtroopers at Bespin aren't crack troopers - they're ordinary human recruits, not the bred-for-war clones of the Republic. Besides, they could have shot through it if they had been given more than a couple of seconds to aim (all the characters reteated and were out of sight in about a second and a half. Can YOU squint through a smoke cloud or change your helmet optics to thermal vision, THEN aim that fast, especially if you're fairly new?

- Speed of movement, flipping ability, etc. of even "elder Jedi" (and Sith) in the Republic era, vs. relatively sluggish, slowed down movement and grounded nature of fighting in the post-Republic era.

Have you ever found yourself literally freezing solid when you go outside on a winter's day? No, of course not. You lose heat by transferring it to cold air. In space, there is no way to rapidly transfer the heat...so the body must radiate it way. If it takes a long time in frigid air, it would take even longer in frigid space. There is no air in space. Despite what movies like Outland would have you believe, you wouldn't explode if exposed to space. The air would rush out of your lungs explosively, which could damage your lungs and throat. But your skin and vascular system would contain your blood fairly well, protecting it against the vacuum, so your blood would not boil. After a long time, the cells would freeze, and the expanding ice crystals (water expands when it freezes) would rupture the cells, making you look a pretty good mess. But that would take hours, or longer, not seconds.   • "Are you sure the homing beacon is secure aboard their ship? I'm taking an awful risk here Vader, this had better work," or
  • "This bickering is pointless. Vader, release him."

What's the deal with that? It's not like in V, where apparently he can kill his own officers with impunity. In IV, he explicitly obeys, even though it is clear that he has the power to not. RadicalPi 06:23, 6 August 2006 (UTC)


Visor4 replies: The reason behind that is Grand Moff Tarkin and all the other officers shown at the "round table" on the Death Star were either Vader's superior (in Tarkin's case) or Vader's equal in rank. When the Death Star was destroyed, Vader was the only superior officer left, and thus all the officers he tortured or killed without whim in episodes V & VI were of a much lower rank. Since Tarkin was no longer around to keep him in check, Vader let his emotions dictacte his actions.
