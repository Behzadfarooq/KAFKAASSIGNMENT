Allan Quatermain, Junior is a fictional character created by Alan Moore and Kevin O'Neill in their League of Extraordinary Gentlemen graphic novel series, hereafter referred to as "LoEG." Though Allan Quatermain, Jr., said to be the son of Allan Quatermain, Sr., did not play a significant role in either volumes one or two of LoEG, he was mentioned numerous times in the side stories, called the "New Traveller's Almanac," at the back of the issues that constitute volume 2.

Allan Quatermain, Junior is believed to play a significant role in Volume 3 of LoEG, The Black Dossier set to be released October 25, 2006.

[edit] In "The New Traveller's Almanac"

The first and second volumes of The League of Extraordinary Gentlemen are each divided into six individual issues. Each issue has a comic-book-like format in the beginning which tells the main part of the LoEG story, and a second part which chiefly composed of text with some illustrations telling a side story which takes place in the same world as LoEG. In Volume 1, the side story was "Allan and the Sundered Veil," and in Volume 2 the side story was "The New Traveller's Almanac," which was a narrative in the form of a classified travel guide to the world of LoEG. The metafiction of the New Traveller's Almanac is that the travel guide is owned by Campion Bond, the spymaster of the League, and that the guide was published in the year 1930. The guide tells the story of the survivors of the 1898 League whose story composes of the majority of the first two volumesâ€™ main stories. One seemingly new member joining these survivors in their continuing adventures around the world, according to the guide book, is Allan Quatermain, Junior.

This, though, has been taken by many fans to mean simply Allan Quatermain, like in the original works of the Allan Quatermain adventure tales written by H. Rider Haggard, has faked his own death again and has taken on the name and identity of his fictional son. This is supported by the fact that his lover, Wilhelmina Murray, upon the death of the Great White Hunter almost immediately initiated an identical relationship with Allan Quatermain, Junior, and continued to travel with him.

Many would say it was made painfully clear by Moore that both Allan Quatermains are the same person, and that both he and Miss Wilhelmina Harker are now either immortal or at least rejuvenated to younger, healthier states, though the truth in such claims are still to be seen.

Allan Quatermain, Junior, is referred to thusly throughout the "New Traveller's Almanac":

  • Chapter 1: "The British Isles: From the Streaming Kingdom to The Blazing World" (Issue #1, Vol. 2):
    • Not references
  • Chapter 2: "Europe: From Aiolio to Zenda" (Issue #2, Vol. 2):
    • Referred to as "A.J." and later as simply "A." by Wilhelmina Murray and as Miss Murray's "youthful male friend during 1912" by the guide.
  • Chapter 3: "The Americas: In the Rubble of Utopia" (Issue #3, Vol. 2):
    • Not references
  • Chapter 4: "Africa and The Middle East: Lights of the Dark Continent" (Issue #4, Vol. 2):
    • Allan Quatermain, Sr., said to have died (1901). First direct reference to "Allan Quatermain, Junior"
  • Chapter 5: "Asia and the Australias: Visions of Cathay" (Issue #5, Vol. 2):
    • Referred to as "the younger Quatermain" by the guide and as "Allan" by Miss Murray
  • Chapter 6: "The Polar Regions: To The Ends Of The Earth" (Issue #6, Vol. 2):
    • Referred to as "the younger Quatermain" by the guide and as "Allan" by Miss Murray

[edit] In The League of Extraordinary Gentlemen: The Black Dossier

This volume was set to be released May 30, 2006, but has since been postponed to October 25, 2006. However, a promotional illustration (see top) has been released depicting a man thought to be Allan Quatermain, Jr., and Wilhelmina Murray.
