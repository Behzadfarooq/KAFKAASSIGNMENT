"it seems to portray Allison Quets as a victim of the Florida adoption system,"

Having a lot to do with her status as a victim of the Florida adoption system.

Also, why has her revocation of relinquishment been modified to include the word alleged? Is there any source that contests the fact that she revoked her relinquishment almost immediately? AMProSoft 05:08, 3 January 2007 (UTC)

No, but there has been an almost intentional overlooking of some finer points of Florida adoption law. Revoction only applies in cases of duress. Whether or not she was under duress when she signed is one of the points still being litigated. If it's decided she wasn't, then the revocation is void. ChiTwnG 11:23, 3 January 2007 (UTC)

Read Wikipedia:Neutral point of view for our policy on neutrality in articles. There are also legal considerations here, especially as it would seem that no final legal decision has been taken on the issue. --Mel Etitis (Μελ Ετητης) 10:09, 3 January 2007 (UTC)

I do not see her as being under duress. She approached the first couple, fought with them about having them pay for the delivery, went to a different couple and wanted open visitation and when they wouldn't enter that agreement, she returned to the first couple. All that took some time, time enough to reconcider allowing any adoption. She also concidered just adopting out the boy and keeping the girl. It is obvious that the entire time she had the children she must have been pondering adoption and which couple and how much money would exchange hands. This was not, in my opinion, a coercion situation or a spur of the moment decision that she regretted later. Adoption/Foster Advocate

[edit] Revision

I have attempted to revise this, trying to take a more neutral position. I tried to add the perspective of law enforcement (violating existing law) as well as the adoptive parents. I understand Quets supporters feel she was wronged by the Florida courts, and I tried to leave that in. I removed the lines about open adoption, because I don't feel it's valid to her arguement. Quets argues that she revoked her consent, not that open adoption puts her at risk. I also cited news sources in North Carolina for my info, although the titles aren't particularly neutral.Mikimoe 15:49, 1 February 2007 (UTC)
