All-Ireland Senior Hurling Championship 1942

The All-Ireland Hurling Championship 1942 ran from May to September of that year. The All-Ireland Hurling Final was won by Cork who beat Dublin by 2-14 to 3-4.

[edit] All-Ireland Senior Hurling Championship

[edit] Paths to the final

In the Leinster Championship Dublin continued their dominance for a second year in succession. A consequence of this victory was automatic qualification for the All-Ireland final. Cork, in spite of being All-Ireland champions, regained their Munster Championship crown for the first time since 1939, overcoming their great rivals Tipperary in the process. This victory set-up an All-Ireland semi-final meeting with Galway. Cork easily overcame the Tribesmen and reached the All-Ireland final for a second year in-a-row, once again facing Dublin.


[edit] The final

Sunday, September 6 was the date of the 1942 All-Ireland final between Cork and Dublin. It was their second consecutive appearance in the final, with Cork emerging victorious in the previous year's encounter. Once again severe war-time travel restrictions meant that only 27,000 people travelled to Croke Park for the game. Unlike the 1941 decider the Dubs proved to be no pushover. Two great saves from their Kilkenny-born goalkeeper Jim Donegan, to deny Cork's Seán Condon, left them trailing by just 1-7 to 2-1 at half-time. The decisive moment came in the second-half when, with Dublin just two points in arrears, their star midfielder Ned Wade missed an open goal. Soon after that Johnny Quirke, who had scored a goal just before the interval, set up Derry Beckett for a kicked goald and Cork ran out winners by 2-14 to 3-4. Jack Lynch, the future Taoiseach, was captain of the Cork side and received the Liam McCarthy Cup. It was Cork's thirteenth All-Ireland title and it took them to the top of the honours list over Tipperary and Kilkenny.

Thoughts immediately turned to the possibility of a third All-Ireland title in succession. The three-in-a-row was last completed by Kilkenny in 1913. It was a feat achieved only once before by Cork in 1892, 1893 and 1894.
