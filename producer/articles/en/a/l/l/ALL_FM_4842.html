ALL FM is a community radio station operating in south Manchester, UK, on 96.9 FM, and on the internet at www.allfm.org.

The station began in May 2000 as Radio Longsight, with a three-day test broadcast, supported by the Manchester-based charity Radio regen. In 2001, it was granted two temporary Restricted Service Licence broadcasts by the UK Government, under the name ALL FM. This led to a one-year licence in 2002, which was subsequently extended, and the station has been on-air permanently ever since. In 2005, the station was awarded a new licence which will run until the end of 2010.

The acronym "ALL" stands for Ardwick, Longsight and Levenshulme, the areas for Manchester served by the station between 2001 and 2005. Under the terms of its licence from 2006, however, the station serves a wider area of Manchester, including the city centre, Gorton, Moss Side, Hulme, Rusholme, Fallowfield, Withington, Whalley Range, Ancoats, Bradford and Clayton.

ALL FM is a community radio station. It exists in order to serve and provide a voice and artistic output for the residents of south Manchester. It is a not-for-profit company, and its content is mostly provided by volunteers from its coverage area.

[edit] Programming

Some of the shows broadcast on ALL FM include:

  • The Victoria Baths Show, a show produced by the army of volunteers campaigning for the restoration of Victoria Baths in Longsight
  • The Northern Wire, a show playing avant-garde rock. (Blog)
  • Under the Pavement, a show dedicated to political activism (see Under the Pavement website)
  • The Miss Jinny Show, a popular goth and punk show (see The Miss Jinny Show website)
  • Bass Culture, a long running reggae show presented by Lewdubs (see Bass Culture website)
  • Adult Learners' Lunch, a show highlighting adult learning opportunities in south Manchester
  • Persian Seda, believed to be the most widely listened to Iranian pop music show in the world (since pop music is banned in Iran itself)[citation needed]
  • Khatija's Show, a chat show in Urdu and English
  • Kr8z, a popular and eclectic music show
  • Various shows presented by schoolchildren
  • The Islamic Hour, a chat show for muslims and anyone else
  • Asian Angel, playing Bhangra, filmi and British Asian music
  • Law Matters, a legal advice show
  • Church Chat, presenter by local vicar Les Ireland, which broadcasts 10-11am on the first Monday of every month.
  • African Jamboree, bringing music and news from all over Africa
  • Access All Areas, a show tackling issues on behalf of disabled people. Every Thursday 11am-Noon.
  • Standing in the Shadows of Lev, indie show, every Saturday morning 9-11am.
  • Cultural Collage, a world music show that broadcasts every Sunday 10am-Noon.
  • Artbeat, a weekly look at the local and international art scene with Andrew Edwards. Every Sunday at noon.
  • The Community Show, featuring news from and for community organisations
  • The All FM Breakfast Express, Mon-Wed 7-10am, playing unsigned bands alongside releases from more established artists.
  • Nothing But Duttyness, a mix of new music and banter in the studio
  • The Full Irish, a mix of Irish music and news from the Irish community in the area (see The Full Irish website)

The station is unusual in featuring a wide range of different music shows, playing jazz, drum 'n' bass, hip hop, electronica, reggae, country, Irish music, World Music, indie, raag, northern soul, blues, gospel, pop, soul, punk rock, goth, post-rock, classical, house, bhangra, filmi, latin and desi.
