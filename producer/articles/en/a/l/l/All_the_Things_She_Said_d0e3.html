"All the Things She Said" (English version of "Ya Soshla S Uma") was the first single from t.A.T.u.'s English album 200 km/h in the Wrong Lane. The song did very well based on sales and radio airplay. It was the first single by a Russian artist to hit the number one in the UK Singles Chart and the Australian number one singles, a position it held for 4 weeks. It is also t.A.T.u's biggest hit to date, reaching #1 in more than 15 countries. It has received Platinum and Gold certifications all over the world making its physical and digital sales go over 4 Million copies.

t.A.T.u recieved numerous nominations for the song, including 'Best Breakthrough Artist' (Won) and also 'Best Pop Act' in MTV Asia Awards 2004. Only Lena attended the show and received the award, after that, they won 'Best New Artist' in Indonesia Magazine Music Awards 2004.

The song played through out the final scenes of the Birds of Prey series on the WB network, during 2003. It was also used as the entrance music for WWE women's wrestler Victoria from December 2002 to May 2004. The song is also considered by some to be a Gay anthem.

[edit] Music Video

The music video gained much popularity, and critics from their kiss in the video. It was identical to the video for "Ya Soshla S Uma," except of course the singing portions. Ivan Shapovalov directed the video. There is also a "All The Things She Said (HarDrum Remix)" video, identical to the "Ya Soshla S Uma (HarDrum Remix)" video.
