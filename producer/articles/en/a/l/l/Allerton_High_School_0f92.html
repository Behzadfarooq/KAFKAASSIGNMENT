Allerton High School is an 11-19 Comprehensive in Leeds, UK, specializing in Business and Enterprise. The chief executive is Mr. Chris Edwards, and the head teacher is Mrs E. Silson. [1] The school began as a kindergarten offshoot of Leeds Girls High School in 1901, in Chapel Allerton, then expanded into a girl's grammar school. It relocated from Chapel Allerton to its present campus on King Lane in 1939, where it was opened by Princess Mary, Princess Royal and Countess of Harewood. [2] In 1972, as part of the local government reorganization, boys were included in the student body and the school became a 13–18 comprehensive. It later became an 11–19 comprehensive, and in 1992 underwent a physical expansion. As of 2005 there were 1103 students, with an admission limit for September 2004 180 students in each year groups 7 – 11.

The school has a standardised uniform, comprising of a green sweatshirt or cardigan, printed with the school name, a white shirt, a green and white tie, a green skirt (girls only) or black trousers, and black shoes (heels to be no higher than 5cm) with white socks.

The school has seven full-size computer rooms and uses electronic whiteboards in the computer rooms and other rooms. The school has tennis courts, a video conferencing facility, [3] and has plans to build a multi-faith centre on the school site. [4]

The school is involved in the Excellence in Cities initiative. Its motto used to be 'Persuing Excellence...', until 2004 when it was changed to 'Enabling Young People To Achieve Success'.

Minority subjects such as Jewish studies and minority ethnic languages are offered to meet students' needs.

[edit] Future Plans

A new building has been planned to be built by 2008.
