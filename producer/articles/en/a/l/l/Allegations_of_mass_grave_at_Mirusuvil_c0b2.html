Allegations of mass grave at Mirusuvil

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Mirusuvil mass grave [1] refers the mass burial ground of eight (8) minority Sri Lankan Tamil refugees following an incident referred to as Mirusuvil massacre. [2][1]

[edit] The Incident

Eight refugees returning to inspect their property were arrested on 19 December 2000 in a village named Mirusuvil close to Jaffna. They were subsequently murdered by Sri Lankan Army soldiers and buried in a grave at Mirusuvil in Jaffna peninsula, about 16 miles east of Jaffna town. [2][1]

The refugees had returned from Udupiddy, further north. They returned to Mirusuvil on 19 December to inspect their houses and to collect firewood, when they were seized by the Army. According to the evidence of District Medical Officer Dr C Kathirvetpillai, their throats had been slashed. The dead included three teenagers and five year-old Vilvarajah Prasath [2][1]

The murders came to light because one of the arrested one Ponnuthurai Maheswaran escaped from Army custody with serious injuries and informed relatives.[2]

[edit] Case follow up

The police arrested an Army officer and six soldiers in late December of 2000. Five soldiers were identified in identification parades held in Point Pedro on 3 and 4 January 2001. [2]

The government prosecutor charged the arrested soldiers for illegally arresting and killing seven Tamil refugees who came to inspect their homes and then burying them at a mass grave at Mirusuvil. [1]

The government in 2002 agreed to try the arrested army officers without a jury[3] The case was still being heard in 2005
