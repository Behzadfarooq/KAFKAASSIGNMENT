The Guinness Hurling Championship 1994 ran from May to September of 1994. The 1994 championship provided one of the most exciting All-Ireland Hurling Finals in recent years with Offaly defeating Limerick by 3-16 to 2-13. It has become known as the "five minute final."

[edit] Format

The 1994 championship used the traditional "knock-out" format. The provincial champions of Munster, Connacht, Ulster and Leinster automatically qualified for and were drawn against each other in the All-Ireland semi-finals.

[edit] The 5-Minute Final

The 1994 All-Ireland final was contested by the Munster champions Limerick and the Leinster champions Offaly. The final itself saw one of the most exciting of finishes. With four minutes left Offaly trailed by 5 points when they were awarded a free. Johnny Dooley was instructed to take a point, however, he went against the wishes of the management and produced a stunning goal. Another goal 30 seconds later and 4 points brought Offaly back from the dead and ended Limerick's dreams of an All-Ireland title.
