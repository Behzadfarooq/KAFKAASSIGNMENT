All by Myself

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

"All by Myself" is a power ballad written and performed by Eric Carmen in 1975 that has spawned numerous cover versions. It borrows heavily from Sergei Rachmaninoff's Piano Concerto No. 2 in C minor. Carmen's full version has an extended piano solo and lasts seven minutes. In a 2006 poll for the Channel 5 program Britain's Favourite Break-up Songs, Eric Carmen's version of this song was voted seventeenth.

[edit] Eric Carmen version

The song was originally recorded by the author and released in 1976 to great success. It reached #2 on the American charts, and #12 in the UK.

"All by Myself" is the fourth (or third - depends on the country) hit single from Céline Dion's Falling into You album, and is arguably one of Dion's most powerful vocal performances yet, showing off her incredible vocal range. It was released on October 7, 1996 in Europe, January 13, 1997 in Australia and March 11, 1997 in North America.

Dion in the "All by Myself" music video (1996).

There were three music videos made. The first one was released in October 1996. It contains fragments from Dion's photo session for the Falling into You album cover and some scenes from her Live à Paris concert. It was made for the single version. The second version was made for the UK market with the same white t-shirt scenes but adding footage of a young couple in love at Christmas time, instead scenes from Dion's concert. This was made for the album version. Finally, a live music video was released in March 1997 in North America (from the 1996 concert in Montreal).

Céline Dion performed this song many times during her world tours, TV shows and many important music events, including: Grammy Awards (1997), Billboard Music Awards (1997), and Bambi Awards (1996).

She recorded also a Spanish version, called "Sola Otra Vez." It was added to the Falling into You Latin America and Spanish edition, and as a B-side to other singles from that album. It became available worldwide on Dion's compilation The Collector's Series Volume One. A music video was made for "Sola Otra Vez" similar to "All by Myself."

Dion in the "All by Myself" live music video (1997).

The single became one of Dion's biggest hits in the U.S., reching #1 on the Hot Adult Contemporary Tracks (for three weeks) and Hot Latin Pop Airplay (two weeks). It peaked at #4 on the Billboard Hot 100 (#7 on the Billboard Hot 100 Airplay, #5 on the Hot 100 Singles Sales). It was also #1 on the U.S. ARC Weekly Top 40 (for two weeks), and a top ten hit in France, UK, Belgium Wallonie, Ireland and Poland. In Canada, "All by Myself" was released as a promotional single only, hitting #1 on the Adult Contemporary charts. "All by Myself" was certified gold in the U.S. (500,000), and silver in the UK (200,000) and France (163,000).

The song was included on some editions of Dion's 1999 greatest hits album All the Way... A Decade of Song.
