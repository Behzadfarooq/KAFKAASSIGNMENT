Allan Quartermaine (1916-11 November 1974) was an English stockbroker who was killed by the Provisional Irish Republican Army on the 11 November 1974. Allan Quartermaine, at 58 years old, was shot whilst in his chauffeur - driven car at traffic lights in Chelsea.

Quartermaine was a Royal Air Force pilot during World War II and, until his death, was a director of Leslie & Gordon Ltd [1].

It is not known why he was targeted although it is suspected that he was killed as part of the IRA's Marxist philosophy and their campaign against Capitalist persons and institutions.[1][2]

Members of the IRA's Balcombe Street Gang were charged and convicted of the murder of Quartermaine.[3]
