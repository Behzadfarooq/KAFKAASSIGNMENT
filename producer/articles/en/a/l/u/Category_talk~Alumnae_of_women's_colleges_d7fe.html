Isn't there a better way of doing this? I would think that alumnae of women's colleges should be categorized under the college that they attended, and then those colleges can be grouped together as women's colleges. But then again, we are forced to have this "Living People" category which doesn't make much sense to me. — Shadowhillway 17:15, 15 April 2006 (UTC)

I would see this as a comparison: We list people from New York collectively, rather than putting them all under their separate towns. When there are enough people from one town or city to merit a separate category, we make it a subcategory. That's what we're doing now with Mount Holyoke, for example.
Also, if we were to only do individual college categories, there would only be a handful of women's colleges with enough notable alumnae to merit their own categories. Schools with very notable alumnae, such as Chatham/Rachel Carson and Moore/P!nk, would be left out. It makes more sense to group everyone together, and then break off into subcategories when the lists for those colleges get long enough. We're getting there.
Women's colleges have a shared tradition that's even more documented than that of Ivy League graduates. Women's college alumnae tend to identify very strongly with the fact that they attended women's colleges, and not just their individual alma maters. Grouping them together doesn't hurt anything, I don't think. It actually makes a tremendous deal of sense to me, particularly because of the pride most students feel for attending a women's college.
Disclaimer: Not attending a women's college, and never have. It's an academic interest of mine. Beginning 17:50, 15 April 2006 (UTC)
Sounds good. I'm sure the future category math feature will be helpful for situations like this one here. — Shadowhillway 15:09, 16 April 2006 (UTC)
