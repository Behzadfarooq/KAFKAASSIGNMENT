The most notable alumni of Saint Ignatius High School in Cleveland, Ohio include:

[edit] Arts and Entertainment

  • Jack Riley, 1954 - Comedian/Actor - The Bob Newhart Show, Spaceballs, Rugrats, among others[1]
  • Brian K. Vaughan, 1994 - award-winning comic book writer of Y: The Last Man, writer of Wonder Woman [2][3][4]
  • Billy Matsumoto, 2006 - Champion on ABC show Master of Champions, also appeared on Good Morning America; juggled torches while playing Dance Dance Revolution [5]

[edit] Business

  • Murlan J. Murphy, 1935 - Inventor of Murphy's Oil Soap[6]
  • Charles Geschke, 1956 - Co-founder of Adobe Systems[6][7][8]
  • James E. Rohr, 1962 - CEO of PNC Financial Services [9]
  • George Samenuk, 1973 - Former chairman and CEO of McAfee, Inc.[9]

[edit] Law and Order

  • David Ferrie, 1936 - Purportedly involved in John F. Kennedy's assassination[citation needed]
  • Danny Greene, Expelled around 1950, Mobster and racketeer [10]
  • Francis E. Sweeney Sr., 1952 - Retired Ohio Supreme Court Justice [11][12]
  • Martin J. Sweeney, 1981 - Current President of Cleveland City Council[6]

[edit] Journalism

  • Kenneth L. Woodward, 1966 - Former chief religious editor for Newsweek[8][13]
  • Richard F. Hlabse (Dick Russ), 1971 - TV personality and Broadcasters Hall of Fame member. Managing editor, WKYC TV 3 Cleveland, Ohio.[citation needed]
  • Jack Corrigan, TV and Radio Annoucer for the Colorado Rockies[14]
  • Bill Sammon, 1978 - Senior White House Correspondent for The Washington Examiner[citation needed]
  • John Chandler, 1996 - Sports Journalist at News Channel 5[15]

[edit] Religion

  • Timothy Broglio, 1970, Archbishop, Vatican Ambassador to the Dominican Republic and Puerto Rico[6][8]

[edit] Sports

  • Larry Dolan, 1951 - Owner, Cleveland Indians[16]
  • Brian Dowling, 1965 - Former Yale and NFL quarterback (inspiration for the character B.D. in the Doonesbury comic strip)[6]
  • Mike Hegan, 1960- TV and Radio Announcer, Cleveland Indians, former Major League Baseball player
  • Mike Buddie, 1989 - Pitcher for the Milwaukee Brewers[12]
  • Tim Mack, 1990 - 2004 Olympics Gold Medalist (Pole Vault) [6][17]
  • Juan Porter, 1993 - Columbus Destroyers' Offensive lineman [18]
  • Eric Gohlstin, 1994 - NFL and Arena League lineman [19][20]
  • Scott Mutryn, 1994 - Amsterdam Admirals' quarterback[21]
  • Dan O'Leary, 1995 - NFL tight end [22]
  • Chris Hovan, 1996 - Defensive Lineman, Tampa Bay Buccaneers [23]
  • Drew Haddad, 1996 - NFL wide receiver[24]
  • Matt Kata, 1996 - Second Baseman, Cincinnati Reds [25]
  • Chris Gizzi, 1997 - Linebacker, Green Bay Packers [26]
  • LeCharles Bentley, 1998 - Offensive Lineman, Cleveland Browns[6][27]
  • Dave Ragone, 1998 - Quarter Back, Berlin Thunder [28]
  • Jacob Bell, 1999 - Guard/Tackle, Tennessee Titans [29]
  • John Baco, 2000 - Pitcher, Fordham Rams [30]
  • Jon Gannon, 2001 - Coach with Atlanta Falcons, current youngest coach in the NFL [31][32]
  • Anthony Gonzalez 2003 - Split End, Ohio State Buckeyes [33]
  • Brian Hoyer, 2004 - Quarter Back, Michigan State Spartans [34]
  • Mike Massey, 2004 - Tight End, The University of Michigan [35]
  • John Conroy, 2001 - Offensive Guard, The Ohio State University[citation needed]
