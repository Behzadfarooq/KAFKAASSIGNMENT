Aldo Gordini (born May 20, 1921 - died January 28, 1995) was a Formula One driver from France. Born in Bologna, Italy, he was the son of Amédée Gordini, owner of the French sports car manufacturer Gordini. Aldo worked for the family's racing team as a mechanic and occasionally drove in Grand Prix motor racing events and Formula 2 races.

He participated in one Formula 1 race on July 1, 1951. He scored 0 championship points. That same year he also drove for the Gordini team at the 24 hours of Le Mans but fuel pump problems forced him out of the race.

Aldo Gordini died in Paris in 1995.
