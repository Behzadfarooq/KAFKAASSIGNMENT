Aldo Andretti (born February 28, 1940 in Montona d'Istria, Italy (now Motovun, Croatia)) is the twin brother of Mario Andretti and the father of John Andretti and Adam Andretti. He is the uncle of Michael Andretti and Jeff Andretti, and great-uncle to Marco Andretti.

[edit] Background

Aldo and Mario were born in Italy, where they were inspired by watching the great Alberto Ascari race at Monza. After World War II Istria (which is now part of Croatia) was occupied and annexed by Yugoslavia. His family, like many other Italian Istrians, fled in 1948. They lived in a refugee camp from 1948 to 1955. The family settled in Nazareth, Pennsylvania and the brothers discovered racing on an oval track in their home town.

[edit] Racing career

Together they rebuilt a 1948 Hudson Hornet into a stock car in 1959. They began racing in the old Hudson, and they each had two wins after the first four weeks. Aldo fractured his skull in a serious crash near the end of the season, but he returned the following season.

Aldo continued racing on the USAC and IMCA circuits.

Aldo suffered severe damage to his face in a crash in 1969 and quit racing.[1] Mario described the accident in his book What's it like out there: "Aldo continued racing until August 17, 1969, when he had an accident, which was not his fault, in an IMCA sprint race at Des Moines, Iowa. He was driving a sprint car that I had bought for him."[2] Mario went on to win the Indianapolis 500, the Daytona 500, and the Formula One World Championship.
