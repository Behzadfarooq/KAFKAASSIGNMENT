"Always Tomorrow" is a single by American singer and songwriter Gloria Estefan. It was released in 1992 worldwide as the leading and first single from her fourth solo album Gloria Estefan Greatest Hits.

[edit] Song History

The first single released from the second Gloria Estefan compilation album, this time an adult contemporary song, this song it was very acoustic and shows in almost all the entire song Gloria performing at the guitar or at least it could be understand like that way, because in the video she appears performing the guitar, and the tour while she sung the song she plays the acoustic guitar too.

The video of the song is the most simple and less complicated of all the videos that she has ever release, and it shown how they rebuild again a place, because this song is better know for used as a special theme for the hurricane Andrew relief, all the royalties that she received with the single sales were benefited the victims of this hurricane at the South of the Florida. Gloria made his objective helping to gather over $4 million dollars.

This song was always used by Gloria for make a lot of relief’s, also was created a special medley with "Coming Out of the Dark" in a compilation made by various artists called "Hurricane Relief: Come Together now" created in 2005 for the Hurricane Katrina relief. This acoustic medley version only can be found at that compilation.

[edit] Chart performance

The single made a great performance for being a ballad song, because Gloria used to have the high positions then only with the dance songs. It was the first time when Gloria appeared at the Colombian chart, and make a great performance at the Top 5. At the Adult Contemporary chart Gloria performed at the Top 5.
