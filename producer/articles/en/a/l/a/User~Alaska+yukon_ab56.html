[edit] AYAP

[edit] What is AYAP?

AYAP also known as the Alaska-Yukon Amalgamation Project is a political action group whose end goal is the amalgamation of the State of Alaska and the Territory of Yukon.

Started in March 2006 the AYAP is the first of many steps necessary to unite Alaska and the Canadian Arctic Territories. Also known by more optomistic members as AYNAP Alaska-Yukon-Northwest Amalgamation Project. The Northwest Territories are not our main focus but are worked on in parallel to our Yukon project. There are even hopes of bringing Nunavut into the Alaskan Union but cultural differences may override citizen benefits in this case.

AYAP or AYNAP operates under a larger idea of a United North America the hope that Canadian provinces will enter the United States in a larger amalgamated governance and AYAP attempts to answer the question of what will happen to the Canadian territories under a United North America.


[edit] Information for Alaska and Yukon

Total Area = 2,200,297 square kilometers or 849,539 square miles

Total Population (based on Alaska 2000 census + Yukon 2005 census) = 658,159 people

Total Gross State Product (based on Alaska 2004 + Yukon 2005) = 35.545 billion $US dollars

[edit] Goals of AYAP

1) To educate the voters of Alaska and Yukon to the benefits of amalgamation (politics)

2) To produce potential candidates for state and territory offices as Pro AYAP and bring the issue of amalgamation center stage for the two regions.

3) To bring the regions closer economically and physically through elimination of border units and integration of state and territory services.


[edit] Current Actions

A few members of AYAP are active posters at the United North America forum always attempting to network. The summer of 2006 the project should move into the public light during the Alaskan Legislative and Gubernatorial Elections of 2006. Developing an effective message that touches and unites voters directly is the objective this summer as well as finding a legislature in office willing to make changes at the state level conducive to an Alaska-Yukon amalgamation.


University of Alaska - Whitehorse (UAW) is a proposed project that it is hoped will be taken on by the AYAP. This provides an opportunity to meet the goals of the Yukon in establishing a university and the goals of Alaska in providing education and research opportunities in the Arctic climates. By establishing an Alaskan university in the Yukon we create a bond that slowly begin to dissolve the international border separating our regions.

[edit] Current Vision for a United North America

1. Amalgamation of Alaska and one or more Arctic Territories

2. Statehood for British Columbia to connect our lands
