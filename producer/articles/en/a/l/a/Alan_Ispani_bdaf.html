Alan Ispani was baptised in Corrientes, Provincias Unidas del Río de la Plata (now known as Argentina), on February the 23rd of 1647. His birth date is uncertain, but parents customarily baptised their children two days after birth.

In 1665, at the age of eigteen, he moved to Buenos Aires. The events of the next ten years of his life are uncertain, but in 1675, at the age of twenty eight, he committed his first murder, the first of sixteen. Ultimately, at the age of fifty-seven, he was arrested and condemned to death on the charge of attempting treason against the royal crown of Spain for his attempt to murder the viceroy Francisco Fernández de la Cueva Enríquez. Ispani died on March 30, 1704.
