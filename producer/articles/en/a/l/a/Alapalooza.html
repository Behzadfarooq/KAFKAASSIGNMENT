Alapalooza is the eighth album by "Weird Al" Yankovic, released in 1993. (see 1993 in music). The cover sleeve is a spoof on the movie poster of the hit movie Jurassic Park, based on Michael Crichton's novel. The name is a parody of the music festival Lollapalooza.

[edit] Track listing

  1. "Jurassic Park" (Webb, Yankovic) – 3:55
    • parody of "MacArthur Park", written by Jimmy Webb and originally recorded by Richard Harris, which recaps the plot of the movie Jurassic Park which had been released earlier that year.
  2. "Young, Dumb & Ugly" (Yankovic) – 4:24
    • about a group of "rebels without a clue" who try to act tough, but only look silly
    • style parody of Bon Scott-era AC/DC
  3. "Bedrock Anthem" (Flea, Frusciante, Kiedis, Smith, Yankovic) – 3:40
    • parody of the "Under the Bridge" intro and "Give It Away" by Red Hot Chili Peppers, about life in Bedrock, the city home to The Flintstones.
  4. "Frank's 2000" TV" (Yankovic) – 4:05
    • style parody of R.E.M.'s early work, about "Keeping Up With The Joneses" taken to absurd lengths. For the record, a 2000 inch television would have a 166.67 (50.8m) foot diagonal.
    • Although a style parody of R.E.M.'s early work, some say it is similar to "Shiny Happy People" by R.E.M.. It also features the "Losing My Religion" mandolin.
  5. "Achy Breaky Song" (VonTress, Yankovic) – 3:23
    • parody of "Achy Breaky Heart" by Billy Ray Cyrus, about the revulsion inspired by constant play of that song. The speaker mentions several other groups and artists he would rather hear, including Donny and Marie, Barry Manilow, New Kids on the Block, the Village People, Vanilla Ice, the Bee Gees, Debby Boone, ABBA, Slim Whitman, Zamfir, Yoko Ono, Tiffany.
  6. "Traffic Jam" (Yankovic) – 4:01
    • style parody of Prince's "Let's Go Crazy" about the perils of getting caught in gridlock
  7. "Talk Soup" (Yankovic) – 4:24
    • about a man who wants to get on Geraldo, Sally Jessy Raphael, or any daytime talk show and talk about his bizarre relationships. The song's title is a reference to the cable show Talk Soup. The song ends with the same "E!" clip used at the end of the show.
    • style parody of "Sledgehammer" by Peter Gabriel and "Superstition" by Stevie Wonder
  8. "Livin' In The Fridge" (Hudson, Perry, Tyler, Yankovic) – 3:35
    • parody of "Livin' on the Edge" by Aerosmith, is a cautionary tale about expired food gaining sentience
  9. "She Never Told Me She Was A Mime" (Yankovic) – 4:54
    • about a man who misses the obvious signs his girlfriend is a mime
  10. "Harvey The Wonder Hamster" (Yankovic) – 0:21
    • an anthem for Harvey The Wonder Hamster
  11. "Waffle King" (Yankovic) – 4:25
    • about a man's ascendance to celebrity status through his wondrous waffle recipe, and the resultant ego boost he receives
    • style parody of "Sledgehammer" by Peter Gabriel
    • first released as the B-side of the single "Smells Like Nirvana", from Yankovic's previous studio album, Off The Deep End
  12. "Bohemian Polka" (F. Mercury, Yankovic) – 3:39
    • "Bohemian Rhapsody" by Queen set to a polka beat. With its higher tempo, Polka ends a full two minutes and twenty seconds sooner than Rhapsody. This is the only all-polka song of Yankovic's to date that features a single song, as opposed to the polka medleys that appear on most of his other albums.
