Alan Brien

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Alan Brien (born March 12, 1925) is a British journalist best known for his novel Lenin. This took the form of a fictional diary charting Lenin's life from the death of his father to shortly before his own demise in 1924.

During his career in journalism, Brien worked as a critic, columnist and foreign correspondent for a variety of publications, most notably The Sunday Times, Punch, the New Statesman and The Observer.

Brien was married to the British journalist and feminist writer Jill Tweedie.
