James Alan Ball (26 September 1924 - 2 January 1982), was an English footballer and manager.

Born in Farnworth, Lancashire, Ball played as an inside-forward for Bolton B.F., Southport (46+2 league appearances) (1946-1947), Birmingham City (1947-1948), Southport (41 appearances, 9 goals) (1948-1950), Oldham Athletic (7 appearances, 1 goal) (1950-1952) and Rochdale (5 appearances, one goal) (1952).

He managed Halifax Town (1967-1970) and (1976-1977) and Preston North End (1970-1973).

At the time of his death in a car crash, aged 57, he was working as a football coach in Cyprus. The taxi driver responsible for the crash was given a one-year driving ban, while Ball's widow, Violet, was granted Â£25,000 in compensation. Their son, Alan Jr., played in England's 1966 FIFA World Cup winning team and followed his father into management.
