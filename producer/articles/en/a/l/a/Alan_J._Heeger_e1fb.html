Alan Jay Heeger (born January 22, 1936 in Sioux City, Iowa) is a United States chemistry and physics academic and Nobel Prize winner.

He earned his Ph.D in Physics from UC Berkeley in 1961. [1] He won the Nobel Prize for Chemistry in 2000 along with Alan G. MacDiarmid and Hideki Shirakawa "for their discovery and development of conductive polymers". He is currently a professor at the University of California, Santa Barbara. His sons are the neuroscientist David Heeger and the immunologist Peter Heeger.
