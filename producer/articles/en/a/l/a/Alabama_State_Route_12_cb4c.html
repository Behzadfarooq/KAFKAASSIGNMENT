State Route 12 is the unsigned partner route assigned to U.S. Highway 84 throughout Alabama. S.R. 12 begins at the Mississippi state line near Isney in southern Choctaw County. The eastern terminus of S.R. 12 is at the Georgia state line at near Gordon in southeastern Houston County.

U.S. Highway 84 and S.R. 12 cross the southern part of the state; as such, it passes through a series of smaller towns and communities. Dothan, in the southeastern corner of the state, is the only city whose population exceeds 50,000 that the routes pass through. Other cities and towns the U.S. 84 and S.R. 12 pass through include:

  • Grove Hill
  • Monroeville
  • Evergreen
  • Andalusia
  • Opp
  • Enterprise

U.S. 84 and S.R. 12 intersect the following Interstate highways, U.S. highways, and major state routes in Alabama:

  • U.S. Highway 43 and unsigned State Route 13 at Grove Hill
  • Interstate 65 near Evergreen
  • U.S. Highway 31 and unsigned State Route 3 at Evergreen. U.S. 31, U.S 84, S.R. 3 and S.R. 12 overlap through the city and split near Herbert in eastern Conecuh County.
  • U.S. Highway 29 and unsigned State Route 15 in Andalusia. The routes briefly overlap through the city.
  • U.S. Highway 331 and unsigned State Route 9 in Opp.
  • U.S. Highway 231, U.S. Highway 431 and unsigned State Route 1 and unsigned State Route 53 in Dothan. These six routes overlap along Ross Clark Circle (State Route 210), which bypasses the city.
