Alan Longmuir

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Sleeve for Longmuir's 1977 release "I'm Confessing"

Alan Longmuir (born June 20, 1949) was the bass guitarist for the massively successful 1970s pop group, the Bay City Rollers.

He was born at Simpson Memorial Maternity Pavilion Hospital, Edinburgh, Scotland, and is the elder brother of the group's drummer, Derek Longmuir. In early 1976, at the height of the band's popularity, Alan was replaced by Ian Mitchell, a man 10 years his junior. Tam Paton, then the group's manager, revealed that Alan had tried to commit suicide. Longmuir would return to the group in 1978.

Longmuir played the lead in the 1981 film Burning Rubber, an auto-racing melodrama filmed in South Africa.

Lately in poor health, he suffered a heart attack in 1995 and a stroke in 1997.

Today, Longmuir lives with his wife, Eileen, in Scotland.
