Alanta – (dialectal Aukštaitian name Alunta) is a small town in Lithuania, in Molėtai district. It is situated on a crossroad of two roads: Molėtai–Anykščiai and Utena–Alanta–Ukmergė. The town has its St. Jacob's church (built in 1909). There are 2600 people living in the elderate of Alanta and more than 450 live in Alanta.

[edit] Etymology of the name

The name "Alanta" is derived from the River Alanta, which flows near the town. The river's name derived from the ancient Lithuanian verb "alėti" (to stream merrily, to run)

[edit] History

Alanta was granted to Kristinas Astikas by Sigismund Kestutaitis in 1436 as a commendation for his aid in defeating Švitrigaila. In the 16th century the Catholic church was built. Grand Duke of Lithuania Stefan Batory gifted Alanta to Gaspar Bekesy (Lithuanian: Kasparas Bekešas), Hungarian general, in 1581. After 1598 the town belonged to Radvilas and from 1828 until the First World War it belonged to the Pamarnacki family. From the 18-th century to the revolt in 1863 in Lithuania Alanta had a parish school. In the years 1861–1950 it was the center of vicinity. Alanta suffered many historic events like Napoleon's invasion of the Russian Empire, WWI or WWII because it was on the crossroad of important roads.

The Alanta estate these days

[edit] The estate palace of Alanta

Nowadays the estate palace is recovered and the park got in trim. There is a library and an ethnographical museum in the accommodation of the estate. The founder of the library Elvyra Satkūnaitė (rest eternal) was granted as "The best librarian of Lithuania" in 1996.
