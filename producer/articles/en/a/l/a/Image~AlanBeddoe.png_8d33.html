Alan Beddoe

Source: [1], from Library and Archives Canada, item C-033702.

Photographer: Jules Alexandre Castonguay (1877 - 1972), worked as a professional portrait photographer 1903 - 1949.

[edit] Licensing

Although the date of the photograph is not known, one of the two licenses applies. If the photograph was taken before January 1, 1949 (because the photographer retired in 1949, this is likely true):

 This Canadian work is in the public domain in Canada because its copyright has expired for one of the following reasons:
1. it was subject to Crown copyright and was first published more than 50 years ago, or

it was not subject to Crown copyright, and

2. it is a photograph that was created before January 1, 1949, or
3. the creator died more than 50 years ago.
The media description page should identify which reason applies, and should also have a rationale explaining the copyright status of the work in the U.S. Some Canadian photographs created before 1949 may be copyrighted outside of Canada, such as e.g. the images of Yousuf Karsh.

Copyright status in the U.S. unclear. If the image was published (in Canada) and taken before January 1, 1946, it would be PD in the U.S because its Canadian copyright would then have expired before January 1, 1996.
