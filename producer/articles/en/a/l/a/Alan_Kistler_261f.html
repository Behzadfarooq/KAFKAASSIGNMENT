Alan P. Kistler (born on January 25, 1982) is an internet blogger who lives in New York City (his home since August, 2000) and who has become known to many comic fans and several professional writers as a "comic book historian." Most of his work can be found at the comic book news blog MonitorDuty.com, though he also maintains a livejournal site where he writes pieces of fan-fiction.

In the book WEBSLINGER: Unauthorized Essays on Your Friendly Neighborhood Spider-Man, contributing author Michael A. Burstein references Alan Kistler as a "comic book historian" who helped him with his essay.

Alan Kistler's articles are cited in many Wikipedia articles and have frequently been linked to by other comic book based web-sites and forums. These articles tend to be long essays (ranging from 20 - 90 pages) detailng the histories of various comic book super-heroes and villains, focusing not just on how their origins and interprations were altered over the year but the editorial decisions that led to these changes and commentary on what fans responded to. He has also interviewed comic book writers such as Mark Waid and Matt Wagner and has posted over 200 pages worth of material detailing the history of the various incarnations of Doctor Who, as well as the history of the program itself and its many spin-offs.

Alan Kistler's notoriety for his internet articles and super-hero profiles has gotten him the attention of several science-fiction writers as well as a documentary currently in production by Trey Mojo Productions. This documentary focuses on comic book movies and TV adaptations and includes interviews with Lynda Carter and Tom Mankiewicz (writer and creative consultant on Superman). Trej Mojo contacted Alan Kistler due to his extensive articles and profiles and credited him as a comic book historian during his interview. The release date of this documentary is still pending.

Although known for oped pieces, commentaries and non-fiction histories, Alan Kistler has often stated he would like to pubish his own fiction pieces and is particularly interested in writing for DC Comics, Marvel Comics and Doctor Who. When he was in high school, he posted stories on several sites on-line and one of them, entitled "CRIME SYNDICATE" (based loosely on the super-villain Crime Syndicate team) recieved weekly e-mails for over a year from many fans. He is frequently seen at the Midtown Comics Grand Central Store in New York City. In his spare time, he makes short films with his friend Cynthia Lau, including a series of short comedies called "Coffee and Donuts" starring two detectives of the same names. Profesionally, he has worked for ABC/CHANNEL 8 News in Rosslyn, Virginia and worked as a production assistant on the independent film 'Raisins Not Virgins.' He has had cartoons and articles published in the Fordham University Observer and was art editor for the Little Hoya, a publication of Georgetown Prepatory School in North Bethesda, Maryland. His comic strips in the Little Hoya often starred a character called "Sizzler", presumably based on himself since this is his nickname among many friends. Several comic book fans have e-mailed MonitorDuty.com to offer their support that he will one day write for the professional comic book industry.

Alan Kistler has credited some of his notoriety to Michael Hutchison, editor of MonitorDuty.com, and award winning science-fiction writer Michael A. Burstein, who has often advertised notices of Alan Kistler's latest articles on his own livejournal. Alan Kistler has also been seen at conventions advertising signings for his friend sci-fi/fantasy writer Aaron Rosenberg.
