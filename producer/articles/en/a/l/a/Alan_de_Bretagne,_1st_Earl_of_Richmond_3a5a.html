Alan de Bretagne, 1st Earl of Richmond

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Alan de Bretagne (c. 1116-1146) was the son of Stephen, Count of Tréguier. He was the first to be styled Earl of Richmond, although Stephen and some of his predecessors had been territorial lords of Richmondshire before him. Alan married Bertha, daughter and heiress of Conan III, Duke of Brittany.

His heir by Bertha was Conan IV, Duke of Brittany and an illegitimate son was Brian Fitz Alan, progenitor of the Bedale barons. Another son was Reynald, knight of Fritheby. They also had Constance, who married Alan III, Vicomte of Rohan and Enougen, abbess of St. Sulpice.
