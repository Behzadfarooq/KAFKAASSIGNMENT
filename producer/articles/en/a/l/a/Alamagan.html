Alamagan

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

The Northern Marianas island of Alamagan is located 421 nautical miles north from Saipan and is 11.12 km² (4.295 sq mi) in area. The island's volcano (Manerea Peak) has a large waterfall at the summit. The volcano last erupted in 2006. The west side is cut by deep gorges covered with high savanna grass. The southeast side is a steep slope of bare lava. There are deep valleys with caves. Coconut palms grow on the gradual slopes. Warm fresh water springs are located on the northern part of the west coast.

Alamagan is the home of the endangered Nightingale Reed-warbler Acrocephalus luscinia which presently can be found only here and on [[Saipan] and possibly Argentina .

Historically the island has been sporadically populated; as of late 2005 there were 44 inhabitants on Alamagan. The 2000 census reported an official population of 5,459 persons. The island is part of the Northern Islands Municipality.
