Alan Prince

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Alan Sanford Prince (born 1946) is a professor of linguistics at Rutgers University. Prince, along with Paul Smolensky, developed Optimality Theory a theory about phonology. He went to high school in Fairfax, Virginia, got his BA with "great distinction" from McGill, and received his Ph.D. from MIT.
