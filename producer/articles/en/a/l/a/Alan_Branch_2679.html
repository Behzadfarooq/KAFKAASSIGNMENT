Alan Keith Branch (born December 29, 1984) is an American football defensive tackle for the Michigan Wolverines. Alan Branch has decided to skip his senior season with the Wolverines in order to declare for the 2007 NFL Draft.

[edit] High school career

Branch attended Cibola High School in Albuquerque, New Mexico. In addition to playing on the defensive line, Branch also played offensive tackle as well as running back and punt returner in high school. He scored 3 rushing touchdowns and 2 receiving touchdowns in his senior season and 5 punt return touchdowns for his career.

Branch was a fairly highly regarded recruit coming out of high school, ranked as high as #6 nationally among defensive tackles.

Branch was also a three year starter on the varsity basketball team, earning all-district honors as a sophomore and was second team all-state as a junior.

[edit] Awards

  • Selected to play in 2004 US Army All-American Game
  • Gatorade Player of the Year in New Mexico
  • All-State as a junior and senior

[edit] College career

Branch, along with fellow lineman LaMarr Woodley, anchored one of the best defenses in college football in 2006. He had 57 tackles, 17 tackles for loss, and nine sacks in 35 career games at Michigan. He had his first interception and a fumble recovery against Ohio State on November 18, 2006. Branch played both defensive tackle and defensive end at Michigan.

Branch, a sure-fire first round pick has been reported by several sources to have said that he wishes to play for the Washington Redskins. He grew up a Redskin fan, due to a loose connection through an uncle. He dreams of being a Redskin and them having a top ten pick has a lot to do with his decision to come out in the draft this year, forgoing his senior year at Michigan.[citation needed]

  • 2006 ESPN.com All-American Team
  • 2006 All-Big Ten Conference First Team (coaches and media)

[edit] Professional career

Branch decided to skip his senior season at Michigan. He is projected to be one of the top picks in the 2007 NFL Draft [1]
