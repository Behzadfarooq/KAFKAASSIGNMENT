State Route 69 is a 259 mile long route that extends from Jackson in Clarke County to Guntersville in Marshall County. It is one of the longest marked state routes in Alabama. Other cities and towns along the route include Greensboro, Tuscaloosa, Northport, Jasper, Cullman, and Arab.

The southern terminus of S.R. 69 is at its junction with State Route 177. The route begins in the Black Belt (region of Alabama), and passes through rural areas of west Alabama before assuming a northeastward trajectory from Jasper to its northern terminus in Guntersville at the junction with U.S. Route 431 and State Route 79.

[edit] Junctions with Major Routes

  • The southern terminus at State Route 177 in Jackson
  • U.S. Highway 43 at Jackson
  • U.S. Highway 84 at Nanafalia
  • U.S. Route 43 at Linden. The two routes overlap until they split at Providence in Marengo County
  • U.S. Highway 80 near Prairieville in western Hale County.
  • Interstate 359 and U.S. Highway 11 at Tuscaloosa. Northbound I-359 and S.R. 69 are co-routed with southbound U.S. 11 until they junction with U.S. 43 near the campus of the University of Alabama south of downtown Tuscaloosa.
  • Interstate 20/Interstate 59 at Tuscaloosa
  • A third junction with U.S. Highway 43 at the northern terminus of I-359. U.S. 43 and S.R. 69 overlap until they split in Northport.
  • U.S. Highway 82 in Northport
  • Future Interstate 22/Corridor X/U.S. Highway 78 near Jasper.
  • State Route 5 in Jasper. Northbound S.R. 69 is co-routed with southbound S.R. 5 along the former route of U.S. 78 through Jasper. S.R. 5 and S.R. 69 split near the southeastern city limits of Jasper.
  • Interstate 65 in southern Cullman County at Exit 299. S.R. 69 is routed on I-65 between Exits 299 and 304. S.R. 69 is one of two signed Alabama state routes to be co-routed on an Interstate highway. (The other is State Route 5, which is routed along Interstate 20/Interstate 59 in Tuscaloosa County and Jefferson County).
  • U.S. Highway 31 at Cullman. U.S. 31 and S.R. 69 are co-routed as they approach downtown Cullman.
  • U.S. Highway 278 at Cullman. U.S. 278 and S.R. 69 are co-routed as they head east from downtown Cullman. The two routes split east of Cullman.
  • U.S. Highway 231 at Arab
  • The northern terminus at the junction with U.S. Highway 431 and State Route 79 at Guntersville
