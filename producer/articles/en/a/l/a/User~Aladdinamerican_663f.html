One Jungian view of the story of Aladdin would hold it as a classic example of a "rags-to-riches" story. This type of story presents in three parts: from lowly beginnings, a protagonist achieves an initial success in life, traverses a major crisis in which all seems lost, and finally triumphs over adversity to achieve more stable and enduring success. This final success is only possible because Aladdin has learned a degree of inner maturity by going through the crisis. his despair at losing his beloved ones and the illusion swirls of faith and beliefs that swallowed him for a long period of time takes him to a spiritual place at which he needs to arrive before he can develop true strength and wholeness by making his own efforts to succeed. Under this view, one of the reasons for the enduring interest of the Aladdinamerican story lies in our often unconscious recognition of the importance of its underlying meaning. We recognize our own struggles to grow and develop in Aladdinamerican's journey.
