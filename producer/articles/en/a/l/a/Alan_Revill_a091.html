Alan Revill (March 27, 1923 â€” July 6, 1998) was an English cricketer. He was a right-handed batsman and a right-arm off-break bowler. He was born in Sheffield.

Revill's career began at Derbyshire, where he debuted during the 1946 County Championship, the first to take place after the ending of the Second World War. An upper-middle order batsman, he excelled despite a near-innings defeat by Essex, coming close to scoring a half-century on his debut. Swiftly dropped down the order in spite of these impressive performances, Revill was to find his confidence knocked back by the end of the season, and from that moment on his averages tumbled as he was not to find a steady partner. He was not always blessed by luck in his career, on one occasion he was stuck on the hand by a bouncer and, shaking off the glove in pain, was aghast to see it fall on the wicket, dislodge a bail and dismiss him 'hit wicket'.

However, the following season was a promising one for Revill, buoyed by a debut Test appearance by team-mate and regular strike partner Cliff Gladwin. The season, altogether impressive as Derbyshire finished fifth in the table, saw Revill score his first of sixteen centuries for the team, a tally which at the time, saw him slot easily into the list of the top-ten all-time scorers for Derbyshire, before the high-scoring days of Donald Carr and all-time run-leader Kim Barnett, amongst others.

A top-half finishes in 1948, which saw five half-centuries, was unfortunately cut short in 1949, despite Revill scoring a career-best 156 not out against Leicestershire, as Derbyshire were brought down to earth by the touring New Zealanders, despite Revill's best intentions and explosive batting getting his second-innings tally up to 145, the second-best score of his career.

With the turn of the new decade came a temporary upturn in fortunes, thanks to the acquisition of Arnold Hamer, John Kelly, and Derek Morgan, all of whom proved their worth with the bat, earning county caps, Morgan being county captain for five seasons. Derbyshire regularly found themselves in the upper half of the table thanks to the opening batting partnership of Revill and Charlie Elliott. Revill topped the averages in the 1953 season, while in 1954 the team found themselves achieving third place in the table, for a short time envisioning recapture of the trophy won only once in the team's history.

After a short blip lasting through to 1956, Derbyshire were once again near the top of the table in 1957, though powerless to stop a rampaging Surrey team from capturing the title, while Revill continued with steady performances including three half-centuries.

These continued good performances caught the attention of Leicestershire, who took Revill under their wing in 1958, having finished bottom of the County Championship table the previous year. While Surrey retained their title in 1958, Revill scored a century in his first season with Leicestershire, though the team once again finished in the lower reaches of the table. While the following season brought two distressing seasons for the Leicestershire team, 1960 saw Revill demote himself to the Second XI, where he played in 1960 and 1962 before leaving the club at the age of 39.

Revill died at the age of 75 in London. Revill's father, Thomas, played first-class cricket for Derbyshire between 1913 and 1920.
