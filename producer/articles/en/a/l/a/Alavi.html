The surname Alavi (often spelled as Alvi) (Arabic: علوي) signifies ancestry from Hazrat Ali ibn Abi Talib (Arabic: علي بن أﺑﻲ طالب), the first Caliph of Islam and the fourth Caliph of Sunnism. Ali was the cousin and son-in-law of Prophet Muhammad and titled Amir al-Mu'minin (Arabic: أمیر المؤمنین) by his follower (Commander of the Faithful)

The Alavi family can be found in the Middle East and South Asia. Alavis are scattered in Northern Indian provice of Uttar Pradesh with their main concentration in Kakori near Lucknow. Alavi of Kakori referred as Moulvizadigan (Moulvis) or Makhdoomzadigan (Makhdooms) indicating whether they are descendent of Mullah Abu Bakr Jami Alavi, who settled in Kakori in 1461 or descendent of Qari Amir Saifuddin Alavi, who settled in Kakori in 1552. The spelling of (Arabic: علوي) varied greatly. Although, the Alavi spelling is most prevalent in Iran and Indian sub-Continent, but Alvi, Alawi and Alwi spellings are frequently found in India and some Middle Eastern Countries. Awan is second name of this tribe, belonged to Hadrat Qutub Shah, a renowned sufi and minister of Mehmood Gaznavi, the great. Alavi Awan tribe has possessed majority in Iran, Iraq, Inda, Pakistan, Indonesia, Malysia and Egypt. (For details see book: TARIKH-E-ALVI AWAN)

Alavi was also the name of a city in India at the time of the Buddha (500 BC), and Buddha went to visit there several times.
