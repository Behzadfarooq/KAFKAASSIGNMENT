Alan Fisher

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Alan Fisher is a journalist, currently working as the London correspondent for Al Jazeera International. He was born and raised in Motherwell and studied at journalism at at Napier College in Edinburgh. He joined Moray Firth Radio in Inverness and then NorthSound Radio in Aberdeen. He moved to television, initially Grampian Television and then Scottish Television as a reporter on Scotland Today. He joined GMTV as Ireland correspondent setting up their bureau in Belfast. He was promoted to Chief Correspondendant and reported from Bosnia, Rwanda and Iraq during the first Gulf War.[1] He has also appeared in Bad Girls where he played a reporter.[2]
