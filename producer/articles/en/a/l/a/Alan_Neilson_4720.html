Alan Neilson was born on 26 September 1972 in Wegberg, Germany is a professional footballer currently with Conference South side Salisbury City, where he plays in midfield.

Former Wales International, Alan Neilson started his professional football career with Newcastle United back in the early 1990s as a trainee. He made 35 first team appearances before moving south to Southampton.

During his time with Southampton played 42 games and scored 13. Neilson played at the Saints for nearly three years before moving to Fulham where he joined his former manager Kevin Keegan at the club. He signed for Â£225,000 from Southampton.

Neilson had more than his fair share of injury problems in his first season with Fulham after a promising start to his Craven Cottage career. In October 2001, Neilson moved to Grimsby Town, but played just eight times before being released in February 2002.[1]

Later that month Neilson joined Luton Town[2], where he went on to make 42 appearances and scored 11 goals. Neilson joined Nationwide Conference side Tamworth in 2005[3], joining former Southampton team mate Richard Dryden.

Neilson left Tamworth to continue his coaching at both Luton Town and at Barnfield College. He was undecided as to whether to retire from playing or not[4], but in February 2007 signed for Salisbury City.[5]
