Alain Goma was born on October 5, 1972 in Sault, France. He is a football player currently without a club after being released by Fulham F.C. in May 2006. He was Fulham's captain under Jean Tigana.

He kick-started his football career, playing for Paris suburb team RC Versailles from 1982. After spending six years there, Alain went on to join the academy of AJ Auxerre in 1988. After much practise, he won his first team place in a league game in 1991.

In 1992, He was chosen to play for the French U21 National Team. At the end of the season 1992/93, Alain played in the semi final of the UEFA Cup with AJ Auxerre but unfortunately losing on penalties to another team. At the end of the season at the French club, he signed his very first professional contract.

With AJ Auxerre, this individual at 22 years won his first French cup in 1994. Alain then went on to win both the cup and the French league in 1996. Before his departure in 1998, he helped AJ Auxerre reach the quarter finals of the UEFA Champions League.

After ten full years at Auxerre, Alain moved to Paris Saint-Germain, where they claimed the Champions' Trophy, which is the French Charity Shield. Not many achievements could be won at the Paris-based club, so, after 30 games for Paris St Germain, 200 appearances with AJ Auxerre and 2 full International caps under his belt, Alain joined Newcastle United F.C. for GBP4.7 million.

He became an important player at the start of the 2000/2001 season but, following a dispute with the Magpies, it became apparent he would be moving on after two seasons. At 21 April 2001, he made his debut in a 1-1 draw against Portsmouth F.C., at Fratton Park and became a vital player for Fulham in their debut Premiership season.

Alain proved to be worth every penny of his transfer fee, becoming one of the first names on the teamsheet at Fulham.

His form earned him a new contract during the 2003/2004 season to keep him at the club until the summer of 2006. Despite missing two months due to injury, he made his 100th appearance that season.

In 2004/2005 his appearances became less frequent with the emergence of younger players such as Zat Knight and 2005/2006 season commences with Goma on the fringes of the first team squad.

He earned 2 caps for the France national football team, once in 1996 and once in 1998.
