Aladzha Monastery is a medieval Orthodox Christian cave monastery complex in northeastern Bulgaria, 17 km north of central Varna and 3 km west of Golden Sands beachfront resort. It is situated in a protected forest area adjacent to Zlatni Pyasatsi Nature Park. The monastery caves were hewn into a 25-m high vertical carst cliff near the upper edge of the Frangen plateau on several levels. The complex also includes two small nearby catacombs.

It was an active hesychast monastic community, dedicated to the Holy Trinity, during the Second Bulgarian Empire since the 12th century and perhaps survived until the early 18th century. Today, it is a popular tourist destination. Its present name appeared in the late Ottoman period; Alaca (Turkish: motley) referred to its colourful murals.
