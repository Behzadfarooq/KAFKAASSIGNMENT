Alan Gilbert (conductor)

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Alan Gilbert is an American conductor, born in New York in 1967, the son of two musicians in the New York Philharmonic Orchestra.[1] His father, Michael Gilbert, is retired from the New York Philharmonic, and his mother, Yoko Takabe, continues to play as a member of the orchestra. Gilbert studied the violin and viola. He has studied at Harvard University, the Curtis Institute of Music and the Juilliard School of Music. He has served as Assistant Concertmaster of the orchestra of the Santa Fe Opera. He received the Seaver/National Endowment for the Arts Conductors Award in 1997.

Gilbert became the chief conductor of the Royal Stockholm Philharmonic Orchestra in January 2000 and will relinquish that position in 2008 to Sakari Oramo. In 2003, he became the first Music Director of the Santa Fe Opera (having first conducted a production of Giuseppe Verdi's Falstaff there in 2001), but his contract concluded at the end of the 2006 season. As of November 2006, it is not certain whether Gilbert will continue in this position, although a recent feature article reports that he will be on "official sabbatical from June through August 2007" to spend more time with his family.[2], while an examination of the Santa Fe Opera's 2007 "Season Overview" page does not list him as conducting any of the productions.

Gilbert has guest-conducted with many American orchestras, including the New York Philharmonic, Chicago Symphony Orchestra, Saint Louis Symphony Orchestra, Atlanta Symphony Orchestra, Boston Symphony Orchestra, San Francisco Symphony Orchestra, Los Angeles Philharmonic Orchestra and the Cleveland Orchestra.
