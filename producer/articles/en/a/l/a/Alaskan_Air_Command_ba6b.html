[edit] Mission

The mission of the Alaskan Air Command (AAC) was to provide early warning of an aerial attack on the United States and Canada. The AAC did this through conducting air defense of Alaska, supporting SAC elements operating through and around Alaska, and performing other operational support missions as directed by the Commander-in-Chief, Alaskan Command HQ and Headquarters USAF.

[edit] History[1]

The Alaskan Air Command (AAC) was activated on 21 Dec 1945. It was composed of elements of the wartime 11th Air Force. AAC was headquartered at Davis AFB on the island of Adak, with the Command divided into two air defense sectors - Aleutian and Yukon. The defense of the Yukon sector was directed from Ladd AFB.

Command Headquarters soon were moved to Elmendorf AFB. During the summer of 1947 the Aleutian sector was deactivated, and defense operations were centered at Elmendorf and Ladd AFBs.
