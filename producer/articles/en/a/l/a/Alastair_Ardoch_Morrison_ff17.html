A. A. Murdoch (born Melbourne 21 September 1911 - died 15 March, 1998) was an Australian graphic artist and author who, inter alia, in the 1960s documented Strine in a series of books which were published under the pseudonyms of Afferbeck Lauder and Alter Ego. Morrison also illustrated the books.

He was the brother of the journalist and playwright, Guy Morrison.

In the early 1960s he was appointed chairman of the Currency Note Design Group, an advisory committee which assisted with the design of new banknotes which were issued when Australia adopted decimal currency on the 14th February 1966.
