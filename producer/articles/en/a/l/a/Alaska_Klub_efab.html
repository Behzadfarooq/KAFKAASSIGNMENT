The Alaska Klub was established in 2004 and is the official undergraduate organization for Alaska at Harvard University. It is best known for hosting the annual Human Iditarod sled dog race and the Annual Cambridge Polar Bear Swim. It maintains an open membership of Alaskans and Alaska-lovers alike and charges no dues nor requires a competence process.

[edit] Token Events

[edit] Human Iditarod

Founded in 2005, the Human Iditarod is an athletic challenge for the homo sapien modeled after the Iditarod Trail Sled Dog Race hosted in Alaska. Teams of five 'dogs' and one 'racer' are given one week to construct a sled out of given and found materials and then must race in a series of challenges culminating in an all teams race to the finish.

[edit] Polar Bear Swim

The annual Cambridge Polar Bear Swim, first held in 2005, is conducted conicident with the Alaska Polar Bear Festival held annually in Seward. AK members, non-members, and Cambridge community members meet at the crack of dawn fueled by doughnuts and hot cocoa, sing the Alaska flag song and barrel into the frigid waters of the Charles.

[edit] Traditions

[edit] Primal Scream Pace Run

On the night before finals each semester at midnight, Harvard University undergraduates have a tradition to perform a nude run around Harvard Yard. Since 2004 the Alaska Klub has lead a pace lap where its members lock arms with the word "A-L-A-S-K-A-!" written across their chests flying Alaska flag capes to kick off the run.

[edit] History

The AK (as it is colloquially known) was founded in 2004 by Lief Fenno '07 and Corey Rennell '07-'08. Previous to receiving official college sponsorship, an informal club met lead by Caitlin Stern '05.

[edit] Leadership

Alaska Klub officers are elected at the end of each school year. All members have the right to an office of responsibility. Offices of President, Vice President, Secretary, and Tresurer are permanent while other offices are created or disappear on a one year basis depending on Klub needs.
