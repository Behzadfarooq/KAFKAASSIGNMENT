The Alaska Wild is a professional indoor football team in the Intense Football League scheduled to begin play in the 2007 season. The team will play its home games at Sullivan Arena in Anchorage, Alaska.

[edit] Team history

The Alaska Wild was organized May 6, 2006 to become Alaska's first professional football team. The name "Alaska Wild" was chosen from over 1,500 names submitted online on the team's website. They were originally intended to join the af2, yet on October 3, 2006, it was announced that due to financial concerns, the team would suspend operations until the 2008 season. However, the team has withdrawn that announcement and will play in 2007 in the Intense Football League.

On February 20, 2007, the Alaska Wild held tryouts for a $65 registration fee and subsequently, March 4, 2007, they signed defensive back Devin "4.25 forty" Miles to a one-year 45,000 dollar contract[1].
