Alan Jeffery Anderson (born on October 16, 1982, in Minneapolis, Minnesota) is an American professional basketball player for the NBA's Charlotte Bobcats. Anderson re-signed with the Bobcats[1] for whom he played during the previous season. He was waived by the team in November 2006.[2] He plays the shooting guard/small forward positions and attended Michigan State University. Prior to Anderson's 2007 re-signing, he had been playing with the NBA D-League's Tulsa 66ers, averaging 15.8 points per game.[1]

Anderson participated in the 2000 USA Basketball Youth Development Festival, leading the North squad to a gold medal.

Anderson was raised with his brother Tylan Ward by Dorisene Anderson a single mother since the age of 16.[citation needed]

Anderson featured in a commerical his sophomore year in High School. AMCE a company in New York promoting minority students to become more interested in math and science.

An extremely versatile player, he has the ability to play four different positions ... One of MSU's best ballhandlers, he was the primary point guard his junior season ... Ranks eighth in MSU history in career free-throw percentage at 81.3 (265-of-326).

[edit] College

Anderson as a senior averaged 13.2 points, 5.5 rebounds and 1.6 assists. All-Big Team honors. He also helped lead the Spartans to the 2005 Final Four. Voted MVP by the team and media as a senior. He earned his four-year degree from Michigan State University on May 7, 2005 in Community Family Services.
