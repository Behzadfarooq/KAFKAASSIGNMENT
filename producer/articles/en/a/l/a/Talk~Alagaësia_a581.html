[edit] Added.

I added a section called Similarities with Middle-Earth. As this debate of similarity is so fierce in other sites, I did think that this debates and many different views deserve a section too.

Oh, yes. I normally do Korean Wiki, and English is my second language, so grammar or spelling mistakes are bound to happen. It would be grateful if anyone would go through some of those possible mistakes. (And I see someone already has, too.)

I find the whole story structure very similar to Star Wars. In a fantasy world. Maybe Star Wars could be included in the Conceptual similarities section?
Think about it.
  • A young guy, abandoned by his parents, living in the middle of nowhere is about to become The Knight.
  • He starts his training and his trainer dies (Brom=Obi-wan).
  • He finds a fighting mate to help him go on and find the Rebels (Murtagh=Han Solo)
  • He falls in the middle of an Empire vs Rebels war, on the rebels' side.
  • The King wants him on his side to empower his plans (Glabatorix=Emperor Palpatine)
  • He starts a looong training on a far, far place, trained by an ooooold trainer (Oromis=Yoda)
  • He discovers to be the son of the villain (this time Morzan=Darth Vader).
  • Magic-energy resembles The Force.
  • Last but not least... Saphira=X-Wing!! (joking on this one! :-D)
Isn't it worth to mention?
More: I find unfair for good-old-Christopher to list all the similarities and not the original ideas. They're original to me... I haven't seen such ideas before.
  • The explanation of how magic works
  • The introduction of Dragons and their ability to choose when to be born
  • The link between Dragon and Raider
  • The idea that elves were not immortal from the beginning of time but just because of their alliance with Dragons
  • The transformation of Eragon... he almost changed his race!
--Aldo Ercolani 00:11 25/11/2006 (GMT+1)

[edit] Pronunciation

Alagaësia uses a non-English accent, ë. Does anyone know how to prononounce 'Alagaësia'? If we could figure out how, it would be nice to add that to this article. --SonicChao talk 00:26, 30 December 2006 (UTC)

It says in the back of the book...ɑlʌɡeɪziːɑ looks good to me, I'm going to add it, but if it's wrong, please change it. --SonicChao talk 23:57, 2 January 2007 (UTC)

Does the "back of the book" say what syllable is stressed? By the lengthening of the /i/, I would guess it's supposed to be [ˌɑ·lʌ·ɡeɪ·'ziː·ɑ], but that's just an "educated guess" based on normal English stress patterns, which would actually prefer that the inititial syllable be [a] and final syllable be [ʌ], ratherˌ than [ɑ]... Without the benefit of this IPA provided in the back of the book, I would have thought perhaps [ˌɑ·lʌ·'ɡeɪ·zjɑ] or [ˌɑ·lʌ·'ɡeɪ·ʒɑ] would have been more "natural" pronunciations for the name. Just my 2¢. Well, actually, probably, with the diaeresis on the ë, I'd have been more likely to guess [ˌɑ·lʌ·ɡa·'eɪ·zjɑ], but I digress... It appears the pronunciation is just as random as the decision to use "ë" instead of just "e"... Tomertalk 06:59, 8 January 2007 (UTC)
You can change it if you want. I am not an expert in the IPA. I took a guess based on 'al-uh-GAY-zee-uh', and how I would pronounce that. --SonicChao talk 20:55, 9 January 2007 (UTC)
