Alan Gorrie (born July 19, 1946 in Perth, Scotland) is a Scottish bassist and founding member of the Average White Band.

Gorrie and others formed the Average White Band in Glasgow in 1971. They became a successful funk/R&B group, topping charts internationally with the AWB album and Pick Up the Pieces single.

In addition to his credits with AWB, he can also be credited with collaborating with, among others, Daryl Hall on his "Soul Alone" album and tours and his "Can't Stop Dreaming" album. Gorrie resides in the United States and continues to have a home in Scotland as well.

Some of his interests/hobbies include art, sailing, eating, cooking and drinking, (see Paulin) especially single malt whisky.
