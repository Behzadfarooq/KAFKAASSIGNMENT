Alastair Watson

Major Alastair Watson, MA the Black Watch (retired), has been Private Secretary to His Royal Highness The Duke of York since July 2003.

He was born in February 1953 in Wuppertal, West Germany, educated at Eagle House, Wellington College and New College, Oxford, before he joined the Black Watch (the family regiment in which his father (Major General AL Watson CB) and brother (Patrick Watson) have served), in 1975.

Service included: training at Sandhurst: three operational tours in Ulster; an operational tour in Belize; Ops Officer of a mechanised infantry battalion in the British Army of the Rhine; two year course at RMCS Shrivenham and the Staff College, Camberley; SO2 in MO3 in the Ministry Of Defence; a tour in Berlin as a Company Commander (just prior to the Berlin Wall coming down) and an operational requirements/tactical doctrine role at UK's Headquarters Land Forces.

On retiring from the British Army in early 1991, he spent 11 years with Fired Earth, including a six year stint as Sales Director, and was in the management team during the sale of the Plc to PPMV (1999) and on to Aga Foodservices plc (2001), before joining the Royal Household.
