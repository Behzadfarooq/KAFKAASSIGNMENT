Alan Goodall

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Alan Goodall (born December 2, 1981 in Birkenhead) is a professional football full back, who currently plays for Rochdale.

[edit] Welsh Premier Career

Alan joined League of Wales club Bangor City in the summer of 2001.

In his first season at the Farrar Road club, he played in the Welsh Cup final picking up a runners-up medal in the defeat against Barry Town at Park Avenue, Aberystwyth.

He was part of the team that lost narrowly in the 2002-03 UEFA Cup against FK Sartid, who were knocked out by U.E.F.A. Fair Play winners Ipswich Town in the following round and 2003-04 Intertoto Cup losing to Gloria Bistriţa.

In 91 League of Wales appearances for City, Alan scored ten goals. Towards the end of the 2003-04 season, he spent time on trial at Wrexham A.F.C., before joining Rochdale in the summer of 2004.

[edit] Professional Career

At the end of the 2005-06 season, Alan's second at Spotland, he had made 74 League Two appearances scoring five times.
