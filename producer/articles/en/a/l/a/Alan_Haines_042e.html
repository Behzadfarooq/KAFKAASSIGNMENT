Alan Haines

Alan Haines (born 6th June 1924) is a British actor and playwright who spent 4 years in the Royal Navy during World War II - including at D-Day on his 20th birthday [1] and appeared in many West End shows and touring productions, as well as in the cult TV series Dad's Army and Van der Valk[2] and three notable films: Perfect Strangers, Dad's Army and The Man in the White Suit[3]

[edit] Works

  • The Prince of Portobello Play (1962)[4]
  • Autobiography Haines (2006) The Mad Mad-Century Rag London: MER Publishing (2006)
