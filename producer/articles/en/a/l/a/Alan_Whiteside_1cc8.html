Alan Walter Whiteside (born March 18, 1956) is an AIDS researcher and author. He was born in Kenya and grew up in Swaziland where he attended Waterford_Kamhlaba[1]United World College. In 1980, he received a MA in Development Economics at the University of East Anglia. At UKZN, he is Professor of Economics and director of the Health Economics and HIV/AIDS Research Division: [2] . He is a member of the Governing Council of the International_AIDS_Society and a Governor at Waterford Kamhlaba. from 2003 to 2006 he served on the UN Commission on HIV/AIDS and Governance in Africa.His energies are also put into working with HEARD, a group of 35 researchers, research assistants and administrative staff who focus on the health issues and the HIV/AIDS pandemic. He has co-written many books on AIDS, including AIDS in the Twenty-First Century. He currently divides his time between Norwich, UK, with his partner Ailsa, and his children, Douglas and Rowan, his office in Durban and various airline lounges.

[edit] List of his recent books

  • AIDS in the Twenty-First Century: Disease and Globalisation (co-authored with Tony Barnett, Palgrave, second edition 2006)
  • The HIV Pandemic Local and global implications, (co-edited with Eduard J Beck, Nicholas Mays, and Jose M Zuniga,Oxford University Press, 2006, Oxford 840 pages
  • Poverty, AIDS and Hunger' (preface by Bono) (co-edited with Anne Conry, Malcolm Blackie, Justin Malewezi and Jeffrey Sachs, Palgrave 2006)
  • The Political Economy of AIDS in Africa (co-edited with Poku Nana K, Ashgate 2005)
  • Global Health and Governence:HIV/AIDS (co-edited with Poku Nana K, Palgrave 2005)
  • AIDS. The Challenge for South Africa (with Clem Sunter)
  • Implications of AIDS for Demography and Policy in Southern Africa
