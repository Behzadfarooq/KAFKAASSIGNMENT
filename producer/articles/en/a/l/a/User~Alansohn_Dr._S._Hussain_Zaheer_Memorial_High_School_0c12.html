IICT - Dr. S. Hussain Zaheer Memorial High School was established in 1979 and was named for scientist Dr. Syed Hussain Zaheer. It is located in the heart of the city of Hyderabad, in the Indian state of Andhra Pradesh. The school operates under the auspices of the Indian Institute of Chemical Technology, which allows students the opportunity to interact with the scientists of the IICT. Mrs. Kaiser Sayeed is the school's Principal

The school's connection with the Indian Institute of Chemical Technology allows students to learn directly from the top scientists in India and around the world. The IICT has developed a science fair at the school called "Ignited Minds" in conjunction with National Science Day in India. Zaheer Memorial School will also be the site of the Dr A. V. Rama Rao Science Musuem. The school has had the privilege of having laureates of the Nobel Prize in Chemistry, Robert H. Grubbs (who won the Nobel Prize in 2005), K. Barry Sharpless (2001) and Koichi Tanaka (2002) speak to students as part of the school's relationship with the IICT.[1]

[edit] Management and facilities

The school is owned and operated by the Indian Institute of Chemical Technology (IICT), an internationally-renowned research institute, and managed by the Science Society. IICT provides all infrastructural support for the school.

The school includes a well-furnished Library and a well-equipped science laboratories, a modern computer laboratory and the opportunity to interact with world-famous Scientists.

[edit] History

The school opened on June 25, 1979, with 129 students in Kindergarten and Class 1 and has grown to 2,600 students. The school prided itself in offering quality education for low prices in the early 1980's and 1990's. It has large play grounds and enjoys a park like setting.

[edit] Awards and recognition

The school has flourished with Laurels and attained state level position at SSC Public Examination in 1995 in Andhra Pradesh. Students at Dr. S. Hussain Zaheer Memorial High School have achieved several prizes at District, state and National level in Science Fairs, various competitions, athletics & sports activities including cricket, basketball, earning several trophies and cash awards. Many students obtained merit position in VII and X and scholarships from the Education Department of the Government of Andhra Pradesh.

[edit] Extracurricular activities

The school offers playgrounds for Cricket, Basketball, Volleyball, Table Tennis, Shuttle and Badminton.

On July 18, 2004, Dr. Zaheer Memorial High School became the champion of the inter-school hockey tournament, defeating Moulana Azad High School 3-0 in the tournament final at Gymkhana Grounds to earn the first annual V. Purushotham memorial trophy.[2]. In the 2006 tournament, Zaheer Memorial, lost in the tourney quarterfinals, falling to Vijaya High School (Kapra) by a score of 2-1.[3]
