[edit] Religion

Republic of Abkhazia is a secular country where the adherents of all religions (as well as atheists) have equal rights before the law according to the Constitution of Abkhazia.[1]. The prevalent religion is Orthodox Christianity and Islam also has a large number of followers. However most of the people who declare themselves Christian or Muslim do not attend religious services. There is also a very small number of Judaists, Jehovah's Witnesses and the followers of new religions.[2]

[edit] History

[edit] Ancient history IV-XVI cc

The earliest accounts of the introduction of Christianity into Abkhazia date from the 1st century, when it was first preached by the Apostles of Jesus, St. Andrew and St. Simon the Canaanite.[3] In 325 Stratophilus, the bishop of Pityus participated in the First Œcumenical Council in Nicaea.[4] Justinian the Great built a temple in Pityus in 551 and appointed the bishop and several priests. Since the second half of the VI c. the church of Abkhazia (which consisted of the bishoprics of Sebastopolis, Phasis and Nicopsia became autocefalous and its head, the bishop of Pityus received the title of catholicos. The catholicos of Abkhazia participated in the Second Œcumenical Council in Nicaea.

The first catholicos of Abkhazia whose name is known is Eudemon I who built the Gelati Monastery together with David the Builder and is depicted there. Shortly the union of the kingdoms of Georgia and Abkhazia the catholicoses were no longer elected (?).

After the break-up of Georgian kingdom that followed the Mongol invasion Abkhazia became independent princedom and the bishop of Pitsunda (Pityus) was again elected by the local clergy.

[edit] Football

Football was the sport number one during the Soviet times. The main club of the republic - Dinamo Sukhumi played mostly in the lower leagues of Soviet football however Abkhazia produced numerous football talents who played in the top Georgian team Dinamo Tbilisi and in other Soviet teams. Natives of Abkhazia Nikita (Mkrtych) Simonian, Avtandil Gogoberidze, Niyazbey Dzyapshipa, Georgi Gavasheli, Temuri Ketsbaia were among the most prominent footballers of Soviet Union.

Dinamo Sukhumi achieved the best result in 1991 finishing on the 10th place in the first league (second tier of Soviet football).1st league of 1991 Soviet Championship It was the only Georgian team to participate in the Soviet football system in 1990-1991 as other Georgian teams left it for political reasons before the 1990 season.[1] Several players of the 1990-1991 Dinamo Sukhumi squad including Sergei Ovchinnikov and Adzhindzhal brothers played later for Russian teams and achieved various degrees of success, as did Oleg Dolmatov, Dinamo's coach in 1990-1991.

After Abkhazia became de-facto independent it organised its own amateur Abkhazian Football league in 1994 which is not part of any international football union. The league consists of 9 teams: Nart (Sukhumi), Gagra, Kiaraz (Pitsunda), Samurzakan (Gali), Afon (New Athos, Ertsakhu (Ochamchira), Kudry (Gulripsh) and Abazg-AGU (Sukhumi).[2] The Gagra football club was the 2006 champion.[3] The Abkhazian team will probably participate in the Amateur League of the Southern Federal District (fourth tier of Russian football pyramid).[4]

[edit] Other sports

The majority of the Abkhazia's population have Russian citizenship so Abkhazian sportsmen participate in the international competitions as Russian citizens. They had the biggest successes in boxing (2005 European Champion David Arshba [5]; 2006 Russian Championship prise-winner Aslan Akhba) and freestyle wrestling (2006 American Airlines Freestyle Wrestling Tournament winner Denis Tsargush [6]).

"Sukhum" basketball club plays in the Southern Division of the First League (fourth tier of the Russian basketball system).
