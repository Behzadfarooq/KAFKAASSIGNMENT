[edit] Accuracy

I put this section in:

The accuracy of some of Caruba's articles has been called into question, and he is regarded by some to be a sloppy journalist. A prime example is a January 2003 article he authored about mercury [7]. A partial list of the article's many errors and misleading statements includes: He writes that "The US is responsible...for 3.6 percent of the worldâ€™s total [mercury emissions]...That means 97.4 percent comes from someplace else." But 3.6 and 97.4 add up to 101. He says that Decemeber 6, 2002 was a Monday when in fact it was a Friday. He mistakenly writes that Jillian Waldman works for the "Public Information Research Group." He means Public Interest Research Group (PIRG). He claims that "there is NO evidence that mercury emissions have killed a single person on the face of the earth." In reality, hundreds were killed by Mercury emissions into the waters around Minamata Japan. He further claims that "For the record, there was one instance of mercury poisoning" ignoring thousands of documented lethal and non-lethal mercury poisonings. Later in the article he calls the FDA's recommendations on fish consumption "artibitrary" and "hocus pocus," implying they have no scientific basis. In fact the agency's reasoning was outlined on its website, and was "to limit consumer's methyl mercury exposure to levels ten times lower than the lowest level at which adverse effects where found to occur in adults."[8]"

Caruba (warning signs) took it out. He obviously doesn't like it, and I can see why he wouldn't. But I think it belongs, though I admit that I'm rather new here.
