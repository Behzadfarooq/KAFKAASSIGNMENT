Ala kachuu

Ala kachuu is a practice of bride stealing still practiced in Kyrgyzstan. In what extent it actually happens is controversial.

"Kyz ala kachuu" means "to take a young woman and run away." Young man effectively captures a woman either by force or by guile, often accompanied with friends or male relatives. They forcefully take her to his family home. There the woman is locked into a room until the man's relatives convince her to put on a scarf of a married woman as a sign of acceptance. Sometimes, if the woman successfully resists the persuasion and returns home, her relatives try to convince her to agree to the marriage.

The practice was allegedly suppressed during the Soviet period but after collapse of Soviet Union, practice of Ala Kachuu began to (re)surface. There are conflicting reports of whether it continues in the original way or not. Some sources state that the practice was originally a form of elopement, not a bride theft.

Sometimes the kidnapping may be just a wedding formality, where the woman comes along willingly, but recurrence of this is unclear.
