Alain Johannes

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Alain Johannes is a musician, and along with his partner Natasha Shneider and drummer Jack Irons (who played for Red Hot Chili Peppers, What Is This?, and Pearl Jam), is a founding member of Eleven.

In 1987 Alain Johannes with Natasha Shneider released an album as 'Walk the Moon' under the MCA label.

During his time with Eleven, they released their debut album Awake in a Dream in 1991 which included the pop based singles "Rainbows End" and "All Together". They followed this with regular releases Eleven in 1993, Thunk! in 1995, Avantgardedog in 2000, and Howling Book in 2003.

Johannes is endorsed by Line 6 Amplifiers, and is listed as a user of the Flextone series of amps.

Johannes was also a significant contributor to Chris Cornell's 1999 solo album, Euphoria Morning, and was the only other contributor on the 1996 debut album by Jason Falkner, Jason Falkner Presents Author Unknown.

In 2005, he joined Queens of the Stone Age to play guitar as well as bass for the recording of Queens of the Stone Age's 2005 effort, Lullabies to Paralyze. He had previously guested on Lullabies' predecessor, Songs for the Deaf, co-writing the track "Hanging Tree".

Johannes is currently a member of Brody Dalle's post-Distillers effort, Spinnerette.[1]
