State Route 25, a state highway in Alabama, is a long yet relatively insignificant state route. From Leeds to the Georgia state line, S.R. 25 is the unsigned partner route of U.S. Route 411. The southern terminus of S.R. 25 is at the junction with State Route 5 near Pine Hill in Wilcox County, and the northern terminus is at the Georgia state line in Cherokee County, approximately 20 miles east of Centre.

[edit] Route Description

For the first 50 miles of its route, S.R. 25 travels in a north/south direction. The route initially travels through rural areas of west central Alabama, connecting numerous small towns in the Black Belt region of the state. From Greensboro to its terminus, the orientation of S.R. 25 is in a general northeasterly/southwesterly direction. As the route begins its northeastward trajectory, it continues passing through rural areas and small towns, skirting the southern area of the Birmingham metropolitan area in Shelby County.

[edit] Junctions with Interstate highways, U.S. Routes and Major State Highways

  • U.S. Highway 80 and unsigned State Route 8 at Faunsdale
  • State Route 14 and State Route 69 at Greensboro
  • State Route 5 at Harrisburg in southwestern Bibb County. The two routes overlap until they split at Brent
  • U.S. Highway 82 and unsigned State Route 6 at Centreville
  • U.S. Highway 31 and unsigned State Route 3 at Calera
  • Interstate 65 at Calera
  • State Route 70 at Columbiana
  • State Route 76 north of Wilsonville
  • U.S. Highway 231 and U.S. Highway 280 and their unsigned partner routes, State Route 53 and State Route 38 at Harpersville. S.R. 25 overlaps U.S. 231 and S.R. 53 until they split at Vincent
  • U.S. Highway 78 and unsigned State Route 4 east of Leeds. These routes overlap until they junction U.S. Highway 411 in Leeds. North of Leeds, S.R. 25 ceases being a marked route, and becomes the unsigned partner route of U.S. 411.
