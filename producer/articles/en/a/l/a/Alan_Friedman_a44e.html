Alan Friedman was born in New York City on April 30th, 1956. He was educated at NYU (B.A. Politics and History), the London School of Economics (International Relations) and the Johns Hopkins School of Advanced International Studies (M.A. International Economics and Law).

Alan Friedman is a media entrepreneur, former award-winning journalist with the Financial Times of London, the International Herald Tribune and The Wall Street Journal, best-selling author (Agnelli, Spider’s Web), television talk show host and a former contributing editor of Vanity Fair.

Seen as one of Europe’s most respected economic and political commentators, economic and global political journalist, author and also a well-known TV personality.


[edit] Journalistic Career

Former anchor of the Alan Friedman Show, a peak talk show on the Sky TG24 news channel in Italy, as other programmes he had anchored, it was dedicated to national and international economics and politics. From 1994 to 2003 Mr. Friedman was Global Economics Correspondent of the IHT. From 2003 to 2005 he was global economy columnist for the Wall Street Journal Europe and Executive Director of World Business, a CNBC Europe peak programme.

From 1979 to 1993 he worked for the Financial Times of London, as a columnist on bonds, a banking correspondent and a foreign correspondent in Italy and the United States. His professional relationship with the International Herald Tribune and RAI made way, in 1999, to a joint venture for the co-production of the weekly programmes “World Business / Pianeta Economia” (Planet Economy), produced by FBC.

Alan Friedman was awarded the British Press Award (equivalent of the Pulitzer award) four times during his 14-year career with the Financial Times. He was recognized as the journalist who broke the Iraq-Gate scandal in 1991 that saw the CIA involved in the sale of weapons to Saddam Hussein. Before collaborating with the Financial Times, Alan Friedman had worked in the administration of President Jimmy Carter in the role of Presidential Management Intern. He began his professional career working for the staff of Hon. Bella Abzug (Dem.NY) and of Hon. Donald Fraser (Dem-Minn.).

Amongst his publications stand out the bestsellers AGNELLI and the Network of Italian Power, (Harrap, New American Library, Longanesi and Planeta, 1988) and Spider’s Web: The Secret History of How the White House Illegally Armed Iraq (Bantam, Longanesi, Faber and Faber, 1993). His television career began at the beginning of the ‘80s with regular appearances on the BBC “Newsnight” broadcast. In 1991 he participated, with Ted Koppel of the ABC “Nightline” programme, in the production of a series of broadcasts by ABC / Financial Times on the scandal of the weapons sale to Iraq. Alan Friedman has been the only American journalist to have received the Medal of Honour (1997) from the Italian Parliament and he has been awarded many other acknowledgements for his work on global economy.
