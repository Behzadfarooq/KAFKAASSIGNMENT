Alaba

Alaba is one of the 77 woredas in the Southern Nations, Nationalities and Peoples' Region of Ethiopia. This woreda is named after the Alaba people, and covers part of their homeland. While in their latest population estimates the Central Statistical Agency (CSA) includes it one of the woredas of the Kembata Alaba and Tembaro Zone, the list of second administrative level bodies maintained by the United Nations Geographic Information Working Group categorizes Alaba as a "special woreda", that is a district that is not part of any Zone.[1]

Located in the Great Rift Valley, Alaba is bordered on the south by an exclave of the Hadiya Zone, on the southwest by Kedida Gamela, on the west and north by the Hadiya Zone, and on the east by the Oromia Region; the Bilate River defines its western boundary. Towns in Alaba include Alaba Kulito and Alem Gebeya.

Based on figures published by the CSA in 2005, this woreda has an estimated total population of 270,210, of whom 135,593 were males and 134,617 were females; 30,297 or 11.21% of its population are urban dwellers, which is greater than the Zone average of 8.8%. With an estimated area of 973.76 square kilometers, Alaba has an estimated population density of 277.5 people per square kilometer, which is less than the Zone average of 429.40.[2]
