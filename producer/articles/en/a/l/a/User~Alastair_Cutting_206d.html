[edit] Brief biog

I lived in Jammalamadugu and Ootacamund in South India as a child of medical missionary parents; and on returning to Britain I went to Watford Grammar School for Boys, and then did a teaching & youth work degree (B.Ed.) through the Birmingham University's Westhill College.

After theological college at St John's College, in Nottingham, I was ordained deacon in 1987, and then priest in the Church of England in 1988 in Sheffield Cathedral, to minister in the parish of All Saints, Woodlands, South Yorkshire.

After a second curacy at Wadsley Parish Church, in 1991 I moved to run the church-based arts centre The Nave, in St. Margaret's Uxbridge. Then in 1996 I moved to become vicar of St John the Evangelist Copthorne, near Crawley in West Sussex, close to Gatwick Airport. In 2003 I completed an Masters degree (M.A.) in Christian Spirituality from London University's Heythrop College, with an emphasis on Celtic and Maori Christian spirituality. I was elected to the General Synod of the Church of England in 2005.

I have been married to Kay, a Kiwi-born Brit, since 1984: and we have two daughters. As a family we are great fans of New Zealand.
