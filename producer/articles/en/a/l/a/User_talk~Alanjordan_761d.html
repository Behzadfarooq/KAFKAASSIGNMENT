[edit] Welcome to Wikipedia!

Dear Alanjordan: Welcome to Wikipedia, a free and open-content encyclopedia. I hope you enjoy contributing. To help get you settled in, I thought you might find the following pages useful:

  • Five Pillars of Wikipedia
  • Community Portal
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • How to edit a page
  • How to revert to a previous version of a page
  • Tutorial

Don't worry too much about being perfect. Very few of us are! Just in case you are not perfect, click here to see how you can avoid making common mistakes.

If you are stuck, and looking for help, please come to the Wikipedia Boot Camp, where experienced Wikipedians can answer any queries you have! Or, you can just type {{helpme}} on your user page, and someone will show up shortly to answer your questions.

Wikipedians try to follow a strict policy of never biting new users. If you are unsure of how to do something, you are welcome to ask a more experienced user such as an administrator. One last bit of advice: please sign any dicussion comment with four tildes (~~~~). The software will automatically convert this into your signature which can be altered in the "Preferences" tab at the top of the screen. I hope I have not overwhelmed you with information. If you need any help just let me know. Once again welcome to Wikipedia, and don't forget to tell us about yourself and be BOLD!

[edit] Your first article

Wouldn't you know - there was a pre-existing article, Small office/home office, which is about the same concept as your first article, Homesourcing. I've merge tagged both articles. As an author this should be a piece of cake for you. Eh?  ;-) --hydnjo talk 13:11, 12 March 2006 (UTC)

Also, since you're kind of new here (I think) you should read Wikipedia:Merging and moving pages for some tips about how to and Wikipedia:Disambiguation for tips about leaving footprints in case someone looking for Home-office (your subject matter) ends up as I did today at Home Office (not your subject matter). [edit] Response

Homesourcing does have applications for small businesses. That is because a person can generate income via homesourcing. However, this article that I've written isn't really geared toward homesourcing. It is likely to be of more interest to companies with 10 ore more employees, and has major interest for people associated with large companies.

I would be more comfortable writing a second article for the SOHO merge. I'll make that change within a day. Please take a look at it and give me your impressions. Alan Jordan 15:43, 12 March 2006 (UTC)

With the edits in both articles and the inter-referencing I think the distinctions between the two articles are more obvious. Feel free to edit the Small office/home office as needed to cover SOHO but I don't think much more is needed, I'll leave that up to you. Please feel free to contact me on my talk page if you think I can help with anything. hydnjo talk 16:35, 12 March 2006 (UTC)
