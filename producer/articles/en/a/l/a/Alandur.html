Alandur is a city and a municipality in Kancheepuram district in the state of Tamil Nadu, India. It is a southern suburb of Chennai, a city situated on the east coast of the southern India. It is a thickly populated suburb flanking the railway line, close to the St. Thomas Mount Railway Station, Chennai. Alandur also neighbours the St. Thomas mount cantontment and the very prestigious Officers training academy of the Indian army. Alandur has a mixed population with a notable number of Anglo-Indians.

[edit] Geography

Alandur is located at 13.03° N 80.21° E[2]. It has an average elevation of 12 metres (39 feet).

[edit] Demographics

As of 2001 India censusGRIndia, Alandur had a population of 146,154. Males constitute 51% of the population and females 49%. Alandur has an average literacy rate of 84%, higher than the national average of 59.5%; with 53% of the males and 47% of females literate. 10% of the population is under 6 years of age.
