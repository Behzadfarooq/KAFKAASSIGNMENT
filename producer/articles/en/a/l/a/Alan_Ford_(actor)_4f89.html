Alan Ford is an English actor. He was born on March 14, 1945 in London, and trained at East 15 Acting School.

[edit] Career

He is perhaps best known today for his roles in the Guy Ritchie crime capers Lock, Stock and Two Smoking Barrels and Snatch and guest starring in The Bill. He also appeared in Exorcist: The Beginning and, much earlier, as 'Roosta' in the original radio version of The Hitchhiker's Guide to the Galaxy.

Ford also had a brief role in the film, An American Werewolf in London, he plays the taxi driver who says the line; "It puts you in mind of the days of the old demon barber of Fleet Street, doesn't it?", in response to the recent wave of murders around London.

He has also proved himself a superb comic actor, albeit one playing the familiar 'East End Thug' role, in the Armando Iannucci productions Knowing Me, Knowing You... with Alan Partridge and The Armando Iannucci Shows. Despite the stereotyping of his character, his timing and presence within these shows were simultaneously hilarious and terrifying. He has also portrayed a similar role in radio adverts for Virgin Media.

He is famed for his very East London cockney accent which adds to his 'mobster' roles.
