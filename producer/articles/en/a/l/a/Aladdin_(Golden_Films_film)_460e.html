Originally released in 1992, Aladdin is a 49-minute animated film based on the classic Arabian Nights tale, Aladdin.

Aladdin lives humbly but comfortably with his mother in their small home in the city of Baghdad until one day he receives a visit that will change his life and the life of his mother. Haseem arrives, impersonating Aladdin's uncle and asks of the boy to walk down into an underground cave where he is to find a magic oil lamp. Aladdin, once down, refuses to climb back up and enraged, Haseem locks him inside the cave for all eternity. Soon, Aladdin discovers the secret of the magic lamp, a genie who will help him escape from the cave, bring wealth and confort to his mother and marry the beautiful princess Leila.

Aladdin was produced by Golden Films and the American Film Investment Corporation, it was distributed to DVD in 2002 by GoodTimes Entertainment.
