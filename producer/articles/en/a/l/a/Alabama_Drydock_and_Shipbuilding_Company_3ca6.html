Alabama Drydock and Shipbuilding Company

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

The Alabama Drydock and Shipbuilding Company (ADDSCO) of Mobile, Alabama, was one of the largest marine production facilities in the United States of America during the Twentieth Century. During and after World War II, ADDSCO became the largest employer in southern Alabama, raising Liberty ships, tankers and other vessels from the keels up, often at breathtaking speeds during the stateside war effort.

Thousands of Alabamans, including a young man named Hank Williams, made their way to the great industrial center of Mobile to find work, and ADDSCO hired thousands on, regardless of their backgrounds. However, racial equality was an ongoing problem there following the war, addressed repeatedly by both management and union actions leading up to the breakthroughs of the Civil Rights Movement of the 1960s.
