Alan Hinkes OBE (April 26, 1954- ) is a British mountaineer from Northallerton in North Yorkshire. He is the first and only British mountaineer to have summited all 14 mountains with elevations greater than 8000 metres, the so-called Eight-thousanders. He summited the last one, Kangchenjunga, on May 30, 2005.

He was awarded a Honorary Fellowship of the University of Sunderland in 1999.

[edit] List of major climbs

  • 1987 - Shishapangma
  • 1988 - Manaslu
  • 1990 - Cho Oyu (disputed [1])
  • 1991 - Broad Peak
  • 1995 - K2
  • 1996 - Mount Everest - North Face. Filmed for the TV documentary Summit Fever.
  • 1996 - Gasherbrum I
  • 1996 - Gasherbrum II
  • 1997 - Lhotse
  • 1997 - Nanga Parbat - Forced to abort attempt after sneezing resulted in a prolapsed disc in his back.
  • 1998 - Nanga Parbat
  • 1999 - Makalu
  • 2000 - Kangchenjunga - Attempted, but turned back because of bad weather conditions. Broke arm on descent.
  • 2002 - Annapurna
  • 2004 - Dhaulagiri
  • 2005 - Kangchenjunga
