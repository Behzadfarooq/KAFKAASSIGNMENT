Alagir

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Map of North Ossetia, Russia

Alagir (Ossetian and Russian: Алаги́р) is an industrial town in the Republic of North Ossetia-Alania, an autonomous republic within Russia. It is situated on the west of the Ardon River, located 54 km west of the republic's capital Vladikavkaz, at 43°4′N, 44°9′E. Population: 21,496 (2002 Census).

The town was established in 1850 by Prince Mikhail Semyonovich Vorontsov, the namestnik of the Caucasus, near an ancient silver/lead mine in the nearby Alagir Gorge. It was built up as a fortified settlement around a smelting plant and became a major mining center; it was granted town status in 1938. Alagir's economy is still dominated by mining and mineral extraction, but it also has a significant woodworking, canning and manufacturing industry.
