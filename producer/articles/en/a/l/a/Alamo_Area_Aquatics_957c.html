Alamo Aquatics (Alamo Area Aquatic Association) is a swim club based in San Antonio, Texas.

[edit] History

The Association was established in 1977, with heavy focus on individual attention and preparation of young swimmers for high level competition. Over the years, the team has lobbied heavily for the creation of new pools in San Antonio, Texas. The team now operates in four 50-meter facilities across the city. With a new pool just completed in the Northside Independent School District area. In the city of San antonio, where the team is located, the Alamo Area Aqautics Association is, by far, the largest single swim team, with competition mainly in the form of "The Wave" another local team.

[edit] Teams

The seven teams that make up the Association train in the: Walker and Davis Natatoriums (North East ISD), San Antonio Natatorium (San Antonio ISD), Palo Alto Natatorium (A part of the Alamo Community College District), at Northside Aqautics Center (Northside ISD), the Northside Natatorium, the Coronado Pool in Universal City, St. Mary's University, Boerne City Pool, and the TMI Episcopal Natatorium. The Association consists of well over 1100 athletes that compete regularly in swim meets across Texas and the outhern Zone. Currently the AAAA team is sanked by US Swimming in the top 25 clubs in the nation based on individual performances of athletes at competitions, this evaluation is constantly monitered and updated frequently by USA Swimming.

[edit] Notable swimmers

AAAA team has produced many national level athletes including: Walter Soza, Bruce Hayes, and Greg Losey. Several of which have earned medals for the USA at the Summer Olympic Games. Currently the several divisions of the Association qualify a good number of athletes to the US Speedo Sectionals level or higher including several who have Junior National times or better.
