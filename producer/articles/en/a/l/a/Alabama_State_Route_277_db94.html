Alabama State Route 277

State Route 277 in Alabama was first commissioned in March 2003 along part of Old U.S. Route 72 south of the present US 72 expressway. The highway had previously been decommissioned in the 1990s when the new four-lane alignment of US 72 had opened to the north, and the roadway was redesignated as Jackson County Road 74. It was the last two-lane section of US 72 in Alabama prior to the relocation.

County leaders and city leaders in Bridgeport and Stevenson were not happy about the road being their responsibility and were turned down for requests to take the road back several times. The roadway has many bridges and is designed in such a way that maintenance of the road was deemed too expensive for Jackson County. After a large effort by the county and local state legislators to get the route back under state control, ALDOT reluctantly re-commissioned the highway as SR 277, insisting that they would swap mileage with State Route 35 west of Scottsboro. However, that mileage swap never occurred and SR 35 west of Scottsboro remains a state-controlled highway.

SR 277 does not include all of Old US 72. The route is 9-miles long and only includes the portions between the interchange of Old US 72 and US 72 east of Stevenson to US 72 just east of Bridgeport.
