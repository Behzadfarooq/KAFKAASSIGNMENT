Alan Davidson (footballer)

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Alan Edward Davidson (born 1 June 1960 in Melbourne, Victoria) was an Australian football player.

He was a defender and won 76 international caps between 1980 and 1991 with the Socceroos, the Australian national football team, scoring 2 goals in those appearances.

Davidson began his domestic football career in 1977, playing for Altona City which had been his junior club. The following year, he transferred to South Melbourne, where he played until the end of the 1986 season, making 155 appearances and scoring 13 goals. His first Australian representative honours were in 1979, when he played for the Under-21 team in that year's youth international.

He moved to England for the 1984-85 season but made only three appearances for Nottingham Forest before returning to the southern hemisphere. He played one more season with South Melbourne before transferring to Melbourne Croatia, making 133 appearances and scoring 2 goals from 1987 to the end of the 1991-92 season.

At the end of his playing career, he returned to South Melbourne for two seasons, (1994-95 and 1995-96), with a brief spell with the Malayasian club, Pahang in between. His penultimate season (1996-97) was with the Collingwood Warriors, and his last season (1997-98) was back with the Melbourne Knights. Davidson spent 2000 coaching the Bulleen Zebras.
