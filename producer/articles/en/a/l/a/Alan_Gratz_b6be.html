Alan Gratz

Alan Michael Gratz (born January 27, 1972 in Knoxville, Tennessee) is the author of Samurai Shortstop, a historical novel for young adults. Gratz currently lives just outside Atlanta, Georgia.

  • Samurai Shortstop (Dial Books, 2006)

[edit] Produced Plays

  • Measured in Labor: The Coal Creek Project (Knoxville Actors Co-op, 2004)

  • The Gift of the Magi (adapted from the short story by O. Henry, Knoxville Actors Co-op, 1999)

[edit] Grants and Awards

  • Finalist, 2002 Marguerite deAngeli Contest (now known as the Delacorte Dell Yearling Contest for a First Middle-Grade Novel)
  • Co-winner, 2003 Kimberly Colen Memorial Grant from SCBWI [1]

[edit] Education

Gratz holds a Bachelor's degree in Creative Writing and a Master's degree in English Education, both from the University of Tennessee, Knoxville.

[edit] Trivia

  • Gratz taught for a semester at Palacký University in Olomouc, Czech Republic.
