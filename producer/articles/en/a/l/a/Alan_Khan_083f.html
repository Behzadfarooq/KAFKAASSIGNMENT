Alan Khan

Alan Khan is a top media personality in South Africa. He was Deputy MD of East Coast Radio, [1] in Durban, and also presented the weekday morning show called the BIG Breakfast with former beauty queen and TV drama star Sorisha Naidoo. Khan started his career at TNT Radio back in 1990. In 1993, he joined Capital Radio 604 where he worked until the station's last day of broadcast, which was on November 29, 1996. Alan co-hosted the last show on Capital Radio with colleague Kenny Maistry. Alan decided to branch out into television, where he covered powerboating on Super Sport in the 90's and then co-hosted am2day, the daily morning national TV show on SABC2.

He was also on stage acting as MC during the official Millennium Party in Durban[2], and has interviewed several prominent personalities, including Nelson Mandela, Alex Ferguson, Steffi Graf, Janet Jackson, Cliff Richard, Archbishop Desmond Tutu, Sepp Blatter, Sachin Tendulkar and Stevie Wonder amongst others. Alan is happily married to sociologist and gender advocate Dr Mariam Seedat, and has two sons Nasser and Ameer. He loves sport and supports Everton FC [3] in the English Premier Soccer League.

Alan has been invited to stand as a member of the global media selection panel that nominates candidates for the annual Laureus World Sports Awards. Alan is one of the few high profile South African men that have come out in support of initiatives to combat the spread of domestic violence, child abuse and rape. He has supported organisations including the Network Against Violence on Women and Children, The Advice Desk for Abused Women and ChildLine. Alan has been a judge for the South African Boeke prize since 2002.

In November 2006, Khan was appointed CEO of South Africa's biggest independent commercial radio station, Jacaranda 94.2 FM' [4] - which is based in the economic hub of Gauteng province in South Africa. Jacaranda is also owned by the listed black empowerment media giant Kagiso Media, the same company that owns East Coast Radio and that has stakes in other stations in Cape Town, Bloemfontein and Johannesburg.

In March 2007, Khan launched the inaugural 94.2 LIFES GREATEST WEEKEND OUT alongside Michael Bolton and Westlife, together with a host of top South African musicians including Dr Victor & the Rasta Rebels, Danny K, Freshly Ground and Eden.
