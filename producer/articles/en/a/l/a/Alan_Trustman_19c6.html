Alan Trustman

Alan Trustman is a screenwriter, teacher and former attorney and trader of currencies and precious metals.

[edit] Industry Memberships and Honors

Member, American Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences 1968 to present;
Member, Mystery Writers of America;
Member, International Association of Crime Writers;
Winner, Mystery Writers of America Edgar Allen Poe award for best screenplay Bullitt, 1968;
Chairman, Mystery Writers of America 1994 Best Picture Edgar Committee,
Honor, screenplay award from Delray Beach, FL, film festival 2007.

[edit] Other Industry Activities

Toronto Film Festival panelist, 1995.
Austin, Texas Film Festival panelist, 1999.

[edit] Education

John D. Runkle School, Brookline, MA, first in class 1944.
Boston Latin School, first in class 1945.
The Phillips Exeter Academy, first in class 1948.
Harvard College, magna cum laude and Phi Beta Kappa 1952.
Harvard Law School, magna cum laude and winning oralist Ames Competition 1955.

[edit] Business & Legal Experience

1955-1969: Partner at Law Firm of Nutter, McClennen & Fish, Boston
1961-1969: Founder and Director of Damon Corporation; frequency and medical equipment.
1973-1977: Director and member of Executive Committee of World Jai-Alai, Inc., parimutuel jai-alai.
1969-present: Private investor, currency and precious metal trading.

[edit] Family

Married Renee Rapaporte, 1955, divorced 1969, children John 1956, Laurie 1958 and Steven 1963.
Married Deborah Weisgall 1969, divorced 1987.
Married Michelle Urry 1987, widowered 2006, stepson Caleb Urry 1976.
