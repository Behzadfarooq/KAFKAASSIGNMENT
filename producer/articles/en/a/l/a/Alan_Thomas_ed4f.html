Alan Thomas

Alan Thomas is a British-based guitarist who performs on both classical and electric guitar.

He was born in Atlanta, Georgia, USA, and studied at Indiana University and the University of California at San Diego, where his teachers included Scott Tennant and the composer Brian Ferneyhough. He moved to the UK in 1997, the year in which he became the only guitarist to date to win first prize at the International Gaudeamus Interpreters Competition in the Netherlands.

Thomas is particularly dedicated to performing contemporary music, and he has premiered many new works by composers including Laurence Crane, Paul Davies, Gerardo Dirié, Gabriel Erkoreka, Michael Finnissy, Christopher Fox, Bryn Harrison, Petros Ovsepyan, Alwynne Pritchard, Joby Talbot and Ian Willcock. According to his website, his repertoire also includes pieces by Louis Andriessen, Cornelius Cardew, Aldo Clementi, James Dillon, Richard Emsley, Brian Ferneyhough, Graham Fitkin, Betsy Jolas, György Kurtág, Tod Machover, Tristan Murail, Per Nørgård, Roger Reynolds, Giacinto Scelsi and Howard Skempton, alongside more familiar pieces by Benjamin Britten, Elliott Carter, Hans Werner Henze, Steve Reich and Toru Takemitsu.

He has taught at the University of California and Trinity College of Music, and currently teaches at Ratcliffe College and Bedford School.
