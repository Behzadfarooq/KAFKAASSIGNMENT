Alain-Philippe Malagnac d'Argens de Villele

Alain-Philippe Malagnac d'Argens de Villele (1950–2000) was the lover of French writer Roger Peyrefitte, their relationship being a subject of several of the latter's works. Malagnac was also an art collector and the husband of singer Amanda Lear.

Scion of a French family, at fourteen, Alain-Philippe had a minor role in the 1964 film Les Amitiés particulières (English title: This Special Friendship), based on the award-winning autobiographical novel by Roger Peyrefitte centring on an intense friendship between an older and younger boy in at a strict Catholic boarding school. On the set of the film, de Villele met the Peyrefitte, and the two later began a relationship which lasted for two decades, the men remained close friends thereafter. Their relationship formed the background to Peyrefitte's novel, Notre Amour (Éd. Flammarion, 1967) and to L'Enfant de cœur, (an allusion to de Villele's role as a choirboy (Enfant de Choeur) in the film)

At the age of sixteen de Villele became Peyrefitte's personal secretary. They lived and travelled together, and he was eventually adopted by Peyrefitte.

As an adult de Villele's career (often financed by Peyrefitte) included proprietor of Le Bronx one of the first openly gay night clubs in Paris, and briefly managing French singer Sylvie Vartan, a disastrous undertaking which almost bankrupted Peyrefitte, who was forced to sell artworks and erotic antiquities to pay the resulting debts.

In 1978 de Villele met Amanda Lear in Paris, and in April 1979, while on a trip to the United States, they married. Their marriage lasted twenty one years.

On Saturday, December 16th, 2000, he was killed by smoke in a fire at his recently-bought farm house in Saint-Étienne-du-Grès.[1]. His death followed by only six weeks that of Peyrefitte.
