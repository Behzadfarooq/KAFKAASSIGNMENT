[edit] KPDF-CA - SPEEDY:db-advert

Article KPDF-CA.

Wow. "Doesn't seem to speak of anything useful" is quite a bold indictment. It's also an opinion disputed by many and a sweeping generalization. If you look at the edit logs, you'll see that several editors had a hand in this article. Imperfect as it is, it is certainly not blatant advertising, and does not meet the criteria for speedy deletion. Even if you think something is advertising, please consider a standard AfD listing instead, to give contributors to that article a chance to respond and defend it. Better yet, consider an {{unreferenced}} tag. A word to the wise is sufficient, and it's much better to improve a deficient article than to delete one. (See the 3rd bullet point in the things to bear in mind paragraph of the etiquette guideline.) The tag has been removed, not by me, but by another contributor and member of TelevisionStationsProject. dhett 06:36, 22 December 2006 (UTC)

Sure, thanks for the counter-point. I filed an AfD, let's see where it goes. If you could find some independent sources to cite for the article it would support keeping it. Alan.ca 06:45, 22 December 2006 (UTC)
