Alabama State Route 73

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

State Route 73, also known as SR 73, is a numbered state highway in Alabama. While always in the northeastern part of the state, SR 73 has existed on various corridors. The original State Route 73 extended from U.S. Route 278 and State Route 74, near Piedmont, Alabama, north to U.S. Route 411 and State Route 25. Serving the unincorporated towns of Spring Garden and Rock Run, the highway was decommissioned in the 1960s and is today Cherokee and Calhoun County Road 29.

State Route 73 was reincarnated in the 1970s as a spur from State Route 71 in Higdon north to Jackson County Road 90 in Bryant on top of Sand Mountain. SR 73 was extended in the 1980s north to the Tennessee state line to connect to newly-designated State Route 377, which ascends Sand Mountain to end at State Route 156 east of South Pittsburg, Tennessee.
