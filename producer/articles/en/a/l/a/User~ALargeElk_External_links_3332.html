[edit] My personal manifesto

External links exist to provide extra information that is not included within Wikipedia. Links to commercial websites have their place, but this is limited. Sometimes a commercial site will have the best information available on a particular topic, in which case it is appropriate to link to that information - but only if the information is presented in line with the standards that Wikipedia upholds internally. Primarily this means NPOV, and in this instance it means a limited emphasis, if any at all, on selling the company's own products.

For example, suppose that you are writing an article on widgets and you discover that Acme Widgets Inc. has a page on its website with a long, detailed history of widget manufacture. As long as the history itself is NPOV it's appropriate to include an external link to it, even though the site as a whole exists to sell Acme products. However, if it's a long, detailed history of widget manufacture with particular emphasis on the great advances made by Acme products... then thanks, but we'll live without it. (Long term, of course, we should be looking to have our own article on the history of widget manufacture).

External links can occasionally be useful for linking to directories of other links. For example, it would be much more appropriate to link to a comprehensive webring of fan sites for a particular celebrity than to link to each of the fan sites individually (or only to those where the webmaster happened to add themselves).

On the whole, I am ruthless with external links. We do not need spam and we are not a link farm.
