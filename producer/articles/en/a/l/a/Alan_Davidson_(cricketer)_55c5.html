Alan Keith Davidson (born June 14, 1929, Lisarow, Gosford, New South Wales) is a retired leading Australian cricketer of the 1950s and 1960s. He was an allrounder: a capable hard hitting left handed batsman, and an outstanding left-arm fast-medium opening bowler.

Davidson played his state cricket for New South Wales. He made his name playing for the Australian 2nd XI against New Zealand in 1949/50 and made his Test debut against England at Trent Bridge in 1953. He also toured England in 1956 and 1961, and went on further tours to India, Pakistan and the West Indies. Richie Benaud wrote of Davidson: "I have never known a more penetrative bowler... For the five years after 1957/58, whenever the side was in trouble, the captain... always looked to Davidson and seldom did he fail". (Willow Patterns, 1969).

Davidson was a Wisden Cricketer of the Year in 1962. He was also awarded an OBE. He published his autobiography Fifteen Faces in 1963.
