Alam Channa (born 1953, Sehwan, Sindh province, Pakistan, died July 2 1998, New York) was the world's tallest living man at 233.6 cm (7ft 8in) high.[1]

Channa's abnormal growth was noticed by his family when he ten years old, and continued until he was 26. Channa worked at the shrine of Shahbaz Qalander at Sehwan.[citation needed]

Channa died of kidney failure in a New York hospital and his body was laid to rest in his ancestral town of Dadu, Pakistan. He was survived by his wife Naseem, whom he married in 1989, and a son.[2]
