Alaiye

Alaiye (علاعية) is the Seljuk name for the modern Turkish city of Alanya, derived from the name of the Sultan `Ala' ad-Din Kay-Qubad. It refers to city-state in a specific period in time and the beylik which developed around there, at times under the Karamanoğlu dynasty. Before the influence of the Karamanoğlu dynasty, in 1291 Henry II of Jerusalem tried to invade Alaiye in which he was unsuccessful. During the Karamanid influence which started in 1293 by the capture of the beylik by Mecd' ad-Din Mahmud on behalf of the Mamluk which actually acquired the beylik from the Karamanids in exchange of 5,000 gold coins in 1427, there were again attempts to invade the beylik. In 1366 such an attempt of Peter I of Cyprus was unsuccessful. The beylik existed in some form of independence between the years of 1293 and 1471 bordered by the second rule of `Ala' ad-Din Kay Qubadh III, who centered his rule there, and the Ottoman general Gedik Ahmet Pasha's victory against Kasım Bey and the Karamanids. During this period no major state existed in Anatolia, following the defeat of the Seljuk Sultanate of Rûm by the Mongol Empire at the Battle of Köse Dag.
