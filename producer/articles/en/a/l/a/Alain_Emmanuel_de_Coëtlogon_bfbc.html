Alain Emmanuel de Coëtlogon

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

The Battle of the Cap de la Roque, won by Coëtlogon in 1703 (1803 print).

Alain Emmanuel de Coëtlogon (born December 4, 1646 at Rennes, died June 6, 1730 in Paris) was a Marshal of France during the reign of Louis XIV and Louis XV.

He was the seventh son of Louis de Coëtlogon, vicomte de Méjusseaume, counsellor to the King in the Parlement of Bretagne who died in 1657, and Louise Le Meneust de Brétigny. He enjoyed in Rennes an excellent education, and joined a military academy, where he graduated in 1668.

He served the mayor part of his career under Tourville, four years older then himself. In June 1672 he fought in the Battle of Solebay on Tourville's ship under vice-admiral Estrées. He also distinguished himself in the battles of Schooneveld in june 1673 and Texel in august 1673.

On january 26 1675 , just 29 years old , he is made captain of his own ship. He fights with Tourville around Sicily and is wounded in the Battle of Agosta where De Ruyter was mortally wounded.

In 1680 Alain Emmanuel de Coëtlogon studies theology and considers to enter the church. He goes back to sea, but is profoundly changed by this spiritual experience. He leads an austere, celibate life.

In 1683, he commands different ships first around Denmark , then in North Africa and in 1688 he takes part in the bombardment of Algiers under Jean II d'Estrées .

In the War of the Grand Alliance, he is part of the fleet under Chateau-Renault which lands the French troops in Ireland, and fights in the Battle of Bantry Bay on May 11, 1689.

Coëtlogon is promoted to squadron leader on november 1 1689.

During the Battle of Beachy Head (1690) he commands a division of the van under Tourville and displayed bravoury in destroying several Dutch ships. He also participates in the Battles of Barfleur and La Hogue (1692) and the Battle of Lagos (1693) where he , with eight ships , hunted down and captured five Dutch ships under the guns of the Gibraltar forts. In November 1693 he participates in the defense of Saint-Malo against an English raid.

On february 1 1694, he is made a knight in the Order of Saint Louis, and in May 1701 Coëtlogon is promoted to lieutenant general and given the title of marquis, which he would never use.

In 1702, during the War of the Spanish Succession , Coëtlogon is send to the Americas with five ships in service of Spain to protect the Spanish colonies. He stays a long time in Vera Cruz (Mexico), receiving conflicting orders.

He was most famous for winning the Battle of Cap de la Roque, off Lisbon on May 22, 1703 , were Coëtlogon onboard the Vainqueur and with four other ships Le Monarque, L'Éole, L'Orgueilleux and La Couronne, defeated five Dutch ships: Muydenberg, Roosendaël, Rotterdam, Rescherner, and Gasterlandt. The victory wasn't complete because the Dutch convoy escaped.

His last battle was the Battle of Vélez-Málaga (august 24 1704). He then became Naval commander in Brest and received many decorations.

The last four years of his life, he lived in a Jesuite orde. On June 1 1730, he is finally made Marshal of France, six days before his death.

Several ships were named after Coëtlogon, especially a frigate which was active during the Boshin war (1868-1869) in Japan, and later an armoured cruiser.
