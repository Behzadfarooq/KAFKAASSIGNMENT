The Alarfaj (Arabic: العرفج) family is a family of the Aniza tribe originating in Saudi Arabia with a branch in Kuwait. The family is named after the Rhanterium epapposum which is a local plant that grows in the deserts of Saudi Arabia and Kuwait known locally as Arfaj. The plant's flower is also the national flower of Kuwait.

The family began in Al-Hasa which is a city along the east coast of Saudi Arabia around the year 1591 AD by Arfaj Al-Aniza, of the Aniza tribe.

[edit] Kuwait Branch

The Kuwait branch of the Alarfaj family began with the migration of AbdulAziz Abdullah Alarfaj to Kuwait in the 1960's. AbdulAziz Alarfaj had lived in Saudi Arabia in the city of Al-Hasa but left the country due to its conquest by Ibn Saud. AbdulAziz Alarfaj then traveled as a merchant around the Persian Gulf until he eventually settled down temporarily in India where he married and had several children before settling permanently in Kuwait. His son, Saud AbdulAziz Abdullah Alarfaj is now the current head of the Kuwait branch of the family and runs a group of over ten companies known as Alarfaj Group.
