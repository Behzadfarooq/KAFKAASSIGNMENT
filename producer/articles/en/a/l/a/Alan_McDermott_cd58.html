Alan McDermott (born January 22, 1982 in Dublin) is an Irish football player who is currently playing with Kildare County F.C. in the eircom league First Division.

The ex-Manchester United trainee, McDermott joined Hamilton Academical in March 2003[1]. He went on to have spells with Shelbourne and Newry [2] before signing for Kildare County toward the end of 2005.

At the beginning of the 2006 season he had a brief stint with Dundalk before re-signing for Kildare County to fill a much needed void in the County defence[3]. He is holding down a steady place with Kildare due to injuries first to Phil Byrne and then to Ian Malone. He has appeared on the score sheet twice during the current stint with Kildare, firsty in a defeat to Finn Harps in Ballybofey with a headed goal in September 2006 and he headed a second in agaonst Athlone in Station Road.

McDermott has also represented his country at underage level, winning the player of the tournament award in an invitational in Portugal in 2000 for the Under 17's [4]. He has also represented an Eircom League U21's team [5].
