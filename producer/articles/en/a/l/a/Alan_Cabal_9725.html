Alan Cabal

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

A long-time writer for New York City-based alternative newspaper New York Press, Alan Cabal is a 21st century practitioner of so-called gonzo journalism. Known for tackling highly-controversial subjects, Cabal now writes for upstart left-wing political magazine Counterpunch and other publications.

Bemoaning the loss of its iconoclastic edge, Cabal left the New York Press in 2005 after the newspaper ran a controversial satire entitled The 52 Funniest Things About the Upcoming Death of the Pope.

Ironically, Cabal described himself as a "certified Satanist" who resigned in protest over the article, which he termed "a waste of paper, and a mere insult, not in the least bit challenging, to New York City's Roman Catholic population."[1], [2], [3]

In a list of his favorite quotations, Wayne State University Department of English professor Steven Shaviroplaces Cabal alongside gonzo journalism godfather Hunter S. Thompson with a quote from Cabal that could be a one sentence definition of the gonzo approach to journalism:

"Honestly, at this point, all I really care about is novelty and making sure I have ringside seats for whatever awful spectacle is about to unfold."[citation needed]
