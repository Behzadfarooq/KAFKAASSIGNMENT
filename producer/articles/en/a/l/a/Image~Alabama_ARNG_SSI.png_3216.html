[edit] Shoulder Sleeve Insignia.

[edit] Description:

  • Resting on a bar of white and red twists, a sprig of cotton plant, green leaves with white full bursting boll, all on a gray disc 2 1/4 inches (5.72 cm) in diameter within an 1/8 inch (.32 cm) blue border.

[edit] Symbolism:

  • Cotton is a great element of wealth in the State. As the predominant original settlement within the State was of English origin, the twists of the wreath are white and red.

[edit] Background:

  • The shoulder sleeve insignia was originally approved for Headquarters and Headquarters Detachment, Alabama Army National Guard on 15 December 1948.
  • The insignia was redesignated for Headquarters, State Area Command, Alabama Army National Guard on 15 August 1985.
