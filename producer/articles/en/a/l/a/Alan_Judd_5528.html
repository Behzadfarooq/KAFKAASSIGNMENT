Alan Judd

Alan Judd (born in 1946) is a former soldier and diplomat who now works as a full time writer in the United Kingdom. He writes both books and articles, regularly contributing to a number of publications, including The Daily Telegraph and The Spectator. His books include both fiction and non-fiction titles, with his novels often drawing on his military background.

  • Legacy (2001)
  • The Kaiser's Last Kiss (2003)
  • Dancing with Eva (2006)

[edit] Non fiction Titles

  • Ford Madox Ford (1990)
  • First World War Poets (Character Sketches) (1997)
  • The Quest for C: Mansfield Cumming And the Founding of the Secret Service (1999)

[edit] Awards

In 1991, he won a Guardian Fiction Award for his book The Devil's Own Work.
