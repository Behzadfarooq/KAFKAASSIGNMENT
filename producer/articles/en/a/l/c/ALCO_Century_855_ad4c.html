The ALCO Century 855 was ALCO's most powerful diesel-electric locomotive and, at the time, the most powerful diesel locomotive ever built. Powered by a pair of ALCO's 251C diesel engines, and rated at 5500 horsepower (4.1 MW), it was ALCO's answer to the EMD DD35 and the GE U50. Only two cab units and one booster were built, all for Union Pacific Railroad, which had requested double-engined locomotives from EMD and GE in order to replace their aging fleet of gas turbine locomotives. Poor performance led to their early retirement, eventually being scrapped by February of 1972.
