I like to do cleanups and copyedits to articles I come across, starting stubs, verifying info -- basically helping keep wikipedia free of detritus, crappy writing, bad formatting, especially of the anonymous user kind.

[edit] On my docket

Articles I've worked on (created or extensively edited), and could use some competent help. Please no "indy journalists" or conspiracy-minded yahoos out to use wikipedia to promulgate their own agendas:

  • Director of the National Clandestine Service (lots of names to be stubbed and/or expanded upon)
  • James Pavitt
  • Private military company
  • Billy Waugh - I've been reading this remarkable man's autobiography Hunting the Jackal and wanted to see if there was anything on him here. Lo and behold, there was an article already in the works, which wasn't all that good. I figure this legend deserved something much better, so I've been improving it little by little. I have some information, from his book as well as other sources -- I just have compile and add it here.
  • Gary Berntsen - I'd love to meet this guy. His book, Jawbreaker is an amazing account of the CIA operation to topple the Taliban. Now Berntsen has a private consulting firm (who doesn't?) Maybe he needs a marketing exec...
  • Jonathan Idema - A charlatan, pathological liar, and quite possibly off his gourd. It's been interesting trying to craft as neutral an article as possible.
  • Robin Moore - In addition to working on Waugh's article, this one has been a welcome antidote to writing about Idema. The article could use some insight from Hopkinsville residents, or those who've read his books. The Green Berets is on my reading list (if I can find a copy -- I believe it's out of print.)
  • Special Forces Association - I created this, and now I'm expanding it with the names and locations of SFA chapters, their websites, and any notable members.
  • Randall Shughart and Gary Gordon - The content of the articles for these heros and Medal of Honor awardees is total crap, a cut-and-paste job from the official MOH site. These guys deserve better. They could use a proper biography as well as a better-written section on the events leading up to their deaths. UPDATE: I've gotten around to doing some significant copyedits, notably removing the verbatim official medal citations. I also pared down the events in Mogadishu and a few other wikipedians have been great to spot and fix my typographical errors (I type really fast). I'm hoping to find some more background of their lives, but being members of Delta Force, I bet it's pretty difficult.

[edit] WP rant

By and large, I appreciate that Wikipedia exists. I've been active here since October 2003 and contributed in my own way to make sure WP upholds a certain standard. Over the years I've seen a mission creep of sorts as WP has gotten more complex, complicated, bureaucratic, what have you. I've read plenty of beefs other users have had with admins and got into a recent dispute with one myself. Of course it revolves around the latest WP Doublespeak regarding what makes sources attributable. You have to read it to believe it: WP:ATT/FAQ.

The money quote:

“ The Bible is a primary source. [snip] Primary sources that have been published by a reliable source may be used for the purposes of attribution in Wikipedia, but only with care, because it's easy to misuse primary sources. For that reason, edits that rely on primary sources should only make descriptive claims that can be checked by anyone without specialist knowledge. Any interpretation of primary source material requires a secondary source. ”

So, if the article the Bible quotes Genesis 1:1 using the Bible as the source, it's not attributable. WTF?! I'm laughing inside. Really. 2+2=5, indeed...
