Alcis (god)

The Alcis were a pair of twin male gods worshiped by the Germanic peoples. They were briefly mentioned in chapter 43 of Tacitusâ€™ Germania. According to Germania, their worship took place in a grove with no visual representation of the deities. Their priests were said to wear women's clothing. The Alcis had dominion over brothers and young men. It should also be noted that Tacitus equates the Alcis to the Roman Castor and Pollux.
