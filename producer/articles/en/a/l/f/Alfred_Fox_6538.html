Alfred Fox, (September 9, 1794 – May 20, 1874)[1] of Falmouth, Cornwall, United Kingdom, was owner and developer of Glendurgan Garden. He was a member of the Quaker Fox family of Falmouth .

[edit] Business interests

He ran the fishing and pilchard-salting and export part of the diversified Fox family business. He was also involved in the firm’s other activities:

  • Shipbroking at Falmouth
  • Iron Founding at Perranarworthal
  • Mining and smelting tin and copper, in Cornwall and South Wales, at Neath Abbey[2].

He was Vice Consul of the U.S.A. in Falmouth, 1858-65 and Consul in Falmouth, 1863-74 [3]. He and other family members acted as Consul in Falmouth for a large number of foreign countries.

In 1859, he was elected as Chairman of the Directors of the Company authorised by Parliament to improve Falmouth Docks [4].

[edit] Family

[edit] Parents

He was the son of Robert Were Fox (1754 - 1818) and his wife, Elizabeth Tregelles (1768 – 1849)

[edit] Marriage and children

He married Sarah Lloyd on May 16, 1828 [5] and they had twelve children:

  • Alfred Lloyd Fox (May 26, 1829 – June 23, 1885), married Mary Jane Fox (died October 21, 1919)[6]
  • Theodore Fox (April 4, 1831 – September 27, 1899). Married Harriet Howell Kirkbridge. [7]
  • Rachel Elizabeth Fox (February 6, 1833 – August 18, 1923),[8] who married first Samuel Lindoe Fox (June 16, 1830 – 1862) ,[9] in November 1862 and second Philip Debell Tuckett (1833 – 1894)
  • Sarah Charlotte Fox (March 15, 1834). On October 27, 1852 she married Sir Robert Nicholas Fowler ,(September 12, 1828 – May 22, 1891 ), a banker, M.P. for the Penryn and Falmouth Constituency 1868 – 1874 , Conservative M.P. for the City of London Constituency 1880 –1891 and Lord Mayor of London. He was created a Baronet in 1885,[10].
  • Mary Fox, (August 11, 1835 – August 3, 1892)[11] who married Joseph Whitwell Pease (1828 – 1903) 1st Baronet Pease of Hutton Lowcross.
  • Howard Fox (December 10, 1836 – November 15, 1922). Married Olivia Blanche Orme [12]. U.S. Consul 1874-75 [13]. His daughters gave Fox-Rosehill Gardens in Falmouth [14] to Carrick District Council in 1974. Author of Observations in further illustration of the history and statistics of the Pilchard Fishery(1879)
  • Helen Maria Fox (November 17, 1838 – May 1, 1928). Married John William Pease[15].
  • Lucy Ann Fox, (October 5, 1841 – December 26, 1934)[16] who married Thomas Hodgkin (July 29, 1831 – March 2, 1913), Banker and historian on August 7, 1861 [17]. Lived at Treworgan, Mawnan Smith [5].
  • Charles William Fox (June 13, 1843 – June 18, 1866) [18].
  • George Henry Fox (September 27, 1845 – January 13, 1931). Married Rachel Juliet Fowler.[19]. They had seven children and lived at Glendurgan from 1938[5](.
  • Wilson Lloyd Fox (January 27, 1847 – January 13, 1936). Married (1) Augusta Mary Rogers [5](2) Constance Louis Grace Rogers [20]
  • Sophia Lloyd Fox (September 28, 1848 – December 25, 1870)[21].
