Alfred Freiherr von Oppenheim

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Alfred Paul Ernst Freiherr von Oppenheim known in America as Alfred Oppenheim (b. May 5, 1934 in Cologne, Germany – d. January 5, 2005) was a German billionaire and banker. Oppenheim was a descendant of Salomon Oppenheim, who founded the Credit Institute in Bonn and moved to Cologne in 1978. He studied at Amherst and Harvard until 1960. In 1964, he became a senior partner of Sal. Oppenheim jr. & Cie. KGaA, and in 1978 he took over leadership of the board. From 1993 he was a member of the shareholders' committee and the governing body of the bank.

Oppenheim was also Vice-President of the German Chamber of Commerce (Deutsche Industrie- und Handelskammertag), President of the Franco-German Chamber of Commerce in Paris. He also served as president of the German Council on Foreign Relations Berlin. He founded the Alfred Freiherr von Oppenheim Foundation for Scientific Research.

On October 10, 1996, Yad Vashem, Israel, decided to recognize Baron Alfred von Oppenheim's father Baron Friedrich Carl von Oppenheim (posthumously) as Righteous Among the Nations.

On 14 October 2004 he was awarded the Nordrhein-Westfalen Decoration of Honour. He was a fellow of the University of Cologne, holder of the Federal Cross of Merit First Class of the Federal Republic of Germany. In 2003, he was awarded the Croix de Commandeur from the French Legion of Honour for encouraging and deepening political and cultural ties between Germany and France.

In 1962 he married his wife Baronness Jeanne von Oppenheim, with whom he had three children. Oppenheim and his wife owned one of the largest private collections of world photography and donated it to an American Museum. Oppenheim died in January 2005 following a short but serious illness.
