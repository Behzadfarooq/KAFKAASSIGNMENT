Alfred (name)

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

In English the meaning of the name Alfred is: Sage; wise; elvin. From the Old English Aelfraed, meaning elf counsel. Also from Ealdfrith or Alfrid, meaning old peace. Famous bearers: Alfred the Great was a 9th-century king, Alfred Lord Tennyson was a famous 19th-century poet.

People called Alfred:

  • Alfred the Great, king of Wessex and first king of England
  • Alfred, video game character from the Fatal Fury series
  • Alfred Pennyworth, butler to DC Comics character Batman
  • Alfred, man who has been living in the departure hall of Terminal 1 of the Charles de Gaulle International Airport since 1988
  • Alfred Hitchcock
  • Alfred Kinsey, American biologist and professor of entomology and zoology, famous for his research on human sexuality.
  • Alfred Nobel
  • Alfred Rosenberg
  • Alfred Schnittke, Russian Composer
  • Alfred Sung
  • Alfred Wegener, German scientist
  • "Weird Al" Yankovic, a parody/comedy musician with the real name Alfred Matthew Yankovic
  • Michael Alfred Peszke, Polish-American psychiatrist and historian
  • Michael Alfred
  • Alfred the Sartan, a fictional character from The Death Gate Cycle
  • Alfred Hawthorne Hill, real first and middle name, of the famous English comedian Benny Hill
