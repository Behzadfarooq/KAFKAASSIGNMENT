Alfred Polizzi

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Alfred "The Owl" Polizzi (1900-1994) was a Cleveland mobster and helped establish criminal syndicate operations in Northeast Ohio.

Born in Sicily, Polizzi immigrated as a child to the United States with his family in 1900. During the 1920s and 30s, Polizzi worked with Antonio Milano, Moe Dalitz and his brother Joseph Polizzi and, in 1935, ruled over the Cleveland rackets until moving to Coral Gables, Florida following his conviction of tax evasion in 1944. Described by Senate investigators as "...one of the most influential members of the underworld of the United States, his influence reportedly extended from Ohio to central Florida and southern California during Peter Milano's leadership of the Los Angeles crime family during the 1970s.
