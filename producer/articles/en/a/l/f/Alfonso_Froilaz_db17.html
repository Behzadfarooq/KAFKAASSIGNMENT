Alfonso Froilaz

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Alfonso Froilaz, called the Hunchback or, in Spanish, el Jorobado, was the king of Galicia for a short time, from 925 to 926. He seems to have claimed Asturias and León as well, as the inheritor of Fruela II, but was driven from the realm by his cousins Sancho, Alfonso, and Ramiro, the sons of Ordoño III. Sancho succeeded him in Galicia while the rest of his father's realm went to Alfonso.
