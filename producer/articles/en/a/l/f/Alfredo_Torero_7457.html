Alfredo Torero

Alfredo Augusto Torero Fernández de Córdova (* September 10, 1930 in Huacho, Lima Region, Peru, † June 19, 2004 in Valencia) was a Peruvian anthropologist and linguist.

Alfredo Torero became famous by publishing his article "The Dialects of Quechua" in 1964 and is considered the founder of Andean Linguistics. He included cultural aspects of the Andean peoples into his linguistic investigations. Besides Quechua and Aymara, he investigated extinct languages like Mochica and Puquina.

The present classification of the Quechuan languages is based fundamentally on his investigations and those of his colleague Gary Parker, who got similar results independently. An important finding of his research is the fact, that the origin of Quechua is not to be found in the Inka capital Cuzco but in the Lima Region.
