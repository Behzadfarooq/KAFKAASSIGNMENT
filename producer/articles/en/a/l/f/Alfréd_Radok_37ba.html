Alfréd Radok

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Alfréd Radok (1917-1976) was a distinguished Czech stage director. Radok was born on December 17th in Prague. He worked as the stage director of the National Theatre in Prague in the years 1948 to 1949 and 1966 to 1968. Radok's work belongs to the top of the Czech stage direction of the 20th century. He is often cited as a formalist in his work. Radok died on April 22nd 1976 in Vienna.

He was the father of stage director David Radok.
