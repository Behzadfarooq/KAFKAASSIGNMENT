  • The text you submitted tells how you plan to distribute the CD and asks users to improve it. When I mention promotion, the copyright isn't the problem. Wikipedia generally only includes CDs by already established artists (see WP:NMG) and not CDs that may or may not take off. Articles are also supposed to cite reports or coverage by 3rd party sources. I called it promotional because it appeared you were trying to get an audience through advertising the project on Wikipedia - see WP:NOT (especially the section Wikipedia is not a soapbox which covers self-promotion and advertising). Basically, it says it's inappropriate for people to articles in which they have a personal stake (like their projects or business). I've been a tad wordy, but I hope this clarifies things. - Mgm|(talk) 20:13, 29 March 2006 (UTC)
  • From what I can tell the article was never created in the first place. At least not under the title Planktum Live CD (remember that capitalization counts). I don't think 72 people using it can really be considered as "taking off". Other CDs listed here have sold millions and millions of copies, and listed websites have a large number of visitors. To get something undeleted, you should go to WP:DRV and explain why there's no copyright violation, but first we need to know what title you posted it under.
