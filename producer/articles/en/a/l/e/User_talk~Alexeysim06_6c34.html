[edit] Re: your email

Good morning. Thanks for your email. I am sorry, I don't understand your question fully, but I will try and explain. Wikipedia is not meant to be an advertising service. When pages get created by relatively new users, which look quite like an advertisement, they run the risk they get speedily deleted early on. This happens even faster when there is a suspicion of a conflict of interest.

If you want to start a subject on a page where you are involved with, it is best to stay a bit away from it, best is to add a request, or maybe a very small article (a 'stub'), which is very minimal (only containing one or two sentences, and an appropriate {{stub}}-template. From then, wait until others write more of the article, or see what their judgement is (you can of course repair some errors if they are getting in the document, but don't add more info to the article).

If you find other articles that are too much an advertisement, you can add an appropriate 'warning' template to that or start a discussion on the talkpage and wait until others answer. I see you were questioning MedCalc on the talkpage (which is an appropriate way to go). If I would judge the situation, that article has now been edited by 13 different people (some vandalism, which got reverted by at least one very experienced editor), and grew from a two-line page to a reasonable article in just over one year (you see that it can take very long before an article gets to a decent version). I would not judge this as advertising with this page-history, that would be different if this was the work of only one editor. Hope this explains your question. Have a nice day! --Dirk Beetstra T C 08:57, 31 March 2007 (UTC)
