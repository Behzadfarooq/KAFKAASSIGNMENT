Alexis Rhodes, known in Japan as Asuka Tenjouin (天上院明日香, Tenjōin Asuka?), is a fictional character in the anime series Yu-Gi-Oh! GX (Yu-Gi-Oh! Duel Monsters GX in Japan) and the manga series of the same name. Her family name has been written as "Tenjoin," "Tenjouin," and "Tenjyoin" in Roman characters. Her given name in the Japanese version means "Tomorrow's Fragrance". In the English version, Alexis is voiced by Priscilla Everett, while Sanae Kobayashi takes the role in the Japanese version.

Regarded as the "queen" of her resident Obelisk Blue dormitory, Alexis is the principle female protagonist of the series. A motivating factor of her strong-willed outlook during the first season is the search for her missing brother, Atticus.

[edit] Fictional character biography

Alexis, residing in the Obelisk Blue dormitory along with Zane Truesdale (Ryo Marufuji) and initially Chazz Princeton (Jun Manjoume), is one of the top students at Duel Academy (Duel Academy). She does not follow the crowd nor does she regard herself as better than other people like some students in the dormitory, but has a stern pride as an Obelisk Blue duelist.

Alexis mourns for her long-lost older brother, Atticus Rhodes (Fubuki Tenjouin). With the help of Zane, she constantly searches for clues surrounding his disappearance, until it is revealed that he joined the Shadow Riders (Seven Stars), under the control of Nightshroud (Darkness).[1] Defeated by Jaden in a Shadow Duel and relieved of this persona, the incident leaves Atticus amnesic, prompting his sister to engage in a Duel with Titan to restore his memories.[2]

Later in the series, Chazz comes out about his infatuation with Alexis after Jaden defeats the final Shadow Rider.[3] Atticus helps Chazz with his crush and he steals all seven Spirit Keys needed to revive the Sacred Beasts in order to force Alexis to duel with him. Although Chazz plays suggestive cards such as Hidden Wish and Love Letter to show her how much he cares, Alexis wins in the end, stating that she cannot return his feelings, because she "loves dueling." Chazz's crush on Alexis continues throughout the series, although after having his free will sapped by Sartorius (Takuma Saiou), his interests fade, until he is rescued by Jaden and returned to his former self.

Following Chazz's entrance into the Society of Light, Alexis duels with him to keep him from taking over Obelisk Blue completely. She fails, however, and meets the same fate as her fellow elites. Sartorius would subsequently strip Alexis of her free will completely, rendering her cold and heartless.[4] Providing her with a new White Night (白夜, Byakuya?, White Night's in the Japanese version) deck, he orders her to retrieve the key to the mind control satellite of Misgarth, which his good personality entrusted to Jaden, but was freed from his influence upon being defeated.

[edit] Deck

Alexis plays a Cyber Girl deck. Her tactics are well-balanced due to the variety of different effects her monsters possess, ranging from early and devastating direct attacks to negation of opposing card abilities. She is also skilled at concentrating on a single tactic while dueling, devoting all her cards to the summoning and strengthening of her fused warrior Cyber Blader, or her Cyber Angel Ritual series (based on the Seven Lucky Gods of Japanese mythology). With her White Night deck, she creates a physical cold in the dueling ring which prevents her opponent from using Spell and Trap cards.
