[edit] Introduction

Interwood FC are a Sunday league football team who's home ground is Maple Cross in Hertfordshire, England. Interwood play in the Olympian League Sunday[1], Division 2. The team was born from the joining of two 5-a-side squads playing in the Pitch Invasion Watford league [www.pitch-invasion.com/] where Interwood and AFC Interwood merged to form the larger squad of Interwood FC. Now in their 3rd season of Sunday League Football Interwood have finally risen above the relegation spots to make the top 4 their home.


[edit] Now and the future

Under the guidance of club secretary Joseph Reeves and 'hands off' manager Ben "rant" Wynes, the captain Graham Clarke has led Interwood into contention for the Division two championship in 2006/7. On the pitch top scorer Richard "that goal was" Nice has been commanding from the top of the pitch displaying both goal scoring and goal creating potential with 14 assists to date. Interwood have pioneered a new technique in training whereby the squad are not picked until the Wednesday before the game, shunning traditional training methods and practice sessions in favour of Saturday sessions in Watford town centre. Interwood have learnt their football on the park, playing from the heart and at times have suffered.

[edit] Gareth Mannall Retires

After a heated a tense start to the 2006/7 season, Gareth Mannall sensationally quit having been left on the bench in a tense game against AFC Seniors. being made to run the line throughout the game whilst young manager Ben Wynes brought on a new squad member Mac McHugh, Mannall was heard to say "...you're taking the p**s". The afternoon passed and result sunk in, but the following morning a series of emails between Gareth and his team mates was to lead to the retirement of journeyman Mannall. What follows is an abridged transcript of the email exchange that would lead to Gareth's last major involvement with Interwood FC.

19/09/2006 Sent by Gareth: I've quit.

Gareth
Sent by Mike Gilder:
Why Quit?
Sent by Gareth: I did not a game last week. I was not brought on to the pitch when the person who was playing my position (left midfield) was taken off with an injury, rather a new (untested) player, was brought on. To make matters worse I was not brought on when a member of the squad was struggling and communicated this. In my mind it doesn't seem worthwhile committing to something where my commitment to the cause (i.e turning up every week) is not rewarded and where any new players coming in to the team get priority over me. I appreciate I may not be the best player in the team, but unlike other members I will always be there; one person can make a lot of difference once team number dwindle. I am going to miss playing, but I will not miss the frustration that has been associated with it.

All the best for the season, Gareth

[edit] Mike Gilder, father

In the early hours of 23/10/2006, Mike "chopper" Gilder, Interwood legend and midfield general became a father. The next day the team put aside any shame and welcomed a new squad member.

Sent by Mike Gilder:
Hi All,

I have great pleasure in letting you all know that Joshua Stewart Gilder was born at 05:03 yesterday morning, He weighed 7lbs and is 35 cm long. He has lots of dark brown hair and is a handsome little devil (If I do say so myself!). Both Mum and baby are doing really well, although he is having some trouble feeding (im told this is common to start !)

He took us by complete surprise as he was breach and we were convinced he would be late. here is rundown of events, it was VERY quick. But please don't panic when reading this as it was still controlled and everyone was brilliant :

12:30am - Alex finally work me up from a deep sleep to let me know her waters had broken. 12:40am - My brain finally turned on and I said "****" and called the Hospital. As he was thought to be breach we were told to go in straight away. 1:10am - The midwives inspected Alex's belly and had trouble deciding if the baby was still breach. They then did a scan and couldn't find the head !!!, They then realised the head was 'engaged' and pretty far down. We were then advised to go home and come back when the contractions started and were a few mins apart. 2:30am got home, went to bed 2:45am - Alex started to have 'BIG' contractions ! 3:00am - Contractions 'BIG' and two mins apart !!!!, I called the delivery suite to say we were coming back ! 3:04am - Alex had the urge to push !!, I went '****' and called an ambulance. 03:25am - Ambulance arrived, and gave alex gas and air 03:45 - Ambulance men decided to take alex to hospital as we could not wait for the midwife to turn up 04:00 - Got taken to delivery suite in the hospital, Alex was inspected and the midwife (Jane Speed, who was Brilliant) found that she was 10cm dilated and told her to 'PUSH AWAY' (with the contractions) 05:03 - Joshua shot out !!

Im rushing back to the hospital now, so will send pics later. Please pass this on to anyone I may have missed.

Take care and see all soon.

Mike, Alex and Joshua xx
