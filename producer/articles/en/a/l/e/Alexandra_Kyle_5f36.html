Alexandra Kyle

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Alexandra Kyle (born November 11, 1988) is an American actress from Los Angeles, California.

Alexandra Kyle is best known for her roles in 1996's Eye for an Eye starring Sally Field and Kiefer Sutherland. She co-starred again with Sutherland in 1996's A Time to Kill, which also starred Sandra Bullock, Samuel L. Jackson, Matthew McConaughey, Ashley Judd and Kevin Spacey.

She had a lead role in 1998's In Quiet Night, and made guest appearances spots on JAG, ER and The Practice.

In 2004 she had a part in Gary Winick's film 13 Going on 30 (starring Jennifer Garner) as the young Tom-Tom, the younger version of Judy Greer's character Lucy.

Most recently, she made a guest appearance on "Without a Trace" on CBS, which aired as the fourth episode of the fifth season. (2006)
