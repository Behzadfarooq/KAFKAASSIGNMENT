[edit] My Popart

Mypopart.com logo

My Popart, LLC. a privately held corporation that creates one of a kind works of art from everyday photos. The company is based in Huntington Beach, California, co founded by Judy Fleenor and Kinda Hibrawi. Using new technology and materials to revive the old art form of Silkscreening pioneered by Pop Culture icon Andy Warhol, [My Pop Art] takes any of your favorite photographs and digitally paints a one-of-a-kind. Stretched on wooden bars ready to hang, the paintings come alive as the vibrant colors “pop” off the wall. The final product is a complete personalized Pop Art Portrait on Canvas. This new technology allows the portraits to be reprinted at any size, allowing for the one of kind portraits to be flawlessly reproduced and shared with loved ones everywhere. Popart Prints are based on the art form of screen printing, made popular in the 1960s. During the 1960s, screen printing came into greater prominence, particularly due to the Pop artists, who were attracted to its bold areas of un-modulated color, its flat surfaces, and its generally "commercial" look. Andy Warhol used this printmaking technique even when he was creating images on canvas. Today collectors spend hundreds of thousands of dollars on original Popart. Why has Pop Art returned so strongly, 47 years after its birth? Some say Pop has gained respect over time and proven to be a solid investment. Others say it's simply time for the pendulum to swing back that way. Still others see striking parallels between now and the 1960s, with the answer resting deep in American's need to be comforted by familiar things

[edit] Pop Art Defined

Sample Pop Art Created by www.mypopart.com

Pop Art was a visual artistic movement that emerged in the late 1950s in England and the United States. Characterized by themes and techniques drawn from mass culture, such as advertising and comic books, pop art is widely interpreted as either a reaction to the then-dominant ideas of abstract expressionism or an expansion upon them. Pop art, like pop music, aimed to employ images of popular as opposed to elitist culture in art, emphasizing the banal or kitschy elements of any given culture. Pop art at times targeted a broad audience, and often claimed to do so. However, much pop art is considered very academic, as the unconventional organizational practices used often make it difficult to comprehend.


[edit] The Inspiration

After the birth of her son, Spencer, Fleenor became inspired to create a larger-than-life painting from the countless photos taken of Spencer and her dog Zoey. Family and friends loved this unique style of art and wanted the same kind of portraits for their home and or office. People were drawn to this style of painting for many reasons - sentimental and personal. Realizing there was a real need to create personalized artwork that captured the emotion of their favorite photograph, Fleenor and Hibrawi quit corporate America and pursued their real dream.


[edit] Giving Back to the Community

PetSmart Charities Campaign

My Pop Art is committed to help make a difference by providing beautiful artwork to deserving organizations. The monies raised help supply much needed relief and promote community awareness. On August 29, 2005 Hurricane Katrina hit the Gulf Coast. In response to the devastation My Pop Art launched a relief effort on behalf of all the animals who suffered right along with their human companions in the disaster areas. Deeply committed to animal protection issues, [My Pop Art] donated proceeds from the artwork to help many animal organizations such as, United Animal Nations, Best Friends Animal Society and the Humane Society. My Pop Art was chosen to create a unique artwork for PetSmart Charities 2006 Fall campaign. The campaign, Just A Buck, Change Their Luck™ featured limited edition shirts and mouse pads with collectable artwork by Judy Fleenor. The monies raised will help save the lives of millions of animals. During this campaign, customers can donate a dollar when they make a purchase at any PetSmart store or purchase these products online at [www.petsmart.com]. On July 12, 2006 the tragic situation in the Middle East had escalated and My Pop Art created a Limited Edition print for UNICEF, United Nations Children’s Fund. The painting was created to help in the humanitarian relief to promote the idea of hope for Lebanon and its people.100% of the net proceeds from the purchase of the Limited Edition Print will provide much needed relief and urgent supplies for the children caught in the Middle East conflict. MyPopart will continue giving back to the community by providing beautiful works of art to help raise money for worthy causes.
