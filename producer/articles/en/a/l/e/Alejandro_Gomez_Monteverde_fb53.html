Alejandro Monteverde (born 13 July 1977 in Tampico, Tamaulipas) is a Mexican film director. His first film, Bella took top prize at the 2006 Toronto International Film Festival by winning the “Peoples Choice Award”,[1] an award which has also been won by such films as Chariots of Fire, American Beauty, Life Is Beautiful, Crouching Tiger, Hidden Dragon and Hotel Rwanda.[2]

Bella marks the feature directorial debut for Monteverde, who also co-wrote its original screenplay. Bella features Manuel Perez, Angelica Aragon, Jaime Terelli and Ali Landry.

Bella's filmmakers received honors from the Smithsonian and the Whitehouse. The Smithsonian Latino Center honored Monteverde and Bella's filmmakers with their "Legacy Award" at a private screening in Miami hosted by Florida Governor Jeb Bush and his wife Columba Bush, a native Mexican.[3] The director of the Department of Citizenship gave the director of Bella, Alejandro Monteverde, the "American by Choice" award for Bella's posotive contribution to Latino art and culture in the U.S.[4] The president and first lady saw the film and invited Bella's director to sit with Laura Bush in her private box during the annual "State of the Union" speech in 2007.[5][6]

Monteverde married the American actress Ali Landry in April 2004. [7]

[edit] Business career

Monteverde co-owns Metanoia Films, in conjunction with Eustace Wolfington, financier and producer Sean Wolfington, actor and producer Eduardo Verastegui and former Fox Business Affairs executive Leo Severino.[8] Metanoia Films owns half of the film Bella.
