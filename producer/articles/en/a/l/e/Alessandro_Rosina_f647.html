Alessandro Rosina (born January 31, 1984 in Belvedere Marittimo, Cosenza) is an Italian footballer, currently playing for Torino F.C. of Serie A. He was brought up in the Parma youth system, and then was loaned to Hellas Verona to gain experience. He moved back to Parma where he didn't really feature and was sold to Torino, where he was the driving force behind their 2005/2006 promotion campaign. During the current season he is doing extraordinary things and according to reports major clubs such as Arsenal, Chelsea, Liverpool, Barcelona, Inter, and Roma are all interested in his services.[1] He is a regular with the Italy U-21 squad, and he is tipped to be a future star.


[edit] Trivia

Rosina has been nicknamed "Rosinaldo" by the Torino fans because of his immense skill, technique, and flair which you would expect from a Brazilian player.
