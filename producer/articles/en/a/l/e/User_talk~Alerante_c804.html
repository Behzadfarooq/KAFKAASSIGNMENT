[edit] Fedora external links

Thanks for the help with the Fedora article. My problem at the moment is that I'm not sure how external links are treated in the English Wikipedia - quite a lot of articles have twenty or more links, and I'm not sure if everyone agrees with me that there should be only a handful which are really important and provide high quality and additional content. Btw., if there are any clear guidelines about the number and quality of external links in the English Wikipedia, please give me a link to them. I just know the German guidelines, and they seem to be quite different (or, maybe just stricter). --Liquidat 23:47, 18 December 2006 (UTC)

The English Wikipedia has an external links policy at WP:EL, which agrees with you (and me) in that "Links should be kept to a minimum." I usually accept three categories of links without discussion:
  1. the official Web site(s)
  2. reputable listings (IMDb, dmoz, etc.)
  3. and reliable sources (of course!)
Any external links that simply duplicate the content of (1) and (2) I usually get rid of; e.g., Fedora already has a torrent page, so we don't need to link anyone else's. After that I prefer discussing any other links that don't blatantly violate policy on the talk page; that's how a rather ugly argument at phpBB got settled — I see you're trying to start a discussion already. :)
If I get time I might try to do some more of the cleaning up you suggested on Talk:Fedora Core#Some points...; the article has been growing organically, I think, with people adding facts but not looking at it from the overall perspective of the whole page. æ² ✆ 2006‑12‑19t00:09z
Thanks for the answer and the help! --Liquidat 07:31, 19 December 2006 (UTC)
