Alexander Werth, (1901-1969) was a British writer, journalist, and war correspondent, wrote about France in the prewar period and about Russia in World War II, especially Stalingrad and Leningrad. He was a fluent Russian speaker. His most well known work is Russia at War 1941 to 1945. He was the Moscow correspondent for the Guardian newspaper from 1946 to 1949. He was one of the first outsiders to be allowed into Stalingrad after the World War II battle. Other works include: France 1940-1955: the de Gaulle Revolution; Moscow 41; The Last Days of Paris: a Journalist's Diary; Leningrad; The Year of Stalingrad; and Musical Uproar in Moscow.
