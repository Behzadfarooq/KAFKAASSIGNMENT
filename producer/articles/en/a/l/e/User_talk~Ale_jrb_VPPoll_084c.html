[edit] Leave Feedback!

Thanks for choosing to take part in our poll. To leave feedback in answer to the questions, post them below in a new section.

[edit] Squirepants101's feedback

1. I'd give VandalProof a 7.

2. I use it very often. Most of the time, I don't use Internet Explorer much when I'm on Wikipedia.

3. Before I got approved for using VandalProof, I used to use navigation popups to revert vandalism. Now, I only use popups when VandalProof stops working properly. I also tried Vandal Fighter for a very short time only to find MascotGuy sockpuppets. Then I stopped using it because it did not have a rollback feature.

4. I would recommend VandalProof to someone who wanted to stop vandalism in its tracks, but I might want to tell them that there may be a few bugs in it.

5. I like to use the rollback buttons, especially when they automatically give the vandal a warning. This saves time. It is a similar case with the other buttons. I also like the fact that you prod an article and tag articles for speedy deletion.

6. My least favorite part of VandalProof are those annoying runtime errors that cause VandalProof to close.

7. The feature that I would most like to see added to VandalProof is the revamped custom rollback feature. Squirepants101 01:06, 31 March 2007 (UTC)

[edit] Philippe's feedback

1. Rate VandalProof out of 10, 10 being the best. 10 - it rocks.

2. How often do you use VandalProof? Daily

3. Do you ever use other vandal fighting applications? If so, why? New Page Watcher, mostly for its very cool AfD feature.

7. Finally, what feature would you most like to see added to VandalProof? A "open AfD" feature... that's a feature that New Page Watcher got right... it's very cool.

Philippe 20:30, 31 March 2007 (UTC)

[edit] VP Feedback from Katalaveno

Comments from Katalaveno (talk • contribs • count)

1. Rate VandalProof out of 10, 10 being the best.
9 out of 10.
If this rating is based on a comparison of all the antivandal software tools, I must admit I have only used about 4 programs. In terms of the features I really want, VP is the best. (I have used the built in undo, godmodelight and popups). Popups is second.

2. How often do you use VandalProof?
Everytime I RC patrol which is almost everyday. Until I am made aware of a better tool, I will choose VP every time. I also still use popups at times to revert.

3. Do you ever use other vandal fighting applications? If so, why?
Popups. VP reverts all the vandal's edits to the previous editor's version. If that version is vandalism too, I tend to use popups on the article history page to revert to the last good version.

4. Would you recommend VandalProof to someone else if they want to help stop vandalism? Is there an application you'd recommend above it?
Already have recommended it to Wikipedia users. No... from what I know, VP is the best. I admit I have not used Twinkle or Vandal Fighter. Never felt the need to look at those as VP gives me all I want and need so far...

5. What if your favourite feature or part of VandalProof?
Speed. I know in vandal fighting one still must be careful and review every edit. I do and I have only made a few mistakes. But, overall, the whole process of looking at the RC, deciding if it is vandalism, rolling it back and notifying the user is fast with VP. For such a long time I manually viewed the RC page, manually viewed the diff, reverted the vandalism, and manually added my warning on the talk page. After I finally got approved for VP, I was a very happy camper. The vandals can be very fast, I like VP because it makes me fast too. After a few warnings, the vandals usually stop.

I also love the colour coding of the RSS feed RC list panels on the left. I like the ability to set preferences. I like the versatility of VP... you can use it for more than reverting vandalism.

6. What is your least favourite part of VandalProof?
It used to be the constant crashes. That was fixed. Seriously, can't think of much else.


7. Finally, what feature would you most like to see added to VandalProof?
I have two very specific changes I would love to see:

  1. I would like to see the RC panels on the left side and the diff window on the right each have the ability to be undocked as independent windows with the capability to be resized. I am thinking of a full screen diff window and a floating/smaller window on top displaying the recent changes.
  2. I would like to be able to view a vandal's/IP's contribs page and have the ability to revert en mass a block of edits. Reason: I have seen IP vandals copy/pasting in the exact same crap on 20 to 30 articles within a minute or two (e.g., the 'how to vandalize Wikipedia' text). In THOSE cases, I would like the ability to highlight a block of edits and revert them all. I realize this would be a potentially dangerous feature but in those specific cases it would be nice to be able to do this.

Just want to thank everyone who is involved with VP! Obviously, if Wikipedia allows anyone to edit any article as one of it core values, then we need VP and other tools like it to fight against users to seek to diminish the excellent work of our positive contributors.

And thanks for this chance to share some thoughts. It is appreciated. KatalavenoTC 01:05, 2 April 2007 (UTC)

[edit] VP poll

1. Rate VandalProof out of 10, 10 being the best.

8. The program is very helpful, even if it is a little rough around the edges sometimes.

2. How often do you use VandalProof?

A few times a week.

3. Do you ever use other vandal fighting applications? If so, why?

No.

4. Would you recommend VandalProof to someone else if they want to help stop vandalism? Is there an application you'd recommend above it?

Sure. And not that I know of.

5. What if your favourite feature or part of VandalProof?

The rollback and warn functions being integrated into one easy button is nice.

6. What is your least favourite part of VandalProof?

Going in to only, for instance, work on the main namespace as opposed to all the other namespaces requires an awful lot of clicking to unselect all the other namespaces. It would be nice if that were automated a little more.

7. Finally, what feature would you most like to see added to VandalProof?

See above. Also, I don't know all the template names by heart, so it might be nice if a descriptive phrase were used as opposed to just something like "drmafd". It would also be nice if the RC filter were tightened up, so that, for instance, "skyscraper" doesn't ping "rape". Although I think that's being done for 1.36.

Malerin 03:07, 2 April 2007 (UTC)
