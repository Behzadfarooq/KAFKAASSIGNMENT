Alessandro Varotari

Alessandro Varotari (April 4, 1588 – July 20, 1649), (also commonly known as il Padovanino) was a Venetian painter of the late-mannerist and early-baroque styles, best known for having mentored Pietro Liberi, Giulio Carpioni, and Bartolommeo Scaligero[1].

Born in Padua, hence his nickname, he was the son of the local painter and architect Dario Varotari, who likely provided his earliest training. Early paintings, such as The incredulity of St. Thomas[2], Virgin and Child[3], and the Penetecost[4] show the influence of Titian, and by 1614 he had relocated to Venice. He is known to have traveled to Rome twice (mid-1610s and 1625), where he was often employed in producing copies of major paintings from major Renaissance artists of the prior generation[5]. He painted a major battle canvas in ‘’The victory of the Carnutesi (Celts) over the Normans’’[6]
