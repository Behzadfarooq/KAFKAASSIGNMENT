Alex Cline

A veteran of numerous domestic and European tours as well as over eighty recordings, Alex has sonically contributed to the music of such bandleaders as Gregg Bendian, Tim Berne, Rob Blakeslee, Arthur Blythe, Bobby Bradford, James Carney, John Carter, Jey Clark, Dan Clucas, Buddy Collette, Mark Dresser, Harris Eisenstadt, Marty Ehrlich, Phillip Gelb, Vinny Golia, Burton Green, Phillip Greenlief, Henry Grimes, Richard Grossman, Charlie Haden, Stephanie Haynes, Joseph Jarman, Lynn Johnston, Henry Kaiser, Yusef Lateef, Steuart Liebig, Charles Lloyd, Kate McGarrey, George McMullen, Tom McNalley, Myra Melford, Roberto Miguel Miranda, Frank Morgan, Mark O’Leary, Andrea Parkins, Wayne Peet, Don Preston, John Rapson, Susan Rawcliffe, Dana Reason, Kim Richmond, William Roper, Adam Rudolph, Theo Saunders, Elliott Sharp, Judi Silvano, Wadada Leo Smith, G.E. Stinson, Horace Tapscott, Walter Thompson, Takako Uemura, Biggi Vinkeloe and Michael Vlatkovich.

Influenced by the drumming styles of Tony Williams, Jack DeJohnette and Sonship Theus as well as the extended percussion vocabularies of Frank Perry, Pierre Favre, and Tony Oxley, Cline began playing drums with his twin brother, guitarist Nels Cline, at age 11; their first combo was called Homogenized Goo. After beginning a musical association with woodwind artist Jamil Shabaka in 1976 as Duo Infinity, Alex’s musical activities began to flourish. In 1977 Alex became a member of Vinny Golia's group as well as the Julius Hemphill Trio (along with Baikida Carroll), formed the electric improvisational trio Spiral (with brother Nels and synthesizer player-multi-instrumentalist Brian Horner) and began performing solo percussion concerts.

In 1979, Alex, Nels Cline, bassist Eric von Essen, and violinist Jeff Gauthier formed Quartet Music, a group that enjoyed continued success in its performances and four recordings over an eleven-year period and was awarded grants from the National Endowment for the Arts and the California Arts Council. In 1982, Cline made his solo debut with Not Alone (9Winds), a double LP of percussion music. In 1987, he recorded The Lamp and The Star (ECM), his first album as a bandleader-composer. As the leader of his own group, the Alex Cline Ensemble, he can be heard on Sparks Fly Upward and The Constant Flame, two releases on Cryptogramophone Records, an LA-based independent creative-jazz label. Cline’s other improvisational collaborations include right of violet (9Winds) and The Other Shore (Cryptogramophone), both with Jeff Gauthier and ex-Shadowfax guitarist G.E. Stinson, and Cloud Plate (Cryptogramophone) with Stinson, vocalist Kaoru and koto player Miya Masaoka. Other groups Cline has led are Alex Cline’s Band of the Moment (with its ever-changing personnel) and the Rain Trio (with Eric Barber and Scott Walton). He has also been involved in duo percussion collaborations with Ron George, Peter Erskine, Christopher Garcia, Andrea Centazzo, Gregg Bendian, and Dan Morris, as well as involved in performing the works of composers Robert Eriksson, Harold Budd and David Means.

Alex has served as composer and/or performer for numerous modern dancers and dance companies in Los Angeles, including Margaret Schuette, Linda Fowler, the Momentum Company’s "Soundspace" concerts, Dance/LA, the UCLA Dance Company and has enjoyed a longstanding involvement with Will Salmon’s Open Gate Theatre company. He has participated in performance collaborations with visual artists Yoshio Ikezaki, Norton Wisdom, Kio Griffith and 2-Tu. He has worked on feature and cable television film soundtracks, done numerous sound workshops and percussion clinics, plus lecture-demonstrations on Asian metal percussion instruments. Cline has also been the curator of [Open Gate Theatre’s Sunday Evening Concerts series], a monthly new music/creative jazz showcase held in Eagle Rock, California since 1997. When he’s not working as a musician, Alex works as an interviewer/interview series developer-coordinator at the UCLA Library Center for Oral History Research. Template:Jazz, Free Jazz, Jazz Fusion
