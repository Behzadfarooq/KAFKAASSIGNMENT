[edit] AA player number

The game's homepage misleads with the "total registered players", because it actually refers to the total number of accounts. However, the older a game, the less that number has meaning. After over four and a half years, the vast majority of accounts is not used anymore. Accounts were abandoned, banned, automatically created, created as an additional account by one player, or never used at all. There are a lot more reasons why this number cannot tell us the actual amount of players. If you want to compare the popularity, simply look here: http://archive.gamespy.com/stats/ For the real amount of players, one might try to combine statistics such as from aaotracker. On the left-hand side in the middle, there's a "Buddylist" which tells us how many players currently playing are registered with aaotracker and how many aren't. Only ~43% are not registered. This service claims to have ~336,000 active registered players. If we add the number of unregistered players, it results in almost half a million regular players. Way too high, because that would mean only 1.2% of the regular players are currently playing and the average time a player plays per day is less than 20 minutes. americasarmy.com even claims 116 h/day in 30days average playtime, which would mean 3.8 hours a day, would it not? This would lead to 37 thousand regular players when combined with the number of currently playing players (I used 6000). Unfortunately, statistics are often wrong or labeled wrong. For example on homepage of aaotracker it reads "those registered players have logged over 3 BILLION player rounds, nearly 2 million player hours, and over 1100 Fan Sites have registered for our community listing." A player is lucky to play 15 rounds in an hour, but 1500 rounds/hour is nonsense. I personally estimate the true number of active players between 40 and 70 thousand players. Hope I could help you. --Theodore Witness 22:25, 28 January 2007 (UTC)

[edit] Edit summaries

When editing an article on Wikipedia there is a small field labeled "Edit summary" under the main edit-box. It looks like this:

The text written here will appear on the Recent changes page, in the page revision history, on the diff page, and in the watchlists of users who are watching that article. See m:Help:Edit summary for full information on this feature.

Filling in the edit summary field greatly helps your fellow contributors in understanding what you changed, so please always fill in the edit summary field, especially for big edits or when you are making subtle but important changes, like changing dates or numbers. Thank you. -- MarcoTolo 03:31, 7 February 2007 (UTC)
