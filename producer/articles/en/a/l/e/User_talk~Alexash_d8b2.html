[edit] AfD Nomination: Manifest Limited

Hello, and welcome to Wikipedia! We welcome and appreciate your contributions, but all Wikipedia articles must meet our criteria for inclusion (see What Wikipedia is not and Deletion policy). Since it does not seem to me that Manifest Limited meets these criteria, I have started a discussion about whether this article should be kept or deleted.

Your opinion on whether this article meets the inclusion criteria is welcome. Please contribute to the discussion by adding your comments at Wikipedia:Articles for deletion/Manifest Limited. Don't forget to add four tildes (~~~~) at the end of each of your comments to sign them.

Discussions such as these usually last five days. In the meantime, you are free to edit the content of the article. Please do not remove the "articles for deletion" template (the box at the top). When the discussion has concluded, an administrator will consider all comments and decide whether or not to delete the article. Iridescenti 17:12, 23 March 2007 (UTC)


If you want to contest the deletion, go to it's talk page - I've nominated it but it's now down to other editors (including yourself) to make the decision, which will be taken after five days (unless an admin decides it's such blatant advertising that it warrants immediate deletion). Leaving abusive messages on my talk page because I didn't respond immediately to something you posted on a page I'm not watching is not going to make the slightest difference one way or the other to the process.
The reason multinational companies are not AfD is that they are - generally - notable. Being AfD'd doesn't necessarily mean your article will be deleted, but you need to provide at least some evidence as to what makes this company more notable than any other company in the same business.
All of this is not a dig at you, but Wikipedia isn't a business directory and only companies that are genuinely notable have entries. As I say, it's down to you (and anyone else with an interest in the article) to prove that the article's notable enough to keep. - Iridescenti 19:18, 23 March 2007 (UTC)
