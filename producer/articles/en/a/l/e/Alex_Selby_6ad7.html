[edit] Biography

[edit] Early life

Alex Selby born Mexico,1882 is an Mexican actor/ pool player/ voice actor.

At the age of 9, Alex was considered to be a child pool prodigy, but didn't have the patience to stay with the game and never reached pro status. However his pool skills proved useful when he was cast as the young wannabe pool star in the mexican 1987 hit "8 balls of fire"

However Alex's foster father did not want his son in the buisness so early and pulled him out of any future films until he was "of age"


When he was nineteen he was accepted at Chabot College, the same prestigous theatre school that Tom Hanks went to. He studied there for three years before audioning for the role of Tico in the minor box office movie Mysteries of the seven seas.


[edit] Car accident

Shortly after completing "Bloody hell, Harry!" in Adalaide, Australia. Alex was hit by a speeding car driven by an intoxinated teenage Bradley Clempson, who was notorious around those parts for being a trouble maker. Alex was parylized afer the incident and could never walk again.

Alex quotes this as "the worst day of my life, makes you treasure each step you make, it may be your last"


[edit] Career

No longer able to move around freely, his manager Phil Dodd suggested voice work. Alex took the advice and auditioned for the role of Shin-Chan in the mexican dub. He got it straight a way and voiced the character until the series end.

He has since appeared in many videogames such as:

Total Overdose

Geist

Gun

and in the Television series The Grim Adventures of Billy and Mandy
