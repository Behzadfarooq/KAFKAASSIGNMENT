Alex Désert

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Alex Désert (born July 18, 1970) is a Haitian American actor who has appeared in TV series The Flash, Becker, Boy Meets World and the short-lived FOX series The Heights (called "Love and Rock" in France). His first strong television presence was on a controversial CBS program entitled "TV 101" where he co-starred with friend Matt LeBlanc.

From 1998 until 2004, he played the role of Jake Malinak, the blind magazine stand owner on the American sitcom Becker along side Ted Danson.

As a film actor he has appeared in Swingers, co-starring with Vince Vaughn and Jon Favreau, as well as in PCU with Jeremy Piven and Jon Favreau.

He is a founding member and lead singer of the well known Los Angeles ska band Hepcat. This ska band is known internationally and has developed a cult status separate and aside from his acting career.

Alex Désert has lent his voice in the role of "Zip" in Crystal Dynamic's Lara Croft Tomb Raider: Legend.
