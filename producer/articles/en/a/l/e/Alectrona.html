Alectrona

Alectrona was an early Greek goddess (of the sun), who became a daughter of Helios once myths of Classical antiquity came along. Her center of worship was at Rhodes, which is also the center of worship of her father, Helios. She was possibly a goddess of the morning or man's waking sense, which causes men to wake up in the morning, as her name means "rooster" (Alectrona is the feminine genitive of Αλεκτορ, alektor, the Greek word for "Rooster"), which would be an appropriate name for a solar goddess.
