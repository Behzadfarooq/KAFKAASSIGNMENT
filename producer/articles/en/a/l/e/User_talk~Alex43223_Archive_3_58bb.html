YSIT

Hi, Alex. I'm the one who made the Yellow Submarine Improv Troupe article (currently being considered for deletion), and I thought I might advocate a bit to save it from deletion; any of your attention and time you're willing to offer, I'm grateful for. The thing about improvational theater in general is that, unless you're Second City or something, it has a tendency not to show up on the radar. Some of the most successful troupes from Improv Boston and Improv Asylum don't show up at all when you use Google. Improvisational theater in general can be a tad incestuous, and you often see the same faces popping up over and over again. In this particular instance, no less than three troupes composed of entirely YSIT members have made their rounds at the Brattle Theater, Improv Boston, and Improv Asylum, which are pretty much the biggest local venues for improv here. The Jane Curtin bit was included mostly as interesting tidbit (she was not paid, by the way, but volunteered to do a session). I'll grant you that it isn't earth-shattering, but considering that its tendrils have reached everywhere from New York theater directors to Boston improvisors who have since moved to Chicago, I feel it has enough interest to justify an article. Because of the troupe's size and its membership's liklihood to go on to college theater, I get no end of questions about it. I can understand how this would create problems with verifiability or pointless edit wars, but I think the same thing could be said of any niche topic, and part of why I keep coming back to wikipedia is because of the information available on things that have a lot of specificity. I see that Irishguy and someone who is likely a current member of the troupe have had some edit wars and it is likely that Irishguy was in the right, but I don't think it justifies wholesale deletion of the article. If there are any specific changes you believe should be made that would improve the article's reliability or importance, please let me know and I will be happy to help however I can. Sorry for rambling on a bit. If you could contact me at jferrisjr_at_gmail.com, I'd be happy to have a conversation with you without cluttering up your page any further (I can also respond to e-mails a bit more quickly). I may be a bit late getting back to you tomorrow night, and I humbly request that the admins refrain from not deleting the YSIT article until at least then, if that's at all possible. Thank you again for your time and consideration -- Universal Traum 04:27, 21 February 2007 (UTC)
