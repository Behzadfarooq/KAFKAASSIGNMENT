Alex, real name Alexsandro de Souza, (born September 14, 1977 in Curitiba) is a Brazilian football (soccer) attacking midfielder, who is currently playing for Fenerbahçe S.K. in Turkey after being bought from Cruzeiro in 2004 for 5 million dollars.[2] He is formerly known for his role as team captain on the Brazilian National Team after rising to prominence with Palmeiras. He is a skillful left-footed player who is adept at taking free-kicks, penalties, and corners for his team, as well as scoring from the run of play. He has tallied numerous goals for every team he has played for and is also known for his playmaking skills; more often than not leading his current league in assists. His flashy dribbling and wise on-field decisions have made him a quick fan favorite at Fenerbahçe[3]. On April 9, 2006 he scored 2 goals in Turkcell Super League game against Sivasspor. The second one was his 250th career goal.

Many were disappointed with to see Alex left off of Brazil's 2006 World Cup squad[4][5][6] when the less proven Ricardinho, who had also recently be named the most hated player in Brazil by Placar[7], was selected. A fan campaign had been started prior to the World Cup in hopes to promote Alex's selection. [8] Alex played in 1997 FIFA World Youth Championship, 2000 Olympics football tournament, and had captained Brazil to the 2004 Copa América Championship. [9]

In 1999, IFFHS named Alex the 3rd best striker in the world.[10]

Alex's current contract expires at the end of the 2006-2007. Fenerbahçe have stated the desire to keep him.[11] Alex meanwhile, will also listen to offers from Beşiktaş.[12]
