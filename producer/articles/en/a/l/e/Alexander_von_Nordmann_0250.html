Alexander von Nordmann

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Alexander von Nordmann (May 24, 1803 - June 25, 1866) was a Finnish zoologist.

Nordmann was a professor at Odessa, and collected natural history specimens in southern Russia. He became professor of Zoology at the university of Helsinki in 1849.

The standard author abbreviation Nordm. may be used to indicate this person in citing a botanical name.
