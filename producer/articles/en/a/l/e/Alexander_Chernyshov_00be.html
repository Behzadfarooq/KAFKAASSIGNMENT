Aleksandr Alekseyevich Chernyshov (Russian: Александр Андреевич Чернышев; 21 August 1882–28 April 1940) was an electrical engineer. He was a graduate of Saint Petersburg Polytechnical Institute in 1907, where he remained until the end of his life. His research consisted of radio engineering and high-voltage techniques. He won the Lenin Prize in 1930.
