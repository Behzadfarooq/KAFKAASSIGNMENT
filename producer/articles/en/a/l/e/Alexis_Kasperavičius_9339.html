Alexis Kasperavičius

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Alexis Kasperavičius (aka Alex Kasper or Alex Kasperavicius) is a well known security consultant and trainer in the computer and legal security field as well as a multimedia producer.

Alexis was born on April 14, 1968 in Cleveland, Ohio) and raised in Los Angeles, California. Growing up in the Los Feliz district, Alexis was a technology enthusiast from early on, primarily interested in the telephone system, absorbing anything available on how the systems worked and eventually developed a good working knowledge of the entire system.

In the 1980's Alexis met Lewis DePayne, Kevin Mitnick, Dave Harrison, Lenny Dicicco, Terry Hardy and others. Discovering their mutual interest in telephones and computers, the group became fast friends and over the 1980’s and 1990’s all worked together on discovering various aspects of the telephone system during deregulation and the emerging Internet.

Alexis currently teaches on a variety of topics relating to Social Engineering and co-authored the Certified Social Engineering Prevention Specialist (CSEPS) workshop and certification along with Mitnick who jokingly refers to him as “my un-indicted co-conspirator.”

He currently lives in Los Angeles, California.

Produced video game Return Fire for 3DO and Time Warner Interactive, in 1995.

Lead developer and producer of Digital Dreamgirls in 1996.

Produced ([1]) Eyeball Eddie at the University of Southern California in 1999.

Founding producer of iWin.com (1999).

Co-founder of (Nexspace) (2000).

Co-hosted The Dark Side of the Internet on KFI (AM) with Kevin Mitnick (2001).

Co-founded Defensive Thinking (now Mitnick Security Consulting) with Mitnick (2003).
