[edit] Diacritics

Alensha, thanks for your note. Yes, we know about the diacritics problem. By now, every Wikipedia except the English-language one should now use Unicode, which supports almost all known characters in most known scripts. However, the English-language Wikipedia, because it was the first to be set up, and the largest, will be the last to be converted to Unicode; at the moment, it only supports ISO 8859-1 characters in article titles, and only supports non-8859-1 characters by using HTML entities in article Wikitext. However, this is all planned to be sorted soon, when the English Wikipedia will finally be converted to Unicode. This is why the "wrongtitle" template is so useful: by flagging articles now, we will greatly ease the task of finding and converting them when the Unicode recoding occurs.-- The Anome 13:59, Mar 21, 2005 (UTC)
