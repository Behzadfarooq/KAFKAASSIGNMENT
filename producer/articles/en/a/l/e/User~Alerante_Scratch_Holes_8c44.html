Holes is a Newbery Medal-winning novel written by Louis Sachar.

Spoiler warning: Plot and/or ending details follow.

[edit] Background

The novel presents two storylines separately from the main plot, both of which occur several decades beforehand.

The first focuses on Elya Yelnats, Stanley's "no-good-dirty-rotten-pig-stealing-great-great-grandfather", who never actually stole a pig at all. During his teenage years in Latvia, he wishes to marry Myra Menke, a beautiful but empty-headed lady pledged by her father to be married on her fifteenth birthday. However, he is up against Igor Barkov, a fifty-seven-year-old pig farmer, and Myra's father has expressed his desire for a fat pig.

[edit] Story

The plot revolves around Stanley Yelnats, a boy wrongly convicted of stealing the shoes of famous baseball player Clyde "Sweet Feet" Livingston. Faced with a choice between jail and a stay at Camp Green Lake in Texas, he picks the latter, only to realize that the so-called "lake" dried up over a century beforehand. Inmates must dig one hole every day in order to "build character", five feet wide and deep.
