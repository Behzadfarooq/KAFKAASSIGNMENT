Aletheuo is a band formed in July of 2006 in North Carolina. Although their primary genres are listed as Hard Rock, Metal, Progressive Rock, and Blues-Rock, they often experiment with elements from genres such as Jazz, Blues, Bluegrass, Fingerstyle, and Neo-Classical. Aletheuo is a Christian band, in that they uphold Christian morals in their music and at their performances. Major influences for the whole band include: Dream Theater, Megadeth, Alter Bridge, and The Jimi Hendrix Experience.

[edit] Origins of the Band's Name & Band Bio

αληθευων -> alhqeuwn -> aletheuo
Aletheuo (al-ayth-yoo'o) is a word from Koine Greek (transliterated into English) that means "To Speak or Profess The Truth."

Aletheuo was officially formed in July of 2006, but Nick, Stephen, and Ryan had previously played together. Ryan has played bass since 2002, though he has not taken many lessons. Nick had taken 5 years of fingerstyle guitar lessons, and 1 year of classical. Stephen had played guitar, bass, and drums (as well as sung) in the past. He began in the band playing drums until March of 2007, when he switched to guitar after having practiced and taken lessons in the metal/shred style. He is also in a high school choir class. Ben had taken mandolin lessons for about 1.5 years, and sings in the same choir class. Richard Trotter is in high school marching band and jazz band.

[edit] Songs

Aletheuo has recorded four songs (five if including a song done before Aletheuo was officially formed).

Blues Jam is a recorded jam session, based off of a few blues-rock riffs. The recording has no editing or anything done to it, to show what we really sound like when we just sit down and jam. Nick used a Fender Fat Strat for this recording. It's arguably more a combination of blues/rock/funk then strictly blues, but the band was unable to come up with a more fitting name.

The Song of Storms is our cover of a Koji Kondo song, found in some of the Legends of Zelda games. It was recorded all acoustic with guitar and mandolin.

The Demon Slayer Suite (just Demon Slayer for short) is an epic metal song that centers around the theme of the angel Michael fighting Satan to cast him out of Heaven, as mentioned in the book of Revelations. It is highly structured, featuring solos and multiple guitar harmonies. It comes in two movements: an calm introduction movement, called Rise of Michael, and the powerful main movement, called Demon Slayer. It is copyrighted (©) 2006. The spoken part in the intro is actually from Revelation 12:7-9, which reads:

7 And there was war in heaven, Michael and his angels waging war with the dragon. The dragon and his angels waged war, 8 and they were not strong enough, and there was no longer a place found for them in heaven. 9 And the great dragon was thrown down, the serpent of old who is called the devil and Satan, who deceives the whole world; he was thrown down to the earth, and his angels were thrown down with him.

The guitar was recorded on Nick's Washburn Prototype CTS. The drums were recorded with only four mics. The bass switches between Ryan's Ibanez in the first movement to Nick's Washburn T-25, but is all played by Ryan. Demon Slayer has had many minor changes done to it since the original recording. Changes include rerecording the guitar in the intro, adding the voices in the intro, and changing the voice reading the verses from Revelation, as well as various tweaks on the mixing.

The Time Has Come is the band's first finished original song with lyrics/singing. Its theme centers on the perspective of a Jew in Jesus's time who must decide whether to take that leap of faith and believe in Him. It is a mix of hard rock and metal, with the metal side unquestionably showing through at the end-of-song breakdown. The song features vocals by Ben with backing harmonies by Nick, with lyrics also written by Nick and Ben. It is copyrighted (©) 2007. The rhythm guitar was recorded with Nick's Washburn Prototype CTS & Stephen's Ibanez S-series.
