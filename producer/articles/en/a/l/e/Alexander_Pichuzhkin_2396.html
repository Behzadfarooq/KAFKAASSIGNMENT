Alexander Pichuzhkin

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Alexander Pichuzhkin (Russian: Александр Пичушкин) is a Moscow-based serial killer convicted in 2006 of the Bitsa Park murders. Pitchushkin claimed that he committed 61 murders (more than famous Russian serial killer Andrei Chikatilo) between 1992 and 2006. He admitted that wanted to spit further than Chikatilo and to kill 64 men, according to a number of chessboard squares.
