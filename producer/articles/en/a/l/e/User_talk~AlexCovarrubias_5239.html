About Mesoamérica, I was frustrated with the whole discussion and continuous reverts on Middle America (disambiguation), and I was afraid we were heading towards another edit war, and I spoke hastily to Corticopia. What I meant, is that there are two points of view (POV) which can be backed up with sources. I had the impression that both of you were trying to overrule the each other arguments with sources, where a middle ground could have been found. Moreover, I believe that sources should be classified or qualified: not all sources are equally valid, and one source versus a million sources will probably be best qualified as "infrequently this is the case". That was my issue with Mesoamerica. The geopolitical use of the term, while valid and sourced, is infrequent, in that Mesoamerica is an anthropological term first, and then a geopolitical term. By not using "qualifiers" (or adjectives) we give the impression that both definitions are equally used in synonymity.

So you claim it covers all the countries that the first two books did not, without actually being able to say where it says it. I'm not saying this is what happened, but if you can't provide a page number then this is what it looks like. You are the one who needs to come up with the specifics, not the people who question you. It is wrong to give that much "edit power" to a person with such little real interesent in the subject. One of our most important principles is that anyone can edit any article. Your point of view is not more important than anyone else's just because you are more interested in the topic. Nobody can own an article, and nobody can prove that they are more qualified than anyone else to edit. Kafziel Talk 17:52, 6 April 2007 (UTC)
I think my words were misunderstood. I never said I own the article, or that no one is entitled to edit the artile. Oddly enough, Kermakesh haven't edited the article at all, just "complained" about what he "thought" was not correct (the GCC states listed). I was complaining that the system is weak in the way it let editors advance their biases by tricking the system and playing with the rules (I still haven't seen any official policy indicating a source is "not valid" when a page number is missing). However, I appreciate a lot your help, since it was very usefult to end the issue yesterday. Thanks again. AlexCov ( Let's talk! ) 18:12, 6 April 2007 (UTC)
Well, a lot of people like to point out Wikipedia's flaws. I always tell them that if you can find a better Wiki, I'd like to see it. Besides, he's not tricking the system; I'm a person, not a robot, and I'm not easily fooled. I've seen a lot of this sort of thing over the years, and asking for a page number is quite normal. Perhaps you have difficulty finding a specific policy because it goes without saying; any time you quote a book, you should include chapter and page. That's not just on Wikipedia, that's in every research paper from high school and up. Any time you want to cite a source that is not readily available to everyone, you will need to be as specific as possible. On the other hand, having {{fact}} tags on an article is not a bad thing, either. They don't do any harm. If you don't find the sources, someone else will.
Anyway, I am glad I could help resolve the situation. Please just remember to be more patient, and don't assume that other users are biased or less qualified.
