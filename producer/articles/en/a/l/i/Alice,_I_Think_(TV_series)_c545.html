"Alice, I Think" redirects here. For the "Alice, I Think" books, see Alice, I Think (books).

Alice, I Think is a television series based on the Susan Juby book of the same name. Fifteen year old Alice is a "hyper-critical, socially-retarded narcissist with mind-numbingly poor judgement.", played by Carly McKillip. Alice's brother, MacGregor, is played by Connor Price. Alice's father, John, is played by Dan Payne, and her mother, Diane, is played by Rebecca Northan. Other characters include Marcus, Aubrey, Bob, Finn, Linda, Becky, Karen, Violet, Rosie and Geraldine. The show takes place in Smithers, British Columbia.

The show first aired on The Comedy Network on May 26, 2006. It currently airs Sundays at 7pm ET/PT on CTV, Fridays at 8pm ET/PT and Saturdays at 8:30pm ET/PT on The Comedy Network.

[edit] Trivia

In episode 12, Alice's therapist Bob brings a huge piece of cheese from Switzerland. It is actually norwegian Jarlsberg cheese.
