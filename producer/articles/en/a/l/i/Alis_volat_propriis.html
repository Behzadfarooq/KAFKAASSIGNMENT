Alis volat propriis

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Alis volat propriis is a Latin phrase meaning "She flies with her own wings" and is the state motto of Oregon.[1]

The motto was originally adopted by the Oregon Territorial Legislature in 1854 as a reference to the 1843 vote by Oregon Country settlers at Champoeg to form a provisional government independent of either the United States or Great Britain. In 1957, the Oregon Legislature changed the motto to "The Union", but in 1987, the legislature readopted the original motto.[2]
