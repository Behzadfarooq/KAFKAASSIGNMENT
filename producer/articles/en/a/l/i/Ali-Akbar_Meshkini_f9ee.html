Ayatollah Ali Akbar Feyz-Ani (born 1922 in Meshkin),[1] famously known as Meshkini, is an Iranian cleric and politician. As of 2007, he is the chairman of the Iranian Assembly of Experts, which selects the Supreme Leader of Iran and supervises his activities. He is the author of many books on Islamic jurisprudence and general issues of Islam.

Meshkini is the chair of Society of Seminary Teachers of Qom.[2] Despite his old age and poor health[3], he became a candidate for re-election in 2006 Assembly of Experts elections.[4]

After the Israel-Lebanon conflict, Meshkini stated that the "victory of the Lebanese Hizbollah over the Zionist regime was a divine phenomenon."[5]
