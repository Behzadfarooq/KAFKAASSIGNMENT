Alison Lambert studied clarinet at the Royal Northern College of Music where she was a major scholarship recipient, before accepting a scholarship to study with Professor Wolfgang Meyer in Karlsruhe, Germany.

Upon graduation she moved to Israel to take up the position of Principal Bass Clarinet with the Jerusalem Symphony Orchestra under the baton of Lawrence Foster, with whom she toured extensively, also regularly broadcasting live on Israeli television and radio. Successive international tours include Schleswig Holstein Music Festival and Salzburg Music Festival. Alison has appeared as soloist internationally with the Hallé Orchestra, Hillel Orchestra, Jerusalem, Baden-Baden Philharmonic Orchestra, Collegium Musicum Karlsruhe and Collegium Musicum Heidelberg. Radio broadcasts include a live performance for BBC Proms/Radio 3 of Magnus Lindberg’s Clarinet Quintet.

She currently works regularly as clarinettist with the RSC and her many chamber music concerts have seen her appear alongside such artists as John Nettles, Julian Lloyd Webber and most recently with Sir Willard White as part of the Bromsgrove Festival.

Alison has been clarinet tutor at the Royal Welsh College of Music & Drama since September 2003.
