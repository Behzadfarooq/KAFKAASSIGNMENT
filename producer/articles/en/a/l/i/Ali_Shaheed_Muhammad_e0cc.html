Ali Shaheed Muhammad (born August 11, 1970, Brooklyn, New York) is a hip-hop DJ who enjoyed moderate fame as a member of A Tribe Called Quest. With Q-Tip and Phife Dawg, the group released five albums from 1990 to 1998. After they disbanded, Ali formed the R&B supergroup Lucy Pearl with refugees from En Vogue (Dawn Robinson) and Tony! Toni! Toné! (Raphael Saadiq), releasing one album in 2000. On October 12, 2004 he released his debut solo album, Shaheedullah and Stereotypes. Ali, Q-Tip and Phife have since announced plans to record a new album as A Tribe Called Quest.
