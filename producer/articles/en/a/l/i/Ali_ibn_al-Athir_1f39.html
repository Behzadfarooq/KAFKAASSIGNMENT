Abu al-Hasan Ali ibn Muhammad ibn Muhammad[2], better known as Ali 'izz al-Din Ibn al- Athir al-Jazari[3] (1160 - 1233) was a muslim historian born in Cizre, a town in present-day Şırnak province in south-eastern Turkey, from the Ibn Athir family. According to the 1911 Edition of Encyclopaedia Britannica, he was born in Jazirat Ibn Umar in Kurdistan [3]. According to the Encyclopaedia of Islam, he was Kurdish [4].

[edit] Biography

He spent a scholarly life in Mosul, but often visited Baghdad. For a time he was with Saladin's army in Syria and later lived in Aleppo and Damascus. His chief work was a history of the world, al-Kamil fi at-tarikh (The Complete History). He includes some information on the Rus' people in his chronology.
