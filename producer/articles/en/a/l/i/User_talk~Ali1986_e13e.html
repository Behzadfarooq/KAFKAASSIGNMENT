[edit] Mazandaran

Hi. I don't recommend you revert my edits to Mazandaran-related articles. The opinion that Shahnameh Mazandaran is different from modern Mazandaran is rather prevalent amongst scholars, both medieval and modern. This is also mentioned in my copy of Shahnameh, the translation by Dick Davis. I have read it, in case you were wondering, and once I learn enough Persian, I hope to read the original version. Anyway, the previous versions were written very poorly and contained a lot of speculative passages without sources, which I am inclined to think were OR. Before you do anything, please discuss with me; hopefully we will be able to find common ground on this matter. The Behnam 19:00, 14 January 2007 (UTC)

Also, if you are able to source the information about Mazandarani culture & music on the Mazandarani People page, that would be an improvement. Even if your only sources for such information are Persian-language, it is acceptable so long as we have a few other Persian Wikipedians vouch that the sources do indeed say what they are cited as saying. The Behnam 21:59, 14 January 2007 (UTC)


Hi, thanks for the response. True, "div" means "demon" in both Mazandarani and Persian, but there is no reason to believe that the name was given because modern Mazandaran is the same as Mazandaran from the Shahnameh. Perhaps the names were given because people in Mazandaran thought that they lived in the Mazandaran mentioned in Shahnameh. It is even possible that the naming was not related at all to the Shahnameh. Anyway, I am very willing to correct the English in your contributions, to the best of my ability; I am not perfect either :) .

I have read a small amount of Ibn-i Isfandiyar's History of Tabaristan, though I look forward to reading it in its entirety. I will definitely add to the article you are creating as I read more about the Kingdom of Tabaria. While I understand that you may believe that Shahnameh's Mazandaran is identical to modern Mazandaran, it is important to remember that Wikipedia is about verifiability, not truth. I am able to cite numerous reliable sources for the view that the Mazandaran in Shahnameh appears different from modern Mazandaran. Even if we manage to find a variety of reliable sources equating the two Mazandarans, you should understand that this leads to describing both views in the articles. Usually, mainstream scholarly views are given the most attention in the articles, but if you are able to back-up your view with actual scholarly material as well, then it should also receive mention. Hence, both views would receive mention.

I'm sorry if this all seems harsh, but it wouldn't be right to exclude a common scholarly view from the articles. While I have met numerous Iranians who believe the two to be the same, I have yet to find scholarly material arguing that identification. I welcome you to provide such material to back-up your position, and I look forward to incorporating such information into the articles. The Behnam 08:32, 16 January 2007 (UTC)

[edit] Two Articles in need of your attention

There are two entries at Wikipedia, which have falsely created -- they are Turco-Persian and Turko-Persian Tradition. Both entries are factitious. I have requested the entries to be deleted. My reasons are:

The term Turko-Persian Tradition (or Turco-Persian) does not exists academically and it is a factitious entry! Check the Encyclopaedia Iranica to confirm -- The correct name for that culture is the Persianate culture not the "Turko-Persian". Turkophones (mostly of mixed race and Persianized in culture) only spoke in Turkic dialects and were in the military. That is not enough participation in creating and forming the culture to deserve the name "Turko-Persian Tradition" – This is misinformation. All the elements in that area, which have to do with tradition and culture, were drawn from the Iranian culture (Persian, Kurdish, Azari, Baluchi, Tajik, Luri, Gilaki, Talishi, Mazandarani, etc.), and the Islamic faith, not much Turkic elements (like shamanism, yurts etc.) were incorporated in. That is what makes the name "Turko-Persian" an imaginary one and therefore the entry should be deleted.

Any contributions would greatly appreciated. Bā Sepās Surena 02:25, 17 January 2007 (UTC)

[edit] Reliable sources

Wikipedia is an encyclopedia where the information you insert have to be reliable. In order to do this, you need source for what you insert. Currently, the sources you keep using are not verifiable, and even if you verify that such book exists because it is such an obscure and unreliable information, which is most probably a hoax it can not be used on Wikipedia. See WP:VERIFY, important points in there:

-> The burden of evidence lies with the editor who adds or restores material. Any material that is challenged or likely to be challenged needs a reliable source, which should be cited in the article. If an article topic has no reliable, third-party sources, Wikipedia should not have an article on it.

-> Articles should rely on reliable, third-party published sources with a reputation for fact-checking and accuracy

There is also a section for publications in other languages than English. It is generally preferred for the translation to be a published translation so editors can check and verify it themselves.

Therefore, I ask you to stop using unreliable and unverifiable sources. Many thanks for understanding this as it can save us a lot of time. --Rayis 14:59, 1 March 2007 (UTC)
