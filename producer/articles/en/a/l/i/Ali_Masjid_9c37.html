Ali Masjid

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

InsertformulahereAli Masjid Fort is a famous landmark located at the highest point of Khyber Pass in Pakistan. It is located around 10 miles east of the city of Landi Kotal (West of Peshawar) and has an elevation of 3,174 feet.

Fort Ali Masjid was named in memory of Hazrat Ali (peace be upon him) the cousin of Holy Prophet Hazrat Muhammad (peace be upon him and his family). A mosque and a shrine has been built here in the memory of Hazrat Ali (pbuh), who visited this place according to a local tradition. There is also a huge boulder which carries the marks of a hand believed to be that of Hazrat Ali (pbuh).

Even Khyber Pass has been named after the Khyber of Arabia, where Hazrat Ali (pbuh) accomplished a great deed of valour.
