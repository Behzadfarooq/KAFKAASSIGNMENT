Alisa Weilerstein (1982â€“ ) is an American cellist. She started playing the cello at age four and promptly showed her talent for the instrument performing her first public concert six months later. She made her debut at age 13 with the Cleveland Orchestra playing the Rococo Variations of Tchaikovsky. As a soloist she has performed with a number of other major orchestras and in musically significant venues on four continents. She also is active in chamber music and performs with her parents, violinist Donald Weilerstein and pianist Vivian Hornik Weilerstein, as the Weilerstein Trio, currently the trio in residence at the New England Conservatory in Boston.

Alisa Weilerstein has received a number of honors. In 2000-2001 she won an Avery Fisher Career Grant and was selected to play in the ECHO "Rising Stars" program and Chamber Music Society II, the young artists' program of the Chamber Music Society of Lincoln Center. In 2006 she was awarded the Leonard Bernstein Prize at the Schleswig-Holstein Music Festival.

Her life has not been all music. In 2004 she graduated from Columbia University in New York City with a BA in Russian history.
