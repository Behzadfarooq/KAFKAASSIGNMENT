Alistair Cockburn

Alistair Cockburn (name pronounced "Co-burn", in the Scottish way) is one of the initiators of the Agile movement in software development. He was one of more than 15 co-authors, in 2001, of the Agile software development manifesto. In 2005, he helped co-author the agile PM Declaration of Interdependence.

Cockburn received his PhD degree from the University of Oslo in 2003. He is a principal expositor of the Use case for documenting business processes and behavioral requirements for software, and inventor of the Cockburn Scale for categorizing software projects.

The methodologies in the Crystal family (Crystal Clear), described by Alistair Cockburn, are considered examples of lightweight methodology.
