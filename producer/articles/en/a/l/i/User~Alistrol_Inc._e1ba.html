Q-What is normal blood pressure?

Normal blood pressure is less than 130 mm Hg systolic (as the heart beats) and less than 85 mm Hg diastolic (as the heart relaxes)

Q-What is high blood pressure?

Blood pressure is considered high if it is above the optimal levels. Blood pressure of 140/90 mm Hg or higher is considered high blood pressure. High blood pressure is defined by the College of Family Physicians of Canada as "hypertension that occurs when your blood moves through your arteries at a higher pressure than normal" (CFPCA, 2006).

Q- Why high blood pressure is harmful?

It is estimated that one in every four Americans has high blood pressure. Once it develops it can last a lifetime. It is considered as a silent killer as it has no warning signs or symptoms. High blood pressure has harmful affects on all the body systems especially on vital organs, i.e brain, heart and kidneys. Hypertension increases the risk of developing heart diseases. It also is associated with kidney diseases and stroke.


Q-Who can develop High Blood Pressure?

Anyone can develop high blood pressure but it is more common in: • African Americans- get it earlier and more often then Caucasians. • Old age- 60% of Americans over 60 have hypertension. • Overweight. • Family history of high blood pressure •

Q- How do I know that I have High Blood pressure?

Some people with high blood pressure are asymptomatic, whereas others may experience headache, blurring of vision, fatigue, palpitation, numbness, numbness and tingling of feet and hand, etc.

Q- How can my doctor know that I have High Blood pressure?

Your doctor may take measurements of blood pressure on several occasions. A persistent high reading of 140/90 or higher indicates high blood pressure.

Q- How high blood pressure affects the Brain?

Hypertension or high blood pressure is a major cause of stroke. Increased pressure in the blood vessels can rupture it thereby causing bleeding in the brain. High blood pressure promotes clotting which can block the narrowed arteries in the brain.

Q- How high blood pressure affects the heart?

High blood pressure is a known cause of heart diseases. It may deprive the heart muscles of oxygen, causing chest pain, also called as angina. If the blood flow to the heart is totally blocked, it is called heart attack. High blood pressure can also result in heart failure, a condition when the heart is unable to pump enough blood to meet the body’s requirement.

Q- How high blood pressure affects the Kidney?

Kidneys functions to filter the blood remove and dispose the toxins from the body. High blood pressure may constrict the blood vessel wall making filtration difficult. As a result, toxins may accumulate in the body. In severe cases, the kidneys may fail altogether.

Q- How high blood pressure affects the eyes?

Blood vessels in the eye may break and bleed due to high blood pressure. It may lead to blurred vision or even blindness.


Q- How is high blood pressure treated?

High blood pressure can be treated by lifestyle changes, weight management and medications.


Q-How can I prevent High blood pressure?

High blood pressure is preventable by modifying few factors. • Adopt a healthy life style • Limit sodium • Weight management • Healthy eating • Exercise • Avoid alcohol and smoking • Manage Stress

Q: What causes high blood pressure?

The College of Family Physicians of Canada state the "exact cause isn't known" because high blood pressure is subject to change often during the day depending on many factors" (CFPCA, 2006). Therefore the College of Family Physicians strongly urges that you consult with a physician in order to determine whether you have high blood pressure, and if so how to prevent heart disease and stroke (CFPCA, 2006). It is recommended by the College of Family Physicians that you "should have your blood pressure taken once every year or two by your doctor" (CFPCA, 2006).
