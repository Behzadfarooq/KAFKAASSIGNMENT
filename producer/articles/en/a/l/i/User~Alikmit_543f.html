Ali Khademhosseini is an Assistant Professor of Medicine and Health Sciences and Technology at Harvard-MIT's Division of Health Sciences and Technology and the Harvard Medical School. He has developed a number of apporaches based on micro- and nanoscale technologies to control cellular behavior with particular emphasis in developing microscale biomaterials and engineering systems for tissue engineering and drug delivery. These approaches may be important for generating artificial organs for patient therapy. His research has been published in a number of highly cited manuscripts in respected journals and patented. He received his Ph.D. in bioengineering from MIT under ther supervision of Prof. Robert Langer, and MASc and BSc in chemical and biomedical engineering from University of Toronto while working with Prof. Michael Sefton and Prof. Peter Zandstra.
