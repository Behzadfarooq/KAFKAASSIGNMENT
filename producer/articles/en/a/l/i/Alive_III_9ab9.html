Alive III is a live album released by the American hard rock band Kiss in 1993, and certified gold in 1994.

The recording of Alive III took place over multiple dates (in Cleveland, Detroit and Indianapolis) during the band's 1992 tour in support of Revenge. It was the first live album the band had released since 1977's Alive II, and the first live recordings released since 1985's "Animalize Live Uncensored" concert film. The recording of "I Was Made For Loving You", was actually recorded at one of the bands' soundchecks, but had the crowd overdubbed in the song to make it appear as if it is "live". Also the fact that keyboardist Derek Sherinian appears on Alive III leads to much speculation, that they faked vocal parts on stage, more specifically Gene Simmons, as he did on the "Hot In The Shade" tour in 1990 which is proven in bootleg videos from two of the Auburn Hills concerts which also are not mixed whatsoever, and also that the band is very secretive about having side keyboard players on possibly many of their tours through the 80's and early 90's. Though this may seem negative, Kiss fans are no stranger to the fact that most of Alive I and Alive II had certain parts doctored in the studio, and that some of the songs are not even recorded "live" in concert but at Electric Ladyland studios with Eddie Kramer.

The liner notes of Alive III include a family tree showing the various Kiss lineups from 1973-1993, as well as bands that the then-current and former members of Kiss were in. It was designed by the band's Japanese fan club.

The re-release of Alive III (as part of the Kiss Alive! 1975-2000 box set) contains an additional track, "Take It Off," as do the international CD and US vinyl versions of the album.
