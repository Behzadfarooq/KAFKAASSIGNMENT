[edit] Introduction to Covenantal theology

Presbyterian theology is similar with covenant theology. It has its roots in classical and historical attachments from theologians as far back as the early Church fathers. St. Augustine was one of the early church fathers who believed that God in his divine will and act decreed or covenanted with mankind as from the Garden of Eden when Man fall into sin. Within then, the promise of the ‘’seed of the woman’’ God decreed His own futuristic plan of redemption by the atonement of the promised seed who will bruised the head of the enemy that is the symbolic representation in the serpent in the Garden. That tradition goes down along with other luminous church theologians like John Calvin the author of the Institutes of Theology, a vast and clearly defined works of art and Christian writings in the definition of the classical covenant theology position as followed by many other theologians in the covenanted believing churches.

In history and following, it is possibly the earliest written and widely used form of creed after the Apostolic Creed and confession of Westminster Abbey for the Presbyterian churches and what it stands for by its covenant theological position, however there are claims by the earliest Anabaptists and other believers and followers of the teachings of Christ who are unrelated historically or any wise with that dogmatically thinking and believing system.

Of course theologically speaking there are too many systems as derived from the Bible and we acknowledge it as “Biblical Theology” by some or others who only regarded the Bible as a part and parcel of God given system within the scope of traditions and important church Fathers teachings whose succession and divine authority was instituted and originated from God through Jesus and the 12 apostles.

Modern Evangelicals and believers would like to disagree and debate too, that the Grace of God was fundamental to the understanding of faith in Jesus as revealed all through the Bible from Genesis to Revelation within all the 66 books of the Bible, and many different system of theological distinctions were emphasized in contrast with covenantal theology. Fundamental to the core of disagreement is the approach of Rationalism and Armenian thinking that contrasted with the dogmatic approach taken by covenantal theologians. For instance, theology is taken from the Bible without regard to a complete closed system from “knowing it all to blindly accepting all”. Rather it works from the inquirer to search and find for facts and reality in the Bible whether such things are so as recorded in the Bible.
