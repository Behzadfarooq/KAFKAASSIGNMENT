I'm afraid the text must either be licensed under the GFDL, or permission must be granted so freely that it is compatible with the GFDL (such as being released into the public domain). Permission to reproduce is not sufficient, as we have no legal right to change the text itself, for example. You might try emailing the author to ask if he (or she) would be willing to license the text under the GFDL. Have a look at Wikipedia:Copyrights for more info. (P.S. Online, typing in all-capitals is generally viewed as "shouting" and can be harder to read; see Netiquette). — Matt Crypto 18:30, 14 December 2005 (UTC)
