"Alive" is the other part of the double A-side along with "I'm Gonna Be Alright" (Track Masters Remix), the second and final single from Jennifer Lopez' remix album, J to tha L-O!: The Remixes. Released in 2002, the single failed to chart in the U.S. becoming Lopez' first single to do so. However, it peaked at number one on the Hot Dance Singles Sales chart and became another top five hit on the Hot Dance Club Play chart for her. The music video was directed by Jim Gable.

[edit] Song information

The song was written by Lopez, her then-husband, Cris Judd, and Cory Rooney (which also produced the track). "Alive" was written to the soundtrack of Lopez' film Enough (although it was not included on the soundtrack album). Following the themes of that film, the song is about the struggles of battered women, who are able to overcome their abusive husbands to make it out alive. To explain the message of the song, Lopez sings, "The strength to stand alone... the power of not knowing and letting go... I guess I've found my way it's simple when it's right... Feeling lucky just to be here tonight... And happy just be me and be alive." The song has become an anthem for battered women across the country and several women have written to Lopez about how that song has affected them. On an episode of The Oprah Winfrey Show, a woman even came up to Lopez and explained how that song kept her going even after the abuses she endured.

[edit] Awards

In 2002, Jennifer Lopez won a Latin Billboard Award, in the Best Selling Dance Single category, with the Thunderpuss Remix of the song.
