Aline d'Arbrant

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Aline d' Arbrant , french feminist writer, born in Nancy in 1952, author of The Gynarchy .

Aline d' Arbrant, near of American Valerie Solanas she met on several occasions in the USA after her release from jail, today is the main theorist of gynarchy, extremist feminist philosophy not to be confused with gynocracy (women's power) or with matriarchy (mothers power).

The gynarchy of Aline d' Arbrant preaches an absolute women's power whose inescapable purpose would be, according to it, a lesbian golden age where the woman would not even need men sexually, this one then would find blooming and happiness by serving the powerful lesbian sorority. These ideas are exposed clearly and with conviction in her main theoretical work: The Gynarchy , already translated from french into English and Italian.

Aline d' Arbrant , which was an active member of the french Circle of Omphale and of the swiss Gynarchy Club , is the foundress of the society Gynarchy International [1] which has the role to diffuse her ideas throughout the world. It is also the author of a dozen novels, published initially with the Diachronic Editions, but now exhausted in French language. They are now available in the form of electronic books on the site of Gynarchy International [2]. Among those:

  1. Diary of a Bitch ,
  2. Anne and her slaves (also available in illustrated edition),
  3. When Woman Took Power .
