Alicia is a town in Lawrence County, Arkansas, United States. The population was 145 at the 2000 census.

[edit] Geography

Alicia is located at 35°53′40″N, 91°5′1″W (35.894482, -91.083663)GR1.

According to the United States Census Bureau, the town has a total area of 0.3 km² (0.1 mi²), all land.

[edit] Demographics

As of the censusGR2 of 2000, there were 145 people residing in the town, consisting of 46 families in 53 households. The population density was 430.7/km² (1,126.7/mi²). There were 61 housing units at an average density of 181.2/km² (474.0/mi²). The racial makeup of the town was 100.00% White.

Of the 53 households, 26.4% had children under the age of 18 living with them, 67.9% were married couples living together, 13.2% had a female householder with no husband present, and 13.2% were non-families. 13.2% of all households were made up of individuals; 3.8% were comprised of a lone individual over the age of 65. The average household size was 2.74 and the average family size was 2.96.

Distribution of the population by age was fairly even, with 24.8% under the age of 18, 9.7% from 18 to 24, 24.1% from 25 to 44, 25.5% from 45 to 64, and 15.9% who were 65 years of age or older. The median age was 37 years. For every 100 females there were 107.1 males. For every 100 females age 18 and over, there were 98.2 males.

The median income for a household in the town was $20,833 and the median income for a family was $22,083. Males had a median income of $20,250, compared with $25,313 for females. The per capita income for the town was $12,942. 10.0% of families and 20.4% of the population were living below the poverty line, including 72.2% of residents under eighteen and none of those over 64.
