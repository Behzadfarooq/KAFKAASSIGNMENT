Alice Beer

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Alice Beer (born 1 May 1965 in England) is a British television presenter

A trained journalist, Beer's first job in 1987 was as a secretary on the BBC programme That's Life!. Since then she has worked her way up to being a researcher, producer on Kilroy and Gloria Live. Finally from 1992 until 1999 she was co presenter on the BBC consumer programme Watchdog with Anne Robinson.

After leaving Watchdog, she has lead a roving reporters life, appearing on shows such as "Healthcheck", "Face Value", "Real Rakeovers", as well as reporting for programmes such as "Holiday" and "Summer Holiday"

She was also a regular presenter on The Heaven and Earth Show, and did two series of Hot Property for Five. She currently presents a programme for BBC London 94.9

Between December 2004 and February 2005, she presented a promotional video for Top Up TV . The looped film ran 19 hours each day on the Top Up TV Sampler channel. The channel was replaced by Xtraview before closing altogether in September 2005.

[edit] Trivia

Fellow presenter and one time Daily Mirror editor Anne Robinson once wrote a newspaper article trying to find her a boyfriend. Alice and her partner Paul (the Chief Executive of large media company Unique) live in London, and are parents of twin baby girls, Phoebe and Dora (born April 2003) [1]. Her hobbies include photography.
