Algarvia

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Algarvia (from Algarve + ia) is a parish in the district of Nordeste in the Azores. The main industry is agriculture. It is connected with a road linking to Ribeira Grande - Nordeste. The mountains are to the south. On July 16, 2002, the parish was split from Nordestinho and also created Santo António de Nordestinho and São Pedro de Nordestinho.

[edit] Nearest parishes

  • Santo António de Nordestinho
  • São Pedro de Nordestinho

[edit] Geography

  • Location:
    • Latitude: 37.85 (37°51') N
    • Longitude: 25.233 (25°14') W
  • Altitude: 212 m (698 ft)

Algarvia has a school, and a square (praça).
