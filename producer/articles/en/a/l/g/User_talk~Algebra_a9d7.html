as user la-2, and being algebra, I'd like to know if you could help me translating the names of the major modern math branches. Like algebraic geometry, would that be algebraicarum geometry, how would you do differential topology?, group theory? sttistics? etc...

Apologies for the late reply. I do not believe that the Romans had very many branches of mathematics. Mathematics itself is 'mathematica' and geometry is 'geometria'. As for group theory, that would probably be 'ratio globus' (ratio meaning theory, and globus meaning crowd or cluster). That's all I'm aware of. Algebra 04:33, 28 January 2006 (UTC)
