Algebra homomorphism

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

A homomorphism between two algebras over a field K, A and B, is a map such that for all k in K and x,y in A,

  • F(kx) = kF(x)
  • F(x + y) = F(x) + F(y)
  • F(xy) = F(x)F(y)

If F is injective then F is said to be an isomorphism between A and B.
