Algoasaurus (IPA: /ælˌgoʊəˈsɔɹəs/; meaning "Algoa Bay reptile") was a genus of sauropod dinosaur from the Tithonian-early Valanginian-age Late Jurassic-Early Cretaceous Upper Kirkwood Formation of Cape Province, South Africa. It was a neosauropod; although it has often been assigned to the Titanosauridae,[1][2] there is no evidence for this, and recent reviews have considered it to be an indeterminate sauropod.[3][4]

The type species, A. bauri, was named by Robert Broom in 1904 from a dorsal vertebra, femur and an ungual phalanx. The fossils were recovered in 1903 from a quarry by workmen who did not recognise them as dinosaur specimens, so many of the samples were made into bricks and thus destroyed.[5] The animal may have been around 9 m (30 ft) long when it died.[6]
