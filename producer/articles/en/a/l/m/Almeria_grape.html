Almeria grape

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

You have new messages (last change).

The Almeria grape is a mild white grape, grown in both the US and Spain. It is unusual among cultivated grapevines in that it is pistillate, and requires a second grape cultivar to provide pollen, but has remained in cultivation for centuries despite this due to its quality as a table grape.
