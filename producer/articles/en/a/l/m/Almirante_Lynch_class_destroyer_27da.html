Almirante Lynch class destroyer

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

The Almirante Lynch class destroyers were a group of ships built for the Chilean Navy just before World War I. The Chileans had long been customers of British shipyards and ordered six ships from J. Samuel White in 1911. These destroyers were larger and more powerful that contemporary British destroyers. Two ships were delivered before the outbreak of war and served in the Chilean Navy until 1945. The remaining four ships were purchased by the British in 1914 and fought in the Royal Navy during World War I as the Faulknor class Flotilla leaders. The three surving ships were re-sold to Chile but being worn out were scrapped in 1933

[edit] General Characteristics

  • Displacement: 1430 tons standard, 1850 tons full load
  • Length: 101 m
  • Beam: 9.9 m
  • Draught: 3.35 m
  • Machinery: 6 Foster-White Mixed fired boilers, 3 shaft Parsons direct turbines, 30,000 hp
  • Speed: 31 knots
  • Range: 4,205 nm at 15 knots
  • Armament
    • 6 - 4 in guns ( ex British ships had 2-4.7 inch & 2-4 inch guns)
    • 4 - Machine guns ( 2 pom poms in ex British ships)
    • 6 - 21 inch torpedo tubes (4 in ex British ships)
  • Crew: 160 (197 in ex British ships)
