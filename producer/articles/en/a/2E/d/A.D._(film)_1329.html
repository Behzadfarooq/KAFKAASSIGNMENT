A.D. (film)

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

A.D. (Anno Domini) is a British/Italian miniseries from 1985 in 6 parts which tells the Acts of the Apostles. It was adapted from Anthony Burgess's novel The Kingdom of the Wicked, which was itself a sequel to Burgess's book Man Of Nazareth, which Burgess adapted from his teleplay for Jesus of Nazareth. Strictly speaking, A.D. is not a sequel to Jesus of Nazareth, as both series were made by different production companies and feature different actors (James Mason and Ian McShane appear in both series, but play different characters).

[edit] Plot

The Story tells the life histories about Saint Peter and Paul of Tarsus after the Crucifixion of Jesus, and their individual fates in the old Rome in time of Persecution of Christians.
