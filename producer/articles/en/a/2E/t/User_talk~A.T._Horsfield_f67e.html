I want to thank you for all of the time and effort you've been putting in on edits to pages covering northwestern New Jersey. Our effort consists of over two dozen people, bound together by a common interest and just about nothing else. There are no rules and even fewer regulations, but if you want to chip in or ask for some help about a subject of interest, this might bethe place for you. Alansohn 03:58, 10 April 2006 (UTC)

RE: your comment on my talk page. Balesville is a variation stemming from the Bale brothers...However, modern parlance and common usage is "Baleville." I think the use of the current usage is more correct. —ExplorerCDT 14:52, 28 September 2006 (UTC)

    • Crazy Enough, If I remember, the Post Office approved the name "Baleville" in the 1870s (formerly the P.O. was "Pleasant Valley") and the Board of Geographic Names called it "Balesville", or vice versa. Both are still used, don't get me wrong, but more people (including Hampton Township's government) call it "Baleville." That's government for you.—ExplorerCDT 15:35, 26 October 2006 (UTC)
