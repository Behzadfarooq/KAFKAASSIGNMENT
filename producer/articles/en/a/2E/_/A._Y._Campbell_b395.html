A. Y. Campbell

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Archibald Young Campbell (1885-1958) was a University of Cambridge classicist, translator, and published poet of the 1920s and 1930s.

His works, including Horace (1924) (also Horace Odes and Epodes, University of Liverpool Press, 1953) and The Agamemnon of Aeschylus (1936), are remembered, if subject to some scholarly amusement.
