Arthur Hendriks, born Arthur Lemiere Hendriks in 1922 was a Jamaican poet.

[edit] Life

Hendriks was educated at Jamaica College, Kingston and pursued a career in poetry. He was particularly well known for his contributions to the Christian Science Monitor, The Daily Gleaner - for which he also contributed as a columnist and literary critic - and BIM (Barbadian Literary Magazine).

He also experienced an extensive broadcasting career in which he became Director of Caribbean Broadcasting (Barbados), Thompson Television (London) and Trinidad and Tobago Television. Also in 1965 he was still holding a post as General Manager and Consultant to the Jamaican Broadcasting Company.

[edit] Works

Despite much of his work depicting the life and landscape in Jamaica his poetry is far too diverse to be described as regionally orientated. Some of his notable published works were:

  • These Green Islands, and Other Poems (Bolivar Press, 1971)
  • To Speak Simply: Selected Poems 1961-1986 (Hippopotamus Press, 1988)
  • On this mountain: and Other Poems (Deutsch, 1965)
  • Check (Headland Publications, 1988 - compilation with Harris and Hendricks)
  • Naked Ghost and Other Poems (Outposts Pubns., 1984)
  • The Islanders, and Other Poems (Savacou Co-operative, 1983)
  • Muet (Outposts Pubns., 1971)
  • Madonna of the Unknown Nation (Workshop P, N4, 1974)

Hendriks died in 1992 having been father to three daughters.
