[edit] It's AJ, not A.J

If you look on any wrestling site, you will see that's how it's spelled. How hard is it to check things, before reverting back or whatever happened?!

Look on TNA's official site, it's spelled AJ. Just about every site lists him as "AJ" not "A.J". I realize the periods make it look neater or whatever, but it simply isn't how his name is spelled.

"A.J. Styles" is grammatically correct. As stated in Wikipedia:Naming conventions (common names), the most common name should not be used where it is erroneous, e.g. tidal wave. McPhail 18:18, 26 June 2006 (UTC)


I still don't see why it's a big deal! Why make it grammatically correct, when it is NOT how the name is spelled by wrestling companies? The right way to spell it, should be the version on Wikipedia... not some wrong way, just because it's "grammatically correct".

But some wrestling companies spell his name "A.J.", e.g. NWA Wildside. Moreover, "AJ" is patently incorrect, given that he has acknowledged that it is his initials. McPhail 00:13, 5 July 2006 (UTC)
I believe that it should be spelled AJ. Sure, it may not be grammatically correct, but neither is Rhyno for example. Also, in the wikipedia article for CM Punk it says CM Punk and not C.M. Punk. 83.233.58.34 23:30, 16 December 2006 (UTC)
CM ain't initials. --Aaru Bui DII 09:59, 17 December 2006 (UTC)
Fair enough. Take a look at http://www.stylesclash.net/, which is apparently Styles's official site. They write both AJ and A.J. (and even Aj in the title). Since it's mentioned as both, it should probably be A.J. styles since that is grammatically correct. I stand corrected. 83.233.58.35 06:24, 26 February 2007 (UTC)
