Why Wikipedia is Inspiring

  • Knowledge should never have a price tag (Microsoft will gladly sell me Encarta for $75). The opportunity to learn must be open to everyone, regardless of economic condition.
  • Wikipedia is where individuals are changing the world. This community is supported by many hard-working people who devote much of their own time without any expectation of material rewards.
  • Internet encyclopaedias go head to head
"Move over, Britannica!"
  • MyWikiBiz: A company that actually charges business to create a "NPOV" article to advertise them on Wikipedia - all for the "low" price of $49-$99.
Wikipedian Work
  • I usually use Wikipedia as reference source to compare with the research materials I have, and editing sometimes is an effective study strategy, since the information becomes imprinted onto the mind. When browsing articles, I sometimes sneak in an edit or two if I find something in my study materials worthy of inclusion in the article.
  • Writing articles is my main focus at present. I won't usually edit a well-established article too much - I prefer to find little-known but interesting subject with which to start a new article or work to expand current stubs.
