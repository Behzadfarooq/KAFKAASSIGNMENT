AC Legnago Salus is an Italian football club located in Legnago (VR), Veneto. It currently plays in Promozione.

[edit] History

The club was founded in 1921 and changed names many times. In 1945-46 season Legnago won the Serie C championship, but lost the final match to get the access to Serie B championship. The club played in Quarta serie championship (now called Serie D) in three different periods. The 1st from 1952 to 1954, then from 1971 to 1980, and at last from 1993 to 2002. In 1971-72 season the club reached the 2nd place and got the access to the play-off; Legnago lost the match 1-0 against Vigevano, so it could not play in Serie C. The match was played in Brescia in front of 7000 spectators. In 1994-95 season Legnago reached the 3rd place behind Treviso and Triestina with Gigi Manganotti as manager. On 30th November 1997 has been signed the record number of spectators, 1200 in the match Legnago-Trento. After a long period of time in Serie D, at the end of 2001-02 season the club was retroceded to Eccellenza, and one year later (2003) was retroceded to Promozione. Legnago Salus is currently 1st in the 2006-07 season, and is trying to return into upper series.
