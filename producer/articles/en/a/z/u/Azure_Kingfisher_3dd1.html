The Azure Kingfisher Alcedo azurea is a small kingfisher (17-19 cm), in the river kingfisher family, Alcedinidae. It is found in Northern and Eastern Australia and Tasmania.

Very colourful, deep blue to azure back, white to buff spot on side of neck and throat, rufous-buff with some blue-violet streaks on breast and flanks; red feet with only two forward toes. A fron view reveals two large white spots which look rather like large eyes.

There are three races: A. azurea in east and southeast Australia; A. ruficollaris in northern Australia from Cape York to the Kimberly, and A. diemenesis in Tasmania.

Often difficult to see until it quickly darts from a perch above water. Feeds on yabbies and small fish.

Habitat includes the banks of vegetated creeks, lakes, swamps, tidal estuaries and mangroves.

Nest in a chamber up to 1 metre long in an earthen creek bank. 5-7 white, rounded, glossy eggs. Voice is a high-pitched, shrill, 'pseet-pseet'.
