Azzedine Alaia

Azzedine Alaia (Arabic:عز الدين عليّة , original - Alaïa, pronunciation - Aliya) is a Tunisian-born fashion designer, most successful in the 1980s.

Azzedine Alaia was born in Tunis, Tunisia in 1940. He studied sculpture at the Ecole des Beaux-Arts, Tunis. After his graduation, Alaia began working as a dressmaker’s assistant. Alaia soon began dressing private clients, and in 1957 Alaia moved to Paris to work in fashion design. In Paris he worked for Guy Laroche for two seasons and for Thierry Mugler until the late 1970s. In 1980 he produced his first ready-to-wear line and was voted Best Designer of the Year by the French Ministry of Culture. By 1988 he had opened boutiques in Beverly Hills, New York, and Paris. His seductive, clinging clothes were a massive success, and he was named by the media 'The King of Cling'. Stars such as Tina Turner, Raquel Welch, Madonna, Brigitte Nielsen, Naomi Campbell, and Stephanie Seymour were all regular customers of Alaia's.

Alaia was referenced in the mid-90's teen hit Clueless starring Alicia Silverstone. When mugged at gunpoint, Silverstone's charecter protests to kneeling in a parking lot because she is wearing one of the famous clingy dresses designed by the "totally important designer."

Sometime in the mid-1990s, Alaia virtually vanished from the fashion scene.

His clothes are now back in Barneys New York on the Contemporary Floor alongside Proenza, Balenciaga, and Dolce & Gabbana. His shoes are sold at Bergdorf Goodman. Carine Roitfeld was photographed during February 2007 fashion week in one of his coats. NYTimes declared that she was the only woman at any of the Fall 2007 shows that "looked like the future." Victoria Beckham stated that Alaia is her favourite designer and wore his creation, a gift from husband David Beckham, to two Academy Award parties in February 2007.
