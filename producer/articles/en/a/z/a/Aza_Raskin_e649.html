Aza Raskin

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Aza Raskin, 2005

Aza Raskin is a user interface designer and president of Humanized, Inc. He is the son of noted human-computer interface expert Jef Raskin.

[edit] Biography

Raskin (b. 1984) is the oldest of three children by Jef Raskin and Linda S. Blum. Raskin gave his first talk on user interface at age 10 at the local San Francisco chapter of SIGCHI[1]. By 20, he was speaking internationally[2]. He holds a bachelors degree in math and physics from the University of Chicago, and briefly attended graduate school at Caltech.

Shortly after Jef Raskin's death in 2005, Aza Raskin founded Humanized to carry on his father's legacy. Raskin currently leads the Raskin Center for Human Interfaces[3].
