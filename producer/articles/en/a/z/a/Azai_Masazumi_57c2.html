Azai Masazumi (浅井政澄, Azai Masazumi?) (????-1570) a retainer of the Japanese clan of Azai following the Sengoku period of the 16th century of Japan. Masazumi was a very close relative to the likes of Azai Nagamasa.

Masazumi served as second only to Isono Kazumasa during the Battle of Anegawa. During that battle, he led his army to charge into into the Oda army through their main camp. But the Oda army made a surprise attack to their flanks, and Masazumi along with many others suffered a total breakdown.
