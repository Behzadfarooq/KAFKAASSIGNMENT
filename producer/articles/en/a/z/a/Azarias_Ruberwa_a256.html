Azarias Ruberwa

Azarias Ruberwa (born 1964) is one of four vice-presidents in the transitional government of the Democratic Republic of the Congo. He was sworn in on 17 July 2003.

Ruberwa was trained as a lawyer at the University of Lubumbashi, where he also practiced law before the AFDL war in 1996. He has a wife Chantal Namajana and they have 3 kids Christian 13, Ruth 11, and Wilson 5.


Ruberwa also leads the Congolese Rally for Democracy (RCD), a rebel group turned political party. He ran for president in the 2006 elections and came in sixth place with 1.7% of the vote.

[edit] Trivia

Ruberwa is one of the few prominent politicians of Tutsi origin, a fact that some count against him [1].
