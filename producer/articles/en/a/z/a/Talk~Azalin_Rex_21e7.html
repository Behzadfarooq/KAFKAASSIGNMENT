[edit] name?

Is "Rex" the last name or is it his title (rex is latin for king)? Dread Lord CyberSkull ✎☠ 00:30, 11 April 2006 (UTC)

  • "Rex" is his title. The nation this character rules has a Latin-based language. Further, on several instances in the books Azalin appears in, he signs documents as "Azalin Rex." - CNichols 23:21, 10 May 2006 (UTC)

[edit] Does "S" deserve her own page?

The scholar/wizardess "S" is listed in Azalin's affiliations section, but she has a lot of facts to her name. Should we start a new page just for her? She narrates all five of the Ravenloft Gazetteers and should be considered as major a character as Rudolph van Richten. - HuManBing

  • Such a split seems reasonable to me. Actually, the entire Ravenloft category needs to be brought up to the standards set by the other RPG campaign settings discussed in Wikipedia. Major concepts, publication history, bibliography, geography, major NPCs... all should have at least the broad strokes filled in. Also, the entire section has a large amount of fanon included in it. Wikipedia articles need to focus strictly on Ravenloft canon. - CNichols 20:11, 14 May 2006 (UTC)
  • I'm not sure I see where the fanon sections are in this article. Aside from occasional conjecture (identified as such), pretty much everything can be traced to a TSR, WotC, or Sword and Sorcery product. - HuManBing 09:21, 16 May 2006
    • Really, I was more thinking of Ravenloft domains and extrapolating its contents to all Ravenloft articles, which is probably unfair. - CNichols 15:31, 16 May 2006 (UTC)

[edit] Disambiguation?

Azalin also is the name of a female cartoon character in a little-known Japanese anime. I am fairly certain that character post-dates the Azalin of Ravenloft fame, but I know nothing else of the anime or that character's background. The only lead I have on her are Google Image Search results for Azalin, which bring up too many of her picture for it to be a coincidence.

Do we need to include a disambiguation page for her too?
