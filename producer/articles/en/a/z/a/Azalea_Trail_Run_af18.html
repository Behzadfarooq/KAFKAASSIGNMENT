The Azalea Trail Run is an annual race held through the streets of Mobile, Alabama. The most recent Azalea Tail run was March 25m 2006. Many people participated, somewhere around 7000 people. People who participated in this race either ran the 10k (6.2 miles), the 2 mile, or the fun run(1 mile).

In ATR 2001, Joseph Kimani of Kenya came within two seconds of setting a new world record in the men's open division in 27:41, and then in ATR '01 Abraham Chebii and Thomas Nyariki ran the 3rd and 4th fastest times ever in a 10K, finishing in 27:26 and 27:30, respectively! In ATR '94, the current world record for men masters (28:56) was set by Martín Mondragón of Mexico and in ATR '99 John Campbell of New Zealand established a world's best time for male grandmasters (over 50) of 31:02.
