Azabu Junior and Senior High School (麻布中学校・高等学校, Azabu Chūgakkō Kōtō Gakkō?), commonly called Azabu High School in English, is an academic institution located in the Azabu district of Minato ward, Tokyo. Azabu teaches students in 7th to 12th year in education. The school's administrative body is called Azabu School (麻布学園, Azabu Gakuen?). Azabu University, which is famous for its academically distinguished program in veterinary medicine, is not related to Azabu School in any way.

Azabu High School admits pupils from around the country, and many of its alumni have succeeded in fields like politics, business, literature, and entertainment. Founded as a middle school in 1895 by Soroku Ebara, it moved to its present location in 1900. It was reorganized into junior and senior high schools under the education reforms of 1948.

The school has exchange programs with Shawnigan Lake School, Canada, Winchester College, England and Ningbo Li Huili Vocational High School, China.

Famous alumni include:

  • Former Prime Minister Ryutaro Hashimoto
  • Current Agriculture Minister Shoichi Nakagawa
  • Current Finance Minister Sadakazu Tanigaki
  • Former Chief Cabinet Secretary Yasuo Fukuda
  • Yoshiaki Tsutsumi, president of Seibu Railway, former wealthiest person in the world
  • Takeo Fukui, president and CEO of Honda Motor Co., Ltd.
  • Yoshiro Nakamatsu, inventor of the floppy disc
  • Morio Kita, novelist
  • Iou Kuroda, manga artist
  • Takashi Yuasa, international lawyer, commentator
