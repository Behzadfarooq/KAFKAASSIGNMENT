Azar Karadaş (pronounced Karadash) is a Norwegian association football player, born August 9, 1981. He also has Turkish citizenship. His mother is Norwegian, and his father is Turkish-Cypriot.

Although he has the ability to play in central defence, Karadaş is renowned as a lethal forward player. Indeed, Karadaş' physical attributes and heart make him look tailor-made for Norway's no-frills approach to the international game.

[edit] Career

Beginning his career at S.K. Brann, Karadaş signed for Rosenborg B.K. in a shock transfer on March 13th 2002. A former favourite at Brann, Karadaş received death threats after moving to the bitter rivals.

At Rosenborg, Karadaş' performances soon earned him a call up to the Norwegian Under-21s, and it was not long before foreign eyes began to be trained on the teenager. He further enhanced his reputation by scoring 2 goals against the mighty Internazionale Milan in a UEFA Champions League game.

Karadaş was linked with many of Europe's major clubs, including Roda JC, Liverpool and Udinese Calcio, however SL Benfica manager Giovanni Trapattoni convinced the talented youngster to join his team, Karadaş transferring for the 2004/05 season.

After 1 season at Benfica, where he scored 6 goals and helped the team to win the national championship after 11 years, Karadaş was loaned to FA Premier League club Portsmouth for the 2005/06 season, with the option to make the transfer permanent after the season's end. His spectacular volley which helped then-relegation-threatened Portsmouth earn a crucial 1-1 draw against Bolton Wanderers F.C. in 2006, helped make him known in England

[edit] Coming home?

June 16 Norwegian press reported that SL Benfica and SK Brann had agreed on a transfer for Azar back to Bergen. The deal was speculated to be worth £ 695 000 (€ 102 000), where Karadaş was to give up £ 435 000 worth of bonuses Benfica owned him. All that remained before Karadaş could put on Brann's red jersey again, was his personal contract. However, the deal went sour when Karadaş refused to give up a £ 350.000 compensation which Benfica had to pay him whenever Karadaş was sold from the Lisbon club. Benfica believed that by selling Karadaş to a bigger international club, they could get a better transfer fee for him, and therefore his compensation will cost the club less, than a Brann transfer would.

[edit] Kaiserslautern

Despite being declared unwanted in Lisbon, Karadaş returned to Benfica after his transfer to Brann fell through. His agent Karadaş worked to find a new club for his cousin and on August 16, 2006 the news broke out that Karadaş was in negotiations with German side 1. FC Kaiserslautern, who got relegated to the Second Bundesliga after the 2005-06 season and plan to get a quick promotion back to the top flight. The clubs agreed with a one-year loan deal and Karadaş will play for Kaiserslautern until the end of the 2006-07 season.

Karadaş debuted for Kaiserslautern on August 27, 2006 in their 2-0 home victory over SC Paderborn 07 in the Second Bundesliga. He started the match, but was substituted in the 61st minute. On September 9, 2006, Karadaş scored his first goal for the club in their 2-0 away victory over amateur side 1. FC Gera 03 in the first round of the German Cup. His first league goal came on September 29, 2006 in Kaiserslautern's 1-1 away draw at SpVgg Unterhaching. Until winter break, he made 10 league appearances for the club, but did not score any further goals and was also sent off with two yellow cards in derby at 1. FC Köln, which ended in a 2-2 draw, on the final day before winter break.
