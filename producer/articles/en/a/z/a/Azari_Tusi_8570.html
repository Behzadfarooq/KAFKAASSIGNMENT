Azari-e Tusi (Persian: آذری طوسی‎ ) Shaikh Jalaluddini Alii Hamza ibni Malik Hashimii Bayhaqii Isfarainii Marvazi (1380 Esfarayen – 1462 Esfarayen) was a Persian poet. Azari-e Tusi lived for a while in Tus.

Azari was a poet in Bahmani Sultanate. The name of his father was Ismail Shakh Baghdadi Ali ibn Malik. His nickname in some literature and tazkiras is Nuriddin. His book "Javahir-ul-Asrar" was written in 1430. Azari was born in Isfarain and from his early years he began writing poems and signed as Azari. He made piligrimage to Mecca twice by foot and wrote a book there which he called as Saye-al-sabak. After returning from the pilgrimage he went to India and went to Ahmad Shakh Bahmani's castle and found lots of respect there.
