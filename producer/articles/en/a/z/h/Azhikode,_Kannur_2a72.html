AZHIKODE Azhikode is a panchayath (Local self govt) situated in the Kannur district of Kerala . It is about 5 km from Kannur. The place is most famous as the birth place of Sukumar Azhikode, an influential thinker and literary critic. This village is situated on the cost of the Arabian sea. Azhikkode has a proud history in the political movements in kerala. Two royal familes ruled azhikkode before indian Independence, Chirackal and Arackal. Arackal is the only muslim royal family in kerala.

People

The people of Azhikode engage in a wide range of jobs. There are Fishermen,Retailers,Govt employees,Cigar workers, Handloom weavers,factory workers and Farmers among the people. A large number of people migrated to middle east for better job prospects. But they never settle there; They return home after their fifties to spend the rest of their lives in their hometown.

Most of the people here can be classied as belonging to the middleclass, but in the fishermen area, we can see both poor and middle class families.

[edit] RELIGION

The Hindu community ,The broad divisions of the community are Thiyya, Nairs, Ambalavasis, Brahmins and Kammalan.

  The Thiyyas form the majority among the Hindu community . Though they correspond to the Ezhavas in the southern parts of the State, both the communities disclaim any such connection.

  The Muslims, here known as Mappilas, form the second largest community



   The district has the unique distinction of having had the State's lone Muslim royal house - the Arkkal royal family. The royal family played an important role in the political as well as Islamic history of the district.



  Though Christianity is believed to have been introduced in Kerala by the Apostle St. Thomas in 52 AD, the religion made progress only with the advent of the Portuguese towards the close of the 15th century. The Christians belong mainly to four Churches; the Syro Malabar Church, the Latin Catholic Church, the Church of South India and the Orthodox Syrian Church.


During the last few decades, there has been a large influx of Syrian Catholics from the Travancore-Cochin area of the State.

[edit] Art and Culture

Theyyam is the flagship owner of folks in north malabar., Chirakkal rulers rose into prominence as a great centre of the arts.

The gift of the district in the field of art and culture is its folk arts. The social situation that prevailed here gave rise to various folk art forms. The assimilation of different styles of Aryan and non-Aryan culture and the influence of Jain and Buddhist philosophies kindled its growth further.

Though the social set-up has been effaced by time, the cultural life of the folk remains unchanged.

many temples and kaavu(small temple) s can be found in azhikkode, they have annual festivals. Many sports related events organaised by the youth clubs, cricket and football are the games which attract more people An annual youth festival named keralothsavam is organised by the panchayath
