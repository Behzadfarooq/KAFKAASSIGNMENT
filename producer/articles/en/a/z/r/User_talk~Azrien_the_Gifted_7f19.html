[edit] Azrien Unlimited

Wikipedia is a source of knowledge and as such should provide the people with anything they might wish to know. It is because of this that I feel this business should be kept on Wikipedia.

Wikipedia is not the Yellow Pages: it is not intended -- and is vigorously protected from becoming -- a promotional or advertising vehicle. See also Wikipedia:Notability (companies and corporations) for inclusion standards. Short version: if you have to rely or use Wikipedia to promote your company, it's not notable to be here. If your company is notable enough someone else will be moved to write about it.

Wikipedia should have all the information it can hold. Anyone should be able to access any information they see fit.

Wikipedia is not an indiscriminate collection of information - "Wikipedia is not an indiscriminate collection of items of information. That something is 100% true does not mean it is suitable for inclusion in an encyclopedia."

Unlike a search engine Wikipedia offers clean, safe ways of finding information.

And it's kept that way through collective efforts to maintain quality control -- such as eliminating attempts to use it as self-serving attempts to use it for advertising.

And finally, Wikipedia is not a bureaucracy: self-serving attempts to use pseudo-legal arguments or to manipulate rules and standards in the face of common sense and consensus has a nickname around here, "wikilawyering", and such attempts are common, expected, and -- ultimately -- doomed to failure. --Calton | Talk 01:00, 3 June 2006 (UTC)
