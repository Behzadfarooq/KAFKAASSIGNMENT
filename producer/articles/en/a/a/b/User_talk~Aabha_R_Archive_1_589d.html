[edit] Help in categorization

Hi Beland,

I can see that you are very busy at the moment, so sorry for adding on to your work. I would like to help with the Categorization projects. I could start with Category:Images of people. Could you please tell me once what exactly needs to be done? I don't want to mess up anything, so it would be nice if you (or someone else) could. Thanks. -Aabha (talk) 07:33, 24 December 2005 (UTC)

Oh, it's great you're volunteering - many hands make light work. There's growing consensus that free/public domain/copyleft images should be moved to the Commons. I have updated Wikipedia:Moving images to the Commons. I've never done it myself, so probably best to find an image person if you have any questions beyond the instructions there. There will be some "fair use" images left over. These I suppose can be left in the category. There are some other categories under Category:Wikipedia images by type which should probably be under Category:Images of people, instead of above it or along side of it in the category tree. To put an image or category into Category:Images of people, edit that page and add [[Category:Images of people]] near the bottom (above any interlanguage links). When you do this, you will see the image appear under Articles in category "Images of people" (possibly not on the first page, depending on the letter of the alphabet it starts with). Notice in Category:Images of people there are some people who appear above this header. This is because someone put a link to the image in the category page, instead of putting a link to the category on the image page. Go to the category page and hit "edit", and you will see what needs to be removed. You can add those images to the category using the correct method, or you can do the move-to-Commons thing if appropriate. If you have more than say, 50 "fair use" images of people left over in Category:Images of people, it's best to sort them into subcategories. You can make a new category by simply pretending it exists and adding an image to it. After you save that, follow the "red" link you just made. Edit the new category and put in a link to the "parent" category you want it to have. This will turn the links to it blue, because now it has a description page. (You may have to hit reload/refresh in your browser to see the color change.) I hope that all makes sense...probably it will be clearer after you read the tips page and figure out what the different copyright licenses mean. Thanks again! -- Beland 11:18, 24 December 2005 (UTC)

[edit] Football infoboxes

Hi Aabha

For the patterns, have a look at Template_talk:Football_kit. la1 is the left arm, b1 is the body and ra1 is the right arm for the first choice kit. la2, b2 and ra2 are those for the away kit. The different patterns that you can use are listed at Template_talk:Football_kit, or you can leave them all blank if the kit is unpatterned (no stripes etc.). The other fields use hex triplets to give a colour for different parts of the kit. If you don't understand hexadecimal notation, you probably don't want to (it's counting to base 16 using A-F to represent 10-15 and "10" for 16, not that that will explain it), so the easiest thing is to copy one of the colour values from further down that page or find a club with a similarly coloured kit and copy the value that's used there. And thanks again for your message on my RFA. I was flattered. Cheers, CTOAGN (talk) 11:39, 11 February 2006 (UTC)
