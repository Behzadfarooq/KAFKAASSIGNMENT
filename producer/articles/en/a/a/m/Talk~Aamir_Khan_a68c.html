User Elyaqim put an Arabic script version of Aamir Khan's name on the English page. As this is provocative in the context of Indian politics, I have moved this notation

(عامر حسین خان)

to the discussion page. Either we have both Arabic and Devanagari script, or we have neither. To do otherwise is rabble-rousing.

Why is the Devanagari script still there while the Arabic has been removed? Fkh82 02:31, 10 February 2006 (UTC)
Because he's Indian and not an arab.--Dangerous-Boy 08:01, 14 March 2006 (UTC)
Urdu is written in Arabic script, it has nothing to do with being an Arab. And isn't Urdu one of India's official languages?Fkh82 21:13, 7 May 2006 (UTC)
Yes, it is one of the official languages. Just put the urdu after the devanagari, i mean, Khan is an urdu name. Basawala 19:55, 8 May 2006 (UTC)
Using Devnagri script doesn't seem right. The "Kh" sound is not properly recognized in Hindi (if at all, it needs a "bindi" or dot under the first letter). The name isn't in Hindi, so the Hindi script isn't right, it should be in the proper Persian script (not Arabic) to have the proper pronunciation. -AA
