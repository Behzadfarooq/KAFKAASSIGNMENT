Aaron Lubarsky

Aaron Lubarsky is a film editor primarily know for his work on the HBO documentary Journeys with George. He also worked on the cult hit mock-umentary Filmic Achievement with Writer/Director Kevin Kerwin. After graduation from Stanford University's Documentary Film Program, he worked as a documentarian at Lucasfilm on "The Making Of Star Wars: Episode One". In 2002, he founded PostDox, specializing in non-fiction-style post-production editing.

[edit] Work, Awards, and Recognition

His short documentary, 2000's Uncle Eugene, won him a Golden Gate Award for his work as Writer/Director/Producer/Cinematographer. He also wrote the film's score. In 2002, Aaron served as Co-Director, Producer, and Editor of the documentary Journeys with George that followed George W. Bush on the campaign trail. The film was nominated for five Emmy Awards, and Aaron took home a statue for "Outstanding Picture Editing for Non-Fiction Programming" in 2003. Aaron has also served as the editor on such movies as Speedo, The Art of Nothing, Lookalike, Seoul Train, Filmic Achievement, Matthew Barney: No Restraint, and Sportsfan. He also served as cinematographer, director and producer on Sportsfan; director and producer on Lookalike; and as director on Seoul Train. In 1998, while at Stanford University's Documentary Film Program, Aaron's thesis film Wayne Friedman's Notebook won him a Student Academy Award and a Student Emmy Award.
