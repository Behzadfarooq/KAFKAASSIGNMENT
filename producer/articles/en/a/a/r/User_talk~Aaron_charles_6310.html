[edit] La. Metro/Parishes

Cameron Parish is not at all metropolitan (including pre-Rita). L.C., Alex., and Monroe all, according to U.S. Census, don't have metropolitan areas outside of respective "inner-city" parishes. In other words, they all consist of one parish. Any inclusion of other parishes should be noted as being un-official.

In fact, neither one really deserves it's own article. At most, they are best simply as sections in respective parish articles.

Of course L.C., Alex. and Monroe are M.S.A.'s, but each one only contains one parish officially (i.e. L.C., Calcasieu; Monroe, Ouachita; Alex., Rapides). Any other "parishes within those metro areas" are not legal. Jennings micropolis and metropolitan L.C. make-up urban conglomerent. I have to retrieve the exact http. Gimme a sec.--J. [edit] Louisiana/New Orleans blues

I have reverted your evaluation of these two articles. See Wikipedia:WikiProject World music/Assessment -- as that project definites it, "world music" articles don't include specific genres like Louisiana blues or New Orleans blues. Those are much more appropriate for Wikipedia:WikiProject Music genres on purely utilitarian grounds (an article on Folk music in Louisiana or African American music in New Orleans, for example, would be "World music" articles because they're fundamentally about the music of a people or place. Louisiana blues is just a music genre that happens to be closely associated with a place. Tuf-Kat 20:56, 9 July 2006 (UTC)

[edit] Image:ULLvTexas-A&M-Game.jpg

Thanks for uploading Image:ULLvTexas-A&M-Game.jpg. I notice the 'image' page specifies that the image is being used under fair use, but its use in Wikipedia articles fails our first fair use criterion in that it illustrates a subject for which a freely licensed image could reasonably be found or created. If you believe this image is not replaceable, please:

  1. Go to the image description page and edit it to add {{Replaceable fair use disputed}}, without deleting the original Replaceable fair use template.
  2. On the image discussion page, write the reason why this image is not replaceable at all.

Alternatively, you can also choose to replace the fair use image by finding a freely licensed image of its subject or by taking a picture of it yourself.

If you have uploaded other fair use media, consider checking that you have specified how these images fully satisfy our fair use criteria. You can find a list of 'image' pages you have edited by clicking on this link. Note that any fair use images which are replaceable by free-licensed alternatives will be deleted one week after they have been uploaded, as described on criteria for speedy deletion. If you have any questions please ask them at the Media copyright questions page. Thank you. --ccwaters 01:35, 8 November 2006 (UTC)
