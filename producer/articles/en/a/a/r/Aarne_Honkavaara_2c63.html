Aarne Honkavaara (born June 7, 1924 in Tampere, Finland) is a former Finnish ice hockey player and coach, and nicknamed the "Grand Old Man" of Finnish ice hockey.[citation needed]

[edit] Career

Honkavaara started top flight hockey in the late 1940s for his hometown club, Ilves. His first season came in 1943-1944, but was cancelled after five games due to World War II. After the war, he returned to hockey. The 1944-45 Season was a short one for Honkavaara, who played in only three games, and garnered seven goals and three assists in those appearances. Honkavaara's first full season came in 1945-1946, when he scored 25 goals and made 10 assists in just eight games. He dominated the game for six more seasons (1946-1952), scoring more points than games played in each.

During 1950, Honkavaara had a brief stint with the Canadian team, the Sarnia Sailors, playing in IHL. Honkavaara's tenure there was short. Although his departure was attributed to problems with his work permit, the real reason was the anti-foreigner attitude of the management, common in North America at that time.[citation needed] Although Honkavaara played only two exhibition games for the Sailors, he learnt valuable information about hockey during his trip to Canada, which he would later use in his coaching career.

Honkavaara's career ended in 1953 when a broken shin bone, sustained in an international game against Poland, forced him to retire from active playing. He accepted a position as player-coach for Ilves. Although he played only in a few games in his first two seasons (1957-1958) as a player-coach, Ilves managed to rise back to the top of Finnish hockey teams. Honkavaara then became full-time coach for Ilves for a number of seasons before he was replaced. During his career, he won seven Finnish Championships as a player (1945, 1946, 1947, 1950, 1951, 1952 and 1957) and three as a coach (1957-58, 1959-60, 1971-72).

Internationally, Honkavaara represented team Finland as a player in three tournaments: the 1949 World Championships, the 1951 World Championships and the 1952 Winter Olympics. Honkavaara played for Finland in 47 games, scoring 46 goals and assisting 12 others. This remains the all-time highest goals per match ratio in team Finland's history.

Honkavaara was also the head coach of the Finnish National team between 1954-1959. He was assistant coach to Joe Wirkkunen in the Squaw Walley Olympics 1960 and Innsbruck Olympics 1964. Honkavaara was the assistant coach of the national team at the World Championships of 1963, 1965 and 1966.

[edit] Legacy

Aarne Honkavaara is considered perhaps the greatest player in Finnish ice hockey history, and one of the pioneers of ice hockey in pre- and post-war Finland. In 1979, he was instrumental in founding the Finnish Ice Hockey Museum. In 1985, Honkavaara was named as Jääkiekkoleijona ("Hockey Lion") number 10 by the Finnish Ice Hockey Association and admitted to the Finnish Ice Hockey Hall of Fame. The award for SM-Liiga's best regular season goalscorer is named the "Aarne Honkavaara Trophy" after him. Due to his accomplishments, Honkavaara was awarded with a Lynces Academici special award in 1987; he is the only one to receive that honour.

[edit] Awards and Achievements

  • Finnish Player of the Year (1950, 1952)
  • Lynces Academici Forward Award (SM-sarja 49-50, 51-52)
  • Top Scorer (SM-sarja 49-50, 50-51, 51-52)
  • Top Goalscorer (SM-sarja 44-45, 45-46, 47-48, 51-52)
  • Most Assists (SM-sarja 49-50, 50-51)
  • Most Finnish Championships, 7 (1945, 1946, 1947, 1950, 1951, 1952, 1957, 1958)
  • Most goals in one SM-sarja game, 8 (1948)
  • Lynces Academici Special Award (1987)
  • IIHF Golden Award (1974)
  • Jääkiekkoleijona # 10 (1985)
  • Jersey Number 7 retired by Ilves
  • A brief stint with Sarnia Sailors (IHL) in Canada 1950 (First fully Finnish player in North American league).
