Aaron Taylor

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Aaron Taylor (b. November 14, 1972 in San Francisco, California) is a former college football analyst for ABC Sports and co-host of the network's college football coverage with John Saunders and Craig James. Prior to that, he was a professional football player for the Green Bay Packers and the San Diego Chargers. He was an offensive guard in the NFL, and an offensive tackle at Notre Dame.

A graduate of De La Salle High School in Concord, California, he attended the University of Notre Dame where he was a consensus All-American in 1992 and won the Lombardi Award in 1993. He was also an Outland Trophy finalist in 1993. Despite being regarded as a member of Notre Dame's top 25 players since 1970, Taylor saw limited success on the football field, retiring after just five years in the NFL. He was a 1994 first-round pick by the Green Bay Packers and was a member of the Packers' 1997 Super Bowl XXXI championship team. Taylor remained with the team until 1998 before joining the San Diego Chargers, where he played until his retirement in 1999.

Aaron Taylor has broken off negotiations on a contract with ABC Sports. The 33-year-old says he wants to devote more time to the Aaron Taylor Impact Endowment Fund.
