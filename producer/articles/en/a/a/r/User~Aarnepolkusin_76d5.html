[edit] Meta

This page is undergoing various minor transitions. Please wait.

This is my user page. A bad joke, to tell the truth. It is, to date, my greatest contribution to Wikipedia, which is indeed a sad state of affairs, but as long as my information metabolism remains itself, I will use Wikipedia more for collecting all kinds of strange pieces of information and only do a random edit now and then. (Besides, lately, I've been doing most of the other things besides Wikipedia that could count as interesting facets of my life.)

So, desperately trying to abstract some kind of order, some kind of common denominator out of all these disparate pieces of knowledge whirling about around me, I have tried to collect these shards of information into a heap and then to find a way they can be combined that makes sense. In a sense, that should be something I am or represent: this is also an experiment in classification, ie. finding different things one can define or categorize me by, as well as (to some (mostly imaginary (nested)) extent) wikification.

Many of the things I mention here are ones I've first read about in Wikipedia, or ones I've only read about in Wikipedia - I don't know if that's a good thing or a bad thing.

Seeing how little sense most of this page makes, I think you won't mind me not making too much edits, after all. People seem to consider my own edits to this page vandalism. That's probably enough. Besides, my ventures into knowledge, whether in itself or as an abstract theoretical concept, aren't anything I could call very NPOV - whatever information I may have collected can hardly be very encyclopædic at this point. Maybe later.

I wish I were leet enough to be able to be as terse, minimalistic and powerful as a writer as Wittgenstein was. Or, even more ideally, to manage to squeeze all of this into a few surreal haikus. I've seen enough stuff like that to know that's as possible as anything & to hate myself for writing like this (besides everything else), to some extent. I also wish I cared. For the sake of my grades, if nothing else. Maybe I've just been listening to my mother too much, but still. A sad state of affairs, you know.

I probably should use my sandbox more. I might want to draw some mind maps, too, although I always thought I hated them. This sort of began as an attempt at one. It's hard to see things as sequential and unified when it's so clear that they're really trees or networks & n-furcated, n > 1. I'll probably end up getting a wiki of my own at some point because of that. Meanwhile, the parasite welcomes anyone curious to explore what his random meanderings can offer to them.

[edit] Me

This user contributes using Linux.
The following includes some things you might not want to know, really.

So I'm a Finnish wiki newbie born in 1987. I'm more or less - at least biologically and socially - male, a still somewhat closet-bound bisexual at that, with a slightly neurotic preference for girls. My mind is sometimes a bit ambiguous, but probably male enough to make gender reassignment surgery a bit far-fetched. I haven't got a problem with being an occasional transvestite, which is the extent of my genderqueerness as of today. I could do with being a lot less shy than I am. I can't say I feel quite at home right now, but that might just be me thinking too much. I enjoy being androgynous which, most of the time, I am not to such a great extent. There's something about drag kings that fascinates me. I've been a somewhat misandric feminist for quite some time now. Sexism and all this heteronormative Gleichschaltung stereotyping are what I can't really stand (ie. I should do something about that misandry thing).

Besides being a sexual being, which I tend not to think that I am to such a great degree, I just sit at the computer never doing anything that makes sense. I write down juvenile, pretentious and omphaloskeptical philosophical ideas in thick A5 size notebooks I call my diary. I'm just your run-of-the-mill teenage cynic nihilist psycho who thinks he kind of likes Derrida, runs Debian Linux, and seems to be enjoying his miserable little life more than ever. A preference for free software can be gathered from this, as well as an interest in all kinds of silly things such as anything meta, semiotics and aesthetics, and whatever textual and drenched in discourse.

Right now, all I do besides sitting at the computer, writing, and making weird noises seems to be cycling. I play some chess and tetris, but I suck at both. There's plenty of things I should do. Time isn't something I have, so it seems. Unless it's just lack of motivation, which is a lot more likely. Don't ask me why. And stop asking me why I won't sleep at regular hours, you one-size-fits-all Gleichschaltung bastards, you!

Anyone having read this nonsense jusqu'à ici will probably have noticed I'm an arrogant bastard. (How the hell do you say jusqu'à ici in English, any way? Lovely, these blackouts.) As an arrogant bastard, I like things surreal, absurd and avant-garde. I'm interested in psychoanalysis. I'm a girly vegetarian hippie. And a pacifist commie, to boot. I'm your very own abstract expressionist whose mind is, in fact and deep down, best suited for roles of accompaniment, however strange that might seem. Also a grammar nazi, to some extent. Also, I'll have to mention that I really find this page silly and juvenile, I'm just writing this way because I'm fed up with being annoyed by this style of writing. So, in case you hate the style I'm writing in, then, uh, great minds think alike, or something. (so that's, like, phunny to teh max)

[edit] Why

The following two paragraphs are boring and unusually disorganized. Please skip them, unless you have masochistic tendencies.

Before delving into any specific interests I have, besides these meta-things and things I more or less have turned out to be, it might make sense to outline some main pathways my curiosity habitually takes. From an aesthetical point of view, the interesting stuff is mostly what is "passed over in silence": ugliness, fear, wars, death, and "evil", for example. Although less is more, and minimalism is the best thing since sliced bread, this doesn't make sense without having a practical purpose (ie. you might call me a proponent of functionalism and pragmatism); hence, the positive side of aesthetics an sich is just about as useful as any of those cute political theories going on about their great ideals and their revolution and having nothing to say about things in the much larger set of possibilities you get by ditching those would-be ideals and mutinous uprisings and just looking at what's actually happening. Now, I really hate politics like nothing else, but I do have to say I'm a fan of Mohandas Gandhi and Johan Galtung. Either way, there's much more fun to be had with the much greater part of things that's not perfect, all Golden mean and Platonic. (There's a Marquis de Sade quote I could use here, and Sigmund Freud's "Das Unheimliche" seems to include some interesting points about this.) Besides, it's not like there's anything wrong with pure'n'clean mathematics, it's just that confusing it with the real world is a no-no.

Now, what I just said isn't anything new - it's just something along the lines of what I liked doing meta about at the time I wrote that. Or, rather, what I end up with when I stop paying attention for a moment, which I do often. Most of the time, even. That hardly means I like it. I "think" too much, ie. I usually end up over-intellectualizing things and, generally, desperately trying to identify and define things as something and not just going with the flow. What follows is living in a constant fear of something, hypochondria, and generally, more or less, a sense of trying to escape illusions I appear to find myself living in. Paranoia. Withdrawal, too. I'm in dire need of an Ockham shave. One of the downsides of leading a Bohemian life... (Hitler.)

Music is one of the things I love the most in this world. In addition to double bass and guitar (I can't really play piano worth shit, but that makes me a novice pianist, doesn't it?), I'm trying to learn to sing properly.

If I don't end up studying linguistics (or become a speech therapist), I'll probably study folk music or possibly become a piano tuner.

[edit] Vocal

What I am is a bass baritone with a range of about three octaves from D to d², more or less. Being the impatient bravado extravaganza guy I've grown up to be, I, having never had many lessons, enjoy learning various extended techniques of phonation such as Tuvinian throat singing and something that sounds a bit like Maja Ratkje, or Keiji Haino, or Dani Filth, or whatever. Ululation might be the word, although I wouldn't hold my breath. I've fiddled around with beatboxing since I was a little boy, and suchlike. A cappella. No one loves me.

Vocalists, and things vocal, worth a mention (besides the ones already mentioned) include Janis Joplin, Freddie Mercury, Chuck Schuldiner, Devin Townsend, Rob Halford, Geddy Lee, Gennadi Tumat, Albert Kuvezin, khoomei, kargyraa, sygyt, yoik, Wimme Saari, Howlin' Wolf, John Lee Hooker, Captain Beefheart, The King's Singers, hymns, Leonard Cohen, Tuomari Nurmio, John Mayall, and Jim Pembroke. Ville Valo, too, sort of. The female alto is a type of voice I love (see also Angela Gossow and Arch Enemy). Kate Bush. Indica. Mike Patton & Mr. Bungle.

I've been having some Alexander technique & singing lessons lately, so possibly I'll manage to refine my voice to some extent. The intricate sword it might be resembles, at the moment, a worn-out Mora knife with a red plastic handle.

[edit] Instrumental

As a guitarist, I'm a thirteen-to-the-dozen Derek Bailey copycat with some detrimental Štěpán Rak and Robert Fripp influences, ie. a boring shredder (but I'll blame Jan Hammer for that, and Adrian Belew is someone I'll envy for all my life, probably). Greg Ginn is leet, as is John Lee Hooker. I have no classical training, but I sort of wish I had.

On the piano, I could mention Thelonious Monk and Keith Tippett as influences (and I should listen to Cecil Taylor), but really, all I can get out of keyboard instruments is a mess of uninspired, badly motivated and usually quite percussive free improvisation. I've tried to play some Bach on the piano lately, but haven't really got anywhere yet.

And as for the bass - my main instrument is classical double bass, but it seems I've always wanted to play cello after all (well duh) - I tend to concentrate on the electric and clanky side of things à la Les Claypool or John Wetton. I like Charlie Haden as a bass player. Edgar Meyer, Pekka Pohjola, Michael Manring, Justin Chancellor, and Marcus Miller aren't that bad, either. Marino Pliakas rocks.

I often find myself wanting to sound like a Tuvinian igil while playing the double bass - I experiment a lot with harmonics and multiphonics. On the other hand, one of the first things I ever tried was sounding like Apocalyptica. I've played too much spiccato for my own good - I'm not supposed to know it yet. I should have a phenomenally varied and versatile bowing technique with all these influences, but really I just suck. (The bass is a percussion instrument, either way. And, sadly, I don't have a spare bow for my tendencies of aggression toward musical instruments - and even the strings are pricy enough. Although I've only broken two, and I didn't have to pay for either of them.)

Trey Gunn and Enver Izmailov are leet. Just wish I could tap worth anything in particular. My brain has tubular tendencies.

I'm sort of glad that I'm not really a drummer or a pianist, because Tomas Haake makes me mad enough already, as does hearing someone play Ligeti's piano etudes.

[edit] Electronic

Impulse Tracker is what I first made music with, but I nevertheless have ended up knowing all too little about electronic music. I know I like Pan Sonic, Rinneradio, Squarepusher, Venetian Snares, Kraftwerk, and Erkki Kurenniemi, to mention most of them. Aavikko. I like old four-channel Amiga mods as well as chiptunes (the first computer I had all for myself was an Amiga 500 (in 1996!)). I could mention Skaven of Future Crew. There's plenty of people in the demoscene I respect. CNCD. Mark Knight. Bjørn Lynne. There's something fascinating about Karsten Obarski's very early mods. Not to mention the sound of the Commodore 64, the MOS6581. Martin Galway and Rob Hubbard are leet.

Then there's Jean Michel Jarre. I kind of like him, but then again, I kind of don't. And Vangelis I just don't know well enough.

[edit] Admitted influences

Although Sturgeon's law seems to make almost unreasonably good sense in the case of free jazz, the philosophy involved makes sense to me, if nothing else. Ornette Coleman. Raoul Björkenheim. Derek Bailey is definitely an important influence.

I like instruments such as (double) bassoon, bass clarinet, kantele, hammered dulcimer, igil and jouhikko. Percussion, too (tuned, too): great percussionists are something I consider demi-gods. Daniel Erlandsson, Dave Lombardo, Bill Bruford, Pat Mastelotto, and Edward Vesala are ones I might mention (and Kassara). Cello, as I said, is what I really want to play; having only once had a chance to play one yet, I end up being influenced by Apocalyptica, Alamaailman Vasarat, and Péter Pejtsik, as a double bassist, and that's not something you'd like to hear and, at the very least, bad technique. (whips oneself)

I also like progressive rock, however unfashionable that might sound. King Crimson, Yes, Dream Theater, Van der Graaf Generator, Meshuggah, Wigwam, even Queen; with a slight stretch of imagination, also Procol Harum. One might classify Metsatöll, Yat-Kha, and Huun-Huur-Tu as folk music, which probably means I like folk music (duh). Klezmer. Gamelan. M.A. Numminen. There is something perverse about the vocal music of the Setos.

The Quantic Soul Orchestra isn't all that bad. Jacob Fred Jazz Odyssey rocks. Steamboat Switzerland (Marino Pliakas's right hand technique is just insane. I saw him live with Peter Brötzmann and Michael Wertmüller in Tampere. I'm a fan.). Disciplina Kičme. Youngblood Brass Band. Black Flag.

I seem to remember once piecing together a list of artists-worth-a-mention I've seen live, but I can't find it anymore.

I've always been interested in languages. I'm one of these silly imposter syndrome Wunderkinds, learning to read when I was four and all. I'll probably proceed to study linguistics after finishing high school. I enjoy Finno-Ugric languages, from what I know from studying some Estonian and Hungarian (both of which I understand quite a bit better than, say, Mari, but not that well) and, of course, being a native speaker of Finnish. I prefer agglutinative to analytic, but studying a few of the better known Germanic and Romance languages hasn't exactly been repulsive, either. Most of my language skills are passive: for example, I might call my Norwegian level 2 if not for having to switch to Swedish for speaking and writing (and besides, in some contexts I seem to understand Norwegian _better_ than Swedish, even though Swedish is all I've ever had lessons with).

I enjoy these games of apparent meaning. Having finally managed to read it, I'm quite positive my relationship to Douglas Hofstadter's Gödel, Escher, Bach is one of love-hate: many of the ideas Hofstadter expresses are fascinating, but I can't help thinking he's over-simplifying and over-popularizing things here and there. (Should read more Jean-Yves Girard, sez this) I should read some more Wittgenstein, among others.

There's plenty of things in anthropology, animal psychology, ethnomusicology etc. that I'm interested in from a vaguely linguistic point of view. The development of meaning, in a sense. Then again, I'd rather study cultural history or art history searching for things of that nature. I'm interested in ethnology, from a completely non-nationalist point of view - nationalism is a silly nineteenth-century construct for people (& peoples) with low self-esteem.

[edit] Things one should & should not

I seem to be interested in things such as urban exploration, martial arts, parkour, yoga and Alexander technique, but as always, I just haven't got around to doing anything much about them. (Pitäis, niinq.) Well, Alexander technique I've sort of begun with. I've tried my hand as a composer, I've written cute angst poems, I've even done some (pretty pathetic) things one might classify as visual arts, I've even sneaked around in places of natural beauty, SLR in hand, taking photographs. I used to play Nethack. These are also things I should do.

Specific books I probably should read (* marking ones I've read partially): Philosophical Investigations, The Divine Comedy, The Master and Margarita, Kalevala*, Kalevipoeg, For Whom The Bell Tolls*, The Egyptian*, Brave New World, Ulysses, Le Ton Beau de Marot, The Anatomy of Disgust, In Search of the Miraculous*

Authors I don't think I want to read: Dan Brown, J.K. Rowling, Tom Clancy (though I've ended up reading the first Harry Potter book after all, but still)

I think I should know Robert Crumb better. I should watch more movies. (and read more comics, too)

I should know more about lesbian culture. 'Cos it's cool. Besides, butchy style is sexy. I'm definitely the femme in my current relationship.

I should also know the work of, among others, Richard Stallman, Sigmund Freud, and Oliver Sacks a lot better than I do now. People I probably should read include Kate Bornstein, Paul Graham, Alan Turing (or, rather, that biography by Andrew Hodges, for a start), Don Knuth, Robert Bringhurst, Jakob Nielsen, Ursula K. Le Guin, Joseph Heller, Leo Tolstoy, Ernest Hemingway, Jonathan Swift, Richard Dawkins, Alexander Luria, Friedrich Nietzsche...

Cartesius is someone I probably should read - to find out if I really hate him.

I should listen to hip hop, punk, noise and industrial, Einstürzende fucking Neubauten, like, and Public Image Limited, and definitely more to funk and other grr teh oovy things one doesn't listen to in an attempt to rediscover the joys of feeling disoriented. As well as Frescobaldi and Rameau, I suppose, and anything polyphonic, and I should definitely get a grip on atonality, just because it makes a lot of sense to me just as an idea. Not to speak of most of what's happened in music, like, uh, ever, besides György Ligeti and Johann Sebastian Bach (and I don't know shit about them, either). Iannis Xenakis, definitely, and Sofia Gubaidulina, and, like, Steve Reich, or something. Polyrhythms. I might like Alfred Schnittke.

Astor Piazzolla. Buena Vista Social Club.

I don't know enough about metal, either. Or the history of many parts of the world. Croatia, for an arbitrary example.

IDM is something I should know. Breakbeats of various, preferably complicated, kinds. No wave. Glitch.

I should listen more to basses and altos, either way, and do more stuff like that (I can't even get a decent metal growl). I just might become the scrawniest Sarastro ever at some point of my life, you know.

I should do more constrained writing like just using four channels, I've hardly ever really tried that.

I should listen to Level 42, probably.

I like inharmonicity: it sounds more complex than run-of-the-mill stuff. Bells (should get a grip on Russian Orthodox bell ringing), metal, thick strings. Should get a ring modulator. And while we're at it, I think I need a DigiTech Whammy pedal, too.
