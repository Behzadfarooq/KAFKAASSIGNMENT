Aaron Russell (November 24, 1979 in Batesville, Arkansas, USA) is an American singer/songwriter and lead singer of the Alt.Country Band The Jaybirds. Russell's career began in various high school bands in his childhood home of Weiner, Arkansas. Russell now records for Depot Records in Batesville, and will be releasing his new album in May of 2007. Russell pioneered the sound of the no lead instrument sound, where no lead breaks are involved in the musical composition.

[edit] Early bands

The first incarnation he was involved in was Solid Greenheads. The band included Matt Hurdle (Guitar), Mark Knight (Bass), and Junior Gant (Drums). The band won the Arkansas Rice Festival Talent Show in 1997. Soon after the name was changed to One Right of the Bulleye. The next version of the band was on the more serious side, rebuilding and renaming the group Cheat You Fair, Russell now enlisted Ryan Ivy (Guitar) Kevin Boward (Drums), and Gump Lynn (Bass) as members of the new band. Playing mostly covers and sappy love songs the band struggled for a year and a half before finally disbanding in 2005.

2005 was a lost year for Russell. He did not perform or write any new music and actually hocked all of his equipment. Something changed in the winter of that year and he placed a call to Ivy to reform and start fresh. Ivy was sceptical at first, but soon Boward followed suit and the trio became Broken Hill; a more country version of Cheat You Fair playing only original content written by Russell. The trio recorded the EP "Broken Hill" in February and quickly sold out of CD's, but with no management, no label support, and a tour falling apart they again broke up in May of 2006.

[edit] The Jaybirds

Russell regrouped and decided to go solo with a studio band for the first time in his career. Dubbing them The Jaybirds, Russell began to compile new music for his new album in the Winter of 2006. He entered the studio in February 2007 and began recording the upcoming album Fiddles and Flowers, to be released on Depot Records in May 2007. Russell announced in March 2007 via his website that the Jaybirds will be touring this summer at local venues around Arkansas and Texas. The tour will feature ex-Broken Hill bandmate Ryan Ivy back on bass for the first time since the split of Broken Hill in 2006.

[edit] Influences

Russell lists the following Artists as influences on his writing style: Steve Earle, Ryan Adams, The Band, Counting Crows, Neil Young

[edit] Early bands

[edit] Trivia

  • Russell plays Guitar, Harmonica, Mandolin, and Piano
  • He has been married twice. To Michelle Vogel 2000-2005. They have a daughter, Maggie,6 and twin sons Nick and Alex, 3. To Stacey Treadway 2007-Present.
  • Russell is deathly afraid of frogs.
  • Russell received a baseball scholarship from Williams Baptist College but chose not to attend. He decided to walk on at Arkansas State University where he was cut.
  • Russell is an avid international sports fan supporting Derby County Football Club (England), Collingwood Football Club (Australia), F.C. Dallas (USA), and the Chicago Cubs (USA).
  • Russell lived in Australia from 2000-2003.
