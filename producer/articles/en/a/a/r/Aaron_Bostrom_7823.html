Aaron Keith Bostrom (born January 2, 1979 in San Fransisco) is a professional American freestyle BMX rider. He is considered to be the one of the most influential street riders in modern, freestyle BMX. Amongst Bostrom’s slieu of nicknames, are “Boss," “AB Strong,” “Big A,” “Double AA,” “Jesus Christos” and “Bosseltoph.” He is the first person to ride up a round rail and is known for his pedal grinds. He has been exclusively featured by every BMX magazine and several video sections. He has signature clothing and has traveled the globe for his skills.

Aside from his athletic career, Bostrom is famous for his contributions to the progression of the BMX industry through skatepark design and construction. He currently runs a skatepark building company, Blend Skate Parks with Matt Gibeut, or better known as "Burly Matt."

Aaron’s sponsors have included Fit Bicycle Company, Tree Bicycle Company, Duffs Shoes, Mutiny Bikes, Hustler Magazine and he currently represents Profile Racing, Micreation, Baco and Volume.

[edit] Personal

Aaron Bostrom grew up in Lakewood, Ohio. He started to ride street after watching a group of twenty BMX riders zoom past, interrupting his solo skateboarding session. Not having any money to purchase or maintain a bike himself, he waited for friends to pass down their old junky parts until he could hit the streets of Cleveland. For anyone that knows him or has seen Aaron’s ‘bike check’ in the February issue of BMXplus!, knows that he still isn’t picky about having nice or new parts, or anything new for that matter, and is especially appreciative of those that help him with making his bike better.

He has traveled the world and lived in his car for years to ride his bike. He currently resides in California where he rides his bike, surfs and snowboards. He is also an avid gardener, vegetable eater and poet.
