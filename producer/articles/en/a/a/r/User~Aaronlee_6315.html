AaronLee is a graduate student in the Northwestern University Department of Linguistics. In general I am interested in phonetics, phonology and the interaction between sound and meaning (mainly syntax and semantics). My main focus is on prosodic systems, especially sentence-level prosody, and much of my work deals with intonation. As much as I can, I do comparative experimentally-based fieldwork on various languages.

My other specialization is in the study of the Sei Yap dialects, including the Taishan dialect, and working with linguists in China on developing a common dictionary and transcription system. I was raised speaking the Kaiping accent of the Sei Yap dialects, particularly the accent as spoken by the Kwan (Guan) family around Chikan (赤坎).
