Aaron Francis McKenny (born 1983 in Overland Park, Kansas to John and Sharmin McKenny), a Global Trade Services(GTS) and Sales and Distribution(SD) SAP consultant in the Business Consulting Services practice at IBM. McKenny is also the co-founder and Chief Executive officer of the Tau Alpha Phi Society.

McKenny currently resides in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania although he is more frequently on the road per his job as a consultant.

[edit] Activities

When he is not working, McKenny can frequently be found working on starting Tau Alpha Phi Society with his colleagues Jessica Stevens and Anna Tai. For fun he also enjoys listening to music, playing the guitar, swing/salsa dancing, and practicing martial arts.

[edit] Achievements and Leadership Experience

[edit] Pre-collegiate

In middle school McKenny took the SAT and because of his high score among students his age was able to participate in OPPTAG, an Iowa State University summer program for talented and gifted students.

In high school he attained the rank of Eagle Scout in Boy Scouts of America. His junior and senior years he was admitted into the EntrePrep program at the Kauffman Center for Entrepreneurial Leadership in Kansas City, Mo. The EntrePrep program sparked a keen interest in McKenny which he used to take first place in entrepreneurship at the state level and competed at the national competition of the Future Business Leaders of America organization.

[edit] Undergraduate Studies

McKenny was very involved in extra-curricular activities during his undergraduate career at the University of Arizona. In his four years as an undergraduate he held various leadership positions in several organizations including Phi Beta Lambda where he held the state president position for 1-1/2 years, and Tau Alpha Phi Society which he co-founded and was president. In his participation with Phi Beta Lambda, McKenny placed at the national level every year that he was able to compete. This included three first place and one second place showings over a period of three years.

In 2003, McKenny was admitted into the University of Arizona's highly-competitive McGuire Entrepreneurship Program. As a part of this program, McKenny teamed up with colleagues Jason Haun, and Shamus Ankrom to write a business plan for The WalkOn Group. In March 2004, McKenny and Haun entered a the plan into the Northwest Venture Championship at Boise State University and took second place in the undergraduate division.

As a senior, McKenny was recognized by the University of Arizona's Dean of Students with the Robie Gold Medal Award, an awards given to two graduating seniors (one male, one female) who have demonstrated above-average scholastic ability, citizenship, leadership, and promise for the future.

McKenny graduated Magna Cum Laude with honors in May, 2005 with three majors (Management Information Systems, Operations Management, and Entrepreneurship). His honors thesis was "The WalkOn Group: A Business Plan".

[edit] Professional

In his first year as a consultant at IBM, McKenny was recognized twice by the practice. First, he was awarded the Above and Beyond award for his contributions to his client, then with the Knowledge Sharing award for his contributions to the practice. He was nominated for the Above and Beyond award a second time; however, since he had received the award once already that year, he was unofficially ineligible to receive it again.
