[edit] About

Aaron Walden is a native of the Cherokee Nation Jurisdictional Area in what is today known as Oklahoma. He is descended from the Ross, Lowery, and Shorey families of Cherokees with Scottish heritage.

Activities include poetry, traditional music, photography, origami, and writing.

He is a graduate of the Business Technology program at the Housing Authority of the Cherokee Nation.

Having spent most of his life in Oklahoma, Mr. Walden lived in the Philippine Islands as a teenager.

[edit] Contributions

Some of Walden's contributions to Wikipedia have included:

  • Developing the Bowed instruments category.
  • Added articles to the Simple English Wikipedia.
  • Added categories and articles to the Cherokee Wikipedia.
  • Extensive additions to the Game Gear categories.
  • He is a participant in the Wikipedia:WikiProject Indigenous peoples of North America.

[edit] Note Regarding Unicode Text

In order to view Cherokee text on Wikipedia, it is important to have a Unicode font installed that supports the Cherokee language. The TrueType font Marin is such a font. It can be downloaded for free from Mark E. Shoulson's site.

The following text should show up on your screen if you have a properly installed Unicode font with Cherokee support:

[edit] Cherokee Script

Aaron Walden's great-great-great-great-great grandfather, Major George Lowery, Jr. (1770-1852), a town chief of the Eastern Cherokees, presided over the court case in which Sequoyah proved his method of writing the Cherokee language. Through Sequoyah's efforts, the Cherokee Nation became literate within a very short time.

Though some characters in the Cherokee typeface resemble Latin letters, it need not be assumed that Sequoyah originally based the system on the Latin alphabet, as his handwritten characters bore little resemblance to them. Click here to view the original version. The letters were thus adapted in the development of a font for the first Cherokee printing press, which was to be bilingual, so that Cherokee and Latin characters could be fitted onto the same press.
