Aaron DeVon Glenn (born July 16, 1972 in Humble, Texas) is an NFL cornerback for the Dallas Cowboys. He spent his first eight seasons in the NFL with the New York Jets before being acquired by the Houston Texans through the expansion draft in 2002. He would then spend three seasons with the Texans before his release after the 2004 season. He is a three-time Pro Bowler. He signed with the Dallas Cowboys reuniting him with Bill Parcells, who coached him in New York. Glenn also played with safety Marcus Coleman from 1996-2004,2006. They played with the Jets, Texans, and Cowboys together, also with Parcells.

[edit] Junior College Career

Glenn attended Navarro College in Corsicana, Texas, where as a sophomore, he won All-Conference and All-America honors, after posting two interceptions, 60 tackles, and 15 pass deflections.

[edit] College career

Glenn attended Texas A&M University. As a junior he was the Southwest Conference Newcomer of the Year, an All-Southwest Conference pick, and an All-American pick. As a senior, he was an All-Southwest Conference pick, and an All-American pick.

[edit] High school career

Glenn was a four-year letterman for the Nimitz High School Cougars football team in Houston, Texas.
