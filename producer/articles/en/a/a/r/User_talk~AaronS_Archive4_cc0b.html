[edit] This comment isn't about Tony

But is instead a commondation for what a well-worded notice to ANI, combined with a slight suggestion on how to do it better.

  1. Always good to see someone who understands that they might in fact be the one who is in the wrong. Kudos. I attempt to maintain this myself, but when the other party doesn't share that sensibility it's easy to respond with "No, I'm right!"
  2. Diffs to the exact edit where whomever said whatever always go over well with the crowd. Especially if it's a prolific editor or a busy page, people often cannot be bothered to hunt htings down. So instead of "At Page foo Bar said 'cheese', 'king', and 'blade'" put "At Page foo Bar said [diff1 'cheese'], [diff1' king'], and [diff1' blade']."

Thanks, happy editing.
brenneman {L} 01:36, 13 July 2006 (UTC)
