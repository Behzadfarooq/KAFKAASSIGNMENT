Aaron Kraten

Aaron Kraten is an artist born in San Francisco, California.

[edit] Early life

Born in 1974 Kraten had moved from the city to southern California. As a teenager he revered a pro skateboarder artist local Mark Gonzales. Kraten is interested in street graffiti and skateboarding, worked full time in factories & as a courier, while establishing himself as an artist. He gained recognition in the 2000s, and subsequently set up a factory-like studio in a Costa Mesa loft on the East side.

Kraten's early work was in the form of conceptual painting, one of the best-known being Workers Compensation 2003, consisting a table found in the trash and entirely fingerpainted.

[edit] Work

Kraten works on all sorts of different mediums , from Refrigerator doors and discarded windows to dismanteled doghouses and old street signs. Kraten works with his hands giving a rich and textured quality to his work , never using brushes and with no art school experience Kraten tends to have this very gritty look about his work.

[edit] History

Kraten grew up in Huntington Beach , CA and always enjoyed drawing. Kraten carried around these small 100 page sketchbooks which he drew in often. In 1999 Kraten was working at a thrift store called Stateside. There Kraten started experimenting with painting on found objects. Kraten started displaying work in the Thrift Store and sold quite a bit of art off those walls.

Soon after, Kraten began submitting his work to galleries and museums in Southern California. Kraten has shown in Museums such as Laguna Art Museum, Museum of Contemperary Art Los Angeles and Galleries such as Gallery 23, Gallery C , Seven Degrees, Subject Matter. Kraten shows about 4 times a year and posts new work on his website often. Kraten has drawn over 58 - 100 page sketchbooks and over 150 paintings. He currently works in the video game industry and paints full time.
