Aaron S. Daggett

Aaron Simon Daggett (June 14, 1837 â€“ May 14, 1938) was the last surviving Union General of the American Civil War when he died at the age of 100.

Daggett was born in Maine in 1838. He attended Bates College (then called the Maine State Seminary) in Lewiston, Maine, in 1860.

Daggett enlisted as a private in the 5th Maine Volunteers on April 1861, and became a second lieutenant in May 1861. He fought at the First Battle of Bull Run, and became a captain in August 1861. Daggett went on to fight at West Point, Gaines' Mill, Golding's Farm, White Oak Swamp, Second Bull Run, South Mountain, Antietam, Rappahannock Station, and Fredericksburg.

He became a major of the 5th Maine in January 1863 and fought at Second Fredericksburg, Gettysburg, Mine Run, Petersburg, and the Wilderness, where he was wounded. In March 1865, he was brevetted a colonel and Brigadier General of the U.S. Volunteers for "gallant and meritorious services during the war."

After the war, Daggett became a captain in the 16th U.S. Infantry in 1866. He had also been brevetted a major in the Regular Army for gallant and meritorious services at Rappahanock Station and lieutenant colonel for services at the Wilderness. Daggett believed in the abolition of slavery and fought alongside African-American soldiers during the Civil War with the 5th Maine.

Daggett went on to fight in the Spanish-American War and in Spain, China, and the Philippines and receive the Purple Heart and the Gold Star. Daggett died at the age of 100 in 1938, making him the last surviving general of the Civil War.

Daggett's grandson was a prominent civil rights activist at the University of New Hampshire.[1]
