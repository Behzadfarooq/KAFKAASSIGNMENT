Aaron Gorrell

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Aaron Gorrell (born March 31, 1981 in Wollongong, New South Wales) is an Australian rugby league player for Les Catalans in Super League (Europe), having previously played for St George Illawarra Dragons in the National Rugby League competition. His position of choice is at hooker.

Gorrell was made famous by slotting in the last-minute conversion in a round 15, 2006 game against the Brisbane Broncos at Suncorp Stadium. The Dragons won the match 18-16, prompting Ray Warren to say: "I Don't know! Yes I do! The Dragons have got home!!"" Gorrell had converted rookie Brett Morris's try in the 78th minute after coach Nathan Brown gave him the goalkicking duties after Mathew Head had kicked the last two conversions.

[edit] Career highlights

  • Junior Club: Albion Park-Oak Flats
  • First Grade Debut: Round 5, St George Illawarra v Parramatta, WIN Stadium, April 14, 2002
  • First Grade Record: 22 appearances scoring 2 tries & 39 goals
  • Super League: 2007-2010 signing wioth Les Catalans
