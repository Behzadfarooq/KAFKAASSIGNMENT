Aaron Razel (אהרן רזאל) is an Israeli musician and his music explores topics such as Torah, Orthodox Judaism and living in Israel.

Song of Zion

[edit] Biography

Aaron Razel was born in New York in 1974 and came to Israel with his family when he was one month old. He started playing various instruments at an early age, mainly piano, guitar, violin and recorders. At the same time, he composed small pieces for different ensembles.

When Aaron was in 4th grade he and his family become religious so he moved to a religious school. But when he was in 7th grade he changed school again to the "High-School Near The Music Academy" in Jerusalem.

In 1988 he started harpsichord playing with David Shemer and a year later composition with Andre Hajdu.

At the age of 15 he was received at the Jerusalem Rubin Music And Dance Academy at the harpsichord and composition departments where he obtained a Bachelor of Music degree in 1994. And he completed his MA degree in composition at Bar-Ilan University under the guidance of Andre Hajdu.

During his period of studies in the Music Academy, Aaron performed a lot of harpsichord playing with different ensembles throughout the country. At the same time, he composed various chamber music works which were performed at the Jerusalem Theatre, Tel Aviv Museum, etc. He has been wining AICF scholarships since 1990.

In 1992 the Israel Chamber Orchestra performed his piece Shalom Rav Shooveh. In addition to his activities in the field of classical music, Aaron Razel composed 'light' music as well, and together with is brother, Jonathan Razel, had a record published by Phonokol in 1991.

He completed his army service as "outstanding musician" and on his army period he become less religious. He then went through self-searching which led him to visit in Zfat and in 1996 he moved there. (biography from his webpage)
