Aaron Harding

Aaron Harding (February 20, 1805 - December 24, 1875) was a United States Representative from Kentucky. He was born near Campbellsville, Kentucky where he attended the rural schools. He became familiar with the classics, studied law, was admitted to the bar in 1833 and commenced practice in Greensburg, Kentucky.

Harding was elected prosecuting attorney of Green County in 1833. He was member of the Kentucky House of Representatives in 1840 and was elected as a Unionist to the Thirty-seventh and Thirty-eighth Congresses and as a Democrat to the Thirty-ninth Congress (March 4, 1861-March 3, 1867). He was a delegate to the Union National Convention in 1866. After leaving Congress, he resumed the practice of law in Danville, Kentucky. He died in Georgetown, Kentucky in 1875 and was buried in Georgetown Cemetery.
