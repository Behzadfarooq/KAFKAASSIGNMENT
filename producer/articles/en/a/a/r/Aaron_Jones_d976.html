Aaron Jones, (born Poole, England) is a Scottish folk musician and instrumentalist. He was Instrumentalist of the Year in 2005 at the Scots Trad Music Awards and is a regional committee member of the Musicians Union of Scotland and Northern Ireland. He has toured in the USA, Canada, Australia and Europe with countless artists and groups such as the major Scottish folk group Old Blind Dogs and has made numerous TV and radio appearances worldwide.


[edit] Acclaim

Aaron is a founding partner in the traditional music resource www.tradmusic.com which launched in September 2002 and receives over 350,000 hits per week. Aaron has recorded on numerous CDâ€™s and continues to work regularly with some of the biggest names in traditional folk music, both as a performer and recently, as a producer.

Aaron is currently a member of Old Blind Dogs which incidently won Folk Band of the Year at the 2004 Scots Trad Music Awards. The band, was nominated in the Best Live Act category in the 2005 Awards, and are planning a new studio release in early 2007 on the Compass/Vertical record labels. Aaron is currently touring worldwide the band.

He is noted for his musical abilites on the Irish bouzouki, guitar, bass and singing and has regularly played with fellow acclaimed musician Claire Mann who is known for her musicianship in folk music on the flute and the fiddle. Claire as well as being highly regarded as a tutor of traditional music has toured extensively with bands such as Craobh Rua Tabache, and Christy O'Leary.


[edit] Personal life

Aaron Jones currently resides in Edinburgh, Scotland.
