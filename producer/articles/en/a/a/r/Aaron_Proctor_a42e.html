Aaron Daniel Proctor (born July 11, 1981 in Chester, Pennsylvania) is an American professional wrestling promoter and manager, 2006 California gubernatorial candidate, and 2007 mayoral candidate residing in Pasadena, California.

[edit] Professional wrestling career

His debut in wrestling was on May 28, 1998, in the Philadelphia, Pennsylvania area with the name "The Toreador Vampire". Afterwards, Proctor was part of the three-man group who promoted professional wrestling events in the Los Angeles, California area under the aegis of "Pacific Coast Wrestling/Pacific Championship Wrestling". There, he made his debut in November, 2002 and remained with the company until 2004. He also played mostly a heel (or villain) commissioner for PCW and referred to himself as "The Noticeable One". After a few short-lived stints in both Southern and Northern California (the latter with Supreme Pro Wrestling and North American Wrestling), Proctor left the industry and has been completely inactive since May, 2005.

During his professional wrestling stint, he had accompanied and managed professional wrestlers such as The Messiah, Trent Acid, Johnny Kashmere, B-Boy, Lil Cholo, Mace, "Ridiculous" Mike Rayne, Piercstin Steele and "Super" Dave Drexler.

[edit] Political ambitions

Proctor officially announced his write-in campaign for California governor for the General Election on November 7th, 2006. His campaign, as drawn out on his MySpace, detailed his aspirations for decriminalizing marijuana, abolishing the California state income tax, and offering incentives for people to attend their local community college. Proctor had received subtle yet vocal support from those in the California Goth subculture. On October 24, 2006, Proctor dropped out of the gubernatorial race, citing a snafu by the Los Angeles county Registrar.

He ran for the position of city-wide elected mayor of Pasadena, California, for the election held in March 2007. His opponent was current Mayor, Bill Bogaard. Proctor has detailed his aspirations on his website - including the introduction of rent control to Pasadena as well as abolishing overnight parking permits and proposing a city-wide proposal against the War in Iraq.

Nomination papers were issued to Proctor by the City of Pasadena on November 13, 2006. Proctor filed his signatures on November 22, 2006 and qualified for the March 6, 2007 ballot on December 5, 2006.

Proctor lost the election, receiving 1,472 votes (11.2% of the vote) on March 6th, 2007. He plans to stay involved in politics on a local level.
