Captain Aaron Kelly is a character in the reimagined Battlestar Galactica TV program. He is played by Ty Olsson. The character has been part of the show since the beginning (the 2003 Miniseries). Captain Kelly is the landing signal officer (LSO) aboard the Battlestar Galactica.[1] At the beginning of the second season, Captain Kelly was third in line of command, after Commander Adama and Colonel Tigh.[2] The character of Aaron Kelly has no direct analog in the original Battlestar Galactica

Spoiler warning: Plot and/or ending details follow.

Captain Kelly was first shown greeting Commander Adama in a corridor before the decommissioning ceremony for the Galactica. Later in the Miniseries, Kelly is shown collecting the dogtags of the fatalaties of the Cylons' earlier nuclear attack on the Galactica.[3]

Kelly became executive officer of Galactica after Adama was shot and fell comatose, as Tigh became commanding officer and commander of the fleet. In that capacity, Kelly strongly voiced his opposition to Lt. Felix Gaeta's plans to network Galactica's computers together to find the rest of the fleet after the fleet jumped using out-of-date jump coordinates, resulting in Galactica ending up alone.[2]

Kelly was shown performing his duties as LSO in the second-season episode "Final Cut". He is on duty when Lt. Louanne "Kat" Katraine panics and finds herself unable to safely land her Viper due to stimulant abuse. This episode was also the first to give the character's first name as Aaron.[1]

Kelly was still serving aboard the Galactica when she jumped away from New Caprica to avoid the Cylon invasion fleet.[4] He served in the CIC during the Battle of New Caprica, in which Galactica fought a diversionary action to allow the civilian population to escape from the Cylons.[5] Two weeks before the trial of former President Gaius Baltar were set to start, Kelley assassinated his first public defender and attempted to kill his second lawyer twice. Major Lee Adama uncovered the plot and confronted Kelly. The disciplinary action taken against him have yet to be revealed.
