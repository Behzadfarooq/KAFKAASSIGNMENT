Aaron Owens aka "AO" (born March 20, 1976) is an American streetball player from North Philadelphia. He has been with the AND1 Mixtape Tour since 2000 as one of the original members of the team.

Owens is reported to stand 6'3" (190.5 cm) tall and weigh 165 pounds (75 kg). Noted as an exceptional ballhandler and passer, he averages over 20 assists per game. One of his favorite moments from the tour was bouncing the ball off the backboard to peg an opponent in the back of the head.[1]

[edit] Background

"AO" inbounding the ball.

Owens, a Philadelphia native, attended Simon Gratz High School where he played alongside current NBA stars Rasheed Wallace and Aaron McKie. He also played for the NBDL Mobile Revelers in 2002-03, averaging 11.6 points, 2.7 rebounds and 4.4 assists per game.

When not playing ball, Owens keeps the team's morale up with his sense of humor. During a Season Four episode of the ESPN2 series "Streetball," he delivered a deadpan "So, is this what it has come to?" line just before the rims of his glasses lit up with blue neon. In the same episode he tried to persuade the tour manager's pet zebra to chase him. He was eventually paid to do so.

[edit] Trivia

  • Often throws his shoes and parts of his uniforms to fans after a game.
  • Leaves the designated "autograph" area after the game to meet and greet the fans waiting in line
  • Averages around 10-20 points per game and 20+ assists per game. With "ankle breakers" and "playground moves" included as statistics, he averages a triple-double.
  • Since 2004, Owens has sported a Mohawk hairstyle.
  • On the back of his jersey for the AND 1 Streetball Mix Tape Tour, Owens wears "AO" as his number and "Philly" as his name.
