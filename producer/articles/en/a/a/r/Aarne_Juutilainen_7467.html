Aarne Edward Juutilainen (* 18.10.1904 Sortavala 1904 - † 28.10.1976 Helsinki), nicknamed "The Terror of Morocco", was a Finnish army captain, who served (20.6.1930-20.6.1935) in the French Foreign Legion at The Moroccon colonial war. After this period he returned Finland and became a national hero at The Battle of Kollaa

His father Agnatic agnatic belonged to the danish clan and one his brother was Top Gun Pilot Ilmari Juutilainen

He was the one of key figures in The Battle of Kollaa at Finnish Winter War 1939-40. As a reference and source: The most famous war photo was his own battle troopsparticipated in the divine service during the Christmas day 1939.

[edit] Winter War

In November 1939, when Finland was invaded by the Soviet Union, no one expected that the small nation could resist the largest military force in the world. And no one anticipated that 1939 would be one of the coldest winters in recorded history, what historians have exactly called a frozen hell.

[edit] The Battle of Kollaa

The Battle of Kollaa is remembered from the Major General Hägglund's sentence "Will Kollaa hold? (Kestääkö Kollaa?)" which Lieutenant Aarne Juutilainen answered: "Yes it will hold unless we are told to run."

In fact, during his command at The Kollaa during december 1939 he negotiated together with Woldemar Hägglund about strategy of The Kollaa Front, which was extremely important at The Winter War. The Battle of Kollaa was the one of main battles in The Winter War. "Unless we are told to run" means exactly that, because a week ago he was received regiment-order by the regiment messenger office, cadet Ahti Vuorensola, to withdraw. And he didn't.

Afterwards lieutenant-colonel and regiment commander Wilhelm Teittinen, who commanded JR/34 at The Kollaa Front gave all honor "He created the Kollaa Spirit" just to captain Aarne Edward Juutilainen, why Kollaa held.

Of course, this was all teamwork and name such as freeherr Carl von Haartman and Simo Häyhä were important with other soldiers at The Kollaa Front during The Winter War.
