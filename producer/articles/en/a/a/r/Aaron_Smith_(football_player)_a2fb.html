Aaron Douglas Smith (born April 9, 1976 in Colorado Springs, Colorado) is a professional football player with the Pittsburgh Steelers in the National Football League. He plays left Defensive End in a 3-4 defensive alignment.

Smith played college football at the University of Northern Colorado. While there he was the team captain for two straight years and set a university career record with 44 sacks.

Smith was drafted by the Steelers in the 4th round (109th pick overall) of the 1999 NFL draft. He became a full-time starter in the 2000 season, starting 15 of 16 games that year. He has remained the Steelers starting left defensive end since that time. Smith has had 269 tackles, 29.5 sacks, 14 passes defensed, and 1 interception in his NFL career through the 2005 season.

Smith was named to the 2005 Pro Bowl as an injury replacement. He had 1 tackle and 1 sack in the game. Smith won a Super Bowl ring with the Steelers in Super Bowl XL following the 2005 season.

On February 27, 2007, the Steelers resigned Aaron Smith to a 5-year, $25 million contract which includes $12.5 million in the next two years. This contract makes him the highest paid defensive player currently on the team.
