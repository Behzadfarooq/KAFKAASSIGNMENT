The AAA Liberty District is a high school conference in the state of Virginia that includes schools exclusively from Northern Virginia. AAA is the largest enrollment class of the Virginia High School League and also typically the most competitive level in Virginia high school sports.

The Liberty District schools compete in the AAA Northern Region with the schools from the AAA Concorde District, the AAA National District, and the AAA Patriot District.

[edit] About the district

The Liberty District was founded in 1993 as part of an attempted realignment of the AAA Northern Region. The charter members were all Fairfax County schools, which were Fairfax, Falls Church, Langley, Lee, Madison, Marshall, and McLean High Schools.

All Liberty District schools are part of Fairfax County Public Schools except for Stone Bridge which is part of Loudoun County Public Schools. Currently the district's geography stretches as east as McLean, as south as Alexandria, and as north and west as Leesburg.

The smallest member is Marshall with roughly 1,400 students. The largest member is Woodson with about 2,100 students. Marshall and South Lakes are both considered to be AA in size by the VHSL, but have elected to play up in the Liberty instead, due to their reluctance to play against schools in the AA Dulles District which are also farther away.

The Liberty District contains most of the smaller AAA schools located in the western part of the Northern Region and six of its eight members play Division 5 football. Langley and Woodson play Division 6 football, but are the two smallest Division 6 schools in the Northern Region, and were bumped up to Division 6 when Hayfield of the Patriot District fed a considerable amount of its student body into South County.

[edit] Member Changes

The Liberty District has experienced the most change of all four Northern Region districts. In 1996, the Liberty District moved Falls Church to the National District to reunite it with its primary rival Stuart, but added Broad Run and Park View High Schools of Loudoun County who played in the AA Northwestern District. When a new Loudoun County high school opened in 1997, they were AA size again. This was remedied in 1999 when Broad Run and Park View returned to the Northwestern, and Woodson was added to reunite it against its rival Fairfax.

In 2003, South Lakes joined the district, bringing the Liberty back to eight teams. In 2005, the Liberty went through a significant change by removing charter members Fairfax and Lee to the Concorde and Patriot Districts respectively. Its geography expanded considerably eastward and westward by adding Jefferson of Alexandria from the Concorde and Stone Bridge of Ashburn from the AA Dulles District.

[edit] Athletics

Athletically, Liberty schools are best known for the strength of their swimming, cheerleading, field hockey, boys basketball, and softball teams with many of their members winning Northern Region and/or state titles in the last few years. All Liberty District members field teams in all VHSL sports except field hockey and indoor track where Stone Bridge does not field a team because Loudoun County Public Schools currently does not sponsor them as varsity level sports.

In terms of overall performance in athletics, Stone Bridge was 8th place in the standings for the AAA Wachovia Cup for Athletics. The Bulldogs were also the top Division 5 school based on the standings. Marshall also placed 30th in the Athletic standings.

[edit] Academics

Liberty District members are very strong academically. All of its members are above average performers in the state on standardized tests and all offer rigorous curricula for their students which include courses in AP and for the IB programs. Jefferson is one of the nation's top magnet schools in science and technology. Langley, Madison, McLean, and Woodson High Schools are consistently the highest performing comprehensive high schools in Fairfax County and also are among the best in the nation.

The Liberty is one of the strongest districts in the state in VHSL academic activities. Langley, Jefferson, and McLean finished in the top 10 for the AAA Wachovia Cup for Academics in the 2005-2006 school year placing 3rd, 4th, and 7th respectively. Marshall was 12th and Woodson was 19th.
