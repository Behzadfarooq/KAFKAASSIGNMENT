The American Association of Grant Professionals (or AAGP) is a nonprofit membership association that builds and supports an international community of grant professionals committed to serving the greater public good by practicing the highest ethical and professional standards. Since its inception in 1998, AAGP has grown rapidly, with over 1,200 members, as of 2006.

To achieve its mission, AAGP seeks to serve as a leading authority and resource for the practice of grantsmanship in all sectors of the field, advances the field by promoting professional growth and development, enhances the public image and recognition of the profession within the greater philanthropic, public, and private funding communities, and promotes positive relationships between grant professionals and their stakeholders.

AAGP is directed by an eleven-member board and has two types of members, legacy and regular. Legacy members are those individuals who joined the Association before the end of the first annual conference (November, 1999). Regular members are individuals who joined after the first conference.

As of 2006, the organization had established chapters operating in: Alabama, Arizona, California, Washington D.C., Florida, Georgia, Illinois, Indiana, Iowa, Kansas, Massachusetts, Missouri, Nebraska, New York, New Jersey, Ohio, Puerto Rico, Tennessee, Texas, and Virginia.
