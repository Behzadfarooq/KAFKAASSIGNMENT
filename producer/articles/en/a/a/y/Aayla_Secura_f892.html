Aayla Secura is a character in the fictional Star Wars universe. In the films Attack of the Clones and Revenge of the Sith, she was portrayed by Amy Allen. The character was created for Dark Horse Comics by John Ostrander and Jan Duursema. She appears primarily in the Star Wars: Republic and Star Wars: Clone Wars series. The character is unusual in that she is one of the few Expanded Universe characters to appear in the Star Wars films.

[edit] History

[edit] Early years

Secura was discovered on her home planet, Ryloth, by the Jedi Quinlan Vos, who trained her as his padawan. While on Ryloth, her uncle, Pol, robbed her and Vos of their memories. Then unaware of her Force potential, she produced a huge outburst, killing her uncle. She then fled the planet.

She fell under the spell of ancient Anzati Dark Jedi Volfe Karkko, who was being held prisoner on Kiffex. In a trance, she freed Karkko and attacked Vos. At this time, she regained her memory and realized what was happening. After this conflict, Vos and Secura were separated. Jedi Master Tholme took over her training, and she soon became a Jedi Knight.

[edit] Attack of the Clones

Aayla appears twice In Attack of the Clones, as she is seen while Obi Wan is going to visit Yoda and the younglings. The next time she is seen is at the battle of Geonosis, where she was one of the 200 Jedi sent to Geonosis to rescue Padmé Amidala, Obi-Wan Kenobi and Anakin Skywalker, and one of the few survivors of the ensuing battle with Count Dooku's Separatist forces. After the Battle of Geonosis, Secura and Jedi Ylenic It'kla went undercover for a mission to Corellia.

[edit] Clone Wars

Secura once again went undercover while trying to find Vos on The Wheel space station. She found Vos, who was distraught about what he believed to be his failure to prevent the Battle of Geonosis. Secura then reassured her Master that the battle had not been his fault, and they gathered enough information to predict the attack on Kamino. Secura flew a starfighter during that battle.

A few months later, Secura was sent to Hypori. She was one of the first to encounter General Grievous, and was one of the few survivors.

Later, she went on a Mission to Devaron, and wore a disguise once again. She was part of a Jedi taskforce to investigate CIS raids on Republic supply lines. While on this mission, a Devaronian Senator was killed. Aayla, Tholme, and The Dark Woman went down to the planet. While there, Aurra Sing attacked, and Secura engaged her in battle. On this mission, Aayla began to develop a close relationship with Kit Fisto. It is reported that outside of Jedi law, the two might have eventually married.

In volume 2 of Cartoon Network's Clone Wars microseries, she is seen being treated by clones, along with Shaak Ti and Ki-Adi-Mundi, after a fierce battle with Grievous.

[edit] Revenge of the Sith

Months later, Secura and Commander Bly were sent to Honoghr. Secura discovered Vos there, who was also looking for the same thing she was: the SIP. Together they found it, but Vos would not let her have it, and they got into a struggle in which Vos succumbed to his anger and the dark side. Aayla dropped her weapon, and Vos was unable to kill his protegé and friend. Bly took advantage of Vos' indecision and wounded him. He and Secura then escaped.

While searching for the second Sith, Secura and Tholme went to Anzat and later Saleucami, where the Separatists were building an army of Morgukai clone troopers. She flew a star fighter in the ensuing battle, which lasted for five months. When it finally ended, Secura and Bly were sent to find Barriss Offee on Felucia. Their mission was to stop the Commerce Guild from taking over the planet. Before the mission was over, however, Order 66 was issued and Bly was ordered to kill her. Secura was distracted by a Felucian bird, alerting her towards the danger around her. However, it was too late, and she died at her former comrade's hand. As stated by a 501st soldier in Battlefront II he hoped that "her death was quick, she earned that much", because she had saved them from being eradicated by Acklays, droids and helped them maintain their sanity while they suffered from the planet's flesh-eating viruses. They did not want to kill her, though they were replaced before Order 66 by another garrison.

[edit] Depiction in Star Wars Films

The Aayla Secura character was included in Attack of the Clones after George Lucas noticed a painting of the character near the end of that film's production. Lucas was so impressed by the artwork that he decided to include the character in the film. Amy Allen, a production assistant at Industrial Light and Magic, was given the role, and digitally added to scenes in the Jedi Temple and during the climactic battle on Geonosis. Amy Allen reprised the role for Revenge of the Sith and was even given a few lines of dialogue, although these were cut from the final film. John Ostrander had originally planned to kill the character off during her first appearance in Jedi: Twilight but he was convinced to let her live by Jan Duursema's daughter. Rather than dying in the comic media for which she was written, Secura's death is shown during the massacre of the Jedi in Revenge of the Sith.
