AAI Corporation (Aircraft Armaments Incorporated) is an aerospace and defense manufacturing firm in Hunt Valley, Maryland. AAI is a subsidiary of United Industrial Corporation, and is currently a major developer and producer of unmanned aerial vehicles. AAI employs more than 2,000 and reports annual sales in excess of $350 million.

[edit] History

AAI was established in Hunt Valley, Maryland in 1950, and has since expanded with further key facilities in Charleston, South Carolina as well as field offices throughout the United States.

In the mid 1990's the company formed a spin-off company called "ETI" (Electric Transit Incorporated) and began to dabble in electric public transit buses with Škoda in the Czech Republic. Using the method of lowest-bidder wins, ETI and Škoda won a contract for a medium sized trolleybus fleet in Dayton, Ohio, and went on to win a fairly large job in San Francisco, California. The designs of fundamental systems within the fleets turned out to be majorly flawed before even a handful of buses were put into service. The mounting design problems were coupled with growing personnel, manufacturing, management, legal, and vendor problems in the US and overseas. Insiders soon discovered that the technology coming from Škoda (used in most of the major systems) turned out to be using methods and materials dating back to the 1940s. AAI/ETI, Škoda, and all of the vendors began growing more focused on lawyers, legal battles, and internal audits until it became obvious to the transit agencies' managements that the project had been abandoned from a perspective of progress. Due to these two major business failures, Škoda closed their doors on their US transit business in 2003. Shortly thereafter AAI/ETI decided to follow suit thereby coming clean in their abandonment of the public transit business for good.

AAI Corporation maintains an operating subsidiary in the United Kingdom, ESL Defence Limited, which is focused on electronic warfare systems.

[edit] Products

AAI provides a range of (mostly aviation-related) testing, simulation, maintenance, and logistics services to the United States armed forces.

[edit] Unmanned aerial vehicles

Since 1985, AAI has become a leading producer of unmanned aerial vehicles:

  • RQ-2 Pioneer (first successful design; in partnership with Israel Aircraft Industries)
  • RQ-7 Shadow now offered in three versions:
    • Shadow 200 tactical UAV
    • Shadow 400 shipboard UAV
    • Shadow 600 enhanced tactical UAV
  • AAI iSTAR VTOL UAV

In addition to their line of vehicles, AAI produces a mobile Tactical Ground Control Station for UAV operations. The station is normally configured for use with Shadow UAVs, but can be reconfigured for other types.

[edit] Training and testing systems

  • Training systems
    • On Board Trainer [1] (OBT) systems for naval weapon and radar system training
    • Moving Target Simulator [2] (MTS) systems for battlefield simulation training
    • Battle Readiness Electronic Warfare Trainer BREWT, a desktop electronic warfare threat trainer for pilots
    • Howitzer Crew Trainer (HCT), a component of the Fire Support Combined Arms Trainer (FSCAT) of the United States Army
  • Testing systems
    • Advanced Boresight Equipment (ABE) for improving weapon system accuracy, used by NAVAIR, Boeing, and Eurofighter
    • Joint Service Electronic Combat Systems Tester (JSECST) for ensuring effectiveness of aircraft electronic warfare systems prior to take off
    • Electro-optic testers by ESL Defence Limited
    • C-17 Maintenance Training System by AAI Services - Goose Creek, SC
    • F-22 Maintenance Training System by AAI Services - Goose Creek, SC

[edit] Advanced programs

  • PDCue counter-sniper system for detection and target acquisition
  • MHU-204 munitions handling unit for the United States Air Force for use with B-52, B-1B, and B-2A bombers
  • Cargo monorail system for the United States Navy for use on LHD class amphibious assault ships
  • M646 76 mm armor-piercing FSDS-T round
  • Lightweight Small Arms Technologies program for the U.S. Army.
