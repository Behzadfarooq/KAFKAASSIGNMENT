[edit] Trademark issue

Asahi Foods registered açaí as a trademark in Japan. The case has been dealt with by the Brazilian foreign office -- and Brazilians have strong feelings regarding this. One of the consequences is that the Brazilian government is distributing to Patent Offices all over the world a list of about 3,000 names of plants, animals, foodstuffs, beverages and spices typical of Brazil to prevent foreign companies from doing this type of brand piracy (which is tantamount to an attempt to steal what belongs to us). jggouvea 01:39, 6 March 2007 (UTC)

You nonbelievers out there, when the medical community catches up, you'll jump on. Life is short, don't be so skeptical.

MonaVie testimony is highly suspect. Its smart to be skeptical where money-making is involved. MonaVie is a Multi-Level Marketing company charging $40/750 ml of juice, and promising members easy money via a pyramid scheme in addition to easy health from its overpriced juice.
It is interesting to us, Brazilians, how something that we just drink for the taste, is quickly accepted by gullible foreigners as a miraculous cure-all. ;-) Really, really funny it is. Açai is a fruit juice! Just that. OK, it contains some substances that may be stimulating (much the same way as caffeine) and it is a bit more nutritious than the average fruit, but it is still only a nutritious fruit juice. But we Brazilians are already expecting to see some foolish gringo say it cures cancer, reverses strokes, burns the excess calories you ingest, is a booster for manhood and the like. Isn't it funny how people tend to think that anything foreign and unknown to them is miraculous (even when another people know that thing pretty well for centuries)?
