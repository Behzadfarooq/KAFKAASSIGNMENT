Archon (disambiguation)

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Look up archon in Wiktionary, the free dictionary.

Archon (Gr. αρχων, pl. αρχοντες) is a Greek word that means "ruler" or the like.

Archon may also refer to:

  • Archon of Pella, a satrap of Babylonia and one of the Diadochi
  • Archon (butterfly), a genus of butterfly
  • Archons of the Ecumenical Patriarchate, an award of merit bestowed by the Patriarch of Constantinople
  • Archon (computer game), a computer game from the 1980s
  • Archon (Dungeons & Dragons), a fantasy race featured in the Dungeons & Dragons role-playing game
  • Archon (convention), a science fiction/gaming convention held in Collinsville, Illinois USA
  • Protoss Archon, a Protoss creature in the computer game StarCraft
  • Archon, Aisne, a commune of the Aisne département in northern France
  • Archon X Prize, a genomics contest
  • Archons, the villainous race of hyper-dimensional entities from the comic book series The Invisibles
  • Archons, the crew of the U.S.S. Archon in the original series Star Trek episode "The Return of the Archons"
  • Archons, the leaders of the Dark Eldar in the tabletop wargame Warhammer 40,000
  • The Archon is the second book of Oracle Prophecies Trilogy by Catherine Fisher. In the trilogy, the Archon is the title of the God-on-Earth.
