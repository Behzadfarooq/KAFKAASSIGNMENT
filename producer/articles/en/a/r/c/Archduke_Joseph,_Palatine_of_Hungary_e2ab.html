Archduke Joseph, Palatine of Hungary

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Archduke Joseph c. 1840

Palatine Joseph of Hungary or Archduke Joseph Anton Johann of Austria (9 March 1776, Florence – 13 January 1847, Buda), in Hungarian József nádor, was the Palatine of Hungary between 1796 and 1847. He was the eighth son of Emperor Leopold II of Austria and Maria Luisa of Spain.

He was very popular among the Magyars and became the founder of the Hungarian branch of the Habsburg family.

His son, Palatine Stephen of Hungary became the last Palatine of Hungary.

He had several children, most of them born from his third marriage and late in his life:

  • Archduke Joseph Charles Louis of Austria (1833-1905), the younger son of Palatine Joseph of his third marriage with Maria Dorothea of Wurttemberg.
  • Archduchess Elisabeth Franziska of Austria, who married 1) Archduke of Austria-Este and 2) Archduke of Austria-Teschen
  • Henriette of Austria who became Queen Consort of the Belgians as wife of Leopold II of Belgium
