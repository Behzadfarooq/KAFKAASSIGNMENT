Archie Birkin

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Charles Archibald Cecil "Archie" Birkin (30 March 1905 â€“ 7 June 1927) was a British motorcycle racer, brother to Tim Birkin one of the "Bentley Boys" of the 1920s.

[edit] Background and family

He was born into a wealthy Nottingham family in 1896. He was the son of Sir Thomas Stanley Birkin, 2nd Bt. and Hon. Margaret Diana Hopetoun Chetwynd.

[edit] Racing career

During an early morning practice session for the 1927 Isle of Man TT, Archie Birkin swerved to avoid a fish-van travelling to Peel and collided with a wall and was killed. The corner in Kirk Michael on the A3 primary road where the accident occurred was renamed 'Birkins Bend.' From 1928 practice sessions for the Isle of Man TT Races and Manx Grand Prix where held on closed-roads.
