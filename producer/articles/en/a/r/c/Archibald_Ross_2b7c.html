Archibald Ross

Sir Archibald Ross OBE (1867 - March 19, 1931) was a pioneering marine engineer.

He was 18 years of age when he became a pupil of Messrs. R & W Hawthorn at their St.Peters Works, Newcastle, England and all his working life was spent with this well-known engineering and ship-building company. During Sir Archibald's regime turbines and boilers for some of the heaviest units in the Royal Navy were built. He attended the National Labor Conference at Washington, USA in 1910 as an employers' representative. His work on the Merchant Shipbuilding Advisory Committe and on the Admiralty Shipbuilding Council was recognized officially in June 1921, when he was made a Knight Commander of the Order of the British Empire, this following the CBE that he had been awarded three years previously.

He served two terms as President of the North-East Coast Institution of Engineers and Shipbuilders, and was a director of Messrs. Ruston-Bucyrus Ltd, specialists in excavating machinery which incorporated the old established firms of Ruston and Hornsby Ltd. of Lincoln and the Bucyrus-Erie Co. of South Milwaukee USA.

He was also a member of the Institution of Civil Engineers, the Institution of Mechanical Engineers, on the Council of the Institution of Naval Arcitects, and a member of the Institute of Metals.

He was a great lover of music and president of the North of England Musical Tournament.

He married in 1896 and had four sons, three of whom served in the War, in the Royal Navy.

One son, George Campell Ross, Lieutenant Commander was captain of the battleship HMS Rodney and later Admiral of the Royal Navy. He married, on September 16, 1929 Alice Behrens, daughter of the banker Paul Behrens (of Bank Salomon & Oppenheim) in Berlin, the first German women to marry an Englishman after World War I.
