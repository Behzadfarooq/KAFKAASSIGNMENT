Archdeaconry of Lothian

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

The Archdeaconry of Lothian was a sub-division of the diocese of St Andrews, one of two archdeaconries within the diocese. The Lothian archdeaconry was headed by the Archdeacon of Lothian, a subordinate of the Bishop of St Andrews. In the medieval period, the Archdeaconry of Lothian contained three deaneries with a total of 111 parish churches. The deaneries were Linlithgow (36 churches), Haddington (35 churches) and Merse (40 churches).
