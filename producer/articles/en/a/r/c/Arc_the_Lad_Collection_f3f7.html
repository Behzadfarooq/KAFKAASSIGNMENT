The Arc the Lad Collection is a compilation of the Arc the Lad RPG series. The games were localized by Working Designs, which closed in December 2005. Plans to localize the games had been fostered by Working Designs since the late 1990’s, however it wasn’t till the new millennium that the plans came to fruition. The releases of Lunar: Silver Star Story Complete, and Lunar 2: Eternal Blue Complete, known for the quality of not only the localization but also the quality of the packaging itself, preceded Arc the Lad.

[edit] Background

On April 18, 2002, after a series of unmet release dates, the Arc the Lad Collection was released in North America. This was the first time North American gamers were able to play the extremely popular Japanese RPG series.

The collection encompasses six discs, and four games. Arc the Lad 1; which was released on June 30, 1995 in Japan, set the stage for the series, and pioneered such features as computer generated videos, and orchestrated music. A first for RPGs of the time, which are now commonplace. The second game featured in the compilation is Arc the Lad 2; released less than 5 months after Arc 1, on November 1, 1995. Originally both games were intended to be one, however due to time constraints and deadlines, Arc 1 was released ahead of time. Both games were extremely commercially successful in Japan, due to the unique features such as computer generated video and orchestral music, as well as an intriguing storyline that enthralled fans. An additional innovative feature in console role-playing games is present in Arc 2. This is the ability to import saved game data from Arc 1 at the beginning of Arc 2. Items, character levels, and other bonuses can be included in Arc 2.

Continuing the compilation is Arc Arena: Monster Tournament. This further builds on the concept of data transfer and allows players to trade items and weapons with other players by importing their respective saved games. Monsters, who can be captured and controlled in Arc 2, can also be used in this game to fight tournament-style matches. Arc the Lad 3, which was released on October 28, 1999, is the last game featured in the collection. This game features an updated battle and graphics system, while still staying true to its roots.

The collection also features the documentary disc Making of Arc the Lad, which includes exclusive interviews with Victor Ireland, the former president of Working Designs. It comes with a leather-bound, 150-page, full-colour instruction booklet. It also includes the Omake(O-Ma-ka)Box (Omake is the Japanese word for extra). The Omake Box features cardboard miniature standees of all 22 characters, four analog stick covers - one of Arc, one of Elc, one of Alec and one of the Arc the Lad emblem - and finally a memory card featuring Arc's face.

Despite the time and effort spent on the collection, it was not the financial smash it should have been. Modern North American RPG fans were not impressed by the graphics and sound effects, which upon North American release seemed dated. This is despite the fact that the effects present in Arc the Lad 1 and 2 revolutionized RPGs when they were originally released. Many potential fans were deterred by the long delays, as well as the price tag, which some considered too be too high, even with four games and a plethora of extras.

[edit] Gameplay

[edit] Battle

Each game in the Arc the Lad series, up until Arc the Lad: End of Darkness (known as Generations in Japan), uses a turn-based-strategy RPG style. Each character or enemy is assigned agility points which determines the order in which they receive their turn in battle. The character or enemy with the next highest agility statistic goes next and so on, until the round is finished and a new round begins. Agility can increase as a character’s level increases, can be altered by equipping items, or augmented temporarily with spells and items. When it is a character's turn they may perform several actions. They may move around the battle screen - how far a character moves depends on their Range statistic. The Range stat is set at the beginning of the game, and cannot increase as a character levels up. It can be increased by equipping certain items and can be decreased by using spells. Characters are able to move up to an enemy by jumping over obstacles, which requires a jump statatistic of one, or by jumping over allies and enemies, requiring a jump statatistic of two. Once characters have an enemy in range, characters can do a number of things. Characters can use a basic attack - how much damage the character's attack deals depends on the character's attack statistic. As a character's point|experience level increases so does this statistic. Further alterations to statistics are accomplished through the equipping of accessory items. Spells and items can also augment statistics temporarily.

The battle system is, however, not all melee-based. Each character has a plethora of abilities available, ranging from magic spells to special techniques that either deal damage to enemies, heal ally characters, or augment statistics. These abilities are selectable though the ability wheel in Arc the Lad, however in later titles, a list interface was created for easier use. As in most RPGs, abilities and spells require MP to use. If the character does not have enough MP, certain techniques become unusable. However, MP can also be absorbed, restored and depleted using spells or items. The power of a character’s spell depends on a character’s magic statistic, and higher experience levels increase this statistic. As the magic statistic increases so will the effectiveness characters spells.

Other options in battle include the use of items. The range a character can throw the item depends on the character’s throw level. As this ability increases, so does the distance the character can throw an item.

Characters may also equip items during battle, or the player may check a character’s status in the status menu. Once it is the enemy's turn to attack they may perform actions similar to those of the player’s characters. How much damage a character takes from the enemy depends on the character's defense rating; the higher the defense, the less damage the character will receive. When the player’s characters are attacked, they have a chance to counter the enemy. The chance of a successful counter increases as the character’s counterattack level increases. When an enemy uses an item against a character, there is a chance the character may catch it and keep it or throw it back. This chance increases as the character’s catch level increases. When a character is hit his or her HP will decrease. Once the hit points of a certain character reach zero, the character is removed from combat. No characters are permanently lost by being felled in battle.

[edit] Equipment

In every RPG, how characters use and equip items, weapons and armor is very important. Throughout the Arc the Lad series, the equipment system changes from its very simple beginnings to a full-blown equipment system in later titles. In Arc the Lad, only accessories can be equipped, and can only be chosen at the start of battle. Accessories boost statistics in various ways, and can be dropped by defeated enemies, received from opening chests in battle, received from NPCs, or found in the few explorable areas the game presents. Unlike most RPGs, the weapons and armor a character uses cannot be changed. Despite some accessories bearing weapon- or armor-like names, such as the Phantom Set of items (gauntlet, ring, shield, and sword), there are no weapon or armor systems.

Arc the Lad 2 boosts the gameplay by including a revamped weapon system. Weapons, armor, items, and accessories can now be equipped on characters. Items, armor, and weapons can be bought in stores, found in battle, found in explorable areas or created in the combination shop. The combination shop requires that ingredients be brought and assembled. Each character has several types of equipable weapons. For example, Shu can equip battle shoes or fire arms such as assault rifles and shotguns. Weapons can also be improved, and can gain a +1 beside their name to indicate their increased parameters. This new equipment system also improves the battle system, as some weapons, such as guns and spears, have further range than swords and other short-range melee weapons.

Lieza, one of the principle characters in Arc the Lad 2, can also tame monsters, which can sometimes use human equipment. These monsters can be used in battle as well, as party selection is variable in Arc the Lad 2, based on the amount of characters the player has recruited and whether certain characters are usable at certain times in the game.

A similar system is found in Arc the Lad 3, however the synthesis guild replaces the combination shop, and, to many fans, the synthesis guild is far more indepth than the combination shop. Recipes must be found by the players, unlike in Arc the Lad 2 where the shop would tell you exactly what to use. The monster system is limited to a card system, where monsters can be trapped in cards and later used for attacks.

[edit] Exploration

Exploration is very simple in the beginning. In Arc the Lad, the world is only explored in a limited capacity. The player selects an area on a world map and then proceeds through the events and battles present in that area. Sometimes after a battle is fought, the player can explore the area in a limited capacity.

By contrast, Arc the Lad 2 has a fully explorable world. The world map is richly coloured and details, where characters can roam freely, instead of having a simple overhead view map. Cities and dungeons also allow the player to freely explore.

[edit] Side Quests

In Arc the Lad 1 there are only three major sidequests. The first is the fifty floor dungeon in Alatos and the second is the Battle Arena. Both are explored in the storyline, but can be revisited for the sidequests later in the game. A harder tournament in the coliseum is attainable upon loading a clear game saved game. Also the completing of Chongara's Monster Book, which requires Chongara to seek out every monster in the game (including the party, his summons and some of the games bosses, e.g Dark Arc) This can also be continued in Arc 2, as Lieza has the same ability.

Arc the Lad II features battle arenas and hidden dungeons; even the fifty floor dungeon makes a return. A full-blown sidequest system through the Hunters Guild has been implemented. In this system players can hunt rare monsters found on wanted posters and pursue individual sidequests in order to gain funds and standing in the guild. The quests range from dark quests dealing with murder, to more comedic quests, some featuring returning antagonists. Usually the Hunters Guild quests deal with themes not present in the main storyline of the game.

Arc The Lad III transforms this system into the breadth of the game. Most plot advancement is accomplished through guild quests.

[edit] Story

[edit] Arc the Lad

Main article: Arc the Lad (video game)

The story begins ten years ago with the flame on Mount Cion near the village of Touvil on the continent of Seirya being extinguished. Yoshua, Arc's father, must leave Arc's mother, Polta, and Arc to travel up the mountain, he never returns. Ten years later Kukuru of the Sacred White Clan travels up the mountain to extinguish the flame in order to escape her obligation to wed the crown prince of Palencia, however this prince is never mentioned in detail. When she extinguishes the flame an evil spirit is released and a terrible storm hits the village.

The game cuts to Arc’s house, where Arc is eager to open the chest containing his father’s sword and armor. Polta finally agrees to open the chest, and with the sword and armor Arc travels the long road up the mountain. On the way to the top Arc encounters Kukuru who wants to relight the flame; Arc takes her torch and claims he will relight it for her. Near the summit, the Ark Ghoul, who was released when Kukuru extinguished the Flame Cion, fells Arc. As Arc lies dying in the snow, he is saved when he receives the power of the Spirits/Guardians and is sent on a quest to save the world from humanity's greed. Arc relights the Flame Cion, which causes the Arc Ghoul, who was about to fell Kukuru, to disappear.

Arc is summoned to Palencia Castle by the King himself, as his deeds become well-known nation-wide. Kukuru tells him she will complete her training and then join him on his quest, for their destinies are intertwined. At the castle gate he sees soldiers leaving, but lagging behind the rest is a small podgy soldier who cries out, "Wait for me." Upon arriving in the throne room, Arc tells the King of his quest. The King's minister, Andel, is skeptical, but the King believes Arc. The King tells Arc that his brother, the crown prince, who disappeared TEN years ago, was under a similar assumption. But Andel says he does not trust Arc, and that Arc must be tested; he must destroy all the monsters on the Colbo Plains.

Arc leaves the castle and travels to the Colbo Plains, where he meets the small, podgy soldier from before. He is on the run from some monsters and tells Arc that his battalion has been killed and he is scared of fighting. Together Arc and the soldier defeat the monsters. The soldier tells Arc his name is Poco and that he was not afraid when fighting with Arc. On Arc’s return to the castle, he must help Kukuru defeat some monsters invading the castle, she joins Arc’s party permanently. The King then gives Arc’s party the use of the Silver Noah airship and of its driver Chopin. Arc then departs for Milmarna, as instructed by the king.

When Arc’s party arrives at Capitol HQ they are met by General Yagun and his green monkey who says Arc must prove himself by defeating all the monsters in the Ruvag Woods and Sembara Marsh areas, before being permitted to enter Toyoke Forest. When Arc fells all the monsters, he travels to Toyoke Forest where Arc must defeat more enemies until his guide runs away as instructed. Arc at last meets the Forest spirit. She tells Arc to gather the five power stones. She reveals that Arc's father is still alive and visited her ten years ago, where she nursed him back to health. A further earth-shattering revelation is that Arc’s father is none other than the missing crown prince. Upon receiving the Hero Crest from the Spirit Arc, he is instructed to return to Seirya.

Once Arc returns to Seirya he is told to return to the castle. Once there the player witnesses a scene where Andel throws a man with ginger hair into a dungeon. The player sees the man in the dungeon mad with rage as he breaks free from the cell and ascends to the warehouse; there Arc meets him and helps him fight the Palencia military corps, who are actually monsters. The man is revealed to be Tosh, and upon using Kukuru’s mirror of truth, it is revealed that Tosh is of pure intent. After the battle, Tosh refuses to join Arc and storms off in a huff.

Arc returns to Touvil to find the town has been burnt down. A postman arrives delivering a letter from Yoshua, Arc’s father. Arc's father suggests that Arc should seek out the Ancient Book at the Ancient Monolith. At the Ancient Monolith, Arc and company get sucked into the Illusion World, where they defeat the Illusion Lord. It turns out "the book" was in fact an old man called Gogen. Gogen is a 3,000 year-old sorcerer who fought as one of the Seven Braves. Alatos is the first destination for the new team. A scene involving Tosh is next shown. Tosh is told by the spirit of his sword that it is his destiny to follow Arc. Tosh rushes to the sky port and joins Arc on his quest.

Upon arriving in Alatos, Arc meets Chongara, the fat shopkeeper. He says he will not show Arc where the Light Guardian resides until he fetch the shopkeeper the Summon Pot from the fifth Forbidden Ruins. When Arc returns to Chongara's shop he lets Arc and company stay the night for free. He travels with Arc to Azenda Highway and helps the party beat the monsters there. After Arc finishes, he receives the Light Stone from the Light Guardian. Chongara has a flashback where he remembers Yoshua, Arc's father, rescuing him and making him swear to help Arc. Chongara, despite his weight, catches up to Arc's gang and joins them. Gogen uses the Light Stone to reveal the location of the next stone. The team decides to head to Greyshinne and retrieve the next spirit stone.

Upon Arc’s arrival in Greyshinne it is decided that Arc must travel to Mount Amaidar. But first, he must pass through the temple of the Amaidar Monks, a place where some of the most fearsome fighters in the world are trained. The temple master tells Iga, the temple’s strongest monk, to repel the "invaders." Iga challenges Arc to a one-on-one duel in the temple's main grounds. After Arc defeats Iga, he is taken to the head monk, who proclaims that Iga is a disgrace. But it turns out that the head monk and his chief followers are all monsters, as revealed by Kukuru’s mirror. Iga joins Arc to beat his former master. After the battle, Iga is temporarily disheartened, but soon accompanies Arc to the top of the mountain. Monsters are already there, defiling the sacred shrine. After another battle, Iga laments the destruction of the sacred texts. But the Earth Guardian reprimands him, telling the party that he had lost faith in humanity, but that Arc is different. As a result, the Earth Guardian gives Arc the Earth Stone. The party returns to the sky port where Gogen finds the location of the Wind Stone by using the Earth Stone - the small country of Niedel.

Upon arriving in Niedel Arc goes to the Tournament Arena under the suspicion that the Wind Orb Tournament has something to do with the Wind Stone. Arc and his party do not trust the tournament announcer. After winning the tournament, Arc receives the orb but whilst trying to figure out its secrets, he is attacked by the announcer, who turns out to be a monster. After defeating him, the wind orb breaks, releasing the Wind Spirit. The Wind Spirit gives Arc the Wind Stone and his blessing. Back at the Skyport Gogen uses his magic to find the location of the next stone, it is the Water Stone in the desert nation of Zariban.

Arc is met in Zariban by a local villager. He leads him back to the Saryu village. Once there, you will witness it set alight by the Romalian Army. Enraged by the genocide of the Saryu tribesmen, Arc challenges the Romalian military; they too appear to be monsters. After defeating them, the Saryu tribesmen warn Arc of the danger surrounding the Water Guardian. Arc and this party hurry off to the temple of the Water Guardian. The temple is full of monsters. After Arc rids the temple of monsters, the Water Guardian gives Arc the Water Stone. Back at the Skyport, Gogen discovers the final power stone, the Fire Stone, is in Seirya.

Back to Palencia Catle, where the group sneak in to avoid the evil Andel. Down in the secret wharehouse beneath the castle, Arc discovers the research lab. The scientists turn into their monster forms. After defeating the evil scientists, Arc and the group walk over to the glass cylinder hooked up with wires, which hold a mysterious something inside. It is broken open to reveal the Fire Spirit, who tells of how he was captured and used to power the evil intent of Andel, a Romalian general. He tells Arc and the group of his hatred for humanity. He gives Arc the Fire Stone and then departs. Unfortunately, a scientist activates the castle's self-destruct system, and the group heads for the elevator. Arc stays behind to fend off the monsters, Kukuru jumps off the elevator to help Arc. The two defeat all of the monsters and then enter the elevator.

Arc and Kukuru catch up with the others and Arc decides to rescue the King and help him escape. Then Andel kills the King in front of Arc and the others, telling them they are too late. Arc runs to the King who apologises to his nephew saying that he drove his brother, Yoshua, Arc's father, away by scheming with Andel so he could take the throne. He then dies in Arc's arms. Chopin rescues all of the group in the Silver Noah. Andel then gives a news report saying that the destruction of Palencia Caastle was the fault Arc's group and frames Arc for the death of the King.

The group does not give up hope; they know they have to find the Arc. Chongara comes up with the idea of disguising themselves as Hemo-Jii. They sneak back to Touvil, and Arc and Kukuru ascend to the summit alone. Once nearly there, Arc and Kukuru share a passionate kiss confessing their love for each other. Arc and Kukuru extinguish the flame once more and free the Ark Ghoul who says he will meet them at Sabatico Falls. After fighting their way down the mountain, the party travels to the nearby waterfall, Sabatico Falls. Once there, they must battle the Ark Ghouls and their friends the Arch Fiends. After the battle, Arc and Gogen use the Power Stones to create a path through the waterfall to the cave of the Ark.

Once in the cave the voice of the Ark tells them they will need testing to see if they are worthy. They must fight the evil forms of themselves. Having defeated their shadow selves, Arc and Kukuru are blessed by the spirits, and the seal on the Ark is broken. Arc receives the Power of Bravery, and becomes the Chosen Hero and Kukuru receives the Divine Power, becoming the Holy Mother. The spirits say that the power of the Ark stemmed from the goodness of humanity itself. Arc and the gang leave, only to find themselves utterly surrounded by Andel and his soldiers. They are captured and taken up a mountain, but the ground, which is unsettled by the awakening of the Dark One, shakes and splits. The soldiers are swallowed and the gang escapes. Kukjuru remains to keep the forces of Darkness sealed. Although they are sad, the gang leaves her behind and flies off in the Silver Noah into the sunset.

[edit] Arc the Lad II

Our story begins with a flashback of one the last surviving members of the fire tribe. His name is Elc, and he has nightmares of his childhood being taken to a laboratory of sorts. He is awakened by his good friend Lynx to undertake a job. Elc is a hunter, a person who takes on small jobs for money, who lives in the city of Prodias. Elc ends up at Aldia Skyport, where a crazed mutant ninja named Alfred takes a woman hostage. Elc busts through the window, and tries to subdue him with his fire abilities, but Alfred ends up running through a plane. While on this cargo plane, Elc hears a noise coming through a room, and there he meets Lieza, a country girl, and her wolf beast, Paundit. She proceeds to explain that she can "talk" to monsters, and not to tell. Elc, then runs off, only interested in capturing his quarry. Albert, upon seeing Elc, summons bats to help him fight off Elc, but Lieza and Paundit have their own ideas, and help the rogue hunter succeed in taking down the crazed mutant. As soon as they do, members of the local mafia, the Cabal, appear, not to pay Elc, but to kidnap Lieza and Paundit. After firing their guns and shooting Lieza, Elc escapes with an injured Lieza and heads to Indigos, to ask his friend and fellow hunter, the wind ninja, Shu, for assistance. Shu, who is busy doing another job, decides to lend Elc his apartment, and tells him to look up Dr. Lado, a freelance doctor, to give medical assistance to Lieza. Elc finds him in the ruined city, where, Lado is about to meet his demise at the hands of an ex-patient, who somehow has become a mutant. Elc stops him and asks the Doctor to give medical assistance to Lieza. Lado accepts, and heals her. Elc then tells Lieza to get rest, meanwhile, as he sleeps, he has another dream, depicting him in this lab with a girl named Mariel, with powers opposite his, those of ice.
