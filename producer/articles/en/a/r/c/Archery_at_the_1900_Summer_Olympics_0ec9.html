Archery at the 1900 Summer Olympics

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

You have new messages (last change).

At the 1900 Summer Olympics, six of the archery events that took place in Paris were considered Olympic, with 153 archers competing in them. The identities of 17 of those archers are known, though a number of those are known only by their surnames. It was the first time that archery was featured in the Olympics. All six events were for men. Only France, Belgium, and the Netherlands sent archers. None of the Dutch archers are known by name and none won any medals.

The events included here are those currently considered Olympic by the International Olympic Committee. They exclude many of the archery events held in France in 1900 connected to the World's Fair. Those events, if included, would put the number of archers over 5,000. They are excluded typically because of their status as French national championships rather than international contests or because they were essentially qualifiers for the six events listed.

[edit] Events

[edit] Au Cordon Doré, 50 metres

The team events served as qualifiers, with the top eight archers competing in the individual competition of the Au Cordon Doré at 50 metres event.

Rank Name Nation Score
1 Henri Hérouin France 31
2 Hubert van Innis Belgium 29
3 Émile Fisseux France 28
4 Henri Helle France 27
5 Edouard Beaudoin France 26
6 Denet France 26
7 Galinard France 26
8 Lecomte France 25

[edit] Au Cordon Doré, 33 metres

The team events served as qualifiers, with the top eight archers competing in the individual competition of the Au Cordon Doré at 33 metres event. The identities of the top three archers are the only ones known. No scores are recorded for any of the archers.

Rank Name Nation
1 Hubert van Innis Belgium
2 Victor Thibaud France
3 Charles Frédéric Petit France
4-8 Unknown competitors

[edit] Au Chapelet, 50 metres

The team events served as qualifiers, with the top six archers competing in the individual competition of the Au Chapelet at 50 metres event. The identities of the fifth and sixth place archers are unknown. All of the scores are unknown.

Rank Name Nation
1 Eugène Mougin France
2 Henri Helle France
3 Emile Mercier France
4 Hubert van Innis Belgium
5-6 Unknown competitors

[edit] Au Chapelet, 33 metres

The team events served as qualifiers, with the top six archers competing in the individual competition of the Au Chapelet at 33 metres event. The identities of only the top three archers in the event are known.

Rank Name Nation
1 Hubert van Innis Belgium
2 Victor Thibaud France
3 Charles Frédéric Petit France
4-6 Unknown competitors

[edit] Sur la Perche à la Herse

There were at most 126 other competitors in this event, as 129 archers competed in the two "à la perche" events. However, whether all of those archers entered both events or not (and if not, how many actually did contest each) is unknown. The event was contested on 15 July and 16 July 1900.

Rank Name Nation
1 Emmanuel Foulon Belgium
2 Auguste Serrurier France
Emile Druart Belgium
4-129? Unknown competitors
