Architecture & Morality is an album by Orchestral Manoeuvres in the Dark, released in 1981. It was to be the groups most commercial and critically well received album, selling over 3 million copies, released just eighteen months after their debut it demonstrated both their continuing musical maturity and advancement of their style.

The album continues where Organisation left off, moving away from typical electro-pop. The artwork was produced by Peter Saville Associates.

[edit] Notes

Tracks 10-16 of the remastered album are Bonus tracks from the 2003 reissue and were B-sides from the album's three singles, except "Gravity Never Failed" which was an aborted single.

"The Romance Of The Telescope" and "Of All The Things We've Made" would appear on OMD's next album in new recordings.

Architecture & Morality was released several times with varying artwork, most notably in yellow, blue and grey but even green versions are available. The original cover from 1981 is light yellow/orange in a die-cut sleeve.

"Joan of Arc (Maid of Orleans)" is often simply referred as "Maid of Orleans", for its single release it was named "Maid of Orleans: The Waltz of Joan of Arc".
