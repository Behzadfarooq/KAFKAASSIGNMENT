[edit] January, 2006

  • innate : intrinsic, present from birth.
  • concede : To acknowledge, often reluctantly, as being true, just, or proper; admit.
  • assort : to arrange or assign according to type.
  • blazing : burning brightly, conspicuously brilliant or resplendent.
  • resplendent : shining brilliantly.
  • prowess : extraordinary ability, bravery. E.g. PS3 owes its prowess to IBM's Cell processor.
  • capricious : whimsical; changeable.
  • levitate : to rise or cause to rise into the air and float in apparent defiance of gravity.
  • unilateral : Performed or undertaken by only one side. unilateral disarmament.
  • ideology : set of doctrines or beliefs that form the basis of a political, economic, or other system.
  • flamboyant : marked by or given to strikingly elaborate and colorful display or behaviour.
  • glitch : a minor malfunction, mishap or technical problem. E.g. computer glitch, a glitch in the negotitations
  • secular : Worldly rather than spiritual, Not specifically relating to religion or to a religious body: secular music
  • delude : to deceive the mind or judgement of. The ability to delude yourself may be an important survival tool.
  • smear : to stain by or as if by spreading or daubing with a sticky, greasy, or dirty substance.
  • taunt : to ridicule; to reproach in a mocking, insulting, or contemptuous manner.
  • emaciate : to make or become extremely thin, especially as a result of starvation.
  • chalk up : to credit or ascribe. E.g. They chalked up their success to experience.
  • lucid : clear, easily understandable, intelligible, mentally sound, sane , rational.
  • despondent : feeling dejected, depressed, hopeless.
  • recoup : to receive an equivalent for, to make up for, recover, reimburse. E.g. recoup loss
  • hinder : to get in the way, to obstruct or delay the progress of.
  • juxtapose : to place side by side, especially for comparison or contrast.
  • epiphany : a comprehension or perception of reality by means of a sudden intuitive realization.
  • aversion : an intense dislike; avoidance of a thing, situation, or behavior because it has been associated with an unpleasant or painful stimulus

[edit] Idioms and Phrases

  • in all likelihood : in all probability, almost certainly.

[edit] March, 2006

  • uxorious : Excessively fond of or submissive to a wife.
  • confer : to bestow ( a medal etc. ), to meet in order to deliberate together or compare views.
  • ordain : to appoint, authorize, confer holy orders.
  • trepidation : A state of alarm or dread; apprehension.

[edit] April, 2006

  • soar : arise e.g. interest rates soared.
  • redeem : convert into cash. e.g. redeem stocks.
  • assess : to determine the value, significance or extent of.
  • mitigate : to moderate the intensity or force, alleviate.
  • alleviate : to make the pain more bearable. e.g. a drug that alleviates headache
