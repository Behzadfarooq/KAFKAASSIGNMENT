Archie Griffin Award

You have new messages (last change).

The Archie Griffin Award, named after the only two-time Heisman Trophy winner in history, was first presented to Virginia Tech quarterback Michael Vick in 1999. The specially designed ring is the only college football award presented after all bowl games are completed, and signifies college football's Most Valuable Player for the entire season. Recent winners include Oklahoma quarterback Josh Heupel, Miami QB Ken Dorsey and USC QB Matt Leinart.
