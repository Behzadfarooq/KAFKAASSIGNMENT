Archdeaconry of Cleveland

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

The Archdeaconry of Cleveland is an archdeaconry, or subdivision, of the Church of England Diocese of York in the Province of York. It stretches west from Thirsk, north to Middlesbrough, east to Whitby and south to Pickering. It has a varied geography, including the southern parts of the conurbation of Teesside and the open moors of the North York Moors National Park.

[edit] People

The archdeaconry is led by the Bishop of Whitby, currently the Right Reverend Robert Ladds, and by the Archdeacon of Cleveland, currently The Venerable Paul Ferguson.

[edit] Organization

In common with other archdeaconries, Cleveland is further subdivided into deaneries, shown below.

  • Guisborough
  • Helmsley
  • Middlesbrough
  • Mowbray
  • Pickering
  • Stokesley
  • Whitby
