The Roman Catholic Archdiocese of Hamburg is an archdiocese of the Roman Catholic Church seated in Hamburg. It was erected on 24 October 1994 by Pope John Paul II. Its district includes the states of Hamburg and Schleswig-Holstein, and the Mecklenburg part of Mecklenburg-Western Pomerania. As of 2004 the archdiocese has 400,000 Catholics out of the total population of its district of 5.9 million. There is a total of 163 parishes in the archdiocese. The suffragans are the Bishop of Hildesheim and the Bishop of Osnabrück. The current archbishop of Hamburg is Werner Thissen (since 22 November 2002).

[edit] List of archbishops of Hamburg

  1. Ludwig Averkamp (1995-2002)
  2. Werner Thissen (since 2002)
