Arney is a small village in County Fermanagh, Northern Ireland. It had a population of 114 people (along with Skea) in the 2001 Census. It lies to the southwest of Enniskillen, between the village of Bellanaleck and the Five Points road junction. Arney takes its name from that of a river feeding Upper Lough Erne. The village consists of around twenty houses and has two pubs: Tipplers and Connelly's. It lies within the Fermanagh District Council area.
