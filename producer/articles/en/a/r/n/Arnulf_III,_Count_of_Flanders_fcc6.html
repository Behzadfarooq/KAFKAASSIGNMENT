Arnulf III, Count of Flanders

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Arnulf III (c. 1055 – February 22, 1071, in battle), was Count of Flanders and Count of Hainaut, as Arnulf I from 1070 to his death.

He was the eldest son of Baldwin VI, Count of Flanders and Richilde, Countess of Mons and Hainaut. He succeeded his father in both counties. With his untimely death at the Battle of Cassel, Flanders and Hainaut were separated again.
