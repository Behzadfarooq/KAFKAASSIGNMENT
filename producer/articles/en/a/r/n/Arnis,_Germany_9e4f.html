Arnis (Danish: Arnæs) is a small town in the district of Schleswig-Flensburg, in Schleswig-Holstein, Germany. It is part of the Amt Kappeln-Land. It was founded in 1677. The local Skipper's Church was erected in 1673. Arnis has a population of c. 300 and is the smallest town in Germany.
