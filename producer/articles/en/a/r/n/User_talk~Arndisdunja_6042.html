[edit] The Yule Lads

I am glad to see that you posted an article on The Yule Lads. I had been thinking of writing that article myself. However we need to move it to Yule Lads if you have no objection. By normal Wikipedia naming convention we do not use "the" in the title of an article unless "the" is actually part of the proper name, such as The New York Times. I would like to see the article expanded to include a description of each of the lads and what each one does.

I have added the categories Category:Nordic folklore and Category:Christmas characters to the article. I am not sure about the category Category:Elves. Are the lads considered to be elves?

I am interested in the lads because I spent two years in Iceland, '95–'97 and enjoyed it very much. There is a group of us here in California who were in Iceland at that time, and each year we celebrate the lads coming down from the mountains. Today we saw Stúfur on his way down. I would also like to see pictures of each of the lads if we can find some non-copyrighted pictures. •DanMS 19:55, 14 December 2005 (UTC)

  • P.S. I also added a link to the Icelandic Wikipedia [[is:Jólasveinn]]. If there is a similar entry in the other languages, you could add a similar link to those Wikipedias. •DanMS 20:32, 14 December 2005 (UTC)

[edit] Three weeks of admin tools

Today three weeks have passed since I was granted access to the administrator toolbox. During this time I have made use of it in the following way:

  • Protections and unprotections: 1
  • Blocks and unblocks: 4
  • Deletions and restorations: 69
  • Rollbacks: 246

I've found that the rollback tool is much more useful than I'd thought for vandalism patrol. In fact it makes that task so easy that I've been doing it more than before. On the other hand I've been surprised by how little the blocking tool is needed. Having done a significant amount of vandalism patrol I have still only blocked one solitary vandal. The great majority of addresses which send out a vandal edit do so only once. Those who do it more often usually stop after a warning or two. Only rarely is a block actually needed and in those cases someone usually beats me to it.

As a side note I haven't retired from writing articles either. I'm still hoping to bring Freyr up to featured status but even though I've already performed more edits on it than on Hrafnkels saga back in the day, a lot of work remains to be done. Community expectations for featured articles have gone up and so have my own ambitions. I'm currently waiting for a couple of books I ordered to arrive and then I may be able to make the final push.

I'm trying my best to live up to the trust you showed in me by supporting my RFA. If ever you feel uncertain whether I'm using the admin tools in the best interests of the project, let me know. I am at any time willing to relinquish the mop and reapply for it to address concerns people have and ensure that I'm not using the admin tools without being trusted to do so. Haukur 22:39, 12 February 2006 (UTC)
