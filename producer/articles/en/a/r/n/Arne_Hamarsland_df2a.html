Arne Hamarsland

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Arne Hamarsland (born 24 July 1933) is a former Norwegian middle distance runner who specialized in 1500 metres. He represented IL Gular.

At the 1960 Summer Olympics he finished ninth in the 1500 m final in 3:45.0 minutes. He competed at the 1958 European Championships without reaching the final. [1] He became Norwegian champion in 800 m in 1960 and 1961 [2] and in 1500 m in the years 1955-1956, 1959-1961 and 1963. [3]

[edit] Personal bests

  • 800 metres - 1:49.1 min (1958) [4]
  • 1500 metres - 3:39.8 min (1958) - twelfth among Norwegian 1500 m runners. [5]
