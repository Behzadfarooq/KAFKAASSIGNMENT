Arnoldo Mondadori (Poggio Rusco Mantua, November 2, 1889 - Milan, June 8, 1971) was a noted Italian publisher.

His publishing house is famous in and outside of Italy for publishing giallo books (primarily mystery and crime fiction) in the 1930s through the 1960s.
