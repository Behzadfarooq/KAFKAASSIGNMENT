This festival is supposed to be celebrated in order to praise the Saivaite Devotees namely Nayanmars who are 63 in numbers. The procession is celebrated by taking Nayanmars in a "Pallaq" which is highly decorated with ornaments and flowers, facing Lord Shiva and Parvati and proceeding further.

Lord KapaleeswararSwamy
Goddess Karpagambal

Huge gathering takes place at Mylapore. Lot of sweets, savouries, juices, buttermilk are served to the pilgrims. At each stage, there is Aarti and offering flowers to the God and Goddess by a small angel flying from top of the decorated shed. Its nice to see the ancient tradition being followed.

Nardhana Vinayagar
Singara Velar

Prior idols that are taken in procession are Lord KapaleeshwararSwamy, Karpagambal, Nardhana Vinayagar, Singara Velar, Nayanmaars, Kolavizhi Amman, Mundakanni Amman, Thiruvalluvar (Saint) and other Gods and Goddess.

KolaVizhi Amman

KolaVizhi Amman Temple is considered to be the ancient temple before Kapaleeshwarar Temple. The Goddess has beautiful eyes (Kola Vizhi) and she cures diseases. The temple is at Mylapore near Kutchery Road. People used to offer Pongal worship during the Tamil Month 'Adi' for Goddess.

Mundakanni Amman

MundaKanni Amman Temple is very famous for "Snake Worship". The Goddess present has got "Big Eyes" (Munda Kannu). It is a place for treating diseases. We could see a snake Worship behind the Sannidhi. People will offer milk to snakes. People will celebrate by offering 'Paal Kudam' (Milk Pot) to Goddess. The Temple is near Kutchery Road. Celebrations will be during the Tamil Month 'Adi' for the Goddess.
