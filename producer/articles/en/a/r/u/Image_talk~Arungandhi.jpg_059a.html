[edit] Definitions of 'fame' and 'encyclopedic'

Dear Dreamguy,

You have to admit, you can 'feel' that this foto with wife is 'encyclopedic'. The primary reason you revert this is because of user aesculapius' recent insistence on his own baby foto being included in Wikipedia in his father's article, and the many 'gray' areas of policy that this foto evokes. Here is my sense of all of this:

  1. Fame, whether we are conscious of it or not, is something like the ripples on a pond. In the case of Arun Gandhi, the main reason that we have an article about him in Wiki is because of his most famous grandfather, Mahatma Gandhi.
  2. Though most may not be conscious of it, we do have a very well developed system of subconsciously ranking fame. Mahatma Gandhi had fame of what I would call of the first order. If it could be said that electronics CEO Michael Parodi had any fame, it would certainly be of a very very low order.
  3. Now, liken the fame of Mahatma Gandhi to a massive boulder, the largest one that might naturally occurr, falling down a rock-slide off of a cliff next to a pristine alpine lake, and down into the lake. The ripples might stretch out and might even reach the opposite shores. Thus even the grandson of Gandhi, and even the wife of the grandson of Gandhi, are still within the 'ripple-zone of Gandhi.
  4. Liken the 'fame', if there is any, of Michael Parodi to a very small pebble being cast into that same lake. Obviously the ripple-zone is much smaller. So even the immediate family members of Parodi are not considered to be noteworthy because of their relationships to Parodi, and not possessing any 'fame' of their own due to their relationship to him.

I would be willing to guess that if it had not been for the Parodi controversy, you would not have ever reverted, or even particularly noticed the change in the Arun Gandhi photo. Nobody would have. It is only because this controversy is asking us all some very good questions about the nature of fame, and of encyclopedia worthiness that you have reverted this.

I am not saying that once this controversy is over, that we could not revert back to the mug-shot of Arun Gandhi. I am only proposing here that before we do this, perhaps this discussion page would be a good spot for a little discussion about the topics of 'fame' and 'encyclopedia worthiness'. Once the Parodi thing is resolved and a reasonable discussion of this has completed here, I will have no objection to a reversion.

So many thanks for your reading my humble consideration, Fotogenii 16:27, 12 August 2005 (UTC)
