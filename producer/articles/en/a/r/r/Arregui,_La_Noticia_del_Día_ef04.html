Arregui, La Noticia del Día (English language: The Notice of the Day) is a 2001 Argentine comedy film drama directed and written by María Victoria Menis with Diana Iceruk. The film premiered on 2 August 2001 in Buenos Aires. Starring Enrique Pinti and Carmen Maura, Maura was nominated for a Silver Condor Award for Best Actress in 2002 for her role in the film.

[edit] Overview

Spoiler warning: Plot and/or ending details follow.

The film is a tragicomedy and addresses social issues of legal corruption, unemployment, an absence of prospects for young people, accelerated decadence in the standard of living, increasing violence in the country. The story revolves around the life of Leopold, (Enrique Pinti, a middle-class father; his wife Isabel (Carmen Maura) working for a decadent comedian; Beto, the son, a taxi driver; and Martita, the daughter, a divorced and unemployed hairdresser. All live a mundane in a 40 square-meter apartment. One day Leopoldo takes it upon himself to donate blood. The next day, he is summoned once more to the hospital. His life is about to change forever.
