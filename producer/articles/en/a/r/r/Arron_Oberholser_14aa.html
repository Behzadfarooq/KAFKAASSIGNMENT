Arron Oberholser (born February 2, 1975) is an American golfer.

Born in San Luis Obispo, California. He attended San Jose State University and turned professional in 1998. Oberholser played on the Canadian Tour in 1999 and 2000, finishing second on the Order of Merit in the latter year. In 2001 he was a member of the second tier tour in North America, the Nationwide Tour, but only competed three times due to a wrist injury. In 2002 he finished second on the Nationwide Tour money list and won a place on the elite PGA Tour. In his first three seasons on the PGA Tour he played well enough to retain his card and had a best finish of second at the 2005 Wachovia Championship, where he lost a playoff. In February 2006 he achieved his first PGA Tour win at the AT&T Pebble Beach National Pro-Am and in 2006 he featured in the top fifty of the Official World Golf Rankings.

[edit] Professional wins

  • 1999 Ontario Open, Eagle Creek Classic (both Canadian Tour)
  • 2002 Samsung Canadian PGA Championship, Utah Classic (both Nationwide Tour)
  • 2004 Shinhan Korea Golf Championship (PGA Tour "Challenge Season")
  • 2006 AT&T Pebble Beach National Pro-Am (PGA Tour)
