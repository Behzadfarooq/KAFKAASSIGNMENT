Arroyo Grande High School (AGHS) is an American public high school located in Arroyo Grande, California. It serves grades 9-12 as part of the Lucia Mar Unified School District (LMUSD).

[edit] Campus

[edit] Renovation project

New pool and poolhouse construction

In 2004 local voters passed a school bond called Measure A-04. Its objective was "to repair, upgrade, equip and construct school facilities at Arroyo Grande High School, including the school library and restrooms, upgrade electrical wiring to accommodate technology, install energy efficient heating/ventilation systems, classrooms, cafeterias, plumbing, qualify for State matching funds, reduce overcrowding, by issuing $21,350,000 of bonds at legal interest rates, appoint a Citizens' Oversight Committee and perform annual audits to ensure that no bond money is wasted or used for administrator's salaries". [1]

The Citizen's Oversight Committee has worked to designate priorities and timelines. The first phase in 2005 involved renovation of restrooms and drainage systems, and extensive renovation of the 500 wing of classrooms. Work is now under way to build a new swimming pool, administration and student support center, a new multi-purpose room with food services, and renovation of many other classroom wings. The old administration building has been demolished and the administrative staff has temporarily relocated to a building across from the library.

The final phase will involve new tennis courts, food court, and parking area to be located in a section of campus currently used by the maintenance department and some old classrooms that will be removed. It is hoped these will be completed by September 2007, however construction of buildings from other phases will go on into 2008.
