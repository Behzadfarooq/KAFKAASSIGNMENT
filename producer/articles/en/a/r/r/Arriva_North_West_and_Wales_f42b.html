Arriva North West & Wales is a division of Arriva that operates bus services around North West England and Wales. It was made up from several previous bus operators including the North Western Road Car Company (in Merseyside, Cheshire and Lancashire), most of MTL North (formerly Merseybus / Merseyside PTE), the Bee Line Buzz Company (in Greater Manchester), and most of what was once Crosville Motor Services in Cheshire and Wales.

The boundaries of its operational regional are: West to Holyhead, South to Aberystwyth, East to Stockport, and North to Southport & Chorley.

In 2005, operations grew following the acquisition of Blue Bus.

[edit] Fleet

The Arriva North West and Wales fleet includes at least two batches of vehicles which are unique in Britain, these being the Neoplan N4016 and N4009 citybuses originally purchased by MTL. This photo shows one of the smaller N4009s operating the S3 service.

The fleet consists of approximately 1,334 operational vehicles as of January 2007, consisting of a wide variety of makes and types.

Recent deliveries have seen large numbers of new VDL SB120 and SB200 enter the fleet, joining an already sizeable fleet of DAF SB120 and SB220. As of January 2007, there are already more than two hundred DAF and VDL low-floor single deckers in the fleet, with more on order. The majority feature Wright Cadet and Commander bodywork, although the East Lancs Myllennium, Alexander ALX300, and Plaxton Centro and Prestige bodies are also represented.

On the double-deck side of the fleet, an influx of 30 Volvo B7TL with Alexander ALX400 bodywork has rejuvenated the double-deck fleet at Speke depot, these being supplemented by seven mid-life DAF DB250 with similar bodywork from Arriva London, and three Dennis Trident with Plaxton President bodywork acquired with the Blue Bus business in Bolton.

Despite new deliveries, the most common vehicle type in the fleet remains the Dennis Dart SLF and its smaller variant the MPD, accounting for over 320 vehicles. There are also a sizeable number of older step-entrance Dennis Darts, bringing the total fleet of Darts to over 500. The low floor examples carry a mix of Plaxton Pointer, Marshall Capital and Alexander ALX200 bodywork; the step-entrance ones, a mixture of types including Plaxton Pointer, East Lancs EL2000 and Northern Counties Paladin.
