The Arrondissement of Sens is an arrondissement of France, located in the Yonne département, in the Bourgogne région. It has 10 cantons and 108 communes.

[edit] Cantons

The cantons of the arrondissement of Sens are:

  1. Cerisiers
  2. Chéroy
  3. Pont-sur-Yonne
  4. Saint-Julien-du-Sault
  5. Sens-Nord-Est
  6. Sens-Ouest
  7. Sens-Sud-Est
  8. Sergines
  9. Villeneuve-l'Archevêque

[edit] History

[edit] 1970s

The cantons of Sens-Nord-Est, Sens-Ouest and Sens-Sud-Est resulted from the reorganization of the old cantons of: Sens-Nord:
Fontaine-la-Gaillarde, Maillot, Malay-le-Grand, Malay-le-Petit, Noé, Passy, Rosoy (commune supprimée), Saint-Clément, Saligny, Sens (parts of the commune), Soucy, Vaumort and Véron. Sens-Sud :
Collemiers, Cornant, Courtois-sur-Yonne, Égriselles-le-Bocage, Étigny, Gron, Marsangy, Nailly, Paron, Saint-Denis, Saint-Martin-du-Tertre, Sens (parts of the commune) and (to be checked) Subligny.

[edit] Before 1926

From 1800 (year VIII on the French Revolutionary Calendar) until 1926, the arrondissement included all the current cantons, except Cerisiers, Saint-Julien-du-Sault and Villeneuve-sur-Yonne, which were part of the old arrondissement of Joigny.
