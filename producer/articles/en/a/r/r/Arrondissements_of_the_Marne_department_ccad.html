Arrondissements of the Marne department

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

You have new messages (last change).

The 5 arrondissements of the Marne department are:

  1. Arrondissement of Châlons-en-Champagne, (prefecture of the Marne department: Châlons-en-Champagne) with 8 cantons and 100 communes. The population of the arrondissement was 100,647 in 1990, and was 100,112 in 1999, a decrease of 0.53%.
  2. Arrondissement of Épernay, (subprefecture: Épernay) with 10 cantons and 164 communes. The population of the arrondissement was 92,772 in 1990, and was 91,471 in 1999, a decrease of 1.4%.
  3. Arrondissement of Reims, (subprefecture: Reims) with 17 cantons and 175 communes. The population of the arrondissement was 299,528 in 1990, and was 310,289 in 1999, an increase of 3.59%.
  4. Arrondissement of Vitry-le-François, (subprefecture: Vitry-le-François) with 6 cantons and 113 communes. The population of the arrondissement was 50,499 in 1990, and was 49,044 in 1999, a decrease of 2.88%.
  5. Arrondissement of Sainte-Menehould, (subprefecture: Sainte-Menehould) with 3 cantons and 67 communes. The population of the arrondissement was 14,771 in 1990, and was 14,313 in 1999, a decrease of 3.1%.
