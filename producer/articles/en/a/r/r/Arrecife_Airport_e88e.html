Arrecife Airport (IATA: ACE, ICAO: GCRR) is an airport serving the Canary Isle of Lanzarote. The location is near Arrecife.

Lanzarote Airport was the 8th busiest airport in Spain in 2004 when just over 5.5 million passenger movements were recorded. Tenerife South Airport and Gran Canaria Airport are the only other airports in the Canary Islands that handle more passengers.

Most of the passengers passing through Lanzarote Airport come from the European Union. Visitors from the European Union account for about 70% of all passengers and more than 75% of those visitors come from the UK or Germany with nearly an even split between scheduled and charter flights.

The main "internal" Spanish flights arrive from Madrid Airport , Gran Canaria and Tenerife North which is the main inter island hub for the Canary Islands.

Getting to and from the airport is fairly straight forward, the Line 4 bus runs into the center of Arrecife roughly every 30 minutes between 0820 and 1920 each evening. The bus fare for this journey is around €0.90 and from the Arrecife bus terminal you can transfer to buses that will take you to Puerto del Carmen, Femes, Maguez, Orzola, Los Valles, Montana Blanc, Conil, La Asomada, San Bartolome, Teguise, Tias and Puente Mujeres.

There is normally a good selection of Taxis outside the main arrivals hall and they will take you into Arrecife for about €8 or onto Costa Teguise for €15 and Playa Blanca for just over €30.

Lanzarote Airport receives flights from a huge number of places across Europe with the choice of fifteen UK airports to fly from if you live in the UK.
