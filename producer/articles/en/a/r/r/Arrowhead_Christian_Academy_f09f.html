[edit] History and Campus

Co-founded by Dr. Larry W. Poland, the current head of Mastermedia International, Inc., and Gordon Shipps in 1979. The small private school consists of grades 7-12 and is based on incorporating Christian teachings and ideals into all aspects of education.

Arrowhead Christian Academy has been accredited by the WASC (Western Association of Schools and Colleges) since July 1996. ACA is also a member of the Association of Christian Schools International (ACSI).

Today the main campus building, originally a large hosiery factory, houses middle and high school classrooms, a science wing with computer centers, a main computer lab fully stocked with updated CPUs and a gymnasium/multipurpose auditorium. The Library Research Media Center featuring 24 new workstations along with a wide assortment of research materials and books. Sports facilities include athletic fields; an extensive exercise facility (the "Barn") and the 20,000 square foot Eagle Arena (the "super gym"). In 2004, a five-acre soccer field was completed which facilitates three full-size practice fields as well as one competition field. North Campus is home to the Center for the Arts Facility, with classrooms for music and art.

[edit] Arts and Journalism

"Wings"
ACA’s yearbook, Wings, known for its groundbreaking design and thought-provoking writing has garnered consistent national honors for the past 10 years from the Columbia Scholastic Press Association, the National Scholastic Press Association and the Quill and Scroll Society, advised by Crystal Kazmierski (2000 JEA National Yearbook Adviser of the Year). These awards include 11 National Scholastic Press Association Pacemakers[1] and 11 Columbia Scholastic Press Association Crown awards.[2] "Wings" year after year is added to the president's collection[3] at Walsworth Publishing and consistently pushes the envelope in the scholastic journalism world. In April of 2006. "Wings" was inducted into the NSPA Hall of Fame for having earned 10 consecutive All-American ratings in their annual critique service. All pre-press work is done in-house using Adobe InDesign CS2 and Photoshop.

Cambiata
ACA's high school choir performs annually at the ACSI Musicale in Pasadena, California. Under the direction of Ellen Beery (1999-2004), Ruth Goodman (2005-2006) and Nancy Gilmore (2006-Current), they have participated in many choral-related events with the Kerygma Honor Choir.

Kerygma
ACA's Honor Choir has, for the past four years, won the gold award at the invitational Heritage Festival at Disneyland in Anaheim, CA. On April 1, 2006, Kerygma was awarded a Silver Award. Kerygma has also performed with Cambiata and competed to perform as a small ensemble at ASCI musicale as well as performing at local venues under Ellen Beery (1999-2004), Ruth Goodman (2004-2005) and Nancy Gilmore (2005-Current).

Drama
Audience seating is set up for theatre-in-the-round productions that earn critical acclaim. Selection of scripts is reserved for top quality plays such as "Steel Magnolias," "The Glass Menagerie," "Fools," "The Mousetrap," "Bad Seed," "Black Comedy" and "Art." Students are coached by Fine Arts Department chair Crystal Kazmierski as well as drama and English teacher Nathan Smith and math teacher Steve Smith, who have numerous years of performance/directing experience. Former directors include Wes Harty.

[edit] Sports

Member of CIF’s High School Christian League and Mountain Valley Middle School League. The Eagles Varsity Football Team has been a three-time CIF Champion (1997, 1999, 2001), twice more a finalist and a Div. V state champion. Varsity Soccer (Girls) were CIF southern section Div. VI champions 2005, Varsity Volleyball Team (Boys) won the 2002 Citrus Belt League Championship and Varsity Cross-Country Teams have advanced as far as CIF Division Finals. Basketball, softball, and baseball have won league championships and have advanced as far as CIF semifinals.

[edit] Statement of Faith

  • The Bible is the inspired Word of God, inerrant in the original documents and of authority in all matters of faith and practice.
  • There is one God, eternally existent in three persons-Father, Son and Holy Spirit.
  • The deity of our Lord Jesus Christ, in His virgin birth, His sinless life, His substitutionary death, His bodily resurrection, His ascension into heaven, His high priestly ministry and His personal return in power and glory.
  • Man is sinful by nature, that regeneration by the Holy Spirit is essential for salvation and that salvation is a free gift of God's grace received by personal faith in Jesus Christ.
  • The present ministry of the Holy Spirit, by whose indwelling the believer is sealed until the day of redemption and by whose filling the believer is empowered to live a godly life.
  • The resurrection of the saved and of the lost; of the saved, to everlasting life and fellowship with God, and of the lost, to eternal, conscious judgment and separation from God.
  • The spiritual unity of all believers in our Lord Jesus Christ.
