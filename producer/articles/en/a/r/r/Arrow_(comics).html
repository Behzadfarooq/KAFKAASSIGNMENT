Arrow (comics)

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Arrow was a fictional superhero originally published by Centaur Publications. He first appeared in 1938 in Funny Pages #21 (numbered Vol. 2, #10, dated September 1938). After Centaur Publications went out of business, Arrow, along with most other company properties, lapsed into public domain. He was briefly revived by Malibu Comics, where he appeared as part of their Protectors/Genesis imprint. He starred in the Protectors series and the Arrow one-shot. The character has been dormant after Marvel Comics acquired Malibu Comics, though some of his Golden Age adventures were reprinted by AC Comics.

Today, he is mostly remembered for being the first American superhero to rely on archery as a primary gimmick.

[edit] Golden Age

The original Arrow was Ralph Payne, a member of US intelligence agency. Feeling that he could not effectively fight crime in his civilian guise, he turned to costumed crime fighting, becoming the Arrow. His primary weapon was his skill with bow and arrow, though he was good at fighting even without his weapons. Like many heroes of his era, he fought criminals, Nazi spies and saboteurs.

[edit] Malibu Comics

In Malibu Comics' Protectors/Genesis universe, the Arrow was a former intelligence agent named Rick Parker. Born somewhere in Texas, he grew up to become an avid hunter. His weapon of choice was the bow and arrow, because he felt that said weapons represented the greatest challenge and gave the prey the fairest chance. Following a stint with the military, Rick was recruited into one of the intelligence branches of the US Government. He was posted in a small, struggling Latin American country caught in the throes of civil war. While there, he witnessed the massacre of an entire village by the modernized forces loyal to that country's government. He eventually learned the country was being funded by the US government. Sickened by what he learned, Rick resigned. He decided to use his archery skills to fight for justice, adapting the crime-fighting identity of The Arrow, the hunter of men.

The Arrow primarily operated alone. At one point, he teamed up with a number of heroes during a battle in Brinkston - an event whose ultimate outcome convinced him to retire. When the members of the Protectors were asked to face Mr. Monday and re-form the super-hero team, Arrow requested to join his former comrades but was refused due to his violent approach.

[edit] Trivia

  • According to Alex Ross, the costume of the Red Hood, a minor character from Kingdom Come, was at least partially based on the original Arrow.
