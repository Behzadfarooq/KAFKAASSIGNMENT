Arizona Model United Nations, abbreviated AMUN, is the Model United Nations club at The University of Arizona. AMUN attends two collegiate Model United Nations conferences, one in the fall and one in the spring.

[edit] High School Conference

AMUN also hosts a High School Model United Nations Conference. The high school conference is attended by students from the U.S. southwest and Sonora, Mexico and is bilingual; both English and Spanish are spoken by the various councils. In 2007 The Organization of American States was simulated entirely in Spanish and all other councils were simulated in English. It is the stated goal of the conference to become tri-lingual by introducing a French speaking council.
2007 Conference
On the weekend of March 23-24, 2007 the AMUN High School conference reached its 45th annual session. 22 Nations were represented in the United Nations, and 27 nations were represented in the Organization of American States. The Organization of American States discussed preperation for free trade, the rights of women, and illicit drugs. The Security Council, The First Committee of the General Assembly, The Second Committee of the General Assembly, The Third Committee of the General Assembly, The Economic and Social Council, and the Commission for Social Development all met. Also a "Historical Security Council" met to simulate the actions of the security council in the year 1990.

[edit] High School Conference Award Winners


2007 Conference
At the 2007 AMUN conference awards were given to the "Best Novice Delegation", the "Best Experienced Delegation", and the "Best Overall Delegation" for each council excluding the Security Council and Historical Security Council, which have a different system of nomenclature.

  • Best Novice Delegation The First Committee of The General Assembly: Christopher Handel representing the People's Republic of China, Desert Mountain High School.
  • Best Experienced Delegation The First Committee of The General Assembly: Egypt, Ironwood High School.
  • Best Overall Delegation The First Committee of The General Assembly: Sweden, Ironwood High School.
  • Best Novice Delegation: The Second Committee of The General Assembly: Srishti Nayak representing the Lebanon, Desert Mountain High School.
  • Best Novice Delegation Economic and Social council: Lyndsi Sherman representing the People's Republic of China, Desert Mountain High School.
  • Best Overall Delegation Council on Social Development: Ariel Sim representing the People's Republic of China, Desert Mountain High School.
  • Best High School overall: University High School.
  • Best Student Leadership: Desert Mountain High School.
