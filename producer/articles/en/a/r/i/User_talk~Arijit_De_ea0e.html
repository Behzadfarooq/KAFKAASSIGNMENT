Arijit De (born 1970) is a former business journalist and currently works for a multinational bank.

He has been the Chief of Bureau at Business Standard, and has also worked with Financial Express and Business Today.

As a journalist, Arijit has tracked India's largest business houses and reported on the travails of some of the largest global corporations, like Enron, Unilever, etc. His biggest newsbreaks include the Tata Group's acquisition of Tetley of UK (the world's largest tea brand), and the Tata Group's decision to exit from ACC, India's largest cement maker. Some of the major corporate controversies Arijit has reported on include the Mallya-Kishore Chhabria battle, insider trading charges against Hindustan Lever, Kumar Birla's bid to acquire L&T's cement division, Sterlite's hostile takeover attempt on Indal, among others.

A Master's in Economics from Calcutta University, Arijit has been a Press Fellow at Wolfson College, Cambridge University.

Arijit has taken a break from journalism and now works with a leading MNC bank in Mumbai, India. In his spare time, he works on his first book - a fiction based on his experiences as a journalist.
