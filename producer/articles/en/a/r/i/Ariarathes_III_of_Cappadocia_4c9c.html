Ariarathes III of Cappadocia

Ariarathes III (in Greek Aριαράθης; reigned 255–220 BC), son of Ariamnes, ruler of Cappadocia, and grandson of Ariarathes II, married Stratonice, a daughter of Antiochus II, king of Syria, and obtained a share in the government during the life-time of his father. He was the first ruler of Cappadocia to proclaim himself around 250 BC king (basileus), and it is known that he sided with Antiochus Hierax in his war against Seleucus II Callinicus. Ariarathes is also said to have expanded his kingdom adding Cataonia to his dominions.1
