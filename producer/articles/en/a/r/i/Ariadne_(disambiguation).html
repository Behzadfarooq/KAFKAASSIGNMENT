Ariadne (disambiguation)

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Ariadne was a Greek goddess. This name has several additional uses:

  • Ariadne auf Naxos, an opera by Richard Strauss
  • HMS Ariadne, name of various Royal Navy ships, including
    • HMS Ariadne, faced USS Alfred in 1778
    • HMS Ariadne (1891), a Diadem class cruiser
    • HMS Ariadne (F72), a frigate of the British Royal Navy
    • HMS Ariadne (M65), an Abdiel class minelayer that served in World War II
  • SMS Ariadne, a German ship of the First World War
  • Ariadne Genomics, an American computational biology software development company
  • Ariadne's thread (logic), an algorithm in puzzle-solving.
  • Ariadne, a Byzantine Empress from the late 5th century.
  • Ariadne the spider, a character in the British childrens' animated TV series Creepy Crawlies.
  • Ariadne, a psychedelic drug
This disambiguation page lists articles associated with the same title. If an internal link led you here, you may wish to change the link to point directly to the intended article.
