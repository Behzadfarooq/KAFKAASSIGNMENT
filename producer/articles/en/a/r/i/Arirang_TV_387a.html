Arirang TV

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Arirang TV (아리랑 TV) is an international broadcast network based in Seoul, South Korea, operated by The Korea International Broadcasting Foundation. Arirang TV provides English-language information on current events, culture and the history of Korea. The term "Arirang" is taken from a traditional Korean folk song. The channel is free-to-air in much of Europe and North America, Asia (Excluding Korea), Australia and Pacific Region provided via Astra 1E, Hotbird 3, PAS 2, 8, 9 and 10. While on Arabic viewers seen on Sattelite AsiaSat 3 and ArabSat 2D. Arirang TV also provides a wide range of VOD (video on demand) services, which allow to watch show and broadcast via the internet.

[edit] About Arirang

Arirang TV is a public service agency that spreads the uniqueness of Korea to the world through cutting-edge broadcasting mediums. Arirang TV's objective is to burnish Korea's image in international communities and to improve relationships with foreign countries through close cooperation with broadcasting companies overseas.

Arirang TV is dedicated to the development of broadcasting, media, and the advertising industry, along with the advancement of culture and art. Arirang's core business is to deliver programs worldwide via satellite for 24 hours a day in order to improve the image of Korea.

As Korea's premier English-language network, Arirang broadcasts three channels: Arirang World, Arirang Korea and Arirang Arab. The network strives to be Korea's messenger to the world as they promote and share their culture with viewers in more than 188 countries. Arirang TV is viewed in more than 53 million households and on every continent through seven broadcast satellites.

[edit] Criticism

Arirang TV is viewed by many observers as biased and unrealistic in its coverage of South Korea, many of their programs showing characteristics of propaganda. It has also been known to censor negative views of South Korea [1] [2].
