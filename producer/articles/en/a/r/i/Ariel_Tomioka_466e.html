Ariel Tomioka (born 10 December 1948 in Honolulu, Hawaii) is an American writer and poet. She is best known for her writings related to integration of male/female energies.

Tomioka worked as a writer-in-residence at California public schools for many years. She is a full-time writer and public speaker; and she presents workshops on the subjects of male-female relationships, dreams, creativity, and what she calls a process of self mastery.[1] She is the author of the 1990 novel On the Breath of the Gods, which was reprinted in 1994.
