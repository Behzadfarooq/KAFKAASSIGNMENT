Around the Fur is Deftones' second major label album, released in 1997. The songs "My Own Summer (Shove It)" and "Be Quiet and Drive (Far Away)" were both released as singles with accompanying videos.

Around the Fur is the album that put Deftones at the forefront of the 1990s alternative metal scene, after the underground, fan-base building success of their major label debut, Adrenaline.

The song "Headup" features Max Cavalera of Soulfly. The song was written by Max and Chino as a way of venting some of their pain over the loss of Max's step son, and Chino's friend, Dana Wells.[citation needed] Soulfly is taken from a portmanteau invented for the song.[citation needed]

Tracks 2 and 8 are often mislabeled as "Ihabia" and "Dai Th Flu" respectively. The correct titles are as they appear in the track listing below.

The band Fightstar covered the opening track "My Own Summer (Shove It)" for the free Kerrang! compilation album of metal classics "High Voltage: A Brief History of Rock."
