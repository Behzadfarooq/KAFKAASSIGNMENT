Hmmm...What *are* the proportions of this flag? WFD says 1:2, but Whitney Smith's Flags Through the Ages and Across the World says "~2:3" for the civil flag (sun-less) and 2:3 for the state and war flags. Smith also states that « over the years, not only has there been disagreement over whether the Argentine blue should be called celeste, azure, azure-celeste, sky blue, or celeste-blue, but the exact meaning of these terms has never been precisely defined ». The October 7, 2004 flag was from http://www.portalargentino.net/simbolos/bandera.htm (signed by the Argentine embassy to Spain, of all things) and is what I used as a starting point. My Spanish isn't good enough, so could someone translate the argument? Proportions of 14:9 are pretty close to 2:3, I note...And the colours cited do match those in Smith's book better than the WFD's.

The Flag 1 and 2 pictures are not good proofs, as a JPG's colour balance is unreliable (depends on the camera settings, etc.). But I will concede the WFD blue seems too dark. Note, by the way, that Flag 1 has proportions of 4:7, as best as can be measured from the photo.

In the mean time, I'll upload a 14:9 flag with the portalargentino colours.

Regarding shape and size, the text on that page states: Las medidas de la Bandera Oficial son 1,40 m de largo por 0,90 m de alto (es decir, una proporción 9/14). (The official flag measures are 1,40 meters width and 0,90 meters height (that is to say, a 9/14 proportion) ). There's actually no official position regarding colors. So the page uses the Pantone's Cerulean Blue as celeste (rgb 155, 196, 226). RGB 153, 205, 255 would be the "web-safe" version. About the pics, Argentine people dont really care that much about their flag as other do -americans for instance-. But those JPGs are about the usual color you get to see. In fact you see lighter ones, as flags get older and colors fade away. - SpiceMan 21:08, 12 Dec 2004 (UTC)
