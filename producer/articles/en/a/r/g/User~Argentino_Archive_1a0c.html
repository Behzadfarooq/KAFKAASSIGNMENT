[edit] Yapeyú from es WP

Yapeyú es una ciudad argentina, en la provincia de Corrientes, del departamento San Martín

La ciudad se fundó el 4 de diciembre de 1626; su fundador fue el jesuita Pedro Romero, que le dio el nombre de "Villa de Nuestra Señora de los Santos Reyes Magos y Yapeyú". El término Yapeyú viene del guaraní y significa "fruto maduro" y en otros tiempos fue también el nombre del río que hoy en día se conoce como Guaviraví.

Esta ciudad es la villa natal del general José de San Martín.
Translated:
'''Yapeyú''' is an [[Argentina|argentine]] town, in [[Corrientes Province]], in the San Martín department. It was founded on December 4, 1626; the founder was the jesuit [[Pedro Romero]], who gave the town the name "Villa de Nuestra Señora de los Santos Reyes Magos y Yapeyú". The word "Yapeyú" comes from guaraní language and means "mature fruit" and in other times, was the name of the river that is called today Guaviraví. I was one of the 30 missions established at the begining of the XVII century, that were destroyed in 1817 by the portuguese army. The city was later rebuilt, and the ruins of the little fortess, wich at the end of the XVIII century had been house of lt.Juan de San Martín her wife, Gregoria Matorras, were preserved. In this town, on February 25, 1778, was born general [[José de San Martín]], ''the Liberator'', ''the Father of the Homeland'' ==Sources== *[[es:Yapeyú|Spanish Wikipedia, from wich was translated a part]] *[http://www.argentinatotal.com.ar/info_turis/provincias/corrientes/ciudades/yapeyu/yapeyu.htm] {{Argentina-stub}} {{Argentina-geo-stub}}
