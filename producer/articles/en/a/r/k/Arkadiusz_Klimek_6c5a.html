Arkadiusz Klimek (born 25 March 1975, in Iława) is a Polish professional footballer currently playing for Scottish Premier League club Heart of Midlothian on loan from FBK Kaunas.

Klimek started his career with local side Jeziorak Iława before moving to Stomil Olsztyn in 1995 then Zagłębie Lubin three years later. After 6 seasons with Zagłębie he decided to try his luck abroad, moving to Greece in 2003 with Panionios Nea Smirna. He returned after a single season, joining Wisła Płock in 2004.

In 2005 Klimek left Poland for a second time, joining Lithuanian champions FBK Kaunas. He helped Kaunas win the Cup in his debut season, although they finished runners-up to Ekranas Panevėžys in the A Lyga. The following season his 8 league goals helped Kaunas regain the league title and also earned him a move to Scotland, with Hearts in January 2007.[1] He made his Maroons debut in a 1-0 defeat of Inverness Caledonian Thistle in March 2007.
