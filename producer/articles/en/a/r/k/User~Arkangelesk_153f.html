The Full Name of Arkangelesk is: Trent Vardan Armen Sion Vasil 'Lamajutak' Esmerian (Armenian: Թրենդ Վարտան Արմեն Սիոն Վասիլ Էսմէրիան, Arabic: ترنت فارضان ارمين سيون فاسيل لمجتك اسميريان).

Arkangelesk was born Trent Vardan Armen Esmerian (Թրենդ Վարտան Արմեն Էսմէրիան, Трент Вардан Армен Эсмерян). Esmerian derives from the Armenian word: "Esmer", (Էսմէր) meaning 'This mind is complete with other bizzare concepts'. He was born in the Armenian Soviet Socialist Republic, or Armenian SSR (Հայկական Սովետական Սոցիալիստական Հանրապետություն, Армянская Советская Социалистическая Республика) (Now Armenia) in 1985, outside of Yerevan (Երեւան, Ереван), in a small isolated village known as Hanrakitabutyun (Հանրագիդապութիւն/Հանրագիդապությունն, Ханракитабутюн).

He enjoys the study of languages and their writing systems, such as his native Armenian (Հայերեն), Russian (Русский), Korean (한국어), and Arabic (العربية).

Vardan is a Buddhist, but more refined - a Vaharkian; the word deriving from the Armenian meaning "Man on a journey", possibly refering to the Armenian phrase: "Թանտիկա Վեց Ու Շկրավան Ճիրախյունն Տվարոնամ Էնիկ Վշարղորց Չունջեմ Վահարքիա" (transliteration: Tandiga Vetz U Shgravan Djirakhyunn Dvaronam Enig Vsharghortz Chunchen Vaharkia; basic translation/interpretation: The spiritual journey of Vaharkia that can and will be exercised by the completely free mind of a man whose purpose is only to discover the truth and distribute this truth to his brethren) in a loving and completely humane form.

Vaharkism is actually a term created by Buddhists living in Armenia during the creation of Soviet Armenia(After the Transcaucasian SFSR split into Armenia, Georgia, and Azerbaijan. Vaharkism is not very popular in the world, with just over 600 (90%) Vaharkians living in Armenia; it is therefore, still considered Buddhism - and even followers of Vaharkism often refer to their beliefs as Buddhism. Vaharkian's philosophy does not have to support Communism, as it is commonly mistaken - instead, it more strict, believing that there are no levels in society, and that we are all equal. It strongly abolishes any system which calls itself communism which infact is a dictatorship, much like Stanlinist Russia, or North Korea, along with any other extreme political system. Vaharkism concentrates more on the spiritual quest of recieving enlightenment. It has actually been called a seperate philisophical ideology from Buddhism by many people who are actualy aware of it's existence; because followers are not subject to religous rules and are not forced to believe in certain things, for example, the Buddhist's belief of reincarnation. A follower can be religiously Muslim and call him or herself a Vaharkian, as the main principle of Vaharkism is to live life to the fullest extent possible - as long as the follower does this, he is a Vaharkian; therefore, theoretically, there could be many Vaharkians in the world, but many are simply not aware there was such an ideology.
