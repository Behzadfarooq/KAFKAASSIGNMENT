Arkansas Highway 90

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Highway 90, or AR-90 for short, is an east-west highway in northeastern Arkansas between its western terminus at its junctions of U.S. Highway 63 at Ravenden, Arkansas in Lawrence County and the Missouri state line between Rector, Arkansas and Kennett, Missouri where it continues as Route 84 at a bridge across the St. Francis River. It is entirely undivided surface highway that passes through rich cotton country.

It meets AR-34 in Delaplaine, with which it duplexes for seven miles to O'Kean, has a very short (one mile) duplex with AR-135 at no identifiable town at either place, and meets US-49 and AR-139 in Rector. It passes through only three Arkansas counties:

  • Randolph County
  • Greene County
  • Clay County
