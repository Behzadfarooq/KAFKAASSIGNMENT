[edit] Wrecked Ship Contradiction

There are two seperate mentions of the Wrecked Ship area of Super Metroid in this article, one saying the ship belonged to the space pirates, the other makes vague references to "ancient race". So, which is true and which is appropriate?

If we simply go by what's in the game's documentation and the game itself, the facts supporting the space pirate origin is the bombu enemy and maintenance robots which are seen in other games in the control of Space Pirates. But this is very tenuous evidence since Space Pirates are pirates afterall, Metroid Prime logs show that they often acquire technology and material through piracy.

And the facts pointing to an unknown origins are the ghosts appearing having no resemblance to Space Pirates and that the ship seems ancient enough to have been there while the Chozo inhabited Zebes.(The chozo statue leading to the Chozo-made Gravity Suit)

And since there's an official source stating that the ship is not the space pirate mothership from Fusion, why is there mention of what "fans" think? The mothership's self-destruction caused a massive explosion that simply cannot have left anything as intact and as functional as the Wrecked Ship in Super Metroid.Dr. Lobotomy

>No, K2L was a colony OF Earth. In space.

As such it is not official information. I vote it gets removed from the article until the actual games ever say it existed. Even then, the article is titled "Areas in the Metroid series". Unless it's actually IN a game as in a playable area (which "area" implies"), it should not be in the article except maybe in a section for places mentioned in the games. MrPotter19

[edit] Proposal to split

Since the descriptions of every main area (Zebes (which has been split out already), SR388, Tallon IV, the BSL, Aether, and the Alimbic Cluster (as I understand it, haven't played Hunters)) mostly lack stub-like qualities, I propose to split those areas out again into seperate articles. We could keep this article for areas like the Ceres Space Colony, Frigate Orpheon (although, unlike the CSC, its exact location is specified as "in orbit of Tallon IV"), the K2-L colony, and the minor planets mentioned in Metroid Prime (Twin Tabula, Bilium, Dormine II). Furthermore, to avoid moving this article to the backyard of the articles on the Metroid Series (where the Creatures in the Metroid series articles currently reside), in my opinion we should place a short description with a "Main Article" link for each of the major areas mentioned above -- something that the Zebes split lacks at the moment of writing.

When considering the effects of the Creatures in the Metroid Series merge and clumsy split -- an enormous, unmaintainable mess that no-one likes to edit -- and the fact that said mess is already starting to show up in here, the reason for the above proposal becomes obvious. --De Zeurkous (zeurkous@zeurcomp.nichten.info), Saturday January 27 12:43 UTC 2007

The only problem I can see with the idea is that I'm not sure the setting of each Metroid game is notable enough for its own separate article. Zebes is, because three games take place there (counting Zero Mission) and it's mentioned as Samus's homeworld, also has Smash Bros. stages set there, and is generally important to Metroid. Each of the other areas you mentioned is the setting of one game, and ought to be covered by this page and that game's page. --Herald Alberich 05:03, 28 January 2007 (UTC)
This is not about notability -- if the information isn't notable at all, it should not be included -- but about structuring said information. WP:N#Merging IMO doesn't apply here either -- the locations play a key role in the gameplay of the Metroid games, and (as I pointed out) most of them aren't exactly stubs --De Zeurkous (zeurkous@zeurcomp.nichten.info), Sunday January 28 11:17 UTC 2007
Since there seems to be no further objection, I'll split the article. --De Zeurkous (zeurkous@zeurcomp.nichten.info), Thursday February 8 14:20 UTC 2007
Split complete -- now let's clean up this mess^7 --De Zeurkous (zeurkous@zeurcomp.nichten.info), Thursday February 8 15:38 UTC 2007
