"Are You Gonna Go My Way" is the first single to be released by Lenny Kravitz from the album by the same name. It was released in March 1993. It was written by Lenny Kravitz and Craig Ross.

[edit] Composition

"Are You Gonna Go My Way" is an uptempo and anthemic rock song. It starts off with a riff that many considered as a Jimi Hendrix rip-off. The song is not solely performed by singing; it also consists of a solo guitar that is played for more than a minute. Lyrically, the song is based on the concept of having fun and feeling good energies, and Kravitz invites everyone to "go that way" he feels.

[edit] Chart performance

"Are You Gonna Go My Way" is one of Kravitz's most successful songs, considered as one of his signature songs. It was a hit in USA, reaching the top twenty on the Billboard Hot 100 charts. Besides, it reached top 5 in UK, peaking at number four.[1]

[edit] Music video

Video still from the music video

‎ The music video consisted of Kravitz and his band playing, surrounded by people dancing and having fun. The background scenography consists of a futuristic and architectonical place, something usual on Mark Romanek's videos, the director of the video. He would later direct more videos for Kravitz, such as "Is There Any Love In Your Heart" and "If You Can't Say No".

"Are You Gonna Go My Way" was one of the most played videos in 1993 on MTV [2] and has been considered on many MTV's best music video shows. It won the 1993 MTV Video Music Award for Best Male Artist, and was played live during the show with John Paul Jones of Led Zeppelin fame on bass.

The video is featured in the DVD for the special tour edition for Greatest Hits album. It is also featured in Directors Label Volume 4: The Work Of Director Mark Romanek.

[edit] Awards

The success of the song and the music video helped Kravitz to be nominated and won awards, such as:

  • Grammy Awards for Best Rock Vocal Performance, Solo: Nominated
  • Grammy Awards for Best Rock Song: Nominated
  • MTV Video Music Awards for Best Art Direction: Nominated
  • MTV Video Music Awards for Best Male Artist: Won

[edit] Trivia

The song was covered by Metallica in a medley for "MTV Hits" at the 2003 MTV Music Video Awards

A remixed version is played as the opening theme song in Gran Turismo 3.
