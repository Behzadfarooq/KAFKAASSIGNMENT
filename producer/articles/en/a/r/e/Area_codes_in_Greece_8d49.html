Area codes in Greece

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

This is a list of dialing codes in Greece. The first digit represents a region, and the second an area within the region. Zone 9 is used for other numbers such as toll. Numbers starting with zero are not available and some 800 numbers are used for toll-free numbers. All numbers now start with only 2, and never with a zero after November 2002. 6 8 and 9s are now used for other telephone purposes after the change.

Three-digit codes are used with seven-digit subscriber numbers, four-digit codes with six-digit numbers, and five-digit codes with five-digit numbers, such that the full telephone number is (usually) ten digits.

The country calling code of Greece is 30. The international call prefix depends on the country you are calling from, e.g. 00 for most European countries, and 011 from North America.

[edit] Non-geographic prefixes

All numbers 10 digits except where specified

118xx - directory services
12x, 13x, 12xx, 13xx - network customer services (specific to each carrier)
138xx - customer servicer and sales for telcos (accessible from all networks)
14x, 14xx - information services (weather, news etc.)
15x, 15xx - national public services (public sector numbers)
181xx, 182xx, 183xx - Short Codes for private companies
5 - VPNs
692 - HERMES Paging Service (obsolete)
693 - TIM Hellas mobile
694 - Vodafone Hellas mobile
697 - |CosmOTE mobile
699 - Q-Telecom mobile
70 - Universal Personal Number Service (not yet available)
800 - toll-free numbers
801 - “ONEphone” shared-cost services (low local call charges apply nationwide)
807xxxx - phonecard services
89 - data services (Internet dialup, Hellaspac packet relay, ERMES email etc.)
901 - premium rate services
909 - premium rate adult-only services

The prefix 3 is reserved for future geographic codes.
