Arena Joinville

Arena Joinville is the home ground of Joinville Esporte Clube. It is located at Bucarein neighborhood, near Boa Vista and Guanabara neighborhoods.

[edit] Information

The stadium's capacity, currently 15,000 people, will be expanded to 30,000 (24,000 seated) in 2007. The estimated budget for the expansion is R$ 7 million.

Arena Joinville is one of the Brazil's most modern football stadiums, and its design is inspired by European football arenas, such as the Amsterdam ArenA.

It is a multifuncional stadium, where not only football matches are played, but it also hosts Brazilian and international shows and other events.

The stadium is owned by the Prefeitura Municipal de Joinville (Municipal Prefecture of Joinville, in English).

Arena Joinville was inaugurated on September 25, 2004.

[edit] Records

The first stadium match was a friendly match, played between the All-Stars of Joinville city against a Masters All-Stars. The match ended with a 3-1 victory of the Masters team. The first stadium goal was scored by Masters' player Donizete Pantera.

The stadium attendance record was also in the inauguration match. 15,000 people watched the match.

[edit] Shopping Arena

A shopping mall is being constructed inside the stadium, and it will be composed of an estimated number of more than a hundred stores, turning it into one of the biggest shopping malls in the city.
