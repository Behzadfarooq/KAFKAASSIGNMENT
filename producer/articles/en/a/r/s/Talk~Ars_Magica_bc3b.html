What edition and what books should I get if I want to try to get into the game? -Tubby

The current publisher of the Ars Magica roleplaying game, Atlas Games, (http://www.atlas-games.com/) recently released the newest (4th) edition of the core rulebook to be freely distributed. It is all you need to get started, although there are a lot of different sourcebooks and adventures available. You'll find the link to the download from their website, on this page: http://www.atlas-games.com/arsmagica/index.html
-Bearnárd

There is now a 5th edition (not published electronically). But the 4th Ed. download is still an excellent starting point. Townmouse 22:00, 27 July 2005 (UTC)

[edit] Play-acting innovation?

Could someone explain this quote from the article: other systemic innovation was the insistence on play-acting the characters. Without an explanation of how the system supports it, it seems a bit odd as playing characters (presumely it means adopting a 1st person stance) was definitely not innovative when the game came out. Ashmoo 06:26, 27 September 2005 (UTC)


Unlike most RPG's, and computer "role"-playing games, the system is not based around combat, or quests, but about character development and interaction, and not "beating" the system. In most games, a low strength is a weakness. In Ars Magica, it's an opportunity to define your character.
There is a FAQ for the game, as well as many other game resources on the Project Redcap web site, which includes information specific to 4th and 5th editions, as well as general information on the history of the game. Ayerik 08:15, 1 December 2005 (UTC)
