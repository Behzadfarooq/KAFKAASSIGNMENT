[edit] Agoura Hills Neigborhoods

Hi... the stuff you posted on Agoura Hills neighborhoods is not really correct. It seems a lot of the neighborhood names are made-up and are not commonly used by residents. Do you think you could put the whole series of pages on hiatus until you get the facts straight? If you are to do a section on this, I would suggest dividing neighborhoods by homeowners associations (i.e. Fountainwood, Hillrise, Old Agoura, Morrison Ranch, Chateau Springs, Lake Lindero, etc.) because these are actually widely used by everyone. I know the City Planning Dept. has a map of the city divided by homeowners associations (done by GIS) that you may be able to obtain if you contact them.

[edit] Welcome to the Southern California WikiProject

Glad to have you on board.

Some suggestions:

For your articles on districts in Agoura, the article titles are usually "Community, City, State" [Although there is some debate on that issue, that's the general consensus]. My personal opinion is that if the articles don't have much change of growing larger than they currently are, it's better to merge the info into the main Agoura Hills, California article or into a Neighborhoods of Agoura Hills, California article (otherwise they end up an easy target for vandalism and spam).

For the articles that you are creating on districts and communities in the city of Los Angeles, you can learn quite a bit by looking at already existing articles. Looking at Little Tokyo, Los Angeles, California, for example, you can see how they can be formatted and even some of the categories they can be put into. BlankVerse 13:16, 29 November 2006 (UTC)

Under the criteria for speedy deletion, articles that do not assert notability may be deleted at any time.
