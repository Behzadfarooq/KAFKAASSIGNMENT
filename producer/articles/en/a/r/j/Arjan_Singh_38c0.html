Arjan Singh

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Marshal of the Indian Air Force Arjan Singh DFC was born on April 15, 1919 in Lyalpur, and educated at Montgomery India(now in Pakistan). He entered RAF Cranwell in 1938 and was commissioned as a Pilot Officer in December 1939. He was awarded the Distinguished Flying Cross (DFC) in 1944, and commanded the Indian Air Force Exhibition Flight in 1945. He was Chief of the Air Staff or CAS, from August 1, 1964 to July 15, 1969, and was awarded the Padma Vibhushan in 1965. He was Lt. Governor of Delhi and was made Marshal of the Air Force in January, 2002 (the highest rank ever given in the history of the Indian Air force).
