Arlington Catholic High School is a coeducational Catholic high school in Arlington, Massachusetts. Arlington Catholic High School was established in 1960 under the direction of Msgr. Oscar Oâ€™Gorman. It was built by the parishioners of St. Agnes Parish and opened as a regional high school staffed by the congregation of the Sisters of St. Joseph. Located in a suburban Boston community, the current student body represents 42 cities and towns in addition to the Town of Arlington. Enrollment in grades 9-12 is 791 students.


[edit] Notable Alumni

  • Msgr. Paul McInerney '64 - former secretary to Bernard Cardinal Law and former director of Boston Catholic Television
  • Mark J. Sullivan '72 - current Director of the United States Secret Service
  • Julianne Nicholson '89 - actress on Law & Order: Criminal Intent
