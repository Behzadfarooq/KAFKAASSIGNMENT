[edit] alter even if sourced

I think perhaps the map should have the state-by-state fields deleted even if properly sourced. The US has not recognized, and this map obscures this fact by coloring most of it green. I'm sure there are local, subnational political units that have passed resolutions of this nature in other countries too, after all. Where should we stop? Town councils?

Actually, there's a very clear place to stop: Countries. Entities with foreign policies that they are responsible for. Anything else is rather cheap talk: What consequences do Delaware, or Denver, or for that matter the University of Florida, face passing some sort of resolution? Jeopardized relations with Turkey?

I don't think I'm being pro- or anti-recognition here; whatever their differences, I think people on both sides would be opposed to obscuring the US's refusal to recognize. Anti-recognition wouldn't want to make it look like such an influential country has called it a genocide, when it decidedly has not; and pro-recognition would demand genuine recognition, and want to show Americans that their country does not recognize even though a lot of countries have. —The preceding unsigned comment was added by 205.212.75.128 (talk) 19:14, 21 February 2007 (UTC).
