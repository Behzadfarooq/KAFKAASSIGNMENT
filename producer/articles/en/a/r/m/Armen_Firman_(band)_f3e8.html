Armen Firman (band)

Armen Firman is an indie Australian rock group, who formed in 2002. Hailing from the country's south, the Melbourne band has developed a strong underground following as a result of their energetic live shows.

Featuring a sound based on melodic chordal riffs and lead guitar parts, their sound has been likened to Kashmir or early Radiohead, whom the band name as an influence.

The band is comprised of vocalist Tom Whitty and his brother Sam on guitar, Matt Millar on drums, James Saunders on bass and Rob Rambaldi on keyboards.

The band met at school in Wangaratta, Australia but base themselves in Melbourne. Originally performing under the name The Flight, the name change to Armen Firman occurred in 2005 when they also added their keyboardist.

In early 2005, the band released their debut EP called 'Our Love Is Deceased' which featured two tracks, 1. It's Too Late and 2. Not My Love. The former was played within days of being printed on national Australian radio station, Triple J.

In 2006 the band recorded a six track EP called 'Beggars and Choosers'. The tracklist for the EP was:

Fade Me 3.07 Come On 3.26 Break you gently 3.12 I feel it all 1.56 My Black Eyes 3.28 City Please 2.37

While 'Come On' received some national airplay, the band never officially released the EP.

In late 2006, Armen Firman built a home studio, which was used to record their 3rd untitled EP, known unoficially as 'No. 5 Record'. The first single 'Porch Dweller' saw bassist James Saunders sing lead vocals for the first time in Armen Firman's recording history. Porch Dweller received airplay on several radio stations and reached the top of the Indie charts in national radio broadcaster Triple J's Unearthed initiative. It has since been played on more than one occassion on Triple J's Home and Hosed program.

Armen Firman also created a MySpace profile in mid 2006 which features a live video of 'Porch Dweller', filmed and edited by Dean Carter. See http://www.myspace.com/armenfirman

The band's only major performance for 2006 was at the Folk, Rhythm and Life Festival in their homestate of Victoria. See www.bilyana.com for festival details.

Armen Firman's first show of 2007 was on Australia Day the 26th of January 2007 at The Northcote Social Club with supports The Razor Cartel and Chainmber Made. The performance was somewhat of a global event, as it was broadcast through online live music broadcaster, www.globalmusiccam.com. Photos and a review of the performance were posted online at Australian music website www.thedwarf.com.au. The performance provided the footage used for the unofficial Porch Dweller filmclip that is featured on the Armen Firman Myspace website.

Armen Firman supported Bertie Blackman on 15th of February at Sodens Hotel, Albury, NSW, and the 17th of February at The Evelyn Hotel, Fitzroy, VIC, as part of Bertie Blackman's Australian 2007 Fast Bitch Tour.

All Armen Firman gig dates are listed on the band's myspace at http://www.myspace.com/armenfirman
