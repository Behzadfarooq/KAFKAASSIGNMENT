Armenian battalions

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Group of Armenian Soldiers During World War I

Armenian battalions were battle groups formed by Armenian volunteer conscripts during the period of Balkan Wars and World War I, between 1912-1920. The below national armies had battalions formed by Armenians, mostly fighting against the Ottoman forces:

  • During the Balkan Wars period 1912-13, Armenians had an auxiliary battle group within Bulgarian Army, fighting against Ottoman forces - see wikipedia article Andranik Toros Ozanian#Balkan Wars
  • Between 1916-20, the French Army employed an Armenian Legion in Adana (Turkey) and Arara (Palestine), fighting against Ottoman and German forces - see wikipedia articles French Armenian Legion and Sarkis Zeitlian
  • There were also Armenian volunteer units in Russian Army and British Army during World War I, fighting Ottoman forces in Western Armenia, now in modern Turkey - see wikipedia article Armenian volunteer units
