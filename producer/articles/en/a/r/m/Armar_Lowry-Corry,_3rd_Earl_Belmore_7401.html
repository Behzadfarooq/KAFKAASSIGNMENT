Armar Lowry-Corry, 3rd Earl Belmore

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Armar Lowry-Corry, 3rd Earl Belmore (December 28, 1801 - December 17, 1845) was an Irish nobleman and politician and the eldest son of Somerset Lowry-Corry, 2nd Earl Belmore.

He was Member of Parliament for County Fermanagh from 1823 to 1830 and was appointed High Sheriff of County Fermanagh in 1832.

He was married on May 27, 1834 to Emily Louise Shepherd, youngest daughter and co-heir of William Shepherd, of Brabourne, Kent, and had issue:

  • Somerset Lowry-Corry, 4th Earl Belmore
  • Admiral the Hon. Armar Lowry-Corry RN (May 25, 1836 - August 1, 1919), who was married on February 8, 1868 to Geraldine King King, fifth daughter of James King King MP, of Staunton Park, Herefordshire, and had issue: (i) Arthur (1869-1946), (ii) Lionel (1870-1871), (iii) the Rev. Gerald (1871-1929), who was a Catholic priest and was a chaplain to the forces between 1914 and 1919, (iv) Rosamund Florence (1873-1932), (v) Algernon Frederick (1874-1875), (vi) Adrian (1876-1921), who was married to Geraldine Hartcup and was the father of Galbraith Lowry-Corry, 7th Earl Belmore, (vii) Muriel (1877-1965), (viii) Evelyn (1879-1963), (ix) Hubert Armar (1881-1927), (x) Ethel Mary (1882-1883), (xi) Bertram (1883-1884), (x) Walter (1885-1946)
  • The Hon. Frederick Cecil George Lowry-Corry (June 24, 1839 - May 12, 1855)
  • Colonel Hon. Henry William Lowry-Corry (1845–1927)

Lord Belmore died on December 17, 1845 and was succeeded by his eldest son.
