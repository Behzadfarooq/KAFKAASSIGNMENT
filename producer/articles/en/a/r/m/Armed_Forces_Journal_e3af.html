Armed Forces Journal

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Armed Forces Journal (AFJ) is a monthly journal for military officers and thought leaders in government and industry, the people who set military policy and strategy for the nation.

Founded in 1863[1], AFJ is published today by the Defense News Media Group, a unit of the Army Times Publishing Company, which is a part of Gannett Company (NYSE:GCI).
