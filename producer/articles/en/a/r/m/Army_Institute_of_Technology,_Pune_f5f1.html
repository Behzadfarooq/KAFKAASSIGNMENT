Army Institute of Technology (AIT) is an engineering college located at Dighi Hills in Pune, Maharashtra, India and affiliated to the University of Pune. It was established for the wards of Indian Army personnel (serving or retired) and conducts a national competitive exam to select 240 students each year in four disciplines of engineering.

[edit] Disciplines

The disciplines or majors offered are Mechanical Engineering, Computer Engineering, Electronics and Telecommunications Engineering and Information Technology. All courses are under the bachelors' program offered by the University of Pune, one of the best-rated universities of the country. Admission is based on merit, as per the AIEEE (All India Engineering Entrance Exam). Unlike other colleges in this region, AIT draws its student population from all across the nation.

[edit] History

AIT was founded in August 1994 by Gen B.C Joshi PVSM, AVSM, ADC. The Institute was inaugurated by Gen S. Roy Chaudhary PVSM, ADC on 24th Feb, 1995. The first batch of BE students graduated in 1998.

[edit] Culturals

AIT is fairly accomplished in culturals. AITians have a long history of winnning prizes in cultural festivals all over the country, especially in the Pune region. The institute has repeatedly won Verve, a cultural festival that attracts many colleges every year for a plethora of events and is one of the largest congregations of colleges in the country.

[edit] Governance

Members of the Board and their brief background:

AIT functions under the aegis of Army Welfare Education Society (AWES), which registered as a society in April 1983. The board of Governors was reconstituted in 2004 with Chief of Army Staff (COAS) as the President, Vice Chief of the Army Staff (VCOAS) and all army commanders as vice presidents with nominated heads of arms/services as members. Adjutant general has been appointed as chairman of the executive committee. The Board of Governors that formulates broad policy measures for AIT is at Army HQ with Adjutant General, Army HQ as its Chairman. Chief of Staff HQ, Southern Command is one of its members.

[edit] Students

AIT is a predominantly residential school where all stutents are wards of The Indian Army's personnel. As such there is a very healthy mix of people from all over the country which gives the institute a very lively feel. The students engage in all sorts of extra curricular activites like sports, dramatics, debates and hence are regular winners at most inter college fests held in pune.
