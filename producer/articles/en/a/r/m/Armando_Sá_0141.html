Armando Miguel Correia de Sá (born 16 September 1975 in Maputo) is a Mozambican football (soccer) defender. He is currently on loan at English team, Leeds United A.F.C. from RCD Espanyol. He has been capped 21 times for the Mozambique national football team.

He also played for Portuguese teams C.F. Os Belenenses (1992-94), Vilafranquense (1994-96), Bragança (1997), Vila Real de Santo António (1997-98), Rio Ave (1998-2001), Sporting de Braga (2002), SL Benfica (2002-04) and Spanish club Villarreal CF (2004-05) and holds a Portugal passport.

On 3 January 2007, Sa joined English Championship side, Leeds United until May 2007, with a view to a longer deal. Sa made his away debut as a substitute for the Yorkshire outfit in Leeds' 3-1 defeat to West Bromwich Albion in the FA Cup. Sa has now played several matches for United playing in various positions such as right back, left back and also in midfield. It is not yet known is Leeds manager, Dennis Wise will extend Sa's loan deal at the end of the season.

[edit] Honours and awards

  • 2004: Portuguese Cup (SL Benfica)
  • 2004: Intertoto Cup (Villarreal CF)
  • 2006: Copa del Rey (RCD Espanyol)
