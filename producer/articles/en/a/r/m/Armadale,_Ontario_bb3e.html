Armadale, Ontario is a former rural postal office that overlaps both Markham, Ontario and Scarborough, Ontario.

A postal station was established in 1869 along what it is Passmore Avenue. Only a church remains of the former postal station.

Other buildings in the small community included:

  • Armadale Free Methodist Church (1880)
  • blacksmith shop
  • brick making yard
  • several farms
  • general store operated by the Beare Family
  • Temperance Hotel and Tavern

The Markham section was developed in the 1980s and 1990s as a residential community from farm land. To the south, the land has remained underdeveloped with many abandonded farms and apple orchards. Small industrial and commercial parks were built in the area. Along Steeles east of Markham was a small farm selling vegetables with industrial parks lining the south part of the farm. But this farm will soon disappear as a new mall with focus on Asian clientle will be built on the property. T & T Supermarket was recently opened to the east of the farm along Middlefield Road and Steeles Avenue.
