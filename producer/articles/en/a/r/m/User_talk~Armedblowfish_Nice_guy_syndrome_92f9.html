I'm the one who originally added the tags. Here's why:

Fact-fiction: as I said earlier, there is no hard evidence at all in favor of this "syndrome". It does not exist in the medical literature, it has no actual survey data backing it, just pseudonymous posts on message boards. The label "syndrome" doesn't even come from the so-called "nice guys" themselves, but from people who dislike them. Really, this isn't much more than a pattern inferred from various flame wars.

Importance: You can argue, of course, that the mere presence of this argument frequently on the net is grounds for inclusion. In that case, somebody has to find links to actual boards or groups where this was discussed, and cite that as proof.

Jargon: Look how many sweeping generalizations about men and women this article makes. NOT ONE, repeat, not ONE, has survey data backing it. "Women may like...men prefer..." these are just ideas pulled out of someone's ass.

The tags should be kept until (and IF) these concerns are addressed. Tyronen 17:12, 29 June 2006 (UTC)
