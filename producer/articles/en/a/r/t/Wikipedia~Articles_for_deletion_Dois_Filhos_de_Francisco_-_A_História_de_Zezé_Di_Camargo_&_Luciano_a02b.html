[edit] Dois Filhos de Francisco - A História de Zezé Di Camargo & Luciano

Virtually no context or content, no cited verifications of notibility. Knowing Is Half The Battle 06:01, 17 October 2006 (UTC)

  • Delete, Possibly Speedy Delete As Nominator Knowing Is Half The Battle 06:07, 17 October 2006 (UTC)
    • Keep Nice cleanup and verification, Smerdis! I was under the impression that this was a poorly written article about a non notable film. This should be expanded, please accept my appoligies for arguing for its deletion. Knowing Is Half The Battle 16:42, 17 October 2006 (UTC)
  • Delete per nom. MER-C 08:08, 17 October 2006 (UTC)
  • Keep The article needs to be expanded, but the film was wildly popular in its native Brazil. The fact that English-speaking audiences may not be as familiar with it isn't grounds for deletion. GassyGuy 08:56, 17 October 2006 (UTC)
I'm not really in research mode, so I don't have a great source to offer, but here's a site that mentions the film and should give you some idea of its popularity: [1]. The film does have an entry on Portuguese Wikipedia, although it appears to do more to document the plot than its significance: [2] (this may be undeserved criticism, as I don't speak Portuguese.) Anyway, it seems a pretty clear keep to me, though the article definitely needs cleaning and expansion. Hopefully somebody else can find better sources. GassyGuy 09:10, 17 October 2006 (UTC)]
  • Keep per GassyGuy. Appears to be a notable motion picture; has its article on Portuguese Wikipedia. The text here was obviously by someone for whom English was not a first language, and I have tried to clean it up some. Suggest that this should at minimum move to the simpler Dois Filhos de Francisco, or better yet, to an English language title if it ever be released in an English language version. - Smerdis of Tlön 15:59, 17 October 2006 (UTC)
  • Keep. Well known movie in Brazil. --Carioca 04:32, 18 October 2006 (UTC)
  • Keep per nom's change of mind. Her Pegship 03:51, 19 October 2006 (UTC)
