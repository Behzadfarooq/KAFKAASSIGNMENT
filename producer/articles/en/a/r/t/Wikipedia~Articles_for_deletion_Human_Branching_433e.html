  • Hello, Cladogenesis deals with the speciation of one species into two or more species due to a reproductive branching event. "Human Branching" does not require speciation to take place - it is simply the genetic variation that exists between any two populations that have had isolated reproduction from one another over some period of time. While Human Branching can become Cladogenesis, Human Branching need not be Cladogenesis.--Kengineer 01:19, 13 February 2006 (UTC)

  • Human Branching is the simple self-descriptive term that simply means what it says. It can be any amount of genetic distance, from one generation and up.
