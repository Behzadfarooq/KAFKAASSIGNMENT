[The major problem with the article is that there is little to no possibility of verification. Much of Pokemon Elite 2000's (PE2K) history is unavailable due to forum deletions and what not. For example, the deletion of the forum at proboards can be verified only by outside "archival" sources that list likely one page of the actual website. Another example (which cannot be verified) was the history of this website and the forums at http://www.pokecommunity.com (PC), which due to the fact that PC had been reincarnated once already, it would be difficult to verify that PE2K actually did merge with PC at one point, and then decided to leave the PC community. Finally, a third example is that it is impossible to verify the actual "start" of the forums. We have a rough idea of the timeframe, and through deduction from the same "archival" sites, we have made only a rough estimate of the time when the forums start, but not the exact date. We know it's most likely around the date of February 10th, 2002, but the exact date may be a day or two earlier depending on the time delay between the creation of the forums and the post done by Ryan.

Link for estimation of forum start date: http://web.archive.org/web/20020220205411/pokemonelite.proboards5.com/index.cgi?board=general

Verification of early Global Moderators (time of promotion is lost, but promotional order of the first three global moderators is as such, but cannot be verified except by memory of Kenny c002): 1. http://web.archive.org/web/20021022190018/pokemonelite.proboards5.com/index.cgi?action=viewprofile&username=ash_pikachu_98 2. http://web.archive.org/web/20021010180154/pokemonelite.proboards5.com/index.cgi?action=viewprofile&username=SyrusTheTriumphant 3. http://web.archive.org/web/20030605005810/pokemonelite.proboards5.com/index.cgi?action=viewprofile&username=Kenny_C.002

Most of the current events in PE2K, however, can be verified. For example, the current "WAR" can be verified via forum posts by global moderators of PE2K.

Source: http://web.archive.org/web/20020802104421/http://pokemonelite.proboards5.com/index.cgi http://web.archive.org/web/20040211203456/http://pokemonelite.proboards5.com/ http://www.pokemonelite2000.com/forum/announcement.php?f=44] Kenny c002 00:09, 15 July 2006 (UTC)

rust - "fails WP:WEB - just another pokemon site"

Erm... we do have one requirement from the WP:WEB...

"The content is distributed via a site which is both well known and independent of the creators, either through an online newspaper or magazine, an online publisher, or an online broadcaster."

There's PE2K Radio, a Pokemon Radio station that frequently endorses us (Link is at the bottom of the page). It was originally a Pokemon Radio station, but became PE2K Radio when they began endorsing us. I'm going to have an article up about that tomorrow.


Sorry, but that quote refers to your content. As in, the particular shows and/or music that you play. However, that content is distributed through a site that is NOT well known (4lifenet). If anything, you should have an article *purely* on 4lifenet and "pe2k" radio, and nothing on the website/forums.--I AM a Citation 14:27, 15 July 2006 (UTC)

rust--We only host PE2K Radio on 4lifenet. If we could, we would host is on Pokemonelite2000.com, but the owner of PE2K Radio (ELMO) wouldn't be able to update it, only Ryan can, and he hasn't been very active for the past few weeks...


Consider that the Pe2K radio WAS hosted on the pokemonelite website. Then you would be attempting to justify its existence because its distributing content on a well known website (itself), when its status AS a well-known website hasn't been established. The actual forum is NOT content! The actual posts and/or updates on the website are NOT content. The actual radio shows and the copyrighted music IS content. The official pokemon music is already mentioned on wikipedia on the pages for the movie soundtracks and the pokemon games. The only thing that might even be slightly legitimate on wikipedia is the original content [radio shows] which currently are distributed through 4lifenet. If they were distributed through pe2k, your argument would be WEAKENED. Why? The content would no longer be "independent of the creators," and the actual website which distributed the content (pe2k) is not much more well known (so unknown in fact, that its presence on Wikipedia is being challenged!). Pokemon Elite 2000 has no place on Wikipedia, and until the Radio Shows (the only things that MAY conform to Wikipedia policy) are distributed through a more known website, nothing from pe2k should have any place --I AM a Citation 20:21, 15 July 2006 (UTC)
