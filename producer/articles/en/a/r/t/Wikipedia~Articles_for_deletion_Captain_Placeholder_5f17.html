Wikipedia:Articles for deletion/Captain Placeholder

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

< Wikipedia:Articles for deletion

This page is an archive of the discussion about the proposed deletion of the article below. This page is no longer live. Further comments should be made on the article's talk page rather than here so that this page is preserved as an historic record.
The result of the debate was not easy to work out. There is no consensus to delete. The main question is whether to keep or merge with World of Warcraft. I will call this a keep for now because the World of Warcraft article does not appear to have a space for specific, individual NPCs. If anyone has a better place to merge this article, that will also be acceptable. Sjakkalle (Check!) 07:50, 18 Jun 2005 (UTC)

[edit] Captain Placeholder

Delete - not just a minor character from a game, but a temporary minor character that doesn't even exist any more --Stormie 12:04, Jun 5, 2005 (UTC)

Keep - However, one that has had a substantial effect on the community and has, on many servers, become a significant part of the culture. --John Kenneth Fisher 14:31, Jun 5, 2005 (UTC)

  • Merge with World of Warcraft -- I agree that Captain Placeholder seems to have had a substantial effect on the WoW community and as such is notable enough in that context; however, letting the good Captain have an article of his own might be a overdoing it a little, especially as he has passed on. -- Captain Disdain 17:35, 5 Jun 2005 (UTC)
  • Keep - what John said. I think that keeping the Captain separate would keep the place tidier; it functions well as a separate article, while in the WoW one it'd make a significant non sequitur, being irrelevant to the description of the game. -- Kizor 00:41, 11 Jun 2005 (UTC)

Merge with World of Warcraft. Alternatively, an article on the WoW community and its related topics (Leeroy Jenkins, for example) would probably be appropriate. Junjk 14:04, 6 Jun 2005 (UTC)

  • Keep as above comment ··gracefool |☺ 08:21, 8 Jun 2005 (UTC)
  • Keep in that respect. I think articles about the communities of online games is a very good idea. --CGally81 22:33, 10 Jun 2005 (UTC)
  • Merge with World of Warcraft --Nova Cygni 01:18, 15 Jun 2005 (UTC)


[edit] Aren't we done now

We're over the 5 days, but not sure of the procedure to declare this VFD period ended. --John Kenneth Fisher 15:59, Jun 17, 2005 (UTC)

This page is now preserved as an archive of the debate and, like some other VfD subpages, is no longer 'live'. Subsequent comments on the issue, the deletion, or the decision-making process should be placed on the relevant 'live' pages. Please do not edit this page.
