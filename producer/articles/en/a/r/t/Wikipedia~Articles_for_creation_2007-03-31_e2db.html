The Bucks County Intermediate Unit (BCIU) is a branch of the Pennsylvania Department of Education. It is the 22nd of the 29 regional educational service agencies in Pennsylvania created by the state’s General Assembly in 1971, and is headquartered in Doylestown.

The BCIU provides specialized education services to thirteen public school districts and three vocational and technical schools in Bucks County with total student populations approaching 100,000 children. It also operates the Bucks County Alternative school in Levittown. The agency has over 1,300 employees and provides educational services at many sites throughout the county. Funded through a combination of federal, state, and local school district dollars as well as grants, the BCIU coordinates many educational programs designed to help infants, young children, students, and adults.

The "Bucks County Intermediate Unit" is managed by a thirteen member school board composed of one representative from the school board of each of the thirteen public school districts it serves. The highest ranking officer of the organization is it's Executive Director who reports to the board.

[edit] Services

  • K–12 special education
  • Professional development for educators and parents.
  • Library services, including, professional educational and media libraries.
  • Early childhood (pre-school) services
  • Collaborative purchasing services
  • Curriculum evaluation and development
  • Computer and Technology services including curriculum integration.
  • Safe & drug free school educational programs
  • Programs to assist homeless children and their families
  • Non-Public school services

[edit] Do-Ashkaft Cave

This Middle Paleolithic cave site is located at north of Kermanshah, near Taq-e Bostan. It is located about 1600m asl and its entrance faces south, over-looking the national park of Kuhestan. The site was discovered by two Iranian researchers, F. Biglari and S. Heydari in 1999 and during next four years was sampled each month which resulted in a rich Middle Paleolithic lithic collection. The Mousterian occupants of the cave made their tools on local raw material outcrops around the cave.

Jan Scholten (born December 23, 1951 Helmond, Netherlands) is a doctor and the author of numerous books and research papers on homeopathy.

After finishing medical school in 1982 he studied with a number of noted homeopaths including Alphons Geukens, Jost Kunzli, Roger Morrison, Bill Gray, George Vithoulkas and Rajan Sankaran.

His book Homeopathy and the Elements, which examined homeopathic remedies within the context of the periodic table, is now considered a standard reference book in the field. His book Homeopathy and Minerals has been translated into eleven languages.

Scholten also founded the journal Interhomeopathy, launched a company called Harp Software and founded Homeopathie Stichting, a foundation for the promotion of homeopathy.

[edit] Joe Salter

With his election on January 12, 2004, Joe Salter became the 62nd speaker of the Louisiana House of Representatives. He is the first representative from his district (Sabine, Red River, and parts of Vernon and DeSoto parishes) to serve as House Speaker.

A veteran of the House for more than 19 years, Representative Salter formerly served on the Board of Aldermen for the Village of Florien. He was first elected to the House of Representatives in 1986 to fill an unexpired term and was reelected without opposition to serve his first full term in 1987. He was reelected in 1991, 1995, 1999 (without opposition), and with 76 percent of the vote in October, 2003. During his tenure in the House, he has served on the House Education Committee and on the key committees which address the budget and financing of state government – Appropriations Committee, the Joint Legislative Committee on the Budget, and the Joint Capital Outlay Committee.

He has served as Chairman of the House Legislative Bureau and Rural Legislative Task Force, and as a member of the Special Committee on Louisiana Homeland Security.

Representative Salter serves as Vice Chairman of the Board of Directors of the Louisiana School for Math, Science and the Arts and on the Advisory Board of Northwestern State University - Leesville/Fort Polk. On a regional and national level, he is a member of the National Conference of State Legislatures (NCSL) and serves on their Education Committee, the Southern Regional Education Board Legislative Advisory Council, and Education Commission of the States.

His contributions to his community and the state have been recognized by numerous organizations. Among his honors and awards are: Louisiana Federation of Teachers Legislative Golden Apple Award (1986, 1987, 1990) and Representative of the Year Award (1991 and 2001); Louisiana Association of Educators Friend of Education (1994), Outstanding Serve Award and Legislative Honor Roll (1997), and Outstanding Legislator (2001); Louisiana Library Association Distinguished Legislator of the Year (1989); Louisiana Council on Aging Directors’ Association, Champion of Senior Citizen Issues in Louisiana Special Award (2001); Fleur de Lis Health Care Leadership Award (2002); Guardian of Small Business Award( 1991) Louisiana Rural Health Association, Legislator of the Year (1995); Louisiana Public Broadcasting President’s Award (2000); Police Jury Association of Louisiana Friend of the Parishes (1997).

He is a member of the Coushatta-Red River and Sabine Parish chambers of commerce, Toledo Bend Citizens Advisory Committee, and Toledo Bend Lake Association.

Representative Salter is dedicated to serving the people of Louisiana and is committed to moving our state forward by continuing to improve education, health care, and economic development.
