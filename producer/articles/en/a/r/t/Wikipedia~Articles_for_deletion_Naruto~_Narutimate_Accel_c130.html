[edit] Naruto: Narutimate Accel

Naruto: Narutimate Accel (edit|talk|history|links|watch|logs)

There are no sources for this game except for a poster in Japanese that the article links to. A Google search for "Narutimate Accel" brings up nothing. Contested prod. ... discospinster talk 18:00, 22 November 2006 (UTC)

  • Note: This debate has been included in the list of Japan-related deletions. -- Aurunei 18:40, 22 November 2006 (UTC)
  • Keep There is a source. Nothing comes up on Google because it was recently announced and the game is in Japanese (and you're searching for an English term). --Zeno McDohl (talk) 19:30, 22 November 2006 (UTC)
  • Delete WP:WINACB--BITTECK 20:16, 22 November 2006 (UTC)
    WP:NOT#CBALL does not apply. The article does not contain speculation and there are first and second-party sources, both of which are reliable. –Gunslinger47 02:12, 28 November 2006 (UTC)
  • Delete as only source right now is a single magazine scan, not enough to pass WP:RS. Feel free to recreate when more reliable sources about the game are found. NeoChaosX (he shoots, he scores!) 22:39, 22 November 2006 (UTC)
    The scan has been corroborated, and the issue of Weekly Shonen Jump most certainly contains an advertisement for it. Further, the developers have officially announced it and revealed its website]. –Gunslinger47 02:12, 28 November 2006 (UTC)
    In that case, change my vote to a keep. NeoChaosX (he shoots, he scores!) 02:47, 28 November 2006 (UTC)
  • Delete per above. Game needs some noteriaty before it deserves an article. – Someguy0830 (T | C) 00:57, 23 November 2006 (UTC)
  • Keep Was announced less than a day ago in Shueisha's Weekly Shonen Jump, the magazine that the Naruto manga is published in. That's about as reliable as it gets; might as well be V-Jump. CyberConnect2 has also announced that the official website will be opened on the 27th. No, google searches won't net anything, webcrawlers don't work that fast. Especially when you aren't using the Japanese title. WtW-Suzaku 01:54, 23 November 2006 (UTC)
    Comment Aside from the fact that it exists, what exactly do you know about it? Nothing. One cover does not a definitive source make. – Someguy0830 (T | C) 03:24, 23 November 2006 (UTC)
    Comment We know that it exists and that's enough. --Zeno McDohl (talk) 05:52, 23 November 2006 (UTC)
    Knowing it exists is not enough. We don't know anything about it. You can write a grand total of one sentence that isn't crystal balling. – Someguy0830 (T | C) 08:07, 23 November 2006 (UTC)
    CommentUm, what? At the very least it deserves a stub, until more information comes out. A higher resolution scan is now out, so translations should follow shortly.
    http://img217.imageshack.us/img217/9307/narutimateaccelannouncerr1.jpg - From Weekly Shounen Jump Issue #52 WtW-Suzaku 21:59, 23 November 2006 (UTC)
  • Keep - The newest in a series of games, and obviously noteworthy. - !Malomeat 06:42, 23 November 2006 (UTC)
  • Keep. The source is reliable. It's not crystal-balling. There are hits for the Japanese. This is not a great stub, but it can be fixed. Dekimasu 06:47, 23 November 2006 (UTC)
  • Keep. Like Malomeat said, it's the next in a series of games that already has articles here. If it gets deleted now, it'll just get remade when more info comes out. The Splendiferous Gegiford 00:59, 24 November 2006 (UTC)
  • Comment. This is the fifth game in the Ultimate Ninja series, aka Narutimate. See here for the four other Ultimate Ninja articles:
    • Naruto: Ultimate Ninja
    • Naruto: Narutimate Hero 2
    • Naruto: Narutimate Hero 3
