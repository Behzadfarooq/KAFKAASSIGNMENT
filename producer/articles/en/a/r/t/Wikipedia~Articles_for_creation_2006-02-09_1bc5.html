[edit] Staten Island Historical Society

The Staten Island Historical Society is a private not-for-profit 501(c)(3) founded in 1856 to "collect whatever relates to the history of Staten Island". The society operates Historic Richmond Town under an agreement with the City of New York, Department of Cultural Affairs. In addition to Historic Richmond Town, the society owns and operates the Decker Farm (the last privately owned working farm in New York City), the Billiou-Stillwell-Perine House and the Judge Jacob Tysen House. The society has a collection of over 70,000 artifacts, 50,000 photographs, including the largest collection of the work of Alice Austen, and a significant collection of other archival materials. The society is open to the public Wednesday through Sunday. the archives are opewn by appointment.

[edit] Nonrenewable resource

Important! Currently Nonrenewable resource redirects to Renewable resource. The two terms are opposite things, and so Nonrenewable resource deserves its own entry/article. Anyone publishing this article must remove the redirect.

A nonrenewable resource is a natural resource (such as a mineral, plant, etc,) that is used too quickly by something to be replenished, or that cannot be replenished at all. Examples are rain forests, fossil fuels, such as oil, natural gas, etc.

Nonrenewable resources cannot be renewed because the earth's processes by which they are formed either take too long to create the new material, are very complicated and require intense earth processes (heat, pressure), or it is impossible to regenerate what has been lost or used (as in the case of an extinct animal species).

[edit] John Insley Blair

John Insley Blair

Born in Warren County, New Jersey, 22 August 1802, John Insley Blair was a successful railroad magnate and a philanthropist. While Blair received limited education as a young man, he was successful in a general country store business in the New York-New Jersey area and expanded his business interests into other lines such as flour mills, manufacture of cotton, and ultimately coal and iron production. The coal and iron business led to Blair's association with and investment in railroads. By the end of the 19th century, John I. Blair headed more than a dozen railroads, including the Delaware, Lackawanna & Western and the Union Pacific Railroad, and was engaged in railroad building in Iowa, Wisconsin, Kansas, Nebraska, Dakota, Missouri, and Texas. He also built the first railroad across Iowa from the Mississippi to the Missouri rivers, and subsequently more than 2,000 miles in Iowa and Nebraska. Some quipped that Blair built railroads to use steel from his mills and towns to provide traffic for his railroads. In 1890, at 88, he capped his great career by founding the famed banking firm of Blair & Co.

Blair gave generously to various causes, and the most significant beneficiaries of his gifts were the Presbyterian Church, [Princeton University], and [Blair Academy]. From 1866 until his death in 1899, Blair was also a Trustee of Princeton University. Princeton's Blair Hall, built in 1897 with funds donated by John Insley Blair, is considered a masterpiece of the Tudor Gothic style and features Blair Tower and Blair Arch.

In politics, Blair ran for governor of New Jersey in 1868 as a Republican candidate but did not win. One of his daughters married Charles Scribner, founder of the publishing-house in New York.

Blair was also father-in-law to Charles Scribner, founder of the publishing house of Charles Scribner's Sons.

In The Wealthy 100, a study of financial worth as a percentage of the GNP, John I Blair is ranked as the #19th wealthiest man in American History.

[edit] Sessomaru

Sesshoumaru is Inu-Yasha'a older brother. He appears to be very cold and emotionless. Emotions are difficult for him, but his own journey (originally to hunt and kill Inu-Yasha) will have very special plans for him.

He, too, possesses a sword given by his late father: this one forged from the right fang. It's known as Tenseiga, and there is an odd twist to it. Whereas Inu-Yasha's Tetsusaiga can slay 100 demons in a single swing, Tenseiga's stroke can save 100 souls. It is a sword of life rather than death, and the cold Sesshomaru finds the sword to be quite worthless.

But Tenseiga sticks diligently to its destiny of saving lives. Once Inu-Yasha managed to discover the secret to Tetsusaiga's power, a stroke from the sword almost killed Sesshoumaru, but Tenseiga stepped in and spirited Sesshoumaru away. The truth is that the unique swords are a twist of fate designed by his and Inu-Yasha's father to ensure that neither brother can overcome the other. For a while, Sesshoumaru couldn't make a weapon that could overcome Tetsusaiga, but then again, with Tenseiga in his possession, not even Tetsusaiga can strike him down. (Not that he'd be able to wield the sword himself anyway. Tetsusaiga can only be wielded by someone with human blood. Inu-Yasha can wield it because he's half human but Sesshoumaru can't because he's a full-blooded demon). Tenseiga, however, will play a role in Sesshoumaru's personal journey, as he learns the truth about its powers of life.

In the meantime, Sesshoumaru finds another way to challenge Inu-Yasha. Learning of the fallen demon Goshinki, who had managed to bite Tetsusaiga in two, he consults a twisted disciple of Toutousai named Kaijinbou, who takes a fang from the fallen demon and forges a new sword: Toukijin. Despite all the evil embodied in that sword, Sesshoumaru is able to overcome it and master the sword, finally gaining the chance to face his brother on equal terms.

BasicNeeds is an international NGO, registered as a UK charity in 1999. It has been set up to initiate programmes in developing countries, which actively involve mentally ill people and their carers and enable them to satisfy their basic needs and exercise their basic rights. The organisation intends to support local governments, organisations and a developing consumer movement to work with mentally ill people and their carers, promoting their well being while tackling both their poverty and social injustice. In order to achieve its aims and objectives, BasicNeeds has developed a Community Mental Health and Development Model. Information about the Model can be found at www.basicneeds.org.uk . Furthermore, discussion and knowledge about mental health and development issues in the developing world has been initiated on the BasicNeeds web-journal www.mentalhealthanddevelopment.org

The organsiation currently works in India, Sri Lanka, Laos PDR, Ghana, Tanzania, Uganda and Kenya. Exploratory work and feasibility studies have also been conducted in Bangladesh, Cambodia, Vietnam, Malawi and Nigeria. The organisation already supports 22,130 mentally ill people (July 2005) and 15,678 carers. Of these, some 19,109 (86%) mentally ill people have received treatment and 13,418 (70%) have been stabilised.

[edit] Unit factor analysis

Unit factor analysis, also known as the “conversion system” among other identifiers, is a system of developing solutions to many elementary and complex problems in science and mathematics. It is based on the fact that from any algebraic statement of equality, such as 2.54cm = 1 inch can be obtained two multipliers, or factors, who’s value is one (1). For example:

Dividing each side of the above statement of equality by 2.54cm the expression

2.54 cm = 1 inch
2.54 cm 2.54 cm

From which we obtain the factor that equals one (1) (1 inch/2.54 cm). If we divide both sides of the equation by 1 inch, we obtain the unit factor (2.54 cm/1 inch). An example of the usefulness of the system is that it enables one to organize a problem in a consistent manner that is easy to compute with a calculator and each step is suggested by the previous step e.g. What is the price of gasoline in Euros per liter (excluding taxes), if the price is $2.50 per gallon? We probably know many of the, so called, conversion factors, and if we do not know them or if they have constantly changing value, as does Euros/Dollar, we can easily look them up. Start with what is given or known

($2.50/gallon) X (1 gallon/4 quarts) X (1.06 quart/ 1 liter) X ( 1 Euro/ $1.18) = 0.56 Euro per liter.

Very little is known about the actual identity of the Go Hard Brothers, as they perform under the pseudonyms "Go Hard Brother 1" and "Go Hard Brother 2" but they have given some clues into their lives past and present. The duo claim to have met in the small spa town of Cheltenham in 1999 and are believed to have been school friends throughout their adolscence. They cite the somewhat stifled Cheltonian atmosphere as the main reason behind their experimental and avant-garde musical style and a desire to break free of traditional musical roles and genres. Go Hard Brother 2 has publicly stated that the cosmopolitan society of Brighton, his birthplace has been a major factor behind the multiculural aspects of the band's music.

Bold text Music: Important Features and Problems with Catagorisation

Although the GHBs define themselves as experimental electronic-jazz/dance, they encompass a range of other genres into their music. On their debut, and to date sole EP, "Stick A Fork In It It's Done", they display a clear interest in rock, art rock and post punk in particular, hip hop, jazz, blues and power ballads. "We wanted to create a sound that reflects the growing diveristy within society today" says Go Hard Brother 1, and they have received considerable praise from their peers on the underground scene and several music critics, such as the notorious Jassy S for achieving their aim.

The GHBs are not fond of ostentatious or elaborate lyrics, claiming that any poetic lyrics would simply distract the listener from the essence of their music, the instruments. However, in almost all their songs, short and often intentionally humorous phrases can be heard, often mimicing Tim Westwood, who the band claim is "the funniest man alive today" and who is a personal hero and role model for both men. The track "China is Rubbish" has caused some outrage within the Chinese community but the band cite the controversial Communist dictatorship as the motivation behind the political satire rather than the republic itself.

Some common features of Go Hard Brothers Songs include: A variety of different sections and genres A fast tempo A "come down section" that is both preceded and proceded by a musical climax Various "wigger" comments Synthesizers A use of repetition and the recycling of ideas

Bold textInfluences


Tim Westwood, Landscape, Mylo, the Prodigy, Herbie Hancock, Charlie Parker, Radiohead, M, Queen and Blur are all thought to be major influences.

[edit] Seismic shock

When an earthquake occurs, a large object (such as a asteroid) strikes the earth, or another large disaster (volcanic eruption, tsunami, atomic bomb explosion) occurs, they may produce seismic waves. A seismic wave is only a general term for many specific types of waves, such as a primary wave. Seismic waves travel from the epicenter of an earthquake or from the site of impact at speeds that may exceed the speed of sound in all directions, and can cause anywhere from no damage to great damage.

[edit] In the Earth

When a seismic wave occurs on the earth, waves are released in all directions. The released waves may travel in different speeds depending on what they are moving through (air, water, rock, magma). When they impact an objects, such as a building, they may cause the destruction of that object, depending on how strong the wave is. The intensity of a wave is measured through use of a seismograph.

Seismic waves are most often caused by earthquakes, but can also be caused by volcano eruptions, asteroid impacts, or even man-made causes, such as a bomb explosion or collapsing buildings.

[edit] In Politics

In politics, a seismic wave may also refer to a shocking statement or action made by a well known person. This often causes a positive or negative reaction from the public.

[edit] Sid Curtis

Sid Curtis was literally born into theatre; her father was a magician, her mother created costumes. Sid made her stage debut at the age 9 months - as a prop - and has never left the business. She was extremely active in community theatre and would always join the crew if there wasn't a place for her in the cast. Through this method, she learned all aspects of theatre, costuming, make up, props, scenic, lighting and stage management. At the age of thirteen, she joined up with a college student to create Story Hour, a traveling puppet act that visited rural Louisiana Libraries to promote reading.

Naturally, Sid went to an Arts high school - Booker T. Washington High School for the Performing and Visual Arts, in Dallas, Texas - where she was an honors student who acted in most of the plays and designed lights, sets and make-up for the others. She received an acting scholarship to the College of Santa Fe, however, since she had been a diligent student in high school, she found the curriculum at the college to lack stimulation and took a job as the Technical Director for the local Road House - The Armory for the Arts. As the TD, she oversaw all technical aspects of the facility as well as acting as house lighting designer for the musical acts that came through. While in Santa Fe, Sid and a few colleagues expanded on the existing children's theatre camp to create a year-long Children's Theatre troupe. Sid designed all the sets and lighting for the productions and starred in several of the plays. Southwest Children's Theatre is still in existence today.

From Santa Fe, Sid was recruited to become the House Lighting Designer for The McCallum Theatre in Palm Desert, California. There she designed lights for such legends as Cab Calloway, Sarah Vaughan and Ella Fitzgerald. After two seasons with The McCallum, Sid decided to move on to New York. She found a job as the House Lighting Designer for the Bottom Line in Greenwich Village and began to design for established artists and up-and-comers. The Bottom Line led to touring and Sid spent approximately four years on the road with various acts from Public Enemy to Carlene Carter.

Taking a break from life on the road, Sid returned to Manhattan and freelanced around town. In 1995, she went back on the road again to act as Lighting Director for Laurie Anderson's US Bright Red Tour - that gig led to Sid going on a worldwide tour with Lou Reed as the Lighting Designer. Sid decided to end her touring days on that high note and began to romance MTV, positioned to move in to a new phase of her career.

In the late 1990's, Sid was hired to develop an in-house Art department for MTV's new flagship studio in Times Square. For many years, Sid designed the majority of the sets for studio productions as well as maintaining the studio's appearance and developing strange props on a daily basis.

Shortly after the 2004 tsunami that devastated the coast of the Indian Ocean, Sid and her partner, James Clement Cook, traveled to Sri Lanka to provide food relief and film a documentary film about a children's home. Currently, Sid is working in film as a film producer on several independent film and studio films.

[edit] Hugh Paulsen (Wing Commander)

Hugh Paulsen is a Captain in the Confederation Navy and minor character in Wing Commander IV : The Price of Freedom. He initially arrives on the TCS Lexington in order to brief Capt. William Eisen on orders from Confederation HQ. However, upon attending a briefing, Col. Blair finds that Eisen has been relieved of command for reasons unknown. It is around this time that a new Colonel appears on the ship, flying under the callsign "Seether". He is evidently given preferential treatment by Paulsen i.e he is not arrested for murdering an unarmed prisoner of war.

Later in the game, Eisen and Maj. Todd "Maniac" Marshall defect to the Union of Border Worlds. Blair and Lt. Winston "Vagabond" Chang are tasked to pursue. Blair accepts Eisen's invitation, believing that the Kilrathi War-era Confederation he fought for is not the Confederation he now serves.

Paulsen continues to command the Lexington against Blair and his compatriots. After the Lexington is disabled in the Salinas Nebula, Paulsen abandons the ship with both Seether and an unknown shuttle pilot. The depth of Paulsen's complicity with the Black Lance is revealed when he asks Seether, a subordinate officer, for guidance and orders. Seether responds by cutting his throat. Paulsen was always a subordinate, most likely turned to the Black Lance's cause by Admiral Tolwyn, with whom he shared a devotion to duty and certain perception of the Confederation.

Paulsen is a gruff and unfriendly character, but one who gives respect to those who serve him well.

NOTES

1) Although Blair MUST defect at some point (the game is unwinnable otherwise), he does not have to do it the first time he is offered. If he takes the offer up, he must fight three of the pilots from the Lexington's roster before landing on the Intrepid. If he refuses, he must fight Vagabond to the death. In this case, Blair sees more of Seether and Paulsen, even flying on Seether's wing at one point, and it is implied that Maniac cannot forgive him for killing Vagabond. If Blair refuses twice, the Lexington is destroyed and Blair either dies in the fighting or drifts forever in his ship.

2) Paulsen is played by the late Jon Spencer, best known for his role as Leo in The West Wing.

3) It is not clear whether or not Paulsen was aware of the true horror of Tolwyn's actions and plans, it is perfectly possible he was simply following orders.
