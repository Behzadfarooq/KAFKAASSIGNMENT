[edit] Gravity Lens

I was about to write this article, but now it seems redundant, but the other article needs "Expert help." What do to?

"A gravity lens is a physical phenomenon, due to the curvature of space from General Relativity General relativity. Necessary for the observation of this phenomenon, is a star of sufficient distance to have two light rays traveling towards the observer, with an occluding object of sufficient mass to bend the frames of reference so that instead of the two light rays diverging from the observer, the frames of reference cause the diverging light rays to converge upon the observer.
"Like an optical lens a gravity lens magnifies light by collecting it. Also like a optical lens, a gravity lens has a focal length, but it would be measured in light years.
See: Gravitational lensing"
