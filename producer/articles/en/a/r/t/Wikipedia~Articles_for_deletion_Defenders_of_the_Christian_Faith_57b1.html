[edit] Defenders of the Christian Faith

Defenders of the Christian Faith (edit|talk|history|links|watch|logs)

I proded this, but TruthbringerToronto removed it. This article has no sources. A yahoo search "Defenders of the Christian Faith" bring us less than 800 hits, the first being wikipedia, second a wiki mirror. Most of the hits don't seem to be about this group. For example, some refer to a group in Dorchester, Massachusetts. PatriotBible 02:07, 10 January 2007 (UTC)

  • Keep. (Removed prod. Once notable, always notable. If its leader was the basis of a character in It Can't Happen Here, then the group is notable, even through it is (or was) both unpleasant and incorrect. --TruthbringerToronto (Talk | contribs) 02:12, 10 January 2007 (UTC)
  • You selectively read. The article, unsourced, says "Winrod may have served as the prototype for the character..." Again, that concerns Gerald B. Winrod, and not his organization. PatriotBible 02:15, 10 January 2007 (UTC)
  • Delete "Defenders of the Christian Faith" contains no sources, failing WP:CITE, and it also appears to fail WP:NOTE. I can find zero g-hits that refer to this organization specifically beyond a small handful that refer to the organization as a footnote. Certainly nothing that shows notability. -- wtfunkymonkey 02:21, 10 January 2007 (UTC)
  • Comment. It's an inadequate article, but take a look at Charles Coughlin for an example of another demagogue from that era who had a lot of influence. Other references to this group can be found by searching for its name in combination with that of Gerald B. Winrod :

http://www.google.com/search?q=%22Gerald+B.+Winrod%22+%22Defenders+of+the+Christian+Faith%22 See http://ajcarchives.org/AJC_DATA/Files/1939_1940_4_YRUS.pdf (page 216) for example, which refers to Winrod and Defenders in the same sentence. (American Jewish Yearbook which covers the period from July 1, 1938 to June 30, 1939.) --TruthbringerToronto (Talk | contribs) 02:35, 10 January 2007 (UTC)

  • Delete this, and use as redirect to Fidei defensor. Grutness...wha? 02:51, 10 January 2007 (UTC)
  • Merge to Gerald B. Winrod. However I'd like to say that the number of Google hits isn't really proof of anything when we're discussing 1930s Kansas.
