[edit] Electroadhesion

If your request was declined and you want to make changes and try again, copy and paste your article into a new submission and make your changes there.


Electroadhesion relates to the electrostatic effect of astriction between two surfaces subjected to an external electric field. Examples include paper retention in plotters, electrostatic robot grippers and similar applications.

Electroadhesion is loosely divided into two areas of usage. The prehension of electrically conductive subjects in which the usual laws of capacitor theory (D = Eε) and that used with electrically insulating subjects where more advanced electrostatic theory (D = Eε + P) applies.

The European Network of Mobility Centres – ERA-MORE


The ERA-MORE (European Network of Mobility Centres) provides tailor-made assistance to researchers and their families moving from one country to another. The aim is to ease mobility within Europe and to make Europe more attractive for researchers as a place to live and to work.

More than 200 Centres in 32 countries (EU 27 plus Turkey, Iceland, Norway, Switzerland and Israel) offer quality support to solve a wide range of problems, including the most practical ones encountered by mobile researchers:

• coverage under the social system scheme in the new country • recognition of diplomas • work permits • finding an apartment, a kindergarten or school for the children, a language course,..

The Centres are open to incoming, outgoing or returning researchers independently of their nationality and employment status.

A researcher might contact the Centres directly or find an answer on the European Researcher's Mobility Portal (http://ec.europa.eu/eracareers). The European Researcher's Mobility Portal is a one-stop shop for researchers seeking to advance their careers and personal development by moving into another country. Job opportunities, funding schemes and practical information are updated regularly. The European portal is complemented by the 31 national portals that provide targeted information such as guides for researchers or FAQs.

The ERA-MORE was launched by the European Commission as one of the concrete implementations of the European Commission mobility strategy in the context of the Lisbon strategy.

[edit] University of Melbourne Cricket Club

The University of Melbourne Cricket Club (aka Uni, MUCC) was founded in 1856. It is a founding member of the Victorian Cricket Association, now Cricket Victoria.

The Club has won 3 1st XI premierships - in 1928/29, 1990/91 and 1995/96. The 1st XI has been runners up four times and participated in the finals a further 14 times.

The Club has won the Club Championship four times (1964/65, 1995/96, 1997/98 and 1998/99) and 10 premierships in other grades.

The 1st XI is currently captained by Nick Williams. The club coach is Hugh Van Cuylenberg.

As California real estate market conditions got tough, they sold the company and Stockton moved on. Later that decade, 1988, Stockton and his first wife divorced. Trying to make ends meet, Stockton started working for an automotive paint repair company called Altracolor in the early 1990s.Started working for an automotive paint repair company called Altracolor in the early 90's to make ends meet. In 1997 Stockton married his second wife, Janet Frankland, who bore his fourth child, Cameron Stockton later that year.

However, it was not until Memorial Day 2003 that Stockton began his own . Stockton and his close friend, Casey Oosterbaan, set their beers down by the BBQ for a spirited game of Cornhole, a beanbag tossing game. After building quite a thirst, they returned to the grill only to be confronted with 7 open beers. Tragically, they did not know which beer was their own. At this moment, one said to the other, “If only beers came with alarms so we could beep'em and find'em, like when you lose your car in a parking lot.” This thought was greeted with hilarious laughter and a realization: this was a gift that needed to be shared with the rest of the world. The motivation led Stockton and Oosterbaan to start RC Products, a company dedicated to the betterment of people's lives. Over a span of three years, they built the world's premier beverage locating device: the Beer Pager. Today, other life-changing products are under development at RC Products.

Medieval children’s Game called Duck on a Rock was played with a good size stone (the drake) placed upon a very large one or a tree stump. One boy stayed near it to guard it. The others threw stones (ducks) at it in an attempt to knock it off. Once it was knocked off, the throwers all rushed to retrieve their ducks. If one of them was tagged before returning to the throwing line with his duck, he became the guard. The guard could not tag anyone until he had picked up his duck at his feet, nor could he chase anyone till he, the guard, had replaced the drake upon the rock or stump. Resent findings believe basketball was baste on this game.

[edit] Bishop William J. Philbin

The Most Reverend William J. Philbin D.D. was a Roman Catholic Bishop of The Diocese of Down and Connor. He was Born in Kiltimagh, Ireland on the 26 January 1907.

[edit] Ministry

He was Ordained a Priest 21 June 1931. In 1953 he was appointed Bishop of Clonfert, Ireland. He was then appointed Bishop of The Diocese of Down and Connor in 1962. He retired as Bishop in 1982 and his successor was Cahal Daly. He died on the 23 August 1991.

[edit] The Jockey Club Scholars Alumni Association

This request for creation has been reviewed. The result is below.
If your request was declined and you want to make changes and try again, copy and paste your article into a new submission and make your changes there.

The Jockey Club Scholars Alumni Association Ltd. was registered in November 2004. It is a non-profit organization comprised of recipients of The Hong Kong Jockey Club Scholarship with an objective to contribute to the society through a network of young elites. The scholarship was established in 1998 with a grant of HK$102 million by The Hong Kong Jockey Club Charities Trust for a ten-year period. It provides approximately 26 scholarships each year for local and mainland students to pursue three-year, first-degree courses at eight universities in Hong Kong as well as the Academy of Performing Arts. Scholars are chosen by the tertiary institutions to which they have applied on the basis of outstanding academic ability and talents, and interest in community service. Each scholar receives about HK$290,000 to cover tuition, academic expenses and living costs during the three years. Renewal of the scholarship is dependent on the level of the students' performance.

Recipients of the scholarship automatically become members of the Alumni. As at 4 October 2005, the Alumni have a total of 172 members. After a two-year pilot project funded by the Trust to develop the scholars’ community spirit, the Alumni is being registered as a non-profit organization and going to apply the recognition of charity status. The Alumni is committed to serving the society, extending the community spirit of The Hong Kong Jockey Club.

[edit] Plot

An outlaw band flees a posse and rides into Refuge, a small town where no one carries a gun, drinks, or swears. The town is actually Purgatory, and the peaceful inhabitants are all famous dead outlaws and criminals such as Doc Holiday and Wild Bill Hickok who must redeem themselves before gaining admittance to Heaven...or screw up and go to Hell. The residents must either defend themselves against the outlaws and risk eternal damnation... or die a second time.
