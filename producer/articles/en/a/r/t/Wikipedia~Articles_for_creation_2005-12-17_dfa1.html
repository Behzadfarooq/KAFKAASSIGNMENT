[edit] Léon-Bernard Giot

Léon-Bernard Giot (March 4, 1956) is a Belgian baritone singer, organist and conductor. Born Léon-Bernard Mathilde Giot, he is usually known as Bernardo Giotto.

Giot was born in Ghent in the Department of East Flanders ( Belgium ) and showed talent for the piano from his early childhood. He founded several choirs and orchestras at the age of 15. Three years later he was sent, on a year-long study to St Niklaas with the virtuoso soprano Marie-Thérèse Maesen. Giot's first notable performances, date from this time. After his return home he made a concert tour. A concert in Brussels brought the young man overnight fame in the whole of Europe.

In 1975, Giot got a job as breathing coach at the Belgian Royal Federation of Music, where he stayed until 2005. He performed as baritone solo and at the Choir of the European Community in most of the European countries.

Giot worked as conductor of choirs and orchestras he founded in Ghent, Brussels and Luxemburg and he founded and conducted the biggest international choir meeting : The Annuel Christmas-Choir-Festival in Ghent.

Léon-Bernard Giot was a prolific breathing and singing teacher whose list of pupils amounts to over 2500 persons, man and women, boys and girls. Some of them became famous singers too. Better known today, however, are his manuscripts about his methods for a natural breathing to protect the voice as much as possible, all written for his students, which have established a secure place in singers environment.

Though his lack of time, as he played the organ and sang in more than 12 different churches all over the country, Giot founded The International School for Singing and Breathing in Melle near Ghent. He was greatly admired as he was the youngest conductor and the youngest organ player of the whole country at the age of 15.

Giot was a noted baritone, and invented a unique singers diction adapted to the natural breathing. He discouvered the exact difference between speaking and singing : the natural vibrato. He was also a significant conductor, being one of the first to refuse any recording of his concerts.

Dr. Drew Pinkskey's book Cracked is a fictional account based on the lives of past addict patients seen through drug rehab at [Las Encinas Medical Center] in Southern California.

Dr. Pinskey, the host of the popular radio show loveline has dedicated his life to improving the lives of addicts and people in need of all ages. Through his book, he was able to show common challenges addicts face going through drug rehabilitation.
