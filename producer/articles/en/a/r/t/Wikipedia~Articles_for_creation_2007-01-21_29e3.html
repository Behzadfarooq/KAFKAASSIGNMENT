Steven Erich Hubbard was born on July 29th, 1989 in Farmington Hills, Michigan. Born to Brian R. Johnstone (father) and to Tracy J Hubbard (Mother). During Steven Erich Hubbard's early years, he was interested in such hobbies as Computer Science, Mathematics and Analytical Theory. Steven Hubbard has contributed to both business and mathematics at the early age of sixteen. He has also built his own corporations and has been known for his extensive knowledge of tax law.

[edit] Early Life

Steven Hubbard was born into a middle-income family in the outskirts of Farmington Hills. During the early 1990's, Steven Hubbard suffered burns onto his hands due to an accident involving a kitchen stove. At the age of five, Steven Hubbard has always been interested in mechanical and electrical operations of specific equipment. While only having two friends, Craig Cowing and Robert Cowing, he was a lonely child due to his primary being out in the outskirts of Plymouth after the move from the urban parts of Plymouth. In 1998, he received his own computer running Windows 98 and began experimenting with TCP/IP Protocol and basic WindowsTM commands.

[edit] The Elementary Years

Steven Hubbard began schooling in 1995 in Isbister Elementary School. He was first perceived as an IEP special education student due to his shyness and overall general disinterest in people. In the 1st, 2nd, 3rd grades, he was influenced by many childhood actions, and became disinterested in both school and life. However, in 1998 to 1999, he became more interested in the world around and his grades increased greatly over the next three years. He was accredited for his accomplishments during these years and was a speaker during his elementary school graduation.

[edit] The Middle School Years

Steven Hubbard began his middle school education in June 1999 at West Middle School, located in Plymouth, MI. During the sixth grade, he became interested in the general sciences which include Physics and Mathematics. However during both the seventh and eighth grades, he became ill with a condition known as Tachycardia. He became ill, missing 1/3 of the semester which would later affect him in his high school years. Steven Hubbard also gained more support of teachers and friends during this period than in the Middle School Years. Receiving average grades during this period, Steven Hubbard accelerated himself both academically and emotionally and in the eight grade, has achieved the honor roll status six times from seventh to eight grade.

[edit] The High School Years

Steven Hubbard began his high school education in June 2004 at Plymouth High School. During the ninth and tenth grades, he experienced both an intellectual and business revolution which will further lead to the development of two LLC's and his present Corporation. Known for his scholarly attitude toward education, he achieved a very high honor roll status at the tenth grade. Currently in the eleventh grade, dramatic events occurring in both is family life caused himself to slide into a depression, his overall academic caliber decreased. During this time period, Steven Hubbard becomes interested in both Calculus and Number Theory.

[edit] The Corporate Formation

Steven Hubbard during the early years, started both Vendalo Ahora! LLC and Sell it Now. Sell it Now was his first company, formed as a sole proprietorship. However, the business quickly failed due to the lack of capital provided and later formed Vendalo Ahora! LLC and Pizazz Internet Services, Inc.. Later formed as a small family business, sold goods and services out to the general public. From July 2006 to the later end of December 2006, the company performed drop shipping services out on Ebay, however, suffering lack of capital and legal issues. Pizazz Internet Services provided domains, hosting, e-mail and SSL certificates. Steven Hubbard later dissolved the company and began his present company, S & H Enterprises Corp. Currently for the 2006 fiscal year, his annual gains reached $14,000 dollars.

[edit] Mari Batista/Mar√≠a Elena Batista

This request for creation has been reviewed. The result is below.


Mar√≠a Elena "Mari" Batista currently is Puerto Rico's most notable sports administrator. As the city of San Juan, Puerto Rico's Director of Sports and Recreation, she helped revive the previously underused and dilapidated Hiram Bithorn Stadium, attracting MLB's Opening Day Game in 2001, twenty-two yearly home games of the now defunct Montreal Expos (now known as the Washington Nationals) in 2003 and 2004 and the 2006 Baseball World Classic games. In the summer of 2006 she brought NBA's "Basketball without Borders" program to San Juan. In September 2006, Mayor Jorge Santini inaugurated the San Juan Municipal Sports Magnet School. In December, another of her projects, the $28 million world-class San Juan Natatorium opened to the public and attracted winter training from over 10 stateside college swim teams, including the West Point Military Academy. What is most notable of this particular Batista accomplishment is her success in getting the mayor and the city council to make this major investment even though there was no major sports event over the horizon that required such a facility. In early 2007, the first phase of San Juan's Golf complex, a reuse of the city's former sanitary landfill, opens to the public.

Prior to her career as a sports administrator, she was a well-known competitive swimmer, having represented Puerto Rico in several Central American and Caribbean Games, PanAm Games and the 1988 Olympics in Seoul, South Korea. After graduating from Puerto Rico's Sacred Heart University in 1994, she served as the Sports Director of Puerto Rico's Department of Education Sports Magnet School, obtained an MBA and began teaching business at Turabo University in Caguas, Puerto Rico. She is the daughter of former Superior Court Judge Elpidio Batista, who served a stint as President of the Puerto Rico Winter Baseball League, is married to Senate President Kenneth McClintock and is the mother of two children.

The rape of Bianca Montgomery was a controversial storyline in the popular soap opera All My Children. On July 8th, 2003 teenage Bianca was raped by the soap's villian Michael Cambias. Shortly thereafter, Michael turned up dead and another storyline about his murder began. Eventually, it was revealed that Bianca herself killed Michael in self defense. Biance ended up pregnant as a result of the rape, which spawned into All My Children's drawn out baby switch storyline.
Although the storyline brought high ratings and earned Bianca's portrayer Eden Riegel a daytime emmy, it was extremely controversial. Bianca was one of the first open lesbians on daytime television, and before the rape, was paired with another woman named Lena. The couple was surprisingly popular. They were named among top couples in Soap Opera Digest polls for many weeks in a row. Despite their many fans, Lena and Bianca exchanged little affection onscreen. The duo shared only one kiss before the rape storyline, and although they spent the night together, it was only shown through a "morning after" scene.
All My Children was criticized for sending the message to viewers that two woman in a consenting relationship was too much for television, but a violent act like rape was okay. Fans also complained that AMC was using the rape as a tool to disrupt Bianca and Lena's relationship. The rape would force Bianca not to indulge in affection or sex with Lena for many months.
Head writer Megan McTavish defended the accusations by saying Bianca's rape was because she was beloved by all of Michael's enemies, not because of her sexual orientation. However, fans claimed she was not addressing the real issue. Many fans believed that even if Bianca was not raped because she was gay, the storyline was still unintentionally homophobic because Bianca was allowed to be raped, but barley allowed to share affection with Lena.

[edit] Dan Besse

Dan Besse is a City Council Member for the City of Winston-Salem, North Carolina. He represents the Southwest Ward and is a Democrat. He has a website for that work at http://www.danbesse.org. He is running for Lieutenant Governor of North Carolina in 2008. He has a web site for that effort at http://www.DanBesse2008.org.

Besse was first elected to the Winston-Salem Board of Aldermen in 2001. During his first term, he and other members voted to change the name of the body from the Board of Aldermen to the City Council. Facing a Republican challenger in the 2005 election, Besse was re-elected overwhelmingly with 79% of the vote to his opponent's 21%.

In addition to representing his constituents directly on the Council, Besse does his part to carry out the other work of the City Council by representing the City of Winston-Salem on regional groups including the Piedmont Triad Partnership (the Triad's leading regional economic development organization), the Piedmont Triad Early Action Compact (the oversight group for the regional clean air plan), and the Winston-Salem Urban Area Transportation Advisory Committee.

Besse has served the state as a member of planning and regulatory bodies as well as those which are more focused on planning. These include the:

  1. N.C. Climate Action Plan Advisory Group (2006-present);
  2. N.C. Environmental Management Commission (1993-2005);
  3. N.C. Sedimentation Control Commission (1994-2002);
  4. N.C. Coastal Resources Commission (1985-1993)(Chair 1985-1990);
  5. N.C. Emergency Response Commission (1987-1992).

Besse is an alumnus of the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill, having taken his B.A there in 1977 and his J.D. there in 1980. He also became a licensed member of the North Carolina State Bar in 1980. He worked as a legal services lawyer, representing poor families in civil cases in eastern North Carolina for a decade. Besse is now in private practice. He has spent nearly two decades working with leading state environmental conservation groups, sometimes as a volunteer, sometimes as an employee, and sometimes as a part of his law practice.

Besse has worked as a teacher of environmental law and policy and general political science as an adjunct or visiting faculty member at N.C. State University, Guilford College, Duke University, Winston-Salem State University, and Forsyth Technical Community College.

Besse lives in Winston-Salem, was born on December 28th, 1954, and has been married to Kay House since 1977. Besse is a member of the Unitarian-Universalist Fellowship of Winston-Salem and enjoys running and reading as hobbies.

[edit] Hector O'Neill

Hector O'Neill has served as mayor of the city of Guaynabo, Puerto Rico for the past thirteen years. Elected in 1993 in a special election after the death of Alejandro "Junior" Cruz, he has been reelected in 1996, 2000 and 2004 by ever wider victory margins. As mayor, he has rebuilt the city's downtown area and attracted Univision's Puerto Rico operations to headquarter there, including the Univision television affiliate, AM and FM radio stations, their Telefutura TV station and their music company. He developed Puerto Rico's first, and so far only, municipal expressway, which gives access to Guaynabo's rural communities and is developing the island's first municipal forest, with land holdings which already exceed a square mile.

During the 1990's, he headed the influential Puerto Rico's Mayors Federation and remains one of the most outspoken supporters of municipal autonomy.

In 2001, he was named Person of the Year by Caribbean Business weekly business newspaper, which rarely selects an elected official for such an honor.

Prior to becoming mayor, O'Neill served as a municipal assemblyman in Guaynabo, a legislative aide and a senator. Elected to the Senate in 1988, he was reelected in 1992 and served in 1993 as chairman of its Nominations Committee.

He is married to the former Alba Alvelo and is the father of eight sons.

[edit] Feminist perspective on the role of education in society

Until the late 1980's, girls, on average, did worse in education than boys. However, girls now outperform boys, in most levels of education. Feminists encouraged equal opportunities in schools. The changes encouraged by the feminists are thought to affect the image and ambitions of girls, which may improve their educational achievement.

However, some feminist researchers are still concerned that some girls are still underachieving because of disruptive boys in the classroom. They argue that teachers may be so tied up with controlling these boys, that girls don't get the attention they deserve. Many feminist researchers are also concerned about the narrow subject choices that girls make at higher education levels. Girls are still more likely to take subjects relating to the arts, whereas boys are more likely to take science-related subject

Finally, some feminists think that the current concern about boys underachieving is simply a 'moral panic', and it reflects anti-female sentiments and a patriarchal society. Feminists think that girls should just be praised for this change in achievement, rather than everyone worrying that boys might be failing, and what this might mean.
