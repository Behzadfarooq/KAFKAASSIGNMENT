[edit] Roles of Women in Western Literature

In traditional English literature, drawing on Judeo-Christian mythology, there were three "roles" for women, and every female character was a variation of one of them.

Eve - the temptress, the seductress. She is beautiful and alluring and ultimately the failing of man, or at least the main character.

Joan of Arc - the warrior. She is brave, inspired by God, a virgin, on a mission. She doesn't take a husband or boyfriend. She usually ends poorly (Joan of Arc was burned at the stake)

The Virgin Mary - the virgin, the mother. Completely asexual, she is nurturing and loving and innocent.

[edit] K.T.B. Menon

Dr. K.T.B Menon was one of the most successful Indian businessmen in Kuwait until he passed away in 1992 at Bangalore, India. As a young man, 'KTB' as he was popularily known, served with the British Army before being wounded in Basra and moved to Kuwait in 1948. He decided to stay on in Kuwait in search of better opportunities in the developing oil rich country and was soon working for the Kuwait Oil Company. Later he joined a family business owned by the Al Gharabally family. A young Abdulla Gharabally and KTB Menon decided to venture out on their own and soon established the ASH Al Gharabally Co. in 1958. The enterprising KTB Menon, soon recognised the growing need for electricity and water in Kuwait and quickly brought in Japanese giants like Mitsubishi Heavy Industries into Kuwait to build the infrastructure. With his excellent relationships in the Kuwait Government he was soon awarded several contracts to build power plants and water desalination plants. By the mid 1960's he had already established himself as a prominent Indian businessman in Kuwait. KTB was well known for helping find employment opportunities, loans, capital etc. to several Indians in Kuwait. In 1978, he was awarded a doctorate in humane letters in the USA for his outstanding contributions to society. His charismatic personality and generous nature won him many friends internationaly. He had a great passion for horse racing and owned several horses with the likes of Dr. M.A.M. Ramaswamy and Dr. Vijay Mallya with whom he had substantial business interests.

[edit] Illegal opcode

An Illegal Opcode, also called an Undocumented Instruction, is an instruction to a CPU that is not mentioned in any official documentation released by the CPU's designer or manufacturer, which nevertheless has an effect. Illegal opcodes were common on older CPUs designed during the 1970s or earlier, such as the MOS Technology 6502 and the Zilog Z80. They exist as a side-effect of the wiring of transistors in the CPU, and usually combine functions of the CPU that were not inteded to be combined. While most illegal instructions have useless or even highly undesirable effects (such as crashing the computer), a few might by accident do something that can be useful in certain situations. Such instructions were sometimes exploited in computer games of the 1970s and 1980s to speed up certain time-critical sections. Another common use of them was in the ongoing battle between copy protection implementations and cracking. Here, they were a form of security through obscurity, and their secrecy usually didn't last very long.

A danger associated with the use of illegal instructions was that, given the fact that the manufacturer didn't guarantee their existence and function, they might disappear or behave differently with any change of the CPU internals or any new revision of the CPU, rendering programs that use them incompatible with the newer revisions. For example, a number of older Apple II games don't work correctly on the newer Apple IIc, because the latter uses a newer CPU revision that does away with illegal opcodes.

Modern CPUs, such as the i386 and its decendants or the Power PC processor, usually don't have illegal opcodes any more. Their manufacturers guarantee that the CPU will behave in a well-defined way when it finds an unknown opcode in the instruction stream: usually, this means triggering a certain exception or fault condition. The operating system's exception or fault handler will then usually terminate the application that caused the fault. Another, less common way of handling illegal instructions is by defining them to do nothing (equivalent to the CPU's official NOP instruction).

Today, the details of these instructions are mainly of interest for exact emulation of older systems.

[edit] Tatyana Alexeyevna Afanasyeva

Tatyana Alexeyevna Afanasyeva (Kiev, November 19, 1876 Leiden, April 14, 1964) was a Russian /Dutch mathematician. On December 21, 1904 she was married to Paul Ehrenfest an Austrian physicist and a mathematician, (18801933). They had 2 daughters and 2 sons.

Afanasyeva was born in Kiev in the Ukraine. After her father died she was brought up by an uncle in St Petersburg, Russia, where she attended a women's pedagogical school and a Women's College. In 1902 she transferred to Gttingen, where she met Ehrenfest. The couple got married in 1904. She collaborated closely with her husband, more famously on their classical review of the statistical mechanics of Boltzmann ^ . She published many papers on various topics such as randomness [5] and entropy, and teaching pedagogy ^ .

[edit] Storck Brewery

Storck Brewery was located in Slinger, Wisconsin - formerly Schlesingerville. Founded in the late 1800's, it went through a series of owners. Prohibition made it land upon hard times, but they got through by making ice cream and selling milk. However, like many other breweries, they still sold beer on the side and were ultimately raided by the Feds, resulting in a grusome murder of one of the workers. After prohibition, they resumed their brewing but ultimately could no longer meet demand and shut down in the early 1960's. The building now stands in ruins by the Slinger railroad tracks. There is a beautiful painting of the Storck on the side that you can still see to this day.

The remains of the Storck Brewery have since been demolished, as of Summer 2006. The site is being used as an auto graveyard, and except for a pile of bricks, there is nothing that remains but the glittering memories of what used to be the best brew haus in the State of Wisconsin.

[edit] Lorenzo Magnani

Lorenzo Magnani teaches Logic and Epistemology in the Department of Philosophy at the University of Pavia, Italy, where he directs the Computational Philosophy Laboratory. He is visiting professor at the Sun Yat-sen University, Canton (Guangzhou), China. He recently was Weissman Distinguished Visiting Professor at The New York City University (Baruch College, NYC) (2003), and Visiting Pro-fessor at Georgia Institute of Technology, Atlanta (1999-2003). His primary research interests are in the area of philosophy of science, logic, and artificial intelligence. Currently he is studying the proc-esses of conceptual innovation and change in science. A major objective of his research is to create a working synthesis between epistemological/historical perspectives and investigations of representa-tions and reasoning practices carried out in the sciences of cognition. His historical research centered on 19th and 20th century geometry and philosophy of geometry. Visiting researcher at the Universities of Pittsburgh (CMU, 1992), Montreal (McGill, 1992, 1993), Waterloo (Philosophy Department, 1993) and Atlanta (Georgia Tech, 1995, 1998, 1999), Visiting Professor of Philosophy of Science and Theo-ries of Ethics at Georgia Institute of Technology, Atlanta (1999-), and Weissman Distinguished Visit-ing Professor at Baruch College, The City University of New York, NTYC (2003). [edit] Reintegracionismo or Reintegracionism

The reintegracionismo or reintegracionism movement is a lingustic view from Galicia, Spain, which aims at the reincorporation of the Galician in the Portuguese.
Pressently, there are two views in Galicia about the Galician language:

  1. The isolacionist considers Galician and Portuguese as two distinct languages. Therefore it adopts differenciated spelling and writting rules, more similar to Spanish. That's the view of a majoraty of public organizations of Galicia and the view supported by the Spain central governement.
  2. The reintegracionist considers Galician as one more variety of the Portuguese linguistic system. For this reason it supports the using of spelling rules similar to the ones used in the portuguese speaking countries.

Broader view: The reintegracionismo is a cultural, besides linguistic attitude, made by people who believe that the galician dialects (or Galician rightfully belong to the countries of the Lusofonia and their language is in fact a variant of the world known as Portuguese. Galician therefore is part of the same linguistic diassystem, which presently as two norms, the one from Portugal and PALOPs (portuguese speaking african countries), and another from Brazil, all former collonies of Portugal. According to some of them, a third norm, a galician one should be added, the norm AGAL. Others consider that simply standard portuguese should be used, being that also the end goal of some who now defend AGAL but just as a provivional transition norm to ease it's aceptation by the public and the press. Consequently, the reintegracionists think that the NOMIGa (Normas Ortogrficas e Morfolxicas do Idioma Galego) arbitred by the RAG (Real Academia Galega) and ILG (Instituto da Lingua Galega) are innadequate, as they consider definitive the contamination by spanish of the galician accents. TThe last reform of the ILG-RAG norms, done in 2003, accepts that conception and conserves the most proeminent spanish charactheristisc namelly, the spanish , instead of the portuguese NH, the spanish LL, instead of LH and the spanish endings -era, -ble, -cin. Also, the lexical choices tend to be in aggreement with the ones used in Spanish. As a transition norm, AGAL (Associaom Galega da Lngua) and other groups use a norm galaico-portuguese very similar to the standard portuguese: NH, LH, and the endings -aria, -vel as in portuguese but also the termination -om, isntead of the portuguese -o or the spanis -cin. Other groups, such as MDL (Movimento Defesa da Lngua), use the AGAL and the standard portuguese norm conspicuously, while the AAG-P (Associao de Amizade Galiza-Portugal) and the IFG-P (Irmandades da Fala da Galiza e Portugal) opt for the common norm for portuguese decided in the Lisbon aggreement of 1990. They are not the only groups who use these "reintegrated" norms, being that their more wide use has come to be recognized recently as acceptable in court.
