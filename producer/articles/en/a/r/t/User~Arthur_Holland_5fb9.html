Photo of some penguins. I don't need a reason.

I imagine the Wikipedia community, and indeed all users of the internet, will be thrilled to learn that I am 32, live in South London, am of Anglo-Dutch descent (with a dash of Scottish and Austrian thrown in for good measure), and am not actually called Arthur. Bet you can't guess what I'm really called.

I will at some stage write a proper user page. For now I shall make do with a list of Good Things and Bad Things.

[edit] Good Things

  • P. G. Wodehouse
  • Douglas Adams
  • Mayonnaise
  • Krokets Yum. Especially with mayonnaise.
  • Skiing
  • New Zealand
  • The West Wing
  • Broadband internet access. I've only recently made the leap from dial-up, and I can happily report that it's a Very Good Thing Indeed.
  • Pork scratchings (sorry).
  • Wayne Rooney (see Bad Things).
  • Mariella Frostrup's voice.

[edit] Bad Things

  • Litter bugs. Don't do it kids!
  • People who fail to understand that the left-hand side of the London Underground escalators are for people who want to keep walking.
  • 80s TV theme tunes as mobile ring tones.
  • Salad cream
  • The rather improbable levels of excitement reached by people in adverts for such products as toilet roll, lavatory cleaners, yoghurt, and life insurance.
  • Cutting edge fashion. You know what I'm talking about. Yes, you with the Lonsdale boots and the camo pants and the Hoxton fin.
  • People who haven't grasped the difference between "there" and "their". And in extreme cases, "they're".
  • Wayne Rooney (see Good Things).
  • Brian Sewell's voice.
