[edit] Lionheart (1987 film)

Lionheart is a 1987 film loosely based on the historical Children's Crusade. Directed by Academy Award winner Franklin J. Schaffner, the story follows an exhiled young knight, played by Eric Stoltz, who leads a band of orphans to join the Crusade of Richard the Lionheart while protecting the children from the Black Prince Gabriel Byrne, a disillusioned crusader turned child slave trader. Francis Ford Coppola, who had initially planned to direct the film, is credited as Executive Producer. The screenplay was written by Menno Meyjes and Richard Outten from a story by Menno Meyjes. Oscar-winning composer Jerry Goldsmith wrote the memorable score.

[edit] Haddadin

Haddadin (or Haddadeen) is the name of an Arab Christian tribe originally from the southern part of greater Syria (what is known now as the Hashemite Kingdom of Jordan). The tribe claims to originate from Yemen. Elders of the tribe claim that the name is related to the city of Al-Hudaydah in Yemen, where the tribe comes from. Some claim that the origin is related to the root Haddad which means Judge in classical Arabic. For many centuries the tribe lived in Shobak, and then Kerak. Some historic sources indicate that the first Arab resistance against the first Crusades came from the Haddadin tribe. Late 19th century part of the tribe moved from Kerak a city in southern Syria to Ma'in a city in the land of Balqa (middle of Jordan). Many Arab Christians in Jordan, Palestine, Syria, and Lebanon claim to be originated from the Haddadin tribe. Palestinians originating from the city of Ramallah and Syrians originating from the city of Safita claim to be originating from this tribe. The tribe belonged to the Greek Orthodox Church until about 1900 when one of its leaders converted to join the Greek Catholic Church. The tribe was active in the public life of modern Jordan. Jeries Haddadin, served as chief judge in the courts of Kerak, Amman, Jerusalem when the city was under Jordanian administration, and many other cities in the country. He also served as a member of the second government lead by late PM Wasfi Tel as a minister of Agriculture and Justice. Munther Haddadin, a member of the government lead by former PM Abdessalam Al-Majali as a minister of Water and Irrigation. Bassam Haddadin, Khalil Haddadin , and Saad Haddadin Members of Parliamant. Haitham Haddadin, Naji Haddadin Jeries Haddadin, Jamil Haddadin Judges.

[edit] Jewbu

Jewbu (somtimes spelled Jewboo) is a portmanteau word that combines "Jew" and "Buddhist." It is not considered pejorative or politically incorrect. The official definition is, "a Buddhist of Jewish descent who wishes to form a synthesis or reconciliation between their newfound Buddhist spirituality and the Jewish tradition in which they were raised." [edit] Modality Effect

The modality effect refers to the process by which the brain exhibits an increased memory capabilities when utilizing multiple sensory modes.

This applies to all sensory modes however the current empirical research has focused solely on the Visual and Auditory modalities. These studies they found that during the mind would have enhanced recall when a presentation included both auditory and visual sensory modes Vs. Visual only.

[edit] Jorge Godoy

Admiral Jorge Godoy is the current Chief of Staff of the Argentine Navy

Naval Career Admiral JORGE OMAR GODOY was born on 17th January, 1946 in the city of Mar del Plata. He graduated from the MILITARY NAVAL SCHOOL as Midshipman in December, 1968.

As Line Officer (Surface Warfare), he performed services on board various Navy units, such as: the Oceanographic Ship A.R.A. “CAPITAN CANEPA” (1969), Destroyer A.R.A. “SANTA CRUZ” (1970), Tanker A.R.A. “PUNTA MEDANOS” (1973), Aviso A.R.A. “SOMELLERA” (1974/75), Destroyer A.R.A. “SEGUI” (1976/77), Frigate A.R.A. “LIBERTAD” (1978), Aircraft Carrier A.R.A. “25 DE MAYO” (1979), Buoyance Ship A.R.A. “SAN JULIAN” (1981), Destroyers “DOMECQ GARCIA” (1982) and A.R.A. “LA ARGENTINA” (1982/84), Transport Ship A.R.A. “BAHIA SAN BLAS” (1986) and Corvette A.R.A. “PARKER” (1991).

In 1972, he attended the NAVY OFFICER SCHOOL specializing in Propulsion, and later took courses in Engines and Operations.

His shore assignments included service at MAR DEL PLATA NAVAL BASE in the rank of Ensign, as Secretary to the Commander, Submarine Force; at PUERTO BELGRANO NAVAL BASE as Lieutenant, at the Engines Service; at the COMMAND OF NAVAL OPERATIONS in the ranks of Lieutenant Commander and Commander, as Chief, Surface Operations and Electronic Warfare Division from 1987 to 1989, and as Captain, in the capacity of Chief, Logistics Department in 1995; and at the NAVAL TRANSPORT COMMAND as Executive Officer in 1990. In 1996 and 1997, he was appointed Argentine Navy Delegate to the HONORABLE NATIONAL CONGRESS. In 1999, he served as Chief, ARGENTINE NAVY MAINTENANCE AND YARDS. In the rank of Rear Admiral, he performed duties as Director, HIGH TECHNOLOGY OFFICE in 2000, and as Assistant Secretary of Institutional Relations, NAVY GENERAL SECRETARIAT in 2001 and 2002.

In 1982, he joined the first staff of the Destroyer A.R.A. “LA ARGENTINA” as Chief, Engines Department while the vessel was under construction and during acceptance tests at the Blohm + Voss Shipyard in Hamburg, Germany. He continued serving as a staff member in 1983 and 1984, with the ship already commissioned in the Sea Fleet.

In 1985, he attended the Command and Staff course and in 1990, the Direction and Management course at the NAVAL WAR COLLEGE. In 1987, he completed the Joint Military Planning Course.

In 1989, he was awarded a Doctor's degree in Political Science from the Kennedy University. In 1990, he took a specialization course in Naval Management. Vice admiral Godoy is also a university graduate in Naval Systems.

He commanded the Buoyance Ship A.R.A “SAN JULIAN” in the rank of Lieutenant in 1981; the Transport Ship A.R.A. “BAHIA SAN BLAS” as Lieutenant Commander in 1986; the Corvette A.R.A. “PARKER” as Commander in 1991, and the Frigate A.R.A. “LIBERTAD” as Captain in 1998.

In 1992 and 1993, he served as Chief of the Engines Department, ARGENTINE NAVAL COMMISSION IN EUROPE, Bonn, Germany.

He was promoted to the rank of Captain by the end of 1993, and in 1994 he attended the advanced course on Strategy for the Armed Forces.

On 3rd February, 1998 he was appointed Commanding Officer of the Training Ship Frigate A.R.A. “LIBERTAD” and received a distinction from the Association “Personalities of my City", in the city of Mar del Plata.

He was promoted to the rank of Rear Admiral by the end of 1999 and assumed the position of Director, High Technology Office.

In 2000, he was awarded the Meritorious Service Medal “Almirante Joaquim Marques Lisboa, Marqués de Tamandaré” by the Navy of Brazil.

In 2001, he was appointed Assistant Secretary of Institutional Relations, NAVY GENERAL SECRETARIAT.

He assumed as Commander, SOUTHERN NAVAL AREA and Commander, “ALMIRANTE BERISSO” NAVAL BASE, USHUAIA, on 16th December, 2002.

In 2003 the President of the Federative Republic of Brazil decorated him for NAVAL MERIT, in the degree of "GRAND OFFICER".

He was promoted to the rank of Vice admiral on 27 th may, 2003. Later on he was promoted to Admiral.

On 17th September, 2003 he was awarded the “ORDER TO NAVAL MERIT” in the degree of “COMMANDER” by the Commander-in-Chief of Chilean Navy, Admiral Miguel Angel Vergara Villalobos. He also holds the Legion of Merit, awarded by the United States.

Admiral GODOY is a Naval Officer who has sailed for more than 20 years across the South Atlantic seas, the southern maritime coasts, and the Chilean, Uruguayan and Brazilian coasts.

He is married to former María Laura Valdivia Lúpiz and they have two children.

[edit] Newism: A New Art Movement

Newism Ok, this “Art Movement is the most significant of the end of the 20th Century”, Max Doerner cited and predicted it in his book of 1933 “The Materials of the Artist and Their Use in Painting” ( the use of Synthetic Resins as mediums for artists in the future…). This Art Movement it is called: NEWISM. This Movement is a result of the use of a new and a revolutionary medium called Plastic Paint Medium, which was created by a Cuban Contemporary Artist: Josignacio in 1984 in Havana, Cuba. This technique consist in the use of epoxy resins as agglutinating medium and pigments as colorants, obtaining as a result, a real plastic finish with a totally new visual effect called for the first time in 1985 Newism by Cuban Art critic Leonel Lopez-Nussa and has been followed by the critic and art students all over.

In 1996, in the Museum of Art of Fort Lauderdale, Florida, USA, one of Josignacio’s artworks : “3 O’clock”, was awarded with The Best in Show price at the Hortt 38 Juried Art Show, the oldest and most prestigious annual show of Florida. Former director of Museum of Art, Dr. Kenworth W. Moffett called this technique: "This new technique is the result of the marriage of art and technology", and renowned Cuban intellectual Dr.Jose A. Portuondo said: “Josignacio is the artistic representative of the Plastic Era”,

“This Plastic Paint Medium is a real revolution in the painters technique field, and the new visual effect that it is derived is, no doubt about it, A new and a very important School, named New Ism, Plastic Ism or whatever you want to name it, it does not matter…..”. Dr. Roberto J. Cayuso (art critic and publisher).

In early 80’s in Connecticut and New York also appears a group of painters called “The New New Painters”, which began to work with a new kind of experimental new acrylic painting, obtaining similar visual effects to the ones of Josignacio. Today there is a place located in Toronto's Queen West Gallery District, the Museum of New New Painting and this is the first centre devoted to the exhibition and study of this controversial art movement. The aim of the museum is to create a space that is open to all, where the New New painters can be studied and discussed. Original pieces are on view, and a library of books and catalogues has been assembled, documenting the history of the movement.

The New New Painters group are: Lucy Baker, Steve Brent, Joseph Drapell, John Gittins, Thomas Fertig, Roy Lerner, Gruters, Marjorie Minkin, Graham Peacock, Irene Neal, Bruce Piermarini, Jerald Webster.

Please take notice that this could be called the biggest change in the medium of Western Painting since oil replaced tempera in the early 16th century, most of the technical innovations have been made by the American paint maker Sam Golden, and his son, Mark.

1-Key dates when the art movement was active: The NEWISM was born in 1984. 2-Geographical information: La Habana, Cuba in 1984. 3- Characteristics for which the art movement is best known: The use of innovative painting mediums such as Plastic Paint Medium, a new real visual effect. 4-Key artists linked with the movement: Josignacio, Lucy Baker, Graham Peacock, Roy Lerner, Joseph Drapell 5- Other relevant information: This also has been a movement in North America and Canada since the early 80’s called The New New Pinters.

[edit] Walt Disney Classics

[edit] Sources

Walt Disney Classics is a series of videos created in 1984 by Walt Disney Home Entertainment to release animated Disney features to video because of the large demand at the time.

The released 22 animated Disney features to video from 1984 to 1994 (up to 1998 in Europe and Japan), starting with Dumbo in October 1984. The line officially started in the {United Kingdom|U.K]] in July 1985 with this release and the video premiere of Pinocchio, which was also out in the US and Canada.

Disney's first four video releases (Dumbo, Pinocchio, Robin Hood, and The Sword in the Stone) were packaged in black padded clamshells, but in 1986, they switched to slide-in cases to reduce the costs.

The line was unofficially called off in 1994 and picked up by the Walt Disney Masterpiece Collection.

[edit] [New Orleans Blaze Women's Tackle Football Team]

Who Are the New Orleans Blaze?

Well, the Blaze is a women's full-contact, semi-professional football team; one of the first in Louisiana. The Blaze is a licensed team with the National Women's Football Association (NWFA). The league was formed in 2000 by Catherine Masters, CEO/President, in Nashville, TN. The league started with two teams, and in the 2006 season there will be 40 teams across the country. Also in the 2006 season, the leagur will be looking forward to fulfilling arrangements with The Football Network, Google Videos, and some motion picture deals in Hollywood. The 2005 Championship Game drew over 5,000 fans to the University of Louisville Venue. The Blaze are proud to have been in the play-offs for their 2005 efforts.

The team was featured by the league as most improved franchise in 2004, when the new owners took control of the team. The team has been featured in The Times-Picayune, Fox 8 News with Patrick Evans, and Cox Metro Sports hosted by Darryl Green. The team has also shown their presence on Disney Radio, KKND, 106.7, and co-hosted on SUNY 106.1.

The season begins for the team in November, with conditioning, training, and mini-camps. The Blaze play four home games and four away games in a regular season.

The Blaze have held many members of the league all-star team in the past two years, including Lisa Lunsford (linebacker), Rontashala Williams (running back), and Chris Zboril (quarterback). These players stats put them above the rest of their peers in the league.

The team will be on hiatus in the 2006 season, playing only two exhibition games, due to the effects of Hurricane Katrina.
