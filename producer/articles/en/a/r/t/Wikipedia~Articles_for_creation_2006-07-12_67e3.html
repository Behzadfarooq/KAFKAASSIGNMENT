[edit] Basawon Singh (Sinha)

Birth and early Chidhood He was born in a poor Bhumihar Brahmin family in Jamalpur(Subhai), Vaishali on March 23, 1909. He used to walk eight miles to go to the nearest school to get education. His formal education came to an abrupt end when he was rusticated from collegein Matriculate because of joining the Nationalist Movement.

Entry into freedom movement Participated in Non-Cooperation Movement after Gandhi's visit to Hajipur in 1920-21.

Becomes a Revolutionary Joins the Hindustan Socialist Republican Army headed by Yogendra Shukla in 1925, absconds in 1929 after the famous Lahore Conspiracy Case, co-accused in many conspiracy cases namely Bhusawal, Kakori, Tirhut and Deluaha cases, carries on the movement alongwith Chandrashekhar Azad and Keshav Chandra Chakravarty, arrested and thrown in Bankipore Central jail(June, 1930), escaped on the third day, rearrested, served a seven -year sentence, undertook a 57-day hunger-strike against the imposition of cross-bar fetters in jails, released in June, 1936.

Joined Congress Socialist Party and begins Trade Union work Joins CSP in December,1936, appointed its labour secretary, establishes trade unions in the coal fields, sugar mills, mica mines and railways of Bihar, formed Japla labour union in 1937, formed Baulia Labour union in 1937, organized the workers of Jamalpur Workshop alongwith Shivnath Bannerjee, formed the Gaya cotton and Jute Mill Labour Union, formed the Tata Collieries Labour Association alongwith Subhash Chandra Bose, went on to become its president when Bose left India in 1941, organized coal workers of Talcher, Rajgangpur (Orissa) and Satna (MP); established Mica Workers Union, Gomia Labour Union (Explosives), later these unions affiliated to HMS, active in AIRF since 1936, president of OT Railway Union from Agra to Nefa and NE Railway Mazdoor Union.

[edit] Merox

Merox: - Merox stands for Mercaptan Oxidation. A Process wherein Petroleum fractions such as LPG, gasoline and heavier fractions is treated to remove mercaptan sulfur (Merox extraction) or to convert mercaptan sulfur to less-objectionable disulfides (Merox sweetening).

The method of treatment is the reaction of caustic (sodium hydroxide, NaOH) with the feed stream. The oxidation of mercaptans (thiols) converts them to disulphides, which are soluble in petroleum products and are not corrosive.

Atomistix A/S, headquartered in Copenhagen, Denmark, is a leading provider of software products within atomic scale modeling and has sold and distributed its software solutions on five continents. In Singapore the company has the department Atomistix Asia Pacific.

[edit] History

The company was founded in October 2003 by Dr. Kurt Stokbro, Dr. Jeremy Taylor and Dr. Thomas Magnussen. Dr. Stokbro and Dr. Taylor are co-authors on the article [1] introducing the eletron transport method and program TranSIESTA (based on the SIESTA program [2]) for acedemia. This method, and methods used in Dr. Taylors Ph.D. research, was the starting point for Atomistix first product, TranSIESTA-C. The C refers to the program being written in the programming language C as opposed to Fortran in which TranSIESTA was written.

Since the very beginning the company has been working in close collaboration with the Nano-Science Center at the Niels Bohr Institute of Copenhagen University, to enhance the product development.

[edit] Business

The management consists of:

  • Dr. Thomas Magnussen, Co-founder and Chief Executive Officer (CEO).
  • Dr. Kurt Stokbro, Cofounder, Exec. VP Business development
  • Dr. Jeremy Taylor, Co-founder and Chief Technical Officer (CTO).


[edit] Products

  • TranSIESTA-C
  • Atomistix ToolKit (ATK)
  • Virtual NanoLab (VNL)
  • IView

Atomistix was the first to develop a system of integrated software modules based on quantum theory - the Atomistix Virtual NanoLab‚Ñ¢. This program has a user friendly interface to make complicated calculations more accesible. The setup of the interface resembles more that of an experiment. Its underlying engine is the Atomistix ToolKit (ATK) which is the next generation of TranSIESTA-C. The methods used in the software products are based primarily on Density Functional Theory (DFT) and Non-Equilibrium Green's Function (NEGF) techniques and also all the underlying quantum mechanics.

[edit] Frederick Branch

The Frederick Branch of the Baltimore and Ohio Railroad runs from Frederick Junction on the west side of the Monocacy River to its terminus at South Street in downtown Frederick, Maryland. The branch officially was opened in December of 1831 with a ceremonial train (pulled by horses) carrying directors of the company and various politicians arriving from Baltimore. The branch first provided a rapid means for the many mills in the city to ship flour to Baltimore for sale. Over the years outbound freight traffic diversified to include milk, bricks, limestone and some manufactured goods from Frederick. The branch connected with two other railroads within Frederick. The Pennsylvania Railroad connected near East Street and South Street in downtown Frederick and the Hagerstown and Frederick Railway connected near the small B&O Railroad yard and terminal along South Street in downtown Frederick. Passenger service lasted until November of 1949 and the branch gradually fell into disrepair as local customers switched to trucks. As of 2006 the only remaining freight customer on the branch is Wickes Lumber which receives car loads of building materials via a local freight. In December of 2001 passenger traffic returned to Frederick in the form of MARC commuter trains. The state of Maryland funded upgrades to the Frederick Branch and to the portion of the Old Main Line that ran from Frederick Junction to Point of Rocks. A small yard was also constructed along Reichs Ford Road with capacity for three entire train sets. Two new stations were constructed to handle passengers, one in downtown Frederick near the original branch terminus at South Street and another located behind a shopping center near Frederick Junction.

[edit] Quartz (OpenSymphony)

Quartz Enterprise Job Scheduler ("Quartz") is an open source project which is part of the OpenSymphony family of OSS products.

Quartz can be integrated into standard Java and Java Enterprise Endition applications, to provide job scheduling functionality to the application - which enables the application to become event driven with time-based events.

The main concepts in Quartz are "Jobs" and "Triggers". A Job is a unit of work (application code) that can be performed. A Trigger defines when a Job will be executed, and how frequently it will repeat (if ever).

Quartz includes many "Enterprise Class" features, such as clustering (fail-over and load-balancing), and support for JTA transactions.

[edit] History

Quartz was founded in the spring of 2001 by James House as an open source software project on SourceForge.net.

In the spring of 2004 Quartz joined the OpenSymphony family of open source projects. At the same time, its source code repository was moved to java.net.
