[edit] Intel nahalem

Nahelem originally was a code name for Intel's microprocessor which was to be a successor to the tejas, the cancelled incarnation of the pentium 4 after the prescott core. The Nahelem was projected to run at clock speed over 10GHZ at a front side bus of 1200mhz. It was slated for a late 2005 release, but was cancelled due to unexpected heat problems in the 90nm prescott cores.

Nahelem now refers to a new micro architecture slated to succeed the Intel's Core Micro architecture. Little is known about the platform except that it use a 45nano meter manufacturing process. The first presumed processor using the nahelem architecture is code named bloomsfield a quad core chip built on the 45 nanometer process using a shared 8megabyte cache

[edit] Cloak and Dagger (society)

The Society of the Cloak and Dagger is a secret society at Georgetown University. It was founded in 1892 in response to the creation of The Yard, the first student association at Georgetown. Originally founded as the Laenasican Society, the group consisted of upperclassmen student leaders advocating for more student involvement in campus life. (The Yard would not assume responsibility beyond athletic affairs until 1919.) In 1899, the society became a secret senior society, modeling themselves as the Catholic version of Skull and Bones and Scroll and Key at Yale University. They would be joined on campus in later years by the Stewards and Crux Orbis.

Originally the society was compromised of seniors, but in latter years juniors and sophomores were allowed to join. Membership has fluctuated between as few as 5 and as many as 20. Many take their repetitive use of the number five and their self-reference as "The Brotherhood of Five" to indicate that membership stands at 5 or a multiple 5 (similar to the Seven Society). Their seal is a shaded dagger in a circle with five stars above the hilt. They communicate to students and other campus groups via cryptic poems, often in the shape of the Roman numeral V. There is no known way to communicate with the group though they have occasionally requested replies to letters by dropping notes at famous Georgetown landmarks, such as the statue of founder John Carroll.

Most recently, the society captured campus attention when their seal appeared in a basement room beneath Healy Hall in a room that had formerly been the meeting place of Crux Orb is, who had abandoned their meeting place due to excess knowledge of its location.

[edit] Bowlplex

Bowlplex plc operates 16 "family entertainment centres" across the UK. Although centred around bowling, each complex also has a Grill, serving mainly fried food and soft drinks; The Stadium Bar, which are late licensed until 2am; Video World with Sega arcade games; and a Pool Pit with American-style pool tables. Each centre is either "entertainment" or "modern" themed, and offers corporate, birthday and seasonal packages as well as straightforward bowling.

All Bowlplex centres are located within 'Multi-Leisure' environments. they are only developed where other leisure/recreation operators are present. This allows for better walk in trade (as it is known). Not being the main/only draw for each site is a fundamental element of it's success. People who visit the areas may be there to visit another operator and notice the Bowlplex and visit there also (better brand awareness).

[edit] Staff

Bowlplex employs over 650 staff across it's centres. Most of these staff are General Assistants, working a mixture of shifts on Customer Care, the bar or the Grill.

Each centre also has five bowling co-ordinators (3xf/t & 2xp/t), responsible for supervising the staff and managing bookings and reception, and duty managers, responsible for the running of each centre. Each Duty Manager has a specific job role, in which they are responsible for. They are: Assistant Manager Operations (AMO - Operational aspect of each department), Assistant Manager Employee Systems (AME - HR and Training), Assistant Manager Centre Support (AMC - Back office and auditing) and General Manager (GM - Oversee's the overall running of each centre)

Each centre also has a team of technicians responsible for servicing and fixing the lanes as well as maintaining the centre.

[edit] Market

Bowlplex pricing tends to be lower than most competitors, and as such is often seen as a "budget" competitor in the bowling market. Bowlplex has also tried to establish itself as an all-round entertainment centre, with the bar staying open later than the lanes on Friday and Saturday nights with a live DJ.

[edit] Metawork

Quite often, employees in an organisation are made to do certain activities which do not DIRECTLY result in the achievement of the goals that have been set for those employees. For example, in a company manufacturing cars, the work of an employee in the final conveyor-line would be to assemble parts to create cars. If that employee is also asked to do planning, or to attend meetings to decide the policies of the company, his or her primary work of assembling cars would be affected. His or her goal of assembling car would NOT be met. Planning or forming policies of the company are definitely important activities but are NOT the primary goals of an employee in the conveyor-line. Such activities which do not DIRECTLY lead to achieving the goals are known as METAWORK. It also denotes those activities which are not required for meeting the goals of the organisation.

[edit] [online home loan] or [home loan online]

A home loan online can be either an official 1003 application or an unofficial application used to start the process of attaining a home loan/mortgage loan. Mortgage brokers and lenders use this convenient means to originate leads of potential customers interested in a home loan.

A common misconception is that an online home loan application can result in the immediate approval of a home loan. This is false due to the fact that mortgage loans are attained only after intense scrutiny of an applicants personal financial history which includes, but is not limited to: income, job history, and credit history. Companies that advertise home loans online are primarily referring to beginning the process of pre-approval for the loan. An official home loan is never finalized until after the submission of a 1003 Uniform Residential Loan Application and after the loan officer or broker contacts you to verify, collect, and sign all additional legal paperwork.

This being said a home loan online form is a great tool to begin the approval process of your loan and many times can result in faster pre-approval.

[edit] United States Championship

The WWE United States Championship is a professional wrestling title. It was formerly the National Wrestling Alliance and then the World Championship Wrestling United States Championship but is now a World Wrestling Entertainment title exclusive to the SmackDown! brand as a secondary championship.

[edit] Headline text

Although the NWA had crowned a United States Heavyweight Champion in 1950, "Nature Boy" Buddy Rogers being given the first honor, over the years the many promotions that conformed the NWA began recognizing their own champions. The United States championship recognized by Mid Atlantic Championship Wrestling (later World Championship Wrestling) actually started in 1974, with former World champion Harley Race being given the first belt. The Mid-Atlantic version became the Undisputed NWA United States Championship in 1981, when the San Francisco promotion (the only remaining NWA promotion to recognize its own U.S. champion) closed down. In 1991 the title became the WCW United States Heavyweight Championship.

When Canadian wrestler Lance Storm held the United States Championship, he renamed it the Canadian Heavyweight Championship for the duration of his reign, and covered the faceplate of the belt with a sticker of the Canadian Flag.

The title was a WCW championship until, after the World Wrestling Federation's purchase of WCW in 2001, United States Champion Edge defeated the WWF Intercontinental Champion, Test, to merge both titles. Edge then held the WWF Intercontinental Championship, and the WCW United States Championship became inactive.

The title was reactivated as a WWE belt by SmackDown! general manager, Stephanie McMahon. This was done shortly after the RAW brand reactivated the WWE Intercontinental Championship as its secondary championship, and thus the United States Championship would be the equal counterpart for SmackDown!. Eddie Guerrero defeated Chris Benoit in the finals of a tournament at Vengeance 2003 to become the first WWE United States Champion.

At the Armageddon 2004, then-champion John Cena revealed a new redesigned, custom-made, United States championship, resembling a disc on a turntable. However, after Orlando Jordan defeated Cena for the title in March 2005, Cena's belt was unceremoniously dumped into a garbage can and burned by John "Bradshaw" Layfield and The Cabinet. Jordan was then presented with the original WWE United States Championship belt, which is still used today.

[edit] USS Carlinville (PC-1120)

USS Carlinville was laid down on 19 June 1942 at the Defoe Shipbuilding Company in Bay City, Michigan; launched on 24 August 1942; and commissioned as USS PC-1120 on 18 January 1943. During World War II, PC-1120 was assigned to the South Pacific area of the Asiatic-Pacific theater, She took part in the Bismark Archipelago operations-Arawe, New Britain, from 15 December 1943 through 13 January 1944; Eastern New Guinea operations-Saidor occupation, from 2through 4 June 1944; Western New Guinea operations-Biak Island operation, from 18 through 21 June 1944; Noemfoor Island operation, from 2 through 4 July 1944; Cape Sansapor operation, and the Morotai operations, starting on 15 September 1944.

Following these, she moved north to the Philippines where she took part in the Leyte landings from 15 through 25 October 1944; the consolidation of the Southern Philippines-Palawan Island landings, from 28 February through 3 March 1945; and the Borneo operations and Tarakan Island operations, from 27 April through 5 May 1945

She was decommissioned in January 1947 and placed in the Pacific Reserve Fleet at the Columbia River. While still de-commissioned,she was named Carlinville on 15 February 1956. She was struck from the Naval Register on 1 April 1959, and she was turned over to the Maritime Commission and eventually sold to an Alaskan fisherman. In 1975, the former subchaser was owned by the Diesel Electric Company of Seattle, Washington, and working as an inter-island personnel and cargo transport in the Hawaiian Islands as the M/V Island Transport.

In 1976 she was seized by the United States Marshall Service in a bankruptcy ruling and sold to Greenpeace for $70,000. With Greenpeace, whe was renamed the M/V Ohana Kai (Hawaiian for "Children of the Sea"), and registered in Panama, and used to harass the Russian whaling fleet which was operating south of the Hawaiian Islands. She was later sold to a group that wanted to take it crab fishing, but this venture did not work out. One year, a December storm tore it from its moorings and it ended up on the mud flats off Emeryville.

She was then sold to Clause Von Wendel, who lived aboard her at Berkley, California. After many years of court orders and law suits, the city of Berkley and the owners of the marina had Von Wendel and his followers evicted. In April of 1991, the ex-Carlinville was sold for scrap and towed to a scrap yard in Alameda.

[edit] advomentary motion picture film or video

Advomentary - an advocacy documentary motion picture. An advomentary uses documentary production styles to promote a specific viewpoint or “spin”. The objective of advomentary content is primarily persuasion. An advomentary is intended to promote a specific ideology, judgment, attitude, or perspective based upon presentation of the related facts.


Typically an advomentary would cover a political, economic, ecological, or social issue. Other possible themes could be technical topics such as promoting a clean energy technology or a new medical innovation. Any topic that generates controversy is an appropriate theme for an advomentary.


The word advomentary is a linguistic portmanteau which is formed by combining the words advocacy and documentary.


Advomentaries are typically low-budget productions that are financed by interest groups or producers with a strong opinion upon an issue. Although these productions are often supportive of social objectives, they are intended to make a profit. The viewing public has shown over and over again that they will pay for the privilege of being wooed by a persuasive presentation.



Advomentary vs. Documentary


A documentary is an objective presentation of the facts with non-controversial interpretation. An advomentary uses similar production techniques to promote a specific interpretation of the facts where there is often sharp division of opinion and may also advocate viewer action.


Documentaries are intended to inform.


Advomentaries are intended to inform and persuade.



Advomentary vs. Infomercial


The typical infomercial promotes a product, service, charity, or political goal. The typical advomentary promotes an idea or perspective. Infomercials are television productions that are designed to produce a direct quantifiable response from the viewer population. Advomentaries are movie productions designed to produce a qualitative response within the viewer.


Infomercials promote sponsored products or services.


Advomentaries promote broad-based ideas or viewpoints.



Advomentary vs. Propaganda


Advomentaries are intended to be an honest attempt to present a case using factual background information. Propaganda is an attempt to persuade using any available fact or fiction to promote a self-serving agenda. Propaganda is often non-factual and can use demagoguery, fear, and hate to achieve its goals.


Advomentaries use facts to promote a sincere viewpoint.


Propaganda uses facts and/or nonfacts to promote a cynical viewpoint.



History


Advocacy motion pictures are almost as old as film itself. The unrivalled power of film and video to persuade was apparent from the start. Until recently, advocacy content was rarely intended for profit and required financing from interest groups and sponsors who considered it promotional expense.


The modern era of advocacy film most likely started in 1989 with Michael Moore’s first hit independent production “Roger and Me”, an investigation of General Motors treatment of employees and the devastating impact of GM layoffs on Flint, Michigan. With that film, Moore proved that people will pay to watch advocacy content and that these productions can generate massive profits. Moore developed a distinctive guerilla moviemaking style that has become synonymous with the genre.


Later productions expanded on Moore’s methods by incorporating classical narrative documentary and investigative journalism techniques to make their case. Higher quality film and video have allowed for slicker productions but many directors have found that the low-budget look often makes for more compelling advocacy.


The market power of advomentary content is now clearly established. Michael Moore’s recent “Fahrenheit 9/11” grossed over $200 million in profits and sold 3 million DVDs. This success has inspired big-gun producers to enter this genre. Noted TV producer Davis Guggenheim was behind Al Gore’s “An Inconvenient Truth”. Other big Hollywood-style productions are now being planned.

[edit] Recipes from Mauritius

The eating habits of the Mauritians inevitably reflect the ethnic diversity of its people: Creole rougailles, Indian curries, Muslim bryanis, Chinese sweet-and-sour pork, French delicate dishes, English bacon and eggs, ...... you name it, you'll get it there. Basic ingredients of the Creole cuisine are the tomatoes (known as pommes d'amour), onions, ginger, garlic and chillies. Palm heart and Camarons (giant prawns), venison and wild boar are favourite items of French cuisine. Fresh fish and seafood set the keynote for Chinese cooking. The traditional blends of home crushed spices are the sauce base for mouth glowing Indian curries. The delicate blend of spiciness and subtle mix of ingredients constitute the setting for the event-related Muslim cuisine. Local vegetables and fruits abound all year round in a colourful selection of mouth watering delights. Recipes can be accessed at http://ile-maurice.tripod.com

[edit] UNEF

The Union Nationale des Etudiants de France (National Union of Students of France) or UNEF is the main French students's union. Bruno Julliard is its current chairman.

[edit] History

The UNEF was created in 1907 as a grouping of local students' associations in order to represent the students with national and local authorities. From 1918 to 1940, the UNEF created university restaurants and sanatoriums and originated the French students' welfare system (creation of the Conseil Supérieur des Oeuvres Sociales in 1936). After World War II, the UNEF adopted the Charter of Grenoble (la Charte de Grenoble) which recognise the student as an "intellectual young worker", which gives him rights and duties. In 1948, thanks to the lobying of the National Union, the French government created the Mutuelle Nationale des Etudiants de France and the students' social security system. In the 1950s, many members of the UNEF supported the independance of Algeria and members who were against any political position created a non-political, non-unionist organisation called Fédération Nationale des Etudiants de France. In the 1960s, the UNEF was divided into many political influences and in 1971 the Union was splited between the UNEF-Renouveau (later UNEF-Solidarité Etudiante, under the communist influence) and the UNEF-Unité Syndicale (under the influence of non-communist left-wing parties). The movement is partially reunited in 1981 with the creation of the UNEF Indépendante & Démocratique (UNEF-ID) by the grouping of UNEF-US and other groups. The final reunification occured in 2001, with the merging of UNEF-ID and UNEF-SE into one UNEF which proclame its independance from any political party. The UNEF is now the main students' and youth organisation in France and played a major role in the mobilisation against the Contrat Première Embauche in 2006.
