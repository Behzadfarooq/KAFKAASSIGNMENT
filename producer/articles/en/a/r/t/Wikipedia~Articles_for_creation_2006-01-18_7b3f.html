It is of the "grammar" or academic type as distinguished from the "technical institute" or "senior comprehehensive type". [edit] MV Flare

MV Flare, a bulk carrier en route from Rotterdam to Quebec, broke in two during severe weather approximately 45 min west of Saint-Pierre-et-Miquelon on the 16th of January 1998. The stern section of the bulk carrier sank within 30 minutes while the bow remained afloat for days. 21 crew members perished, 4 survived. Some of the crew on the sinking stern section saw the bow of another deep sea vessel appear to approach them, only to realize that it was the separated front half of the Flare - the propellor on the stern section still turning had brought them back toward it.

TelphinUSA is a VoIP service company which offers IP telephony for local, long-distance and international calling by using Voice over Internet Protocol (VoIP) technology. VoIP converts the voice signal from a telephone into a digital signal that travels over the Internet, then converts that signal back to voice at the other end so a person can speak to anyone with a regular phone number. A broadband Internet connection is required - cable modem, DSL, T1, or any high-speed, always-on connection. Beyond that, the only thing needed is an adapter.

All clients connected to the TelphinUSA network may call any phone number anywhere in the world within the range of their calling plan (World, North America, Europe, Asia, and Russia). VoIP services also allows to set up virtual numbers. Direct telephone numbers are traditional telephone numbers which we use for local, long-distance and international calls. With VoIP telephony, the number on a phone doesn't have to have the area code or even the country code of the location where a person is using it. No matter where the telephonee is located, virtual numbers allow to call her/him at local charges.

[edit] Parabelle

A Parabelle is a French poetic template that consists of four stanzas of six lines each. In stanzas one through three, the first and second as well as the third and fourth lines must be identical (1=2, 3=4). The fifth and sixth lines then must consist of only the words from the first four lines of their respective stanza. The fourth stanza must contain all the words from the preceding three stanzas.

[edit] Bill Goodwin (announcer)

This request for creation has been reviewed. The result is below.
If your request was declined and you want to make changes and try again, copy and paste your article into a new submission and make your changes there.


Bill Goodwin (July 28, 1910 -- May 9, 1958) was an American radio and television announcer and motion picture actor.

Among the group of classic-era radio show announcers who were also an actual character on their programs (e.g., Don Wilson on The Jack Benny Program; Harlow Wilcox on Fibber McGee and Molly; Wendell Niles on Lum & Abner;, and, Kenny Delmar on The Fred Allen Show), the one who may well have been the best at that craft was Bill Goodwin.

Goodwin, a California native, was heard in both the early 1940's and the early 1950's as one of the announcers for Bob Hope's weekly radio comedy/variety show. His most familiar work, though, came as the 'girl-crazy' 'man-about-town' spokesperson on the shows of George Burns and Gracie Allen.

In the first series of the Burns/Allen show, before 1940, Goodwin was depicted as a rival alongside Burns for Allen's affections. From 1940 onward, Goodwin played the announcer of happily-married George and Gracie's fictitious 'radio-show-within-a-show,' yet his character remained notorious as the biggest 'wolf' in Hollywood. Goodwin's pleasant, 'smiling' voice was mixed with his superb sense of pace and timing.

Goodwin's energetic vocal personality resulted in some of the best 'integrated commercials' in radio history (in which the mid-show commercial in particular is done as part of that episode's plot). The live audiences can be heard offering as much laughter during his 'plugs' as during his regular joke routines with Burns and Allen. One constant 'laugh-getter' was his 'breaking down' into near hysteria if the show's other characters and guests were ignorant of the benefits of the sponsor's product.

Goodwin reprised his role in the first season of the Burns/Allen television program in 1950, but did not continue long in that format. He would regain some television fame as an announcer by being the narrrator for the ground-breaking animated "Gerald McBoing-Boing" program in 1956.

He moved from announcing into acting, appearing in 31 films in supporting roles from 1941 to 1958. The most notable of those films were the 1942 World War II drama Wake Island; both of the Al Jolson bio-pics, in 1946 and 1949; and, Alfred Hitchcock's 1945 mystery Spellbound.

Goodwin died of a heart attack at the relatively young age of 47.

[edit] Don Maitz

Don Maitz is a fantasy and science fiction illustrator and graphic designer. He is married to author/illustrator Janny Wurts and lives in Florida. Maitz's best-known work is probably the pirate he created for Captain Morgan's Spiced Rum, but his work is quite diverse; he has illustrated numerous book covers and exhibited his fantasy art in numerous galleries throughout the United States.

[edit] Awards

Maitz has twice won the Hugo Award for best artist, and received a Howard Award from the World Fantasy Convention. The Association of Science Fiction and Fantasy Artists has conferred on him ten Chesley Awards as of this writing.

[edit] Armando Aste

Armando Aste is one of the most influential Italian alpinists of the postwar period. He was born on January 6, 1926 in the South Tyrolean city Rovereto near Trent, Italy.

He was leading the first Italian ascent of the Eiger north face in 1962, together with Pierlorenzo Acquistapace, Gildo Airoldi, Andrea Mellano, Romano Perego and Franco Solina. He also climbed for the first time the North Tower of Paine in Patagonia.

His most important books are "Pilastri del cielo" (Nordpress, Italy, 2000) and "Cuore di roccia" (Manfrini stampatori, Italy, 1988).
