Being one of the hardest working bands (if not THE hardest working band) in the United Kingdom, having played 686 gigs in 6 years, having sold at least 5000 records/cd's simply by touring hard without funding or any kind of publicity other than diy promotion makes The Dangerfields a fairly notable band in my opinion.


But appart from that The Dangerfields meet the following Wikipedia Music Criteria:

  • Has gone on an international concert tour, or a national concert tour in at least one large or medium-sized country,[1] reported in notable and verifiable sources.

They have done multiple Tours of The UK and Eire many lasting up to a month or more at a time (2 years ago they played 40 gigs in 40 nights) they have also done 3 Mainland European Tours: 2 Tours of Germany in November 2002 & October 2003 and a Tour of Netherlands/Germany in April 2004. And have a European Tour scheduled for August 2006

http://www.thedangerfields.com/pastshows.php

---

  • Has been featured in multiple non-trivial published works in reliable and reputable media (excludes things like school newspapers, personal blogs, etc...).

The Dangerfields have been featured in a 2 page spread in Alternative Ulster a widespread Independant music publication based in Northern Ireland as well as having multiple interviews in many Underground fanzines and webzines

http://www.alternativeulster.com/ http://www.punkoiuk.co.uk/interviews/dangerfields.htm http://www.metalireland.com/interv/dangerfields.html

---

  • Contains at least one member who was once a part of or later joined a band that is otherwise notable; note that it is often most appropriate to use redirects in place of articles on side projects, early bands and such.

Singer/Drummer Andrew Griswold has played Drums for The Queers a very well known American pop punk band.

http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Queers

And former Dangerfields members include: Roxy Micheals of The Undead/Marky Ramone & The Intruders and Jane Strain of popular UK Ska band The Amphetameanies

http://undead.postmortemrecords.com/news/news.php http://www.myspace.com/roxymichaels http://www.amphetameanies.co.uk/

---

  • has won or placed in a major music competition.

The Dangerfields were picked by Bruce Dickinson of Iron Maiden to play a BBC Radio Roadshow gig in Support of Therapy? in a National CompEtition on his BBC 6 Music Rock Show

http://www.bbc.co.uk/6music/shows/bruce_dickinson/

---

  • Has been the subject of a half hour or longer broadcast on a national radio network.

The Dangerfields were interviewed and had tracks from their album played in a section of Bruce Dickinsons BBC 6 Music Rock Show lasting 30 mins and have recorded a BBC Session for BBC Radio Ulster

http://www.bbc.co.uk/6music/shows/bruce_dickinson/ http://www.bbc.co.uk/northernireland/atl/
