[edit] Neil Perry (Band)

Neil Perry was a screamo band from New Jersey formed in 1998 by former members of You And I, and Red All Over. They released a whole slew of releases, and totalled fourty tracks at their demise. These fourty songs were released on a discography 2xCD entitled Lineage Situation by Level-Plane records. Bonus material with this included a 35 minute video of live shows, tour footage, and various other scraps of footage compiled during the band's span.

Neil Perry was Josh Jakubowski (vocals/guitar), Chris Smith (guitar/vocals), Jon Marinari (vocals/bass), and Justin Graves (drums).

Members went on to, or were involved with, Hot Cross, The Now, Superstitions Of The Sky, Joshua Fit For Battle, You And I, Welcome The Plague Year, Track Of Monarchs, A Life Once Lost, etc.

Neil Perry was a character in the 80's movie, Dead Poet's Society.

[edit] Have A Look (Song)

Have A Look is Vanessa Amorosi's debut Australian single, released in 1999. The song; written by Vanessa Amorosi, Mark Holden & Shaun Imrei; managed to peak at number 13 on the Australian singles charts. It also got Gold status in seven weeks. The track was produced by renowned record producer Steve Mac.

The video features Vanessa travelling around the Sydney train network, and well as walking around the Bondi Beach area and Liverpool Shops.

The track was later remixed for it's European release, to make it more pop and suitable for those markets.

Kosmas the Aetolian

Ag. Kosmas Aetolos (the Aetolian)

Kosmal Aetolos was born in 1714 in Mega Dendro, Aetolia, son of an Epirote father. He received his basic education in Zigditsa near Mt. Parnassos, and later studied at the Athonias School of Mt. Athos, where he became a monk.

He believed that religion and education would save the Greek nation from annihilation. For this reason he went to Constantinople and, with the help of his elder brother, Chrysanthos, who was a teacher at the patriarchal school, he obtained the relevant permission by the Patriarch and started preaching.

Kosmas Aetolos travelled around Epirus, especially Northern Epirus, from 1768 to 1779. His activity was a catalyst for the search for national self-knowledge. Father Kosmas turned against the Turkish oppression, blamed the unequal distribution of taxes by the nobles, took care for the establishment of hospitals and the relief of poor people and orphans; but mostly, he strongly contributed to the establishment of schools for the preservation of the Greek language and culture.

The Turkish authorities arrested him in the village Kolikontasi, outside Berati; he was hanged near the river Apsos, in 1779. A monastery was established later at this site, consecrated to Ag. Kosmas.

[edit] Electronic Dog Fences e.g. dogguard

This device has been in existance for a while now, but I do not see any mention of it on Wikipedia -hence this note.

Electronic Dog fences are usually Fences that operate using radio signals to define a perimeter that the dog is limited to. The perimeter is identified by an underlying metallic cable that is put into the ground at several meters below surface during installation.

The collar with a receiver is fixed to the dog as the usual collars are and a transmitter sits inside the house in any general location. The transmitter transmits a signal to the receiver and the boundary and when the pet goes near to the underground fence, it is warned by a beep and then treated by a small veterinarian approved electric shock. After a while, the dog will know its limits after the learning process is completed. The dog, however, continues to wear the collar which is not cumbersome to become a concern. The success of this device is overwhelming and is in common use today.

The product is usually registered for warranty purposes and the collar if seen on a dog in the certain area can be tracked back with some limitations to the owner using the company records.

P.D. = 100*abs(Experimental - Theoretical)/Theoretical

Therefore, after seeing his father in such a state, Allen used the exorcist's weapon embedded into his deformed right arm to kill Mana.

Allen was born with a deformed right arm. In it, though, is a piece of Innnocence, a special anti-akuma weapon wielded only bestowed upon those destined to become exorcists. The weapon allows him to destroy the tortured souls embedded in Akuma, thus destroying the Akuma and freeing the soul. His weapon also allows him to see the soul inside an Akuma, thus letting him find them among a crowd of humans.

[edit] The Society

Allen now belongs to a society of exorcists with similar talents. All have pieces of Innocence, but they are not all who they seem or eager to halp Allen. He may have teamates for his exorcising missions, but he must face his destiny alone...

[edit] Jimmie Fletcher

Jimmie Fletcher is a Country Music singer and West Texas Music Hall of Fame inductee. Jimmie first recorded for Mercury and later on Winston. He toured with most of the members of The Grand Ole Opry and at one time played regularly in Mexico. In San Angelo he had daily radio shows, and by choosing the best talent available through the years for his band, the Drifting Ranch Hands, Jimmie provided dance music for the large crowds that followed him. Jimmie made a music career out of playing just two clubs in San Angelo, "The Dixie" and "The Boots & Saddle". In the 70's, Jimmie ended his successful music career playing seven years at the famous "Ramblin Rose" in Llano, Texas. Jimmie Fletcher's origonal releases include "Take a Look at Me", "Things You Didn't Mean", "Whats Wrong With You", and "No Quittin' Place".
