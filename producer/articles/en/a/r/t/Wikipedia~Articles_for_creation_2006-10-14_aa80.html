[edit] Drive by Staffing

Drive by staffing is a term used primarily in investment banks to describe the act of an Associate level employee or above staffing analysts or associates within proximity. This staffing method supersedes the standard protocol of staffing analysts and associates through a designated staffer, and is linked to higher levels of anxiety on the part of the staffee. Recently, steps have been taken at some leading bulge bracket firms to reduce drive-by staffing, but these efforts are mitigated should they originate anyone less senior than the highest ranked member of sector group.

The etymology of the term remains disputed.

Jimmy Page performing White Summer on his DC-3 at the Royal Albert Hall in 1970

The DC-3 was a popular solid-body electric guitar produced by Danelectro, primarily designed for rock. It first achieved much usage in the golden days of rock and roll and rockabilly: the late 1950s, and then became popular in the "surf rock" genre of the early-mid 1960s, from groups such as The Ventures. Production was stopped on the guitars around 1969, but some famous guitarists, most notably Jimmy Page of Led Zeppelin, continued to play their DC-3s in concert throughout the '70s and '80s. Page, for example, memorably used it on all of his extended "White Summer" instrumentals, (which was almost, in its own way, a 10-minute tribute to the beautifullly-designed instrunment.) Then in the late 1970s, he substituted it for his Les Paul as his primary guitar. Finally in the mid-1990s the design was reisseued, and remains a popular and very affordable "retro-style" guitar.

Sword of the Samurai

Sword of the Samurai was produced by Genki and was released in 2002. It is a videogame made for the PS2 - Playstation 2. Image:SwordOfTheSamurai.jpg

Warning: This part contains certain parts of the game, and might spoil the story.

Gameplay

The game is based on Samurai - particulary on the fighting of these warriors. As you first start the game, you get to choose a face, suit and name for your character. Then you see a short video of your character walking by 2 fighting samurai. One of them loses his sword, which flies through the air, and you character catches. You then challenge a swordsman.

If you never have played this game before, this battle is unbelievably hard - practically unable to win, as you don't know the controls, and certain moves yet. But winning or losing makes your start a bit different, although it doesn't make differ a lot, as you can still join the Dojo, and go to the recruiter. (If you win this battle, you meet the recruiter, which gives you certain asignments. If you lose, you meet the headmaster of the Dojo, which asks you to join him).

Another feature of this game is, your character developes after each battle, thus getting stronger, more agile or recovers faster. After most battles, certainly at the start, you learn new moves too, which you can implant into new 'forms'.

Forms are consists of a stance, an attack and a defence, which you can fill in yourself, after learning more and more moves. Every points starts with a certain amount of points, which you can distribute among moves you want to use in this form.

Controls

As most likely you play this game on an original PS2 gamepad, the controls are: - square: To break the defence of an enemy. - round: To attack your enemy, tap this button several times and you will start an attack sequence. - cross: Continuesly pressing this button will keep you in an defensive stance, in which you can block most of the enemy's attacks. Tap this button once, at the point an enemy attacks, and you will overpower him, making you able to break his defence, and hitting him at least one time.

[edit] Cylindro - Conoidal Bullet

The Cylindro-Conoidal Bullet was invented by Captain Norton of the British 34th Regiment in 1823. It had a hollow base, so that, when fired, the bullent would expand and seal the bore. The origin of his idea is an interesting one: when in Southern India, he examined the blow pipe arrows used by the natives, and found that their base was formed of elastic locus pith, which by its expansion agianst the inner surface of the blow pipe prevented the escape of air past it.

In 1836, Mr Greener, a London gunsmith, improved on Norton's bullet by inserting a conoidal wooden plug into its base. Although both inventions were rejected by the British Ordinance Department, the idea was taken up in France, and in 1849 M.Minnie adopted Greener's design and produced the deadly "Minnie Bullet"

[edit] Lyman Allyn Art Museum

The Lyman Allyn Art Museum is located in New London, Connecticut. The Museum was established in 1926 by Harriet Upson Allyn who named it for her father. There are over 15,000 works in the permanent collection of the Museum, featuring a prominent collection of American art, with paintings by John Trumbull, Rembrandt Peale, Thomas Cole, Frederick Edwin Church, and Albert Bierstadt.

The collection is housed in a Neo-Classical building designed by Charles Platt and completed in 1932. It is located on a 20 acre campus.

Healthy People 2010 was started in January 2000 by the US Department of Health and Human Sevices. It is a mationwide health promotion and disease prevention plan that is composed of 467 specific objectives, 28 goals, and two overarching goals to be achieved by 2010. National data has been gathered from over 190 sources, seven Federal Government Departments, and many voluntary and private non-governmental organizations. Ten leading health indicators that are the immediate, major health concerns have been identified. They are: physical activity, overweight and obesity, tobacco use, substance abuse, a responsible sex life, mental health, injury and violence, environmental quality where you live, immunization, and access to health care. Healthy People 2010's two overarching goals are to increase the quality and years of life, and to eliminate health diparities.
