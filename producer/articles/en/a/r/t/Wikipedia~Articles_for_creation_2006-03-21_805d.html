[edit] Axis Dynide (Band)

Axis Dynide are a heavy progressive rock band formed in Sheffield,U.K. August 2005. The band consists of three members who have been playing music together since September 2001 under the old band Ritalin which dissolved because of the lead singers (Will Horton) differences in August 2005. The members of Axis Dynide consist of Tamir Ali (Bass, vocals), Kriss Kinneavy (Guitar, vocals), and Taran Ali (Drums). There music has been compared and described in many different ways. So to translate Axis Dynides music, the music although heavy and tuneful, also has a rather bizzare surreal image to their music at the same time which is seen by many punters as dark and melodical.


[edit] Influences

There influences are very varied from each other.To summarise, the band are into a wide range of music like blues,jazz, rock,psychedelic,progressive rock,trip-hop,avant-grade,metal(although it would not be so cool to describe some of Axis Dynides influences as metal), and rockabilly. Kriss Kinneavy the guitarist has been compared for his guitar style to the likes of Omar Rodriguez Lopez (The Mars Volta) and Jimi Hendrix in which he admires the most of all the guitarists.The other influences Kriss grew up with are The Doors, Can, The Meteors, and his favourite idol Syd Barrett.

Taran Ali (Drummer), admits his influences mainly range of jazz, rock and ambient music. But in more focus on the drumming aspect, he grew up listening to drummers like Danny Carey (Tool) and Jon Theodore (The Mars Volta). He also greatly learns rudiments and fill tricks from drummers such as Neil Peart (Rush), Bill Bruford (Yes, King Crimson), Steve Gadd (Independent), Lenny White (Return To Forever), and Alan White (Yes).

Tamir Ali (Bassist), shows his influence of bass playing from bands such as Soundgarden and Alice In Chains for the more heavy low bass sound which has frequently been complemented by various reviewers of live gigs. However, he also likes artists such as Frank Zappa and Morphine and is quickly grasping Mark Sandman's (Morphine) unique style of playing.

[edit] Thant Myint-U

Thant Myint-U is a Senior Officer in the Executive Office of the United Nations Secretary-General. From 2004-5 he was the Acting Chief the Policy Planning Unit in the Department of Political Affairs and a member of the secretariat of the Secretary-General's High-Level Panel on Threats, Challenges and Change. He is a veteran of three UN peacekeeping operations, in Cambodia and the former Yugoslavia and has also served with the UN's Office for the Coordination of Humanitarian Affairs. In 1994 he was the spokesman in Sarajevo for the UN Protection Force.

Thant Myint-U was born in New York city to Burmese parents in January 1966. His maternal grandfather was U Thant, the third Secretary-General of the United Nations. Thant Myint-U was educated at Harvard and at Cambridge Universities and completed his PhD in history at Cambridge in 1996. In 1994 he had been elected a Fellow of Trinity College, Cambridge. In 1999 he was also appointed a Fellow of the Cambridge Centre for History and Economics. In 2000 he published his first book, The Making of Modern Burma. His second book, The River of Lost Footsteps, will be published in 2006.

[edit] Indira Jocy, a new singer in making in Nepal

Indira Jocy


“Do you have the guts to compete?” The advertisement published in newspapers on 2062 Magh attracted her. It was an announcement of a mega television program to hunt singing talents across the country. Indira Jocy, 19, decided to accept the challenge.

Jocy, from Nawalparasee had been living in Kathmandu for studies. A student of 11, she had been singing from early childhood in local programs. She had been acquiring formal musical trainings from DO RE MI music school for some time. Little did she imagine at that time that she would get a national recognition through the program.

When she reached the studios for primary auditions, she could see a never ending queue of aspiring singers. She waited patiently for 6 hours and yet her turn did not come that day. She had to go the next day for her turn. She says,

“ I sang Aruna Lama's “Eklai Basda.” I was bit nervous to sing in front of the judges like Deepak Jungam, Bimala Rai and Aalokshree, but they selected me for the second round.”

She crooned “Udi Jun Bhane” on the second round. When she passed it to be selected amongst 206 from 9000 plus participants through out the country, she sensed some sort of achievement. they had to sing along with music for next round. Despite other participant's advice not to go for a pop number, she tried out “Mayako Baato” by Sugam Pokharel. Till then she was just another face in the crowd but her decision to attempt a different genre of song proved to be fruitful. Not only the organizers and the judges, but even the viewers were taken by storm by her singing that time which made an long lasting impression. Her capability to feel and improvise a song was revealed in that performance. Even an average audience of television could clearly feel her passion for singing, the depth of her voice and modulation.

That was the beginning and came subsequent rounds, she surprised the judges by her sudden improvisation skills and distinctive style of singing. She made the songs sound pleasantly different which attracted the listeners. She secured her place in the top twenty by singing “Mayaluko Maya Meetho” in duet round. She had covered up for her partner's mistake and it was noticed and appreciated by the judges.

By now she was being recognized and stopped by strangers to wish her. She says, “That gave me a feeling of responsibility. I felt that I should work even harder to be able to meet the audience's expectation. To reach the Top Ten, we were supposed to prove ourself in three different types of songs.”

She was a bit laid back till then, but after that, she did her Reeyaz regularly and never missed a class in DO RE MI.

“My teachers Ishwar Amatya and Bhanubhakta Pokharel guided and inspired me”



She sang Tara Devi's classic “Ukalee Oraleeharuma” in modern semi classical, Reema Gurung's “Lahuredai” in folk and Naleena Chitrakar's “Gori Kaali” in pop category, came out with flying colors and secured therself in the much coveted Top Ten. The judges had praised her whole heartedly and accepted that she had unlimited potential.

By this time, she was a hot favorite amongst the viewers. Shopkeepers around the town had started to cut and paste her pictures from newspapers in their shops. Her fans had started showering her with emails in the program's id, not only congratulating her but even to propose marriage.

Top Ten were supposed to sing in Aalokshree's new album. After she recorded her own song, “Sayyoun Juni”, Aalokshree said that it was hard to believe she was a new comer. She had performed like a seasoned veteran.

Tope Ten went out for open concerts around Kathmandu, Bhaktapur and Banepa. Once again her popularity amongst the crowd was confirmed. Indira Jocy performed in front of thousands of viewers with confidence. Banners and placards with her name could be seen in the crowd. Many came with gift and cards for her. Al the ten were popular but Indira stole the show. After a concert at Bhrikutimandap, Indira had to be escorted away by security to save her from the mass of excited autograph hunters.

“Melawa Devi was a renowned classical singer respected by veteran Indian contemporary singers. This is a very complicated and hard song. Normally other singers can not even attempt this. The way you did justice to it is worth applauding. I am confident that your voice will cover the country from Mechi to Mahakali”

Her album “Step-1” is also in the making which is expected to hit the market by February 14, 2006. [edit] Teaching Drum Outdoor School

‘’’Teaching Drum Outdoor School’’’ is a non-profit wilderness skills school located in Three Lakes, Wisconsin. The school consists of an administrative center, sometimes referred to by the Algonquian name Nad’mad’ewining, a small wilderness camp (Mashkodens), and a larger primitive camp (Nishnajida).

[edit] Wilderness Guide Program

The heart of Teaching Drum is a year-long wilderness immersion program where students live continuously in a primitive Northwoods camp replete with wigwams, lean-tos and other primitive structures while learning and applying traditional hunter-gatherer crafts and skills such as tracking, foraging, shelter building, canoeing, and hide tanning. Students in the course, which is named the Wilderness Guide Program, also focus on improving interpersonal skills, and on developing wilderness and self-awareness through methods such as communal clan living, talking circles, dream analysis, sweat lodge ceremonies, and a general sharing of observations. The course takes place at the school’s primitive camp named Nishnajida, which is near a small lake in a headwaters area of the Nicolet National Forest. The course is run under the expert guidance of Tamarack Song. There are typically 10-15 students accepted into the course each year. The school has been running the course since 1999.

[edit] Approach

In comparison with other wilderness skills schools Teaching Drum has an unorthodox approach. This is reflected in the school’s insistence that it is more of a “Native Lifeway” school than a traditional primitive skills school. The emphasis is on holistic experiential learning, spiritual healing, and the development of awareness. This is seen as being more effective than individually learning isolated skills out of context. In fact, heightened awareness is seen as critical to the effective learning and application of wilderness skills.

[edit] Other Activities

Approximately 10 staff people also work and live at the school’s administrative center, Nad’mad’ewining. They mainly give support to the year-long program, but also work on wilderness skills, writing, and publishing projects throughout the year.

Adjacent to the administrative center, some staff and former Wilderness Guide Program students live at a primitive camp, Mashkodens. The school also hosts month-long summer internships here.

Saal is a community in the administrative district Northern Pomerania in Mecklenburg-Lower Pomerania (Germany). The community is under administration of the small city of Barth. Chow Nasty is a blues-rock trio based in San Francisco, composed of singer/guitarist Damon Harris, bassist Joey Enos, and multi-instrumentalist Zac Hewitt. Formed in Chicago in 2003, the band was created after Enos had expressed interest in starting a group whose songs were based around simple, primal chants. Originally the band included a fourth member, drummer Sven Zeissner, but immigration issues forced him out of the country and the remaining members replaced him with a drum machine, affectionately titled Sven 2.0. Frequently compared to Liquid Liquid, the Make-Up and Jon Spencer Blues Explosion, Chow Nasty stands apart from most rock groups because of their unique sonic structure, which in addition to the standard guitar and bass includes the aforementioned drum machine, as well as a Farfisa organ, synthesizer, and harmonica, all of which are commandeered by Hewitt.

Harris, Enos, and Hewitt, all native Californians, moved back to their home state at the start of 2004, and quickly gained a reputation as one of the best live acts in the Bay Area and became a favorite of local club bookers who needed strong local support for diverse touring acts, including !!!, Cameo, Chromeo, MC Hammer, Louis XIV, Very Be Careful, the Bellrays, Death From Above 1979, Morris Day and the Time, the Soledad Brothers, the Ohio Players, and the Eagles of Death Metal.

After opening for Chromeo in San Francisco in July of 2004, the band was asked to play with them the following night in Los Angeles, and the show proved to be wildly important for the young group. Rivera eventually became their manager in October of 2005, and Laursen would direct their first electronic press kit, or EPK, in March of 2006.

With their strong Bay Area following and a burgeoning LA fanbase, the band spent much of 2005 touring up and down the West Coast, opening an old-school funk show at Concord Pavillion, playing a sold-out show with Junior Senior at Silver Lake club Spaceland, and doing a show at the historic Fillmore in San Francisco. They were also picked to be an official band of popular energy drink Sparks, along with hotly-tipped acts like the Locust and the Fever. In October of 2005 the band shot a video for their song "Ungawa," after being approached by the directing team of Ally and Helder, who'd filmed the clip for "Van Nuys (Es Very Nice)" by Los Abandoned. In January of 2006 the video for "Ungawa" had release dance parties at Mighty in San Francisco (sponsored by humor weekly The Onion) and at Little Pedro's in Los Angeles (sponsored by online hip-hop site Fusicology), each night featuring a performance by Chow Nasty as well as a line-up of local DJs, including Wolf, who spun at both shows. In March of 2006 the group recorded their debut album at Prairie Sun Studios, the rural ranch recording spot that's a favorite of Tom Waits. Featured on the album is the track "I Was Wrong," a duet between Harris and Lisa Kekaula, singer of the Bellrays and Grammy-winning vocalist with Basement Jaxx. The same month Chow Nasty was selected to headline the annual happy hour show as part of the weeklong San Francisco music festival Noise Pop Festival.

[edit] [(Fulton Street Elevated)]

The Fulton Street El was an Elevated Railroad built by the Union Elevated Rairoad and leased to the Brooklyn Elevated Railroad for operation. Later these companies combined as the Brooklyn Union Elevated Railroad. In 1900 The Brooklyn Union Elevated Railroad was absorbed in to the BRT (Brooklyn Rapid Transit), and its later the BMT (Brooklyn Manhattan Transit), successor to the BRT. The lined serviced neighborhoods in Brooklyn and Queens from the 1880’s until the 1950’s.

The Elevated originally started at two seprate terminuses, one at Park Row and Manhattan, and one at Sands Street in Brooklyn. From their the line proceeded down Fulton Street in to Brooklyn. In the 1910’s during the Dual Contracts of both the IRT and and BMT lines, the Fulton Street El was extended down Fulton Street in to eastern sections of Brooklyn and eventually down Pitkin and Liberty Avenues in to Ozone Park, Queens were it terminated at Lefferts Avenue.

The Els demise came in the form of the IND Fulton Street Subway which was built in the 1936, and directly paralled the El from Sands Street station to the Rockaway Avenue station in Brooklyn. On the day that New York City acquired all the properties of the BMT and the IRT, June 1, 1940, service from Fulton Ferry and Park Row to Rockaway Avenue was abandoned. Service remained from Rockaway Avenue to Lefferts Avenue in which lead to an interesting oddity. Commuters from Queens and Eastern Brooklyn would have to ride the remaining portion of the El to Rockaway Avenue and then get off their trains and transfer to the IND subway at Rockaway Avenue to continue on in to Manhattan, a major inconvenience. According to nyssubway.org "There was also a 14th Street-Fulton service, which ran from 8th Avenue IND subway in Manhattan via the 14th Street Canarsie line to the Atlantic Avenue station in which the Canarsie El, Fulton Street, and the Broadway Jamaica Els all convened together. From there trains switched onto the Fulton Street El to Lefferts Avenue (later Boulevard). There was a non-rush hour cutback of El service to Eastern Parkway in 1952, with Rockaway Avenue served in rush hours only."

During the Post War years the subway under Fulton Street was extended reaching the Atlantic Avenue station in 1948 and later to Euclid Avenue in Brooklyn. Another planned extension would take the subway right under the remaining portion of the El on Liberty Avenue in Queens, making the rest of the El obsolete. However, in 1955 the transit authority took over the Long Island Railroads Rockaway line and was anxious to connect it to the IND Eighth Avenue/Fulton Street Line. Spending much of it money renovating the Rockaway line, the city decided to connect the Fulton Street Subway with the remaining portion of the El. On April 26, 1956, a connection was opened from the IND Fulton Street Subway line at Euclid Avenue to the Fulton Street El at the 80 Street/Hudson Avenue station, and the "A" train began running via this connection through to Lefferts Boulevard. The section from Rockaway Avenue to Hudson Avenue was then abandoned and torn down. Two months later, the connection from Rockaway Blvd. to the old LIRR Rockaway branch was opened and IND service to the Rockaway began.

Today the Fulton Street El section over Liberty Avenue in Queens from the 80th Street/Hudson Avenue Station to Lefferts Boulevard is all that remains of the Fulton Street El.

[edit] Deductive Closure Principle

The Deductive Closure Principle is a philosophical arguement that takes this form

Premise: If you know some proposition P. Premise: If you know that p entails some proposition Q

Conclusion: Then you also know Q

This arguement is used in the philosophy of skepticism for the support of Descartes' Dreaming Argument and the more modern Pollock's Brain In A Vat Argument.

[edit] Strike Team (television)

The Strike Team is an elite group of LA police officers featured on the FX cop drama The Shield. Based off the real-life CRASH gang unit, the Strike Team consisted of corrupt LAPD cops who utilized various morally-questionable tactics to bring in criminals at the cost of their own morality. The Strike Team was created by Shawn Ryan.


== Origins ==

The Strike Team is a brutal, four-man task force created to combat gang-related crimes in the fictional LA neighborhood of Farmington (The Farm). The ST served as a sort of first-wave assault team for the neighborhood's experimental Farmington Division (The Barn). In reality, the Strike Team was created by crooked Assistant Chief Ben Gilroy as a vehicle for his friend Detective Vic Mackey (also giving Mackey the authority to recruit his own teammates for the team) and through which he can carry out his malicious plans. Its members consist of:

== Trauma ==

The Strike Team was a guise for its members' use of corrupt police tactics to inflict their own type of street justice and/or for their own advancements. Suspicious of their motives, then-Captain David Aceveda recruited Detective Terry Crowley to join the Strike Team to expose them for corruption.When members Detective Mackey and Vendrell discovered this, Mackey killed Crowley. Since then, the Strike Team used increasingly more morally-ambigious methods to bust criminals, blurring the line of cop, criminal, and vigilante. At the end of Season Two, the Strike Team succeeded in ripping off an Armenian Mob Money Train. The aftershocks of this carried over into Season Four. The season chronicled the various personal and professional consequences that troubled the Strike Team as a result. Among them included Detective Tavon Garris getting taken off duty thanks to Detective Vendrell's jealousy and racism and the threat of Armenian hitman Margos Dezerian who was sent to kill them once their involvement in the Money Train ripoff was revealed. Despite Detective Mackey's success at eradicating Margos' threat, he couldn't prevent the Strike Team from falling apart, the aftermath of a falling-out between Vendrell and Lemansky and Mackey. (Due to his failed efforts at trying to bust the Strike Team, Aceveda planted a type of fail-safe device that declared if one person left the mostly four-man Strike Team, the team was forced to disband. In this case, that man was Detective Lemansky.) However, Mackey succeeded in convincing new Farmingto Division Captain Monica Rawling and the other former Strike Team members including newcomer Army to come back to Farmington.
