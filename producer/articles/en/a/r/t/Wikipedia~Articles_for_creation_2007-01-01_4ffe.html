[edit] MUTT (MUD Client)

Mutt™
Developer: Bob Free
Latest release: 0.1L / August 20, 1996
OS: Windows
Use: MUD client
Website: MUTT™ - Multi-User Trivial Terminal

Mutt™ is a text-based MUD client for Windows systems. It was written by Bob Free in 1994 as the first Windows-based MUD client.

This should not be confused with an open source e-mail client that was released a year later bearing the same name.


[edit] Overview

Like most other MUD clients, Mutt™ supports triggers, and scriptable actions.

What makes Mutt™ notable is the fact that it fits in a 32K binary footprint, and that is supported multi-threading in Windows 3.1, before Microsoft provided threading support in it's 16-bit operating systems.

Mutt™ supports a C-like scripting language called MUTTER™.

[edit] System Requirements

Mutt™ runs on all versions of Windows that support Winsock, including Win3.1

[edit] MUTTER™

MUTTER™ is a C-like scripting language that allows users to automatically respond to trigger events occuring on a connected MUD. Deleted copyvio material

[edit] 3rd Party Add-ons

Kynn Bartlett has provided TinyMUTT utilities for TinyMUSH and FurryMuck MUDs.

Etienne Varloot has provided MUTT™ Compagnion to provide macro aliasing.

Pete McVay has provided a MUTT™ WinHelp file.

[edit] tamcock

The tamcock is a near-marathon distance run traversing the roads that surround two large lakes in the greater Meadville (Pennsylvania) area. It's name is derived from the first syllable of the first lake encountered (Lake Tamarack) and the last syllable of the second lake encountered (Lake Woodcock). The total distance of the route is approximately 24-miles. The run was first completed in the year 2005 by a group of three runners - the fourth failed to finish, bowing out at mile 12. This run spans a variety of elevations and passes through several scenic county parks in the tranquil rural countryside of Crawford County. [3]

OVERVIEW
Callidus Software (NASDAQ: CALD) is an American manufacturer of software and services for Enterprise Incentive Management (EIM) and Sales Performance Management (SPM). The company was founded in 1996 and is headquartered in San Jose, California, USA. Callidus produces a range of enterprise software and services products, including software that helps companies to improve business results and shareholder value through effective management of compensation programs and sales operations. The Callidus TrueComp software suite is the foundation of EIM and SPM systems in many large companies to align business activities toward the goal of profitability.

The company helped to pioneer the EIM category with the launch of TrueComp in the spring of 1999. Callidus followed the TrueComp launch with a series of products built on the platform, including TrueInformation™, TrueResolution™ and TrueAnalytics™. Callidus launched an on-demand service in 2006 powered by Sun’s Grid computing initiative that makes the Callidus platform available to mid-sized companies. In addition, the company offers professional services to help clients effectively organize incentive structures and sales operations.

Callidus Software completed an initial public offering in November 2003 and is currently traded on the Nasdaq National Stock Market under the symbol CALD. In late 2005, Robert Youngjohns, former head of global sales at Sun Microsystems (SUNW) and IBM veteran (IBM), joined the company as chairman and CEO. The company has partnered with some of the world’s largest technology consultancies, integrators and software vendors, including Accenture, Deloitte Consulting, IBM and SAP AG.

AfriZiki is a pioneering African music distribution service established in 2006 by two gentlemen from Kenya, Kyalo Kibua and James Muriithi. AfriZiki’s founders hope ultimately to increase the popularity of pan-African music across the continent of Africa, as well as the rest of the world, motivated by not only by altruistic ideals, but by their great passion for African music, as well as their fervent desire to expose the vast trove of magnificent but unheralded African music to a far wider audience.

The first such distribution service for African music, AfriZiki is intended to level the playing field for talented but struggling African musical artists, allowing them to compete directly with more established performers. One of AfriZiki’s fundamental goals is to provide emerging African musical artists the means to overcome many of the challenges and obstacles that have traditionally beset the African music industry, significantly increasing their chances for success. AfriZiki allows African musical artists to exercise far more control over their career destinies than ever before. AfriZiki does this by:

• Allowing African musical artists to market their music via a simple, direct, widely-used distribution channel- the Internet • Significantly reducing production and distribution costs, and minimizing administrative overhead through online digital distribution of music • Allowing African musical artists to retain full ownership of their works, and to maximize the profits they receive from them • Reducing the potential for “pirating” and copyright infringement by making music widely available to listeners at low costs • Giving listeners the flexibility to preview individual tracks prior to downloading them, so they can purchase the specific music they like • Allowing African musical artists to access to information about their fans’ musical tastes, using free AfriZiki customized reports

• Allowing African musical artists to network with interested individuals, groups, and organizations via e-mail to promote their works in a variety of venues and settings • Allowing African musical artists to access meaningful statistics about their listening audiences that can help them plan, organize, and promote more effectively and efficiently • Expanding African musical artists’ potential listening audiences to global proportions

For more information, including how to partner with AfriZiki as a fan and listener, a contributing artist, a commercial distributor, or a corporate sponsor, please visit the AfriZiki website at www.afriziki.com, send an e-mail to info@afriziki.com, or contact Greg Kozak at gkozak@starpower.net.

Eyes Beyond Seeing (1995) is a film starring Keith Hamilton Cobb (Andromeda, Noah's Arc, The Young and the Restless) as an enigmatic mental patient who claims to be the second coming of Jesus Christ. The movie is noteworthy for its portrayal of Christ as a black man.

Directed by Daniel Robert Cohn and produced by Randall Emmett (16 Blocks, Home of the Brave, Narc) the film aired on UPN as a movie of the week in 2000. It was shot on location at the Kings Park Psychiatric Center in Long Island, New York.

The film also features a cameo appearance by legendary comic Henny Youngman in his last movie role before he died.

[edit] Paper Faces

Genres House/Techno

Paper Faces is a remix project by the forming members of Zoot Woman, namely Adam Blake and Stuart Price. They began remixing in 2001. Some notable artist's remixes include Madonna, Scissor Sisters, Felix Da Housecat, Armand Van Helden as well as Zoot Woman.
