[edit] Paramus Park

This request for creation has been reviewed. The result is below.

If your request was declined and you want to make changes and try again, copy and paste your article into a new submission and make your changes there.


Paramus Park is a shopping mall located near Route 17 in Paramus, New Jersey. It was built on 66 acres of land in 1974 by the Rouse Company. Today, Paramus Park has 110 stores. Its anchor department stores are Macy's (was A&S until mid-1990s) and Sears. The waterfalls located in the center of Paramus Park was also referenced by Dean Friedman in his song Ariel, the only top 40 song to use the word Paramus. However, these waterfalls were removed when the mall was renovated in 2001, giving the Mall a sterile look.

[edit] Nordia

This request for creation has been reviewed. The result is below.

Nordia is a call-centre company operating in Canada. A partnership between Bell Canada Enterprises and Excell Global communications. Services provided include cellular phone, paging, and digital satellite customer service and activation, income tax software support, TTY operators, and many other services.

Based in Montreal, Quebec, Nordia also has offices in Quebec City, Sherbrooke, Kitchener and Corruna.

Nordia was formed in response to the competitive telecommunications industry changing, and the level of pay and benefits Bell Canada was paying its employees in this department was much higher than the industry norm for call centres.

[edit] Coastline Community College

If your request was declined and you want to make changes and try again, copy and paste your article into a new submission and make your changes there.

Coastline Community College has an international reputation as one of the nation's most innovative institutions. Founded in 1976, Coastline continues to deliver education through technology and a comprehensive curriculum.

 Unlike other colleges, Coastline does not have a traditional campus. Instead, instruction is offered in nine different cities within the college service area, at approximately 50 sites close to students' homes and workplaces. These teaching sites include banks, senior centers, high schools, office buildings, shopping malls, and many other facilities. In addition to these sites, the College has main area learning centers based in Costa Mesa, Fountain Valley, and Westminster. In 1997, the College opened a new center in Garden Grove which features the latest in technology, specialized labs, and large lecture facilities.

Coastline Community College fulfills its mission by providing:

-General education and transfer courses for a comprehensive Associate in Arts degree plus career and technical courses for occupational certificates;
-Learner-centered strategies supported by a full range of technology-mediated instruction (telecourses, internet, Pocket PC, CD-ROM, video conferencing, Seaport CMS etc.) for site-based and distance learning classes;
-A systematic assessment of student outcomes at the course, program, and institutional levels;
-Entrepreneurial activities and courses relevant to local and global educational, economic, and social needs;
-A collegial decision-making process that reflects the philosophy of participatory governance.

His early writings included "Potret Seorang Penyair Muda Sebagai Si Malin Kundang (1972)"(The Portrait of A Young Poet as Malin Kundang) and "Seks, Sastra, Kita (1980)" (Sex, Literature, Us). The magazine was forced to be closed down twice by the government during New Order era because of its vocal and criticizing voices to the autocratic and corrupt regime. Goenawan Mohamad was also famous for his brilliant short weekly articles at Tempo, "Catatan Pinggir" (translated and published in English as 'Sidelines'). The 'sidelines' had been compiled into 5 books until now.

                                                              [edit] USS Eurana

                                                              This request for creation has been reviewed. The result is below.


                                                              The Eurana was built in 1916 by the Union Iron Works (later called Bethlehem Shipbuilding, owned by Bethlehem Steel) in San Francisco, CA. The time period of construction was from January 1, 1916 to December 31, 1916. It was issued the hull number 120, and released to its new owner, The American South African Line, as a Passenger Freighter. Its weight was 6000 tons, and its displacement was 9300 tons. The length of the Eurana was 410; Beam 562; Draft 26; Speed 10kts; Complement 70; and Armament two 4 guns (after being acquired by the US Navy).

                                                              The US Navy acquired the Eurana on September 13, 1918, commissioning it the same day, with the command going to Lieut. Comm. J. Foster, USNRF. It operated for the Navy under the name USN Eurana, with the Hull # SP 1594, as a Section Patrol Craft. From October 4, 1918 to September 14, 1919, it made 4 voyages from east coast ports to carry passengers and cargo for the Army Expeditionary Force and the Army of Occupation to LaPallice, Verdon, Bordeaux, St. Nazaire, and Brest, France. She was present in Brest on June 29, 1919 to honor President W. Wilson leaving France in the George Washington. The Eurana was decommissioned from the US Navy on October 9, 1919 at Norfolk, and returned to the Army for transfer to her owner.

                                                              The Euranas name was later changed to the Alamar, and was used in WWII. At Murmansk, on May 27, 1942, the Alamar was hit by several aerial bombs on her afterdeck and set afire after Stukas (dive bombers) attacked for 1 hours. Flames soared high from the high test gasoline stored topside. The Alamar fell back from the other ships, out of control, nearly colliding with another injured ship. At 12:30 pm, both ships slipped beneath the water without losing any sailors. The Alamar was actually scuttled.

                                                              On July 5, 1942, the Massmar, with survivors from the Alamar, hit a mine and was sunk. This also occurred at Murmansk. 23 of the Alamar crew survived.
