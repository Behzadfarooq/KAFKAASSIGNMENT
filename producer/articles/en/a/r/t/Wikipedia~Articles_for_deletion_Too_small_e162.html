[edit] Too small

As far as I can tell this article has no foundation in reality. In mathematical terms, it is meaningless. Therefore it should be deleted. Stefán Ingi 09:54, 5 January 2006 (UTC)

  • Delete as Complete, Total and Utter Bollocks. This proves that Wikipedia is unbounded, since for any stupid article x, there exists an even more stupid article y... Segv11 (talk/contribs) 10:00, 5 January 2006 (UTC)
  • Delete per nom. Essexmutant 11:34, 5 January 2006 (UTC)
  • This was marked for speedy deletion per CSD:G1 (patent nonsense) which it is not. (Patent nonsense is a jumble of characters and words with no meaning whatsoever.) I removed the tag. However I also vote to delete. Stifle 11:50, 5 January 2006 (UTC)
  • Definition in my opinion should be - smaller than is usable, adjective describing something that is not large enough for some context. —the preceding unsigned comment is by 24.225.83.207 (talk • contribs) 11:36, 5 January 2006 (UTC)
  • Delete per nom. -- jaredwf 14:42, 5 January 2006 (UTC)
  • Delete This is totally useless as a reference article. -- (aeropagitica)  15:52, 5 January 2006 (UTC)
  • Redirect to Infinitesimal, maybe? -- Plutor 17:48, 5 January 2006 (UTC)
  • Delete maybe, just maybe, Redirect as above. Dan 20:29, 5 January 2006 (UTC)
  • Delete, no redir.--SarekOfVulcan 01:41, 6 January 2006 (UTC)
  • Delete, no redirect. Infinitesimals denote quantities which are sufficiently small, not excessively small. Ryan Reich 01:09, 7 January 2006 (UTC)
  • Delete, do not redirect. -- Jitse Niesen (talk) 19:20, 8 January 2006 (UTC)
  • Delete: too big. Charles Matthews 22:22, 8 January 2006 (UTC)
  • Just delete, no redirect. --Chan-Ho (Talk) 09:03, 9 January 2006 (UTC)
