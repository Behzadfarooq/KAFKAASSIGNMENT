[edit] MARK W. LEE

Mark W. Lee (b.1950) is an American novelist, poet and playwright. He has worked as a war correspondent and some of these real-life experiences have appeared in his fiction.

Lee was born in Minneapolis, Minnesota. He attended Yale University where he became friends with the Pulitzer prize winning poet and novelist Robert Penn Warren. Lee dedicated his first novel to Warren. [1]

After graduating from Yale in 1973, Lee lived in New York City for several years where he worked as a taxi driver, a language teacher and a security guard. In New York, he became friends with artists Frank Moore and Lillian Mulero. His poetry and nonfiction appeared in the Atlantic Monthly, the Times Literary Supplement and a variety of literary journals.

Lee has always done elaborate research for his literary work. When writing a long poem about Henry Hudson, he walked alone down the eastern shore of Hudson Bay. His poem, Hudson Bay, 1611, was published in The Sewanee Review.

In the early 1980s, Lee traveled to East Africa where he worked as a foreign correspondent for Reuters and the London Telegraph. During the civil war that followed the fall of dictator Idi Amin, he was one of the few western journalists living in Uganda. After being expelled from the country for writing about military atrocities, Lee returned to the United States. He found that he could no longer write poetry and began writing plays and novels.

His first play, California Dog Fight, was set at an illegal dog fight the Sacramento delta. It premiered at the Manhattan Theatre Club {1985) and went on to an award-winning production at the Bush Theatre in London. Lee's next play, Rebel Armies Deep Into Chad, premiered at New Haven's Long Wharf Theatre in 1989. Rebel Armies is about the confrontation between two white journalists and two African prostitutes. It has been performed at many theatres throughout America. [2]

Lee's play, Pirates (1992) premiered at the South Coast Repertory Theatre in Costa Mesa where it won the American Express California Playwrights competition. An American Romance (1997) premiered at the Road Theatre in Los Angeles. It won 12 regional theatre awards. Lee's play, Century City (1998), premiered at the WPA Theatre in New York. The Private Room, Lee's controversial play set in the prison cells of Guantanamo, premiered at the New End Theatre in London in 2004. [3]

Mark Lee's first novel, The Lost Tribe, was published in 1998 by Picador USA. The book describes an epic journey of Africans and Americans looking for the contemporary descendants of the Lost Tribes of Israel.

In 2000, Lee traveled to East Timor and wrote articles about the civil war for the Atlantic Monthly and the Los Angeles Times.

Lee's second novel, The Canal House, was published by Algonquin Books of Chapel Hill in 2003. The critically praised novel describes the dangerous world of war correspondents and aid workers. It is set in Africa and East Timor.

Mark Lee has been deeply involved in human rights activities for PEN, the international writers' organization. He traveled to Ethiopia to help imprisoned writers, started the Seattle Chapter of PEN and obtained group health insurance for members of both PEN America and PEN USA. His work appears in Politically Inspired a collection of essays and short stories about the Iraq war published by MacAdam/Cage in 2003.

Lee currently lives in London. He has two children.

[edit] Wellsite Geologist

A wellsite geologist is a scientist who works in the field in the petroleum industry. He/she usually has a formal degree in geology and several years oilfield experience which is usually gained through working as an office geologist for an oil company and specialized courses in petroleum geology. The job entails long hours at the well site, and may be international in scope. Some of the duties of the wellsite geologist are viewing cuttings samples through a microscope, advising on the drilling process, correlation of electric logs, and producing a strip log of the lithology encountered. At the wellsite he is known as the mudlog geologist, or mudlogger. The wellsite geologist is normally in charge of the well testing process and accounts for the oil and gas encountered while drilling. He is assisted by the company man and the drilling engineer and may supervise a crew of directional drillers. While the company man is in charge of the drilling process, it is the wellsite geologist who the investors are most interested in due to the fact that a hole in the ground is worth nothing until it encounters petroleum in the form of oil or gas. After the oil and/or gas has been found, the petroleum engineers take over the job of producing the well. An experienced wellsite geologist can earn several hundred thousand dollars a year during boom times.

[edit] Widham, Wiltshire

The name Widham comes up on many web sites but with no information on the place, so the following is to give a little insight and history of where we are.

We are on current OS maps and maps dating back to the 1700’s. To include Andrews’ and Dury’s Map of Wiltshire, 1773, Andrews’ and Dury’s Map of Wiltshire, 1810 and the 1896 OS map.

Widham is a small hamlet which is now encompassed within the village and Parish of Purton, Wiltshire.

Originally Widham was a few houses along the Highway and parts of Witts Lane and the Toll House at Collins Lane, with Widham Common in the centre. Purton eventually engulfed the Hamlet and only one small area is now left known as ‘Widham’ and this was split in two during the mid 19th Century when the railway line was built between Swindon and Gloucester.

Widhill, in the Parish of Cricklade, can be found approximately 4 miles to the north near to the A419 at Blunsdon.

During the Enclosure Act the Common at Widham was Awarded to the Earl of Shaftsbury, with 'foot rights' to the cottages around the common to the highway which had been a private road with Tolls collected at the Toll House. The highway at this time became a public road however Tolls continued to be collected into the 1800's.

Pincocks Orchard, being one of the last remaining private Orchards in Purton and dating from the 1700's lies behind the houses on the south of the railway. Originally Thomas Rincock's Orchard and now reduced in size to around half an acre. Planted with Greengage and other fruit trees.

Meaning of the word ‘Widham’ could come from two words - Druid and Hamlet.

The word 'Dru- wid' means 'oak-knowledge' and 'Ham' meaning Homestead or pennisular. (On the Andrews & Drurys Maps, 1773 & 1810, of Wiltshire, Widham can be seen within the centre of two small rivers/streams, which lead into the River Ray, which feeds into the River Thames at Cricklade.) The English word 'wisdom' traces its origins to the Primitive German word Wid, meaning 'to know'. Wid in turn, is derived from the Sanskrit word Veda, meaning 'External Knowledge'

[edit] Lifelink Samaritans

In 1966 a group of concerned citizens of Launceston recognised the need for a community based, non-religious, non-political organisation to be established offering friendship to people suffering loneliness, alcoholism, suicidal tendencies, financial difficulties and other problems causing emotional hardship.

Following extensive discussions and deliberations as to how best to set up such a service, in December 1968 a limited roster of specially trained volunteers commenced a telephone befriending service with the eventual aim of establishing a 24 hour caring, confidential and non-judgemental service.

1989 saw a Face-to-face befriending service introduced to provide a short term caring relationship which aims to help people to discover and use the strengths within themselves, as well as the resources available within the community.

In November 1996 a “1300” telephone number was introduced to cater to callers from beyond the Launceston area, extending this service to callers anywhere in Tasmania for the cost of a local call.

Lifelink Samaritans are active in service to youth particularly in the area of suicide awareness and prevention and have produced a booklet entitled Youth Suicide—a Guide for Parents as well as a Youth Card which provides telephone contacts who will listen and help our young people.

Lifelink Samaritans Tasmania Incorporated has a strong relationship with The Samaritans in the UK and has a “twin” relationship with the Northampton branch of the organisation there. We are also forging a stronger relationship with Samaritans in Western Australia and look forward to developing this in the near future.

In recent times the organisation undertook to man a site at the annual Agfest, which allowed us to disseminate information to a wider audience, both local and interstate, and provide literature covering the services we currently offer to all Tasmanians in need of emotional support.

Nominal funding assistance is received from the Tasmanian Government, but the organisation relies heavily on the generosity of the Tasmanian people to sustain its services. All members of Lifelink Samaritans are volunteers and no honoraria are paid to office bearers.

[edit] Ercole Spada

Ercole Spada (August 26, 1938) is an Italian automobile designer. His most notable designs were made in the 1960s, for the Zagato design studio house, where Spada was chief stylist. During this period some of the most notable sportcars by Aston Martin, Ferrari, Maserati, as well as Alfa Romeo, Abarth, Fiat and Lancia were clothed by Spada's designs.

Spada earned a degree in Industrial Engineering from all'istituto tecnico Feltrinelli in 1956. Following a military service, Spada joined Zagato, in February 1960. The first design created by Spada for Zagato was the Aston Martin DB4 GT Zagato. Many avantgarde yet instantly-classic designs were to follow, soon becoming cornerstones of automotive design. Spada's designs were acknowledged as seminal by his fellow designers as well as by generetions of new designers; One example of the attraction of Spada's work was that of the Mazda MX-3, which aimed to capture the magic of the Alfa Romeo Giulia GTZ design, according to its creators. Shortly before leaving Zagato, Spada designed one of the most modern looking cars of the era, the Alfa Romeo Junior Z, as well as the popular and dramatic Lancia Fulvia Sport.

Spada joined Ford in 1970 to become chief designer at the Italian Ghia design house. This led to the creation of the ill-fated Ford GT70, which did not enter production at the last moment.

After leaving Ford and following a short stay in Audi, Spada joined BMW design center as chief stylist in 1976. During his stay with BMW, Spada created two major all-new designs, the BMW E32 7-series (1987–1994), and the BMW E34 5-series (1989-1996). Both embraced similar classic yet progressive lines, contributing to the success of the German company.

In 1983 Spada returns to lead an Italian design house, this time I.DE.A. where he designs a strings of compact and luxury cars, for Fiat – the groundbreaking Tipo and Tempra siblings, the Lancia Dedra and Delta II as well as the Kappa. Other projects include the Alfa Romeo 155 and the Daihatsu Move. During his stay in I.DE.A. Spada had competed and won over major design contracts from Fiat, putting him in direct competition with his fellow Italian design maverick, Giorgetto Giugiaro, born on the same year and on the same month as Spada.

Returning to Zagato in 1992, Spada brought with him new creative energy, which led to the introduction of the Ferrari FZ93, based on a regular 512 TR mechanics, as well as other notabe designs.

Currently, Spada continues to work as a designer. He has joined his son, Paulo Spada, to create Spadaconcept, a new design house aimed at automotive and industrial design.

[edit] Richard Ellis, Photographer

Richard Ellis was born 1960, grew up in West Palm Beach, Florida. Attended Southern Methodist University, 1978 and George Washington University, 1979-1982. He began his professional career in 1979 working as a freelance photographer for United Press International in Washington, DC. In 1984, he joined Reuters, the British based global news service, as a staff photographer in Hong Kong covering the People’s Power Revolution in the Philippines and dozens of stories throughout Asia. In 1986, he became chief photographer, South Asia based in New Delhi covering major news throughout the region including the Soviet war in Afghanistan becoming the first western photographer allowed into Kabul. Ellis sent to China for the Tiananmen protests hid on the first public bus allowed into the square following the massacre providing the first pictures of the aftermath. Posted to China in 1989, he continued to cover post-Tiananmen China and was the last foreign correspondent to see Deng Xiaoping alive. Nominated for a Pulitzer Prize as part of the Reuters team covering Tiananmen. In 1991, assigned to cover the 1st Gulf War, Ellis worked outside the controlled news pool traveling the desert of Saudi Arabia providing uncensored images. He was nominated for the Pulitzer a second time as part of the Reuters coverage during the war. In 1991 he established the Reuters UK photo service before moving to Moscow in 1992 as chief photographer, former Soviet Union. While covering the collapse of the communist regime in Afghanistan he captured images of the execution of a secret policeman that resulted in a third nomination for a Pulitzer Prize, the first for his solo images. Ellis left Reuters in 1994 after exposing faked pictures shot by Russian photographers for an American news magazine. Returning to Washington, DC in 1994, he worked as a freelance photographer for the Sygma News Agency, covering the Presidency of Bill Clinton. While working in Washington, Ellis started the first digital, Internet based news photo agency called Newsmakers in 1995. In 1999, Newsmakers was acquired by Getty Images and became the foundation of the Getty Images News Service, which Ellis ran until 1991. Ellis continued with Getty Images in a variety of roles becoming Senior Vice President of Business Development in 2004. His photographic work has been published extensively in every major magazine and newspaper worldwide. Richard Ellis has the unique distinction of being the only individual to ever create a global news photo wire service. He currently lives with his wife and two daughters in Charleston, SC.

Starting in the 9th century Scotland, it takes readers on a journey through different times and places, reviving remarkable moments and important events in mankind’s history. After a twelve-century long ride throughout the world, it brings us back to the very same place where it all began: Scotland, where in the year 2020 the saga’s epilogue is revealed.

The blend of historical facts with fiction is a vivid element throughout the entire series until Book 7, in which past, present and future merge to create the final confrontation blending history, prophecy and fiction as never before!

Orlando Paes Filho, the creator of the saga, first envisioned Angus in 1978 and has not stopped working since. Après la seconde guerre mondiale (WWII), la France avait besoin d'un Jet d'entraînement. Battu par le Fouga Magister en 1953 dans le concours de la Marine Française, le MS.755 Fleuret ressucita en tant que MS.760 Paris IR (conçu par René Gauthier), avion militaire à quatre-sièges de communications à grande vitesse et d'entraînement avancé. Il a été employé intensivement par les français, Néerlandais et d'autres militaires européens entre 1959 et 1997. En 1955, une association de courte durée est menée avec Beechcraft pour lancer le Paris comme Jet d'affaires sur le marché américain mais celui ci fut bientôt éclipsé par le "Hot-Rod" Model 23 de chez Lear.

Le 26 juillet, 1954, le prototype MS.760A Paris a décollé pour son premier vol avec Jean Cliquet aux commandes. Avec son stabilisateur vertical en forme de T, des ailes basses, et deux réacteurs Turboméca Marboré de 400kg montées côte à côte à l'arrière à l'intérieur du fuselage, le Paris a offert une plateforme caractérisée par une stabilité inhérente. L'avion possède quatre sièges, deux à l'avant et deux à l'arrière, un train d'atterrissage escamotable à trois roues . Le 18 juillet 1956, le gouvernement français a commandé 50 avions dont 14 pour la l'Aéro-Navale, au fabricant basé à Tarbes; Morane-Saulnier. Le premier avion a été livré le 9 février, 1959 à l'aéroport naval (N.A.S.) Dugny-Le Bourget, avant d'aller au C.E.P.A. (Centre Expérience Pratique Aéronautique) en 1959-60, pour subir les vols d'essais nécessaires au développement de programmes de formation et de matériels. Il a été également acheté par plusieurs pays à travers le monde comme le Brésil et l'Argentine (48 avions ont été construits sous licence à Cordoue en Argentine).

Le MS.760B Paris II avec diverses améliorations systèmes et des réservoirs à carburant intégrés dans les ailes, a volé pour la première fois le 12 décembre 1960. En 1961, les usines ont commencé la production du Paris II, équipé de bi-réacteur Marboré IV de 480 kg, de réservoirs à carburant de bout d'aile, d'une climatisation et d'un plus grand compartiment à bagages. Le 24 février 1964, une version à six passagers, sous le nom de MS.760C Paris III a fait son premier vol, mais ne fut jamais commercialisé. La production de Paris II a cessé, et la production de Paris III n'a jamais commencé. Un total de 165 avions (Paris I et II) ont été produits pour les Armée de l'Air (36 avions) et Marine française (14 avions) ainsi que les Armées de l'Air du Brésil, de l'Argentine et du Paraguay.

Les 14 "Morane" (le surnom de l'avion dans l'Aéro-Navale) ont été assignés a l'escadrille 11.S à partir du 9 février, 1959. Le dernier avion, le numéro 88, a été fourni le 27 juillet 1961 . En 1965, le MS.760 numéro 48 a été brièvement affecté à l'escadrille 3.S basé au N.A.S d'Hyères, qui avait déjà piloté sur MD.312 Flamant, MS.733 Alcyon, le MH.1521m Broussard, le SO.30p Bretagne et le Br.1050 Alizé. Un Morane s'est écrasé le 4 janvier 1968 à Rennes. À compter de 1970,les 12 avions restants ont été affectés à l'escadrille 2.S basée au N.A.S. de Lann-Bihoué. En mai 1972 ils ont été envoyés au S.R.L. Le 1er septembre 1981 cette unité est devenue l'escadrille 57.S (celle ci a été dissoute depuis la fermeture du N.A.S. De Port-Lyautey le 15 janvier 1962). Huit MS.760 Paris étaient sur les roles de l'unité, à côté trois de Dassault Falcon 10 MER. Après 40 ans de bons et loyaux services, ces vénérables travailleurs de force ont été mis à la retraite en octobre 1997 au N.A.S Landivisiau. Les huit derniers avions étaient les numéros 32, 40, 41, 42, 47, 85, 87 et 88.

Leurs missions étaient:

- La formation IFR et tous temps des pilotes de Super-Étendard et Crusader.

- La formation avancée pour de nouveaux pilotes et formation de compétence pour d'autres pilotes.

- A.L.P.A. (Amiral commandant Les PORTE-AVIONS et Aéronautique Navale) et liaisons des 1ère et 2ème région aérienne .

Deux avions (les No31 et 33) sont conservés au musée de des traditions de l'aviation navale de Rochefort . Le No32 est possédé par l'association "Armor aéro passion", le No40 par le musée de l'Air et de l'Espace du Bourget , le No42 par le lycée technique Tristan Corbière, le No46 par le musée de Savigny-les-Beaunes, le No85 par l'association "Les Ailes de l'Aéro" et les No87 et No88 ont été vendus à des propriétaires privés américains .

John Ayes, founder of Hyper Visualism, A New Genre A New Perspective.

Before Hyper Visualism was the work to reconsile and experiment with his chosen medium to find a correct way to express with color upon his canvases.

AARP Segunda Juventud/Encore link: http://www.aarpsegundajuventud.org/english/nosotros/2005-AS/05AS_browneayes.html


It all began with monchromatic paintings in burnt umber, blue or black. It was eventually discovered that one could elicit from the medium its full tonal scale and half tones without having to add titanium or flake white. The process was simple. By adding thinner to the medium one could work subjects from their darkest value to their lightest values. The end result yielded a painting that was close to figurative art with some degree of photo realism. The next phase was difficult. It entailed the addition of other colors within the same work. These were also used monochromatically.

Successive canvases were experiments entailing the addition of white colors to create tints. This technique was used sparingly because in the artist's view the colors loose some of their quality of transparency.

Despite Ayes color blindness of the primary colors his art has been published locally and nation wide in magazines, news papers, showcased on Floridian local TV and is within the collection of Governor Jeb Bush and Florida Attorney General, Charlie Crist.

Ayes Most recent exhibit was at the Orlando Main Public Library. The exhibition celebrated Hispanic Heritage Month.

Jon started his fascination with TV and video in the year 2002 releasing several shows titled "Talking With Jon", with a primary audience first comprised of friends and classmates. The show still exists today and thanks to faster video formats available in 2005 and 2006, Jon was able to go world wide with his work and continues to gain a loyal following.

[edit] Google Video

Jon is known best for his massive archive of useless videos on Google Video. In 2006 he stirred up the gaming community by releasing several videos about the Sony PSP and Nintendo DS wherein he used 15 minute long to make up false facts, citing "the radio" and "the news" as his sources of information. Fanboys were infuriated by the videos Jon released.

The notorius "gaming system videos" spread rapidly over gaming message boards and and various gaming websites, which lead to a lot of controversy.

The PSP and DS videos are only the tip of a very large iceberg. Jon has made a name for himself by way of having the most videos on google video and regardless of your search, you're likely to find something he posted. Almost all his videos are posted under the moniker "Moldy Toaster" and topics range from "things that rhyme with Paris Hilton" to "video game tips".

Jon is "probably" one of the most hated people on the Internet. A lot of people are strongly against Jon but its been said he has more followers than haters at this point.

Jon's video collection has grown so fast its hard to keep track of. Its estimated he has over 3000+ videos, but that's only an estimate. Jon may not be a Hollywood movie star but one thing is certain, he has made a name for himself and is quickly becoming an Internet sensation. Regardless of whether you hate or love him, he's here to stay and his success keeps growing.

Born Terence Alan MacNeil on June 13, 1944 in Wichita, Kansas. "It was raining babies" (said his father) that day in the hospital where Terry was born, so much so that Terry was abandoned on the stainless steel table he was left on while the presiding doctor was dealing with other births. Terry MacNeil changed his name to Nandi Devam in 1979 when he and his wife and two daughters formally converted to Hinduism, in a ceremony at the Kadavul Hindu Temple on the Garden Island of Kauai. Nandi Devam now owns Camel Records, which records and produces budding enthusiastic singer/songwriters. Gracious (or Gracious!) were a British quintet who operated during the early 1970s, when they released two albums, 'Gracious!' (released 1970 on Vertigo) and 'This Is... Gracious' (posthumously released in 1972 on the Philips International label). The band was formed in the mid-Sixties in Esher, a well-to-do spot south of London in Surrey's stockbroker belt, by guitarist Alan Cowderoy and drummer/vocalist Paul 'Sandy' Davis, who were at school together. This was prior to them adopting the name under which they recorded - they were first called Satan's Disciples (many years before punk/metal bands began using similarly lurid names), according to Cowderoy: "It was a Catholic school, so we thought this was the most contentious name we could come up with, and we played a couple of school concerts. It was an out and out pop group - we were just doing other people's songs, and then gradually we started putting in our own tunes. It was all covers in the first school concert - 'Sweets For My Sweet', all the popular hits of the day - and we went down fantastically well. That was probably in 1964 or '65". Schooldays over and several personnel changes later, keyboard player Martin Kitcat (his real name, apparently) joined first on piano and later, as eventual bass player Tim Wheatley recalled, a Hohner pianet, a trendy type of electric piano.
