[edit] Sigvard Arne Eklund

This request for creation has been reviewed. The result is below.


Dr. Sigvard Arne Eklund a former director of the IAEA security council born in 1911 in Kiruna, Sweden. He obtained the degree of Master of Science in 1936 and of Doctor of Science in 1946. From 1937 to 1945 he was first Assistant and then Senior Scientist at the Nobel Institute of Physics, and from 1946 to 1950, Senior Scientist at the Research Institute for National Defence in Stockholm. He was Assistant Professor of Nuclear Physics at the Royal Institute of Technology in Stockholm from 1946 to 1956. From 1950 to 1956, he was Director of Research at the then Swedish Atomic Energy Company (AB Atomenergi, Stockholm). Before his appointment as Director General of the IAEA in 1961, Dr. Eklund was Deputy to the Managing Director of AB Atomenergi from 1950, and also Director of the Reactor Development Division at AB Atomenergi from 1957. In 1957 he was Secretary General for the Second International United Nations Conference on the Peaceful Uses of Atomic Energy.

[edit] The Blind / The Sightless

This request for creation has been reviewed. The result is below.


The Sightless, also known as The Blind was written in 1890 by the Belgian playwright Maurice Maeterlinck. It is an intense one act play, which was one of the pieces mentioned in a list of Maeterlinck’s most characteristic plays. This list was highlighted in the speech given to honour Maeterlinck with the Nobel Prize in 1911. The play was originally written in French later to be translated into several different languages, as for example English and German. The piece is not only a study of human condition in general but it appears to confront its reader with the effect of hope on the latter. Unlike most other theatrical pieces The Sightless does not introduce its readers to readily shaped characters, but twelve depersonalised beings. These beings were not provided with names but titles describing their general condition, for instance the eldest blind man. This method of writing gives an audience, or theatre professional an opportunity to read between the lines and interpret the piece according to personal desire. Maeterlinck has since been considered as the founder of a new type of dramatic writing, Symbolism. Parallels have often been drawn with Samuel Beckett’s Waiting for Godot, which was produced in the mid 20th century and appears to construct a similar world of waiting and hoping, which is evident in The Sightless.

גנטיקה והתקרחות

ההתקרחות השכיחה ביותר בקרב גברים נקראת התקרחות אנדרוגנטית (Androgenetic Alopecia) , ובה איבוד השיער נגרם בשל רקע תורשתי-הורמונלי. במי שיש לו נטייה גנטית להקרחה, משפיעים ההורמונים הגבריים (האנדרוגנים) על זקיקי שיער. ההורמון האחראי על איבוד השיער בקרקפת הוא ה-'די הידרו טסטוסטרון'- DHT. הוא נקשר לקולטנים בזקיק השערה, וגורם להרס והזדקנות מוקדמת של הזקיק.

מכיוון שהשינויים הם מקומיים בשיער, הרי שרוב המקריחים יהיו בעלי פרופיל דם הורמונלי תקין.

התופעה קיימת גם בנשים, אם כי ההופעה הקלינית בהן שונה ופחות בולטת.

השכיחות באכלוסיה: גברים: נטייה זו קיימת בקרב כ-60% מכלל הגברים. התחלת התהליך היא בכל שלב אחרי גיל ההתבגרות, בישראל הממוצע הוא בגילאי 18-25 שנה. מכיוון שאיבוד השיער הדרגתי מאוד, הקרחת תגיע לביטוי מלא רק בשנות ה-40 לחיי הגבר הישראלי. נשים: בין 30% ל-40% מהנשים נושאות את הנטייה. ההופעה תחל בד"כ מאוחר מאשר בגברים- בגיל מעבר (הבלות), בעקבות השינוי ההורמונלי המאפיין גיל זה.

לא כל מי שנושא את הגן להתקרחות יאבד את שיערו, גם קצב וגיל ההתקרחות יהיו שונים מאדם לאדם. השוני נובע מההשפעות השליליות האחרות הפועלות על השיער, ודרגת החומרה שלהן.

בכ-80% ממקרי ההתקרחות הגנטית יש גם עלייה משמעותית בשומניות עור הקרקפת, המחמירה מאוד את הבעיה.

צורת ההתקרחות: רוב האנשים יתלוננו על שיער שבהדרגה מאבד מנפחו והופך לדק עד לקרחת. זהו תהליך איטי בד"כ, שעשוי לקחת שנים. לרוב רק לאחר פגיעה ב- 30%-35% מהשיער תורגש הירידה בנפח השיער.

המראה בגברים: בתחילה יש נסיגה של קו השיער הקדמי, בעיקר באזור המפרצים. בשלב מאוחר יותר נראה אזור עגול מקריח בקודקוד האחורי של הקרקפת הגברית. התקרחות זו עלולה להתרחב, והיא תמשך בהתחברות האזורים הקרחים מקדימה ומאחורה, ולאחר מכן אף הרחבתם לצדדים.

המראה בנשים: התופעה מאופיינת בשיער דליל ודק בכל הקרקפת, עם שימור של קו השיער הקדמי. זו הידלדלות שיער מפושטת, שתראה במהלך הזמן כ"שביל מתרחב".

מניעת התקרחות: הרקע הגנטי הוא רק גורם אחד בתהליך השלם של ההתקרחות. ניתן להתנגד לגנטיקה ולבלום את השפעתה: בדומה לנטייה תורשתית להשמנה, שניתן להתנגד לה ע"י תזונה נכונה, פעילות גופנית וכו'. אדם בעל נטייה לאיבוד שיער (רקע גנטי להתקרחות), אשר דואג לבטל השפעות שליליות מקומיות על שיערו, יצליח להשאיר את התכונה הזו רדומה, וכך לשמור על שיערו לשנים רבות. בכל מקרה של התקרחות, יש בעיה מקומית המשפיעה לרעה על תנאי המחייה של השיער, לכן יש להפנות את הטיפול אל הקרקפת ואל תנאי המחייה של השיער.

[edit] Javier Solis

Born Gabriel Siria Levario in Mexico City in September 1st 1931 , Javier Solis became known as The King of Boleros Rancheras. He is one of the most recognized Mariachi solo artists of all time in Mexico. Coming from humble beginnings in the famous “barrio de Tacubaya” at a young age Solis had to drop out of school to help support his family. He trained as an amateur boxer for six years with aspirations of turning pro but quit after his father urged him to choose a more "decent" career. As a young man he used to sing in local competitions for small prizes, but was eventually banned from participating because he so dominated the competition. At age 16, Solis went to Puebla, Mexico to sing with Mariachi Metepec, but it wasn't until years later when Julio Rodriguez of the trio Los Panchos heard him singing at a local bar that he got his first break as a recording artist. They took him to audition with CBS records where he signed a recording contract and cut his first album in 1950. His first hit, "Lloraras," came two years later and prompted his producer Felipe Valdes Leal to give him the name Javier Solis. The vocalist came to international acclaim in 1957, making appearances in the United States, Central, and South America. Solis was the first to sing songs in a style now known as "Boleros-Rancheras." He sang boleros typically associated with trio music but now accompanied by mariachis, also adding waltzes and tangos to his repertoire as a prolific interpreter of many styles of music. His hit songs include "Entre Sombras y Recuerdos” “Payaso”, “e te olvida”, “El loco”, “Me recordaras”, “Sombras”, “En mi viejo San Juan”.

Javier Solis gained interest in the cinema of his time & began his acting career in 1959; filming more than 20 movies with stars such as Maria Victoria, Lola Beltran, and Luis Aguilar. When his last film was Juan Pistolas was finished Javier started to complain about hard pains around his stomach area. He later was sent to Hospital Santa Fe in Meixco City where Javier Solis died in April 19th 1966 at age 34 from complications resulting from gall bladder surgery, Sadly leaving behind him a bittersweet musical legacy that ironcally was cut short due to his tragic passing. He is missed by many & he is still remembered by both the young & old & he lives through his music & will never be forgotten.

[edit] (Young) Pioneers

(Young) Pioneers was a Richmond, Virginia Punk Rock band that existed in one form or another from 1995-1999. They are noted for their politcal working class lyrics and soul inspired style to their music arrangements. The original lineup was Adam (Stackolee) Nathanson of Born Against fame on guitar, harmonica and lead vocals, Tim Barry of Avail fame on Bass and Brooks Headley on drums. They later replaced Tim Barry with Marty (Violence)Key on Bass and Fred (The Fist) LaPier on drums. (Young)Pioneers released several recordings the first being "First Virginia Volunteers" on Vermiform Records in 1995. Their next release "We March" added Marty Violence to the mix as the bands sound continued to evolve. The "Crime Wave" E.P. was the best received release on Vermiform Records as the bands sound really seemed to come together. The album cover "Crime Wave" was a play on The Impressions album cover for "Times Have Changed". The Band then signed on with Lookout! Records and released their classic 7in E.P. "On Trial" and a split 7' with Avail entitled "The Fall of Richmond". The next release for (Young) Pioneers was the "Free the (Young) Pioneers" Full length with their final drummer Fred The Fist. The album received some good reviews but the band broke up shortly after its release in January of 1999 playing their last show in Seattle that month. The band has toured with acts such as Avail, The Locust, Karp, The Peechees, Slaves and Modest Mouse.

Triplegia is a medical condition is which there is paralysis of three limbs. It is frequently associated with cerebral palsy, although other medical conditions, such as a stroke, can also lead to it. In cases of cerebral palsy it is often thought of as hemiplegia overlapping with diplegia or quadriplegia with much less involvement of one limb. In most cases both of the legs and one arm are affected, but both arms and one leg can be affected also. Resources for children with quadriplegia are generally the most beneficial for children with triplegia.

[edit] Herbie Hancock "My Point Of View"

"My Point Of View" was Herbie Hancock's second album for jazz label Blue Note as a band leader, and composer, which was originally released in 1963. Hancock composed all of the five songs on the original LP, although some arrangements were assisted by his backing band. It has been said of the songs on this album that they "do not conform to any style" (Ira Gitler), although the songs do seem to share Hancock's sense of fluidity and movement.

The first track, "Blind Man, Blind Man" was written because Hancock "wanted something that reflected my Negro background", and was inspired by an old blind man from his neighbourhood in Chicago. The following track, the slightly longer "A Tribute To Someone" was written in 1959, when Hancock was a student at the Berklee School of Music in Boston, Massachusetts. The track was originally written when it evolved from a jam session with his fellow student, trumpeter John Scott. The third track, "King Cobra" was written, because Herbie Hancock was tired of the same chord progressions usually associated with jazz. The title was also a slight reference to the Cobra car, that the pianist owned at the time of "My Point Of View"'s recording. The next track, "The Pleasure Is Mine", is a change in the album's movement, in that Hancock switches the more uptempo style of "King Cobra" for a more relaxed ballad style. The song was allegedly influenced by one of Hancock's preferred pianists, Bill Evans. The final track of the original LP, "And What If I Don't" is based around a 32-bar theme, that gives the song a bluesy feel, and was inspired by Hancock's upbringing, when he used to listen to Rhythm and Blues records.

The remastered edition of "My Point Of View", released in 1999, also features an alternate take of the first track, "Blind Man, Blind Man".

The band which features in the recording session of March 1963 consisted of:

Herbie Hancock (Piano)
Donald Byrd (Trumpet)
Grachan Moncur III (Trombone)
Hank Mobley (Tenor Saxophone)
Grant Green (Guitar)
Chuck Israels (Bass)
Tony Williams (Drums)
The tracklisting of the album, in its remastered edition, is:

1. Blind Man, Blind Man (8:15)
2. A Tribute To Someone (8:40)
3. King Cobra (6:51)
4. The Pleasure Is Mine (4:00)
5. And What If I Don't (6:30)
6. Blind Man, Blind Man (Alternate Take) (8:21)

All compositions are by Herbie Hancock.

The Eels XI (nicknamed "The Eels") are an association football team playing in the University of Bristol Intramural league. Now celebrating their third season together, they can currently be found in Accenture Division 4.

[edit] History

The origin of the name can be traced back to the team's founder, Nigel Brown, as his smile was said to resemble an eel. However, the name has also become synonimous with characteristics held by many of the players, namely that they are slippery, slimey and spineless. In addition, it has been suggested that the name is appropriate in describing their fluent style of football.

The original Eels XI squad was predominantly composed of members of Churchill Hall, a hall of residence of University of Bristol. The team began it's history in University of Bristol Intramural Division 4, which they went on to win in their first season. As a result and due to the dynamic nature of promotion and relegation in the Intramural league, the next season they played in Division 1.

[edit] 2006/07 Season

The team underwent a dramatic overhaul between the 2005/06 and 2006/07 seasons due to the graduation of several key players, potentially leaving them short on numbers. However, the shortfall was plugged by the influx of talent from the continent in the form of several final year students returning from their year abroad, their link with the team being fellow returner, Rupert Manwaring. He was made manager of the team but remains a regular player. Nicholas Hatton retained the captaincy.

At the time of the Christmas break, Eels XI were undefeated at the top of Division 4 having won five out of five games played.

[edit] Laurie King

Laurie King is an American editor, anthropologist, and journalist. She is the former editor of the Middle East Report, which is published by the Middle East Research and Information Project (MERIP) from 1998-2000. As editor, she was a frequent commentator on national and international news programs, including MSNBC and CNN International, on political, cultural, and military developments in the Middle East and US foreign policies in the region, and was often invited onto Radio South Africa and Irish Radio.

From 1995-1997, she was the Editor-in-Chief for Al-Raida ("The Pioneer"), the quarterly journal of the Institute for Women's Studies in the Arab World, Lebanese American University, Beirut, Lebanon, and wrote extensively on women's human rights issues in the Arab Islamic context

She was a Fulbright scholar in 1992-1993 and conducted field research for her Ph.D. in sociocultural anthropology in Nazareth, Israel which focused on the political identity of Palestinians in this region.

Her thesis and subsequent research resulted in her name being entered into the rosters of Campus Watch, an organization dedicated to monitoring radical intellectuals who they feel are apologists for Palestine.

For many years, Laurie King operated under the name King-Irani, due to her marriage to George Irani, a Lebanese Maronite Christian. The marriage broke down in 2006.

Laurie King is a founding member of the Electronic Intifada, which reports on news in the Middle East and the Israeli-Palestinian conflict from a Palestinian perspective.

One of her most interesting achievements in recent years is being a part of the International Campaign for Justice for the Victims of Sabra and Shatila, upon which she still spends considerable time. The legal team for this organization successfully indicted Ariel Sharon for war crimes against humanity, in Belgium, because there was a local law that allowed all international war criminals to be tried there. The day after Sharon was indicted, Donald Rumsfeld flew to Belgium and lobbied for the government to change the law, which it ultimately did.
