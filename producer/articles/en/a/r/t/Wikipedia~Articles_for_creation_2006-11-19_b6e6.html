[edit] One Site Unit Training

This request for creation has been reviewed. The result is below.


One Station Unit Training, sometimes referred to as One Site Unit Training, is a term used by the United States Army to refer to a training program in which recruits remain with the same unit for both Basic Combat Training (BCT) and Advanced Individual Training (AIT). Immediately following Basic Training graduation, the unit seamlessly transforms from a BCT unit into an AIT unit. There is no relocation and same Drill Sergeants who conducted the Basic Training continue to instruct all of the participating recruits in their Advanced Individual Training. This streamlines the training schedule and helps to produce more camaraderie between recruits. There are a variety of Military Occupational Specialies (MOS) and training stations that have OSUT training. 11B (infantry) at Ft. Benning, Georgia follows an OSUT program. 21B (combat engineer) and 31B (military police) at Ft. Leonard Wood both also follow an OSUT program.

[edit] European Gateway

The European Gateway was built in Germany in 1975 for Townsend Thoresen. She entered service on the 9th of June 1975.

Disaster struck the ship on the night of the 19th of December 1982 when she was involved in a collision with the Speedlink Vanguard in the approaches to Felixstowe harbour. The European Gateway was holed below the waterline and took on an increasing list to starboard. Within 30 minutes she had rolled over onto a sandbank. Of the 34 passengers and 36 crew 6 were lost.

The European Gateway lay with her starboard side resting on the sandbank until the new year of 1983. She was salvaged by a Dutch salvage Company on the 26th of February 1983.

[edit] Robert Bardzimashvili (1934-2003)

Robert Bardzimashvili [Роберт Бардзимашвили] founder, lead vocalist, creative director, guitarist of Orera [Орэра]. Formed in 1958, Orera became a Soviet sensation on the scale of Beatlemania. It also launched the careers of mega stars actor/singer Vakhtang Kikabidze and Nani Bregvadze. In 1975, Robert Bardzimashvili left Orera to form one of the first Georgian/Soviet rock bands. Jimmy Hendricks and other classic rock contemporaries heavily influenced the music of Via-75, and the group enjoyed tremendous popularity throughout the Soviet Union as one of the first and officially sanctioned [soft] rock bands in the country. In the post Soviet years, Mr. Bardzimashvili headed the Tbilisi Opera. More recently, he established a music school for children, the Lakhti Studio. Mr. Bardzimashvili was awarded the highest official honor of the National Artist of the GSSR, and in 2000 Orera received a star on the Plozhad Zvezd in Moscow, Russian's equivalent of the Hollywood Walk of Fame.

[edit] fusion torch

The Fusion Torch is a concept for the application of ultra-high temperatur plasmas found in fusion reactors. Because of the special properties of fusion plasmas it is possible to reduce any material to its basic elements for separation. This effectively closes the cycle from use to reuse.

The developers of the fusion torch concept envisoned the use of fusion torches on an industrial scale in order to

  • reuse any material that has been used by reducing it to the elements it was made of
  • use any ores and be able to take advantage of even minute concentrations

In their paper of 1969 B.J. Eastlund and W.C. Gough defined 3 "traps" that could hamper the advancement of mankind.

  1. Population (Food)
  2. Entropy (Ressources, Energy, Pollution)
  3. War (Human needs and behavior)

They stated referring to Entropy:

(...)"The use of the fusion torch in conjunction with controlled fusion power offers a potential solution to the entropy trap in materials. - i.e. man's exhaustion of nature's stored resources." (...)

In terms of energy needs they estimated the following:

"An "all" electric city of 10,000,000 people, by the year 2000 will need 140,000 megawatts of electrical capacity.(1.) If just 10,000 megawatts were used in a fusion torch then somewere between 2,700 and 27,000 tons of material could be processed per day."

They already speculated that the fusion torch concept would be useful (...) for the separation of uranium from reactor fuel element material.(...)

[edit] Artemide

Artemide is a design-oriented Italian lighting manufacturer founded by Ernesto Gismondi and Sergio Mazza in 1958. Based in Pregnana, a suburb of Milan, the company manufactures an extensive programme of technical and decorative luminaries designed by famous international designers and architects.

The programme of Artemide includes the collections Artemide, Collezioni, Metamorfosi, e.light, Modern Classic, Artemide Architectural and DZ Outdoor. Designers who have collaborated with the company include Mario Botta, Sir Norman Foster, Michele De Lucchi, Richard Sapper, Ettore Sottsass, Enzo Mari, Neil Poulton and Karim Rachid.

Artemide is best known for technical desk lamps such as the Tizio desk lamp designed by Richard Sapper in 1972, the Tolomeo desk lamp, designed by Michele De Lucchi and Giancarlo Fassina in 1986 and the Talak desk lamp, designed by Neil Poulton in 2004.

[edit] Awards

The company has won numerous accolades, including the Compasso d'Oro award for lifetime achievement in 1995 and the European Design Prize in 1997. In 2006 the company won two Best of The Best Red dot design awards for lamps designed by designer Neil Poulton and by architects Herzog & de Meuron.

Artemide lamps are included in the permanent collections of many museums, including the Musée des Artes Décoratifs de Montreal, The Metropolitan Museum of Art in New York, The Museum of Modern Art in New York and the Galleria Nazionale d'Arte Moderna in Rome.

[edit] Will W.

Will W. (also known as: Will Wong; born in Oshawa, Ontario, Canada) is a Toronto-based singer/songwriter, actor and noted celebrity photographer. Although Canadian-born, Will is of Chinese descent, with his family having immigrated to Canada from Hong Kong in 1976. He has recorded in Chinese under the name "Will Wong" independently and is set to release his debut English LP Connection May 8th, 2007.


A promotional photo from Will W.'s album "Connection"

Musical Training
Throughout his early years, Will starred in a few local productions in the Greater Toronto Area such as Oliver! and Joseph and the Amazing Technicolour Dreamcoat as well as having participated in local jazz and gospel choirs. While majoring in Psychology at the University of Toronto, Will was guided under the tutelage of C-pop (Chinese pop) legend Teresa Carpio, helping him bridge the distance to his Chinese roots musically in exploring a traditional and contemporary repertoire. Will went on to be a finalist in the nationally-televised singing competition Fairchild Television New Talent Singing Contest in 1998 and won the Best Performer Award in the internationally-prestigious TVB Eastern America New Talent Singing Contest held in Boston, Massachusetts in 1999.

Recording Career
Support was strong in the Chinese-Canadian community for this promising artist, garnering him an award in the 2001 Fairchild Television Songquest Competition and signaling a celebrated debut as a songwriter.

In 2002, Will merged his writing and singing abilities together and released his debut Chinese language EP, Will | I Am. The eight track Mandarin and Cantonese EP, was described by critics from international publications Sing Tao Daily and Ming Pao Daily as a fine balance of the Eastern and Western, receiving favourable reviews across-the-board from fans and publications alike. Publicity and airplay on multicultural radio outlets in Canada led to the album's initial pressing selling-out quickly with in-store and online retailers. More notably, Will | I Am was the first-ever Canadian release to be featured on the internationally popular YesAsia.com and the EP’s success was evinced in its impressive debut in the second position on the online store's "Most Talked About" chart.

Will's popularity was proven in the sell-out success of his album-supporting concert in November 2002 held at North America's largest indoor Asian shopping centre, Pacific Mall. Fans could not get enough of his emotionally resonsant voice and alluring personality. Buzz surrounding Will then quickly spread back to Asia and international publisher Warner/Chappell HK Ltd. added Will to its roster of exclusive songwriters in November 2003. Will’s songs have captured the interest and attention of some of the industry’s emerging talent.

Actor/Model/Photographer
Will has spent a bit of time focusing on print modeling, appearing as the face for Dose Magazine in a nation-wide advertising campaign. Also, Will has studied with acting coaches Theresa Sears & David Switzer, appearing in a series of independent films such as Welcome which screened at the World Wide Short Film Festival; Pump and Dump Classic which screened at the Indie Can Film Festival; and the box office success 16 Blocks.

Being multi-faceted, Will is also a noted celebrity photographer, with his photos having appeared in online and print publications such as The Toronto Sun, The Toronto Star, Canoe, 24 Hours, The Globe and Mail and more.

Current Status
After almost four years in the making and a brief battle with Cancer, Will is set to release his debut English LP Connection. The pop music album is described as a genre-hopping collection of guilty pleasures. The album’s talented roster includes co-producer Robin Eccleston, Masia One, Adrian Eccleston, Moulann, Brownman Ali, George Gao and many, many more. Early previews of the album are available at Will's Myspace pages (http://www.myspace.com/willariah or http://www.myspace.com/willwmusic).

[edit] Mrs Bob Cratchit's Wild Christmas Binge by Christopher Durang

Mrs Bob Cratchit's Wild Christmas Binge is a full length play from American playwright Christopher Durang. Here he presents an alternative Christmas show, taking much loved and well known Christmas stories like A Christmas Carol by Charles Dickens and the film It's A Wonderful Life and giving them an absurd Durang twist. The story begins by closely following A Christmas Carol but when we reach the character of Mrs Cratchit, things start to change. This character, often over looked in Dickens is presented here as a harassed, over worked and despairing figure, a far cry from the embodiment of grace and perfection depicted in the original. Mrs Cratchit bemoans her pitiful life where she is surrounded by her weak and pathetic husband Bob, the sickenly self pitying crippled Tiny Tim, their twenty odd children, most of whom live in a huddle in the root cellar and their unattractive overweight daughter Little Nell (brought in from Dicken's Old Curiosity Shop for good measure). So when the Ghost of Christmas Past's magical powers prove to be totally incompetent, Mrs Cratchit is opened up to another world and finds herself wandering through Scrooge's past life on her way to get drunk and drown herself in the river, all the while Scrooge is completely missing the Ghost's message about the goodness of Christmas and finds himself drawn to Mrs Cratchit who "speaks her mind". Trying to put things right, the Ghost brings Mrs Cratchit home for Christmas where the Cratchits give a joyful rendition of "Yummy Yum Yum" before settling down to a Christmas dinner cooked by one of the children that proves totally inedible. With Tiny Tim and Little Nell both fearing for their mortality and competing with each other for sympathy, Mrs Cratchit finally gives up and leaves the family for good. It's here that Scrooge and Mrs Bob finally meet as A Christmas Carol gives way to It's A Wonderful Life and the Ghost finds her position and story usurped by Clarence the angel- now with his wings and eager to help someone else. Before anyone can stop him Mrs Bob suddenly doesn't exist: an attempt to emulate his recent success with George Bailey. But this time it back fires. Mrs Cratchit sees that her husband Bob is better off without her, she having been replaced by a perfect version and Tiny Tim and Little Nell are now incarnated as a dog and a horse respectively. Much to the annoyance of the Ghost, Mr Scrooge now doesn't exist either. With events spiralling out of his control, Clarence turns to Tess and Monica (from Touched by an Angel) who reveal that Mrs Cratchit and Mr Scrooge belong together and should live in 1970s New York. The final scene sees Mrs Cratchit and Scrooge as Leona and Harry Helmsley and with Bob Cratchit and his nice wife, they sing the final number "What is Christmas" while the Ghost is left to ponder the true moral of this tale.

Background The show was commissioned by City Theatre, Pittsburgh and premiered on 7 November 2002.

[edit] Lost Battalion Games

Lost Battalion Games is a wargame company founded in the spring of 2003. The original principals were Debbie and Jeff Billings, John Caskey and S. Craig Taylor, Jr. Mr. Caskey left after a few months but the other founders are still with the company. Two very unusual features of this company is that they operate under a print-on-demand production system and, although hobby shop orders are accepted, most of their sales are generated from their rather extensive and interesting Web site.

When the company was founded, Mr. Taylor, who had formerly been with Battleline, Yaquinto and Avalon Hill, conducted research that determined that the old distribution system for manual wargames was pretty much defunct. Where distributors used to order grosses of games, now they wanted their usual discount for orders of dozens. Distributors also still practiced their old habit of being very tardy with payments, using smaller game companies orders like they were interest-free loans. Games printed in a conventional manner and quantities could no longer be moved quickly through the system. Selling directly from the Lost Battalion Web site without the usual discounts meant that the slightly higher printing costs could be absorbed without asking absurdly high game prices. Lost Battalion Games, without most of its capital sitting on warehouse pallets waiting to be sold, was also enabled to invest a higher proportion of its capital in publishing new titles.

Web site sales are driven by attracting consumers with an extensive and eclectic grouping of advertisements, game information, replays, historical and humorous articles and free scenarios plus and electronic newsletter.

Bruce Lee

Dvajsetega julija bo minilo 33 let od smrti Bruce Lee-ja, ki je ena najskrivnostnejših ugank dvajsetega stoletja. Ker Bruce Lee predstavlja nekakšno prelomnico borilnih veščin sem se odločil tudi o njem napisati kar nekaj vrstic.

Bruce Lee se je rodil v San Franciscu leta 1940, po tradicionalnem kitajskem horoskopu v znamenju zmaja oz zmajevem letu. Starši so mu dali ime Lee Hoi Chuen, vendar je medicinska sestra vpisala ime Bruce Lee, kakor da bi bila strokovnjakinja za zveneča imena za filmsko industrijo. Z dvema sestrama in dvema bratoma je odraščal v Hongkongu med številnimi udomačenimi opicami in kupico služabnikov. Izžareval je veliko energije in vedno zašel v težave tam kjer ni bilo treba. Pri trinajstih letih je začel trenirati posebno vrsto kung fuja ki se imenuje Wing tsun (ving čun se prebere) pri kateri je tehnično znanje bolj poudarjeno kot drobljenje moči. Vsi filmi ga prikazujejo kot pridnega študenta, ampak v resnici je bil zelo živahen. Takrat mu je bil očitno najbolj pri srcu ples, saj je bil Hongkonški prvak v plesanju čačača-ja. Pri 16 letih je kung fu dojel po svoje, svoje veščine ni hotel izpopolnjevati, temveč jo je vneto študiral na ulicah. Bil je v bandi, ki je bila oborožena z vsemi mogočimi nevarnimi rekviziti, med drugim z britvicami v težkih čevljih. Vsa njegova druščina je bila pod vplivom negativcev iz ameriške produkcije B. Ulični pretepi so bili takrat povsem vsakdanji, dovolj je bil že sprehod po kakšni od temačnih ulic. Bil je nevaren mulec, znan daleč naokoli. V poznih najstniških letih mu ulica ni ponujala več izzivov in boji so se nadaljevali v skritih podzemnih prostorih, kjer je na nogah ostal le še zmagovalec. Usmiljenja ni bilo, a Bruce Lee je ponavadi zmagal in kdor je stavil nanj je dobil, vendar vse to na drugi strani zakona. V zadnjem boju je nasprotniku razbil lobanjo zaradi česar so ga iskali varuhi zakona, vendar je njegov oče rekel da je Bruce že dolgo v ZDA, kasneje se je res nastanil v ZDA. Preden je končal srednjo šolo je spoznal kasnejšo ženo Lindo Emery - žensko večne lepote, malce zmedenega videza in uganko v očeh. Leta 1963 sta se z Lindo preselila v okolico Hollywooda kjer sta skušala vzbuditi navdušenje za kung fu, ki je bil takrat na zahodu povsem neznan. [edit] Razza

Razza, is the Bangla title (last name) for king in the People's Republic of Bangladesh. The common misconception is that the title Razza is interchangeable with the title Raja. The Bangla sobdo (word) Razza does trace it's origin from the word Sanskrit word Rajah, but is characterized by the affiliation with the culture, heritage, and state which is Bangladesh. Another common misconception is that the title Razza is interchangeable with the last name of Reis. Razza is the Bangla equivelent for the Portuguese word Reis, both translate to king. The Bengalis or Bangla families who Have Reis as the last name have more descendence from a prominent Portuguese lineage, while Razza is a related to a more native lineage: atleast when it comes to name. The northeastern areas of Bangladesh, such as Sylhet and Sumangonj, have many prestigious families that have the last name Razza.
