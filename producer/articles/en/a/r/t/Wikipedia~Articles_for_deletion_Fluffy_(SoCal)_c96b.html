  • Comment I have to admit, I am a newbie with Wikipedia. I was going to move the page into the band format (when I figured out how to do that). In terms of Notability, Blonde Vinyl Records is considered by some to be a pre-cursor to the much more-successful Tooth And Nail Records because it released avant-garde CCM, which was quite unusual at the time, and pushed the envelope of what Christian Rock could be. Later, another Avant Garde label, Flying Tart, which also released a track from Sixpence None The Richer on a Christmas Compilation before they were a cross-over success, released records by the band Fluffy. Fluffy's records were released through national distriubtion channels (for the CCM market) and the recorded works span a period of six years. The source for a majority of the information on the article comes from CD's that I own, and I guess I just don't know how to source that.
