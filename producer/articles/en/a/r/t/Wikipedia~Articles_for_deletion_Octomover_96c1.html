  • Okay, here are some of the big points
  1. Every last word is original research - none of this information comes from reliable, third party sources - he's writing is because he's researched it himself. That makes the entire article unacceptable, per WP:OR
  2. Every last word is unverifiable. The article does not cite a single, reliable source. That makes the entire article a violation of WP:V, a policy which cannot be overridden by concensus.
  3. The bottom half of the article is contact information for the purpose of promoting the company - all of that is in violation of WP:SPAM - it's just a piece of spam he's trying to drum up some business with.
  4. The article repeated uses phrases like "An octogon is better than a square when it comes to turning, because it doesn't have corners that will hit furniture." - this is a violation of non-negotiable policies like WP:NPOV
  5. The company itself fails the guideline of WP:CORP for inclusion.
  6. There are plenty more, but I hope the more egregious ones will give you the flavour of the problem. WilyD 16:36, 24 August 2006 (UTC)
