[edit] Auxigro

According to the manufacturer's website [1], Auxigro is an agrochemical that makes onions, melons, tomatoes and peppers grow larger.

What makes me suspocious about this entry is that the product consists of glutamic acid and γ-aminobutyric acid, [2] something that one would not expect to be taken up through the leaves. Indeed, a study done by the university of Florida [3] shows that the product has no effect on crop size or yield. That, together with only 300 unique Google hits [4] leads one to conclude that this entry is marketing spam. Dr Zak 17:32, 18 July 2006 (UTC)

The fact it doesn't supposedly make the crops grow larger but is still used for this reason may indicate that it is used to do something else to the crop. Possibly taste better as it contains glutamic acid. Maybe. —The preceding unsigned comment was added by 202.172.125.45 (talk • contribs).
'fraid not. The datasheet is here [5], and it states "to increase total yield". Dr Zak 04:51, 19 July 2006 (UTC)
