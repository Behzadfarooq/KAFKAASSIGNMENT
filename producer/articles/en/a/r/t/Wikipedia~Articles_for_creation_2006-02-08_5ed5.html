[edit] THE ACT OF INTERMIXING

Luis Fernando Ponce has arranged a set up of objects, on a little “stage”; each in front of what could be a heavy theatre curtain. These objects can primarily be identified as things, which externalise and exemplify the American society. In the majority of the images, the objects are portrayed alone or sometimes limited to a couple, so one in clear view can take a good look and identify each object. The light exenterates each of them, reminiscent of how stage lighting directs the eye for attracting attention. Making them stand out to tell a story as in monologs and small dialogs, and only by bringing all of these images together do a fully coherent story become apparent. These things carry references to the impression made on the mind and emotion elicited in the observer.

To portray objects could seemingly appear like a simple act, but to the genre of still life there is always a variety and complicated topics connected to it. And it is within this longstanding tradition Luis Fernando Ponce follows. These objects, in the photographs, of simple recognisable forms, strike a key by their portrayal of formal still life. Why do these objects meet on the classical scene? A single point is made accessible in a plausible ‘universal’ language. In this photographical series the objects are in the centre of the picture plane, and are the centre of Ponce’s pictures universe. In the western view the man function as the measures of all things. The meanings of the world are dependent on our own understanding and vintage point. The Coca Cola bottle, as seen in one of the images, does in itself not solely earn its meanings out of its own evolutionary history, but becomes first meaningful in terms of the anthropocentric metaphors we assign to it.

All photographs describes in the sense that they offer descriptive, visual information, with greater or lesser detail and clarity. These photographs go far beyond the interest of portraying objects as mere visual facts. They are objectified icons to exemplify two historically distinctly different societies. The images draw upon objects, which hold inter-woven symbolism and influences from the American society over the Guatemalan society. They shift between the literal and the allegorical, exploring the projection of one culture over another. Luis Fernando Ponce’s images resonate with sentiment: one cultures slipping away and the evident superseding of another, and sometimes even unwillingly witnessing the embracing ‘implementation of today’s world order culture’. Luis Fernando makes statements about the influence of this dominant culture over another. He faces the viewer straight on in his desire to ask them to be conscientious in the adaptation of elements of another culture into their own lives. The images are direct social commentary on ‘the great game’ of exploitation and new colonialism.

The work of Luis Fernando Ponce falls in the category of interpretative photography. The objects are depicted to hold and attempt scientific accuracy. The images of the Coca Cola bottle, gun, radio, plane etc. are bearers of cultural mythologies. In their staged way they carries personal and subjective interpretations. Although they present an apparent clear directorial mode, many of these images play with ambiguity, and are open to a variety of readings. Interpretative photographs need of course to be interpreted, and places the interpretative responsibility on the viewer. The construction of meaning may shift. It shifts naturally according to the audience, by each spectator, and their cultural making. That’s what makes for an interesting spectacle.


by Malin Barth She is a curator with an emphasis on photography, and has curated and written on various artists such as Andres Serrano, Tina Modotti, and Edward Weston.

[edit] Structural moving

Structural moving is moving a building off its foundation by some means of transportation, such as a large truck or boat. This is most commonly done with small, compact houses, such as trailer homes. According to the International Association of Structural Movers, buildings such as barges, ships, lighthouses, movie theaters, grain elevators, libraries, etc, have all been moved in the past.

Structural moving is a difficult and expensive job. The building being moved is typically lifted straight off its foundation and then transported to a new, prebuilt foundation at another location. They can be transported mainly by semi-trailer or specialized boats.

Structural moving may be performed for any number of reasons, but it can be that the building:

  1. Must be moved, because it is a historical structure
  2. Must be moved, because it is in the way of development
  3. Is in danger of a natural disaster

etc.

[edit] IKK2

IKK2 is the name of a protein that plays a significant factor in the state of brain cells after a stroke. In a stroke, a communications network between cells called NF-kB is activated by IKK2. If this signal activating NF-kB is blocked, then damaged cells within the brain stay alive, and according to a study performed by the University of Heidelberg and the University of Ulm, the cells even appear to make some recovery.

[edit] STAN (Sekolah Tinggi Akuntansi Negara/State College of Accountancy)

STAN is State College of Accountancy under the Ministry of Finance of The Republic of Indonesia. It was founded in 1950's by Gandhi (an official of the Ministry of Finance of Republic of Indonesia) to supply the need of accountants and employee with adequate government financial ability. The name was STIKN (Sekolah Tinggi Ilmu Keuangan Negara/State College of Government Financial management). It was located in Jl. Purnawarman, South Jakarta Indonesia. In 1960's the name was changed to IIK (Institut Ilmu Keuangan / Institute of Government Financial Management). The Institute provided some majors in Accountancy, Budget and Treasury, Taxation, Custom and Excise. The graduates of IIK entitled Drs. (Doktorandus - a Dutch academic title)like/equal to graduates of Universities ran by Ministry of National Education.

In 1979 the name was changed one more time: STAN (Sekolah Tinggi Akuntansi Negara/State college of Accountancy) and relocated to Jurang mangu Timur Village (Suburb of Tangerang Regency). The college offers majors of accountancy, Taxation, Custom and Excise, State Claim and Auction, Budget and Treasury. As it is under the Ministry of Finance, almost all of its graduates are employed by the Ministry of Finance. Some are taken by Supreme Audit Board of Republic of Indonesia (BPK RI) and State finance and Development Supervisory Board (BPKP RI).

The School is dived into two programs: Diploma III and Diploma IV. Prior to August 2004, the graduates of Diploma IV are entitled Accountant title. But since the issuance of education regulation, the title is no more attainable.

[edit] Robert W. Field

Robert W. Field is the Haslam and Dewey Professor of Chemistry at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology, where he has been a professor since 1974. His AB degree is in chemistry from Amherst College, and his PhD is in chemistry from Harvard University, where he worked with Bill Klemperer. He was a postdoc with Herbert Broida at the University of California at Santa Barbara.

He is a physical chemist, specializing in spectroscopy of small molecules in the gas phase. He performmed the first microwave-optical and optical-optical double resonance experiments on small molecules, and invented the Stimulated Emission Pumping (or "Pump and Dump") spectroscopic method. He is also particularly known for studies of the molecules acetylene (C2H2) and calcium monofluoride (CaF).

He is the recipient of the Broida Prize, the Plyler Prize, the Lippincott Award, and the Nobel Laureate Signature Award. He is a Fellow of the American Physical Society and the American Academy of Arts and Sciences.

His active research group at MIT includes more than ten graduate students and postdocs working on experimental, theoretical and computational physical chemistry of small molecules.

[edit] [RSA SecurID]

RSA SecurID (r) is an authentication system that uses respective software on one side and the authenticator on another side. Authenticator is a small (car alarm size) electronic device with an in-built RSA codes generator. Each device has its own unique code generating algorithm that is identified by the serial number of the device engraved on the reverse side of it. Generated codes are device-dependent (due to the above mentioned in-built algorithm), random, being displayed on an LCD screen and they change periodically. This device along with relevant software, which validates the codes to see if they are correct and derive from a designated authenticator, is considered to be rather strong yet convenient mechanism of authorization and access control.

Officiaris (Latin) clerk at a bishop's court, recorded instances dating back to 1202.

Oficiar (Spanish) dubious Kincardine legend involving Spanish Armada survivors who supposedly told captors they were an 'officer' to be held for ransom instead of getting killed and later marrying into local families. Probable spelling variation of Officiar.

Orfroiser (old French) occupational surname maker of orphrey (gold embroidery).

Spanish word 'Oficiar' means to officiate or to minister.

[edit] Hate media

Hate media have been defined as media (usually TV, radio or press, though Internet plays an increasing role) which encourage violent attitudes and activities, tension or hatred between races, ethnic or social groups, or countries. They generally do so for political ends. Conflict is fostered by offering biased, one-sided views (which often build upon prejudice and stereotypes), in order to deceive citizens and to mobilise their support for violent action.

In the words of Journalist Keith Ferrell [5], in their efforts to draw readers' support, most hate sites:

1. Capitalize on Paranoia;
2. Suggest Sanction from Above; and 
3. Exploit Fear of Armageddon

Acording to Radio Netherlands’ Media Network hate media are a constant danger: hate radio killed more than 800,000 people in the last decade, so its influence should not be ignored.

Among examples are the case of three Rwandan media executives who were found guilty of inciting violence during the genocide of 1994. Two worked for a radio station which broadcast lists of people to be killed and revealed where they could be found. The three were given long jail terms (BBC [6]). Indeed, it is claimed that hate radio first appeared in this region.

Europe has nothing to be proud of: the Balkans conflict was preceded by a steady build-up of hate activity by several media.

At a lower key, the COPE radio station in Spain has been widely accused, particularly in Catalonia [7] and the Basque Country [8] of breeding conflict, through two programmes: "La Mañana" and "La Linterna". The Madrid-based radio station was severely rebuked by the Audiovisual Council of Catalonia in a report dated December 19 2005. [9]

It also has switches on the top of The Ramlink for convience to the user. It has two device swap switches (8 & 9) to swap with another drive for programs that only allow you to use device 8 or 9 to run. It has a system reset switch on it that allows a "WARM" reset of the computers memory contents allowing you to reset your computer now without having to switch it off completely ( a nice little concept). In addition to these features, it also has 2 toggle type switches on the top of the unit; one for switching from JIFFY DOS MODE (enable/disable) and "STOCK" mode, and the other for REU usage. The REU switch allows switching from Ramlink Mode (NORMAL)and DIRECT RAM EXPANSION MODE (DIRECT).

It has a socket in the back allowing "FAST" parallel access to the CMD SERIES HARD DRIVES, making the hard drive faster than normal (due to The Ramlinks incredible speed.
