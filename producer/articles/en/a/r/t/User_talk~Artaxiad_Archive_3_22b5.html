[edit] Reverting

Hey how do i revert articles?

I noticed alot of vandalism in the recent changes area im trying my hardest to mark the articles that are nonsense to be deleted but for the regular articles how do i revert? Nareklm 22:56, 25 November 2006 (UTC)

Take a look at WP:REVERT. Basically, you bring up the history of the article (using the history tab), you click on the version you want to roll back to, then you hit "Save page". It's the same as manually removing all of the vandalism but you're assured of getting the exact version that you clicked on. Just make sure you don't revert any good changes while you do it. —Wknight94 (talk) 23:05, 25 November 2006 (UTC)

[edit] New pages patrol

Glad to see another editor on new pages patrol! Please be sure to leave a warning on the talk page of any editor who vandalizes an article, such as the changes you just reverted on the Flintridge Preparatory School article. This editor has created two articles tagged db-bio and vandalized the article about a school---and he only just got started! We need to make sure we warn him properly, in the hopes that he might knock it off. Thanks. ---Charles 02:08, 26 November 2006 (UTC)

Take a look through WP:VANDAL for all of the warnings that are usually given to vandals. CAT:UW has others as well. When you see one has gotten a final warning (usually {{test4}}) and then vandalized again, you get to add xyr to WP:AIV. If you think it's bad now, wait until a school day when all the little kiddies sit in front of a computer during study hall and do all sorts of stupid things. Be sure to remember to use subst when you add the "test" templates or someone will remind you!  :) —Wknight94 (talk) 02:21, 26 November 2006 (UTC)
Go get 'em! There is also a helpful IRC channel which you can find out about at Category:Vandalism Control Network members. I haven't looked around that category much but I've used the IRC channel and reverted quite a few bits of vandalism. Another route you might want to take - and I do this from time to time - is to watch the Recent Changes screen, set it to show the last 500, and look from the bottom of that screen up. You'd be surprised how many small pieces of vandalism get by the front line of vandal catchers (not to knock them in any way at all! There's just too much to catch). Those are the ones that, if you don't catch them there, they'll fall off the recent changes list and could sit in the system for quite a while. Next thing you know, it's another John Seigenthaler Sr. Wikipedia biography controversy. —Wknight94 (talk) 02:35, 26 November 2006 (UTC)
