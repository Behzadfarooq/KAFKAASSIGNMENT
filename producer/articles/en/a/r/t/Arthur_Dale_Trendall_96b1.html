Arthur Dale Trendall

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

New Zealander Arthur Dale Trendall [1909-1995] was an art historian and classical archaeologist whose work on identifying the work of individual artists on Etruscan ceramic vessels at Apulia and other sites earned him international prizes and a knighthood. Educated at the University of Otago (1926-29) and the University of Cambridge (1931-33) (where he was a student of John Beazley), Trendall was professionally associated with the University of Sydney and Australian National University.
