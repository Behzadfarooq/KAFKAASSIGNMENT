[edit] River Country at Point Mallard

Advert for an as yet unbuilt resort development

  • Delete. Gazpacho 22:16, 26 December 2005 (UTC)

The object of www.Wikipedia.org is to provide information on numerous subjects. To suggest that this page should be deleted because it has not yet been built is completely against the purpose of this website. The "River Country at Point Mallard" is pure fact, it is in the process of being approved and could become reality. I'm sure that there are other pages on things that have not yet been built or put into action, if this page should be deleted because "River Country" has not yet been built, then I suggest that you go forth and strive to delete those other pages of which advertise some other unbuilt structure, unapproved bill, or project.

  • Keep.AlabamaGuy2007 26 December 2005
  • Actually, the point of Wikipedia is to create an encyclopedia of general knowledge. An article about some hypothetical resort development does not belong. If the thing actually does get built then maybe it can have its own page, but that page shouldn't read like ad copy like this one does. Delete. --Cyde Weys votetalk 22:41, 26 December 2005 (UTC)
  • Delete for crystal balling and advertising. Eddie.willers 22:44, 26 December 2005 (UTC)
  • "I suggest that you go forth and strive to delete those other pages of which advertise some other unbuilt structure, unapproved bill, or project." Agreed. Delete; Tom Harrison (talk) 22:49, 26 December 2005 (UTC)
  • strive, we will delete MNewnham 00:26, 27 December 2005 (UTC)
  • Delete per nom. It's not even scheduled to be built yet, just proposed. --Quarl 01:01, 27 December 2005 (UTC)
  • Delete until River Country developers put some facts on the ground. Endomion 01:14, 27 December 2005 (UTC)
  • Delete This project is not completed and appears to be an advertisement to attract developers and customers. Ajwebb 01:21, 27 December 2005 (UTC)
  • Delete --NaconKantari 01:22, 27 December 2005 (UTC)
