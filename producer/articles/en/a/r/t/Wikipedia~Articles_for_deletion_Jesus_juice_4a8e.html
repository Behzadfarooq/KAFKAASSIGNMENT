[edit] Jesus juice

Jesus Juice was nominated for deletion on 2005-07-13. The result of the discussion was "delete". For the prior discussion, see Wikipedia:Articles for deletion/Jesus Juice.
  • Note - The contents of Jesus juice are not the same as the deleted one, which consisted of patent nonsense, so this is not a G4 speedy.

Michael Jackson trial-cruft - apparently there is such a thing. This is a non-notable neologism with no encyclopedic value, IMO. This could possibly be mentioned in 2005 trial of Michael Jackson, but if not, no loss. Delete. GTBacchus(talk) 07:12, 8 January 2006 (UTC)

  • Delete per nom Dbchip 09:13, 8 January 2006 (UTC)
  • Delete per nomination. Saberwyn - The Zoids Expansion Project 09:39, 8 January 2006 (UTC)
    • Merge as second option, per below. -- Saberwyn.
  • Delete to get Jesus Juice, the holy refreshment! Sceptre (Talk) 14:26, 8 January 2006 (UTC)
  • Keep. 134,000 results for "Jesus juice" on google. [1] I think that warrents keeping it. Trilemma 16:08, 8 January 2006 (UTC)
  • Delete. Jesus juice is a term Michael Jackson is alledged to call white wine (sic) - Who cares? Not encyclopedic (sp.?). --kingboyk 16:43, 8 January 2006 (UTC)
  • Merge into 2005 trial of Michael Jackson. I've heard the neologism used often enough that it appears to be here to stay, but I can't see it sustaining an article of its own. --Aaron 18:55, 8 January 2006 (UTC)
  • Merge. I agree with Aaron's reasoning. -- Mikeblas 19:00, 8 January 2006 (UTC)
  • Merge as per above Dustimagic 20:07, 8 January 2006 (UTC)
  • Merge per above suggestions into something related to Michael Jackson. This term is very notable. Cyde Weys votetalk 23:40, 8 January 2006 (UTC)
  • Delete or failing that, I will agree to Merge to form consensus. This shouldn't deserve its own article, though. Eusebeus 03:56, 9 January 2006 (UTC)
  • Delete Waste to space and time! --161.74.11.24 19:30, 10 January 2006 (UTC)
