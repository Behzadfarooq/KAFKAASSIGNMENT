[edit] Master Exploder

Master Exploder (edit|talk|history|links|watch|logs)

Was not released as a single and is an otherwise non notable song. Joltman 19:18, 6 December 2006 (UTC)

  • Support The crowd reaction part is not of encyclopedic value. Infested-jerk 19:21, 6 December 2006 (UTC)
  • Redirect to The Pick of Destiny, which someone has already done. --Light of Shadow 01:52, 12 December 2006 (UTC)
  • The song is of note because it is oft cited by critics and fans alike as the best scene in the movie.
 Relisted to generate a more thorough discussion so that consensus may be reached
 Please add new discussions below this notice. Thanks, Whispering 01:25, 12 December 2006 (UTC)
  • Delete - nn song. --Wooty Woot? contribs 02:25, 12 December 2006 (UTC)
  • Delete per above. MER-C 03:25, 12 December 2006 (UTC)
  • Delete for similar reasons I have stated for the AfD above this one, except with more determination. Bubba hotep 13:46, 12 December 2006 (UTC)
  • Delete delete it as unencyclopedic non single release.--John Lake 18:31, 14 December 2006 (UTC)
  • keep One of the best songs on the film. It IS notable —The preceding unsigned comment was added by Slogankid (talk • contribs) 18:45, 16 December 2006 (UTC).
  • Keep Although the song has not been released as a single, it was performed by the band in its entirety in the film. That is an unofficial music video, which has leaked onto the internet. I do agree, however, that the "What's different" section is pointless, but I do believe that this song has enough notability to have its own article. Milchama 19:40, 17 December 2006 (UTC)
Comment I don't believe that having a music video automatically makes a song notable. Joltman 13:24, 18 December 2006 (UTC)
Comment The movie is sometimes billed as a musical. In that case, I believe that all songs in a nationally distributed musical qualify for their own articles. If it was just a song on an album, then it would be questionable. Milchama 18:38, 18 December 2006 (UTC)
  • keep: The movie's possible status as a musical makes the song's context, as revealed throught the article, significant. —The preceding unsigned comment was added by Ellissound (talk • contribs) 09:25, 19 December 2006 (UTC).
