[edit] American Hearing Impaired Hockey Association (AHIHA)

The American Hearing Impaired Hockey Association (AHIHA) was established in 1973 by [[Chicago Blackhawks] great Stan Mikita, and a local business man by the name of Irv Tiahnybik, after Irv discovered the difficulties his hard-of-hearing son was having with his hearing teammates. The hockey camp grew quickly, and drew deaf and hard-of-hearing ice hockey players from all over the country.

The organization hosts the annual, week-long Stan Mikita Hockey Camp in Woodrige, IL at the Seven Bridges Ice Arena. The camp itself is free, though players do have to supply their own equipment and pay for their hotel room.

In addition to sponsoring the camp, AHIHA also helps advocate for its players, provides scholarships, and helps children obtain hearing aids. The Deaflympics ice hockey team is typically comprised almost solely of AHIHA players, and will be representing the USA once again in 2007 in Salt Lake City.

[edit] North Posey Senior High School (NPHS)

North Posey Senior High School (NPHS) is the highest school of the MSD of North Posey County, a consolidated school district in Posey County, Indiana. The school educates approx. 550 students at the secondary level. The current Principal is Linda Crick and the current Assistant Principal is Scott Strieter. The school is located along rural highway IN-165 between Poseyville and Wadesville. The school district educates the communities of Poseyville, Wadesville, Cynthiana, Griffin, Stewartsville, St. Wendel, and Blairsville in northern Posey County. The school district was consolidated in 1959 from the previous Poseyville, Griffin, Cynthiana, and Wadesville schools.

The schools athletic teams are referred to as the Vikings and the school's colors are Red, Black, and White. They compete in the Pocket Athletic Conference. The school's fight song is "Go Ye North Posey" to the tune of "Go Ye Northwestern".

"Go ye North Posey, Vikings down the line, With our colors flying, We will cheer you all the time, Rah Rah Rah, Go ye North Posey, Fight for victory, Spread for the fame of our fair name, Oh go you Vikings win this game, V-I-K-I-N-G-S, Vikings, Vikings, Yes, Yes, Yes"

The 2005 baseball team captured the IHSAA 2A State Championship, the school's first state title in any sport. The football team also made the 2A state final in 2005 before losing the final game and finishing runner-up. The school also has won 5 state Spell Bowl titles and 8 state academic championships in Class 3.

[edit] Jeremy Travis

Jeremy Travis became the fourth President of John Jay College of Criminal Justice on August 16, 2004. Prior to his appointment, President Travis served four years as a Senior Fellow affiliated with the Justice Policy Center at the Urban Institute, a nonpartisan research and policy organization in Washington, D.C.

From 1994-2000, Travis directed the National Institute of Justice, the research arm of the U.S. Department of Justice.

Prior to his service in Washington, Travis was Deputy Commissioner for Legal Matters for the New York City Police Department from 1990-1994.

He was also Chief Counsel to the U.S. House Judiciary Subcommittee on Criminal Justice in 1990, chaired by then-Rep. Charles E. Schumer.

Travis served as Special Advisor to New York City Mayor Edward I. Koch from 1986-89 and as Assistant Director for Law Enforcement Services for the Mayor‚Äôs Office of Operations in 1986.

From 1984-86, he was Special Counsel to the Police Commissioner of the NYPD.

[edit] Bonkers candy

Bonkers was a candy offering from Nabisco featuring a soft chewy and fruity outside, with a soft chewy, even fruitier inside. The candies came with several of them individually wrapped inside a long rectangular package. They came in various flavors, including orange, strawberry, grape, and shockingly at the time, watermelon, and chocolate. They were very popular in the mid-eighties, due to a series of unusual television commercials, where one or more straitlaced stick-in-the-mud characters would say how much they loved their Bonkers candy. When the character took a bite of the candy, the explosion of flavor was so intense that a large human-sized fruit, such as a bunch of grapes, would fall from above, landing on them and causing a hysterical fit of laughter from the victim. The popularity of Bonkers waned over the following decade until they became a rarity and were discontinued by the manufacturer. However, they were so popular in their heyday that they still have quite a large nostalgic following among candy lovers.
