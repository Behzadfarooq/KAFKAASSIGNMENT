As the person who nominated this for deletion, I feel I must respond.

Under the policy of WP:BIO, subsection People still alive, I personally believe that miss Austin does not meet any of the points given. That is my reason for nominating this and several related articles for deletion.

There is (as far as I know), some level of belief that the creator and contributors to an article should demonstrate in the text of the article that the subject is appropriate or 'worthy' for an encyclopedia article. (See Wikipedia:Policies and guidelines, Key Policies, point 1.). My personal opinion is that when the only information that can be provided on a person is their name', a set of basic physical statistics, a weblink, a date, and three letters falls under the non-encyclopedic arc of knowledge.

The only reason I know POM stands for 'Pet of the Month' is that I found this article via the List of Penthouse Pets list. Someone coming in from another 'angle' would be left confused and unknowledgeable, and could easily make the assumtion that it stands for pommie. In which case, the article would make even less sense than it does now. Again, as far as I know, there is some level of belief that an encyclopedia article should be comprehensible to somebody with either close to or no prior knowledge of the subject.

And finally, the term cruft, is 'Articles for Deletion' shorthand for "This article is trivia of interest only to hardcore fans of a specific film, television series, book, game, pop singer, web forum, etc.". This is detailed in a list of shorthand terms at Wikipedia:Guide to deletion.

I do not wish to be mean or offenside, and if I come across as this, I apologise. You have raised some points, and I have tried to answer them. Saberwyn -10:52, 9 December 2005 (UTC)
