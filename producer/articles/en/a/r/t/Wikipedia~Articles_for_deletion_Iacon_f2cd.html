Reaching for freedom within music: Iacon was founded in its original form in 2001 by Log Roper and Ben Javens. Initially a group focussing on DJing, Turntabalism & processing sound with a multitude of effects. Iacon provided soundtracks to exhibitions and film club nights. These events steadily developed Iacon towards the production of original event specific pieces of work using a wide variety of instruments. The introduction of Simon Osgathorp into the fold helped confirm the Iacon sound and allowed for additional possibilities when performing live.

In late 2003, Iacon moved into a studio space in Digbeth, Birmingham, UK. This provided a permanent space for experimentation and for new works to be developed and produced. Iacon have drawn from improvisation and developed a multi-instrument approach to their sound that can be played live. In any song each member may play up to three instruments over the course of the track. In 2004 Iacon began readying this approach for a piece of work entitled, Golden Age of Static.

Golden Age of Static explores an analogue escape from some of the traditional constraints that performers and audiences face alike. The performance takes place via the airwaves, transmitting to over one hundred second hand radios of different ages and design, ranging from modern to retro, resulting in differing degrees of tone, EQ, warmth and volume, creating a new listening experience. Usually sound artists or musicians within a concert environment are bound by the performance template of having a PA with all the leads & wires that are necessary to support this. In a normal performance set up a PA produces sound from a single direction/stereo split, which Iacon interpreted as a limitation on the audio experience of the audience i.e. a two-dimensional stationary sound.

Golden Age of Static is a performance that stretches the audienceâ€™s experience as well as the performers. Instead of the audience experience being non-interactive and docile, facing in a single direction for the whole event, audience members are invited to participate by bringing there own portable radios, engaging with the sound, moving around the venue. Hearing a wide variety of radio transmissions will alter the whole listening experience, the sound will evolve and mutate as the audience meander through the unfolding dynamic sonic adventure.

Golden Age of Static, Iacon's first foray into experimenting with Radio as a medium for performance took place on Saturday 24th September 2005 at Decoy Warehouse, Green Street, Digbeth, Birmingham, UK.

In Summer 2006, Iacon have a monthly summer residency in Birmingham. They plan to go back to their roots and provide a soundtrack to summer evening nights via a series of events. The initial one in June 06 is to feature all three members of Iacon playing and manipulating four turntables simultaneously in order to create a unique soundscape taking in influences from a diverse range of sources including Krautrock, Electronica, Minimalism, Hip-Hop, Math Rock & Jazz.
