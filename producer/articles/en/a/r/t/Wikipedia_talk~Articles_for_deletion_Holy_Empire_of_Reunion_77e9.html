[edit] Fictional Country

If there is a category Category:Fictional countries for Fictional Countries, why can´t Reunion be listed? Maybe racism because we are mostly Brazilians? Claudre 11:37, 6 January 2006 (UTC)

Reunion would need to be a notable fictional country. While I'm sure it's very notable to you, it needs to meet the guidelines laid out by consensus of the Wikipedia community, such as WP:WEB. As for racism, I think you'll find non-notable websites deleted with equal passion regardless of their national affiliation. --JeremyStein 14:57, 6 January 2006 (UTC)

[edit] Shortening the article

Maybe the article is too long, what about if I left only the "what reunion is", "what reunion stands for" parts, and exclude all the explanations about how things work? I believe the fact that we have been targeted by SO MANY newspaper/media attention and even books about the subject of fictitious countries makes us notable. How does one count the votes? Are only the votes of registered wikipedians counted? 200.142.120.138 18:21, 6 January 2006 (UTC)

I think removing the text on its inner workings would be a good start. It's important also to assert why it's significant to the world, not just to you. Ask yourself why newspaper articles have decided to mention it. Regarding vote counting, it's largely up to the judgment of an administrator. See Wikipedia:Deletion guidelines for administrators. --JeremyStein 20:47, 6 January 2006 (UTC)
