[edit] Travis Randall

Travis Randall (Travis Michael Randall) was born December 9th 1972 in Spooner Wisconsin. Travis was the long time bass player for notable 1990's indie band Stone Bogart. Travis continued on to play in bands (Pink Slip, Midnight Fight, Defaulting) and has recently been connected to the band Guns and Roses. He joined Stone Bogart as the bass player never having played the bass or guitar. Travis is a trained drummer and continues to make guest appearances on various CD's playing bass, guitar, and drums.

In 1999 Stone Bogart released it's 2nd critically acclaimed CD “Whadayabench” in which Travis has song writing credits. Travis was known for his often alcohol fueled stage performances. Travis and drummer Steve Doerr were considered to be a highlight in the Tempe AZ music scene in the late 90’s. Upon the release of their 1996 release of “Enjoy” Stone Bogart was voted #2 best band in France by France’s main rock radio station.

In 2001 Travis founded Rock and Roll BBQ. Rock and Roll BBQ quickly became a popular cooking and music website (www.rockandrollbbq.com) and quickly grew into a small enterprise.

In 2004 Rock and Roll BBQ became a television show. The pilot was filmed with Jimmy Kimmel and Alice Cooper and was originally accepted and quickly dropped by Food Network. Food Network considered the food/music format to be too aggressive for their network.

In 2004 Rock and Roll BBQ released three original BBQ sauces that are available around the world. Travis is releasing and touring a solo CD in 2007. The rumored title of the CD is “The Whiskey is Talkin’ (So you need to Quiet Right Down)”

In 2005 Travis appeared in the award winning indie movie "Never Been Thawed".

[edit] William L. Jorgensen

William L. Jorgensen (b. October 5th 1949) is the Conkey P. Whitehead Professor of Chemistry at Yale University. He is considered a pioneer in the field of computational chemistry. He has over 300 publications in the field, and currently holds the position of editor of four academic journals: Encyclopedia of Computational Chemistry, Journal of Chemical Information and Computer Sciences, Journal of Chemical Information and Modeling (formerly JCICS), Journal of Chemical Theory and Computation. He also hols spots on the editorial advisory boards of many other journals. He has also been involved in the founding of multiple biotech companies.

[edit] Educational Information and Past Employment

Jorgensen earned a Bachelor's degree from Princeton University in 1970 and a Ph.D. from Harvard University in 1975 in Chemical Physics while studying under organic chemistry legend E. J. Corey. Jorgensen then worked at Purdue University from 1975-1990 first as an Assistant Professor and then later as a Professor. He joined the Yale faculty in 1990 and has remained there since.

[edit] Research Interests

Jorgensen's research interests are broad and include the calculation of free-energies in reactions usin quantum mechanical, molecular mechanical, in addition to Monte Carlo methods, the calculation of protein-ligand bindings affinities, all of which are relevent to both academic research and the pharmaceutical industry. Another relevant research topic of his is in the development of better NNRTI's, which are used for the treatment of HIV. Much more can be learned about his research at the Jorgensen Research Group webpage.

[edit] Tolosa-Hunt Syndrome

This request for creation has been reviewed. The result is below.


Tolosa-Hunt syndrome is a rare disorder characterized by severe headaches and pain around the sides and back of the eye, along with weakness and paralysis (ophthalmoplegia) of certain eye muscles. Symptoms usually affect only one side of the head (unilateral). In most cases, affected individuals experience intense sharp pain and paralysis of muscles around the eye. Symptoms subside without intervention (spontaneous remission) and recur without a distinct pattern (randomly). In addition, affected individuals may exhibit paralysis (palsy) of certain facial nerves and drooping of the upper eyelid (ptosis). Other symptoms may include double vision, fever, chronic fatigue, headaches, a feeling that one's surroundings are spinning (vertigo), pain in the joints (arthralgia), and/or abnormal protrusion of one or both eyeballs (exophthalmos). The exact cause of Tolosa-Hunt syndrome is not known, but the disorder is thought to be associated with inflammation of the areas behind the eyes (cavernous sinus and superior orbital fissure).

Airos DX: Phantom Twilight is a video game from Nintendo design for Wii and Gamecube. The game takes many legends and myths about a fantasy world thousands of years ago. The Gamecube version is the same as the Wii. It is rated T for some blood, violence, and some Nudity. The main character, Airos, is a sixteen year old teen, whose life gets turned upside down with many supernatural things.

[edit] Plot

In the year 1698, Airos, a 16-year-old teenager is on a vacation, when all of the sudden, he notices weird, and unatural things. He follows a dark being into the Forbidden Woods, and he finds a sword. After a landslide, he returns back to his village. He checks out his house, in the basement, to discover a young woman. The girl disappears, and follows Airos to the Phantom Twilight.

Once Airos is inside the dark realm, he transforms into a Dark Demon. He is imprisoned, and held executed. The girl appears again, letting Airos free. Her name is Persephone. Airos and Persephone make an escape to an important room. There, Airos speaks with Rauru, guardian of the sages. Airos is told who rules the twilight. Then, Airos must find the Four Goddesses. Airos sets out to find the four.

Airos finds the Four Goddesses, and is afterwards, ambushed by King Tyrant.
