[edit] Out of Order with Jed The Fish

Radio show. Gets 48 Google hits (when excluding Wikipedia mirrors). Advertisement? Punkmorten 22:19, 12 July 2006 (UTC)

  • Delete no local radio shows, please. AdamBiswanger1 01:53, 13 July 2006 (UTC)
  • Comment. As I doubt whether there are any truly global radio shows, aren't all radio shows therefore local? Markb 14:19, 13 July 2006 (UTC)
  • Comment Well there are many syndicated radio shows, such as the Free Beer & Hot Wings Morning Show or Howard Stern. AdamBiswanger1 17:42, 13 July 2006 (UTC)
    • It is easy to draw a perfectly logical distinction between at least three levels (local, regional, national). I suppose if there were a truly global radio show, you would argue that everything is still local because no radio-waves from it have reached the Andromeda galaxy yet? — Haeleth
My point is quite simple, all radio stations are local, and hence regional. I can't receive Free Beer & Hot Wings Morning Show (and from it's title I'm probably not missing anything), it is therefore a local programme. Haeleth , pop over to Andromeda and let me know what the local radio is like. Markb 06:51, 14 July 2006 (UTC)

Talk 19:19, 13 July 2006 (UTC)

  • Delete. At least this one made an attempt at inflating its self-importance. Any "world famous... DJ" is suspect though. Not notable. Grandmasterka 05:32, 13 July 2006 (UTC)
  • Delete: per previous two --David.Mestel 06:41, 13 July 2006 (UTC)
  • Delete, another non-notable radio show. --Coredesat talk. o.o;; 07:32, 13 July 2006 (UTC)
  • Delete for a lack of google hits. MichaelBillington 12:23, 13 July 2006 (UTC)
  • Delete per above. Zos 16:57, 13 July 2006 (UTC)
  • Delete Google hits are not the definitive criterion in deciding deletions but it does provide one means of measure. This show fails WP:N -- Alias Flood 18:23, 13 July 2006 (UTC)
  • Keep Nationally syndicated, top-whatever countdown. See Delilah Rene, American Top 40, American Country Countdown, Country Countdown USA, etc. for precedent. ONUnicorn 18:26, 13 July 2006 (UTC)
  • Delete, no evidence of notability, no influence in the press, no major fansites.
    The "unofficial site" linked in the article is particularly amusing: the text is a straight copyvio of the Westwood One page, and the "message board" link leads to a Yahoo! group set up exactly a week ago, with a grand total of 1 member. Looks like this isn't a particularly popular show... — Haeleth Talk 19:19, 13 July 2006 (UTC)
