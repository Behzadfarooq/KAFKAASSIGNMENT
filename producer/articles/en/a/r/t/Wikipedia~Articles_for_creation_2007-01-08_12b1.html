[edit] Mikea

This request for creation has been reviewed. The result is below.


Mikea is a social identity for Malagasy speaking horticulturalists/foragers of southwestern Madagascar. Mikea identity is flexible and complex, linked to living in the forest and using forest resources. Foraging and maize horticulture connect the Mikea with local, regional, and global economies. Being Mikea also entails managing connotations of primitivism with the social and economic consequences of government, researcher, missionary, and tourist interest in hunter-gatherers.

[edit] Horse Chestnut (horse)

This request for creation has been reviewed. The result is below.


Horse Chestnut (born on 19 August 1995)is a champion thoroughbred racehorse bred in South Africa by Harry and Bridget Oppenheimer at their Mauritzfontein Stud in Kimberley. He won the South African Triple Crown and was awarded both Horse of the Year and Champion 3-year-old Colt at three. He ran 10 races, winning 9 and being placed 3rd once. Some notable wins include the Group I J&B Met over 2000m by 8-and-a-quarter lengths, the Group I SA Derby over 2450m by 10 lengths and the Group I Classic over 1800m by 4 lengths. In his only international race, the Group III Broward Handicap over 1700m, Horse Chestnut won by 5-and-a-half lengths. During his preparation for the Group I Donn Handicap, Horse Chestnut fractured a piece of his splint bone on his near-foreleg, resulting in his early retirement from racing. The Oppenheimer's subsequently sold the majority of shares in Horse Chestnut to Seth Hancock's Claiborne Farm in Kentucky where he went to stud.


Horse Chestnut was sired by the champion stallion Fort Wood (USA) out of the South African broodmare London Wall. His pedigree includes such big names in thoroughbred breeding as Fall Aspen (USA), Sadler's Wells (USA), Shantung (FR), Grey Sovereign (GB), Hail To Reason (USA) and Northern Dancer (USA).


Some of Horse Chestnut's most notable offspring include Lucifer's Stone, Spanish Chestnut, Smart Enough and Fete.

Football Club Woot (FC Woot) is a English football club currently based in Runcorn. The club was formed in November 2006 by Runcorn supporters, many of whom decided to withdraw their financial support for the club following the clubs decision to move the club away from Runcorn. FC Woot currently enjoy a healthy rivalry with FC Riverside and Riverside Plate.

FC Woot have become something of a cult figure amongst the Runcorn population, with local newspapers reporting the games frequently. FC Woot’s first season is currently being played with the team making a respectable start against tough opposition. Manager Karl Schön was unavailable for the beginning of the season due to personal reasons, which will also be a contributing factor to the teams less than impressive start.

Regardless of the start, both the club and the fans have high hopes for the current season.

[edit] History

[edit] Formation

The club was formed by the ideas of current player Gareth Reed and current manager Karl Schön. In November 2006 the club officially joined the league and, after a pre season friendly with rivals Riverside Plate, played their debut match on 5 December against Beechwood resulting in a 3-1 defeat for FC Woot.[1]

[edit] 2006-07 season

Before the season began, FC Woot played a friendly match against rivals Riverside Plate resulting in FC Woot suffering a crippling 9-1 defeat against a much stronger side. In the disappointment of the result though, history was made that day. Striker Gareth Reed scored the first goal in the club’s history. The early defeat dented the confidence of the side going into the season. [2] The first match of the season also resulted in defeat, 3-1 the score this time against a strong Beechwood side. FC Woot however, put these defeats behind them and on the 19 December they recorded the first win in their history with a 3-2 victory over Runcorn FC. This result was flattering for Runcorn as they were dominated by FC Woot throughout. The first match of 2007 however, FC Woot took a big step back with another poor performance against the Pavillions Veterans, losing 5-1. [3] Many blame the defeat on the winter break resulting in a lack of preparation and consistency.

[edit] Smith & Sons Cafeterias

Smith & Sons Cafeteria (or S&S Cafeteria, as it is more commonly known) is a small chain of cafeteria-style restaurants in the deep south that serve traditional home-cooked style foods, such as roast beef, mashed potatoes, fried okra, etc. The first S&S was started in Columbus, GA. in 1936. Later on, they moved their corporate headquarters to Macon, GA, where they currently operate two of their restaurants. They also have locations in Atlanta, Augusta, and in South Carolina and Tennessee. They had a restaurant in Roanoke, Virginia, but due to the falling popularity of cafeteria and home cooked foods, they were forced to close their doors. Through their S&S Food Administrators, they also provide food service to hospitals, nursing homes, and certain industries.

For people who wish to have S&S style food at home, they also sell food mixes for their biscuits, beef gravy, cornbread, dressing, whipped potatoes, and poultry gravy.

1989 Tiananmen Square Massacre By, Melissa Ishikawa Intro: Imagine, in only two single days, ten thousand people are severely injured or killed. The survivors are either imprisoned, or sentenced to death. Only because they lead a protest against the government. But they took it too far, and got too violent. Did the citizens and students of Beijing, China deserve the punishment for taking a stand for what they believed in? Summary of What Occurred: Tiananmen was built in 1417 in the Ming dynasty. During the Ming and Qing dynasties, no public square existed at Tiananmen, the area was filled with offices for imperial ministries. Tiananmen Square was badly damaged after the Boxer Rebellion. So the area was cleared to build the Tiananmen Square. Tiananmen Square has held many political events such as the proclamation of the Peoples Republic of China lead by Mao Zedong on October 1, 1949, and for mass rallies during the Cultural Revolution. It also held a number of protest movements such as the May Fourth Movement of 1919 for science and democracy, protests after the death of Zhou Enlai in 1976, and the Tiananmen Square protests of 1989. Many problems existed in China even four years before the unfortunate event at Tiananmen Square. Leader Deng Xiaoping’s ideas focused more on economic reform. The west made an assumption that this would slowly move china towards capitalism and democracy. “The Chinese thought of a compromise between a market economy, a command economy, and a socialist economy [would] be possible. However, the Chinese seemed to be having spurts—moving ahead, then stopping.” Said Pye, who is an expert on China. When Deng began his reforms, discontent grew and led to agricultural problems, leading to government to give peasants IOUs instead of cash for their grain. Because of Deng’s reforms industry slowed, including low labor productivity and slothfulness. Large differences between state-set and free-market prices created difficulties, including an overheated economy and high levels of corruption (Tinanmen Square Protests of 1989). The workers of China were against Stalinism and capitalism. (Conachy). About five thousand unarmed troops were turned back by students and citizens as they attempted to reach Tiananmen Square on foot on June 3, of 1989. Later that day, police of Beijing fired tear gas on crowds gathered near the Zhongnanhai leadership compound. In the late afternoon of that day, ten to fifteen thousand troops in large trucks and convoys moved toward the city center. The troops in the trucks and convoys were helmeted and armed with automatic weapons. By the evening of June 3, the crackdown at Tiananmen Square had begun. Trucks and tanks came into the city from several different directions. Civilians turned out in massive numbers to prevent the troops from advancing on the square for seven hours! Demonstrators set things on fire, and besieged troops with rocks, bottles, and bricks. PLA troops broke the windows of shops, banks, and other buildings. A column of fifty tanks and trucks entered Tiananmen from the east on the morning of June fourth, 1989. “More than seventy tanks moving at fast speed headed toward the square”. This was within an hour, reported nearby cable fifteen. Deaths from the military assault on Tiananmen Square range from one-hundred-eighty to five-hundred! Thousands more had been injured. One woman said she witnessed one tank running over eleven people. About ten thousand people were killed and/or injured in the Tiananmen Square Massacre (Tiananmen Square, 1989 The Declassified History). A total of forty thousand soldiers moved into the Tiananen Square in the night of June 3, 1989. Some forty thousand people were arrested in only June and July after the Tiananmen Square Massacre, and dozens of workers were sentenced to death (Conachy). The violence had occurred in the night between June 3rd and 4th of 1989 (Tiananmen Square, 1989 The Declassified History). Portable Cord

A portable cord, which is also known as portable cordage or flexible cord, is a cable with multiple conductors used for functions requiring flexibility. The bendable cord can be employed for power in a range of applications, such as operating motors in small and large tools, equipment, power extensions, appliances and machinery.

Portable cords may be used in commercial, industrial and residential applications. They work well on jobsites where resistance to oil, chemicals and abrasion is vital and also perform well in extreme environments - both the heat and the cold, outside or inside. Additionally, some portable cords can be water-resistant or water submersible. Because of their characteristics, portable cords are commonly utilized in a range of facilities, such as construction sites, mills, mines, sports complexes, or even marinas.

Although the construction of a portable cord varies depending on the type, a standard cord has at least two stranded copper conductors. The copper stranding, insulation and jacket directly influence the physical properties of the cord.

A variety of portable cords, differing in styles, lengths and thicknesses, exist in the marketplace. Common types include Type SJT, SVT, SEOW, SJ, SJOW, SO and SOW. Each has specific applications associated with it. All portable cords are usually comprised of thermoset, thermoplastic elastomer, or thermoplastic. Thermoset cords have heavy-duty-grade rubber jackets and are extremely sturdy. Thermoplastic elastomer cords have medium-duty-grade thermoplastic elastomer jackets and perform well in cold conditions. Thermoplastic cords have a light-duty plastic compound jacket and thus work for light-duty use.


Portable Cord Connotations

The letters used to describe portable cords hold significance. Below are the meanings for each letter.

S = 600 Volt Service Cord J = Junior Service - 300 Volt T = Thermoplastic E = Elastomer - thermoplastic that looks and feels like rubber O = Oil Resistant Outer Jacket OO = Oil Resistant Outer Jacket and Oil Resistant Insulation W = CSA Weather and Water Resistant (approved for indoor and outdoor use)

Safety Even though portable cords are essentially safe, always handle them carefully. Breakage in a portable cord could be a potential hazard, so be certain equipment is running properly. A qualified person should inspect contractor-grade portable cords before and after each use to check for damage.

[edit] Nottebohm case

The Nottebohm case standed in front of the International Court of Justice in 1953. The Applicant was Lichtenstein and the Respondent was Guatemala.

The case in dispute regarded the granting of the Lichtenstein nationality to Mr. Nottebohm. The ICJ decided that Mr. Nottebohm could not claim his being a Lichtenstein national, unless there had been a solid connection between him and the State of Lichtenstein.

[edit] Black River (Connecticut River), Vermont, USA

This request for creation has been reviewed. The result is below.


The watershed, or drainage basin, of the Black River consists of some 202 square miles in southeastern Vermont, almost all of which lies in Windsor County.

The Black River begins in a small body of water called Black Pond, in Plymouth, Vermont. Winding its way toward the town of Ludlow, its narrow flow picks up a variety of small brooks and streams and is cooled by overhanging branches. Passing through Amherst Lake, Lake Rescue, and Echo Lake, the Black River picks up more tributaries and winds through the town of Ludlow, the home of Okemo Mountain Ski Resort. Tumbling through impressive Cavendish Gorge, the river widens as it passes under the Downers Covered Bridge and enters Perkinsville.

The North Branch of the Black River, its headwaters in Reading, VT, joins the main stem in Weathersfield at the flood control dam. Built by the Army Corps of Engineers between 1957-60, the dam holds back waters to protect the downstream towns of North Springfield and Springfield from floods.

The Black River pushes on through these final two towns, past small dams and many industrial sites, until its final confluence with the mighty Connecticut River at Hoyt's Landing, across from New Hampshire's Fort No. 4 in Charlestown.

Since the settlement of Springfield in the mid-1700's, the river has been at its heart. A source of power for the many industrial factories built on her shores, many small hydro dams were built near the impressive Comtu Falls in the center of Springfield.

Flooding was a common occurrence on the Black River, and there are many residents who still remember the rampage of 1927. A late-season tropical storm brought torrential rains to the lower towns of the watershed in early November. The small tributary streams overflowed their banks, gushing thousands of gallons of cold water into the already turbid waters of the Black River. Homes were lost, hundreds of thousands of dollars of damage was done, and businesses were destroyed. However, citizens pulled together to save each other and to rebuild their towns and communities.

In recent years, citizens have pulled together to take part in improving the health and in celebrating the heritage and recreation of the Black River, from the Lake Rescue Association and its invasive plant removal activities to the Black River Action Team and its annual cleanup project, visual assessment, and educational programs.

From the lakes in Plymouth to the mountain in Ludlow, from the gorge in Cavendish to the trails and waterfall in Springfield, the Black River is a must-see in Windsor County. There are many sites for recreation, whether a paddle trip, bicycle tour or hike is planned.
