[edit] Briggs Raushcer Reaction

Chemicals:

  • hydrogenperoxide 30%
  • potassium iodate
  • malonic acid
  • manganese(II) sulfate monohydrate
  • conc. H2SO4
  • starch, soluble

Apparatus and glass wares:

  • magnetic stirrer
  • 4 magnetic stirring bars
  • stirring bar remover
  • crystallizing dish 140 × 75
  • 3 beakers 1500 mL
  • 3 beakers 500 mL
  • beaker 150 mL
  • 10 mL measuring pipette (fully graduated 0.1 mL divisions)
  • pipette bulb
  • measuring cylinder 500 mL
  • 3 measuring cylinders 250 mL


Safety precautions:

Iodine is produced. Iodine is toxic - may be fatal if swallowed or inhaled. Harmful by inhalation and through skin absorption. Readily absorbed through skin. Very destructive of mucous membranes and upper respiratory tract, eyes and skin. Severe irritant.

The experiment should be performed in a well ventilated room. Safety goggles and gloves must be worn when handling sulfuric acid, malonic acid and 30% H2O2.


Experimental procedure:

The following solutions are prepared: Solution A: 410 ml of 30% hydrogenperoxide are diluted to 1.0 liter with distilled water in a 1500 mL beaker. Solution B: 43 g of potassium iodate are dissolved in about 800 ml of dist. water + 4.3 ml of conc. sulfuric acid in a 1500 mL beaker. The mixture is made up to 1000 mL with dist. water Solution C: 16 g of malonic acid and 3.4 g of manganese (II) sulfate monohydrate are dissolved in about 500 ml of dist. water in a 1500 mL beaker. 3 g of soluble starch are dissolved in 100 mL of boiling dist. water in a 150 mL beaker. The starch solution is poured into the solution of malonic acid and manganese sulfate. The mixture is diluted with dist. water to 1000 mL.

250 mL of each the solutions A and B are poured into a crystallizing dish. The mixture is stirred vigorously with a magnetic stirrer.

Result:

The initially colorless solution becomes amber almost immediately. Then it suddenly turns blue-black. The blue-black fades to colorless. Thus the color of the solution oscillates between clear, brownish and deep blue

[edit] North-South divide in Italy

Italy, like many countries, has a north-south divide, with the northern part being the wealthiest and the southern the poorest.

[edit] Origins

The divide came into being in the late Middle Ages when the city-states of Northern Italy prospered economically and culturally as a consequence of the Renaissance, while the south was a Spanish colony all but in name. As a result the South was exploited and ripped of the money and resources it needed to prosper.

In the 19th century the division became more marked as the northern states, which were ruled by rulers who were friendlier to industrialisation than their southern counterparts, industrialised. Furthermore Northern Italy was more suitable for industrialisation as it was located closer to the industrial heartland of Europe (the United Kingdom, Belgium and Northeastern France) than the South.

As a result the North became industrialized while the South remained agricultural. This division has magnified until the present day as time progressed as the North had gained an industrial base for economic growth, with major Italian corporations (such as Fiat) originating in the North, while the South has a fragile agricultural base that doesn’t offer a lot of economic perspective. The emergence of the Mafia in the South hasn’t helped it either.

[edit] Consequences

The north-south division has a large economic, social, cultural and political impact.

[edit] Economic consequences

The economic consequences are huge with the North being twice as wealthy as the South. The GDP per capita of the North approaches that of other Western European economies, while the South would have the one of the lowest GDP per capita of the former EU-15 if it were independent, scoring only slightly than Portugal and Greece, but far worse than Spain.

[edit] Social and cultural consequences

The divide has created social and cultural differences as well. The mentality of Southern Italy corresponds more with the stereotype of the easy-does-it, colourful Italian, while the mentality of the North resembles the average Western European mentality of punctuality and even stress. The mentality of Southerners is generally more conservative as well, with extended families being more common in the South than in the North, which has a nuclear family model. Family is socially more important in the South than in the North as is the church. It should be noted that these descriptions are mostly stereotypes and that the truth is more balanced.

[edit] Political consequences

The huge differences between North and South have given rise to separatist movements in both North and South, even though Italy has been one country for almost 150 years. The most notable separatist movement is Lega Nord, who wants Northern Italy to declare independence as the state of Padania. Seperatist tendencies are less marked in the South but the South wants more money and autonomy for its economic development. Northerners and Southerners feel disdain for each other with Southerners being characterized as lazy, illiterate and criminal by Northerners, while Southerners see Northerners as arrogant, selfish and aloof.

[edit] Homer Spit

The Homer Spit, a long, narrow finger of land jutting 4.5 miles into Kachemak Bay is located at the southern tip of Alaska's Kenai Penninsula land mass. It is the terminus of the Seward Highway, and is therefore, along with the city of Homer, frequently called the "End of the Road". The Spit is home to manny forms of wildlife including bald eagles, seaotters, seals, numerous shorebirds, and the Homer harbor's 1500 charter and commercial boat operators. It's geograpical uniqueness is rivalled only by its unique, even mystical, ability to capture a wayward visitor's soul with it's panoramic views of Katchemac Bay, Cook Inlet, the surrounding mountains, and the local salty oddities.

[edit] Jawn Henry steam turbine locomotive

In 1954, the Norfolk & Western received a Baldwin-Lima-Hamilton and Babcock & Wilcox-built a steam-turbine-electric named "Jawn Henry" that looked similar to the C&O attempt. It was shorter, weighed less, and had a Babcock & Wilcox "marine type" water tube flash boiler that operated at 900 psi., General Electric traction motors, and rode on four, three axle trucks. All axles were powered so the "Jawn Henry" was a C-C-C-C locomotive. The name given to this locomotive, "Jawn Henry" was taken from the legendary black construction worker who pitted his hammer and drill against a steam drill and died in the attempt.

Although it could be said that the "Jawn Henry" was superior to the Y6b, it lasted only three years. There were a number of problems with 2300. Most of them were worked out during its short life. Some of these problems included the turbine blades getting out of whack when the locomotive coupled onto a train, coal dust kept getting into the electrical machinery, and problems with the feedwater heater and semi-automated boiler controls. Although none of these were a "show stopper", the locomotive was mainly used as a "pusher" and was scrapped in 1958. Perhaps if the 2300 was introduced 10 years earlier and did not have to compete against a four-unit "F" consist, it may have had a longer life.

[edit] Sam Hamill

Sam Hamill is an American poet, perhaps best known for his translations from ancient Greek, Latin, Estonian, Japanese, and Chinese. He was founding editor of Copper Canyon Press and was editor there from 1972 through 2004.

A veteran of the United States Marine Corps between 1961 and 1965, Hamill founded Poets Against War in 1993, compiling the largest single-theme poetry anthology in history, and editing a best-selling selection, Poets Against the War (Nation Books, 2003).

Arterial Duct


The arterial duct is a normal fetal communicating vessel between the pulmonary artery and the aorta. It usually closes within the first few days of life. Since the fetus does not use it's lungs, the arterial duct redirects blood with waste to the placenta, where gas exchange occurs during the 9 months of pregnancy.

Professional: Healthcare executive and entrepreneur. Founded APM Management Consultants in 1974 and built it into national largest healthcare management consulting firm.
