Art in America

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Art in America, published since 1913, is an illustrated monthly art magazine covering the visual art world both in the US and abroad, but concentrating on New York City. The magazine is now published by Brant Publications, itself founded in 1984, which is based in New York's Soho district. Art In America magazine is considered to be one of the two most important journals in the world covering contemporary art, its chief competitor being Artforum, also based in New York City. Art in America bills itself as "the World's Premiere Art Magazine." Noted for its scholarly yet readable style, especially under current Editor in Chief Elizabeth C. Baker, Art in America is widely read by art dealers, collectors, historians, art professionals, and others. It contains news and criticism of painting, sculpture, photography, installation art, performance art, video and architecture in exhibition reviews, artist profiles, and feature articles.
