[edit] Ajitha

Doesn't require encyclopediac entry rail 01:10, 26 August 2005 (UTC)

  • Expand. K. Ajitha evidently (based on Google search that turned up many references to her and her organization as well as oral history interviews-- apparently her group was involved in a sting operation that uncovered a major sex scandal) is a fairly prominent activist for women's rights and more could be said than is present in the existing article.Crypticfirefly 04:15, 26 August 2005 (UTC)
  • Keep; Seems fair | Celcius 04:49, 26 August 2005 (UTC)
  • Abstain - --Bhadani 05:09, 26 August 2005 (UTC)More comments: I would suggest the editors interested in the entry to please give some sources, if possible, to assess continuation of this entry in wikipedia. I am not doubting good faith. Thanks. --Bhadani 05:15, 26 August 2005 (UTC)
  • Keep. The article was previously a copyvio from [1] which could be used as a reference to expand the article. Wikipedia's article should assert her notability more strongly though. KeithD (talk) 08:08, 26 August 2005 (UTC)
  • Keep seems encyclopedic, although article could be improved, have made some minor improvements myself. PatGallacher 10:27, 2005 August 26 (UTC)
  • Keep, if copyvio is sorted, as IINAG, at least, intends to fill it out (changed from comment). The Hindu Times remembers that she was arrested for attacking police stations in this article from 2003. Same paper also cites her as Anweshi president here in 2004. Alf 13:02, 26 August 2005 (UTC)
  • Keep, but expand: I think that K. Ajitha is notable enough for a Wikipedia article. If it survives this vote, I will try to fill it out somedeal.IINAG 13:59, 25 August 2005 (UTC)
  • Keep and expand as above. Trollderella 16:43, 26 August 2005 (UTC)
  • Keep and expand. *drew 22:06, 27 August 2005 (UTC)
  • Definite Keep She is quite famous (some may say infamous) in Kerala Tintin 05:50, 29 August 2005 (UTC)
