[edit] Robert Prendergast

Sir Robert Prendergast (born Noveber 24 1964, Ham Common, Surrey), is an British entrepreneur. It is estimated that Prendergast is worth upwards of 52 million according to the Sunday Times Rich List 2004.


[edit] Early Life & Career

Prendergast was educated at Richmond Catholic School, despite being atheist, until the age of 14. He left school to work with his older brother in Limerick, Ireland until he was 15, as a brick layer. Prendergast suffered from a mild case of epilepsy during schooling resulting in him not being a good student. From here, Prendergast began to work closely with his brother (a labourer in the south of Ireland) and began his own labouring firm closer to cork at the age of 19.

By 1985, Prendergast was employing over 400 staff, and eventually sold the business in the 1987 for approximatly £110,000. After this, he moved back to England, buying a farmhouse cottage near the most southernly point of Cornwall. In 1989, a local paper the cornish times, published an article suggesting that Prendergast had his first child that year. This then bought crisitisms towards the NHS and suggested breaches of the data protection act. Prendergast never took the case to court.

In the early 90's Prendergast invested in the Disneyland Paris project, and subsequently moved to Paris during this time. He's believed to have worked for 2 - 3 years on the project, before relocating to central Germany to work in late 1993.

[edit] Fakr ad-Din ar-Razi

Fakr ad-Din ar-Razi was a Muslim theologian of the 12th and 13th centuries. He traveled around central Arabia debating scholars on topics dealing with the Koran. He showed no mercy towards rivals and enimies. An example; he helped the Shah of the time kill his brother in one of the Shah's torture chambers. By forcing sects to re-examen their faith, he helped to unify Islam. He wrote over one hundred books and his commentaries on the Koran and other holy texts are considered to be masterpieces.

[edit] RGMII

RGMII is an abbreviation of Reduced Gigabit Media Independent Interface.

RGMII is a reduced pin count version of GMII, reducing the number of used pins from 22 to 12. This is achieved mainly by using Double Data Rate on the data lane.

RGMII is generally used in Ethernet systems to connect Media Access Control (MAC) block to Physical Layer Device (PHY). It can be also used as a direct data connection between two MAC blocks.

Signals used in Data transmit:
- TXD(3:0), 4 bit wide data
- TX_CTL, Transmit Control
- TX_CLK, Transmit Clock

Signals used in Data receive:
- RXD(3:0), 4 bit wide data
- RX_CTL, Transmit Control
- RX_CLK, Transmit Clock

In addition, A control bus called MDIO is used with 2 more pins:
- MDIO, Data
- MDC, clock

Transit bolts are a type of bolt used to secure part of a mechanical appliance during transit. They are typically 4 to 12 inches in length, although any size is possible as it depends on the type of device being transported.

The most common example of transit bolts is the securing of the drum of a washing machine while it is transported from shop to property (or property to property). The drum of a washing machine is weighted and would damage the electronic internals if it were to swing into them while being transported.

Failing to remove transit bolts before using the machine can, ironically, also damage the machine.

Many workers of UVa cannot afford to live in Charlottesville. The Dick Schaap Award was established in 2002 to honor the memory of one of Americas pre-eminent sports writers, Dick Schaap. The award is presented by the Nassau County Sports Commission and is given out to the journalist, in any medium, that best exemplifies the principles and talents of Dick Schaap during the past year. The award recipient is determined by confidential balloting of the Dick Schaap Selection Committee, which is comprised of respected members of the media, and chaired by Dick's son, ESPN reporter Jeremy Schaap.

Although the recipient need not be a "sports journalist," he/she must convey the passion and insight for the stories and people he/she covers as Schaap did. Whether it was a sports segment, a cultural news story as he did on "20/20," an autobiography, newspaper story, or "parting shot" from his Sunday morning talk show on ESPN, Schaap's professionalism shined and was admired by many fellow journalists, as well as his readers & viewers.

[edit] MOISES MONTEFERRO

The multidisciplinary artist Moises Monteferro (born in Vigo (Spain), February 27, 1963 is New York based since 1995.

Largely self-taught, his first abstractions gave way to a series of paintings with a strong expressionist style where monochromes and nervous brush strokes were his main characteristics. In the mid 1970's, Monteferro worked with the Saragossa's painter Fernando Artal. During this time, he was greatly influenced by Antoni Tapies, and his works began to acquire texture and consistency.

In the early 80's, Monteferro discovered the works of Miguel Barcelo in Documenta7, Kassel (1982). In Santiago de Compostela, he became acquainted with the Galician post-dictatorship avant-garde movement, while he was studying in the University.

From 1988 thru 1991, Monteferro regularly traveled to Paris, residing in the city the last six months of 1991. During an exhibition in the Galerie Yvon Lambert (1990) in Paris, he finally met Barcelo in person. From that moment, he decided to leave his home (Spain) in search for his identity.

In 1992, he began his search in Mexico, where he resided for almost two years. His first individual exhibition was held in Mexico City in the Galleria MADRAS (1993). His work evolved towards contrasts and colors without abandoning his obsession for texture and "substance". Personally experiencing the Zapatista uprising in the early 1990's, he traveled to Chiapas, southern Mexico. Abour this time, he began his interest in photography.

In 1994, Monteferro returned to Spain. After a brief stay, he moved to Milan, Italy, where he became acquainted with the work of Francesco Clemente and to the reactions of other Italian painters towards Povera art. This generated a rupture in his artistic conceptions. His work, while remaining abstract, began to develop figurative elements. Monteferro held an exhibition in the DICIANNOVE Gallery of Milano the same year. An infinite longing for novelty and experimentation compelled him to travel to America. In 1995, Monteferro established his studio in New York, where he still lives and works.

His current works reflect human shapes, landscapes and writings concealed through abstraction, which, in his own words, "try to write poetry on a different media, canvas. In essence, I am a poet with my hands stained with oil and melancholy."

[edit] Olizier Zunz

Olivier Zunz is the commonwealth professor of history at the University of Virginia, where he has been since 1978. A prominent social and intellectual history of the United States, Zunz began as an urban historian, working on the history of inequality in Detroit at the turn of the century. He has since broadened the scope of his scholarship to produce excellent studies of how the U.S. became "corporate" from the 1880s to the 1920s and in explaining the meaning of the "American century" during the whole of the twentieth century. He has published numerous books and articles.
