[edit] Air Tool Care and Use

KC Tools International:

AIR REGULATORS: Clean, dry and lubricated air at the correct pressure is the most important requirement for perfect operation of air tools. KC Tools range of air tools are designed to operate at 90 P.S.I. unless otherwise specificed.

MOISTURE SEPARATORS: On installations where the pipping system does not provide clean, dry air the use of filters at hose connections points is recommended.

STRAINERS/FILTERS: Many tools arte provided with air strainers or filters. To eliminate air restriction these strainers must be kept clean and free of obstructions. Strainers or filters should be used at each hose outlet.

LUBRICATION: Many pneumatic tools, due to weight and space limitations do not contain built-in lubricators. Air line lubricators are recommended with all air tools.

MOISTURE ELIMINATION: The amount of moisture carried by compressed air varies with the temperature. As the temperature drops after compression, water is precipitated. To eliminate as much water as possible, compressors should be equiped with intercoolers, aftercoolers and full size receivers. These should be kept drained and in good working condition.

COMPRESSOR AIR INTAKE: Locate the air intake to obtain the coolest, driest and cleanest air possible. Intakes should be of ample size and as direct as possible. Avoid dusty or wet locations.

PIPING SYSTEM: Air mains and lines should be large enough to avoid excessive pressure loss under heavy working conditions. Pipe lines should be provided with drains for trapping water before it reaches the hose outlet. The ‘pitch’ of the mains line should be in the direction of the air flow so that gravity and air flow will carry the water to traps located at regular intervals. Provision for quick and positive inspection of operation for leakage should be made when traps are installed. Taps in the drains are usually provided for draining and inspection of water collected. To prevent moisture from reaching the tools, down pipes or hose connections should never be taken directly from the bottom of air pipes or mains. Connections should be made at the top of the a main line. Valves and hose connections should be large enough to pass the maximum amount of air required by the tool or tools on the line.

KC Tools International

Polymers

Polymer is a term used to describe molecules consisting of structural units and a large number of repeating units connected by covalent chemical bonds. The term is derived from the Greek words: polys meaning many, and meros meaning parts . The key feature that distinguishes polymers from other molecules is the repetition of many identical, similar, or complementary molecular subunits in these chains. These subunits, the monomers, are small molecules of low to moderate molecular weight, and are linked to each other during a chemical reaction called polymerisation.

Polymers are dived into four sections: Thermosets Thermoplastics Elastomers Coordiantion polymers

Polymers are often named in terms of the monomer from which they are made. The attractive forces between polymer chains play a large part in determining a polymer's properties. Polymers can be seen as tangled spaghetti chains, pulling any one of the spaggheti strand out is a lot harder the more tangled the chains are.

Thermosets

Thermosets are polymer materials that cure, through the addition of energy, to a stronger form. The energy may be in the form of heat through a chemical reaction or irradiation. Thermoset materials are usually liquid, powder and designed to be moulded into their final form. The curing process transforms the thermoset into a plastic or rubber by cross-linking. Energy is added to cause the molucar chains to link into a rigid 3d structure. A Thermoset material cannot be melted or remodeled after its cured. Thermoset materials are usually stronger than thermoplastic materials and are also better suited to high tempreture. You cannot recycle thermosets like thermoplastics.

Thermoplastics

A thermoplastic is a material that is plastic or deformable, melts to a liquid when heated and freezes to a brittle, glassy state when cooled sufficiently. Most thermoplastics are high molecular weight polymers whose chains associate through weak forces. Thermoplastics are elastic and flexible.Thermoplastics can go through melting/freezing cycles repeatedly and the fact that they can be reshaped upon reheating gives them their name.

Elastomers

The term elastomer is often used as the term rubber. Elastomer comes from two terms: Elastic (describing the ability of a material to return to its originol shape and a chunk is removed) and mer. ( Taken from polymer). Each link of the chain is usually made from carbon, hydrogen, oxygen and silicon. To make the chain ‘Mers’ and linked together. Elastomers primary use is for seals and modleded flexible parts. Elastomers are usually thermosets but also may be thermoplastics. The molucar structure of an elastomer can be described as a spagheti and meatball design, the meatballs signifying cross-links.

Coordination polymers

Coordination polymer is the term given in inorganic chemistry to a metal coordination compound. One of the first coordination compounds ever studied systematically, Prussian Blue, is a coordination polymer based on Fe-CN-Fe links.

Polymerization

Polymerization is a process of reacting monomer molecules together in a chemical reaction to form three-dimensional networks or polymer chains . There are many forms of polymerization and different systems exist to categorize them. Polymerization dates back to the beginning of DNA based life. The first polymers of the 19th century were actually formed my changing natural polymers.

Polyethylene Polyethene is a thermoplastic heavily used in consumer products. Its nanes originates from the monomer ethane, which created the polymer. Polyethylene is created through polymerization of ethene. Polyethylene is classified into several different categories based mostly on its density. Polyethylene was first created German chemist Hans von Pechmann, who prepared it by accident in 1898 while heating diazomethane. Polystyrene

Polystyrene is a polymer made from the monomer styrene, a liquid hydrocarbon that is manufactured from petroleum. At room temperature, polystyrene is normally a solid thermoplastic, but can be melted at higher temperature for molding or extrusion, then resolidified. Polystyrene was accidentally discovered in 1839 by Eduard Simon.

Polyester Polyester is a category of polymers, or, more specifically condensation polymers. Usually, polyester refers to cloth woven from polyester fiber. Polyester clothing is generally considered to have a less natural feeling to it compared to natural fibers. Polyester fibers are often spun together with fibers of cotton, producing a cloth with some of the better properties of each. polyester generally refers to the large family of plastics. Polyester is combustible but due to its thermoplastic nature.

Polycarbonate Polycarbonates are a particular group of thermoplastics. They Are easily modedled and formed as these plastics are wildly used in manufacturing.

Electroactive polymers

Electroactive polymers are polymers whose shape is modified when a voltage is applied to them. Thye can be used as sensors. These are often called artificial muscels because they are used in the field of robots.

Biopolymers Biopolymers are a special class of polymers found only in living organisms. Starch, proteins and peptides, DNA, and RNA are all examples of biopolymers. [edit] Rhyndaston Tunnel

Rhyndaston Tunnel is 955m long on the main rail line from Hobart to Launceston/Burnie. The tunnel is on a slope of 1 in 40 and rises in the direction of Launceston. The tunnel was built in 1873-1876 and enlarged in 1964-1965. Despite this, current diesel locomotives in operation on often stall metres from the uphill end due to oxygen deprivation (See Rathole Tunnel in Wikipedia).

The Australian Federal government in conjunction with the Tasmanian government have allocated money to the maintenance of the main rail line and may investigate widening the tunnel further or finding an alternative uphill route.

Pacific National, the freight company utilising the rail line have also signed a Memorandum of Understanding with the State Government to invest in new locomotives. These may not have the same difficulties as the current ones in travelling uphill through the tunnel.

[edit] Longmynd Hike

The Longmynd Hike is a 50 mile competitive hike that must be completed in under 24 hours. It takes place annually (usually the first weekend in October) in the Longmynd Hills near Church Stretton, Shropshire. The hike has been running since 1967 (with the exception of 2001 due to the foot and mouth outbreak), with Fl/Lt Barker winning the first Longmynd staff in a time of 21 hours 24 minutes. The current course record holder is David Wade with his 2004 time of 8 hours 3 minutes.

[edit] Bokomolech

Bokomolech is a band from Athens, Greece. The members met sometime in 1991 in the National Technical University of Athens. They started making an impression on indie audiences in 1993, after having played at the Indie Free Festival. Their name comes from a Ukrainian physician, Alexander Alexandrovic Bogomoletz, who came up with a drug of the same name some time in the 1950's, supposedly postponing the ageing of cells in the human body. Their debut LP " Xero " (1995 - Lazy Dog Records, which an important indie label from Salonica)was described by some critics as the best Greek Rock Record (with english lyrics) ever. They later signed with Hitch-Hyke and released the E.P. " Insect(songs) ". Steve Albini was impressed by their music and invited them to Chicago to work on a record together at his own well-known studios "Electrical Audio". The product of this collaboration was a double L.P., "Jet Lag". Steve and Shellac fitted an extra day in their European tour that autumn, so that they could play in Athens with Bokomolech. Their next release was another E.P., entitled "Slowburner", which was produced by one of the best musicians/producers in Greece, Coti.K. " Exit: Trance " is their last L.P. to date and was recorded in the summer of 2003, once again with Steve Albini, but this time in Iain Burgess' Black Box Studio in rural France. The album was very well received (as always). It was their first L.P. that got properly distributed abroad:Exit: Trance is out in Portugal and Germany.


Bokomolech are: Dimitris Ioannou - singing / dimitris@bokomolech.gr Lila Katerinaki - bass / lila@bokomolech.gr Vlassis Karagiannis - drums / vlasis@bokomolech.gr Kostas Ragoussis - guitar / kostas@bokomolech.gr

Past members: Evi Fraghedaki - guitars (on all L.P.'s except "Exit: Trance")

[edit] Paddy Reilly

Paddy Reilly served as President of NUI, Galway Students' Union from July 2004 to June 2006. He oversaw significant structural changes to the union, including the incorporation of the union's commercial interests and the renovation of the union's club premises, the College Bar.

A native of Gortletteragh, Co. Leitrim, he was educated at Moyne Community School, Co. Longford from 1992-98, before completing a Bachelor's degree in electronic and computer engineering at NUI, Galway (1998-2002).

In September 2001, he was elected Clubs' Captain of NUI, Galway Students' Union, a part-time position. In March 2002, he was elected to serve as Vice-President / Education Officer. In March 2003, he was re-elected unopposed to the position, becoming the first officer to serve two terms in this position. In March 2004, he was elected as President and was subsequently re-elected in March 2005. He is the only the second person to have served two terms as President of the union (after Paddy Jordan in 1999-2001) and is the only person to have served four terms as a full-time officer (or five terms in total).

This article primarily concerns the biography of predoninantly well-known writer and student activist in the Maritimes, Canada who has written published articles for the Brunswickan.

Basil Khan (born Basil Junaid Khan, pronounced "Bāassil Junayd Khân") was born on May 26, 1984, and is currently the the 2006/07 VP public affairs representative for the Muslim Student Association at the University of New Brunswick in Fredericton, NB.

Born to first generation Pakistani parents, his father, a graduate from NED, Karachi, originally migrated to the U.S. only to complete a Masters in Civil Engineering from IIT. He was later impressed by the somewhat stable and highly educated society, but shifted to Toronto, Ontario to avoid a military draft to the Viet Nam war. His mother completed an Arts and Education degree from Lahore, Pakistan. The two currently reside in Alberta. His family is a member of ISNA and have attended conferences in Chicago and Toronto. His mother is a member of Women’s division of the Muslim Association of Calgary, and Basil Khan himself has volunteered for MAC. The Khan family has travelled in various parts of the word, including Dubai, Thailand, Saudi Arabi, Turkey, New York, Florida, and California.

Basil was born and raised in Sarnia, Ontario (15 minutes from the U.S. border) in Saint John’s hospital. Surrounded by a predominantly non-Muslim Caucasian society of 75,000, he attended the local high school and a weekly Islamic school along with 200 other first-generation Muslim families who previously founded the mosque.

Basil's family origins range from Mogul ancestors to Muzaffarnagar, Uttar Pradesh, with traces leading back to Persia, Turkey, and Afghanistan. The majority of his family relatives currently reside in Lahore, Islamabad, and Karachi. He is the youngest of three. His oldest sister is a medical doctor in Syracuse, NY, and is married to an oncologist, who is a graduate from Aga Khan Medical College. The younger sister obtained an honours in education from York University, Toronto and is married to a lawyer, a graduate from the University of Bridgeport School of Law. Both currently reside in the U.S.

At 17, Basil’s father found a job in the oil-rich province of Alberta in Calgary, where he completed high school. In 2003, Mr. Khan was accepted into the engineering program at the University of New Brunswick, but in 2006 changed into the BBA program. He is still currently an active Muslim-Canadian, with connections in Toronto, New York, Florida, and Ohio. In an interview with the International Student Newsletter on campus he stated "My goal is to bridge the gap between the Muslim World and the non-Muslim world, as there is much tension, especially after September the eleventh. I believe both these societies have much to learn from each other, for the better." He emphasezes his role as a citizen, and that he basically wants to “help people.”

[edit] The Virtual Machine Shop

The Virtual Machine Shop is an online training assistant providing machine tool instruction for the student, hobbyist, and professional machinist. It is supported by donation, advertisement, and volunteers from academia and industry.

HISTORY

The Virtual Machine Shop (VMS) has its roots in a grant from the United States Department of Labor in 2001. The two year grant was awarded to a consortium of colleges and universities and to Gateway Cities Partnership, an economic development agency in southeast Los Angeles, California. The consortium of Colleges and Universities were:

California State University, Long Beach

Cerritos College

Compton College

Long Beach City College

Rio Hondo College

University of Southern California

Over a two year period instructors and industry professionals developed videos and animations of machine tool processes and placed them on the web as the VMS. At the conclusion of the grant the VMS was no longer supported by consortium members and in 2004 Ronald Smith, Department Chair of the Machine Tool Technology Department at Cerritos College, collected the various assets and re-published them on the web.

LINK

http://www.jjjtrain.com

TYPE OF INSTRUCTION

The Virtual Machine Shop is not a curriculum and it does not have a course outline. Instead it provides a vast collection of reference data supported by videos, animations, and images that provide overviews of machine tool processes.


NEED

The need for an online Virtual Machine Shop stems from the rapid closure of manual arts programs in high schools and colleges in the United States and the virtual disappearance of traditional apprenticeship programs in industry.

[edit] minor misdemeanor crimes could be aggravated felonies for foreign citizens

Under IIRIRA minor misdemeanor offenses could have drastic immigrations consequences to a lawful permanent resident. See INA § 101(a)(43)(A). The Board of Immigration Appeals (hereinafter “BIA” or “Board”) agrees that misdemeanor convictions fall within the definition of aggravated felony as set forth in Rodriguez-Rodriguez, Int. Dec. 3411 (BIA 1999) (endorced by the Fifth U.S. Circuit Court of Appeals (hereinafter “Fifth Circuit”) in Zavala-sustaita, 214 F.3d 601 (5th Cir. 2000)). However, the issue presented in Rodriguez-Rodriguez, was whether a felony offense under Texas state law met the definition of § 101(a)(43)(A) of the Act, and not whether a misdemeanor conviction can be an aggravated felony. The Board also relies on the Fifth Circuit’s decision in Urias-Escobar, 281 F.3d 165 (5th Cir. 2002) as support for the proposition that misdemeanor offenses are always encompassed by the aggravated felony definition under § 101(a)(43). In Urias-Escobar, the Fifth Circuit held that a misdemeanor assault conviction for which the alien had been sentenced to one year constituted an aggravated felony within the meaning of § 101(a)(43)(F) of the Act, which defines an aggravated felony as a crime of violence for which the term of imprisonment is at least one year. However, Urias-Escobar should not be determinative in issue presented here, because the aggravated felony definition in § 101(a)(43)(A) makes no reference to any specific sentence length, or to any federal provision of law.

In Matter of Crammond, the Board, sitting en banc, in an 11-9 decision, addressed the exact issue presented, and determinated the matter in alien’s favor. However, Crammond was subsequently vacated on jurisdictional grounds after the Board learned that the alien had departed the U.S.. Matter of Crammond, 23 I. & N. Dec. 9 (BIA), vacated, 23 I. & N. Dec. 179 (BIA 2001).

Subsequently to the former Attorney General John Ashcroft’s reorganization and downsizing of the Board, which left only conservative members seated on the Board (see, Courts Criticize Judges’ Handling of Asylum Cases: Pattern of BIAS Alleged, by Adam Liptak, The New York Times, December 16, 2005. See also, Article from Immigration Bulletin, October 1, 2004), a three-member penal decided Matter of Small, 23 I. & N. Dec. 448 (BIA 2002), in which the Board reconsidered its position in Crammond. There, the Board held that a misdemeanor conviction was an aggravated felony within the meaning of § 101(a)(43)(A). Such desperate measures not only violate equal protection of the law, it smacks discrimination based on alienage. In effect, the same offense which might be a misdemeanor for U.S. citizens is classified an aggravated felony for foreign citizens, a crime that is not even a felony. Moreover, the traditional rule of lenity should be applied to reach the conclusion that misdemeanor offenses are not to be included within the aggravated felony definition in that section. See e.g., INS v. Errico, 385 U.S. 214 , 225 (1966) (indicating that doubts as to the correct construction of a statute affording relief from deportation should be resolved in the immigrant’s favor “because deportation is a drastic measure and at times equivalent of banishment or exile”) (quotation marks omitted); INS v. St. Cry, 533 U.S. at 320 (same). Further, the due process clause of the constitution requires that statutes defining criminal offenses set forth prohibited conduct with sufficient specificity so as to provide fair notice to a person of ordinary intelligence that there conduct is included within the definition of an offense, and that they are subject to penalty for violation. The finding of the Executive Office of Immigration Review (“EOIR”) that alien’s misdemeanor convictions are to be included violates due process, because lawful permanent residents are not given fair notice that in pleading guilty to a misdemeanor offense, they are subject to removal from the U.S. as aggravated felons, barred from discretionary relief from removal, and permanently barred from ever returning to the U.S.

Notes:

The series saw the introduction of the mecha units VV-54 Mugen Caliber(a jeep type Vehicle) in later transformer type shows the VV-54 became known as the Roadbuster, VH-64 Ovelon Gazzette (a cobra type helicopter) in later transformer type shows the VH-64 became known as the Whirl, and VT-61 (a double barrel Bradley type tank) into popular culture. The show was originally aired in Japan, and subsequently released with English, Spanish, Japanese, and Arabic soundtracks.

[edit] Death To The Brutes

Death To The Brutes is the title of a historical anarchist poster that was originally printed in France in August of 1943. The poster was signed "International Revolutionary Syndicalist Federation (F.I.S.R.)". 150 copies of the poster were produced.

The text of the poster illustrates well the militant pro-worker, cosmopolitan, anti-militarist and anti-government sentiments common to the Anarcho-syndicalist movement.

Original copies of the poster are held by the C.I.R.A. Library, Centre Internationale de Recherches sur l'Anarchisme, avenue de Beaumont 24, CH-1012 Lausanne, Switzerland.

[edit] Text of Death To The Brutes

Proletarians!

From East to West, from South to North, throughout the world, for three years, to a greater or lesser extent, you have been paying the price of the battle unleashed by your masters. Thousands of proletarians of all countries are dying, while men of finance, politics and war, brutes that they are, congratulate each other, giving speeches, sharing out the benefits, and dividing the wealth and privileges among them selves.

Remember, you veterans of the "war to end all wars," when you came home in 1918, still blood-stained from that infamous butchery which left ten million dead, twenty million injured, ten million permanently disabled, three million missing and millions of widows and orphans--then you said, and promised, NEVER AGAIN! Now, again, the military beasts have got their hands on you. All over the world, men are no longer men, they are serial numbers.

How long will this last? Until the proletarians of the whole world understand that they have only one enemy: their bosses. Until the proletarians of the whole world fraternize, unite and finally charge forward, armed with bayonets still wet with their brothers' blood, to stab in the ass all the governing and war- mongering charlatans.

Proletarians: In 1919 and in 1936 you shouted, "Death to The Brutes!" Now, in 1943, don't shout, ACT. Death to ALL of them, whether they wear the swastika, the red star, the Order of the Garter, the Lorraine Cross or the francisque.

LONG LIVE LIBERTY!

LONG LIVE PEACE!

LONG LIVE THE SOCIAL REVOLUTION!

[edit] LEAPS

The Lothians Equal Access Programme for Schools provides support and encouragement to state school students in Edinburgh and the Lothians whose ability to fulfil their academic potential has been adversely affected by social, cultural or economic circumstances.

LEAPS aims to increase participation in higher education amongst students from non-traditional backgrounds through a programme that includes a pre-application interview and enquiry service, school and university-based events and an eight-week summer school for school leavers. Our work is based upon the premise of creating 'equal opportunity for equal potential'.
