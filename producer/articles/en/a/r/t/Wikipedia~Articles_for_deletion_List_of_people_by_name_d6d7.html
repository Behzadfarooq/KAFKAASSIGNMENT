However, this is not a list that exists for the sake of being a complete reference, as with e.g. lists of the rulers of a country. This is explicitly a navigation tool, and to the extent that it improves access to bios at all, it is succeeding in its primary task.
    _ _ It's also the case that it is regretably far behind the ideal of keeping up with the full complement of bios that we have. IMO reasons for this include the extent of ignorance and underappreciation of its role, and i hasten to add, the exquisite principle of many freely roaming eyes, which practically guarantee that many resources will be undervalued by most of the editors. My current focus is on usability e.g., for those adding names, making the process as self-explanatory as feasible, and for users of all kinds, making navigation within the list as intuitive and even unconscious as feasible -- the reason for the experimental format used on the pilot pages (List of people by name: Caa-Cal is probably the one pilot to view, if you're viewing only one) listed at User:Jerzy/Argus for LoPbN Templates#Pilot pages. There may emerge a bot-master with a mission to, e.g., detect admissions and expulsions from the descendants of Category:People, which would be a giant-step toward "maintainability", and i might personally turn into that bot-master, tho not this month.
    _ _ Bottom line: poorly maintained, but far from unmaintanability.
    --Jerzy•t 17:42, 8 December 2005 (UTC)
  • Keep Could be maintained by a bot. Andjam 04:51, 9 December 2005 (UTC)
  • Freeze, if that's an option; otherwise Delete. if people are correct that this could be handled by a bot, then it's possible, but this bot would have to be really smart and I would guess that it would at least take a long time to develop such a bot, with this page being unreliable in the interim. There must be scores of new "people" pages created every day -- some of them useful, some of them deleteable. some of them LOOKING like they talk about "people", but really talking about eponymous locations & objects, or about fictional characters, etc. Plus, when there's a "list of people by name", why shouldn't MY name be on it?--Mareino 19:14, 9 December 2005 (UTC)
