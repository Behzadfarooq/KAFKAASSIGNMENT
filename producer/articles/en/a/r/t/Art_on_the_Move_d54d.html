Art on the Move is an annual summer arts program held in Detroit, Michigan. As of 2005, the program counted 20 years of programming. The organization promotes cultural values by sponsoring art installations during the summer months. These temporary installations are created by resident artists, who in turn mentor young artists as the public works are executed and erected. Funding for Art on the Move has come from architectural firms in Southwestern Michigan, the City of Detroit and Detroit's Empowerment Zone Development Corporation. According to an interview with staffer Carol Beach, the National Endowment for the Arts had granted financial support to Art on the Move for 2005.

The program has shifted from its original connection with the Detroit people mover, and now provides programming and exhibits for the Detroit Festival of the Arts. At the 16th annual festival, Elizabeth Hoffman, a sculptor trained at Wayne State University fabricated and installed Morphologic Assemblage with the assistance of 500 residents of Detroit's empowerment zone.

Carryn Maslowski has served as Executive Director of Art on the Move

Art on the Move in Detroit has begun a program for alumni, artists who are grateful to Art of the Move for early career recognition. Some of the following artists with Detroit roots are Art on the Move alumni.

  • Lulu Cameron
  • Mare L. Costello
  • Tyree Guyton
  • Elizabeth Hoffman
  • Todd Mitchell
  • Barbara Sivak
