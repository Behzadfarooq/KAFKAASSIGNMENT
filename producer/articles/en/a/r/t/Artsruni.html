Artsruni

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Artsruni (also transliterated as Ardzruni or Ardzrouni) was a region and also a ruling family in old Armenia c. 300–800

In the middle of the 4th century the family was deposed. Chavash survived, and recovered power. In 369 the state was led by Merujan Artsruni who guided the Persian troops to Armenia and defeated the general (sparapet) Mamikonian. The latter recovered power soon after, and Merujan was killed.

Around 772 the Artsruni submitted to the families of Amatuni, Rshtuni, Teruni of Dariunq (before a possession of the Bagratuni) and governed over the regions of Maku, Artaz, Great Zab Valley and Van river. In 908 they forged the principality of Vaspurakan.
