[edit] Speculation on WC3 Human Campaign Storyline

Arthas was essentially forced to kill his own people in order to halt the plague. This was becaused other methods to halt the plague could not succeed while his father was reluctant to impose the quarantine suggested by the Kirin Tor. In hindsight, this would have changed the flow of the entire campaign if the quarantine was enforce.

Regardless, Uther's reluctance to take aggressive measures to cut the cancer from the city (I'm referring to the infected peasants here) slowed Arthas' efforts. This might be seen as forgiveable due to Uther's concientous objection to Arthas' "the end justifies the means" policy. However, when in Northrend, Arthas is taking on the undead in their own territory and has taken Mal'Ganis by suprise. Then Uther comes in and screws everything up: by interferring on moral grounds, it was Uther who made the fall of Lorderan a certainty, as opposed to simply being highly probable.

If anyone wants to paraphrase that analysis, your welcome to, I don't think I could raise that point in a NPOV way. --202.173.194.130 08:06, 14 October 2005 (UTC)

[edit] Arthas' psychopathy

I am putting back in the category "Fictional Psychopaths," because I believe (based on a lay observation) that Arthas qualifies as a psychopath.

Specifically, he fits over 50% of the clinical symptoms of psychopathy:

  1. Glibness/superficial charm - In spades
  2. Grandiose sense of self-worth - Definitely
  3. Need for stimulation/proneness to boredom - His dialogue indicates this
  4. Pathological lying - Unlike the classic psychopath, Arthas is rather blunt with his vicious intentions; however, this is likely because he is in a position of power.
  5. Conning/manipulative - Whoa yeah.
  6. Lack of remorse or guilt - He explicitly states this.
  7. Shallow affect - During the brief period after he has acquired Frostmourne, but before he has destroyed Malganis, he shows this trait.
  8. Callous/lack of empathy - Again, in spades.
  9. Parasitic lifestyle - One could argue that the undead lifestyle is the very epitome of parasitism.
 10. Poor behavioral controls - Not enough information
 11. Promiscuous sexual behavior - Not enough information
 12. Early behavioral problems - Not enough information
 13. Lack of realistic, long-term goals - Not enough information; however, his lack of direction after the Scourge has succeeded in summoning Archimonde suggests that he does indeed lack such direction.
 14. Impulsivity - Yes.
 15. Irresponsibility - Very yes.
 16. Failure to accept responsibility for own actions - Yes.
 17. Many short-term marital relationships - Not enough information
 18. Juvenile delinquency - Not enough information
 19. Revocation of conditional release - Not enough information
 20. Criminal versatility - Not enough information

Arthas satisfies 11 of the 20 and may possibly satisfy 12 clinical symptoms on just a superficial observation. A satisfactory observation of all of the clinical symptoms is diagnostically unnecessary (mental illnesses never perfectly demonstrate every symptom); in my admittedly unprofessional opinion, Arthas Menethil, the Lich King, is a psychopath. Iceberg3k 23:18, 24 August 2006 (UTC)
