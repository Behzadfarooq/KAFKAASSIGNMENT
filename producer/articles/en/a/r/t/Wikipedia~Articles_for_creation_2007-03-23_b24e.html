[edit] Brothers In Arms: War Stories

Brothers In Arms: War Stories is the first outing of the Brothers in Arms (BIA) franchise on the Nintendo DS. The game takes place in various theatres of war in World War: II starting out fighting in Africa and moving on to the Allied invasion of Normandy. Brothers In Arms: War Stories is one of the few Nintendo DS games to recieve an "M" rating by ESRB along with Resident Evil: Deadly Silence. It is as of date unknown if the game will feature a multy player mode, but many suspect that it will.

Katie Gearlds inspirational journey is story of determination, strength, and incredible motivation to become the greatest. A native of Beech Grove, Indiana, Katie grew up playing with the boys and developed a tough and smooth style of basketball. With no goal in their back yard hours were spent dribbling and playing keep away between her younger brother, T.J., and Katie. After picking up a ball at the age of four she has never seemed to be able to put it down. All family vacations, and trips were centered around AAU tournaments, or high school basketball. Katie was named MVP in 2002 of the AAU 17-and-under national tournament after leading Indiana’s Finest Black Cats to the national title.

In 2003, while attending Beech Grove High School, she established herself as one of top women’s basketball players in the country. Katie catapulted her team to a Class 3A state championship. They became the first team in Beech Grove High School history to win a state championship. During her high school career she scored 2,521 points, ranking fourth in Indiana history. Gearlds led the state in scoring as a senior in 2002-03 averaging 30.1 points per game, along with 9.4 rebounds, 5.4 assists and 5.1 steals per game. In addition to basketball,

Katie's AAU and High School basketball careers have given her what some claim to be "legendary" status. People will always remember her three free throws to tie the semi state game at the end of regulation in front of a packed crowd. She was quoted in the Indianapolis Star as saying "I felt so much pressure because I didn't want to let my teammates, my coaches or the community down." It wouldn't have mattered if she did miss those shots, she already had brought all of Beech Grove close to greatness and everyone is appreciative. She will be talked about around here for a long time to come. For the whole community, I will say Thank You, Katie!

In 2003, at the conclusion of her senior year Katie Gearlds was bestowed the honor of becoming Indiana Miss Basketball. In addition Gearlds was also named the 2003 Gatorade Indiana Player of the Year, and 2003 Nike/WBCA All-American, and 2003 McDonald’s All- American. Gearlds became the MVP of the McDonald’s All-American game by scoring 17 points to lead the West team to a victory. Gearlds became the fifth Indiana Miss Basketball player to play at Purdue. Playing at Purdue allowed Gearlds to reconnected with her former AAU teammate, Sharika Webb. During her freshman year, Gearlds, established herself as the teams second leading scorer. By coaches vote she earned Big Ten Freshman of the Year accolade. In addition, Gearlds was named to the Big Ten All-Freshman Team, Honorable mention All- Big Ten, and the Big Ten All-Tournament Team. Purdue University student recognized Katie’s accomplishments by bestowing her the Gimlet Freshman Award.

During her sophomore she shot a Purdue single-seasoned record of 89 percent from the free throw line. In addition she was the team’s assists leaders with five or more assist in eight games. She led the team in minutes per game 35.6. In addition, to remaining the second leading scorer. She was voted Second team All-Big Ten by coaches. After to completion of the year Gearlds underwent ankle reconstructive surgery and was treated for chronic compartment syndrome.

Gearlds seized the opportunity to step up and impact her team during her junior season. Gearlds accepted the challenge and became the teams top leading scorer putting up double figures in 26 of 33 games. In addition she remained Purdue’s career free throw percentage leaders (.844). She also lead the team in assists with 118 total for the year. Katie was also the team’s second best shot blocker for the second year in a row with 28. During her sophomore and junior season Katie Gearlds has started every game, in addition to averaging 35.7 minutes per game. During her Junior year Katie earned Kodak All-District honors, First team All-Big Ten, and Big Ten All-Tournament Team. This past fall Katie was chosen as the recipient of the Durham Brother’s Award. This prestigious award is given by athletic administration to athletes who display character and excellence in all aspects of life.

In preparation for her senior season Katie Gearlds played this past summer under Illinois head coach, Theresa Grentz, in the Big Ten Foreign Tour in Sweden, Norway, and Denmark. Upon her return to the states, Gearlds underwent surgery on both legs for chronic compartment syndrome. Katie’s progress and recover has gone without complications and she is expected to be able to play pain free. Katie’s name appears on 2006 watch list for the WBCA Wade Trophy. This May she will graduate with a bachelor’s degree from Purdue University in sociology.

[edit] STEVE BURKS NCAA FOOTBALL

Steve Burks started officiating football games in 1979. As a scholarship winner and highly recruited running back at Richmond's "Meadowbrook High School 1972-1975,Burks signed with Coach "PatDye" at East Carolina University. After leaving East Carolina 1979 Steve was asked to join his local officials association located in Richmond Virginia. Burks found a new home in the game he loved and worked almost every position. Beginning with youth league football and then on to high school games, Burks found officiating a very rewarding and challanging avocation. By 1990 Burks moved on to college football beginning with the Southland Conference, Mid- American Conference,Western Athletic Conference and Southeastern Conference. Burks has officiated an estimated 175 NCAA Division 1A football games. Durring his career Burks has officiated games at Notre Dame, Army, Hawaii,Stanford, Oklahoma, to name a few. Burks has just completed the honor of working his 6th Bowl game (Liberty Bowl). At present he continues to take the field each weekend of the fall season in his position as "umpire. Burks has been quoted as saying that without the help and support of his supervisors and fellow officials none of this would have been possible. I truly love the college game and everyone associated with college football.

[edit] VERICUT

VERICUT is a solids-based software program that interactively simulates the material removal process of an NC program. The program depicts three to five-axis milling/ drilling and wire EDM operations as well as multi-axis turning and combination mill/turn machining. VERICUT enables an NC programmer to verify the accuracy and quality of the NC program using a computer. Inefficient motion or programming errors that could potentially ruin a part, damage the fixture, or break the cutting tool can be corrected before the program is run on an NC machine tool.

NC Programs from any CAM system and machine code data (G codes) are processed directly in VERICUT.

VERICUT software is developed by CGTech Inc., an Irvine, California based company. VERICUT version 1.0 was released in 1988. VERICUT version 6.1 was released in March of 2007.

[edit] CNC Machine Tool Simulation

VERICUT shows material removal at the workpiece level and simulates entire machine tools as they appear on a shop floor. The program also simulates NC machine controls and supports advanced control functions. The program automatically checks for machine tool collisions and over travel, reducing the possibility of a machine crash.

Machine Simulation detects collisions and near-misses between all machine tool components such as axis slides, heads, turrets, rotary tables, spindles, tool changers, fixtures, work pieces, cutting tools, and other user-defined objects. A user can set up near-miss zones around the components to check for close calls, and detect over-travel errors. Machine movements can also be simulated while stepping or playing backwards in a Review Mode.

A selection of customizable machine models is included with VERICUT software or machine models can be built from scratch. Machine components can be designed in a CAD system or defined in VERICUT. A “Component Tree” feature is used to connect the pieces and manage the kinematics of the machine.

[edit] Optimizing NC programs

VERICUT is equipped with NC program optimization capabilities. Based on initial programmed feed rates, VERICUT automatically determines the optimum safe feed rate for each cut. Cycle time can be reduced significantly. Optimized feed rates also result in longer cutting tool life, fewer broken cutting tools and scrapped parts, better quality parts due to minimized cutter deflection, and a better finish on part surfaces and edges due to constant tool pressure.

[edit] Setting Up and Running a Simulation in VERICUT Software

Three things are required to verify an NC program in VERICUT: a stock model, a cutting tool description, and a tool path. Stock can be defined in VERICUT or imported from a CAD system. Cutting tool descriptions can be contained in the NC program file or retrieved from a library of cutting tools. Interfaces to CATIA, Unigraphics, Pro/E, Mastercam, EdgeCAM, HyperMILL, PowerMILL and TDM are also available.

After the stock model, cutting tool description, and the NC program are defined, VERICUT cuts a three-dimensional solid part. When an error is detected, VERICUT provides a history of the error. By clicking on the error in the simulated NC program, the actual NC program command that caused the error is displayed for identification and correction.

[edit] Viewing and Analyzing a Simulated Part

Tools for viewing and analyzing the cut model are provided. The part can be zoomed, sectioned, reversed, and rotated at any angle. The part can be displayed in translucent mode, revealing areas not visible in a solid model such as the intersection of drilled holes. VERICUT takes accurate three-dimensional measurements of all model features including machined surfaces such as fillet radii, corner radii, hole diameters, distances/angles, gaps, wall and floor thicknesses, part volumes, and depths. The cut model can be compared to the original design model to perform constant gouge checking and identify excess material left on the part.

[edit] Hippocamp Ruins Sgt. Peppers

Hippocamp Ruins Sgt. Peppers is a remix and/or mash-up album based on the original Beatles 1967 album 'Sgt. Peppers Lonely Hearts Club Band'. The album, released in October 2006, was publicised with the name of net-label Hippocamp (who were also responsible for the controversial Hippocamp Ruins Pet Sounds album) however, like that one, this release was organized by the musicians themselves, so as not to infringe on copywrite.

This release also featured many new artists, notably, Fredo Viola, MichL Bridge, Yoshi & Futureboots.

[edit] X Window System desktop environment

An 'X Window System desktop environment combines a window manager with a suite of standard applications that adhere to human interface guidelines and runs under the X Window System. Whereas a window manager is analogous to the Aqua user interface for OS X, a Linux desktop environment is analogous to Aqua as well as all of the default OS X graphical applications and configuration utilities. Initially, CDE was available as a proprietary solution, but was never popular on Linux systems due to cost and licensing restrictions.[citation needed] In 1996 the KDE was announced, followed in 1997 by the announcement of GNOME. Xfce is a smaller project that was also founded in 1997, and focuses on speed and modularity. A comparison of X Window System desktop environments demonstrates the differences between environments. Today, GNOME and KDE are the dominant solutions, and often installed by default on Linux systems. Each of them offers:

  • To programmers, a set of standard APIs, a programming environment, and human interface guidelines.
  • To translators, a collaboration infrastructure. KDE and GNOME are available in many languages.[1][2]
  • To artists, a workspace to share their talents.[3][4]
  • To ergonomics specialists, the chance to help simplify the working environment.[5][6][7]
  • To developers of third-party applications, a reference environment for integration. OpenOffice.org is one such application.[8][9]
  • To users, a complete desktop environment and a suite of essential applications. These include a file manager, web browser, multimedia player, email client, address book, PDF reader, photo manager, and system preferences application.

In the early 2000s these two environments reached maturity.[10][citation needed] Still active, the Appeal[11] and ToPaZ[12] projects focus on bringing new advances to the next major releases of both KDE and GNOME respectively. Although striving for broadly similar goals, GNOME and KDE do differ in their approach to user ergonomics. KDE encourages applications to integrate and interoperate, is highly customizable, and contains many complex features, all whilst trying to establish sensible defaults. GNOME on the other hand is more prescriptive, and focuses on the finer details of essential tasks and overall simplification. Accordingly, each one attracts a different user and developer community. Technically, there are numerous technologies common to all Linux desktop environments, most obviously the X Window System. Accordingly, the freedesktop.org project was established as an informal collaboration zone with the goal being to reduce duplication of effort.
