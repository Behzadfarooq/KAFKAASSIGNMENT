[edit] Royden Mills

Royden Mills is Canadian born Sculptor and instructor who has created sculpture that is sited in various places internationally. State University of New York, Odette Sculpture Park in Windsor Ontario, Franconia Sculpture Park in Minnesota are a selection of places where one can find examples of his large scaled work. He has created many large scaled works and has exhibited in Japan, Poland, USA, and Canada. The work has often dealt with sculpture that exposes the audience to an elevated sense of physical presence especially in regards to a sense of interior and exterior, and an essence of vulnerability in tradidtionally non momumental subject matter.

[edit] MreB

MreB is a protein found in bacteria that has been identified as a homologue of actin, as indicated by similarities in amino acid sequence. The conservation of protein structure suggests the common ancestry of the cytoskeletal elements actin, found in eukaryotes, and MreB, found in prokaryotes. Indeed, recent studies have found that MreB proteins polymerize to form filaments that are similar to actin microfilaments.

MreB controls the width of rod-shaped bacteria, such as Escherichia coli. A mutant E. coli that creates defective MreB proteins will be spherical instead of rod-like. Also, bacteria that are naturally spherical do not have the gene encoding MreB. Prokaryotes carrying the mreB gene can also be helical in shape. MreB filaments sit right beneath the bacterial cell membrane and give bacteria a strong, rigid structure, as if the bacterium were being stretched out from the inside.

[edit] (Constantine's Basilica)

Constantine's Basilica (from the Greek "Basilus" meaning King) was an adaptation of a Roman civic meeting hall built in Rome, begun by his predecessor Maxentius and completed by Constantine after his victory over Maxentius, reuniting the divided Roman Empire. It is now in ruins, but enough of it remains to indicate that it consisted of a central nave and two side aisles with a small apse at one end, and doors at the other end. A large marble portrait bust of Constantine may have been placed in the hall. Construction seems to have been in the form of large arched ceilings of poured concrete probably faced with stone or marble. This or other basilicas such as Trajan's may have served as models for the first Roman Christian churches such as Old St. Peter's, as they are essentially the same plan as the majority of western Christian churches, a long nave flanked by two side aisles.

Cleethorpes Pier was opened on August Bank Holiday 1873, to the designs of J & A Dowson, with Head Wrightson of Stockton on Tees as Engineers. A pavilion was added in 1883, which was destroyed by fire in 1903. A replacement pavilion was opened in 1905 nearer to the shore, whilst a smaler building was added at the pier head.

In 1936 Cleethorpes Council bought the pier off its previous owners, the railway company(now the LNER) that had been involved with the structure's construction. The pier was sectioned during the Second World War as a defence measure. Money was later offered as compensation, but the council claimed there was a ¬£26,000 shortfall. Thus demolition of ther isolated pier neck took place in 1949, salvaged material helping to build both Wonderland, another Cleethorpes' attraction, and a stand at Leicester City's Filbert Street ground.

Modernisation of the pavilion occured in 1968, but following an unsuccesful 1980 Summer Show, the pier was sold to a private owner the following year. After threats to its future, local club owner Mark Meyer bought the pier in 1985, converting it into a nightclub, and re-naming it Pier 39.

Since then further developments have included the addition of shelters, and widening of the entire structure.

Tracing descent from the ruling families in the region facilitated marriage alliances that provided another connection. Sources both in Sulu and Brunei show that Sulu seventh sultan Muhammad ul-Halim was related to the Brunei royal family. The mother of Sultan Badar ud-Din I was a Tirun from the northeast coast of Borneo. When Spanish governor Corcuera attacked Sulu in 1638 Rajah Bongsu, the Sulu Sultan, was helped by Makassar warriors. Although Arab, Persian and Indian Muslims were limited to traders and missionaries who came for visits, some stayed after marrying local women. Inter marriage within this sultans, warriors, traders and missionaries made up the proud Suluk tribe.

[edit] Joyce Trimmer

Mrs. Joyce Trimmer (born November 10, 1927 in London, England) was the mayor of the city of Scarborough, Ontario from 1988 to 1994.

A Bachelor of Arts Graduate from the University of London, Trimmer taught business studies at Victoria Park Collegiate from 1973 to 1974. Concern over the development of the Tam O'Shanter golf course grounds in Scarborough prompted Trimmer to run for the Board of Control for Scarborough in the 1974 Ontario municipal elections, and was elected, serving for five terms until 1988.

In 1988, upon the retirement of incumbent Gus Harris, Trimmer ran for mayor of Scarborough, defeating former Scarborough Alderman and ex-Liberal MP Norm Kelly by 4,800 votes.

As both a councillor and later as mayor, Trimmer was a noted supporter of environmental preservation, such as for the Rouge River (Ontario) valley, and encouraged business development in various parts of Scarborough, mainly around the city centre area.

A supporter of the 1996 Toronto Olympics bid, as well as of the Metro Toronto Police in her commitment to fight crime and thus to restore Scarborough's reputation, Trimmer was easily re-elected in 1991 with 79% of all ballots cast.

Shortly after retiring from active municipal politics in 1994, Joyce Trimmer was called upon by Ontario Progressive Conservative Party leader Mike Harris to lead a task force on the question of the amalgamation of Metropolitan Toronto. Although the task force was disbanded upon the start of the 1995 Provincial election campaign, the recommendations of the Trimmer task force brought about the plans for amalgamation which came into effect in 1998.

[edit] Multiple Description Coding

In multiple description coding, the multimedia data (mostly used for video) is broken into several streams with some redundancy among the streams. When all the streams are received, one can guarantee low distortion at the expense of having a slightly higher bit rate than a system designed purely for compression. On the other hand, when only some of the streams are received (or even one stream), the quality of the reconstruction degrades but still a correct streaming video is reconstructed.

This way MDC adjusts and matches the quality of the multimedia data according to the receivers bandwith. One with low bandwith will still be able to view the multimedia content without objectionable jiggies or buffering pauses.

Many papers have been made about MDC, possible applications in the future could be live streaming TV over P2P-Networks.

[edit] Hallstatt Civilization

The ancient Hallstatt civilization was a nation in Central Europe first dated from 1500 to 1000 BCE. It is noted for its use of bronze and iron, being among the first civilizations in the Iron Age (along with the Aegean civilizations, Mesopotamian civilizations, and Indian civilizations.) Known for domesticating animals, agriculture, and pottery, Halstatt was among the first great European civilizations.It was also a leading producer of iron sometime around 1000 to 500 BCE.

Halstatt conquered or had large influence over northern Italy, Germany, Scandanavia, the former Yugoslavian region, the Celts in Transalpine Gaul and Catalonia, the Neolithic cultures, and the Illyrians. It is supposed that Hallstatt originated in southern Germany and possibly the alps.

The Hallstatt civilization may have spoke Germanic.
