[edit] Liston B. Ramsey

Liston B. Ramsey (1919 - 2001) was an influential leader of the North Carolina state legislature from 1961-2001. Born in Madison County, North Carolina, Ramsey graduated from Mars Hill College, located in Madison County, in 1940 when it was a junior college. A Democrat and a devoted admirer of President Franklin Roosevelt, Ramsey was first elected to the North Carolina General Assembly, the lower house of the state legislature, in 1960. He was re-elected 18 times, and during his 38 years of service he became one of the most powerful legislators in North Carolina history. In 1981 he was elected Speaker of the General Assembly; he served a then-record four terms as Speaker. Ramsey used his power to bring large sums of state money to the western mountains of North Carolina, many of whose citizens felt that they had long been neglected by the state government in favor of the more densely-populated piedmont and coastal sections of North Carolina. Since North Carolina's governor had no veto power in the 1980's, Ramsey was considered by many political observers to be the most powerful man in the state government during the eighties.

Despite his accomplishments, some Democratic legislators from North Carolina's urban areas resented what they felt was Ramsey's secretive, rural-oriented leadership. In 1989, 20 Democratic legislators led by State Rep. Joe Mavretic joined with 45 Republican state assemblymen to oust Ramsey as the Speaker of the General Assembly. Despite this defeat, however, Ramsey continued to be an influential member of the legislature until his retirement in 2000. He died in 2001. A section of Interstate 26 running between Asheville, North Carolina and Johnson City, Tennessee is named in his honor. The Ramsey Center for Regional Studies, located at Mars Hill College, was opened in 2002 to maintain his official papers and to preserve the heritage and culture of the people of the Southern Appalachian Mountains.

    Frank was born in 1959 in Rockville Centre and grew up in Manhasset. He later played football, basketball and lacrosse at Cranwell School and Canterbury School in New Milford, Connecticut. He was Canterbury's lacrosse captain his senior year.

Frank is presently an account executive with Merrill Lynch and has served on the board of the 9/11 charity Tuesday's Children, which seeks to help children orphaned by the terrorist attack. He is an ordained deacon in the diocese of Rockville Centre and is still active in many local sports leagues.

    [edit] Mumford Cove

    Mumford Cove is a private association in Southeastern Connecticut located adjacent to Groton Long Point, bordered by NoankConnecticut's Haley's Farm nature preserve, Palmer's Cove, and Fisher's Island Sound. It is a part of the town of Groton, Connecticut. The neighborhood was first developed in the late 1960's, and a number of the original ranch, raised-ranch, and colonial homes remain. More recently, new construction and high home values have made Mumford Cove a desireable location in the Mystic/Groton area. Homes currently for sale range in price from about $500,000 to $2.5 million, while the average home in Groton sells for about $220,000.

    Mumford Cove has private tennis courts, a large beach, a marina, a boat launch, picnic grounds, communal sail boats, a playground, and other amenities for residents, as well as neighborhood-wide parties, tennis matches, and picnics. A former home for Ella Grasso, Mumford Cove is now notable for resident Claire Gaudiani, prominent for her role as Chairman of the New London Development Corporation, which organized the eminent domain seizures that lead to a recent Supreme Court case, and her presidency of Connecticut College.

    Mumford Cove is notable for being an entirely private community; unlike its neighbor Groton Long Point, it is not a borough of Groton, and therefore maintains its roads, snow removal, dredging, garbage collection, and other neccesities independently, mainly through annual dues paid by residents. This status makes it illegal to walk, drive, park, or use any facilities if you are not a resident or a guest of a resident. Additionally, Mumford Cove has strict rules in regards to new construction, notably pertaining to the size of homes, the percent of the lot used for the home, and the setbacks from both neighbors and the road. For these reasons, lots tend to be larger, homes more proportionally sized, and living more private than in Groton Long Point, Noank Village, and other beach communities in the area. Mason's Island, in nearby Stonington, Connecticut, is the most similar locale in the Southeastern region of the state.

    Mumford Cove was marred by ecological crises in the 1970's, when raw sewage was routinely pumped into the waters of the cove and salt marshes of the area. Since this practice was halted, the EPA has studied and assesed the health and safety of the waters off of Mumford Cove, which have now nearly returned to their natural levels.

    [edit] Bill Curry (Connecticut politician)

    Bill Curry was a Connecticut politician who was elected Comptroller and was a two time candidate for Governor on the Democratic party ticket.

    Curry entered politics in the late 1970's winning a seat in the Connecticut Senate in suburban Hartford. He was aligned with liberals such as Toby Moffett and Ralph Nader. He supported a number of tax increases in the legislature and opposed policies favored by oil companies.

    Curry ran unsuccessfully for the 6th District congressional seat vacated by Moffett in 1982. He was defeated by fellow state senator Nancy Johnson, a Republican from New Britain.Following that election he practiced law in South Windsor until 1990, when he won the office of Comptroller over Republican Joel Schiavone.

    Curry left the Comptroller's office after one term to seek the Governorship. He defeated the favorite of the party establishment , State Senate President John Larson in a primary due to his strong support from labor unions. In the general election he faced Republican John Rowland, the former congressman who had run against Lowell Weicker in 1990, Connecticut Party Lieutenant Governor Eunice Groark, an ally of Weicker and hardline conservative Tom Scott. Rowland stressed a tax cutting agenda, while Curry advocated policies favored by unions and criticised Rowland for being too close to large corporations such as United Technologies. Rowland won the 1994 election by a 36%-33% margin over Curry.

    Following the election, Curry went to work as an aide in the Bill Clinton White House working with political consultant Dick Morris. He later worked as a consultant in Connecticut. He was nominated again by the Democrats to run for Governor in 2002. His campaign that year against the incumbent Governor John Rowland charged the governor had wasted state money on questionable deals for political allies. Rowland responded by attacking Curry's record on taxes and stressing improvements in education during his term. Rowland won the 2002 election by a 56% to 44% margin.

    Curry is now a columnist for the Hartford Courant.

    [edit] I minus E basis

    Basic life assurance and general annuity business (section 431F ICTA)

    Basic life assurance and general annuity business is defined as being life assurance business not fitting within any other category of business. It is often abbreviated to BLAGAB. BLAGAB is taxed on the so called I minus E basis (ie the company is taxed on its investment return minus its expenses of management). The I minus E basis raises the UK Exchequer more revenue than it would get if it were taxed on a trading basis. This is because a trading computation would tax Premiums plus Investment return minus Expenses minus Claims, and the expectation is that policy holder claims will be greater than the premiums they pay, as policy holders tend to hold life assurance policies as an investment that they hope will grow. To ensure the Exchequer does not lose out in a year where a trading basis would yield greater tax revenues, E (expenses of management) is restricted so the I minus E cannot be lower than the measure of trading profits, with any restricted E being carried forward and deemed to be E of the subsequent period.

    Before 1 January 1992, there were separate tax computations for basic life assurance business and for general annuity business, since then the two categories have been combined into BLAGAB.

    Capital redemption business written since 31 December 1937 has been treated as though it were BLAGAB from the first accounting period of a company ending on or after 1 July 1999. Before then, it was treated as a separate business taxed on an I minus E basis.

    [edit] Edgewise

    This hardcore band from West Chester, Pennsylvania was founded in 1988 and continued with various lineup changes until 1996. In 1990, Edgewise released its first EP, "Silent Rage", on legendary Smorgasboard records, featuring Al Spina on vocals, Darren Saxton on guitar, Vince Spina on bass, and Jack Collins on drums. Influenced by bands like Cro-Mags, Youth of Today, and Black Sabbath, they combined screaming vocals with metal riffs to produce a unique hardcore sound. Shortly before release, Vince Spina moved to lead guitar and Matt Tincani joined on bass.

    The Release of "Silent Rage" was followed by a tour of the eastern US in 1990 playing with notable hardcore bands including Turning Point, Judge, Integrity, Endpoint, and commercial punk rock legends Green Day. They played in clubs ranging from the Zodiac, the Unisound and Lost Horizon to Victory Records owned Club Blitz.

    In late 1990 Edgewise returned to Why Me? Recording (Starkweather, Turmoil, Brody (Taking Back Sunday), Turning Point) to cut some tracks for compilations. Tolerance was featured on the genre defining East Coast Assault Vol. 1 on the Chord Magazine/Too Damn Hype Label.

    In 1991, Edgewise embarked on a second tour this time heading across the country and playing shows with Lifetime, UpFront, Earth Crisis, Strife, and 411. Returning to Clubs like The Lost Horizon in Syracuse, NY but also hitting all agaes hardcore shows at the legendary Gilman St. Warehouse and Spanky's Cafe for a hardcore fest.

    In 1993, Edgewise released its first full length LP, "Massacre of the Innocents", on Vince Spina's label, Harvest Records. Guitar player Darren Saxton and Drummer Jack Collins left the band prior to recording "Massacre of the Innocents", which featured a new drummer, Derek Wilson. Edgewise embraced a more guitar-focused, commercial sound for the album, whose guitar solos and layered tracks departed from the band's earlier style. Edgewise played only a handful of shows to support "Massacre of the Innocents", and the band was defunct soon thereafter.


    A new opportunity came in 1995, when European label Gain Ground offered to re-release the "Silent Rage" EP, and to release the band's second full length album. Edgewise reformed with former drummer Jack Collins on vocals, Vince Spina and Darren Saxton on guitars, Matt Tincani on bass, and Turning Point's Ken Flavell on drums.     A jumbo coin is a metal disc which looks like an enlarged coin. For example a standard half dollar coin is 30.61mm in diameter, a jumbo coin made to resemble a half dollar can be anywhere from 3inches to 12inches in diameter. Frequently the jumbo coin is used as a climax ending for a coin magic routine.

    [edit] The Peter Gzowski Festival of Stories

    The Peter Gzowski Festival of Stories is a day-long festival which takes place in the Town of Georgina, Ontario, Canada each year on the third Sunday of August.

    The Festival of Stories, with gracious permission from the late Peter Gzowski, took on the name to commemorate the dedicated community member and his enthusiasm for literacy and for the Festival of Stories.

    The Peter Gzowski Festival of Stories is a completely free day for children of all ages with the exclusion of adult programs.
