Art Porter, Jr.

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Arthur L. 'Art' Porter, Jr., (3 August 1961 - 23 November 1996), was an American jazz saxophonist. He was the son of legendary jazz musician Art Porter, Sr., as well as the namesake of "The Art Porter Bill".

[edit] Early Life

Art Porter, Jr. was born in Little Rock, Arkansas in 1961. At the age of 9 Porter joined his father's band as a drummer and played with them into his teenage years. Soon after this, Porter began to be drawn to the saxophone after noticing it's melodic abilities, and began to play it during shows with his the band, "The Art Porter Trio". When Porter turned 16, he began to be barred from clubs because he was under 21. It was during this time that he was arrested and charged with working under-age in a night club serving alcoholic beverages. Then Arkansas Attorney General, and future President of the United States Bill Clinton, who was also a saxophonist, intervened to get the charges dropped and pushed for the law to be changed to allow under-age musicians to appear in adult facilities as long as their legal guardian accompanied them. This law became known as "The Art Porter Bill".

[edit] College

Porter attended Berklee College of Music and Northeastern Illinois University where he studied music, earing a Bachelor's Degree in Arts in the latter. He later traveled to Roosevelt University, where he would earn his Master's Degree. Porter studied piano under Ellis Marsalis, a former saxophonist himself.

[edit] Career

In the mid 1980s he moved to Chicago, Illinois and studied tenor saxophone with Von Freeman and performed with Pharoah Sanders and Jack McDuff. During the 1990s he developed an interest in R&B and hip hop and merged elements of these into his performances. Soon after this, Porter signed with Verve Records/Polygram Records and produced several albums, beginning in the summer of 1992 with Pocket City(shown left), followed by Straight to the Point, Undercover and finally Lay Your Hands On Me . These albums were known as by people wanting their "smooth jazz" not too smooth. Porter and his father performed for President Clinton during his 1993 inauguration, playing Amazing Grace at a prayer breakfast.

[edit] Death

In 1996 Porter traveled to Thailand to appear at the Thailand International Jazz Festival. Prior to the festival on 23 November he went boating on the Kratha Taek reservoir in Sai Yok, Thailand. Tragically, the boat Porter was traveling on overturned, and Porter, along several others, drowned. After the disaster, Porter was survived a wife and two elementary age sons. In 1998, the album For Art's Sake was posthumously released in his honor. It was filled with songs Porter had composed and played, but had not yet put on an album, as well as songs by other artists dedicated to Porter.

[edit] Achievements

Porter has been inducted into the Arkansas Jazz Hall of Fame and the Arkansas Entertainers Hall of Fame. Porter received the Arkansas Jazz Hall of Fame Lifetime Achievement Award in 1998.
