[edit] Filter Factory

Filter Factory (edit|talk|history|links|watch|logs) – (View log)

Wikipedia is not an instruction manual. Especially not for obsolete software. Article was prodded, but tag was removed. See also Wikipedia:Articles for deletion/Creating filters in photoshop 5.5--the text in Filter Factory was copied from Creating filters in photoshop 5.5. --Akhilleus (talk) 18:18, 21 February 2007 (UTC)

 Relisted to generate a more thorough discussion so that consensus may be reached
 Please add new comments below this notice. Thanks, Cbrown1023 talk 02:23, 27 February 2007 (UTC)
  • Keep, 33 Google News Archive results suggest this is that rarity, a notable plug-in. Article should not be written as a how-to, though. --Dhartung | Talk 03:44, 27 February 2007 (UTC)
  • Keep per Dhartung's reasoning. I tagged it with {{howto}}, which should eventually clear up the writing problem. Mermaid from the Baltic Sea 04:36, 27 February 2007 (UTC)
  • Delete. If you remove the "how-to" part of the article, you are left with exactly ONE sentence: "Filter Factory is a plug-in in Photoshop which allows the user to create custom filters." Plugins for software applications are not inherently notable, and are unlikely to ever meet the primary notability criterion. A Train take the 17:07, 27 February 2007 (UTC)
  • Transwiki to Wikibooks. It is a how-to article and most of the sources are how-to in nature as well. So, I think the content is better suited to Wikibooks.--Kubigula (talk) 22:08, 5 March 2007 (UTC)
