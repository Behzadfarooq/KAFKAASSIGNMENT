[edit] The SCP is dedicated to bringing a safe form of foam combat to the Pacific Northwest.

[The SCP] is a 12 year old boffer/live combat orientated group based out of Kent, Washington. It was founded by Justin Gilmore in 1995, the group has been dedicated to the fighting aspect of Live Action Role-play ever since. Originally fighting in parks or in small fields behind apartments with friend, Justin found more and more people wanted to fight, and decided to officially found a group. After the first season of play, the group had just over twenty members and by the end of the third the group had blown up to near seventy five. Today the founding Kingdom of Earth Haven boasts an average weekly player base of ninety to one hundred players. The SCP has expanded with two new Kingdoms found in Eugene (New Avalon) and Salem (Strifeland), Oregon. These two Kingdoms were founded by members who moved away from the Kent area, but wished to keep enjoying the fun of the game. While these two Kingdoms are still growing, they show that The SCP is beginning to come into its own.

The SCP's abbreviated name once stood for Shadow Clan Players at one point and at another it stood of Swordfighting Character Productions. These days the player's have decided that the name no longer stands for anything; it's just the name of the group that has given them a safe place to have fun.

While most groups have developed in-depth systems with races, classes and armor. The SCP has stuck to a more simple approach to keep the game easily understandable, and open to a broader age range of players (player can start participating at thirteen years old). Another rare aspect to The SCP is that they are a free to play game; there is no membership fee or dress code. The focus is to keep the game open to all those who wish to participate; having fees and dress codes simply eliminates younger players who can't afford it.

The SCP World has little back-story, except that which was developed by players. Much of its history will be remembered as legend and myth as was done with our real world's history. The group also allows players to choose races they have seen in books, movies or other sources; this allows players to develop more unique characters. There are a few awards or honors within The SCP, most are achieved through combat. The most notable of all is Kingdom Champion, which is awarded to the player who wins their Kingdom's Tournament. These tournaments generally take up a full day, with as many as seventy-two or more players participating. Some of the other titles that players can take on are: King, Lord, Knight, Champion and Magistrate.

Within The SCP there are seven notable Knighthoods. The Knights of Earth Haven, the Dark Knight Order, the Dragon Knight Order, Order of the Tear, the Fallen Knights, Daughters of Aegis and the Exalted Knights of New Avalon. Each has a unique Code of Conduct and training regime, as well as out looks on battle and each other. It is up to each Knighthood as to how a player can join their Order; most require a player to have been an active member of The SCP for at least one full season.

[edit] Australian Institute of Professional Photographers (AIPP)

The Australian Institute of Professional Photography (AIPP) operates as a not for profit organisation. The AIPP exists to encourage professionalism amongst photographers, and a recourse for clients that have experienced unethical behaviour. The AIPP run regular workshops and events to promote professionalism amongst its members. As well as these training and informational events, the AIPP holds an annual awards to encourage photographers to out perform each other. The Australian Professional Photography Awards (APPA) are held annually in a different state each year.

[edit] Ayya Sucinta Bhikkhuni

Ayya Sucinta, a German-born member of the Bhikkhuni Sangha, has trained with the late Ayya Khema, at Amaravati Monastery in England with Ajahn Sumedho (where she began her monastic life) and at the Bhavana Society in America with Bhante Gunaratna. One of the few Western Theravadan bhikkhunis in the world, she received Full Ordination at the historic International Gathering of the Sangha in Bodhgaya, India in 1998. In America she has stayed and taught at the Carolina Buddhist Vihara, Dhammadharini Vihara & the Bhavana Society.

Ayya Sucinta left the United States for Australia in January 2007 at the invitation of the Melbourne Buddhist Society to become the Resident Teacher at Buddhaloka Center in Melbourne and to support the foundation of the first Theravadan Buddhist monastery for fully ordained women in Australia, tentatively named: “Sanghamittarama”. The Sanghamittarama Project has the blessings of Ajahn Brahm, the Spiritual Advisor to the BSV and Bhante Gunaratana of the Bhavana Society in West Virginia, where Ayya took her first ordination has also offered his full support.

[edit] Ayya Sudhamma Bhikkhuni

Ven. Sudhamma Bhikkhuni, from Charlotte, North Carolina, became a samaneri or female Buddhist novice in 1999 at the Bhavana Society, under the tutelage of Bhante Gunaratana. In early 2003, Ayya Sudhamma traveled to South Asia, where she became the first American-born woman to gain Theravada bhikkhuni ordination in Sri Lanka. In July of 2003, she returned to the United States at the invitation of the Carolina Buddhist Vihara where she currently resides and teaches. In 2005 she participated in the founding of the North American Bhikkhuni Association and was recognized for her achievements as an Outstanding Woman in Buddhism at the United Nations in Bangkok on International Women's Day in 2006.

[edit] Los Chicros

  • Los Chicros is an indie rock/pop band from Paris, France formed in 2001 by Philippe Monthaye and Mathieu Warsky. Their current line-up is: Arnaud Cambraye, Victor Le Masne, Olivier Marguerit, Philippe Monthaye and Mathieu Warsky.
  • The word "Chicro" is French slang for "cheapskate". They called themselves that because their use of cheap instruments like Casio synthesizers gives them a distinctive sound.
  • Their first published song was called Walking Backwards and was featured on the 2002 Record Makers compilation of new French talents I Hear Voices.
  • In 2004, Back in the Wild was issued as a 12" single with two other original songs and remixes by French artists Le Tone and Double T and American band Greenskeepers from Chicago.
  • In 2005, they released an EP called Too Cool For School which featured seven songs and remixes by Le Tone, Syd Matters, Ben's Symphonic Orchestra and Turzi.
  • During 2005 and 2007, they were involved in various soundtrack projects, like Japanese director Nobuhiro Suwa's Un Couple Parfait (starring Valeria Bruni Tedeschi and Bruno Todeschini) and Nicolas Klotz's La Question Humaine(starring Mathieu Amalric and Michael Lonsdale). Greenskeepers' remix of Back in the Wild was also heard in ABC show Grey's Anatomy (Season 2, Episode 17).
  • Their latest album Sour Sick Soul, featuring the single Diskonoise, was released in Japan in 2006 and in Europe in 2007, met by unanimous critical acclaim.

[edit] Raouf Salama Moussa

Dr Raouf Salama Moussa (1929-2006, Arabic: رؤف سلامة موسى, DMG Rāūf Salāma Mūsā) a notable Egyptian bacteriologist and editor.

He was the first and eldest son of Salama Moussa a famous Coptic Egyptian journalist and reformer in the 1920s.

After his school days he studied Veterinary Medicine and Bacteriology in Leeds, GB. Until 1979 he taught Bacteriology at the University of Alexandria/Egypt and worked as a Salmonella specialist for an international aliment company in Vevey, Switzerland.

From 1978 Dr Moussa returned to Egypt and founded a publishing and printing house, Al-Mustaqbal (The Future), which became one of the best known readers in the Arabic World in a very short time.

Besides his various articles in newspapers and magazines Al-Mustaqbal offers works about his father Salama Moussa, by the famous Islam-Critic Farag Fouda, who was murdered in 1992, the Egyptian Psychiatrist Nawâl El Saadâwi, various scientific books, dictionaries and literature about Egyptian Geography, History and Culture in Arabic, English, German and French.
