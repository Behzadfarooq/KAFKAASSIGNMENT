Arturo Andrés Roig (born on September 16, 1922) is an Argentine philosopher.

Born in Mendoza, he entered the Universidad Nacional de Cuyo, and left it in 1949 after obtaining a degree in Education Sciences (Profesor de Enseñanza Secundaria, Normal y Especial en Filosofía). Later on he continued his studies at the Sorbonne.

Roig returned to Argentina, and in 1955 he began teaching philosophy at the Universidad Nacional de Cuyo, with a special interest in local philosophers. Soon after, this interest expanded to national and Latin American philosophers.

He is famous for being one of the best developed examples of Latin American Philosophy, and for his vast amount of scientific works, for which he has received widespread recognition and awards.
