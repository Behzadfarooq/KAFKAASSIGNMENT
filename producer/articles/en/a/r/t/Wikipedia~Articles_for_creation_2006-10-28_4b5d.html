[edit] David Dineen-Porter

David Dineen-Porter is a noted member of the Toronto Alternative Comedy Scene scene, and an musician of indie music(performing under the name PDF Format (the Band.

His comedy style is indicative of the innovation of that scene. His musical style is heavily influenced by the Chiptune scene. He is also the prime organizer of "Friends of P64", a group of musicians who use the Sequencer software Prophet 64 for the Commodore 64.

[edit] Early Comedy Career

David Dineen-Porter began doing comedy in the group Someone and the Somethings out of university. He was quickly introduced to Tom Middleditch (currently of Chicago's improv Olympic) by Leslie Gottleib, the current organizer of Laugh Sabbath (one of Toronto's most noted alternative comedy shows).

Together, they formed The Iliads, a sketch troupe, which ultimately superceded Someone and the somethings.

During this time, he began to experiment with solo performances, helping organize Rad Maneuvers along with The Gurg (Chris Locke and Brian Barlow).

Also during this time, he was considered, but ultimately rejected, for a Tim Sims Encouragement Award, for new and unique comedic performers. (2003) The performance considered (and rejected) contained several sketches, performed solo, with the use of multimedia and extensive use of pre-recorded audio, which were to become central features of current alternative comedy.

[edit] Establishment

Through Catch 23, David developed a presence in the Improv community, and was simultaneously loved and hated for his extremely bizzare of sometimes confrontational performance style.

During 2004, David organized a marathon 3 night sketch show called Yay, Stepdad!, which included himself, his partner Tom, Levi MacDougall, Tim Polley, Paul Schuck (collectively, The Distractions, and Chris Locke and Brian Barlow (collectively The Gurg).

He followed this up with a "birthday" show (5 months before his birthday) entitled Hexiquest (http://www.eyeweekly.com/eye/issue/issue_08.26.04/arts/meet.php there is an article in Eye magazine about it).

The Iliads went on Hiatus in January of 2005, as Tom MIddleditch moved to Chicago in order to study at the Second City school there.

David began performing solo, as without the Iliads, and having alienated the improv community with his angry and perhaps selfish performances, he was without partners.

[edit] Standup

After beginning to do standup, David was rapidly a hot item at many of the alternative standup nights, including but not limited to The Joke Club run by Nick Flanagan at the Drake Hotel. Moving into all-audio performance, and maintaining a steady stream of standup performances, David is now considered one of the central figures in Toronto's alternative comedy scene, though he remains a controversial figure among his contemporaries who appear to hold very strong positive or negative opinions of him and his work.

He has performed extensively with the East Village alternative comedy scene (at Invite Them Up, Gabe and Jenny, Greg Johnson Show, Mo Pitkins).

[edit] Current Career

David runs the successful standup comedy night Circular Laughing Breads (formerly at the Toronto venue "the Bagel" hence the name) with Now Magazine music reviewer Jason Richards (who recently was embroiled in major controversy following a virulent and racist exchange between himself and musician K-os http://canada.com.dose.ca/music/story.html?id=1f70bd7c-35fa-46b9-bb9f-a55cb29c5ace&k=97556 , http://www.torontoist.com/archives/2006/10/hiphop_artist_a.php)).

David's very controversial, vitriolic, and exhaustive defense of Jason Richards against K-os allegations that he was a "Sambo" controlled by his white masters at the Magazine (who he alleged ordered Jason to review his album with a 3/5) on the highly influential toronto music scene message board Stillepost.ca proved a major catalyst for schism in the toronto arts community on the topic of race in music (http://www.stillepost.ca/boards/index.php?topic=61571.0 ) perhaps sparking the recent Toronto Star articles on Race Stereotypes and their impact on minorities' participation in music.

Also, David Dineen-Porter was awarded (in 2006, October) an artist's residency at The Drake Hotel to complete the composition of a Rock Opera, for the duration of February 2007, to be perfomred in March 2008. However, this project is in development, and it is possible this is a Hoax perpetrated by David, as he is fond of tricking people into believing things that are not true.

[edit] Asperger's Syndrome and Physical Maladies

David is reported (by himself) to have Aspergers Syndrome and Attention Defficit Disorder, which could explain his controversial standing in the comedy community, and why he is easily dispised as arrogant or crazy. It may also explain why he sometimes exhibits paranoid behaviour or appears not to adequately predict how severely his actions will affect people's emotions.

Some see this as a hoax used as an excuse to "get away" with actions that some are annoyed by.

David also suffered for 6 years from Muscular (or functional) Dysphonia, a habitual condition affecting Vocal Onset, leading to etnsion and swelling in the Vocal Chords. The inability to freely use his voice is one possible reason why David has favoured, until recently, quiet performances, and heavily logical content, which does not require him to shout, or perform characters.

David claims to suffer from every minor physical malady known to mankind, including allergies, lactose intolerance, chronic back-ache, TMJ disorder, and so on, but there is no way to substantiate these claims.

[edit] Wise Woman Tradition

The Wise Woman Tradition is an approach to health. The term began with Susun Weed through her teachings and books.

The Wise Woman Tradition is contrasted with two other Traditions of Healing - the Heroic Tradition that sees people as dirty and in need of cleaning, and the Scientific Tradition that sees people as parts and machines to fix. In the Wise Woman Tradition health is viewed as a spiral, ever-evolving, always returning to itself, but never at the same place. It shows health as flexibility, never static, never balanced, and always whole. Health is nourished in the Wise Woman Tradition.

The Wise Woman Tradition draws from the wisdom that has been passed down for generations. While perhaps a relatively new phrase, the approach to health is old and resembles that of nealers of many cultures, such as shamans, witches, and lay midwives. It is a term and an approach now used amongst many who practice herbalism, as well as other other fields such as midwifery and life counseling.

The Wise Woman Tradition puts the responsibiliy and power of health into the hands of people. Though some use it in their approach to helping others, at its core is the idea that we are all healers inside, and have at our doorstep all we need to care for ourselves and our loved ones.

[edit] Fyodor Pavlovitch Karamazov

Fyodor Pavlovitch Karamazov is the father of the title characters, brothers Dmitri Fyodorovitch Karamazov (nicknamed Mitya), Ivan Fyodorovitch Karamazov, and Alexei Fyodorovitch Karamazov (nicknamed Alyosha) in the novel The Brothers Karamazov by Fyodor Dostoevsky. The novel is centered around the events surrounding Fyodor Pavlovitch's murder. Dmitri, the eldest son, is accused of the murder because of an ongoing squabble between the two over Dmitri's inheiretance and a woman, Agrafena Alexandrovna Svetlova, often called Grushenka. Dmitri, however, accused Fyodor Pavlovitch's eppilectic servant Smerdyakov of the crime. Smerdyakov was also rumored in the novel to be Fyodor Pavlovitch's illegitimate son. This has lead to the literary debate as to whether there were actually four, not three, "Brothers Karamazov"; Smerdyakov being the fourth in addition to Dmitri, Ivan and Alyosha. Smerdyakov later confesses his guilt of the murder to Ivan before commititng suicide.

[edit] Serositis

Definition of Serositis

Serositis: Inflammation of the serous tissues, which line organs of the human body. Examples are the pleura of the lungs, pericardium of the heart, and the peritoneum of the abdomen.

This condition can be a complication of chronic dialysis or a sign of lupus.

[edit] Ural Tansykbaev

Ural Tansykbaevich Tansykbaev (Russian: Урал Тансыкбаевич; also spelled Тансыкбаев; also spelled Ural Tansiqbaev; O'rol Tansiqboev; Oral Tansykbayev; Tansikbaev) (1 Jan 1904 Tashkent (Uzbek SSR - 18 April 1974 Nukus, Kara-Kalpak ASSR)Uzbek painter of Kazakh origin, Peoples Artist oh the USSR.

[edit] Biography

He finished a Russian-Uzbek primary school in 1916, graduated from the 7th grade in 1919 (then considered final) and worked as a worker at tobacco factory, winery (1919-1921). He studied with Russian painters and followers of The Wanderers, first under Nikolay Vasilyevich Rozanov (1869-1940)his art studio of Tashkent Art Museum (now Uzbekistan Art Museum) (1924-1928), and later in the Art and Pedagogical Technical School, Penza (1928-1929), under Ivan Silovich Goryushkin-Sorokopudov (1873-1954) and Nikolay Philippovich Petrov (1872-1941), where he became interested in Fauvism and the work of the French Expressionists, influences noticeable in the increased decorativeness and heightened sense of colour in his early work. From 1929 he participated in art exhibitions. Since 1932 he had been steering committee member of the Union of Artists of Uzbekistan. In 1938 he was at art decoration of the first national kazakh ballet "Kalkaman and Mamir" (music of Vasily Vasilyevich Velikanov at Kazakh State Theatre of Opera and Ballet (now Abai Academic Theatre of Opera and Ballet in Alma-Ata (based on the poem "Kalkaman and Mamir" of the historian and poet Şekerim Kudayberdiulı (Shakarim Qudaiberdiulı(1858-1931), the student of Abai Kunanbaiuli).

As a member of Uzbekistan delegation he paid a visit to the front (1942). Jointly with artists M.Arinin and S.Cheprakov he made more than 30 monumental paintings for Uzbek pavilion at All union Agricultural Exhibition in Moscow (now VVTS) (1952-1955). He was elected member-correspondent of the Academy of Arts of the USSR (1954), real member of the Academy of Arts of the USSR (1958). That year he was awarded a silver medal at World Exhibition in Brussels (Universal Exibition Brusselles 58. The theme of his early paintings is connected with the searches of expressive means, forms of reflection of the reality. They are intensive and enriched by their colours, decorative. Since the beginning of the 50-s the main genre in his art had become landscape. Tansiqbaev participated in many exhibitions in Uzbekistan, in Moscow and abroad. His art was marked by numerous governmental rewards.

[edit] Entheogenic Drugs and the Archaeological Record

[edit] Contemporary Indigenous Traditions

There are several Modern cultures that continue to practice the application of mind altering drugs for religious purposes. Some of these cultures include the Shamans of Siberia (who employ Fly Agarics to endues hallucinations), the Huichol Indians of Mexico (who use Peyote Cactus) and Rastafarians of the Caribbean (who use marijuana).

[edit] Issues within the archaeological record

There are several issues with identifying Entheogenic drugs within an ancient society.

1. Identification of an Entheogenic substance. Was the substance smoked? Ingested?

2. Evidence of Entheogenic Inspiration within Artifacts. Does the artifact give direct evidence of drug use for Entheogenic purposes?

3. Artifact interpretation. As an observer of art that is 20,000 years old, are we seeing patterns that the original creator did not mean to create? In other words, do our own personal interests and biases alter the way we perceive artwork that may have not been intended by the author?

[edit] Archaeological Evidence of Entheogenic Drugs in Ancient Societies

Entheogenic Drugs have been used by various groups for thousands of years. There are numerous historical reports as well as modern contemporary reports of indigenous groups using entheogens. However, there is much debate as to what role entheogens have played in ancient societies and which societies did indeed use entheogens.

[edit] The New World

Mesoamerican Entheogenic drug use is the most iconic in popular consciousness. The Mayans, Olmecs, and Aztecs have well documented Entheogenic complexes. North American cultures, too, have long established traditions of entheogens.

[edit] Olmec Entheogens

The Olmec (12,000 BCE to 400 BCE) lived in Central America and are largely viewed by many as the mother culture of Aztecs and Mayans. The Olmecs left no written works on their belief structures, so many interpretations on Olmec beliefs are largely based off of interpretations of murals and artifacts. Archaeologists are lead to believe that the Olmecs used entheogens for 3 reasons. 1. Burials of Bufo Toads with priests 2. The use of entheogens in later olmec inspired cultures 3. Sculptures of Shamans and other figures have strong Therianthropic imagery.

[edit] Mayan Entheogens

The Mayans (250 BCE to 900 CE) flourished in Central America and were prevalent even till the arrival of the Spanish. The Mayan religious tradition is extremely complex and very well developed. Unlike the Olmec, the Mayans have religious texts that have survived to this day. The Mayan religion displays characteristic Mesoamerican mythology, with a strong emphasis on an individual being a communicator between the physical world and the spiritual world. Mushroom stone effigies, dated to 700 CE, give evidence that Mushrooms were at least revered in a religious way. The most direct evidence of Mayan entheogen use comes from modern descendents of the Maya, whom use entheogenic drugs today.

[edit] Aztec Entheogens

The Aztec Entheogen Complex is extremely well documented. Through historical evidence, there is proof that the Aztecs used several forms of pshycoactive drugs. These drugs include Ololiuqui (the seed of Rivea corymbosa), Tenonacatl (translated as “mushroom of the gods, a Psilocybe mushroom) and sinicuichi (a flower added to drinks). The Xochipilli statue, according to R.G. Wasson, gives the identity of several entheogenic plants. Other evidence for entheogen use of the Aztecs come from the Florentine Codex, a series of 12 books vividly describing the use of entheogenic drugs within Aztec culture and society.

[edit] North American Entheogens

There are several contemporary indigenous groups who use entheogens, most notably Native Americans of south western United States. Various tribes from California have been known to use strong alcoholic drinks as well as Peyote in order to achieve visions and religious experiences.

[edit] The Old World

[edit] The Paleolithic

During the Paleolithic, there is ample evidence of drug use as seen by preserved botanical remains and coprolites. Some scholars suggest that the Shanidar Cave (a Paleolithic site in Iraq) Flower Burial shows evidence of Shamanistic Death Ritual, but these claims are still being debated. The most direct evidence we have from the Paleolithic in terms of artwork comes from Tassili, Algeria. From this region, there are several therianthropic images portraying the painter and the animals around him as one (an often cited affect of many hallucinatory drugs, Ego death). One image, in particular, shows a man who has formed into one common form with a mushroom.

There are several Paleolithic sites that display therianthropic imagery. However, there is some debate as to whether or not sites like Lascaux or Chauvet were entheogenically inspired.

There have been several reports stating that the Bible and the Vedas have several references to entheogenic drugs. Although most scholars largely reject these claims, they are accepted by many fringe groups.

Also public journals gained popularity such as Die Kunde (The Message) and Germanen-Erbe (Germanic Heritage) proliferated. With the journals and videos the citizens thought they were being given good visuals and interpretations of different archaeological sites and the German prehistory. The Nazi’s also pushed the public to get involved in the search for the past. The Nazi’s used the appeal of patriotism as a tool to get the public involved in the prehistory hunt. For example, one amateur organizations membership flyer of the Amt Rosenberg stated, “Responsibility with respect to our indigenous prehistory must again fill every German with pride!” The goal of the organization was also stated as, “the interpretation and dissemination of unflasified knowledge regarding the history and cultural achievements of our northern Germanic ancestors on German and foreign soil.” Along with appealing to public patriotism there were open-air museums that reconstructed Neolithic and Bronze Age lake settlements at Unteruhldigen. This public musems also gained emense popularity and pushed the people to believe and search for there Germanic past.


[edit] The Archaeologists

Before the funding and push of Nazi Archaeology there was little knowledge of the subject and German prehistory. This made it even easier for the Nazi’s to push there ethnocentric views onto the uninformed public. Although the Nazi organization’s search appealed to the public it also appealed to the scholars. Scholars for archaeology had long been envious of the advancements in archaeology their neighbors to the near east had made. Before the Nazi rise to power there was little funding contributed to the study and with Hitler the funds were made available for scholars to make great advancements beyond their neighboring countries. Under the Nazi rule archaeology went from having one chair in prehistory in Marburg in 1933to having nine chairs in 1935. Once archaeology started gaining popularity scholars were also able to take out old ruins and display them in museums. The Romisch Germanisches Zentral Museum in Mainz became the Zentral Museum fur Deutsche Vor und Fruhgeschichte in 1939.Though it is ironic that once archaeology finally got reconiztion the archaeologists ended up doing more harm than good to the sites and the people. For example at the prehistoric site Biskupin, in Poland the Nazi destroyed the entire site.


[edit] Investigators

Archeologist during the Nazi reign can be divided into three groups: the party-liners, the Mitlaufer, and the opposition.

[edit] The party-liners

The Party-liners or acquiescent used the Nazi reign to further their own careers by conducting “politically correct” research. These people are referred to as Germanomanen or Germanonmaniacs. These included people like Herman Weirt, co-founder of the Ahnernerbe Organization, who attempted to prove that northern Europs was the cradle of Western Civilization. Also, Hans Reinerth and Oswald Meghin who became high-ranking party officials due to their cooperation. Though most of this group was composed by second hand or untrained scientist backed up by amateur equivalents.

[edit] The Mitaufer

The Mitaufer was the group the majority of archaeologist fit into. This group was know as the group of passive fellow-travelers. These people taught what they were told to teach and accepted Nazi funds with no questions.By taking inaction this group allowed the Nazi party to gain more power.

[edit] The Opposition

The Opposition was the third party and these are those who had critical opposition of the regime. This group was a minority and most wree persecuted for race or political views. For example Gerhard Bersu an archaeologist was prematurely retired for not following NS ideology. Jewish prehistorians and other scholars were also forced to leave.


[edit] Aftermath

The affect of the false nationalism created by Nazi archaeology was a paralysis on the study of archaeology that continued decades past the war. As described by Wilhelm Unverzagt: “After Germany’s collapse it initially seemed virtually impossible to begin rebuilding the discipline with any hope of success. The new wielders of political power viewed prehistory with deep mistrust, an attitude which seemed understandable in view of the abuse of the results of prehistoric research on the part of National Socialist leaders with regard to questions of education and politics.” The surviving older generation was faced with a reduced student population after the war. Those students at universities were burdened with: “a humiliating defeat and the disorientation experience of being methodologically ‘deprogrammed’. There was neither time nor desire to examine the reasons for the ‘German prostitution of archaeology’”

[edit] MAID (Massive Array of Ilde Disks) Technology

MAID (Massive Array of Idle Disks) technology was first introduced in 2002 and, since 2003, has been gaining interest for both data protection and archive data storage. MAID operates on the principle that when long-term data is stored on disk, all disks don’t need to spin all the time. Instead, only those disks that contain data that is requested by a specific application are powered on, and then are powered off when they are not needed. Because MAID storage systems are “energy efficient,” they significantly reduce the power consumed by a storage array.

A MAID is usually constructed with low-cost Serial Advanced Technology Attachment (SATA) drives, which have shorter mean time between failures (MTBF) ratings than more expensive drives. To increase data availability, MAID can also provide traditional levels of RAID data protection – just like current disk arrays – giving the advantage of data protection that tape systems do not.
