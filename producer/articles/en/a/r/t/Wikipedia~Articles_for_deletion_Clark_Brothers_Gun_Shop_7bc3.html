  • Keep. The article is less awful now. --TruthbringerToronto (Talk | contribs) 06:26, 31 October 2006 (UTC)
    • I'd say if the claim that it's "one of the most famous gun stores in the United States" is verifiable it's a keeper. But articles that just happen to mention the store in the context of gun violence aren't enough to establish that claim. ~ trialsanderrors 06:33, 31 October 2006 (UTC)
  • Delete. I've lived in northern Virginia for going on 15 years. I frequently take day trips out towards the mountains with my folks, and we almost always pass through Warrenton. I don't recall once ever seeing anything about this place. I've never heard of it. Hell, I'd be more inclined to support an article on the Sperryville Emporium... --User:AlbertHerring Io son l'orecchio e tu la bocca: parla! 07:50, 31 October 2006 (UTC)

  • Delete lacks verification.-- danntm T C 14:54, 31 October 2006 (UTC)

  • Keep Albert Herring the stores on Rt 29 6 miles south of Warrenton going to Culpepper, not on 211 which goes to Sperryville. More press all stories about Clark Brothers Gun Shop: Washington Post Nov 6,1977 Angus Phillips, Washington Post Aug.31,1975 "Shooters will aid childrens hospitol",Washington Post Jun 19,1978 Blaine Harden,Guns,Washington Post Nov.21,1986 Bill Sautter "Standing Around Shooting the bull".
