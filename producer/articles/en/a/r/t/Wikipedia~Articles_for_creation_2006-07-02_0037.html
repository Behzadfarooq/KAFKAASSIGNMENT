Shas'o Viorla Shovah Kaius Montyr (or Farsight if translated) was thought to be the greatest Tau commander of his time, famous for his fight against the Orks. He was favored by the great Ethereals, leaders of the Tau, and was trusted to lead their most crucial campaigns.

O’Shavah was entrusted with leading the force at Damocles Gulf and had done an amazing job, bringing the humans to their knees. Eventually he defeated his opponent, scattering the survivors in terror. He was to be greeted back a T’au as a hero, but he never came.

As soon as the war was over he led his men to the other side of the Tau Empire and began selling their services as mercenaries. No one is so sure what went through O’Shavah’s mind when he did it. Believing he had lost O’Shavah to the darkness, Aun’Va placed the young Commander Shadowsun as leader of the Tau army, making her the first female general in the history of the Tau Empire.

O’Shavah and his soldiers have been sighted all over the galaxy, but always disappearing without a trace, leaving the question to the Ethereals: is he friend or foe?--Daniel Christie

Thomas Choate Moore, Grammy-award-winning record producer, was born on June 21, 1965, in Connecticut. He studied oboe at the North Carolina School for the Arts, moving on to the Cleveland Institute of Music as a student of John Mack, pricipal oboist of the Cleveland Orchestra. In 1996 Mr. Moore began his association with Telarc Records, a Cleveland-based recording company known for the superior quality of its recordings. Mr. Moore garnered two Grammy awards, Best Classical and Best Choral Album of 2002, for his producing work on Vaughn Williams' "A Sea Symphony." He also won a Grammy for Best Crossover Album of 2004 for his work on "4 by 4" by the Turtle Island String quartet. Mr. Moore resides in Cleveland.

Unorthodox: The WebManga is a webcomic drawn and written by KayZee. It was launched on January 14 2005. It was originally a bunch of silly drawings and sketches that she and her friends kidded about during their high school years.

Along with the webcomic there are other fun things on the site that the author encourages the readers to get involved in, such as the Vote Caster (which is currently asking viewers to choose the bonus back-story that is to come later).

Spoiler warning: Plot and/or ending details follow.

[edit] Main Characters

More info can be found on the website: [1]

  • Kioko Satou – A 17-year-old immature senior at Mousaki High School in Japan, she can be gluttonous at times but has no trouble dealing with her weight. She likes to read, but prefers shouen-ai manga over everything else. Her boyfriend is Benjiro, and despite her wandering eyes for bishounen she is still loyal to him. Her other friends are Su-Sihn, Jerolin, and Mina. First appears on Page 5 of Chapter 1.
  • Benjiro Takahashi – Benjiro, 17, is Kioko’s boyfriend. He sees no problem in the relationship except for the few months’ age difference. He could be jealous at times, especially when Kioko ogles at other guys, but he knows that she is still loyal to him. But he also has a secret (which most people know about anyway), every time he experiences a strong emotion (love, pain, fear, etc) he turns into a catboy! That is all thanks to Meu-Cu, the mischievous cat demon. When Benjiro was 7 and was playing roughly with a cat, Meu-Cu sought to teach him a lesson by putting a spell on him. Unfortunately, Meu-Cu forgot to take the spell off! First appears on Page 5 of Chapter 1.
  • Jerolin Nakamura – Jerolin, 18, is the most mysterious of the seniors. He has a natural talent for poetry, philosophy, and history. His mind-set is that of a general but with the attitude of a saint. But what is most interesting about Jerolin is his family history, for he is of human, elf, vampire, and werewolf descent. Thanks to this he is very skilled and agile, making him the best swordsman in the school. But his parentage also comes with a dark side: for once a month he must go into hiding because he turns into a monster. However, Su-Sihn helps him by brewing a potion that prevents the transformations. He spends his free time training to become a Kensai. First appears on Page 9 of Chapter 1.
  • Su-Sihn Matsumoto – Su-Sihn, 18, is a senior with a marvelous writing talent. Her genres of choice are fantasy and horror that rarely end happily. She has a very vivid imagination, so much so that she has 35 imaginary friends who are bats (this completely baffles the school psychologist). After school Su-Sihn practices her witch-craft and sorcery in her parents’ basement. Her powers have gotten her out of, and sometimes into, awkward situations. On the side for extra profit she sells potions and brews. First appears on Page 7 of Chapter 1.
  • Mina Smith – 15-year-old Mina moved from Ohio, USA to Japan because of her father’s job outsourcing. It wasn’t easy adjusting to a new life. For one she could barely understand Japanese and the fact that she is taller than everyone in her freshman class. When she’s not trying to decipher her schoolwork she enjoys playing video games and writing fanfiction. Although she is friends with Kioko, Mina is often disturbed by her love for shouen-ai manga. The only thing she’s afraid of is spiders. First appears on Page 1 of Chapter 1.
  • Meu-Cu – Meu-Cu is an ancient cat-demon who randomly appears to cause mischief. The only people who can actually see him are Benjiro and Mina. Long, long ago Meu-Cu was once a regular pet golden cat of a Japanese Emperor. One day when the Emperor was visited by a Kirin, a sacred Japanese unicorn, Meu-Cu jumped on the beast’s back and was carried off to the Immortal Lands. There, Meu-Cu drank from the holy river and was given eternal life, but it also turned him into a leg-less ball-like cat demon. Meu-Cu is also the one responsible for turning Benjiro into a catboy. First appears on Page 5 of Chapter 1 (look at the bottom-right corner of the first panel, he’s the yellow thing in the bushes)

[edit] Supporting Characters

More info can be found on the website: [2]

  • Kyoshi Kobayashi – Kyoshi is Jerolin’s archrival on the school Kendo team. However, they weren’t always adversaries, at one time they were inseparable friends. Until one day, when Kyoshi visited Jerolin when he was out of school, sick. But Kyoshi didn’t know that Jerolin was really out of school because it was time for one of his ‘transformations’. Kyoshi had brought with him a couple of bottles of sake to ‘help ease his pain’. Jerolin, who was in a great deal of pain, downed most of the bottles. Their drunkenness spiraled down into a series of unfortunate events that Jerolin deeply regretted. Jerolin vowed to never trust Kyoshi ever again. Even long after that day, despite teasing Jerolin most of the time, Kyoshi finds himself wanting things to be as they were before. First appears on Page 10 of Chapter 1.
  • Haruaki Watanabe – Haruaki is a quiet junior who Mina has a crush on. Haruaki is shy around girls, mostly due to a relationship that ended badly in his freshmen year. However, he feels comfortable around Mina and he takes it as a sign that maybe things will be better for him when it comes to girls. He doesn’t plan to get involved with Mina specifically, but Hanna doesn’t know that. First appears on Page 11 of Chapter 1.
  • Hanna Matsushita – Hanna is a freshman and childhood friend of Haruaki. She was the one that helped him out last year when he was ending his relationship with another girl. It’s one of the reasons that she is very (if not overly) protective of Haruaki. Being this way, she has made it her goal to keep the American, Mina, away from him and to make her high school career a living hell. Hanna and Mina are also on the same Tennis team, which causes a lot of tension during practices. But deep down inside Hanna wants Haruaki for herself because she cares about him too much to lose him to another girl. First appears on Page 12 of Chapter 1
  • Taniko Matsushita – Taniko is Hanna’s cousin. She looks up to Hanna like a big sister, but Taniko often ends up doing her cousin’s bidding. Taniko is terrified of Hanna’s explosive temper. First appears on Page 12 of Chapter 1.
  • Aykia Sasaki – sensei – Aykia is the newest and youngest of the teachers (he’s 28 and the rest of them are late 30’s+) at Moussaki High School. He is often teased by the older teachers who like to call him “Aki-chan”. As hard as he tries not to be, he often appears immature, hyper, and sometimes whiny. He teaches freshman Algebra. First appears on Page 16 of Chapter 1

[edit] Chapter 1: First Dayz

The first chapter mostly introduces the main characters and some supporting characters. It is Monday and the first day of the new term at Mousaki High School. Mina is running late and has trouble getting breakfast at the local fast-food place because she can hardly speak Japanese. She barely make the train to school and we learn that she is in Japan.

Meanwhile, we are introduced to Kioko and her friends. Benjiro and Su Sihn meet up with her and they head inside, where they find Jerolin and Kyoshi. Mina finally makes it and feels tiny compared to the size of the huge school. Mina finds her classroom and meets Haruaki, much to Hanna’s distaste. As Mina’s class begins, the new teacher, Sasaki-sensei, is introduced. During his class, Mina encounters Meu-Cu and unintentionally disrupts the lesson.

A typical Sith holocron

A Sith holocron was a device used by Sith to store information. Sith holocrons originated from secrets shared with the Sith King Adas by Rakatan soldiers trying to gain the trust of the Sith ruler. Adas made a holocron based on Rakatan design and presumably the art of holocron making originated among the ancient Sith from this. They were immediately identifiable with respect to Jedi holocrons, as they were usually pyramidal in shape, and etched with strange symbols and ancient hieroglyphs. Inscriptions usually lined the bottom of the device, and various dark incantations were found all over the smooth surface. At the holocron's apex rested a black crystal, used to power the holocron's store of information. The design of the holocron was not the only way to discern its affiliation: Sith holocrons emitted a dark presence, creating tremors in the Force upon activation. It took a great deal of willpower for a Jedi to access the dark devices, and some of the more inexperienced Jedi often felt sick and ill from merely laying their gaze upon a Sith holocron.

Internally, however, Sith holocrons worked in much the same way as did a Jedi holocron. The black crystal of the Sith holocron was made up of intricate lattices and vertices, capable of storing massive amounts of information, as well as the holocron's Sith gatekeeper. The holocron was able to capture the appearance and cognitive networks of the holocron's owner, and transform this into a three-dimensional hologram. This gatekeeper acted as a guide through the holocron's store of information, as a search and recovery engine with a more personal touch. These gatekeepers could relate stories from the holocron's archives to events in the life of the one who activated the holocron.

Image:CuriousJedi.JPG

Jedi gatekeepers could sense the ability and level of a Jedi, and offer or restrict information accordingly—so that Padawans were not shown information too advanced and dangerous for their basic stage. However, Sith holocrons usually allowed the activator unlimited access to its entire dark side repository. The gatekeeper would draw the viewer in with tales of power, and could even turn some Force-users to the Dark Side. The pull of the Sith holocron was strong, indeed. Which the Jedi Historian, Atris discovered as she collected the holocrons that slowly turned her to the dark side. As of 3,996 BBY, only one known Sith holocron existed, having been captured by Odan-Urr during the Great Hyperspace War; forty years later, Atris kept scores of them in the Telosian Jedi Academy. The holocron captured by Odan-Urr was said to contain information dating back to around 100,000 BBY.

Most of the Sith holocrons were created long before the Clone Wars, and as of the Battle of Geonosis, the Jedi Archives on Coruscant contained most of the only surviving Sith holocrons in the Galaxy. These devices were locked away to all but the most senior Jedi—in fact, most of the Jedi in the Temple did not even know Sith holocrons resided in the building. The holocrons were kept in case the Jedi would ever face the threat of the Sith again, whereupon Jedi Masters could consult the holocrons for ideas on how to defeat the Dark Lords. However, the Sith holocrons were not as safe as the Archives Director believed: Jedi student Lorian Nod was able to steal a Sith holocron from the Archives, though the theft ended in his expulsion from the Jedi Order. Count Dooku stole at least one Sith holocron from the Temple during the First Battle of Coruscant. The remaining Sith holocrons in the Temple at the end of the Clone Wars were (according to Sidious) forgeries, but Sidious had his apprentice Darth Vader recover them nonetheless.

Several ancient Sith holocrons were kept by Darth Plagueis and were presumably inherited by Darth Sidious. Another Sith holocron was created by Darth Andeddu, an ancient Sith Lord. This holocron was recovered during a competition between Quinlan Vos and Tol Skorr for Count Dooku during the Clone Wars, while Quinlan was allied with Dooku. It contained much dark side knowledge as well as a red lightsaber crystal embedded in its core, possibly used to power Darth Andeddu's own Sith lightsaber. The holocron itself was kept by Tyranus while the red crystal was used in Quinlan Vos' lightsaber for a brief time.

A Toydarian named Zippa tried to sell Lorn Pavan a cubical Holocron in 32.5 BBY that Pavan suspected was a Sith Holocron, but as the deal fell through, this was never confirmed as a Sith Holocron.

Zhuanglang is a county in Gansu Province, China. It is a poor rural county, one of eight county-level divisions in Pingliang District in the east of Gansu Province. Zhuanglang County is bordered in the west by Jingning, in the south by Qin'an and Zhangjiachuan Hui Autonomous County, in the west by Huating (all in Gansu Province) and in the north by Ningxia Hui Muslim Autonomous Region. Nearby major cities include Pingliang, Tianshui, Guyuan and Lanzhou.


The county town of Zhuanglang is known as Shuiluo, and is a busy administrative, educational and trading centre in the centre of the county. Other market towns in the county include Nanhu, Zhudian and Handian in the north, south-west and south-east respectively.


The people of Zhuanglang are predominantly farmers, producing wheat and potatoes on the terraced hills. The farmers of Zhuanglang were proclaimed in the 1980s for having terraced all available hillside, and a monument in the county town records the famous 'Zhuanglang Spirit' by which this was achieved.


In recent years education has been a priority for the local government, and with the help of World Bank funding, schools in Zhuanglang have been well supported. Nearly all children now complete primary education, while there are 23 junior middle schools, and 3 senior middle schools which send students on to colleges and universities.


The cultural sites of Zhuanglang include:

Yunyasi. The beautiful Yunya temple forms the centrepiece of a protected forest park in the far east of the county, around 40km from the county town.

Zi Jin Temple. This temple was re-opened in 1986 after the Cultural Revolution and is in the centre of the county town. Close by is an earth fortress dating from the era of Mongol invasions through this region.

Chen Dong Temple. This more isolated Taoist temple is to be found in the north-east of the county.


The highest point of the county is Taomushan at 2857m above sea level.


Population (2005 est.) Total = 420,000 Ethnic composition = 99% Han Chinese, 1 % Hui Muslim Major Towns Shuiluo = 30,000 Nanhu = 10,000 Zhudian = 6,000 Handian = 5,000

Llyn Trelfesg is part of the Valley Lakes RSPB reserve on the west coast of Anglesey near to Royal Airforce Valley and within the parish of Llanfair-yn-Neubwll. It was owned by the Magee family until sold to the Royal Society for the Protection of Birds in the 1990s. It is a man-made lake, formed when building material was needed to construct the adjacent RAF base. The lake is surrounded by marsh, scrub and grazing land and is attractive to view. It contains fresh water fish species such as perch, roach, rudd and eels. It has breeding populations of Mute Swans, Greylag Geese, Canada Geese, Mallard, Teal, Ruddy Duck, Coot, Moorhen and Reed Warblers. Slow worms, common frogs, common lizards and adders are found around its shores as are foxes, rabbits and brown hares. Water voles were common in the 1990s.

Between 1965 and 1986 he taught Italian and Maltese at secondary and sixth-form level, from 1986 onwards was first assistant head and, since 1993, head in Government schools. "Paceville", the winner of the Govenrment award for 1991 remains the best testimony of modern Maltese life, while his other award-winning novel "Il-Ħolma Maltija"(1994) was considered by "The Times" as reaching a level and stature previously unknown in Maltese literature. Sammut published a great number of short stories, some of them as collected works like "Il-Qtil ta' Sqaq il-Ħorr" (1988) and "Newbiet" (1998). Sammut's passion for history is evinced in his books "Ir-Rivoluzzjoni Franċiża" (1989) and "Bonaparti f'Malta" (1997, translated into French as "Bonaparte à Malte") among others. In insular Celtic languages, a prepositional pronoun is an inflected pronoun that indicates the pronoun is related to the preposition. For instance, in Scottish, to say "before him," one would not say ro e but roimhe, an amalgamation of the two words. Prepositional pronouns also have emphatic forms (roimhesan, in that case).

Pierre Bezukhov is a character in Lev Tolstoy's novel "War and Peace" Pierre, whom many critics regard as a reflection of Tolstoy himself, attracts our sympathy in his status as an outsider to the Russian upper classes. His simplicity and emotional directness contrast with the artificiality of fakes such as the Kuragins. Though the attendees at Anna Pavlovna’s party consider Pierre uncouth and awkward, this very awkwardness emphasizes his natural unpretentiousness. We see his love of fun in his expulsion from St. Petersburg for excessive partying, and his generosity in his bank-breaking largesse toward friends and acquaintances following his inheritance. Pierre, though intelligent, is not dominated by reason, as his friend Andrew is. Pierre’s emotional spurts occasionally get him into trouble, as when his sexual passions make him prey to the self-serving and beautiful Helene. His madcap escape into the city of Moscow and his subsequent obsessive belief that he is destined to be Napoleon’s assassin show his submission to irrational impulses. Yet there is also a great nobility in Pierre’s emotions, and his search for meaning in his life becomes a central theme of the novel. We feel that his final marriage to Natasha represents the culmination of a life of moral and spiritual questioning.
