Randy Napoleon is one of the most sought after guitarists in New York today. He is currently touring with Warner Brothers recording artist Michael Bublé and preparing to record a second album with his own group.

Napoleon began backing Bublé in the fall of 2004. Since then, he has appeared with the Canadian-born top-of-the-charts crooner on TV in Amsterdam, Barcelona, Berlin, London, Milan, Paris, Sydney, Toronto (Canada AM and CityLine) and the U.S. (Letterman; Leno; The View; Today, Regis and Kelly; The Ellen Degeneres Show; a PBS special, Caught in the Act; The Radio Music Awards, Dancing with the Stars), on BBC radio, and on the stages of prestigious music halls all over the globe.

His career took off one month after moving to New York City, when Napoleon joined pianist Benny Green's trio and achieved international exposure. Since that auspicious debut on the New York scene, he continued to make a name for himself as a forward thinking musician with a passion for the jazz guitar tradition.

Napoleon played many major European festivals and toured Japan with the Clayton-Hamilton Jazz Orchestra (CHJO), named by readers of Downbeat Magazine as best big band in 2003 and by Jazz Times as #2 big band last year. In 2004, he appeared on a TV special in Tokyo with CHJO.

Napoleon's connection to this LA-based Jazz Orchestra dates back to his college days, when Jeff Hamilton heard him perform with the University of Michigan Big Band. Through his association with CHJO, Napoleon has performed with a Who's Who list of jazz artists, including George Benson, Michael Brecker, Frank Foster, Kenny Garrett, Shirley Horn, Al Jarreau, Diana Krall, Joe Lovano, Christian McBride, Lewis Nash, Nicholas Payton, John Pizzarelli, Dianne Reeves, Frank Wess, Nancy Wilson, and Snooky Young.

He has also played with major jazz artists including Rodney Whitaker and Rick Roe, with master band leaders Grady Tate and Freddy Cole, and with top cabaret performers, including Eric Comstock. He has formed partnerships with some of the finest musicians of his own generation, among them Josh Brown, Julius Tolintino and Sachal Vasandani. He has appeared with Paul Anka, Chris Botti, and Laura Pausini. His trio, a collaboration with organist Jared Gold and drummer Quincy Davis, continues to attract attention nationally.
