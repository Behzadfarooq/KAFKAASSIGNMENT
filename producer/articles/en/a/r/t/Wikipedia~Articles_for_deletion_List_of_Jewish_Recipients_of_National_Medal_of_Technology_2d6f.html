    • Because Buddhism is not an ethnicity? Kappa 18:08, 16 November 2005 (UTC)
      • But Jewishness isn't necessarily one either. Assume we had a List of Jewish entertainers. Would Sammy Davis, Jr. be included? --howcheng [ t • c • w • e ] 22:05, 16 November 2005 (UTC)

  • delete all such lists where religion is likley irrelevant to the achievement. DES (talk) 17:59, 16 November 2005 (UTC)
    • No reason to suppose that being Jewish is irrelevant - in similar lists, such as Jewish Nobel Prize winners and Jewish Fellows of the Royal Society, it is clear that the proportion of Jews in these categories is quite high, so there is prima facie evidence of some link - RachelBrown 13:37, 18 November 2005 (UTC)
  • Keep all lists of achievement by ethnicity. Kappa 18:08, 16 November 2005 (UTC)
  • Delete they did not win the award for being Jewish so the list is pointless --TimPope 18:37, 16 November 2005 (UTC)
  • Delete per DESiegel, and for the reasons I mentioned in other such lists. Nandesuka 19:56, 16 November 2005 (UTC)
  • Move to List of recipients of the National Medal of Technology (national in what country?) and seriously EXPAND, or if that fails delete. The fact they're Jewish has nothing to do with them winning the medal. Makes no point in sorting that way. Let's get a list of winners first and worry about sorting later. - Mgm|(talk) 20:19, 16 November 2005 (UTC)
  • Delete Wikipedia is not a home to lists of random unrelated things. Durova 23:49, 16 November 2005 (UTC)
  • Delete as non-encyclopedic, per nom. Jtmichcock 03:04, 17 November 2005 (UTC)
  • Keep being a certain nationality often has nothing to do with being a certain profession either so according to that argument all the professions by nationality categories/lists should be merged. Arniep 18:09, 17 November 2005 (UTC)
  • Delete There's an infinite amount of these lists - List of Jewish Recipients of National Medal of Science should probably be voted on too. 65.9.143.76 00:10, 18 November 2005 (UTC)
  • Delete, trivia. Radiant_>|< 12:39, 18 November 2005 (UTC)
  • Keep - there has been a recent flood of these AfDs; nobody can explain convincingly why this applies to Jews but not (say) Armenians or gay people. - RachelBrown 12:55, 18 November 2005 (UTC)
    • This isn't specifically about Jews. There exists neither a List of Armenian Recipients of National Medal of Technology nor a List of LGBT Recipients of National Medal of Technology, and if there were I would also vote to delete those. Radiant_>|< 13:11, 18 November 2005 (UTC)
      • So why haven't you slapped an AfD on say Category:Armenian chess players or List of gay, lesbian or bisexual composers? - RachelBrown 13:18, 18 November 2005 (UTC)
        • Because I was unaware of them, of course. It's a big wiki. In my opinion, Category:Armenian chess players should stay, because we categorize just about every person in Category:Countryname profession. I don't quite see the point of LGBT composers, though, especially considering the sorry state that mess is in. I've put on tags to {{merge}} it and note that it's mostly {{unsourced}}. The answer to questions such as this is generally, {{sofixit}}. If you perceive a problem, doing something about it is more useful than complaining. Radiant_>|< 01:00, 19 November 2005 (UTC)
          • I have replied to Mr Radiant on his talk page and won't waste space by repeating it here. - RachelBrown 09:30, 19 November 2005 (UTC)
          • I agree with Radiant. We already had this discussion about the distinction between category and list. As for the LGB composers -- that needs to be checked out. Antidote 03:28, 19 November 2005 (UTC)
  • Delete per above. Lists per country are interesting, lists per nationality are not.  Grue  18:29, 18 November 2005 (UTC)
