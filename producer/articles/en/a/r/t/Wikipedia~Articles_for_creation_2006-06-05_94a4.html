[edit] Maud and other poems

'Maud' (1855), by Alfred, Lord Tennyson, appeared in the book Maud and other poems, his first collection since becoming poet laureate in 1850. The narrative of the poem concerns an unnamed lover. The first part of the poem dwells on the funeral of Maud's father, and a feeling of loss and lament prevails. Then the appearance of Maud transforms the narrative into a pastoral dwelling on her beauty. The appearance of Maud's brother causes conflict, and the poet kills him in an unnarrated duel. Maud's death impacts the pyschological state of the protagonist, and an emotinal longing for contact with the deceased echoes the tones of his earlier work, In Memoriam. The poem ends in Part III with the poet leaving to fight in the Crimean war, and parallels may be drawn between the death of Maud's brother, and the apparently justified killing of soldiers in war.

The interpretation of 'Maud' is complicated by the compromised position of the narrator; his emotional instability of the poet. The puzzle of the outside sphere of 'Maud', for example, the point of view of Maud herself, remains unresolved. The poem is a distorted view of a single reality, and the variation in meter can be seen to reflect the manic-depressive emotional tone of the speaker. While the poem was Tennyson's own favourite, it was met with much criticism in contemporary circles.

In 'Maud', Tennyson returns to the poetry of sensation, and dwells on a consciousness constituted of fragments of feeling. He deliberately denies an autonomous voice, and the ending is deeply ironic. The complex of feeling is ephemeral, and the culmination of these feelings ends in the unsatisfactory conclusion of the Crimean War. Tennyson is expressing the feelings of an age where identity, intellect and modernity where contentious issues. He does not offer a clear, linear answer. The chivalric style of the love-poem is combined with a contemporary cynicism, and so the Victorian tendency to look to remote cultures (here, medievalism) is insufficient. The interweaving of death and life images gives expression to the greater concern for the afterlife, and the movement of the human race into a greater age from past monuments.

[edit] Cardio Kickboxing

Cardio Kickboxing is a sport specific equipment based fitness program created by Frank Thiboutot at The Bay Club in Portland, Maine in 1992. The original circuit training format was developed to promote the sport of kickboxing through kickboxing for fitness. The techniques used during the one-hour workout are based on authentic kickboxing techniques which provide both resistance and cardio vascular benefits to its participants. The techniques learned can also be used for self-defense purposes. Similar copycat programs have been promoted with or without the use of equipment with cardio kickboxing becoming almost a generic term for this type of workout. However, Cardio Kickboxing is a registered U.S. Trademark, #1,890,451. For more information, please visit: www. cardiokickboxing.com.

[edit] Richard Lipsey

Richard Lipsey is a Canadian economist born in Victoria (British Columbia), British Columbia. He attended Victoria College (now part of the University of British Columbia) before going on to the London School of Economics. During the 1960s he was a dean at the University of Essex before returning to Canada. During the 1970s he became the Sir Edward Peacock professor of economics t Queen's University in Kingston and in 1983 he acted as a senior economic advisor to the CD Howe Institute in Toronto. He eventually returned to British Columbia to be a professor emeritus at Simon Fraser University. He is an officer of the Order of Canada and a fellow of the Royal Society of Canada and the Econometric Society. In 2006 he won the prize and gold medal for achievement in research from the Social Sciences and Humanities Research Council of Canada.

He is also the author or co-author of several economics textbooks including Positive Economics, Theory of the Second Best and Economic Transformations:General Purpose Technologies and Long Term Economic Growth.

Perth Airport (IATA: PSL, ICAO: EGPT) is a general aviation airport located in Perth Scotland. It is located in the village of New Scone just outside the City of Perth. It opened in 1936. A flight training school training military pilots was established by Airwork Ltd shortly after the airport was opened. Before the war a number of scheduled airline services operated from Perth to various domestic locations. After the war Airwork moved into civilian pilot training.

By 1960 Airwork acquired Air Service Training (AST) an engineering training school, which it relocated to the airport. The whole operation took on the AST name. AST gained a world wide reputation for aviation training, being known as Britain's Air University. Students of more than 100 countries have been trained at Perth. Following a worldwide downturn in aviation, AST pulled out of pilot training in 1996.

The airport was bought by Morris Leslie Ltd. Perth airport remains Scotland's main airport for general aviation and is home to the Scottish Aero Club founded in 1927. The airport is home to three flight training schools as well as numerous aviation as well as non aviation related businesses.

[edit] The Puerto Rican Commission

The Puerto Rican Commission was a political group composed of prominent, pro-American Puerto Rican expatriates that accompanied a U.S. expeditionary force sent to assist in the invasion of Puerto Rico during the Spanish-American War of 1898. The Commission or "volunteers," as they were otherwise known, that landed at Arroyo on 1 August 1898 consisted of: Pedro Juan Besosa, José Budet, Domingo Collazo, Mateo Fajardo, Emilio González, Antonio Mattei Lluveras, Rafael Marxuach, Rafael Muñoz, and Warren Sutton (the only American in the group). Once disembarked, the Commission assisted in effectuating the transition of police, administrative, juridical, and–most importantly–political power to American military and civilian authority. Many of the original Commission "volunteers" had, pre-war, promoted insurgent and anexionist causes, as a result of which they had awaken the vigilance of Spanish authorities. The Puerto Rico Commission's 1 August 1898 landing at Arroyo was both the definitive manifestation and the fruition of their anexionist and pro-American goals.

[edit] Nathan Holley

Born in the small town of Nipigon, Ontario on February 8th, 1983, Nathan Holley started playing music at the early age of 5. Using his parent’s pots and pans to build his own drums and singing at the top of his lungs, Nathan's musical hunger was very apparent. By the age of 11 Nathan scraped together his life savings his kind mother had slowly been donating too, and bought his first drum set. Playing with his brother Shane Holley, who was amazingly talented on the guitar, Both Nathan and Shane slowly honed there musical skills. At the age of 12, Nathan started taking piano lessons, which was the musical basis he translates to every other instrument. In elementary school band, he also played the alto saxophone and percussion. As the years went on Nathan's skills increased, from singing and playing drums at the same time, too composing music on the guitar and bass. Using his computer Nathan composed various songs, playing all the instruments and creating unique vocal harmonies. After 1 year of Film school in Vancouver BC, Nathan strived to find talented musicians who share the same passion toward music as he did. In early 2006, after endless searching, Nathan found Colin Dodds, a keyboardist and guitarist, who was currently looking for a drummer for his duo project he initially created with Alex Hudson in 2004, called "The Reasonable Men". Hitting it off right away, Nathan instantly fit in, contributing unique drum beats and smooth vocal harmonies. Alongside Colin Dodds on keyboard, there was lead singer Alex Hudson on guitar, Ryan Krumins on lead guitar and Evan Henderson on bass. The band merges diverse influences (rock, folk, funk and blues) with classic pop songwriting to create a style that is uniquely their own. With upcoming studio time in the summer of 2006, and a slate of live shows, Nathan Holley is getting ready to show Vancouver, his hidden talent that's been behind closed doors, until now.

[edit] Pimp That Snack

Pimp My Snack is a British website which received media attention during April 2006. Inspired by the MTV program Pimp My Ride, the website features guides on creating giant versions of everyday snacks. Articles are submitted by members of the public and voted upon.

The website creator Pete Wilcock, from Cheadle Hulme in Manchester, has been interviewed on national radio, newspapers, and popular magazines such Zoo, Nuts, and Loaded.

[edit] Ouachita National Recreation Trail

The Ouachita National Recreation Trail (or Ouachita Trail, OT) runs 223 miles through the beautiful Ouachita Mountains of Arkansas and Oklahoma. The Western terminus is in Talimena State Park in Oklahoma; the Eastern terminus in Pinnacle Mountain State Park, near Little Rock, Arkansas. The entire trail is open to hikers and backpackers, and mountain bikers may ride 137 miles of the trail.
