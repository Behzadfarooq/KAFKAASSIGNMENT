[edit] Cult Dynamite

Cult Dynamite is a localised and enigmatic phenomenon, which was founded in the North Shore of Sydney, Australia.

In mid-2005, multiple motor vehicles were attacked, with the locution 'Cult Dynamite' being written in permanent marker on the windscreeen (or other glass panels) of the motor vehicle. These attacks typically happen in the late evening. The mastermind/s of this phenomenon are unknown, however it is widely believed that this movement has many supporters and therefore copycat attacks are likely to have occurred. There is also ambivalence as to the denotations in the phrase. It has been suggested that the Cult Dynamite phenomenon is a reaction to political and social pressures, with cult portraying an image of public veneration towards authority, and dynamite suggesting something that has the potential to generate extreme reactions and emotion.

[edit] Preston B. Whitmore

Preston B. Whitmore is the eccentric billionaire who funds the expedition to discover Atlantis in the 2001 Disney film, Atlantis: The Lost Empire. He was played by actor, John Mahoney.

Born in 1844 in Seattle, Washington, Whitmore was a graduate of Georgetown University (Class of 1866) before becoming the founder and sole owner of Preston Whitmore Industries, Ltd., an empire that encompassed logging operations in the states of Washington and Oregon; the Southern Rail Freight line; Whitmore Mining and Petroleum in Texas, Alaska Territories, and South Africa; Whitmore Stockyards in Houston, Texas; and P.W.I. Shipbuilding in Norfolk, Virginia.

Whitmore is also considered an eccentric, practicing Yoga long before it was fashionable and favoring a hiring system based on merit rather than on one’s ancestry, gender or ethnic background (a practice decades ahead of its time!)

An avid collector of rare antiquities, Whitmore spent sizable fortunes on expeditions to retrieve precious relics with the help of his longtime friend and advisor Professor Thaddeus Thatch (who he met back at Georgetown). Whitmore even accompanied Thatch on a number of these expeditions spanning the globe, sharing his friend’s ambition to find treasures and places of myth.

He also listened to Thatch talk endlessly about the Shepherd’s Journal, an ancient text which allegedly contained a guide to the lost empire of Atlantis. Doubting the existence of this text, Whitmore made a bet with Thatch that if he could find the Shepherd’s Journal, then he would not only fund the expedition to find Atlantis, but that he would kiss Thatch “full on the mouth.” Of course, Thatch did find the Journal in Iceland and Whitmore was forced to make good on his promise.

Unfortunately, Thatch was by now a laughingstock among his colleagues and he died a broken man. Whitmore was then forced to replace him with Thaddeus’s grandson, Milo Thatch, who shared his grandfather’s passion for Atlantis. And so, after hiring the finest crew available at the time, designing and building Ulysses, a state-of-the-art submarine and equipping his team with all the gear and supplies they would need, Whitmore saw the expedition off. The only reason he didn’t join the expedition himself was because of ill health.

Later, Whitmore helped cover up for Atlantis after learning their very survival depended on surface-dwellers being ignorant of its existence. For his generous assistance, Whitmore was given a special Atlantean crystal pendant that helped restore his full health and vigor.

Whitmore finally visited Atlantis himself in the Disney sequel, Atlantis: Milo’s Return, and was instrumental in reassembling the team for a series of adventures back on the surface. His fate thereafter is unknown.

[edit] Ed Labetski

Ed Labetski is a talented artist who is mostly known for his digital painings. It might not be accepted as an art-form by everyone, but he creates it like any other conventional art. He tries to make digital paintings more acceptable by making the result look to people in a familiar way, while using exclusively computers to create them. He has also held exhibitions of his printed digital paintings[5][6][7] (in fact, the very first of its kind in Estonia) to bridge the gap. Ed does not have a degree in art, but he has studied conventional ways extensively and also paints using conventional techniques like oil.

Ed Labetski is also the author of Unitube webcomic.

[edit] Biography

Ed Labetski was born in 1982.

Ed's parents have lived in Estonia for years. It was then a border-state of the Soviet Union. It's likely that there the fear of nuclear disaster was driven into subconscious particularily firmly during the Cold War and was passed on to the children. When Ed was a child, the Soviet Union collapsed, which was accompanied by rapid money devalvation voiding everyone's lifetime's savings. Bread had to be aquired for food stamps. Shops were rather empty, though it wasn't much different from Soviet Union era.

A turbulent time came along with Ed's turbulent teen years. His little sister has been sent to mental institution. People had to learn to value simple things, though many chose to drown their sorrow in alcohol, which happened also in his family. Estonia regained its independence and Soviet version of history was replaced by a contradicting account. This shook many people's world views. Some met it with angry defiance, others learnt to be healthily sceptical.

Things were going well for Estonia -- it could be proclaimed a part of Europe now and become a very fast developing country embracing Western world. Computerisation became a high priority and schools have been provided with computers so people could get comfortable with them at an early age. Ed took interest in art and seized an opportunity of using the Internet to self-teach different aspects of art -- from oil to 3D.

Ed tried to enter an art university, but was rejected. He went to work as a computer designer instead and kept drawing in his spare time developing a recognisable style. He has held exhibitions[5][6][7] and received several offers to buy his paintings. In 2003, he went to Spain on a whim and after some time was hired to paint over the entire bar's interior, then worked as a computer designer. In 2005, he returned to Estonia and became an art director of an advertising company. He still draws in his spare time.

[edit] FCI Ray Brook

The Federal Correctional Institution, Ray Brook is a medium security men's prison in the northern Adirondack Mountains at Ray Brook, NY. Constructed midway between two popular resort towns, Lake Placid and Saranac Lake, to first serve as the Olympic Village for the 1980 Winter Games, Ray Brook was designed to be converted to a prison and was wholly financed by the federal government. Its isolated location in the woods about four miles west of Lake Placid, and its spartan accomodations drew nearly universal scorn from the world's premier winter athletes, who disliked spending two weeks in a jail. Among the most critical were the members of the Soviet Union's acclaimed hockey team, which was upset by the "Miracle on Ice" United States squad that won the gold medal.

[edit] Hand Turkey

A hand turkey is a drawing made to resemble a wild turkey (Meleagris gallopavo) by tracing the outline of the artist's open hand and adding various elements such as a wing, beak, eye, or feathers. In the sketch, the artist's thumb corresponds to the turkey's head, and the fingers correspond to the standing feathers typical of a turkey. Because it is so easy to draw, a hand turkey is a favorite project for children, especially during Thanksgiving because of the importance of the wild turkey in that holiday.

[edit] Ronald Reagan (Revision)

You need an edit to your Ronald Reagan entry (I'm quoting Wikipedia here):

"His economic policy of supply-side economics, popularly known as "Reaganomics", is noted for a 25% cut in the income tax, reduction in inflation, reduction in interest rates, increased military spending, increased deficits and national debt, a temporary solution to the Social Security issue, elimination of loopholes in the tax code, continued deregulation of business, a sharp recession in 1981-1982 followed by a very robust economic expansion starting in '82 — the greatest peacetime economic expansion in U.S. history.[citation needed] "

The reason a "citation [is] needed" is that this "very robust...greatest peacetime economic expansion in U.S. history" never occurred during Reagan's presidency (perhaps during Clinton's). Yes, an average business cycle recovery occurred, but the post-Reagan spin machine continues to tout it as "Morning in America" (from the Wall St. Journal) The details for why this was no such thing are contained in Paul Krugman's "Peddling Prosperity: Economic Sense and Nonsense in an Age of Diminished Expectations"

Krugman convincingly makes the case that presidential policy (as opposed to Federal Reserve policy) influences productive capacity, or capital investment most directly, and the course of the business cycle indirectly. Because of Reagan's enormous deficits, capital investment actually declined during his administration, while the business cycle, peak-to-peak, that most nearly coincided with the term of his presidency was unexceptional in how much higher the later peak was than the earlier one. It was certainly not the greatest peacetime economic expansion, and if you factor in the growth in income disparities, it was disproportionately throwing money at the rich.

One further revision in the Reagan article: The S&L Bailout is correctly cited by your Reagan entry as a scandal his administration fostered. What you do not include is the magnitude of the scandal. The following is a quote that pretty much speaks for itself:

"The theft from the taxpayer by the community that fattened on the growth of the savings and loan (S&L) industry in the 1980's is the worst public scandal in American history. "...the swindles, like the industry itself, were a backwater, and, worse, the subject was inherently technical. [edit] Al (Adolph) Schwimmer

[edit] Sources

Al Schwimmer was born in the US in 1917 and grew up under impoverished circumstances during the great economic collapse. Despite numerous difficulties Al obtained a pilot’s license and education and was certified as a flight engineer. In 1942 he volunteered to the US army and during WWII served on the trans-Atlantic supply routes, demonstrating great courage which won him a citation at the end of the war. In 1947 he initiated contact with the Hagana representative in the US after being informed that assistance was needed to establish a mechanism for smuggling Holocaust refugees by air from Europe to Israel. Appalled by the sight of Holocaust survivors in Europe, he immediately volunteered to the cause. While in the throes of planning, he was asked to change the plan and to organize the purchase of aircraft and aviation equipment, and to recruit pilots and crew for the war anticipated following the establishment of Israel. Al left his position at TWA and devoted himself to this mission. He saw himself totally committed to helping the struggle to establish a Jewish state in Israel: He recruited scores of volunteer pilots, mechanics and air and ground crew; initiated the smuggling of planes to Israel in violation of the US embargo; and purchased all types of arms which was vital for the survival of the young country fighting for its life, often putting his own life at risk. When this became known to the US government, a warrant was issued for his arrest. Al played a pivotal role in the struggle to establish Israel, as a central figure in the recruitment of Machal (Hebrew acronym for overseas volunteers) volunteers, in obtaining military knowledge and know-how from around the world, and in locating sources of ammunition. His personality and total commitment to the Zionist ideal were legendary the world over, when he managed to accomplish what appeared to be impossible.

Al left the easy life in the US and despite the difficulties and risks, embarked on a new path which required superior leadership skills, firm social values and adherence to the goal. After playing a central role in establishing the Israeli Air Force, arming and equipping it, he returned to the US at the end of 1949 to stand trial. He was convicted of the illegal export of aircraft and of violating the laws of neutrality, was fined and stripped of his civil rights for life. In 1950 he founded a private airline company, and a plant for the repair and maintenance of planes in California. But this chapter of his life was short-lived: in 1951 on a visit to the US by Ben-Gurion, the Israeli Prime Minister urged him to return to Israel and to set up an aircraft industry, which would address the maintenance needs of the air force and EL Al aircraft. Yet again Al rose to the Zionist challenge. He sold the plant and left the US for Israel. His goal: to establish what was to become one of the country’s finest industries, and the jewel in the crown of the security industry – Israel Aircraft Industries. 30 years later, when he resigned from his position as CEO, Israel Aircraft Industries was the largest plant in Israel, with a payroll of more than 20 thousand employees. The industry became Israel’s no. one exporter. His personality, humility, leadership and exceptional management remain imprinted at Israel Aircraft Industries until this very day. Even after retiring, Al did not cease serving the country. He placed himself, his knowledge and his extensive connections around the world at the disposal of Israel whenever needed, and in some cases participated in missions which till today have not been publicized. On his last day in the White House, Bill Clinton granted amnesty to Al Schwimmer and annulled his conviction. This, despite the fact that Al refused to express penitence or remorse for what he had done. His strong stance, obliteration of his personal benefit and clear commitment to his nation, are exemplary. Until today, Al contributes and helps in enhancing the ties between Israel and the Diaspora, and continues to see himself as an emissary of the Zionist concept with all his might and all his soul.

For his activity, his courage and his leadership Al has won numerous prizes and citations. But in his eyes the most important of all is a special award of recognition, awarded to him by the David Ben-Gurion Foundation (2003), 30 years after the death of the founder of the State of Israel. In his letters Ben-Gurion cited Al Schwimmer’s role in establishing the country: “Out of all the support we have received, the most important asset which US Jewry gave us at the time was Al Schwimmer”. [edit] Gene Hart

Gene Hart (June 28, 1931 - July 14, 1999) was an American Professional Sports Announcer for the Philadelphia Flyers of the Nation Hockey League and the Philadelphia Phantoms of the American Hockey League.

Hart was born in New York and soon moved to Southern New Jersey, where he graduated Pleasantville High School. He then graduated Trenton State College with a Bachelor of Arts degree in Education.

After serving time in the Military, Hart began officiating high school football, baseball, and basketball in South Jersey. After one game at Atlantic City High School, the school’s athletic broadcaster Ralph Glenn was walking around frantically to find a person to go with him to Trenton to announce a game. He explained his situation to Hart, and Hart agreed to go with him. Thus began one of the most successful announcing careers in hockey history.

Hart continued to announce with Glenn on a regular basis in Southern New Jersey, and keep several side jobs as well. When Philadelphia was granted and NHL expansion team in 1967, Hart submitted his tapes to the team, which would be called the Flyers. Since the Flyers could not afford one of the more experienced Canadian announcers, Hart got the job.

He figured he would only be on the staff for a few years until the Flyers could afford a better broadcaster. He was mistaken. Gene Hart was the voice of the Philadelphia Flyers for 29 years, from the team’s inception until the end of the 1994-1995 season. Hart announced more then 2,000 NHL games, 6 separate Stanley Cups, 5 NHL all-star games, and the NHL Soviet Union all-star series.

He came out of retirement in 1997 to announce for the Philadelphia Phantoms, the Flyers’ minor league affiliate. He announced the team’s Caulder Cup championship series before retiring at the end of the 1999 season.

Gene Hart passed away from a variety of illnesses on July 14, 1999. He was 68 years old.

The Great Burn was a catastrophic nuclear war on Earth in the fictional Babylon 5 universe which took place in the year 2762, 500 years after the founding of the Interstellar Alliance. The nuclear exchange was the culmination of a growing political divide among the human inhabitants of Earth, with one side favoring Earth adopting an increasingly isolationist position in interstellar politics, as well as an authoritarian, Orwellian, domestic policy. The opposing side in the conflict favored continued strong relations with the Interstellar Alliance and a more liberal society that is accepting of alien cultures. The widespread devastation of the nuclear exchange killed most human life on the planet, and left the surviving civilization in a state of pre-industrial technological development which took several centuries to recover from.

[edit] Rob Smith (musician)

Rob Smith is a singer/songwriter from Dublin, Ireland.


Rob Smith is a singer/songwriter and musician from Dublin, Ireland. He was once the drummer of a Beatles-influenced quintet called The Gears. They parted in 2000. He decided to spend his time writing solo material. He did not play his first proper solo gig until 2004. He is best known for choosing not to release any material "until my following is in the hundreds, if not thousands. Until then there's no point" he once said.

He has compiled a collection of rarities and demos and calls it "The Soul Shaker L.P.". These CD's are sometimes given out either for free or for 1euro or 2euro. In December 2005 a copy of this demo went onsale on eBay and sold for UK£24.33 with eleven people bidding.

He has regularly toured with the likes of Aslan, The Stone Roses Experience to name but a few and has played venues such as Olympia Theatre, Vicar Street, The Village, Castlebar Royal Theatre, Dolans Limerick, Belfast Limelight, Glasgow Garage, Barcelona Las Ramblas amongst many others. In June 2006 he is set to play his first ever UK headline show at Liverpool's Cavern Club.
