[edit] Zombie Wars (Role-playing Game)

Zombie Wars is a new and independant roleplaying game or RPG developed by Lazy Bum Entertainment in April 2006. It consists mainly of the moderator and founder BaronessStormrage (an ambiguously gendered person who generally sees to the everyday play of Zombie Wars and keeps the world balanced, throwing in occasional weather, NPCs, or nerfs) and ShotgunNinja, one of the founding members.

Zombie Wars is a fledgling game, and is currently working through its open-alpha testing, in an attempt to find bugs. Current traffic is relatively low, compared to other text-based RPGs, but its community is steadily growing. Zombie Warsis free to play, supported by pop-up adds and banner hosting, and is guaranteed to contain no spyware or adware. Currently, there are no plans for any monentary fee.

Gameplay is divided into short sessions, called "drafts" which last until one faction stands victorious. At the end of each draft, all players are asked to either play the exact same character, or are freely allowed to create a new one from scratch and the war begins again.

Currently, there is only one reality, or realm included in gameplay, but with the addition of more GMs and moderators, there may be more added.

[edit] History

As this is a very young game, there isn't much of a history to speak of. The game actually began as a joke among friends about who among them would survive a zombie attack. This led the group to start forming their original "zombie chats" where they would use an IRC client or an instant messaging chatroom to host minirole-play sessions, with players divided up into zombies. These gradually evolved into the game that we have today. The gameplay was originally very abstract, but evolved into the d20 system used under the Open Game License. Even the classes have been greatly revamped, and although they began with less than 10, at the time of writing, they sport upwards of 50 classes, and add more whenever the heads, John Phillips and David Dong, decide to add more.

[edit] Gameplay

[edit] Overview

Unlike most text-based RPGs, Zombie Wars uses sort of a distorted time-scale. At points where the GM considers play to be too monotonous, e.g., travel, the GMs will increase the pace of gameplay. However, during massive battles where some members aren't on, a few minutes of in-game time can stretch out for hours. The pace depends strongly on the players and the GM. For this reason, the staff encourages members to log in frequently.

Upon creating an account, players are given a choice of Faction (Human, Hellspawn, Zombie) and a class (far too numerous to be listed). Each Class and Faction has its own strengths and weaknesses; some have access to unique spells, some have bonuses to offense or resource production, and so on. Different races also have a different number of bonus lives or "respawns".

[edit] Strategy

Essentially, gameplay revolves around defeating all other factions and establishing your own faction as the dominant one on earth. However, the players have been known to attempt backstabbing, and many a zombie has had his brains eaten by his own ally. The path to world domination can be taken in many senses, such as subjugating the others, seducing them into joining your side, gathering magic artifacts, or even just butchering every one that you see (especially useful when the other side only has a few hundred on earth).

[edit] Geography

The entire Zombie Wars campaign takes place on a post-apocalyptic earth where a supernatural disease has been killing humans and turning them into zombies. The hellspawns have come from, well, Hell, but plans have not been made to begin play in this area.

[edit] History

Allen East High School is a four-year college preparatory high school in Allen County, Ohio. It was formed in the autumn of 1965 with the consolidation of the two eastern Allen County School Districts: Lafayette-Jackson and Auglaize-Local. It is the newest school in the county and is made up of a largely rural population.

The high school (grades 9-12) has, on average, between 270-300 students. The district is beginning to see a sharp rise in population due to the construction of its new buildings. The kindergarten class of 2006-2007 is the largest in the history of the school, with over 150 children.

As with many schools in Northwestern Ohio, Allen East is constructing new school facilities to accomodate its growing population. Its outdated facilities, such as the current high school (constructed in 1931 as Lafayette High School), will be torn down. The new school will open for the 2007-2008 school year.


[edit] Athletics

Allen East has earned a reputation in its area for having consistently well-performing sports teams. Despite only winning one state team title in its history, the athletic department at Allen East boasts numerous state appearances, regional, sectional, and district titles. Their mascot is the mustang and their official school colors are Royal Blue and White. They belong to the Northwest Conference, the oldest conference in the Allen County area. Other member schools are Ada, Bluffton, Columbus Grove, Convoy Crestview, Delphos Jefferson, Lima Central Catholic, Van Wert Lincolnview, Paulding, and Spencerville. Their historical rivals are the Ada Bulldogs, Lima Bath Wildcats, Delphos Jefferson Wildcats, and the Spencerville Bearcats.

Notable sports moments: 1981 Boys Cross Country State Title, Football Division IV State Semi-Finalists, Girls Basketball Division III State Runners-Up, 7 Individual Track & Field State Titles.

[edit] Extra-Curricular Activities

Although noted for its athletic prowess throughout the years, Allen East High School is renowned throughout the state as a leader in the performing and visual arts. The Allen Mustang Marching Brass, led by director Dennis Dellifield, has garnered a national reputation as being one of the premier show bands in the country. They were the Grand Champions of the 2002 Peach Bowl Parade. The instrumental music program at AE has seen as many as 70% of its student body participating in any given year. The choral program is also noted for its originality and talent.

[edit] Michelle Caruso-Cabrera

Michelle Caruso-Cabrera is a co-anchor of Worldwide Exchange (Monday-Friday, 4-6am ET), CNBC's first global daily business news program utilizing the resources of CNBC, CNBC Europe, and CNBC Asia. Prior to Worldwide Exchange, she anchored CNBC's Wake Up Call.

In 2005, Caruso-Cabrera reported for NBC News from Baghdad, covering the Iraqi elections and its impact on Iraq's post-war economy and has also traveled to Cuba, Brazil, and Venezuela to report on and produce groundbreaking stories about the economics of those countries.

Caruso-Cabrera joined CNBC in 1998 from CBS affiliate WTSP-TV in St. Petersburg, Florida, where she spent 4 years as a general assignment reporter. Prior to joining WTSP-TV, she was a special projects producer for Univision, where she won an Emmy Award for a five-part series on children with AIDS, as well as an Emmy nomination for a report on sexual abuse by clergymen. At Univision, she gained extensive experience covering Latin America. She began her journalism career in 1991 as a stringer for The New York Times, reporting on education issues.

Caruso-Cabrera writes a personal finance column for Shape en Espanol, focusing on issues of particular importance to Latinas. Previously, she was a monthly contributor to People en Espanol.

Caruso-Cabrera has also been awarded Broadcaster of the Year from the National Association of Hispanic Journalists and was named one of the "100 Most Influential Hispanics" in the country by Hispanic Business Magazine. She earned a bachelor's degree in economics from Wellesley College.

[edit] The Mary Ellis Grave Site

Mary Ellis has resided on what she probably thought would always be a stately piece of property overlooking the scenic Raritan River for the past 170 years. In actuality though, Mary has been had anything but a peaceful rest. Mary came to rest beneath the asphalt of the Loews Theater parking lot on Route 1 in New Brunswick in a romantic, and ultimately tragic story.

Mary Ellis came to New Brunswick in the 1790's to stay with her younger sister Margaret, as the legend goes. It was around this time that she met and fell in love with a man who was a sea captain, and former Revolutionary War officer. The Captain sailed down the Raritan and out to sea one day, vowing that when he returned he and Mary would be wed. He even left her his beloved horse to look after in his absence. Every day after her captain's departure, Mary would ride his horse from her sister's house, on what is now Livingston Avenue, down to the banks of the river to eagerly await a glimpse of her lover's returning ship.

In 1813, she purchased a parcel of farmland overlooking the river from which she would maintain her daily vigil. Mary died there fourteen years later in 1827, still faithfully anticipating her captain's return. She was buried on the property, along with her sister, some other family members, and according to local lore, the captain's horse.

The plot, which was originally surrounded by an ornate wrought iron fence, lay in a rural, wooded setting until the twentieth century. Over the years Mary's farm was paved over, and for many years was the site of the Route 1 Flea Market. Stranded high and dry in the market parking lot, Mary’s four-foot-high grave remained adrift in a sea of tarmac and bargain hunter-mobiles. Until recently, the small, grassy island was enclosed by a chain-link fence and sporadically maintained by Mary's descendants. Often though, the weeds on the plot were so tall that one could park right next to the grave without even noticing the two and a half foot tall marble head stone.

Those who did take note of the parking lot grave have always been curious about, and sometimes even inspired by it. It’s been alleged that the early 1970’s pop hit "Brandy," was really an ode to Mary. The Looking Glass, the band that wrote and recorded the song, were in fact from New Brunswick, and did record other songs based on local sites. The band's bassist, Pieter Sweval was a brother of Phi Sigma Kappa at Rutgers. "Brandy," the fine girl, according to the song, was in love with a ship captain who could not leave his true love, the sea, to marry her. Sound familiar?

After the demolition of the Route 1 Flea Market a few years ago the property was redeveloped and became the Loews Cineplex. The parking lot was re-graded, making Mary’s grave stand even taller than before. Still parked in a prime spot, Mary’s grave has been given a new retaining wall, and a few small trees have even been planted close by. The final resting-place of Mary Ellis seems to have been given a new lease for now.

[edit] Northern Bettong

The Northern Bettong is a small potoroid which is restricted to some areas of mixed open Eucalyptus woodland and Allocasuarina forest bordering rainforest in far northeastern Queensland, Australia.


The Bettong is a solitray animal and is Nocturnal. They spend the day in a well concealed nest constructed beneath either a grass tree within a clump of grass or from other litter collected at ground level. Nesting material is carried using their prehensile tail. Ectomycorrhizal fungal sporocarps are the staple diet of the northern bettong. These are dug from beneath the soil at the bases of trees, and work to date suggests that substantial nightly movements are often required to detect this patchily distributed food source. Other foods in the diet include grass roots and tubers, lilies, herbs and sedges. Northern bettongs appear to breed all year round, and like other kangaroos the young is carried in a pouch until it is old enough to follow the mother as a 'young-at-foot'.


The Northern Bettong is an endangered animal, and only populates three regions ofQueensland, Austalia. All withtin 80 miles of eachother.

[edit] Classroom Performance Systems (CPS)

Classroom Performance Systems (CPS) is a technological way to assess students. The instructor is able to ask objective and subjective questions to each student. The questions come from a computer bank, and are displayed for each student to view. Each student can answer their test questions at their own pace and respond with a remote control devices. A radio or infrared transmitter picks up the students response and applies it to the appropriate remote control device that answered the question. The responses are tallied by a computer and provide detailed reports. This wireless system allows instructors to obtain immediate feedback from each individual student. The system keeps a log of every created class session, so the student records are always available.

[edit] Multiple Description Coding

Multiple Description Coding is a coding technique which codes a single media stream into minimally two independent descriptions. Any, or an arbitrary combination, of these descriptions on the decoder side are used to reconstruct the media stream. Quality improves with the number of descriptions correctly received. Its main advantage, over real-time single description (SD) video coding, is its robustness to errors in an unreliable network.

[edit] Father Renzo Mancin

Father Renzo Mancin is the The Chief Scout of Ethiopia.

The Ethiopia Scout Association is the national Scouting association of Ethiopia. The Association was re-established in 2002 after being inactive since 1999. Due largely to the efforts of Father Mancin.

[edit] History of The Ethiopia Scout Association

The Scout movement was first introduced in Ethiopia circa 1933, however the association was forced to terminate its activities due to the Second Italo-Abyssinian War of 1935-1936. Scouting was revived in Ethiopia in 1948, when Scout activities gained a foothold in the schools of Ethiopia. Ethiopia was recognized as a member of the World Organization of the Scout Movement in 1969.

Ethiopia is also a former member of WAGGGS, last mentioned in 1984, now part of the Ethiopia Scout Association.

Ethiopian Scouts assisted during the nation's droughts and famines in the 1970s and 1980s, as well as literacy campaigns and other community services. In the 1970s, Ethiopian Scouts established a new training and camping center at Lake Langano. The Ethiopia Scout Association enxperienced its second demise in the early 1970s, when the marxist military regime Derg dissolved the association and confiscated its properties and funds, under the accusation that the values of faith enshrined in the Scout promises were incompatible with the philosophy pursued by the new regime.

After twenty years of effort, with the emergence of the democratic system, and in accordance with the proclamation number 512/59, the Ethiopia Scout Association was re-established in 1995. The 35th World Scout Conference, convened in Durban, South Africa July 26-30, 1999, voted to remove Ethiopia from WOSM membership because the national Scout organization had ceased to exist (although earlier that year an Ethiopian contingent had attended the World Scout Jamboree in Chile). The Ethiopia Scout Association was readmitted to the WOSM at the World Scout Conference in Greece in July, 2002, the result of ten years effort by Father Renzo Mancin, the Chief Scout of Ethiopia, commissioners and other members of the Ethiopia Scout Association, who worked to get the association recognized again at the world level. Soon after this most recent recognition by WOSM, with the support of the Africa Regional Office, the Ethiopia Scout Association organized a number of training courses, and conducted recruitment drives and public relation activities.

Presently there are 3,650 Scouts (as of 2004), compared to 9,829 in 1972.

The mission of scouting is to contribute to the education of the young people through a value system based on the scout promise and scout law, to help build a better world where people are self-fulfilled as individuals and play a constructive role in the society.

[edit] Subaudible Tone

A subaudible tone is a tone that is used to fire an automated event at a radio station. A subaudible tone is not inaudible; however, it is usually at a volume level that is not noticable to the average listener at normal volume levels. These tones are included in the audible main portion of audio in the case of satellite; on tape, these often are filtered. Normally, subaudible tones are at one of the following frequencies: 25, 35, 50, 75 Hertz(HZ) or combinations of those frequencies. Until computerized radio automation became inexpensive and common, 25 and 35 HZ were used either in the audio stream or on a special channel (in the case of "carts") on the tape to indicate to a radio station's automation system that it was time to fire another event; with the advent of computers and digital satellite, these tones are relegated to triggering commerical announcements and legal ID's on a dwindling number of radio networks, as tones in the audio have been supplanted by relay closures sent independent of audio on digital satellites (Starguide being a prominent system).

[edit] ICMP tunnel

"ICMP tunnel" is a way to establish connection between two remote computers (named client and proxy) using only ICMP echo request and reply packets. Typical example of this technique would be tunneling complete TCP traffic over ping requests and replies.

[edit] Technical Details

Tunneling is based on arbitrary data that could be injected into the echo packet and sent to the remote computer. Remote computer could reply in the same manner, injecting his answer into another ICMP packet and sending it back. The client performs all communication using ICMP echo request packets, while the proxy use echo reply packets. In theory, it is possible to have the proxy also use echo request packets (and make implementation much easier), but these packets are not necessarily forwarded to the client as client could be behind translated address (NAT). This bidirectional data flow can be abstracted with ordinary serial line.

Tunneling is often used ilegally, to bypass firewalls which do not block icmp packets, or establish hard to trace, encrypted communication channel between two computers without direct network interaction.
