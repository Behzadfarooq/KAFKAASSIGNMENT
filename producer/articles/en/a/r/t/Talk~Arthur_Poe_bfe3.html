Where do we learn that Mr. Poe's first name is Arthur? If we learn it in the movie, it should probably not be considered canon. 69.138.229.246 03:39, 23 January 2006 (UTC)

It was in Lemony Snicket: The Unauthorized Autobiography, which featured a transcript of him having a conversation with his sister. She called him "Arthur". Squidward2602 14:39, 26 March 2006 (UTC)

[edit] Associate of Count Olaf

Could someone give me the page number of the dialogue that this is based on? Does the "end of the End" refer to Chapter Fourteen?
However, in the end of The End, it is revealed that Mr. Poe is/was an associate of Count Olaf, after a verbal parley Olaf had with Ishmael. Clamster5 00:08, 9 November 2006 (UTC)

I'm pretty sure that that's just a misinterpretation of Olaf's mention of a person named Mr. Poe having some adoption papers hidden under their hat. The way it's spoken makes Olaf's relationship with this Poe utterly ambiguous, so I'm changing that. Later addition: I see several allusions to the "Poe is evil" theory throughout this article; perhaps they could all be collected together in one place, although I'm fairly confident that the only evidence for the idea is that he isn't very helpful (in which case, why didn't he hand over the Baudelaires to Olaf and Fernald in TRR?). Plus Olaf specifically says in TPP that Poe isn't one of his associates - or rather, he doesn't disagree with the statement when the Baudelaires make it, merely saying that Poe (among others) might as well be.
