Supporting Game Music and Musicians Everywhere



The Introduction

Sakari is a music web site dedicated mostly to the genre of video game music. Musicians that write game music from around the world are always welcomed to submit their original music to the site, chat in the forums, and become Local Musicians to the site.

The History

Sakari is a Japanese word that, in essence, means “at one’s peak”, or “at one’s best”. This name was chosen because the content would be no less than the best someone could produce at that very moment.

Sakari has been around for longer than most people realize. Sakari first started as a personal web site to one David Schooley. He posted original and remixed songs onto the site, free for anyone to download. This initial site was built in 1999. Later, in 2001, it developed into a much larger site and was registered under the domain www.sakari.org, which contained 200+ arrangements from Final Fantasy, Parasite Eve, and other popular video games.

The site hit its peak in the winter of 2004 with over one thousand members and more than 400 combined arranged and original songs. However, shortly after, Mr. Schooley closed down the site due to lack of funding (as there were never any ads on the site), and lack of time to put towards the site (as he was the only staff member). The site traffic slowed, the forum posts became fewer and fewer, and eventually the domain expired. Shortly afterwards, the domain was purchased by DotaCom Corporation, who purchased it simply to own it.

In the fall of 2005, whispers about Sakari started making their way around the gaming and game making worlds. Since the original domain, sakari.org, was no longer available, a new and creative domain was registered for Sakari. The site would also no longer be a simple site containing video game arrangements; it would be one that contained original music. The music would be free to anyone, and it would come from individual composers, not companies. The new domain is http://www.sakari-infinity.net, and it has become a home for many amateur game music composers.

The Concept

On their own, amateur composers can produce entire CDs of music, and even become THE composer for independent game making groups / communities. However, that is where it usually stops... Their names are obscured, their music is lost, and they often-times fade away.

What were to happen, though, if there existed an entire community of game musicians? What if a web site devoted itself to hosting and showcasing their music, and for free no less? This is what Sakari has become. Any registered member of the Sakari site can upload their music to the site for review. Once a member of the Sakari staff reviews the music, it is added to the site and categorized.

The Members

Sakari prides itself on its members. Among the names in the forums and on the site are many unknown musicians, and some more well-known musicians. Musicians from other forums, where they were one of possibly 2 or 3 total, have drifted eventually over to Sakari where they have found themselves in the presence of many more peers who are also musicians. Even though not all the members are musicians, everyone there appreciates the music of their peers.

The Staff

The staff members at Sakari are all musicians by background and training, and have the musical and technical know-how to give flowering musicians the information and feedback they need to become better composers.

As of the posting date of this article, there are 3 Staff members at Sakari. They include the owner and administrator: David Schooley (illustrationism), and friends and musicians Joseph Aleman (ominouschaos) and Dave O’Boyle (SickAndWretched).

[edit] The Events

Sakari holds monthly music competitions. Typically, on the first of the month, a theme and rules are given and submissions are accepted. Submissions are only accepted through the 24th of that month. Pieces are judged, scored, and critiqued; then results are returned and awards are given. The awards, rules, themes, and submission dates vary from month to month, but usually stay similar. These competitions have become popular, drawing in sometimes a dozen interested musicians.

[edit] The End?

Nope. Sakari has been back for a year, to date (8/19/06), and it’s still growing rapidly. Musicians, gamers, game makers, and music appreciators have all started hearing about the site, and even a few professional musicians have started taking interest in the site... Who knows what the future will bring, but for now we can all enjoy the myriad of music that we can find on this site.

[edit] jMusic

This request for creation has been reviewed. The result is below.


jMusic is a music programming library written in the Java programming langauge. jMusic was written by Andrew Sorensen and Andrew Brown and released publicly in November 1998. It is designed to assist composers and music software developers by providing support for music data structures, modifications, and input/output to various file formats. jMusic has a data structure that is based on a musical score metaphor, and consists of a hierarchy of notes, phrases, parts and score. jMusic also has a sound synthesis architecture and "instruments" can be created from a chain of "audio objects" (similar to unit generators in other languages). A jMusic score can be rendered with jMusic instruments to an audio file. More information and extensive tutorials on jMusic are available at the jMusic web site http://jmusic.ci.qut.edu.au.

The Pittsburgh Black Sox are a semi-professional baseball club of the National Adult Baseball Association (NABA)—competing in the Pittsburgh Chapter of that organization.

[edit] History

[edit] 2003

The Pittsburgh Black Sox were founded in 2003 under the name Pittsburgh Stampeders as part of the humble, four-team beginnings of the NABA Pittsburgh Chapter. After a brief regular season, all four teams were seeded based upon their regular-season records and put into a single-elimination playoff bracket. The Stampeders were eliminated in the first-round though against the Pittsburgh Maulers (baseball club)—the last time that the team would compete under the name Stampeders.

[edit] 2004

In 2004, the Black Sox underwent an ownership, managerial, and name change when Kenny Rayl took over the team as owner-manager and remained on as a player. The team made a sparkling debut under their new moniker in '04 as the NABA Pittsburgh Chapter doubled in size to include eight teams. Again, all of the teams were included in the playoffs and seeded based on their regular-season records; however, only the four worst regular-season teams did not receive a first-round bye. The B-Sox, as they came to be known, were not one of these teams and thus waited in the quarterfinals for an opponent. That opponent turned-out to be the Pittsburgh Ducks, but the rested B-Sox proved to be too much for the tired Pittsburgh Ducks|Ducks, winning to move-on to the semifinals against another team beneficial of a first-round bye—the Pittsburgh Yankees. The Sox were winners over the Yankess as well, and advanced to a championship showdown against the Pittsburgh Warriors, fresh off of a semifinal win against the Pittsburgh Rangers after receiving byes in the first two playoff rounds as the top seed. A thrilling championship game saw the Black Sox squeak-out a win over the Warriors as late-season acquisition and centerfielder Dan “The Man” Stell scored the winning run to clinch the league title.

[edit] 2005

2005 saw the NABA Pittsburgh Chapter grow to include nine teams and switch to a two-division format in which the champions of the league’s regular season divisions were awarded first-round byes in the playoffs. The league also disqualified the three worst teams from the playoffs that year and switched to a best-of-three series format for the semifinals and finals of those playoffs. The B-Sox, winners of the league’s Monongahela Division, put that valuable rest from their bye to good use in the semifinals in grinding-out a gutsy, one-run, come-from-behind win over the Pittsburgh Rangers in Game 1 and a two-run, extra-inning win over the Rangers in Game 2. The sweep of the Rangers allowed the B-Sox to advance again to the league's championship round, which again pitted the Sox against the Pittsburgh Warriors, winners of the league’s Allegheny Division. But this time, the Sox would need to win twice in the championship round to defend their title. However, they were unceremoniously swept two games to none by the Warriors, who more-than-adequately avenged their championship defeat from the previous year.

[edit] 2006

2006 marked some significant changes in the NABA Pittsburgh Chapter. First, although the league remained at nine teams, it switched-back to a single-division format. Second, and most importantly, in an attempt to gain more legitimacy to the league’s competition with other Pittsburgh-area semi-pro baseball organizations, the NABA Pittsburgh Chapter switched from allowing aluminum bats to allowing only wooden ones in 2006. Seemingly no team was hurt more than the powerful B-Sox, who could no longer rely on a high-powered offense to win games when their regular starting pitchers were off. As a result, the team limped to a sluggish start in '06, and only a key, late-season stretch of winning baseball got the Sox into the playoffs. In fact, it took a gutsy and vital performance in their second-to-last regular-season game of the year that clinched the sixth and final playoff spot for the Sox as again the league’s three worst teams were excused from playoff competition. Even though the league had gone back to a single-division format in '06, the two top-finishing teams from the regular season were again given first-round byes in the playoffs. The Sox, as the lowest seed though, were not beneficiaries of a bye, and needed to survive a tough, single-elimination, first-round 7-4 win over the Pittsburgh Bulldogs in order to reach the semifinals. Then, in what has become somewhat of a tradition, the Sox would need to get by the rival Pittsburgh Warriors if they hoped to win another championship. Well-rested and owners of the league’s best regular season record, the Warriors were favored to beat the upstart Sox, but they were swept two games to none in the semis, as the Black Sox continued their aggressive pursuit of a second league title.

The Sox now await a best-of-three championship series against the Robert Morris University Colonials, slated to begin with Game 1 on Thursday, August 24 @ 8:30 p.m. @ Moore Park in the South Hills of Pittsburgh. Game 2 and Game 3 (if necessary) are slated for Sunday, August 27 @ 5 and 8 p.m. @ John Herb Field in the North Hills of Pittsburgh. The Sox are vying to become the lowest playoff seed in NABA Pittsburgh Chapter history to win a league championship after already becoming the lowest seed to even reach the championship round.

A re-evaluation of the comedogenicity concept

Zoe Diana Draelos MDa, b, Corresponding Author Contact Information, E-mail The Corresponding Author and Joseph C. DiNardo MSc

aFrom the Department of Dermatology, Wake Forest University School of Medicine, Winston-Salem bDermatology Consulting Services, High Point cPharma Cosmetix Research LLC, Richmond

Background

Comedogenicity is an important consideration in the development of topical medications, cosmetics, and skin care products. The concept of “acne cosmetica” was developed to link the use of certain ingredients to comedo formation. Animal models were originally used to determine the comedogenic potential of raw materials with the assumption that finished formulations containing these ingredients would also be comedogenic. Based on this assumption, dermatologists were presented with lists of substances to avoid in patients with the ability to develop comedones. Objective

We sought to use a modification of the Mills and Kligman human assay for assessing comedogenic potential of finished cosmetic products. Methods

Six individuals with prominent follicular orifices and the ability to form comedones on the upper aspect of the back were enrolled. Each person received patches to the upper aspect of the back saturated with 0.2 to 0.5 mL of the finished cosmetic study products 3 times weekly for 4 weeks. Cyanoacrylate biopsies were performed to determine the number of follicles and microcomedones per square inch. Limitations

Only a finite number of finished cosmetic products could be analyzed. Conclusion

Finished products using comedogenic ingredients are not necessarily comedogenic.

All the important culturable prawns belong to the genus Macrobrachium which is largest genus of the family Palaemonidae. About 200 species have been recorded from this genus from different parts of the world of which 25 inhabit from Indian waters. Some of the important candidate species for Indian freshwater aquaculture are Macrobrachium rosenbergii, M. malcolmsonii, M .choprai and M. idella. However, M. rosenbergii, the giant freshwater prawn is the most important among them due to its faster growth and larger size. It is commonly known as “Scampi”.

For example, animals of genus Macrobrachium are referred to as freshwater prawns in Australia and freshwater shrimp in United States of America. Having various controversies in the use of these terms, the FAO (1999) defined separately on the basis of their habitat. “Prawns” are those which grown in freshwater, and shrimps which grown in marine and brackishwater environments.

    HABITS AND HABITAT

    M. rosenbergii inhabits the tropical freshwater environments connected directly or indirectly to the sea. It is also encountered in estuaries and sometimes in seas. M. rosenbergii lives in extremely turbid conditions in nature. This prawn is nocturnal bottom dwelling and sluggish in nature. During day-time, they protect themselves from sunlight and remain half buried in the sediment. They prefer shallow regions, which are rich in aquatic vegetation and organic detritus. Larvae of M. rosenbergii are planktonic Juveniles and adults are benthic.

    IDENTIFICATION

    In M. rosenbergii males, the second pair of pereopods are thicker than the other species. It has large carpus of pereopod than merus. M. rosenbergii has long rostrum slightly bent upward (rostrum formula: 11-14/8-10) and the tip is slightly pinkish in colour upto subadult stage. Telson of this species extends upto uropod end. Distinct black bands found on the dorsal side at the junction of all the abdominal segments. Juveniles of this species can be identified by the continuous longitudinal dark bands on the carapace and transverse on each segment of abdomen.     DISTINGUISHING CHARACTERS OF MALE AND FEMALE

    CHARACTER MALE FEMALE Size: Males grow much larger with proportionately larger head than female. Females are smaller in size with a small head. Claws: Second pereopod very long, robust, spinous claws or chelipeds. Much smaller and slender claws than males. Gonopore: A pair of gonopores are present at the base of coxal segments of the 5th pair of walking legs which are covered with a thin membrane.. A pair of gonopores are present at the base of coxal segments of the 3rd pair of walking legs which are covered with thin membrane. Appendix masculina: It is present at the junction between the appendices internae and the endopods of the second pair of pleopods Appendix masculina is absent. Only appendices internae is present. Abdominal pleura: In mature males, the ventral surface of the first abdominal pleura becomes slightly pointed centrally. In mature females, the 1-3 abdominal pleura elongated and widen to form a brood chamber. Reproductive setae: Absent Special reproductive setae appear on the thorax and pleopod of maturing female.

    Food and feeding Adults are omnivorous and consume algae, aquatic plants, mollusks, aquatic insects, worms and other crustaceans. Larvae are zooplanktivorous and their food includes minute crustaceans, small worms, etc. When starved, the giant prawns can become cannibalistic. Age and Growth The life span of M. rosenbergii is 2-3 years. The maximum size recorded is 326 mm (males) and 283 mm (females). Growth occurs through moulting (ecdysis) i. e. shedding of the exoskeleton (the outer hard covering or cuticle). Growth is found to be rapid in males. Normally, these prawns grow to about 50 g under culture conditions in 6-7 months.

    REPRODUCTION Maturation and Fecundity Giant prawn becomes capable of reproduction from the age of 5 to 6 months. The minimum size at maturity for M. rosenbergii is 175 mm (35 g) for males and 150 mm (25 g) for females. Eggs are oval and 0.6 – 0.7 mm in diameter along the long axis. Fecundity is less for ‘first broods’ ranging from 5000-100000 eggs depending on its size and condition. Females lay eggs 3 to 4 times a year. Normally it breeds throughout the year with a peak at the beginning of rainy season. Sexual dimorphism Sexes are separate in M.rosenbergii they can be identified by the following way, Male Male develops second pair of pereopods with prominent spines. Male gonopores are situated at the base of the fifth pair of pereopods and are covered by flaps. Male has bigger head than female. The ventral side of the first abdominal somite has central lump or point. Mature males are of three different morphotypes, i.e. blue claw (BC) which are relatively larger and possess long blue claws covered with prominent spines with high claw to body length ratio, orange claw (OC) males which possess medium sized, spine less and often orange coloured claws, with lower claw to the body length ratio and small males (SM) which possess short delicate claws, often with little pigment and translucent, and have lowest claw to the body ratio. Female Sexually mature females have smaller heads than males. Gonopores appear on the coxae of the third pair of pereopods and are covered with membrane. Female exhibits a typical brood chamber, which is formed by the first, second, and third abdominal pleurae. Female Macrobrachium rosenbergii also exhibits reproductive setae on the pleopods and thorax, which are functionally distinct. The ovipositing setae which are mostly permanent, on the coxae of the last three pairs of periopods guide the eggs during spawning, and ovigerous setae, which only occur following pre-spawning moult and are used to secure the eggs to the pleopods for brooding. Reproductive system Male Male reproductive system consists of a pair of testes, a pair of vasa differentia and a pair of vasiculeae seminalis. The testis is soft white and elongated structures which fuse at their anterior ends. Each testis consists of a large number of coiled, narrow and thin walled seminiferous tubules, embedded in the connective tissue. A single layer of germinal epithelium, the cells of which undergo spermatogenesis. The products of both the testes pass through two separate coiled and narrow tubes called vasa deferens. On reaching ventrally, near the base of the 5th pair of legs each vas deferens swells into a club-shaped vesicular seminalis, into which spermatozoa are stored. Each vesicular seminalis opens to the exterior via male genital pore at the base of the coxa of the 5th pereopods.

    Female Female reproductive system consists of a pair of ovary and a pair of oviduct. The ovaries are white, compact and sickle shaped structures, touching each others at both the ends but leaving a gap in the middle. The shape and size of the ovary changes with age and season. They get greatly enlarged during breeding season and may reach upto first abdominal segment. The smaller and immature ova lie toward the center while mature towards the surface. Each ovary gives rise to a short, wide and thin walled tube, oviduct which runs vertically downwards to open outside through a female gonopores on the inner side of the coax of the 3rd pereopods.

    Mating and Spawning Mating occurs only between a soft- shelled female and a hard shelled male. Female undergoes pre-spawning moult (PSM), which stimulates the male for mating. Mating occurs after PSM and at night. The male deposits the sperm mass between the 3rd to 5th pereopods of the female. Females lay eggs 8-12 hours after mating. The eggs get fertilized while they come out of the female gonopores. These eggs are then directed towards the brood pouch by the peculiar movement of the ovipositing setae. Finally, the eggs get attached to the ovigerous setae present in the brood chamber and are continuously aerated by the female through fanning of pleopods. Eggs hatch in 15 -25 days depending on water temperature. The hatching process is completed in 1 or 2 nights with the newly hatched larvae being dispersed into the water by vigorous movement of pleopods.

    Life cycle The berried females with different stages of egg development migrate to the estuaries, because the larvae of giant freshwater prawn invariably require brackishwater for their further development. The hatching and the larval development take place in brackishwater. The newly metamorphosed post larvae stay in brackishwater initially (for 2 to 3 weeks) and then migrate upstream. Adults inhabit freshwater bodies, mature and mate there.

    REQUIREMENTS OF GIANT FRESHWATER PRAWN HATCHERY

    1. Site selection - Large scale commercial prawn hatcheries should coastal sites having adequate supply of sea water and freshwater free form pollutants. - The site should be amenable for provision of infrastructural facilities such as availability of electricity, accessibility, etc. - Small scale, medium scale and backyard hatcheries can be established at any place where good quality of freshwater is available along with either concentrated brine solution or artificial sea water. - Broodstock should be available in the vicinity of the hatchery.

    2. Infrastructure facilities: Infrastructure facilities include earthen broodstock ponds to maintain adequate supply of brooders, water supply system, aeration system, main hatchery shed for housing broodstock holding tanks, hatching tanks, larval rearing tanks, post-larvae holding tanks, Artemia nauplii production unit, Moina culture unit, laboratory, power house, pump house, etc.

    2.1 Broodstock ponds: - Continuous supply of good quality brooders is one of the prerequisites for the successful operation of commercial hatchery. - Site for broodstock ponds should be in the vicinity of hatchery complex. - The texture of the soil should be sandy-loam or sandy-clay loam with pH 6.5 to 8.0. - The site should have perennial source of freshwater free from pollutants. - Minimum two ponds required for maintenance of broodstock.

    2.2 Water supply system The water supply system consists of storage tanks for sea water, freshwater and brackishwater (mixed water), filtration unit and water distribution system to various sections of the hatchery.

    2.2 .1. Freshwater: - Freshwater may be obtained from a tube well or from a perennial canal free from pollutants. - CaCO3 hardness should be < 50 ppm. - Fresh water storage tanks - Filter units.

    2.3 .2. Sea water: - Inland land locked states can obtain required saline water either through transportation of concentrated brine solution or artificial sea water. - Seawater storage tanks. - Salts for making artificial seawater. - Coastal hatcheries pump seawater from inter-tidal zone during high tide, through an under ground filter point.

    2.2 .3. Brackishwater (mixed water) - Mixing tanks - Supernatant water from the freshwater and seawater storage tanks is to be pumped into the mixing tanks so as to get desired salinity (14±2 ppt) with through aeration or with an agitator. - If the freshwater source is from open canal, the mixed water may be treated with bleaching powder to get 5 to 10 ppm chlorine to kill the microflora and microfauna. - After 10-12 hours of chlorination (contact period), agitate water to remove residual chlorine; If still chlorine is present treat with sodium thiosulphate to remove residual chlorine. As far as possible avoid use of sodium thiosulphate, because it is toxic to the larvae. - 1 ppm residual chlorine needs 7 ppm of sodium thiosulphate. - Add 10ppm EDTA to the dechlorinated water to settle heavy metals, if any. - Alternatively water may also be treated with ozone and probiotics.

    2.2 .4. Water supply grid: - PVC rigid pipe should be used for water supply grid. - The grid should be fixed above the ground level with proper slope for draining and cleaning as and when required. - Polypropylene control valves to be fixed at appropriate place in the grid.

    3. Aeration system: - Uninterrupted pure air supply to all the units of hatchery is very essential to meet the desired DO2 levels. - Oil- free air-blower should be used to get desired quantity of air. - Stand-by air blower is essential. - A shed for installation of air blowers. - Air supply grid should be PVC and provided with polypropylene valves at appropriate place. - The aeration grid should be fixed above the ground level with proper slope to drain condensed water and to clean as and when necessary. - 4. Power supply system: - In addition to regular electric supply, a stand by generator set is essential to operate desired machinery/ equipment. - Power hose for installation of generator. - 5. Main Hatchery Complex: - The main hatchery complex is a shed covered with asbestos cement sheets. - It consists of broodstock unit, larval rearing unit, Artemia hatching unit, postlarval rearing unit.

    5.1. Broodstock unit: - This is an asbestos roofed shed. This unit has provision to hold berried females and hatchlings of eggs of berried females. The unit is provided with freshwater, brackishwater and air supply grids of PVC all along the walls inside the shed.

    5.1.1. Berried females holding tanks: - The broodstock unit consists of various size (5.0 to 10.0 ton capacity) cement tanks/plastic pools/ FRP tanks to hold the berried females collected from the broodstock ponds. - The berried females having different stages of egg development are stocked in separate tanks i.e. the prawns with orange colour eggs, the prawns with brown colour eggs and the prawns with grey colour eggs. 5.1.2. Hatching tanks: - The size of hatching tanks varies from 250 to 500 l capacity. - Normally FRP tanks are used for hatching.

    5.2. Larval rearing unit: - Larval rearing unit plays a major role in the successful operation of prawn hatchery. - The larval rearing shed should be situated with a North-South orientation having large windows on eastern and western sides for proper ventilation. - The roof of the shed is covered with asbestos cement sheets interspersed with one translucent fibre glass sheet after every three sheets, which allows light intensity of 200 to 500 Lux. - The hatchery should have concrete flooring with proper slope and drainage facility. - The unit should be provided with freshwater, brackishwater and air supply grids of PVC all along the walls inside the shed.

    5.2.1. Larval Rearing Tanks: - Prawn larvae are reared in different types and sizes of tanks made up of cement, FRP, Ferro-cement, etc. - Circular tanks are with smooth inner surface preferred for better management. - Corners of the tank bottom should be rounded off for easy and efficient cleaning and to avoid the growth of bacteria. - Tank should have proper drainage system. - Small hatcheries need 0.25 to 1.0 ton and commercial hatcheries use 1.0 to 5.0 ton capacity tanks. - Colour of the tanks plays major role in growth and survival. - Dark colours i.e. red, black, dark grey, geen,blue etc. are preferred. - The tanks should be provided with aeration.

    6. Artemia Hatching Unit: - This is an asbestos covered shed. - Artemia hatching tanks are 50 to 500 l capacity FRP cylindro-conical jars. The conical part of the jar is translucent and the inner surface of the jar is white in colour. - The jar should have a lid. - Aeration should be provided at the centre of the cone to avoid settlement of eggs. - An outlet valve should be provided at the cone of the jar to harvest the Artemia nauplii after hatching. - Two florescent tube lights to be fixed one feet above the jar to get 1000 Lux. 7. Post-larval Rearing Unit: - This is an asbestos roofed side open shed. - It consists of 5.0 to 10.0 ton capacity cement tanks/plastic pools/FRP tanks. The size of the tanks vary depending on the production capacity of hatchery. - Aeration should be provided in all the tanks.

    8. Laboratory: - A small laboratory is required for water analysis, larval observation, wet feed preparation, etc.

    9. Equipments: Salinity refractometer, chloroscope, pH meter, thermometer, analytical balance, mixer, cooker, refrigerator, oven, hotplate, sieves, plankton nets, 100 micron bolting silk cloth, plastic wares, and glass wares pumps, etc.

    10. Other Facilities: - In addition to the above facilities, overhead tanks, store, office, staff quarters, etc. are required.

    [edit] Waksman Institute

    The Waksman Institute is a research facility located in Rutgers, New Brunswick opened in 1954. The research conducted here focuses on genetics, microbiology, and molecular biology. The institute was named after the nobel prize winning scientist Selman Waksman who discovered a vaccine for tuberculosis.

    [edit] Waksman Student Scholars Program

    The Waksman Institute hosts a high school research program called the Waksman Student Scholars Program (WSSP). Two students from each participating school are trained in molecular biology lab techniques and bioinformatics during the summer institute. During the school year, these students form a research team back at their high school and continue their research.

    This request for creation has been reviewed. The result is below.


    Omus dejeani is a species of flightless tiger beetle (Cicindelidae) that occurs from British Columbia almost to northern California in dense, coastal forest. It is the largest species of the genus, at between 15-20 mm.

    The genus Omus is placed within the tribe Omini, considered the most primitive of the tiger beetles. Omus shares Omini with the North American genera Amblycheila and the African Platychile.

    It can be found quite commonly in suitable habitat in the spring. It is most often collected with pitfall traps, but can be found walking along trails at night and on cloudy days during the right time of year.

    [edit] Hook (Album)

    This request for creation has been reviewed. The result is below.
    If your request was declined and you want to make changes and try again, copy and paste your article into a new submission and make your changes there.

    Released December 2002

    Genre Rock

    Zero's follow up to Albummed

    Review "Nice stuff, but tends to drift into elevator-music territory at times", so said this 40 something friend (who's never seen Zero live) the first time I played Hook to him. I insisted he give it a closer listen, and made him take the CD home. "Awesome stuff man! where have you been hiding these guys!" was his reaction the next day. "That CDs been playing a lot at home" was the weary reaction of his wife a couple of days later. "That guitarist! God! He reminds me of Larry Carlton!" he exclaimed a week later (To compare a guitarist to Larry Carlton is just about the greatest compliment you can pay him... ever!). Two months later I still haven't gotten the CD back, and he's asked me for copies to send to his friends abroad.

    What an awesome EP Hook is. Zero boys, take a bow.

    Lets take this outside in...

    Its an EP, something they've knocked together at home, with programmed drums. No fancy production or inlay, no distribution deal. A cover designed overnight by old friend Saumi (of Cusp fame). Its something they've done to bridge the gap between their first album Albummed (of which very few copies were made) and the next album due end-2003. They've made it quick, and they've made it cheap... they're selling at an all-time low of 50 bucks. They're selling it at shows, giving it away free. They're getting they're music heard by as many rockers as they can . And everyone who hears this album is going to wait for their next album, and kill to get it.

    How better can a band go about getting itself heard?

    Hook is an 8 song evolution of Zero, the band. The best of what they've done in the last 3-4 years (36 songs, they say). It has this touch of 'lets wrap up this stage of life, put it on CD, and then move ahead', and the end result is awesome.

    Zero's music is not difficlt to relate too, matter of fact it almost too easy. Every song follows a simple and basic verse-chorus-bridge format, and has that inevitable 'hook' for the listener to latch onto.... catchy stuff that would appeal to the pop fan as well as the rocker, making them just about the only band around bridging the pop-rock divide with its own music.

    The album opener Not My Kind of Girl is the very core of the 'Zero sound'. Clean guitars, smooth vocal harmonies with just that dollop of power that Rajeev throws in, beautiful lead work, and a solid bass and drum backing to make the pieces fit. Warren's guitar tone is something to die for, and what he's done, throughout most of the album, is very cleverly overlay many guitar tracks, playing lead, rhythm, and fills, but still keep a straightforward sound. They can still play the tracks live with just one guitar and sound bang on is what I'm saying.

    The next track 'Lost' sounds different, recorded at Claver's studio for the F.O.K compilation album. Its not my favourite track on the album, but watch out for the lead solo bit.

    Spitleaf. what can I say. It starts out with a vaguely Boston-ish sound. Very late-eighties rock intro, kicking into one beautiful piece I can best describe as exhilirating. Warren once told me that the reason they don't do this song live is because it would need three of him playing... three guitar tones taking up the tune where the last left off and running with it. Beautiful melody, the soaring sound and the sustain, the clear notes ringing out, the slow-down bridge with the guitar crying, the volume control, the power-ending, the harmonized parts. Exhilirating. This song is like diving into crystal, clear and beautiful, and splitting into so many facets if one just listens carefully.

    PSP 12" is an absolute killer, one of the best songs they've done, and a number that marks a pretty significant milestone in the band's evolution. Far as I can remember its the song that started Zero on the heavier riff-based songs they're doing today. The verse is melodic, and the chorus kicks in with a punch. The By-standing-By refrain is something thats led to quite a bit of speculation. A song in which Bobby shines with his bass playing. A beautiful spoken-word bridge with Sidd overlaying a vocal line, a kickass solo and and Warren going crazy with a rare bit of speed soloing on the outro.

    Christmas in July is another instrumental number, and a Warren showcase. It is, in fact, the number that has led to more Zero popularity in the rock circle than any other number, thanks to a nod or two (possibly unintentional) to Satriani. This song is followed by Lucy, possibly their biggest number from their old bag of songs, and a song that I love to hear for the many guitar tracks layered to fill up the song. The song with the Pearl Jam-ish intro (gets many rockers at their shows on their feet screaming 'Evenflow!'), a lovely the lead part, and ending riff.

    Lijo is the third instrumental number of Hook, a light nod along number with clean guitars, a somewhat Knopfleresque sound, and just this little touch of overdriven bit thrown in for good measure. The album ends with Not My Kind of Girl reprise, a gem of a song, with the country-ish acoustic rhythm and guitar fills, and Sidd's 'crying', a neat little trick with vocals played backwards.

    Thats Hook for you, some album from some band. Now listen up. This is where I tell you to not take this album seriously. You'll kill its spirit if you try. Don't search for messages, don't wait for complexity, don't get set to headbang, don't look for a hugely unique sound. Just don't frown in concentration and wait for anything, or you'll miss the point. The point is the hook, thats all you need to catch, and from then on your journey's for you to enjoy. Feel the fun thats gone into making these songs... enjoy!

    - Sandy (Feb 2003)

    Amblycheila cylindriformis is a species of flightless tiger beetle. It is found in the Great Plains, and shows a preference for native grasslands.

    It shares its genus with at least five other species in the United States, with a few more found in Mexico. It is placed within the tribe Omini.

    Amblycheila cylindriformis can be found walking along bare ground at night, or collected in pitfall traps.

    Loophead Lighthouse is situated on the tip of the Loophead Peninsula in West Clare. It separates the Shannon Estuary from the Atlantic. There has been a lighthouse on the site since 1670. The original was a stone cottage with a metal brazier on the roof which housed a warning fire. In the last century the current tower was erected. In 1991 it was fully automated. The lighthouse is maintained by the Commissioner of Irish Lights. The adjoining buildings now form self-catering accommodation for tourists.
