[edit] Land O Lakes High School

Land O' Lakes High School is a four-year public high school in Land O' Lakes, Florida. It is part of the District School Board County in Pasco County, Florida. This is currently under construction and is a work in progress.

[edit] 1970's

Land O' Lakes High School opened its doors in 1975 to 1,400 student in grades 7 through 12 after spending two years on double sessions at Sanders Memorial Elementary while the school was under construction. Mr Leroy McClain was appointed principal. To ease crowding, Pine View Middle School was built in 1978 to house students in 6th through 8th grades.

[edit] 1980's

Mr. McClain continued to serve as principal until 1985. Mr. Albert Bashaw, who previously served as principal of Bayonet Point Middle was named principal. In January 1988, Jennifer Colhouer, a popular freshman at the school, was found murdered in her home by her 6 year old brother Jeremy.

[edit] 1990's

Mr. Bashaw contined to serve as principal until 1997, when he was named the district's director of student services. Mr. Max Ramos, former teacher and assistant principal of Land O Lakes was named principal. Additionally, he served as principal at Hudson and Pine View Middle schools. In September 1999, 17 year old Robert Crawford and 16 year old Steven Tuttle, both students at the school were shot and killed near the intersection of U.S. 41 and State Road 54 over a drug deal gone wrong. Laurence Joey Smith and Faunce Levon Pearce were arrested, tried, and convicted for their roles in the murders.

[edit] Battle of Murkhana

The Battle of Murkhana was a fictional battle in the Star WarsUniverse, that took place at the end of the Clone Wars. It is detailed at the beginning of the Labyrinth of Evil novel.

Murkhana was the capital of the Corporate Alliance, and a key objective for the Republic. Republic forces also hoped to capture Passel Argente, the leader of the Corporate Alliance.

Republic forces were commanded by Jedi Master Roan Shryne. The attack began with Republic clone troopers landing in LAAT gunships. Murkhana's atmosphere had been sprayed with laser-disrupting aerosols, forcing both sides to rely upon missiles for the early stages of battle. Some of the gunships were shot down by missiles from Hailfire droids.

The clone troopers landed and were able to clear beachheads with supporting fire from gunships and space forces. Murkhana's capital city was protected by energy shields, as well as armies of battle droids, tank droids, and mercenaries, including the elite Vibroblade Brigade.

Master Shryne led a small strike team of ARC troopers and disabled the shield generator. Republic forces then fought their way through several heavily defended causeways into the city. Chaos and confusion reigned supreme, as native Koorivar attempted to flee the city in the midst of heavy fighting between the Republic and Confederate armies. The tide soon turned in the favor of the Republic, since much of the Confederate army was made up of unreliable mercenaries who surrenderd to the clone forces.

However, in the midst of the battle, Order 66 was given, and the clones turned on their Jedi commanders. Roan Shryne, along with Bol Chatak and her apprentice, Olee Starstone. They concealed themselves among the surrendered mercenaries.

Darth Vader soon landed on the planet with an elite force of clone troopers to punish a traitorous clone who allowed Shryne to escape. Vader was challenged by Bol Chatak, who slashed one of his robot arms before she was killed by Vader.

Shryne and Starstone eventually were able to escape the planet.

In 1980, Babcock began his broadcasting career at WABC TV in New York as a guest commentator on Good Morning New York, then hosted by Spencer Christian, covering personal finance and consumer issues. He went on to work as a producer for The Wall Street Journal Report. His first stint as a staff reporter came in 1988, working in Columbus, Ohio at WBNS TV, where he covered finance, the environment and human interest stories.

From there, Babcock moved to Seattle's KIRO TV, where he did enviornmental reporting. In October of 1992, Babcock joined the Consulting Group at Smith Barney in Portland, Oregon. He works there to help individuals, trusts and corporations make informed decisions about investing their money.

[edit] pateamine A

Abbreviated as PatA. A marine natural product first isolated from the sea sponge Mycale sp. found off the coast of New Zealand that inhibits eukaryotic translation initiation. Pateamine A binds to eukaryotic initiation factor 4A and increases its ATPase and helicase activities. In cellular assays, treatment with PatA induces the formation of stress granules.

[edit] ditch lights

Ditch lights are part of a locomotive's exterior light set. They are positioned on the forward end of the locomotive, often on the hood, forward platform ("porch") or pilot beam and angled to shine to each side of the roadbed. They usually shine a steady white but may flash at crossings to provide an additional alert to cross-traffic or pedestrians.

[edit] R A Bragg

Russell Arthur Gower Bragg (born 23 April 1945, in North Sydney, Nova Scotia, Canada) is a nationally-recognized educator turned Canadian-American novelist.

R A Bragg has scribbled freelance (magazine, news service), been formally recognized provincially as a scriptwriter (1992 Government of Newfoundland Arts and Letters Award for Dramatic Script), and nationally as a master educator (1990 Marshall McLuhan Distinguished Teacher Award). Bragg has travelled widely in western, central and eastern (communist) Europe and the UK, and began notes toward his novel in Moscow in 1988.

R A Bragg's 2003 novel, By the White Book; A Far Angelus Novel, is an investigation of several questions involving such seemingly diverse areas as religion, genetic manipulation and alien technology woven into a storyline involving human, near-human and non-human characters alike.

Possibly the most important question posed in his novel as the background to the action centers on how Christianity may develop from the second or third century onward in near-total isolation from the rest of the early Christian community on the high, arid plateau of Angelus, a far planet hanging over the Milky Way Galaxy.

By the White Book; A Far Angelus Novel includes appendices describing in detail the extra-galactic setting, flora and fauna, and a short course in the Latin-based language of the inhabitants of Angelus and representative word list. Two web sites supplement the appendices.

[edit] PreOperational Maintenance

PreOperational Maintenance (POM) is a U.S. military term referring to the administrative preparation of soldiers prior to a deployment. This preparation is usually conducted by having the soldiers move through stations. Stations may include: Medical (shot records), Financial (direct deposit pay), Legal (wills, powers of attorney, life insurance), and a personnel equipment inspection.

[edit] Eftimie Murgu

(1805-1870) Born in the Banat, the son of an officer in a Habsburg military border regiment, Murgu was a true rabble-rouser, national militant, and a man in almost continuous motion and trouble from 1839 to 1848. He studied philosophy at Seghedin and law at Pest, where he completed a doctorate in 1834. He was appointed to teach philosophy at the Academia Mihaileana in Iasi, but moved to Bucuresti's St. Sava School in 1837 after conflict with Prince Mihail Sturdza. He was part of a revolutionary plot in Muntenia in 1840 (along with Dimitrie Filipescu, J. A. Vaillant, Nicolae Balcescu, and Cezar Bolliac), arrested, and expelled. Back in the Banat, he became a feared spokesman for national and social reform, and even union of Banat with Muntenia. This led to a lengthy prison term from 1845 to 1848.

Released in April 1848, Murgu picked up where he had left off. He was elected deputy to the Pest Parliament, called and presided over the Lugoj Romanian assembly in June, 1848, and tried to establish a Romanian army in the Banat. He also tried to establish links with the Muntenian revolution. He was one of the most intransigent Romanian leaders in 1848 and particularly militant on questions involving the national language. At the same time, he attempted in 1849 to reach an accord with the Magyars As a consequence of his radical activities, he was once more imprisoned, from 1849 to 1853. He returned to politics in 1861, but his popularity had peaked.

[edit] Hjelmslev's theorem

Hjelmslev's theorem is the statement that if points P, Q, R... of one and the same line are isometrically mapped to points P´, Q´, R´... of another line in the same plane, then the midpoints of the segments PP`, QQ´, RR´... also lie on one and the same line. The proof is easy, if one assumes the classification of plane isometries. If the given isometry is odd, in which case it is necessarily either a reflexion in a line or a glide-reflexion (the product of three reflexions in a line and two perpendiculars to it), then the staement is true of any points in the plane whatsoever: the midpoint of PP´ lies upon the axis of the (glide-)reflexion for any P. If the isometry is even, compose it with reflexion in line PQR to obtain an odd isometry with the same effect on P, Q, R... and apply the previous remark. The importance of the theorem lies in the fact that the proof does NOT presuppose the parallel postulate and is therefore valid in non-Euclidean geometry as well. By its help, the mapping that maps every point P of the plane to the midpoint of the segment P´P´´, where P´and P´´ are the images of P under a rotation (in either sense) by a given acute angle about a given center, is seen to be a collineation mapping the whole hyperbolic plane in a 1-1 way onto the inside of a disk, thus providing a good intuitive notion of the linear structure of the hyperbolic plane.

Robert A. Hatch, a public-relations executive who helped shepherd "Sesame Street" onto the air in 1969, died on June 16 at his home in Santa Barbara, Calif. He was 75.

A newspaperman by training, Mr. Hatch joined Carl Byoir & Associates, a major public relations firm, in 1957, eventually becoming a vice president there. In 1965, on leave from the firm, he became deputy director of public information for the Peace Corps, which was then just four years old. Returning to Byoir, Mr. Hatch became the account executive for the Children's Television Workshop in 1969. (Founded in 1968, the organization, which produces "Sesame Street" and other educational programs for children, is now known as the Sesame Workshop.) Mr. Hatch joined Children's Television Workshop as its vice president for public affairs in 1972.

Before a single episode of "Sesame Street" was broadcast, Mr. Hatch helped solicit support for the idea of television as an educational tool among parents, in Congress and though it seems surprising today, among educators themselves.

"Sesame Street" was first broadcast on Nov. 10, 1969, on PBS. Today it appears on 350 PBS stations and in 120 foreign countries.

Robert Adrian Hatch was born on Jan. 7, 1931, in Salt Lake City. He earned a bachelor's degree in political science from Utah State University in 1952 and later studied at Northwestern University's Medill School of Journalism. After service in the Air Force, he worked as an editor for Copley News Service.

Mr. Hatch's first marriage ended in divorce. Besides his brother, of Santa Barbara, he is survived by his wife, the former Linda Pollard; two children from his first marriage, Jennifer, of Upper Nyack, N.Y.; and Graham, of Lisbon; and a sister, Elaine Thomas of Sacramento.

In 1986, Mr. Hatch bought two weekly newspapers, The Lakeville Journal in Connecticut and The Millerton News in New York, which he published and edited until 1995.

[edit] back burner

The noun back burner is used as a metaphor (most often as "put on the back burner"). It is an idiom which is English in origin, and is used to emphasize reduced priority. It's origin is culinary in nature, and is simple to understand if you are familiar with the placement of cooking items on a stove. Many stoves have front and back burners; those items requiring consistent attention to should be kept on the front burner (stirred, watched for browning/melting, and the like) whereas things that can be left to simmer, boil, or otherwise do not require immediate or constant attention can be placed on the back burner. It is applicable to several situations where something has less priority than others.

Examples:

"Because of the gravity of the murder case, other cases had to be put on the back burner." - "That project was moved to the back burner until we finish with higher priorities." - "My taxes still aren't done; with April a few weeks away, I'm afraid my tennis lessons are on the back burner."

Alternatively, front burner can be used to emphasize increased priority.

Examples:

"With so many people in the community afraid, the murder case was put on the front burner." - "This project was moved to the front burner in order to meet the deadline." - "Forget tennis lessons - my taxes are on the front burner until they're done."
