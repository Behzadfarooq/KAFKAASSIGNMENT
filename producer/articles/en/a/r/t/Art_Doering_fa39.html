Arthur L. Doering, Jr. was a golfer who played in PGA Tour events from the 1930s through the 1960s, and later played on the Senior PGA Tour.

Doering was a member of the golf team and the Class of 1940 at Stanford University in Palo Alto, California. He turned professional in 1945. His best career finish in a major championship was a T-41 at the 1938 U.S. Open while he was a student a Stanford and still an amateur.

Doering earned his first PGA Tour victory at the 1951 Greater Greensboro Open.

In the early 1960s, Doering was President and head pro at the Yolo Fliers Club in Woodland, California. During his tenure, he oversaw the building of a new clubhouse.
