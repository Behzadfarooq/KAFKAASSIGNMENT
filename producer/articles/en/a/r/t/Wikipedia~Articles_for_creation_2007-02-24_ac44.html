[edit] Wilfrido Ma. Guerreo

Wilfrido Ma. Guerrero was born in Ermita, Manila in 1917. He studied at the Ateneo de Manila University, at the University of the Philippines, and briefly, at Columbia University. He wrote his first play at the age of 14, and at the age of 15, his play, "No Todo Es Risa", was produced at the Ateneo. He was later a reporter and proofreader for La Vanguardia, and drama critic for the Manila Tribune. He worked in the late 30’s and early 40’s as a scriptwriter in local films. He organized and directed an acting troupe, the Filipino Players (1941-1947). In 1947 the late Bienvenido Gonzalez, president of the University of the Philippines, appointed Guerrero assistant professor of dramatics, despite the lack of a formal degree. He became director of the UP Dramatic Club for which he produced and directed over 120 plays. He wrote and directed for radio for one whole year (1950-1951) for Purico's "Dulaan ng Buhay". In 1959 he adapted six of his plays and directed them for TV's Caltex Caravan. Guerrero has extensively observed the theater and drama schools in the US (on a UP scholarship, 1956-57) and in England (on a British Council scholarship, 1965). In 1962 he organized and directed the UP Mobile Theater. In the 19 years under his direction, the UPMT delivered no less than 2500 performances The UP Mobile Theater has been the recipient of two awards: The Citizen's Council for Mass Media trophy (1966) and the Balagtas Award (1969). Several Guerrero plays have been translated into and produced in several foreign and local dialects and several of his plays have been produced abroad: "Half an Hour in a Convent" at the Pasadena Playhouse, California "Three Rats" at the University of Kansas "Condemned" in Oahu, Hawaii "One, Two, Three" (premiere performance) at the University of Washington, Seattle "Three Rats and "Wanted: A Chaperon" at the University of Hawaii "Conflict" in Sydney, Australia.

He has been the recipient of three national awards: the Rizal Pro-Patria Award (1961), the Araw ng Maynila Award (1969), and the Republic Cultural Heritage Award (1972) He was proclaimed as a National Artist of the Philippines for Theater in 1997.

This Mighty Fire was formed in 1987 in Sheffield. The initial line-up was Moony (vocals/guitar), Wayne Wilkinson (guitar), Anthony Wilkinson (guitar), Peter Yarrow (bass) and Peter Gray (drums). The Wilkinson brothers departed in 1988, leaving a trio that became the definitive line-up. TMF contributed the track 'Ready & Waiting' to the compilation album 'The Great Sheffield CD' and attracted interest from a number of record companies before splitting in 1990. Moony went on to join the mildly successful Speedy, while Yarrow and Gray have disappeared from the music scene - Yarrow briefly appearing with local band Trigger Nation.

[edit] Life

Ido Reizan (1859-1935) was a journalist, writer, poet, and liberal activist. He was involved in Freedom and People's Rights Movement (自由民権運動, jiyu minken undo?), which appears to have forced him into a nomadic lifestyle. He was born Wada Tsuneshige into a family of samurai in Sōma Nakamura han and later married into the Idos, who were an Azabu-stationed samurai family. Reizan is his pseudonym. His wife was Ido Sumi. He graduated from Sendai Teaching College, which in the Meiji period became the Faculty of Education of Tohoku University. Reizan worked as a journalist and an editor-in-chief at various newspapers including Osaka Mainichi Shimbun, Sanyo Shimbun, and Tokyo Yokohama Shimbun. He was a prolific writer who wrote and edited 27 books, the subjects of which ranged from criminal law to Chinese poetry. (See Japanese Wikipaedia for a list of his publications.)

[edit] Mortified

Mortified is a national grassroots comedy collective offering a public excavation of teen angst artifacts-- journals, letters, lyrics, home movies, poetry, fiction, scripts, schoolwork. While its roots are as a recurring stage event that began in Los Angeles, California, it has spawned theatrical chapters in numerous cities (San Francisco, Boston, New York City, Los Angeles, Chicago), published books (Mortified: Real Words Real People Real Pathetic), frequently contributes to radio/television series like This American Life, produces videos under the banner Mortified Short Bus, and boasts an online community titled Woe and Tell. Founded by David Nadelberg, it is operated by a network of producers including Neil Katcher, Scott Lifton, Giulia Rozzi and more.

History of Spyderchat, from Don Alexander (http://spyderchat.com/phpBB2/viewtopic.php?t=38853&start=0)

The History of Spyderchat

Fall of 1999: The creator was a 17-year old kid name Nathan and he started the Board back in the fall of 1999. It was started on EZBoard. We had about 17-20 members.

January 2000: I came on in January and, once I ordered my car in February, created a site called daspyda.com which was essentially on online brochure. You can see the basic structure in the hyperlinks above - History, Specifications, etc. You can also see my feeble attempt at creating a wallpaper from officially-released first photos of the car in the Wallpaper section of the Downloads Page.


April 2000: Once EZBoard became inundated with ads, I decided to consult with some Charter members and we persuaded Nathan to give up the keys. He never was going to buy one, anyhow. That was about May of 2000. We formed a small committee to guide us through the transition. I decided at the very onset that we would not have any nudity or obscene language and would not tolerate flaming. I really think that is one of the things which has made SC successful as it is work and family-friendly. Anyone can go on at anytime and not have to feel embarrassed by what's posted.

June 2000: So, we started clean with phpBB on a shared company server owned by MR G - Eugene Goyfman - a guy of about 24 who is a database and programming genius from Russia but lives near Chicago. I then merged the pages that constituted daspyda.com into SC and you see the result as it is today.

Spring, early summer 2001: Unfortunately, one person who broke away and started his own board used a username and password of a Spyderchat Mod who registered on his site to copy threads onto his board and delete them from SC. (this has never been proven for fact, but all evidence pointed to it)

Spring 2003: A couple years later, one of MR G's clients on the shared company server mounted an attack on another client and we got caught in the middle and our database was wiped out. Over the next couple years, we built back up, but would occasionally suffer power losses due to ice and snow storms.

June 2006: To eliminate that issue, last year I decided to move the board to a server farm with power redundancy and 24/7 support.

[edit] Isidar Mithrim

As known in the novel "Eragon", Isidar Mithrim (ĭ-SIH-dhar MĒ-thrim) is also known as the Star Saphire, which is located at the top of the Varden's base, Tronjheim. This is a magnificent dwarf city crafted through many years of cutting through the solid marble. The central column, called Farthen Dûr, is a little over a mile high, with the Isidar Mithrim mounted at the top. People on the top floor, best reached by entrance via dragon flight, may stand on the saphire. The Isidar Mithrim is a natural saphire of enormous size, estimated to be about 100 feet across. It has the design of a star on it, hence the name. This saphire is shattered at the end of the book, distracting the shade Durza, while Eragon defeats him. For further plot details, see Eragon. Naturally, the dwarves are angry at Arya for shattering the Isidar Mithrim (dwarves and elves have always been rivals anyway) though Eragon's dragon Saphira claims to be able to put it back together through the special magic dragons have. Such magics are currently known to be uncontrollable according to the books, so the validity of this statement is questionable.
