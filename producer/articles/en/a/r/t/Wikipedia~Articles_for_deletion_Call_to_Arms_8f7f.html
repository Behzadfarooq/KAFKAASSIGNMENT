[edit] Call to Arms

Mod for Battlefield 1942. 104 unique Googles for searchstring ["Call to Arms" "battlefield 1942" -wikipedia]. Most of these appear to be entries in "lists of mods" run by game fansites. Can't find any critival reviews. Project website has ceased to exist. Article contents are little more than a list of the weapons and vehicles in the game mod. Information appears to be unverifiable through the use of reliable sources, and does not appear to meet the proposed guideline at Wikipedia:Notability (software) -- Saberwyn 02:27, 1 April 2006 (UTC)

  • Delete per WP:V and WP:SOFTWARE --TBC??? ??? ??? 02:41, 1 April 2006 (UTC)
  • Delete as modcruft. Royboycrashfan 03:02, 1 April 2006 (UTC)
  • Delete, modcruft. --Terence Ong 06:31, 1 April 2006 (UTC)
  • Delete, I don't think game mods are notable. JIP | Talk 14:26, 1 April 2006 (UTC)
    • Objection: Some game mods are quite notable, such as the Natural Selection mod for Half-Life. (But no vote.) Zetawoof(ζ) 23:13, 1 April 2006 (UTC)
      • ...oh, and Counter-Strike! Probably the best known of all mods; certainly notable. Zetawoof(ζ) 11:07, 2 April 2006 (UTC)
  • Delete --Edwy 17:08, 1 April 2006 (UTC)
  • Delete Cruft. —Cuiviénen, Saturday, 1 April 2006 @ 18:15 (UTC)
    • Objection : There are a number of less than notable items - both software and entertainment related - that nonetheless should be catalogued and maintained - Mind you, that does not mean include all un-notable items - However, Battlefield 1942 was noteworthy, and I believe a mod to that game should be included - if only as a footnote. -- Mkamensek (talk) -The LeftOverChef 23:16, 1 April 2006 (UTC)
      • List of Battlefield 1942 mods contains basic information and a link to the external site of hundreds of external mods. I'm more than happy for the basic information to stay there, but don't think it notable enough per WP:WEB and WP:SOFTWARE to merit its own article at this point in time. -- Saberwyn 23:34, 1 April 2006 (UTC)
  • Delete per above. --Khoikhoi 03:08, 2 April 2006 (UTC)
  • Delete nn. Remy B 14:33, 4 April 2006 (UTC)
