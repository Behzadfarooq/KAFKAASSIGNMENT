[edit] Donald Crews

Donald Crews is a writer and illustrator of several well-known children's picture books. He was twice the winner of the Cadecott Honors. Common subjects of his include modern technology (especially travel vehicles), and childhood memories.

[edit] Style

He portays these subjects with very clean lines and very uniform textures. This style creates a very impressive and unique view of city life. His art tells a story without relying on unnessesary words. The story is often extremely simple, with very few humans.

[edit] Biography

Donald Crews was born in Newark, New Jersey in 1938. He had an older brother, and two sisters. His mother worked as a seamstress, and his father worked at the railroad, and several other odd jobs. For the summers he would travel down to rural Florida to stay with his grandmother, who he called "Bigmama". He loved loved the big city, but he also loved being at the farm, and this caused him to create two, massively different types of art later on in his life.

From a young age, his talent for drawing was encouraged by his family and his teachers. When he got into high school, one of his teachers became a mentor to him, and personally made sure that he got into art school.

He graduated from Cooper Union for the Advancement of Science and Art in New York City in 1959, and married another graduate, and graphic artist, Ann Jonas. He was drafted into the army in 1963, and sent to Frankfurt, Germany. Their first daughter, Nina was born in Germany, and their second was born a year lter in New York.

While in Germany, he worked on several pieces for his portfolio, including the extremely odd children's book We Read: A to Z. After several suggestions from friends, he submitted it, and it was published by Harpers. The book relied on abstract ideas, rather than tired cliches that were ussually associated with ABC books. One classic example is the entry for the letter C: "Cc, corner: where the yellow is" is illustrated with a yellow square in the far corner of a red page in the book.

He created several other books over the next few years, but it was 1979's Freight Train that won him a Caldecott Honor, and the respect of other artists in that field. He won another Caldecott Honor the next year for Truck. Several other transportation themed books followed, such as School Bus (1984), Flying (1986), and Sail Away (1995). Children loved these books that took everyday objects and followed them on their travels throughout the day. His memories from his summers in florida first appeared in Bigmama's and later in Shortcut. These stories are vastly different from his previous works, in that they focus on humans, and tell a more linear story.

[edit] Katz v. United States

Katz v. United States 389 U.S. 347 (1967) Docket Number: 35 Abstract

Argued:


October 17, 1967

Decided:


December 18, 1967

Subjects:


Criminal Procedure: Search and Seizure Facts of the Case

Acting on a suspicion that Katz was transmitting gambling information over the phone to clients in other states, Federal agents attached an eavesdropping device to the outside of a public phone booth used by Katz. Based on recordings of his end of the conversations, Katz was convicted under an eight-count indictment for the illegal transmission of wagering information from Los Angeles to Boston and Miami. On appeal, Katz challanged his conviction arguing that the recordings could not be used as evidence against him. The Court of Appeals rejected this point, noting the absence of a physical intrusion into the phone booth itself. The Court granted certiorari. Question Presented

Does the Fourth Amendment protection against unreasonable searches and seizures require the police to obtain a search warrant in order to wiretap a public pay phone? Conclusion

Yes. The Court ruled that Katz was entitled to Fourth Amendment protection for his conversations and that a physical intrusion into the area he occupied was unnecessary to bring the Amendment into play. "The Fourth Amendment protects people, not places," wrote Justice Potter Stewart for the Court. A concurring opinion by John Marshall Harlan introduced the idea of a 'reasonable' expectation of Fourth Amendment protection.

[edit] USS Premonition

The USS Premonition is a fictional starship from the science fiction franchise Star Trek. It is set in the Next Generation era and is commanded by Captain Thaddeus Deming. According to the classic strategy game by Activision, Star Trek Armada, the Premonition was constructed in the near distant future. Designed as a timeship, the Premonition was outfitted with a Temporal Drive, which produced a swirling blue and white wormhole, or in this case timehole, allowing the ship to travel backwards or forwards through time.

The starship was constructed after a Borg holocaust, when the Borg, according to Captain Deming, have assimilated every major planet in the Alpha Quadrant, from Bajor to Vulcan to Earth itself. Although not directly stated in Star Trek Armada, according to the novel Star Trek Premonition by Samuel Ordonia, which focuses more on what happened in the alternate future of Borgified Earth, the Premonition was humanity's last ditch effort to save itself from extinction. Eventually, the time came when the Premonition was required to travel back in time, only to be trailed by two Borg ships.

The battered starship arrives in a time that coincides not long after the events depicted in Star Trek Insurrection, as in the game Star Trek Armada, there is a reference to recent hostilities between the Federation and the Sona. In addition, in the first mission of the game Armada, Captain Picard states that the Dominion War has recently ended. This first mission is the time period of which the Premonition emerges with its cyborg companions from the future. After a brief firefight, the Premonition is rescued by Picard. Ironically, Picard mutters to himself that the Premonition looks familiar.

Deming warns the captain of the Enterprise E that a Borg attack on Starbase 612 will soon occur. Deming states that this attack was the first of the Borg invasion "that eventually took over the Alpha Quadrant." Although initially reluctant, Picard agrees to travel to Starbase 612, only to find that Deming was right. However, Picard is able to rally the remaining forces that are still battling the Borg to score the victory against the Borg.

However, this attack was only the first. After the Borg continue to struggle to conquer the Alpha Quadrant, they eventually attack Earth, using their latest weapon, the alternate version of Picard, Locutus. Locutus defeats the defense forces of Earth, which included the Enterprise E and the Premonition. The Premonition sends the Enterprise E back in time to change events more, and in the battle of Earth (take two), the Federation forces are assisted by their new Klingon and Romulan allies.

The mission of the Premonition is a success when Picard's double Locutus is supposedly killed and the Omega Particle, a substance of tremendous power, is destroyed. However, all is not as easy as it seems. Locutus, viewing the inevitibility of defeat, travels back in time himself to kill Picard.

As the universe changes and reforms to a universe lacking a Picard, Deming and the Premontion find themselves the only ones capable of changing this tragedy. According to Deming, the Premonition has a different quantum signature than the other figures of that time period. Apparently, this has protected the Premonition from the changes in the altered timeline. The Premonition travels once more back in time to Picard's first mission with the Enterprise D, to Farpoint Station. The Premonition defeats Locutus, finally killing the Borg, and returns straight to its own time without another word with Picard. This Farpoint Incident explains why Picard hazily recognizes the Premonition when he rescues it from the Borg.

The Premonition is presumed to have returned to a time period with a brighter future. Neither the Premonition nor others of its class are ever mentioned in any Star Trek television show or movie, indicating that it is only a figment of fanfiction. Then again, the existence of a future Federation timeship is not unrealistic, as in the Star Trek Enterprise series, there are repeated references to an organization bent on protecting the timeline. Obviously, the protection of time requires a timeship, and there is a first for everything.

Jeremy St.Louis (born October 22, 1971 in Winnipeg, Canada) is a Canadian television personality. Jeremy currently works for Fox Sports International on an award-winning show called Fox Sportsworld Report - a daily sports show focusing primarily on European soccer leagues and major soccer competitions. The show is viewed in Canada, the United States, Europe, the Caribbean and parts of the Middle East. It boasts a world-wide viewership into the millions.

Jeremy is one of the main personalities on the program along with co-anchor Michelle Lissel and analyst Bobby McMahon. The trio are considered to be one of the most informed and entertaining authorities on soccer in North America.

Prior to his stint with The Report, Jeremy worked as a Weather Anchor for Canwest Global Television in Winnipeg and has a Bachelor of Science degree from Lakehead University in Thunder Bay, Ontario. He graduated with his Journalism diploma from the Southern Alberta Institute of Technology in Calgary, Alberta in 1996.

Jeremy lived in Winnipeg until he was 11-yrs old before his family re-located to Red Deer, Alberta where he stayed until 1990. He cut his chops in the spotlight as a teenager at Lindsay Thurber Comprehensive High School where he was an active member of the Student Council and quarterback of the Raiders football team for three seasons.

In that time, the school played in three straight championship games winning two titles. Jeremy often speaks of his high school football coach Jay Hetherington as the man who taught him the only latin phrase he'd ever need to know: Nihil Sine Labore - nothing without labor.

[edit] Wiener Singakademie

Wiener Singakademie The Wiener Singakademie was founded in 1858 as the first mixed choir of Vienna. In 1913 the choir acquired its long-sought-for home base with the opening of the Wiener Konzerthaus.

The choir has performed under such famous conductors as Gustav Mahler, Wilhelm FurtwÃ¤ngler, and Sergiu Celibidache and has been led by celebrities such as Johannes Brahms and Bruno Walter. Since the beginning of the 1998/1999 musical season the Wiener Singakademie is prepared by its artistic director, Mr. Heinz Ferlesch for its many and varied musical challenges.

As a concert choir the Wiener Singakademie has a versatile repertoire. From the beginning its repertoire was shaped by two considerations which are still valid today: maintaining the music of the traditional masters while also including contemporary works.

At the moment, 100 singers shape the Wiener Singakademie. Through consistent choral practice and the sponsoring of individual voice training, the singers are given the opportunity to develop their voices and to be placed optimally within the choir.

[edit] Coalescence in Computer Memory Management

In Informatics, coalescing is the act of merging two adjacent free blocks of memory. When an application frees memory, gaps can fall in the memory segment that that application uses. Among other techniques, coalescing is used to reduce external fragmentation, but is not totally effective. Coalescing can be done as soon as blocks are freed, or it can be deferred until some time later (known as deferred coalescing), or it might not be done at all.

Coalescence and related techniques like heap compaction, can be used in garbage collection.

[edit] Habitat

They are invertibrate safe mouthbrooders from the reefs around the Banggai Islands of Sulawesi in Indonesia. They are shallow water fish that are found from the surface to about 300 feet. They are usually hide in the corals and under ledges by day and forage the bottom for food by night.


[edit] Captivity

Image:Banggai.jpg The Banggai Cardinal can do well in captivity given the correct water conditions. They will tolerate some nitrate but little or no ammonia or nitrite. They usually benefit from a power jet, wave maker or similar device in the home aquarium as they are often found where the water is quite turbulent.

[edit] Feeding

Banggai cardinals just love being fed frozen or live foods. This includes mysis, brine shrimp, chopped mussels, chopped prawns and other meaty foods. In fact if you put the food in front of a wave maker or power jet they go absolutely beserk trying to catch as much as possible.


[edit] Reproduction

In the aquarium it has been possible to feed the babies on brine shrimp fry within 24 hours of their emergence from the males mouth. They have been known to use long spined sea urchins (Diadema setosum) as protection against predators.

Otomycosis is a fungal infection (Aspergillus niger >80% of cases)of the auditory canal and middle ear often associated with bacterial infections. Otomycosis is often unrecognized and treated with antibiotics or corticosteroids. Antifungal treatment is the regular course of treatment, typically the AZOLE family is used (i.e Miconazole). [edit] Inventory Turns and GMROI in Distribution

In distribution it is important to manage inventory effectively as it is one of the largest assets. The two classic measurements are Turns and Gross Margin Return on Investment (GMROI). The following equations are for a multi-warehouse set up where product transfers take place. Keep in mind that we are only dealing with product sold and transferred from stocking shelves, not sold direct from vendor or cross-docked. In the following, COGS stands for Cost of Goods Sold (from stock), COGT stands for Cost of Goods Transferred (from Stock), GM stands for Gross Margin Dollars for Stock Sales, and AvgInv stands for Average Inventory Value (take starting inventory value for the period and average it with the ending, or take multiple snapshots and average them). Typically these values are based on a rolling 12 months.


Turns = (COGS + COGT) / AvgInv


GMROI = GM / (AvgInv * R) where R is COGS / (COGS + COGT)


Note that if you only have one warehouse these equations reduce to the ones typically found on the World Wide Web since COGT will be zero.

The sweet spot for Turns depends on your Gross Margin Percent. Generally, the lower the GMP the higher the turns needed to be profitable. For a profitable general line industrial distributor a turn value of 5 is typical.

The sweet spot for GMROI is generally 1.25 to 1.75.

Note that if you have large differences in carrying cost across the product groups you distribute, then you will want to add a Carrying Cost Factor to AvgInv to properly compare product groups.

[edit] SOC medium

SOC is a specialised microbiological medium, primarily used for culture of E. coli after transformation, before plating.

SOC consists of 2% w/v tryptone, 5 % w/v yeast extract, 0.5% sodium chloride, 2.5 mM potassium chloride, 10 mM magnesium chloride, 20 mM magnesium sulphate and 10 mM glucose. This formula sees some variation; in particular, SOC is sometimes prepared without one or other of the magnesium salts.

SOC was developed by Douglas Hanahan in the early 1980s, during his pioneering work on the transfer of genes into bacterial and mammalian cells. It is a derivative of another medium, SOB, differing in that it contains glucose.

[edit] Origin of the Name

The name SOC derives from the fact that the medium is a version of SOB medium which, because of the glucose, gives rise to catabolite repression. SOB itself is so named because its inventor, somewhat hyperbolically, proclaimed it to be a super optimal broth.

[edit] Chet Williamson

Chet Williamson (born 1948 in Lancaster, Pennsylvania) is an American writer of suspense and horror fiction. His novels include Ash Wednesday, Soulstorm, Second Chance, McKain's Dilemma, Reign and Dreamthorp.

Also a prolific writer of short stories, many of which were collected in Figures in Rain, he is possibly best known for writing the children's picture book, A Pennsylvania Dutch Night Before Christmas (illustrated by James Rice), published in 2000. He has been nominated for the Bram Stoker Award, the World Fantasy Award and Mystery Writers of America's Edgar Award. His short stories have been printed in magazines including The New Yorker, Playboy, Esquire, Twilight Zone and The Magazine of Fantasy & Science Fiction.

An avid collector of music, he has performed for five years as singer and guitarist in the Celtic duo Fire in the Glen, with writer Tom Knapp on fiddle and bodhran. A 1970 graduate of Indiana University of Pennsylvania, he also has been a public school teacher, actor and freelance writer. He has lived for many years in Elizabethtown, Pennsylvania.
