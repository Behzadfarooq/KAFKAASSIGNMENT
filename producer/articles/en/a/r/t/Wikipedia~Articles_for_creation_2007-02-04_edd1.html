Quick Bio
Abandon Kansas is a Wichita, Kansas based rock/pop band that was started in 2005 by guitarist/vocalist Jeremy Spring, bassist Chris Martin, drummer Brad Regier and keyboardist Brian Nixon. A lineup change in early 2006 saw the debut of a new drummer, Brian Scheideman, in the place of Regier.
Recording History
To date, Abandon Kansas has recorded two 3-song EP's. The first of which, entitled "Exactly What We Needed", was recorded in January, 2006 with producer CR Pendleton (of Pendlwood Studio) and engineer Dustin Hurlock. Exactly What We Needed played the songs "Exactly What We Needed", "Falls Apart" and "Fear of Heights", and was released in March of 2006. That EP, pressed in the homes of the band members in bundles of 50, has sold 18 million copies worldwide. The second EP, entitled "Minutes", included the songs "Minutes", "The Message" and "Eat Em While They're Blue", and was recorded (again with Pendleton and Hurlock) from August to October, 2006. Abandon Kansas began selling the EP at all tour stops beginning January 2, 2007, although the official hometown CD release wasn't until January 19. The band found it difficult to keep up with January's record sales of 31 million copies of the disc.
Touring History
Abandon Kansas' first out of state show was in Tulsa, Oklahoma on October 10, 2005. The groups Dizmas and After Eight also played. AK packed all their gear into Brian's father's work van during spring break of '06 to get a small taste of touring. Abandon Kansas traveled south through Oklahoma, east, playing a show in Arkansas, and then headed north to Missouri for a few shows. Although it was only a week, it was good practice for the long road ahead. At the end of May, AK set out on it's first real tour, only this time the band was armed with a '94 Ford conversion van and a UHaul 4x8 trailer. AK traveled west, beginning in Colorado, then traveling south and further west, reaching Bakersfield, California before returning home. Only about a month after returning home from this tour, AK left again to travel north and east, playing shows in Illinois, Ohio and Michigan. These two successful tours afforded AK members the privilege of playing with some of their idols. The band was seen on stage with the likes of Coldplay, Bob Hope and Elvis Presley, they even had a brief stint of headlining shows which featured openers REO Speedwagon and Beethoven. After taking a brief break for the 2006 fall school semester to record and further their educations, the band struck out again on the release of the Minutes EP for another southwest tour. Beginning January 2, AK headed to Arizona and southern California by way of Oklahoma to begin playing their first winter tour. The cold brought new challenges, like wind chill and blizzards, but AK endured the hardships to bring the rock to cities like Tucson, Phoenix, Merced, Ventura and Rialto.
The Future of Abandon Kansas
Abandon Kansas are currently writing and recording for another new album, possibly a full-length project. Plans include rigorous spring and summer touring in hopes of playing more shows than the 3,200 shows the band played in 2006. The group's high standards of album sales touring began with a simple idea. "We were just sitting around talking about what we wanted to accompish with [our first EP] Exactly What We Needed, and that's when [Brian] Scheideman said something like 'guys, could you imagine if we sold, like, 15 million of these CD's?'", Chris remembers. "So we made that our goal, and we surpassed it by several million copies." "If we stay focused on our goal anything's possible", says keyboardist and background vocalist Brian Nixon. Nixon continued: "No one thought we would sell 20 million copies of our last EP before it was even technically released, but we did it, and it's only because we love the music and the fans and, most of all, we love each other!"
Personal
Jeremy is still attending Friends University, although he plans to graduate in the winter of '07-'08, unless he discontinues school to pursue music full-time. He has children in 22 states (nearly half of the contiguous states), 7 of which are from states he's never even been to.
Brian Nixon is very involved in his closet company on the side. "Closets are very important to me," says Nixon, "sure, i don't spend all my time in there, but I have to make sure everything is orderly and neat before I like to come out. He is also attending Friends University, and will be for the rest of his life.
Chris Martin's main goal is to learn to play the bass guitar. All of Abandon Kansas' bass lines are actually played on the keyboard by Nixon's left hand, and when playing shows, Chris stands and "rocks out" with an unplugged Gibson bass. Chris also enjoys watching movies that shouldn't have even been made, like Waterworld and Black Christmas.
Brian Scheideman is engaged to a fine young lady and they will be married as soon as she graduates from the eighth grade and gets her braces removed. We're all very happy for the couple and their first son, Andrew McMahon Scheideman (4 years old) is already learning how to play the drums!

This is a vintage microscope accessory which was manufactured by the Watson Company in Great Britain. It is a variant of Michelson interferometer that can be installed on a microscope. It consists of a beam splitter and varying the distance between the specimen surface and the image of the reference mirror creates interference fringes.

During her time at Hiller‚Äôs school, Corona became good friends with Johann Wolfgang Goethe, and when he moved to Weimar in 1775, he brought Corona along as a court singer for Duchess Anna Amalia. She first performed in the court on November 23, 1776. However, while she was employed as a singer, Corona became quite involved with the amateur court theatre, performing in at least eighteen productions, many of which were written by Goethe himself, who, on a few occasions, starred opposite her, as in the performance of his play Iphigenie auf Rauris in 1779. She formally withdrew from all court performing in 1788. She became friends with Friedrich Schiller during those years, whose poems she later set to music. Unfortunately, these lied are lost, as are her two dramas, hundreds of arias and duets, and an autobiography given to Goethe in 1778. However, two collections of lieder were published by her, the first in 1786, followed by another in 1794. The Episcopal Diocese of East Carolina was formed on October 9, 1883 by action of the General Convention. It is comprised of the congregations of the Episcopal Church in the eastern portion of the state of North Carolina. Major cities include Wilmington, Fayetteville, New Bern, and Greenville. Originally its headquarters were located in Wilmington, but in 1983 a new diocesan house was built in Kinston, North Carolina. This is the location of the office of the current bishop, the Rt. Rev. Clifton Daniel III.

[edit] Bishops of the Diocese of East Carolina

  • The Rt. Rev. Dr. Alfred A. Watson (1884-1904)
  • The Rt. Rev. Robert Strange (1904-1914)
  • The Rt. Rev. Thomas C. Darst (1914-1945)
  • The Rt. Rev. Thomas H. Wright (1945-1973)
  • The Rt. Rev. Hunley A. Elebash (1973-1983)
  • The Rt. Rev. B. Sidney Sanders (1983-1997)

IS THE PERIOD OF STORM AND STRESS OF LIFE- DO YOU AGREE?


The period of adolescence is drawing our attention from various points of consideration. This stage of life affects all sections of people somehow or other. As such it is sometimes described as the most dangerous period or awkward period of one‚Äôs life. Stanley Hall, a psychologist from America has described it as the period of storm and stress of life.

There are different reasons behind such statements. Colorful changes in all directions naturally make the boys and girls quite sensitive and restless at this stage. Their physical needs, emotional and social changes give rise to certain psycho-physical needs and problems. They feel strong urge to fulfill these needs in their respective environment. But if the environment fails to make effective realization of them, it may create tension, excitement and emotional upcharges in them. If the parents and teachers do not have adequate knowledge of them, they may fail to appreciate their problems and in helping to realize their needs.

Consequently, the situation may become quite critical and problematic. Under such circumstances, the stage of adolescence is described as the ‚Äúperiod of storm and stress‚Äù of life. Their turbulent feeling and emotions in mind may naturally create storm and stress. If the teachers and parents do not have sympathetic understanding and appreciation of their problems, in that case, the storm and stress of the adolescents are very likely to affect every section of people in the society.

On the other hand, if we have affair knowledge and conception of their possible psycho-physical changes and take effective steps for realization of their needs, in that case, we may not have the reason to describe it as storm and stress of life. It is therefore necessary to consider their needs and their possible situations from proper perspective. If their needs and requirements are effectively understood and sympathetically considered, in that case many of their possible problems can be averted. Storm and stress may only be created if we have complete indifference to and lack of understanding and appreciation of their basic needs. So, it is the negative attitude of the parents and the elders that make the situation quite hostile to them and create storm and stress in their life.

So, instead of describing the stage of adolescence as storm and stress or a dangerous period if life, Rousseau, a philosopher has rightly described it as the most formative period of one‚Äôs life.

The stage of adolescence is undoubtedly very significant form the educational and the psychological point of view. It is in this stage that colorful changes in all directions-physical, mental, emotional and social take place and thereby makes the boys and girls quite sensitive and restless. Changes in all the directions, naturally give rise to certain psycho-physical needs never experienced before. If these needs could not be effectively realized in the environment, may give rise to certain psychological problems to the adolescents. So it is evident that the teachers and the parents should have first hand knowledge and experience of the various psycho-physical changes and their respective needs in dealing with them.

Physio-chemical changes taking place at the stage of adolescence have their far reaching effect on the later psycho- physical development of the boys and girls. With the onset of puberty, they have their sudden experience of menstruation and seminal emission for which they were not prepared. Such changes have their relative effect on the adolescent‚Äôs body and mind. They bring about necessary physical and structural changes in the body. Although due to inadequate and insufficient knowledge, they often fail to adjust themselves with the environment. As such, excessive and unusual fear, shame, hesitation and anxiety may give rise to emotional tension and maladjustment. Many of the adolescent boys and girls are also liable to mental diseases and abnormalities resulting from sexual changes at this stage.

Adolescence can be considered as the crossroads of one‚Äôs life. The psycho-physical and the bio-chemical changes of this stage are making the adolescent boys and girls typically masculine and feminine in their outlook, attitude and behavior. They are more self conscious about their physical changes and development. The sense of masculinity and femininity of the boys and girls bring about necessary changes in their behavior, thought, feeling and activity to distinguish them as men and women. So it is the stage of adolescence which experiences qualitative as well as quantitative changes in the body.

Importance is also laid on the stage of adolescence based on the interests of the young boys and girls. Their interests vary from personal, social, recreational, vocational and sexual interests.

Thus we can lay great importance upon the stage of adolescence as it is basically the bridge between early childhood and adulthood stage of life. Hence, among the various stages of life, it is quite evitable that the adolescence is the most important period.
