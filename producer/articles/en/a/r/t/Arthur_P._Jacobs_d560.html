Arthur Jacobs
Born March 7, 1922
Los Angeles, California
Died June 27, 1973
Los Angeles, California
Occupation Film producer

Arthur Jacobs was a twentieth century film producer responsible for numerous classic films of the 1960s and 1970s, including the Planet of the Apes series, Doctor Dolittle, Goodbye, Mr. Chips, Play It Again, Sam and Tom Sawyer.[1] Tom Sawyer, a musical which featured both a script and song score by the Sherman Brothers was to be the first in a five picture deal with the prolific brothers.[citation needed] Each film was to be a musical with an Americana theme. However, tragedy struck during the filming of Huckleberry Finn and Jacobs died suddenly of a heart attack. Between the loss of Jacobs and a protracted recovery from knee surgery of Robert B. Sherman, Huckleberry Finn suffered creatively.
