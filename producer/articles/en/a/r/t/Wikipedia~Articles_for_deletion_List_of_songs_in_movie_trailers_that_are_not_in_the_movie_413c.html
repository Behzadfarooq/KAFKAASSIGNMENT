[edit] List of songs in movie trailers that are not in the movie

This nomination was not completed; listing now. Flowerparty■ 02:41, 30 September 2005 (UTC)

I don't really think this list is even "important" enough to be in an encyclopedia. I don't think that anyone would ever go looking for th is information and go "wow that's interesting", but I could be wrong. But I just think the list is pretty pointless. Private Butcher 00:59, 28 September 2005 (UTC)

  • Delete, for what I said above, I think it should be deleted. Private Butcher 00:59, 28 September 2005 (UTC)
  • Keep. Some people think special song trivia is interesting. --SuperDude 04:33, 29 September 2005 (UTC)
  • "Interesting," but unencyclopedic. Delete. --Jeffrey O. Gustafson - Shazaam! - <*> 02:53, 30 September 2005 (UTC)
  • Delete, not encyclopedic. — JIP | Talk 05:07, 30 September 2005 (UTC)
  • Delete not encyclopedic, ambigious criteria. Xoloz 05:44, 30 September 2005 (UTC)
  • Keep. Just because Wikipedia is text-based is no reason to have a text-based bias. Joaquin Murietta 07:32, 30 September 2005 (UTC)
    • Eh? WP has plenty of articles on non-text things, including many songs, and these interesting creatures called humans, which are not text. It is, however, a requirement (not a bias) that things with articles here be encyclopedic, which many hold this isn't. Xoloz 08:43, 30 September 2005 (UTC)
  • I wonder whether it's common to use songs not in the movie on the trailer. If it is the list could be endless. Otherwise, it may actually be informative. - Mgm|(talk) 09:23, 30 September 2005 (UTC)
    • It is, as Average Earthman notes. I can't tell you how many movie trailers I've seen featuring a song by Enya or featuring O Fortuna from Carmina Burana. Al 12:42, 30 September 2005 (UTC)
  • Delete. This is actually very common, so the list will be very long. The soundtrack is often the last thing to be fixed in a film, often after the publicity machine has started, which is why trailers often have the theme tunes from completely different films. Average Earthman 09:27, 30 September 2005 (UTC)
  • Delete. Unmaintainable; it seems every trailer produced these days features a song not on the soundtrack. android79 12:50, 30 September 2005 (UTC)
  • Delete, unmaintainable (Al is correct, for example), fails WP:Importance. Please consider joining the centralized discussion about "Lists of songs". Barno 15:17, 30 September 2005 (UTC)
  • Delete unencyclopedic: a fairly random happenstance. -Splashtalk 20:58, 30 September 2005 (UTC)
  • Delete. --Agamemnon2 21:36, 30 September 2005 (UTC)
  • Delete. So many movie trailers do this, it could almost be List of movie trailers. --Carnildo 21:55, 30 September 2005 (UTC)
  • Delete, but I sure would be interested in an article about why/how this phenomenon happens, with perhaps a few notable singles from soundtracks that were never in movies. As a list, for the reasons many people listed above, it just won't do. --Jacquelyn Marie 21:59, 30 September 2005 (UTC)
    • It happens because soundtracks are the last thing to be added to a film (if the making of the film extras are to be trusted). The music isn't finalised when the trailer is released, so they add appropriate music instead. I think. Oh, and delete. Sabine's Sunbird 23:06, 30 September 2005 (UTC)
  • Delete, unmaintainable. >: Roby Wayne Talk • Hist 23:41, 30 September 2005 (UTC)
  • Delete Amren (talk) 00:12, 1 October 2005 (UTC)
  • Delete as unencyclopedic listcruft. MCB 00:54, 1 October 2005 (UTC)
  • Delete, unmaintainable. Extraordinary Machine 11:45, 1 October 2005 (UTC)
  • Keep. I find it interesting. It contains facts. Wikipedia does not have paper restictions-- Nick Dillinger 05:36, 2 October 2005 (UTC)
  • Delete This is an encyclopædia, not an indiscriminate collection of information. --Mel Etitis (Μελ Ετητης) 22:20, 2 October 2005 (UTC)
