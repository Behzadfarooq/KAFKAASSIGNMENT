Arthur "Art" Shephard is a fictional character on the ABC television series, Desperate Housewives. The character is played by Matt Roth.

[edit] History

Spoiler warning: Plot and/or ending details follow.

Art is the new neighbor in the Youngs' old house who is caring for his ill sister, Rebecca. He and Lynette Scavo meet for the first time when they bump into each other at the local supermarket. The store is held up by Carolyn Bigsby, who has come to kill her husband Harvey (the store owner) for cheating on her. She is about to shoot Lynette for screaming that she deserved to be cheated on, but Art throws a can at Carolyn's head. She falls over and fires the gun, but only grazes Lynette's left arm.

When Lynette is released from the hospital, she bakes Art a cake as a "thank you" gift. She and Parker bring it over, but when he isn't home, they leave it on his kitchen table. While Lynette is writing a note to him, Parker follows a toy train into his basement which is full of toys and impresses both. However, Lynette is horrified when she finds floor-to-ceiling polaroids of half-naked young boys in a side room. She begins to doubt herself, however, when various people play down her suspicions, Art's sister comes to his defense, and the neighborhood starts a picket line outside of his house.

Lynette regrets her actions altogether when Art's sister is taken to the hospital, and then is revealed to have died there. She apologizes to Art, but he merely taunts her in response; he explains that he had to hide his pedophilia from his sister, but now he is free of those restrictions. He then ominously says that he's going to be moving to a new neighborhood.

[edit] Trivia

Art's last name was originally supposed to be Washburn. It was changed to Shephard.
