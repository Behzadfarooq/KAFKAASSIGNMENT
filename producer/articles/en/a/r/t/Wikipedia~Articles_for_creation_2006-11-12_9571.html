[edit] Baan Tawai at Chiang Mai

City of Chiang Mai (http://www.chiangmainews.com), after Bangkok the second largest city in Thailand (with population only under 200.000, comparing to Bangkok's close to 7 Mio people), is the lands' "northern capital". With many typical features of local culture, unusual leisure activities, lanna (as this part of country is known) food (http://www.lannacafe.org), local markets and shopping, featuring special local items and handicraft or handmade items of great variety, Chiang Mai area is the must stop in Thailand for most of the tourist or business people who come to Thailand. Along with the beaches of south, 24-7 busy and awake, energetic Bangkok, with only 55 minutes long flight from Bangkok Suvarnabhumi airport (http://www.airportthai.co.th) Chiang Mai is easily accessible and it's visit completes the journey of major destinations in Thailand. Leisure searchers in CM area can choose from options like elephant riding, rafting, or visit a center for extreme sports (http://www.chiangmai-xcentre.com). Anyone can become a shopper in Thailand easily. Typical shopping items for tourists are handicrafts, silk products, ceramics, carvings, wood products - they can be found in small shops and market places around the city. However, Baan Tawai with surrounding area and places along the way to village of Hang Dong, 15 minutes drive from the city by the south-bound highway, is the center for local handicrafts, ceramic and wood-art shops, or places selling antiques. Tourist come here to find cheap but interesting gift items - wooden toys, figures, vases, plates, carvings, ceramics, decorative objects and accessories. Some shops offer huge vases and pots, or different kinds of ceramics for garden or porch, or indoor display. Items of a higher historic or artistic value are often found available. Teak wood products have here a long tradition. Teak wood is the commodity of choice for craftsmen making wood carvings and also furniture makers. Antique furniture shops scattered around the area carry a lot of furniture from British colonial era (http://www.goldentriangle.co.th). The British colonized the neighboring Burma, along with other places around the region in the period of 1870s-1940s. They influenced the local architecture as well as the interior design. Baan tawai does not need to be presented as a place for shopping only - it is a place which provides for the visitor an insight to local temporary and historic art, the wood - workmanship which has a long tradition here, along with a variety of other unusual items and products of the local craftsmen.

The author uses a few links in the article to favorite webs covering Chiang Mai information and places for Thai-typical arts. I believe along with other information in this article they will be useful for so many visitors who come to Chiang Mai all year round

[edit] Kevin Navayne

Mr. Kevin Navayne was born August 20, 1977 in Spanish Town, Jamaica 15 miles outside the capital of Kingston, Kevin is the third child from his father a retired Jamaican Police officer and the 2nd child from his mother a 2nd grade math teacher. In order to give Kevin and his brothers a better life his parents picked up and left the only place they called home and moved to the United States to a town named East Hartford a suburb of the city Hartford in Connecticut.

Kevin grew up middle class. His parents of Jamaican descent, but his maternal great grandmother was Jewish and from the country of Yemen. He grew up like a lot other middle class kids in the suburbs but Kevin was attracted to drugs and money. He looked up to the guys in school who drove the nice cars and had the nice clothes. He didn't worry about the future he worried about the present. He says that the only reason why he attended school was for the fact that he was allowed to play baseball.

Kevin Navayne was a very gifted baseball player. Leading the semi-pro American Legion baseball team of East Hartford to the American Legion World Series and leading his East Hartford High School team to state champion status 2 out of 3 years while there. Baseball was his life. His junior year in high school however he was expelled from East Hartford High for having 5 fist fight in the same year. It was in May when the new Principal in an effort to clean up the school from the troubled teens which included Kevin Navayne had enough and kicked him out.

With few options but very strict parents Kevin's father gave him an ultimatum. Either straighten out or get out. With a little bit of trickery and some connections Kevin's father was able to fake an address in a neighboring but affluent town and was able to get Kevin into Glastonbury High School. Over the year Kevin proved himself to not only fit in, but to get his grades up so that he would be able to graduate on time with his peers.

In 2000 Kevin moved to New York and on his very first acting audition earned a part as a construction worker in the HBO movie Disappearing Acts starring Wesley Snipes and Sanaa Lathan. In 2002 discovered by model super agent Beth Ann Moore who also represented Tyson Beckford he did ad campaigns for Lacoste and IZOD. Over the next 4 years he did fashion shows for countless designers. In mid 2006 he was signed to the international modeling agency Limited Model Management. Known as their top model at the moment he is in talks to model for LL Cool J's fashion line Todd Smith, Sean John (P. Diddy's clothing line) and Phat Farm along with Ralph Lauren's Black Label. He is also looking to get back into acting.

Kevin has been labeled a cross between Tyson Beckford, and Tyrese Gibson but with a sharper look. Kevin loves to jet ski, loves motorcycles, watching cartoons and eating cereal. He states that he's a pretty down to earth guy.

[edit] Racing Identities

Racing Identities was an Australian progressive pop band formed in 1985 and disbanded in 1987. Their members include Paul McTigue on lead guitar and vocals, Trevor Dooley on bass and vocals and John McVeigh on drums. The three met while working for the ATO in Sydney and began practicing at a house McVeigh was minding and had their first gig at the croydon park hotel, where Keith Crook saw the band and invited them to record a few of their songs at Loft Studios in Sydney.They recorded a single "The Way That It Ends" which debuted at number four on the DoubleJ charts. An self-titled album quickly followed which sold over 100,000 copies in Australia, but the band fell apart in November of 87'. The band went on a sellout reunion tour in August of 93' a few weeks after McVeigh's first son had been born. In 1997 McTigue and Dooley formed another band, but according to McTigue nad Dooley, they weren't as popular without McVeigh.

[edit] Eric Tscherne

Eric Tscherne was born in Cleveland Ohio, 01/26/1976. He grew up in the surrounding area. This is where he first became interested in Custom Car Culture and Design.

From 1994-1999 He attended and graduated from the University of Cincinnati College of Design, Architecture, Art, and Planning (DAAP) He majored in Industrial Design and focused on Transportation Design.

Graduation brought him to L.A. where he signed on with the Hot Wheels Design Team. Working at Hot Wheels, Tscherne developed many vehicles collectors have come to love including:
1964 Buick Riviera
Ford Focus Dragster
Nissan Z concept
Sho-Stopper
Seared Tuner
Muscle Tone
Rockster
Ferrari 360 Modena 'Tooned
Toyota Supra 'Tooned
Tourque Screw
and many others.

Tscherne is credited with ushering in the "Tuner" trend into the previously all-American brand. Tscherne is an influential member of the "Tuning Community". His designs have appeared in publications like Super Street, Import Tuner, Lowrider Edge and many other magazines. He has worked for: Ford, Mopar, Chevy Performance, NuFormz, Wings West and many other aftermarket companies to create various products to restyle a person's car, wings, body kits, wheels,etc.

Tscherne also is associated with the "Cartoon" look becoming popularized in die-cast. His Car Tunerz then renamed Hot Tunerz line captured the essence of a car in a cute stylized replica. This line sold well for 2 years before fizzling out on the pegs

An off-shoot of the Car-Tunerz look were the 'Tooned vehicles produced by Hot Wheels, Mattel from 2004 through 2005. Many castings are still used, however the 'Tooned name along with many other "Cartooned" Hot Wheels vehicles are not. Tscherne has been said to have created the Hardnose look as well. Collectors generally do not like these or other cartoon styled Hot Wheels

Tscherne appeared on an Episode of Monster Garage. He was the Designer for the Red Rocker in an episode that brought the Mattel classic Rock 'em Sock 'em Robots game to life. He is credited as the designer of the Red Scion xB.

in 2004 Tscherne left Hot Wheels after 5 years. Not much is known about the time between this and in 2006 when Tscherne surfaced again this time as Creative Director for RC2's JL Full Throttle line.

The JL Full Throttle line is supposed to be his vision. The JL Full Throttle line is composed of cars by Chip Foose and a series of Trucks called Rock Rigs. Tscherne also helped to bring Chip Foose's dream car Hemisfear to life at the 2006 SEMA show. JL Full Throttle, a brand of RC2's funded the construction of the Hemisfear while simultaneously building scale replicas. Tscherne was on hand to help unveil the concept and answer questions about the diecast and build.

[edit] payback period Rule

The payback period is the number of years required for a firm to recover its initial investment required by a project from the cash flow it generates. Payback period rule is one of the most popular alternatives to net present value (NPV).

It is an investment decision rule which states that all investment projects have payback periods equal to or less than a particular cutoff period are accepted, and of all of those that pay of in more than the particular cutoff period are rejected.

However, there are some drawbacks in applying this rule. Timing of cash flow within the payback period is not being considered within the payback period. As compare to NPV, it does not discount the cash flow properly.

Another problem is payback period ignores cash flow occurring after the payback period. This makes decision maker need to have an artificially short-term orientation, which may lead to decision not in shareholders‚Äô best interests.

There is no comparable guide for choosing the payback period so the period is arbitrary to some extent. Unlike, NPV approach, it can go to the capital market to get the discount rate.

[edit] Petenera (Flamenco)

La Petenera Corta de Preparacion - La Petenera Grande - La Solea Petenera

Peteneras are flamenco songs full of emotion, melancholy and sentimental feelings, usually lamenting aspects of injustice. There is, however, a lot of mystery about its origins. This type of 'cante' is by some believed to be of Sephardi Jewish origin, due to a few of its lyrics, however, experts do not exclude any other origin. This particular song ows its origin to a flamenco singer called 'La Petenera' from Paterna de la Rivera in the Andalusian province of Cadiz.

1) The name 'Petenera' has neither Andalusian nor 'Gypsy' roots.

2) Andalucians pronounce 'paternera' as 'patehnera' with a very audible letter 'h'.

3) The singer 'Juanelo' was acquainted with a singer called 'La Petenera'.

4) Both tradition and legend have given us some clues about the life of this singer who,

[edit] Unisys Agile Business Suite

Unisys Agile Business Suite (AB Suite or just Abs) is a replacement for Unisys' LINC 4GL. It aims to facilitate:

  • Object-Oriented programming
  • Component-based development
  • Service-oriented architecture

on Unisys Corporation's Mainframe computer, Microsoft Windows and Linux servers. That's not to say that it can't be used on other servers.

[edit] Websites

[edit] Official

http://www.unisys.com/products/software/application__development/agile__business__suite.htm

[edit] Developer

[edit] Physical Description

Developer runs within Visual Studio. This means that:

  • It has a Visual Studio look.
  • Visual Studio components like the debugger are used for common development tasks.
  • You can generate and deploy your systems together with Windows components that depend on your system (or on which your system depends) in a single operation.

Your Model is stored using Microsoft SQL Server.

[edit] Overview for people not familiar with the LINC 4GL

ABSuite is a development environment in which you can store all of the design and business rules for one or more of your enterprise systems.

Within a Model, you put one or Segments, which represent a particular system. A Segment will have other classes (persistent and non-persistent, with or without user-interfaces) methods and variables. Although you can use SQL in some cases, most methods are written in a special propreitary language. Various conventions dictate when methods will be executed.

When you select "Build":

  • Corresponding code to exhibit the required behaviour is generated and compiled.
  • Database tables which match the generated code are deployed.
  • Graphical screens for the user interface are generated.

Purchasers of AB Suite would normally take the product Computer-based training or engage Unisys trainers to learn how best to use it.

[edit] What's New

  • Type_polymorphism and Inheritance (computer science) on most types of object
  • you can put a Method (computer science) on almost any object
  • Information hiding
  • You can use exactly the same Windows services (eg RATL, NOF) which access the Windows Runtime to test behaviour with the debugger.

[edit] What's Changed

  • Many behavioural facets which were implicit in LINC are explicit in AB Suite. This means more objects and more logic if you desire identical behaviour.
  • Many constraints which were imposed in LINC have now been lifted. If you specify to do something that you couldn't do before, generated code should either tolerate any mistake that the developer made or exhibit new behaviour to match what the developer specified. Note however that the Runtimes are bug-compatible with past releases so any behaviour that you could specify in past releases and caused buggy behaviour at run time will still do just that ie using DT; LAST with a large Oracle database table is still a bad idea.
  • Multiplicity means much. Multiplicity of 0 means that you have defined a class but no instances of it. Multiplicity of 1 means that you have 1 instance of a class. Higher multiplicity gives you an array of the class.
  • Syntax options that better match Visual Basic and Visual C# like if and while are now available. The old ones are still available also. Semi-colons are unnecessary.
  • Statements and predicates go across 1 line only (unlike the many lines in LINC) and precedence is defined using brackets (no more OR has precedence over AND).

[edit] Migration

If you have an existing LINC LCIF, you can easily load that into an AB Suite Model. Your Ispecs, Reports, SDs and GLGs will be converted to different types of classes and objects with different methods. As stated above, you will notice objects and logic defined explicitly where it wasn't before. As your knowledge of the new product improves, you might decide that the behaviour implemented is unnecessary.

Non-native English speakers may observe additional comments in English that they didn't put there. These describe changes made during the migration. Native English speakers may observe this too.

[edit] Runtime

Separate Runtimes should be available for:

  • Microsoft .NET, which behaves consistently with the Windows NT LINC Runtime
  • J2EE, which behaves consistently with the Unix LINC runtime
  • Mainframe platforms like in past releases (refer to Unisys' website for details on whether these will be in COBOL or utilise the Java Virtual Machine for each mainframe platform)

They all should offer functionality identical to that offered in the past LINC release with which they are consistent.

[edit] Client Tools

Graphical screens generated with AB Suite should be compatible with past releases of LINC if the corresponding screens are absolutely identical. Conversely, systems generated with AB Suite should be accessible with older graphical screens.

Watch Unisys' website for any exciting new developments.

[edit] Facebows

Facebows are caliper-like devices commonly used in Removable Prosthodontics (Dentistry). They help to facilitate recording and transferring of information from the patient to the articulator thus allowing the maxillary (upper) cast to be mounted onto the articulator. However, it has nothing to do with orientating the mandibular cast with the articulator - as sucj, it is not a "true" Maxillo-mandibular record.

Their main function is to record the relationship of the jaws to the opening axis of the jaws (hinge axis), and orient the casts in the same relationship to the opening axis of the articulator; it does this by locating the bite fork relative to 3 reference points (1 anterior and 2 posterior reference points). The anterior reference point is the orbital margin and the posterior reference point is the skin over the condyles for the Conventional facial facebow. For the Conventional earpiece facebow, the anterior reference point is the nasion area, and the posterior references are the external auditory meatus.

Without a facebow record, errors in the inclination and positioning of the maxillary cast will manifest as errors in achieving balanced articulation of the denture in situ.


Parts of different types of Facebows

[edit] Types of Facebows

1. Kinematic This face bow type is more accurate and relies on several tracings on the path of the condyle to determine the exact location of the intercondylar hinge axis. However, it is technically demanding and time consuming and as such is not a popular choice amongst new dentists.

2. Conventional This face now is the more common type used; it utilizes population averages to predict the position of the condyle as being ~12mm along a hypothetical line drawn from the tragus to the canthus of the eye. This may lead to a slight inaccuracy when locating the transverse hinge axis as not everyone follows the population mean. However, it still manages to produce excellent results when used properly.

Types of conventional facebows include 'Ear piece face bows' (e.g. Whipmix facebow) where the external auditory meatus is used as the posterior landmark and the 'facial face bows' where the condyles on the side of the face are utilized as posterior anatomical landmarks (e.g. Dentatus face bow).

[edit] Asysco Lion

Lion is short for "LI on NT and is a product which emulates LINC behaviour on Windows NT.

[edit] About Asysco

Asysco began as a consultancy firm supporting users of Unisys Corporation's LINC 4GL. Their customers consisted not only of Dutch finance and insurance companies but of many LINC users around the world who used their software tools to enhance their LINC development experience.

As Mainframe computer users looked to move away from mainframes including Unisys ones, Asysco has provided services to move to more modern platforms. They are recognised as a partner in the Mainframe Migration Alliance (http://www.mainframemigration.org/) and as a partner with Microsoft (http://members.microsoft.com/CustomerEvidence/search/ListCompany.aspx?PageMode=2).

A lot of this consists of migrating customers' COBOL code. However, for LINC customers to move to Windows they have Lion. The introduction of this product meant that Asysco now competed directly with Unisys for Microsoft Windows customers in addition to selling complementary products and services.

Asysco has many stories of successful migrations. At the same time, they have many customers who use their services and support-tools while continuing to use LINC, either on a mainframe or on Windows.

[edit] About LION

Lion originally consisted only of a generator which generated code in the Delphi programming language for use with Microsoft SQL Server given a LINC LCIF. As explained at http://www.bergmans.demon.nl/linc.html (in Dutch but with diagrams in English), an additional application development tool has since been developed such that it now consists of LionDev for developing and LionGen for generating Delphi systems.

[edit] Radical Moderatism

[edit] Definition

Radical moderatism holds that most political issues and questions are unscientific, unanswerable, or irrelevant. It differs from moderatism in that the latter tends to mild-manneredly emphasize compromise, the merits of political arguments from all sides, and the status quo. Radical moderatism, as the name suggests, urges radical rejection of contemporary political priorities, while asserting that government-regulated economics cannot be optimized and social issues cannot be morally or ethically decided.

[edit] As A Political Outlook

Radical moderates are often frustrated with contemporary politics in democratic countries because they disagree with political rhetoric that ranges from issue-based partisan sloganeering to extremism. Some think of mainstream politicians confident in their own views on an issue as unreasonably self-righteous. To many radical moderates, variations of libertarianism are the ideal manifestation of radical moderate beliefs. Because governments cannot effectively solve political problems, they should not try.

Other radical moderates cite political reality in advocating the election of moderate candidates. One strategy they hope to implement is to vote in primaries in favor of the most moderate candidate, so that mainsteam parties' moderate candidates have a better chance of winning primaries.
