Chevy & the Titans is a country/rock band from Grand Rapids, Michigan founded in 1970. They sang songs about driving tractor-trailer trucks, such as "Drivin' In My Big Ol' Truck" & "22 Tons of Detroit Diesel Power". Though an obscure group, Chevy & the Titans has been credited with contributing to the tractor-trailer craze of the 1970s.

Andrew Gower is the creator of the game RuneScape and lead developer of Jagex Ltd, a Java-game distributor and creator.

[edit] Details

Andrew Gower is currently 27 years old. From 1990 to 1997, he went to The Becket School, in Nottingham. From 1997 to an unknown date, he went to Fitzwilliam College at the University of Cambridge. He excelled in Psychics, Mathematics, Chemistry, Computer Science, and other things.

From 1990 to 1997, Gower spent his free time programming computer games. During this time he learned multiple programming languages including BASIC, 68000 Assembler, C, and 80 x 86 Assembler. He was a jack of all trades. Gower designed all of his programs, graphics, sound, music, etc. His software was highly acclaimed. He created the game Destruction Imminent for the Atari ST, which was commercially published by Goodman's International. He then continued to computer-programming, and wrote an entire 3D engine and Object Modeling program in C and optimised assembler.

Starting in 1997, Andrew became a freelance JAVA programmer, who was paid to create Java-based games for websites. He ported his entire 3D engine to Java and wrote a Java audio and graphics library which included up to 350 functions.

In the summer of 1999, Andrew worked for GamesDomain, which was a very popular gaming website at the time. He created games for them, and also carried out other programming tasks for them.

Andrew Gower enjoyed/s Karate, Sailing, Programming, Playing the keyboard, reading science-fiction books, playing board games, and kite-flying.

[edit] Creations

Andrew Gower has created several games, as well as other things.

He Ray Traced a couple of pictures. There were a total of 4 ray traced pictures posted on his personal web-page in 98-99.

He made a screen-saver called "Fireworks". It displays pixels being exploded in a fire-work-like fashion on-screen. It was created in Direct-X.

Andrew's big project at the time was DeviousMUD, a multi-user RPG game. It was being coded in Java. It's based on a MUD engine, which he turned graphical instead of text. It was similar to RuneScape, since the game is, after all, its predecessor. It was going to include weapons, armor, quests, monsters, and usual RPG elements. [edit] Medzhibozh, Ukraine

This request for creation has been reviewed. The result is below.
If your request was declined and you want to make changes and try again, copy and paste your article into a new submission and make your changes there.


A town located at 49° 26'N, 27° 25'E within the country of Ukraine, population approximately 4,500.

Also known as Mezibezh, Medzibozh, Mezhibezh, Miedzyboz, Mezhbiz, Mezhybizh, Меджибож, Меджибіж, and many other variants. It has a history that began in the 12th century and was a strategic stronghold during various invasions. Located on the Yuzhny Bug river for which it is named (its name literally means "between the Buzhenka and Bug rivers"), it was a prosperous town and district capital particularly during the 18th century. It is notable as the home of a vibrant Jewish community that spawned the Chasidic movement. Traditionally, Medzhibozh was part of Podolia Province, but since the Soviet rule is now part of Khmelnitsky Oblast.


[edit] History

[edit] Earliest History

Medzhibozh is first mentioned in the annals of Russian history as an estate of the Kievan Rus. It was given to Prince Svyatoslav by the prince of Kiev in the year 1146. In 1148, ownership transfered to Rostislav the son of Yuri Dolgaruky Svyatoslav. By 1360, the territory passed into the hands of the Lithuanians. It suffered numerous attacks, most notably by the Tatars in 1453, 1506, 1516, 1546, 1558, 1566, and 1615. In the 1500s, the territory was controlled by Sieniawska and Potocki Polish noble families. In 1511 work began to replace the wooden palisades with massive stone fortifications, many of which can still be seen today. A dam was built across the Yuzhny Bug river to provide a defensive lake, and a rhomboid castle with four towers was built. The state-of-the-art fortifications made Medzhibozh one of the strongest military sites in the region and thus led to its prosperity in later centuries.

The first records of Jews in Medzhibozh show up in the early 1500s. In these records, various Jews are granted special privileges by the Polish Kings, including a proclamation in 1566 by King Sigizmund II August that Medzhibozh Jews are forever released from paying taxes. The earliest known burial in the Jewish cemetery dates from 1555. In 1571 a census was recorded, listing 95 Russians, 35 Jews, and 30 Poles.

[edit] Khmelnitsky Massacres

In 1648 Bogdan Khmelnitsky's invaded the region. At the time, there were approximately 12,000 residents of Medzhibozh and environs. A Jewish historish lid records that 5,000 Jews were massacred by Khmelnitsky that year in Medzhibozh. Jan Casimir and Khmelnitsky negotiated a treaty in 1649 to end the hostilities, but pogroms continued in 1651 and 1653. By 1661, only a handful of Jews remained in Medzhibozh. The year 1664 marked the last of the cossack pograms. By 1678, only 275 Jews were counted in the region's census.

[edit] Turkish Rule and later Polish Period

Weakened by the cossack uprising, Podolia was invaded and occupied by Turkey in 1672. Medzhibozh became part of the Turkish Ejalet of Kamieniecki. In 1682, Medzhibozh was recaptured by the Poles under Jan Sobiesky.

After Medzhibozh was repatriated from the Turks, it went through what many consider its golden age during the 17th and 18th century. Under the Sieniawski family and later the Czartoryski family, the town prospered. Medzhibozh apparently successfully defended itself from several Haidamak attacks. By the mid 1700s, Medzhibozh was the seat of power in Podolia Province. It had a population of 2,500 Jews, which was more than half of the town's population.

[edit] Russian Rule

Medzhibozh fell into Russian hands during the second partition of Poland in 1792. The Czartoryski family continued to own the town until Prince Adam Czartoryski was forced into exile in 1831. During Russian rule, the seat of power for Podolia moved from Medzhibozh to Kamenets-Podolski. The economy of Medzhibozh deteriorated as the nearby town of Letichev flourished and the railroad bypassed the town to the south.

In the late 1880's through World War I, Medzhibozh was the center of military activity, housing an important garrison within its castle grounds.

[edit] Soviet Rule

After the 1917 Bolshevik Revolution, the territory was occupied by German and Hungarian troops until the end of World War I. Medzhibozh was the scene of numerous pogroms during the Ukrainian Civil War of 1919-1922. The town changed hands many times as different militia units from either the Bolsheviks, Ukrainian Nationalists, Poles, or Whites gained temporary control. What little wealth was left was stripped in these pogroms turning the entire area into ruins.

Under Soviet rule starting 1922, the region's economy improved. Electricity, schools, roads and other infrastructure were built. Several kolkhozi (collective farms) were established near Medzhibozh.

In the early 1930s, pressure from the government to collectivize and the needs of private peasants resulted in severe food shortages that resulted in famines throughout Ukraine.

[edit] World War II

Medzhibozh fell to Nazi forces during Operation Barbarossa on July 8, 1941 with relatively light resistance. It remained in Nazi hands until it was liberated by Soviet troops on March 22, 1944.

Medzhibozh was astride an important east-west supply road that the Nazis wanted to expand into an autobahn-like highway. This road led directly between the city of Proskurov with routes westward into Germany and the city of Vinnitsa with routes to the eastern front. Vinnitsa was the site of Hitler's headquarters bunker in Soviet territory where he personally directed the war between 1942 and 1943.

A Jewish ghetto was established in Medzhibozh and in Letichev to assist Organisation Todt in providing human labor for the road building project. Because of this special road project, Medzhibozh retained its Jews longer than most of the surrounding communities, where Einsatzgruppen units executed entire populations of Jews shortly after Nazi occupation. However, when the road project was completed in the summer of 1942, the Einsatzgruppen units were called in. Three separate mass shootings of Jews occured between August 21, 1942 and October 31, 1942 that eliminated all Jews in the ghetto. Soviet authorities reported that 2,558 Jews were murdered in ravines to the west of town.

[edit] Jewish Culture

Medzhibozh was the center of Jewish culture in this region. Many key rabbinic leaders lived here during the 17th through 20th centuries. The earliest important rabbi to make Medzhibozh home was Rabbi Joel Sirkes (1561-1640), a key figure in Judaism at that time. He lived in Medzhibozh from 1604-12.

Perhaps the most important Jewish rabbi was Rabbi Israel ben Eliezar Baal Shem Tov "Besht" (1698-1760). He lived in Medzhibozh from about 1742 to his death in 1760. His grave can be seen today in the Medzhibozh old Jewish cemetery. The Baal Shem Tov is considered one of the key Jewish personalities of the 18th century who has shaped Judaism into what it is today. His work led to the founding the Chasidic movement, established by his disciples who also lived in Medzhibozh. In Medzhibozh, the Baal Shem Tov was known as a "doktor" and healer to both Jews and non-Jews. He was known to have been given a special tax-free dispensation by the Czartoryski lords and his house shows up on several town censuses.

Many different rabbinic dynasties came from Medzhibozh. There were two fundamentally different rabbinic leaders in the town, those who were Chasidic and those who were not. In general, both groups got along, but the Chasidic leaders believed themselves to have a special connection with God and their followers were cult-like in their devotion to their "rebbe". The non-Chasidic leaders tended to follow a scholarly path and were more responsible for the Jewish institutions, such as observance of Kashrut, the social structure of the town, liaison with the town's nobles, and control of the Jewish court.

Chasidic leaders included Rabbi Boruch of Medzhibozh 1757-1811, the Baal Shem Tov's grandson. Rabbi Boruch was notable for his principle of Malkhuyot (kingship), feeling that his family pedigree with the Baal Shem Tov entitled him to special treatment. He grew rich and powerful at the expense of his followers. Known for his "melancholy" (probably a mental illness) he had a fiery temper and was often irrational. His followers brought in Hershel of Ostropol. Hershel was one of the first documented Jewish comedians and his exploits are legendary within both the Jewish and non-Jewish communities. Hershel is also buried in the old Jewish cemetery in Medzhibozh, though his grave is unmarked.

Another Chasidic leader, Rabbi Abraham Joshua Heshel of Apt (1748-1825)"The Apter Rebbe", made Medzhibozh home from 1813 until his death in 1825. He apparently filled the power vacuum left after Rabbi Boruch had died. The Apter Rebbe is also buried in the old Jewish cemetery in Medzhibozh, very close to the Baal Shem Tov's grave. The Heshel family became one the foremost Chasidic rabbinic dynasties and various descendents remained as Medzhibozh rebbes well into the 20th century. This family took wives several times from the Friedman rabbinic family of the Sadigura Rebbe. The famous American Rabbi and civil rights proponent, Rabbi Abraham Joshua Heschel (1907-1972) is a notable descendant.

The non-Chasidic rabbinic leadership of Medzhibozh was controlled by the Rapoport-Bick dynasty. Rabbi Dov Berish Rapoport (d. 1823) was the first to make Medzhibozh his home. He was the grandson of Rabbi Khaim haCohen Rapoport of Lvov (d. 1771), a notable sage during the mid 18th century. Dov Berish Rapoport's grave can be seen today at the old Jewish cemetery in Medzhibozh. Other rabbis of this dynasty include Rabbi Isaac Bick (1864-1934) who immigrated to America in 1925 and founded a synagogue in Rhode Island. Rabbi Chaim Yekhiel Mikhel Bick (1887-1964) was the last rabbi of any sect to reside in Medzhibozh. He left Medzhibozh for New York in 1925.

[edit] Jewish Institutions in Medzhibozh

Medzhibozh was the home to at least two synagogue buildings and probably numerous small minyanim. One synagogue still stands today but is used for other purposes. It is a stone fortress-like building thought to be Rabbi Sirkes' synagogue. The other synagogue, the Baal Shem Tov's old wooden synagogue, was torn down during World War II to use as firewood for the Jewish ghetto.

Medzhibozh also contains two Jewish cemeteries. The old Jewish cemetery contains the grave of the Baal Shem Tov and other famous and notable Jews. It has turned into something of a tourist attaction, a magnet for Chasidic Jews from all over the world. The new Jewish cemetery has graves from the early 1800s through to the 1980s. A Nazi mass killing site outside of town holds the graves of almost 3,000 Jews in 3 different trenches.

[edit] Sites to See

Today, Medzhibozh is dominated by a castle and fortifications built during the Polish period. Many of these fortifications are deteriorating, however inside the castle is a museum which describes some of the history of the area. The castle itself consists of four towers and overlooks the main road and the dam.

Just outside the castle, the dam and the lake are still in working order. Adjacent to the dam are two old mill buildings that are no longer used but used to be a valuable concession (arenda) during Tsarist and Polish times.

North of town is the old Jewish cemetery, which has turned into a tourist attraction primarily for Chasidic Jews making a pilgrimage to see the Baal Shem Tov's grave. Legend has it that this cemetery remained protected and well-preserved during World War II because the local Ukrainian population remembered the Baal Shem Tov's healing powers during his lifetime and they were afraid of his powerful magic even beyond the grave. The old Jewish cemetery contains a modern building over the graves of the important Jewish dignitaries. Other gravestones in this cemetery are worth visiting as the artwork on many stones shows a level of cultural achievement matching the rise of importance of the town. The oldest burial in this cemetery dates from 1555.

Toward the central western portion of town is the new Jewish cemetery, which is only in fair condition. Here Jews are buried from the early 1800s through modern times.

Outside of town to the west, and adjacent to the Yuzhny Bug river, is the Nazi mass killing site where approximately 3,000 Jews were buried. A monument marks the site. The three ravines that holds the graves are covered in concrete.

[edit] Coup Overthrowing Prince Sihanouk of Cambodia

Carlos2earth 05:05, 28 December 2006 (UTC) During the anticommunist epoch of U.S history much attention was given by the American government to identifying and/or overthrowing foreign governments not party to the anti-communist effort. During the 1950's American Secretary of State John Foster Dulles participated in forming an alliance named Southeast Asia Treaty Organization, which pledged to stop the spread of communism in the area.

Prince Norodom Sihanouk of Cambodia, seeking to keep his country neutral, did not join SEATO, leading Dulles to believe Sihanouk was simpathetic to the communist cause (If they're not with us they're against us.) That set in motion CIA plots against Sihanouk.

During the early American intervention in Vietnam the CIA established contact with the plotting brother-in-law of Sihanouk, Lon Nol. Money was paid Lon Nol to fund a private army called the Khmer Serai, trained by American Special Forces in Vietnam with a "cover" they were there to aid the defense of South Vietnam, as hired mercenaries. The Khmer Serai did participate in combat missions against Viet Cong and NVA, but their primary purpose was to receive US military training.

The recruits were paid with CIA funds, with their American Special Forces advisors as conduits and paymasters. They were supplied the same US weapons and uniforms as other VN indigenous forces, and a red scarf distinguished them as Khmer Serai.

In 1970, as Prince Sihanouk vacationed in France, Lon Nol convened the National Assembly of Cambodia and Sihanouk was deposed, in a reportedly bloodless coup, with Lon Nol backed by his US-trained private army, the Khmer Serai.

In subsequent years Prince Sihanouk has rightfully claimed the coup that caused his overthrow was aided by the U.S. government, and of course American involvement is denied, particulary by Henry Kissenger of the Nixon Administration.

This writer was a member of A341 (Bu Dop), a US Army Special Forces advisory team, which trained and led Khmer Serai elements 1966-67. This is an attempt to correct the historical record of The American War in Southeast Asia.

[edit] Vintry Ward

The London ward of Vintry is bounded by Queen Victoria Street EC4, Cannon Street EC4, Queenhithe EC4 and Cousin Lane EC4. The ward has several large office complexes such as Thames Court, Vintners Place and Senator House and Walbrook Wharf (City Of London Corporation). The Vintners Hall, a Livery Company, is situated by Southwark Bridge. Mansion House Underground station is within the ward as well as a major trunk road, Upper Thames Street, which is managed by Transport for London.

The Ward was so named because of the Vintners (wine-merchants from Bordeaux, who settled on this part of the Thames' bank). They landed their wines here and were obliged to sell what they landed within forty days. In 1320 it was the second richest ward in the City, Dowgate being the richest. In Stow's time it contained four parish churches: St. Martin Vintry; St. James Garlickhithe; St. Michael Paternoster Royal; St. Thomas Apostle. Only St James and St Michael survive.

    Pham Ngu Lao was a native of Phu Ung village, Duong Hao district (currently belonging to An Thi district). He was born in 1255 and died in 1320.

    On that day, Hung Dao Vuong and his entourage were heading for the royal capital. There was a man sitting by the side of the road weaving a basket. When the vanguards approached to clear the way, he continued sitting patiently as if he did not notice anyone. When they used their lances to stab him into his thigh, he continued sitting without stirring. Seeing that, Hung Dao Vuong stopped and asked him questions, he then answered that he was thinking of a sentence in a military manual, so he did not notice anything. Knowing that he was a talented man, Hung Dao Vuong let him sit with him in his palanquin and left for the royal capital. Since then, he became a special guest of Hung Dao Vuong, who had fondness for him and married off his daughter to him. He also recommended Pham Ngu Lao to the royal court. In the two resistance wars against the Nguyen invaders (1285-1288), Pham Ngu Lao made many contributions.

Under the reign of Minh Tong, in 1318, Chiem Thanh army staged an invasion; he led the army to defeat the enemy. He died in 1320 at the age of 66. The king ordered a five-day recess of court to mourn over him.

    • Chris Boulton, English, Lead Vocals
    • Ryan Sutton, Canadian, Lead Guitars and Backing Vocals
    • Jamie Pope, English, Bass and Backing Vocals
    • Tom Pope, English, Rythym or Lead Guitars and Backing Vocals
    • Yann Clavaizole, French, Percussions


    Releases by the group include:

    • Double Demons (Single + B-Side, 2004)
    • The Little House (4 Song EP, 2005-2006)
    • Nothing Ventured, Nothing Gained (Album To Be Released, 2007)
    • Soundtrack to Hellphone (Film Sountrack To Be Released, 2007)


    The founding members first met in September 2002. Originally the group played without a drummer, yet had a natural percussion to them. After playing two concerts with the existing lineup, on oppurtunity presented itself in the form of Yann, an aspiring drummer. The group decided to let him in. After joining the band, it was revealed that Yann was in fact the son of Producer/Musician Denis Clavazole (of Daniel Darc and Jean-Louis Murat fame). The expertise of Denis came as help to the band, serving as a guiding light to help focus the band's creative element, without ever controling it. Over the span of 4 years the band wrote over 40 songs, recorded a single, an EP, and an album. Early March 2007 should mark the release of The Elderberries debut album, Nothing Ventured, Nothing Gained, featuring artwork by Chuck Sperry. The band is appreciated not only for its relentless rock, but also it's youth (The majority of the band being only 16 at the release of its first single), and their charm. Other aspects of the band may be found to be appealing, such as the presence of family (Jamie and Tom being brothers, much like Angus and Malcom Young of AC/DC), and the seemingly endless supply of good songwriting.

    The Elderberries have also worked on some soundtracks, notably a Quicksilver DVD, and recently the next big French release, Hellphone. The Elderberries were also voted 30 points in advance in the Printemps de Bourges selection of 2005-2006. They have played over 80 concerts to date (December 2006), including numerous concerts with The Datsuns and Kill The Young (the latter being very good friends with the band).
