Childhood:

He had the best childhoods possible. He was reared in a lower-middle class neighborhood (in a suburb north of Atlanta, Georgia) and had unconditionally loving parents who taught him priceless lessons, including good morals, human decency, appreciation and respect for others, and the importance of not being materialistic or falling for money. He played sports, mostly baseball, nearly everyday ever since he was 7 years old. Those sweltering summer days were the best days he ever had. he learned a lot from the early eras.

Early Education:

He worked pretty hard in primary school (elementary and middle-school). A few friends called him the human-calculator. He liked Math a lot.

Secondary Education:

He started to slack off in high school; he cared more for having fun with my friends. He aimed for A's, but seldom stressed too much over low B's, and barely graduated in the top 33% of my class (132/420).

College Education:

He's started to work hard in my academics. he has decent grades, and has developed a higher, genuine care to learn. Moreover, he's recently been trying to have more fun outside of the classroom, and consequently has learned a good deal more about the human race.

His experience at Florida Tech definitely has been memorable.
