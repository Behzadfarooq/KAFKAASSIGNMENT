[edit] Railway parade

What we have here, ladies and gentlemen, is an suburban Australian street with no claims made for any notability whatsoever. As such, it's either show those claims, or go. Grutness...wha? 00:28, 26 September 2006 (UTC)

  • Delete per nom.UberCryxic 00:40, 26 September 2006 (UTC)
  • Strong Delete — Kill kill. That is in no way notable what so ever! Delete per nom —— Eagle (ask me for help) 01:49, 26 September 2006 (UTC)
  • Delete, unless article establishes how the street is special (e.g. on a Monopoly board, major provincial route, historical route), for all I know this is a cul-de-sac.-- danntm T C 03:14, 26 September 2006 (UTC)
    • Comment A quick look on whereis.com.au (unfortunately, I can't seem to be able to link you to the exact page) seems to indicate that it's a suburban street that runs about 1km (at most) along a railway line. Not a cul-de-sac, but not even a main road either. Lankiveil 05:27, 28 September 2006 (UTC).
  • Delete -- unless some claim to notability is established. - Longhair 03:56, 26 September 2006 (UTC)
  • Delete - this is currently a substub of one sentence length. A search of sources available online through the ACT public library system indicate that it cannot be easily expanded. There are a number of Railway Parades in Australia and it seems that this street in Pascoe Vale, Victoria is one of the less notable ones. Capitalistroadster 04:15, 26 September 2006 (UTC)
