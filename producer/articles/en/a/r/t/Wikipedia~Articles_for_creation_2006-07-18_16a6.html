[edit] Carter High School

This request for creation has been reviewed. The result is below.


Carter High School is a relatively young school located in the Kwa-Zulu Natal Province of South Africa. Having opened its doors for the first time in January 1977 with 5 teachers and 53 pupils, it has grown into a large well respected school with an enrolment of approximately 1200 pupils and an academic staff of around 50. The learner population is drawn from more than 20 primary schools and comprises a great number of cultural backgrounds.

The school derives its name from the Honourable Mr Justice T.F. Carter who sat on the Natal bench. The schools motto is, "Justitia et Veritas" ("Justice and Truth"), which links the schools present aims with the ideals for which Judge Carter stood.

At Carter, a holistic approach to education is underpinned by the principles of Justice and Truth within a caring environment where achieving a balance between the needs of the individual and those of society is a vital goal. Carters aim is to develop informed, responsible individuals capable of making decisions and interacting successfully within the context of their homes, families, workplaces and society.

They are Rectifier operation for conversion of A.C to D.C Inverter operation for conversion of D.C to A.C i. RECTIFIER OPERATION For conversion of A.C to D.C we may use uncontrolled rectifiers i.e. diode rectifiers or controlled rectifiers i.e. thyristor rectifiers. In most of the cases uncontrolled rectifiers are used because of economy. IGBT inverters are used if we want to use the regenerative power from inverter circuit to feed back to supply terminals.

ii. INVERTER OPERATION For conversion of D.C to controlled A.C we use voltage source inverter circuit in general. Now days mostly IGBT are exclusively used for inverter circuits. D.C link capacitor is used in the middle stage in order to smooth the D.C input to the inverter circuit.

Rectifier is a device which converts A.C to D.C .In most of the frequency converter circuits diode bridge rectifier is used for rectifier operation because of its easy operation and less cost. For understanding the rectifier operation easily we consider single phase bridge rectifier as shown bellow. During positive half cycle diode D1 and D4 are forward biased. During negative half cycle diode pair D3 and D4 are forward biased and they will conduct current through the load during negative half cycle. On 18 June 1716, he was cr. BARON CONINGSBY OF CONINGSBY, Co. Lincoln, both being with a spec.rem. An ardent supporter of the revolution of 1688, and throughout his life resolutly resisted, somerimes with more zeal than discression the aims of the Jacobite fashion. When William crossed to Ireland, Coningsby was with him, and when the King was wounded at the battle of the Boyne, he was by his master's side. He was appointed joint reciever and paymaster-general of the forces employed in the reduction of Ireland, and from 1690 to 1692 he acted as junior of the three lord justices of Ireland, together with Lord Sydney, the treaty of Limerick, so it is said, having been arranged through his skill. His political opponents accused him of having used his position to gratify his greed. The embezzlement of stores, the appropriation of the estates of rebels, the sale of pardons, and dealings in illicit trade were among the offences imputed to him; but such among the ofences were of slight moment so long the royal influence was at his back.

Through the kings favour he was created Baron Coningsby of Clanbrassil in Ireland on 1 April 1692, sworn as privy councillor on 13 April 1693, and pardoned under the great seal in May l694 for any transgressions which he might have committed whilst in office in Ireland. From 1695 to his death he held the honourable office of chief steward of the city of Hereford, an appointment which involved him in a duel with Lord Chandos, another claimant of the post, but no mischief was done.

In April 1697 he received a grant under the privy seal of several of the crown manors in England, and in October 1698 he was again created the vice-treasurer and paymaster of the forces in Ireland. During Queen Annes reign he acted consistently with the whigs. but his services received slight acknowledgement even when his friends were in office. All that Godolphin did was to write a civil letter or two complimenting Lord Coningsby on his judgement and experience in parliamentary affairs, and it was not until October 1708 that Coningsby was sworn of Annes privy council. He was one of the managers of Sacheverells trial, and, like most of time prominent whigs, he lost his seat in parliament through the tory reaction which ensued.

With the accession of George I he resumed his old position in public life, and once more basked in court favour. He was included in the select committee of twenty- one appointed to inquire into the negotiations for the treaty of Utrecht, and, according to Prior, was one of the three most inquisitive members of that body. As a result of their investigations, the impeachment of Bolingbroke was moved by Wallpole, that of Harley by Coningsby a family feud had long exited between the two Herefordshire families of Harley and Coningsby and Ormondes by Stanhope. Two years later Harley was unanimously discharged, but this concord of opinion was only obtained by Coningsby and some others withdrawing from the proceedings.

For his zeal in behalf of the Hanoverian succession he was well re-warded. The lord-lieutenancy of Herefordshire was conferred on him in November 1714, and in the following month he obtained the same pre-eminence in Radnorshire. A barony in the English peerage was granted to him on 18 June 1715, and he was raised to the higher dignity of Earl Coningsby on 30 April 1719. In the later years of his life Coningsby was involved in perpetual trouble, he was a widower, without any male heir, and with innumerable lawsuits.

For some severe reflections on Lord Harcourt, the lord chancellor, in connection with these legal worries, he was, as Swift notes in his diary, committed to the Tower on 27 Feb. 1720. After having having been in ill health for some time, he died at the family seat of Hampton, near Leominster, on 1 May 1729.

By his first wife, Barbara Gorges, whom he married in February 1674, and from whom he was divorced, he had four daughters and three sons, and his grandson by this marriage succeeded to the Irish barony, but died without issue on 18 Dec. 1729. his second wife, whom he married in April 1639, was Lady Frances Jones, daughter of Richard, Earl of Ranelagh, by whom he had one son, Richard, who died at Hampton on 2 April 1708 when two years old, choked by a cherrystone; and two daughters, Margaret and Frances. The second countess was buried at Hope-under- Dinmore on 23 Feb. 1714-15, aged 42; and Lord Coningsby was buried in the same church in 1729, under a handsome marble monument, on which the childs death is depicted in striking realism.

Her issue male, John, the only child of this daughter, Margaret, countess of Coningsby, by her husband, Sir Michael Newton, died an infant, the victim of an accidental fall, said to have been caused though the fright of its nurse at seeing an ape, and on the mothers death in 1761 the title became extinct. Coningsbys troubles in law arose from his purchase of the manors of Leominster and Marden. [edit] Fire Dance

Loie Fullers dancing progressed from Vaudeville skirt dancing to inspirational abstract art when she extended her skirts with bamboo rods. Fire Dance, or Le Danse de Feu, was one of the first dances with these rods and it was also one of the first to utilize the new electric lights to their full effect. Fire Dance began as the Dance of the Sun in her 1895 production of Salome. It premiered as a solo in 1896 to a stunned and mesmerized audience.

Part of what captured the audiences attention was Loes abstract (or what we now call Modern) movement, but the other part was the way in which she utilized the new electric stage lights. Before this time, Ballets and Operas we performed with gas front lights to a still illuminated house. They also used all kinds of lavish sets or at least painted backdrops. Loe did away with any kind of colorful background and hung the stage in black velvet. She also turned out the house lights. From the darkness she would appear, first as a ghostly creature then as an illuminated jewel in the center of the stage.

The trap door was removed and replaced with a pane of glass. From below this, two of the new electric lights shined upwards through the glass. Another light came from directly above the dancer, while still others came from the sides at all angles. Doing away with the standard front light only created a depth to her movement the audience was not used to. Now add to that her work with color.

Loe was the first to create and use color scrollers. She took large circles of glass and painted on gelatin of various reds, oranges, yellows, greens, blues and violets. These disks were then mounted at the front of the light and were turned by the hand. For Fire Dance she had over 14 electricians manning a light each and they would take the cue to change to the next color from Loies brother, who was her master electrician. Stationed at one of the lights below Loe, he would take a cue from her foot, then turn his wheel slowly to the next blue or purple. Seeing this change the electricians at the shins would rotate to their next color, and so on up to the final man hanging from the cat walk directly above Loe. These effects in the pitch black of the stage and house, where what made her living fire such a success.

This success was due in part to the Art Nuevo movement of that time in Paris. The idea of a women becoming fire was directly in line with the link of humans and nature. She inspired artists like Toulouse Lautrec, Jules Cheret and Pierre Roche. August Rodin did a number of studies of dancers, based on inspiration and coaxing from Loe. And Loe taught and mentored Isadora Duncan before Duncan went on to be given the crown of mother of Modern dance.

[edit] Constable Robin Cameron, RCMP

Constable Robin Cameron, 29, originally of Beardy's-Okemasis First Nation Reserve, Saskatchewan, along with her partner, Constable Marc Bourdages, 26, originally of Saint-Eustache, Quebec, responded to a reported assault July 7th, 2006 near the small community of Spiritwood, located in central Saskatchewan. Curtis Alfred Dagenais, 41, was involved in a dispute with his mother and sister, apparently concerning inheritance of family property. Dagenais feld upon Cameron and Bourdages arrival and led two RCMP vehicles on a 15 km chase down country roads. The chase ended in the area south of Maldrid, Saskatchewan. Dagenais fired several high-powered shotgun bullets into the front windshield of the lead car containing Constables Robin Cameron and Marc Bourdages. Both officers sustain tremendous head injuries. The officer in the second car was able to return fire as Dagenais turned his shotgun on her, however Dagenais succeeded in slipping into the surrounding dense bush as she contacted aid for the wounded Bourdages and Cameron.

Cameron received care at Royal University Hospital in Saskatoon, Saskatchewan for nine days, however the severe brain damage she had received, coupled with her past stated wishes, compelled her family to end further medical intervention. Constable Robin Cameron passed away around 11 PM, July 15th, 2006. Her partner, Constable Marc Bourdages past away around 1 AM the following morning. Both left behind young children, devastated families, and sent shock, anger and grief across Canada. Cameron's father, a retired RCMP officer himself, issued a family statement thanking the community, RCMP and Canada for the tremendous support he and his family have received since the tragedy. He went on to account how Cameron was a role model to her family and community having achieved her childhood dream of becoming a member of the Royal Canadian Mounted Police. Many felt that her achievement was especially exceptional as she had endured difficult teenage years during which she became a young mother and temporarily dropped out of high school. Suspect Dagenais remains at large and is now the focus of an intense manhunt and a nationwide warrant for his capture. It is believed that Dagenais is still concealing himself in the dense brushland surrounding Maldrid, Saskatchewan. RCMP have deployed tracking dogs, however Dagenais is hiding in an area well-known to him.

The deaths of Cameron and Bourdages have prompted statements of grief and support from both the Premier of Saskatchewan and the Prime Minister of Canada, in addition to several high ranking RCMP officers. The chief training depot of the Royal Canadian Mounted Police is located in Regina, Saskatchewan, both Cameron and Bourdages trained there, and the depot experienced a bittersweet graduation ceremony for a new class of recruits the day the statment of Cameron and Bourdages' deaths were announced. The most recent tragedy to befall the RCMP prior was the deaths of several young RCMP officers ambushed by a sniper in Alberta. [edit] Kill the Hippies

Kill the Hippies is a Kent, Ohio-based punk rock band founded in 1993. They have completed many national tours and continually turn down major label interest. They distinctly play with the "Ohio Sound," made popular by bands like Devo and Rubber City Rebels. They continue those traditions of song craft and social commentary with typical Midwestern cynicism, but the strenght and importance of the group is in the fact that, despite critical acclaim and interest from major record labels, they continue to perform and record without corporate funding or contracts. One could see them perform in a major arena or a rural VFW hall.

[edit] Restriction Landmark Genomic Scanning

Restriction Landmark Genomic Scanning (or RLGS) is a genome analysis method that allows for rapid simultaneous scanning of thousands of landmarks. RLGS employs direct labeling of DNA, which is first cut by a specific series of restriction enzymes, and then labeled by a radioactive isotope (usually Phosphorus 32). A two-dimension electrophoresis process is then employed, yielding high-resolution results. The radioactive second-dimension gel is then allowed to expose a large sheet of film, which is then developed, yielding a visual representation of the results in the form of an Autoradiograph. These auto-rads can be examined against each other, revealing any changes in gene expression that lead to visual differences in the film. Each autoradiograph contains thousands of spots, each corresponding to a labeled DNA restriction landmark.

RLGS becomes very useful when doing full-genome scans, and can effectively do the work of thousands of polymerase chain reactions at once. It readily detects alterations deviating from normal, and thus is exceptionally effective in identifying hyper/hypomethylation in tumors, deletions or amplifications of genes, or simply changes in gene expression throughout the development of an organism.
