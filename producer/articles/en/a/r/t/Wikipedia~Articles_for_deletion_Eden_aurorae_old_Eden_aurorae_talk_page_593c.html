At present I can find very little to indicate notability of the guild and the machinima series having been documented in third-party sources. A Google search for "Eden aurorae" Warcraft guild returns just 27 unique results. Likewise a search for "Tales of the Past" machinima returns just 32 unique results. These could be considered very poor showings for an online game guild and a purely internet-distributed machinima series. Please note Wikipedia's policies require a certain degree of notability, and most importantly that information can be verified through reliable third-party sources. Furthermore, judging by your choice of username I would guess you are a member of this guild; Wikipedia strongly discourages editors from working on articles about bands/clubs/groups/companies they are directly involved in as a conflict of interest; if something is notable enough to warrant a Wikipedia article, it will be noticed by someone unconnected to them and an article created. ~Matticus TC 11:51, 5 February 2007 (UTC)

The notability of Tales of the Past II is not in question here. The members of the guild may have been involved in producing a popular machinima film, but that does not by extension make the guild itself notable. The fact that you and the other guild members know information about the guild is immaterial; Wikipedia requires information to be sourced from independent, non-trivial published sources (once again, the conflict of interest issue comes up too). But I will take this and the related articles to AfD for a broader discussion. ~Matticus TC 12:54, 5 February 2007 (UTC)
