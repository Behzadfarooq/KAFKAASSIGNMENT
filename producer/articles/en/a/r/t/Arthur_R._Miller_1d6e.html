Arthur R. Miller
Born 1937
Occupation Law Professor

Arthur R. Miller is the Bruce Bromley Professor of Law at Harvard Law School. Miller is co-author, with Professor Jack H. Friedenthal and Professor Mary Kay Kane, of the book Civil Procedure (West Publishing 1985), a major reference work for lawyers and law students in the United States.

For the past several years he has also been a visiting Professor at New York University School of Law. Miller was also a legal advisor for ABC's Good Morning America and Court TV,[citation needed] and the host of a weekly television show titled Miller's Court on WCVB-TV.

Miller graduated from University of Rochester in 1955 and Harvard Law School in 1958. After graduating from law school, he worked for three years at the New York law firm Cleary Gottlieb before beginning his academic career. He has taught at the University of Minnesota, The University of Michigan and has been an advisor and supplementary lecturer at Concord Law School [1] (online). He currently teaches at Harvard Law School and New York University School of Law.[citation needed]

[edit] Trivia

Miller claims to be the infamous Rudolph Perini,[1] a pseudonym author Scott Turow used in his autobiographical account of Harvard Law School, One L, to describe a particularly abrasive professor.[2]
