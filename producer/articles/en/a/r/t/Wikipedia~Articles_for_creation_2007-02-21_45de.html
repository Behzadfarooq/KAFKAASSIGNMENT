Markham Woods Middle School is located in seminole county, FL. It has a student body of approx. 850 students. The school logo is the mustangs and was voted for by the students. The school has many standard electives such as band, P.E./Gym, choire, art and computers. There are three major groups of students that they can be placed. The Gifted program, the Anvanced program and regular academics.

[edit] Grades

Markham Woods has three grades, 6th, 7th, and 8th. Each grade has an assistant principal.

[edit] Athletics

Although it does not have high school sports such as lacrosse, football and basketball, Markham Woods has competive athletic programs such as volleyball, cross country, and track. Volleyball is a year-round program while the track and cross country programs are by semester.

[edit] Cross-Country Running

The cross country running program starts up for all middle schools in Seminole county, FL in the late August area. It ends in Late october. Markham woods competes with other middle schools in the county such as Rock Lake, Millennium, Sanford, Indian Trails and Greenwood Lakes middle schools. Events in cross country are the mile, 2 miles and relay of a mile.

[edit] Track

Track starts in late Febuary or early March. The program competes with the same schools as the cross country program. Track has events such as the 100 meter dash, 200 meter dash, 400 meter dash, and the 400 meter relay.

[edit] Volleyball

Volleyball is the standard rules and plays with the same opponents as track and cross country. The program runs year round, the only school program in athletics to do so.

[edit] Administration

The principle of Markham woods is Mr. Roger Gardner. There are three assistant principals, one for each grade level. Mr. Rodney Bellamy is the assistant principal for the 6th grade. Mrs. Tina Johnson is the assistant principal for the 7th grade and Mrs. Margaret Gunderson is the assistant principal for the 8th grade.

[edit] Construction

The beginning of construction was in October of 2005. The School was finished and ready to open its doors around June of 2006. The first day the school was operating like a regular school would was on July 31,2006. This was just in time for the 2006-2007 school year to start.

"The Paul Reddy Show" is the name of a single-episode talk show parody hosted by a fictional person, Paul Reddy. The single episode (the show was intended as a one-time parody, and by no stretch of the imagination a real show) was written by musician John Mayer and his friend Brian Sack (who played Paul Reddy), and appeared first only the DVD side of Mayer's DualDisc release of the album Heavier Things in 2004. Mayer has since posted the clip to his personal blog.

Mayer created the show because he needed material for his DualDisc release and, as he put it, he was "Not really a fan of putting poorly-produced iMovies on the back of million-dollar budget major label albums" (Source Two). In the show, "Paul" and John discuss Mayer's music writing process, his growing fame, and some of Mayer's personal thoughts. The subtlety and deadpan quality of Brian Sack's delivery as "Paul" bungles the interview are the source of the show's humor, and it is generally silly while making no appearance of being so.

Archived discussion follows below. Please do not modify it.
This request for creation has been reviewed. The result is below.


Wembdon is a rural village near Bridgwater, in Somerset England.

History

Wembdon was listed in the Domesday book of 1086 as having "5 villagers and 6 smallholders with with four ploughs". The name Wembdon is believed to mean "Huntsman's Hill", refering to Wembdon Hill itself where Saxon* burials have been discovered. Wembdon Hill is also the site of St. Johns Well, which was renowned for it's healing powers from the 15th centuary onwards. St. Georges Church, Wembdon was granted by William Testard, lord of Wembdon masnor, to St John's Hospital, Bridgwater in 1284. The church was badly damaged by fire in March 1868, and as the certificate for fire insurance had expired, had to be rebuilt with significant local aid.

  • These are in fact belived to be British burials which date to the Saxon period.

Wembdon Today

Wembdon is now home to the aforementioned Anglican church, a small shop (now combined with the post office), a pub (The Cottage Inn), a small Garage (Wembdon Motors) and a fruit juice processing and packaging plant (Gerber Foods). A Church of England VC Primary school (Wembdon st. Georges) can also be found in the village. In 2002 the village was bisected by a Distributor road, built to ease traffic congestion in Bridgwater town centre. The road had been part of local town planning since the 1980's, and building proceeded despite some local resistance.

Local Events

Wembdon Village Day- Wembdon is host to a Village Day which occurs towards the end of August every year, centred around competitions involving arts and crafts, fruit and vegetables, baking, jam making and wine making and many other events. It takes place on the playing field and nearby Parish centre building. A large car boot sale is also in place on the day and local radio station BCR FM conduct a live show from a stage during the event.

Guy Fawkes Night- On the saturday closest to the 5th of November a bonfire is set up on the Playing field, and a fireworks display is shown over the evening.

Christmas- The local Church hosts a Christmas day service in addition to a carol service. The Cottage Inn also reguarly stages Christmas Eve event of Santa Spotting.

Bishi Bashi (ﾋﾞｼﾊﾞｼ) is a series of Konami arcade games and Sony PlayStation games. All games in the series comprise playing through a wide variety of competitive minigames against other players. The arcade games support 1 to 6 players[1] and the PlayStation game allows 1 to 8 players; the game will provide computer opponents if there are not enough players.

[edit] Arcade games

The arcade games released so far include[2]:

  • Bishi Bashi Championship Mini Game Senshuken in 1996
  • Super Bishi Bashi Champ[1] and Handle Champ in 1998
  • Hyper Bishi Bashi, Gachaga Champ and Step Champ in 1999
  • Hyper Bishi Bashi Champ[3] and Anime Champ in 2000
  • Salaryman Champ in 2001
  • Great Bishi Bashi Champ in 2002

The arcade game controls are very simple; each player has a red, blue and green button, positioned left, right and centre respectively. Each minigame explains its controls before play commences.

[edit] PlayStation games

Some of the minigames from the arcade games have also been ported to the Sony PlayStation and released under the name Bishi Bashi Special (ビシバシスペシャル)[4]

  • Bishi Bashi Special was released in 1998 in Japan and released in Europe in 2000[5][6]
  • Bishi Bashi Special 2 was released in 1999 in Japan only[7]
  • Bishi Bashi Special 3 was released in 2000 in Japan only[8]

Unlike the arcade game, the ported minigames do not always use the red, green and blue buttons of the PlayStation joypad; "button bashing" minigames allow the player to use the analog sticks or the shoulder buttons as well as the regular buttons.

[edit] Examples of minigames

Hyper pie throwing
A bride, with the groom holding her train, must run from the altar of a church down the aisle to where the wedding guests are seated. The faster the player mashes two of the game buttons, the faster the bride runs. Once she reaches the front row, the player presses and holds the third game button, which allows them to pick an angle for the pie's trajectory. Letting go of the button unleashes the pie, which flies several rows back and hits one of the wedding guests. All wedding guests, except the pied victim, stand and applaud. The game is judged on how many rows the pie flew, and the winner is whomever threw their pie the furthest.
Shake the can into outer space!
Players mash all the game buttons to shake a soft drink can. After a fixed period of time, all cans are placed on Space Shuttle launchpads, and launched into space with their pent-up fizz. The winner is whoever's can flies the furthest from Earth. If the player is particularly good, their can will be powerful enough to slam into the moon.
