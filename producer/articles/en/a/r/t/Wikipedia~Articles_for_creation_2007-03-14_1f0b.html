Josh Penry is State Senator from Grand Junction, Colorado, who is widely recognized as an up-and-comer in the Republican Party in Colorado. Penry, 31, served in the Colorado House of Representatives for one term, and then jumped into a hotly contested race for the Colorado Senate against a popular former State Representative. Penry ran away with the once-competitive Senate primary in a landslide, and was eventually elected by a wide margin in the General Election. Penry is the youngest member of the Colorado Senate.

Penry has been identified as a rising political star by an array of publications, including the National Journal. The Rocky Mountain News called Penry and State Representatives Rob Witwer, Cory Gardner and Matt Knoedler the "Rock Stars" of the Colorado GOP.

Penry is a former standout quarterback at Mesa State College. He was named the National Scholar Athlete of the Year by the American Football Coaches Association and the Burger King Company. The award, won the prior year by Peyton Manning, came with a $135,000 endowment award to Mesa State.

Before his election to the Legislature, Penry served as a top aide to Congressman Scott McInnis.

[edit] The Federal League - An Ohio High School Athletic Conference

The Federal League was originally created back in 1964 as an outgrowth of the Stark County AA League. Member schools compete for conference titles in nineteen different sports, including nine in the fall season (football, boys & girls cross country, boys & girls golf, boys & girls soccer, girls volleyball and girls tennis), five in the winter season (boys & girls basketball, boys & girls swimming/diving and wrestling), and five in the spring season (boys & girls track, baseball, softball, and boys golf). Boys and girls bowling will become a league sport in the 2007-2008 school year, while ice hockey, gymnastics, field hockey and boys volleyball are among other sports that various schools participate in, but not on the conference level.

[edit] Member Schools for Football

Current members of the Federal League, in all sports but swimming/diving (Fitch does not have a swim team) are the Austintown Fitch Falcons (member since 2004), Boardman Spartans (member since 2004), GlenOak Golden Eagles (member since 1975), Hoover Vikings (member since 1968), Jackson Polar Bears (charter member) Lake Blue Streaks (member since 1987), McKinley Bulldogs (member since 2004), and Perry Panthers (charter member).

Past members of the Federal League included Navarre Fairless (1964-1974), Canton Glenwood (1964-1974), Alliance Marlington (1964-1984), Magnolia Sandy Valley (1964-1967), Canton South (1964-1986, 1988-1989), Louisville (1968-1989), Canton Oakwood (1968-1974), Alliance (1983-2002), New Philadelphia (1988-1996), Canton Timken (1988-1994) and Wooster (1988-2002). Note that Canton Glenwood and Oakwood combined to form Canton GlenOak prior to the 1975-1976 school year.

[edit] All-Sports Trophy

The Federal League All-Sports Trophy has been awarded to the member school with the best cumulative athletics program each year, since the 1987-88 school year. In the event one school wins the trophy three years in a row, it is considered retired, and that school gets to physically keep the trophy. Otherwise, the physically trophy rotates each year to the winning school. The initial winner of the trophy in 1987-88 was Perry, while GlenOak won it the two following years. Since then, the only two schools to have won the trophy are Jackson and Hoover, Jackson having won it in '91, '92, '93 (retired), '94, '96, '97, '99, '00, and '01 (retired), and Hoover having won it in '95, '98, and every year since 2002, retiring it after the 2003-2004 season. It is probable that Hoover will again retire it after the 2006-2007 school year, as they have a likely prohibitive lead on second place Jackson after the 2006-2007 winter season.

BIO

Nicky Kalliongis is an accomplished musician and music technician in addition to being a seasoned music industry professional with expertise in the area of Artist and Repertoire. [edit] Maximum Concurrency

Archived discussion follows below. Please do not modify it.

Maximum Concurrency is a term used to describe a desire to complete as many tasks as possible in the shortest amount of time. For example, if there are ten tasks to perform and nine of those tasks take 1 minute, and 1 of the tasks takes 10 hours, completing the tasks in a plan optimizing toward maximum concurrency would complete the nine one minute tasks, and then begin working on the 10 hour task, regardless of the order the tasks were assigned. This means within the first hour, 9 tasks will be completed. Without regard to maximum concurrency, if the first task in the list to be completed is the 10 hour task, it will take 10 hours and 8 minutes to complete nine tasks.

[edit] Juan Carlos Quintero-Herencia

Born in Santurce, Puerto Rico in 1963, Quintero Herencia is a poet, essayist and literary critic. Member and co-editor of the Poetry journal Filo de juego (1982-1987) the Instituto de Cultura Puertorriqueña published, in 2002, his early poetry under a volume entitled El hilo para el marisco/Cuaderno de los envíos (2002). This volume received the Puerto Rican Pen Club Poetry Prize in 2004. Quintero Herencia has published other collections of poetry such as La caja negra (1996, Isla Negra) and Libro del sigiloso (2007, Terranova). Quintero Herencia poetry is complex and contains an intense desire for experimentation and baroque imagery. In Quintero's recent poetry a concern for the specific nature of the political gesture of poetry is a recurrent topic. As an essayist Quintero-Herencia established himself with Fulguración del espacio: Letras e imaginario institucional de la Revolución cubana (1960-1971) (Beatriz Viterbo, 2002), a contextual and critical reading of the crucial debates which defined the Cuban intellectual field during the 1960’s. His second book, La máquina de la salsa: Tránsitos del sabor (Ediciones Vértigo, 2005) is a comprehensive critical balance on the Salsa phenomenon. As a Professor, Quintero-Herencia teaches undergraduate and graduate courses in Latin American and Caribbean literatures and cultures at the University of Maryland, College Park (USA). He also teaches Literary Theory. Before going to the University of Maryland, he taught at the University of Puerto Rico, Río Piedras and Brown University. [edit] Sources
