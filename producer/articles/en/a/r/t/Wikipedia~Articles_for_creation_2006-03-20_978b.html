Three years following, he experimented in fluid mechanics research, until he made baby steps into the world of computer animation. [edit] Kim Mohan

Kim Mohan is an American editor. He edited Dragon (magazine) from 1984-1994. He also edited Hugo Gernsback's Amazing Stories (magazine) from 1991 through 1995, and then picked up the job again later. Amazing Stories (magazine) finally folded in 2000. From 1992 through 2001, he received Locus Poll Award nominations varying between "Best Editor" or "Best Magazine or Fanzine". He also received Honorable Mention as "Best Professional Editor" for 1993 and 1994 by the Hugo Awards.

Kim Mohan wrote the Cyborg Commando trilogy (Planet in Peril, Chase Into Space and The Ultimate Prize) in collaboration. The History Channel "In Search of History: The Truth About Science Fiction" features Kim Mohan, Harlan Ellison, and Larry Niven in a discussion about literature and movies.

Kim Mohan was also editor of Dragon (magazine) when Dave Trampier abruptly vanished. Better known as Tramp, the cartoonist drew Wormy for 123 issues of the magazine.

[edit] ADC Theatre, Cambridge, England

The ADC Theatre is owned by the University of Cambridge's student theatre society, the Amateur Dramatic Club, which was founded in 1855 by F C Burnand. Since 1973 the University has managed the theatre. During University terms, the theatre presents a variety of student drama, dance, comedy and music with a weekly two-show policy of a 'main show' at (usually) 19:45 followed by a shorter 'late show' at 23:00. Out of term the theatre is used by local amateur groups. Main shows generally open on a Tuesday evening, late shows on a Wednesday.

The theatre's permanent staff consist a Manager & Licensee, a technical housekeeper and a publicity housekeeper. All are University employees and they generally only serve one or two years in post, often going on to pursue a professional theatre career afterwards. All backstage roles are undertaken by volunteers.

The theatre boasts a 228 seat auditorium with a proscenium arrangement, full height counterweight flying throughout and an impressive array of lighting, sound and scenic equipment. In 2005 and 2006 the theatre underwent a refurbishment programme to provide better front-of-house facilities, full disabled access and better office accommodation.

The choice of repertoire is decided by the theatre's Executive Committee once a term; the Amateur Dramatic Club is guaranteed some slots.

The theatre's website - which includes online booking - is at www.adctheatre.com

The University's Amateur Dramatic Club website is at www.cuadc.org

[edit] Woodchurch, Kent

The village of Woodchurch is located eight miles southwest of Ashford, Kent on the B2067 Tenterden to Hamstreet road. Woodchurch is located in an area of rich farmland and has a traditional wooden smock mill which is open as a tourist attraction in summer. There is also a village life museum located on Susan's Hill.

The village itself surrounds the triangular green, a popular venue for cricket matches. There is a school, surgery, post office, general store, butcher's shop, garage and three pubs in the village as well as a model shop.

One mile out of Woodchurch is the South of England rare breeds centre.

[edit] Prasat

[edit] Sources

A prasat is a tower sanctuary of Khmer origin. The word prasat often refers to many collective structures of a temple. When it was adopted by the Thais it because an exclusive symbol of royalty and religion, with a cruciform pattern and topped off with a prang. For example, the Golden Meru traditional funeral pyre is in the form of a Prasat
