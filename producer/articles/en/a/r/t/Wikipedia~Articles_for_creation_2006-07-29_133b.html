[edit] Itchenor Fortnight

Also known as Junior Fortnight, this is an annual sailing regatta run by Itchenor Sailing Club. It lasts a duration of 2 weeks, usually the last week of July and the first week of august. There are 10 events, run in either the morning or the afternoon, and four classes of boat take part: Mirrors (4 Events), Toppers(2 Events), Lasers(2 Events)and 420's(2 Events). The races themselves take place in Chichester Harbour. In 2006 there were over 225 entries.

[edit] Royce Hall

Located at the University of California, Los Angeles, Royce Hall was completed in 1929 and was one of the campus's first four main buildings, all of which are built in the Lombardian Romanesque style. Designed by architect David Allison, Royce Hall was specifically modeled after the Sant' Ambrogio Basilica in Milan, Italy. Named after American philosopher Josiah Royce, the building contains a concert hall, classrooms, offices, and meeting rooms. The famous two-towered facade is synonymous with UCLA and has at various times been incorporated into the University's logos.

The 1,833-seat concert hall has renowned acoustics - Isaac Stern said they were second only to Carnegie Hall's, and the Los Angeles Philharmonic often shunned their former home at the Dorothy Chandler Pavilion to instead make their recordings at Royce Hall. In 1994 the building was severly damaged by the Northridge Earthquake, and estimates were that had the earthquake lasted an additional five seconds, the towers would have come crashing down. With government assistance from FEMA, the building was repaired, strengthened to withstand future earthquakes, and remodeled. Inside the concert hall panels and doors were added that can be opened or closed to create a more ambient sound for musical events or a drier sound for spoken word events. The restored Skinner pipe organ now has 104 ranks and more than 6,600 pipes.

UCLA Live manages the performing arts functions of the hall. Artists who have performed there include George Gershwin, Duke Ellington, Arnold Schoenberg, Leonard Bernstein, Ella Fitzgerald, Ravi Shankar, Frank Zappa, Leontyne Price, Paul Robeson, and Ian McKellen; speakers who have appeared on its stage include Albert Einstein, John F. Kennedy, and Shirin Ebadi.

[edit] Eric J. Heller

Eric (Rick) Heller (b. 1946) is a full professor of chemistry and of physics at Harvard University. Heller is also known for producing digital art based on the results of his numerical simulations.

Heller is known primarily for his work in the 1970's and 1980's on time dependent quantum mechanics. In particular, Heller pioneered a time-dependent wavepacket picture of quantum mechanics, which allowed the excited-state dynamics of large quantum mechanical systems to be understood without calculating eigenstates. Heller's most widely applied method is one called "frozen Gaussians," a method of wavepacket propagation. Heller's work laid the foundations for a theoretical understanding of femtochemistry.

In physics, Heller is known for his work on quantum chaos, particularly on scar theory. Heller's work in the last ten years has focused on the study of two dimensional electron gases, quantum mirages in quantum corrals, scattering theory, as well as freak waves in the ocean. Many of these studies make use of the time-dependent quantum mechanics ideas from his earlier work.

Heller is a member of the International Academy of Quantum Molecular Science, the National Academy of Sciences, and a number of other scientific societies. He has held faculty positions at a number of institutions, including the University of Washington, Los Alamos National Laboratory, and University of California, Los Angeles. At Harvard, he served for several years as the director of the Institute for Theoretical Atomic Physics (ITAMP).

[edit] Mateusz Lewczuk

Born in Polan on Sept 18th 1981, Mateusz Lewczuk spent the first seven years of his life in Warsaw. He then moved with his family to Canada where he resided in Campbell River for the duration of his addolesence. His post secondary education began at north island collage, and after completing the first year of his undergraduate degree he moved on to complete his bachelors of science at the University of Victoria(UVic). Soon after he applied and was accepted to the masters program in high energy particle physics at UVic. Since then he has presented his ongoing work to his collaborators and scientists outside of his collaboration. He is currently set to defend his thesis on high multiplicty tau decays which will be the most accurate measurement of this decay to date. Barring any major disasters during his defense, Mateusz will go on to do his PhD, possibly in the study of CP violating three pion tau decays.
