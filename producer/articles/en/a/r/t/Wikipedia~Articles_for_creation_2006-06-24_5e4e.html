Rivaji, born on 5th July, 1979 as the first son of Tholoor Philipose Philipose (Raju) and Saramma Philipose (Ammini) in Kozhencherry, a town in the Pathanamthitta district of Kerala. He is the grandson of famous Freedom Fighter and Congress leader T P Philipose (Unnichayen) and Annamma Philipose. Ribu Philip is Rivaji's brother and Priya Anna Philip is his sister. Kozhencherry is a major town in Pathanamthitta district of Central Travancore region (South Central Kerala) in Kerala state.

When he was 10, Rivaji's father passed away.

Rivaji done his school education in Marthoma English Medium School, Kozhencherry and ST Thomas High School, Kozhencherry. Later he done University Education in ST Thomas College Kozhencherry, and Madurai Kamraj University. He completed B Sc in Computer Sciences and Master of Computer Applications from Madurai Kamraj University with flying colours.

Rivaji is now working as a Senior Software Engineer & System Analyst for a top MNC in Mumbai.

Rivaji is not new to politics and Congress. He is a born Congressman. When he was in sixth standard, he joined KSU (Kerala Students Union), the students union of Congress in Kerala state. He became a staunch member of KSU and enabled his brother Ribu to enter into KSU. Ribu contested in School parliament Election and won with a thumping majority.

However, Rivaji hesitated to contest any election in School.Despite his involvement in politics, Rivaji was top in studies also. He won USS Merit Scholarship, 3rd Rank in Kerala State Private Schools Competitive Examination, and won scores of prizes in state-level Quiz Competitions.

During his University Education, Rivaji actively worked for KSU.

After the education, he retired from active politics but continued as a Congressman. But in 2000, he quit the Congress in protest against the soft attitudes of Congress leadership to Communists and he formed Akhand Bharatiyawadhi Rashtrya Congress (United Indiaist National Congress). Later he merged his party with Congress and became Vice President of the IT cell of Congress. After two year he became the President of the organization.

[edit] Cardiothoracic Ratio

The Cardiothoracic Ratio = The Transverse Cardiac Diameter/The Transverse Chest Diameter as a percentage.

The Transverse Cardiac Diameter is the horizontal distance between the most rightward and leftward borders of the heart seen on a postero-anterior (PA) chest radiograph. The Transverse Chest Diameter is measured from the inside rib margin at the widest point above the costophrenic angles on a PA chest film.

A cardiothoracic ratio is considered abnormal if it is more than 50% is in an adult and more than 66% in a neonate.

The cardiac diameter itself can also be measured and (in normal individuals) is less than 15.5 cm in males, and less than 14.5 cm in females. A change in diameter of greater than 1.5 cm between two X-rays is significant.

Possible causes of a ratio greater than 50% include:

  • cardiac failure
  • pericardial effusion
  • left or right ventricular hypertrophy

[edit] Pan Delta Super Racing Festival

The Pan Delta Super Racing Festival is a motorsport event staged regularly from 2005 at the Zhuhai International Circuit in Guangdong province, People's Republic of China.

In 2005, on the basis of the Zhuhai International Circuit recruiting 5 major championships, namely the ZIC Touring Cars Championship, Hongkong Touring Car Championship, Macau Touring Car Championship, Lotus Challenge and the Asian Forumla 3 Series, a new event known as the “Pan Delta Super Racing Festival” was launched from May to August where the Formula cars and Touring cars were the main features. Crowds averaged 12,000 per event.

In 2006, the Pan Delta Super Racing Festival has strengthened its influences, not only have Superbike races been added into the events, more than 10 television stations from all over China have united together to broadcast the races. Through the Pan Delta Super Racing Festival, Zhuhai International Circuit hopes to combine Tourism, Economy, Culture in the region and stand tall in the Pan Pearl River Delta, nurture the motorsport culture and market, develop motorsport promotional channels and allows motorsport to become an entertainment event that everyone will want to see.

An increase in the crowd size was witnessed for the April and June events, averaging 15,000. Henan Xinfei Electrical Co. Ltd, also known as Frestech - a refrigerator manufacturer, has been secured as the title sponsor of the event and Coca Cola (China) is official drinks sponsor.

[edit] Vietnam Travel - Halong bay

Vietnam Travel Guide

Ha Long literally means descending dragon(s) and according to local myth, the story goes as follows: Long ago when their forefathers were fighting foreign invaders from the north, the gods from heaven sent a family of dragons to help defend their land. This family of dragons descended upon what is now Ha Long bay and began spitting out jewels and jade. Upon hitting the sea, these jewels turned into the various islands and islets dotting the seascape and formed a formidable fortress against the invaders. The locals were able to keep their land safe and formed what is now the country of Vietnam. The Dragon family fell so much in love with this area for its calm water and for the reverence of the people of Vietnam that they decided to remain on earth. Mother dragon lies on what is now Ha Long and where her children lie is Bai Tu Long. The dragon tails formed the area of Bach Long Vi known for the miles of white sandy beaches of Tra Co peninsula.

This myth is in line with the Vietnamese myth of their origin Con Rong Chau Tien. This myth describes the union between a king (representing the dragon) and his bride (representing a goddess) giving birth to 100 children which are the ancestors of the Vietnamese people. The Ha Long myth illustrate the Vietnamese belief of their origin and the fact that throughout their history, they are aided by their ancestors, the dragon and the gods, in the defense of their land.

[edit] Octatron SkySeer

The Octatron SkySeer™ is an autonomous, computer controlled unmanned aerial vehicle (UAV) designed for easy transport and launch. It is designed and manufactured by Octatron, Incorporated of St. Petersberg, Florida, USA.

Intended for short-range operations, the electric-powered SkySeer resembles a normal radio controlled model airplane or two-meter glider. Once unfolded from its storage tube and its electronics initialized, the SkySeer is then hand-launched by its operator. All of the drone's basic flight functions are handled by GPS including landings. Range is approximately two miles/3.2km and is extendable via Octatron's NetWeaver™ interface.

The basic platform is very low cost; a fully loaded SkySeer retails for approximately US$25,000 to $30,000 which includes all electronics, video surveillance equipment, ground station and computer interfaces. Video interface is in real time and allows the operator to literally see what the drone sees and to record it as such. The ground station's recorder can store up to twenty hours of high-quality MPEG-2 format video which can then be transfered to DVD or Flash.

The Los Angeles County Sheriff's Department is currently experimenting with the SkySeer as a means of crime prevention. At present, the experiment has been suspended by the Federal Aviation Administration because of that agency's claim of a lack of the proper permits. Although the FAA does not regulate model aircraft, it does have jurisdiction over unmanned aerial vehicles.

Negotiations between the two agencies are underway.

  • Weight: 3.125 lbs (1.42kg)
  • Endurance: 45 to 60 minutes at cruise speed of 23 mph (37km/h)

[edit] ACORD (Association for Cooperative Operations Research and Development)

ACORD is a non profit organisation that aims to introduce standardisation in the insurance industry across the world. This is perhaps the first time that such an attempt is being made in any industry.

ACORD's concerns are P & C insurance, life & annuities, reinsurance, insurance broking and any other intermediary involved in the business of insurance (of any kind).

ACORD addresses the following major areas in the insurance value chain:

1. Standards in electronic data transmission and communication. ACORD has developed XML codes for different insurance purposes. These codes are available for free download (www.acord.org) and integration into software packages by anyone who wishes to. 2. Standards in paper forms and documents in use in insurance industry. Of late, this has transformed into standards in EDI (Electronic Data Interchange).

ACORD operates out of offices in New York and London.

Most recent mobile phones have infra-red connectivity for data sharing with other phones or PCs. This connectivity is not really good for gaming because of its line of connectiviy should not be disturbed. No good player would enjoy this type of connectivity without moving his hands.

[edit] Bluetooth multiplayer games

Mobiles are connected through a wireless protocol called Bluetooth using special hardware. The games are designed to communicate with each other through this protocol to share the gaming information. The basic restriction is that both the users have to be within a definite distance to get connected. In this type of connection the game mode can only be one to one or two players more like a peer to peer connection between two PCs.

[edit] GPRS multiplayer game

In today’s gaming world, massive multiplayer is most loved by gamers. GPRS connection which is common among GSM mobile phones can be used to share the data globally. Developers can connect a mass number of mobile games with a single server and share data among the players. This is the latest craze in mobile game development. Some developers have even achieved cross platform games. A player with a mobile can play against another with a pc. Today the mobile multiplayer game development is still in turn based games and small RGP games. Since most of the counties have a week GPRS speed in their carriers. In these types of games the game communicates with a global server which acts like a router between the mobile phones.

3G and WiFi are just emerging in most of the countries, and has a good speed which is good for gaming. WiFi connectivity can only be used for a intra-networked multiplayer game.

[edit] oBIX

oBIX (for Open Building Information Exchange) is a standard for Web Services-based communication with building control systems. Building control systems include those electrical and mechanical systems that operate inside a building, including Heating and Cooling, Security, Power Management, and Life/Safety Alarms that are in nearly all buildings as well as the myriad of special purpose systems that may be tied to particular buildings such as A/V Event Management, Theatre Lighting, Medical Gas Distribution, Fume Hoods, and many others.

oBIX is a web services protocol protocol to enable communications between building mechanical and electrical systems, and enterprise applications. The protocol enables facilities and their operations to be managed as full participants in knowledge-based businesses.

The purpose of oBIX (open Building Information Exchange) is to enable the mechanical and electrical control systems in buildings to communicate with enterprise applications, and to provide a platform for developing new classes of applications that integrate control systems with other enterprise functions. Enterprise functions include processes such as Human Resources, Finance, Customer Relationship Management (CRM), and Manufacturing.

oBIX is being developed within OASIS, the Organization for the Advancement of Structured Information Standards. Version It is expected to be released for public review during 2006.

[edit] Background

Presently most mechanical and electrical systems are provided with embedded digital controls (DDC). Most of these devices are low cost and not enabled for TCP/IP. They are installed with dedicated communications wiring. Larger DDC controllers provide network communications for these dedicated controllers. There are several well established binary protocols (BACnet, LonTalk, Modbus, DALI) that are used on these dedicated networks in addition to numerous proprietary protocols. While these binary protocols can be used over TCP/IP networks - they have challenges with routers, firewalls, security, and compatibility with other network applications. There is an added challenge in that the industry is split between several largely incompatible protocols.

Because oBIX integrates with the enterprise, it will enable mechanical and electrical control systems to provide continuous visibility of operational status and performance, flagging problems and trends for system analysis or human attention.

OBIX provides a technology that enables facilities operators, owners and tenants to make decisions based on a fully integrated consideration of all life-cycle, environmental, cost, and performance factors.

[edit] Scope

oBIX provides a publicly available web services interface specification that can be used to obtain data in a simple and secure manner from HVAC, access control, utilities, and other building automation systems, and to provide data exchange between facility systems and enterprise applications. In addition, the TC will develop implementation guidelines, as needed, to facilitate the development of products that use the web service interface. Rlease 1 provides:

  • A standard for the exchange of data between systems in a secure manner.
  • Eventing of data (alarms and events)
  • Logs of data for trending variables such as temperature

The intent of the OASIS committee is to complete several iterations of this standard, each of which will add in additional functionality. In addition the committee intends to address the issues of interoperability and conformance testing, and further revision and maintenance of the specification.

[edit] Alighiero Boetti

Alighiero Boetti, born in Turin, Italy on 1940 and who died in 1994 in Rome, is one of the most influential artist of the postwar period. Historically located in more than one art movement -it is said to be both part of the Arte Povera movment as well as part of the european conceptual art- his artistic practice makes use of several different mediums like performance, photography, mail art, embroidery, prints, drawing and painting.

The harmony of dualism was an essential principle in the thoughts, feelings and actions of Alighiero Boetti, who attained international acclaim with his colourful embroidered pictures ("Arazzi").

In a photomontage with the title "I Gemelli" (English: The Twins), he not only combined two photos of himself into a double self-portrait; but in the subtitle, the dualistic principle is expressed once again: Alighiero e Boetti. In the words of the artist: "Alighiero is the child-like part, outwardly directed, determining a familiar environment. Alighiero, that is what I am named and called by those who know me. Boetti is more abstract, if only because a surname is itself yet another category."

Alighiero Boetti is an autodidact. His artistic career begins in the second half of the sixties. After his first exhibition in 1967, when he becomes associated with the Arte Povera movement. He is interested in oriental culture and other related disciplines, such as philosophy and alchemy.

In 1971, he travels to Afghanistan for the first time. He stays there for one month and, through 1979 - the year of the invasion of the Russian army - returns every year for longer periods of time. It is also in 1971 that he commissions his first embroidered pictures, which are crafted by Afghani women. The production of these embroidered pictures continues until his death. According to Anne Marie Boetti partner of the artist for some time, establishing a hotel in the Afghan city of Kabul was a way to create a more direct relationship with the city and its community. Allegedly, the One Hotel lasted until 1981 when the building was destroyed by the civil war. Boetti made his last trip to Afghanistan in 1979 and until his death he regreat not being able to go back. During the 1980s he went to meet Afghan communities that lived in Pakistan. He kept producing embropidery works until 1991 along Afghani women.

"From the very outset of his career as an artist, Alighiero tried to find an artistic form suitable for expressing the fundamental structures of the world which he discerned in appropriate aesthetic terms", writes Rolf Lauter. "When the works were in the realisation phase, Alighiero often resorted to a dialogical communication system between the artist, on the one hand, and collaborators, assistants and outside parties handling the work, on the other."

The placing of texts and word into grid-like structures plays a special role in this process. The "Arazzi piccoli", for example, are generally comprised of four by four, but often also of five by five or more squares of letters, which are embroidered in random colour combinations. Read from top to bottom, they reveal sayings such as "Dare tempo al tempo" (To give time time), "Ordine e Disordine" (Order and Disorder), etc.

The "Arazzi grandi", on the other hand, are comprised of 25 x 25 squares of letters. "Twenty-five is the square of the holy number five and is therefore also the centre of magical squares. It consists of the sum of the numbers 1 + 3+ 5 + 7 + 9, and thus contains all the holy numbers which can be used in magic." (Boetti)

Among the most widely known works by Alighiero Boetti are the embroidered maps of the world, which derive from conceptual works of the years 1967 to 1971. In September 1971, he took the first preliminary drawing to Afghanistan. Once the artist specified the colours of the threads to be used, four women then worked simultaneously on the embroidery, which, depending on the format, took between one and two years to complete.

Equally popular among collectors are Boetti's "Lavori Biro" (Ballpoint Pen Works). The works in this group were executed with "common" ballpoint pens on paper, in the colours blue, black, red and green. Some of these were later mounted on canvas spanned on stretcher frames, while others were mounted on cardboard and placed in Plexiglas frames.

At least with the earlier works, the artist asked the individuals working on the pictures to produce hatches which conformed as much as possible to a basic grid of small squares in order to make the technique used to create the pictures more visible. Later, the structures varied according to the individuals' own patterns.

"The extent of the delegated work varies from the minimum, as in the case of the Biro (Ballpoint Pen), where there is nothing creative to do at all, to the maximum, as in the case of the Tutto (Everything). I asked the assistants to draw everything, all possible forms, abstract and figurative, and to join these together until there was no space left on the paper. Then I brought the drawings to Afghanistan, where I had them embroidered with threads of ninety different colours, whereby I stipulated that the exact same amount of each colour had to be used."

From 1987 onwards, Boetti works together with his Iranian assistant Mahshid Mussari, who collected the most varied motif patterns for the realisation of the last and largest "Tutto". After weeks of preparatory work, the oversized canvas could finally be sent to Peshawar in December 1993. After ten months, the completed work could finally be sent back to Rome, although Alighiero was never able to see it himself. He died in 1994 as a result of a brain tumour.

This extraordinary work, which measures circa 2.5 x 6.5 meters (circa 8 x 21 feet), is now in the collection of the Museum of Modern Art in Frankfurt am Main. This "Tutto" is a symbol for the complexity of the world, its cultural diversity and its development; and, especially in light of the most recent political events, it can be viewed once again from an entirely new perspective.

[edit] Niel Brandt

William Nielsen Brandt (born June 10, 1970; also known as Niel Brandt) is a professor in the Department of Astronomy & Astrophysics at the Pennsylvania State University. He is best known for his work studying the X-ray properties of active galaxies, X-ray binaries, and other cosmic X-ray sources.

[edit] Education

Brandt was born in Durham, North Carolina but mostly grew up in Janesville, Wisconsin. He attended Milton High School in Milton, Wisconsin and Phillips Exeter Academy in Exeter, New Hampshire. His undergraduate studies were done at the California Institute of Technology (B.S. 1992), where he was awarded three Carnation Merit Awards, a Barry Goldwater Merit Scholarship, and the George Green Prize for Creative Scholarship. His graduate studies were done at the Institute of Astronomy of the University of Cambridge (Ph.D. 1996; Advisor: Andrew C. Fabian, FRS), where he was supported by an Isaac Newton Studentship and a National Science Foundation Graduate Research Fellowship.

[edit] Career

From 1996-1997 Brandt held a postdoctoral fellowship at the Harvard-Smithsonian Center for Astrophysics. In 1997, he took up an assistant professor appointment at the Pennsylvania State University. He was promoted to associate professor in 2001 and full professor in 2003.

[edit] Research and Teaching

Brandt uses X-ray satellites, including the Chandra X-ray Observatory and XMM-Newton, to study the physics and evolution of active galaxies, X-ray binaries, and other cosmic X-ray sources. He also performs complementary observations at other wavelengths. Some of his main topics of interest include deep extragalactic X-ray surveys, active-galaxy outflows, active galaxies with high mass-accretion rates, and the first active galaxies to form in the Universe. Brandt is an author of more than 200 research papers on these subjects. He has also worked on investigations of the cosmic microwave background radiation and the effects of neutron-star natal kicks.

Brandt leads an X-ray research group including postdoctoral researchers, graduate students, and undergraduate students. His postdoctoral researchers and students have gone on to win distinguished fellowships and permanent positions.

Brandt regularly teaches courses on introductory astronomy, high-energy astrophysics, black holes, and active galaxies. He also is actively involved in public outreach, teaching high-school and middle-school teachers about galaxies and cosmology.

[edit] Staurosporine

[edit] Sources

Staurosporine is a potent inhibitor of protein kinases with an IC50 in the nanomolar range. It is an alkaloid from the bacterium Streptomyces sp. In particular, it acts as an inhibitor of protein kinase C (Protein Kinase C represents a family of Ca(2+)- and phospholipid-dependent enzymes which catalyzes the covalent transfer of phosphate from ATP to serine and threonine residues on proteins.) and of platelet-derived growth factor (PDGF) receptor protein tyrosine kinase activity. Staurosprorine is often used in in vitro models to induce cell death in a transcription-independent manner. It has a highly anti-proliferative activity. Staurosporine might be considered as a prototype neurotropic drug in view of its ability to induce neurite outgrowth and to increase tau protein levels. Because it mimics some of the neuroprotective effects of NGF and might block certain signals required to enhance cellular levels and/or beta amyloid processing, staurosporine was by some researchers considered to as a candidate in the treatment of Alzheimer's disease. The ability of staurosporine to promote neuronal regeneration and brain cholinergic neurons survival has been also demonstrated in animal studies.

As the protein kinase C (PKC) isoenzyme family mediates a central cytoprotective effect in the regulation of cell survival, activation of PKC, and subsequent recruitment of numerous downstream elements such as the mitogen-activated protein kinase (MAPK) cascade, opposes initiation of the apoptotic cell death program by diverse cytotoxic stimuli.

Thus, inhibition of PKC, which has been shown to initiate apoptosis in a variety of malignant cell types, is of interest in cancer research. Selective targeting of PKC may prove useful in improving the therapeutic efficacy of established antineoplastic agents. Although staurosporine itself is too toxic to be applied, there are more specific PKC inhibitors available.

Indeed, staurosporine and its derivatives possess antineoplastic activity in vivo in human tumors grown as xenografts in nude mice. Staurosporine analogs are also potent modulators of the cyclin-dependent kinase system, which determines the progression of cells through the cell cycle.

The induction of mitochondrial apoptotic processes by the protein kinase inhibitor staurosporine indicates a possible role for PKC in the modulation of the intracellular mechanisms leading to Programmed Cell Death. Abnormalities in both levels and activity of PKC, found in some chronic neurodegenerative syndromes, lead to the possibility that PKC dysfunction could be involved in the pathogenetic mechanisms of disease.

[edit] Condensation Trap

A condensation trap is a trap used to collect condensation from dew or moisture in the air. It is usually a plastic bag placed over the leaves of a plant and tied to the stems. When the trap is placed at night or in the early morning, as the air heats during the day the bag will create a green-house effect collecting moisture from the air inside in the bag. This trap is most commonly used in a survival situation where water or clean water is scarce such as in a desert environment.

[edit] New Canada Project

The exact term "New Canada", according to Rick Anderson (http://ca.groups.yahoo.com/group/NewCanadaProject/message/47), was coined by founder, first and only leader of the Reform Party of Canada, E. Preston Manning, through his book in 1992 (http://www.cc.umanitoba.ca/cm/cmarchive/vol20no4/revthenewcanada.html) and his New Canada Act of 1998 (http://www.uni.ca/library/reform_nca.html).


However the "Project" actually started when Don MacAlpine from Nipigon in the Thunder Bay-Superior North riding, got the ball rolling on this idea when he was originally asked by Paul Hellyer to run as a candidate of record for the Canadian Action Party in 1997, but because that party wasn't democratically reformed enough, he took it upon himself to create Take Back Democracy (http://www.takebackdemocracy.ca) right after then ran it up about until the 2004 federal general election.


After that election's success in electing and making Independents like Chuck Cadman, Carolyn Parrish, David Kilgour, Pat O'Brien and Bev Desjarlais, he came up with the idea of a Coalition or Association of Canadian Independent Democratic Representatives, then went on to promote it on his Steps to Democratic Empowerment (http://www.nwconx.net/donmac/) during the federal general eleciton of 2006 where a Québécois Independent from Portneuf—Jacques-Cartier named André Arthur was actually elected.


Soon Don MacAlpine retired both Take Back Democracy and Steps to Democratic Empowerment webpages to push harder for his Coalition or Association of Canadian Independent Democratic Representatives across the country, contacting the 90 non-affiliated Independents who ran in the least election who had not been elected, meanwhile other Independent-minded Canadians were already organizing, strategizing and planning out the next step themselves in other regions from the Atlantic to the Pacific.


Up right until now, the most advanced so far was the Independent movement in Ontario which included Brad Harness from Ailsa Craig in the Lambton—Kent—Middlesex riding representing the New Canada Party (http://www.newcanada.ca), Mike Houghtling from Waterloo in the Kitchener—Waterloo riding representing United We Gather, Joshua E. Eriksen from Keady in the Bruce—Grey—Owen Sound riding representing the Canadian Independence Alliance Bloc Coalition, Tim Hickey from Belleville in the Prince Edward—Hastings riding representing National Challenge for Change in Canada (http://www.shareholderinthedream.ca), Ronnie Amyotte in the Mississauga—Erindale riding representing Block Canada, Bill Cook from Tara in the Bruce—Grey—Owen Sound riding and John Gault from Toronto in the Trinity—Spadina riding representing the Representative Party of Ontario (http://www.representativeparty.on.ca) and Partap Dua from Markham in the Markham—Unionville riding and Mohamed Elrofaie from Mississauga in the Mississauga East—Cooksville riding representing the United Peoples Party of Canada (http://www.uppc.ca) along with a few other individual united Independent supporters for an "organically grassroots meetup" that would be the first "Common Parliament for the Common People" of Canada in Ontario held inside the International Market Café at the International Trade Centre in Mississauga on Saturday, April 1, 2006.


Though when united, the group was too busy strategizing, planning and sharing ideas to formally organize and agree to an official Independent platform, eventually they did create a Common Ground Agreement later on their New Canada Project listserv is (http://ca.groups.yahoo.com/group/NewCanadaProject), which is temporary active for the interim until a democratic grassroots congress is held with a open and free vote by the membership at large on the validity of the document in time for the upcoming election.


Its Common Ground Agreement is a platform document that melds both the principles and policies agreed upon by through general consensus, with those ideas added to it from the Fireweed Democracy Project (http://www.fireweeddemocracyproject.ca) sessions Richard Anderson from Merrickville in the Leeds—Grenville riding led on CPAC (http://www.cpac.ca) from April 6-9, who has also joined in talks on the listserv this spring, has been appointed to the national advisory boards of Fair Vote Canada (http://www.fairvotecanada.org), Manning Centre for Building Democracy (http://www.manningcentre.ca) and the progressive Canada 2020 (http://www.canada2020.ca) this summer.


Once the Agreement was made, the evolution of the Project spawned the genesis of the New Canada Project website (http://www.newcanada.ca) owned by Brad Harness, which is projected to be the starting and finishing point for the Common Ground Agreement through a Wiki-styled Open Living Platform co-administrated by Craig Hubley from Toronto in the Toronto—Danforth riding and Andrew C. Thomas from Scarborough in the Scarborough-Guildwood riding for all Independent-minded Canadians to use, add, edit and create one brand-new Canadian vision for the future through direct participatory democracy online, the Project's deadline being late December as a general federal election is expected to be held by the governing Tories by the group some time in Winter 2007.


Also, there is another New Canada Project recently being used separately, as the New Canada Project for Social Justice and Strategic Leadership (http://www.newcanada.tk) has been launched on Saturday, June 17, 2006 of this summer by Adam Henley from Peterborough in the Peterborough riding according to a post on the group's listserv (http://ca.groups.yahoo.com/group/NewCanadaProject/message/46).

[edit] Y Mountain

Y Mountain is located directly east from Brigham Young University in Provo, Utah. There is a trail that ascends a mile up the face of the mountain up to a giant Y built over a hundred years ago as the insignia for BYU. For years this trail has been the most hiked trail in Utah Valley and provides a beautiful view of Provo, Orem and the rest of the many cities in Utah Valley and Utah Lake (a large freshwater lake that feeds the Great Salt Lake).

The large white Y on the side of the mountain has become the nationally recognized insignia for BYU and the reason why BYU is often called the "Y". It is made of concrete and is 380 feet high and 130 feet wide. No other college in the United States has a symbol larger, in fact, the Y is even larger than the letters in the "Hollywood Sign" in California.

There are 14 strands of lights around the perimeter of the Y, which are lit 5 times a year by the Intercollegiate Knights. It is lit for Freshman Orientation, Homecoming, Y Days, and graduation in April and August. When the Y is lit, club members are selected to guard the Y in the night and make sure it remains lit until dawn.

[edit] History

Early 1906 - When the junior class of Brigham Young University wanted to paint '07 on the side of the mountain, the senior class became upset. To settle this conflict BYU President George H. Brimhall proposed that they paint the letters BYU on the mountain instead.

April 2006 - The plans to construct the BYU on the mountain were begun. Professor Ernest D. Partridge was assigned to conduct a survey and designed the emblem. When this was complete, a line of students and faculty passed buckets of lime, sand, and rocks up the mountain in order to fill up the letters. After six hours of hard labor, only the Y had been completed, so the filling in of the remaining two letters was postponed and later abandoned.

1907 - The BYU Y Day tradition began. This consisted of thousands of students hauling, by bucket brigade, gallons of whitewash up the mountain to paint the giant Y. This tradition lasted until 1973. It was abandoned due to erosion of the mountain.

1908 - A three-foot high wall was erected around the letter to keep it together. This required an additional 20,000 pounds of concrete.

1911 - Serifs were added to the top and bottom of the Y, giving it its current look.

1924 - Tradition of "lighting the Y" began. Each year during special evenings, mattress batting was placed buckets and soaked in kerosene. The buckets were then set around the edges of the Y and lit with torches. This ended in 1970 when it was decided that this tradition was hazardous. After being discontinued, a small building was constructed which contains a generator that lights up 14 strands of 25-watt light bulbs which outline the Y.

1960s - BYU began to use a helicopter to carry 1000s of pounds of whitewash to repaint the Y. This is now redone every 5 years.

May 20, 2006 - The 100th anniversary of the Y was celebrated. Many people hiked the Y that day to commemorate the occasion.

[edit] Beverage Reef

This little known place in the South Pacific is a sunken atoll. No land at all, just a circular reef about 5 miles wide by 8 miles long with a little passage on the west side to go inside and be surrounded by breakers and ocean on all sides. It is speculated that fewer people have actually been inside this reef than to the top of Mt. Everest. The weather conditions have to be just perfect to go inside the reef. A wreck still visible on the reef is a testament to this. Once inside it is possible to anchor in water that is crystal clear and underwater visibility to depths approximating 200 ft. Snorkeling inside the reef may be experienced with sharks, fish and rays.

[edit] Samm Simpson

Born in Iowa in 1953, this grandmother, radio/tv production pro, former VP of marketing for a financial services firm, and life long peace and justice advocate chose to run for public office for the first time in 2006. Seeking to unseat Bill Young -- 36 year Republican US Congressperson from Florida's district 10 -- Simpson blends a fiery oratorical style with a populist/progressive platform, while balancing her commitment to her Christian faith with an equal respect for the separation of church and state, all the while promoting the religious diversity historically associated with the United States.

Stances on Issues

1) The Environment. Simpson is closely aligned with many proposals that are familiar to Green Party adherents. From an Apollo program for renewable fuel sources and technologies, to environmentally friendly architectural design and community planning, Simpson could easily be referred to as a supporter of Al Gore's vision of man's environmental impact and the benefits of conservation and rethinking our exploitation of fossil fuels, as laid out in his film "An Inconvenient Truth".

2) Health Care. Simpson supports affordable health care for all US citizens, using the single payer model. She sees the negotiating power of the US government as key to providing the most cost efficient services for Americans.

3) Terrorism. Simpson calls for increased utilization of diplomatic and economic tools as well as law enforcement techniques to address the vast majority of the problems of international terrorism. Additional focus must be placed upon the motivation behind the attacks, not simply attempts at their prevention.

4) Iraq Occupation. Simpson supports withdrawal of US forces, ala Murtha, at the "earliest practicable date", with an over the horizon presence to assist the government in power against armed uprisings. But day to day security operations should be in the hands of the Iraqi people.

5) Constitutional Issues. Simpson is a staunch supporter of three co-equal branches of government, rejects the concept of the "unitary executive", and suggests that congress is duty bound to exercise it's oversight powers vis a vis the executive, even during times of war in as much as it must insure that it's laws are being faithfully executed. Congress must have robust authority to insure the laws it passes are followed. Rule of law trumps presidential prerogative.

6) Elections. Simpson calls for an open, transparent, accessible, and verifiable voting process, with paper ballots and streamlined re countability in all states. Voter registration processes should promote participation by the largest number of citizens possible, and at least one national holiday should be set aside for voting, with more total hours of accessibility to the polls allowed for.

7) Deficit spending. Simpson calls for a balanced budget, aggressive debt reduction, and a return to tax policies comparable to those of the 1990's. She views the national debt as a national security issue.

For more, see her website at http://www.sammsimpsonforcongress.com

[edit] Ben Kuchera

B.D.Kuchera, also know as Benjamin Daniel Kuchera/Ben Kuchera grew up in Indianapolis, Minneapolis, Chicago, Los Angeles, and Cincinnati. He had a keen interest in art at an early age and began as an illustrator at the Minneapolis College of Art and Design. He would also later paint, draw, write and animate as well as make films. Filmmaking however, became his greatest passion.

After graduating from high school, Kuchera wrote and directed his first 16mm film, a short called "Rest Stop 2 Miles" which he shot on location in rural Illinois. This was shown on regional PBS. He would soon after build on his experience by undertaking his first feature length project, "Mayfly Days," also shot on 16mm film.

As he wrote, shot and edited "Mayfly Days" over the next several years along with his close friend Michael J Taylor, Kuchera attended the School of the Art Institute of Chicago and later the California Institute of the Arts where he received his bachelor's degree in Film/Video in 1999. He accepted a full-ride scholarship to study Fine Arts and Electronic Media at the University Cincinnati and completed two years before leaving to work in the commercial film and video production industry.

Married to Sarah Jane Bajus in 2004, Kuchera now lives in Lexington, Kentucky. He has years of commercial and documentary video experience under his belt and is developing several scripts and documentaries through his company Silver Screen Arts - a production and post-production studio. He has also discovered Fine Art to be completely incompatible with the commercial Arts, and has begun a journey into the Arts and storytelling. Kuchera is now a college professor and independent filmmaker and Artist.

Kuchera is a devout Protestant Christian, and gave his life to Jesus Christ on June 5th, 2005, and was baptized on June 12, 2005. Kuchera asked his wife to marry him on June 5th 2004, and was married on June 12, 2005. The significance of the parallels of his marriage to his wife, and his devotion to Christ were far from coincidental.
