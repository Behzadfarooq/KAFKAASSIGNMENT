[edit] Nikolo Balkanski

Nikolo Balkanski studied fine art in Bulgaria and Finland and exhibited his first one-man show at age twenty-one. He moved after finishing his studies in Helsinki University of Art to United States. He built a strong following in Europe as master of portraiture and landscapes, with major exhibitions in Bulgaria, Finland, Sweden, England, France and the United States. He is highly regarded as an insiprational teacher and mentor.

Balkanski's work has been exhibited with Artists of America, the Colorado Governor's Invitational, where he received Collector's Choice award in 1999, Great American Artists, Artists of the West, and Arts for the Parks, where he won Best Landscape in 1995.

Born in Sofia, Bulgaria, Nikolo Balkanski currently lives in Lakewood, Colorado. His work is represented by Hayden-Hays Gallery, Colorado Springs; Gallery East, Loveland; Borsini - Burr Galleries, Half Moon Bay, Calif,; Spirits in the Wind Gallery, Golden, Colo,; Weatherburn Gallery, Naples, Fla,; and Knox Galleries, Denver, Beaver Creek, Colo,, and Harbor Springs, Mich.

Nikolo Balkanski plays with light and texture and employs strong thick brush strokes to convey a complete image. Within his landscapes, pallet knives layer pebble upon rock, creating depth until streams appear to trickle from their frames.

Image:Balkanski-Mayin Taos.jpg

"For me it is a real luxury to work on location." Nikolo said of his opportunity to paint such things as mountain scenes in the field. Since his move to the States, his work has undergone a natural metamorphosis. The subjects he paints are not the only things that have changed, but his techniques as well. "Before I did the Baltic Sea, and now I do the mountain streams of Colorado."

Art critics call Balkanski an impressionist - but more complex. Upon seeing Nikolo's exhibition in London, John Allen described his work as "Alert, a moment of being, engaged with a world that could dissolve to chaos but harmonized by attention...moment of meaning the fateful conjunction of type, personality and environment."

The painter's years of work have produced a good variety of subject and color which appear in galleries and museums across the United States and Europe. He received his formal training in Bulgaria and Finland and currently lives and works in Denver, Colorado.

[edit] Bishop Dominick Lagonegro

Bishop Dominick Lagonegro serves the Hudson Valley in the Roman Catholic Archdiocese of New York. He was born on March 6, 1943 in White Plains, New York. He was ordained as a priest in 1969 and as a Bishop in 2001. His motto is "Chritus Primus" or "Christ first" and is noted for his passionate homilies, care for the community, and support of traditional values. In addition to his duties as pastor, he has served as regional coordinator of the Dutchess Vicariate since 1997. Among the important functions of that role is to assist new pastors with questions that they may have, and to meet regularly with the priests who serve in the local parishes. He also can be called to represent the cardinal at vicariate Masses and gatherings.

His first assignment was at St. Vito's in Mamaroneck, where he served as parochial vicar for seven years. From 1977 to 1980 he served at St. Joseph's in Kingston and also taught a religion class at John A. Coleman Catholic High School. He was parochial vicar at Holy Trinity parish in Poughkeepsie, 1980 to 1989. He was named pastor of St. Denis-St. Columba's in Hopewell Junction in 1989 and founding pastor of St. Columba's in 1992.

[edit] Illinois Council on Responsible Fatherhood

The Illinois Council attempts to engage interested participants in the policy issues and concerns that affect fathers and families in Illinois through various public forums and events. Organizers ultimately hope to build a network of organizations and individuals working together to promote responsible fatherhood across the state. To accomplish this, the Council reaches out to lawmakers, churches, social service organizations, and individuals that will participate in the events and forums.

The Illinois Council on Responsible Fatherhood is the first governmental initiative of its kind in Illinois and will serve as a model for other states across the country seeking to restore responsible fatherhood as a societal norm in all communities.

The Illinois Legislature established the Council as a statewide commission under the office of Gov. Rod Blagojevich in 2003 and was extended by executive order in 2006. Jeffery M. Leving, a Chicago attorney, serves as its chair.

“Countless studies and mounting anecdotal evidence continue to demonstrate how children who grow up in father-absent homes are much more likely to be poor, to use drugs, to experience educational, emotional and behavioral problems, to be victims of child abuse and to engage in criminal behavior,” Leving concluded.

The Council uses feedback from the forums to analyze regional differences and identify solutions to promote the positive involvement of fathers in the lives of their children in Illinois. These findings are then presented to the Governor and State Legislatures.

[edit] Jeffery Leving

Jeffery M. Leving received his Juris Doctor from IIT-Chicago Kent College of Law in 1979. He is licensed to practice law in Illinois, the U.S. District Court for the Northern District of Illinois, the U.S. 7th Circuit Court of Appeals and the U.S. Supreme Court. His professional associations include the Chicago and Illinois Bar Associations and the Federal Trial Bar; and the Decalogue Society of Lawyers. Leving has been named one of “America’s Best Lawyers” by Forbes Radio and selected by his peers as one of Illinois’ top attorneys.

He is the author of two books. “Fathers’ Rights” (Basic Books), published in 1997, presents hard-hitting and fair advice for every father involved in a custody dispute, and “Divorce Wars” (HarperCollins) is a field guide to the winning tactics, preemptive strikes, and top maneuvers when divorce gets ugly.

His past experience includes co-authorship of the Illinois Joint Custody Law, and testimony before both branches of the Illinois Legislature on Joint Custody, Grandparent's Visitation and Child Support Accountability bills. He lectures locally and nationally on family law issues. His law practice is concentrated in Matrimonial and Family Law and he heads a team of strategic and aggressive attorneys at his law practice in Chicago.

Leving was the Chicago Attorney from the Elian Gonzalez case. He represented Manuel Gonzalez, at Juan Miguel's request, to be designated as Elian's care giver in the early stages of the federal case.

Leving was appointed as Chair of the Council on Responsible Fatherhood for the State of Illinois by Governor Rod Blagojevich.

Leving is also the publisher of Leving’s Divorce Magazine, a magazine for divorced men that focuses on parenting and men’s legal issues.

[edit] Lens size in inch

Lens size

Lens sizes are often indicated with a measure in inch, referring to the lens diagonal. However the indication does not mean that e.g. With a lens on 1”inch” diagonal a image with a diagonal of 1 inch or 25.4mm can be projected in the image plane without vignetting or severe distortion. The relation between lens diagonal in “inch” and effective diagonal of the image, rather comes from times where images were acquired with electron tubes, and where e.g. a 1inch tube had an image diagonal of 16mm. [edit] Passing Places

Passing Places, a Contemporary Scottish Text by Steven Greenhorn, chronicles the plight of two Motherwell boys, Alex (an employee in a local sports shop) and Brian (unemployed, but apparently in a perpetual state of self-education), as they flee from Alex's psycotic boss Binks (owner of aforementioned sports shop, who is able to converse with his brother Ronnie, who died at birth) after stealing a surfboard from the sports shop. They hit the road in a run-down Lada, and make for the one place they think they will be safe, Thurso. Along the way, they encounter many weird and wonderful characters, make new friends, and fall in love with the Scotland that they have never been able to get to know, until now.

Central themes: Poverty, Unemployment, Education, Self-discovery, Friendship. Minor themes: The Stigma of Mental Illness, Crime, Exploration.

[edit] Shaun Murphy (Director, Producer, Writer)

SHAUN MURPHY

Shaun Murphy is a Producer, Director and Presenter of TV & Documentary, born August 26, 1965 in Sydney, Australia.

Murphy is currently Executive Producer of the documentary ‘Dying for Everest’ (2007), exploring the tragic death of Englishman David Sharp on Mt Everest and Co-Producing the untitled Stacy Peralta documentary on the Gangs of Los Angeles (2007), a hardball look at the history and growth of the two dominant LA gangs, the Bloods and the Crips.

Shaun is best known in the USA for his lively on-the-road look at America’s new fuel options in the TV series Coolfuel Roadtrip (2005) which he Executive Produced, Produced and Presented. The series provided the ‘what’s next after oil’, using food, vegetable oil, cow pies and corn whiskey as fuel in planes, cars, trucks and boats.

In Australia, Murphy Produced and Directed the documentary ‘Coolfuel Aussie Style’ (2003) and Produced and Directed the documentary Racing The Moon (1999) where he became the first person to paddle a sea kayak from Brisbane to Sydney. Murphy also Produced and Presented for the award-winning Australian wildlife & environment TV documentary series Totally Wild (1995-98).

A former outrigger canoe champion in Australia and a cricketer in England, Murphy studied & worked as a Horticulturist and Landscape Designer before turning to the media where he worked as a freelance writer. He travelled extensively throughout South America, Europe and Asia before settling back in Australia and embarking on his producing career. He entered the world of television and documentary in 1995.
