Dean Moxey (born London, January 14, 1986) is a professional footballer with Conference side Exeter City.

Moxey is a winger who plays primarily on the left side of midfield, although he is also able to play as a left back or full-back. He has been capped by England at the National Game XI level.

Moxey, began his career as a trainee with Exeter City. Upon their relegation from the Football League in 2003, many senior players departed, giving Moxey and other trainees their share of first-team action. Dean was considered one of the best youth players to emerge during this period. Since his debut on 18 October 2003 against Conference side Woking, Dean has established himself as a first team regular.

On 8 January 2005 Moxey starred in City's 2005 FA Cup run against Manchester United at Old Trafford, where the squad earned a 0-0 draw, and a replay at St James Park.

Since the departure of former Exeter City manager Alex Inglethorpe in 2006, Moxey has been use mainly as a substitute by new manager Paul Tisdale during the 2006–07 season.
