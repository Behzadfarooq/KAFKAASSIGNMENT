Arturo Escobar (anthropologist)

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Arturo Escobar V. (born in Manizales, Colombia) is a Kenan Distinguished Professor in the Department of Anthropology at the University of North Carolina, Chapel Hill. His research interests are related to political ecology; the anthropology of development, social movements; latin american development and politics. Escobar's research uses critical techniques in his provocative analysis of development discourse and practice in general. He also explores possibilities for alternative visions for a post-development era. He is a major figure in the post-development academic discourse, and a serious critic of development practices championed by western industrialized societies.
