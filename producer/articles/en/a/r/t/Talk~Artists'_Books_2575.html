 It is requested that a photograph or photographs be included in this article to improve its quality.

One photo wouldn't do it. It would have to be many.... I haven't actually uploaded photos yet and I've been nervous to try it because of copyright issues.--Sue Maberry 18:44, 28 July 2006 (UTC)

Wonder if something like Clive Philpot's various diagrams of the field could be of use as an image (instead of trying to find a representative example(s), a task that is probably impossible). Philpot's diagrams are described in detail in the 'Definition' chapter of S Klima's 'Artists books: a critical survey of the literature': Example: in a 1993 Art Libraries Journal article, Philpot uses a two-circle Venn diagram with Art as one circle and Book as the second; another space within the intersecting area is delineated as 'artists books' and this is further broken down into bookworks, books, and book objects. This is maybe not a perfect solution, but would give someone with no knowledge of the field an instant visual 'way in', and of course could be qualified with a subtitle ('so-and-so's diagram of the field of artist's books'). Emily Artinian 09:00, 2 August 2006 (UTC)

What about copyright issues? Sue Maberry 04:42, 22 August 2006 (UTC)
