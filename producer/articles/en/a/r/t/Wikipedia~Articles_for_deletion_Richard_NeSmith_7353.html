  • Delete per all of above. And a badly written resume at that. Edeans 05:58, 1 February 2007 (UTC)
  • Delete and mildly chuckle at the inclusion of "Who’s Who Among American High School Students" in the article. Andrew Lenahan - Starblind 08:09, 1 February 2007 (UTC)
I understand your chuckle. That's how I found out about this article. I was searching for the "National Dean's List" and he came up. I am "nominated" for the current book, and I was in it years ago before the internet was anything. Not this time. Royalbroil T : C 15:11, 1 February 2007 (UTC)
  • Delete per above. MER-C 11:08, 1 February 2007 (UTC)
  • Keep, is the Dean of Education. So it would seem he is more than just a "common professor". Must say, at first I was suspecting this was a hoax at first glance and had already written out my delete vote... but because he is the Dean (even if it is merely of a nothing at all school it seems, maybe...) I'm going to give him the chance of getting a proper article being written. But if that doesn't happen in a few months (or whatever) from now, then it should be deleted. I've made a start on it myself to help along future editors, by including three links. One of them the CV, in which it mentions publications etc.. Mathmo Talk 12:47, 1 February 2007 (UTC)

  • delete in this case I do not think notability could be demonstrated. He is associate professor at a small non-research college calling itself a university, and that is not usually notable. Deans are probably not intrinsically notable, except at a few universities. But in major universities they invariably come from among the more distinguished professors, so the situation rarely occurs. For notability as an academic administration of a small almost entirely undergraduate college, he'd need to be president. As a final touch, the article on the college here doesn't mention the school of education. DGG 06:08, 2 February 2007 (UTC)
  • Delete. Does not appear to be notable, and I have concerns about the quality of the references. WMMartin 15:41, 2 February 2007 (UTC)
