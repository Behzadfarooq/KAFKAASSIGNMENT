[edit] Jason Kehl

Jason Kehl is one of the top American rock climbers in the world. Born on October 18, 1976 in Maryland, Jason Kehl did not start climbing until around the age of 18. However, even with a relatively slow start to the sport of climbing, Kehl has successfully been making huge impacts in all different aspects of climbing. Kehl is known for his unique sense of style, including make-up and visual effects, collecting dishelved dolls, and dynamic screaming while climbing. More recently, Kehl has been pushing the envelope of high ball bouldering, including first ascents of "Evilution V12" in the Buttermilks California which measures to be well over 45ft in height, and the first non-roped ascents of "The Fly" 5.14d/V14 in Rumney, New Hampshire and "Straight out of Squampton" 5.13+ in Squamish, Bristish Columbia. Jason has also starred in many climbing videos such as: Dosage I & III, The Road, The Life, and Best of the West. Jason Kehl is a full time climber, living on the road getting paid to climb from his many sponsers like: Five Ten, Revolution Climbing, Blurr, and So Ill Holds. He also runs his own website with updates from his lifelong roadtrip, pictures, and his personal artwork.

[edit] Beth Rodden

Beth Rodden is one of the strongest most successful American rock climbers in the world. Born in Davis, California, Beth began climbing at the young age of 14 at the local indoor climbing gym, Rocknasium. Beth fell in love with the sport, and through dedication and hard work, she improved quickly and won the Junior National Championships in 1997, 98, and 99. Soon, plastic and indoor competitions lost their splendor to Beth, so she soon turned to the outdoors for rock climbing on real rock. Beth proved to be successful both indoors and outdoors, and by the age of 18 Beth Rodden redpointed "To Bolt Or Not To Be" 5.14a in Smith Rock, Oregon, which made her the youngest woman to ever complete such a grade. Beth made many other notable climbing ascents, which was interrupted by a horrifying kidnapping event in Kyrgystan when Beth was in the middle of a 2000 climbing trip with her now husband Tommy Caldwell, John Singer, and a friend Jason. Although all four climbers made it out safely, Beth was left in fear and put her climbing career on hiatus while she focused on her family and recovering from the terrifying event. Beth Rodden married her long time boyfriend Tommy Caldwell in 2001, and eventually started to climbing again. She came back stronger than ever and has great accomplishments such as freeing "Luking Fear" 5.13c on El Cap and the first female first ascent of a 5.14b route, "The Optmist" at Smith Rock. Currently, Beth lives with Tommy in Estes Park, Colorado and they still climbs and has sponserships with Marmot, La Sportiva, and Petzl, to name a few.

[edit] Adam Osterhoff

Adam Osterhoff is an American Rock Climber with many accomplished ascents of boulder problems and routes worldwide. Born in Rhode Island, Adam's climbing career started at an early age, and by the age of 14 he was competing regularly on the JCCA (Junior Competitive Climbing Association) circuit. After graduating high school, Adam moved to Boulder, Colorado to attend the University of Colorado at Boulder with a major in architecture. There, Adam met Luke Parady, strong-house climber from the North East, who was his freshman roommate, and together they drove headlong into the Colorado climbing scene. Adam Osterhoff has grabbed ascents of boulder problems such as "Chunk o Blitz" V11 in Pawtuckaway, New Hampshire, "SUAPM" V10 and "Neil's Nuts" V10 in Lincoln Woods, Rhode Island and routes such as "Supernova" 5.14b in Rumney, New Hampshire. Adam is also noted to have put up the first ascents of hard boulder problems such as "Whispers of Wisdom" V11 at Emerald Lake, Colorado and "Barbwire Beard" V11 at Upper Chaos Canyon, Colorado. Adam also makes appearances in climbing videos such as the So Ill DVD and The Life. Currently Adam is an architect for a major Colorado construction and a traveling climber.

[edit] Sockets Direct Protocol

The Sockets Direct Protocol (SDP) is an InfiniBand specific Protocol defined by the Software Working Group (SWG) of the InfiniBand Trade Association (IBTA). It defines a standard wire protocol over IBA fabric to support user-mode stream sockets-compatible (SOCK_STREAM) networking over IBA. SDP utilizes various InfiniBand features (such as remote DMA (RDMA), memory windows, solicited events etc.) for high-performance zero-copy data transfers. SDP is a pure wire-protocol level specification and does not go into any socket API or implementation specifics. Work is under way to separate the SDP wire-protocol, which is transport and O/S agnostic, from the IBA transport dependencies. This separation will enable transports other then IBA fabrics.

SDP facilitates direct mapping of stream connections to InfiniBand reliable connections (or virtual circuits). IP-Over-IB (IPoIB) specifies a mapping of IP (both v4 & v6) protocols over IBA fabric and treats the IBA fabric simply as the link layer. IPoIB specification is currently being worked on by IETF working group and will be published as an IETF RFC. The IPoIB RFC will define the packet level format of IP packets on the IBA fabric, and also describes an InfiniBand address resolution protocol (IBARP).Conceptually, the IPoIB driver in Linux will look like a network driver and will plug-in underneath the IP stack as any standard Linux network device. The IPoIB driver exposes a network device per IBA port on the host system, and these devices could be used to assign (statically or dynamically - using protocols such as DHCP) IP addresses. The SDP stack simply makes use of these IP assignments for endpoint identifications.

SDP only deals with stream sockets (TCP), and if installed in a system, allow bypassing the OS resident TCP stack for all TCP connections between any endpoints on the IBA fabric. All other socket types (such as datagram, raw, packet etc.) are supported by the Linux IP stack and operate over the IPoIB drivers. The IPoIB stack has no dependency on the SDP stack; however, the SDP stack depends on IPoIB drivers for learning about local IP assignments and for address resolution.

Nowadays latest linux kernel can support the complete infinband modules

[edit] Bujidao

Bujidao are a four piece band largely from North Yorkshire, comprised of David Budd (lyricist/vocalist/guitarist), Laurence Budd, Jo Birch and Tom Du Plessis. They have been described as an amalgamation of Turin Brakes, Jeff Buckley, Cat Stevens, and Paul Simon.


Biography

The name ‘bujidao' reflects the broad eclectic background of the two founding members David and Laurence Budd during Bujidao's inception. Having played and sung together for the best part of 20 years David and Laurence decided to make it official in the spring of 2003 and went on to perform as an acoustic duo at venues in Manchester and Leeds until winter 2005. During this exploratory period, David and Laurence were able to develop the unique sound of bujidao and become a solid performing, and prolific writing, duo.

Early 2006 has seen the addition of a cellist in the form of Thomas Du Plessis, an anthropology student at the University of Manchester , and percussionist Jo Birch who, as well as being an incredible drummer, also brings expertise as a professional sound engineer and producer to the band.

Bujidao are appearing at venues throughout the North West during the Spring / Summer of 2006, including: Manchester 's Late Rooms and Night and Day Café, Leeds' Joseph's Well and New Roscoe, and as far afield as the Dart Music Festival in Dartmouth in May.

[edit] Bristol Combination

Bristol is one of, if not the, biggest rugby communities in England, and the Bristol Combination has played a vital role in sustaining local junior rugby, and providing many of the players who have gone onto represent Bristol, England and the British Lions. The Bristol and District Rugby Football Combination was founded in 1901 to promote rugby union and the mutual well being of rugby clubs in Bristol and the surrounding area. Clubs who are members of the Combination are also members of one of the two Constituent Bodies of Gloucestershire RFU or Somerset RFU. 53 clubs are members of the Combination, with Bristol Rugby Club as an honorary member.

The Combination runs cup competitions in the Bristol area, as well as sevens tournaments and merit table competitions for teams within clubs who do not play in formal leagues. The CHARLES SAUNDERS COMBINATION CUP is a knock out tournament organised by the Combination for local clubs. It also provides the Bristol and District Rugby Development Officer. It also manages a charitable trust ground.

Its representative side is a showcase for local talent, and has regularly provided a step up from junior rugby to the top of the game for players whose abilities might otherwise go unrecognised. This was more true in the past, but in recent years a number of players have gone on from the Combination to play for Bristol United (the club’s second team) and in some cases the first team as well.

Traditionally, the Combination has looked after the interests of the city’s junior clubs, while the senior clubs (Bristol and Clifton) have been concerned with competing on a national stage. More recently, Clifton have dropped out of the National Leagues and their position as the second team in the city has been taken by Dings Crusaders RFC.

[edit] Royal dynasty of medieval Serbia (?)

Royal dynasty of medieval Serbia. Stefan Nemanja, Grand Zhupan of Rascia, is considered a founder. His second eldest son, Stefan (the Firstcrowned) Nemanjic was crowned by the Pope. Nemanja's youngest, third son Sava (Sabbas) became a monk at an early age. He would become the first bishop of the Serbian Church and its founder as distinctive church organization in the Eastern Orthodox tradition (prior to that Serbs of Rascia and other Serbian dukedoms in Southeast Europe were mostly members of the Greek Orthodox Church, some being Catholic, and some gnostic Bogomil). Nemanja's eldest son, Vukan Nemanjic was the Prince of Zeta (today's Montenegro). Vukan has descendants who haved lived in Montenegro through the Ottoman period. Sucurs of Piva, Montenegro, and Zaharics of Uzice, Serbia, succeed him. Milutin and Dragutin Nemanjic ruled an extended Serbian medieval kingdom. Stefan Dushan Nemanjic, son of Stefan Decanski, married Jelena, sister of Bulgarian tsar Ivan Alexander. He expanded his lands from Macedonia (in Serbian kingdom) to Bulgarian and Greek parts of Macedonia (from Balkan mountain in Bulgaria to Kavala in northern Greece) and was crowned Emperor of Serbs and Greeks. Nemanjics ruling lineage ceased with Dushan's son Uros. The empire that included today's Serbia, northern Albania and Greece, and western parts of Bulgaria was divided between Mrnjavcevics, Lazarevics and Brankovics, feudal lords of medieval Serbia.

[edit] Richmond v. J.A. Croson Co.

Richmond v. J.A. Croson Co. 488 U.S. 469 (1989) Docket Number: 87-998 Abstract

Argued:


October 5, 1988

Decided:


January 23, 1989

Subjects:


Civil Rights: Affirmative Action Facts of the Case

In 1983, the City Council of Richmond, Virginia adopted regulations that required companies awarded city construction contracts to subcontract 30 percent of their business to minority business enterprises. The J.A. Croson Company, which lost its contract because of the 30 percent set-aside, brought suit against the city. Question Presented

Did the Richmond law violate the Equal Protection Clause of the Fourteenth Amendment? Conclusion

In a 6-to-3 decision, the Court held that "generalized assertions" of past racial discrimination could not justify "rigid" racial quotas for the awarding of public contracts. Justice O'Connor's opinion noted that the 30 percent quota could not be tied to "any injury suffered by anyone," and was an impermissible employment of a suspect classification. O'Connor further held that allowing claims of past discrimination to serve as the basis for racial quotas would actually subvert constitutional values: "The dream of a Nation of equal citizens in a society where race is irrelevant to personal opportunity and achievement would be lost in a mosaic of shifting preferences based on inherently unmeasurable claims of past wrongs."

[edit] Taynuilt, Argyll

Taynuilt is a small village on the west coast of Scotland with a population of around 800. The name Taynuilt means "House by the stream" and could refer to the present Taynuilt Hotel which stands at an ancient crossroads between Glasgow and Oban and is situated beside a stream. Taynuilt is set at the foot of Ben Cruachan which is a 3600 ft mountain with many rocky peaks. There are two large rivers running through the village, the River Awe and the River Nant, both flowing from lochs of the same name to Loch Etive. Taynuilt also contains a well preserved ancient iron furnace (1753) known as Bonawe Iron Furnace.

[edit] RIAS (Remote Infrared Audible Signage)

Remote Infrared Audible Signage (RIAS) is a system of infrafed transmitters broadcasting the messages ordinarily depicted graphically on textual signs. Its purpose is to enable print-handicapped (blind/low-vision/illiterate/foreign) travelers to access the information sighted people fluent in the local language obtain by visual means.

The system was developed at Smith-Kettlewell Eye Research Institute.

[edit] Discrete Event Systems

Discrete Event Systems (DES)are discrete state varibles and event-driven state transion systems. Some systems are naturally DES such as sequential digital systems. DES can be modelled using Automata or PetriNet.

Unlike DES, physical systems are continuous state varibles and time-driven state transition systems, which are modelled by differential or difference equations. However, physical systems can be modelled as DES by abstraction, which are called Hybrid Systems.
