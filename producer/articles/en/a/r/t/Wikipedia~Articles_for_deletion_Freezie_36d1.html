The result of the debate was keep. ugen64 21:32, 24 Mar 2005 (UTC)

[edit] Freezie

What is it about this Nintendo item called Freezie that makes it inclusion-worthy on Wikipedia? Or is every Nintendo item that can be cross-referenced between two videogames inherently noteworthy? "Freezie" +Nintendo = 209 unique google hits. [1] --GRider\talk 18:11, 7 Mar 2005 (UTC)

  • Strong Keep. Articles like this are one of the strengths of Wikipedia. Where else would all this information go, if not in this article? Meelar (talk) 20:16, Mar 7, 2005 (UTC)
  • See Super_Mario_Bros._3 - Merge all items related to the same game into one article for easy comparison. In this case, that would be both the Mario series. Radiant! 20:37, Mar 7, 2005 (UTC)
  • Delete, gamecruft. Wyss 22:16, 7 Mar 2005 (UTC)
  • Keep Andrew Lenahan - Starblind 22:17, Mar 7, 2005 (UTC)
  • Merge or keep. Information like this is one of the strengths of wikipedia. Kappa 22:18, 7 Mar 2005 (UTC)
  • Keep, like the others. Xezbeth 22:23, Mar 7, 2005 (UTC)
  • Delete like all the rest of this gamecruft. ComCat 04:00, 8 Mar 2005 (UTC)
  • Delete, not notable - 209 hits isn't really all that great for a game, nintendocruft. Megan1967 06:56, 8 Mar 2005 (UTC)
  • Keep, like Goomba and Koopa. These things are notable enough and of some interest to those interested in the Mario Bros. series. Sjakkalle 07:55, 8 Mar 2005 (UTC)
  • Trim, merge, and redirect, as above. -R. fiend 18:38, 8 Mar 2005 (UTC)
  • I will say merge with Mario Bros. and Super Smash Bros. Melee, redirect to the former. Andre (talk) 22:55, Mar 8, 2005 (UTC)
  • Keep-- There are a lot of articles just like this one that have never undergone VfD. 67.81.191.226; 09:32, Mar 9, 2005 (UTC)
  • Keep, but cleanup. I notice quite a few factual inaccuracies. The notion that Freezies were not in Ice Climbers (which is not a series) is absurd - that's where it originated. - Chardish 07:17, 10 Mar 2005 (UTC)
    • Comment never doubt the ability of gamers to dig up obscure trivia. Ice Climber was a series, consisting of the original, an arcade Vs. edition, and a deluxe updated Japan-only Famicom edition. There was also a GBA remake. Andrew Lenahan - Starblind 00:34, Mar 12, 2005 (UTC)

This page is now preserved as an archive of the debate and, like some other VfD subpages, is no longer 'live'. Subsequent comments on the issue, the deletion, or the decision-making process should be placed on the relevant 'live' pages. Please do not edit this page.
