[edit] Georgia State Route 10

not relevant to an encyclopaedia, more relevant on a route-planner Helzagood 22:19, 29 January 2006 (UTC)

  • Keep State route --Jaranda wat's sup 01:46, 30 January 2006 (UTC)
  • Keep All in 01:50, 30 January 2006 (UTC)
  • Keep all state routes. -- Grev 01:56, 30 January 2006 (UTC)
  • Keep. It is a state route. Carioca 02:20, 30 January 2006 (UTC)
  • Speedy Keep. For the time being, precendent dictates that all roads that an American state has bothered to number are worthy of inclusion. Youngamerican 02:32, 30 January 2006 (UTC)
  • Speedy keep per Youngamerican; nominator is unfamiliar with AFD guidelines. —Quarl (talk) 2006-01-30 13:24Z
  • This is not a speedy keep. A speedy keep can only arise if the nominator withdraws or the nomination was in bad faith or disruptive. Stifle 23:54, 31 January 2006 (UTC)
  • comment, I do not understand why a road is relevant to an encyclopaedia? Helzagood 01:25, 1 February 2006 (UTC), if interstate roads of the states are worthy of inclusion, does the same rule extend to interstate roads in other countries?Helzagood 01:37, 1 February 2006 (UTC)
    • Yes, it does. Please see Votes for deletion/Precedents. Crypticfirefly 04:12, 1 February 2006 (UTC)

Please accept my apologies, then, this article should remain. I still don't think roads are worthy of entry in an encyclopaedia, but rules are rules. 81.77.158.140 17:35, 1 February 2006 (UTC)

  • Delete line on a map belongs on an atlas, not in an encyclopaedia. --TimPope 22:32, 2 February 2006 (UTC)
  • Keep numbered highways. ...Scott5114 01:53, 3 February 2006 (UTC)
  • Keep per precedent that we keep all like roads.Gateman1997 05:21, 3 February 2006 (UTC)

I agree with you, TimPope, but I am afraid that the rules of Wikipedia do clearly state that interstate roads in all countries are valid articlesHelzagood 19:54, 3 February 2006 (UTC)
