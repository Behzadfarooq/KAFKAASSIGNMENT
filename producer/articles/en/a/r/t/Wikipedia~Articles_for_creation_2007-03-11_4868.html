[edit] Arthur Lincoln Pauls, D.O.

Archived discussion follows below. Please do not modify it.
This request for creation has been reviewed. The reviewer comments appear below the article text.

Arthur Lincoln Pauls, D.O. was a Canadian-born, British-trained doctor of Osteopathy who founded Ortho-Bionomy(R). Born on 2/12/29 in Saskatchewan, Canada, Arthur was in the Royal Canadian Air Force from 1948-1953. After his military service he worked as a postman, photographer, playwright, camera salesman and driving instructor. Arthur moved to England in 1958. Starting in 1960 in London, England, Arthur started to study Judo. He quickly ascended through the ranks and began to teach judo lessons in the mid-1960's eventually attaining the rank of 4th Dan (4 black belts).

In 1972, Arthur started studying Osteopathy since he had often sought treatment from osteopaths and could then assist his judo students recover from injury. While a student, Arthur encountered Dr. Lawrence Jones' "Spontaneous Release by Positioning". Due to his Judo instruction, Arthur felt that the manipulations of osteopathy were unnecessarily forceful and that there had to been a more efficient way to balance the patient's structure. Between his introduction to Jones' method and 1978, Arthur worked to evolve the concept that became "Ortho-Bionomy". By integrating Judo, osteopathy, Jones' Release techniques and homeopathy, Arthur created something different. He called his method "Ortho-Bionomy: The Evolvement of the Original Concept".

Arthur subsequently began teaching Ortho-Bionomy, first in Europe and later in the United States. Later he formed the Society of Ortho-Bionomy International(C) to assist students and Practitioners in their evolvements. This organization is one of several around the globe that carries Arthur's legacy of Ortho-Bionomy. Arthur's students are now found in Europe, North America, New Zealand and Australia.

Arthur and his second wife, Anne, had two children Tanya and Harreson, who are also Ortho-Bionomists and live in British Columbia, Canada.

Arthur Lincoln Pauls was the author of two books, "Shivambu Kalpa", published in 1978 and "The Philosophy and History of Ortho-Bionomy", published posthumously in 2002 by Arthur's family in B.C. Shivambu Kalpa dealt with the topic of auto-urine therapy, taking its title from the Sanskrit word for "water of Shiva". "The Philosophy and History of Ortho-Bionomy" details Arthur's development of Ortho-Bionomy and offers his perspective on the philosophy of Ortho-Bionomy.

Arthur passed away in 1997.

Bohn Hall is the largest dormitory at Montclair State University that houses approximatly 650-700 students. Most students are freshmen and sophomores, however, a minority of students are made up of junior and seniors. It is a co-ed dormitory with co-ed floors, however they are split by same-sex wings. There are some floors that are all girl, such as the 15th and 16th floors. There are same-sex resident assistant(RA) assigned to every wing. The director of Bohn Hall is Aryan Bryant and the assistant director is Caroline Cunha.

Residing on the main campus which is a dry campus, that is alcohol is NOT permitted on premises, Bohn Hall is a dry dorm. Rooms are designated as triples (holds 3 persons), doubles (holds 2 persons), singles (holds 1 person). All rooms contain windows and sinks, and accomadates every resident with a bed, desk, chair, and closet space.

In early March of 2006, a fire erupted on the 7th floor. The causes of fire are still under investigation. Although no one was hurt, one student, Alan Alam (age 20; Succasunna, NJ), had heroically carried his roommate Andrew DiServio (age 19; Freehold, NJ). That story was broadcasted on ABC channel 7 news. Students were allowed back to the dormitory later that day, however, those residing on the 7th floor were allowed to return 3 days after the incident.

In the summer of 2004, there was $1.2 million spent on renovations to the bathrooms in Bohn Hall.

[edit] Key West Literary Seminar

The Key West Literary Seminar is one of America's preeminent writers' festivals, held each January in Key West, Florida.

The Seminar's history dates to 1983, when writers began gathering for a conference in the small tropical town that had been home to Ernest Hemingway, Tennessee Williams, Robert Frost, Wallace Stevens, Ralph Ellison, Richard Wilbur, John Ciardi, John Dos Passos, and Elizabeth Bishop. Each year since, a growing international audience has attended lectures and panel discussions centered at the San Carlos Institute, a historic 19th-century building owned by the Government of Cuba. The seminar begins each year with the John Hersey Memorial Address and also features a series of writers workshops and receptions.

[edit] 2007

The 2007 Seminar, titled "Wondrous Strange: Mystery, Intrigue, and Psychological Drama," drew a roster of novelists and poets including Ian McEwan, Margaret Atwood, Joyce Carol Oates, Billy Collins, Michael Cunningham, and Jeffrey Eugenides.

[edit] Patrick Ruffini

Patrick Ruffini is a prominent American political consultant specializing in the use of the Internet. He is widely credited as one of the pioneers in the use of the internet in political campaigning.

Ruffini also is an active blogger on conservative and Republican Party politics, both on his own blog and as a contributor to Hugh Hewitt's blog. He also served as webmaster for the website of prominent columnist and author Michael Barone.

Ruffini was the webmaster for the 2004 reelection campaign of President George W. Bush. In that capacity he was a protege of Karl Rove and Ken Mehlman. The Bush re-election campaign that year was widely credited with linking their internet operations with the "72 Hour Task Force" voter turnout plan. Ruffini later served as ecampaign director for the Republican National Committee.

He is presently serving as an internet campaign consultant for the 2008 presidential campaign of Rudy Giuliani.

Ruffini is a graduate of the University of Pennsylvania. He presently lives in Northern Virginia.

[edit] Nefopam

Nefopam (Acupan, Ajan, Nefadol, Nefam, Oxadol, Pangesic, Silenton) is a centrally acting, non-opioid analgesic closely related to orphenadrine, phenyltolxamine, and diphenhydramine. Its analgesic potency is midway between that of aspirin and hydrocodone. It is widely used in European countries and Canada at this time. It is not currently used in the United States. It can be used either by itself, in combination with NSAIDs, or (especially for neuropathic pain) in conjunction with opioids.

[edit] Aysha Wills

Classical/Folk musician/Social activist Aysha Wills was born in Calgary in 1994, but spent her first five years in Hong Kong. At age 5 she moved to Edmonton and chose flute as her instrument. She subsequently picked up several other instruments, including Viola, Piano, Bass guitar, Guitar, Accordion, Piccolo, Tin Whistle and vocals. Aysha made many friends among Albertan musicians, both classical and modern, who appreciated her enthusiasm, raw talent and appetite for learning. When the tragic 2004 Indian Ocean earthquake struck Asia, Aysha called on friends such as Shelley Younge (Edmonton Symphony Orchestra), Stewart MacDougall (kd lang and the Reclines, Ian Tyson, Great Western Orchestra), Ron Rault (Willie and the Walkers, Pontiac), Haley Simons (Hammerhead Consort), multiple Juno Award winner Bill Bourne. and the Edmonton Symphony Orchestra to stage a benefit concert at the Francis Winspear Centre for Music, which eventually raised over $700,000. For her efforts, Aysha was awarded the Award of Distinction, the City of Edmonton’s highest honour, the Alberta Centennial Medal and was named Global TV’s Woman Of Vision. She also received congratulations from Prime Minister Paul Martin, Premier Ralph Klein and Lieutenant Governor Norman Kwong. Since then, Aysha has been involved with organizing the charity Change For Children’s 30th anniversary concert at the Francis Winspear Centre for Music, and a benefit for the victims of the July 2006 Java earthquake, as well as donating her talents to the Voices For Hospice charity concert at the Winspear in November 2006. She has played at the Edmonton Folk Music Festival, North Country Fair, the Selmar Theatre and the Arden Theatre with Bill Bourne, as well as contributing flute to his new CD. Aysha has studied and/or played with such internationally renowned artists as Chumbawamba, Maxence Larrieu, Paddy Keenan, K'naan, Marten Root, Peter Lloyd, Teddy Thompson, Philippe Bernold, Baka Beyond, Kevin Crawford, Chris Norman, Jean Michel Veillon and many others. She loves to collaborate and learn from master musicians from the whole spectrum of music. Aysha has been offered a place at the world renowned Amsterdam Conservatory, one of the top flute programmes in the world, and will commence her studies in September 2007 at the remarkably young age of twelve. She continues to be committed to social justice, and is presently working on her first CD with Bill Bourne producing. She will be touring extensively throughout 2007 in support of Bill Bourne’s CD ‘Boon Tang’.
