[edit] Spanish profanity

I hate I created this articles some months ago, it was doomed from the start as people added some meaningless phrases they probably fabricated. Put I (and me) out of its misery and delete it. εγκυκλοπαίδεια* 23:17, 21 December 2005 (UTC)

Do the same to Portuguese profanity as well. εγκυκλοπαίδεια* 23:18, 21 December 2005 (UTC)
  • Delete per nomination. Durova 23:41, 21 December 2005 (UTC)
  • Delete. I put the {{accuracy}} tag there just yesterday, because so many of the "definitions" were wrong, or just "missing" something. If not deleted, it at least needs to be completely rewritten. Or maybe this is the kind of thing that belongs in Wiktionary. For example, I've written articles there on chingar, pendejo and cabrón. --Andyluciano 00:00, 22 December 2005 (UTC)
  • Keep, part of Spanish and Latin American cultures. --Vizcarra 00:24, 22 December 2005 (UTC)
  • Withdraw nomination hereby do so as I am rewriting the article (from the Spanish article), hopefully with some help. It confuses me as to how inaccuracies are so wide in the article as the vast majority of those who have been working on the article are native Spanish speakers. εγκυκλοπαίδεια* 01:03, 22 December 2005 (UTC)
  • Keep, should be required reading for every student of the Spanish language. How else are you going to know what people are saying to you?-gadfium 02:38, 22 December 2005 (UTC)
  • Keep and let Encylcopedist rewrite. -JCarriker 02:54, 22 December 2005 (UTC)
  • Keep after rewrite--Bkwillwm 02:57, 22 December 2005 (UTC)
  • I change my vote to keep. Que hay esperanza. Andyluciano 03:26, 22 December 2005 (UTC)
