[edit] DE-CIX

DE-CIX is an Internet Exchange Point in Frankfurt (Germany). Founded in 1995, DE-CIX established an environment for the bilateral settlement-free exchange of Internet Traffic (Peering) between all types of Internet Service Providers including broadband and content providers. DE-CIX is the third largest Internet Exchange in the world. As of January 19th 2007, DE-CIX connected 197 members on 320 ports and the peak traffic rate was 112.8 Gbit/s. Originally DE-CIX was started by 3 German ISPs (EUnet, MAZ, XLink). Since 1994 the company DE-CIX Management GmbH is the operator of the DE-CIX. The company is a wholly owned subsidiarie of the eco Internet Industry association (a non profit organisation).

DE-CIX offers a variety of Peering services including Public Peering over a shared switching platform, dedicated Peering solutions over dark fiber and [colocation] services. The main service is the Public Peering Service. Internet Providers are connected at 9 Colocation centers throughout the city of Frankfurt. This invludes ancotel, Avaya, Interxion, I.T.E.N.O.S, Fibernet and Telecity.


DE-CIX is a founding member of Euro-IX, the European Internet Exchange association.

[edit] Murina cyclotis (round-eared tube-nosed bat)

This request for creation has been reviewed. The result is below.


[edit] Geographic Range

Murina cyclotis is widespread in Central and Southeast Asia. It is found in Sri Lanka, India, Nepal, as far north as northern Myanmar, south through Indochina, Vietnam, Malaysia and parts of Indonesia and the lesser Sunda islands. They're also found in the Philippine Islands on the islands of Biliran, Camiguin, Catanduanes, Luzon, Mindanao, Sibuyan, and Siqujor. (Bates and Harrison, 1997; Heaney, 2005; Lekagul and McNeely, 1988; Medway, 1983; Myers, Smith, and Lama, 2000; Wilson and Reeder, 1993)


[edit] Habitat

Murina cyclotis occurs at elevations as low as 250 m in the foothills to 1,500 m in the montane forests. Round-eared tube-nosed bats are found primarily in the tropical forests of southeast Asia. Coastal areas inhabited by M. cyclotis are bordered by the South China Sea and Philippine Sea, which suggests reliance on humid regions. (Bates and Harrison, 1997; Heaney, 2005; Medway, 1983; Phillips, 1980)


[edit] Physical Description

Round-eared tube-nosed bats range in weight from 9 to 12 g, with total length between 80 and 89 mm. Only one wingspan measurement (229 mm) has been reported for this species. Females tend to be slightly larger than males. Round-eared tube-nosed bats are diagnosed by tubular nostrils and short, round ears. They have triangular tragi, which are long, narrow, and tapering to a point. The anterior braincase is ballooned and the upper lip possesses a protruding fringe of hairs. Murina cyclotis has small eyes which are closer to the ears than the nostrils. Two different color patterns have been noted. In one color variant, the dorsal fur is a reddish-brown, with the hairs increasing in lightness from the base to the tip; the ventral fur is grayish white, with a brownish tinge. In the other color variant, the dorsal fur is grayer, without the reddish tinge; the ventral fur is grayish white without any brownish tinge. This species has a semi-transparent wing membrane, relatively long thumbs, and a tail shorter than the length of its head and body. The feet are hairy, small, and the claws are relatively long and sharp.

Three subspecies of M. cyclotis have been identified. M. c. cyclotis is found from northeastern India to Vietnam, M. c. eileenae is in Sri Lanka and has slightly darker and duller fur, and M. c. peninsularis is found on the Malay Peninsula and is identified by a relatively wider anterior rostrum and more massive teeth.

No seasonal variation in morphology has been reported. (Corbet and Hill, 1992; Lekagul and McNeely, 1988; Medway, 1983; Myers, Smith, and Lama, 2000; Phillips, 1980)


[edit] Reproduction

There is little information reported for mating systems in M. cyclotis. (Phillips, 1980)

There is little information reported for general reproductive behavior in round-eared tube-nosed bats. Pregnant females in Pahang, Malay Peninsula were reported to carry two fetuses in the months of February and May. (Medway, 1983)

There is no information reported for parental investment in M. cyclotis. However, as most bats, females primarily care for the young, nursing them until they can fly.


[edit] Behavior

Murina cyclotis individuals feed in open forest areas, and fly approximately 1.8 m above the ground. Their flight is slow but maneuverable. They roost in large dead and dry leaves of the cardamon plant. Several individuals often roost together on a single leaf or bunch of leaves, which serves to camouflage the bats. A closely related species Murina florium has been found roosting in enclosed plant-material nests and hanging nests of bird species, presumably to provide protection where other roosting options are not available. Another closely related species, Murina aurata, is thought to hibernate in trees during the winter. This suggests possible hibernation of M. cyclotis>, but this is not known for certain. (Bates and Harrison, 1997; Kingston et al., 1999; Schulz, 1998)


[edit] Communication and Perception

Round-eared tube-nosed bats detect their environment through echolocation and minimally through the visual field, as their eyes are small and reduced. They are also sensitive to chemical cues. (Phillips, 1980)

These bats make use of echolocation to avoid obstacles and capture airborne prey. Calls are frequency-modulated (FM), with low intensity and short duration. The calls are at high frequency, starting at 152 to 180 kHz and sweeping down to an end frequency of 43 to 86 kHz. This large bandwidth allows these bats to accurately locate targets in the cluttered forests they inhabit. Furthermore, the use of an extremely high echolocation frequency may reduce the need for both visual and other auditory signals. (Bates and Harrison, 1997; Kingston et al., 1999; Schulz, 1998)


[edit] Food Habits

Round-eared tube-nosed bats eat insects in the damp forests they inhabit. A closely related species, M. florium (Flores tube-nosed bats) has been observed to eat in a manner possibly similar to M. cyclotis. While eating a moth, M. florium individuals were observed perching by both feet and thumb claws. They placed separated pieces of the insect into the uropatagium. After feeding on the edible parts, they released the waste to the ground below. This posture was also used by M. florium to ingest fecal matter (coprophagy). (Bates and Harrison, 1997; Bonaccorso, 1998)


[edit] Predation

The reddish-brown color of M. cyclotis makes it difficult for predators to detect as they roost among dead leaves.

There are no known predators of M. cyclotis, however the skulls of a closely related species, M. florium, have been recovered from owl pellets. Most bats are preyed on by owls as they fly and by snakes at roosts. (Bonaccorso, 1998; Phillips, 1980)


[edit] Ecosystem Roles

Round-eared tube-nosed bats eat insects and impact their populations in the areas in which they live. However, they may be too uncommon to have much impact.


[edit] Economic Importance for Humans: Positive

Because they are insectivorous bats, round-eared tube-nosed bats reduce the number of insects in the areas they inhabit, making them beneficial for agriculture and in reducing insect-borne diseases.


[edit] Economic Importance for Humans: Negative

Murina cyclotis may carry and transmit diseases such as rabies to humans, although this possibility is statistically quite low.


[edit] Conservation Status

Round-eared tube-nosed bats do not appear to be endangered. They are one of the more abundant Murina species, but have not been studied extensively. Although individuals are relatively rare, the distribution is very broad. Because they haven't been studied well, it's possible that distinct regional populations have not yet been diagnosed. Their occupation of tropical forests in southeast Asia suggest that they will persist wherever forests persist. (Bates and Harrison, 1997; Phillips, 1980)


[edit] Other Comments

The type specimen is from Darjeeling, India. (Wilson and Reeder, 1993)

It's the Steven Wright of cartoons... very dry. I avoid, as much as possible, the flailing arms and bulgy eyes that tell you something is funny. I feel it's better to let the humor form in the mind of the reader. Basically they're funny pictures with funny titles."

Rob Esmay was raised in Tacoma, Washington, and attended college in Santa Clara, California. Esmay cites his favorite artists as Thomas Nast, Toulouse-Lautrec, Daumier, Bruegel and Goya.

Hello, My Name is "..." is a web-comic started by "J.Treece" of the Novacaine Lollipop forums (www.crisishour.com/forum/). It centers around one character who doesn't even know much about himself. He has a screw lodged in the left side of his skull, a red dotted line around his cranium, a bad buzz cut, a black eye, and a wallet with nothing in it but an unmarked credit card, and a buisness card that reads "Hello, my name is "...""

What happens from there? That's for the open list of artists to decide. The comic is illustrated and written by anyone who volunteers themselves. They stat by registering on the Novacaine Lollipop forums, and then post a page request on the right thread. The only rules for the artists are as follows:

"Rule #1. - You must ask to join in. You must be given a page by a mod or admin. It wouldn't be too smooth if you and someone else did the same page number having the story go into two different directions. So wait your turn.

Rule #2. - Don't try to end the story.

This is to keep it as fun an interactive as possible. Don't ask for page 5 and then kill off the characters, or have a cosmic being eat the planet they live on or something. Let's see how long this can live.

Rule #3. - No pinups (except on permission by an admin*)

To keep the story going and as entertaining as possible, No pinups should be used. There is a four panel minimum per page.

Rule #4. - You control the story.

The person chosen for a page is responsible for that pages content, and storyline. Please try to follow any story lines or plots created before your page. You may ask any member of the board to assist you in writing or drawing your page. At the same time they also have the right to accept or deny your offer. Please respect the other members on the board as well as the story we've all contributed to.

Rule #5. - Go nuts.

Push your imaginations to it's boundaries, and then a little further than that. Let's see the best of the best here. There are no restrictions on nudity, language, or mediums. Although the line between nudity and bad taste is obvious, so if you think something won't fly ask an admin. Things that won't fly with me are - Child Porn, Excessive sexual imagery, continuous bashing/abuse of genders, sexual preferences, and race."

AS it stands, pages are being reserved faster than they can be completed. The project promises to be lengthy, and will most likely satisfy the tastes of any web-comic reader.

Alan Palmieri

On August 3, 2006 Alan Palmieri www.jeffersoncountytn.gov/index.cfm/m/index.cfm/m/43 won the Jefferson County, Tennessee www.jeffersoncountytn.gov/ General Election for County Mayor by defeating a 20 year incumbent. Prior to this political victory, he served as Mayor of Jefferson City, Tennessee www.jeffcitytn.com/ for five non consecutive terms. One term was for two terms and the other was for three. Never defeated for his bid for Mayor, Alan elected to step down and allow someone else to fill the office. “I believe in term limits. I don’t think a person can continue to represent the people at the level needed when they serve too long.” “Starting out wanting to help solve problems, if one servers too long they become part of the problem instead of a solution.” “It’s best to server – step down and re-enter if one wants to sincerely serve the people.” “Stay too long and one starts working for his or her self instead of the people.”

During his five terms as Mayor, Alan never proposed or supported a property tax increase or a utility rate increase. Even during difficult economic times his tenure as Mayor was noted for financial stability. Each time he left office he did so leaving the city in better financial condition than when he entered office.

Being approached by residents in the White Pine community, Alan led the charge to protect the property of county residents when neighboring municipality Morristown (Hamblen County) attempted to take residents homes and land for an industrial park. He proposed, and the city council of Jefferson City supported, his plan to annex the area protecting the residents from losing their homes and land. Due to the annexation move, Morristown changed their plans and the residents of White Pine were protected.

Within his first ninety days as Mayor of Jefferson County, Alan proposed a financial plan to County Commission that would eliminate the county’s 65 million dollar debt load, finance a 60 million dollar school building project, develop a new industrial park and build a needed county complex, and building a cash reserve for future projects without increasing property taxes. On January 16, 2007 County Commission failed to take action on the proposal, even so far as to discuss it. Still response from the community has been overwhelmingly in support of his plan.

Alan has a diverse background in the private sector – international as well as domestic operation, government and politics. Prior to being elected county mayor he owned and operated his own management – government relations business and provided services to individuals and companies on a global basis. During his career, in addition to operating his own business, he has held top management positions with leading industry firms. Most recently he was Vice President and Director of Special Projects for GLG Life Tech www.glglifetech.com

GLG Life Tech is a global company with offices in the United States, Canada, and China. They have recently signed an agreement with Weider Global Nutrition www.weider.com/ to promote the highly recognized and respected Weider Nutrition name in China.

Working with the U.S Department of Labor and the Tennessee Department of Education, Alan helped promote one of the states most successful and recognized School to Career and Education Edge Programs. He also presented numerous seminars on working with departments of education and government agencies. In addition he served as a panelist evaluating various partnerships and quality of their programs. Alan has presented Professional Development Credit Classes in management and government relations at the University of Tennessee.

The owner of Palmieri Bodybuilding www.palmieribodybuilding.com an internet source for information on natural drug free bodybuilding. He has authored several books, courses, CD’s, reports, and material on bodybuilding. Articles and material have been published in leading bodybuilding periodicals and magazines such as MuscleMag International www.emusclemag.com/ “REPS!” Magazine, and the IFBB Newsletter. He has also appeared in such industry magazines as Muscle & Fitness www.muscleandfitness.com/. Alan is the author of the CD-ROM Vince Gironda Legend And Myth. The CD is recognized as the most complete single source of information on The Iron Guru Vince Gironda and his diet and exercise methods.

Alan has personally trained winners of major, local, and state bodybuilding events, high school and college athletes and teams, professional athletes and entertainers. He has served as a judge for numerous bodybuilding contests and promoted and held several bodybuilding events.

Alan has served as the past State of Tennessee President of the IFBB (International Federation of Bodybuilders) www.ifbb.com/ and was awarded the prestigious IFBB Certificate of Merit. He has also been recognized for his service toward the growth and development of bodybuilding and fitness and was presented with the IFBB Diploma of Honor. In October 2005 Alan was inducted into the respected Steve Speyrer Classic Bodybuilding Hall of Fame www.classicanatomymagazine.com/ located in Louisiana.

During the 1980’s Alan owned and operated a 30,000 square foot gym, one of the largest and best equipped in the southeast. For two years, he operated his own Karate / Self Defense studio. He also has wrestled professionally and worked as a bouncer and bodyguard.
