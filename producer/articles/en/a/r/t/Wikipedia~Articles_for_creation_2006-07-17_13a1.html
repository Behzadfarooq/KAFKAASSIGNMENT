I HISTORY

Kumai came from Bugis language “kembali ke pangkuanku”. It means “return to my lap”. Kumai was one part of Kutaringin Monarchy. It was lead by Pangeran Bendahara. But, he gave Kumai to Muhammad Cik (Kumai’s Village leader). And then he gave his position to the Amsar, and Amsar is choosen by government to be a regent in Kumai. Besides that, we could not find the others history, because there is no authentic evidence that we could found. Actually, there are many historical places in Kumai. It is because it was a basis for Indonesian fighter to attack Dutch and also Japanese. The tragedy was happened on January 14th 1946. Many our warrior were killed there, but they could maintain their base. And for remembering that moment, the government has built a monument and a park there. The name of the park is “Taman Bahagia”. It is located in Sei. Tendang and on every January 14th, all of the people there celebrate it for remembering Kumai’s fighter. Kobar regent always attends for it. Kumai District as the biggest district in West Kotawaringin regency is divided into three districts namely Kumai, Pangkalan Lada and Pangkalan Banteng districts. Those districts which have width about 4.456 km2 has been mutualy agreed by Local House of Representative (DPRD). The Regent at that time, H Abdul Razak signed the region regulation about the district forming in plenary meeting forum of council. The final result was a long process from various solutions with the first plan. The Modification happened either from the name of district and opinion about Kumai which was intended by government to divide into four political districts plus one regional political district of coastal area. In that plenary meeting, seven fractions of council supporter had agreed and had accepted the Raperda (Regional government meeting) dividing into Kumai District specified as Region regulation. " By saying ‘Bismillahirahmanirrahim’, we express to accept the Raperda become the Region regulation about regional forming of Pangkalan Lada district and Pangkalan Banteng, while Kumai as main district," said Sumanto, S.E, chief of Army Fraction of Republic Indonesia/ police. Regent of West Kotawaringin at that time, H Abdul Razak after signing the region regulation explained, the result of formula and agreement between regency goverment and DPRD have specified two new districts as a part of Kumai district. He said " By forming two new districts, we expect that it can support the public service in governance affairs, development issue and social affairs." The capital of Pangkalan Lada district is Pandu Senjaya ( ex Pangkalan Lada SP 4). The district is initialy proposed as a Center Kumai covering seven countrysides, namely Makarti Jaya, Pangkalan Lada, Pandu Senjaya, Lada Mandala Jaya, Sumber Agung, Sungai Rangit Jaya and Purba Sari countrysides. Karang Mulya is The capital of Pangkalan Banteng District (ex Natai Kerbau SP 4). The most east district initialy is proposed by as East Kumai covering eight countrysides that are Desa Mulya Jadi, Pangkalan Banteng, Karang Mulya, Marga Mulya, Arga Mulya, Amin Jaya, Sido Mulya and Kebun Agung. At that time, the Regent hoped, if two new district could have the larger ones opportunity in existing exploiting the potency of resources.

II MOTTO

The motto of Kumai is “Beriman” which is an acronym of Bersih (clean), Indah (beautiful), Ramah (sociable) dan Aman (peaceful). The motto itself literally is translated as “Religious”.

III GEOGRAPHY

Kumai has total area about 2.921,00 km2. In the north side, the location is near with Arut Utara district, Seruyan regency in the East, Java Sea in the South, and Arut Selatan district in the Western.

IV TOPOGRAPHICS

North: plain and kinds of land/ground which hold up to erosion Center: raddle, lake and bog. South: bog, alluvial which has many aqueous/water.

V ICLIM

There are 2 seasons in Kumai, that are dry and rain season. The temperature is about 20o-30o celcius, the mount inclination is about 0-100m and 0-80 %.

VI DEMOGRAPHICS

There are 39.086 people living in Kumai. Kumai has 155 RT (neighborhood administrative unit) and 23 RW (residence administrative unit).

The capital of Kumai District is Kumai. It is divided into 3 sub districts and 14 regions. The sub districts are Kumai Hulu, Kumai Hilir and Candi and the regions are Sei. Cabang, Teluk Pulai, Sei. Sekonyer, Batu Belaman, Pangkalan Satu, Kubu, Sei. Bakau, Sei. Bedaun, Teluk Bogam, Keraya, Sebuai, Sei. Kapitan, and Bumiharjo.

VII ETHNIC BACKGROUNDS

Majority of the people are Melayu and Banjarese. The others ethnic are Maduranese, Javanese, Bugisnese, Sundanese, Dayaknese, Chinese and some foreign citizens (volunteer, researcher and also tourist).

VIII RELIGION

Majority of the people are Moslems (85%). The others are Hindus, Buddhists, Christiants (Protestant and Catholic). Amount of religious service places are: 1. Mosque: 33 2. Small Mosque: 83 3. Church: 3 4. Other: 1

IX LANGUAGE

Kumai Language (Coastal Language) is the daily language of Kumai. It looks like Banjar language, because some of the resident originated from Banjar. The Ianguage in Kumai are highly varied because Kumai is a port city and many people visited it from others area.

1. TNTP (Tanjung Puting National Park) Tanjung Puting national park is a place for conservation area that has function as protection system of life. It protects many variety of plants, animal and the ecosystem in natural resources. Tanjung Puting National Park is the largest protected area of swamp forest in South-East Asia. Tanjung Puting first received a level of protection in 1935 and was later officially designated as a Wild Reserve in 1939. In 1982, it was declared as a National Park. The Park was established primarily for the protection of Orangutan (Pongo pygmaeus) and its formation was instigated and aided by the efforts of Dr. Birute Galdikas and Orangutan Foundation International. When this area become national park, Tanjung Harapan has moved into the other side of Sekonyer river (across the original area). At that time the original width establishment was 300,040ha. After the movement, it has increased into 415,040ha. The Park receives aid from Orangutan International Canada and it is recognized as a World Biosphere Reserve by the United Nations. Tanjung Puting occupies most of the peninsula between Teluk Kumai and the Seruyan River and consists of varied habitats including peat and freshwater swamp forests, lowland tropical rainforests and heath forests. The Park is drained by several black water rivers eminating from the northern and eastern parts. Tanjung Puting National Park is famous primarily because of two primates: the orangutan and the proboscis monkey (endemic to Borneo). At Camp Leakey research is conducted on Orangutans, Gibbons, other animals and ecological processes. At Camp Leakey orangutans are also rehabilitated and returned to the wild. Since 1971 over 200 orangutans have been returned to the wild as part of Dr. Birute Galdikas Orangutan Research and Conservation Project This park is divided into 4 places that have their each potency of natural resources. [edit] History of KCC Mall

KCC started in 1951 as a textile store in Koronadal City, South Cotabato. It then expanded in 1989 into a supermarket department store format called KCC Shopping Center.

In 1992, KCC opened its first store in General Santos City called KCC Warehouse Plaza. The warehouse club format helped it establish a small but promising foothold in General Santos City.

In 1996, KCC Warehouse Plaza converted into an airconditioned hypermarket cum mall concept and was renamed KCC Mall of Gensan. With renowned tenants such as Jollibee, Greenwich, Penshoppe, and others, KCC’s was able to better serve its customers.

After four more years and learning from its General Santos experience, KCC Shopping Center expanded into KCC Mall of Marbel, the first in Koronadal, bringing more options to shoppers there—four cinemas, mall tenants such as Jollibee, Greenwich, Chowking, Guess, etc. After a tragic fire in 2002, KCC Mall of Gensan rebuilt from scratch in mid 2004 and brought better shopping value for its shoppers.

The Institute for Private Enterprise is an Australian political group established for the purposes of promoting the cause of private enterprise and reducing the role of the government in economic affairs. Des Moore is the Institute's Director.[1].

[edit] History

After resigning in 1987 from the position of Deputy Secretary in the Treasury due to fears that government policy would lead to recession, Des Moore in 1996 formed the Institute with the aim of assisting in the promotion of private enterprise and greater deregulation of the Australian economy[2].

[edit] Beliefs

The Institute believes in a rolling-back of Government regulation in the Australian economy. The Institute however still believes in government assisting the disadvantaged, eliminating private monopolies and having a legal system which allows private enterprises to flourish by encouraging risk-taking and economic growth[3]. The Institute has focussed on reform of Australia's labour market in recent years, and has ties with the controversial H.R. Nicholls Society.

The PGM (in German :Prüfgemeinschaft Mauerbohrer e.V.) certifies hammer and percussion drill bits for concrete and similar materials. The PGM is located in Remscheid (Germany) and has been found in 1978. The drill bits have to fulfil close tolerances in order to secure, that the manufactured holes are suitable for safe anchor fixings. In a hole which is too big or not exact, the anchor fixing can not be safe.

Drill bits certified by the PGM are marked with the registered PGM mark on the drill bit itself and on the packaging.

The tolerances are monitored by the PGM. New developed drill bits have to proof their suitability for anchor fixings by extensive comparision tests. Additionally, the drill bit manufacturers must have state-of-the-art manufacturing facilities and a quality system, which is also monitored by the PGM.

The certification criteria are developed in close co-operation with the approval bodies for anchors and with leading anchor manufacturers.

[edit] Concentrated Photo Voltaics

The sun delivers its energy to the earth in a relatively diluted form, from 600-1200W per m2. The solar panel industry (Photo Voltaic or PV) has so far developed panels that use this energy, with a 12-14% efficiency so far. However, the current (2006) global shortage of high-quality silisium has meant a renewed interest in concentrating the solar rays before applying a PV panel.

Several manufacturers such as Spectrolab has been developing CPV panels for satellites initially, and are today achieving much higher efficiencies (35%) than traditional flat panel. Even if the cost is higher per cm2, the overall cost of a system may be lower as the concentration ratio equals the reduction ratio for the CPV material. With a 1m2 concentrator working at 200x ("200 suns"), the need for PV material is only 1/200 or 0,5%. 100x is commonly viewed as having the same

To concentrate the sun, there are a number of optical systems being proposed. Most are either based on fresnel lenses or parabolic mirrors. All CPV systems must track the sun from morning to sunset with high precision, however this is yields 25-30% more energy throughout the day.

CPV is a subset of CSP (Concentrated Solar Power) which also includes thermal systems.

[edit] Transpired air collectors

Transpired air collectors

Transpired air collectors use the sun's energy to heat cold outside air for use in buildings. In so doing, they reduce the amount of space heating needed to keep buildings comfortable on cold days. Since about 13% of the energy we use in the United States goes to heat homes and commercial buildings, this new technology could result in significant energy conservation, saving millions of dollars in heating costs in the process. And because transpired air collectors dramatically reduce utility bills, they can literally pay for themselves in just a few years.

This new technology provides an effective means for substituting renewable energy for fossil fuel consumption, particularly in cold climates.

How It Works

Transpired air collectors use a simple, elegant technology to capture the sun's heat to warm buildings. The collectors consist of dark, perforated metal plates installed over a building's south-facing wall. An air space is created between the old wall and the new facade. The dark outer facade absorbs solar energy and rapidly heats up on sunny days — even when the outside air is cold.

A fan or blower draws ventilation air into the building through hundreds of tiny holes in the collectors and up through the air space between the collectors and the south wall. The solar energy absorbed by the collectors warms the air flowing through them by as much as 40° F.

Unlike older space heating technologies, transpired air collectors require no expensive glazing, which caused energy loss due to reflection. The lack of glazing, together with design refinements, allow the new collectors to capture a record-breaking 80% of available solar energy.

Coastal Transportation is a U.S. flag marine transportation company offering scheduled, palletized cargo service between Seattle and Western Alaska, including the Aleutian Islands and Alaska Peninsula.

Coastal operates a fleet of cargo vessels averaging 200 feet in length with hold capacities averaging 63,000 cubic feet. All vessels are self-sustaining, allowing the flexibility to perform cargo operations both in remote Alaskan ports and alongside other vessels at sea. Coastal generally offers weekly sailings yearound with the exception of repair periods, which are normally scheduled during winter months.

Coastal operates cargo terminals in Seattle, Washington and Dutch Harbor, Alaska. Both facilities are capable of accommodating vessels up to 400 feet in length and 20 feet in draft. In Seattle, we offer connecting service via container, truck and railcar.

[edit] HFSLIP

HFSLIP
Developer: TommyP
Latest release: 60712 / 12 July 2006
OS: Microsoft Windows
Use: Windows Installation
Website: http://hfslip.org

HFSLIP is an advanced open source Hotfix and Service Pack slipstreaming utility designed to update Windows 2000 (SP4), XP (SP1, SP2), and 2003 (Gold, SP1) installation sources.

The binaries (individual files) of fully supported hotfixes are slipstreamed directly. This means that original files are being replaced with the newer versions present in the hotfix executables you can obtain from the Microsoft Download Center. In the rare occasion that a hotfix isn't supported (usually because of it being a non-standard package or requiring a special installation procedure), it is integrated instead. HFSLIP leaves these uncommon hotfixes or updates as they are and places them in the SVCPACK folder, to be installed automatically near the end of Windows setup.

[edit] Features

  1. Slipstreaming of Service Packs
  2. Slipstreaming of Windows Update Agent and Windows Genuine Advantage
  3. Slipstreaming of Internet Explorer 6 and Outlook Express 6 (Windows 2000)
  4. Slipstreaming of DirectX 9.0c (Windows 2000, Windows XP SP1 and Windows 2003 Gold)
  5. Windows Media Player 10 (Windows XP SP2)
  6. Windows Media 9/10 Codecs (Windows 2000, XP and 2003)
