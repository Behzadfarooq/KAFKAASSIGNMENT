[edit] Jeff Ooi

I'd say he's pretty non-notable, all he has achieved are a few contributions to a few Malaysian blogs.. Keep, see down. SoothingR 16:29, 31 January 2006 (UTC)

  • Keep. He's an influential Malaysian blogger. See Technocrati [1]. Try even Google [2]. And take a look at Reporters beyond Borders [3]. He might not be known worldwide but that's because the blog covers mainly Malaysian related stuff and majority of its readers are Malaysians. So, deleting this blog might constitute systemic bias. __earth (Talk) 06:42, 1 February 2006 (UTC)
  • Keep per Earth. Ardenn 07:38, 1 February 2006 (UTC)
  • Keep. Not only that, but Jeff Ooi was even mentioned by BBC News in the Malaysian lock-up detainee abuse scandal and also in two other BBC articles! [4] [5] [6] He's undeniably the most influential person in the Malaysian blogosphere. Seems a bit disturbing to me that you nominated an article for deletion based on a personal opinion and without doing an in depth search first. No offence intended though. --Andylkl [ talk! | c ] 12:21, 1 February 2006 (UTC)
I did do an in-depth search, but couldn't find these BBC articles which you refer to now. All information which I had found didn't seem to make him meet the notability-guidelines. I apoligize for my oblivion. Thanks for the information, and for this reason I'm changing my vote to keep.SoothingR 13:49, 1 February 2006 (UTC)
Ah, no problems about that. Though just curious, what led you to think he was in the Singaporean blogging scene? :) --Andylkl [ talk! | c ] 14:05, 1 February 2006 (UTC)
I stumbled upon Cowboy Caleb (who is Singaporean, according to his article) by accident. After I nominated that for deletion, I started wandering around in more articles relating to the Southeast-Asian blogscene.SoothingR 15:35, 1 February 2006 (UTC)
