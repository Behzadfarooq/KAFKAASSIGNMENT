[edit] List of computer and video games

This page is a list of lists that are all included in http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Category:Lists_of_games. -- Wikipedical 02:15, 9 January 2006 (UTC)

Update: I can see there are many supporters of this page, but I would like to clarify my position. I nominated this for deletion because it is an incomplete and selective list of lists. Categories are useful because they are in a sense 'list of lists,' and they are also more than that. I believe there is no point in trying to revise this page.

In response to recent discussion, I would like to point out that there are zero redpages on this page. -- Wikipedical 20:39, 9 January 2006 (UTC)

  • Mind you, thats because you created the redlinked List of ZX Spectrum games after the AfD debate started. Thanks for your contribution. -- Saberwyn - The Zoids Expansion Project 20:57, 10 January 2006 (UTC)

[edit] Keep

  1. Keep as the gateway to all our game lists. -- JJay 05:02, 9 January 2006 (UTC)
  2. Keep. There is no way for a category to encompass the existing sub-sections on the page, nor to include redlinks that indicate articles that need to be written. Turnstep 15:04, 9 January 2006 (UTC)
  3. Keep per Turnstep, looks like a useful list. feydey 15:42, 9 January 2006 (UTC)
  4. Keep — categories are often useless for multi-variate data. Some of the sub-lists include multiple parameters per game, so they can't be converted to lists without destroying information. This page organizes those lists nicely. — RJH 16:07, 9 January 2006 (UTC)
  5. Keep as this list is specific, contrary to the category which lists List of board games and other topics unrelated to computer and video games. Those interested in CVGs should be able to look this up. Punkmorten 19:55, 9 January 2006 (UTC)
  6. Keep - Allows more articles from redlinks, which is not possible with categories. Andros 1337 23:31, 9 January 2006 (UTC)
  7. keep please this list is useful Yuckfoo 03:38, 10 January 2006 (UTC)
  8. Keep. Good navigation aid. (Also, why does this article's AFD need these non-standard 'keep' and 'delete' sections?) HollyAm 00:40, 11 January 2006 (UTC)
    • Buggered if I know, Wikipedical reorganised it. Highly unconventional. -- Saberwyn
    • Maybe they want to see a running tally of the "votes"? Which kind of de-emphasizes the flow of a discussion. Turnstep 04:01, 11 January 2006 (UTC)
  9. Keep. It is a useful list. Carioca 03:38, 11 January 2006 (UTC)
  10. Keep this useful list. Categories may not work that well. --Andylkl [ talk! | c ] 11:22, 13 January 2006 (UTC)
