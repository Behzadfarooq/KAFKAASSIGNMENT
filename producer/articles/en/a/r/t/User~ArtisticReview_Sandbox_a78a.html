What personal weakness would you like to work on in the next few years and how do you think you might do this?

A personal weakness I would like to work on and refine is my confidence in communicating and presenting in front of others. I am capable of expressing my ideas and knowledge very well in my writing; however I have slight difficulty in communicating and discussing my ideas and knowledge in a class, or in front of groups of people who I am not acquainted with. In the next few years, my first step in combating my weakness will be to visualize and set a goal for myself, which will serve as my motivation to become a more confident, proficient and competent speaker. As I will be entering first year next year and will be taking many seminar courses, I will be introduced to professors who are well versed in presenting. By listening to good speakers, I can become familiar with the techniques and principles of what it takes to communicate effectively. It is important to practice constantly, and seize the opportunity to speak whenever I can. Entering a small program such as the McMaster Health Science program is ideal in overcoming this area of dissatisfaction. Studying in a small, intimate group will greatly help by giving me many opportunities to communicate my ideas couple with my reasoning. I be more comfortable speaking in a smaller group of people, and also have an increased opportunity for class participation which is a necessary component to be involved in. Attending such a University such as McMaster with such a rich academic stratosphere will be helpful through mere presence, due to the fact that I will have a fresh start and will have the opportunity to improve my profile as a confident and adequate speaker. With a positive goal, optimistic attitude, motivation, and practice, I am on the road to overcoming my weakness.

What are the values of the Program and what evidence can you describe to support that you subscribe to these values?

The values of the Program include the ability to work both independently and in small groups, an innovative and interdisciplinary approach to the study of health through problem-based and self-directed learning, inquiry skills and the application of critical thinking and inquiry to scientific study, creative experimental design, taking an active role in one’s own learning, and the interest in comprehending of all aspects of health. I believe my requisites and the work I have done in the International Baccalaureate Program provides evidence that I subscribe to these values. The IB requires students to take part in an interdisciplinary project called the Group 4 Project; a scientific investigation that requires a problem to be identified. With a team of six student over six weeks, we designed our own creative experiment, combined our knowledge and skills from Biology, Chemistry, and Physics, and applied our inquiry skills to make knowledgeable conclusions based on our results. Because each group requires one student from each science course, we were required to investigate the same problem from different perspectives; similar to the Program’s interest in understanding all aspects of health. Also, because I was the only chemistry in the group, I worked independently from a chemistry perspective, taking an active role in the project. Finally, we self-assessed our projects, and were peer-assessed, demonstrating self-directed learning and critical evaluation. I believe the extended essay which I wrote, I conducted independent research on the gender differences in mathematic ability. The essay demonstrated independence and self-directed learning, and honed my inquiry skills. A true testament of my subscription to the values of the Program
