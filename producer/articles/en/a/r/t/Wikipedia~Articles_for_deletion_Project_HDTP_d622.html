  • Don't Delete -- Give it a break. Nothing is being advertised; there is no need to delete an article which seeks only to inform. I fail to see an issue here.—The preceding unsigned comment was added by Eleriah (talk • contribs) 23:00, February 14, 2006.
    • Comment Please sign your messages with ~~~~.—The preceding unsigned comment was added by Drat (talk • contribs) 2006-02-14 04:07:30.
      • Comment Gah, the irony!--Drat (Talk) 13:36, 15 February 2006 (UTC)
  • Delete - Singular unreleased game mod, not notable as of yet anyway - Skysmith 12:12, 14 February 2006 (UTC)
  • Delete per above. Daniel Case 16:57, 14 February 2006 (UTC)
  • Don't Delete - Keep the article. I wrote it to inform people about the project. It isn't my fault people came along and put in a bunch of POV to make it sound like an advertizement. We need to fix the article not delete it completely. Besides the mod is in beta testing phase and is going to be released in a matter of weeks. --Jayson Virissimo 17:47, 14 February 2006 (UTC)
  • Delete. Can't be notable, since it hasn't even been released yet. If you wish to inform people about the project, do it on your website. --Kinu 17:50, 14 February 2006 (UTC)
    • Kinu I am not a part of the mod I am just a fan of Deus Ex; I don't have any say about what they put on their website. It doesn't make sense to say that it is not notable simply because it hasn't been released. There are hundreds of articles with the "This article or section contains information about a computer or video game in production." tag in them which goes to show that even unreleased material with a significant enough following is noteworthy.--Jayson Virissimo 17:54, 14 February 2006 (UTC)
      • Yes, but those are usually well anticipated games, etc. (as opposed to mods for games), that are made by known publishers with a known history.--Drat (Talk) 02:24, 15 February 2006 (UTC)
        • I see your point. Perhaps I will rewrite the article in several months time if it has gained significant popularity.--Jayson Virissimo 01:22, 16 February 2006 (UTC)
  • Delete per Kinu. —Wrathchild (talk) 17:59, 14 February 2006 (UTC)
  • Delete - The time for this will be if it's released and it gains a substantial following from Deux Ex players then it might warrant a mention in the Deus Ex article. exolon 22:09, 14 February 2006 (UTC)
  • Delete per Kinu Ruby 01:37, 15 February 2006 (UTC)
  • Delete per exolon. Isn't it funny how "Do not delete" and "Don't delete" only show up on this kind of article? Stifle 12:04, 15 February 2006 (UTC)
