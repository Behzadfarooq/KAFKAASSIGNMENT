  • What we are looking for is verifiability ie third party accounts from reputable sources of its influence on rugby. We are also looking for some sort of indication of notability under WP:WEB such as :
  1. Having been the subject of national or international media attention:
  2. A forum with more than 5,000 users that has made a verifiable impact beyond its own user community; or
  3. Having an Alexa ranking of 10,000 or better.

We are not interested in statements that x is a troll or y is an admin. What we are looking for is verifiable evidence that significant people in the rugby world contribute to the forum or that it has an impact on rugby as a sport. Capitalistroadster 06:03, 8 December 2005 (UTC)

  • Multiple personas from one person are sock puppets. From the sock puppet article:

    A meat puppet is a variation of a sock puppet; a new internet community member account is created by another person at the request of a user solely for the purposes of influencing the community on a given issue or issues.

    You have to concede that the current situation is almost exactly the definition of a meat puppet; it looks as if new members are being recruited on some sort of "save this article" campaign. Obscure does not = non-encyclopedic, but unimportant/unreferenced/non-notable does. The article isn't up for deletion because the topic is obscure, it's up for deletion because the article itself fails to assert the notability of the subject. This does not neccessarily mean that the subject isn't notable, just that the article doesn't provide evidence that it is. If you really want to save this article then take heed of the suggestions being made here: include references to the media, provide evidence that famous rugby players post there, demonstrate that the site has influenced some aspect of the game or society, show that the forum has been cited in a newspaper or other published medium. If the forum really has done that then I'm quite sure it is notable, but the article doesn't provide any evidence of this, see what I'm saying? Maybe the article doesn't do the topic justice. Heaven knows it's in a god-awful state at the moment. So rewrite the article, and maybe, just maybe, you can save it from deletion. What could then happen is that people here would re-read the improved article and hopefully change their votes because of the improvement.
