[edit] Kelly Brock

Kelly Brock (born October 15, 1967) is a Canadian singer, songwriter, recording artist and voice-over actor based in Vancouver, British Columbia.

Born in Vancouver, British Columbia, Brock's first band was with the North Vancouver cowpunk, roots-rock band, the Lost Durangos. She recorded her first record; a 12" EP entitled Evil Town with the four-piece group in 1986 when she was 18 years old.

After leaving the the Lost Durangos, Brock joined the new wave/pop/rock band Innocent III. They recorded a single in 1988, featuring the songs "Out Of Touch", produced by Brian 'Too Loud' Macleod and "You're Not There", produced by Paul Hyde, founding member of the Canadian rock group, The Payola$. After a few years of singing and fronting bands, Brock decided to pursue a solo career and embarked on co-writing and co-producing her own songs. To date, she has produced three commercially and critically successful independent CD's entitled, Kelly Brock (1997), So Close (2000)and Dirty Dishes (2002). Brock's songs have also appeared in a variety of film and television programs including Dawson's Creek, Smallville, Joan of Arcadia, The Chris Isaak Show, The Surreal Gourmet and critically-acclaimed film director, Anne Wheeler's "Better Than Chocolate".

She is currently working on her latest CD endeavor entitled, Rebel Kind, a sassy, fun, country-rock album with her long-time co-writer and co-producer, Craig Zurba. The CD is scheduled to be released in January 2007 to Canadian retailers and on the internet at: CDBaby.com and iTunes.

Brock is also a voice-over actor and studio session singer and has provided her speaking and singing voice on many advertising campaigns including: Tourism Whistler, Encore Recycling, Sask Tel, Shaw Cable, Alaska Digi-Tel, Church’s Chicken, Just Ladies Fitness, Canadian Cancer Society, Open Road Toyota, The BC Lions, Mastercard and Yukon Pepsi, to name a few. Kelly has also lent her talent to some narrative documentaries including: Vancity Community Leadership Video, Alberta Department of Safety: Booster Seats, First Citizen’s Forum and Interior Health's Recruitment Video.

She has also been performing live for many years at music festivals, in night-clubs and at sporting events. As well, she also sings back-ups with the popular 80's rock group, Loverboy and will be featured in their up and coming live concert DVD release.

[edit] RackStar

[edit] Introduction

The Rackstar Server Appliance Software is a free-to-use open source server appliance system developed by RAQTweak based on the Sun/Cobalt RaQ 550 interface software and a modified version of the Fedora Core 4 operating system. The Rackstar software runs on any type of system including Cobalt RaQ servers.

[edit] History

Together with renowned names such as Tim Hockin, Patrick Baltz and other ex-Sun/Cobalt Networks developers RAQTweak rewrote Cobalt RaQ software code, which had been released by Sun Microsystems under BSD license on December 23, 2003.

Leslie Herps, founder of RackStar, announced the development of the RackStar Server Appliance software in the summer of 2004. Since then the software was released as beta software. The final version of the Server Appliance OS was released under the name Rackstar Server Appliance 1.0 in May 2006.

[edit] License

The RackStar software is open source and includes open source plugins and is therefore free of charge.

[edit] Features

Some features are:

  • Security update technology
  • 7 supported languages
  • Different GUI styles
  • Automated Dns
  • SMTP-AUTH
  • Microsoft Frontpage Extensions Support (2002/2003)
  • Apache::ASP
  • Apache_Tomcat 5 / JavaServer_Pages
  • MySQL / Postgresql Plugin

[edit] Tom Wilson - Detroit Junior Wings

Tom Wilson was the General Manager of the Detroit Junior Wings from 1974 to 1979. His team captured the National Junior A Championship in the 1974-1975 season. Notable players on that team were Mark Wells and Ken Morrow both of the 1980 Miracle on Ice team.

Wilson later moved to coaching the junior A team in the Great Lakes Hockey League (Michigan) while still serving as General Manager. Wilson held many other positions in amatuer hockey including: Director of Public Relations (Detroit Junior Wings) 1972-1974 ; General Manager & Coach (Datavision Falcons)1980-1984; Executive Director (St. Clair Shores Saints) 1976-1979

His leadership and fierce competitivess was his trade mark during his hockey years in Michigan.

[edit] Howard Zahniser

This request for creation has been reviewed. The result is below.


Howard Clinton Zahniser (1906-1964) was born on February 25, 1906, in Franklin, Pennsylvania, and grew up in nearby Tionesta, Pennsylvania along the banks of the Allegheny River close to the Allegheny National Forest. He attended college at Greenville College in Greenville, Illinois, where he graduated with a B.A. in 1928.

Zahniser went on to become executive secretary of The Wilderness Society from 1945 through 1964. In his capacity with The Wilderness Society, Zahniser drafted the federal Wilderness Act in 1956, which was introduced in the United States Congress that same year -- in the House of Representatives by Congressman John P. Saylor of Pennsylvania, and in the Senate by Hubert Humphrey of Minnesota. Zahniser was the main proponent of the Wilderness Act over the subsequent eight years that it took to pass the legislation, including overseeing numerous rewrites and attending all 18 public hearings on the bill.

Zahniser died of heart failure on May 5, 1964, just a few months before President Lyndon Johnson signed the Wilderness Act into law in September of that year. The Wilderness Act established America's National Wilderness Preservation System, which today permanently protects more than 106 million acres of federal public land for the benefit of future generations of people and wildlife alike. Wilderness areas must be designated by act of Congress under the framework of the Wilderness Act that Zahniser created on Forest Service, National Park Service, U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service, and Bureau of Land Management lands. There have been more than 100 seperate wilderness bills signed into law since 1964. Every United States President since 1964 has signed substantial wilderness legislation during his time in office.

Zahniser is buried in the Tionesta Riverside Cemetary, his rough-hewn marker, taken from the surrounding forest, facing his beloved Allegheny River.

[edit] Zentropa (film company)

Zentropa, also called Zentropa Production and Zentropa Entertainments, is a Danish film production company, one of the largest in Scandinavia. It was founded in 1992 by director Lars von Trier and producer Peter Aalbæk Jensen. Zentropa owns many subsidiary companies and a huge studio complex, Filmbyen, where many of Denmark's other leading film companies are located. Zentropa is known for going aggressively against conventional thinking. In 1995, it launched Dogme95, at set of rules designed to challenge the conventional film language. Though initially ridiculed, Dogme95 resulted in such critically praised films as Thomas Vinterberg's Festen (later also a play). In 1998, through its subsidiary company Puzzy Power, Zentropa became the first manstream studio to produce hardcore porn videos. In 2003, Zentropa produced Lars von Trier's Dogville, starring Nicole Kidman and filmed on an empty soundstage with the set merely indicated by chalk marks on the floor. Most of the company's radical experiments spring from Zentropa's co-owner Las von Trier. The company was named after Lars von Trier's film Zentropa.

[edit] The Fight (book)

'The Fight' is writer Norman Mailer's account of the historic 'Rumble In The Jungle'; the fight between Mohammed Ali and George Foreman that took place in Kinshasa, Zaire on October 30th 1974. It is a factual account of the fight itself (Mailer, along with many other American writers and reporters, was there in person), as well as an analysis of the racial and social climate surrounding it.

'The Fight', Norman Mailer, Little, Brown & Co 1975

[edit] Commodore John W. Anderson, Master Mariner and Captain of the Superliner S.S. UNITED STATES

Commodore John W. Anderson was the longest serving captain of the S.S. UNITED STATES, the fastest ocean liner in history. In 1952, he relieved Commodore Harry Manning as master of the superliner after the recordbreaking voyage on which she broke the translantic speed record previously held by the QUEEN MARY and captured the Blue Riband for America.

Commodore Anderson was born in 1899 in Jersey City, New Jersey to parents of Norwegian and English descent. His grandfather spent twenty-five years at sea in Norwegian sailing ships. He entered the New York Nautical School (which later became the SUNY Maritime College)in 1915, and served as a cadet aboard the schoolship NEWPORT, formerly a gunboat in the Spanish-American War. After graduation he went to sea as a quartermaster and junior officer on several famous old liners like the MANCHURIA and NEW YORK. He later sailed to Australia, India and China on freight ships and tankers as chief mate and obtained his first command in 1921. In 1934 he was appointed to command the AMERICAN IMPORTER, his first passenger ship for United States Lines, trading to the British Isles. One notable voyage took place in 1935, when he transported Colonel and Mrs. Charles A. Lindberg and their son Jon to England after the kidnapping of their younger child.

Following the outbreak of World War II, the Swedish motor liner KUNGSHOLM was seized at New York, placed under the American flag and purchased by the U.S. government. The United States Lines were appointed agents by the War Shipping Administration to run the ship as a troop transport and Commodore Anderson was selected to command her. The ship, renamed JOHN ERICSSON, left New York at the end of January 1942 in convoy with a full complement of 6,000 U.S. soldiers for Australia and Noumea. Their ultimate mission was to garrison the island of New Caledonia. Returning to the Atlantic, the JOHN ERICSSON resumed her mission and by the close of the war had safely transported some 300,000 troops to various theaters of war, despite frequent attacks by enemy submarines and aircraft.

In 1940 Commodore Anderson took command of the S.S. WASHINGTON, the second largest U.S. passenger liner of the time, and in 1949 he was named master of the 34,000-ton luxury liner AMERICA, then the nation's largest and finest passenger liner. While in command of the AMERICA, Commodore Anderson gained a reputation for outstanding seamanship, unfailing good nature and insistence on crew discipline and courteous service that helped dispel the myth that American passenger ships were inferior to European liners.

At the end of World War II, the nation's leading naval architect, William Francis Gibbs, was asked to design a supership of great size and speed that would not only be the finest luxury liner afloat, but would also be capable of carrying a full division of troops in wartime. The result was the 53,000-ton superliner UNITED STATES, built in close cooperation with the Maritime Administration and the U.S. Navy. In September 1952, following the voyage on which she broke the speed record previously held by the QUEEN MARY, crossing the Atlantic in just 3 days, 10 hours and forty minutes at an average speed of nearly 36 knots, Commodore Anderson assumed command of the UNITED STATES. One year later he was appointed Commodore of the entire United States Lines' fleet of 55 passenger and cargo vessels.

The passenger lists of the UNITED STATES overflowed with the names of the social, artistic and political luminaries of the era. Among the passengers who crossed the ocean with Commodore Anderson were former Presidents Eisenhower and Truman, Senator and Mrs. John F. Kennedy, Eleanor Roosevelt, Emperor Haile Selassie, Prince Rainer of Monaco and his bride, Grace Kelly, Greta Garbo, Cary Grant, John Wayne, Spencer Tracy, Katherine Hepburn, Marilyn Monroe, Sir Laurence Olivier, Marlon Brando, Sean Connery, Walt Disney, Tennessee Williams, Leopold Stokowski, Leonard Bernstein, Salvidor Dali, and even the Mona Lisa, travelling in a tightly guarded suite enroute to the Metropolitan Museum of Art exhibition in 1963. Perhaps the most celebrated couple were the Duke and Duchess of Windsor, who made their first trip in 1952 and never crossed the Atlantic on another liner.

In 1956 the UNITES STATES began to experience labor problems that would eventually lead to her layup. One of the most serious came in February 1957. A tugboat strike had been in progress for a week and when the ship arrived in New York the port was at a standstill and there were no tugs to help her dock. Described by the New York Times as "the loneliest merchant mariner in the port" that day, Commodore Anderson successfully docked the huge 990-foot, $78 million dollar liner without tug assistance and without mishap, a navigational feat he would have to perform again on several occasions, including the final voyage before his retirement.

Commodore Anderson ended his career in 1964, having served as master of the UNITED STATES for 12 years. During his long career he received many honors. In 1957 he was presented with the Merchant Marine Achievement Award by the American Legion, and in 1958 he was cited by the Council of American Master Mariners for outstanding maritime achievement.

Commodore Anderson died on February 15, 1976 and his uniform and other S.S. UNITED STATES memorabilia are on display at the Maritime Industry Museum at Fort Schuyler.

[edit] Ganapati Upanishad

Tha Ganapati Upanishad is a blessing relating to the Hindu God Ganesh (also Ganessa and Ganesha.) It is part of the text of the Atharvaveda, one of the four Vedas (sacred Texts.)


"Om ! O Devas, may we hear with our ears what is auspicious;

May we see with our eyes what is auspicious, O ye worthy of worship !

May we enjoy the term of life allotted by the Devas,

Praising them with our body and limbs steady !

May the glorious Indra bless us !

May the all-knowing Sun bless us !

May Garuda, the thunderbolt for evil, bless us !

May Brihaspati grant us well-being !

Om ! Let there be Peace in me !

Let there be Peace in my environment !

Let there be Peace in the forces that act on me !

Auspiciousness to those who hear ---- thus the Santi.

1. Om Gam. I bow to Ganapati.

2. You clearly are the tattva. You alone are the creator. You alone are the maintainer. You alone are the destroyer. Of all this you certainly are Brahman. You plainly are the essence.

3. Always I speak amrta. The truth I speak.

4. Protect me. Protect the speakers. Protect the hearers. Protect the givers. Protect the holders. Protect the disciple that repeats. Protect that in the east. Protect that in the south. Protect that in the west. Protect that in the north. Protect that above. Protect that below. Everywhere protect! Protect me everywhere!

5. You are speech. You are consciousness. You are bliss. You are Brahman. You are being-consciousness-bliss. You are the non-dual. You are plainly Brahman. You are knowledge. You are intelligence.

6. You create all this world. You maintain all this world. All this world is seen in you. You are earth, water, air, fire, ether. You are beyond the four measures of speech. You are beyond the three gunas. You are beyond the three bodies. You are beyond the three times. You are always situated in the muladhara. You are the being of the three Saktis. You are always meditated on by yogins. You are Brahma, you are Visnu, you are Rudra, you are Agni, you are Vayu, you are the sun, you are the moon, you are Brahma, bhur-bhuvah-svar.

7 Ga is the first syllable, after that the first letter, beyond that m, then the half-moon all together. Joined with m, this is the mantra form.

8 The letter ga is the first form, letter a the middle form, m the last form. Bindu the higher form, nada the joining together, samhita the junction. This is the vidya of Lord Ganesa.

9 Ganaka is the seer, nricad-gayatri the metre, Sri Mahaganapati the devata. Om ganapataye namah.

10 Let us think of the one-toothed, let us meditate on the crooked trunk, may that tusk direct us.

11. One tusk, four arms, carrying noose and goad, with his hands dispelling fear and granting boons, with a mouse as his banner.

12. Red, with a big belly, with ears like winnowing baskets, wearing red, with limbs smeared with red scent, truly worshipped with red flowers.

13. To the devoted a merciful deva, the maker of the world, the prime cause, who at the beginning of creation was greater than nature and man.

14. He who always meditates thus is a yogin above yogins.

15. Hail to the lord of vows, hail to Ganapati, hail to the first lord, hail unto you, to the big-bellied, one-tusked, obstacle-destroyer, the son of Siva, to the boon-giver, hail, hail!

16. He who studies this atharva text moves towards Brahma. He is always blissful. He is not bound by any obstacles. He is liberated from the five greater and the five lesser sins. Evening meditation destroys the unmeritorious actions of the night. At both evening and morning he is liberated from the bad and he attains dharma, artha, kama and moksa.

17. This atharva text should not be given to those not pupils. If from delusion a person so gives, he is a bad person.

18. He who wants something may accomplish it by 1,000 recitations of this. He who sprinkles Ganapati with this becomes eloquent. He who recites this on a fourth day becomes a knower of vidya. This is an artharva saying: ‘‘He who moves towards Brahmavidya is never afraid.’’ He who worships with fried grains becomes famous and becomes intelligent. He who worships with sweet-meat (modaka) gains the desired fruit. He who worships with samit and ghee by him all is attained, all is gained by him. He who makes eight brahmËnas understand this becomes like the sun’s rays. In a solar eclipse, in a great river, or in front of an image having recited (this) he gets accomplished in the mantra. He becomes liberated from great obstacles. He is freed from great misfortunes.

Praising them with our body and limbs steady !

[edit] Michael Schultheis

American painter Michael Schultheis has a background in mathematics and economics and is inspired by the incredible beauty and elegance in the world of analytics. His work explores the visual models that most effectively communicate the essence of math. By allowing the paintings to operate like a chalkboard, gradually filling up with notations and illustrations, he shows the progression of ideas on a topic. In this way, he translates the intricate world of numbers and relationships into something everyone can see, and invites the viewer to consider the often-unseen relationship between math and human experience.

Michael received a B.A. in Economics from Washington State University, studied Math at the University of Washington, and received an M.S. in Labor Economics from Cornell University. He also studied Italian and History in Siena, Italy. He currently lives and works in Seattle, WA. A recent body of his work was exhibted in 2006 at the National Academy of Science in Washington, DC.

[edit] Fractal Design Corporation

Fractal Design Corporation was a 1990s software company that created, and/or developed, a variety of artistic software products that have outlived the company itself. Notably these were Painter 3D, Ray Dream Studio (today Carrara), Expression (today Microsoft Expression). These products tended to be cross-platform for MacIntosh and Windows.

Ray Dream Studio was a 3d modeller/animator with an unusual user interface whereby objects hovering inside the corner of a cube could be manipluated by dragging their shadows on any of the 3 visible walls of their container. Ray Dream was cross-platform for MacIntosh and Windows, fairly easy for a non-expert to use, and cheap ($200 in 1994). Ray Dream eventually became Carrara Carrara_(software) and is now sold by Eovia Corporation.

Expression was a 2-dimensional image editor with the unusual feature of combining a bitmap editor and a vector graphics editor in the same layout. It was written by Water_Lou. This software was originally supported on MacIntosh and Windows, but changed hands several times becoming Creature_House_Expression and is now Microsoft Expression.

Painter 3D was for digitally "painting" bitmapped images onto the surface of computerized 3d models. It was purchased by Corel and evolved to Corel Painter IX until it was discontinued.

Fractal Design Corporation was founded by Tom Hedges and Mark Zimmer in 1991, merged with MetaCreations in 1997, and divested itself of all its graphics software products in 1999. Today what is left of MetaCreations is ViewPoint Corporation[4], which appears to no longer sell graphics software.

[edit] Susan Dory

American painter Susan Dory creates abstract paintings, which investigate issues of repetition variation, time and precarious balance. Her most recent body of work is rooted in nature and combines intuitive and unusual color combinations, which allow the compositions to grow and evolve organically.

From her artist's statement:

"I am interested in creating complexity in my work through the repetition of a single element and pay particular attention to the geometry created within the negative spaces of my mark making.

I embrace and celebrate the modernist tenants of minimalism, which champion the ideas of repetition, sparseness and restraint. I employ these notions as a starting point when composing my paintings, however during the creative process I yield to my desires and end up creating physical depth through the plasticity of paint. In the end, I actualize a more maximalist approach to painting.

The layers of paint in my work encapsulate moments of time, creating visual documents of my daily practice of painting. In the finished work, there is a notable topography on the painting's surface, which is due to the thick applications of paint and multiple layering.

The palette reflects my fascination with psychological responses created by color, specifically how it can evoke personal memories akin to the effects of olfactory memory. Through color, I continue to investigate memory, the history of private life, and the notion of sentimentality."

Susan Dory has received numerous awards and recognition for her work including the Pollock-Krasner Foundation Grant, the Willard R. Espy Foundation Artist-in-Residence Fellowship and the Behnke Foundation’s Neddy Artist Fellowship.

[edit] Himilce Novas

Himilce Novas is a novelist, poet, playwright, historian, and radio commentator. She has written for The New York Times, The Christian Science Monitor, Connoisseur, and Cuisine, and is also the author of seven acclaimed published books, both fiction and non-fiction. She teaches American Literature and Writing and lectures widely on Latino history and culture.

Born into a family of prominent intellectuals with a journalistic tradition, Himilce Novas grew up in New York City and showed early signs of a promising writing career when, still in her early teens, several of her poems were published in a literary journal by Nobel Prize winner Camilo José Cela. As she rose through the ranks of the publishing industry in Doubleday & Co, Scholastic, Time Inc, etc, Novas continued her creative writing pursuits, turning her pen to playwrighting. Her play, "Free This Day: A Trial in Seven Exhibits," was published by Joseph Papp in Scripts and produced off Broadway to great critical acclaim.

Novas is the author of numerous works of fiction and non-fiction. Her fiction works include Mangos, Bananas and Coconuts: A Cuban Love Story (Arte Público Press, 1996; Riverhead/Penguin, 1997 paperback) and Princess Papaya (Arte Publico Press, 2005). Among her works of non-fiction are the popular Everything You Need to Know About Latino History (Plume/Penguin 1994;1997;2003); Latin American Cooking Across the USA (Knopf, 1997); La Buena Mesa (Knopf, 1997); Latino Art and Culture in the United States (The National Museum of American Art, Smithsonian Institution, 1996); Everything You Need to Know about Asian American History (Plume/Penguin, 1996; 2003 ); The Hispanic 100: A Ranking of the Latino Men and Women Who Have Most Influenced American Thought and Culture (Citadel Press, 1995); Remembering Selena: A Tribute in Pictures and Words / Recordando a Selena: Un tributo en palabras y fotos (St. Martin's Press, 1995).

Himilce Novas has taught literature and writing as visiting author at vatious colleges and universities, among them the University of California at Santa Barbara (UCSB), Wellesley College and Tulane University.

While living and teaching in Santa Barbara, CA, Novas produced and hosted THE NOVAS REPORT, a talk radio program dealing with books and other cultural and contemporary issues from a unique international perspective and a solution-oriented approach to government, education, lifestyles and the arts. The Novas Report ranked as the Number One weekend talk-show.

Aside from her professional and literary endeavors, Novas, who resides in the West but spans both coasts, is an eloquent motivational speaker, lecturing across the country at the most prominent universities and prestigious speaking forums. [edit] Betsy Eby

American painter Betsy Eby’s work emerges from her love of material, surface and the attraction that is created when boundaries are pushed. Her mixed-media material consists of molten beeswax, pigment and damar varnish. She manipulates the viscosity then engages in torching, spattering, dripping and layering her material. Not only is Eby addressing the formal structure of composition and color, she must balance this with the dimension of the wax itself, the way the wax obfuscates color, all the while building to a finished surface that glistens like poured resin. It is a process of constant push and pull. The result is paintings uniquely transparent, with lush and active surfaces, which are simultaneously sensual and contemplative.

[edit] Himilce Novas

68.104.202.232 22:25, 7 October 2006 (UTC)Himilce Novas22:25, 7 October 2006 (UTC)68.104.202.232 Himilce Novas is a novelist, poet, playwright, historian, and radio commentator. She has written for The New York Times, The Christian Science Monitor, Connoisseur, and Cuisine, and is also the author of seven acclaimed published books, both fiction and non-fiction. She teaches American Literature and Writing and lectures widely on Latino history and culture.

Born into a family of prominent intellectuals with a journalistic tradition, Himilce Novas grew up in New York City and showed early signs of a promising writing career when, still in her early teens, several of her poems were published in a literary journal by Nobel Prize winner Camilo José Cela. As she rose through the ranks of the publishing industry in Doubleday & Co, Scholastic, Time Inc, etc, Novas continued her creative writing pursuits, turning her pen to playwrighting. Her play, "Free This Day: A Trial in Seven Exhibits," was published by Joseph Papp in Scripts and produced off Broadway to great critical acclaim.

Novas is the author of numerous works of fiction and non-fiction. Her fiction works include Mangos, Bananas and Coconuts: A Cuban Love Story (Arte Público Press, 1996; Riverhead/Penguin, 1997 paperback) and Princess Papaya (Arte Publico Press, 2005). Among her works of non-fiction are the popular Everything You Need to Know About Latino History (Plume/Penguin 1994;1997;2003); Latin American Cooking Across the USA (Knopf, 1997); La Buena Mesa (Knopf, 1997); Latino Art and Culture in the United States (The National Museum of American Art, Smithsonian Institution, 1996); Everything You Need to Know about Asian American History (Plume/Penguin, 1996; 2003 ); The Hispanic 100: A Ranking of the Latino Men and Women Who Have Most Influenced American Thought and Culture (Citadel Press, 1995); Remembering Selena: A Tribute in Pictures and Words / Recordando a Selena: Un tributo en palabras y fotos (St. Martin's Press, 1995).

Himilce Novas has taught literature and writing as visiting author at vatious colleges and universities, among them the University of California at Santa Barbara (UCSB), Wellesley College and Tulane University.

While living and teaching in Santa Barbara, CA, Novas produced and hosted THE NOVAS REPORT, a talk radio program dealing with books and other cultural and contemporary issues from a unique international perspective and a solution-oriented approach to government, education, lifestyles and the arts. The Novas Report ranked as the Number One weekend talk-show.

Aside from her professional and literary endeavors, Novas, who resides in the West but spans both coasts, is an eloquent motivational speaker, lecturing across the country at the most prominent universities and prestigious speaking forums. [edit] Holland Symfonia

Created in January 2002, Holland Symfonia is the merging of the Nederlands Balletorkest and the Noordhollands Philharmonisch Orkest. Both orchestras with their own rich cultural history in the Netherlands. This combined expertise ensured the creation of a dynamic orchestra which can be used in a wide variety of musical settings. Holland Symfonia has three main tasks: performing symphonic concerts, accompanying ballet-, opera- and theatre groups and providing the public with ‘specials' and educational projects. The Philharmonie concert hall in Haarlem is the orchestra's home while the Muziektheater in Amsterdam is used for its musical accompaniments. In the 2006/2007 season Holland Symfonia will celebrate its fifth anniversary.

Since the creation of the orchestra, Holland Symfonia has been the permanent partner of Het Nationale Ballet and it regularly works in unison with the Nationale Reisopera and the Nederlands Dans Theater. In addition, Holland Symfonia accompanies a number of choirs including the Toonkunstkoor Amsterdam.

[edit] Zivilcourage

"Zivilcourage", a German word, expresses the concept of engaged social responsibility. In its usage in the 20th Century, it refers to the courage to oppose fascism. Because this term has no exact correlative in the English lexicon, it should be adopted and cross referenced to the entry in the Wikipedia German edition: http://de.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zivilcourage .
