Magnant is an RTS which features the military expansion of a colony of ants; since the main objective of the game is to destroy all enemy colonies.

Magnant enters properly in the RTS category and is somehow considered as an ant simulation game; it is not considered completely an ant simulation game because it concentrates on many unrealistic ant features, such as: Ants carrying weapons, ants using siege weapons such as catapults or ballistics, etc.

John C. Friend-Pereira[1] is an Irish political (former student) activist and has been active in Irish and European Student Movement. In 2002 he was a member of the Executive Committee of ESIB. Prior to that he served as Equality Officer in USI for 2001-2002. He was also active in his local Students Union in Mary Immaculate College from 1998-2004 and in the NUI Galway Students Union from 2004-2005. John C. joined the Labour Party in 2003 and was the Public Relations Officer for the Party in Galway West and Laois/Offaly. In June 2006 sought the party nomination for the 2007 General Election. He is currently for the National Learning Network in Tullamore and is the Public Relations Officer for the Labour Party in Laois/Offaly for the General Election.

[edit] Early Days

John C. was born in Tullamore Regional Hospital in January 1979. Both his parents were teachers in the Tullamore area until their recent retirements. He was educated at Ballinamere NS just outside Tullamore and later at Colaiste Colm C.B.S in Tullamore and also at the Marist College, Athlone.

[edit] Education

John C. studied Liberal Arts in Mary Immaculate College and received as B.A (Hons) in History and Media & Communications Studies in 2004. He then studied International Human Rights Law in the Irish Center for Human Rights at NUI Galway and received his LL.M (Hon) in 2005. In 2005 he also worked with Prof. Katrina Tomaseski UN Special Rapporteur on the Right to Education (1998-2004). His masters thesis was titled ['An SWOT Analysis of the Right to Education in Ireland']

[edit] Professional

Since completing his studies John C as worked as a Manager for Dunnes Stores and is currently working for the National Learning Network has the Facilator of the Fresh Start programe in Tullamore. John C as a number of publications which can be found on his website [www.johnc.ie ]

[edit] Mary Immaculate College Students Union

In Mary I. he became involved in the Students' Union, first as a class representative and later as Welfare Officer 1998-2000 andthen President of the S.U 2000-2001. During this time he gained a lot of experience in relation to education, housing, equality and health issues. During his time in Mary I, John C. became invovled in a wide range for activities including: The Social and Political Society, International Student Society, Gluaisteacht and USI.

Working in Mary Immaculate College Students' Union (MICSU), his many achievements encompassed the following:

[edit] Stephen Street, Melbourne

This request for creation has been reviewed. The result is below.
If your request was declined and you want to make changes and try again, copy and paste your article into a new submission and make your changes there.


Stephen Street, was established in April 1837 as one of the 8 north/south streets on Robert Hoddle's original survey of Melbourne. It was situated between Russell Street to the west and Spring Street to the east.

Garryowen surmises a few years after its naming that "Stephen Street is a tribute to a permanent under-secretary of state for the colonies." [2]

It is suggested that it was named after Sir James Stephen, the Permanent Underdersecretary of State in London who was at the peak of his power within the Colonial Office at the time of the naming of the streets of Hoddles Grid. [3] Alternatively, it could have been named after his father of the same name who die in 1832 after a distinguished career in the service of England.

Stephen Street, was renamed Exhibition Street to celebrate the 1880 International Exhibition and the 1888 Centenial Exhibition held at the Royal Exhibition Buildings during the 1880's Boom times of 'Marvellous Melbourne'. Its name was officially changed by the Nelbourne City Council on 5 December 1898.[4]

The Exhibition Buildings are not actually on Exhibition Street, instead, the Western Entrance faces Rathdown Street, Carlton which is a continuation of Exhibition Street.

[edit] Ashok Jhunjhunwala

Dr. Ashok Jhunjhunwala is a Professor in the Department of Electrical Engineering, Indian Institute of Technology at Chennai, India and was department Chair till recently. He received his B.Tech degree from IIT Kanpur, and his MS and Ph.D degrees from the University of Maine. From 1979 to 1981, he was with Washington State University as Assistant Professor. Since 1981, he has been teaching at IIT Madras.

Dr.Jhunjhunwala leads the Telecommunications and Computer Networks group (TeNeT) at IIT Madras. This group is closely working with industry in the development of a number of Telecommunications and Computer Network Systems. TeNeT group has incubated a number of technology companies which work in partnership with TeNeT group to develop world class Telecom Access products. The group has also incubated a company which aims to install and operate telephone and Internet in every village in India.

Dr. Jhunjhunwala is a Director in the Board of SBI. He is also a Board member of several Telecom and IT companies in India, including Polaris, Sasken, Tejas and HTL, NRDC, and IDRBT. He was a former Board member of VSNL & BSNL.

Most Latin verbs are regular (meaning they follow the general pattern). The verb ending changes according to the person (I, you, he, she, it, we, you, they) and the tense. A verb does not need the person stated to keep its meaning. eg. rather than saying "ego porto (I carry)" you would use, simply "porto"

Present Tense

Latin endings
Person Singular Plural
1st o mus
2nd s tis
3rd t nt

The letter that comes before "re" (normally "a", "e" or "i")in the infinative of the verb is added before the endings listed above.

Example: portare (infinative)

        porto- I carry
        portas- You carry (singular)
        portat- He, she or it carries
        portamus- We carry
        portatis- You carry (plural)
        portant- They carry

Kevin "The Baptist" Murray is a little known Scottish phenomenon who it is claimed possesses miraculous, magical powers.

He is by no means a Holy Man or even a religious man, but his powers have led to him being nicknamed 'The Baptist' after a certain incident. The incident in question happened sometime in 2006. When at a social gathering a reveller took ill, The Baptist cupped water in his hads and allowed the victim to drink deeply, Splashing a little on his face. Almost instantenously the illness of the victim vanished And he felt great and was able to carry-on with the celebration of the night.

Again in 2006, he was seen to allow an unacceptable container to hold far more liquid than should have been possible, with only the application of his right index finger.

Most notable of his powers though are his powers of ordaining temporary bad luck on an individual of his choice, known as a hex or a jinx[[6]] Witnessed by many people, The Baptist is seen to make a gesture of his hand and muttering some words in a low gutteral chanting and the individual is plagued by a temporary bout of bad luck. This works mostly in competitive circumstances where the opposition has been a "jammy git" and deserves the wrath of The Baptist.

Nobody knows where his powers come from, but many theories have been derived. Foremost amongst these theories is that on a visit to Kashmir, a younger Kevin Murray stumbled upon the tomb of a Holy Man known as Yus Asaf[Yus Asaf]. Upon inspection of the shrine he found a single die (or dice) on a neck-rope and took it with him. Upon putting on this makeshift medallion his powers came to him. This can not be 100% confirmed or denied, but The Baptist is regualrly seen sporting a necklace fitting this description which he regularly uses to "roll the dice one last time"

Another, less popular myth is that upon a visit to Oak Island, The baptist accidentally discovered the entrance to the legendary Money Pit[Money Pit] and somehow is now ordained with a secret power it was holding. This may explain the apparent impossibility of entering said Money Pit.

Many other theories have arisen: He is the second coming of Christ, he is the descendant of Christ, the Sang Real of Templar legend, or simply that he makes the whole thing up.

Needless to say, his powers are growing. A recent addition to his impressive repertoire is reported to be some sort of mystical head garment, looking like a ten gallon hat or stetson giving him great powers of perception and foresight. Reports that he uses this to regularly win high-stakes poker matches are unestablished.

Whatever the reason behind his powers, real or not, The Baptist is definitely a living Scottish legend and as one report says "a helluva guy"

Griffith Jenkins Griffith (January 04, 1805 ‚Äì July 06, 1919), was an American immigrant from Bettws who made millions in a mining syndicate in the 1880s. He donated 3,015 acres to the city of Los Angeles which became Griffith Park, he donated the money to build the park's Greek Theater and Griffith Observatory.

On September 3, 1903, Griffith shot his wife in a Santa Monica hotel, however she jerked her head to one side and was able to save her life, unfortunately it left her disfigured and blind in one eye. Griffith only served two years in San Quentin likely due to the fact that his defense team was lead by the ex-governor of California.

In 1912 he offered to donate $100,000 to the city of Los Angeles to build an observatory, however the city refused the offer responded, in part "On behalf of the rising generation of girls and boys, we protest against the acceptance of this bribe . . . This community is neither so poor nor so lost to sense of public decency that it can afford to accept this money.". However several years after his death the city did accept the offer.

The harsh, aggressive, metallic tone of Dog Day Sunrise's music is emblematic of the band's honest and independent attitude. A musical recipe of 2 decades of life and pain, combined with their individual influences in many genres of music to create a tasty dish that is addictive for many of today's heavy music fans. This must see band, combines intelligent lyrics, heavy melodic guitar, and a massive rhythm backbone to achieve a ground breaking sound that proves to hold it's own in the industry today.

Dog Day Sunrise was originally formed as a power-trio in 1996. A year later, 1997 saw the first glimpse of what this group had to offer in the form of their first studio recorded "Demo", a cassette comprised of 3 of their original songs. 1,000 copies of this "Demo" had been pressed, and within two weeks of its release, every copy had been sold. From 1997 to 2000 Dog Day Sunrise concentrated on live performances over the North East as well as new material for their first full-length release entitled "Chemical Carnival" (Currently there are over 12,000 copies of the record independently distributed in the US). With their music and reputation spreading rapidly, Dog Day Sunrise was becoming a household name for heavy music fans.

In Dec. of 2002 Dog Day Sunrise hit the studio with legendary recording producer Alex Perialas (Metallica, Anthrax, Testament, Violence, and many others), at Pyramid Studios in New York to record their second full-length release Seasons That Suffer. Currently the band is supporting the record with live performances, online exposure, and radio play covering many of the North-Eastern US states.

Dog Day Sunrise has toured extensively in about 10 states and has shared the stage with such national acts as Fuel, Dope, Dog Fashion Disco, Raging Slab, LA Guns, Bang Tango, Joan Jett, Jackyl, Blue Oyster Cult, Survivor, Eddy Money, along with many others. 2003 promises to add to this growing list of national exposure.

Dog Day Sunrise has gathered an enormous fan base over the years with an online street team of over 8,000 members and proves to be growing daily. The band plans to continue touring the north east this year and will begin working on showcasing to labels at the beginning of summer 2003. With their newest singles 'Outtayermind', 'Loneliest Day', and 'Wrench' climbing the local and regional charts, D.D.S is ready to graduate from regional performances to the national scene.

A marriage by proxy, or proxy marriage, is one in which a third person physically stands in for an absent party in the marriage. Thereby, either the bride or the groom is not present at the ceremony.

Many countries do not carry out or recognize marriages by proxy, and currently in the United States, they may only be carried out in four states: California, Colorado, Texas and Montana, often limited to armed forces posted overseas.
