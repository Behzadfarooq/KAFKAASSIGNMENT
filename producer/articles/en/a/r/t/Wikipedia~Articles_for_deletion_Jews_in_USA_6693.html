  • Regarding the Copy-vio aspects of this discussion:
  1. This page is not empowered by make copyvio decisions.
  2. Copy-vio discussion is therefore off-topic here, and a disruption of the VfD process.
  3. The previous discussion of copy-vio does not cast doubt on any votes, since no one gave copy-vio as the reason for their vote.
  4. It has long been policy (but i have made no effort to be sure there have been no changes) that the introduction of copyright-protected material cannot be remedied by removal of the problematic material from the article by editing, so it requires the special permissions needed to remove it from the history as well.
  5. The reason for prohibiting editing of a page under copy-vio consideration is that it is impractical to extract the new contributions from every copy-vio-ed page, leading to loss of those contributions. Thus
    1. Those who edit an article that already contains a Copy-vio notice deserve to have their contribs go to waste.
    2. Those who edit an article after the introduction of material that is later determined to be copyright protected, but before the Copy-vio notice is added, may have a legitimate gripe; i don't know if there is a solution in practice, but they should discuss the situation with someone experienced with copy-vio handling.
  6. In the case of this page, post-copyvio-notice editing has apparently already begun, and taking it off VfD won't fix that.
  7. I am adding this article to the copy-vio page, on the basis that there are disputed opinions here of copy-vio, and that is where the expertise on what is or isn't a violation is assembled. (Copyright law is a matter for experts, despite opinions of others that they understand everything about it.)
  8. If the article is voted for deletion under VfD (which seems likely, given the 9-Del/1-Keep tally), and completes before a copyvio decision is reached (which seems plausible, with a little over 3 days left), the copy-vio consideration becomes moot.
  9. Therefore (and especially since there is no agreement about the copyvio being real), IMO this VfD should continue.
  10. Two interested editors delayed voting under the influence of the copy-vio confusion, and others may have been put off by it without saying so. Therefore if any additional Keep votes from well-established registered users come by the end of the 28th, the possibility of the misguided discussion changing the result becomes plausible, and i will re-link this page so discussion continues until the end of the 30th, as if it had been nominated today, so there is no question of it being deleted without 5 full days of consideration.
  11. I will transfer the copy-vio discussion presently on this page, where it is an irrelevant distraction and impediment, to the copy-vio page, replacing it with a note and link to its new location.
