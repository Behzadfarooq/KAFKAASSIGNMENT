[edit] RuneScape Holiday Drop Items

RuneScape is a MMORPG by Jagex.


If you already play RuneScape, you'll have a good enough feel to understand this article. On special days, players can recieve certain items - many of which have no purpose except to look good or be "fun" - and these items cannot be obtained any other way, ever again, making them the rarest and most expensive items in the game. The most coveted item is the legendary Party Hat. These have no purpose - except that they do look good on your head - but are more like trophies for players who want to demonstrate they are masters of the game: enormously wealthy, powerful, experienced, and knowledgeable. The reason anyone wearing a Party Hat is all of these things is: the Party Hats are absurdly expensive at tens or hundreds of millions in gold coins! For most players, 50 or 100 million gold pieces is a ludicrous amount. In Draynor Village, there is an NPC - and old man who is retired from adverturing - in the house across the street from the bank. Approach him, and you will notice he wears a Blue Party Hat on his head! If you ask to buy it from him, he denies, saying he is too proud of it and worked too hard for it to sell or trade it. To obtain a Party Hat, you need to trade with a player in the game.


The dropping of rare items on holidays such as Halloween or Christmas is a well-known tradition throughout the RuneScape land. These items would be found in random places at certain times during the special day, and anyone who found one would have a great surprise! Since these items were only dropped on that day, they are considered rare and have become quite valuable (expensive too!).

Q&A

Here are some common questions about Holiday Items, along with their answers.

Q: Will there still be holiday item drops? A: There aren't item 'drops' exactly. Instead, NPCs are apparently going to visit RuneScape during the holidays and hand out a special item. These characters stay in RuneScape for a week or 2 and will freely hand out holiday items to any player that wants one.

Q: Why did Jagex stop doing those original drops on holidays? A: Holiday drops were stopped after the "Halloween 2003 Debacle." A great deal of people complained about not getting a Halloween Scythe, and Jagex decided not to have another item drop after that. Q: How do I get a Holiday Item? A: In order to get a Holiday item, you need to trade from another player. All tradeable holiday items are quite expensive. Scythes and Bunny Ears are not tradeable, and only people who managed to find one on that specific Holiday have one.

Q: What were holiday drops like? A: In past holidays, special holiday items would be dropped all over RuneScape in random places and different times during the day. Usually there would be several drops, and people would be running around trying to pick one up.

[edit] Prabhupada is 'popular' name of the founder of ISKCON also known as Hare Krishna movement.

His Divine Grace A. C. Bhaktivedanta Swami Prabhupada appeared in this world in 1896 in Calcutta, India. He first met his spiritual master, Srila Bhaktisiddhanta Sarasvati Gosvami, in Calcutta in 1922. Bhaktisiddhanta Sarasvati, a prominent religious scholar and the founder of sixty-four Gaudiya Mathas (Vedic institutes) in India, liked this educated young man and convinced him to dedicate his life to teaching Vedic knowledge. Srila Prabhupada became his student and, in 1933, his formally initiated disciple.

At their first meeting Srila Bhaktisiddhanta Sarasvati requested Srila Prabhupada to broadcast Vedic knowledge in English. In the years that followed, Srila Prabhupada wrote a commentary on the Bhagavad-gita, assisted the Gaudiya Matha in its work, and, in 1944, started Back to Godhead, an English fortnightly magazine. Single-handedly, Srila Prabhupada edited it, typed the manuscripts, checked the galley proofs, and even distributed the individual copies. The magazine is now being continued by his disciples in the West.

In 1950 Srila Prabhupada retired from married life, adopting the vanaprasta (retired) order to devote more time to his studies and writing. He traveled to the holy city of Vrndavana, where he lived in humble circumstances in the historic temple of Radha-Damodara. There he engaged for several years in deep study and writing. He accepted the renounced order of life (sannyasa) in 1959. At Radha-Damodara, Srila Prabhupada began work on his life's masterpiece: a multivolume commentated translation of the eighteen thousand verse Srimad-Bhagavatam (Bhagavata Purana). He also wrote Easy Journey to Other Planets.

After publishing three volumes of the Bhagavatam, Srila Prabhupada came to the United States, in September 1965, to fulfill the mission of his spiritual master. Subsequently, His Divine Grace wrote more than fifty volumes of authoritative commentated translations and summary studies of the philosophical and religious classics of India.

When he first arrived by freighter in New York City, Srila Prabhupada was practically penniless. Only after almost a year of great difficulty did he establish the International Society for Krsna Consciousness, in July of 1966. Before he passed away on November 14, 1977, he had guided the Society and seen it grow to a worldwide confederation of more than one hundred asramas, schools, temples, institutes, and farm communities.

[edit] Vascular Medicine

Vascular medicine is an emerging field in the US. It is a medical specialty devoted to the evaluation and management of patients with diseases relating to arteries, veins and lymph vessels.

Arterial diseases include the aorta (aneurysms/dissection) and arteries supplying the legs hands, kidneys, brain, intestines. It also covers arterial thrombosis and embolism; vasculitides; and vasospastic disorders.

Venous diseases include venous thrombosis, chronic venous insufficiency, and varicose veins.

Lymphatic diseases include primary and secondary forms of lymphedema.

It also involves modification of risk factors for vascular disease like high cholestrol, high blood pressure.

Currently there is a shortage of practitioners in this field. Mainly due to lack of training programs and lack of awareness in patients and physicians of this as a distinct medical specialty. With an increasing burden of vascular diseases in our again population, the establishment of a critical mass of physicians with interest in vascular diseases is paramount.

In 2005, the first vascular medicine boards were administered by the American Board of Vascular Medicine. (www.vascularboard.org)

Vascular Medicine Training Programs:

1. Cleveland Clinic- Ohio. This is the nations largest and most reputed training program. Typically a one year fellowship which consists of 3 months in vascular medicine consult service, 3 months in the vascular ultrasound laboratory, 3 months in the outpatient clinic including wound care clinic, and 15 day rotations in vascular surgery, interventional radiology, interventional cardiology, hematology and pathology, research block and elective.

2. Mayo Clinic

3. Harvard

[edit] Ain't Waistin' Time No More

"Ain't Waistin' Time No More" is a song off the Allman Brothers Band album Eat a Peach. The song was written by Gregg Allman in late 1971 as a tribute to his brother Duane Allman who had been killed that October in a motorcycle accident. The song speaks to the idea of the fleeting nature of life with lyrics such as "time goes by like hurricanes, and faster things." It is the opening track for 'Eat a Peach,' and would also come to serve as a tribute to Berry Oakley, the band's bass player, who was killed in November 1972 in a motorcycle accident near the same location as Duane Allman's fatal crash.

[edit] son ar chistr

A traditional folk song from Brittany, a region in France, called 'Bretagne'. The title means 'song of cider'. It is covered by a couple of artists in the past decades. Sometimes the song is known under 'son ar ghistr'.


[edit] History

Originally written in 1929 by Jean-Bernard and Jean-Marie Prima. It was published for the first time by Polig Monjarret in 1951. It was supposedly used by the Dutch socialist party as their hymn.

[edit] Giorje Kaniouras

Mr. Giorje Kaniouras (born August 8, 1974) is a Canadian citizen and prominent Torontonian. He is a banker and a noted public speaker who has worked tirelessly to enhance the image of Toronto, Ontario, Canada on the global scene. Giorje has written on what it means to be a Canadian as a child of immigrants, how Toronto is well positioned to benefit from increased globalization, and given many speeches on the Canadian economy and the effects of the ever changing social milieu on Toronto.

Giorje was born in East York Hospital the evening of August 8th, 1974 at 8PM to Evangelos and Sophia (nee Eliopoulos) Kaniouras. Giorje's parents arrived independently on Canadian shores as part of the wave of mass Southern European immigration that enhanced and changed the dynamics of many Canadian urban centers from the 1960's onward. Sophia immigrated to Toronto from Kalamata, Hellas in 1968 and Evangelos immigrated to Montreal from Trikala, Hellas in 1966.

Giorje's early years were spent in the Annex, an ethnically diverse, central Toronto neighbourhood that shaped Giorje's outlook on multiculturalism and globalization. The family moved to Vancouver, BC when Giorje was in his early teens as his father started a large scale food services company. This move allowed Giorje to appreciate the rising political power of Western Canada that was to come decades later. Upon the family's return to Ontario they relocated to Scarborough, Ontario a blue collar borough of Toronto. This was a pivotal time in Toronto's history as the city was undergoing radical changes ethnically (new areas of immigration), economically (manufacturing vs. service economy) and politically (conservative vs. liberalism). Giorje has wrote that this was the most important time in recent Toronto history as it created new power groups and entrenched Toronto as the centre of Canada's social and economic life.

Giorje attended Western Ontario Universe and York University before embarking in banking career with the Royal Trust of Canada. This experience exposed Giorje to more traditional Toronto power cent res and allowed him to focus on bringing together these traditional groups with emerging ethnic power cent res. Giorje is currently employed as a Director with the National Bank Canada and focuses much of his energy on speaking to the public on foreseeable economic and social trends, which he believes Toronto should benefit from. He has sat on many Toronto committees and lobby groups and recently been approached on numerous occasions to run for a seat in local politics.

[edit] Kevin Ford

Kevin Ford is a musician based in Swindon, England, who produces work similar to Aphex Twin, although sometimes diversifies into more sampled-based production like Amon Tobin. He is a secretive artist, who only works under pseudonyms, and is widely heralded by the inner-sanctum of labels such as Warp as a pioneer in the IDM and Glitch fields.

Kevin Ford began developing his unique sound during the 1970s using analog synthesizers, under the name Legion of Aquarius. In the 1980s, he is credited with loaning Aphex Twin classic Roland drum machines and a TB303, which he taught Aphex Twin to use. This is where reality breaks down into legend, although fans of Warp Records have long sought C90 cassettes produced during the time, which Ford handed out to friends at raves and acid house parties in London.
