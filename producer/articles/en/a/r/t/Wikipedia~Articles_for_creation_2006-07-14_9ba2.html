[edit] AN/TPQ-37 Firefinder radar

The AN/TPQ-37 FIREFINDER is a mobile radar system manufactured by the Hughes Aircraft Company, later acquired by Raytheon. The system is a long range version of "weapon-locating radar", designed to detect and track incoming artillery and rocket fire to determine the point of origin for counterbattery fire. It is currently in service at brigade and higher levels in the United States Army and by other countries. The radar is trailer mounted and towed by a 2 1/2 ton truck. A typical AN/TPQ-37 system consists of the Antenna-Transceiver Group, Command Shelter and 60KW Generator. [edit]


Operation

The AN/TPQ-37 is an electronically-steered radar, meaning the radar does not actually move while in operation. The radar scans a 90-degree sector for incoming rocket, artillery and mortar fire. Upon detecting a possible incoming round, the system verifies the contact before initiating a track sequence, continuing to search for new targets. The incoming round/rocket is tracked during it's initial upward/launch trajectory (i.e., the linear portion of it's flight path) prior to reaching apogee. A computer program analyzes the track data and then extrapolates the round's point of origin. This calculated point of origin is then reported to the operator with map coordinates, thus allowing friendly artillery to direct counterbattery fire towards the enemy artillery. The system has a reported range of up to 50 kilometers. The system may also be operated in a friendly fire mode to determine the accuracy of counterbattery return fire. [edit]


Nomenclature

Per the Joint Electronics Type Designation System (JETDS), the nomenclature AN/TPQ-37 is derived thusly:

"AN/" indicating a system nomenclature derived from the JTEDS "T" for 'transportable', indicating it is carried by a vehicle but is not an integral part of said vehicle (compare with 'V' for vehicle-mounted). "P" indicating a RADAR. "Q" for a special-purpose radar, in this case counterbattery. "37" is an arbitrary numerical designator

Crunk Masters Kings is a drinking game based off of the timeless and commonly played game "Kings (drinking game)." The commercially distributed game created by the company Crunk Masters, LLC adds an element of pictoral rule representations.

[edit] The Rules

The rules of Crunk Masters Kings are as follows:

HOW IT ALL STARTS Place an empty cup in the center of a table. Arrange Crunk MastersTM Kings playing cards face down in a circle around the cup. Select one player to draw the first card. Each card represents an action and is explained further below. Ace Never Ever: Make a statement starting with "Never have I ever..." Anyone who has ever performed the said action drinks. Each player says one statement in turn.


2 for You: Pick someone to drink.

3 for Me: Drink.

4 Social: Everyone drinks.

5 for Guys: All guys drink.

6 for Chicks: All girls drink.

7 to Heaven: Every player throws one hand up toward the sky. The last person to do so drinks.

8 Pick-A-Mate: Drink with a partner of your choosing.

9 Bust-A-Rhyme: Say a sentence. Each following player must say another sentence that rhymes with the last word in the first sentence. The first player that fails to rhyme drinks.

10 Waterfall: Begin to drink, everyone at the table must drink in succession. The first player can stop whenever they please. Everyone else must continue drinking until the player to their right has stopped drinking. Good luck to the person at the bottom of the waterfall!

Jack Categories: Name a category, such as "makes of automobiles." Each other player must name items in the category, such as "Ford." The first player that fails to name an item drinks.

Queen Question: Ask a question to any random player. That player must respond by asking a question to another player. The first player to fail to ask a question (by making a statement) drinks.

Kings Make-A-Rule: The first three times the King is drawn, the player pours some of their beverage into the cup in the center of the table. Additionally, that player is allowed to create a new gameplay rule. Each rule will have drinking consequences should players break them.


HOW IT ALL ENDS The player to draw the fourth and final King card must drink the contents of the cup. Following this, another round of Crunk MastersTM Kings or another Crunk MastersTM Party Game may begin.

[edit] Zi Lan Liao

Zi Lan Liao is an international concert circuit performer on the Ku-Cheng (Chinese harp). She has performed at the Royal Albert Hall and Royal Festival Halland has toured Spain, Italy, France, Holland, Finland, the USA and Australia. She participated the WOMAD Recording Week in Bath.

Zi Lan began to learn the Ku-Cheng at the age of three when she lived in Canton. By the age of 9 she was winning prizes in China, including the prestigious National Youth Music Competition. She continued her music studies at Chetham's School of Music and the Royal Academy of Music, now specializing in the western concert harp besides Chinese traditional dance and the Ku-Cheng.

Apart from playing traditional and music written by Chinese composers, Zi Lan also has had composers outside China write music for her, which has widened her repertoire from classical to contemporary and electronic music. Her collaborations include Indian singers, African percussionists, and western concert flautists as well as Peter Gabriel and Nigel Kennedy. Her collaboration work with other world artists was released on the Realworld Various CD. The track Heaven And Earth, a collaboration with Jah Wobble was released on CDs by Island Record in November 1995 worldwide. Her Ku-Cheng Concerto The River and The Five Tone Dragon had been broadcast around Europe. She recorded music for the Oscar-winning film The Last Emperor.

[edit] Robert Cremona

Robert Cremona was born in England but spent his school years in Malta. At a very young age he showed a talent for the piano and guitar. He was composing his first tunes at the age of six.

Despite suffering from kidney failure in 1977 at the age of twenty, Cremona went on to form a band called 'Strange Cargo' singing and performing his own songs.

In the 1990's he toured Europe and the UK promoting his solo albums "The Blue Bird", "All In The Mind" and "Reasons For All Seasons". After losing a second kidney transplant he concentrated on instrumental compositions and scored numerous pieces for television production companies.

He has won awards for some of his compositions and continues to play despite suffering a stroke in 2002 and being back on a kidney dialysis machine.

Today he spends most of his time as a Volunteer Tutor in the UK for the National Health Service's 'Expert Patients Programme' and lectures to medics, patients and their families about living with a long term chronic illness.

[edit] Cohort Effect

The term "cohort effect" is used to describe variations in the characteristics of an area of study (such as the incidence of a characteristic or the age at onset) over time among individuals who are defined by some shared temporal experience or common life experience, such as year of birth, or year of exposure to radiation.

Cohort effects are important to epidemiologists searching for patterns in illnesses. Certain illnesses may be socially affected via the anticipation phenomenon, and cohort effects can be an indicator of this sort of phenomenon.

Cohort effects are important to resource dependency and economics theorists when these groups affect structures of influence within their larger organizations. Cohorts in organizations are often defined by entry or birth date, and retain some common characteristic (size, cohesiveness, competition) that can affect the organization. For example, cohort effects are critical issues school enrollment.

In order to determine whether a cohort effect is present a researcher may conduct a cohort study.

[edit] 11 minutes that wrecked Mumbai

Seven blasts in seven locations within an 11-minute span rocked Mumbai on Tuesday evening.

Suburban trains along the Western Line were targeted and at least 216 people were killed and nearly 700 were injured.

Here’s a brief timeline of the blasts:

The first blast occurred at 1820 hrs (IST) on a Slow Train at Jogeshwari that had halted at platform number one.

The second blast was at Bandra station at 1823 hrs (IST). The bomb placed in the first-class men's compartment of a Borivali Fast Train.

The third explosion happened a minute later at 1824 hrs (IST) at Mahim aboard a Fast Train going from Churchgate going to Borivali.

At 1827 hrs (IST) the fourth blast at Matunga on a Fast Train coming from Churchgate. At the same time a fifth blast occurred at Borivali on a Fast Train to Virar in a First-Class Men's Compartment.

The sixth explosion happened at 1830 hrs (IST) in a first-class compartment of a Borivali bound Slow Train at Khar.

The last blast was reported at 1840 hrs (IST) as the train neared Bhayandar station.

All the seven blasts were high-intensity explosions and took place when the trains were either entering or leaving the station, leading the police to suspect that the explosive devices were remote-controlled or fitted with timers.

Many of the passengers from adjoining compartments were injured while trying to jump out of moving trains.

Locals and rescue teams reached the stations before the police and used bedsheets, gunny-bags to remove the dead bodies and move the injured to nearby hospitals.

Bodies were strewn across the tracks and coaches torn apart. The injured were immediately rushed to nearby hospitals like Hinduja, Leelavati, Bhabha, KEM, Sion and Nanavati.

Chief Minister Vilasrao Deshmukh has announced a Rs 1 lakh compensation to the relatives of the dead and free treatment to the injured.

[edit] Tom Galvin

Tom Galvin (born 1965) is a former political journalist who is now a policy strategist and public relations consultant in Washington, DC. He is frequently quoted on technology policy issues for clients such as VeriSign and Cisco.

He began his career in 1987 at Congressional Quarterly, where he worked as a congressional reporter. He gained prominence after leaving CQ and joining the New York Post in 1994 as an investigative reporter covering Whitewater. After leaving the Post for crosstown rival the New York Daily News, his articles were cited in a federal investigation that Whitewater Counsel Ken Starr's office was leaking to the press.

In 1998 he left journalism and joined Policy Impact Communications working with Ed Gillespie. Later that year he left to join high-tech company Cisco Systems. In 2001 he became Vice President of Corporate Communications for Internet infrastructure firm VeriSign. He was a frequent critic of Internet regulator ICANN after VeriSign sued it claiming it was blocking the company's ability to conduct business. He was quoted as claiming, "Working the ICANN process is like being nibbled to death by ducks."

In 2005 he founded 463 Communications with partners Jim Hock and Sean Garrett. The firm is based in DC and San Francisco.

[edit] John Terryl Plumeri aka Terry Plumeri

Johnterryl Plumeri Johnterryl Plumeri has just completed recording the 4th, 5th and 6th symphonies of Tchaikovsky as conductor of the Moscow Philharmonic for a June 2006 release on the GMMC CD label. In the past, Johnterryl's recordings with the Moscow Philharmonic of his original compositions have received such words of praise from Fanfare Magazine as, "These well-prepared composer-conducted performances and recordings are both technically and esthetically stunning. The emotional curve of Plumeri's music has a dramatic inevitability that carries the listener along without questioning and leaves him fully gratified, very much like the best of Tchaikovsky's efforts." The Washington Post has said of these recordings, "Plumeri conducts the Moscow Philharmonic in a performance sensitive to the music's smallest nuances."

Johnterryl's innovative style of bowing jazz solos on the acoustic bass has garnered comments like "Stunning…a very high level of music" from the Washington Post. "Johnterryl Plumeri's bowed bass work is endlessly compelling" from allaboutjazz.com. "Extraordinary bowing facility" from Jazz Improv Magazine. In speaking about his latest jazz release Blue In Green, Jazz Improv Magazine has said "The performances and solo improvisations on the parts of Johnterryl Plumeri on bass, David Goldblatt on piano and Joe La Barbera on drums are outstanding. Blue In Green is an album bubbling with creativity, interactivity, magnificent improvisations, and the combined experience, sensitivity, and desire of three consummate musicians performing eight essential pieces from the jazz and standard repertoire. Go and listen."

While on scholarship to Manhattan School of Music in New York City, Johnterryl studied with Robert Brennand, principal bass of the New York Philharmonic. During his period as a bassist with the National Symphony Orchestra in Washington D.C., he studied composition and conducting with the Hungarian conductor/composer Antal Dorati, himself a student of the legendary Bela Bartok.

During the past 15 years he has written the music to 55 feature films, which includes the score to the award winning crime story One False Move, which has recently been added to the "New York Times List of the 1000 Best Films Ever Made". His score for One False Move was nominated for Best Score by the IFP Spirit Awards.

Johnterryl's lectures on music have been heard at the Smithsonian Institute, Georgetown University, Maryland Art Institute and University of Southern California as well as in his private classes in the Los Angeles area on the Vocabulary and Psychology of the Music of Bernard Herrmann in the Alfred Hitchcock thriller, Psycho.

Johnterryl has played with such jazz greats as Cannonball Adderley, Herbie Hancock, Wayne Shorter, Quincy Jones, Arthur Prysock, Frank Sinatra, Joe Williams, Les McCann, Yusef Lateef, John Abercrombie and Woody Herman. Notable performances include Carniegie Hall/New York City, Albert Hall/London, Herodicus Atticus Theater/Athens, Tchaikovsky Hall/Moscow, as well as the Newport, Monterey and Montreux jazz festivals.

The Islamic University of Gaza (IUG) is an independent Palestinian institution located in Gaza. It is the first higher education institution to be established in Gaza. IUG began with three faculties in 1978 and currently has eight faculties introducing BA. B.Sc., MA, M.Sc., Diploma and higher diploma in a variety of disciplines.

Many IUG graduates obtained Masters and Doctorate degrees from international universities all over the world.

IUG is a member of four associations: International Association of Universities, Community of Mediterranean Universities, Association of Arab Universities and Association of Islamic Universities. IUG has developed several academic links and signed several agreements of academic cooperation with American, European and Arab Universities. It has also conducted several projects in collaboration with local and international organizations and institutions situated in Palestine and abroad.

IUG Mission:

  • To provide high quality education to Palestinian students, particularly those living in Gaza Strip
  • To encourage academic and scientific research which participate in dealing with various challenges facing the Palestinian people.
  • To offer courses and degree and non-degree programs that meet the arising needs of the local community in Gaza Strip.
  • To promote knowledge and professional skills among the Palestinians and in the region.

[edit] Retiming

If your request was declined and you want to make changes and try again, copy and paste your article into a new submission and make your changes there.


Retiming is an optimization technique for digital circuits invented by Charles E. Leiserson and refined by him and James B. Saxe. Retiming adds registers at some points in a circuit and removes them from others in such a way that the functional behavior of the circuit as a whole is preserved. Retiming can be used to transform a given synchronous circuit into a more efficient circuit.
