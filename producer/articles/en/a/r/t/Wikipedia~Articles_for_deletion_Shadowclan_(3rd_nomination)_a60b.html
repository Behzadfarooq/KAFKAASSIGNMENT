[edit] Shadowclan

Shadowclan (edit|talk|history|links|watch|logs) – (View log)

Non notable except in the small communities with which they interact. A "consensus" by members of the guild shouldn't outweigh the fact that they fail nearly every aspect of Wikipedia:Notability (web). Their references seem to be a mixture of self links, a "screenshot of the week" style site, a gaming site (Ultima Online) with 150 such articles about the same types of events with different groups, an "interview" by a member of the group with their leader (The Only-War link) and an article on a minor WoW fansite. A quick google search brings up 534 for "shadowclan guild" (no quotes), 1440 for "shadowclan warcraft" (no quotes) and 1380 for "shadowclan ultima online" (no quotes). If we are going to allow a group with no mainstream coverage, what's the point of having notability guidelines at all? Chad Hennings 19:24, 4 February 2007 (UTC)

  • Comment Moved this current AfD to a separate page; the nominator created this one on the page of the first nomination. NeoChaosX (talk, walk) 19:53, 4 February 2007 (UTC)
  • Keep. This is getting a little old. As copied from another discussion, this group has:
- been interviewed for a commercially-published book (a "...for Dummies" book, no less). [1]
- been written into the official documentation of at least one game in which it was involved, Dark Age of Camelot. [2]
Arguably, this group isn't under the auspices of WP:WEB, but it meets it anyway: "The content itself has been the subject of multiple non-trivial published works whose source is independent of the site itself." Now brace for impact of a deluge of single purpose accounts from Shadowclan itself and groups that don't like Shadowclan. Simões (talk/contribs) 20:11, 4 February 2007 (UTC)
