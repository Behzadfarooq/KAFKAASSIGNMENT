Arthur Drago is one of the world's top practitioners of Matsubayashi Shorin-ryu (Okinawan) karate, currently with an 8th Dan rank.

[edit] History

Sensei Drago began his study of martial arts in New York City at the Sullivan Street Dojo in 1964, under the supervision of Sensei Ernest Ferrara. There, he moved up the ranks, earned his black belt, and felt ready to take his journey a step further. In 1967, himself and his friend, Zenko Heshiki, opened the new Satsuma Bushi Dojo on West 72nd Street in Manhattan, NY.

He moved to Virginia in 1971, and soon opened his Budo Kai Dojo on April 7, 1973 in Front Royal, VA. Also known as the "Front Royal Karate Club," it is still in operation today, and Sensei Drago still teaches classes there six days a week.

Sensei Drago earned the prestigious 5th Dan (aka 5th degree black belt) from Soke Takayoshi Nagamine in 1991, received 7th Dan in 2001, and was caught by surprise to receive his 8th Dan from Sensei Ferrara at the US Shorin-ryu Doshi Kai in 2004.

He is the sensei of Sensei Betsy Toth (3rd Dan), who owns the Drago Chi Budo Kai Karate Dojo in Washington, DC.
