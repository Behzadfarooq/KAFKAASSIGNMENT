You have new messages (last change).

You should check out the site before being so rash. It has higher production value than 99.9% of the fan productions out there. Also, the guy who posted this article has nothing to do with the film. He's just a fan--part of a growing fan base. The film hasn't been released yet, but it's in the can. I think that deleting this article now would only be a delay in the process.

That's kind of missing the point. No one is doubting that the film exists - the problem is, as an unreleased film from an (no offense) minor production company, and as such, is a non-notable project with no published credible sources for the information, only fan forums and the like. That may change. The article may survive the AfD, it may not - it's not an automatic deletion, it's a vote. Present your case, and you may come out ahead. Or not. Who knows?  :)
The point is, no one is questioning whether you made the film or not - but what is being questioned is whether it's notable enough for inclusion right now, and some editors apparently feel not. Convince them otherwise, if you can. MikeWazowski 13:53, 3 September 2006 (UTC)
