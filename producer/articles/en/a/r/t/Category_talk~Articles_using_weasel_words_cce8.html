[edit] Good idea, bad choice of words

This template includes the phrase "Who says this?" in the tag. Who says something is not a matter of weasel words but of attribution, and since we have our shiny new attribution policy, WP:ATT, this template and its category should be renamed or moved to Template:Views needing attribution needed and Category:Articles lacking proper attribution. Doing so also removes the temptation of some to misuse and abuse this template. FeloniousMonk 17:43, 17 March 2007 (UTC)

Actually, for assertions that aren't disputed the question of who says it is distinct from the question "Is it a fact?" Sometimes "Who says it?" isn't a challenge.
Like the recent case of the hockey player who hit another player with his stick. An article might say, "Jones is considered too violent to play in the NHL." Well, nobody's disputing that a deliberate assault, after a play, is "too violent". But readers might just want to know who's doing the 'considering'. Was it Boston fans, or NHL officials, or sportswriters, or professors of ethics?
Better to say, "Fans considered Jones too violent..." or "Officals considered Jones too violent". For a matter like this, which is not politically sensitive or controversial, most readers would probably be satisfied. So it wouldn't be a case of {cite needed}. Readers and editors would simply trust that fans or officials felt that way.
Correcting {cn} requires looking stuff up and providing a properly formatted citation, often a link to an external website. Correcting {weasel word} usually just requires rewriting the sentence so the verb has an obvious subject. --Uncle Ed 23:42, 18 March 2007 (UTC)
Rewriting a sentence so that a verb has an obvious subject in this context entails identifying the speaker of what has been spoken or the author of what was written. In other words it means the passage tagged needs a proper attribution and a source. So the issue with this category being not about weasel words but rather attribution that I pointed out remains. I see you've been very busy the last two days tag bombing articles close to your ideological bent with {weasel words} which is a more pejorative tag than {fact}, [att], or {cn}, so it's not too difficult to see what your getting on about here. FeloniousMonk 05:53, 19 March 2007 (UTC)


Thanks for this explanation, I finally get it! "Weasel words" is a new term to me (yes, even though I'm in late 40s) and I was fascinated by the many examples. I completely lost sight of the idea that such a tag would seem pejorative. It would have an 'explosive' impact (hence 'tag-bombing', eh?). You might even reasonably think it shows an ideological bent, even though I just googled phrases like "according to some".
Now that I understand your objection, I will even go so far as to agree to a template-for-deletion request. The last thing I want is more pejorative connotations around here. If you'll set up the deletion request, I will second it. Really. :-) --Uncle Ed 06:12, 19 March 2007 (UTC)
