Since the goal of the ARD and the biweekly special article I made a while back on Wikipedia:WikiProject Fact and Reference Check are quite similar, I've decided to 'steal' your articles on a biweekly basis ;) We don't have a voting system there, so it's wonderful that you guys have set this up.

[edit] Coordination with FA Review

Many of the FAs being reviewed are older nominations from the so-called "'brilliant prose' days" of '04 and earlier, when the referencing criteria for FAs were not as specific and strict as today. A number of the FAs under review do not have any references at all: just a ==References== section heading followed by the {{Unreferenced}} tag. This was the case with the current ARD, Spacecraft propulsion, but it holds true for several other FAs under review and FFAs as well. So, I certainly see the possibility of mutual benefit from coordination between the two projects. -Fsotrain09 16:24, 20 November 2006 (UTC)

Of course! In fact, what you could do is take a list of the oldest Featured Articles, evaluate its sourcing situation, then put it up for FAR and ARD simultaneously. ★MESSEDROCKER★ 01:00, 21 November 2006 (UTC)
