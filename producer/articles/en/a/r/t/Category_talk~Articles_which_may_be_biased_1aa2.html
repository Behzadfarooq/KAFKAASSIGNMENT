[edit] use of these tags

In examining some of the articles listed here, I was unable to decipher what may originally have been considered biased, due to the lack of discussion on the talk page. I've removed a few tags after copyediting as well. DGG 04:32, 26 December 2006 (UTC)

Further to this, should there not be quite a strong policy which says that POV-check flags added with no justification given on the Talk page just get reverted? (But more politely than that!) It takes so little effort to add a POV-check or the (now-deprecated) bias flag that I feel the requirement to go to the Talk page and add a line explaining the concern is not exactly onerous, but rather a bit of a check against people going round scattering these flags like confetti. Maybe this is clearly stated somewhere but if so I would like to know where, and I would like it to be more clearly worded on the page http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Template:POV-check which explains the template. Otherwise, I feel, it encourages a very lazy style of working - just drop the tag on the page and then b*gger off, leaving others to try and figure out what the problem was, and, incidentally, adding yet further to the monster backlog here at http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Category:Articles_which_may_be_biased ... what do you think? 82.45.248.177 11:00, 27 December 2006 (UTC)
