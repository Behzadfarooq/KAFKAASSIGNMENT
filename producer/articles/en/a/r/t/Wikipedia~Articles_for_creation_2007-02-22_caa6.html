[edit] Robert Harper 1951- , Actor

Archived discussion follows below. Please do not modify it.
This request for creation has been reviewed. The result is below.
If your request was declined and you want to make changes and try again, copy and paste your article into a new submission and make your changes there.

Mr. Harper is a professional stage, film + television actor, and a member of Screen Actors Guild (SAG), American Federation of Television and Radio Artists (AFTRA) and Actors Equity (AEA). He is a member of the Academy of Television Arts and Sciences (ATAS) and one of 1400 actors worldwide accepted into the Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences (AMPAS).

Mr. Harper was born in New York City, schooled in New York, Massachusetts, Maryland, and New Jersey, and educated at Rutgers College. As an undergraduate, he played leading roles in 10 of the 23 mainstage productions, was an honor's student, and graduated with High Distinction in English literature. He was awarded a Regents Fellowship to the University of California for graduate work, but chose instead to join the repertory company at the Arena Stage in Washington, D.C.

At the Arena, he performed in plays by Shakespeare, Miller, Ibsen, Hecht & MacArthur and Wilder as well as the American premieres of The Ascent of Mt. Fuji and The Tot Family. He has performed in a Eugene O'Neill revival and a Tom Griffen premiere at the Long Wharf Theatre in New Haven. On Christmas Eve 1981, he originated a role in the off-Broadway production of Zeks--a play about Russian political prisoners. He made his Broadway debut in revivals of Once in a Lifetime and The Inspector General (each at Circle in the Square) and last appeared on Broadway in Arthur Miller's The American Clock in roles he created during the play's world premiere at the Spoleto Festival USA in Charleston, S.C.

Mr. Harper has supporting roles in the motion pictures Creepshow, Once Upon a Time in America, Final Analysis, Wanted Dead or Alive, Deconstructing Harry, Gunmen, Molly, The Insider, and appears in Amazing Grace and Chuck, Twins, Mommie Dearest and The War of the Roses.

He co-stars in the cable films Running Mates (HBO), Payoff, The Wrong Man, and J. Edgar Hoover (all for Showtime); and Not Quite Human (Disney Channel). In network telefilms, Mr. Harper starred in Dempsey, Deadly Deception, Outback Bound, The Case of the Hillside Stranglers, Babies, Held Hostage, Love and Lies, Paper Angels (for American Playhouse), and in the Hallmark Hall of Fame's presentation of My Name is Bill W.

On television, he starred as the melancholic lawyer Si "Bubba" Weisberger in the acclaimed CBS series Frank's Place (1987-1988) and as the acerbic Judge Irwin Hawes in the ABC series Philly (2001-2002). He starred in two true crime CBS miniseries: Ruby Ridge and Murder Ordained. He had recurring roles as an attorney on L.A. LAW and as a Federal Agent on Wiseguy. He has guest starred on episodes of NYPD Blue, Law & Order, Picket Fences, Murphy Brown, Roseanne, Newhart, The Cosby Mysteries, The Commish, Gabriel's Fire, Home Court, Matlock, Commander in Chief, Gilmore Girls and Murder, She Wrote.

While a student at Rutgers he co-starred in Neil Cuthbert's, The Soft Touch--which won the American College Theatre Festival's first New Play Award (1974) and was performed at The Kennedy Center in Washington. He returned twice to Rutgers as a Guest Artist-- the first time for the premiere of William Mastrosimone's Devil Take the Hindmost (1977) and later in the premiere of Ben Bettenbender's Widow's Walk (1984). A longtime member of the Modern Language Association (MLA), he delivered a paper at the 1996 convention in Washington D.C.

[edit] Security Information and Event Management (SIEM)

Security Information and Event Management (SIEM) are technology services and systems used to gather, store, correlate and analyze security log data from information systems.

Security log data is then used by network security organizations to response and mitigate attacks on their network(s), making it possible to see, for example, all the virtual private network connections that are active when a behind-the-firewall server comes under attack. Or in the case of an incident discovered after the fact, such as the theft of credit card numbers, to produce post-mortem reports for police and regulators from archived data.

Regulated industries that carry out critical privacy-based services across electronic networks (such as utilities, financial services and health care) are users of SIEM services and systems.

[edit] Bill Sindelar

Bill Sindelar was born in Cleveland, OH. He moved to Los Angeles to pursue an acting career.

Sindelar got a job as host of G4's Blister, a show dedicated to action video games. Blister was the first show to air on the new network when it launched in 2002 and quickly became popular, as did Sindelar himself. The Blister Declassified series, which premiered in mid-2003, was intended to be a 3-part series on Sindelar's quest to find the Polybius while looking at action games as usual. After the 1st 2 parts of Blister Declassified aired and just after production finished on the final part, G4 announced a merge with Techtv and cancellation of shows on both networks in early 2004. Blister was one of the victims of the merger. While the show's staff wanted G4 to air the final part of Blister Declassified as the series finale, G4 decided to air Skun'd, a parody of MTV's Punk'd, as the final episode. After Skun'd aired in early 2004, Blister was never seen again and the final part of Blister Declassified never aired. While some of the staff was moved to work on G4's other shows, Sindelar was laid off.

After leaving G4, Sindelar moved on to other projects. He joined the Wayne Brady and Friends improv. tour as an Improv player (later joined by fellow G4 host Cory Rouse). He began a career in theater, with roles in Grease, Damn Yankees, and Hotel Clyde and Seamore. He has done audience warm-up work on a number of shows including American Idol, The ShowBiz Show with David Spade, and Survivor finales. He currently is a spokesman for Best Buy, appearing as an employee in HD-TV ads.
