Armenia


  • Ancient Armenia = Biainili-Urartu {-13C to -7C}
    • Wars with the Assyrians
    • Migration of Armenian speaking peoples from north

Armenia as Persian Satrapy [Armina] {-520 to -331}

Armenia as semi-autonomous client kingdom {-331 to -188} Persian satraps submit but are not conquered Armenia remains largely independent client kingdom of Seleucids Sophene forced to pay tribute by Antiochus III {272}

Armenian kingdom {-188 to +12} Orontid (Eruandids) and Artashesid dynasties Armenianized descendants of Persian satraps Artashes I {-188 to 165} asserts independence, unites Armenia, takes title of king founds new capital at Artashat international trade Hellenistic + Iranian + Armenian culture Syncretized Iranian-Zoroastrian gods Bagaran and Artashat as major cultic centers Artawazd I {c. -125 to -95?}, defeated and becomes client of Parthians Tigranes (Tigran) II, the Great {-95 to -55} Allies with Pontus Conquers all eastern Anatolia, Syria, northern Mesopotamia, and NW Iran Defeats Seleucids, rules Antioch {-84 to -71} Builds Tigranakert (Tigranocerta) as second capital Becomes second greatest state in the Near East (after Parthia) Hellenized court Defeated by Roman Lucullus who captures Tigranakert {-69} Pompey finished subjugation {-66}, and makes Tigranes a client king in Armenia Struggle between Rome and Parthia for Armenia as client kingdom Antony’s campaign -35 Armenia divided into pro and anti-Roman factions


Ashakuni (Arsacid = Parthian) Dynasty {12-180/428} Romans attempt to install non-Armenian Zeno on the throne fails Parthian king Vologeses installs his younger brother Tiridates (Terdat) in Armenia Armenian royal family is related to Parthians Tiridates I {53-75} Campaigns of Roman Corbulo {54-63} Southern Armenia conquered Romans plan formal annexation Armenian guerillas continue the fight Compromise of Rhandeia {63} Arsacid prince will rule Armenia Rome will choose and crown the ruler Crowning of Tiridates I in Rome by Nero Extravagant coronation (Cassius Dio 62) Defense of the Caucasus frontier Nomadic incursions {72-73} Fortification of the passes = Garni castle Campaigns of Trajan {114-116}; Armenia is annexed by Rome 117, Armenians revolt and restore their king; Hadrian makes peace Sasanids overthrow the Parthian Arsacid kings {224-226} Armenian Arsacids resist Sasanids, draw closer to Rome Sasanids defeat Rome {244-260} Sasanids invade and occupy Armenia {252-278/293} Attempts to spread Zoroatrianism Armenia partitioned between Rome and Sasanids {278} 298, Victory of Diocletian over the Sasanids; Peace of Nisibis restores Armenia Restored Arsacid Tiridates IV to throne in 298 with Roman support

Armenian Society Mixed Armenian-Iranian-Hellenistic cultural elements Closest cultural links with Iran Pagan religion with gods, heroes and mythology Syncretism with Greek and Iranian gods Great temple complexes bind people together Shared mythic tradition by poetic bards (gusan) Tribal society with bonds of loyalty to clans, with nacharars = chiefs Independent warlords with mountain castles Proud, warlike, independent-minded Owed military service to the king Agrarian and herding mountain valley clans strongly independent


Tiridates IV, the Great {298-330} Alliance with Rome, Christianization Earlier Syriac Christian influences in south Armenia Apostle Thaddeus preaches from Edessa = Apostolic foundation Tomb at Artaz Gregory the Illuminator, the Great Royal family, fled Sasanid invasions, and raised a Christian in Cappadocia Returns and preaches; tortured by Tiridates Vision of king’s sister; Gregory miraculously survives; heals the king Converts the court {314?}, becomes bishop of Armenia, evangelizes Withdraws to cave to die as hermit

Fourth century Destruction of pagan shrines Patriarchate a hereditary office (priests can marry) Christians trained in Greek and/or Syriac Mashtots creates Armenian alphabet and literature Civil wars and murders of Christian kings by Armenian nobles = destabilization Sasanids continually invade and attempt conquest (allied with nobles) Arshak {350-364} helps Julian’s campaign against Persia {364} Disastrous Roman defeat Romans withdraw support of Armenia Persians overwhelm Armenia; plunder and depopulate Churches turned into Fire Temples Arshak blinded and imprisoned in “castle of oblivion” {364-367} Ongoing resistance to Sasanids and civil war among Armenian factions Peace of Ekleac {387} Partition of Armenia into Byzantine and Persian halves Roman half dividing to Armenian provinces Persian half (Persarmenia) = province ruled by the marzpan = viceroy



The Age of the Marzpans {428-652}

Western Armenian loses its independent identity Division into four provinces; loses all independence Major source for military recruitment by Byzantines Persarmenia Keeps Christian identity Closely link with Syriac Christians Rejection of Council of Ephesus, 431 = split from Byzantine Church Fiercely independent nacharars (warlords) Persian appointed Marzpan Patriarch of the Armenian Church Great Armenian Revolt {450-451} [sources: Eghishe and Ghazar] Zoroastrian persecutions; high taxes; military service demands in East Revolt of warlord Vardan Battle of Avarayr {2 June 451} = Armenians destroyed by Sasanids = epic mythic battle in hopeless defense of Armenia against outside tyranny guerilla war continues; Persians install new tolerant Marzpan Second Armenian Revolt {481-485} Initial Armenian victories; Sasanid counter-attack Disastrous defeat of Sasanids against Hephthalites in the east {484} Sasanids make peace and install Armenian rebel Vahan as Marzpan {485-510} Peace and stability restored Increasing wars with Byzantium in the Sixth century Justinian’s Persian Wars [Procopius] Armenian Revolt {571-575} Second Partition of Armenia {591} Much of Persarmenia given to Byzantines for support of Khusrow II Maurice attempts to enforce Orthodoxy on Armenians Armenian ecclesiastical and political resistance to Byzantine rule Persian Wars {610-629} Khusrow II conquers and plunders Armenia {610} Heraclius campaigns in Armenia {624-629}

Armenian Christianity Early links with Syriac Christians Accept Nicea Schism with Byzantines at Council of Ephesus {431} Rejection of Council of Chalcedon {451} Alienation of Armenians from Byzantines Independent Armenian Patriarch (Katolikos) in Persarmenia Acceptance of legitimacy of Sasanid King of Kings; appoints Patriarch Allows accommodation between Armenians and Persians
