Araguapaz is a small town and municipality in north-western Goiás state, Brazil. The population was 7,271 (estimate for July 2005) in a total area of 2,127 km². It is a major producer of cattle.

  • Elevation: 304 meters
  • Became a city: 1982
  • Postal code: 76720-000

Araguapaz is located 268 kilometers northwest of the state capital, Goiânia, in the Rio Vermelho Microregion. Connections with Goiânia are made by highways GO-070 / Goianira / Itaberaí / Goiás / GO-164 / Faina. Highway GO-530 continues northwest for 53 kilometers to Aruanã on the Araguaia River. [1]

Municipal boundaries:

  • North: Mozarlândia
  • South: Faina
  • East: Morro Agudo de Goiás
  • West: Aruanã and Matrinchã

It is in the mini-basin of the Rio do Peixe, a tributary of the Araguaia River. Another important river in the region is the Tesouras.

Economy

The economy is practically dependent on the raising of cattle, especially fattening for the urban market. More than ninety percent of the landowners are from large urban areas like Goiânia and São Paulo. There were 140,712 cows in 2003, of which 6,500 were milk cows.

The main agricultural products were bananas, coconut, hearts of palm, pineapple, rice, manioc, corn (250 hectares), and soybeans.

  • Financial institution in 2004: Brasdesco S.A.
  • Gross Domestic Product: 34.1 million Reais in 2002, which was 0.11% of the state GDP of 31.298 billion Reais in the same year.
  • Gross Domestic Product per capita: 4,680 Reais, which was below the state average of 5,291 Reais in 2002.

There were 335 automobiles and pickup trucks, 56 trucks, and 291 motorcycles in 2004.
