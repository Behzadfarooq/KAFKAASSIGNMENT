[edit] Counter vandalism

Awesome, we can always use the extra hands reverting vandalism. Have you checked out Category:Wikipedia counter-vandalism tools? My favorite vandalism reverting tools are the godmode light, which lets you revert vandalism in a single click, and the IRC chatrooms. Bots report suspicious edits so you can find vandalism more quickly, and you can chat with other RC patrollers at the same time. The channel I use (which you have to go through a slightly bureaucratic process to get approved for, and your account might not be old enough yet) is #vandalism-en-wp, but you don't have to be approved to join #vandalism-en-wp-2, in which the same bots report. You can ask me or check out the IRC tutorial for more info. I also use ARV, a really useful tool for reporting vandals to WP:AIV when they need blocking. Keep in touch and let me know if you need anything. Peace, delldot | talk 05:20, 24 December 2006 (UTC)

[edit] An Automated Message from HagermanBot

Hello. In case you didn't know, when you add content to talk pages and Wikipedia pages that have open discussion, you should sign your posts by typing four tildes ( ~~~~ ) at the end of your comment. You may also click on the signature button located above the edit window. This will automatically insert a signature with your name and the time you posted the comment. This information is useful because other editors will be able to tell who said what, and when. Thank you! HagermanBot 17:50, 30 January 2007 (UTC)
