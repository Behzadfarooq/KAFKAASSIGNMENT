Aramu

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Aramu was the first known king of Urartu. The Armenian version of his name is Aramé, and he is believed to be the prototype of Aram, one of the legendary forefathers of the Armenian people. Historical Aramu had no Aramaic origin, and the phonetic similarity is coincidental. [1]
