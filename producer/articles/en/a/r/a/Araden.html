Araden (Syriac: ܐܪܕܢ) is a village in the northern Iraqi governorate of Dohuk. It is located 20-30 kilometers (12-19 miles) east of the city of Zakho and lies in a valley. The village of Inishk lies within viewing distance from it. The name Araden means "Land of Eden" in Assyrian Aramaic.

The inhabitants are Assyrian and speak a dialect of Syriac with elements of both Chaldean Neo-Aramaic and Assyrian Neo-Aramaic.

Chaldean Catholicism is the dominant religion of the village. One of the oldest churches in Christianity, the church of Sultana Mahdokh, established in 325 AD, is located in Araden. Originally prior to the 18th century the inhabitants belonged to the Church of the East.

Today, there are about 150 homes in the village. also there are three main families Rayes family, the chieftains of the village, Kasha and Sana.

There is a large community of people from Araden in Metro Detroit (Warren, Sterling Heights, and Troy), Michigan. Every year Assyrians of Araden origin or Aradnayeh gather on January 12th, when there is a celebration for the Assyrian Saint Sultana Mahdokht, called Shera. This is a big event in the Chaldean community of Michigan, as the attendance is overwhelming. Also every year on 15th of June, Aradnayeh get together in Shelby Township, for a large celebration at Stony Creek Metropark.

[edit] Famous Citizens

  • Afram Rayis - Moses-esque figure for the refugees of Araden under the regime of Saddam Hussein between 1980 and 2000. Rayis was honored by former president Ronald Reagan in the late 1980's.
