[edit] AfC and the GFDL

Jorge, when you create an article from WP:AFC, don't forget to note the article's submitter in the edit summary. Even though the text in these two cases originates from a PD book — and labeled as such — the submitter has, at the very least, done some wikification that must be credited to satisfy the GFDL. I made the note in today's two articles' second edit, so they're all set, but if there are any articles that you've created in the past, I'd be worth going back and checking to make sure you listed their original authors. ×Meegs 03:41, 14 March 2006 (UTC)

[edit] Mark Murphy article

I exapanded your Mark Murphy article some. Next time you creat an article about an NFL player and don't have much info, I suggest you check him out at pro-football-reference.com or databasefootball.com. databasefootball.com is especially good for defensive players because it lists defensive and special teams stats, unlike pro-football-reference.com.

Also you can check wikipedia's Super Bowl articles or USA today's play-by-play Super Bowl list(Wikipedia's Super Bowl page has a link to it) to see if the player has made any great plays in a Super Bowl if he played in one.

And finally, pro-football-reference.com lists postseason stats for players, which is also usefull. However, it only does this for offensive plays.

--Chainclaw

For defensive players, you should check databasefootball.com, not PFR. Though I gotta admit Murphy was a little hard to find there because his name is spelled wrong for some reason.
