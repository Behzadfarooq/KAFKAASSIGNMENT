Wild Sarsaparilla or False sarsaparilla (Aralia nudicaulis) is a flowering plant of northern and eastern North America which reaches a height of 1–2 feet (30-60 cm).

Leaves are compound, large and finely toothed. Tiny white flowers, in clusters 1.5–2 inches (4-5 cm) wide, bloom from May to July, and ripen into purple-black comestible berries. The berries taste a little spicy and sweet.

The roots have been used as substitutes for true Sarsaparilla (Smilax sp.) in herbal medicine.
