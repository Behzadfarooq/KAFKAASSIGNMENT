Arambag (Bengali: আরামবাগ) (also spelt Arambagh) is a city and a municipality in Hooghly district in the state of West Bengal, India. It is the head quarter of Arambagh subdivision. [1]

[edit] Demographics

As of 2001 India censusGRIndia, Arambag had a population of 56,129. Males constitute 51% of the population and females 49%. Arambag has an average literacy rate of 66%, higher than the national average of 59.5%; with 57% of the males and 43% of females literate. 12% of the population is under 6 years of age.

[edit] Politics

In 2006 State assembly elections Binoy Dutta of CPI (M) won the seat and Bivabindu Nandi of Trinamool Congress was in second position. In 2001 election, Binoy Dutta won, with Sk Hassan Imama of Trinamool Congress in second position. In 1996, Binoy Dutta had won with Abdus Sukur of INC in second position. In 1991, Benode Das of CPI (M) won with Jalim Singha Roy of INC in second position. In 1987, Benode Das won with Abdul Mannan of INC in second position. In 1982, Abdul Mannan of INC won, with Ranjit Chakraborti, Independent, in second position. In 1977, Ajoy Kumar Dey of Janata Party, won, with Madan Mohan Saha of CPI (M) in second position. [2]

Anil Basu of CPI (M) won the Arambag parliamentary seat to the 13th Lok Sabha defeating his nearest rival, Chunilal Chakraborty of BJP. [3]
