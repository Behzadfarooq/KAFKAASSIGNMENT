[edit] Fair use images

Hi, I noticed you've been adding {{fairuse}} to images, such as Image:John Waterbury.jpg. As you may have noticed from the template's text, this is a deprecated tag, since "fair use" is not a generic escape clause, it's only valid for specific kinds of uses. So we have {{fairusein}}, where you could say that this image is valid in John Waterbury but not elsewhere, or {{publicity}}, which is often true of official photos posted by institutions. In any case, those of us emptying out the fair use category (an Augean task if ever there was one!) would sure appreciate it if you didn't add more images in. Thanks! Stan 07:05, 1 January 2006 (UTC)

[edit] Leaving, the RfA, etc.

I would like to first echo comments made on my talk page by David [2]. His comments are spot-on. Second, I'm going to reiterate what I said on your talk page earlier; you are an asset to Wikipedia. I want to encourage you to understand that becoming an admin is not a badge of honor. It is not a recognition for a job well done. It's not a certificate saying you passed review, or any other kind of imaginable honor. If you take it like that, then you will be sorely disappointed on becoming an admin because there are precious few "honors" past adminship, and getting another one is unlikely to happen. You would become depressed at not getting another "honor". Adminship isn't even a promotion per se; you would just be granted software rights to do certain things you couldn't do before.

With particular regards to leaving; I understand your reasons. But, I am not sure you personally believe the basis of those reasons if you thought about it for a bit. You are a reasonable person. You know that ArbCom elections mean being elected for a three year post. You would not put yourself up for ArbCom if you didn't feel you could fulfill the duty given you if elected. Thus, I think you did think of your upcoming time constraints and felt you could handle it anyways. So, I do think you have the time to still be involved in Wikipedia. Plus, I think being involved here will help you academically anyways. I'll lay odds your English skills are getting better! :)

Nobody says you have to be wildly active to contribute to Wikipedia or even be an admin. You've been editing a LOT lately. You can reduce the amount of time you spent here, up your grades, get your arm/toe taken care of, etc., and still be a fantastic asset to Wikipedia. A large number of people really want you here.

I'll make you an offer. I've never made an offer like this before. My guidelines for admin nomination are probably the highest around. Now, I'm not saying I will nominate you. But, I am offering to work very closely with you to set you up for the next RfA. I will be very happy to conduct periodic reviews of your work, say once a week, and make comments as appropriate to correct errors and point out how something might have been done better in my opinion. I'll do as much as I can to foster you along towards the next RfA so you are ready for it. When, after three months have passed, you go up for RfA, I won't nominate you but if you work with me and take most of my advice I will give you a very supportive vote, citing good work and the experience I had with you over the prior few months. For better or worse, my vote at RfA seems to carry some weight. If I vote in favor of you, I am confident several other people will vote in favor as well.

Now, first piece of advice; do NOT accept the next RfA that comes your way without first letting me review your answers and having an opportunity to discuss it with you. Second, don't accept an RfA until at least April 10th. Third, don't leave. We need you around here too much. Please! No, scatch that..when I say please, I mean please in THIS BIG of a way. Ok? Deal?

Now wait...before you respond...DON'T respond until you've had 24 hours to mull over this and wind down from the withdrawn RfA #4. You'll be much calmer and happier. THEN tell me we have a deal :) --Durin 03:24, 7 January 2006 (UTC)
