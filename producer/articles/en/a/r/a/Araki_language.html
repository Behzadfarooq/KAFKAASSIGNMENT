Araki is a nearly extinct language spoken in the small island of Araki, south of Espiritu Santo I. in Vanuatu. It is spoken today by about 5 speakers; it is progressively being replaced by the neighbouring language of Tangoa.

Araki was described in 2002 by the linguist Alexandre François.

The local name of the island is [ˈɾaki].

[edit] Characteristics

Araki is one of the few languages of Vanuatu, and indeed of the world, possessing a set of linguolabial consonants.
