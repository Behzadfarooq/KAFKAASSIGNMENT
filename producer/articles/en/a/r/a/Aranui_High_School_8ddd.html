Aranui High School is a large secondary school in Christchurch, South Island, New Zealand. Well known for its Academy Programme which includes sporting and cultural pursuits in the senior years.

Its senior drama class has won the National Shakespeare Festival twice in a row.

They came 1st in 2005 with their version of Shakespeare's Midsummer Night's Dream, winning the following awards:

Organisers' Award For Very Co-operative & Courteous Schools

British Council Award for Outstanding Song/Music - Ana Buchanan

Sam Wanamaker Memorial Aware For Most Promising Actor - Awhi Kuka-Sweet as Oberon

Russell Armitage Award For Outstanding Individual Comic Role - Andy Bibby as Bottom

Russell Armitage Award For Outstanding Presentation of a Comedy - Aranui High School for A Midsummer Night's Dream


They came 2nd in 2006 with their version of Shakespeare's Hamlet, winning the following awards:

Toi Whakaari: NZ Drama School Award for Best Technician - A.J. White

Dymocks Award for Special Individual Performance - Te Awhiroa Kuka-Sweet

Stratford Shakespeare Society Award for Outstanding Presentation from a Tragedy

New Zealand Theatre Federation Award for Outstanding Production

They were also runners up in the 2006 International Theatre in Paradise Festival on Norfolk Island.
