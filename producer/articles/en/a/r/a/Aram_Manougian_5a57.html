Aram Manougian, (Aram Manoukyan) (b. 1879) or less known as "Sarkis (Serge) Hovanessian". He is also known as "Aram of Van". He is an Armenian patriot and revolutionary who is credited as a political, military and spiritual leader of Armenian people. Aram Manougian with a great vision joined the Armenian Revolutionary Federation, at a very early age. Many consider him as a founder of the Democratic Republic of Armenia.

[edit] Early years

Aram Manougian was born in the village of Zeyva (now David Bek) in Yelizavetopol province of Russian Empire (today Kapan region of Armenia).In 1903, was involved by ARF for defense of the Gandzak, and then in Kars. He was in Van in the autumn of 1904, where he promoted freedom in Lake Van, or Vaspurakan. 1911 spent a year in Geneva, then after returning to Van Province headed a leadership of ARF in Lake Van. In Lake Van he was very active teaching at schools, communicating press, working towrds national establishments, promoting youth circles. He was known to Ottoman administration as the "trouble maker" and was jailed by them in an attempt to suppress his freedom of speech. He was a suspect in the case Bedros Kapamajian, and jailed. After attaining freedom, he served under ARF in different places. With the beginning of the World War I he headed to protection of Van Province, thanked to him in 1915 he had rescued Van and was proclaimed as the governor.

[edit] Free Vaspurakan

Further information: Free Vaspurakan

He become the revolutionary organizer of the revolution in Van, in 1915. He was the backbone to the uprising in Van. As a result of his effort, he was given the position of governor of the independent city of Vaspurakan.

[edit] National Council

Further information: Armenian National Council of Karabagh

1917 spent in Tiflis, participated in activity of a National bureau and ARD bureau, but more was engaged in issues of immigrants from Vaspurakan and other places. By the end of 1917 was the actual chief of the Armenian Congress of Eastern Armenians.

[edit] DRA

Further information: Democratic Republic of Armenia
The members of the First Cabinet, during the James Guthrie Harbord's interview for the Harbord report on Middle East

Aram Manukyan was the minister of internal affairs and foodstuffs of the first government of the Republic of Armenia, and after murder of Karchikyan, about one month from November 15, 1918 to December 13, 1918 occupied a position of the acting minister of labor and public trusteeship.

Aram Manukyan was the chairman and manager of defense (defense minister). Due to his excellent organizing gift and firm will, the attack of Turks on Yerevan direction was repulsed. He was one of the organizers of fight at Battle of Sardarapat.
