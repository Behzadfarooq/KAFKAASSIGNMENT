Ararat may refer to:

  • Ararat, a geographic area:
    • Mount Ararat, the highest peak of Turkey.
    • Little Ararat, Mount Ararat's neighbor, also in Turkey.
    • Ararat plain, a geographic region covering most of Armenia.
    • Ararat River in Virginia and North Carolina in the United States.
  • Ararat, a place name:
    • Ararat, Armenia, a town in Armenia, named after the mountain.
    • Ararat, a province (marz) in Armenia, also named after the mountain.
    • Ararat, City of Refuge, an attempt to found a Jewish State in Grand Island, NY
    • Ararat, Victoria, Australia
    • Ararat, a town in Surry County, North Carolina
  • Ararat, an historical place name:
    • Ararat, a traditional transliteration for the name of the ancient civilization of Urartu.
    • Republic of Ararat, a breakaway Kurdish state on the territory of modern Turkey that lasted from 1927 to 1931.
    • Ararat (Jewish homeland), an early 19th century plan by Mordecai Manuel Noah for a Jewish homeland at Grand Island in New York
  • Ararat, a football (soccer) club:
    • FC Ararat Yerevan, Yerevan, Armenia.
    • FC Ararat Tehran, Tehran, Iran.
  • Ararat, a film:
    • Ararat, a 2002 film by Atom Egoyan about the Armenian Genocide.
    • A Girl From Ararat Valley, a 1949 Soviet Armenian film.
  • Ararat for other uses:
    • 96205 Ararat, an asteroid named after the mountain.
    • Ararat (brandy), a brandy from Armenia.
    • HMAS Ararat, the name of two ships in the Royal Australian Navy.
