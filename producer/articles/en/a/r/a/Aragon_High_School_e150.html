Aragon High School is an American public high school in San Mateo, California, and is part of the San Mateo Union High School District (SMUHSD).

[edit] History

Aragon High School was established in 1959 to accommodate development and population growth in San Mateo. The campus is located in the Foothill Terrace neighborhood [3] to the north of California State Route 92 and west of El Camino Real on the border with Hillsborough [4].

SMUHSD residents approved Measure D in November 2000 which authorized funding for school renovation and modernization. [5] Aragon is currently in the final stages of its modernization project started in August 2001. Two new buildings were added to the campus as part of this project. [6]

Aragon offers numerous extracurricular activity groups. Recently there has been such an abundance of new clubs and teams that the school newspaper, The Aragon Aristocrat, questioned the legitimacy of these clubs and wondered whether they were created simply to pad students' college applications.[citation needed]

The Silver Sword Program recognizes students' extracurricular activities and volunteer hours. Silver Sword awards are given to graduating students with more than 500 points and to the student with the most points in their respective class.

[edit] First Step

The First Step program provides an elective one-week introduction to Aragon High School for incoming students who may have been at a disadvantage academically or socially in middle school.

[edit] Music

The Aragon Symphonic Band placed first in the Heritage Band Festival competition held in Las Vegas in 2004, and subsequently traveled to Boston to compete at the national level in April 2005.[citation needed]

In January 2005, the Aragon Jazz Ensemble won both the Division and Sweepstakes titles at the Folsom Jazz Festival held in Folsom, California.[citation needed] They placed fourth in the Monterey Jazz Festival in April 2005.[citation needed]

In November 2006, the Aragon Chamber Choir (the school's audition-only group) placed third in a high school singers' competition at Jack London Square in Oakland, California, sponsored by radio station KBLX, consequently winning $500 for the music department.[citation needed]

Additionally, Aragon's orchestras and choirs are also quite renowned in the area.[citation needed] Taught by David Martin, they have both achieved success at regional competitions.[citation needed]

Aragon's Symphonic band has recently been invited to Beijing to play in the 2008 Olympics.[citation needed]

[edit] Robotics

The Aragon Robotics Team (ART) is a student-run organization founded in 2000 which provides opportunities for exploring robotics through collaborative design and team competition. [8]

[edit] Social Sciences

The Model United Nations Club was re-established in 2003 and has attended conferences at Stanford, UC Berkeley, and UC Davis. The club has won "best club" awards 4 times in 9 conference appearances.[citation needed]

Aragon also boasts a Mock Trial team, which competes with similar teams in the area.

The Harvard Model Congress Club sends delegates once a year to a conference held in San Francisco, California by Harvard students.

For the past few decades, there has been an ongoing rivalry between Aragon and nearby Hillsdale High School, ritualized every year by the fall football rally.[citation needed] The Dons have been able to reach the CCS playoffs for the past three years, defeating such schools as Serra and Mitty High School, while compiling a 21-game winning streak against division opponents. Over the past three years, their record is 30-5. They have won 4 Peninsula Athletic League championships in 5 years.[citation needed] The head coach is currently Steve Sell, an Aragon alumnus who played quarterback on the football team during the early 1980s.
