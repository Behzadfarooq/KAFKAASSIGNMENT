[edit] Arab map

It's a mouthful, so I'm not going to repeat it here. I listed the problems at Image talk:Ethnic Arabs Map.GIF.GIF [sic] and Image talk:Arabs Map.GIF (the former has the information of the second talk page, plus a couple more notes). — ዮም | (Yom) | Talk • contribs • Ethiopia 07:03, 23 August 2006 (UTC)

My response wasn't quick. I posted those a few days ago. Sorry if it seemed a little agressive. It just seemed as if you were purposely trying to aggrandize the extent of Arabs in Africa.
Regarding Egypt's Red Sea Coast, I don't think you understand me correctly. I'm not saying all of Egypt's Red Sea coast, just the southeastern part, and all of Sudan's. It is not Arabic speaking, but Beja-speaking, a Cushitic language (or possibly an independent branch of Afro-Asiatic). See the maps here (The site doesn't show it, but those in Egypt are called Ababda) and here. In the second map, that little yellow enclave is inhabited by the Hedareb Beja from Eritrea and the Tigre people from Eritrea (related closely to the Tigray-Tigrinya people, but generally Muslim). So, as you can see, most of that area is not inhabited by Arabs. It's mostly desert and inhabited by various Beja groups (some near Eritrea being Tigre-speaking). Note that the territories in Eastern Sudan have been out of Sudanese control for some time due to civil war, so it can be safely said that these groups do not consider themselves Arabs.
For Mauritania, you're probably right, but the Demographics of Mauritania does show a substantial non-Moor population. Regarding the use of "Arab influence," I'm not sure it's even necessary, since the map is to show the distribution of Arabs as an ethnicity. Perhaps instead you should just put dots of green where Arabs live in those countries. Apparently there's a small population in Mogadishu, and I know there are a few near Eritrea's northern coast.
Regarding the Saharawi, the reason I point that out is because I think they don't consider themselves Arab, but they do speak Hassaniya, so maybe I am wrong on this.
Thanks for your offer to help out on maps, I'll be sure to ask for your help when I'm having difficulties. Selam, — ዮም | (Yom) | Talk • contribs • Ethiopia 17:56, 23 August 2006 (UTC)
