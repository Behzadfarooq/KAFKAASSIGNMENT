Aragh Sagi is a type of alcoholic drink in Iran. Usually home brewed and sold at a profitable price.

Aragh refers to a set of aromatic liquids that are produced from herbs and seeds by distillation, for example mint or anise. In the context of alcoholic drinks then it is produced from raisins. People usually are clear about the type of Aragh in Persian (e.g. aragh from anise or aragh of mint). If it is not specified it is possibly referring to aragh of raisins. Aragh Sagi is also referring to the Aragh from raisins.

Sag means dog in Persian and Sagi means doggy.
