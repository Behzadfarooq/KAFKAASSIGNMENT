Araçu is a small town and municipality in central Goiás state, Brazil. The population was 4,400 (2005 estimate) in a total area of 154 km².

  • Elevation: 716 meters
  • Became a city: 1958
  • Postal code: 75410-000

Araçu is part of the Anápolis Microregion and is a short distance northwest of the state capital, Goiânia. It has boundaries with Itaberaí, Itauçu, and Caturaí.

The distance to the state capital, Goiânia, is 75 km. and highway connections are made by GO-070 / Goianira / GO-523 / Caturaí / GO-222.

Economy

The economy is based on cattle raising (19,780 head in 2003), services, public administration, and agriculture. The main agricultural products in 2003 were bananas (1.85 km²), coffee (0.74 km²), garlic (0.1 km²), rice (6 km²), sugarcane (1.77 km²), beans (5.5 km²), corn (18 km²), and tomatoes (0.15 km²).

  • Gross Domestic Product: 14.8 million Reais in 2002, which was 0.05% of the state GDP of 31.298 billion Reais in the same year.
  • Gross Domestic Product per capita: 3,498 Reais in 2002, which was below the state average of 5,921 Reais in the same year.

See [1]

The registered motor vehicles in 2004 were: automobiles (257), trucks (13), pickups (15), and motorcycles (158).

The population was employed in agriculture, transformation industries, commerce (31 units), and public administration.

There were no financial institutions reported in 2003.
