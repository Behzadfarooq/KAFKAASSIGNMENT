[edit] Working for Aramark

I work at the Aramark owned Starbucks in the University of Tennessee at Knoxville Hodges Library. Lets just say, it sucks. Working at the Starbucks means that we do close to twice as much work as any of the other Aramark locations on campus (seeing as we make Aramark the most money on campus), and how are we rewarded for this? We get paid 6.25 an hour, starting, and every year you stay with the company you get a .25 pay raise.

If this is fair I'm a purple elephant named Biscuit. We're opening two new Starbucks locations on campus and my manager is appointing the two full time workers who have worked at Starbucks for the shortest amount of time to manage those stores. Aramark bullies you and makes you think that if you loose your job there you will NEVER get a job again.

Working for Aramark is like having the Man and the Man's entire family tree step on you/

I worked for Aramark at the Saratoga Performing Arts Center, selling concessions at concerts. They are so cheap that they don't salt the french fries (despite the fact that they sell a handful-sized basket of them for upwards of $4). They don't give us a scoop for the fries, either. We have to use our hands, and the fries (right out of the frier) burn through the rubber foodservice gloves. Also, one day when the grill broke at a Dave Matthews concert, the manager had the cooks steam the hamburgers and chicken sandwhiches, and they were still partly raw on the inside. We started at $7.75/hour, though. Maybe it's because New York has a higher minimum wage. 69.67.229.110 19:45, 22 June 2006 (UTC)
I worked for Scott & White Hospital for ten years. I delivered clean linen for the hospital"s Laundry department. When my department was taken over by Aramark, I worked FOR Aramark AT Scott & White in the same position. When my Scott & White boss left, he was replaced by an Aramark manager. The quantity and quality of hospital linens immediately plummetted. The Aramark manager claimed that I "always left too much" and that a 500-bed hospital "didn't need that much linen". The truth was that Aramark, a for-profit company, wasn't eligible for any of the vendor discounts that Scott & White, a nonprofit company, could get. The Aramark manager slashed production to cut costs. I'm told that in the 18 months since I left, he's gone through 14 delivery men.

[edit] Not exactly NPOV...

While I hate Aramark, this article clearly violates Wikipedia's NPOV standards. The third paragraph of the criticism section reads: "Aramark has a lengthy history of employee abuses, including an insistence on paying only minimum wage..." Not only is this clearly taking sides (not NPOV), it's not true. Just look a few articles up on the talk page and you will see that someone got paid 6.25/hr. working at a Starbucks Aramark place.

Also note that the entire legal issues section got wiped out by the same person who changed a lot of the article and basically turned it into an advertisement in the first place. I believe the legal issues section should be brought back to help restore some NPOV to this article. It's a lot better than having the company itself edit this article or have people see the lack of NPOV and feel the need to vandalize this. TOH 01:06, 3 March 2007 (UTC)

[edit] Levels of Service

I go to University of North Carolina at Greensboro where all food on campus is Aramark. I was talking to the director of Dining Services and he told me that there are different classifications of service depending on the contracts established with the individual institutions. The naming scheme goes something like "Aramark 3", with the number increasing with increased quality of service. If we can find out more about this, then it certainly would be a quality addition to this article.
