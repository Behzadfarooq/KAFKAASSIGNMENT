Note: Information below copied here from Arab world article's discussion page. ntennis 05:55, 29 July 2006 (UTC)

Somalia is included in the Arab league and has many cultural ties to the Arab world, but it's population does not speak Arabic as a first language, and could not be considered ethnically/racially Arab. To be honest, there's not much logic to the color-coding right now: southern Mauritania is also clearly non-Arab, and several Sahel states are at least partially Arab in the same fashion that Somalia is (or isn't, depending on your point of view). And then there's the Arab-dominated areas of Iran, Berber regions in North Africa, and the Kurdish parts of Syria, etc. I think it would be better to have one or more of the following options: 1. Arab league members or 2. all countries where a majority speaks Arabic as a first language, and/or it's the main official language, or 3. a color-coding that marks all areas dominated by Arabic-speaking people(s) with no regard to state borders. Of course, in cases 1 and 2 it should be noted that not all of the population of all the colored countries is or considers itself to be Arab. Arre 18:30, 29 January 2006 (UTC)

Another problem is that the Saharawi are shown as Arab, as well as the Beja of the Red Sea coast of Sudan and Southeastern Egypt. Furthermore, large areas of southern Algeria and Libya should be light green due to the Berber and Tuareg populations living there. The Berber populations in the Maghreb also need to be represented somehow. — ዮም | (Yom) | Talk • contribs • Ethiopia 00:54, 21 August 2006 (UTC)

[edit] Mauritania, Darfur, Comoros, Chad, Eritrea

  • Mauritania has a 70 % Arab majority.
  • Darfur, the land of Fur peoples is shown as Arab.
  • Union of the Comoros, an Arab League member, is not shown on the map.
  • Chad has Arabic as an official language.
  • Eritrea has Arabic as a "working language" and is an observer at the Arab League. Behemoth 12:56, 14 March 2007 (UTC)
Eritrea is not an Arab country, though, and shouldn't be included in the "Arab World" map, although some sort of note can be made on its observer status for the Arab league. Also note that, in addition to Darfur, the area inhabited by Bejas (SE Egypt and NE Sudan) is shown as Arab, when it is not. The regions of Al Qadarif, Kassala, and Red Sea should be colored as light green, showing that the area is part of an Arab state, but ethnically non-Arab. — ዮም | (Yom) | Talk • contribs • Ethiopia 02:31, 19 March 2007 (UTC)
