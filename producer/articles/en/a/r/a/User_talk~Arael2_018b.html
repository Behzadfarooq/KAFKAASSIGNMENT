[edit] DictionaryForMIDs

Hello, and welcome to Wikipedia! We welcome and appreciate your contributions, such as DictionaryForMIDs, but we regretfully cannot accept copyrighted text or images borrowed from either web sites or printed material. This article appears to be a direct copy from http://dictionarymid.sourceforge.net/what.html, and therefore a copyright violation. The copyrighted text has been or will soon be deleted.

If you believe that the article is not a copyright violation, or if you have permission from the copyright holder to release the content freely under the GNU Free Documentation License (GFDL) then you should do one of the following:

  • If you have permission from the author leave a message explaining the details at Talk:DictionaryForMIDs and send an email with the message to "permissions-en (at) wikimedia (dot) org". See Wikipedia:Requesting copyright permission for instructions.
  • If a note on the original website states that re-use is permitted under the GFDL or released into the public domain leave a note at Talk:DictionaryForMIDs with a link to where we can find that note.
  • If you own the copyright to the material: send an e-mail from an address associated with the original publication to permissions-en(at)wikimedia(dot)org or a postal message to the Wikimedia Foundation permitting re-use under the GFDL, and note that you have done so on Talk:DictionaryForMIDs.

It is also important that the text be modified to have an encyclopedic tone and that it follows Wikipedia article layout. For more information on Wikipedia's policies, see Wikipedia's policies and guidelines.

If you would like to begin working on a new version of the article you may do so at Talk:DictionaryForMIDs/Temp. Leave a note at Talk:DictionaryForMIDs saying you have done so and an administrator will move the new article into place once the issue is resolved. Thank you, and please feel welcome to continue contributing to Wikipedia. Happy editing! -- ReyBrujo 17:42, 3 February 2007 (UTC)

[edit] Re: Deleting my article

I will put it bluntly: We do not care about whether you claim to be the developer or not. We care about copyright status. You have been creating an article with information copied from a site that does not state it is releasing the information under a free license, therefore it can be deleted. Had you paused to read the warnings you would have understood that. Free software has its own license, and you would not like someone to take your code and said it was created by himself, copyrighting it, right? Well, we do not like when someone copies information from a site that does not state it is releasing information under a free license. -- ReyBrujo 18:05, 3 February 2007 (UTC)

Ok, apparently you haven't yet understood, so let me explain: any information you post in internet is copyrighted by you. That is not something optional, it is how copyright works. Even if you have no idea about that, you own the copyright of anything original you create. That includes the software and site content. In order to be usable by Wikipedia, you must release all rights by explicitly stating that the information is released under a free license (GFDL, in example) or public domain. The site has no notice about that at all. The only copyright notice in the site is this one, which states The copyright conditions of DictionaryForMIDs are defined by the GNU General Public License (GPL), see file COPYING. DictionaryForMIDs is free SW. As you can see, it makes no reference about the site at all, thus it is considered the text in the site is copyrighted by you, and copying that in Wikipedia is considered, by our standards, copyright violation. You can edit your site and add a note below stating "The information in this page is released under the GNU Free Documentation License, and then yes, you would be able to post that information in Wikipedia. Or you can mail the foundation stating you have released that information under a free license. Or you can create an article with your own words and _not_ with content copied. As for leaving Wikipedia and making bad publicity, feel free to do that, people will understand why the pages have been deleted unless you post only "your side" of the story. As I said, everything would have been solved had you read the warnings that were posted in your page instead of keep recreating the article. -- ReyBrujo 18:29, 3 February 2007 (UTC)
