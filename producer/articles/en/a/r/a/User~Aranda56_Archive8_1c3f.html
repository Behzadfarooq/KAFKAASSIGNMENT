[edit] Participant alert regarding Wikiproject on Advertising

The Wikiproject No Ads, created as a backlash against the Answers.com deal, has served an important function in providing a space for users to express their disagreement with the Foundation proposal. While the current controversies about userboxes raise questions about political and social advocacy on Wikipedia, there should be greater flexibility regarding advocacy about Wikipedia in the Wikipedia namespace. Reported and linked by Slashdot and other press sources as a unique and spontaneous occurence in Wikipedia history, it has apparently had some impact as, despite being scheduled to begin in January, not a peep has been heard about the trial and proposed sponsored link since the deal's controversial announcement months ago. Currently, however, there is an attempt to delete the project or move it off Wikipedia altogether. Since the Foundation has provided no additional information and has not attempted to answer the specific questions that participants in the project raised, it is unclear if the Answers.com deal has been abandoned or simply delayed. Until the situation becomes more clear, I believe the group should still have a place in the Wikipedia namespace. Sincerely, Tfine80 00:17, 10 February 2006 (UTC)

[edit] Branch Davidian reversion

Why have you reverted the Branch Davidian article? Probably, you thought I was trying to get away with gutting the page, but there has been no vandalism; all of the material I removed from B_D is now at Waco Siege. Nor is this some sudden move. Someone else proposed it months ago, and I added my agreement on January 20. Then, to make sure nobody missed it, I added a headlined section on the Talk page ten days later, telling people I was going to make the move, and asking them to speak up if they opposed it. If you don't think the section on the standoff should be moved, why didn't you speak up then? If you think there should be more time for objections to be raised, how much longer?

I am not a "vandal." If you've been following my edits to the page, you can see that I haven't been capricious or malicious. Check my contribs. In the description of my edit, I said, "REMOVED MATERIAL TO WACO_SIEGE," and in my changes I said in italics, "For more information, see Waco Siege," so you could have gone to W_S and seen that it was all there. The only changes I made were in the intro paragraph, to make it fit the opening of an article rather than the middle of an article, and turning the subsections into editable sections. I copied it over faithfully, even factually incorrect information and slanted POV that goes against my own POV, so that there would be continuity in the history when I make my edits.

If you really don't think it's a good idea to open the Waco Siege article, I'd like you to make your case on the Talk: Branch Davidian page, rather than branding it as "vandalism." Peace and AGF. --WacoKid 06:07, 12 February 2006 (UTC)

Okay, now that I understand your objections better, I am re-editing the article to leave more information about the standoff, hopefully enough to meet with your approval. However,
1) I still don't think you should have used the "rvv" code for your edit. First, it's inaccurate, since it wasn't vandalism. Second, it gave no information about the actual reason for your reversion, and thus no guidance to me about how I could accommodate you.
2) There also is an alternative way you can handle this. If you felt that I had left too little information about the siege in the article, you could have added back enough information to meet your satisfisfaction, rather than doing a full revert. You can also adopt this approach if my next edit is still too brief for your liking. After all, how am I supposed to know just how much information you believe is "enough?" Especially since...
3) We may have a difference of opinion on how much the BD article should say about the siege.
a) One reason for the move is that the siege controversy is a black hole, and that moving the most controversial issues to another page will hopefully free the BD page to go deeper into the history and faith of the Branch Davidians. In this respect, I would like the BD page to say as little as I can get away with about the siege. The more that gets said, the greater the chance of crossing someone's political tripwires, and ending up with two hotly disputed pages rather than one.
b) I feel that the siege should be kept in perspective, as a key event, but not as the main focus of the article. With the siege getting disproportionate attention in the BD entry, other sections of the article were seeing little development. While the short paragraph I left may seem undercooked, that is not out of place in an article where other aspects are underdeveloped. That is, one of my concerns is not just how much the BD article should ultimately say about the siege, but that what it says be kept in proportion to the rest of the article. As more is added to the entry about other subjects, it may be appropriate to say more about the siege.
c) I also felt that the move was something that was being talked about, but that no one was actually stepping forward to do. Thus, I felt that the most important thing to do was to get it done, and if I left the BD article covering the siege too briefly, to worry about that later.
I don't want this to turn into a revert seesaw. My next edit will leave a bit more about the siege, but if you're still dissatisfied, I think you should add in what you think is missing, rather than making another total revert. Only you know exactly what level of detail you want and which facts you believe to be the salient ones. I don't want to grope around, being reverted, until you feel I "get it right." Also, since there seems to be no disagreement that Waco Siege will become the main article for the siege, the longer the BD page remains in its old state, the greater the chance that people will make edits to a section that is now deprecated, and will ultimately disappear. --WacoKid 19:53, 12 February 2006 (UTC)

[edit] Three weeks of admin tools

[edit] Three weeks of admin tools

Today three weeks have passed since I was granted access to the administrator toolbox. During this time I have made use of it in the following way:

  • Protections and unprotections: 1
  • Blocks and unblocks: 4
  • Deletions and restorations: 69
  • Rollbacks: 246

I've found that the rollback tool is much more useful than I'd thought for vandalism patrol. In fact it makes that task so easy that I've been doing it more than before. On the other hand I've been surprised by how little the blocking tool is needed. Having done a significant amount of vandalism patrol I have still only blocked one solitary vandal. The great majority of addresses which send out a vandal edit do so only once. Those who do it more often usually stop after a warning or two. Only rarely is a block actually needed and in those cases someone usually beats me to it.

As a side note I haven't retired from writing articles either. I'm still hoping to bring Freyr up to featured status but even though I've already performed more edits on it than on Hrafnkels saga back in the day, a lot of work remains to be done. Community expectations for featured articles have gone up and so have my own ambitions. I'm currently waiting for a couple of books I ordered to arrive and then I may be able to make the final push.

I'm trying my best to live up to the trust you showed in me by supporting my RFA. If ever you feel uncertain whether I'm using the admin tools in the best interests of the project, let me know. I am at any time willing to relinquish the mop and reapply for it to address concerns people have and ensure that I'm not using the admin tools without being trusted to do so. Haukur 22:28, 12 February 2006 (UTC)
