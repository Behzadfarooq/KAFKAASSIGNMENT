[edit] We can't allow fair use images in user pages

I had to remove the Windows Vista logo from your user page because it violated fair use images rule #9. If you have questions about why this is nessary, you can ask Durin on his talk page. Will (Talk - contribs) 03:56, 26 January 2007 (UTC)

Furthermore, images like the "Homemade Vista Logo" you made are also copyright infringements, because they look too similar to the original they're imitating (it's the idea that's copyrighted, not a particular alignment of pixels). Therefore, you may not simply put a GFDL tag on it and pretend it's an original image. Please stop looking for loopholes and putting Wikipedia in risk of violating US copyright law. Thank you, –ú–∏—à–∞13 23:07, 26 January 2007 (UTC)

Ask a lawyer (or someone at WP:CP or WP:VPP) how far the line goes. But anything even resembling the logo is a possible copyright violation, including 4 straight rectangles, red, blue, green and yellow, aligned in a specific way. –ú–∏—à–∞13 10:43, 27 January 2007 (UTC)
