Ardmore, Barra

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Ardmore (Scottish Gaelic: Aird Mhòr) is a small village in the north east of the Isle of Barra in the Outer Hebrides, Scotland.

In 2002 a small ferry port was built in the village to serve a new ferry link between Barra and Eriskay as part of the Sound of Barra Integrated Transport Project. The ferry service started in spring 2003. A new causeway had previously been built linking Eriskay to South Uist.

This new daily service on the 'MV Loch Bhrusda' has frequent sailings throughout the day and takes only 40 minutes compared to 90 minutes for the Castlebay - Lochboisdale service on the larger CalMac ship the 'MV Clansman' which was previously the main connection between the islands of Barra and South Uist. This service however was not daily and only formed part of the route from Oban-Castlebay-Lochboisdale. Now with the establishment of the small vehicle ferry between Barra and Eriskay, the 'MV Loch Bhrusda' takes the most traffic from Barra with the exception of large lorries which use the 'MV Clansman'.
