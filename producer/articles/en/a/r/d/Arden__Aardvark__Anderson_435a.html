Arden "The Aardvark" Anderson

Arden "Aardvark" Anderson (b. December 18, 1972 in Harlem, New York) was an American super heavyweight boxer, and former USBA Super Middleweight champion. He had a career record of 31-4-1, with 21 wins coming by way of knockout, and was knicknamed "The Aardvark," because of his ugly face and tendency to display his buckteeth.

However, his boxing career has been hampered by legal troubles stemming from allegations of hate crimes and racially motivated violence. He was suspended from the USBA in January of 1994 as a result of the board of directors perception of racism, most notably his tendency to only fight black opponents, whom he is quoted as referring to as "In need of a good beating".

[edit] Biography

Anderson started off his career by scoring 19 wins and only 2 losses in the superheavyweight division. This led to a title fight against Sven Ottke who beat Anderson by unanimous decision. Anderson was deeply upset by the pairing, refusing to give full effort. Later, in a televised interview he explained the situation, claiming that it was against his principles to hit a "white brotha".

Anderson's next bout earned him infamy on November 23, 1991 at the Roseland Ballroom in Manhattan, New York. After losing by unanimous decision to Richard "The Alien" Grant, Anderson had his gloves removed and went to Grant's side of the ring for an expected exchange of congratulations. Suddenly, Anderson hit the unsuspecting Grant with a right hook to the jaw. Grant's jaw was badly broken. Anderson was arrested and charged with aggravated assault.

Anderson's last bout before being banned from the sport for life was a split-decision loss on August 10, 1994.
