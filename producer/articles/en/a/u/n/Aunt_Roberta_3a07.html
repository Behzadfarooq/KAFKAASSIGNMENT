[edit] History

The beverage is based on a mixed drink invented in the late 19th century by an Alabama woman known today only as Aunt Roberta. Little is known about Roberta except that she was the mulatto daughter of a slave owner who fled an abusive home life and alcoholic father at the age of eleven. Early on, she made her living picking cotton, and was nomadic for much of her early life. She spent some time as a prostitute before running a bootleg shack, where she began her career in the alcoholic beverage business by selling drinks made of homemade gin and moonshine. Most of the patrons of her establishment were homeless people, and it's estimated that 34 people died over the two years she sold her homemade concoction.

Later on, she came up with her signature cocktail accidentally, while intoxicated. She could never re-create it, and died in poverty at the age of 32.

The drink was made famous when an accquaintance of Aunt Roberta's, a raccoon hunter named Billy Joe Spratt, introduced his version of the cocktail to New York after Aunt Roberta's death in 1886. Spratt opened a series of bars based on the Aunt Roberta cocktail, and within two years became a millionaire.
