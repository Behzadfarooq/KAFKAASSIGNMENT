Auvers-sur-Oise is a commune in the northwestern suburbs of Paris, France. It is located 27.2 km. (16.9 miles) from the center of Paris.

[edit] History

On August 1, 1948, 17% of the territory of Auvers-sur-Oise was detached and became the commune of Butry-sur-Oise.

[edit] Van Gogh

Church of Auvers-sur-Oise painted by Vincent van Gogh ca. 1890

Auvers-sur-Oise is perhaps most famous for being the final resting place of both Vincent van Gogh and his brother Theo. Visitors to the Ravoux Inn can take a tour of both the inn and the immediate area of sites important to van Gogh's local history. Indeed, on the upper floor of the inn one can view the bed where van Gogh died after attempting suicide by shooting himself in the stomach.

Vincent and Theo van Gogh's graves in Auvers-sur-Oise

Auvers-sur-Oise's other famous resident from this period is Dr. Paul Gachet, who treated and befriended van Gogh during his stay in the village. His fame comes due to being the subject of a portrait that van Gogh did of him, which became one of the highest-priced paintings ever sold at auction. Visitors taking the tour can view various locations made famous via van Gogh's paintings.

[edit] Transport

Auvers-sur-Oise is served by two stations on the Transilien Paris – Nord suburban rail line: Chaponval and Auvers-sur-Oise.
