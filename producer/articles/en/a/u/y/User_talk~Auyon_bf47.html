Simply stated, here is how an article becomes FA. Someone (or a group of people) writes the article with enough pictures, and inline references, then requests a (or a number of) peer reviews. Many issues arise from these peer reviews. After dealing with all of these, one submits the article for FA status, and other wikipedians will support or object. Depending on the response, a comittee finally accepts of rejects the nominations. Look up Rabindranath Tagore or Bangladesh (and specially their talk pages, where the peer reviews etc are archived)--ppm 16:09, 31 July 2006 (UTC)

[edit] link to Persian

Hello, when you want to link to the article about something Persian, please do not link to Persian, as that is a disambiguation page (which nothing should be linked to). Instead link to the one of the options found on that page such as Persian people, Persian language, or Iran, by writing out [[Persian language|Persian]] or [[Iran|Persian]]. Regards, Jeff3000 06:13, 10 September 2006 (UTC)
