Exhibitions at Espoo City Museum, WeeGee

AURORA KARAMZIN and the Stages of Power

The Aurora Karamzin and the Stages of Power exhibition at Espoo City Museum is a brilliant overview of the life of the aristocracy in the 19th century. Aurora Karamzin (née Stjernvall, 1808-1902) is one of the best-known Finns and her life story provides a unique insight into Finnish history.

In her younger days, Aurora was lady-in-waiting to Empress Alexandra Feodorovna, consort to Emperor Nicholas I of Russia. It was on the recommendation of the empress that, in 1836, Aurora married Prince Paul Demidoff, Councillor of State and Huntmaster of the Imperial Court. Paul Demidoff was a member of a Russian family that had acquired a huge fortune through mining, thus making him one of the wealthiest men in Russia.

Aurora Demidova was widowed in 1840 and left with a son, Paul (1839-1885), who was just a few months old. A rich widow, she lived in her palace in St Petersburg, Russia and on her Träskända estate in Espoo, Finland. In 1846, Aurora married Captain Andrei Karamzin, son of Russian historian Nikolai Karamzin. This marriage, too, was short-lived since Andrei Karamzin was killed in the Crimean War in 1854.

During her lifetime, Aurora Karamzin was a remarkable benefactor, well known for her extensive charitable work. There was also much splendour in her life and she travelled extensively in Russia and Europe, bringing many international impulses to Finland.

The exhibition shows 19th century Russian and European cultural history through Aurora Karamzin’s remarkable life story. The exhibition features rare objects of the era now on show in Finland for the first time, including items on loan from the State Hermitage Museum in St Petersburg, the Museum of the History of St Petersburg and Hillwood Museum in Washington D.C. Besides objects on loan from Finland, there are items from England and France. Exhibition themes include the lifestyle of the gentry, court life in St Petersburg, decorations and jewellery, the Demidoffs’ business, travelling and charity work.

In conjunction with the exhibition, there is also the Salonki workshop, where visitors can try on clothing and learn about the fashion secrets of the day. A host of other events will be held including lectures, workshops and guided theme tours for both children and adults alike during the exhibition.
