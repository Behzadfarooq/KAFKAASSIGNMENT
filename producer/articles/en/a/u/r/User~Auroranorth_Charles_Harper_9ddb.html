Charles Harper, b. 15 July 1842, was a farmer, Western Australian legislator and newspaper proprietor, whose most notable achievements include the founding of Guildford Grammar School. Charles was the only son of father Charles Harper and mother Julia Gretchen, née Lukin. [1]

[edit] Early Life

Little is known about Harper's early life, but we do know that he was educated by his father near Toodyay until he was 16. In 1856, his mother gifted him a horse and cart, a barrel of salt pork, a gun and £50 and sent him off by himself to find a farm. He leased land between York and Beverley, where he farmed for several years and made the 'Harper fence', using local timber instead of imported wire. In 1864 Harper joined the hunt for land in the Yilgarn district and made important observations. In 1866, Harper sailed for Roebourne in the Pilbara to take up pearling. With this profit, Harper bought a one-third interest in 883,000 acre de Grey station in 1871. In 1878 Harper sold his share in this station and bought into a smaller station - he held this until 1904.

[edit] Print Career

In 1879 Harper bought into The Western Australian Times. On 23 March that same year, Harper married Fanny de Burgh of Caversham and settled at Woodbridge, near Midland and Guildford.
