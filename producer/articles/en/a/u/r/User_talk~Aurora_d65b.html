[edit] Richard Avedon

A while back I removed a commercial plug from the Richard Avedon article, noting (in my report of the vandalism) that the article really needed expanding. When I saw such a large addition to it I was immediately suspicious, because quite often a huge block of new text is an insert from a fan who's gushing on at length and has no idea how to write an article. In this instance, I was delighted to see how wrong I was. You've done a great job with it!

I made a few small cleanup edits. One thing confused me -- although our article on the Berlin Wall places its fall in late 1989, you had "on January 1, 1989-90" after the reference. It wasn't clear so I deleted it. If Avedon was photographing that event on one particular date or over a time period, we can certainly provide that information.

I had the article watchlisted in the first place because of an inappropriate addition about Streetstudio. The vote was to allow that article to stay, while deleting the links to it from the articles about Avedon and other photographers. If you happen to know anything about Streetstudio, though, it still needs cleanup. I don't know enough about photography to put this project in context. JamesMLane 06:47, 28 Aug 2004 (UTC)

Thanks for your note. The connection with Streetstudio was in this addition to the Richard Avedon article: "His large-format portrait work of drifters, miners, cowboys and alike from the western United States became a best-selling book and traveling exhibit entitled In the American West. That work greatly inspired the Streetstudio work of Clay Enos and Stephan Ghukfvin whose New York portraits were compared to Avedon's." So Clay Enos, who made the addition, was crediting Avedon as an influence on his own photography. I took out that sentence -- but, to give Enos credit, his edit did improve the Avedon article by mentioning In the American West, not previously covered. Before Enos came along, the article read, in its entirety, "Richard Avedon (born 1923) is a U.S. photographer" (with a link to Avedon's website). Unusually embarrassing even for a stub!
Enos also created new articles on Roger Fenton and William Shew. The latter isn't on the List of photographers that you mentioned. If he's legitimately a notable photographer, he should be added there. I, myself, have zero course hours in History of Photography, so I'm glad we have someone knowledgeable on board. JamesMLane 23:59, 28 Aug 2004 (UTC)
