Aufhausen Priory

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Aufhausen Priory (Kloster Aufhausen), formerly Aufhausen Oratory, is now a Benedictine monastery, founded in the late 17th century as an Oratorian community, located at Aufhausen near Regensburg in Bavaria, Germany.

The original community, dedicated to Saint Mary of the Snows, was founded and inspired by the local priest, J.G. Seidenbusch, towards the end of the 17th century. It was not dissolved during the secularisation of Bavaria in 1802–03, but remained under the title "Königliches Nerianerinstitut" (Royal Nerian Institute), at least until 1829. The last provost died in 1886.

It was re-settled in 1890 from the Benedictine house at Metten, and partly re-built.
