Auchinloch

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Auchinloch (Gaelic: Achadh an Locha) is a village in North Lanarkshire, near Lenzie. The village's name - "Field of the Little Loch" - derives from its proximity to a little lake locally called the "Gadloch". Industrialization came in the 1880s, with the opening of the Lumloch Colliery, and then in the 1920s Wester Auchengeich Colliery. The latter closed in 1968.

The village contains a primary school, Auchinloch Primary and Auchinloch Hall, a council-run hall. An independent church also meets in the hall. The Auchinloch Gala is held in June.

Just outside Auchinloch is the Wallace Well, also Cardyke Farm Shelter, a branch of Cats Protection.
