[edit] Auchentoshan Distillery

Auchentoshan is one of the few remaining Lowland Malt whisky distilleries in Scotland. Established in 1823 the distillery lies to the North-West of Glasgow and is a short journey from the city to Loch Lomond.

Unusually for a Scottish distillery, Auchentoshan practices triple distillation. Generally the final stage of Scotch whisky production involves distilling the fermented mash in two copper stills. In Auchentoshan, a third still gives a final spirit strength of 81% ABV (162% proof). This triple distillation, complemented by unpeated malt, gives Auchentoshan a more delicate flavour than many Scotch Whiskies. Maturation is in ex-bourbon barrels and ex-sherry butts.

[edit] Core Range

  • Auchentoshan Select
  • Auchentoshan 10 year old
  • Auchentoshan 3 wood
  • Auchentoshan 21 year old

Special bottlings are released periodically, including the oldest, a 41 year old Auchentoshan distilled in 1962 and released in 2003.

[edit] Other Information

The distillery has a visitor centre and conference facilities, both completed in 2005, and is open to the public for tours.
