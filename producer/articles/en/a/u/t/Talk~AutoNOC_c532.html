For starters, read WP:SOFTWARE, the notability guideline for software. That will help. -- Scientizzle 23:41, 31 January 2007 (UTC)
Again, I don't believe 50% or more of the software packages listed on this page List_of_Network_Management_Systems meet this criteria to a greater extent than AutoNOC, and many that are listed are far less familiar to people. If the primary problem is the source of the article, we can have 3rd parties post the page if that is the primary issue. I don't have a problem with eliminating the entire list and purging all the pages for all the software listed on that page, but to selectively exclude certain software from that list without a binding, consistent, uniform criteria is problematic in my view. I simply don't believe half of the packages listed on that page are 'notable', certainly not more than AutoNOC. If the goal is to get rid of this page, fair enough. But all the others should be thrown out as well. I've been involved in this space for close to a decade and maybe 50% of the software on this list I've never even heard of. I doubt highly even half the other packages monitor the same amount of total network traffic. Do a web search on the package as well to educate yourself about it. Just because a wiki editor isn't familiar with it, doesn't mean the right people aren't. AutoNOC monitors possibly 1-2% of total global Internet traffic. Doesn't that make it notable enough? I will work on getting permission from people to publish and source those numbers (not easy to get the permission), but they are real. The problem here is one of consistent application of wiki policies. The process appears to be selectively critical and selectively enforcing. Where are the related markings on the other pages and scrutiny that this page is receiving?
