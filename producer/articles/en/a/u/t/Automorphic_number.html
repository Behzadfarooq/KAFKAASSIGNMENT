In mathematics an automorphic number (sometimes referred to as a circular number) is a number whose square "ends" in the number itself. For example, 52 = 25, 762 = 5776, and 8906252 = 793212890625.

The automorphic numbers begin 1, 5, 6, 25, 76, 376, 625, 9376, ... (sequence A003226 in OEIS)
