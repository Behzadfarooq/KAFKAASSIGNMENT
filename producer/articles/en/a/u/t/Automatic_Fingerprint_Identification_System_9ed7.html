Automatic Fingerprint Identification System

Automated Fingerprint Identification System (AFIS) or Automated Fingerprint Verification System refers to a computer system capable of establishing the identity of an individual through fingerprints. While AFIS usually referes to criminal and federal systems, Automated Fingerprint Verification is used to refer to more civilan applications such as attendance and access control systems. On a technical level, verification systems verify a claimed identity (a user might claim to be John by presenting his PIN or ID card and verify his identity using his fingerprint) where as an identification system determines the identity based solely on fingerprints. (No identity is presented). The US government maintains an extensive database system containing fingerprint and criminal history system that is maintained by the Federal Bureau of Investigation (FBI) and Criminal Justice Information Services Division (CJIS) in the United States. Almost every state has its own AFIS also. Criminal and federal AFIS systems have extended capabilities such as latent searching capability, electronic image storage, and electronic exchange of fingerprints and responses.
