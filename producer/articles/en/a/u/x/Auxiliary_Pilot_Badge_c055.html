Auxiliary Pilot Badge

The Auxiliary Pilot Badge was a qualification badge of the United States Army Air Force which was issued during the years of the Second World War. The Auxiliary Pilot wings was presented to members of the United States Army Air Forces or civilian pilots serving the USAAF in non-military duty and was authorized in three designs. The badge appeared as the standard USAAF Pilot Badge with one of three letters centered on the badgesâ€™ shield, or escutcheon. The letter on the badge indicated the qualification type.

The Glider Pilot wings was issued to those personnel who completed training as pilots of military gliders. The wings were issued only during the Second World War, and Glider Pilots often said that the "G" on the shield stood for "Guts". The USAAF trained approximately 6,600 Glider Pilots during the war. These wings should not be confused with the Glider Infantry Badge which was created in 1944 to recognize glider infantrymen of U.S. Airborne Divisions.

The Service Pilot Badge was awarded to non-military personnel who performed military-related duties such as pilot or aircrew training, gunnery training, aircraft courier missions and aircraft ferrying.

The Liaison Pilot Badge was the last of the three Auxiliary Pilot Badges and was considered the rarest. The Liaison Pilot Badge was presented to military aviators assigned as permanent liaisons to the Air Forces of allied countries, such as the Royal Air Force and French Air Force. The Liaison Pilot Badge was also presented to foreign military personnel who were performing the same duties within the United States.

The Glider Pilot wings are currently awarded to cadet instructor pilots at the USAF Academy glider flight training program, by permission of the Academy Superintendent and the Nationa WW2 Glider Pilots' Association.
