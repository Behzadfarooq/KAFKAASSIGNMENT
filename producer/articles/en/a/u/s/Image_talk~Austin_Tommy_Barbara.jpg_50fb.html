A few misconceptions that I think you might have:
  • "free use" is not a term I'm familiar with. If you meant "fair use", then that's a very specific and narrowly defined legal term which must be explained and defended on a case-by-case basis.
  • "I dont think copyright applies" is a dangerous misconception of copyright law. Copyright applies automatically to any work created by anyone in the United States (and most of the developed world). Only an explicit release of a work into the public domain can remove copyright from it.
  • Not knowing the source of an image for sure is the most likely way to have an image removed from Wikipedia.
If you would like to defend the use of this image, I suggested describing exactly how it came to you (URL, name of photographer, watever you have) and explaining the context in which you intended to use it. Others can make the determination of how that does or does not fall under fair use. -Harmil 17:53, 25 March 2006 (UTC)
