[edit] Requested move

WP:RM page - * Talk:Austin Healey — Austin Healey → Austin Healey (rugby) – The other meaning of Austin Healey (the car brand) is much more common - at least the top 100 hits in google are all for the car Spute 10:21, 7 December 2005 (UTC)

  • Talk:Austin-Healey — Austin-Healey → Austin Healey – The car brand (currently Austin-Healey article) should not be hyphenated, it is not hyphenated in its own logo- or at Enthusiast web sites, such as the top hit on Google. Hyphenation is sometimes used though, so Austin-Healey should redirect to Austin Healey Spute 10:21, 7 December 2005 (UTC)

[edit] Voting

Add *Support or *Oppose followed by an optional one sentence explanation, then sign your vote with ~~~~
  • Support Yes, it should be moved to Austin Healey. The name of this car was never hyphenated. That would be akin to hyphenating 'Aston Martin'. By the way, the Austin Healey car is substantially more well known in the world than Austin Healey the rugby player. I suggest renaming Austin Healey the rugby player to 'Austin Healey (Rugby Player)'.
  • Support The car's name is not usually/always hyphenated, and indeed isn't in the Austin Healey logo. [Image:Wingspr.JPG]. Spute 10:33, 7 December 2005 (UTC)
  • Oppose Choice of target to move to, should have been Austin Healey (car) or somesuch. GraemeLeggett 10:51, 7 December 2005 (UTC)


[edit] Discussion

Add any additional comments
  • Comment. The See Also section lists two similar initiatives — Nash-Healey and Jensen-Healey — and they are both hyphenated, so we'd be breaking a trend by renaming this one. We'd also have to rename Austin-Healey 100, Austin-Healey 3000 and Austin-Healey Sprite. I don't see much issue with keeping both pages where they are — there are dablinks at the top of both, so a wrong hit will quickly be redirected to the desired location. I'm on the verge of voting oppose here, but will refrain due to lack of knowledge of either rugby or vintage sports cars. --Qviri 10:42, 7 December 2005 (UTC)
  • Comment Google should not be used as a measure of acuuracy for anything without careful consideration of the results. GraemeLeggett 10:48, 7 December 2005 (UTC)
    • Yes, that's true. I was simply trying to find if anyone has ever put anything on the web about the rugby player, Austin Healey... and there's nothing down to 100 hits (when i stopped looking any further). I'd say that's fairly conclusive, and doesn't require much in the way of consideration? 82.69.4.121 10:53, 7 December 2005 (UTC)
      • Search for Austin Healy rugby and you'll find stuff.GraemeLeggett 11:05, 7 December 2005 (UTC)
        • Yes, but as far as use of the words Austin Healey goes, the rugby player is a very minor usage ('Austin Healey car' has 2,930,000 hits, 'Austin Healey rugby' only 149,000 - 5% as many). I don't know, i'm not massively opinionated on this, whichever way the consensus falls is fine by me, but i've had said Austin Healey should go to the most common use.Spute 12:49, 7 December 2005 (UTC)
    • I think the Austin Healey should go directly to the article about the car, without going via a dablink, since it's so much more common a usage of the words. As for hypenation, it is debatable (and inconsistent) in the cases of all three cars (Austin-Healey,Nash-Healey and Jensen-Healey), but even if the car article stays at Austin-Healey, i think Austin Healey should be a redirect to the car page.Spute 10:50, 7 December 2005 (UTC)
      • Why break a content-ful page that has a dablink at the top anyway by making it into just a redirect? If someone's looking for the car, it'll just take them one click more — not even if they're coming from google (where car article is the first wikipedia result) or from another article that will have the car linked correctly. --Qviri 11:12, 7 December 2005 (UTC)
