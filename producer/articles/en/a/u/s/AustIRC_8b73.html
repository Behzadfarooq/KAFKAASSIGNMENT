AustIRC

AustIRC is an IRC network which provides a more friendly and secure alternative to many other IRC networks such as DALnet and EFNet. AustIRC was forked from AustNet on the 12th of February 2007, It now has servers located in Australia and the United States.

[edit] About

AustIRC feature their own service system and IRCD. It uses ChanOP (Channel Registration) and NickOP (Nickname Registration) services.

[edit] Forked from AustNet

February 12th 2007 saw a major split within Austnet with most of the Administrators, IRCop's and Servers moving to a forked network called AustIRC.

The forked network now consists of the majority of former Austnet staff and several former Austnet servers.

The trigger for the split was a controversial ballot in which it was proposed to dismiss one of the IRCop's as the Chief Operations Officer and upgrade the Austnet IRC services. The ballot was due to be completed in March, however before completion the IRCop who was likely to be affected decided to take matters into his own hands. This caused the bulk of Austnet's administrators to move operations and the proposed upgraded services to a new network.

Some people using both AustNet and AustIRC have reported having channels suspended on AustNet which contain links to the new network. Austnet claim that this in accordance with their anti-spam policy, however others have viewed this as an attempt by Austnet to stifle the growth of the forked network.

At the time of writing Austnet has four linked servers and AustIRC has seven.
