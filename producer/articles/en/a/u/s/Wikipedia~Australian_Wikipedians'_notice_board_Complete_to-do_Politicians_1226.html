[edit] Checklist of Members of the House of Representatives 1901-2004

There will be some problems with this checklist:

  • Some people will appear twice, since they served in both the House and the Senate
  • Some names will appear twice because two MPs had the same name (eg the two Kim Beazleys). You will need to check at my website to figure out who these were.
  • Some names appear under different forms to those in articles, eg Bob Hawke appears as Robert Hawke
  • Some MPs and Senators will need to be disambiguated from other people of the same name.

Adam 03:41, 4 Oct 2004 (UTC)

If you create one of these, please remove the name from here and add it to the created articles page. Ambi 08:33, 6 Oct 2004 (UTC)

Please note that I have now created Australian Commonwealth ministries 1901-2004, in which every MHR and Senator who ever held ministerial office is listed and wikified. If you create new articles for any such person, ensure that the form of the name you use is the same one I have used in that article, so that we don't inadvertantly create two articles for the same person (eg, don't create an article called James Lloyd when we already have one called Jim Lloyd). Adam 06:33, 21 Nov 2004 (UTC)

[edit] Clarification

  • The two Frank Bakers were son and father
Frank Baker (Francis Matthew John): ALP MP for Oxley Qld 1931-34, Griffith Qld 1934-39
Frank Baker (Francis Patrick): ALP MP for Maranoa Qld 1940-43
  • The two Harry Jenkins were father and son
Hon Dr Harry Jenkins: ALP MP for Scullin Vic 1969-86
Harry Jenkins: ALP MP for Scullin Vic from 1986
  • The two John McLeays were father and son. (I've now given them separate pages Rocksong 02:43, 24 April 2006 (UTC)).
Hon Sir John McLeay: Liberal MP for Boothby SA 1949-66
Hon John McLeay: Liberal MP for Boothby SA 1966-81
  • The two David Watkins were father and son
Hon David Watkins: ALP MP for Newcastle NSW 1901-35
David Watkins: ALP MP for Newcastle NSW 1935-58
  • The two John Forrests are not related
Rt Hon Sir John Forrest: Protectionist, Liberal and Nationalist MP for Swan WA 1901-18
John Forrest: NPA MP for Mallee Vic from 1993
  • The two Eric Harrisons are not related
Eric Harrison: UAP MP for Bendigo Vic 1931-37
Rt Hon Sir Eric Harrison: UAP, Liberal MP for Wentworth NSW 1931-56
  • The two Tony Smiths are not related
Tony Smith: Liberal MP for Dickson Qld 1996-98
Tony Smith: Liberal MP for Casey Vic from 2001

[edit] Tables

I created these tables to keep track of all the pollies.. obviously it will be extended to cover all electorates and all federal elections. I just wanted to show people what I was planning before I did the whole thing. Eventually, I will expect it to look like Wikipedia:WikiProject Sports Olympics/Articles.

Note these are NOT intended to be used in articles, they are for managing existing and forthcoming articles. The idea is to use the red/blue links to see what has been created, and what needs to be done. The 'related changes' can also be used to keep track of recent edits.

The shading indicates party affiliation - I will get rid of this if people think it isn't useful, or makes the links harder to read - and if people really want, I can attempt to use various shades of colours to indicate safety levels of seats, but that might be a bit much! Also note I'm not attempting to make the initials of the MPs unique, they are just there so there is some text to link the MP article to.

-- Chuq 23:28, 16 Oct 2004 (UTC)
