[edit] How much time can you spend on Wikipedia?

I am constantly amazed at the amount of time that some people (I wonder who?) spend on Wikipedia. Every time I edit something, it seems that others are immediately on top of it. I'm all for the advancement of human knowledge, but can information turn into an obsession?

Eh, I'm not nearly as bad as a lot of other Wikipedians. In fact, I haven't really been that active recently since my parents disapprove of my Wikipedia activities. I only have maybe a 1000 edits (including my IP edits), most of which are minor. There are some who have a 100,000 edits, most of which aren't minor. I know you think we're crazy, but Wikipedia is only as awesome as it is because most of us Wikipedians tend to be a little obsessive-compulsive when it comes to editing. Maybe you can't relate to this, but Wikipedia is totally addictive. I feel as if I'm using my intelligence for community service. Like, the edits I make will impact articles that are read by thousands. It's sooo addicting. Oh, and I keep an eye on the edits of you and Shamus, which explains why I often immediately edit articles right after you. Btw, don't forget to sign your talk page edits. Sue H. Ping (talk • contribs) 21:29, 4 February 2007 (UTC)
