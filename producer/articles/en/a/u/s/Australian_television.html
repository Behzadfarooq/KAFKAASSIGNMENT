Australian television began in Australia with black and white transmissions in 1956. Colour television was introduced in 1974. Television broadcast in Australia contains a wide of range of programming, either made in Australian or imported from overseas. They are broadcast on Free-to-air television and Pay TV (cable television or satellite television). Digital transmissions began in parallel with existing analogue services in January 2001.

[edit] History

Planning for Australian television began in the late 1940s and in 1954, the Federal Government announced that a government-funded service and two commercial services would be introduced in Sydney and Melbourne from 1956. The 1956 Summer Olympics in Melbourne were a catalyst for its introduction. The Nine Network began broadcasting on 16 September 1956 and it was soon followed by the Australian Broadcasting Corporation on 5th November 1956.

[edit] Notable Programs

Over the years, there have been many notable Australian-made television series and programs, police dramas being arguably the most popular genre. The earliest of these, Homicide made by Crawford Productions, revolutionised Australian television drama production. It was followed by Division 4 and Matlock Police, which also enjoyed great popularity and long runs. Other successful police drama series have included Cop Shop, Police Rescue, Blue Heelers, Water Rats and Stingers. Medical dramas have also proved popular with shows such as Flying Doctors, GP, A Country Practice and All Saints.

There have also been a number of notable Australian-made television mini-series, including the following Against the Wind, All the Rivers Run, Bodyline: It's Just not Cricket, Brides of Christ, The Dismissal and The Timeless Land.

Australia has also produced many popular soap operas including Bellbird, Number 96, The Sullivans, Prisoner, Sons and Daughters, Neighbours and Home and Away.
