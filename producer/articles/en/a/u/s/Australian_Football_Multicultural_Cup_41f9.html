Australian Football Multicultural Cup

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

The Australian Football Multicultural Cup is an amateur Australian rules football competition coordinated by Aussie Rules International (formerly International Australian Football Council) allowing players born overseas to represent their country of origin.

It is played in Melbourne, one of the world's most multicultural cities, on National Harmony Day (21st March) each year.

[edit] Background

Australian Football is also known for a high participation rate amongst migrants, particularly amongst Europeans. Many such players reach the highest levels and some like Ron Barassi, Stephen Silvagni, Robert DiPierdomenico and Alex Jesaulenko are legends of the game. Some Australian Football League clubs, such as Carlton are renowned for the high number of players of mediterranean origin.

8 teams are presently represented in the Multicultural Cup and include Australia (an all indigenous team consisting of aboriginal players), Croatia, Israel, Lebanon, Greece, Italy, Vietnam and Turkey.

Almost a quarter of Victoria's population was born overseas and come from 233 countries, speak over 180 languages and dialects and follow 116 religious faiths.

[edit] History

The first Multicultural Cup was held in 2004, with Israel 8.9 (57) defeating Samoa 1.0 (6) in the Grand Final.

The 2005 Multicultural Cup was won by Greece 5.5 (35) over Turkey 3.3 (21) in front of a crowd of 2,300 at Optus Oval in Carlton, Melbourne.

The event was not held in 2006, primarily due to a clash with the 2006 Commonwealth Games being held in Melbourne but is likely to return in 2007.
