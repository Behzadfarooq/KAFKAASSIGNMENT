Australia is an upcoming film slated to be directed by Baz Luhrmann and starring Nicole Kidman and Hugh Jackman. The AUD$130 million feature, financed by 20th Century Fox, will tell the story of an English aristocrat, played by Kidman, who settles on an Outback station in the 1930s and becomes romantically involved with an Outback drover, played by Jackman. The pair are caught up in the bombing of Darwin during World War II. Additionally, David Wenham[1] will play the antagonist, a station manager who is plotting to possess the station, which is called Faraway Downs.

[edit] Production

Australian writer Stuart Beattie is involved in the project. Both Russell Crowe and Heath Ledger[2][3] were once scheduled to star in the film, but neither of them are any longer involved.[4][5].

Filming began in the Kimberley on December 20, 2006.[1]

It has been confirmed that principal photography will take place in the North Queensland town of Bowen. Further filming in northern Australia around Kununurra and on Carlton Hill station will commence in April, 2007. Some filming was done in Camden, on the outskirts of Sydney.[6]
