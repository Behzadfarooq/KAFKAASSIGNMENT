Australian rules football in Finland

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Finland Lions in 2006

The Finland Ice Breakers are the representative Australian rules football team in Finland. They were formerly known as the Lions. They are the current holder of the Central European AFL Championships, having won the tournament in September 2006. The team primarily consists of local Finns, as well as a few Australian expatriates, and some other nationalities are also represented (English, Irish).

While the team was started by Australian expatriates, it has been captained by a Finn ever since the 2nd match, and Finnish representation now exceeds 50%.

[edit] History

  • Nov 5, 2005, vs Czech Lions, Prague, Czech Republic: Finland 6.5 (41) def. Czesky Republika 5.7 (37).
  • May 13, 2006, vs Stockholm Dynamite, Myllypuro, Helsinki: Sweden def. Finland by 12 points (scores not available).
  • Aug 5, 2006, vs Stockholm Dynamite, Årstafältet, Stockholm: Stockholm 7.7 (49) def. Finland 6.8 (44).
  • Aug 26, 2006, vs Stockholm Dynamite, Myllypuro, Helsinki, Finland 9.2 (56) def. Stockholm 5.4 (34).
  • Sep 16, 2006, Central European AFL Championships, Prague:
    • Czech Republic 8.11 (59) def. Finland 8.4 (52),
    • Finland 15.9 (99) def. France 3.6 (24),
    • Finland 8.4 (52) def. Croatia 5.4 (34),
    • Finland 13.3 (81) def. Austria 6.2 (38),
    • Grand Final: Finland 14.6 (90) def. Croatia 6.9 (45).

[edit] Nickname and colours

The Finnish jumper is primarily blue, with a white rectangle in the centre with the blue cross of the Finnish flag through it, and the coat of arms immediately below it. The lion is the national symbol of Finland.

[edit] Trivia

The Lions' first home game on May 13, 2006 is claimed as the northernmost international game of Australian rules ever played, being hosted in Helsinki, the northernmost capital of Europe.

The Lions' first ever match - in Prague on November 11, left the Lions with the enviable title of the only undefeated AFL Nation in the world up to and including 2005.
