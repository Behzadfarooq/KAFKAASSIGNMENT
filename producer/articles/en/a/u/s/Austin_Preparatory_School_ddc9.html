Austin Preparatory School is a coeducational Catholic school located in Reading, Massachusetts. It was founded by the Augustinian order in 1961. It is colloquially referred to as Austin Prep. The school serves students in grades 6-12.

[edit] History

Austin Preparatory School first accepted students in September of 1963.

The School was founded by the Augustinians of the Province of St Thomas of Villanova. Since 1973 the School has been owned and operated by a lay Board of Trustees, and is an incorporated, non-profit organization. Augustinian friars continued to serve formally at Austin until 1992, while there are no Augustinians currently on the faculty, the current staff does include three priests and one female religious.

In 1987 Austin opened a Junior School Program, and further expanded in 1992 by admitting young women to become co-educational. The current enrollment (2006 - 2007) of 700 students includes approximately 350 young men and 350 young women. The Junior School enrolls roughly 250 students and the secondary division enrolls 450 students.

[edit] Demographics

Students come to us from over 50 towns ranging from Boston's near suburbs through the Merrimack Valley and southern New Hampshire.

Most of the young men and women who attend AP are Caucasian, but there is also a healthy amount of minorities.

[edit] Patron Saint

St. Augustine of Hippo as pictured during the Renaissance

The motto of the school is "God, Self, Neighbor", and the patron saint is Saint Augustine. The "Austin" in Austin Prep is a shortened form of Augustine. Augustine was a key figure in the doctrinal development of Western Christianity and is often referred to as a "Doctor of the Church" by Roman Catholics. Two of his many surviving works, namely "The Confessions" (his autobiography) and "The City of God" are Western Classics, and are read and studied by Christians of many denominations around the world. Augustine is also considered to be one of the theological fountainheads of Reformation, because of his teaching on salvation and grace; Martin Luther himself also having been an Augustinian friar. Augustine was not a Biblical fundamentalist.

Other American Augustinian Schools with the same patron include Richland, New Jersey; San Diego, California - both in the United States; Manila in the Philippines; a school in Malta, schools in Dungarvan and New Ross, Ireland and St. Augustine's College, Brookvale, in Sydney, Australia.
