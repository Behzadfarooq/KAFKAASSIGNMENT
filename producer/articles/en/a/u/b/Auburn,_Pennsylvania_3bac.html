Auburn is a borough in Schuylkill County, Pennsylvania, USA. The population was 839 at the 2000 census.

[edit] Geography

Auburn is located at 40°35′45″N, 76°5′34″W (40.595715, -76.092642)GR1.

According to the United States Census Bureau, the borough has a total area of 4.3 km² (1.7 mi²). 4.3 km² (1.7 mi²) of it is land and 0.60% is water.

[edit] Demographics

As of the censusGR2 of 2000, there were 839 people, 325 households, and 242 families residing in the borough. The population density was 195.1/km² (505.4/mi²). There were 340 housing units at an average density of 79.1/km² (204.8/mi²). The racial makeup of the borough was 98.93% White, 0.83% African American, 0.12% Asian, and 0.12% from two or more races.

There were 325 households out of which 33.5% had children under the age of 18 living with them, 57.2% were married couples living together, 11.7% had a female householder with no husband present, and 25.5% were non-families. 22.5% of all households were made up of individuals and 10.8% had someone living alone who was 65 years of age or older. The average household size was 2.58 and the average family size was 3.02.

In the borough the population was spread out with 24.7% under the age of 18, 6.8% from 18 to 24, 32.1% from 25 to 44, 23.7% from 45 to 64, and 12.8% who were 65 years of age or older. The median age was 38 years. For every 100 females there were 100.2 males. For every 100 females age 18 and over, there were 92.1 males.

The median income for a household in the borough was $36,905, and the median income for a family was $40,833. Males had a median income of $33,021 versus $20,707 for females. The per capita income for the borough was $15,705. About 8.3% of families and 7.7% of the population were below the poverty line, including 12.4% of those under age 18 and 1.8% of those age 65 or over.
