August von Fligely

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

August von Fligely (26 September 1811 â€“ 12 April 1879) was an Austrian Field Marshal Second Lieutenant, comparable to Lieutenant-General in the United States Army, and a geographer. A pioneer in arc measurement thory, he provided for the creation of quality maps in the third land survey of Austria-Hungary. The northernmost peak in Europe, Cape Fligely, was named after August von Fligely. He was director of the military geographical institute in Vienna from 1854 to 1872.
