[edit] Copyrighted text

I reverted the article because I think that David-snowball may have inserted a large amount of copyrighted text in this edit. I am not suggesting that he was intentionally violating copyright. I think he just did not know. I have had difficulty finding the pages that most of the text came from because the college appears to have removed or moved a lot of their web pages. However, the "music" content came from here. It is a Google cache because the page is no longer online. David-snowball's contributions can be seen here. He has uploaded five pictures from the college's website (his only other edits are to this article). He claims that they can be used freely, but I have not found that on their website and the music page above has a copyright notice. It would be rather unusual for them to do so, but not unbelievable. Because all of the text was added at once, some of it confirmed to be from the college's website, and he uploaded five pictures from the college's website, I think that the rest of the text came from there too. Unless the copyright status can be confirmed, I think that the article should not include the text and that the pictures should be deleted. -- Kjkolb 10:10, 7 March 2006 (UTC)

He's a professor here, and the chair of Augie's Speech major, so I assume he would know. Loque
