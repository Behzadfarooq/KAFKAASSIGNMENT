Augusta Regional Airport (IATA: AGS, ICAO: KAGS), also known as Augusta Regional Airport at Bush Field, is a municipal airport located 6 miles (10 km) south of Augusta, Georgia. This airport is publicly owned by City of Augusta.

In 2000, Bush Field airport changed its name to Augusta Regional Airport.

Airport is currently undergoing a major expansion and renovation project to attract more airlines and prevent travelers from driving to the Columbia or Atlanta airports.

[edit] Facilities

Augusta Regional Airport at Bush Field covers 1,411 acres and has two runways:

  • Runway 17/35: 8,001 x 150 ft. (2,439 x 46 m), Surface: Asphalt
  • Runway 8/26: 6,002 x 150 ft. (1,829 x 46 m), Surface: Asphalt
