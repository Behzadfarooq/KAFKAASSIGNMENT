Auggie Vidovich II

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Auggie Vidovich II (born February 20, 1981) is a driver in the NASCAR Busch Series who does not currently have a ride.

Vidovich was born in Lakeside, California. Vidovich has competed in the Elite Division Southwest series for several years. He won the championship in 2003, and finished second in 2000, 2001, and 2005. In 2005 Vidovich won the Toyota All-Star Challenge. At the end of the season, Vidovich tried out for Roush Racing's Craftsman Truck Series team in the TV show, Driver X, but was not selected. However, in the spring of 2006, Roush decided to hire Vidovich to run one truck race in place of a struggling David Ragan. Midway through the 2006 season, Vidovich was signed to pilot the #4 Geico Dodge Charger for the Biagi Brothers in the Busch Series, and recorded a best finish of 15th at the Milwaukee Mile.

In 2007, Vidovich was to have remained with Biagi-DenBeste Racing, but the team shut down because of lack of funding.
