August Vermeylen

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

August Vermeylen (1872–1945) was a Belgian writer and literature critic. In 1893 he founded the journal Van Nu en Straks (E: today and tomorrow). He studied history a the Universite Libre de Bruxelles (ULB), and became a professor of literature and of art history at the ULB (1901–1923), and in 1930 he was the first rector of the Flemish Universiteit Gent (Ghent). In addition to many works of literary and art criticism, he wrote poetry and in 1906 a novel, De wandelende Jood (E: the wandering Jew). The cultural organization, the Vermeylenfonds was named after him.
