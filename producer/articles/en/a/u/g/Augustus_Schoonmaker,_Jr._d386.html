Augustus Schoonmaker, Jr.

Augustus Schoonmaker, Jr. (born March 2, 1828, Rochester, New York; died April 9, 1894, Kingston, New York) was New York State Attorney General under Governor Lucius Robinson from November 6, 1877 to November 4, 1879. A Democrat from Kingston, New York, he was defeated for reelection by Hamilton Ward, Sr.

Schoonmaker was originally a teacher and superintendent of schools in Ulster County, New York. In 1853, he was admitted to the New York Bar. He was County Judge in Ulster County from 1864-72, and a member of the New York State Senate from 1876-77.

After his term as state Attorney General, Schoonmaker served on the New York Board of Civil Service Commissioners (1883-1887) and was an original appointee of President Grover Cleveland to the Interstate Commerce Commission from 1887-1890.

Schoonmaker died of tonsillitis in Kingston in 1894.
