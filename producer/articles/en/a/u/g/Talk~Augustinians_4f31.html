I would question the conclusion here that the Rule was not written by Augustine himself. Certainly there is scholarly debate over its dating, but as far as I am aware that is now mostly considered within the context of his life and other works. The Rule very closely reflects his own theological concerns and spirituality, and is easily placed as an instruction either for his own monastery in Hippo or for one of the various communities he patronized as a bishop. Can any major modern scholars or arguments be cited to the contrary? --ADMH 12:50, 28 May 2006 (UTC)

The point is AMDG that you need to post a reference to show this. I went to an Augustinian school (and a novitiate) and I was outraged to see the (arguably Protestant) assertion that the rule is not sourced from Augustine himself.
