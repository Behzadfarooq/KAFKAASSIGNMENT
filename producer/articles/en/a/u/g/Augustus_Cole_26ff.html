Augustus "Cole Train" Cole is a fictional character in Epic Games' third-person shooter, Gears of War. He is one of the survivors of Alpha Squad, along with Baird.

[edit] Background

Spoiler warning: Plot and/or ending details follow.

Known by players for energetic and aggressive attitude, Cole is one of Gears of Wars main characters. Marcus Fenix encounters him during Act 1 of the game, while searching for the surviving members of Alpha Squad. He impresses the members of Delta Squad by managing to single-handedly defeat numerous enemies. The characters in the game recognize Cole from his previous career as a “Trashball” defensive lineman. He is also infamous for his outspokenness, characterized by his catchphrase, “Bring it on sucka! That’s my kinda shit!”.

[edit] Trivia

  • Lester Speight, also known as Mighty Rasta, voiced Cole. Speight is more famously known for his acting roles as "Terrible" Terry Tate made for Reebok. A possible nodded to Lester's past role is Cole's nickname, Cole Train, bearing similarity to Terry Tate's nick name, "Pain Train".
  • Cole is often nicknamed Gus, after his first name Augustus.
  • When wounded and waiting to be healed sometimes Cole says, "I can't die. Mama said I can't die."
  • He is very well known for his song at the end credits of the game, where many of his more comical lines are taken and put into a rap compilation. Jokes have been made in webcomics such as in VG Cats.[1] The character's nickname has also been the end of jokes due to the similarities with the famed Jazz saxophonist Coltrane.
  • Cole shares a few similarities with the antagonist of the X-Files episode Sleepless, which aired during the shows second season, including their name and the fact that both characters are muscular African American men. Additionally, the X-Files's Cole is a former U.S. Marine.
