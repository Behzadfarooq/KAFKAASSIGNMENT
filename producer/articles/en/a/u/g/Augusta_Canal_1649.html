Augusta Canal

The Augusta Canal was devised to harness the power of the fall line of the Savannah River for mills and to provide drinking water for Augusta, Georgia.

The brain-child of lawyer Henry Cumming, it was surveyed and designed by J. Edgar Thomson who was over-seeing the completion of the Georgia Railroad. It was chartered in 1845 and completed in 1847. The canal is part of the Historic Augusta Canal and Industrial District, named a National Historic Landmark in 1977.
