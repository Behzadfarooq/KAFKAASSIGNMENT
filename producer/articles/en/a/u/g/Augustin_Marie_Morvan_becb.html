Augustin Marie Morvan

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Augustin Marie Morvan (1819 - 1897) was a French physician, politician, and writer. He is best-known for treating the first recorded case of the eponymous Morvan's syndrome, a rare neurological disorder in which the patient suffers from acute insomnia. Morvan served as a deputy to the French National Assembly that inaugurated the Third Republic in 1871.
