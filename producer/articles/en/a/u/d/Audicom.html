Audicom is the name of the first PC audio system dedicated to broadcasting. Designed in the 80's by Solidyne included a compression audio card and the software for running it. Originally replaced the box / cassette systems, but now they implement all the music including a unique feature based on AI to select the music. Along the years several companies imitated the concept developed by Solidyne (Dinesys, Dinesat, Dinasty, Radio 5, Dalet, etc.), the current version of Audicom is 7.5.


History of versions:
Audicom (mid 80's): first version, included a mono audio card: ADX922

Audicom II (aprox. 1988): most popular version for DOS systems, exported to more than 30 countries.

Audicom III & 4: not released, developed internally. Audicom 4 was the first version intended to be a Windows version (for Windows 16 bits), but due to most problems in Win 16 plataform was cancelled in favor of a Win32 system (Audicom 5).

Audicom 5 (dec-1997): first Windows system. There was two versions (both Win32 applications):ECAM and multimedia. The multimedia version included the option of an 'audio co-processor card' (a compressor-expanser for greater dynamic range with conventional sound cards). Audio mixing on was done on hardware, multimedia version required 2 cards for mixing and 3 cards for mix and cue.

Audicom 6 (oct-2000): Only version multimedia. New play engine based on DirectX, allowed mix on the same audio card. First broadcasting audio system to support .MP3. Allowed crossfade of .MP3's. Was partnered with a PCI audio card: SX46.

Audicom 7 (08-2002): Multiuser support, network ready, included autoEdit (automatic editing of MP3's files without recompressing), fully .MP3's mixing. Was partnered with an advanced PCI audio card: DSP48.
