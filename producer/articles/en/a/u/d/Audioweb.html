Audioweb

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Audioweb were a rock/reggae/electronica band based in Manchester, England. They formed in 1991, originally as The Sugar Merchants.

[edit] Personnel

The band members were (right to left):

  • Martin "Sugar" Merchant (vocals)
  • Sean McCann (bass)
  • Robin File (guitar)
  • Rob "Maxi" Maxfield (drums)

[edit] Career

The members sung or 'toasted' vocals on their music. They scored two Top 40 hits in the UK singles chart - "Policeman Skank...(The Story Of My Life)", and a cover version of The Clash's "Bankrobber". Famous admirers of the group were U2 and Ian Brown; indeed, Audioweb supported U2 at Wembley Stadium in August 1997.

The band split up in 1999. Bassist McCann now plays with Badly Drawn Boy, whilst Maxi has drummed for Ian Brown since 2001.
