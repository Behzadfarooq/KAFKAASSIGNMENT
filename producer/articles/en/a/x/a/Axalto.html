Axalto was a smart card manufacturer with over 4,500 employees in 60 countries, one of the world's leading providers of microprocessor cards (Gartner, 2005) and also a major supplier of point of sale terminals.

In June 2006 Axalto merged with rival Gemplus to form Gemalto.

Axalto business covered the telecommunications, public telephony, finance, retail, transport, entertainment, healthcare, personal identification, information technology and public sector markets. The company recorded sales of over $992 million in 2005 and was fully-listed on Euronext, the pan-European market.

[edit] History

  • Formerly, Axalto is the Smart Card and Terminal Department of Schlumberger.
  • After Schlumberger purchased Sema, the Smart Card and Terminal Department becomes a part of SchlumbergerSema.
  • And then after Schlumberger sold Sema to Atos Origin, the Smart Card and Terminal Department becomes Axalto.
  • On 2004, Axalto IPO.
  • On December 7, 2005 it announced its merger plan with main competitor Gemplus, to create Gemalto.
  • On May 19, 2006 the European Commission approves the merger between Axalto and Gemplus
  • On June 02, 2006 the new company, Gemalto, is officially created
