[edit] Methacholine moved to Metacholine

I'm wondering about the basis for changing the article title Methacholine to Metacholine. Methacholine is the spelling used in both of the external resources linked in the article, and a quick Google test (I am mindful of the limitations of Google tests), shows the spelling "methacholine" to be about 20 times more common. PubMed concurs, showing the spelling "methacholine" to occur in 32 times as many entries. The MeSH controlled vocabulary spelling is "methacholine". What are the reasons for preferring the spelling "metacholine"? --Tabor 18:50, 7 October 2006 (UTC)
