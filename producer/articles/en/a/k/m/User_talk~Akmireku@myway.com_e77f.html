[edit] Protection templates

Please don't add page protection templates – {{semi-protected}} or {{protected}} – to articles, unless the articles have actually been protected from editing. (Only an admin can protect a page.) If you would like to request that a page be protected, please visit Wikipedia:Requests for page protection (shortcut link WP:RFPP).

As an aside, it's also not appropriate to add speedy deletion notices to other users' pages [1]. You may be blocked from editing if you continue. TenOfAllTrades(talk) 13:33, 24 October 2006 (UTC)

Once again, please don't add protection notices to articles. If you'd like to draft an article in a sheltered place before adding it to the main encyclopedia, create a subpage or sandbox in your user space. TenOfAllTrades(talk) 14:54, 24 October 2006 (UTC)

Dear Akmireku@myway.com,

Welcome to Wikipedia!

Unfortunately, using your e-mail address as your username is not a good idea. Wikipedia content is extensively copied and the site itself is one of the most visited sites in the world. Any edit you make on Wikipedia will have your username attached to it, and using your email address will make you a tempting target for spammers. We recommend that you change your username at Wikipedia:Changing username in order to prevent abuse.

If you need any help, simply contact me on my talk page, or go to Wikipedia:Help desk. Another option is to place {{helpme}} on your own talk page, and someone will come shortly to help. [edit] Wikipedia:Requests for adminship

Hello and thank you for your recent edit, above. Before you decide to reapply for the position of administrator on the English Wikipedia, please read Wikipedia:Guide to requests for adminship, Wikipedia:Administrators' how-to guide and Wikipedia:Administrators' reading list. You will find that the acceptance criteria for the position of admin are quite high and the editors who contribute ask very rigorous questions. They also do not look kindly on vandals or those who edit in ignorance of established processes. Please take this message as a friendly reminder to consider your actions before editing in future. The above edit can be construed as vandalism, which can lead to you being blocked from editing for a period of time. Regards, (aeropagitica) 19:24, 24 October 2006 (UTC)
