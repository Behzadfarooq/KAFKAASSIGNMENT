Andrew Kaszowski

Andrew Kaszowski is a 25-year-old native of London, Ontario, Canada. He is a second-generation Canadian of Polish ancestry.

A professional communicator, Andrew specializes in the synergy of communication and design. He owns and operates Impression One, a freelance business carving a niche in creative web design, graphics, media relations and writing projects.

Andrew also holds the position of Communications Consultant with the Lawson Health Research Institute, the research institute of the hospital network in London, Ontario (London Health Sciences Centre and St. Joseph's Health Care London). Andrew contributes to informing the public and internal audiences about the breakthrough work done at Lawson.

His passion for healthcare began with an internship experience in the Communications and Marketing department of Toronto's Mount Sinai Hospital. In addition to internal and external communication and branding support, Andrew served as special project coordinator of the 2005-06 annual report. He has also participated in an internship as fundraising developer for Earth Day Canada.

In May 2006, Andrew graduated from the inagural class of the University of Guelph-Humber in Toronto - a new affiliate program of the University of Guelph and Humber College. Through four years of study he earned an Honours Bachelor of Applied Arts in Media Studies and a Diploma in Public Relations. Andrew graduated with distinction and was chosen to deliver the graduating students' address representing the students in the Media Studies Program.

Prior to studying at Guelph-Humber, he spent one year studying English and Communication Studies at Wilfrid Laurier University with a minor in Business. He attended John Paul II Catholic Secondary School and Blessed Sacrament Catholic Elementary School in London.

Other prior jobs include assembly line worker building pickup truck frames at Formet Industries (a part of Magna International), sales associate positions with Roots Canada and Gap, and many years as a lifeguard/aquatic supervisor/swimming instructor with the London Y, Best Western Lamplighter Inn, and East Park.

He is an active volunteer in his community. In his first year at Guelph-Humber, Andrew helped found the university's student government and was elected president by his fellow students. He also served as an orientation volunteer and Resident Assistant. Andrew is also a member of the International Association of Business Communicators and is involved with the London, Ontario chapter, having contributed his time to creating the organization's new website that launched in September 2006. Through his involvement at the Lawson Health Research Institute, Andrew serves on the TechAlliance biotechnology student outreach initiative for the city of London and is the London-area media representative for Rick Hansen Wheels In Motion 2007.
