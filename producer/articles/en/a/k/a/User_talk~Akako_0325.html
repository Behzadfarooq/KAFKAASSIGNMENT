[edit] Some suggestions

Spell-checking: Very few people are great spellers and I know that I'm not one of those great spellers. I use the SpellBound extension in the Mozilla Firefox internet browser to check much of what I write on the Wikipedia, including (sometimes) the talk page messages that I write.

Graphics in your signature: That is generally frowned upon. Any graphic adds greater strain on the Wikipedia servers than just text. Adding a graphic to your signature is an unnecessary strain on the Wikipedia servers.

Robbinsdale School District: I stay away from school articles as much as possible. Your best bet is to find a school district article that is done well, and then try to copy it as much as possible. Because it is an encyclopedia article, you should try to highlight anything that is interesting or unique about the school district. Have there been any lawsuits, or any major awards, etc.

For example, where I live, in the Long Beach Unified School District, the school district has pioneered going back to requiring students wear school uniforms. They have also converted a couple of schools to single-sex schools, which is highly unusual for a public school district. The district also has a one-room school (at Two Harbors on Catalina Island), which is something that may be unique for an urban school district. Those are items that should be covered in the LBUSD article. You also might ask for help at the School WikiProject.

BlankVerse 08:07, 8 December 2005 (UTC)
