Fire Emblem: Goddess of Dawn (ファイアーエムブレム　暁の女神, Faiā Emuburemu: Akatsuki no Megami?) is a Japanese tactical role-playing game video game announced for the Wii on May 9, 2006 at the E3 convention [2] and developed by Intelligent Systems Co., Ltd. and published by Nintendo Co., Ltd.. It is the tenth Fire Emblem series title and the first series title for the Wii. It was officially released on February 22, 2007 in Japan[1]. The North American, European, or Australasian release dates have not been announced by an official party, Intelligent Systems, or Nintendo. What has been shown indicates that the FMV cutscenes will be rendered in the same cel-shaded manner as those in Fire Emblem: Path of Radiance on the Nintendo GameCube, and gameplay videos show that the engine from Path of Radiance is intact.

[edit] Production and media history

Official artwork for two of the game's characters were made public at E3. One is an image of an older Sothe, a character that originally appeared as a playable Thief unit in Fire Emblem: Path of Radiance. The other character, Micaiah, is a new character. She is Sothe's older sister, but not by blood. She is one of the branded, a group of people mentioned in Fire Emblem 9, who have both laguz and beorcs in their ancestry. A video of the game depicts her wielding light magic.[3]

The Japanese subtitle Akatsuki no Megami was confirmed on September 14, 2006.[4] Video footage of the game from the same event depicted Micaiah and some of the new cast during both a story sequence and a combat scene.

On January 23rd, 2007, Famitsu revealed the game will have a total of 44 chapters compared to Path of Radiance's 29, and character height and geography will play a role in gameplay. Goddess of Dawn will also feature three new weapon and magic types, determined to be Knife, Dark Magic and Strike (Laguz weapons). Famitsu later gave the game a score of 33/40 (8, 8, 8, 9).

[edit] Gameplay

A playable demo kiosk was made available at the November 2006 Nintendo World event held in Japan. Unlike most games designed specifically for the Wii, Goddess of Dawn makes use of the Virtual Console classic controller. On top of that, the game will also use the Wii Remote and GameCube controller.

[edit] Plot

Three years have passed since the Continent of Tellius was shaken by the war between Crimea and Daein. The victorious Crimea began rebuilding its lands under their Queen Elincia, orphan of the fallen King.

On the other hand, the defeated Daein is put under the rule of the Begnion Empire. However those of the old Daein Kingdom are troubled with the oppression by the imperial garrison, and live each day with despair.

In order to escape from this plight, and to save the lives of many Daein citizens, several brave warriors rise by the capital of Nevassa. Their courageous actions are set to shake the whole continent...

[edit] Groups and places

The Dawn Group is a gang of chivalrous thieves, commanded by Noyce. Sothe and Micaiah fight under this group, as they fight back Begnion oppressors at Daein.[5] They are renown for their silver-haired girl and savior, Micaiah.

After the Kingdom of Daein was defeated by Crimea in Fire Emblem: Path of Radiance, the country was put under the control of Begnion. The Dawn Group sets out to end tyranny of the Begnion garrison. When the prince of Daein reveals himself, the Dawn Group help him reclaim his throne and, in the process, Daein's independence.

Deep in the Death Desert, a huge wasteland north east of Daein, lies the sanctuary of the thought to be extinct Wolf tribe. At one point it was the Kingdom of Hatari, now it is an illusion kingdom ruled by Queen Nike.

The Kingdom of Crimea is the homeland of Queen Elincia and Ike. After hearing of Daein stabilising, the nobles are angered with Elincia for helping Daein and failing to hold onto their rule of Daein three years ago. The nobles conspire to overthrow Elincia and encouraging civilians to rebel and fight, like General Ike once did.

The Kingdom of Gallia, surrounded by mountains and vast forests, is home to the Beast tribe. It is ruled by the Lion King, Caineghis, and has friendly ties with Crimea. Gallia and Begnion go to war when a Gallian ambassador is assassinated by the Beorc.

Serenes forest lies in the very centre of Tellius. Over 20 years ago, it was the Kingdom of Serenes, but was devastated in the Serenes Massacre. The Heron prince and princess revived the forest, but left for Gallia.

The Begnion Empire is home to two opposing factions- the Apostle's group and the senate. The senate were responsible for the garrison's illegal actions at Daein. When Gallia and Begnion go to war, Apostle Sanaki wishes to make peace, but the senate overthrow her and, instead, plan to awaken the Goddess.

[edit] Voice cast

In the Japanese language FMV cutscenes, Michihiko Hagi, Takashi Nagasako, Juri Takita and Junji Majima return as Ike, the Narrator, Elincia and the Black Knight, respectively from Path of Radiance. Micaiah's Japanese seiyū (voice actor) is Natsuko Kuwatani, the Goddess of Dawn is voiced by Kumi Sakuma, Sothe is voiced by Kazutaka Ishii, Rolf is voiced by Yuu Asakawa and Mia is voiced by Akiko Hasegawa. Other seiyū including Tatsuo Suzuki, Keiichi Kuwabara, Akiyuki Tanzawa and Miho Miyagawa voiced additional roles as well.
