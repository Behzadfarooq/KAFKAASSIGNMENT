Akar Kessel is a character in R.A. Salvatore's best-selling novel The Crystal Shard.

Akar, once the apprentice of the wizard Morkai the Red, almost destroyed Ten Towns. He was left to die by Dendybar the Mottled, who had, prior to the abandonment, acted as a friend to Kessel. He had told Kessel to kill his master in return for great power in the city as the new head of the mage's guild. After being left in the Spine of the World by Dendybar, Kessel came across a shard of crystal that emitted warmth. The crystal was called Crenshinibon and it held great power. Using the power of the crystal, Kessel began to build an army of orcs, goblins, trolls, and many other beasts. He used Crenshinibon to create a solid crystal tower called Cryshal-Tirith. The great demon Errtu wanted to retrieve Crenshinibon. He was summoned by an apprentice and killed his summoner as the runes of the summon were wrong and allowed him to refuse orders. He was drawn to Crenshinibon and formed an alliance with its new owner so that he could get it after Kessel died. After much preparation, Kessel set out to attack Ten Towns. Kessel was eventually killed by an avalanche after fighting Drizzt Do'Urden and Crenshinibon was buried under the snow. Ten Towns killed the remaining goblins and orcs of Kessel's army.

Kessel's name may have been inspired by that of the fictional spice mine of the planet kessel, featured in the jedi academy trilogy and mentioned in star wars episode 4 a new hope.
