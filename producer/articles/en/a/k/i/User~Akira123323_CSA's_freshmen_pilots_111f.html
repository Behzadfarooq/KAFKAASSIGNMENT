[edit] The class

1-E is a 46-member body, united by two heads, our adviser, Mrs. Ruvic D. Fule, and our co-adviser, Ms. Edna E. Esmero. As it is, we are all music junkies, socialites, intellectuals, athletes, and religious citizens all in one. It seems that the mold of an Augustinian can really have a lasting impact on who you really are.

Also, the people of 1-E can also be very comedic.

1-E has 31 girls and 15 boys. Shockingly, out of the 15 boys stand out only four homosexuals. One really is playing and one we accuse. The pilots (including me) already know who they are.

[edit] Events of 1-E

[edit] Au revoir!

1-E will be temporarily split from the Philippines as World Heritage Day is coming! For the time being on Monday, October 24, 2005, 1-E will be temporarily part of France since we have adopted the country as our project!

On the 24th also is the Sabayang Pagbigkas competition.

Okay, we became French and competed well in the Sabayang Pagbigkas. We got first in the S.P., second in the Reader's Theater, third in the General Information Quiz, and fifth in the national costume parade.

[edit] Christmas Lakwa

As announced by Norence, the Amigas Christmas Lakwa was last Saturday, December 17, 2005. It was so much fun! Hopefully there's another one next year.

[edit] "The Cans of 1-E"

In order to get a free day, 1-E students are urged to please bring cans for the can drive being held by the CSA Faculty Department. Anyway, if we have 5,000 cans, we get a free day (I admit I'm being redundant)!

1E has now accumulated more than 5000 tin cans!
