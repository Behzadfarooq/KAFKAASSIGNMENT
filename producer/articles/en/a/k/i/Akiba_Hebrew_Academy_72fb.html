Akiba Hebrew Academy

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Akiba Hebrew Academy is a pluralistic Jewish Day School, for grades 6 to 12, located in Merion, Pennsylvania. It was founded in 1946. The current headmaster is Rabbi Phillip Field, who is retiring in June 2007.

[edit] Name Change

On Thursday, February 22, 2007 the Akiba Hebrew Academy released an email to alumni and community members informing then that "Akiba Hebrew Academy’s Board of Directors announced that it approved a $5 million dollar gift from The Barrack Foundation, headed by Leonard and Lynne Barrack. The Barrack Foundation gift includes a name change in the memory of Leonard Barrack’s brother, Jack M. Barrack." Controversy and disappointment by students and alumni has ensued as it did years ago when a similar name change was proposed and later rejected by the Board. That controversy, leading to widespread student anger and community reaction, began when the Perelman Family offered a donation of $2 million for a name change.

[edit] History

Akiba was originated by Dr. Joseph Levitsky and co-founded by Rabbi Simon Greenberg, Rabbi Elias Charry, and Dr. Leo L. Honor. The school first met at the YM & YWHA at Broad and Pine Streets in Center City Philadelphia. Akiba spent four years at the YM & YWHA before it moved to Har Zion Temple at 54th and Wynnefield Ave where it was located until 1958.

[edit] Alumni

Well known alumni include writer Mitch Albom, national journalist Jake Tapper, scientist Dan Bricklin, fiction writer David Goldstein, political columnist Samuel J Abrams, author David Wolpe, sports radio host Rob Charry, actor Gideon Glick and political scientist Barnett Rubin

[edit] Athletics

Akiba men's sports teams (The Cougars) participate in the Tri-County Independent School League (TCISL). Girl's teams are members of TCISL as well as the Penn-Jersey League.

In the season, the Lady Cougars basketball team captured titles in both TCISL and Penn-Jersey. The men's basketball team last won a title in, captained by Nati Passow and Marshal Leasak and coached by Barnett Kamen and Martino Johnson.Martino Johnson has now been coach of the Cougars for 10 years and has no plans of resigning any time soon. Both the male and female basketball teams have produced a slew of prospects who have continued their careers on the college level.

The 2006 Akiba men's soccer team, led by Jordan Cooper, David Barnett, Boaz Leberman, and goalkeeper Sam Tennenbaum, capped off a successful season by winning the TCISL title for the first time since 1991.

Akiba fielded its first varsity baseball team in 1994. The team was coached in 1994 by Greg Marks and lead by pitcher Jeffrey Schildhorn, first-baseman Gabriel Moreen, and catcher Morris Levin. Levin was the first baserunner in school history. The team was no-hit in its first game by Westmont Christian pitcher Jared Lenko. Ari Fuchs recorded the team's first homerun.
