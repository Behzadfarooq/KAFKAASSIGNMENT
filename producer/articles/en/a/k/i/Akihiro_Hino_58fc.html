Akihiro Hino (日野 晃博, Hino Akihiro?) (born July 20, 1968) is President and CEO of Japanese video game developer Level-5.

[edit] Biography

Prior to founding Level-5 in October 1998, Hino began his career in the video game industry in the early 90's at the now defunct Japanese developer Riverhillsoft, specifically on the Overblood series for the original PlayStation. Hino acted as the lead programmer on the first Overblood, and was subsequently promoted to lead designer and director for the sequel, Overblood 2.

Since the creation of Level-5, Hino has designed, planned and produced all five of the studio's released titles, Dark Cloud, Dark Chronicle and Rogue Galaxy for the PlayStation 2, Jeanne d'Arc for the PlayStation Portable, and Professor Layton and the Mysterious Village for the Nintendo DS. He also directed Square Enix's Dragon Quest VIII: Journey of the Cursed King for the PlayStation 2.

Currently Hino is once again assuming designing and producing duties for Level-5's three upcoming titles, Shirokishi Monogatari for the PlayStation 3, Professor Layton and Pandora's Box for the Nintendo DS, and the third as-yet untitled Professor Layton installment, also for the Nintendo DS. He is also producing the upcoming Dragon Quest IX: Hoshizora no Mamoribito, also for the DS, which is his studio's second collaboration with RPG powerhouse Square Enix.
