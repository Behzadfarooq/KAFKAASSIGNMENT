Akim Aliu (born April 24, 1989 in Okene, Nigeria) is an amateur ice hockey player.

Aliu currently plays for the Sudbury Wolves of the Ontario Hockey League. Aliu started his OHL career as a 2005 first-round pick of the Windsor Spitfires. Once in Windsor, he met feisty winger Steve Downie, the team's first-round pick in 2003. Downie had just been drafted to the Philadelphia Flyers, a team known for players being a bit on the rough-and-tumble side, including former Flyers general manager Bobby Clarke. Aliu and Downie had much tension between them from day one. Downie cross-checked Aliu at practice, and they even fought one another. However, it did not end there. Aliu and Downie got into another fight at practice, and this one was caught on tape. [1] He was traded to the Sudbury Wolves for Keaton Turkiewicz and draft choice Jordan Cheechoo after the fight.

Aliu has played better in Sudbury, while Downie has led the Canadian Junior Hockey team to back-to-back gold medals.

Aliu is still predicted to go in the late first round of the 2007 NHL Entry Draft.

Due to reported attitude problems, the Wolves suspended Aliu indefinitely with several games still remaining in the 2006-07 season. According to a report in the Sudbury Star March 20th, after speaking to his agent the coach agreed for Akim Aliu to join the team in practice with the understanding that he would be a good "teammate". Aliu is expected to be a top NHL prospect for the 2007 draft. Aliu has played the first game of the playoff.
