Aki Riihilahti

Aki Pasinpoika Riihilahti (born September 9, 1976 in Helsinki) is a Finnish footballer who currently plays for 1. FC Kaiserslautern in the German 2. Bundesliga. He is known as a hard working, defensive midfielder.

Riihilahti started his career at HJK Helsinki where he made his Veikkausliiga debut in 1995. He won the Finnish championship once and the Finnish Cup twice with HJK, and also gained UEFA Champions League experience in the 1998-99 season. Riihilahti then moved to Norway's Vålerenga I.F. for the 1999 season. After two seasons in Norway he joined England's Crystal Palace in 2001, and eventually became a fans' favourite at the club. In Palace's 2004-05 Premiership season he showed he has what it takes to play in one of Europe's top leagues. However, his next season, back in the Championship, following Palace's relegation, was not so successful because injuries limited his chances. After his contract with Palace expired in the summer of 2006, he was picked up by Kaiserslautern on a one-year deal.

Riihilahti made his debut for the Finnish national team on February 5, 1998 against Cyprus. He has been a regular for Finland for the most of 2000s and is set to continue to do so in the near future.

Riihilahti has also become something of a cult figure because of columns he writes for newspapers, such as The Times and the Finnish Iltalehti. He also writes columns for his website. He is famed for being popular at junior school for his canny ability to stand on his head for over two minutes.
