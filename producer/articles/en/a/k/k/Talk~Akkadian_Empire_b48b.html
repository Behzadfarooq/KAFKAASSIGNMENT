Why is this titled "Semitic Empire of Sargon of Akkad"? Did Sargon of Akkad have a different Empire besides the Semitic one? Was there a Semitic Empire of someone else? Did the empire end with the death of Sargon? How could the title of an empire refer to only one person? -- Zoe

Excellent questions all. Today people often use the word "semitic" as a racial or ethnic term (because anti-semitism is a form of racism), but in this context, "semitic" is solely a linguistic category -- what it means is that the language "Akkadian" is similar to Hebrew, Arabic,and other similar "semitic" languages. Just as many people who speak English identify themselves as belonging to different races and ethnic groups, so may Akkdian speakers have identified themselves differently. Sargon was one of many rulers of an empire whose written texts are in Akkadian. That is the most sense I can make of this title. Slrubenstein
