[edit] WP:CP

Hi, you've reported copyright infringements to WP:CP in the last week, a new measure was recently passed to allow the speedy deltion of new pages that are cut and paste copyvios. Please follow these instructions if you come across this type of copyvio. Thanks. --nixie 00:14, 6 October 2005 (UTC)

Blatant copyright infringements may now be "speedied"

If an article and all its revisions are unquestionably copied from the website of a commercial content provider and there is no assertion of permission, ownership or fair use and none seems likely, and the article is less than 48 hours old, it may be speedily deleted. See CSD A8 for full conditions.

After notifying the uploading editor by using wording similar to:

{{nothanks-sd|pg=page name|url=url of source}} -- ~~~~

Blank the page and replace the text with

{{db-copyvio|url=url of source}}

to the article in question, leaving the content visible. An administrator will examine the article and decide whether to speedily delete it or not.

[edit] Images

I understand your concern with copyrighted images, however, I don't believe it's correct to remove them from a page before they are deleted if they are relevant to the page. Deletion of {{no source}} images are up to admin discretion and part of that is if pages use the image. So please, leave the image on the pages even when they are up to be deleted... whoever deletes the images should remoev it from whatever page it's on. There is a templat (I forget the title) that you can add which will show a note that the image doesn't have proper copyright status and it would be perfectly legitimate to add that to pages, but not to remove images. [edit] Peruvian Music

Hi there, I started an article about the premier composer of Peruvian Vals music Felipe Pinglo Alva, I had a hard time coming up with anything else. If you have any input, I'd love to see it! Keep up the good work! Pozole 14:39, 29 November 2005 (UTC)

Hello, thanks for the encouragement! With respect to what to call the Vals, there are two names it enjoys from what I know. Inside Peru, it is known as vals criollo and by peruvian expats, it's referred to as vals peruano. I think the proper thing to do is to call it Peruvian Vals to differentiate it from Argentine Vals Criollo, which is a known genre in its own right and perhaps explain that in Peru it's simply known as Vals Criollo. By the way, here are a couple sites that are excellent sources of information, one of them is a pretty exhaustive listing of Musica Criolla.

http://www.boletindenewyork.com/musicacriollacomposiciones.htm http://www.listamusicacriolla.com/Primera.html

Pozole 05:10, 30 November 2005 (UTC)
