I don't understand the situation at Surrealism, and trying to figure it out is making my head hurt. It's clear that User:Classicjupiter2 has a bunch of socks. It's also clear that there's been conflict on Talk:Surrealism since Summer 2004 about Wigdor: see Talk:Surrealism/Archive_02#Keith_Wigdor_and_Daniel_C._Boyer, for instance.

For more evidence see [1], [2], [3], [4].

The dispute spreads to a bunch of Surrealism-related articles. One of the lamer spectacles in this is the long-running revert war at Chicago Surrealist Group--see the page history; the pretext for the reverts eventually becomes whether a photograph can be produced that shows members of the group meeting Andre Breton.

As far as I can tell, there's a real-life dispute between different artists/groups about who's really a Surrealist, and the dispute shows up in a range of WP articles as edit wars over content & the inclusion of external links, e.g. [5] [6]. As Classicjupiter2 puts in in this post, "there has been a three year plus edit war in regards to any mention of the 'Chicago Surrealist Group'."

Some of what's going on is promotion of the artist Keith Wigdor, probably self-promotion, by a number of accounts, including:

  • -keith-wigdor- (talk • contribs • page moves • block user • block log)
  • 24.168.67.238 (talk • contribs • page moves • block user • block log)
  • 24.168.92.117 (talk • contribs • page moves • block user • block log)
  • and other IPs in the 24.168 range.

Note also Keith-Wigdor (talk • contribs • page moves • block user • block log) and Nancy-Wigdor (talk • contribs • page moves • block user • block log), which were spoof acounts created to attack Wigdor, now blocked, and Wikipedia:Requests for comment/24.168.92.117. The activity from those IPs tails off in March 2005. Classicjupiter2's account was created on 4 March 2005, shortly after Keith Wigdor was nominated for deletion on 2 March (see Wikipedia:Articles for deletion/Keith Wigdor). That article is deleted, so its history is inaccessible, but judging from comments on other pages, there seems to have been a lot of edit warring there by 24.168.*.* and other users. Classicjupiter2 also edited as 24.168.67.238, as this diff shows. A bunch of people asked him about his previous history as an IP editor on his talk page: [7], which he had previously acknowledged in post on User:Alai's talk page ([8]) asking for assistance in creating a VfD on

CJ2 initiated a bunch of AfD/VfDs--Wikipedia:Articles for deletion/Portland Surrealist Group, on 4 March 2005; Wikipedia:Articles for deletion/Surrealist Subversions, on 6 March; Wikipedia:Articles for deletion/Craven Destiny on 8 March; Wikipedia:Articles_for_deletion/Brandon_Freels on 9 March; Wikipedia:Articles for deletion/Chicago Surrealist Group on 10 March. Most of these articles were deleted for non-notability, but the AfDs seem to have been started as "revenge" for the deletion of Keith Wigdor. If this data has any relevance, it's to show that CJ2 has been involved in a long-running dispute on Surrealism articles. I see plenty of evidence of contentious editing and talk page comments that could be considered uncivil. But I don't think we can or should ban users just for being a pain.

However. Since fall 2006, if not earlier, CJ2 has been using multiple accounts. Entire sections of Talk:Surrealism are "dialogues" between CJ2 and his socks, e.g. Talk:Surrealism#History of Surrealism, where he and two confirmed socks, User:Surreal-one and User:12.196.6.162 all talk to each other. After the article goes into mediation the problem gets worse. Near the bottom of the page, where one of CJ2's socks makes personal attacks on the mediator, who then closes the mediation until the sock problem can be addressed. Go back to 29 July 2006 or so, and there's a forest of SPA's contributing to the talk page, including Jacques Stenzack, Classic8uranus, Sloppy hairpiece, and Classicsaturn7. These look like puppets of someone, but maybe not CJ2: he accuses them here of being sockpuppets of somebody else. I don't know what's going on, but Talk:Surrealism/Archive 07 is a sockpuppet playground.
