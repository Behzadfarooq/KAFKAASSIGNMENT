AfanFM (known for a short time as Afan107) is a community radio station broadcasting to the Welsh borough of Neath Port Talbot. It was granted a five year licence to broadcast in 2005 by radio regulator Ofcom.

It was set up by then-18-year-old "youth champion" Craig Williams who was disgruntled with the lack of adequate training opportunities. Craig became one of the youngest operators of a Restricted Service Licence when AfanFM began its first on air trial in October 2003 for 3 days - broadcasting from the 1214ft Foel Fynyddau transmission site. The station went on to broadcast a further four RSLs.

AfanFM was awarded its permanent broadcast licence on April 20th 2005 - the first non pilot applicant in the UK to be awarded by Ofcom. It is one of six Community Radio stations in Wales. When the station eventually launches, it will play a predominantly alternative mix of music from artists like Keane, Coldplay, Razorlight, Muse, Feeder, Manic Street Preachers etc. It'll also play current and classic dance & chart hits from the past decade.

When the station launches, AfanFM will be broadcast from two FM transmission sites, one each in the main townships of Neath and Port Talbot, both of which will operate on separate FM frequencies, which are currently in the clearance process undertaken by government regulator Ofcom - the main reason for the station's delay in launching. In October 2006, Ofcom confirmed the first frequency allocation, for its Port Talbot facility, to be 107.9FM.

Work is also expected to begin on its new regional "state of the art" broadcast complex at the AquaDome Leisure Complex on Aberavon Beachfront (Port Talbot) in the coming months. The new complex will house two studios, a production studio and offices.

AfanFM is backed and funded by a range of partners which include Neath Port Talbot County Borough Council, Welsh Assembly Government, South Wales Police and New Sandfields Aberavon.

Current Station Manager Chris Griffiths took over from Afan FM's autocratic founder Craig Williams who stepped back to assume the position as Chairman after the station's final RSL in August 2006 and went on to work for Town and Country Broadcasting, who operate a group of radio stations across South Wales.

In August 2006, the station also teamed up with the organisers of the Pontardawe Festival to launch "Festival FM" - a station dedicated to the Pontardawe area as well as providing coverage of the 2006 Festival.


Former AfanFM alumni include:

Ben Evans (now at GCap Media, London), JJ (96.4FM The Wave), Chris "Bubble" Reynolds (96.4FM The Wave), Tara Jones (GCap Media), Adam Court (Bridge FM).
