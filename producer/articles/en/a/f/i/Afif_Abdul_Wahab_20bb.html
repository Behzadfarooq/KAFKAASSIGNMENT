Afif Abdul Wahab

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Dr. Afif Abdul Wahab Sr. (d. 2003) was born and raised in El Mina, Lebanon. After receiving his Medical Doctorate from the American University of Beirut, he opened up a hospital in his hometown of El-Mina for a short period before moving to Saudi Arabia in 1940. Dr. Abdul Wahab opened up the first Arab-owned hospital in Jeddah, The Lebanese Hospital in Jeddah, and was one of the first medical doctors to live and work in Saudi Arabia. During the Petroleum boom in the Gulf, Dr. Abdul Wahab received the Saudi Passport from King Faisal Bin Abdul Aziz, who was the man behind the Arab Oil Embargo, that led to a world economic crisis in 1973. Dr. Abdul Wahab entered into the industrial world by opening up the first Car Exhaust pipe factory in the KSA, and moved from a one time doctor to a leading entrepreneur.

He retired and moved to London in 1981, before moving back to Lebanon at the end of the Lebanese Civil War in 1992. He participated in the country's resurrection by getting involved in charity work as a philanthropist, establishing several scholarships and funds at local universities and foundations.

Dr. Abdul Wahab received the National Cedars Medal from Lebanese president Suleiman Frangieh, which is the highest honor given to a Lebanese Citizen. He died in January 2003, aged 87.

The Abdul Wahab Family is considered one of the largest families in the City of El-Mina, with a 600 year old history. Doctors, Scientist, Business Men and Politicians have been all members of this family that has stretched to Beirut, Munich, London, New York and Paris. Noted members of this family include navy pioneers Khayreddine Abdul Wahab and Adel Abdul Wahab who founded the Khayreddine and Adel Abdul Wahab Corporation, Abdul Ghani Abdul Wahab(city mayor),Dr. Rida Abdul Wahab(doctor, businessman), Dr. Amyn Abdul Wahab(surgeon), Mou'nes Abdul Wahab(author),Haitham Abdul Wahab, Hayan Abdul Wahab, and Afif Abdul Wahab Jr.
