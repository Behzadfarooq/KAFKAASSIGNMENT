[edit] Strictly Platonic

Some of the people, places or things you have written about in the article Strictly Platonic may not be sufficiently well-known to merit articles of their own. The Wikipedia community welcomes newcomers, and encourages them to become Wikipedians. On Wikipedia, all users are entitled to a user page in which they can describe themselves, and this article's content may be incorporated into that page. However, to merit inclusion in the encyclopedia proper, a subject must be notable. We encourage you to write or improve articles on notable subjects. Friday (talk) 18:24, 28 October 2006 (UTC)

I've seen this band play a bunch of times and they're making headlines here in New York. My Chemical Romance had them open during a NY show. This band is up and coming and me being I fan...want to support them.

I moved this to your user page for now. It's likely to be deleted if left in article space. We'd need sources and some indication that they meet WP:MUSIC guideline for inclusion in order to have an article about them. Friday (talk) 18:49, 28 October 2006 (UTC)

Okay, fair enough. Thank you.

So, if this band meets the any of the things listed on WP:MUSIC they can have their own page? How would I let you know?

It's not quite that simple. If you really want, we can restore the article and put it through what's called Wikipedia:Articles for deletion. However if this happens a lot of people may come along and say things like "vanity page" or "non-notable band". This one is IMO a somewhat grey area. It's not the kind of garage band we usually get vanity articles about, but I see no indication of significant media coverage either. The other thing you should know is that if we do have an article about this band, it must be neutral, verifiable, and not contain original research. In other words, reports of fans based on seeing a show cannot be used. To get the kind of coverage you might actually want, you should use the band's own homepage. So most of the content in the current page would have to go. Things like "will bring New York punk to another level" are right out. In short, this is a fairly bland place to write about bands. It is, after all, an encyclopedia, not a music review magazine. I'll help you out however I can if you wish, but I'm not really sure what to advise you, here. Friday (talk) 19:10, 28 October 2006 (UTC)

I would love for you to help me in anyway. I just don't want to have this deleted. Any revisions you can do to it would be greatly appriciated.
