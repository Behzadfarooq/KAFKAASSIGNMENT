A few things are beyond me here:

  • "Chinese" is not a language - can someone specify? Down on the list of Notable Alumni is a person who translated "Lord of the Rings", don't skip him.
  • Is there a way to present (very NPOV) information about the history of Taiwan as a country, to explain why all these different governments ran this school and why all these students were from different countries?
  • Comments and additional information need translation to English, especially toward the bottom
  • The "Uncle Omelet" link is 404 - I tried changing ".org" to ".edu" but that didn't help. Can someone who can read the websites find and restore it?
  • In U.S. common-use, a "principal" is the in-charge of a school for kids, but a "president" is the in-charge of a university (and usually a "director" is in-charge for a research institution). There are a lot of Notable Alumni who were listed as "Principal" (which literally means "first", so okay for a start) of a university - I changed them all to "President" however they may not hold/have held that actual job. Fact-checking this could go along with getting some citations for this article. ~ Otterpops 19:13, 1 April 2007 (UTC)
