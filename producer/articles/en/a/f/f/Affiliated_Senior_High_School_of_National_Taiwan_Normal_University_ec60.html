The Affiliated Senior High School of National Taiwan Normal University (Traditional Chinese:國立臺灣師範大學附屬高級中學,附中,師大附中) (HSNU), is considered a top school among Taiwan's public high schools. The campus is located in the Da-an District in Taipei, Taiwan.

[edit] History

HSNU was founded as "Taipei State Third High School" in 1937 under Japanese rule. Until the end of World War II 90% of the student body was Japanese.

On December 5, 1945, the government of the Republic of China changed the school's name to "Taiwan Province Third High School" and then again on January 1st, 1946, to "Taiwan Province Taipei He-ping High School". Under the name "He-ping High School", the school's purpose was to educate Japanese children who did not return to Japan until April, 1947 - at the time, most of the teachers of He-ping High Schools were Japanese. Many people called "Chien-Kuo High School" (first), "Cheng Kung Senior High School" (second) and "He-ping High School" (third) the "The Three Province High Schools", while the name "The Five Province High Schools" grouped these with "Taipei First Girls High School" and "Zhong-shan Girls High School".

In 1947, China's political situation changed again. Government administrators' families who had lived in China came to Taiwan, and the government allowed their children to study at He-ping High School. At that time, Taiwan Province Teachers' College (now National Taiwan Normal University) was preparing to establish an experimental high school as a teachers' training ground so the college applied for the establishment of a new school. On August 1, 1947, the He-ping High School's became "The Affiliated Senior High School of Taiwan Province Teachers' College". There was also a junior high school section. In October 1949, the school took in 310 students from the National Revolution Army's Childrens' School students, the largest number taken by a school in that area.

In 1955, the name of the governing teachers' college was changed to "Normal University", in 1961 the junior high school section closed, and in 1967, the teachers' college changed name again, from "Province Municipal" to "National"; the high school's name became "The Affiliated High School of National Taiwan Normal University". However, until an associate professor of the education department, Huang Zhen-Qiu, became HSNU's principal, the high school and the university were not closely tied. In 1980, when the junior high school reopened, the school's name finally became "The Affiliated High School of National Taiwan Normal University".

[edit] School Features

HSNU is notable among high schools in the area for encouraging excellence in extra-curricular areas.

[edit] Uniform

Under Japanese rule boys' high school uniforms in Taiwan were similar to military uniforms, khaki from top to bottom. In 1983, HSNU made its school uniform a light blue button-down shirt with dark blue trousers, the first school not to wear khaki. The girls' uniform was designed later, when girls started to enroll in the school's special music class: a white button-down shirt and a light blue suit skirt. The girls' uniform comes with a suit jacket but many students choose to wear the more comfortable black athletic jacket.

[edit] Experimental Classes

In addition to normal classes, there are many kinds of experimental classes in HSNU. For example, between 1950 and 1961, HSNU tried a system with a four-year junior high school with two years of senior high school. The experimental science class was established in 1978 (Class 420 and Class 430 were created in the first year).

[edit] School Anthem

HSNU's school anthem was composed during principal Huang's era by Shi Wei-Liang (史惟亮, Music Teacher). The text, in Chinese, was written by Guo Tang (郭橖, History Teacher) and Xiao Hui-Kai (蕭輝楷, Compilation Leader).

Chinese Version:

附中附中我們的搖籃，漫天烽火創建在台灣。
玉山給我們靈秀雄奇，東海使我們擴大開展。
我們來自四方，融會了各地的優點。
我們親愛精誠，師生結成了一片。
砥礪學行，鍛鍊體魄，我們是新中國的中堅。
看我們附中培育的英才，肩負起時代的重擔。
附中青年，絕不怕艱難。
復興中華，相期在明天。
把附中精神，照耀祖國的錦繡河山。

Translation:

HSNU, HSNU, our cradle,
Established in Taiwan in boundless beacon war fire.
Mount Jade gives us cleverness and grandeur,
The East China Sea makes us broad-minded.
We come from far and near and integrate all our merits together.
We are loving, earnest and sincere so that teachers and students live together harmoniously.
We encourage each other to form moral character and strong bodies,
We are the backbone of the new age of China.
Elite nurtured by HSNU can take up the heavy responsibility of a new generation,
We are never afraid of difficulties.
Let's cooperate to make efforts for the rebirth of China,
Let's enlighten the land of enchanting beauty, our fatherland, with the spirit of HSNU.
