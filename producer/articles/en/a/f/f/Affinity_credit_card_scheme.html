Affinity credit card scheme

Cornell University affinity credit card

An affinity credit card scheme is a group scheme where a designated organisation is paid a small percentage of all transactions on the credit card. This payment is funded by interchange, the percentage of each transaction that the card issuer receives.

University alumni associations, professional associations, and sports teams are all popular affinity credit card partners. One can even get credit cards proclaiming one's religion or national origin.

MBNA was the pioneer of affinity credit card schemes, and its parent Bank of America is the largest operator of them.
