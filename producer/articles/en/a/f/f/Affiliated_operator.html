Affiliated operator

In the context of von Neumann algebras, a closed and densely defined operator A is said to be affiliated with a given von Neumann algebra M if A commutes, in a suitable sense, with any operator in the commutant of M.

[edit] Details

Let H denote the underlying Hilbert space, L(H) bounded operators on H, and M ⊂ L(H) be a von Neumann algebra. A closed A with dense domain Dom(A) is affiliated with M if for all m` in the commutant M` of M, m`A ⊂ Am`, i.e.

for all h ∈ Dom(A), m`h lies in Dom(A) and (m`A)( h) = (Am`)(h).

The notation A η M is often used to denote that A is affiliated with M.

When A is bounded, the von Neumann double commutant theorem states that the notions of "being affiliated with" and "is an element of" coincide. That this is a natural extension to unbounded operators can be seen by the following:

Proposition. For a closed closed A with dense domain Dom(A), the following are equivalent:

  1. A η M.
  2. A* η M.
  3. If A = UH is the polar decomposition of A, then U, H η M.

Proof:

To see that 1. and 2. are equivalent, take m` in the commutant M` and h ∈ Dom(A*). For all g ∈ Dom(A), we have <Ag, m`h> = <(m`*)Ag, h> = <Am`* g, h> = <m`*g, A*h> = <g, m`A*h>. So m`h ∈ Dom(A*) and A*m`h = m`A*h.
Now if A η M, it follows from the uniqueness of polar decomposition that 3. holds. The converse can be shown by direct caculation. This proves the proposition.

As a special case, a (not necessarily bounded) projection P is affiliated with M if and only if both its range Ran(P) and the orthogonal complement Ran(P)⊥ are invariant under each element of M`. Correspondingly, a (not necessarily closed) subspace M of H is an affiliated subspace of M if PM η M, where PM is the projection onto M.

For a closed and densely defined A, its range projection, denoted by rp(A), is the projection onto the closure of its range. From the relation AA* = (AA*)½(AA*)½, we have that rp(A) is the projection onto the range of G = (AA*)½ where A* = VG is the polar decomposition of A*. When A η M, rp(A) ∈ M, since rp(A) is the strong operator limit: rp(A) = s-lim 1[0, n](G). Also, rp(A) is equivalent to rp(A*) in the sense of Murray-von Neumann: in the polar decomposition A* = VG, the initial space of the partial isometry V is the closure of Ran(G) = Ran(A) and its final space is the closure of Ran(A*).

In the case where M is a commutative von Neumann algebra of the form L∞(X, μ), where a σ-finite measure space (X, μ), then a closed and densely defined A acting on L2(X, μ), is affiliated with M if and only if A is multiplication by f for some measurable f that is finite almost everywhere. This can be shown by expressing X as a countable disjoint union of sets of finite measure X = ∪Xn then approximate the characteristic functions on Xn by elements of Dom(A).

The set of all affiliated operators forms an (associative) C-algebra if the composition of two operators is defined to be the closure of their composition as relations. This algebra is closed under the operation of taking adjoints, and forms a *-algebra with that involution, the algebra of affiliated operators.

It turns out that this algebra can equivalently be described as the Ore localisation of M with respect to all non-zero-divisors in M.[citation needed]
