[edit] Main namespace redirects to wikipedia namespace

from Wikipedia:Village pump (policy)#Main namespace redirects to wikipedia namespace:

Hi, we have the style-guide Wikipedia:Avoid self-references to keep the Wikipedia namespace separate from the encyclopedia one. However consider redirects such as AfD which redirect the user automatically to the Wikipedia namepace. Rather than a redirect should they be a link on the page using the {{selfref}} template, e.g.:

{{selfref|In Wikipedia, AfD may refer to [[Wikipedia:Articles for deletion]]}}, displayed as:
In Wikipedia, AfD may refer to Wikipedia:Articles for deletion

? Thanks/wangi 14:15, 3 January 2006 (UTC)

In that case, I'll be bold and edit AfD to test the water... Thanks/wangi 17:33, 6 January 2006 (UTC)

I'd say the redirect should be kept, atleast until the need arise for a disambiguation page. Scoo 19:32, 6 January 2006 (UTC)

  • I'm sorry to see this changed, since AfD was my preferred shortcut for Wikipedia:Articles for Deletion. It actually seems unlikely that the capitalization "AfD" is used by any of the items at AFD. If I'm among the only people using it that way, though, I'll bite the bullet in the interests of following the style guidelines... Brighterorange 21:51, 27 January 2006 (UTC)
    • What's wrong with using WP:AFD?wangi 13:06, 28 January 2006 (UTC)
      • There's nothing wrong with it other than it being twice the length. Brighterorange 14:32, 30 January 2006 (UTC)

Sorry, but the integrity of the encyclopedia wins out over the convenience of not having to type WP:. Cross-namespace redirects are disallowed primarily because they blur the line between encyclopedic content and the process that goes into making said content, and they break on mirrors. Remember, you're not just writing for Wikipedia, you're writing for everyone who uses our GFDL'd content. --Cyde Weys 22:46, 5 May 2006 (UTC)
