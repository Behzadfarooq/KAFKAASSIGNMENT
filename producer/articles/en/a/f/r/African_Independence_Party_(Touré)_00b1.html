The African Independence Party (French: Parti Africain de l’Indépendance) is a political party in Burkina Faso, led by Soumane Touré. It was formed in 1999 when Touré broke away from the original PAI.

Touré's PAI joined the government and obtained the legal recognition to the name PAI.

At the last legislative elections, 5 May 2002, the party won 3.6 % of the popular vote and 5 out of 111 seats.

Touré won 1.1 % of the vote in the presidential elections of 2005.

The party publishes L'Avant-Garde.
