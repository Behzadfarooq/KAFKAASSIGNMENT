Originally known as The Oyo's because of their last name, The African Mafia also known as A-Mob is comprised of 9 biological brothers hailing from the Bed-Stuy section of Brooklyn, New York. They supposedly come from the largest family in Brooklyn with 31 siblings and over 100 cousins. They are the nephews of the quasi-religious leader Dr. York aka Malachi Z. York. The African Mafia members are second generation African-Americans, having grandparents from Northern Africa. In 2003 one of The African Mafia's key members was released from prison after serving 11 years while another member was arrested and charged with murder.

The group decided in early 2000 to focus more on the entertainment industry and have thus far been successful in working with several of Hip-Hop's big named artist thru their mixtape ventures. They have been called the first "official family" of mixtapes. Their monthly mixtapes have featured artists like 50 Cent, The Game, Beenie Man, Heather Hunter, Lil Ceas, Madd Rapper, Maino, Dylan, Dipsets DukeDaGod and ByrdGangs NOE, among others. The African Mafia's violent CD cover images, depicting decapitations and gunshot wounds have helped the brothers to distinguish themselves from other mixtape DJ's.

In 2006 African Mafia "Boss" Mazi O. announced on 99.5FM WBAI in New York that the group is now affiliated with Jimmy "Henchmen" Rosemonds Czar Entertainment.
