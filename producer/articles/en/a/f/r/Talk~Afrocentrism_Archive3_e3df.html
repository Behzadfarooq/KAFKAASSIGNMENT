[edit] Relation of Semitic Peoples to Afrocentrism

While I am conscious of the differences between language, ethnicity, and race, I can't help but point out the interesting problems that come with the consideration of Semites in their relation to Africans. The Semitic languages, including Hebrew, Arabic, and Amharic (the official language of Ethiopia!) are classified as being "Afroasiatic," and are related to many north African languages such as Hausa, Berber languages, and even (according to most scholars) ancient Egyptian. These languages have not (yet) been connected to Africa's other main language families, Niger-Congo, Nilo-Saharan, and Khoisan. Check out this map: [1] (http://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/d/da/Afro-Asiatic.png)

Another field of study, ethnogenetics, teaches us that the African peoples are genetically much more diverse than all of the non-African peoples. In fact, if we divided humanity into, say, one hundred different groups based on a genetic family-tree, only one of the hundred would be non-Africans. Check out this picture (a partial family tree of humanity, with the non-Africans representing a minor branch) to see what I'm talking about: [2] (http://www.duke.edu/~dcb11/Posting/tree.bmp). Alright, so what does this mean? First, it confirms the "Out-of-Africa" hypothesis vividly; humanity began in Africa and remained there for millenia, evolving and differentiating into different (African) peoples before leaving Africa in relatively recent times. This is, of course, a key point for Afrocentrics to remember.

Okay, so how do Semites fit into this? The linguistic classification suggests an African origin of Semites separate and much earlier than the other non-African peoples (Europeans, Chinese, etc...). The genetic study, amazingly (and perhaps for shady reasons), omits Semites from the research! WHY??? Arabs are most notably absent, a key world group that is left out. In any case, we can be greatful that the Hausa were included. They are Afroasiatic, like the Arabs, and as can be seen on the tree, are one of the oldest groups in the world.

So, the Afroasiatic peoples, like the Hausa, are older than most of the other African groups.
