For the sake of consistency with all Nova Scotia communities, should this entry and its redirect Africville, Nova Scotia, be reversed so that the latter becomes the main article? Cheers,Plasma east 07:29, 31 Mar 2005 (UTC)

Given that Africville is a "historic" town, in that no one actually lives there anymore, and it is more of an idea than a place now, I would suggest we leave it at Africville until another Africville, needing disambiguation is added to Wikipedia. Peregrine981 12:08, Mar 31, 2005 (UTC)
Even though it is historic, it was an actual community which existed for most of its lifetime outside the boundaries of the city of Halifax, or present-day Halifax Regional Municipality, so it would have been known as "Africville, Nova Scotia" in any postal or civic addressing (if there ever was a latter), and likely in any published gazetteer on community names. Everything in HRM outside of what is today the downtown core of Halifax was at one time an unincorporated community in Halifax County ie. Rockingham, NS; Armdale, NS; Spryfield, NS; Fairview, NS; etc. The only "unofficial" neighbourhood names which would not be characterized as <place name>, Nova Scotia, or be categorized under Category:Nova Scotia communities, would be things such as "South End, Halifax", "North End, Halifax", "West End, Halifax", "Clayton Park, Halifax", "Hydrostone", etc.Plasma east 02:37, 11 Jun 2005 (UTC)
I agree, this page should be moved. -- Earl Andrew - talk 18:33, 18 Jun 2005 (UTC)
I'm going to move the article to "Africvile, Nova Scotia".Plasma east 06:15, 20 Jun 2005 (UTC)
I could be wrong on this, but I'm pretty sure there was never an official town called Africville, Nova Scotia. If anything, it was just a neighbourhood of Halfax, so should be Africville (Halifax) or something similar. Fawcett5 15:24, 22 Jun 2005 (UTC)
Whether a place was ever incorporated does not mean it should have the province following it or not. Africville was more than a neighbourhood of Halifax, it was a community way out in the Bedford Basin. Of course a neighbourhood like Jollimore (just pulling a name off a map) shouldn't have ",Nova Scotia" after it. -- Earl Andrew - talk 17:37, 22 Jun 2005 (UTC)
It only became part of Halifax after the city expanded to take in the entire peninsula back in the 50's (I think), so it was an unicorporated rural community, same as there are many across Nova Scotia and in other provinces today. Most unincorporated communities were governed by their county or district governments. If you follow the logic that only incorporated communities should be included, then the Halifax, Nova Scotia entry should be merged into the Halifax Regional Municipality, Nova Scotia entry, as Halifax as a city does not exist any more.Plasma east 2 July 2005 17:54 (UTC)
