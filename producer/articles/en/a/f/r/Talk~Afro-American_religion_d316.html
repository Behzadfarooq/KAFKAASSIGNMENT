[edit] Vodou origins

We have made what we believe are some important historical changes to the Vodou origin section of the table. The first being replacing Haiti as a primary origin of Vodou's evelopment. Haitian Vodou is a syncretize Diaspora religion developed in the New World. It is another branch and/or root religion of its mother religion of Vodou in West Africa. Secondly, the Vodou religion developed throughout many regions in Africa. There is evidence that the Vodou religion merely migrated to West Africa with its adherents from the Sudanic regions. Nonetheless, to the chagrin of other nations in West Africa, Benin (former Dahomey) continues to lay claim to being the home of the Vodou religion. Unfortunately, reinforced by western anthropologists, this claim is believed to be more for economic and political reasons than any attempt at historical accuracy. Lastly, we have found no historical evidence that the Yoruba had a hand in the development of the Vodou religion in West Africa. We are aware that the religioustolerance.org page continues to attribute the development of the Vodou religion to the Yoruba, however, this is an error that we are working to correct. One of the earliest homes of the Ewe and Fon groups was ancient Ketu and other regions in Nigeria. They are believed to have migrated to this region from the east as early as the 11th century. It was only with the expansion of the growing Oyo empire in competing for land to expand, that these Vodou groups were forced to migrate southwesterly. Any remnants of the Vodou religion that existed in the region may be a result of their earlier presence. Currently, there are what are know in Benin as Nago, meaning Yoruba worshipers of the Vodou religion in some parts of southern Benin. However, it is known by the locals that they descend from populations of either warrior families, or early immigrants to that region, especially during the infamous Dahomean wars with the Oyo empire. There, one might find a mixture of both the Orisa, Gelede, and traditional Vodou deities being honored. --MWHS 01:48, 30 December 2006 (UTC)
