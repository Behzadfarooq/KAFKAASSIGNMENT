XIN is a series of flash cartoons produced by LiFe.1 (life-point-one) Entertainment. The animation is by Steven Lim and Max Mao, with original music by Steven Lim. It is comprised of two chapters, each made up of 9 sessions, though the sessions increased in length as the series went on, to the point that the last session is more than 10 times longer than the first. The plot centers around high school students trying to overthrow their corrupt education system. Much of the action involves students fighting each other or their teachers using wuxia-like martial arts.

[edit] Setting

XIN is set in the present day, in a fictional, unnamed city and country believed to be based in part on Taiwan. Most of the events of Chapter 1 take place in Public High School #1368, while Chapter 2 takes place on the grounds of Varron Academy. The world of XIN contains many people who have superhuman strength, agility, and stamina, including nearly all of the main characters. While these abilities are attributed to qi control, no reason is given why some characters are capable of them and others are not.

An important difference from the real world is that, in the school system, extreme forms of corporal punishment, such as beatings, are sanctioned by law in place of suspension or expulsion. To apply this law to students with powerful qi many schools use a "Pillar System," wherein a heirarchy of qi-using students (often led by a "school captain") is authorized to corporally punish other students. The members of a heirarchy are shown to frequently be corrupt dictators, beating up anyone who breaks the rules they have set. Administrators generally ignore such behavior, preferring draconian order to chaos.

[edit] Characters

Main article: List of XIN characters

One of the most notable traits of XIN is that its characters are not easily classified into protaganists and antagonists. Ghai, Xin and Legend, for example, all have similar and altruistic goals, but their differing methods cause them to distrust one another and fight. All three could be considered antagonists at some point in the story.

[edit] Xin

Xin is a Freshman at Public High School (PHS) 1368, and later Varron Academy. He transfers to 1368 in Session 3, after having previously transferred to numerous other schools. He systematically dismantles the student heirarchy at a school and documents evidence of its corruption, then moves on before he is noticed (and potentially asked to head a new heirarchy). This pattern is disrupted when he realizes that PHS 1368's captain is a xi√° like himself. He later resumes it at Varron Academy, eventually defeating and shaming Dimir, who he has learned is behind the corrupt system. He is a balanced qi-fighter whose greatest weakness is his frail body; it is a rare fight in which he does not break at least one bone. He overcomes this by using "the technique of Bone Shredding," which channels his qi to instantly knit his bones.
