The Association for Promoting the Discovery of the Interior Parts of Africa, commonly known as the African Association, was a British society dedicated to the exploration of West Africa. [1] The major goals of the association was to discover the location of Timbuktu (little about the Islamic learning center, in modern-day Mali, was known by Europeans) and trace the course of the Niger River. Among those employed or otherwise associated with the organisation were Joseph Banks, Mungo Park, John Ledyard, and Johann Ludwig Burckhardt.[1]
