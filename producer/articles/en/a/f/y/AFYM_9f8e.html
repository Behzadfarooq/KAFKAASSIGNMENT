The AFYM, (the Abertawe Festival for Young Musicians) is an annual musical festival in Swansea for young musicians (5-18 years). It comprises of 6 days (at the end of January or start of February) of classes and competitions of varying age groups, with a finishing competition in the Taliesin theatre in March.

[edit] History

The Festival is the brainchild of two musicians and teachers who, frustrated by the lack of festivals in the Swansea locality, decided to start their own. And a month or two later the first AFYM was held. In that first year, 1993, the festival attracted 70 competitors, using one hall - the Taliesin Arts Centre at Swansea University - and employing one adjudicator. Since 1993, the festival has gone from strength to strength. From only one adjudicator and seventy competitors in 1993, the festival attracted 500 competitors, 6 adjudicators and over 67 classes covering three halls (Guild Hall Swansea since 2000).

[edit] Performing History

The festival offers classes for young musicians of all age groups and ability. Classes vary from ensemble competitions, to voice and solo instrumental competitions. In recent years the festival has attracted a large number of young musicians from all over South Wales and beyond. Anual and loyal competitors include Tomos Xerri (Cardiff), Oliver Feng (Swansea), Nia Thomas (Mumbles), Cameron Plumb (Mumbles).

Tomos Xerri (born in 1991) is an advanced harpist and pianist and competes in every competition available to his age group and very rarely doesn't win them. Since 2003 he has been invited to play in the AFYM Concert in the Taliesin every year and has played a vast repetoir ofpiano pieces by romantic and classical composers and in the AFYM 2005, he composed his own piece, with which he played and won called "Night and Day".

Oliver Feng is a young pianist and annually competes in competitions on the piano. He is a fan of early baroque music with which he often wins competitions.

Nia Thomas (born in 1991) is a keen pianist and harpist who has annually competed in the AFYM since the start of the century. In 2002, she won a piano duet competition and has won several silver and gold medals since, competitng on both the piano and the harp in a variety of styles from jazz to classical.

Cameron Plumb (born in 1991) is an advanced pianist and harpist who has annually competed in the AFYM since 2002. Generally playing Baroque or Romantic piano and harp music he has won several silver and gold medals. In 2006 he played in the Taliesin Concert and has completed Grade 7 on the piano. He also came second in the Urdd National Eisteddfod playing the harp in 2003.

[edit] Trivia
