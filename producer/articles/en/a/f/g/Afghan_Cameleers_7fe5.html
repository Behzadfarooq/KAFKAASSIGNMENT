Afghan Cameleers were a group of Afghan camelmen who were brought to Australia so the vast Australian outback could be explored. The Afghans were specifically chosen due to their expertise with camels, strong work ethic and their experience in working under harsh climates.

The Afghan contribution to Australia has largely been forgotten due to the popular Eurocentric historical view prevalent in Australian society. Without the Afghans, much of the development of the outback would have been very difficult, if not impossible. Whole communities, towns, mining establishments, pastoral properties and some well-known explorations in the interior have been made successful because of their contributions.

[edit] History

The first cameleers arrived in Australia in 1838.

[edit] Contribution to Australia

  • Overland Telegraph Line
  • The Ghan train route, formerly known as The Afghan Express
  • Railways
  • Transporting minerals and natural resources interstate
  • Opening the outback
  • Used as life saving services, saved lives of many Europeans
  • Established of Australias first Mosques and Muslim cemetries

[edit] Famous Cameleers

Mahomet Allum is by far Australia's most famous Afghan cameleer. He is widely known as Adelaide's Miracle Man due to his extensive free health and philanthropic services for the public and his curing of hundreds of Australians, which he claimed was a fundamental pillar of his religion.
