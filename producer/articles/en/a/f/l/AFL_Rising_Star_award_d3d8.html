AFL Rising Star award

The NAB Rising Star award is given annually to a standout young player in the Australian Football League. It was previously known as the Norwich Rising Star award and first began in 1993.

Every round, an Australian Football League rising star nomination is given to a standout young player. To be eligible for the award, a player must be under 21 on January 1 of that year, have played 10 or fewer senior games and not been suspended during the season. At the end of the year, one of the 22 nominees is the winner of award.

The past winners of the AFL Rising Star award are:

  • 1993: Nathan Buckley (Brisbane Bears)
  • 1994: Chris Scott (Brisbane Bears)
  • 1995: Nick Holland (Hawthorn Football Club)
  • 1996: Ben Cousins (West Coast)
  • 1997: Michael Wilson (Port Adelaide Football Club)
  • 1998: Byron Pickett (Kangaroos Football Club)
  • 1999: Adam Goodes (Sydney Swans)
  • 2000: Paul Hasleby (Fremantle Football Club)
  • 2001: Justin Koschitzke (St Kilda Football Club)
  • 2002: Nick Riewoldt (St Kilda Football Club)
  • 2003: Sam Mitchell (Hawthorn Football Club)
  • 2004: Jared Rivers (Melbourne Football Club)
  • 2005: Brett Deledio (Richmond Football Club)
  • 2006: Danyle Pearce (Port Adelaide Football Club)
