I'm involved in afternet so its not appropreate for me to edit the article, but I think someone else might consider adding the noteable tidbit that AfterNET's first home-grown channel services bot "X2" (x2serv.sf.net) was the first channel services to be released as opensource. Before that time, you had to be a strong software developer and code your own services or pay to run a private IRC network with services. They were heavily guarded secrets. Networks considered services a barrier to entry for competitors and wouldn't share. After Afternet released X2 under the GPL the barrier was gone and, many, many others released their services also within the next year or so. It probably increased the speed at which IRC fragmented into the thousands of little IRC networks we see today versus just efnet and undernet. --Rubin / 72.131.72.5 05:12, 21 February 2006 (UTC)
