After Every Dark Night There Is A Brighter Day
Studio album by Max King
Released 2006

After Every Dark Night There Is A Brighter Day is the title of the fifth studio album by Max King recorded in the summer of 2006. The 14 track album contains the songs "You Can Go On", "Back In The Rain" and 'Notes In Your Pocket'.

[edit] Recording Process

The album demonstrated a development in the sophistication of the recording process used by King (including the early use of the 6-track bouncing technique) as well as boasting a bigger and heavier sound compared to the stark, acoustic predecessor Mo'Rum. Although not as sharp and focussed as his rugged earlier albums, the length, range and might of After Every Dark Night... make it easily the most ambitious project of King's career, and the sound quality of the songs makes it the easiest and most commercial listen. The album's front cover displays the artwork of Antonio Ciseri's Ecce Homo and is of Christ being presented to the judging crowd during his trial.

[edit] Influences and Sound

The record was originally planned to be a double-album, and indeed the tracks are separated under the subtitles A and B, but due to a lack of material to strecth two discs, a single album was produced. King claims the album's sound is partly influenced by the heavy rootsy blues-rock records of the 1970s, sighting influences such as Led Zeppelin and The Rolling Stones. King has stated he was listening to Exile On Main Street a lot during the album's recording and notes the sweltering summer heat and isolation of the sessions gives it a distinctive sense of time and place for him.

However the musical range is ever-present as samples of blues, country, rap and techno are displayed, and each track usually boasting a small orchestra of instruments including brass, synth, Eastern strings and King's first use of a harmonica in his vocals.

Upon release King stated he didn't know where to place the record as it was such an evolution on previous recordings. However the recent selection of a 'best of' compilation has seen more tracks taken from After Every Dark Night... than any other of Max King's albums.
