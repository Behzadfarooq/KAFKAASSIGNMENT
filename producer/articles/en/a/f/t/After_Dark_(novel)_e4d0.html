After Dark (アフターダーク, Afutā Dāku?) is a novel by Japanese author Haruki Murakami. Originally published in 2004, a Russian version was published in 2005, a Dutch version in 2006. The Chinese (simplified characters) version was published in 2005 and translated by Lin Shaohua (traditional Chinese:林少華, simplified Chinese: 林少华) . It was published in French on 4 January 2007 as Le passage de la nuit by Éditions Belfond. An English translation is due to be released May 8th, 2007.

[edit] Plot summary

Spoiler warning: Plot and/or ending details follow.

Alienation is a returning motif in the works of Murakami, and the central theme in this novel set in a major Japanese city over the course of one night. Main characters include Mari, a 19-year old girl, who is spending the night reading in a Denny's. There she meets Takahashi, a trombone-playing student, who also knows Mari's sister Eri and insists that the group of them have hung out before. Meanwhile, Eri is being watched in her sleep by someone sinister. Eri also suffers from social withdrawal, a condition often referred to as hikikomori.

Mari crosses ways with a fighting champion, now working as a manager in a love hotel (who Takahashi knows and referred to Mari), a Chinese prostitute who has been raped in this same love hotel and a sadistic computer expert. The story takes place in a world between reality and dream.
