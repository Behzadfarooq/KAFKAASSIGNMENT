Acclamator-class assault ship

The 752-meter-long Acclamator-class Assault Ship/Transport in the fictional Star Wars Universe and is a relative of later Star Destroyers. The Acclamator was the first capital ship designed for use with the Grand Army of the Republic, referred sometimes simply as the "Republic assault ship." Unlike the Star Destroyer, the Acclamator was not designed as a space combat starship or even a cruiser; rather, it was designed as a heavily armored and armed troop transport for planetary bombardments and surface landings. However, it is one of the earlier Star Destroyer concepts.

[edit] History

[edit] Clone Wars

The Acclamator was designed by Kuat Drive Yards, the wealthiest starship builders in the galaxy and owners of many other important starship builders like Kuat Systems Engineering and Rothana Heavy Engineering. It was Kuat's Rothana yards that produced many of the Acclamators, along with almost all of the Republic's ground units used during the Clone Wars. While Kuat Systems Engineering (KSE) produced the Venator-class Star Destroyer, the Eta-2 Actis Jedi interceptor, the Delta-7 Aethersprite Jedi starfighter, the V-wing, and the Unstable-Terrain Assault Transport (UT-AT), Rothana Heavy Engineering (RHE) constructed the AT-TE, AT-HE, AT-RT, AT-OT, AT-XT, SPHA-T, LAAT/i and LAAT/c gunships, Infantry Support Platforms (ISPs), and the TX-130S Fighter Tank. The Acclamators transported each and every previously mentioned weapon to the corners of the galaxy during the Clone Wars.

The Acclamators, due to their design as a troop transport meant to land on a planet's surface, had a lighter weapon system than most of the Separatist starships and Republic cruisers to come. This does not mean the Acclamator lacks defenses: the primary weapons of an Acclamator are 12 quad turbolaser turrets that are removable and are easy to place on various locations on the ship. For support, the Acclamator also has 24 laser cannons mounted on the ship's perimeter to scatter attacking starfighters or pirates. For planetary bombardments, the Acclamator also has 4 heavy missile/proton torpedo launchers. These weapons are more than enough to destroy smaller frigates or starfighters, and before landing on a planet, Acclamators are often used to attack and destroy fleeing freighters and ships. The planetary focus of the Acclamator greatly affected its design: the turbolasers are not as accurate as the weapons on most other ships, and the laser cannons, while great for destroying starfighters, are too weak to seriously damage capital ships. Both the missile launchers and the turbolasers take several minutes to reposition in order to have the greatest firing arc against capital ships. As the Clone Wars progressed, almost all of the Acclamators had starfighter and cruiser escorts, since the Acclamator could carry enough weapons to place a siege on an entire planet, but was vulnerable to attack while doing so.

Unlike the Venator, Victory, and Imperial Star Destroyers of the Galactic Civil War and the late Clone Wars, the Acclamator was designed to carry a huge complement of ground vehicles. Around the opening of the Clone Wars and the Battle of Geonosis, one Acclamator could carry over 16,000 clone troopers. Three hundred-twenty speeder bikes, mostly the 74-Z models produced by the Aratech Repulsor Company, were stored aboard for reconnaissance duty, although the AT-RT walker and BARC speeder would eventually replace them. Eighty Republic Gunships performed the role of air transports, while 48 AT-TE walkers and 36 SPHA-T walkers rounded out the invasion forces. During the middle and end of the Clone Wars, many Acclamators were modified to carry several squadrons of V-19 Torrent starfighters and space-capable Republic gunships. During the Battle of Muunilist, Acclamators carried V-19s and LAAT/ls and released them in space, causing a massive dogfight with an invading Separatist force. Interestingly, some Acclamators were used to transport orphaned children from civilian casualties to Bespin, as Bespin served as a sanctuary for orphans during the Clone Wars. Many famous people, including Boba Fett and Aurra Sing, ended up in these ships.

The other systems of Acclamator-class vessels, particularly concerning speed and defense, are what makes this troop transport unique in the Republic and later Imperial starfleets. The Acclamators uses a redundant, criss-cross deflector shield array that provides shield to where the ships is receiving the heaviest laser fire. However, the shields are rarely activated in this manner because it places a lot of strain on an already overtaxed generator. The shields of an Acclamator are strong enough to hold off most battleships, but many Acclamators have a better chance of running than surviving a direct assault. The Acclamator has the fastest hyperdrive ever built for a capital ship: a 0.6 hyperdrive array (3,500 G) that takes up the entire fin-like structure that protrudes from the stern of the vessel. 0.6 is unusually fast for a ship that is 752 meters long, allowing a ship 28 times the size of the Millennium Falcon to enter and exit hyperspace at almost the exact same time. (The Falcon has a 0.5 hyperdrive; the lower the number, the better, and .1 is a very slight difference.) This ensured that the fleet invading Geonosis arrived in minutes after departing from Kamino and Rothana, and many lives were saved and many droids demolished by the timely arrival of these behemoth carriers.

The Acclamators participated in almost every battle during the Clone Wars, particularly during the opening stages. On Rhen Var, Kashyyyk, Thule, Raxus Prime, Geonosis, Muunilist, Jabiim and Tatooine, Acclamators transported many Republic ground vehicles and, the majority of the time, were the main reason behind the Republic victories. The Acclamators use huge, retracting doors in the bottom of the ships to act as loading ramps when depositing vehicles. During the second year of the Clone Wars, the Republic tried to turn Acclamators into carriers by adding smaller hangars, but with the advent of the Venator-class Star Destroyer, the need for a starfighter carrier was nullified, and the Acclamators returned to troop transport.

[edit] Post-Clone Wars

After the Clone Wars, almost all of the Acclamators underwent serious modifications. Many of the Acclamators were melted down and used for scrap - few modern Imperial warships lack armor plating derived from a decommissioned Acclamator. Some of the Acclamators were used as freight transports, and a few of them still served under the Empire to do what they were made for - the transport of ground vehicles and infantry. A few of these ships were modified for starfighter transport; some Imperial versions were modified to carry up to 4 squadrons of starfighters, such as TIE Bombers and TIE Fighters - since Acclamators were outdated ships even in the Galactic Civil War, it was rare to see them shuttling more advanced craft like TIE Interceptors or TIE Defenders. Unfortunately, the Acclamators that didn't end up becoming parts of another ship were turned into slave ships, known as "slavers", since they were used to transport the thousands of slaves the Empire obtained from various sieges of backwater planets. Because the Acclamators could carry almost double the number of infantry an Imperial-class Star Destroyer could, they were deemed perfect to shuttle the scum of the Empire's backbone and slave force. Still others, as Acclamator-class frigates (Mark II), were used as sub-Star Destroyer capital ships at least up to a few years prior to the Battle of Yavin. Even by the time of the Battle of Yuuzhan'tar during the Yuuzhan Vong invasion, Acclamator-class ships were still being scrounged up and pressed into duty as combat warships.

There appear to have been several variants of the Acclamator dedicated for space combat rather than planetary assault. One example may be the Rand Ecliptic, which had two docking bays that had openings on both port and starboard. Other examples, with a ventral hangar bay similar to later Star Destroyers, were seen orbiting an unnamed world around the time of the Battle of Naboo in the Star Wars Tales comics. The Acclamator-class assault ship (Mark II) was upgraded for space combat, and it seems probable that these ships were those redesignated as frigates at some point before the Battle of Yavin.

[edit] Appearances

[edit] Movies

The Acclamator-class assault ship was seen in Attack of the Clones.

[edit] Expanded Universe

Acclamator-class vessels are common in Clone Wars-era Expanded Universe sources. They made small-screen appearances on the Clone Wars miniseries of cartoons engaged with enemy Trade Federation superfreighters at the Battle of Muunilist. Assault ships also made appearances in the novel The Cestus Deception and in the Boba Fett' young readers' series.

[edit] Games

Republic Acclamator-class Assault Transports were featured in Star Wars: Republic Commando. In fact, one level had the Delta Squad protagonist commando unit boarding the abandoned ship Prosecutor and doing battle with Separatist enemies in its bowels. Acclamator-class ships also appeared as destructible targets in Star Wars Battlefront II in space combat levels and as Imperial starship units in Star Wars: Empire at War, designated as frigates. Acclamator-class Assault Transports also appear in the [Star Wars] game "Star Wars: The Clone Wars", for the Nintendo GameCube. It is extremely-accurately displayed, and clearly shows the various advantages, and uses, of the ship. An "Acclamator II-class Assault Ship" card also featured in the "Revenge of the Sith" expansion for the Wizards of the Coast Star Wars Trading Card Game.
