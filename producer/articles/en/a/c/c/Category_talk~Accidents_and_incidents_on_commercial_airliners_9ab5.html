[edit] Subcategorisation

Could someone please help me sub-categorize the remaining accidents? GCarty 11:17, 25 July 2005 (UTC)

I've worked a bit on it. See below for remaining issues and suggested solutions.
There are some general questions we need to answer first, though. For example, an accident is rarely caused by a single factor. I believe that we should file each accident and incident under all major factors resulting in the event. This, however, raises the issue of what constitutes a "major" contributing factor.
One might, for example, argue in many cases that pilots could have done more to recover from a situation caused by something outside of their control, but are such sentiments always reasonable? The pilot error article states that it is generally not reasonable, except when failing to observe due dilligence in poor weather. What about problems that arguably should have been detected during pre-flight check (as in the case of Helios), does that constitute pilot error? If such a check is part of the standard protocol (and I can't see why it wouldn't be), then according to the pilot error article, it probably does.
Another problem is that, in many cases, there is disagreement over the cause between multiple investigating agencies and/or between agencies and the general public. In such cases, I generally prefer to file articles under causes favoured by both sides, leaving the details of the dispute to the article itself.
We should also seek greater uniformity and (to a reasonable extent) genericness in the naming of categories. "Accidents and incidents" is better than "crashes". "Explosions" and "fires" are better than "in-flight explosions" and "in-flight fires". "Crew error" might be better than pilot error (since some accidents and incidents are caused by, for example, flight engineers). "Pilot error" is a more established term, but "procedural mistakes by the crew" and similar terms have been used in official reports.
We may want to consider whether or not "commercial airliners" is a suitable term. To me it seems tautological. Is there such a thing as a "non-commercial airliner" (given that "airliner" is defined by Webster as "an airplane operated by an airline")? I suggest a naming convention of "Airliner accidents and incidents caused by X" and changing the title of this category to "Airliner accidents and incidents".
Below, I propose creating a category for accidents with cause undetermined. (The causes of incidents are rarely left undetermined, but such a category might be preferrable, since it would be more inclusive, and those who resent Law Frenchisms, "undetermined cause" might be preferrable.) Possible arguments against this are that articles may get "lost" in here if they are filed as such early on and not updated upon publication of a conclusive report. Another issue is that many reports do not determine a cause, but rather finds probable causes. The arguments for are hopefully obvious, and it should be noted that many investigations never determine a cause.
Your input is very welcome.
—LX 06:23, 7 May 2006 (UTC)
