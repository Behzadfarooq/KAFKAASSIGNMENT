Accrediting Commission International is an unrecognized accreditation group based in Beebe, Arkansas.

International Accrediting Commission was an accreditation mill ran by George S. Reuter Jr. Reuter was fined $15,000 by the State of Missouri in an undercover sting operation when he offered to "accredit" a school in Missouri.[1] Reuter retired and paid the fee, but later transferred the agency to John Scheel who moved the company to Beebe, Arkansas and changed the name to Accrediting Commission International for Schools, Colleges, and Theological Seminaries.[2] Today the Accrediting Commission International remains unrecognized by the United States Department of Education.[3]

The University of North America was ordered to pay $2.5 million dollars for "claiming accreditation by the International Accrediting Commission".[4] Once the operation was closed, the operator of University of North America, Edward Perry Reddeck (who served prison time for educational fraud) "moved to Utah and reopened his degree mill under the name North American University.[5] That operation was closed and Reddeck opened the Gold Coast University.[6]
