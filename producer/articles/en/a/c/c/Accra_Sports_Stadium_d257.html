Accra Sports Stadium is a multi-use stadium in Accra, Ghana. It is Ghana's 2nd largest stadium, with a capacity of 40,000 and is considered by many to be the National Stadium. As the designated venue of some of the 2008 African Cup of Nations matches, the stadium is currently being rebuilt upgraded and modernised to meet FIFA standards. Work on the stadium will be completed in October 2007, [1] and the capacity will be increased to 40,000.

The stadium is also the home of one of Africa's most popular clubs, Accra Hearts of Oak, but Ghana's National Team matches are sometimes played there.

The stadium was renamed after Ohene Djan in 2004, the first Director of Sports in independent Ghana. This renaming was quite controversial and oppossed by many in Accra's Ga community who felt that a prominent Ga should be given the honor since the stadium is a center piece of downtown Accra.Source

The Accra Stadium is used mostly for football matches and it can also host athletics.

During the 2000 African Cup of Nations in Ghana and Nigeria, the stadium hosted 9 matches of that tournament.
