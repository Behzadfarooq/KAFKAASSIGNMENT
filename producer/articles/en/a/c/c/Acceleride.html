Acceleride is a proposed transit system by Brampton Transit and will be using the bus rapid transit option similar to that currently used by YRT/VIVA in neighbouring York Region.

Brampton Transit has made arrangements to purchase 19 vehicles and build a new terminal, called Eastgate, pending provincial funding for the project. The city of Brampton hopes to connect the city with transit operators in Toronto, central Mississauga and GO Transit, as well as using Highway 7, Highway 407 and Highway 403. The system will make use of exclusive bus lanes and queue-jump lanes at major intersections (of which some have already been built).

[edit] Routes

The first two routes planned are the following:

  • BY Line: Brampton - York Region BRT line, an east-west route using Highway 7 and Queen Street with connections to VIVA.
  • Main Line: Hurontario/Main Street Line, running north-south along those two streets with connections to Mississauga Transit.

Other routes will be added when warranted, with Steeles Avenue, Bovaird Drive and Dixie Road identified as likely possibilities.

Brampton-Mississauga BRT line will also be using Highway 407 and 403. Future plans would eventually see the BRT converted to a LRT option in downtown Brampton.

Other possible links include:

  • Airport link to Airport Corporate Centre
  • Brampton-Milton BRT line using Highways 401 and 407

[edit] Terminals

Currently, there are no new terminals built, but the city is committed to build one on Highway 7 near the York Region border, tentatively called Eastgate. It is likely that the current terminal in downtown Brampton will be used.

[edit] Fares

With York Region's VIVA as a precedent, it is probable that the Acceleride routes will share the same fare structure as the conventional Brampton Transit system.

[edit] Vehicles

Brampton's Acceleride will likely use the same vehicles, standard and articulated buses used by VIVA.
