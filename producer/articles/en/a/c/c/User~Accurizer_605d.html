Hello, fellow Wikipedian! Thanks for stopping by!

I first came to Wikipedia in January 2006 as an anonymous editor. I found the encyclopedia to be a useful tool, and I still do. Initially, I was somewhat of a WikiGnome. I enjoyed fixing errors that I found in topic areas in which I am knowledgeable. My interests include history, technology, casino gaming, transportation, government, politics, business, and other areas.

I became a registered user in order to start a few articles and my goal has been to average one new article per month (I know, I'm falling behind!) Below you can find the "Things I've started". As with everything else on Wikipedia, these things are a work in progress. I hope you will take a minute to improve them (or at least add them to your watchlist in order to keep an eye out for vandalism).

My favorite task is new pages patrolling, which provides a diverse blend of opportunities, including anti-vandalism, verifying new contributions thorough research, welcoming new contributors, and helping with the deletion process.

Unfortunately, the amount of vandalism seems to be on the rise. Lately, I've been primarily occupied with reverting it whenever I have time. This isn't why I came here, but it is an important task. I've accumulated over 6,000 pages on my watchlist by just following vandals around. The increasing amount of time spent by dedicated editors reverting vandalism is a concern for me and it should be for the entire community as well. It is truly the greatest threat to the encyclopedia's credibility. Hopefully you can help out!

[edit] My RfA policy

I have participated in RfA discussions only when I was familiar with the editor prior to the nomination. In an admin candidate, some things I have considered important are maturity, resourcefulness, dedication to the project (about 1 year and 5,000 helpful edits), an understanding of policy, and a reasonable amount of positive communication with others. I have not supported editors who have engaged in personal attacks, 3RR, edit warring, or vandalism.
