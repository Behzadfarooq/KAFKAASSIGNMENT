This language is still somewhat prejudicial and lacks NPOV. Here's a proposal

This is a list of people who have been accused — but not convicted - of being spies for the Soviet Union. These allegations are based upon the conclusions drawn by the National Security Agency and the Federal Bureau of Investigation.

Alternatively,

This is a list of people who have been accused of being spies for the Soviet Union. These are based upon the identifications made by the National Security Agency and the Federal Bureau of Investigation. Few were ever incdicted, and none were ever convicted in a court of law. nobs 20:40, 21 November 2005 (UTC)

To "identify" someone as a "Soviet spy" is a per se allegation that the person committed a very specific crime - espionage. Such "identifications" have not been substantiated by a court of law. They are tantamount to a prosecutor's allegation or an indictment - presenting only one side of the case and assuming it as factual. In our adversarial system of law, such "identifications" cannot be construed as truth. They must be presented as what they are - allegations of criminal behavior. FCYTravis 03:50, 29 November 2005 (UTC)
