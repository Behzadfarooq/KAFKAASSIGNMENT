[edit] Mississippi School Districts

In response to your message on my talk page, let me first say I am sorry that I have offended you. Such was not my intent. I am relatively new here and just started patrolling new pages in earnest, and I am still finding a comfort level with that.

That being said, I have to ask: Are you just adding articles about these districts because they exist, or is there something that makes them notable? Although some people insist the contrary is true, it is my understanding that WP is not a directory of school districts. Articles should indicate notability before they are posted; this is why a lack of asserted notability is one of the CSD.

There are many editors who develop their articles in notepad or /sandbox before posting them to the main space. We really have to have some minimum standards for articles so that WP is not diluted by filler. And I'm sure you've seen plenty of articles that are left by editors in the condition of these. How am I to know whether or not a particular editor understands that such articles should be expanded, and how am I to know that you are still working on an article? Patrolling new pages is hard work, even with the available utilities, but patrolling day or week old pages is much harder.

Anyway, if you are expanding the article, there's no problem, because it should survive AfD, and if you don't get to it in time, you can always recreate it later when you can do so in a form that overcomes any AfD objections.

Finally, as for agreeing with Metzenberg, when I tagged Jewish opposition to evolution for deletion (http://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Jewish_opposition_to_evolution&oldid=115017545), it was a list of four sources, three stub tags and the words "Jewish opposition to evolution". There was no article content whatsoever. Shouldn't that come first? (Bizarrely, no content was included until almost an hour after the article was created, and then only after it had been tagged for deletion three times (twice by me, once by another editor). --Butseriouslyfolks 17:11, 14 March 2007 (UTC)
