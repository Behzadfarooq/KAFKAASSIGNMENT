    [edit] Edit Summary usage

    When editing an article on Wikipedia there is a small field labeled "Edit summary" under the main edit-box. It looks like this:

    The text written here will appear on the Recent changes page, in the page revision history, on the diff page, and in the watchlists of users who are watching that article. See m:Help:Edit summary for full information on this feature.

    Filling in the edit summary field greatly helps your fellow contributors in understanding what you changed, so please always fill in the edit summary field, especially for big edits or when you are making subtle but important changes, like changing dates or numbers. Thank you. Tellyaddict 11:42, 8 April 2007 (UTC)

    Why is my section 'Stuff About Me' All Cramped Up, If You See What I Mean?

    Userpages are generally protected upon any good-faith request by their respective users -- I've gone ahead and protected your userpage, if you'd like me to look into protecting any sub-pages, feel free to let me know, or see WP:RFPP. Hope that helps! Luna Santin 07:49, 9 February 2007 (UTC)

    isnt there supposed to be some kind of message sayiing 'protected'? ACBest 07:53, 9 February 2007 (UTC)

    On articles, yes; if you like, you're more than welcome to add {{sprotect}} to the top of your userpage. You can also use {{sprotect2}}, which is a little less intrusive (just a lock icon in the corner). Either way, it's your option -- many users with protected pages choose to omit the template. You're still able to edit the page, and any established account will be able to do the same; the template's only there for notification when needed. If that makes sense.     [edit] 3RR

    Please refrain from undoing other people's edits repeatedly. If you continue, you may be blocked from editing Wikipedia. Note that the three-revert rule prohibits making more than three reversions in a content dispute within a 24 hour period. Additionally, users who perform a large number of reversions in content disputes may be blocked for edit warring, even if they do not technically violate the three-revert rule. Rather than reverting, discuss disputed changes on the talk page. The revision you want is not going to be implemented by edit warring. Thank you. Kamope · talk · contributions 15:09, 11 February 2007 (UTC)

    [edit] Clock and Category

    {{helpme}}

    2 things.

    First, is it me, or is the wikipedia server time a hour slow, as the clocks changed 1 hr forward?

    secondly, can i create [[Category:Talk pages belonging to ACBest]] or [[Category:ACBest's Talk Pages]] Thanks, and happy april fools day (no this isnt a joke!) ACBestMy Contributions 11:26, 1 April 2007 (UTC)

    1. The Wikipedia servers run on UTC, which is the same as the UK wintertime time zone. During summertime in the UK, the UK clocks go an hour forwards but Wikipedia remains in the original time zone. (The UTC timezone is used by Wikipedia to show times for people anywhere in the world; in the USA, where the time can be somewhat different from UK time, Wikipedia still displays times in UTC.)
    2. Categories generally aren't used for individual users. Special:Prefixindex/User talk:ACBest should find all your talkpages (likewise, Special:Prefixindex/User:ACBest should find all your userpages), as long as you've named them in the conventional way with User:ACBest at the start.
    Hope that helps! --ais523 11:40, 1 April 2007 (UTC)

    Thanks! ACBestMy Contributions 11:44, 1 April 2007 (UTC)
