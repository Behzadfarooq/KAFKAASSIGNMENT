The Bruny Island Ferry links Bruny Island to the Tasmanian mainland. Operated by North Western Shipping and Towage Co. Pty Ltd and owned by the Tasmanian Government, the ferry Mirambeena travels between Kettering on the Tasmanian mainland and Roberts Point on Bruny Island. The service is subsidised by the Tasmanian Government.

[edit] History

Four ferries have operated on the route since 1954:

  • S.S. Melba (1954-1961, then as a reserve until approximately 1977)
  • Mangana (1961-early 1980's, then as a reserve for the Harry O'May until 1991)
  • Harry O'May (early 1980's-early 1990's)
  • Mirambeena (early 1990's-)

[edit] S.S. Melba

The S.S. Melba was built in 1921 as a cargo steamer to operate on the Derwent River. She commenced operations as a single-deck vehicular ferry on the Bruny Island run in 1954, and was the primary Bruny Island ferry until the arrival of the Mangana in 1961. After that date, she continued on the Bruny Island run, assisting during peak periods and when the Mangana was under repair.

In 1975, the partial collapse of Hobart's Tasman Bridge created an urgent short-term need for ferries operating across the Derwent River. The Melba, despite frequent strandings due to her deep draft, operated between Hobart and Bellerive.

[edit] Mangana

The Mangana, originally known as the George Peat, was one of two identical single-deck ferries built around 1930 to operate a service across the Hawkesbury River.

After a stint operating on Auckland Harbour, the ship (now known as the Ewan W. Alison) was purchased by the Tasmanian Government in 1959. Although both the Ewan W. Alison and its sister ship the Alexander Alison were purchased from their New Zealand operators for use on the Bruny Island run, the Alexander Alison sunk on April 30, 1960 while being towed across the Tasman Sea. Seven months later, the Mangana was successfully towed across the Tasman, and commenced operations on the Bruny Island run on March 27, 1961.

The Melba stayed on to fill the gap left by the loss of the Mangana's sister ship.

The Mangana was the primary Bruny Island ferry until the early 1980's, staying on as a reserve ferry for the Harry O'May until 1991.

[edit] Harry O'May

Formerly the Hong Kong ferry Man On, the Harry O'May operated the Bruny Island route in the 1980's and 90's. Its two decks greatly increased carrying capacity relative to the smaller earlier ferries.

[edit] Move from Barnes Bay to Roberts Point

Prior to 1983, the Bruny Island end of the ferry service terminated at Barnes Bay. The trip from Kettering to Barnes Bay took around 35 minutes. The change of route to terminate at Roberts Point meant that the one-way trip time was reduced to 12 minutes.

[edit] Mirambeena

The Mirambeena began operation on the Bruny Island run in the early 1990's. It is a two-deck ferry equipped with a Voith-Schneider propulsion system.

The much smaller M.V. 'Southern Condor' has operated the route while the Mirambeena is undergoing service. [1]
