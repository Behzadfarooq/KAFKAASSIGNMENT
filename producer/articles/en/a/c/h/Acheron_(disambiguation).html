Acheron may refer to:

  • Acheron, a river in Greece, believed in ancient Greek mythology to branch into the underworld
  • Acheron, a river in the Greek-settled region of Italy near Pandosia
  • Acheron, a river in the Greek-settled region of Italy near Cosenza (modern Caronte river)
  • Acheron (band), a Florida death metal band
  • HMS Acheron, the name of several ships of the Royal Navy
  • Acheron, a French privateer in the film Master and Commander: The Far Side of the World
  • Acheron, a kingdom of darkness in Robert E. Howard's antediluvian world; Conan the Barbarian fought to keep it from resurfacing
  • Acheron, is another name for the moon LV-426 in the Alien movie series
  • Acheron, the leader and caretaker of the Dark Hunters in Sherrilyn Kenyon's Dark Hunter series.
  • Acheron, the name of a planet that's covered by a single huge world-wide organism in Unreal II: The Awakening on the 3rd level.
  • Acheron, the main protagonist of Inverloch webcomic
  • Acheron Hades, a diabolical villain in Jasper Fforde's book The Eyre Affair
  • "The Bells of Acheron", a song on the Swedish doom metal band Candlemass' album Ancient Dreams
  • Acheron, the name of one of the swords of the character Cervantes in the video game Soul Calibur II
  • Acheron, the name of a GWR Firefly Class locomotive
  • Acheron, the name of a GWR Hawthorn Class locomotive
  • Acheron (plane), one of the Outer Planes in Dungeons and Dragons cosmology.
