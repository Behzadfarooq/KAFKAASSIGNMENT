Acres Homes, Houston, Texas

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Acres Homes is a wooded urban/inner-city community in northwest Houston, Texas.

Established during World War I, it was once considered to be the largest unincorporated African American community in the South. It was originally subdivided into large lots sold by the acre, hence the name "Acres Homes" and marketed to African Americans as a suburban area, which was not far from the city. Still primarily African American, the community now includes a combination of large areas of pine forests with only a scattering of homes: small tract homes built in standard suburban subdivisions, and large comfortable homes on well-maintained wooded lots. There is little commercial or industrial development. It covers 5,733 acres (9.0 square miles). The area is also known for its high crime rate.

This area is referred to by many as "The 44", because of the "Acres Homes Limited" METRO bus route #44. The Antioch Missionary Baptist Church is also in the area.

[edit] Demographics

As of the censusGR2 of 2000, there were 72,199 people residing in Acres Homes. The racial makeup of the neighborhood was 25.7% White, 60.1% African American, 4.5% Asian, and 9.7% from other races, ##% from two or more races. Hispanic or Latino of any race were 21.8% of the population.

[edit] Public Services

Water and sewer service was introduced into the original subdivisions during the 1970s as the area was annexed by the City of Houston. It is represented by Houston City Council Districts A and B.

The area is served by the Houston Police Department's North Division [1]. The Acres Homes Storefront Station is located at 6719 West Montgomery [2].

The area is served by the Acres Homes Branch of the Houston Public Library.

[edit] Education

The eastern part of the community is located in the Houston Independent School District (7 public schools, including Booker T. Washington High School) the western part is in the Aldine Independent School District and served by Eisenhower High School. Some areas outside of the city limits that are still considered to be "Acres Homes" are within the Klein Independent School District.
