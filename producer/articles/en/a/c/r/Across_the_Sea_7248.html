Across the Sea is the fifth song from Weezer's sophomore album, Pinkerton. It is one of the most popular songs on the album among Weezer fans and is often considered one of the band's best overall. Singer/songwriter Rivers Cuomo wrote the song after he received a letter from a Japanese girl during a depressing winter at Harvard University. This song also discusses when Rivers was an adolescent and was considering becoming a monk. This song features a Beach Boys like break down. When asked in 2006 about the girl, he commented that "I dont know anything about her and I've never contacted her" [1].

The song appears in Come On and Kick Me!: The String Quartet Tribute to Weezer and Only in Dreams: Classical Music Inspired by Weezer.
