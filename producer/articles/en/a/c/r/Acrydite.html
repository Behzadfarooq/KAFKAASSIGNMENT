Acrydite is a phosphoramidite that allows the synthesis of oligonucleotides with a methacryl group at the 5' end (less commonly 3' or internal). Acryl oligonucleotides have been tested, but the acryl group is not stable to storage. Acrydite modified oligonucletides can react with nucleophiles such as thiols (Michael addition chemistry), this forms the basis of the ez-rays chemistry which was used for microarrays. More importantly, Acrydite modified oligonucleotides can be incorporated, stochiometricaly, into hydrogels such as polyacrylamide, using standard free radical polymerization chemistry, where the double bond in the Acrydite group reacts with other activated double bond containing compounds such as acrylamide.

[edit] History

The idea of acrylamide modified DNA was developed by T Christian Boles, while working at Mosaic Technologies, a now defunct biotechnology company which was located in Waltham, MA. The IP was licensed, along with a microarray technology ("ez-rays") to Matrix Technologies, of Hudson, NH, now part of Thermo Electron Corp. Acrydite is commercially available from Biosearch Technologys, Novato, CA. Acrydite modifed oligonucleotides can be obtained from vendors such as IDT.

[edit] Hybridgel

The first use of Acrydite was in a technology called hybridgel [1].
In Hybridgel, Acrydite modified oligos are incoporated into a standard polyacrylamide gel system; as complementary ss nucleic acid moves past the immobilized Acrydite oligos, the complementary DNA is captured. Hybridgel like techonology is widely used as a DNA purification system, as in a DNA sequencing technology developed at berkely [2].
Acrydite technology can also be used to purify DNA, as in the Pre-gen colon cancer test [3] developed by Exact Sciences.

[edit] Polonys

Mitra and Church at Harvard Medical School [4] devloped a novel DNA sequencing technology based on Acrydite modified probes; this technology is used by Agencourt.

[edit] Acrydite and PCR

Surprisingly, the Acrydite group survives PCR, so Acrydite modifed PCR products can be prepared and used as oligos. However, as is common with polymers that are longer than the persistance length, the coupling of Acrydite modifed PCR products is inefficient, as the ends of the molecules are not accessible.

[edit] Acrydite and Protein

Acrydite oligos have been used to study protein DNA interactions [5]

[edit] Acrydite and DNA Computing

Recent experiments by Braich et al. solving the Satisfiability Problem have demonstrated that Acrydite can be used in DNA computing to implement the extraction operation. Previously, a biotin-avidin magnetic bead system was used for this purpose.


[edit] Exact Scientific Colon Cancer Test

Acrydite technology has been evaluated by Exact Scientific for use in it's colon cancer test.
