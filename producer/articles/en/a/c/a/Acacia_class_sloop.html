General Characteristics
Displacement: 1200 tons
Length: 250 (p.p.), 262.5 (o.a.)
Beam: 33 feet
Draught: 11 (mean), 11.25 - 12 feet
Propulsion: Machinery: 1 set 4-cylinder triple expansion. Boilers: 2 cylindrical. 1 screw
Range: Coal: 130 tons normal. 250 tons max. = about 2000 miles at 15 kts.
Speed: Designed H.P. 1400 or 1800 = 17 kts., but actually required about 2200 I.H.P. for this speed
Complement: 77 men
Armament: Designed to mount 2 x 12 pdr. and 2 x 3 pdr. AA, but wide variations

The Acacia class was a class of sloops that were built under the Emergency War Programme for the Royal Navy in World War I as part of the larger "Flower Class", which were also referred to as the "Cabbage Class", or "Herbaceous Borders".

They were single-screw Fleet Sweeping Vessels (Sloops) with triple hulls at the bows to give extra protection against loss when working.

  • Daphne — built by Barclay Curle, completed May 1915
  • Foxglove — built by Barclay Curle, completed March 1915, to guardship 1942, base ship 1943, sold for scrapping 1946
  • Hollyhock — built Barclay Curle, completed May 1915
  • Honeysuckle — built by Lobnitz & Company, Renfrew, completed April 1915
  • Iris — built by Lobnitz, completed June 1915
  • Jonquil — built by Charles Connell & Company, Scotstoun, completed May 1915
  • Laburnum — built by Connell, completed June 1915, hulked as RNVR drill ship in Singapore 1935, abandoned 1942
  • Larkspur — built by Napier & Miller, Old Kilpatrick, completed 1915
  • Lavender — built by Archibald McMillan & Son, Dumbarton, war loss
  • Lilac — built by Greenock & Grangemouth Dockyard Company, Greenock, completed April 1915
  • Lily — built by Barclay Curle, completed June 1915
  • Magnolia — built by Scotts, completed June 1915
  • Mallow — built by Barclay Curle, to Royal Australian Navy 1919, sunk as target 1935
  • Marigold — built by Bow MacLachlan & Company, Paisley, completed May 1915
  • Mimosa — built by Bow MacLachlan, completed July 1915
  • Primrose — built by William Simons & Company, Renfrew, completed June 1915
  • Sunflower — built by D. & W. Henderson & Company, Glasgow, completed May 1915
  • Veronica — built by Dunlop Bremner & Company, Port Glasgow, completed May 1915
