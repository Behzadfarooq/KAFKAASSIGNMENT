Acabion the manufacturer of the Acabion GTBO concept car. Its Headquarter is in Lucerne, Switzerland. It primary officials are the Stuttgart and Lucerne based engineers Lenka Mikova (CEO) and Peter Maskus (CResO), who worked for Porsche, BMW, VW and the Mercedes-Benz group of DaimlerChrysler.

[edit] Acabion concept car

The Acabion concept is a two-seat vehicle with an unconventional layout. The body itself is designed to minimize aerodynamic drag and as such is drop shaped. The driver is placed at the front of the cockpit with the passenger behind. Power is provided by a hybrid drive system mounted behind the passenger compartment, incorporating a turbocharged 1.3-liter motor as well as a small electric motor for low speed (below 40 mph) operations. Under normal conditions the Acabion concept drives on its two main wheels not unlike a motorcycle, but at high speeds or if balance is lost at low speeds the two small side wheels are designed to lower themselves automatically to aid stability. A pendulum mounted behind the passenger’s seat also assists in holding the vehicle upright.

[edit] Performance

Due to it’s claimed 880 lb curb weight and powerful motor the Acabion concept projects a high power to weight ration of 1500 hp per ton in the GTBO 55 model and 2000 hp per ton in the GTBO 70. This combined with the aerodynamic shape of the vehicle allows rapid high-speed acceleration. The developers claim the vehicle will be able to accelerate from 180 mph to 280 mph in only 9 seconds. Designers also claim that the Acabion’s aerodynamic shape will allow for low fuel consumption even at high speeds.

[edit] Related bionic concepts

Related bionic concepts to the Acabion are to be found in the airplane "Smartfish" from Switzerland or in the speed-boat Bionic Dolphin from California, USA.
