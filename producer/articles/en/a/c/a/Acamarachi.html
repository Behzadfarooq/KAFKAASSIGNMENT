Acamarachi, also known as Pili, is a stratovolcano located in the Antofagasta Region of Chile.

It has a steep-sided cone with slopes of up to 45 degrees. It is at the southeast end of a small volcano group stretching to Colachi to the northwest. It has an old summit crater with a summit lava flow thought to date from the Holocene era.
