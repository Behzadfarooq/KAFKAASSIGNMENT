[edit] 60 as passing?

Throughout my time in school in the United States (mostly but not exclusively in New York state), any grade below 65 was treated as failing and represented an F if it was a final grade (a D- might be given as a warning for borderline failure during a semester of high school, for example). This has also been true at the universities I've taught at as a professor. I believe that 65 as the minimum passing grade is still a common if not the prevailing standard in the U.S. or parts of it, and should be mentioned somewhere in the article other than as part of "the Nines System," which I've never heard of. Here's the grading system I've found most common

Below 65 - F 65-below 67.5 - D 67.5-below 70 - D+ 70-below 72.5 - C- 72.5-below 75 - C etc.

Michael 00:45, 3 Jan 2007 (UTC)

Well, scoring systems are different among schools and even between teachers within schools. Right now, different departments in my school have different scores. And all my different schools have different scales. For me, these two seemed to be the most common...
A - 90 - 100
B - 80 - 89
C - 70 - 79 etc.
A = 93 - 100
B = 86 - 92
C = 78 - 85
D = 70 - 77
F = Below 70
So I don't think it's too wise to say that any of these are the standard US grading system yet...JpKllA 03:01, 10 January 2007 (UTC)

[edit] Grade Point Scale...

From my experience...the following gpa system seems to be the most popular...

Regular Course

A = 4 points B = 3 C = 2 D = 1 (or 0 depending on school) F = 0

Honors Course

A = 5 B = 4 C = 3 D = 2 (or lower depending on school) F = 0

A.P. Course

A = 6 B = 5 C = 4 D = 3 (or lower depending on school) F = 0

Whatever. It might be pointless now since the ones in the article already get the point across.JpKllA 03:19, 10 January 2007 (UTC)
