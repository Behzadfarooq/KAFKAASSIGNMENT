[edit] solution to create pedigree tree from template?

I would like to look into a possibility to use this template as a source for creating academic lineage trees. I guess this could work with some type of categorization method as well or better. Any ideas anyone? Also, I would suggest to put one more box into the template, denoting degree, year, location, type of degree? Or would that be too much? I tried this template here. --Carboxen 06:30, 15 June 2006 (UTC)

If categories of the type, 'Category:Students of Aristotle' were set up and populated then clicking on this category link from the 'Alexander the Great' article would give you a page listing all the students of Aristotle. If that category page itself was then added to 'Category:Students of Plato' and similar links established within all the category pages then you could browse up and down the hierarchy. However, you can also do that with this template... on the Artistotle article you can click the link in this template to go up the chain to Plato and from there up to Socrates. The difference is that there are automated tools for displaying tree structures from Wikipedia categories: ([1] [2]). There is no comparative means of doing so for templates except to build and populate a template for that specific purpose - see Template:Familytree for one which could be used to build such info. --CBD 12:02, 15 June 2006 (UTC)
Thank you CBD, these are very good suggestions on how to make use of existing tools. I think it would not be useful to use the Template:Familytree unless its contents and structure could be created by a db query on categories (categories are a great suggestion). What I mean is, that the first (Template:Familytree) has its strength in the easy graphic representation, the second (categories) in its query capability. The external links you quote are also a good idea but I fear categorization could get out of hand with that approach (unless one would limit it to non-living persons) - as there is no sense in having a category for only two or three entries (the maximum is determined by the number of descendents per generation). This could easily lead to thousands of categories with each only 1 or two entries, and would force us all to think about a new category standard. Similarly, it would lead to few categories with few (1) entries, once one goes back in time enough to hit the Rennaiscance. --Carboxen 03:31, 17 June 2006 (UTC)

[edit] This template obsolete, use Template:Infobox Scientist

Please note that the {{Infobox Scientist}} as endorsed by the Wikiproject Biography contains a more widely used and more extensive function similar to the above. It should probably be recommended to not further use the above template (however, it was good and I used it as well). For an exemplaric use of the infobox, see for example: Carl Friedrich Gauss (featured article). --Carboxen 07:15, 10 November 2006 (UTC)
