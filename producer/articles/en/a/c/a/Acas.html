Acas, the employment relations service (previously known as the Advisory, Conciliation and Arbitration Service)[1]is an independent non-departmental public body of the Government of the United Kingdom. Its purpose is to promote good industrial relations practice and its main activities in doing this are to provide information on employment legislation through its national helpline (08457 47 47 47) and to resolve individual and collective disputes between employers and employees. Acas is an impartial organisation that does not take sides in disputes but tries to help the parties find suitable resolutions to their dispute. Acas is commonly misunderstood to be a trade union.

Acas was founded in 1975, and receives its funding through the Department of Trade and Industry. Its current chair is Rita Donaghy.

Acas services have evolved considerably over the last few years, and the organisations is seen to be more proactive in its approach to employment issues. In particular, its expanded services now include:-

E-Learning - Includes guidance on issues such as Discipline; Absence; Contracts; and redundancy. Training - Sessions on employment issues Publications - Range of booklets, leaflets and handbooks giving practical guidance and advice for both employers and employees from writing a contract of employment, to holiday entitlement to the latest employment legislation. Consultancy - Consultancy and guidance on setting up best practice. eManager - Expert software systems designed to help employers record details of discipline and grievance cases in the workplace and to guide them through the decision making process. Videos - Video clips introducing some of Acas' services to help in the workplace.
