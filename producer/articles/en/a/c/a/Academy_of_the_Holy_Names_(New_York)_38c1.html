The Academy of the Holy Names, in Albany, New York State, is a Middle States accredited Catholic girls' college-preparatory school for ages pre-kindergarten through twelfth grade. The school was founded in 1884 by the Sisters of the Holy Names of Jesus and Mary. In 2005, it had 530 students and a student:faculty ratio of 11:1. The mission of AHN is to prepare its students to become intellectual, moral, spiritual, and cultural leaders.

[edit] School Traditions

[edit] School motto

Esse Quam Videri: "To Be Rather than to Seem"

[edit] School Prayer

This is said every morning during prayer, after a reading from Scripture.

"Blessed Mother Marie Rose, we firmly believe in the power of your intercession with almighty God: Father, Son, and Holy Spirit. We beg you to hear our humble prayers and to obtain for us the favors which we now ask in the silence of our hearts. God, our father, knows all, can do all things, and loves us. Sure of God’s love and strong in our faith, together with you, Blessed Mother Marie Rose, we accept God’s most holy will. Through Jesus Christ. Amen."

[edit] Song Contests

Each Thanksgiving and Christmas, Holy Names's Student Government sponsors song contests in which the four Upper School classes participate. For the Thanksgiving song contest, each class writes original lyrics to the tune of an existing song; for the Christmas contest, each class performs a carol. In addition, the teachers sing their own (non-competitive) song, often teasing the student body for well-known habits. The song contest is an excellent example of the playfully competitive atmosphere at AHN.

[edit] Academics

The Academy is divided into Lower, Middle, and Upper Schools.

[edit] Upper School Programs

School services include the following programs:

  • Advanced Placement classes, currently offered in Biology, American History, English, and Calculus
  • Advanced Physics and Chemistry classes
  • A Science Research program with a 3 year duration in which the student recives 12 college credits
  • A religious development program including mandatory service learning
  • University in the High School program, which offers college credit for French 4 and 5, Spanish 4 and 5, and psychology through the University at Albany
  • A comprehensive college guidance program

[edit] Advisor System

Each freshman entering the Upper School is assigned a teacher as an academic advisor, with whom she will meet once a week throughout the school year. In consecutive years, the student may request to be paired with a teacher with whom she relates, or stay with her original advisor.

[edit] Sports

The Academy of the Holy Names has an extremely successful sports tradition in the Colonial Council. Many of the teams regularly place first in the Council, and some go even further--in 2005, the AHN Cross Country team won the Division A State Championship. Both Varsity and JV teams are offered, as well as Frosh Basketball. Holy Names competes in the following sports:

  • Fall
    • Soccer
    • Cross Country
    • Tennis
    • Cheerleading
  • Winter
    • Basketball
    • Indoor Track and Field
    • Basketball Cheerleading
  • Spring
    • Softball
    • Track and Field
    • Lacrosse

Cheerleaders at Holy Names cheer for the football team of Christian Brothers Academy.
