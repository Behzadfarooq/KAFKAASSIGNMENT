Academy Bus Lines is a transportation service provider that offers scheduled bus service, sightseeing tours, convention transportation, corporate transfers, sports teams, parades, major sporting events and private charters. Academy Bus is headquartered in Hoboken, New Jersey. The company was founded in 1968. Academy is the largest privately owned transportation company in the United States. Academy Bus is a full service charter bus company specializing in sightseeing, major events and conventions, airport transfers, casino trips, multi-day tours, and commuter bus routes, and has vast experience with all types of ground transportation, operating more than 600 motor coaches.


Academy offers scheduled bus transportation services within New Jersey and offers routes travelling to and from the Port Authority Bus Terminal, Midtown and Crosstown Manhattan and the Wall Street area. Academy operates service from multiple locations in New Jersey, within the state and into New York City, from multiple locations including U.S. Route 9, Lakewood Township, Old Bridge Township, Route 36, Route 35, Long Branch, Westampton Township / Willingboro Township, Hightstown, Point Pleasant / Shore Points, PNC Bank Arts Center, Jackson Township, Sayreville and Forked River.

[edit] New Jersey Transit contracts and other relations

Through the No. 22 Hillside Corporation and Academy Express, LLC, Academy operates a number of New Jersey Transit routes under contract:[1][2]

  • No. 22 Hillside, Hudson County: 2, 22, 23, and 88
  • Academy Express, Middlesex County and Union County: 801, 802, 803, 804, 805, 810, 811, 813, 814, 815, 817, 818, 819, 822
  • Academy Express, Bergen County: 751, 752, 753, 755, 756, 762, 772, and 780
  • Academy Express: Rutgers University campus shuttles[3]

While most of the buses used on the local routes are owned by NJ Transit, most of the Rutgers University shuttle buses are owned by Academy.[citation needed]

A number of privately-operated Academy routes were taken over by NJ Transit in 2004, some of them for direct operation by NJ Transit and some for contract to No. 22 Hillside:

  • No. 22 Hillside's 123 Union City-New York and 125 Journal Square-New York buses, operated under contract,[4][5] were taken over directly by NJ Transit on June 26, 2004.[6]
  • Academy's 22 Hillside-Bergenline route became NJ Transit's 22 Hillside and Academy's Boulevard East route[7] became NJ Transit's 23 North Bergen-Hoboken on June 27, 2004.[8]
  • The 126 Hoboken-Port Authority route, with some trips operated by NJ Transit and some by Academy,[9] was fully taken over by NJ Transit on September 4, 2004.[10]

The Bergen County routes (once numbered B1-B30) that were once contracted to Community Coach, a Coach USA subsidiary, were turned over to Academy Express by 2003.[citation needed]

Academy Express also operated the 972 Harmon Cove Shuttle from its introduction in 2003[11] to its replacement by new 129 trips in 2004.[12]

[edit] Scheduled Casino Service

Academy Bus operates several regularly scheduled line services to casinos in Atlantic City, NJ, and Mohegan Sun and Foxwoods Casinos in Connecticut. Academy provides service to Atlantic City, NJ from multiple locations including the Upper East Side, Upper West Side, Port Authority Bus Terminal in Midtown Manhattan, and the North and South Shores of Staten Island. In addition, service is also provided from locations within Hudson, Union, Monmouth, Ocean, Burlington, and Mercer Counties in New Jersey, and Bucks County in Pennsylvania. Academy also provides service to Mohegan Sun from Cheesequake Park-n-Ride located on the Garden State Parkway in New Jersey, the Port Authority Bus Terminal and Upper East Side of Manhattan, and from locations within Bergen County in New Jersey. Service to Foxwoods is provided from the Cheesequake Park-n-Ride in Sayreville, NJ, the Port Authority Bus Terminal and the Upper East Side in Manhattan, The Bronx and Westchester County in New York, and from locations in Connecticut.

[edit] Terminals

Academy Bus terminals are situated in the following New Jersey locations:

  • Hoboken (#22 Hillside)
  • Leonardo
  • Perth Amboy (New Jersey Transit Middlesex and Plainfield/Rutgers University operations)
  • Westampton
  • Toms River
  • Pomona
  • Secaucus (heavy maintenance facilities)

[edit] Corporate structure

Academy Bus, LLC is a limited liability corporation and holding company that owns three operating companies: Academy Lines, LLC, Academy Express, LLC, and No. 22 Hillside Corporation.[13]
