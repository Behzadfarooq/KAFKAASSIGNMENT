Academia Chilena de la Lengua

The Academia Chilena de la Lengua (Spanish for Chilean Academy of Language) it gathers a group of academics and experts on the use of the Spanish language in Chile. It's a member of the Asociación de Academias de la Lengua Española.

It was founded in Santiago de Chile on June, the 5th, 1885. It started out with 18 members designated by the Real Academia Española (Spanish Royal Academy). Nowadays it has 36 academics, plus members in several Chilean regions and abroad. The honoris causa members are: the permnent member (Gabriela Mistral), an honor member (John Paul the II) and, since 1999, an illustrious member Víctor García de la Concha).
