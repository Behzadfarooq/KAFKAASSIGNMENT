Acacia cardiophylla (West Wyalong Wattle) is an evergreen shrub that grows to around 2 to 3m tall, has feathery like leaves and bright yellow flowers during it's July to November blooming period. Acacia cardiophylla is indigenous to central and southern New South Wales. It can be found in the new Australian Garden at the Royal Botanic Gardens in Cranbourne, Victoria, Australia.
