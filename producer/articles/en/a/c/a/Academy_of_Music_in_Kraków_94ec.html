The Academy of Music in Kraków (Akademia Muzyczna w Krakowie) is located in downtown Kraków, Poland. It was first conceived as a conservatory in 1888 by Władysław Żeleński, and is known as the home of renowned contemporary Polish composers Witold Lutosławski (1913-1994) and Krzysztof Penderecki (b.1933), who in 1962 struck international fame with his piece, "Threnody to the Victims of Hiroshima".
