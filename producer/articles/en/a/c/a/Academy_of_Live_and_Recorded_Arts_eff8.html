Academy of Live and Recorded Arts

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

The Academy of Live and Recorded Arts (ALRA) is one of the most respected and prestigious drama schools in the world, located in Wandsworth, south London, England. Now including training in acting and stage management at degree and postgraduate level, it also runs introductory workshops for younger people.

[edit] History

Founded by Sorrel Carson-Hanau, a talented and individualistic actress and director and her son Mark Hanau, in 1979 under, what was considered at the time, a set of refreshingly innovative and unique teaching principles. ALRA sought to remedy what it saw as "a stale and uninspiring mire" that had descended upon the British Drama School scene. After an impressively new and exciting beginning, in the late 1990's Sorrel Carson-Hanau was forced to retire due to ill health and subsequently ALRA underwent an unfortunate trough in reputation, becoming an 'unfashionable' member of the London Drama School circuit.

In the new millennium, however, ALRA has seen a happy reinvention, with a new principal in the form of Pat Trueman leading the way by personally selecting new tutors and heads of department, many of them specifically drawn from top British Drama Schools (such as the Guildhall school, LAMDA, RADA and Drama Centre, to name but a few).

Rising phoenix-like from the flames, ALRA now exists as a remarkable school. Recently being awarded an 'astounding grade' by OFSTED- the official UK School Assessment Body (and the highest award they offer), it is one of the country's (and, indeed, the world's) most respected and prestigious Drama Schools, producing some of the industry's greatest young actors, and unsurpassed in Britain in terms of education in 'acting for camera' and 'voice'.

[edit] Acceptance

ALRA is one of the most selective of British drama schools, accepting around 30 new students each year onto its BA(hons) in Professional Acting course. However, no educational requirements need to be met and admission is based purely on suitability and successful audition, students now being handpicked by the current principal, Pat Trueman. ALRA also teaches a Stage Management Foundation Degree course that lasts 2 years, and a Post-Graduate Acting Diploma course.

[edit] Qualifications

All students completing the 3 year BA (hons) Professional Acting course successfully will be awarded the BA (Hons) in Acting (validated by the University of Greenwich) and the National Diploma in Professional Acting (validated by Trinity College London). In addition to this, students will be offered Equity membership through NCDT accreditation.

All students completing the 1 year Postgraduate Acting course successfully will be awarded the National Advanced Certificate in Professional Acting. In addition to this, students will be offered Equity membership through NCDT accreditation.

All students completing the 2 year Stage Management Foundation Degree course successfully will be awarded a National Foundation Degree in Stage Management, and will have at least one work placement with a Professional Theatre, Production Company or Technical Service Company.

[edit] Grants & Funding

The Acting courses at ALRA are, fortunately, a part of the government's Dance and Drama Award scheme, offering to bear the brunt of tuition fees and maintenance costs for those who qualify. For more information on this scheme, please see the official website, linked at the bottom of this page.

For non-acting courses, standard student loans are available.

[edit] Voice Department

The ALRA voice department, currently headed up by John Wild is considered by many to be the greatest in Britain, and arguably is the most innovative in comparison to contemporary rivals.

Drawing significantly from European teachings, the ALRA voice departments differs greatly from those of other British Drama Schools, being considerably more intensive, and emphasizing various different aspects of theory, anatomy, practise and educative technique.

The ALRA voice department is a source of considerable pride for ALRA, as well as being the envy of many rivals, and a bastion of respectability within the profession.

[edit] Acting for Camera

ALRA offers the most intense and developed education in performing for film and television out of all of the UK based Drama Schools, and is distinctly proud of this fact. Whilst most schools offer a short 2 or 3 week workshop as part of a 3 year course, ALRA offers weekly lessons with respected professionals (Directors, Producers, Camera-Men and Working Actors) in the television and film industry throughout the entirety of the course.

One of ALRA's main selling points, along with their Voice Department, the Acting for Camera aspect of the Academy's education remains at the core of their teaching's, and remains one of the most respected in Europe.

[edit] The Royal Victoria Patriotic Building

ALRA is currently housed in the historic Royal Victoria Patriotic Building. The building was originally intended for the ‘Education and Training of three hundred Orphan Daughters of Soldiers, Seamen and Marines who perished in the Russian War, and for those who hereafter may require like succour’ . Originally named as the Royal Victoria Patriotic Asylum, the building was designed by Major Rhode Hawkins in a heroically ornate Gothic style. The foundation stone was laid by Queen Victoria on 11 July 1857 and the first phase was completed in 1858 to an almost entirely symmetrical plan form. The result was judged to be ‘bold, picturesque and effective’ by The Building News (October 8,1858). The first inmates were received on 1 July 1859.

The recipient of numerous bouts of renovation and extension, the Royal Victoria Patriotic Building (RVPB) is now grade II listed. Having had many incarnations and uses since its first role in the 19th century, the RVPB currently houses ALRA; a top British Drama School, a pub/restaurant, private flats and various private studios.
