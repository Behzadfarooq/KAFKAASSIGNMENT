I was discussing this with User:Yelyos, and I realized I don't really remember much about PACE...but it was somewhat different than described here. When I was involved in it (1989-1991), there was no separate school for it, it was just one class of students from all sorts of different schools who went to a different school (St. Anthony in Thornhill) for a couple of days each week. I suppose there may have been a slightly different program for the Catholic schools (in the York Region school board, in my case), or maybe it just hadn't been set up fully yet, if this was established in 1993. The PACE Program is something altogether different and unrelated to the Academy for Gifted Children. It is an enriched program for "gifted" students, but it is entirely separate from the PACE to which the article refers.-me1322

There may be a different program with the same name, but I'm fairly certain that, in the context of the article, P.A.C.E. is just another name for the school: "P.A.C.E. is a private school that..."[2] (also note the title of the home page: "Academy for Gifted Children - PACE Home").
