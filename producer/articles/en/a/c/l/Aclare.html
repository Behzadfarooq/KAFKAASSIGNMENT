Aclare (Irish: Áth an Chláir) is a village in County Sligo, Republic of Ireland, and the main village in the parish of Tourlestrane. It straddles the border of the townlands of Lislea and Carns and is situated on the Inagh (also spelt "Eignagh") river, a tributary of the Moy.

Within eyeshot of the village is Belclare Castle, a ruined fortress of the O'Hara family.

The village, as well as the surrounding townlands, has a strong musical tradition, and is well-known for its musicians and singers, particularly in Irish traditional music.
