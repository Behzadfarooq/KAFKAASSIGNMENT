"Act Naturally" is a song written by Johnny Russell and Vonnie Morrison, originally recorded by Buck Owens, whose version reached number 1 on the Billboard Country Singles chart in 1963,[1] his first number 1.[2] In 2002, Shelly Fabian of About.com ranked the song number 169 on her list of the Top 500 Country Music Songs.[3]

The song has been covered by many other artists, including Loretta Lynn and the Beatles.[4]

[edit] The Beatles version

The Beatles covered the song in 1965 on their album Help! (in the United Kingdom) and as the B-side of the "Yesterday" single in the U.S..[5]

The Beatles' version is sung by Ringo Starr.[6] Stephen Thomas Erlewine of All Music Guide called it "an ideal showcase for Ringo's amiable vocals."[7] They performed the song during an The Ed Sullivan Show appearance which was taped on August 14, 1965 and broadcast on September 12, 1965.[8]

The Beatles recorded the song on June 17, 1965 in 13 takes. The first 12 takes were evidently used to work out the arrangement: the master was take 13, the only take with vocals. It was mixed the following day. The Beatles almost recorded a song by their engineer Norman Smith, but realized that Starr didn't yet have a vocal on Help!, and so "Act Naturally" was recorded instead, the last cover they recorded until the Get Back/Let It Be sessions in 1969.[9] Smith later had some success as a performer, using the name Hurricane Smith.[10] In 1973 his song "Oh Babe, What Would You Say?" reached 3 on the Billboard Pop Singles chart in the U.S.[11]

Capitol Records removed "Yesterday" and "Act Naturally" from the U.S. version of Help!. The two songs were included on Yesterday and Today, released in the U.S. on June 20, 1966.[12]
