Action of 12 October 1798

This battle took place on 12 October 1798 near western Ireland, after a British force intercepted and attacked a French force which was trying to land troops in Ireland.

The French force of the battleship Hoche, 8 frigates and a schooner had left Brest under Jean-Baptiste-François Bompart on 16 September 1798. On 17 September it was spotted and tailed by British frigates. Over the next week or 2, Bompart sailed west to try to give the impression that he was heading for the West Indies, but the British frigates, along with several other ships which had joined them, wouldn't leave him, despite his turning on them several times. Eventually he gave up and headed north to his true target, Lough Swilly. His mission was to land troops there which were to meet up with troops previously landed at Kilcummin Head on 22 August, but these had surrendered to a British force at Ballinamuck on 8 September, before Bompart had even left France.

On 12 October Bompart's fleet was attacked near Tory Island by a force of 3 battleships, 5 frigates and a brig-sloop under John Borlase Warren. Hoche was defeated and forced to surrender by Robust, after which the French frigates were pursued. 4 of them passed Anson firing at her in turn and damaging her significantly. 3 of the French frigates were caught that day, one 2 days later, one 6 days later by Anson and the brig-sloop Kangaroo, and one 8 days later by the frigate Fisgard. Only Romaine, Sémillante and the schooner Biche (which had left earlier) made it back to France.
