Active cancellation

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

You have new messages (last change).

Active cancellation is a theoretical military jamming system that involves the sampling of an incoming radar signal, analyzing it, then returning this signal slightly out of phase, thus "canceling" it out. While there are no official systems using this in service, it is rumored to be in use on United States Air Force stealth aircraft like the B-2, F-117, F-22A, and possibly the F-35. It is also speculated that the Thales SPECTRA aircraft protection suite also uses this system.
