Hello - and welcome to The Fancruft Manifesto!!! Bring on the diluted synth prog!!!

The aim - to assist in and to generate the proper recording of notable underground musicians and artists. This is my area of expertise, the thing I know most about and can show most about. If you have a problem with that, ask yourself how a project like Wikipedia will survive without people with the energy and enthusiasm to update, add and discuss their specialist subjects. What would you actually have?

The activity - Neil Campbell, Matthew Bower, Volcano The Bear, Alex Neilson, Lea Cummings, A Band, Simon Wickham-Smith, Vibracathedral Orchestra, Nurse With Wound, Craig Scanlon, David Jackman and, probably most to the point, an extensive upgrade for Richard Youngs; anyone having any additional info on any R!!! release, especially pre-Advent, please make thyself known.

Did a bunch of Nurse With Wound albums, should really do some more. Anyone fancy a Sugar Fish Drink?

The Fall - The Official Best Thing In The Universe Ever (hey, it's a userpage, I can opinionate...) - a few things needed done so the gaps in the studio discography have been duly plugged - Middle Class Revolt, Cerebral Caustic, The Light User Syndrome and Levitate are now there, Fall In a Hole deserved an article, as did The Twenty-Seven Points. Some corrective and additional work done to Room to Live, Dragnet and Perverted By Language amongst others; Basically, if it's an album and it's by The Fall, I've been sticking my nose in. Added the Holy Grail of Falldom, The Complete Peel Sessions 1978â2004 as well. Took ages, that did. I'm going to do a couple of those live albums (just done Live in London 1980, Live 1977 and Touch Sensitive... Bootleg Box Set, fool that I am) but I ain't doing 'em all. Live From The Vaults? Nein danke! Since individual group members are now being worked through, I added the genius that is Craig Scanlon. More to come????

The future - presently under consideration - Colin Potter, maybe Andrew Chalk (but he's so determindly underground that it would be tough to support), Chocolate Monk (label), Beta-lactam Ring Records and so on. There's always more to be said. Things change all the time....
