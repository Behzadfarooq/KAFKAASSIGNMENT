Since it is mentioned that the mother in the ending scene is Nagase, and the child is her daughter, which is mostly speculation, I feel it also worthy of mentioning (at least here, I can't say if it fits in the article itself) that many people speculate that Blaze is the father.

For: 1. Nagase is almost definitely the mother, since the hair style is quite similar. 2. Blaze is likely the father, as the game often alludes to a deeper relationship between Edge and Blaze. 3. Attractive females are rarely added to games for the sake of having attractive women, especially when the main character is male. There's usually a romance intended. 4. Since the Razgriz squadron disbanded, the military would have no rights to keeping the two from having a relationship.

Against: 1. The child is blonde, unless the sunlight is hitting her hair the right way. Both Nagase and Blaze have dark hair (the picture early in the game shows Chopper's arm covering Blaze's face, but you can see short, dark hair).

Blondness is a recessive gene. If Blaze and Edge both carry the recessive gene, there is a 1/4 chance their child could be blond. ⇒ SWATJester Ready Aim Fire! 16:47, 10 February 2006 (UTC)
Some children (my father and brothers for example) are blonde at an early age, but their hair darkens at puberty (my brothers' hair is starting turn brown, and my father's is jet black.

2. Again, it is speculation. Nothing in the game indicates that Blaze and Nagase ever became anything more than what is blatantly stated. It is left to the player's interpretation.

Take this as you will, but I can only support it with opinions. Still, if the child can be called Nagase's, then perhaps it would be wise to include this bit of speculation as well.


((Anonymous))


Sure, if it's listed as speculation.⇒ SWATJester Ready Aim Fire! 16:47, 10 February 2006 (UTC)

[edit] Ace Mode

A single missile taking you down is not true. In most planes, a single missile will annihilate you, but not in the SU-37. After taking one hit in the SU-37, my damage meter read 92%. It may have been from where I was hit, although has not happened to me in every other plane. Thought I'd bring this up, since the article states that you can only take one hit by a missile in Ace mode before dying. —The preceding unsigned comment was added by 24.153.124.246 (talk) 16:50, 9 March 2007 (UTC).

Also, the S-32 is not an SU-47 Berkut, but an S-37 Berkut http://aeroweb.lucia.it/rap/RAFAQ/six5th_5.html 24.153.124.246 16:56, 9 March 2007 (UTC)

Wrong. S- is the prefix used for all Sukhoi forward-swept wing aircraft while experimental, while T- is used for normal swept wings. The S-37 was just a name for the Su-47, they're the exact same aircraft. The S-32 was a much earlier prototype related to the S-37/Su-47 that Wikipedia doesn't have an individual article for. ZakuTalk 18:56, 9 March 2007 (UTC)
