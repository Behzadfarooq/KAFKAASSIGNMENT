So, return greetings from Fastifex and his anonymous, more experienced alter ego!

I must say I'm imazed how Anon (yes, even shortened, as if it were a dirty word) seems to be taken by some vandals as a license to abuse serious contributors, naturally rarely hindered by any knowledge of the subject, or even without reading the contribution, to simply undo any additions to their precious (often premature) brainchild, or to mark an article as, say, in need of deletion, to be moved to Wiktionary, or 'not up to standard' in stead of doing some usefull edit (refrase, move and link ...). I suppose it's somewhat naive to hope they will refrain from such abuse on account of my account status. as an Admin suggested, but still here goes the proof of the account pudding Unlike many, apparently, I feel the only thing that realy matters is content, not credits, file history, social aspects of a Wiki-'community' or some sacred cause that blinds the required objectivity.
