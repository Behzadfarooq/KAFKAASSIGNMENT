Bone: Acetabular notch
Right hip bone. External surface. (Acetabular notch visible near center, inside blue line.)
Latin i. acetabuli
Gray's subject #57 237
Dorlands/Elsevier i_05/12447218

The acetabulum presents below a deep notch, the acetabular notch, which is continuous with a circular non-articular depression, the acetabular fossa, at the bottom of the cavity: this depression is perforated by numerous apertures, and lodges a mass of fat.

The notch is converted into a foramen by the transverse ligament; through the foramen nutrient vessels and nerves enter the joint; the margins of the notch serve for the attachment of the ligamentum teres.
