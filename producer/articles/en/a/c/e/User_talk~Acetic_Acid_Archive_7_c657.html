RfC of Mario Roering and Peter Lee

Hi! I read your comments on the RfC for Mario Roering and Peter Lee. I have previously considered submitting a mediation request, but both of these users ignored my request that they agree to mediation, and I was under the impression that mediation could only procede with the consent of the users involved. Is it totally without precedent for a RfC to include two users? It seems to me that we could have filed separate RfC's, but they would be essentially duplicates of each other. I also think that it is difficult to consider the behaviour of each user separately from that of the other. Thanks, JeremyA (talk) 04:18, 26 August 2005 (UTC)

I was going to leave almost exactly the same message. Also note that both users have explicitly rejected offers of informal mediation from both myself and JeremyA and have rejected the Arbitrators authority to intervene. Process is less important than outcome remember, and most of the RfC rules are to prevent frivolous filings. This is certainly not one of those, as you will be aware from reading the evidence. Nevertheless, if you insist that the only useful way to proceed is with separate but essentially duplicative RfCs, we shall so file. -Splash 04:27, 26 August 2005 (UTC)
Thank you. Whilst I generally don't have all that much time for WP:IAR (it seems to be used in too cavalier a way for the most part), perhaps this once it is useful. Particularly since we seem to have imported a conflict from nl: and the admins there have been invited to comment here. I'm sure our processes are hair-raising enough without having to go through them twice! As you say, if either user simply refuses to acknowledge the RfC on these grounds, we will go to Arbitration. Part of the reason we didn't go direct to RfAr is because we are third-parties bringing the case and imagine the Arbitrators would want to see good evidence that we weren't just interfering but had tried to help. -Splash 16:46, 26 August 2005 (UTC)
