Ace Records (US)

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Ace Records was a record label that was started in August 1955 in Jackson Mississippi by Johnny Vincent, with Teem Records as its budget subsidiary. Its records were distributed since 1962 by Vee-Jay Records. Ace Records stopped when Vee-Jay went broke. The label was relaunched in 1971 and sold in 1997.[1]

Ace recorded such artists as Earl King, Frankie Ford, Jimmy Clanton, Huey "Piano" Smith, Joe Tex and Bobby Marchan.[2]
