ACME Communications

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

ACME Communications (NASDAQ: ACME) is a television broadcasting company that owns seven television stations. Six stations are CW Television Network affiliates. The seventh, part of a duopoly in Albuquerque, New Mexico, is a MyNetworkTV affiliate. ACME Communications also co-produces the national television show The Daily Buzz with Emmis Communications. The show is produced out of Emmis' WKCF-TV in Orlando, Florida.

[edit] Stations owned by ACME

  • KWBQ 19 - Albuquerque, New Mexico - CW
  • KASY 50 - Albuquerque, New Mexico - MNTV
  • WBDT 26 - Dayton, Ohio - CW
  • WBXX 20 - Knoxville, Tennessee - CW
  • WIWB 14 - Green Bay, Wisconsin - CW
  • WBUI 23 - Decatur, Illinois - CW
  • WBUW 57 - Madison, Wisconsin - CW

[edit] Stations formerly owned by ACME

  • KPLR 11 - St. Louis, Missouri - (sold to Tribune, 2003)
  • KWBP (now KRCW) 32 - Salem-Portland, Oregon - (sold to Tribune, 2003)
  • KUWB (now KUCW) 30 - Salt Lake City, Utah -(sold to Clear Channel, 2005)
  • KFBT (now KVCW) 33 - Las Vegas, Nevada - (sold to Sinclair Broadcast Group, 2000)
  • WTVK (now WXCW) 46/6 - Naples-Fort Myers, Florida - (sold to Sun Broadcasting, 2006)
