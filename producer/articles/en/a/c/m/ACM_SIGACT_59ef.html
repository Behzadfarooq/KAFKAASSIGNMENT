ACM SIGACT or SIGACT is the Association for Computing Machinery Special Interest Group on Algorithms and Computation Theory, whose purpose is support of research in theoretical computer science.

[edit] Publications

SIGACT publishes a quarterly print newsletter, SIGACT News. Its online version, SIGACT News Online, is available since 1996 for SIGACT members, with unrestricted access to some features.

[edit] Conferences

SIGACT sponsors a number of annual conferences.

  • COLT: Annual Workshop on Computational Learning Theory
  • IOPADS: I/O in Parallel and Distributed Systems
  • PODC: Annual ACM Symposium on Principles of Distributed Computing
  • POPL: ACM Symposium on Principles of Programming Languages
  • SCG: Annual ACM Symposium on Computational Geometry
  • SODA: ACM/SIGACT-SIAM Symposium on Discrete Algorithms
  • SPAA: ACM Annual Symposium on Parallel Algorithms and Architectures
  • STOC: Annual ACM Symposium on the Theory of Computing
  • PODS: Principles of Database Systems

[edit] Awards and prizes

  • Gödel Prize, for outstanding papers in theoretical computer science (sponsored jointly with EATCS)
  • Donald E. Knuth Prize, for outstanding contributions to the foundations of computer science (sponsored jointly with IEEE TCMFCS)
  • ACM awards co-sponsored by SIGACT
    • Paris Kanellakis Theory and Practice Award, for theoretical accomplishments of significant and demonstrable effect on the practice of computing
    • Eugene L. Lawler Award for Humanitarian Contributions within Computer Science and Informatics
  • ACM-SIGACT Distinguished Service Award
  • Danny Lewin Best Student Paper Award
  • Best Paper Award (SIGACT) for ACM STOC and IEEE FOCS conference papers
