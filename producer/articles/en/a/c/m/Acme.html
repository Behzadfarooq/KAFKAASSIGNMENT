Acme

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Acme (Greek: ακμή, the peak, zenith, prime) denotes the best of something.

Look up acme in
Wiktionary, the free dictionary.

In contemporary culture and society it is used in diverse contexts:

  • Acme Corporation is a fictional company that exists in the world of Looney Tunes cartoons. Subsequently it has been adopted widely in all forms of fiction as the archetypal name for a fictional company or corporation. (Sometimes made a backronym to: A Company (that) Makes Everything - though this is in dispute.)
  • ACME Workers' Club is a variety entertainment social club that runs in Edinburgh (Scotland) once a month on a Friday night.
  • Acme XI: The last known son of the royal family in ancient Olympia. Legend has it the lineage continues until today but is kept confidential amongst people who know.
  • ACME: An International E-Journal for Critical Geographies is a peer-reviewed, online-only academic journal founded in 2002.
  • The ACME Detective Agency is the detective organization in the Carmen Sandiego computer games. In the games, the user is always an ACME agent.
  • The Acme Packers, a name used by the Green Bay Packers American football team in their first year in the NFL, when they were sponsored by the Acme Packing Company.
  • Acme thread form is a screw thread used in specialized applications such as vises and leadscrews.
  • Acme (ADL) is an architecture description language by Carnegie Mellon University.
  • ACME (album) is the sixth album by New York City blues/indie rock group the Jon Spencer Blues Explosion.
  • Acme (band) is a German metalcore band.
  • Acme (text editor) is a text editor and development environment originally for the Plan 9 from Bell Labs operating system.
  • Acme is the Perl software language's module namespace for bizarre or humorous software packages.
  • ACME (Automated Classification of Medical Entities) is a component of the Mortality Medical Data System (MMDS).
  • Acne vulgaris is a skin condition originally called acme in Greek, but whose spelling has been corrupted.
