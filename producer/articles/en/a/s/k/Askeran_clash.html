The Askeran clash (February 22-23, 1988) was a conflict in the late 20th century during the Armenian-Azerbaijani conflict. As as result of this clash between an Azeri crowd and Armenian residents near Askeran, Azerbaijan, allegedly left two Azerbaijani's died. These deaths started rumours essentially lead to Sumgait Pogrom and the Nagorno-Karabakh War. The announcement on Azerbaijani radio of these two deaths at Askeran was the spark for two days of pogroms by Azerbaijanis against Armenian residents in the city [1]. The violence was led, to some degree, by refugees from Armenia, perhaps as many as 2000 [2] of them made even more desperate by being forced to take shelter in the appalling conditions of Sumgaits shantytown. [3]

[edit] Background

On February 22, a crowd of Azeris surrounded the local Communist Party headquarters, demanding information about rumors of an Azerbaijani having been killed in Stepanakert. They were informed that no such incident had occurred, but refused to believe it. Dissatisfied with what they were told, thousands began marching toward Nagorno-Karabakh, “wreaking destruction en route.” The authorities mobilized roughly a thousand police to stop the riots; the result was a clash in the Askeran region of Nagorno-Karabakh that left two Azerbaijanis dead, and 50 Armenian villagers, also an unknown number of Azerbaijanis and police, injured. [4] [5]

On Azerbaijani Radio, deputy attorney general Katusev reported the fact that ‘two inhabitants of the Agdam district fell victim to murder’, giving to Muslim surnames [6]. These news, according to Tamara Dragadze, were announced to show the gravity of the conflict and to calm down sentiments. [7]
