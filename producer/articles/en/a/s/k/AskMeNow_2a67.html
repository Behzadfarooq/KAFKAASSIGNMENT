AskMeNow is an Irvine, California based mobile search company that officially launched in the United States in November 2005. The company's primary offering is a consumer mobile search product which utilizes proprietary technology to offer a natural language based interaction and dynamic content provision platform. The Company generates revenues through usage fees and one to one contextual mobile advertising sponsorships.

AskMeNow also powers mobile offerings for a number of content providers such as Merriam-Webster, Encyclop√¶dia Britannica, and Guinness World Records.[1]

AskMeNow has distribution partnerships with Alltel in the US and Rogers Wireless in Canada.

[edit] How It Works

There are two ways AskMeNow consumer search product can be accessed [2]

  • SMS (Text Messaging) - AskMeNow allows users to find information on their mobile devices by sending questions via SMS messages. AskMeNow answers them using a combination of proprietary search technology and human assistance. The answers are sent back as SMS text messages, SMS to WAP messages, or email.
  • Mobile Applications - A GUI is offered via mobile applications. AskMeNow applications are offered on platforms including BlackBerry, Treo, Windows Mobile, J2ME, Symbian and Pocket PC.
