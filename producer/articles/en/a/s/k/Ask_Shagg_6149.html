Ask Shagg is a syndicated daily comic strip drawn by Peter Guren and distributed by Creators Syndicate. In each strip Guren answers questions from readers about the animal kingdom. Readers receive a free Shagg doll if their question appears in the strip.

[edit] About the Strip

Shagg E. Dawg, the furry canine hero of the Ask Shagg strip, is the authority kids turn to for all things animal. Shagg, with occasional help from pals Rosko the Cat, Mouth the Myna Bird and Slippy the Flying Squirrel, can tell you everything from what an okapi is (the only living relative of the giraffe) to what color a skunk's spray is (honey-yellow).

Both hilarious and informative, Shagg's strip helps children laugh and learn at the same time. Look out for Shagg's list of "Historical Shaggs," which includes distinguished pooches such as Rube E. Dawg, whose owner, Erno Rubik, invented the Rubik's Cube.

Ever wonder what a cicada is? You might found out in Shagg's "Animal of the Month" feature, where you'll learn not just about its drum-like organs, but Shagg will explain that it has "a face only a mother could love."

[edit] Answer Sources

With two dogs, two cats, two horses, two ponies and countless godlfish, Ask Shagg creator Peter Guren doesn't have to go too far to find firsthand answers to readers' animal questions.

Of course, if Guren gets stumped, he is just an Internet away for up-to-date reference material to dig through -- even the toughest questions from pensive 9-year-olds become creative cartoons with all the right answers.
