ASD Simplified Technical English (STE) is a Controlled Language writing standard for aerospace maintenance documentation. The earliest version was created by the Dutch aircraft company Fokker in the 1980's, but the European Association of Aerospace Manufacturers (AECMA) adapted it for general use as an international standard. Until 2005, the standard was known as AECMA Simplified English. At that time, AECMA became part of the Aerospace and Defence Industries Association of Europe (ASD).

The STE standard is specified in ASD Specification STE100, which is maintained by the Simplified Technical English Maintenance Group (STEMG). The standard contains a set of restrictions on the grammar and style of procedural and descriptive text. It also contains a dictionary of roughly 1000 approved general words. Writers are given guidelines for adding technical names and technical verbs to their documentation. STE is mandated by several commercial and military specifications that control the style and content of maintenance documentation.
