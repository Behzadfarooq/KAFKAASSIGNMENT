Asriel86, aka Aaron Dunlap, aka me is an online journalist. I write for the online gaming website Gaming Horizon.

I was born March 17th, 1986 in Pontiac, Michigan. I attended a private school, Springfield Christian Academy, through elementary and Oakland Christian School through junior high. Not satisfied with the limited educational facilities of most private Christian schools, I transferred to Clarkston High School, a public school, in Clarkston, Michigan. There, I took special interest in writing and computer science classes.

I currently attend the University of Michigan in Flint, Michigan, and am majoring in English with a minor in Computer Science.

[edit] Gaming Horizon

In 2002, I applied for and received a position at the volunteer-staffed Gaming Horizon. I've written several reviews, all archived at [1] and some editorial features, including:

  • Phantom: Rewound, detailing the history of Infinium Lab's Phantom game system.
  • Take My Life...Seriously, an editorial taking a look at gamer stereotypes in the media.
  • God Mode, an editorial regarding games that are overly complicated or difficult.
  • E3, I Knew Thee Well, dealing with the decline of luster in E3.

The day before the 2004 Electronic Entertainment Expo, I published the first-ever-seen (I swear) photographs of the controversial "Phantom" game system here. The next day, once the E3 expo began, I wrote another popular article with further Phantom features and a hands-on report.

[edit] Blog

I made my own website once I started at Gaming Horizon to act as a portfolio of my writing. In mid-2004, I transformed the site into my own blog. There, I usualy post updates about his daily life as well as essays or any type of content I feel like. My website still acts as a portfolio of my writing, as well as some amature photography.
