Assad class corvette

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

You have new messages (last change).

The Assad Class Corvette were originally built for Iraq during the Iran-Iraq War, by Fincantieri in Italy. They were completed just before Operation Desert Storm, and were never delivered beacause of the UN arms embargo. Six ships were ordered in 1981. Four ships were sold to the Malaysian Navy as Laksamana Class Corvettes in 1995. The two remaining ships were laid up in La Spezia from 1990, but in 2005 it was announced they would finally be delivered to the New Iraqi Navy.[1]
