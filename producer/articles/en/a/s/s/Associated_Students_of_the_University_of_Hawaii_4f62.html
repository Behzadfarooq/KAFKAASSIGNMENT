The Associated Students of the University of Hawaiʻi (ASUH) is the undergraduate student government representing all full-time, classified, undergraduate students at the University of Hawaiʻi at Mānoa. ASUH was chartered by the University of Hawaiʻi Board of Regents in 1912 and is now in its 94th year of serving and representing students. ASUH strives to serve students by advocating on their behalf with various entities, including the university administration, faculty, staff, community groups and government officials. ASUH also serves students by utilizing ASUH student fee money to fund diversified student programs and events on-campus.

[edit] Leadership and structure

The Associated Students of the University of Hawaiʻi at Mānoa leadership consists of four officers: President, Vice President, Treasurer and Secretary. The Executive Committee and General Senate are elected by the constituents of ASUH. The Executive Committee comprises the four officers, the four senators-at-large, and all standing committee chairpersons. The General Senate comprises the elected officers, the senators-at-large, and senators representing their individual colleges.

In April 2006, Grant Teichman was re-elected President of the ASUH during Spring 2006 elections. Teichman is only the second President to be re-elected to a second term in the past three decades. The ASUH officers are President Grant Teichman; Vice President Jesse Jones; Treasurer Shahla Hirahara; Secretary Dovid Kanarfogel.

Vacancies will be filled during Special Elections in the fall.
