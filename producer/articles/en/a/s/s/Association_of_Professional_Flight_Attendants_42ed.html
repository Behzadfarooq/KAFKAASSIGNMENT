The Association of Professional Flight Attendants (APFA) is the largest independent flight attendant union in the United States representing the U.S.-based flight attendants of American Airlines. APFA union headquarters are located in Euless, Texas. The foreign national flight attendants of American Airlines, based in Bogot√°, Buenos Aires, Lima, and Santiago de Chile, have their own unions. For example, Sindicato de Tripulantes de Cabina de American Airlines (STCAA) represents the Santiago-based crews. [[1]]

After American Airlines acquired TWA in 2001 the APFA "Seniority Integration Agreement" stapled all of the TWA flight attendants to the bottom of the seniority list regardless of how many years of service they had. This agreement was drawn up strictly between the APFA and American Airlines without the consultation or consent of the TWA union. In fact, the airline forced the TWA flight attendants to give up the only protection they had in their contract in the event of such an acquisition.

In 2001 American Airlines merged with TWA and to meet their promises of "fair and equitable treatment" AMERICAN AIRLINES provided a facilitator to integrate the flight attendants into their work force. But the Association of Professional Flight Attendants (APFA), representing American's flight attendants, refused to meet with him. As a result the simple fact is that there was no integration of the seniority lists and the APFA stapled the TWA flight attendants to the bottom of the seniority list. And when the cuts came, they came from the bottom up, no matter how long they had worked at TWA. The result - 100% of TWA Flight Attendants (4,200) were furloughed as of July 2, 2003.
