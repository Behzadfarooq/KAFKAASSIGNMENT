Made the Article Neutral - how does one get the alert that this is "spam" removed? Burns 21:15, 17 January 2007 (UTC)

It doesn't appear entirely neutral. The {{advert}} tag is there to alert other editors and users that the article may not present a neutral point of view. The note at the bottom that editors should "Mark blatant advertising for speedy deletion with {{db-spam}}" is not there to suggest that this article is spam. Rather, it exists to let others know that if they think it is spam that they should use the db-spam tag instead. As for neutrality, it would seem like this article does indeed read like an advertisement. All of the text implies that this association has done great things, which is not bad in itself, but there is no balance. In the 19 year it has existed, has there been no criticism of the ACTE? Have they never failed at something they attempted? The text as it is gives no indication that that is the case and therefore seems to be more of an advertisement or an article praising the association. It doesn't read like an encyclopedia article. I'm not saying you have to go dig deep for the failures and criticism if it's not there. Hope these suggestions help. You can also view WP:NPOV for a review on the NPOV policy if that helps. --Rkitko 02:39, 18 January 2007 (UTC)
