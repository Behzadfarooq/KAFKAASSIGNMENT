Thank you for expressing interest in joining the AMA!

With the exception of Arbitrators, anyone may join who is interested in resolving disputes and reducing conflict on Wikipedia. Advocates should be willing to comply with the guidelines stated.

[edit] How to join

First add yourself to the Members List using the {{AMA-listing}} template and inform the Coordinator that you have joined.

Second, be sure to completely read over the Guide to Advocacy and the AMA Handbook. These documents are full of good advice and experienced knowledge about the AMA.

Third, although optional, set yourself up with the AMA Alerts system. This way you can be contacted when there are cases that need assistance, as well as be informed of general announcements from the Association.

[edit] Meetings

From time to time, the AMA has Meetings which are announced over the AMA Alerts system and all AMA Members are encouraged to voice their opinions, suggest and vote on issues that come up. For more information, visit the main Meeting Page and Meeting Proposals area.

[edit] Userboxes

There are two userboxes that you can use to indicate both your membership and your current case status. Please read up on them below:

[edit] AMA Alerts

AMA Alert posted: There are 15 cases that need Advocacy (view by date filed). A discussion on how to restructure and improve the AMA is going on at AMA talk page and meeting points are being drafted for the next Meeting. Everyone is invited to help build a consensus as to how to improve and correct issues with the AMA.

The quickest form of coordinating communication within the Association is the AMA Alerts board. To subscribe add the {{AMA alerts}} template to the top of your talk page. When an Alert is posted, it will display it in an orange box, and when no alerts are present it becomes invisible.
