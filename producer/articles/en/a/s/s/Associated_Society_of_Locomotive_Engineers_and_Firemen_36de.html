The Associated Society of Locomotive Engineers and Firemen (ASLEF) is one of two trade unions representing train drivers in the United Kingdom (the other is the National Union of Rail, Maritime and Transport Workers). Its current general secretary is Keith Norman. ASLEF is part of the International Transport Workers' Federation and the European Transport Workers' Federation.

The union was founded in February 1880 when a group of 56 drivers and footplate staff broke away from the Amalgamated Society of Railway Servants following the failure of the 'all-grades' ASRS to support drivers who had had their wages cut and their working hours extended by the Great Western Railway in 1879. Despite the problems and divisiveness posed by breakaway unionism, ASLEF was a progressive union and supported the Independent Labour Party.

A relatively small union (18,500 members in 2007), ASLEF is extremely effective in protecting its members' interests, thanks to the very high degree of support it receives from its members, which has often led to its being characterised in the press as a "militant left-wing" union. In the period immediately after the privatisation of British Rail in the mid-1990s ASLEF succeeded in negotiating spectacularly improved pay and conditions of service for its members from the new train operating companies; Sunday has never formed part of the basic working week for train drivers in the UK, being worked as overtime. In the late BR period, drivers received a fairly low basic salary of around £12,000 per annum, supplemented by an extremely complicated series of enhancements for overtime. Thus many drivers had to work Sundays in order to make a good income. Post privatisation, ASLEF negotiated amended terms such that train drivers are now among the best-paid blue-collar workers in the UK, with the average salary in 2007 being over £30,000 for a basic 35-hour week – often worked over four days rather than five, with the result that few drivers now need to work overtime and some companies, particularly Central Trains, have experienced difficulty in staffing trains on Sundays.
