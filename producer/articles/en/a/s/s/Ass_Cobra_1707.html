Ass Cobra is the Norwegian band Turbonegro's 1996 album, their third full-length one. It was first released on Amphetamine Reptile Records in Europe in early May, then on Boomba Records in Germany and in 1997 on Sympathy For The Record Industry in the USA. It was re-released in 1998 on Bitzcore Records (Germany) and on Get Hip Records (USA) and in 2003 on Epitaph Records (USA) and Burning Heart Records (Sweden).

[edit] The sound

Ass Cobra featured a move towards mid-70's punk & glam territory - in the spirit of proto punk pioneers á la The Dictators, The Ramones or late Iggy & The Stooges - plus a bit of New York Dolls' travesty thrown in. Ass Cobra paves the way harshly, with a sinister wash of cymbals and loud guitars, articulate riffs, and stellar songwriting — think Poison Idea meets Alice Cooper. It is here, also, where Turbonegro developed their signature look: sailor caps, denim from head to toe, mustaches all around, and decidedly butch homoerotic accoutrements. Though Turbonegro did quite a bit of recording prior to this album, this was the one that set the pace, and the one that enabled them to record their masterpiece, Apocalypse Dudes. Moshable magazin remarked: "The very best in obnoxious drunk punk, already a classic buy-or-die thing!"

[edit] Miscellanea

  • "Just Flesh" begins with a prank phone call by Happy-Tom where he calls what is presumed to be Norway's Progress Records under the guise of being Steve Ignorant of Crass. Tom (as Steve) becomes hostile with the employee over royalties owed for his contributions to an Israelvis record, resulting in the classic line: "See I'm sitting here, contemplating a bit, and I wonder...where's the fucking cash?"
  • The album sleeve is a parody of The Beach Boys' Pet Sounds album cover. Charles Manson also employed this design after he recorded an hour's worth of music and improvisation from his cell for what would become 'Live at San Quentin'.
  • The first European pressings had a sleeve misprint which listed "Screwed & Tattooed" as being one of the included tracks when in fact it was a bonus track for SFTRI's edition. Some say only about 250 escaped destruction. This edition came with 'Hank wrapped in a snake' poster.
  • Sympathy For The Record Industry's edition tray card features an alternate image of a bloody prison cell instead of the cold nude man walking as seen on other versions.
  • All Bitzcore Records colored vinyls have been pressed between 100 and 200 copies. Also came with 'Hank n' snake' poster.
  • The Get Hip Records editions were almost always different in one way or another from its German counter-part. This strictly 'limited American only pressing' for Get Hip Records was on yellow/greenish wax.
  • The 2003 Epitaph Records version contains mpeg videos from their performance in Kristiansand, Norway at Quart Festival '02.
  • The 2003 Burning Heart Records version comes with Digipak and contains enhanced video clips of "Denim Demon" and an exclusive live-take of "The Midnight NAMBLA", shot at Quart festival 2002.
