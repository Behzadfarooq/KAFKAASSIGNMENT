[edit] Rationale for citations

Most political and other assassinations are very difficult to pin on the culprits unless an impartial court case settles the issue like in Rajiv Gandhi murder case. Even then the there are reasonable questions as to whether innocent Sri Lankan Tamils were scapegoats to apolitical process as the real culprits are outside the jurisdiction of Indian law at present. The author of the Tamilnation.org article is self admittedly a pro-LTTE writer. He took a list of 47 people that a researcher from Peradeniya University published and whittled it down to 40 thus admitting at least 40 were the handiwork of the LTTE. He went to argue that at least Sam Thambimuttu in the list is more notorious than prominent. I have used the 40 from the list as the base line for this article and have added other notorious and prominent Tamils assassinated by the LTTE from neutral sources. RaveenS

[edit] Two accuracy questions

1. The word "assassination" is confined to the killing of rulers. In the modern context, it might at most be used for the killing of a government minister or even a government MP. All other killings are simply "murders." I therefore wonder if the title of the page is accurate when many of those listed as assassinated had no connection to the government.

Is there is another list of murders in Wikipedia ? Please look for one and if available then we can modify the name if there is other such listsRaveenS
I checked it, we dont have any list of murders only assasination see category Category
Assassinations
see http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Assasinations,
Assassination is the murder of a political figure or other important individual. An added distinction between assassination and other forms of killing is that an assassin usually has an ideological or political motivation, though many assassins (especially those who are not part of an organised movement) also show elements of insanity. Other motivations may be money (as in the case of a contract killing), revenge, or as a military operation.Dutugemunu 06:47, 26 March 2007 (UTC)

2. Sam Thambimuththu, is a civilian who represented the EPRLF in parliament as an MP after the EPRLF gave up arms. He is listed as an "assassinated" "militant." This is not only inaccurate in terms of both terms but also gives the impression that he was a government man toting a gun. He never was a government man and never did carry a gun. He stood in opposition to the government for Tamil rights and for ordered government. Erbear 16:34, 21 October 2006 (UTC)
