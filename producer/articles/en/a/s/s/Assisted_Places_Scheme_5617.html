Assisted Places Scheme

The Assisted Places Scheme was a scheme established in the UK by the Conservative government in 1980 by which children who could not afford to go to fee-paying independent schools were provided with free or subsidised places if they were able to pass the school's entrance examination.

Claiming the practice to be elitist and wasteful of public funds, the Labour government of Tony Blair abolished the Assisted Places Scheme upon its election in 1997. However, it allowed children who were already in receipt of an assisted place to complete the remainder of the current phase of their education. The result of abolition has been to reduce the social range of pupils that are educated at independent schools, though many have taken steps to provide their own funding for pupils from poorer backgrounds.

A successor to the Assisted Places Scheme, known as the Open Access Scheme, has been established and financed by the Girls' Day School Trust in partnership with the Sutton Trust. It has gained support from prominent Conservatives, including David Willetts, the current Shadow Education Secretary.
