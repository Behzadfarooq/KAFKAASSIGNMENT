[edit] Why say Assamese are Ahom?

I notice that the beginning of the article makes a statement "people who are Assamese in descent" however, to my surprise, the word "Assamese" in the sentence is wiki-linked to Ahom which should definitely not be the case. Not all Assamese people are Ahom. I would like to change that sentence/statement but would appreciate any feedback before I make the change. Deepraj 10:26, 14 March 2007 (UTC)

[edit] Assamese is not a linguistic category

The Assamese identity is a complex identity, not necessarily linguistic. Here is Debojit Saha calling himself Assamese.[3] He says: "It gives me immense pride to be an Assamese. No matter where I stay, my heart is always in Assam, 'mur moromor ghar...' .‚Äù The "Assamese" people seem to have no problem with that, and neither does he. The same goes with Seema Biswas. She too has called herself Assamese. So the "Assamese" and "Bengali" categories should not be taken as mutually exclusive. It was suggested that this category be removed to avoid addressing this confusing issue. If the consensus is to retain this category, then so be it. Let us trudge through who is an Assamese and who is not. This becomes very tricky is some cases. Chaipau 09:44, 27 March 2007 (UTC)

Agreed with Chaipau. Let me cite some more example of the complexity of the Assamese identity.
(1) Some Bodo people like to regard themselves as Assamese, whereas some don't.
(2) Unlike the Bodos, some other ethno-linguistic groups like Karbi, Deuri, Rabha, Mising, Ahom, Rajbonshi etc consider themselves integral part of the Assamese identity.
(3) Just like Debojit Saha and Seema Biswas, most Bengali-speakers born and broght up in Assam call themselves "Assamese" along with "Bengali". The same goes with the Nepalis, Biharis and other 'people' of Assam. They are as fluent in Assamese as in their respective languages.

-Bikram98 21:41, 27 March 2007 (UTC)
