Association of Greater Manchester Local Authorities

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

The Association of Greater Manchester Local Authorities (or AGMLA) is an association which represents the ten local authorities of Greater Manchester, of North West England. It was created in 1986 after the Greater Manchester County Council was abolished.

The AGMLA seeks to act as a single voice for Greater Manchester, and also seeks to promote co-ordination on strategic issues affecting the county. It also provides some services across Greater Manchester, and also funds the Greater Manchester County Records Office.

AGMLA is intending to have a permanent office in Brussells to represent Greater Manchester at a European level.[1]

In recent years the AGMLA has invited representative from local unitary authorities in North West England to be associate members. These are Blackpool, Blackburn with Darwen and Warrington.
