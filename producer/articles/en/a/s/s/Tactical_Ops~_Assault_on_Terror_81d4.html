Tactical Ops: Assault on Terror is an online multiplayer computer game, comparable to Counter-Strike. Started as an Unreal Tournament (S.W.A.T. 1.0 beta to S.W.A.T. 1.1, Tactical Ops 1.2 to 2.2) mod, it soon became available as a retail version, which was released in April 2002 in the United States. The retail version is a standalone which does not require Unreal Tournament and contains additional maps not found in the free download.

The game is developed by Kamehan Studios, the last version being 3.5.0.
In 2004, fans of the game got permission from Atari to create a patch. This version (3.5.0) was released on March 16, 2005. According to CSports.net, Tactical Ops is one of the 20 most popular online games.

[edit] Reception

Some would consider it to be a Counter-strike clone but it was actually praised for its improved graphics, due mainly to the superiority of the Unreal engine over the aging Goldsrc engine used for Half-Life. Nonetheless, there was criticism that many maps were undetailed, and that there were only 3 player models in the initial version, with skins being used to distinguish between different special forces and terrorists. In general most of the maps in the retail version far exceeded the quality of maps in Unreal Tournament, and as such many of its developers went on to develop other retail games[citation needed], such as Shag moving to Epic Games[citation needed].

[edit] Sequel

On April 1, 2005 Tactical Operations: Crossfire was announced, an unofficial sequel to Tactical Ops created by people from both the original TO team, the 3.5 patch, and TOST (Tactical Ops Serveradmin Tool, tool that helps catch cheaters). The first public beta was released on April 28, 2006. Tactical Operations: Crossfire is a free mod for Unreal Tournament 2004. So far, there are no plans to release the game in a stand-alone package.

While the community was unsure of its success in the early versions (beta to 1.2), the release of 1.5 has shown a surge in players as well as more solid gameplay[citation needed]. Crossfire relied mainly on the newer and better graphics to attract players, but with the fixed bugs, refined gameplay, as well as other eye candy, Crossfire is clearly becoming the mod to play for Unreal Tournament 2004. The TOC developers have worked hand-in-hand with the community to improve the fun factor and fluidness of the game. The upcoming release of the 2.0 patch and its addition of an anti-cheat component should secure TOC's place in the successful mods of UT2k4.

The names in parentheses are used by the game in the single-player mode (for legal reasons) but are almost universally modified by other software on multiplayer servers for accuracy; thus they are rarely seen online.
