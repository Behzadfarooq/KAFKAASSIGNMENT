Association of Mouth and Foot Painting Artists of the World

The Association of Mouth and Foot Painting Artists of the World was founded in Liechtenstein in 1956. It supports artists who, for reasons of illness, accident or congenital disability have no use of their hands, and who use either their mouth or feet to create their work.

With its motto of "No Pity Please", the Association maintains high standards of artistic ability, and members are admitted on the basis of the quality of their work, not their physical disability. Prominent members include Alison Lapper and Simona Atzori.

The Association only accepts those with significant artistic talent as members. The personal abilities of each individual artist remains in the foreground, not his or her handicap. In this way the association is able to assist artists in being able to find buyers and supporters for their work on the international market.

The Association acquires the reproduction rights of a given artwork and distributes it internationally through their partner publishers as an art print, a postcard or a calendar. The original works are sold at exhibitions held throughout the world.

The organization was founded when seventeen mouth and foot painting artists got together in 1956 in order to make their mutual idea come true ˜ - an organization that would stand by handicapped artists and get them the recognition they deserved as artist as well as financial independence.

The AMFPA represents 650 mouth and foot painting artists in 60 countries around the world. They receive grants or are taken on as members and receive a regular income for their artistic proficiency.

The 45 year history of the Association has been filled with exhibitions, honors, and public distinctions which were followed by the media. Works produced by the organization can be seen in prominent locations, such as the UN Headquarters in Geneva or the European Council in Strassburg.

[edit] A. Erich Stegman

The first president of the association - A. Erich Stegman - was a prominent figure in the development of Mouth and Foot Painting. Born in 1912 in Darmstadt, Germany, Stegman lost the use of both arms and hands from polio at the age of two. Regardless, his artistic talents were soon recognized and supported by his teachers. He was able to paint and spatula in oil at the same degree as he mastered water colors. He could draw on stone with a feather or an engraving tool as well as make linoleum cuts, wood cuts and sculptures -- all of which he did with the tool in his mouth.

He was educated at the School of Higher Education for Book Trade and Graphics in Nuremberg and studied with the artists Erwin von Kormöndy and Hans Gerstacker. He made a living selling his art cards and prints at marketplaces. After the Second World War Stegmann established his own publishing company with the poignant name "Dennoch" ("Anyway").

Stegmann always reached out to other mouth and foot painters and in 1953/54 he organized the group "Lodge of Mouth and Foot Painting Artists" as a type of artists community. From there, the international "Association of Mouth and Foot Painting Artists" was formed and A. E. Stegmann was voted its president for life.
