[edit] Interpretation of their opposition to inappropriate automated speed limit enforcement

Let's analyse the following paragraph from the article and see if it is worthy of inclusion:

The ABD state that they are opposed to inappropriate automated speed limit enforcement, arguing for objective criteria to be used in establishing limits and enforcement policies in their manifesto[8], but this is widely seen as an evasion and interpreted as opposition to all automated speed limit enforcement, or even that they actually oppose speed limits.[9]

  • Up to the first footnote reference ([8]) the sentence appears to be reflecting their stated position.
  • The next bit ", but this is widely seen as" appears to be a text-book example of weasel words, as described in WP:AWW. A more acceptable form would be something like ". Journalist XXX, in his weekly column in The YYY newspaper, suggests that this is...".
  • The next bit "an evasion". Evading what? It isn't clear at all what is meant by this.
  • The next bit "and interpreted as opposition to all automated speed limit enforcement". Who specifically interprets their stated position as meaning this and where is the supporting citation, quote the precise sentence from the article to clarify this. I couldn't glean that from the cited article, so couldn't fix the problem.
  • The last bit "or even that they actually oppose speed limits". This is even more bizarre and an equally unsupported assertion.

This paragraph is, in my opinion, POV-inspired, and not worthy of inclusion. It needs a serious clean-up, with all opinions clearly and verifiably attributed, or the whole lot should be removed. Remembering that original research is also excluded from articles - see WP:NOR, and paraphrasing, or "reading between the lines" of the cited article may well be construed as original research, so is probably best avoided.-- de Facto (talk). 13:31, 9 August 2006 (UTC)
