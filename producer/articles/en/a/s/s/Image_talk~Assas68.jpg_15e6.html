[edit] Copyright status unclear

There's no information about copyright status on the source page for the image. I've reproduced the entire statement here because the Web site's copyrights page (http://www.thehighchaparral.com/copyright.htm) is somewhat vague about rights to the images used on the site.

The mission of this website is to honor the work of the cast and crew of The High Chaparral and to encourage the legitimate and authorized release of the DVDs of this show for the many fans around the world who are eager to purchase them. We try to maintain as accurate an archive as possible and we include other Western and historical resources that fans who enjoy the realism of The High Chaparral are likely to appreciate. No one benefits financially in any way from this site. While not an officially sanctioned site, the Powers That Be who represent the copyright holders of The High Chaparral are well aware of it.

The majority of the photos on the site were taken from the television show or by set photographers. The story lines came from NBC press kits. Character highlights, character descriptions, and synopses were written by fans specifically for this site. Articles came from a variety of publications. With the exception of the brief vignette above, we don't offer fanfiction, but instead have links to other sites that do. Material is attributed whenever possible, but much of what is contained in this web site is still under the copyright protection of someone other than ourselves. We are honest and upfront about this, and we sincerely hope that all site visitors understand that we do not wish to profit from or discredit The High Chaparral or any other copyright holders in any way. If the owners of any material on this site are not attributed and would like to be, or would like their material removed, please let us know and we will respond promptly.

We actively discourage commercial trafficking in unauthorized copies of The High Chaparral video tapes or DVDs and whenever we become aware of them, we report infringements to the attorney for Bonanza Ventures, Inc. For information on spotting bootleggers go to the DVD Info page. We continue to encourage the legitimate commercial release of The High Chaparral on DVD and remain part of an eager and ready market whenever it becomes available. For more information on how you can help in these efforts, visit the DVD Lobby page. Until we are successful in our DVD efforts, our goal is simply to honor the many contributions - large and small - that went into making this show the legend that it still is and to keep that legend alive for The High Chaparral fans around the world.

Questions about copyright issues regarding The High Chaparral can be addressed to:

Because I still think Fair Use applies in this instance, I've left the image in place. But I've made inquiry via e-mail to both the webmaster and the attorney, and will act according to the results. --Chris 17:59, 14 July 2006 (UTC)
