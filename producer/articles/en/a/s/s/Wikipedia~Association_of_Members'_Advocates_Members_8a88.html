Below is a complete list of the members of the AMA and their current case status. Some members have even formed AMA Teams that specialize in certain fields, that compliment their abilities or to help with difficult cases.

If an Advocate is available and you wish to make a direct request, simply click on one of their preferred methods of contact. When making a direct request, be sure to be a complete as possible in explaining your problem, providing the Advocate with links to the article, talk, and user pages in question.

Once a request is received, the Advocate will (as soon as they are able) make a decision whether or not to take your case. If they cannot, re-file your request either with another available Advocate, or post it on AMA Requests for Assistance.

Filing a direct request with multiple available Advocates at the same time is considered bad form and is discouraged and in the Association's experience will delay you obtaining an Advocate.
