The AVP (Association of Volleyball Professionals) was founded in 1983 to cater to the sport of beach volleyball, its players, and fans. In 2001, after a revamp, the organization took over the beach volleyball tour in Manhattan Beach, California. With this move, the AVP became the focal point around which professional beach volleyball in the United States revolves. The international counterpart to the AVP is the FIVB.

The AVP represents professional beach volleyball via tours that are broadcast over television networks like NBC and Versus. The Nissan Championship tour began on September 3, 2005.

The AVP is led by Leonard Armato, the commissioner of the AVP.

At all tournament finals, the players are introduced by Chris McGee, commonly known as "Geeter."

The top players on the 2005 AVP tour were Jake Gibb / Stein Metzger on the men's side and Kerri Walsh-Jennings / Misty May-Treanor on the women's side.

Currently, the top players on the 2006 AVP tour are Phil Dalhausser/Todd Rogers on the men's side. Misty May-Treanor/Kerri Walsh-Jennings continue to be the top players on the women's side. The rivals, respectively are Mike Lambert/Stein Metzger on the men's side and Rachel Wacholder/Elaine Youngs on the women's side.

The current sponsor of the 2006 AVP tour is Crocs.

In late 2006, the AVP got into a dispute with the California Coastal Commission with renting out beach space for events in Hermosa Beach, Manhattan Beach, Huntington Beach, and Santa Barbara.

[edit] Official Anthem

"We Are the AVP" is the official anthem of the AVP. It was written by AVP player Mike Lambert. Lambert has played the song at many AVP tournaments. Its lyrics include:

We are the AVP.
It's a sexy sport,
On a little little court,
The AVP.

We've got Chicago
And Manhattan,
Comin' live and direct
On NBC.

It's an Olympic Sport,
We've got the matching shorts,
We are the AVP.
