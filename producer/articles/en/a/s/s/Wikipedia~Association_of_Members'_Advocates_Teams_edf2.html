Below is a complete list AMA Teams (AMAT), their current case status, and their roster of members. AMAT members must first be listed as individual advocates but do not necessarilly need to be accepting individual cases. If an AMA Team is available and you wish to make a direct request, simply click on one of their preferred methods of contact. The advantages of a team is that you get the advice of several specialized people. When making a direct request, be as complete as possible in explaining your problem, and provide the AMAT with links to the article, talk, and user pages in question.

Similar to individual advocates, once a request is received, an AMAT will (as soon as they are able) make a decision whether or not to take your case. If they cannot, pease re-file your request with either another available AMAT, another individual advocate, or post it on AMA Requests for Assistance.

Filing a direct request with multiple available advocates or AMAT's at the same time is considered bad form. It is discouraged and in the "Association's experience" will delay your effective time in obtaining help.

[edit] Founding and joining an AMAT

Only active AMA members may form an AMA Team (AMAT). Make sure you are listed on the regular AMA members' list prior to forming or joining otherwise your request may be removed. If you only wish to be part of a team clearly state this in your individual application and place status to "unavailable". If you wish to join an already existing team, make sure they are accepting new members and contact the team by their prefered method. AMAT's should comply with the guidelines stated for individual advocates. There are no requirements to have an administrator in your team however as a team leader you may be interested to request Admin coaching. Again, to join a team, add yourself to the regular AMA members list.

Next, be sure to announce your intentions that you wish to either form a team (Proposed AMATs) or join a team (Members looking for a team). You may use the {{AMA-listing}} template and then contact the interim AMAT Coordinator.

Teams must consist of at least 3 users prior to being placed in Wikipedia:Association of Members' Advocates/Teams#Current AMATs section.

[edit] Userboxes

Teams may wish to create their own user boxes. Assides from the regular AMA templates there are currently no userboxes that you can use to indicate your membership to a team.

[edit] AMA Alerts

Larger Teams may wish to form their own method of communication based on a similar system used by the AMA Alerts. The quickest form of coordinating communication within the Association is the AMA Alerts board. There are currently no teams that utilise this process.
