Assumption Catholic Secondary School is a Catholic high school in Burlington, Ontario.

[edit] School population

The current school population is around 1200 students for the 2006-2007 school year. This population has been steadily increasing every year as a result of the Burlington's continuous growth. In the summer of 2006, portables were added in order to deal with the increasing enrollments. Assumption receives most new students from local elementary schools including St. Raphael, St. Paul, St. Patrick, St. John, Ascension, and Holy Rosary.

Assumption, like all publicly funded Catholic schools in Ontario, also accepts non-Catholics into the school; however, they are expected to take part in the religious activities and mandatory religious classes.

The Catholic school's parishes are Holy Rosary, St. Raphael's, St. John's, and St. Patrick's. It belongs to the Diocese of Hamilton, under Bishop Anthony Tonnos.

[edit] Academics

Assumption prides itself in having a strong degree of academic success. Honor Roll students are honored with an annual Honor Roll Breakfast, and Academic Awards are also given to the students with the highest mark in each course and to overall outstanding students who are active in the school and community.

[edit] Athletics

Assumption offers a wide variety of sports teams. The main rivalries for Assumption are with nearby Nelson High School and Catholic rivals Notre Dame. Assumption honors their best athletes with awards that are given out at the annual Athletic Banquet.
