Association for the Scientific Study of Consciousness

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Founded in 1994, the Association for the Scientific Study of Consciousness (commonly referred to as the ASSC) is a professional membership organization that aims to encourage research on consciousness in cognitive science, neuroscience, philosophy, and other relevant disciplines in the sciences and humanities, directed toward understanding the nature, function, and underlying mechanisms of consciousness. In 2007 the executive committee of the association was composed as follows: Michael Gazzaniga (President), Daniel Dennett (Past-President), Alan Cowey (President-Elect); and six Members-at-Large Axel Cleeremans, Christof Koch, Alva NoÃ«, Thomas Metzinger, Susana Martinez-Conde, and Patrick Wilken (who also acts as Executive Director).

[edit] Activities

Since 1997, the ASSC has organised annual conferences to promote interaction and spread knowledge of scientific and philosophical advances in the field of consciousness research. In 2006 the tenth annual meeting was held at St. Anne's College, Oxford, from June 23rd-26th. The key organizers for the 2006 meeting were Geraint Rees and Patrick Wilken. The eleventh meeting is being organized by Susana Martinez-Conde and Stephen Macnick and will be held at the Imperial Palace Hotel in Las Vegas between the 22nd and 25th of June 2007. The 2008 meeting is under the planning of Allen Houng, and will be held between the 23rd and 26th of May in Taipei. The meeting cycle will then revert to as yet an undecided location in Europe.

In addition to organizing annual meetings, the association promotes the academic study of consciousness in a number of other ways:

  • As of late 2007, the association intends to relaunch and publish the open-access journal Psyche.
  • the society also publishes occasional edited books on selected topics. To date three books have been published: (2005) in Steven Laureys: Progress in Brain Research, The boundaries of consciousness: neurobiology and neuropathology. Elsevier. ISBN 0-444-51851-7. ; (2003) in Axel Cleeremans (Ed.): The Unity of Consciousness: Binding, Integration, and Dissociation. Oxford University Press. ISBN 0-19-850857-3. ; and (2000) in Thomas Metzinger: The Neural Correlates of Consciousness: Empirical and Conceptual Questions. MIT Press. ISBN 0-262-13370-9. ;
  • the society awards the annual William James Prize for an outstanding published contribution to the empirical or philosophical study of consciousness by a graduate student or postdoctoral scholar within five years of receiving a PhD or other advanced degree.
