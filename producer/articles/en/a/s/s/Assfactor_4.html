Assfactor 4

Assfactor 4 was a hardcore punk band from Columbia, South Carolina formed by two members of Tonka (Jay and Alex) and two members of Unherd (Eric and Kevin). They formed in Fall 1992 and broke up in late 1997. Assfactor 4's sonic approach drew heavily from San Diego's early-1990's group Heroin, but their song structure was notably more akin to 1980's thrash and hardcore, placing Assfactor 4 in a unique position within the DIY emo-hardcore scene of the era. Their contemporaries in the southeast U.S. included Columbia's Eurich, Premonition, Rights Reserved, and In/Humanity; Wilmington, NC's Cornelius; Florida's Palatka, End of the Century Party, and Asshole Parade; and Richmond, VA's Action Patrol. Assfactor 4 became increasingly more influential in the years after their breakup, providing the framework for the jokingly-named "emo violence" sub-genre of the late 1990's, eventually making way for such bands as Orchid, Jerome's Dream, and Blame Game. HeartattaCkzine readers voted Assfactor 4 "Coolest Band to Hang Out With" in 1995.
