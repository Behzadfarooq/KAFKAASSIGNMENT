The once thriving Assyrian community in Iran was diminished from around 200,000 at the close of the 20th century to a mere 5,000 while the total population of Assyrians in all of Iran hovers at around 15,000 to 20,000 (total population of Iran is estimated at 68,017,860).

In addition to Assyrian communities found in major urban centers such as Tehran, there are also Assyrian villages in the area of Urmia in northern Iran. [1]

Assyrians are entitled to one reserved seat in the Iranian Parliament, that was procured by George Malek-Yonan. The seat is currently occupied by Younatan Botkilia (elected in 2000, reelected in 2004), and before him by Shamshoon Maqsudpour. Other prior representatives were Wilson Betmasour, Homer Ashurian, and William Ebrahimi.

[edit] History

There were about 32,000 Assyrians in Iran at the time of the 1976 census.[1] Many emigrated after the revolution in 1979, but at least 20,000 were estimated still to be living in Iran in 1987. The traditional home of the Assyrians in Iran is along the western shore of lake urmia[1] . During World War I virtually the entire Assyrian population fled the area which had become a battle ground for Russian and Turkish armies.[1]
