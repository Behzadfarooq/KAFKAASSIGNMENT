The Assistants Association of Lucas County, Ohio (AALC) is an organization that informally represents the Executive Assistants employed by the Board of Lucas County Commissioners, whose county seat is Toledo, Ohio.

The AALC was established in 2007 to provide a voice to Executive Assistants who, under Ohio Revised Code, are unclassified and exempt from certain protections of the Fair Labor Standards Act.

Currently, the Board of Lucas County Commissioners employs three Executive Assistants, one for each of the three Commissioners. Associate memberships are also available to other Administrative Assistants under the Board's supervision who perform similar duties, classified or otherwise.

[edit] History

In 2007, the AALC elected David Mann as its President, Abbey Mortemore as its Vice President, and Gabrielle Seay as its Sergeant-at-Arms. As a matter of principle, the AALC has no officer position for "Secretary."

Mottoes of the AALC include the formally-adopted, "Helping us help you." as well as the less-formal, "Working for you!"

As one of its first actions, the AALC implemented a new Board of County Commissioner's policy regarding the availability of coffee to county staff outside the Commissioner's departments. This policy had the effect of making higher-quality coffee available in a way that provided savings to the taxpayers of Lucas County.

On March 8, 2007, David Mann resigned his position as President of the AALC due to a pending change in position at Lucas County. That same day, through consensus decision making, Gabrielle Seay was elected as the next President of the AALC, with Abbey Mortemore remaining as Vice President. The position of Sergeant-at-Arms currently remains vacant.
