Ascham School is an independent, non-denominational, non-selective, K-12, day and boarding school for girls in the suburb of Edgecliff, in the Eastern Suburbs of Sydney, New South Wales, Australia.

There is no rigid, structured teaching at Ascham; instead, through suggested discipline, encouragement in self-initiative and a system of support, the school aims to create a context which is conducive to higher education. Ascham is also one of the few schools worldwide to follow the 'Dalton Plan', an educational philosophy created by Helen Parkhurst in 1916. The 'Dalton Plan' aims to produce independent and confident leaders.

Today Ascham has an enrolment of approximately 300 in the Junior school and 600 in the Senior school, with 110 boarders from years 6 to 12.

Ascham is a long-standing member of the Association of Heads of Independent Girls' Schools (AHIGS).

[edit] History

Ascham school was established in 1886 by Miss Marie Wallis, as a private, day and boarding school for girls, at its current location in Edgecliff. The school was named after Roger Ascham, tutor to Queen Elizabeth I. The school adopted the 'Dalton Plan' as its method of teaching in 1922.
