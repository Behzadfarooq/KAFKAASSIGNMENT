Hi Andrew, I noticed you were editing the Conservapedia article. I suggest you brush up on WP:NPOV (we can't say here whether any such bias is actual, and the other comment was an unsourced POV rebuttal statement). Also, you may want to look at out guideline for editing when one has a possible conflict of interest. JoshuaZ 23:45, 4 March 2007 (UTC)

Normally editing articles you have a vested interest in is frowned upon, it might be best to stick to the talk page and post your thoughts there. Jimmy Wales got in a fair bit of hot water for editing his "own" article... -- Tawker 23:56, 4 March 2007 (UTC)
The guideline states "If you feel it necessary to make changes to Wikipedia articles despite a real or perceived conflict of interest, we strongly encourage you to submit content for community review on the article's talk page." Pursuant to that guideline I would suggest that you ok any changes that are not minor (such as grammar, spelling and style issues) on the talk page before making them. JoshuaZ 23:57, 4 March 2007 (UTC)
The one exception you may edit an article on yourself or on organizations you are part of is when you are removing unsourced statements, especially those that are potentially libelous. This includes removing vandalism, like I just did when I noticed that some anonymous vandal changed a quote of yours in Conservapedia into a profanity-laced piece of trash. Jesse Viviano 03:15, 7 March 2007 (UTC)
