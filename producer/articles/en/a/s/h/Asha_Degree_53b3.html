Asha Degree

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Asha Degree was a North Carolina girl who was 9 years old around the time of her disappearance. On February 14th, 2000 she was last seen in her bed at 2:30 AM by her father. Her brother heard noises in the night, and thought it was her stirring in her sleep. At 6:30 AM, Asha's parents came into her room to wake them up, but she was nowhere to be found. Authorities were contacted immediately, and a search began shortly thereafter, but no traces of her were found. She hasn't been seen or heard from since.

She was reportedly walking south on Highway 18 north of Shelby at approximately 4:00 AM by two motorists. At the time, she was apparently near the intersection of Highway 180. After that, she was seen leaving the highway into the darkness. No sightings of the child have been reported since then.

Her black book bag and black Tweety Bird purse were missing from her room when she disappeared. All doors and windows were locked before the family went to bed. Asha kept a house key in her black book bag, which she took with her on her journey.

Degree's pencil, marker and Mickey Mouse hair bow were discovered in the doorway of a tool shed at Turner's Upholstery on Highway 18 on February 17, 2000, three days after her disappearance. Her belongings were found near the stretch of road where Degree was seen walking in the early morning hours of February 14. A search of the area failed to produce additional evidence. The items were discovered more than one mile from Degree's residence.

A contractor uncovered Degree's book bag, which has her name and telephone number written on it, approximately 50 yards from Highway 18 in August 2001, 18 months after she disappeared. The item had been double-wrapped in black plastic bags and was found more than 26 miles from her family's home. Authorities have never released the contents of the bag, but it reportedly contained Degree's name on a piece of paper, clothing and a pencil case. Nearby some animal bones and a pair of men's khaki pants were found. In October of that year, a plastic trash bag similar to the one Asha's book bag was wrapped in was found. The bag was sent to a police lab for analysis. Investigators announced that they considered Degree's disappearance to be a criminal matter and foul play was suspected after the book bag's discovery. The surrounding area was thoroughly searched afterwards, but additional evidence was reportedly not located.
