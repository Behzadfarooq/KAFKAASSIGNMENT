[edit] Thanks for leaving a note...

on my talk page. I cannot claim credit for all the dentaltwin bandwidth on the 'net-- at least half belongs to my brother Steve, who spends most of his time on CraigsList and the sci.med.dentistry Usenet group (to the exclusion of dentistry, I might add).

Although the talk page for dental amalgam is a bit tense, the main article is (to my mind) about as NPOV as you could reasonably expect. I doubt I could accomplish anything useful by stepping into the fray, other than to get involved in vitriol and invective. Not my cup of tea, as you Brits might say, but thanks for the invite. Perhaps I can get Steve to step in ;-)

By the way, I have my first case of true mercury sensitivity in my practice-- the first in over 28 years. It was confirmed by patch test by an "environmental dermatologist"-- never heard of that subspecialty, but he's apparently held in high regard by mainstream docs. It's the first case of mercury sensitivity he's seen too-- so there's nearly 60 man-years of experience and one case. Doesn't seem to merit the controversy it's garnered. Previous to the test, this patient was thought to have a severe case of erosive lichen planus-- confirmed by biopsy. Now let's see if it heals when I remove all the amalgams.--Mark Bornfeld DDS 20:44, 6 January 2006 (UTC)
