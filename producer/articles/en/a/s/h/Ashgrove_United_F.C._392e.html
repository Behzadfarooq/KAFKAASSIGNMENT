Ashgrove United Football Club is an Irish football (soccer) club. The club's home ground is Grove Park in Tralee, county Kerry. They currently play outside any recognised league in tralee due to lack of players and funding. Ashgrove United were founding members of E.S.A Football League in 1992 and of the E.S.A. Cup in 1993. It is a relatively young club but is the most successful football club in the Strand Road Blennerville area.

[edit] Founding

The team was initially founded in a closenet estate and had produced some quality players, such as Billy Clifford, Cathal Moriarty, David Reidy. But after a substantial increase in the number of houses a new influx of youth would occur that would galvinise the club.


[edit] Rivalry

At such an early stage Ashgrove's main competition was against local rivals Lohercannon. These matches would occur quite frequent and gave Ashgrove a great chance to blood some youngsters like Peter Kelly Peter Wheeler and Christopher Foley.

[edit] Early Years

Eventually the more experienced players stood aside and let the youth flourish and it payed off. Such youngsters as Chris Foley, Peter Kelly and Darren Fitzgearld made up the main body of the team with Peter Wheeler deputising for the injured Robert Boylan in goal. It wasn't long before a divide in the camp occurred and youth and experience went there separate ways over a lack of appearances for so called "Big Name" players.

[edit] The Split

With many experienced players having left the set up Ashgrove once again called on youth and drafted Seán Flanagan, Mike Joe Carey and Stephen Higginson in to shore up the holes created in the defense. But there impact was not as big as the previous influx of potential stars.


[edit] The Dip

With Foley sidelined for the entire summer season with a dislocated elbow, Ashgrove signed a new player in Adam Moss, this caused a slight disruption to the harmony that was the Ashgrove dressing room. All of a sudden the flowing movement of the team had absorbd into Adam running from goal to goal with the ball. All of a sudden Ashgrove were on the losing side of certain games and looking a shadow of there former selves. This dip carried on through out the summer and by the time Foley had returned in late October the fixture list was nearing it's end and due to an inflamed disagreement with the ground's men over grass length all remaining games were abandoned.

[edit] Sucesss Returns

Despite the loss of 3 key players in Darren Fitzgearld, Cathal Moriarty and Connor Burke, Ashgrove returned to winning ways when Foley returned to action. Ashgrove begun to expand their competitive view and for the first time took on teams like Pembroke Square, Caheranne and Cahills Park. Ashgrove's dominance quickly returned going unbeaten throughout the summer months. This was mainly down to the immense team-spirit they showed, with Peter Wheeler in goal, Stephen Higginson and Seán Flanagan in defense the team did not shed many goals, but with Kelly, Moss and Foley the team looked unbeatable. One famous victory is the 32-9 against Pembroke Square and the 10-0 away victory versus Cahills Park which saw Cahills Park throw in the towel with just 25 minutes on the clock, in front of the own fans. The team looked unstoppable at this time.

[edit] Success Continues

The side after finally securing some great stability and comradery, were dealt another cruel blow with the departure of Peter Kelly and Stephen Higginson soon followed. This resulted in the return of some old faces such as Brendan Quill and Neill -Squeak- o'Connor. With Squeak back where he belonged - in between the sticks - Wheeler moved outfield playing wide off Foley with Moss in a more central role which result in long ball tactics to an extent. But the main benefit of this was the partnership of Foley and Wheeler. They had a somewhat telepathic closeness on the playing field which resulted in some very liquid fluency arriving to the attack. This one touch passing embedded with checky flicks and tricks saw them demolish some very sturdy defenses. But this was only the beginning for this partnership. Also the arrival of tough tackling right back Mitchell Donnelly was a bonus.

[edit] More Competition Same Results

Due to a severe lack of wins against an unbeatable Ashgrove, Cahills Park refused to play against the mighty Grove, this resulted in the need for new competition and tougher opponents. This criteria would be filled by The Kerries - a much older physical team than Ashgrove. But despite there physical presence they were no match for the speed of Moss, the passing of Wheeler or the finishing of Foley. Even young Mitchell Donnelly would not hesitate to throw in a tackle against people doule his age. Despite missing some key players for certain games Ashgrove dominance continue with Moss and Wheeler creating so much havoc for The Kerries defense and with Foley rounding off all moves in style. Moss and Foley were simply a different class and although not all moves ended with a goal, the quality was apparrent, and with Wheeler aiding with many assists and the odd goal, Ashgrove's dominance never looked under any real threat. This trio looked simply formidable whilst in the same team. With Brendan Quill running the show with immense ease from the centre of the park.

[edit] The Merger

Due to a severe lack of finance at Ashgrove and the clubs sheer refusal to sell on any of it's better players to bigger clubs the club had no choice but to form a year long partnership with The Kerries who would act as a feeder club to a certain extent with the investment of players and finance. This saw the arrival of David o'Brien and Keith Nolan. This saw Ashgrove revert to competitive games with Cahills Park and Ballyrickard. They brushed aside opponents with relative ease and trade mark dominance.

[edit] End Of An Era

Due to what Chris Foley called "a lack of respect" from certain senior figures on the board, certain team mates and certain blocks of the fan base, Foley questioned the mutuality of the love he and the club had shared. This caused him to question his future at the club and he refused to sign a new contract till all matters were sorted out concerning him and his future at the club. The board refused to be held to ransom and with The Kerries now owning a stake in Ashgrove, and with the club riden with debt, it was mutually decided to sell the contract of All Time Top Goalscorer Chris Foley to The Kerries in exchange for the clearance of all debt and removal of the feeder club tag. This angered many fans and attendance dropped dramatically after this decision. Foley too was said to be unhappy about the way his love story with Ashgrove had ended but said he would now concentrate on doing his utmost for his new club. But since not officially a signing of The Kerries management team Foley was played out of position and asked to play a more defensive role but was still expected to lead the goalscoring charts. Foley never really adapted to a style of play that didn't play to his strengths, also a number of niggling injuries had mounted to further add to his horrid time at the club. Due to a change in ownership Wheeler and Moss were also drafted into to boost The Kerries fortunes. But much like Foley they failed to recapture their Ashgrove form. Foley was told he was no longer needed and decide to end his contract early.

[edit] Boy Wonder Returns And European Success

With Francis Quill having won the presidential election with promises of a big name manager, fans grew increasingly frustrated at the lack of activity in finding this big name. With Vincente Del Bosque neglecting to take up the role, Quill turned to an old friend in Chris Foley who would be offered the player/manager role. He returned to action on the 6th of June 2006 opperating in the defensive midfield role but ran from box to box. He quickly added Moss and Wheeler to the line up for a nominal fee, and resigned old teammate Seán Flanagan and his affiliate Darren Creen to shore up the defense. These signings would prove to be quite shrewd, as for the first time Ashgrove were now faced with daunting European trips to A.F.C. Polska, the reigning champions of the Skelp Polska League. A very physical team with height strength and speed, they were possibly Ashgrove's most formidable opponents ever, out strengthening them in every department. But under Ashgrove will never step away from a battle, even one as big as this, the challenge seemed virtually impossible. Although out-muscled at times, Ashgrove went on to win the fixture incredibly 10-8.This was mainly down to an immense defensive display from Dylan Flynn, a bullish performance from Mitchell Donnelly, a hatrick from Anthony o'Connor and Foley running the show with all his efforts, and bagged a hatrick himself. Foley currently has 12 goals in his 8 appearances since his return to the club.
