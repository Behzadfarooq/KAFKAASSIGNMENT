The Ashland Stakes is an American race for thoroughbred fillies restricted to three-year-olds only. Run at a distance of one and one-sixteenth miles for a purse of $500,000, it has been at Keeneland since 1936â€”except from 1938 to 1945 when it was discontinued or held at Churchill Downs. The Grade I race's time in early Spring sets up it up as the major prep to the Kentucky Oaks.

The Ashland Stakes was named for the homestead and breeding farm of statesman Henry Clay in Lexington, Kentucky. The race was run at Keeneland's very first meeting.

Silverbulletday won this race in 1999, Go for Wand in 1990, Princess Rooney in 1983, and Our Mims was third in 1977. Bewitch took the Ashland Stakes in 1948.


[edit] Past winners

Winners since the beginning of the new century are:

  • 2006 - Bushfire (Cornelio Velasquez) (Wait a While placed, Balance third)
  • 2005 - Sis City (Edgar Prado) (Runway Model placed, Memorette third)
  • 2004 - Madcap Escapade (Rene R. Douglas) (Ashado placed, Last Song third)
  • 2003 - Elloluv (Robby Albarado) (Lady Tak placed, Holiday Lady third)
  • 2002 - Take Charge Lady (Tony D'Amico) (Take The Cake placed, Belterra third)
  • 2001 - Fleet Renee (John Velazquez) (Golden Ballet placed, Latour third)
  • 2000 - Rings a Chime (Shane Sellers) (Zoftig placed, Circle of Life third)
