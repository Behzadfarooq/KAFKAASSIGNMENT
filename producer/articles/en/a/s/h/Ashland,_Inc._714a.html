Ashland, Inc. (or simply, Ashland) NYSE: ASH is an American chemical company, which operates in more than 100 countries throughout the world. Operations include Performance Materials, Distribution, Valvoline, and Water Technologies; with each operating as its own division under the corporate umbrella. Ashland was founded in 1924 as Ashland Refining Company of Ashland, Kentucky by Paul G. Blazer. The original oil refinery, located in Catlettsburg, Kentucky, is still in operation today and is currently owned by Marathon Oil. Ashland is best known for their automotive lubrication products which are sold under the name Valvoline. Ashland also operates Valvoline Instant Oil Change Centers, a quick lube franchiser that competes with Pennzoil's Jiffy Lube in the United States. Valvoline products are also featured at all Express Care locations which are independently owned and operated. The company is named for Ashland, Kentucky, the city in which the company was founded in.

[edit] History

In 1998, Ashland, Inc.'s petroleum division merged with Marathon Oil to form Marathon Ashland Petroleum, LLC.

In 1999, Ashland, Inc. moved their headquarters from Russell, Kentucky to Covington, Kentucky.

In 2005, Ashland, Inc. sold its shares of Marathon Ashland Petroleum, LLC, to Marathon Oil, effectively dissolving the remnants of their petroleum division.

In 2006, Ashland transferred APAC, (their paving and construction division) to Oldcastle Materials of Dublin, Ireland.
