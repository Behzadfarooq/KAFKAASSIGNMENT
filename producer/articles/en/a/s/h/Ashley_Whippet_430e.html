[edit] Dodgers Stadium

Ashley first gained notoriety on August 4, 1974 when Stein, then a 19-year-old college student, smuggled her into Dodgers Stadium during a nationally televised baseball game between the Los Angeles Dodgers and the Cincinnati Reds.[1] Stein jumped the fence and went onto the baseball field with Ashley, where he threw discs (Frisbees) for the dog to catch. The crowed was in awe at Ashley's disc-catching ability, as she ran up to 35 miles per hour and leaped 9 feet in the air to snag the Frisbees.[2] The stunt was so novel that the game was stopped and Joe Garagiola continued to announce the flying disc action on the field. Finally, after eight minutes, Stein was escorted off the field and arrested.[1] The nationally televised exhibition of Ashley's skill did much to fuel interest in the disc dog sport.

[edit] Frisbee Dog World Championship

The next year, Stein helped to organize the first Frisbee Dog World Championship for flying-disc catching dogs and their handlers, which continued as an annual event.[3] Stein and Ashley won the first three championships in 1975, 1976 and 1977.[4] In those early years the Dog World Championship took place alongside the "human" Frisbee championships at the Rose Bowl in Pasadena, California. Eventually, it turned into a contest series and, in the early 1980's, was named the Ashley Whippet Invitational (AWI) in honor of Ashley.[5]

[edit] Floating Free

Ashley's skills as a disc dog were featured in the 1977 documentary entitled Floating Free, which was nominated for an Academy Award in the category of Best Live Action Short Film.[6] The eleven minute short was shot at the 1977 World Frisbee Championships and showcases the various events at the competition.[7]

[edit] Other notable performances

In 1977 Ashley performed at Super Bowl XI as the pre-game entertainment.[8] She also performed at the White House for a young Amy Carter.
