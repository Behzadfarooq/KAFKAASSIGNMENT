Asia Motors, Ltd

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Asia Motors Co. Ltd., founded in 1965, was a South Korean motor vehicle manufacturer of large and medium duty trucks, special military vehicles, lightweight cars, civilian jeeps (a small look-alike called the Rocsta), motor vehicle components, to include engines, and buses and mini-buses. Kia owned 28.28% of issued stock. It was completely merged with Kia in 1999 and Asia Motors currently has no automobile production. The Australian Asia Motors distributors, an independent company called Asia Motors Australia, continues to use the Asia Motors name but now sources it's products elsewhere.

The Rocsta's replacement, the Retona, was sold under the Kia badge.
