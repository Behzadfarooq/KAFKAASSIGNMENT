[edit] Fair use rationales

If you look at the current license of the first image on that page, it says "To the uploader: please add a detailed fair use rationale for each use, as described on Help:Image page#Fair use rationale, as well as the source of the work and copyright information." The fair use rationale is used to tell other editors or people reading the article that even though you do not own the rights to the image since you didn't create it, you are still using it to illustrate for educational purposes on Wikipedia. Some examples of a fair use rationale for some of the images I've added include Image:Smokin-poster2.jpg, Image:Bluesbrotherssoundtrack.jpg and Image:Mcveighmugshot.jpg. I'd recommend searching through the GA/FAs that have similar topics to your article, as you're more likely to find a fair use rationale that would be more appropriate for your logos on the page. Let me know if you need more clarification or have any other questions. --Nehrams2020 21:01, 11 March 2007 (UTC)
