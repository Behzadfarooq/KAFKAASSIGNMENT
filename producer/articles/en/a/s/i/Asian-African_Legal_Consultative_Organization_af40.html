Asian-African Legal Consultative Organization

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

The Asian-African Legal Consultative Organization is an international governmental organization formed in 1956, initially to serve as an advisory board to member states on matters on international law. It was an outgrowth of the Bandung Conference, held in Indonesia during April, 1955.
