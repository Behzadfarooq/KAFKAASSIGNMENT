[edit] Asteroid Belt: Origin

Someone had asked elsewhere (I think on the Talk:Asteroid page) whence the term "Asteroid Belt". I'm writing here my research efforts.

  • The first paper (according to the NASA ADS) to bear the words "Asteroid Belt" in its title is Dirk Brouwer, The problem of the Kirkwood gaps in the asteroid belt, Astronomical Journal, Vol. 68, pp. 152-159, 1963
  • The first paper to use "asteroid belt" in its abstract is Frank J. Kerr and Fred L. Whipple, Possible explanations of the secular acceleration of PHOBOS and Jupiter V, Astronomical Journal, Vol. 56, p. 131, 1951
  • No papers whatsoever use the German term "Asteroidengürtel" in their title or abstract (an odd result).

Urhixidur 17:11, 2005 Jan 9 (UTC)

[edit] Informative

The article here is concerned only with the main orbital grouping of asteroids (red but not blue in the second diagram in the article), which is the "main belt", while Asteroid discusses all the minor bodies inwards of Jupiter.
