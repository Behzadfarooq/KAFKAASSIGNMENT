Astrosoniq (sometimes spelled A5tro5oniq) is a band from Oss, the Netherlands. They have been known to call themselves the "wizards of Oss." They could be variously categorized as space rock, psychedelic rock, or stoner rock -- or all three.

All their album covers feature hair growing out of skin. Sometimes a woman, sometimes a man, sometimes a circle of flesh surrounded by hair. [1]

[edit] Musical Style

Their musical style is characterized by extremely psychedelic guitar riffs which always push the boundaries of normal rock. They take Earth's music, put it in a spaceship, and fly it around the galaxy, into black holes, and inside the sun. The result is stoner music with an incredible space flavor. Every song is distinctly catchy and pleasant to the ear. "Astrosoniq knows no musical bounds, and thereâ€™s no sonic trip too far out for them."[2]

"Astrosoniq is characterised by Marcel's massive swinging drumbeats, Erik's detailed, pounding basslines, (ba)ron's power riffs and fine, sometimes outragious solos and Fred & Erik's catchy vocals, often covered with an alien sauce of weird samples and space-sounds, this all being submerged in an extremely fat and unpolished sound."[3] They integrate sound clips from old movies, Wonder Woman episodes, and other sources. Most continue the themes of fantasy and space, but some disuss the music itself. For example, "Solar Machine," off the album "Made in Oss," begins with a woman saying "We come in peace. We call ourselves the Psi women, and we reign supreme on the world of Regulus Four."

[edit] Musical Influences

The band sites classic heavy rock artists as their influences, which can be seen in the heavy guitar riffs and drums. However, the band has progressed light years past the style of such legendary bands Black Sabbath, Led Zeppelin, Hawkwind, Funkadelic, Thin Lizzy and KISS.

[edit] Line-Up

Marcel van de Vondervoort drums, produces, and mixes the music. Ron van Herpen is guitar and vocals. Erik de Vocht is bass and vocals. Fred van Bergen is vocals. Bidi is responsible for space sounds. Willum does the artwork for the band.

[edit] Discography

In 2006 the band released Speeder People. 2005 saw the release of a 6-song EP Made in Oss. In 2002 Astrosoniq released Soundgrenade. Their first album, Son of A.P. Lady, was released in 2000.
