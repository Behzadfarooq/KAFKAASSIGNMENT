[edit] The planets

These are the astrological glyphs as most commonly used in Western Astrology
Name Symbol Symbol represents Meaning of symbol
Sol (the Sun) Solar symbol (Circled dot) divine spirit (circle) surrounding seed of potential
Moon Crescent Moon mind or evolving human spirit (half-circle )
Mercury Mercury's winged helmet and caduceus mind (half-circle ) poised over divine spirit (circle ) and matter (cross )
Venus Venus' hand mirror divine spirit (circle ) over matter (cross )
Earth Earth; also a Solar symbol (Sun cross) Earth -- the cardinal directions
Mars Mars' shield and spear matter (cross ) over divine spirit (circle )
Jupiter Jupiter's thunderbolt or eagle mind (half-circle) rising above the horizon of matter (cross )
Saturn Saturn's sickle or scythe matter (cross ) taking precedence over mind or human spirit (half-circle)
Uranus H in symbol taken from discoverer's last name, Herschel; the other symbol is derived from a combination of the Mars and Sun symbols
Neptune Neptune's trident mind or human spirit (half-circle ) transcending matter (cross )
Pluto Modification of Neptune's astrological symbol; PL monogram for Pluto and Percival Lowell mind (half-circle) transcending matter (cross ) to reach for divine spirit (circle)

[edit] Miscellaneous symbols

Name Symbol Symbol represents Explanation
Ascending Node Lunar node [if used] Not all astrologers use the lunar nodes; however, their usage is very important in Vedic astrology. They are alternately known as the "Dragon's Head" (Rahu, Caput Draconis, or Anabibazon) and the "Dragon's Tail" (Ketu, Cauda Draconis, or Catabibazon). The two nodes together are most commonly referred to simply as the nodal axis, the lunar nodes, or the Moon's nodes.
