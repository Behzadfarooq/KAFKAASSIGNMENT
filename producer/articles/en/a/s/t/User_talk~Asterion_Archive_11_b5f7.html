[edit] Matriz

Olá. Bom Natal! it is not a Parish Church. well, it is also a parish church (Igreja Paroquial), but this is the main church of the municipality. I Think the English term is "First Church", translating directly into English it would be "Mother Church". As the name Matriz is important I choose to use the name Matriz directly, maybe not the best option, but I don't see an English name often used for these kind of churches. If there is, we can change it.--Pedro 14:34, 25 December 2006 (UTC)

I see. What about "Main Church"? Asteriontalk 14:36, 25 December 2006 (UTC)
  • Is Main church often used in English? ---Pedro 14:41, 25 December 2006 (UTC)
  • A Igreja Matriz is also a Parish Church. But there are 14 parish churches. In each parish there can be several others. Matriz is the main one of these parish churches. Maybe in English there's not these kind of churches, and they only have cathedrals. But in Portugal cathedrals (Catedral or Sé) is only found in main cities (historically). The Name of Igreja Matriz can be: Igreja Matriz da Póvoa de Varzim (lit. Mother Church of Póvoa de Varzim) or Igreja Paroquial Nossa Senhora da Conceição (Parish Church of Our lady of Conception).--Pedro 15:00, 25 December 2006 (UTC)
  • I've seen and used that term: "First Church", but that page also uses "Matriz Church in Itajai City", maybe because Matriz, like in here, is also a popular name. Well, I don't know. "First Church" then? For what I've seen, in the US several cities can be part of the same parish (church). While in here, each city can have several parish churches. In Póvoa, traditionally 3, now 5, with the city's expansion. --Pedro 15:18, 25 December 2006 (UTC)
  • Thanks! I don't know, maybe the Brits have somekind of Matriz Church, and we don't know it yet. As for the article, I would appreciate some more help with the English. Feliz Navidad! --Pedro 16:15, 25 December 2006 (UTC)
