I am going to place this in the superstition category myself and to forestall complaints, the main astrology article clearly states: "The generally established opinion of the scientific community is that astrology is superstition, with no actual predictive ability." And not just scientists: astrology is widely considered a superstition by many lay people as well as by Christian and and Islamic doctrine (though the former has had an ambiguous relationship with it).
Note the definition of superstition: "An irrational belief that an object, action, or circumstance not logically related to a course of events influences its outcome" [1] (Wiki is close in its description). Superstitions deal first and foremost with causality. And that, of course, is what Astrology is all about. The category is entirely appropriate.
With others, I am more ambivalent. Belief in a God certainly should not be labelled superstition as it's been no more falsified than it has been proven. Reincarnation, I suppose, I'd place somewhere in-between. Marskell 16:44, 3 October 2005 (UTC)
