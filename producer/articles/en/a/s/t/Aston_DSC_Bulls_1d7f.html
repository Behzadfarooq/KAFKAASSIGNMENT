The Aston Delco Sports Club Bulls is a rugby league team based in Aston Township, Delaware County, Pennsylvania in the United States.

[edit] History

The Bulls were founded as the Glen Mills Bulls in 1998.

The Bulls have been the most dominant side in the American National Rugby League since its inception. Out of the nine AMNRL Championships through 2006, the Bulls have won six.

The side played a touring Australian side, the Coogee Dolphins, as a curtain-raiser to the 2004 Liberty Bell Cup, in which the Australia Kangaroos took on the USA Tomahawks in an international in Philadelphia.

In 2007 the Bulls moved from Glenn Mills to Aston and changed their name to Aston Delco Sports Club Bulls (Aston DSC Bulls for short.)

[edit] Honors

  • AMNRL Championship: 1998, 1999, 2000, 2001, 2004, 2005 (6 times)

[edit] Trivia

  • The Bulls' logo is made to look the Canterbury Bulldogs', a better known side from the NRL
