[edit] RE: V or B

Pronounciation for Spanish names must be given in standard Spanish, which is understood as the unmarked pronunciation, not in regional variations: i.e. Sevilla is not down as /se·biː·ʝa/ although this is the local pronunciation (instead /se·biː·ʎa/). I have no intention on edit-warring on this, I just want you to understand that this is not a matter of respecting local sensibilities but writing an encyclopedia. By the same rule, most -if not all- the placenames in Latin America containing "za", "ce", "ci", "zo" or "zu" would be transcribed as /sa/, /se/ and so on, because the local seseo. Regards, E Asterion u talking to me? 21:50, 11 July 2006 (UTC)

First of all, thanks because you write me a message. Answering it, you can see that in the article it mentions "Castilian Spanish", so at it's meaning a specific variation of Spanish used there, I think it's possible to explain how is the pronunciation of the name of the city. Anyway, I'm agree with you that the Spanish words should be in stantard pronunciation for nouns, adjectives, etc... but I think that the name of a city should be recommended to give the local form. If sevillans really pronounces their city as /se·biː·ʝa/, I think it should written in the article, and the same way for Latin American toponyms. Local forms of a language in some cases should be respected and protected instead of being banned.--Joanot Martorell ✉ 06:43, 12 July 2006 (UTC)
