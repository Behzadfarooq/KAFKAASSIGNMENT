Asaka High School (安積高等学校, Asaka Kōtō Gakkō?), abbreviated as Anko (安高, Ankō?), is the regionally prestagious prefectural high school in Koriyama city, Fukushima, Japan. It was founded on September 11, 1884, originally as Fukushima Junior High School. After renamed Asaka Junior High School in 1948, it was changed into high school due to the restruction in Japanese education system on April 1, 1948.


Asaka High School had been kept as a boys' school for long time, but recently it became coeducational. Simultaneously, it abolished its rule urging students to wear school uniform.

The old building, which had been formaly used as the main building of former Fukushima ordinary junior high school Fukushima Jinjo Chugakko (福島尋常中学校, Fukushima Jinjō Chūgakkō?), is conserved in the front of its site, as Asaka history museum. It was designated to a governmental cultural heritage in 1977.

Asaka High School has made a point of "Frontier Sprit", "Literary and Military arts" and "Spartan".[1]


Now it is in rivalry with Fukushima High School and Iwaki High School.
