James Herald (born: January 13, 1940) is an Australian-British-Canadian-American-Mexican-German-Japanese-Saudi Arabian-Austrian-Bahaman-Belgiumese-Bermudan-Dominican-French-Polish-Guamese-Netherlandian-Irish-Italian-Spanish-Swish-Icelandic-Brazilian-Kenyan-Thai-Indian-Nepalese-Turkish-Russian-Jamican-Puerto Rican-Danish professional wrestler because he is related to all of these places. He is the second son of the legendary David Herald and belongs to the legendary Herald family.

[edit] Career

Herald attended the Bond University and graduated in 1964 at the age of 24. After that, he started supporting his brother Arthur in his wrestling matches. Later, Herald decided to become a wrestler and then he did not care for his elder brother Arthur and went to compete on his own.

[edit] Japan

He made his professional debut on June 13, 1964 in Japan. He started off with Japan Pro Wrestling Alliance. He defeated Bill Dromo to get his first Japanese Heavyweight Championship. He would later capture the title for sixteen times and was the first and only wrestler to become the Japanese Heavyweight Champion for sixteen times.

[edit] Australia

In 1967, Herald left Japan Pro Wrestling Alliance and started wrestling for his hometown Australia and wrestled for WCW Australia. It was the first promotion of Australia and was the only promotion in those days. Herald also won the Australian Heavyweight Championship and this was for forty-eight times.

[edit] United States

In February 1969, Herald had a match for an American promotion in his hometown Australia. That match was promoted by AWA and was for the Midwest Heavyweight Championship. Herald won the match by defeating the-then champion Mike DiBiase.

After winning the Midwest Heavyweight title, he started appearing in the United States and appeared as the Midwest title holder. He held the title for more than two years and received his second Midwest title in April 1972 in Omaha, Nebraska. He was the final Midwest Champion.

[edit] United Kingdom

In May 1972, Herald started wrestling for United Kingdom. In United Kingdom, he wrestled for the British Wrestling Federation and was loved by fans and he did a very good performance in the United Kingdom.

[edit] Canada

In 1973, Herald started wrestling in Canada and entered All Star Wrestling promotion and often wrestled The Brute for the Vancouver Pacific Heavyweight Championship.

Herald later left to wrestle for Stampede Wrestling where he got the Stampede British Commonwealth Mid-Heavyweight Championship, Stampede World Mid-Heavyweight Championship and Stampede Pacific Heavyweight Championship.
