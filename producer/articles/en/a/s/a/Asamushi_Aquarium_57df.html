Asamushi Aquarium

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

ASAMUSHI AQUARIUM (浅虫水族館 Asamushi Suizokukan), also called Aquarium Asamushi, is an aquarium in the Asamushi area of Aomori, Aomori, Japan. Overlooking Aomori Bay, it is the Northernmost aquarium in Honshū. It keeps and displays 11,000 creatures, including those from Aomori Prefecture's abundant marine resources and about 514 species of rare aquatic animals from around the world.

[edit] Outline

The aquarium is the largest general aquarium in the Tōhoku region. There are corners ranging from cold water to warm water, including such marine animals as sea otters, sea lions, seals, and dolphins, as well as popular animals such as penguins. They also put on dolphin and sea lion shows.

2nd floor

  • Tropical rain forest corner
  • Environmental "touch corner"
  • Dolphin show pool
  • Dolphins

[edit] The building

  • Year of establishment: 1983
  • Year of completion:1983
  • Designer: Kankyō Sekkei Jimusho (Environment Design Office)
  • Total area: 8813.11m²
  • Open hours: 9am - 5pm year-round (extended to 6pm in the busy season)
  • Transportation: 10 minutes on foot from the Asamushi-Onsen Station on the JR Tōhoku line.
