Asad Aleem (born: 10th March, 1962) is a Pakistani actor who is also a professional wrestler with his persona Jim Samuel. This article is about his wrestling career. He has feuded with all the World Champions of the promotions where he has worked and has cost them their titles.

He is currently wrestling for four promotions and he is the World Champion of three of them. In NWA Virginia he is the current NWA Virginia Heavyweight Champion under the ring name Sean Denny. In NWA Main Event, he is the current NWA Mid-America Heavyweight Champion under the ring name Lee Condry. In Professional Championship Wrestling he is the current PCW Heavyweight Champion under the ring name Nobe Bryant.

[edit] World Wrestling Federation (First run, March 1984 - October 1984)

Asad's professional wrestling career started in March 1984 and he is a self-trained wrestler. He started wrestling as Asad the Great in WWF in March 1984. He defeated The Iron Sheik in his debut match on March 29, 1984. He started feuding with Greg Gagne as a heel.

He kept on defeating Gagne and then defeated Ron Garvin in June 1984. Asad had his first tag team match where he teamed with Greg Valentine and then defeated the WWF World Heavyweight Champion Hulk Hogan and Ron Garvin. After June 1984, he went on to feud with several tag teams in WWF.


In July 1984, he feuded with British Bulldogs, in August 1984, he went on to feud with US Express, in September 1984, he feuded with The Hart Foundation and in October 1984, he feuded with Moondogs.

[edit] World Wrestling Council (October 1984 - March 1985)

After leaving WWF, Asad went to wrestle in Puerto Rican World Wrestling Council on October 29, 1984. He debuted by defeating Bruiser Body. He attacked Invader #1 and then feuded with Valiant during his entire career in WWC. He later captured the WWC Carribean Heavyweight Championship by defeating Gonzales but lost the title to Gonzales on March 15, 1985 and the rules were that whoever would lose this match would quit WWC.

[edit] American Wrestling Association (First run, March 1985 - November 1985)

Asad debuted in American Wrestling Association on March 23, 1985 by defeating Jerry Blackwell as The Competitor. He then feuded with Stan Hansen and defeated him several times. He later defeated Nick Bockwinkel in June 1985 and got a chance for the AWA world title in a match with Rick Martel. He had a good chance to deliver a Body slam to Martel in the match but before he could slam Martel, Martel slapped in Boston crab and Competitor had to submit.

He would later have a rematch with Martel which was a No Disqualification Match. He hit Martel with a chair and then took the AWA Championship and then he hit Martel with the belt and tried to do pinfall but Martel locked in Boston crab and Competitor submitted once again. He wanted to get the AWA Championship at all cost.

He feuded with Rick Martel for the AWA title but he couldn't get it. He had several matches for the title but he failed and he left AWA in November 1985. He had his last match in AWA with Mad Dog Vachon.

[edit] World Class Championship Wrestling (November 1985 - April 1986)

Asad started making appearances with the World Class Championship Wrestling between November 1985 and April 1986. He feuded with Matt Osborne. He later feuded with Buddy Roberts for the WCCW television title but couldn't get the title. He had several matches with Roberts for the title but failed.

He defeated Roberts in a non-title match and then cost Roberts, his Television title on March 24, 1986 when Roberts had a match with Lance Von Erich for the title. From behind, Asad hit Roberts with a chair which gave Von Erich, his Television title. He then feuded with Von Erich to get the title until April 14, 1986.

[edit] American Wrestling Association (Second run, April 1986 - July 1989)

Asad returned to AWA on April 25, 1986. He feuded with Curt Hennig and Scott Hall, then AWA Tag Team Champions. He defeated both of them in a handicap match due to his return. He cost Hennig and Hall the AWA Tag Team title in their match with Buddy Rose and Doug Somers where they defended the title.

Asad later changed his ring name from The Competitor to Soldat Ustinov, a Turkish wrestler on December 14, 1986. Later, in 1987, he formed a tag team with Boris Zhukov under Sheik Adnan El Kassey's management. They feuded with Midnight Rockers for the AWA Tag Team title.

On May 25, 1987, Ustinov and Zhukov defeated Midnight Rockers and got the AWA World Tag Team Championship but they both later lost it to Jerry Lawler and Bill Dundee on October 11, 1987.

Later, in 1988, Ustinov teamed up with Tejiho Khan who was a new partner for Ustinov and was the wrestler whom Adnan Al-Kassey brought for his management.

On February 18, 1989, Ustinov had a non-televised match for the AWA World Heavyweight Championship against the AWA World Champion Larry Zybszko for the title. Ustinov was disqualified for hitting Zybszko with the chair and this cost him the match. Ustinov lost the match and couldn't get the title.

Ustinov later challenged the AWA Television Champion Greg Gagne for the title and this time, Ustinov won the match due to countout but couldn't get the title. He started feuding with Gagne for the title until April 1989 and then he once again went to become the AWA World Champion.

He feuded with Zybszko for a short time for the championship. Ustinov left Adnan Al-Kassey and went on his own and then he left AWA in July 1989.

[edit] Universal Wrestling Association (July 1989 - March 1991)

After leaving AWA, Asad went to wrestle in Mexican promotion Universal Wrestling Association. He debuted in that promotion on July 18, 1989 where he wrestled with his former ring name The Competitor. He wrestled in UWA and feuded with Kim Utperra. He then wrestled with UWA World Champion Mendo Mazzarro and then chased him off to get the title until March 15, 1990 because on that day, he was able to get of the best of Mazzarro.

He pinned Mazzarro and then captured the UWA World Championship. He later lost the title to Jake Diego. He defeated Último Dragón and got the UWA World Welterweight Championship. He lost it to Midio Digano and then feuded with UWA World Champion Jack Protzo.

He didn't appear for sometime but returned on October 24, 1990 winning the UWA World Championship after defeating Protzo and then he changed his ring name from The Competitor to World Champion. He defeated Kerry Ugiano in a match and then later, defended it against El Samurai. He regained the title by defeating this challenger.

On February 22, 1991, he lost his UWA World title to Kouji Ishinriki and then he had a rematch with Ishinriki for the title. He won the match by countout but couldn't win the title. He left the promotion in March 1991.

[edit] Super World of Sports (March 1991 - June 1992)

Asad started wrestling for the Japanese promotion Super World of Sports where he debuted on March 18, 1991. He feuded with Isao Takagi and defeated him on his debuting match. They both feuded with each other and then their feud ended when they both teamed up in a tag team match.

Asad later went to wrestle with the SWS Light Heavyweight Champion Naoki Sano and the conditions were that if he would win the match, he would wrestle with Sano for the title. Asad won the match and got a title shot.

They both wrestled with each other on April 4, 1992 for the title and Asad lost the match with a roll-up by Sano and this roll-up cost Asad the match. He feuded with Sano for the title until June 1992 when the promotion closed.

[edit] Frontier Martial-Arts Wrestling (June 1992 - August 1993)

Asad started wrestling in Frontier Martial-Arts Wrestling where he debuted on June 25, 1992 where he wrestled as New Death. He wrestled with Mr. Pogo in his debut match and he lost the match. He got his first victory over Atsushi Onita.

He feuded with Terry Funk and then went on his best to defeat him. Asad defeated Funk in many matches. Later in March 1993, he teamed with The Gladiator and then teamed with The Gladiator for the rest of his time with FMW. He had his last appearance with FMW when he interrupted in Mr. Pogo and Atsushi Onita by attacking on Onita.

[edit] Global Wrestling Federation (August 1993 - December 1993)

Asad went to wrestle for the Texan promotion based in Dallas, Global Wrestling Federation where he debuted on August 29, 1993 by defeating Rick Harris. He feuded with Sam Houston.

He used the ring name Kerry Wilson in the promotion that was a tribute to the former superstar of GWF, Kerry Von Erich. He later challenged Rip Rogers for a Brawl match and then defeated Rogers in the match. He went to feud with Cactus Jack for the rest of November 1993.

He wanted to become the GWF World Title and so, he challenged the champion Iceman Parsons to a match on December 4, 1993. He lost the match but demanded for a rematch on December 11, 1993 and lost it too.

He defeated Stevie Ray in a match on December 18, 1993 by showing that he was a powerful wrestler and could defeat Parsons. On December 25, 1993, Wilson announced that he would leave GWF and on that day, Parsons had to defend the title against Chris Adams where Wilson attacked Parsons giving Adams the victory and the title.

[edit] United States Wrestling Association (December 1993 - April 1994)

Wilson left for the United States Wrestling Association promotion in December 1993. For the first time, Wilson had a debut match in any promotion which was a title match. In this match, Wilson wrestled for the USWA Heavyweight Championship against the champion Brian Christopher.

Wilson lost the match but kept on feuding with Christopher for the title. He had his final match on February 14, 1994 when the champion was Doug Gilbert. He then wrestled with former rival Brian Christopher in another match and then defeated Christopher after a Spinebuster to win the match.

He defeated Christopher in several matches until March 25, 1994. On that day, he claimed to become the USWA Unified World Heavyweight Champion. In April 1994, he wrestled with then-champion Jerry "The King" Lawler and lost the match and then he quit the promotion.

[edit] East Coast Wrestling Association (April 1994 - June 1994)

Asad started wrestling in East Coast Wrestling Association under the name Mr. Death. He debuted in April 1994 by defeating Steve Corino. He used a back supplex on Corino to win the match. He defeated Ace Darling in his second match and kept on defeating several wrestlers in ECWA.

He later wanted to get a title shot for the ECWA World Championship. He feuded with Lance Diamond for the title until June 1994. The conditions were that who would lose this No-Disqualification Match would have to quit ECWA.

He chokeslammed Diamond and went for the pin where Diamond had a roll-up which sot Asad his career in ECWA. He quit ECWA and Diamond regained the title.

[edit] UWF International (June 1994 - August 1994)

Asad had success in Japan with the ring names World Champion and New Death and now he wrestled in another Japanese promotion UWF International where he debuted in June 1994 under the ring name Mr. Death that he used in ECWA.

He couldn't appear in UWF regularly because of home problems, so he wrestled just three matches in that promotion. Two in July and one in August. In July, he had his matches with Bob Backlund which he won and in August, he also won his match against Nobuhiko Takada by defeating him after a Powerbomb.

As a result, Samuel suffered a Banzai Drop.

Samuel participated in a Battle Royal match and it was announced that whoever would win this match would wrestle with the WWF Champion Bret Hart for the title at Survivor Series (1994). Samuel eliminated his rival Yokozuna, Jeff Jarrett and Diesel but after he eliminated Diesel, the legendary Bob Backlund eliminated him.

Samuel feuded with Yokozuna and they both had several matches with each other. Their last matach occurred on December 15, 1994 which was a Last Man Standing match. Samuel won the match by giving Yokozuna, a flying elbow drop. [edit] Organization of Modern Extreme Grappling Arts (February 1998 - April 1998)

Samuel left WWF in February 1998 and then debuted in the independent promotion OMEGA based in Cameron, North Carolina. Samuel defeated Adam Birch and then he feuded with Jeff Hardy and defeated him and became the OMEGA Heavyweight Champion.

He later captured all other titles in OMEGA and had so much success and was the most favorite of fans. He also captured the New Frontier Championship and the Cruiserweight Championship and he also captured the tag team championship with Adam Birch. In April 1998, Samuel left OMEGA and returned to WWF.

[edit] World Wrestling Federation (Third run, April 1998 - August 1998)

Samuel returned to WWF in April 1998 and then he defeated the Intercontinental Champion The Rock due to his return and then he challenged Triple H for a match. He got the best of Triple H and then pinned him after a chokebomb. He defeated the mixed martial artist Ken Shamrock in a match.

He started feuding with Stone Cold Steve Austin and defeated him in a No Disqualification match. He started feuding with Val Venis and then he defeated Venis in a No-Hold Barred match. He also had a Intercontinental title match with Triple H which he won by disqualification when Triple H hit him with a chair. Samuel left WWF in August 1998.

[edit] World Championship Wrestling (August 1998 - January 1999)

Samuel left WWF and then started wrestling for the WWF rival World Championship Wrestling in August 1998. He debuted in the promotion as Chip Minton in a match with Eddie Guerrero which he lost after a brainbuster by Guerrero. Minton demanded for a rematch and then he defeated Guerrero in a rematch by chokeslamming him.

He wrestled in a 4-Way dance match which was a #1 Contender's match for the WCW United States Championship. This match also consisted of Bret Hart, Hulk Hogan and Raven. Minton pinned Raven and then he chokebombed Hart and went for the pin but Hogan attacked on Minton and then Minton chokebombed Hogan and pinned him and Hart pinned Minton and it was announced that the winner of this match would wrestle with the United States Champion Lex Luger on August 13 edition of Thunder.

Minton attacked the WCW Champion Goldberg on the August 20 edition of Thunder in his match with Raven and then Minton and Goldberg feuded with each other.

Note: Goldberg's match with Raven was a non-title match.

Minton wanted to be the champion and then he defeated Golberg in a non-title match and then a Battle Royal match occurred between Minton, Goldberg and Hogan for the WCW Championship. Minton was eliminated by Goldberg and then Minton attacked on Goldberg and then he chokebombed Hogan and Goldberg eliminated Hogan.

Minton eliminated Glacier, Bobby Eaton, Alex Wright, Super Calo, Booker T and Norman Smiley. After Minton eliminated Smiley, he was eliminated by Villano V and then Minton got very angry and took his revenge of his elimination with Villano V in a match and then he defeated Villano V in the match.

Minton then wrestled his last match with the WCW Television Champion Scott Steiner for the Television title which Minton won by countout. He won the match but couldn't get the TV title. The match occurred in January 1999 and Minton was not seen in WCW after this match.

[edit] Extreme Championship Wrestling (January 1999 - August 1999)

Minton made a surprising appearance in Extreme Championship Wrestling in January 1999 by defeating the FTW Heavyweight Champion Sabu in a non-title match. They both later wrestled in another match for the title which Sabu won by roll-up.

Minton wrestled with the ECW Television Champion Rob Van Dam in a match and then defeated him by chokebombing him. One week later, Minton and RVD wrestled in a match where Minton powerslammed RVD and that was for the Television title which he lost by countout.

Minton started feuding with Balls Mahoney until June 1999 in an Anywhere Falls match where Minton pinned Mahoney in a cage. Minton started feuding with the ECW World Heavyweight Champion Taz for the title. He defeated Taz in a non-title match on August 27, 1999 on the first episode of ECW's flagship broadcast ECW on TNN. This was Minton's last match in ECW.

[edit] World Wrestling Federation (Fourth run, September 1999 - February 2000)

[edit] 1999

Minton returned to World Wrestling Federation in September 1999. He once again used the ring name Jim Samuel and he wrestled in WWF to gain popularity because he had lost the popularity that he had created in WWF for himself. He returned to WWF by defeating Jeff Hardy, one half of the tag team The Hardy Boyz.

[edit] 2000

Samuel started feuding with the WWF European Champion British Bulldog for the title and defeated him in several matches. In February 2000, when Kurt Angle became the champion, Samuel called him the son of a bitch.

[edit] Australian Wrestling Federation (February 2000 - July 2000)

Samuel left WWF in February 2000 and then he went to wrestle for the Australian promotion Australian Wrestling Federation where he captured the AWF Heavyweight Championship for five times. He left AWF in July 2000.

[edit] AWA Superstars of Wrestling (July 2000 - June 2001)

Samuel left AWF in July 2000 and then debuted in the Minnesota-based AWA Superstars of Wrestling under the ring name Ricky Enrique. He defeated in that promotion on July 22, 2000 by defeating Horshu.

Enrique later defeated the new AWA Champion The Patriot on July 29, 2000 to become the AWA Champion. This was his first reign as a world champion. He was recognized as a champion for eleven months. He lost the title to Eric Priest on June 3, 2001. After that match, he left AWA Superstars of Wrestling.

[edit] World Wrestling Federation/Entertainment (Fifth run, June 2001 - April 2006;January 2007)

[edit] 2001

Enrique returned to World Wrestling Federation in June 2001 and he wrestled once again as Jim Samuel. He defeated Matt Hardy on his returning day and then he started feuding with the WCW Cruiserweight Champion X-Pac for the Cruiserweight title.

Samuel defeated X-Pac in several non-title matches but couldn't win any title match. He defeated Chavo Guerrero in a match on Sunday Night Heat by powerbombing him. Samuel then started wrestling in matches on Heat and got success and became an undefeated wrestler on Heat.

[edit] 2002

Samuel is best known for having many notable feuds with top superstars in WWF in 2002. His first feud in 2002 was with Spike Dudley whom he defeated in a match. They both had a rematch which Samuel won by countout. Samuel chokebombed Dudley in a match with a jobber.

Samuel later started feuding with Christian and then he defeated Christian in several matches but in future, Christian was having good luck who defeated Samuel in several matches. Samuel eliminated Christian in a battle royal while Samuel was not a part of that match and then Booker T eliminated Christian.

Samuel ended his feud with Christian in a match on RAW. Samuel left Heat and started appearing on RAW. He defeated several wrestlers in many matches and feuded with them and these were Shawn Michaels, John Cena, Nathan Jones, Ric Flair, Brock Lesnar, Big Show, The Undertaker, Kane and Full Blooded Italians.

His most popular feud was with Hulk Hogan for the WWE Undisputed Championship who was the champion at the time. He wrestled in a match with Hogan on the May 9, 2002 episode of SmackDown! which Samuel won after a chair shot from Hogan.

Hogan regained the title but Samuel challenged Hogan of another match on May 16, 2002. Samuel did his best to win the match and he chokebombed Hogan and went for the pin but he missed the pin because the referee was knockedout in the match and this was his bad luck.

A rematch occurred between Samuel and Hogan on May 19, 2002 when it was 2002 Judgment Day. Before the match could start, Samuel claimed that he didn't want to win by cheating even he wanted to win with his own hands and then he called a wrestler to replace him and it was The Undertaker. Samuel then went out of the ring and left the building.

He returned to WWE as a Raw exclusive feuding with The Rock for the WWE Undisputed Championship. He defeated Rock in a non-title match and then chokebombed Rock in his match with Brock Lesnar for the Undisputed title and then Lesnar delivered F-5 to Rock which gave Lesnar the victory and then Samuel feuded with Lesnar for the title but it was a short feud.

[edit] 2003

Samuel returned to WWE in 2003 attacking Brock Lesnar in his match with Kurt Angle on July 23, 2003 for the WWE Championship which also involved Samuel's former rival Big Show. He also attacked Big Show and this gave Angle the victory and then Samuel became a SmackDown! exclusive superstar.

Big Show challenged Samuel of a match and then Samuel won the match by a chokebomb. He pinned Show and then he won the match. He teamed up with Mr. America in a match against Big Show and A-Train which the team of Samuel and Mr. America won after Mr. America pinned A-Train after a legdrop.

Samuel started teaming up with Mr. America in several matches against heelitic wrestlers by the year of 2003.

[edit] 2004

Samuel started feuding with Acolytes Protection Agency and wrestled in handicap matches with the APA in early 2004 until the APA were fired. Samuel requested of a a 4-Way dance match which was also a #1 Contender's match for Brock Lesnar's WWE Championship. The other wrestlers in the match were Eddie Guerrero, John Cena and The Undertaker.

Samuel was pinned by Cena after a roll-up and then he chokeslammed the three wrestlers. Samuel then left WWE in June 2004. He later appeared and wrestled a match in Velocity in August 2004 where he defeated Rey Mysterio by chokeslamming him. He then started appearing for SmackDown! and Velocity.

[edit] 2005

Samuel made an appearance in July 2005 on RAW as a heelitic wrestler who challenged John Cena for the WWE Championship. They both wrestled in a match and then Cena won the match by a F-U. Samuel had a rematch with Cena which Cena won by disqualification when Samuel hit him with the belt.

[edit] 2006

Samuel spent his time with WWE in early 2006. He appeared in a match with Kane and The Big Show on March 27, 2006 edition of RAW where he teamed up with Shawn Michaels and Kane and The Big Show had to defend their World Tag Team Championship.

Samuel and Michaels won the match by disqualification but later, they both had a fight with each other. Michaels delivered a back supplex to Samuel and then he was attacked by Mr. McMahon. Later, Samuel appeared on March 31, 2006 edition of SmackDown! in a #1 Contender's Match for the World Heavyweight Championship.

He defeated Randy Orton to qualify for that match but later was attacked by Mark Henry, so he couldn't appear on WrestleMania 22 to get the match, so he was replaced by Orton.

Samuel appeared on April 3, 2006 edition of RAW where he insulted Shawn Michaels but was interrupted by Mr. McMahon and then Spirit Squad attacked Samuel. Samuel and McMahon had an I Quit Match on April 10, 2006 edition of RAW which was no contest because both of the men were fainted in the end. Samuel didn't appear after that match.

[edit] 2007

On March 12, 2007 edition of WWE RAW, Samuel returned to WWE by defeating Vladimir Kozlov in a dark match. Samuel hasn't joined WWE but he has signed a contract to wrestle for WWE in dark matches.

[edit] NWA Virginia and independent circuit (April 2006 - March 2007)

Samuel joined the Virginia based territory of NWA in April 2006 under the ring name Sean Denny. He defeated The Barbarian in his debut match and feuded with him.

On July 8, 2006, like other promotions, Denny challenged the world champion of NWA Virginia, Damian Wayne for a title match and for the first time, he became a world champion by challenging an opponent. He defeated Wayne and got the NWA Virginia Heavyweight Championship.

He suffered an injury on March 31, 2007, so he had to vacate his Virginia Heavyweight title.

[edit] Independent circuit (April 2006 - September 2006)

Denny also wrestled for many independent promotions between April 2006 and September 2006 and he captured many heavyweight championships of independent promotions.

[edit] NWA Florida (September 2006 - November 2006)

Samuel debuted in NWA Florida in September 2006 where he feuded with the FCW Heavyweight Champion Bruce Steele for the title but failed to get the title. He feuded with Steele until November 2006.

[edit] International Wrestling Syndicate (November 2006 - January 2007)

Samuel started wrestling for the Canadian promotion International Wrestling Syndicate in November 2006 feuding with IWS Heavyweight Champion Viking for the title but couldn't get the title and then he defeated the IWS Canadian Champion Dick Morley to become the champion.

Samuel reigned as a champion for just a week and then he lost it to Dan Paysan on December 2, 2006. He feuded with Paysan for the championship and had several matches with Paysan until January 7, 2007.

[edit] NWA Main Event (December 2006 - present

Samuel debuted in NWA Main Event promotion on December 23, 2006 where he challenged the NWA Mid-America Heavyweight Champion J.T. Quest and then he won a four-corners survival match to win the title and is the current champion.

[edit] Wrestle Zone Wrestling (January 2007 - March 2007)

After Samuel left IWS, he started wrestling for the British Wrestle Zone Wrestling promotion in January 2007 where he captured the wZw Heavyweight Championship which he lost to Anthony McIntyre on March 3, 2007. On March 10, 2007, he defeated McIntyre in a non-title match to get a rematch.

Samuel wrestled with the champion Anthony McIntyre for the wZw Heavyweight Championship on March 17, 2007 and then he chokeslammed McIntyre and was very angry and then he went off the ropes and accidentally, he knockedout the referee and then McIntyre hit him with a chair and then pinned him to regain the title.

Later, on March 24, 2007, Samuel challenged McIntyre for an "I Quit Match" and was for the title which Samuel lost and then he quit wZw.

[edit] Professional Championship Wrestling (February 2007 - April 2007)

Samuel joined the Texan promotion Professional Championship Wrestling in February 2007 where he debuted on February 17, 2007 as Nobe Bryant who had his debut match for the PCW Heavyweight Championship against the champion Dick Marcony. He defeated Marcony and got the championship and is the current champion. On April 1, 2007, Bryant lost his PCW Championship to Chris Knight.

[edit] Other media

Jim Samuel has appeared in a cartoon based on him called Asian Wrestler.
