[edit] Recent Updates

Asa is no longer on drivers@mozilla.org (see http://www.mozilla.org/about/drivers). In addition, Asa's role at the Mozilla Corporation is no longer as the Quality Assurance leader. He is now strictly marketing and, more specifically, in charge of SpreadFirefox and FirefoxFlicks as well as other community marketing programs. He may have founded the QA program, but no longer works on it. 17:08, 18 May 2006 (UTC)

[edit] Wording - NPOV and quality

In the opening there is the following text: "As the Quality Assurance lead, Asa works with Mozilla's QA and testing community to ensure excellence and to certify applications for release." (My emphasis.)

It is not NPOV to say something ensures excellence. Imagine the wording "Internet Explorer's QA team ensures excellence in their product". In other words, the wording presumes that the product has excellence - something many people believe, but which is an assertion not appropriate for an encyclopaedia.

Also, as a matter of politeness and formality, it is poor form to refer to the subject by his first name.

A much better wording would be something like: "As the Quality Assurance leader, Dotzler works with Mozilla's QA and testing community to improve the calibre of the product and to recommend when a product is of sufficient standard for general public release."

Would anyone object to that wording? El T 11:37, 28 October 2005 (UTC)

Changed wording to form above. El T 03:24, 29 October 2005 (UTC)

  • Realised that the Quality Assurance link (to quality control) actually obviates the need for the stuff about the specifics of his job. El T 03:22, 1 November 2005 (UTC)

[edit] Quotes

This first quote isn't even from Dotzler, but from Mozilla and has therefore been removed:

Mozilla Organisation (about):
"The mission of the Mozilla project is to preserve choice and innovation on the Internet."

These next quotes are misleading and have therefore been removed. Dotzler has discussed innovative features in IE7 Beta, but in that same discussion also mentioned features which the browser has replicated from Mozilla Firefox and Apple Safari.

Asa (blog), regarding IE7:
"There are several great extensions that offer a similar experience, but IE's seems the cleanest and easiest to use. We should probably just adopt something similar to that."
"This is a nice feature. I think we should do something similar."
"A nice feature in IE's search is the ability to easily remove search providers. We should definitely do this."
"That's pretty nice. I wonder what it would take to develop a small service that did something similar for Firefox."

For transparency purposes, here's my edit. jareha 00:50, 5 February 2006 (UTC)

Removed more quotes that don't belong in this article. jareha (comments) 06:55, 29 May 2006 (UTC)
