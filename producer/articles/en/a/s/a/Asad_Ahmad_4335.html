Asad Ahmad is a newsreader and reporter on the BBC's regional service for London, BBC London News.

He presents BBC London's flagship 18:30 news bulletin on BBC One as well as other bulletins.

Before joining BBC London, he had presented editions of BBC Scotland's flagship news bulletin Reporting Scotland as well as news bulletins on Breakfast.

Asad is the 2006 Royal Television Society Reporter of the Year for the London area. He helped BBC London win the RTS London News Story of the Year Award 2005 for their coverage of the Olympic 2012 bid, where he reported live from Singapore.

Asad started at the BBC on the News Trainee Scheme in 1996 before working at BBC Birmingham as a Political Reporter and TV Presenter.

He then moved to BBC Scotland as a TV and Radio reporter where he covered Scotland's lead up to a devolved parliament. Asad was one of the launch presenters and correspondents for Newsnight Scotland.

Asad moved to London to work as a reporter for BBC Breakfast before being asked to join their presenting team with Moira Stuart, Jeremy Bowen, Sophie Raworth, Sarah Montague and Darren Jordan. He also presented the News round-up on Breakfast with Frost and the news on News24 with Sian Williams.

Asad became the BBC's Scotland Correspondent and covered the Foot and Mouth outbreak for the 1, 6 and 10 o'clock news and Radio 4 as well as the race riots in Oldham and Bradford.

Since moving back to London, Asad has become a recognisable face around the capital and he works towards improving Education standards in inner cities, and understanding between communties. He is also involved in a working party looking at homelessness in the capital. Asad can sometimes be heard on the radio presenting on the BBC Asian Network. He has also previously presented the Newsdrive programme on BBC Radio Scotland and he has been shortlisted several times for awards for his special reports on BBC radio.

Other programmes Asad has presented includes a late night discussion programme for BBC Choice, Newsline Scotland, East for BBC2, Music and current affairs programmes including a Pakistan Earthquake Special programme for BBC1.

Asad was invited by the US State Department to take part in their prestigious Leadership Programme where he delivered lecturers and met leading figures.

Previously Asad has worked as a Trainee Foreign Exchange Dealer in the City of London, a researcher in the House of Lords and he has read Law.
