Asaita

Asaita is a town in northeastern Ethiopia, and the current capital of the Afar Region of Ethiopia. Located in the Afambo woreda, part of the Region's Administrative Zone 1, this town has a latitude and longitude of 11°34′N, 41°26′E.

Based on figures from the Central Statistical Agency in 2005, this town has an estimated total population of 22,718, of whom 12,722 were males and were 9,996 females.[1] According to the 1994 national census, this town had a population of 15,475.

To the southeast of Asaita, located at the southern edge of the Danakil Desert, are a group of twenty salt lakes which cover the territory to the border with neighboring Djibouti. These lakes include Lake Gamarri, known for its flamingos, and Lake Abbe, the final destination of the Awash River.

Asaita was the seat of the Aussa Sultanate, the chief Afar monarchy. The town of Semera, which is as of 2005 under construction but squarely placed on the Awash - Asseb highway, will eventually become the new regional capital. Asaita, on the other hand, is 50 kilometers south by unpaved road from this highway.
