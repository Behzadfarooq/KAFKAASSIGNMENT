Richard Joseph Rage (born: October 16, 1967) is an American professional wrestler who is the eldest son of the legendary wrestler and the mixed martial artist, the late Donald Rage (1942-2007). He is best known for his persona Rick Rage.

[edit] Early life

Richard received his studies and at high school, he was trained as a professional wrestler from young age and then he went to a wrestling school to learn wrestling. In 1986, at the age of 19, he started wrestling.

[edit] Professional wrestling (1986-2002)

[edit] Early career

Richard started wrestling at the age of 19 in 1986. He was very strong wrestler who weighed 260 pounds and he was very powerful. He was trained by his father Donald Rage and the very well known Lex Luger. He wrestled for NWA Florida during his early days and was teamed with the Southern Heavyweight Champion Lex Luger.

Richard went to wrestle in NWA World Championship Wrestling. He debuted in that promotion in 1986 who was managed by his mother Jenna Rage. He feuded with Magnum T.A. and Tully Blanchard during his time with WCW. He left it soon in 1987.

[edit] New Japan Pro Wrestling (1987-1993)

[edit] 1987

Richard rose to fame as a professional wrestler in June 1987 when he joined New Japan Pro Wrestling. He started wrestling in NJPW as Rick "The Head" Rage. He wrestled in several tag team matches with Riki Chōshū. He became a famous wrestler when he defeated the wrestling legend Antonio Inoki who was also the then-IWGP World Heavyweight Champion.

He then teamed up with his brother David Rage who debuted during those days. They both feuded with IWGP World Tag Team Champions Yoshiaki Fujiwara and Kazuo Yamazaki for the title but they both failed.

Rick defeated Fujiwara in a singles match and later, David defeated Yamazaki in a singles match. Rick and David were wrestling towards success and had a good luck. They both defeated Antonio Inoki and Tatsumi Fujinami on December 28, 1987 and it was the greatest victory in their tag team.

[edit] 1988

In 1988, Rick left his tag team with David and went to compete for singles. He started feuding with Antonio Inoki for the IWGP World Heavyweight Championship. He was a rival of Inoki for that world title and he said that he would take it at any cost. The son of the professional trained heavyweight wrestler Donald Rage, Rick would prove himself.

He challenged Inoki of a No Holds Match but regained the title after Inoki delivered him Enzuigiri over a table. Rick suffered a very bad injury and couldn't come for so many days. He returned to New Japan in April 27, 1988.

He was then involved in a feud with his brother David. They both feuded with each other. He defeated David several times and then he had a No Disqualification Match with David on July 13, 1988 and this time, Rick had no one in his support because his mother supported his younger brother David. Rick hit David with a chair and went for the pin but he was distracted by his mother and then David gave a Low Blow to Rick and pinned him which made Rick lose the match.

They both put their eyes on Tatsumi Fujinami's IWGP World Heavyweight Championship and then they both shook hands with each other so that they could take Fujinami's title. They both wrestled in a Triple Threat Match where Fujinami defended his title against both brothers.

After they both attacked Fujinami, then Rick went for the pin but David kicked there. They both started quarreling there and then this match cost both of them the match because Fujinami gave up a Low Blow to both of them. Rick took his revenge with David by defeating him in a match.

Rick and David feuded with each other once again and then they both wrestled with each other to end the feud in a match which Rick won.

[edit] 1989

Rick started of 1989 feuding with The Great Muta. He wrestled with Muta several times and kept on defeating him. Rick and Muta had a final match. Rick attacked Muta but was distracted by David and then Rick lost by a roll-up. He started feuding with his brother David once again.

Later, Rick defeated Shinya Hashimoto on April 25, 1989 in a #1 Contender's Match and then he started feuding with Big Van Vader for the title. Their feud ended on May 16, 1989 and then his brother Mark Rage debuted in NJPW.

Rick started teaming up with the newcomer, his brother Mark and then they defeated Shinya Hashimoto and Salman Hashimikov. They both defeated many wrestlers in tag team matches until August 1989. Afterwards, in the late 1989, they wrestled for the IWGP World Tag Team Championship.

Rick and Mark feuded with Shinya Hashimoto and Masa Saito for the titles several times but couldn't win any match. They won a tag team match for the titles but that was by countout thus Hashimoto and Saito regained the titles.

[edit] 1990

Rick and Mark wrestled on January 5, 1990 for the tag team titles with Hashimoto and Saito and this time, they could win the match but Mark turned on Rick by attacking him. Rick couldn't forget it and then he feuded with Mark and once again, his mother was not by his side and she once again supported Mark.

Rick defeated Mark in a match and then later, David returned feuding with Rick and then David and Mark created a tag team against Rick who needed a partner and his mother was also supporting David and Mark. Rick started feuding with the IWGP World Heavyweight Champion Big Van Vader for the IWGP World Heavyweight Championship.

He had a match with Vader on February 24, 1990 when he was going to defeat Vader but his brothers David and Mark came to rescue the champion and attacked on Rick. Rick was totally in trouble and then surprisingly, the famous star World Champion speared David and Mark.

Rick then shook hands with World Champion and then they both defeated David and Mark in a tag team match on March 3, 1990. Rick later defeated David in a match and David was injured so he was forced to quit New Japan who went to WWF and then Rick feuded with Mark.

Rick defeated Mark and distracted him in his several matches. Rick had then a final match with Mark to end feud on June 16, 1990 and then he kicked Mark up and threw him over the turnbuckles throwing him out of the ring. Rick kept on beating him and knocked him out into the steel steps.

Rick started feuding with Pegasus Kid. He wrestled with Pegasus Kid in many matches and tried to win but he couldn't win in a single match. On September 23, 1990, he defeated Pegasus Kid by a roll-up and he felt very glad by getting the victory over Pegasus Kid.

Rick started feuding with Akira Nogami in the late 1990s and it was one of his most important feuds in his career with New Japan.

[edit] 1991

Rick is best known for developing the careers of his brothers in New Japan as teaming with them. He teamed with David in 1987 and Mark in 1989. In 1991, his brother Eric Rage debuted and then he started teaming with Eric. They both wrestled against David and Mark.

Rick and Eric were both face and their brothers David and Mark were both heel. These two tag teams feuded until April 15, 1991 when a tag team match occurred and David was not present on that match.

Everyone was surprised and then David was fired from New Japan for not coming into the match. This match became a handicap match and then Eric pinned Mark and then Rick and Eric feuded with Mark. Later, it was discovered that David had died and Rick felt very sad about that.

All these three brothers took leap from New Japan and then these three brothers returned on August 24, 1991 and then they reunited. Rick teamed up with both of them and Mark turned into face. Rick and Mark together defeated the then-IWGP World Tag Team Champions Steiner Brothers in a non-title match.

They feuded with Steiner Brothers for the titles but they didn't surrender the titles to Rick and Mark. Later, on November 5, 1991 when they had to defend their titles against Hiroshi Hase and Keiji Mutoh, Rick and Mark came and injured Scott Steiner during that match.

Later, Steiner Brothers challenged them to a match and had a tag team match which the Steiner Brothers won.

[edit] 1992

Rick and Mark defeated Steiner Brothers on January 7, 1992 which was the greatest victory they ever had. Later, on Mark left the tag team once again and competed on his singles. Rick tried to include Eric in his team but he also refused. Rick was now totally alone.

He defeated The Cobra on January 24, 1992 and then he defeated Pegasus Kid who was his rival in the mid-1990 on February 8, 1992. Then he defeated El Samurai on February 15, 1992. He had defeated three cruiserweights during this time because he wanted to get the IWGP Junior Heavyweight Championship.

Rick defeated the-then IWGP Junior Heavyweight Champion Jushin Liger in a non-title match on February 24, 1992 and then on March 3, 1992, Rick and Liger wrestled for the title which Rick lost. He started feuding with Liger for the title and cost him title on June 26, 1992 when Liger defended it against El Samurai.

Rick started feuding with his former rival Pegasus Kid once again and their feud ended in November 1992. Rick then started feuding with the new IWGP Junior Heavyweight Champion at the time Ultimo Dragon and then Rick wrestled with him several times for the title but couldn't get it.

[edit] 1993

On January 8, 1993, Rick received a telegram from his brother Eric that he would return to New Japan if they both would have a One-On-One match. Rick and Eric both of them wrestled with each other on January 17, 1993 and Rick threw Eric over the turnbuckles and when he was running from the ropes, his mother caught his legs.

Rick's mother was this time towards Eric and Rick didn't like this thing that why his mother never supported him. When Rick turned back, Eric kicked him and pinned him to get the victory. After that day, Eric was not seen in New Japan and he commited suicide. Rick was very sad and then he contributed to this.

Mark, who was very close to Eric also felt very sad. Rick and Mark wrestled as a tag team until May 1993 and after that, Mark also commited suicide. Rick became a rival of The Great Muta and then he feuded with Muta until October 1993 and then he feuded with the IWGP World Heavyweight Champion Shinya Hashimoto for the title.

[edit] World Wrestling Federation/Entertainment (1994-1996)

Rick started off in the world's most popular wrestling promotion World Wrestling Federation in March 1994 as a manager of his father the legendary Donald Rage who wrestled in WWF at the time. He started wrestling in WWF in September 1995 after his father left WWF and retired from professional wrestling.

[edit] 1995

Rick debuted in WWF in September of 1995 defeating Hunter Hearst Helmsley. He started wrestling as a bodyguard of Vince McMahon as a heel and was to destroy Triple H.

[edit] 1996

In 1996, Rick started attacking the Clique members and feuding with them. He hated 1-2-3-Kid and feuded with him and defeated him several times.

[edit] World Championship Wrestling (1997-1999)

[edit] 1997

Rick later joined World Championship Wrestling in 1997 defeating "Macho Man" Randy Savage. Afterwards, he targetted Rick Steiner who was his rival in New Japan. They both feuded with each other.

[edit] Road agent

In 1998, Rick became a road agent and fell to semi-retirement. He made several appearances in WCW defeating great names from 1997 to 1999 such as Ric Flair, Hulk Hogan, Lex Luger, Konnan, Chris Kanyon, Disco Inferno and Scott Hall.

[edit] Australian Wrestling Federation (2000-2001)

Rick started wrestling in Australian Wrestling Federation where he captured the AWF Heavyweight Championship and had a feud with Jim Samuel in AWF.

[edit] Retirement

Rick returned to World Wrestling Federation which had now become World Wrestling Entertainment and he feuded with The Rock for the undisputed title. He retired in September 2002.

[edit] Personal life

Rick is married to Diana Gibson. They both knew each other from childhood and got married on June 16, 1989 and are currently having three children.
