Donald Joseph Rage (June 10, 1942 - March 24, 2007) was an American wrestling promoter, professional wrestler, mixed martial artist, footballer, basketballer, hockey player and racer who was a legendary World Champion, Hall of Famer and he wrestled in many places over the world.

[edit] Early life

Donald Rage was a young army lover who joined the U.S. army in 1958 as a young boy where he played football until 1961. He became a World Champion of football and then he started playing basketball and played basketball for many leagues until 1966. After that, he was inactive as a soldier, so he was retired from U.S. army.

He became an amateur wrestler and then he adopted the Greco-Roman style in 1971. He left wrestling and then he became a racer who took in many World Champion Series of races. He started playing hockey in 1976. He left hockey in 1980 and left to become a professional wrestler.

[edit] Professional wrestling career (1980-1995)

[edit] World Wrestling Federation (1980-1983)

Donald Rage entered the world of professional wrestling in the mid-1980. He got his training in wrestling and then went to wrestle for World Wrestling Federation. He debuted by attacking Buddy Roberts. He started feuding with Roberts.

He became a heel wrestler. He tortured Roberts in his career and then went on to feud with Rocky Johnson until 1982. He got his first great victory by defeating Rick Martel. He later changed his ring name from Donald Rage to Don Rose.

He defeated Killer Khan in a match which was his another great victory but his greatest victory in WWF was when he defeated the legendary Bob Backlund. He went on to defeat many legendary names in WWF and became an undefeated wrestler. He feuded with Tonga Kid in the early 1983.

[edit] World Class Championship Wrestling (1983-1986)

Rage debuted in World Class Championship Wrestling in the early 1983 by defeating David Von Erich. He then targetted Kerry Von Erich by attacking him in a match with Iceman Parsons and this put Rage in a feud with Kerry Von Erich. Both of them later had a match with each other which Rage won after a roll-up.

He later defeated Kevin Von Erich in a match and then he started feuding with The Von Erichs. He teamed up with Michael P.S. Hayes and Buddy "Jack" Roberts in a 6-Man Tag Team Match and then he feuded with Von Erich family.

By the early 1984, he feuded with Iceman Parsons. He changed his ring name from Donald Rage to Kelly Kiniski and quickly captured the WCCW Television Championship by defeating Parsons in the early 1984. In May 1984, Kiniski had a match with Killer Khan for the Television title and he was injured in the match, so he gave his title to Killer Khan.

He couldn't come for some days because of his injury and he told that he had a severe pain, so he announced that he would return in WCCW in August. He started feuding with Jake Roberts and Chris Adams for the rest of his career in WCCW.

[edit] World Wrestling Federation (1986-1987)

Donald Rage returned to World Wrestling Federation in 1986 where he was involved in a feud with the WWF World Heavyweight Champion Hulk Hogan. He feuded with him for the World title for a short time. He later went on to feud with Tito Santana, "Macho Man" Randy Savage and Butch Reed.

[edit] Pacific Northwest Wrestling (1987-1990)

He left WWF in 1987 and started wrestling in Pacific Northwest Wrestling where he wrestled under the ring names respectively Liontamer, Scottamer and finally Scott Peterson. His career became hit in PNW after he captured the NWA Pacific Northwest Heavyweight Championship in 1988. He left PNW in 1990.

[edit] United States Wrestling Association (1990-1993)

In 1990, he joined the promotion United States Wrestling Association where he wrestled under the ring name David "Bane" Mosher. He got the USWA Unified World Heavyweight Championship in USWA and feuded with Jerry "The King" Lawler for most of his time with USWA. He left USWA in 1993.

[edit] World Wrestling Federation (1993-1995)

Donald Rage returned to WWF in 1993. Now during his return he wrestled in WWF until two years and for his time with WWF, he feuded with Diesel, The "Heartbreak Kid" Shawn Michaels, Razor Ramon, Bret Hart, The Undertaker, Owen Hart, Yokozuna and Crush. He retired from professional wrestling in 1995.

Donald Rage became a mixed martial artist in 1995 after retiring from professional wrestling.

[edit] Ultimate Fighting Championship (1995-1996)

He debuted in Ultimate Fighting Championship promotion on July 14, 1995 as a mixed martial arts fighter. He fought under the ring name Paul Varelans and he debuted on UFC 6: Clash of the Titans and participated in the UFC 6 Tournament.

He defeated Cal Worsham and then he lost to David L. Abbott in the semifinals. On UFC 7: The Brawl in Buffallo, he was not sure that he would win but he was sure that he would show a better result and he showed a better result in the UFC 7 tournament.

He defeated Gerry Harris in the quarterfinals and then he defeated Mark Hall in the semifinals and then it was the championship match in the finals which he lost to Marco Ruas. At the Ultimate Ultimate 1995 pay-per-view event, he lost the match to Dan Severn.

He had a match with Joe Moreira at UFC 8: David VS Goliath. He won the match by unanimous decision at 10:00 and he was injured in this fight and because of the injury, he was unable to compete in the tournament and was replaced by Sam Adkins.

He didn't appear in UFC for a little time but he made his last appearance on UFC at Ultimate Ultimate 1996 by losing to Kimo Leopoldo.

[edit] Fighting Network RINGS (1996-1998)

After UFC's Ultimate Ultimate 1996 event, Donald left UFC and then went to fight in Japan and he started fighting for the Japanese martial arts promotion (formerly professional wrestling promotion) Fighting Network RINGS where he captured the Heavyweight Championship of RINGS. He left RINGS in 1998.

[edit] PRIDE Fighting Championships (1998-2001)

After leaving RINGS, Donald left for PRIDE Fighting Championships in 1998 and debuted in PRIDE on the PRIDE 4 event of PRIDE held on October 11, 1998 where he fought under the ring name of Allan Goes where he fought Kazushi Sakuraba to a draw. Later on PRIDE 8, he defeated Carl Malenko by a choke.

Later, he defeated Vernon White on PRIDE 9. On PRIDE 13, he lost for the first time in PRIDE and got his loss to Mark Coleman. He didn't appear until PRIDE 18 (which was his last appearance with PRIDE) when he received his second loss in PRIDE to Alex Stiebling.

[edit] Promoting career

After leaving the Fighting Network RINGS, Donald Rage went to London, England where he founded the professional wrestling promotion Heavyweight Wrestling Federation and he became a promoter who put his wrestling cards in HWF and including great wrestlers to his promotion's roster.

[edit] Personal life

Donald Rage got married to Jenna Doan who was his girlfriend in college days. They both fell in love and got married in 1966. They both had together ten sons:

  • Richard Rage (b. 1967)
  • David Rage (1968-1991)
  • Mark Rage (1970-1993)
  • Eric Rage (1972-1993)
  • Kerry Rage (1975-1998)
  • Robinson Rage (1977-2000)
  • Matthew Rage (1978-2001)
  • Benjamin Rage (1980-2000)
  • Nicholas Rage (1981-2004)
  • Jack Rage (1984-2007)

Rage's wife died from heart attack in 1996 when three of her sons died and she was very sad at their deaths. Rage is survived by his eldest son Richard Rage.

[edit] Death

During the midnight of March 23, 2007, Donald Rage's tenth son Jack Rage was shot in a fight when he was returning to disco and it was mentioned on TV and Mr. Rage couldn't bare this and he suffered heart attack by this. On March 24, 2007, he was taken to hospital and then the doctors did operation for three hours. Later, it was proclaimed that he was dead.
