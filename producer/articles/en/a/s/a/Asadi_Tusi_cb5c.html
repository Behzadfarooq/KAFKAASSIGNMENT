Asadi Tusi

Abu Mansur Ali ibn Ahmad Asadi Tusi (born: Tus, Iranian province of Khorasan - died: 1072 Tabriz, Iran) is arguably the second most important Persian poet of Iranian national epics, after Ferdowsi who also happens to come from the same town of Tus.

Asadi's most significant work is Garshasp-nama [The Book (or Epic) of Garshasp]. His other important contribution was a lexicon of the Modern Persian language (فرهنگ لغت فرس).

During Asadi's time, and for some time after, much of the Khorasan province was under violent attacks and rivalry by various Turkic groups. Many native intellectuals left Khorasan as a result of these conflicts, while many of those who remained lived in seclusion. As a result of the violence, Asadi left Khorasan for the Iranian province of Azarbaijan (not to be confused with the present-day Republic of Azerbaijan) and stayed there until his death. His tomb is in city of Tabriz.
