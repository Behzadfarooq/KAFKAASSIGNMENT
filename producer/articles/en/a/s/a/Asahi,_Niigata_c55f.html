Asahi, Niigata

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

This article is about the village in Niigata Prefecture, Japan. Asahi is a common name in Japan, for other uses see Asahi.

Asahi (朝日村; -mura) is a village located in Iwafune District, Niigata, Japan. The village has no special relationship with the adjacent Asahi in Yamagata Prefecture.

The village was named after the Asahi Mountains.

As of 2005, the village has an estimated population of 11,836 and a density of 18.69 persons per km². The total area is 629.32 km².

The village was established in 1896 by merging Tatekoshi, Miomote, Takane, Shionomachi, and Sarusawa villages.

Neighboring municipalities are:

Southwest: Murakami
Northwest: Sanpoku
North: Tsuruoka (Yamagata Prefecture)
Northeast: Nishikawa (Yamagata Prefecture)
Southeast: Oguni (Yamagata Prefecture)

Asahi village has a sister relationship with Kiyose,Tokyo.
