Asaki (あさき) is an artist hired by Konami Corporation to create music for their Bemani game series. His lyrics are mostly ballad based and sung in a scat singing style (indeed, many listeners have a hard time understanding his singing), while his music incorporated the style of Japanese Visual kei, classical Japanese, classical European, and jazz, with most of his music appearing in the Guitar Freaks/Drummania series. He also did Elegy (Shiawase wo utau uta), Lament (Shizuku) and Enzetsu (Saru no kyou) for the Pop'n Music series, as well as Industrial Review (Kigonshu) for the beatmania IIDX series. Compared to most other Bemani musicians, he's the most reclusive, and is quite unheard of in North America, especially among bemani fans who do not play Drummania often. This is due to the fact that most of his songs are featured on Drummania and are very difficult, and thus not commonly heard by beginners.

About all that is known about Asaki is that he likes curry and cats, and was quoted in a Konami interview that he had previously been a bassist in a visual kei band. He also composes his own music, writes his own lyrics, and plays all of his own instruments. Even in a recently publicized photo, his face was covered up with a cat plushie.

[edit] Discography

Asaki released his first album, "Shinkyoku" (神曲) (officially pronounced as Shinkyoku) in September 16, 2005. The CD track list is the following:

  1. Sanagi (蛹)
  2. Hotaru (蛍)
  3. Shiawase wo utau uta (幸せを謳う詩)
  4. Konoko no nanatsu no oiwaini (この子の七つのお祝いに)
  5. Yogo no oto (予後の音)
  6. Gekkou chou (月光蝶)
  7. Akai suzu (赤い鈴)
  8. Shizuku (雫)
  9. Shinkyoku (神曲)
  10. Sorasumi no hiyodori to (空澄みの鵯と)

The album title, which translates to "Divine Songs", is a pun; pronounced "shinkyoku", or "new song", it can refer to a type of music for the shakuhachi, which would refer to his zen-influenced music. Incidentally, 神曲 is the Japanese title for Aligheri's Divine Comedy.

[edit] Trivia

  • He is sometimes depicted as a cat with the letter "A" on its forehead
  • Based from Asaki's comment on the song "ONIHIME" (from GF11DM10), "ONINOKO GASHODAN" is "12 Asakis". [１２人くらいのあさき]
  • Asaki is a member of わんにゃん☆パニックス (the group who did Die Zauberflöte), along with Hideyuki Ono and TOMOSUKE.
