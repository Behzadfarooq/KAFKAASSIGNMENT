The Fat Wrestling Entertainment Championship is a professional wrestling title.

[edit] History

The title traces its history back to 1929 when it was a boxing championship. It was known as S.B. Heavyweight Championship and the heavyweights wore the boxing belt. The first heavyweight to wear this belt was Shane Clerk. It later became known as S.W.W.F. World Heavyweight Championship and became a wrestling belt in 1942

Now heavyweight boxers (who had become wrestlers) or wrestlers could wear this belt. The belt later became known as Fat Wide Wrestling Federation Championship in 1975 when FWWF had a new wrestling show aired on television. The belt later became Fat Wrestling Federation Championship in 1982 renaming Wide from its name.

In 1977, popular wrestler Sam Pung became the champion and wore this belt. He defeated The Killer to become the champion. He became the longest reigning champion because he lost the title to Rick Serpent in 1991 and reigned for 13 years.

Later, in the 1990s, popular wrestlers such as The "Disco Man" Ilyas Tufail, Asad Serpent, Aamir Mahmud, Hussain Aamir wore the belt.

In 1997, when the promotion closed, the title was abandoned but it was revived in 2005 when the promotion was back as Fat Wrestling Entertainment. There were three brands at the time, Trend, Rock and Rider Waves. It was then Hussain Aamir, superstar from Trend won the title and made it a Trend exclusive.

Several wrestlers from Rock also got the title and also from Rider Waves so this made difficult that on which brand, the title should be but recently, it is a Trend exclusive.

[edit] Belts

  • S.B. Heavyweight Championship (1929-1942)
  • Serpentis Wide Wrestling Federation Heavyweight Championship (1942-1975)
  • Fat Wide Wrestling Federation Championship (1975-1982)
  • FWWF/FWF Sam Pung Championship (1977-1991)
  • Fat Wrestling Federation Championship (1991-1997)
  • Fat Wrestling Federation Disco Championship (1991-1993); 1997
  • Fat Wrestling Entertainment Championship (2005-2006)
  • FWE Championship Ronnie Bilal Belt (2006-present
