[edit] Disambiguation link repair at Ore (disambiguation)

Hi! You changed a link I'd updated at this page to link to Norwegian people rather than Norway. FYI, I had deliberately selected "Norway" as the link target because for modern public figures, I think national rather than ethnic origin is the appropriate emphasis. I haven't reverted your edit because I'd like to discuss the issue with you and see if we can come to agreement on this. Thanks. --Tkynerd 18:50, 7 October 2006 (UTC)

I feel that if I see a wikilink related to a people it should link to an article about a people and not a place. With a slight change to say "a mathematician from Norway." I would expect the link to go to a place. What it comes down to is where the emphasis should be, as well as propriety, if it is the standard for [[Japan|Japanese]], [[United States|American]] or [[United Kingdom|British]], then it should be [[Norway|Norwegian]].—Asatruer 20:17, 7 October 2006 (UTC)
My feeling is that "Norwegian" usually refers to people geographically from (i.e., living in) Norway rather than necessarily people of Norwegian ethnic origin. The page Norwegian people is specifically about Norwegian ethnicity. There is a good parallel example at Mauricio Rojas; you can read the history on the talk page for more of my thoughts on this. To my mind, for (as I posted above) modern public figures, terms of nationality do usually refer strictly to nationality, not to ethnic origin (where these differ). It would be possible to link, say, ethnically Norwegian people to "Norwegian people" and Norwegians who are not ethnically Norwegian to "Norway," but I find that an invidious distinction. It isn't an easy issue, though. Thanks for your consideration. --Tkynerd 20:39, 7 October 2006 (UTC)

[edit] your edit to William H. Gates, Sr.

Do you have any source for that ? Because to me, it seems unlikely that he dropped the II, as you generally don't add a II to your name.

  • An individual is called John Doe Sr..
  • His Son is called John Doe Jr.
  • His grandson is called John Doe III
  • His grand-grandson is called John Doe IV.
  • ad infinitum...

So he should be called "William H. Gates Jr.", if he was the Second of that name, or "William H. Gates III" if he was the third of his name. In this case his son (microsoft-bill) should be "William H. Gates IV"....-- ExpImptalkcon 17:15, 25 October 2006 (UTC)
