Matthew Joseph Rage (August 23, 1978 - December 23, 2001) was an American professional wrestler and was the seventh son of the legendary wrestler and the mixed martial artist Donald Rage (1942-2007). He briefly wrestled in New Japan Pro Wrestling and All Japan Pro Wrestling.

[edit] Early life

Matt was born to the legendary Donald Rage on August 23, 1978. He was a nice son and his father always thought that he was going to become the best wrestler in the family but Matt made his father wrong because in 1993, at the age of 15, instead of becoming a wrestler, he continued to his studies and by 19, he became a wrestler in 1997.

[edit] Career

[edit] New Japan Pro Wrestling (1997-1999)

[edit] Tag team with Robin

Matt started his career in New Japan Pro Wrestling after the death of his mother where his other brothers also wrestled. He started as a tag team with his brother Robin. They both defeated many wrestlers in New Japan as a tag team.

[edit] Singles competition

In 1998, Matt wrestled on singles competition and feuded with Shinya Hashimoto. He became a rival of Hashimoto defeating him on several occassions. He also defeated Hashimoto for the belt but was not recognized as the champion.

Matt then formed a tag team with Kensuke Sasaki and they both had a very successful tag team until 1999.

[edit] All Japan Pro Wrestling (2000-2001)

For a brief time, Matt went to wrestle for All Japan Pro Wrestling as a consultant for Dory Funk, Jr.. He continued to make appearances for AJPW and feuded with Genichiro Tenryu for the AJPW World Heavyweight Championship until 2001.

[edit] Death

Matt, who had a very short career committed suicide on December 23, 2001 by climbing from a 8-storey building and he was drunk during that suicide.
