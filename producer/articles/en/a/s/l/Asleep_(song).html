"Asleep" is a song by The Smiths. It features on the compilation albums The World Won't Listen and Louder Than Bombs, and originally appeared as the B-side to The Boy With The Thorn In His Side. The song, one of the band's bleakest, features mostly piano; the melody was something Johnny Marr had been toying around with for years, originally composed (in simpler form) as a coda to Suffer Little Children. The song was recorded the same day as Rubber Ring, when Marr announced that he had "a little piano thing" he wanted "to put down with Morrissey."

It remains a fan-favourite, although it was only performed live once by the group (at Eden Court, Inverness, on 1 October 1985, the last date of the '85 Scotland tour), after a piano, which couldn't be moved, was discovered on the wings of the stage during the sound check. Morrissey was apparently in a fetal position in the center of the stage by the end of the song (Simon Goddard, The Smiths: Songs that Saved Your Life, pp. 179-182).

[edit] Cover Versions

  • Xiu Xiu performed an acoustic cover version of Asleep in their EP Fag Patrol.

[edit] Trivia

  • In Stephen Chbosky's book The Perks Of Being A Wallflower, Asleep is listed as being the favourite song of the main character, Charlie, and serves a major purpose in the story.
  • The song "I Wish You Were Here" and it's Untitled track following it on Matchbook Romance's album Voices shares similar lyrics and themes from the song. With many considering that Matchbook Romance's were inspired by it.
  • Comedian and Television presenter Russell Brand said on his Radio Show, that he would like "Asleep" to be played at his funeral.
