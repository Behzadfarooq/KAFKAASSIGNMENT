Aspen Knolls

Aspen Knolls is one of the largest housing developments in Staten Island, New York. It contains 944 single family housing units and is located on the island's south shore, by the intersection of Arthur Kill Road and Woodrow Road.

Plans for this development began in the eraly 1990s and were originally meant for housing of Navy families. However, due to Base Realignment and Closure, the housing contract was terminated in November 1994. With the development already planned, its builders decided to go through with construction and sell the homes to regular citizens. Construction began in 1995 and was finished in early 2006. During this time period, people moved into the community as each house was finished being built.

The community today has over 3,000 residents, and boasts a swimming pool, tennis court, basketball court, well maintained walkways, and club house. The community has established strict parking policies in its bylaws, which results in many cars being towed out of the complex. Another prevalent problem is terrorism and vandalism of the community. Despite strict security procedures, cars have been vandalized, homes have been spray painted, and teenagers roam the neighborhood with drugs and alcohol. Aspen Knolls is some of the cheapest real estate in Staten Island, and thus has become a melting pot of minorities. It is also a haven for single parent families.
