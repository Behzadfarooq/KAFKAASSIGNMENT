Aspinall's is an elite, private gambling club located in Mayfair of London, England. It was established by John Aspinall first in the 1960s and has subsequently changed its name several times along with its location. Aspinall's is located at 27-28 Curzon Street.

[edit] History

Club founder, John Aspinall, or "Aspers", was a conservationist and breeder of wild animals who funded his zoos in great part from house winnings. Aspinalls is still one of the most extravagant and elegant gambling house the world has ever seen. It is not a glitzy casino in the style of Las Vegas casinos. In its earlier days, Aspinalls was filled with a lot of Old Etonians and Brit upper-class men who gambled away their inheritances. Once the thin blue line of British aristocracy had begun to run dry as far as funds were concerned, Aspinall was obliged to look elsewhere for clientle. The first notable club member he invited into the club was non other than the present king of Saudi Arabia, Fahd who enjoyed spending time at Aspinall’s, then called the Clermont.

The 1960s in London were politically incorrect times, and nowhere was it more politically incorrect than at Aspinall’s. The notorious Lord Lucan who was a close personal friend of Aspinall's and who murdered his children’s nanny whom he confused for his wife, the intended victim. (“Lucky,” as Lucan was called because of a 10-year run of bad luck, fell on his sword immediately after his heinous act. Aspinall commissioned a bust of Lucan which hangs on a prominent wall to this day.

[edit] Today

Many famous politicians, diplomats from various international background find themsleves mixing at Aspinalls now run by Aspinall's son.
