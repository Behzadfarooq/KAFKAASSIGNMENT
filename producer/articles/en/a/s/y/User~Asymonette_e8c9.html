[edit] Teaching Matters Residency

DISCLAIMER: This is a working draft in progress. The ideas posted on this website are a result of a meeting with several current and future residents and management staff. Please edit the content of the page as you see fit.

Residencies

With each year-long residency Teaching Matters can provide a school with sustained, context-specific and job-enabled professional development.

Our consistent and ongoing presence in a school enables us to institute a customized apprenticeship model, which starts with modeling at the beginning of the residency period and gradually shifts to a scaffolded and co-teaching approach. By the end of the residency period, the scaffolding fades and the classroom teacher is put in the driver’s seat. In addition to being a consistent school-based resource, our professional developer can help structure time for reflection and dissemination of best practice throughout the school.

A core tenet of the Residency program is that the principal and the school administration are the "thought leaders" of the building and that the Teaching Matters resident is an extension of that group. Working in conjunction with school leaders, the resident assists in the creation of a strategic professional development plan tied to the vision of the principal and customized to the school's needs. This plan is later implemented through the delivery of workshops and direct in-classroom support for the teacher (planning, modeling, supporting the teacher, etc.)

Residents will seek numerous ways to enhance curriculum in the school. The will coordinate activities with coaches, conduct teacher study groups, work side-by-side with teachers to develop and implement lessons.

A resident becomes an integral part of the school community and seeks to be embedded within the culture of the school.

[edit] How does a school benefit from having a residency?

• Informed and connected to a network of other schools and other residents • Training for the principal • Exposure & training to new ideas and techniques • Offer useful PD in technology. In classroom support to harness power of technology integration for student learning • Evaluation of technology infrastructure a and continuous discussion of how technology is utilized throughout school • A veteran PD that is on staff for at least 1 day a week throughout the school year • Access to all curricular unites and our writing the city tool / pagekeeper tool • Access to all our city-wide events • After-school workshops done on-site 1X a month OR participation in workshops at Teaching Matters • A technology assessment and implementation plan • A custom school LMS: setup, training and implementation • Ongoing LMS support • Turn-key training to a single staff member • Calendar cycles of programs for principals • Opportunities to participate in Principals 2000 PD assists in finding the solution to an identified problem(s) • Synergetic catalyst between teachers & administration • Offering a full range of TMI programs as well being there for the customized work • PD • Knowing technology needs and ordering • Being able to partner with outside sources and partners • Being a liaison to TMI & all of our special services and events (kick-off parties, film festivals, etc…)

[edit] What is required from the school?

• An empowered principal • Principal may have to draft a proposal / strategy for how the residency will work • Agreement to some type of pre-assessment for the school (not use the teacher survey)


[edit] How does the Residency begin?

School Year start • Introduction to staff • Contacting staff and sending them a copy of the calendar, and highlighting when you will be working with them. • Ideally, the principal or AP would come for an initial meeting at Teaching Matters to be introduced to a menu of project ideas and options and would participate in individualized planning. A proposal with tangible, attainable goals would be crafted (including a potential pacing calendar) and then the resident would begin in a school. If the school opted in, a baseline assessment would be performed by an outside evaluator (with Teaching Matters absorbing costs).

MOVE TOWARD EMPOWERMENT:

Just about all of the PDs doing Residencies in the fall are participating in schools that will be part of the empowerment zones. PDs had different impressions of schools entering the empowerment zones:

• A weight has been lifted off the school – opportunity to get away from the regional structure • A new start for the school • Budgetary opportunities to do new things • Principal is able to put forth their vision • Great opportunity for a new principal that is ready, yet some see the value of having support from the region (LISes, budgetary assistance, etc.)
