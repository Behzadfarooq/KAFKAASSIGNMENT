Asuka Hayashi (林明日香 , Asuka Hayashi?), born May 28, 1989, is a Japanese 17 year old singer who debuted at the age of 13 in January 2003. Hayashi Asuka debuted simultaneously in Japan and Taiwan with the song ake-kaze when she was 13 years old. Her voice is known for being extremely strong and mature, sounding much like an Enka singer or an opera singer. Asuka also heads a band, entitled Belovelover. Hayashi got her contract with Toshiba-EMI when she was only 11 years old. Her debut album, called "Saku" (咲, "Saku"?), was released on the same day in Japan, Taiwan, Hong Kong and Mainland China. She is known throughout Japan, Hong Kong, Taiwan, and the United States.

[edit] Biography

Born and raised in Osaka, Asuka always thought that she would become a singer. She grew up listening to Disney music and imitating foreign Divas as she listened to their music. Asuka managed to debut at such an early age by her piano teacher listening to her singing accompinament of the songs she was learning, and sent a demo tape into Toshiba EMI. She won a contract, and debuted simultaneously in Japan and Taiwan with the song ake-kaze in 2003. She was only 13 at the time. This song reached to number 14 on Oricon Charts; which was an impressive feat for a debut artist.

Her debut album, Saku (咲; or Xiao in Mandarin - means Bloom) was released in Japan, Taiwan, Hong Kong and China in May of 2003. This was the first time a Japanese artist had debuted at the same time in both Japan and China. Saku also broke another record, for it's release made her the youngest artist to ever have a top ten weekly debut in Japan on Oricon charts. Later on in that year, her single Chiisaki Mono (小さきもの) was selected as the ending theme for Pokémon Jirachi Wishmaker (Japanese: Nanayo no Negai Boshi; literally "Wishing Star of Seven Nights"). Later on for the English version of the movie, Asuka sung this in duet with Cindy Mizelle. Asuka's parts were sung in Japanese which had never happened before in Pokémon movies. However, The song was renamed "Make a Wish" for the English version.

Asuka has done a duet with Chinese arist Chen Min on Erhu entitled Tsubame ni Naritai (燕になりたい). There was also a version of this song sung in Mandarin, instead titled Wo Yuan Zuo Yi Zhi Xiao Yan (我願做-只小燕) Asuka also sung her song Renka (蓮花) in Mandarin (Pronounced Lian Hua in Mandarin).

Asuka won the award for New Artist of the Year Award at the 18th Japan Gold Disc Award. Currently Asuka's sales seem to be waining after the extreme success of ake-kaze and Chiisaki Mono.

As Asuka matured more in the music business, she took more control over her own career. Currently she has written the lyrics to the songs Jibun Shinjite (自分信じて), Mou Ichido Anata ni Aitai (もう一度あなたに会いたい), Snowdrop and Taisetsu ni Shiyou ne (大切にしようね), and even composed Snowdrop, Taisetsu ni Shiyou ne, and スノウドロップ herself. Asuka has even started her own rock band, called Belovelover (currently they have only performed live though). Her most recent album, Chou, was produced by herself (her being 16 at the time).

In the distant future, Asuka wants to sing internationally and to study gospel music in New York.[1]
