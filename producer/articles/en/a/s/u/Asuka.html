Asuka

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Asuka is the name of the land where old Japanese capitals or palaces of the 6-7th century, Asuka (飛鳥, Asuka?) existed (the village of Asuka, Nara). The land gave the Asuka period its name.

Also derived from this city is the fairly common Japanese given name Asuka, which can be male or female.

[edit] Places

  • Asuka, Yamato (飛鳥) is the area where ancient palaces and capitals were built.
  • Asuka, Nara (明日香) is a village in Japan's Nara Prefecture; it largely includes the ancient Asuka (飛鳥) area.
  • Asukayama Park (飛鳥山公園 Asukayama-kōen) is a park in Tokyo.

[edit] Companies

  • Asuka (publisher) is a French manga publishing company. [1]

[edit] People

  • Aska (singer-songwriter) (born Ryo Aska (Asuka Ryō)) , a Japanese singer-songwriter, formerly one of the CHAGE and ASKA duo.
  • Asuca Hayashi is a Japanese singer.
  • Asuka Hinoi is a Japanese singer, "chief" of Hinoi Team (named after her).
