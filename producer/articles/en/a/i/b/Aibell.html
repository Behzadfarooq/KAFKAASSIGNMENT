In Irish mythology, Aibell (Aoibhell, Aeval) was a goddess or fairy queen of Munster. She determined if husbands were sexually satisfying their wives.[citation needed]

[edit] Etymology

This theonym appears to be derived from Proto-Celtic *Oibel-ā. The name literally means "burning fire," which may have been a byword for the notion of ‘ardour’ (q.v. [1] [2] [3]). The Romano-British form of this Proto-Celtic theonym is likely to have been *Oebla (q.v. [4] [5] [6] [7]).
