Australian War Memorial (AWM) catalog number D00685

Sourced from: http://www.awm.gov.au/database/collection.asp

Copyright: The AWM record for this photo states that the copyright status is 'clear'. The photo was taken in 1919.

The AWM allows the use of images from its online database whose copyright has expired for non-commercial purposes only on the condition that the AWM's watermark is not removed. Higher resolution images may be ordered through the AWM (Source: email from the AWM to Nick Dowling 6 January 2006).

AWM Caption: London, England. June 1919. Group portrait of members of the AIF Cricket Eleven, which toured the United Kingdom from May to September, 1919, at Lords Ground. Back row, left to right: Staff Sergeant C. S. Winning, Dental Section, AIF Headquarters (HQ); Sergeant H. S. Love, Australian Army Service Corps (AASC); Gunner (Gnr) J. T. Murray, 103rd Battery; Gnr E. Bull, 26th Battery; Lieutenant J. M. Gregory, 4th Divisional Artillery; Captain (Capt) E. J. Long, Deputy Assistant Provost Marshal, Weymouth; Corporal E. A. Oldfield. Middle row: Capt C. T. Docker, General List; Capt C. E. Pellew, 27th Battalion; Lance Corporal H. L. Collins, 10th AASC; Capt C. B. Willis, Dental Section; Sgt A. W. Lampard, 10th AASC; Capt W. L. Trennery, 17th Battalion. Front row: Gnr J. M. Taylor, 101st Howitzer Battery; Warrant Officer W. S. Stirling, AIF Headquarters, Records Section. They are wearing the official team blazer with the AIF rising sun emblem on the pocket.
