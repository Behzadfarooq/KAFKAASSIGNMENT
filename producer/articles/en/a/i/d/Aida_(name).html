Aida (pronounced ['ɑ‿ɪɖɐ]) is female given name. Variants of name are: Aeeda, Aide, Aidee, Ayeeda, Ieeda and Iyeeda.[1]

Name day is on August 31 in memory of Saint Aidano (in Italy?)[2] and on July 28 (and on January 2 too?) in Poland.[3]

There are 34 496 people named Aida in The United States.[4]

[edit] Origin and meaning

It is name of Spanish origin meaning moonlight or light of the moon.[5]

Or it is name of French origin meaning helper.[6]

Or it is of Egyptian origin[7] derived probably from an alteration of the Egyptian name Iiti.[8]

Sometimes it is mentioned as name of Italian origin.[9], [10]

Sometimes it is mentioned as name of Latin origin meaning distinguished or helpful.[11]

It is mentined as name of African origin meaning reward too.[12]

[edit] Scripts

Arabic script:[13] عائدة in Arabic meaning reward or present.[14]

For Chinese transciption see [2].

[edit] Note

Aida is name of an Ethiopian princess in opera Aida by Giuseppe Verdi.

Aida (間) can be a Japanese name.

  • Aida Turturro (1962) - American actress
  • Aida Yespica (1982) - Venezuelan beauty queen
