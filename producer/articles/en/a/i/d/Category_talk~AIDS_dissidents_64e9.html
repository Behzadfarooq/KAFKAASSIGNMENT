[edit] Pseudoscience Category

I have reverted the addition of the Pseudoscience category because it is not clear that all AIDS dissidents belong in this category. Certainly some of them do, but others are simply skeptics that are not putting forward their own theories, and so cannot rightfully be described as practicing pseudoscience (perhaps Pathological skepticism would be more appropriate). Please do not add the category back without discussing it here, since this is clearly a complex issue. --Wclark 05:20, 7 August 2006 (UTC)

I disagree. This was a sub cat of the Pseudoscientists category (now CFD'd) for quite some time. I've re-added it. FeloniousMonk 05:26, 7 August 2006 (UTC)
The Pseudoscientists category was CFD'd because a category – allowing for no annotation – isn't appropriate for this type of use. There is much legitimate debate as to whether all AIDS dissidents should be seen as doing pseudoscience, and so it's POV to apply the label. Pseudoscience doesn't simply mean "incorrect" – it has a more nuanced technical meaning that simply doesn't apply to the position of many AIDS dissidents. If you believe that AIDS skeptics (who fall under the category of AIDS dissidents) are practicing pseudoscience, please justify your claim in detail here. Until then, please do not add the category back. --Wclark 05:32, 7 August 2006 (UTC)
Ah, now I see; it was you who CFD'd the Pseudoscientists category to begin with. First you get, Pseudoscientists, a useful and informative category deleted. Now you're back here at this sub cat, and only this sub cat, objecting to it being included in the meta cat. What's next, CFD the Pseudoscience category? This strikes me as a bit like a campaign... So, considering that, no, inclusion of this sub cat in the Pseudoscience cat is justified for the same reasons and stated above. FeloniousMonk 05:33, 7 August 2006 (UTC)
BTW, your criteria for keeping a cat, annotation, is flawed and baseless. Also, read Wikipedia:Neutral_point_of_view/FAQ#Pseudoscience. I'll likely act to have the Pseudoscientists cat restored. FeloniousMonk 05:36, 7 August 2006 (UTC)
That's not my "criterea for keeping a cat", it's the reason that inherently pejorative labels shouldn't be categories. There is no way to explain – or annotate – the application of a category; something is either in a category, or not. In cases where there is an overwhelming consensus that a particular theory is pseudoscience (such as, say, Phrenology) it is probably acceptible to include it in the category without explanation or qualification. However, for controversial and disputed cases like AIDS dissidents – where some members of the community do put forth theories that are clearly pseudoscientific while others merely express skepticism or criticism of the prevailing view or methodology – it's not reasonable to apply a label without further explanation. A category does not provide any mechanism for such annotation or clarification.
Additionally, I'm not sure what makes you think you can veto the consensus reached on the CFD page for the pseudoscientists category. The pseudoscience category itself is arguably more useful, because placing article B in category A doesn't necessarily imply that B is and A – unless that's directly implied by the name of the category (as is the case with "pseudoscientists" but not with "pseudoscience" – although if the category were named "pseudosciences" then I might be inclined to nominate it for CFD as well). --Wclark 05:54, 7 August 2006 (UTC)
Veto consensus? The cfd was on the suitability of the cat, not on the contents. Just because you consider pseudoscience pejorative doesn't make it so. Guettarda 05:58, 7 August 2006 (UTC)
