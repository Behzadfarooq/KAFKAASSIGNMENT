Quote of the Day

  1. All this will not be finished in the first hundred days. Nor will it be finished in the first thousand days, nor in the life of this administration, nor even perhaps in our lifetime on this planet. But let us begin. —John F. Kennedy

  2. For one human being to love another; that is perhaps the most difficult of all our tasks, the ultimate, the last test and proof, the work for which all other work is but preparation. —Rainer Maria Rilke

  3. You see things and you say 'Why?' But I dream things that never were and say 'Why not?' —George Bernard Shaw

  4. If you are going through hell, keep going. —Sir Winston Churchill

  5. Love is always bestowed as a gift - freely, willingly and without expectation. We don't love to be loved; we love to love. —Leo Buscaglia

  6. The ultimate measure of a man is not where he stands in moments of comfort and convenience, but where he stands at times of challenge and controversy. —Martin Luther King, Jr.

  7. Do not pray for easy lives. Pray to be stronger men. —John F. Kennedy

  8. The glory of Christianity is to conquer by forgiveness. —William Blake

  9. We all agree that your theory is crazy, but is it crazy enough? —Niels Bohr

  10. Do all the good you can, by all the means you can, in all the ways you can, in all the places you can, at all the times you can, to all the people you can, as long as ever you can. —John Wesley

  11. Let every nation know, whether it wishes us well or ill, that we shall pay any price, bear any burden, meet any hardship, support any friend, oppose any foe to assure the survival and the success of liberty. —John F. Kennedy

  12. I find that the harder I work, the more luck I seem to have. —Thomas Jefferson

  13. Make everything as simple as possible, but not simpler. —Albert Einstein

  14. An ounce of action is worth a ton of theory. —Friedrich Engels

  15. How wonderful it is that nobody need wait a single moment before starting to improve the world. —Anne Frank

  16. In the End, we will remember not the words of our enemies, but the silence of our friends. —Martin Luther King, Jr.

  17. 'Tis the most tender part of love, each other to forgive. —John Sheffield

  18. If you want to make an apple pie from scratch, you must first create the universe. —Carl Sagan

  19. There are two ways to live your life. One is as though nothing is a miracle. The other is as though everything is a miracle. —Albert Einstein

  20. Those who dare to fail miserably can achieve greatly. —John F. Kennedy

  21. You are never given a dream without also being given the power to make it true. —Richard Bach

  22. Not everything that can be counted counts, and not everything that counts can be counted. —Albert Einstein

  23. There is no revenge so complete as forgiveness. —Josh Billings

  24. I have been astonished that men could die martyrs for religion - I have shudder'd at it - I shudder no more - I could be martyr'd for my religion - Love is my religion - I could die for that - I could die for you. —John Keats (A Letter To Fanny Brawne, 13 October 1819)

  25. In spite of everything I still believe that people are really good at heart. I simply can't build up my hopes on a foundation consisting of confusion, misery and death. —Anne Frank

  26. The best and most beautiful things in the world cannot be seen or even touched - they must be felt with the heart. —Helen Keller

  27. Faithless is he that says farewell when the road darkens. —J.R.R. Tolkein

  28. How far that little candle throws his beams! So shines a good deed in a weary world. —William Shakespeare

  29. I may disagree with everything you say, but will defend unto death your right to say it. —Voltaire

  30. Great spirits have always found violent opposition from mediocrities. The latter cannot understand it when a man does not thoughtlessly submit to hereditary prejudices but honestly and courageously uses his intelligence. —Albert Einstein

  31. I saw the angel in the marble and carved until I set him free. —Michelangelo
