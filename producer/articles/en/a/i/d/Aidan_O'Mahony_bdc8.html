Aidan O'Mahony is an Irish Gaelic footballer who plays for Kerry. O'Mahony is a Garda Síochána.

[edit] Football Career

Aidan made his debut for the Kerry senior football team in 2004. Aidan was on the starting team on the 2004, All-Ireland winning team against Mayo. He repeated the performance with in the 2006 All-Ireland final which was also against Mayo. Kerry dominated the game, and Mayo were defeated easily by a scoreline of 4-15 to 3-05, and O'Mahony picked up the RTE Man of the Match Award for his marking of Ciaran MacDonald and scoring of 2 points. O'Mahony has two National Football league medals for Kerry from 2004 and 2005. He has also won the Munster club championship with his club Rathmore.

[edit] Suspension

Aidan was suspended from the garda training center in Templemore because of a row that developed into a fight in a night club.He is now free of all charges after a court ruling and is allowed to go back Templemore
