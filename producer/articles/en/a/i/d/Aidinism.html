Aidinism or Aydinism (Persian: آیدینیسم) is a philosophical view that the truth value of certain claims—particularly theological claims regarding afterlife is unknown or (possibly) inherently unknowable. However Aidinists believe in existence of a mono-God. They also strongly believe in metaphysics,telepathy and some other undefined or undiscovered phenomena.

Some people normally list aidinism alongside categories such as Agnosticism, Deism, theism, atheism and non-religious, although this is misleading, since aidinism differs in many key definitions.

Aidinisits claim either that it is not possible to have absolute or certain knowledge or, alternatively, that while certainty may be possible, they personally have no knowledge. Aidinism in both cases involves some form of skepticism.

[edit] History of the term

"Aidinism" was introduced by a Persian scientist, philosopher and musician in 2006 to describe his philosophy. The term aidinist is also used to describe those who do not absolutely commit themselves about religion.

[edit] Term description

Aidinists believe in the existence of one God. They however do not believe in any prophet or messenger to be sent by God to humans. They believe God is much greater than being limited to time and communicating with human beings.
