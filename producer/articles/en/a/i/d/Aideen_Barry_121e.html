Aideen Barry

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Aideen Barry, (b. 1979, Cork, Ireland), is a sculptor and performance artist who graduated from Galway Mayo Institute of Technology with a Bachelor of Fine Art (Distinction) in 2002.

Barry received the Hewlett Packard Award for her 2002 Exhibition The Futility of Conveying Emotion. In 2003 she had her first international solo show in the Centre Culturel Irlandais, Paris, for Bloomsday. Her work the Futility of Conveying Emotion has been shown in Paris, Lorient, London and Shanghai. In 2004, Barry received the Tyrone Guthrie Award, the Sculpture in Context Prize and a Galway County Council Bursary from the Galway County Arts Office. She also received the top prize at the Claremorris Open which was adjudicated by David Thorpe of the 2004 Turner Prize.

In 2005, Barry represented Irish Contemporary Performance as she was invited to part take in Cork 2005, The European Capital of Culture. While on residency in the Blackwater Artists Studios in Cork, she performed two new works commissioned by Bodily Functions. The first piece was entitled Whatgoesaroundcomesaround and the second Root. They were created especially for the 2005 Capital of Culture. In August 2005, she performed her award-winning performance and installation StormReader to an audience of two thousand people, while in an insect-like dress on the surface of a large man-made lake and bird sanctuary in inner city Cork.
