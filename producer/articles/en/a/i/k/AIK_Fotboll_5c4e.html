AIK is a Swedish football club based in Solna just north of Stockholm. The sport club was formed 15 February 1891 and the football department was formed in 1896. The club's full name roughly translates either into the Common Sports Club, referring to Allmän idrott, which, in the early 1900s, was the Swedish expression for athletics, or the Public Sports Club, as opposed to the private sports clubs which were common at time. The club is currently playing in the highest Swedish league, Allsvenskan. King Carl XVI Gustaf and former UEFA president Lennart Johansson are members of the club.

[edit] History

AIK was founded in at 1891 by Isidor Behrens at Biblioteksgatan, which is located in the very central parts of Stockholm. The club then relocated to the suburb Solna in 1937, as the home stadium of the club was changed to Råsunda Fotbollsstadion in Solna. Football was put into practice 1896 and AIK won its first title four years later. AIK took part in the 1999/2000 Uefa Champions League, this has been their only apperenace in the tournament group stage to date. They were drawn in Group B along with FC Barcelona, Arsenal FC and Fiorentina. AIK would finish bottom of the group with only 1 point from 6 games. Their sole point was a creditable 0-0 draw at home to Fiorentina.


[edit] AIK as a club

AIK are by many people considered the biggest club in Sweden, and the fanbase emphasizes this statement. In 2006, AIK had an average attendance of over 21 000 people which is rather good for a Scandinavian-based side. The club have a good youth set-up which have produced a number of good senior players of high standard during the years. The club's main rivals are twin club Djurgårdens IF, and the local derbys between the clubs are by some people regarded as top 10 in Europe. Another fierce rival is Hammarby IF, coming from Stockholm with a big fan base in the southern suburbs. The derbys involving AIK and Hammarby are of course very intense and passionate, but it's not regarded as big as the one with Djurgårdens IF
