[edit] Kings and roads

Answer for question regarding the Wing Road and Sky King: The king of the Wing Road is the Wind King. The status of Wind King is a pre-requisite to becoming the Sky King. For reason(s) yet to be made clear, the unification of all eight Kings of the AT world seems to be essential in the creation of a new Sky King. It is currently assumed that no one has been crowned as the Sky King in the past. Takeuchi Sora was close, but failed due to the betrayal of Kilik. Dingé 17:56, 16 June 2006

Then maybe it would be better to add this explanation to the article? Ti-Ana 15:04, 19 June 2006 (UTC)

The explanation has been added to the main article. There was another incorrent edit to the table of the same nature, so I guess the addition is necessary. Dingé 17:11, 1 July 2006

Comment by 69.224.18.193: "Im quite sure wind and sky are one in the same because of the fact that in the anime series you see Only 7 roads to the center i wondered my self but u see the sky king come down or maby the wind road wouldnt literaly have a road."
Response: The Wind King and the Sky King are completely different as far as titles and attributes are concerned. Your perceptions about the anime's presentation of Roads and Kings are incorrect. Episode 13 of the anime specifically states "when the eight Roads and eight Kings gather, the fabled Sky King will descend." In this scene one clearly sees eight silhouettes radiating from a fixed focal point where a representation of the Sky Kings slowly appears. Moreover, the Trophaeum in both anime and manga show eight protrusions from its summit, symbolizing the eight roads and kings. Dingé 01:23, 29 August 2006 (UTC)

[edit] TV Anime

If I understand it right, the TV anime is complete while the Manga is ongoing. Are there plans to produce a second season, or will the Manga simply continue with the anime over, like Inuyasha? Also, did the Anime close with an original plot or did it end without closure? TwilightxPrince 22:10, 22 October 2006 (UTC)

I would also want to know the reception of the article thinking that would help a lot of people.

The anime follows the manga quite truthfully, except for the battles. They get shifted around a bit chronologically, and sometimes have substantially different 'content' (e.g. the fight with the skull saders occurs before the battle with buccha in the manga, and has ikki applying the air-gear-on-wall trick; it occurs after the buccha fight in the anime, with Ikki wallriding instead of doing any tricks to win), but the end result is always the same. Also, some critical information/subplots are missing from the anime, like the graveyard arc/potemkin battle being left out. The anime has a completely open ending, and didn't really feature plot details not mentioned in the anime (i do not consider changing battle details as part of the plot).
