Air Equator

Air Equator is an airline based in Gan, Maldives. It operates services linking the southernmost island of Gan to other main islands in the Maldives. Its main base is Gan International Airport.

[edit] Code Data

  • ICAO Code: EQU

[edit] History

The airline was established in 2003 and received its air operator's certificate on 10 October 2004 from the Maldives Civil Aviation Department. It started operations on 15 October 2004 with flights from Gan to the capital Malé. It is owned by A Faiz/A Murthy (60%) and Ziaf Enterprises Maldives (40%). The airline discontinued all flights in May 2005 and finally ceased operations in August 2005 after failing to follow certain financial agreements.

[edit] Services

Air Equator operated services to the following domestic scheduled destinations (at January 2005): Gan, Hanimaadhoo, Kadedhoo and Malé.

[edit] Fleet

The Air Equator fleet consisted of 1 Fairchild F-27F aircraft (at January 2005).
