AirTrain (SFO)

AirTrain is a fully automated people mover at San Francisco International Airport (SFO). The system was built by Bombardier at a cost of $430 million US and opened February 24, 2003.

[edit] Routes

AirTrain operates on two lines, Red and Blue. The Red Line travels in a clockwise loop, serving stations at the airport terminals and parking garages in the following order:

  • Garage G/BART
  • International Terminal G
  • Terminal 3
  • Terminal 2
  • Terminal 1
  • International Terminal A
  • Garage A

The Blue Line travels in a counterclockwise loop, serving the same stations in reverse order. In addition, between stops at Garage A and Garage G/BART, it leaves the loop and the serves the Rental Car Center. Going to and from the Rental Car Center, it also stops at West Field Road.

[edit] Roster

The AirTrain fleet comprises 38 cars built by Bombardier of Montreal, Canada. It is their CX-100 System, the same used at Tampa and Denver International Airports. The train cars are wheelchair accessible ().
