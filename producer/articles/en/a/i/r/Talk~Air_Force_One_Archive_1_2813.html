British Air Trafic Control?

Does anyone have ny more infomation about the incident with British in traffic control in Nov. 2003?

Can't help you with that, I'm afraid, even though I am British. However, I thought I'd just comment on the paragraph where you say the range is '7,800 miles - half way round the world'. Perhaps it would be best left at 7,800 miles given that half way around the world is, stricly speaking, more like 12,000 miles given that the circumference at the equator is around 24,000 miles. Just a thought - and I'll leave it to you to change the article. Regards Jerry 08:31, 2 Oct 2004 (UTC)
PS - I've just come across this BBC item on the ATC incident. As you'll see there is no suggestion that the US authorities 'lied' to British ATC, however an air traffic controller seems to have told a pilot of a British Airways aircraft (BA isn't mentioned in the BBC News article but is on other pages identified via Google) that what the pilot thought was Air Force 1 was a Gulfstream. It's not clear whether the controller in question actually knew that AF1 was AF1 and I very much doubt whether it would be possible to obtain primary source material which could prove this one way or the other.
