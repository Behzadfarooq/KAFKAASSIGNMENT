Air Burkina is an airline of Burkina Faso. It is based in Ouagadougou and operates both passenger and charter services to a number of destinations in the region.

[edit] History

The airline was established on 17 March 1967 under the name Air Volta. It was originally part owned by the Burkinabé government, part by Air France and part privately held. After the demise of Air Afrique, on the 21st February 2001 the state of Burkina Faso yielded 86% actions of the company Air Burkina to the which held 66% of the share capital and transferred 56% to IPS a Consortium akefd/ips (WA) Agan Khan Development network.


It purchased its first aircraft, an Embraer EMB-110 Bandeirante, in 1978, and added a second, a Fokker F28, in 1983. The airline has had serious debt problems, reaching a deficit of one billion CFA francs in 1992 (approx. € 1,500,000). However, by the time of privatization in 2001, the debt had largely been alleviated and the company was predicting an annual revenue of around 3.5 billion CFA francs (more than € 5,000,000).

The company saw a general strike in 2002, when workers demanded a 25% wage increase. In the resulting conflict, the director-general of Air Burkina was forced to resign.

[edit] Services

Air Burkina operates the following services (at January 2005):

  • Domestic scheduled destinations:
    • Bobo-Dioulasso (Bobo Dioulasso Airport)
    • Ouagadougou (Ouagadougou Airport)
  • International scheduled destinations:
    • Abidjan (Port Bouet Airport)
    • Dakar (Dakar-Yoff-Léopold Sédar Senghor International Airport)
    • Bamako (Senou International Airport)
    • Lomé (Lomé-Tokoin Airport)
    • Cotonou (Cadjehoun Airport)
    • Niamey (Diori Hamani International Airport)
    • Paris (Orly Airport)
