Air 1 is a Christian music radio network in the United States, operated by the non-profit EMF Broadcasting. Air 1 primarily plays a heavier blend of contemporary Christian music, mainly Christian pop music. In 1986, KLRD began broadcasting Christian music from Yucaipa, California, and went by the on-air moniker K-LORD. In 1994, KXRD was started as a sister station to KLRD. In 1995, KLRD/KXRD changed their name to Air 1 and began broadcasting via satellite from St. Helens, Oregon. In 1999, Air 1 joined with EMF Broadcasting, and finally in 2002, it moved its headquarters to Rocklin, California. Air 1 makes heavy use of broadcast translators to spread the signal across much of the country. As of November 2006, the network lists 65 translators of various power levels.

K-LOVE, Air 1's co-owned network, plays a softer blend of contemporary Christian music.

[edit] Music LAB

Every 3-4 weeks Air 1 listeners can vote on their favorite songs. This process is called the Music LAB (Listener Advisory Board). To vote, listeners must register at Air 1's website to receive an email containing the names of songs/artists. The songs are mostly newly released songs. First the listener must choose whether or not they are familiar with the song. The listener then rates each song from 1-5 and then indicates whether they would like to hear the song more often, if they are hearing it just the right amount, or if they are hearing the song too much. Air 1 then compiles the data collected and produces a weekly top song chart where the listener can hear a sample of the song, get lyrics to it, or click a link to buy it online. Air 1 yearly produces a top countdown from all of the top songs from that year. Past top songs have been "My Savior My God" by Aaron Shust in 2006, "Take Me" by Hawk Nelson in 2005, "Blessed Be Your Name" by Tree 63 in 2004, and "Run" by Kutless in 2003.

[edit] Air 1 Morning Show

The Air 1 Morning Show begins from 4am PST (6am CST) and goes on for five hours. It is Dj'd by Mike Shaeffer and Jeannie Green, two fun loving people with great chemistry. During the morning show, Mike and Jeannie discuss all kinds of varying topics such as relationships, the day's headlines, and even Jeannie's new puppy all while playing the great commercial-free music Air 1 is famous for.

The day's top news are read and discussed every half hour at a quarter before and after the hour.

Mike and Jeannie also discuss the nation's biggest Christian music news as well as feature artist interviews fromt the studio or via telephone.

Every morning three listeners get the oppurtunity to phone in and become contestants on Airhead trivia. Airhead trivia challenges the contestants with five simple questions ranging from many categories from common sense, pop culture, history, or just about anything else. The first contestant with three correct answers or the most after five is declared the winner. No prize is given but the show in itself is very entertaining.

Mike and Jeannie have their own portion of Air 1's website dedicated to the Morning Show where users can look at photos, listen to artist interviews, check out featured links, and watch some videos of artists and other things.

[edit] Unique Air 1 Music

Air 1 currently plays the songs "How to Save a Life" and "Over My Head (Cable Car)" by The Fray, which is notable because The Fray isn't distributed to the Christian market, like U2.

[edit] Information Updates

At regular intervals, Air 1 news staff provide an update on current news stories, and also Positive News Stories from listeners about what is happening in their communities. News stories can be found online at the Air 1 News Page
