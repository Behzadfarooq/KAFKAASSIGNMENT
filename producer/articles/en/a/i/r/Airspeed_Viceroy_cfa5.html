The Airspeed AS.8 Viceroy was a British racing version of the Airspeed AS.6 Envoy built by Airspeed (1934) Limited at Portsmouth. The Viceroy was built to order for Captain T. Neville Stack to compete in the England-Australia MacRobertson Air Race. Only one aircraft, registered G-ACMU, was built.

[edit] History

The Viceroy was a modified Airspeed AS.6 Envoy, the main difference was a strengthened landing gear, and a 270 Imp gallon/1,227 litre auxiliary fuel tank inside the cabin. Although the aircraft started the race from Mildenhall, England it withdrew from the race at Athens. After a number of small faults Neville Stack realised he had no chance of finishing the race. The aircraft returned to Portsmouth and was then stored until July 1936 when it departed to France en route to the Spanish Civil War.

[edit] Specifications

General characteristics

  • Length: 34 ft 6 in (10.52 m)
  • Wingspan: 52 ft 4 in (15.95 m)
  • Height: 9 ft 6 in (2.90 m)
  • Wing area: 339 ft² (31.49 m²)
  • Max takeoff weight: 6,300 lb (2,858 kg)
  • Powerplant: 2× Armstrong-Siddeley Cheetah IV radial piston engine, 280 hp (209 kw) each

Performance

  • Maximum speed: 210 mph (338 km/h)
  • Range: 1,400 miles (2,253 km)
