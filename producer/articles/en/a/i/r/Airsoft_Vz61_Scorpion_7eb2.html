The Airsoft Vz61Scorpion is a replica of a submachine gun, the Vz 61, used in post-Soviet Union countries as a police weapon. Its compact design and high rate of fire made it an excellent choice for not only police squads but also the gangs and thugs they were fighting. The airsoft version is available in 2 different formats. An interesting fact about the Vz61 is that it was featured in the hit video game GoldenEye 007 as the Klobb, or nick-named, the "paper-shreader".

[edit] Gas operated version

The gas operated version is the main choice for users of the Vz61. In the early days of airsoft, gas operated guns were the only choice for airsofters, and were clunky and had external packs that were connected to the gun. The new gas guns or GBB (Gas Blow Back) use 3 different sources of power:

  • Green Gas
  • Propane
  • Co2

The Vz61 uses the green gas power-source, and gets about 300 shots out of a 88 gram tank. The clip that the gun uses has 2 different holds. 20 6mm BBs in the standard, and 31 in the non-standard. No hi-caps (large amount of BBs) are available to the extent of my knowledge.

[edit] Spring operated version

The spring operated Vz61 uses the single shot cocking system usd by cheaper companies and are commonly known as "springers". THis model has the sam setup as the gas version, sam clip size and what not, but a lower fps (feet per second) then the gas version. -Gas 260fps -Spring 230fps A noted change between the both is the spring has the same wood grip as the real world gun, while the gas powered version uses synthetic grips.

[edit] Upcoming AEG version

Tokyo Marui has enjoyed success with its headline SMG model the TM MP7A1, which is the first in a line of small auto-hanguns including the Steyr TMP, the Vz61, and the MAC11 and others. This will be the long awaited mini moter guns that incorparate a smaller moter that has the same power as lager rifle moters and uses less power, which means that a smaller battery can be used, making these small gun possible.
